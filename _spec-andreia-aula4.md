## GRAMÁTICA: Present continuous vs present simple
### Discovery (destaque accent nos -ing):
- "I usually work in the ICU, but this month I am **training** on the new robot."
- "Right now, we are **implementing** the Da Vinci system."
- "These days, the program is **expanding**."
- "At the moment, I am **adapting** to my new role."
Pergunta: "What is the difference between 'work' and 'am training'?"
Reveal: Present simple = routines/facts; Present continuous = am/is/are + -ing, para o que acontece AGORA/temporário/mudando (now, right now, at the moment, currently, these days, this week/month).

### Common Mistake:
- ❌ "At the moment, I adapt to the new role."
- ✅ "At the moment, I am adapting to the new role."
(situação em andamento agora = present continuous, não present simple)

### Questions: "What are you working on right now?" / "Are you training on the new system?"

## CCQs
1. "'I am training' — is it finished, routine, or happening now?" → happening now (temporary).
2. "'I work' vs 'I am working' — which is the routine?" → I work.
3. "'We are implement' or 'We are implementing'?" → implementing (am/is/are + -ing).
