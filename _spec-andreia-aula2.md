## GRAMÁTICA-FOCO: Past Simple (regular + irregular)
### Discovery (destaque accent):
- "I **graduated** from nursing school in 2008." (reg)
- "I **worked** the night shift for six years." (reg)
- "I **led** a small team during the pandemic." (irreg led)
- "I **got** promoted to coordinator." (irreg got)
Pergunta: "What do the blue words have in common? Regular or irregular?"
Reveal: Past simple = -ed (regular) OR irregular form; same for all subjects; time markers (in 2008, ago, for, when).

### Common Mistake (gap dela — escapa pro presente / erra irregular):
- ❌ "I begin my career in the ICU."
- ✅ "I began my career in the ICU."
(irregular begin→began; reforça que ação terminada no passado = passado)

### Questions/Negatives (did):
- "What did you do before robotic surgery?" / "Where did you study?" / "I didn't work in surgery before."

## CCQs (escritas)
1. "When I say 'I led a team', is 'led' present or past?" → past (irregular of lead).
2. "'I graduated in 2008' — am I still graduating?" → no, it's finished.
3. "Do we say 'I goed' or 'I went'?" → went (irregular).
