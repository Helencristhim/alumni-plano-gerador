Negotiating Your Future -- Career Transition

A headhunter da aula 1 volta — agora com a oferta. Negociar sem pedir desculpa por negociar. Key words: relocation, equity package, vesting, notice period, counteroffer, compensation, sign-on bonus, to weigh up, non-compete, deal breaker, to turn down, buyout. Structures: mixed conditionals (passado → presente: "If I had taken it, I would be in London now"; presente → passado: "If I were more risk-averse, I would have turned it down") e gerunds vs infinitives (consider + -ing, decide + to, e depois de QUALQUER preposição sempre -ing).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Não conhecer três destas palavras já custou milhões a executivos muito bons.

Relocation -- moving to another country to take the job (realocação, mudança de país)
"The relocation package covers the move, but not the school fees."
Equity package -- the shares you receive as part of your pay (pacote de ações)
"If I had negotiated the equity package harder, I would own twice as much today."
Vesting -- the schedule over which your shares actually become yours (vesting, período de aquisição)
"Four-year vesting means leaving early costs you real money."
Notice period -- how long you must keep working after you resign (aviso prévio)
"My notice period is three months, and they want me in six weeks."
Counteroffer -- the offer your current employer makes to keep you (contraproposta)
"Accepting a counteroffer usually means leaving anyway, twelve months later."
Compensation -- the whole package: salary, bonus and equity together (remuneração total)
"Compensation is not salary. Confusing them is how executives get underpaid."
Sign-on bonus -- money paid simply for joining (bônus de contratação)
"I asked for a sign-on bonus to cover the bonus I would forfeit."
To weigh up -- to consider carefully, comparing the options (sopesar, ponderar)
"I spent a month weighing up the offer before deciding to accept."
Non-compete -- a clause that stops you joining a rival for a period (cláusula de não concorrência)
"The non-compete was twelve months, and that was my deal breaker."
Deal breaker -- the one thing you will not accept, whatever else they offer (ponto inegociável)
"Everything is negotiable except the deal breaker. Know yours before you start."
To turn down -- to refuse an offer (recusar)
"If I were more risk-averse, I would have turned it down."
Buyout -- money the new employer pays to cover what you lose by leaving (compra do bônus perdido)
"They agreed to a buyout of the bonus I was giving up."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Sarah called back. The offer was on the table: a competitive base, an equity package vesting over four years, London in six weeks, and a twelve-month non-compete. Most executives, at this exact moment, say thank you and say yes. Felipe did not. He said he was considering relocating — note the gerund after consider — and that he had decided to negotiate — note the infinitive after decide. His notice period was three months, so six weeks was not possible. Leaving in March meant forfeiting his bonus, so he asked for a buyout of what he was giving up, or a sign-on bonus that covered it. And then he did the thing that surprised her: he named his deal breaker out loud. The twelve-month non-compete. If the role did not work out, twelve months would leave him unemployable in his own sector. If he had understood that clause five years ago, he would be in a very different position today — a past condition, a present result. He was committed to negotiating everything else in good faith — note the -ing, because to here is a preposition, not an infinitive. Everything was negotiable. One thing was not.

1. "If I had understood that clause five years ago, I would be in a very different position today." Por que NÃO é "I would have been"?
A Porque a condição é passada, mas o RESULTADO é agora — e um resultado presente pede would + verbo, não would have.
B Porque "understand" é um verbo irregular.
C Porque as duas formas são igualmente corretas aqui.
2. Por que "committed to negotiating" e não "committed to negotiate"?
A Porque o to de "committed to" é uma PREPOSIÇÃO — e depois de preposição o verbo sempre vai para -ing.
B Porque "negotiate" não tem forma de infinitivo.
C Porque "committed" exige sempre o gerúndio do verbo seguinte, mesmo sem preposição.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A I am considering to relocate to London.
B I am considering relocating to London.
C I am considering relocate to London.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Mixed Conditionals + Gerunds vs Infinitives

GRAMMAR

Como pesar a vida que você NÃO escolheu — e como dizer o que você está disposto a fazer (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Mixed: passado → presenteCondição no passado, resultado que você VIVE HOJE. O condicional mais útil de uma conversa de carreira.If I had taken that job, I would be in London now.
Mixed: presente → passadoQuem você É explica o que você FEZ. Mais raro, e soa muito sênior.If I were more risk-averse, I would have turned it down.
Verbo + GERÚNDIOconsider, avoid, risk, involve, keep, delay, suggest, mind.I am considering relocating.
Verbo + INFINITIVOdecide, agree, offer, refuse, manage, hope, intend, plan, afford.I have decided to negotiate.
Preposição + GERÚNDIO (sempre)Depois de QUALQUER preposição, o verbo vai para -ing. Sem exceção.committed to relocating / interested in moving
A armadilha de sentidostop doing = você parou de fazer. stop to do = você parou PARA fazer.I stopped negotiating. ≠ I stopped to negotiate.

Atenção (três erros de brasileiro): (1) o mixed conditional "travado" — se o resultado é AGORA, use would + verbo ("I would be in London now"), nunca would have been. (2) "I am considering to relocate" — depois de consider é sempre -ing. (3) O mais traiçoeiro: em "committed to", o to é PREPOSIÇÃO, não infinitivo — então é "committed to negotiating". Teste: se você puder trocar o verbo por um substantivo ("committed to the deal"), é preposição → use -ing.

Stage 2: Put the Negotiation in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma negociação de oferta na ordem correta. Dizer sim cedo demais é o erro que custa mais caro na carreira de um executivo.

?Say what you are considering and what you have decided — without saying yes.
?Ask THEM what the company expects you to push on. Then listen.
?Thank them once. Once, and no more.
?Name your deal breaker out loud, and justify it with a mixed conditional.
?Fix the practical conflicts: notice period, start date, the bonus you forfeit.
