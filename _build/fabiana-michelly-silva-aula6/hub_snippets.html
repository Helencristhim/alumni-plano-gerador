Making Your Case -- Persuasive Language in Negotiations

Cleft sentences for emphasis in supply chain negotiations. New vocabulary: proposal, viable, framework, threshold, commitment, concession, margin, clause, trade-off, counter-offer, stakeholder buy-in, leverage. Expressions: make a compelling case, reach common ground, sweeten the deal.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Proposal -- a formal plan presented for discussion
"I would like to put forward a proposal for the Q4 contract." (Proposta)
Viable -- realistic and capable of working successfully
"This deal is only viable if we commit to minimum volumes." (Viável)
Framework -- a basic structure or set of rules for an agreement
"We need a framework for the twelve-month contract." (Estrutura)
Threshold -- the minimum level that triggers an action
"If volumes fall below the threshold, the discount is void." (Limite mínimo)
Commitment -- a firm promise to do something
"We need a volume commitment before signing." (Compromisso)
Concession -- something you give up to reach an agreement
"We made a concession on price in exchange for faster delivery." (Concessão)
Margin -- the difference between cost and selling price
"Our margin is too thin to offer a bigger discount." (Margem)
Clause -- a specific condition in a contract
"The penalty clause protects us against late delivery." (Cláusula)
Trade-off -- gaining one thing means losing another
"The trade-off is lower price but longer lead time." (Troca)
Counter-offer -- a response to an offer with different terms
"They rejected our price but sent a counter-offer." (Contraproposta)
Stakeholder buy-in -- agreement and support from decision-makers
"We need stakeholder buy-in before finalizing." (Adesão dos interessados)
Leverage -- to use something to maximum advantage
"We can leverage our volume to negotiate better terms." (Alavancar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last week, Fabiana negotiated a new framework agreement with TechFlow Solutions. What she focused on was building a case around volume commitment rather than price alone. It was the freight concession that really got Klaus's attention, not the discount on unit price. The reason this matters is that it gives Bloomberg predictability for supply chain planning. The trade-off was accepting a longer lead time in exchange for a lower threshold. Fabiana also negotiated a revised penalty clause as a key concession. What she recommended next was preparing a formal proposal with exact volume numbers to secure stakeholder buy-in.

1. What structure does the text use to emphasize what Fabiana focused on?
A WH-cleft: "What she focused on was..."
B Passive: "It was focused on by her..."
C Modal perfect: "She should have focused on..."
2. What structure emphasizes the freight concession as the key factor?
A "The freight concession got their attention."
B IT-cleft: "It was the freight concession that got their attention."
C "The freight concession should have got their attention."
3. Why does the text use "The reason this matters is that..."?
A To express regret about the past.
B To emphasize WHY something is important -- giving a clear justification.
C To make a deduction about what happened.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences

GRAMMAR

Cleft sentences DIVIDEM a frase para dar ênfase. / Cleft sentences SPLIT the sentence to add emphasis.

Type / TipoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
IT-cleftIt is/was + [foco] + that/who...It is the margin that concerns us.
WH-cleftWhat + [oração] + is/was...What we need is stakeholder buy-in.
Negative IT-cleftIt is not X that..., it is YIt is not the price, it is the timeline.
Ponto-chave / Key pointCleft sentences são poderosas em negociações porque direcionam a atenção para o que REALMENTE importa.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

You want to emphasize that the timeline, not the price, is your main concern. You say:
A "The timeline is more important than the price."
B "It is not the price that concerns me, it is the timeline."
C "I should have focused on the timeline, not the price."
You want to tell your team what the next priority is. The most persuasive way is:
A "What we need to do next is prepare the formal proposal."
B "We need to prepare the formal proposal."
C "The proposal must have been prepared."
Your manager asks why you made a concession. The best structure is:
A "I made a concession because I wanted the deal."
B "The reason I made a concession is that the vendor's counter-offer included priority allocation."
C "I should have made the concession earlier."
