Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Cleft sentences for emphasis (It is X that... / What I need is...)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which single idea does each sentence push to the front?

"It is the price that worries us most."

"What we need is a firm commitment."

"It was the tariff that pushed our costs up."

"What convinced them was the longer term."

Grammar Rule

Cleft Sentences

StructureUse
It-cleft: It is/was + focus + that/who (it is the price that...)Emphasizes ONE piece of information above the rest
Wh-cleft: What + clause + is/was + focus (what we need is...)Highlights what matters most; frames your main point
Contrast: It is not X but Y that...Corrects a wrong assumption and redirects the focus
Limiting: All + clause + is... (all we are asking for is...)Makes a demand sound small and reasonable

Key idea: the it-cleft connector is that or who, never what. The wh-cleft starts with What and always takes a singular verb: is or was.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"It is the price what worries us most."
"It is the price that worries us most."

In an it-cleft, use that or who -- never what. To start with what, switch to a wh-cleft: What worries us most is the price.
