Chapter 3

The Code

Cleft sentences for emphasis and persuasion

Discovery

What Do These Have in Common?

"It is the volume commitment that makes this deal viable."

"What we need is a guaranteed minimum order per quarter."

"It is not the price that concerns me, it is the unpredictability."

"The reason I am pushing for this is that our planning depends on stable forecasts."

Grammar Rule

Cleft Sentences

TypeStructureExample
IT-cleftIt is/was + [focus] + that/who...It is the margin that concerns us.
WH-cleftWhat + [clause] + is/was...What we need is stakeholder buy-in.
Negative IT-cleftIt is not X that..., it is YIt is not the price, it is the timeline.
The reason... is thatThe reason + [clause] + is that...The reason we pushed back is that the clause was unfair.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What we need a volume commitment from both sides."
"What we need is a volume commitment from both sides."

WH-cleft sentences ALWAYS need is/was after the what-clause. Without it, the sentence is incomplete.

Spot the Error

Find the Mistake

0 / 4 errors found

"What we need a better framework."
"What we need IS a better framework." (WH-cleft needs IS)
"It is the margin what concerns me."
"It is the margin THAT concerns me." (IT-cleft uses THAT, not what)
"The reason we pushed back is because the clause was unfair."
"The reason we pushed back is THAT the clause was unfair." (reason...is THAT, not because)
"What surprised me it was the counter-offer."
"What surprised me WAS the counter-offer." (WH-cleft: what...WAS, no extra IT)
