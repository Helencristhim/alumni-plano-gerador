Advising Clients and Handling Tricky Requests

How to advise clients and handle difficult requests in tourism. Key words: alternative, to recommend, upgrade, flexible, trade-off, downside, to weigh up, peace of mind, tailor-made, worthwhile, to reconsider, second thoughts. Structure: the second conditional (if + past simple, would/could + verb) to give hypothetical advice.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Alternative -- another option you can choose instead (alternativa)
"Let me suggest a smart alternative."
To recommend -- to suggest something is a good choice (recomendar)
"I would recommend the inland hotel."
Upgrade -- a better room or service than the one booked (upgrade, melhoria)
"I could offer a free upgrade."
Flexible -- able to change or adapt easily (flexível)
"The tailor-made package is very flexible."
Trade-off -- a balance between two things you cannot both have fully (contrapartida)
"There is a trade-off: cheaper, but further away."
Downside -- the negative side of something (desvantagem)
"The downside is the longer transfer."
To weigh up -- to consider the good and bad points carefully (ponderar)
"Let us weigh up the two options."
Peace of mind -- a calm feeling from knowing nothing will go wrong (tranquilidade)
"The insurance gives you peace of mind."
Tailor-made -- specially designed for one client's needs (feito sob medida)
"We offer tailor-made itineraries."
Worthwhile -- good enough to be worth the time or money (que vale a pena)
"The extra cost is worthwhile."
To reconsider -- to think about a decision again (reconsiderar)
"You might want to reconsider the dates."
Second thoughts -- new doubts about a decision (dúvidas, hesitação)
"I am having second thoughts about August."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea's client, Stefan, has a tricky request: a luxury trip on a small budget. Andrea never simply says no. Instead, she offers a smart alternative. 'If I were you, I would travel in the low season,' she says. She explains the trade-off clearly: the inland hotel is cheaper, but the downside is that it is further from the beach. She helps Stefan weigh up the options and recommends a tailor-made package for peace of mind. In the end, Stefan agrees it is worthwhile -- and he trusts Andrea even more.

1. 'If I were you, I would travel in the low season' uses:
A the second conditional for hypothetical advice.
B the first conditional for a real plan.
C the past simple for a finished action.
2. In the second conditional, after 'I' we use:
A was.
B were (If I were you).
C am.
3. Which sentence gives polite, hypothetical advice?
A If you had more time, you could visit both cities.
B If you have more time, you will visit both cities.
C You must visit both cities.

GRAMMAR

FormUse / UsoExample
If + past simpleA condição imaginária. An unreal condition.If I were you,
would / could + verbO resultado imaginado ou conselho. The imagined result.I would recommend it.
