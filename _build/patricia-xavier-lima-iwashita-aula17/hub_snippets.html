Negotiating scope and deadlines and managing customer expectations. Key words: negotiate, trade-off, compromise, flexible, realistic, commit, scope creep, balance, agreement, reasonable. Structure: second conditional (if + past simple, would + base verb) and diplomatic modals (we could consider...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Negotiate -- to discuss something to reach an agreement (negociar)
"Let's negotiate a timeline that works for both sides."
Trade-off -- giving up one thing in order to gain another (troca, compensação)
"There's a trade-off between speed and quality."
Compromise -- a solution where each side gives something up (meio-termo, acordo)
"We reached a fair compromise on the scope."
Flexible -- willing to change or adjust your plan (flexível)
"We can be flexible on the timeline."
Realistic -- sensible and possible, not too ambitious (realista)
"Let's set a realistic deadline we can meet."
Commit -- to promise firmly to do something (comprometer-se)
"I can commit to two features, not three."
Scope creep -- extra work added beyond the original plan (aumento gradual do escopo)
"Saying yes to everything causes scope creep."
Balance -- to give fair weight to two different needs (equilibrar)
"We balance what the customer wants and what the team can do."
Agreement -- a decision that both sides accept (acordo)
"We reached an agreement on the next steps."
Reasonable -- fair and sensible, not too much to ask (razoável)
"That sounds like a reasonable request."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

On the call, David asked Patrícia to commit to three new features before next quarter. Instead of saying no, she negotiated. "If we focused on the most important one first, we would deliver value faster," she said. She explained the trade-off: "If we added all three at once, the timeline would slip." To stay flexible, she offered a phased plan: "We could consider a phased rollout." David agreed, and they set a realistic deadline. "If you gave us two extra weeks, we would commit to all three." It was a fair agreement — and it protected the team from scope creep.

1. Por que Patrícia diz "If we focused on one first, we would deliver faster"?
A Porque ela já entregou tudo.
B Porque imagina um resultado (segunda condicional) e oferece um trade-off.
C Porque está falando de uma rotina diária.
2. Na frase "If we added all three at once, the timeline would slip", a estrutura é:
A if + passado simples, would + verbo base.
B if + would, will + verbo.
C if + present perfect, can + verbo.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If we would extend it, we would add the feature."
B "If we extended it, we would add the feature."
C "If we extend it, we would added the feature."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como imaginar um acordo que ainda não é real e oferecer opções de forma diplomática (explicação em inglês e português).

StructureUse / UsoExample
If + past simple, would + base verbImagina um acordo não real ainda. Imagines a deal not real yet.If we extended it, we would add the feature.
We could consider + noun/-ingOferece uma opção gentilmente. Offers an option gently.We could consider a phased rollout.
We would need + nounColoca uma condição justa. States a fair condition.We would need more time for that.
Would it be possible to + verbo?Pergunta com diplomacia. Asks diplomatically.Would it be possible to start with one?
Avoid / EviteNão use "if I would" na cláusula if → troque por "if I had / if we extended" (passado simples).
