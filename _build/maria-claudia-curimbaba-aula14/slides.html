Chapter 3 -- The Superlative Code

Language Discovery

Superlatives + trend language

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words do? Which ones rank value, and which ones describe movement?

"She is the most sought-after artist of the season."

"It reached the highest price of the year."

"Prices are rising and the market is booming."

"Demand has grown and values have soared."

The Rule

Superlatives & Trends

FunctionPatternExample
Short superlativethe + adjective + -est"the highest price", "the fastest-growing segment"
Long superlativethe most + adjective"the most valuable lot", "the most sought-after artist"
Trend nowis/are + verb-ing"prices are rising", "the market is booming"
Trend up to nowhas/have + past participle"demand has grown", "values have soared"

Key idea: use -est for short adjectives (high, fast, low) and the most for longer ones (valuable, expensive, sought-after). Irregular: good → the best, bad → the worst. To describe a market in motion, use the present continuous (are rising) for now and the present perfect (have soared) for the change up to now.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"It is the most expensivest lot in the sale."
"It is the most expensive lot in the sale."

Use the most OR -est, never both. Long adjectives take the most: "the most valuable". Short ones take -est: "the highest price".
