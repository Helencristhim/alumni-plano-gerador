Aula 14 -- Pre-class

The Art Market -- Trends, Artists & Value

Talk about the art market like a collector: read the trends, compare artists and value, and report a sale. Use superlatives (the most sought-after artist, the highest price ever paid) and trend verbs (prices have soared, the market is booming) to describe the market, and report results in the passive voice (the painting was sold for..., the work has been appraised at...). New vocabulary: auction, bid, lot, appraisal, valuation, provenance, authenticate, masterpiece, sought-after, emerging, blue-chip, speculative.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Auction -- a public sale where a work goes to the highest offer
"The painting will come up at the evening auction." (leilão)
Bid -- an offer of a price for a work at auction
"Set your top bid before you walk into the room." (lance / dar um lance)
Lot -- a single numbered item, or set, offered for sale
"Lot forty-seven is the painting I would watch." (lote)
Appraisal -- a professional opinion of how much a work is worth
"The appraisal came in at three hundred thousand dollars." (avaliação / laudo)
Valuation -- the assessed monetary worth of a work
"A smart purchase is one where the valuation supports the price." (valor estimado)
Provenance -- the documented record of every owner a work has had
"The provenance is clean, so there are no questions about its history." (procedência)
Authenticate -- to prove a work is genuine and by the named artist
"The work has been authenticated by the artist's estate." (autenticar)
Masterpiece -- an outstanding work, often an artist's very best
"The masterpiece in lot twelve is the most expensive piece of the night." (obra-prima)
Sought-after -- in high demand because many collectors want it
"She is one of the most sought-after artists on the market." (cobiçado / muito procurado)
Emerging -- new and starting to gain recognition (an emerging artist)
"It is an early painting by an emerging artist." (emergente)
Blue-chip -- established, safe and high in value, with a strong track record
"This is a strong, blue-chip name in the making." (consagrado / de primeira linha)
Speculative -- risky -- bought hoping the value will rise, with no guarantee
"An emerging artist is more speculative than a blue-chip one." (especulativo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tonight I am at the evening auction, watching lot forty-seven. The artist is emerging, but she is already one of the most sought-after names in the room. Prices for her work have soared, and contemporary art is the fastest-growing segment on the market -- it is booming. Before I bid, I check the provenance: the work has been authenticated and it was acquired from the studio, so the history is clean. The appraisal, or valuation, is around three hundred thousand dollars, and a similar piece was sold last month for almost double. The name is still a little speculative, not blue-chip yet, so I set my top bid in advance and I will not chase it. The most expensive masterpiece in the room is not always the best buy.

1. How does the writer rank the artist?
A "one of the most sought-after names" -- a superlative.
B "a quite wanted artist."
C "the most wantedest name."
2. Which phrase describes the movement of prices?
A "prices are sold" -- a passive.
B "prices have soared" -- a trend verb.
C "prices are the cheapest."
3. How is the sale reported -- "a similar piece ___ last month"?
A "was sold" -- passive: was + past participle.
B "sold" -- with no "be".
C "is selling".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Superlatives, Trend Verbs & the Passive

GRAMMAR

Para comparar e ranquear, use superlativos: adjetivo curto = THE + adjetivo + -EST ("the highest price"); adjetivo longo = THE MOST + adjetivo ("the most sought-after artist"). Para descrever o movimento do mercado, use verbos de tendência: soar / climb / rise / boom (subir). Para relatar um resultado, use a voz passiva (be / was / have been + partícipio): "the painting was sold", "the work has been appraised". / Use superlatives to rank, trend verbs to describe movement, and the passive to report a result.

FunctionFormExample
Superlative (short)the + adj + -est"the highest price ever paid"
Superlative (long)the most + adjective"the most sought-after artist"
Trend verbsoar / climb / rise / boom"prices have soared"
Report a resultbe / was / have been + participle"the painting was sold"

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca junte os dois superlativos ("the most highest" está errado -- é "the highest"). E a passiva SEMPRE precisa do BE: "the work WAS appraised", "the painting HAS BEEN sold", nunca "the work appraised". / Never double the superlative, and the passive always needs "be" (was / were / been).
