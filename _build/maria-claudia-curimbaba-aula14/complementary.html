Aula 14 -- The Art Market

Tema da aula: talking about the art market like a collector -- trends, artists and value -- with superlatives (the most sought-after artist, the highest price ever paid), trend verbs (prices have soared, the market is booming) and the passive voice for results (the painting was sold for...). Reforço fora de aula -- marque como concluído ao terminar.

Documentary
"The Price of Everything" (HBO) -- inside the art market

A sharp look at how value is created in contemporary art: auctions, dealers, emerging artists and blue-chip names. Listen for how experts rank works with superlatives ("one of the most important...") and report sales in the passive ("the work was sold for...", "the piece has been acquired by..."). Exactly the language of this lesson.

Dica: assista a uma cena de leilão com legenda em inglês e anote dois superlativos e duas frases na voz passiva sobre uma venda (a work was sold for..., a piece was acquired by...).

Podcast
The Art Newspaper / Artnet -- art-market podcasts

Weekly conversations on market trends: which segments are booming, which artists are the most sought-after, how prices have soared or cooled, and how record sales are reported. Perfect input to review this lesson's trend verbs and superlatives in real, fast English.

Dica: ouça um episódio e, ao final, descreva em voz alta uma tendência do mercado com um verbo de tendência (prices have soared) e relate uma venda na voz passiva (a painting was sold for...).

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- Superlatives & the Passive Voice

Short, clear guides to two grammar points from this lesson: how to form superlatives (the + -est vs the most + adjective) and how to use the passive voice (be + past participle) to report results -- the exact grammar you need to fix your recurring habit of dropping "be".

Dica: pause após cada exemplo e diga a mesma ideia sobre uma obra de arte -- um superlativo (the most sought-after) e uma frase na voz passiva sobre uma venda (the painting was sold).
