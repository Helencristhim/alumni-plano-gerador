Chapter 4: The Code

Who Arranged It

We had the survey designed by a specialist · I am having the baseline taken first

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

We had the questionnaire designed by an external specialist.

I am having the focus groups facilitated in-house, because those conversations should stay in this building.

Our goal is to have the baseline completed before we change anything.

I had Marc review the questions before they went out.

In none of these sentences does the subject perform the action. In all of them, the subject is the person the room will hold accountable for it.

StructureFormWhat it does
Causative passivehave + object + past participle (+ by X)You arranged it; someone else performed it. “We had the survey designed by a specialist.”
Causative activehave + person + base verb (no “to”)You name who you asked. “I had Marc review the questions.” — never “had Marc to review”.
The deadline patternto have + X + completed by + date“Our goal is to have the gap analysis completed by the end of September.”
get instead of haveSame structure, less formal, and it hints at effort: “I finally got the 2024 data re-coded.” Use have with the board; get with your team.
Why it sounds seniorThe plain passive (“the survey was designed by a consultant”) tells the room who typed it, and quietly deletes you. The causative tells them who decided it — and in a diagnostic, the room is not asking who typed it.

Pair it with a hedge and it becomes a plan the board can approve: “The data would suggest that we should have the baseline taken before September, rather than after.”

Register

Disappear, or Take Command

Plain passive — you disappear

Use it when the doer is irrelevant, or when naming them would hurt someone.

“The questionnaire was designed by an external specialist.”

Causative — you command

Use it when the room needs to know who decided, who owns it, and who answers for it.

“We had the questionnaire designed by an external specialist.”

Two words apart, and a career apart. In the first sentence a consultant did some work. In the second, you ran a diagnostic and chose your instrument — and that is the sentence a VP repeats to the board when you are not in the room.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We had designed the questionnaire by an external specialist."
"We had the questionnaire designed by an external specialist."
"I had Marc to review the questions before they went out."
"I had Marc review the questions before they went out."

Both errors are Portuguese arriving on time. In the causative passive the participle comes after the object: had + the survey + designed. And after have + a person, the verb is bare — review, never "to review".

The Kickoff

M
Before you show us the plan, one question. We answered a survey like this two years ago and nobody ever told us what came out of it. Why is yours different?
D
Because that question is phase four of my plan, not an afterthought. The findings are read back to this faculty before the board sees them, and I present them myself. That is in writing, and it goes out before the first question does.
M
Fine. And who wrote the questionnaire? If head office in Brazil wrote it, this faculty will read that in the first line.
D
Nobody in Brazil wrote it. We had the questionnaire designed by an external specialist here, and I had it reviewed by two people in this building before it went anywhere. The instrument is theirs. The decision is mine, and so is the answer if it fails.
M
And if only a third of us answer? Because that is what happened last time, and I suspect you already know it.
D
Then I report it. The data would suggest that a response rate of thirty percent is not a failed survey — it is a trust index with no survey attached to it, and it would be my first finding, not my footnote. I am not promising you a number. I am promising you what I do with it.
