Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The Past Simple of irregular verbs

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the blue words have in common? Do they end in -ed?

"Last weekend I ate feijoada at a Brazilian restaurant."

"We drank fresh juice with our meal."

"My grandmother made a delicious dessert."

"Yesterday I had lunch with my whole family."

Grammar Rule

Special Forms

Base verbPast Simple (irregular)Example
eatateI ate a delicious dish.
drinkdrankWe drank fresh juice.
havehadI had lunch at home.
makemadeShe made a dessert.
gowentWe went to a restaurant.
givegaveHe gave me the recipe.

Key idea: irregular verbs do not take -ed. They have a special past form you must learn. The signal words are the same: yesterday, last night, last weekend, two days ago.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Yesterday I eated a spicy dish."
"Yesterday I ate a spicy dish."

Irregular verbs like eat, drink, have and make do not add -ed. They change form: eat becomes ate, drink becomes drank. You must memorize each one.
