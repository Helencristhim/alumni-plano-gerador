Lesson 3 -- When Things Go Wrong

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 3, Episode 23: "Beach Games"

Watch how managers handle an unexpected crisis and shifting accountability. Notice how characters reflect on what they "should have" or "could have" done differently when a plan falls apart.

Write down every modal perfect you hear ("should have", "could have", "must have"). Pause and repeat each one aloud, then create your own version about a supply chain delay.

Podcast
Supply Chain Now -- Episode: "Managing Disruptions and Building Resilience"

Industry experts discuss how to respond when a supplier fails: identifying the root cause, setting up a workaround, and building contingency plans. Listen for terms like bottleneck, backlog, downtime, and mitigate in real professional contexts.

Note 3 strategies the speakers use to mitigate a disruption. Compare them with how Fabiana handled the TechFlow delay in today's lesson.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Handle a Crisis as a Leader"

A short video on communicating clearly under pressure, escalating issues, and taking accountability. Directly applicable to managing supplier delays and explaining setbacks to senior management.

After watching, record a 60-second escalation message about a delayed shipment. Use at least three new words and two modal perfects.
