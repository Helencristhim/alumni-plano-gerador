Lesson 3 -- When Things Go Wrong

Series
Industry -- Season 1, Episode 3: "Notting Hill"

A trading floor crisis forces the team to handle escalations under pressure. Watch how professionals communicate bad news, assign blame, and propose solutions in a high-stakes financial environment.

Pay attention to modal perfects: "We should have caught this", "They must have known". Write down 3 examples you hear.

Podcast
Supply Chain Now -- "Lessons from the Global Chip Shortage"

Industry experts discuss the biggest supply chain disruption of the decade. Listen for crisis vocabulary: bottleneck, backlog, contingency, and how they describe what should have been done differently.

Listen at 1x speed. Note every time a speaker uses "should have", "could have", or "would have". What solutions do they propose?

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Communicate Bad News at Work"

A 9-minute video on the best strategies for delivering bad news professionally. Directly applicable to escalation calls and crisis communication with stakeholders.

After watching, practice explaining a fictional supply chain delay to a client using the strategies from the video and the modal perfects from today's lesson.
