Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Escalation -- raising an issue to a higher level of authority
"The escalation reached the VP within hours." (Escalonamento)
Bottleneck -- a narrow point that slows down the entire process
"The bottleneck is in the documentation process." (Gargalo)
Backlog -- an accumulation of unfinished work or orders
"We have a backlog of thirty units at the port." (Acumulado)
Disruption -- a major interruption to the normal flow of operations
"The disruption affected all APAC deliveries." (Ruptura)
Contingency -- a backup plan in case the main plan fails
"We need a contingency plan for this route." (Contingência)
Expedite -- to speed up a process or make it happen faster
"Can we expedite the customs clearance?" (Agilizar)
Deadline -- the final date by which something must be completed
"The deadline for clearance is Thursday." (Prazo final)
Inventory -- the stock of goods available for sale or distribution
"Inventory is running low at the Tokyo office." (Estoque)
Penalty -- a financial punishment for breaking rules or missing deadlines
"We will face a penalty from the client." (Penalidade)
Oversight -- a mistake caused by not paying enough attention
"The oversight in compliance caused the delay." (Descuido)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last week, a major disruption hit the APAC terminal shipment. The carrier should have confirmed the customs clearance on Friday, but they did not. As a result, a backlog of thirty units is now sitting at the port. The bottleneck was caused by an oversight in the documentation process — the logistics team must have missed the updated compliance requirements. Fabiana believes the escalation might have been avoided if they had implemented a contingency plan earlier. Now the team is working to expedite the process before the deadline on Thursday, or they will face a penalty from the client. The inventory at the Tokyo office is running critically low.

1. What structure does the text use to criticize the carrier for NOT confirming?
A "should have confirmed" -- the right thing that did NOT happen
B "must have confirmed" -- a strong deduction
C "might have confirmed" -- a possibility
2. Why does the text say "must have missed" about the logistics team?
A Because they are criticizing the team.
B Because they are making a strong deduction about what probably happened.
C Because they are not sure at all.
3. What does "might have been avoided" express?
A Certainty that it was avoided.
B Criticism of someone who did not avoid it.
C A possibility -- maybe it could have been avoided, maybe not.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Perfects

GRAMMAR

Modal + have + past participle = falar sobre o passado com diferentes significados. / Modal + have + past participle = talk about the past with different meanings.

Modal PerfectMeaning / SignificadoExample / Exemplo
should have + ppO certo que NÃO aconteceu (crítica/arrependimento) / The right thing that did NOT happenWe should have flagged this earlier.
must have + ppDedução forte sobre o passado / Strong deduction about the pastThey must have missed the deadline.
might have + ppPossibilidade (talvez tenha acontecido) / Possibility about the pastThe disruption might have been avoided.
Ponto-chave / Key pointTodos usam modal + HAVE + past participle. A diferença está no SIGNIFICADO: should = crítica, must = certeza, might = possibilidade.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

A shipment is two weeks late and you are almost certain the carrier missed the customs forms. You say:
A "The carrier should have missed the customs forms."
B "The carrier must have missed the customs forms."
C "The carrier will miss the customs forms."
Your manager asks why the team did not activate the contingency plan. You express criticism:
A "We should have activated the contingency plan immediately."
B "We must have activated the contingency plan immediately."
C "We might have activated the contingency plan immediately."
You are not sure, but you think the penalty clause may have been waived. You say:
A "The penalty must have been waived."
