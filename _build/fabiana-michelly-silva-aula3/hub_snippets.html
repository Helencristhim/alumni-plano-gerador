Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Escalation -- raising a problem to someone with more authority
"This delay needs escalation to senior management." (Escalonamento)
Bottleneck -- a point in a process where flow slows down
"The bottleneck at the plant slowed every order." (Gargalo)
Backlog -- a build-up of unfinished orders or tasks
"The outage created a backlog of orders." (Pedidos acumulados)
Disruption -- a serious interruption to normal operations
"The strike caused a major disruption." (Interrupção)
Contingency -- a backup plan for when something goes wrong
"Our contingency plan reroutes orders through Singapore." (Contingência)
Delay -- a situation where something happens later than planned
"The shipment will face a ten-day delay." (Atraso)
Setback -- a problem that delays or reverses your progress
"Losing the carrier was a serious setback." (Revés)
Root cause -- the fundamental reason behind a problem
"The root cause of the delay was a power outage." (Causa raiz)
Workaround -- a temporary solution that goes around a problem
"We found a workaround by rerouting the order." (Solução alternativa)
Accountability -- being responsible and answerable for results
"We need accountability from the supplier." (Responsabilização)
Downtime -- a period when a system or operation is not working
"The power outage caused hours of downtime." (Tempo de inatividade)
Shortfall -- an amount that is less than what was needed
"The delay created a shortfall in our supply." (Déficit)
Mitigate -- to make a problem or its impact less severe
"We acted fast to mitigate the disruption." (Mitigar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last week, a power outage at TechFlow's Munich plant caused several hours of downtime. The result was a serious bottleneck and a backlog of two hundred orders. Bloomberg's APAC shipment now faces a ten-day delay. Reviewing the incident, Fabiana said: "The supplier should have warned us sooner, and we could have prepared a contingency plan." She added: "If we had a backup vendor, we would have avoided this shortfall." The root cause report confirmed her suspicion -- the plant must have ignored an early warning. To mitigate the disruption, the team approved a workaround through Singapore and demanded full accountability from the supplier for the setback.

1. Which sentence expresses criticism about something that did NOT happen in the past?
A "The supplier warns us sooner."
B "The supplier should have warned us sooner."
C "The supplier will warn us sooner."
2. What does "must have ignored" express in the text?
A An obligation for the future.
B A logical deduction -- she is almost certain it happened.
C A polite request.
3. What is the "root cause" mentioned in the text?
A A power outage that caused downtime at the Munich plant.
B A late invoice from the carrier.
C A change in the purchase order.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Perfects

GRAMMAR

Usados para analisar, criticar ou deduzir sobre o passado. / Used to analyze, criticize, or deduce about the past.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
should have + p.p.Crítica ou arrependimento / Criticism or regretWe should have escalated the issue sooner.
could have + p.p.Possibilidade não realizada / Missed possibilityThey could have prevented the downtime.
would have + p.p.Resultado imaginado / Imagined past resultA backup plan would have avoided the shortfall.
must have + p.p.Dedução lógica / Logical deductionThe carrier must have lost the documents.
Key point / Ponto-chaveA estrutura nunca muda: modal + have + past participle. Nunca use "of" no lugar de "have".
