Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Modal Perfects -- analyzing past actions and mistakes

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What pattern do the orange words follow?

"We should have ordered the parts earlier."

"They could have warned us about the bottleneck."

"The supplier must have missed the deadline."

"A backup plan would have prevented the shortfall."

Grammar Rule

Modal Perfects

Modal + have + past participleMeaning
should have doneCriticism or regret -- it was a good idea but did NOT happen
could have doneA past possibility or missed opportunity -- it was possible but did NOT happen
would have doneAn imagined past result -- what the outcome would be in a different past
must have doneA logical deduction -- you are almost certain it happened

Structure: The form never changes -- modal + have + past participle. Only the modal changes the meaning. We use modal perfects to analyze, criticize, or deduce things about the past.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We should of escalated the issue much sooner."
"We should have escalated the issue much sooner."

After a modal, always use have + past participle, never "of." And remember: the second verb is a past participle, not the base form.
