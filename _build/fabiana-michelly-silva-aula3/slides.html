Chapter 3

The Code

Modal perfects: should have, must have, might have

Discovery

What Do These Have in Common?

"The carrier should have confirmed the clearance last Friday."

"The logistics team must have missed the updated requirements."

"The disruption might have been avoided with a contingency plan."

"We should have flagged this escalation earlier."

Grammar Rule

Modal Perfects

Modal PerfectMeaningExample
should have + ppThe right thing (but it did NOT happen)We should have flagged this earlier.
must have + ppAlmost certain deduction about the pastThey must have missed the deadline.
might have + ppPossibility (maybe it happened)The disruption might have been avoided.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He must have confirmed the clearance." (used for obligation)
"He should have confirmed the clearance." (criticism -- he did NOT do it)

must have = deduction ("I'm almost sure he missed it"). should have = criticism/regret ("It was the right thing but he didn't do it").

Artifact

Incident Report

INCIDENT REPORT -- APAC Terminal Shipment

Priority: HIGH -- Shipment Delay and Compliance Failure

Summary: A disruption in the APAC terminal shipment has created a backlog of 30 units at the Singapore port. The bottleneck was caused by an oversight in the compliance documentation process.

Root cause: The carrier submitted outdated customs forms. Our logistics team should have verified the forms before submission. The updated compliance requirements must have been missed during the handover.

Impact: Deadline for Tokyo delivery missed. Inventory running low. Penalty clause activated (waived after transparent communication).

Actions taken:

  1. Expedited customs clearance -- expected resolution by Friday
  2. Initiated escalation to VP of Supply Chain
  3. Activated contingency plan with backup vendor
  4. Recommendation: phase out manual tracking, implement automated alerts

Filed by: Marcus Tan, Supply Chain Manager -- Bloomberg Singapore
