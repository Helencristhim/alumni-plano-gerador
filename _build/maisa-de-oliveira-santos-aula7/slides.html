Chapter 3: The Code

On, At, In

prepositions of time

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Are you free on Monday? Let's meet on Tuesday."

"Can we meet at three PM? The call is at noon."

"I have a meeting in the morning and one in the afternoon."

"Let's meet on Tuesday, at three, in the afternoon."

PrepositionUse forExample
ondayson Monday, on Friday
atclock timesat three PM, at noon
inparts of the dayin the morning, in the afternoon

on a day · at a clock time · in a part of the day. To invite: "Are you free...?", "Can we meet...?", "Let's...".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Are you free in Monday?
Are you free on Monday?
Can we meet on three PM?
Can we meet at three PM?

Days take on (on Monday). Clock times take at (at three PM). Parts of the day take in (in the morning).

Dialogue

Can We Meet?

D
Hi Maísa. Can we schedule a meeting this week?
M
Sure! Are you free on Monday?
D
Monday is difficult. I am not available in the morning.
M
No problem. Can we meet on Tuesday?
D
Tuesday is good. What time?
M
Can we meet at three PM, in the afternoon?
D
Three PM is perfect. Let's confirm Tuesday at three.
M
Great. I will book the meeting room and send you an invite.
D
Thank you. Please confirm by email.
M
Of course. See you on Tuesday!
