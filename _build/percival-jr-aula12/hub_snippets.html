Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Interrupt -- to start talking before someone finishes
"Sorry to interrupt, but we are short on time." (Interromper)
Briefly -- in a short way, using little time
"Could you explain it briefly?" (Brevemente)
Off topic -- not about the agenda or the current topic
"That is off topic for now." (Fora do assunto)
Raise a point -- to bring up something to discuss
"May I raise a point about the schedule?" (Levantar uma questão)
Go ahead -- to give permission to start or continue
"Please go ahead, but keep it brief." (Pode falar / siga em frente)
Get back to -- to return to a topic or point later
"Let's get back to that after the report." (Voltar a)
Move on -- to go to the next topic or point
"Let's move on to the supplier decision." (Seguir adiante)
On track -- going in the right direction, as planned
"Let's keep the meeting on track." (No rumo certo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

The meeting is running long. A colleague starts talking about the office chairs. Percival has to interrupt politely: "Sorry to interrupt, but that is off topic for now. Let's get back to the safety report." Karen asks to raise a point about the deadline. Percival says, "Please go ahead, but keep it brief." She makes her point in one sentence. Then Percival says, "Let's move on to the supplier decision," so the team stays on track and finishes on time.

3. How does the team finish on time?
A Percival moves on and keeps the meeting on track.
B They cancel the supplier decision.
C They talk about the chairs for an hour.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Interrupting & Refocusing

GRAMMAR

Para manter a reunião no rumo certo você precisa de quatro funções: INTERROMPER com educação, DAR permissão, TRAZER de volta ao tema e SEGUIR adiante. / To keep a meeting on track you need four functions: to INTERRUPT politely, to GIVE permission, to REFOCUS, and to MOVE ON.

Função / FunctionPatternExample
Interromper / InterruptSorry to interrupt, but ... / Could I just add ...?Sorry to interrupt, but we are short on time.
Dar permissão / PermitPlease go ahead. / Go ahead, but keep it brief.Please go ahead, but keep it brief.
Trazer de volta / RefocusLet's get back to ... / That is off topic for now.Let's get back to the safety report.
Seguir adiante / Move onLet's move on to ... / Let's keep on track.Let's move on to the next point.

Key point / Ponto-chave: comece com Sorry to interrupt, but para interromper com educação, use Let's get back to para refocar, e Let's move on to para seguir. Depois de Let's usamos sempre o verbo base (Let's move on, nunca "Let's to move on"). / Start with Sorry to interrupt, but to interrupt politely, use Let's get back to to refocus, and Let's move on to to go forward. After Let's, always use the base verb.
