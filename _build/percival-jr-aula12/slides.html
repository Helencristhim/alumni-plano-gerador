Chapter 3 -- Interrupting Politely

Interrupt, Refocus, Move On

The language of a focused meeting

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Is each sentence interrupting, giving permission, refocusing, or moving on?

"Sorry to interrupt, but we are short on time."

"Please go ahead, but keep it brief."

"Let's get back to the safety report."

"Let's move on to the next point."

Grammar Rule

Interrupt, Refocus & Move On

Use it forPatternExample
Interrupt politelySorry to interrupt, but ... / Could I just add ...?Sorry to interrupt, but we are short on time.
Give permissionPlease go ahead. / Go ahead, but keep it brief.Please go ahead, but keep it brief.
Refocus the meetingLet's get back to ... / That is off topic for now.Let's get back to the safety report.
Move onLet's move on to ... / Let's keep on track.Let's move on to the next point.

Key idea: start with Sorry to interrupt, but to interrupt politely, use Let's get back to to refocus, and Let's move on to to go forward. After Let's we always use the base verb (Let's move on, not "Let's to move on").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Let's to move on to the next point."
"Let's move on to the next point."

After Let's use the base verb: Let's move on, Let's get back to, never "Let's to move on".

MEETING GROUND RULES -- STAY ON TRACK

EGEA Saneamento -- Risk Team -- Chair: Percival JR

#Rule
1Stay on the agenda. If a point is off topic, we get back to it later.
2To raise a point, ask the chair: "May I raise a point?" The chair will say "Go ahead."
3Keep each point brief. We have a time limit for every topic.
4When a topic is done, the chair says "Let's move on" and we finish on time.
