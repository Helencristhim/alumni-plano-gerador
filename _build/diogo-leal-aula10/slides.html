Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one asks? Which one trades? Which one promises?

"Could we move the go-live to July 5th?"

"Would it be possible to add one week?"

"If we cut the reports, we can keep June 20th."

"I can commit to July 5th."

Grammar Rule

Ask, Trade, Commit

MoveStructureExample
Ask without pushingCould we / Would it be possible to + verbCould we move the go-live to July 5th?
Offer the tradeIf + present, can / will + verbIf we cut the reports, we can keep June 20th.
Make the promiseI can commit to + dateI can commit to July 5th.

The trade is the whole game. "I need more time" is a problem for your stakeholder. "More time, or less scope -- you choose" is a decision. Give two options, and let them pick.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we will cut the reports, we keep the date."
"If we cut the reports, we will keep the date."
"I need that you give me two more weeks."
"I need you to give me two more weeks."

After if, use the present -- the future goes in the other half. And it is I need you to + verb, never "I need that you".
