Never ask for time -- offer a trade: two options, one trade-off, one commitment. New vocabulary: extension, trade-off, buffer, realistic, push back, commit, bandwidth, reasonable.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Extension -- extra time that someone officially gives you
"I am asking for a two-week extension." (Prorrogação)
Trade-off -- you give one thing to get another
"The trade-off is time for scope." (Troca / concessão)
Buffer -- extra time in the plan, kept for safety
"I want a three-day buffer before the go-live." (Folga / margem)
Realistic -- possible in the real world, not just a hope
"July 5th is a realistic date." (Realista)
Push back -- to politely say no, or ask to change a date
"I have to push back on June 20th." (Contestar / negociar)
Commit -- to promise firmly, and take responsibility
"I can commit to July 5th." (Comprometer-se)
Bandwidth -- the time and people a team really has
"The team does not have the bandwidth." (Capacidade / disponibilidade)
Reasonable -- fair and sensible; easy to accept
"That sounds reasonable to me." (Razoável)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo does not say "I need more time". That is a problem, and problems are not gifts. He says: "June 20th is not realistic anymore. The team does not have the bandwidth for the three reports." Then he offers a choice. "Could we move the go-live to July 5th? That is Option A, with the full scope. Or Option B: if we cut the reports, we can keep June 20th." He names the trade-off out loud: time for scope. Sophia does not give him the two-week extension -- she gives one week, so he has to push back on the reports. That sounds reasonable to her. Diogo closes like a professional: "I can commit to June 27th, with a three-day buffer." He asked for nothing. He traded.

1. Why does Diogo not simply say "I need more time"?
A Because that gives the stakeholder a problem, not a choice.
B Because it is impolite to speak to a manager.
C Because the project is finished.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A If we cut the reports, we can keep June 20th.
B If we will cut the reports, we can keep June 20th.
C If we cutting the reports, we keep June 20th.
3. How does Diogo close the negotiation?
A He commits to a date, with a buffer.
B He says he will try his best.
C He asks Sophia to decide everything alone.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Ask, Trade, Commit

GRAMMAR

Pedir sem impor, oferecer a troca e assumir o compromisso. / Asking politely, offering the trade, and committing.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Pedir / Ask politelyCould we / Would it be possible to + verboCould we move the go-live to July 5th?
Trocar / Offer the tradeIf + presente, can / will + verboIf we cut the reports, we can keep June 20th.
Comprometer-se / CommitI can commit to + dataI can commit to July 5th.
Ponto-chave / Key pointDepois de if, use o PRESENTE (nunca "if we will cut"). E é I need you to + verbo, nunca "I need that you". Regra de ouro: nunca entregue um problema -- entregue duas opções.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

You need two more weeks. The strongest opening is:
A "June 20th is not realistic anymore. I have two options for you."
B "I need more time. Sorry."
C "It is impossible. Nobody can do this."
Your stakeholder cannot move the date. You say:
A "If we cut the three reports, we can keep June 20th."
B "Then the project will fail."
C "OK, we will deliver everything on June 20th." (and you know you cannot)
She accepts your option. How do you close?
A "I can commit to July 5th, with a three-day buffer. I will send the plan today."
B "I will try my best."
C "Let us see what happens."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real deadline at Oracle that you cannot meet. Ask your stakeholder for time, out loud, for two minutes: (1) the date is not realistic, and why, (2) Option A and Option B, (3) the trade-off, (4) your commitment and a date. Record it.
Now she refuses: "The date is in the contract. I cannot move it." Answer out loud. Do not accept an impossible date, and do not say no -- find the trade. Record it.
