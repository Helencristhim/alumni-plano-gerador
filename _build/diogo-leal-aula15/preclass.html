Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Query -- a question from a person in the audience
"That is a good query about the timeline." (Pergunta / dúvida)
Response -- what you say back when someone asks a question
"Give a short, calm response and move on." (Resposta)
Defer -- to answer a question later instead of right now
"Let me defer that and come back to it after the meeting." (Adiar)
Recall -- to bring the main message back to mind in a few words
"To recall the main message: revenue is up." (Relembrar)
Fair -- reasonable and right; a fair point is a good, valid point
"That is a fair point about the timeline." (Justo / razoável)
Appreciate -- to be thankful for something, like a question or the time
"I appreciate your time and your sharp questions." (Agradecer / valorizar)
Brief -- short and to the point; a brief summary is a short one
"I will send a brief summary by email." (Breve / curto)
Assumption -- something you think is true without checking it first
"That question is based on a wrong assumption." (Suposição)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After his last slide, Diogo invites questions: "Are there any questions before I close?" A colleague asks a query about the timeline. It is a fair point, so Diogo gives a short, calm response: the new tool saves two weeks. Then someone asks about the exact budget. He does not have the detail now, so he does not guess. Instead, he defers it: "Let me come back to that after the meeting." If a question is based on a wrong assumption, he corrects it calmly and moves on. To close, he recalls the main message in one sentence: the project is on track. He says, "I appreciate your time", and gives a clear next step: "I will send a brief summary by email." A strong closing is short.

1. What does Diogo say to invite questions after his last slide?
A "Are there any questions before I close?"
B "Who has a doubt?"
C Nothing -- he just stops.
2. What does Diogo do when he does not have the detail right now?
A He defers it: "Let me come back to that after the meeting."
B He guesses an answer quickly.
C He ignores the question.
3. How does Diogo close the talk?
A He recalls the message, thanks the audience, and gives a next step.
B He reads every slide one more time.
C He leaves without a word.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Q&A & Closing Phrases

GRAMMAR

Frases para conduzir o Q&A e fechar a apresentação. / Phrases to handle questions and close the talk.

Moment / MomentoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Invite / ConvidarPedir perguntas / Ask for questionsAre there any questions?
Answer / ResponderResponder com gentileza / Answer politelyThat is a fair point. The short answer is...
Defer / AdiarResponder depois / Answer laterLet me come back to that after the meeting.
Close / FecharEncerrar a apresentação / End the talkI appreciate your time. The next step is...
Key point / Ponto-chavePrimeiro convide, depois responda cada pergunta com calma. Se não souber agora, adie com gentileza em vez de adivinhar. No fim, relembre a mensagem, agradeça e dê um próximo passo claro. Um bom fechamento é breve.
