The Nudge -- Chasing a Colleague Who Owes You Work

Chase a colleague who does not report to you: soft opener, the block, the smallest ask, a date, an offer of help. New vocabulary: nudge, peer, rely on, drop the ball, gentle, unblock, owe, check in.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Nudge -- a small, polite push to make someone move
"Just a quick nudge on the API document." (Cutucada / lembrete leve)
Peer -- a colleague at your level, with no authority over you
"She is a peer, so I cannot give her orders." (Par / colega de mesmo nível)
Rely on -- to need someone, and trust that they will deliver
"The whole test phase relies on her document." (Depender de / contar com)
Drop the ball -- to forget or fail to do what you promised
"Somebody dropped the ball, and now we are late." (Deixar a peça cair / falhar)
Gentle -- soft and careful, not aggressive
"A gentle reminder works better than an angry email." (Gentil / suave)
Unblock -- to remove what is stopping the work
"One page would unblock my whole team." (Desbloquear)
Owe -- to have a duty to give something to someone
"She owes me the document since Monday." (Dever (algo a alguém))
Check in -- to ask, politely, how something is going
"I am checking in on the API document." (Dar uma olhada / acompanhar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Camila is a peer: she does not report to Diogo, and she owes him the API document since Monday. He does not send an angry email. He sends a gentle message: "Just a quick nudge on the API document. I am checking in because my team relies on it -- we cannot start the tests, and we lose a day for every day we wait." Then he makes the ask as small as possible: not the whole document, only the endpoint list. One page would unblock the whole test phase. He asks for a date -- "Could you get it to me by Thursday?" -- and he offers something almost nobody offers: help. "If it is bigger than we thought, I will send someone to write it with you." And he confirms it in writing, so that nobody drops the ball.

1. Why does Diogo ask only for the endpoint list?
A Because a small ask is easy to say yes to, and it unblocks his team.
B Because he does not need the rest of the document, ever.
C Because he wants to punish her.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A I am waiting for your answer.
B I am waiting your answer.
C I am waiting by your answer.
3. What does he offer her?
A To send someone from his team to help her write it.
B To talk to her manager.
C To do her whole job for her.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Nudge, Block, Date

GRAMMAR

Cobrar quem não é seu subordinado: leve, específico e com data. / Chasing a peer without authority.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Abrir leve / Open softJust a quick nudge on / I am checking in on + substantivoJust a quick nudge on the API document.
Mostrar o bloqueio / Show the blockwait for / rely on / depend on + substantivoMy team relies on that page.
Data + saída / Date and exitCould you + verbo + by + diaCould you get it to me by Thursday?
Ponto-chave / Key pointPreposição não se traduz: wait FOR (esperar por), depend ON / rely ON (depender de). E o prazo é BY Thursday (até quinta, no mais tardar).

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of a nudge. Record each one. Warm and light -- if you sound annoyed, she will avoid you.

Just a quick nudge on the API document.
Só um lembrete rápido sobre o documento da API.
My team relies on that page to start the tests.
Meu time depende dessa página para começar os testes.
I do not need the full document, only the endpoint list.
Não preciso do documento inteiro, só da lista de endpoints.
Could you get it to me by Thursday?
Você consegue me mandar até quinta?
If it is bigger than we thought, I will send someone to help.
Se for maior do que a gente pensou, mando alguém ajudar.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real person at Oracle who owes you something today. Speak for two minutes: (1) the soft opener, (2) what it blocks, with a cost, (3) the smallest possible ask, (4) a date, (5) a real offer of help. Record it.
Now she answers: "It is on my list. I will get to it this week, I promise" -- for the second time. Answer out loud. Do not get angry, do not compare deadlines. Make it smaller, and get a date. Record it.
