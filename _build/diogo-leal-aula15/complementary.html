Lesson 15 -- Handling Questions & Closing the Talk

Series
The Newsroom -- any press conference or briefing Q&A scene

Watch a speaker take questions from a room. Notice how they invite questions, give short, calm responses, and push harder ones to later instead of guessing.

Write down one phrase used to invite a question and one used to defer one. Say both out loud until they feel natural.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Handling Q&A and Closing a Presentation"

Practical language for the end of a talk: inviting questions, answering politely, deferring with confidence, and delivering a strong closing. Review of today's words: query, response, defer, recall.

Note three closing phrases the host uses. Record a one-minute closing and use all three.

YouTube
"How to Handle Q&A and End a Presentation Strongly in English"

A short, clear guide to the end of a talk: invite questions, answer calmly, defer what you cannot answer, recall the message, thank the audience, and give a next step.

After watching, record a sixty-second closing for a real Oracle presentation. Invite, answer, defer, and finish with a clear next step.
