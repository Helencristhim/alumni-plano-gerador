Grammar Rule

Invite / Answer / Defer

MomentPhrase you use
Invite a question"Are there any questions?" / "I am happy to take questions."
Answer politely"That is a fair point." / "Good query. The short answer is..."
Defer to later"Let me come back to that after the meeting."
Close the talk"To recall the main message... I appreciate your time. The next step is..."

Key idea: First invite, then answer each query with a short, calm response. If you cannot answer now, defer it politely instead of guessing. Then close: recall the main message in one line, thank the audience, and give one clear next step. A strong close is brief.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Who has a doubt? I do not know that, sorry."
"Are there any questions? That is a fair point -- let me come back to that after the meeting."

Invite questions with "Are there any questions?". If you cannot answer now, defer politely -- "let me come back to that" -- instead of just saying "I do not know".
