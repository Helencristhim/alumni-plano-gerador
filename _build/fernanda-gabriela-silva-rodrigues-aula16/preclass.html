Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

At Sucafina, Fernanda has a problem. She cannot access the new spreadsheet. She asks Daniel: "Can you help me?" Daniel says: "Of course. I can fix it. I am busy now, but I can call you later." Their new colleague also cannot open the folder, so Daniel says he can support him this week. They do not struggle alone. "We can manage this and figure it out together," says Daniel.

1. What can Daniel do for Fernanda? (O que Daniel pode fazer?)
A He can fix the spreadsheet. (Ele pode arrumar a planilha.)
B He cannot help her. (Ele nao pode ajudar.)
C He can only call. (Ele so pode ligar.)
2. "I cannot access the folder" -- what does it mean? (O que significa?)
A I can open it easily. (Consigo abrir facil.)
B I am not able to open it. (Nao consigo abrir.)
C I do not want it. (Nao quero.)
3. How do they solve the problems? (Como resolvem os problemas?)
A Each person works alone. (Cada um sozinho.)
B They wait and do nothing. (Esperam e nao fazem nada.)
C They figure it out together. (Resolvem juntos.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Can / Can't

Grammar

Para falar de habilidade (o que voce consegue ou nao consegue fazer), use can + verbo base. / To talk about ability, use 'can' + the base verb.

Uso / UseForma / FormExample / Exemplo
Oferecer / Offer (sim)can + verbo baseI can help you. (Posso te ajudar.)
Negativo / No abilitycannot / can't + verbo baseI cannot open the file. (Nao consigo abrir.)
Pedir / AskCan + you + verbo base?Can you help me? (Voce pode me ajudar?)
Tranquilizar / ReassureWe can + verbo baseWe can figure it out. (Nos podemos resolver.)

Atencao: depois de 'can' use o verbo base, sem 'to' e sem '-ing' -- e 'can help', nunca 'can to help' nem 'can helping'. 'Can' e igual para I, you, we, he, she (can help, nao 'cans' nem 'can helps'). 'Cannot' = 'can't'. / After 'can', use the base verb -- no 'to', no '-ing'. 'Can' never changes and never adds -s.
