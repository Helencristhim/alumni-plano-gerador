Grammar Rule

Feeling Patterns

PatternUseExample
How are you?ask about feelingsHow are you today?
I am / She is + feelingsay how you feelI am happy. She is tired.
very / a little + feelingmake it stronger or softerI am very tired. I am a little nervous.

Key idea: use the verb to be -- am (I), is (he/she), are (you/we/they) -- before the feeling word. The feeling word never changes: "They are happy" (never "happys").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I happy today."
"I am happy today."

Always use the verb to be with feelings. Say "I am happy", "She is tired", "We are excited".
