Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Happy -- you feel good and you smile
"I am happy today." (feliz)
Sad -- you feel bad and you want to cry
"She is sad today." (triste)
Tired -- you have no energy and you want to rest
"I am very tired." (cansado)
Angry -- you feel hot inside because something is wrong
"He is angry now." (bravo)
Excited -- a strong good feeling, like before a trip
"I am excited about the trip." (animado)
Nervous -- you are not calm, like before a test
"I am nervous before a test." (nervoso)
Bored -- nothing is interesting, you have nothing to do
"The movie is long. I am bored." (entediado)
Worried -- you think a lot about a problem
"She is worried about work." (preocupado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is Monday morning. Estephano meets Sara at work. He asks: "How are you today?" Sara says: "I am a little tired, but I am happy." Estephano says: "I am very excited! I have a new project." Sara is not sad and she is not angry. They start the day with a smile.

1. What does Estephano ask Sara?
A "How are you today?"
B "How much is it?"
C "Where are you?"
2. How does Sara feel?
A Sad and angry.
B A little tired, but happy.
C Bored and nervous.
3. Why is Estephano excited?
A Because it is the weekend.
B Because he has a new project.
C Because he is tired.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- To Be for Feelings

GRAMMAR

Falar sobre sentimentos com o verbo to be. / Talking about feelings with the verb to be.

Pattern / EstruturaUse / UsoExample
How are you?Perguntar como a pessoa estáHow are you today?
I am / She is + feelingDizer como você se senteI am happy. She is tired.
very / a little + feelingMais forte ou mais leveI am very tired. I am a little nervous.
Atenção: use sempre o verbo to be -- am (I), is (he/she), are (you/we/they). Diga "I am happy" (nunca "I happy"). A palavra de sentimento não muda: "They are happy" (nunca "happys").
