Chapter 3: Reading the Clues

Guessing with Confidence

Modals of deduction · must, might, could, can't

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"With those crowds, it must be a famous landmark."

"It's so empty -- it might be off the beaten track."

"There's no one here -- it can't be a tourist hotspot."

"This old photo -- the place must have been awe-inspiring."

How sure?Now (present)Then (past)
Almost sure it's trueIt must be a landmark.It must have been stunning.
Possible (maybe)It might / could be a hidden gem.It might have been a market.
Sure it's NOT trueIt can't be overrated.It can't have been empty.

All of these are followed by the base verb (must be) or have + past participle for the past (must have been). We use them to make a logical guess from evidence -- not to talk about obligation.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"It mustn't be a famous place."

✓ RIGHT

"It can't be a famous place."

✗ WRONG

"It must to be worth the trip."

✓ RIGHT

"It must be worth the trip."

Practice

Complete the Deduction

Say it first, then click to check

"Those long lines -- it ___must be worth the trip."
"It's completely empty, so it ___can't be a tourist hotspot."
"I'm not sure, but it ___might be a hidden gem."
"Look at this 1920s photo -- the city must have ___been sprawling even then."
"With that architecture, it ___could be a famous landmark."
Chapter 4: Traveler to Traveler

Comparing Impressions

Pricila talks travel with a fellow guest

Dialogue

Two Very Different Places

T
You've travelled so much, Pricila. Of all the places you've seen, which one really stands out?
P
A tiny village in the mountains, completely off the beaten track. It was unspoiled and picturesque -- a real hidden gem.
T
That sounds peaceful. It must have been hard to reach if it's so far from everything.
P
It was, but it was worth the trip. I got to immerse myself in the local life. What about you -- any big landmark you loved?
T
Honestly, the most famous one I saw was a bit overrated. The bustling, sprawling city around it was far more interesting.
P
I know exactly what you mean. The crowds can't be the real reason to go. For me, the quiet getaways are always the most awe-inspiring.
