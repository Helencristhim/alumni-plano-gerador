Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Check-in desk -- where you start at the airport and drop off your bags
"Go to the check-in desk first." (Balcão de check-in)
Boarding pass -- the ticket you need to get on the plane
"Have your boarding pass ready." (Cartão de embarque)
Gate -- the door where you wait and board the plane
"Go to gate twelve." (Portão de embarque)
Terminal -- a big building at the airport with gates and shops
"Your flight leaves from terminal two." (Terminal)
Security check -- where they scan you and your bags for safety
"Please proceed through the security check." (Inspeção de segurança / raio-x)
Passport control -- where an officer checks your passport before you fly
"Show your passport at passport control." (Controle de passaporte / imigração)
Luggage -- the bags and suitcases you travel with
"Do not leave your luggage unattended." (Bagagem)
Board -- to get on the plane, train, or bus
"We will board at gate twelve." (Embarcar)
Delayed -- late; the flight leaves later than the planned time
"The flight is delayed by forty minutes." (Atrasado)
Customs -- where officers check what you bring into a country
"You go through customs after you land." (Alfândega)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline arrives at the airport for her work trip. At the check-in desk, the agent says: "Put your luggage on the belt, please. Here is your boarding pass. The flight is delayed by forty minutes." Then he gives the instructions: "Go to gate twelve in terminal two. Have your boarding pass ready, and don't forget your passport at passport control." Aline goes through the security check and walks to her gate to board. Notice: an instruction uses the base verb with no subject ("Go", not "You go"), and the negative is Don't + base verb ("Don't forget"). Add Please to be polite.

1. Why is the boarding time later than planned?
A The flight is delayed by forty minutes.
B Aline arrived late.
C The gate is closed.
2. Which instruction is correct?
A "You go to gate twelve."
B "Go to gate twelve."
C "To go to gate twelve."
3. What is the correct negative instruction?
A "No forget your passport."
B "Don't forget your passport."
C "Don't forgets your passport."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Imperative

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para dar instruções. / Use these forms to give instructions.

Uso / UseForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Instrução / Instructionverbo base (sem sujeito)Go to the gate.
Negativo / NegativeDo not / Don't + verbo baseDon't forget your passport.
Educado / PolitePlease + verbo basePlease proceed to security.
Com "and" / With "and"verbo + and + verboCheck in and go to the gate.

Key point / Ponto-chave: a instrução (imperativo) usa o verbo base sem sujeito -- diga "Go", não "You go". O negativo é Don't + verbo base ("Don't forget"), nunca "No forget". Use Please para ser educado.
