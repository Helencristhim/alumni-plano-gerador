Giving instructions with the imperative

What do the orange words have in common?

Grammar Rule

Giving Instructions

UseFormExample
Instructionbase verb (no subject)Go to the gate. Take your bag.
NegativeDo not / Don't + base verbDon't forget your passport.
PolitePlease + base verbPlease proceed to security.
With "and"verb + and + verbCheck in and go to the gate.

Key idea: an instruction (imperative) uses the base verb with no subject ("Go", not "You go"). The negative is Don't + base verb ("Don't forget"). Add Please to be polite.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You go to gate twelve."
"Go to gate twelve."

An instruction has no subject (Go, not You go), and the negative is Don't + base verb (Don't forget), never "No forget".
