Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Option -- one of the possible choices in a decision
"We have three options: expand, wait, or run a pilot." (Opção)
Weigh the options -- to compare the choices carefully before deciding
"Let us weigh the options against our cash position." (Avaliar / ponderar as opções)
Pros and cons -- the advantages and the disadvantages of a choice
"Each path has its pros and cons; let us list them." (Prós e contras)
Recommend -- to advise a choice as the best one to take
"I recommend the safe path; it protects our margins." (Recomendar)
Alternative -- another option you could choose instead
"If we do not expand, the alternative is a small pilot." (Alternativa)
Prioritize -- to rank choices and decide what comes first
"We should prioritize the option that protects the partnership." (Priorizar)
Downside -- the negative side or disadvantage of a choice
"The downside of expanding now is the high cost." (Desvantagem / ponto negativo)
Trade-off -- a balance: you accept one loss to gain something else
"The trade-off of the safe path is that we move slower." (Compensação / troca)
Postpone -- to delay something to a later date
"We could postpone the expansion until next quarter." (Adiar)
Reach a consensus -- to arrive at an agreement the whole group accepts
"Let us reach a consensus before the call with David." (Chegar a um consenso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The decision meeting has three options on the table. Before we choose, we should weigh the options and list the pros and cons of each path. We ought to prioritize the choice that protects the partnership -- should and ought to mean the same here, but ought to sounds a little more formal. The downside of expanding now is the cost, so we had better not commit the full budget at once. The alternative is to postpone and invest in the current line; the trade-off is that we move slower. But the supplier needs an answer by Friday, so we had better decide today, or we lose the deal -- notice that "had better" carries a warning and points to now, not the past. My recommendation is the pilot. Whatever we choose, let us reach a consensus before the call. After should and had better, we use the base verb with no "to".

1. Why does the text use "had better decide today"?
A Because there is a real consequence (the deadline), so it is strong advice with a warning.
B Because the decision happened in the past.
C Because it is softer than "should".
2. What is true about "should" and "ought to" here?
A They have opposite meanings.
B They mean the same; ought to is just a little more formal.
C Should is more formal than ought to.
3. What comes after "should" and "had better"?
A The base verb, with no "to" (should decide, had better warn).
B "to" + verb (should to decide).
C The verb in the past tense.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Should / Ought To / Had Better

GRAMMAR

Para dar conselho há dois níveis. Para conselho normal, use SHOULD ou OUGHT TO (mesmo sentido; ought to é um pouco mais formal). Para conselho forte, com uma consequência real, use HAD BETTER (carrega um aviso). Depois de should e had better, use o verbo base SEM "to"; só ought mantém o "to". Had better NÃO é passado -- aponta para agora e o futuro próximo. / For advice there are two levels. For normal advice use should or ought to (same meaning; ought to is more formal). For strong advice with a real consequence use had better (it carries a warning). After should and had better, use the base verb with no "to"; only ought keeps the "to". Had better is not past -- it points to now and the near future.

FormUseExample
should + base verbnormal advice"we should review the numbers"
ought to + base verbsame, more formal (keep to)"we ought to consider it"
had better + base verbstrong advice + consequence"we had better decide, or..."
Negativesshould not / ought not to / had better not"we had better not delay"

Key point / Ponto-chave: use should/ought to para conselho normal e had better só quando há consequência (aviso). Não use "to" depois de should e had better: "we should to decide" → "we should decide". / Use should/ought to for normal advice and had better only when there is a consequence. No "to" after should and had better.
