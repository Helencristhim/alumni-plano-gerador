Language Discovery

should / ought to / had better

Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. Which is normal advice, which is more formal, and which carries a warning?

"We should weigh the options before we decide."

"We ought to consider the safer alternative."

"We had better decide today, or we lose the contract."

"We had better not commit the full budget at once."

The Rule

Should / Ought To / Had Better

FormUseExample
should + base verbnormal advice / recommendation"we should review the numbers"
ought to + base verbsame meaning, more formal (note the to)"we ought to consider it"
had better + base verbstrong advice, with a consequence (now/future)"we had better decide today, or..."
Negativesshould not / ought not to / had better not"we had better not delay"
Contractionhad better → 'd better in speech"we'd better warn the team"

Key idea: use should or ought to for normal advice (same meaning, ought to is more formal). Use had better only when there is a real consequence -- it carries a warning. After should and had better, use the base verb with no to; after ought, keep the to. Had better is not past tense -- it points to now and the near future.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We should to decide today." / "We had better to warn the team."
"We should decide today." / "We had better warn the team."

After should and had better, use the base verb with no to. Only ought keeps the to: "we ought to consider it".

Should and ought to mean the same; ought to is a little more formal.
