The Story of the Comeback -- Telling How You Got Here

Learn to tell the story of a career the way a founder does. You will meet twelve storytelling words -- turning point, groundwork, momentum, breakthrough, uphill battle, take off, pay off, from the ground up, against the odds, come into its own, grit, in the making -- and master the narrative tenses, especially the past perfect continuous (By the time it took off, we had been working for years).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words founders use to tell their journey. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que fundadores usam para contar a própria trajetória. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Turning point -- a moment when an important change happens / momento decisivo, virada
"The turning point came in the second year of the clinic."
Groundwork -- the basic preparation done before the real work / trabalho de base, preparação
"For years she had been laying the groundwork quietly."
Momentum -- the force that keeps a process growing once it starts / impulso, embalo
"The momentum she had been chasing finally arrived."
Breakthrough -- a sudden, important success or discovery / avanço decisivo, descoberta
"A procedure they had refined produced a breakthrough."
Uphill battle -- a long, difficult struggle / batalha difícil, luta árdua
"The early days were an uphill battle for the whole team."
Take off -- to suddenly start to succeed or grow fast / deslanchar, decolar
"She wondered whether the clinic would ever take off."
Pay off -- to bring a good result after effort / valer a pena, dar resultado
"The years of quiet work finally paid off."
From the ground up -- from the very beginning, from nothing / do zero, do começo
"She built the whole team from the ground up."
Against the odds -- despite little chance of success / contra todas as probabilidades
"Against the odds, the clinic began to grow."
Come into its own -- to become fully effective or successful / firmar-se, atingir seu auge
"By the third year, the clinic had come into its own."
Grit -- courage and determination over a long time / garra, determinação
"What people called luck was really grit."
In the making -- developing over time, not yet finished but on the way / em construção, a caminho
"The success that seemed sudden had been in the making for years."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When Eliane tells her story, she does not begin at the launch. By the time the clinic opened, she had been working toward it for years -- an action going on for a long time up to a point in the past. While the bigger clinics were scaling fast, she was still building her team, the background of the story. Then the turning point came: the past simple carries the events. A procedure her team had refined produced a breakthrough -- the past perfect for what had already finished. Notice the contrast: use had been + -ing to stress how long something lasted, but with state verbs like be or know, use the past perfect simple: the success had been in the making for years.

1. Which tense shows an action going on for a long time up to a point in the past?
A Past perfect continuous -- she had been working toward it for years
B Past simple -- she worked toward it for years
C Present perfect -- she has worked toward it for years
2. Which tense carries the completed events that move the story forward?
A Past continuous -- the turning point was coming
B Past simple -- the turning point came
C Past perfect continuous -- the turning point had been coming
3. Why is it "the success had been in the making" and not "had been being made"?
A "Be" is a state verb, so it does not take the continuous -- use the past perfect simple.
B Because the action was very short.
C Because it happened after the launch.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Narrative Tenses

GRAMMAR

We use the narrative tenses together to tell a story: the past simple moves it forward, the others fill in the background and what had been happening before. / Usamos os tempos narrativos juntos para contar uma história: o past simple faz a história avançar, e os outros preenchem o pano de fundo e o que já vinha acontecendo antes.

Tense / TempoForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Past simple / passado simplescame, opened, producedThe turning point came.
Past continuous / passado contínuowas / were + -ingWhile the market was scaling...
Past perfect / mais-que-perfeitohad + past participleA procedure they had refined...
Past perfect continuous / mais-que-perfeito contínuohad been + -ingWe had been working for years.

State verbs (be, know, have, believe) do not take the continuous: say had known, not had been knowing. / Verbos de estado não vão no contínuo: diga had known, nunca had been knowing.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

In about two minutes, tell the story of a comeback in your career (or your clinic). Start before the turning point: say what had been happening during the groundwork years, using at least three past perfect continuous forms (I had been working / building / refining).
