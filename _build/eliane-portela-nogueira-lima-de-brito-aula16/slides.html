Chapter 4: Narrative Tenses

We had been working...

the tenses that carry a story

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"By the time we opened, we had been working on it for years."

"While the competitors were scaling fast, we built our team."

"The turning point came in the second year."

"A procedure the team had refined produced a breakthrough."

"The success had been in the making for years."

TenseFormUse in a story
Past simplecame, opened, producedthe completed events that move the story forward
Past continuouswas / were + -inga longer background action in progress
Past perfecthad + past participlean action finished before another past moment
Past perfect continuoushad been + -inghow long an action had been going on up to a past point

Good storytelling mixes all four. Use the past perfect continuous to show the years that had been in the making before the turning point. Note: state verbs (be, know, have) do not take the continuous -- say "had been", "had known", not "had been being".

Language Focus

Name the Tense

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

By the time we launched, we worked on it for two years.
By the time we launched, we had been working on it for two years.
When the crisis hit, we were struggling since January.
When the crisis hit, we had been struggling since January.
I had been knowing the risk for months before we acted.
I had known the risk for months before we acted.

For an action that lasted up to a point in the past, use the past perfect continuous (had been + -ing). With state verbs like know, be and have, use the past perfect simple instead (had known), because they do not take the continuous.

Dialogue

The Interview

T
So, before the clinic opened, how long had you actually been working on it?
E
Honestly? By the time we opened, I had been building the plan for the better part of a decade.
T
A decade. And in those early years, what was happening behind the scenes?
E
It was an uphill battle. While the bigger clinics were scaling fast, I was still hiring a team from the ground up.
T
So when did it change? What was the turning point?
E
The second year. A procedure we had been refining for months finally produced a breakthrough.
T
And the world called it an overnight success. Is that fair?
E
Against the odds, the clinic came into its own -- but overnight? No. It had been in the making for years.
T
That is the part people miss. They see the breakthrough, but never the groundwork you had been laying.
