Film
The Pursuit of Happyness (2006)

The true story of Chris Gardner, who had been struggling as a salesman and became homeless with his young son long before anything paid off. It is the definition of a comeback: the uphill battle, the grit, the groundwork nobody saw, and the single turning point when it finally took off. Watch how the story is told -- almost everything meaningful happens in what had been building before the breakthrough.

Tip: after the film, tell Chris's story in three sentences using the narrative tenses ("He had been struggling for months when the turning point finally came"), then do the same for your own career.

Podcast
Business Wars

David Brown narrates the real story of how great companies rose, stalled and came back -- told entirely in the narrative tenses. Every episode is a founder's journey: the groundwork they had been laying, the turning point, the breakthrough that finally paid off against the odds. Listen for how a good story mixes the past simple with the past perfect and past perfect continuous to build tension.

Tip: pick one episode and note three sentences where the host uses "had been + -ing" to say what a company had been doing before its turning point.

engVid -- "Learn English Tenses: PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS"

A clear lesson with Rebecca on exactly this lesson's key grammar: had been + -ing, used to say how long an action had been going on up to a point in the past. She shows how to build it and how it differs from the past perfect simple -- the contrast at the heart of Lesson 16.
