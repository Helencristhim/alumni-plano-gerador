Projecting Forward -- Timelines, Forecasts, and Strategic Plans

Talking about the future of a plan: will + base for predictions (Demand will rise next quarter), be going to + base for plans (We are going to lock the baseline in Q1), present continuous for fixed arrangements (We are presenting the roadmap on Monday), future continuous will be + -ing for an action in progress at a future time (This time next year we will be running the model), and future perfect will have + participle for something finished before a future point (By Q4 we will have hit the target). New vocabulary: projection, timeline, roadmap, outlook, trajectory, scenario, anticipate, initiative, deliverable, baseline, headwind, quarter, scalable, target, pipeline.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Projection -- an estimate of future figures based on current data
"Our projection shows demand will rise next quarter." (Projeção)
Timeline -- a schedule showing when each step happens
"The timeline puts the launch in Q3." (Cronograma)
Roadmap -- a strategic plan showing steps toward a goal over time
"The roadmap has three initiatives." (Roteiro estratégico)
Outlook -- the expected future situation
"The outlook for next year is strong." (Perspectiva)
Trajectory -- the direction something is developing in
"Our trajectory is good, but nothing is automatic." (Trajetória)
Scenario -- a possible future situation you plan for
"We test each scenario before we decide." (Cenário)
Anticipate -- to expect something and prepare for it
"We anticipate two headwinds next year." (Antecipar)
Initiative -- a new plan or effort to achieve a goal
"Our first initiative is to rebuild the pipeline." (Iniciativa)
Deliverable -- a concrete result that must be produced
"Each deliverable has an owner and a date." (Entregável)
Baseline -- a starting point used to measure future progress
"Let us set a baseline before we launch." (Linha de base)
Headwind -- a factor that slows down progress
"Longer lead times are our main headwind." (Obstáculo)
Quarter -- a three-month period in the business year
"Demand peaks in the third quarter." (Trimestre)
Scalable -- able to grow or expand easily
"The plan is scalable, so it grows with demand." (Escalável)
Target -- a result you aim to achieve
"We measure every target against the baseline." (Meta)
Pipeline -- projects or work planned for the future
"The pipeline can absorb a spike in demand." (Fila de projetos)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Forms

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de previsões, planos e o estado de um plano no futuro. / Used to talk about predictions, plans, and the state of a plan in the future.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
will + basePrevisão ou decisão no momento / Prediction or decision nowDemand will rise next quarter.
be going to + basePlano ou previsão com evidência / Plan or evidence-based predictionWe are going to lock the baseline.
present continuousCompromisso futuro já marcado / Fixed future arrangementWe are presenting the roadmap on Monday.
will be + -ing (future continuous)Ação em andamento num momento futuro / In progress at a future timeThis time next year, we will be running the model.
will have + particípio (future perfect)Ação concluída antes de um ponto futuro / Finished before a future pointBy Q4, we will have hit the target.

Key point / Ponto-chave: após when, by the time, as soon as, once em frases futuras, use o presente simples, NUNCA "will". Errado: "By the time we will launch..."; correto: "By the time we launch, demand will have risen."
