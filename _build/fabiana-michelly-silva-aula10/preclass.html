Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Projection -- an estimate of future outcomes based on data
"Based on our projections, demand will grow by twelve percent." (Projeção)
Roadmap -- a visual plan showing milestones and deadlines
"The roadmap shows three key milestones for Q1." (Roteiro)
Scalability -- the ability to grow and handle more demand
"Our strategic priority is scalability across APAC." (Escalabilidade)
Budget allocation -- distributing financial resources to different areas
"We are about to finalize the budget allocation." (Alocação de orçamento)
Procurement cycle -- the complete process from needs to orders
"We may need to accelerate the procurement cycle." (Ciclo de aquisição)
Pilot program -- a small-scale test before full launch
"The pilot program is due to launch in February." (Programa piloto)
On track -- progressing as planned
"We are on track to meet the Q1 milestones." (No caminho certo)
Bring forward -- to move something to an earlier date
"I recommend we bring forward the testing phase." (Antecipar)
Accelerate -- to make something happen faster
"If demand grows, we may need to accelerate." (Acelerar)
Forecast (verb) -- to predict future trends based on data
"The demand forecast indicates a twelve percent increase." (Prever)
Milestone -- a significant checkpoint in a project timeline
"Vendor onboarding is the first milestone." (Marco)
Deployment -- putting a system into active use
"Full deployment is scheduled for mid-March." (Implantação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The strategic plan for Q1 is taking shape. Based on current projections, the team is going to expand the APAC vendor network to five partners. The roadmap shows three key milestones. By the end of Q1, they will have completed the migration to the new platform. The pilot program is due to launch in February, and full deployment is scheduled for mid-March. The budget allocation is about to be finalized. However, the team may need to accelerate the procurement cycle if demand grows faster than forecasted. Marcus will share the revised numbers by Friday. The team is meeting the VP tomorrow to present the plan. Their strategic priority is scalability -- they want to bring forward the testing phase to stay on track.

1. What future form is used for the planned expansion? ("is going to expand")
A Going to -- for a planned decision based on current evidence.
B Will -- for a spontaneous decision.
C Present continuous -- for a fixed arrangement.
2. What does "will have completed" emphasize?
A The action is happening right now.
B The action will be FINISHED before the end of Q1.
C The action was completed in the past.
3. Why does the text use "is meeting" for the VP appointment?
A Present continuous for a fixed arrangement with specific time and place.
B It means the meeting is happening right now.
C It is a general plan without a specific date.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Advanced Future Forms

GRAMMAR

Seis formas de falar sobre o futuro com precisão. / Six ways to talk about the future with precision.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
going toDecisão planejada / Planned decisionWe are going to expand.
willPromessa / decisão espontânea / PromiseI will finalize by Friday.
will have + ppCompleto ANTES de um ponto futuro / Done beforeWe will have completed by Q1.
be about toFuturo imediato (muito em breve) / Very soonWe are about to finalize.
be due toAgendado / Expected / ScheduledThe pilot is due to launch.
Ponto-chaveCada forma tem uma NUANCE diferente. "By" = pista para future perfect. "Tomorrow at 10" = present continuous.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

You have a clear plan to expand vendors next quarter. The best future form is:
A "We are going to expand the vendor network." (planned decision)
B "We will expand the vendor network." (sounds like spontaneous decision)
C "We are expanding the vendor network." (needs specific time for arrangement)
Your manager asks when the migration will be done. You say it will be FINISHED before end of March. Use:
A "By March, we will complete the migration." (missing completion emphasis)
B "By the end of March, we will have completed the migration." (future perfect)
C "We are about to complete the migration by March." (about to = very soon, not months away)
The budget is almost done -- just a few more signatures needed, probably today. You say:
A "We are about to finalize the budget allocation." (immediate future)
B "We are going to finalize the budget allocation." (sounds like a plan, not imminent)
C "The budget is due to be finalized." (sounds like a scheduled event, not happening right now)
