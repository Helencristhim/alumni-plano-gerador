Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Will, going to, future continuous, and future perfect

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Prediction, plan, in progress, or finished before a future point?

"Demand will rise next quarter."

"We are going to lock the baseline in Q1."

"By the end of Q2, we will have rebuilt the pipeline."

"This time next year, we will be running the new model."

Grammar Rule

The Future Forms

StructureUse
will + base -- "Demand will rise next quarter."A prediction or a decision now
be going to + base -- "We are going to lock the baseline in Q1."A plan or an evidence-based prediction
will be + -ing -- "This time next year, we will be running the model."An action in progress at a future time
will have + participle -- "By Q4, we will have hit the target."An action finished before a future point

Key idea: after when, by the time, as soon as, once in a future sentence, use the present simple, not "will". Say "By the time we launch...", not "By the time we will launch...".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"By the time we will launch, demand will have risen."
"By the time we launch, demand will have risen."

After when, by the time, as soon as, once in a future sentence, use the present simple. The future perfect goes in the other part of the sentence.
