Chapter 3

The Code

Advanced future forms for strategic planning

Discovery

Six Ways to Talk About the Future

"We are going to expand the vendor network." (planned decision)

"I will finalize the budget by Friday." (promise/spontaneous decision)

"By Q1, we will have completed the migration." (future perfect -- completed before a point)

"We are about to finalize the budget." (immediate future -- very soon)

"The pilot is due to launch in February." (scheduled/expected)

"We are meeting the vendor tomorrow at 10." (present continuous for fixed arrangement)

Grammar Rule

Advanced Future Forms

FormUseExample
going to + verbPlanned decision / prediction with evidenceWe are going to expand.
will + verbPromise / spontaneous decision / predictionI will finalize by Friday.
will have + ppCompleted before a future pointWe will have completed by Q1.
be about to + verbImmediate future (very soon)We are about to finalize.
be due to + verbScheduled / expectedThe pilot is due to launch.
present continuousFixed arrangement with time/placeWe are meeting them tomorrow.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"By March, we will complete the migration." (simple will -- not emphasizing completion)
"By March, we will have completed the migration." (future perfect -- DONE before March)

Use will have + past participle when you want to emphasize that something will be finished before a specific future point. "By" is your clue.

Spot the Error

Find the Mistake

0 / 4 errors found

"By March, we will complete the migration." (missing emphasis on completion)
"By March, we WILL HAVE COMPLETED the migration." (future perfect for completion before a point)
"The pilot is due to launching in February."
"The pilot is due to LAUNCH in February." (due to + base verb, not -ing)
"We are about to finalized the budget."
"We are about to FINALIZE the budget." (about to + base verb, not past participle)
"I am going to meet them tomorrow at 10 if I have time."
"I AM MEETING them tomorrow at 10." (fixed arrangement with time = present continuous, not going to with conditional)
