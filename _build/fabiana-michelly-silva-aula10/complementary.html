Lesson 10 -- Projecting Forward

Series
Industry -- Season 2, Episode 1: "Exodus"

The new season opens with characters presenting strategic plans and projections to leadership. Watch how they use future forms to describe targets, milestones, and potential risks.

Note every future form you hear (will, going to, will have, about to, due to). Which form do they use most when presenting plans?

Podcast
Supply Chain Now -- "Strategic Planning for Next Year: What Leaders Are Prioritizing"

Industry leaders discuss their strategic priorities and roadmaps for the coming year. Listen for projection language, milestone planning, and how they describe scalability goals.

Write down 3 strategic goals the speakers describe. What future forms do they use? Rewrite each using a different future form.

YouTube
TED Talk -- "How Great Leaders Inspire Action" (Simon Sinek)

A classic 18-minute talk on visionary leadership. Sinek uses future-oriented language throughout to describe where organizations are headed. Directly applicable to presenting strategic plans.

After watching, prepare a 2-minute strategic plan presentation for a fictional Bloomberg initiative using all 6 future forms from today's lesson.
