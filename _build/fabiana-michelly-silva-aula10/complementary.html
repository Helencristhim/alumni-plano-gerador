Lesson 10 -- Projecting Forward

Series
Billions -- "The Future" (Season 5, planning episodes)

Executives present projections, defend a roadmap, and argue over the outlook for the next quarter. A sharp example of how leaders talk about timelines, targets, and scenarios under pressure.

Watch with English subtitles. Note one projection and one risk a character names. Then say each aloud: one sentence with the future perfect ("By Q4, we will have...") and one with the future continuous ("This time next year, we will be...").

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Build a Strategic Plan That Works"

A practical conversation on setting a baseline, drawing a roadmap, and stress-testing a forecast against different scenarios. Directly relevant to the annual planning you lead at Bloomberg.

Note 3 ideas the guest gives for a strong plan. For each, build one sentence about your team using a new word (roadmap, baseline, scenario, headwind) and a future form (will / going to / will have / will be).

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Present a Forecast to Leadership"

A short, practical video on how to open with the outlook, show the projection, and walk a board through the timeline and the main scenarios. A template you can reuse for your own strategic plan.

After watching, record a 60-second forecast for your team. Use at least four new words and three structures: one future perfect, one future continuous, and one "going to" plan.
