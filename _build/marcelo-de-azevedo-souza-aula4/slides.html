Chapter 4: The Code

Gerund or Infinitive?

I love riding · I tend to avoid

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I love riding through the mountains."

"I tend to avoid busy, paved roads."

"What I enjoy most is being alone on the road."

"I find it hard to explain the feeling."

Why green for the -ing form, but blue for to + verb?

FormAfter these verbsExample
Gerund
verb + -ing		enjoy, love, avoid, keep, finish, missI enjoy riding.
Infinitive
to + base verb		tend, want, decide, hope, learn, find it hardI tend to avoid traffic.

Some verbs (like, prefer, start) take both with almost the same meaning: I like riding = I like to ride.

Language Focus

Gerund or Infinitive?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I enjoy to ride off-road."

✓ RIGHT

"I enjoy riding off-road."

✗ WRONG

"I want riding to the top."

✓ RIGHT

"I want to ride to the top."

After enjoy / love / avoid use the -ing form. After want / tend / decide use to + the base verb.

Dialogue

A Hostel in Tirana

F
You look like you have been riding all day. I'm Fiona, from Manchester. Are you doing the mountain route too?
M
I am! I'm Marcelo, from Brazil. I love riding off-road, so I always take the scenic route, even when it's a steep climb.
F
Same here. Most people avoid the hard climbs, but I think they're the best part. So what makes you keep doing it?
M
The solitude, mostly. And the feeling when you finish something demanding. Reaching the top is the most rewarding thing I know.
F
I know exactly what you mean. How do you handle the really long days, though?
M
You learn to pace yourself. I stopped rushing to arrive, and I started enjoying the ride. Honestly, it's exhilarating.
F
That's beautiful. It's so hard to explain this to people who don't ride, isn't it?
M
Impossible! I always just say, 'You had to be there.' But honestly, I wouldn't trade any of it for anything.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can talk about my passion using gerunds (love riding, enjoy being).
I can use infinitives after verbs like tend, want and learn (tend to avoid, learn to pace).
I don't mix them up (not 'enjoy to ride', not 'want riding').
I know the words of adventure: thrill, endurance, solitude, rewarding, off-road, steep climb.
I can tell an adventure story that captures the feeling, not just the facts.
