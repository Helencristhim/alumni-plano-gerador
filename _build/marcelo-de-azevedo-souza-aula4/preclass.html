Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Thrill -- a sudden strong feeling of excitement (emoção intensa)
"The long descent gave me a real thrill."
Endurance -- the ability to keep going through effort or pain (resistência)
"Long climbs really test your endurance."
Solitude -- the peaceful state of being alone (solidão tranquila)
"I ride for the solitude of the open road."
Rewarding -- giving a satisfying sense of achievement (gratificante)
"Reaching the summit is incredibly rewarding."
Demanding -- needing a lot of effort and skill (exigente)
"The route was steep and very demanding."
Liberating -- making you feel free (libertador)
"Cycling alone is a liberating feeling."
Exhilarating -- making you feel very excited and alive (eletrizante)
"The mountain descent was absolutely exhilarating."
Off-road -- away from paved roads, on rough ground (fora de estrada)
"We took an off-road trail deep into the hills."
Steep climb -- a hard, sharp uphill section (subida íngreme)
"A steep climb was waiting for us after lunch."
Rough terrain -- hard, uneven ground (terreno acidentado)
"Rough terrain slows you down, but it feels wild."
Scenic route -- a longer road chosen for its beautiful views (rota panorâmica)
"We always take the scenic route, never the highway."
To pace yourself -- to control your effort so you don't tire too soon (dosar o próprio ritmo)
"On a long ride, you have to pace yourself."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

For Marcelo, adventure is a feeling, not a sport. He loves riding through wild, empty places, and he tends to avoid the easy, paved roads. He enjoys being alone with the road, and he never wants to rush to the end. On his hardest trips, through Albania, Morocco and Yemen, he remembers deciding to keep going when his body wanted to stop. Over the years, he has learned to pace himself and to enjoy the ride. When people ask him why he does it, he finds it hard to explain. He just smiles and keeps pedaling.

1. Por que dizemos "He loves riding through wild places"?
A Porque depois de "love" usamos o gerundio (-ing).
B Porque "love" sempre pede "to" + verbo.
C Porque "riding" está no passado.
2. "He tends to avoid the easy roads" usa "to avoid" porque:
A "tend" é sempre seguido de gerundio.
B depois de "tend" usamos o infinitivo (to + verbo).
C "avoid" nunca aceita gerundio.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A He enjoys to ride off-road.
B He enjoys riding off-road.
C He enjoys rode off-road.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerund (-ing) vs Infinitive (to ...)

GRAMMAR

Como falar da sua paixão com a forma verbal certa (explicação em inglês e português).

FormApós / AfterExample
Gerund
verb + -ing		enjoy, love, avoid, keep, finish, imagine, missI love riding.
Infinitive
to + base verb		tend, want, decide, hope, learn, find it hardI tend to avoid traffic.
Both / Amboslike, prefer, start aceitam os dois com sentido quase igual: I like riding = I like to ride.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada.

Tell the story of one specific moment from a cycling trip -- not the whole trip, just one moment: the climb, the view, the solitude. Use gerunds (love riding, kept going) and infinitives (decided to, learned to), and end with a phrase that captures the feeling. Take your time.
