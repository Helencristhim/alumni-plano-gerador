Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Jargon -- technical words that only your team understands
"No jargon, please: they are not engineers." (Jargão)
Analogy -- a comparison with something everybody knows
"Let me give you an analogy: it is like an old car." (Analogia)
Justify -- to give good reasons for a decision
"I can justify the four weeks in one sentence." (Justificar)
Benefit -- what the company gains: money, time or safety
"The benefit is four hours saved every week." (Benefício)
Cost-effective -- worth the money you pay for it
"It is cheaper to maintain, so it is cost-effective." (Custo-benefício bom)
Maintain -- to keep a system working, month after month
"The old system is expensive to maintain." (Manter)
Legacy -- old technology that still runs the business
"The legacy database is fifteen years old." (Legado)
Complexity -- how many moving parts a system has
"Every new tool adds complexity." (Complexidade)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The steering committee gives Diogo sixty seconds, and the CFO is in the room. He does not open with the database -- that would be jargon in the first ten seconds. He opens with time: "In plain terms, we lose four hours every week keeping a fifteen-year-old system alive." Then he justifies the four weeks with an analogy: the legacy database is like a car from 2010, with six cheap repairs a year. The technical reason comes in one sentence: the new system is cheaper to maintain and safer, which means the client's data is not sitting in a system nobody supports. That is the benefit, and it is cost-effective. Every extra tool would only add complexity, so he does not mention any. And he closes with the cost of doing nothing: the same four hours, every week, forever.

1. Why does Diogo not open with the database?
A Because the executives stop listening at the first technical word.
B Because the database is not important.
C Because he does not know how it works.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A The new system is cheaper to maintain.
B The new system is more cheaper to maintain.
C The new system is more cheap to maintain.
3. What is the function of "which means"?
A It translates a technical fact into a business result.
B It adds a second technical detail.
C It asks a question.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Translate, Compare, Illustrate

GRAMMAR

Explicar tecnologia para quem paga por ela: resultado primeiro, motivo depois, uma analogia. / Explaining technology to the people who pay for it.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Traduzir / Translatefato técnico + which means + resultado de negócioWe are replacing it, which means the system does not stop.
Comparar / Comparecheaper / faster / safer (+ than)The new one is cheaper to maintain than the old one.
Ilustrar / IllustrateIt is like + algo que todo mundo conheceThe legacy database is like a car from 2010.
Ponto-chave / Key pointAdjetivo curto leva -er (cheap → cheaper, safe → safer): nunca "more cheaper". E toda oração precisa de sujeito: "because it is faster", nunca "because is faster".

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of a plain-English answer. Record each one. Slow and simple -- if she has to ask what a word means, you lost her.

In plain terms, we lose four hours every week.
Em termos simples, perdemos quatro horas por semana.
We are replacing it, which means the system does not stop.
Estamos trocando o sistema, o que significa que ele não para mais.
The legacy database is like a car from 2010.
O banco de dados legado é como um carro de 2010.
The new one is cheaper to maintain, and safer.
O novo é mais barato de manter, e mais seguro.
If we do nothing, we pay the same four hours forever.
Se não fizermos nada, pagamos as mesmas quatro horas para sempre.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. The CFO asks why the project needs four weeks. You open with:
A "In plain terms, we lose four hours every week keeping the old system alive."
B "We are migrating from a monolith to a containerized architecture."
C "It is very technical, you would not be interested."
2. She says: "The system works. Why touch it?" The best answer is:
A "If we do nothing, we pay the same four hours every week, forever."
B "Because the technology is old and ugly."
C "Everybody in the market is doing this."
3. She asks how it works. You:
A Give one analogy from real life, in one sentence.
B Explain the architecture, layer by layer.
C Say it is too complex to explain.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real technical decision from your project at Oracle. Speak for 90 seconds to someone who does not work in technology: (1) the result in money or time, (2) the reason, in ONE sentence, (3) an analogy from real life, (4) the cost of doing nothing, (5) what you need. Record it.
Now she interrupts: "Stop. I did not understand a word of that." Start again, out loud, with different words -- and this time do not use a single technical term. Record it.
