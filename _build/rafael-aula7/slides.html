Grammar Recap

Polite Imperatives

FunctionEmail phrase
Send a file"Please find the report attached."
Invite questions"Please let me know if you have questions."
Polite request"Could you send me the contract?"
Formal > informal"I am writing to..." (not "Hey, quick thing")
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I wait your reply."
"I look forward to your reply."
"I send you the file in attachment."
"Please find the file attached."

Use fixed email phrases — not word-for-word translation.

Grammar Practice

Spot the Error

Find the Mistake

"I wait your answer soon."
"I look forward to your reply." (fixed email phrase)
"Please to send me the contract."
"Please send me the contract." (no 'to' after please + imperative)
"Dear Sarah, can you send the file? Thx."
"Dear Ms. Collins, could you send the file? Best regards." (formal register)
"I forward you the email to my team."
"I will forward the email to my team." (will + base verb)
