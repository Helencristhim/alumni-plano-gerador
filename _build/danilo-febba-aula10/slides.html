Chapter 3

The Polite Code

Can / Could you...? for requests

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Can you help me? / Could you help me, please?

Could you send me the report? / Can you lend me a pen?

Sure, of course. / I'm sorry, I can't right now.

Could you check this email? / Can you do me a favor?

FunctionStructureExample
RequestCan you + base verb?Can you help me?
Polite requestCould you + base verb?Could you send it?
Softer... , please?Could you help me, please?
Say yesSure. / Of course.Sure, no problem.
Say no (polite)I'm sorry, I can't right now.I'm sorry, I can't right now.

Could is more polite than Can. After both, use the base verb: no -s, no "to". Can you help (NOT Can you to help, NOT Could you helps).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Can you to help me?"

✓ RIGHT

"Can you help me?"

✗ WRONG

"Could you sends the report?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you send the report?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you can help me?"

✓ RIGHT

"Can you help me?"

After Can / Could, use the base verb: no -s, no "to". Start the question with Can or Could, never with "do".

Chapter 4

In Action

A busy morning at the office

Dialogue

The Favor at the Office

D
Hi Rachel, can you help me for a minute?
R
Sure, Danilo. What do you need?
D
Could you send me the sales report, please? I can't find it.
R
Of course. I can send it now. Anything else?
D
Yes. Could you also lend me your charger? My laptop is almost dead.
R
I'm sorry, I can't right now. I am using it. Maybe in ten minutes?
D
No problem. And can you check this email before I send it?
R
Hmm, I am a little busy now. Can you ask Marcus? He is available.
D
Good idea. Thank you for your help, Rachel.
R
You're welcome. Just do me a favor: send me the final file later.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Rachel send to Danilo?
The sales report.
2. Why can't Rachel lend her charger?
She is using it on her own laptop.
3. Who does Rachel suggest for the email, and why?
Marcus, because he is available.
