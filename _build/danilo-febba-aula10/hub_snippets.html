Polite Requests at Work -- Asking for Help

Peça ajuda educadamente no trabalho usando Can / Could you...? Palavras-chave: favor, lend, borrow, spare, moment, send, available, busy. Gramática: Can you...? / Could you...? para pedidos (Could é mais educado), + please, e respostas (Sure, of course / I'm sorry, I can't right now).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. (Escute cada palavra e leia a tradução.)

Favor -- something kind you do for a person (um favor)
"Can you do me a favor?"
Lend -- to give a thing to someone for a short time (emprestar para alguém)
"Could you lend me your charger?"
Borrow -- to take a thing from someone for a short time (pegar emprestado)
"Can I borrow your pen?"
Spare -- extra, free, and not in use (de sobra / extra)
"Do you have a spare minute?"
Moment -- a very short time (um momento / instante)
"Do you have a moment?"
Send -- to make something go to a person (enviar / mandar)
"Could you send me the report?"
Available -- free and ready to help (disponível)
"Are you available now?"
Busy -- having a lot to do (ocupado)
"I am busy right now."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas. (Repare em Can you / Could you e nas respostas educadas.)

Danilo works in a busy office. Every day he asks his colleagues for help. When he needs a small thing, he says "Can you help me?" When he wants to be more polite, he says "Could you send me the report, please?" His colleague Rachel is often busy, but she is friendly. When she is available, she says "Sure, of course." When she can't help, she says "I'm sorry, I can't right now." Yesterday, Danilo asked Rachel: "Could you lend me your charger?" Rachel had a spare one, so she said yes. A small favor can make a big difference.

1. Qual frase é MAIS educada para pedir algo?
A "Can you send me the report?"
B "Could you send me the report, please?"
C "Send me the report."
2. Como a Rachel diz NÃO de forma educada?
A "I'm sorry, I can't right now."
B "No."
C "I don't can help you."
3. Qual frase está correta?
A "Could you to lend me your charger?"
B "Could you lends me your charger?"
C "Could you lend me your charger?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Can / Could You for Requests

GRAMMAR

Use Can you...? para pedir algo e Could you...? para pedir de forma mais educada. Depois de can/could, sempre o verbo na forma base.

FunctionEstrutura (PT)Example
RequestCan + you + verbo base?Can you help me?
Polite requestCould + you + verbo base? (mais educado)Could you send it?
Softer... , please? (mais suave)Could you help me, please?
Say yesdiz simSure. / Of course.
Say no (polite)diz não com educaçãoI'm sorry, I can't right now.

Atenção (erro comum): Depois de can/could, use o verbo na forma base: "Could you send" (não "Could you sends", não "Could you to send"). A pergunta começa com Can/Could, sem "do": "Can you help me?" (não "Do you can help me?"). E cuidado: você lend (empresta) algo PARA alguém, e borrow (pega emprestado) algo DE alguém.
