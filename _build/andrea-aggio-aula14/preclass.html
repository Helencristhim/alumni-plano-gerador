Aula 14 -- Pre-class

Talking About Trends and the Future of Tourism

How to discuss trends and forecast the future with the future continuous and future perfect. Key words: trend, forecast, emerging, sustainable, demand, to boom, shift, eco-friendly, to predict, mainstream, niche, wellness tourism. Structure: future continuous (will be + -ing) vs future perfect (will have + past participle).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Trend -- a general direction in which things are changing (tendencia)
"Wellness travel is a growing trend."
Forecast -- a statement about what will probably happen (previsao)
"The forecast shows strong demand next year."
Emerging -- starting to develop and become important (emergente)
"Vietnam is an emerging destination."
Sustainable -- able to continue without harming the environment (sustentavel)
"Travelers want more sustainable options."
Demand -- how much people want to buy something (demanda, procura)
"Demand for eco-tours is rising fast."
To boom -- to grow or succeed very quickly (crescer explosivamente)
"Adventure travel is booming."
Shift -- a change in position, direction, or focus (mudanca, virada)
"There is a shift toward local experiences."
Eco-friendly -- not harmful to the environment (ecologico)
"We now offer eco-friendly hotels."
To predict -- to say what you think will happen (prever)
"Experts predict a record season."
Mainstream -- accepted by or common among most people (predominante, comum)
"Remote work has gone mainstream."
Niche -- a small, specialized part of the market (nicho)
"Luxury safaris are a profitable niche."
Wellness tourism -- travel focused on health and well-being (turismo de bem-estar)
"Wellness tourism is our fastest-growing segment."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A journalist asks Andrea for her forecast. 'The biggest trend is sustainability,' she says. 'By 2030, most travelers will have chosen an eco-friendly trip, and demand will still be growing.' She explains that wellness tourism is booming: 'This time next year, we will be selling more retreats than beach packages.' What was a niche is going mainstream, and emerging destinations will have taken a big share. 'Companies that predict this shift early,' she adds, 'will be the ones leading the industry.'

1. 'By 2030, demand will have doubled' uses which form, and why?
A Future perfect -- the action is finished before 2030.
B Future continuous -- the action is in progress in 2030.
C Present simple -- it is a fact now.
2. Which signpost goes with the future continuous?
A by 2030
B this time next year
C last year
3. Which sentence is correct?
A This time next year, we will be running eco-tours.
B This time next year, we will run eco-tours.
C This time next year, we will have run eco-tours.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Continuous & Future Perfect

GRAMMAR

Como descrever o futuro com precisao (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
Future continuouswill be + -ing: em andamento num ponto futuro. in progress at a future point.This time next year, we will be running tours.
Future perfectwill have + participio: concluido antes de um ponto futuro. finished before a future point.By 2030, demand will have doubled.
Marcadores / Signposts'by + data' = perfect; 'this time next year' = continuous.By then, it will have changed.
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleescolha a forma pelo marcador de tempo. let the time signpost decide.By 2030 (perfect) vs next year (continuous).
