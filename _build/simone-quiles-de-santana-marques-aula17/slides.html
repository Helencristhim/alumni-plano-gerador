Chapter 3 -- Verb Patterns

-ing or to

The verbs a chair uses to lead the meeting

Listen and Notice

What Do They Share?

Look at the highlighted words. What form of the verb comes after each one?

"I suggest moving on to the next item."

"I propose to vote on the proposal now."

"Let's avoid going over time on this point."

"The team agreed to allocate ten minutes to each item."

The Verb Pattern Code

Verb Patterns

VerbPatternExample
suggest / recommend+ verb-ingI suggest moving on
avoid / consider+ verb-ingavoid going over time
propose / decide+ to + verbI propose to vote
agree / offer / need+ to + verbwe agreed to allocate

Key idea: some verbs take the -ing form (suggest, recommend, avoid, consider), and some take to + verb (propose, decide, agree, offer, need). It is the verb that decides the pattern, not the meaning.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I suggest to move on. ... I propose voting now."
"I suggest moving on. ... I propose to vote now."

After suggest use -ing (moving), and after propose use to + verb (to vote).

Practice

Choose the Form

"I suggest _____summarizing the points before we vote." (suggest + -ing)
"I propose _____to allocate ten minutes to each item." (propose + to)
"Let's avoid _____going over time on this topic." (avoid + -ing)
"The attendees agreed _____to proceed to the next item." (agree + to)

The objective is to approve the project plan and the budget. We have apologies from two attendees. Simone facilitates.

2 -- The discussion

We allocate ten minutes to each item. After the plan, we proceed to the budget. The chair invites each attendee to speak in turn.

3 -- Closing

The chair summarizes the decisions and confirms the next steps. If there is nothing else, we adjourn.

Dialogue

Chairing the Project Meeting

S

"Good morning, everyone. I will facilitate today. The objective is to approve the plan. We have apologies from two attendees."

P

"Thank you, Simone. I propose to vote on the plan at the end, after we hear from everyone."

S

"Good idea. Let's allocate ten minutes to each item, and let's avoid going over time. I suggest moving on to the plan now."

P

"The plan looks strong. I recommend keeping the same timeline, but we need to allocate more budget to the second phase."

S

"Noted. We all agreed to proceed with the plan and add budget to phase two. Let me summarize the decisions before we close."

P

"Clear summary. If there is nothing else, you can adjourn. Well chaired, Simone."
