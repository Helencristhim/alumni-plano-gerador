Lesson 17 -- Leading a Meeting

Series
Industry -- Season 1, Episode 4 "Sesh"

Watch how a senior banker chairs a tense team meeting: stating the objective, allocating time, inviting each attendee to speak and proposing a vote. Listen for the verb patterns a chair uses: "I suggest moving on", "I propose to vote", "let's avoid going over time".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences the chair uses to manage the meeting, and mark whether each verb is followed by -ing or by to.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Chairing a Meeting: Opening, Managing and Closing"

A practical episode on how to lead a meeting in English: state the objective, note the apologies, allocate time to each item, keep speakers on track, propose a vote, summarize the decisions and adjourn. Exactly the language you need to chair the project and M&A meetings at the office.

Note 5 chairing phrases the hosts use, and underline the verb pattern in each one (-ing or to).

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Verbs followed by -ing or to + infinitive"

A short, clear lesson on the verb patterns from today: which verbs take the -ing form (suggest, recommend, avoid) and which take to + verb (propose, decide, agree). Directly useful for leading a meeting with confidence.

After watching, write five chairing sentences (open, allocate, suggest, propose, adjourn), then record yourself reading them with at least four new words from today.
