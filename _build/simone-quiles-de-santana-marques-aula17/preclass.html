Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Leading a Meeting -- Chairing & Verb Patterns

Learn the language of chairing a meeting: open it, manage the agenda and the speakers, and close it. New vocabulary: facilitate, objective, allocate, attendee, apologies, proceed, summarize, adjourn. Grammar: verb patterns -- verb + -ing (I suggest moving on) vs verb + to (I propose to vote).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Facilitate -- to guide a meeting so it runs smoothly and stays on track
"I will facilitate today's meeting." (Conduzir / facilitar)
Objective -- the clear goal a meeting is meant to reach
"The objective today is to approve the budget." (Objetivo)
Allocate -- to give a set amount of time or resources to something
"Let's allocate ten minutes to each topic." (Alocar / reservar)
Attendee -- a person who takes part in a meeting
"Every attendee will give a short update." (Participante)
Apologies -- a note of the people who could not come to the meeting
"We have apologies from two attendees today." (Ju­stificativas de ausência)
Proceed -- to move forward to the next item or stage
"Let's proceed to the next item." (Prosseguir)
Summarize -- to give a short version of the main points
"Let me summarize the decisions we made." (Resumir)
Adjourn -- to formally close or pause a meeting
"If there is nothing else, we will adjourn." (Encerrar / suspender)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Verb Patterns

GRAMMAR

Alguns verbos são seguidos pelo verbo com -ing (suggest, recommend, avoid, consider) e outros por to + verbo (propose, decide, agree, offer, need). É o verbo que decide o padrão, não o significado. Diga "I suggest moving on" (não "to move") e "I propose to vote" (não "voting"). / Some verbs are followed by the -ing form (suggest, recommend, avoid, consider), and some by to + verb (propose, decide, agree, offer, need). The verb decides the pattern, not the meaning. Say "I suggest moving on" (not "to move") and "I propose to vote" (not "voting").

Verb / VerboPattern / PadrãoExample / Exemplo
suggest / recommend+ verb-ingI suggest moving on.
avoid / consider+ verb-ingLet's avoid going over time.
propose / decide+ to + verbI propose to vote now.
agree / offer / need+ to + verbWe agreed to allocate more time.
