The Rule

Directions & Prepositions

TypeWordsExample
DirectionGo straight / Turn left / CrossGo straight, then turn right.
Placenext to / across fromThe cafe is next to the bank.
Placebetween / on the cornerIt is between the church and the museum.

Key idea: a direction is a command -- it starts with the verb: Go straight, Turn left, Cross the street. No "you", no "to". Use a preposition to say WHERE: next to, across from, between, on the corner, near.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You turn to left at the corner."
"Turn left at the corner."

A direction is a command: start with the verb -- Turn left, Go straight. No "you" and no "to" before left or right.

Key Phrase

The First Question

"Excuse me, how do I get to the square?"

Start with "Excuse me". Then ask the way: "How do I get to the museum?" or "Where is the fountain?"

Make It Yours

Give a Direction

Give simple directions to a place you know.

Make two directions and say where the place is. For example: "Go straight for one block" and "The cafe is next to the bank."
