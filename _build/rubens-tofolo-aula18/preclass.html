Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Reservation -- a room you booked in advance, kept under your name
"I have a reservation under the name Tofolo." (Reserva)
Front desk -- the reception counter where staff help you check in
"Please leave your key at the front desk." (Recepção)
Lobby -- the large open area near the hotel entrance, with seats
"I will wait for you in the lobby." (Saguão)
Checkout -- the time when you must leave the room and pay any extra
"Checkout is at noon, but I asked for a later time." (Saída / hora de deixar o quarto)
Amenities -- the extra services a hotel offers, such as wifi, gym and pool
"The amenities include free wifi and breakfast." (Comodidades)
Wake-up call -- a phone call from the front desk to wake you at a set time
"Could I have a wake-up call at six, please?" (Chamada para acordar)
Housekeeping -- the hotel team that cleans the rooms and brings fresh towels
"Housekeeping comes to clean the room at ten." (Serviço de limpeza)
Deposit -- money you leave in advance for extras, usually returned at checkout
"The hotel asked for a deposit on my card." (Caução / depósito)
Upgrade -- a better room or service than the one you originally paid for
"They gave me a free upgrade to a suite." (Melhoria de categoria)
Vacancy -- a free room that the hotel still has available
"The sign said 'No vacancy', so the hotel was full." (Vaga / quarto disponível)
Complaint -- when you politely tell staff that something is wrong
"I made a complaint because the air conditioning was broken." (Reclamação)
Voucher -- a paper or code that you exchange for a service, like breakfast
"Here is your voucher for free breakfast." (Voucher / vale)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rubens arrives at his hotel after a long flight. At the front desk, he says, "I have a reservation under the name Tofolo. I would like a quiet room on a high floor, please." The receptionist gives him room eight twelve and takes a small deposit on his card. Rubens has an early meeting, so he asks, "Would you mind giving me a wake-up call at six?" The receptionist says, "Not at all." She also gives him a breakfast voucher and tells him that checkout is at noon. Before he goes up, Rubens adds, "Would you mind sending some extra towels to my room?"

1. How does Rubens say what kind of room he wants?
A He says, "I would like a quiet room on a high floor."
B He demands a room without asking.
C He does not say what he wants.
2. How does Rubens ask for favors at the front desk?

B He uses "Would you mind giving..." and "Would you mind sending..".
C He gives orders with no please.
3. What does the receptionist tell him?
A The hotel is full.
B Breakfast has a voucher and checkout is at noon.
C There is no wake-up call service.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- I would like... / Would you mind + -ing

GRAMMAR

Para dizer educadamente o que você quer, use I would like (forma curta I'd like) + substantivo, ou + to + verbo na base. É mais educado que I want. Para pedir um favor a alguém, use Would you mind + verbo no -ing. / To say politely what you want, use I would like (short form I'd like) + a noun, or + to + base verb. It is more polite than I want. To ask someone for a favor, use Would you mind + the -ing form.

Form / FormaExample / Exemplo
Want a thing: I would like + nounI would like a quiet room, please. / Eu gostaria de um quarto silencioso, por favor.
Want to do: I'd like + to + base verbI'd like to check in early, please. / Eu gostaria de fazer o check-in mais cedo, por favor.
Ask a favor: Would you mind + verb-ing?Would you mind giving me a wake-up call? / Você se importaria de me dar uma chamada para acordar?
Ask a favor: Would you mind + verb-ing?Would you mind sending housekeeping up? / Você se importaria de mandar a limpeza subir?

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de I would like, use um substantivo ou to + verbo na base (nunca "I would like check in"). Depois de Would you mind, use sempre o verbo no -ing (giving, sending -- nunca "to give").
