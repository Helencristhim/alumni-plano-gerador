Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Implementation -- the act of putting a plan into real action
"The implementation of each phase brought good progress."
Expansion -- the process of growing bigger or reaching more places
"Right now, we are in the expansion phase."
Framework -- the basic structure of ideas that supports a plan
"We started with a small framework and a clear scope."
Scope -- how big a project is and what it includes
"At first, the scope was small: only three cities."
Timeline -- the order of dates and steps in a project
"We followed the timeline step by step."
Outcome -- the final result of a project or action
"The first outcome was strong."
Phase -- one part or stage of a longer project
"Each phase brought good progress."
Progress -- movement forward toward a goal
"Each phase brought good progress."
Launch -- the official start of a project or product
"Our goal is a national launch by 2027."
Update -- new information about how a project is going
"We share a monthly update with our partners."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Flavia tells a partner the story of a project. "We started this project in 2020, with a small framework and a clear scope." Over time, the project grew. She followed the timeline, and the implementation of each phase brought good progress. The first outcome was strong. "Right now, we are in the expansion phase, and we share a monthly update," she says. "Our goal is a national launch by 2027."

C The current status.

A the past.
B the current status.
C a place far away.
3. Which marker introduces a future goal?
A At first.
B Over time.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Sequencing a Project Story

GRAMMAR

How to order the story of a project in time, with clear markers.

StageMarkerSay
Beginningstarted / at first"We started the project in 2020."
Developmentover time / then"Over time, the project grew."
Nowright now / currently"Right now, we are in the expansion phase."
Goalour goal is"Our goal is a national launch."

Use the past (started, grew) for the beginning and development. Use the present (right now, we are growing) for the current status, and our goal is for the future.
