Chapter 4: The Code

The First Conditional

if + present simple, ... will + base verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If demand rises, we will expand our capacity."

"If a competitor enters, we will compete on quality."

"Unless the market collapses, we will keep investing."

"Last year, demand rose and we expanded the plant."

Three sentences share a structure. One is different. Which one, and why?

PartFormExample
if / when + conditionpresent simple (NOT "will")If demand rises...
resultwill + base verb... we will expand.
unless= "if not" (do NOT add another "not")Unless we act, they will win.
may / mightuse in the result if it is only possibleIf demand cools, we might postpone.
Language Focus

Which Form?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If demand will rise, we will expand."

✓ RIGHT

"If demand rises, we will expand."

✗ WRONG

"Unless we don't act, a competitor will win."

✓ RIGHT

"Unless we act, a competitor will win."

✗ WRONG

"If a downturn hits, we cut costs and postpone."

✓ RIGHT

"If a downturn hits, we will cut costs and postpone."

In the first conditional, the "if" part stays in the present, and the result uses "will". "Unless" already means "if not", so never add a second "not".

Dialogue

Talking the Board Through the Scenarios

A
Izabel, I have read the market outlook. It looks strong but uncertain. What is our plan if things change?
I
Good question, Andy. If demand keeps rising, we will expand our capacity and break into North America faster.
A
And if a bigger competitor enters the segment?
I
If a big player moves in, we will not compete on price. We will compete on quality and lead time, where we are stronger.
A
That makes sense. The board worries about a downturn. What happens then?
I
If a downturn hits, we have a contingency: costs will be cut, and the launch will be postponed, not cancelled. Unless the whole market collapses, we will keep investing in research.
A
Good. And how do you plan to reduce the overall risk?
I
We will diversify. As long as the core sensor line stays strong, we will add two new product families and grow our market share step by step.
