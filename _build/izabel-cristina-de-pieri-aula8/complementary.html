Aula 8 -- The Market Outlook -- Conditions, Scenarios and the First Conditional

Film
The Big Short (2015)

A sharp, funny film about a volatile market and the people who read the outlook before everyone else. Listen for scenario language everywhere: "If the housing market falls, this will happen," "Unless something changes, the whole thing collapses." It is the first conditional applied to real markets, risk, and downturns.

Tip: watch one scene where an analyst explains a scenario. Write down five sentences with "if", "unless", or "as long as", and mark the if-part (present) and the result (will).

Podcast
Planet Money (NPR)

A short, clear economics podcast about demand, competition, downturns, and the scenarios that shape a market. Direct connection to Lesson 8: hear real analysts say what will happen if demand surges, if a competitor moves in, or if the economy slows -- the first conditional in its natural home.

Tip: pick one episode about a market or a company. Write down five "if / unless" predictions the hosts make, then rewrite them as scenarios for AVRT.

YouTube
"The First Conditional -- Conditionals with Georgie" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on the first conditional: if + present simple for a real future condition, and will + base for the result. Exactly the code you practiced in Lesson 8 -- and why "if demand will rise" should be "if demand rises".

Tip: pause after each example and say your own version about AVRT. Then record a 90-second market outlook using "if", "unless", and "as long as".
