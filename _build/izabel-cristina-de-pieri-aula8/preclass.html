Aula 08 -- Pre-class

The Market Outlook -- Conditions, Scenarios and the First Conditional

Reading a market outlook and planning for each scenario. Key words: demand, competitor, capacity, margin, downturn, surge, volatile, diversify, disruption, break into, lead time, market share. Structure: the first conditional -- if + present simple, ... will + base verb; plus "unless" (= if not) and "as long as".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Demand -- how much customers want to buy a product (demanda / procura)
"Last class you shared your roadmap; now the market matters. Demand can surge in one quarter and cool the next."
Competitor -- a company that sells similar products in the same market (concorrente)
"A larger competitor may enter the same segment."
Capacity -- the maximum a company can produce (capacidade produtiva)
"If demand rises, we will expand our capacity."
Margin -- the profit left after costs (margem de lucro)
"If costs go up, our margin will fall."
Downturn -- a period when business or the economy gets weaker (queda / retração)
"If a downturn hits, costs will be cut."
Surge -- a sudden large increase (disparada / forte aumento)
"A surge in demand can double our orders overnight."
Volatile -- changing quickly and unpredictably (volátil / instável)
"The market is strong but volatile."
Diversify -- to add new products or markets to reduce risk (diversificar)
"We will diversify to reduce risk."
Disruption -- a major change that interrupts the normal way of doing things (disrupção / ruptura)
"A new competitor could be a real disruption."
Break into -- to enter a new market successfully (entrar em um novo mercado)
"If demand rises, we will break into North America."
Lead time -- the time between ordering and delivering (prazo de entrega)
"We compete on quality and lead time, not price."
Market share -- the portion of a market that a company controls (participação de mercado)
"We will grow our market share step by step."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the Thursday call, Izabel presents the market outlook. It is strong but volatile, so she has a plan for each scenario. "If demand keeps rising, we will expand our capacity and break into North America," she says. "If a big competitor enters, we will not compete on price; we will compete on quality and lead time." On risk, she is direct: "If a downturn hits, costs will be cut and the launch will be postponed, not cancelled. Unless the market collapses, we will keep investing in research." And on growth: "As long as the core line stays strong, we will diversify and grow our market share."

1. Why is it "if demand keeps rising" and not "if demand will keep rising"?
A Because "demand" cannot take "will".
B In the first conditional, the "if" part uses the present simple, never "will".
C Because the sentence is about the past.
2. What does "Unless the market collapses" mean?
A "If the market does not collapse" -- "unless" already means "if not".
B "Because the market collapses."
C "After the market collapses."
3. In "if a downturn hits, costs will be cut," which part is the result?
A "if a downturn hits" (present simple).
B "costs will be cut" -- the result uses "will".
C Both parts use "will".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Talking about a real future that depends on a condition (explicação em inglês e português).

PartForm / UsoExample
if / when + condiçãoPresent simple — NUNCA "will" depois de "if"If demand rises...
resultadowill + verbo base — a consequência futura... we will expand.
unless= "if not" (não use outra negação junto)Unless we act, they win.
may / mightUse no resultado quando é só possível: If demand cools, we might postpone the launch.

Quiz

Which sentence uses "unless" correctly?
A "Unless we act now, a competitor will break into the market first."
