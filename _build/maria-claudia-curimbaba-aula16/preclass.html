Aula 16 -- Pre-class

Contracts & Agreements -- Legal English

Read and discuss a contract like a lawyer. Use the legal modals shall for obligations (each party shall deliver the goods), must for requirements (the warranty must cover twelve months), and may for permissions (either party may terminate), with shall not / must not / may not for prohibitions, and keep the passive voice for every term (the goods shall be delivered; confidential information must not be disclosed). New vocabulary: indemnity, counterparty, governing law, jurisdiction, remedy, default, covenant, stipulate, confidentiality, force majeure, hereby, in good faith.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Indemnity -- a contractual promise to compensate the other party for a loss
"Under the indemnity clause, the supplier shall cover any third-party claim." (indenização / ressarcimento)
Counterparty -- the other party to a contract, the side you sign with
"As the counterparty, we shall deliver within thirty days." (contraparte / a outra parte)
Governing law -- the body of law that controls and interprets the contract
"The governing law is the law of New York." (lei aplicável / legislação regente)
Jurisdiction -- the place whose courts have legal authority over a dispute
"Any dispute shall be settled in the courts of that jurisdiction." (jurisdição / foro)
Remedy -- the legal solution available when a contract term is broken
"If the supplier fails, the buyer has a clear remedy." (remédio jurídico / reparação)
Default -- a failure to meet an obligation set out in the contract
"If delivery is prevented, neither party shall be in default." (inadimplemento / descumprimento)
Covenant -- a formal, binding promise to do or not to do something
"Each side accepts a covenant to act in good faith." (pacto / compromisso formal)
Stipulate -- to state a condition or requirement formally in a contract
"The agreement stipulates that the warranty must cover twelve months." (estipular / determinar)
Confidentiality -- the duty to keep shared information private and undisclosed
"Confidentiality is essential: the data must not be disclosed." (confidencialidade / sigilo)
Force majeure -- unforeseeable events beyond control that excuse a party from performing
"Under the force majeure clause, neither party is in default." (força maior / caso fortuito)
Hereby -- by means of this document or statement (a formal, legal word)
"The parties hereby agree to the terms of this agreement." (pela presente / por este meio)
In good faith -- honestly, fairly and with sincere intention to keep the agreement
"Each party shall act in good faith at all times." (de boa-fé)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before I sign, I read every clause of the Supply Agreement with my lawyer. The obligations use shall: each party shall deliver the goods within thirty days, and the counterparty shall act in good faith. The requirements use must: the agreement stipulates that the warranty must cover twelve months, and, for confidentiality, the data must not be disclosed. The permissions use may: either party may terminate with sixty days notice, and either party may request an audit. The governing law is the law of New York, and any dispute shall be settled in that jurisdiction. There is a force majeure clause too, so if delivery is prevented, neither party is in default and there is a clear remedy. The parties hereby agree to the terms.

1. How is the delivery obligation stated?
A "Each party shall deliver the goods" -- shall + base verb (obligation).
B "Each party shall to deliver the goods."
C "Each party may deliver the goods."
2. Why does "either party may terminate" use "may"?
A Because it is a firm duty.
B Because it is a permission -- a right, not an obligation.
C Because it is a prohibition.
3. How is the confidentiality term written -- "the data ___"?
A "must not be disclosed" -- passive: modal + be + past participle.
B "must not disclosed" -- with no "be".
C "may not disclose".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Shall, Must & May + the Passive

GRAMMAR

Em inglês jurídico, cada tipo de regra tem seu modal. Shall + verbo base = obrigação ("each party shall deliver"). Must + verbo base = requisito ("the warranty must cover twelve months"). May + verbo base = permissão ("either party may terminate"). Shall not / must not / may not = proibição. Sempre MODAL + verbo na BASE, nunca "shall to deliver". E para escrever o termo em si, use a voz passiva: modal + be + partícipio ("the goods shall be delivered", "the data must not be disclosed"). / Shall = obligation, must = requirement, may = permission, and the passive for every term.

Modal / functionFormExample
shall (obligation)shall + base verb"each party shall deliver the goods"
must (requirement)must + base verb"the warranty must cover twelve months"
may (permission)may + base verb"either party may terminate"
Write the term (passive)modal + be + past participle"the goods shall be delivered"

Key point / Ponto-chave: o modal carrega a regra, e a passiva mantém o foco no termo. A passiva SEMPRE precisa do BE: "the goods shall be delivered", "the data must not be disclosed", nunca "shall delivered" / "must not disclosed". E o modal sempre vem com o verbo na base: "shall deliver", nunca "shall to deliver". / The passive always needs "be", and a modal always takes the base verb.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You want to state a firm obligation to deliver. Which is best?
A "Each party shall deliver the goods within thirty days."
B "Each party shall to deliver the goods within thirty days."
C "Each party may deliver the goods within thirty days."
You want to state a term in the passive, the way a contract does. How?
A "The goods shall delivered within thirty days."
B "The goods shall be delivered within thirty days."
C "The goods shall delivering within thirty days."
You want to express a permission, not a duty. Which fits?
A "Either party may request an audit once a year."
B "Either party shall request an audit once a year."
C "Either party must to request an audit once a year."
