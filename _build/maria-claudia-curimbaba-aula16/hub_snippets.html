Contracts and Agreements -- Legal English

Read and discuss contract clauses with the three legal modals: shall for an obligation (each party shall deliver), must for a requirement (the warranty must cover twelve months), and may for a permission (either party may terminate). New vocabulary: obligation, binding, breach, provision, terminate, warranty, indemnify, waive, enforce, amend.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Obligation -- a duty you are legally or formally required to perform
"Each party has an obligation to deliver on time." (Obrigação / dever)
Binding -- legally enforceable; both sides are tied to its terms
"The contract is binding once both parties sign it." (Vinculante / obrigatório)
Breach -- a failure to follow a term of the contract
"A serious breach lets the other party terminate the deal." (Violação / quebra)
Provision -- a single clause or condition written in the agreement
"Please read every provision carefully before you sign." (Cláusula / disposição)
Terminate -- to bring a contract to an end
"Either party may terminate with sixty days notice." (Rescindir / encerrar)
Warranty -- a formal promise to repair or replace if something fails
"The warranty must cover defects for twelve months." (Garantia)
Indemnify -- to protect another party by paying for their loss or damage
"The supplier shall indemnify the buyer against third-party claims." (Indenizar)
Waive -- to choose not to use or claim a right you have
"The buyer may waive the inspection if it trusts the supplier." (Renunciar / abrir mão)
Enforce -- to make sure the terms of a contract are obeyed, by law if needed
"A court can enforce a binding agreement." (Fazer cumprir / executar)
Amend -- to make a formal change to a clause or document
"If they want to amend a provision, we negotiate it before signing." (Emendar / alterar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The new supply agreement is short but firm. Each party shall deliver its part on time, and the supplier must meet the quality standards in the annex -- these are clear obligations. The buyer may inspect the goods before payment, or it may waive that right -- inspection is only a permission. The warranty must cover defects for twelve months, and the supplier shall indemnify the buyer against third-party claims. If one side commits a serious breach, the other may terminate immediately. Notice the pattern: shall and must mark duties, while may marks a right; the contract becomes binding the moment both parties sign it.

1. Why does the text use "each party shall deliver on time"?
A It is a firm obligation, a duty each side must perform.
B It is only an option the parties can ignore.
C It describes a finished event in the past.
2. Why does the text say "the buyer may inspect the goods"?
A Because inspection is a strict obligation.
B Because inspection is a permission, a right the buyer can use or waive.
C Because inspection is forbidden.
3. When may a party terminate the contract immediately?
A When the other side commits a serious breach.
B Whenever it wants, with no reason.
C Only after twelve months.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Shall, Must, May

GRAMMAR

Em contratos, use shall e must para DEVERES (o que cada lado tem que fazer) e may para uma PERMISSÃO (o que um lado pode fazer). O modal vem sempre seguido do verbo no infinitivo SEM "to". / In contracts, use shall and must for duties and may for a permission; the modal is always followed by the base verb with no "to".

ModalMeaningExample
shall + verbObligation / formal duty"each party shall deliver on time"
must + verbStrong requirement"the warranty must cover twelve months"
may + verbPermission / right"either party may terminate with notice"
shall not / must not / may notProhibition (not allowed)"the party must not disclose the terms"

Key point / Ponto-chave: teste rápido -- se a ação é forçada (um dever), use shall ou must; se é apenas permitida (um direito), use may. NUNCA coloque "to" depois do modal: "the party must to deliver" → "the party must deliver". / Quick test: if the action is forced, use shall or must; if it is only allowed, use may. Never put "to" after a modal.
