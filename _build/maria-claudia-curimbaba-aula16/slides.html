Chapter 3 -- The Code

Language Discovery

Shall, must, and may

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What is different about the orange modal verbs? Which ones force an action, and which one only allows it?

"Each party shall deliver its part on time."

"The supplier must meet the quality standards."

"Either party may request a review meeting."

"The supplier shall indemnify the buyer, and it may inspect the goods first."

The Rule

Shall, Must, May

ModalMeaningExample
shall + verbObligation / formal duty"each party shall deliver on time"
must + verbStrong requirement / condition"the warranty must cover twelve months"
may + verbPermission / right"either party may terminate with notice"
shall not / must not / may notProhibition (not allowed)"the party must not disclose the terms"

Key idea: the modal is always followed by the base verb with no "to" (shall deliver, must meet, may request). Use shall and must for duties one side has to perform; use may for a right one side is allowed to use. Quick test: if the action is forced, it is shall or must; if it is only allowed, it is may.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The supplier must to meet the quality standards."
"The supplier must meet the quality standards."

A modal (shall, must, may) is always followed by the base verb with no to. And do not mix duty with permission: may = allowed, shall / must = required.

Artefact

Curimbaba Holding Supply Agreement

SUPPLY AGREEMENT

Curimbaba Holding · Houston Industrial Partner

1. Delivery (obligation)

Each party shall deliver its part of the project on the agreed date.

2. Quality (requirement)

The supplier must meet the quality standards in Annex A.

3. Inspection (permission)

The buyer may inspect the goods before payment, or may waive this right.

4. Warranty & indemnity (obligation)

The warranty must cover defects for twelve months; the supplier shall indemnify the buyer against third-party claims.

5. Termination (permission)

Either party may terminate with sixty days notice, or immediately upon a serious breach.

Note: "This agreement is binding once both parties sign it. No provision may be amended without written consent."
