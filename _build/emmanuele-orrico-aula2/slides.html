Chapter 4: The Code

How Often?

always · usually · often · sometimes · never

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I always review the demand plan on Monday."

"My medical reps usually send the reports on Tuesday."

"I sometimes travel to other cities."

"We never schedule meetings on Friday."

The purple words say HOW OFTEN. Where do they sit — before or after the verb?

FormUseExample
I / we / they + always, usually, often, sometimes, never + verbThe small word comes BEFORE the main verb.I always review the demand plan.
he / she / my team + verb + sOne person, one team: the verb takes -s. Same rule as lesson 1.My team usually sends the reports.
Negative: I don't / she doesn't + verbTo say what is NOT in your week.I don't travel on Fridays.
Question: Do / Does + person + verb?To ask the Global team about THEIR week, too.Do you travel every month?
How often?always (100%) · usually · often · sometimes · never (0%). Time phrases go at the end or at the start: every week, on Mondays, twice a month.

Ready phrase, no thinking: "Every week, I..." — start with it and the rest of the sentence comes out.

Language Focus

Always or Sometimes?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I review always the demand plan."
"I always review the demand plan."
"I not travel on Fridays."
"I don't travel on Fridays."
"You travel every month?"
"Do you travel every month?"

The small word goes before the verb. The negative needs don't. And a question starts with Do.
