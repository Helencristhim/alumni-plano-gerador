Lesson 02 -- Your Day and Your Work Routine

Series
Emily in Paris — Season 1, Episode 1 (Netflix)

A marketing executive arrives at a French company and spends the whole episode describing her routine — meetings, presentations, schedule, boss. It is your lesson 2 on screen, and the head office is in Paris, just like Sanofi Global.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences where someone says what they do EVERY week (look for always, usually, every day, every morning). Do not try to understand 100% — the goal is to hear the structure of a routine.

Podcast
Business English Pod — BEP 383: Routine Check-in Meeting

A routine call with the manager: what is in progress, what happened during the week, where the problems are. It is exactly the Monday call from lesson 2, with the ready-made sentences you need when the head office asks about your week.

Tip: listen ONE time on the way to work, with no pause. Then listen again and repeat out loud the update sentences ("This week I am...", "Every Monday we..."). The problem is speaking, not listening.

Finding Pharma — Pharma Sales Representative: Day in the Life

An American pharma rep talks about his own day: schedule, visits, calls, reports. It is the vocabulary of lesson 2 (schedule, field visit, medical rep, reports) said by a person who lives it — and who is on the other side of your field team.

Tip: turn on the English subtitles. Write down 3 words you understood and 2 words you did NOT understand, and bring them to class. Then describe YOUR day out loud, using always / usually / often / sometimes / never.
