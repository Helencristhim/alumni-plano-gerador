Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Colleague -- a person you work with in the same company
"My colleague is handling the new accounts." (Colega)
Supervisor -- the person who manages your work and your team
"My supervisor is leading the shipping plan." (Supervisor / chefe)
Counterpart -- a person with the same role in another company or office
"My counterpart in Geneva is reviewing the contracts." (Equivalente / homólogo)
Department -- a section of a company, such as sales or finance
"He is in charge of the logistics department." (Departamento / setor)
Role -- the function or position a person has in a team
"My role is quality and traceability." (Função / papel)
Teammate -- a member of your own team
"My teammate is handling the new samples." (Colega de equipe)
In charge of -- responsible for, or having control of, something
"She is in charge of the finance team." (Responsável por)
Handle -- to deal with or manage a task or a problem
"Rafael is handling the quarterly report." (Lidar com / cuidar de)
Lead -- to guide or be the head of a team or project
"I am leading the logistics project this quarter." (Liderar)
Launch -- to start a new project or product
"We are launching a new traceability platform." (Lançar)
Set up -- to arrange or organize something, such as a meeting
"He is setting up the meetings with the buyers." (Organizar / marcar)
Reach out -- to contact someone to ask for help or information
"Our buyer is reaching out to roasters in Germany." (Entrar em contato)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is a busy week at the Santos office. Bruno, the supervisor, is leading the shipping plan for Europe. In quality control, Camila is handling the samples from three new origins. In the next room, Rafael is preparing the quarterly report and is setting up the meetings with the buyers. The whole team is launching a new traceability platform, and the junior buyer is reaching out to roasters in Germany. Meanwhile, in Geneva, the counterpart and her analysts are reviewing the new contracts. Everyone is working hard, because the new project starts next month.

1. In "she is handling", which two parts form the present continuous?
A A form of "to be" + the verb-ing (is handling).
B Two nouns next to each other.
C A past participle after "to be".
2. Why does the writer use the present continuous so often here?
A Because these are routines that happen every day.
B To describe what people are doing right now, this week.
C Because the actions are already finished.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "We is launching a new platform."
B "We are launching a new platform."
C "We are launch a new platform."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Usado para ações que acontecem agora ou projetos do momento. / Used for actions happening now or current projects.

Rule / RegraDetail / DetalheExample / Exemplo
am / is / are + verb-ingForma do present continuousShe is leading the project.
Happening nowAção em progresso neste momentoWe are launching a platform.
Current projectsUse com now, this week, at the momentThis week he is setting up the calls.
Spelling of -ingmake→making, run→runningI am preparing the report.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o present continuous usa to be + verb-ing. Nunca "she handling" -- diga "she is handling".
