YouTube
"Present Perfect with FOR and SINCE" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: the present perfect for results that are still true now, plus when to use "since" (a point in time) and "for" (a period). It shows the classic mistake of mixing them up. Connection to Lesson 19: this is the structure you use to demonstrate value and justify a renewal.

Tip: pause and build 5 sentences about a real result ("We've grown... since...", "The team has saved... for..."). Then say one line that justifies a renewal out loud.
