Aula 19 -- Pre-class

Renewals & Driving Value

Demonstrating the value you've delivered, justifying a contract renewal, and proposing an upsell. Key words: renewal, contract, ROI, demonstrate, growth, upsell, retain, justify, partnership, track record. Structure: present perfect for results (have/has + past participle, since/for) and persuasion (that's why / this means).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Renewal -- continuing a contract for another period (renovação)
"The contract renewal is coming up next month."
Contract -- a formal written agreement between two sides (contrato)
"Let's review the contract before we renew."
ROI -- return on investment; the value you get back (retorno sobre o investimento)
"The ROI on this partnership is clear."
Demonstrate -- to show clearly that something is true (demonstrar)
"Let me demonstrate the value we've delivered."
Growth -- an increase in size, number, or value (crescimento)
"Adoption growth has been strong this year."
Upsell -- to offer a customer a higher or extra plan (venda adicional)
"After we show value, we can suggest an upsell."
Retain -- to keep a customer with you (reter)
"Our goal is to retain happy customers."
Justify -- to give a good reason for something (justificar)
"These results justify the renewal."
Partnership -- a relationship where two sides work together (parceria)
"This partnership has delivered real value."
Track record -- a history of past results and performance (histórico de resultados)
"We have a strong track record of results."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The contract was up for renewal, and David's finance team wanted proof. So Patrícia led with results. "We have grown adoption by forty percent since January," she said, "and your team has saved many hours." The partnership has delivered real value for six months. She used the present perfect because the results were still true today. The ROI was clear, and the track record spoke for itself — that was enough to justify the renewal. David was ready to renew. Then, because growth had been so strong, Patrícia suggested an upsell to help him retain that momentum.

1. Por que Patrícia diz "We have grown adoption since January" no present perfect?
A Porque o resultado terminou e não vale mais.
B Porque é um resultado que continua verdadeiro hoje.
C Porque é um plano para o futuro.
2. Na frase "the partnership has delivered value for six months", usamos "for" porque:
A "for" indica um período de tempo (seis meses).
B "for" indica um ponto no tempo (janeiro).
C "for" só aparece no passado simples.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "We grew adoption by 40% since January."
B "We've grown adoption by 40% since January."
C "We've grown adoption since six months."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect for Results

GRAMMAR

Como mostrar um resultado que ainda é verdadeiro hoje e transformá-lo em persuasão (explicação em inglês e português).

StructureUse / UsoExample
have / has + past participleResultado ainda verdadeiro agora. A result still true now.We have grown adoption.
since + ponto no tempoQuando o resultado começou. When it started....since January.
for + período de tempoHá quanto tempo dura. How long it has lasted....for six months.
That's why... / This means...Transforma resultado em persuasão. Turns a result into persuasion.The ROI is clear. That's why renewing makes sense.
Avoid / EviteNão use o passado simples com "since" ("we grew... since January") → troque por "we've grown... since January". E não use o present perfect com tempo terminado ("last quarter") → use o passado simples ali.
