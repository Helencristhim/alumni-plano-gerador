Chapter 3: The Bridge

This Time, English Is the Same

“Eu vou” → “I'm going to”. Palavra por palavra.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

O que vem logo depois de going to?

I'm going to travel next month.

She's going to learn the system.

Greg is going to visit São Paulo.

We're going to change the plan.

Depois de going to vem sempre um verbo pelado: travel, learn, visit, change. E em português? “Eu vou viajar”…

Em portuguêsEm inglês
Eu vou viajar.I'm going to travel.
Ela vai aprender.She's going to learn.
Nós vamos mudar.We're going to change.
A regra em uma linha: going to = vou / vai / vamos. E o verbo vem pelado depois, nos dois idiomas: vou VIAJAR → going to TRAVEL. A única peça a mais em inglês é o am / is / are na frente (I'm, she's, we're) — e você já tem isso desde a aula 2.
The Three Forms

Yes, No, and Question

AFIRMATIVO

"I'm going to travel."

"She's going to learn."

Eu vou. Ela vai.

NEGATIVO → NOT

"I'm not going to change."

"We're not going to wait."

O not da aula 2, encaixado.

PERGUNTA → ARE YOU

"Are you going to travel?"

"What are you going to do?"

O are pula pra frente.

Tudo isto você já tem. O going to é a peça nova — e ela é vou. O resto é a aula 2: o am / is / are (I'm, she's, we're), o not pra negar, e o are pulando pra frente pra perguntar (igual a “Are you busy?”). Nenhuma peça nova além do going to.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She going to study English."
"She's going to study English."
"I'm going to changed my job."
"I'm going to change my job."

O primeiro erro é comer o is: em português “ela vai estudar” não tem ajudante, então a mão esquece dele. Em inglês toda frase precisa do am / is / are — é o mesmo is de “She is busy”, da aula 2. O segundo é pôr o verbo no passado depois de going to. Depois de going to o verbo fica pelado: change, nunca changed — igual a “vou mudar”, nunca “vou mudei”.

Listening

David and the Plan

Sound first — no text. Leia as perguntas antes de ouvir.

0:000:00
1. What is David going to do in April?
He is going to visit São Paulo, bring the new policy system, and train Paula's whole team. They are going to work together for three months.
2. David says the future tense is not new. Why not?
Because Paula already uses it in Portuguese every day — "eu vou viajar", "eu vou aprender". Going to is just "vou". Word for word, it is the same thing, and there is nothing new to fear.
3. On the call, what is David NOT going to do?
He is not going to speak fast, and he is not going to use hard words. He is going to speak like a normal person — because that is what Paula's English is ready for now.

Comprehension

About Greg

1. What is Greg going to send Paula this week?
The new system. Then Paula is going to try it with a small client. That is the first step of the plan.
2. When is Greg going to fly to São Paulo, and how long is he going to stay?
He is going to fly on April tenth, and he is going to stay for two weeks. In those two weeks, they are going to train the whole team together.
3. Greg needs Paula's help with the team. Why?
Because she knows the team and he doesn't. But Paula is not going to translate everything — her team is going to learn too.
4. What does Greg ask at the end — and what does Paula say?
He asks if she is going to take the international accounts after April. She says she doesn't know yet — but she is going to say yes. She does not know the future, but she has already decided.
