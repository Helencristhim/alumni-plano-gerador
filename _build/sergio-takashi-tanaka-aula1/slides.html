Chapter 3: The Code

Your Role vs Right Now

I manage · I am reviewing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I manage financial risk at XP."

"Right now, I am reviewing our exposure limits."

"Every week, the team assesses the biggest risks."

"This month, we are preparing answers for the regulator."

Why green for your role and routine, but orange for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb (he/she + s)		Your permanent role, facts, and routine (every week, usually, always)I manage financial risk.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		What is in progress right now or this period (now, right now, this week, at the moment)I am reviewing the report.
Stative verbs
know, need, want, mean		Verbs of state stay in the simple form, even for nowI know the rules (not "am knowing").

Routine = present simple · In progress now = present continuous.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Right now, I prepare the report for the regulator."
"Right now, I am preparing the report for the regulator."
"Every week, we are assessing the exposure limits."
"Every week, we assess the exposure limits."
"I am knowing the compliance rules very well."
"I know the compliance rules very well."

Use the present continuous (am / is / are + -ing) only for what is happening now. Routine and permanent facts stay in the present simple, and stative verbs like know never take -ing.
