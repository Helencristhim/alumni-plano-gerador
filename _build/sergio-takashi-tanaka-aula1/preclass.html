Reactivating your spoken English at the risk desk. Key words: risk, exposure, regulator, compliance, stakeholder, to assess, to mitigate, deadline, on track, report, partner bank, limit. Structure: present simple (your role and routine) vs present continuous (what you are working on right now).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Risk -- the chance that something bad or uncertain will happen
"My job is to measure financial risk at XP."
Exposure -- the amount of money a company could lose if things go wrong
"We are reviewing our exposure to the market this week."
Regulator -- an official body that controls an industry, like the Central Bank
"The regulator asks for a report every quarter."
Compliance -- following the rules and laws set by regulators
"Compliance is a big part of our daily work."
Stakeholder -- a person or group with an interest in company decisions
"Every stakeholder wants a clear view of the numbers."
To assess -- to look at something carefully and judge its level
"We assess the risk of each position every week."
To mitigate -- to make a risk or problem less serious
"We always try to mitigate the biggest exposures first."
Deadline -- the latest time by which something must be finished
"The deadline for the report is Friday."
On track -- going well and likely to finish on time
"The project is on track for this quarter."
Report -- a document that presents information and numbers
"I am preparing the market risk report right now."
Partner bank -- another bank that works together with your company
"Rachel works at our partner bank in London."
Limit -- the maximum amount that is allowed
"The committee is setting new exposure limits this month."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Sergio is a risk manager at XP. He manages financial risk and works with the regulator and international partner banks. "I assess and mitigate our exposure every week," he says. Every Monday, the risk committee meets at nine o'clock to review the report. This is his routine. But right now, things are busy. This week, Sergio is reviewing the new exposure limits, and he is preparing answers for the regulator. At the moment, his team is testing a new model, and the project is on track. He knows the compliance rules very well, so he feels calm before the deadline.

1. Why do we say "I assess and mitigate our exposure every week"?
A It is a routine, so we use the present simple.
B It is happening only right now, so we use the present continuous.
C It finished in the past, so we use the past simple.
2. "This week, Sergio is reviewing the new exposure limits" uses the present continuous because:
A It is his permanent job forever.
B It is in progress now, during this period.
C It is a habit he does every single day.
3. Which sentence about a state of mind is correct?
A "He is knowing the compliance rules very well."
B "He knows the compliance rules very well."
C "He know the compliance rules very well."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to separate your role and routine from what you are doing right now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb (he/she + s)		Your permanent role, facts, and routine. Signal words: usually, always, every week.I manage financial risk.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		What is in progress right now or this period. Signal words: now, right now, this week, at the moment.I am reviewing the report.
Third person -sWith he / she / it / the team, the simple verb takes -s.The committee meets on Monday.
Stative verbsknow, need, want, understand, mean stay in the simple form, even for now: I know the rules (not "am knowing").
