Complementary Activities

Extra material to reinforce your learning outside class. Mark each one as done after you watch or listen.

Lesson 1 -- Back in the Room

Grammar Video
"Present Simple and Present Continuous" -- BBC Learning English (6 Minute Grammar)

A short, clear lesson on the exact contrast from today: your role and routine (present simple) versus what you are doing right now (present continuous). Connection to Lesson 1: the grammar that lets you separate "I manage risk" from "I am reviewing the report" without mixing them up.

Tip: pause after each example and say one sentence about your own work -- one in the present simple, one in the present continuous.

Listening
"Participating in Meetings 1" -- Business English B1-B2

Real meeting language: how people open a meeting, present numbers, agree, disagree, and ask questions. Connection to Lesson 1: exactly the setting where you introduce your role and defend a position on the risk desk.

Tip: listen once for the general idea, then again and write down 5 phrases you could use to give your opinion or disagree politely in a meeting.

Talk
"How Does the Stock Market Work?" -- Oliver Elfenbaum, TED-Ed

A clear, well-narrated animation about markets, shares, and risk in your own professional world. Connection to Lesson 1: listen to how a native speaker explains financial ideas simply -- the same skill you are reactivating for your meetings with partner banks.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Note 5 finance words you already know from reading, and say each one out loud.
