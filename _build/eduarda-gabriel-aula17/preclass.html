Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key memo and concision terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Memo -- a short internal document that informs the team
"Send the deal memo to the partners by noon."
Headline -- the opening line that states the main message
"Lead with the headline: we recommend proceeding."
Rationale -- the reason behind a decision or recommendation
"Give the rationale in one clear sentence."
Scope -- the limit of what a deal or task includes
"Define the scope so no one assumes more."
Bullet Point -- a short item in a list, marked by a dot
"Use bullet points for the key terms, not paragraphs."
Draft -- a working version, before the final one
"Send me the draft before it goes out."
Circulate -- to send a document to a group of people
"Circulate the draft for review before it reaches the partners."
Outline -- the structure or skeleton before you write
"Start with an outline: headline, terms, rationale, scope."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduarda writes a deal memo. She opens with a headline that states the recommendation up front. She lists the key terms as bullet points, not paragraphs. To keep it tight, she cuts every long clause: instead of "the terms that were agreed last week", she writes "the terms agreed last week"; instead of "the deal that is closing on Friday", she writes "the deal closing on Friday". She gives the rationale in one line and defines the scope. Then she sends the draft to her director and will circulate the final version. She always starts from an outline.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use participle phrases to make writing concise. Cut "that is / that are / that was / that were / who is" and keep only the participle. A -ing form (closing, leading) shows an action in progress; a past participle (agreed, shown, sent) shows an action received. A participle phrase corta a relativa e mantem o sentido: em vez de "the deal that is closing", escreva "the deal closing".

Cut thisKeep (participle)Example
that is + -ingjust the -ing verb"the deal closing on Friday"
that were + past participlejust the past participle"the terms agreed last week"
who is + -ingjust the -ing verb"the partner leading the deal"

Common Mistake:

"the deal that is closing on Friday and the terms that were agreed last week"

"the deal closing on Friday and the terms agreed last week"

Num memo, cada "that is" ou "that was" extra e peso morto — corte e deixe so o participio.

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection
Think of a real update you need to send your team. In 3-4 sentences, dictate a tight memo: open with a headline (the recommendation), give two key terms as bullet points, and add one rationale line. Cut at least one long clause into a participle phrase. Use at least two key terms.
