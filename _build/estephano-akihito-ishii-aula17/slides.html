Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

There is / There are

One thing vs. two or more things

Grammar Rule

One or More?

There is

ONE thing (singular)

"There is a computer."

"There is a charger."

There are

TWO or MORE things (plural)

"There are two cables."

"There are headphones."

Key idea: count the things. ONE = is. TWO or more = are.

The Negative

What Is Not There?

To say something is NOT there, we use no:

"There is no printer on my desk."

"There are no extra cables here."

Question form:

"Is there a mouse?" -- Yes, there is. / "Are there headphones?" -- Yes, there are.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

WRONG

"There is two cables."

RIGHT

"There are two cables."

Two or more things always need There are.

Grammar Practice

Is or Are?

Say "is" or "are" before you reveal the answer.

1. There ___ a computer on the desk.

There is a computer on the desk. (one)

2. There ___ three folders on the screen.

There are three folders on the screen. (more than one)

3. There ___ headphones next to the keyboard.

There are headphones next to the keyboard. (plural)

4. There ___ one charger for my phone.

There is one charger for my phone. (one)
