Lesson 3 -- Running and Following Status Meetings

Documentary
Downfall: The Case Against Boeing (Netflix, 1h29)

A whole documentary about what happens when a non-conformance is not flagged up, when a waiver is granted to save factory time, and when nobody triggers the escalation in time. This is your technical world — certification, inspection, supplier and regulator — in native English. And the modal perfects show up in the wild: "this should have been flagged", "they must have known".

Tip: watch it with English subtitles and write down 5 sentences with should have / must have / might have. Then rewrite each one, swapping the aircraft for YOUR signaling package at Motiva.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast — "To Negotiate Better, Start with Yourself" (27 min)

William Ury, co-author of Getting to Yes and co-founder of the Harvard Program on Negotiation. His thesis is exactly what you practiced today: the biggest obstacle in a negotiation is almost never the other side — it is your own reaction when they hand the blame back to you. Listen to it with Mei in mind: how to push back without breaking the relationship.

Tip: listen to all 27 minutes ONCE, without pausing — native pace, no subtitles. Then record 60 seconds, out loud, answering: "What do I usually do when a supplier blames me for their own delay?" The bottleneck is your speaking, not your listening.

YouTube
BBC Learning English — Modals in the past (BBC English Class)

The exact structure of the lesson, straight to the point: should have + past participle (the obligation that failed), must have + past participle (the deduction) and might have + past participle (the possibility). Pay close attention to the contractions in speech ("it should've been sent", "they must've missed it") — that is how the sentence reaches your ear on a call, never spelled out.

Tip: after the video, say 5 sentences about YOUR project — two with should have (an overdue item on the punch list), two with must have (the cause you deduce) and one with might have (the hypothesis you have not confirmed yet). Out loud, with nothing written down first.
