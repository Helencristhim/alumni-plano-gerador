Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Going to (a plan) vs will (a decision, an offer, a prediction)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

When do we use going to, and when do we use will?

"I am going to attend the congress next month." (plan)

"Look at the sky. It is going to rain." (evidence)

"The phone is ringing. I will answer it." (decision now)

"I will confirm the booking tomorrow." (promise)

Grammar Rule

Going to vs Will

UseFormExample
A plan already decidedgoing to + verbI am going to attend the congress.
A prediction with evidencegoing to + verbLook at the clouds. It is going to rain.
A decision at the momentwill + verbThe phone is ringing. I will answer it.
An offer or a promisewill + verbI will confirm the booking tomorrow.
An opinion or predictionI think ... willI think the keynote will be great.

Key idea: after will, use the verb without to. Say will confirm, never will to confirm. Use going to for a plan you already had; use will for a decision, an offer, or an opinion right now.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I will to confirm the booking tomorrow."
"I will confirm the booking tomorrow."

After will, use the base verb with no to (will confirm, will arrange, will be). The same rule applies to every verb.
