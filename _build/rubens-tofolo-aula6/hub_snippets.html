Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Itinerary -- a detailed plan of a journey, day by day
"My itinerary shows three cities in five days." (Itinerário)
Departure -- the moment you leave, especially a flight
"My departure is at six in the morning." (Partida)
Booking -- a reservation you make in advance
"I made a booking at a hotel near the venue." (Reserva)
Deadline -- the latest time to finish something
"The registration deadline is next Friday." (Prazo final)
Arrange -- to plan and organize something
"I will arrange the transport from the airport." (Organizar)
Venue -- the place where an event happens
"The congress venue is a large convention center." (Local do evento)
Keynote -- the most important talk at a conference
"A famous researcher will give the keynote." (Palestra principal)
Registration -- the act of signing up for an event officially
"Registration opens two months before the event." (Inscrição)
Luggage -- the bags you take when you travel
"I am going to take light luggage this time." (Bagagem)
Confirm -- to say that something is definite and final
"I will confirm my booking tomorrow." (Confirmar)
Upcoming -- happening soon in the near future
"The upcoming congress is in October." (Próximo)
Postpone -- to move an event to a later time
"They had to postpone the workshop until May." (Adiar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rubens is planning his trip to the upcoming endocrinology congress. "I am going to attend the congress in Lisbon next month," he says. The decision is already made, so he uses going to. He has a clear plan: "First, I am going to finish my registration before the deadline. I already have a booking near the venue." Then his phone rings. The coordinator asks about transport from the airport. Rubens thinks for a second and decides on the spot: "Do not worry, I will arrange a taxi myself." This is a decision he makes at the moment of speaking, so he uses will. Before the call ends, he makes a promise: "I will confirm everything by Friday." He also gives an opinion about the trip: "I think the keynote will be excellent."

1. Why does Rubens say "I am going to attend the congress"?
A Because it is a plan he already decided.
B Because he is deciding it right now.
C Because it already happened in the past.
2. Why does he say "I will arrange a taxi" with will?
A Because he decides it at the moment of speaking.
B Because it was a plan from last week.
C Because the taxi is already booked.
3. When Rubens says "the keynote will be excellent", he is giving a...
A prediction or opinion about the future.
B fixed plan in his diary.
C description of the past.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Going to vs Will

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar do futuro. / Used to talk about the future.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
am/is/are going to + verbPlano já decidido antes de falar / a plan already decidedI am going to attend the congress.
going to + verbPrevisão com evidência / prediction with evidenceLook at the clouds -- it is going to rain.
will + verbDecisão na hora de falar / decision at the momentThe phone is ringing. I will answer it.
will + verbOferta ou promessa / offer or promiseI will confirm the booking tomorrow.
I think / I am sure + willOpinião ou previsão / opinion or predictionI think the keynote will be great.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de will use o verbo sem to (will confirm, não will to confirm). Use going to para um plano que você já tinha; use will para uma decisão, oferta ou opinião no momento.
