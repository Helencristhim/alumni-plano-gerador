Lesson 6 -- Making Plans

Series
The Last Leg / Departures -- any travel-planning episode

People organize a trip on screen: booking flights, choosing a venue, fixing a departure time, and changing plans at the last minute. Listen for every future form: I am going to... for the plan, and I will... for the on-the-spot decisions.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three sentences with going to (plans already decided) and three with will (decisions, offers, predictions). Then describe your own next trip the same way.

Podcast
Zero To Travel -- "How to Plan a Trip" episodes

A relaxed conversation about planning a journey: registration, deadlines, itineraries, and luggage. Full of natural future language for talking about plans and quick decisions.

Note three sentences that describe a future plan. Then record a short plan for your next congress, using at least three new words from today, such as itinerary, deadline, and venue.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "WILL vs GOING TO"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: going to for plans and predictions with evidence, will for decisions made at the moment, offers, and promises, and why we never say will to do.

After watching, write five sentences about your upcoming trip: three with going to and two with will. Use registration, booking, and keynote at least once each.
