Chapter 3

Making Invitations

Would you like...? / Why don't we...? / How about...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Would you like to join us for dinner?"

"Why don't we have dinner together?"

"How about meeting at seven thirty?"

"Are you free on Friday evening?"

PhraseFormExample
Would you like+ to + base verbWould you like to join us?
Why don't we+ base verbWhy don't we have dinner?
How about+ verb-ingHow about meeting at seven?
Are you free+ on + day / time: Are you free on Friday?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Would you like join us for dinner?"

✓ RIGHT

"Would you like to join us for dinner?"

✗ WRONG

"How about to meet at seven?"

✓ RIGHT

"How about meeting at seven?" (-ing)

✗ WRONG

"Thank you, but I am can't come."

✓ RIGHT

"Thank you, but I can't come." (no "am")

Would you like + to + verb. How about + verb-ing. To decline: I'm afraid I can't (no "am can't").

Chapter 4

The Conversation

Inviting a counterpart to dinner

Dialogue

Would You Like to Join Us?

M
Hi Sarah, I really enjoyed our session today. A few of us are having dinner tonight. Would you like to join us?
S
That is very kind, thank you. I'd love to. What time were you thinking?
M
How about meeting at seven thirty? There is an Italian place near the venue.
S
Seven thirty sounds perfect. Should I bring anything, or shall we just meet there?
M
Just bring yourself. Dinner is my treat tonight. Are you available to stay a little later too?
S
I have some spare time after, so yes. By the way, may I invite my colleague Daniel?
M
Of course, the more the better. I will send you the address. See you at seven thirty.
S
Wonderful. Thank you again for the invitation, Marcos. I am looking forward to it.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Sarah accept the invitation?
She says it is very kind and "I'd love to."
2. What time and place does Marcos suggest?
Seven thirty, at an Italian place near the venue.
3. What does Sarah ask at the end?
If she may invite her colleague Daniel.
