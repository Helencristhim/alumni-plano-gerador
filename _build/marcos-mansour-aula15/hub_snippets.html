Building Relationships -- Social Dinners & Invitations

Inviting, accepting and declining at a social dinner. Key words: invitation, invite, accept, decline, available, reschedule, gathering, get-together, treat, spare. Structures: making invitations (Would you like to...? / Why don't we...? / How about + -ing? / Are you free on...?), accepting (I'd love to / That sounds great) and declining politely (I'm afraid I can't / Maybe another time).

Stage 15.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Invitation -- convite (a request to come to an event)
"Thank you for the dinner invitation."
Invite -- convidar (to ask someone to come or join you)
"Let me invite you to dinner tonight."
Accept -- aceitar (to say yes to an invitation)
"I am happy to accept your invitation."
Decline -- recusar (to politely say no)
"I had to decline politely because I was busy."
Available -- disponível (free and able at a certain time)
"Are you available for dinner on Friday?"
Reschedule -- remarcar (to change a plan to another time)
"Can we reschedule the dinner for next week?"
Gathering -- encontro/reunião (a meeting for a social event)
"We are having a small gathering after the panel."
Get-together -- encontro informal (a casual, informal meeting)
"It is just a relaxed get-together after work."
Treat -- pagar para alguém (to pay for someone as a kind gesture)
"Please, order anything. Dinner is my treat tonight."
Spare -- de sobra/livre (extra and free, not being used)
"Do you have a spare hour for a quick dinner?"

Stage 15.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After the sessions, Marcos meets a counterpart and makes an invitation: "Would you like to join us for dinner tonight? How about meeting at seven thirty?" She is happy to accept: "I'd love to. That sounds great." Notice the forms: after Would you like we use to + verb (to join), and after How about we use the -ing form (meeting). Later, another colleague has to decline politely: "Thank you, but I'm afraid I can't tonight. Maybe another time." Marcos says it is no problem and offers to reschedule: "Are you available on Friday?" This way, every invitation keeps the relationship warm, whether the answer is yes or no.

1. Que forma vem depois de "Would you like"?
A O verbo no -ing (joining).
B "to" + verbo base (to join).
C O verbo sozinho, sem "to" (join).
2. Que forma vem depois de "How about"?
A O verbo no -ing (meeting).
B "to" + verbo (to meet).
C "will" + verbo (will meet).
3. Which is a polite way to decline an invitation?
A "No, I don't want."
B "Thank you, but I'm afraid I can't. Maybe another time."
C "I am can't go."

Stage 15.4: Grammar Tip -- Making, Accepting & Declining Invitations

GRAMMAR

Como convidar, aceitar e recusar (explicação em inglês e português).

FunctionForm / FormaExample
InviteWould you like to + verbo / How about + -ingWould you like to join us? / How about meeting?
Acceptaceitar com entusiasmoI'd love to. / That sounds great.
Declinerecusar com educaçãoI'm afraid I can't. Maybe another time.
DicaApós Would you like = to + verbo. Após How about / Why don't we = -ing / verbo base. Para recusar: I'm afraid I can't (nunca "I am can't").
