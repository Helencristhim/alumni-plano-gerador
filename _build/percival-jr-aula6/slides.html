Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The past simple -- regular and irregular

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words tell you about the time of the action?

"Last Monday I attended the risk meeting."

"I reviewed the audit yesterday."

"Last week I went to Miami."

"The director had a question about the report."

Grammar Rule

The Past Simple

FormUseExample
verb + edregular verbsI reviewed the audit.
special formirregular verbsI went to Miami. (go)
did not + basenegativeI didn't attend the call.
Did + base?questionDid you submit the report?

Key idea: for the past, regular verbs add -ed (attend, attended). Irregular verbs change form (go, went; have, had; make, made). For negatives and questions, use did with the base verb: I didn't go; Did you go?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I didn't went to the meeting."
"I didn't go to the meeting."

After did and didn't, use the base verb, not the past form. The word did already shows the past.
