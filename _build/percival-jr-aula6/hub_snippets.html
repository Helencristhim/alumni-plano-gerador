Vocabulary

Findings -- the things you discover when you check something
"My findings show two small problems." (Constatações)
Summary -- a short text with only the main ideas
"I wrote a one-page summary." (Resumo)
Submit -- to send a document for someone to check
"We submit the report on Friday." (Enviar / submeter)
Approve -- to officially say yes to a plan or report
"The director can approve the budget." (Aprovar)
Evidence -- facts that show something is true
"We collected all the evidence." (Evidência)
Outcome -- the final result of an action or process
"The outcome of the trip was good." (Resultado)
Previous -- the one that came before this one
"I checked the previous report." (Anterior)
Attend -- to go to a meeting, event or class
"I attend the meeting every Monday." (Comparecer / participar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last week was busy. On Monday Percival attended the risk meeting. Then he reviewed the audit and found two small problems. On Tuesday he collected the evidence and wrote a short summary. The team submitted the report on Friday, and the director approved it the same day. Percival didn't find any delay, so the outcome was very good.

1. What did Percival do on Monday?
A He attended the risk meeting.
B He submitted the report.
C He traveled to Miami.
2. Why do we write "he found" and not "he finded"?
A Because "find" is a regular verb.
B Because "find" is irregular: find becomes found.
C Because it is a question.
3. Why is "didn't find" correct and not "didn't found"?
A After didn't we use the base verb.
B Because "find" has no past form.
C Because the subject is plural.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Usamos o past simple para falar de ações terminadas no passado. / We use the past simple to talk about finished actions in the past.

FormUseExample
verb + edregular verbsI reviewed the audit.
special formirregular verbsI went to Miami. (go)
did not + basenegativeI didn't attend the call.
Did + base?questionDid you submit the report?

Key point / Ponto-chave: verbos regulares ganham -ed (review, reviewed). Verbos irregulares mudam de forma (go, went; have, had; make, made). Para negativas e perguntas, use did + o verbo na forma base: I didn't go; Did you go? A palavra did já mostra o passado.
