Chapter 3 -- Describing Clothes

Language Discovery

Describing words before the clothing

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common? Before or after the clothing?

"I want a red dress."

"She likes the blue jacket."

"These are small black shoes."

"I would like a large white shirt."

The Rule

Size + Color + Clothing

SizeColorClothing
areddress
a smallbluejacket
largeblackshoes

Key idea: the describing word comes before the clothing: a red dress (not "a dress red"). When you use both, size comes before color: a small blue dress.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want a dress red."
"I want a red dress."

The describing word comes before the clothing: a red dress, a blue jacket. And size before color: a small red dress.

Practice

Put It in Order

"I want a _____red dress." (color + clothing)
"She likes the _____blue jacket." (color + clothing)
"These are _____small black shoes." (size + color)
"I would like a _____large white shirt." (size + color)
Key Phrase

The Store Question

"Do you have this in a smaller size?"

Then ask to try it on: "Can I try on this dress?" Say what you want: "I want a red dress."

Make It Yours

Describe Your Style

Imagine you are at a clothing store.

Describe two clothes you want, with size and color. For example: "I want a small blue dress" and "I like the large black jacket."
