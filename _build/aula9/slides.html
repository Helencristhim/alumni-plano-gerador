The Partner's Objections

OBJECTIONS -- AGENCY X

Acknowledge it: "I understand your concern..."

Turning the No Around

Sarah objects; Eduardo handles it

S
Honestly, your rate is higher than your competitors.
E
I understand your concern. That said, look at our track record — partners see results fast.
S

E
That's a fair point. Let me reassure you: we'll run a free pilot, so you take no risk.

Great. Free pilot, dedicated support, proven results — it's a win-win.

Chapter 5: Practice

Handle It Step by Step

Acknowledge a price objection.
"I understand your concern about the price. It's an important point."
Concede, then turn with your track record.
"That said, our track record speaks for itself: partners see results in the first quarter."
Reassure on risk.
"Let me reassure you: we'll run a free pilot, so you take no risk before you commit."
Reframe as a win-win.
"Free support makes this a win-win: you get help, and we get a happy partner."
Debrief a lost deal (third conditional).
"If we had addressed their concern earlier, they wouldn't have hesitated, and we would have closed it."
Concede a small point, hold your value.
"You're right that setup takes effort. Even so, the ROI is worth it within three months."
