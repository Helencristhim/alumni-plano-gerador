Supplementary Materials -- Lesson 9

Lesson theme: handling objections. Watch and listen for how people acknowledge a concern, address it, reassure with a track record, and analyze a past deal with the third conditional ("If we had done X, they would have signed").

Series
Mad Men -- Season 1, Episode 1 ("Smoke Gets in Your Eyes")

The famous Lucky Strike pitch is a masterclass in handling objections. Watch Don acknowledge the client's worry, then turn it into an advantage. Notice the concession-then-reframe pattern.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down how the speaker acknowledges a concern before answering it, then reuse that move at Quinto Andar.

Podcast
How I Built This with Guy Raz -- founders overcoming objections

Founders describe the objections and doubts they faced and how they reassured investors and partners. Listen for concession language and how they use their track record to win trust.

Tip: pick an episode about a tough early deal. Note 5 phrases used to acknowledge a concern and reassure.

YouTube
"How To Be More Diplomatic & Polite -- Business English" -- Derek Callan

A focused lesson on softening, conceding, and disagreeing diplomatically -- exactly the concession language ("I take your point, but...", "that said...") you need to handle objections without tension.

Tip: repeat each phrase out loud. Then take a real objection a partner gave you and practice acknowledging it before you answer.
