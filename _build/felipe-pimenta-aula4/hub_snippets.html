Wishes, Regrets, and What Could Be Different

Falar de arrependimento com PESSOAS — não com números. Uma reestruturação contada de dois jeitos. Key words: restructuring, to lay off, severance, morale, attrition, workload, candid, trade-off, to voice a concern, goodwill, headwinds, resentment. Structures: wish / if only + unreal past — wish + past simple (agora), wish + past perfect (passado), wish + would (reclamação sobre o outro).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Metade é planilha, metade é o que a planilha não resolve.

Restructuring -- reorganizing a company, often with job cuts (reestruturação)
"The restructuring took ninety people out of a team of six hundred."
To lay off -- to end someone's job because the company needs fewer people (demitir (por corte de quadro))
"We laid off ninety people, and I signed every letter."
Severance -- the money a company pays the people it lets go (verba rescisória)
"I wish we had offered a longer severance package."
Morale -- how motivated and confident a team feels (moral da equipe)
"Morale did not fall because of the cuts. It fell because of the silence."
Attrition -- people leaving the company slowly, over time (rotatividade, perda natural)
"If only we had managed it through attrition instead of layoffs."
Workload -- the amount of work one person is carrying (carga de trabalho)
"The workload of the people who stayed doubled overnight."
Candid -- honest and direct, even when it is uncomfortable (franco, direto)
"She was candid with the team, and they never forgot it."
Trade-off -- what you give up in order to get something else (troca, sacrifício necessário)
"Every restructuring is a trade-off between cash and trust."
To voice a concern -- to say openly that you are worried about something (expressar uma preocupação)
"Two directors voiced a concern, and I wish I had listened."
Goodwill -- the trust and credit you have built up with people (boa vontade, crédito de confiança)
"We spent ten years building goodwill and lost it in one meeting."
Headwinds -- outside forces that make growth harder (ventos contrários)
"We were facing real headwinds — but that is not an excuse for how we told them."
Resentment -- the quiet anger people keep after they feel wronged (ressentimento)
"Resentment is what silence leaves behind."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The restructuring took ninety people out of a team of six hundred. Felipe signed every letter. The company laid off the ninety on a Tuesday morning, with no warning, and the severance was one month — the legal minimum. Two directors had voiced a concern in March, and nobody actioned it. I wish I had listened to them, Felipe says now. If only we had offered three months instead of one: the cash was there. It was a trade-off, and he got it wrong — he protected the runway and spent ten years of goodwill to do it. Morale did not fall because of the cuts. It fell because of the silence. Three months later, four senior engineers resigned. None of them had been on the list. That is attrition, and it is what resentment leaves behind. The workload of the people who stayed had doubled overnight, and nobody had said a word about it. The headwinds were real. They are still not an excuse. Today Felipe is candid about all of it, in public, because he has learned that I wish we had told them earlier repairs more than any memo ever did.

1. "I wish I had listened to them." O que realmente aconteceu?
A Ele escutou os dois diretores.
B Ele NÃO escutou — e se arrepende disso.
C Ele vai escutar os dois diretores na próxima vez.
2. Por que o texto diz que a moral caiu pelo silêncio, e não pelos cortes?
A Porque ninguêm se importa com demissões.
B Porque as pessoas aceitam a decisão difícil, mas não aceitam não serem avisadas nem tratadas com franqueza.
C Porque os cortes foram pequenos demais para importar.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A I wish I would have listened to the two directors.
B I wish I had listened to the two directors.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Wish / If Only + Unreal Past

GRAMMAR

Como falar de arrependimento — a única gramática desta trilha que carrega emoção (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
wish + past simpleArrependimento sobre AGORA. A realidade é o oposto do que você diz. A regret about now.I wish I had more time. (I don't.)
wish + past perfectArrependimento sobre o PASSADO. A sua ferramenta principal. A regret about the past.I wish I had listened. (I didn't.)
wish + would + verboReclamação sobre o comportamento do OUTRO. NUNCA sobre você mesmo. A complaint about someone else.I wish the board would decide faster.
wish + could (have)Arrependimento sobre CAPACIDADE. A regret about ability.I wish I could have protected that team.
If only...As mesmas estruturas, mais fortes e mais emocionais. Use UMA vez, não cinco.If only we had offered three months.
Por que o tempo 'anda para trás'O passado aqui não é tempo: é DISTÂNCIA da realidade. É o unreal past — o mesmo truque de if I were you.I wish I were in that meeting.

Atenção (o erro nº 1 do brasileiro): "eu queria ter escutado" vira, quase sempre, "I wish I would have listened" — e isso NÃO existe. Sobre VOCÊ MESMO é sempre wish + had + particípio: "I wish I had listened". O wish + would existe, mas só para reclamar do OUTRO: "I wish the board would decide faster". E nunca use presente depois de wish: "I wish I have" está sempre errado.

Stage 2: Put the Difficult Conversation in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma conversa franca sobre um erro de liderança na ordem correta. Inverter duas delas destrói a conversa.

?Name the trade-off you got wrong, without blaming the board or the headwinds.
?Commit to the one thing you can still change — and be specific.
?Listen first. Let them tell you what it cost, without interrupting.
?Ask what would have made the difference for them.
?Say what you wish you had done differently — and stop talking.

Speaking

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

Your Head of People says you lost four good people. You answer:
A "I wish I would have listened to you in March."
B "I wish I had listened to you in March."
C "I wish I have listened to you in March."
You want to say the severance was too small — and you had the cash:
A "If only we would offer three months instead of one."
B "If only we had offered three months instead of one."
C "If only we offer three months instead of one."
The board takes months to approve anything, and it frustrates you. You say:
A "I wish the board would decide faster."
B "I wish the board had decided faster." (about their general habit)
C "I wish the board decides faster."
Someone you laid off asks: "Was there really no other way?" The most senior answer is:
A "The board made the decision. It wasn't really my call."
B "There was a trade-off, and I got it wrong. I wish we had told you earlier and paid you better."
C "I'm so sorry, it was terrible, I felt awful for weeks."
You want to admit a regret WITHOUT sounding weak in front of the board:
A "I feel really bad about what happened to those people."
B "I wish we had managed it through attrition. That was a trade-off I got wrong, and here is what changes."
C "Mistakes were made, but the headwinds were very strong."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

Three months after a restructuring, you address the whole company. Speak for 2-3 minutes. Use "I wish + past perfect" at least twice and "If only" once. Name the trade-off you got wrong. Do NOT blame the board or the headwinds. Finish with the one concrete thing you can still change for the people who stayed. Be candid — and do not apologize more than once.
