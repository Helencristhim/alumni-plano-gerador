Rick Steves' Europe — Riding the Rails: Train Travel Overview (free, no subscription)

Three and a half minutes, and it is the whole lesson from the outside: the station, the sign with the last station on the line, the platform, and people getting on a train with a ticket in their hand. Rick Steves is American, he speaks slowly, and he never hurries. Watch what the passengers do with their hands and their tickets — that is what you are going to do in Lisbon.

Tip: it is short, so watch it three times. The first time, only look. The second time, watch with English subtitles. The third time, pause on every station scene and say your own plan out loud: "I am going to buy a round trip, and I am going to wait on platform two."

Podcast
Context English Podcast — Episode 15: Asking for Directions (A2-B1)

The title is "Lost in a foreign country? Don't panic!" — and the story is Anna, lost on a street, asking a stranger the way. One of her three cities is Lisbon. It is your lesson, from the other side: this time you are the one who is lost. The two hosts speak slowly and explain every expression, and the full text of the episode is free on the same page, under the audio.

Tip: listen once with your eyes closed, and do not read — your ear before your eyes. Then listen again with the free transcript open. Count how many times you hear "Excuse me" (it is there twelve times): that is how normal it is to stop a stranger.

YouTube
Oxford Online English — How to Talk About Transport and Driving in English (A2-B1)

Two teachers, in front of the camera, showing you how people really say it: "take the metro", "use public transport", "get around the city". It is the same channel as the hotel video from Lesson 4, so the voices are already familiar. The video has chapters — you do not need the driving part, and you can go straight to public transport.

Tip: watch only the first part, about public transport, and stop there. Every time a teacher says "take the...", pause and finish the sentence about tomorrow: "I am going to take the tram to Belém." Say it out loud, not in your head.
