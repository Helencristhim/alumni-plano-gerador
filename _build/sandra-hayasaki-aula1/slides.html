Two Tenses

Every Day or Right Now?

Look at the orange words. Which sentences talk about every day, and which talk about right now?

I commute to work every day.

Right now I am working from home.

She manages a team at the office.

This week I am learning a new app.

Grammar Rule

Present Simple vs Continuous

UseStructureExample
Habit / routineverb (-s)I commute every day.
Happening nowam / is / are + verb-ingI am working now.
Temporary periodam / is / are + verb-ingThis week I am studying.
Frequencyadverb + verbI hardly ever relax.

Key idea: use the present simple for habits and routines, and the present continuous for what is happening now or in a temporary period. The frequency adverb goes before the main verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am commuting every day."
"I commute every day."

For a daily habit, use the present simple, not the continuous.
