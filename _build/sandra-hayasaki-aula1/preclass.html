Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Commute -- to travel between home and work
"I commute to the office by train every day." (deslocar-se)
Errand -- a short task outside, like shopping
"I run a few errands on Saturday morning." (tarefa/recado)
Schedule -- a plan of your times and tasks
"My schedule is very busy this week." (agenda/cronograma)
Hardly ever -- almost never
"I hardly ever watch TV during the week." (quase nunca)
Nowadays -- in the present period of time
"Nowadays I work from home more often." (hoje em dia)
Routine -- the things you do regularly each day
"My morning routine starts with a coffee." (rotina)
Manage -- to organize or to deal with something
"I manage a small team at work." (gerenciar/dar conta)
Spare time -- free time when you are not working
"In my spare time I love reading books." (tempo livre)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Let me tell you about my life. I work in an office, so I usually commute by train. My morning routine is always the same: I wake up early and I check my schedule. I manage a small team, so I hardly ever have a quiet day. Nowadays, however, things are changing. This week I am working from home, and right now I am learning a new app for the team. In my spare time, I usually run a few errands, but today I am relaxing instead.

1. How does the person normally go to work?
A She commutes by train.
B She is driving today.
C She walks every day.
2. Which sentence describes something happening now?
A "I usually commute by train."
B "Right now I am learning a new app."
C "I manage a small team."
3. What does "hardly ever" mean in the text?
A Always.
B Almost never.
C Sometimes.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- present simple vs continuous

GRAMMAR

Use o PRESENT SIMPLE para hábitos e rotinas e o PRESENT CONTINUOUS para o que está acontecendo agora. / Use the present simple for habits and routines, and the present continuous for what is happening now.

Use / UsoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Hábito / Habitverb (-s)I commute by train every day.
Agora / Nowam / is / are + verb-ingToday I am working from home.
Frequência / Frequencyalways > usually > often > sometimes > hardly ever > neverI hardly ever watch TV.
Wrong / erradoI am commuting every dayI am commuting every day.
Key point / Ponto-chaveRotina ou hábito? Use o simple. Acontecendo agora? Use o continuous. O advérbio de frequência vem antes do verbo principal.
