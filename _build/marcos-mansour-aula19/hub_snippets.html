Stage 19.1: Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Aprenda as palavras do e-mail profissional. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Attach -- anexar (to add a file to an email)
"Please attach the report to your reply."
Attachment -- anexo (the file you send with an email)
"I could not open the attachment on my phone."
Draft -- rascunho (a first version, not final yet)
"I have written a draft of the contract."
Forward -- encaminhar (to send a received email on to someone)
"Could you forward that message to my team?"
Reply -- responder / resposta (to answer an email)
"I will reply to your message by Friday."
Recipient -- destinatário (the person who receives the email)
"Check the recipient before you press send."
Subject line -- linha de assunto (the title line of an email)
"Write a clear subject line so people open it."
Reach out -- entrar em contato (to contact someone, often new)
"I will reach out to the people I met in Washington."
Sincerely -- atenciosamente (a formal way to close an email)
"Sincerely, Marcos Mansour."
Acknowledge -- confirmar o recebimento (to confirm you received something)
"Please acknowledge this email when it arrives."
Pending -- pendente (still waiting, not finished yet)
"Two replies are still pending from last week."
Enclosed -- incluso / em anexo (included with a letter or email)
"Please find the signed contract enclosed."

Stage 19.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Back from the congress, Marcos opens his inbox. "I have sent the proposal," he tells his partner, "but I haven't heard back yet." A new contact, Rachel, has just replied. She asks: "Have you received my email?" Marcos answers: "Yes, I have already opened the attachment, thank you." He has reached out to three people since Monday, and two replies are still pending. We use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) for actions that connect to NOW -- with yet, already, just, for and since. But for a finished time (like "yesterday"), we use the past simple: "I sent it yesterday."

1. Para uma ação ligada ao AGORA (com yet/already/just), qual tempo usamos?
A past simple (I sent).
B present perfect (I have sent).
C present continuous (I am sending).
2. Como se forma o present perfect?
A have/has + past participle (have sent).
B have/has + verbo base (have send).
C will + verbo (will send).
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I have sent it yesterday."
B "I sent it yesterday."
C "I sending it yesterday."

Stage 19.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

A estrutura do present perfect, com afirmativa, negativa e pergunta (inglês e português).

Forma / FormEstrutura / StructureExample
afirmativa (affirmative)have / has + past participleI have sent it. / She has replied.
negativa (negative)have not (haven't) / has not (hasn't) + past participleI haven't heard back yet.
pergunta (question)Have / Has + sujeito + past participleHave you received it?
marcadores (time words)for, since, yet, already, justI have worked here for five years. (NUNCA com "yesterday")

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação de e-mail.

You finished the report and sent it. You write:
A "I have send the report."
B "I have sent the report."
C "I have sent the report yesterday."
You want to ask a client if your email arrived. You say:
A "Have you received my email?"
B "You have received my email?"
C "Did you have received my email?"
You are still waiting for a reply. You say:
A "I didn't hear back yet."
B "I haven't heard back yet."
C "I don't hear back yet."
You can't find the file. You ask:
A "Could you forward me the attachment again?"
B "Could you forward me the recipient again?"
C "Could you reply me the subject line again?"
