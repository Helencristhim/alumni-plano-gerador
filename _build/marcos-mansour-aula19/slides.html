Chapter 3

The Present Perfect

have / has + past participle

Grammar Discovery

What Do These Have in Common?

I have sent the report.

Have you received my email?

I haven't heard back yet.

She has just replied, and we have already signed.

FormStructureExample
Affirmativehave / has + past participleI have sent it. She has replied.
Negativehave not / has not + past participleI haven't heard back yet.
QuestionHave / Has + subject + past participleHave you received it?
Time wordsfor / since / yet / already / justI have worked here for five years.
Common Mistakes

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have send the email."

✓ RIGHT

"I have sent the email." (past participle)

✗ WRONG

"I have sent it yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"I sent it yesterday." (finished time = past simple)

✗ WRONG

"I didn't receive your reply yet."

✓ RIGHT

"I haven't received your reply yet." (yet = present perfect)

Finished time (yesterday, last week) takes the past simple. A result that matters now, with yet / already / just, takes the present perfect.

Chapter 4

Following Up

A real conversation about an email

Dialogue

The Follow-up Call

R
Hi Marcos, it was great to meet you at the congress in Washington. Have you had time to look at my proposal?
M
Hello Rachel. Yes, I have just read it. Thank you for reaching out so quickly. I have a few questions.
R
Of course. Did you receive the attachment with the full report?
M
I have received your email, but I haven't opened the attachment yet. Could you forward it again?
R
Sure, I have already forwarded it. Please check your inbox and acknowledge it when it arrives.
M
Got it. I have prepared a short draft, so I will reply with my comments by Friday.
R
Perfect. I look forward to your reply. It has been a pleasure working with you.
M
Likewise, Rachel. I will be in touch very soon.
