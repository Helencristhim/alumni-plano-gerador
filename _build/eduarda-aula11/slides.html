Chapter 3 — The Code

The Passive Voice

Look at the purple words. How do we describe a legal process when the action matters more than who does it?

A petition is filed with the court.
Creditors are notified of the stay.
The plan must be approved by the court.
The company is protected from creditors.
FormStructureExample
Present passiveam / is / are + past participle"A petition is filed with the court."
Past passivewas / were + past participle"The plan was filed last week."
Modal passivemodal + be + past participle"The plan must be approved by the court."
Grammar Rule

Passive in Legal English

FunctionPatternExample
State a processis / are + past participle"Creditors are notified of the stay."
Report a past stepwas / were + past participle"The petition was filed yesterday."
Express obligationmust / has to be + p.p."The plan must be approved by the court."
Name the agent... by + agent"... approved by the court."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"The petition filed yesterday by the lawyer."
"The petition was filed yesterday by the lawyer."

The passive always needs be (is / are / was / were) + the past participle. Never drop the "be" verb.

Chapter 4 — Getting There

In the
Deal Room

Dialogue

The Case Briefing

E
David, can you explain how a Chapter 11 case is started?
D
Sure. First, a petition is filed with the court.
E
And what happens to the creditors then?
D
An automatic stay is granted, so collections are stopped.
E
So the company is protected while it reorganizes?
D
Exactly. The firm stays in control as debtor in possession.
E
And when is the reorganization plan approved?
D
Once it is accepted by creditors, it must be approved by the court.
Comprehension

Artifact

Court Notice

In re: Vertex Industries, Inc. — Case No. 11-2026
Document: Notice of Chapter 11 Filing
Filed by: Counsel for the Debtor in Possession

To all creditors and interested parties:

A voluntary petition under Chapter 11 has been filed with this court. As of the filing date, an automatic stay is in effect, and all collection actions are stopped.

The debtor will continue to operate as debtor in possession. A reorganization plan will be prepared and must be approved by the court before it takes effect.

A creditors' committee will be appointed. Questions should be directed to counsel.

1. What has been filed with the court?
A voluntary Chapter 11 petition.
2. What is stopped by the automatic stay?
All collection actions are stopped.
3. What must happen before the plan takes effect?
It must be approved by the court.
