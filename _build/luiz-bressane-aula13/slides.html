Chapter 3: Emphatic Structures

Putting the Weight First

cleft sentences and emphatic inversion for persuasion

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

It was the prosecution that promised certainty.

What the evidence shows is a reasonable doubt.

Not once did the witness mention a weapon.

Never in this trial have we seen direct proof.

StructurePatternStresses
It-cleftIt was + X + that/who...WHO or WHAT
What-cleftWhat... + is/was + Xthe key idea
InversionNever / Not once + auxiliary + subjecta strong negative

After a front negative (Never, Not once, At no time), the auxiliary comes before the subject — just like a question: "Not once did he say..." Use these sparingly, for the one point that must land.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Not once the witness mentioned a weapon."

✓ RIGHT

"Not once did the witness mention a weapon."

✗ WRONG

"What the evidence shows are doubt."

✓ RIGHT

"What the evidence shows is doubt."

After a fronted negative, invert subject and auxiliary, exactly as in a question. In a what-cleft, the verb agrees with the idea after "is/was", which is usually singular.

Chapter 4: Before the Jury

The Closing Exchange

Luiz sums up; the judge frames the jury's task

Dialogue

The Summation

J
Counsel, you have ten minutes. Address the jury with your closing.
L
Thank you, Your Honor. Members of the jury, it was the prosecution that promised certainty — and what they gave you is a reasonable doubt.
L
I ask you to weigh the evidence honestly. Not once did a single witness place my client at the scene.
L
Let me rebut their strongest point. The only fair inference from these facts is that someone else acted that night.
J
Counsel, keep to the evidence on the record.
L
Of course. Let me reiterate the one theme of this case. What you must decide is not whether you dislike the crime, but whether the proof is compelling.
L
I do not plead for sympathy. I plead for a fair reading of the facts when you deliberate.
J
Thank you, counsel. The jury will retire to deliberate.
Comprehension

The Summation

1. What did the prosecution promise, according to counsel?
Certainty — but only delivered a reasonable doubt.
2. What fact did counsel stress about the witnesses?
Not once did a witness place the client at the scene.
3. What did the judge tell counsel to keep to?
The evidence on the record.
4. What does counsel ask for instead of sympathy?
A fair reading of the facts.

Chapter 5: Drill

Sharpen the Closing

Detective · Transformation · Building

Spot the Error

Fix the Emphasis

"Never I have heard a weaker case."
Never have I heard a weaker case. (invert after "Never")
"What matters are the doubt, not the noise."
What matters is the doubt, not the noise. (singular)
"Not once the prosecution proved a motive."
Not once did the prosecution prove a motive. (add auxiliary)
"It was the defense who raised the doubt that mattered, who you must trust."
It was the defense that raised the doubt — one clean cleft.
Transformation

Make It Emphatic

"The defense raised the only real doubt." → cleft
It was the defense that raised the only real doubt.
"The jury must weigh the evidence." → what-cleft
What the jury must do is weigh the evidence.
"The witness never named my client." → inversion
Never did the witness name my client.
"Reasonable doubt decides this case." → cleft
It is reasonable doubt that decides this case.
