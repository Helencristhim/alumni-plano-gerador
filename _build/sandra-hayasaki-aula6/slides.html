The Phrases

Give, Agree, Disagree

FunctionUseful phrases
Give an opinionIn my opinion, ... / I think ... / Honestly, ...
AgreeI totally agree. / That's a good point. / Exactly.
Disagree (politely)I see your point, but ... / I'm not sure I agree. / Actually, ...
It changesIt depends on ... / from my point of view, ...

Key idea: to disagree politely, first show you understand ("I see your point"), then give your own view. This keeps the conversation friendly.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am agree with you."
"I agree with you."

Agree is a verb, so we never use am / is / are with it. Say "I agree" or "I disagree", not "I am agree".
