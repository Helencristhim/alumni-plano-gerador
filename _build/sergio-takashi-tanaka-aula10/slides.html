Chapter 3: The Code

Cause & Effect Connectors

because · so · as a result · consequently

Grammar Discovery

Read and Notice

"Because the limit was crossed, the exposure grew."

"The exposure grew, so the loss was large."

"Prices fell further as a result of the panic selling."

"Two funds sold together, and consequently liquidity dried up."

What do the orange words do?

JobConnectorsExample
Introduce the CAUSEbecause, since, as + clause; due to, owing to + nounBecause the limit was crossed...
Introduce the RESULTso, as a result, consequently, therefore...so the loss was large.
Verbs of causinglead to, result in, cause + noun: "One breach led to a chain of losses."
Grammar notebecause + a full clause (subject + verb); due to / owing to + a noun. "Due to the breach" = "because the limit was crossed".

One links backward to the cause; the other points forward to the result. Same story, two directions.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Because of the limit was crossed, the loss grew."
"Because the limit was crossed, the loss grew."
"Due to the market fell, our collateral lost value."
"Due to the market fall, our collateral lost value."
"By cause of that, liquidity dried up."
"As a result, liquidity dried up."

Use because + a full clause and due to + a noun. For the result, use so, as a result or consequently — never "by cause of".
