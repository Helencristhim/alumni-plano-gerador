Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Cause -- the reason something happens
"The report names the cause of the loss."
Effect -- the change that a cause produces
"The first effect was a margin call."
Consequence -- a result that follows a decision or event
"Every choice on the desk has a consequence."
Impact -- the strong effect one thing has on another
"The impact was wider than the first number."
Root cause -- the first, deepest reason behind a problem
"The root cause was a single limit breach."
Ripple effect -- a small event that spreads to affect many others
"The ripple effect was the real problem."
Contagion -- the spread of financial trouble from one market to another
"Contagion turned one loss into a market problem."
Knock-on effect -- a secondary effect caused by the first one
"The knock-on effect hit our liquidity."
To amplify -- to make an effect larger or stronger
"Panic selling can amplify a small loss."
Fallout -- the negative results that follow a bad event
"The fallout reached our liquidity by Wednesday."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The incident report tells one story. Because a position limit was not checked, the exposure grew for two hours. As a result, the loss was already large when prices moved. The first effect was a margin call. Since two partner funds held the same position, they sold together, and consequently prices fell further. This knock-on effect was a small example of contagion. Due to the speed of the fallout, the team had no time to hedge. In one line: one missed check led to a whole chain of consequences.

1. Why do we say "because a limit was not checked" and not "because of a limit was not checked"?
A "Because" comes before a full clause; "because of" and "due to" come before a noun.
B "Because" and "because of" are exactly the same and interchangeable.
C "Because" can only start a question.
2. Which connector introduces a RESULT?
A because
B as a result
C since
3. What does the report use cause-and-effect language to do?
A Show how one small error led to a chain of larger consequences.
B List the daily routine of the energy desk.
C Describe a plan for a possible future shock.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real situation on the risk desk.

A colleague asks why the loss grew. You answer:
A "Because of the limit was crossed, the exposure grew."
B "Because the limit was crossed, the exposure grew, and as a result the loss was large."
C "By cause of the limit, the exposure grew."
You explain the reason for a late report. Which is correct?
A "The report was late due to the speed of the fallout."
B "The report was late due to the fallout was fast."
C "The report was late for cause of the fallout."
The committee asks about the wider effect. You say:
A "Two funds sold together, consequently of prices fell."
B "Two funds sold together, and consequently prices fell further."
C "Two funds sold together, consequently prices to fall further."
You summarize the whole chain in one line. Which is correct?
A "One missed check led to a chain of consequences."
B "One missed check led a chain of consequences."
C "One missed check resulted a chain of consequences."
