Video
"How the Economic Machine Works" -- Ray Dalio

A clear, thirty-minute animation that shows the whole economy as one chain of cause and effect: credit grows, spending rises, and every action leads to a consequence. Connection to Lesson 10: it is cause-and-effect thinking on a large scale, so watch how one event amplifies into the next.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. After each part, pause and say one sentence with a connector: "Because credit grows, spending rises, and as a result..." Write down three cause-effect pairs you hear.

Podcast
BBC Learning English -- "6 Minute English"

Free, six-minute episodes in clear, slow English, each on one topic and its effects. Connection to Lesson 10: every episode is a small cause-and-effect story, so it is perfect practice for hearing the connectors (because, so, as a result) at a comfortable speed.

Tip: pick one episode about money or the economy, listen once for the main idea, then say two sentences: "Because X happened, ..." and "As a result, ..."

BBC News -- Business

Free, well-written business news in clear English -- close to the Financial Times and The Economist you already read. Connection to Lesson 10: reading is your strength, so use it to feed your speaking. Most stories explain why something happened and what it led to -- pure cause and effect.
