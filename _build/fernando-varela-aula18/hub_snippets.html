Talking About Plans & the Future

Looking ahead with the team: the next steps for the Parque Global. Key words: predict, expect, estimate, upcoming, timeline, outcome, guarantee, promise, probably, phase. Structure: will vs going to (going to for decided plans; will for predictions, decisions, offers and promises -- I think it will work / I will send it / We are going to open in March).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Predict -- prever (to say what will happen)
"I predict a big success."
Expect -- esperar / prever (to think something will happen)
"I expect many patients."
Estimate -- estimar (to guess a number or amount)
"We estimate two years."
Upcoming -- proximo / que vem (happening soon)
"The upcoming launch is in March."
Timeline -- cronograma (a plan of dates)
"The timeline shows the dates."
Outcome -- resultado (the final result)
"The outcome was good."
Guarantee -- garantir (to promise something is certain)
"I guarantee the quality."
Promise -- prometer (to say you will surely do it)
"I promise to help you."
Probably -- provavelmente (almost certainly)
"It will probably work."
Phase -- fase (a stage of a project)
"The first phase is ready."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Sarah talks to Fernando about the Parque Global. "We are going to open the new oncology unit in March," she says. This is a plan they already decided. Fernando is happy. "I think it will be a big success," he says. This is a prediction. Sarah looks at the timeline. "I will send you the dates this week," she says. This is a decision now. "I promise we will support you," she adds. Fernando smiles. "I am sure the outcome will be great."

1. Por que Sarah usa "are going to" para a abertura em marco?
A Porque e uma previsao sem certeza.
B Porque e um plano que ja foi decidido.
C Porque a acao e no passado.
2. "I think it will be a big success" e um exemplo de:
A uma previsao (prediction) com will.
B um plano decidido com going to.
C uma acao que ja terminou.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I will to send the dates."
B "I will send the dates."
C "I will sends the dates."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Will vs Going To

GRAMMAR

Quando usar WILL e quando usar GOING TO para falar do futuro (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
be going to + verbPlano ja decidido / Decided planWe are going to open in March.
will + verbPrevisao / PredictionIt will be a success.
will + verbDecisao agora / Oferta / PromessaI will send it tomorrow.
Atencao!will + verbo base (sem to, sem s)She will help (NAO "will to help" / "will helps")

Quiz
