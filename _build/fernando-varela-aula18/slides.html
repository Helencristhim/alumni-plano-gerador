Chapter 3

Will vs Going To

Two ways to talk about the future

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We are going to open the unit in March." — a plan

"I think it will be a success." — a prediction

"I will send you the timeline tomorrow." — a decision now

"I promise I will support you." — a promise

FormUseExample
be going to + verba plan already decidedWe are going to open in March.
will + verba predictionIt will be a success.
will + verba decision now, an offer, a promiseI will send it tomorrow.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I will to help you."

✓ RIGHT

"I will help you."

✗ WRONG

"She will helps you."

✓ RIGHT

"She will help you."

✗ WRONG

"We are going open the unit."

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to open the unit."

After will, use the base verb with no to and no s. For going to, never forget the to.
