Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Conditional language: if, unless, provided

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What does each orange word do to the offer?

"If you increase the volume, I will lower the price." (condition + result)

"Unless you confirm the date, I cannot offer a discount." (only if not)

"Provided you pay on time, we will give a small discount." (only on this condition)

"If we cannot agree, I will walk away." (condition + result)

Grammar Rule

The Conditional Map

WordMeaningExample
Ifon this condition"If you order more, I will lower the price."
Unlessif not / except if"Unless you confirm the date, I cannot offer it."
Providedonly on this condition"Provided you pay on time, we will give a discount."

Key idea: the condition clause uses the present simple (you order, you confirm, you pay) and the result clause uses will or can. Use if for a normal condition, unless for "if not", and provided for "only on this condition". This is how you bargain: I give you X provided you give me Y.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If you will increase the volume, I will lower the price."
"If you increase the volume, I will lower the price."

After if, unless and provided, use the present simple. Keep will for the result only.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Condition

"_____If you order more, I will lower the price." (normal condition)
"_____Unless you confirm the date, I cannot offer a discount." (if not)
"_____Provided you pay on time, we will give a discount." (only on this condition)
"If you increase the volume, I _____will meet you halfway." (result clause)
