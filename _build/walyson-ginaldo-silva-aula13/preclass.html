Negotiating a Deal -- The Santos Coffee Contract

Learning to bargain on price and terms with conditional language: making offers with if, unless and provided, stating your bottom line, offering a trade-off, naming a deal-breaker, and meeting halfway to close a deal in good faith. New vocabulary: bottom line, leverage, counteroffer, trade-off, terms, meet halfway, walk away, deadlock, leeway, ballpark, deal-breaker, in good faith.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Bottom line -- the limit you cannot pass, like the lowest price
"My bottom line is the floor price from the mill." (Limite mínimo)
Leverage -- an advantage that gives your side power
"Our strong harvest is our main leverage." (Poder de barganha / Vantagem)
Counteroffer -- a new offer you give back after their offer
"They asked for ten percent; my counteroffer was three." (Contraproposta)
Trade-off -- give up one thing to gain another in a deal
"A discount for a bigger order is a fair trade-off." (Troca / Concessão mútua)
Terms -- the conditions of a deal: price, volume, dates
"Let us agree on the terms of the contract." (Condições / Termos)
Meet halfway -- both sides give a little to reach an agreement
"Let us meet halfway and split the difference." (Chegar a um meio-termo)
Walk away -- to leave a deal that is not good for you
"If the price is too high, I will walk away." (Desistir / Sair da negociação)
Deadlock -- a point where neither side will move
"We reached a deadlock on the delivery date." (Impasse)
Leeway -- some freedom or room to move on a condition
"We have some leeway on the volume, not the price." (Margem / Folga)
Ballpark -- an approximate number, not the exact one
"Give me a ballpark price and we will refine it." (Valor aproximado / Estimativa)
Deal-breaker -- a condition that, if not met, ends the deal
"A late shipment is a deal-breaker for us." (Fator decisivo / Ponto inegociável)
In good faith -- honestly and fairly, with a real wish to agree
"We want to negotiate in good faith." (De boa-fé)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson prepares for a negotiation with a buyer. He knows his bottom line: he cannot go below the floor price from the mill. His leverage is the strong harvest from Santos. The buyer asks for a big discount, but Walyson does not just say yes. If they increase the volume, he will give a small discount: a fair trade-off. Unless the buyer confirms a firm delivery date, he will not lower the price, because a late shipment is a deal-breaker. He has some leeway on the volume, so provided they pay on time, he can guarantee an early shipment. His goal is to negotiate in good faith and meet halfway to close the deal.

1. What will Walyson do if the buyer increases the volume?
A He will give a small discount as a trade-off.
B He will raise the price.
C He will walk away immediately.
2. What is Walyson's deal-breaker?
A A bigger order.
B A late shipment.
C A small discount.
3. On what condition can Walyson guarantee an early shipment?
A Only if the buyer pays less.
C Only if he raises the price.
B Provided the buyer pays on time.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Conditional language (if / unless / provided)

GRAMMAR

Usamos a linguagem condicional para negociar: ofereço algo SE você me der algo em troca. / We use conditional language to negotiate: I offer something IF you give me something in return.

Word / PalavraPhrase / FraseUse / Uso
IfIf you order more, I will lower the price.condição normal (se)
UnlessUnless you confirm the date, I cannot offer it.a não ser que / se não (= if not)
ProvidedProvided you pay on time, we will give a discount.desde que (só nesta condição)

Key point / Ponto-chave: a parte da condição usa o present simple (you order, you pay) e a parte do resultado usa will ou can. Nunca use will depois de if, unless ou provided. / The condition clause uses the present simple and the result clause uses will or can. Never use will after if, unless or provided.
