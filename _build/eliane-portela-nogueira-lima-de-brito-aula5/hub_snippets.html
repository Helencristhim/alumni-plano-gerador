Quote -- a written price offer for a product or service

Proposal -- a formal written plan or offer for a deal
"Their proposal included training and support."
Terms and conditions -- the rules and details of an agreement
"Let us review the terms and conditions together."
Warranty -- a promise to repair or replace a product for a set time
"The incubators come with a five-year warranty."
Lead time -- the time between placing an order and receiving it
"What is the lead time for these machines?"
Delivery window -- the period when goods are expected to arrive
"Our delivery window is four to six weeks."
Compliance -- following rules, laws, and standards
"Every device must meet local compliance rules."
Regulatory approval -- official permission from authorities to use a product
"We need regulatory approval before we import."
Exclusivity -- a deal where one partner is the only one allowed
"We might ask for exclusivity in Brazil."
Pilot program -- a small first test before a full rollout
"Let us start with a pilot program in one clinic."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is on a call with a vendor in Texas. She opens politely: "Could you walk me through the pricing model?" The supplier explains the quote, the five-year warranty, and the eight-week lead time. Eliane wants to be careful, so she says, "We should review the terms and conditions first." She is not ready to commit, so she keeps her options open: "We might be interested in a pilot program." Then she pushes gently: "If we sign this quarter, would you improve the delivery window?" She also confirms the important details -- the compliance documents and the regulatory approval -- and asks about exclusivity for Brazil. Finally, she protects herself: "We would need the proposal in writing."

1. "Could you walk me through the pricing model?" Why "could" here?
A It is a command that must be obeyed.
B It is a polite request.
C It describes something that happened in the past.
2. "We might be interested in a pilot program." What does "might" signal?
A A possibility, with no commitment yet.
B A firm decision that cannot change.
C A fact that is always true.
3. Which sentence uses a conditional to negotiate?
A "The lead time is eight weeks."
B "If we sign this quarter, would you improve the delivery window?"
C "We reviewed the terms and conditions."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Verbs for Negotiation

GRAMMAR

Modal verbs make requests and offers sound firm but polite. Each one sends a different message, and the verb after it always stays in the base form (no "to"). / Os verbos modais tornam pedidos e ofertas firmes porém educados. Cada um manda uma mensagem diferente, e o verbo depois dele fica sempre na forma base (sem "to").

ModalUseExample
couldA polite request or a soft possibility / Pedido educado ou possibilidade suaveCould you send a quote?
wouldA hypothetical result or a soft statement / Resultado hipotético ou afirmação suaveThis would work for us.
shouldA recommendation or expectation / Recomendação ou expectativaWe should review the warranty.
might / mayA possibility, no commitment / Possibilidade, sem compromissoWe might ask for exclusivity.
If + would / couldConditional to negotiate / Condicional para negociar: If we move forward, we would need faster lead times. NOT "Could you to send..." → "Could you send...".
