Chapter 3 -- Polite Requests

Could You ...?

Asking for things in a polite way

Listen & Repeat

Polite Requests in Action

"Could you bring the menu, please?"

"Would you mind checking if this dish is vegetarian?"

"Could you tell me what today's special is?"

"I would like the steak well-done, please."

Grammar Rule

Polite Requests: Could you / Would you mind

StructureExampleNote
Could you + verbCould you bring the menu?base verb (not "bringing")
Would you mind + verb-ingWould you mind checking the dish?verb + -ing
Could you tell me + questionCould you tell me what the special is?no inversion: "what the special is"
I would like + nounI would like the steak well-done.polite "I want"

Key idea: use Could you + base verb, or Would you mind + verb-ing, to ask politely. After Could you tell me, the question has no inversion: say "what the special is", not "what is the special". Use I would like to order in a polite way.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Would you mind bring some water?"
"Would you mind bringing some water?"

After Would you mind, use the verb with -ing. After Could you, use the base verb ("Could you bring...").
