Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Menu -- the list of food and drinks a restaurant offers
"Could I see the menu, please?" (Cardápio)
Course -- one part of a meal, such as the starter, the main, or the dessert
"We had a lovely three-course dinner." (Prato / etapa da refeição)
Dish -- a prepared plate of food with a name on the menu
"The pasta is the most popular dish here." (Prato / iguaria)
Order -- to ask the waiter to bring you food or drink
"Are you ready to order?" (Pedir / fazer o pedido)
Well-done -- cooked for a long time, so it is not pink inside (for meat)
"I would like my steak well-done, please." (Bem passado)
Allergic -- having a body reaction to a food, like nuts, so you must avoid it
"I am allergic to nuts, so I ask the waiter." (Alérgico)
Vegetarian -- a person who does not eat meat; or food made without meat
"Is this dish vegetarian?" (Vegetariano)
Takeout -- food you buy at a restaurant to eat at home or elsewhere
"We got takeout and ate it at the farm." (Comida para viagem)
Complaint -- when you tell someone politely that something is wrong
"We had a small complaint because the soup was cold." (Reclamação)
Service charge -- an amount for service that the restaurant adds to your bill
"A ten percent service charge is included." (Taxa de serviço)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At a restaurant, you ask for things in a polite way. Instead of "Bring the menu," you say "Could you bring the menu, please?" After Could you, use the base verb (bring, not bringing). Another polite form is Would you mind + verb-ing: "Would you mind checking if this dish is vegetarian?" To ask a question politely, use Could you tell me + the question with no inversion: "Could you tell me what the special is?" (not "what is the special"). To order, use I would like: "I would like the steak well-done." These polite requests work for any situation: ordering food, making a small complaint, or asking about the service charge.

1. Which polite request is correct?
A Could you bringing the menu?
B Could you bring the menu, please?
C Could you to bring the menu?
2. After "Would you mind", which form do we use?
A the base verb ("Would you mind check...")
B the verb + -ing ("Would you mind checking...")
C to + verb ("Would you mind to check...")
3. After "Could you tell me...", the question is:
A "...what the special is?" (no inversion)
B "...what is the special?"
C "...what does the special?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests (Could you / Would you mind)

GRAMMAR

Como pedir coisas de forma educada. / How to ask for things politely.

Structure / EstruturaExampleNote / Nota
Could you + verbo baseCould you bring the menu?verbo base, sem -ing / base verb
Would you mind + verbo-ingWould you mind checking the dish?verbo + -ing
Could you tell me + perguntaCould you tell me what the special is?sem inversão / no inversion
I would like + substantivoI would like the steak well-done."I want" educado / polite "I want"

Key point / Ponto-chave: use Could you + verbo base, ou Would you mind + verbo-ing, para pedir com educação. Após Could you tell me, a pergunta não inverte: "what the special is", não "what is the special". Para pedir um prato, use I would like. / Use Could you + base verb, or Would you mind + verb-ing; after Could you tell me, no inversion; to order, use I would like.
