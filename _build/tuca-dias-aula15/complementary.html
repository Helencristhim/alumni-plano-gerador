Lesson 15 -- Eating Out: Ordering & Polite Requests

Series / Travel
English with Lucy -- "Ordering Food in a Restaurant in English" (YouTube)

A clear, friendly guide to ordering at a restaurant in English: reading the menu, ordering each course, asking polite questions, and asking for the bill. Full of natural dining vocabulary.

Watch with English subtitles. Then write four polite requests you could use at a restaurant, two with "Could you..." and two with "Would you mind...".

Podcast
All Ears English -- "At the Restaurant: How to Order Politely in English"

A short, practical episode on dining out: ordering a meal, asking about the menu, making a polite complaint, and handling the bill. You will hear words like dish, course, and service charge used naturally.

After listening, record a one-minute voice note ordering a full meal for two. Use Could you, Would you mind, and at least four restaurant words.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Polite Requests: Could you...? and Would you mind...?"

A practical grammar video on making polite requests in English, with Could you + base verb and Would you mind + verb-ing. Exactly today's grammar focus.

After watching, write four polite requests for a restaurant, then change each "Could you..." into "Would you mind..." (remember the -ing).
