Grammar Rule

Polite Requests and Offers

Polite formUseExample
Could you + base verbA polite requestCould you sign here, please?
Would you like + nounA polite offerWould you like a coffee?
Would you like + to + verbA polite offer (action)Would you like to sit down?
May I + base verbAsk permission politelyMay I take your coat?

Key idea: A polite form starts with Could you, Would you like, or May I, and then the base verb. These soft forms are much more polite than direct ones like "Sit down." or "Want a coffee?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You like a coffee?"
"Would you like a coffee?"

For a polite offer, always use Would you like. For a request, use Could you + base verb. Never skip the polite word.
