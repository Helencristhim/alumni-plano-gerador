Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Appointment -- a meeting at a fixed time
"I have an appointment with a new client at ten." (Compromisso / hora marcada)
Reception -- the front desk where guests arrive
"Please wait for me at the reception." (Recepção)
Welcome -- to receive a guest in a warm way
"I welcome every client at the door." (Dar as boas-vindas)
Pleasure -- a happy feeling when you do something
"It is a pleasure to meet you." (Prazer)
Refreshment -- a drink or a small snack you offer
"Would you like a refreshment before we start?" (Algo para beber/comer)
Brochure -- a small booklet about your company
"Here is a brochure about our products." (Folheto)
Agenda -- the list of topics for a meeting
"Let me show you the agenda for today." (Pauta)
Available -- free and ready to meet
"Our director is available now." (Disponível)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diana has an appointment with Rafael at ten. When she arrives, Laura at the reception says: "Welcome to TVT. Would you like a coffee?" Rafael comes down and says it is a pleasure to meet her. "May I take your coat? Could you follow me to the meeting room, please?" He gives her a brochure and shows her the agenda. The polite words make Diana feel very welcome.

1. What does Diana have at ten o'clock?
A An appointment with Rafael.
B A coffee break alone.
C A day off.
2. Which offer is polite?
A "Would you like a coffee?"
B "You want a coffee?"
C "Coffee now."
3. How does Rafael ask Diana to follow him?
A "Could you follow me to the meeting room, please?"
B "Follow me now."
C "You follow me?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests and Offers

GRAMMAR

Usado para receber um cliente de forma educada. / Used to receive a client politely.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Could you + verbo basePedido educado / A polite requestCould you wait here, please?
Would you like + substantivoOferta educada / A polite offerWould you like a coffee?
May I + verbo basePedir permissão / Ask permissionMay I take your coat?
Key point / Ponto-chaveA forma polida usa Could you, Would you like ou May I e depois o verbo base. Soa muito mais educado que "Want a coffee?" ou "Wait here."
