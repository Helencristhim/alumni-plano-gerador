Grammar

Anna has a business lunch with a client. The waiter brings the menu. Anna reads the dishes. "I would like the fish, please," she says. For a drink, she asks: "Can I have a water, please?" At the end, Anna says: "Can I have the bill, please?" The client says: "Your English is good, Anna."

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Ordering Politely

Grammar

Para pedir comida com educacao, use I would like ou Can I have...? e sempre termine com please. Nunca use "I want" (soa rude). Cuidado: "I like" significa gostar; "I would like" significa querer/pedir. / To order food politely, use I would like or Can I have...? and always add please.

Polite formExamplePortugues
I would like + foodI would like the fish, please.Eu gostaria do peixe, por favor.
Can I have + food?Can I have a water, please?Pode me trazer uma agua, por favor?
Always add please..., please...., por favor.
Avoid (rude)"I want the fish.""Eu quero o peixe." (rude)
