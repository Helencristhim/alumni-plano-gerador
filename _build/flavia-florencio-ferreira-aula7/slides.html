Chapter 3: Am, Is, Are + -ing

The Present Continuous

Describing actions happening now

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Right now, I am standing at our booth."

"My colleague is presenting on the stage."

"Two partners are waiting for me."

"The audience is arriving now."

Two parts every time: am / is / are + a verb with -ing. Why am with I, is with my colleague, and are with two partners?

SubjectbeExample
IamI am waiting.
he / she / itisShe is presenting.
you / we / theyareThey are waiting.

We use this for actions happening NOW. Negative: I am not waiting. Question: Are you waiting?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I working right now."
"I am working right now."
"She is present now."
"She is presenting now."
"They is waiting."
"They are waiting."

Always two parts: am / is / are + verb + -ing.

Practice

Am, Is, or Are?

Say it first, then click to check

Detail

Making the -ing

most verbs: + ing · verbs with -e: drop the e · short vowel + consonant: double it

"wait → ___waiting"
"prepare → ___preparing"
"present → ___presenting"
"get → ___getting"
Chapter 4: A Call from the Office

The Office Wants to Know

The conversation begins

Dialogue

A Call from the Fair

T
Hi Flavia! It's Tom, from the office. What is happening at the fair right now?
F
Hi Tom! I am standing at our booth right now. It is very busy!
T
Wonderful! I am reading your report here at the office. What are the other people doing?
F
My colleague is presenting on the stage, and two partners are waiting to talk to us.
T
Great. Are you preparing for your speech?
F
Yes, I am preparing now. The audience is arriving, and I am ready!
Comprehension

About Tom

1. Where is Tom?
At the office.
2. What is Tom doing right now?
He is reading Flavia's report.
3. What does Tom ask Flavia about?
What is happening at the fair, and if she is preparing for her speech.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

On the Call Again

Report the Moment

Free Practice

The Whole Call

Scenario: From "Hello" to "Talk soon": answer the call, say where you are, describe everything that is happening right now — your colleague, the partners, the audience, and your speech — and agree to call again later.

No keywords. The call is yours!
