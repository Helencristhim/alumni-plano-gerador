Lesson 16 -- Closing the Deal

Series
Suits -- Season 2, Episode 6 "All In"

A deal goes to the closing: the parties finalize the price, sign and countersign the documents, and the proceeds are transferred. Listen for how the lawyers report what the other side said, and notice reported speech: "he said the price was final", "she told me she would attend".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 reported sentences you hear (said / told me + the verb one step back) and the closing words around them.

Podcast
Legal English Podcast -- "Closing a Deal and Reporting What Was Said"

A practical episode on what happens at the closing of a deal -- finalizing terms, signing, witnesses, the effective date and the proceeds -- and how lawyers report what each side said in the negotiation. Exactly the language you need for the M&A work you do at the office.

Note 3 things the hosts report someone saying, and rewrite each one as reported speech (said / told me + verb one step back).

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Reported speech: said and told"

A short, clear lesson on how to report what someone said: the difference between said and told, and how the verb moves one step back (is → was, will → would). Directly useful for briefing your team after a closing call.

After watching, write five reported sentences about a deal, then record yourself reading them with at least four closing words from today.
