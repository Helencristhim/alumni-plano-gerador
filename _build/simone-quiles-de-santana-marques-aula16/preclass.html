Aula 16 -- Pre-class

Closing the Deal -- Signing & Reported Speech

Learn the language of signing and completing a deal, and how to report what people said in a meeting: the partner said the price was final, Daniel told me the deal would close on Friday. New vocabulary: closing, completion, finalize, countersign, effective date, ratify, witness, proceeds. Grammar: reported speech -- said / told + the verb one step back.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Closing -- the final stage where a deal is signed and completed
"The closing is scheduled for Friday." (Fechamento)
Completion -- the point when a deal or transaction is fully finished
"Completion takes place on the effective date." (Conclusão)
Finalize -- to complete the terms so nothing more can change
"We finalize the price before the closing." (Finalizar)
Countersign -- to add a second signature that confirms the first
"Each party countersigns the last page." (Referendar / contra-assinar)
Effective date -- the date on which a contract starts to apply
"The effective date is the first of next month." (Data de vigência)
Ratify -- to formally approve and confirm an agreement
"The board ratifies the deal next week." (Ratificar)
Witness -- a person who watches a signing and confirms it took place
"Two witnesses are present at the signing." (Testemunha)
Proceeds -- the money received from a sale or a deal
"The proceeds are transferred the same day." (Valores / receita da operação)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Para reportar o que alguém disse, use said ou told, e mova o verbo um passo para trás: is → was, will → would, can → could. Use told + a pessoa ("told me", "told her") e said sem pessoa ("said that..."). Nunca diga "said me". / To report what someone said, use said or told, and move the verb one step back: is → was, will → would, can → could. Use told + a person ("told me") and said with no person ("said that..."). Never say "said me".

Direct / DiretoChange / MudançaReported / Reportado
"The price is final."is → wasHe said the price was final.
"We will sign Friday."will → wouldHe told me they would sign Friday.
"I can attend."can → couldShe said she could attend.
