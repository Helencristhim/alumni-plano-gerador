Chapter 3

will vs going to

Predictions and decisions vs plans and intentions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Next year, I am going to lead two projects."

"I think I will win the election."

"In five years, I am going to pursue the presidency."

"Don't worry, I will send the proposal today."

FormUseExample
going to + verbplan / intention (decided before)I am going to lead two projects.
will + verbprediction / beliefI think I will win.
will + verbdecision on the spot / promiseI will send it today.
Time wordsnext year, in five years, by 2030, soon
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I will to travel next year."

✓ RIGHT

"I will travel next year." (no "to")

✗ WRONG

"I bought the ticket. I will travel." (already a plan)

✓ RIGHT

"I bought the ticket. I am going to travel." (plan)

✗ WRONG

"I am going pursue the presidency."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to pursue the presidency." (going TO)

will + base verb (never "will to"). going to + base verb for plans. A decided plan = going to; a prediction or a decision on the spot = will.
