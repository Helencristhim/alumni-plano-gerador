Talking About Goals & the Future

Talking about goals, plans and predictions. Key words: goal, ambition, aim, objective, vision, pursue, aspire, long-term, candidate, seek. Grammar: future forms -- "will" (predictions, decisions, promises) vs "going to" (plans, intentions) + time expressions (next year, in five years, by 2030, soon).

Stage 16.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Goal -- meta/objetivo (something you want to achieve)
"My main goal is to lead the association."
Ambition -- ambição (a strong desire to achieve something)
"His ambition is to become president of the board."
Aim -- propósito/alvo (the purpose you want to reach)
"Our aim is to grow the team this year."
Objective -- objetivo (a clear, specific result you want)
"The main objective is to expand into Europe."
Vision -- visão (a clear idea of the future you want)
"I have a clear vision for the next five years."
Pursue -- buscar/perseguir (to work hard to reach a goal)
"I want to pursue the presidency of the association."
Aspire -- aspirar (to strongly hope to reach something high)
"I aspire to lead an international organization."
Long-term -- de longo prazo (lasting far into the future)
"My long-term goal is the presidency."
Candidate -- candidato (a person who runs for a position)
"I am going to run as a candidate for the board."
Seek -- procurar/buscar (to try to find or get something)
"I seek a leadership role in the next few years."

Stage 16.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the dinner, a counterpart asks Marcos about his goals. Marcos shares his plan: "This year, I am going to finish my certification. Next year, I am going to lead two projects." Notice that for plans he already decided, he uses going to. Then he makes a prediction: "I think I will be ready to run as a candidate by then." For predictions and beliefs, we use will. His long-term vision is clear: "In five years, I am going to pursue the presidency." When the mentor offers help, Marcos answers on the spot: "Thank you, I will send you my proposal today." A decision made in the moment also uses will. Remember: will + base verb (never "will to"), and going to + base verb for plans.

1. Que forma usamos para um plano que já decidimos?
A "will" + verbo (will lead).
B "going to" + verbo (going to lead).
C "will to" + verbo (will to lead).
2. Que forma usamos para uma previsão ou crença?
A "will" + verbo base (I think I will win).
B "going to" sempre.
C presente simples (I win).
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I will to pursue the presidency."
B "I am going to pursue the presidency."
C "I am going pursue the presidency."

Stage 16.4: Grammar Tip -- will vs going to

GRAMMAR

Como falar do futuro (explicação em inglês e português).

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample

will + verboprevisão / crença (prediction)I think I will win.
will + verbodecisão na hora / promessa (on the spot)I will send it today.
