Chapter 3: The Code

Who You Are, What You Do

I work at · I manage · I am responsible for

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work at Sanofi."

"I manage the immunology portfolio."

"I am responsible for eight disease areas."

"My team visits doctors every week."

Three sentences say I. One says my team. What changes in the verb?

FormUseExample
I / you / we / they + verbFacts about your job. Things that are always true.I work at Sanofi.
he / she / it / my team + verb + sThe same idea, but for one person or one group.My team visits doctors.
Negative: don't / doesn't + verbTo say what is not true.I don't work in oncology.
Question: Do / Does + person + verb?To ask a colleague about their job.Do you work in immunology?
Ready phrase: I am responsible for + thingNothing to build. Learn it as one block and reuse it forever.I am responsible for eight disease areas.

"I am responsible for..." is not grammar to build. It is a ready phrase: pick it up and use it.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work in Sanofi."
"I work at Sanofi."
"I responsible for eight disease areas."
"I am responsible for eight disease areas."
"My team visit doctors every week."
"My team visits doctors every week."

A company always takes at. "Responsible" always needs am in front of it. And my team is one group, so the verb takes -s.
