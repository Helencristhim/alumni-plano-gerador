Extra material to practice outside class. Check the box when you finish it.

Series
Suits — Season 1, Episode 1 (Netflix)

In this first episode, almost every character has to say who they are and what they take care of — the same task as your lesson 1, at the office. It is business English at native speed, but the scenes are easy to follow: you understand the story even when you do not catch every word.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 moments when someone introduces themselves or describes their job. Do not try to understand 100% — the goal is to hear the structure "I'm... / I work... / I'm responsible for...".

Podcast
Business English Pod — BEP 400: Socializing with Colleagues 1 (Meeting New People)

You already listen to podcasts every day — now with a target. This episode is your lesson 1 in audio: people meeting at work, saying their name, their company, their job and what they take care of. The pace is made for A2-B1, with clear explanations and a lot of repetition.

Tip: listen ONCE on the way to work, with no pause — just let the sound in. Then listen again and repeat the sentences out loud. Your bottleneck is speaking, not listening: saying it out loud is worth more than understanding every word.

YouTube
BBC Learning English — Introduce yourself and make some friends (English at Work, ep. 4)

The BBC made this series for people exactly like you: adults who work in a company and have to introduce themselves in English. This episode practices "My name is... / I work at... / I'm responsible for..." — the three sentences you take from lesson 1.

Tip: after the video, say 5 sentences about YOUR job at Sanofi out loud, with no notes — one for each disease area you manage. Record them on your phone and listen. Speaking alone, out loud, is the practice that opens your mouth in the meeting.
