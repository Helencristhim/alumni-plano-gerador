Grammar Rule

The Four Building Blocks

BlockExample
Role -- present simpleI am an IT project manager.
Experience -- present perfectI have worked here for six years.
Routine -- frequency adverbI usually lead international teams.
Rapport -- a questionWhat do you do for work?

Key idea: Use for with a period (for six years) and since with a point in time (since 2018). Put the four blocks together for a complete, confident introduction.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I work at Oracle since 2018."
"I have worked at Oracle since 2018."

For experience that started in the past and continues now, use the present perfect. Use since with a start point and for with a period.
