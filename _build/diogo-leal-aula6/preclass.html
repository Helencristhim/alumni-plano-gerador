"I always highlight my main strengths." (Destacar)
Confident -- sure of yourself and your skills
"I feel confident when I introduce myself." (Confiante)

"My biggest strength is solving problems." (Ponto forte)

"A good introduction is short and concise." (Conciso)
Memorable -- easy to remember
"I want a memorable first impression." (Memorável)
Pitch -- a short, persuasive self-introduction
"My elevator pitch is thirty seconds long." (Apresentação rápida)

"I build rapport with a friendly question." (Conexão)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo is an IT project manager at Oracle. He has worked in technology for more than ten years, and he usually leads international teams. Tomorrow he introduces himself at a leadership forum, so tonight he prepares a concise and memorable pitch. First, he summarizes his role in one sentence. Then he highlights his main strength: turning a complex problem into a simple plan. He wants to sound confident and build good rapport. A strong introduction uses the present simple for your role, the present perfect for your experience, and a frequency adverb for your routine.

1. How long has Diogo worked in technology?
A For more than ten years.
B Since last month.
C For two weeks.
2. What does he highlight in his pitch?
A His main strength.
B His lunch plans.
C The weather.
3. Which tense describes his experience?
A Present perfect (has worked).
B Past continuous.
C Future simple.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Putting It All Together

GRAMMAR

As estruturas do Bloco 1 em uma só introdução. / The Block 1 structures in one introduction.

Block / BlocoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present SimpleSeu cargo / Your roleI am an IT project manager. I work at Oracle.
PossessivesSua equipe / Your teamMy team and my main project.
Frequency AdverbSua rotina / Your routineI usually lead international teams.
Present PerfectSua experiência / Your experienceI have worked in technology for ten years.
QuestionsCriar rapport / Build rapportWhat do you do? How long have you been here?
Key point / Ponto-chaveUse for + período (for ten years) e since + ponto no tempo (since 2018) com o present perfect. Mantenha a introdução concisa: cargo, experiência, um ponto forte e uma pergunta.
