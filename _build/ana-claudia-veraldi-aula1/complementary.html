Lesson 1 -- Who Is Ana?

Grammar Video
Present Simple and Present Continuous -- 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

A short, clear lesson on the difference between what you do every day (present simple) and what is happening right now (present continuous). Connection to Lesson 1: this is the contrast behind the classic mistake "I am working here for six years" -- once you feel these two tenses, choosing the present perfect for your experience becomes natural.

Tip: pause after each example and say one true sentence about your own work -- one with present simple, one with present continuous.

Business English
Business English B1-B2: Participating in Meetings 1 (Elena Adamova)

Practical language for taking part in a work meeting in English: giving your opinion, agreeing, disagreeing politely, and asking questions. Connection to Lesson 1: exactly the confident, real-time speaking you are building for remote calls and interviews with an international team.

Tip: write down 5 phrases you could use in your next stand-up. Say each one out loud twice.

TED Talk
How to Stay Calm When You Know You'll Be Stressed (Daniel Levitin, TED)

A neuroscientist explains how to prepare for high-pressure moments so your mind stays clear. Connection to Lesson 1: the nervousness that blocks your speaking in fast conversations is exactly this -- and the ideas here help you stay calm in an interview or a live call.

Tip: listen for the main idea first, without stopping. Then watch again and note one strategy you can try before your next English call.
