Chapter 3: The Code

Always vs Right Now

I live · I am restoring

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I live in a small town in the countryside."

"This month I am restoring an old cabinet."

"My dogs sleep in the backyard every afternoon."

"Right now they are barking at the mailman."

Why blue for things that are always true, but green for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Facts, routines and permanent situations — always trueI live in the countryside.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Now, these days, temporary or unfinishedI am restoring a cabinet.
He / she / itPresent simple adds -s; continuous uses isShe fixes it. / She is fixing it.

Time words help: every day, always, usually → simple · right now, today, this week → continuous.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Every weekend I am fixing something."
"Every weekend I fix something."
"Right now I take care of a sick dog."
"Right now I am taking care of a sick dog."
"I am liking this quiet town."
"I like this quiet town."

Routines use the present simple. Things happening now use the present continuous. Verbs of state — like, want, need, know, believe — almost never take -ing.

like · love · want · need · know · believe · understand

"I ___love the silence here." (love)
