Chapter 3: The Code

Your Experience in Three Tenses

I have worked · I test · I worked

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in quality assurance for six years."

"I test software every day."

"I have tested hundreds of systems."

"I worked at WIPRO for six years."

Why green for experience up to now, but blue for a finished job?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now (for / since), when the time is not finishedI have worked here for 6 years.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Facts and routines — what you do every dayI test software every day.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		A finished action at a finished time (at WIPRO, in 2018, yesterday)I worked at WIPRO for six years.

for + a period (for 6 years) · since + a starting point (since 2018).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work here since 2018."
"I have worked here since 2018."
"I have worked yesterday."
"I worked yesterday."
"I am working here for six years."
"I have been working here for six years."

Duration up to now uses have / has + past participle with for / since. A finished time (yesterday, in 2018) always uses the past simple.
