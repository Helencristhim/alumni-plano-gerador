Who Is Ana, Really? -- Your Life, Not Your Job Title

Introducing yourself through your life: your town, your house and your dogs. Key words: neighborhood, countryside, peaceful, to move away, to restore, handy, stray, to take care of, cozy, neighbor, backyard, chores. Structures: present simple (routines and permanent facts) vs present continuous (right now and these days), plus state verbs that never take -ing.

Vocabulary

Neighborhood -- the area around your home and the people who live there

Countryside -- the land and small towns outside big cities

Peaceful -- calm and quiet, with nothing to disturb you
"My street is so peaceful that I can hear the birds."
To move away -- to leave one home and go to live in another place
"We moved away from the city and never looked back."
To restore -- to repair something old so that it looks new again
"I am restoring an old wooden cabinet this month."
Handy -- good at repairing and making things with your hands
"She is very handy - she fixes everything herself."
Stray -- an animal that lives on the street and has no owner
"Both of my dogs were strays before I found them."
To take care of -- to look after someone or something
"I take care of two dogs and a very old house."
Cozy -- small, warm and comfortable in a pleasant way
"The kitchen is small, but it is really cozy in winter."
Neighbor -- a person who lives very near you
"My closest neighbor lives two hundred meters away."
Backyard -- the outdoor space behind your house
"The dogs sleep in the backyard every afternoon."
Chores -- the small jobs you have to do at home again and again
"On Saturdays I do my chores and then I rest."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana lives in a small town in the countryside, about forty thousand people. She grew up in a busy neighborhood in Sao Paulo, but a few years ago she moved away because she wants a quieter life. Her street is so peaceful that she can hear the birds in the morning. Every day, her two dogs wake her up at six, and every afternoon they sleep in the backyard. Both of them were strays before she found them, and now she takes care of them like family. On Saturdays she does her chores in the morning and then she rests. Ana is very handy: this month she is restoring an old wooden cabinet, and right now she is painting the kitchen wall, because she wants the house to feel cozy. Her closest neighbor lives two hundred meters away, and she likes it that way.

1. Why do we say "she is restoring an old cabinet" and not "she restores"?
A It is in progress these days, not finished (present continuous).
B It is something she does every single day.
C It happened once and it is completely finished.
2. "Her dogs sleep in the backyard every afternoon" uses the present simple because:
A It is happening at this exact moment.
B It is a routine that repeats every day.
C It is a temporary situation, just for this month.
3. The text says "she wants a quieter life", never "she is wanting". Why?
A Because the sentence is about the past.
B Because "want" is an irregular verb.
C Because "want" is a state verb, and state verbs do not take -ing.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to say what is always true and what is happening now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he / she + -s)		Facts, routines and permanent situations - always true.I live in the countryside.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Right now, today, these days - temporary or unfinished.I am restoring a cabinet.
Negativedo not / does not + verb · am not / is not / are not + -ingI do not drive to work. / She is not sleeping.
QuestionDo / Does + subject + verb · Am / Is / Are + subject + -ingDo you live here? / Are you working now?
State verbslike, love, want, need, know, believe, understand almost never take -ing: "I like this town" (not "I am liking").
Time wordsevery day, always, usually, never → simple · right now, today, this week, these days → continuous

Lesson 1 — Who Is Ana, Really? -- Your Life, Not Your Job Title

Series
The Repair Shop -- Season 1, Episode 1 (full episode)

People bring broken family treasures to a barn full of craftspeople, who bring them back to life: furniture, clocks, old toys. Every object has a story behind it. If you love restoring things, this is your world in English -- and the restorers explain exactly what they are doing while they do it.

Tip: listen for the present continuous. The restorers narrate their own work: "I am taking this apart", "I am sanding it down". Say one sentence about what you are fixing at home this week.

Podcast
Urban Exodus, E69 -- Academics leave city life for a farm in the Australian bush

Louise and David sold a comfortable city life in Sydney and moved 27 kilometers from the nearest town. They talk about what they gained, what they miss, and what nobody tells you about the silence. It is the same decision you made, told by other people -- in Australian English.

Tip: this is a different accent from the American English you hear in class. Listen once for the general story only, without trying to catch every word. That is the skill we are training.
