The Engine

Simple past: regular and irregular verbs

Grammar Discovery

What Changed?

"Yesterday, I walked for hours."

"We cooked dinner together."

"I went to the mountains."

"We saw a waterfall."

TypePresentPast
Regular (+ed)walk / cookwalked / cooked
Irregulargo / seewent / saw (new form)
Negative—did not + base verb: I did not go.
Question—Did + you + base verb? Did you go? / Did she say?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I goed to the lake."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to the lake."

✗ WRONG

"Did you went there?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you go there?"

✗ WRONG

"I did not saw it."

✓ RIGHT

"I did not see it."

After "did" and "did not", the main verb goes back to its base form. The word "did" already carries the past.
