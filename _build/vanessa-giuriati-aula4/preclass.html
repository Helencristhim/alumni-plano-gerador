Talking About the Past

Simple Past for telling stories. Key words: trip, celebration, decision, surprise, happen, remember, realize, memorable. Grammar: simple past (regular -ed + irregular verbs), negative and question with did, time markers and connectors.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Trip -- a short journey to a place and back (viagem)
"I took a trip to the mountains last year."
Celebration -- a happy event for a special occasion (comemoração)
"The celebration lasted until midnight."
Decision -- a choice you make after thinking (decisão)
"It was a hard decision, but a good one."
Surprise -- something unexpected that happens (surpresa)
"The party was a complete surprise."
Happen -- to take place; to occur (acontecer)
"Something funny happened on the way home."
Remember -- to keep something in your memory (lembrar)
"I still remember that day clearly."
Realize -- to suddenly understand something (perceber/se dar conta)
"I realized that I forgot my keys."
Memorable -- special and easy to remember (memorável/inesquecível)
"It was a truly memorable evening."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Last summer, Marcos went on a trip with his family. They drove to a small town near the sea. On the first day, they walked on the beach and had dinner at a local restaurant. The next morning, something unexpected happened: they saw a group of dolphins in the water. Marcos did not expect that, and he took many photos. "I realized how much I needed a break," he says. It was a memorable trip. He still remembers it today.

1. Why does the text say "they went" and not "they goed"?
A Because "go" is an irregular verb; its past form is "went".
B Because "goed" is informal.
C Because the sentence is in the present.
2. What is the correct negative form of "he expected"?
A He did not expect.
B He did not expected.
C He not expected.
3. What surprised Marcos on the trip?
A The long drive.
B Seeing a group of dolphins in the water.
C The local restaurant.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Simple Past

GRAMMAR

Como falar de ações terminadas no passado. / How to talk about finished past actions.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
Regular (+ed)I walked. We cooked dinner.Eu caminhei. Nós cozinhamos o jantar.
Irregulargo → went; have → had; see → saw.ir → fui/foi; ter → tive/teve; ver → vi/viu.
NegativeI did not go. She did not see it.Eu não fui. Ela não viu.
QuestionDid you go? What did she say?Você foi? O que ela disse?
After "did"The verb goes back to the base form: Did you go? (NOT "did you went"). "Did" already carries the past.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Tell a short story about something that happened to you recently — a trip, a surprise, or a memorable day. Use simple past (regular and irregular), at least three time markers, and two connectors (first, then, after that, finally). Speak slowly and take your time.
