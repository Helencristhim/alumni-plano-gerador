Lesson 15 -- The Job Interview

Film
"The Pursuit of Happyness" -- the interview scene

Chris Gardner tells his professional story under pressure. Watch how he turns a difficult past into a confident answer, mixing what he has done over time with one finished example.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three sentences in the present perfect about your career and two in the past simple about a finished moment.

Podcast
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Answer 'Tell Me About Yourself'"

A clear guide to structuring an interview answer: your track record, your strengths, and one strong example with a result, told in the right tense.

Record a 90-second interview answer: introduce yourself, your experience up to now, one finished example, and why you are the right candidate.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Present perfect vs past simple"

A focused lesson on when to use the present perfect (experience up to now, ever/never/for/since) and the past simple (finished time, last year/ago). Directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, write six sentences about your career, mixing track record, strengths, and one "last year" example. Read them aloud.
