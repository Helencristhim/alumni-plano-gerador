Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Present perfect vs past simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The orange words show experience and finished moments. Which ones have a finished time?

"I have led delivery teams for the last six years."

"Three years ago, I joined the company to fix a late project."

"I have never missed a launch date."

"Last year, we had an outage, and I led the response."

Grammar Rule

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

TenseUse it forExample
Present perfectexperience up to now, no finished timeI have led eight releases.
Past simplea finished moment with a time wordLast year I led the response.
Present perfect signalsever, never, for, since, so farI have never missed a launch.
Past simple signalsyesterday, last year, ago, in 2020Three years ago I joined the team.

Key idea: Use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) for your experience up to now, with no finished time. Switch to the past simple the moment you say when: "I have managed many projects. In 2022, I managed our biggest launch."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Last year I have led the incident response."
"Last year I led the incident response."

With a finished time word (last year, ago, in 2020), use the past simple. Keep the present perfect for experience up to now (ever, never, for, since).

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Tense

"I _____have managed eight releases in my career." (experience up to now)
"Last year, I _____managed our biggest launch." (finished time)
"I have worked here _____since 2021." (present perfect signal)
"Three years _____ago, I joined this team." (past simple signal)
