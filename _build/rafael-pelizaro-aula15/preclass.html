Aula 15 -- Pre-class

The Job Interview -- Telling Your Story

Telling your professional story in a job interview: the present perfect for experience up to now (I have led eight releases) and the past simple for a finished moment (Last year I led the response). New vocabulary: candidate, recruiter, resume, shortlist, strengths, qualification, expertise, track record.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael is a candidate for a senior project manager role. In the interview, he describes his experience up to now with the present perfect. He says, "I have worked in technology for twelve years, and I have led delivery teams for the last six. I have managed eight major releases, and I have never missed a launch date." Then the recruiter asks for a real example, so Rafael switches to the past simple, because the time is finished: "Three years ago, I joined the company to fix a late project. I rebuilt the plan, and we shipped the product on time. Last year, we had a serious outage, and I led the response." His clear track record and his strengths put him on the shortlist.

1. Why does Rafael use the present perfect at the start?
A Because he is talking about tomorrow.
B Because he is describing his experience up to now, with no finished time.
C Because he is asking a question.
2. Why does he switch to the past simple for the example?
A Because the time is finished (three years ago, last year).
B Because the recruiter speaks English.
C Because the past simple is more formal.
3. What put Rafael on the shortlist?
A A long resume with no examples.
B Only his salary expectations.
C His clear track record and his strengths.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Usamos o present perfect para a experiência até agora (sem tempo terminado) e o past simple para um momento terminado com tempo definido. / We use the present perfect for experience up to now (no finished time) and the past simple for a finished moment with a definite time.

Tense / TempoUse it for / Usar paraExample / Exemplo
Present perfectexperiência até agora, sem tempo terminadoI have led eight releases.
Past simplemomento terminado com tempo definidoLast year I led the response.
Present perfect signalsever, never, for, since, so farI have never missed a launch.
Past simple signalsyesterday, last year, ago, in 2020Three years ago I joined the team.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use o present perfect (have/has + particípio) para a experiência geral da sua carreira até agora, com palavras como ever, never, for, since. No momento em que você diz quando (last year, in 2022, three years ago), troque para o past simple. É o erro mais comum em entrevista: "Last year I have led..." está errado; o certo é "Last year I led...".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each interview situation.

The interviewer asks about your experience up to now. The best sentence is:
A "I have led delivery teams for six years, and I have never missed a launch."
B "Last year I have led delivery teams for six years."
C "I will lead delivery teams since six years."
You want to give one finished example. The clearest answer is:
A "Three years ago, I have joined the company to fix a project."
B "Three years ago, I joined the company and fixed a late project."
C "I join the company three years ago to fix a project."
The recruiter asks why you are a strong candidate. The best answer is:
A "I have no real examples, but I am sure I am good."
B "I have the right qualification and a strong track record of results."
C "I do not know, the resume says everything."
