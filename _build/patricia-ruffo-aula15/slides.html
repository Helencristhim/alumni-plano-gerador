Chapter 3 -- Putting It Together

The Four Blocks

Structure, data, methods, cause and effect -- in one talk

Review

Four Tools, One Talk

Each orange phrase does a different job. What is the job of each one?

"Firstly, let me give you a quick overview, then I will move on to the findings."

"The marker showed a steady increase, reached a peak, and then a plateau."

"The samples were analyzed in our lab, and a standard protocol was followed."

"The result is correlated with the treatment, but correlation is not causation."

The Map

How the Blocks Fit

BlockIts job in the talkSignal language
StructureTells the audience where you areFirstly, moving on, to summarize
DataShows the trend in the numbersincrease, peak, plateau, proportion
MethodsGives credibility (how it was done)The passive: was analyzed, was followed
Cause & EffectGives meaning, honestlycorrelated with, but not causation

Key idea: a strong talk uses all four. The structure carries the audience, the data gives the picture, the passive methods give credibility, and the cause-effect language keeps you honest about what the results really mean.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The two lines move together, so the treatment causes the result."
"The two lines are correlated, but correlation is not causation."

A trend in the data shows a correlation. It does not prove the cause. Honest speakers say so.
