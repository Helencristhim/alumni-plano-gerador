Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Plenary -- a large session that all the attendees join together
"My talk is the opening plenary of the symposium." (Sessão plenária)
Audience -- the group of people listening to your presentation
"I always look at the audience, not at my notes." (Plateia / público)
Deliver -- to give a talk or presentation to an audience
"I will deliver a twenty-minute talk on glycemic control." (Apresentar / proferir)
Engage -- to catch and keep the attention of your audience
"I ask a short question to engage the audience." (Envolver / cativar)
Pacing -- the speed and rhythm at which you speak
"Good pacing means I pause after each key number." (Ritmo da fala)
Rehearse -- to practise your talk before you give it for real
"I rehearse my talk two or three times the night before." (Ensaiar)
Critique -- honest, useful comments on how to improve your work
"My coach gave me a helpful critique after the rehearsal." (Crítica construtiva)
Slide -- one screen or page of your presentation deck
"On the next slide, you can see the main chart." (Slide / lâmina)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Patricia is ready to deliver the opening plenary at the Abbott symposium. The night before, she goes through every slide and rehearses the whole talk. Firstly, she explains the framework and the methods: the samples were analyzed in the central lab. Moving on, she describes the trends -- the main marker showed a steady increase, reached a peak, and then a plateau. Then she discusses the cause: the result is correlated with the new treatment, but she is careful to say that correlation is not causation. To keep the audience with her, she asks a short question and works on her pacing, pausing after each key number. Her coach David listens to the rehearsal and gives her an honest critique: the structure is clear, but she should slow down on the methods slide and engage the audience more during the results. She decides to rehearse it twice more before the big day.

1. What does Patricia do the night before her talk?
A She goes through every slide and rehearses the whole talk.
B She writes a brand-new study from scratch.
C She cancels the plenary session.
2. How is she careful about the cause of the result?
A She says the treatment definitely causes the result.
B She says the result is correlated with the treatment, but correlation is not causation.
C She ignores the cause completely.
3. What does David's critique tell her to improve?
A To make the structure less clear.
B To slow down on the methods slide and engage the audience more during the results.
C To remove all the data from the talk.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The full talk, four tools

GRAMMAR

Esta é uma aula de revisão: não há gramática nova. Você reúne as quatro ferramentas do Bloco 3 numa só fala. / This is a review lesson: no new grammar. You combine the four tools of Block 3 in a single talk.

Tool / FerramentaLanguage / LinguagemExample / Exemplo
Structure / EstruturaFirstly, / Moving on, / To summarize"Firstly, let me explain the methods."
Data / Dadosincrease, peak, plateau, proportion"The marker reached a peak and then a plateau."
Methods / MétodosPassive: was/were + past participle"The samples were analyzed in our lab."
Cause & Effectcorrelated with ... but not causation"It is correlated with the treatment, but correlation is not causation."

Key point / Ponto-chave: uma boa fala usa as quatro ferramentas. A estrutura guia o público, os dados mostram o quadro, a voz passiva dá credibilidade aos métodos, e a linguagem de causa-efeito mantém você honesta. / A good talk uses all four. The structure guides the audience, the data shows the picture, the passive gives credibility to the methods, and cause-effect language keeps you honest.
