Lesson 15 -- Review: Conference Presentation

Talk
TED -- a full science keynote (e.g. Hans Rosling, "The best stats you've ever seen")

Watch a complete conference talk and notice the four blocks at work: the structure, the way the speaker describes the data, how the methods are explained, and how cause and effect are discussed honestly.

Watch with English subtitles. Note how the speaker engages the audience and controls pacing. Write down one phrase for each of the four blocks.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Delivering a Presentation with Confidence"

An episode on the delivery side of a talk: how to engage the audience, control your pacing, and handle questions and critique after you present.

Record a 3-minute conference talk that uses all four blocks, then listen back and give yourself an honest critique of your pacing.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to give a great presentation"

A clear lesson on putting a whole presentation together: opening, structure, delivering with good pacing, and engaging your audience from start to finish.

After watching, rehearse your symposium talk once more, focusing on the three points David gave Patricia: pacing, engaging the audience, and adding energy to your key point.
