Chapter 3 -- Setting Expectations

The "This Is What We Need" Language

Obligation, rules, advice, and necessity

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence sets an expectation. Notice the orange words.

"Every manager must own the message."

"The teams have to hit the milestones."

"You should walk the floor and answer questions."

"People need to feel safe to flag a problem."

The Rule

Must / Have To / Should / Need To

FormUse & Example
MustA strong rule from the speaker: "Every manager must own the message."
Have toAn obligation from outside (plan, board, rule): "The teams have to hit the milestones."
ShouldAdvice or a recommendation: "You should walk the floor."
Need toA necessity: "People need to feel safe to speak up."

Tip: all four are followed by the base verb (must own, have to hit, should walk, need to feel). After must and should, never add to. To say something is not necessary, use do not have to or do not need to, not "must not".

Common Mistake

After Must and Should, Use the Base Verb

"Managers must to own the message. The flag is optional, so you must not raise it."
"Managers must own the message. The flag is optional, so you do not have to raise it."

After must and should, use the base verb: never "must to". And "must not" means it is forbidden, while "do not have to" means it is simply not necessary.
