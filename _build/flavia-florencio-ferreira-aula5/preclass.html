Lesson 05 -- Pre-class

A Day at Work

Describe your typical work day. Key words: morning, afternoon, evening, clock, break, lunch, appointment, to arrive, to start, to finish. Structure: telling the time and prepositions of time (at / on / in).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Morning -- the early part of the day, before noon
"I read my emails in the morning."
Afternoon -- the part of the day between noon and evening
"I take a short break in the afternoon."
Evening -- the part of the day after work, before night
"I go home in the evening."
Clock -- a thing on the wall that shows the time
"I always look at the clock before a meeting."
Break -- a short rest during the work day
"We have a coffee break at half past ten."
Lunch -- the meal you eat in the middle of the day
"I have lunch at half past twelve."
Appointment -- a fixed time to meet someone or do something
"I have an appointment with a partner at three o'clock."
To arrive -- to get to a place
"I arrive at the office at eight o'clock."
To start -- to begin something
"We start the meeting at nine o'clock."
To finish -- to end or complete something
"I finish work at six o'clock in the evening."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia has a busy day at work. She arrives at the office at eight o'clock in the morning. She starts with emails, and at half past nine she has a meeting. At half past twelve, she stops for lunch. In the afternoon, she takes a short break and answers calls. She has an appointment with a new partner at three o'clock. She finishes at six o'clock in the evening. On Friday, she finishes early, at four o'clock. She always looks at the clock, because every appointment has a time.

1. Why do we say "at eight o'clock"?
A "at" is for clock times.
B "at" is for days of the week.
C "at" is for months.
2. Why do we say "in the morning"?
A "in" is for clock times.
B "in" is for parts of the day.
C "in" is for exact minutes.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I start work in Monday."
B "I start work on Monday."
C "I start work at Monday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- At / On / In & the Clock

GRAMMAR

How to say when things happen, and how to tell the time.

UseWhenExample
atclock times, noon, nightat nine o'clock · at noon · at night
ondays and dateson Monday · on March 15th
inparts of the day, monthsin the morning · in the afternoon · in April
Telling the timeo'clock (full hour): nine o'clock · a quarter past (:15) · half past (:30) · a quarter to (:45) the next hour.
