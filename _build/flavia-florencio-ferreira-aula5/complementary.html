Grammar Video
"Prepositions of Time: at, on, in" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on when to use at, on, and in for time. Connection to Lesson 5: this is exactly the choice you make in every sentence about your day -- "at nine o'clock", "on Monday", and "in the morning".

Tip: after each example, say one true sentence about your own day with at, on, or in.

"Telling Time in English -- O'clock, Quarter Past, Half Past, Quarter To" -- Study Smart with Lubna

A calm, step-by-step lesson on how to say the time in English. Connection to Lesson 5: it trains the exact phrases you use to schedule a call -- half past twelve, a quarter to ten, and six o'clock.

Tip: look at a clock three times today and say the time out loud in English.
