Chapter 3: The Code

What You Did

started · studied · went · led

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I started my career at nineteen. I worked in public service."

"I studied a lot. I finished two degrees."

"I went to Seoul last year. I led a big team."

"I was young, but I had a big dream."

TypePresentPastNegative
Regular (+ ed)work / studyworked / studieddid not work
Irregular (new form)go / lead / be / havewent / led / was / haddid not go

Regular verbs add -ed. Irregular verbs have a new form to learn. After did not, always use the base verb: "I did not go", never "I did not went".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I go to USP in 2005."
"I went to USP in 2005."
"I didn't studied at night."
"I didn't study at night."

For the past, change the verb: go becomes went. And after didn't, use the base verb (study), never the past form.

Chapter 4: Telling Your Story

The Interview

"Can you tell me about your background?"

Dialogue

Tell Me About Your Background

M
Hello, Maria Clara. I am Mark, a journalist. I traveled from London for this education forum. Can you tell me a little about your background?
MC
Of course, Mark. I started my career when I was nineteen years old, in public service.
M
Nineteen years old! That is very young. I started my career at thirty, so you began much earlier. What did you do after that?
MC
I studied a lot. I went to USP and UFSC, and I finished two degrees.
M
Two degrees. That is a great achievement. Was the doctorate difficult?
MC
It was a big challenge at first. But I used to study every night, so the final exam was a piece of cake.
M
Wonderful. And today, you lead a large team, is that right?
MC
Yes. I led a team of eight people, and now I represent my institution around the world.
M
What a story. I want to write an article about you for my magazine in London. What was the best moment in your career?
MC
The day my granddaughter said my name in English. That was my favorite achievement.

Key Expressions

Two Ways to Tell the Past

One for old habits, one for things that were easy.

I used to work in public administration.

"I used to..." = a habit in the past that is now finished.

The presentation was a piece of cake.

"a piece of cake" = something very easy.
