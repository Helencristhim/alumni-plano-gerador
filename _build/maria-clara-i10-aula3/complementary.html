Video — grammar
Past Simple Tense: Regular and Irregular Verbs (Woodward English)

This is the exact grammar of lesson 3: the two families of past verbs. Regular verbs add -ed (worked, studied, started), and irregular verbs have a new form (went, led, was, had). Clear and slow, with many examples on screen.

Tip: after the video, say five sentences about your own past out loud — three with regular verbs (I worked, I studied, I started) and two with irregular verbs (I went, I led). Don't write them first.

Video — speaking
How to Talk About the Past: Used To, Would, and Past Simple (Speak Confident English)

This is exactly the mission of lesson 3: telling your story. The teacher shows how to talk about old habits with used to and finished actions with the past simple — the language you will use when a colleague asks about your background.

Tip: pause after each example and say a similar sentence about your own life: "I used to work in administration", "I studied at two universities". Speak out loud, don't just listen.

Podcast — listening
A Past Simple Story with Irregular Verbs — Easy Listening Podcast (Elephant English)

Slow, clear listening practice: a simple story told entirely in the past — perfect for your two hours of daily immersion. Understanding a story by ear is the hardest skill, and the only way to train it is to use it, a little every day.

Tip: listen ONCE without stopping — don't worry about the words you miss. Then write down three irregular past verbs that you hear (went, had, was...). Bring them to the next class.
