Lesson 03 -- Pre-class

My Story -- Past Simple for Professional and Personal Narrative

Tell the story of your career: when you started, where you studied, what you led. Key words: degree, public service, leadership, administration, career, experience, achievement, challenge. Structure: the past simple (regular verbs + ed, and irregular verbs like went, led, was, had). And the phrase for an old habit: "I used to...".

Vocabulary

"I started my career in public service."
Leadership -- the skill of guiding a team of people
"Leadership was always part of my job."
Administration -- the work of managing an organization
"I used to work in public administration."
Career -- all the jobs and work in your professional life
"My career started at nineteen."
Experience -- the knowledge you get from doing something for years
"I have many years of experience."
Achievement -- a good thing you worked hard to do
"Two degrees are a great achievement."
Challenge -- a difficult thing that tests you
"The doctorate was a big challenge."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text slowly, twice, and then answer the questions.

My name is Maria Clara, and this is my story. I started my career when I was nineteen years old, in public service. I worked in administration for many years. I also studied a lot: I went to USP and UFSC, and I finished two degrees. It was a big challenge, but I used to study every night, so the exams were a piece of cake. I led a team of eight people, and leadership became a normal part of my day. Two doctorate degrees are my greatest achievement — after my family.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple (I worked / I went)

GRAMMAR

Use the past simple for finished actions in the past. There are two families of verbs: regular verbs add -ed (work → worked), and irregular verbs have a new form to learn by heart (go → went).

TypePresentPast (+)Negative (-)
Regular (+ ed)work / study / startworked / studied / startedI did not work
Irregular (new form)go / lead / be / havewent / led / was / hadI did not go

Watch out: after did not (didn't), always use the base verb: "I didn't study", never "I didn't studied". For a past habit that is now finished, use used to + base verb: "I used to work in administration". Time words: in 2005, at nineteen, for many years, last year, ago.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your rehearsal for the Seoul forum.

Tell your professional story in 4-5 sentences, all in the past: when you started your career, where you studied, what you finished, and what you led. Use: I started..., I studied..., I finished..., I led..., and one sentence with "I used to...". Take your time and don't read from a script.
