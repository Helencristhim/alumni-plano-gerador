Grammar Rule

Asking + giving directions

FormExample
Ask whereWhere is the museum?
Ask the wayHow do I get to the station?
Give a direction (verb only)Go straight. / Turn left.
Tell where to leaveGet off at the next stop.
Say which sideIt is on your left / across from the cafe.

Key idea: ask with Where is or How do I get to. To give a direction, use just the verb (the imperative): Go straight, Turn left, Turn right, Get off. No "I" or "you" before the verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You go straight and you turn left at the corner."
"Go straight and turn left at the corner."

To give a direction, start with the verb. Do not add you.

Grammar Practice
