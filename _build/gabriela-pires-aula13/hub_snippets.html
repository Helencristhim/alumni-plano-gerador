Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. / Listen to each word and read the example.

Subway -- an underground train in a big city
"I take the subway to the city center." (Metrô)
Bus stop -- the place where you wait to get on a bus
"The bus stop is across from the cafe." (Ponto de ônibus)
Station -- a place where trains or the subway stop to take people
"Get off at the next station." (Estação)
Map -- a picture that shows the streets and places of a city
"Look at the map to find the museum." (Mapa)
Ticket -- a small paper you buy to take a bus, train or subway
"I bought a ticket for the bus." (Bilhete / passagem)
Get off -- to leave a bus, train or subway at a stop
"Get off at the third stop." (Descer / saltar)
Corner -- the place where two streets meet
"Turn right at the corner." (Esquina)
Go straight -- to keep going forward, without turning
"Go straight for two minutes." (Siga em frente / reto)
Across from -- on the other side of the street, facing a place
"The cafe is across from the station." (Em frente a / do outro lado)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Asking the way & giving directions

GRAMMAR

Usamos "Where is" e "How do I get to" para perguntar o caminho, e o imperativo para dar direções. / We use "Where is" and "How do I get to" to ask the way, and the imperative to give directions.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Ask whereWhere is + placeWhere is the museum?
Ask the wayHow do I get to + placeHow do I get to the station?
Give a directionverb only (imperative)Go straight. Turn left.
Tell where to leaveGet off + at + placeGet off at the next stop.
Say which sideon your left / across fromIt is across from the cafe.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use Where is ou How do I get to para perguntar o caminho. Para dar uma direção, use só o verbo (o imperativo): Go straight, Turn left, Turn right, Get off. Não use "I" nem "you" antes do verbo.
