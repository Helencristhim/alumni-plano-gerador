Lesson 11 -- Weather Talk

Series
Extra English -- Episode 8: "A Rainy Day at Home"

A simple comedy made for English learners. In this episode the characters talk about the weather -- the sun, the rain, the clouds, and a big storm -- in slow, clear English. Perfect for A1 listening about what the weather is like.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every weather word you hear (sun, rain, cloud, wind, storm). Then say out loud: "There is sun today" and "There are many clouds".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Talking About the Weather"

A short, friendly lesson on the weather: how to say "There is a lot of sun", "There are clouds in the sky", and how to ask "Is there rain today?". The hosts explain There is and There are slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then describe the weather today in three sentences using There is and There are.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "There is / There are + Weather"

A clear video that teaches weather vocabulary (sun, rain, cloud, wind, storm) and how to use There is and There are to talk about the weather, with pictures and simple sentences.

After watching, record a 60-second weather forecast: say what the weather is like today and tomorrow. Use at least three new words with There is and There are.
