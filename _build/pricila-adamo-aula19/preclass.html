"I couldn't keep up with my running routine, so I stopped."
A creature of habit -- pessoa de habitos / rotineira
"I'm a creature of habit -- coffee at seven, walk at eight."
To make time for -- arrumar tempo para

An acquired taste -- um gosto que se adquire com o tempo
"Olives were an acquired taste, but now I love them."
To broaden your horizons -- ampliar seus horizontes
"Trying new things broadens your horizons, even at sixty."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

For years, Pricila was a creature of habit. She would finish work and just watch television. "I enjoy relaxing," she told herself, "but I never make time for a real hobby." One day she decided to take up painting. At first she couldn't stand making such a mess, and she almost gave up. But she kept practising, and slowly she got the hang of it. Now she's hooked. "Painting helps me unwind," she says, "and I'm planning to learn the piano next. Trying new things really broadens your horizons."

1. After "I enjoy", which form is correct?
A "I enjoy relaxing." (-ing)
B "I enjoy to relax." (to + verb)
C "I enjoy relax." (base verb)
2. After "she decided", which form is correct?
A "She decided taking up painting."
B "She decided to take up painting."
C "She decided take up painting."
3. What does the story say happened after she kept practising?
A She gave up for good.
B She never improved.
C She slowly got the hang of it and is now hooked.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerunds vs Infinitives

Grammar

Alguns verbos pedem o -ing (gerundio); outros pedem to + verbo (infinitivo). Verbo + -ing: enjoy, avoid, keep, give up, can't stand ("I enjoy painting"). Verbo + to + verbo: decide, manage, hope, plan, afford ("I decided to take up the piano"). Depois de PREPOSICAO use sempre -ing ("good at planning", "interested in learning"). Como SUJEITO, use -ing ("Travelling broadens the mind"). Alguns mudam de sentido: stop doing = parar a atividade; stop to do = parar PARA fazer; remember doing = lembrar do passado; remember to do = nao esquecer no futuro. / Some verbs take -ing, others take to + verb. After a preposition, always use -ing. As a subject, use -ing. A few verbs change meaning (stop doing vs stop to do; remember doing vs remember to do).
