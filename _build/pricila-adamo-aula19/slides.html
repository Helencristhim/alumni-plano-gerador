Chapter 3: Gerunds vs Infinitives

-ing or to + verb?

enjoy doing · decide to do · stop doing / stop to do

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I enjoy painting in the mornings." (enjoy + -ing)

"I decided to take up the piano." (decide + to + verb)

"I'm good at planning my week." (preposition + -ing)

"Travelling broadens the mind." (gerund as subject)

PatternFormExample
enjoy / avoid / keep / give up / can't standverb + -ingI enjoy painting.
decide / manage / hope / plan / afford / wantverb + to + baseI decided to take up the piano.
after a preposition (good at, interested in)preposition + -ingI'm good at planning.
as the subject of a sentence-ing formTravelling broadens the mind.

Some verbs change meaning: stop doing = quit the activity, but stop to do = pause in order to do it. Remember doing = recall the past; remember to do = don't forget the future.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I enjoy to paint."

✓ RIGHT

"I enjoy painting."

✗ WRONG

"I'm interested in learn the piano."

✓ RIGHT

"I'm interested in learning the piano."

After enjoy use the -ing form, never "to". And after a preposition (in, at, of, about) always use -ing, never the base verb.

Chapter 4: A Conversation About Hobbies

What Are You Into?

Pricila and a friend talk about hobbies and balance

Dialogue

Taking Something Up

P
I've decided to take up painting. I gave up scrolling on my phone every night instead.
D
Good for you! Are you any good? It usually takes a while to get the hang of it.
P
Terrible at first! But I'm into it now -- it's the one thing that helps me unwind.
D
I should make time for a hobby too. I keep working in the evenings and never switch off.
P
Just dabble in something. It doesn't matter if you're good -- it broadens your horizons.
D
You're right. I'll plan to start cooking -- it's an acquired taste, but it sounds relaxing.
Comprehension

About the Conversation

1. Why does Daniel say he never switches off?
He keeps working in the evenings and doesn't make time for a hobby.
2. What does Daniel decide to take up by the end?
Cooking -- he says he'll plan to start, even though it's an acquired taste.
3. What reason does Daniel give for being worth it?
It sounds relaxing, and (as Pricila says) it broadens your horizons.

The Pattern Map

Three Groups

VERB + -ing -- enjoy, avoid, keep, give up, can't stand

"I enjoy painting." · "I can't stand wasting weekends." · "I gave up scrolling."

VERB + to + base -- decide, manage, hope, plan, afford

"I decided to take up the piano." · "I can't afford to waste time."

AFTER A PREPOSITION -- always -ing

"I'm good at planning." · "I'm interested in learning the piano."

And watch the meaning-change pairs: stop doing vs stop to do, remember doing vs remember to do.
