Chapter 3 -- The Code

How Much and How Many

Quantities

Count or No Count?

Look at the orange words. When do we use how many, and when do we use how much?

How many apples do you want?

How many eggs do you need?

How much milk do you want?

How much is the cheese?

Grammar Rule

How Much vs How Many

UseStructureExample
Count (1, 2, 3)How many + pluralHow many apples?
No countHow much + nounHow much milk?
PriceHow much is it?How much is the cheese?
Wronghow much + countHow much apples?

Key idea: use how many for things you can count (apples, eggs) and how much for things you cannot count (milk, cheese, money). For prices: "How much is it?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"How much apples do you want?"
"How many apples do you want?"

Use how many for things you can count, not how much.

Grammar Practice

How Much or How Many?

How _____ apples? (count)  =  How many apples?
How _____ milk? (no count)  =  How much milk?
How _____ eggs? (count)  =  How many eggs?
How _____ money? (no count)  =  How much money?
