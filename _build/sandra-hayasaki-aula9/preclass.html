Aula 09 -- Pre-class

At the Supermarket -- how much and how many

Compras no supermercado. Use how many para coisas que você conta (apples, eggs) e how much para coisas que você não conta (milk, cheese, money). Vocabulário novo: supermarket, milk, eggs, apple, cheese, money, cheap, expensive.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Hi! I am at the supermarket. I have my money and my bag. I ask the clerk: "How many apples do you have?" I count things like apples and eggs, so I say how many. For things I cannot count, like milk and cheese, I say "How much milk?" To ask the price, I say "How much is it?" The apples are cheap, but the cheese is expensive.

1. Which question asks about apples?
A "How much apples?"
B "How many apples?"
C "How apples?"
2. How do you ask the price?
A "How much is it?"
B "How many is it?"
C "How is it many?"
3. Which word means "low price"?
A Expensive.
B Cheap.
C Money.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- how much / how many

GRAMMAR

Use HOW MANY para coisas que você conta e HOW MUCH para coisas que não conta. / Use HOW MANY for countable things and HOW MUCH for uncountable things.

Use / UsoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Conto (1, 2, 3)How many + pluralHow many apples?
Não contoHow much + nounHow much milk?
PreçoHow much is it?How much is the cheese?
Wrong / erradohow much + countHow much apples?
Key point / Ponto-chaveConta? Use how many (apples, eggs). Não conta? Use how much (milk, cheese, money).
