Chapter 3: The -ed Code

Past Simple: Regular Verbs

One ending tells the whole story: -ed

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"We launched a new service in January."

"The board signed the contract."

"We did not finish the audit."

"Did you deliver the report?"

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeverb + -edWe launched the service.
Negativedid not + base verbWe did not finish the audit.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you deliver the report?

In the past, regular verbs add -ed. But after did and did not, the verb goes back to its base form.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Did you delivered the report?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you deliver the report?"

✗ WRONG

"We not finished the project."

✓ RIGHT

"We did not finish the project."

After did or did not, the main verb loses the -ed and returns to its base form.

Dialogue

The Investor's Questions

I
Good morning, Rafael. Tell me, how was last quarter for the company?
R
It was a strong quarter. In January, we launched a new service, and the board signed an important contract.
I
Excellent. Did you also grow the team?
R
Yes. We hired four new lawyers and opened a small office in Rio. The numbers improved every month.
I
And the reports? Did you deliver everything on time?
R
We finished every project and delivered all the reports. We achieved our main goal for the quarter.
