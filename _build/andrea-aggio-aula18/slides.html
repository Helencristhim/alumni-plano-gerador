Coaching and Developing Your Team

Stop doing everything yourself. Delegate, empower, and get the work done through the people you are growing.

Warm-Up

From the Feedback to the Growth

Last time you gave feedback that was candid, kind, and clear -- praising one strength and pinpointing one thing to change. But feedback is only the start. The real job of a leader is not to do everything herself; it is to develop the people who will. Today you step back from the doing and into the coaching. You will delegate a real responsibility, empower a colleague to own it, and get the work done through someone you are growing -- even if that means letting them make a small mistake along the way. By the end of this hour, you will coach and develop your team with confidence.

In one sentence: what is one task you still do yourself that someone on your team could grow by owning?

Chapter 4: Have & Get Done

Get It Done Through Others

have + person + verb · get + person + to + verb · have / get + object + past participle

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This quarter, I'm going to have Oskar lead the negotiations."

"I got him to take ownership of the whole account."

"I had the rate sheets reviewed before the meeting."

In none of these does the speaker do the action herself -- she causes someone else to do it. Two sentences are about a PERSON doing something; one is about a THING being done. How does the verb form change between them?

FormUseExample
have + person + base verbdelegate: arrange for someone to do somethingI had her present the results.
get + person + to + verbpersuade or arrange for someone to do itI got him to take ownership.
have / get + object + past participlearrange for something to be done (by someone else)I had the report reviewed. / I got the slides redesigned.

Causatives let you talk about getting things done through other people. For a PERSON doing something, use 'have + person + base verb' or 'get + person + to + verb'. For a THING that is done for you, use 'have / get + object + past participle'. Notice: 'have' takes no 'to' before the person's verb, but 'get' does. This is the grammar of delegation.

Language Focus

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I had her to present the results."

✓ RIGHT

I had her present the results.

✗ WRONG

"I had the report review before the meeting."

✓ RIGHT

I had the report reviewed before the meeting.

With 'have + person', use the base verb and NO 'to': 'I had her present', not 'I had her to present'. When the object is a THING that is done for you, use the past participle: 'I had the report reviewed', not 'I had the report review'. (Only 'get + person' takes 'to': 'I got her to present'.)

Register & Tone

Sound Professional

Instead of: "Just do it the way I told you."
Say: I'm going to have you run this your way -- I'll stay hands-off and mentor if you need me.
Instead of: "You're not ready to lead this."
Say: It's a steep learning curve, but you have the potential; I'll get you the support to step up.
Instead of: "Send me everything so I can check it all."
Say: Take ownership of it -- I'd rather trust you than micromanage every detail.
Instead of: "I'll just fix the slides myself."
Say: I'll have the slides redesigned by marketing, so you can focus on the negotiation.

Andrea Coaches Oskar to Step Up

I did. This time, I'm not going to lead them. I'm going to have you run the whole process.
O
Me? I've sat in on them, but I've never led one myself. That's a steep learning curve.
A
That's exactly why. You have the potential; you just need the ownership. I'll mentor you, but I'll stay hands-off.

The Development Plan

Team Development Plan

For: Oskar -- Account Executive
Coach: Andrea Aggio -- Commercial Director
Goal: Grow into lead on supplier negotiations this quarter

The stretch: this quarter, I will have Oskar lead the full supplier negotiation, end to end. He has sat in before but never led, so this is real ownership, not a rehearsal.

My role: I'll mentor from beside, not above -- available for the hard questions, hands-off on the day-to-day. I've had the last rate sheets forwarded to him, and I'll get the finance team to review his final numbers before he signs anything.

Accountability: Oskar owns the outcome and answers for it, good or bad. We expect a learning curve, and a small mistake is fine. We debrief after each round and adjust. The aim is not a perfect quarter; it is a leader who can do this without me next time.

Listening 2

Have and Get Something Done

A short tip on the causative structures. Sound first -- no text.

0:000:00
1. After 'have + a person', which verb form do you use?
The base verb, with no 'to' -- 'I had her present'.
2. What does 'get + person' need that 'have + person' does not?
The word 'to' -- 'I got her to present'.
3. Which form do you use for a thing that is done for you?
Have or get + object + past participle -- 'I had the report reviewed'.
Pronunciation

Say It Naturally

Listen for the linking, then repeat twice

"I'm going to have you run it."

'have you' links -- ha-vyoo; 'going to' reduces to gonna in speech; stress RUN.

"I got him to take ownership."

'got him to' links -- go-tim-tuh; stress OWNERSHIP.

"I had the report reviewed."

'had the' links -- had-thuh; stress reVIEWED, the past participle.

"I'll have the sheets forwarded."

'have the' links -- hav-thuh; light stress on FORwarded.
