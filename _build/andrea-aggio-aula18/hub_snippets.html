Coaching and Developing Your Team

How to delegate, empower, and develop your team using causative have and get. Key words: to delegate, to empower, to mentor, ownership, potential, to nurture, hands-off, to step up, accountability, to micromanage, autonomy, learning curve. Structure: have + person + base verb; get + person + to + verb; have / get + object + past participle.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To delegate -- to give a task or responsibility to someone else (delegar)
"A good leader learns to delegate, not to do it all."
To empower -- to give someone the authority and confidence to act (empoderar, dar autonomia)
"I want to empower my team to make their own calls."
To mentor -- to guide and advise a less experienced colleague (orientar, ser mentor de)
"She mentored me through my first big negotiation."
Ownership -- the feeling of being responsible for a task or result (senso de dono, responsabilidade)
"Give people ownership and they rise to it."
Potential -- ability that has not yet been fully developed (potencial)
"He has the potential to lead the whole account."
To nurture -- to care for someone and help them grow (cultivar, desenvolver)
"You nurture talent by trusting it with real work."
Hands-off -- giving people freedom to work without close control (nao intervencionista)
"My style is hands-off once I trust the person."
To step up -- to take on more responsibility when it is needed (assumir o desafio, dar um passo a frente)
"She stepped up the moment I gave her the chance."
Accountability -- being responsible for results and answerable for them (responsabilizacao, prestacao de contas)
"Ownership means accountability, not just credit."
To micromanage -- to control every small detail of someone's work (microgerenciar)
"If you micromanage, people never learn."
Autonomy -- the freedom to make your own decisions (autonomia)
"Autonomy is how people grow into leaders."
Learning curve -- how quickly someone must learn something new (curva de aprendizado)
"It's a steep learning curve, but I'll support him."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea decides to delegate the supplier negotiations to Oskar. Instead of leading them herself, she will have him run the whole process, to give him real ownership. 'You have the potential,' she tells him, 'I just need to empower you to use it.' She promises to mentor him but stay hands-off: 'I'd rather get you to make one small mistake than micromanage you.' To help him prepare, she had the rate sheets forwarded that morning. Oskar knows it's a steep learning curve, but with autonomy and support, he's ready to step up.

1. In 'I'll have you run the process', the verb after 'have you' is:
A the base verb (run), with no 'to'.
B the past participle (run/ran).
C 'to run'.
2. Which sentence uses the causative for a THING done for you?
A I had the rate sheets forwarded.
B I had him lead the call.
C I got her to sign it.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A I got him to take ownership.
B I got him take ownership.
C I had him to take ownership.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Causative: Have & Get

GRAMMAR

Como falar de mandar/fazer alguem fazer algo, e providenciar que algo seja feito (em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
have + person + base verbdelegar a alguem (sem 'to'). delegate to a person (no 'to').I had her present it.
get + person + to + verbconvencer/pedir a alguem (com 'to'). persuade a person (with 'to').I got him to lead it.
have / get + object + participioprovidenciar que algo seja feito. arrange for a thing to be done.I had the report reviewed.
Regra de ouro / Golden rule'have' nao usa 'to' antes da pessoa; 'get' usa. have = no 'to'; get = 'to'.have her go / get her to go.
