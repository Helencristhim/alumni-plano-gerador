Aula 16 -- The Recovery Plan -- Delegating the Fixes

Film
Apollo 13 (1995)

A launch goes wrong, and Mission Control runs the ultimate recovery plan. It is Lesson 16 in a single film: a leader who does not fix everything himself, but delegates. He has the crew shut down systems, gets the engineers to build a filter from spare parts, and holds one owner accountable for each fix. Watch how the turnaround happens by getting the right work done by the right people, on a deadline.

Tip: pick one scene where a problem is delegated. Write three causative sentences about it -- "They had the filter built", "They got the crew to power down", "They had the engineers test it".

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- TED

An organizational psychologist takes you inside how great teams work. Several episodes are, in effect, a recovery plan out loud: how leaders delegate, hold people accountable, get an outside view when the team is too close to the problem, and turn a setback into a turnaround. It is the exact register of Lesson 16 -- deciding who does what, and by when.

Tip: choose one episode on teams or accountability. Note two things the leader delegated, then record a 90-second recovery plan for a real AVRT problem using at least two causative structures.

YouTube
"How to use 'have something done'" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on exactly the code from Lesson 16: the causative "have something done" and "get something done", used when you arrange for someone else to do the work. It shows the word order you practised -- have + object + past participle -- and how it differs from doing the job yourself.

Tip: pause after each example and say your own version about AVRT out loud. Then write four causative sentences about one real recovery -- at least one "have something done", one "get someone to do it", and one "have someone do it".
