The Recovery Plan

Reading a recovery plan and delegating the fixes after a failed launch.

Warm-Up

From the Post-Mortem to the Recovery

Last class you said what you would have done differently. The review is over now, and there are ten fixes to make. A leader does not do all ten alone. She delegates each one, names an owner, and gets the work done by the right people.

When ten problems land on your desk, how do you say who fixes what?

Today

Three Missions

1

Read a recovery plan and see how each fix is delegated to an owner.

2

Master the causative: have / get something done, and get someone to do it.

3

Present your recovery plan out loud: who does what, and by when.

Chapter 4: The Code

The Causative

have / get something done · have someone do · get someone to do

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We are having the firmware rewritten by a firm."

"We got the process audited by a third party."

"We had the vendor flag every delay."

"We got an analyst to rebuild the model."

In each sentence, who does the work — you, or someone else?

PatternFormExample
Result (thing)have / get + object + past participleWe had the firmware rewritten.
Doer (have)have + person + base verbWe had the vendor flag delays.
Doer (get)get + person + to + base verbWe got an analyst to rebuild it.

Keep the order have + OBJECT + past participle. "We had the firmware rewritten" means someone else wrote it. "We had rewritten the firmware" (present perfect) means you did it.

Language Focus

Which Pattern?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We had rewritten the firmware by a firm."

✓ RIGHT

"We had the firmware rewritten by a firm."

✗ WRONG

"We got the vendor rebuild the module."

✓ RIGHT

"We got the vendor to rebuild the module."

✗ WRONG

"We had the vendor to flag every delay."

✓ RIGHT

"We had the vendor flag every delay."

For a result, keep the order have + object + past participle. With "get someone", use "to" + base verb. With "have someone", use the base verb with no "to".

Dialogue

Testing the Plan

A
Izabel, I read the recovery plan. It looks strong, but I want to test it. Are you and your team fixing all of this yourselves?
I
No. That is the point. We are having each problem handled by a specialist. We are having the firmware rewritten by an external security firm, not in-house.
A
Good. And the forecast that caused the excess stock?
I
We are getting an independent analyst to rebuild the model. I do not want the same people who built the bad forecast to grade their own work.
A
What about the process itself? A launch like this should never slip through again.
I
I have had the whole release checklist overhauled. Nobody signs off on a shipment now until quality has certified it, and any missed date is escalated to me the same day.
A
And when the team cannot fix something?
I
Then we bring in a third party rather than let it drift. Every item has one accountable owner who must get the fix done. That is how a setback becomes a turnaround.
