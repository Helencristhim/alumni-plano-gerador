Lesson 16 -- Pre-class

The Recovery Plan -- Delegating the Fixes

Reading the Halo recovery plan and seeing how each fix is delegated to an owner. Key words: turnaround, overhaul, rectify, escalate, accountable, remediation, sign off on, third party, rework, handover, take ownership, get up to speed. Structure: the causative -- have / get something done, have someone do, and get someone to do it.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Turnaround -- a recovery that reverses a failing situation
"This plan turns a setback into a turnaround."
Overhaul -- a thorough repair or redesign of something
"We had the release process given a full overhaul."
Rectify -- to correct a mistake or a fault
"Where we cannot rectify a gap in-house, we bring in help."
Escalate -- to pass a problem to a higher level for action
"Any missed date is escalated to me the same day."
Accountable -- required to explain results and answer for them
"Each item has one accountable owner."
Remediation -- the work of fixing a defect or problem
"Remediation is not open-ended: every task has a date."
Sign off on -- to give something final, formal approval
"Nobody signs off on a shipment until quality certifies it."
Third party -- an outside company or person, not you or the client
"We bring in a third party rather than let it drift."
Rework -- to do a piece of work again to correct it
"We had the contract reworked so delays are flagged."
Handover -- the passing of a task or responsibility to someone else
"Each workstream has a clear handover."
Take ownership -- to accept full responsibility for something
"In the past, nobody took ownership of a fault."
Get up to speed -- to reach the level of knowledge a task needs
"We are getting the whole team up to speed on the process."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Izabel opens the recovery plan. "We will not fix everything ourselves," she writes. "We are having the firmware rewritten by an external security firm, and we will get the whole system retested before any customer sees it." On the forecast, she is clear: "We are getting an independent analyst to rebuild the model." She has already had the release checklist overhauled. The rule is simple: nobody signs off on a shipment until quality has certified it, and every item has one accountable owner who must get the fix done by Thursday.

1. "We are having the firmware rewritten by an external firm." Who writes the code?
A Izabel and her in-house team.
B The external security firm (Izabel arranges it, someone else does it).
C Nobody — it is already finished.
2. "We are getting an analyst to rebuild the model." Which word is required after "analyst"?
A "to" (get + person + to + base verb).
B nothing (get + person + base verb).
C "that".
3. "She has had the release checklist overhauled." What is the correct word order?
A have + object + past participle (had the checklist overhauled).
B have + past participle + object (had overhauled the checklist).
C Both are the same.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Causative

GRAMMAR

Delegating the work: you arrange it, someone else does it.

PatternFormExample
Result (the thing)have / get + object + past participleWe had the firmware rewritten.
Doer with "have"have + person + base verbWe had the vendor flag delays.
Doer with "get"get + person + to + base verbWe got an analyst to rebuild it.
Watch outKeep the order have + object + past participle. "We had the firmware rewritten" (someone else did it) is not "We had rewritten the firmware" (present perfect, you did it). Use "to" after get + person, but no "to" after have + person.
