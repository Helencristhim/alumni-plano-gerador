Aula 05 -- Pre-class

The Art of Small Talk -- Breaking Ice Without Breaking Character

Starting and steering a real conversation. Key words: break the ice, keep the conversation going, read the room, common ground, awkward silence, go-to topic, chat up, layover, jet lag, first-timer, hidden gem, word of mouth. Structures: direct questions, indirect questions (Do you know if...? / I was wondering whether...) and follow-up questions.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To break the ice -- to start a conversation and make people relax (quebrar o gelo)
"A simple question about the flight is a great way to break the ice."
To keep the conversation going -- to make a conversation continue naturally (manter a conversa fluindo)
"Asking about their trip is an easy way to keep the conversation going."
To read the room -- to sense the mood and adjust how you speak (ler o ambiente)
"He read the room, saw she was tired, and kept it short."
Common ground -- a shared interest or experience two people find (interesse em comum)
"We found common ground the moment we started talking about cycling."
Awkward silence -- an uncomfortable pause when no one speaks (silêncio constrangedor)
"There was an awkward silence, so I asked where she was heading."
Go-to topic -- the subject you always turn to when you need one (assunto coringa)
"Travel is my go-to topic; everyone has a story about a trip."
To chat up -- to start a friendly, casual conversation with someone (puxar conversa)
"He chatted up the person next to him while they waited to board."
Layover -- a stop between two flights on a journey (conexão, escala)
"I have a four-hour layover in Reykjavik before my next flight."
Jet lag -- the tired feeling after a long flight across time zones (cansaço do fuso horário)
"The jet lag hit me hard after the flight from Brazil."
First-timer -- someone doing or visiting something for the first time (novato, quem vai pela primeira vez)
"I am a first-timer in Iceland, so I want every local tip I can get."
Hidden gem -- a wonderful place that few tourists know about (joia escondida)
"That little cafe upstairs is a real hidden gem."
Word of mouth -- information passed from person to person in conversation (boca a boca)
"The best places I found on that trip were all word of mouth."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

During a long layover in Reykjavik, a woman sat down next to Marcelo. To break the ice, she asked a direct question: "Are you heading home, or just passing through?" They found common ground quickly. When she needed a tip, she softened it into an indirect question: "I was wondering whether you know if there is anywhere good to eat." Notice the word order: after the opener, we say "the flight is delayed," not "is the flight delayed." To keep the conversation going, Marcelo used a follow-up: "What made you decide to travel through Oslo?" A good follow-up picks up the last thing the person said and goes one step deeper.

1. Por que "Are you heading home?" é uma pergunta DIRETA?
A Porque o verbo auxiliar vem antes do sujeito (are you).
B Porque começa com um abridor educado.
C Porque não tem ponto de interrogação.
2. Na pergunta indireta, dizemos:
A "Do you know where is the cafe?" (com inversão)
B "Do you know where the cafe is?" (ordem normal)
C "Do you know where the cafe be?"
3. Which is a good follow-up question?
A "Where is the bathroom?"
B "What made you decide to travel alone?"
C "Is this seat taken?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Asking Questions (Direct, Indirect, Follow-up)

GRAMMAR

Como fazer perguntas naturais e educadas (explicação em inglês e português).

TypeUse / UsoExample
Direct
aux + subject		Pergunta simples e direta. A simple, direct question; the auxiliary comes before the subject.Where are you from?
Indirect
opener + subject + verb		Mais suave e educada. Softer and more polite; after the opener the order is normal (no inversion).Do you know if the flight is delayed?
Follow-upMantém a conversa viva, cavando mais fundo. Keeps the conversation going by going deeper.What made you decide to come?

Atenção: na pergunta indireta, NÃO inverta depois do abridor. Diga "where the gate is", nunca "where is the gate".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real.

You sit next to a stranger at the gate. How do you break the ice?
A "Who are you and where do you go?"
B "Long layover? Are you heading home, or just passing through?"
C "Tell me your whole life story."
You want to ask a stranger for a tip, politely. Which is best?
A "I was wondering whether you know a good place nearby."
B "Where is a good place, tell me now."
C "Do you know where is a good place?"
They say: "I moved to Oslo last year." Which is a good follow-up?
A "Okay." (and say nothing)
B "What made you decide to move there?"
C "Do you like coffee?"
There is an awkward silence. What is the smart move?
A Panic and reach for your phone.
B Read the room and ask a light follow-up question.
C Stand up and walk away.
