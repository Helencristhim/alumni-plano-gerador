Aula 5 -- The Art of Small Talk

Docuseries
Somebody Feed Phil (Netflix) -- any episode

Phil Rosenthal travels the world and does exactly what this lesson teaches: he breaks the ice with total strangers, finds common ground over food, and keeps every conversation going with warm follow-up questions. It is a masterclass in small talk on the road -- your November trip, on screen. Connection to Lesson 5: notice how he opens with strangers and how he never lets an awkward silence sit.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down three questions Phil asks strangers -- one direct, one indirect, one follow-up.

Podcast
Rough Translation -- NPR, any episode

Stories about how people from different cultures talk, connect, and misunderstand each other -- exactly the world you enter every time you strike up a conversation with a stranger abroad. Clear, well-paced American English, built around real conversations and the art of really listening.

Tip: pick one episode. Pause twice and say out loud one indirect question you would ask the person in the story (Do you know if...? / I was wondering whether...).

Celeste Headlee: 10 ways to have a better conversation | TED

A famous, twelve-minute TED talk that is the perfect companion to this lesson: how to ask real questions, how to read the room, and why listening beats talking. Natural, clear American English at a comfortable pace, on the exact skill you practiced tonight.

Tip: watch once, then choose two of her ten ideas and try them in your next real conversation this week.
