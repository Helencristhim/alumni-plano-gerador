Chapter 3: The Code

Three Kinds of Question

Direct · Indirect · Follow-up

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Where are you from?"

"Do you know if the flight is delayed?"

"I was wondering whether you have been here before."

"What made you decide to come here?"

Why does green invert the verb, but blue keep the normal order?

TypeFormExample
Direct(question word) + auxiliary + subject (inverted)Where are you from?
Indirectopener + if / whether / question word + subject + verb (normal order)Do you know if the flight is delayed?
Follow-upbuilds on the last answer to go deeperWhat made you decide to come?

Indirect questions sound softer and more polite. After the opener, the word order goes back to normal — no inversion.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Do you know where is the gate?"
"Do you know where the gate is?"
"I was wondering whether can you help me."
"I was wondering whether you could help me."

In an indirect question, do not invert after the opener. Keep the normal subject + verb order.

Practice

Fix the Question

Say the correct form first, then click to check

Chapter 4: The Encounter

At the Boarding Gate

A four-hour layover in Reykjavik

Dialogue

Breaking the Ice

N
You look like you could use a coffee. Long layover?
M
Four hours, and the jet lag is starting to hit. I'm Marcelo, by the way. Are you heading home, or just passing through?
N
Just passing through, on my way to Oslo. I was wondering whether you know if there is anywhere decent to eat around here.
M
Funny you should ask. Someone told me about a little place upstairs, a real hidden gem. Word of mouth is the only way you would ever find it.
N
Perfect. So what takes you to Iceland, business or pleasure?
M
Pleasure, mostly. I am a first-timer here, and I have always wanted to see the landscapes. What about you, what made you decide to travel through Oslo?
