Lesson 13 -- The Opening Act: Structuring a Presentation

Series
The Office (US) -- any pitch or presentation scene

Watch a character open a presentation to a group. Notice how they welcome the audience, give the topic, and move from part to part.

Write down the topic and the order of the parts. Re-tell the structure out loud using First, Then, Next, After that, and Finally.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Signposting Language for Presentations"

Practical language to open a talk and guide the audience through your structure. Review of today's words: presentation, audience, topic, outline, overview, signpost.

Note three signpost phrases the host gives. Record yourself opening a short talk and use all three.

YouTube
"How to Start a Presentation in English -- Openings and Signposting"

A short, clear guide to opening a presentation: welcome the audience, give the topic, share an overview of the outline, and signpost each part of the talk.

After watching, record a sixty-second opening for a real Oracle presentation. Use at least three new words and all five signpost words.
