Red Flag -- Raising a Risk Before It Explodes

Raise a risk in front of people who do not want to hear it: warning sign, likelihood, impact, mitigation, decision. New vocabulary: warning sign, likely, dependency, mitigate, contingency, worst case, downplay, flag.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Warning sign -- a small signal that something bad may come
"The first warning sign was the silence from their security team." (Sinal de alerta)
Likely -- probable: it will probably happen
"It is likely that the approval will take four weeks." (Provável)
Dependency -- something you need from someone else before you can move
"The approval is a dependency, and it is not in my control." (Dependência)
Mitigate -- to make a risk smaller, before it happens
"To mitigate it, we can start the tests on the old database." (Mitigar)
Contingency -- a plan B, ready before you need it
"The contingency is to go live with the old database." (Plano de contingência)
Worst case -- the worst thing that can realistically happen
"Worst case, the go-live moves to August." (Pior cenário)
Downplay -- to make a problem sound smaller than it is
"Do not downplay it just because nobody is worried." (Minimizar)
Flag -- to raise a problem, early and out loud
"I want to flag a risk before we plan the sprint." (Sinalizar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eleven days of silence from the client's security team. That is the warning sign, and Diogo will not downplay it. In the meeting he does not say "we have a problem" -- he says: "I want to flag one risk." Then he measures it: "It is likely that the approval takes four more weeks, because that is what it took last year." The approval is a dependency: he cannot control it, and the migration cannot start without it. Worst case, the go-live might move to August. Sophia says he is being dramatic. He does not argue about that. He goes to the plan: "To mitigate it, we start the tests on the old database this week." The contingency is to go live on the old database in June. And he ends with the only sentence that forces a room to move: "I need one decision from you by Friday."

1. Why does Diogo say "I want to flag one risk" instead of "we have a problem"?
A Because a risk has a likelihood, a plan and a decision -- a problem only invites excuses.
B Because he is not sure it is real.
C Because Sophia does not speak English well.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A The go-live might move to August.
B The go-live might to move to August.
C The go-live might will move to August.
3. What does he ask for at the end?
A One decision, with a date.
B More people on the team.
C An apology from the client.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Likely, Impact, Plan

GRAMMAR

Avisar sem virar o pessimista da sala: probabilidade, impacto, mitigação, decisão. / Warning people without becoming the pessimist in the room.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Nomear / Name itThere is a risk that + oraçãoThere is a risk that the approval takes four more weeks.
Medir / Measure itIt is likely that... / may, might, could + verboThe go-live might move to August.
Resolver / Give the way outTo mitigate it, we + verbo / Unless we..., we will...To mitigate it, we start the tests this week.
Ponto-chave / Key pointDepois de might / may / could vem o verbo puro: "might go down" -- nunca "might to go" nem "might will go". E use There is a risk that, jamais "it has a risk".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real risk on your project at Oracle, today. Speak for two minutes: (1) the warning sign, with a number, (2) how likely it is, and why, (3) the impact and the worst case, (4) the mitigation you can start this week, (5) the decision you need, with a date. Record it.
Now the room answers: "Nobody else is worried. Why are you?" Answer out loud. Do not defend your feelings -- repeat the numbers and ask for the decision again. Record it.
