Vocabulary

Audience -- the group of people who watch and listen to your talk
"Welcome the audience and say your name." (Plateia / público)
Topic -- the main subject you talk about
"Today my topic is the new feature launch." (Tema / assunto)
Slide -- one screen or page of your presentation deck
"This slide shows the timeline." (Slide / lâmina)
Deck -- the full set of slides you use for your talk
"My deck has eight slides for this presentation." (Conjunto de slides)
Overview -- a short summary that shows what is coming
"Let me give you a quick overview of the outline." (Visão geral / resumo)
Signpost -- a word that guides the audience to the next part
"First and Then are useful signpost words." (Palavra-guia)
Handout -- a paper or document you give the audience to keep
"There is a handout with the main numbers." (Material impresso / apostila)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Next week Diogo will give his first presentation to the leadership team. He opens by welcoming the audience, saying his name, and giving the topic: the new feature launch. Then he shares a quick overview of the outline, so people know what is coming. He uses signpost words to guide them. "First, I will present the problem. Then, I will show the solution. Next, I will give the timeline. After that, I will talk about the budget. Finally, I will end with the next steps." Each slide covers one part, and there is also a handout with the main numbers. The signposts keep his structure clear and easy to follow.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signpost Words

GRAMMAR

Estruturar uma apresentação com palavras-guia. / Structuring a presentation with signpost words.

SignpostUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
First,Abre a primeira parte / Opens the first partFirst, I will present the problem.
Then, / Next,Vai para a parte seguinte / Moves to the next partThen, I will show the solution.
After that,Continua após um passo / Continues after a stepAfter that, I will talk about the budget.
Finally,Fecha a última parte / Closes the last partFinally, I will end with the next steps.
Key point / Ponto-chaveColoque o signpost no início da frase, com vírgula: "First, I will...". Use First para abrir, Then / Next / After that para continuar, e Finally para fechar. Diga "After that", não "at after"; "Finally", não "at the final".
