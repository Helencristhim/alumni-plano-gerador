Chapter 3 -- Signposting

Grammar Discovery

First, Then, Next, After that, Finally

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words do?

"First, I will present the problem."

"Then, I will show the solution."

"Next, I will give the timeline."

"After that, I will talk about the budget."

"Finally, I will end with the next steps."

Grammar Rule

Signpost Words

SignpostJob
FirstOpens the first part of the talk
Then / NextMoves to the following part
After thatMoves to the next part after a step
FinallyCloses the last part of the talk

Key idea: Signpost words guide the audience through your talk. Put the signpost at the START of the sentence and add a comma: "First, I will present the problem." Use First to open, Then, Next, or After that to continue, and Finally to close. They keep your structure clear and easy to follow.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"At after I will show the budget."
"After that, I will show the budget."

Put the signpost at the start of the sentence with a comma: "After that, I will...", "Finally, I will...". Say Finally for the last part, never "at the final".
