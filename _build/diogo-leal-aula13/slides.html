Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one warns? Which one measures? Which one gives the way out?

"There is a risk that the approval takes four more weeks."

"It is likely that they answer only in July."

"The go-live might move to August."

"To mitigate it, we start the tests on the old database."

Grammar Rule

Likely, Impact, Plan

MoveStructureExample
Name the riskThere is a risk that + clauseThere is a risk that the approval takes four more weeks.
Measure itIt is likely / unlikely that... / may, might, could + verbThe go-live might move to August.
Give the way outTo mitigate it, we + verb / Unless we..., we will...To mitigate it, we start the tests on the old database.

A risk is not a complaint. A complaint has no numbers and no plan, and the room will always answer "let us wait and see". A risk has a likelihood, an impact, a mitigation and a decision -- and a decision cannot be ignored.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"It has a risk that the vendor delay the tests."
"There is a risk that the vendor will delay the tests."
"Maybe the server will down in the migration."
"The server might go down during the migration."

Use There is a risk that -- never "it has a risk". And after might / may / could comes the verb with no "to" and no "will": might go down.

Chapter 5 -- Talking It Through

The Meeting

She will call you dramatic. Say it anyway.

Dialogue

Do Not Be Dramatic

D

"Before we plan the sprint, I want to flag one risk. The security team has been silent for eleven days."

S

"They are always slow. Do not be dramatic."

D

"I am not. It is likely that the approval takes four more weeks -- that is what it took last year."

S

"And if it does?"

D

"The migration cannot start. Worst case, we lose July. It is a dependency, and it is not in my control."

S

"So you are telling me we are going to fail."

D

"No. To mitigate it, we start the tests on the old database this week. The contingency is to go live on the old one in June."

S

"Nobody else raised this. Why you?"

D

"Because I saw the warning sign, and I will not downplay it. I need one decision from you by Friday."
