Aula 13 -- Pre-class

Asking for Directions

Ask where a place is and follow directions: left, right, straight, corner, block, across from, next to, between. Structures: "Where is...?", "How do I get to...?", imperatives (Go/Turn) and prepositions of place.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Left -- the opposite of right
"Turn left at the corner." (Esquerda)
Right -- the opposite of left
"Turn right at the corner." (Direita)
Straight -- forward, not left and not right
"Go straight for two blocks." (Em frente / reto)
Corner -- where two streets meet
"It is on the corner." (Esquina)
Block -- the distance from one corner to the next
"Go two blocks." (Quarteirão)
Across from -- on the other side of the street, facing it
"It is across from the bank." (Em frente a / do outro lado)
Next to -- right beside something
"It is next to the park." (Ao lado de)
Between -- in the middle of two things
"It is between the bank and the store." (Entre)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Elaine wants to find the museum. She stops a man and asks, Where is the museum? It is not far, he says. Go straight for two blocks, then turn left at the corner. The museum is on your right, next to a big park. It is across from the bank. How do I get there? she asks. You can walk, it is five minutes. Elaine says thank you and finds the museum easily.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Asking & Giving Directions

GRAMMAR

Tres estruturas para se orientar. / Three structures to find your way.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Where is + place?Perguntar o lugar / Ask the locationWhere is the bank?
Go / Turn + directionDar direcao (imperativo) / Give directionsTurn left at the corner.
next to / across from / betweenDizer onde fica / Say where it isIt is next to the park.
