Aula 15 -- The Post-Mortem -- What We Would Have Done Differently

Film
Margin Call (2011)

Over one night, a Wall Street firm runs a live post-mortem on a risk it overlooked: what was the root cause, what was the shortfall, and what they would have done if they had seen it sooner. The whole film is the language of Lesson 15 — hindsight, a decisive turning point, and hard choices about what to write off and what to salvage.

Tip: after the "if only we had acted sooner" moments, write down five third conditionals you hear or could say ("If they had flagged the risk, they would have..."). Then rewrite one about the Halo launch at AVRT.

Podcast
How I Built This (NPR) -- with Guy Raz

In almost every episode, a founder looks back at a setback and says what they would have done differently — the exact hindsight of Lesson 15. You hear real post-mortems on launches that fell short, the root causes, and how the founders salvaged momentum instead of writing the business off, all in natural, unscripted English.

Tip: pick one episode and note three moments of hindsight. Restate each as a third conditional about AVRT: "If we had..., we would have...". Then record a 90-second post-mortem of a real decision.

YouTube
"How to use the third conditional -- 6 Minute Grammar" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on exactly the code from Lesson 15: how the third conditional imagines a past that did not happen (If + had + past participle, would have + past participle), the meaning of regret and hindsight, and the mistakes to avoid — especially putting "would" in the if-clause.

Tip: pause after each example and say your own version about the Halo launch. Then record a 90-second post-mortem using at least four third conditionals and one mixed conditional.
