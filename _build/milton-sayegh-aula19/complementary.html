Lesson 19 -- Closing the Investment

Series / Film
Billions -- any closing or negotiation scene

Watch high-stakes deals get negotiated and signed: how the parties set conditions, push back on a clause, and protect themselves before they agree. A sharp model of the language of the close.

Tip: every time a character sets a condition, rephrase it yourself using "provided that" or "as long as".

Podcast
Acquired -- any episode on a deal closing

Investors and founders walk through how real deals actually close: what due diligence uncovers, which clauses matter most, and how terms get negotiated before anyone signs. The vocabulary of this lesson in its real habitat.

Tip: note one term someone negotiates, then write the condition you'd set in its place ("on condition that...").

YouTube
Harvard Negotiation / Y Combinator -- "How to Negotiate a Term Sheet"

A clear, practical talk on what happens between "yes" and "signed": due diligence, the key terms, and how to ask for a change without killing the deal. Native speakers model calm, confident closing language.

Tip: after watching, record yourself agreeing to a deal with one condition: "We can sign, provided that...".
