Closing the Investment

The investor said yes -- now the deal has to survive due diligence and the fine print. Learn the conditional connectors that set the rules of any agreement -- provided that, as long as, unless, on condition that -- alongside the language of the close: due diligence, a clause, an audit, compliance, to disclose, to waive, to undertake, a binding agreement. Open your books, read the terms, negotiate one clause, and sign a deal you fully understand. Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of closing a deal: the check, the terms and the signature. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario do fechamento de um acordo.

Due Diligence -- auditoria previa / verificacao
the careful check of a company's records and numbers before a deal is signed
"Her team will run due diligence before they wire the money."
Clause -- clausula
a single numbered point or condition within a contract
"There's a clause about not taking a competing backer."
Audit -- auditoria
an official examination of a company's financial records by an independent party
"She wants to see audited accounts for the last two years."
Compliance -- conformidade
the state of following the rules, laws and standards that apply to your business
"We must prove full compliance with export rules."
Disclose -- divulgar / revelar
to make information openly known, especially something you are required to share
"We have to disclose every supplier contract."
Waive -- abrir mao de / dispensar
to choose to give up a right, fee or requirement that you could insist on
"She agreed to waive the early penalty."
Undertake -- comprometer-se a
to formally promise or agree to do something, especially in a contract
"We undertake to keep the records open for an annual audit."
Binding -- vinculante / obrigatorio
legally fixed, so both sides must do what the agreement says
"Once we both sign, the agreement is binding."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read how Milton closes the deal, then answer. Notice the conditional connectors. / Leia como o Milton fecha o acordo e responda; observe os conectores de condicao.

The investor was in, but first her team ran due diligence. "I'll disclose everything," Milton said, offering two years of audited accounts and proof of compliance. "We'll sign quickly as long as your questions are clear." Diana had one clause she cared about: a non-compete. "I won't close unless that's in the agreement." Milton read it carefully. "We can agree, provided that the non-compete is limited to one year." She accepted, and added: "I'll waive the early penalty on condition that you hit the first milestone." Milton nodded. "Then we undertake to keep the records open for an annual audit. Once we both sign, it is binding." Two terms negotiated, every line understood, and a deal he could stand behind.

1. "We'll sign quickly as long as your questions are clear." What does "as long as" do here?
A It sets a condition -- the signing depends on the questions being clear.
B It talks about a length of time only.
C It reports what Diana said.
2. After "provided that", which form is correct?
A ...provided that the non-compete will be limited to one year.
B ...provided that the non-compete is limited to one year. (present, not will)
C ...provided that the non-compete would be limited to one year.
3. What does Milton undertake to do?
A To waive the penalty himself.
B To keep the records open for an annual audit.
C To remove the non-compete clause completely.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Conditional Connectors

GRAMMAR

Use these connectors to set the exact condition of a deal. The verb after them is in the present, never "will".

ConnectorMeaningExample
provided thatonly if this is trueWe agree provided that it is one year.
as long aswhile this stays trueWe'll sign as long as the accounts are clean.
unlessexcept if (= if not)We won't close unless the audit passes.
Common mistake: use the present after the connector, not "will" (as long as the accounts are clean, not "will be"); and "unless" already means "if not", so never add a second "not" (unless the audit passes, not "unless the audit doesn't pass"). PT: estes conectores definem a condicao exata de um acordo. Use sempre o presente depois deles (nunca "will"). "Unless" ja significa "se nao", entao nao use outro "not". "Provided that" e "on condition that" = somente se; "as long as" = enquanto for verdade; "unless" = exceto se.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "I'd rather not show those."
B "Happy to disclose the audited accounts for due diligence."
C "We have no records."
You accept a clause but want a one-year limit. Which is correct?
A "We agree provided that it will be one year."
B "We agree, provided that the non-compete is one year."
C "We agree unless it doesn't be one year."
You want to set a hard requirement before closing. Which is best?
A "We won't close unless the audit doesn't pass."
B "We won't close unless the audit passes."
C "We close, audit or no audit."

A "We undertake keeping the records open."
B "We undertake to keep the records open for the audit."
