Chapter 3

The Conditions Code

Set the rules of the deal in words

Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Each line sets a condition for the deal. What do the orange words do, and what tense follows them?

We will sign as long as the accounts are clean.

We can agree provided that the non-compete is one year.

We will not close unless the audit passes.

These are conditional connectors. They set the exact condition for an agreement, and the verb after them is in the present, never "will":

provided that / on condition that = only if this is true — We can agree provided that the non-compete is one year.

as long as = for the whole time this stays true — We will sign as long as the accounts are clean.

unless = except if; it already means "if not" — We will not close unless the audit passes.

Use them to say yes to a deal without saying yes to everything.

The Structure

Four Ways to Set a Condition

ConnectorMeaningExample
provided thatonly if this is trueWe agree provided that it is one year.
as long aswhile this stays trueWe will sign as long as the accounts are clean.
unlessexcept if (= if not)We will not close unless the audit passes.
on condition thatonly if this one term is metShe'll waive the penalty on condition that we hit the milestone.
Watch Out

Common Mistake

We will sign as long as the accounts will be clean.
We will sign as long as the accounts are clean.

After a conditional connector, the verb is in the present, not "will": as long as the accounts are clean. And remember that unless already means "if not" — never add a second "not": say unless the audit passes, not "unless the audit doesn't pass".

Practice

Set the Condition

"We can sign quickly ___ the documents check out." → as long as / provided that the documents check out.
"We will not close the deal ___ the audit passes." → unless the audit passes.
"She'll waive the penalty ___ we hit the first milestone." → on condition that / provided that we hit the first milestone.
"We agree to the clause, ___ the non-compete is limited to one year." → provided that the non-compete is limited to one year.
Chapter 4

Across the Table

Milton and Diana settle the terms

Dialogue

Milton & Diana

D
I'm glad we're here. Before I wire anything, my team runs due diligence. I'll need audited accounts and proof of compliance with the export rules. Nothing personal, it's just how we work.
M
That's fine. I'll disclose everything: two years of audited accounts and all the supplier contracts. As long as your team has clear questions, we'll answer them fast.
D
Good. There's one clause I care about: you don't take a competing backer. I won't close unless that's in the agreement.
M
I understand it, but as written it's open-ended. We can agree, provided that the non-compete is limited to one year. After that, I need to be free.
D
One year is fair. And I'll waive the early-exit penalty on condition that you hit the first milestone. Once we both sign, it's binding — for both of us.
M
Then we have a deal. We undertake to keep the records open for an annual audit, and we'll sign as soon as due diligence is done. Thank you, Diana — for pushing on the terms.

Practice

Sharpen the Close

From quick lines to a full negotiation

Quick Fire

One Line at a Time

1 / 5

1. Offer to open your books for the investor's check.

"Happy to send the audited accounts for due diligence."

2. Agree to a clause, but set a one-year limit (provided that).

"We can agree, provided that the non-compete is one year."

3. Say you won't close unless one thing happens first (the audit).

"We won't close unless the audit passes."

4. Make a formal promise about the annual check (undertake).

"We undertake to keep the records open for an annual audit."

5. Confirm the deal is final once both sides sign (binding).

"Once we both sign, the agreement is binding."

Spot the Error

Fix the Line

"We will sign as long as the accounts will be clean."
"We will sign as long as the accounts are clean." (present after the connector, not will)
"We won't close unless the audit doesn't pass."
"We won't close unless the audit passes." (unless already means "if not")
"We agree provided that you will limit it to one year."
"We agree provided that you limit it to one year." (present after provided that)
"We undertake keeping the records open for the audit."
"We undertake to keep the records open for the audit." (undertake + to + verb)
Build

Sentence Building

Say each line out loud, then click to compare

1. Open due diligence (disclose + due diligence).
"I'll disclose everything you need for due diligence — two years of audited accounts."
2. Negotiate the clause (clause + provided that).
"We can accept the clause, provided that the non-compete is limited to one year."
3. Set a hard line (unless + audit).
"We won't move to closing unless the audit passes."
4. Make a promise (undertake + compliance).
"We undertake to stay in full compliance and keep the records open for an annual audit."
5. Close the deal (waive + on condition that + binding).
"She'll waive the penalty on condition that we hit the milestone, and once we sign it's binding."
