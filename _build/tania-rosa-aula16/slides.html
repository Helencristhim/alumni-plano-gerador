Chapter 3: So & Such

Make It Stronger

so + adjective · such a + (adjective) + noun

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"The food is so fresh." (so + adjective)

"It is such a charming place." (such a + adj + noun)

"The view was so gorgeous." (so + adjective)

"It was such a fantastic trip." (such a + adj + noun)

WordComes beforeExample
soan adjective (alone)It is so good.
such a(adjective) + nounIt is such a good place.
would recommend / shouldgive the tipI would recommend it. You should go.

Use so before an adjective on its own ("so fresh"), and such a before a noun, with or without an adjective ("such a charming place"). To recommend, say I would recommend... or You should....

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"It is so a nice place."

✓ RIGHT

"It is such a nice place."

✗ WRONG

"I recommend you to go."

✓ RIGHT

"I would recommend going." / "You should go."

Chapter 4: Sharing Tips

Where to go, what to skip

Dialogue

Where Should I Go?

F
I am going to your favorite city next month! What do you recommend?
T
Oh, you should definitely visit the old market. It is such a charming place, and the food is so fresh.
F
Nice! And is the big tower worth it?
T
Honestly, it is a little overrated. I would recommend the rooftop restaurant instead — the view is gorgeous.
F
That sounds fantastic. Anything else I should not miss?
T
Yes! The little garden by the river is a must-see. It is so peaceful, and it is definitely worth it.
