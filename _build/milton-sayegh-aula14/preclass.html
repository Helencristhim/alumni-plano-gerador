Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of everything after the deal. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario de tudo que vem depois do acordo.

Invoice -- nota fiscal / fatura
a document that lists the goods, the price, and what the client must pay
"I'll email the invoice the day after we sign."
Shipment -- remessa / carga enviada
a batch of goods sent from the seller to the buyer
"The first shipment leaves São Paulo on Monday."
Lead time -- prazo de entrega
the time it takes from placing an order to delivering it
"Our lead time on a custom piece is three weeks."
Deliverable -- item a ser entregue / entrega
a specific item or result you have promised to provide
"The deliverable is forty necklaces, boxed and labeled."
Milestone -- marco / etapa-chave
an important point or target along the way to finishing something
"Their store opening is the milestone we must hit."
After-sales -- pos-venda
the care and support you give a client once the deal is done
"In luxury, after-sales is what keeps the client loyal."
Onboard -- integrar / orientar (cliente novo)
to guide a new client or team so they can use what you provide
"We'll onboard their staff on how to display the pieces."
Retain -- reter / manter (o cliente)
to keep a client over time, so they come back and buy again
"If we deliver well, we retain them for years."
Paperwork -- documentacao / papelada
the documents and forms needed to make a deal or order official
"I'll send the signed paperwork this afternoon."
Fulfill -- cumprir / honrar (uma promessa)
to carry out and complete a promise, order, or obligation
"If we fulfill every promise, the client stays."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the handover and answer the questions. / Leia o handover e responda.

The deal was signed, so Milton moved to the handover. "I'll send the invoice and the paperwork today," he promised on the spot. The shipping slot was already booked, so he said, "We're shipping the first shipment on Monday, on the lead time we quoted." He already had a plan for the buyer's team: "We're going to onboard them the week the deliverable arrives." The store opening was the milestone, so the goods had to land before it. For after-sales, he added a promise: "I'll call you a week in." If they fulfill every promise, he was sure they would retain the client for years.

1. Milton says "I'll send the invoice today" on the spot. Why "will"?
A "Will" fits a promise or offer decided in the moment.
B "Will" is only for fixed, booked times.
C "Will" means the plan was made last week.
2. The shipping slot is booked for Monday. Which form does he use?
A "will ship" -- because it is the future.
B "We're shipping" -- present continuous for a fixed arrangement.
C "We ship" -- present simple for a one-time plan.
3. He already planned the onboarding. Which sentence is correct?
A "We going to onboard them next week."
B "We're going to onboard them next week."
C "We will going to onboard them next week."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Forms (will / be going to / present continuous)

GRAMMAR

Same future, three uses: a promise decided now, a plan you already have, and a fixed arrangement.

FormUse it forExample
will + basea promise, offer, or prediction decided NOW"I'll send the invoice today."
be going to + basea plan or intention you ALREADY have"We're going to onboard their team."
present continuous (am/is/are + -ing)a fixed arrangement, usually with a time"We're shipping on Monday at nine."
Watch the traps: for a booked, scheduled time use the present continuous, not "will" ("We're shipping Monday", NOT "We will ship Monday"). Don't drop the "be": "We're going to onboard" (NOT "We going to"). PT: o mesmo futuro tem tres usos -- "will" para promessa/oferta decidida na hora; "be going to" para um plano que voce JA tem; e o present continuous (am/is/are + -ING) para um compromisso ja marcado, geralmente com hora. Nao esqueca o "be".
