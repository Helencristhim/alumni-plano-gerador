Lesson 14 -- After the Deal: Delivery & Keeping the Client

Series / Film
The Founder (2016) -- the build-out and franchising of McDonald's

A film about what happens AFTER the deal: delivery, systems, onboarding new owners, and keeping a brand promise at scale. Watch how every milestone, every shipment, and every bit of after-sales either retains a partner or loses them.

Tip: each time a plan is announced, decide which future form fits: "will", "be going to", or present continuous.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- any episode on "delivery", "logistics" or "customer service"

Practical business English for exactly this stage: confirming an invoice, tracking a shipment, talking lead times and after-sales. The host models the future forms naturally while planning next steps with a client.

Tip: note one sentence that plans a future action, then say it back, swapping in "will", "be going to", or present continuous.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "will, going to and present continuous for the future"

A short, clear lesson on exactly the grammar from this class: when to use "will" for a decision made now, "be going to" for an existing plan, and the present continuous for a fixed arrangement. Native speakers model the difference in real situations.

Tip: after watching, describe your next client handover in five future sentences, using each form at least once.
