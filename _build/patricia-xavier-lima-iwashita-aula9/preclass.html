Escalations Under Pressure

Handling a critical escalation on a customer call. Key words: escalation, critical, urgent, impact, severity, downtime, deadline, commitment, unless, as soon as. Structure: first conditional + urgency (If it fails, we will escalate).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Escalation -- moving a problem to a higher, more urgent level of support (escalação)
"This needs an escalation to the engineering team."
Critical -- extremely serious; it needs action right now (crítico)
"The outage is critical, so we cannot wait."
Urgent -- needs a fast response, very soon (urgente)
"This is urgent — please reply as soon as you can."
Impact -- the effect a problem has on the customer's work (impacto)
"The impact on the sales team is huge."
Severity -- how serious a problem is (gravidade, severidade)
"We raised the severity to level one."
Downtime -- time when a system is not working (tempo fora do ar, indisponibilidade)
"Every minute of downtime costs the client money."
Deadline -- the time by which something must be done (prazo)
"We have a hard deadline tomorrow morning."
Commitment -- a promise to do something (compromisso)
"I made a commitment to update him every thirty minutes."
Unless -- except if; if not (a menos que, a não ser que)
"Unless we act now, we will lose the account."
As soon as -- at the moment that; immediately when (assim que)
"As soon as I have news, I will call you."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

David's dashboard is down, and the impact on his team is huge. The problem is critical, so Patrícia raises an escalation. "If it is a server problem, our engineers will start immediately," she says. She makes a clear commitment: "As soon as I have an update, I will call you." She also warns him: "Unless we act fast, we will miss the deadline." The team raises the severity to level one. If the first fix does not work, they will roll back and protect his data.

1. Por que "If it is a server problem" usa o presente, e não "will"?
A Porque a ação já terminou no passado.
B Porque a parte da condição (if) fica no presente; só o resultado usa will.
C Porque é uma rotina que se repete todo dia.
2. "Unless we act fast" tem o mesmo sentido de:
A "If we do not act fast" (se não agirmos rápido).
B "If we act fast" (se agirmos rápido).
C "When we always act fast" (quando sempre agimos rápido).
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "As soon as I will have news, I will call you."
B "As soon as I have news, I will call you."
C "As soon as I have news, I call you next month."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional & Urgency

GRAMMAR

Como falar de um resultado futuro que depende de uma condição (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
First Conditional
If + present, ... will + verb		Resultado futuro real que depende de uma condição. A real future result.If it fails, we will escalate.
as soon as / whenA parte do tempo fica no presente — nunca "will". The time clause stays present.As soon as I have news, I will call.
unless = if notJá é negativo. Não use outro "not". Already negative, no double "not".Unless we act = If we do not act.
Common errorsNão use will depois de if / as soon as / unless. O resultado SEMPRE usa will.
