Chapter 4: The Code

Present Perfect vs Simple Past

Change up to now · Finished time

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The team has expanded since 2023."

"We have not finished the reporting yet."

"Izabel has just presented to the board."

"A year ago, she prepared everything by hand."

Three sentences share a structure. One is different. Which one, and why?

TenseUseExample
has / have + past participleA change or result UP TO NOW (since, so far, just, yet, already)The team has expanded since 2023.
Negative: have not + participleSomething not done yetWe have not finished it yet.
since / forsince + a start point; for + a length of timesince January; for three years
Simple pastA FINISHED time (yesterday, last quarter, a year ago)She prepared everything a year ago.
Language Focus

Which Tense?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We wait since the October."

✓ RIGHT

"We have waited since October."

✗ WRONG

"She has presented yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"She presented yesterday."

✗ WRONG

"We have finish the project."

✓ RIGHT

"We have finished the project."

Use "since" with the present perfect (have waited since October), never the present simple. A finished time (yesterday) takes the simple past. And the present perfect always needs the past participle: have finished, not have finish.

Dialogue

Reporting Progress to the Board

A
Izabel, before we close, can you give the board a sense of how far we have come this year?
I
Of course. Since January, we have expanded the team and reached two new markets in North America.
A
That is impressive. Have you broken even on the new product line yet?
I
Yes, we have. We broke even last quarter, and we have hit almost every benchmark you set.
A
Excellent. And the reporting? Have you automated it?
I
Not completely yet. We have improved it a lot, but I still prepare some numbers by hand.
A
Understood. You have made remarkable progress this year, Izabel.
I
Thank you. A year ago, I could not present in English. I have come a long way.
