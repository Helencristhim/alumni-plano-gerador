The Story So Far -- Talking About Progress

Reporting the company's journey with the present perfect. Key words: expand, scale up, invest in, improve, reach, achievement, headcount, benchmark, ramp up, break even, progress, so far. Structures: present perfect (has/have + past participle) vs simple past; since / for; already / yet / just.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Expand -- to grow bigger or add more (expandir / crescer)
"We have expanded the team since 2023."
Scale up -- to grow operations bigger and faster (ampliar a operação)
"We hired engineers to scale up production."
Invest in -- to put money into something to make it grow (investir em)
"AVRT has invested in new equipment."
Improve -- to make something better (melhorar)
"We have improved the reporting a lot."
Reach -- to arrive at a level, a target, or a place (alcançar / atingir)
"We have reached two new markets."
Achievement -- something done successfully, a result you are proud of (conquista)
"Breaking even was a real achievement."
Headcount -- the number of people a company employs (número de funcionários)
"Headcount has grown from twelve to twenty."
Benchmark -- a standard or target used to compare performance (referência / meta)
"We have hit almost every benchmark."
Ramp up -- to increase something quickly, like output (acelerar / aumentar)
"We have ramped up production this quarter."
Break even -- to reach the point where costs equal income (atingir o ponto de equilíbrio)
"We have broken even on the new line."
Progress -- forward movement; getting better over time (progresso)
"We have made strong progress this year."
So far -- up to now, until the present moment (até agora)
"So far, the results have been strong."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Over the last three years, AVRT has changed a great deal. When Izabel took over as CEO, the company was small. Since then, the team has expanded, revenue has grown every quarter, and the business has reached new markets in North America. AVRT has invested in new equipment and has broken even on the new product line. Izabel has not automated the reporting yet, so she still prepares some numbers by hand. Last week, she presented the year to the board, and for the first time she did it entirely in English. "We have come a long way," she says, "but we have not finished."

1. Why does the text say "has expanded", "has grown", and "has reached"?
A Because the actions happened at one exact finished time.
B Because they are changes up to now — present perfect (has + past participle).
C Because they are in the present simple.
2. "Last week, she presented the year to the board." Why simple past here?
A Because "last week" is a finished time, so we use the simple past.
B Because it is a change up to now.
C Because "present" has no past participle.
3. What has Izabel NOT done yet?
A She has not broken even.
B She has not automated the reporting yet.
C She has not reached any new markets.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Simple Past

GRAMMAR

Talking about change up to now vs a finished time (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
has / have + participleMudança ou resultado ATÉ AGORA (since, so far, just, already)The team has expanded.
have not + participle + yetAlgo ainda NÃO feitoWe have not finished it yet.
since / forsince + ponto inicial; for + duração. NUNCA "since the October"since January; for three years
Simple pastTempo TERMINADO: yesterday, last quarter, a year ago → She presented it yesterday.

Quiz
