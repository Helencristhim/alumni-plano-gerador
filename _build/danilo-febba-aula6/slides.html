Chapter 3

The Grammar

Irregular past, was/were, and did

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I went to the meeting."

"We had a problem on Monday."

"The team were busy, and I was tired."

"Did you find the cause? I didn't go home."

BasePast (irregular)Example
go / have / makewent / had / madeI went to the office.
find / get / takefound / got / tookWe found the error.
to be (was / were)I/he/she/it = was; you/we/they = wereI was late. They were ready.
did + base verbQuestions and negativesDid you go? I didn't go.

Irregular verbs change form (no -ed). After did/didn't, use the base verb.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I goed to the meeting."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to the meeting."

✗ WRONG

"Did you went home?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you go home?"

✗ WRONG

"We was very busy."

✓ RIGHT

"We were very busy."

✗ WRONG

"I have a problem yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"I had a problem yesterday."

Irregular verbs change form: go to went, have to had. After did, use the base verb. Use were with we/you/they.

What Happened?

A story at the office

Dialogue

A Hard Day

R
Hi Danilo! You look tired. What happened yesterday?
D
Oh, we had a hard day. Our main system went down in the morning.
R
That sounds bad! What did you do?
D
First, the team met in a call. Then we found the cause: a bad update.
R
Good. And did you fix it fast?
D
Yes. It took two hours, but we got it back. I wrote a short report after.
R
Nice work! I had a similar problem last month.
D
Really? What happened?
R
Our database was slow for a week. We made a small change, and it got fast again.
D
Great story. We learned a lot from these days.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What problem did Rachel have last month?
Her database was slow for a week.
2. What did Rachel's team do?

3. What happened after the change?
The database got fast again.
