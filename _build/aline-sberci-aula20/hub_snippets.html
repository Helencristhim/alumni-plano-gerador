Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Reception -- the front desk where you check in and ask for help
"Please check in at the reception." (Recepção)
Lobby -- the big open area near the hotel entrance
"I'll wait for you in the lobby." (Saguão)
Suite -- a large, comfortable hotel room with extra space
"We booked a suite on the fifth floor." (Suíte)
Amenities -- the services and extras a hotel offers
"The amenities here are excellent." (Comodidades)
Housekeeping -- the staff who clean and prepare the hotel rooms
"I'll call housekeeping for more towels." (Limpeza / camareira)
Wi-Fi -- the wireless internet you connect to
"Is there Wi-Fi in the room?" (Wi-Fi / internet sem fio)
Towel -- a piece of cloth you use to dry yourself
"There is no towel in the bathroom." (Toalha)
Faulty -- broken; not working the way it should
"The air conditioning is faulty." (Com defeito)
Late checkout -- leaving your room later than the normal time
"Could I have a late checkout, please?" (Saída tardia)
Complimentary -- free; given to you without an extra charge
"Breakfast is complimentary." (Cortesia / grátis)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline checks in at the reception of the hotel. "Is there Wi-Fi in the room?" she asks. "Yes, there is, and it's complimentary," says the receptionist. "Are there any restaurants in the hotel?" she asks. "Yes, there are two." She goes up to her suite, but there is a problem. "There isn't a towel in the bathroom, and there's no internet -- the Wi-Fi is faulty," she tells the reception. They send housekeeping with towels and fix the Wi-Fi. Notice: to ask, use Is there + one thing and Are there + more than one. To report a problem, use There isn't a + singular or There's no + noun.

1. How does Aline ask about the Wi-Fi?
A "Is there Wi-Fi in the room?"
B "Are there Wi-Fi in the room?"
C "There is Wi-Fi in the room?"
2. How does she ask about the restaurants?
A "Is there any restaurants?"
B "Are there any restaurants in the hotel?"
C "There are restaurants?"
3. How does she report the missing towel?
A "There aren't a towel in the bathroom."
B "There isn't a towel in the bathroom."
C "There no towel in the bathroom."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Asking & Reporting with There

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para perguntar o que o hotel tem e reportar um problema. / Use these forms to ask what the hotel has and report a problem.

Use / UsoForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Perguntar (uma coisa) / Ask (one)Is there + a/an + singular?Is there a gym?
Perguntar (mais de uma) / Ask (more)Are there + plural?Are there two beds?
Reportar (uma coisa) / Report (one)There isn't a + singularThere isn't a towel.
Reportar (nenhum) / Report (none)There's no + substantivoThere's no internet.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use Is there para UMA coisa e Are there para MAIS de uma. Para reportar um problema, use There isn't a + singular ou There's no + substantivo. "There's no" serve para coisas no singular e incontáveis (no internet, no water). Não diga "Are there a gym" -- diga "Is there a gym".
