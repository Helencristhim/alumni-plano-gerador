At the Doctor -- Saying How You Feel (should)

Aprenda a dizer como você se sente e a dar conselhos de saúde: doctor, headache, fever, sore throat, cough, tired, rest, medicine. Gramática nova: should + verbo para conselho (You should rest), should not para o negativo (You should not work), e I have a + sintoma (I have a headache). Ritmo lento e claro.

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. Ele usa I have a para sintomas e should para conselho.

This morning Érica did not feel well. She had a headache and a sore throat. She was very tired, and she had a small fever. So she went to see the doctor. The doctor said: "You should rest at home. You should drink a lot of water. You should not go to work today. You can take some medicine for the headache." Érica listened to the doctor. Now she is at home, in bed. She feels a little better. Tomorrow she hopes her cough is gone.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Saying How You Feel & Advice (should)

GRAMMAR

Como dizer sintomas e dar conselhos. Para dizer como se sente, use I have a + sintoma (I have a headache) ou I am + adjetivo (I am tired). Para dar conselho, use should + verbo na base (You should rest). Para o negativo, use should not + verbo na base (You should not work). Lembre: depois de should, NUNCA use "to" nem "-s". / To give advice: should + base verb. To say how you feel: I have a + symptom.

Tipo / TypeEstrutura / Structure
Sintoma / SymptomI have a + sintoma — I have a headache.
Conselho / Advice (+)should + verbo base — You should rest.
Negativo / Advice (–)should not + verbo base — You should not work.
Pergunta / QuestionShould + I/you + verbo? — Should I see a doctor?
Lembre-seDepois de should, o verbo fica na BASE: sem "to" e sem "-s" (She should rest).
