Film
The King's Speech — official trailer (2010)

The purest version of your Thursday: a leader who has the authority and the substance, but has to deliver it in a voice he is terrified will fail him in the room. Watch how the work is never about perfect fluency — it is about walking in as the person you already are and letting the words carry the truth. Connection to Lesson 15: the whole film is Ruth's instruction — reach for the true sentence, not the perfect one, and the room follows the leader, not the grammar.

Tip: watch the trailer, then find the full film. As you watch, note one moment where he stops performing and starts meaning what he says — and write two sentences about how you will do the same on Thursday, using a hedge-then-commit ("I'm not fully sure yet, but here is what I am sure of").

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant — TED Audio Collective

Organizational psychologist Adam Grant takes you inside how the best leaders actually think, argue, and change their minds — the exact register you have been building: disagreeing without damaging the relationship, holding your ground while staying open, and sounding senior by being honest about what you don't yet know. Connection to Lesson 15: it is a season of masterclasses in the hedge-then-commit move — confident about the thinking, humble about the certainty.

Tip: pick any one episode and listen with no pauses. Then record ninety seconds answering, in your own words: "What does success look like for me, personally, in the Canadian role?" Use one hedge ("I'm not fully sure yet"), then commit to the true part, and land it with a cleft ("What matters most is...").

YouTube
Julian Treasure — "How to speak so that people want to listen" (TED, 10 min)

A precise, practical talk on the mechanics of a voice people actually follow — register, pace, silence, and speaking from honesty rather than performance. It is the technical companion to everything you did tonight: not what you say, but how you land it in a room that is deciding whether to trust you. Connection to Lesson 15: it turns "sound like a leader" from an instinct into a set of choices you can make on Thursday morning.

Tip: watch it twice. On the second pass, pick two techniques (pace and silence are the easiest to steal) and rehearse your meeting opening — "Let me frame my mandate..." — applying them out loud until the delivery matches the content.
