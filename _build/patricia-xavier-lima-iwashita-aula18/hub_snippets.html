Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Decline -- to politely refuse a request or offer (recusar educadamente)
"We sometimes have to decline a request from a customer."
Unfortunately -- used to introduce bad or negative news politely (infelizmente)
"Unfortunately, we can't offer a discount this time."
Limitation -- a limit on what a product or team is able to do (limitação)
"Every plan has some limitations on support hours."
Alternative -- another option that you offer instead of the first one (alternativa)
"When you say no, always suggest an alternative."
Honest -- telling the truth in a clear way, even when it is hard (honesto / sincero)
"I will be honest with you about the timeline."
Reassure -- to make someone feel calmer and more confident (tranquilizar)
"I want to reassure you that your account matters to us."
Pushback -- resistance or strong disagreement from the other side (resistência / objeção)
"Expect some pushback when you decline a request."
Boundary -- a clear limit you set on what you can or will do (limite)
"Setting a boundary protects both sides."
Tactful -- careful not to upset people; diplomatic and polite (diplomático / com tato)
"A tactful no keeps the relationship strong."
Capacity -- the amount of work a team is able to handle (capacidade)
"We don't have the capacity for 24/7 support."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Patrícia has to say no, she never starts with a flat "no". First, she thanks the customer and is honest about the limitation. Then she softens the message. I'm afraid we can't deliver it by Friday. Unfortunately, a thirty percent discount is not possible. We're not able to provide 24/7 support at this time. I understand, but the timeline is fixed. After the no, she always offers an alternative and reassures the customer.

1. Por que a Patrícia evita começar a resposta com um "no" seco?
A Porque "no" não existe em inglês formal.
B Porque soa frio e gera mais resistência; o ideal é suavizar primeiro.
C Porque ela nunca pode recusar nada a um cliente.
2. Qual expressão usamos para suavizar uma má notícia ANTES de dizer não?
A "I'm afraid we can't..." (Receio que não possamos...).
B "I'm happy we can't...".
C "No problem, we can't...".
3. Which reply is the most polite and professional?
A "No. That is impossible."
B "Unfortunately, that isn't possible, but here is an alternative."
C "We can't. Discount no."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Softening a No (Hedging)

GRAMMAR

Como dizer "não" com tato em inglês (explicação em inglês e português).

SoftenerUse / UsoExample
I'm afraid...Suaviza a má notícia antes do não. "Receio que..."I'm afraid we can't do that.
Unfortunately,Introduz a resposta negativa com tato (+ vírgula). "Infelizmente,"Unfortunately, that isn't possible.
We're not able to...Um "we can't" mais suave. "Não conseguimos..."We're not able to offer that at this time.
I understand, but...Mostra empatia e então dá o limite. "Eu entendo, mas..."I understand, but the timeline is fixed.

Regra de ouro: nunca comece com um "No." seco. Suavize primeiro, depois ofereça uma alternativa. "At this time" faz o não soar temporário, não definitivo.
