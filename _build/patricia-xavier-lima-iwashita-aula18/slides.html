A Difficult Request — Email from a Key Account

Hi Patrícia, I am writing because my team is frustrated. We were promised the new reporting feature this quarter, and it is still not live. On top of that, our renewal is coming up next month, and I need three things from you before we sign. To be honest, the last few months have not been easy for us.

First, I need the reporting feature delivered by next Friday, with no more delays. Second, I expect a thirty percent discount on the renewal to make up for the wait. Third, I want a dedicated engineer available to my team twenty-four hours a day. For us, these requests are not really negotiable, because the whole team is depending on this tool every day.

I do not want to escalate this, but my CEO is already asking why we still use your platform. I need to see real commitment from your side this week. Please reply by Thursday and tell me exactly how you plan to fix all of this. Thank you, and I hope we can find a way forward. Best regards, Mark.

Adapted for class use from a customer renewal email

Chapter 4: The Code

Softening the Message

I'm afraid · Unfortunately · We're not able to · I understand, but

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I'm afraid we can't deliver it by Friday."

"Unfortunately, a discount isn't possible."

"We're not able to offer that at this time."

"I understand, but the timeline is fixed."

SoftenerUseExample
I'm afraid...soften bad news before you say noI'm afraid we can't do that.
Unfortunately,introduce a negative answer politely (+ comma)Unfortunately, that isn't possible.
We're not able to...a soft "we can't", less blunt than a flat noWe're not able to offer that right now.
I understand, but...show empathy first, then give the limitI understand, but the timeline is fixed.

Never start with a flat "No." Soften first, then offer an alternative. "At this time" makes the no feel temporary, not permanent.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"No. That is impossible."
"I'm afraid that won't be possible, but here is what we can do."
"We can't. Discount no."
"Unfortunately, we're not able to offer a discount, but we can add premium support."

A flat "No" or "impossible" sounds rude in English business. Soften with I'm afraid / Unfortunately, then offer an alternative.

Chapter 5: The Reply Call

Answering Mark

Dialogue

The Reply Call

M
Patrícia, I need the feature by Friday, a discount, and 24/7 support. Can you confirm all three?
P
Thank you for being honest, Mark. I'm afraid we can't deliver the feature by Friday, but I can give you a firm date.
M
That's not what I wanted to hear. What about the thirty percent discount?
P
Unfortunately, a discount that size isn't something we can offer. However, I can add three months of premium support.
M
And the 24-hour engineer? My team really expects it.
P
We're not able to provide 24/7 support at this time. What I can do is set a clear escalation path for urgent issues.
Comprehension

About the Call

1. What does Patrícia say about delivering the feature by Friday?
She is afraid they can't, but she offers a firm date instead.
2. How does she respond to the discount request?
Unfortunately they can't offer a discount that size, but she adds three months of premium support.
3. What does she offer instead of 24/7 support?
A clear escalation path for urgent issues.
