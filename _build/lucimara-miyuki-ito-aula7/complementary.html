Easy English Podcast — How to Sound More Polite in English

This is the whole of Lesson 7, in one calm episode: how could, would and the softening words turn an order into a favor. You will hear the exact frames you practiced today — "Could you...?", "Would you mind...?", "I was wondering if..." — and why the polite version is the one that works.

Tip (do this in the car, on the way there): listen once, all the way through, with no pauses. Then write down every request you hear that starts with could, would or do you know — and bring two you could not have made on your own. They become the mini listening lesson in Lesson 8.

How to Make Polite Indirect Questions in English

The one piece of grammar from tonight that Brazilians get wrong: the word order inside an indirect question. This lesson drills exactly that — "Do you know where the gym is?", never "where is the gym" — and shows how the indirect frame makes any question softer with a stranger.

Tip: after each example, pause and say your own version out loud, about your real trip — "Do you know if the pool is open?", "Could you tell me what time breakfast starts?" Say five before you finish the video.

Hotel Front Desk Conversations — English Speaking Practice

Real front-desk exchanges, at natural speed: checking in, asking about amenities, sorting out a problem. It is exactly the scene you will live in New York in September — the desk, the room, the small requests — in the mouths of people who are not teaching, which is where you will meet it for real.

Tip: this works as pure audio — listen in the car, without looking at the screen. Every time the guest asks for something, pause and ask yourself: was that an order or a favor? Then say the polite version out loud, even if theirs was already polite.
