Where Are We? -- Speaking Up in the Status Meeting

Give your project status out loud in four parts: overall, done so far, blocked, next step. New vocabulary: update, progress, on track, behind schedule, milestone, blocker, at risk, so far.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Update -- fresh news about how the work is going
"Here is my update on Project Atlas." (Atualização)
Progress -- the work already done, moving toward the goal
"We have made good progress this month." (Progresso)
On track -- going as planned; the dates are still safe
"Overall, we are on track." (No prazo / dentro do plano)
Behind schedule -- later than planned; the work is running late
"We are behind schedule on testing." (Atrasado)
Milestone -- an important date or result in the project plan
"The next milestone is the pilot in June." (Marco / etapa)
Blocker -- a problem that stops the team from moving
"We have one blocker: the license approval." (Impedimento)
At risk -- in danger of failing or arriving late
"The June milestone is at risk." (Em risco)
So far -- until now; from the start to this moment
"So far, we have finished the design." (Até agora)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is Monday, and the leadership team wants an update on Project Atlas. Diogo starts with the overall picture: "Overall, we are on track." Then he reports the progress: "So far, we have finished the design and the first tests." He is honest about the problem: "We have not started the migration yet, because we have one blocker -- the license approval." He knows the June milestone is at risk, so he says it early, not at the end. He also remembers the rule: he says "We finished the tests last Friday" when he gives the day, and "We have finished the tests" when he only reports progress. He closes with the next step: a decision on the license by Friday. Two minutes, and nobody has to ask a single question.

1. How does Diogo begin his update?
A With the overall status: "Overall, we are on track."
B With the full history of the project.
C With the list of every task.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A We finished the tests last Friday.
B We have finished the tests last Friday.
C We are finishing the tests last Friday.
3. Why is the June milestone at risk?
A The license approval is blocking the migration.
B The design is not ready.
C The team is on vacation.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Progress vs Moment

GRAMMAR

Falar do progresso até agora e de um momento fechado. / Talking about progress so far, and about a closed moment.

Uso / UseForma / FormExemplo / Example
Progresso até agora / Progress until nowhave / has + past participleSo far, we have finished the design.
Ainda falta / Still missinghave not + past participle + yetWe have not started the migration yet.
Momento fechado / Closed momentpast simpleWe finished the tests last Friday.
Ponto-chave / Key pointA pergunta que decide tudo: você diz QUANDO? Se sim (last week, yesterday, in June), use o past simple. Se não, você está falando de progresso: use have / has. NUNCA "We have finished it yesterday".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

Your manager asks: "How is Atlas going?" You have twenty seconds. Start with:
A "Overall, we are on track, but we have one blocker."
B "In January we started the analysis, and then in February..."
C "It is complicated."
The migration is late and the June date is in danger. You say:
A "The June milestone is at risk, and this is why."
B "Everything is perfect."
C "I will tell you next month."
A stakeholder asks what you need from her. The best answer is:
A "A decision on the license by Friday."
B "Nothing, thank you."
C "Many things, I will send a long list."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak -- do not write first. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Give the status of a real project at Oracle, out loud, for two minutes. Four parts, in this order: (1) Overall, we are... (2) So far, we have... (3) We have one blocker... (4) The next step is... Record it.
Now the hard question. Your stakeholder asks: "Why did you not tell me about the blocker last week?" Answer out loud, in full sentences: give the answer first, then the reason, then what you will do about it. Record it.
