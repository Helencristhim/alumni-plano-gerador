Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Two of these say WHEN. Two do not. Which is which?

"So far, we have finished the design."

"We finished the design last week."

"We have not started the migration yet."

"The team completed two milestones in June."

Grammar Rule

Progress vs Moment

You want to sayUseExample
Progress until nowhave / has + past participleSo far, we have finished the design.
Something still missinghave not + past participle + yetWe have not started the migration yet.
A closed moment (you say WHEN)past simpleWe finished the tests last Friday.

One question decides it: do you say WHEN? If yes (last week, yesterday, in June), use the past simple. If no, you are talking about progress -- use have or has.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We have finished the tests last Friday."
"We finished the tests last Friday."
"So far, we finished two milestones."
"So far, we have finished two milestones."

If the sentence says WHEN, use the past simple. If it says so far or yet, use have or has.

S

S

"You have it. Send me one line by email so I do not forget."
