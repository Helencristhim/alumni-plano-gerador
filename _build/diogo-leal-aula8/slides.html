Opinion frames and hedging

Grammar Rule

Two Opinion Tools

ToolExample
Opinion frame -- In my opinion / I thinkIn my opinion, we should test first.
Opinion frame -- From my perspectiveFrom my perspective, the risk is low.
Hedging -- perhaps / maybe / mightPerhaps we could run a pilot.
Hedging -- it seems / I tend to / slightlyIt seems slightly risky.

Key idea: Opinion frames introduce YOUR idea. Hedging words (perhaps, might, it seems, slightly, tend to) make it softer and more polite -- you sound open, not aggressive. And remember: agree is a verb, so say "I agree", never "I am agree".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am agree with you."
"I agree with you."

Agree and disagree are verbs. Do not use am, is, or are before them. Say "I agree" and "I disagree", never "I am agree".
