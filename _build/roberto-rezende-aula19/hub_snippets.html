Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recall -- to remember and bring back something from the past
"I recall how nervous I used to be on calls." (Recordar / lembrar)
Milestone -- an important point or achievement in your progress
"Closing my first deal was a real milestone." (Marco / conquista importante)
Reflect on -- to think carefully about a past experience
"Let me reflect on what I have learned this year." (Refletir sobre)
Assess -- to judge or evaluate how good a result is
"Let us assess the outcome of the last quarter." (Avaliar)
Setback -- a problem that delays or slows your progress
"The delay was a setback, but we recovered." (Contratempo / revés)
Refine -- to improve something by making small careful changes
"Each setback helped me refine my approach." (Aprimorar / refinar)
Momentum -- the driving force that keeps progress going
"Let us keep the momentum on the big accounts." (Impulso / ritmo)
Consolidate -- to bring skills together and make them stronger
"I want to consolidate my negotiation skills." (Consolidar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the mid-point of the program, Roberto stops to reflect on his progress. He likes to recall where he started. "I used to be very nervous on client calls, and I used to read every line from a script," he says. "The team used to lose deals on price alone." Now everything is different: he leads the call, he assesses each client, and he closes the deal himself. Reaching this point is a real milestone. There were setbacks, like the Shanghai delay, but each one helped him refine his approach. For the second half, his goals are to keep the momentum and consolidate his negotiation style. Notice: to talk about a past habit or state that is not true now, English uses used to + base verb.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Used To + Base Verb

GRAMMAR

Use used to + verbo na base para falar de um hábito ou estado do passado que NÃO é mais verdade. / Use used to + base verb for a past habit or state that is no longer true.

Forma / FormSujeito + used to + base verb
AfirmativaI used to be nervous on calls.
AfirmativaWe used to lose deals on price.
NegativaI did not use to speak up.
PerguntaDid you use to read from a script?

Key point / Ponto-chave: use used to + verbo na base (be, lose, read) para um hábito ou estado que era verdade no passado e não é mais. A forma é igual para todos os sujeitos. Na negativa e na pergunta com did, escreva use to (sem o -d): did not use to, did you use to. NUNCA "used to being" nem "did not used to".
