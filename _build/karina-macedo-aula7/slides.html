Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

going to -- a plan -- will -- a promise

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we talk about the future?

"I am going to prepare a proposal."

"We are going to meet next week."

"I will send you the report today."

"I will call you soon."

Grammar Rule

going to & will

StructureWhen we use it
am / is / are going to + verb (I am going to prepare)A plan you decided before now
will + verb (I will call)A promise, an offer, or a quick decision now
next / soon / tomorrowTime words for the future

Key idea: use going to for a plan you made before, and will for a promise or a quick decision. After will, use the base verb (will call, not will to call). With going, keep the word to (going to call).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I going to send the report. I will to call you."
"I am going to send the report. I will call you."

Use am / is / are before going to, and use the base verb after will (no "to").
