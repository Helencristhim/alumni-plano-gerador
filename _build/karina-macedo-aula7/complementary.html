Lesson 7 -- Plans for the Future: Going to & Will

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 1: "The Dundies"

People at work talk about their plans, set goals, and make promises in simple, everyday English. Great to hear "going to" and "will" used naturally in a business place.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two future sentences: one plan with "going to" and one promise with "will". Then say what you are going to do next week.

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Talking About the Future: going to and will"

A slow, clear podcast on the future forms you need: "going to" for plans and "will" for promises and quick decisions, with many easy examples.

Repeat each example out loud. Then record a short message: say your plan, your goal, and one promise with "will".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Future: 'will' and 'going to'"

A short, practical lesson on today's grammar: when to use "going to" for a plan you decided before, and when to use "will" for a promise or a quick decision.

After watching, write five future sentences about your work. Use "going to", "will", "next", "soon", and "deadline" at least once each, then read them aloud.
