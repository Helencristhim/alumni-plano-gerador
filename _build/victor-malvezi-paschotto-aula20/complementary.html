Lesson 20 -- Coaching & Development Conversations

Series
Ted Lasso — any scene where Ted or Coach Beard draws someone out with questions instead of orders

A masterclass in coaching by asking, not telling. Watch how the best moments turn on a single open question that makes a player find their own answer — and how giving the answer would have shut it down.

Tip: pause after each closed question you hear and rewrite it as an open one (what / how).

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant / The Look & Sound of Leadership — any episode on coaching, mentoring, or asking better questions

Leaders unpack the shift from manager to coach: asking open questions, building cause-and-effect growth plans, and handing people ownership. Ideal prep for the coaching one-on-ones Victor runs at Brink's.

Tip: note one open question the host asks, and one cause-and-effect statement you could turn into a zero conditional.

YouTube
TED / Harvard Business Review — "The GROW coaching model" or a clip on the zero vs first conditional

A practical bridge into the two tools of this lesson: the open questions that make people think, and the zero conditional (if + present, present) that maps a growth plan as a system — "if you protect your mornings, the deep work gets done".

Tip: after watching, write three zero conditionals for your own development plan this quarter.
