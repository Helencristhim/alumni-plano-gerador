Lesson 18 -- Difficult Conversations & Giving Feedback

Series
"Ted Lasso" -- the difficult one-on-one scenes

Watch scenes where a leader gives honest feedback while staying kind and calm. Notice how he addresses the behavior, acknowledges the person's side, and reassures them instead of attacking.

Watch with English subtitles. Pick one feedback scene and report three lines in reported speech: "He told her she...", "She said she would...".

Podcast
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Give Tough Feedback That Helps People Grow"

A practical guide to difficult conversations: address the behavior not the person, acknowledge their perspective, and keep them accountable without making them defensive.

Record a 90-second feedback conversation about a real situation. Address the behavior, report one thing the person told you using reported speech, and agree on a next step.

YouTube
"Reported Speech in English -- Say vs Tell & Backshift"

A clear lesson on reporting what people said: the verb moves one step back (is to was, will to would), and the difference between say and tell. Directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, take five things teammates said this week ("The build is ready", "I will test it") and report each one: "He said the build was ready", "She told me she would test it".
