Aula 18 -- Pre-class

Difficult Conversations & Giving Feedback

Giving clear, kind feedback to your team: address the behavior not the person, acknowledge what is true, ask for the perspective, and reassure. New grammar: reported speech (he said the release was late; she told me she would fix it). New vocabulary: feedback, address, acknowledge, behavior, perspective, defensive, reassure, accountable.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Feedback -- useful, honest information you give someone to help them grow
"Good feedback is not about blame; it is about helping someone grow." (Feedback / retorno)
Address -- to deal with a problem or topic directly, not avoid it
"Last week I had to address a real problem with a developer." (Tratar / abordar diretamente)
Acknowledge -- to accept and say openly that something is true
"I acknowledged that the workload had been heavy." (Reconhecer / admitir)
Behavior -- the way a person acts; what they do, not who they are
"Describe the behavior, never attack the person." (Comportamento)
Perspective -- a person's point of view; how they see a situation
"Then I asked for his perspective, because his side matters." (Perspectiva / ponto de vista)
Defensive -- reacting to protect yourself, so you stop listening
"When people feel respected, they do not become defensive." (Defensivo / na defensiva)
Reassure -- to make someone feel calmer and supported in a hard moment
"I reassured him that I was on his side." (Tranquilizar / dar segurança)
Accountable -- responsible; ready to own the result of your own work
"When people feel respected, they stay accountable and they change." (Responsável / que presta contas)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last week Rafael had to address a difficult problem with a developer. In their one-on-one, he stayed calm and started with the facts. He said the last two releases were late, and he acknowledged that the workload had been heavy. Then he asked for the developer's perspective. The developer told him the test environment had been broken for days, and that nobody had reported it. Rafael reassured him that he was on his side, so the developer did not become defensive. He said he would flag any blocker early, and Rafael focused on the behavior, not the person. The developer stayed accountable, and the hard conversation built trust instead of fear.

1. Why did the developer NOT become defensive?
A Because Rafael attacked the person, not the behavior.
B Because Rafael acknowledged the workload and reassured him he was on his side.
C Because Rafael avoided the problem.
2. Which sentence is correct reported speech?
A "He said me the releases are late."
B "He said the releases were late."
C "He told the releases were late."
3. What did the developer tell Rafael was the real cause?
A The test environment had been broken and nobody had reported it.
B He did not want to do the work.
C Rafael had given him too little feedback.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Para reportar o que alguém disse, o verbo geralmente RECUA um passo no tempo: present vira past, will vira would. Use say sem pessoa (he said that...) e tell com pessoa (he told me that...). / To report what someone said, the verb usually moves one step back in time: present becomes past, will becomes would. Use say with no person, and tell with a person.

Direct / DiretoReported / ReportadoExample / Exemplo
is / arewas / wereHe said the release was late.
present simplepast simpleShe told me she needed more time.
willwouldHe said he would fix it.
past simplepast perfect (had + p.p.)She told me the tests had passed.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use tell + pessoa (told me), nunca "said me". O verbo recua um passo: "He said me he is busy" está errado; "He told me he was busy" está certo. O "that" é opcional.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each feedback situation.

You want to open a difficult conversation without attacking the person. The best opener is:
A "I would like to address something honestly: the last two releases were late."
B "You are always slow and unreliable."
C "Never mind, it does not matter."
You report what a teammate said in the meeting. The correct sentence is:
A "She said me she will fix it."
B "She told me she would fix it."
C "She told she would fix it."
A teammate is getting defensive. The best thing to say is:
A "Stop making excuses."
B "I want to reassure you: I am on your side, and I value your perspective."
C "This is your problem, not mine."
