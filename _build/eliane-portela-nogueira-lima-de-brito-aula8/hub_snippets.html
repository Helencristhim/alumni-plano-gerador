Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are words you have already met. Listen and say each one to reactivate it. / São palavras que você já viu. Ouça e diga cada uma para reativá-la.

Track record -- a history of past performance and achievements / histórico de realizações
"My track record in reproductive medicine is strong."
Credentials -- your qualifications, training and experience / suas qualificações e credenciais
"My credentials open doors in any hospital."
Milestone -- an important stage or event in a project / marco, etapa importante
"Opening the clinic is our next milestone."
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest in a project or decision / parte interessada
"Every stakeholder must stay informed."
Agenda -- the list of topics to cover in a meeting / pauta da reunião
"Let me walk you through the agenda."
Takeaway -- the main point you want people to remember / conclusão principal
"The key takeaway is that patients come first."
Proposal -- a formal plan offered for consideration / proposta formal
"The supplier sent a detailed proposal."
Compliance -- following official rules, laws and standards / conformidade com as regras
"Compliance with regulations is non-negotiable."
Facilitate -- to lead a meeting and help the discussion run smoothly / conduzir a reunião
"I will facilitate today's review meeting."
Consensus -- general agreement among a group of people / consenso
"We reached a consensus on the budget."
Cohort -- a group who go through a program together / turma, grupo do programa
"My executive cohort had sixty peers."
Capstone -- a final project that brings the whole journey together / projeto final de conclusão
"The clinic is the capstone of my career."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane opens her review meeting with confidence. I have been building this clinic for two years, she says, using the present perfect for her ongoing work, and next year we will open, adding a clear future plan. When a supplier proposal comes up, she keeps her questions polite and indirect: Could you tell me how the compliance works? She softens her requests with a modal, Would you be able to adjust the terms?, and makes a careful offer: If we moved forward, we would sign this month. Finally, she describes the executive program she wants to join in the passive voice: Applicants are evaluated on leadership, and the curriculum is taught by faculty from two schools. Six structures, one professional voice.

1. Which structure does Eliane use to talk about her ongoing work?
A The present perfect ("I have been building...").
B The simple past ("I built...").
C The passive ("It was built...").
2. How does Eliane ask about the compliance?
A With a blunt, direct question ("How the compliance works?").
B With a polite, indirect question ("Could you tell me how the compliance works?").
C She does not ask; she tells them.
3. Which sentence in the text is in the passive voice?
A "Next year we will open."
B "Applicants are evaluated on leadership."
C "If we moved forward, we would sign."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each professional situation.

You want to describe your ten years of experience. What sounds most natural?
A "I work here since ten years."
B "I have been working here for ten years."
C "I am working here ten years ago."
You want to ask a supplier a question, but politely. Which is best?
A "Tell me how does the warranty work."
B "Could you tell me how the warranty works?"
C "How the warranty work?"
You want to describe how a program selects students. Which passive sentence is correct?
A "Applicants evaluate on leadership."
B "Applicants are evaluated on leadership."
C "Applicants is evaluate on leadership."
You want to make a careful offer to close a deal. What do you say?
A "If we moved forward, we would sign this month."
B "We sign now, no more talk."
C "Maybe we will sign, maybe not."

Reflection
