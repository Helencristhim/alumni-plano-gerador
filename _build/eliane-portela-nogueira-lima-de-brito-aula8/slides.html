Language Focus

Which Structure?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I work in this field since 2013.
I have worked in this field since 2013.
Could you tell me how does it work?
Could you tell me how it works?
Applicants evaluate on leadership.
Applicants are evaluated on leadership.

Three lessons in one slide: use the present perfect for experience up to now, keep normal word order in indirect questions, and always add "be" for the passive.

Dialogue

The Board Review

M
Good to see you, Eliane. Thanks for the update. Could you walk me through where the clinic project stands?
E
Of course, Mark. I have been building this for two years, and we have just hit a major milestone: the facility is finished. Next year we will open.
M
Excellent. And the supplier we discussed? Do you know whether the compliance checks are done?
E
They are. The proposal has been reviewed and the compliance is confirmed. I asked them politely to adjust one term, and if they agree, we would sign this month.
M
That is a lot of progress. As a stakeholder, I appreciate how clearly you present it. Is every part of the team on the same page?
E
We are. I facilitated a review last week and we reached a consensus on the timeline. Every stakeholder weighed in before we adjourned.
M
Impressive. One more thing — I hear you are applying to an executive program. How are applicants evaluated?
E
On leadership, not only on scores. The curriculum is taught by faculty from two schools, and the capstone is presented to the whole cohort. My capstone would be this clinic.
M
Eliane, two years ago you could barely finish this sentence in English. Listen to yourself now. The key takeaway for the board is simple: you are ready.
Did You Catch It?

About the Meeting

1. What milestone does Eliane report to Mark?
The facility is finished, and the clinic will open next year.
2. What does Mark ask about the supplier?
Whether the compliance checks are done; Eliane confirms the proposal has been reviewed and compliance is confirmed.
3. What is Mark's key takeaway for the board?
That Eliane is ready — she can now present the whole project confidently in English.
