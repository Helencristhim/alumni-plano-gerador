The Rule

Signposting Phrases

FunctionSignpostExample
OpenLet me start by... / I'd like to begin by..."I'd like to begin by giving you an overview."
Order pointsFirst, I'll... / After that... / Then..."First, I'll cover the market."
Move onMoving on to... / Let's turn to..."Moving on to our results."
CloseFinally... / To sum up..."To sum up, we are ready to grow."

Key idea: after start by and begin by, use the -ing form (by giving, by looking). To order your points, use the present and I'll (I will) plus the base verb (cover, show, walk). Signpost every transition so the audience always knows where you are.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I'd like to begin by give you an overview."
"I'd like to begin by giving you an overview."

After begin by and start by, use the -ing form. With First, I'll, use the base verb: "First, I'll cover the market."
