Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Signpost -- a word or phrase that tells the audience where you are in a talk
"Use a signpost before every new section." (Sinalização)
Overview -- a short, general summary of the whole presentation
"Let me start with a quick overview of today's talk." (Visão geral)
Outline -- the main structure or plan of a presentation
"Here is the outline: three points and a conclusion." (Estrutura)
Audience -- the group of people listening to your presentation
"Know your audience before you build the slides." (Plateia)
Cover -- to deal with or include a topic in your presentation
"In this section I'll cover our results." (Abordar)
Walk through -- to explain something to someone step by step
"Let me walk you through the plan for the year." (Explicar passo a passo)
Highlight -- to draw attention to the most important point
"I want to highlight one number on this slide." (Destacar)
Transition -- the moment when you move from one part of a talk to the next
"A clear transition helps the audience follow you." (Transição)
Move on -- to go from the current topic to the next one
"That covers the market. Now let's move on to results." (Seguir adiante)
Takeaway -- the most important idea you want people to remember
"The key takeaway is that our market is growing." (Mensagem principal)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signposting Language

GRAMMAR

Signposting language guia a plateia: frases fixas para abrir, ordenar os pontos, mudar de tópico e fechar. Depois de "start by" e "begin by" usamos o verbo com -ing. / Signposting language guides the audience: fixed phrases to open, order your points, change topic, and close. After "start by" and "begin by", use the -ing form.

FunctionSignpostExample
OpenLet me start by... / I'd like to begin by..."I'd like to begin by giving you an overview."
Order pointsFirst, I'll... / After that... / Then..."First, I'll cover the market."
Move onMoving on to... / Let's turn to..."Moving on to our results."
CloseFinally... / To sum up..."To sum up, we are ready to grow."

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de start by / begin by use o verbo com -ing (by giving, by looking). Para ordenar os pontos, use I'll (I will) + verbo base (cover, show, walk). Sinalize cada transição para a plateia sempre saber onde você está.
