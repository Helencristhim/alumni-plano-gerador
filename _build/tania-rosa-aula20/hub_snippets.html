Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read Tânia's little story and answer the questions. Notice the grammar from your whole journey.

Last month I travelled abroad for the first time, all by myself. At the airport, I checked in and found my gate without help. On the plane, I said, "I would like a coffee, please." Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Your Grammar Toolbox (Review)

Grammar

Esta é a sua "caixa de ferramentas" do programa todo. Use o past simple para falar da viagem que você fez ("I visited Paris"). Use would like para pedir com educação ("I would like a coffee, please"). Use should + verbo para dar conselhos ("You should book early") e had better + verbo para um conselho FORTE ("You had better leave now"). Depois de should/had better, nunca use "to". / This is your toolbox from the whole program. Use past simple for the trip you had, would like for polite requests, should for advice, and had better for strong advice. No "to" after should/had better.

Ferramenta / ToolEnglishPortuguês
past simpleI visited five cities.Eu visitei cinco cidades.
would likeI would like a coffee, please.Eu gostaria de um café, por favor.
should + verboYou should book early.Você deveria reservar cedo.
had better + verboYou had better leave now.É melhor você sair agora.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

You want to talk about a trip you took last year. What do you say?
A "Last year I visited Lisbon and I loved it."
B "Last year I visit Lisbon."
C "Last year I will visit Lisbon."
You want to order food politely at a restaurant.
A "Give me food now."
B "I would like the main course, please."
C "I would like to give food."
A friend is about to travel. You give strong advice about the passport.
A "You had better to keep your passport safe."
B "You had better keep your passport safe."
C "You better keeping your passport safe."
