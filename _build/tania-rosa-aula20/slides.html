Chapter 3: Your Toolbox

Your Grammar Toolbox

past simple · would like · should · had better

Open the Code

Listen and Remember

I checked in at the airport all by myself. (past simple)

I would like a table for two, please. (polite request)

You should bring your passport. (advice)

You had better call the staff now. (strong advice)

ToolUseExample
past simpletalk about the trip you hadI visited five cities.
would likeask politelyI would like a coffee, please.
shouldgive adviceYou should book early.
had bettergive strong adviceYou had better leave now.

These four tools cover most travel talk. After should and had better, the verb has NO "to": "should book", "had better leave".

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I go to the airport."

✓ RIGHT

"Yesterday I went to the airport."

✗ WRONG

"You should to bring your passport."

✓ RIGHT

"You should bring your passport."

Chapter 4: Putting It Together

Tânia Shares Her Trip

A call from the road

Dialogue

A Call from the Road

F
Tânia! How is your trip? Tell me everything.
T
It is amazing! I checked in at the airport by myself, and my flight was on time.
F
That is wonderful. And the hotel? Was your reservation ready?
T
Yes! And today I asked a local for directions and ordered dinner all in English.
F
You should be so proud. Any advice for me before my own trip?
T
Yes: you should learn a little English first, and you had better keep your passport safe.

Comprehension

About the Dialogue

1. What did the friend ask at the start?
How the trip was, and to tell him everything.

His own trip.
3. What two pieces of advice did Tânia give him?
Learn a little English first, and keep his passport safe.
