Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key words for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Appointment -- a planned time to meet someone
"I would like to make an appointment with you." (Compromisso / hora marcada)
Slot -- a specific time you can book, like 10 a.m.
"I have a slot on Wednesday morning." (Horário / vaga na agenda)
Arrange -- to plan and set up a meeting
"Let's arrange a call for next week." (Organizar / combinar)
Convenient -- works well for you; easy and suitable
"Would Wednesday at ten be convenient for you?" (Conveniente)
Free -- not busy; available at that time
"Are you free on Thursday afternoon?" (Livre / disponível)
Reschedule -- to change a meeting to a new time
"I am afraid we have to reschedule our call." (Remarcar)
Postpone -- to move a meeting to a later time
"Can we postpone the meeting to next week?" (Adiar)
Clash -- when two things are at the same time
"The board meeting clashes with our call." (Coincidir / conflitar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the short text, then answer the questions. / Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The supplier wants to arrange a meeting. He asks Percival: "Are you free on Wednesday? I have a slot at ten." But the board meeting clashes with that time, so Percival writes back: "I am afraid we have to reschedule." He does not cancel -- he wants to postpone it. He proposes a new day: "Could we move it to Thursday at three? Would that day be convenient for you?"

1. Why does Percival have to reschedule?
A The board meeting clashes with the Wednesday slot.
B He does not want to meet the supplier.
C The supplier is on vacation.
2. How does Percival ask for a time?
A "Are you free in Wednesday?"
B "Are you free on Wednesday? I have a slot at ten."
C "You will meet me Wednesday."
3. How does Percival propose the new time?
A "Could we move it to Thursday at three?"
B "Could we move it for Thursday at three?"
C "We cancel the meeting now."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Ask for a Time & Move It

GRAMMAR

Para marcar um horário você pergunta Are you free on + dia? e confirma com Would + dia + be convenient? Para mudar, avise com I am afraid we have to reschedule e proponha um novo horário com Could we move it to + novo dia? Use "on" com dias (on Wednesday) e "to" para o novo dia (move it to Thursday). / To book a time, ask Are you free on + a day; to change it, say I am afraid we have to reschedule, then propose Could we move it to + a new day.

Função / FunctionPatternExample
Pedir um horário / Ask for a timeAre you free on + day?Are you free on Wednesday morning?
Confirmar se serve / Confirm it worksWould + day + be convenient?Would Thursday at three be convenient?
Avisar a mudança / Announce a changeI am afraid we have to rescheduleI am afraid we have to reschedule our call.
Propor novo horário / Propose a new timeCould we move it to + new day?Could we move it to Thursday instead?

Key point / Ponto-chave: dizemos free on + dia (não "free in") e move it to + novo dia (não "move it for"). Após have to usamos o verbo base: reschedule (não "rescheduling"). / We say free on + a day and move it to + a new day; after have to we use the base verb.
