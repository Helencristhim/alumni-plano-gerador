Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

Saying Who You Are -- Present Simple for Identity, Role, and Institutional Life

Say what you do every day: your work, your team, your role. Key words: professor, institution, council, delegation, manage, represent, team, role. Structure: the present simple (I work / she works), with the third-person -s and questions with do / does. And the phrase that opens any professional introduction: "I am responsible for...".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Simple (I work / she works)

GRAMMAR

Use the present simple for what you do every day. The secret is the small -s: with she, he, or it, the verb takes an -s. In questions and negatives, the -s moves to does.

SubjectYes (+)No (-)Question (?)
I / you / we / theyI work at a university.I do not work on Sunday.Do you work here?
he / she / itShe works at a school.She does not travel alone.Does she study English?

Watch out: do not forget the -s with she / he (she works, not "she work"). And use doesn't, never "don't", with she / he: "He doesn't speak Portuguese". Place: at an institution (at a university), in a city (in Maceio).

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your rehearsal for the Seoul forum.

Introduce yourself at an international education forum in 4-5 sentences: your name, your work, what you manage, and what you are responsible for. Add one sentence about Clarinha using "She + verb + s". Use: I am..., I work at..., I manage..., I am responsible for... Take your time and don't read from a script.
