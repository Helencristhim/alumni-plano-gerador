Chapter 4: The Code

The Present Simple

I work · she works · do you work?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work at a university. I manage a team."

"She works at a school. She studies English."

"I do not travel alone. My team stays in Brazil."

"Does Clarinha speak English? Yes, she does."

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
I / you / we / theyI workI do not workDo you work?
he / she / itShe worksShe does not workDoes she work?

The secret is the little -s: with she, he, or Clarinha, the verb takes an -s. In the question and the negative, the -s moves to does.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She work at a university."
"She works at a university."
"He don't speak Portuguese."
"He doesn't speak Portuguese."

With she / he / it, add -s (she works), and use doesn't for "no" — never "don't".

Chapter 5: A Real Introduction

The Reception in Seoul

Someone asks: "What do you do exactly?" — and this time, you answer

Dialogue

What Do You Do Exactly?

L
Good evening, and welcome to Seoul. My name is James Lee. I am a professor at this university.
M
Good evening, Professor Lee. Nice to meet you. My name is Maria Clara. I work in education, in Brazil.
L
Wonderful. What do you do exactly, Maria Clara?
M
I am a professor, and I manage a team. I am responsible for international partnerships.
L
That is impressive. So you represent your institution here tonight?
M
Yes, I represent my university and a public council. I wear many hats.
L
I understand very well. Does your team travel with you?
M
No, my team stays in Brazil. This time, I travel alone, and I speak English on my own.
L
That is a big step. Your English is clear and calm. Do you have family in Brazil?
M
Yes. I have three children and one granddaughter. Her name is Clarinha, and she studies English too.
