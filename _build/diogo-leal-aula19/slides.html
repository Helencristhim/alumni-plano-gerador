Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one proves you tried? Which one shows the cost? Which one asks for something real?

"I have asked her three times, and I have offered help."

"I have been waiting for two weeks."

"If we do not have it by Wednesday, we lose the sprint."

"I would like you to call her director today."

Grammar Rule

Tried, Cost, Ask

MoveStructureExample
Prove you triedI have + past participle (asked, nudged, offered) + times / datesI have asked her three times.
Show the costIf we do not + verb + by + day, we + resultIf we do not have it by Wednesday, we lose the sprint.
Ask for something preciseI would like you to + verb (never just "help me")I would like you to call her director today.

Escalation is a last resort, and it is never a surprise. You escalate the PROBLEM, not the person -- and you tell the person first, today, before anybody calls anybody. Tomorrow you still have to work with her.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am waiting since two weeks."
"I have been waiting for two weeks."
"She did not answered my emails."
"She did not answer my emails."

Tempo que começou no passado e continua: I have been waiting FOR two weeks (nunca "I am waiting since"). E depois de did not vem o verbo no infinitivo: did not answer, nunca "did not answered".
