Lesson 19 -- Inbox Zero, Writing Executive Emails

Series
The Office (US) -- any episode with Jim and Pam emailing

Notice how short, friendly work messages get to the point fast. Spot the salutation, the one clear ask, and the close. Compare a casual message with a formal one.

Pick one short exchange and rewrite it as a formal executive email: clear subject line, "Dear...", one polite ask, "Best regards".

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Writing professional emails

A full episode on email structure and polite requests: "Could you please...", "Would you be able to...". Revisits today's words: subject line, recipient, attach, regards.

Write a draft email after the episode. Read it once for clarity and once for politeness, then say it out loud.

YouTube
"How to Write a Professional Email in English"

A clear guide to the four parts: subject line, greeting, body with one polite ask, and close. Learn the difference between formal and casual register, and how to keep it short.

After watching, write three real emails for work: a follow-up, a request, and a short update. Use one polite ask in each.
