Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Read each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Subject line -- the short title at the top that tells the topic of the email
"A clear subject line tells the reader the topic at once." (Assunto / linha de assunto)
Recipient -- the person who receives the email; the reader
"Keep it short so the recipient can read it fast." (Destinatário)
Attach -- to add a file so it is sent together with the email
"I attach the slides for your records." (Anexar)
Draft -- a first version of the email, before it is finished and sent
"I write a draft, read it again, then send it." (Rascunho)
Regards -- a polite way to close an email: "Best regards" or "Kind regards"
"Best regards, Diogo Leal." (Atenciosamente / cordialmente)
Forward -- to send an email you received on to another person
"I will forward her message to the team." (Encaminhar)
Thread -- a chain of emails and replies on the same topic
"Let us keep the budget question in one thread." (Conversa / cadeia de e-mails)
Salutation -- the opening greeting line of an email, like "Dear Maria"
"Use a warm salutation: Dear Maria or Hi Maria." (Saudação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the meeting, Diogo writes a short email. He starts with a clear subject line so the recipient knows the topic at once: "Project Atlas -- summary and next step." Then comes a warm salutation: "Dear Maria." In the body he makes one polite ask: "Could you please confirm the budget by Friday?" He is warm but direct. He also writes: "Would you be able to review the draft before then?" He says he will attach the slides for her records. To close, he writes "Best regards, Diogo Leal." The whole email is three short lines, so a busy manager can read it on the phone.

1. Why does Diogo write a clear subject line?
A So the recipient knows the topic at once.
B To make the email longer.
C To hide the ask.
2. How does he make the ask polite?
A "Could you please confirm the budget by Friday?"
B "Confirm the budget now."
C "Maybe the budget is somewhere."
3. Why is the email three short lines?
A So a busy manager can read it on the phone.
B Because he has nothing to say.
C Because long emails are better.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Polite Request

GRAMMAR

Como pedir algo de forma educada no e-mail. / How to ask for something politely in an email.

Opener / AberturaThen / DepoisExample / Exemplo
Could you please+ verbo base"Could you please send the report?" (Você poderia enviar...?)
Would you be able to+ verbo base"Would you be able to confirm the date?" (Você conseguiria confirmar...?)
I would appreciate it if you could+ verbo base"I would appreciate it if you could review the draft." (Eu agradeceria se você pudesse...)
Common error / Erro comumDepois de Could you use o verbo base, nunca "to" ("Could you send", não "Could you to send"). E é "I would appreciate it if you could", não "I would appreciate if you confirm".
Key point / Ponto-chaveUm pedido educado suaviza uma ordem. "Send the report" vira "Could you please send the report?" Acrescente please e você soa caloroso, claro e profissional.
