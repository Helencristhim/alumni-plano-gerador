Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Last resort -- the final option, when everything else has failed
"Escalation is a last resort, not a first move." (Último recurso)
Involve -- to bring another person into the problem
"I have to involve her director now." (Envolver)
Back me up -- to support you publicly, in front of others
"I need you to back me up in the meeting." (Me dar respaldo)
Transparent -- open: you tell people what you are doing
"I was transparent: I told her I would escalate." (Transparente)
Tension -- the bad feeling left between two people
"There is tension now, and I have to manage it." (Tensão)
Admit -- to say that something is true, even when it hurts you
"I admit I should have asked her earlier." (Admitir)
Resolve -- to end a problem, for good
"I want to resolve this today, not to win it." (Resolver)
Formal -- official: written down, on the record
"This is not a formal complaint about her." (Formal)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Three attempts and no answer. Escalation is a last resort, and Diogo has reached it. He goes to Sophia: "I need to involve you." She tests him before she helps: what have you tried? "I have asked three times -- the fifth, the ninth, the twelfth. I made the ask smaller, and I offered someone from my team to help." Then the cost, with a date: if the page does not arrive by Wednesday, the sprint is lost. Then the ask -- and it is precise, never "help me": "I would like you to call her director today." This is not a formal complaint about a person; it is a request for a page. Diogo knows there will be tension, so he is transparent: he tells Camila himself, this afternoon, before anybody calls anybody. And he admits his own mistake: he should have escalated a week ago. He wants to resolve the problem -- not to win a war -- because on Monday he still needs her to back him up.

1. Why does Diogo tell Camila before Sophia calls?
A Because an escalation must never be a surprise -- or he makes an enemy for life.
B Because he wants her to send the page in the next hour.
C Because Sophia asked him to keep it secret.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A I have been waiting for two weeks.
B I am waiting since two weeks.
C I wait since two weeks.
3. What is a good ask when you escalate?
A "I would like you to call her director today."
B "Please help me with this."
C "Do something about her."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Tried, Cost, Ask

GRAMMAR

Escalar sem virar inimigo: provar o que tentou, mostrar o custo e pedir algo preciso. / Escalating without making an enemy.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Provar / Prove you triedI have + particípio (asked, nudged, offered) + datasI have asked her three times.
Custo / Show the costIf we do not + verbo + by + dia, we + resultadoIf we do not have it by Wednesday, we lose the sprint.
Pedir / Ask preciselyI would like you to + verbo (nunca só "help me")I would like you to call her director today.
Ponto-chave / Key pointDuração que continua: I have been waiting FOR two weeks -- nunca "I am waiting since two weeks". Depois de did not, verbo puro: did not answer. E a regra que salva a relação: a pessoa ouve de VOCÊ, antes de qualquer telefonema.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of a clean escalation. Record each one. Calm and factual -- anger in your voice turns a request into a complaint.

I need to involve you, and this is a last resort.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. A peer has blocked you for three weeks, after three attempts. You:
A Go to your manager with what you tried, the cost, and a precise ask -- and tell the peer first.
B Send an angry email with your manager in copy.
C Wait another week and hope it moves.
2. Your manager asks: "What do you want from me?" The best answer is:
A "One call to her director today, asking for the page by Wednesday."
B "I want you to help me with this."
C "I want you to talk to her about her attitude."
3. Before your manager calls the other director, you:
A Tell your colleague yourself, today, that you are escalating.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real blocker at Oracle that has not moved in weeks. Escalate it out loud, for two minutes: (1) this is a last resort, (2) what you tried -- three attempts, with dates, (3) what you offered, (4) the cost, with a number and a date, (5) the precise ask. Record it.
Now your manager says: "Sort it out yourself. I am not calling another director over one page." Answer out loud. Do not blame the person, do not get emotional -- prove the attempts, give the number, and make the ask smaller. Record it.
