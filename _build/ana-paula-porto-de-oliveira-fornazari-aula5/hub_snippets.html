Lesson 05 -- Pre-class

A Memorable Trip

Narrating a memorable trip. Key words: journey, scenery, adventure, sightseeing, souvenir, breathtaking, landscape, to wander, coast, sunset, unforgettable, to get lost. Structure: past continuous vs past simple (was/were + -ing for the background action, past simple for the sudden moment; while + long action, when + short action).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Journey -- a trip from one place to another, usually a long one
"The journey to the mountains took six hours."
Scenery -- the natural features of an area, such as mountains and rivers
"The scenery along the coast was amazing."
Adventure -- an exciting or unusual experience, often with some risk
"Getting lost in the old town was a real adventure."
Sightseeing -- the activity of visiting interesting places as a tourist
"We spent the whole day sightseeing in the city."
Souvenir -- an object you buy to remember a place you visited
"I bought a small souvenir for my mother."
Breathtaking -- extremely beautiful or impressive
"The view from the top was breathtaking."
Landscape -- everything you can see when you look across a wide area of land
"The green landscape stretched to the horizon."
To wander -- to walk around slowly with no clear direction or plan
"We wandered through the narrow streets for hours."
Coast -- the land right next to the sea
"We drove along the coast at sunrise."
Sunset -- the time in the evening when the sun goes down
"We watched the sunset over the water."
Unforgettable -- so good or special that you can never forget it
"It was an unforgettable trip."
To get lost -- to lose your way and not know where you are
"We got lost in the forest before sunset."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last summer, Ana Paula went on the best journey of her life. One morning, while she was driving along the coast, the sun rose over the sea and the scenery was breathtaking. She stopped in a small town and wandered through the streets. While she was doing some sightseeing, it started to rain, so she bought a lovely souvenir and waited in a cafe. Later, she got lost for a while, but it became a real adventure. That evening, while everyone was watching the sunset, she took her favorite photo. It was an unforgettable day.

1. Why do we say "she was driving" and not "she drove"?
A It is the long background action in progress.
B It is a short, finished action.
C Driving never uses the past simple.
2. In "while she was doing some sightseeing, it started to rain", which action was sudden?
A The sightseeing (was doing).
B The rain starting (it started).
C Both happened slowly.
3. Which sentence about the sunset is correct?
A "While everyone watched the sunset, she was taking her photo."
B "While everyone was watching the sunset, she took her photo."
C "While everyone was watching the sunset, she was took her photo."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

How to tell what was happening in the background and what happened in the moment.

FormUseExample
Past continuous
(was / were + -ing)		A longer action in progress -- the background of the story.I was walking on the beach.
Past simpleA short, finished action -- the moment that happened.I saw a whale.
Negative & questionwas not / were not + -ing; Was / Were + subject + -ing?Were you sleeping when I called?
when & whileUse while with the long action (was doing) and when with the short one (did): "While I was taking photos, I met a friend."
