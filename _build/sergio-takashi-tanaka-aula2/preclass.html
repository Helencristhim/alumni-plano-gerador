Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

The Quarterly Report -- What Happened Last Quarter

Reading a risk report and reporting what happened. Key words: quarter, figures, breach, loss, spike, volatility, default, provision, to downgrade, findings. Structure: the past simple (regular and irregular verbs) to narrate what happened last quarter.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Quarter -- a three-month period in a company's financial year
"The report covers the first quarter of the year."
Figures -- the numbers and amounts in a report
"The figures came back to normal in March."
Breach -- a break of a rule or an agreed limit
"We reported the breach to the regulator."
Loss -- money that a company lost during a period
"The committee recorded a small loss."
Spike -- a sudden, sharp rise in a number
"There was a spike in volatility in January."
Volatility -- how much a price moves up and down
"Market volatility rose sharply last quarter."
Default -- a failure to pay back money that is owed
"A partner bank went into default on a loan."
Provision -- money set aside to cover a possible loss
"We set aside a provision for the loss."
To downgrade -- to lower an official rating or score
"The agency did not downgrade the desk."
Findings -- the main results and conclusions of a review
"The findings of the report were clear."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last quarter was difficult for the desk. In January, market volatility rose sharply, and several positions lost value. In February, a partner bank defaulted on a loan, and the desk recorded a small loss. The team acted fast: it assessed the exposure, set aside a provision, and reported the breach to the regulator. In March, the figures improved, and the regulator did not downgrade the desk.

1. Why do we say "volatility rose sharply"?
A It is a finished action in the past, so we use the past simple.
B It is happening right now, so we use the present continuous.
C It is a routine, so we use the present simple.
2. Which sentence about the past is correct?
A "The bank defaulted last month."
B "The bank default last month."
C "The bank was default last month."
3. How do we make the negative past of "report"?
A "We did not report the breach."
B "We did not reported the breach."
C "We not reported the breach."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple

GRAMMAR

How to say what happened and finished in the past.

FormUseExample
Regular verbs
verb + -ed		A finished action in the past. Signal words: last quarter, in January, two days ago.We reported the breach.
Irregular verbs
new form		Same use, but the verb changes shape.rise → rose, fall → fell, set → set
Was / WereThe past of "to be" (was for I/he/she/it, were for you/we/they).The quarter was hard; the figures were high.
Did / Did notNegatives and questions use the base verb after did: we did not breach the limit; did the bank default?
