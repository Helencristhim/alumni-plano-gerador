Chapter 3: What Happened

The Past Simple

rose · fell · defaulted · reported

Grammar Discovery

Read and Notice

"Last quarter, volatility rose sharply."

"A partner bank defaulted on a loan."

"We reported the breach to the regulator."

"The report showed a small loss."

What do the orange verbs have in common?

FormUseExample
Regular verbs
verb + -ed		A finished action in the pastWe reported / it showed
Irregular verbs
new form		Same use, but the verb changes shaperise → rose, fall → fell
Was / WereThe past of "to be"The quarter was hard; the figures were high
Negative & question
did not / did		Use the base verb after did: we did not breach the limit; did the bank default?

Finished in the past = past simple. After did / did not, the verb goes back to base form.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Last quarter, the market fall a lot."
"Last quarter, the market fell a lot."
"We did not reported the breach on time."
"We did not report the breach on time."
"The regulator downgrade the bank in May."
"The regulator downgraded the bank in May."

Use the past simple for finished actions. Irregular verbs change shape (fall → fell), after did not the verb goes to base form, and regular verbs need the -ed ending.
