Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Last month I booked the flight to Lisbon."

"Yesterday I packed my bag."

"I chose an aisle seat."

"Two days ago I took a taxi to the airport."

All four are finished. So why do green verbs end in -ed, and blue verbs do not?

FormUseExample
Regular verbs
verb + -ed		Most verbs. You add -ed and it is done.book → booked · pack → packed · ask → asked
Irregular verbs
a new word		A small group. They change completely, and you learn them one by one.choose → chose · take → took · go → went
Always the sameThe verb does NOT change with I / you / he / she. One form for everybody.I booked · she booked · we booked
Time expressionsThey tell you it is the past. When you hear one, use the past.yesterday · last week · last night · two days ago

Regular verbs take -ed. Irregular verbs become a new word. Nobody changes for he or she.

Pronunciation

The -ed Trap

-ed is written, but it is almost never a new syllable. Listen, then copy the music.

booked — one syllable, ends like a T
packed — one syllable, ends like a T
checked — one syllable, ends like a T
arrived — two syllables, ends like a D
waited — here -ed IS a new syllable (after T)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I did booked an aisle seat."
"I booked an aisle seat."
"I have packed my bag yesterday."
"I packed my bag yesterday."
"Last week I choose the flight."
"Last week I chose the flight."

One past per sentence. If "did" is there, the verb goes back to normal. And "yesterday" does not change the verb for you — you do.

One checked bag of twenty-three kilos.
2. How much is the fee for excess baggage?
One hundred dollars — but it depends on the weight and on your ticket.

Boarding time is eleven forty, from gate twenty-two.
Listening 2

She Tells You What She Did

Marta is in the same line, for the same flight. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. Which seat did Marta choose, and why?
An aisle seat, because she is tall and she likes to stand up and walk.
2. Why did she pack her bag two times?
The first time it was too heavy — twenty-six kilos, and her allowance is twenty-three.
3. Did she pay a fee?
No, she did not. She took out two books and a pair of shoes and put them in her carry-on.
