Somebody Feed Phil — Season 5, Episode 5: Madrid (Netflix)

Phil is back, and this time he lands in Madrid — a short flight from Lisbon, and the closest thing on television to your own trip. He arrives alone, he asks, he orders, he makes friends, in the same slow and warm American English. He is a man in his sixties crossing Europe on his own, and he is never in a hurry.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Phil spends the whole episode telling you what he did: "I ate...", "I went...", "I took...". Every time you hear one of those past verbs, say it out loud after him. That is the grammar of this lesson, alive and in his mouth.

Podcast
Speak English Now — Episode 353: Summer or Winter? It Depends Where You Live

Georgiana tells the story of Emery, who left Madrid with a suitcase of light clothes and landed in Buenos Aires in the middle of winter. He packed the wrong bag — which is exactly the problem of this lesson. Then she tells the same story again, first in the past and then in the present, so that the verbs come back to you through your ear and not through a table.

Tip: the questions are the point, not the story. When Georgiana asks, stop the audio and answer out loud before she does. There is a free transcript, but open it only on the third listen — your ear first, your eyes last.

YouTube
Oxford Online English — Airport English: At the Airport

Two teachers take the check-in counter apart, line by line: the bag that weighs too much, the excess baggage fee, asking to change your seat, and the questions the agent asks you before anything else. It is the conversation of this lesson, in the order it really happens, with the words you will need at the counter.

Tip: watch the check-in part twice. The second time, pause after every line of the passenger and say the line yourself, out loud, before you start the video again. You are the passenger at that counter.
