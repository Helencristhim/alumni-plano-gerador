Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

Rosângela Checks In -- Navigating the Check-In Counter with Confidence

Tell the agent what you already did, choose your seat and solve a problem with your bag. Key words: book, pack, choose, window seat, aisle seat, checked bag, luggage allowance, excess baggage, boarding time, fee. Structure: the past simple, with regular verbs (booked, packed) and irregular verbs (chose, took, went, had, bought), plus time expressions: yesterday, last week, two days ago. Expression: it depends on.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to every word and read the example. These are the ten words that live at the check-in counter. Three of them are actions you do before you travel.

To book -- to pay for a flight or a hotel before the day of the trip
"I booked the flight last month."
To pack -- to put your clothes and your things inside a bag
"I packed my bag yesterday."
To choose -- to take one option and leave the other
"I chose an aisle seat, not a window seat."
Window seat -- the seat next to the window, where you can look outside
"My husband likes the window seat."
Aisle seat -- the seat next to the corridor, where you can stand up without asking anybody
"I would like an aisle seat, please."
Checked bag -- the big bag that travels under the plane, not with you
"I have one checked bag and one carry-on."
Luggage allowance -- the weight and the number of bags your ticket gives you for free
"My luggage allowance is twenty-three kilos."
Excess baggage -- the weight over your allowance, that you pay extra for
"Three kilos over: that is excess baggage."
Boarding time -- the time when you enter the plane
"Boarding time is eleven forty."
Fee -- the money you pay for something extra
"The fee is one hundred dollars."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Last month Rosângela booked two tickets to Lisbon. On the internet, she chose two seats: a window seat for her husband, and an aisle seat for her. Yesterday she packed her checked bag: three dresses, two pairs of shoes, and a present for her friend. This morning they took a taxi, and they arrived at Terminal 3 at nine o'clock. At the counter, the agent put her bag on the machine: twenty-six kilos. Her luggage allowance was twenty-three. "This is excess baggage," he said. "There is a fee of one hundred dollars, but it depends on the weight." Rosângela did not pay. She opened her bag, she took out the present, and she put it in her husband's bag. The second time, her bag was twenty-two kilos. Then she asked one more question, alone, in English: "What is the boarding time?"

1. "Last month I booked the flight." Why does the verb end in -ed?
A Because "book" is a regular verb, and regular verbs make the past with -ed.
B Because the subject is "I". With "he" it would be different.
C Because the sentence is a question.
2. She chose an aisle seat. Why not "she choosed"?
A Because "choose" never changes in the past.
B Because "choose" is irregular: the past is a new word, "chose". No -ed.
C Because you only use -ed after "I", not after "she".
3. "Rosângela did not pay." Why is it "pay" and not "paid" here?
A Because "pay" has no past form.
B Because the sentence is about the present.
C Because "did" already carries the past. One past per sentence, so the verb goes back to normal.
4. The agent says the fee "depends on the weight". What does this mean?
A The fee is always one hundred dollars, for everybody.
B There is no single answer: the fee changes with the weight of the bag.
C The fee is free if your bag is heavy.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

One tense answers every question at the counter: what you already did.

FormUseExample
Regular
verb + -ed		Most verbs. You add -ed and it is done.I booked the flight. I packed my bag. I asked the agent.
Irregular
a new word		A small group. They change completely: learn them one by one.choose → chose · take → took · go → went · have → had · buy → bought
Negative
did not + verb		"Did" carries the past, so the verb goes back to normal. One past per sentence.I did not pay the fee. (never "did not paid")
Question
Did you + verb?		Same rule: "did" is the past, the verb stays normal.Did you pack this bag yourself? When did you book it?
Always the sameThe verb does not change with I / you / he / she. One form for everybody.I booked · she booked · we booked
Time expressionsThey tell you it is the past. When you hear one, use the past.yesterday · last week · last night · two days ago · a few hours ago

The one rule to remember: one past per sentence. If did is in the sentence, the verb goes back to normal: "I did not pay", never "I did not paid".

Speaking

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Pick the best answer for these real moments at the counter.

The agent asks: "When did you book this flight?" You answer:
A "I did booked it last month."
B "I booked it last month, on the internet."
C "I book it last month."
He asks: "Did you pack this bag yourself?" The correct answer is:
A "Yes, I did packed it."
B "Yes, I did. I packed it yesterday."
C "Yes, I have packed it yesterday."
You want the seat next to the corridor, so you can stand up and walk. You say:
A "I would like an aisle seat, please."
B "I would like a window seat, please."
C "I would like a checked bag, please."
Your bag is 26 kilos. Your allowance is 23. The agent asks for $100. The best thing to say is:
A Say nothing, pay the one hundred dollars, and ask your son later.
B "Is there a problem with my bag? How much is the fee? Could I have a minute, please?"
C "My bag is not heavy. This is not excess baggage."
You ask how much the fee is. The agent answers: "It depends on the weight." This means:
A The fee is the same for every passenger.
B There is no fee today.
C There is no single answer — the fee changes with how heavy the bag is.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the prompt. There is no right or wrong version. Speak for 60 seconds, with no script.

Think about a real trip you took to Europe with your husband. Tell the story in the past: when did you book the flight? When did you pack? Which seat did you choose, and why? How did you go to the airport? What happened with your bags? Use at least six verbs in the past, and finish with one sentence using "it depends on". Take your time and do not read from a script.
