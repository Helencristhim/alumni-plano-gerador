Discover the Pattern

Which Tool?

On Monday I joined the team meeting.

Could you send the email, please?

I was tired because I worked all day.

Hello Doctor Lewis, I am writing to invite you.

ToolExample
Past"I joined the meeting." — was/were + verb-ed
Request"Could you send it?" — Can/Could you + base verb
Connector"tired because I worked" — and / but / because
Email"Hello ... Best regards" — greeting + closing

Four tools, four lessons · you already use them all

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I work late."

✓ RIGHT

"Yesterday I worked late."

✗ WRONG

"Could you to help me?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you help me?"

Past: add -ed. Request: no "to" after Can you / Could you.

Chapter 4: A Calm Meeting

"A Low-Stakes Meeting"

Talk about your week, calmly and clearly

Dialogue

A Calm Meeting

C
Good morning, Graziele. How was your week?
G
It was busy, but it was good. I worked on the new schedule.
C
Well done. Can you tell me about Monday?
G
On Monday I joined the team meeting and I helped a new nurse.
C
Great. Could you send me a short email about it?
G
Of course. I am writing to update you. Best regards, Graziele.
C
Perfect. One more thing. Could you invite Doctor Lewis, please?
G
Yes. I will write a formal email and invite her to the meeting.
C
Thank you. You speak with confidence now.
G
Thank you, Doctor Carter. I feel ready.
