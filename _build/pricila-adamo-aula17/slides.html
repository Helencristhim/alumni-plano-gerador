Chapter 3: The Third Conditional

The Imaginary Past

What would have happened, if...

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"If I had known, I would have booked earlier."

"If I had taken that trip, I would have learned the language."

"I wish I had learned English sooner."

"If only I had taken that trip."

FunctionStructureExample
Unreal past resultIf + had + past participle, would have + past participleIf I had known, I would have booked.
Possible result... could have + past participleIf we had saved, we could have gone.
Past regretI wish + had + past participleI wish I had learned English sooner.
Strong past regretIf only + had + past participleIf only I had taken that trip.

The third conditional talks about an unreal past: if + past perfect (had + past participle), then would/could have + past participle. Compare with last lesson's second conditional (an unreal NOW: "if I missed the train, I would..."). Here the past perfect carries the unreal meaning, so never put "would" in the if-clause.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"If I would have known, I would have booked."

✓ RIGHT

"If I had known, I would have booked."

✗ WRONG

"I would of stayed a few more days."

✓ RIGHT

"I would have stayed a few more days."

Never put would in the if-clause -- use had + past participle. And it is "would have", never "would of" (they just sound the same).

Reflection Words & Meanings

Second vs Third

An Unreal Now vs Past

SECOND CONDITIONAL -- unreal now / future (Lesson 15)

"If I missed the train, I would take a taxi." (it could still happen)

THIRD CONDITIONAL -- unreal past (today)

"If I had missed the train, I would have taken a taxi." (it's over -- it didn't happen)

One extra step back in time: past simple → past perfect, and would + verb → would have + past participle.
