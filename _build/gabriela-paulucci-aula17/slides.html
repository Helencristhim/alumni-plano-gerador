Grammar Rule

Are you...? / I am...

FunctionStructureExample
Officer asksWhere are you + verb-ing?Where are you going?
You answerI am + verb-ing + place.I am going to London.
Officer asksHow long are you staying?How long are you staying?
You answerI am staying for + time.I am staying for one week.

Key idea: the officer asks with Are you ...-ing? and you answer with I am ...-ing. Always keep am in your answer: "I am going", not "I going".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I staying for one week." (no am)
"I am staying for one week."

Always keep am in your answer: I am going, I am staying.
