Chapter 3 -- The STAR Method

Structure Discovery

Situation, Task, Action, Result

Structure Discovery

Listen and Notice

Each line answers a different question. What is the order from start to result?

Situation: "When I joined, the release was eight weeks late."

Task: "My task was to get it back on track without cutting quality."

Action: "I rebuilt the plan, I cut the scope, and I aligned the team."

Result: "We shipped on time, and the client renewed for two years."

The Method

The STAR Answer

StepIt answersExample
S -- SituationWhere were you? What was the context?The release was eight weeks late.
T -- TaskWhat was your goal or the problem?My task was to get it back on track.
A -- ActionWhat did you do? (past simple)I rebuilt the plan and aligned the team.
R -- ResultWhat happened? (a real number)We shipped on time and the client renewed.

Key idea: Open with your experience up to now in the present perfect ("I have led eight releases"), then tell ONE finished story with STAR in the past simple. The Result always carries a number, so it sounds concrete and real.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am good with difficult projects, trust me."
"The release was late. I rebuilt the plan, and we shipped on time."

Do not just give an opinion. Tell a short STAR story: a situation, your action in the past simple, and a result with a real number.
