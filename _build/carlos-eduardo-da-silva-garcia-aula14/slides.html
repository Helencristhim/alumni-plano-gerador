Chapter 4: WH-Questions

Who, Where, What, How Many

How do we build a question?

Discover the Grammar

Read and Notice

"Who is responsible for the data?"

"How many farms are in the program?"

"How often do you verify the properties?"

"Are the producers ready for the audit?"

Question wordAsks aboutExample
Whoa personWho is responsible for the data?
Where / Whata place / a thingWhere are your farms located?
How many / How oftena number / frequencyHow often do you verify the farms?
Whichchoose from a groupWhich criteria are critical?
Yes / NoDo/Does or Is/Are firstAre the producers ready?

With "do you", use the base verb: How often do you verify? Yes/no questions start with Do/Does or Is/Are. / Com "do you", use o verbo base; perguntas sim/nao comecam com Do/Does ou Is/Are.

Language Focus

Which Question Word?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"How often you verify the farms?"

✓ RIGHT

"How often do you verify the farms?"

✗ WRONG

"What means this number?"

✓ RIGHT

"What does this number mean?"

With you/they, use "do" + base verb. With he/she/it, use "does" + base verb. / Com you/they use "do" + verbo base; com he/she/it use "does" + verbo base.

Dialogue

Carlos Asks the Rwanda Team

C
Hi Chantal. Before the audit, I have a few questions. Who is responsible for the farm data in Rwanda?
C
Hi Carlos. Our field team is responsible for the data.
C
Good. How many farms are in the program this year?
C
There are one hundred and twenty farms in our region.
C
And how often do you verify the properties?
C
We verify them twice a year. Now I have a question for you. Where are your farms located?
C
They are in the south of Minas Gerais, near Varginha. Are your producers ready for the audit?
C
Most are ready, but a few farms need more documents.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. Who is responsible for the farm data in Rwanda?
Chantal's field team is responsible for the data.
2. How many farms are in Chantal's region?
One hundred and twenty farms.
3. How often does Chantal's team verify the properties?
Twice a year. Most producers are ready, but a few farms need more documents.
