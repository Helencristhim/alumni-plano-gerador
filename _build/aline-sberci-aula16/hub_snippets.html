Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Arrange -- to plan and organize something for a fixed time
"Let me arrange a meeting for Monday." (Marcar / organizar)
Appointment -- a fixed time you agreed to meet someone
"I have an appointment at noon." (Compromisso / hora marcada)
Book -- to reserve a time, room, or place in advance
"Let me book the meeting room." (Reservar)
Confirm -- to make a time or plan final and certain
"Please confirm the meeting time." (Confirmar)
Reschedule -- to change a meeting or plan to a new time
"Can we reschedule for Friday?" (Remarcar)
Postpone -- to move something to a later time or date
"We had to postpone the call." (Adiar)
Free -- not busy; with no plans at that time
"Are you free on Thursday?" (Livre / sem compromisso)
Slot -- a short open period of time in your calendar
"My best slot is three o'clock." (Horário livre)
Set up -- to create and organize a meeting or event
"Let me set up a planning meeting." (Organizar / criar)
Calendar -- where you see your days, dates, and plans
"Let me check my calendar." (Calendário / agenda)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tom calls Aline to set up a planning meeting. "On Wednesday I am flying to Basel," he says, "so Wednesday does not work. Why don't we meet on Monday afternoon?" Aline checks her calendar: "On Monday morning I am presenting the budget, but I am free in the afternoon. Three o'clock is a good slot." Tom answers, "Let's confirm Monday at three. If something comes up, we can reschedule." Notice: for a plan that is already fixed, we use am / is / are + verb-ing with a future time ("I am meeting the team on Monday"). To suggest a time, we use Why don't we or Shall we + base verb, or How about + verb-ing.

1. Why does Wednesday not work for Tom?
A He is flying to Basel that day.
B He does not like Wednesdays.
C He is on holiday all week.
2. How does Tom suggest a time?
A "We must meet on Monday."
B "Why don't we meet on Monday afternoon?"
C "Why don't we to meet on Monday?"
3. Which sentence is a correct fixed future plan?
A "I meet the team on Monday."
B "I am meeting the team on Monday."
C "I am meet the team on Monday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Fixed Plans & Suggestions

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para falar de planos fixos e sugerir um horário. / Use these forms to talk about fixed plans and suggest a time.

Use / UsoForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Plano fixo no futuro / Fixed future planam / is / are + verb-ing + tempoI am meeting Tom on Monday.
Sugerir (verbo base) / SuggestWhy don't we + verbo baseWhy don't we meet at three?
Sugerir (verbo base) / SuggestShall we + verbo base?Shall we set up a call?
Sugerir (-ing) / SuggestHow about + verbo-ing?How about meeting on Friday?

Key point / Ponto-chave: para um plano já combinado, use be + verb-ing com um tempo futuro ("I am meeting Tom on Monday"). Para sugerir um horário, use Why don't we ou Shall we + verbo base, ou How about + verbo-ing. Depois de Why don't we e Shall we, nunca use to + verbo -- diga "Why don't we meet", não "Why don't we to meet".
