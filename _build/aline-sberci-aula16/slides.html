Grammar Rule

Fixed Plans & Suggestions

UseFormExample
Fixed future planam / is / are + verb-ing + timeI am meeting Tom on Monday.
Suggest (base verb)Why don't we + verbWhy don't we meet at three?
Suggest (base verb)Shall we + verb?Shall we set up a call?
Suggest (-ing)How about + verb-ing?How about meeting on Friday?

Key idea: for a plan that is already fixed, use be + verb-ing with a future time ("I am meeting Tom on Monday"). To suggest a time, use Why don't we or Shall we + base verb, or How about + verb-ing.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Why don't we to meet on Monday?"
"Why don't we meet on Monday?"

After Why don't we and Shall we, use the base verb (meet, set up), never to + verb.

Dialogue

Arranging the Meeting

T

"Hi Aline. We need to set up a planning meeting. Shall we meet next week?"

A

"Sure. Let me check my calendar. On Wednesday you are flying to Basel, right?"

T

"Yes. So Wednesday does not work. Why don't we meet on Monday afternoon?"

A

"Monday morning I am presenting the budget, but I am free in the afternoon. Three o'clock is a good slot."

T

"Perfect. Let's confirm Monday at three. If something comes up, we can reschedule."

A

"Great. I am sending a calendar invite now, and I will set up the meeting room."
