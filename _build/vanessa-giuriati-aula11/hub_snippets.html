Talking About Your Routine

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Occasionally -- sometimes, but not very often (de vez em quando)
"I occasionally work from a cafe."
Constantly -- all the time, without stopping (constantemente/o tempo todo)
"My phone rings constantly at work."
Hardly ever -- almost never; very rarely (quase nunca)
"I hardly ever skip breakfast."
Typical -- normal; what usually happens (típico/comum)
"A typical day starts at seven."
Tend to -- to usually do something; to have the habit of (costumar/ter o hábito de)
"I tend to work better in the morning."
Deal with -- to handle or take care of a task or problem (lidar com/resolver)
"I deal with emails first thing."
Run errands -- to do small jobs outside, like shopping or the bank (resolver tarefas/fazer compras)
"I run errands on Saturday morning."
Prepare for -- to get ready for something that is coming (preparar-se para)
"I prepare for meetings the night before."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

On a typical day, Marina tends to wake up at six. She deals with her emails first, and by noon she handles the hardest tasks. She hardly ever takes long breaks, although she occasionally steps outside for fresh air. In the afternoon she runs errands and then she prepares for the next day. This is her normal week. Right now, however, things are different: she is working on a new project, so this month she is staying later than usual. Her routine is steady, but at the moment something extra is happening.

1. What does Marina deal with first every day?
A Her emails.
B Her errands.
C The next day's plan.
2. Which sentence describes a habit (something always true)?
A "She deals with her emails first."
B "She is working on a new project."
C "She is staying later than usual."
3. Why does the text use "is working" and "is staying"?
A Because they are old habits.
B Because they are temporary, happening around now.
C Because they happened in the past.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo verbal. / When to use each tense.

TenseEnglishPortuguês
Present simple
habits, routines		I check / She checks · usually, every day, on Mondayspara hábitos e rotinas (o que é sempre verdade)
Present continuous
happening now		I am checking / She is checking · right now, this week, at the momentpara algo temporário, acontecendo agora
AffirmativeI work / I am working · She works / She is working
NegativeI do not work / I am not working
⚠ CarefulUse the present simple for habits, even daily ones. State verbs (want, like, know, need) stay simple: say "I know", not "I am knowing".
