Lesson 10 -- Meetings That Matter

Series
The Office (US) -- a team meeting scene

Watch a workplace meeting and notice how the chair opens the meeting, follows an agenda, and makes suggestions. Listen for should, could and might when people propose ideas.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three suggestions you hear with should, could or might, and one meeting word, such as agenda or recap.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "How to Make Suggestions in Meetings"

A clear episode on running and joining meetings in English: opening with an agenda, making polite suggestions, and reaching consensus before you adjourn.

Record a 2-minute meeting: open with the agenda, make three suggestions with should, could and might, allocate one action item, and recap before you adjourn.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "should, could and might (modals)"

A focused lesson on the modal verbs should, could and might for advice, options and possibility. It directly reinforces today's grammar for making suggestions in a meeting.

After watching, write six meeting sentences: two with should, two with could, and two with might.
