Meetings That Matter -- Running a Team Meeting

Learning to run a team meeting in English: meeting vocabulary (agenda, minutes, attendee, recap, action item) and the modal verbs should, could and might to make suggestions and recommendations. New vocabulary: agenda, minutes, attendee, chair, recap, action item, postpone, allocate, brainstorm, proposal, consensus, adjourn.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics to discuss in a meeting
"Let us follow the agenda for today." (Pauta)
Minutes -- the written record of a meeting
"I will take the minutes today." (Ata)
Attendee -- a person who takes part in a meeting
"Every attendee should read the agenda." (Participante)
Chair -- to lead a meeting and guide the discussion
"Walyson will chair the meeting." (Presidir)
Recap -- a short summary of the main points
"Let me recap the main decisions." (Recapitular)
Action item -- a task someone must do after the meeting
"Each action item has one owner." (Tarefa)
Postpone -- to move an event to a later time or date
"We might postpone the launch." (Adiar)
Allocate -- to give money, time or people to a task
"We should allocate the budget carefully." (Alocar)
Brainstorm -- to share many ideas quickly as a group
"Let us brainstorm ideas for the launch." (Gerar ideias)
Proposal -- a plan or idea you suggest for a decision
"Maria shared a strong proposal." (Proposta)
Consensus -- general agreement among all the people
"We need consensus before we decide." (Consenso)
Adjourn -- to close a meeting, often until a later time
"We could adjourn before lunch." (Encerrar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Today Walyson will chair the project meeting for the first time. He sends the agenda to every attendee in advance. He thinks they should start with the budget, because the team needs to allocate the money carefully. After that, they could brainstorm ideas for the launch and review Maria's proposal. The client might join for the last ten minutes, so Walyson will be ready to recap the decisions. If the team reaches consensus, they could adjourn before lunch. Walyson will take the minutes and allocate each action item to one owner.

B Because it is only a possibility, not certain.
C Because the client never comes to meetings.

A When the client arrives.
C When Walyson takes the minutes.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- should / could / might

GRAMMAR

Usamos should, could e might para fazer sugestões e recomendações. / We use should, could and might to make suggestions and recommendations.

ModalUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
shouldadvice / recommendation (forte)We should start on time.
couldsuggestion / option (mais leve)We could postpone the launch.
mightpossibility (incerta)The client might join late.
Negativemodal + notWe should not be late.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de should, could e might vem o verbo base, sem to e sem -s. Diga "he should start", não "he should starts" nem "he should to start". Use should para conselho forte, could para uma opção mais leve e might para algo incerto.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You want to give strong advice to start now. You say:
A "We should start now and follow the agenda."
B "We should to start now."
C "We should starts now."
You want to suggest a possible option, not an order. You say:
A "We must postpone the launch."
B "We could postpone the launch by two weeks."
C "We could to postpone the launch."
You are not sure if the client will come. You say:
A "The client will definitely join."
B "The client might join for the last ten minutes."
C "The client might joins for ten minutes."
