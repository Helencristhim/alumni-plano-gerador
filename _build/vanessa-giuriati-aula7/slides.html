Chapter 3

The Engine

Present perfect: have / has + past participle

Grammar Discovery

Experience vs Exact Time

"I have traveled to many countries."

"Have you ever lived abroad?"

"I have never learned an instrument."

"I went to Paris in 2019." (exact time = past)

UseTenseExample
General experience (no exact time)present perfectI have visited Italy.
Specific finished timesimple pastI visited Italy in 2019.
Formhave / has + past participle: I have done, she has seen, we have been.
Key wordsever, never, already, yet, always, so far — signal present perfect.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have visited Italy in 2019."

✓ RIGHT

"I visited Italy in 2019."

✗ WRONG

"Did you ever eat sushi?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you ever eaten sushi?"

✗ WRONG

"I have went there."

✓ RIGHT

"I have been there."

With an exact time (in 2019, yesterday), use simple past. With "ever / never", use present perfect + past participle (not the simple past form).
