Experiences & Life So Far

Present perfect for life experiences. Key words: experience, achievement, opportunity, discover, improve, attempt, succeed, abroad. Grammar: present perfect (have/has + past participle), contrast with simple past, ever/never/already/yet.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Experience -- something you have done or lived through (experiência)
"Living abroad was a great experience."
Achievement -- something good you have accomplished (conquista/realização)
"Finishing the course was a big achievement."
Opportunity -- a good chance to do something (oportunidade)
"It was a great opportunity to grow."
Discover -- to find or learn something new (descobrir)
"I have discovered a new hobby."
Improve -- to get better at something (melhorar)
"My English has improved a lot."
Attempt -- to try to do something difficult (tentar)
"I have attempted to run a marathon."
Succeed -- to do well and reach your goal (ter sucesso/conseguir)
"She has succeeded in her new role."
Abroad -- in or to a foreign country (no exterior/no estrangeiro)
"I have never lived abroad."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Tatiana feels proud when she looks at her life so far. She has had many experiences, and she has learned a lot. She has lived in two cities and has worked in three companies. She has traveled abroad several times, and she has discovered that she loves new cultures. She has improved her English a lot this year. There are still things she has not done yet: she has never learned to drive, and she has always wanted to study art. Last year, she started a painting class — that part has a specific time, so it uses the simple past.

1. Why does the text use "she has traveled" and not "she traveled"?
A Because it is a general life experience with no exact time.
B Because "traveled" is wrong.
C Because it is in the future.
2. Why does "she started a painting class last year" use the simple past?
A Because there is a specific finished time (last year).
B Because painting is not important.
C Because "started" is always correct.
3. What has Tatiana never done?
A She has never traveled.
B She has never learned to drive.
C She has never worked.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Falar de experiências de vida. / Talking about life experiences.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
AffirmativeI have visited Italy. She has been there.Eu (já) visitei a Itália. Ela (já) esteve lá.
NegativeI have never lived abroad.Eu nunca morei no exterior.
QuestionHave you ever tried sushi?Você já experimentou sushi (alguma vez)?
vs Simple PastI visited Italy in 2019. (exact time)Visitei a Itália em 2019. (tempo exato)
Key wordsever, never, already, yet, so far → present perfect (general). Specific time (in 2019, yesterday) → simple past.
