Aula 2 -- Your Story, Your Credentials -- Introducing Yourself Powerfully

Series
The Good Doctor -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Burnt Food"

A young surgeon has to introduce himself and defend his credentials to a hospital board. Listen to how the doctors present their experience and achievements, and how they switch between the present perfect ("I have done...") and the past simple ("I trained at..."). Direct connection to Lesson 2: introducing yourself with authority at a professional event.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Note 3 sentences where a character describes experience with the present perfect.

Podcast
How I Built This (NPR) -- any founder's origin-story episode

Founders introduce themselves and tell the story of their venture: what they have built, what they have launched, and where they trained. You will hear the present perfect for experience and the past simple for finished events, exactly the contrast from this lesson. Direct connection to Lesson 2: telling your professional story and describing your venture.

Tip: listen twice. Write down 5 phrases the founder uses to describe experience and achievements.

YouTube
"Present Perfect vs Past Simple" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on when to use the present perfect (experience, no exact time) and when to switch to the past simple (a finished event with a finished time). Connection to Lesson 2: the exact grammar behind a powerful professional introduction.

Tip: pause and build your own sentences with "I have worked...", "I have presented...", and "I did my fellowship in 2016".
