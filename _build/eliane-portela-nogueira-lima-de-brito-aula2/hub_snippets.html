Your Story, Your Credentials -- Introducing Yourself Powerfully

Introducing yourself at executive and medical events: the present perfect for experience and achievements ("I have worked", "I have presented") contrasted with the past simple for finished events ("I did my fellowship in 2016", "I studied..."), plus "for" vs "since". GRAMMAR

Eliane is a gynecologist who specializes in reproductive medicine. Over the years, she has worked in assisted reproduction and she has built strong credentials. She did her fellowship in reproductive endocrinology in 2016, and she studied medicine in Teresina before that. As a reproductive endocrinologist, she has performed hundreds of IVF procedures, and she has led a small team in embryology. She has presented her pioneering work at three international congresses. Right now, she is in the process of launching her own clinic, and she is excited about a new partnership with a US supplier of cutting-edge technology. This clinic is her biggest venture yet.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Use the present perfect for experience and achievements with no exact date, and the past simple for a finished event with a finished time. / Use o present perfect para experiência e conquistas sem data exata, e o past simple para um evento finalizado com tempo definido.

FormUseExample
Present perfect (have/has + past participle)Experience and achievements, no exact timeI have worked in this field. / I have presented at conferences.
... for / sincefor + a period, since + a start pointfor eight years / since 2016
Past simpleA finished event with a finished timeI did my fellowship in 2016. / I studied in Teresina.
Common errorNOT "I have worked there in 2015" → with a finished time, use the past simple: "I worked there in 2015."
