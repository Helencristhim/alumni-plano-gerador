Chapter 4: Then vs Now

Experience or Event?

Present perfect and past simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in fertility care for eight years."

"I have presented at three international conferences."

"I did my fellowship in London in 2016."

"I studied medicine in Teresina."

TenseWhen to useExample
Present perfect (have + past participle)Experience and achievements, no exact time"I have presented at conferences."
... for / sincefor + a period, since + a start point"I have worked here for eight years / since 2016."
Past simpleA finished event with a finished time"I did my fellowship in 2016."

If there is a finished time (2016, last year, in Teresina), use the past simple. If it is general experience, use the present perfect.

"I ___studied medicine in Teresina." (finished place and time)

Language Focus

Experience or Event?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have worked there in 2015."

✓ RIGHT

"I worked there in 2015."

✗ WRONG

"I work in fertility care since 2016."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked in fertility care since 2016."

A finished time (in 2015) needs the past simple. For experience up to now with since or for, use the present perfect — never the present simple.

M

E
Yes, I have presented at three international congresses. I did my fellowship in reproductive endocrinology in 2016.
M
Wonderful. And what are you working on right now?
E
Right now, I am in the process of opening my own clinic with cutting-edge technology.
M
That is exciting. Let me introduce you to someone who runs a similar venture in Boston.

I can give a clear 60-second professional introduction and turn the question around.
