Lesson 11 -- Pre-class

What's Happening Right Now -- Describing Live Situations

Give a live status to the Global team: what your team is doing right now in Brazil -- the launch, the field and the results, in real time. Key words: to monitor, to adjust, to handle, situation, on the ground, in real time, status, to run. The grammar of the lesson is the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) for what is happening now, in contrast with the present simple for your routine: "We visit doctors every week" vs "This week, we are visiting doctors in São Paulo." This is a consolidation of the -ing form you already met in lessons 4 and 6. Signal words: right now, at the moment, this week.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are the eight words of a live update.

To monitor -- to watch and check something carefully over time
"We are monitoring the market access very closely."
To adjust -- to change something a little to make it better
"Access is slow, so we are adjusting the plan this week."
To handle -- to deal with a task or a problem
"My team is handling the launch in the South region."
Situation -- what is happening at a certain moment
"The situation on the ground is changing fast."
On the ground -- in the real place where the work is happening
"Our reps are on the ground, visiting doctors every day."
In real time -- happening now, with no delay
"I am giving you the numbers in real time."
Status -- the current state of something
"Let me give you a quick status of the launch."
To run -- to manage or operate something that is active
"The team is running three campaigns at the moment."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

David, from the Global team, calls Emmanuele for a quick status of the Brazilian launch. "Right now, my team is running the launch in three regions," she says. Normally, her reps visit doctors every week — that is the routine. But this week, they are visiting more than twenty doctors a day, and the team is seeing good growth in the South. There is one problem: in the Southeast, access is slow. "We are handling it, so we are adjusting the plan this week," she explains. David asks about the numbers. "We are monitoring everything in real time," she answers. "Our reps are on the ground every day, and I am tracking the results on one dashboard." David is happy: this is exactly the update the VP wants to hear.

1. "This week, they are visiting more than twenty doctors a day." Why the -ing form here?
A Because it is a plan for next year.
B Because it is happening NOW, this week. "This week" is the signal.
C Because "they" is plural.
2. "Normally, her reps visit doctors every week." Why "visit" and not "are visiting"?
A Because it is the routine — always true. "Every week" is the signal for present simple.
B Because it happened last week.
C Because "visit" is an irregular verb.
3. "My team is running the launch." Why "is" and not "are"?
A Because "team" is a past word.
B Because "team" is singular in American English: my team is.
C Because there is only one launch.
4. Which sentence shows Emmanuele is in control of the numbers, right now?
A "We visited the doctors last month."
B "We are monitoring everything in real time."
C "We are going to check the numbers next year."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Routine vs Right Now

GRAMMAR

A consolidation of the -ing form from lessons 4 and 6. Two times, one update: what you ALWAYS do, and what is happening NOW.

TimeUse it forExample
Present simple
(we visit)		Your routine and facts that are always true. Signals: every day, every week.We visit doctors every week.
Present continuous
(we are visiting)		What is happening NOW, this period. Signals: right now, at the moment, this week.This week, we are visiting doctors in São Paulo.
The formam / is / are + verb + -ing. The -ing never changes; only am/is/are changes.I am monitoring · the team is running · we are adjusting

Careful: "team" is singular in American English — "my team is running", not "my team are running". And with "right now" or "at the moment", always use the -ing form, never the simple.
