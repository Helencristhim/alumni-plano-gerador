Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

WISH + past simple / past perfect -- expressing regret

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What tense follows the orange word?

"I wish I knew who my counterpart is."

"I wish I had aligned with the director sooner."

"I wish I had more bandwidth this quarter."

"I wish they would respect the chain of command."

Grammar Rule

The WISH Structure

StructureMeaning
wish + past simple (I wish I knew)Regret about a PRESENT situation -- it is not true now
wish + past perfect (I wish I had known)Regret about a PAST situation -- it did not happen then
wish + would (I wish they would reply)Annoyance with someone's behavior -- you want them to change

Key idea: after WISH we move one tense back. Present regret uses the past; past regret uses the past perfect (had + past participle). We never use a present tense after WISH.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I wish I would have known about the reorganization."
"I wish I had known about the reorganization."

For a past regret, use had + past participle, never "would have." For a present regret, use the simple past: "I wish I knew," not "I wish I know."
