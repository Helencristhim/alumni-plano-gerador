Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Hierarchy -- the levels of authority in an organization
"Understanding the hierarchy tells you who decides what." (Hierarquia)
Counterpart -- a person with the same role in another team
"Klaus is my counterpart on the supplier side." (Contraparte)
Align -- to bring people or plans into agreement
"We need to align with the director before we act." (Alinhar)
Bandwidth -- the time and energy you have for more work
"I don't have the bandwidth to lead another project." (Disponibilidade)
Chain of command -- the order of authority decisions flow through
"Follow the chain of command before you escalate." (Cadeia de comando)
Peer -- a colleague at the same level as you
"I discussed the issue with a peer in logistics." (Colega de mesmo nível)
Subordinate -- a person who works under your authority
"A good manager supports every subordinate." (Subordinado)
Delegate -- to give a task or responsibility to someone
"I will delegate the report to my analyst." (Delegar)
Oversight -- responsibility for supervising work
"The director has oversight of all APAC accounts." (Supervisão)
Rapport -- a relationship of trust and easy communication
"She built strong rapport with the whole team." (Sintonia)
Liaison -- a person who connects two teams
"Maria is our liaison with their logistics team." (Elo de ligação)
Mandate -- the official authority to decide or act
"He has no mandate to approve large discounts." (Mandato)
Buy-in -- agreement and support from the people involved
"We need buy-in from senior management first." (Adesão)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- WISH

GRAMMAR

Usado para expressar arrependimento ou desejo de mudança. / Used to express regret or a desire for change.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
wish + past simpleArrependimento sobre o presente / Regret about nowI wish I knew who my counterpart is.
wish + past perfectArrependimento sobre o passado / Regret about the pastI wish I had aligned with the director sooner.
wish + wouldIncomodo com o comportamento de alguém / Annoyance with behaviorI wish they would respect the chain of command.
Key point / Ponto-chaveApós WISH, recue um tempo verbal. Nunca use "I wish I know" nem "I wish I would have known".
