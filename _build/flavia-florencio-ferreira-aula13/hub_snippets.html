In My Opinion

Express and defend your opinion in meetings and international panels, and agree or disagree politely. Key words: opinion, perspective, argument, evidence, position, counterpart, point, agree, disagree, assert. Structures: In my opinion..., I think that..., I agree, I see your point but..., I am not sure I agree.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Opinion -- an idea or feeling you have about something
"In my opinion, this product has a strong future."
Perspective -- a particular way of seeing a situation
"From my perspective, the local market is not enough."
Argument -- the reasons you give to support your idea
"That is a good argument, but I see it differently."
Evidence -- facts that show that something is true
"We have clear evidence that exports are growing."
Position -- the opinion a person holds on a topic
"My position is that we need more training."
Counterpart -- a person with the same job in another company
"I discussed the plan with my counterpart in Portugal."
Point -- the main idea in what someone says
"I see your point, but I am not sure I agree."
To agree -- to have the same opinion as someone
"I agree with you on this point."
To disagree -- to have a different opinion from someone
"I disagree, and here is why."
To assert -- to say your idea firmly and with confidence
"She asserts her ideas with confidence."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is at an international panel at Sebrae. A counterpart from Portugal shares his opinion. Flavia listens and then gives her position. "In my opinion, small businesses need more support," she says. "From my perspective, they are the heart of the region." The counterpart answers, "I see your point, but I am not sure I agree." Flavia stays calm. "I understand your argument. I agree that exports are important, but I think that we also need local partners." She uses clear evidence and speaks with confidence. To agree or to disagree, she is always polite and professional.

1. How does Flavia start when she gives her idea?
A "In my opinion, ..."
B "I opinion that ..."
C "Opinion is ..."
2. What does the counterpart say to disagree politely?
A "You are wrong."
B "I see your point, but I am not sure I agree."
C "That is a bad idea."
3. What comes after "I think that"?
A A full idea: a subject and a verb.
B The word "to" and a verb.
C A verb with -ing.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Giving Opinions and Agreeing / Disagreeing

GRAMMAR

How to give your opinion and react to other people in a polite, professional way.

StructureUseExample
In my opinion, + ideato give your ideaIn my opinion, we need more evidence.
I think that + ideato give your idea softlyI think that exports are important.
I agree (with you).to say you have the same ideaI agree with your point.
I am not sure I agree.to disagree in a soft wayI am not sure I agree with that.
I see your point, but ...to disagree politely: "I see your point, but I think differently." After I think that always use a full idea (a subject and a verb) — never "I think that to be".
