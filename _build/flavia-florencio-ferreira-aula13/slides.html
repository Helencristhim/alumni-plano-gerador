Chapter 3: In My Opinion

The Language of Opinions

give · agree · disagree

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"In my opinion, we need more evidence."

"I think that exports are important."

"I agree with your point."

"I see your point, but I think differently."

Each orange phrase opens an idea or a reaction. Which ones give an opinion? Which one disagrees — politely?

StructureUseExample
In my opinion, ...give your ideaIn my opinion, we need evidence.
I think that + ideagive your idea softlyI think that exports matter.
I agree (with you).same opinionI agree with your point.
I see your point, but ...disagree politelyI see your point, but...

After I think that, use a full idea (subject + verb). Agree is a verb: say "I agree", never "I am agree".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am agree with you."
"I agree with you."
"I agree of you."
"I agree with you."
"In my opinion is important."
"In my opinion, it is important."

Agree is a verb (no "am"), and it goes with with. After In my opinion, use a full idea.

Detail

Say It Right

agree with · I think that + a full idea

"I agree ___with you."
"I think that we ___need more evidence."
"In my opinion, it ___is important."
"I see your point, ___but I disagree."
Chapter 4: The Panel Discussion

What Do You Think?

The discussion begins

Dialogue

At the Panel

D
Good afternoon, Flavia. In my opinion, small businesses should focus only on the local market.
F
Thank you, David. I see your point, but I am not sure I agree. From my perspective, they can also export.
D
That is an interesting argument. What is your evidence?
F
Many small companies in Sao Paulo already sell to other countries. I have strong evidence for this.
D
I agree that exports are growing. But my position is that training comes first.
F
I agree with that point. I think that training and exports are both important.
Comprehension

About David

1. What is David's first opinion?
In his opinion, small businesses should focus only on the local market.
2. What does David ask Flavia for?
He asks for her evidence.
3. What is David's position?
His position is that training comes first.
