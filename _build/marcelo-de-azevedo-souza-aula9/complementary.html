Aula 9 -- Listening Like a Native

Series
Derry Girls (Channel 4 / Netflix) -- Season 1, Episode 1

Five teenagers in 1990s Northern Ireland, speaking fast, warm, thickly-accented Irish English. It is the perfect ear-training challenge for this lesson: at full speed you will not catch every word, and that is exactly the point -- follow the meaning, and let the accent stop being a wall. Connection to Lesson 9: listen for connected speech and for the moments you would want to say "sorry, could you say that again?"

Tip: watch one scene twice -- first with English subtitles, then without. Write down two lines you only caught the second time.

Podcast
The Allusionist -- with Helen Zaltzman -- any episode

A smart, funny podcast about the English language itself -- where words come from, how they change, and how people really speak. The host talks at a natural, quick British pace, so it is ideal listening practice, and the topic keeps your ear curious. A gentle way to spend twenty minutes training the very skill from tonight.

Tip: listen once without the transcript. When you lose the thread, note the timestamp, then open the transcript on the site and see exactly which words got blended.

YouTube
The Secret to Understand Fast-Talking Native English Speakers (mmmEnglish)

A clear, practical lesson on exactly why native speakers are so hard to follow -- the reductions and blends (gonna, wanna, gotta) that turn simple words into a wall of sound. It is tonight's "code" in video form, with dozens of real examples you can train your ear on.

Tip: pause after each example and say the slow, written version out loud, so you connect the fast sound to the real words.
