Chapter 3: The Code

Connected Speech & Clarifying

How words blend · and how to ask when you miss them

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We're gonna meet at eight." (going to)

"You'll wanna bring boots." (want to)

"We hafta be back before dark." (have to)

"Didja catch that?" (did you)

The orange words are not new words — they are normal words, blended at speaking speed.

You readYou hearWhen you miss it, say
going togonnaSorry, could you say that again?
want towannaSorry, I didn't catch that.
have tohaftaDo you mean ... ?
did youdidjaSo what you're saying is ... ?

Your job is not to speak this way — it is to recognize it, and to ask when a word slips past you.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Yes, yes." (when you did not understand)
"Sorry, I didn't catch that. Could you say it again?"
"What? What?"
"Sorry, could you say that a bit more slowly?"

Pretending to understand breaks the conversation later. A calm "could you say that again?" is what confident listeners do.

Practice

Turn Confusion into a Question

Say the clarifying line first, then click to check

You missed the whole sentence. Say: "___Sorry, could you say that again?"
You missed one word. Say: "___Sorry, I didn't catch that."
It was too fast. Say: "___Could you say that a bit more slowly?"
You want to confirm the meaning. Say: "___So what you're saying is we meet at the harbor?"
Your Listening Decoder

Fast Sound → Real Words

"Whatcha doin'?"  =  What are you doing?
"Lemme check."  =  Let me check.
"Kinda cold."  =  It is kind of cold.
"I dunno."  =  I do not know.
Chapter 4: On the Glacier

The Fast-Talking Guide

Keeping up with Saoirse in Iceland

Dialogue

Keeping Up

S
Right, so we're gonna head up the east side, it's a wee bit steep but the view's unreal, and we hafta be back by three, okay?
M
Sorry, could you say that again? I didn't quite catch the last part.
S
Course, no bother. We go up the east side, and we have to be back down by three. I talk fast, so cut in any time.
M
Thank you. So what you're saying is, we go up, then back by three. Do you mean three o'clock sharp?
S
Exactly, three sharp, before the light goes. See, you're following me grand. Most folk just nod and get lost halfway up.
M
That's my new trick. If I lose the thread, I just ask, instead of pretending. It works much better.
