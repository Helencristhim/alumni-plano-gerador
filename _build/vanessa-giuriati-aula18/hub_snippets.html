Hypothetical Situations & Giving Advice

Imagine a situation and give advice gently. Use the second conditional (if + past simple, would + verb): "If I were you, I'd...", "If I had more time, I would...". Remember "were" for all subjects. Frame it as a suggestion, not an order. Key words: imagine, suppose, recommend, otherwise, mistake, regret, wish, situation.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Imagine -- to form a picture or idea of something in your mind (imaginar)
"Imagine you are sixty — what would you regret?"
Suppose -- to imagine that something is true, as a possibility (supor/imaginar)
"Suppose you tried that — what would happen?"
Recommend -- to suggest that something is a good idea (recommend + -ing) (recomendar)
"I'd recommend talking to the client first."
Situation -- the conditions and circumstances around you at a time (situação)
"If I were in your situation, I'd wait a week."
Otherwise -- if not; or else (the result if you don't do it) (caso contrário/senão)
"Make a plan; otherwise, you'll regret rushing."
Mistake -- something done wrong; an error (make a mistake) (erro/engano)
"It would be a mistake to rush this."
Regret -- to feel sorry or sad about a past choice (regret + -ing) (arrepender-se/lamentar)
"I would regret staying in that job."
Wish -- to want something unlikely, or to be sorry about the past (desejar/lamentar)
"I wish someone had told me that earlier."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A friend asked me for advice about a job offer. I didn't want to give an order, so I imagined myself in his situation. "If I were you," I said, "I would write down the pros and cons first." Then I asked him to suppose he had already taken the job: would he feel excited or anxious? "I'd recommend taking the chance," I added. "Otherwise, you might regret not trying, and it would be a mistake to let fear decide." But I made it clear it was his call. Honestly, I wish someone had pushed me like that years ago. If I had heard that advice earlier, I would have changed careers much sooner.

1. Why did she imagine herself in his situation?
A To tell him exactly what to do.
B To give gentle advice instead of an order.
C To avoid answering him.
2. What was her main advice and reason?
A To refuse the job because it was risky.
B To take the chance, otherwise he might regret not trying.
C To wait forever for the perfect moment.
3. Which is the correct second conditional?
A "If I was you, I will help."
B "If I were you, I would help."
C "If I would be you, I help."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como imaginar uma situação e dar conselho. / How to imagine a situation and give advice.

PartEnglishPortuguês
if + past simple"If I had more time, ..." / "If I were you, ..."A condição (hipotética)
would + verbo"... I would test it again." / "... I'd wait."O resultado imaginado
were para todos"If I were you", "If he were here" (não "was").Use "were" com todos os sujeitos
OrdemPode inverter: "I'd wait if I were you" = "If I were you, I'd wait" (vírgula quando o "if" vem primeiro).
⚠ CarefulDepois de "if", use o past simple (had, knew, were), nunca "would". O resultado leva would + verbo. Não use "if I was you" → "if I were you".

Stage 2: Put the Advice in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de um bom conselho na ordem correta.

?After that, I give the reason and say, because otherwise the deadline would be at risk.
?In the end, I reassure them and say, whatever you decide, I am sure it will be the right call.
?First, I listen carefully and imagine the problem from the other person's side.
?Next, I soften it and make it their choice and say, but what do you think?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

A friend can't decide about a job. You want to advise gently. Best choice:
A "Just quit, do it now!"
B "If I were you, I'd write down the pros and cons first."
C "If I was you, I will help."
You want to imagine a possible outcome. Best choice:
A "Suppose you tried it — what would happen?"
B "Suppose you will try it — what happens?"
C "Suppose you trying it — what would happen?"
You want to recommend an idea correctly. Best choice:
A "I'd recommend waiting a week before deciding."
B "I'd recommend to wait a week before deciding."
C "I'd recommend wait a week before deciding."
You want to warn about a risk and keep it their choice. Best choice:
A "Otherwise, it would be a mistake to rush. But it's your call."
B "You must do exactly what I say."
C "If you would rush, you make a mistake."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Think of a real decision someone close to you is facing — a job, a move, a big choice. Give them advice out loud: imagine yourself in their situation, use the second conditional ("If I were you, I would..."), add a reason with otherwise, imagine the outcome with suppose, and end by leaving the choice to them. Use at least three of today's words (imagine, suppose, recommend, otherwise, mistake, regret, wish, situation) and speak slowly and calmly.
