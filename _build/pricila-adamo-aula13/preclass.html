Stage 1.1: Story Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you tell a travel story in the past.

An anecdote -- uma anedota / um causo
"She told a funny anecdote about getting lost in Rome."
Vivid -- vivido / marcante
"I still have a vivid memory of that evening."
To recall -- lembrar / recordar
"I can still recall the smell of the market."
Eventful -- cheio de acontecimentos
"It was the most eventful day of the whole trip."
Unexpected -- inesperado
"Then something completely unexpected happened."
A turning point -- um momento decisivo
"That trip was a real turning point for me."
To set off -- partir / sair de viagem
"We set off early, before sunrise."
To run into -- encontrar por acaso
"I ran into an old friend at the airport."
To get lost -- se perder
"We got lost in the old town for an hour."
A mishap -- um contratempo / percalco
"Despite a few mishaps, the trip was wonderful."
To end up -- acabar (em / fazendo)
"We ended up in a tiny village we had never planned to visit."
Eventually -- por fim / finalmente
"Eventually, the bus arrived and we got home."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

Pricila tells an anecdote from a recent trip: "We set off early, before sunrise. While we were driving up the mountain, the engine suddenly stopped. It was completely unexpected. We got lost on a tiny road, and eventually we ended up in a village we had never planned to visit. When I was younger, I used to hate moments like that. Back then, I would panic. But that day was a turning point: I still recall it more vividly than the rest of the holiday."

A She used to love them.
B She used to hate them and would panic.
C She never travelled at all.
3. Why does she call that day a turning point?
A Because nothing happened.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous & Past Habits

Grammar

Para contar uma historia, use o past continuous (was/were + verbo-ing) para a acao LONGA de fundo, interrompida pelo past simple depois de "when": "I was walking when it started to rain". Para habitos do passado que nao acontecem mais, use used to + verbo base ("I used to travel fast") ou would + verbo base para acoes repetidas ("Every summer we would drive to the beach"). / Use the past continuous for the long background action, interrupted by the past simple after "when". Use "used to" + base verb for past states/habits, and "would" + base verb for repeated past actions.

Funcao / FunctionEnglishPortugues
Scene + interruptionI was walking when it started to rain.Eu estava andando quando comecou a chover.
Two background actionsShe was talking while the children listened.Ela falava enquanto as criancas escutavam.
Past state / habitI used to travel very fast.Eu costumava viajar muito rapido.
Repeated past actionEvery summer we would drive to the beach.Todo verao a gente dirigia ate a praia.
Negative habitI didn't use to enjoy mishaps.Eu nao costumava gostar de contratempos.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You want to set the scene of your story: you were reading when something happened. What do you say?
A "I read when the phone ring."
B "I was reading when the phone rang."
C "I am reading when the phone rang."
You want to talk about a habit you had as a child but not anymore. What do you say?
A "As a child, I used to collect postcards from every trip."
B "As a child, I use to collect postcards."
C "As a child, I am collecting postcards."
You want to end your anecdote with a happy resolution. What do you say?
A "And nothing happened, the end."
B "We got lost, but eventually we ended up in the most beautiful village."
C "We are getting lost in the village now."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Tell a short, true travel anecdote from your own life. Set the scene with the past continuous ("I was..."), then describe an unexpected moment ("...when..."), and end with how it turned out. Use at least three new words. Take your time.
