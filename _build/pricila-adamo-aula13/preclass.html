Stage 1.1: Words for Telling Stories

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for telling a memorable travel story.

An anecdote -- uma historinha pessoal
"She told a funny anecdote about getting lost in Rome."
Vivid -- nitido e forte na memoria
"I still have a vivid memory of that evening."
To recall -- relembrar / trazer a memoria
"I can still recall the smell of the market."
Eventful -- cheio de acontecimentos
"It was the most eventful day of the whole trip."
Unexpected -- inesperado, sem planejar
"Then something completely unexpected happened."
A turning point -- um momento que muda tudo
"That trip was a real turning point for me."
To set off -- partir / comecar a viagem
"We set off early, before sunrise."
To run into -- encontrar alguem por acaso
"I ran into an old friend at the airport."
To get lost -- se perder no caminho
"We got lost in the old town for an hour."
A mishap -- um contratempo / pequeno acidente
"Despite a few mishaps, the trip was wonderful."
To end up -- acabar em um lugar ou situacao
"We ended up in a tiny village we had never planned to visit."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Narrative (Continuous vs Simple, used to)

Grammar

Use o past continuous (was/were + -ing) para o FUNDO da cena e o past simple para o EVENTO que interrompe. Para um habito do passado, use used to + verbo base. / Use the past continuous (was/were + -ing) for the BACKGROUND of the scene and the past simple for the EVENT that interrupts. For a past habit, use used to + base verb.

Uso / UseEnglishPortugues
BackgroundWhile we were wandering, we got lost.Enquanto andavamos, nos perdemos.
Interrupting eventI ran into an old friend.Encontrei um velho amigo por acaso.
Past habit (used to + base)We used to travel light.Costumavamos viajar leve.
SequenceWe set off, got lost, and ended up in a village.Partimos, nos perdemos e acabamos numa vila.
Common mistakeNOT "used to travelling." Say "used to travel" (base verb).used to + verbo base.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

You want to set the background of a story. What do you say?
A "I was walking through the market when it suddenly started to rain."
B "I walk through the market when it rains."
C "I have walked through the market."
You want to talk about a past habit. What do you say?
A "We use to setting off before sunrise."
B "We used to set off before sunrise on every trip."
C "We are used to set off before sunrise."
You want to describe a surprising twist. What do you say?
A "Nothing happened, as we planned."
B "Then something unexpected happened and we ended up in a tiny village."
C "We used to ending up in villages."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Tell a short travel story in 5-6 sentences. Use the past continuous for the background (I was ...ing when...), the past simple for the events, and "used to" for a past habit. Include at least three words from this lesson (anecdote, eventful, run into, get lost, mishap, end up). Take your time.
