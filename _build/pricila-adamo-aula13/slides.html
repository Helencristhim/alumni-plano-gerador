Chapter 3: Setting the Scene

The Grammar of Stories

Past continuous · used to · would

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I was walking along the beach when I met him."

"While we were driving, the engine stopped."

"When I was younger, I used to travel alone."

"Every summer, we would drive to the beach."

UseFormExample
Scene + interruptionwas/were + -ing ... when + past simpleI was walking when it rained.
Two background actionswhile + was/were + -ingWhile we were driving...
Past state / habitused to + base verbI used to travel alone.
Repeated past actionwould + base verbWe would drive to the beach.
Negative habitdidn't use to + base verbI didn't use to like mishaps.

Use was/were + -ing for the long background action, interrupted by the past simple after "when". Use used to for past states and would for repeated past actions.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I walked when the lights went out."

✓ RIGHT

"I was walking when the lights went out."

✗ WRONG

"When I was a child, I would be shy."

✓ RIGHT

"When I was a child, I used to be shy."

Use used to (not would) for past states like be, have, like. Use would only for repeated past actions.

Chapter 4: A Story Worth Telling

Let's Hear a Real Story

A traveler shares an anecdote

Dialogue

A Traveler's Anecdote

T
Pricila, have I ever told you about the most eventful day of my last trip? We set off early for a quiet village.
P
No, tell me! What happened? I love a good anecdote.
T
Well, while we were driving up the mountain, the engine died, completely unexpected. Then we got lost on a tiny road.
P
Oh no, a real mishap! So what did you do then?
T
A farmer was passing by, and we ended up in his kitchen drinking coffee. Eventually that became the best part of the trip.
P
That's a real turning point! I still recall mine just as vividly.
