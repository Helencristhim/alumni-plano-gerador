Stage 1.1: Plan Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you invite people and arrange to meet.

To invite -- convidar
"I'd like to invite you to dinner tonight."
A plan -- um plano / programa
"Do you have any plans for the weekend?"
Available -- disponivel / livre
"Are you available on Saturday morning?"
To join -- juntar-se / participar
"Come and join us for a walk along the harbor."
A suggestion -- uma sugestao
"Can I make a suggestion? Let's meet a bit later."
To meet up -- encontrar-se
"Let's meet up at the lobby at ten."
To work for -- servir / ser bom para
"Does Friday work for you, or is Saturday better?"
Sounds good -- parece otimo
"Lunch by the water? Sounds good to me!"
To turn down -- recusar (educadamente)
"I had to turn down the trip because I was tired."
To reschedule -- remarcar
"Can we reschedule the tour for the afternoon?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the conversation and answer the questions.

Mark sends Pricila a message: "Would you like to join us tomorrow? We are going to the old harbor." Pricila is happy: "I'd love to! What time shall we meet up?" Mark replies, "How about meeting at the lobby at ten? Does that work for you?" Pricila thinks for a moment. "Could we reschedule for half past ten? I'd like a slow breakfast." Mark agrees: "Of course. Why don't we have lunch by the water after the walk?" "Sounds good -- count me in!" Pricila says.

1. How does Mark invite Pricila?
A He says "Would you like to join us tomorrow?"
B He says "You must come with us."
C He says nothing at all.
2. Why does Pricila ask to reschedule?
A She does not want to go.
B She would like a slow breakfast first.
C She is not available at all.
3. How does Pricila accept the plan in the end?
A "I'm afraid I can't make it."
B "Maybe, I don't know."
C "Sounds good -- count me in!"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Inviting & Suggesting

Grammar

Para convidar em ingles, use "Would you like to" + verbo na forma base (to join, to come). Para sugerir, use "How about" + verbo com -ing (meeting, having) ou "Why don't we" + verbo na forma base (walk, visit). Para aceitar, diga "Sounds good" ou "I'd love to"; para recusar com gentileza, use "I'm afraid I can't make it". / To invite, use "Would you like to" + base verb. To suggest, use "How about" + verb-ing or "Why don't we" + base verb. Accept with "Sounds good"; decline softly with "I'm afraid I can't make it".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

You want to invite a new friend to a city tour. What do you say?
A "You come to the tour."
B "Would you like to join us for a city tour?"
C "Tour now, yes?"
A friend suggests meeting at eight, but that is too early for you. What do you say?
A "Could we reschedule for nine? Does that work for you?"
B "No. Eight is bad."
C "I don't want eight."
You are tired and must say no to a plan, but kindly. What do you say?
A "No, I don't want."
B "I'm afraid I can't make it today, but thank you for the invitation."
C "Go without me, bye."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Imagine you are on a trip and you want to invite a new friend for a full day out. Invite them, suggest a time and a place to meet up, and ask if it works for them. Use at least two invitation or suggestion phrases. Take your time.
