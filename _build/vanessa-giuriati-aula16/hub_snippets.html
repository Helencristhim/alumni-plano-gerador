Aula 16 -- Pre-class

Handling Uncertainty & Buying Time

Stay calm when you don't know the answer. Buy yourself time with fillers (let me think for a second), admit uncertainty with confidence, and give an honest estimate. Then keep the talk soft with question tags (..., isn't it? / ..., can't we?). Key words: unsure, guess, estimate, roughly, approximately, figure out, vague, frankly.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Unsure -- not certain about something; not knowing for sure (inseguro/sem certeza)
"I'm unsure about the exact date."
Guess -- to give an answer you think is right, without being sure (adivinhar/dar um palpite)
"I'd guess it's around twenty."
Estimate -- to give an approximate number, not the exact one (estimar)
"I'd estimate we're up about ten percent."
Roughly -- approximately; about, more or less (not exact) (aproximadamente/mais ou menos)
"It's roughly forty thousand."
Approximately -- close to an amount, but not exactly (a formal "roughly") (aproximadamente)
"There were approximately fifty people."
Figure out -- to find or work out an answer after thinking about it (descobrir/resolver)
"Let me figure out the total and get back to you."
Vague -- not clear or exact; without precise detail (the 'g' is hard, the 'ue' is silent) (vago)
"I only have a vague idea of the date."
Frankly -- honestly and openly, saying what you really think (francamente)
"Frankly, I'm not sure, but I can find out."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

In the meeting, my manager asked for the exact numbers. A year ago I would have frozen, but this time I stayed calm. "That's a fair question," I said. "Let me think for a second." Then I gave an honest answer. "Frankly, I'm unsure of the precise figure, but roughly it's around forty thousand. I'd estimate we're up approximately ten percent." I only had a vague picture, so I added a tag to check: "We can confirm the details later, can't we?" My manager nodded. "You'll send the exact numbers, won't you?" he asked. "Of course," I said. "Let me figure out the total and get back to you."

1. What did she say to buy herself time?
A She said nothing and waited in silence.
B "That's a fair question. Let me think for a second."
C "I have no idea, sorry."
2. How did she answer without the exact figure?
A She invented a precise number on the spot.
B She admitted she was unsure and gave a rough estimate.
C She refused to answer at all.
3. Which tag is correct for "We can confirm the details later, ___?"
A "...can we?"
B "...can't we?"
C "...do we?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Question Tags

GRAMMAR

Como formar uma question tag (a mini-pergunta no fim da frase). / How to form a question tag.

RuleEnglishPortuguês
Positive → negative tag"It's useful, isn't it?"Frase positiva, tag negativo
Negative → positive tag"You don't have it, do you?"Frase negativa, tag positivo
Repeat the auxiliary + pronounis/are → isn't/aren't; do/does; can → can't; have → haven't.Repita o auxiliar + o pronome (she/he/it/they)
Why use itA tag invites the other person to agree and keeps the talk soft and friendly. "We can confirm later, can't we?"
⚠ CarefulMirror it (positive ↔ negative). Don't say "...no?" Use the auxiliary: "You know him, don't you?" (not "...no?"). Use the pronoun, not the name: "Maria is here, isn't she?"

Order
