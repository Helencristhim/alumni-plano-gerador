Chapter 3

Question Tags

A little tag that invites the other person in

Grammar Discovery

The Mirror Tag

"It's useful, isn't it?"

"You can confirm it later, can't you?"

"You don't have the figure, do you?"

"We've met before, haven't we?"

SentenceTagWhy
Positive: "It is useful,"isn't it?Positive sentence → negative tag
Negative: "You don't have it,"do you?Negative sentence → positive tag
The mirror ruleRepeat the same auxiliary (is/are, do/does, can, have) + the pronoun, and flip it: positive ↔ negative. You're inviting the other person to agree — it keeps the talk soft and friendly.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"It's useful, is it?"

✓ RIGHT

"It's useful, isn't it?"

✗ WRONG

"You know him, no?"

✓ RIGHT

"You know him, don't you?"

✗ WRONG

"Maria is here, isn't Maria?"

✓ RIGHT

"Maria is here, isn't she?"

Mirror it: a positive sentence takes a negative tag (and the reverse). Repeat the right auxiliary (is/do/can/have), and use the pronoun (she/he/it/they), never the name again.

Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What did she say to buy herself time?
"That's a fair question. Let me think for a second." She stayed calm instead of freezing.
2. How did she answer without the exact figure?
She admitted she was unsure, then gave a roughly estimate: "roughly it's around forty thousand," and "I'd estimate we're up about ten percent."
3. What did she promise at the end?
To figure out the precise total and get back to him with the exact numbers that afternoon.
