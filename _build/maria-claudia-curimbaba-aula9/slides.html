Chapter 3 -- The Opinion Toolkit

Language Discovery

Giving your opinion, beyond "I think"

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the highlighted phrases all do? Are they all equally strong?

"From my perspective, the new collection is his strongest yet."

"I'd argue that the curator took a real risk here."

"I'm inclined to think the market will reward it."

"What's your take on the large installation?"

The Rule

The Opinion Toolkit

StrengthPhrasesExample
Strong / confidentI'd argue that... / I have to say..."I'd argue that it is his best work."
Neutral / professionalFrom my perspective... / In my view..."From my perspective, it is striking."
Tentative / hedgedI'm inclined to think... / It seems to me... / I suppose..."I'm inclined to think it will sell."
Invite the other viewWhat's your take on...? / How do you find...?"What's your take on the show?"

Key idea: a B2 speaker varies their opinion language instead of repeating "I think". Choose the strength that fits: assertive when you are confident, hedged when you want to sound diplomatic or uncertain. And keep a conversation alive by inviting the other person's view with "What's your take on...?".

Diplomatic Register

Soften It with Hedging

Hedging makes an opinion sound polite, open, and less absolute. Useful when you disagree or you are unsure.

"It seems to me that the early pieces are stronger."

"I suppose it depends on what you collect."

"I see your point, but I'm inclined to think it's overpriced."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"According to me, the exhibit is brilliant."
"From my perspective, the exhibit is brilliant."

Never say "according to me". Use "according to" only for other sources ("according to the curator"). For your own view, say "From my perspective / In my opinion / I'd argue that". And it is "I agree", never "I am agree".

Practice

Build the Opinion

"_____From my perspective, the show is a real success." (neutral, professional)
"I'd _____argue that the curator took a brave risk." (strong, confident)
"I'm _____inclined to think the market will reward it." (tentative, hedged)
"What's your _____take on the large installation?" (invite the other view)
