Chapter 3: Should & Had Better

Give Some Advice

should + verb · should not · had better

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

You should ask for a refund. (should + verb)

You should not throw away the receipt. (should not + verb)

You had better call the staff now. (strong advice)

Should I make a complaint? (question)

FormUseExample
should + verbnormal adviceYou should ask for help.
should not + verbadvice NOT to do somethingYou should not wait.
had better + verbstrong advice / warningYou had better call now.

After should and had better, use the verb with NO "to": "should ask", "had better call".

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"You should to ask for a refund."

✓ RIGHT

"You should ask for a refund."

✗ WRONG

"She shoulds call the staff."

✓ RIGHT

"She should call the staff."

Chapter 4: Sorting It Out

Tânia Asks for Help

A problem at the hotel

Dialogue

At the Front Desk

F
Good evening, Tânia. You look worried. Is there a problem?
T
Yes, I am sorry to complain. The air conditioning in my room is broken, and the hot water is missing.
F
I am very sorry. That is our mistake. You should make an official complaint, and we will fix it tonight.
T
Thank you. Should I wait, or had I better change my room now?
F
You had better change rooms now. The staff will replace your room and give you a small refund.
T
That is perfect. Thank you for sorting this out so quickly.
Listening

A Phone Complaint

Listen first. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What two things are wrong in the room?
The air conditioning is broken and the hot water is missing.
2. What does the guest ask the staff to do?
To fix it today, or to replace the room.
3. How does the guest end the call?
By asking the staff to sort it out as soon as possible.
Comprehension

About the Dialogue

1. What did the staff member say about the problem?
That it was their mistake and they would fix it tonight.
2. What strong advice did he give Tânia?
She had better change rooms now.
3. What will the staff give Tânia?
A new room and a small refund.

The Delayed Flight

0:000:00
