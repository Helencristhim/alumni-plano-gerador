Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Comparatives and superlatives for data: higher than, the most

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"Group A is higher than group B."

"The increase was faster than we expected."

"Month four shows the highest value."

"This group had the most significant response."

Grammar Rule

Comparatives & Superlatives

FunctionExample
Compare two (short adj.)Group A is higher than group B.
Compare two (long adj.)It was more significant than the control.
The top of all (short)Month four shows the highest value.
The top of all (long)This group had the most significant response.
Describe the movementThe score rose sharply / fell slightly.

Key idea: short adjectives take -er than and the -est; long adjectives take more ... than and the most .... Add adverbs like sharply, steadily and slightly to describe how fast the data moves.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Group A is more higher than group B."
"Group A is higher than group B."

Use either -er than or more ... than, never both together.

Grammar Practice

Compare the Data

"The treatment group was _____higher than the control group." (compare two, short)
"Month four shows _____the highest value of all." (top of all, short)
"This result was _____more significant than the previous one." (compare two, long)
"The score rose _____sharply in the first quarter." (how it moved)
