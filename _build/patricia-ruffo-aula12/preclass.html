Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Trend -- the general direction in which data moves over time
"The overall trend is clearly positive." (Tendência)
Increase -- a rise; when a value goes up
"We saw a steady increase in the first quarter." (Aumento)
Decline -- a fall; when a value goes down over time
"The control group showed a slow decline." (Queda / declínio)
Peak -- the highest point that a value reaches
"The marker reached a clear peak in month four." (Pico)
Plateau -- a period when a value stays almost flat after rising
"After the peak, the values reached a plateau." (Estabilização / platô)
Fluctuate -- to go up and down in an irregular way
"The figures fluctuate a little from week to week." (Oscilar)
Proportion -- a part of a whole, often shown as a percentage
"The proportion of responders rose to seventy percent." (Proporção)
Axis -- a reference line on a chart (horizontal or vertical)
"The vertical axis shows the average score." (Eixo)

At the team meeting, Patricia presents the results of a six-month study. She first explains the chart: the horizontal axis shows the months, and the vertical axis shows the average score. After that, the values did not fall; they reached a stable plateau. The numbers fluctuate a little from week to week, which is normal. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & trend language

GRAMMAR

Para descrever dados, comparamos valores e descrevemos o movimento da linha. / To describe data, we compare values and describe how the line moves.

Function / FunçãoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Compare two (short)adj + -er + than"Group A is higher than group B."
Compare two (long)more + adj + than"It was more significant than the control."
Top of allthe + -est / the most + adj"Month four shows the highest value."
Describe the movementverb + adverb (sharply, slightly)"The score rose sharply / fell slightly."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Adjetivos curtos usam -er than e the -est; adjetivos longos usam more ... than e the most .... Nunca combine os dois (errado: more higher). Use advérbios como sharply, steadily e slightly para dizer o quanto o dado se moveu.
