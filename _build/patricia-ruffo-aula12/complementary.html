Lesson 12 -- Describing Data, Trends & Charts

Documentary
Explained (Netflix) -- any data-driven episode

Watch how presenters describe trends, increases, declines and peaks on real charts, and how they compare groups with simple comparatives.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three sentences that describe a trend (rose sharply, reached a peak, leveled off) and one comparison (higher than, the most).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Describing Trends and Charts in English"

A focused episode on the language of data: trend verbs, comparatives and the words you need to present a chart clearly in a meeting.

Record a 2-minute description of an imaginary line graph: introduce the axes, describe the trend, point out the peak and plateau, and give one proportion.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Describing graphs and trends in English"

A clear lesson on the vocabulary and grammar for describing data: increase, decline, peak, plateau, fluctuate, and the comparatives that compare two lines.

After watching, write six sentences about a chart: two with an increase, two with a decline, and two comparing groups (higher than, the highest).
