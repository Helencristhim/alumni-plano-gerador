Lesson 14 -- Crisis Communication

Series
The IT Crowd -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Yesterday's Jam"

A comedy about an IT team dealing with systems that go wrong. Watch how they describe what was happening when things broke, and notice the gap between a calm response and a panicked one.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three past-continuous sentences about what the characters were doing when a problem started ("The server was failing while...").

Podcast
Google SRE -- "Postmortem Culture: Learning from Failure"

A clear discussion of how strong teams handle outages: declaring incidents, setting severity, communicating on a status page, and running blameless postmortems to prevent the next disruption.

Record a 90-second incident update: what was happening when it started, what severity you set, how you remediated it, and what the postmortem will cover.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Past continuous: was/were + -ing"

A focused lesson on the form and meaning of the past continuous, including the contrast with the past simple using "when" and "while". Directly reinforces today's grammar discovery.

After watching, write six past-continuous sentences about an outage, mixing incident, severity, war room, and a "when/while" interruption. Read them aloud.
