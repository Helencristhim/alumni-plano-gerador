Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Incident -- an unplanned event that interrupts a service
"We opened a war room as soon as the incident started." (Incidente / ocorrência)
Outage -- a period when a service is down or unavailable
"The outage lasted about an hour before we restored it." (Indisponibilidade / queda)
Severity -- how serious or urgent a problem is
"We set the severity to one because customers were affected." (Gravidade / severidade)
War room -- a focused meeting where the team fixes a crisis
"We opened a war room and called the on-call engineer." (Sala de crise)
Status page -- a public page that shows if a service is up or down
"I updated the status page so customers knew about the outage." (Página de status)
Remediate -- to fix a problem and stop further damage
"We remediated the issue by restarting the service." (Sanar / corrigir)
Disruption -- an interruption that stops normal work or service
"The disruption affected about two thousand customers." (Interrupção / transtorno)
Postmortem -- a review after an incident to learn and prevent it
"In the postmortem, we found the root cause and wrote action items." (Análise pós-incidente)

Last night, Rafael's payment service had an incident. At about eleven p.m., while most of the team was sleeping, the service went down. When the on-call engineer joined, the database was running out of memory. While the team was remediating the problem, Rafael updated the status page so customers knew about the outage. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Continuous

GRAMMAR

Usamos o past continuous para descrever uma ação em progresso em um momento do passado, muitas vezes interrompida por outra ação. / We use the past continuous to describe an action in progress at a moment in the past, often interrupted by another action.

Part / ParteStructureExample / Exemplo
Affirmative / Afirmativowas / were + verb-ingthe service was returning errors.
Plural / youwere + verb-ingcustomers were trying to check out.
Negative / Negativowasn't / weren't + verb-ingwe weren't expecting an outage.
Interrupted / Interrompidopast continuous + when + past simpleI was watching the dashboard when the alert fired.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o past continuous usa was/were + verbo-ing para uma ação em progresso no passado. Use was com I/he/she/it e were com you/we/they. Uma ação longa (past continuous) costuma ser interrompida por uma curta (past simple) ligada por when ou while. É perfeito para narrar o que estava acontecendo durante um incidente.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each crisis situation.

You want to describe what was happening at the start of the incident. The best sentence is:
A "Customers were trying to check out, but the service was returning errors."
B "Customers trying to check out, but the service returning errors."
C "Customers will try to check out tomorrow."
A customer asks why the site was down. The clearest, honest reply is:
A "Nothing happened, the site was fine."
B "We had an outage. We remediated it, and the service is now restored."
C "It is your internet, not our problem."
Your manager asks what comes next after the incident. The best answer is:
A "Nothing, the outage is over, so we are done."
B "We will run a postmortem to find the root cause and prevent it next time."
C "We will lower the severity and ignore it."
