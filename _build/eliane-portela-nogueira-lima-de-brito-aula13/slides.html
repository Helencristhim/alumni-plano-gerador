Chapter 3

The Art of Emphasis

Cleft sentences: What we offer is... · It is X that...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"What sets us apart is our technology."

"It is our data that wins the room."

"The reason we win is one differentiator."

"All a good pitch needs is one clear message."

TypeStructureExample
Wh-cleft (What)What + clause + is + XWhat we offer is a lower cost.
It-cleftIt is + X + that/who + clauseIt is our data that wins.
The reason...The reason (that) ... is + XThe reason we win is our edge.
All-cleftAll (that) ... is + XAll we need is one message.

A cleft splits one idea into two parts to spotlight X. Use one "is" only (never "What we offer it is"), and a What-clause is always singular ("What matters is", never "are").

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

What we offer it is a lower cost.
What we offer is a lower cost.
Is our data that wins the room.
It is our data that wins the room.
What matters most are trust.
What matters most is trust.

Three rules in one slide: never add a second subject ("What we offer it is"); an It-cleft must begin with "It is"; and a What-clause always takes "is", never "are".

Chapter 4

The Pitch Meeting

A founder pitches an investor, one strong line at a time

Dialogue

Pitching the Clinic

J
So, Doctor, you have three minutes. What makes your clinic different from the incumbent?
E
What sets us apart is one thing: it is our technology that lowers the cost per cycle. That is our differentiator.
J
Many founders say that. What I want to see is traction. Do you have any?
E
We do. What convinced our first partners was the pilot: forty cycles, and one clear use case.
J
That is compelling. And the pain point you solve?
E
It is cost. What patients feel most is the price, and that is exactly what we fix.
J
Good. And what is the upside for me — the return on investment?
E
The reason this is a standout is the margin. What you get is a model that grows, not a single clinic.
J
Then you have my buy-in. It is founders like you that I invest in.
Comprehension

Did You Read Him?

1. What does James say he wants to see, more than a claim?
Traction — real proof of growth.
2. What did James call compelling?
The pilot: forty cycles and one clear use case.
3. At the end, what does James offer, and who does he say he invests in?
His buy-in; he says it is founders like Eliane that he invests in.
