The Pitch -- Presenting Your Case with Impact

Learn to pitch like a founder. You will meet twelve pitch words -- pitch deck, compelling, traction, differentiator, pain point, use case, return on investment, upside, buy-in, standout, tailor, resonate -- and master cleft sentences (What we offer is... / It is X that...) to put the spotlight on your single strongest point.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words founders use when they pitch. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que fundadores usam ao apresentar um pitch. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Pitch deck -- the short slide presentation for investors / apresentação de slides do pitch
"My pitch deck is ten slides, not thirty."
Compelling -- so convincing it is hard to refuse / convincente, irresistível
"A compelling story beats a long list of features."
Traction -- early proof that a business is growing / tração, prova de crescimento
"Our pilot gave us the first real traction."
Differentiator -- the one thing that makes you different / diferencial
"Our differentiator is a technology that cuts the cost per cycle."
Pain point -- the specific problem a customer feels / dor, problema do cliente
"The pain point our patients feel most is cost."
Use case -- a real example of how a customer uses it / caso de uso
"Let me show you one clear use case from the pilot."
Return on investment -- the profit an investor earns back / retorno sobre o investimento
"The return on investment is strong by year three."
Upside -- the possible gain if things go well / potencial de ganho
"The upside is a whole new market of patients."
Buy-in -- the support of the people who must agree / adesão, apoio de quem decide
"We walked out with the investor's buy-in."
Standout -- the clearly best or most noticeable one / destaque
"In a room of ten pitches, we want to be the standout."
Tailor -- to adjust something to fit an audience / adaptar, personalizar
"I tailor the pitch to the person in the room."
Resonate -- to connect so a message feels true / ressoar, fazer sentido
"A pitch that resonates with one investor may not with another."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is rehearsing her pitch. Instead of listing ten features, she uses emphasis. What sets us apart is our technology, she says, so the spotlight lands on the differentiator. Then, it is our data that wins the room -- an It-cleft, where "It is... that..." frames the strongest point. She adds, the reason investors say yes is the traction, and all a good pitch really needs is one clear message. Notice two things: a What-clause always takes "is" (never "are"), and you never add a second subject -- it is "What we offer is...", not "What we offer it is...".

1. In "What sets us apart is our technology", where does the emphasis fall?
A On "our technology" -- the part after "is".
B On "What" -- the first word.
C There is no emphasis; it is a normal sentence.
2. Which sentence is a correct It-cleft?
A "Is our data that wins the room."
B "It is our data that wins the room."
C "It our data is that wins the room."
3. Why is it "What matters is trust", not "What matters are trust"?
A Because "trust" is plural.
B Because a What-clause is always singular, so it takes "is".
C Because "matters" needs "are".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences (Emphasis)

GRAMMAR

A cleft splits one idea into two parts so the spotlight falls on the part you choose. Use one "is" only (never a second subject), and a What-clause always takes "is". / A cleft divide uma ideia em duas partes para dar ênfase ao que você escolhe. Use só um "is" (nunca um segundo sujeito), e a What-clause sempre fica no singular ("is").

Type / TipoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Wh-cleft (What)What + clause + is + XWhat we offer is a lower cost.
It-cleftIt is + X + that/who + clauseIt is our data that wins.
The reason...The reason (that) ... is + XThe reason we win is our edge.
All-cleftAll (that) ... is + XAll we need is one message.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

In about two minutes, pitch your clinic out loud to an investor. Lead with your differentiator (a What-cleft), name the pain point you solve (an It-cleft), show your traction with one use case, and close with the return on investment and your ask for buy-in.
