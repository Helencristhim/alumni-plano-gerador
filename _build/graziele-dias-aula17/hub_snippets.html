Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia a apresentacao dos grupos e responda as perguntas. Ela usa -er + than e more + than. (Read and answer.)

Good morning. We have 75 medical groups. The ICU is larger than the lab. The emergency team is faster than the lab. The new wing is more modern than the old wing. But patient safety is more important than speed.

1. Which group is larger? (Qual grupo e maior?)
A "The ICU." (A UTI.)
B "The lab." (O laboratorio.)
C "The old wing." (A ala antiga.)
2. Which word do we use to compare? (Qual palavra usamos para comparar?)
A "and"
B "than"
C "with"
3. What is more important than speed? (O que e mais importante que a velocidade?)
A "The new wing." (A ala nova.)
B "Patient safety." (A seguranca do paciente.)
C "The lab." (O laboratorio.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives (-er than / more ... than)

Grammar

Como comparar duas coisas em ingles. / How to compare two things in English.

Palavra / WordComparativo + than
Curta / Short: large, fast+ -er + than: "larger than", "faster than" (maior que, mais rapido que)
Longa / Long: important, modernmore + palavra + than: "more important than", "more modern than" (mais importante que)
Erro comum / Common mistakeNAO use "more" com palavra curta: "more larger" (errado) -> "larger" (certo). NAO esqueca "more" na longa: "importanter" (errado) -> "more important" (certo).
Dica / TipSempre use than para comparar duas coisas. (Always use than to compare two things.)

Curta + -er · longa + more · sempre + than. (Short + -er · long + more · always + than.)
