Lesson 6 -- How Long Have You...?

Talk
Josh Kaufman -- "The first 20 hours: how to learn anything" (TEDxCSU)

The perfect video for tonight's grammar. It is all about how long you have to keep at something before you get the hang of it. He picks up the ukulele and shows you the whole curve, from feeling stupid to actually playing.

Tip: after it, say out loud how long you have been doing your own hobby: "I have been doing it for ___ / since ___."

Video
Restoration Reflections -- My Year-Long Furniture Transformation

This is your hobby on screen. The whole video is one long answer to "how long have you been working on this?" -- a year of sanding, repairing and painting, told from start to finish. Exactly the kind of thing you have been doing in your own workshop.

Tip: notice how she separates what she has been doing from what she has finished (how long vs how many). Then describe your own longest project the same way.

Talk
TED -- "Time to fall in love again with your inner amateur"

A short, warm talk about doing something purely for the love of it, for years, with no plan to turn it into work. It is the mindset behind your restoration and behind this whole course: an hour where you lose track of time.

Tip: the speaker has been an amateur at several things. Catch one "I have been..." sentence and copy its shape about your own hobby.
