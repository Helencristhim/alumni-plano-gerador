Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Accent -- the way people from a country or region pronounce a language
"Rahul speaks English with an Indian accent."
Intonation -- the way the voice rises and falls when we speak
"Her intonation goes up at the end of a question."
Word stress -- the part of a word we say with more force
"In deploy, the word stress is on the second part."
Pace -- the speed at which someone speaks
"He spoke at a fast pace, so I asked him to slow down."
To clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"Could you clarify what you mean by staging?"
To rephrase -- to say the same idea again using different words
"I did not understand, so she rephrased the question."
To catch -- to hear and understand what someone says
"Sorry, I did not catch the last word."
Filler word -- a small sound or word, like um, used while thinking
"He used a few filler words while he found the answer."
To mishear -- to hear something wrongly
"I misheard the time; I thought he said nine, not five."
Context -- the situation around a word that helps you guess its meaning
"Use the context to guess a word you do not know."
To confirm -- to check that something is true or correct
"Let me confirm the details before we finish."
Unclear -- hard to hear or understand
"The line was unclear, so I asked him to repeat it."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On a call with Rahul, Ana could not always catch every word. His accent, his intonation, and his pace were new to her. But she used good strategies. When the line was unclear, she used the context to guess. When she misheard a detail, she asked a clarifying question: "Could you say that again?" When she wanted to check a fact, she said, "So you are saying the test failed twice?" to confirm it. Asking Rahul to rephrase was never a problem.

1. Why do we say "Could you say that again?" instead of "Say that again"?
A "Could you...?" is a polite request; "Say that again" sounds like an order.
B They mean different things; one is about the past.
C "Could you...?" is only for questions about the future.
2. "So you are saying the test failed twice?" is used to:
A ask about the meaning of a single word.
B confirm what you heard.
C change the subject of the call.
3. Which question asks about the meaning of ONE word?
A "Could you spell that name for me?"
B "So you are saying it is not ready?"
C "What do you mean by staging?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Clarifying Questions

GRAMMAR

How to check what you heard, politely and clearly.

FunctionFormExample
Ask to repeat / slow downCould you + base verb ...?Could you repeat that?
Ask about one word or ideaWhat do you mean by + noun?What do you mean by staging?
Confirm what you heardSo you are saying (that) + clause?So you are saying it failed twice?
Check a detailCould you spell / say ...?Could you spell the name for me?
Common mistakeDo not say "What means staging?"; say "What do you mean by staging?". Start a request with "Could you...?", not just "Repeat, please."
