Chapter 4: The Code

Could you repeat that?

Clarifying questions to check understanding

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Sorry, could you repeat that, please?"

"What do you mean by staging?"

"So you are saying the test failed twice?"

"Could you spell the name for me?"

FunctionFormExample
Ask to repeat / slow downCould you + base verb ...?Could you repeat that?
Ask about one word or ideaWhat do you mean by + noun?What do you mean by staging?
Confirm what you heardSo you are saying (that) + clause?So you are saying it failed twice?
Check a detailCould you spell / say ...? (Could you spell the name for me?)

A clarifying question checks what you heard. It is polite and professional, and it keeps the conversation clear.

Language Focus

Which Clarifying Question?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"What means staging?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you mean by staging?"

✗ WRONG

"Repeat, please."

✓ RIGHT

"Could you repeat that, please?"

Use "What do you mean by...?" to ask about a word, not "What means...?". Start a request with "Could you...?" to sound polite, not abrupt.

Dialogue

A Call with Rahul

R
Hi Ana. So, this afternoon we deployed the build to staging, and the login is fine now.
A
Sorry Rahul, could you say that again, a little slower? I want to get every word.
R
Of course. We deployed the new build to the staging server. The login page works now.
A
Got it. What do you mean by staging? Is that the test server?
R
Yes, exactly. Staging is the test server, before production.
A
Okay. And the checkout? So you are saying it still has a bug?
R
Right. The test failed twice, so please do not move it to production yet.
A
Understood. Let me confirm: login is fine, checkout is not ready. I will send a summary.

About the Conversation

The test failed twice, so there is still a bug.
