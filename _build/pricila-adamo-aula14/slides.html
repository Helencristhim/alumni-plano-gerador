Phone & Messages

Calling, taking messages & arranging by phone

Chapter 1: The Phone Rings

When You Can't See the Face

Last class you learned to invite people and make plans. Very often, you arrange that plan over the phone -- a hotel calls about your room, a friend calls to change a time. On the phone you can't see the face, so clear phrases really help. Today you learn to make a call, take a message, and ask someone to call back.

In one word: how do you feel about speaking English on the phone?

Chapter 3: Taking a Message

Passing On a Message

Offer · relay · ask someone to call back

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"He's not here. Can I take a message?"

"Could you tell him that the meeting is at noon?"

"Could you ask her to call me back?"

"I'm calling to confirm my booking."

FunctionPhraseExample
Offer to writeCan I take a message?He's out. Can I take a message?
Relay infoTell + person + that + sentenceTell him that the room is ready.
Relay a requestAsk + person + to + base verbAsk her to call me back.
Say why you callI'm calling to + base verbI'm calling to confirm my booking.

Use tell + that + a full sentence to pass on information, and ask + to + base verb to pass on a request. Start a call with "I'm calling to..."

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Tell him the room is ready him."

✓ RIGHT

"Tell him that the room is ready."

✗ WRONG

"Ask her to calling me back."

✓ RIGHT

"Ask her to call me back."

Chapter 4: On the Line

A Call for Someone Else

Pricila answers and takes a message

Dialogue

Pricila Takes a Message

M
Hello, this is Mark. I'm calling to speak to Paulo. Is he there?
P
Hi Mark. I'm afraid he's out right now. Can I take a message?
M
Yes, please. Could you tell him that our dinner is now at eight, not seven?
P
Of course. Dinner at eight instead of seven. Should he call you back?
M
Please ask him to text me if the new time doesn't work. My number is five-five-five, two-one-oh-three.
P
Got it -- five-five-five, two-one-oh-three. I'll give him the message. Take care, Mark!

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Make the Call

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play

Call the Hotel

Scenario: You phone your hotel to change your arrival day. The receptionist answers. Say why you are calling and ask for what you need.

Key phrases:

I'm calling to... Could you tell me...? Thank you for your help.
Role-Play

Take the Message

Scenario: Someone calls and asks for your travel friend, who is out. Offer to take a message, write down the name and number, and promise to pass it on.

Tools:

Can I take a message? tell + that / ask + to I'll pass it on.
Free Practice

Your Phone Call

Scenario: Have a full phone call with the teacher. Greet, say why you are calling, deal with one small problem (the person is out, or the line is busy), leave or take a message, and end the call politely.

No key phrases. Make a real call from start to finish!
