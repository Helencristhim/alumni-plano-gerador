Stage 1.1: Words for Staying Connected

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for keeping in contact.

To keep in touch -- manter contato
"We promised to keep in touch after the trip."
To lose touch -- perder o contato
"We slowly lost touch after she moved away."
To drop someone a line -- mandar um recado rapido
"Drop me a line when you land safely."
To catch up -- colocar o papo em dia
"Let's catch up over coffee when I'm back."
To reach out -- tomar a iniciativa de contatar
"I'll reach out as soon as things calm down."
To fall out of touch -- ir perdendo o contato aos poucos
"After a busy year, we fell out of touch."
To touch base -- dar um alo rapido para se atualizar
"Let's touch base next week about the trip."
To reconnect -- reconectar / retomar o contato
"It was lovely to reconnect after all these years."
A catch-up -- uma conversa para por o papo em dia
"We had a long catch-up over dinner."
To keep someone posted -- manter alguem informado
"Once you book anything, keep me posted."
To check in on -- ver como alguem esta
"I message her every few weeks to check in on her."
A long-lost friend -- um amigo que sumiu ha muito tempo
"On the trip I ran into a long-lost friend from school."
To drift apart -- se afastar aos poucos
"We never argued; we just drifted apart."
To look someone up -- procurar e retomar contato
"If you're ever in Brazil, look me up."

Grammar

"When I get home, I will finally reconnect with everyone I met. As soon as I land, I will drop my sister a line so she knows I am safe. And before I forget, if you are ever in Brazil, look me up -- I really do not want us to drift apart or lose touch again."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Time Clauses

Grammar

Depois de when, as soon as, once, after, before e until, use o PRESENTE simples, mesmo com sentido de futuro. O will (ou going to) fica na oracao de RESULTADO, nunca na oracao de tempo. / After when, as soon as, once, after, before and until, use the PRESENT simple, even for future meaning. Will (or going to) goes in the RESULT clause, never in the time clause.

Parte / PartEnglishPortugues
Time clause (present)As soon as I land, ...Assim que eu pousar, ...
Result clause (will)... I will text you.... eu te mando mensagem.
when + presentWhen I get home, I will call you.Quando eu chegar em casa, eu te ligo.
