Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recommend -- to say that an option is the best one to choose
"I would recommend the cloud option for the long term." (Recomendar)
Make the case -- to give clear reasons to support an idea
"Let me make the case for the new tool in two minutes." (Defender / argumentar a favor)
Win over -- to change someone's mind so they agree with you
"Her honest answer helped win over the whole board." (Conquistar / convencer)
Buy-in -- the agreement and support of the people involved
"We need buy-in from leadership before we start." (Apoio / adesão)
Advocate -- to publicly speak in favor of a plan or idea
"I will advocate for this plan in the next review." (Defender publicamente)
Endorse -- to officially support and approve a plan or person
"Leadership endorsed the new rollout plan." (Endossar / aprovar)
Sway -- to influence someone's opinion or decision
"One clear number can sway a whole meeting." (Influenciar)
Cost-effective -- giving good value for the money you spend
"The new tool is more cost-effective over a year." (Custo-benefício / econômico)
Downside -- the negative part or disadvantage of an option
"The one downside is the setup takes two weeks." (Desvantagem / ponto negativo)
Weigh up -- to compare the good and bad sides before deciding
"Let's weigh up both options before we choose." (Ponderar / avaliar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Natalie wants to recommend a new cloud tool. To win support, she will make the case clearly. She leads with emphasis: "What we need is a faster rollout." She explains that the tool is "not only faster, but also more cost-effective." Then she will weigh up both options out loud. To build trust, she names one honest downside: the setup takes two weeks. She believes this honesty will sway leadership and help her win them over. Finally, she asks for their buy-in directly, instead of just hoping they agree.

1. Which sentence emphasizes the main idea most strongly?
A "What we need is a faster rollout."
B "We need a faster rollout, maybe."
C "A rollout would be needed."
2. Why does Natalie name a downside herself?
A To weaken her own recommendation.
B To build trust and help win leadership over.
C Because she has no benefits to share.
3. How does Natalie end her case?
A She asks for buy-in directly.
B She just hopes everyone agrees.
C She ends without any ask.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Emphasis for Persuasion

GRAMMAR

Para persuadir, damos ênfase ao que importa. Use not only X, but also Y para unir duas ideias e dar peso às duas (X e Y na MESMA forma), e What we need is + substantivo para empurrar a ideia principal para a frente. / To persuade, we emphasize what matters. Use not only X, but also Y to join two points and give weight to both (X and Y in the SAME form), and What we need is + noun to push your main idea to the front.

StructureUseExample
not only X, but also Ygive weight to two points"...not only faster, but also cheaper."
X and Y same formkeep both sides balanced"not only recommend it, but also name the risk."
What we need is + nounpush the main idea to the front"What we need is a faster rollout."
Neutral sentenceless emphasis, plain order"We need a faster rollout."

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de not only, mantenha as duas partes na mesma forma (adjetivo + adjetivo, verbo + verbo). Errado: "not only faster, but also it is cheaper." Certo: "not only faster, but also cheaper." Use What we need is... para destacar a ideia central.

Stage 2: Build a Persuasive Pitch in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of a persuasive recommendation in the correct order.

?You name one honest downside, because that builds trust and helps you win the room.
?You lead with the recommendation, so people hear your main idea right away.
?You ask for buy-in directly, instead of just hoping that everyone agrees.
?You give one strong reason and show the benefit.
?You handle objections calmly and weigh up the options out loud.
