Lesson 19 -- How Much, How Many

Talk
Hans Rosling -- The Best Stats You've Ever Seen (TED)

A famous, energetic talk about numbers and data: patient figures, averages, ratios and the big picture behind them. It is exactly the skill you use when you show the Brazil dashboard to the Global team.

Tip: turn on English subtitles. Treasure hunt: write down 3 sentences with a quantifier (many, a lot of, a few, a little) and one number word (average, ratio, portion). Do not try to understand 100% -- listen only for how he talks about "how much" and "how many".

Podcast
6 Minute English -- 'Money' Mega-Class (BBC Learning English)

A calm, clear BBC podcast set about money, numbers and quantities: how much, how many, a lot of, a little. Slow British teachers and lots of real quantity language for your next field update with Global.

Tip: listen once with no pauses, then again and repeat out loud every sentence with a quantifier ("a lot of demand", "a few hospitals", "not much time"). You already listen to podcasts every day -- give one to your English.

YouTube
Quantifiers -- much, many, a lot of, a few, a little (Learn English with KT)

A short, clear lesson on the exact grammar of this class: when to use much and many, a lot of, a few and a little, with countable and uncountable nouns. Perfect to review before your next Global data call.

Tip: turn on English subtitles. Pause after each rule and say one example with your own work (patients, demand, hospitals, access, time): "We have many patients", "a lot of demand", "a few hospitals", "a little time".
