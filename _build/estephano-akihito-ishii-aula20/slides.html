Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

I Attack, We Win

Action verbs in the game

Grammar Rule

Who Does the Action?

I / We / They

verb -- NO -s

"I attack the enemy."

"We win the match."

The team / He / It

verb + -s

"The team wins."

"He defends the base."

Key idea: remember Lesson 18 -- with the team / he / it, the verb gets an -s: wins, attacks, defends.

The Negative

What I Do Not Do

To say what we do NOT do, we use do not / does not:

"I do not attack alone."

"The team does not lose often."

Question form:

"Do you play every day?" -- Yes, I do. / "Does your team win?" -- Yes, it does.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

WRONG

"The team win the match."

RIGHT

"The team wins the match."

With the team, he, or it, the verb needs an -s.

Grammar Practice

Which Verb?

Say the correct verb before you reveal the answer.

1. I (attack) the enemy.

I attack the enemy. (I = no -s)

2. The team (win) the match.

The team wins the match. (the team = +s)

3. My friends (defend) the base.

My friends defend the base. (they = no -s)

4. He (not lose) the game.

He does not lose the game. (negative = does not + verb)
