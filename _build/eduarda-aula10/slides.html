Grammar Rule

The Meeting Arc

StageSkillPhrase
1. Openbreak the ice / social questions"What brings you here?"
2. Contributejump in / yield the floor"Sorry to jump in, but..." / "Go ahead."
3. Defendopinion + first conditional"In my view... If we wait, we will lose the deal."
4. Closesum up / report / confirm"To sum up... Are we on the same page?"
Common Mistakes

Watch Out!

"If we will wait, we will lose." / "He said me that..."
"If we wait, we will lose." / "He told me that..."

Keep present simple in the if-clause, and use tell + a person (not "say me").

Chapter 4 — Getting There

All Four Skills,
One Meeting

Open, contribute, defend, and close.

Dialogue

The Strategy Meeting

D
Welcome to the strategy meeting. Eduarda, would you like to start?
E
Sure. Nice to see you all again. In my view, we should act now.
A
Interesting. Can you back that up?
E
Of course. The data suggests the market will recover. If we wait, we will lose the deal.
D
Sorry to jump in, but what is the downside?
E
If the market falls, our capital stays protected. Anna, please go ahead.
A
Thanks. So, to sum up, we act now but stay flexible?
E
Exactly. The key takeaway: act now, review in two weeks. Are we all on the same page?
Comprehension

About the Meeting

1. What does Anna ask Eduarda to do?
To back up her opinion.
2. What does David want to know?
The downside (the risk).
3. How does Anna paraphrase the decision?
"Act now but stay flexible."
Artifact

Strategy Meeting Summary

STRATEGY MEETING — SUMMARY
Chair: David · Lead: Eduarda · Also: Anna
Decision: Act now, review in two weeks.

Opinion: In my view, we should act now.

Evidence: The data suggests the market will recover.

Risk: If the market falls, our capital stays protected.

Action items: Eduarda updates the model; David calls creditors; Anna prepares the timeline.

1. Which opinion frame opens the case?
"In my view, we should act now."
2. Which sentence is a first conditional?
"If the market falls, our capital stays protected."
3. How many action items are there?
Three.

Speaking

Oral Drilling

1. Break the ice with a new person (A6).
"Hi, I do not think we have met. What brings you here?"
2. Take your turn politely (A7).
"Sorry to jump in, but I have a quick point."
3. Give an opinion and back it up (A8).
"In my view, we should act now. The data suggests the market will recover."
4. Argue a consequence (A8).
"If we wait, we will lose the deal."
5. Report what a colleague said (A9).
"Daniel said that we will move now."
6. Sum up and confirm (A9).
"To sum up: act now, review in two weeks. Are we all on the same page?"
