Building Partnerships in Latin America

Growing the oncology network across the region: proposing an alliance with a partner hospital in Bogota. Key words: partnership, agreement, alliance, mutual, benefit, commit, trust, sign, long-term, reach. Structure: the first conditional (if + present, will + verb -- If we sign the agreement, we will start in March; If both teams commit, the alliance will be strong).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Partnership -- parceria (two groups working together)
"We want a partnership in Latin America."
Agreement -- acordo (when both sides say yes)
"We have an agreement."
Alliance -- alianca (a strong partnership between groups)
"The alliance helps both hospitals."
Mutual -- mutuo (shared by both sides)
"The benefits are mutual."
Benefit -- beneficio (a good thing you get)
"The main benefit is shared research."
Commit -- comprometer-se (to give your full effort)
"Both teams commit to the plan."
Trust -- confianca / confiar (to believe someone is honest)
"We trust our partner."
Sign -- assinar (to make it official with your name)
"We will sign the agreement on Friday."
Long-term -- de longo prazo (for a long time in the future)
"We want a long-term partnership."
Reach -- alcancar / chegar a (to finally get to an agreement)
"Let us reach an agreement."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Sarah tells Fernando about a new partnership. A hospital in Bogota wants to build an alliance with Einstein. "If we sign the agreement next month, we will start the program in March," she says. The benefits are mutual: both hospitals will share research and patients. "If both teams commit, the alliance will be strong," Fernando adds. He wants a long-term partnership, not just one year. "If we build trust now, we will work together for years. Let us reach an agreement this week."

1. Na frase "If we sign the agreement, we will start in March", qual e a condicao?
A "If we sign the agreement" (a condicao).
B "we will start in March" (a condicao).
C Nao ha condicao na frase.
2. Depois de "if", qual tempo verbal usamos?
A will + verbo (futuro).
B o presente simples.
C o passado simples.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If we will commit, the alliance will be strong."
B "If we commit, the alliance will be strong."
C "If we commit, the alliance is be strong."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como falar de uma condicao e seu resultado no futuro (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

PartForm / FormaExample
Condicao (if)if + presente simples / if + present simpleIf we sign the agreement,
Resultadowill + verbo base / will + base verbwe will start in March.
SignificadoResultado real e possivel / a real future possibilityIf both teams commit, the alliance will be strong.
Atencao!NUNCA will depois de if; will + verbo base (sem to, sem s)If we sign (NAO "If we will sign" / "we will to start")
