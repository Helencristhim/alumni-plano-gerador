Chapter 3

The First Conditional

A condition and its result: if + present, will + verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If we sign the agreement, we will start in March."

"If both teams commit, the alliance will be strong."

"If the terms are fair, we will accept the deal."

PartFormExample
Condition (if)If + present simpleIf we sign the agreement,
Resultwill + base verbwe will start in March.
Attention!no will after ifIf we sign (NOT "If we will sign")
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If we will sign, we will start."

✓ RIGHT

"If we sign, we will start."

✗ WRONG

"If the terms are fair, we will to accept."

✓ RIGHT

"...we will accept."

✗ WRONG

"If we commit, the alliance is be strong."

✓ RIGHT

"...the alliance will be strong."

After if, use the present simple. In the result, use will and the base verb with no to.

A Partnership with Bogota

S
Good news, Fernando. The hospital in Bogota wants to build a partnership with Einstein.
F
That is excellent. What does the agreement look like?
S
If we sign next month, we will start the program in March.
F
Good. If the benefits are mutual, my team will commit fully.
S
They are. Both hospitals will share research and patients.
F
I like it. If we build trust now, the alliance will last for years.
S
I agree. Let us reach a long-term agreement this week.
F
Perfect. If the terms are fair, we will sign on Friday.
