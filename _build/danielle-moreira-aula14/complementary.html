Series
The Bear — Season 1, official trailer & series (FX / Hulu)

A high-pressure kitchen is one long masterclass in the difference between managing people and developing them. Watch Carmy oscillate between barking orders that shrink his team and the rarer, harder moments where he actually coaches — asks a real question, names someone's growth edge, and empowers them to run their own station. The best 1:1 in the whole show is the one where he stops fixing and starts listening. Connection to Lesson 14: every scene is the choice between the command that gets compliance and the guidance that grows a person.

Tip: watch the trailer, then find Season 1 on Disney+. As you watch, note ONE moment where a character coaches instead of commands, and rewrite one of Carmy's orders as guidance — “I would rather...” or “you had better...” + the bare verb.

Podcast
Coaching Real Leaders with Muriel Wilkins — HBR Podcast Network

This is the exact conversation you are learning to run, recorded live: an executive coach works through a real leadership dilemma with a real leader, in real time. Listen to how Muriel opens with a question and then stays quiet, how she reframes the person's story instead of solving it, and how she guides without ever handing over an order. It is a whole show built on the coaching mindset. Connection to Lesson 14: it models active listening, reframing, and warm-but-directive guidance — the register you will use in every 1:1 in Canada.

Tip: pick any one episode and listen once with no pauses. Then record ninety seconds answering: “What did the coach do INSTEAD of giving advice?” Use the words active listening, reframing and coachable moment, and one advanced modal.

YouTube
Julian Treasure — “5 ways to listen better” (TED, 8 min)

The single skill that makes or breaks a coaching 1:1 is the one nobody teaches: listening. In eight minutes Julian Treasure explains why we are losing the ability to listen and gives five practical tools to get it back — including the discipline of staying with someone's silence instead of rushing to fill it. This is the mechanics behind “active listening” from the lesson. Connection to Lesson 14: it is the warm-up drill for the hardest part of any 1:1 — hearing the thing under the thing before you say a word.

Tip: watch it twice. On the second pass, pick two of Treasure's tools and write, in English, how you would use each one in your next mentoring 1:1. Then bring one to Lesson 15 — the full simulation.
