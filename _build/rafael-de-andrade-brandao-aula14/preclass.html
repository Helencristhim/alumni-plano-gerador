Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Rise -- to go up over time (past: rose)
"Sales rose from January to April." (Subir)
Fall -- to go down over time (past: fell)
"The numbers fell in the summer." (Cair)
Trend -- the general direction over time
"The trend was positive all year." (Tendência)
Peak -- the highest point
"Sales reached the peak in October." (Pico / auge)
Sharply -- a lot and fast
"Sales rose sharply in spring." (Acentuadamente)
Slightly -- only a little
"The numbers fell slightly in July." (Levemente)
Steady -- staying about the same
"In December, the trend was steady." (Estável)
Quarter -- three months of the year
"Each quarter has three months." (Trimestre)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael will describe the full-year sales trend to the board. He uses a line chart. In January, sales were low. Then, from February to April, sales rose sharply, by almost thirty percent. In the summer, the numbers fell slightly, because of the holidays. In September, sales climbed again and reached the peak of the year in October. Finally, in November and December, the trend remained steady. Overall, the general trend was positive.

1. What did sales do from February to April?
A They rose sharply.
B They fell sharply.
C They stayed steady.
2. When did sales reach the peak of the year?
A In October.
B In January.
C In the summer.
3. Which sentence is correct for a finished year?
A "Sales rose sharply in spring."
B "Sales sharply rise in spring."
C "Sales rised sharply in spring."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Trend Verbs & Adverbs

GRAMMAR

Usado para descrever como os números se movem ao longo do tempo. / Used to describe how numbers move over time. O verbo dá a direção; o advérbio dá a quantidade.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
rise / roseIrregular: subiu / went upSales rose sharply
fall / fellIrregular: caiu / went downThe numbers fell slightly
climb / drop + -edRegular: subir / cairSales climbed, then dropped
verbo + sharply / slightlyAdvérbio = quanto / how muchrose sharply, fell slightly
Key point / Ponto-chaveO advérbio vem depois do verbo. Diga "rose sharply" (não "sharply rose"). O ano terminou, então use o passado simples.
