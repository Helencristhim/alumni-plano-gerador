Chapter 3 -- Movement Over Time

Trend Verb + Adverb

Say which way, and how much

Discover the Pattern

Listen and Notice

"Sales rose sharply from February to April."

"In the summer, the numbers fell slightly."

"In October, sales reached the peak of the year."

"In December, the trend remained steady."

Grammar Rule

Describe the Movement

FormUseExample
rise / rose (up)Irregular: went upSales rose sharply
fall / fell (down)Irregular: went downThe numbers fell slightly
climb / drop + -edRegular: up / downSales climbed, then dropped
verb + sharply / slightlyAdverb = how muchrose sharply, fell slightly

Key idea: The verb gives the direction (rose, fell); the adverb comes after and gives the amount (sharply, slightly). The year is over, so use the past simple.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Sales sharply rose from February to April."
"Sales rose sharply from February to April."

The adverb goes after the trend verb. The past of rise is rose (not "rised").

Grammar Practice

Complete the Trend

"Sales rose _____sharply, a lot and fast." (adverb: a lot)
"In December, the trend _____remained steady." (stayed the same)
