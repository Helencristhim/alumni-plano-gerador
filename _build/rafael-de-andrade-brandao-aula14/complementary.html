Lesson 14 -- Describing Trends

Series
The Apprentice (US) -- any "boardroom results" episode

Watch the candidates report how their numbers moved over the task. Listen for trend language: the numbers went up, went down, and the peak. Notice the past simple when they describe what already happened.

Write down every sentence that describes a rise or a fall. Then describe an imaginary year out loud using rose, fell, sharply, slightly, and the peak.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Describing Trends and Graphs"

Practical phrases for describing charts: how to say numbers rose or fell, how to add adverbs like sharply and slightly, and how to point to the peak. Listen for the new words: rise, fall, trend, peak, sharply, slightly, steady, and quarter.

Note 3 sentences that combine a trend verb with an adverb. Practice each one out loud and describe your own line chart.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Describing graphs and trends in English"

A short, clear lesson on trend language: verbs of movement (rise, fall, climb, drop) and adverbs of degree (sharply, slightly, gradually). Perfect for walking an audience through a line chart.

After watching, record yourself describing last year's real results. Use trend verbs, adverbs, and at least three new words from this lesson, all in the past simple.
