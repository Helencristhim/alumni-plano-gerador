Aula 16 — Block 3: Handling Tough Questions After the Pitch

Series
Shark Tank / Dragons' Den — the Q&A after a pitch

Skip the pitch and watch the questions. Notice how the strongest founders stay composed under scrutiny, treat a hostile question as valid, and never walk their numbers back. Listen for hypothetical answers ("If we lowered the price, we would lose margin").

Tip: pick one tense exchange and write the founder's answer as a second conditional -- "If ..., we would ...".

HBR IdeaCast — an episode on handling tough questions and staying composed

Leaders explain exactly what this lesson covers: how to stay composed under pressure, treat scrutiny as normal, give a defensible answer, add a caveat, and stand by a recommendation. Clear American English, real boardroom situations.

Tip: note three moments where a guest stays calm under a pressing question, then rewrite each answer using a second conditional.
