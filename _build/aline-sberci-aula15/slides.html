Grammar Rule

Opinions, Agree & Disagree

FunctionPhraseExample
Give an opinionI think / In my opinion + sentenceI think we should wait.
AgreeI agree / Exactly / That is trueI agree with you.
Disagree politelyI see your point, but... / Actually...I see your point, but I prefer April.
Be unsureIt depends / I am not sureIt depends on the budget.

Key idea: say I agree or I disagree -- never "I am agree". To disagree politely, start with I see your point, but or Actually, then give your opinion.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am agree with the plan."
"I agree with the plan."

Agree and disagree are verbs. Never use am / is / are before them.

Artefact

Meeting Agenda

Team meeting · Tuesday, 10 a.m.

Campaign Launch -- Decision

1. Launch date: March or April? Share your opinion.

2. Budget: is it ready? It depends on the final numbers.

3. Marketing team: who agrees with an early launch?

4. Risks: anyone who disagrees, please explain why.

Goal: hear every point of view and find a fair solution together.

Dialogue

In the Meeting

T

"So, in my opinion, we should launch in March. What is your point of view, Aline?"

A

"I agree with an early launch. Exactly -- March is good for the marketing team."

T

"Great. And the budget is ready now, so we can move fast."

A

"Actually, I disagree there. I see your point, but I prefer to wait two weeks on the budget."

T

"That is a fair point. It depends on the final numbers. Let us check them first."

A

"I suggest we launch in March and finalize the budget next week. I think that works for everyone."
