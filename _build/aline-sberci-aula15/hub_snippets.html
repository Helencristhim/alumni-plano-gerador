Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Convince -- to make someone believe or accept your idea
"He could not convince the team." (Convencer)
Reasonable -- sensible and fair; a good and balanced idea
"That sounds reasonable to me." (Razoável)
Doubt -- to not be sure that something is true or will work
"I doubt we can finish by Friday." (Duvidar)
Compromise -- a solution where each side gives a little to agree
"Let us find a compromise." (Meio-termo)
Prefer -- to like one thing more than another
"I prefer the safer plan." (Preferir)
Point of view -- your own way of seeing or thinking about something
"I want to hear your point of view." (Ponto de vista)
Exactly -- used to agree strongly; that is completely right
"Exactly! I think the same." (Exatamente)
Actually -- used to correct gently or share what is really true
"Actually, I see it differently." (Na verdade)
Depends -- to change based on another fact or condition
"It depends on the final numbers." (Depende)
Fair point -- a good idea you respect, even when you do not fully agree
"That is a fair point, but I prefer to wait." (Bom argumento)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is Tuesday, and Aline is in a team meeting. Tom shares his opinion: "In my opinion, we should launch in March." Aline says, "I agree. Exactly -- March is good for the team." Then Tom says the budget is ready. But Aline is not sure: "Actually, I disagree there. I see your point, but I prefer to wait two weeks." Tom answers, "That is a fair point. It depends on the final numbers." Finally, Aline suggests a solution that works for everyone. Notice: we say I agree and I disagree (never "I am agree"), and we give an opinion with I think or In my opinion + a full sentence.

1. What is Tom's opinion?
A They should launch in March.
B They should cancel the campaign.
C They should wait one year.
2. How does Aline disagree politely?
A "You are wrong about that."
B "I see your point, but I prefer to wait."
C "No, that is a bad idea."
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I am agree with you."
B "I agree with you."
C "I am agree with the plan."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Opinions, Agree & Disagree

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para dar sua opinião e responder de forma educada. / Use these forms to give your opinion and respond politely.

Function / FunçãoPhrase / FraseExample / Exemplo
Dar opinião / Give an opinionI think / In my opinion + fraseIn my opinion, we should wait.
Concordar / AgreeI agree / Exactly / That is trueI agree with you.
Discordar educadamente / DisagreeI see your point, but... / Actually...I see your point, but I prefer April.
Estar em dúvida / Be unsureIt depends / I am not sureIt depends on the budget.

Key point / Ponto-chave: diga I agree e I disagree, nunca "I am agree" -- agree e disagree já são verbos. Para discordar com educação, comece com I see your point, but ou Actually, e depois dê sua opinião. Para dar opinião, use I think ou In my opinion + uma frase completa.
