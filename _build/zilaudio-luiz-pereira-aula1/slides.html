Chapter 3

The Code

Grammar · Discovery

What Do the Green Words Have in Common?

I am a farmer.
She is a buyer from Chicago.
I grow soybeans.
He grows corn.
FormIt saysExample
am / is / areWHAT you areI am a farmer.
verb (I / you / we)What you DOI grow soybeans.
verb + s (he / she / it)What HE doesHe grows corn.

Two families. Be says what you are. A normal verb says what you do. Never both together.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am work in Sorriso."
"I work in Sorriso."
"Am a farmer and a lawyer."
"I am a farmer and a lawyer."

Use be OR a normal verb — never the two together. And English always needs the subject: I am, he works. You can never drop it.
