Complementary Activities

Three things to do between Lesson 1 and Lesson 2. They are all about your world — the farm, the crop, the people who buy it. Mark each one as done when you finish it.

YouTube
Learn English on the Farm — Bob the Canadian

Bob is an English teacher and a farmer, like you. He walks around his farm and names everything in slow, clear English, with subtitles on the screen. This is the same vocabulary you studied today — field, crop, harvest — but spoken by a real farmer, on real land.

Tip: watch it once with the subtitles ON. Then watch it again with the subtitles OFF. You will understand more the second time than you expect — your ear is your strongest skill, so use it.

News — read and listen
US Trade War Helps Farmers in Brazil, but Not Iowa — VOA Learning English

A news story about Brazilian soybeans and American farmers — your crop, your business, in English. VOA Learning English reads every story one third slower than normal news, so you can follow it. There is an audio player at the top of the page and the full text below it.

Tip: play the audio FIRST, without reading. Write down three things you understood. Only then read the text and check. Do not translate — the goal is to catch the general idea, not every word.

Video lesson
Basic English Lesson: How to Talk about Yourself — engVid (Alex)

The exact structure of Lesson 1: I am, my name is, I like. Alex speaks slowly and writes everything on the board. Our lesson gave you the sentences; this video shows you the pattern behind them, so you can build your own.

Tip: after the video, take the quiz on the same page. Then say your own introduction out loud five times, without reading: name, what you are, what you grow, where you live.
