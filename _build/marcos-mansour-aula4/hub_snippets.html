Stage 4.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Break the ice -- quebrar o gelo (to start a friendly conversation)
"A simple question is a great way to break the ice."
Small talk -- conversa casual (light, friendly conversation about easy topics)
"Small talk helps people feel comfortable."
Host -- anfitrião, organizador (the person or company organizing an event)
"The host welcomed everyone to the congress."
Venue -- local do evento (the place where an event is held)
"This venue is close to the hotel, which is convenient."
By the way -- a propósito (a phrase to introduce a new, related topic)
"By the way, are you staying for the dinner?"
Mention -- mencionar (to say or refer to something briefly)
"You mentioned you work in valuation, right?"
In common -- em comum (a shared interest or experience between people)
"We have a lot in common -- we both love travel."
Topic -- assunto, tópico (the subject you are talking about)
"The weather is always a safe topic for small talk."
Enjoy -- aproveitar, gostar (to take pleasure in something)
"Are you enjoying the congress so far?"
Catch up -- colocar a conversa em dia (to talk and share news after some time)
"Let's catch up later over coffee."

Stage 4.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the coffee break, Ellen wants to break the ice. Instead of a closed question, she asks an open one: "What did you think of the morning session?" Marcos answers and adds a detail. Then he keeps it going: "By the way, where did you travel from?" Ellen replies, and Marcos connects topics: "You mentioned you are from Chicago -- how is the valuation market there?" They soon find they have a lot in common. "Speaking of tonight," Ellen says, "are you staying for the dinner? Let's catch up there."

1. Por que "What did you think...?" é melhor que "Did you like it?" no small talk?
A Porque é mais curta.
B Porque é uma open question e convida uma resposta mais longa.
C Porque usa o passado.
2. Quais palavras Marcos e Ellen usam para LIGAR um tópico ao outro?
A "host, venue, topic"
B "by the way, speaking of"
C "enjoy, catch up"
3. What makes the conversation feel natural and easy?
A They only answer yes or no.
B They find something they have in common and ask questions back.
C They stop talking after one question.

Stage 4.4: Grammar Tip -- Open Questions & Connectives

GRAMMAR

Como manter a conversa viva (explicação em inglês e português).

ToolUse / UsoExample
Closed question (Do/Is/Are...?)Resposta rápida sim/nãoDo you like it?
Open question (What/How/Why + do...?)Convida uma resposta real e longaWhat do you think of it?
By the way / Speaking of...Acrescentar ou ligar um novo tópicoBy the way, where are you from?
Really? / That's interestingMostrar interesse e devolver uma pergunta: Really? Tell me more.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação de networking.

You want to break the ice at the coffee break. The best opener is:
A "Do you like it here?"
B "What did you think of the morning session?"
C "Who are you?"
The other person answers "Yes." You want to keep the conversation going. You say:
A "By the way, where did you travel from?"
B (say nothing and wait)
C "Okay, bye."
You discover you both work in valuation. The natural thing to say is:
A "We have much in common things."
B "It sounds like we have a lot in common!"
C "How do you call this?"
You want to end the chat but keep the contact warm. You say:
A "It was great talking to you. Let's catch up at the dinner."
B "I go now."
C "Finish. Goodbye."
