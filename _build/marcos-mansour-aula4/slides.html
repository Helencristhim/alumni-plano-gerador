Chapter 3

Open Questions

Questions that keep the conversation going

Grammar Discovery

Open vs Closed

Closed: "Do you like the congress?" → Open: "What do you think of the congress?"

Closed: "Is this your first time here?" → Open: "How did you hear about this event?"

"By the way, are you staying for the dinner?"

"Speaking of travel, how was your flight?"

ToolUseExample
Closed question (Do/Is/Are...?)Quick yes/no factsDo you like it?
Open question (What/How/Why...?)Invite a real, longer answerWhat do you think of it?
By the way / Speaking of...Add or link a new topicBy the way, where are you from?
Follow-up: Really? / That's interestingShow interest, keep it goingReally? Tell me more.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Do you like it?" — "Yes." (conversation dies)

✓ RIGHT

"What do you think of it? And you?"

✗ WRONG

"How is the weather?" — "Good." (then silence)

✓ RIGHT

"Lovely, isn't it? By the way, where are you from?"

✗ WRONG

"How do you call this?" (from Spanish)

✓ RIGHT

"What do you call this?"

Ask open questions (What/How/Why). Add a detail, then bounce a question back. Use "by the way" to add a topic.

Chapter 4

At the Coffee Break

A natural networking conversation

Dialogue

Keeping the Ball Rolling

E
That morning session was excellent, wasn't it? What did you think of it?
M
It really was. I especially liked the part about new technology. By the way, where did you travel from?
E
From Chicago. It was a long flight! Speaking of travel, is this your first time in Washington?
M
It is, actually. I am enjoying the city a lot. You mentioned you are from Chicago — what is the valuation market like there?
E
Very busy right now. It sounds like we have a lot in common.
M
We do! By the way, are you staying for the dinner tonight?
E
I am! Maybe we can sit together and catch up more.
M
I would like that. It has been great talking to you.
