Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On the call, the client has an objection: the price is too high for their budget. Roberto does not argue. "I understand your concern," he says. Then he makes a polite suggestion: "You could try our smaller model -- it is more affordable." The client hesitates, so Roberto offers an alternative: "We should look at the volume together. For a bigger order, we could offer a more reasonable price." Finally, he reassures them: "We could adjust the delivery date." They reach a fair compromise. Notice: to suggest politely, English uses the modal verbs should and could + the base verb (no to). Should is stronger advice; could is a lighter option.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Should & Could for Suggestions

GRAMMAR

Use should e could para fazer sugestões educadas, em vez de dar ordens. / Use should and could to suggest politely instead of giving orders.

Uso / UseForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Conselho / Should (advice)should + verbo baseYou should consider the volume.
Opção / Could (option)could + verbo baseYou could try the smaller model.
Negativa / Negativeshould not + verbo baseYou should not rush the decision.
Oferta leve / Soft offerwe could + verbo baseWe could adjust the date.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de should e could, use o verbo na forma base, SEM to: diga "could try", não "could to try". Should é um conselho mais forte ("é uma boa ideia"); could é uma opção mais leve ("uma possibilidade"). Os dois transformam um "não" em um caminho a seguir, sem soar como ordem.
