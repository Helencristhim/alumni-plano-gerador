Connecting Past and Present -- Your Career Timeline

Present perfect vs past simple: when the time is finished (I started in 2016) and when it connects to now (I have worked since 2018), plus since and for. New vocabulary: recently, so far, lately, ago, turning point, breakthrough, decade, throughout, gradually, look back on.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recently -- a short time before now, in the recent past
"Recently I have trained a whole new team." (Recentemente)
So far -- up to this point in time, until now
"So far I have assisted in hundreds of procedures." (Até agora)
Lately -- during the recent period of time up to now
"Lately I have started to mentor younger nurses." (Ultimamente)
Ago -- a point in the past, measured back from now
"I started in the ICU more than ten years ago." (Atrás)
Turning point -- a moment of important change in a story or career
"Joining the surgical center was a turning point." (Ponto de virada)
Breakthrough -- an important development or success after hard work
"Training the team alone was a real breakthrough." (Grande avanço)
Decade -- a period of ten years
"For almost a decade I worked in intensive care." (Década)
Throughout -- during the whole of a period of time
"Throughout my career I have kept learning." (Ao longo de)
Gradually -- slowly and steadily, little by little
"I moved gradually from the ICU into surgery." (Gradualmente)
Look back on -- to think about a time or event in the past
"When I look back on my path, I feel proud." (Relembrar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Usado para conectar o passado terminado com o que continua até agora. / Used to connect finished past time with what still continues now.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
verb + ed / irregularPassado simples: tempo terminado / Past simple: finished timeI started in the ICU ten years ago.
have / has + past participlePresente perfeito: ligado ao agora / Present perfect: connected to nowI have worked here since 2018.
since + ponto no tempoInício de um período que continua / Start of a period that continuesI have been a nurse since 2010.
for + duraçãoQuanto tempo durou / How long it has lastedI have worked here for a decade.

Key point / Ponto-chave: com tempo terminado (ago, in 2016, last year) use o passado simples. Com ação que continua ou experiência geral (since, for, so far, recently) use o presente perfeito. Nunca "I have started ten years ago".

Lesson 6 -- Connecting Past and Present

Series
The Good Doctor -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Burnt Food"

A surgical drama where doctors and nurses describe their training, their past cases, and what they have achieved so far. Perfect for hearing the past simple and the present perfect side by side in a hospital setting.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two sentences in the past simple (a finished moment) and two in the present perfect (an experience connected to now). Then describe your own career using the same pattern.

Podcast
The Nurse Keith Show -- "My Nursing Journey So Far"

Nurses look back on their careers and explain how they moved gradually from one role to another. Listen for since and for, and for time words like recently, lately, and throughout.

Note three sentences that connect the past to the present. Then record a short summary of your own journey, from the ICU to robotic surgery, using at least three new words from today.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "Present Perfect vs Past Simple"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: when to use the past simple for finished times and the present perfect for experiences and actions that continue. Includes since vs for.

After watching, write five sentences about your career: connect what you did in the past with what you have achieved so far. Use ago, in 2016, since, for, and recently at least once each.
