Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Past simple (finished) vs present perfect (connected to now)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Is the time finished, or does it connect to now?

"I started in the ICU more than ten years ago."

"I have worked with robotic surgery since 2018."

"Two years ago I moved into the surgical center."

"So far I have assisted in hundreds of procedures."

Grammar Rule

Past Simple & Present Perfect

StructureWhen we use it
Past simple: verb + ed / irregular (I started, I moved)Finished time: ago, in 2016, last year, when
Present perfect: have / has + past participle (I have worked)Experience or time connected to now: recently, so far, already, yet
since + a point in time (since 2018)The start of a period that still continues
for + a length of time (for a decade)How long something has lasted

Key idea: if the time is finished and you can say exactly when, use the past simple. If the action started in the past and continues, or the moment is not important, use the present perfect.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have started in the ICU ten years ago."
"I started in the ICU ten years ago."

A finished time like ten years ago always needs the past simple. Use the present perfect only when the time is open or connected to now (since, so far, recently).
