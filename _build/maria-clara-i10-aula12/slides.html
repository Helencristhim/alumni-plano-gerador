Useful Language

How to Reconstruct an Event

The background (was / were + -ing) → the event (past simple) → the question

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which action is long? Which action is short?

"At two o'clock, we were setting up the room."

"I was testing the microphones when the news arrived."

"While we were changing the schedule, he made a decision."

"What were you doing when the minister arrived?"

Grammar Rule

The Background and the Event

The questionStructureExample
What was happening? (the background, long)was / were + verb-ingWe were setting up the room.
What happened? (the event, short)past simpleThe news arrived.
The event cuts the backgroundbackground + when + past simpleI was testing the microphones when the news arrived.
Two long actions togetherwhile + was / were + -ingWhile we were changing the schedule, he was reading the agenda.
Open the scene (new today)What were you doing when...? · Were they waiting when...?What were you doing when the minister arrived?

Two questions build any story. "What was happening?" gives the scene. "What happened?" gives the event. Use when before the short thing, and while before the long one. In a question, were comes before you: "What were you doing...?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you were doing when the news arrived?"
"What were you doing when the news arrived?"
"While the news arrived, we were changing the schedule."
"When the news arrived, we were changing the schedule."

In a question, were comes before you. And use when before the short event (the news arrived), while before the long background (we were changing the schedule).
