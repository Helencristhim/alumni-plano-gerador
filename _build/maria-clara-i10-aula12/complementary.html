Lesson 12 -- What Were You Doing and What Did You Do?

Series
Formula 1: Drive to Survive — Season 1, Episode 1 (Netflix)

This series is a debrief, again and again. After every race, the drivers reconstruct what happened: "I was leading the race when he hit me." The background in past continuous, the event in past simple — the exact code of your lesson, spoken by people under real pressure.

Tip: watch with English subtitles and go straight to the interviews after the race. Treasure hunt: write down 3 sentences with "was / were + verb-ing". Do not try to understand 100% — look ONLY for the background of the story.

Business English Pod — BEP 136: Meetings — Making a Progress Report

This is your debrief, in a meeting room. A professional reports what happened: what the team completed, what went wrong, and where the work is now. It is the same job you did today with the director — the sequence, the details, and an accurate version.

Tip: listen ONCE on your way to the university, with no pauses. Then listen again and write down every sentence in the past. Ask yourself for each one: is this the background, or is this the event?
