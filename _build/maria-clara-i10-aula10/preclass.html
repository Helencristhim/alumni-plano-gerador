Lesson 10 -- Pre-class

Telling the Full Story -- Past Continuous for the Unexpected

Tell the story of a moment that surprised you: was / were + verb-ing for the background, past simple for the surprise, with 'when' and 'while'. New vocabulary: interrupt, unexpected, react, situation, urgent, moment, calm, suddenly.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Interrupt -- to stop someone or something for a short time
"The phone rang and interrupted the meeting."
Unexpected -- happening when you do not expect it
"The news was completely unexpected."
React -- to do or say something because of what happened
"At first, I did not know how to react."
Situation -- the things that are happening at one time
"It was a difficult situation, but I handled it."
Urgent -- so important that you must do it now
"I received an urgent call during dinner."
Moment -- a very short time
"At that moment, the lights went out."
Calm -- quiet and relaxed, not nervous or worried
"I stayed calm and kept speaking."
Suddenly -- quickly and when you do not expect it
"Suddenly, the interpreter left the room."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the story and answer the questions.

It was the first official dinner of the trip. Ana was sitting next to the rector, and her interpreter was translating every sentence. Everything was calm. Then, suddenly, something unexpected happened. The interpreter got an urgent message and left the room. For a moment, Ana was alone with the rector. It was a difficult situation. But Ana did not know how to react -- so she stayed calm. While the waiters were serving the coffee, she was speaking to the rector in simple English. It was not perfect, but it was hers.

1. What was Ana doing when the interpreter left?
A She was sitting next to the rector at the dinner.
B She was leaving the room.
C She was calling a taxi.
2. Which sentence uses the past continuous correctly?
A While the waiters were serving the coffee, she was speaking.
B While the waiters serving the coffee, she speaking.
C While the waiters was serve the coffee, she was speak.
3. How did Ana handle the situation?
A She stayed calm and spoke in simple English.
B She said nothing and left the dinner.
C She waited for a new interpreter in silence.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Background and the Surprise

GRAMMAR

The long action sets the scene; the short action is the surprise.

PartStructureExample
Background (long action)was / were + verb-ingI was presenting the report.
Surprise (short action)when + past simple...when the interpreter left.
Two long actionswhile + was / were + -ingWhile we were having dinner...
Key pointUse was with I / he / she / it, and were with we / you / they. The short surprise is past simple (left), not "was leaving". Use when before the surprise and while before the long action.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. There is no wrong way to tell your story.

Take a real moment on a trip or at work, when something happened out of the blue. Tell the story out loud, for two minutes: (1) the background -- what were you doing? (was / were + verb-ing), (2) the surprise -- what happened suddenly? (past simple), (3) how you stayed calm and reacted. Record it.
Now finish your story with one honest sentence about you and English today. Not a beginner anymore -- improving. Say it out loud and record it.
