Film
The Terminal (2004) -- a traveler stuck in an airport

A man arrives at a New York airport, and out of the blue his country changes and he cannot leave. Everything is unexpected. He stays calm and speaks simple English to survive -- exactly the story of this lesson.

Tip: watch the first 20 minutes with English subtitles. Write down every sentence in the past: what was he doing when the problem started?

Podcast
Business English Pod -- BEP 194: Small Talk Before a Meeting

Two colleagues share short stories before a meeting -- a trip, a busy week, a small problem. This is the calm, simple story-telling you use at a dinner or a round table, with the full transcript on the page.

Tip: read the transcript out loud and take one speaker's part. Then close it and tell one story of your own, in the past.

YouTube
BBC 6 Minute Grammar -- Past Simple and Past Continuous

The exact grammar of this lesson, in six clear minutes: was / were + verb-ing for the background, and past simple for the action that interrupts it, with 'when' and 'while'.

Tip: after the video, record three sentences about your week. Each one must have a long action (was / were + -ing) and a short surprise.
