Useful Language

How to Tell the Story

The background (was / were + -ing) → the surprise (past simple)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which action is long? Which action is short?

"At 9 a.m., we were waiting for the rector."

"I was presenting the report when the interpreter left."

"While we were having dinner, the lights went out."

"What were you doing when the news came?"

Grammar Rule

The Background and the Surprise

PartStructureExample
Background (long action)was / were + verb-ingI was presenting the report.
Surprise (short action)when + past simple...when the interpreter left.
Two long actions togetherwhile + was / were + -ingWhile we were having dinner...

The long action sets the scene; the short action is the surprise. "I was preparing the agenda (long) when the phone rang (short)." Use when before the short action, and while before the long one.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We was having dinner when the lights went out."
"We were having dinner when the lights went out."
"I was presenting when the interpreter was leaving."
"I was presenting when the interpreter left."

Use was with I / he / she / it, and were with we / you / they. The short surprise is past simple (left), not continuous (was leaving).
