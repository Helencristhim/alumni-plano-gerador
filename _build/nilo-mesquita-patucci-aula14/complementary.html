Lesson 14 -- Negotiation Basics

Series
Suits -- any negotiation or deal-making scene

Lawyers open deals, make offers and counteroffers, push for leverage, hit a deadlock, and then meet halfway on a compromise -- all with soft, professional language. You will hear exactly the kind of give-and-take a real negotiation needs, and how confident people propose terms politely.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one soft proposal (would/could) and one moment where both sides meet halfway.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Negotiation Skills and Making Offers"

Practical episodes on opening a negotiation: proposing terms politely, making offers and counteroffers, looking for common ground, and breaking a deadlock with a compromise. Exactly today's language in real business deals.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to soften a proposal. Then record yourself opening an imaginary sponsorship deal using two of them.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Polite Requests and Offers with would and could"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to soften proposals and requests with would like to, could, and would, and how to make conditional offers with If plus would.
