Chapter 3 -- Soft Proposals

Grammar Discovery

Propose softly, sound professional

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

"We would like to propose a three-year deal." (softer than: We propose...)

"We could offer more visibility." (softer than: We offer more visibility.)

"If you accept a longer contract, we would lower the price." (a conditional offer)

"Would you be open to a compromise?" (a polite question)

Grammar Rule

Soft Proposals

Direct (too hard)Soft formSoft proposal
We propose a deal.would like toWe would like to propose a deal.
We offer more visibility.couldWe could offer more visibility.
Accept a longer deal.If ... wouldIf you accept a longer deal, we would lower the price.
Compromise?Would you ...?Would you be open to a compromise?

Key idea: soften proposals with would like to, could, and would. For a conditional offer, use If + present simple (you accept) and would + base verb (we would lower).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If you would accept a longer deal, we would lower the price."
"If you accept a longer deal, we would lower the price."

After if, use the present simple (you accept). Put would only in the result, with a base verb (would lower).

Grammar Practice

About the Briefing

1. How should Nilo open the negotiation?

Warmly and clearly, but politely -- he should propose, not make a hard demand.

2. Which two words make every proposal sound soft and professional?

Would and could -- for example, "we could offer" or "we would lower the price".
