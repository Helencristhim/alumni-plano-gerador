Disruption Management -- The Subjunctive (It is essential that + base verb)

The subjunctive in formal recommendations (It is essential/critical/imperative that + subject + base verb, e.g., "that the team be notified", "that we escalate", "that the supplier provide") for communicating a crisis to leadership with urgency. New vocabulary: outage, breach, containment, flag up, recovery plan, postmortem, stopgap, severity, notify, brief, fallout, damage control.

Outage -- a period when a system or service stops working
"At two a.m. we had a system outage at the main hub." (Queda / interrupção do sistema)
Breach -- a serious failure or break of security, a contract, or a protocol
"The outage was followed by a data breach in the portal." (Violação)
Containment -- the act of stopping a problem from spreading further
"Containment is in progress, so the failure will not spread." (Contenção)
Flag up -- to raise an issue urgently so a higher level sees it
"I recommend that we flag up any further failure to me directly." (Sinalizar / alertar)
Recovery plan -- a documented set of steps to restore normal operations
"I asked that each supplier provide a written recovery plan." (Plano de recuperação)
Postmortem -- a review held after an incident to learn what went wrong
"We will run a postmortem to find the root issue." (Análise pós-incidente)
Stopgap -- a temporary fix used until a permanent solution is ready
"The team applied a stopgap that keeps orders flowing." (Solução paliativa)
Severity -- how serious or damaging an incident is
"The severity is high, so we treat it as priority one." (Gravidade)
Notify -- to formally inform someone of something
"It is critical that the suppliers be notified within the hour." (Notificar)
Brief -- to give someone the key facts they need, concisely (verb)
"It is essential that leadership be briefed before the market opens." (Informar / dar um briefing)
Fallout -- the negative consequences that follow an incident
"On the fallout side, we should prepare a statement now." (Repercussões / consequências)
Damage control -- actions taken to limit harm to reputation or operations
"For damage control, no one should comment publicly yet." (Controle de danos)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When you communicate a crisis to leadership, the language has to sound formal and urgent. English does this with the subjunctive. After triggers like it is essential / critical / imperative, or verbs like recommend, insist, and ask, we add that + a subject + the base form of the verb. Two things change. First, the verb takes no -s, even for he, she, or it: "We ask that each supplier provide a recovery plan" -- not "provides". Second, the verb "be" stays as be, never is or are: "It is essential that leadership be briefed", "It is critical that the team be notified". So in a briefing you might say: "The severity is high. I recommend that we escalate immediately, and it is imperative that no one comment publicly until our statement is ready."

1. After "It is essential that leadership ___ briefed", which word is correct?
A be -- the subjunctive keeps the verb "be" as the base form.
B is -- because leadership is singular.
C are -- because it sounds more formal.
2. Why do we say "We ask that each supplier provide" and not "provides"?
A Because "supplier" is plural.
B The subjunctive uses the base form, so there is no -s even for he/she/it.
C "Provides" is simply a spelling mistake.
3. Which structure triggers the subjunctive?
A It is essential / I recommend / we insist + that + subject + base verb.
B will + be + verb-ing.
C have + past participle.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

An outage just hit and leadership has not been told. You want to recommend, formally and urgently, that they be informed. You say:
A "It is essential that leadership be informed now, and I recommend that we escalate immediately." (subjunctive, formal)
B "It is essential that leadership is informed." (wrong: should be "be")
C "Maybe someone could tell them at some point." (vague, not urgent)
You want each affected supplier to send a recovery plan. The best, most formal line is:
A "We ask that the supplier provides a recovery plan." (wrong: no -s in the subjunctive)
B "We ask that each supplier provide a recovery plan by end of day." (base form, correct)
C "Tell the supplier to maybe make a plan." (informal, vague)
News could leak before your statement is ready. For damage control, you recommend:
A "It is imperative that no one comment publicly until the statement is ready." (protects the narrative)
B "Everyone should post about it right away." (worsens the fallout)
C "It is imperative that no one comments." (wrong: no -s -- should be "comment")
