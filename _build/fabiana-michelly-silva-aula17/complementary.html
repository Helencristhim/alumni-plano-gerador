Lesson 17 -- Disruption Management

Series
The Playlist (Netflix) -- The crisis-management episodes

A drama about a company facing a sudden legal and reputational threat. Watch how leaders escalate, run containment, weigh the fallout, and decide who gets briefed first -- the same choices you practiced today.

Pick one crisis scene and write three sentences using the subjunctive: "It is essential that ...", "I recommend that ...", "We ask that ...".

Podcast
The Crisis Communications Podcast -- "Briefing Leadership Under Pressure"

An episode on managing an incident in real time: rating severity, running containment, controlling the fallout, and notifying the right people first. Directly relevant to escalating a supply-chain disruption.

Note two recommendations the host makes and rewrite each one with the subjunctive (It is critical that ... be ...).

YouTube
"The Subjunctive in English -- It is essential that he be..." -- Advanced Grammar

A clear guide to the exact structure in this lesson: the mandative subjunctive after essential, critical, recommend, and insist -- why we say "that he be" and "that she provide" with the base form and no -s.

After watching, write a six-line crisis email to leadership using the subjunctive in at least four lines.
