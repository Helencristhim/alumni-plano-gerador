Chapter 3

Recommend with Urgency

The subjunctive: It is essential that + subject + base verb

Discovery

The Base Form After That

It is essential that the team be notified now. (be, not is)

I recommend that we escalate immediately. (base form)

It is critical that leadership be briefed before the market opens. (passive subjunctive)

We ask that the supplier provide a recovery plan. (no -s, even with he/she/it)

I insist that the engineer be included in the postmortem. (insist + that)

It is imperative that no one comment publicly yet. (negative: that no one comment)

Grammar Rule

The Subjunctive

TriggerStructureExample
It is essential / critical / imperative+ that + subject + base verbIt is essential that the team be notified.
recommend / suggest / propose+ that + subject + base verbI recommend that we escalate now.
ask / request / insist+ that + subject + base verbWe ask that the supplier provide a plan.
Key 1: no -she/she/it still takes the base verbthat he provide (not provides)
Key 2: be, not is/arethe verb "be" stays as bethat leadership be briefed
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"It is essential that the team is notified and that he provides a plan." (after that, use the base form: be, not is; provide, not provides)
"It is essential that the team be notified and that he provide a plan." (base form after the trigger: be and provide)

After essential / critical / imperative / recommend / insist / ask + that, the verb is always the base form: drop the -s for he/she/it, and use be instead of is/are.

Chapter 4

Brief the Leadership

Escalating the overnight incident to senior leadership

Listening 1

The Crisis Brief

Marcus briefs leadership on the overnight incident

0:000:00
1. What two failures does Marcus report, and how severe are they?
A system outage at the main hub at about two a.m., followed by a data breach in the supplier portal; the severity is high, so he recommends treating it as a priority-one event.
2. What does Marcus say is essential and critical before the market opens?
It is essential that leadership be informed before the market opens, and it is critical that the affected suppliers be notified within the hour.
3. What does he propose for damage control, and what comes after containment?
He proposes that no one comment publicly until the statement is ready; once the outage is contained, they will run a postmortem and publish a recovery plan.
Artifact

Incident Report

Bloomberg Supply Chain -- Incident Report: Hub Outage & Portal Breach (Lead: Fabiana)

SUMMARY
02:00 -- a system outage at the main hub, followed by a data breach in the supplier portal. Severity: high.
STATUS
Containment in progress. A stopgap keeps orders flowing while a permanent fix is built.
RECOMMEND
It is essential that leadership be briefed before the market opens. It is critical that the suppliers be notified within the hour. We ask that each one provide a recovery plan.

Damage control: we propose that no one comment publicly until the statement is ready. Once contained, we insist that the on-call engineer be included in the postmortem to capture the root issue.

Comprehension

Recommendations Spotted

1. Inform leadership: "It is essential that leadership _____ informed."
"...that leadership be informed." (be, not is -- subjunctive after essential)
2. Ask the supplier: "We ask that each supplier _____ a recovery plan."
"...that each supplier provide a recovery plan." (provide, no -s, even with supplier)
3. Public side: "I propose that no one _____ publicly yet."
"...that no one comment publicly yet." (comment, base form, negative subjunctive)
