Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Phone -- a thing you use to talk to people far away
"I use my phone every day." (telefone)
Call -- to use the phone to talk to someone
"I call my friend." (ligar)
Answer -- to pick up the phone when it rings
"I answer the phone." (atender)
Message -- words you leave for someone to read or hear later
"I leave a message." (recado)
Speak -- to talk to someone
"Can I speak to Sara?" (falar)
Wait -- to stay for a short time until something happens
"Please wait a moment." (esperar)
Busy -- not free; the line is in use right now
"The line is busy." (ocupado)
Repeat -- to say something one more time
"Could you repeat that?" (repetir)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Estephano calls Better Tech. Sara answers the phone: "Hello, Better Tech." Estephano says: "Can I speak to Mr. Lima?" Sara says: "The line is busy. Could you wait?" Estephano waits. Then Sara says: "Could you call back in ten minutes?" Estephano says: "Sure. Could you take a message for him?"

1. What does Estephano say to talk to Mr. Lima?
A "Can I speak to Mr. Lima?"
B "How much is it?"
C "Where are you from?"
2. Why must Estephano wait?
A The store is closed.
B The line is busy.
C The phone is cheap.
3. Which sentence is a polite request?
A "I want Mr. Lima now."
B "Could you wait?"
C "Give me the phone."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Could you...?

GRAMMAR

Pedir as coisas de forma educada no telefone. / Asking for things politely on the phone.

Request / PedidoUse / UsoAnswer / Resposta
Could you repeat that?Pedir para repetirYes, of course.
Could you speak slowly?Pedir para falar devagarSure, no problem.
Could you wait a moment?Pedir para esperarYes, I can wait.
Atenção: Could you é mais educado que "Can you". Depois de Could you vem o verbo base, sem "to" e sem "s": "Could you repeat that?" (nunca "Could you to repeat").
