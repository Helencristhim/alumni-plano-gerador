Chapter 3 -- The Code

Ask Politely

Could you...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. What kind of word comes after "Could you"?

"Could you repeat that, please?"

"Could you speak slowly?"

"Could you wait a moment?"

"Could you call back later?"

Grammar Rule

Polite Requests

RequestUseAnswer
Could you repeat that?ask to say it againYes, of course.
Could you speak slowly?ask to talk slowSure, no problem.
Could you wait a moment?ask to waitYes, I can wait.

Key idea: use Could you + base verb to ask politely. "Could you" is more polite than "Can you". After "Could you" use the base verb -- no "to", no "s".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you to repeat that?"
"Could you repeat that?"

After Could you, use the base verb. Do not use "to". Say "Could you repeat that?"
