Lesson 14 · Asking for Clarification in Global Meetings

Checking In and Speaking Up

You don't have to understand every word. You only have to stay in the conversation.

Chapter 1: The Meeting

Last Time You Compared Results

"Brazil is the biggest market in Latin America."
"Asthma has the best performance this quarter."

Today the meeting is live and fast. The skill is not comparing — it is staying in the room when you miss a word.

Today

Three Moves

1

Clarify — ask for the word or the point again: "Could you repeat that?", "What do you mean by...?"

2

Hold the floor — keep your turn when someone interrupts: "Let me finish, please."

3

Buy time — two calm seconds instead of a panic: "Let me think for a second."

This is the class that ends the freezing. Today you build the toolkit.

The toolkitWhen you use itWhat you say

The one ruleThere is no new grammar here. Learn ONE phrase from each box by heart, and you always have something to say — even when you did not catch a word.

Silence is not the only option. These three boxes are the exit from the freeze.

Language Focus

Which Tool?

Common Mistake

Polite, Not Abrupt

"Repeat, please!"
"Sorry, could you repeat that, please?"
"What? I don't understand."
"Sorry, what do you mean by that, exactly?"
"No, no, I talk now!"
"Let me just finish this point, please."

One direct word can sound hard in English. The small words — sorry, please, could, just — are what make you sound professional and calm, never weak.

Listening 1

David's Voice Message

Before the meeting. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What two things does David need from Emmanuele?
A short update on the Brazil numbers (just the headline), and her main concern for the second half.
2. What time is the review now?
Eleven — they moved it.
3. What does David say she should do if something is not clear?
Not stay quiet — just ask him to clarify, or say "sorry, can you repeat that".
Listening 2

Claire Wraps Up

The end of the meeting. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What are the three action points Claire summarizes?
Emmanuele sends the Brazil update by Thursday; the access team follows up on the public market; the pricing question goes on next week's agenda.
2. Why does Claire want to "take the pricing question offline"?
It is too big for today's meeting — they will discuss it separately, later.
3. What does Claire offer at the end?
To clarify anything she explained too fast, if people write to her.
