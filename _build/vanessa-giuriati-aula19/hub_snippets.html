Dealing with Problems & Complaints

Stay calm when something goes wrong. Take responsibility instead of making excuses, apologize honestly, and offer a real solution. Use should have / could have + past participle (we should have warned you; we could have avoided this) to own the past. Key words: complaint, apologize, fault, delay, refund, replace, inconvenience, urgent.

Vocabulary

Complaint -- a statement that you are not happy because something is wrong (reclamação)
"We received a complaint about the late order."
Apologize -- to say you are sorry for something you did wrong (pedir desculpas)
"I apologize for the delay."
Fault -- responsibility for a mistake or a problem (culpa)
"It was my fault, and I'm sorry."
Delay -- a situation where something happens later than planned (atraso)
"There was a long delay with the shipment."
Refund -- money given back when something goes wrong (reembolso)
"We offered her a full refund."
Replace -- to put a new thing in place of one that is wrong or broken (substituir/trocar)
"We'll replace the wrong item tomorrow."
Inconvenience -- trouble or difficulty caused to someone (transtorno/incômodo)
"I'm sorry for the inconvenience."
Urgent -- needing immediate action or attention (urgente)
"We sent it by urgent delivery."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

We received an angry complaint from a client. The order was late, and we had sent the wrong item. My first instinct was to defend myself, but instead I chose to apologize honestly. "You're right, and it was our fault," I said. "We should have warned you about the delay much earlier, and we should not have sent the wrong item. We could have avoided this whole situation." Then I offered a solution. "Let me give you a full refund and replace the item by urgent delivery. I'm truly sorry for the inconvenience." The client softened. Taking responsibility, instead of making excuses, turned an angry moment into trust.

2. What did she admit she should have done?
A She should have charged more.

3. Which sentence correctly admits a mistake?
A "We should of sent the right item."
B "We should not have sent the wrong item."
C "We should not have send the wrong item."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Should Have / Could Have

GRAMMAR

Como falar do passado para assumir responsabilidade. / How to talk about the past to take responsibility.

FormEnglishPortuguês
should have + past participle"I should have called you."Era o certo, mas não fiz (arrependimento)
could have + past participle"We could have avoided this."Era possível, mas não aconteceu
should not have + past participle"We should not have sent the wrong item."Foi um erro ter feito isso
Why use itIt is how you take responsibility for the past in an apology. "We should have warned you about the delay."
⚠ CarefulAlways use have + the past participle (called, warned, sent). It sounds like "should've", but you write "should have", never "should of". Don't drop the "have": "I should have called", not "I should called".

Order
