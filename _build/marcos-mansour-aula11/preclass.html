Stage 11.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Passport -- passaporte (the official document for travel)
"May I see your passport, please?"
Boarding pass -- cartão de embarque (the ticket to get on the plane)
"Please have your boarding pass ready."
Immigration -- imigração (passport control when you enter a country)
"All passengers must go through immigration."
Customs -- alfândega (where they check what you bring in)
"Follow the green signs to customs."
Declare -- declarar (to tell officials what you are carrying)
"Do you have anything to declare?"
Baggage -- bagagem (the bags and suitcases you travel with)
"The baggage claim is at carousel five."
Gate -- portão (where you board the plane)
"Boarding starts at gate twenty-two."
Departure -- partida, saída (when a flight leaves)
"The departure is at eight in the evening."
Arrival -- chegada (when a flight reaches its destination)
"Our arrival in Washington is at ten p.m."
Layover -- conexão, escala (a stop between two flights)
"I have a short layover in Miami."

Stage 11.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcos arrives in Washington for the congress. At immigration, an officer asks the usual questions: "May I see your passport, please? What is the purpose of your visit?" Marcos answers clearly: "I am here for a conference." The officer continues: "How long are you staying? Where are you staying? Do you have a return ticket?" Marcos gives short, direct answers: "For five days. At a hotel near the venue. Yes, I do." Finally: "Do you have anything to declare?" Marcos says, "No, nothing to declare." The officer smiles: "Welcome to the United States."

1. Qual é a melhor forma de responder ao oficial de imigração?
A Respostas longas e cheias de detalhes.
B Respostas curtas, claras e diretas.
C Não responder.
2. O que significa "What is the purpose of your visit?"
A "Qual é o seu nome?"
B "Qual é o motivo da sua viagem?"
C "Onde fica o portão?"
3. Which is a correct answer to "How long are you staying?"
A "I am here a conference."
B "For five days."
C "At a hotel."
