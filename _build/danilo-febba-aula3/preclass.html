Vocabulary

Developer -- a person who builds software (desenvolvedor / desenvolvedora)
"Our developers write code."
Analyst -- a person who studies data and information (analista)
"She is a data analyst."
Leader -- a person who guides the team (líder)
"He is a calm leader."
Member -- a person in a group (membro / integrante)
"I am a member of the data team."
Skill -- something you can do well (habilidade / competência)
"Communication is an important skill."
Responsibility -- a duty, something you must do (responsabilidade)
"My responsibility is data quality."
Expert -- a person who knows a lot about something (especialista)
"She is an expert in Python."
Goal -- what you want to achieve (meta / objetivo)
"Our team's goal is to deliver fast."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas. (Repare nos plurais e possessivos em negrito.)

Danilo is the leader of a small data team. He works with five people. The team has two developers, Bruno and Lucas. Their main skill is Python. Maria is the analyst, and her responsibility is data quality. Pedro is an expert in cloud systems; his skills are very strong. Ana is a new member, and her goal is to become a developer. "Our goal is to deliver good data, fast," Danilo says. He is proud of his people.

1. Por que dizemos "two developers" com -s no final?
A Porque a ação aconteceu no passado.
B Porque o plural (mais de um) recebe -s.
C Porque "developer" é verbo.
2. "Her responsibility is data quality" -- a palavra "her" mostra:
A De quem é a responsabilidade (da Maria).
B Uma ação terminada ontem.
C Um plano para o futuro.
3. Qual frase está correta?
A "He works with five persons."
B "He works with five people."
C "He works with five peoples."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Plurals & Possessives

GRAMMAR

Como falar de mais de uma pessoa (plural) e de quem algo pertence (possessivo).

FormUse (PT)Example
noun + -s / -esPlural (mais de um)one developer → two developers
person → peoplePlural irregular (não "persons")two people work here
name + 'sPossessivo (a quem pertence)Rachel's task (a tarefa da Rachel)
my / your / his / her / our / theirAdjetivo possessivo (concorda com o dono)her goal, their skills
plural subject + areSujeito plural usa "are"my colleagues are friendly

Atenção (erro comum): Não diga "the task of Rachel" -- diga "Rachel's task". Não diga "two persons" -- diga "two people". E lembre: "her" concorda com o dono, não com a coisa (a Maria é mulher → her goal).
