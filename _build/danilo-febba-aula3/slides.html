Chapter 3

The Grammar

Plurals and possessives to talk about your team

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have two developers on my team."

"Five people work on the project."

"Rachel's task is data quality."

"Their skills are strong. Her goal is clear."

StructureUseExample
noun + -s / -esPlural (more than one)one developer → two developers
person → peopleIrregular pluraltwo people (NOT two persons)
name + 'sPossessive (it belongs to)Rachel's task
my / your / his / her / our / theirPossessive adjectivetheir skills, her goal

Add -s for plural. Use 's for "belongs to". Possessive adjectives agree with the owner.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The task of Rachel is hard."

✓ RIGHT

"Rachel's task is hard."

✗ WRONG

"I work with two persons."

✓ RIGHT

"I work with two people."

✗ WRONG

"My colleagues is friendly."

✓ RIGHT

"My colleagues are friendly."

Use 's for "belongs to". Use "people" (not persons). Plural subject takes "are".

Dialogue

Tell Me About Your Team

R
So Danilo, tell me about your team. How many people do you work with?
D
I work with five people. We are a small data team.
R
Nice! Who are the members?
D
We have two developers. Their main skill is Python. And Maria is our analyst.
R
And what is your role?
D
I am the leader. My responsibility is the team's goals.
R
My team is bigger. I work with eight people in the product department.
D
Eight people! Who is your leader?
R
My manager is Sofia. Her skill is planning, and our goal is a new app.
D
That sounds like a great team, Rachel!

To make a new app.
