Lesson 5 -- The Things I've Done

Podcast
The Moth Radio Hour -- "American Dreams"

The best possible listening for tonight's grammar. Every storyteller on that stage is doing exactly one thing: telling you what they have done with their life. Listen for how often they open with "I have never..." or "Have you ever..." before they get to the dates.

Tip: count how many times you hear "have" plus a past participle in the first five minutes. Then tell one of your own experiences the same way.

Podcast
Urban Exodus, E69 -- Academics leave city life for a farm in the Australian bush

Louise and David spend the whole hour describing a turning point: what they had done before, what they have done since, and what they have never regretted. Same shape as your own story, in an Australian accent.

Tip: they never give exact dates - notice that, and notice which tense that forces them into.

Series
The Repair Shop -- Season 1, Episode 1 (full episode)

Watch it once more with tonight's ear. Every person who walks into that barn tells the story of an object: where it has been, who has owned it, what it has survived. It is the present perfect doing the emotional work of the whole programme.

Tip: listen for "it has been in the family", "I have had it since...". Then say one sentence about the oldest object in your own house.
