The Things I've Done -- Experiences of a Lifetime

Talking about what you have done, without saying when. Key words: achievement, to take up, to give up, bucket list, once in a lifetime, to regret, milestone, to come across, adventurous, comfort zone, to look back on, turning point. Structures: present perfect for experience (ever, never), been vs gone, and when a date forces the past simple.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Achievement -- something difficult that you succeeded in doing
"Learning to swim at thirty-four was my proudest achievement."
To take up -- to start doing a new activity regularly
"I took up furniture restoration about four years ago."
To give up -- to stop doing something you used to do
"I gave up that job four months later."
Bucket list -- the list of things you want to do before you die
"My sister has a bucket list with forty things on it."
Once in a lifetime -- happening only one time in your whole life, and unforgettable
"It was a once in a lifetime trip, and I nearly said no."
To regret -- to feel sorry about something you did, or did not do
"I have never regretted saying yes to something frightening."
Milestone -- an important event that marks a stage of your life
"Buying that house was a real milestone for us."
To come across -- to find something by chance, without looking for it
"I came across the cabinet at a street market."
Adventurous -- willing to try new things, even when they feel risky
"I have never been very adventurous, and I know it."
Comfort zone -- the situations where you feel safe because you know them
"Speaking English is completely outside my comfort zone."
To look back on -- to think about something that happened in your past
"When I look back on it, that was the best decision I made."
Turning point -- the moment when an important change begins
"That dinner was the turning point of my whole life."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana has lived in two very different places, and she has never thought of herself as an adventurous person. She likes her comfort zone. She has never written a bucket list, and she has never climbed a mountain, and she is completely at peace with both of those facts. But she has done one enormous thing: she sold most of what she owned and left the city. She did that in 2019 - notice the date, and notice the tense. When she looks back on it, that decision was the turning point of her life and her proudest achievement. Since then she has taken up furniture restoration, she has come across pieces in street markets that nobody else wanted, and she has rescued two dogs. She has never regretted any of it. Not for one single day.

1. Why is it "she did that in 2019" and not "she has done that in 2019"?
A Because the sentence names a specific time, and a date forces the past simple.
B Because the action was not important enough for the present perfect.
C Because "do" has no present perfect form.
2. "She has never regretted any of it." Why is there no "not"?
A Because the sentence is a question.
B Because "never" is already the negative, so "not" would be double.
C Because "regret" is an irregular verb.
3. Which sentence correctly describes her experience with no date?
A "She has taken up furniture restoration."
B "She has took up furniture restoration."
C "She has taken up furniture restoration in 2021."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect for Experience

GRAMMAR

The tense you use when WHEN does not matter.

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience in your life so far. The time is NOT stated.I have lived in three countries.
everIn questions only.Have you ever been to Portugal?
neverThe negative. Never add "not" as well.I have never climbed a mountain.
been vs gonebeen = went and came back · gone = went and is still thereShe has been to the market (she is back). / She has gone to the vet (she is out).
The switchThe moment you say WHEN (in 2019, last year, yesterday), you must use the past simple.I moved here in 2019.
In one lineNo date → have done. Date → did. That is the whole rule.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real conversation.

Someone asks about your experience with old furniture. You answer:
A "I have restored several pieces."
B "I have restored several pieces in 2021."
C "I have restore several pieces."
You want to say you moved here in 2019. The correct form is:
A "I have moved here in 2019."
B "I moved here in 2019."
C "I have been moved here in 2019."
You want to say you have zero regrets. The natural sentence is:
A "I have not never regretted it."
B "I have never regretted it."
C "I never have not regretted it."
Your friend went to the market and is back home now. You say:
A "She has gone to the market."
B "She has been to the market."
C "She has went to the market."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

Someone at a dinner asks you: "So what have you done with your life?" Answer with experiences and not with dates: three things you have done, one thing you have never done and would like to, your proudest achievement, and the biggest turning point you have had. If you name a specific year, remember to switch to the past simple. Take your time.
