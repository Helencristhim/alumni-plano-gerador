Chapter 3: The Code

Have You Ever...?

Experience, without a date

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have lived in three countries."

"Have you ever been to Portugal?"

"I have never climbed a mountain."

"I learned to swim in 2013."

Three of the green ones never say when. The blue one does. What changed?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience in your life so far. The time is NOT stated.I have lived in three countries.
ever / neverever in questions · never = the negative (do not add "not")Have you ever...? / I have never...
been vs gonebeen = went and came back · gone = went and is still thereShe has been to Lisbon. / She has gone to Lisbon.
Past SimpleThe moment you say WHEN, you must switch.I learned to swim in 2013.

The rule in one line: no date → have done. Date → did.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have learned to swim in 2013."
"I learned to swim in 2013."
"I have not never climbed a mountain."
"I have never climbed a mountain."
"She is back. She has gone to Lisbon."
"She is back. She has been to Lisbon."

A date forces the past simple. Never is already negative, so it never takes "not". And been means she came back; gone means she is still there.
