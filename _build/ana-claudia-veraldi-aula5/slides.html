Chapter 3: The Code

Yesterday vs So Far

I tested · I have found

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Yesterday, I tested the checkout page."

"I have found three bugs so far."

"I fixed the login bug on Monday."

"The team has just finished the sprint."

Why green when you say a finished time, but orange for the news right now?

FormUseSignal words
Past Simple
verb + -ed / irregular		A finished action at a specific past timeyesterday, last week, on Monday, at 9 am, two days ago
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		News and results that matter now, with no specific timejust, already, yet, so far, recently

Never put a specific past time with the present perfect: say "I found it yesterday," never "I have found it yesterday".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have finished the tests yesterday."
"I finished the tests yesterday."
"Yesterday, I have closed two bugs."
"Yesterday, I closed two bugs."
"The team has ran all the tests."
"The team has run all the tests."

A finished time (yesterday, on Monday) always takes the past simple. The news now (so far, just, already) takes have / has + past participle. Learn your irregular participles: run, not ran; written, not wrote.

Thanks. One action item: can we sync up about the release this afternoon?
