The Code -- Grammar Discovery

Present simple, past simple, present perfect

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"I manage procurement for the APAC region."

"We negotiated the contract last quarter."

"I have worked with this vendor for three years."

"The shipment arrived yesterday, but we haven't checked it yet."

What do the orange words have in common? Can you spot three different tenses?

The Rule

Three Tenses

TenseWhenSignal WordsExample
Present SimpleRoutines, facts, habitsevery day, usually, alwaysI manage procurement.
Past SimpleCompleted actions with timeyesterday, last week, in 2024We negotiated the contract.
Present PerfectPast action with present result / durationalready, yet, for, sinceI have worked here for 3 years.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I worked here for five years.
I have worked here for five years.
We already sent the shipment.
We have already sent the shipment.

Use present perfect with "for", "since", "already", and "yet" -- the action connects to NOW.

I have already contacted the logistics team, and they have confirmed that the delivery date needs to be moved to July 25th. This impacts our Q3 milestone.

Could you please:
