Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Supply chain -- the entire system of producing and delivering a product
"We need to optimize our supply chain to reduce costs." (Cadeia de suprimentos)
Vendor -- a company or person that sells goods or services
"Our main vendor is based in Singapore." (Fornecedor)
Procurement -- the process of finding and buying goods for a company
"The procurement team handles all equipment purchases." (Aquisição)
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest or concern in a project
"We briefed all stakeholders before the meeting." (Parte interessada)
Compliance -- following rules, regulations, or standards
"All vendors must meet our compliance requirements." (Conformidade)
Logistics -- the detailed coordination of a complex operation
"The logistics of the rollout were challenging." (Logística)
Milestone -- a significant event or achievement in a project
"Reaching Q3 targets was a major milestone." (Marco)
Forecast -- a prediction of future trends based on data
"The demand forecast looks strong for next quarter." (Previsão)
Shipment -- goods sent from one place to another
"The shipment arrived two days late." (Remessa)
Delivery -- the act of bringing goods to a destination
"We expect delivery by the end of the month." (Entrega)
Leverage -- to use something to maximum advantage
"We can leverage our relationship with the vendor." (Alavancar)
Deployment -- the process of putting resources into use
"The terminal deployment is scheduled for October." (Implantação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Fabiana works in the supply chain department at Bloomberg. Every day, she coordinates with vendors and manages procurement for specialized trading equipment. Last quarter, her team handled a critical shipment delay when a logistics partner failed to meet compliance standards. "We have dealt with similar situations before," she explains. "The key is having a solid forecast and keeping all stakeholders informed. I have worked at Bloomberg for several years, and every milestone we reach teaches us something about leveraging our vendor relationships for better delivery outcomes."

1. Which tense does Fabiana use to describe her daily routine?
A Present simple: "works", "coordinates", "manages"
B Present perfect: "has worked", "has coordinated"
C Past simple: "worked", "coordinated"
2. Which tense describes the shipment delay as a completed past event?
A Present perfect: "have handled"
B Past simple: "handled", "failed"
C Present simple: "handles", "fails"
3. Why does Fabiana say "We have dealt with similar situations" using the present perfect?
A Because it happened at a specific time last year.
B Because it is happening right now.
C Because the experience connects past actions to the present moment.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Tense Review (B2 Diagnostic)

GRAMMAR

Revisão dos 3 tempos verbais presentes no texto. / Review of the 3 tenses found in the text.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present SimpleRotinas, fatos permanentes / Routines, permanent factsFabiana works in supply chain.
Past SimpleAções completas no passado / Completed past actionsHer team handled a delay last quarter.
Present PerfectExperiência acumulada, conexão passado-presente / Accumulated experienceWe have dealt with similar situations.
Key difference / Diferença-chavePast simple = QUANDO importa ("last quarter"). Present perfect = a EXPERIÊNCIA importa ("before", "for years").
