Lesson 9 -- Asking Smart Questions in Meetings

Series
The Resident -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

Doctors and nurses run fast meetings and ask sharp questions under pressure: What do we do now? Have you checked the chart? When did the symptoms start? Listen for how they ask questions in different tenses.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down five questions the characters ask and label each tense (present, past, present perfect, future). Then turn three of them into your own meeting questions.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "The Art of Asking Better Questions"

The hosts explain how good questions move a meeting forward: how to clarify, follow up, bring up a concern, and ask for input. Full of natural language for taking part in a meeting.

Note three questions the speakers recommend. Then record yourself asking four meeting questions, using at least three new words from today, such as clarify, bring up, and feedback.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "How to Form QUESTIONS in English"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: the helper verbs do, did, have, and will, the word order of questions, and how to make polite, indirect questions.

After watching, write eight questions for a real meeting: two present simple, two past, two present perfect, and two future. Use clarify, concern, and input at least once each.
