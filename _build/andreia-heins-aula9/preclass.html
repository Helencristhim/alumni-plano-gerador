Asking Smart Questions in Meetings -- The Meeting Q&A

Question forms in every tense (What do you think? When did it start? Have you finished? Will it work?) plus polite, indirect questions (Could you tell me...). New vocabulary: clarify, follow up, elaborate, bring up, point out, concern, assumption, rephrase, input, feedback.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clarify -- to make something clearer by explaining or asking
"Could you clarify when the simulations start?" (Esclarecer)
Follow up -- to ask a further question based on an answer
"I would like to follow up on your last point." (Dar seguimento)
Elaborate -- to explain something in more detail
"Can you elaborate on the new schedule?" (Detalhar)
Bring up -- to mention or introduce a topic in a meeting
"I want to bring up a safety concern." (Mencionar)
Point out -- to make someone notice something important
"Let me point out a problem with the timeline." (Destacar)
Concern -- a worry you want to ask or talk about
"My main concern is patient safety." (Preocupação)
Assumption -- something you believe is true without checking it
"We should not make that assumption without data." (Suposição)
Rephrase -- to say something again in different words
"Could you rephrase the question, please?" (Reformular)
Input -- opinions and ideas that people contribute
"I would like your input on the budget." (Contribuição)
Feedback -- comments about how good or useful something is
"Can I get your feedback on the plan?" (Retorno)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia is in a planning meeting. Before she speaks, she thinks about her questions. "What do we need before the first workshop?" she asks David. Then she wants to clarify the dates: "When did the supplier confirm the new console?" David answers, and Andreia decides to follow up: "Have we trained enough nurses for the night shift?" She also wants to bring up a concern: "What will we do if the equipment fails during a session?" David asks her to elaborate, so she explains her idea. At the end, he asks for her feedback, and he thanks her for her input.

1. Which helper verb does Andreia use for her past simple question?
A "did" (When did the supplier confirm...)
B "will" (What will we do...)
C "have" (Have we trained...)
2. What does Andreia want to bring up in the meeting?
A A concern about what happens if the equipment fails.
B A complaint about her salary.
C A request to leave early.
3. Why does David ask Andreia to elaborate?
A Because he wants her to explain her idea in more detail.
B Because he wants her to stop talking.
C Because he did not hear the question.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Question Forms

GRAMMAR

Usado para perguntar em qualquer tempo verbal. / Used to ask questions in any tense.

Tense / TempoHelper / AuxiliarExample / Exemplo
Present simpledo / doesWhat do you think?
Past simpledidWhen did it start?
Present continuousam / is / areWhat are you testing?
Present perfecthave / hasHave you finished?
FuturewillWill it work?

Key point / Ponto-chave: a ordem é sempre QUESTION WORD + HELPER + SUBJECT + MAIN VERB. O auxiliar carrega o tempo, então o verbo principal fica na forma base: diga "When did it start?", nunca "When did it started?". E nunca tire o auxiliar: "What do you think?", não "What you think?".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best question for each situation.

You want to ask about the coordinator's opinion (present simple). Which is correct?
A "What do you think about the plan?"
B "What you think about the plan?"
C "What thinks you about the plan?"
You want to ask when something happened (past simple). Which is correct?
A "When did the training started?"
B "When did the training start?"
C "When the training did start?"
You want to ask politely for clarification. Which is best?
A "Could you tell me when the meeting starts?"
B "Could you tell me when does the meeting start?"
C "Tell me when starts the meeting?"
