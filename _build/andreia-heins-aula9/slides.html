Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Question forms in every tense (do, did, have, will)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What word comes right after the question word in each tense?

"What do you think about the plan?"

"When did the training start?"

"Have you finished the report?"

"Will the supplier deliver on time?"

Grammar Rule

Question Forms

Tense / HelperExample
Present simple: do / doesWhat do you think about the plan?
Past simple: didWhen did the training start?
Present continuous: am / is / areWhat are you testing today?
Present perfect: have / hasHave you finished the report?
Future: willWill the supplier deliver on time?
Polite / indirectCould you tell me when the meeting starts?

Key idea: the order is always QUESTION WORD + HELPER + SUBJECT + MAIN VERB. The helper carries the tense, so the main verb stays in its base form: say "When did it start?", never "When did it started?".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you think about the plan?"
"What do you think about the plan?"

Every English question needs a helper verb. For present simple, add do or does. Never drop the helper.
