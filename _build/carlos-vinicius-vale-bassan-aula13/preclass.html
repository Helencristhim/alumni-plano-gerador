Aula 13 -- Pre-class

Block 3 -- Measuring the Change & Reporting to the Board

Take the change past the people and report the results to the board. Track metrics against a baseline, compare to a benchmark, show adoption, say what is on track, and report the gap honestly. New vocabulary: metric, baseline, target, benchmark, adoption, on track, headcount, dashboard. Grammar focus: present perfect continuous (have/has been + verb-ing) for progress over time.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Metric -- a specific number you track to measure progress
"Adoption is the metric the board cares about most." (métrica / indicador)
Baseline -- the starting point you measure all progress against
"We have tracked every metric against the baseline." (linha de base / ponto de partida)
Target -- the goal number you are aiming to reach
"Adoption is at eighty-five against a target of ninety." (meta)
Benchmark -- a standard you compare your results against
"Our adoption is above the industry benchmark." (referência / parâmetro)
Adoption -- how many people actually use the new system or process
"Adoption has been climbing every week since launch." (adesão / uso)
On Track -- heading to the target as planned, not falling behind
"Four of the five metrics are on track." (no caminho certo / dentro do prazo)
Headcount -- the number of people on a team or in a company
"We have not hit the headcount savings target yet." (número de pessoas / quadro)
Dashboard -- one screen that shows all the key metrics at a glance
"The dashboard shows every metric against its target." (painel)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect Continuous

GRAMMAR

Para relatar progresso ao longo do tempo, use o present perfect continuous: have / has + been + verbo-ing. Ele descreve uma ação que começou no passado e continua até agora, com ênfase na duração ou no progresso: "Adoption has been climbing every week"; "We have been tracking five metrics". Use has com he/she/it e have com I/we/they; nunca esqueça o been (não "has climbing"). Use since para o ponto de início (since the town hall) e for para a duração (for three months). Compare com o present perfect simples (Aula 10), que foca no resultado pronto: "we have hit the target". / Use the present perfect continuous (have/has been + -ing) for an action that started in the past and is still going on, stressing duration or progress; use the present perfect simple for the finished result.

FormExample
I / we / they + have been + -ing"We have been tracking five metrics."
He / she / it + has been + -ing"Adoption has been climbing every week."
since + a start point"Engagement has been rising since the town hall."
for + a length of time"The teams have been using it for three months."
