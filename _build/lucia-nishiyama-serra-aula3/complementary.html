Lesson 3 -- Past Simple vs. Present Perfect in Interviews

YouTube · BBC Learning English
6 Minute Grammar: Present Perfect and Past Simple

Six minutes, two presenters, and the exact choice you practiced tonight: past simple for finished events, present perfect for experience that still counts. They give three clear rules and say the sentences at natural speed, so your ear starts to hear which tense a native speaker reaches for and why.

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, pause after each rule and build one sentence about your own career for each tense: "In 2015, I led...", "Since then, I have led...".

Interview Skills
The Best "Tell Me About Yourself" Answer (Self Made Millennial)

A recruiter breaks down the opening question of almost every American interview. Watch how a strong answer moves from what you did to why it matters now -- exactly the past-to-present bridge you built tonight. Notice how often the best answers use the present perfect to show relevance.

Tip: after watching, write your own answer to "Tell me about yourself" in three sentences -- one past simple fact with a year, then two present perfect sentences ("I have led...", "I have owned..."). Say it out loud until it feels natural.
