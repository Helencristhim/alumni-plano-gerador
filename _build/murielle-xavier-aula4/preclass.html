Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

What Is Happening Right Now -- Describing Live Situations in Meetings

Narrating live action in meetings and operations: who is presenting, what teams are doing, how projects are going. New words: currently, at the moment, ongoing, update, progress, review, on track, behind schedule. Grammar: present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) -- affirmative, negative, and question.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Currently -- at this present time; now (atualmente)
"I am currently leading two projects."
At the moment -- right now; at this exact time (neste momento)
"At the moment, the team is in a meeting."
Ongoing -- still happening; not finished yet (em andamento)
"The system change is still ongoing."
Update -- new information about how something is going (atualização)
"I am sending you a quick update by email."
Progress -- forward movement toward finishing a goal (progresso)
"The team is making good progress this week."
Review -- to look at and check something carefully (revisar)
"Two managers are reviewing the hiring plan."
On track -- going as planned, with no delay (no caminho certo)
"The project is on track for next month."
Behind schedule -- late; slower than the plan (atrasado)
"We are a little behind schedule on the report."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

It is a busy morning at Minerva Foods. Right now, the finance team is presenting the quarterly results, and two managers are reviewing the new hiring plan. Murielle is working on a report at the moment, and Sofia is updating the slides. The HR team is not hiring new people this quarter; instead, they are training the managers they already have. The main project is still ongoing, but it is on track and the team is making good progress. "Are we falling behind schedule?" a manager asks. "No, everything is going well," Murielle answers.

1. Why do we write "is presenting" and "are reviewing"?
A Because these actions finished in the past.
B Because these actions are happening right now (present continuous).
C Because they are routines that happen every day.
2. "The HR team is not hiring this quarter." This sentence is:
A A negative in the present continuous (is + not + verb-ing).
B A question in the present continuous.
C A past simple sentence.
3. Which question is correct in the present continuous?
A "We falling behind schedule?"
B "Do we falling behind schedule?"
C "Are we falling behind schedule?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Use o present continuous para ações acontecendo AGORA. A fórmula é am/is/are + verbo-ing. (Use the present continuous for actions happening now.)

FormUse (PT)Example
am / is / are + verb-ingAção acontecendo agora / action nowI am working / she is presenting / we are reviewing
am / is / are + not + verb-ingNegativa / negativeWe are not hiring this quarter.
Am / Is / Are + subject + verb-ing?Pergunta / questionIs she presenting now?
present simple vs continuousRotina vs agora / routine vs nowI usually lead meetings, but now I am taking notes.
right now / at the moment / currentlyMarcadores de ação presente: The team is reviewing the plan at the moment.
