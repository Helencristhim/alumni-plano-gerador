What Is Happening Right Now -- Describing Live Situations in Meetings

Narrating live action in meetings and operations: who is presenting, what teams are doing, how projects are going. New words: currently, at the moment, ongoing, update, progress, review, on track, behind schedule, deadline, backlog, catch up. Grammar: present continuous vs present simple (now vs routine) and present perfect continuous for actions still in progress (have been working).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Currently -- at this present time; now (atualmente)
"I am currently leading two hiring projects."
At the moment -- right now; at this exact time (neste momento)
"At the moment, the team is in a meeting."
Ongoing -- still happening; not finished yet (em andamento)
"The system change is still ongoing."
Update -- new information about how something is going (atualização)
"I am sending you a quick update by email."
Progress -- forward movement toward finishing a goal (progresso)
"The team has been making good progress this week."
Review -- to look at and check something carefully (revisar)
"Two managers are reviewing the hiring plan."
On track -- going as planned, with no delay (no caminho certo)
"The project is on track for the deadline."
Behind schedule -- late; slower than the plan (atrasado)
"We are a little behind schedule on the report."
Deadline -- the date or time by which something must be finished (prazo final)
"We still have three days before the deadline."
Backlog -- a pile of work that is waiting and still needs to be done (acúmulo de trabalho)
"The team has been clearing the backlog of open roles."
Catch up -- to do work you did not finish and reach where you should be (colocar em dia)
"I am trying to catch up on my emails this morning."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

It is a busy morning at Minerva Foods. Murielle usually leads the weekly meeting, but right now she is taking notes while the finance team is presenting the quarterly results. Two managers are reviewing the new hiring plan, and Sofia is updating the slides. The HR team has been working on the backlog of open roles all week, and they have been making good progress. They are not hiring new people this quarter; instead, they are training the managers they already have. The main project is still ongoing, but it is on track for the deadline. "Are we catching up on the reports?" a manager asks. "Yes, we have been catching up since Monday," Murielle answers.

1. Murielle "usually leads" the meeting, but today she "is taking" notes. Why the two different forms?
A Both describe a fixed daily routine.
B "Usually leads" is a routine (present simple); "is taking" is happening now (present continuous).
C "Is taking" is a finished past action.
2. Why do we say "The HR team has been working on the backlog all week"?
A The action started in the past and is still going on now (present perfect continuous).
B The action is a finished routine that never changes.
C The action will only start next week.
3. Which sentence correctly contrasts a routine with what is happening right now?
A "I am usually leading meetings, but now I take notes."
B "I usually lead meetings, but right now I am taking notes."
C "I usually leading meetings, but right now I taking notes."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous vs Present Simple (+ Present Perfect Continuous)

GRAMMAR

Use o present continuous (am/is/are + verbo-ing) para o que acontece AGORA, e o present simple para rotinas. Para uma ação que começou no passado e ainda continua, use o present perfect continuous (have/has been + verbo-ing). (Now vs routine vs still in progress.)

FormUse (PT)Example
present simple: subject + verbRotina, sempre / routine, alwaysI usually lead the weekly meeting.
present continuous: am / is / are + verb-ingAgora, temporário / now, temporaryRight now I am taking notes / she is presenting.
negative & question (continuous)Negativa e pergunta / negative & questionWe are not hiring now. Is she presenting?
present perfect continuous: have / has been + verb-ingComeçou antes e ainda continua / started before, still goingWe have been working on the policy all morning.
now vs routine vs still in progressI usually review plans (routine), today I am reviewing the backlog (now), and I have been reviewing it since Monday (still in progress).
