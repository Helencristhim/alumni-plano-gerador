Chapter 3

The Present Continuous

Talking about actions happening now, as we speak

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The team is reviewing the numbers."

"We are hiring three new analysts."

"I am working on the report now."

"Is she presenting right now?"

FormUseExample
am / is / are + verb-ingAction happening nowI am working / she is presenting / we are hiring
am / is / are + not + verb-ingNegativeWe are not hiring this quarter.
Am / Is / Are + subject + verb-ing?QuestionIs she presenting now?
present simple vs continuousRoutine vs right nowI usually lead meetings, but now I am taking notes.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The team reviewing the numbers."

✓ RIGHT

"The team is reviewing the numbers."

✗ WRONG

"She presents in the meeting right now."

✓ RIGHT

"She is presenting in the meeting right now."

✗ WRONG

"We not hiring this quarter."

✓ RIGHT

"We are not hiring this quarter."

The present continuous always needs two parts: be (am/is/are) plus the verb with -ing.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Telling a colleague what is happening live

Dialogue

A Call During a Busy Morning

L
Hi Murielle, is this a good time? What is happening over there?
M
It is busy! Right now the finance team is presenting the quarterly results.
L
Nice. And what are you working on at the moment?
M
Two managers and I are reviewing the new hiring plan. It is ongoing.
L
Are you hiring a lot of people this quarter?
M
No, we are not hiring right now. We are training the managers we have.
L
Good plan. Is the project still on track?
M
Yes, it is on track. We are not behind schedule. I am sending you an update now.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is the finance team doing right now?
They are presenting the quarterly results.
2. Is Murielle's team hiring this quarter?
No, they are not hiring; they are training the managers they have.
3. Is the project on track or behind schedule?
It is on track; it is not behind schedule.

"She is presenting to the board at the moment." (right now = present continuous)

"We are not hiring this quarter." (negative needs are + not)

"Are you working on the report?" (question starts with Are)
