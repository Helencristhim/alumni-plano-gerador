Chapter 3

The Present Continuous

Talking about actions happening now, as we speak

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I usually lead the meeting, but today I am taking notes."

"The team is reviewing the numbers right now."

"We have been working on the backlog all morning."

"Are you catching up on the reports?"

FormUseExample
present simple: subject + verbRoutine, alwaysI usually lead the weekly meeting.
present continuous: am / is / are + verb-ingHappening now, temporaryRight now I am taking notes / she is presenting.
negative & question (continuous)Negative / questionWe are not hiring now. Is she presenting?
present perfect continuous: have / has been + verb-ingStarted before, still going nowWe have been working on the backlog all morning.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She presents in the meeting right now."

✓ RIGHT

"She is presenting in the meeting right now."

✗ WRONG

"The team reviewing the numbers."

✓ RIGHT

"The team is reviewing the numbers."

✗ WRONG

"We are working on the backlog since Monday."

✓ RIGHT

"We have been working on the backlog since Monday."

Use the present continuous (be + -ing) for now, and the present perfect continuous (have/has been + -ing) for something that started earlier and is still going.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Telling a colleague what is happening live

Dialogue

A Call During a Busy Morning

L
Hi Murielle, is this a good time? What is happening over there?
M
It is busy! Right now the finance team is presenting the quarterly results, and I am taking notes.
L
You usually lead those meetings, right? So what are you working on this week?
M
Two managers and I have been reviewing the hiring plan all week. We are clearing the backlog of open roles.
L
Are you hiring a lot of people this quarter?
M
No, we are not hiring right now. We are training the managers we have.
L
Good plan. Is the project still on track for the deadline?
M
Yes, it is on track. We have been catching up since Monday, so we are not behind schedule. I am sending you an update now.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is the finance team doing right now?
They are presenting the quarterly results.
2. Is Murielle's team hiring this quarter?
No, they are not hiring; they are training the managers they have.
3. Is the project on track or behind schedule?
It is on track; it is not behind schedule.

"The team is reviewing the backlog now." (add the verb be: is)

"Are you catching up on the reports?" (question starts with Are)
