Lesson 4 -- What Is Happening Right Now -- Describing Live Situations in Meetings

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast (Harvard Business Review) -- any episode on running meetings

Leaders describe what their teams are doing now, what is on track, and what is behind schedule. You will hear the present continuous used naturally to give live status updates. Direct connection to Lesson 4: describing live action in meetings and operations.

Tip: write down 5 sentences in the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) that you hear, then use them to describe your own week.

Series
The Office (US) -- any meeting scene in the conference room

Watch a meeting unfold live: people are presenting, taking notes, reviewing ideas, and interrupting each other. Notice how characters narrate what is happening right now. Connection to Lesson 4: live action in meetings and the present continuous.

Tip: pause a meeting scene and describe it out loud: "Michael is talking, Jim is smiling, Pam is taking notes."

YouTube
"Present Continuous Tense -- am, is, are + verb-ing" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on the present continuous: how to form affirmatives, negatives, and questions for actions happening now, and how it is different from the present simple. Connection to Lesson 4: the exact grammar you use to describe a live situation.

Tip: pause and build your own present-continuous sentences with present, review, update, hire, and work.
