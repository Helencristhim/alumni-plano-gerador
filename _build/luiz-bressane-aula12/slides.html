Chapter 3: Conditionals

If This, Then That

first conditional (real) vs second conditional (hypothetical)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

If counsel continues, I will object.

If the question calls for speculation, the judge will sustain it.

If the court allowed that, it would prejudice the jury.

If I were the prosecutor, I would withdraw the question.

TypeIf-clauseResult clauseUse
Firstif + present simplewill + base verbreal, likely
Secondif + past simplewould + base verbunlikely, hypothetical

Never put will or would inside the if-clause. With the verb "to be" in the second conditional, use "were" for every subject: If I were the judge...

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If counsel will continue, I object."

✓ RIGHT

"If counsel continues, I will object."

✗ WRONG

"If I was the judge, I would overrule it."

✓ RIGHT

"If I were the judge, I would overrule it."

The if-clause carries the condition, never the modal: keep will and would in the result clause only. In the second conditional, "to be" becomes "were" for every subject.

Chapter 4: In the Courtroom

Objection and Ruling

Luiz objects; the judge sustains or overrules

Dialogue

At the Bench

J
Counsel, you are on your feet. What is your objection?
L
Objection, Your Honor. The question calls for speculation. If the witness answers, the jury will hear a guess, not a fact.
J
Sustained. Rephrase your question. And please do not interject again before I rule.
L
Thank you. I also move to strike the previous answer; it had no foundation.
J
On what ground is it inadmissible?
L
Relevance, Your Honor. If that statement stays on the record, it would be prejudicial to my client.
J
Approach. We will settle this at a sidebar, away from the jury.
L
Thank you, Your Honor. If the court overrules me, I will note it for appeal.
Comprehension

At the Bench

1. What was the ground for the first objection?
Speculation.
2. How did the judge rule on it?
She sustained it.
3. Why does the previous answer have no foundation?
No basis was established before it, so it is inadmissible.
4. Where will the dispute be settled?
At a sidebar, away from the jury.
