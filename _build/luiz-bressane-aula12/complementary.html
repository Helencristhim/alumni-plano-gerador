The Practice (Hulu) — Season 1, Episode 1: “Pilot”

A Boston defense firm fights through a tense trial. Watch how the lawyers rise to object the moment a question turns improper, name a single ground (relevance, foundation, speculation), and react when the judge sustains or overrules them — exactly tonight's skill.

Dica: Assista com legenda em inglês e anote cada objection que ouvir. Para cada uma, escreva o ground e reescreva a frase como um condicional ("If the witness answers, the jury will hear a guess").

Opening Arguments — an episode on courtroom objections

Two lawyers break down when to object and what each ground really means: relevance, foundation, speculation, and when a question is argumentative. A natural B2 model of formal, well-timed interruptions in real legal English.

Dica: Ouça 15 minutos a 1x. Pause sempre que ouvir um ground de objection e diga em voz alta a objection completa antes de continuar.

BBC Learning English — “Conditionals: first and second” (6 Minute Grammar)

A concise, native-speaker review of the first and second conditional: if + present with will, if + past with would, and why "were" is used for every subject. A clean reinforcement of tonight's grammar before the homework is recorded.

Dica: Assista, pause, e escreva cinco objections como condicionais ("If the court allowed that, it would prejudice the jury"). Grave as oito objections do dever de casa em voz alta.
