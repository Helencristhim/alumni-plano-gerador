Getting Things Done Through Others -- Delegation

Entregar a tarefa E a autoridade — e parar de ser o gargalo do próprio time. Key words: to delegate, to hand over, deliverable, workstream, to loop in, bandwidth, direct report, to outsource, to chase up, to onboard, remit, backlog. Structures: causative have/get — have/get + objeto + particípio (o resultado sem o autor), have + pessoa + infinitivo SEM to, get + pessoa + TO + infinitivo.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Sem estas palavras, delegar em inglês vira "please" e "thank you" — e isso não delega nada.

To delegate -- to give someone a task AND the authority to do it (delegar)
"He delegates the task but keeps the decision, so nothing moves."
To hand over -- to pass responsibility for something to someone else (repassar, transferir)
"I handed the monthly close over to my controller in January."
Deliverable -- a concrete output someone owes you by a date (entregável)
"Name the deliverable and the date, or it will never arrive."
Workstream -- one parallel track of work inside a bigger project (frente de trabalho)
"Each workstream has one owner. Never two."
To loop in -- to add someone to a conversation they need to be in (incluir na conversa)
"Loop me in only if the number moves more than five percent."
Bandwidth -- the capacity someone has to take on more work (capacidade, disponibilidade)
"She does not have the bandwidth, and saying yes would be a lie."
Direct report -- a person who reports to you (subordinado direto)
"I have six direct reports and I was doing the work of three of them."
To outsource -- to pay an outside company to do the work (terceirizar)
"We outsourced the payroll and got two weeks a month back."
To chase up -- to follow up on something that is late (cobrar, dar follow-up)
"If you are chasing it up every week, you have not really delegated it."
To onboard -- to bring a new person or vendor up to speed (integrar, fazer o onboarding)
"We had the new controller onboarded in three weeks."
Remit -- the scope of what someone is responsible for (alçada, escopo de responsabilidade)
"That decision is inside her remit, so it is not my call."
Backlog -- the pile of work that is waiting to be done (fila de trabalho, backlog)
"My backlog was not a workload problem. It was a delegation problem."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

For two years Felipe told himself he had a workload problem. He had six direct reports and he was doing the work of three of them. His backlog was not a workload problem; it was a delegation problem. He would delegate a task and keep the decision, which is not delegation at all — it is just moving the work and keeping the worry. He was still chasing the monthly close up every week, and a task you chase every week has not been handed over. So he changed it. He had the payroll outsourced, which gave his team four days a month back. He had the new vendor onboarded in three weeks. He had his controller rebuild the forecast model — note the form: have + person + verb, with no to. And he finally got his FP&A director to take the monthly close off his desk — note this form too: get + person + to + verb. The deliverable was named, the date was fixed, and, for the first time, she signed it off herself. It was inside her remit. He asked to be looped in only if a number moved more than five percent. He had the audit trail reviewed once a quarter — and notice that in that sentence nobody is told who reviewed it, because it does not matter.

1. "I had the numbers audited before the board met." Quem auditou os números?
A O Felipe auditou pessoalmente.
B Outra pessoa auditou — e a frase de propósito NÃO diz quem, porque o que importa é o resultado.
C Ninguém auditou ainda.
2. Por que "I had my controller rebuild" não tem "to", mas "I got her to take" tem?
A Porque depois de have + pessoa o infinitivo vem SEM to, e depois de get + pessoa o to é obrigatório.
B Porque "rebuild" é irregular e "take" é regular.
C Porque a primeira frase está no passado e a segunda no presente.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A I had my controller to rebuild the model.
B I had my controller rebuild the model.
C I had my controller rebuilding the model.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Causative have / get

GRAMMAR

Como dizer que o trabalho foi feito — sem dizer que foi você que fez (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
have + objeto + particípioO trabalho foi feito. QUEM fez é invisível — e é esse o ponto. The result matters, not the hands.I had the numbers audited.
get + objeto + particípioMesmo sentido, um pouco mais informal, com um toque de esforço. Slightly more informal.We got the payroll outsourced.
have + PESSOA + infinitivo SEM toVocê nomeia a pessoa. Depois de have, NÃO existe to. Soa a AUTORIDADE.I had my controller rebuild the model.
get + PESSOA + TO + infinitivoVocê nomeia a pessoa. Depois de get, o to É OBRIGATÓRIO. Soa a PERSUASÃO.I got her to rebuild the model.
A nuance que o board escutahave = você tinha a alçada. get = você teve de convencer. Escolha de propósito.I had him do it. / I got him to do it.
Por que isso importaSem a causativa, o brasileiro diz "I audited the numbers" — e assume autoria de trabalho que não fez.NÃO: I audited it.  SIM: I had it audited.

Atenção (o erro nº 1 do brasileiro): o to no lugar errado. Depois de have + pessoa, NUNCA use to: "I had my controller rebuild". Depois de get + pessoa, SEMPRE use: "I got her to rebuild". E o erro mais grave não é gramatical: é dizer "I audited the numbers" quando você não auditou. Isso confunde o board sobre quem fez o quê — e um CFO vive dessa distinção.

Stage 2: Put the Handover in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de um handover real na ordem correta. Pular a última é o motivo pelo qual a maioria das delegações falha.

?Name the deliverable and the date — not "improve the close", but a shape and a day.
?Say out loud the thing you are giving up: the sign-off. And do not take it back.
?Say what is moving into her remit — the whole thing, not the boring parts.
?Set the escalation rule: when, exactly, does she loop you in?
?Create the bandwidth: name what comes OFF her desk to make room.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. São as cinco frases de uma delegação de verdade — aquela em que a autoridade vai junto.

I had the numbers audited before the board met.
Mandei auditar os números antes da reunião do conselho.
I got her to take the monthly close off my desk.
Consegui que ela assumisse o fechamento mensal.
From March, the close is inside your remit — you sign it off.
A partir de março, o fechamento está na sua alçada — você assina.
Loop me in only if a number moves more than five percent.
Me inclua só se algum número variar mais de cinco por cento.
The deliverable is the March close, on the fifth working day.
O entregável é o fechamento de março, no quinto dia útil.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

Pick one real task you still do that a direct report should own. Hand it over, out loud, for 2-3 minutes. Use have + object + past participle at least twice, have + person + bare infinitive once, and get + person + to + infinitive once. Name the deliverable and the date, say who signs it off, say what comes off their desk to create the bandwidth, and set the escalation rule. Then say the sentence you find hardest: what you are giving up.
