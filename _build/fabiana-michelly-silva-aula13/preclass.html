Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Consensus -- general agreement reached by a whole group
"We are close to consensus on the dual-supplier plan." (Consenso)
Endorse -- to give formal support or approval to a plan
"Which sourcing option do we endorse today?" (Endossar / apoiar)
Defer -- to delay a decision until a later time
"Let us not defer this decision any further." (Adiar)
Weigh up -- to carefully consider and compare two options
"Weigh up the cost against the protection." (Ponderar / avaliar)
Ratify -- to make a decision official by formal approval
"We recommend that the board ratify the top two vendors." (Ratificar / homologar)
Veto -- to formally reject or block a proposal
"Nobody chose to veto the plan." (Vetar)
Shortlist -- a reduced list of the best candidates or options
"Operations drew up a shortlist of three vendors." (Lista final / pré-selecionados)
Finalize -- to complete and confirm something as final
"I will finalize the paperwork by Friday." (Finalizar / concluir)
Deadlock -- a situation where no agreement can be reached
"Let us not leave this room in deadlock." (Impasse)
Abstain -- to choose not to vote on a decision
"One colleague decided to abstain." (Abster-se)
Recommendation -- a formal suggestion about what should be done
"My recommendation is to ratify the top two vendors." (Recomendação)
Unanimous -- supported by everyone, with no one against
"The vote was almost unanimous, with one abstention." (Unânime)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A good chair relays the room before the vote. Marcus reminded everyone that the risk review had flagged the single-supplier exposure. Finance said the dual-supplier plan would cost more, but they advised that it would protect us against a default. Operations told the room they had drawn up a shortlist, and they recommended that the board ratify the top two vendors. Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed before the end of the month. Notice how each reported sentence shifts the tense back and drops the question mark. Nobody chose to veto, one colleague said she would abstain, and the rest signed off, so the room reached consensus.

1. The original was "The plan will cost more." Why does the report say "would cost"?
A After a past reporting verb, "will" shifts back to "would".
B "Would" expresses a polite request.
C "Would" and "will" are interchangeable.
2. Why is "whether" used in "Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed"?
A It introduces a wh- question.
B It reports a yes/no question, which uses if or whether.
C It shows the speaker is uncertain about the grammar.
3. Why is the base verb "ratify" used after "recommended that"?
A Recommend / suggest / propose that take the base verb for every subject.
B It is a typing error for "ratifies".
C The subject "board" is plural.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Como relatar o que alguém disse, perguntou ou recomendou. / How to report what someone said, asked, or recommended.

Tipo / TypeMudança / ShiftExample / Exemplo
Statementpresente → passado; will → would; can → couldShe said the plan cost more.
Perfectpresent perfect → past perfect (have → had)They said they had drawn up a list.
Wh- questionordem de afirmação, sem do/does/did / statement orderHe asked which option we would endorse.
Yes/no questionuse if / whetherShe asked whether we could sign.
Ponto-chaveApós recommend / suggest / propose that, use o verbo na FORMA BASE para qualquer sujeito (that we ratify). / Use the base verb after recommend that.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

You are relaying a wh- question your manager asked earlier. You say:
A "She asked which vendor we would choose." (statement order)
B "She asked which vendor do we choose." (no do in a reported question)
C "She asked which vendor we will choose?" (no question mark; backshift will to would)
You want to report a recommendation correctly. The best sentence is:
A "They recommended that we ratifies it." (base verb, not ratifies)
B "They recommended that we ratify it." (recommend that + base verb)
C "They recommended that we will ratify it." (no will after recommend that)
You are reporting a yes/no question Legal asked. You say:
A "Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed." (if/whether + backshift)
B "Legal asked can the contracts be signed." (no question word order in reports)
C "Legal asked that the contracts could be signed." (use whether, not that, for a question)
