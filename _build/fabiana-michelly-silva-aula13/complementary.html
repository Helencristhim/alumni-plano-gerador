Lesson 13 -- The Decision Meeting

Series
Succession (2018) -- Boardroom & vote-counting scenes

A masterclass in steering a room to a decision. Watch how characters relay what others said, count votes, recommend, abstain, and try to avoid deadlock under pressure.

Pick one scene and report three lines as reported speech (she said that..., he asked whether..., they recommended that...).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Build Consensus and Make Better Decisions"

Harvard Business Review experts discuss how teams move from debate to consensus: weighing options, handling dissent, and avoiding decision deadlock. Directly relevant to chairing a decision meeting.

Note two recommendations the guests make. Report each one using "they recommended that..." with the base verb.

YouTube
"How to Run an Effective Decision-Making Meeting" -- Harvard Business Review

A short HBR guide on structuring a decision meeting: framing the options, gathering input, reaching consensus, and recording who agreed to what. Essential framing for any sourcing or vendor decision.

After watching, write 5 lines of meeting minutes for one of your own decisions, using a different reported-speech structure in each line.
