Chapter 3

Relaying the Room

Reported speech: statements, questions, and recommendations

Discovery

What Did They Say?

Direct: "The plan costs more." Finance said that the plan cost more. (present → past)

Direct: "It will protect us." They advised that it would protect us. (will → would)

Direct: "We have drawn up a shortlist." Operations said they had drawn up a shortlist. (present perfect → past perfect)

Direct: "Which option do we endorse?" Marcus asked which option we would endorse. (question → statement order)

Direct: "Can the contracts be signed?" Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed. (yes/no → if/whether)

Direct: "Ratify the top two." They recommended that we ratify the top two. (recommend that + base verb)

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

TypeShiftExample
Statementpresent → past; will → would; can → couldShe said the plan cost more.
Perfectpresent perfect → past perfectThey said they had drawn up a shortlist.
Wh- questionkeep the wh-word, use statement word order, no do/does/didHe asked which option we would endorse.
Yes/no questionuse if / whetherShe asked whether we could sign.
Recommend / suggest / proposethat + subject + BASE verb (subjunctive)They recommended that we ratify it.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He asked which option do we endorse? They recommended that we ratifies it." (no do in reported Q, base verb after recommend that)
"He asked which option we would endorse. They recommended that we ratify it." (statement order; base verb)

In a reported question, drop do / does / did and the question mark, and use statement word order. After recommend / suggest / propose that, use the base verb for every subject.

Comprehension

Reported Spotted

1. Statement (will → would): "They advised that it _____ protect us."
"They advised that it would protect us." (Direct: "It will protect us.")
2. Perfect (present perfect → past perfect): "They said they _____ drawn up three vendors."
"They said they had drawn up three vendors." (Direct: "We have drawn up three vendors.")
3. Recommend that + base verb: "They recommended that we _____ the top two."
"They recommended that we ratify the top two." (Direct: "Ratify the top two.")
