Chapter 3

Present Continuous

is / are + verb-ing for change in progress

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Technology is changing the way we work."

"More firms are adopting artificial intelligence."

"The market is becoming more data-driven."

"AI is not replacing us."

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeis / are + verb-ingTechnology is changing...
Negativeis / are + not + verb-ingAI is not replacing us.
QuestionIs / Are + subject + verb-ing?Are firms adopting AI?
UseFor change in progress now (right now, these days), not fixed routines.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Technology changes the way we work right now."

✓ RIGHT

"Technology is changing the way we work right now."

✗ WRONG

"More firms adopting AI."

✓ RIGHT

"More firms are adopting AI."

✗ WRONG

"The tools is automating tasks."

✓ RIGHT

"The tools are automating tasks."

For change in progress now, use is/are + verb-ing, and match is/are to the subject.

Chapter 4

Trends in Action

Two colleagues talk about change

Dialogue

Is AI Taking Over?

E
Everyone is talking about AI. Honestly, I am a little worried it is taking our jobs.
M
I understand. But AI is not replacing us; it is supporting us. It is automating the slow tasks.
E
So what exactly is changing in our daily work?
M
The market is becoming more data-driven. Algorithms are comparing thousands of properties, so we are spending less time on spreadsheets.
E
That sounds useful. Are the clients noticing the difference?
M
Yes. We are delivering faster reports, and the data is giving us new insight. The firms that are adapting now are gaining an advantage.
E
Okay, I feel better. I am learning the new software too.
M
Exactly. The world is not slowing down, so we keep adapting. The human still makes the final call.
Comprehension

3. Who makes the final call, according to Marcos?
The human appraiser still makes the final call.
