Block 1 Review -- My Professional Identity

All tenses together in one self-presentation: present simple (who you are), past simple (how you started), present perfect (what you have accomplished), present continuous (what you are doing now), and the future (where you are going). New vocabulary: introduce, overview, expertise, strength, highlight, accomplish, role, career path, aspiration, summarize.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Introduce -- to present yourself or someone to others
"Let me introduce myself to the team." (Apresentar)
Overview -- a short, general description of something
"Let me give you a quick overview of my career." (Visão geral)
Expertise -- special skill or knowledge in a field
"My expertise is the da Vinci robotic system." (Especialidade)
Strength -- something you are good at; a strong point
"My main strength is staying calm under pressure." (Ponto forte)
Highlight -- to draw attention to something important
"Let me highlight one important strength." (Ressaltar)
Accomplish -- to finish something successfully; to achieve
"I have accomplished a lot in the operating room." (Realizar)
Role -- your job or function in a team or company
"My role today is in robotic surgery." (Função)
Career path -- the series of steps in your professional life
"My career path started in the intensive care unit." (Trajetória)
Aspiration -- a strong hope or goal for the future
"My aspiration is to lead the training program." (Aspiração)
Summarize -- to give the main points in a few words
"To summarize, I am ready for the next step." (Resumir)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia stands up to introduce herself at the program kickoff. She gives a short overview of her career path. "I am a nurse, and my role is in robotic surgery," she says (present simple). Then she goes back in time: "I started in the intensive care unit" (past simple). She wants to highlight her experience: "Since then, I have trained six nurses" (present perfect). She also describes the present: "Right now, I am studying for a certification" (present continuous). Finally, she shares her aspiration: "Next year, I will lead the program" (future). To summarize, she presents her whole professional identity, using every tense.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- All Tenses Together

GRAMMAR

Usado para escolher o tempo verbal de cada parte da sua apresentação. / Used to choose the tense for each part of your presentation.

Part / ParteTense / TempoExample / Exemplo
Who you are todayPresent simpleI work in robotic surgery.
How you startedPast simpleI started in the ICU.
Experience up to nowPresent perfectI have trained six nurses.
What is happening nowPresent continuousI am studying now.
Where you are goingFuture (will / going to)I will lead the program.

Key point / Ponto-chave: escolha o tempo pelo TEMPO de cada parte. Use o present perfect para algo que começou no passado e continua agora: diga "I have worked here for six years", nunca "I work here for six years". Para uma ação terminada com palavra de tempo passado (last year, in 2010), use o past simple.
