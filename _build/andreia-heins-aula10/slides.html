Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Review

All the tenses together: which one for which part of your story

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Each sentence is one part of Andreia's story. What tense does each part use?

"I work in robotic surgery." (today)

"I started in the intensive care unit." (years ago)

"I have trained six new nurses." (up to now)

"Right now, I am studying for a certification." (now)

"Next year, I will lead the program." (future)

Grammar Rule

One Tense per Part

Part of your storyTenseExample
Who you are todayPresent simpleI work in robotic surgery.
How you startedPast simpleI started in the ICU.
Experience up to nowPresent perfectI have trained six nurses.
What is happening nowPresent continuousI am studying for a certification.
Where you are goingFuture (will / going to)I will lead the program.

Key idea: choose the tense by the TIME of each part. Use the present perfect for something that started in the past and continues now: say "I have worked here for six years", never "I work here for six years".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I work here since 2010."
"I have worked here since 2010."

For an action that started in the past and continues now, use the present perfect. Use since + a point in time, and for + a period.
