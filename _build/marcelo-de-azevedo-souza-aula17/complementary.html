Aula 17 -- The Story Worth Telling -- Dramatic Emphasis

Tema da aula: telling an unforgettable story with dramatic emphasis (emphatic inversion + vivid words). Reforço fora de aula -- marque como concluído ao terminar.

Film
The Secret Life of Walter Mitty (2013) -- directed by Ben Stiller

An ordinary man finally steps into the adventure of his life -- Iceland, the Himalayas, jaw-dropping landscapes that stop him in his tracks. It is a feast of the exact language you practised tonight: awe-inspiring, unforgettable, once in a lifetime, beyond words. Watch how the film turns a quiet life into a spellbinding, remarkable story. Connection to Lesson 17: every scene is a story worth telling -- notice how the images do the emphasis that your inversions do in speech.

Tip: after the film, describe one scene out loud using two dramatic openers -- "Never have I seen...", "Not only was it..., but...".

Podcast
The Moth -- any episode

Real people telling true, unforgettable stories live, with no notes. It is the purest master class in tonight's skill: ordinary storytellers who make a whole room feel the moment. Listen for how they build a story -- the pause before the surprise, the vivid detail, the single dramatic line that stops you in your tracks. Great ear training for turning an experience into a story worth telling.

Tip: pick one story, then retell its high point in 20 seconds using an inversion -- "Little did I know...", "Never have I...".
