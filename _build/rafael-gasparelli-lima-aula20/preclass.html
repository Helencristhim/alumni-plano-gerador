Stage 1.1: Key Words

Vocabulary

Eight words a director uses to manage a whole deal. Listen to each one and notice how it is used.

Stakeholder -- parte interessada (pessoa ou grupo com interesse no negócio)
"Every stakeholder must approve the final terms."
Milestone -- marco (etapa importante de um projeto)
"Signing the contract was a big milestone."
Scope -- escopo (todo o trabalho que o projeto inclui)
"The scope of the deal covers two countries."
Timeline -- cronograma (plano de datas do início ao fim)
"Our timeline puts the signing in March."
Deliverable -- entregável (resultado concreto que você deve entregar)
"The first deliverable is the signed contract."
Accountable -- responsável (deve responder pelo resultado)
"Each director is accountable for one milestone."
Follow-through -- cumprimento (fazer o que foi prometido depois do acordo)
"Good follow-through builds a strong partnership."
Commitment -- compromisso (promessa séria de fazer algo)
"We made a clear commitment to deliver on time."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story of a full deal. Notice the five grammar tools of the arc, then answer the questions.

This year, our team has reached two important milestones. At the first meeting, the client said the scope was too wide. In my opinion, that concern was fair. We told them that each stakeholder must approve the plan and may request small changes. Then we made a simple offer: if they accepted the timeline, we will start the next week. The client told us they would sign, and our commitment and good follow-through closed the deal.

1. How does the team talk about results up to now?
A "has reached two milestones" (present perfect)
B "reach two milestones" (present simple)
C "will reach two milestones" (future)
2. In the conditional offer, what comes after "if"?
A "if they will accept"
B "if they accepted / accept" (present, not will)
C "if they would accept"
3. How is "We will sign" reported in the text?
A "They said me they will sign."
B "They told us they would sign."
C "They told that they will sign."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Five Tools Together

Grammar

Num negócio real, você mistura as cinco estruturas: present perfect para resultados (we have signed), opiniões para se posicionar (in my opinion), must/may para regras (must approve / may request), primeira condicional para ofertas (if you confirm, we will proceed -- "if" com presente, nunca "if you will") e reported speech para relatar (said sem pessoa, told + pessoa; is→was, will→would). / In a real deal you mix all five: present perfect for results, opinions to take a position, must/may for rules, if + will for offers, and said/told to report.

Present perfectWe have reached a milestone.Nós atingimos um marco.
OpinionIn my opinion, the timeline is realistic.Na minha opinião, o cronograma é realista.
Modals (must/may)Each stakeholder must approve the scope.Cada parte interessada deve aprovar o escopo.
First conditionalIf you confirm, we will proceed.Se você confirmar, nós prosseguiremos.
Reported speechHe said the deal was a milestone.Ele disse que o negócio era um marco.

Quiz
