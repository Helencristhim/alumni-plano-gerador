Chapter 3: All the Grammar in One Place

The Grammar Toolbox

Five tools, one deal.

Discover the Code

One Deal, Five Tools

We have signed three contracts this year.

In my opinion, the timeline is realistic.

Each party must respect the scope and may request changes.

If you confirm today, we will proceed with the signing.

The client said the deal was a milestone.

ToolUse it to...Example
Present perfecttalk about results up to nowWe have signed three contracts.
Opinionssay what you thinkIn my opinion, the timeline is realistic.
Modals (must/may)say duty and permissionEach party must respect the scope.
First conditionalmake a conditional offerIf you confirm, we will proceed.
Reported speechreport what people saidHe said the deal was a milestone.

In a real deal you mix all five tools: present perfect for results, opinions to take a position, must/may for the rules, if + will to make offers, and said/told to report. You already know each one — today you use them together.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"If you will confirm, we will proceed."

✓ RIGHT

"If you confirm, we will proceed."

✗ WRONG

"He said me the scope is final."

✓ RIGHT

"He told me the scope was final."

When you mix tools, keep each rule. In first conditional, the "if" part uses the present, not "will". In reported speech, use "told" with a person and move the verb back one step.
