Never Back Down -- The Quarterly Project Review

Seis semanas de atraso na instalação e uma fornecedora que lembra a promessa de outro jeito: como abrir a reunião pelo NÚMERO, como transformar memória em REGISTRO (data + verbo de relato + recuo), e como conceder o que é verdade sem entregar a folga do projeto. Key words: schedule slippage, variance, critical path, float, recovery schedule, contingency plan, risk mitigation, post-mortem review, steering committee, buy-in, to make headway, to reach a consensus, to take a stance, to bring about. Structure: discurso indireto e verbos de relato (On 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship... / your company undertook to submit...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Na aula 6 você nomeou o defeito. Aqui você nomeia o ATRASO — e quem paga por ele. São as palavras que separam "estamos atrasados" de um cronograma que se defende numa sala: a variação, o caminho crítico, a folga que não existe. Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo.

Schedule slippage -- time lost against the plan, a few days at a time, until the date is gone (derrapagem de prazo)
"The schedule slippage on the installation package is now six weeks."
Variance -- the measured difference between the plan and reality -- the number in the report (variação (medida, plano x real))
"The schedule variance is minus thirty working days against the plan."
Critical path -- the chain of activities that decides the end date: delay any of them and the project moves (caminho crítico)
"Signal installation is on the critical path, so this delay is not absorbable."
Float -- the spare time an activity has before it starts delaying the whole project (folga (de cronograma))
"That activity had ten days of float. The supplier has used all of it."
Recovery schedule -- the revised plan that shows exactly how the lost time will be won back (cronograma de recuperação)
"A recovery schedule is required within ten days, not another apology."
Contingency plan -- what you do if the recovery fails -- the plan you hope not to use (plano de contingência)
"Our contingency plan is to commission the line in two stages instead of one."
Risk mitigation -- action taken now to make a risk smaller, before it becomes a delay (mitigação de risco)
"Dual sourcing the cable was risk mitigation, and it is why we are only six weeks late."
Post-mortem review -- a session held after the fact, to find out why it happened and never repeat it (revisão pos-projeto (lições aprendidas))
"We will hold a post-mortem review once the line is commissioned, not now."
Steering committee -- the senior group that decides what the project team is not allowed to decide alone (comitê diretor / comitê gestor)
"If we cannot agree a date today, this goes to the steering committee in April."
Buy-in -- real agreement from the people who could block you -- not just their signature (adesão, apoio real)
"I have the approval. What I do not have yet is buy-in from the operations team."
To make headway -- to make real progress against something that is pushing back (avançar, progredir (contra resistência))
"We are finally making headway on the interface drawings."
To reach a consensus -- to arrive at a position that everybody in the room can live with (chegar a um consenso)
"We reached a consensus on the sequence, not on the date."
To take a stance -- to state a position publicly, and then stand behind it when it is challenged (assumir uma posição publicamente)
"Motiva has taken a stance on the delay: no payment against an unapproved schedule."
To bring about -- to cause something to happen, usually after effort and resistance (provocar, ocasionar (um resultado))
"It took two escalations to bring about a resolution."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The quarterly review opens at ten, and Elena Vargas opens it with the good news: her team is working weekends and they are finally making headway on site. João Guilherme lets her finish, and then he puts the number on the table. The schedule variance on the signaling installation is minus thirty working days. The activity sits on the critical path, so the float is zero and nothing absorbs it. Six weeks of schedule slippage, and the passenger waits six weeks longer.

Elena has been preparing her version for a month. The interface drawings arrived late from Motiva, she says, and her team could not start. She is partly right, and João says so: to some extent, both parties share responsibility, and the drawings were issued eight days late. Then she goes further. I never promised you the fifteenth, she says. I said we would do our best. And this is the moment the meeting is won or lost — not by raising the voice, but by reading the record. On 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship on the fifteenth, and the minutes were circulated to you that same afternoon. In the last review your team told us that the installation was on track. Your planner claimed that the cable had already been dispatched; the carrier's records show it left the warehouse eleven days later. And on 12 June your company undertook to submit a recovery schedule within ten days. Twenty-two days have passed, and nothing has been received.

Elena stops. That is fair, she acknowledges. The schedule was not sent, and I should have called you. Nobody was accused, nothing was denied, and the argument about memory is over — because a dated record cannot be argued with, only explained. So she asks what he needs. He asks for three things: a recovery schedule by Friday, a contingency plan if the retest fails, and a weekly call, so that the next slip reaches him in three days and not in three weeks. She asks for one thing back: two weeks of float from the testing activity. He refuses. The float belongs to the project. And if they can reach a consensus today, none of this has to go to the steering committee in April.

1. "On 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship." Por que o verbo recua de will para would?
A Porque would é mais educado do que will.
B Porque o recuo marca que a frase é uma CITAÇÃO do que ELA disse, e não uma promessa que JOÃO está fazendo agora. Sem o recuo ("you assured us the relay will ship"), a frase deixa de ser o registro dela e vira o compromisso dele.
C Porque em inglês técnico é proibido usar will.
2. Elena diz "I never promised you the fifteenth". O que ENCERRA a discussão?
A Levantar o tom e repetir que ela prometeu.
B O REGISTRO: a data (3 de junho), o verbo de relato (assured us) e a ata circulada na mesma tarde. Contra um registro datado não se discute — só se explica.
C Chamar o comitê diretor imediatamente.
3. "Your planner claimed that the cable had already been dispatched." O que o verbo CLAIMED comunica que SAID não comunicaria?
A Que João DUVIDA da afirmação — claim marca que o que foi dito não se confirmou. Said seria neutro; claimed já abre a checagem.
B Que o planejador gritou.
C Que a afirmação era oficial e está comprovada.
4. Which sentence is correct?
A "Your company assured us to submit a recovery schedule within ten days."
B "Your company undertook to submit a recovery schedule within ten days."
C "Your company said us that it would submit a recovery schedule within ten days."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech & Reporting Verbs

GRAMMAR

Como transformar o que o outro disse em registro — e por que o verbo que você escolhe decide o quanto ele prende (explicação em inglês e português).

Eles disseram / They saidFunção / FunctionVocê relata / You report
will → wouldA promessa recua um passo no tempo — e passa a ser um REGISTRO do que eles prometeram, não uma promessa sua."You assured us that the relay would ship on the fifteenth."
is / are → was / wereO presente deles vira passado seu: era verdade para eles, naquele momento."Your team told us that the installation was on track."
has been → had beenO perfeito vira mais-que-perfeito. É a linha que DATA uma alegação — e datar é o que permite checar."Your planner claimed that the cable had been dispatched."
can → could · must → had toOs modais também recuam. Must é a exceção: vira had to."They said they could recover two weeks, and that we had to wait."
say x tellTELL exige a pessoa (told us / told me). SAY NÃO aceita pessoa direta: é said that ou said TO me. "He said me" simplesmente não existe."He told us that..." · "He said that..."
O verbo escolhe o PESOsaid (neutro) · claimed (você duvida) · assured us (comprometeram-se, com confiança) · acknowledged / conceded (admitiram contra o próprio interesse) · denied (negaram) · undertook / committed to (obrigação formal)."They assured us" prende. "They said" não prende.
Para que serve"You are always late" é uma acusação — e uma acusação se nega, se defende e se discute por três semanas. "On 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship on the fifteenth" é o MESMO fato virado REGISTRO: tem data, tem verbo de relato, tem recuo. Contra um registro não se discute — só se explica. E quem explica, negocia.

Atenção (três erros de brasileiro): (1) "He said me that..." — em português dizemos "ele ME disse", mas SAY nunca leva a pessoa: é told me (ou said to me); (2) NÃO recuar o verbo — em português o recuo é opcional ("ele disse que o relê SAI dia 15"), mas em inglês "he said the relay will ship" deixa de citar o outro e vira uma promessa SUA; (3) "They assured us TO send the schedule" — assure pede that + oração; quem pede infinitivo é undertake / commit / promise: "they undertook to send the schedule".

Collocações da revisão de prazo: compress the schedule (nunca "reduce"), secure buy-in, reach a consensus (nunca "arrive at"), mitigate a risk, make headway (NUNCA "have progress"), absorb a delay, take a stance, bring about a resolution.

Hedging (para conceder sem ceder): "To some extent, both parties share responsibility for..." · "One could argue that the delay was partly caused by..." · "That is fair, and I will record it. It does not change what is on the critical path."

Stage 2: Put the Review Meeting in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de uma revisão trimestral de prazo na ordem correta — do número até a consequência.

?Say why nothing absorbs it: the activity is on the critical path and the float is zero.
?Close with the demand and the consequence: a recovery schedule by Friday, or the steering committee in April.
?Put the promise on the record with the date: on 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship.
?Open with the number: the schedule variance is minus thirty working days.
?Name the obligation that was never met: your company undertook to submit a recovery schedule.
?Concede what is true: to some extent, both parties share responsibility for the late drawings.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. São as cinco frases que abrem, sustentam e fecham uma revisão de prazo. Repare no acento: "VA-ri-ance" (na primeira sílaba, não "va-ri-AN-ce") e "com-MIT-tee" com o -ee curto. E diga SKED-jool, não SHED-jool.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da revisão trimestral — inclusive quando a fornecedora nega a promessa ou pede a folga do seu projeto.

Elena abre a reunião com a boa notícia ("the team is working weekends"). Você responde:
A "Thank you, Elena. Let me start with the number, and then I would like to hear you. The schedule variance on the installation is minus thirty working days, and the activity is on the critical path."
B "Weekends are the minimum I expect at this point."
C "That is good to hear. So, how are things going in general?"
"I never promised you the fifteenth. I said we would do our best." A resposta mais forte é:
A "You did promise it, and everybody in that call heard you."
B "That is why I am reading from the minutes. On 3 June you assured us that the relay would ship on the fifteenth, and the minutes were circulated to you the same afternoon."
C "All right, perhaps I misunderstood you. Let us move on."
"The interface drawings arrived late from your side." Ela está PARCIALMENTE certa. Você responde:
A "That has nothing to do with the delay we are discussing."
B "You are right, and I accept that the delay is shared."
C "To some extent, both parties share responsibility, and the drawings were issued eight days late. The slippage we are discussing, however, is thirty days."
"Give us two weeks of float from the testing activity and we recover everything." Você recusa:
A "I am afraid the float belongs to the project, not to the package that is late. If I hand it over now, I have nothing left when something unplanned happens."
B "Fine, take the two weeks, but this is the last time."
C "I do not have the authority to discuss float with you."
Ela pede que a reunião não seja escalada. Você diz sim NOS SEUS TERMOS:
A "I cannot promise anything, it is out of my hands."
B "If we can reach a consensus today — a recovery schedule by Friday and a contingency plan — this does not have to go to the steering committee in April."
C "Do not worry, I will keep this between us whatever happens."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Fale por 2 minutos, sem script e sem parar para se corrigir. Tom: preciso no número, imparcial na concessão, firme no registro.

You are chairing the quarterly review with a supplier who is six weeks behind. In ninety seconds: open with the variance and say why the float does not absorb it, concede the part that is genuinely yours (the drawings were eight days late), put two commitments on the record with their dates (On 3 June you assured us that... / On 12 June your company undertook to...), refuse to hand over the float from the testing activity, and close by saying what you need by Friday and what happens if it does not arrive.
