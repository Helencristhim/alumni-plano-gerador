Lesson 5 -- Email Essentials

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 18: "Take Your Daughter to Work Day"

Watch how the team writes and reads work emails. Notice the polite requests and the email words: inbox, reply, forward, and attach. Hear how "could you" and "would you" make a request sound friendly.

Write down every polite request you hear. Pause and repeat each one, then rewrite it as a short email line.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Writing Polite Emails"

Practical tips for professional emails: a clear subject, a polite request with "could" and "would", and a friendly closing. Listen for the new words: attach, reply, forward, draft, and recipient.

Note 3 polite request lines the host suggests. Practice each one as a "Could you...?" or "Would you...?" question.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to Write a Professional Email"

A short, clear lesson on email structure: subject line, greeting, polite request, and closing. Perfect for practicing "could" and "would" in real work situations.

After watching, record a 60-second email out loud. Use at least three new words and one request with "could" or "would".
