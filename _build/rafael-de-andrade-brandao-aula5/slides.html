Chapter 3 -- The Polite Code

Grammar Discovery

Could and Would -- making polite requests

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What pattern do the orange words follow?

"Could you send the report?"

"Would you check the inbox?"

"I would like to confirm the date."

"Could I ask a question?"

Grammar Rule

Could / Would for Politeness

FormExample
Could you + verbCould you send the file?
Would you + verbWould you check the email?
I would like + to + verbI would like to confirm the date.
Could I + verbCould I ask a question?

Key idea: Use could and would to make a request polite. After the modal, use the base verb with no to (could you send, not could you to send).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you to send the file?"
"Could you send the file?"

After could and would, use the base verb with no to. Only "would like" takes to before the next verb.

Artefact

A Professional Email

New Message

From: Rafael Brandao

To: Diana Costa

Subject: Sales Report -- May

Dear Diana,

Thank you for your message. I would like to send you the May sales report. Please find the file attached.

Could you confirm that you received it? I am happy to answer any questions.

Best regards,
Rafael Brandao

Dialogue

A Polite Request

R

"Hi Diana, thank you for your message. When would you like the report?"

D

"Good morning, Rafael. I would like it by tomorrow morning, please."

R

"No problem. I am finishing the draft now. Could I add two new ideas?"

D

"Yes, please. Would you also attach the price list?"

R

"Sure. I will reply with everything in one email. Anything else?"

D

"That is perfect. Could you forward it to my assistant too? Thank you so much."
