Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Attach -- to add a file or document to an email
"Please attach the file to the email." (Anexar)
Reply -- to answer a message or an email
"I will reply to your message today." (Responder)
Forward -- to send an email to another person
"Could you forward this email to Diana?" (Encaminhar)
Subject -- the short title that explains the email
"The subject line is short and clear." (Assunto)
Draft -- an email you write but do not send yet
"I am writing a draft of the email." (Rascunho)
Inbox -- the place where your new emails arrive
"I have ten new emails in my inbox." (Caixa de entrada)
Request -- when you politely ask for something
"I have a small request for you." (Pedido)
Recipient -- the person who receives the email
"Check the recipient before you send." (Destinatário)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is Monday morning, and Rafael is opening his inbox. He has a new request from a client. The client writes: "Could you send the new prices today? I would like to see them before noon." Rafael writes a short reply. He adds a clear subject and attaches the file. Then he asks his assistant: "Would you forward this email to the finance team?" A polite email always uses could and would. These small words make every request sound kind and professional.

1. What does the client want?
A The new prices, before noon today.
B A meeting next week.
C A phone call this evening.
2. Which sentence is more polite?
A "Could you send the report?"
B "Send the report now."
C "I want the report."
3. Why does Rafael use "could" and "would"?
A To sound polite and professional.
B To sound angry.
C To write a longer email.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Could / Would for Politeness

GRAMMAR

Usado para pedidos educados. / Used for polite requests in emails.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Could you + verbPedido educado / Polite requestCould you send the file?
Would you + verbPedido educado / Polite requestWould you check the email?
I would like + to + verbVontade educada / Polite wantI would like to confirm the date.
Could I + verbPermissão educada / Polite permissionCould I ask a question?
Key point / Ponto-chaveDepois de "could" e "would", use o verbo SEM "to" (could you send, não could you to send). Estas palavras deixam o pedido educado.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "Could you send me the file, please?"
B "Send me the file."
C "I want the file now."
You start a formal email to a client. The best opening is:
A "I am writing to confirm our meeting."
B "Hey, what is up?"
C "Give me an answer."
You finish a professional email. The best closing is:
A "Thank you for your time. Best regards, Rafael."
B "Bye."
C "That is all."
