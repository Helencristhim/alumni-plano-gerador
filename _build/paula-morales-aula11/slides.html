Chapter 3: The Code

One Word in Portuguese. Two in English.

Quanto custa? Quantas reuniões? — um quanto, dois em inglês.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

O que vem logo depois de how much e de how many?

How much is it?

How many nights?

How many meetings do you have?

How much does it cost?

Depois de many vem sempre coisa no plural. Depois de much, nunca. E na frase 4: por que does aparece, se na frase 1 não tem?

A perguntaInglêsEm português
Dá pra contar com o dedo?How many nights? How many meetings?Quantas noites? Quantas reuniões?
Não dá? É massa, se mede?How much is it? How much money?Quanto é? Quanto dinheiro?
Depois de manysempre plural: nights, meetings, peopleO -s nunca falta.
Com verbo, entra o doesHow much does it cost?Tem verbo (cost) → precisa do ajudante, igual à aula 8.
A regra em uma linha: conta no dedo = many. Não conta = much. Dinheiro nunca se conta no dedo (você conta NOTAS, não dinheiro) — então é sempre how much. E o does só aparece quando há um verbo de verdade na pergunta: How much IS it? não precisa, porque o is já é o verbo.
Quantifiers

Some and Any

AFIRMATIVA → SOME

"I have some questions."

"I have some meetings tomorrow."

Algumas. Existem.

NEGATIVA → ANY

"I don't have any meetings on Friday."

"We don't have any budget for that."

Nenhuma. Zero.

PERGUNTA → ANY

"Do you have any time on Thursday?"

"Are there any rooms available?"

Tem algum? Tem alguma?

Uma exceção, e ela é a educada. Quando você está pedindo ou oferecendo — e não perguntando de verdade — o some volta: “Could I have some information about the policy?” · “Would you like some coffee?” Não são perguntas: são pedidos com cara de pergunta — o mesmo truque do could da aula 9. Não trave nisso: se sair any, ninguém nota. O que importa é nunca pôr some na negativa.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"How much costs the room?"
"How much does the room cost?"
"How many money is the budget?"
"How much is the budget?"

O primeiro erro é o português empurrando: “Quanto custa o quarto?” tem o verbo grudado no quanto, e a mão traz ele junto. Em inglês o does entra e o verbo vai pro fim — é o mesmo does de “Where does the flight arrive?”, da aula 8. Você já tem essa peça. O segundo se resolve com uma pergunta: dá pra contar dinheiro no dedo? Não — você conta notas. Então é much.

Chapter 4: The Trap

Thirteen. Thirty.

A parte que ninguém te contou — e ela não é sobre você.

The Trap

Where the Stress Falls

TEENS — A FORÇA NO FIM

thir-TEEN · 13

fif-TEEN · 15

eigh-TEEN · 18

Se o fim é alto, é teen.

TENS — A FORÇA NA FRENTE

THIR-ty · 30

FIF-ty · 50

EIGH-ty · 80

Se a frente é alta, é ten.

"It's thirteen dollars." · "It's thirty dollars."
"Sorry — one-three, or three-zero?"

A regra da sílaba funciona na maioria das vezes — e não em todas. Na fala rápida ela some, inclusive para quem nasceu falando inglês. Por isso existe a segunda frase: “One-three, or three-zero?” Ela não é frase de iniciante. É o que o americano pergunta ao americano, porque a língua dele é ambígua — não porque ele é ruim nela.

Listening

David and the Numbers

Sound first — no text. Leia as perguntas antes de ouvir.

0:000:00
1. Why are thirteen and thirty so easy to confuse?
The difference is one sound, and it is at the end of the word — where nobody is listening anymore. The same problem happens with fifteen and fifty, sixteen and sixty.
2. David is American. Does he ever mishear numbers?
Yes — every week, in every currency. He was born with this language and he still mishears it. That is why the trick exists: "One-three, or three-zero?" It is not a beginner's question. It is the question he asks too.
3. David says the number is "the last thing". The last thing to do what?
The last thing that becomes automatic in any language. Not the hardest — the last. And he says Paula reads a wrong figure in a policy in two seconds in Portuguese: that does not go away in English, it is just slower.

Comprehension

About Marcus

1. How much is the blue one?
Thirteen ninety-nine — one-three, not three-zero. And four of them cost fifty-six dollars.
2. Marcus works in Miami. Does he ask for the digits too?
Yes — in Spanish. He says everybody asks him that one. Confirming a number is not a beginner's move: it is what everyone does, in every language.
3. What colors does Marcus have? What doesn't he have?
He has some in blue. He doesn't have any in black, and they are not available in green — only blue.
4. Marcus says "You learn fast." Does Paula agree?
No. She says: "I don't learn fast. I just ask." And that is the whole lesson — asking is not what you do when you are bad at English. It is what you do when you want the right number.

1. How much is the room? And how many nights?
The room is one hundred and eighty dollars a night — one-eight-zero, not eighty. Two nights, so the room is three hundred and sixty dollars.
2. How much does breakfast cost? Is it expensive?
Twenty-two dollars each, forty-four dollars for two. And yes — that is expensive for breakfast.
3. How much is the total?
Four hundred and twenty-two dollars. Four-two-two.
4. There is an $18.00 minibar charge. You did not open the minibar. What do you do?
You ask. "Excuse me, I have a question about the bill. What is the eighteen dollars?" It is your money, it is their mistake, and asking about it is not rude in any language.
Listening 2

Sofia's One Thing

Sound first — no text. Leia as perguntas antes de ouvir.

0:000:00
1. What does Sofia see Paula do on the phone, in Portuguese?
She repeats the number back. Every time a broker gives her a figure, she says it out loud and waits for him to confirm it. Sofia says Paula does not know she does it — she has done it for twenty years.
2. Why does a wrong number matter so much in insurance?
It is not an embarrassment — it is money. It is somebody's factory. That is why confirming a figure is not a weakness in this business: it is the job.
3. Sofia says nobody has ever lost an account for one thing, and people lose accounts for another. Which is which?
Nobody has ever lost an account for confirming a figure. People lose accounts for guessing — for nodding, for writing down eighty when the man said eighteen. And she says the ones who do not ask are the ones she worries about.

Role-Play

Semi-free — The Charge That Isn't Yours

Scenario: Check-out, eleven o'clock, and there is an eighteen-dollar minibar charge on your bill. You did not open the minibar. Ask for the bill, ask what the charge is, and say it is not yours. Then check the total again — and get the digits. No sorry in front of anything: the mistake is theirs.

Keywords:

Can I have the bill, please? What is the eighteen dollars? I didn't use the minibar. How much is the total now?
