Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Indirect questions: polite and clear

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"Could you tell me when the order will arrive?"

"Do you know if she is available?"

"I was wondering if you could call back later."

"Could you tell me how I can reach the manager?"

Grammar Rule

Indirect Questions

FormExample
Direct questionWhen will the order arrive?
Indirect (Wh-)Could you tell me when the order will arrive?
Yes/No (if/whether)Do you know if she is available?
Polite openerI was wondering if you could help me.

Key idea: after the polite opener, use statement word order (subject + verb), with no do, does or did. Use if or whether for yes/no questions.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you tell me where is the office?"
"Could you tell me where the office is?"

In indirect questions, use statement word order: subject + verb -- and no extra do, does or did.

Grammar Practice

Make it Polite

"Could you tell me when the shipment _____will arrive?" (arrive -> future)
"Do you know _____if she is available?" (yes / no link word)
"I was wondering _____if you could help me." (polite opener)
"Could you tell me where the office _____is?" (statement word order)
