Lesson 8 -- Handling Phone Calls

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 2: "Errors and Omissions"

Watch how lawyers and assistants handle business phone calls: answering, putting people through, taking messages and asking for information politely.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three phone expressions you hear (for example, hold on, put you through, leave a message) and one polite question.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Telephone English: Making and Taking Calls"

A clear episode on phone language: answering professionally, asking the caller to hold on, transferring calls, and using indirect questions to sound polite.

Record a 2-minute phone role-play: answer a call, say one company is not available, offer to take a message, and ask for a detail using an indirect question.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Indirect Questions: Being Polite"

A focused lesson on indirect questions, such as Could you tell me...? and Do you know if...?. It directly reinforces today's grammar for polite phone calls.

After watching, turn six direct questions into indirect ones. Three should start with Could you tell me and three with Do you know if.
