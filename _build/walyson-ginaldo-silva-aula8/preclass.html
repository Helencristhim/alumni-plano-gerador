Handling Phone Calls -- Phone Expressions and Indirect Questions

Making and taking business phone calls politely: phone expressions (hold on, put through, transfer, leave a message, call back) and indirect questions (Could you tell me...? Do you know if...? I was wondering if...?). New vocabulary: hold on, put through, transfer, extension, voicemail, get through, hang up, call back, available, leave a message, caller, speak up.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Hold on -- to wait on the line without ending the call
"Could you hold on for a moment, please?" (Esperar na linha)
Put through -- to connect a caller to another person
"I will put you through to the sales team." (Conectar / passar a ligação)
Transfer -- to move a call to another person or department
"Let me transfer your call to logistics." (Transferir a ligação)
Extension -- an internal phone number inside a company
"You can reach me on extension two-oh-four." (Ramal)
Voicemail -- a system that records phone messages
"Please leave a message on my voicemail." (Correio de voz)
Get through -- to succeed in reaching someone by phone
"I could not get through to the office this morning." (Conseguir falar / completar a ligação)
Hang up -- to end a phone call
"Please do not hang up; I will be right back." (Desligar)
Call back -- to return a phone call later
"I will call you back this afternoon." (Retornar a ligação)
Available -- free and able to talk now
"She is not available at the moment." (Disponível)
Leave a message -- to record information for someone who is away
"Would you like to leave a message?" (Deixar um recado)
Caller -- the person who is making the phone call
"Could you tell me who the caller is?" (Quem liga / interlocutor)
Speak up -- to talk more loudly so people can hear you
"Could you speak up, please? The line is not clear." (Falar mais alto)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson needs to call a supplier about a pending order. First, he calls the main office. The receptionist answers and says the manager is not available right now. Walyson stays polite and uses indirect questions. He says, Could you tell me when the manager will be back? and Do you know if he has read my email? The receptionist offers to put him through to the assistant, or to take a message. Walyson asks her to hold on for a moment while he finds the order number. In the end, he leaves his extension and asks the assistant to call him back. Before he hangs up, he says, I was wondering if you could confirm the delivery date. A clear, polite call saves a lot of time.

1. Why does Walyson call the supplier?
A Because he needs to ask about a pending order.
B Because he wants to cancel everything.
C Because he is on holiday.
2. What does the receptionist say about the manager?
A He has already left the company.
B He is not available right now.
C He will never call back.
3. What does Walyson ask the assistant to do?
A To hang up immediately.
B To change the order.
C To call him back and confirm the delivery date.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Usamos perguntas indiretas para soar mais educados ao telefone. / We use indirect questions to sound more polite on the phone.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Direct questionWh- + auxiliary + subject?When will the order arrive?
Indirect (Wh-)Could you tell me + Wh- + subject + verbCould you tell me when the order will arrive?
Yes/No questionDo you know + if/whether + subject + verbDo you know if she is available?
Polite openerI was wondering if + subject + couldI was wondering if you could help me.

Key point / Ponto-chave: em perguntas indiretas, use a ordem de uma frase afirmativa (sujeito + verbo), sem do/does/did, e use if ou whether para perguntas de sim/não. Diga "Could you tell me where the office is?", não "where is the office?".
