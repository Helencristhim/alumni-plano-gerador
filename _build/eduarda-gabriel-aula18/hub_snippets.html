Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key meeting-minutes terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Minutes -- the official written record of a meeting
"I will write up the minutes after the call."
Attendee -- a person who is present at a meeting
"List every attendee at the top of the minutes."
Apologies -- a record of who was absent but sent word
"Apologies were received from two directors."
Quorum -- the minimum number present needed to decide
"There was a quorum, so the vote was valid."
Resolution -- a formal decision recorded in the minutes
"The board passed a resolution to proceed."
Owner -- the person responsible for an action item
"Give each action an owner and a deadline."
Tabled -- postponed to a later meeting
"The pricing item was tabled until next week."
Adjourn -- to formally close or suspend the meeting
"The chair adjourned the meeting at noon."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduarda writes the minutes of the board call. She lists the attendees and the apologies from those absent, and confirms there was a quorum. Because minutes are written after the meeting, she reports everything in the past. The chair said the deal was on track (not "is"); the CFO said they would close on Friday (not "will"); a director said they could proceed (not "can"). She records each decision as a resolution, gives every action an owner, marks the unresolved item as tabled, and notes that the chair adjourned the meeting.

1. Why does Eduarda write "the chair said the deal was on track" instead of "is on track"?
A Because the deal failed.
B Minutes are written after the meeting, so the tense backshifts to the past.
C Because "is" is never used in business English.
2. How does she report "we will close on Friday"?
A "She said they would close on Friday."
B "She said they will close on Friday."
C "She says they close Friday."
3. What does she do with an item that was not resolved?
A She deletes it.
B She marks it as tabled.
C She records it as a quorum.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use reported speech with backshift for minutes. Because the meeting is over, every quote moves one step into the past: is/are → was/were, will → would, can → could, present simple → past simple. Use said + (that). Como as minutes sao escritas depois da reuniao, o tempo verbal recua um passo: "is" vira "was", "will" vira "would", "can" vira "could".

Live (direct)Minute (reported)Example
is / arewas / were"He said the deal was on track."
willwould"She said they would close on Friday."
cancould"They said they could proceed."

Common Mistake:

"He says the deal is on track and we will close on Friday."

"He said the deal was on track and they would close on Friday."

As minutes registram o que ja foi dito — tudo recua um passo: is → was, will → would, can → could.
