Stage 1.1: Small Talk Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you start friendly conversations with strangers.

Stranger -- desconhecido / estranho
"I started a chat with a friendly stranger at the cafe."
Icebreaker -- quebra-gelo
"A comment about the weather is a classic icebreaker."
To chat -- conversar / bater papo
"We chatted for ten minutes while waiting in line."
Coincidence -- coincidência
"What a coincidence -- we are both from Brazil!"
In common -- em comum
"We have a lot in common: we both love travel."
Awkward -- constrangedor / sem graça
"The silence felt awkward, so I asked a simple question."
Polite -- educado / cortês
"It is polite to smile and say hello first."
Small world -- como o mundo é pequeno
"You know my cousin? Small world!"
To get along -- se dar bem (com alguém)
"We got along right away and exchanged numbers."
To break the ice -- quebrar o gelo
"A friendly question is the best way to break the ice."
Welcoming -- acolhedor / receptivo
"The people there were so welcoming to visitors."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the conversation and answer the questions.

Tânia is visiting an art gallery abroad. A friendly stranger smiles and says, "Wonderful museum, isn't it?" That simple comment is a perfect icebreaker. Tânia replies and they begin to chat. "Where are you from?" the man asks. "I'm from Brazil," she says. "You enjoy art, don't you?" he laughs. "What a coincidence -- I painted for years!" They have a lot in common, and the silence is no longer awkward. The people there are very welcoming. It really is a small world.

1. How does the stranger break the ice?
A By commenting on the wonderful museum.
B By complaining about the tickets.
C By asking for money.
2. Which sentence is a tag question?
A "Where are you from?"
B "Wonderful museum, isn't it?"
C "I'm from Brazil."
3. Why is it a "small world"?
A The museum is very small.
B They both turn out to share a love of art.
C They live on the same street.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Questions & Tag Questions

Grammar

Para fazer small talk, você combina perguntas diretas (Where are you from?) e tag questions -- aquele "né?" no final da frase. A tag inverte a forma do verbo: frase afirmativa leva tag negativa, e vice-versa. / To make small talk, you mix direct questions with tag questions (the "né?" at the end). The tag reverses the verb: a positive sentence takes a negative tag, and the other way around.

Tipo / TypeEnglishPortuguês
Wh- questionWhere are you from?De onde você é?
Yes/No questionDo you like the city?Você gosta da cidade?
Tag (+ frase / - tag)It's a lovely day, isn't it?É um dia lindo, né?
Tag (- frase / + tag)You aren't from here, are you?Você não é daqui, né?
Tag (verbo principal)You like art, don't you?Você gosta de arte, né?
