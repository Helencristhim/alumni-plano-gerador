Chapter 3: Question Forms

Questions & Tags

The engine of every conversation

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Where are you from?"

"Do you like the city?"

"It's a lovely day, isn't it?"

"You're a tourist, aren't you?"

FormStructureExample
Wh- questionWh- + auxiliary + subjectWhere are you from?
Yes/No questiondo/are + subject + verbDo you like the city?
Tag (+ / -)positive sentence + negative tagIt's lovely, isn't it?
Tag (- / +)negative sentence + positive tagYou aren't from here, are you?

A tag question repeats the auxiliary verb and flips it. We use it to be friendly and to invite agreement.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"It's lovely, is it?"

✓ RIGHT

"It's lovely, isn't it?"

✗ WRONG

"You like it, like you?"

✓ RIGHT

"You like it, don't you?"

Chapter 4: A Chat Abroad

A Stranger at the Museum

Tânia meets a friendly traveler

Dialogue

Tânia Meets a Traveler

D
Wonderful museum, isn't it? Are you visiting the city too?
T
Yes, I am! It really is beautiful. You're a traveler too, aren't you?
D
I am! I'm from Australia. Where are you from?
T
I'm from Brazil. What a coincidence -- I lived in Sydney for two years!
D
Small world! We have a lot in common, then. The people here are so welcoming, aren't they?
T
They really are. Well, I should keep exploring. It was nice chatting with you. Have a lovely day!
