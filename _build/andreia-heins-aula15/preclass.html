Aula 15 -- Pre-class

Reaching Agreement

Agree and disagree politely, reach a compromise, and turn the discussion into clear action items with owners and deadlines. New vocabulary: agree, disagree, point, compromise, consensus, action item, vote, assign, follow-up, finalize.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agree -- to have or accept the same opinion as someone
"I think we all agree on the workaround." (Concordar)
Disagree -- to have a different opinion from someone
"I'm afraid I disagree about the timeline." (Discordar)
Point -- an idea or opinion you make in a discussion
"That is a fair point about the deadline." (Argumento / observação)
Compromise -- a middle solution where each side gives a little
"As a compromise, we add a buffer." (Meio-termo / acordo)
Consensus -- general agreement among the whole group
"Borrowing a console is the consensus." (Consenso)
Action item -- a specific task someone must do after a meeting
"Each action item needs one owner." (Tarefa / ação a executar)
Vote -- to formally choose a decision as a group, often by raising hands
"Let us vote on the final plan." (Votar)
Assign -- to give a task or responsibility to a person
"Let us assign one owner to each task." (Atribuir / designar)
Follow-up -- a later check to confirm a task was done
"Each task needs a follow-up date." (Acompanhamento)
Finalize -- to complete and agree on the final version of a plan
"We finalize the plan in the meeting." (Finalizar / fechar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

In the meeting, Andreia helps the team reach a decision. On the workaround, she says, "I agree. Borrowing a console is the right move. That is our consensus." On the timeline, opinions differ. A colleague wants a full month. Andreia does not say "no" directly. Instead she says, "I see your point, but a full month is too long." Then she offers a compromise: "Why don't we keep the date and add a three-day buffer?" The team accepts. Now they assign the action items. "I will set the vendor deadline by Friday," says Andreia. "David, can you follow up with cardiology by Thursday?" Each task has one owner and a follow-up date. With everyone on board, they finalize the plan today.

1. Andreia says "I see your point, but a full month is too long." What is she doing?
A Disagreeing politely -- she recognizes the other person first, then differs.
B Agreeing completely with the colleague.
C Refusing to discuss the timeline at all.
2. When Andreia assigns an action item, what does she always include?
A Only the task, with no owner and no date.
B One owner for the task and a clear deadline.
C A general promise to do it "soon".
3. What is the compromise the team accepts on the timeline?
A Keep the original date but add a three-day buffer.
B Delay the whole project by a full month.
C Cancel the practice sessions completely.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Agreeing, Disagreeing & Assigning

GRAMMAR

Como concordar, discordar com educação e atribuir tarefas. / How to agree, disagree politely and assign action items.

Function / FunçãoPhrase / FraseExample / Exemplo
ConcordarI agree / Good pointI agree, that is our consensus.
Discordar com educaçãoI see your point, but...I see your point, but the date is tight.
Meio-termoAs a compromise...As a compromise, we add a buffer.
Atribuir tarefaCan you / Would you + verbo base?Can you follow up by Friday?

Atenção / Note: "agree" é verbo -- diga I agree (não "I am agree"). Para discordar com educação, reconheça o outro primeiro ("I see your point") e depois use but. Ao atribuir uma tarefa, use Can you ou Would you + verbo base, e sempre nomeie o responsável e o prazo. / "agree" is a verb; recognize the other person before disagreeing; name the owner and the deadline.
