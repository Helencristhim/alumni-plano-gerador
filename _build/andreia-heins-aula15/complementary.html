Lesson 15 -- Reaching Agreement

Series
The Resident -- any episode with a board or team decision

Watch how the medical staff debate a plan, disagree politely and reach a compromise before they finalize a decision. A natural model for agreeing, pushing back gently, and assigning who does what.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two phrases people use to disagree politely (for example, "I see your point, but...") and one moment where someone assigns a task with a deadline.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run a Meeting That Ends in a Decision"

The hosts explain how to move a group from discussion to a clear decision: build consensus, handle disagreement, reach a compromise, then assign action items with one owner and a deadline each.

Note three tips for closing a meeting. Then record a one-minute close on a real decision at your work, using at least four new words from today, such as consensus, compromise, action item and deadline.

YouTube
Business English -- "Agreeing and Disagreeing Politely in Meetings"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to agree, how to disagree politely without sounding rude ("I see your point, but..."), and how to suggest a compromise to reach a decision.

After watching, write five sentences for a real meeting: one to agree, two to disagree politely, one to offer a compromise, and one to assign an action item with a deadline. Then say them out loud.
