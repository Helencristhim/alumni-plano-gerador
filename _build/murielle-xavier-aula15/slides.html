Chapter 3

Comparative Adjectives

Short, long, and irregular forms

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Our turnover rate is lower than last year."

"This region is more competitive than the others."

"Our results are better than the forecast."

"The gap is as wide as last quarter."

TypeFormExample
Short adjectiveadj + -er + thanhigher than, lower than
Long adjectivemore + adj + thanmore efficient than
Irregulargood→better, bad→worsebetter than, worse than
Equalityas + adj + asas high as last year
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

This region is more bigger than the North.

✓ RIGHT

This region is bigger than the North.

✗ WRONG

Our results are gooder than last year.

✓ RIGHT

Our results are better than last year.

✗ WRONG

Turnover is more high this quarter.

✓ RIGHT

Turnover is higher this quarter.

✗ WRONG

The gap is as wider as before.

✓ RIGHT

The gap is as wide as before.

Short adjectives add -er; never use more with them. Long adjectives use more. Good and bad are irregular: better, worse. Use as + base adjective + as for equality.

Chapter 4

The Comparison

Comparing regions before a global meeting

Dialogue

Two Regions

E
Murielle, I have the turnover numbers for both regions. Which one looks stronger?
M
Let me see. The South region is bigger than the North, but its turnover is higher.
E
Interesting. So the North is more efficient, even though it is smaller.
M
Exactly. The disparity is significant. The North retains staff far better than the South.
E
And costs? Is the North more expensive to run?
M
No, it is roughly the same. The margin in results is wider than the margin in cost.
E
So our recommendation is clear. The North model is better than the South model.
M
Yes. I will present it as the more competitive option for the whole company.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Which region is bigger, the South or the North?
The South region is bigger than the North.
2. Why is the North the better model?
It is more efficient and retains staff far better, at roughly the same cost.
3. How will Murielle present the North model?
As the more competitive option for the whole company.
