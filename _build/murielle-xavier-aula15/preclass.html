Bigger, Better, Different -- Comparatives in Global HR

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Significant -- meaningful or large enough to matter (significativo)
"The gap between the two teams is significant."
Slightly -- by a small amount (levemente / um pouco)
"Turnover is slightly lower than last year."
Considerably -- by a large amount (consideravelmente)
"The branch is considerably more efficient."
Roughly -- approximately, about (aproximadamente)
"The costs are roughly the same in both regions."
Margin -- the difference between two amounts (margem)
"The margin between our best and worst teams is wide."
Disparity -- a big and unfair difference (disparidade)
"There is a disparity between the two departments."
Comparable -- similar enough to be compared (comparável)
"The two regions are comparable in size."
Ratio -- a relationship between two numbers (proporção / razão)
"The attrition ratio is higher in Sales."
Competitive -- as good as or better than others (competitivo)
"Our salaries are more competitive than before."
Narrow -- small in difference or width (estreito / pequeno)
"The gap between them is very narrow."
Outweigh -- to be more important than (superar / pesar mais que)
"The benefits outweigh the costs."
Discrepancy -- a difference that should not be there (discrepância)
"There is a discrepancy between the target and the result."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparative Adjectives

GRAMMAR

Use comparativos para comparar duas coisas. Adjetivo curto: adjetivo + -er + than (bigger than). Adjetivo longo: more + adjetivo + than (more efficient than). Irregulares: good → better, bad → worse. Igualdade: as + adjetivo + as (as high as).

TipoFormaExemplo
Adjetivo curtoadj + -er + thanTurnover is higher than last year.
Adjetivo longomore + adj + thanIt is more efficient than before.
Irregulargood→better, bad→worseResults are better than the forecast.
Igualdadeas + adj + asIt is as high as last year.

Atenção: adjetivos curtos NÃO usam more (não diga more bigger). Adjetivos longos usam more, nunca -er. Better e worse são irregulares.

Quiz
