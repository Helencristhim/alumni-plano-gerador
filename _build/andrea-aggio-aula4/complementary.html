Aula 4 -- Talking About Your World

Series
Down to Earth with Zac Efron -- Any Episode (Netflix)

Zac travels the world and, at the end of each trip, tells the story of what he saw and learned — all in natural past-tense English. It is a perfect model for narrating a journey. Connection to Lesson 4: notice how he describes where he went, what happened, and how each experience turned out.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 5 past-simple verbs (2 regular, 3 irregular) that he uses to tell a story.

Podcast
Zero To Travel -- "Travel Stories" episodes

Real travelers tell the stories of their most memorable trips: the challenges, the highlights, and how everything turned out. It is rich, natural past-tense storytelling in English. Connection to Lesson 4: how people structure a story from beginning to outcome.

Tip: listen twice. Note how each storyteller describes the challenge and then the outcome of the trip.

YouTube
"Past Simple: Regular and Irregular Verbs" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on today's exact grammar: regular -ed endings, irregular past forms, and how to make questions and negatives with 'did'. Connection to Lesson 4: the grammar that lets you tell any story about a trip with confidence and accuracy.

Tip: pause after each example and tell one sentence about your own last trip, then turn it into a question with 'did'.
