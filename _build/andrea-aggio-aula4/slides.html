Chapter 4: The Code

The Past Simple

I attended · I went · Did you...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I attended WTM last November."

"We signed a new contract in Portugal."

"I went on a fam trip to Porto."

"Did you meet any new suppliers?"

Which verbs just add -ed, and which change completely?

FormUseExample
Regular past
verb + -ed		Finished action at a finished timeWe signed the contract.
Irregular past
special form		Common verbs change completelyI went / met / did / flew.
Questions & negatives
did + base verb		Use 'did' + the base form (no -ed)Did you meet them? / I did not go.

Common irregulars: go → went, meet → met, do → did, fly → flew, take → took.

Language Focus

Regular or Irregular?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I go to WTM last year."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to WTM last year."

✗ WRONG

"Did you signed the contract?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you sign the contract?"

A finished time (last year) needs the past simple. After 'did', always use the base verb, never the -ed form.

Dialogue

How Was the Trip?

R
Andrea! Welcome back. How did the trip to Portugal go?
A
It turned out great! I visited six hotels and did a full inspection of each one.
R
Impressive. Did you manage to negotiate good rates?
A
I did. The contract negotiation was tough, but we agreed on a fifteen percent commission.
R
That is a great deal. What was the biggest challenge?
A
The schedule — we drove four hundred kilometers in three days. But I ended up with three new partners.
R
Three partners in one trip? That is a real achievement.
A
Thank you! The whole outcome was better than I ever expected.
