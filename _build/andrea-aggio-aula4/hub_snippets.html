Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Highlight -- the best or most memorable part of an experience (ponto alto, destaque)
"The highlight of the trip was the hotel inspection."
Achievement -- something good you succeeded in doing (conquista, realização)
"Signing that contract was a real achievement."
Challenge -- a difficult task that tests your skills (desafio)
"The biggest challenge was the tight schedule."
Outcome -- the final result of an action or event (resultado)
"The outcome of the meeting was very positive."
To turn out -- to happen in a particular way in the end (acabar sendo, resultar)
"The trip turned out better than expected."
To end up -- to finally be in a situation or place (acabar em, terminar)
"We ended up signing three new contracts."
Tour operator -- a company that creates and sells travel packages (operadora de turismo)
"We partnered with a local tour operator."
Contract negotiation -- the discussion to agree the terms of a deal (negociação de contrato)
"The contract negotiation took two days."
Hotel inspection -- a visit to check a hotel's quality (vistoria de hotel)
"I did a hotel inspection before signing."
Fam trip -- a familiarization trip for agents to experience a destination (viagem de familiarização)
"We joined a fam trip to Portugal."
Rates -- the prices a hotel charges (tarifas, diárias)
"We agreed on special rates for next season."
Commission -- the percentage a company earns on each sale (comissão)
"The commission on that package is fifteen percent."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Last November, Andrea attended WTM in London. She went with a clear goal: to find new hotel partners. The first two days were a real challenge, because the halls were huge and the schedule was tight. On the third day, she met Ricardo, a tour operator from Lisbon. He invited her on a fam trip to Portugal, where she did a full hotel inspection of six properties. The contract negotiation was tough, but in the end it turned out perfectly: they agreed on great rates and a fair commission. Andrea ended up signing three new contracts. The highlight, she says, was the moment she realized her English no longer got in the way.

1. Why do we say "Last November, Andrea attended WTM"?
A Because it is a finished action at a finished time (past simple).
B Because it is happening right now.
C Because it is a routine she repeats every week.
2. "She met Ricardo" uses 'met' (not 'meeted') because:
A The verb 'meet' is regular and adds -ed.
B The verb 'meet' is irregular, so its past form changes completely.
C It is a present tense verb.
3. Which past question is correct?
A Did you signed the contract?
B Did you sign the contract?
C Did you signing the contract?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Como contar uma história no passado com a forma verbal certa (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Regular verbs
verb + -ed		Ação terminada, tempo terminado. Finished action, finished time.We signed the contract.
Irregular verbs
special past form		Verbos comuns mudam completamente. Common verbs change.I went / met / did / flew.
Questions & negatives
did + base verb		Use 'did' + verbo na base (sem -ed). Use 'did' + base form.Did you meet them?
Common irregularsgo → went · meet → met · do → did · fly → flew · take → took · be → was/were

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

A colleague asks: "Where did you go last month?" You answer:
A I go to Portugal last month.
B I went to Portugal last month.
C I am going to Portugal last month.
You want to ask about a past deal. Which is correct?
A Did you signed the contract?
B Did you sign the contract?
C Do you signed the contract?
You want to describe how a hard trip finished well. You say:
A It was tough at first, but in the end it turned out perfectly.
B It is tough, but it turns out perfectly.
C It will be tough, but it turned out perfectly.
You want to share a professional achievement. The best sentence is:
A I ended up with three new partners after the trip.

Reflection
