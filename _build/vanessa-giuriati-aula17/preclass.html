Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Explaining & Giving Reasons

Always say why. Don't just give a flat answer — explain the reason behind it. Give the cause with because and since, and the result with so and that's why. When you connect cause and result, people follow your thinking and trust you more. Key words: reason, cause, lead to, affect, avoid, solve, struggle, aware.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Reason -- the cause or explanation; the "why" behind something (motivo/razão)
"The reason is that two people were sick."
Cause -- the thing that makes something happen (or: to make it happen) (causa/causar)
"The cause of the delay was a sick team."
Lead to -- to result in something; to make something happen as a result (levar a/resultar em)
"The delay led to extra costs."
Affect -- to have an effect on something; to change it (verb, with 'a') (afetar)
"The rain affected the schedule."
Avoid -- to stay away from something, or stop it from happening (evitar)
"We changed the plan to avoid more delays."
Solve -- to find an answer or solution to a problem (resolver/solucionar)
"We need to solve this before the launch."
Struggle -- to have difficulty doing something; to try hard against a problem (ter dificuldade/batalhar)
"We struggled to finish on time."
Aware -- knowing that something exists or is happening (ciente/consciente)
"I'm aware of the problem and we're on it."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A client was upset, so I explained the delay calmly. "The main cause is that two key people were sick, so we lost almost a week," I said. We struggled to cover the gap, and since we want the product to be solid, we chose to test it properly. Rushing would lead to bugs. That's why we delayed, to avoid a bad launch. The sick team also affected our schedule. I told him we were fully aware of the pressure and would solve the last issues by Friday. He went quiet, then said, "Thank you for being clear." I learned this: people can accept bad news, but not being kept in the dark. When you explain the reason behind a decision, a complaint turns into a conversation.

1. What was the cause of the delay?
A The client changed his mind.
B Two key people were sick, so they lost almost a week.
C The budget was too small.
2. Why did they choose to delay rather than rush?
A Because they wanted a longer holiday.
B Because rushing would lead to bugs, and they wanted to avoid a bad launch.
C Because the client asked them to wait.
3. Which linker correctly gives the result in "Two people were sick, ___ we lost a week"?
A "...because..."
B "...so..."
C "...since..."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cause & Result Linkers

GRAMMAR

Como ligar a causa e o resultado. / How to link the cause and the result.

LinkerEnglishPortuguês
because / since (cause)"We were late because the train broke down."Introduz a CAUSA (porque)
so / that's why (result)"The train broke down, so we were late."Introduz o RESULTADO (então/por isso)
Word orderCause first → comma: "Since it rained, we stopped." Cause after → no comma: "We stopped because it rained."Causa antes = vírgula; causa depois = sem vírgula
TipAlways say why. Connecting the cause and the result lets the other person follow your thinking.
⚠ CarefulPick ONE linker per link: "Because it rained, we stopped" (not "Because... so..."). Use because + full sentence, not "because of" + sentence. so and that's why introduce the result, not the cause.

Stage 2: Put the Explanation in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de uma explicação clara na ordem correta.

?After that, I add the result with so and say so we lost almost a week of work.
?In the end, I reassure the other person, since we want the product to be solid, we chose to test it properly.
?First, I state the fact clearly and say we have to delay the launch.
?Next, I connect it all with that is why and say that is why we need a few more days.
?Then I give the reason with because and say because two key people were sick.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

You want to give the reason for being late. Best choice:
A "I'm late because the train broke down."
B "I'm late because of the train broke down."
C "Because I'm late, so the train broke down."
You want to give the result of a problem. Best choice:
A "The team struggled, so we asked for more time."
B "The team struggled because we asked for more time."
C "The team struggled, that's why because we asked for time."
You want to reassure a client calmly. Best choice:
A "We're aware of the issue, so we'll solve it by Friday."
B "It's not my problem, sorry."
C "I don't know what happened."
You want to explain a decision with "that's why". Best choice:
A "Rushing would lead to bugs. That's why we delayed."
B "That's why rushing would lead to bugs, we delayed."
C "We delayed because that's why rushing leads to bugs."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Think of a real decision or problem from your work or life. Explain it out loud: state the fact, give the cause with because or since, add the result with so or that's why, and say what you did about it. Always say why. Use at least three of today's words (reason, cause, lead to, affect, avoid, solve, struggle, aware) and speak slowly and calmly.
