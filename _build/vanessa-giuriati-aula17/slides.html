Chapter 3

Cause & Result

Linking words: because, since, so, that's why

Grammar Discovery

Connecting the Two

"We were late because the train broke down."

"Since two people were sick, we lost a week."

"The train broke down, so we were late."

"The train broke down. That's why we were late."

LinkerIntroducesExample
because / sincethe CAUSE (the reason)We were late because the train broke down.
so / that's whythe RESULT (what happened)The train broke down, so we were late.
Word orderCause first? Use a comma: "Because the train broke, we were late." Cause after? No comma needed: "We were late because the train broke." "so" and "that's why" always come before the result.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Because it rained, so we stopped."

✓ RIGHT

"Because it rained, we stopped."

✗ WRONG

"It was late because of the train broke."

✓ RIGHT

"It was late because the train broke."

✗ WRONG

"That's why the train broke, we were late."

✓ RIGHT

"The train broke. That's why we were late."

Pick ONE linker per link (because OR so, never both). "because" + full sentence (not "because of" + sentence). "so" and "that's why" introduce the result, not the cause.

Chapter 4

Real Voices

Explaining, out loud

Dialogue

Explaining the Delay

D
Vanessa, why is the launch delayed again?
V
I understand your frustration. The main cause is that two key people were sick, so we lost almost a week.
D
A week is a lot. Couldn't someone cover for them?
V
We tried, but the team struggled to cover the gap. Since we want the product to be solid, we chose to test it properly.
D
So rushing it could have made things worse?
V
Exactly. Rushing would lead to bugs. That's why we delayed, to avoid a bad launch.
D
All right. I appreciate you being clear. When can we launch?
V
We're aware of the pressure, and we'll solve the last issues by Friday.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What was the cause of the delay?
Two key people were sick, so they lost almost a week, and the team struggled to cover the gap.
2. Why did they choose to delay instead of rushing?
Because rushing would lead to bugs. That's why they delayed — to avoid a bad launch, since they want the product to be solid.
3. How did the client react to her explanation?
He appreciated her being clear, and the complaint turned into a calm conversation about the new date.
Listening

Why Explaining Matters

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. What did she use to do, and why didn't it work?
She just gave short, flat answers (we're late, the project failed). People nodded but never really understood her.
2. What simple habit changed everything?
Always saying why: "we're late because the supplier missed the deadline." The word "because" turns a flat statement into a real explanation.
3. What is the "other side" she also learned?
Giving the result too: "we lost a week, so we changed the plan." Connecting cause and result lets the other person follow her thinking and trust her.
Listening

A Calm Explanation

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. Why was the client upset, and what did he do differently this time?
The client was upset about a delayed launch. Instead of just apologizing, he explained the reason calmly.
2. What reason did he give?
The reason was that two key people were sick, so they lost a week. Because they want the product to be solid, they chose to test it properly instead of rushing.
3. What did he learn about explaining?
People can accept bad news, but not being kept in the dark. Calmly explaining the reason turns a complaint into a conversation, and clients trust you more.

0 / 4 errors found

"Because it rained, we stopped early." (use because OR so, not both)
"We were late because of the train broke down."
"We were late because the train broke down." (because + full sentence, not "because of")
"The team struggled, that's why is the delay."
"The team struggled. That's why we're delayed." (that's why + result, normal sentence)
"Two people were sick because we lost a week."
"Two people were sick, so we lost a week." (the result needs "so", not "because")
