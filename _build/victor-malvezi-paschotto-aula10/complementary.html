Lesson 10 -- Difficult Conversations: Handling Tension with Care

Series
Ted Lasso (Apple TV+) -- any episode with a one-to-one between a coach and a struggling player

Ted is a master of the difficult conversation: he opens with empathy, stays candid about the problem, and almost never puts the other person on the defensive. Watch how he handles a tense moment with a player or a colleague — he names the issue honestly but keeps it constructive, defuses the tension, and protects the relationship every time.

Tip: pause after a hard conversation and write how Ted opened it. Could you rewrite his first line with "I was hoping..." or an I-statement?

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- any episode on feedback, conflict, or psychological safety

Adam Grant unpacks how the best leaders give hard feedback without damaging trust: why people get defensive, how a little empathy defuses tension, and how to stay candid yet constructive. It is the exact mindset Victor needs before a tough one-to-one at Brinks.

Tip: after listening, draft three lines for one real conversation: a tentative opener, one candid sentence about the issue, and a closing line to clear the air.

YouTube
Adar Cohen -- "3 ways to lead the toughest conversations" (TED)

A short, practical TED talk on how to walk into the conversations everyone avoids: ask honest questions, share the real reason, and stay in the room when it gets hard. It maps perfectly onto the open-with-care, stay-candid skill from this class.

Tip: watch with a notebook. For one conversation you've been avoiding, write the first question you would ask to open it without an accusation.
