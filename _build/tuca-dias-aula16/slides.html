Chapter 3 -- Too / Not Enough

Too Big or Not Big Enough

Saying why a size is wrong

Listen & Repeat

Too & Not Enough in Action

"This jacket is too big for me."

"These boots are too expensive this month."

"This shirt is not big enough. Do you have a larger size?"

"The fitting room was not warm enough, but the jacket fits."

Grammar Rule

Too & Not Enough: too / not ... enough

StructureExampleNote
too + adjectiveThis jacket is too big.more than you want ("too" before the adjective)
too + adjectiveThese boots are too expensive.a problem: the price is more than you want
not + adjective + enoughThis shirt is not big enough.less than you need ("enough" after the adjective)
not + adjective + enoughThe coat is not warm enough.NOT "enough warm" -- enough goes after

Key idea: too goes before the adjective and means "more than you want" (too big = larger than you need). enough goes after the adjective; not ... enough means "less than you need" (not big enough = too small). Say "big enough", never "enough big".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"This shirt is not enough big."
"This shirt is not big enough."

Enough always comes after the adjective ("big enough"). Too comes before the adjective ("too big").
