Size -- the number or letter that shows how big a piece of clothing is
"Do you have this in a smaller size?" (Tamanho)
Fit -- to be the right size and shape for your body
"These boots fit perfectly." (Servir / caber)
Try on -- to put on clothes in the shop to see if they fit
"Can I try this on before I buy it?" (Experimentar / provar)
Fitting room -- a small private room in a shop where you try clothes on
"The fitting rooms are at the back." (Provador)
Browse -- to look at things in a shop slowly, without deciding to buy yet
"I am just browsing, thank you." (Olhar / dar uma olhada)
Aisle -- the long, narrow space between rows of shelves in a shop
"Shoes are in the first aisle." (Corredor)
Cashier -- the person at the desk who takes your money and gives a receipt
"You pay at the cashier near the door." (Caixa / atendente do caixa)
Discount -- an amount taken off the normal price, so you pay less
"These boots have a thirty percent discount." (Desconto)
Faulty -- broken or not made correctly, so it does not work as it should
"The shirt was faulty; there was a hole in it." (Com defeito)
Exchange -- to give back an item and get a different one instead
"Can I exchange this for a bigger size?" (Trocar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When you shop for clothes, you often say why something is wrong. To say that something is more than you want, use too before the adjective: "This jacket is too big." "These boots are too expensive." To say that something is less than you need, use not + adjective + enough: "This shirt is not big enough." Notice that too goes before the adjective, but enough goes after it. We never say "enough big" -- we say "big enough". If the size is wrong, you can ask the assistant for a different size, try it on in the fitting room, or, after you buy it, exchange it or ask for a refund if it is faulty.

1. Which sentence means the jacket is larger than you want?
A This jacket is too big.
B This jacket is not big enough.
C This jacket is enough big.
2. Where does "enough" go?
A before the adjective ("enough big")
B after the adjective ("big enough")
C after the noun ("big size enough")
3. "This shirt is not big enough" means the shirt is:
A too small for you
B too big for you

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Too & Not Enough

GRAMMAR

Como dizer que algo é demais ou de menos. / How to say something is too much or not enough.

Structure / EstruturaExampleNote / Nota
too + adjetivoThis jacket is too big.mais do que o ideal / more than you want
too + adjetivoThese boots are too expensive."too" vem ANTES / before the adjective
not + adjetivo + enoughThis shirt is not big enough.menos do que o ideal / less than you need
not + adjetivo + enoughThe coat is not warm enough."enough" vem DEPOIS / after the adjective

Key point / Ponto-chave: too vem ANTES do adjetivo e significa "demais" (too big = grande demais). enough vem DEPOIS do adjetivo; not ... enough significa "de menos" (not big enough = pequeno demais). Diga "big enough", nunca "enough big". / "Too" goes before the adjective; "enough" goes after.
