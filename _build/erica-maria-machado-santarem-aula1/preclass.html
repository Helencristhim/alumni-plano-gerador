Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Hello! My name is Ana. I am from Curitiba, but now I live in Sao Paulo. I work at a biotechnology company. My job is interesting and I really like it. My friend Pedro is a researcher too. He works in the same laboratory. We are colleagues and good friends. Every day, I meet new people at the company. When I introduce myself, I say: "Hello, my name is Ana. Nice to meet you."

1. Why do we say "I am from Curitiba" and not "I from Curitiba"?
A Because we need the verb to be (am) after "I".
B Because "from" is a verb.
C Because "Curitiba" needs the word "am".
2. Why does the text say "He works" with an -s, but "I work" without it?
A Because "work" always has an -s.
B Because in present simple, he/she/it adds -s to the verb.
C Because "He" is plural.
3. Which sentence uses the verb to be correctly?
A "We is colleagues."
B "We are colleagues."
C "We am colleagues."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Verb To Be + Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Como usar am, is, are e o present simple para se apresentar.

Subject (Sujeito)To beExample
I (eu)amI am Erica. (Eu sou a Erica.)
He / She / It (ele/ela)isShe is from Sao Paulo. (Ela é de SP.)
You / We / TheyareWe are colleagues. (Nós somos colegas.)
Present simple (I/you/we/they)no -sI work, I live. (Eu trabalho, eu moro.)
Present simple (he/she/it)Adicione -s: She works, he lives. Dica: para idade, use to be — "I am 55 years old" (nunca "I have 55 years").
