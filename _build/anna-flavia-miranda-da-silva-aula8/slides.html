Chapter 3: Going To

Talk About the Future

am / is / are + going to + verb

Discover the Code

Listen and Notice

"I am going to send the report."

"She is going to call the partner."

"We are going to have a meeting next week."

Subjectam / is / are + going to + verb
Iam going to send
He / Sheis going to call
We / They / Youare going to have
QuestionAre you going to join?

Use going to for a plan you decided before. The verb after it never changes: go, send, call, have.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I going to call the partner."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to call the partner."

✗ WRONG

"She is going to sends the report."

✓ RIGHT

"She is going to send the report."

Chapter 4: Scheduling a Meeting

Book a Meeting

Choose a day, a time and confirm

Dialogue

Choosing a Day

A
Hello, Mr. Lima. I am calling about our meeting. Are you free next week?
L
Hello, Anna. Let me check my calendar. I am busy on Monday and Tuesday.
A
Are you free on Wednesday? We are going to need one hour.
L
Yes, Wednesday is good. What time are we going to meet?
A
At two o'clock. I am going to send you a calendar invite today.
L
Perfect. I am going to prepare the report. See you on Wednesday.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Make the Plan

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play

Ask About the Week

Scenario: You call a partner to plan a meeting. Greet the partner and ask if they are free next week, using the key phrases.

Key phrases:

I am calling about our meeting. Are you free next week? We are going to need one hour.
Role-Play

Choose a Day and a Time

Scenario: The partner is busy on Monday and Tuesday. Offer another day, choose a time, and say you are going to send a calendar invite.

Key phrases:

Are you free on Wednesday? At two o'clock. I am going to send an invite.
Free Practice

Book the Whole Meeting

Scenario: Call a partner, ask about next week, find a free day, choose a time, say what you are both going to do, and confirm the appointment.

No key phrases. Make the plan!
