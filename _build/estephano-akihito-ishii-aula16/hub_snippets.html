Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Situation -- a moment when you must do or say something
"At the store is a real situation." (situação)
Survive -- to be okay in a difficult moment
"I can survive a day in English." (sobreviver)
Request -- when you politely ask for something
"Can I have water? is a polite request." (pedido)
Direction -- the way to a place: left, right, straight
"I ask for directions to the station." (direção)
Health -- how well or sick your body is
"I talk about my health at the clinic." (saúde)
Feeling -- how you are inside: happy, sad, tired
"Happy is a good feeling." (sentimento)
Weather -- sun, rain, cold or hot outside
"The weather is cold and rainy." (tempo/clima)
Shop -- to buy things at a store
"I shop for water and coffee." (fazer compras)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Estephano has a busy day. At the store, he says: "Can I have a coffee?" Then he is lost, so he asks: "How do I get to the station?" A man says: "Go straight and turn left." In the afternoon, Estephano is not well. He says: "I have a fever and my head hurts." So he calls the clinic. He can survive a busy day in English!

1. What does Estephano say at the store?
A "Can I have a coffee?"
B "How do I get to the store?"
C "I have a coffee."
2. What does he ask when he is lost?
A "Can I have the station?"
B "How do I get to the station?"
C "My station hurts."
3. How does Estephano feel in the afternoon?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Block 2 Patterns

GRAMMAR

Os quatro padrões-chave do Bloco 2. / The four key patterns of Block 2.

Pattern / EstruturaUse / UsoExample
Can I have...?Pedir algo na lojaCan I have a coffee, please?
How do I get to...?Pedir direçõesHow do I get to the station?
I have a + problemDizer um problema de saúdeI have a fever.
My + body part + hurtsDizer que uma parte do corpo dóiMy head hurts.
Atenção: cada situação usa o seu padrão. Na loja use Can I have; para direções use How do I get to; para saúde use I have a (não "I am") e My ... hurts com -s.
