Lesson 11 -- Reading the Room

Series
Shogun (2024) -- Episode 1: "Anjin"

A masterclass in high-context, cross-cultural communication. Watch how meaning is carried by silence, etiquette, and indirect language, and how characters must read between the lines to survive negotiations.

Note three moments where a character says one thing but means another. How would you describe each person using a relative clause (the lord who..., the translator whose...)?

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "Negotiating Across Cultures"

Harvard Business Review interviews negotiation experts on high-context vs low-context cultures, directness, and the role of reciprocity. Directly relevant to working with global supply-chain partners.

Write down two cultural differences the guests describe. Rewrite each as a non-defining relative clause using which or who.

YouTube
Erin Meyer -- "The Culture Map" (Talks at Google)

The author of The Culture Map explains how communication, persuasion, and trust differ across cultures. Essential framing for anyone negotiating with international vendors.

After watching, prepare a 2-minute briefing for a colleague on the etiquette of one high-context culture, using at least four relative clauses.
