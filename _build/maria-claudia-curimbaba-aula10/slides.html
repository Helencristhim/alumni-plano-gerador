Chapter 3 -- Linking Language

Language Discovery

The words that connect your ideas

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What job does each highlighted word do? Do they all do the same thing?

"Firstly, we ran a clean meeting and agreed on the agenda."

"Moreover, the numbers were presented clearly to the board."

"However, the first offer was lower than we hoped."

"In conclusion, the deal was approved and the quarter was a success."

The Rule

The Linking Toolkit

FunctionLinking wordsExample
Sequence / orderFirstly... / Then... / Finally..."Firstly, we set the agenda."
Add a pointMoreover... / In addition..."Moreover, costs went down."
ContrastHowever... / On the other hand..."However, the offer was low."
Conclude / summarizeIn conclusion... / To sum up... / All in all..."In conclusion, we succeeded."

Key idea: a B2 speaker does not just list facts -- they signpost them. Choose the linking word for the job: order, add, contrast, or conclude. The right marker makes a long summary easy to follow and sound polished.

Integration

Connect the Whole Block

One smooth summary chains the structures of Block 1 together: passive reporting, a conditional, and an opinion.

"To recap, the proposal was approved by the board." (passive)

"Moreover, if we sign this week, we will expand by June." (first conditional)

"All in all, from my perspective, it is the right move." (opinion)

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The report was wrote by the team."
"The report was written by the team."

The passive is be + past participle -- written, not "wrote"; sent, not "sended"; done, not "did". And for your own view, say "From my perspective", never "according to me".

Practice

Choose the Link

"_____Firstly, we reviewed the agenda and the minutes." (sequence)
"The plan is cheap. _____Moreover, it is fast to roll out." (add a point)
"The numbers were strong. _____However, costs rose in March." (contrast)
"_____In conclusion, it was a great first quarter." (conclude)
