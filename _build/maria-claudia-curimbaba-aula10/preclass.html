Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each phrase and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

To recap -- to quickly go over the main points again
"Let me recap what we agreed in the meeting." (Recapitular/retomar)
To sum up -- to give the main idea in a few words
"To sum up, the quarter was a success." (Resumir)
In a nutshell -- very briefly, in a few words
"In a nutshell, the deal is a good one." (Em poucas palavras)
On the whole -- considering everything together; generally
"On the whole, the partners are happy." (No geral)
All in all -- after considering everything; overall
"All in all, it was a strong first block." (No fim das contas)
Key takeaway -- the single most important point to remember
"The key takeaway is to keep it simple." (Lição principal)
To consolidate -- to combine separate parts into a single strong whole
"Let's consolidate everything we learned." (Consolidar)
Moving forward -- from now on; in the future
"Moving forward, we will meet monthly." (Daqui pra frente)
In conclusion -- used to introduce your final point or summary
"In conclusion, the strategy is working." (Em conclusão)
Moreover -- in addition; what is more
"The plan is cheap. Moreover, it is fast." (Além disso)
All things considered -- after thinking about every factor
"All things considered, we should sign." (Considerando tudo)
The bottom line -- the single most important fact or result
"The bottom line is we made a profit." (O essencial/resultado final)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Over dinner, David asks me to recap the quarter. "Firstly, the meetings ran cleanly," I say. "Moreover, the numbers were presented well, and the deal was signed on time." David nods. "However, costs rose in March," I add, "so margins were tighter." Then I close the summary: "In conclusion, on the whole it was a strong start. The key takeaway for me is confidence." David smiles. "Moving forward, Block 2 is about presentations. All things considered, you're ready." "The bottom line," I reply, "is that I want to polish one thing -- the passive. I still say 'was wrote' when I mean 'was written'."

1. Which linking word does Maria use to ADD a second positive point?
A "Moreover" -- it adds another point.
B "However" -- it adds another point.
C "Firstly" -- it adds another point.
2. Which sentence is in the passive voice?
A "The meetings ran cleanly."
B "The deal was signed on time."
C "Costs rose in March."
3. What is the correct passive form of "I still say 'was wrote'"?
A "was written" -- be + past participle.
B "was wrote" is already correct.
C "was writing" -- be + -ing.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Linking & Summarizing

GRAMMAR

No nível B2, uma boa síntese não lista fatos soltos -- ela os CONECTA com linking words e os fecha com uma frase de resumo. E lembre: a voz passiva é be + partícipio passado (written, sent, signed). / At B2, a good summary does not list loose facts -- it CONNECTS them with linking words and closes with a summary phrase. And remember: the passive is be + past participle (written, sent, signed).

FunctionLinking wordsExample
Sequence / orderFirstly... / Then... / Finally..."Firstly, we set the agenda."
Add a pointMoreover... / In addition..."Moreover, costs went down."
ContrastHowever... / On the other hand..."However, the offer was low."
Conclude / summarizeIn conclusion... / To sum up... / All in all..."In conclusion, we succeeded."

Key point / Ponto-chave: escolha a linking word pela FUNÇÃO (ordem, adição, contraste, conclusão) e feche com uma frase de resumo. Na voz passiva, use sempre o partícipio passado: "the report was written" (nunca "was wrote"), "the email was sent" (nunca "was sended"). / Choose the linking word by its FUNCTION and close with a summary phrase. In the passive, always use the past participle: "was written", never "was wrote".
