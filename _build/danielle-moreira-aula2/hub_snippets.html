The Language of Culture -- Diagnosing and Describing Culture

Diagnosticar e descrever cultura organizacional em inglês: ler os números, nomear o problema e dizer a verdade difícil sem perder a sala. Key words: cultural diagnosis, core values, psychological safety, onboarding experience, culture fit, belonging, disengagement, attrition, turnover rate, toxic culture, exit interview, pulse survey, to foster a culture of, to look into, the elephant in the room. Structures: third conditional (if + had + particípio / would have + particípio) e modal perfects (should have / could have / might have) + hedging executivo (It would appear that... / To some extent...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Este é o vocabulário em que um diagnóstico cultural é escrito — o que separa uma opinião sobre a cultura de uma leitura profissional dela.

Cultural diagnosis -- a structured reading of what a culture really is, based on evidence (diagnóstico cultural)
"Before I propose anything, I would like to run a cultural diagnosis."
Core values -- the few principles a company will not trade away, even under pressure (valores essenciais)
"Core values are not what is on the wall. They are what survives a hard decision."
Psychological safety -- the belief that you can speak up or disagree without being punished (segurança psicológica)
"Psychological safety is built in the meeting where someone disagrees with you."
Onboarding experience -- the first weeks of a new hire, and how those weeks actually feel (experiência de integração)
"The onboarding experience was excellent for one week, and abandoned by the second."
Culture fit -- how well a person's way of working matches the way the team already works (aderência cultural)
"We hire for culture fit, and then we punish the first person who thinks differently."
Belonging -- the feeling that you can be yourself at work and still be part of the team (pertencimento)
"Belonging is high here. Safety is not. Those are two different problems."
Disengagement -- when people still do the job but stop caring about the outcome (desengajamento)
"Disengagement is quiet. It is the last thing you see and the first thing that happens."
Attrition -- the slow loss of people over time, one by one (perda gradual de talentos)
"Attrition never arrives as a crisis. It arrives as a resignation, and then another one."
Turnover rate -- the percentage of people who leave the company in a year (taxa de rotatividade)
"Our turnover rate climbed from nine percent to twenty six percent in three years."
Toxic culture -- a culture where fear, blame or disrespect have become normal (cultura tóxica)
"It is not a toxic culture. It is a specific problem that nobody names."
Exit interview -- the conversation with someone who is leaving, where the truth comes out (entrevista de desligamento)
"They filed the exit interviews. Nobody ever read them."
Pulse survey -- a short, frequent survey that measures how people feel right now (pesquisa de pulso)
"If they had run a pulse survey that year, they would have seen it coming."
To foster a culture of -- to create the conditions for a behavior to grow (cultivar uma cultura de)
"You foster a culture of speaking up by protecting the first person who does it."
To look into -- to investigate something properly before you judge it (investigar, apurar)
"Before we judge the team, we should look into what happened in that meeting."
The elephant in the room -- the obvious problem everyone can see and nobody names (o elefante na sala)
"Let me name the elephant in the room: nobody here disagrees with a director."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o caso e responda às perguntas.

Northline Academy was a network of bilingual schools with an excellent reputation and a serious problem. In three years, its turnover rate climbed from nine percent to twenty six percent. The company had written a beautiful set of core values, printed them and hung them in every corridor — and it had never once run a cultural diagnosis to find out whether anyone lived by them. The onboarding experience was excellent for one week and abandoned by the second. Managers stopped disagreeing with directors in meetings. The people who stayed delivered the curriculum and quietly stopped bringing new ideas: that is disengagement, and it is far more expensive than the attrition it produces. When the board finally asked what had gone wrong, the answer was uncomfortable. If leadership had run a pulse survey in that first year, they would have seen psychological safety collapsing. If someone had looked into the exit interviews instead of filing them, they would have found the elephant in the room by the second one. They should have addressed it during onboarding, and they could have fostered a culture of speaking up at almost no cost. The two coordinators who resigned might not have left if the culture had felt safe. The culture did not fail because it was a toxic culture. It failed because nobody was willing to name what everybody could already see.

1. Por que a frase "If leadership had run a pulse survey, they would have seen it" usa had run e não would have run?
A Porque a metade do if sempre leva had + particípio — o would fica só na metade do resultado.
B Porque a pesquisa foi feita, só que tarde demais.
C Porque a ação ainda pode acontecer no futuro.
2. "The two coordinators might not have left" — por que might e não would?
A Porque é uma crítica direta à liderança.
B Porque especula com prudência: era provável, mas ninguém pode afirmar com certeza.
C Porque o fato aconteceu e está confirmado.
3. According to the text, what came FIRST at Northline Academy?
A The attrition — people resigned, and only then did they stop caring.
B The disengagement — people stopped bringing ideas long before anyone resigned.
C The toxic culture — the text says fear and blame were normal from day one.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional + Modal Perfects

GRAMMAR

A gramática do diagnóstico: todo diagnóstico cultural fala de um passado que NÃO aconteceu — "se tivessem feito X, não teria acontecido Y" (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Condição (if)
if + had + particípio		O passado que NÃO aconteceu. The past that did not happen.If they had run a pulse survey...
Resultado
would have + particípio		A consequência que, por isso, também não aconteceu....they would have seen it coming.
should have + particípioCrítica: era obrigação e não foi feito. Judges.We should have addressed it earlier.
could have + particípioOpção que existia e não foi usada. Softer.They could have fostered a culture of speaking up.
might have + particípioEspeculação prudente, sem acusar. Speculates.The team might have felt unsafe.
Negativahad not + particípio / would not have + particípioIf they hadn't ignored it, she wouldn't have resigned.
InterrogativaWhat + would + sujeito + have + particípio + if...?What would you have done if you had known?
Hedging executivoIt would appear that... (tudo indica que...) · To some extent... (até certo ponto...) — a mesma verdade, dita de um jeito que a sala aceita ouvir.

Atenção (erro nº1 do brasileiro): o would NUNCA entra na metade do if. "If we would have known" está errado — o certo é "If we had known, we would have acted". Na fala, tudo contrai: If we'd known, we'd have acted.

Escrita: é sempre should have, nunca "should of" — mesmo que, na boca de um nativo em velocidade normal, os dois soem exatamente iguais.

Collocation: em inglês não se diz "do a diagnosis" — diz-se run / carry out / conduct a cultural diagnosis. E é discuss the results, nunca "discuss about the results".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua primeira sessão de diagnóstico com o time acadêmico.

The pulse survey shows belonging at 78% and psychological safety at 41%. Nathan asks what it means. You say:
A "Your culture is toxic. People are afraid of each other."
B "It would appear that the issue is safety, not belonging: people are proud to be here, and they still do not feel safe enough to disagree."
C "The numbers are fine. Forty one percent is normal in academia."
Nine exit interviews were filed and never read. Which sentence is grammatically correct?
A "If someone would have read them, we would have known."
B "If someone had read them, we would have known."
C "If someone had read them, we would know it a year ago."
You want to criticize a decision the directors made, without turning them into the accused. The most senior form is:
A "You should have seen this coming. It was obvious."
B "To some extent, the team might have felt that disagreeing carried a cost — that is a system problem, not a people problem."
C "Nobody here has the courage to say what they think."
You want to say you will investigate the numbers properly before judging anyone. The natural collocation is:
A "Before I take a stance, I want to look into the exit interviews."
B "Before I take a stance, I want to look the exit interviews."
C "Before I take a stance, I want to do a research in the exit interviews."
A director says: "Nobody ever complained to me, so the survey must be wrong." You answer:
A "Then the survey is wrong. Let's move on."
B "That is exactly the problem with this leadership team."
C "To some extent, that is the finding: people might not have felt they could bring it to you. Could you tell me what happens here when someone disagrees with a director?"
