Follow-ups & Action Items

Turning a meeting into clear tasks and reporting what was agreed. Key words: action item, owner, next step, assign, due, minutes, task, outstanding, circle back, wrap up. Structure: reported speech (said / told + backshift).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Action item -- a specific task that someone must do after a meeting (item de ação / tarefa definida)
"We turned the discussion into clear action items."
Owner -- the person who is responsible for one task (responsável pela tarefa)
"Each action item has an owner and a due date."
Next step -- the following action you take to move the work forward (próximo passo)
"We agreed on the next step before we closed the call."
Assign -- to give a task to a specific person (atribuir / designar)
"We assigned the follow-up email to the support team."
Due -- the day by which a task must be finished (com prazo / a vencer)
"The onboarding deck is due on Friday."
Minutes -- the written notes that record what a meeting decided (ata da reunião)
"Patrícia will share the minutes with the client today."
Task -- a piece of work that one person has to do (tarefa)
"The onboarding deck is the main task right now."
Outstanding -- still not done or finished; still open (pendente / em aberto)
"The billing question is still outstanding."
Circle back -- to return to a topic or person at a later time (voltar a tratar depois)
"Let's circle back to the billing question on Monday."
Wrap up -- to finish or bring a meeting or task to an end (encerrar / finalizar)
"Before we wrapped up, we reviewed the open items."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After the sync, Patrícia wrote the minutes and reported the team's decisions. Priya said the dashboard was ready for the client. David told the team he would send the technical notes by Wednesday. Priya also said she had finished the first report. Everyone told Patrícia they would confirm their tasks by the end of the day.

1. Por que usamos "told the team" e não "said the team"?
A Porque "told" só existe no passado.
B Porque "tell" precisa de uma pessoa depois (told the team / told me); "say" não.
C Porque "told" significa "perguntou".
2. A fala original foi "The dashboard is ready". Por que reportamos como "was ready"?
A Porque na fala reportada recuamos o verbo no tempo: is → was.
B Porque "was" e "is" significam a mesma coisa.
C Porque o dashboard quebrou depois.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "He said me he would send the notes."
B "He told me he would send the notes."
C "He told that he would send the notes."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Como reportar o que alguém disse (explicação em inglês e português).

Point / PontoRule / RegraExample
sayNão leva pessoa depois. "say (that)..."She said (that) it was ready.
tellSEMPRE leva pessoa: "tell me / the team".She told me (that) it was ready.
backshiftRecue o verbo: is→was, have→had, will→would, can→could."I will send it" → He said he would send it.
erro comumNunca "said me"; nunca "told that" sem pessoa.✓ He told me... / ✓ He said that...

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

You report what a colleague told you personally. You say:
A "He said me he would send the notes."
B "He told me he would send the notes."
C "He told he would send the notes."
In the meeting Priya said "The data is ready." Later you report:
A "Priya said the data is ready."
B "Priya said the data was ready."
C "Priya told the data was ready."
A task is not finished yet. You describe its status:
A "It is the owner."
B "It is still outstanding."
C "It is the minutes."
You want to return to a topic later in the week. You say:
A "Let's circle back to it on Monday."
B "Let's assign it on Monday."
C "Let's wrap up to it on Monday."
