Chapter 4: The Code

Reporting What People Said

said · told · is → was · will → would

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Priya said the data was ready."

"David told the team he would send the deck."

"She said she had finished the report."

"He told me he was working on the fix."

Direct speechReported speech
"The data is ready."She said the data was ready.
"I will send it."He said he would send it.
"I have finished."She said she had finished.
"I can help."He said he could help.

say has no person after it ("she said that..."). tell always needs a person ("she told me / the team that..."). When you report later, shift the verb back: is→was, will→would, have→had, can→could.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"He said me he would send the notes."
"He told me he would send the notes."
"She told that the report was ready."
"She said that the report was ready."
"Priya said the data is ready last week."
"Priya said the data was ready last week."

Use told + person (never "said me"). Use said with no person (never "told that"). When you report later, shift is→was.

Chapter 5: The Sync Recap

Wrapping Up the Sync

Dialogue

The Sync Recap

P
David, let's wrap up. Can you recap the action items from the sync?
D
Sure. Priya said the dashboard was ready, so onboarding can start this week.
P
Good. And you told the team you would send the technical notes, right?
D
Yes. I said I would send them by Wednesday. You own the deck after that.
P
Exactly. The deck is due on Friday. What is still outstanding?
D
The billing question. Let's circle back to it on Monday.
Comprehension

About the Recap

1. What did Priya say about the dashboard?
She said it was ready, so onboarding can start this week.
2. When did David say he would send the technical notes?
By Wednesday.
3. What is still outstanding, and when will they circle back to it?
The billing question; they will circle back on Monday.
