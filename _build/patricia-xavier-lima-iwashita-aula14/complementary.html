Aula 14 -- Follow-ups & Action Items

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 21: "Conflict Resolution"

Watch a meeting where the team lists problems and decides who will handle what. Listen for the action items, the owners, and the moments when one person reports what another person said. Connection to Lesson 14: turning a meeting into clear tasks and reporting decisions to the team.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 things people decide, then report each one with "She said..." or "He told the team...".

HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run Better Meetings (and Follow Up on Them)"

A Harvard Business Review episode on running meetings that end with clear action items, owners and next steps -- and on the follow-up that makes them stick. Listen for the words from this lesson (action item, owner, due, outstanding, wrap up) used by real managers. Connection to Lesson 14: making sure a meeting turns into work that actually gets done.

Tip: pick one episode and note 5 phrases you could use to wrap up your own sync and assign the action items.

YouTube
"Reported Speech -- Say & Tell + Backshift" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to report what people said with "say" and "tell" and how to shift the verb back in time (is → was, will → would, have → had). Watch for why "tell" needs a person and "say" does not. Connection to Lesson 14: this is the grammar that lets you recap a meeting and report what your team agreed.

Tip: pause after each example and report one thing a colleague said to you today, using "said" or "told" with the correct backshift.
