Prepositions of Place and Movement -- English Grammar (Learn Easy English)

Tonight's grammar in one clear lesson: where things are (across from, next to, opposite, between) and how you move (along, past, through, toward). It shows exactly the difference you practiced -- a place preposition tells you WHERE, a movement preposition tells you HOW to get there.

Tip: after each example, pause and build your own about your walk to the museum -- "It is across from the park", "Walk along Fifth, past the church". Say five before the video ends.

Asking for and Giving Directions in English (Nmodel)

The exact scene from tonight's role-play: someone stops a stranger, asks the way, and follows the answer, with the phrases and prepositions on screen. It is the same language you practiced -- "Could you tell me how to get to...?", "go along", "turn at the corner", "it is across from..." -- so asking the way in New York feels familiar before you get there.

Tip: this works as pure audio -- listen in the car on the way to Guarulhos. Every time someone asks for directions, pause and say your own version out loud.
