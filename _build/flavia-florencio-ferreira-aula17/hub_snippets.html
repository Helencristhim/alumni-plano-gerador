Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Certification -- an official document that proves something is true
"A geographic indication is a certification of origin."
Designation -- an official name or title given to something
"'Coffee of the Cerrado' is a special designation."
Origin -- the place where something comes from
"The label shows the origin of the coffee."
Territory -- an area of land in one region or country
"This product comes from one special territory."
Artisanal -- made by hand in the traditional way
"Our producers make artisanal cheese."
Denomination -- the official name that shows where a product is from
"The denomination protects the name of the region."
Producer -- a person or company that makes a product
"The producers who make it follow strict rules."
Label -- a small paper on a product with information about it
"The label that you see proves its denomination."
Role -- the job or part you have in a team or meeting
"Let me explain my role at Sebrae."
To explain -- to make something clear and easy to understand
"Let me explain what a geographic indication is."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is at a bilateral meeting. A buyer asks about her role. "I am a consultant who helps small businesses," she says. Then she wants to explain a geographic indication. "It is a certification that shows the origin of a product," she says. "It is a designation which connects a product to one special territory." She describes the producers who make the artisanal products. "The label that you see proves its denomination," she says. Her role is to explain, so she uses short, clear sentences.

1. Which word does Flavia use for a person, like a producer?
A who
B which
C where
2. In "a certification that shows the origin", what does "that" define?
A the certification (a thing)
B the buyer (a person)
C the meeting (a place)
3. What is Flavia's role at the meeting?
A To buy artisanal coffee.
B To explain, as a consultant who helps small businesses.
C To make the products.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Defining Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

How to define a person, a thing, or a place in one clear sentence.

WordUse it forExample
whoa persona producer who makes the product
thata thing (or a person)a document that proves the origin
whicha thingthe name which shows the origin
wherea place: "the territory where it is made". Use who for people and that or which for things. The small word comes right after the noun it defines, and you never leave it out.
