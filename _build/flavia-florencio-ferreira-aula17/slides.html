Chapter 3: That, Which, Who

Define It in One Line

that · which · who

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"A certification is a document that proves the origin."

"A producer is a person who makes the product."

"Denomination is the name which shows the origin."

"Artisanal means products that are made by hand."

Each orange word connects two ideas and defines the noun before it. Which one do you use for a person? Which ones do you use for a thing?

WordUse it forExample
whoa persona producer who makes it
thata thing (person or thing)a document that proves it
whicha thingthe name which shows it

Use who for people, and that or which for things. The small word comes right after the noun it defines.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"A producer is a person which makes the product."
"A producer is a person who makes the product."
"A certification is a document who proves the origin."
"A certification is a document that proves the origin."
"The territory is the region gives the name."
"The territory is the region that gives the name."

Use who for people, that or which for things, and never forget the small word.

Detail

Say It Right

who for people · that / which for things

"It is a certification ___that shows the origin."
"The producers ___who make it follow strict rules."
"The label ___that you see proves its denomination."
Chapter 4: Two Sides of the Table

The Meeting

A buyer asks; a consultant explains

Dialogue

Explaining Your Role

M
Nice to meet you, Flavia. I am a buyer who works for an Italian food company.
F
Nice to meet you, Marco. I am a consultant who helps small Brazilian producers export.
M
Great. Can you explain what a geographic indication is, exactly?
F
Sure. It is a certification that shows the origin of a product from one special territory.
M
I see. My company only buys artisanal coffee that has a clear denomination.
F
Perfect. Our producers make artisanal coffee, and the label that you see proves its origin.
