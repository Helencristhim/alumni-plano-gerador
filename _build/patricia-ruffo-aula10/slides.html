Chapter 3 -- The Review

Everything from Block 2

Persuade, question research, defend your data

Block 2 Map

Three Skills

1. Make the case (persuasion)

Cause and effect: "The evidence is strong, therefore the result is reliable."

2. Question research (be critical)

Hedging: "It seems the sample may be too small."

3. Defend your data (under pressure)

Concede or stand by: "I acknowledge your point, but I stand by my data."

Mixed Review

Put It Together

"The evidence is strong, _____therefore the result is reliable." (cause and effect)
"It _____seems the sample may be too small." (hedging)
"I _____acknowledge your point, but I stand by my data." (defending)
"Let me _____rebut that counterpoint with new evidence." (debate)
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You are wrong and your study is bad."
"I disagree with that point, and here is why the data suggests otherwise."

In a debate, you rebut the argument with evidence; you never attack the person.

Artefact

The Debate Rules

ABBOTT SCIENCE PANEL -- DEBATE RULES

MotionFunctional ingredients should be approved within 12 months.
ModeratorTimes each speaker and keeps the debate fair.
JudgeGives feedback and decides the more persuasive case.

Rule 1. Each speaker has two minutes to articulate one main argument.

Rule 2. Every claim must be valid, which means it must be supported by reliable evidence.

Rule 3. When you rebut a counterpoint, question the data, never the person.

A Round on the Panel

"I have a counterpoint. Your study used a very small sample, so the result may not be reliable."

P

"That is a fair point. Let me rebut it: a second, larger study found the same result, so the conclusion is still valid."

D

"All right, but can you articulate the main benefit in one sentence?"

D

"I see. That was a clear and persuasive answer."

"Thank you. I am happy to take any other counterpoint from the panel."
