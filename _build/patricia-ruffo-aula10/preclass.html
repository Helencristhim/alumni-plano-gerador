Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Debate -- a formal discussion where two sides argue opposite opinions
"The debate about the new study lasted an hour." (Debate)
Motion -- the statement or topic that the debate is about
"The motion is that the study should be published now." (Moção / tema)
Counterpoint -- an opposite argument that answers your point
"She raised a strong counterpoint about the sample size." (Contra-argumento)
Moderator -- the person who leads a debate and keeps it fair
"The moderator gave each speaker two minutes." (Mediador)
Rebut -- to answer an argument and show why it is wrong
"He used new data to rebut the counterpoint." (Refutar / rebater)
Articulate -- to express your ideas clearly and in good order
"She can articulate her main argument in one sentence." (Articular / expressar com clareza)
Persuasive -- able to convince other people that you are right
"Her case was clear, calm and very persuasive." (Persuasivo / convincente)
Valid -- logical, sound and supported by good evidence
"Your point is valid because the data supports it." (Válido)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the Abbott science panel, the moderator reads the motion and reminds everyone of the rules. Patricia speaks first, and she is careful to articulate her main argument in one clear sentence. The evidence is strong, therefore her case is persuasive. Then a colleague raises a counterpoint: he says the sample may be too small. Patricia does not get angry; she stays calm and decides to rebut the point with new data from a larger study. Because the data supports her, the argument is still valid. This is the secret of a good debate: you question the data, not the person, and you support every claim with evidence.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Language of debate (Block 2 review)

GRAMMAR

No debate, juntamos as estruturas do Bloco 2: causa e efeito para persuadir, hedging para questionar e marcadores para defender. / In a debate, we combine the Block 2 structures: cause and effect to persuade, hedging to question, and markers to defend.

Function / FunçãoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Persuade (cause & effect)..., therefore ... / ... leads to ..."The evidence is strong, therefore the result is valid."
Question research (hedging)It seems / it may / it suggests ..."It seems the sample may be too small."
Rebut a counterpointLet me rebut that ..."Let me rebut that with new data."
Defend / concedeI acknowledge ..., but I stand by ..."I acknowledge your point, but I stand by my data."

Key point / Ponto-chave: No debate científico, você articula um argumento persuasivo, ouve o counterpoint, e o rebate com evidência para mostrar que o ponto é válido. Questione os dados, nunca a pessoa. / In a scientific debate, you articulate a persuasive argument, hear the counterpoint, and rebut it with evidence to show the point is valid. Question the data, never the person.
