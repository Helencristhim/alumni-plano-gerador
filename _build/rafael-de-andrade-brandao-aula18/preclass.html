Aula 18 -- Pre-class

Review & Consolidation 3 -- The Full Team Meeting

Bring all of Block 3 together: chair a full team meeting from start to finish -- open and set the agenda (Let us get started), report results and trends (As you can see, sales rose), invite input and handle questions (Are there any questions?), and close with action items (To sum up, the next steps). New vocabulary: chair, minutes, point, input, deliverable, next steps, status, takeaway.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Chair -- to lead and run a meeting
"Rafael will chair today's meeting." (Presidir / conduzir)
Minutes -- the written record of a meeting
"I will send the minutes after the meeting." (Ata da reunião)
Point -- one topic on the agenda
"Let us move to the next point." (Ponto / tópico)
Input -- the ideas and opinions people share
"Thank you for your input." (Contribuição / opinião)
Deliverable -- a task or result a person must complete
"The report is your main deliverable." (Entrega / resultado)
Next steps -- what the team will do after the meeting
"Let us agree on the next steps." (Próximos passos)
Status -- how a task or project is going right now
"What is the status of the budget?" (Situação / andamento)
Takeaway -- the main idea you remember after a meeting
"The key takeaway is to grow sales." (Conclusão principal)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael will chair the quarterly team meeting. He opens it: "Let us get started. We have three points today." Then he reports the results: "As you can see, sales rose last quarter." He invites the team to speak: "Are there any questions? Thank you for your input." A team member asks about the budget, so Rafael checks the status and assigns the deliverable to an owner. To close, he says: "To sum up, the key takeaway is to grow sales. Let us agree on the next steps." After the meeting, he sends the minutes to everyone.

1. What does Rafael do at the start of the meeting?
A He opens it and sets the points for the day.
B He sends the minutes.
C He leaves the room.
2. How does Rafael close the meeting?
A With the key takeaway and the next steps.
B By asking for a discount.
C Without any summary.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "As you can see, sales rose last quarter."
B "As you can see, sales did rose last quarter."
C "As you can see, sales rises last quarter."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Meeting Flow

GRAMMAR

Como conduzir uma reunião do início ao fim. / How to run a meeting from start to finish. Cada estagio tem uma frase-sinal; no past simple, o verbo já mostra o passado (rose, não did rose).

Stage / EstagioSignal phrase / Frase-sinalExample / Exemplo
Open / AbrirLet us get startedLet us get started. We have three points.
Report / ReportarAs you can seeAs you can see, sales rose last quarter.
Questions / PerguntasAre there any questions?Are there any questions? Thank you for your input.
Close / FecharTo sum upTo sum up, here are the next steps.
Key point / Ponto-chaveNo past simple, o verbo já mostra o passado: diga rose, não did rose. E use agree on the next steps, não agree the next steps.
