Chapter 3 -- Running the Meeting

The Flow

From open to close, stage by stage

Discover the Flow

Listen and Notice

Open: "Let us get started. We have three points today."

Report: "As you can see, sales rose last quarter."

Questions: "Are there any questions? Thank you for your input."

Close: "To sum up, here are the next steps."

The Meeting Flow

Four Stages

StageSignal phraseSkill from Block 3
1. OpenLet us get started.Starting a meeting
2. ReportAs you can see, sales rose.Reporting & trends
3. QuestionsAre there any questions?Handling questions
4. CloseTo sum up, here are the next steps.Closing & action items

Key idea: A good chair guides the team through four stages. Open with Let us get started, report with As you can see, invite input with Are there any questions?, and close with To sum up plus the next steps.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Sales did rose last quarter."
"Sales rose last quarter."

In the past simple, the verb already shows the past: rose, not did rose. Also say agree on the next steps, not agree the next steps.

Grammar Practice

Questions: "Are there any _____questions?" (invite input)
Close: "To sum up, here are the next _____steps." (what we do after)
