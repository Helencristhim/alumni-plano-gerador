Lesson 18 -- Review & Consolidation 3: The Full Team Meeting

Series
The Office (US) -- a full team meeting scene

Watch a manager chair a team meeting from start to finish. Listen for the meeting flow: how the chair opens, walks through the points, invites input, and closes with action items. Notice the meeting words: chair, point, input, next steps, and status.

Write down every signal phrase the chair uses (Let us get started, As you can see, Are there any questions?, To sum up). Then chair a short meeting out loud using the same flow.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Chairing a Meeting from Start to Finish"

Practical phrases for running a full meeting: opening, reporting results, inviting input, handling questions, and closing with the next steps. Review the new words: chair, minutes, point, input, deliverable, next steps, status, and takeaway.

Note the signal phrase for each stage of the meeting. Practice each one out loud as if you are chairing your own quarterly meeting.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to run a meeting in English"

A short, clear lesson on the full meeting flow: open with Let us get started, report with As you can see, invite input with Are there any questions?, and close with To sum up and the next steps. Perfect for a chair who wants to keep a meeting on track.

After watching, record yourself chairing a full mini-meeting: open, report, invite input, and close with the takeaway and the next steps.
