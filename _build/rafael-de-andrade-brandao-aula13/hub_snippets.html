Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Present -- to show information to a group
"I will present the results." (Apresentar)
Highlight -- the most important point
"The highlight was the new contract." (Destaque)
Results -- the numbers at the end of a period
"The results were excellent." (Resultados)
Increase -- to go up
"Sales increased by fifteen percent." (Aumentar)
Decrease -- to go down
"Our costs decreased by five percent." (Diminuir)
Chart -- a picture of numbers
"Look at the chart on this slide." (Gráfico)
Audience -- the people who listen to you
"The audience asked good questions." (Plateia / público)
Growth -- when something gets bigger over time
"The growth was strong this year." (Crescimento)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On Friday, Rafael will present the quarterly results to the team. He prepared a report with four slides and a chart. First, the highlights: last quarter the team signed three new contracts. Then, the numbers: sales increased by fifteen percent, and costs decreased by five percent. The chart shows strong growth in the last three months. Finally, Rafael will present his plan for the next quarter. The audience is small, only eight people, so he will keep it simple and clear.

1. What will Rafael present on Friday?
A The quarterly results.
B A new contract.
C A travel plan.
2. What happened to sales and costs last quarter?
A Sales increased and costs decreased.
B Sales decreased and costs increased.
C Both stayed the same.
3. Which sentence is correct for a finished quarter?
A "Last quarter, our sales increased."
B "Last quarter, our sales increase."
C "Last quarter, our sales will increase."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple & Signposting

GRAMMAR

Usado para reportar resultados que já aconteceram. / Used to report results that already happened. Use signposting para organizar a apresentação.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Verbo + -ed (regular)Passado da maioria dos verbos / Most past verbsSales increased, costs decreased
was / werePassado de "to be" / Past of "to be"The growth was strong
First, ... / Then, ...Ordenar os pontos / Order your pointsFirst, the highlights. Then, the numbers.
Finally, ...Seu último ponto / Your last pointFinally, my plan for next quarter
Key point / Ponto-chavePara um período terminado, use o passado simples. Diga "sales increased" (não "sales increase").
