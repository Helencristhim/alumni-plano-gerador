Lesson 13 -- Presenting the Numbers

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 21: "Conflict Resolution"

Watch how Michael presents results and slides to his team. Notice the signposting and the past simple when he reports what happened. Listen for words like results, chart, and report.

Write down every sentence that reports a past result. Then present an imaginary quarter out loud using First, Then, and Finally.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Giving Presentations: Reporting Results"

Practical phrases for presentations: how to welcome the audience, signpost your points, and report numbers in the past simple. Listen for the new words: present, report, results, increase, decrease, chart, audience, and growth.

Note 3 sentences with signposting (First, Then, Finally). Practice each one out loud and report your own numbers.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The past simple: talking about finished actions"

A short, clear lesson on the past simple: regular verbs with -ed and the irregular was / were. Perfect for reporting results that already happened.

After watching, record yourself presenting last month's results. Use the past simple, signposting, and at least three new words from this lesson.
