Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Finalize -- to complete and settle something for good, leaving nothing open
"We agreed to finalize the terms by Friday." (finalizar / concluir)
Sign off -- to give final, formal approval to a plan or deal
"The board signed off on moving ahead." (aprovar / dar o aval)
Outstanding -- still open and unresolved, not yet completed
"Two items are still outstanding: valuation and staffing." (pendente / em aberto)
Contingent -- depending on something else happening first
"The deal is contingent on final due diligence." (condicionado / dependente)
Proceed -- to go ahead with a plan or deal that has been agreed
"The board agreed to proceed with the acquisition." (prosseguir / seguir adiante)
Formalize -- to make an agreement official, usually in a written document
"We plan to formalize the terms in a signed contract." (formalizar / oficializar)
Commitment -- a firm promise to do something, with an owner and a date
"Each next step needs a commitment and an owner." (compromisso / comprometimento)
Walk through -- to go over each step or item carefully, one by one
"Let me walk through the outstanding items before we close." (detalhar / percorrer passo a passo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about closing the deal and answer the questions.

The board signed off, and Carlos has to close the deal well. A strong close does three things. First, recap the outcome in one line: the board agreed to proceed with the acquisition, contingent on final due diligence. Second, name the outstanding items honestly -- two are still open, the valuation and the staffing plan -- and walk through each with an owner. Third, agree the next steps. Here the verb pattern matters: some verbs take to + verb (we agreed to proceed, we plan to formalize the terms), and others take the -ing form (we look forward to working together, we suggest signing on Friday). Mixing these up is the most common mistake at this level. Finally, confirm the commitment -- an owner and a date for each step -- and close warmly. A good close is short and clear, and it turns a yes into an action.

1. What three things does a strong close do?
A Recap the outcome, name the outstanding items, and agree the next steps.
B Repeat the whole discussion, then say thank you.
C Hide the open items so the close sounds cleaner.
2. Which verb form follows "look forward to" and "suggest"?
A "to + verb": "look forward to work", "suggest to sign".
B The "-ing" form: "look forward to working", "suggest signing".
C The past simple: "look forward to worked".
3. What does a commitment turn a yes into?
A A longer discussion.
B An apology for the cost.
C An action -- an owner and a date for each next step.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerund vs Infinitive

GRAMMAR

Quando você combina dois verbos, o primeiro decide a forma do segundo. Alguns verbos pedem to + verbo base: agree, decide, plan, hope, promise ("the board agreed to proceed"). Outros pedem o gerúndio (-ing): suggest, recommend, avoid ("we suggest signing on Friday"). Cuidado especial com look forward to: aqui o to é uma preposição, então vem o -ing: "look forward to working" (NUNCA "to work"). Não há lógica -- aprende-se em grupos. / The first verb decides the form of the second: to + base verb after agree/plan/decide, the -ing form after suggest/recommend/look forward to. The "to" in look forward to is a preposition, so it always takes the gerund.

PatternVerbsExample
to + base verbagree, decide, plan, hope, promise"We agreed to proceed."
verb + ingsuggest, recommend, avoid"We suggest signing on Friday."
look forward TO + ing"to" is a preposition here"We look forward to working together."
