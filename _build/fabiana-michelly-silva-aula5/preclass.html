Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics planned for a meeting
"Let us follow the agenda so nothing is missed." (Pauta)
Quote -- a formal statement of the price for goods or services
"The supplier sent a quote for the new order." (Orçamento)
Renegotiate -- to discuss an agreement again to change its terms
"We need to renegotiate the contract this quarter." (Renegociar)
SLA -- a contract defining the standard of service promised
"The SLA sets a delivery time of five days." (Acordo de nível de serviço)
Incoterms -- rules that define who pays and who is responsible in shipping
"Under these incoterms, the seller pays the freight." (Termos de comércio internacional)
Customs -- the authority that controls goods crossing a border
"The shipment is still being held at customs." (Alfândega)
Tariff -- a government tax on imported goods
"A new tariff raised the cost of every unit." (Tarifa)
Reroute -- to send a shipment along a different path
"They had to reroute the cargo through another port." (Redirecionar)
Fulfillment -- the whole process of getting an order to the customer
"Faster fulfillment keeps our customers happy." (Atendimento de pedidos)
Surcharge -- an extra charge added on top of the normal price
"A fuel surcharge was added to the freight." (Sobretaxa)
Expedite -- to make a process or shipment happen faster
"We asked the vendor to expedite the order." (Agilizar)
Consignment -- a batch of goods sent together to a customer
"The first consignment arrives next Monday." (Remessa)
Action item -- a specific task assigned to a named owner in a meeting
"Each action item has an owner and a deadline." (Item de ação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before the vendor call, Klaus sent the agenda. Three points had to be covered. First, the SLA must be renegotiated, because delivery targets keep being missed. Second, the latest consignment was rerouted through Rotterdam, and a surcharge was added to the freight. Third, a new tariff is being applied at customs, so the quote must be revised. To prepare, Fabiana had the numbers checked by finance and got the contract reviewed by legal. On the call, the goods were expedited, every action item was assigned an owner, and the order was fulfilled on time.

1. Which sentence is in the passive voice?
A "Klaus sent the agenda."
B "The consignment was rerouted through Rotterdam."
C "Klaus reviews the contract."
2. What does "Fabiana had the numbers checked by finance" mean?
A She arranged for the finance team to check them.
B She checked the numbers herself.
C The numbers were never checked.
3. Why must the quote be revised?
A Because a new tariff is being applied at customs.
B Because the agenda was too long.
C Because the SLA was cancelled.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Passive & Causative

GRAMMAR

Usado para focar na ação ou para organizar que outra pessoa faça algo. / Used to focus on the action or to arrange for someone else to do something.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
be + past participlePassiva: foco na ação / Passive: focus on the actionThe order was fulfilled on time.
modal + be + p.p.Passiva modal: obrigação, regra / Modal passive: rule, obligationThe SLA must be renegotiated.
have + object + p.p.Causativo: organizar que outro faça / Causative: arrange for someone elseWe had the contract reviewed by legal.
get + object + p.p.Causativo informal: conseguir fazer / Informal causative: manage toI will get the quote revised by Friday.

Key point / Ponto-chave: no causativo, a ordem é sempre have/get + a COISA + past participle. Nunca "we had reviewed the contract by legal" (isso é passado perfeito, sentido diferente).
