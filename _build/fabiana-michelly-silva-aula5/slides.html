Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Advanced passive voice + causative (have / get something done)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Who does the action -- and does it matter?

"The shipment was rerouted through Rotterdam."

"The SLA must be renegotiated before we sign."

"We had the consignment inspected at customs."

"I need to get the quote approved today."

Grammar Rule

Passive & Causative

StructureMeaning
Passive: be + past participle (it was shipped)Focus on the action; the doer is unknown or unimportant
Modal passive: modal + be + p.p. (must be renegotiated)Rules, obligations, and possibilities about the action
Causative: have + object + p.p. (we had it reviewed)You arrange for someone else to do the action
Causative: get + object + p.p. (I will get it done)More informal; you manage to make it happen

Key idea: in the passive, the object becomes the subject. In the causative, the order is always have / get + the THING + past participle -- the thing receives the action, done by someone else.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We had renegotiated the SLA by the vendor."
"We had the SLA renegotiated by the vendor."

For the causative, the order is have / get + the thing + past participle. The thing receives the action, done by someone else -- not by you.
