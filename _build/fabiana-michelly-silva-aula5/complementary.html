Lesson 5 -- The Vendor Call

Series
Industry -- Season 2, Episode 3: "Wargames"

Tense client and vendor calls where prices are renegotiated and terms are pushed under pressure. Listen for how professionals run a structured call, set an agenda, and close with clear next steps.

Watch with English subtitles. Note every passive sentence ("the deal was closed", "the terms were changed") and one moment where someone has work done by another team. Then describe one scene using three new words from today.

Podcast
Supply Chain Now -- "Running Effective Supplier Meetings"

Practical advice on building an agenda, renegotiating an SLA, handling customs and tariffs, and closing a call with action items. Listen for how experts describe what is being done and what they have done by other teams.

Write down 3 phrases the speakers use to assign action items. Compare them with how Fabiana and Klaus closed the vendor call in today's lesson.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Run a Meeting People Actually Want to Attend"

A short, practical video on chairing a meeting: setting the agenda, keeping the discussion on track, and ending with owned action items. Directly applicable to running calls with international suppliers like TechFlow.

After watching, record a 60-second mock vendor call closing. Use at least three new words, two passive sentences, and one causative ("we had ... done" / "I will get ... done").
