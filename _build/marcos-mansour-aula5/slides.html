Chapter 3

This Is... & Have You Met?

Introductions + the third person -s

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This is Sarah, my colleague."

"Have you met my counterpart from Chicago?"

"She leads the valuation team."

"He manages our Chicago office."

ToolUseExample
This is + nameIntroduce a person directlyThis is Sarah.
Have you met + name?A polite way to introduceHave you met John?
he / she + verb+s3rd person ALWAYS takes -sShe leads, he manages.
It is a pleasure to meet youPolite reply when introducedA pleasure to meet you.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She lead the team." (no -s)

✓ RIGHT

"She leads the team." (he/she + -s)

✗ WRONG

"Have you meet my counterpart?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you met my counterpart?"

✗ WRONG

"This is Sarah. He works with me." (wrong gender)

✓ RIGHT

"This is Sarah. She works with me."

Add -s with he/she. Use the past participle "met" after "have". Match he/she to the person.

Chapter 4

Making the Introduction

Connecting two people at the congress

Dialogue

This Is My Colleague

E
Marcos, it is good to see you again! Are you here with your team?
M
I am! My colleague Sarah is here too. This is her on the program -- she leads our valuation team.
E
Oh, so she leads the team? I would love to meet her. What does her team focus on?
M
She focuses on industrial assets. Have you met my counterpart from Chicago, David? He manages your partner office there.
E
David! Yes, he is a mutual friend -- what a small world. It is a pleasure to discover we are connected.
M
It really is! Let me connect you with Sarah at lunch. I am sure you would love to get to know her.
E
That would be wonderful. Please introduce us -- I will be at the lunch table by the window.
M
Perfect. On behalf of our team, thank you, Ellen. I will bring Sarah over and introduce you both.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Marcos say Sarah does?
She leads the valuation team and focuses on industrial assets. (3rd person -s)
2. Who is the mutual friend, and what does he do?
David, Marcos's counterpart in Chicago -- he manages Ellen's partner office there.
3. What does Marcos offer to do at lunch?
To connect Ellen with Sarah and introduce them both.
