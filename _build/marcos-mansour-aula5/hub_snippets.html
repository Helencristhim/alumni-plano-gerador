Introducing & Presenting Other People

Presenting one person to another. Key words: introduce, counterpart, mutual, acquaintance, connect, on behalf of, pleasure, get to know, lead, manage. Structures: This is... / Have you met...? + the third person -s (she leads, he manages).

Stage 5.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Introduce -- apresentar (to present one person to another)
"Let me introduce you to my colleague."
Counterpart -- equivalente, contraparte (a person with the same role in another company)
"I would like you to meet my counterpart from Chicago."
Mutual -- mútuo, em comum (shared by two or more people)
"We have a mutual friend in the industry."
Acquaintance -- conhecido (a person you know a little, not a close friend)
"He is a business acquaintance from last year's event."
Connect -- conectar, ligar pessoas (to bring two people together)
"Let me connect you two -- you have a lot in common."
On behalf of -- em nome de (representing or speaking for another person or group)
"On behalf of my firm, welcome to the congress."
Pleasure -- prazer (a feeling of happiness; used in polite introductions)
"It is a pleasure to meet you."
Get to know -- conhecer melhor (to learn more about a person over time)
"I would love to get to know your team better."
Lead -- liderar, chefiar (to be in charge of a team -- she leads)
"She leads the valuation team in Sao Paulo."
Manage -- gerenciar (to organize and be responsible for -- he manages)
"He manages our office in Chicago."

Stage 5.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the congress, Marcos wants to introduce his colleague to a new contact. "This is Sarah," he says. "She leads our valuation team." Then he turns to the contact: "Have you met my counterpart from Chicago, David? He manages the office there." The contact smiles. "It is a pleasure. Actually, David is a mutual friend!" Marcos is happy to connect them. "On behalf of our team, welcome. I am sure you would love to get to know each other."

1. Por que dizemos "She leads" e "He manages" com -s no final?
A Porque é plural.
B Porque he/she (3ª pessoa) SEMPRE leva -s no present simple.
C Porque é uma pergunta.
2. Quais expressões Marcos usa para APRESENTAR alguém?
A "pleasure, mutual, connect"
B "This is... / Have you met...?"
C "on behalf of, counterpart"
3. Why does Marcos describe what Sarah and David do?
A To show he is busy.
B To help the contact understand each person's role.
C Because he forgot their names.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação de apresentação.

You want to introduce your colleague Sarah. You say:
A "This is Sarah. He leads the team."
B "This is Sarah. She leads the team."
C "This is Sarah. She lead the team."
You want to ask politely if two people already know each other:
A "Have you meet before?"
B "Have you met my counterpart from Chicago?"
C "You know him already, no?"
Someone is introduced to you. The most polite reply is:
A "It is a pleasure to meet you."
B "Okay, hello."
C "Yes, I know."
You welcome guests for your whole team. You say:
A "In behalf of my team, welcome."
B "On behalf of my team, welcome."
C "For my team behalf, welcome."
