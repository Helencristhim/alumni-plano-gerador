Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Warranty -- a written promise that the work will meet a standard
"The warranty covers any defect for twelve months." (Garantia)
Amendment -- a written change added to a contract
"Any new work needs a written amendment both sides sign." (Aditivo / emenda)
Penalty -- a payment the vendor owes if they break a rule
"There is a penalty for every week of delay." (Multa / penalidade)
Liability -- legal responsibility for damage or loss
"The liability clause limits our risk in a data breach." (Responsabilidade)
Comply -- to follow the rules or terms of an agreement
"The vendor must comply with the ninety-day deadline." (Cumprir)
Party -- one of the two sides that sign a contract
"Any amendment must be signed by both parties." (Parte)
Breach -- breaking a contract or a security rule
"A data breach would trigger the liability clause." (Violação / quebra)
Sign off -- to give final approval to a document or contract
"We only sign off when both parties agree on the terms." (Aprovar / dar o aval)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael is reviewing a new vendor contract before he and his legal team sign off. Overall, the terms look fair, but he wants to test a few clauses with hypothetical questions. He looks at the scope first: "If the scope changed later, we would need a written amendment." Then he checks the deadline: the vendor must comply with ninety days, and "if the vendor missed that deadline, we would apply a penalty." Finally, he reads the liability clause: "If a data breach happened, our exposure would be limited to the contract value." He is not comfortable with that cap, so he tells his team: "If I were you, I would ask for a higher limit." He uses the second conditional to explore situations that are not real now, but possible.

1. When does Rafael want to sign off on the contract?
A Immediately, before anyone reviews it.
B After he and the legal team review the clauses.
C Never, because the terms are unfair.
2. What would happen if the vendor missed the deadline?
A They would apply a penalty.
B They would extend the scope for free.
C Nothing would change in the contract.
3. What does Rafael say about the liability cap?
A "The cap is perfect, sign it now."
B "We do not need a liability clause."
C "If I were you, I would ask for a higher limit."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usamos o segundo condicional para falar de situações hipotéticas ou improváveis no presente ou futuro. / We use the second conditional to talk about hypothetical or unlikely situations in the present or future.

Part / ParteStructureExample / Exemplo
If-clause / CondiçãoIf + past simpleIf the vendor missed the deadline,
Result / Resultadowould + base verbwe would apply a penalty.
Negative / Negativowould not (wouldn't)we wouldn't sign without legal review.
Advice / ConselhoIf I were you, I would...If I were you, I would ask for a higher limit.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o segundo condicional usa past simple na condição (não é passado real) e would + verbo no resultado. Com o verbo to be, use were para todas as pessoas: If I were you.... É perfeito para negociar contratos sem se comprometer ainda.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each contract situation.

You want to explore what happens if the vendor delivers late. The best hypothetical question is:
A "If the vendor missed the deadline, what penalty would apply?"
B "When the vendor missed the deadline yesterday?"
C "The vendor will miss the deadline for sure."
You give a colleague advice about the liability cap. You say:
A "If I was you, I will sign it now."
B "If I were you, I would ask for a higher limit."
C "You signed it, so it is fine."
Legal asks if the contract is ready. The most accurate answer is:
A "I already signed off without reading it."
B "Not yet. We sign off only after both parties agree on the clauses."
C "There are no terms in this contract."
