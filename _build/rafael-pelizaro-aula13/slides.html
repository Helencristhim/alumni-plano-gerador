Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The second conditional

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The orange words show imagined situations. What form follows "if"? What follows "we"?

"If the vendor missed the deadline, we would apply a penalty."

"If the scope changed, we would need a written amendment."

"If a data breach happened, our liability would be limited."

"If I were you, I would ask for a higher limit."

Grammar Rule

The Second Conditional

PartStructureExample
If-clauseIf + past simpleIf the vendor missed the deadline,
Resultwould + base verbwe would apply a penalty.
Negativewouldn't + base verbwe wouldn't sign it yet.
Advice (to be)If I were you, I would...If I were you, I would ask for more.

Key idea: Use the second conditional for situations that are imagined or unlikely now. If + past simple in the condition, would + base verb in the result. With "to be", use "were" for everyone: "If I were you...".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we signed today, we will get a discount."
"If we signed today, we would get a discount."

In the second conditional, use would, not will, in the result. With "to be" in the if-clause, use were for every person, even "I".

Artefact

The Contract Summary Sheet

SERVICE AGREEMENT -- KEY CLAUSES

Vendor: NimbusCloud / Rafael Pelizaro

CLAUSE 1 -- SCOPE

The vendor will deliver the cloud migration and three months of support. Any new work requires a written amendment signed by both parties.

CLAUSE 2 -- DEADLINE & PENALTY

The vendor must comply with a 90-day deadline. For late delivery, a penalty of 2% of the total fee applies per week.

CLAUSE 3 -- LIABILITY

In a data breach, the vendor's liability is capped at the total value of the contract.

SIGN-OFF

This agreement takes effect only when both parties sign off on the terms above.

Dialogue

The Discussion with Sofia

S

"Thanks for reviewing our contract, Rafael. I think the terms are fair. Is there anything you would like to discuss?"

R

"Mostly, yes. But I have one question about the scope. If we needed extra work later, how would we handle it?"

S

"Good question. Any new work needs a written amendment that both parties sign. And we will comply with the 90-day deadline."

R

"My main concern is the liability. If a serious data breach happened, the cap would be too low. If I were you, I would offer a higher limit."

S

"I understand. If you needed a higher limit for security incidents, we could add a separate insurance clause for that risk."

R

"That works for me. Send the new clause to my legal team, and if everything looks good, we would sign off by the end of the month."
