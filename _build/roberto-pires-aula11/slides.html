Chapter 3: Polite Asking

Ask the Polite Way

"Could you tell me how to get to...?"

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Where is the square?"

"Excuse me, could you tell me where the square is?"

"Could you tell me how to get to the museum?"

"Is it far from here?"

FormStructureExample
DirectWhere + is + place?Where is the square?
Polite (indirect)Could you tell me + where + place + is?Could you tell me where the square is?
Polite (how to)Could you tell me how to get to + place?Could you tell me how to get to the museum?
Check distanceIs it + far / near?Is it far from here?

After Could you tell me, the word order is normal (subject + verb): "where the square is", not "where is the square". It sounds softer and more polite to a stranger.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me where is the bank?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me where the bank is?"

✗ WRONG

"How I get to the square?"

✓ RIGHT

"How do I get to the square?"

Chapter 4: On the Street

Asking a Local

Roberto finds his way to the cathedral

Dialogue

Roberto Asks the Way

R
Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the cathedral? I think I'm a bit lost.
L
Of course! Go straight along this street, and take the second turn after the fountain.
R
Thank you. Is it far? Can I get there on foot?
L
Oh, it's very near, only five minutes. You'll see a big square with a tall stone tower.
R
And the cathedral is on the square?
L
Yes, it's right there, opposite a small bakery. Just follow the signposts. Enjoy!
