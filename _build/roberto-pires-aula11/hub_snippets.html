Stage 1.1: Walking & Directions Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Estas palavras ajudam voce a encontrar o caminho a pe.

Landmark -- ponto de referencia
"The cathedral is a great landmark to walk toward."
Square -- praca
"We met at the main square in front of the church."
Fountain -- fonte / chafariz
"Turn right at the fountain in the middle of the square."
To get lost -- se perder
"It is easy to get lost in the narrow old streets."
Pedestrian crossing -- faixa de pedestres
"Wait at the pedestrian crossing for the green light."
Roundabout -- rotatoria
"Take the second street out of the roundabout."
Path -- caminho / trilha
"Follow the narrow path down to the river."
Opposite -- em frente a / do lado oposto
"The pharmacy is opposite the bank."
Signpost -- placa de sinalizacao
"Follow the brown signposts to the museum."
On foot -- a pe
"The old town is best explored on foot."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia a situacao e responda as perguntas.

Roberto wants to explore the old town on foot. He soon gets a little lost, so he stops a friendly local. "Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the cathedral?" he asks politely. The woman smiles. "Go straight along this street and turn after the fountain. The cathedral is on a big square, opposite a bakery." Roberto checks the distance: "Is it far?" "No, only five minutes. Just follow the signposts," she says. Roberto thanks her and walks off, looking for the tall tower -- his landmark.

1. How does Roberto ask politely?
A "Could you tell me how to get to the cathedral?"
B "Where cathedral is?"
C "Give me the cathedral."
2. Where is the cathedral?
A Inside the bakery.
B On a big square, opposite a bakery.
C Next to the metro station.
3. What landmark does Roberto look for?
A The bakery.
B The fountain.
C The tall tower.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Questions for Directions

Grammar

Para pedir direcoes a um desconhecido, a forma educada e usar perguntas indiretas. Comece com "Could you tell me..." e depois use a ordem normal (sujeito + verbo): "where the square IS", nao "where IS the square". Para destinos, use "how to get to + lugar". / To ask a stranger for directions, the polite form uses indirect questions. Start with "Could you tell me..." and then use normal word order (subject + verb): "where the square IS", not "where IS the square". For destinations, use "how to get to + place".
