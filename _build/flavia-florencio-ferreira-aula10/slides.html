Chapter 4: How Often?

Once a week · twice a day · every morning

How to say how often you do things

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

I do exercise three times a week.

I drink water every morning.

I rest twice a day.

ExpressionUse it forExample
once / twice / three times + a + periodhow many times in a periodI walk twice a day.
every + morning / day / weekeach one, with no breakI sleep well every night.
positionusually at the end of the sentenceI do exercise three times a week.

once = 1 · twice = 2 · three times = 3 · then "a" + day / week / month.

Grammar Practice

Say How Often

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I do exercise three ___times a week." (3x)
"___Every morning, I drink a glass of water." (each day)
"I rest ___twice a day." (2x)
"Once a ___month, I take a full day to relax." (1x)
"I get eight hours of sleep every ___night." (each night)
Language Focus

How Often?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I exercise two times a week."

✓ RIGHT

"I exercise twice a week."

✗ WRONG

"Every days I walk in the park."

✓ RIGHT

"Every day I walk in the park."

Say twice (not "two times"), and use every + a singular word: every day, every morning, every night.

Dialogue

Flavia and Lucas

L
Hi Flavia! You always look full of energy. What is your secret?
F
Thank you, Lucas! I take care of my health every day. It is all about good habits.
L
Really? What do you do every morning?
F
Every morning, I drink water and I do exercise for twenty minutes.
L
That is great. And how often do you rest during the day?
F
Twice a day, I stop and I relax for ten minutes. It helps with stress.
L
I have a lot of stress at work. Maybe I need more balance.
F
Yes! Start small. Three times a week, I walk in the park. You can try it too.
