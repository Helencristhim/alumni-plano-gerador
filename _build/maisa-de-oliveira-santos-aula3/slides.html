Chapter 3: The Code

What You Do Every Day

present simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work in finance. I manage a small team."

"David works with clients. He writes reports."

"She analyzes the numbers every day."

"What do you do? Do you manage a team?"

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
I / you / we / theyI workI do not workDo you work?
he / she / itShe worksShe does not workDoes she work?

The routine tense: he / she / it adds -s. In questions and negatives, use do / does — and the main verb loses the -s.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

She work in finance.
She works in finance.
What you do?
What do you do?

With he / she / it, the verb takes -s (she works). To ask a question, you need do or does first.
