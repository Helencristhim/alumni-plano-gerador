Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

Talking About the Past -- My Story

Telling your own story and trajectory. Key words: childhood, to grow up, background, to graduate, career, to relocate, milestone, turning point, to achieve, challenge, to overcome, memory. Grammar: the past simple -- regular and irregular verbs, and negatives and questions with did.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Childhood -- the time in your life when you were a child
"My childhood was happy and full of good memories."
To grow up -- to change from a child into an adult over the years
"I grew up in a small town in the countryside."
Background -- your education, family history, and past experience
"She has a strong background in science."
To graduate -- to finish your studies and receive a diploma
"I graduated from university in 2010."
Career -- the series of jobs you have over the years in one field
"He built a long career in medicine."
To relocate -- to move to a new city or country to live or work
"We relocated to another country for my job."
Milestone -- an important event or stage in your life
"Buying our first home was a real milestone."
Turning point -- a moment when an important change begins
"That trip was a turning point in my life."
To achieve -- to succeed in doing something after a lot of effort
"She achieved her dream of becoming a doctor."
Challenge -- a difficult task that tests your ability
"The new job was a big challenge for me."
To overcome -- to succeed in dealing with a problem or difficulty
"He overcame many problems to get here."
Memory -- something from the past that you remember
"That day is my favorite memory."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana Paula has an inspiring story. She grew up in a small town, and her childhood was simple and happy. Her family valued education, and that background shaped her dreams. She studied hard and graduated from medical school with pride. Her first years as a doctor were a real challenge, but she overcame every difficulty and built a strong career. The real turning point came when she decided to relocate to the United States. It was a huge milestone. The first months were hard, but she achieved her goal step by step. Today, that brave decision is her proudest memory.

1. Why do we say "She grew up" and not "She growed up"?
A "Grow" is an irregular verb, so its past form is "grew".
B "Grow" never changes in the past.
C "Grew" is the plural of "grow".
2. Which sentence is in the correct past simple form?
A "She decided to relocate to the United States."
B "She decide to relocate to the United States."
C "She did decided to relocate to the United States."
3. How do we make the negative of "She studied medicine"?
A "She did not study medicine."
B "She did not studied medicine."
C "She not studied medicine."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

How to talk about finished actions in the past.

RuleDetailExample
Regular verbsAdd -ed to the base verb.I work → I worked.
Irregular verbsThe form changes; you learn each one.I grow up → I grew up.
NegativesUse did not (didn't) + the base verb.She did not study law.
QuestionsUse did + subject + the base verb: Did you graduate in 2010?
