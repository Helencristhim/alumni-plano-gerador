Chapter 4: The Past Simple

grew · studied · became

Talking about finished actions in the past

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I grew up in a small town."

"She studied medicine for six years."

"They moved to a new country."

"He became a doctor in 2010."

FormRuleExample
Regularbase verb + -edstudy → studied, move → moved
Irregularthe form changesgrow → grew, be → was/were, become → became
Negativedid not + base verbI did not study law.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb: Did you graduate in 2010?

Regular verbs add -ed · irregular verbs change form · after did / did not, always use the base verb.

Grammar Practice

Put the Verb in the Past

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I ___grew up in a small town." (grow)
"She ___studied medicine at university." (study)
"We ___moved to a new city in 2015." (move)
"He ___became a doctor after many years." (become)
"They ___did not have much money." (not / have)
Language Focus

Regular or Irregular?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I go to college in 2005."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to college in 2005."

✗ WRONG

"She didn't studied medicine."

✓ RIGHT

"She didn't study medicine."

Use the past form for finished actions (went, not go). After did / didn't, use the base verb (study, not studied) -- do not mark the past twice.

Dialogue

David Tells His Story

A
Hi David. I love hearing how people started. Where did you grow up?
D
I grew up in a small town in the south. My childhood was quiet but happy.
A
That sounds lovely. And what did you study?
D
I studied engineering. I graduated in 2008, and then I started my career.
A
Was your first job a big challenge?
D
Yes, it was. I knew almost no one, but I slowly overcame the difficulties.
A
Was there a turning point for you?
D
Definitely. I relocated to a new city, and that decision changed everything. I achieved my dream there.
