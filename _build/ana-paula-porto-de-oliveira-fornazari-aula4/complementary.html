Lesson 4 -- Talking About the Past

TED Talk
The Danger of a Single Story -- Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie (TED)

A famous storyteller talks about her own childhood and background, and how the stories we tell shape the way we see people. She narrates her life in clear, natural past simple. Connection to Lesson 4: listen for how she describes where she grew up and the turning points in her story.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down five past simple verbs she uses to tell her own story.

TED Talk
The Power of Believing That You Can Improve -- Carol Dweck (TED)

A short, inspiring talk about how people overcome challenges and achieve their goals through effort and belief -- exactly the spirit of your own journey as a doctor. Connection to Lesson 4: this is the language of challenge, to overcome, and to achieve, all words from today's lesson.

Tip: after watching, tell your own story of a challenge you overcame, in three or four past simple sentences.

TED Talk
Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance -- Angela Lee Duckworth (TED)

The speaker left one career to start a new one, and studied why some people never give up. It is a real story of a turning point and a new beginning, told in natural past simple. Connection to Lesson 4: notice the words career, turning point, and to achieve as she tells her story.

Tip: pause when she talks about her career change, and describe a milestone or turning point in your own life.
