Stage 1.1: Words for Dining Out

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for describing food and ordering.

Savoury -- salgado, nao doce
"I prefer something savoury, like cheese, to a sweet dessert."
Hearty -- substancioso e reconfortante
"After the hike we wanted a hearty stew."
Succulent -- suculento e macio
"The lamb was wonderfully succulent."
A generous portion -- uma porcao farta
"They gave us such a generous portion."
Peckish -- com uma fominha, nao faminto
"I am only peckish, so a starter is enough."
A craving -- um desejo forte por algo
"I have a real craving for chocolate."
To cater for -- atender a uma dieta ou necessidade
"Do you cater for gluten-free diets?"
To be in the mood for -- estar a fim de comer algo
"I am really in the mood for seafood."
To swap -- trocar um item por outro
"Could I swap the fries for a salad?"
Bland -- sem graca, sem tempero
"The soup was a bit bland, so I added salt."
To splurge on -- gastar muito num mimo
"On holiday we splurge on a fine dinner."
To whip up -- preparar um prato rapidinho
"The chef whipped up a special for us."
To box up -- embalar as sobras para levar
"Could you box up the rest, please?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read Pricila's restaurant story and answer the questions.

"When we splurge on a special dinner, I love having the meat cooked exactly how I like it. Last night the lamb was so succulent that I nearly ordered a second plate. I asked the waiter to get my fries swapped for a salad, and I had the sauce served on the side. It was such a generous portion that we could not finish it, so we had the rest boxed up to take home. The chef even whipped up a savoury little extra -- the whole meal was so good that we can't stop talking about it."

1. Which structure means you arrange for someone else to do something?
A have/get + object + past participle ("I had the steak cooked").
B will + base verb.
C going to + verb.
2. When do you use "such"?
A Before an adjective alone ("such succulent").
B Before a noun phrase ("such a generous portion").
C Only in questions.
3. What did they do with the leftovers?
A They left them on the table.
B They had them boxed up to take home.
C They gave them to the chef.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Causative + so/such

Grammar

Para dizer que voce faz alguem fazer algo por voce, use have/get + objeto + participio passado. Para enfatizar, use so + adjetivo e such + (a/an) + substantivo. / To say you arrange for someone to do something for you, use have/get + object + past participle. To emphasise, use so + adjective and such + (a/an) + noun.

Uso / UseEnglishPortugues
Causative (formal)I had the steak cooked rare.Mandei preparar o bife mal passado.
Causative (get)Could I get it changed?Da para trocar?
so + adjectiveThe lamb was so succulent.O cordeiro estava tao suculento.
such + noun phraseIt was such a generous portion.Era uma porcao tao generosa.
Common mistakeNOT "so a generous portion." Say "such a generous portion."such + substantivo; so + adjetivo.
