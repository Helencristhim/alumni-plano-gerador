Chapter 3: The Code

What Happened

We achieved · We missed · We met · We went

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Last quarter, we achieved our target in dermatology."

"We missed the target in asthma."

"In April, I presented the plan to the Global team."

"In May, my team met sixty new doctors."

Three purple verbs end the same way. The green one does not. What is the difference?

FormUseExample
Most verbs + -edThe quarter is finished. The action is over.We achieved the target.
Some verbs change completelyNo -ed. A new word. Learn them one by one.meet → met · go → went
Negative: did not / didn't + verbThe verb goes back to normal — the "past" is inside did.We didn't achieve the target. (never "didn't achieved")
When?last quarter · last week · in April · two months ago · yesterday
Ready phrase: It was a difficult quarter.Nothing to build. One block, use it forever.It was a good quarter for dermatology.

You do not conjugate anything. You take the verb you already use — achieve, miss, meet — and you put it in the finished form.

The Irregulars

Five Verbs the Global Team Always Hears

No -ed. New word. Five is enough.

go → went — "Last month, I went to Paris."

have → had — "We had a challenge with market access."

give → gave — "I gave a short presentation in June."

meet → met — "My team met sixty new doctors."

come → came — "The growth came from the private market."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We didn't achieved the target."
"We didn't achieve the target."
"Last quarter we exceed the target."
"Last quarter we exceeded the target."
"The growth comed from the private market."
"The growth came from the private market."

After didn't, the verb goes back to normal — the past is already inside did. And the five irregular verbs never take -ed: it is came, never "comed".
