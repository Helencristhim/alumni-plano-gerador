Chapter 3 -- Conditions for a Deal

Grammar Discovery

unless / as long as / provided that

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. They all set a condition for the deal. What is the difference?

We can sign a longer contract, as long as you improve the response time.

We will commit to more volume provided that the unit price drops.

Unless you guarantee the uptime, we cannot agree.

I will accept the deal as long as we keep the exit clause.

The Rule

Setting Conditions

ConjunctionMeaningExample
as long asonly if (positive condition)We can sign as long as you improve the response time.
provided thatonly if / on the condition thatWe will commit provided that the price drops.
unlessif not / except if (negative condition)Unless you guarantee uptime, we cannot agree.

Key idea: After unless, as long as and provided that, use the present tense, not will: "provided that you reduce the price" (not will reduce). Unless = if not; as long as / provided that = only if.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We will sign provided that you will reduce the price."
"We will sign provided that you reduce the price."

After provided that, as long as and unless, use the present: "you reduce", not "you will reduce".

Grammar Practice

Set the Condition

"We cannot agree _____unless you guarantee the uptime." (if not)
"We will extend the term _____provided that the price drops." (only if)
"I will accept the deal _____as long as we keep the exit clause." (only if)
"Provided that you reduce the price, we _____will sign today." (main clause uses will / future)

About the Negotiation

1. How did Rafael prepare for the negotiation?

He listed everything they wanted and separated the deal-breakers from the small concessions; his biggest leverage was the two-year commitment, and he opened with the relationship, not the price.

2. What conditions did Rafael set during the talk?

A longer contract as long as they improved the response time; more volume provided that the unit price dropped; and no agreement at all unless they guaranteed the uptime.

3. What was the result, and why was it a good one?

Both sides made a concession: the vendor lowered the price and Rafael extended the term. They did not settle for less; they found a win-win deal, and the relationship is stronger than before.

The Negotiation Plan

NEGOTIATION PLAN -- CLOUDSCALE RENEWAL

Lead: Rafael Pelizaro

MY LEVERAGE

A firm two-year commitment and higher volume. Volume matters to any vendor, so this is my main source of power.

DEAL-BREAKERS (no agreement without these)

Unless they guarantee 99.9 percent uptime, we cannot agree. Unless there is a 90-day exit clause, we do not sign.

CONDITIONS I CAN OFFER

We will commit to more volume provided that the unit price drops. We can extend the term as long as they improve the response time.

MY CONCESSIONS (small things I can give up)

A longer contract term, and an earlier payment schedule, as long as the price is right.

GOAL

Find common ground and a win-win deal with mutual benefit. Get to yes without losing the relationship.
