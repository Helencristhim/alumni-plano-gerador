Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Leverage -- an advantage or source of power that helps you in a deal
"My biggest leverage was our two-year commitment." (Vantagem / poder de barganha)
Concession -- something you give up so both sides can reach a deal
"In the end, we both made a concession." (Concessão)
Deal-breaker -- a condition so important that, without it, there is no deal
"I separated the deal-breakers from the small concessions." (Condição decisiva)
Win-win -- a result where both sides benefit, not just one
"I wanted a win-win, not a win for one side." (Ganha-ganha)
Common ground -- the points both sides already agree on
"A good negotiation is a search for common ground." (Terreno comum)
Bargain -- to discuss terms to get a better price or condition
"We bargained over the unit price for an hour." (Barganhar / negociar)
Settle -- to reach a final agreement that ends a discussion
"We did not just settle for less; we found a fair deal." (Fechar / chegar a acordo)
Mutual -- shared by both sides equally
"We found a deal with mutual benefit for both companies." (Mútuo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last month Rafael had to renew the contract with the main cloud vendor. He prepared carefully: he listed his deal-breakers and his small concessions, and he knew his main leverage was a two-year commitment. In the meeting, he started with the relationship and looked for common ground, because he wanted a win-win. Then he set his conditions. He said they would commit to more volume provided that the unit price dropped, and they could extend the term as long as the vendor improved the response time. He was also very clear about one thing: unless the vendor guaranteed the uptime, there would be no deal at all. After they bargained for a while, both sides made a concession and settled on a fair deal with mutual benefit.

1. Why did Rafael start with the relationship and common ground?
A Because he only cared about the lowest price.
B Because he wanted a win-win, a deal good for both sides.
C Because he wanted to end the partnership.
2. Which sentence sets a condition correctly?
A "We will commit provided that the price will drop."
B "We will commit provided that the price drops."
C "We will commit unless the price drops."
3. What did "unless the vendor guaranteed the uptime" mean for the deal?
A If the vendor did not guarantee uptime, there would be no deal.
B The uptime did not matter at all.
C The deal would happen no matter what.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Conditional Conjunctions

GRAMMAR

Numa negociação, você coloca condições para o acordo. as long as e provided that = só se (only if); unless = se não / a menos que (if not). Depois dessas conjunções, use o PRESENTE, nunca "will". / In a negotiation you set conditions for the deal. as long as and provided that = only if; unless = if not. After these conjunctions, use the present tense, not "will".

ConjunctionMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
as long asonly if / só seWe can sign as long as you improve the SLA.
provided thatonly if / na condição de queWe will commit provided that the price drops.
unlessif not / a menos queUnless you guarantee uptime, we cannot agree.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de unless, as long as e provided that, use o PRESENTE: "provided that you reduce the price" (não "will reduce"). Unless = se não; as long as / provided that = só se.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each negotiation situation.

You want a longer contract, but only if the vendor lowers the price. The best sentence is:
A "We will sign a longer contract provided that you lower the price."
B "We will sign a longer contract provided that you will lower the price."
C "We will sign a longer contract unless you lower the price."
Uptime is a deal-breaker. The clearest way to say it is:
A "We can agree if you do not guarantee the uptime."
B "Unless you guarantee the uptime, we cannot agree."
C "We will agree as long as you do not guarantee the uptime."
You want to open the negotiation in a constructive way. The best opener is:
A "Your price is too high and you are wrong."
B "Before the price, let us find some common ground. I want a win-win."
C "Take it or leave it."
