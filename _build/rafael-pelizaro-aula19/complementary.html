Lesson 19 -- Negotiating & Reaching Agreement

Series
"Suits" -- the negotiation and closing scenes

Watch scenes where lawyers negotiate a deal: they use their leverage, set conditions, and bargain to a win-win. Notice how they look for common ground before they push on the terms.

Watch with English subtitles. Pick one negotiation scene and write three conditions using "unless", "as long as", and "provided that".

Podcast
Harvard Business Review -- "Negotiating to Get to Yes"

A practical guide to negotiation: find common ground, know your leverage, protect your deal-breakers, and trade concessions to reach a win-win without damaging the relationship.

Record a 90-second negotiation plan for a real deal: name your leverage and one deal-breaker, and set two conditions using "unless" and "provided that".

YouTube
"Unless, As Long As & Provided That -- Conditional Conjunctions in English"

A clear lesson on setting conditions: "unless" means "if not", while "as long as" and "provided that" mean "only if". The verb after them stays in the present, never "will". Directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, write five conditions for a real deal at work: "We can renew provided that you cut the cost", "Unless the SLA improves, we will switch vendors".
