Chapter 3

The Past Tense

Past simple & time markers to tell your story

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I studied engineering in São Paulo."

"A firm hired me as a junior appraiser."

"Later, I founded my own company and became a partner."

"When did you graduate? Where did you start?"

StructureUseExample
Regular: verb + -edFinished past actionsI studied. They hired me.
Irregular verbsMust be memorizedget → got, become → became, begin → began
did + base verbQuestions & negativesDid you graduate? I did not start there.
Time markersWhen it happenedin 2004, then, later, after that, two years ago
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I becomed a partner in 2015."

✓ RIGHT

"I became a partner in 2015."

✗ WRONG

"When did you graduated?"

✓ RIGHT

"When did you graduate?"

✗ WRONG

"I start my career in 2005."

✓ RIGHT

"I started my career in 2005."

Learn irregular verbs by heart (became, got, began). After "did," use the base verb: Did you graduate?

Chapter 4

Sharing Your Background

A career conversation at the congress

Dialogue

How Did You Get Here?

E
Marcos, it's good to see you again! Tell me, how did you get into valuation?
M
Well, I studied civil engineering in São Paulo and graduated in 2004.
E
Nice. And what was your first job?
M
A large firm hired me as a junior appraiser. After a few years, they promoted me to team leader.
E
That's a great path. So when did you start your own company?
M
In 2015, I founded my own firm and became a partner. Winning my first big client was a real milestone.
E
Impressive. You really built something from the ground up.
M
Thank you. It took years of experience, but it was worth it. What about your career path?

Detective

Spot the Error

0 / 4 errors found

"I becomed a partner in 2015."
"I became a partner in 2015." (become is irregular: became)
"When did you founded your company?"
"When did you found your company?" (after did, use the base verb)
"Last year I start my own firm."
"Last year I started my own firm." (last year = past, use started)
"I graduated on 2004."
"I graduated in 2004." (use in with years)
