Stage 2.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Graduate -- formar-se (to finish your studies at a university)
"I graduated as an engineer in 2004."
Degree -- diploma, formação (a qualification from a university)
"I have a degree in civil engineering."
Hire -- contratar (to give someone a job)
"A large firm hired me as a junior appraiser."
Found -- fundar, abrir (to start a company; past: founded)
"I founded my own valuation firm in 2015."
Promote -- promover (to move someone to a higher position)
"After three years, they promoted me to team leader."
Experience -- experiência (knowledge gained over years of work)
"I have over twenty years of experience in valuation."
Achievement -- conquista, realização (something important you did successfully)
"Founding my own firm was my biggest achievement."
Client -- cliente (a person or company that pays you for your work)
"My first client was a small construction company."
Milestone -- marco (an important point that marks progress)
"Winning that contract was a real milestone."
Career path -- trajetória profissional (the series of jobs in your working life)
"My career path took me from site work to leadership."

Stage 2.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Sofia studied economics in Toronto and graduated in 2006. After university, a bank hired her as a financial analyst. She worked there for five years and gained a lot of experience. Then, ten years ago, she moved into valuation. In 2018, she became a senior partner at her firm. "Winning my first international client was a real milestone," she says. When a colleague asked, "How did you get into this field?", Sofia answered: "I started in finance, and step by step, I built my career path."

1. Por que dizemos "she became" e não "she becomed"?
A Porque "become" é um verbo regular.
B Porque "become" é irregular: o passado é "became".
C Porque a ação acontece no presente.
2. Na pergunta "How did you get into this field?", por que usamos "get" e não "got"?
A Porque, depois de "did", usamos o verbo na forma base.
B Porque a pergunta é sobre o futuro.
C Porque "get" nunca muda de forma.
3. Which sentence correctly uses a time marker with the past simple?
A "She graduate on 2006."
B "She graduated in 2006."
C "She is graduated since 2006."

Stage 2.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple & Time Markers

GRAMMAR

Quando e como usar o past simple para contar sua trajetória (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
verb + -ed (regular)Ações terminadas no passadoI studied. They hired me.
irregular verbsDecorar (não seguem regra). Cuidado com o espanhol!become → became, get → got, begin → began
did + verbo basePerguntas e negativasDid you graduate? I did not start there.
Time markersQuando aconteceu: in 2004, then, later, after that, two years ago (use in com anos, não "on")

Quiz

Someone asks about your first job. The best answer is:
