Chapter 3 -- The Conditional Code

Grammar Discovery

First conditional and polite requests

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words show about future results and polite requests?

"If we submit the bid on time, the client will review it."

"The bid will be valid if the forms are complete."

"Could you send the quotation today?"

"Would you mind clarifying the new clause?"

Grammar Rule

First Conditional and Polite Requests

FormUseExample
If + present, will + verblikely future resultIf we submit, they will review.
will + verb, if + presentsame, other orderIt will be valid if it is complete.
Could you + verb?polite requestCould you send the quotation?
Would you mind + verb-ing?very polite requestWould you mind clarifying the clause?

Key idea: after if, use the present simple, not will. Put will in the result part. For requests, use Could you + base verb, and Would you mind + verb-ing.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we will submit the bid, the client will review it."
"If we submit the bid, the client will review it."

After if, use the present simple. The word will goes in the other part of the sentence, the result.
