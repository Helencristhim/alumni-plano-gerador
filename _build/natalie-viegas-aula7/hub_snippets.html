Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Procurement -- the process of buying goods and services for a company
"Our procurement team buys software for the government." (Compras / aquisições)
Tender -- a formal process where companies offer to do work at a price
"The ministry opened a tender for cloud services." (Licitação)
Bid -- a formal offer to win a contract at a certain price
"We submitted our bid before the deadline." (Proposta)
Vendor -- a company that sells goods or services to a client
"Microsoft is a vendor for many public agencies." (Fornecedor)
Clause -- one specific condition or part of a contract
"The contract has a clause about data security." (Cláusula)
Compliance -- following the official rules and laws
"Public contracts require strict compliance." (Conformidade)
Approval -- official permission to go ahead with something
"We are waiting for the manager's approval." (Aprovação)
Negotiate -- to discuss terms in order to reach an agreement
"We negotiate the price and the timeline." (Negociar)
Quotation -- a written document with the prices for a job
"Please send us a quotation by Friday." (Orçamento)
Shortlist -- to choose the best options from a larger group
"The client shortlisted three vendors." (Pré-selecionar)

GRAMMAR

Natalie is preparing a bid for a government tender. She tells her team the plan. "If we submit the bid on time, the client will review it on Monday. If our compliance documents are complete, the bid will be valid." She also needs help from the finance team, so she sends a polite message: "Could you send me the quotation today? And would you mind checking the new clause about data security?" Natalie knows that if the client shortlists her company, they will negotiate the final price next week.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional and Polite Requests

GRAMMAR

Usamos o first conditional para falar de um resultado provável no futuro, e pedidos educados para soar profissional. / We use the first conditional for a likely future result, and polite requests to sound professional.

FormUseExample
If + present, will + verba likely future resultIf we submit on time, the client will review it.
will + verb, if + presentsame idea, other orderThe bid will be valid if the forms are complete.
Could you + verb?a polite requestCould you send the quotation?
Would you mind + verb-ing?a very polite requestWould you mind clarifying the clause?

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de if use o presente, não o futuro: "If we submit..." (não "If we will submit..."). O will fica na outra parte da frase. Para pedidos, use Could you + verbo no infinitivo, e Would you mind + verbo com -ing ("Would you mind sending...?", não "Would you mind to send...?").
