Aula 10 -- Pre-class

Travel English I -- Travel Vocabulary & Polite Requests

Travel vocabulary and how to make polite requests at the airport and the hotel. New vocabulary: boarding pass, gate, aisle seat, luggage, check-in, departure, customs, reservation, front desk, itinerary. Grammar: polite requests with Could I / Could you + base verb, I would like to + verb, and Would it be possible to + verb.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Boarding pass -- the pass that lets you get on the plane
"Please keep your boarding pass ready." (Passe de embarque)
Gate -- the door where you get on the plane
"The departure gate is number twenty-two." (Portão de embarque)
Aisle seat -- a seat next to the walking path on a plane
"Could I have an aisle seat, please?" (Assento no corredor)
Luggage -- the bags and cases you travel with
"I would like to check one piece of luggage." (Bagagem)
Check-in -- to register for a flight or a hotel room
"Check-in for the flight opens at seven." (Check-in / registro)
Departure -- the time when a plane or train leaves
"The departure time is nine in the morning." (Partida)
Customs -- where officers check what you bring into a country
"Go through customs after you land." (Alfândega)
Reservation -- a booking you make in advance
"I would like to confirm my reservation." (Reserva)
Front desk -- the reception desk at a hotel
"Ask at the front desk for your key." (Recepção)
Itinerary -- the plan of a trip with times and places
"Please review the itinerary for the trip." (Itinerário)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests

GRAMMAR

Para pedir algo de forma educada, use Could I + verbo na forma BASE (para você mesma) ou Could you + verbo na forma BASE (para a outra pessoa), SEM to, SEM -ing e SEM -s. Use I would like + to + verbo (ou + substantivo) para um desejo educado, e Would it be possible to + verbo para ser ainda mais educada. "Could I have a seat?" (não "Could I to have a seat?"). / To ask for something politely, use Could I or Could you + the base verb, with no to, no -ing and no -s. Use I would like + to + verb for a polite wish, and Would it be possible to + verb to be extra polite.

Type / TipoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Ask for yourselfCould I + base verb"Could I have an aisle seat?"
Ask the other personCould you + base verb"Could you check my luggage?"
Polite wishI would like + to + verb / + noun"I would like to confirm my reservation."
Extra politeWould it be possible to + verb"Would it be possible to get a late check-out?"
