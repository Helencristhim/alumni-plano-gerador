Chapter 3 -- Have It Done

Grammar Discovery

The causative: have / get something done

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Who really does the job in each sentence?

"I had my hair cut yesterday." (a barber cut it)

"She got her phone repaired." (a technician fixed it)

"We are having the house painted." (painters paint it)

"He needs to get his car serviced." (a mechanic services it)

Grammar Rule

Have / Get Something Done

FormStructureExample
Havehave + object + past participleI had my hair cut.
Get (informal)get + object + past participleI got my phone fixed.
QuestionDid you have/get + object + pp?Did you have your car serviced?
Negativedid not have/get + object + ppI did not get it repaired.

Key idea: we use the causative when ANOTHER person does the job for us. The object comes BEFORE the past participle: have + my car + serviced. The focus is on the result, not on who did it.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I had repaired my phone at the shop."
"I had my phone repaired at the shop."

Put the object before the past participle: have + my phone + repaired. "I had repaired my phone" means YOU repaired it.
