Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each service word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Repair — to fix something that is damaged or broken
"I had my bike repaired last week." (consertar)
Replace — to put a new thing in place of an old one
"I got my cracked screen replaced." (substituir)
Install — to put equipment in place and make it ready to use
"We had new software installed." (instalar)
Deliver — to bring something to a place for someone
"They delivered the package to my door." (entregar)
Service — to check and fix a machine so it keeps working well
"We have the car serviced every year." (revisar)
Appointment — a fixed time to meet someone or use a service
"I made an appointment with the dentist." (hora marcada)
Mechanic — a person who repairs cars and machines
"The mechanic fixed the engine quickly." (mecânico)
Plumber — a person who fixes water pipes and taps
"We called a plumber for the leak." (encanador)
Tailor — a person who makes and changes clothes to fit you
"The tailor shortened my trousers." (alfaiate)
Technician — a person trained to repair technical equipment
"A technician set up the new network." (técnico)
Estimate — a guess of how much a job will cost
"The garage gave me an estimate." (orçamento)
Inspect — to look at something carefully to check its condition
"They inspected the roof for damage." (inspecionar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last weekend I got many things done before my school trip. First, I had my old passport renewed at the office. Then I got my phone repaired, because the screen was cracked, and a technician also replaced the battery. On Sunday, my family had the car serviced by a mechanic, and we are having new tyres delivered this week. We did not have time to clean everything ourselves, so we are getting the house cleaned too.

B Another person (the office)
C No one

B I got my phone repaired.
C I repaired got my phone.
3. What order do have and get follow?
A have + past participle + object
B have + object + past participle

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — The Causative

GRAMMAR

Usamos o causativo quando OUTRA pessoa faz o trabalho para nós. Estrutura: have/get + objeto + particicio passado. / Use the causative when another person does the job for you: have/get + object + past participle.

Form / FormaStructureExample
Havehave + object + past participleI had my hair cut.
Get (informal)get + object + past participleI got my phone fixed.
Question / PerguntaDid you have/get + object + pp?Did you have your car serviced?
Negative / Negativadid not have/get + object + ppI did not get it repaired.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o objeto vem ANTES do particicio: have + my car + serviced. "I had repaired my phone" significa que VOCÊ consertou. Para serviço de outra pessoa, use "I had my phone repaired".
