Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Negotiate -- to talk with someone to reach a deal both sides accept
"We negotiate the software price every year." (Negociar)
Supplier -- a company that provides goods or services to your business
"The supplier sent us a quote for the licenses." (Fornecedor)
Quote -- a price a supplier offers for a product or service
"The first quote was over our budget." (Cotação)
Proposal -- a formal offer or plan you present to the other side
"Their proposal looked good, but the price was high." (Proposta)
Terms -- the conditions of a deal: price, length and payment
"We agreed on the terms of the contract." (Termos / condições)
Discount -- a reduction in the normal price
"She offered a ten percent discount." (Desconto)
Counteroffer -- a new offer you make in reply, with different terms
"I made a counteroffer with support included." (Contraproposta)
Agreement -- when both sides accept the same terms
"We reached an agreement that works for both." (Acordo)
Flexible -- willing to change or adapt to find a deal
"The supplier was flexible on the price." (Flexível)
Commitment -- a firm promise to do something, like sign for a period
"We offered a two-year commitment." (Compromisso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael has to renew the team's software, and the first quote from the supplier is over budget. He reads the proposal and prepares his terms before the call. He stays polite. "Would you consider a discount," he asks, "for a two-year commitment?" The supplier is flexible. She offers ten percent off, but she would need faster payment. Rafael makes a counteroffer: "That would work. We would accept the longer term, but only with support included." After a few minutes, they reach an agreement that works for both sides. Rafael uses would to keep every offer polite and soft.

A A discount in exchange for a longer commitment.
B A full refund of last year's contract.
C A completely new supplier.
2. Why does Rafael use would so much?
A To talk about a finished moment in the past.
B To keep his offers and requests polite and soft.
C To give the supplier a direct order.
3. What does Rafael require in his counteroffer?
A A higher price for faster delivery.
B Nothing in return for the discount.
C Support included at no extra cost.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Would for Proposals

GRAMMAR

Usamos would para fazer ofertas e pedidos mais educados e suaves numa negociação. / We use would to make offers and requests softer and more polite in a negotiation.

Form / FormaStructureExample / Exemplo
Affirmative / Afirmativowould + base verbWe would accept these terms.
Question / PerguntaWould + subject + base verb?Would you consider a discount?
Negative / Negativowould not (wouldn't) + base verbWe would not accept that price.
Contraction / ContraçãoI'd / we'd / that'd + base verbThat'd work for both sides.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de would, use o verbo na base, sem "to" (would suggest, não would to suggest) e nunca no passado (would accept, não would accepted). Use would para soar educado: "Would you consider...?" é muito mais suave que "Give me a discount."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "Give me a lower price."
B "Would you consider a discount for a longer deal?"
C "I would to ask a discount."
You want to accept a trade but add one condition. You say:
A "We would accept, but only with support included."
B "We would accepted the offer."
C "We accept nothing today."
