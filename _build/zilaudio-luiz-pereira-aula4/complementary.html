Work emails: Office English episode 1 — BBC Learning English

A short, funny story in a real office. Two colleagues write and read work e-mails, and the teacher stops to explain the useful phrases — the same language of Lesson 4: the subject, the request, the reply. Clear, slow English with subtitles on the screen.

Tip: watch once with the subtitles ON. Then watch again and write down three e-mail phrases you hear. Say each one out loud, as if you were replying to David.

Everyday Grammar: The Simple Past Tense — VOA Learning English

The exact grammar of Lesson 4: the past simple for actions that are already done. The video shows regular verbs with -ed and the irregular ones you learn one by one — like send → sent. There is a short video at the top and the full text below it, in slow English.

Tip: watch the video first. Then read the text and find five past verbs. For each one, decide: is it regular (-ed) or irregular? Say the base verb and the past out loud: "send, sent".

How to write professional emails in English — engVid (Alex)

The teacher Alex shows the most common phrases to sound professional in an e-mail: how to open, how to make a request, how to confirm, and how to close with "Best regards". These are the building blocks of the reply you practised in class.

Tip: after the video, write a short reply to a buyer — four lines only: thank, confirm, the deadline, and the close. Then read your e-mail out loud before you send it.
