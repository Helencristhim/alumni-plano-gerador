Chapter 4

The Code

Grammar Discovery

What Is Already Done

I sent you our price last Monday.
I received your numbers on Wednesday.
My team asked me for two documents.
I attached a short form to this e-mail.
Base verbPast simpleType
askaskedregular — add -ed
receivereceivedregular — add -d
attachattachedregular — add -ed
sendsentirregular — a new word

The past simple is for actions that are done and finished. Most verbs take -ed. A few are irregular and you learn them one by one: send → sent.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I send you the price yesterday."
"I sent you the price yesterday."
"I attach the form last week."
"I attached the form last week."

Something already done → past simple. Regular verbs take -ed: attach → attached. And learn the irregular ones: send → sent.

Grammar · Past Simple

Regular & Irregular

TypeBase → PastIn an e-mail
Regular (-ed)call → calledI called you this morning.
Regular (-ed)need → neededMy team needed two documents.
Irregularwrite → wroteI wrote to you last week.
Irregularsend → sentI sent the attachment on Monday.

Regular verbs are easy: just add -ed. Irregular verbs are a new word each — learn them one by one.

Your Reply

The E-mail You Send Back

To: David Miller · Subject: Re: Soybean order
Dear David,
Thank you for your e-mail. I received your message and the attachment.
I confirm the total tons and the final price.
I will send the form back by Friday, before the weekend.
Best regards, Ziláudio.
