Aula 12 -- Who's Who at AVRT -- Describing People, Companies and Markets

Film
The Founder (2016)

The story of Ray Kroc, the salesman who turned McDonald's into a global company, and the two brothers whose restaurant started it all. It is a case study in the exact vocabulary of Lesson 12: the founder who built it, the flagship product that made its name, the venture that grew, and the incumbent that everyone had to catch. Every character is a person whose choices you can describe with a relative clause.

Tip: watch one scene with English subtitles. Write three sentences about the people and company using relative clauses ("the man who...", "the company that...", "the brothers whose..."). Check who vs which vs that.

"Using defining and non-defining relative clauses" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on exactly the code from Lesson 12: who, which, that, where and whose, and the difference between defining clauses (no commas, essential) and non-defining clauses (commas, extra information). It covers the classic traps, including why you never use "that" after a comma.
