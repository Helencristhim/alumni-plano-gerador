Who's Who at AVRT -- Describing People, Companies and Markets

Reading a company profile of AVRT and describing who is who. Key words: founder, stakeholder, subsidiary, headquarters, flagship, venture, niche, cutting-edge, proprietary, incumbent, mentor, track record. Structure: relative clauses -- defining (no commas: who/which/that/where/whose) and non-defining (with commas: who/which, never that) -- to describe and add information about people, companies and markets.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Founder -- the person who started a company (fundador / fundadora)
"The founder who started AVRT is Izabel."
Stakeholder -- anyone with an interest in a company's success (parte interessada)
"Every company has stakeholders who care about its future."
Subsidiary -- a company owned or controlled by a larger one (subsidiária)
"AVRT opened a subsidiary, which handles North American sales."
Headquarters -- the main office where a company is run (sede)
"The headquarters where AVRT operates is just outside Valinhos."
Flagship -- a company's most important product or service (carro-chefe)
"Its flagship sensor is the product that made the company's name."
Venture -- a new business project that involves risk (empreendimento)
"It began as a small venture that grew into a serious player."
Niche -- a specialized segment of the market (nicho)
"AVRT serves a niche which most large firms ignore."
Cutting-edge -- the most advanced; state of the art (de ponta / última geração)
"Clients who need cutting-edge sensors come to AVRT."
Proprietary -- owned exclusively by one company; patented (proprietário / exclusivo)
"The proprietary software that runs the sensors is AVRT's own."
Incumbent -- the established company that already dominates a market (empresa estabelecida / dominante)
"The incumbent that once dominated the segment is being caught."
Mentor -- an experienced person who advises and guides someone (mentor / mentora)
"A mentor who advised Izabel early on shaped the company."
Track record -- a history of past performance and achievements (histórico / reputação)
"A track record which clients trust is worth more than any advertisement."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

AVRT, which was founded in Valinhos in 2015, is a company that designs wireless sensors. The founder who started it, Izabel, is a CEO whose background is in electronics. Its flagship product, the sensor that made the company's name, is used by factories that track equipment in real time. The engineer who leads research, and whose designs won two awards, built the team. AVRT serves a niche which large firms ignore, and the headquarters where it operates sits just outside Valinhos. The incumbent that once dominated the segment is now the company AVRT is catching.

1. "The founder who started it is Izabel." Why "who"?
A "who" introduces a relative clause about a person.
B "who" is used for things and places.
C "who" always needs commas.
2. "AVRT, which was founded in 2015, is a company..." Why the commas and "which"?
A It is a defining clause that tells us which AVRT.
B It is a non-defining clause: extra information, commas, "which" (never "that").
C Because "that" would be more formal here.
3. "a CEO whose background is in electronics." What does "whose" show?
A Possession -- the background belongs to the CEO.
B A place where something happens.
C A question about who someone is.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Describing nouns (explicação em inglês e português).

WordUse / UsoExample
whopessoas (people)The founder who started it.
which / thatcoisas (things); "that" só em definingThe sensor that we make.
wherelugares (places)The city where AVRT is based.
whoseposse (possession)A CEO whose background is in electronics.
Defining vs non-definingsem vírgula = essencial; com vírgula = informação extraAVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing.
Cuidado / Watch outNunca use "what" como pronome relativo: errado "the company what makes..." → certo "the company that/which makes...". Na non-defining (com vírgula), use só which/who, nunca "that".

Quiz

A "AVRT is a company what makes sensors."
B "AVRT is a company that makes sensors."
C "AVRT is a company who makes sensors."
You add extra, non-essential information about AVRT. Which is best?
A "AVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing."
B "AVRT, that was founded in 2015, is growing."
