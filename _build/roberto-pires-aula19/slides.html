Chapter 3: The Order Code

When, After & Before

Put the steps of the journey in order

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"When the plane lands, you turn on your phone."

"After you leave the plane, you go to immigration."

"Before you leave customs, you show your passport."

"When there is turbulence, you fasten your seatbelt."

WordExampleMeaning
When + presentWhen the plane lands, you turn on your phone.at that moment / every time
After + presentAfter you leave the plane, you go to immigration.this happens second
Before + presentBefore you leave, you show your passport.this happens first

Use when, after and before to put two actions in order. After these words, use the present simple, even when you talk about the future: "When the plane lands..." (NOT "when the plane will land"). You can start with the time word or put it in the middle: "You fasten your seatbelt when there is turbulence."

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"When the plane will land..."

✓ RIGHT

"When the plane lands..."

✗ WRONG

"After you will leave the plane..."

✓ RIGHT

"After you leave the plane..."

Dialogue

In Your Seat

F
Welcome on board, sir. Please fasten your seatbelt before we take off.
R
Of course. When can I open my tray table?
F
After the plane takes off, you can open it. The cabin crew will serve drinks soon.
R
Thank you. And what do I do when there is turbulence?
F
When the sign is on, stay in your seat and keep your seatbelt fastened. Before we land, please close your tray table.
R
Got it. After we land, where do I go for immigration?
F
Just follow the signs after you leave the plane. Immigration is first, and customs is after that.
