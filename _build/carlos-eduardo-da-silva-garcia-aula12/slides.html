Chapter 4: Quantifiers

All, Most, Some, A Few, None

How many does each word mean?

Discover the Grammar

Read and Notice

"All the criteria are important."

"Most farms comply with the rules."

"Only a few farms are missing documents."

"None of the farms failed completely."

QuantifierHow manyExample
all100% -- everythingAll the criteria are checked.
mosta big part (more than half)Most farms comply.
some / severala part / a numberSome farms are above 70%.
a fewa small numberA few farms are missing documents.
none0 -- nothingNone of the farms failed.

Use a plural noun after the quantifier: most farms, a few criteria, none of the farms. Say a score as "78 out of 100". / Use substantivo plural depois do quantifier.

Language Focus

Which Quantifier?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Most of farms comply."

✓ RIGHT

"Most farms comply."

✗ WRONG

"The score is 78 out from 100."

✓ RIGHT

"The score is 78 out of 100."

Say "most farms" or "most of the farms" -- not "most of farms". A score is "X out of Y". / Diga "most farms" ou "most of the farms"; score = "out of".

E
And do most of the farms comply?
C
Yes. Most farms comply, but a few farms are missing some documents.
E
Did any farm fail the audit?
C
None of the farms failed completely. The average score is 78 out of 100.
E
Great result. How many farms score above 70 percent?
C
Some farms are above 70 percent, and several are close to full compliance.
