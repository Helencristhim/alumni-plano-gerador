Chapter 3: Reported Speech

When the Tense Moves Back

say / tell + backshift, one step into the past

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I see the defendant" → She said that she saw the defendant.

"I saw him" → He testified that he had seen him.

"I will testify" → She said that she would testify.

"I can identify him" → He stated that he could identify him.

Direct speechReported speechExample
present simplepast simple"I see" → she saw
past simplepast perfect"I saw" → he had seen
willwould"I will" → she would
cancould"I can" → he could

The tense moves one step into the past. Use "say (that)..." with no listener; use "tell someone (that)..." with a listener.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"He said me that he was late."

✓ RIGHT

"He told me that he was late."

✗ WRONG

"She said that she will testify."

✓ RIGHT

"She said that she would testify."

Use "tell" when a listener is named (tell me, tell the court); use "say" when no listener follows. And after a past reporting verb, the tense shifts back: will becomes would.

Chapter 4: In the Courtroom

Comparing the Statements

Luiz and a colleague review the testimony

Dialogue

What Did the Witness Say?

S
Luiz, what did the first witness tell the court?
L
She testified that she had seen our client leave before midnight.
S
And did the second witness confirm that?
L
No. He claimed that he had heard nothing, but later he admitted that he had been asleep.
S
So one of them must have changed the story.
L
Exactly. In her deposition she said the lights were off; today she asserted that they were on.
S
Then we argue that the testimony is unreliable.
L
Agreed. I will request that the transcript be read back to the court.
Comprehension

About the Testimony

1. What did the first witness testify?
That she had seen the client leave before midnight.
2. What did the second witness finally admit?
That he had been asleep.
3. What changed between her deposition and today?
Whether the lights were off or on.
4. What will Luiz request?
That the transcript be read back to the court.
