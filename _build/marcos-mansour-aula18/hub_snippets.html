"I showed my boarding pass at the gate."

"I confirmed my reservation at the front desk."
Menu -- cardápio (the list of food and drinks)
"Could I see the menu, please?"
Server -- garçom/garçonete (the person who brings your food)
"I asked the server for a glass of water."
Bill -- conta (the paper that shows how much you pay)
"Could we have the bill, please?"
Landmark -- ponto de referência (a famous place to find your way)
"The hotel is near a famous landmark."
Crosswalk -- faixa de pedestres (where you cross the street)
"Turn left at the crosswalk."
Invitation -- convite (when someone asks you to an event)
"I received an invitation to the dinner."
Goal -- meta/objetivo (something you want to achieve)
"One of my goals is to grow internationally."
Negotiate -- negociar (to discuss terms to reach a deal)
"I negotiated a small partnership at the congress."

Stage 18.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A confident communicator uses the right structure at the right moment. At the airport, Marcos showed his boarding pass. At the hotel, he said: "Could I have the key, please?" -- a polite request. At the restaurant he asked the server: "Could we have the menu and, later, the bill?" To find the congress, he asked for directions and heard imperatives: "Go straight and turn left at the crosswalk." That evening he got an invitation: "Would you like to join us?" He spoke about his goals: "Next year, I am going to grow internationally." Finally, he closed a deal: "If you send the terms, we will sign." Remember the golden rule: NEVER use will after if -- the if-clause uses the present.

1. Para pedir algo educadamente (o menu, a conta), qual estrutura usamos?
A imperativo (Give me the menu).
B pedido polido (Could I have the menu?).
C condicional (If I have the menu).
2. Para dar direções, qual forma do verbo usamos?
A imperativo / verbo base (Go straight, turn left).
B will + verbo (You will go straight).
C Could + verbo (Could you go straight?).
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If you will send the terms, we will sign."
B "If you send the terms, we will sign."
C "If you send the terms, we sign."
