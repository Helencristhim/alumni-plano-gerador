One Moment, One Structure

MomentStructureExample
Asking / orderingCould / Would likeCould I have the bill?
Directionsimperative (base verb)Go straight, turn left.
InvitingWould you like / How aboutWould you like to join us?
Future planswill / going toI am going to grow.
Making a dealif + present, will + verbIf you send it, we will sign.
Common Mistakes Review

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Give me the menu."

✓ RIGHT

"Could I have the menu, please?" (polite request)

✗ WRONG

"Go to straight and turn to left."

✓ RIGHT

"Go straight and turn left." (no "to")

✗ WRONG

"If you will send the terms, we will sign."

✓ RIGHT

"If you send the terms, we will sign." (present after if)

A confident communicator picks the right structure: polite requests to ask, imperatives for directions, and never "will" after "if".
