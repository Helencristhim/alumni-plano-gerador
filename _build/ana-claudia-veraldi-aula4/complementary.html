Lesson 4 -- My Dogs

Documentary
Inside the Mind of a Dog (Netflix)

Now is the right moment for this one. Scientists and trainers spend the whole film describing dogs: their size, their age, their temperament, what frightens them and what excites them. It is ninety minutes of exactly the vocabulary you learned tonight, used by native speakers about animals like yours.

Tip: every time someone describes a dog, pause and repeat the sentence about one of yours. Listen especially for -ed and -ing adjectives - the film is full of them.

Podcast
The Moth Radio Hour -- "American Dreams"

Come back to this one with today's lesson in your ear. Storytellers describe people the way you described your dogs tonight: a few well-chosen adjectives and you can see the person. Notice how rarely they use more than three.

Tip: pick one storyteller and count the adjectives they use for a person. Then describe someone in your life with the same economy.

Series
The Repair Shop -- Season 1, Episode 1 (full episode)

Still worth your time, and it fits tonight perfectly. The restorers describe every object before they touch it: a small wooden box, a beautiful old clock, a filthy little chair. That is adjective order in its natural habitat.

Tip: pause when someone describes an object and say the same phrase about something in your own house.
