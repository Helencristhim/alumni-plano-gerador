Chapter 3: The Code

A Big Old Brown Dog

Order matters · and so does -ed vs -ing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"She is a big old brown dog."

"He is a small noisy young one."

"After the walk, I am exhausted."

"That is because the puppy is exhausting."

Try saying the blue words in a different order. And who is tired in the green pair — me, or the dog?

FormUseExample
Adjective orderopinion → size → age → shape → color → origina lovely big old brown dog
How manyTwo or three is natural. More than that sounds like a list.a big old dog
-ed adjectiveHow the PERSON feelsI am exhausted / frightened / bored
-ing adjectiveWhat CAUSES the feelingthe puppy is exhausting / the film is boring

If you say "I am boring", you are telling people that YOU are the boring thing. Careful with that one.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She is a brown big old dog."
"She is a big old brown dog."
"After the walk I am exhausting."
"After the walk I am exhausted."
"I have two blacks dogs."
"I have two black dogs."

English fixes the order: opinion, size, age, shape, color. The -ed form is how you feel; the -ing form is what causes it. And an adjective in English never takes an -s.

Practice

-ed or -ing?

Say it first, then click to check

"After a long walk I am ___exhausted." (how I feel)
"The puppy is absolutely ___exhausting." (what causes it)
"She was ___frightened of the storm." (how she felt)
"The thunder was ___frightening for both of them." (what caused it)
"He is a ___big old brown dog." (big / brown / old — in order)
Detail

Put Them in Order

opinion → size → age → shape → color

brown / big / a / dog → ___a big brown dog
old / lovely / an / house → ___a lovely old house
young / noisy / a / puppy → ___a noisy young puppy
wooden / small / a / table → ___a small wooden table
