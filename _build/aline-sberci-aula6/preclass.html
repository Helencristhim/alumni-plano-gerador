Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Order -- to ask for food or drink that you want to buy
"I would like to order a coffee." (Pedir)
Refill -- a second cup of the same drink, often free
"The first refill is free here." (Reposição)
Takeaway -- food or drink you take with you, not eat inside
"I want a coffee for takeaway, please." (Para viagem)
Counter -- the long table where you order and pay
"Please pay at the counter." (Balcão)
Receipt -- the paper that shows what you bought and paid
"Can I have the receipt, please?" (Recibo)
Barista -- the person who makes and serves coffee
"The barista is very friendly." (Barista)
Decaf -- coffee that has no caffeine in it
"I drink decaf in the evening." (Descafeinado)
Tip -- extra money you leave for good service
"I always leave a tip." (Gorjeta)
Snack -- a small amount of food you eat between meals
"A muffin is a nice snack." (Lanche)
Mug -- a big cup with a handle, for hot drinks
"She drinks coffee from a large mug." (Caneca)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Could I have + Countable / Uncountable

GRAMMAR

Usado para pedir educadamente e para escolher entre a, some, any, how much e how many. / Used to ask politely and to choose between a, some, any, how much and how many.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
a / an + countableUma coisa que dá para contar / One countable thingCould I have a muffin?
some + uncountablePedidos e frases afirmativas / Requests and positivesCould I have some milk?
any + question / negativePerguntas e negativas / Questions and negativesIs there any sugar?
how much / how manyQuantidade: incontável / contável / QuantityHow much sugar? How many cups?

Key point / Ponto-chave: palavras incontáveis (milk, coffee, sugar, water) nunca usam a ou an. Use some ou a little. Contáveis (muffin, cup, sandwich) usam a, an ou um número.
