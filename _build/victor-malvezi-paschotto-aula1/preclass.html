Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Growth -- an increase in size, value, or importance over time
"We have achieved consistent growth in the Latin American market."
Strategy -- a plan of action designed to achieve a long-term goal
"Our expansion strategy focuses on emerging markets."
Target -- a specific result or goal that you aim to achieve
"We exceeded our revenue target by fifteen percent last quarter."
Stakeholder -- a person or group with an interest in an organization
"All stakeholders were informed about the new compliance policy."
KPI -- Key Performance Indicator: a measurable value showing progress
"Customer retention is one of our most important KPIs."
Deliverable -- a tangible output that must be produced to complete a project
"The main deliverable for this sprint is the market analysis report."
Oversee -- to watch over and direct work, a project, or people
"I oversee growth initiatives across five regional markets."
Carry out -- to perform, execute, or complete a task or plan
"The team carried out the pilot program in three cities."
Make headway -- to make progress, especially when it is difficult
"We have made headway on the new security platform despite challenges."
Performance -- how well a person, team, or company does their work
"The team's performance this quarter has been outstanding."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Marcus joined a multinational security company as a Growth Manager two years ago. Since then, he has overseen multiple expansion projects and has made headway on entering new markets. His role involves developing strategies to meet ambitious targets and working closely with stakeholders across the region. Last quarter, his team exceeded the revenue target by ten percent, which was a major achievement. "I have carried out three pilot programs this year," he explains. "The key deliverables are always tied to clear KPIs, and that keeps our performance on track." Before this role, Marcus worked at a consulting firm where he gained experience in corporate strategy.

1. Why does Marcus say "I have carried out three pilot programs this year" using the present perfect?
A Because the pilot programs happened at a specific time last month.
B Because the experience started in the past and the year is still ongoing.
C Because he is carrying out a program right now.
2. Why is "Last quarter, his team exceeded the target" in the simple past?
A Because "last quarter" is a finished, specific period in the past.
B Because the team is still exceeding the target now.
C Because simple past is used for future plans.
3. Which sentence shows an action that started in the past and continues to the present?
A "Marcus joined the company two years ago."
B "Before this role, Marcus worked at a consulting firm."
C "He has overseen multiple expansion projects since then."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Simple Past

GRAMMAR

When to use each tense in professional contexts.

TenseUseExample
Simple PastCompleted action at a specific time in the pastI joined Brink's in 2022.
Signal words (Past)yesterday, last week/month/year, in 2022, agoWe exceeded the target last quarter.
Present PerfectExperience connected to the present, ongoing resultI have overseen multiple projects.
Signal words (Perfect)since, for, already, recently, so far, this yearWe have made headway since January.
Key differenceSimple past = WHEN it happened matters. Present perfect = the RESULT or EXPERIENCE matters, not when.
