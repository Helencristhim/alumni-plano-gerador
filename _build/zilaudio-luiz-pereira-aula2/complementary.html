Complementary Activities

Three things to do between Lesson 2 and Lesson 3. They are all about the numbers of a farm — the size, the crop, the weather, the machines. Mark each one as done when you finish it.

News — read and listen
Short Corn Is Growing Stronger in US Midwest — VOA Learning English

A real story about a corn farmer in Iowa — the same farm you read about in class. It talks about hectares, the harvest, the weather and new machinery, in slow, clear English. There is an audio player at the top of the page and the full text below it.

Tip: play the audio FIRST, without reading. Write down three numbers you hear (hectares, tons, years). Only then read the text and check. Do not translate — catch the general idea, not every word.

YouTube
English Conversation and Listening Practice from the Farm — Bob the Canadian

Bob is an English teacher and a farmer. He walks around his farm and talks about the machines, the season and the work, in slow, clear English, with subtitles on the screen. This is the vocabulary of Lesson 2 — machinery, season, storage — spoken by a real farmer, on real land.

Tip: watch it once with the subtitles ON. Then watch it again with the subtitles OFF. Every time Bob says a number, pause and say it out loud yourself.

Video lesson
Learn English: MUCH & MANY — engVid

The exact grammar of Lesson 2: when to say how much and when to say how many. The teacher speaks slowly and writes everything on the board. Our lesson gave you the farm examples; this video shows you the rule behind them, so you never mix them up.

Tip: after the video, take the quiz on the same page. Then make five questions about your own farm — three with "how many", two with "how much" — and say them out loud.
