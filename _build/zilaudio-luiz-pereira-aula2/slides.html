Chapter 4

The Code

Grammar Discovery

How Much or How Many?

How many trucks do you use?
How much rain do you get?
The farm has two thousand hectares.
It produces fifty tons per hectare.
Ask withFor things you can...Example
How manyCOUNT: trucks, silos, acres, tonsHow many trucks?
How muchNOT count: rain, soybean, storage, machineryHow much rain?
has / producesthe farm / it — third person takes -sThe farm has ten silos.

Can you count it? Use how many. Is it one big mass? Use how much. And with the farm or it, the verb takes -s.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"How much trucks do you have?"
"How many trucks do you have?"
"The farm have two silos."
"The farm has two silos."

Count it → how many. One big mass → how much. And the farm / it always takes has, produces, grows — with the -s.
