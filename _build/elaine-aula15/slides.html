How to Ask

Ask for sizes, colors, and to try things on

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Do you have this in a bigger size?"

"Can I try it on?"

"Is it on sale?"

"How much is it?"

StructureUseExample

Use "Do you have...?" to ask if the store has a size or color.

At the Store

Buying a Shirt

L
"Hi! Can I help you find anything?"
E
"Yes, please. Do you have this shirt in a bigger size?"
L
"Let me check. Yes, we have it in medium and large."
E
"Do you have it in blue?"
L
"Yes, here is the blue one. Would you like to try it on?"
E
"Yes. Where is the fitting room?"
L
"The fitting room is at the back, on the right."
E
"Thank you. It fits well! How much is it?"
L
"It is forty dollars, but today it is on sale: thirty dollars."
E
"Great, a discount! Is there a cheaper one?"
L
"This one is the best price. It is a good deal."
E
"Perfect. I will take it. Thank you!"
