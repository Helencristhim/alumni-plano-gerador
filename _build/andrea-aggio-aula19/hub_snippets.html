Handling High-Stakes Conversations

How to stay composed under pressure, de-escalate, and hold your ground -- using mixed conditionals. Key words: high-stakes, composure, to de-escalate, pushback, to stand your ground, non-negotiable, to concede, ultimatum, deadlock, to walk away, to defuse, to salvage. Structure: past 'if' + present result (had done ... would be); present 'if' + past result (were/did ... would have done).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

High-stakes -- with a lot to win or lose; where the outcome really matters (de alto risco, com muito em jogo)
"This is a high-stakes call -- the whole account depends on it."
Composure -- calmness and self-control under pressure (compostura, autocontrole)
"She kept her composure even when he raised his voice."
To de-escalate -- to make a tense situation calmer (desescalar, acalmar a tensao)
"I tried to de-escalate before anyone said something they'd regret."
Pushback -- strong resistance or opposition to a plan or idea (resistencia, oposicao)
"We got serious pushback on the new cancellation terms."
To stand your ground -- to refuse to change your position under pressure (manter sua posicao)
"I stood my ground on the deposit -- it protects us."
Non-negotiable -- that cannot be changed or discussed (inegociavel)
"The insurance clause is non-negotiable for us."
To concede -- to admit a point or give something up in an argument (ceder, conceder um ponto)
"I conceded on the dates so I could hold firm on price."
Ultimatum -- a final demand with a threat if it is refused (ultimato)
"He gave me an ultimatum: sign today or lose the rate."
Deadlock -- a situation where no agreement is possible (impasse)
"We reached a deadlock and agreed to sleep on it."
To walk away -- to leave a deal rather than accept bad terms (abandonar a negociacao, desistir)
"If they won't move, I'm prepared to walk away."
To defuse -- to remove the tension or danger from a situation (neutralizar a tensao)
"A calm question can defuse a heated moment."
To salvage -- to save something from a difficult or failing situation (salvar, recuperar)
"We salvaged the relationship even though the deal fell through."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A supplier, Klaus, gives Andrea an ultimatum: match the rate today or move the group. Instead of fighting, she keeps her composure and works to de-escalate. 'If we had locked in last season's rate,' she reflects, 'we wouldn't be at this deadlock now.' She meets the pushback calmly: she can concede on payment terms, but the cancellation protection is non-negotiable -- 'if I dropped it, I'd be exposing both of us.' She knows her limit and is prepared to walk away: 'If I were the kind of supplier who caved, you wouldn't have trusted us for six years.' Even if the deal falls through, she wants to salvage the relationship.

1. In 'If we had locked in the rate, we wouldn't be stuck now', the result clause is:
A present -- 'would + base verb' (wouldn't be).
B past -- 'would have + participle'.
C future -- 'will be'.
2. Which sentence mixes a present fact with a past result?
A If I were more assertive, I would have pushed back.
B If I had prepared, I would be calmer now.
C If I prepare, I will be calmer.
3. Which sentence is correct for a past cause and a present result?
A If I had set the boundary, I would be calmer now.
B If I had set the boundary, I would have been calmer now.
C If I set the boundary, I would be calmer now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Mixed Conditionals

GRAMMAR

Como juntar dois tempos numa so frase -- passado que afeta o presente, e presente que explica o passado (em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
Past → presentcausa no passado, resultado agora. past cause, present result.If I had prepared, I would be calmer now.
Present → pastfato permanente, resultado no passado. present fact, past result.If I were braver, I would have said no.
As formas / The formspast perfect + would + base verb; OU past simple/were + would have + participio.had done ... would be / were ... would have done.
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleolhe cada clausula sozinha e de a ela o tempo a que pertence. read each clause on its own.nao force tudo num so tempo.
