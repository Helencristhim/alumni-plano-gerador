New finance words: forecast, expenses, overhead, profit, allocate, surplus, projection, cash flow. PT: fale de dinheiro com seguranca em uma chamada de review -- orcamento, previsao e lucro -- e use o terceiro condicional para refletir sobre uma decisao do passado.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The precise language of a financial review. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario preciso de uma reuniao financeira.

Forecast -- previsao / projecao
a prediction of future numbers, like sales or growth, based on current data
"The forecast shows fifteen percent growth next quarter."
Expenses -- despesas / custos
the costs a business pays out, such as shipping, materials or salaries
"Shipping expenses went up again this quarter."
Overhead -- custos fixos / despesas gerais
the ongoing fixed costs of running a business, like rent, admin and utilities
"Our overhead stays the same whether we sell ten pieces or a hundred."
Profit -- lucro
the money left over after all costs and expenses are paid
"Higher shipping costs cut into our profit on every order."
Allocate -- alocar / destinar
to set aside a part of the budget for a specific purpose
"Let's allocate more to logistics to protect the margin."
Surplus -- excedente / sobra
an extra amount of money left over after all costs are covered
"The collection came in under budget, so we have a surplus to reinvest."
Projection -- projecao (de numeros futuros)
a calculated estimate of future figures, shown over time
"The projection on page two covers the next three quarters."
Cash flow -- fluxo de caixa
the movement of money into and out of a business over time
"Profit looks good, but the cash flow is tight until clients pay."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read how Milton reviews the quarter, then answer. Notice the third conditional. / Leia como o Milton revisa o trimestre e responda; observe o terceiro condicional.

On the quarterly review, Milton opened with good news: the spring collection came in under budget, leaving a healthy surplus, and the forecast pointed to fifteen percent growth. Then he flagged a concern. The overhead from shipping and customs expenses was climbing and eating into the profit on every order. Looking back, he was honest: "If we had allocated more to logistics on the winter orders, we would have kept the margin." He admitted that if he had read the early projection more carefully, he would have seen the problem sooner. His plan was simple: allocate the surplus to shipping up front and watch the cash flow until clients paid. "If we protect the margin now," he said, "next quarter's profit will hold."

1. "If we had allocated more, we would have kept the margin." What time is this about?
A The past -- an imagined result that did not happen (third conditional).
B A real plan for the future.
C Something happening right now.
2. What is the correct structure of the third conditional?
A If + present, will + verb.
B If + had + past participle, would have + past participle.
C If + would have, would have.
3. Why does Milton mention overhead?
A Because it is falling fast.
B Because it is climbing and eating into the profit on every order.
C Because it has nothing to do with profit.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Third Conditional

GRAMMAR

Use the third conditional to talk about an imagined past -- a decision that is now impossible to change.

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeif + had + participle, would have + participleIf we had allocated more, we would have kept the margin.
Negativeif + had not + participle, would not have + participleIf we hadn't lost the margin, we wouldn't have cut staff.
Questionwhat would have happened if + had + participle?What would have happened if we had read the forecast sooner?
Key contrast: first conditional = real future (If we cut overhead, we will save). Third conditional = imagined past (If we had cut overhead, we would have saved). PT: o terceiro condicional fala de um PASSADO que NAO aconteceu e do resultado imaginado. Estrutura: se + tinha/houvesse + participio (had + participle), teria + participio (would have + participle). NUNCA use "would have" na parte do "if" -- so no resultado. E a lingua de refletir sobre o que voce teria feito diferente.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation. / Escolha a melhor resposta.

You want to report that costs stayed below plan. Which is best?
A "We spent too much again."
B "The collection came in under budget, so we have a surplus."
C "I don't know the numbers."
You reflect on a past mistake. Which third conditional is correct?
A "If we would have allocated more, we would have kept the margin."
B "If we had allocated more, we would have kept the margin."
C "If we allocated more, we will keep the margin."
Profit looks fine but money is tight until clients pay. How do you say it?
A "The forecast is wrong."
B "Profit looks good, but the cash flow is tight until clients pay."
C "We have no profit at all."

A "Let's ignore the overhead."
