Lesson 17 -- Financial Discussions: Budgets, Forecasts & the Third Conditional

Series / Film
Shark Tank -- any pitch and Q&A segment

Watch founders present budgets, forecasts, margins and cash flow under pressure -- and hear investors ask exactly the financial questions Karen would. A live model of the vocabulary from this lesson.

Tip: each time a number comes up, name it out loud -- is it the forecast, the overhead, the margin or the profit?

Podcast
Business English Pod -- any episode on "finance", "budgets" or "financial reviews"

Practical English for talking money: presenting a budget, reading a forecast, explaining cash flow and discussing profit margins. The host models the exact phrases you'd use on a quarterly review call.

Tip: note one phrase for flagging a concern, then say it out loud as if Karen were on the call.

YouTube
BBC Learning English / English with Lucy -- "the third conditional"

A short, clear lesson on the third conditional: if + past perfect, would have + past participle. Native speakers model how to talk about an imagined past -- exactly what you need to reflect on a financial decision.

Tip: after watching, record three third-conditional sentences about real decisions in your business this year.
