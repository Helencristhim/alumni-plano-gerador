Chapter 3

The Third Conditional

Looking back at a decision you can't change

Discovery

What's the Pattern?

Read these three lines. What do the orange words have in common?

"If we had allocated more to shipping, we would have protected the margin."

"If I had read the forecast sooner, I would have seen the problem coming."

"If the overhead had been lower, the profit would have looked very different."

If + past perfect, ... would have + past participle

We use the third conditional to talk about a past situation that did not happen — and to imagine its different result. It is the language of looking back: regret, lessons learned, and "what if."

The Structure

First vs Third

ConditionalTimeStructureExample
Firstreal futureif + present, will + verbIf we cut overhead, we will save money.
Thirdimagined pastif + had + participle, would have + participleIf we had cut overhead, we would have saved money.

The third conditional is for things that are now impossible to change — the decision is already made and the quarter is already closed.

Watch Out

Common Mistake

If we would have allocated more, we would have saved money.
If we had allocated more, we would have saved money.

In the third conditional, the if part uses had + past participle — never "would have." Save would have for the result only.

Practice

Complete the Sentence

"If I _____ (read) the forecast, I would have seen the problem." → had read
"If we had cut the overhead, the profit _____ (be) higher." → would have been
"If we _____ (allocate) more to shipping, we would not have lost margin." → had allocated
"If the cash flow had been stronger, we _____ (reinvest) the surplus sooner." → would have reinvested
Chapter 4

The Review

Milton and Karen on the quarterly call

Dialogue

Milton & Karen

K
Thanks for joining the review, Milton. Walk me through the quarter — how did the spring collection do?
M
Strong, actually. It came in under budget, so we have a surplus, and the forecast for next quarter looks good — around fifteen percent growth.
K
That's great. And the overhead? Last time the shipping expenses were a worry.
M
Still high — it's eating into the profit. Honestly, if we had allocated more to logistics on the winter orders, we would have kept the margin.
K
Agreed. So what's the plan? Look at the cash flow on the projection before you answer.
M
I'd allocate the surplus to shipping up front. If we protect the margin now, next quarter's profit will hold.

Spot the Error

Fix the Line

"If we would have allocated more, we would have saved money."
"If we had allocated more, we would have saved money." (if-part = had + participle)
"If I read the forecast, I would have seen the problem."
"If I had read the forecast, I would have seen the problem." (past perfect, not present)
"The overhead is eating into our profits margin."
"The overhead is eating into our profit margin." (singular: profit margin)
"If the cash flow had been stronger, we will reinvest sooner."
"If the cash flow had been stronger, we would have reinvested sooner." (result = would have + participle)
