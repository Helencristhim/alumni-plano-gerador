Defining What Matters -- Investment Narratives

Definir, e não listar — e controlar a vírgula que muda o FATO. Key words: thesis, moat, traction, unit economics, churn, scalability, addressable market, differentiator, track record, to underpin, value creation, exit. Structures: relative clauses — defining (sem vírgula: diz QUAL) vs non-defining (com vírgula: comenta TODAS), that/which/who, whose, relativas reduzidas (the risks driving churn) e preposição no fim.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Repare que cada exemplo JÁ contém uma relative clause — é assim que se define sem listar.

Thesis -- the core argument for why an investment will work (tese de investimento)
"The thesis that underpins this round is very simple."
Moat -- the durable advantage your competitors cannot copy (barreira competitiva, moat)
"A moat that a competitor can buy is not a moat."
Traction -- real evidence that customers actually want it (tração)
"Traction is the only number investors believe without checking."
Unit economics -- whether a single customer makes you money (economia unitária)
"The unit economics, which improved all year, are now positive."
Churn -- the rate at which customers leave you (churn, taxa de cancelamento)
"The churn that worried us in March has halved."
Scalability -- whether growth gets cheaper as you get bigger (escalabilidade)
"Scalability is what separates a business from a job."
Addressable market -- the customers you could realistically reach (mercado endereçável)
"The addressable market, which nobody has measured properly, is the weak part."
Differentiator -- the one thing that makes a customer choose you (diferencial)
"Our differentiator is the licence that took four years to win."
Track record -- the proof of what you have actually done before (histórico comprovado)
"Investors back a track record, not a forecast."
To underpin -- to be the foundation that something rests on (sustentar, embasar)
"The assumption that underpins the model is the one to attack first."
Value creation -- the real increase in worth that you produce (geração de valor)
"Value creation is not the same as growth, and the board confuses them."
Exit -- how the investors eventually get their money out (saída, exit)
"Nobody funds a company whose exit nobody can describe."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Ingrid backed the company in 2022 on a thesis that turned out to be half right. This time Felipe led with the argument, not the features. The differentiator, he said, is a regulatory licence that took four years to win and that a competitor cannot simply buy — and a moat that a rival can buy is not a moat at all. The churn that had worried her in March has halved. The unit economics, which improved every quarter this year, are now positive. Notice the difference. The churn that worried her has no commas, because it tells you WHICH churn. The unit economics, which improved, has commas, because there is only one set of unit economics and the clause is merely extra. Then Felipe did the thing that investors remember. He named his own weakest number: the addressable market, which nobody has measured properly. The assumption that underpins the whole model is a market size he cannot prove. Nobody funds a company whose exit nobody can describe — and nobody trusts a CFO who hides the number the data room will find anyway.

1. "The churn that worried her in March" NÃO tem vírgula. Por quê?
A Porque a clause DEFINE de qual churn estamos falando — ela é essencial, e sem ela não se sabe qual.
B Porque "churn" é uma palavra curta e não precisa de vírgula.
C Porque toda relative clause com that pode ou não levar vírgula.
2. "The unit economics, which improved every quarter, are positive." O que as vírgulas fazem aqui?
A Elas marcam informação EXTRA sobre a única economia unitária que existe — se você remover a clause, o fato sobrevive.
B Elas indicam que existem várias economias unitárias e essa é uma delas.
C Elas são obrigatórias sempre que se usa which.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A Our lead investor, that has backed us since 2022, is in.
B Our lead investor, who has backed us since 2022, is in.
C Our lead investor which has backed us since 2022 is in.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses (a vírgula que muda o fato)

GRAMMAR

Como definir sem listar — e por que, para um CFO, a vírgula não é estilo: é risco (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Defining (SEM vírgula)Diz QUAL. Essencial — tire a clause e você não sabe mais de quem se fala. Tells you WHICH one.The analysts who left took the model. (only those)
Non-defining (COM vírgula)Informação EXTRA sobre TODOS. Tire a clause e o fato sobrevive. Extra information.The analysts, who left, took the model. (all of them)
that / which / whothat SÓ em defining. which e who nas duas. NUNCA that depois de vírgula.the metric that matters / the metric, which matters,...
Relativa reduzidaCorte that is / which are. Duas palavras a menos, e soa muito mais nativo.the risks driving churn
whosePosse — para pessoas E para empresas.a company whose exit nobody can describe
Preposição no fimInglês natural e correto. Não force from which fora de contrato.the fund we raised from

O teste que nunca falha: tire a clause da frase. Se você ainda sabe QUAL deles está sendo mencionado, a clause era extra → ponha as vírgulas. Se você NÃO sabe mais, ela era essencial → sem vírgulas. E por que isso importa para você: "The analysts who left took the model" significa que ALGUNS saíram. "The analysts, who left, took the model" significa que TODOS saíram. Mesmas palavras, fatos diferentes — e num contrato isso é dinheiro.

Stage 2: Put the Investment Pitch in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de um pitch de investimento na ordem correta. Começar pelas features, e não pela tese, é o erro que faz o investidor parar de ouvir.

?Name the moat — and say precisely why a competitor cannot buy it.
?Name your OWN weakest number, before they find it in the data room.
?Define the company in one sentence. An argument, never a list.
?Show the unit economics and what changed this year.
?Give the traction: the number they will believe without checking.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. Diga, em cada uma, se a clause é defining ou non-defining — e por quê.

Our differentiator is a licence that took four years to win and that a competitor cannot buy.
Nosso diferencial é uma licença que levou quatro anos para ser obtida e que um concorrente não pode comprar.
The churn that worried you in March has halved.
O churn que preocupou você em março caiu pela metade.
The unit economics, which improved every quarter, are now positive.
A economia unitária, que melhorou a cada trimestre, agora é positiva.
The assumption that underpins the model is the one I cannot prove.
A premissa que sustenta o modelo é a que eu não consigo comprovar.
I would rather tell you now than have you find it in the data room.
Prefiro te contar agora a deixar você descobrir na data room.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

Only two of your six analysts left. You want to say THOSE TWO took the model:
A "The analysts, who left, took the model with them."
B "The analysts who left took the model with them."
C "The analysts, that left, took the model with them."
You want to add extra information about your only lead investor:
A "Our lead investor, that has backed us since 2022, is in."
B "Our lead investor, who has backed us since 2022, is in."
C "Our lead investor who has backed us since 2022 is in."
An investor asks for your thesis. The best answer is:
A "We have great technology, a strong team, good margins and happy customers."
B "Our differentiator is a licence that took four years to win and that a competitor cannot buy."
C "Our thesis is that we will grow a lot next year."
You want to sound native and cut two words from "the risks that are driving churn":
A "the risks which driving churn"
B "the risks driving churn"
C "the risks that driving churn"
The investor is about to find your weakest number in the data room. The senior move is:
A Say nothing — she might not notice it.
B "The addressable market, which nobody has measured properly, is the weak part. I would rather tell you now."
C Defend the number and hope the conversation moves on.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

An investor who has already backed you once asks: "Give me the new thesis, in one sentence." Answer for 2-3 minutes. Define the company — do not list features. Use at least two defining clauses (no commas) and one non-defining clause (commas), and reduce one relative clause. Name the moat and why it cannot be bought. Then name your own weakest number before she does, and say what you will do about it.
