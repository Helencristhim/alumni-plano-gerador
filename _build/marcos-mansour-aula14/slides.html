Chapter 3

Giving Directions

Go straight / Turn left / Take / Cross

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Go straight for two blocks."

"Turn left at the traffic light."

"Take the second street on the right."

"Cross the road at the crosswalk."

VerbFormExample
Gobase verb, no subjectGo straight.
Turn+ left / rightTurn left.
Take+ the ... streetTake the second street.
ImperativeNo "I", no "you", no "to". Just the base verb: Go, Turn, Take, Cross.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You go straight two blocks."

✓ RIGHT

"Go straight for two blocks."

✗ WRONG

"Turn to left at the light."

✓ RIGHT

"Turn left at the light." (no "to")

✗ WRONG

"To cross the road here."

✓ RIGHT

"Cross the road here." (base verb)

The imperative uses the base verb only: Go, Turn, Take, Cross. No subject, no "to".

Chapter 4

Finding the Way

Asking a local for directions

Dialogue

How Do I Get There?

M
Excuse me, how do I get to the conference center from here? Is it far?
L
No, it is not far, about a ten minute walk. Go straight down this street for two blocks.
M
Two blocks, straight. And then?
L
At the second traffic light, turn left onto K Street. Walk past the coffee shop.
M
So I turn left at the second light, and walk past the coffee shop. Where is the building exactly?
L
It is the tall glass building on the corner, right across from the park, next to the subway station.
M
Across from the park, next to the subway station. That is very clear. Thank you so much.
L
You are welcome. If you reach the museum, you have gone too far. You cannot miss it.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How far is the conference center?
Not far, about a ten minute walk.
2. What does the local say to do at the second traffic light?
Turn left onto K Street and walk past the coffee shop.
3. Where exactly is the building?
On the corner, across from the park, next to the subway station.
