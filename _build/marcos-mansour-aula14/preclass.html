Structures: imperatives for directions (Go straight / Turn left / Take / Cross) and How do I get to...? / Where is...? / Is it far?

Stage 14.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Corner -- esquina (where two streets meet)
"The bank is on the corner of K Street."
Block -- quarteirão (the area between two streets)
"Go straight for two blocks."
Sidewalk -- calçada (the path beside the road)
"Stay on the sidewalk and keep walking."
Crosswalk -- faixa de pedestres (where you cross safely)
"Cross the road at the crosswalk."
Avenue -- avenida (a wide, important street)
"Walk down this avenue for three blocks."
Traffic light -- semáforo (the signal that controls cars)
"At the second traffic light, turn left."
Landmark -- ponto de referência (a famous building to find your way)
"The museum is a good landmark to follow."
Subway station -- estação de metrô (where you take the underground train)
"The hotel is next to the subway station."
Intersection -- cruzamento (where two roads cross)
"Turn right at the next intersection."
Across from -- em frente a (on the opposite side of)
"The hotel is across from the park."

Stage 14.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After dinner, Marcos leaves the restaurant and asks a local for directions. "Excuse me, how do I get to the conference center? Is it far?" The woman answers using the imperative: "Go straight for two blocks. At the second traffic light, turn left. Walk past the coffee shop. The building is on the corner, across from the park, next to the subway station." Notice the directions: each one starts with a base verb (Go, Turn, Walk, Cross), with no subject and no "to". Marcos repeats the steps to confirm and says: "Thank you so much."

1. Que pergunta Marcos usa para pedir o caminho?
A "Where you go?"
B "How do I get to the conference center? Is it far?"
C "Conference center please."
2. Como começam as direções no texto?
A Com "You" + verbo.
B Com o verbo base (Go, Turn, Walk), sem sujeito.
C Com "To" + verbo.
3. Which is the correct imperative for directions?
A "You turn to left."
B "Turn left at the traffic light."
C "To turning left."

Stage 14.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives for Directions

GRAMMAR

Como dar direções com o imperativo (explicação em inglês e português).

VerbForm / FormaExample
Goverbo base, sem sujeitoGo straight for two blocks.
Turn+ left / right (sem "to")Turn left at the light.
Take / Cross+ o objetoCross the road at the crosswalk.
DicaImperativo = só o verbo base. Sem "I", sem "you", sem "to". Para perguntar: How do I get to...? / Where is...? / Is it far?
