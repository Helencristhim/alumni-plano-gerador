"The phone is ringing." (Tocar / chamar)

"Can I leave a message?" (Recado / mensagem)
Hold on -- to wait for a moment
"Please hold on a moment." (Aguardar / esperar)
Voicemail -- a recorded message when nobody answers
"I left a voicemail." (Caixa postal / correio de voz)
Busy -- not free to talk right now
"The manager is busy right now." (Ocupado)
Speak to -- to talk with a person on the phone
"Can I speak to the manager?" (Falar com)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Elaine wants to call the front desk. She picks up the phone and the line starts to ring. A person answers. Elaine says, Hello, this is Elaine. Then she asks, Can I speak to the manager? The manager is busy right now. So Elaine asks, Could you take a message? The person says, "Please hold on one moment." At the end, Elaine leaves her phone number and says goodbye.

1. Como Elaine se identifica no telefone? / How does Elaine identify herself on the phone?
A I am Elaine me.
B Hello, this is Elaine.
C Here Elaine speaking me.
2. Como Elaine pede para falar com o gerente? / How does Elaine ask to talk to the manager?
A Can I speak to the manager?
B I speak the manager now?
C Manager talk me?
3. O gerente esta ocupado. O que Elaine faz? / The manager is busy. What does Elaine do?
A She asks, "Could you take a message?"
B She hangs up and says nothing.
C She shouts on the phone.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Phone Phrases

GRAMMAR

Falar de forma educada no telefone. / Speaking politely on the phone.

Phrase / FraseUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
This is...Dizer quem voce e / Say who you areHello, this is Elaine.
Can I speak to...?Pedir uma pessoa / Ask for a personCan I speak to the manager?
Could you...?Pedido educado / Polite requestCould you take a message?

Lembre: no telefone diga This is (nao "I am") para se identificar. Use Can I e Could you para pedidos educados. / On the phone, say "This is..." (not "I am...") to identify yourself. Use "Can I" and "Could you" for polite requests.
