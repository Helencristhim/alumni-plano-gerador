Lesson 11 -- Holding the Room

Talk
Nancy Duarte -- The Secret Structure of Great Talks (TED)

Duarte takes apart the shape that makes an audience follow a presenter. Connection to Lesson 11: this is signposting made visible -- where the map is, why it matters, and how the room always knows where it is.

Tip: watch once for the idea, then again and write down every signpost phrase she uses to move the audience.

Podcast
Think Fast, Talk Smart -- Matt Abrahams (Stanford GSB)

The whole method for speaking under pressure -- exactly the skill of fielding a hard question from a board. Connection to Lesson 11: how to park a question, circle back, and answer without panicking.

Tip: after watching, practise parking one question out loud: "That is the right question -- let me circle back to it in two minutes."

YouTube
Julian Treasure -- How to Speak So That People Want to Listen (TED)

On pace, weight and the way you deliver a hard line -- the opposite of glossing over. Connection to Lesson 11: slow down on the weak number instead of speeding up, and let the room feel that you own it.

Tip: pick the number you least want to present and say it out loud, slowly, with one clean caveat.
