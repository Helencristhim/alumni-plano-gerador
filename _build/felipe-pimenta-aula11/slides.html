Chapter 3: The Code

Signposting: The Language That Guides a Room

Phrases that carry no facts — and control everything

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Let me start by giving you the agenda."

That brings me to the headline number."

Let me circle back to that question in two minutes."

To sum up, three things matter tonight."

The orange phrases carry no facts. So tell me: what exactly do they do?

FunctionSignpostExample
Open / agendaLet me start by... · I'll cover three things"Let me start by giving you the agenda."
Move onThat brings me to... · Moving on to..."That brings me to the headline number."
Drill downLet me drill down into... · If we look more closely"Let me drill down into the churn."
DeferLet me circle back to... · I'll come back to that"Let me circle back to that in two minutes."
Flag a caveatI want to flag... · One caveat here"I want to flag one figure with a caveat."
ConcludeTo sum up... · The bottom line is..."To sum up, three things matter tonight."

A signpost adds no fact. It tells the room where they are — opening, moving, drilling down, deferring, or closing. Delete it and no number is lost; only the map is. That map is what keeps a board following you instead of hunting you.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"So, a lot happened this quarter, let me just walk you through the slides one by one."
"Let me start with the bottom line, then drill down into the three drivers."
"The margin dropped a bit, but anyway, moving on quickly to the next slide."
"I want to flag one caveat here: the margin fell two points, and here is exactly why."
"The number is, um, I am not sure, maybe some millions?"
"In ballpark terms it is around three million; I will confirm the exact figure and circle back."

The senior move is never to hide. Open with a map, flag the weak number instead of glossing over it, and give a ballpark with a promise rather than inventing a figure. A board forgives a miss. It does not forgive being managed.

Chapter 4: The Board

The Chair Who Interrupts

She asks the hardest question at the worst moment

Dialogue

Presenting the Miss

D
Felipe, you have twenty minutes and a nervous board. Where are you taking us?
F
Let me start with the agenda: the headline number, one caveat, and the fix. The bottom line is that we are two million below plan.
D
Two million. Is that the revenue line or the margin?
F
That is exactly the right question. Let me circle back to it in ninety seconds, when the slide in front of it makes the answer obvious.
D
Fine. But do not gloss over the margin. The last CFO did, and it cost him this room.
F
I will not. Let me drill down into it now, because I want to flag one figure with a proper caveat: the gross margin fell two points, and here is precisely why.
D
Good. And the number you owe me — revenue or margin?
F
Revenue. In ballpark terms the shortfall is one point four million of revenue and six hundred thousand of margin; I will confirm the exact split tonight. To sum up: below plan, one honest caveat, and a fix that is already working.
