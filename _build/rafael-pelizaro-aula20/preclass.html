Closing the Deal -- Confirming the Final Terms

The final step of a deal: finalize the terms, confirm every detail, get the sign-off and signature, agree the effective date, and plan the kick-off. New grammar: question tags (..., didn't we? / ..., won't you?). New vocabulary: finalize, sign-off, term sheet, signature, effective date, kick-off, proceed, formalize.

After Rafael and the vendor reached an agreement, the deal was not done yet. First he had to finalize the terms: he checked every line of the term sheet against what they had agreed. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Question Tags

GRAMMAR

Para confirmar um detalhe, você faz uma afirmação e acrescenta uma pequena pergunta no fim: o question tag. Regra do espelho: frase POSITIVA leva tag NEGATIVO ("We agreed, didn't we?"); frase NEGATIVA leva tag POSITIVO ("We didn't change it, did we?"). O tag repete o verbo auxiliar (do/did, will, be) e o pronome. / To confirm a detail, you make a statement and add a short question at the end: the question tag. Mirror rule: a positive statement takes a negative tag; a negative statement takes a positive tag. The tag repeats the auxiliary verb and the pronoun.

Statement / FraseTagExample / Exemplo
Positive (past)didn't + pronounWe agreed on the term, didn't we?
Positive (will)won't + pronounYou will sign it, won't you?
Positive (be)isn't / aren't + pronounThe date is the first, isn't it?
Negativepositive auxiliary + pronounWe didn't change it, did we?

Key point / Ponto-chave: o tag é um espelho. Frase positiva -> tag negativo ("..., didn't we?"); frase negativa -> tag positivo ("..., did we?"). O tag repete o auxiliar (did, won't, isn't), nunca o verbo principal.
