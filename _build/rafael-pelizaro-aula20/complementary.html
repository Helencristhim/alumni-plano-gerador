Lesson 20 -- Closing the Deal

Series
"Suits" -- the contract signing scenes

Watch scenes where the deal is finally closed: the lawyers confirm the final terms, prepare the documents for signature, and move to the formal sign-off. Notice how they double-check each detail before anyone signs.

Watch with English subtitles. Pick one signing scene and write three confirmations using question tags: "We agreed on this, didn't we?", "You will deliver on time, won't you?"

Podcast
Harvard Business Review -- "Closing the Deal and Getting to Signature"

A practical guide to the final stage of a deal: finalize the term sheet, confirm every detail, formalize the agreement, and plan the kick-off. Learn how the best managers turn a verbal yes into a signed contract.

Record a 90-second closing checklist for a real deal: the terms you would confirm with question tags, and the steps from sign-off to kick-off.

YouTube
"Question Tags in English -- Confirming and Checking Information"

A clear lesson on question tags: a positive statement takes a negative tag, and a negative statement takes a positive tag, repeating the auxiliary verb. The exact tool you use to confirm the final terms of a deal. Directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, write five confirmations for a real deal at work: "We finalized the budget, didn't we?", "You will send the sign-off, won't you?"
