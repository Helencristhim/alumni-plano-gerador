Chapter 3 -- Confirming with Tags

Grammar Discovery

Question tags: ..., didn't we? / ..., won't you?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange tags. The sentence is a statement, then a short question confirms it. What is the pattern?

We agreed on the twelve-month term, didn't we?

You will keep the uptime guarantee, won't you?

The effective date is the first, isn't it?

We didn't change the exit clause, did we?

The Rule

Confirming with Tags

StatementTagExample
Positive (past)didn't + pronounWe agreed on the term, didn't we?
Positive (will)won't + pronounYou will keep the uptime, won't you?
Positive (be)isn't / aren't + pronounThe date is the first, isn't it?
Negativepositive auxiliary + pronounWe didn't change it, did we?

Key idea: The tag is a mirror. A positive statement takes a negative tag; a negative statement takes a positive tag. The tag repeats the auxiliary verb (do/did, will, be) and the pronoun. Use it to confirm a detail and get a clear yes.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We agreed on the price, agreed we?"
"We agreed on the price, didn't we?"

The tag is a mirror: a positive statement takes a negative tag, and it repeats the auxiliary (did, not the main verb): "..., didn't we?"

Grammar Practice

Add the Tag

"We agreed on the final price, _____didn't we?" (positive past statement)
"You will sign the contract this week, _____won't you?" (positive will statement)
"The effective date is the first, _____isn't it?" (positive be statement)
"We didn't change the exit clause, _____did we?" (negative statement)
