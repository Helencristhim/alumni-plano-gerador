Words for Tone & Clarity

How formal or informal you sound
Register
The level of formality you use, chosen to fit the reader
"For a senior partner, raise the register and avoid slang."
Short, with no wasted words
Concise
Short and clear, saying only what is needed
"Keep the email concise: three sentences, not three paragraphs."
The greeting that opens a message

The opening greeting of an email or letter
"Match the salutation to the reader: Dear Mr. Costa, not Hi."

Sign-off
The polite phrase that closes a message before your name

Key idea: a communication audit is not a new skill — it is the whole of Block 4 in one checklist. You set the right register, you lead with the bottom line, you make the case persuasive, and you fit each channel. Same message, adjusted for every reader.

Watch Out

Common Mistake

Hey, send me the contract asap, thx.
Dear Mr. Costa, I was wondering if you could send the contract by Friday.

The same request needs a different register for a senior partner. Use a proper salutation, soften the request, and choose the channel that fits — chat shorthand belongs in chat, not in a formal email.

The Rule

Right reader, right channel

Match the register and the channel to the person reading it

Register: "Is the salutation and sign-off right for this reader?"

Structure: "Is the bottom line in the first line?"

Persuasion: "Does each point link to a clear benefit?"

Polish: "Is it concise, and did I proofread the attachment name?"
