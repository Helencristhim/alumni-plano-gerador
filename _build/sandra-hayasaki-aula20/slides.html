Chapter 3 -- The Frame

Beginning, Turning Point, Hope

A simple shape for any story -- using everything you learned

Listen and Notice

The Four Parts of a Story

Each sentence is one part of the frame. Notice the orange parts.

Beginning"At the beginning, I only knew a few words."

Turning point"The turning point was the day I decided to keep going."

Reflection (a wish)"I wish I had started earlier, but I have no regret."

Hope (a reason)"Although the road was long, this is just one more beginning."

The Frame

Tell Any Story

PartStructureExample
Beginningpast simple"At the beginning, I knew few words."
Turning pointthe day / when + past"The turning point was the day I decided to go on."
ReflectionI wish + past (had)"I wish I had started earlier."
Hopebecause / although + idea"Although it was long, I am ready for more."

Tip: This frame brings everything together: past simple for the story, I wish I had for a gentle regret, and because / although to connect your reason and your hope.

Common Mistake

Wishing About the Past

"I wish I have started earlier." / "Because it was long, so I am proud."
"I wish I had started earlier." / "Although it was long, I am proud."

For a regret about the past, use I wish I had + past participle, and use only one linker per sentence.
