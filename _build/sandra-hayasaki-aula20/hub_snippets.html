Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Journey -- a long path or process you go through, step by step, over time
"My English journey started with just a few words." (jornada / caminho)
Beginning -- the first part of something; where it starts
"At the beginning, I was too shy to speak." (começo / início)
Milestone -- an important point or achievement you reach along the way
"My first full conversation was a big milestone." (marco / conquista)
Chapter -- one part or section of a longer story or life
"This is a new chapter in my life." (capítulo)
Recall -- to bring a memory back to your mind; to remember
"I recall my first day of class very clearly." (relembrar / lembrar)
Grateful -- feeling thankful for something good that happened
"I am grateful for every teacher I have had." (grato / agradecido)
Memorable -- special and easy to remember for a long time
"That trip was the most memorable of my life." (memorável / inesquecível)
Storyteller -- a person who tells stories in an interesting, clear way
"Little by little, I became a real storyteller in English." (contador de histórias)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

I want to tell my English journey. At the beginning, I only knew a few words, and I was shy. Because I never gave up, things slowly changed. The turning point was my first full conversation; that was a memorable milestone, and I still recall it with pride. I wish I had started earlier, but I have no regret. Although the road was long, I am grateful for every step. Today I feel like a storyteller. This is not the end; it is one more chapter, and I am ready for it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Story Frame

GRAMMAR

Para contar uma história, use quatro partes. O começo e o ponto de virada usam o past simple (At the beginning I knew few words). A reflexão usa I wish I had + particípio para um arrependimento do passado (I wish I had started earlier). A esperança usa conectores como because e although (Although it was long, I am ready). / Use past simple for the beginning and turning point, "I wish I had" + past participle for a regret, and because / although to connect your reason and your hope.

PartStructureExample
Beginningpast simple"At the beginning, I knew few words."
Turning pointthe day / when + past"The turning point was the day I went on."
ReflectionI wish I had + participle"I wish I had started earlier."
Hopebecause / although + idea"Although it was long, I am ready."
