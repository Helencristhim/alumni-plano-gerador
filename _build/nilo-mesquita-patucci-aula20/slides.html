Chapter 3 -- A Different Past

Grammar Discovery

Imagine a past that did not happen

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

"If we had gone to court, we would have lost the deal."

"If I had signed the first draft, we would have lost the exclusivity."

"If we had not protected the goodwill, we would not have renewed."

"If we had accepted the first offer, we would have lost money."

Grammar Rule

The Third Conditional

If + past perfect (imaginary condition)would have + past participle (result)
If we had gone to court,we would have lost the deal.
If I had signed the first draft,we would have lost the exclusivity.
If we had not protected the goodwill,we would not have renewed.
If we had accepted the first offer,we would have lost money.

Key idea: the third conditional imagines a past that did not happen. Use if + past perfect (had + past participle) for the condition, and would have + past participle for the result. It is perfect for lessons learned: it proves the value of the right decision by imagining the wrong one. (Compare: 2nd conditional = imaginary present/future "would"; 3rd = imaginary past "would have".)

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we would have gone to court, we would have lost the deal."
"If we had gone to court, we would have lost the deal."

In the if part use the past perfect (had + participle). Would have goes only in the result.

About the Briefing

1. What grammar does Sofia recommend for lessons learned, and why?

The third conditional (if + past perfect, would have + past participle). It is perfect for lessons learned because it shows the value of the right decision by imagining the wrong one.

2. What is the difference between a turning point, a decisive moment, and a pivotal one?

A turning point is when everything changed direction; a decisive moment is the one that settles the matter; a pivotal moment is the one everything turned on. A strategic decision is part of a bigger plan.

3. What does Sofia say a partnership review and the whole block are really about?

Looking back at the whole journey, naming the turning points, and using the third conditional to show why each decision mattered -- carrying the lessons into every future contract.

1. What were the two turning points, and what would have happened if Nilo had chosen wrong?

The price fight (if he had accepted the first offer, they would have lost money) and the bad clause (if he had signed the first draft, they would have lost the exclusivity).

2. Why was renegotiating the late payment the pivotal decision?

Because if they had gone straight to court, they would have destroyed the goodwill and lost the partnership forever. Instead they sent a notice, offered a remedy, and renegotiated.

3. What is the biggest lesson of the whole negotiation block?

A great deal is not about winning once -- it is about the decisions that protect the relationship, season after season. If they had not built trust, none of it would have happened.
