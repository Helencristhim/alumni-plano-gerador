Overcome -- to finally beat a difficulty or problem

"Protecting the relationship was a strategic choice." (Estratégico)
Decisive -- the action or moment that settles the matter
"Finding the bad clause was the decisive moment." (Decisivo)
Pivotal -- the single moment everything turned on

"The outcome was a long-term partnership." (Resultado)

"Its legacy is a partnership that is still growing." (Legado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

To reflect on the lessons learned, Nilo imagines a past that did not happen. For this he uses the third conditional: if plus the past perfect (had + past participle), and would have plus the past participle. He says, "if I had signed the first draft, we would have lost the exclusivity." The bad clause was the decisive moment, the real turning point. The late payment was a setback; in hindsight, renegotiating instead of fighting was the pivotal decision: "if we had gone to court, we would have destroyed the goodwill." On reflection, the whole thing turned on one strategic idea: after you sign, you are two partners. The outcome was a landmark deal, and its legacy is a partnership still growing.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Third Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usado para imaginar um passado que NÃO aconteceu. / Used to imagine a past that did NOT happen.

If + past perfect (condição imaginária)would have + past participle (resultado)
If we had gone to court,we would have lost the deal.
If I had signed the first draft,we would have lost the exclusivity.
If we had not protected the goodwill,we would not have renewed.
If we had accepted the first offer,we would have lost money.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o terceiro conditional imagina um passado que não aconteceu. Use if + past perfect (had + partícipio); nunca would have depois de if. O resultado usa would have + partícipio. (Compare: 2º conditional = presente imaginário "would"; 3º = passado imaginário "would have".) / If + past perfect; would have only in the result.
