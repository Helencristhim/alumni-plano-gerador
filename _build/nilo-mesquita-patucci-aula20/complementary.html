Lesson 20 -- Negotiation VII: The Full Deal & Lessons Learned

Series
The Last Dance -- any episode reflecting on a whole career or season

People look back on a long journey, name the turning points and setbacks, and reflect on the decisions that defined it. Listen for how they imagine a different past ("if we had not...", "if they had...") and how they describe a landmark moment and its legacy.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one third conditional you hear ("if they had..., they would have...") and one reflection word from today (turning point, decisive, legacy).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "Lessons Learned from a Career-Defining Deal"

Leaders reflect on the deals that shaped them: the turning points, the setbacks, the pivotal decisions, and what they would have done differently. Exactly today's reflection vocabulary and the third conditional in real business stories.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to reflect ("in hindsight...", "if we had..."). Then record yourself reflecting on your biggest deal with three third conditionals.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The Third Conditional (if + past perfect, would have)"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to imagine a past that did not happen, why we use the past perfect after "if", and where "would have" goes in the sentence.

After watching, take five real decisions from your career and reflect on each with the third conditional ("if I had not..., I would have..."), then say them out loud.
