Chapter 3: The Code

Routine vs Right Now

I manage · I am leading

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I manage twelve premium accounts."

"I work with teams in India and the United States."

"I am leading the Bradesco rollout."

"We are deploying the new dashboard this week."

What is different about the green verbs?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I manage / she manages		Routine duties, permanent factsI manage twelve accounts.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Happening now, current projectsI am leading the rollout.
Signal wordsevery day, usually → simple · now, currently, this week → continuous

he / she / it adds -s in the simple: manages, works, leads.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am responsible to manage adoption."
"I am responsible for managing adoption."
"I working on the rollout now."
"I am working on the rollout now."

Use responsible for + verb-ing. And a current action is always am / is / are + verb-ing — never drop the verb "to be".
