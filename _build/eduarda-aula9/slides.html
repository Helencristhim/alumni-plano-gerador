Chapter 3 — The Code

Reporting Verbs

Look at the purple words. How do we report what other people said?

Daniel said that we will move now.
Anna suggested that we wait a week.
You mentioned that the deadline is close.
The team agreed to review in two weeks.
Reporting verbStructureExample
say / mention / explain+ (that) + clause"He said that we will move now."
tell+ someone + (that) + clause"She told me that the data is strong."
suggest / recommend+ (that) + clause"Anna suggested that we wait."
Grammar Rule

Reporting & Paraphrasing

FunctionUseful PhraseExample
Report (say)... said (that) ..."Daniel said that we will move now."
Report (tell)... told someone (that) ..."She told me that the data is strong."
ParaphraseIn other words, ... / Put simply, ..."In other words, we stay flexible."
Sum upTo sum up, ... / The key takeaway is ..."To sum up, we act now."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"He said me that the deal is closed."
"He told me that the deal is closed." / "He said that the deal is closed."

Use tell + a person ("tell me"). Use say + (that) without a person.

Speaking

Oral Drilling

1. Start your summary at the end of a meeting.
"Before we finish, let me sum up the main points."
2. Report what a colleague said.
"Daniel said that we will move now."
3. Paraphrase a decision more simply.
"In other words, we act now but stay flexible."
4. Give the key takeaway.
"The key takeaway is: act now and review in two weeks."
5. Check that everyone understood.
"Just to confirm, are we all on the same page?"
6. Assign the next steps.
"Each person has one action item, and we report back Friday."
