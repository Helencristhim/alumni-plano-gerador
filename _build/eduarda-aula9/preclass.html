Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key summary expressions for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Sum Up -- to give a short version of the main points
"Let me sum up the main points."
Recap -- to go over the main points again briefly
"Let me recap the decisions."
In Other Words -- used to say the same thing in a simpler way
"In other words, we act now but stay flexible."
Key Takeaway -- the most important point to remember
"The key takeaway is: act now and review later."
Follow -- to understand what someone means
"Do you follow me so far?"
On the Same Page -- to share the same understanding
"Are we all on the same page?"
Put Simply -- used to introduce a clear, simple version
"Put simply, we agreed to act now."
Action Item -- a task someone must do after the meeting
"Each person has one action item."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

At the end of the committee call, Eduarda closes the meeting. "Let me sum up the main points," she begins. She reports the decisions: "Daniel said that we will move now, and Anna suggested a short extension." A colleague does not follow, so Eduarda paraphrases: "In other words, we act now but stay flexible." She gives the key takeaway: act now, review in two weeks. Then she checks understanding: "Put simply, are we all on the same page?" Everyone agrees. Finally she assigns one action item to each person, and Clara offers to send a recap email.

1. What does Eduarda do at the end of the meeting?
A She starts a new topic.
B She sums up the main points and reports the decisions.
C She leaves without a summary.
2. Which reporting verb reports Daniel's words?
A "asked"
B "said that"
C "wanted"
3. Why does Eduarda paraphrase with "in other words"?
A Because a colleague did not follow her summary.
B Because she changed her mind.
C Because the meeting is starting.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Reporting verbs = report what someone said: reporting verb + (that) + clause. Atenção: tell precisa de pessoa ("tell me"), say não ("say that...").

VerbStructureExample
saysay + (that) + clause"He said that the deal is closed."
telltell + someone + (that) + clause"She told me that the data is strong."
suggestsuggest + (that) + clause"Anna suggested that we wait."

Common Mistake:

"He said me that the deal is closed."

"He told me that the deal is closed." / "He said that the deal is closed."

"tell" leva pessoa (tell me); "say" não (say that).
