Lesson 12 -- What Are You Wearing?

Series
Extra English -- Episode 9: "A Day at the Clothes Store"

A simple comedy made for English learners. In this episode the characters go shopping for clothes -- a shirt, a dress, shoes, and a jacket -- in slow, clear English. Perfect for A1 listening about what people wear.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every clothing word you hear (shirt, dress, shoes, jacket, pants, hat). Then say out loud: "I am wearing a blue shirt" and "She has a red dress".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Talking About Clothes"

A short, friendly lesson on clothes: how to say "I am wearing a blue shirt", "She has a red dress", and how to put the color before the clothing word. The hosts explain adjective order slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then describe what you are wearing today in three sentences, always with the color before the clothing word.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Clothes Vocabulary + Adjective Order"

A clear video that teaches clothes vocabulary (shirt, dress, shoes, jacket, pants, hat) and how to put the adjective before the noun, like a blue shirt, with pictures and simple sentences.

After watching, record a 60-second description of your favorite outfit: what you wear and the colors. Use at least three new words with the color before the clothing word.
