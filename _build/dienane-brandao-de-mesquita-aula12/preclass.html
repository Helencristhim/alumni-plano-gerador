Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dienane gets dressed in the morning. Today she wears a blue shirt and black pants. On her feet, she wears white shoes. It is a little cold, so she also takes a green jacket. She does not wear a hat today. For work, she always wears a nice shirt and dark pants. For a party, she wears a red dress. In English, the color comes before the clothing word: a blue shirt, not a shirt blue.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adjective + Noun

GRAMMAR

Em inglês, o adjetivo (cor, tamanho) vem ANTES do substantivo. / In English, the adjective (color, size) comes BEFORE the noun.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
um adjetivocolor + nouna blue shirt
dois adjetivossize/opinion + color + nouna nice red dress
com wearwear + adjective + nounI wear black shoes.
pluralo adjetivo não leva stwo white shoes

Key point / Ponto-chave: em inglês a cor vem antes da roupa: a blue shirt (não "a shirt blue"). Com dois adjetivos, tamanho ou opinião vem primeiro, depois a cor: a nice red dress. O adjetivo nunca leva s.
