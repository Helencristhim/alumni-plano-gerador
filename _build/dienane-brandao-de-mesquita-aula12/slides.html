Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Adjective before the noun

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Where does the color go -- before or after the clothing word?

"I am wearing a blue shirt."

"She has a red dress."

"He wears black shoes."

"I like my new white hat."

Grammar Rule

Adjective + Noun

FormStructureExample
One adjectivecolor + nouna blue shirt
Two adjectivessize/opinion + color + nouna nice red dress
With wearwear + adjective + nounI wear black shoes.
Pluralno s on the adjectivetwo white shoes

Key idea: in English the adjective comes before the noun: a blue shirt, not "a shirt blue". With two adjectives, size or opinion comes first, then the color: a nice red dress. The adjective never takes an "s".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am wearing a shirt blue."
"I am wearing a blue shirt."

In English, the color comes before the clothing word. In Portuguese it is the opposite, so be careful.
