The Rule

A Period or a Start Point

WordExample
FOR + a period of timeI have worked here for twelve years.
SINCE + a start point (year)I have been a manager since 2018.
SINCE + a start point (month / day)I have lived here since March.
FOR + a short periodI have known him for two months.
Question -- how longHow long have you worked at Sanofi?

Key idea: use the present perfect (have/has + participle) to say how long. Use FOR with a period of time (for five years), and SINCE with a start point (since 2015).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have worked here since five years. I am a manager for 2018."
"I have worked here for five years. I have been a manager since 2018."

Use for with a period of time (for five years). Use since with a start point (since 2018). Always with the present perfect.
