Rick Steves' Europe — European Travel Skills: Part 1 (free, no subscription)

The public television series that teaches travelers how to cross Europe on their own. Part 1 begins exactly where your lesson begins: arriving in Europe with a passport in your hand. Rick Steves is calm, he never hurries, and he speaks some of the clearest American English on television. He is also a man who has crossed a hundred borders and still explains every step slowly, as if it were the first time.

Tip: watch the first ten minutes with English subtitles, twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, count how many times he uses a past verb to tell you what he did: "I took the train", "I did not book a hotel". That is the grammar of this lesson, in the mouth of a real traveler.

Podcast
Context English Podcast — Episode 3: At the Airport (A2-B1)

Mike and Anna walk through one airport, from the door to the gate, and give you the exact sentence for each moment. Two of them are your lesson: "Do I need to take my laptop out of my bag?" at the security check, and "Could you say that again slowly, please?" when somebody speaks too fast. The whole episode is at your level, and the full transcript is on the same page.

Tip: listen first without reading — your ear before your eyes. Only on the second listen, open the transcript and read while you listen. When you hear "Could you say that again slowly, please?", stop the audio and say it out loud three times. That is the phrase that replaces your translator.

YouTube
Daily English Talk — Airport Immigration English: What to Say at the Border (A1-B1)

Fourteen minutes at passport control, question by question. It is the officer of your lesson: why are you visiting, how long are you staying, where are you staying, do you have anything to declare. You hear the question at real speed first, and then the answer comes slowly, with the words on the screen.

Tip: pause after every question the officer asks, and answer out loud as yourself — fourteen days, tourism, with your husband — before the video gives you the model. Do it twice this week. The second time will already be a piece of cake.
