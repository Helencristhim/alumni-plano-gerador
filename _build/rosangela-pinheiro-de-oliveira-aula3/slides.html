Chapter 3: The Code

No, and Why?

I did not bring · Did you pack?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I did not bring liquids in my carry-on."

"I did not understand the announcement."

"Did you pack this bag yourself?"

"Did you declare everything?"

All four are in the past. One little word is in every sentence: did / Did. So look at the verb after it — bring, understand, pack, declare. Where is the past?

FormUseExample
Yes
verb in the past		The past you already know, from Lesson 2.I packed my bag. I chose a seat.
No
did not + verb		"Did" carries the past, so the verb goes back to normal.I did not bring liquids. (never "did not brought")
Question
Did you + verb?		Same rule. "Did" opens the question, the verb stays normal.Did you pack this bag? Did you declare it?
Short answerYou do not repeat the verb. Just "did" or "did not"."Did you pack it?" — "Yes, I did." / "No, I did not."
Always the same"Did" does not change with I / you / he / she.I did not · she did not · we did not

One past per sentence. When did arrives, the verb relaxes and goes back to normal.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I did not brought any liquids."
"I did not bring any liquids."
"Did you packed this bag yourself?"
"Did you pack this bag yourself?"
"I not understand the officer."
"I did not understand the officer."

One past per sentence. "Did" is the past, so the verb relaxes: bring, not "brought". And in the past, "not" never works alone — it needs did.

The Repair Kit

When You Do Not Understand

Officers speak fast. Every traveler asks them to repeat. These three phrases are yours.

"I am sorry, I did not understand. Could you repeat that, please?"
"What does that mean?"
"I did not catch that — could you say it more slowly, please?"

Look at them again: "I did not understand", "I did not catch that". The grammar of today is already inside the most useful phrases of your trip.

Chapter 4: At Security

The Officer Asks

Three questions · one tray · and one word you do not know

Dialogue

The Officer at Security

B
Good morning. Passport and boarding pass, please. Did you pack this bag yourself?
R
Good morning. Yes, I did. I packed it yesterday, at home.
B
Thank you. Did you bring any liquids in your carry-on? Water, perfume, cream?
R
No, I did not. I know the liquid restriction, so I left my water at home.
B
Perfect. Now put your bag in the tray, and your phone and your keys too.
R
I am sorry, I did not understand. Could you repeat that, please? What does "tray" mean?
B
Of course. A tray is this box, here. Put your bag inside, and it goes through the X-ray machine. Then you walk through the metal detector.
R
Thank you. I understand now. And where is the departure lounge, please?
B
After the duty-free, on your right. Your gate is 22. Have a good flight, Mrs. Oliveira.
Comprehension

About the Officer

1. What two questions did the officer ask about the bag and the liquids?
"Did you pack this bag yourself?" and "Did you bring any liquids in your carry-on?"
2. What did the officer say a tray is?
A box. You put your bag inside, and it goes through the X-ray machine.
3. What did the officer do when she did not understand?
He said "Of course" and explained again, slowly. He was not angry.
4. Where is the departure lounge, and what is the gate?
After the duty-free, on the right. The gate is 22.
Listening

The Security Rules

An announcement in the security area. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What is the rule for liquids?
Only small bottles — one hundred milliliters or less, in a small clear bag.
2. What three things do you take out and put in the tray?
Your laptop, your phone and your keys. Coats and belts go in the tray too.
3. What do you do after the metal detector?
Take your things from the tray, and go to the departure lounge. The duty-free is on the right.
Listening 2

She Tells You What She Did Not Do

Teresa is in the departure lounge, at the next seat. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. Why did the officer stop Teresa at the security check?
She forgot a bottle of perfume in her carry-on — it was too big for the liquid restriction.
2. What did Teresa do when she did not understand the officer?
She asked him to repeat and to speak more slowly. She says she did not feel bad about it.
3. What did she buy at the duty-free, and why?
Another perfume — because after security there is no liquid restriction on what you buy there.
