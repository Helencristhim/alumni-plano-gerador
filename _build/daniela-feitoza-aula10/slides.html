Stress Rule

Strong Words vs Weak Words

Type of wordIn the sentenceExamples
Content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives, adverbs)Stressed — loud and longreport, finished, happy, quickly
Function words (articles, prepositions, pronouns, auxiliaries)Unstressed — short and softa, the, to, of, and, I, you
Key pointStress the words that carry the meaning. Reduce the small grammar words, and use contractions to keep the rhythm smooth.
Common Mistake

Robotic vs Natural

✗ ROBOTIC

I. WANT. A. COFFEE. (every word strong)

✓ NATURAL

I want a coffee. (strong: want, coffee)

✗ STIFF

"I am ready. I do not agree."

✓ NATURAL

"I'm ready. I don't agree."

Push the meaning words, soften the small ones, and use contractions to flow.

Chapter 5

Tricky Rhythm

Drill it until it feels easy

Quick Fire

Say It With Rhythm

1 / 5

1. Stress the content words: "I need the report by Friday."

Strong: NEED, REPORT, FRIDAY. Soft: the, by.

2. Make the contraction: "I am not sure about that."

"I'm not sure about that." (I am to I'm)

3. Which words are weak in "a cup of coffee"?

a and of are weak (uh); cup and coffee are strong.

4. Link the words: "Can I ask a question?"

Link Can-I and ask-a so it flows: "Ca-nI-a-ska question?"

5. Put emphasis on the key word: "I want THIS one, not that one."

Push THIS for contrast emphasis: "I want THIS one."
