Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Schwa -- the short, relaxed uh sound in unstressed syllables, like the a in about (PT: som neutro — o "átono" uh)
"The a in about is a schwa sound."
Content word -- an important word that carries meaning: a noun, a main verb, or an adjective (PT: palavra de conteúdo)
"In I love coffee, love and coffee are content words."
Function word -- a small grammar word, like a, the, to, of, or and (PT: palavra funcional)
"The and to are function words."
Weak form -- the short, soft way we say a function word, like to said as tuh (PT: forma fraca)
"In I want to go, to has a weak form."
Linking -- joining the end of one word to the start of the next so they flow (PT: ligação de sons)
"Turn it off links into one smooth phrase."
Contraction -- two words joined with an apostrophe, like I am to I'm (PT: contração)
"I am becomes the contraction I'm."
Emphasis -- extra force on the most important word in a sentence (PT: ênfase)
"Put emphasis on the word that matters most."
Unstressed -- said with less force, quieter and quicker than a strong word (PT: átono)
"Small words are usually unstressed."
Reduce -- to make a sound shorter or weaker (PT: reduzir)
"We reduce the small words to keep the rhythm."
Flow -- smooth, connected speech where the words run together (PT: fluidez)
"Good rhythm gives your speech a natural flow."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Strong & Weak Words

GRAMMAR

Quais palavras são fortes e quais são fracas numa frase (bilingue EN + PT).

Type of wordRhythmExamples
Content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives, adverbs)Stressed — loud and longreport, finished, happy, quickly
Function words (articles, prepositions, pronouns, auxiliaries)Unstressed — short and softa, the, to, of, and, I, you
ContractionsJoined — two words, one soundI'm, don't, we'll, it's
Regra (PT)Dê força às palavras de conteúdo (as que têm sentido) e reduza as palavrinhas de gramática. Use contrações (I'm, don't) e ligue as palavras para a fala fluir — assim o inglês soa natural, não robótico.
