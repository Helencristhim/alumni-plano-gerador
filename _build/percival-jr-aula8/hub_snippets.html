Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Forecast -- a statement about what will happen in the future
"The forecast for the company is very good." (Previsão)
Predict -- to say what you think will happen later
"Experts predict a busy quarter." (Prever)
Expect -- to think that something will happen
"We expect good results this year." (Esperar)
Budget -- the money a company plans to spend on something
"The board will approve the budget on Monday." (Orçamento)
Launch -- to start a new project, product, or service
"We are going to launch a new safety project." (Lançar)
Hire -- to give a person a job at your company
"We are going to hire two new engineers." (Contratar)
Expand -- to make something bigger, or to grow
"The business will expand to two new cities." (Expandir)
Upcoming -- happening soon, in the near future
"The upcoming meeting is on Friday." (Próximo)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Going to vs Will

GRAMMAR

Usamos "going to" para planos decididos antes e "will" para previsões e decisões rápidas. / We use "going to" for plans decided before and "will" for predictions and quick decisions.

FormUseExample
be going to + verbplano decidido antes / a plan decided beforeWe are going to hire two engineers.
will + base verbprevisão ou decisão rápida / prediction or quick decisionI think it will succeed.
won't = will notprevisão negativa / negative predictionThe budget won't change.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use going to para um plano que você já decidiu (am, is, are going to + verbo). Use will para uma previsão ou decisão rápida (will + verbo base, sem "to"). Os dois falam do futuro: We are going to launch... I think it will succeed.
