Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Going to and will

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Are the orange words about the past or the future? What is the difference between them?

"Next month, we are going to hire two engineers."

"I think the project will succeed."

"We are going to launch a new project."

"The board will approve the budget on Monday."

Grammar Rule

Going to vs Will

FormUseExample
be going to + verba plan decided beforeWe are going to hire two engineers.
will + base verba prediction or quick decisionI think it will succeed.
won't = will nota negative predictionThe budget won't change.

Key idea: use going to for a plan you decided before (am, is, are going to + verb). Use will for a prediction or a quick decision (will + base verb, with no "to"). Both talk about the future.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Next year we will to expand the team."
"Next year we will expand the team."

After will, use the base verb with no "to". And with going to, never forget am, is, or are.
