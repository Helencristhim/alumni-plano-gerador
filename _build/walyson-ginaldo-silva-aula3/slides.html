Grammar Rule

The Passive Voice

RuleExample
Form: am / is / are + past participleCoffee is grown at high altitude.
The agent is optional, with byThe cherries are harvested by hand.
Use it when the action matters, not the doerThe beans are roasted and ground.
Watch the irregular participlesThe cherries are taken (take) to the mill.

Key idea: the passive puts the action first. Use a form of "to be" plus the past participle, and add "by" only if you want to name who does it.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The cherries are harvest by hand."
"The cherries are harvested by hand."

The passive needs to be + past participle (are harvested, is grown), never the base verb on its own.

Grammar Practice

Make It Passive

"The coffee _____is grown on mountain farms." (grow)
"The ripe cherries _____are harvested by hand." (harvest)
"The green beans _____are graded by quality." (grade)
"Every lot _____is traced back to the farm." (trace)

Dialogue

The German Buyer with Carla

C

"Good morning, Walyson. The buyer from Germany wants to know how this coffee is produced. Can you walk her through the supply chain?"

W

"Of course. It all starts at the origin: the coffee is grown on small farms, and the ripe cherries are harvested by hand."

C

"And after the harvest? She is very interested in how the quality is controlled."

W

"After the harvest, the beans are milled and dried, then they are graded by quality before they are exported from Santos."

C

"Perfect. She also asked if a single bag can be traced back to one farm."

W

"Yes. Every lot is traced from the grower to the port, so each blend is documented before it is roasted abroad."
