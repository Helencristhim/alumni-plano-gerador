Lesson 3 -- The Coffee Supply Chain

Documentary
Netflix -- "Rotten" (Season 2): The Bitter Truth (coffee episode)

A documentary that follows coffee along its supply chain, from the growers and the harvest to the export market. Listen for how each step of the process is described.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down five passive sentences you hear (for example, "the coffee is grown..."). Then describe one step of the chain in your own words using the passive voice.

Podcast
Cup of Excellence -- "From Farm to Cup"

A short, clear episode on how green coffee is harvested, milled, graded, and exported. Perfect for hearing the supply-chain vocabulary and the passive voice used naturally by industry people.

Record a 90-second audio explaining the journey of your coffee from origin to cup. Use at least four new words from today and the passive voice at every step.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The Passive Voice"

A focused lesson on how the passive is formed (to be + past participle) and when to use it to describe a process. Directly reinforces today's grammar discovery.

After watching, write six true sentences about how coffee is produced, one passive form for each step: grown, harvested, milled, graded, exported, roasted. Read them aloud with the correct stress.
