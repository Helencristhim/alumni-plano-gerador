The Coffee Supply Chain -- Passive Voice (Origin to Cup)

The passive voice (to be + past participle) to describe a process: the coffee is grown, the beans are roasted. New vocabulary: harvest, grower, ripe, mill, grade, roast, blend, origin, export, supply chain, brew, trace.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Harvest -- to pick the ripe coffee cherries from the plants
"The cherries are harvested by hand between May and September." (Colher / colheita)
Grower -- a farmer or company that grows the coffee
"Each lot is traced back to a single grower." (Produtor / cafeicultor)
Ripe -- fully developed and ready to be picked
"The fruit is picked only when it is ripe." (Maduro)
Mill -- to process the cherries and remove the outer fruit
"At the mill, the beans are dried and cleaned." (Beneficiar)
Grade -- to sort the beans by size and quality
"The beans are graded before they are exported." (Classificar)
Roast -- to heat the green beans until they turn brown
"The beans are roasted in small batches." (Torrar)
Blend -- a mixture of beans from different origins
"Each blend is documented before it is roasted." (Blend / mistura)
Origin -- the place or country where the coffee is grown
"This origin is known for a sweet, balanced cup." (Origem)
Export -- to send goods to another country to be sold
"The best lots are exported from the port of Santos." (Exportar)
Supply chain -- the whole path a product follows from producer to consumer
"He walks the buyer through the supply chain." (Cadeia de suprimentos)
Brew -- to make coffee by passing hot water through the ground beans
"A small sample is brewed for the buyers." (Coar / preparar o café)
Trace -- to follow a product back to its source
"Every step is traced from the farm to the port." (Rastrear)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Every morning, fresh coffee is enjoyed around the world, but the journey is long. The coffee is grown on small farms, and the ripe cherries are harvested by hand. At the mill, the fruit is removed and the green beans are dried in the sun. Then the beans are graded by quality, and the best lots are exported from Santos. Abroad, the coffee is roasted, a blend is created, and finally it is brewed into your cup. Every step is recorded, so each bag can be traced back to the grower.

1. In "the coffee is grown", which two parts form the passive?
A A form of "to be" + the past participle (is grown).
B Two nouns next to each other.
C An adverb plus a verb.
2. Why does the writer use the passive so often in this text?
A To name the workers who do each task.
B To focus on the process and the action, not on who does it.
C Because the actions happened only once in the past.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "The cherries are harvest by hand."
B "The cherries are harvested by hand."
C "The cherries harvest by hand."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Usada para focar na ação, não em quem a faz. / Used to focus on the action, not on the doer.

Rule / RegraDetail / DetalheExample / Exemplo
am / is / are + past participleForma do passivo no presenteCoffee is grown at high altitude.
Agent with "by"O agente é opcional, introduzido por "by"The cherries are harvested by hand.
Focus on the actionUse quando importa a ação, não quem fazThe beans are roasted and ground.
Irregular participlesgrow→grown, take→taken, send→sentThe cherries are taken to the mill.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o passivo usa to be + past participle. Nunca "the beans are roast" -- diga "the beans are roasted".
