Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Opinion, agree, and disagree phrases

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Does each phrase give an opinion, agree, or disagree?

"In my opinion, we need more time."

"I agree with you. That is a good point."

"I see your point, but I am not sure."

"Maybe we could move the deadline."

Grammar Rule

Meeting Phrases

FunctionPhraseExample
give an opinionIn my opinion / I thinkIn my opinion, we need more time.
agreeI agree / a good pointI agree. That is a good point.
disagree (polite)I see your point, but / I am not sureI see your point, but I am not sure.
suggestMaybe we could / Why notMaybe we could move the deadline.

Key idea: in a meeting, start with a phrase before your idea. To disagree, stay polite: say I see your point, but instead of You are wrong.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"No. You are wrong. My idea is better."
"I see your point, but maybe we could try another way."

In a meeting, a hard no can sound rude. Use a soft phrase like I see your point, but to disagree in a polite way.

Dialogue

In the Meeting

T

"Let us discuss the new deadline. In my opinion, the current date is fine."

A

"I see your point, but I disagree. I am not sure the team has enough time."

T

"That is a good point. What do you suggest?"

A

"Maybe we could ask the client for two more days. Can you clarify the budget first?"

T

"Good idea. So our decision is: ask for two more days. Thank you for your help."

A

"Perfect. Let me summarize: we ask for two more days and check the budget."
