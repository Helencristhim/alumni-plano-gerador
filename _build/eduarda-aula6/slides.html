Forming Questions

Wh- questionWh- + auxiliary + subject + verb?"What do you do?"
Present perfectHave/Has + subject + past participle?"Have you been here before?"
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"What you do for work?" / "You are from where?"
"What do you do for work?" / "Where are you from?"

In English questions, the auxiliary (do, are, have) comes before the subject. Never drop it.

Speaking

Oral Drilling

1. Start a conversation with someone standing alone.
"Hi, I do not think we have met. I am Eduarda, from Houlihan Lokey."
2. Ask what the person does for work.
"What do you do? Which firm are you with?"
3. Ask if it is their first time at the event.
"Is this your first time at the Restructuring Summit?"
4. Find common ground.
"Have you worked on any cross-border deals? I find them fascinating."
5. Make small talk about the city.
"Have you been to São Paulo before? The food here is amazing."
6. End the conversation and keep in touch.
"It was a pleasure meeting you. Let's stay in touch."
