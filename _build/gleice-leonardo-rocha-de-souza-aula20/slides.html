Chapter 3 -- Your Toolbox

Your Grammar Toolbox

Experiences, the past, for, and since

Listen and Notice

Find the Tool

Look at the orange words. Each sentence uses a different grammar tool.

"I have visited many clients in my career."

"I started this job in 2012."

"I have worked in this field for ten years."

"I have been a manager since 2018."

The Rule

Your Experiences Grammar

ToolExample
Life experience, no time -- present perfect (have/has + participle)I have visited many clients.
Finished moment, with a date -- past simpleI started this job in 2012.
Duration -- for + a period of timeI have worked here for ten years.
Start point -- since + a point in timeI have been a manager since 2018.

Key idea: use the present perfect for a life experience with no time, the past simple for a finished moment with a date, for to give a duration (for ten years), and since to give a start point (since 2018).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have started this job in 2012. I have worked here since ten years, and I am a manager for 2018."
"I started this job in 2012. I have worked here for ten years, and I have been a manager since 2018."

Use the past simple for a finished date (started in 2012). Use for + a period (for ten years) and since + a start point (since 2018).
