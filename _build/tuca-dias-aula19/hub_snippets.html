Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Overcooked -- cooked for too long, dry or hard
"I am afraid this steak is a little overcooked." (Cozido/passado demais)
Undercooked -- not cooked enough, still raw inside
"There seems to be a problem; the chicken is undercooked." (Mal passado/cru)
Bland -- having little or no flavor
"The soup is a little bland; it needs more salt." (Sem sabor/sem graça)
Seasoning -- salt, pepper, and spices that add flavor
"The dish needs a little more seasoning." (Tempero)
Crispy -- pleasantly hard and crunchy
"The chicken was wonderfully crispy." (Crocante)
Portion -- the amount of food served to one person
"The portion was generous and very filling." (Porção)
Dressing -- a sauce, usually with oil, for a salad
"The salad came with a light, fresh dressing." (Molho de salada)
Compliment -- to say something kind to praise a meal
"Please give the chef my compliments." (Elogio/elogiar)
Leftovers -- food not eaten, taken home to eat later
"The portion was huge, so I took the leftovers home." (Sobras/restos)
Delicious -- having a very pleasant taste; very tasty
"The meal was absolutely delicious." (Delicioso/gostoso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When something is wrong with your food, you do not have to be rude. Two soft openers make a complaint polite. With I'm afraid, add a full sentence: "I'm afraid this steak is a little overcooked." Here, "I'm afraid" is not about fear; it is a gentle way to give bad news. With There seems to be, add a noun: "There seems to be a mistake with the bill." You can soften even more with a little or a bit: "The soup is a bit bland." And when the food is wonderful, give a compliment: "The chicken was delicious; my compliments to the chef!" Polite feedback, good or bad, helps everyone.

1. Which is the polite way to say the steak is too cooked?
A This steak is bad!
B I'm afraid this steak is a little overcooked.
C I afraid this steak overcooked.
2. What follows "There seems to be"?
A a full sentence (the steak is cold)
B a noun (a mistake, a problem)
C a base verb (meet, go)
3. The meal was wonderful. How do you praise the chef?
A It was delicious. My compliments to the chef!
B There seems to be a problem.
C I'm afraid the food is bland.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- I'm afraid & There seems to be

GRAMMAR

Duas formas gentis de reclamar de algo, sem ser rude. / Two gentle ways to complain about something, without being rude.

Structure / EstruturaExampleNote / Nota
I'm afraid + fraseI'm afraid the steak is overcooked.jeito gentil de dar má notícia / soft bad news, not fear
There seems to be + substantivoThere seems to be a mistake.use um substantivo / use a noun
suavizar / softena little / a bit overcooked"a little" e "a bit" deixam mais gentil / make it gentler
elogiar / compliment"It was delicious. My compliments to the chef!"elogie também as coisas boas / praise the good too

Key point / Ponto-chave: para reclamar com gentileza, não seja direto demais. Use I'm afraid + uma frase ("I'm afraid the soup is bland") ou There seems to be + um substantivo ("There seems to be a mistake"). Use a little ou a bit para suavizar. E quando a comida está boa, faça um compliment: "It was delicious; my compliments to the chef!" / After I'm afraid, use a sentence; after There seems to be, use a noun.
