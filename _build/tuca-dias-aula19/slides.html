Chapter 3 -- Softening

Complaining Politely

I'm afraid... & There seems to be...

"There seems to be too little seasoning."

Grammar Rule

Softening: I'm afraid / There seems to be

StructureExampleNote
I'm afraid + sentenceI'm afraid the steak is overcooked.a soft way to give bad news; not about fear
There seems to be + nounThere seems to be a mistake.use a noun (a mistake, a problem)
soften the worda little / a bit overcooked"a little" and "a bit" make it gentler
compliment"It was delicious. My compliments to the chef!"praise the good things too

Key idea: To complain politely, do not be blunt. Use I'm afraid + a full sentence ("I'm afraid the soup is bland"), or There seems to be + a noun ("There seems to be a mistake"). Add a little or a bit to soften it more. And when the meal is good, give a compliment: "It was delicious; my compliments to the chef!"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"This food is bad!" / "It have a mistake."
"I'm afraid this dish is a little overcooked." / "There seems to be a mistake."

Use I'm afraid before a full sentence, and There seems to be before a noun. Soften with a little or a bit.
