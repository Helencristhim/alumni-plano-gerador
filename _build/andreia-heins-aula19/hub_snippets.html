Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Constructive -- helpful and meant to make the work better, not to criticize harshly
"Let me give you some constructive feedback." (Construtivo)
Praise -- to say warmly what someone did well
"I want to praise the team for their hard work." (Elogiar)
Strengths -- the things a person or a team does well
"One of your strengths is staying calm under pressure." (Pontos fortes)
Improve -- to make something better than before
"Here is one way to improve the report." (Melhorar)
Suggestion -- a gentle idea you offer to help, not an order
"May I make a small suggestion?" (Sugestão)
Acknowledge -- to accept feedback and show that you heard it
"Thank you. I acknowledge your point and I will work on it." (Reconhecer)
Perspective -- the way one person sees or understands a situation
"From my perspective, the timing was the main issue." (Perspectiva)
Address -- to deal with a problem or topic directly
"Let us address the delay before the next project." (Tratar / abordar)
Framework -- a simple structure you follow, such as praise first, then a suggestion
"I use a simple framework: strengths first, then one improvement." (Estrutura / modelo)
Honest -- truthful and clear, but still respectful and kind
"Let me be honest and direct, but I say it kindly." (Honesto / sincero)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia must give the team some constructive feedback after the console rollout. She uses a simple framework: strengths first, then one suggestion. She begins with praise: "Your communication was excellent." Then she gives the difficult part softly, with a past modal: "You could have explained the risk earlier." Note: the form is could have + past participle (explained), and it points to a better past choice politely. She adds her view: "From my perspective, we should have tested the console first." She wants to improve the process, not to blame anyone, so she is honest but kind. Finally, she will address any questions and acknowledge the team's effort.

1. Andreia says "You could have explained the risk earlier." What does this mean?
A In the past, explaining earlier was a possible, better choice -- but it did not happen.
B She is talking about something the team will do tomorrow.
C The team explained the risk early and she is happy.
2. Which form follows could / should / would in past modals?
A The base verb: "could have explain."
B have + past participle: "could have explained."
C of + past participle: "could of explained."
3. What is Andreia's feedback framework?
A Praise a strength first, then give one suggestion -- honest but kind.
B List every mistake first, then end abruptly.
C Only criticize, never praise.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Modals

GRAMMAR

Como dar feedback suave sobre algo que já aconteceu. / How to give soft feedback about something that already happened.

ModalForma / FormExemplo / Example
could havecould have + particicpio passadoYou could have explained it earlier.
should haveshould have + particicpio passadoWe should have tested it first.
would havewould have + particicpio passadoI would have asked for more time.
Negativashould not have + particicpioWe should not have rushed the report.

Atenção / Note: Os past modals falam de uma ação passada que não aconteceu. A forma é modal + have + particicpio passado (could have explained). Could have = uma escolha possível/melhor no passado; should have = o conselho certo no passado; would have = o que eu teria feito. Tornam o feedback suave e educado. Use have, nunca of (should have, não "should of"). / Past modals use modal + have + past participle to talk about a past action that did not happen.
