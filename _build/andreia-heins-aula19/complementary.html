Lesson 19 -- Giving & Receiving Feedback

Series
Ted Lasso -- any episode where the coach gives a player feedback

Watch how Ted praises a strength first, then gives one gentle suggestion to improve, staying honest but always kind. A natural model for feedback that helps instead of hurts.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one moment of praise and one suggestion. Notice when a character softens the message with "you could have..." or "you should have..." -- those are past modals.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Give and Receive Feedback Well"

The hosts explain a simple framework: praise a real strength first, then give one constructive suggestion, and acknowledge the other person's perspective -- honest but kind.

Note three tips for giving feedback. Then record a one-minute piece of feedback using at least four new words from today, such as constructive, strengths, suggestion and acknowledge.

YouTube
Business English -- "Past Modals: should have, could have, would have"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to soften feedback about the past with "You could have explained it earlier" and "We should have tested it first" -- modal + have + past participle.

After watching, write five feedback sentences for a real situation at work: one piece of praise, two suggestions with past modals, one sentence that acknowledges another perspective, and one that stays honest but kind. Then say them out loud.
