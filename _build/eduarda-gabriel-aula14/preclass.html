Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key monitoring and compliance terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Covenant -- a formal promise written into a loan agreement
"The borrower must respect every financial covenant."
Compliance -- following the rules and terms of an agreement
"We check the borrower's compliance every quarter."
Monitor -- to watch and track something over time
"Our team monitors the debt levels closely."
Milestone -- a key target or checkpoint to reach by a date
"The borrower has hit every milestone so far."
Flag -- to point out a problem so others notice it
"The analyst flagged a late report this week."
Escalate -- to raise an issue to a higher level for action
"We escalated the problem to the credit committee."
Remediation -- the action of fixing a problem or breach
"They agreed on a remediation plan for the issue."
Track Record -- a history of past results and performance
"The borrower has a solid track record so far."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

A month after the Vertex deal closed, our monitoring team got to work. We have been monitoring all the financial covenants since the closing, and the borrower has been complying with every milestone. Last week, an analyst flagged one item: the reporting package arrived late. We have been following up with their finance team, so I have already escalated it to the credit committee. 2. Why is "have been monitoring" used instead of "monitor"?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use the present perfect continuous to report an action that started in the past and is still going on (or stopped very recently). Form: have / has been + verb-ing. Usamos isso para acoes em andamento desde um ponto no passado ate agora. Use since + ponto no tempo (since the closing) e for + periodo (for three weeks).

PartFormExample
Ongoing actionhave / has been + verb-ing"We have been monitoring..."
Start pointsince + point in time"...since the closing."
Periodfor + period"...for three weeks."

Common Mistake:

"We monitor the covenants since the closing."

"We have been monitoring the covenants since the closing."

Para uma acao que comecou no passado e continua ate agora, use have/has been + verbo-ing — nunca o presente simples.
