How AVRT Works -- Describing Processes with the Passive

Describing how AVRT makes, checks, and reports. Key words: manufacture, assemble, inspect, certify, approve, dispatch, monitor, audit, submit, store, label, review. Structure: the passive voice -- be (is/are/was/were/has been) + past participle.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Manufacture -- to make products in a factory (fabricar)
"The units are manufactured in Valinhos."
Assemble -- to put parts together into a finished product (montar)
"The parts are assembled on site."
Inspect -- to examine something carefully for quality (inspecionar)
"Each unit is inspected before shipping."
Certify -- to officially confirm something meets a standard (certificar)
"The product is certified to a strict standard."
Approve -- to officially accept a decision or document (aprovar)
"The batch is approved for dispatch."
Dispatch -- to send goods out to customers (expedir / despachar)
"The orders are dispatched to North America."
Monitor -- to watch and check something over time (monitorar)
"Every shipment is monitored to the client."
Audit -- to officially examine financial records (auditar)
"The accounts are audited every quarter."
Submit -- to send a document to someone in authority (submeter / enviar)
"The report is submitted to the board."
Store -- to keep data or goods safely (armazenar)
"The delivery data is stored in one system."
Label -- to attach information to a product (rotular / etiquetar)
"Each order is labeled before packing."
Review -- to examine something to check it is correct (revisar)
"The figures are reviewed by Izabel."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At AVRT, every component follows a careful process. The parts are designed in-house, and the units are manufactured in Valinhos. Each batch is inspected by the quality team, and once a product is certified, it is approved for shipping. The orders are labeled and dispatched to North America, and every shipment is monitored to the client. Each quarter, the accounts are audited by an external firm. The report is reviewed by Izabel, and once the figures are confirmed, it is submitted to the board. Last week, the full report was submitted on time, and this quarter every account has been audited already.

1. Why do we say "the units are manufactured" and not "the units manufacture"?
A Because "units" is plural.
B It is passive: the units receive the action, so we use be + past participle.
C Because it is a question.
2. Look at "has been audited". Why is "been" there?
A The present perfect passive is has/have + been + past participle.
B "been" is a spelling mistake.
C Because the action is in the future.
3. Why does the writer use the passive so much in this text?
A Because the doer is always unknown.
B Because the process and the steps matter more than who does each one.
C Because the passive is shorter than the active.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Describing a process when the action matters more than the doer (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
is / are + past participlePassivo no presente: ação habitual; quem faz não importaThe units are manufactured.
was / were + past participlePassivo no passado: ação terminadaThe report was submitted.
has / have been + past participlePassivo no present perfect: resultado até agora (NÃO esqueça "been")The accounts have been audited.
by + agente (opcional)Só quando o autor importa: inspected by the quality team.
