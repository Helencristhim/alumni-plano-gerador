Aula 6 -- How AVRT Works -- Describing Processes with the Passive

Series
How It's Made (Science Channel) -- any episode

A documentary that shows how everyday products are manufactured, from raw material to finished item. The narration is almost entirely passive voice: "The metal is cut, the parts are welded, and each unit is inspected." It is the exact language you use to describe the AVRT process.

Tip: watch one five-minute segment and write down ten passive sentences ("It is heated", "They are packed"). Then describe one AVRT step the same way.

Podcast
Grammar Girl -- Quick and Dirty Tips (passive voice episodes)

Short, clear episodes on English grammar and style, including when to use the passive voice and when to avoid it. Direct connection to Lesson 6: it explains why "The report is submitted" often sounds more natural in business than naming the doer.

Tip: search "passive voice" on the site. Note two cases where the passive is the better choice, and two where the active is clearer.

YouTube
"Present and past passives -- 6 Minute Grammar" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on the passive in the present and past: be + past participle, and how only the "be" changes to show the tense. Exactly the code you practiced in Lesson 6.

Tip: pause after each example and make your own AVRT version. Then record a 90-second description of how a product is manufactured and shipped.
