Chapter 4: The Code

The Passive Voice

be + past participle · the action, not the doer

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The components are manufactured in Valinhos."

"Each unit is inspected before it is dispatched."

"The report was submitted to the board yesterday."

"A year ago, Izabel prepared every report by hand."

Three sentences share a structure. One is different. Which one, and why?

FormUseExample
is / are + past participlePresent passive: a regular action; the doer is not importantThe units are manufactured.
was / were + past participlePast passive: a finished actionThe report was submitted.
has / have been + past participlePresent perfect passive: a result up to nowThe accounts have been audited.
by + agent (optional)Add the doer only when it mattersinspected by the quality team.
Language Focus

Which Form?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The components are manufacture in Valinhos."

✓ RIGHT

"The components are manufactured in Valinhos."

✗ WRONG

"The report submitted yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"The report was submitted yesterday."

✗ WRONG

"The accounts have audited this quarter."

✓ RIGHT

"The accounts have been audited this quarter."

The passive always needs "be" + the past participle: are manufactured, was submitted. In the present perfect passive, do not forget "been": have been audited.

Dialogue

Walking a Partner Through the Process

A
Izabel, a new partner asked me how everything actually gets made at AVRT. Could you walk us through the process?
I
Of course. First, the components are designed in-house, and the units are manufactured here in Valinhos.
A
And how is quality controlled?
I
Every batch is inspected by the quality team, and each unit is tested. Once a product is certified, it is approved for shipping.
A
Good. What happens after that?
I
The orders are labeled, packed, and dispatched to North America. Every shipment is monitored, and the data is stored in one system.
A
And the numbers? How is the reporting handled?
I
Each quarter, the accounts are audited, the report is reviewed, and then it is submitted to the board.
