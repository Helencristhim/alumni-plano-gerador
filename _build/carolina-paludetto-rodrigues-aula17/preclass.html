Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Advice -- words that help someone know the right thing to do
"My advice is to study a little every day." (Conselho)
Suggest -- to offer an idea so someone can think about it
"I suggest you take a short break." (Sugerir)
Problem -- a difficult situation you want to solve or fix
"I have a problem with my homework." (Problema)
Worried -- a feeling when you think something bad may happen
"She is worried about the big test." (Preocupado/a)
Stressed -- feeling a lot of pressure and worry, with no rest
"He feels stressed before every exam." (Estressado/a)
Deserve -- to earn something good because of your effort
"You worked hard, so you deserve a rest." (Merecer)
Focus -- to put all your attention on one thing
"You should focus on one subject at a time." (Concentrar-se)
Calm -- relaxed and quiet, not nervous or worried
"Take a deep breath and stay calm." (Calmo/a)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Carolina's friend Bruno has a problem. He feels stressed and worried about a big test. He asks Carolina for advice. She says: "You should make a study plan and you should focus on one subject at a time. You shouldn't study all night, because you need to sleep. Why don't you ask the teacher for help?" Then she suggests a short break to stay calm. "You work hard," she says, "so you deserve to feel better. Should I help you make the plan?" Bruno smiles. Good advice always helps.

1. What should Bruno do, according to Carolina?
A Make a study plan and focus on one subject.
B Study all night before the test.
C Stop going to school.
2. We use should + base verb to...
A talk about the past.
B give advice about the good thing to do.
C make a prediction about next year.
3. What does Carolina say Bruno shouldn't do?
A He shouldn't study all night.
B He shouldn't ask the teacher.
C He shouldn't make a study plan.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- should for advice

GRAMMAR

Usamos should + verbo na forma base para dar conselho (a coisa boa a fazer). O negativo é shouldn't (a coisa a evitar). Para perguntar, Should I...? Para suavizar, Why don't you...? / We use should + base verb to give advice (the good thing to do). The negative is shouldn't (the thing to avoid). To ask, Should I...? For softer advice, Why don't you...?

TypeRuleExample
Positiveshould + base verbYou should rest
Negativeshouldn't + base verbYou shouldn't worry
QuestionShould + subject + verb?Should I ask for help?
Soft adviceWhy don't you + verb?Why don't you take a break?
Same for allverb never changesShe should ask

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de should, o verbo fica sempre na forma base: she should rest (não "she should rests"), you should focus (não "you should to focus"). O negativo é shouldn't (= should not). Para pedir conselho, use Should I...? Para um conselho mais suave, use Why don't you...? Diferença da aula 16: lá usávamos will para PREVISÕES; aqui usamos should para ACONSELHAR um amigo.
