Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Board -- the group of senior directors who make the top decisions
"The board has ten minutes for your update." (Diretoria / conselho)
Handout -- a printed page you give the audience to follow along
"Each director gets a one-page handout." (Folheto / material de apoio)
Slide -- one single screen or page of a presentation
"They read every slide carefully." (Slide)
Audience -- the group of people you are speaking or presenting to
"The board is a tough audience." (Plateia / audiência)
Overview -- a short, general summary you give at the beginning
"Let me give you a brief overview." (Visão geral)
Highlight -- an important, positive point you want people to notice
"Let me share two highlights." (Destaque)
Summarize -- to give only the main points in a short, clear way
"To summarize, the platform is faster and cheaper." (Resumir)
Key point -- the single most important idea you want people to remember
"They want the key point first." (Ponto-chave)
Recommendation -- what you advise the group to do next
"My recommendation is to approve the go-live." (Recomendação)
Confident -- calm and sure of yourself, clear and in control
"Leave the directors confident in the project." (Confiante)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael is preparing the board presentation for the Phoenix platform. He knows the directors are a busy audience, so he keeps the agenda short and puts the key point on the first slide. He opens with a one-paragraph overview. Then he shares two highlights. "The platform is faster. In addition, it is cheaper," he writes. He is also honest. "The pilot works well. However, the security review took longer. As a result, go-live moves to April." Finally, he closes the agenda. "To summarize, the delay is under control, and my recommendation is to approve the April go-live." He uses connectors to link every idea, because a clear, confident summary is what an executive audience remembers.

1. Where does Rafael put the key point?
A On the first slide, in a short overview.
B On the very last slide, after all the detail.
C He does not mention a key point.
2. Which connector does he use to introduce the security issue?
A "In addition" -- to add a good point.
B "However" -- to show a contrast.
C "To summarize" -- to close.
3. What is Rafael's recommendation?
A To cancel the project.
B To remove every slide from the deck.
C To approve the April go-live.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Formal Connectors

GRAMMAR

Usamos conectores para ligar ideias numa apresentação formal. Eles começam uma nova frase e levam vírgula depois. / We use connectors to link ideas in a formal presentation. They start a new sentence and take a comma after them.

Job / FunçãoConnectorsExample / Exemplo
Add / AdicionarIn addition, Also, MoreoverIt is faster. In addition, it is cheaper.
Contrast / ContrasteHowever, On the other handIt works. However, the review was slow.
Result / ResultadoAs a result, Therefore, SoIt was slow. As a result, go-live moves.
Sum up / ResumirTo summarize, In short, OverallTo summarize, the delay is under control.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o conector começa uma nova frase e leva vírgula logo depois: "..., it is cheaper." Cuidado: However não é although -- não use "However the budget is tight, ..."; use "The budget is tight. However, ...". E não cole duas frases só com vírgula antes de therefore.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each board situation.

The board has ten minutes and asks you to begin. The best opening is:
A "Let me give you a brief overview. Then I will share two highlights."
B "Let me explain every slide in full detail first."
C "I am not sure where to start, sorry."
You want to add a second positive result. You say:
A "However, the cost is lower."
B "In addition, the cost is lower."
C "To summarize the cost is lower."
A delay pushes go-live by a month. You report it as:
A "The review took longer, therefore go-live moves to April."
B "The review took longer. As a result, go-live moves to April."
C "However the review took longer, go-live moves to April."
