The Board Presentation -- Executive Communication

Presenting project results to the board in a calm, formal way: connectors (however, therefore, as a result, in addition, to summarize) and a formal register. New vocabulary: overview, highlight, outcome, bottleneck, takeaway, roadmap, benchmark, insight.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Overview -- a short, general summary of the main points
"Let me give you a brief overview of the project." (Visão geral / resumo)
Highlight -- an important point you want people to notice
"The main highlight is that we delivered on time." (Destaque)
Outcome -- the final result of a project or decision
"The outcome of the sprint was very positive." (Resultado)
Bottleneck -- a point where progress slows down or gets stuck
"Testing was the main bottleneck this sprint." (Gargalo)
Takeaway -- the main message you want people to remember
"My main takeaway is that the project is healthy." (Mensagem principal)
Roadmap -- a plan that shows the main steps over time
"The roadmap shows the new dates for each phase." (Plano / cronograma)
Benchmark -- a standard you use to compare your results against
"Compared to our benchmark, this delay is normal." (Referência / parâmetro)
Insight -- a clear, useful understanding from data or experience
"My insight from the data is that quality improved." (Percepção / entendimento)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael presents his project to the board. He keeps it short and formal. First, he gives an overview: the login feature was delivered this quarter. Then he shares two highlights: the team delivered on time, and the costs stayed in budget. However, he is honest about one bottleneck in testing. Therefore, the next sprint starts two days late, and he shows the new roadmap. He compares the delay to a benchmark from a past project, so the board sees that it is normal. In addition, he shares one insight from the data: quality improved. To summarize, the outcome is positive and the main takeaway is that the project is healthy. He uses connectors to link his ideas and a calm, formal tone.

1. How does Rafael open his board presentation?
A With a short overview of the main points.
B With every small technical detail.
C With a complaint about the team.
2. Which connector does he use to introduce the bottleneck?
A "In addition" (it adds a similar idea).
B "However" (it shows a contrast).
C "Yeah, so" (an informal filler).
3. What is his main takeaway for the board?
A The project is in serious danger.
B Testing should be removed.
C The project is healthy and the outcome is positive.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Connectors and Formal Register

GRAMMAR

Usamos connectors para ligar duas ideias e mostrar a relação entre elas. / We use connectors to link two ideas and show the relationship.

Function / FunçãoConnectorExample / Exemplo
Contrast / ContrasteHoweverIt is ready. However, testing was slow.
Result / ResultadoTherefore / As a resultWe found a delay. Therefore, dates change.
Addition / AdiçãoIn additionCosts are low. In addition, quality is high.
Summary / ResumoTo summarizeTo summarize, the project is healthy.

Key point / Ponto-chave: num registro formal, comece a frase com o connector e use vírgula: However, ... / Therefore, ... / To summarize, ... Evite fillers informais como yeah e and stuff.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each board-update situation.

You start a board update. The most professional opening is:
A "Let me give you a brief overview of the project."
B "Yeah, so basically a lot of stuff happened."
C "I will read every technical detail now."
You want to introduce a problem after good news. The correct sentence is:
A "It is ready, therefore testing was slow."
B "It is ready. However, testing was slow."
C "It is ready and stuff but slow."
You want to close with a clear message. You say:
A "To summarize, the project is healthy and the roadmap is realistic."
B "Anyway, that is it, bye."
C "I have no idea about the outcome."
