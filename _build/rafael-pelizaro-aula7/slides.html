Board Brief — Phoenix Platform Go-Live

Next Thursday I present the Phoenix platform to the board. The board is a tough audience: they have ten minutes, they read every slide, and they want the key point first. So here is a short brief. I open with a one-paragraph overview, I keep the agenda to three items, and I save the detail for the questions. The goal is simple: leave the directors confident that the project is in safe hands.

Here is the overview. Phoenix replaces four old systems with one cloud platform, and it is now ninety percent built. Let me share two highlights. First, the pilot cut the average login time from eight seconds to two. In addition, the running cost is fifteen percent below the old budget. However, I will be honest about one issue: the security review took longer than planned. As a result, the full go-live moves from March to early April.

To summarize, the message for the board is short and clear. The platform is faster, it is cheaper, and the one-month delay is under control. My recommendation is to approve the April go-live and release the final budget now. I will close the agenda with that single key point, and I will keep my tone calm and formal, because a confident summary is what an executive audience remembers.

Adapted for class use from an internal IT board-presentation brief

Chapter 4: The Code

Linking Ideas with Connectors

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"The platform is faster. In addition, it is cheaper."

"The pilot works well. However, the review took longer."

"The review took longer. As a result, go-live moves to April."

"To summarize, the delay is under control."

JobConnectorsExample
Add an ideaIn addition, Also, MoreoverIt is faster. In addition, it is cheaper.
Show a contrastHowever, On the other handIt works. However, the review was slow.
Show a resultAs a result, Therefore, SoIt was slow. As a result, go-live moves.
Close / sum upTo summarize, In short, OverallTo summarize, the delay is under control.

A connector links two ideas. It usually starts a new sentence, with a comma right after it: "..., it is cheaper." This keeps a presentation clear and formal.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

However the budget is tight, we will deliver.
The budget is tight. However, we will deliver.

However is not although. It starts a new sentence and takes a comma after it.

The review took longer, therefore go-live moves.
The review took longer. Therefore, go-live moves.

Do not glue two sentences with only a comma. Put a full stop before therefore, then a comma after it.

Chapter 5: Into the Boardroom

Presenting to the Director

Dialogue

The Board Update

D
Thanks, Rafael. You have ten minutes. Give us the overview first.
R
Of course. Phoenix replaces four old systems with one cloud platform, and it is ninety percent built.
D
Good. Keep it short. What are the highlights?
R
Two. The pilot cut login time from eight seconds to two. In addition, the cost is fifteen percent below budget.
D
And the issue I heard about? Be honest with the board.
R
The security review took longer. As a result, go-live moves to April. To summarize: faster, cheaper, delay under control. My recommendation is to approve April.
Comprehension

About the Update

1. What does Diana ask Rafael to give first?
The overview, kept short.
2. What are the two highlights Rafael reports?
Login time cut from eight seconds to two, and cost 15% below budget.
3. What is Rafael's recommendation to the board?
To approve the April go-live.
