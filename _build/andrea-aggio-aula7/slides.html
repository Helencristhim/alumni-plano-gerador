Chapter 4: The Code

Clarify Like a Pro

Would you mind...? · Do you mean...? · So you are saying...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Would you mind repeating that?"

"Do you mean the group rate or the net rate?"

"So you are saying the deadline is Friday, right?"

Repeat, check meaning, or confirm -- what is each phrase doing?

FormUseExample
Would you mind + -ing?A polite request to repeat or slow downWould you mind repeating that?
Do you mean...?Check which option the speaker meansDo you mean today or tomorrow?
So you are saying...?Confirm the message in your own wordsSo you are saying it includes breakfast?

After 'Would you mind', use the -ing form (no 'to'): repeating, speaking, slowing down.

Language Focus

Repeat, Check, or Confirm?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Would you mind to repeat that?"

✓ RIGHT

Would you mind repeating that?

✗ WRONG

"Please repeat more slow."

✓ RIGHT

Would you mind speaking more slowly?

After 'Would you mind', use the -ing form. To talk about speed, use the adverb 'slowly', not the adjective 'slow'.

Dialogue

The Fast Buyer

M
Hi Andrea, we run a small hotel group and we want to push our shoulder season and lock in some volume.
A
Sorry, I did not catch that last part. Would you mind repeating it?
M
Of course. We want more bookings in the quieter months. Is that clearer?
A
Much clearer, thank you. Your accent is new to me, so I want to follow along well.
M
No problem. There is a lot of background noise here at the booth too.
A
So you are saying you want a better rate for the low season. Do you mean for groups only?
M
Exactly. Groups of twenty or more. You got the gist perfectly.
A
Great. Let me paraphrase the plan so we are aligned, and then we can talk numbers.
