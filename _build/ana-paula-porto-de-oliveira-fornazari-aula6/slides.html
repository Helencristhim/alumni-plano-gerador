Chapter 4: Adjective Order

a lovely small old house

Putting adjectives in the right order

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"She has a lovely small old house."

"He is a tall young Brazilian man."

"It is a charming little Italian cafe."

"They have a beautiful big white dog."

PositionTypeExample
1opinionlovely, charming, beautiful
2sizesmall, little, big, tall
3ageold, young, new
4color / originwhite, Brazilian, Italian
5the nounhouse, man, cafe, dog

opinion → size → age → color → origin → noun. The noun always comes last.

Grammar Practice

Put the Adjectives in Order

Say the full phrase out loud, then tap to check

"a ___lovely small old house" (small / lovely / old)
"a ___tall young Brazilian man" (Brazilian / young / tall)
"a ___charming little Italian cafe" (little / charming / Italian)
"a ___beautiful big white dog" (white / big / beautiful)
"a ___warm generous old woman" (generous / old / warm)
Language Focus

Read the Order

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"a little cozy cafe"

✓ RIGHT

"a cozy little cafe"

✗ WRONG

"a room cozy and warm"

✓ RIGHT

"a cozy, warm room"

Opinion comes before size (cozy little, not little cozy). Adjectives go BEFORE the noun (a cozy room, not a room cozy).

Dialogue

David Describes a Friend

A
David, you know my friend Marina, right? How would you describe her?
D
Marina? She is one of the most outgoing and cheerful people I know.
A
That is so true. And she is incredibly generous too.
D
Absolutely. She is also very reliable, and she never lets you down.
A
Do you know her new place? What is it like?
D
It is a cozy little apartment near the old square. Not spacious, but charming.
A
I heard the square gets very lively on weekends.
D
Yes, it gets lively and crowded, full of music and food. I love it there.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. How does David describe Marina's personality?
Outgoing, cheerful, generous, and reliable.
2. What is Marina's new apartment like?
A cozy little apartment near the old square, charming but not spacious.
3. What happens in the square on weekends?
It gets lively and crowded, full of music and food.
