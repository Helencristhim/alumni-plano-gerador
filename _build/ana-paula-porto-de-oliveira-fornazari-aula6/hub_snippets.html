Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Outgoing -- friendly and confident, and enjoys meeting new people
"My sister is very outgoing and makes friends everywhere."
Generous -- happy to give money, time, or help to others
"He is generous and always shares what he has."
Reliable -- someone you can trust to do what they promise
"She is reliable, so I can always count on her."
Easygoing -- relaxed and calm, and not easily upset
"My father is easygoing and never gets stressed."
Ambitious -- having a strong wish to succeed or achieve a lot
"She is ambitious and wants to open her own clinic."
Cheerful -- happy and positive in a way that people can see
"He is cheerful and always has a smile."
Stubborn -- not willing to change your mind, even when you are wrong
"My uncle is stubborn and never admits he is wrong."
Cozy -- small, warm, and comfortable in a pleasant way
"Their living room is small but very cozy."
Spacious -- with a lot of open space inside
"The new house is bright and spacious."
Crowded -- full of people, with little free space
"The beach is always crowded in summer."
Lively -- full of energy, activity, and excitement
"The market is lively and full of music."
Charming -- pleasant and attractive in a way that people like
"It is a charming old town by the river."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

My aunt Rosa is my favorite person. She is a generous and easygoing woman who loves everyone. She runs a cozy little cafe on the corner, and it is the most charming place in our neighborhood. It is not spacious, but it feels warm and friendly. In the morning it is quiet, but by lunchtime it becomes lively and crowded. When friends ask about her, I always say the same thing: she is a warm, generous old lady with a beautiful little cafe.

1. Why do we say "a cozy little cafe" and not "a little cozy cafe"?
A Opinion adjectives (cozy) come before size adjectives (little).
B Size adjectives always come first in English.
C The order of adjectives does not matter in English.
2. Which phrase has the correct adjective order?
A "a beautiful little cafe"
B "a little beautiful cafe"
C "a cafe beautiful little"
3. Where do adjectives usually go in English?
A Before the noun: "a charming place."
B After the noun: "a place charming."
C Either before or after, it is the same.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adjective Order

GRAMMAR

How to put two or more adjectives in the right order.

RuleDetailExample
Before the nounAdjectives come BEFORE the noun.a cozy room (not "a room cozy")
The orderopinion → size → age → color → origina lovely small old house
Two adjectivesJoin with "and" or a comma.She is kind and funny.
Same typeUse a comma between adjectives of the same type: a warm, generous woman.

Reflection
