Lesson 6 -- Describing People & Places

TED Talk
Your Body Language May Shape Who You Are -- Amy Cuddy (TED)

A famous, warm talk about how we read and describe other people from the way they stand and move. She uses rich adjectives to describe confident and shy people. Connection to Lesson 6: listen for the words she uses to describe personality and presence.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down five adjectives she uses to describe people.

TED Talk
The Power of Vulnerability -- Brené Brown (TED)

A researcher describes the human qualities that make people warm, generous, and easy to connect with -- exactly the personality words from today. Connection to Lesson 6: she is a natural storyteller who describes people and their character in clear, everyday English.

Tip: after watching, describe one generous or easygoing person you know, in three or four sentences.

TED Talk
The Happy Secret to Better Work -- Shawn Achor (TED)

A funny, fast-talking speaker describes cheerful, positive people and the places that make us feel good. It is full of natural adjectives for personality and mood. Connection to Lesson 6: notice how he describes cheerful people and lively, positive environments.

Tip: pause and describe a cheerful person and a lively place in your own life, using at least four adjectives.
