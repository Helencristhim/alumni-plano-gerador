Aula 12 -- Pre-class

Negotiating & Agreeing

Back in São Paulo, Fernando negotiates the terms of the partnership with Sarah: making a proposal, accepting or rejecting an offer, and finding a fair compromise. Key words: negotiate, proposal, offer, terms, accept, reject, fair, deal, agree, compromise. Structure: would for polite proposals (I would suggest..., Would you accept...?) and the first conditional (If we agree, we can sign today).

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Back in São Paulo, Fernando meets Sarah to negotiate the partnership. "I would suggest a two-year deal," he says. Sarah likes the proposal, but she cannot accept the full price. "Would you accept a small compromise?" Fernando asks. "If we agree on the price today, we can sign this week." Sarah smiles: "If you reduce the price, I will accept the deal. The terms are fair for both of us."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Would & First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como fazer uma proposta educada e falar de resultados (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

StructureUse / UsoExample
I would + verbo (base)Proposta educada (sem "to")I would suggest a deal
Would you + verbo?Pergunta educadaWould you accept this offer?
If + presente, can + verboPrimeiro condicional (resultado agora)If we agree, we can sign
If + presente, will + verboIf you reject, we will find a compromise (NAO "If we will agree")

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situacao na negociacao.

You want to make a polite proposal. You say:
A "I would to suggest a deal."
B "I would suggest a two-year deal."
C "I suggest you a deal now."
You ask the partner politely to say yes. You say:
A "You accept the offer?"
B "Would you accept this offer?"
C "Would you to accept this offer?"
You explain a result with the first conditional. The correct sentence is:
A "If we will agree, we can sign today."
B "If we agree on the price, we can sign today."
C "If we agreeing, we sign today."
Both sides give a little. You suggest the middle. You say:
A "Let us reject the deal."
B "Let us find a fair compromise."
C "We have no terms."
