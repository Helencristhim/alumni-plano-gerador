Chapter 3

The Polite Proposal Code

I would suggest · Would you accept? · If we agree, we can sign

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I would suggest a two-year deal."

"Would you accept this offer?"

"If we agree on the price, we can sign today."

"If you reject the terms, we will find a compromise."

StructureUseExample
I would + verbpolite proposalI would suggest a deal
Would you + verb?polite questionWould you accept this offer?
If + present, can + verbfirst conditional (result now)If we agree, we can sign
If + present, will + verbIf you reject, we will find a compromise (NO "will" after if)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I would to suggest a deal."

✓ RIGHT

"I would suggest a deal."

✗ WRONG

"If we will agree, we can sign."

✓ RIGHT

"If we agree, we can sign."

✗ WRONG

"Would you to accept the offer?"

✓ RIGHT

"Would you accept the offer?"

After would, use the base verb (no "to"). After if, use the present (no "will"): If we agree, we will sign.

Chapter 4

Closing the Deal

Fernando and Sarah negotiate the terms

Dialogue

Negotiating the Terms

S
Welcome back, Fernando. Shall we negotiate the terms of our partnership?
F
Yes, thank you. I would suggest a two-year deal to start.
S
That sounds fair. What is your proposal for the price?
F
If we agree on the price today, we can sign this week.
S
I like your offer, but I cannot accept the full amount.
F
I understand. Would you accept a small compromise?
S
Yes. If you reduce the price, I will accept the deal.
F
Then we agree. It is a fair deal for both of us.
