Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diana accepts the proposal, so now Rafael needs to schedule a meeting to sign the contract. He looks at his calendar for next week. Tuesday morning does not work, because Diana is going to attend a supplier visit, so they reschedule for Wednesday afternoon. Rafael says he will confirm the new time, and he will invite her team. The deadline is Friday, so they won't postpone the meeting again. After the call, they are going to sign the contract on Wednesday.

1. Why does Rafael need to schedule a meeting?
A To sign the contract.
B To visit a supplier.
C To buy a new calendar.
2. Which day do they confirm?
A Wednesday afternoon.
B Tuesday morning.
C Saturday.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I will confirm the time."
B "I will confirms the time."
C "I will to confirm the time."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Will / Going to

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar sobre o futuro: decisões, planos e arranjos. / Used to talk about the future: decisions, plans, and arrangements.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
will + verbo baseDecisão no momento, oferta ou promessa / A decision now, an offer, a promiseI will confirm the time
be going to + verbo basePlano que você já tem / A plan you already haveWe are going to sign it
won't + verbo baseFuturo negativo / A negative futureI won't postpone the meeting
Key point / Ponto-chaveUse o verbo base depois de will e going to. Diga "will send" (não "will sends") e "going to visit" (não "going visit").
