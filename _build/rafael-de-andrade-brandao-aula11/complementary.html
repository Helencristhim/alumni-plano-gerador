Lesson 11 -- Scheduling and Confirming Meetings

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 1: "The Dundies"

Watch how the team schedules and confirms an event, and how they reschedule when there is a problem. Notice the future forms: will for quick decisions and going to for plans.

Write down every sentence with will or going to. Pause and repeat each one, then schedule an imaginary meeting out loud.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Arranging and Confirming Meetings"

Practical phrases for scheduling: how to propose a day, confirm a time, and reschedule politely. Listen for the new words: schedule, confirm, reschedule, postpone, calendar, deadline, attend, and invite.

Note 3 sentences that use will or going to. Practice each one out loud and change the day and time.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "will vs going to: future plans and decisions"

A short, clear lesson on the two future forms: when to use will for a decision at the moment of speaking, and going to for a plan you already have. Perfect for scheduling meetings.

After watching, record yourself scheduling a contract meeting. Use will and going to, and at least three new words from this lesson.
