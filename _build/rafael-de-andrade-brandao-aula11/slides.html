Chapter 3 -- Will / Going to

Grammar Discovery

Will and going to -- talk about the future

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What pattern do the orange words follow?

"I will send a calendar invite."

"I am going to visit a supplier on Tuesday."

"We are going to sign the contract."

"OK, I will confirm Wednesday afternoon."

Grammar Rule

Will / Going to

FormUseExample
will + verbA decision now, an offer, a promiseI will confirm the time
be going to + verbA plan you already haveWe are going to sign it
won't + verbA negative futureI won't postpone the meeting
Key pointUse the base verb after will and after going to. Say will send (not "will sends") and going to visit (not "going visit").

Key idea: Use will for a decision you make right now, and going to for a plan you already made. Always use the base verb after them.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I will sends the calendar invite."
"I will send the calendar invite."

After will and going to, use the base verb. Never add -s.
