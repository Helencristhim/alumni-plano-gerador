Grammar Rule

Three Ways to Talk About the Future

FormWhen we use itExample
willa prediction or a decision nowIt will rain. I will help you.
going toa plan or intention you already haveI'm going to study more.
present continuousa fixed arrangement (often with a time)I'm meeting Mark at three.

Key idea / Ideia-chave: use will for predictions and on-the-spot decisions; going to for plans you already have; the present continuous for fixed arrangements. / Use will para previsões e decisões na hora; going to para planos que você já tem; o presente contínuo para combinados marcados.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I already have a plan. I will learn to cook this year."
"I already have a plan. I'm going to learn to cook this year."

For a plan you already have, use going to. Use will for a decision you make right now.
