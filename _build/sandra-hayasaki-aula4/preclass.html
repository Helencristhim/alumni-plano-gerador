Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Probably -- most likely, but not certain
"I will probably stay home this weekend." (provavelmente)
Hopefully -- I hope this will happen
"Hopefully, the weather will be nice." (com sorte / tomara que)
Plan -- something you decide to do
"My plan is to study English every day." (plano)
Expect -- to think something will happen
"I expect the meeting to finish at five." (esperar / prever)
Arrange -- to organize or plan in advance
"I will arrange a meeting for Monday." (organizar / marcar)
Decision -- a choice you make
"It was a difficult decision, but I am happy." (decisão)
Soon -- in a short time from now
"My English classes start soon." (logo / em breve)
Goal -- something important you want to reach
"My main goal is to speak English with confidence." (meta / objetivo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

This year I have a clear goal: to speak English with confidence. I have made a decision — I'm going to take a class twice a week. My classes start soon, and I'm arranging my schedule now. On Saturday I'm meeting a friend to practice. I expect it to be hard at first, but hopefully I'll get better fast. I'll probably make mistakes, and that is okay.

1. Which form do we use for a plan the speaker already has?
A will
B going to
C past simple
2. "I'm meeting a friend on Saturday." What does this tell us?
A It is a fixed arrangement for the future.
B It happened last week.
C It is happening at this moment.
3. Which sentence makes a prediction?
A "My classes start soon."
B "Hopefully, I'll get better fast."
C "I'm arranging my schedule now."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Talking About the Future

GRAMMAR

Três formas comuns de falar do futuro em inglês. / Three common ways to talk about the future in English.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
willprediction or decision now / previsão ou decisão na horaIt will rain. I will help you.
going toa plan you already have / um plano que você já temI'm going to study more.
present continuousa fixed arrangement / um combinado marcadoI'm meeting Mark at three.
Key point / Ponto-chaveUse will para previsões/decisões na hora; going to para planos que já existem; o presente contínuo para combinados com hora marcada.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

A friend drops something. You decide to help at that moment. What do you say?
A "I'm going to pick it up."
B "Don't worry, I'll pick it up."
C "I pick it up."
You decided last week to take a trip. How do you tell a friend?
A "I'm going to take a trip next month."
B "I will take a trip next month, I just decided."
C "I take a trip next month."
You have a fixed appointment with the dentist on Friday at 10. What do you say?
A "I will see the dentist on Friday."
B "I see the dentist on Friday."
C "I'm seeing the dentist on Friday at ten."
