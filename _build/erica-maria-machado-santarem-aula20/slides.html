Chapter 3

The Helping Code

Three patterns for real life

Grammar Review

Listen and Remember

"I have a headache. You should rest."

"Can I have a painkiller, please?"

"How much is it?"

"Excuse me, how do I get to the station?"

SituationHow to say it
At the doctorI have a + symptom — I have a headache.
AdviceYou should + verb — You should rest.
At the pharmacyCan I have + thing + please? — Can I have a painkiller, please?
Getting helpHow do I get to + place? — How do I get to the station?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You should to rest."

✓ RIGHT

"You should rest."

✗ WRONG

"I want a painkiller."

✓ RIGHT

"Can I have a painkiller, please?"

✗ WRONG

"Where I go to the station?"

✓ RIGHT

"How do I get to the station?"

Advice: "You should + verb". Polite request: "Can I have... please?". Directions: "How do I get to...?"
