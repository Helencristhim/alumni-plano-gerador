Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. Ele junta os três códigos: I have a... / You should..., Can I have...? e How do I get to...?

One morning Érica did not feel well. She had a headache and a fever. She went to the doctor and said: "I have a headache." The doctor said: "You should rest and drink water." Then Érica went to the pharmacy. She said: "Can I have a painkiller, please?" The pharmacist read the label and said: "How much is it? It is twelve reais." On the way home, Érica was lost. She asked a man: "Excuse me, how do I get to the station?" He said: "Go straight and turn left." Now Érica is home. She rested, and she feels much better.

1. How did Érica feel in the morning?
A She felt great.
B She had a headache and a fever.
C She was hungry.
2. What advice did the doctor give?
A "You should rest and drink water."
B "You should run."
C "You should work."
3. How did Érica find the station?
A She used a map.
B She asked a man for help.
C She took a taxi.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Three Real-Life Codes

GRAMMAR

Os três padrões para a vida real. No médico: I have a + sintoma e o conselho You should + verbo simples (sem "to"). Na farmácia: Can I have + coisa + please e How much is it? para o preço. Para pedir ajuda: How do I get to + lugar? e respostas como Go straight / Turn left. / At the doctor: I have a... / You should... At the pharmacy: Can I have...? How much is it? Getting help: How do I get to...?

Situação / SituationEstrutura / Structure
No médico / At the doctorI have a + sintoma — I have a headache.
Conselho / AdviceYou should + verbo — You should rest. (sem "to")
Na farmácia / At the pharmacyCan I have + coisa + please? — Can I have a painkiller, please?
O preço / The priceHow much is it? — How much is it?
Pedir ajuda / Getting helpHow do I get to + lugar? — How do I get to the station?
