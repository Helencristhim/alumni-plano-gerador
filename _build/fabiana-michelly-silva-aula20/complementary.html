Lesson 20 -- End-to-End Communication

Series
The Newsroom (HBO) -- The end-of-episode wrap-ups

Watch how the anchors take a complex story from the opening problem to the final point without a single gap, signposting each stage and closing with a sharp recap. Notice the discourse markers that give the whole piece its cohesion and throughline -- exactly what you practiced today.

Pick one segment and write the four markers used: a sequence one, a contrast one, a result one, and a summary one.

Podcast
TED Talks Daily -- a 12-minute talk that tells one story end to end

Choose any talk where one speaker takes an idea from problem to conclusion. Listen for how they signpost stages, segue between them, and synthesize the whole thing in a final recap. A model for the coherent, extended turn you are building.

Note the speaker's opening sequence marker and their closing summary marker, and write down the throughline of the talk in one sentence.

YouTube
"Discourse Markers and Cohesion in Spoken English" -- Advanced Fluency

A clear guide to the exact structures in this lesson: the four functions of discourse markers (sequence, contrast, result, summary) and how they create the cohesion of a coherent, extended turn -- to begin with, that said, consequently, to sum up.

After watching, record a 90-second end-to-end summary of a project, using at least one marker of each of the four functions.
