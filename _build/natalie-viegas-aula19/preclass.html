Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Stand firm -- to keep your position and not change it under pressure
"We will stand firm on the deadline -- it cannot move." (Manter a posição)
Hold the line -- to refuse to change your position, especially on price
"I had to hold the line on the rate during the call." (Não ceder)
Pushback -- the resistance or objection you get from the other side
"We got strong pushback on the new terms." (Resistência / oposição)
Justify -- to give a good reason that supports a decision
"Let me justify the price with the long-term value." (Justificar)
Rationale -- the set of reasons that explain a decision
"The rationale for the rate is the extra support we provide." (Justificativa / razão)
Non-negotiable -- a point that is fixed and cannot be changed
"The delivery date is non-negotiable for us." (Inegociável)
Assertive -- confident and direct without being aggressive
"Stay assertive: calm, clear, and confident." (Assertivo / firme e calmo)
Leeway -- freedom or room to be flexible on a point
"We have some leeway on the timeline, but not on quality." (Margem / flexibilidade)
Firm -- strong, clear, and not easily changed
"My answer is firm: that price is final." (Firme)
Worthwhile -- worth the time, money, or effort it takes
"A fair deal makes the long talks worthwhile." (Que vale a pena)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The client gave Natalie strong pushback on the rate. She did not cave in. She decided to hold the line, but she stayed calm and assertive. First, she gave her rationale: the price covers extra support. Then she set a clear condition: "Unless we sign a longer contract, we cannot lower the rate." She showed there was some leeway: "We can move on the timeline, as long as the volume stays the same." But quality was non-negotiable, so she had to stand firm. In the end, a longer term for a better rate made the deal worthwhile for both sides.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Conditional Connectors

GRAMMAR

Para negociar com firmeza sem parecer rígida, você define condições. Use as long as / provided that para dizer "somente se isto for verdade", e unless para dizer "se não". Depois do conector, use o presente (stays, sign), e na outra parte use can / will / cannot. / To stay firm without sounding rigid, you set conditions. Use as long as / provided that to say "only if this is true", and unless to say "if not". After the connector, use the present; in the other part, use can / will / cannot.

ConnectorMeaningExample
as long as + presentonly if this is true"...as long as the volume stays the same."
provided that + presentonly on this condition (formal)"Provided that you sign, we will hold the price."
unless + presentif NOT (the negative condition)"Unless we sign longer, we cannot lower the rate."
if + present (neutral)plain condition, less weight"We can lower it if you sign longer."

Key point / Ponto-chave: unless já significa "if not", então nunca use "not" de novo. Errado: "unless you do not sign." Certo: "unless you sign." E depois do conector use o presente, nunca "will": "as long as the volume stays", não "will stay".

Stage 2: Build a Confident Negotiation in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of a confident negotiation in the correct order.

?You set a clear condition, using as long as or unless, to show where you can be flexible.
?You listen to the pushback calmly and do not cave in right away.
?You confirm the terms and make the deal worthwhile for both sides.
?You give your rationale, so your position never sounds like a flat no.
?You stand firm on the non-negotiable points, but offer some leeway on the smaller details.
