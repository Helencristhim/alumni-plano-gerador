Chapter 3: The Polite Code

Could I & Would You

Ask and offer in a polite way

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Could I have your passport, please?"

"Could you bring my bags up, please?"

"Would you like a wake-up call?"

"I'd like a late check-out, please."

FormExampleUse
Could I + verb...?Could I have a key card?ask for something for you
Could you + verb...?Could you call a taxi?ask someone to do something
Would you like + noun?Would you like breakfast?offer something politely
I'd like + nounI'd like a late check-out.say what you want, politely

After Could I, Could you and Would you like, use the base verb (no "to"): "Could I have..." (NOT "Could I to have"). I'd like is the short form of I would like. Add please to sound even more polite.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Could I to have a key card?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could I have a key card?"

✗ WRONG

"I want a late check-out."

✓ RIGHT

"I'd like a late check-out, please."

Dialogue

At the Front Desk

C
Good evening, and welcome. Could I have your name and passport, please?
R
Of course. I'm Roberto Pires. Could I have a quiet room, please?
C
Certainly. Here is your key card for room 402. Would you like the porter to bring your bags up?
R
Yes, please. And could I have a wake-up call at six tomorrow?
C
No problem. The hotel has nice amenities: a gym and free breakfast. Is there anything within walking distance you would like to see?
R
Yes! Could you tell me where the old square is?
C
It's just two minutes away, around the corner. And if you would like a late check-out, please tell us tomorrow. Enjoy your stay!
