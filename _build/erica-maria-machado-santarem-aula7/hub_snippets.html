"I work in a busy office."

"Lunch is my favorite meal."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. Veja o toolbox das seis aulas em ação.

Hello! I am Érica. I have a small family, and we live in a quiet neighborhood. My routine is busy. I always wake up early and I drink some coffee. I start work at eight o'clock. I work in an office with a good colleague. Every week she has a meeting and we write a report. At noon, I eat my favorite meal. I do not drink any coffee in the afternoon. On Friday, I have an appointment with my doctor.

1. "She has a meeting." Why do we use "has"?
A Because the subject is she (he/she/it uses has).
B Because it is a question.
C Because it is a negative sentence.
2. "I always wake up early." Where does "always" go?
A After the verb.
B Before the main verb.
C At the end of the sentence.
3. When does Érica have an appointment?
A At noon.
B On Friday.
C At eight o'clock.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Your Toolbox from Lessons 1-6

GRAMMAR

Esta aula junta as estruturas das seis aulas: verbo to be, have/has, presente simples com advérbios (always, usually, never), horas e some/any. / This class brings together the structures from six lessons: the verb to be, have/has, present simple with adverbs (always, usually, never), telling time, and some/any.

FerramentaExemplo
to beI am, she is, we are — eu sou, ela é, nós somos
have / hasI have a family, she has a routine — eu tenho, ela tem
presente simplesI always work, she usually works — eu sempre trabalho, ela geralmente trabalha
horasat eight o'clock, at half past six — às oito horas, às seis e meia
some / anysome coffee (+), not any tea (−/?) — um pouco de café / nenhum chá

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

A new colleague asks your age. You say:
A "I am 55 years old."
B "I have 55 years old."
C "I am 55 years."
You talk about your colleague's routine. Which is correct?
A "She have a busy routine."
B "She has a busy routine."
C "She has a busy routine?"
You want to say you have no meetings today. Which sentence is correct?
A "I do not have some meetings today."
B "I do not have any meetings today."
C "I not have any meetings today."
How do you describe your morning with "always"?
A "I wake up always at six."
B "I always wake up at six."
C "Always I wake up at six."
