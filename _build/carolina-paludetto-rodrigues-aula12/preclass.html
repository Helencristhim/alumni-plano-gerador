Weekend Plans -- going to for future plans

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Plan -- something you decide to do in the future
"What is your plan for the weekend?" (Plano)
Hang out -- spend relaxed, free time with friends
"We hang out at the park on Saturdays." (Passar o tempo / curtir)
Picnic -- a meal you eat outside, often in a park
"We are going to have a picnic in the park." (Piquenique)
Sleepover -- a night when you sleep at a friend's house
"Marina is going to have a sleepover at my place." (Festa do pijama)
Bake -- to cook cakes or bread in an oven
"We are going to bake a chocolate cake." (Assar / fazer bolo)
Ride -- to travel on a bike, a horse, or in a vehicle
"I am going to ride my bike on Sunday." (Andar / pedalar)
Festival -- a big public event with music, food, and fun
"Our school is going to have a festival." (Festival)
Mall -- a big building with many shops inside
"We are going to the mall on Saturday." (Shopping)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- going to for future plans

GRAMMAR

Usamos be (am/is/are) + going to + verbo para falar de planos futuros. / We use be (am/is/are) + going to + a verb to talk about future plans.

FormStructureExample
Affirmative (I)am going to + verbI am going to play.
he / she / itis going to + verbShe is going to bake.
we / you / theyare going to + verbWe are going to ride.
Negativeam/is/are + not + going toHe is not going to come.
Questionam/is/are + subject + going toAre you going to study?

Key point / Ponto-chave: use be going to para falar de planos futuros. Depois de "going to" sempre vem o verbo na forma base (play, have, bake, ride) -- nunca com -s nem -ing. Na fala, usamos as contrações: I'm going to, she's going to, they're going to. Cuidado: não esqueça o verbo be: "I am going to", não "I going to".
