Chapter 4: The Code

Narrative Tenses

Past simple · past continuous · past perfect

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Our striker scored in the final minute."

"We were watching at the bar when they scored."

"I had never seen a comeback like that."

"By the time I arrived, the game had already started."

Why do green verbs tell the story now, but blue reaches back to what happened earlier?

TenseUse it forExample
Past simplethe main events, in orderOur striker scored. We won.
Past continuousthe background, an action in progressWe were watching when they scored.
Past perfect
had + past participle		an action completed BEFORE another past momentThe game had already started when I arrived.

Past perfect is the step further back in time. It is the difference between "the game started when I arrived" and the natural "the game had already started when I arrived".

Language Focus

Which Happened First?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"When I arrived, the game already started."

✓ RIGHT

"When I arrived, the game had already started."

✗ WRONG

"I was watch the game when they scored."

✓ RIGHT

"I was watching the game when they scored."

For an action before another past action, use had + past participle. For the background, use was/were + -ing.

Dialogue

A Pub in Edinburgh

I
You're Brazilian, aren't you? Then you must have seen some football. Did you catch the match last night?
M
I did. What a nail-biter. I've followed that team for years, and I'd never seen a comeback quite like it.
I
Same here. We were losing two to nothing, and half the pub had already left. Then the striker came on.
M
And everything changed. That's the thing about a good rivalry — the whole room was gripped until the final whistle.
I
So who do you actually support? Be honest — are you a real supporter, or just here for the atmosphere?
M
Real loyalty, since I was a boy. In Brazil, football isn't just a sport — it's who you are. My father took me to my first game, and I've never stopped going.
