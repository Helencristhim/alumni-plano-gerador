Football, Culture and the Conversations That Actually Happen

Talking football and telling the story of a great match. Key words: supporter, rivalry, underdog, comeback, draw, striker, offside, nail-biter, loyalty, gripped by, bragging rights, tournament. Structures: narrative tenses -- past simple (scored), past continuous (were losing) and past perfect (had never seen) -- plus present perfect in conversation (I've been a fan since...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Supporter -- a devoted fan who follows a team (torcedor)
"He has been a supporter of the same club since he was a boy."
Rivalry -- fierce, long-running competition between two teams (rivalidade)
"The rivalry between the two clubs goes back a hundred years."
Underdog -- the team or player expected to lose (o azarão)
"Everyone loves it when the underdog wins."
Comeback -- a recovery from a losing position (virada)
"It was the greatest comeback the club had ever made."
A draw -- a game that ends with the scores equal (empate)
"The match ended in a two-two draw."
Striker -- the player whose main job is to score goals (atacante)
"Their striker scored twice in the second half."
Offside -- an attacking position the rules do not allow (impedimento)
"The goal was ruled out because the striker was offside."
A nail-biter -- an extremely tense, close game (jogo de tirar o fôlego)
"The final was a real nail-biter, decided in the last minute."
Loyalty -- faithful support that lasts through good and bad years (lealdade)
"Real loyalty means supporting the team even when they lose."
To be gripped by -- to be completely absorbed by something (ficar fisgado por)
"The whole bar was gripped by the final ten minutes."
Bragging rights -- the right to boast after a win (o direito de se gabar)
"Winning the derby gives you bragging rights all year."
Tournament -- a series of games to decide a champion (torneio, campeonato)
"The whole country stops during the World Cup tournament."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcelo had watched hundreds of games, but he had never seen a night like that one. His team were losing two to nothing at half-time, and most people had already left the bar. Notice the tenses: had watched and had left (past perfect) point to actions before that moment; were losing (past continuous) is the background in progress. Then the main events came, in the past simple: the striker scored, the room stood up, and the underdog won in the final minute. A good story mixes all three: the main events, the background, and what had happened even earlier.

1. Por que "he had never seen a night like that" usa o past perfect?
A Porque aponta para algo antes daquele momento no passado.
B Porque é uma ação do futuro.
C Porque é o evento principal da história.
2. "They were losing two to nothing" descreve:
A Um evento único e completo.
B O pano de fundo, uma ação em andamento (past continuous).
C Algo que aconteceu antes de tudo.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "When I arrived, the game already started."
B "When I arrived, the game had already started."
C "When I arrived, the game will already start."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Narrative Tenses

GRAMMAR

Como contar uma história no passado (explicação em inglês e português).

TenseUse / UsoExample
Past simpleOs eventos principais, em ordem. The main events, in order.The striker scored. We won.
Past continuous
was/were + -ing		O pano de fundo, ação em andamento. The background, in progress.We were watching when they scored.
Past perfect
had + past participle		Uma ação ANTES de outra no passado. An action before another past moment.The game had already started when I arrived.

Atenção: para a ação mais antiga, use had + participío. Diga "the game had already started", nunca "the game already started".
