Lesson 19 -- The Sales Pitch: Persuasive Language & Benefits

Series
Shark Tank -- a strong pitch scene

Watch an entrepreneur pitch a product to investors. Listen for how they sell the value, not just the price, and turn each feature into a benefit. Notice the selling words: pitch, value, offer, save, and worth.

Write down one feature and the benefit the speaker links to it. Then pitch your own service and turn one feature into a clear benefit out loud.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Making a Persuasive Sales Pitch"

Practical phrases for selling: turning features into benefits, selling the value, and using persuasive language. Review the new words: pitch, persuade, convince, value, offer, save, guarantee, and worth.

Note one example of "Not only ... but also ..." and one of "that is why". Practice each one out loud as if you are pitching to your own client.

YouTube
"Features vs Benefits -- How to Sell Anything"

A short, clear lesson on the secret of every sales pitch: a feature is what the product is, a benefit is what the client gets. Learn to link the two with "that is why" and to sell the value, not just the price.

After watching, record yourself giving a full mini-pitch: sell the value, link two features to benefits, remove the risk, and ask for the next step.
