Aula 19 -- Pre-class

The Sales Pitch -- Persuasive Language & Benefits

Open Block 4: learn to sell. Turn a feature (what the product is) into a benefit (what the client gets) with "that is why", add power with "Not only ... but also", sell the value (not the price), and ask for the next step. New vocabulary: pitch, persuade, convince, value, offer, save, guarantee, worth.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Pitch -- a short, persuasive talk to sell something
"Rafael gave a great sales pitch." (Apresentação de vendas)
Persuade -- to make someone want to do something
"I want to persuade the client to buy." (Persuadir / convencer)
Convince -- to make someone believe or agree
"The benefits will convince them." (Convencer)
Value -- how useful or worthwhile something is
"Show the client the value of your offer." (Valor)
Offer -- what you present for sale, with its terms
"This offer includes free support." (Oferta / proposta)
Save -- to use less time or money than before
"Our service will save you two hours a day." (Economizar / poupar)
Guarantee -- a promise it works, or your money back
"We offer a full guarantee, so there is no risk." (Garantia)
Worth -- a smart, good use of money or time
"This offer is really worth it." (Que vale a pena)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael gives a sales pitch to an important client. First, he sells the value: "This is not a cost, it is an investment." Then he links each feature to a benefit: "Our service is fast, and that is why it will save you two hours a day." He adds power: "Not only is it reliable, but it is also simple." To remove the risk, he says: "We offer a full guarantee." Finally, he wants to persuade and convince the client: "This offer is really worth it. Are you ready to start a trial?"

1. What does Rafael sell -- the price or the value?
A The value -- he calls it an investment.
B Only the price.
C Nothing at all.
2. How does Rafael turn the feature "fast" into a benefit?
A "It is fast, and that is why it will save you time."
B "It is fast." (and stops there)
C "It is the cheapest."
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Not only is it reliable, but it is also simple."
B "Not only it is reliable, but also simple."
C "Not only reliable is it, simple also."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Persuasive Language

GRAMMAR

Como transformar uma característica em benefício e vender com força. / How to turn a feature into a benefit and sell with power. Uma feature é o que o produto é; um benefit é o que o cliente ganha.

Tool / FerramentaPattern / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Link / Ligarfeature + that is why + benefitIt is fast, and that is why it will save you time.
Emphasis / ÊnfaseNot only ... but also ...Not only is it reliable, but it is also simple.
Value / Valornot a cost, it is an investmentThis is not a cost, it is an investment.
You / Vocêtalk about what the client getsYou will win back two hours a day.
Key point / Ponto-chaveDepois de Not only no início, o verbo vem antes do sujeito: diga is it, não it is. E é worth it, não worthy it.
