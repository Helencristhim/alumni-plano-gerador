Chapter 4: The Tense Toolkit

Three Tenses, One Story

Present simple, past simple, and comparatives together

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Every morning I study English before work."

"Last year I started to learn English again."

"My pronunciation is better than before."

"Yesterday I practiced for one hour."

TenseUse it forExample
Present simpleroutines and habits (now)I study every morning.
Past simplefinished actions in the pastI started, I practiced.
Comparativesprogress and changeMy English is better than before.

Routine? present simple. Finished action? past simple. Progress? comparative. Mix them in one story.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Tense

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"Every morning I ___study English." (study -- routine)
"Last year I ___started a new routine." (start -- finished action)
"My speaking is ___better than before." (good -- progress)
"Yesterday I ___practiced for an hour." (practice -- finished action)
"Now I am ___more confident than before." (confident -- progress)
Language Focus

Name the Tense

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I go to the class."

✓ RIGHT

"Yesterday I went to the class."

✗ WRONG

"My English is more good than before."

✓ RIGHT

"My English is better than before."

For finished past actions use the past simple (went, not go). The comparative of good is better, never "more good".
