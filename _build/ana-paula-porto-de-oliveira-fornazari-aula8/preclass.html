Midpoint Review -- How Far Have I Come?

A midpoint review of Block 1. Key words: journey, milestone, turning point, achieve, overcome, challenge, improve, routine, neighborhood, generous, unforgettable, habit. Grammar review: present simple for routines, past simple for finished actions, comparatives for progress.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These words came up across Lessons 1 to 7. Listen to each one and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Journey -- a long process of learning or change over time
"Learning English has been a long journey for me."
Milestone -- an important moment or achievement on the way
"My first full conversation was a big milestone."
Turning point -- a moment when an important change begins
"That class was a real turning point for me."
To achieve -- to succeed in doing something after effort
"I want to achieve fluency in English."
To overcome -- to succeed in dealing with a difficult problem
"She worked hard to overcome her fear of speaking."
Challenge -- something difficult that tests your ability
"Understanding fast speech is still a challenge."
To improve -- to get better at something
"My pronunciation has improved a lot this month."
Routine -- the things you do regularly, in the same order
"Studying every morning is part of my routine."
Neighborhood -- the area around your home
"I can now describe my neighborhood in English."
Generous -- happy to give time, help, or money to others
"My teacher is generous with her feedback."
Unforgettable -- so special that you cannot forget it
"My first full conversation was unforgettable."
Habit -- something you do often, almost without thinking
"Reading in English is now a daily habit."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A few months ago, I decided to change my life. English was a real challenge, and my old routine had no time for study. So I built a new habit: now I study every morning before work. My first full conversation was an unforgettable milestone. Slowly, I began to overcome my fear. Today my English is much better than before, and I am more confident than I was at the start.

1. Which sentence correctly describes a daily routine (present simple)?
A "I study every morning before work."
B "I studied every morning now."
C "I am study every morning."
2. Which sentence is in the past simple?
A "My first conversation was unforgettable."
B "My English is better now."
C "I study every day."
3. Which sentence correctly shows progress (comparative)?
A "My English is better than before."
B "My English is more good than before."
C "My English is gooder than before."

GRAMMAR

TenseUse it forExample
Present simpleroutines and habits (now)I study every morning.
Past simplefinished actions in the pastI started, I learned.
Comparativesprogress and changeMy English is better than before.
Irregular formsSome forms are irregular: go → went, good → better (never "more good").

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

Look back at your English journey so far. Where did you start? What was the biggest challenge? What milestone are you most proud of? How has your English improved? Mix the past simple (I started, I learned), the present simple (I study, I practice), and comparatives (better, more confident). Take your time.
