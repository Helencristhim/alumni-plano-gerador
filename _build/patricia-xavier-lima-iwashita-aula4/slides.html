Chapter 3: The Code

Direct vs Indirect Questions

What is the status? · Could you tell me what the status is?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me what the next step is?"

"Do you know if the report is ready?"

"I was wondering when the call starts."

"Can I ask who owns this task?"

What is the word order of the green words?

TypeStructureExample
Direct (Wh-)question word + verb + subjectWhat is the status?
Indirect (Wh-)opener + question word + subject + verbCould you tell me what the status is?
Direct (Yes/No)auxiliary + subject + verbIs the report ready?
Indirect (Yes/No)opener + if / whether + subject + verbDo you know if the report is ready?
OpenersCould you tell me... · Do you know... · I was wondering... · Can I ask... → no do/does/did after them: "when does it start" → "when it starts"

After the opener, use statement word order: subject first, then verb.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Could you tell me what is the status?"
"Could you tell me what the status is?"
"Do you know when does the call start?"
"Do you know when the call starts?"

After an opener like "Could you tell me..." or "Do you know...", use statement word order (subject + verb). Do not invert, and do not add do/does/did.

Chapter 4: Asking David

Get It Clear

You ask the questions on the call

Dialogue

The Question Call

P
David, before we start, could you tell me what the goal of this call is?
D
Sure. The first item on the agenda is the rollout status.
P
Thanks. Do you know if the security approval is ready?
D
Not yet. I will raise it with their team this morning.
P
Got it. One more thing — can I ask who owns the user training?
D
That is on your side. Let me recap the action items at the end.

Ask the Questions

Role-Play

Open the Call

Scenario: The call is starting and you are not sure of the plan. Ask David two polite questions to clarify the goal and the agenda.

Get the Details

Keywords:

Do you know if... I was wondering who... I have one concern about...
