Watch the meeting scenes where people clarify, confirm, and raise concerns. "Indirect Questions -- Polite English" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to turn a direct question ("What is the status?") into a polite indirect one ("Could you tell me what the status is?"). Watch for the word-order change and the use of "if/whether" for yes-no questions. Connection to Lesson 4: this is the grammar that makes your questions on calls sound polite and correct.

Tip: pause after each example and say the direct question first, then the indirect version out loud, using a real question you need to ask your team this week.
