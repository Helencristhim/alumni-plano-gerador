Asking Effective Questions on Calls

Asking clear, polite questions to get the answers you need on a call. Key words: clarify, confirm, double-check, rephrase, agenda, raise, address, concern, recap, detail. Structure: question forms & indirect questions.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Clarify -- to make something clearer and easier to understand (esclarecer)
"Could you clarify what you mean by that?"
Confirm -- to say that something is definitely true or agreed (confirmar)
"Let me confirm the date with you."
Double-check -- to check something again to be completely sure (verificar de novo)
"I want to double-check the deadline."
Rephrase -- to say something again in a different way (reformular)
"Could you rephrase that, please?"
Agenda -- the list of topics to discuss in a meeting (pauta)
"The first item on the agenda is the rollout."
Raise -- to bring up a topic or question to discuss (levantar / trazer à tona)
"I would like to raise one point."
Address -- to deal with a question, problem, or concern (tratar / lidar com)
"Let us address the security issue first."
Concern -- a worry or doubt you want to talk about (preocupação)
"I have one concern about the timeline."
Recap -- a short summary of the main points (recapitulação / resumo)
"Let me recap the action items."
Detail -- a specific, small piece of information (detalhe)
"Could you give me more detail on that?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Before the call with David, Patrícia prepares her questions. She wants to sound polite, so she uses indirect questions. Instead of "What is the deadline?", she asks, "Could you tell me what the deadline is?" Instead of "Is the approval ready?", she asks, "Do you know if the approval is ready?" She also writes, "I was wondering when the call starts," and "Can I ask who owns the training?" Notice the word order: after the opener, the subject comes first, then the verb — and there is no do or does.

1. Por que a forma indireta usa "what the deadline is" e não "what is the deadline"?
A Porque a pergunta indireta sempre usa o passado.
B Porque depois do opener usamos a ordem de afirmação: sujeito + verbo.
C Porque "deadline" é um verbo irregular.
2. Para uma pergunta de sim/não ("Is the approval ready?"), a forma indireta acrescenta:
A "if" ou "whether" antes do sujeito.
B "do" antes do sujeito.
C nada; a ordem fica igual à pergunta direta.
3. Which indirect question is correct?
A "Do you know when does the call start?"
B "Do you know when the call starts?"
C "Do you know when starts the call?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Direct & Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Como transformar uma pergunta direta numa indireta e educada (explicação em inglês e português).

TypeStructure / EstruturaExample
Direct (Wh-)Pergunta direta: palavra-Wh + verbo + sujeito.What is the status?
Indirect (Wh-)Educada: opener + Wh + sujeito + verbo.Could you tell me what the status is?
Direct (Yes/No)Pergunta direta de sim/não: auxiliar + sujeito.Is the report ready?
Indirect (Yes/No)Use if/whether: opener + if + sujeito + verbo.Do you know if the report is ready?
Openers & do/doesOpeners: Could you tell me... · Do you know... · I was wondering... · Can I ask... · Sem do/does depois: "when does it start" → "when it starts".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

You want to ask for the deadline politely. You say:
A "Could you tell me what the deadline is?"
B "Could you tell me what is the deadline?"
C "Could you tell me what the deadline does?"
You are not sure the report is ready. You ask:
A "Do you know is the report ready?"
B "Do you know if the report is ready?"
C "Do you know if is the report ready?"
You did not understand what David said. You ask him to:
A "Sorry, could you rephrase that, please?"
B "Sorry, could you raise that, please?"
C "Sorry, could you agenda that, please?"
At the end of the call, you want a short summary. You say:
A "Could you clarify the main points?"
B "Could you recap the main points?"
C "Could you concern the main points?"
