From São Paulo to Milan -- Your Professional Story

Tell your professional story from past to future, using narrative time linkers to connect the moments of your journey. Key words: journey, trajectory, milestone, turning point, vision, purpose, commitment, passion, legacy, to overcome. Linkers: Looking back..., At first..., Over the years..., As a result..., Looking ahead...

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Journey -- a long process of change and growth over time
"My professional journey started in Sao Paulo."
Trajectory -- the path your career follows over the years
"My trajectory took me from a small office to global missions."
Milestone -- an important moment or step in your story
"My first international client was a big milestone."
Turning point -- a moment when an important change begins
"My first mission abroad was my turning point."
Vision -- a clear picture of the future you want
"My vision is to reach new markets in Europe."
Purpose -- the reason why you do your work
"My purpose is to help small businesses grow."
Commitment -- a strong promise to keep doing something
"My commitment to my clients is strong."
Passion -- a very strong love and interest for something
"I work with passion every day."
Legacy -- something good you leave behind for other people
"I want to leave a strong legacy for small businesses."
To overcome -- to succeed in dealing with a hard problem
"At first, I had to overcome many problems."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is on stage at an event in Milan. She tells her professional journey. "Looking back, I started in Sao Paulo with one small idea and a lot of passion," she says. "At first, the work was hard, and I had to overcome many problems." She describes her trajectory and the important milestones on the way. "My turning point was my first international mission. As a result, today I lead missions for small businesses." Her commitment is strong, and her purpose is clear. "Looking ahead, my vision is to reach new markets," she says. "Above all, I want to leave a good legacy."

B "Look back I ..."
C "Back looking, ..."
2. What does "As a result" show?
A A question.
B A result of what happened before.
C A place.
3. Which linker does Flavia use to talk about the future?
A "Looking ahead, ..."
B "Looking back, ..."
C "At first, ..."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Narrative Time Linkers

GRAMMAR

How to connect the moments of your story, from the past to the future.

LinkerUseExample
Looking back, ...to talk about the pastLooking back, I started small.
At first, ...to talk about the beginningAt first, the work was hard.
Over the years, ...to talk about a long periodOver the years, I grew a lot.
As a result, ...to show a resultAs a result, I work abroad now.
Looking ahead, ...to talk about the future: "Looking ahead, I want to reach new markets." Always put a comma after the linker. Use the past for the past (started, grew) and "want to + verb" for the future.
