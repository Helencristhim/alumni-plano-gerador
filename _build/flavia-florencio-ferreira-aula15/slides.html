Chapter 3: Looking Back, Looking Ahead

Connect the Moments

looking back · as a result · looking ahead

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Looking back, I started with one small idea."

"Over the years, I reached many milestones."

"As a result, I now lead international missions."

"Looking ahead, I want to reach new markets."

Each orange linker opens the sentence and puts it in time. Which ones talk about the past? Which one shows a result? Which one talks about the future?

LinkerTimeExample
Looking back, ...the pastLooking back, I started small.
Over the years, ...a long periodOver the years, I grew a lot.
As a result, ...a resultAs a result, I work abroad now.
Looking ahead, ...the futureLooking ahead, I want to grow.

Always put a comma after the linker. Use the past for the past (started, grew) and want to + verb for the future.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Looking back I start small."
"Looking back, I started small."
"As a result I working abroad."
"As a result, I work abroad now."
"Looking ahead, I want reach Milan."
"Looking ahead, I want to reach Milan."

Put a comma after the linker. Use the past for the past, and want to + verb for the future.
