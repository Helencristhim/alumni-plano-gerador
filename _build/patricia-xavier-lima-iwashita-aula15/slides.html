Chapter 3: Four Skills

The Code of Block 3

Directness · Roadmap · Feedback · Follow-ups

Grammar Discovery

Four Jobs, Four Skills

"Could we go over the roadmap together?"

"We are launching the new feature in May."

"Perhaps it might be better to simplify the dashboard."

"Priya said she would book the training."

Four colors, four jobs. What is each sentence for?

SkillJobExample
Polite directness
Could we / Would you...		Clear, but respectful across culturesCould we go over the roadmap?
Future forms
will / going to / am-is-are + -ing		Plans and the roadmapWe are launching it in May.
Diplomatic softening
perhaps / it might be better to		Giving feedback gentlyPerhaps it might be better to simplify it.
Reported speech
said / told + backshift		Following up on what people agreedShe said she would book it.

In reported speech, will becomes would: "I will send it" → "She said she would send it."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She said she will send the notes."
"She said she would send the notes."
"We will launching the feature in May."
"We are launching the feature in May."
"Your dashboard is bad. Change it."
"Perhaps it might be better to simplify the dashboard."

In reported speech, backshift will to would. Use the Present Continuous for an arranged plan. And soften feedback with perhaps / it might be better to.

Chapter 4: The Global Sync

On the Sync with David

A global product sync, start to finish

Dialogue

The Global Sync

D
Thanks for joining across the time zones, Patrícia. Could we go over the roadmap first?
P
Of course. So, we are launching the new feature in May, and the next release is on track.
D
Great. One piece of feedback: the client deck had a lot of detail.
P
That is a fair point. Perhaps it might be better to start with the big picture. My takeaway is to simplify it.
D
Perfect. And what did Priya say about the training?
P
She said she would book it once the deck is ready. Let us align on the date and circle back by email.
