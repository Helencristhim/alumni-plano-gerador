Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Revisão do Bloco 3. Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Directness -- being clear and to the point, but polite (objetividade)
"In the US, directness is normal on a call."
Rapport -- a good, friendly connection with someone (sintonia)
"I build rapport before the meeting starts."
Time zone -- a region with the same standard time (fuso horário)
"We only share two hours across the time zones."
Roadmap -- a plan of what you will build and when (roteiro do produto)
"Let me walk you through the roadmap."
Release -- a new version you deliver to customers (versão/lançamento)
"The next release goes live in May."
Feature -- a function or part of the product (funcionalidade)
"The new reporting feature is almost ready."
Feedback -- comments on what is good and what to improve (feedback)
"Thanks for the feedback on the deck."
Takeaway -- the main point you keep from a meeting (conclusão principal)
"My main takeaway is to simplify the dashboard."
Align -- to agree and move in the same direction (alinhar)
"Let us align on the roadmap first."
Action item -- a task someone must do after a meeting (item de ação)
"Each action item has an owner and a date."
Owner -- the person responsible for a task (responsável)
"Priya is the owner of the training."
Circle back -- to return to a topic again later (retomar depois)
"Let us circle back on the dates by email."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Patrícia is getting ready for a global product sync with David and Priya. The team works across three time zones, so she keeps it short. She opens politely: "Could we go over the roadmap first?" Then she shares the plan: "We are launching the new feature in May." David gives some feedback, and she softens her reply: "Perhaps it might be better to simplify the deck." Finally, she reports a follow-up: "Priya said she would book the training." They align on the dates and agree to circle back by email.

1. Por que "We are launching the new feature in May" usa o present continuous?
A Porque a ação terminou no passado.
B Porque é um plano combinado para o futuro.
C Porque é uma rotina de toda semana.
2. "Priya said she would book the training" usa o reported speech porque:
A Relata o que a Priya disse; "will" vira "would".
B É uma pergunta indireta educada.
C É uma ação acontecendo agora.
3. Why does Patrícia say "Perhaps it might be better to simplify the deck"?
A To give an exact deadline.
B To soften the feedback and stay diplomatic.
C To report what someone else said.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Four Skills of Block 3

GRAMMAR

As quatro habilidades que você usa num sync global (explicação em inglês e português).

SkillUse / UsoExample
Polite directness
Could we / Would you...		Claro, mas respeitoso entre culturas. Clear but respectful.Could we go over the roadmap?
Future forms
will / going to / am-is-are + -ing		Planos e o roadmap. Plans and the roadmap.We are launching it in May.
Diplomatic softening
perhaps / it might be better to		Dar feedback com diplomacia. Giving feedback gently.Perhaps it might be better to simplify it.
Reported speech
said / told + backshift (will→would)		Relatar follow-ups e o que ficou combinado. Reporting follow-ups.She said she would book it.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

You open the roadmap part of a global sync. The most polite, direct option is:
A "Could we go over the roadmap first?"
B "Go over the roadmap now."
C "Roadmap. Please."
You describe a planned launch. Which is correct?
A "We will launching the feature in May."
B "We are launching the feature in May."
C "We are launch the feature in May."
A teammate's deck is too long. The most diplomatic feedback is:
A "Perhaps it might be better to start with the big picture."
B "Your deck is bad."
C "This deck is wrong, change it."
You report what Priya promised. You say:
A "Priya said she will book the training."
B "Priya said she would book the training."
