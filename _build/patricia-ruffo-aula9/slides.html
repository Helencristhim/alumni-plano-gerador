Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Concede vs. stand firm: I concede that ... but I stand by ...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"I recognize the concern, but I stand by the central finding."

"While it is true the sample is small, the result is still robust."

"I concede that we need more data; however, the core conclusion holds."

"That said, our finding agrees with two independent studies."

Grammar Rule

Concede, Then Stand Firm

MoveExample
Concede a fair pointI recognize the concern about the sample.
Concede with a clauseWhile it is true the data is limited, ...
Turn and contrast..., but / however the result is robust.
Stand by the coreI stand by the central finding.

Key idea: under pressure, the strongest answer is concede + contrast + defend. Give the listener a fair point with I recognize or I concede that, turn with but, however or that said, then stand by the core result.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I concede to need more long-term data."
"I concede that we need more long-term data."

After concede and recognize, use that + a clause, not the infinitive.

Grammar Practice

Concede and Defend

"I _____recognize the concern, but I stand by the finding." (concede a point)
"While it is true the sample is small, the result is _____robust." (stand firm)
"I concede _____that we need more data; however, the conclusion holds." (correct connector)
"That said, our finding survives further _____scrutiny." (critical examination)
