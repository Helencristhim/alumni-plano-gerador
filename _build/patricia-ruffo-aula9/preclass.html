Defending Your Data Under Pressure

Answering tough questions calmly: clarifying your data, conceding a fair point and standing by your robust result. New vocabulary: clarify, recognize, stand by, rebuttal, anecdotal, robust, address, scrutiny.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clarify -- to explain something so it is easier to understand
"Let me clarify what the data actually shows." (Esclarecer)
Recognize -- to accept that a point or fact is true or fair
"I recognize the concern about the control group." (Reconhecer)
Stand by -- to keep supporting and defending something you said
"I stand by the central finding of the study." (Manter / sustentar)
Rebuttal -- a reply that argues against a criticism
"Her rebuttal answered every objection clearly." (Réplica / refutação)
Anecdotal -- based on personal stories, not on careful research
"That claim is anecdotal, not statistical." (Anedótico / casual)
Robust -- strong, reliable and hard to disprove
"Our result is robust across every test." (Robusto / sólido)
Address -- to deal with a problem or concern directly
"We addressed that concern in the analysis." (Tratar / abordar)
Scrutiny -- careful and critical examination of something
"The data held up under heavy scrutiny." (Escrutínio / exame rigoroso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

During the Q&A, a reviewer challenges Patricia. First, she stays calm and decides to clarify exactly what the data shows. She does not get defensive. Instead, she chooses to recognize the fair point about the control group, and she explains that the team already addressed it in the supplementary analysis. The reviewer says the evidence sounds anecdotal, but Patricia has a strong rebuttal: the result is robust and held up under repeated scrutiny. So while she concedes that more long-term data is needed, she still decides to stand by the central conclusion, because it agrees with two independent studies.

1. What does Patricia do first under pressure?
A She stays calm and clarifies what the data shows.
B She gets defensive and refuses to answer.
C She agrees that the whole study is wrong.
2. How does she respond to the "anecdotal" comment?
A She says nothing and changes the subject.
B She gives a rebuttal: the result is robust and held up under scrutiny.
C She admits the result was just luck.
3. What does she concede, and what does she stand by?
A She concedes everything and stands by nothing.
B She concedes more data is needed, but stands by the central conclusion.
C She stands by the sample size and ignores the conclusion.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Concede, then stand firm

GRAMMAR

Sob pressão, conceda um ponto justo e depois sustente o resultado central. / Under pressure, concede a fair point and then stand firm on your core result.

Move / MovimentoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Concede a fair pointI recognize / I concede that + clause"I recognize the concern about the sample."
Concede with a clauseWhile it is true (that) ..."While it is true the data is limited, ..."
Turn and contrastbut / however / that said"..., however the result is robust."
Stand by the coreI stand by + noun"I stand by the central finding."

Key point / Ponto-chave: A resposta mais forte sob pressão é conceder + contrastar + defender. Use I recognize ou I concede that + oração (nunca infinitivo), vire com but, however ou that said, e então stand by o resultado central.
