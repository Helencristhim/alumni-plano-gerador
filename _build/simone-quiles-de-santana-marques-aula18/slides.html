Chapter 3 -- The Second Conditional

If we lowered, we would...

Talking about possible, imaginary deals

Listen and Notice

What Do They Share?

Look at the highlighted words. What verb forms come after "if" and in the other part?

"If we lowered the price, we would close the deal today."

"If they asked for a discount, we would offer a longer contract."

"If we gave them flexible terms, they would accept the price."

"We would make a concession if they moved on the deadline."

The Second Conditional Code

If + Past, Would

If-partResult-partExample
If + past simplewould + verbIf we lowered the price, we would close.
If + past simplewould + verbIf they asked, we would offer a discount.
would + verb (first)if + past simpleWe would agree if they moved on price.
if + were (all persons)would + verbIf I were you, I would make a counteroffer.

Key idea: the second conditional talks about an IMAGINARY or unlikely situation: if + past simple, would + verb. It is different from the first conditional (if + present, will), which talks about something real and likely.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we would lower the price, we would to close the deal."
"If we lowered the price, we would close the deal."

After if use the past simple (lowered), and in the result use would + base verb (would close).

About Daniel's Briefing

Dialogue

Negotiating the Supply Deal

P

"Thank you for meeting us, Simone. The price is high. We would like to bargain a little. Could you offer a discount?"

S

"The price is our bottom line, but I have a counteroffer. If you signed a longer contract, we would lower the price."

P

"A longer contract is a big concession for us. If we agreed to it, would you be flexible on the payment dates?"

S

"Yes. That is a fair trade-off. Our leverage is fast delivery, so if you accepted the price, we would deliver two weeks early."

P

"We were close to a deadlock a moment ago, but this works. This is a mutual deal: good for both sides."

S

"I agree. We have a deal. I will put the new terms in writing, and we can sign this week."

About the Negotiation

1. What counteroffer did Simone make to the other side?

She said the price was the bottom line, but if they signed a longer contract, she would lower the price.

2. What concession did the partner ask for, and what was the trade-off?

The partner asked Simone to be flexible on the payment dates. The trade-off was a longer contract and flexible payment in exchange for the price.

3. How did they avoid a deadlock and close the deal?

They used trade-offs and stayed flexible, so they found a mutual deal that was good for both sides and agreed to sign that week.
