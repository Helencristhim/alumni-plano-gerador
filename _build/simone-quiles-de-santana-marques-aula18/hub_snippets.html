Negotiating Terms -- Bargaining & the Second Conditional

Learn the language of negotiating a deal: make a counteroffer, ask for a concession, propose a trade-off and close a mutual deal. New vocabulary: counteroffer, concession, leverage, bargain, trade-off, deadlock, flexible, mutual. Grammar: the second conditional -- if + past simple, would + verb (If we lowered the price, we would close today).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Counteroffer -- a different offer you make in reply to the other side's offer
"They offered ten, so we made a counteroffer of eight." (Contraproposta)
Concession -- something you give up so both sides can reach a deal
"We made one concession on the deadline." (Concessão)
Leverage -- the power or advantage that helps you get a better deal
"Our fast delivery gives us leverage in the talks." (Vantagem / poder de barganha)
Bargain -- to discuss terms back and forth until you agree on a price
"They like to bargain before they sign anything." (Negociar / pechinchar)
Trade-off -- a balance where you give up one thing to gain another
"A lower price for a longer contract is a fair trade-off." (Compensação / troca)
Deadlock -- a point where neither side will move and the talks are stuck
"We reached a deadlock on price, so we changed the topic." (Impasse)
Flexible -- willing to change or adapt your terms to reach a deal
"We can be flexible on the payment dates." (Flexível)
Mutual -- shared by both sides; good for everyone involved
"We found a mutual solution that worked for both companies." (Mútuo / recíproco)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Simone negotiates the supply deal. The other side wants a discount, but the price is the bottom line. Simone makes a counteroffer: if they signed a longer contract, she would lower the price. A longer contract is a big concession, so the partner asks Simone to be flexible on the payment dates. That is a fair trade-off. Simone's leverage is fast delivery: if they accepted the price, she would deliver two weeks early. They were close to a deadlock, but in the end they find a mutual solution and close the deal.

3. How do they close the deal?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

O second conditional fala de uma situação imaginária ou pouco provável. Estrutura: If + passado simples, ... would + verbo base. Diga "If we lowered the price, we would close today" (não "if we would lower" nem "we would to close"). Com o verbo to be, use were para todas as pessoas: "If I were you...". É diferente do first conditional (Aula 14: if + presente, will), que fala de algo real e provável. / The second conditional talks about an imaginary or unlikely situation. Structure: If + past simple, ... would + base verb. Say "If we lowered the price, we would close today" (not "if we would lower" or "we would to close"). With to be, use were for all persons: "If I were you...". It is different from the first conditional (if + present, will), which talks about something real and likely.

If-part / Parte com ifResult / ResultadoExample / Exemplo
If + past simplewould + verbIf we lowered the price, we would close.
If + past simplewould + verbIf they asked, we would offer a discount.
would + verb (first)if + past simpleWe would agree if they moved on price.
If + were (all persons)would + verbIf I were you, I would make a counteroffer.
