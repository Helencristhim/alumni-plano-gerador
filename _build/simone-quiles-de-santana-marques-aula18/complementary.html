Lesson 18 -- Negotiating Terms

Series
Suits -- Season 2, Episode 1 "She Knows"

Watch how the lawyers bargain over the terms of a deal: making counteroffers, trading concessions and using leverage to avoid a deadlock. Listen for second conditional sentences they use to picture possible outcomes: "If we offered X, they would accept Y".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences where a character uses "if + past, would" to make an offer, and underline the trade-off in each one.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Negotiating in English: Making and Responding to Offers"

A practical episode on the language of negotiation: stating your bottom line, making a counteroffer, asking for a concession, proposing a trade-off and finding a mutual solution. Exactly the language you need for the supply and M&A deals at the office.

Note 5 negotiation phrases the hosts use, and mark which ones are in the second conditional (if + past, would).

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "The Second Conditional"

A short, clear lesson on the grammar from today: if + past simple, would + verb, to talk about imaginary or unlikely situations. Directly useful for picturing possible deals at the negotiation table.

After watching, write five second conditional sentences for a negotiation (price, contract, payment, delivery, deadline), then record yourself reading them with at least four new words from today.
