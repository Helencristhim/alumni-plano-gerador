Chapter 3 -- Asking for Help

Fix the Sound & Screen

Say the problem, then ask for the fix

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Which part says the problem, and which part asks for the fix?

"I can't hear you. Can you unmute?"

"I can't see you. Can you turn on your camera?"

"Can you share your screen?"

"There is an echo. Could you mute, please?"

Grammar Rule

Say the Problem & Ask for the Fix

Use it forPatternExample
Say the problemI can't + verbI can't hear you.
Say the problemI can't + verbI can't see you.
Ask for the fixCan you + verb?Can you unmute?
Ask politelyCould you + verb, please?Could you share your screen, please?

Key idea: use I can't + verb to say the problem (I can't hear you), and Can you + verb? to ask for the fix (Can you unmute?). After can't and can you we use the base verb -- hear, see, unmute, share.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I can't to see you. Can you to share your screen?"
"I can't see you. Can you share your screen?"

After can't and can you we use the base verb, with no to and no -ing: I can't see you, Can you unmute?
