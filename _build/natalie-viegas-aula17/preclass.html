Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Moderate -- to lead and guide a meeting, keeping it on track
"Natalie will moderate the status meeting this week." (Conduzir / mediar)
Steer -- to guide a discussion in the right direction
"A good moderator steers the conversation gently." (Direcionar / guiar)
Summary -- a short version of the main points so far
"Let me start with a quick summary of last week." (Resumo)
Agenda item -- one single topic on the list of things to discuss
"The next agenda item is the testing timeline." (Item da pauta)
Raise a point -- to bring up a topic or question for the group to discuss
"Thanks for raising that point." (Levantar uma questão)
Off-topic -- not connected to the topic the group is discussing now
"That is a bit off-topic, so let's park it." (Fora do assunto)
Defer -- to decide to deal with something later, not right now
"Let's defer that question to the end of the call." (Adiar / postergar)
Double-check -- to check a detail again to be sure it is correct
"Let me double-check that number and get back to you." (Conferir / verificar)
Follow-up -- a task that someone agrees to do after the meeting
"I'll log that as a follow-up with an owner." (Tarefa / ação a fazer)
Close out -- to finish and end a meeting, summarizing the next steps
"Let's close out: here are the next steps." (Encerrar / finalizar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Natalie is going to moderate the status meeting. She plans to open with a quick summary, then steer each agenda item. To sound polite, she will use indirect questions: "Could you tell me how the testing is going?" and "I would like to know whether the budget is approved." When a colleague raises a point that is off-topic, she will defer it and log it as a follow-up. If she is not sure of a number, she will not guess; she will double-check it after the call. Finally, she will close out with the next steps and the owners.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Usamos perguntas indiretas para soar mais educados e profissionais. Abrimos com Could you tell me.../Do you know.../I would like to know..., e depois usamos a ORDEM NORMAL (sujeito + verbo), sem do/does/did. Para perguntas de sim/não, usamos if ou whether. / We use indirect questions to sound more polite. Open with Could you tell me.../Do you know.../I would like to know..., then use NORMAL word order (subject + verb), with no do/does/did. For yes/no questions, use if or whether.

OpenerThenExample
Could you tell me + question wordsubject + verb (normal)"...how the testing is going."
Do you know + question wordno do/does/did inside"...when the call starts."
I would like to know + if / whetherfor yes/no questions"...whether the budget is approved."
Direct questionverb before subject"How is the testing going?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois do opener, use a ordem normal (sujeito + verbo) e NUNCA do/does/did dentro da pergunta. Errado: "Could you tell me where is the report?" Certo: "Could you tell me where the report is?" Para sim/não, use if ou whether (I would like to know whether the budget is approved).
