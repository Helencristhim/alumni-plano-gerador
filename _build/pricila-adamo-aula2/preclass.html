Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story of Pricila's turning point and answer the questions. Notice which action happened first.

By the time Pricila finally decided to step back from the clinic, she had been dreaming of change for years. The early days had brought more than one setback, and at first the idea felt daunting. But she had always trusted her gut feeling. In hindsight, selling part of the practice was a real leap of faith -- yet it paved the way for a far more rewarding chapter. When she looked back, she realized that one quiet decision had become the defining moment of her whole life.

1. Which action happened FIRST?
A She had been dreaming of change (before she decided to step back).
B She decided to step back, then started dreaming.
C Both happened at the exact same time.
2. What tense marks the earlier of two past actions here?
A Present perfect ("has decided").
B Past perfect ("had become", "had been dreaming").
C Future ("will become").
3. How does Pricila describe that quiet decision?
A As a forgettable mistake.
B As the defining moment of her life.
C As a setback she never overcame.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Perfect (Narrative Tenses)

Grammar

Quando duas acoes acontecem no passado, use o past perfect (had + particicio) para a que veio ANTES, e o past simple para a que veio depois. Para uma acao anterior que estava em andamento, use had been + verbo-ing. / When two actions happen in the past, use the past perfect (had + past participle) for the EARLIER one, and the past simple for the later one. For an earlier ongoing action, use had been + verb-ing.

Forma / FormEnglishPortugues
Past perfect (earlier action)By the time I arrived, she had left.Quando cheguei, ela ja tinha saido.
Past perfect + past simpleShe opened a new shop after she had sold the old one.Ela abriu uma loja nova depois de ter vendido a antiga.
Past perfect continuousI had been dreaming of change for years before I left.Eu vinha sonhando com mudanca havia anos antes de sair.
NegativeI had not realized how much I needed it.Eu nao tinha percebido o quanto eu precisava daquilo.
Common mistakeNOT "When I arrived, she left." (if she left first) -- say "she had left".A acao anterior leva had + particicio.
