Chapter 3: Before and After

What Had Happened First?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"By the time I arrived, she had left."

"She opened a new shop after she had sold the old one."

"I had been dreaming of change for years before I left."

"When the fire happened, she had already decided to start over."

TenseUseExample
Past perfectthe earlier of two past actionsShe had left before I arrived.
Past perfect continuousan earlier action, ongoingI had been waiting for hours.
Past simplethe later / main actionThen the door opened.

Pattern: had + past participle for the earlier action; had been + -ing for an earlier ongoing action. The later action stays in the past simple.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

When I arrived, she already left.
When I arrived, she had already left.

Use the past perfect (had left) for the action that happened first.

By then, she had went to Paris.
By then, she had gone to Paris.

After had, always use the past participle (gone, not went).

Chapter 4: A Life in Chapters

Telling the Story

Dialogue

A Turning Point

I
Pricila, can you tell me about a defining moment in your life?
P
Of course. By the time I turned fifty, I had been running my clinic for nearly twenty years, and I had a real setback -- my health forced me to slow down.
I
That must have felt daunting. How did you overcome it?
P
In hindsight, it was a pivotal moment. I had been dreaming of travel for years, so I took a leap of faith and embarked on a new chapter.
I
And looking back now, do you have any regrets?
P
None at all. Against all odds, I put the old life behind me, and this chapter has been the most rewarding of them all.
