Complementary Materials — Lesson 14

Series
How It's Made — any episode

Every episode describes how a product is built, step by step, almost entirely in the passive voice. Listen for "is assembled", "are inspected", "is monitored" and "is shipped".

Tip: pause after each step and repeat it as a passive sentence in your own words.

Podcast
TED Talks Daily — episodes on how things are designed

Speakers explain how systems and products are designed, tested and improved. Notice how the passive keeps the focus on the process, not the person, and where "by" is added.

Tip: write down three passive sentences from the talk, one in the past with "by + doer".

YouTube
BBC Learning English — "the passive voice"

A short, clear video on exactly this lesson's grammar: how to form the passive with be + past participle, and when to drop or keep the doer with "by".

Tip: after watching, describe how your phone is updated using five passive sentences.
