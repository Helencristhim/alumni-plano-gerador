Assemble — to put parts together to make a complete thing

Submit — to send something to be checked or approved
"The form is submitted online." (enviar/submeter)
Assign — to give a task or job to a person
"Each step is assigned to a team." (atribuir)
Monitor — to watch and check something over a period of time
"The system is monitored all night." (monitorar)
Verify — to check that something is correct or true
"Every payment is verified before use." (verificar)
Trigger — to cause an action or reaction to start
"An error triggers an alert." (disparar/desencadear)
Handle — to deal with a task, problem or situation
"Complaints are handled quickly." (lidar com)
Dispose of — to get rid of something you no longer need
"Old files are disposed of safely." (descartar)
Streamline — to make a process simpler and more efficient
"The whole flow is streamlined." (otimizar)
Bottleneck — a point in a process where everything slows down
"Testing is the main bottleneck." (gargalo)
Glitch — a small, temporary fault in a machine or system
"A glitch is fixed in seconds." (falha/bug)
Rollout — the launch of a new product, system or update
"The rollout is started slowly." (lançamento)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Mark explains how an app update is built. First, the new code is written and then it is submitted for review. Once it is approved, every feature is inspected, and that stage is the main bottleneck. The rollout is started slowly: a small group is monitored first, and if a glitch is triggered, the release is paused. Old files are disposed of safely. In the past, one update was approved by the lead engineer, and the bugs were handled overnight. Notice that the form is always a form of be plus the past participle, and the tense lives in the verb be.

1. What is the form of the passive voice?
A be + past participle
B have + past participle
C will + base verb
2. Where does the tense (present or past) live?
A in the past participle
B in the verb "be" (is/are or was/were)
C in the word "by"
3. How is the doer added when it matters?
A with "from"
B with "by"
C with "of"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — the passive voice

GRAMMAR

Para descrever um processo ou instruções, o inglês usa a voz passiva: uma forma do verbo "be" + particípio passado. / To describe a process or instructions, English uses the passive voice: a form of "be" + the past participle.

Tense / TempoStructureExample
Present singular / presenteis + p.p.The order is processed.
Present plural / presenteare + p.p.The files are submitted.
Past singular / passadowas + p.p.The system was monitored.
Past + doer / com o agentewas/were + p.p. + byIt was approved by the manager.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o tempo verbal fica no be (is/are = presente, was/were = passado), e o verbo principal vira sempre o particípio passado. O agente (quem faz) entra no fim com by, mas só quando importa — num processo, ele costuma ser omitido porque a ação importa mais que a pessoa.

Stage 2: Put the Steps in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of building a passive sentence in the correct order.

?After that, you add the past participle of the main verb, like processed, assembled or handled.
?First, you decide what the result of the action is, because in the passive the result comes first.
?Finally, if the doer is obvious or general, you simply leave it out.
?Then, you choose the correct form of be: is or are for the present, was or were for the past.
?Next, if the doer is important, you add it at the end with the word by.
