Chapter 4: Verb Patterns

Gerund or Infinitive

suggest + -ing · decide + to · preposition + -ing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I suggest starting with the sales figures."

"We decided to postpone the launch."

"Thank you all for coming today."

One verb is followed by the -ing form, one by to + infinitive, and one word is a preposition followed by -ing. Which pattern does each one use?

FormUseExample
Verb + -ingafter suggest, recommend, avoid, consider, keep, finishI suggest reviewing the budget first.
Verb + to + infinitiveafter decide, agree, plan, need, offer, hope, wantWe agreed to meet again next week.
Preposition + -ingafter for / before / after, and 'look forward to'Thanks for coming. I look forward to hearing from you.

Some verbs take the -ing form (suggest, recommend, avoid, consider, keep), others take to + infinitive (decide, agree, plan, need, offer, hope). After any preposition -- including the 'to' in 'look forward to' -- always use -ing. Learn each verb with its pattern.

Language Focus

Choose the Right Pattern

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I suggest to start with the budget."

✓ RIGHT

I suggest starting with the budget.

✗ WRONG

"We decided postponing the launch."

✓ RIGHT

We decided to postpone the launch.

'Suggest' and 'recommend' are always followed by the -ing form, never by 'to'. 'Decide', 'agree', and 'plan' are always followed by to + infinitive. Learning the verb with its pattern is the fastest way to stop guessing.

Chapter 5: Voices

Voices in the Boardroom

Listen to a manager close a meeting, then run your own out loud.

Register & Tone

Sound Professional

Tap to see the polished, professional version

Instead of: "Ok, we're done, bye."
Say: That covers the agenda. Thank you all for coming -- meeting adjourned.
Instead of: "Just figure out the contract, someone."
Say: Let's assign the contract follow-up, with an owner and a deadline.
Instead of: "We're not deciding this today, moving on."
Say: This topic needs more work, so I suggest moving it to the next meeting.
Instead of: "Everybody get it? Good."
Say: Let me recap the takeaways so we're all on the same page.
Dialogue

Andrea Wraps It Up

A
Alright, I think we've covered everything on the agenda. Sven, before I adjourn, let me recap so we're all on the same page.
S
Please do. I want to make sure we agreed on the group rates for next season.
A
We did. The main takeaway is that we'll offer a five percent discount, and we reached consensus on that. I suggest reviewing the final numbers by email before we sign.
S
Sounds good. And who is handling the contract?
A
That's an action item for me. I'll assign the follow-up to my team and plan to send you a draft on Thursday.
S
Perfect. Can you also send the minutes so I can share them with my board?
A
Of course. Thank you all for coming. I think this was a good use of everyone's time. Let's plan to meet again once the draft is ready. Meeting adjourned.

The Record

Meeting Minutes

Meeting: Escape Turismo -- Rates Review, next season
Chair: Andrea Aggio -- Commercial Director
Attendees: A. Aggio, S. (partner agency), sales team

Objective: to approve the group rates for next season and agree on next steps.

Decisions: the group reached consensus on a five percent discount. The marketing decision was postponed to the next meeting, as the team agreed it needs more work.

Action items: Andrea to send the contract draft by Thursday and assign the follow-up to her team. Chair to circulate these minutes to all attendees. Next meeting: to be confirmed once the draft is reviewed.

Pronunciation

Say It Naturally

Listen for the linking, then repeat twice

"I suggest starting with the numbers."

'suggest starting' links -- suh-JEST-STAR-ting; keep the -ing crisp.

"We agreed to postpone the launch."

'agreed to' reduces -- uh-GREED-tuh; stress post-PONE.

"Thank you all for coming."

'for coming' links -- fer-CUM-ing; warm, falling tone to close.

"Let's plan to meet again next week."

'plan to meet' -- plan-tuh-MEET; light stress on MEET and WEEK.
