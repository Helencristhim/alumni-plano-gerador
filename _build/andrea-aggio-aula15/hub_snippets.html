Leading and Wrapping Up a Business Meeting

How to open, steer, and close a business meeting using gerund and infinitive verb patterns. Key words: agenda, to chair, objective, attendee, minutes, action item, to recap, takeaway, consensus, on the same page, to assign, to adjourn. Structure: verb + -ing (suggest, recommend) vs verb + to + infinitive (decide, agree, plan); preposition + -ing.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Agenda -- the list of items to be discussed in a meeting (pauta)
"Let's stick to the agenda so we finish on time."
To chair -- to lead and run a meeting (presidir, conduzir)
"Andrea will chair today's meeting."
Objective -- the specific result you want to achieve (objetivo)
"The objective today is to approve the budget."
Attendee -- a person who is present at a meeting (participante, presente)
"We had twelve attendees on the call."
Minutes -- the official written record of a meeting (ata)
"I'll send the minutes by tomorrow morning."
Action item -- a specific task assigned during a meeting (tarefa, ponto de acao)
"Each action item has an owner and a deadline."
To recap -- to summarize the main points quickly (recapitular)
"Before we close, let me recap what we agreed."
Takeaway -- the key point or conclusion to remember (conclusao principal)
"The main takeaway is that demand is rising."
Consensus -- general agreement across the group (consenso)
"We reached consensus on the new rates."
On the same page -- in full agreement, sharing one understanding (alinhados)
"Let's make sure everyone is on the same page."
To assign -- to give a task or responsibility to someone (atribuir, designar)
"I'll assign the follow-up to the sales team."
To adjourn -- to formally close or end a meeting (encerrar, suspender)
"If there's nothing else, we can adjourn."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea chairs the meeting. 'The objective today is to approve the new rates,' she says, 'so let's stick to the agenda.' After the discussion, the group reaches consensus, and everyone is on the same page. 'I suggest reviewing the final numbers by email,' Andrea adds, 'and we agreed to send a draft by Thursday.' Before she adjourns, she recaps the main takeaway and confirms each action item. 'I'll assign the follow-up to my team, and I'll send the minutes to every attendee. Thank you all for coming.'

1. 'I suggest reviewing the numbers' uses which form, and why?
A The -ing form -- 'suggest' is always followed by a gerund.
B To + infinitive -- 'suggest' is followed by 'to'.
C Present simple -- it is a fact.

A suggest
B agree
C avoid
3. Which sentence is correct?
A Thank you for coming to the meeting.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerund vs Infinitive

GRAMMAR

Como escolher entre -ing e to + infinitivo depois de certos verbos (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
Verb + -ingapos suggest, recommend, avoid, consider, keep. after these verbs, use the -ing form.I suggest reviewing the budget.
Verb + to + infinitiveapos decide, agree, plan, need, offer, hope. after these verbs, use to + infinitive.We agreed to meet again.
Preposition + -ingapos qualquer preposicao (for, before, after, 'look forward to'). always -ing.Thanks for coming.
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleaprenda o verbo junto com seu padrao. learn the verb with its pattern.suggest + -ing vs decide + to.
