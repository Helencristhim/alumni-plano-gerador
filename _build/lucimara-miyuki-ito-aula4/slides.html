Chapter 3: The Code

If · Unless · As Long As

The grammar of a plan B — and of every negotiation you have ever run.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If my flight is delayed, I will ask for a voucher."

"If they cancel the flight, they will rebook me at no extra cost."

"Unless they find me a seat tonight, I will end up sleeping at the airport."

"I will be fine as long as I board before midnight."

The blue half is the future. So why is the verb in the orange half in the present?

PieceFormExample
The conditionif + present simple — never "will" hereIf the flight is delayed...
The resultwill + base verb...I will ask for a voucher.
unless= if not. The negative is already inside it.Unless they rebook me, I will file a complaint.
as long as
provided that		= only on this one condition. Same grammar as "if".I'll be fine as long as I board before midnight.
The orderEither half can come first. Comma only when the condition opens the sentence: "If it is delayed, I will ask." / "I will ask if it is delayed."

This is the first conditional: a real, possible future. Not a dream — a plan B.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the flight will be canceled, I will ask for a voucher."
"If the flight is canceled, I will ask for a voucher."
"Unless they don't rebook me, I will file a complaint."
"Unless they rebook me, I will file a complaint."

After if, English uses the present — even when you are talking about tomorrow. And unless already means if not: a second negative turns your sentence upside down.

Detail

Where Does the "Will" Go?

The condition half never takes "will" — only the result half does

"If the flight ___is delayed, I will ask for a voucher." (be — the condition)
"If the flight is delayed, I ___will ask for a voucher." (ask — the result)
"I ___will be fine as long as I board before midnight." (be — the result)
"Unless they ___rebook me tonight, I will look for an alternative route." (rebook — the condition)

Chapter 5: Practice

Train the Ear

The gate · The phone call · And what to say when the plan breaks

Listening 1

The Announcement at the Gate

Sixty people stop talking at the same time. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. Why is the flight canceled?
Because of the weather in the northeast.
2. If you have a meeting tomorrow morning, what should you do?
Go to the desk — they will rebook you at no extra cost.
3. What is different if you booked through a travel agent?
You have to call the agent directly — the airline cannot change that reservation at the gate.
4. Who gets a hotel voucher, and who gets only a meal voucher?
Every passenger gets a meal voucher; only passengers with a long wait also get a hotel voucher.
Listening 2

The Airline Calls You Back

Good news, and one condition. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What has Karen already done?
She has already rebooked her through Charlotte — the seat is confirmed.
2. What time does she board in Miami, and what time does she land at JFK?
She boards at five o'clock and lands at Kennedy at six ten in the morning.
3. What is the condition — and what happens if she misses it?
She must check in at the counter before four thirty. If she is not there by four thirty, the system will release her seat.
4. Why does she have to check in at the counter and not online?
Because a different airline operates the first part of the trip.
Strategy

When the Plan Breaks

Don't ask for a favor. Ask for the options — and offer a condition.

What are my options if the next flight is full?
If you can get me there before eight, I'll take any route.
Let me check I understood: I board at five, and I check in again before four thirty.
Who can I talk to about compensation? I'd like to file a complaint tonight.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

The Rebooking Desk

Dialogue

Eleven at Night in Miami

B
Next, please. How can I help you tonight?
L
Good evening. My flight to New York has been canceled, and I have a meeting in Manhattan at nine tomorrow morning. What are my options tonight?
B
Let me look. The next direct flight is overbooked, I'm afraid, and the one after that is full too. Nothing before tomorrow afternoon.
L
Tomorrow afternoon is too late for me. If you can get me into JFK before eight in the morning, I'll take any route — I don't need a direct flight.
B
All right. Then I can rebook you through Charlotte. You board here at five in the morning, and you land at Kennedy at six ten.
L
That works. As long as I'm in Manhattan by eight, I'll be fine. And what happens if that flight is late too?
B
Then we sort it out in Charlotte — your connection is protected, so if you miss it, we put you on the next one. Here is a meal voucher and a hotel voucher for tonight. The shuttle leaves from door three, every twenty minutes.
L
Thank you. Sorry, I didn't catch the door number for the shuttle. And one more thing: if I want to request compensation for tonight, where do I file the complaint?
Comprehension

What Did Brian Say?

1. What does Brian say about the next two direct flights?
The next one is overbooked, and the one after that is full — nothing before tomorrow afternoon.
2. Which route does he offer, and at what time does she board?
Through Charlotte — she boards in Miami at five in the morning and lands at Kennedy at six ten.
3. What does he say will happen if the Charlotte flight is late too?
They will sort it out in Charlotte — the connection is protected, so if she misses it, they put her on the next one.
4. What does he give her, and where does the shuttle leave from?
A meal voucher and a hotel voucher — the shuttle leaves from door three, every twenty minutes.
