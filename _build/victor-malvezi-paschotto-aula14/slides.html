Chapter 3

The Grammar

Facts in the past, commitment in the future

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Three lines — what is each one doing with time?

"We missed the target this quarter." (the bad news — a past fact, active voice)

"We will close the shortfall by quarter end." (firm promise — will)

"We're meeting the supplier on Thursday." (arranged step — be + -ing)

Honest about the past, concrete about the future.

The Rule

The Damage-Control Toolkit

MoveStructureExample
Lead inSoftening opener + the plain fact"There's no easy way to say this: we came in below plan."
Own it (active)We + past simple, active voice + I take responsibility"We missed the target, and I take responsibility for it."
Firm promisewill + base verb"We will close the shortfall by quarter end."
Arranged stepgoing to / be + -ing (a fixed plan)"We're meeting the supplier on Thursday."
ContingencyIf + present simple, … will + base (first conditional)"If the pilot slips, we will pull the stopgap budget."

The key thing to keep clean:

Own the bad news in active voice: "We missed the target" (not "mistakes were made" — passive blame-shifting that everyone sees through). Apologize once, not five times. Then switch to the future: will for a firm promise, be + -ing / going to for an arranged step you have already fixed, and the first conditional (if + present, will + base) to name a contingency. Honest past, concrete future.

Common Mistake

Shaky vs Steady

✗ PASSIVE BLAME-SHIFT

"Mistakes were made and the target was missed."

✓ ACTIVE, OWNED

"We missed the target, and I take responsibility."

✗ OVER-APOLOGY, NO PLAN

"I'm so sorry, so sorry, it's all my fault, really sorry."

✓ ONE SORRY + PROMISE

"I'm sorry. Here's what we will do to fix it."

Own it in active voice. Apologize once, then pivot to a concrete plan with will.

Chapter 4

Breaking It

The call nobody wants to make

Dialogue

The Hard Call

B
Victor, thanks for calling. I have a board update tomorrow — are we on track for the quarter?
V
That's exactly why I'm calling. There's no easy way to say this: we came in below plan, with a shortfall of about ten percent.
B
That's a real setback. What happened — and who dropped the ball?
V
Honestly, we missed the target, and I own that fully. The root cause was a late forecast on my side, not the team's effort. I'm sorry it landed on you the day before your board.
B
I appreciate the honesty. But the board will ask me how we fix it. What's the plan?
V
Two things. We will close the shortfall by quarter end, and we're meeting the supplier on Thursday to course-correct the pipeline.
B
And if Thursday doesn't land? I need to know the downside is covered.
V
It is. If the supplier slips again, we will pull the stopgap budget and protect your core accounts first. I'll send you a one-page summary tonight so you can head off any surprises at the board.
B
That works. A bad quarter I can defend; being blindsided I can't. Send the summary.
