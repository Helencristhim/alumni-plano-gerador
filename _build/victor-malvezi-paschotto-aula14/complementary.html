Lesson 14 -- Damage Control: Delivering Bad News & Reassuring Stakeholders

Series
Chernobyl (HBO) -- the crisis-briefing scenes, or any corporate-apology / product-recall press conference on YouTube

Watch leaders deliver genuinely bad news under pressure. Notice who stays transparent, owns the miss in active voice, and offers a concrete plan — versus who hides behind passive blame-shifting ("mistakes were made") and over-apology. The contrast is the whole lesson.

Tip: pause after each delivery and label the moves — lead in, own it, reassure — and spot any passive blame-shift.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- any episode on crisis communication, delivering bad news, or accountable leadership

Harvard Business Review experts break down how strong leaders break bad news: be transparent, take accountability, name the root cause, and reassure with a real plan, not vague comfort. The exact toolkit from this class, in real executive voices — ideal prep before Victor's own stakeholder calls at Brink's.

Tip: write one go-to line for each move — lead in, own it, will-promise, arranged step, contingency — that you would actually say.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review / TED -- "How to Deliver Bad News" or crisis-communication breakdowns

A short, practical guide to breaking bad news well: lead in honestly, own the result without over-apologizing, and reassure with future commitments — will for promises, arranged steps, and if-contingencies. A clean bridge from fielding tough questions (Block 4, Lesson 13) to steadying the room after the answer is bad.

Tip: draft one bad-news script for a real situation you expect at work, then read it aloud and check each move.
