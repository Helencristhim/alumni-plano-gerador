Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Flight -- a journey by plane from one city to another
"My flight departed at six in the morning." (Voo)
Arrive -- to reach a place at the end of a trip
"We arrived at the airport at eight." (Chegar)
Depart -- to leave a place at the start of a trip
"The plane departed on time." (Partir)
Schedule -- a plan that shows the times and dates of events
"The schedule was very busy." (Cronograma)
Client -- a person or company that buys your service
"We met an important client in Brasilia." (Cliente)
Colleague -- a person you work with at the same company
"A colleague picked me up at the airport." (Colega)
On time -- at the right time, not late and not early
"The flight arrived on time." (No horário)
Highlight -- the best or most important part of an event or trip
"The plant visit was the highlight of the trip." (Ponto alto)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Two weeks ago Percival traveled to Brasilia. First, his flight departed at six and arrived on time at eight. Then, a colleague met him at the airport. After that, they had a long meeting with the client. Later, they visited the new water plant, and that was the highlight. Finally, Percival returned to the hotel.

1. What happened first in the story?
A His flight departed at six.
B They visited the water plant.
C He returned to the hotel.
2. What do words like first, then and finally show?
A The place of the action.
B The order of the actions.
C The price of the trip.
3. Why are all the verbs in the past simple?
A Because the whole story happened in the past.
B Because it is a plan for the future.
C Because there is a client.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Telling a Story in Order

GRAMMAR

Usamos marcadores de tempo e sequenciadores para contar uma história em ordem, com todos os verbos no passado. / We use time markers and sequencers to tell a story in order, with every verb in the past simple.

TypeWordsExample
time markerstwo weeks ago, yesterday, at sixYesterday I departed at six.
sequencersfirst, then, after that, later, finallyFirst I arrived, then I met the client.
past simpleevery verb in the pastI arrived, I met, I returned.

Key point / Ponto-chave: uma história tem três partes. Os marcadores de tempo dizem quando (two weeks ago, yesterday). Os sequenciadores dão a ordem (first, then, after that, later, finally). E todos os verbos ficam no passado do começo ao fim: do not jump back to the present.
