Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Telling a story in order

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words tell you about WHEN and in what ORDER?

"Two weeks ago I traveled to Brasilia."

"First, my flight departed at six."

"After that, a colleague met me at the airport."

"Finally, I returned to the hotel."

Grammar Rule

Telling a Story

TypeWordsExample
time markerstwo weeks ago, yesterday, at sixYesterday I departed at six.
sequencersfirst, then, after that, later, finallyFirst I arrived, then I met the client.
past simpleevery verb in the pastI arrived, I met, I returned.

Key idea: a story has three parts. Time markers say when (two weeks ago, yesterday). Sequencers give the order (first, then, after that, later, finally). And every verb stays in the past simple from start to end.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"First I arrived, then I take a taxi."
"First I arrived, then I took a taxi."

In a past story, every verb stays in the past. Do not jump back to the present in the middle of the story.
