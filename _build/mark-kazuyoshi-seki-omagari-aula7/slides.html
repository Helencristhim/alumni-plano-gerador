Grammar Rule

Connectors by Function

FunctionConnectors
Addmoreover, in addition, furthermore
Contrasthowever, on the other hand, whereas, despite
Examplefor instance
Resulttherefore, as a result
Concludein conclusion

Key idea: most connectors start a sentence and take a comma after them (However, ... / Moreover, ...). Use despite before a noun (Despite the rain), and whereas to compare two ideas in one sentence.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Despite of the difficulties, we won."
"Despite the difficulties, we won."

Use despite directly before a noun. Never write "despite of". For a full sentence, use although or however.

Artefact

The Opinion Plan

MARK'S OPINION PLAN

Topic: Should schools start later?

My view: In my view, schools should start one hour later.

Reason 1: First of all, teenagers wake up tired.

Reason 2: Moreover, students who sleep more get better grades.

Example: For instance, one school tried it and saw better results.

Other side: However, some parents work early. On the other hand, the school could open a study room.

Conclusion: In conclusion, the benefits are stronger; therefore, we should try it.

Dialogue

Planning the Debate

N

"Mark, we present our opinion tomorrow. How should we start our argument?"

M

"First we state our view clearly. Moreover, we add reasons. For instance, students learn more with breaks."

N

"Good. In addition, we should mention the other side, so the teacher sees we are fair."

M

"Exactly. We can say: however, some people disagree, and on the other hand, longer breaks cost time."

N

"Nice. Therefore, we sound balanced, whereas last time we only gave one side."

M

"Right. In conclusion, we repeat our main point. As a result, our argument will be strong and clear."
