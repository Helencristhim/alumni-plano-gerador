Chapter 3

The Sequence Code

First · Then · After that · Finally

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"First, you collect the sample."

"Then, you label the tube."

"After that, you store the sample."

"Finally, you record the data. You need to ensure it is correct."

StructureUseExample
First, ... Then, ...order the stepsFirst, you collect
After that, ... Finally, ...continue / close the stepsFinally, you record
you + verb (present simple)each step, no -ingyou store the sample
you need to + verbyou need to label the tube (NOT "need label")
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"First you collecting the sample."

✓ RIGHT

"First, you collect the sample."

✗ WRONG

"You need collect the sample."

✓ RIGHT

"You need to collect the sample."

✗ WRONG

"After, you store the sample."

✓ RIGHT

"After that, you store the sample."

Use present simple for each step (you collect, not collecting). Say need to + verb. Use after that, not after alone.

F
Of course. First, you collect the sample from the patient.
S
Okay. And then?
F
Then, you label the tube with the patient number.
S

After that, you store the sample in the fridge and analyze it.

And the last step?
F
Finally, you record the data. Always ensure every step is correct.
