Describing a Process (Protocol)

Explaining the clinical-study sample procedure step by step: collect, label, store, analyze and record. Key words: step, sample, collect, analyze, label, store, record, procedure, prepare, ensure. Structure: describing a process with present simple + sequence words (first, then, after that, finally) and you need to.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Step -- passo / etapa (one part of a process)
"The first step is collecting."
Sample -- amostra (a small part for study)
"We take a blood sample."
Collect -- coletar / recolher (to take or gather something)
"First, you collect the sample."
Analyze -- analisar (to study something carefully)
"We analyze the sample in the lab."
Label -- etiquetar (to put a name or number on something)
"Then, you label the tube."
Store -- armazenar / guardar (to keep something in a safe place)
"After that, you store the sample."
Record -- registrar / anotar (to write down information)
"Finally, you record the data."
Procedure -- procedimento (the official way to do something)
"We follow the procedure."
Prepare -- preparar (to get something ready)
"You need to prepare the tubes."
Ensure -- garantir / assegurar (to make sure something is done)
"Ensure every step is correct."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

A new colleague asks Fernando about the sample procedure. He explains the steps in order. "First, you collect the sample from the patient," he says. "Then, you label the tube with the number. After that, you store the sample in the fridge and analyze it. Finally, you record the data. You need to prepare the tubes before you start, and always ensure every step is correct."

1. Por que dizemos "you collect" e nao "you collecting"?
A Porque a acao e no passado.
B Descrevemos cada passo do processo com present simple (you collect, you store), sem -ing.
C Porque "collect" e um substantivo.
2. Para que servem as palavras "first", "then", "after that" e "finally"?
A Para fazer perguntas.
B Para colocar os passos do processo em ordem (sequencia).
C Para falar do futuro.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "You need prepare the tubes."
B "You need to prepare the tubes."
C "You need preparing the tubes."
