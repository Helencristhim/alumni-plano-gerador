Chapter 4: Adverbs of Frequency

always · usually · never

How often, and where the word goes

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I always drink coffee in the morning."

"She usually walks to work."

"She is never late for work."

"They are often tired after work."

How oftenAdverbPosition
100%alwaysBEFORE the main verb: I always drink coffee.
~90-70%usually / oftenBEFORE the main verb: She usually walks.
~50-10%sometimes / rarelyBEFORE the main verb: We sometimes nap.
0%neverBEFORE the main verb: I never skip breakfast.
to beWith am / is / are, the adverb comes AFTER: She is never late. They are often tired.

Before the main verb (I always drink) · after the verb to be (She is never late) · time phrases at the end (every day).

Grammar Practice

Put the Adverb in Place

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I ___always drink coffee first." (always + drink)
"She ___usually takes a stroll after dinner." (usually + take)
"My commute ___is often long." (often + is)
"We ___sometimes take a nap on Sunday." (sometimes + take)
"They are ___never bored on weekends." (never + are)
Language Focus

Where Does the Adverb Go?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I drink always coffee."

✓ RIGHT

"I always drink coffee."

✗ WRONG

"Always I am tired at night."

✓ RIGHT

"I am always tired at night."

Put the adverb BEFORE the main verb (I always drink), and AFTER the verb to be (I am always tired). Do not start the sentence with it.

Dialogue

Tom Talks About His Day

A
Hi Tom. You always seem full of energy. What is your daily routine?
T
Well, I usually wake up early. I always go for a workout before breakfast.
A
Every day? That is impressive. Is your commute long?
T
It is often long, about an hour. So I sometimes take a short nap on the train.
A
Smart. And how do you unwind in the evening?
T
I often go for a stroll in the park. It is a good habit for my health.
A
Do you ever just relax at home?
T
On the weekend, yes. I catch up with friends, and I rarely do any chores.

3. What does Tom often do to unwind in the evening?
He goes for a stroll in the park.
