Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

My Everyday Life -- Routines & Free Time

Describing your routine and free time. Key words: errand, chore, commute, workout, leisure, to unwind, groceries, nap, stroll, habit, to catch up, schedule. Grammar: adverbs of frequency (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never) and where they go in the sentence.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Errand -- a quick trip to do a small task, like the bank or the pharmacy
"I usually run one or two errands after work."
Chore -- a small job you do at home, such as cleaning or the dishes
"On Sunday I do the chores around the house."
Commute -- the regular trip between your home and your workplace
"My commute takes about thirty minutes each way."
Workout -- a period of physical exercise, like running or the gym
"I do a short workout three times a week."
Leisure -- free time when you rest and do the things you enjoy
"I keep my Sundays for leisure and family."
To unwind -- to relax and let go of stress after a busy day
"After work, I like to unwind with a walk."
Groceries -- the food and everyday items you buy at the supermarket
"I buy my groceries once a week."
Nap -- a short sleep, usually taken during the day
"On weekends I sometimes take a short nap."
Stroll -- a slow, relaxed walk that you take for pleasure
"We often take an evening stroll after dinner."
Habit -- something you do regularly, often without thinking about it
"Reading before bed is a good habit."
To catch up -- to reconnect with someone, or finish something you were behind on
"On Saturday I catch up with old friends."
Schedule -- a plan that shows the times when you do each activity
"I keep a simple schedule during the week."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana Paula has a busy but calm schedule. She always drinks a coffee before her commute, which usually takes about thirty minutes. She often does a short workout in the morning, but she rarely takes a nap during the day. After work, she sometimes runs a few errands, and then she likes to unwind. In the evening, she often takes a slow stroll in her neighborhood. On the weekend, she never works. She catches up with friends, does the groceries, and finishes the chores at home. A relaxed evening is her favorite habit.

1. Why do we say "She always drinks coffee" and not "She drinks always coffee"?
A The adverb of frequency goes before the main verb.
B "Always" must go at the end of the sentence.
C "Always" is a noun, so it goes after the verb.
2. How often does Ana Paula work on the weekend?
A She always works on the weekend.
B She never works on the weekend.
C She usually works on the weekend.
3. Which sentence puts the adverb in the correct place with the verb "to be"?
A "She never is tired after work."
B "She is never tired after work."
C "She is tired never after work."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

How to say how often you do things, and where the adverb goes.

RuleDetailExample
The scalealways (100%) > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never (0%).I usually walk to work.
Main verbsThe adverb goes BEFORE the main verb.I always drink coffee.
The verb "to be"With am / is / are, the adverb goes AFTER the verb.She is never late.
Time expressionsPhrases like every day, once a week or on weekends usually go at the END of the sentence.
