Lesson 2 -- My Everyday Life

Documentary
Mental Health and Resilience -- The Secrets of Inner Strength (DW Documentary)

A calm, well-made documentary about wellbeing and healthy daily habits -- a subject you care about as a doctor. People describe how they rest, exercise and unwind to stay strong. Connection to Lesson 2: listen for how often people do things (they always, usually, sometimes, or rarely do them).

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down three sentences with an adverb of frequency about the habits in the film.

TED Talk
The Power of Habit -- Charles Duhigg (TEDx Talks)

A clear, friendly talk about how habits work and how small routines shape our day -- exactly the theme of this lesson. The speaker uses everyday examples in natural English. Connection to Lesson 2: notice the vocabulary of routine (habit, schedule) and the adverbs of frequency he uses.

Tip: pause after each story and describe one of your own habits, using "usually", "often" or "always".

YouTube
Adverbs of Frequency: How to Use Them and Where They Go -- English In A Minute (BBC Learning English)

A short, clear video on the exact grammar from today: how often, and where the adverb goes in the sentence. Connection to Lesson 2: this is the difference between "I always drink coffee" and "She is never late".

Tip: pause after each example and make one true sentence about your own week -- one before a main verb, one after the verb "to be".
