Chapter 3: The Code

Could You Tell Me...?

Direct and indirect questions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me what the client wants?"

"Do you know if the feature is in scope?"

"Can I ask what the deadline is?"

"I was wondering whether the report needs charts."

What happens to the word order after the orange openers?

TypeDirectIndirect (polite)
Wh- questionWhat does the client want?Could you tell me what the client wants?
Yes/No questionIs the feature in scope?Do you know if the feature is in scope?
Word orderStatement order after the opener: subject + verb, no do/does/did. Use "if" or "whether" for yes/no.

Indirect questions sound more polite on a call with a client or a product owner.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Could you tell me what does the client want?"
"Could you tell me what the client wants?"
"Do you know is the feature in scope?"
"Do you know if the feature is in scope?"
"Can you tell me where is the specification?"
"Can you tell me where the specification is?"

After the opener, use statement order: subject + verb, drop do/does, and use if for yes/no questions.

Chapter 4: On the Call

Clarifying with Bruno

A requirements call about a new feature

Dialogue

The Requirements Call

B
Hi Ana, thanks for joining. I want to introduce the new export feature before you test it.
A
Thanks, Bruno. Before we start, could you tell me what the main use case is?
B
Sure. The end user clicks Export and gets a report as a PDF file.
A
Got it. Do you know if the report has to include the charts, or just the tables?
B
Good question. The specification says both, the charts and the tables.
A
Perfect. And can I ask what the constraint on the file size is?
B
The file must be under ten megabytes. That is a hard constraint.
A
Understood. So, just to confirm, the acceptance criteria are a PDF under ten megabytes with charts and tables. Is that right?
B
Exactly right. You have gathered everything. Now you can test the right thing.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Clarify the Feature

Role-Play

The First Questions

Scenario: A product owner gives you a new feature. Ask about the use case and who the end user is, using polite indirect questions.

Keyword chips:

Could you tell me what the use case is? Do you know who the end user is? Can I ask what the specification says?
Role-Play

Scope and Constraints

Scenario: Continue the call. Ask whether the feature is in scope, and ask about one constraint, like the file size or the deadline.

Keywords:

Do you know if... in scope? Can I ask what the constraint is? Is the deadline fixed?
