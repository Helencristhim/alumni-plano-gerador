Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Breakthrough -- an important moment when you suddenly get much better
"Handling objections was a real breakthrough for me." (Avanço decisivo)
Review -- to look back at something to check or remember it
"Let us review the last four lessons." (Revisar)
Checkpoint -- a point where you stop to check how you are doing
"Lesson fifteen is a mid-program checkpoint." (Ponto de verificação)
Strength -- something you are good at; a strong skill
"Handling objections is now a real strength." (Ponto forte)
Improve -- to make something better, or to get better at it
"I want to improve my pronunciation." (Melhorar)
Goal -- something you want to reach or achieve
"My goal is to speak more fluently." (Meta / objetivo)
Confident -- feeling sure of yourself and your ability
"I feel more confident on calls now." (Confiante)
Fluent -- able to speak smoothly and easily, with few pauses
"He is becoming fluent in business English." (Fluente)
Guidance -- helpful advice that shows you what to do next
"My teacher gave me clear guidance." (Orientação)
Overcome -- to deal with and beat a problem or difficulty
"I want to overcome my fear of speaking." (Superar)

GRAMMAR

Lesson fifteen is a checkpoint: a moment to review the last four lessons. Roberto looks back at his four tools. In lesson eleven he learned to run a meeting: "Let us start with the agenda." In lesson fourteen, should and could to handle objections: "You could try the smaller model." His biggest strength is handling objections, and that was a real breakthrough. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Your Four Tools (Lessons 11-14)

GRAMMAR

Revise as quatro estruturas das aulas 11 a 14 e quando usar cada uma. / Review the four structures from lessons 11 to 14 and when to use each one.

Ferramenta / ToolForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Reunião (L11) / Meetinglet us / we need to + verboLet us start with the agenda.
1ª condicional (L12)if + presente, will + verboIf we order more, you will give a discount.
Voz passiva (L13)is / are + particípio passadoThe engines are made in Resende.
Should / Could (L14)should / could + verbo baseYou could try the smaller model.

Key point / Ponto-chave: as quatro ferramentas cobrem quase toda uma ligação de vendas: use a linguagem de reunião para abrir e organizar, o first conditional para fechar um acordo, a voz passiva para apresentar o produto, e should/could para lidar com objeções. Saber QUANDO usar cada uma é o seu verdadeiro progresso.
