Lesson 12 -- Reading Contracts II

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 2 "Errors and Omissions"

The lawyers negotiate the conditions of a deal: when an agreement terminates, what each party must comply with, and on what condition they will sign. Listen for sentences with if, unless and provided, and for words like provision, jurisdiction and waiver.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 conditions you hear (one with if, one with unless, one with provided) and the legal nouns around them.

Podcast
Legal English Podcast -- "Conditions and Termination Clauses"

A practical episode on the conditions of a commercial contract: when it terminates, the governing law and jurisdiction, and how parties amend or enforce the terms. The hosts read real conditional clauses with if, unless and provided -- exactly the language you use for contracts and M&A at work.

Note 3 conditions the hosts read, and rewrite each one using a different word: if, unless or provided.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "First conditional: if, unless and provided that"

A short, clear lesson on how to talk about real conditions with if, unless and provided that, and why we use the present tense after them. Directly useful for reading and discussing the conditions in the contracts you review.

After watching, write five conditions about a contract (using if, unless and provided), then record yourself reading them with at least four legal words from today.
