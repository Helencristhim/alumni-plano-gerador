Lesson 09 -- Pre-class

Passing It On -- Reporting Back After Meetings and Calls

Learn to relay what people said, accurately and professionally. The grammar focus is reported speech: the backshift ("is" becomes "was"), say vs tell, and reporting verbs. New words: relay, recap, paraphrase, reiterate, loop in, flag, heads-up, disclose, confidential, verbatim, get back to, run by.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it. / Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo.

Relay -- to pass a message or information along to someone / repassar, transmitir
"Let me relay what was decided in the meeting."
Recap -- to summarize the main points again, briefly / recapitular, resumir
"Let me recap our call with the supplier."
Paraphrase -- to say the same thing again in different words / parafrasear
"Let me paraphrase what the director said."
Reiterate -- to repeat something to make it clear or emphatic / reiterar, reforçar
"She asked me to reiterate that it is urgent."
Loop in -- to include someone in a message or decision / incluir alguém (na conversa)
"I will loop you in on the next email."
Flag -- to draw attention to a problem or an issue / sinalizar, apontar
"He flagged one issue with the delivery."
Heads-up -- an early, informal warning about something / um aviso prévio
"I wanted to give you a quick heads-up."
Disclose -- to reveal official or private information / revelar, divulgar
"He disclosed that the factory was moving."
Confidential -- meant to be kept private and not shared / confidencial
"The decision is confidential until Friday."
Verbatim -- word for word, exactly as it was said / ao pé da letra, literalmente
"I wrote the key points almost verbatim."
Get back to -- to return to someone later with an answer / dar retorno a alguém
"He said he would get back to me tomorrow."
Run by -- to share an idea with someone to get feedback / consultar, submeter (uma ideia)
"Let me run the new timeline by the team."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

David missed the board meeting, so Eliane relays what was said. She keeps it clear by shifting each verb one step into the past. The chair said that the proposal had been approved, she reports, and she asked me to reiterate that it was confidential. When she relays the finance director, she uses "tell" with a person: He told me that the delivery window was too long. She reports a question without inversion: They asked whether I could get back to the supplier. Notice that "is" became "was", "has been" became "had been", and "will" became "would". That one step back is the heart of reported speech.

1. Why does Eliane shift each verb one step into the past?
A Because she is reporting what was said (reported speech uses the backshift).
B Because everything happened many years ago.
C Because the past is easier to pronounce.
2. Which verb does Eliane use WITH a person (an object)?
A "said" ("She said the team...").
B "told" ("He told me that...").
C Both are always the same.
3. How does she report the question?
A With inversion: "They asked could I get back...".
B With "whether" and no inversion: "They asked whether I could get back...".
C As a direct quote only.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

To report what someone said, use a reporting verb + (that) + the verb one step into the past. Use "tell" with a person and "say" without one. For questions, use "asked if / whether" and drop the inversion. / Para relatar o que alguém disse, use um verbo de relato + (that) + o verbo um passo atrás. Use "tell" com uma pessoa e "say" sem pessoa. Para perguntas, use "asked if / whether" sem inversão.

Direct / DiretoReported / IndiretoShift / Mudança
"It is fixed."He said (that) it was fixed.is → was
"It has been approved."She said it had been approved.has → had
"We will sign."They said they would sign.will → would
"You must reiterate it." (to me)He told me I had to reiterate it.tell + person
"Can you help?"She asked whether I could help.if / whether, no inversion

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

Imagine you have just come out of a real meeting at your clinic and a team member missed it. In about two minutes, debrief them: relay what at least three different people said (using the backshift and both "say" and "tell"), flag one concern, give a heads-up about anything confidential, and say what you will get back to them about. Speak naturally and do not stop to edit.
