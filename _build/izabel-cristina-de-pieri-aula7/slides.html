Chapter 3

The Code

Talking about the future: will, going to, present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Fine, I will send you the roadmap tonight."

"We are going to grow revenue by fifteen percent."

"We are launching the new line in the third quarter."

"We are meeting the partners on Tuesday."

StructureUseExample
will + baseInstant decision, offer, or prediction with no evidenceI will send it tonight.
be going to + baseA plan already decided, or a prediction from evidenceWe are going to launch in Q3.
am / is / are + -ingA fixed arrangement, usually with a time or placeWe are meeting them on Tuesday.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I meet the partners next Tuesday."

✓ RIGHT

"I am meeting the partners next Tuesday."

✗ WRONG

"We will launch the line in Q3." (already decided)

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to launch the line in Q3."

✗ WRONG

"We are going to launching it in April."

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to launch it in April."

Use the present continuous (am/is/are + -ing) for fixed arrangements with a time. Use "going to + base verb" for plans already decided. Save "will" for instant decisions, offers, and predictions.

Chapter 4

In the Room

Presenting your plan on the Thursday call

Dialogue

The Plan for the Quarter

A
Izabel, thank you. Let us look at the road ahead. What is the plan for the third quarter?
I
Thank you, Andy. We are launching the new wireless sensor line in Q3. The timeline is set, and the first phase kicks off in early April.
A
Good. And the numbers? What does your forecast look like?
I
Our forecast is strong. We are going to grow revenue by about fifteen percent, and the projection for the new line alone is very promising.
A
Encouraging. Are you planning to invest more in research?
I
Yes. We are going to allocate more budget to research, so the pipeline stays full. I will send you the full roadmap after the call.
A
Perfect. One more thing. The partners will ask about risk. How are you preparing?
I
I anticipated that. We always keep a contingency: if a supplier is late, we have a backup plan ready. And I am meeting the regional partners next Tuesday to agree on the scope.
