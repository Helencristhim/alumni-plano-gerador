The Road Ahead -- Talking About Plans and the Future

Presenting your plan for the next quarter. Key words: roadmap, timeline, forecast, projection, target, allocate, kick off, scope, pipeline, phase, contingency, anticipate. Structures: the three future forms -- will (instant decisions / predictions), be going to (decided plans / evidence-based forecasts), and present continuous (fixed arrangements).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Roadmap -- a plan showing how something will develop over time (plano / roteiro)
"Last class you described how AVRT works. Now I will bring a clear roadmap for the next quarter."
Timeline -- the schedule showing when each stage happens (cronograma)
"The timeline is set: the line launches in April."
Forecast -- a statement of what is likely to happen in the future (previsão)
"Our forecast is a fifteen percent rise in revenue."
Projection -- an estimate of future numbers (projeção)
"The projection for the new line is very promising."
Target -- to aim for a specific result (mirar / ter como meta)
"We are targeting twelve new accounts by December."
Allocate -- to assign money or resources to a purpose (alocar / destinar)
"We are going to allocate more budget to research."
Kick off -- to start a project or activity (dar início a algo)
"The first phase kicks off in early April."
Scope -- the range of what a project covers (escopo / abrangência)
"First, let us agree on the scope of the new market."
Pipeline -- projects or products being developed for the future (carteira de projetos em desenvolvimento)
"More research keeps the pipeline full."
Phase -- a distinct stage in a plan (fase / etapa)
"The first phase is the pilot; the second is the full launch."
Contingency -- a backup plan for if something goes wrong (plano de contingência)
"We always keep a contingency if a supplier is late."
Anticipate -- to expect something and prepare for it (antecipar-se / prever e se preparar)
"Anticipate the questions the board will ask."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the Thursday call, the board asks Izabel for the road ahead. She is ready. "In the third quarter, we are launching the new wireless sensor line," she says. "The timeline is set, and the first phase kicks off in April." On the numbers, she is confident: "Our forecast is strong. We are going to grow revenue by about fifteen percent, and the projection for the new line is very promising." A partner asks about research. "We are going to allocate more budget to it, so the pipeline stays full. I will send you the full roadmap after the call." Finally, on risk: "We always keep a contingency, and I am meeting the regional partners next Tuesday to agree on the scope."

1. She says "we are launching the new line in Q3." Why the present continuous?
A Because it is happening right now, as she speaks.
B It is a fixed arrangement for the future (present continuous = am/is/are + -ing).
C Because the action is a habit.
2. Look at "we are going to grow revenue." Why "going to" and not "will"?
A It is a plan already decided / a prediction based on evidence, so we use "be going to".
B Because "will" is never used for the future.
C Because "grow" cannot follow "will".
3. Which sentence is an instant decision made at the moment of speaking?
A "We are meeting the regional partners next Tuesday."
B "I will send you the full roadmap after the call."
C "We are going to allocate more budget to research."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Talking About the Future

GRAMMAR

The three future forms (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
will + baseDecisão na hora, oferta ou previsão sem prova concretaI will send it tonight.
be going to + basePlano JÁ decidido, ou previsão com base em evidênciaWe are going to launch in Q3.
am / is / are + -ingCompromisso marcado (arranjo fixo), geralmente com data/horaWe are meeting them on Tuesday.
Cuidado / Watch outNão use o presente simples para arranjos: errado "I meet them Tuesday" → certo "I am meeting them on Tuesday".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each executive situation.

You have a fixed meeting with the partners next Tuesday. How do you say it?
A "I meet the partners next Tuesday."
B "I am meeting the partners next Tuesday."
C "I will meeting the partners next Tuesday."
The launch is a plan you already decided. Which is correct?
A "We are going to launch the new line in Q3."
B "We will launching the new line in Q3."
C "We are going to launching the new line in Q3."
A partner asks for the plan and you decide, right now, to send it. Which is best?
A "Sure, I will send you the roadmap tonight."
B "Sure, I send you the roadmap tonight."
C "Sure, I am going to sending you the roadmap tonight."
Which sentence talks about the numbers with an evidence-based forecast?
A "Revenue is going to grow by about fifteen percent."
B "Revenue grows by about fifteen percent next year."
C "Revenue will to grow by about fifteen percent."
