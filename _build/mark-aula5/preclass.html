Aula 05 -- Pre-class

What Would I Do?

Second conditional for hypothetical scenarios and debates: dilemma, consequence, outcome, scenario, hesitate, tempting, principle, regret. Structures: "If I found..., I would...", "What would you do if...?", "If I were you...". Mission: defend your choices in the Dilemma Game.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example.

Dilemma -- a choice where every option has a problem
"Telling the truth or protecting a friend — a real dilemma."
Consequence -- the result that follows your action
"Every choice has consequences." (Consequência)
Outcome -- how a situation ends
"Nobody could predict the outcome."
Scenario -- an imagined situation, used to think or test
"Picture this scenario: you find a lost phone."
Hesitate -- to pause because you are not sure
"I would not hesitate for a second." (Hesitar)
Tempting -- attractive, even when you know it is wrong
"Keeping the money would be tempting."
Principle -- a rule you follow because you believe in it
"It is against my principles to lie." (Princípio)
Regret -- the sad feeling about something you did, or did not do
"I would regret it forever." (Arrepender-se)

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At debate club, Sofia presents a scenario: "If you found a wallet with five hundred dollars, what would you do?" Mark does not hesitate: "Keeping it would be tempting, but I would take it to the office. If I kept it, I would regret it — that is the real consequence. And if I were rich one day, I would want people to return MY wallet. It is a question of principle."

1. Which form follows "if" in the second conditional?
A "If you would find..."
B "If you found..." (past simple)
C "If you will find..."
2. What would Mark do with the wallet?
A He would take it to the office.
B He would keep it.
C He would hide it.
3. Which form of "be" does Mark use after "if I"?
A was
B were ("If I were rich")
C am

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Situações imaginárias no presente/futuro. / Imaginary situations, present or future.

PartFormExample
ConditionIf + past simpleIf I found a wallet... (Se eu encontrasse)
Resultwould + base verb...I would return it. (eu devolveria)
QuestionWhat would you do if...?What would you do if you won?
to bewere (all persons)If I were you... (Se eu fosse você)

NEVER "if + would" / "if + will". The past form creates distance from reality — it is NOT about the past.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A friend asks for your hypothetical opinion about quitting the team.
A "If I were you, I would talk to the coach first."
B "If I was you, I will talk to the coach."
C "If I would be you, I would talk."
The debate question is about winning the lottery.
A "If I will win, I would donate half."
B "If I won, I would donate half."
C "If I would win, I donate half."
You want to challenge your opponent with a hypothetical.
A "What you would do if you saw it?"
B "What would you do if you saw it?"
C "What would you did if you see it?"
You admit the temptation but defend your principle.
A "It would be tempting, but it is against my principles."
B "It is tempting so I would do it."
C "Temptation is my principle."
