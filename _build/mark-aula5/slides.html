Chapter 3 -- The Grammar of Imagination

Second Conditional

If + past simple, would + verb

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"If I found a wallet, I would return it."

"What would you do if you saw a friend cheating?"

"If I were you, I would tell the teacher."

"I would not hesitate if the outcome were clear."

PartFormExample
Condition (imaginary)If + past simpleIf I found a wallet...
Resultwould + base verb...I would return it.
Special: to bewere (all persons)If I were you / If she were rich

Past form, but NOT about the past — about an imaginary present or future. The past creates distance from reality.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If I would find a wallet..."

✓ RIGHT

"If I found a wallet..."

✗ WRONG

"If I was you, I would stop."

"If I were you, I would stop."

Sofia's Defense

A model answer: choice + consequence + principle.

0:000:00
What would Sofia do if she could read minds?
She would NOT use the power — she would turn it off.
What consequence does she mention?
If she knew everyone's thoughts, she would lose her real friendships.
The Debate

Mark vs Sofia

S
Okay, card seven: if you found a wallet with five hundred dollars, what would you do? Be honest!
M
Honestly? Keeping it would be tempting. But I would take it to the school office.
S
Why? Nobody would know. There would be zero consequences.
M
I would know. That is the consequence. If I kept it, I would regret it every time I used that money.
S
Interesting. So it is a question of principle, not fear. New scenario: what if your best friend found it?
M
Ouch. That is a real dilemma. I would not report him... but I would try to convince him to return it.
S
And if he refused? Would you hesitate then?
M
Yes, I would. And that is the honest answer — the outcome would depend on the friendship. Your turn, Sofia!

Chapter 7 -- The Final Round

Defend Your Choice

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Round 1: Guided

Three What-Ifs

Scenario: Answer three hypothetical questions with full sentences: a superpower, a million dollars, a time machine.

Keyword chips:

If I had... I would... because...
Round 2: Semi-free

Choice + Defense

Scenario: Dilemma: your team would win the championship if you played unfairly — just once, and nobody would see it. Make your choice, defend it with a consequence and a principle, then survive my counter-attack.

Keywords:

I would... because the consequence would be...
Round 3: Free

The Devil's Advocate

Scenario: The hardest game in debate: defend the position you DISAGREE with, for two minutes — what would happen, what the consequences would be, why someone would choose it. Pure hypothesis, pure skill.

No keywords. Argue like a champion!
