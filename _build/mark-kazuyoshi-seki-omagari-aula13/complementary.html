Complementary Materials — Lesson 13

Series
How I Met Your Mother — Season 1

The whole show is one big look back at the past. The narrator constantly explains what he should have done, what could have happened, and how a single choice changed everything. Listen for "should have", "could have" and "would have".

Tip: every time a character talks about a past choice, pause and rephrase it with a modal perfect in your own words.

Podcast
TED Talks Daily — episodes on decisions & mistakes

Speakers look back at choices and explain what they should have done and how things could have turned out differently. Notice how they mix "should have" for criticism with "would have" for imagined results.

Tip: write down two sentences with "I should have..." and one with "I would have... if...", then say them out loud.

YouTube
BBC Learning English — "modals of deduction in the past"

A short, clear video on exactly this lesson's grammar: should have for criticism, could have for missed chances, and might/may have for guesses about the past.

Tip: after watching, record five sentences about a real event, one for each modal: should, could, might and would have.
