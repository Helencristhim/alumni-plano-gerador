Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

Grammar Discovery

should / could / might / would + have + past participle

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words tell us about each past situation?

"I should have studied more." (the right thing, not done)

"I could have taken the earlier train." (a chance that existed)

"She might have missed the bus." (a guess about the past)

"I would have passed if I had tried." (imagined result)

Grammar Rule

modal + have + past participle

StructureUseExample
should have + p.p.the right thing you did NOT do (criticism)I should have studied more.
could have + p.p.a possibility that existed but did not happenI could have taken the earlier train.
might / may have + p.p.a guess when you are not sureShe might have missed the bus.
would have + p.p.the imagined result of a different choiceI would have passed if I had tried.

Key idea: every modal perfect looks BACK at the past. The form is always modal + "have" + past participle. In fast speech, "should have" sounds like "should've" and "would have" like "would've" -- never "should of".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I should of called you."
"I should have called you."

After a modal, always use have plus the past participle, never of. It sounds like "should've", but you write "should have".

Comprehension

About the Tips

1. What is the form of a modal perfect?

A modal plus "have" plus the past participle, for example "should have studied".

2. Which modal do you use for a guess about the past?

"might have" or "may have", for example "she might have missed the bus".

3. Why should you not write "should of"?

Because the correct form is "should have"; it only sounds like "should've".
