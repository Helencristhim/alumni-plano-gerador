Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Concern -- a worry you can explain with facts
"I have one concern about the new vendor." (Preocupação)
Alternative -- another option, ready to use
"Let me offer an alternative." (Alternativa)
Convince -- to make someone believe you, with reasons
"The data will convince her, not my opinion." (Convencer)
Assume -- to think something is true without checking
"Do not assume the team agrees." (Presumir)
Evidence -- facts that prove what you say
"I have evidence from the last release." (Evidência)
Hesitate -- to stop before speaking, because you are unsure
"Do not hesitate. Say it in the meeting." (Hesitar)
Downside -- the bad part of a good idea
"The downside is two extra weeks of testing." (Desvantagem)
Worth it -- good enough to pay the price
"The risk is real, and it is not worth it." (Valer a pena)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Sophia has already decided: a new vendor starts on Monday. Diogo does not hesitate, and he does not attack her either. He starts soft: "I see your point, but I have one concern." Then he proves it: "I am not sure that will work, because in March the same decision cost us three extra weeks." That is evidence, not an opinion, and evidence is what will convince her. He names the downside -- two weeks of onboarding, taken out of testing -- and he does not assume she can read his mind. Finally, he offers an alternative: "What if we give them the reports instead?" The risk of losing the test phase is not worth it. Sophia still decides. But now she decides with the facts.

1. Why does Diogo talk about March?
A To give evidence: the same decision cost three extra weeks.
B To blame the vendor from last year.
C To change the subject.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A I do not agree with you.
B I am not agree with you.
C I no agree with you.
3. What does he do at the end?
A He offers an alternative.
B He refuses to work on the project.
C He tells her she is wrong.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Soften, Prove, Offer

GRAMMAR

Discordar sem virar inimigo: suavizar, provar com fato e oferecer saída. / Disagreeing without becoming the enemy.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Suavizar / SoftenI see your point, but / I am not sure that...I see your point, but I have one concern.
Provar / Provebecause + fato (não sentimento)...because we tried it in March.
Oferecer / OfferWhat if we + verbo / I would suggest + substantivoWhat if we give them the reports instead?
Ponto-chave / Key pointEm inglês, disagree é VERBO: "I do not agree" / "I disagree with you" -- nunca "I am not agree" nem "I disagree of". E regra de ouro: nunca discorde de mãos vazias -- traga evidência e uma alternativa.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of a respectful no. Record each one. Calm voice, no apology -- you are contributing, not complaining.

I see your point, but I have one concern.
Entendo seu ponto, mas tenho uma preocupação.
I am not sure that will work, because we tried it in March.
Não tenho certeza de que vai funcionar, porque tentamos isso em março.
The downside is two weeks of onboarding.
A desvantagem são duas semanas de onboarding.
What if we give them the reports instead?
E se dermos os relatórios a eles, em vez disso?
I do not agree, but I will support the decision.
Não concordo, mas vou apoiar a decisão.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. Your director announces a decision you think is wrong. You say:
A "I see your point, but I have one concern."
B "That is a terrible idea."
C Nothing. You complain to your team later.
2. She asks why. The strongest answer is:
A "Because in March the same decision cost us three extra weeks."
B "Because I have a bad feeling about it."
C "Because nobody likes this vendor."
3. She keeps her decision anyway. You:
A Accept it out loud, and ask for the two extra weeks you need.
B Say yes, and hope it fails so you are proved right.
C Repeat your concern three more times.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real decision at Oracle that you disagree with. Speak for two minutes: (1) soften, (2) your concern in one sentence, (3) the evidence -- a fact, a date, a number, (4) the downside, (5) your alternative. Record it.
Now she says: "You are being negative. The decision is made." Answer out loud. Do not repeat your concern -- accept the decision, and ask for exactly what you need to make it work. Record it.
