Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one softens? Which one proves? Which one offers a way out?

"I see your point, but I have one concern."

"I am not sure that will work, because we tried it in March."

"What if we give them the reports instead?"

"I would suggest a two-week pilot."

Grammar Rule

Soften, Prove, Offer

MoveStructureExample
Soften the noI see your point, but / I am not sure that...I see your point, but I have one concern.
Prove itbecause + a fact (not a feeling)...because the same thing cost us three weeks in March.
Offer the way outWhat if we + verb / I would suggest + nounWhat if we give them the reports instead?

Never disagree with empty hands. A concern with no evidence is an opinion, and opinions lose to power. A concern with evidence and an alternative is a contribution -- and it makes you look senior, not difficult.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am not agree with you."
"I do not agree with you."
"I disagree of the new vendor."
"I disagree with the new vendor."

Disagree is a verb -- never "I am not agree". And you disagree with a person or a plan, never "of".

Chapter 5 -- Talking It Through

The Conversation

She wants facts, not feelings -- and she will say so

Dialogue

I Have One Concern

S

"The vendor starts Monday, on the integration. The client is happy. Any problem with that?"

D

"I see your point, and I understand the client. But I have one concern, and I do not want to hesitate about it."

S

"Go ahead. But bring me facts, not feelings."

D

"In March we added a vendor during the test phase. Integration took three extra weeks. That is my evidence."

S

"That was a different team. Do not assume it will happen again."

D

"Fair. But the downside is the same: two weeks of onboarding, and those weeks come out of testing."

S

"So you want me to tell the client no?"

D

"No. I would suggest an alternative: the vendor takes the reports, my team keeps the integration. The client still sees a new team."

S

"That might work. Convince me on Friday -- with the March numbers."

Dialogue Review

About Sophia

1. Why does Sophia want the vendor on the project?
The client is happy, and the client feels safer with a new team.
2. What does she ask Diogo to bring?
Facts, not feelings -- and the March numbers on Friday.
3. Does she accept the alternative immediately?
No. She says it might work, and asks him to convince her on Friday.

She Does Not Want to Hear It

"The decision is made. The client already knows."

She pushes back twice: first she says you are being negative, then she says you had time to speak earlier. Hold your ground, stay calm, and end with an alternative she can take to the client.
