Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

used to and would: the past versus now

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"I used to translate every word in my head."

"I used to feel nervous before meetings."

"Every morning, I would practice new vocabulary."

"Now I think in English and I feel confident."

Grammar Rule

used to and would

FormExample
Past habit / stateI used to feel nervous.
Past repeated actionI would practice every morning.
NegativeI did not use to feel confident.
Now (present)Now I feel confident.

Key idea: use used to + base verb for past habits and states that are no longer true. Use would + base verb only for repeated actions, not for states. In the negative and question, drop the -d: use to.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I use to feel nervous before meetings."
"I used to feel nervous before meetings."

In the affirmative, write used to with a final -d. For past states, use used to, not would.

Grammar Practice

Past versus Now

"I _____used to feel nervous on the phone." (past state)
"Every day, I _____would review my new words." (past repeated action)
"I did not _____use to understand fast English." (negative, no -d)
"Now I _____feel more confident." (present, now)
