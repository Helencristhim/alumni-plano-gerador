Lesson 01 -- Pre-class

Who Is Maria Clara? -- Diagnostic + Your First Words in English

Introduce yourself, without an interpreter: your name, your city, your work and your family. Key words: name, live, work, university, family, granddaughter, travel, nice to meet you. Structure: the verb TO BE (am / is / are) for who you are. And the phrases that save any conversation when you don't understand.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text slowly, twice, and then answer the questions.

Hello. My name is Maria Clara. I am from Maceio, in Brazil. I live in Maceio, and I work at a university. I have a big family: three children and one granddaughter. Her name is Clarinha. She is three years old, and she studies English at school. In September, I travel to China. It is my first trip without an interpreter. I am a little nervous, but I am ready. I speak English, and I am improving.

1. Where does Maria Clara live?
A In Maceio, in Brazil.
B In China.
C At a university in Canada.
2. How old is Clarinha?
A She has three years.
B She is three years old.
C She is thirty years old.
3. Which form is correct with "we"?
A "We is a big family."
B "We are a big family."
C "We am a big family."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Verb TO BE (am / is / are)

GRAMMAR

One verb, three shapes: am, is, are. It says who you are — your name, your city, your family.

SubjectYes (+)No (-)Question (?)
II am Maria Clara.I am not nervous.Am I late?
he / she / itClarinha is three.Clarinha is not here.Is Clarinha here?
you / we / theyWe are a family.We are not late.Are you from Brazil?

Watch out: age uses to be (I am 40 years old), never "I have 40 years". And do not use "am / is / are" before another verb: say "I work at a university", not "I am work".

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Listen to the sentence, repeat it out loud, and only then record. Listen and record as many times as you want — repetition is the method, not a sign of difficulty.

Hello! My name is Maria Clara. Nice to meet you.
I am from Maceio, and I work at a university.
I have a big family. My granddaughter is three years old.
Sorry, I don't understand. Could you repeat, please?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real moment of your trip.

A kind colleague says "Hello! My name is David." You answer:
A "Hello! My name is Maria Clara. Nice to meet you."
B "Yes."
C "Who are you?"
He asks "What do you do?" The best answer is:
A "I do university things."
B "I work at a university."
C "I am work at university."
He asks your age. You say:
A "I have 40 years."
B "I am 40 years old."
C "My age 40."
He speaks very fast and you don't understand. You say:
A Nothing. You smile and hope he stops.
B "Sorry, I don't understand. Could you repeat, please?"
C "Me no English."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your BASELINE: we will listen to it again later in the program, and you will hear the difference with your own ears.

Imagine you meet a new person on your first day in China. Introduce yourself in 4-5 sentences: your name, your city, your work, and one sentence about your granddaughter Clarinha. Use: I am..., I live..., I work..., I have... Take your time and don't read from a script.
