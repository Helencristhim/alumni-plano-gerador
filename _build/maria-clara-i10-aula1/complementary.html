Complementary Activities

You have two hours a day for English. These three activities are for that time — short, slow, and made for beginners. Listen, watch, and mark each one as done. No pressure: one small step at a time.

Video — grammar
Basic English Grammar: the verb TO BE (English with Ronnie, engVid)

This is the grammar of lesson 1: am, is, are — the verb that says who you are. Ronnie is clear, warm, and slow, and she uses simple examples. This is the verb behind "I am Maria Clara" and "She is my granddaughter".

Tip: after the video, say five sentences about your own life out loud — two with "I am", one with "She is" (about Clarinha), and two with "We are" (about your family). Don't write them first.

Video — speaking
How to Introduce Yourself in English — Slow Shadowing Practice for Beginners

This is exactly the mission of lesson 1: introducing yourself. The speaker goes slowly and pauses, so you can repeat after her — this is called shadowing. It builds the automatic sentences you will need in China: name, city, work, family.

Tip: don't just listen — SPEAK. Pause after each sentence and say it out loud, copying the rhythm. Do it once in the morning and once at night, for one week.

Podcast — listening
Learn English with Podcast — One Hour Easy Listening (Beginner A1-A2)

One hour of slow, clear English for beginners — the perfect activity for your two hours of daily immersion. Understanding real speech is the hardest skill, and the only way to train the ear is to use it, a little every day.

Tip: listen ONCE without stopping — don't worry about the words you miss. Then write down three sentences you understood clearly and two you could not catch. Bring the two to the next class; they become our mini listening drill.
