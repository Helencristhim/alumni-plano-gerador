Film
The Best Exotic Marigold Hotel (2011) — Judi Dench, Bill Nighy, Maggie Smith

Seven British retirees fly to India to live in a hotel that looks nothing like the photographs. Watch Evelyn, the one played by Judi Dench. She is a widow of forty years, her husband did every practical thing in her life, and she has never worked, never travelled alone, never opened a bank account. She is about seventy. In the first ten minutes she cannot finish a phone call. By the end she has a job, a salary, and a life — in a country where she knew nobody. She is not brave in a big loud way. She is brave once a day, in small rooms, with strangers.

Tip: watch with English subtitles, and watch Evelyn only — ignore the other six stories the first time. Every time she starts a sentence with "I would rather", "In my opinion" or "I used to", say it out loud after her: they are your expressions, in her mouth. One warning: this cast is very British and they speak fast. Use the subtitles without guilt, and remember that in Europe you are going to hear exactly this.

Speak English Now — Episode 382: Stop Comparing Yourself When Learning English (A2-B1)

This is the right episode for today, and not by accident. A progress check has one rule: you measure yourself against the woman who sat down for Lesson 1 — not against your son, not against the people who were in your old classroom, not against Yuko. Georgiana explains why comparing is the fastest way to stop, and why the person who feels "behind" is almost never behind: they are just looking at the wrong person. You already left one course because the class moved at somebody else's speed. That was never about your English.

Tip: listen once with your eyes closed, no text — your ear before your eyes, the way you like it. Then read the free transcript while you listen again. Then do this: say out loud three things you can do today that you could not do in Lesson 1. Not three things you still get wrong. Three things you can do.

YouTube
Speak English With Vanessa — THINK and SPEAK in English: your vacation (free, no subscription)

Vanessa is American, she speaks clearly and slowly, and this lesson is built exactly like your Lesson 10: she gives you a situation from a trip, you answer out loud, and then she gives you her version so you can compare. It is the same thing we did today with Quick Fire, except you can pause her and you can repeat as many times as you want. And there is one more reason this video is here: the whole point of it is to stop translating in your head. That is the last wall between you and two minutes without a single Portuguese word.

Tip: this one only works out loud, so do not watch it in silence — you would be wasting it. Pause after every question and answer in a full sentence before she does, even if it comes out wrong. Do it twice in the week: the first time you are going to pause a lot, and the second time you will not. That difference is the lesson.
