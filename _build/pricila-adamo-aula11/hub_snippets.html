Stage 1.1: Small Talk Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you start friendly conversations with strangers.

Stranger -- desconhecido / estranho
"I started a chat with a friendly stranger at the cafe."
Icebreaker -- quebra-gelo
"A comment about the weather is a classic icebreaker."
To chat -- conversar / bater papo
"We chatted for ten minutes while waiting in line."
Local -- morador local / nativo do lugar
"The locals were so friendly and gave us great tips."
Coincidence -- coincidencia
"What a coincidence -- we are both from Brazil!"
In common -- em comum
"We have a lot in common: we both love travel."
Awkward -- constrangedor / sem graca
"The silence felt awkward, so I asked a simple question."
Polite -- educado / cortes
"It is polite to smile and say hello first."
Small world -- como o mundo e pequeno
"You know my cousin? Small world!"
To get along -- se dar bem (com alguem)
"We got along right away and exchanged numbers."
To break the ice -- quebrar o gelo
"A friendly question is the best way to break the ice."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the conversation and answer the questions.

Pricila is waiting for a train in London. A friendly stranger smiles and says, "Beautiful station, isn't it?" That simple comment is a perfect icebreaker. Pricila replies and they begin to chat. "Where are you from?" the man asks. "I'm from Brazil," she says. "You've been to Rio, haven't you?" he laughs. "What a coincidence -- my daughter lives there!" They have a lot in common, and the silence is no longer awkward. It really is a small world.

1. How does the stranger break the ice?
A By commenting on the beautiful station.
B By complaining about the train.
C By asking for money.
2. Which sentence is a tag question?
A "Where are you from?"
B "Beautiful station, isn't it?"
C "I'm from Brazil."
3. Why is it a "small world"?
A The station is very small.
B His daughter lives in Brazil, where Pricila is from.
C They live on the same street.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Questions & Tag Questions

Grammar

Para fazer small talk, voce combina perguntas diretas (Where are you from?) e tag questions -- aquele "ne?" no final da frase. A tag inverte a forma do verbo: frase afirmativa leva tag negativa, e vice-versa. / To make small talk, you mix direct questions with tag questions (the "ne?" at the end). The tag reverses the verb: a positive sentence takes a negative tag, and the other way around.

Tipo / TypeEnglishPortugues
Wh- questionWhere are you from?De onde voce e?
Yes/No questionDo you like the city?Voce gosta da cidade?
Tag (+ frase / - tag)It's a lovely day, isn't it?E um dia lindo, ne?
Tag (- frase / + tag)You aren't from here, are you?Voce nao e daqui, ne?
Tag (past)You enjoyed the trip, didn't you?Voce curtiu a viagem, ne?
