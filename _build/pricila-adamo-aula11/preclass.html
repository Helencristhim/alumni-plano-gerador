Stage 1.1: Words for Great Conversations

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for confident small talk.

To strike up a conversation -- puxar conversa
"She struck up a conversation with the guest beside her."
To hit it off -- se dar bem logo de cara
"We hit it off right away and talked for hours."
Rapport -- sintonia / boa conexao
"A good teacher builds rapport with every student."
An acquaintance -- um conhecido, nao amigo intimo
"He's an acquaintance from my old running club."
A lull -- uma pausa ou silencio na conversa
"There was an awkward lull, so I asked about her trip."
To read the room -- sentir o clima do ambiente
"He read the room and changed the subject gently."
Tactful -- com tato / diplomatico
"She gave a tactful answer that hurt no one."
To chime in -- dar um pitaco / entrar na conversa
"May I chime in? I think there's another option."
To change the subject -- mudar de assunto
"It got tense, so I gently changed the subject."
To put someone at ease -- deixar alguem a vontade
"His warm smile put me completely at ease."
To come across as -- passar a impressao de
"Asked directly, it can come across as rude."
To keep the conversation going -- manter a conversa fluindo
"A follow-up question keeps the conversation going."
To make a good impression -- causar boa impressao
"She made a good impression on her new neighbours."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

Grammar

Para soar mais educado, comece com Could you tell me... / Do you know... e depois use ordem de afirmacao (sem inverter, sem do/does). Pergunta sim/nao leva if/whether. / To sound more polite, start with Could you tell me... / Do you know... and then use statement order (no inversion, no do/does). A yes/no question takes if/whether.

"What time does it open?"Do you know what time it opens?Voce sabe que horas abre?
