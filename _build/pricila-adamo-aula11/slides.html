Chapter 3: Asking Indirectly

The Polite Question

Indirect questions · Could you tell me... / I was wondering whether...

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Could you tell me where the station is?"

"Do you know what time the tour starts?"

"I was wondering whether breakfast is included."

"Would you mind telling me how this works?"

Direct (blunt)Indirect (polite)
Where is the station?Could you tell me where the station is?
What time does it start?Do you know what time it starts?
Is breakfast included?I was wondering whether breakfast is included.

After the opener (Could you tell me / Do you know / I was wondering whether...), use statement word order: no inversion and no do/does. For yes/no questions, add if or whether.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me where is the station?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me where the station is?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you know what time does it start?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you know what time it starts?"

Chapter 4: A Conversation Abroad

At the Hotel Breakfast

Pricila strikes up a chat with a fellow guest

Dialogue

Two Guests, One Conversation

P
Good morning! The coffee here is wonderful, isn't it? I hope you don't mind me asking -- do you happen to know whether the museum is open today?
G
It is! And yes, it opens at ten. You're not from here either, are you? I was wondering where you were from.
P
I'm from Brazil, travelling alone for a few weeks. It's so easy to strike up a conversation here -- everyone is lovely.
G
I know what you mean. I'm from Canada, here for work. Could you tell me how long you're staying? You might like the food market on Sundays.
P
Another week, so I'd love that. Would you mind telling me where exactly it is? I never know who to ask.
G
Just behind the cathedral. Honestly, it's been so nice chatting. We've really hit it off -- isn't it funny how a question over coffee turns into a real conversation?
Listening

A Polite Request

Listen first. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00

Whether the old market is open on Sunday, and which bus goes to the cathedral.
2. How does he make his questions polite?
He uses indirect forms: "I was wondering if...", "Do you happen to know whether...", "Would you mind telling me...".
3. Why does he prefer to ask a person?
It's much nicer than staring at a map.

About the Conversation

He is from Canada, there for work.
2. What does he recommend to Pricila?
The food market on Sundays, behind the cathedral.

They really hit it off.
Listening 2

Pricila's Secret

A reflection. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What did Pricila used to believe about good conversation?
That you had to be funny or clever.
2. What simple change did she make?
She stopped trying to be interesting and started being interested.
3. What does she say you really need?
Genuine curiosity about the person in front of you, not perfect words.

A Note Left at Reception

A note for Pricila

Left at the front desk

"Dear Pricila, it was lovely to strike up a conversation over breakfast -- I think we really hit it off! I'm heading to the Sunday market. I was wondering whether you'd like to join me, and could you let me know if ten o'clock works for you? Do you happen to know whether it's far to walk? Hope to see you there. -- Your fellow guest."
