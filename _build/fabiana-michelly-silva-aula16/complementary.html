Lesson 16 -- Leading a Project Kick-off

Series
Halt and Catch Fire -- Project launch episodes

A drama about teams starting ambitious tech projects. Watch how leaders set direction at a kick-off, assign ownership, and struggle with dependencies and scope creep as the work ramps up.

Pick one launch scene and write three sentences in the future continuous describing what the team will be doing next.

Podcast
Projectified (PMI) -- "Setting a Project Up for Success"

An episode on running a strong kick-off: defining the charter, mapping stakeholders, clarifying ownership of each work stream, and controlling scope. Directly relevant to a supply-chain integration project.

Note two phrases the host uses to describe future plans, and rewrite each one in the future continuous.

YouTube
"Future Continuous vs Future Perfect" -- English Grammar

A clear guide to the exact contrast in this lesson: will be + verb-ing for an action in progress at a future point, versus will have + past participle for an action finished by then.

After watching, write a six-line kick-off opening for a real project, using will be + -ing in four lines and will have + participle in one.
