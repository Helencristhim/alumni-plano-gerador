The Presentation Floor -- Signposting a Short Talk

Give a short, clear presentation to a group: structure it in three parts and signpost every step with first, next, then, finally and to sum up, and present data with there is / there are. New presentation vocabulary: overview, audience, slide, handout, figure, highlight, point out, move on, body, conclusion, walk through, signpost.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Overview -- a short general description of a topic
"Let me give you a quick overview of the project." (Visão geral)
Audience -- the group of people who listen to your talk
"Face the audience, not the screen." (Plateia / público)
Slide -- one screen or page of a presentation
"On this slide you can see the results." (Slide / lâmina)
Handout -- a document you give the audience to keep
"I prepared a one-page handout for everyone." (Material impresso)
Figure -- a number that shows data or a result
"The key figure is a forty percent increase." (Número / dado)
Highlight -- to make an important point stand out
"I want to highlight one important result." (Destacar)
Point out -- to show or mention an important detail
"Let me point out the figure on the right." (Apontar / mostrar)
Move on -- to go on to the next point or slide
"Let us move on to the budget." (Seguir adiante)
Body -- the main middle part of a presentation
"In the body, I cover three slides." (Corpo / parte central)
Conclusion -- the final part, where you sum up your points
"In my conclusion, the project is on track." (Conclusão)
Walk through -- to explain something step by step
"Let me walk you through the results." (Explicar passo a passo)
Signpost -- a word that guides the audience, like first or next
"Use signposts so people can follow you." (Marcador de discurso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Next week, Nilo will present the compliance project to the FIFA committee. He plans his presentation in three parts. To open, he gives an overview: "First, let us look at the results." Then he guides the audience with signposts: "Next, I will explain the budget," and "Then let us move on to the timeline." When he reaches the data, he is careful with the form: "On this slide there is one key figure," but "On the next slide there are two charts." Notice the rule: there is goes with a singular noun (one figure), and there are goes with a plural noun (two charts). To close, he uses a clear signal: "Finally, to sum up, the project is on track."

1. Which signpost does Nilo use to open his presentation?
A First, let us look at the results.
B Finally, let us look at the results.
C To sum up, let us look at the results.
2. Which sentence is correct for one key figure?
A On this slide there is one key figure.
B On this slide there are one key figure.
C On this slide there have one key figure.
3. How does Nilo signal the end of his talk?
A Finally, to sum up, the project is on track.
B First, to sum up, the project is on track.
C Next, to sum up, the project is on track.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signposting & There is / There are

GRAMMAR

Usado para guiar a plateia ao longo de uma apresentação e introduzir dados. / Used to guide the audience through a talk and introduce data.

Stage / EtapaSignpost / MarcadorExample / Exemplo
Opening / AberturaFirst / To start withFirst, let us look at the results.
Moving forward / AvançarNext / Then / After thatNext, I will explain the budget.
Changing topic / Mudar de tópicoLet us move on toLet us move on to the timeline.
Closing / FechamentoFinally / To sum upTo sum up, the project is on track.
Showing data / Mostrar dadosThere is / There areThere is one figure. There are two charts.

Key point / Ponto-chave: os signposts dizem à plateia onde você está na apresentação: first abre, next / then / after that avança, finally / to sum up fecha. Para dados, use there is + singular (one figure) e there are + plural (two charts). / Signposts guide the audience; use there is for one thing and there are for two or more.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "Today I would like to give you a quick overview."
B "To sum up, I would like to give you a quick overview."
C "Finally, I would like to give you a quick overview."

A "On this slide there is two charts."
B "On this slide there are two charts."

A "Next, let us move on to the budget."
