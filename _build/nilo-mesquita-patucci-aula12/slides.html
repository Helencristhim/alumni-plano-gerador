Chapter 3 -- The Signposts

Grammar Discovery

Guide the audience step by step

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

"First, let us look at the results." (the opening step)

"Next, I will explain the budget. Then we move on to the timeline." (the middle steps)

"Finally, to sum up, the project is on track." (the closing step)

"On this slide there is one key figure, and there are two charts." (data)

Grammar Rule

Signposting a Talk

Stage of the talkSignpostExample
OpeningFirst / To start withFirst, let us look at the results.
Moving forwardNext / Then / After thatNext, I will explain the budget.
Changing topicLet us move on toLet us move on to the timeline.
ClosingFinally / To sum upTo sum up, the project is on track.
Showing dataThere is / There areThere is one figure. There are two charts.

Key idea: signposts tell the audience where you are. Use first to open, next / then / after that to move forward, and finally / to sum up to close. For data, use there is + a singular noun and there are + a plural noun.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"On this slide there is three figures."
"On this slide there are three figures."

Use there is with a singular noun (one figure) and there are with a plural noun (three figures).
