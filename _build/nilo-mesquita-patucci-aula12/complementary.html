Lesson 12 -- The Presentation Floor

Series
Mad Men -- any client pitch scene

Executives walk a client through a slide deck: they open with an overview, signpost each part, point out the key figures, and close with a clear conclusion. You will hear real presentation language in a high-stakes business context.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three signposts a presenter uses (first, next, to sum up) and note how they introduce a figure.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Presentation Skills"

Practical episodes on giving presentations in English: opening with an overview, structuring the body, signposting transitions, presenting data, and wrapping up with a conclusion. Exactly today's vocabulary in real business situations.

Note three signposting phrases the presenter uses. Then record yourself opening a talk with an overview and two transitions.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Signposting in Presentations"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to guide an audience with first, next, then, finally, and to sum up, plus how to introduce data with there is and there are.

After watching, give a short presentation about your next real project. Use a different signpost for each section, and present one figure with there is and one with there are.
