Lesson 15 -- Free Time & Hobbies

Series
Peppa Pig -- "The Weekend" / "Going Out for the Day"

Short, slow episodes about free time: going out, going for a walk, staying home, and relaxing on the weekend. Simple sentences and clear pronunciation -- perfect for the free-time words and frequency adverbs from today's lesson.

Watch with English subtitles. Each time you hear a free-time word (go out, stay home, go for a walk), say it with a frequency word: "I usually go out on Saturday."

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Free Time and Hobbies"

A slow, clear podcast about free time: what people do on the weekend and how often they do it, using always, usually, often, sometimes and never.

Repeat each phrase out loud. Then record a short answer to "How often do you go out?" using a frequency word.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Adverbs of Frequency and Free Time"

A short, practical lesson on today's language: how to use always, usually, often, sometimes and never, and how to ask "How often do you...?", in clear American English.

After watching, write five sentences about your weekend with a frequency word: "I always... / I usually... / I never...". Then read them aloud and check your pronunciation.
