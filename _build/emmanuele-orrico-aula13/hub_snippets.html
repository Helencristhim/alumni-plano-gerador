Lesson 13 -- Pre-class

Bigger, Better, Stronger -- Comparing Products, Markets and Results

Compare Brazil for the Global team: how it compares to last year, which indication is the best, and where your advantage over the competitor is. Key words: competitor, benchmark, advantage, indication, portfolio, ranking, efficacy, to outperform. The grammar of the lesson is comparatives and superlatives — a consolidation of what you met in lesson 8. Compare two things with -er than or more ... than ("Brazil is stronger than last year", "atopic dermatitis is more important than DPOC") and pick the top of all with the -est or the most ("the biggest indication", "the most important market"). Careful with the irregulars: good → better → best, bad → worse → worst.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are the eight words of a comparison.

Competitor -- another company that sells a similar product
"Our main competitor launched a new product this year."
Benchmark -- a standard number you use to compare results
"Last year is our benchmark for growth."
Advantage -- something that makes you better than others
"Fast access is a big advantage in this market."
Indication -- a health problem that a medicine treats
"Atopic dermatitis is our strongest indication."
Portfolio -- all the products a company sells
"Immunology is the biggest part of our portfolio."
Ranking -- a list that shows what is first, second and third
"Brazil moved up in the global ranking this quarter."
Efficacy -- how well a treatment works
"The new treatment shows higher efficacy than the old one."
To outperform -- to do better than someone or something
"This quarter, Brazil is outperforming the forecast."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

David, from the Global team, asks Emmanuele how Brazil compares to last year. "Brazil is much stronger than last year," she says. "Our growth is higher, and access is faster." David wants to know which indication is performing the best. "Atopic dermatitis is our best indication," Emmanuele answers. "It is bigger than asthma in the private market." Then David asks about the competitor. "We have one clear advantage: higher efficacy," she explains. "That is why we are ahead of the curve." Finally, David asks if this is her most important market. "Yes. Brazil is one of the most important markets in our portfolio, and we are outperforming the forecast." David is happy: this is exactly the comparison the VP wants to hear.

1. "Brazil is stronger than last year." Why "stronger" and not "the strongest"?
A Because "strong" is an irregular word.
B Because she compares TWO things — this year and last year. Two things take -er + than.
C Because Brazil is the only market.
2. "Atopic dermatitis is our best indication." Why "best" and not "goodest"?
A Because "good" is irregular: good → better → the best. "Goodest" does not exist.
B Because "indication" is a long word.
C Because it happened in the past.
3. "Brazil is one of the most important markets." Why "most important" and not "importantest"?
A Because "important" happened last year.
B Because "important" is a long word — long words take "the most", not "-est".
C Because there are many markets.
4. Which sentence shows Brazil is doing better than expected?
A "We visited the doctors last month."
B "We are outperforming the forecast."
C "The competitor is bigger than us."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives and Superlatives

GRAMMAR

A consolidation of what you met in lesson 8. To compare TWO things, use the comparative. For the top of ALL, use the superlative.

AdjectiveComparative (two things)Superlative (top of all)
Short word
big, high, low, strong		+ -er than
bigger, higher, stronger		the + -est
the biggest, the highest
Long word
important, complex		more ... than
more important		the most ...
the most important
Irregular
good, bad		better / worse thanthe best / the worst

Careful: never use two forms together — "more bigger" does not exist. It is "bigger" (compare two) or "the biggest" (top of all). And "good" is irregular: better, the best — never "gooder" or "goodest".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Give a full comparison, out loud: open with "Compared to last year...", say what is stronger or higher, name your best indication and how it compares to another (present continuous is fine), show your advantage over the competitor (higher efficacy), and end by placing Brazil among the most important markets in the portfolio.
