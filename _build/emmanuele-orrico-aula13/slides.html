Chapter 3: Bigger and the Biggest

Compare Two, Then All

bigger than · the biggest of all

Grammar Discovery

Bigger or the Biggest?

"Q3 is stronger than Q2." (two quarters)

"Brazil is the biggest market in the region." (all markets)

"Atopic dermatitis is more important than DPOC this year." (long word)

"Our results are better than last year — the best quarter of all." (irregular)

Two things → -ER + than. The top of all → THE -EST. Long words → more / most. And good → better / best.

AdjectiveComparative (two)Superlative (all)
Short word
big, high, low, strong		+ -er than
bigger, higher, stronger		the + -est
the biggest, the highest
Long word
important, complex		more ... than

Irregular
good / bad		better / worse thanthe best / the worst

Nothing brand new today — you met these in lesson eight. The skill now is to choose fast: comparing two, or the top of all?

The Form

-er / -est · more / most · better / best

short wordhigh → higher than → the highest
long wordimportant → more important → the most important
irregulargood → better → the best · bad → worse → the worst

Never both together: "more bigger" does not exist. It is bigger, or the biggest.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Brazil is more bigger than last year."
"Brazil is bigger than last year."
"This is the more important market."
"This is the most important market."
"Our results are gooder than last year."
"Our results are better than last year."

Short words take -er / the -est: "bigger", "the biggest". Never add "more" too. Long words take more / the most. And "good" is irregular: better, the best.
