Chapter 3

The Possessive Code

my, your, his, her + have / has

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have a daughter."

"She has a big family."

"This is my apartment."

"Her neighborhood is quiet."

Subjecthave / hasPossessive
Ihavemy (I have my family.)
He / She / Ithashis / her (She has her home.)
You / We / Theyhaveyour / our / their
Possessive 'sFor names: "Maria's house" = the house of Maria.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She have a daughter."

✓ RIGHT

"She has a daughter."

✗ WRONG

"He house is big."

✓ RIGHT

"His house is big."

✗ WRONG

"My parents has a car."

✓ RIGHT

"My parents have a car."

Use has only with he, she, and it. Use have with I, you, we, and they. And remember: he is a person, but his shows possession.

"His apartment is small." (use his, not he)

"We have a nice neighborhood." (we = have)
