Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Hello! I want to tell you about my family. I have a small family. I live with my husband and my daughter. My daughter has a cat, and she loves it. We live in an apartment in a quiet neighborhood. My parents live in a house near us. Their house is small but beautiful. Every Sunday, we visit my parents. My mother has a lovely garden, and her garden is full of flowers. I love my world.

1. Why does the text say "I have a family" but "My daughter has a cat"?
A Because "have" and "has" are the same.
B Because he/she/it uses has, and I/you/we/they use have.
C Because a cat needs the word "has".
2. The text says "Their house is small." What does "their" mean here?
A The house belongs to the parents (them).
B The house belongs to one woman.
C The house belongs to me.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "My parents has a house."
B "My parents have a house."
C "My parents is a house."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Possessives + Have/Has

GRAMMAR

Como dizer o que pertence a você e usar have/has. / How to say what belongs to you and use have/has.

Subject (Sujeito)have / hasPossessive + Example
I (eu)havemy — I have my family. (Eu tenho a minha família.)
He / She / Ithashis / her — She has her home. (Ela tem a casa dela.)
You / We / Theyhaveyour / our / their — They have their house.
Possessivo com 'sPara nomes: "Maria's house" = a casa da Maria. (o 's mostra posse)
Cuidado / CarefulNão confunda he (ele, pessoa) com his (dele, posse): "His house", nunca "He house".
