Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words great speakers use to make a message land. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que grandes oradores usam para fazer a mensagem impactar.

Underscore -- to emphasize how important something is / enfatizar, ressaltar
"Let me underscore the one number that matters."
Drive home -- to make a point forcefully and completely clear / deixar um ponto bem claro, martelar
"Drive the growth figure home with conviction."
Hit home -- to have a strong, real effect on someone / causar forte impacto, tocar
"Slow down, and the message will hit home."
Gravitas -- a serious, weighty manner that earns respect / seriedade, peso, autoridade
"She delivered the number with real gravitas."
Command the room -- to hold everyone's full attention when you speak / dominar a sala, prender a atencão
"When she opened, she commanded the room."
Soundbite -- a short, striking, quotable phrase / frase de efeito, citacão marcante
"Give them one soundbite they will repeat."
Conviction -- strong belief shown in the way you speak / conviccão, firmeza
"Say the number with conviction, not apology."
Call to action -- a clear request telling people what to do next / chamada para acão
"End with a call to action: join us now."
Pause for effect -- a deliberate silence to make words land / pausa dramática
"State the number, then pause for effect."
Understate -- to make something sound less important than it is / minimizar, subestimar
"Do not understate your own results."
Hook -- an opening line that grabs attention / gancho, isca inicial
"Open with a hook, not a summary."
Punchy -- short, forceful and effective / direto e forte, impactante
"Keep the opening punchy, not wordy."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When Eliane wants a milestone to land, she moves a negative or limiting word to the front of the sentence and then inverts the subject and the auxiliary, exactly like a question. Never before have we grown this fast -- notice how "have we" follows the fronted phrase, not "we have". With the present or past simple there is no auxiliary, so she adds do, does or did: Not only did we open a clinic, but we also broke into a new market. The pattern Only after / Only when inverts the MAIN clause, not the time clause: Only after the pilot succeeded did investors take us seriously. And No sooner ... than works the same way: No sooner had we opened than the demand doubled. The effect is emphasis -- the sentence sounds deliberate and carries gravitas.

1. What happens to the subject and the verb after a fronted negative word?
A They invert: the auxiliary comes before the subject (Never before have we...).
B They stay in normal order (Never before we have...).
C The verb is dropped completely.
2. In the present or past simple, what do you add to invert?
A Nothing -- you just move the verb.
B Do, does or did before the subject (Not only did we open...).
C The word "very" before the verb.
3. In "Only after the pilot succeeded did investors believe us", which clause inverts?
A The time clause: "did the pilot succeed".
B The main clause: "did investors believe".
C Neither clause inverts.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Emphatic Inversion

GRAMMAR

We front a negative or limiting adverbial for emphasis, then invert the subject and the auxiliary, just like a question. / Colocamos um advérbio negativo ou limitante no início para dar ênfase, e depois invertemos o sujeito e o auxiliar, como numa pergunta.

Fronted phrase / InícioStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Never / Rarely / Seldom+ auxiliary + subjectNever before have we grown this fast.
Not only ... , but alsoinvert the first clause (add did)Not only did we open, but we also expanded.
Only after / when / by ...invert the MAIN clauseOnly after it worked did they believe us.
No sooner ... than+ auxiliary + subjectNo sooner had we opened than demand doubled.

Watch out: in the present or past simple there is no auxiliary, so add do / does / did and use the base verb (Not only did we open, never "Not only we opened"). / Atenção: no presente ou passado simples não há auxiliar, então acrescente do / does / did com o verbo na forma base.
