Film
The King's Speech (2010)

Tom Hooper's Oscar-winning film is about a man learning to make his words land under enormous pressure: King George VI, with a stammer, working with a speech coach to deliver the speech of his life. It is the perfect companion to this lesson -- watch how pace, the pause for effect, gravitas and conviction turn a fragile voice into one that commands the room. The final wartime broadcast is a master class in delivery.

Tip: after the final speech scene, write two emphatic sentences about the King's journey -- one with "Never before..." and one with "Only after... did...".

Podcast
Think Fast, Talk Smart -- Stanford GSB

Matt Abrahams, a Stanford lecturer, hosts this show entirely about the skills in this lesson: how to open with a hook, deliver a soundbite, speak with conviction and command the room. Episodes on structuring a message, on pausing, and on making an idea land connect directly to emphatic inversion -- the grammar that carries the emphasis. This is how communication experts talk about impact in real business English.

Tip: pick one episode and note two techniques the host recommends, then rephrase one of your own points as an emphatic, inverted sentence.

YouTube
BBC English Masterclass -- "Inversion 1: After Negative or Limiting Adverbs"

A short, clear BBC Learning English masterclass on exactly this lesson's grammar: inversion after negative or limiting adverbs -- Never before have we..., Not only did we..., Rarely do we... It shows step by step how the subject and the auxiliary swap places for emphasis. Watch it to lock in the structure you use to make your milestones land.

Tip: after the video, say three emphatic sentences about your own work out loud, each starting with a different fronted phrase (Never before..., Not only..., Only after...).
