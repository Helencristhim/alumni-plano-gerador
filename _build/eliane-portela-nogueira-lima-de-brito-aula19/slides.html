Chapter 3

Emphatic Inversion

Front the negative, flip the subject, add force

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Never before have we grown this fast."

"Not only did we open a clinic, but we also broke into a new market."

"Only after the pilot succeeded did investors take us seriously."

"Rarely do audiences remember the data; they remember the story."

Fronted phraseThenExample
Never / Rarely / Seldomauxiliary + subjectNever before have we grown this fast.
Not only ... , but alsoinvert the first clauseNot only did we open, but we also expanded.
Only after / when / by ...invert the MAIN clauseOnly after it worked did they believe us.
No sooner ... than / Not until ...auxiliary + subjectNo sooner had we opened than demand doubled.

Move the auxiliary before the subject, exactly like a question. In the present or past simple there is no auxiliary, so add do / does / did: "we grew" becomes "did we grow". Never keep normal word order after a fronted negative adverbial.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Never before we have grown this fast.
Never before have we grown this fast.
Not only we opened a clinic, but we also expanded.
Not only did we open a clinic, but we also expanded.
Only after it worked investors believed us.
Only after it worked did investors believe us.

After a fronted negative or limiting phrase, invert the subject and the auxiliary, just like a question. If the verb is present or past simple, add do / does / did. Only the "Only after..." type inverts the main clause, not the time clause.

Making the Update Land

J
Eliane, your investor update is full of great news. But can I be honest? Right now, it does not land.
E

Because you understate everything. You need a hook, and you need gravitas.
E
So how do I open? With the big number?

Open with a soundbite. Not only should you state the milestone, but you should also pause for effect.

Exactly. Rarely does an update end with a clear call to action. Do that, and you will command the room.
