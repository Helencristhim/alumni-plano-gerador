Chapter 3: Your Toolkit

Four Toolkits

Questions · Opinions · Numbers · Orders

Review the Code

Listen and Remember

Where is the meeting room?

I think the plan is good. I agree.

ToolkitExample
Questions (L11)Where is the meeting? What is your name?
Opinions (L12)I think... I agree. / I do not agree.
Numbers (L13)The total is bigger / higher than last year.
Orders (L14)I would like... / Can I have...?, please.

Four toolkits, one day. Ask with question words, share your opinion, present the numbers, and order politely.

Common Mistakes

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Where you work?"

✓ RIGHT

"Where do you work?"

✗ WRONG

"The total is more big."

✓ RIGHT

"The total is bigger."

✗ WRONG

"I want a coffee."

✓ RIGHT

"I would like a coffee, please."

Remember: use do in questions, use -er for comparatives (bigger, higher), and order with I would like + please.

Anna's Full Day

Dialogue

Anna and a Colleague

C
Good morning, Anna. Welcome. The team is on the third floor.
A
Thank you. Where is the meeting room, please?
C
It is next to the coffee room. We talk about the numbers at ten.
A
Good. In my opinion, the total is bigger this year.
C
I agree. And at noon we have a business lunch with a client.
A
Perfect. I would like to read the menu first.
