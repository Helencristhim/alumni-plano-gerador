YouTube · BBC Learning English
6 Minute Grammar: The Present Perfect with 'just', 'already' and 'yet' (free)

Six minutes, two presenters, and exactly the three little words you learned tonight — just, already, yet — from a different mouth, with different examples. They say the sentences at normal speed, so your ear hears where "have" gets small and joins the word before it. Six minutes is nothing: you can do it while the coffee is on the stove.

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, pause after every example and build your own sentence out loud, about a real arrival: "I have just arrived in Lisbon", "I haven't unpacked yet". One thing to notice: they are British, and your material is American. The grammar is identical — only the music of the voice changes, and in Europe you will hear both. Good practice for the ear.
