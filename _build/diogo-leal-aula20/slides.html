Useful Language

How to Survive the Hot Seat

The straight answer → the reason → what you own → the number or the hour → the plan

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one answers? Which one owns it? Which one buys you the time you need?

"Yes. The go-live moved twice, and I am accountable for the date."

"That is on me. I should have escalated a week earlier."

"I do not have that number now. You will have it by five."

"Let me explain what protects the new date."

Grammar Rule

Answer, Own, Prove

MoveStructureExample
Answer firstYes / No / a number -- one sentence, then the reasonYes. The go-live moved twice. The reason is the approval.
Own the part that is yoursThat is on me + I should have + past participleThat is on me. I should have escalated earlier.
Never guessI do not have that number now. + I will send it by + hourI do not have it now. I will send it by five.

The calm person owns the room. They are not looking for a perfect project -- they are looking for somebody who is accountable, who answers straight, and who never guesses. One honest "I do not know, by five o'clock" is worth more than ten confident guesses.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I will send you it tomorrow."
"I will send it to you tomorrow."
"Let me to explain the number."
"Let me explain the number."

Com send, o pronome objeto vem primeiro: send it to you (nunca "send you it"). E depois de let me vem o verbo puro: let me explain, nunca "let me to explain".

What I Learned

I answer first -- yes, no, or a number -- and explain after.
