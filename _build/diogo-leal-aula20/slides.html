Chapter 3 -- Still Waiting

Still Waiting

Talk about time and waiting -- without blame

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words tell us about time and waiting?

"I have not received your reply yet."

"As of today, the budget is still pending."

"As of Friday, I am still awaiting the report."

"We have not heard back yet."

Grammar Rule

Yet, Still, As Of

WordPatternExample
yethave/has not + verb + yet"I have not received it yet."
stillbe + still + adjective"The budget is still pending."
as ofAs of + time, ..."As of today, the date is not confirmed."

Key idea: These words chase a reply without blame. "You did not send it" sounds like an attack. "I have not received it yet" or "it is still pending" is calm and factual. Yet goes at the end; still goes before the adjective; as of + a time opens the sentence.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I did not receive your reply yet. The budget is pending still."
"I have not received your reply yet. The budget is still pending."

Use have not + verb + yet -- never "did not ... yet". And still goes before the adjective: "still pending", not "pending still".
