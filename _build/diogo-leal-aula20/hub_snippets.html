Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Accountable -- the person who answers for the result, good or bad
"I am accountable for this date." (Responsável (presta contas))
Criticism -- what people say when they think you did badly
"Criticism is information. Listen, and then answer." (Crítica)
Defend -- to explain and protect a decision, with facts
"I can defend every date in this plan." (Defender)
Blame -- to say that a person is the cause of the problem
"The room wants to blame somebody. Do not join." (Culpar)
Straight answer -- yes, no, or a number: before the explanation
"Give them a straight answer, then the reason." (Resposta direta)
Calm -- steady: your voice does not change under attack
"Stay calm. The calm person owns the room." (Calmo)
Stand by -- to keep supporting something, even under pressure
"I stand by the decision, and here is why." (Manter / sustentar)
Credibility -- the trust people give you, built answer by answer
"One honest "I do not know" saves your credibility." (Credibilidade)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Three directors, twenty minutes, and the first question is an attack: whose fault is the delay? The instinct is to defend yourself and explain the context. That instinct loses the room. Diogo gives a straight answer instead: "Yes. The go-live moved twice, and I am accountable for the date." Then the reason -- the approval -- and then the part that is his: "That is on me. I should have escalated a week earlier." He does not blame anybody who is not in the room. He stands by June 27th, and names the two things that protect it. When they ask for a number he does not have, he refuses to guess: "You will have it by five o'clock." That honesty is what builds his credibility. He stays calm -- the calm person owns the room -- and he takes the criticism as information. And he does not leave without asking for the one thing he needs: the client's security team, on a call, this week.

1. Why does Diogo answer "Yes" before explaining?
A Because explaining before answering makes the room think you are hiding something.
B Because the delay was not important.
C Because he wants the meeting to end quickly.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A I will send it to you by five.
B I will send you it by five.
C I will you send it by five.
3. They ask for a number he does not have. He:
A Says he will not guess, and gives an hour to send it.
B Gives an approximate number, to look confident.
C Says the finance team should answer that.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Answer, Own, Prove

GRAMMAR

Sobreviver ao comitê: responder direto, assumir sua parte e nunca chutar número. / Surviving the hot seat.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Responder / Answer firstYes / No / um número -- uma frase, e SÓ ENTÃO o motivoYes. The go-live moved twice.
Assumir / Own itThat is on me + I should have + particípioThat is on me. I should have escalated earlier.
Não chutar / Never guessI do not have that number now. + I will send it by + horaYou will have it by five.
Ponto-chave / Key pointCom send: send it TO you (nunca "send you it"). Depois de let me, verbo puro: let me explain. E a regra que salva a reputação: um "I do not know, by five o'clock" honesto vale mais do que dez chutes confiantes.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of the hot seat. Record each one. Steady, slow, no apology in the voice -- the calm person owns the room.

Yes. I am accountable for the date.
Sim. Eu sou o responsável pela data.
That is on me. I should have escalated earlier.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. A director asks: "Whose fault is the delay?" You:
A Answer straight, name the cause, and own the part that is yours.
B Explain the context for three minutes, then answer.
C Say it was the vendor, the client and the security team.
2. They ask for a number you do not have. You:
A "I will not guess it in this room. You will have it by five."
B Give an approximate number, to look confident.
C Say nothing, and hope they forget.
3. Before you leave the hot seat, you should:
A Ask for the one thing you need to protect the date.
B Apologize one more time.
C Promise a date you are not sure about.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real problem on your project at Oracle -- a delay, an overspend, a decision that failed. Defend it out loud, for two minutes, in front of three directors: (1) the straight answer, (2) the reason, (3) the part you own, (4) the number you will NOT guess -- and when you will send it, (5) the one thing you need from them. Record it.
Now a director says: "So you admit the project is out of control. Should we replace you?" Answer out loud. Do not defend yourself, do not raise your voice -- answer straight, hold the date, and say what protects it. Record it.
