Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Read each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Chase -- to politely ask again about something when you have had no reply yet
"I will chase the reply if I do not hear back by Friday." (Cobrar / correr atrás de uma resposta)
Reminder -- a short, polite note that asks again about something
"Just a gentle reminder about the budget." (Lembrete)
Pending -- still open and waiting; not finished or confirmed yet
"As of today, the budget is still pending." (Pendente)
Awaiting -- waiting for something, like a reply or a decision
"I am still awaiting your reply on the budget." (Aguardando)
Overdue -- past the deadline; it should already be done
"The report is two days overdue." (Atrasado / vencido)
Escalate -- to raise an issue to a manager or higher level when it is stuck
"If it stays overdue, I will escalate to the director." (Escalar / elevar o problema)
Loop in -- to add the right person, like a manager, to the email chain
"I will loop in our director so we can move fast." (Incluir / envolver alguém)
Urgent -- needs fast action because time is short
"The matter is now urgent -- the launch is at risk." (Urgente)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo sent his email on Monday, but Maria did not reply. So he writes a follow up. He stays warm: "Just a gentle reminder about the budget." He gives a clear status without blame: "I have not received your reply yet. As of today, the budget is still pending." The deadline is close, so the task is almost overdue. He makes a polite ask -- "Could you please confirm by Friday?" -- and prepares to escalate: "If I do not hear back, I will loop in our director." He marks the matter urgent, but adds, "I am sure we can solve it quickly." Calm, clear, and firm.

1. Why does Diogo write a follow-up?
A Maria did not reply, and the budget is still pending.
B Because he wants to complain.
C Because the task is finished.
2. How does he give the status without blame?
A "I have not received your reply yet; it is still pending."
B "You did not answer me. You are late."
C "Where is my answer?"
3. What does he plan to do if there is no reply?
A Loop in the director and mark the matter urgent.
B Stay silent and wait forever.
C Cancel the project.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Chasing Without Blame

GRAMMAR

Como cobrar uma resposta sem culpar a pessoa. / How to chase a reply without blaming the person.

Word / PalavraPattern / PadrãoExample / Exemplo
yethave/has not + verbo + yet"I have not received it yet." (Ainda não recebi)
stillbe + still + adjetivo"The budget is still pending." (Ainda está pendente)
as ofAs of + tempo, ..."As of today, the date is not confirmed." (Até agora / a partir de hoje)
Common error / Erro comumUse have not + verbo + yet ("I have not received it yet"), nunca "did not receive yet". E still vem ANTES do adjetivo ("still pending"), não "pending still".
Key point / Ponto-chaveEstas palavras cobram sem culpar. "You did not send it" soa como ataque. "I have not received it yet" ou "it is still pending" é calmo e factual.
