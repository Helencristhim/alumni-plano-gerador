Chapter 3: The Future Code

Going To & Will

"I am going to..." / "I will..."

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Next month I am going to visit Italy."

"We are going to book a flight tomorrow."

"Don't worry, I will help you pack."

"I think the trip will be amazing."

FormExampleUse
am/is/are going to + verbI am going to visit Italy.a plan you already have
will + verbI will help you pack.a decision now / a promise
will + verbThe trip will be amazing.a prediction (what you think)

Use going to for plans you already decided (I am going to book a flight). Use will for a decision you make now, a promise, or a prediction (I will help you / It will be great). Use time words like next week, tomorrow, next year.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am going book a flight."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to book a flight."

✗ WRONG

"I will to visit Rome."

✓ RIGHT

"I will visit Rome."

That sounds exciting! Did you book your flight yet?

Nice! Do you have an itinerary for each day?

I will write a simple one, with time for sightseeing. My budget is small, but it will be a great adventure.
