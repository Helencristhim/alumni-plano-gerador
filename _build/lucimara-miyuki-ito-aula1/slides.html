Chapter 3: The Code

Your Story in Three Structures

I work · I'm preparing · I've worked for / since

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work in the chemical industry and I negotiate with our suppliers."

"Right now, I am preparing a new product registration."

"I have worked in this industry for twenty years."

"I have been a director since 2015."

Why green for twenty years and since 2015, but blue for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I + verb		Your job, your routine, what is always trueI work in the chemical industry.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Happening now, or this week / this month — not every dayRight now, I am preparing a registration.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		It started in the past and it is still true today (for / since)I have worked here for twenty years.

for + a period (for twenty years) · since + a starting point (since 2015).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work in the chemical industry for twenty years."
"I've worked in the chemical industry for twenty years."
"I am responsible of production."
"I am responsible for production."

Time that continues until today takes have / has + past participle with for / since. And the word after responsible is always for.
