Complementary Activities

Your 40 minutes to work and 40 minutes back are the biggest asset of this program. These materials were chosen for the car — audio first. Mark them as done after you listen.

Podcast
American English Podcast — Ep. 208: How to Improve the Four Skills (Listening, Reading, Speaking and Writing)

This is the base podcast of your program: American English, controlled pace, full transcript on the website. This episode goes straight to your pain point — how to really train your ear, instead of just having English playing in the background.

Tip (do it in the car): listen ONCE, without pausing. Then write down 3 sentences you understood clearly and 2 you could NOT catch. Bring those 2 to lesson 2 — they become the mini listening drill of the next class.

Podcast — current affairs
CNN 10 — the news of the day in 10 minutes

Ten minutes, American English, a firm but clear pace, and a new episode every weekday. It is the perfect format for the drive to Guarulhos: it fits the commute, and the topic is the news — the same kind of small talk that comes up at a trade fair dinner.

Tip: at the beginning, listen to the same episode TWICE (once on the way there, once on the way back). The second time you will catch whole sentences that you missed. That is not repetition for nothing — that is how the ear is trained.

YouTube
English Tenses: Learn PRESENT PERFECT with a game (engVid)

The structure of lesson 1 — have / has + past participle with for and since. It is the sentence that opens any professional conversation of yours: “I’ve worked in the chemical industry for twenty years.” The lesson introduces it; this video makes it stick.

Tip: after the video, say 5 sentences about YOUR life — two with present perfect (for / since), two with present continuous (what you are doing this month) and one with present simple (your routine). Out loud, without writing them first.
