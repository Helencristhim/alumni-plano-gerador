Chapter 3

How to Ask

Ask which line, how to get there, and about transfers

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Which subway goes to Times Square?"

"How do I get to the airport?"

"Where is the nearest subway?"

"Do I need to transfer?"

StructureUseExample
Which + noun + goes to...?Choose between options (lines, buses)Which bus goes to the park?
Where is the nearest + place?Find the closest oneWhere is the nearest subway?
Do I need to + verb?Ask if something is necessaryDo I need to transfer?

Use "Which" to choose between options; "How do I get to" for the way (from Lesson 13).

At the Station

Buying a Ticket

E
"Excuse me, which subway goes to Times Square?"
S
"The red line goes to Times Square. The station is over there."
E
"How much is a ticket?"
S
"A single ticket is three dollars. Here you are."
E
"Thank you. Where is the platform?"
S
"Go down the stairs. The platform is on your left."
E
"Do I need to transfer?"
S
"No, the red line is direct. No transfer."
E
"How many stops is it?"
S
"It is about six stops. Watch the schedule on the screen."
E
"Great. Where is the bus stop, just in case?"
S
"The bus stop is across the street. Have a safe trip!"
