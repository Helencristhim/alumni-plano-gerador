Chapter 4: Separable and Inseparable

Split It or Keep It

How phrasal verbs work

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I filled out the form." = "I filled the form out."

"I picked her up at six."

"I ran into a friend."

"Please look after the kids."

TypeRuleExample
Separableobject can go in the middleI filled out the form / I filled the form out.
Separable + pronounpronoun MUST go in the middleI picked her up. (not "picked up her")
Inseparablewords stay togetherI ran into a friend. (not "ran a friend into")

Separable? The object can move (fill it out). Inseparable? The words stay glued (run into her). A pronoun always sits in the middle of a separable verb.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Verb

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"Please ___look after the children." (take care of)
Language Focus

Separable or Not?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I pick up her after work."

✓ RIGHT

"I pick her up after work."

✗ WRONG

"I ran a friend into today."

✓ RIGHT

"I ran into a friend today."

A pronoun (her, him, it) always goes in the middle of a separable verb (pick her up). Never split an inseparable verb like run into; keep the words together.

Dialogue

A Busy Day

A
Tom, my mornings are so busy right now. I wake up at six and I barely stop until night.
T
I know that feeling! Do you get up right away, or do you stay in bed for a while?
A
I stay in bed for ten minutes. Then I get up, make coffee, and drop off my daughter at school.
T
That is a lot before work! Do you ever run into people you know on the way?
A
All the time! This morning I ran into my neighbor, and we get along so well that we talked for ages.
T
Nice. And how is the paperwork going? You had a lot of forms to fill out, right?
A
So many forms. I almost gave up on one of them, but I never give up for long.
T
Good for you. Do you have plans tonight, or will you turn down everything and rest?
A
I have to pick up my daughter and look after her. I do not want to put off my rest, so no plans tonight!
