Chapter 4: Used To

The Way It Used To Be

How we talk about old habits and states

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I used to walk to the same bakery every morning."

"She used to live in a small town."

"We used to study together until midnight."

"I used to feel homesick all the time."

FormStructureExample
Affirmativesubject + used to + base verbI used to walk to the bakery.
Negativesubject + did not use to + base verbI did not use to worry.
QuestionDid + subject + use to + base verb?Did you use to live there?

"Used to" + base verb means it was true in the past but is not true now. In the negative and the question, the "d" disappears: I did not use to, Did you use to.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Form

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

Language Focus

Habit or State in the Past?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am used to walk to the bakery." (past habit)

✓ RIGHT

"I used to walk to the bakery." (past habit)

✗ WRONG

"I used to living in a big city now." (comfortable now)

✓ RIGHT

"I am used to living in a big city now." (comfortable now)

"Used to + base verb" is a PAST habit that is not true now (I used to walk). "To be used to + -ing" means you are comfortable with something now (I am used to living here, from Lesson 13). Different structures, different meanings.

Dialogue

Then and Now

A
You know, this street reminds me of my old neighborhood back then.
D
Oh yeah? What was your life like back then?
A
Very quiet. I used to walk to a little bakery every morning. I really miss it.
D
I know that feeling. I used to live in a small town too. I have fond memories of it.
A
Really? Was it hard to adjust when you moved?
D
At first, yes. I used to feel lost all the time. But eventually, this city became home.
A
That is exactly it. Nowadays my lifestyle is so different, but I have fond memories of both places.
D
Same here. Sometimes I look back and feel nostalgic, but I would not change a thing.
Did You Catch It?

About Dave

1. Where did Dave use to live?
In a small town, and he has fond memories of it.
2. How did Dave use to feel when he first moved?
He used to feel lost all the time; at first it was hard to adjust.
3. How does Dave feel about the city now?
Eventually it became home, and he would not change a thing.
