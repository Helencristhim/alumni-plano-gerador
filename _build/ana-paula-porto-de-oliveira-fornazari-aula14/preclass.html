Lesson 14 -- Pre-class

The Way I Used To Live

Talking about past habits and how everyday life changes. Key words: to miss, nowadays, back then, to look back, to remind, familiar, fond, nostalgic, to realize, to adjust, lifestyle, eventually. Grammar: used to for past habits and states -- I used to walk, I did not use to worry, Did you use to live there?

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words to talk about the past and how life has changed. Listen to each one and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

To miss -- to feel sad that someone or something is not with you
"I really miss my family back home."
Nowadays -- at the present time, in contrast with the past
"Nowadays, I feel much more confident."
Back then -- at that time in the past
"Back then, I did not speak any English."
To look back -- to think about something in your past
"When I look back, I feel proud of myself."
To remind -- to make someone remember something
"This song reminds me of my old home."
Familiar -- well known to you and easy to recognize
"The streets are familiar to me now."
Fond -- having warm, gentle feelings about someone or something
"I have fond memories of that little town."
Nostalgic -- feeling happy and a little sad when you remember the past
"Old photos always make me nostalgic."
To realize -- to suddenly understand or notice something
"I realized that I could belong to two places."
To adjust -- to change a little so you feel comfortable in a new situation
"It took me months to adjust to the new city."
Lifestyle -- the way a person lives from day to day
"My lifestyle is much busier nowadays."
Eventually -- in the end, after a long time
"At first it was hard, but eventually it felt like home."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Back then, before I moved, my lifestyle used to be very simple. Every morning I used to walk to the same little bakery, and everything felt familiar. I never used to worry about small things. Nowadays, my life is very different. At first I used to miss my old home a lot, and a song on the radio still reminds me of those days and makes me nostalgic. But eventually I started to adjust, and now I look back and realize that I have fond memories of both places.

1. "My lifestyle used to be very simple" means...
A My lifestyle was simple in the past, but it is not simple now.
B My lifestyle is still simple today.
C My lifestyle will be simple in the future.
2. Which sentence is correct in the negative?
A "I did not use to worry about small things."
B "I did not used to worry about small things."
C "I do not used to worry about small things."
3. Which sentence talks about a past habit that is not true now?
A "I walk to the bakery every morning."
B "I used to walk to the bakery every morning."
C "I will walk to the bakery every morning."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Used To for Past Habits

GRAMMAR

Use "used to" + the base verb to talk about a habit or a state that was true in the PAST but is not true now. The form is the same for every subject (I, you, she, they). In the negative and the question, the "d" disappears: use to.

FormAffirmativeNegative & Question
Past habit (action)I used to walk to work every day.
They used to visit us.		I did not use to walk to work.
Did you use to walk there?
Past state (feeling)I used to feel homesick.
She used to love this song.		I never used to feel homesick.
Did she use to live here?
RememberCareful: "used to do" (a past habit, this lesson) is NOT the same as "to be used to doing" from Lesson 13 (to be comfortable with something). Here we talk about the PAST: used to + base verb, true before but not now.
