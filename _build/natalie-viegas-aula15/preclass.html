Project Kick-off -- Giving Updates

Run a project kick-off, give updates, and describe what will be happening with the future continuous (will be + verb-ing: "Next week I will be onboarding the team"). New vocabulary: onboard, timeline, workstream, owner, assign, status update, progress, on track, bottleneck, phase.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Onboard -- to bring a new person or client into a project and get them ready
"We will onboard the new users next week." (Integrar)
Timeline -- the planned order and dates of the steps in a project
"The timeline is tight, but realistic." (Cronograma)
Workstream -- one separate part of a project, with its own tasks and goal
"Each workstream has a clear owner." (Frente de trabalho)
Owner -- the person responsible for a task or part of the project
"Every deliverable needs a clear owner." (Responsável)
Assign -- to give a task or responsibility to a specific person
"I will assign the data workstream to Maria." (Atribuir)
Status update -- a short report on how the work is going right now
"I will lead the weekly status update." (Atualização de status)
Progress -- movement forward toward a goal; how much is done
"We will review progress at the end of each phase." (Progresso)
On track -- going as planned, according to the schedule
"The first sprint is on track for Friday." (No prazo / dentro do plano)
Bottleneck -- a point in the process that slows down all the rest of the work
"The data migration could become a bottleneck." (Gargalo)
Phase -- one distinct stage of a project, with a start and an end
"We will wrap up the first phase in April." (Fase / etapa)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Continuous

GRAMMAR

Usamos o future continuous para descrever uma ação EM ANDAMENTO num momento futuro, ou um plano já organizado. A forma é will be + verbo-ing, igual para todos os sujeitos. / We use the future continuous for an action in progress at a future moment, or a planned part of the work. The form is will be + verb-ing, the same for every subject.

SubjectFormExample
Iwill be + -ingNext week I will be onboarding the team.
Shewill be + -ingIn March she will be leading the calls.
Wewill be + -ingWe will be wrapping up phase one.
Theywill be + -ingThey will be running the first sprint.

Key point / Ponto-chave: sempre mantenha o be: will be + verbo-ing. Nunca diga "will onboarding" nem "will being onboarding". Compare: "I will send the report" (ação única, completa) vs "I will be sending weekly reports" (ação em andamento ao longo do tempo).
