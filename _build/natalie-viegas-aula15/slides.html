Chapter 3 -- Planning the Work

The Future Continuous

What will be happening, step by step

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What is happening in the middle of a future moment?

Next week, I will be onboarding the new users.

During March, she will be leading the status calls.

This time next month, we will be wrapping up the first phase.

By Friday, the team will be running the first sprint.

Grammar Rule

The Future Continuous

Subjectwill be + verb-ing
Iwill be onboarding the team next week.
Shewill be leading the status calls in March.
Wewill be wrapping up the phase next month.
Theywill be running the first sprint by Friday.

Key idea: use will be + verb-ing for an action that will be in progress at a future moment, or for a planned part of the work. The form is the same for every subject. Compare: "I will send the report" (one completed action) vs "I will be sending weekly reports" (an action happening over time).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Next week I will onboarding the team."
"Next week I will be onboarding the team."

Always keep the be: will be + verb-ing. Never say "will onboarding" or "will being onboarding".

Dialogue

The Kick-off Call

N

"Welcome to the kick-off, everyone. Let me walk you through the timeline and the workstreams. Each one has a clear owner."

T

"Great. So next week, my team will be onboarding the new users, right? Which workstream will you be assigning to me?"

N

"Exactly. You will be owning the onboarding workstream. I will be leading the weekly status update, so we always know if we are on track."

T

"Sounds good. What about the data migration? I am worried it could become a bottleneck."

N

"Good point. We will be monitoring it from day one. By Friday, the team will be running the first sprint, so we will catch any problem early."

T

"Perfect. So this time next month, we will be wrapping up the first phase. Let us get started."
