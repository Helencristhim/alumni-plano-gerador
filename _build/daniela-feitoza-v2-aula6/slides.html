The Process Rule

Present simple for systems, processes and facts

Process Discovery

Listen and Notice

When does the verb take an -s? When do we use does and does not?

Process Rule

Facts vs Forms

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb(+s for it)"SAP processes data automatically."
NegativeSubject + does not + verb"The system does not crash often."
QuestionDoes + subject + verb?"Does SAP integrate with your CRM?"
UseFacts that are always true"The module tracks payments."

Remember: he / she / it + verb + s (the module tracks). After does / does not, no -s.

Common Mistake

Wrong Form vs Right Form

✗ WRONG

"The system track every payment."

✓ RIGHT

"The system tracks every payment." (it + verb + s)

✗ WRONG

"The system does not tracks errors."

✓ RIGHT

"The system does not track errors." (does not + verb)

Present simple: add -s with he/she/it. After "does" or "does not", use the base verb with no -s.

Listening

The Coach’s Tip

A voice note about describing systems. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. Why do we use the present simple to describe a system?
Because these are facts that are always true, like "SAP processes thousands of transactions every day."
2. What happens to the verb with "it", "the system" or "the module"?
The verb takes an -s: "the system processes", "the module tracks".
3. How do you make a question and a negative?
Use "does" for the question ("Does SAP integrate?") and "does not" for the negative ("The system does not make mistakes").
