Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Experience -- the knowledge and skill you get from doing a job (experiência)
"I have a lot of experience with global teams."
Background -- your education and past work history (formação, histórico)
"Tell me a bit about your background."
Achievement -- something important you succeeded in doing (conquista)
"Closing that deal was my biggest achievement."
Career -- your working life and the jobs you have over time (carreira)
"I started my career in technical support."
Lead -- to be in charge of a team or a project (liderar)
"I lead the onboarding for our biggest account."
Handle -- to deal with a difficult situation or problem (lidar com)
"I handle our most demanding clients."
Responsibility -- a duty you are in charge of (responsabilidade)
"Adoption is my main responsibility."
Previously -- before now, in an earlier time (anteriormente)
"Previously, I worked at a fintech company."
Recently -- a short time ago, not long ago (recentemente)
"I recently joined the customer experience group."
So far -- up to now, until this moment (até agora)
"So far, I have led three rollouts."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Patrícia has worked in customer success for eight years. She started her career in technical support, and in 2015 she moved to a fintech company. There, she handled the largest clients. In 2019, she joined Salesforce. Since then, she has led three major rollouts and has supported more than thirty premium accounts. "So far, I have never missed a renewal," she says proudly. When a new colleague asked about her background last week, she told her whole story with confidence.

1. Por que "she joined Salesforce in 2019" usa o past simple?
A Porque a ação ainda continua hoje.
B Porque há um tempo terminado e específico (in 2019).
C Porque é uma rotina que se repete toda semana.
2. "Since then, she has led three rollouts" usa o present perfect porque:
A O período começou no passado e continua até agora.
B A ação terminou num dia específico do passado.
C É algo que ela vai fazer no futuro.
3. Which sentence about experience up to now is correct?
A "I work here since 2019."
B "I have worked here since 2019."
C "I am working here since 2019."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo para falar da sua experiência (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Past Simple
verb + ed / irregular		Ação terminada com tempo definido. Finished action with a clear past time.I joined in 2019.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experiência até agora, tempo não terminado. Experience up to now.I have worked here since 2019.
since vs forsince + ponto no tempo · for + duração. Point in time vs length of time.since 2019 · for eight years
Irregular participlesParticípios irregulares comuns. Common irregular participles.lead → led · be → been · do → done
Signal wordsin 2019, last year, ago → past simple · for, since, so far, recently, ever, never → present perfect

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

A colleague asks: "How long have you been in customer success?" You answer:
A "I am in customer success since eight years."
B "I have worked in customer success for eight years."
C "I work in customer success since eight years."
You want to give a dated fact about when you started. Which is correct?
A "I have joined Salesforce in 2019."
B "I joined Salesforce in 2019."
C "I join Salesforce in 2019."
You describe a job you had before this one. The best answer is:
A "Previously, I worked as an account manager."
B "Previously, I have worked as an account manager."
C "Previously, I am working as an account manager."
You want to talk about your experience up to now, using "so far". You say:
A "So far, I have led three major rollouts."
