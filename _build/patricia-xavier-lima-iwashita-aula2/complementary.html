Aula 2 -- Talking About Your Experience

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

Mike walks into a job interview and has to talk about his background, his experience and what he has done so far — under pressure. Listen for how characters mix the Past Simple (finished, dated facts) with the Present Perfect (experience up to now). Connection to Lesson 2: describing your career and achievements with confidence.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences where someone talks about past experience, and mark whether they used Past Simple or Present Perfect.

Business English Pod -- "Talking About Your Career & Work Experience"

Short, clear episodes with the exact phrases people use to describe their background and achievements on a call or in an interview. Hear the natural difference between "I worked there for five years" (finished) and "I have worked here since 2019" (up to now). Connection to Lesson 2: the language you need to present your experience to global teams.

Tip: listen twice. Note 5 phrases you could use to describe your own career and experience.

YouTube
"Present Perfect vs Past Simple" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's contrast: the Present Perfect for experience and unfinished time (for, since, so far) versus the Past Simple for finished, dated actions (in 2019, last year). Connection to Lesson 2: this is the grammar that will make you talk about your experience without hesitation.

Tip: pause and say one Past Simple sentence (a dated fact) and one Present Perfect sentence (experience so far) about your own career after each example.
