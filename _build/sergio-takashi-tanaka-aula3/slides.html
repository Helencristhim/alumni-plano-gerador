Chapter 3: The Code

Done — and It Still Matters

We have reviewed · The bank has breached

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We have reviewed the exposure this week."

"The counterparty has breached the limit twice."

"We have not escalated it to the board yet."

"Last quarter, we reduced the position."

Why have / has + verb for this week, but the simple past for last quarter?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		A past action with a result or a link to NOW. No finished time. Signals: already, yet, just, so far, since, for.We have reviewed the report.
Past Simple
verb + -ed / irregular		A finished action at a finished time. Signals: last quarter, yesterday, in January.We reviewed it last quarter.
The past participleRegular = -ed (review → reviewed, breach → breached). Irregular changes shape: do → done, fall → fallen, be → been, take → taken.

Result up to now = present perfect · Finished time = past simple.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have reviewed the report yesterday."
"I reviewed the report yesterday."
"The bank has breach the limit."
"The bank has breached the limit."
"We have escalated it two weeks ago."
"We escalated it two weeks ago."

Use the present perfect (have / has + past participle) for a result up to now, with no finished time. A finished time like yesterday or two weeks ago needs the past simple, and after have or has the verb is always the past participle (breached, done, fallen).

Chapter 4: The Committee Meeting

Report and Defend

The committee has questions

Dialogue

In the Committee

C
Sergio, I have read your report, and I have two concerns. The counterparty has breached the limit twice, and the buffer looks thin.
S
Thank you, Claudia. You are right. We have already reduced the position, and we have added a hedge this week.
C
Good. But you have not escalated it to the board. Why not?
S
We have not escalated it yet because the exposure has fallen below the threshold since Monday.
C
I see. So what do you recommend for the committee?
S
I recommend that we raise the buffer and flag the counterparty to the regulator today.
Comprehension

About Claudia

1. What has Claudia read?
Sergio's report.
2. What are her two concerns?
The counterparty has breached the limit twice, and the buffer looks thin.
3. What does she ask Sergio to explain?
Why the team has not escalated the issue to the board.
