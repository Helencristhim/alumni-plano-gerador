Talk
"How to make hard choices" -- Ruth Chang (TED)

A short, clear talk about how we make difficult decisions and defend them with reasons -- exactly what you do when you recommend an action in a committee. Connection to Lesson 3: notice how the speaker weighs options and justifies a choice, and hear the present perfect when she talks about what we have done and learned.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Then say one committee recommendation out loud in the present perfect: "We have reviewed the options, and I recommend..."

Podcast
"The Indicator from Planet Money" -- NPR

Ten-minute episodes on one number or one decision in the economy, in clear English. Connection to Lesson 3: each episode reports what has happened and why it matters now -- perfect present-perfect practice for a busy risk manager.

Tip: pick one recent episode, listen once for the main idea, then summarize it in three sentences using the present perfect: what has changed, what people have done, and what is still outstanding.

Read
The Guardian -- Economics

Free, well-written economics and finance news in clear English, close to the Financial Times you already read. Connection to Lesson 3: reading is your strength, so use it to feed your speaking. Many headlines report what a bank, a regulator, or a committee has done and how it still matters now.

Tip: read one short article, then close it and tell the story out loud, mixing the two tenses: the past simple for the finished events, and the present perfect for the result up to now.
