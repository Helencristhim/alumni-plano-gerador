Midpoint Review -- How Far Have You Come?

A midpoint review of your English journey. Key words: progress, milestone, confidence, fluency, comfort zone, to keep up with, strength, area for improvement, breakthrough, to take stock, self-assessment, halfway point. Structures: past simple, present continuous, and future forms (going to / will) used together to describe your journey.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Progress -- steady improvement over time (progresso)
"I can see real progress in my speaking."
Milestone -- an important point you reach (marco)
"This class is an important milestone."
Confidence -- the feeling of being sure of yourself (confiança)
"My confidence has grown a lot."
Fluency -- speaking smoothly and easily (fluência)
"Fluency matters more than perfection."
Comfort zone -- the easy, familiar situation (zona de conforto)
"Speaking English pushes me out of my comfort zone."
To keep up with -- to stay at the same level as something (acompanhar)
"I can keep up with fast conversations now."
Strength -- something you are good at (ponto forte)
"Small talk is now a real strength."
Area for improvement -- something you still need to work on (ponto a melhorar)
"Pronunciation is my main area for improvement."
Breakthrough -- a sudden, important step forward (avanço)
"Last week I had a real breakthrough."
To take stock -- to review your situation carefully (fazer um balanço)
"It is a good moment to take stock."
Self-assessment -- evaluating your own work honestly (autoavaliação)
"This lesson is a self-assessment."
Halfway point -- the middle of a journey (meio do caminho)
"We are at the halfway point of the program."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Today is a special class: the halfway point of the program. Andrea decides to take stock. Six months ago, she started the course and could barely speak in a meeting. Right now, she is improving her fluency every week and can keep up with fast speakers. Her confidence has grown, and small talk is now a real strength. She still has one area for improvement: pronunciation. But she can see clear progress and a real breakthrough. Next month, she is going to present Escape Turismo at a fair in Berlin, all in English.

1. 'Six months ago, she started the course' uses the past simple because:
A it describes a finished action in the past.
B it is happening right now.
C it is a future plan.
2. 'Right now, she is improving her fluency' is in the present continuous because:
A it is a finished past action.
B it describes something in progress now.
C it is a prediction.
3. Which sentence correctly talks about a future plan?
A Next month, she is going to present in Berlin.
B Next month, she presented in Berlin.
C Next month, she is present in Berlin.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past, Present, and Future Together

GRAMMAR

Como falar do passado, do presente e do futuro na mesma conversa (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Past simpleAção terminada no passado. A finished past action.Six months ago, I started.
Present continuousAção em progresso agora. An action in progress now.Right now, I am improving.
going to / willPlano ou previsão para o futuro. A plan or prediction.Next month, I am going to present.
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleA palavra de tempo escolhe o tempo verbal: 'ago' -> passado; 'right now' -> presente contínuo; 'next' -> futuro.ago -> started; now -> am improving; next -> am going to
