Chapter 4: The Code

Past, Present, Future

started · am improving · am going to present

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Six months ago, I started this program."

"Right now, I am improving my fluency."

"Next month, I am going to present in Berlin."

Past, present, or future -- which time does each sentence talk about?

FormUseExample
Past simpleA finished action in the pastSix months ago, I started.
Present continuousAn action in progress nowRight now, I am improving.
going to / willA plan or prediction for the futureNext month, I am going to present.

The time word chooses the tense: 'ago' -> past simple, 'right now' -> present continuous, 'next' -> future.

Language Focus

Past, Present, or Future?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I am starting the course."

✓ RIGHT

Yesterday I started the course.

✗ WRONG

"Right now I improve my accent."

✓ RIGHT

Right now I am improving my accent.

Use the past simple for finished past actions, and the present continuous (am/is/are + -ing) for what is happening right now.

Dialogue

The Midpoint Interview

D
Andrea, we are at the halfway point of your program, lesson eight of fifteen. How do you feel about your progress?
A
Honestly, I feel much more confident. Six months ago, I froze in every English meeting.
D
That is a real breakthrough. What are you working on right now?
A
Right now, I am improving my fluency and learning to keep up with fast speakers.
D
Excellent. And what are your plans for the second half?
A
Next month, I am going to present Escape Turismo at a fair in Berlin, all in English.
D
Wonderful. What is still your main area for improvement?
A
My pronunciation. But I am taking stock today, and I can see real progress.
