Lesson 12 -- Experiences So Far

Grammar Video
The Present Perfect to Talk About Experiences (Speak Confident English)

A calm, clear lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to use the present perfect to talk about your life experiences, and how it is different from the past simple. The teacher speaks slowly and gives natural examples with "ever" and "never". Connection to Lesson 12: this is the difference between an experience (I have traveled) and a finished moment in the past.

Tip: pause after each example and make one true sentence about yourself, one with "I have..." and one with "I have never...".

Conversation
English Conversation About Life Experiences -- Present Perfect and Past Simple (English Panda)

A friendly, natural conversation where people catch up and talk about the experiences they have had, mixing the present perfect and the past simple. It is spoken clearly and repeats the key structures, so it is a comfortable way to hear today's grammar in real speech. Connection to Lesson 12: listen for "Have you ever...?" and "I have never..." every time someone talks about an experience.

Tip: every time you hear a life experience, pause and say your own version out loud with "I have..." or "I have never...".

Unusual Experiences -- Present Perfect and "Have You Ever" (Teacher Belen)

A short, fun speaking-practice video full of "Have you ever...?" questions about unusual experiences. Connection to Lesson 12: it is a mini version of the Quick Fire, letting you rehearse your own "I have..." and "I have never..." answers.
