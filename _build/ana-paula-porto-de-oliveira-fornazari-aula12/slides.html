Chapter 4: The Present Perfect

Have and Have Never

The present perfect for life experiences

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have traveled abroad several times."

"She has volunteered at a clinic."

"Have you ever tried diving?"

"I have never seen snow."

FormUse it forExample
have / has + past participlea life experience (time not important)I have traveled abroad.
neverno experienceI have never seen snow.
ever (?)asking about experienceHave you ever tried diving?

Experience in your life, no exact date? Present perfect. Add "ever" in questions and "never" for none.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Form

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I ___have traveled abroad several times." (experience)
"She ___has volunteered at a clinic." (experience)
"Have you ever ___tried diving?" (question)
"I ___have never seen snow." (no experience)
"We ___have explored many cities." (experience)
Language Focus

Ever or Never?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I never been abroad."

✓ RIGHT

"I have never been abroad."

✗ WRONG

"Did you ever went to Asia?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you ever been to Asia?"

Always keep have or has with the past participle (I have been, not "I been"). For a life experience with ever or never, use the present perfect, not the past simple.

Dialogue

Have You Ever?

A
Tom, I was just making a list of all the things I have done in my life. It is longer than I thought!
T
That sounds fun! So, what is the most exciting thing you have ever done?
A
I think it was when I traveled abroad and explored the old city of Rome. It was the highlight of my year.
T
Amazing! Have you ever tried anything really thrilling, like diving?
A
Yes! I have gone diving once, and I felt so brave. But I have never been skydiving.
T
Me neither. What about helping others? Have you ever volunteered?
A
I have! Volunteering at a clinic was one of the most rewarding things I have ever done.
T
You have really accomplished a lot. Is there anything left on your bucket list?
A
Just one big thing: I have never seen snow. That is my dream of a lifetime.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. In the conversation, what was the highlight of her year?
Traveling abroad and exploring the old city of Rome.
2. What has she never done?
She has never been skydiving, and she has never seen snow.
3. What does Tom think about her experiences?
He thinks she has really accomplished a lot.
