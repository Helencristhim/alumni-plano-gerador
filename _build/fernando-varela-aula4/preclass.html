Aula 04 -- Pre-class

Small Talk & Networking

Starting and keeping an informal conversation at a congress (breaking the ice, keeping the talk going, asking questions back). Key phrases: how's it going, by the way, what about you, trip, enjoy, actually, weekend, catch up, break, nice to see you. Structure: open vs. closed questions + follow-ups (How was...? / What do you think of...? / Where are you from?) and conversation connectors (So, ... / By the way / Actually).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada frase e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

How's it going? -- como vao as coisas? / tudo bem? (a friendly hello)
"Hi Sarah! How's it going?"
Nice to see you -- bom te ver (a warm greeting for someone you know)
"Nice to see you again, David!"
By the way -- a proposito (a phrase to add a new topic)
"By the way, what do you think of the talk?"
What about you? -- e voce? (a question to ask back)
"I'm from Brazil. What about you?"
Trip -- viagem (a journey to a place)
"How was your trip to Chicago?"
Enjoy -- gostar, aproveitar (to like something and have a good time)
"I enjoy big conferences."
Actually -- na verdade (in fact, really)
"Actually, I really like it."
Weekend -- fim de semana (Saturday and Sunday)
"How was your weekend?"
Catch up -- colocar o papo em dia (to talk and share news)
"Let's catch up during the break."
Break -- intervalo, pausa (a short pause, a coffee break)
"See you at the coffee break."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

At the congress, Sarah sees Fernando at the coffee break. "Nice to see you! How's it going?" she says. "How was your trip?" Fernando asks. "It was long, but I enjoyed it," Sarah answers. "By the way, what do you think of the congress?" "Actually, I really like it," Fernando says. "Where are you from?" "I'm from Chicago. What about you?" They decide to catch up during the break.

1. Por que dizemos "Where are you from?" e nao "Where you are from?"
A Porque a acao aconteceu no passado.
B Na pergunta aberta, a palavra interrogativa vem primeiro, depois o verbo (are).
C Porque "from" precisa vir no fim.
2. "How was your trip?" e uma pergunta aberta porque:
A Pede uma resposta longa e mantem a conversa.
B So aceita "yes" ou "no".
C E o passado de "travel".
3. Which follow-up question keeps the conversation going?
A "Okay."
B "What about you?"
C "Goodbye."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Open Questions & Follow-ups

GRAMMAR

Como abrir e manter uma conversa com perguntas abertas e conectivos (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

TypeUse / UsoExample
Open questionHow / What / Where + verbo — pede resposta longa, mantem o papoHow was your trip?
Closed questionDo / Did / Are + ... — resposta yes/no, fecha o papoDid you enjoy it?
Follow-upDevolva a pergunta para o outroWhat about you? / And you?
ConnectorsLigam ideias na conversa: So, ... · By the way · Actually

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situacao de networking.

A partner says: "Nice to see you!" You answer:
A "Yes."
B "Nice to see you too! How's it going?"
C "I'm a manager."
You want the partner's opinion of the talk. You ask:
A "What do you think of the talk?"
B "What you think of the talk?"
C "You like the talk, yes?"
You want to ask where the partner is from. The correct question is:
A "Where you are from?"
B "Where are you from?"
C "From where you?"
The partner tells you about their city. To keep the conversation going, you say:
A "Okay. Bye."
B "Nice! What about you, do you enjoy big events?"
C "I don't know."
