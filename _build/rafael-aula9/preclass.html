Vocabulary Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Estude cada palavra de números/dados, ouça a pronúncia e leia o exemplo.

Increase•aumento
When a number goes up
"Revenue showed a strong increase."
Decrease•queda / redução
When a number goes down
"Agriculture had a small decrease."
Profit•lucro
Money the company keeps after costs
"Profit is higher than last year."
Growth•crescimento
When something gets bigger over time
"The iron division had the highest growth."
Quarter•trimestre
A period of three months
"Revenue grew this quarter."
Forecast•previsão
A prediction about future numbers
"We forecast steady growth."
Figure•número / cifra
A number, especially in a report
"All the figures are in the report."
Percentage•porcentagem
A part of one hundred (%)
"Revenue grew by twelve percent."

Grammar Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Leia o relatório. Os números e comparativos estão em negrito. Depois, responda as perguntas.

In the first quarter, Grupo Picunha had good results. Total revenue increased by twelve percent. The iron division had the highest growth, and it grew faster than agriculture. The agriculture division had a small decrease of three percent.

Profit is higher than last year. The figures are clear: this quarter is better than the last one. Rafael's forecast for the next quarter is steady growth.
1. By what percentage did revenue increase?
A Three percent
B Twelve percent
C Twenty percent
2. Which division grew more?
A The iron division (highest growth)
B The agriculture division
C Both the same
3. How does profit compare to last year?
A Lower than last year
B Higher than last year
C The same as last year

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Comparativos/superlativos e como ler números.

Comparatives, Superlatives & Numbers
USO FORMA / EXEMPLO
Comparar 2
(adjetivo curto)		adjetivo + -er than: higher than
Adjetivo longomore + adjetivo + than
O melhor/maiorthe + adjetivo + -est: the highest
Números7.5% = seven point five percent; 1,500,000 = one million five hundred thousand

✗ "more high" / "increased of 12%" (errado)

✓ "higher" / "increased by 12%" (correto)
