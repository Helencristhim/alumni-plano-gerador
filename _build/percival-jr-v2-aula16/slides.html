Chapter 3 -- Polite Replies

Yes, No & Short Answers

Answer politely, and repeat the auxiliary

Grammar Notice

Notice the Answer

What do the highlighted answers have in common?

"Have you sent the report?" -- "Yes, I have."

"Can you send it again?" -- "Certainly, I can."

"Did you read my query?" -- "Yes, I did."

"Can you meet on Monday?" -- "Unfortunately, I cannot."

Grammar Rule

Polite Replies

QuestionShort answerPolite full reply
Have you sent it?Yes, I have / No, not yetYes, I have. It is done.
Can you help?Yes, I can / I am afraid I cannotCertainly, I can help.
Did you read it?Yes, I did / No, I did notYes, I did. Thank you.
Are you free Monday?Yes, I am / No, I am notUnfortunately, I am not.

Key idea: the short answer repeats the auxiliary of the question -- "Have you...?" -> "Yes, I have", "Can you...?" -> "Yes, I can", "Did you...?" -> "Yes, I did". For a polite yes use Certainly or Yes, of course; for a polite no use Unfortunately, I cannot or I am afraid not.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Have you read it? -- Yes, I have read. Can you help? -- Yes, I do."
"Have you read it? -- Yes, I have. Can you help? -- Yes, I can."

The short answer repeats only the auxiliary (have, can, did, am), not the main verb. And it must match the question: Have you...? -> I have, not I do.
