Chapter 3

The Engine

Modals for requests, offers & clarification

Grammar Discovery

Casual to Formal

"Can you help me?" (casual)

"Could you possibly help me?" (polite)

"Would you be able to help me?" (formal)

"Would you mind helping me?" (very polite)

JobPhrases
RequestCan you...? / Could you (possibly)...? / Would you be able to...?
OfferWould you like...? / Can I help you with...? / Let me know if you need anything.
PermissionCan I...? / Could I...? / May I...? (formal)
ClarifyCould you repeat that? / I didn't catch that. / What do you mean by...? / Could you say that more slowly?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Would you mind to help me?"

✓ RIGHT

"Would you mind helping me?"

✗ TOO DIRECT

"Send me the file." (to a client)

✓ POLITE

"Could you send me the file, please?"

✗ PRETENDING

(nodding, but not understanding)

✓ CLEAR

"Sorry, I didn't catch that."

After "would you mind", use the -ing form. Match the politeness to the situation. Never pretend to understand — just ask for clarification.

Chapter 4

Real Voices

Rescheduling a meeting, politely

Dialogue

Can We Reschedule?

V
Hi Daniel, I have a small request. Could we possibly reschedule tomorrow's meeting?
D
Of course. Is everything okay? Can I help with anything?
V
Thank you, I'm fine. Something urgent came up. Would Thursday work for you?
D
Thursday afternoon is good. Sorry, what time did you say exactly?
V
Let me clarify: any time after two. I'll confirm by email too.
D
Perfect. Let me know if you need anything else.
V
I really appreciate your flexibility. Thank you so much.
D
No problem at all. You handled that very professionally.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Daniel react to the request?
He agrees, asks if everything is okay, and offers to help.
2. What does Daniel ask to clarify?
He asks what time exactly she said.
3. What does Daniel say at the end?
No problem, and that she handled it very professionally.

Never Pretend

0:000:00
