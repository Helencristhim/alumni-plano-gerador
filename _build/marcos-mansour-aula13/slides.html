Chapter 3

Making Orders

I would like / I'll have / Could I get

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I would like the tomato soup."

"I will have the grilled salmon."

"Could I get the vegetables instead of the potatoes?"

"For dessert, I'll have the fruit tart."

Phrase+ FormExample
I would like+ nounI would like the soup.
I'll have+ nounI'll have the salmon.
Could I get...?+ nounCould I get the vegetables?
ChangeUse instead of to swap: vegetables instead of potatoes.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I will having the salmon."

✓ RIGHT

"I will have the salmon."

✗ WRONG

"I would like have the soup."

✓ RIGHT

"I would like the soup." (or: I would like to have the soup)

✗ WRONG

"Bring me the salmon."

✓ RIGHT

"I'll have the salmon, please."

I would like / I'll have / Could I get + the dish. Use "instead of" to swap.

Chapter 4

Dinner in Action

A guest orders a full meal

Dialogue

I Will Have the Salmon

S
Good evening. Here is the menu. Can I get you a beverage to start?
M
Yes, please. Could I get a glass of sparkling water? And what do you recommend for the main course?
S
The grilled salmon is excellent tonight. Would you like an appetizer first?
M
To start, I would like the tomato soup. For the main course, I will have the salmon. Could I get the vegetables instead of the potatoes?
S
Of course. Do you have any allergies I should know about?
M
Yes, I have a mild allergy to nuts. Please make sure there are no nuts in my dish.
S
I will tell the kitchen. Would you like dessert?
M
For dessert, I'll have the fruit tart. And could we have the bill whenever you are ready? Thank you.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What did the guest order for the main course?
The grilled salmon, with vegetables instead of potatoes.
2. What allergy did the guest mention?
A mild allergy to nuts.
3. What did he order for dessert, and what did he ask for at the end?
The fruit tart, and he asked for the bill.
