YouTube · BBC Learning English
6 Minute Grammar: The Second Conditional

Six minutes, two presenters, and the exact structure you practiced tonight: if plus the past, would plus a verb, for imagined and unlikely situations. They say each example at natural speed, so your ear starts to hear when a native speaker reaches for "would" instead of "will".

Tip: watch it twice. The second time, pause after each example and build one interview sentence about your own field: "If I found a breach, I would...", "If I were leading the team, I would...".

YouTube · Interview Skills
15 Situational Interview Questions & Answers (STAR Method)

A career coach walks through the exact "what would you do if..." questions that scare most senior candidates, and models strong answers out loud. Listen for how each answer opens calmly, describes a clear approach, and shows judgment, step by step -- the same shape you built tonight.

Tip: pick three questions from the video. For each one, say your own answer in the second conditional first, then compare it to the coach's. Notice how often a strong answer starts with "If I..." and names a clear next step.

TED Talk · Decision-Making
Ruth Chang: How to Make Hard Choices

A philosopher explains why the hardest decisions are hard: not because you lack information, but because the trade-offs are real and neither option is simply better. This is the thinking behind every strong hypothetical answer -- how you weigh criteria and stand behind your judgment when there is no perfect option.

Tip: after watching, take one hard trade-off from your own work (speed vs. safety, cost vs. quality). Say out loud, in two second-conditional sentences, what you would do and why: "If I had to choose, I would... because...".
