Chapter 3 -- Opinions & Suggestions

Ask, Give, Suggest

The language of a meeting

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Is each sentence asking for an opinion, giving an opinion, or making a suggestion?

"What do you think about the safety project?"

"I think this topic is the most urgent."

"Let's start with the budget."

"How about meeting on Tuesday?"

Grammar Rule

Ask, Give & Suggest

Use it forPatternExample
Ask for an opinionWhat do you think (about ...)? / Do you agree?What do you think about the plan?
Give an opinionI think (that) ... / In my opinion, ...I think we should start now.
Make a suggestionLet's + verb / How about + verb-ing?Let's start with the budget.
Agree / DisagreeI agree (with you). / I disagree.I agree with you. / I see your point, but...

Key idea: use What do you think? to ask, I think / In my opinion to give your view, and Let's or How about + -ing to suggest. We say I agree (not "I am agree").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am agree with the budget."
"I agree with the budget."

Agree is already the verb. Say I agree / I disagree, never "I am agree".
