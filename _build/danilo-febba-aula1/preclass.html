Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Lead -- to be the main person guiding a team or project
"I lead a data engineering team in São Paulo."
Oversee -- to manage and be responsible for the work of an area
"I oversee three data squads."
In charge of -- responsible for something or someone
"I am in charge of the whole data platform."
Based in -- located in a particular place
"Our team is based in São Paulo."
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest in a project or decision
"I report to several stakeholders on the board."
Pipeline -- the system that moves, cleans and transforms data
"I design and maintain our data pipeline."
Deploy -- to put software or a system into real use
"We deploy new AI models every month."
Scale -- to grow a system so it handles much more work or data
"We are scaling the platform for global teams."
Deadline -- the date when something must be finished
"We have a tight deadline at the end of the quarter."
Get in touch -- to make contact or communicate with someone
"Let's get in touch after the summit."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Danilo works as a data engineering lead at a tech company based in São Paulo. He oversees a team of engineers and is in charge of the company's data pipeline. "I lead the data team, and I work with many stakeholders," he explains to a new colleague. Right now, his team is scaling the platform and is deploying a new AI model before an important deadline. Danilo loves networking at summits because it helps him meet new people. When he meets someone interesting, he always says: "Let's get in touch after the event." His colleague asks: "What do you do? What are you working on?" and Danilo answers with confidence.

1. Why do we say "Danilo works" with an -s at the end?
A Because the action happened in the past.
B Because in the present simple, he/she/it takes an -s.
C Because "Danilo" is plural.
2. "Right now, his team is scaling the platform" uses the present continuous to express:
A A project happening now, around the moment of speaking.
B A permanent fact that never changes.
C Something that happened only once last year.
3. Which questions does the colleague ask correctly?
A "What you do? What you working on?"
B "What do you do? What are you working on?"
C "What do you working? What are you do?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

When to use present simple for your role and present continuous for your current project.

FormUseExample
I / You / We + verbFacts, role and routines (no -s)I lead a data team.
He / She / It + verb+sThird person always takes -sShe oversees the project.
am / is / are + -ingHappening now / current projectWe are scaling the platform.
WH + do + you + verbAsk about a fact or routineWhat do you do?
WH + are + you + -ingAsk about now: What are you working on? / Where are you based?
