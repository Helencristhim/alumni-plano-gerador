Chapter 3

The Grammar

Present simple to talk about your job

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work at a tech company."

"I am a data engineer."

"She manages the data team."

"What do you do? Where do you work?"

StructureUseExample
I / You / We + verbFacts and routines (no -s)I work at a tech company.
He / She / It + verb+sThird person adds -sShe manages the team.
am / is / areThe verb to be (job, name, place)I am a data engineer.
WH + do / does + youAsk a questionWhat do you do? Where do you work?

Facts and routines = present simple. Add -s for he / she / it.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am work at a tech company."

✓ RIGHT

"I work at a tech company."

✗ WRONG

"She manage the data team."

✓ RIGHT

"She manages the data team."

✗ WRONG

"I have 41 years."

✓ RIGHT

"I am 41 years old."

No "am" with present simple verbs. Add -s for he/she/it. Use "to be" for age.

Nice to meet you too! So, what do you do here?
D

I am from São Paulo. And you?

R
Great! Let me show you the kitchen. We have good coffee here!
D
Perfect. Thank you, Rachel!
