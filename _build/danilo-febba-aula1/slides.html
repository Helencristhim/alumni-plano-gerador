Chapter 3

The Code

Present simple & present continuous to talk about your work

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I lead a data engineering team."

"I am in charge of the data platform."

"Right now, we are scaling our pipeline."

"What do you do? What are you working on?"

StructureUseExample
I / You / We + verbFacts, role and routines (no -s)I lead a data team.
He / She / It + verb+sThird person adds -sShe oversees the project.
am / is / are + -ingHappening now / current projectWe are scaling the platform.
WH + do / are + youAsk about someoneWhat do you do? What are you working on?

Permanent role = present simple. Right now = present continuous (-ing).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am leading a data team." (as my permanent role)

✓ RIGHT

"I lead a data team."

✗ WRONG

"Right now we scale the platform."

✓ RIGHT

"Right now we are scaling the platform."

✗ WRONG

"My colleague oversee the pipeline."

✓ RIGHT

"My colleague oversees the pipeline."

Permanent role = present simple. Right now = continuous (-ing). Add -s after he/she/it.

Meeting Rachel

Dialogue

At the Summit

R
Hi! I don't think we've met. I'm Rachel Carter. Nice to meet you.
D
Nice to meet you too, Rachel. I'm Danilo Febba. I lead a data engineering team.
R
Great to meet you! Which company are you with, and where are you based?
D
I work for a tech company based in São Paulo. I'm in charge of our whole data pipeline.
R
Impressive. I'm based in Seattle, and I work on the platform side. What are you working on right now?
D
Right now we're scaling our pipeline and deploying a new AI model. It's a big project with many stakeholders.
R
That sounds exciting. We work with teams like yours. Do you have a tight deadline?
D
End of the quarter, as always! Let's get in touch — here is my business card.
R
Perfect. Here is mine too. It was great meeting you, Danilo.
D
Same here, Rachel. Let's stay in touch after the summit.
