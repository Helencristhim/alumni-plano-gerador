Chapter 3

Going To

Plans, intentions, and predictions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We are going to launch an initiative."

"I am going to draft the proposal."

"She is going to lead the expansion."

"The rollout is going to be gradual."

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeam/is/are + going to + verbWe are going to launch it.
Negativeam/is/are + not + going toI am not going to rush it.
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + going toAre you going to draft it?
Predictiongoing to for evidenceIt is going to be busy.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We going to launch the initiative."

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to launch the initiative."

✗ WRONG

"I am going not to rush it."

✓ RIGHT

"I am not going to rush it."

✗ WRONG

"She is going to leads the team."

✓ RIGHT

"She is going to lead the team."

Always use am/is/are before "going to". The verb after "going to" stays in the base form. For questions, invert: "Are you going to...?"

Chapter 4

Project Kickoff

Presenting plans for the next quarter

Dialogue

The Quarter Ahead

M
Murielle, I want to walk you through our plans for next quarter. We are going to launch a new hiring initiative in March.
M
That sounds ambitious. What is the main objective?
M
Our objective is to reduce turnover, so we intend to improve the onboarding process.
M
Good. And what is the first step you are going to take?
M
First, I am going to draft the proposal this week. Then we are going to allocate more budget to the training team.
M
Are you going to rush the rollout, or is it going to be gradual?
M
I am not going to rush it. We anticipate a busy pipeline, so the rollout is going to be gradual and the expansion is going to follow the timeline.
M
Perfect. Send me the draft and I am going to review the timeline with you.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is Marcus going to launch in March?
A new hiring initiative.
2. What is the main objective of the plan?
To reduce turnover, by improving the onboarding process.
3. Is Marcus going to rush the rollout?
No, he is not going to rush it; the rollout is going to be gradual.
