Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Headache -- a continuous pain in the head
"I have a terrible headache today." (Dor de cabeça)
Sore throat -- pain in the throat, often when you swallow
"My sore throat makes it hard to talk." (Dor de garganta)
Fever -- a body temperature that is higher than normal
"She has a slight fever this morning." (Febre)
Cough -- to push air out of the throat with a sudden noise
"I started to cough last night." (Tossir / tosse)
Dizzy -- feeling that you might fall, as if things are turning
"I felt dizzy when I got up." (Tonto / tontura)
Ache -- a continuous, dull pain in part of the body
"My whole body aches." (Doer / dor)
Swollen -- larger than normal because of an injury or illness
"His ankle is red and swollen." (Inchado)
Painkiller -- a medicine you take to reduce or stop pain
"I took a painkiller, but it did not help." (Analgésico)
Prescription -- a doctor's written order that lets you buy medicine
"If she gives me a prescription, I will get the medicine." (Receita médica)
Rest -- to stop working or moving so your body can recover
"If you rest, you will feel better." (Descansar)
Sick leave -- time away from work that you take because you are ill
"If the doctor agrees, I will take sick leave." (Licença médica)
Get better -- to become healthy again after being ill
"If you take the medicine, you will get better." (Melhorar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Sofia calls Rubens because she feels sick. She has a fever, a sore throat and a bad headache, and she felt dizzy this morning. She took a painkiller, but her whole body still aches. Rubens gives her simple advice. "If you rest, you will feel better tomorrow," he says. "If you take the medicine on time, you will get better faster." He also warns her: "If the fever does not go down, you should see a doctor." For now, Sofia decides to rest and take sick leave. Good advice is clear and calm: tell the patient what to do, and what will happen if they do it.

1. What symptoms does Sofia have?
A A fever, a sore throat, a headache, and she feels dizzy.
B A broken arm and a swollen knee.
C Only a small cough.
2. What does Rubens say will happen if she rests and takes the medicine?
A She will feel worse.
B She will feel better and get better faster.
C Nothing will change.
3. When does Rubens say she should see a doctor?
A Right now, immediately.
B If the fever does not go down.
C Never, it is not necessary.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- the first conditional

GRAMMAR

O primeiro condicional fala de algo real e possível no futuro. Use if + presente simples para a condição, e will + verbo na base para o resultado. A ordem pode mudar; se o if vier primeiro, use vírgula. / The first conditional talks about a real, possible future. Use if + present simple for the condition, and will + base verb for the result.

Pattern / PadrãoExample / Exemplo
If + present, will + base verbIf you rest, you will feel better. / Se você descansar, vai se sentir melhor.
Will + base verb + if + presentYou will get better if you take the medicine. / Você vai melhorar se tomar o remédio.
Negative conditionIf the fever does not go down, see a doctor. / Se a febre não baixar, vá ao médico.
Advice with shouldIf you cough a lot, you should drink water. / Se você tossir muito, deve beber água.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de if, use o presente simples, NUNCA will (if you rest, não "if you will rest"). O resultado precisa de will + verbo na base (you will feel better). Quando o if vem primeiro, use vírgula entre as duas partes.
