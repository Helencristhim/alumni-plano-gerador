Chapter 3: The Passive Voice

When the Action Comes First

be + past participle, across every tense

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The defendant was remanded into custody."

"A motion has been filed by counsel."

"The hearing is being adjourned right now."

"The verdict will be delivered tomorrow."

TensePassive formExample
Past simplewas / were + p.p.The defendant was remanded.
Present perfecthas / have been + p.p.A motion has been filed.
Present continuousis / are being + p.p.The hearing is being adjourned.
Futurewill be + p.p.The verdict will be delivered.

The object becomes the subject. The doer is dropped, or added with "by". The verb is always be + past participle.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The judge was adjourned the case."

✓ RIGHT

"The case was adjourned by the judge."

✗ WRONG

"A warrant was issue last night."

✓ RIGHT

"A warrant was issued last night."

The person who acts is not the subject of a passive sentence — it moves after "by". And the main verb must be a past participle: issued, not issue.

Chapter 4: In the Courtroom

The Case of Andrade

Luiz and a colleague review the file

Dialogue

Where Does the File Stand?

S
Luiz, where does the Andrade file stand?
L
A warrant was issued on Monday, and our client was detained that evening.
S
Was bail requested at the first hearing?
L
It was, but it was denied, so he was remanded into custody.
S
Has the evidence been shared with us yet?
L
Not fully. A motion for complete disclosure has been filed.
S
Good. And the next session?
L
It was adjourned until Friday. The ruling will be delivered then.
Comprehension

About the Procedure

1. What happened to the client after the warrant?
He was detained that evening.
2. Why was he remanded into custody?
Because bail was requested but denied.
3. What has been filed about the evidence?
A motion for complete disclosure.
4. When will the ruling be delivered?
On Friday, when the session resumes.
