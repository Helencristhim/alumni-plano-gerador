Chapter 3: The Code

Modals of Deduction

Guessing with must, might, could and cannot

Grammar Discovery

How English Guesses

The square is full and there is music. It must be a festival.

The shops are all shut. It cannot be a normal Monday.

He looks exhausted. He must have walked for hours.

There are no roads here. They cannot have driven.

Guessing about now: modal + be. Guessing about the past: modal + have + past participle.

How sure?Now (modal + be)Past (modal + have + participle)
almost certainIt must be closed.It must have closed.
possibleIt might / could be closed.It might / could have closed.
impossibleIt cannot be closed.It cannot have closed.

After a modal, use the bare verb: must be, never "must to be". For the past, always have + past participle: "must have gone", never "must have went". For a negative guess about the past, English says cannot have (can't have), not "must not have".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"He must to be a local guide."
"He must be a local guide."
"She must have went home already."
"She must have gone home already."

After a modal, use the bare verb: must be, not "must to be". For the past, use have + past participle: "must have gone", never "must have went". And a negative past guess is cannot have, not "must not have".

Chapter 4: At the Harbor

A Mystery by the Water with Solveig

A local at the next table, and a crowd nobody can explain

Dialogue

By the Harbor

S
Look at all those people down by the pier. They are pointing at the water and nobody is moving. Something must be going on. It cannot be a normal Tuesday morning.
M
You are right, that is strange. The fishing boats have not gone out either. There might have been a storm warning. Or, taking a wild guess, someone could have fallen in.
S
I do not think so. Nobody looks frightened. Look, they are smiling and filming. It must be something wonderful, not an accident.
M
Come to think of it, that old man has been standing there since dawn. He must have known this was coming. Odds are he has seen it before.
S
Good eye. He cannot have just wandered down by chance. And the children would not be here on a school day unless it were something special.
M
Whales, then. My hunch is that a pod came into the fjord. Let us go and find out before I jump to any more conclusions.

Comprehension

About the Mystery

1. What first made Solveig sure it was not a normal Tuesday?
A crowd was pointing at the water and nobody was moving; the fishing boats had not gone out.
2. Why did she decide it was not an accident?
Nobody looked frightened; people were smiling and filming, so it must be something wonderful.
3. What did they deduce about the old man?
He could not have wandered down by chance; he must have known something was coming.
