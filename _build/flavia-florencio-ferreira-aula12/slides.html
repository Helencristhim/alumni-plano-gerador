Chapter 4: Connect the Ideas

and · but · because

How to link two ideas in one sentence

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

We prepare them, and we connect them to partners.

My pitch is short, but it is powerful.

I love this project because it helps people.

ConnectorUse it forExample
andadd an ideaWe prepare them, and we connect them.
butshow a differenceIt is short, but it is powerful.
becausegive a reason (Why?)I love it because it helps people.

After because, use a full idea: subject + verb ("because it is important").

Grammar Practice

Choose the Connector

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"We have a problem, ___but we have a solution." (contrast)
"I help small businesses, ___and I connect them to partners." (add)
"I love this project ___because it helps real people." (reason)
"My pitch is short, ___but it is clear." (contrast)
"The result is more sales ___and more jobs." (add)
Language Focus

and, but or because?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I love it, because important."

✓ RIGHT

"I love it because it is important."

✗ WRONG

"It is small and but important."

✓ RIGHT

"It is small but important."

After because, add a full idea (it is important). Use only one connector between two ideas -- never "and but" together.

Dialogue

Flavia and Lucas

L
Hi Flavia! We have one minute before the next talk. Can you present your project?
F
Of course! I am a consultant at Sebrae, and I help small businesses. Here is my pitch.
L
Great. What problem do you solve?
F
Many small businesses have a problem: they cannot reach global markets. But we have a solution.
L
Interesting! And what is the benefit?
F
The result is more sales and more jobs. I love this work because it helps real families.
L
That was a clear pitch, and it was only one minute!
F
Thank you, Lucas! A short message is powerful because people remember it.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. What does Lucas ask Flavia to do?
To present her project in one minute.
2. What does Lucas want to know after the problem?
He asks what the benefit is.
3. What does Lucas say about Flavia's pitch at the end?
He says it was a clear pitch and only one minute.
