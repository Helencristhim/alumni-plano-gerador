Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia has a one-minute pitch for her project. She starts with the problem: small businesses need help to grow. But she also has a solution. She helps them prepare, and she connects them to global partners. She loves this work because it brings a real result: more sales and more jobs. Her message is short and clear, because she only has one minute.

1. Flavia uses "but" in the text. What does "but" show?
A A reason for something.
B A difference or contrast between two ideas.
C A list of numbers.
2. "...because it brings a real result." What does "because" give?
A A reason.
B A contrast.
C A question.
3. Why is Flavia's message short and clear?
A Because she has all day.
B Because she only has one minute.
C Because she does not like her project.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Connectors (and / but / because)

GRAMMAR

How to link two ideas in one sentence.

ConnectorUse it forExample
andto add an ideaWe prepare them, and we connect them to partners.
butto show a differenceMy pitch is short, but it is powerful.
becauseto give a reason (answers "Why?")I love it because it helps people.

Remember: after "because" you need a full idea (subject + verb): "because it is important". Use only one connector between two ideas -- never "and but" together.

Quiz

A "We prepare them, and we connect them to partners."
B "We prepare them, because we connect them to partners."
C "We prepare them, but and we connect them."
