Chapter 3 — The Code

The Formal Register

Look at the purple phrases. How do we make a request sound polite and professional?

I am writing to confirm the timeline for the deal.
Could you please send the figures by Friday?
I would appreciate the report by end of day.
Please find attached the latest deck.
FunctionFormal formulaExample
State your purposeI am writing to + verb"I am writing to confirm the timeline."
Soft requestCould you please + verb"Could you please send the figures?"
Polite askI would appreciate + noun"I would appreciate the report by end of day."
Point to a filePlease find attached + noun"Please find attached the latest deck."
Grammar Rule

Direct vs Formal

Direct (too blunt)Formal register
"Send me the numbers.""Could you please send me the figures?"
"I want the report today.""I would appreciate the report by end of day."
"Here is the deck.""Please find attached the deck."
"Reply fast.""I look forward to your prompt reply."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"Give me the numbers by Friday."
"Could you please send the figures by Friday?"

To a client, a request needs a softener — "could you please" or "I would appreciate" — never a bare command.

Artifact

The Polished Email

To: j.carter@vertex-industries.com
Cc: James Whitfield (Director)
Subject: Vertex Restructuring — Timeline & Updated Deck

Dear Mr. Carter,

Thank you for your message yesterday; I appreciate the update on your side. I am writing to confirm the timeline for the next phase.

Could you please send the latest figures by Friday, if possible? I would appreciate the data ahead of our call. Please find attached the updated deck for your review.

I look forward to your prompt reply. Best regards, Eduarda Gabriel.

1. What does Eduarda do before making her request?
She acknowledges the client's message and thanks them.
2. How does she ask for the figures politely?
"Could you please send the latest figures by Friday, if possible?"
3. How does she point to the deck?
"Please find attached the updated deck."
