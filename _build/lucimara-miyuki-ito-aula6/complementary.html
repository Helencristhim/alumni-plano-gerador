Podcast — for the car (the drive to Guarulhos and back)
American English Podcast — Ep. 186.2: The Violinist in the Metro

The true story of the violinist who played, anonymous, in a subway station — and almost nobody stopped. It is natural American English, narrated, in the exact setting of tonight's lesson: the platform, the crowd, the rush. The goal here is not grammar: it is your ear. It is the gap you named yourself, and these are your eighty minutes a day in the car.

Tip (the exercise that pays the most): listen ONCE all the way through, without pausing, without understanding everything — and do not get annoyed about that. The second time, pause every 3 or 4 sentences and sum up OUT LOUD, in one sentence, what you have just heard. Bring 2 sentences you could NOT put together: they become the listening mini-lesson of Lesson 7.

YouTube — the practical step by step (you can listen without watching)
How to Ride the NYC Subway — With Tips & Tricks From a Local

The video that solves, in practice, the sixty-two minutes between JFK and the hotel: the turnstile, the moment you tap in, the difference between uptown and downtown, and the trap of the express train that runs straight past your station. It is tonight's vocabulary, all of it, shown on the screen and spoken at real speed.

Tip: watch it BEFORE you fly in September — and then a second time, the night before. While you watch, say out loud, for every rule that comes up: "I have to..." or "I don't have to..." or "I can't...". Speaking out loud on your own feels silly, and it is what turns the structure into something automatic.
