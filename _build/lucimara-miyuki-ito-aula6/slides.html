Chapter 3: The Code

Have To · Don't Have To · Can't

The grammar of a rule — and the one negative that is not a NO.

Grammar Discovery

Four Rules. One Is Not a No.

1. "You have to tap your card before you go through the turnstile."

2. "You don't have to buy a ticket — you can tap your credit card."

3. "You can't smoke on the platform."

4. "You are allowed to take a bike on the subway, but not at rush hour."

Two of these are negative. Break number 3 and you are in trouble. Break number 2 and... nothing happens. Why?

FormMeaningExample
have toObligation. Somebody else's rule, not your idea. This is what Americans SAY.You have to tap your card.
mustThe same obligation — but you mostly read it. Signs, rules, contracts. Rare out loud.Passengers must hold the handrail.
don't have toNO obligation. It is optional. You may do it — nobody is stopping you. This is not a prohibition.You don't have to buy a ticket. (But you can.)
can't / must notProhibition. Forbidden. Do it and there is a consequence.You can't smoke on the platform.
can / be allowed toPermission. Be allowed to is the formal one — the rule says yes.You are allowed to take a bike.
The questionDo I have to...? / Am I allowed to...? — never Do I must...?. Have to is a normal verb: it needs do.

Two negatives, two opposite worlds: don't have to = you are free. can't = you are not.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"You mustn't buy a ticket — you can tap your credit card."
"You don't have to buy a ticket — you can tap your credit card."
"Do I must transfer at Times Square?"
"Do I have to transfer at Times Square?"

In Portuguese, não precisa and não pode both start with the same word. In English they are opposites: don't have to sets you free, mustn't stops you. And have to is a normal verb — it needs do to ask a question.

Chapter 5: Practice

The announcement · The podcast · And the question that saves you

Listening 1

The Voice in the Train

Nobody repeats an announcement. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. Where do passengers going to the AirTrain have to get off?
At Jamaica Station — it is the next stop, and they have to leave the train there.
2. Do you have to tap your card again for the transfer?
No — you don't have to. The fare covers the whole trip.
3. Why can't the train stop at Sutphin Boulevard this weekend?
Because of construction, it is running express from Jamaica to Queens Plaza. To reach those stations you have to take the local train on the other platform.
4. What are passengers not allowed to do?
They are not allowed to hold the doors — it delays the train for everyone.
Listening 2

Three Things Nobody Tells You

She learned all three the hard way. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does every tourist check — and what do they forget to check?
They check the letter or the number of the line. They forget to check whether the platform says uptown or downtown.
2. She once rode forty blocks the wrong way. What does she have to do to fix that?
She has to go up, cross the street, and come back down — the train does not stop and turn around for you.
3. Why does she say people stand in line at the machine for nothing?
Because you don't have to buy anything — no card, no ticket, no app. You can just tap your credit card at the turnstile.
4. When is the express train a bad idea?
When your station is a local station — the express takes you straight past it, and then you have to come back.
Strategy

Ask a Question with a One-Word Answer

You can't control how fast they speak. You can control the answer you ask for.

Do I have to transfer, or does this train go all the way?
Which platform do I have to be on — uptown or downtown?
So I don't have to buy a ticket — I can just tap. Is that right?
Let me check I understood: I take the E downtown, and I transfer at 42nd. Right?

Comprehension

What Did Marcus Say?

1. Why can't she go through the turnstile, and what does he tell her to do?
The turnstile is too narrow for her suitcase — she has to use the wide gate on the left, and she has to tap her card there too.
2. What does Marcus say about buying a MetroCard?
She doesn't have to buy one. If she has a credit card, she can just tap that — same card, same fare.
3. Which train and which platform does he give her — and does she have to transfer?
The E train, on the downtown platform. It is the express, so it skips most stations, but it stops at 42nd — so she doesn't have to transfer at all.
4. What is the one thing he says she really can't do?
She can't hold the doors when she gets on the train.
