Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Commodity -- a raw material or product bought and sold in large quantities
"Coffee is one of the most traded commodities in the world." (Commodity / matéria-prima)
Sourcing -- the process of finding and obtaining materials or products
"Our sourcing team works directly with farmers in Brazil." (Aquisição / sourcing)
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest or concern in a business or project
"We briefed all stakeholders before the quarterly review." (Parte interessada)
Report to -- to work under someone's supervision, to have a manager above you
"I report directly to the director of operations." (Responder a / reportar para)
Oversee -- to watch over and manage work, a project, or people
"She oversees the entire export operation from Santos." (Supervisionar)
Headquarters -- the main offices of an organization or company
"Sucafina's headquarters are in Geneva, Switzerland." (Sede / matriz)
Negotiate -- to discuss something in order to reach an agreement
"We need to negotiate better terms with the shipping company." (Negociar)
Expertise -- special skill or knowledge in a particular area
"His expertise in green coffee grading is well known in the industry." (Especialização / expertise)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson works at Sucafina, a Swiss multinational that trades coffee commodities around the world. Every day, he coordinates with stakeholders from different countries and reports directly to the director. He oversees key operations at the Santos office and helps negotiate contracts with international sourcing partners. "I have worked with coffee trading for several years," he explains. "The industry has grown a lot, and my expertise in the Brazilian market has helped us connect with new origins. Our headquarters are in Geneva, but Santos is where the action happens."

1. Which tense does Walyson use to describe his daily routine at Sucafina?
A Present simple: "works", "coordinates", "reports"
B Present perfect: "has worked", "has grown"
C Past simple: "worked", "coordinated"
2. Why does Walyson say "I have worked with coffee trading for several years" using the present perfect?
A Because it happened at a specific time last year.
B Because the experience started in the past and continues to the present.
C Because it is happening right now.
3. In the sentence "Santos is where the action happens," which tense is used and why?
A Present perfect, because it connects past to present.
B Present simple, because it describes a permanent fact.
C Past simple, because Santos was important before.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo verbal. / When to use each tense.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present SimpleRotinas, fatos permanentes / Routines, permanent factsWalyson works at Sucafina.
Present Simple (3rd person)He/she/it + verbo com -s / adds -sHe reports to the director.
Present PerfectExperiência acumulada, conexão passado-presente / Accumulated experienceI have worked with coffee for years.
Present Perfect (structure)have/has + past participleThe industry has grown a lot.
Diferença-chave / Key differencePresent simple = o que você FAZ sempre ("I work"). Present perfect = o que você já FEZ até agora ("I have worked for years").
