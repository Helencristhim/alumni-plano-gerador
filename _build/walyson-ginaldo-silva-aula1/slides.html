Chapter 3

The Code

The grammar behind first impressions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work at Sucafina. I report to the director."

"He oversees operations. She negotiates contracts."

"I have worked in coffee for several years."

"The industry has grown a lot recently."

StructureUseExample
Present SimpleRoutines, permanent factsI work at Sucafina.
3rd person -sHe / she / itHe oversees operations.
Present PerfectExperience until nowI have worked here for years.
Key signal wordsPresent simple: every day, always, usually. Present perfect: for, since, already, recently.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have 29 years."

✓ RIGHT

"I am 29 years old."

✗ WRONG

"He work at Sucafina."

✓ RIGHT

"He works at Sucafina."

✗ WRONG

"I work here since 2019."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked here since 2019."

In Portuguese, "Eu tenho 29 anos" uses "ter" (have). In English, we say "I am 29" with the verb "to be."

Chapter 4

The Coffee Trail

A conversation at the International Coffee Summit

Dialogue

Meeting at the Summit

S
Hi! I do not think we have met. I am Sarah Martinez, from the Geneva headquarters.
W
Nice to meet you, Sarah! I am Walyson Ginaldo Silva. I work at Sucafina here in Santos.
S
Santos! That is where all the magic happens. What do you do at the Brazil office?
W
I report directly to the director. I help oversee operations and work with stakeholders from different origins.
S
Interesting! I work on the trading desk. We negotiate contracts with buyers in Europe. How long have you been in the coffee industry?
W
I have worked with coffee commodities for several years now. My expertise is in the Brazilian market, especially sourcing from different regions.
S
That is great! We should definitely connect more. Here is my business card.
W
Thank you, Sarah! And here is mine. It is a pleasure to meet you.
