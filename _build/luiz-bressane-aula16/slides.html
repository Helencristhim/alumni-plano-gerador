Chapter 3: The Causative

Have It Done

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

We had the detention reviewed within hours.

We got the order quashed the same day.

The judge had the custodian produce the prisoner.

I will get my client released tonight.

StructurePatternMeaning
have something donehave + object + past participlearrange for it to be done
get something doneget + object + past participlesame, more informal / with effort
have someone dohave + person + base verbmake / instruct a person to act
get someone to doget + person + to + verbpersuade a person to act

The causative says someone ELSE does the action for you. A defender does not free the prisoner himself — he has the detention reviewed and gets his client released. Use the past participle for a thing ("the order quashed"), and the base verb for a person you direct ("had the custodian produce the prisoner").

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We had the detention review."

✓ RIGHT

"We had the detention reviewed."

✗ WRONG

"The judge had the custodian to produce him."

✓ RIGHT

"The judge had the custodian produce him."

For a thing, use the past participle: "have it reviewed / quashed / released." For a person you direct, use the base verb with no "to": "have the custodian produce him." Only "get someone TO do" keeps the "to".

Chapter 4: Before the Bench

Demanding the Body

Dialogue

The Habeas Corpus Hearing

J
Counsel, you have filed a petition for habeas corpus. Tell the court why this detention is unlawful.
L
My Lord, my client has been held for six days. No warrant, no charge, no hearing. The detention is plainly arbitrary.
L
He has been held incommunicado, with no access to counsel. I ask the court to issue the writ and have the custodian produce him before this bench today.
J
The State says it is investigating. Is that not a lawful basis to hold the detainee?
L
No, My Lord. Investigation is not detention. Liberty cannot be removed without a lawful order. If they have grounds, let them charge him; if not, we must get him released tonight.
L
And any statement taken in those six days was the fruit of coercion. I ask the court to have it excluded and to recognise the writ as the citizen's first safeguard.
J
The writ is granted. The State must justify the detention by noon or have the detainee released.
Comprehension

The Hearing

1. For how long, and on what basis, had the client been held?
Six days — with no warrant, no charge and no hearing.
2. What did counsel ask the court to have the custodian do?
Issue the writ and have the custodian produce the client before the bench that day.
3. Why did counsel say investigation was not a lawful basis?
Investigation is not detention; liberty cannot be removed without a lawful order.
4. What did the court finally order?
It granted the writ: the State must justify the detention by noon or have the detainee released.

Chapter 5: Drill

Sharpen the Petition

Detective · Transformation · Building

Spot the Error

Fix the Causative

"We had the detention review by a judge."
We had the detention reviewed by a judge. (past participle for a thing)
"The judge had the custodian to produce him."
The judge had the custodian produce him. (base verb, no "to")
"We finally got our client release."
We finally got our client released. (past participle for a thing)
"I will get the warden disclose the record."
I will get the warden to disclose the record. (get + person + to + verb)
Transformation

Make It Causative

"A judge reviewed the detention for us." → have something done
We had the detention reviewed (by a judge).
"The authorities released our client." → get something done
We got our client released.
"The court ordered the custodian to bring the prisoner." → have someone do
The court had the custodian bring the prisoner.
"We persuaded the warden to disclose the record." → get someone to do
We got the warden to disclose the record.
