Aula 19 -- Pre-class

Handling Objections -- Acknowledging Concerns & Concession Connectors

Learn how to handle an objection: acknowledge the concern, then answer with a concession connector. New vocabulary: objection, acknowledge, valid, reassure, reluctant, hesitation, overcome, willing. Grammar: concession connectors -- however, nevertheless, on the other hand (The price is high. However, our quality is much better).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Objection -- a reason or concern the other side raises against your offer
"The client raised one objection about the price." (Objeção)
Acknowledge -- to show you heard and understand the other side's concern
"I always acknowledge the concern before I answer it." (Reconhecer)
Valid -- fair and reasonable; based on good logic
"That is a valid point, and I understand your worry." (Válido / procedente)
Reassure -- to make someone feel calm and confident again
"Let me reassure you: our delivery record is excellent." (Tranquilizar / dar segurança)
Reluctant -- not yet wanting to do or agree to something
"The client was reluctant to sign a long contract." (Relutante / hesitante)
Hesitation -- a short pause that shows you are not sure or feel doubt
"After a short hesitation, the client agreed to talk." (Hesitação / receio)
Overcome -- to deal with a problem successfully and get past it
"We overcame the objection and signed the deal." (Superar)
Willing -- ready and happy to do something or to help
"We are willing to find a solution that works for you." (Disposto / disposta)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On the client call, the client raises an objection: the price is too high. Simone does not argue. First, she acknowledges the concern and says it is a valid point. Then she adds her answer with a connector: "The price is high. However, our quality is much better." The client is also reluctant to sign a long contract. Simone understands the hesitation and says: "It is a long contract; nevertheless, it protects you from price changes." Finally, the client worries about delivery. Simone answers: "You worry about delivery. On the other hand, our record is excellent," and she offers a guarantee to reassure the client. She is willing to find a solution, so together they overcome the objection.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Concession Connectors

GRAMMAR

Os concession connectors (however, nevertheless, on the other hand) ligam duas ideias que contrastam: primeiro você admite a objeção, depois dá a sua resposta. São mais formais que "but". Pontuação: quando começam a segunda frase, vem depois de ponto final ou ponto-e-vírgula, e levam vírgula depois: "The price is high. However, our quality is better." Já "but" liga dentro da mesma frase, com vírgula antes: "The price is high, but the quality is better." / The concession connectors (however, nevertheless, on the other hand) join two contrasting ideas: first you admit the objection, then you give your answer. They are more formal than "but". Punctuation: when they start the second sentence, they come after a full stop or semicolon, and they take a comma after: "The price is high. However, our quality is better." "But" joins inside one sentence, with a comma before it.

ConnectorPosition / PosiçãoExample / Exemplo
However,Starts the answer; comma afterThe price is high. However, our quality is better.
Nevertheless,More formal; comma afterIt is a long contract; nevertheless, it protects you.
On the other hand,Shows the other side; comma afterYou worry about delivery. On the other hand, our record is excellent.
butJoins inside one sentence; comma beforeThe price is high, but the quality is better.
