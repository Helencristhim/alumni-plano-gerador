Chapter 3 -- Concession Connectors

The price is high, however...

Joining two contrasting ideas, formally

Listen and Notice

What Do They Do?

Look at the highlighted words. What job do they have in the sentence?

"The price is high. However, our quality is much better."

"The contract is long; nevertheless, it protects you from price changes."

"You worry about delivery. On the other hand, our record is excellent."

"That is a valid point; however, we are willing to find a solution."

The Concession Connector Code

However, Nevertheless

ConnectorPositionExample
However,Starts the answer; comma afterThe price is high. However, our quality is better.
Nevertheless,More formal; comma afterIt is a long contract; nevertheless, it protects you.
On the other hand,Shows the other side; comma afterYou worry about delivery. On the other hand, our record is excellent.
butJoins inside one sentence; comma beforeThe price is high, but the quality is better.

Key idea: concession connectors (however, nevertheless, on the other hand) join two contrasting ideas and sound formal. They take a comma after them. They are more professional than "but", which joins inside one sentence.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The price is high, however our quality is better."
"The price is high. However, our quality is better."

Use a full stop or semicolon before however, and a comma after it.

From Objection to Solution

1. What does Simone do when the client raises an objection?

First, the client raises an objection and Simone listens carefully without interrupting. Next, she acknowledges the concern and says it is a valid point.

2. How does Simone add her side of the story?

She answers with a concession connector, such as however or nevertheless, to add her side of the story.

3. What is the final step?

Finally, the client is no longer reluctant, the hesitation is gone, and together they overcome the objection.
