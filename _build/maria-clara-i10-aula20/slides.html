Grammar Rule

The Four Moves of an Introduction

MoveWhat you doExample
1. OpenGreet the room and thank themGood afternoon. Thank you for having me here today.
2. RoleSay who you are and where you workI am a professor and institutional leader at a federal university.
3. ExperienceShare how long and your expertiseI have worked in higher education for over twenty years.
4. CloseLook forward and open a partnershipI am looking forward to exchanging ideas with you.

The easy way to remember: always in this order — Open, then your Role, then your Experience, then a warm Close. Sixty to ninety seconds. You do not need perfect English — you need a clear, calm introduction that is yours.

Grammar Practice

Build Your Introduction

Say each part of your introduction out loud. Then click to check.

1. Open the introduction and thank the room.
Good afternoon. Thank you for having me here today.
2. Say it is an honor to be there.
It is a real honor to be with you.
3. Say your name, your role, and your institution.
My name is Maria Clara. I am a professor and institutional leader at a federal university in Brazil.
4. Say how long you have worked in your field.
I have worked in higher education for more than twenty years.
5. Say what your expertise is.
My expertise is in institutional leadership and international cooperation.
6. Say one thing you are proud of.
I have led teams and projects in several countries.
7. Close by looking forward to the conference.
I am looking forward to exchanging ideas, and I hope this visit will start a productive partnership.
8. Finish warmly and invite questions.
Thank you very much. I am happy to answer any questions.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am working here since twenty years."
"I have worked here for twenty years."
"My expertise are in leadership."
"My expertise is in leadership."
"I look forward to exchange ideas."
"I am looking forward to exchanging ideas."

Use I have worked... for + time for your experience, My expertise is (singular), and looking forward to + -ing for your close.

Anchor Phrases

Two Phrases That Carry the Whole Pitch

One opens with warmth. One closes looking forward.

"It is an honor to be here."

Opens with warmth and respect. It shows you value the room.

"I am looking forward to exchanging ideas."

Closes looking forward. It opens the door to a partnership.
