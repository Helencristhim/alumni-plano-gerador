Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Polite requests: Could you / Would you mind

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes after the orange words?

"Could you help me with this report?"

"Could you send me the file, please?"

"Would you mind helping me with this report?"

"Would you mind sending me the file?"

Grammar Rule

Polite Requests

PatternAfter itExample
Could youbase verb (help, send)Could you help me, please?
Would you mindverb + -ing (helping)Would you mind helping me?
Say yesagree to helpSure. / Of course. / No problem.
Say yes (to mind)agree to helpNot at all. / Of course not.

Key idea: use Could you + base verb or Would you mind + verb-ing. Add please to sound even more polite. With Would you mind, the answer Not at all means yes, I will help.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Would you mind to send me the file?"
"Would you mind sending me the file?"

After Would you mind, use the verb with -ing. After Could you, use the base verb.

Hi Aline -- quick favor!

My workload is heavy today and I am very busy. Could you help with two things?

1. Could you cover the 10 a.m. client call for me?

2. Would you mind double-checking the budget file before I send it?

If you have a spare minute, could you also hand the printed report to Maria?

Thank you so much! I will grab you a coffee and help with your tasks later this week.
