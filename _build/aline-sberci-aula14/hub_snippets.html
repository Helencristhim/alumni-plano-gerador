Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Favor -- a kind thing you ask someone to do for you
"Can I ask you a quick favor?" (Favor)
Lend -- to give something to someone for a short time
"Could you lend me your charger?" (Emprestar)
Borrow -- to take something for a short time and give it back
"Can I borrow your laptop for a minute?" (Pegar emprestado)
Hand -- to pass something directly to a person
"Could you hand me that file, please?" (Passar / entregar)
Cover -- to do a task in someone's place while they are away
"Could you cover the call for me?" (Cobrir / substituir)
Spare -- free or extra (time, a moment) that you can give
"Do you have a spare minute?" (De sobra / livre)
Workload -- the amount of work a person has to do
"My workload is very heavy this week." (Carga de trabalho)
Busy -- having a lot of work or things to do
"I am very busy this morning." (Ocupado)
Grab -- to get something quickly; informal way to say get
"Could you grab me a coffee?" (Pegar rápido)
Double-check -- to check something a second time to be sure it is correct
"Would you mind double-checking the numbers?" (Conferir de novo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is a Monday morning, and Aline is very busy. Her workload is heavy today. Her battery is low, so she asks Tom a favor: "Could you lend me your charger?" Tom says, "Sure, no problem." Then Aline asks for more help: "Would you mind covering the afternoon meeting for me?" Tom answers, "Not at all. I have a spare hour." Later, Aline asks Maria: "Would you mind double-checking the report?" To thank them, Aline says she will grab them a coffee. Notice the pattern: Could you is followed by the base verb (lend, cover), and Would you mind is followed by the verb with -ing (covering, double-checking).

1. What favor does Aline ask Tom first?
A To lend her his charger.
B To grab her a coffee.
C To double-check the report.
2. What comes after "Would you mind"?
A The base verb (cover).
B The verb with -ing (covering).
C The verb with to (to cover).
3. How does Tom agree to cover the meeting?
A "Not at all. I have a spare hour."
B "No, I cannot help."
C "I am too busy today."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Could you / Would you mind

GRAMMAR

Use estes dois padrões para pedir ajuda de forma educada. / Use these two patterns to ask for help politely.

Pattern / PadrãoAfter it / Depois vemExample / Exemplo
Could youverbo base (help, send, lend)Could you help me, please?
Would you mindverbo + -ing (helping, sending)Would you mind helping me?
Say yes / Aceitarconcordar em ajudarSure. / Of course. / No problem.
Yes to "mind" / Aceitarconcordar em ajudarNot at all. / Of course not.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de Could you use o verbo base (Could you lend me a pen?). Depois de Would you mind use o verbo com -ing (Would you mind lending me a pen?). Com Would you mind, a resposta Not at all significa "sim, eu ajudo". Adicione please para soar ainda mais educado.
