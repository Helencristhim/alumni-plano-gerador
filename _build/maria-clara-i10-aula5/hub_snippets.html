Lesson 05 -- Pre-class

Now and Always -- Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Choose the right tense: your routine (present simple: I work, I travel) versus what is happening now (present continuous: I am working, I am traveling). Key words: routine, temporary, permanent, currently, colleague, appointment, to confirm, to update. Signal words: always, usually, every day, now, at the moment, this week, currently. And two phrases for an official dinner: "It depends on..." and "I would rather...".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Take your time — listen twice, then say it out loud. These are the words of your routine and of this moment.

Routine -- the things you do regularly, again and again
"My morning routine is always the same."
Temporary -- for a short time only, not forever
"This trip is temporary, only one week."
Permanent -- for a long time; the opposite of temporary
"My home in Maceio is permanent."
Currently -- at this moment, right now
"I am currently working on a big project."
Colleague -- a person who works with you
"My colleague is arriving from China today."
Appointment -- a planned meeting at a fixed time
"I have a dinner appointment at seven."
To confirm -- to say that something is certainly true or agreed
"I confirm my seat before every meeting."
To update -- to give the newest information about something
"I update my team every morning."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Habit or Now?

GRAMMAR

English has two presents. Use the present simple for a habit, a routine, or a permanent fact (I travel). Use the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) for something happening now or a temporary situation (I am traveling). One question decides: habit or now?

MeaningTenseFormSignal words
Habit / routine / factPresent simpleI travel / she travelsalways, usually, every day
Now / temporaryPresent continuousI am traveling / she is travelingnow, at the moment, this week, currently

Watch out: in the present simple, add -s for she/he/it: "she travels". In the present continuous, don't forget am / is / are: "I am preparing", never "I preparing". Two useful phrases for a dinner: It depends on... (a careful answer) and I would rather... (a polite preference).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real moment at the conference and the dinner.

A colleague asks "How is your normal week?" You answer:
A "I usually travel with a delegation and meet my team every month."
B "I am usually traveling with a delegation."
C "I traveling with a delegation."
The provost asks "What are you doing this week?" You answer:
A "This week, I attend a conference in Seoul."
B "This week, I am attending a conference in Seoul."
C "This week, I am attend a conference in Seoul."
The waiter offers you fish or meat. The most polite answer is:
A "It depends on the day, but I would rather have the fish."
B "I want fish. Give me fish."
C "I am rather the fish."
Which sentence is correct for something happening right now?
A "Right now, I prepare my slides."
B "Right now, I am preparing my slides."
C "Right now, I preparing my slides."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your rehearsal for the Seoul dinner.

Describe your two worlds in 4-5 sentences. First, your routine with the present simple (where you live, where you work, how you usually travel). Then, this week with the present continuous (what you are doing now, what you are preparing). Use: I live..., I work..., I usually..., This week I am..., At the moment I am... Take your time and don't read from a script.
