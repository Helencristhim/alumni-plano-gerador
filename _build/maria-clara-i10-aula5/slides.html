Chapter 3: The Code

Habit or Now?

I travel · I am traveling

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I usually travel with a delegation, but this week I am traveling alone."

"She studies at a bilingual school, and this semester she is studying French."

"We meet every month, but today we are meeting the rector."

"I live in Maceio, but right now I am staying in a hotel in Seoul."

MeaningTenseFormSignal words
Habit, routine, fact (always)Present simpleI travel / she travelsalways, usually, every day
Now, this moment, temporaryPresent continuousI am traveling / she is travelingnow, at the moment, this week, currently

One question decides everything: habit or now? For a habit, use the present simple. For something happening now, use be + verb-ing. Don't forget the -s for she/he in the present simple: "she travels".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Right now, I talk with the provost."
"Right now, I am talking with the provost."
"Every day I am traveling to work."
"Every day I travel to work."

For something happening now, use am/is/are + verb-ing. For a habit (every day, usually), use the simple form. Ask yourself: habit or now?

Chapter 4: The Official Dinner

A Seat Next to the Provost

"So, tell me — what do you do?"

Dialogue

Small Talk at the Dinner

P
Good evening. I am Professor Han, the provost of the university here in Seoul. Welcome. So, tell me — what do you do in Brazil?
MC
Good evening, Professor Han. I work in public administration, and I live in Maceio. I usually travel with a delegation.
P
A delegation! But tonight you are here alone, I think. Are you traveling by yourself this time?
MC
Yes. This week, I am traveling alone. It is temporary — just for this conference.
P
Very brave. And what are you working on at the moment?
MC
Currently, I am preparing a presentation about leadership. My colleague is helping me with the slides.
P
Wonderful. The waiter is here. Would you like the fish or the meat tonight?
MC
It depends on the day, but tonight I would rather have the fish, thank you.
P
Excellent choice. You speak English very well. Do you practice every day?
MC
I study every day, and I am improving. Right now, my granddaughter is learning English with me.

Key Expressions

Two Phrases for the Table

One for a diplomatic answer, one for a polite preference.

It depends on the day.

"It depends on..." = a careful answer when it is not a simple yes or no.

I would rather have the fish.

"I would rather..." = a polite way to say what you prefer.
