Lesson 7 -- Used To and Would for Narrative Authority

YouTube · BBC Learning English
Used To & Would -- 6 Minute Grammar

Six minutes, two BBC presenters, and exactly the two forms you practiced tonight: "used to" for past states and habits, and "would" for repeated past actions. They explain, at natural speed, why you can say "I used to be a developer" but not "I would be" -- the state-versus-action rule that is the heart of the lesson.

Tip: watch it twice. The second time, pause after each example and build one sentence about your own career: "I used to...", "Every day I would...".

BOX SET: "Used to" and "would" -- 5 lessons in 28 minutes

Five short BBC lessons back to back, all about telling the past with "used to" and "would." It covers the affirmative, the negative ("didn't use to"), and the question form ("Did you use to...?") -- the same shapes you drilled tonight -- with plenty of natural examples so your ear starts to expect them.

Tip: keep it as background listening on a busy week. Whenever you hear a "used to" or a "would," say a version about your own 41 years out loud.

TED Talk · Technology
Kevin Kelly: How Technology Evolves

The co-founder of Wired traces how technology has evolved across whole eras, and why the landscape keeps transforming faster. It is the big-picture backdrop to your own story: the shifts you have lived through are part of a much longer wave. Perfect vocabulary practice for evolve, era, landscape, shift, and transform.

Tip: after watching, record two minutes about one era of your career. Contrast what the landscape used to be with how it has transformed, using at least three of tonight's words.
