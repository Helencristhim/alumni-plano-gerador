The Big Idea

Two Words Paint the Past

used to = the whole picture

states & habits that are gone: "I used to be a junior developer."

would = the daily habit

repeated past actions only: "Every night I would stay late."

The magic is the contrast. When you say what you used to do, the listener hears how far you have come — and that distance is your authority.

Chapter 3: The Contrast Code

I Used To... I Would...

The grammar of a story of change

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I used to be a junior developer at IBM."

"Every night I would stay late to check the servers."

"The landscape used to be much slower than it is today."

"We would guard our computer time like gold."

The blue form describes how things were (a state). The orange form describes what you did again and again (a habit). Neither is true today. Why?

FormUseExample
used to + base verbPast states AND habits that are no longer true.I used to be a developer.
I used to manage data centers.
would + base verbRepeated past actions only — never states. (No "I would be...")Every night I would stay late.
We would guard our time.
MeaningBoth describe the past through contrast with the present. Saying what you used to do shows how far you have come. (Simple past just reports an event; used to signals "not anymore.")

Negative: didn't use to (drop the "d"). Question: Did you use to...? (drop the "d").

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I would be a junior developer in the 1980s."
"I used to be a junior developer in the 1980s."
"I use to manage data centers by hand."
"I used to manage data centers by hand."
"Did you used to work with mainframes?"
"Did you use to work with mainframes?"

Use used to for past states (never "would be"). Keep the d in the affirmative ("used to"), but drop it after did: "Did you use to...?" and "I didn't use to...".

Detail

State or Action?

state → only "used to"  |  repeated action → "would" works too

"I ___used to be the person with all the answers." (state)
"Every morning I ___would arrive at six and start the backups." (repeated action)
"I ___used to believe on-premise was safer than the cloud." (state)
"When a server failed, someone ___would drive in at midnight." (repeated action)
Chapter 4: The Interview

Meet Mark

A senior recruiter for a VP of AI Safety role

Dialogue

Walk Me Through Your Story

M
Lucia, your resume spans four decades, from IBM in the eighties all the way to Google today. Take me back to the beginning. What did a normal day use to look like for you at IBM?
L
Back when I was at IBM, I used to manage entire data center migrations by hand. Every night I would stay late, checking each server one by one. That whole landscape is obsolete now.
M
That is a completely different world. So what changed? What was the biggest shift you lived through?
L
The move to the cloud transformed everything. I used to believe that on-premise was safer, but the industry evolved, and I evolved with it. Today I lead in a completely new era.
M
I love that. When you reflect on those forty-one years, what does that long journey give you that a younger candidate does not have?
L
Perspective. I have watched three technologies become obsolete. So when a new one arrives, I do not panic. I used to fear change; now I look for it.
M
That is exactly what we need on the AI safety team. Last one: how would you say a senior leader's job today is different from what it use to be?
L
We used to give orders and expect people to follow. Now the job is to build trust and to reflect out loud, so the team learns how you think, not just what you decide.
