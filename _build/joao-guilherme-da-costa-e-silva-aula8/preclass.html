Aula 08 -- Pre-class

Say Exactly What You Mean -- Writing the RFI

Seis unidades de intertravamento reprovaram no ensaio de EMC — e as quarenta foram construídas com a mesma configuração de aterramento. Como escrever o RFI que fecha o escopo nas seis, registra o fato sobre as quarenta e não pode ser lido de dois jeitos. Key words: request for information (RFI), request for proposal (RFP), interoperability, electromagnetic compatibility (EMC), grounding, interlocking, fail-safe, redundancy, acceptance criteria, workaround, ambiguity, to spell out, to devise, to pin down. Structure: orações relativas — restritiva x não-restritiva (a vírgula que muda o contrato), relativas reduzidas e preposição + which.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Na aula 7 você transformou memória em registro. Aqui você escreve o documento — e um documento técnico não é lido, é interpretado. São as palavras do esclarecimento formal: o que se pergunta, o que se mede, e o que se aceita só provisoriamente. Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo.

Request for information (RFI) -- a formal written question sent to a supplier when a document does not tell you enough (solicitação de informação (esclarecimento técnico formal))
"The RFI that we submitted on Monday has not been answered."
Request for proposal (RFP) -- a formal invitation asking suppliers to propose a solution, with a price, for work not yet awarded (solicitação de proposta (abre porta comercial))
"The rework is inside the contract, so this is an RFI, not an RFP."
Interoperability -- the ability of two different systems to work together and understand each other (interoperabilidade)
"Each system passed alone. What has not been demonstrated is interoperability."
Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC) -- whether equipment can operate without disturbing other equipment through electrical noise (compatibilidade eletromagnética)
"Six units failed the electromagnetic compatibility test on 14 July."
Grounding -- the connection that gives electrical noise a safe path away from the equipment (aterramento)
"It was the grounding configuration, common to all forty units, that caused the interference."
Interlocking -- the signaling logic that makes two conflicting movements impossible at the same time (intertravamento)
"The interlocking units that failed are on the northbound platform."
Fail-safe -- designed so that when it fails, it fails into the state that is safe (à prova de falhas (falha segura))
"A signal that loses power goes to red. That is what fail-safe means."
Redundancy -- a second, independent path that keeps the system working when the first one fails (redundância)
"The redundancy that protects the northbound line has not been demonstrated."
Acceptance criteria -- the written conditions a deliverable must meet before anybody is allowed to accept it (critérios de aceitação)
"The acceptance criteria that apply to this test are in clause 7.4, not in the email."
Workaround -- a temporary way of operating that lets you carry on while the real fix is still missing (solução paliativa / contorno)
"A workaround is acceptable for the trial run. It is not acceptable for the handover."
Ambiguity -- when a sentence is clear, but can honestly be understood in two different ways (ambiguidade)
"The ambiguity in clause 7.4 is what the supplier is relying on."
To spell out -- to state something in complete detail, leaving nothing for the reader to assume (detalhar, explicitar (sem deixar nada subentendido))
"The RFI has to spell out which units, against which criterion, and by when."
To devise -- to think out and design a solution that did not exist before (conceber, bolar (uma solução))
"The workaround that your team devised is not written down anywhere."
To pin down -- to identify something exactly, when it has been vague or moving until now (identificar com precisão, cravar)
"Until we pin down the source of the interference, any fix is a guess."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Forty interlocking units were delivered for Line 5, and on 14 July six of them failed the electromagnetic compatibility test. João Guilherme has to write the request for information that goes to the German supplier, and he has one afternoon to decide what the document actually asks for. The six that failed are the six installed closest to the traction return cable. But all forty were built to the same grounding configuration — and that is the sentence that will cost somebody money.

Read the two versions carefully, because they contain the same words. "The interlocking units that failed the EMC test must be reworked." No commas: the clause RESTRICTS. It says WHICH units — those six, and nobody else. Now the second: "The interlocking units, which failed the EMC test, must be reworked." Two commas, and the clause no longer restricts anything: it simply ADDS a fact about all of them. It says that all forty failed. And all forty did not fail, so João has just written a claim he cannot prove, and the first person to notice will be their lawyer. One comma. Thirty-four units.

So he writes it the way it has to be written. The six units that failed are to be reworked at the supplier's cost. The grounding configuration, which is common to all forty units, must be re-verified — a fact about the whole batch, stated as a fact, without pretending the whole batch failed. Then he compresses, the way every standard on his shelf compresses: the report issued on 14 July does not cover the configuration, and the units radiating above the limit are the six on the northbound platform. And he closes with the two questions that no ambiguity survives: please confirm the standard to which the design was certified, and spell out the conditions under which the EMC test was performed — on the bench, or as installed? The supplier's answer arrives nine days later. Their team has devised a workaround, ferrite filters, and they are careful too: the filters will meet the acceptance criteria applicable to the trial run. Not to the handover. In a technical document, nobody writes a relative clause by accident.

1. "The interlocking units that failed the EMC test must be reworked." Quantas unidades o fornecedor tem de retrabalhar?
A As quarenta — a oração descreve as unidades em geral.
B As SEIS — sem vírgulas, a oração RESTRINGE: ela diz QUAIS unidades. Tire a oração e você nem sabe mais do que se está falando.
C Depende do contrato; a gramática não decide isso.
2. E na versão "The interlocking units, which failed the EMC test, must be reworked"? O que MUDA?
A Nada — a vírgula em inglês é só uma pausa de leitura.
B Fica mais formal, mas o sentido é o mesmo.
C MUDA TUDO: com as duas vírgulas a oração deixa de restringir e passa a ACRESCENTAR um fato sobre TODAS. A frase agora afirma que as quarenta reprovaram — o que é falso, e será usado contra quem escreveu.
3. "The report issued on 14 July does not cover the grounding." Que estrutura é essa?
A Uma relativa REDUZIDA: caiu o which was. Sobrou o particípio passado, com sentido PASSIVO — o relatório QUE FOI emitido. É assim que toda norma técnica é escrita.
B Um erro: falta o verbo principal.
C Um gerúndio, como em português ("o relatório emitindo").
4. Which sentence is correct?
A "Please confirm the standard to that the design was certified."
B "Please confirm the standard to which the design was certified."
C "Please confirm the standard which the design was certified to it."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses & Precision

GRAMMAR

Como a vírgula decide o escopo — e por que a mesma frase, com e sem ela, compromete o fornecedor com seis unidades ou com quarenta (explicação em inglês e português).

Estrutura / StructureO que faz / What it doesExemplo
Relativa RESTRITIVA
sem vírgulas
(that / which / who)		RESTRINGE: diz QUAIS. Tire a oração e você não sabe mais do que se fala. É ela que define o ESCOPO do trabalho — e escopo é dinheiro."The six units that failed the EMC test must be reworked." — só aquelas seis.
Relativa NÃO-RESTRITIVA
entre duas vírgulas
(NUNCA that)		ACRESCENTA um fato sobre TODAS. Tire a oração e a frase continua de pé. Depois de vírgula, só which (coisas) ou who (pessoas)."The units, which failed the test, must be reworked." — TODAS as quarenta reprovaram.
Relativa REDUZIDA
particípio passado		Cai o which was / that were quando o sentido é PASSIVO. É a compressão de toda norma técnica que ele lê."the report which was issued on 14 July" → "the report issued on 14 July"
Relativa REDUZIDA
-ing		Cai o which is / that are quando o sentido é ATIVO: a unidade está FAZENDO, não sofrendo."the units which are radiating above the limit" → "the units radiating above the limit"
Preposição + whichO registro formal do RFI e da especificação. A preposição vem ANTES de which — e depois de preposição nunca cabe that."the standard to which the design was certified" · "the conditions under which the test was performed"
Para que serveSeis unidades reprovaram. Quarenta têm o mesmo aterramento. "The units that failed" compromete o fornecedor com seis. "The units, which failed," afirma que as quarenta reprovaram — o que é falso, e o advogado deles vai apontar isso na primeira leitura. E "the grounding configuration, which is common to all forty units, must be re-verified" consegue as quarenta pelo motivo certo. A gramática aqui não é enfeite: ela é o escopo do contrato.

Atenção (três erros de brasileiro): (1) "The RFI that we submitted it on Monday" — em português dizemos "o RFI que a gente mandou ELE"; em inglês o pronome NÃO se repete: o that já é o objeto; (2) "the configuration, that is common to all forty" — depois de vírgula, that é impossível: use which; (3) "the cable installing last week" — o cabo não está instalando nada: ele FOI instalado, então é installed (particípio passado). O português cobre os dois casos com a mesma forma; o inglês não.

Collocações do RFI: submit an RFI (nunca "send"), respond to technical queries (nunca "answer to"), verify compliance with the specs (nunca "compliance TO"), ensure interoperability, validate the design, exceed the emission limit, implement a workaround, fall outside the scope, meet the acceptance criteria.

A pergunta que se faz antes de enviar: "Can this sentence be read in a second way that costs me money?" Se puder, ela ainda não está pronta. Ambiguidade não é confusão: a frase ambígua parece perfeitamente clara — e é exatamente por isso que passa.

Stage 2: Put the RFI in Order

Order

Coloque os passos do RFI na ordem correta — do escopo restrito até o prazo de morte da solução paliativa.

?Close the workaround: the ferrite filters are accepted for the trial run only, and not for the handover.
?Add the fact about all forty: the grounding configuration, which is common to the whole batch, has not been verified as installed.
?Compress the evidence: the report issued on 14 July does not cover the configuration.
?Ask formally: please confirm the standard to which the design was certified.
?Restrict the scope: the six interlocking units that failed the EMC test are to be reworked.
?Ask formally again: please spell out the conditions under which the test was performed.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. Repare na PAUSA das vírgulas na segunda frase: em inglês falado a não-restritiva se OUVE — há uma pausa antes e depois, e ela é o que avisa o interlocutor de que você está falando de todas as quarenta. E diga in-ter-op-er-a-BIL-i-ty, com o acento na quinta sílaba.

The six interlocking units that failed the EMC test must be reworked at the supplier's cost.
As seis unidades de intertravamento que reprovaram no ensaio de EMC devem ser retrabalhadas às custas do fornecedor.
The grounding configuration, which is common to all forty units, must be re-verified.
A configuração de aterramento, que é comum às quarenta unidades, precisa ser reverificada.
The report issued on 14 July does not cover the grounding configuration.
O relatório emitido em 14 de julho não cobre a configuração de aterramento.
Please confirm the standard to which the design was certified.
Por favor, confirme a norma segundo a qual o projeto foi certificado.
The workaround that your team devised does not meet the acceptance criteria that apply at handover.
A solução paliativa que a equipe de vocês concebeu não atende aos critérios de aceitação válidos no handover.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da call de esclarecimento técnico — inclusive quando a fornecedora argumenta que trinta e quatro aprovações provam o projeto, ou oferece um paliativo como se fosse a solução.

Você quer que o fornecedor retrabalhe APENAS as seis unidades reprovadas. Você escreve:
A "The interlocking units, which failed the EMC test, must be reworked at your cost."
B "The six interlocking units that failed the EMC test on 14 July must be reworked at your cost."
C "Please rework the units with the interference problem as soon as possible."
Você quer registrar que o aterramento é o MESMO nas quarenta — sem afirmar que as quarenta reprovaram:
A "The forty units, which failed the EMC test, share the same grounding configuration."
B "The grounding configuration, which is common to all forty units, has not been verified in the installed condition."
C "All the units have a grounding problem that needs to be looked at."
Ingrid diz: "Trinta e quatro unidades passaram, logo o projeto está correto." A resposta mais forte é:
A "That is a reasonable hypothesis, and it may well be right. But thirty-four units passing a test does not demonstrate a design — and two of them passed by almost nothing."
B "That is not true and you know it."
C "All right. If thirty-four passed, let us close the design question."
Ela oferece filtros de ferrite "que resolvem em uma semana". Você responde:
A "Perfect. If the emissions come down, we can consider it closed."
B "I will take the filters for the trial run. I will not take them for the handover, because a workaround does not meet the acceptance criteria that apply at handover."
C "We do not accept workarounds under any circumstances."
Você precisa saber COMO eles ensaiaram (na bancada ou instalado). Você pergunta:
A "How did you do the test exactly? Can you explain it to me?"
B "Please spell out the conditions under which the EMC test was performed, and confirm the standard to which the design was certified."
C "The conditions which you tested the design in them are not clear."

You are opening the clarification call on RFI-114.
