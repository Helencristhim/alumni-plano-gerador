Q3 Project Review — Banco Atlas Migration

Every quarter, our team stops to look back. We call it the quarterly review, and it has one simple goal: to recap what we have done and to plan what comes next. In the last three months, the Banco Atlas cloud migration has been our main objective, and I am happy to say the project is still on track.

Let me share the numbers. We set a target of moving forty systems to the cloud, and so far we have moved thirty-eight. Two key deliverables have gone live, and the average response time has improved by almost half. Our most important metric, customer satisfaction, has reached eighty-two percent. That result has exceeded the goal we agreed on in the summer.

Of course, the quarter has not been perfect. We have had one setback: a security test found a problem, so the final rollout has slipped by two weeks. Even so, the team has made real progress and the client has noticed. Next quarter our objective is clear — finish the migration, close the gap, and turn this achievement into a long-term contract.

Adapted for class use from an internal IT project status report

Chapter 4: The Code

Results with the Present Perfect

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"We have moved thirty-eight systems so far."

"The metric has reached eighty-two percent."

"The result has exceeded our target."

"The team has made real progress this quarter."

FormUseExample
have / has + past participlea result or change up to NOW (no exact time)We have moved 38 systems.
has + past participlethird person: it / he / she / the teamIt has reached 82%.

Use the present perfect for results so far, with words like so far, already, yet and this quarter. The verb after have / has is the past participle (moved, reached, gone, made).

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

We have moved 38 systems last week.
We moved 38 systems last week.

With a finished past time (last week, yesterday, in 2022), use the past simple, not the present perfect.

Two deliverables have went live.
Two deliverables have gone live.

After have / has, use the past participle (gone, not went; done, not did).

Chapter 5: In the Review Meeting

Presenting the Quarter

Dialogue

The Review Call

D
Thanks for the recap, Rafael. Let us start with the headline: where are we on the migration?
R
We are on track. We have moved thirty-eight of the forty systems, so we are almost there.
D
Good. And the metric the board cares about?
R
Customer satisfaction has reached eighty-two percent. It has exceeded the target we set in the summer.
D
That is a real achievement. Any setback I should know about?
R
One. A security test found a problem, so the rollout has slipped by two weeks. The team has already made a recovery plan.
Comprehension

About the Meeting

1. How many systems has the team moved so far?
Thirty-eight of the forty systems.
2. Has customer satisfaction reached its target?
Yes, it has reached 82%, above the target.
3. What setback does Rafael report?
A security test found a problem, so the rollout has slipped by two weeks.
