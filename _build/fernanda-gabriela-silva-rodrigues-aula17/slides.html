Chapter 3: The Code

Cheaper, Faster, the Best

-er + than for two · the + -est / the most for all

Discover the Grammar

What Do We Add to Compare?

"Andino is cheaper than Verde."

"Verde is more expensive than Andino."

"Verde is the best option."

UseFormExample
Compare two (short word)word + -er + thanAndino is cheaper than Verde.
Compare two (long word)more + word + thanVerde is more expensive than Andino.
The best of all (short)the + word + -estAndino has the lowest price.
The best of all (long)the most + wordVerde is the most reliable.
Irregulargood → better → the bestVerde is the best option.

Short words add -er / the -est (cheaper, the cheapest). Long words use more / the most (more expensive, the most expensive). "good" is irregular: better, the best.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Andino is more cheaper."

✓ RIGHT

"Andino is cheaper."

✗ WRONG

"It is the most cheap option."

✓ RIGHT

"It is the cheapest option."

Use -er OR more, never both: "cheaper", never "more cheaper". Short words take the -est ("the cheapest"), not "the most cheap".
