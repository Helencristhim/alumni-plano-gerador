Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

At Sucafina, Fernanda must compare two coffee suppliers. Andino is cheaper than Verde, and the price is the lowest. But Verde is faster, and the quality is higher. Verde is more expensive than Andino, but it is the most reliable supplier. For an important client, faster is better than cheaper. So Fernanda and Daniel choose Verde. It is the best option for this order.

1. Which supplier is cheaper? (Qual fornecedor e mais barato?)
A Andino is cheaper. (Andino e mais barato.)
B Verde is cheaper. (Verde e mais barato.)
C Both are the same. (Os dois sao iguais.)
2. Why is Verde the best option? (Por que Verde e a melhor opcao?)
A Because it is the cheapest. (Porque e o mais barato.)
B Because it is faster and the most reliable. (Porque e mais rapido e o mais confiavel.)
C Because it has no price. (Porque nao tem preco.)
3. "More expensive than" -- what does it mean? (O que significa?)
A It costs less money. (Custa menos dinheiro.)
B It costs more money than the other. (Custa mais dinheiro que o outro.)
C It is free. (E gratis.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

Grammar

Para comparar duas coisas, use -er + than (palavras curtas) ou more + palavra + than (palavras longas). Para dizer a melhor de todas, use the + -est ou the most. / To compare two, use -er + than or more + word + than. For the best of all, use the + -est or the most.

Uso / UseForma / FormExample / Exemplo
Comparar dois (curto) / Compare two (short)word + -er + thancheaper than (mais barato que)
Comparar dois (longo) / Compare two (long)more + word + thanmore expensive than (mais caro que)
A melhor de todas (curto) / The best (short)the + word + -estthe cheapest (o mais barato)
A melhor de todas (longo) / The best (long)the most + wordthe most reliable (o mais confiavel)
Irregular / Irregulargood → better → the bestthe best option (a melhor opcao)

Atencao: use -er OU more, nunca os dois -- e 'cheaper', nunca 'more cheaper'. Palavras curtas usam the -est ('the cheapest'), nao 'the most cheap'. 'good' e irregular: better, the best. / Use -er OR more, never both. Short words take the -est. "good" is irregular: better, the best.
