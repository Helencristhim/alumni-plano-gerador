Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Inviting and suggesting plans

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we suggest, invite, accept, and refuse?

"Let's go to the movie."

"Would you like to come to my party?"

"I'd love to! What time?"

"I'm sorry, I can't tonight."

Grammar Rule

Inviting and Suggesting

FunctionStructureExample
Suggest togetherLet's + verbLet's go to the movie.
InviteWould you like to + verb?Would you like to come?
AcceptYes, I'd love to!I'd love to. What time?
Refuse politelyI'm sorry, I can'tI'm sorry, I can't tonight.

Key idea: to suggest a plan together, use Let's + verb. To invite, use Would you like to + verb. To say yes, say I'd love to. To say no in a kind way, say I'm sorry, I can't.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Let's to go to the movie."
"Let's go to the movie."

After Let's and Would you like to, use the simple verb: go, come, meet (no "to" after Let's, no "-ing").
