Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dienane wants to invite her friend Marcos. She calls him: "Would you like to come to my party?" Marcos answers: "I'd love to! When is it?" Dienane says: "On the weekend, not tonight." Then Marcos makes a plan too: "Let's go to the movie on Sunday." In English, we invite with Would you like to + verb. We suggest a plan together with Let's + verb. To say yes, we say I'd love to. To say no in a kind way, we say I'm sorry, I can't.

1. How do we invite someone in English?
A "Would you like come to my party?"
B "Would you like to come to my party?"
C "You like come to my party?"
2. How do we suggest a plan together?
A "Let's go to the movie."
B "Let's to go to the movie."
C "Let's going to the movie."
3. When is the party?
A On the weekend.
B Tonight.
C On Monday.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Inviting and Suggesting

GRAMMAR

Em inglês, convidamos com Would you like to + verbo, e sugerimos um plano juntos com Let's + verbo. / In English, we invite with Would you like to + verb, and we suggest a plan together with Let's + verb.

Function / FunçãoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
sugerir juntosLet's + verboLet's go to the movie.
convidarWould you like to + verbo?Would you like to come?
aceitarYes, I'd love to!I'd love to. What time?
recusar com educaçãoI'm sorry, I can'tI'm sorry, I can't tonight.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de Let's e Would you like to, use o verbo simples: go, come, meet (sem "to" depois de Let's, sem "-ing"). Para aceitar diga I'd love to; para recusar com educação diga I'm sorry, I can't.

Lesson 15 -- Making Plans & Invitations

Series
Extra English -- Episode 2: "Hector Goes Shopping" (party scene)

A simple comedy made for English learners. The friends plan a party and invite people in slow, clear English -- Would you like to come? Let's meet on the weekend. Perfect for A1 listening about invitations and plans.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every invitation you hear (Would you like to..., Let's...). Then say out loud: "Would you like to come to my party?" and "Let's go to the movie".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Making Plans and Invitations"

A short, friendly lesson on plans: how to invite with "Would you like to...?", suggest with "Let's...", say yes with "I'd love to", and say no kindly with "I'm sorry, I can't". The hosts explain the phrases slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then invite a friend to a movie on the weekend, using at least two phrases from today.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Inviting and Making Plans"

A clear video that teaches plan vocabulary (invite, plan, weekend, tonight, party, movie) and how to invite and suggest, like "Would you like to come?" and "Let's go to the movie", with pictures and simple sentences.

After watching, record a short voice message: invite a friend to a party or a movie, say the day and the time, and use Let's and Would you like to.
