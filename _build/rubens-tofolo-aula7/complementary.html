Lesson 7 -- At the Restaurant

Series
Chef's Table / Somebody Feed Phil -- any restaurant episode

Guests order full meals on screen: they choose an appetizer, ask the server for the main course, mention an allergy, and ask for the bill. Listen for polite ordering language: I would like..., could I have...?, and what do you recommend?

Watch with English subtitles. Write three polite requests with would like or could I have, and one question with what do you recommend. Then order your own imaginary dinner the same way.

Podcast
The Splendid Table -- episodes about eating out

A relaxed conversation about food and restaurants: choosing a main course, a side dish, and a dessert, talking about a spicy dish, and leaving a tip. Full of natural language for ordering politely.

Note three sentences that describe ordering or recommending food. Then record a short order for dinner using at least three new words, such as appetizer, side dish, and beverage.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "How to Order Food in English"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: would like for polite requests, could I have and can I have for polite questions, asking what do you recommend, and useful restaurant words like bill, tip, and refill.

After watching, write five sentences for a meal: two with would like, two with could I have, and one question with what do you recommend. Use appetizer, dessert, and bill at least once each.
