Grammar Rule

Asking and Giving Directions

FunctionStructureExample
Ask the wayExcuse me, how do I get to + place?How do I get to the bank?
Go forwardGo straight (+ for ... blocks)Go straight for two blocks.
Change directionTurn left / Turn right (+ at...)Turn left at the traffic light.
Say where it isIt is on your left / rightThe bank is on your right.

Key idea: to ask the way, say Excuse me, how do I get to + the place. To answer, use direction words: Go straight, Turn left, Turn right, Cross the street. To say where a place is, use is on your left or is on your right.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Turn to left at the corner."
"Turn left at the corner."

We say Turn left and Turn right (no "to"). And a place is on your left or on your right.
