Lesson 14 -- Getting Help in the City

Series
Extra English -- Episode 6: "A Trip Around Town"

A simple comedy made for English learners. In this episode the characters get lost in the city and ask for directions in slow, clear English -- go straight, turn left, turn right, cross the street. Perfect for A1 listening about directions.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every direction word you hear (turn, straight, left, right, cross, traffic light). Then say out loud: "How do I get to the bank?" and "Go straight and turn left".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Asking for Directions"

A short, friendly lesson on directions: how to ask "Excuse me, how do I get to...?" and how to answer with "Go straight", "Turn left", "Turn right" and "Cross the street". The hosts explain the phrases slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then describe the way from your home to a shop, using at least three direction words.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Directions: Asking the Way"

A clear video that teaches direction vocabulary (turn, straight, left, right, cross, traffic light) and how to ask for help, like "Excuse me, how do I get to the bank?", with pictures, maps and simple sentences.

After watching, record short directions from the bus stop to your office: use go straight, turn left or right, and say which side it is on.
