Meeting an International Partner

Welcome a partner from abroad and ask and answer basic questions. Key words: visitor, guest, to welcome, to introduce, company, abroad, agenda, country, contact, detail. Structure: question forms with do / does and question words (what, where, who).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Visitor -- a person who comes to a place to meet people or see things
"Next week we have a visitor from abroad."
Guest -- a person you invite to an event or a meeting
"I always welcome my guest at the front desk."
To welcome -- to greet someone in a friendly way when they arrive
"We welcome partners from other countries."
To introduce -- to tell people someone's name for the first time
"Let me introduce my team to you."
Company -- a business that makes or sells products
"His company works with small businesses."
Abroad -- in or to another country
"Greg is a partner from abroad."
Agenda -- a list of the topics you will discuss in a meeting
"I prepare an agenda before every meeting."
Country -- a land area with its own government and people
"He works with businesses in his country."
Contact -- a person you know who can help you in business
"I am his main contact here in Brazil."
Detail -- a small piece of information
"Please send me one more detail about the visit."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Greg is a visitor from a company abroad. He sends an email to Flavia with many questions. "What does Sebrae do?" he asks. "Where is your head office? Do you work with small businesses? Does your team help companies that sell abroad?" Flavia reads every detail and writes clear answers. On the day of the visit, she is ready to welcome her guest and introduce her team, and to answer every question with confidence.

1. Why does Greg use "does" in "What does Sebrae do?"
A Because Sebrae is like "it" (he / she / it), so we use "does."
B Because every question uses "does."
C Because "do" and "does" mean different things.
2. Why does Greg say "Do you work with small businesses?"
A We use "do" only for the past.
B We use "do" with you, I, we, and they.
C We use "do" with he, she, and it.
3. Which question asks for a place?
A "What does Sebrae do?"
B "Where is your head office?"
C "Do you work with small businesses?"
