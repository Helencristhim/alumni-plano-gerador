Chapter 4: Do, Does & Question Words

do · does · what / where / who

How to build a question

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Do you work with small businesses?

Does Greg work abroad?

What does your company do?

Where is your head office?

Helper / wordUse it forExample
doI, you, we, theyDo you work here?
doeshe, she, itDoes she work here?
what / where / whoa thing, a place, a personWhat does your company do?

do = I/you/we/they · does = he/she/it · after does, the main verb has no -s.

Language Focus

Do, Does, or a Question Word?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"What your company do?"

✓ RIGHT

"What does your company do?"

✗ WRONG

"Does you work with small businesses?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you work with small businesses?"

Never drop do or does in a question. Use do with you, I, we, they, and does with he, she, it. After does, the main verb has no -s.

Dialogue

Greg Visits the Office

G
Hello Flavia. Thank you for the warm welcome.
F
Welcome to Sebrae, Greg. Let me introduce my team and our agenda.
G
Thank you. I am from a company in the United Kingdom, and we work with small businesses.
F
That is our world too. What does your company do exactly?
G
We help small businesses sell their products abroad. Do you do the same?
F
Yes, we do. We help them grow and sell to other countries.
G
Great. Who is my main contact after today?
F
I am your contact, Greg. Here are all the details.
