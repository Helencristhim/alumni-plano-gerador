Chapter 3 -- Your Toolbox

Your Grammar Toolbox

Now, future, compare, the best, and results

Listen and Notice

Find the Tool

Look at the orange words. Each sentence uses a different grammar tool.

"Right now, I am calling a new customer."

"Next month, we are going to launch a new service."

"Our product is faster and more reliable than the old one."

"It is the most popular choice on the market."

"This year, we have reached our sales target."

The Rule

Your Business Grammar

ToolExample
Now -- present continuous (am/is/are + -ing)I am calling a customer now.
Future plan -- going to (+ base verb)We are going to launch a service.
Compare two -- comparative (-er / more + than)It is faster and more reliable than the old one.
The best -- superlative (the -est / the most)It is the most popular choice.
Result -- present perfect (have/has + participle)We have reached our target.

Key idea: use the present continuous for what is happening now, going to for a future plan, comparatives (with than) to compare two things, superlatives (with the) for the best, and the present perfect for a result up to now.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I calling a customer. It is faster then the old one. We have reach the target."
"I am calling a customer. It is faster than the old one. We have reached the target."

The present continuous needs to be (am/is/are). Comparatives use than. The present perfect uses have/has + the past participle.

Practice

Choose the Right Tool

"Our model is _____more reliable than the old one." (reliable -- compare)
"It is _____the most popular product on the market." (popular -- the best)
"This year, we _____have achieved our main goal." (achieve -- result)
