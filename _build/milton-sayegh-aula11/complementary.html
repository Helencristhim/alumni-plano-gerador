Lesson 11 -- Problem Solving: When Things Go Wrong

Series
The Bear (FX/Hulu) -- a kitchen-crisis episode (e.g. S1E7 "Review")

A high-pressure business where things go wrong constantly. Watch how the staff name a problem fast, stay calm, and commit to a fix instead of making excuses — the same control you need when a luxury client calls with a complaint.

Tip: each time something breaks, write the first conditional the team uses or could use: "If we ___, we will ___."

Podcast
The Customer Service Revolution (John DiJulius) -- any episode on service recovery

Real cases of how strong businesses turn a complaint into loyalty: apologize once, own the problem, and resolve it with a clear offer of replacement or compensation. The exact moves from this class, in English, from real companies.

Tip: note one phrase the host uses to apologize with control, and one offer of compensation. Reuse them with a real client.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "First and second conditionals" explained

A short, clear lesson on exactly the grammar from this class: if + present + will for real situations, and if + past + would for hypothetical, softer ones. Hear native speakers use both forms in natural business contexts.

Tip: after watching, say five conditionals out loud about your own export work — three first, two second.
