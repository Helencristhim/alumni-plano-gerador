Warm-up

The Box Arrives

The Idea

Name It, Own It, Fix It

Name the problem

Key Expressions

Phrases That Keep the Client

"Thank you for flagging this — let me apologize for the inconvenience." — opens with control, not panic
"Let me make sure I understand the problem before I promise anything." — names the problem clearly
"If you send the faulty pieces back today, we will ship replacements on Wednesday." — commits to a dated fix
"To make up for the trouble, we would cover the express freight ourselves." — offers compensation, with control
"You have my word: if anything slips, I will call you, not the day before." — closes on a clear commitment
Chapter 3

If This, Then That

First & second conditionals for solving problems

Grammar Discovery

Two Kinds of If

Same little word — why does one say will and the other would?

If you send them today, we will ship on Wednesday. (a real, likely fix)

If the clasps fail again, we will replace the whole batch. (a real promise)

If I were you, I would choose the replacement. (soft advice, hypothetical)

If we offered a refund, you would lose the pieces for the opening. (an imagined result)

Real and likely → will. Hypothetical and softer → would.

Grammar Rule

First vs Second Conditional

TypeStructureUse it for
First (real)If + present simple, ... will + base verbFixes you can actually deliver: "If you confirm now, we will start tonight."
Second (hypothetical)If + past simple, ... would + base verbAdvice & gentle offers: "If I were you, I would keep the order." Softer, less pushy.
Word orderResult first → no comma"We will resolve this if you confirm now."
Watch Out

Common Mistake

If you will send the pieces, we will ship Wednesday.
If you send the pieces, we will ship Wednesday.

After if in a real condition, use the present simple — not will. The will belongs only in the result half of the sentence.

Practice

Complete the Conditional

"If you confirm today, we _____ the replacements tonight." → will start (first conditional)
"If I _____ you, I would keep the rest of the order." → were (second conditional)
"We will cover the freight if the delay _____ on us." → is (present after if)
"If we offered a full refund, you _____ lose the pieces for the opening." → would (second conditional)

Practice

Your Turn to Solve

From quick responses to full resolutions

Quick Fire

Respond on the Spot

1 / 5

1. A client says the order is two days late. Reassure them with a dated fix.

"If you confirm the address now, we will dispatch today and you will have it by Friday."

2. The client is unsure whether to wait for a replacement. Give gentle advice.

"If I were you, I would wait for the replacement — the quality would be exactly right."

3. A stone is loose on one piece. Offer two clear options.

"If you return it, we will reset the stone for free; or, if you prefer, we will issue a refund on that piece."

4. The client asks what happens if the same fault appears again.

"If it happens again, we will replace the whole batch and review the supplier — you have my word."

5. Close the call with a calm commitment to resolve it.

"You have my word: I will resolve this today, and to make up for the trouble, we would cover the freight."

Spot the Error

Fix the Conditional

"If you will return the pieces, we will refund you."
"If you return the pieces, we will refund you." (present after if, not will)
"If I was you, I would choose the replacement."
"If I were you, I would choose the replacement." (fixed form were in the second conditional)
"If we offered a discount, you will keep the order."
"If we offered a discount, you would keep the order." (past + would, not will)
"We resolve it if you would confirm today."
"We will resolve it if you confirm today." (first conditional: present + will)

Sentence Building

Use the words to make a conditional, then click to compare

1. if / you / send / today / we / ship / Wednesday (real)

2. if / I / be / you / I / wait / the replacement (advice)

3. if / the delay / be / on us / we / cover / the freight (real)
"If the delay is on us, we will cover the freight."

"If we offered a refund, you would lose the pieces for the opening."
5. if / it / happen / again / we / replace / the whole batch (real promise)
"If it happens again, we will replace the whole batch."
