Which Structure Do You Use?

Say the structure, then click to check

1. You want a colleague to do something, politely.
Could you...? — "Could you set up a call?"
2. You talk about a plan for next week.
going to — "We are going to confirm the price."
3. You offer to help a colleague.
can — "I can help you with the report."
4. You compare two suppliers.
cheaper than — "Andino is cheaper than the other one."
5. You say a task is done.
have/has + participle — "I have finished the report."
Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"We going to confirm the price."

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to confirm the price."

✗ WRONG

"Andino is more cheap that the other."

✓ RIGHT

"Andino is cheaper than the other."

✗ WRONG

"I has sent the report."

✓ RIGHT

"I have sent the report."

Remember: "be" + going to for plans, "cheaper than" for comparisons (not "more cheap that"), and "have" with I/you/we/they.

Chapter 4: At Work

The Full Conversation

planning the week on a big order

Dialogue

Planning the Week

D
Fernanda, could you set up a call with the supplier for Thursday?
F
Sure. I will arrange it. So we are going to confirm the price on the call?
D
Yes. We have two suppliers. Can you compare them for me?
F
Of course. The Andino supplier is cheaper and faster than the other one, so I think we should choose them.
D
Good. One more thing: the new colleague has a problem with the report. Can you help him?
F
Yes, I can support him. And here is the progress: we have already sent the contract, but the deposit has not arrived yet.
D
Are we still on track for the deadline?
F
Yes. We were a little behind schedule, but now we are on track. I will send you the update today.
