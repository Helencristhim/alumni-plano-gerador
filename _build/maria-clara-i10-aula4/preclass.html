Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

What Is Happening Right Now -- Present Continuous for Live Situations and Travel Updates

Say what is happening right now: at the airport, in the delegation, at the conference. Key words: arrive, depart, conference, schedule, agenda, announce, present, participate. Structure: the present continuous (be + verb-ing) for something happening now or soon. And the phrase for a future you are happy about: "I am looking forward to...".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Take your time — listen twice, then say it out loud. These are the eight words of a day of travel.

To arrive -- to reach a place, to get there
"The delegation is arriving at the airport now."
To depart -- to leave a place and go away
"Our flight departs at noon."
Conference -- a big formal meeting for work or study
"I am speaking at an international conference."
Schedule -- a plan that shows the times of events
"The schedule shows three talks today."
Agenda -- the list of topics for a meeting
"The rector is reading the agenda."
To announce -- to tell people something officially, out loud
"The airport announces the next flight."
To present -- to show and explain your work to a group
"Tomorrow I am presenting my work."
To participate -- to take part in an activity with other people
"I am participating in the forum."

GRAMMAR

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Continuous (I am working / she is arriving)

GRAMMAR

Use the present continuous for something happening right now or around now (this week, these days). It always has two parts: the verb be (am / is / are) + verb-ing.

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
II am workingI am not workingAm I working?
he / she / itShe is workingShe is not workingIs she working?
you / we / theyWe are workingWe are not workingAre we working?

Spelling of verb-ing: make → making (drop the e), sit → sitting (double the consonant), travel → traveling. Compare: the present simple is for routines ("I travel once a month"), the present continuous is for now ("I am traveling right now"). Useful phrase: I am looking forward to + noun or verb-ing ("I am looking forward to the conference").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real moment at the airport and the forum.

An officer asks "What are you doing here?" You answer:
A "I am arriving for the education conference."
B "I arrive for the education conference."
C "I am arrive for the conference."
You want to talk about a plan for tomorrow. The best sentence is:
A "I present my work tomorrow."
B "I am presenting my work tomorrow."

A colleague asks "Are you participating in the forum?" You answer:
A "Yes, I am."

You want to say you are excited about the trip. The best sentence is:

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your rehearsal for the airport in Seoul.

Imagine you are at the airport in Seoul right now. Describe what is happening around you in 4-5 sentences, all in the present continuous: what you are doing, what the delegation is doing, and what you are looking forward to. Use: I am..., The delegation is..., I am looking forward to... Take your time and don't read from a script.
