Video — grammar
Learn English Tenses: The Present Continuous (engVid)

This is the exact grammar of lesson 4: be + verb-ing for what is happening right now. The teacher speaks slowly and shows many examples on screen — am / is / are, and the -ing spelling rules (make → making, sit → sitting).

Tip: after the video, look around your room and say five sentences out loud about what is happening now: "I am sitting at my desk", "The phone is charging". Don't write them first.

Video — speaking
Airport Vocabulary: Speak English at the Airport (English with Lucy)

This is exactly the mission of lesson 4: your arrival in Seoul. Lucy shows the real phrases you need at the airport — arrivals, departures, gates, and the announcements you will hear — in clear, slow American-friendly English.

Tip: pause after each phrase and say what you are doing at that moment: "I am arriving now", "I am looking for my gate", "I am waiting for my suitcase". Speak out loud, don't just listen.

Podcast — listening
Traveling Soon: English Phrases for the Airport (All Ears English, ep. 2297)

Natural American conversation about the day of travel — the airport, the check-in, the departure — perfect for your two hours of daily immersion. Understanding real speech by ear is the hardest skill, and the only way to train it is a little every day.

Tip: listen ONCE without stopping — don't worry about the words you miss. Then write down three present continuous sentences you can use at an airport (I am boarding, they are announcing, we are departing). Bring them to the next class.
