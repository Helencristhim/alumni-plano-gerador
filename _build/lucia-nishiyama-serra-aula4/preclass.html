Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

First Conditional in Executive Presentations -- Owning the Room in an AI Safety Briefing

Building arguments and making predictions with the first conditional. Key words: governance, compliance, framework, exposure, to mitigate, threshold, to enforce, to roll out. Structure: first conditional (if + present simple, will + base verb) for predictions, risks, and recommendations, plus unless and as long as.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Governance -- the system of rules and decisions that controls how an organization is run
"If our governance is clear, the whole team will make better decisions."
Compliance -- the act of following the official rules, laws, or standards for your work
"If we ignore compliance, we will face serious problems later."
Framework -- a clear structure of rules and ideas you use to plan and decide
"If we build a strong framework, we will scale with confidence."
Exposure -- the amount of risk or possible harm you are open to
"If we act now, we will reduce our exposure significantly."
To mitigate -- to make a risk or a problem less serious or less likely
"If we add these controls, we will mitigate most of the risk."
Threshold -- the level at which something starts to happen or becomes acceptable
"If the risk passes this threshold, we will pause the launch."
To enforce -- to make sure a rule or a policy is actually followed
"If we do not enforce the policy, no one will follow it."
To roll out -- to introduce a new policy or program step by step
"If the board approves, we will roll out the framework next quarter."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Lucia is briefing the board on AI safety. "If we do not set a clear threshold, our exposure will grow," she says. "But if we roll out this framework and enforce it, we will mitigate most of the risk. Unless leadership aligns on this, we will not be able to scale. As long as we stay in compliance, we will keep the board's trust." She does not sound worried; she sounds decisive. Every sentence connects a condition to a result, and every result uses will. That is why the room listens.

1. Why does Lucia say "If we do not set a clear threshold, our exposure will grow"?
A It is a real, likely result: a condition (present) plus a prediction (will).
B It describes something that already happened last year.
C It is an imaginary situation that will never happen.
2. "Unless leadership aligns on this, we will not be able to scale." What does "unless" mean here?
A Because leadership aligns.
B If leadership does not align.
C After leadership aligns.
3. Why does the text say she "sounds decisive"?
A Because she uses "maybe" and "I think" in every sentence.
B Because she only describes problems.
C Because every result uses "will", which sounds like a decision, not a doubt.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

How to make predictions, present risks, and recommend action in a briefing.

FormUseExample
Affirmative
If + present, will + verb		A real, likely result: a prediction or a recommendation.If we enforce this framework, we will mitigate the risk.
Negative result
will not / won't		Say what will not happen if a condition is not met.If leadership does not align, we won't be able to scale.
Question
What will happen if ...?		Ask about a likely result.What will happen if we do nothing?
Unless
(= if ... not)		A warning.Unless we act, we will fall behind.
As long as / provided thatA condition or a promise: "As long as we stay compliant, we will keep their trust." After if, use the present -- never will. Use will (not would) for a real, likely plan; would is the second conditional.
