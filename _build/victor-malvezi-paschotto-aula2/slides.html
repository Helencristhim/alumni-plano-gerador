Chapter 3

The What-If Engine

Third Conditional for post-performance analysis

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If we had launched the campaign earlier, we would have hit the Q3 target."

"If they had invested in digital earlier, they would have doubled their traction."

"If the headcount had not decreased, we would have outperformed every region."

"We would have reached the milestone if we had addressed the pipeline issue sooner."

What pattern do you notice?

Grammar Rule

The Structure

PartStructureExample
IF clauseIf + subject + had + past participleIf we had launched earlier...
Result clausesubject + would have + past participle...we would have hit the target.
UseImaginary past situations and their imaginary results. The action did NOT happen.
NegativeIf + subject + had not + PPIf we had not lost two people, we would have exceeded the target.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If we would have launched earlier, we would have hit the target."

✓ RIGHT

"If we had launched earlier, we would have hit the target."

✗ WRONG

"If we had launched earlier, we had hit the target."

✓ RIGHT

"If we had launched earlier, we would have hit the target."

WOULD goes in the result clause, never in the IF clause. The IF clause always uses HAD + past participle.

Hedging Language

Sound Diplomatic

Soften your statements when presenting data

"It would appear that..."

"It would appear that the variance is due to seasonal factors."

"Roughly speaking..."

"Roughly speaking, we are looking at a fifteen percent increase."

Chapter 4

The Review Room

Presenting Q3 data to a skeptical VP

Dialogue

The Q3 Review

V
Rachel, thank you for joining. I would like to walk you through the Q3 pipeline and revenue stream data for LATAM.
R
Go ahead, Victor. I have noticed some variance between the forecast and the actuals. Can you account for that?
V
Absolutely. Roughly speaking, the conversion rate dipped mid-quarter due to a delayed campaign launch. If we had launched on schedule, we would have hit the original target.
R
That is concerning. What about the headcount reduction? How has that affected the pipeline?
V
Fair point. We lost two team members. If we had not lost them, we would have outperformed every benchmark. That said, the pipeline still grew by thirty-two percent.
R
Thirty-two percent is strong. But can you make a compelling case for why we should increase investment in LATAM next quarter?
V
It would appear that LATAM is gaining traction faster than any other region. The current numbers are just the tip of the iceberg. With additional headcount, the forecast shows we could reach a milestone of five million in revenue.
R
Five million. That is ambitious. Send me the full breakdown by Friday. I want to address this with the board next week.
Comprehension

She asks him to make a compelling case for increasing investment in LATAM next quarter.
3. What does Rachel want by Friday and why?
She wants the full breakdown because she plans to address it with the board next week.

New from the Dialogue
