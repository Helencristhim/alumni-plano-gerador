Complementary Activities

Extra materials to reinforce learning outside of class. Mark as done after watching or listening.

Lesson 2 -- Owning the Room: Presenting Growth Data

Series
Billions (Showtime) -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

High-stakes financial world where hedge fund managers present data to stakeholders, defend their strategies, and use persuasive language under pressure. Pay attention to how characters frame numbers, use hedging language ("it would appear"), and make compelling cases to convince skeptical counterparts. Direct connection to Lesson 2: presenting data to a skeptical VP, using numbers persuasively, and defending performance with confidence.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 expressions the characters use when presenting financial data or defending a position to a superior.

Podcast
Freakonomics Radio -- Episode 500: "How to Make a Prediction"

This episode explores how experts make predictions based on data, what causes forecasts to go wrong, and how to present uncertain results with integrity. The hosts interview economists and decision scientists who discuss variance, benchmarks, and the language of data-driven conclusions. Direct connection to Victor's work: how to talk about forecasts confidently without overpromising, and how to use hedging language like a professional.

Tip: listen to the first 30 minutes. Note how the speakers use words like "roughly," "it appears," and "the data suggests" when discussing uncertain outcomes.

YouTube
TED Talk -- Hans Rosling: "The best stats you've ever seen"

Hans Rosling's legendary presentation transforms dry global health data into a captivating story using visualization and storytelling. He demonstrates how to present numbers in a way that is engaging, persuasive, and clear to any audience. Direct connection to Lesson 2: how to "own the room" with data, make statistics accessible to non-technical stakeholders, and turn a data presentation into a compelling narrative.

Tip: watch the full talk (20 min). Notice how Rosling uses simple language to explain data, makes comparisons to benchmarks, and keeps the audience engaged. Practice repeating 3 of his key phrases about data trends.
