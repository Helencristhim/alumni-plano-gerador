Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Outperform -- to do better than a competitor or standard
"Our LATAM division outperformed all other regions in Q3."
Benchmark -- a standard or reference point for comparison
"We use industry benchmarks to measure our progress."
Conversion rate -- the percentage of leads that become customers
"Our conversion rate improved from 2.8% to 3.2% after the campaign."
Pipeline -- the total value of potential deals in progress
"We have a strong pipeline of $2.4 million for Q4."
Traction -- progress or momentum in growing a business
"The new product is gaining traction in the Brazilian market."
Headcount -- the total number of employees in a team or company
"We need to increase headcount by five to support the expansion."
Revenue stream -- a distinct source of income for a company
"Our main revenue stream comes from enterprise contracts."
Forecast -- a prediction of future performance or results
"The forecast for Q4 suggests a twelve percent revenue increase."
Variance -- the difference between expected and actual results
"The variance between forecast and actuals was due to the delayed campaign."
Alignment -- agreement among people toward a common goal
"We need alignment from all regional leads before the board meeting."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After Q3 results were released, the Growth Manager reviewed the pipeline and revenue stream data for the LATAM region. The conversion rate had dropped to 3.2%, below the benchmark of 4%. He analyzed the variance and concluded that the delayed campaign launch was the main factor. If the team had launched the campaign two weeks earlier, they would have hit the original target. The region's pipeline still grew by 32%, outperforming other divisions, but the headcount reduction had slowed progress. If they had not lost two team members, they would have outperformed every benchmark. The Growth Manager prepared a forecast for Q4, predicting a fifteen percent increase in revenue. He needed to make a compelling case to the VP Americas that LATAM was gaining traction and deserved additional investment. "These results are just the tip of the iceberg," he explained. If we had had the full team, we would have reached the five million milestone."

1. Why does the text say "If the team had launched the campaign earlier, they would have hit the target"?
A Because the team launched the campaign on time and hit the target.
B Because the team did NOT launch on time, and this is an imaginary past result.
C Because the team will launch the campaign in the future.
2. What is the function of the third conditional in business analysis?
A To describe actions that are happening right now.
B To make predictions about the future.
C To analyze what could have been different in the past and its imaginary result.
3. In the sentence "If they had not lost two team members, they would have outperformed every benchmark," what does the negative form indicate?
A They DID lose two members, and the sentence imagines the opposite scenario.
B They did not lose any members and everything went well.
C They are planning to hire two new members next quarter.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional

GRAMMAR

How to talk about imaginary past situations and their imaginary results.

PartStructureExample
IF clauseIf + subject + had + past participleIf we had launched earlier...
Result clausesubject + would have + past participle...we would have hit the target.
Negative IFIf + subject + had not + past participleIf we had not lost headcount...
Negative resultsubject + would not have + past participle...we would not have missed the target.
Key pointThe third conditional talks about the PAST. The situation is IMAGINARY. It did NOT happen. WOULD goes in the result, NEVER in the IF clause.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A VP asks why the conversion rate was below the benchmark. What do you say?
A "The numbers were bad because of problems."
B "The variance was primarily due to a delayed campaign launch. If we had launched on schedule, we would have hit the 4% benchmark."
C "It is not our fault. The market was difficult."
You need to present the pipeline growth diplomatically. What do you say?
A "The pipeline is definitely going to double next quarter."
B "I think the pipeline might maybe possibly grow."
C "Roughly speaking, the pipeline has grown by thirty-two percent, and it would appear that this trend will continue into Q4."

A "If we had not lost two team members, we would have outperformed every benchmark in the region."
