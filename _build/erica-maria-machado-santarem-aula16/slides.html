Chapter 3

The Future Code

I am going to... · Are you going to...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am going to relax."

"She is going to visit me."

"Are you going to go out?"

"I am not going to work."

TypeHow to talk about a future plan
PositiveI am / she is / we are going to + base verb — I am going to relax.
NegativeI am not going to + base verb — I am not going to work.
QuestionAre you going to + base verb? — Are you going to go out?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I going to go out."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to go out."

✗ WRONG

"I am going to relaxing."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to relax."

✗ WRONG

"You are going to travel?"

✓ RIGHT

"Are you going to travel?"

To talk about a future plan, use be (am, is, are) + going to + base verb: I am going to relax. For a question, put be first: Are you going to go out?
