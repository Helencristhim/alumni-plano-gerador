Bad News First -- Explaining a Delay

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Delay -- when something happens later than planned
"The migration is delayed by two weeks." (Atraso)
Heads-up -- an early warning, before the news is official
"I want to give you a heads-up." (Aviso antecipado)
Root cause -- the real reason behind a problem
"The root cause is the license approval." (Causa raiz)
Underestimate -- to think something is easier than it really is
"We underestimated the tests." (Subestimar)
Impact -- the effect of the problem on the plan
"The impact is two weeks on the go-live." (Impacto)
Apologize -- to say sorry in a formal, professional way
"I apologize for the delay." (Pedir desculpas)
Recovery plan -- your plan to fix it and win back time
"Here is my recovery plan." (Plano de recuperação)
Reschedule -- to move something to a new date
"We are going to reschedule the go-live." (Reagendar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo does not wait for the client to call. He calls first, and he gives a heads-up: "The migration is delayed by two weeks." Then he goes to the root cause: "We are late because the license approval took nine days. We also underestimated the tests, and that is why the date moved." Then the impact: "The go-live moves from June 20th to July 5th. Nothing else changes." He apologizes once -- only once -- and moves on: "I apologize for the delay. Here is my recovery plan. We are going to test in parallel, and we are going to reschedule the go-live to July 5th. I will send you the new plan by Friday." Cause, impact, plan. The client is not happy, but he trusts Diogo.

1. Why does Diogo call the client first?
A To give a heads-up before anyone else does.
B To ask the client for a new plan.
C To cancel the project.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A We are late because the approval took nine days.
B We are late because of the approval took nine days.
C We are late because of took nine days.
3. How many times does Diogo apologize?
A Once, and then he moves to the plan.
B Five times, in every sentence.
C Never.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cause, Result, Plan

GRAMMAR

Ligar causa e consequência, e apresentar o plano. / Linking cause and result, and presenting the plan.

MoveForma / FormExemplo / Example
1. O que aconteceu / What happenedpast simple + because + causaWe are late because the approval took nine days.
2. O que significa / What it meansso / that is why + resultadoThe license arrived late, so the go-live moves to July.
3. O que você vai fazer / What you will dobe going to / will + planoWe are going to test in parallel.
Ponto-chave / Key pointbecause + frase (We are late because the approval was slow); because of + substantivo (because of the approval). E prazo é by Friday, NUNCA "until Friday". Nunca pare no move 1: causa sem plano soa como desculpa.

Quiz

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a delay that really happened at Oracle. Call the person who needed to hear it, out loud, for 90 seconds. Three moves: (1) what happened and the root cause, (2) the impact and the new date, (3) your recovery plan. Record it.
Now the angry client. He asks: "Why should I trust your new date, if you missed the first one?" Answer out loud: answer first, reason second, plan third. Do not apologize more than once. Record it.
