Aula 16 -- The Trip That Changed Everything

Film
The Motorcycle Diaries (2004) -- directed by Walter Salles

A young man sets off across South America on an old motorcycle, expecting an adventure and coming home a different person. It is the purest example of tonight's lesson: a trip that becomes the catalyst for a whole life. If he had never taken that journey, he would not have become who he became. Watch how an ordinary road trip turns into a formative, life-changing experience. Connection to Lesson 16: it is a story you can retell in mixed conditionals ("If he hadn't gone, he wouldn't be...").

Tip: after the film, sum up the change in two mixed conditionals -- "If he hadn't travelled, he wouldn't be...", "If he weren't so curious, he would never have...".

Podcast
On Being with Krista Tippett -- any episode

Long, thoughtful conversations with people looking back on the moments that shaped who they became. It is the perfect companion to tonight's lesson: guest after guest reflects on the turning points, the wake-up calls and the experiences that set them on a path. You will hear natural, reflective English full of the language of change -- exactly what you practised tonight. Great ear training for a slower, more reflective register.

Tip: after an episode, describe the guest's turning point in two mixed conditionals -- "If they hadn't..., they wouldn't be...".

Grammar: Mixing conditionals -- BBC English Masterclass (BBC Learning English)

A clear, reliable BBC lesson on exactly tonight's grammar: how to mix conditionals to link a past cause with a present result, and a present truth with a past result. It puts the rules from your Grammar Tip into short, memorable examples -- the perfect warm-up before you tell your own story of a trip that changed you.

Tip: pause after each example and rebuild it as a sentence about your own life, one past-to-present and one present-to-past.
