Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At thirty-one, Marcelo almost did not take the trip that became the catalyst for everything. If he had listened to his doubts, he would not be the man he is today. The month abroad was a real eye-opener; it broadened his horizons and shaped the person he became. It was, without question, the most formative experience of his life, and in a way a wake-up call. If he weren't the kind of man who says yes, he would never have gone. When we link a past cause to a present result, English mixes two conditionals: If I had gone (past), I would be happier now (present). And when a present truth explains a past result: If I weren't so cautious (present), I would have gone (past).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Mixed Conditionals

GRAMMAR

Como ligar passado e presente em inglês (explicação em inglês e português).

Type / TipoIf-clauseResult
past → present
passado → presente		if + had + partícipio
If I had gone		would + verbo base
I would be happier now
present → past
presente → passado		if + past simple (were)
If I weren't so cautious		would have + partícipio
I would have gone

Atenção: nunca use would na if-clause: "if I had gone", nunca "if I would have gone". Use were (não "was") para um estado presente irreal: "if I weren't so cautious". E um resultado no passado sempre pede would have + partícipio, nunca "would" sozinho.

Stage 2: Put the Steps in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de montar uma mixed conditional na ordem correta.

?For a present result, use would + base verb (I would be happier now).
?Find the two times: a cause and a result, in the past or the present.
?For a past result, use would have + past participle (I would have gone).
?For a past cause, use 'if' + had + past participle (if I had gone).
?For a present truth, use 'if' + were (if I weren't so cautious).
