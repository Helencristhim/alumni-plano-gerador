Rick Steves' Europe — Lisbon (free, no subscription)

This is the city of your lesson, and of your next trip. For twenty-five minutes Rick Steves walks through Lisbon: the old back streets, the yellow trams, the warm custard tarts, the fado music at night, and a short trip to the royal castles of Sintra. He is American, he speaks slowly, and he never hurries. This is the street where your hotel is.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Every time Rick says a verb with -ing — "we are walking", "they are selling" — stop the video and say the sentence out loud. Then say one about you: "We are staying at the Hotel Lisboa for six nights."

Podcast
Adept English — Episode 866: This One Thing About Lisbon Stunned Me (A2-B1)

Hilary is an English teacher, and she visited Lisbon in May. Now she tells you everything she saw: the blue and white tiles on the walls, the seven hills, the viewpoints over the river, and the custard tarts. She speaks slowly and clearly, because she is speaking to students. The full text of the episode is free on the same page, under the audio.

Tip: listen once with your eyes closed, and do not read — your ear before your eyes. Then listen again with the free text open. After that, write three things you want to see, and say them out loud: "I am looking forward to seeing the tiles."

Oxford Online English — Hotel English: At the Front Desk (A2-B1)

Two teachers show you the hotel, moment by moment: giving your name at the front desk, asking about your reservation, taking your room key, and asking what time breakfast is. It is exactly the front desk of this lesson, with real people in a real hotel. The video has chapters, so you can go straight to the part you need and watch it again.

Tip: go to 4:39, "Checking In", and listen twice. Then stop the video and say your own words out loud: "Good morning. We have a reservation under the name Pinheiro de Oliveira. We are staying for six nights."
