Lesson 6 -- The Follow-Up: Scheduling a Meeting

Series
Emily in Paris -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Emily in Paris"

A marketing professional makes calls, sets up meetings, and follows up with new contacts in a new city. Perfect to hear people arrange a day and a time in simple, polite English.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two ways people suggest a meeting (Can we...? / Let us...). Then say out loud how you would arrange a meeting with Robert.

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Days of the Week and Telling the Time"

A slow, clear podcast on saying days and clock times in English -- exactly the words you need to confirm a meeting: on Monday, at ten in the morning, at two in the afternoon.

Repeat each day and time out loud. Then record a short message to a client: suggest a day, a time, and confirm with "Let us meet...".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Making Arrangements / Prepositions of Time: in, on, at"

A short, practical lesson on today's grammar: when to use on with days, at with clock times, and how to suggest a meeting politely with Can we...?

After watching, write five sentences to schedule a meeting with Robert. Use Can we...?, Let us..., on, and at at least once each, then read them aloud.
