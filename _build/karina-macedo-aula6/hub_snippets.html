The Follow-Up -- Scheduling a Meeting

After the aviation fair, follow up with a new contact and arrange a meeting. Suggest a day and a time politely with Can we...? and Let us..., and use on with days (on Tuesday) and at with clock times (at ten). New vocabulary: schedule, appointment, available, calendar, confirm, reply, attach.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Schedule -- to arrange a day and a time for something
"Can we schedule a meeting next week?" (Agendar)
Appointment -- a meeting arranged for a fixed day and time
"I have an appointment on Tuesday." (Compromisso)
Available -- free and ready to meet at a certain time
"I am available on Wednesday morning." (Disponível)
Calendar -- where you keep your days, meetings, and plans
"Let me check my calendar." (Agenda)
Confirm -- to say that something is certain and final
"I will confirm the day and the time." (Confirmar)
Reply -- to answer a message or an email
"Please reply to my email when you can." (Responder)
Attach -- to add a file or document to an email
"I will attach the price list." (Anexar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the aviation fair, Robert sent Karina a message. He wanted to schedule a meeting. Karina opened her calendar to find a free day. "Can we meet on Tuesday?" she wrote. "I am available at ten in the morning." Robert was happy. He asked her to reply to his email and confirm the day and the time. Karina also said she would attach the price list, so they could talk about the numbers in the meeting.

1. Which question suggests a meeting?
A "Can we meet on Tuesday?"
B "I am available."
C "I will attach the price list."
2. Why does Karina say "at ten in the morning" and not "on ten"?
A Because we use "at" with a clock time.
B Because we use "at" with a day.
C Because "on" and "at" are the same.
3. What does Robert ask Karina to do?
A Reply to his email and confirm the day and the time.
B Buy a new aircraft.
C Cancel the meeting.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Suggesting a Meeting (on / at)

GRAMMAR

Usado para sugerir uma reunião e marcar dia e hora. / Used to suggest a meeting and set a day and a time.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Can we + verb ...?Sugestão ou pedido educado / A polite suggestion or requestCan we meet on Tuesday?
Let us + verbPropor fazer algo juntos / Propose doing something togetherLet us meet online.
on + dayCom dias e datas / With days and datesI am available on Tuesday.
at + clock timeCom horas do relógio / With clock timesThe meeting is at ten.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use on com dias (on Monday) e at com horas (at 3 PM). Depois de "Can we" e "Let us", o verbo fica na forma base: Can we meet, não "Can we to meet".
