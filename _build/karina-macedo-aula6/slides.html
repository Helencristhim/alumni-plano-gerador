Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Can we...? -- Let us... -- on a day -- at a time

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we suggest a day and a time?

"Can we meet on Tuesday?"

"Let us meet at ten in the morning."

"I am available on Wednesday."

"The meeting is at two in the afternoon."

Grammar Rule

Suggest a Day & a Time

StructureWhen we use it
Can we + verb ...? (Can we meet?)A polite suggestion or request
Let us + verb (Let us meet)To propose doing something together
on + day (on Monday)With days and dates
at + clock time (at 3 PM)With clock times

Key idea: after Can we and Let us, the verb stays in the base form (meet, not to meet). Use on with a day and at with a clock time.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Can we meet in Monday on three?"
"Can we meet on Monday at three?"

Use on with a day (on Monday) and at with a clock time (at three). Never "in Monday" or "on three".
