The Rule

I Have a... / My ... Hurts

UsePatternExample
An illnessI have a + wordI have a headache.
A body partMy + part + hurtsMy stomach hurts.
Ask for helpCan you help me?Can you help me, please?

Key idea: say I have a + headache / fever / stomachache for an illness, and My + body part + hurts for pain. To ask for help, say Can you help me?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am headache."
"I have a headache."

Use have a for an illness: "I have a headache", "I have a fever". Never "I am headache".

Key Phrase

The Help Phrase

"I feel sick. Can you help me?"

Then say what is wrong: "I have a headache" or "My stomach hurts."
