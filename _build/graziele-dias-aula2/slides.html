Chapter 3: The Code

I work · she works

Present simple: do and don't

Discover the Grammar

Listen and Notice

"I work with many teams."

"She works in cardiology."

"We work in the same office."

"I don't work in cardiology."

SubjectVerb (+)Negative (-)
I / you / we / theyworkdo not work (don't)
he / she / itworksdoes not work (doesn't)

He/she/it add -s. The negative uses don't or doesn't + verb. / Ele/ela usa works; no negativo, don't / doesn't.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"She work in cardiology."

✓ RIGHT

"She works in cardiology."

✗ WRONG

"I doesn't work here."

✓ RIGHT

"I don't work here."

Good morning, Graziele. Can I ask about your role here?

Of course. I am a coordinator. I work with many medical teams.
C
Interesting. Do you work in one department?
G
No, I don't work in one department. I work with many departments.
C
And who works with you?
G
My colleague Ana works with me. She manages the office.
C
Does she work in cardiology too?

No, she doesn't. She works with the surgery team.
