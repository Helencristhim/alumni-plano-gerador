Aula 13 -- Pre-class

Presenting Your Company at a Trade Fair

How to pitch your company and describe it with relative clauses. Key words: to showcase, elevator pitch, to stand out, flagship, track record, selling point, to specialize in, cutting-edge, established, brochure, prospect, turnover. Structure: relative clauses (who / which / that / where; defining vs non-defining).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To showcase -- to present something proudly to an audience (mostrar, exibir com orgulho)
"At the fair, we showcase our best destinations."
Elevator pitch -- a short, persuasive summary of your business (discurso rapido de venda)
"Her elevator pitch lasts thirty seconds."
To stand out -- to be clearly better or different from others (destacar-se)
"Great service is what makes us stand out."
Flagship -- the most important, best product or brand (carro-chefe)
"Patagonia is our flagship destination."
Track record -- a history of past results and achievements (historico de resultados)
"We have a strong track record with groups."
Selling point -- a feature that makes people want to choose you (diferencial, ponto forte)
"Flexibility is our main selling point."
To specialize in -- to focus on one particular area (especializar-se em)
"We specialize in luxury group travel."
Cutting-edge -- very modern and advanced (de ponta, de ultima geracao)
"We use cutting-edge booking technology."
Established -- existing for a long time and well respected (consolidado, estabelecido)
"We are a well-established agency."
Brochure -- a printed booklet with information about a company (folheto, catalogo)
"Take a brochure from our stand."
Prospect -- a possible future client (cliente em potencial)
"Every prospect who visits gets a demo."
Turnover -- the total amount of business a company does in a period (faturamento)
"Our turnover grew last year."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the fair, a prospect stops at Andrea's stand. 'We are an agency that specializes in luxury group travel,' she says. 'Our flagship, which is Patagonia, stands out every season.' She points to the cutting-edge platform where clients compare packages in real time. 'We are a well-established company that has a strong track record,' she adds, handing him a brochure. Every prospect who visits gets a demo -- and that is exactly why Escape Turismo's turnover keeps growing.

1. 'An agency that specializes in travel' -- 'that' refers to:
A a thing (the agency).
B a place.
C a person.
2. Which sentence uses commas correctly (non-defining clause)?
A Patagonia which is our flagship sells out.
B Patagonia, which is our flagship, sells out.
C Patagonia, that is our flagship, sells out.
3. Which relative word describes a place?
A who
B which
C where

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Como descrever pessoas, coisas e lugares numa mesma frase (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
who / that (pessoas)para pessoas; info essencial, sem virgulas. for people.The team that runs our groups.
which / that (coisas)para coisas; info essencial, sem virgulas. for things.A package that includes flights.
where (lugares)para lugares. for places.The region where we specialize.
Non-defining / info extracom virgulas; nunca use 'that'. commas, never 'that'.Patagonia, which is our flagship, sells out.

Quiz

You describe your flagship with extra information. You say:
