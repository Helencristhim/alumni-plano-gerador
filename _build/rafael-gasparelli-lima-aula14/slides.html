Chapter 3: The Irregular Code

Past Simple: Irregular Verbs

No -ed here. These verbs change their shape.

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"The firm grew slowly in the first years."

"It became a respected firm."

"We did not win the first time."

"Did the firm buy a new office?"

BasePast (irregular)Example
growgrewThe firm grew fast.
becomebecameIt became a leader.
buildbuiltWe built a strong team.
buyboughtThey bought an office.
winwonWe won the award.

Irregular verbs do not add -ed. Each one changes its form and you must learn it. But after did and did not, the verb goes back to its base form: Did the firm grow? (not Did the firm grew?)

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"The company growed fast."

✓ RIGHT

"The company grew fast."

✗ WRONG

"Did the firm bought the office?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did the firm buy the office?"

Irregular verbs never take -ed. And after did or did not, the verb returns to its base form.

Dialogue

The Reporter's Interview

J
Good morning, Rafael. For our article, can you tell me how the firm started?
R
Of course. In 1998, two lawyers founded the firm with a single desk. In the first years, it grew slowly.
J
And when did the firm become bigger?
R
In 2005, we bought a new office and took over two local competitors. We built new departments and won our first award.
J
Impressive. And the recent merger, how did it change the company?
R
After the merger, the firm became one of the largest in the region. We opened offices in three countries.
