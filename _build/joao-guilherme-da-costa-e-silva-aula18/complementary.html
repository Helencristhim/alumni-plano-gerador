Article
ArgonDigital — "Using the Correct Requirements Terms: Shall, Will, Should" (~8 min read)

The professional version of tonight's lesson, written for the people who draft specifications for a living. It shows exactly why the modal is the contract: shall states a mandatory, verifiable requirement; should states a goal you can depart from; will states a fact or intent, not an obligation. This is the discipline behind the conformance statement you had to sign — every word carries a different level of obligation, and mixing them up is one of the most expensive small mistakes in engineering.

Tip: take a real clause from a Motiva or client specification and label its modal — shall, should, may, or will. If any line is ambiguous, rewrite it so the force is unmistakable.
