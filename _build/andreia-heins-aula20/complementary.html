Lesson 20 -- Block 2 Review: Leading a Supplier Meeting

Series
The Office (US) -- any conference-room meeting episode

Watch how a meeting is opened, how the chair runs through the agenda, invites people to take the floor, and tries to reach a decision. A fun model of what a chair should -- and should not -- do.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two phrases the chair uses to open or close the meeting. Notice what happens when nobody is on the same page or when an item should have been tabled.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run a Meeting People Want to Attend"

The hosts explain how a good chair leads a meeting: open with a clear agenda, keep it on time, decide with the group, and close with clear next steps and one owner per task.

Note three tips for chairing a meeting. Then record a one-minute meeting opening and closing using at least four new words from today, such as chair, run through, next steps and takeaway.

YouTube
Business English -- "How to Chair a Meeting in English"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to open, run through the agenda, invite people to take the floor, take a show of hands, table an item, and close by agreeing the next steps and adjourning.

After watching, write five sentences for a real meeting at work: one to open, one to invite someone to take the floor, one to take a show of hands, one to table an item, and one to close with the next steps. Then say them out loud.
