Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Chair -- to lead a meeting: open it, guide the discussion, and keep it on time
"I will chair today's meeting. Let us begin." (Presidir / conduzir)
Run through -- to go over the agenda points quickly, one by one
"Let me run through the agenda before we start." (Revisar / passar por)
Take the floor -- to start speaking to the whole group, with everyone's attention
"Maria, would you like to take the floor?" (Tomar a palavra)
On the same page -- when everyone agrees and understands the same thing
"Good. I think we are all on the same page now." (Alinhados / de acordo)
Show of hands -- a quick, informal vote where people raise their hands
"Let us take a show of hands: who agrees with the plan?" (Votação de mãos)
Table -- to postpone an item and discuss it at a later meeting
"We are short on time, so let us table this point for next week." (Adiar)
Next steps -- the actions people will take after the meeting ends
"Before we close, let us agree on the next steps." (Próximos passos)
Sign off -- to give your final approval to a plan or decision
"Once everyone agrees, I will sign off on the budget." (Aprovar / dar o aval)
Adjourn -- to formally close a meeting, usually until a later date
"There is nothing else, so let us adjourn the meeting." (Encerrar)
Takeaway -- the most important point you remember from a meeting
"The main takeaway is that we approved the second console." (Ponto principal)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia will chair the supplier meeting. She opens and runs through the agenda so everyone is on the same page. A good chair uses all the Block 2 language at the right stage. To discuss options, she uses the second conditional: "If we ordered two consoles, we would save time." To give feedback on the past, she uses a past modal: "We could have planned this earlier." When it is time to decide, she takes a show of hands; if there is no time for one point, she will table it. Once everyone agrees, she will sign off on the plan. To close, she gives the main takeaway, agrees the next steps with a polite request -- "Could you send the summary?" -- and then she will adjourn the meeting.

1. Andreia says "If we ordered two consoles, we would save time." Which structure is this?
A The second conditional -- to discuss a possible option.
B A past modal -- to give feedback about the past.
C A simple order -- to tell people what to do.
2. There is no time for one agenda point. What does a good chair do?
A Adjourn the whole meeting at once.
B Table the point for a later meeting and keep on time.
C Sign off on it without any discussion.
3. How does Andreia close the meeting?
A She gives the takeaway, agrees the next steps with a polite request, and adjourns.
B She stops talking and leaves with no summary.
C She starts a brand-new agenda from the beginning.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Chair's Flow

GRAMMAR

Como conduzir uma reunião usando toda a linguagem do Bloco 2 em cada etapa. / How to chair a meeting using all the Block 2 language at each stage.

Etapa / StageEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Open / Abrirpergunta / conviteShall we start? Let me run through the agenda.
Discuss / Discutirfirst / second conditionalIf we ordered two, we would save time.
Feedbackpast modal (could/should have)We could have planned it earlier.
Close / Fecharpedido educado + next stepsCould you send the next steps? Let us adjourn.

Atenção / Note: Um bom chair conduz a reunião em quatro etapas, usando a estrutura certa em cada uma: abrir com pergunta/convite; discutir opções com a primeira ou segunda condicional; dar feedback com past modal; e fechar com pedido educado e os próximos passos. Esta aula revisa TODA a linguagem do Bloco 2 numa única reunião real. / A good chair leads the meeting through four stages, using the right structure at each one.
