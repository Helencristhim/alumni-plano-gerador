Lesson 18 -- Making Plans: Social Invitations

Series / Social
Friends -- Season 1: any episode with a party or get-together

The friends are always inviting each other, accepting and turning down plans, and arranging where to meet. A perfect, natural source of social invitation language.

Watch with English subtitles. Note every invitation you hear, then write two of your own with How about and Why don't we.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "Making Plans in English: How to Invite, Accept and Decline"

A short, practical episode on inviting friends, suggesting plans, and saying yes or no politely. You will hear How about and Why don't we used in real conversations.

After listening, record a one-minute voice note inviting a friend to a get-together. Use How about, Why don't we, and at least four new words.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Making Suggestions: How about, Why don't we, Let's"

A clear video on the structures for suggesting plans, including How about + -ing and Why don't we + base verb. Exactly today's grammar focus.

After watching, write four invitations: two with How about and two with Why don't we. Then write a polite way to accept one and turn down another.
