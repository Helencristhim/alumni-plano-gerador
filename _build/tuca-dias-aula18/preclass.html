Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When you want to invite a friend or suggest a plan, two phrases sound friendly and natural. With How about, use the verb with -ing: "How about meeting for coffee on Saturday?" You can also use a noun: "How about dinner on Friday?" With Why don't we, use the base verb, with no -ing and no "to": "Why don't we have a small get-together?" Both are softer than a command, and easy to use with friends. To answer, you can accept warmly ("That sounds great, I'd love to!") or politely turn it down ("I'd love to, but I'm not available that day."). These phrases help you make plans and arrange a get-together.

B How about meeting for coffee on Saturday?

2. Which sentence uses Why don't we correctly?

3. You are busy and cannot go. How do you politely say no?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- How about & Why don't we

GRAMMAR

Duas formas amigáveis de sugerir um plano e convidar alguém. / Two friendly ways to suggest a plan and invite someone.

Structure / EstruturaExampleNote / Nota
How about + verbo-ingHow about meeting for coffee?o verbo recebe -ing / verb takes -ing
How about + substantivoHow about dinner on Friday?também aceita um substantivo / a noun also works
Why don't we + verbo baseWhy don't we meet at the cafe?verbo base: sem -ing, sem "to" / base verb
respostas / answers"That sounds great!" / "I'd love to, but..."aceitar ou recusar com gentileza / accept or turn down politely

Key point / Ponto-chave: as duas frases são jeitos amigáveis de sugerir algo junto. Depois de How about, use o verbo com -ing ("How about meeting") ou um substantivo ("How about dinner"). Depois de Why don't we, use o verbo base, sem -ing e sem "to" ("Why don't we meet"). Para responder, você accept (aceita) ou faz o turn down (recusa) com gentileza. / After How about, use verb-ing; after Why don't we, use the base verb.
