Lesson 13 -- Handling Q&A: Tough Questions Under Pressure

Series
The Newsroom (HBO) -- any press-conference or on-air Q&A scene, or a White House press briefing on YouTube

Watch a spokesperson field hostile, loaded questions live. Notice how the strong ones buy a second, clarify what is really being asked, hedge what they cannot confirm, and defer cleanly — while the weak ones answer off the cuff and overcommit.

Tip: pause after each tough question and decide which tool you would use — buy time, clarify, hedge, or defer — before they answer.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- any episode on handling tough questions, executive presence, or thinking on your feet

Harvard Business Review experts break down how leaders stay calm under pressure: how to read a loaded question, hedge honestly, and buy a moment to think. Exactly the toolkit from this class, in real executive voices — perfect prep before Victor faces his own board Q&A.

Tip: write down one go-to phrase for each tool — buy time, clarify, hedge, defer — that you would actually say at Brink's.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review / TED -- "How to Answer Tough Questions" or media-training breakdowns

A short, practical guide to handling hard questions: how to buy time without stalling, ask clarifying questions, and use qualifiers so you never overcommit. A clear bridge between presenting (Block 3) and fielding the questions that follow.

Tip: watch with a notebook and draft one indirect clarifying question and one hedged answer for a real ask you expect at work.
