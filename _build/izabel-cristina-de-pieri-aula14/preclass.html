Aula 14 -- Pre-class

The Case for the Deal -- Making an Argument with Emphasis

Reading a written business case for the Nordic Sense deal and separating fact from opinion. Key words: compelling, merits, objection, counterargument, caveat, assertion, substantiate, bias, rebut, the bottom line, watertight, at face value. Structure: cleft sentences and emphasis -- it-cleft (It was X that ...), what-cleft (What ... is X), and "The reason ... is that ...".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Compelling -- so convincing that it demands attention (convincente / persuasivo)
"What makes the proposal compelling is speed."
Merits -- the strong points or advantages of something (méritos / pontos fortes)
"The merits are clear, but the case must answer the objections."
Objection -- a reason someone gives for disagreeing (objeção)
"The first objection is price, and that concern is fair."
Counterargument -- a point made against another argument (contra-argumento)
"One counterargument deserves a direct rebuttal."
Caveat -- a warning or limiting condition attached to something (ressalva)
"A second caveat is integration between the two teams."
Assertion -- a confident statement presented as true (afirmação / asserção)
"That assertion rests on a bias toward size."
Substantiate -- to support a claim with solid evidence (comprovar / fundamentar)
"An independent audit will substantiate every number."
Bias -- a tendency that unfairly distorts judgment (viés)
"The claim rests on a bias toward size."
Rebut -- to argue against a point and disprove it (refutar / rebater)
"I would rebut that assertion with our track record."
The bottom line -- the single most important fact or result (o essencial / o ponto principal)
"The bottom line is that we would gain a full year."
Watertight -- so strong that it has no weakness (irrefutável / à prova de falhas)
"Read fairly, the case is close to watertight."
At face value -- accepting something exactly as it appears, without checking (pelo valor aparente / ao pé da letra)
"We should not take their figures at face value."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Izabel writes the memo. "What makes the case compelling is speed," she begins. "It is their proprietary technology that puts us in the medical market a year sooner." She does not hide the objections. "The main one is price," she admits, "so we will substantiate every number and not take their figures at face value." When a director assumes a rival will copy the design, she rebuts it: "What the record shows is the opposite. It was reliability, not scale, that won our clients." She ends with the recommendation: "The reason we back the deal is that the upside is real and the downside is manageable."

1. "It is their proprietary technology that puts us in the market sooner." What is being emphasized?
A Their proprietary technology (the it-cleft puts it in the spotlight).
B The medical market.
C The word "sooner".
2. "What the record shows is the opposite." Why "What ... is"?
A It is a real question.
B It is a what-cleft: it emphasizes what comes after "is".
C Because "record" is plural.
3. "The reason we back the deal is that the upside is real." Which word is correct after "reason"?
A "is that" (the reason ... is that ...).
B "is because" (the reason ... is because ...).
C "is why".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences & Emphasis

GRAMMAR

Putting one idea in the spotlight (explicação em inglês e português).

Structure / EstruturaForm / FormaExample
It-cleft
ênfase com "It"		It + be + X + that / whoIt was the lead time that won it.
What-cleft
ênfase com "What"		What + clause + be + XWhat won it was the lead time.
The reason ... is thatThe reason + clause + is thatThe reason we back it is that it saves a year.
Cuidado / Watch outComece a it-cleft com It (errado: "Was the lead time that..."). Na what-cleft não ponha um it extra antes de "was" (errado: "What convinced them it was..."). Depois de "The reason ..." use is that, nunca "is because".
