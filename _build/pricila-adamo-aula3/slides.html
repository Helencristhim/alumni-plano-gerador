Chapter 3: By This Time Next Year

What Will You Be Doing?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"This time next year, I will be travelling."

"By December, I will have finished the course."

"At noon tomorrow, we will be flying to Lisbon."

"By next spring, she will have walked the trail."

TenseUseExample
Future continuousin progress at a future timeI will be travelling next week.
Future perfectfinished before a future timeBy June, I will have finished.

Forms: will be + -ing (in progress) and will have + past participle (completed). Use signpost words like this time next year and by December.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

At 9 tomorrow, I will travel to Rome.
At 9 tomorrow, I will be travelling to Rome.

Use the future continuous (will be + -ing) for an action in progress at a future moment.

By June, I will have went abroad.
By June, I will have gone abroad.

After will have, always use the past participle (gone, not went).
