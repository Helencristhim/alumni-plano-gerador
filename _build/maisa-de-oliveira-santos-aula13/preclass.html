Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Maísa reports the numbers to David. "Sales increased last month," she says. "They went up by ten percent. Our profits grew, and they are higher than last year." David asks about the costs. "Costs decreased in March," Maísa answers. "They are lower now." David is happy: "So sales rose and costs fell. That is a very good result." But one number fell fast. "Can we look at the trend together?" Maísa asks. The general trend is positive, but she wants to check that one number with David.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Increase / Decrease & Higher / Lower

GRAMMAR

Para dizer que um número mudou no passado, use increased / went up (subiu) ou decreased / went down (caiu). Para comparar, use higher than (mais alto que) ou lower than (mais baixo que). / To say a number changed, use increased / went up or decreased / went down. To compare, use higher than or lower than.

Estrutura / StructureUso / UseExemplo / Example
increased / went upsubiu / went up (past)Sales increased. / It went up.
decreased / went downcaiu / went down (past)Costs decreased. / It went down.
higher thancomparar: mais / moreProfits are higher than last year.
lower thancomparar: menos / lessCosts are lower than before.

Atenção: increase já significa "subir", então nunca diga "increased up". E com palavras terminadas em -er (higher, lower) nunca use "more" — diga "higher", nunca "more higher". Compare sempre com than, nunca "that": Profits are higher than last year. Passado irregular: rise → rose, fall → fell, grow → grew, go → went.
