Chapter 3: The Code

Went Up, Went Down

the past, and higher / lower

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Sales increased last month."

"Costs decreased in March."

"Sales went up by ten percent."

"Profits are higher than last year."

StructureUse forExample
increased / went upa number went up (past)Sales increased. / It went up.
decreased / went downa number went down (past)Costs decreased. / It went down.
higher thancompare: moreProfits are higher than last year.
lower thancompare: lessCosts are lower than before.

Increased and went up mean the same: the number is bigger. Decreased and went down mean the number is smaller. To compare, use higher than or lower than — never say "more higher".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Sales increased up.
Sales increased.
Profits are more higher.
Profits are higher.

Increase already means "go up", so do not add "up" after it. And with -er words like higher and lower, never use more — say "higher", not "more higher".

Chapter 4: Reading the Numbers

Reporting to David

the month, up and down

Dialogue

The Numbers This Month

M
David, here are the numbers for last month. Sales increased a lot.
D
That is great news, Maísa. How much did they go up?
M
They went up by ten percent. Our profits grew and are higher than last year.
D
Excellent. And what about our costs?
M
Good question. Costs decreased in March. They are lower now.
D
So sales rose and costs fell. That is a very good result.
M
Yes. But one number dropped fast. Can we look at the trend together?
D
Of course. Let me check it with you now.
