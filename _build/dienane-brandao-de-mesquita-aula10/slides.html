Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Saying what you like and love doing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes after like and love?

"I like reading books."

"She loves cooking for her family."

"We like dancing on weekends."

"I don’t like swimming in cold water."

Grammar Rule

Like and Love + -ing

FormStructureExample
Affirmativelike / love + verb + -ingI like reading.
Negativedon’t like + verb + -ingI don’t like running.
QuestionDo + subject + like + verb + -ing?Do you like dancing?

Key idea: after like and love, the verb takes -ing: read → reading, cook → cooking, dance → dancing, swim → swimming. We do not say "to": "I like reading", not "I like to reading".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I like to reading books."
"I like reading books."

After like and love, the verb takes the -ing form. Never use "to + -ing".
