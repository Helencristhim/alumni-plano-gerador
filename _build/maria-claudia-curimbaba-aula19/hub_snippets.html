"Building the relationship first is a Brazilian custom." (Costume)

"Keep your warm mindset and add a little US punctuality." (Mentalidade)

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before her trip to Houston, Maria Claudia studies the local custom. US meetings value punctuality and directness: they start exactly on time and state the point early, whereas Brazilian meetings often build rapport first with a little small talk. Unlike a traditional firm, the Houston office has a flat hierarchy, so a junior may openly disagree with a director. The email etiquette is informal in tone but precise, which is different from the more formal Brazilian opening -- notice that the preposition is always "from", never "of" or "than". The American sense of humor is similar to hers, while the sense of personal space is not. Her plan is wise: keep her warm mindset and her custom of building rapport, but adapt to their punctuality and respect their etiquette, from a firm handshake to the right gesture when she disagrees.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparing & Contrasting

GRAMMAR

Para comparar duas culturas, use conectores e preposições certas. WHEREAS e WHILE + oração contrastam duas ideias completas (mesmo sentido; whereas é um pouco mais formal). UNLIKE + substantivo marca uma diferença clara e oposta. Para comparações mais suaves, SIMILAR TO + substantivo (parecido) e DIFFERENT FROM + substantivo (diferente). Atenção à preposição: é sempre different FROM, nunca "of" nem "than". / To compare two cultures, use the right connectors and prepositions. WHEREAS and WHILE + clause contrast two full ideas (same meaning; whereas is more formal). UNLIKE + noun marks a clear, opposite difference. For softer comparisons, SIMILAR TO + noun and DIFFERENT FROM + noun. The preposition is always different FROM -- never "of" or "than".

FormUseExample
whereas + clausecontrast two ideas (formal)"we warm up, whereas they start on time"
while + clausesame meaning, softer"they are direct, while we build rapport"
unlike + nouna clear, opposite difference"unlike Brazil, the hierarchy is flat"
similar to + nounsofter: alike"their humor is similar to ours"
different from + nounsofter: not alike (always from)"the etiquette is different from ours"

Key point / Ponto-chave: whereas/while ligam duas orações; unlike vem antes de substantivo. A preposição é sempre "different from": "their food is different of" → "their food is different from ours". / whereas/while join two clauses; unlike takes a noun. The preposition is always "different from", never "of" or "than".

Stage 2: Compare Cultures in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of comparing cultures fairly in the correct order.

?You mark a strong contrast with unlike, when one culture does the opposite of the other.
?You observe the local custom before you judge it, so you compare fairly.
?You adapt your own mindset where it helps, while you keep your own strengths, such as warmth.
?You name one clear difference using whereas or while, such as punctuality.
?You soften the comparison with similar to and different from, because most differences are partial.
