Chapter 3 -- The Code

Language Discovery

whereas / while / unlike / similar to / different from

Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. Which join two ideas, which takes a noun, and which preposition follows "different"?

"US meetings start on time, whereas Brazilian meetings warm up first."

"Americans value directness, while Brazilians often build rapport first."

"Unlike a traditional company, this firm has a flat hierarchy."

"Their humor is similar to ours, but personal space is different from ours."

The Rule

Whereas / While / Unlike

FormUseExample
whereas + clausecontrast two full ideas (more formal)"we warm up, whereas they start on time"
while + clausesame meaning, a little softer"they are direct, while we build rapport"
unlike + nouna clear, opposite difference"unlike Brazil, the hierarchy is flat"
similar to + nounsofter comparison: alike"their humor is similar to ours"
different from + nounsofter comparison: not alike (always from)"the etiquette is different from ours"

Key idea: use whereas and while to contrast two full ideas (a comma joins them; whereas is a little more formal). Use unlike before a noun for a clear, opposite difference. For softer comparisons use similar to and different from -- and remember the preposition is always from, never "of" or "than".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Their etiquette is different of ours." / "Their etiquette is different than ours."
"Their etiquette is different from ours."

The preposition is always different from -- never "of" or "than". And unlike takes a noun ("unlike Brazil"), not a full clause.

Practice

"_____Unlike Brazil, this firm has a flat hierarchy." (+ noun)
"Their sense of humor is similar _____to ours." (similar + ?)
"The personal space is different _____from ours." (different + ?)
