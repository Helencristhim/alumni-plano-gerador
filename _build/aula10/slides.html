Chapter 4: In Context

The Board Debate

Eduardo chairs; a board member pushes back

E
Let's move on to the Agency X deal. In my view, if we close it, we'll open the region.
S
I have an objection. The 3% rate is below our target.
E
That's a valid point. But if we lowered it to 3.5% and dropped exclusivity, we'd meet halfway.
S
And the risk? Switching is a concern for the board.
E
I understand your concern. Let me reassure you: a free pilot makes it a win-win.
S
Fair. Could we have done this sooner?
E
Honestly, if we had moved last quarter, we would have closed it already. Let's not wait again.
E
So we're on the same page. Let's wrap up. To recap: approve the deal, send the minutes, confirm by Friday.

Chapter 5: Practice

Run the Board Move by Move

Open and set the agenda.
"Thanks for joining. Let's get started. First, results; then, the deal; finally, next steps."
Give an opinion with a first conditional.
"In my view, if we reinvest now, we'll keep the momentum."
Disagree and find common ground.
"I see your point, but if we wait, we'll lose the window. Let's find common ground."
Negotiate with a second conditional.
"If we lowered the rate to 3.5% and dropped exclusivity, we'd meet them halfway."
Handle an objection.
"I understand your concern about the risk. That said, a free pilot makes it a win-win."
Close with action items and a third conditional.
"To recap, I'll send the minutes. If we'd moved earlier, we would have closed two more deals. Let's not repeat that. Let's wrap up."
