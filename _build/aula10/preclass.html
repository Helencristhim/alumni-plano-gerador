Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Review

Vocabulary

The key words from Block 2. Review the meaning and click Listen to check your pronunciation.

Agenda -- the list of topics to be discussed in a meeting
"The first item on the agenda is the quarterly results."
Action Item -- a specific task a person must complete after the meeting
"Your action item is to confirm the contract by Friday."
Common Ground -- shared opinions or interests that people agree on
"Let's find common ground on the rate."
Counteroffer -- a return offer made in response to another offer
"Their counteroffer was three percent."
Meet Halfway -- to compromise; each side gives up a little to agree
"Let's meet halfway at 3.5%."
Objection -- a reason someone gives against a proposal
"The board's main objection was the risk."
Reassure -- to remove someone's doubts and make them feel confident
"Let me reassure the board: we'll pilot it first."
Win-Win -- a result where both sides benefit
"A free pilot makes it a win-win for everyone."

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Your board meeting uses all four Block 2 tools. Keep this table in mind.

ToolUse it forExample
Imperatives + sequencingChairing the meeting"Let's start. First... then... finally..."
First conditionalReal future result"If we close it, we'll grow."
Second conditionalHypothetical trade"If we lowered it, we'd meet halfway."
Third conditionalImagined past (debrief)"If we had moved, we would have closed it."

Watch out: never put "will" or "would" after "if". First: "if we close..."; second: "if we lowered..."; third: "if we had moved...".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz
You're chairing. How do you open and set the agenda?
A "Thanks for joining. Let's get started. First, results; then, the deal; finally, next steps."
B "So... maybe we talk now?"
C "Let's to begin the points."
A board member objects to the rate. Best response?
A "You don't understand the market."
B "That's a valid point. If we lowered it and dropped exclusivity, we'd meet them halfway."
C "If we would lower it, we win."
Closing the meeting after a missed chance, which is correct?
A "If we had moved sooner, we would have closed it. Let's not wait again. Let's wrap up."
B "If we would have moved, we close it."
C "Okay, finished, bye."

Stage 5: Run the Board Meeting

Reflection
Plan and record a 90-second board meeting: open and set a three-item agenda, present one result, give an opinion with a first conditional, propose a negotiated trade with a second conditional, handle one objection, and close with action items plus a third-conditional reflection.
Model: "Thanks for joining. Let's get started. First, results; then, the deal; finally, next steps. We've grown 40% this quarter. In my view, if we close the Agency X deal, we'll open the region. I hear the concern about the rate. If we lowered it to 3.5% and dropped exclusivity, we'd meet them halfway. A free pilot makes it a win-win. Honestly, if we'd moved last quarter, we would have closed it already. To recap: approve the deal, send the minutes, confirm by Friday. Let's wrap up."
