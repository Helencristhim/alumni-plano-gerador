Lesson 7 -- Saying the Unsaid

Series
Succession -- Season 2, Episode 5: "Tern Haven"

A high-stakes corporate meeting where power dynamics, emphasis, and unspoken tensions drive every sentence. Watch how characters use dramatic emphasis and indirect language to communicate what everyone is thinking but nobody says.

Note 3 moments where a character emphasizes a point by restructuring the sentence. How does word order change the impact?

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run Effective Post-Mortems"

A 25-minute episode on conducting productive reviews after failures. Listen for how leaders frame problems with emphasis, acknowledge mistakes, and set expectations for transparency.

Write down 3 phrases the speaker uses to emphasize key lessons. Try rewriting them with fronting and inversion from today's lesson.

YouTube
TED Talk -- "The Power of Vulnerability" (Brene Brown)

A 20-minute talk on how vulnerability and transparency build stronger relationships. Brene Brown uses emphatic language to drive her points home. Applicable to how you communicate difficult truths in professional settings.

Note how Brene Brown uses sentence structure for emphasis. After watching, practice opening a meeting about a failure using at least 3 inverted sentences.
