Lesson 7 -- Saying the Unsaid

Series
Succession -- Season 2, Episode 5: "Tern Haven"

A cross-functional, high-stakes meeting where almost nothing important is said directly. The real message lives in the subtext, the pushback, and the elephant in the room nobody names. A masterclass in reading the unsaid.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two moments where a character downplays or sugarcoats something. Then rewrite one line as a direct sentence, and one as an emphatic inversion ("Never have I...", "Not only... but also...").

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- "How to Disagree Productively"

Practical advice on surfacing tacit tension in meetings, handling pushback without defensiveness, and naming the elephant in the room instead of glossing over it. Listen for how skilled leaders read between the lines.

Note 3 phrases the host uses to emphasize a key point. Compare them with how Klaus and Fabiana framed their concerns in today's lesson, then say one out loud as an inversion.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Run a Cross-Functional Meeting"

A short, practical video on aligning product, engineering, and operations when interests collide. Covers how to read the room, handle unspoken resistance, and emphasize what matters most -- directly applicable to your meetings at Bloomberg.

After watching, record a 60-second meeting opening. Use at least three new words and three inversions -- two negative-adverbial ("Rarely does...", "Never have I...") and one "Not only... but also...".
