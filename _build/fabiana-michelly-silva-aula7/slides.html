Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Fronting and inversion for emphasis (Never have I... / Not only... but also...)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What happens to the verb after the first word?

"Never have I seen such pushback in a meeting."

"Not only did we slip, but we also lost trust."

"Rarely does engineering push back this hard."

"Only after the demo did they finally agree."

Grammar Rule

Fronting & Inversion

StructureUse
Never / Rarely / Seldom + aux + subject (never have I seen...)Emphasizes something rare or extreme, formally
Not only + aux + subject ... but ... also (not only did we slip...)Adds a second, heavier point with weight
Only after / when / Not until + clause + aux + subjectMarks the one moment something finally happens
Little + aux + subject (little did we know...)Shows surprise: no one expected it

Key idea: after a fronted negative adverbial, the auxiliary (have, did, do, is) comes BEFORE the subject, exactly like a question. Use it for one strong point, not every sentence.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Never I have seen such pushback."
"Never have I seen such pushback."

After a fronted negative adverbial, invert: the auxiliary comes before the subject. Same for Not only: Not only did we miss it, not "Not only we missed it".
