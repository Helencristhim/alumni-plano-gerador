Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Scrutiny -- close, critical examination of something
"Our team has never faced this level of scrutiny." (Escrutínio)
Transparency -- openness and honesty in communication
"We need radical transparency going forward." (Transparência)
Credibility -- the trust and respect others have for you
"We lost credibility with the Tokyo office." (Credibilidade)
Disconnect -- a gap between things that should be connected
"There was a disconnect between what was promised and delivered." (Desconexão)
Withhold -- to keep back or hide information
"At no point should anyone withhold information." (Reter)
Deflect -- to redirect blame or attention away
"Nobody tried to deflect blame during the meeting." (Desviar)
Acknowledge -- to recognize or admit something is true
"Only after the complaint did we acknowledge the problem." (Reconhecer)
Scope -- the range or extent of something
"Had I known the full scope, I would have acted sooner." (Escopo)
Maturity -- the level of responsibility in an organization
"Not deflecting blame is a sign of maturity." (Maturidade)
Stance -- a publicly stated position on an issue
"Seldom does leadership take such a strong stance." (Posicionamento)
Elephant in the room -- the obvious problem nobody wants to mention
"Let me address the elephant in the room." (O elefante na sala)
Impact -- the effect or influence of an event
"Had we communicated earlier, the impact would have been minimal." (Impacto)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The cross-functional meeting was intense. Rarely had the team faced this level of scrutiny. Not only did they miss the Q3 deadline, but they also lost credibility with the Tokyo office. There was a clear disconnect between what was promised and what was delivered. Marcus took a firm stance on transparency: "Under no circumstances should anyone withhold information about delivery risks." Only after the client complained did the team acknowledge the problem. Little did they realize the full scope of the impact. Nobody tried to deflect blame, which showed real maturity. Finally, someone addressed the elephant in the room: the system itself needed to change.

1. What structure does "Rarely had the team faced..." use?
A Fronting with inversion: negative adverb + auxiliary + subject
B Cleft sentence: It was... that...
C Passive voice: was faced by the team
2. What is the effect of putting "Under no circumstances" at the beginning?
A It makes the sentence informal.
B It adds dramatic emphasis and authority to the rule.
C It makes the sentence conditional.
3. What does "Only after the client complained did the team acknowledge" tell us?
A The team waited too long -- they only acted AFTER the complaint.
B The team acted before the complaint.
C The client did not complain.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Fronting and Inversion

GRAMMAR

Quando uma expressão negativa vai para o INÍCIO da frase, sujeito e verbo INVERTEM. / When a negative expression goes to the START, subject and verb INVERT.

Expression / ExpressãoNormalInverted (Emphatic)
Rarely / SeldomWe rarely see this.Rarely do we see this.
Not only... but alsoWe missed + we lost.Not only did we miss, but also...
Under no circumstancesThis should not happen.Under no circumstances should this happen.
Ponto-chaveA inversão cria ÊNFASE dramática. Use em reuniões, apresentações e momentos de liderança.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

You want to emphasize that your team has NEVER experienced this before. The most emphatic way is:
A "Our team has never faced this."
B "Never before has our team faced this level of scrutiny."
C "It is our team that has never faced this."
You want to set a firm rule about withholding information. The most authoritative way is:
A "Under no circumstances should team members withhold risk information."
B "Team members should not withhold risk information."
C "I wish team members would not withhold information."
You want to say two bad things happened at once. Use inversion:
A "We lost the contract and the vendor trust."
B "Not only did we lose the contract, but we also damaged the vendor relationship."
C "It was the contract and the trust that we lost."
