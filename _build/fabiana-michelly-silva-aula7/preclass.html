Aula 07 -- Pre-class

Saying the Unsaid -- Emphasis and Focus in Cross-Functional Meetings

Fronting and inversion for emphasis: negative-adverbial inversion (Never have I seen such pushback, Rarely does engineering agree, Little did we know) and correlative inversion (Not only did we slip, but we also lost trust; Only after the demo did they agree). New vocabulary: cross-functional, undercurrent, tacit, pushback, caveat, subtext, hedge, understatement, read between the lines, elephant in the room, downplay, unspoken, nuance, gloss over, sugarcoat.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Cross-functional -- involving several different teams or departments at once
"This is a cross-functional project across product and operations." (Interdepartamental)
Undercurrent -- a hidden feeling or tension running beneath the surface
"There was an undercurrent of frustration in the meeting." (Tensão latente)
Tacit -- understood or accepted without being said directly
"There was a tacit agreement to keep the issue quiet." (Tácito)
Pushback -- resistance or opposition to a proposal or decision
"The new timeline met strong pushback from engineering." (Resistência)
Caveat -- a warning or condition you add to a statement
"I agree, with one caveat about the budget." (Ressalva)
Subtext -- the real meaning under the words that are actually spoken
"The subtext was that they felt rushed and ignored." (Subtexto)
Hedge -- to soften a statement so you avoid fully committing
"Stop hedging and give us a clear date." (Evitar se comprometer)
Understatement -- saying less than you mean to sound calm or measured
"Calling a three-week slip a delay was an understatement." (Eufemismo)
Read between the lines -- to understand a meaning that is hinted at but not stated
"Read between the lines: they are not happy with the plan." (Ler nas entrelinhas)
Elephant in the room -- an obvious problem that no one wants to mention
"Let us address the elephant in the room: the launch is late." (O óbvio que ninguém menciona)
Downplay -- to make something seem less important than it really is
"Do not downplay the risk to the launch date." (Minimizar)
Unspoken -- felt or understood but never said in words
"There was an unspoken rule about working weekends." (Não dito)
Nuance -- a small but important difference in meaning or tone
"His tone added a nuance the email had missed." (Sutileza)
Gloss over -- to avoid dealing with something difficult by treating it lightly
"Do not gloss over the missed deadline in the report." (Passar por cima)
Sugarcoat -- to make bad news sound more pleasant than it is
"Do not sugarcoat the numbers for the board." (Suavizar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Fronting & Inversion

GRAMMAR

Usado para dar ênfase forte e formal ao colocar um advérbio negativo no início. / Used to add strong, formal emphasis by fronting a negative adverbial.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Never / Rarely / Seldom + aux + subjectÊnfase em algo raro ou extremo / Emphasizes something rare or extremeNever have I seen such pushback.
Not only + aux + subject ... but ... alsoAdiciona um segundo ponto com peso / Adds a second, weighty pointNot only did we slip, but we also lost trust.
Only when / after / Not until + clause + aux + subjectMarca o único momento em que algo ocorre / Marks the one moment it happensOnly after the demo did they agree.
Little + aux + subjectSurpresa: ninguém esperava / Surprise: no one expected itLittle did we know how deep it ran.

Key point / Ponto-chave: após o advérbio negativo no início, a ordem inverte como numa pergunta: o auxiliar (have, did, do, is) vem ANTES do sujeito. Não se diz "Never I have seen"; diz-se Never have I seen.
