Chapter 3

Future Perfect & Continuous

Finished by a point, or in progress at a moment

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"By next year, we will have opened the second clinic."

"This time next month, I will be presenting to the board."

"By our third anniversary, we will have hired the whole team."

"This time next spring, we will be running both sites at once."

TenseStructureUse
Future perfectwill have + past participlean action finished BEFORE a point in the future (by next year, by December)
Future continuouswill be + -ingan action in progress AT a moment in the future (this time next month, at 10 tomorrow)
Signal wordsby + time / this time next..."by" points to completion; "this time next / at" points to a moment in progress

Ask yourself: is it FINISHED by that future point (will have + past participle), or HAPPENING at that future moment (will be + -ing)? Use "by" for completion and "this time next / at" for the moment in progress.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

By next year, we will open three clinics.
By next year, we will have opened three clinics.
This time next week, I will present to the board.
This time next week, I will be presenting to the board.
By 2027, the team will have grew a lot.
By 2027, the team will have grown a lot.

Use "will have + past participle" for what is finished by a future point, and "will be + -ing" for what is in progress at a future moment. After "have", always use the past participle: grown, not grew.

Dialogue

Mapping the Year

N
So, Eliane, a year from now, where will the clinic be?
E
By this time next year, we will have opened the second site and, I hope, broken into a whole new market.
N
Ambitious. And day to day, what will that look like?
E
This time next spring, I will be running two teams at once, so I am building the roadmap now.
N
Good. What is on the horizon that excites you most?
E
Two things in the pipeline. If the pilot works, it will be a real game changer and a springboard into a new market.
N
Just be careful not to scale up faster than you can hire.
E
Agreed. By December, we will have hired eight people, and we will be ramping up the systems first.
N
That is the right trajectory. A year from now, you will not be pitching an idea. You will be reporting growth.
