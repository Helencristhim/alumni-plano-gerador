Film
Steve Jobs (2015)

Danny Boyle's film is built around three product launches, and Jobs is always talking about what the company will have become and what they will be building next. It is a master class in casting a roadmap: on the horizon, the game changer, the trajectory. Listen for how a leader describes the future to pull people toward it.

Tip: after a launch scene, write two sentences about the company's plan -- one future perfect ("By the next launch, they will have shipped...") and one future continuous ("This time next year, they will be building...").

Podcast
The a16z Show

Andreessen Horowitz's podcast is all about where technology and business are going -- what is on the horizon, what will be a game changer, and how companies scale up and break into new markets. Founders and investors constantly use the future perfect and future continuous to describe roadmaps. Direct connection to Lesson 17: this is how people talk about the road ahead in real business English.

Tip: pick one episode and note two predictions -- one with "will have + past participle" and one with "will be + -ing".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Future perfect, or future perfect continuous?"

A short, clear BBC Learning English explainer on the future perfect -- will have + past participle -- the tense at the heart of this lesson's roadmap. It shows exactly when to use "will have done" to talk about what will be finished by a point in the future. Watch it to lock in the form you use to say "by next year, we will have opened the clinic".

Tip: after the video, say three sentences about your own plans using "By [a date], I will have..." out loud.
