Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words founders use to map growth. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que fundadores usam para mapear o crescimento. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Scale up -- to grow the business or operation to a bigger size / expandir, crescer em escala
"Do not scale up faster than you can hire."
Roadmap -- a plan showing the steps toward a future goal / plano de etapas, roteiro
"I am building the roadmap for the next two years."
On the horizon -- coming in the near future; expected soon / no horizonte, por vir em breve
"There is a new treatment on the horizon."
Ramp up -- to increase in volume, speed or intensity / intensificar, acelerar
"We will ramp up hiring before the second site opens."
In the pipeline -- being developed or prepared; coming soon / em preparo, a caminho
"There are two projects in the pipeline for next year."
Down the road -- at a later time in the future / mais adiante, futuramente
"A third clinic is possible down the road."
Ahead of schedule -- earlier than the planned time / adiantado, antes do previsto
"If we stay ahead of schedule, we open in the spring."
Game changer -- something that dramatically changes the situation / algo que muda tudo, um divisor de águas
"That new device could be a real game changer."
Springboard -- something that launches you to a higher level / trampolim, alavanca
"The pilot will be a springboard into a new market."
Foothold -- a secure first position from which to make progress / ponto de apoio, base inicial
"The first clinic gave us a foothold in the region."
Trajectory -- the path or direction something is developing in / trajetória, rumo
"The trajectory of the clinic is clearly upward."
Break into -- to enter a new market or field successfully / entrar (num mercado), conquistar espaço
"Next year we hope to break into a new market."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When Eliane maps the year, she uses two futures. By next year, she will have opened the second clinic -- an action finished before a future point. Notice the pattern: will have + past participle. This time next spring, she will be running two teams at once -- an action in progress at a future moment: will be + -ing. The signal words help you choose. "By" points to completion (by December, we will have hired eight people); "this time next" or "at ten tomorrow" points to a moment in progress (at ten tomorrow, we will be presenting). After "have", always use the past participle: grown, not grew.

1. Which tense shows an action finished before a point in the future?
A Future perfect -- by next year, we will have opened the clinic
B Future continuous -- by next year, we will be opening the clinic
C Present simple -- by next year, we open the clinic
2. Which tense shows an action in progress at a future moment?
A Future perfect -- this time next spring, we will have run both sites
B Future continuous -- this time next spring, we will be running both sites
C Past simple -- this time next spring, we ran both sites
3. Which signal word points to completion by a future point?
A "By" -- by December, we will have hired eight people.
B "At ten tomorrow" -- a moment in progress.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Perfect & Future Continuous

GRAMMAR

We use these two futures together to map a plan: the future perfect for what is done by a point, and the future continuous for what is happening at a moment. / Usamos estes dois futuros juntos para mapear um plano: o future perfect para o que estará concluído até um ponto, e o future continuous para o que estará acontecendo em um momento.

Tense / TempoForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Future perfect / futuro perfeitowill have + past participleBy next year, we will have opened three clinics.
Future continuous / futuro contínuowill be + -ingThis time next month, I will be presenting.
Signal for completion / conclusãoby + time (by December)By December, we will have hired eight people.
Signal for a moment / em progressothis time next... / at ten tomorrowAt ten tomorrow, we will be running the pilot.

Watch out: after "have", use the past participle (grown, opened, built), never the past simple. / Atenção: depois de "have", use o particípio (grown, opened, built), nunca o passado simples.
