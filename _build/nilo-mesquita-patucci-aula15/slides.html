Chapter 3 -- Hypothetical Offers

Grammar Discovery

Persuade with the second conditional

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

"If you kept our logo, we could give you an extra category."

"I understand your concern. That is a fair point." (acknowledge first)

Grammar Rule

The Second Conditional

If-part (imagined)Result (the offer)Full hypothetical offer
If + past simplewould + base verbIf we lowered the fee, would you sign for four seasons?
If we added a categorywould you reconsiderIf we added two categories, would you reconsider?
If you kept our logowe could giveIf you kept our logo, we could give you an extra category.
Acknowledge the objectionI understand...I understand your concern. That is a fair point.

Key idea: for a polite, hypothetical offer, use If + past simple (we lowered) and would or could + base verb (you sign). The past tense does not mean the past here -- it makes the offer softer and only imagined.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If we would lower the fee, would you sign for four seasons?"
"If we lowered the fee, would you sign for four seasons?"

After if, use the past simple (we lowered). Put would only in the result, with a base verb (would you sign).

Comprehension

About the Briefing

1. What should Nilo do first when the other side raises an objection?

Stay calm and acknowledge their concern -- "I understand your concern" or "that is a fair point" -- never fight it.

2. Which grammar does Sofia recommend to persuade the other side?

The second conditional -- "if we lowered the fee, would you sign" -- a soft, hypothetical way to make an offer.

3. According to Sofia, what is an objection really?

Not a no -- it is a door. You open it with a good question and a smart hypothetical offer.

1. How did Nilo answer the first objection about the budget?

He did not argue -- he acknowledged the concern, asked a question, then made a hypothetical offer to spread the payment across the season.

The logo on the training kit -- he protected it but offered a concession, an extra category, so the sponsor would keep the logo.
