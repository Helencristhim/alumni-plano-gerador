Lesson 15 -- Negotiation II: Closing the Deal

Series
Billions -- any closing or objection-handling scene

Powerful people face hard objections, protect their deal-breakers, trade concessions, and close under pressure. Listen for how they acknowledge a concern before answering it, and how they use hypothetical "what if" offers to move the other side toward a yes.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one moment where someone acknowledges an objection ("I understand...") and one hypothetical offer ("if we... would you...").

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Handling Objections and Closing the Deal"

Practical episodes on the hard part of a negotiation: acknowledging objections, asking the right question, making hypothetical offers with the second conditional, protecting a deal-breaker, and moving to close. Exactly today's language in real business deals.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to acknowledge an objection. Then record yourself answering an imaginary objection to your Corinthians deal using two of them.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The Second Conditional (If I had..., I would...)"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to form the second conditional with If + past simple and would/could + base verb, and how to use it for polite, hypothetical offers and suggestions.

After watching, turn five direct offers about your next deal into hypothetical offers with "If we... would you...?", and say each one out loud.
