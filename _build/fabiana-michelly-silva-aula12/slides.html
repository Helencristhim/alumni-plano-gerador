Chapter 3

The Odds

Modals of probability and deduction

Discovery

How Sure Are You?

"Port delays must be the main threat -- every shipment is late." (deduction: almost certain)

"The exchange rate may move against us this quarter." (possible)

"Congestion might ease by Q3, but it could also get worse." (possible, less sure)

"A small swing could hurt our margins." (possible outcome)

"That supplier can't be our only option -- it is too risky." (deduction: impossible)

"Doing nothing is bound to fail; a default is unlikely to happen." (near-certain / improbable)

Grammar Rule

Modals of Probability

ModalCertaintyExample
mustAlmost certain it IS true (deduction)Delays must be the main threat.
can'tAlmost certain it is NOT trueThat can't be our only option.
may / might / couldA real possibility (might/could = less sure)The rate may move against us.
be likely / bound toProbable / near-certain to happenDelays are likely to continue.
be unlikely toProbably will NOT happenA default is unlikely to happen.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The port might to get worse, and the cost could increases." (no to, no -s after a modal)
"The port might get worse, and the cost could increase." (modal + base verb)

After might, may, could, must, can't use the base verb -- no to and no -s. Use must (not have to) when you are deducing that something is true.

Bloomberg Sourcing -- Risk Register (Q3 Plan)

PORT DELAY
Likelihood: high. It is likely to continue and could slip the launch. Mitigate with a two-week buffer.
SUPPLIER
Exposure to one factory is our biggest vulnerability. A default is unlikely, but the impact would be severe. Safeguard: a second supplier.
CURRENCY
The rate may swing either way. A downturn could hurt margins. Hedge with finance so we are not caught off guard.

Rule of thumb: A threat you can name is a threat you can mitigate. Doing nothing is the one option that is bound to fail.

Comprehension

Modals Spotted

1. unlikely to (improbable): "A default is _____ happen..."

3. can't (deduction -- impossible): "The rate _____ stay flat all year."
"The rate can't stay flat all year."
