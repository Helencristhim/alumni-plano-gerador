Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Exposure -- the degree to which a business is open to loss
"Our exposure to a single supplier is dangerously high." (Exposição ao risco)
Mitigate -- to reduce the seriousness or impact of a risk
"A two-week buffer helps mitigate shipping delays." (Mitigar / atenuar)
Threat -- a possible danger or source of harm to a plan
"Port congestion is the biggest threat to the launch." (Ameaça)
Vulnerability -- a weak point that could be exploited
"Relying on one factory is a serious vulnerability." (Vulnerabilidade)
Fallback -- an alternative plan used if the first one fails
"We have no fallback if this carrier goes bankrupt." (Plano alternativo)
Buffer -- extra time or stock that absorbs a delay or shock
"We build a two-week buffer into every shipment." (Margem de segurança)
Downside -- the negative side or potential loss of a decision
"The upside is speed; the downside is higher cost." (Lado negativo)
Likelihood -- how probable it is that something will happen
"The likelihood of a port delay is high this quarter." (Probabilidade)
Hedge -- an action taken to reduce financial loss
"Finance will hedge against the currency swing." (Proteção financeira / hedge)
Safeguard -- a measure put in place in advance to prevent harm
"A second supplier is our main safeguard." (Salvaguarda)
Off guard -- unprepared or surprised by an unexpected event
"The sudden price jump caught the team off guard." (Desprevenido)
Downturn -- a decline in business or economic activity
"A market downturn could cut demand by half." (Queda / retração)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A good risk review names the uncertainty out loud. Port congestion might ease by Q3, but it could just as easily get worse, so we cannot assume smooth sailing. The exchange rate may move against us, and with thin margins, even a small swing could hurt. When every shipment arrives late, delays must be the main threat -- the evidence is too strong to ignore. A single-supplier model can't be safe; it is far too exposed. A default is unlikely to happen, but the impact would be severe, so it is worth a contingency. And doing nothing is bound to fail. The team that names the downside is the team that can mitigate it.

1. In "delays must be the main threat", what does "must" express?
A A logical deduction -- the speaker is almost certain it is true.
B An obligation -- the delays are required to happen.
C A possibility -- the speaker is unsure.
2. Why is "can't" used in "A single-supplier model can't be safe"?
A It expresses a lack of ability.
B It is a deduction -- the speaker is almost certain it is NOT true (impossible).
C It gives permission.
3. What is the difference between "might ease" and "is bound to fail"?
A "Might" is a possibility; "bound to" is near-certain.
B They mean exactly the same thing.
C "Might" is past; "bound to" is future.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modals of Probability

GRAMMAR

Modais para expressar o quanto você tem certeza sobre o presente e o futuro. / Modals to express how sure you are about the present and the future.

ModalCerteza / CertaintyExample / Exemplo
mustQuase certo que É verdade (dedução) / Almost certain (true)Delays must be the main threat.
can'tQuase certo que NÃO é (dedução) / Almost certain (false)That can't be our only option.
may / might / couldPossibilidade real / A real possibilityThe rate may move against us.
be likely / bound toProvável / quase certo que ocorre / Probable, near-certainDelays are likely to continue.
Ponto-chaveDepois do modal, use o verbo na FORMA BASE -- sem to e sem -s (might GET, could HURT). / After a modal, use the base verb.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

The evidence is overwhelming and you want to state a near-certain deduction. You say:
A "Delays must be the main threat." (deduction = must)
B "Delays must to be the main threat." (no to after a modal)
C "Delays have to be the main threat." (have to = obligation, not deduction)
You want to flag a possibility you are not sure about. The best sentence is:
A "The rate will move against us." (will = certainty, too strong)
B "The rate might move against us." (might = possibility)
C "The rate might moves against us." (no -s after a modal)
You are almost certain a single-supplier model is NOT safe. You say:
A "This can't be safe -- it is far too exposed." (deduction = can't)
B "This mustn't be safe -- it is far too exposed." (mustn't = prohibition, not deduction)
C "This couldn't to be safe." (no to after a modal)
