Lesson 12 -- The Risk Conversation

Series
Chernobyl (2019) -- Episode 1: "1:23:45"

A masterclass in risk denial and crisis management. Watch how leaders weigh likelihood against impact, ignore a clear vulnerability, and get caught off guard when the worst case arrives.

Note three moments where someone underestimates a threat. Rewrite each as a modal of probability (it could..., it must..., it can't...).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "Managing Risk in an Uncertain World"

Harvard Business Review experts discuss how companies assess threats, build buffers, and hedge against shocks across global supply chains. Directly relevant to running a risk review.

Write down two risks the guests describe. For each, say how likely it is and how you would mitigate it, using a different modal of probability.

YouTube
"Supply Chain Risk Management" -- MIT Center for Transportation & Logistics

An MIT session on identifying exposure, weighing likelihood vs impact, and designing safeguards and fallbacks for resilient supply chains. Essential framing for any sourcing decision.

After watching, prepare a 2-minute risk register for one of your own projects, using a different modal of probability in each line.
