Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key turn-taking expressions for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Jump In -- to join a conversation quickly when you get the chance
"Sorry to jump in, but I have a quick point."
Chime In -- to add your comment to a discussion
"Feel free to chime in at any time."
Yield the Floor -- to stop speaking and let someone else talk
"I'll yield the floor to my colleague."
Go Ahead -- used to invite someone to speak or continue
"Please, go ahead. I would like to hear your view."
Hear Out -- to listen until someone finishes before you respond
"Let's hear her out before we react."
Talk Over -- to speak at the same time as someone else
"Sorry, I did not mean to talk over you."
Wrap Up -- to bring a point or a meeting to an end
"Let's wrap up this topic and move on."
Hang On -- to ask someone to wait a moment
"Hang on, let me finish my point."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

In the weekly committee call, three people are speaking. David, the chair, asks for opinions. Anna starts to explain, but Eduarda has urgent news. "Sorry to jump in," she says, "but the creditors have a hard deadline." Anna replies, "Hang on, let me finish my point." Eduarda apologizes: "Of course, please go ahead. I did not mean to talk over you." Later, David invites Eduarda to chime in. She suggests they hear everyone out and then wrap up the timeline. Because everyone takes turns and uses polite discourse markers like "well", "anyway", and "as I was saying", the meeting stays calm and productive.

1. Why does Eduarda apologize to Anna?
A Because she is late.
B Because she talked over Anna before she finished.
C Because she disagrees with the deadline.
2. Which phrase invites someone to speak?
A "Hang on."
B "Please, go ahead."
C "Sorry to jump in."
3. What does Eduarda suggest at the end?
A To hear everyone out and then wrap up the timeline.
B To end the meeting immediately.
C To ignore the creditors.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Discourse markers = small words/phrases that signal your turn in a conversation (palavrinhas que sinalizam DE QUEM é a vez de falar). Each function has its own marker.

FunctionPhraseExample
InterruptSorry to jump in, but..."Sorry to jump in, but the deadline is close."
Hold the floorHang on, let me finish..."Hang on, let me finish my point."
YieldGo ahead / Please, you first"Please, go ahead."
ReturnAnyway, as I was saying..."Anyway, as I was saying, the timeline is tight."

Common Mistake:

"Stop! I want to talk." / "No, listen to me."

"Sorry to jump in, but..." / "If I could just add one thing..."

Tradução direta soa rude em inglês. Um marcador suave abre espaço sem ser agressivo.
