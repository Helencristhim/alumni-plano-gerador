Grammar Rule

Signposting Your Turn

FunctionUseful PhraseExample
InterruptSorry to jump in, but... / If I could just add..."Sorry to jump in, but the deadline is close."
Hold the floorHang on... / If I could finish my point..."Hang on, let me finish my point."
YieldGo ahead / Please, you first / I'll hand over to you"Please, go ahead."
ReturnAnyway... / Going back to your point..."Anyway, as I was saying..."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"Stop! I want to talk." / "No, listen to me."
"Sorry to jump in, but..." / "If I could just add one thing..."

A soft discourse marker opens space politely. A direct command sounds rude.

Speaking

Oral Drilling

1. Interrupt a discussion politely.
"Sorry to jump in, but I have a quick point."
2. Hold the floor when someone interrupts you.
"Hang on, let me finish my point."
3. Invite a colleague to speak.
"Please, go ahead. I would like to hear your view."
4. Apologize for talking over someone.
"Sorry, I did not mean to talk over you."
5. Return to your point after a tangent.
"Anyway, as I was saying, the timeline is tight."
6. Suggest wrapping up a long topic.
"Let's hear everyone out and then wrap up."
