Chapter 3: Say What You Think

Opinions: agree & disagree

How to speak your mind with respect.

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I think we should open the office."

"In my opinion, the cost is too high."

"I agree with you."

"I see your point, but I disagree."

FunctionPhraseExample
Give an opinionI think / In my opinion + sentenceI think we should wait.
AgreeI agree (with you)I agree with you.
DisagreeI disagree / I see your point, but...I see your point, but I disagree.
Correct gentlyActually, + sentenceActually, the cost is lower.

After I think or In my opinion comes a full sentence (subject + verb). To agree, say I agree — never "I am agree". To disagree with respect, start with I see your point, but... Use Actually to correct an idea gently.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am agree with you."

✓ RIGHT

"I agree with you."

✗ WRONG

"In my opinion is a good plan."

✓ RIGHT

"In my opinion, it is a good plan."

Agree and disagree are verbs: say I agree, never "I am agree". And after In my opinion always put a full sentence with a subject (it is, we should, the plan works).

Dialogue

The Board Discussion

P
Rafael, the proposal is to open a second office in the south. What is your point of view?
R
In my opinion, this is the right moment. I think the new clients there need a local office.
P
I see your point, but I have a concern. The cost is very high this year.
R
That is a reasonable concern. Actually, we can start with a small office and grow later.
P
That is a good compromise. I agree with that plan. Shall we vote?
R
Yes, let us vote. If most of us approve, we open the small office in the south.
