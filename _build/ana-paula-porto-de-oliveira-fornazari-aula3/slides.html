Chapter 3: The Map

There Is vs There Are

One place · Many places

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"There is a bakery on the corner."

"There are two parks near my home."

"There is a pharmacy next to the square."

"There are many shops across from the station."

Why there is in some sentences, but there are in others?

FormUseExample
There is
(There's)		One thing — singularThere is a bakery on the corner.
There areTwo or more things — pluralThere are two parks near my home.
Prepositions of placeSay WHERE it is: next to, across from, between, behind, in front of, on the corner of, nearThe cafe is next to the pharmacy.

Use there is / there are to say what exists — never "have" (in Portuguese "tem", in English there is).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Have a park near my house."
"There is a park near my house."
"There is many stores downtown."
"There are many stores downtown."
"The bank is in front the church."
"The bank is in front of the church."

Use there is / there are (never "have") to say what exists, match is with singular and are with plural, and never drop the of in "in front of".
