Lesson 3 -- My Neighborhood & My City

Documentary
Nature's Response to Urban Sprawl (DW Documentary)

A calm, beautiful documentary about how nature lives inside our cities -- parks, squares, avenues and the animals that share our streets. It is full of the exact vocabulary from this lesson. Connection to Lesson 3: listen for the places in a neighborhood (park, square, block, avenue) and for "there is / there are".

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write three sentences with "there is" or "there are" about places you see in the film.

TED Talk
The Walkable City -- Jeff Speck (TED)

A friendly, clear talk about what makes a neighborhood pleasant to live in: sidewalks, short blocks, shops on the corner and less traffic. The speaker describes cities in natural, everyday English. Connection to Lesson 3: notice the city words (avenue, block, sidewalk, downtown) and how he says where things are.

Tip: pause after each idea and describe your own neighborhood with a preposition of place (next to, across from, near).

YouTube
"There is" and "There are" -- Learn English Grammar (ABA English)

A short, clear video on the exact grammar from today: when to use "there is" and when to use "there are". Connection to Lesson 3: this is the difference between "There is a bakery" and "There are two parks".

Tip: pause after each example and make one true sentence about your street -- one with "there is", one with "there are".
