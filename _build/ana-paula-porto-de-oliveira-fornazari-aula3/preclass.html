Lesson 03 -- Pre-class

My Neighborhood & My City

Describing your neighborhood and city. Key words: avenue, sidewalk, corner, bakery, pharmacy, square, landmark, crosswalk, traffic, block, convenient, downtown. Structure: there is / there are with prepositions of place (next to, across from, between, behind, in front of, on the corner of, near).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Avenue -- a wide street in a city, often lined with trees
"There is a busy avenue near my apartment."
Sidewalk -- the paved path at the side of a street where people walk
"The sidewalks here are wide and clean."
Corner -- the point where two streets meet
"There is a coffee shop on the corner."
Bakery -- a shop that makes and sells bread and cakes
"There is a small bakery across from my building."
Pharmacy -- a shop where you buy medicine
"The pharmacy is next to the supermarket."
Square -- an open public space in a city, surrounded by buildings
"There is a beautiful square in the center of town."
Landmark -- a famous building or place that is easy to recognize
"The old church is the main landmark in my neighborhood."
Crosswalk -- the marked place where people cross the street
"Always use the crosswalk to cross the avenue."
Traffic -- the cars and vehicles moving on the roads
"There is a lot of traffic in the morning."
Block -- a group of buildings surrounded by four streets
"The gym is two blocks from my house."
Convenient -- easy to reach and close to what you need
"My neighborhood is very convenient for shopping."
Downtown -- the central business area of a city
"I work downtown, near the main square."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana Paula lives on a quiet avenue downtown, and she loves her new neighborhood. There is a small bakery on the corner, right across from her building, and there are two pharmacies near the square. The old church is the main landmark, and there is a lovely park behind it. Everything is very convenient: there are shops, a market and a gym only two blocks away. In the morning there is a lot of traffic on the avenue, so she always uses the crosswalk to cross safely. On weekends, she takes a stroll along the quiet sidewalks.

1. Why do we say "There is a small bakery" and not "There are"?
A Because it is one place (singular).
B Because it is more than one place (plural).
C Because the bakery is far away.
2. "There are two pharmacies" uses "are" because:
A There is only one pharmacy.
B There is more than one pharmacy (plural).
C The pharmacies are closed.
3. Which sentence about the park is correct?
A "Have a park behind the church."
B "There is a park behind the church."
C "There is a park in front the church."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There is / There are + Prepositions of Place

GRAMMAR

How to say what exists in a place and where it is.

FormUseExample
There is
(There's)		One thing -- singular. Say what exists (never "have").There is a bakery on the corner.
There areTwo or more things -- plural.There are two parks near my home.
Negative & questionThere is not / There are not; Is there...? / Are there...?Is there a pharmacy near here?
Prepositions of placeSay WHERE it is: next to, across from, between, behind, in front of, on the corner of, near. (Never drop the "of": in front of.)

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real situation.

A visitor asks: "Is there a bakery near here?" You answer:
A "Yes, have a bakery on the corner."
B "Yes, there is one on the corner, across from my building."
C "Yes, there are a bakery on the corner."
You want to say your area has many shops. The best answer is:
A "There is many shops downtown."
B "There are many shops downtown."
C "Have many shops downtown."
You give directions to the pharmacy. Which is correct?
A "It is next to the market, in front of the square."
B "It is next the market, in front the square."
C "It is next to the market, in front square."
A friend asks why you like your area. You say:
A "It is very convenient. There are shops and a park near my building."
B "It is very convenient. There is shops and a park near my building."
C "It is very convenient. Have shops and a park near my building."
