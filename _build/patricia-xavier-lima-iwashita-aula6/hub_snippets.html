Onboarding & Driving Adoption

Guiding a new customer from day one to real value. Key words: enable, walkthrough, configure, license, champion, training, best practice, value, goal, success plan. Structure: sequencing words (first, then, after that, once, finally).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Enable -- to give someone the ability or power to do something (capacitar / habilitar)
"Our goal is to enable your team to work on their own."
Walkthrough -- a step-by-step demonstration that shows how something works (demonstração guiada)
"We book a hands-on walkthrough for your users."
Configure -- to set up the settings and options so a tool works for you (configurar)
"We help you configure the platform on day one."
License -- a paid right that lets a person use the software (licença)
"We set up your first license together."
Champion -- the person on the customer side who promotes and supports the tool (defensor interno)
"We confirm who your internal champion will be."
Training -- sessions that teach users how to use the product well (treinamento)
"We move on to training once your users feel ready."
Best practice -- the most effective, proven way of doing something (boa prática)
"We share the best practices our strongest teams use."
Value -- the real benefit or worth a customer gets from the product (valor / benefício)
"Adoption grows when people see the value for themselves."
Goal -- the result a customer plans and wants to reach (objetivo / meta)
"First, we agree on your main goals."
Success plan -- a written plan of a customer's goals and the steps to reach them (plano de sucesso)
"We review your success plan every month."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When a new customer signs up, Patrícia follows a clear order. First, she runs a kickoff call and agrees on their goals. Then, her team helps them configure the platform and set up the first license. After that, she books a hands-on walkthrough for the users. Once the users feel comfortable, she moves on to training and shares the best practices. Finally, when the team is fully onboarded, she reviews the success plan and checks the progress on adoption.

1. Por que usamos "After that" e não apenas "After" para ligar o próximo passo?
A Porque "after" só existe no passado.
B Porque "after" sozinho não liga dois passos; o correto é "after that".
C Porque "after that" significa "finalmente".
2. Depois de "once", qual forma do verbo usamos?
A O presente: "once the users feel comfortable" (nunca "will").
B Sempre "will": "once the users will feel".
C Sempre o passado: "once the users felt".
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "After, we book a walkthrough."
B "After that, we book a walkthrough."
C "After that, we will booking a walkthrough."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Sequencing Words

GRAMMAR

Como colocar os passos de um processo em ordem (explicação em inglês e português).

WordUse / UsoExample
FirstO primeiro passo. Vem com vírgula no início.First, we agree on your goals.
Then / After thatO próximo passo. Use "after that" (não "after" sozinho).Then, we configure it. After that, we train.
Once"assim que" / "depois que" + presente (nunca "will").Once your users feel ready, we start.
FinallyO último passo.Finally, we celebrate the wins.
