Your Customer Success Plan — A Step-by-Step Guide

Welcome to your success plan. This short guide explains how we help a new customer get real value from the platform, step by step. Our goal is simple: to enable your team to work with confidence and to reach the results you care about. Every customer is different, so first we agree on your main goals, and then we build the plan around them.

The plan follows a clear order. First, we run a kickoff call and confirm who your internal champion will be. Then, our team helps you configure the platform and set up your first license. After that, we book a hands-on walkthrough, so your users can see exactly how the product works. Once your users feel comfortable, we move on to training and share the best practices that other teams use every day.

Adoption does not happen by accident; it grows when people use the product and see the value for themselves. For that reason, we review your success plan every month and check your progress against your goals. If something is not working, we adjust the next steps together. Finally, when your team is fully onboarded, we celebrate the wins and plan the next phase of growth.

Adapted for class use from a customer onboarding success plan

Chapter 4: The Code

Putting Steps in Order

First · Then · After that · Once · Finally

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"First, we agree on your goals."

"Then, we configure the platform."

"After that, we book a walkthrough."

"Once your users feel ready, we start training."

WordUseExample
Firstthe first stepFirst, we agree on your goals.
Then / After thatthe next step (use "after that", not "after" alone)Then, we configure it. After that, we train.
Onceas soon as / after a step is done — + present, not "will"Once your users feel ready, we start.
Finallythe last stepFinally, we celebrate the wins.

These words mark the order of the steps. Put a comma after them at the start of a sentence. After once, use the present ("once you log in"), never "will".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We configure the tool. After, we invite the users."
"We configure the tool. After that, we invite the users."
"Once you will log in, you configure your dashboard."
"Once you log in, you configure your dashboard."

Use after that (not "after" alone) to link the next step. After once, use the present, never will.

Chapter 5: The Kickoff Call

Walking Through the Plan

Dialogue

The Kickoff Call

P
Welcome, Mike. First, let us agree on your main goals, and then I will build your success plan around them.
M
Sounds good. Our main goal is to get the whole sales team using the tool every day.
P
Perfect. Then we configure the platform and set up your first license. Who will be your internal champion?
M
That will be me. After that, what is the next step for my users?
P
After that, I run a hands-on walkthrough. Once your users feel comfortable, we move on to training and best practices.
M
I like this plan. So adoption really grows step by step.
Comprehension

About the Call

1. What is Mike's main goal?
To get the whole sales team using the tool every day.
2. Who will be the internal champion?
Mike himself.
3. What happens once the users feel comfortable?
They move on to training and best practices.
