Saying Goodbye -- Ending the Visit & Keeping in Touch

Ending a client's visit with a warm goodbye and a plan to keep in touch: thanking your guest, talking about the future, and seeing them off. New grammar: "be going to" for future plans and intentions. New vocabulary: farewell, see off, grateful, memorable, hospitality, productive, fruitful, stay in touch, stop by, looking ahead, sincerely, regards.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Farewell -- a goodbye, especially when someone is leaving for a long time
"We gave Clara a warm farewell at the end of her visit." (Despedida)
See off -- to go with someone to the place they are leaving from, to say goodbye
"I went to the airport to see Clara off before her flight." (Acompanhar / Despedir-se de alguém que parte)
Grateful -- feeling thankful, wanting to say thank you for something
"I am very grateful for the chance to work with you this week." (Grato / Agradecido)
Memorable -- so good or special that you will remember it for a long time
"The dinner last night was truly memorable." (Memorável / Inesquecível)
Hospitality -- the friendly, generous way you treat a guest or visitor
"Thank you for your wonderful hospitality during my visit." (Hospitalidade)
Productive -- getting a lot of useful work or good results done
"It was a very productive week; we finished the whole plan." (Produtivo)
Fruitful -- producing good, useful results; successful
"Our talks were fruitful, and we agreed on the main terms." (Frutífero / Proveitoso)
Stay in touch -- to keep contacting each other after you part
"Let us stay in touch by email after you go home." (Manter contato)
Stop by -- to make a short, casual visit to a place or person
"Please stop by our office in Geneva the next time you travel." (Dar uma passada / Visitar rapidamente)
Looking ahead -- thinking about and planning for the future
"Looking ahead, I am sure our companies are going to grow together." (Pensando no futuro / Olhando adiante)
Sincerely -- a formal, polite way to end a letter or email, before your name
"I closed the thank-you email with Sincerely, Walyson Silva." (Atenciosamente)
Regards -- friendly good wishes, often used to close a message (Best regards)
"Please give my best regards to your team in Geneva." (Cumprimentos / Saudações)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the short text and answer the questions.

Clara's visit to Santos is over, and Walyson goes to the airport to see her off. It has been a memorable and productive week, and Walyson is very grateful for the chance to host her. Clara thanks him for his warm hospitality and says it was a truly fruitful trip. Now they talk about the future. Walyson says: "Next week I am going to send you the final figures, and I am going to call my manager about the next steps." Clara replies: "We are going to stay in touch by email, and next year you must stop by our office in Geneva." Looking ahead, they are both sure their companies are going to build a long relationship. Walyson is going to write a thank-you email and close it with Sincerely and best regards. Then he gives Clara a warm farewell.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- be going to (future plans)

GRAMMAR

Para falar de planos e intenções que você já decidiu, use SUJEITO + am/is/are + GOING TO + verbo na forma base (I am going to send the report). É o tempo dos planos: o que você vai fazer a seguir. / To talk about plans and intentions you already decided, use SUBJECT + am/is/are + GOING TO + base verb. It is the tense of plans: what you are going to do next.

Form / FormaExample / Exemplo
Affirmative / Afirmativa"I am going to send the report."
Negative / Negativa"She is not going to miss the flight."
Question / Pergunta"Are you going to visit us soon?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: use am com I, is com he/she/it, are com you/we/they. Depois de going to, sempre o verbo na forma base (send, stay, visit), nunca "to send". / Use am/is/are correctly, and always the base verb after going to.
