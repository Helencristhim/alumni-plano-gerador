Chapter 3 -- Plans for the Future

Grammar Discovery

be going to for future plans

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do all these future plans have in common?

"I am going to send you the final figures." (my plan)

"We are going to stay in touch by email." (our plan)

"She is going to visit us in Geneva next year." (her plan)

"I am not going to forget this productive week." (negative)

Grammar Rule

be going to

FormExample
Affirmative"I am going to send the report."
Negative"She is not going to miss the flight."
Question"Are you going to visit us soon?"

Key idea: use am / is / are + going to + base verb for plans and intentions you decided before now. Use am with I, is with he/she/it, are with you/we/they. The verb after going to is always the base form (send, stay, visit), never "to send".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am going to to send you the report."
"I am going to send you the report."

After going to, use the base verb (send), never "to send". Always keep the verb be: "I am going to send", not "I going to send".

Artefact

The Thank-You Email

FROM: WALYSON SILVA -- TO: CLARA -- SUBJECT: THANK YOU

Dear Clara,

Thank you for a truly memorable and productive visit. I am very grateful for the chance to host you here in Santos, and I hope you enjoyed our hospitality.

As we agreed, I am going to send you the final figures next week, and I am going to talk to my manager about the next steps. Looking ahead, I am sure our companies are going to build a long, fruitful relationship.

Let us stay in touch by email, and please stop by our office whenever you travel to Brazil again.

Sincerely,
Walyson Silva
Best regards to your team in Geneva.
