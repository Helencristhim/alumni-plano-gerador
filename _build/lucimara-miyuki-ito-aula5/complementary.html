American English Podcast — Ep. 106: A Dream Trip to the Maldives ("Off the Beaten Track")

This is the whole of Lesson 5, in natural American English: a conversation about the dream trip — the one that has not happened yet. You will hear the second conditional in the wild (“if I had the money, I would…”) and the expression you learned today: off the beaten path (in the British version, track — same idea).

Tip (do this in the car, on the way there): listen ONCE, all the way through, with no pauses. Then write down EVERY sentence that starts with if or has a would in it — and bring 2 that you could NOT put together on your own. They become the mini listening lesson in Lesson 6.

American English Podcast — Ep. 109: Dream Houses with Pete

Two Americans describing their dream house — in other words, forty minutes of “I would…”, “I’d rather…” and “if I could…”, with no blackboard anywhere. The topic is not travel, but the grammar is exactly yours: the world that does not exist yet. Notice how the would almost disappears in fast speech — it turns into a single d glued to the pronoun.

Tip (on the way back): listen, and every time an “I’d” goes by, repeat the whole sentence OUT LOUD. Then answer their question out loud, alone in the car: “If you could live anywhere in the world, where would you live?” — 60 seconds, without stopping.

Native English Conversations — 2nd Conditional: "What would you do if you had $1m?"

Two native speakers, Matt and Mark, having a real conversation — no teacher, no whiteboard. They ask each other hypothetical questions and answer at real speed: “What would you do if…” / “I’d probably…”. It is today’s grammar in the mouth of people who are not teaching anything, which is exactly where you will meet it in September.

Tip: this works as pure audio — listen in the car, without looking at the screen. The rule of the exercise: pause every 3 or 4 sentences and sum up OUT LOUD, in one single sentence, what you have just heard. It is tiring for the first five minutes, and it is the listening practice that pays off the most.
