Travel & the Airport (Chicago)

Flying to the US: documents, the airport, and going through immigration in Chicago. Key words: passport, boarding pass, gate, customs, immigration, luggage, flight, departure, arrival, declare. Structure: travel questions and short answers (Where are you staying? How long are you staying? Do you have anything to declare? -- Yes, I do / No, I do not).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Passport -- passaporte (an official travel document)
"Here is my passport."
Boarding pass -- cartao de embarque (the ticket to enter the plane)
"I have my boarding pass."
Gate -- portao de embarque (the door where you board the plane)
"The flight leaves from gate 22."
Customs -- alfandega (where they check your bags)
"I go through customs."
Immigration -- imigracao (where they check your passport)
"The immigration officer asks questions."
Luggage -- bagagem (your bags and suitcases)
"My luggage is heavy."
Flight -- voo (a trip by plane)
"My flight is on time."
Departure -- partida / embarque (when the plane leaves)
"Departure is at nine."
Arrival -- chegada (when the plane lands)
"The arrival is on time."
Declare -- declarar (to tell customs what you carry)
"I have nothing to declare."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Fernando lands in Chicago. He goes to immigration. The officer says, "Good evening. May I see your passport?" Fernando gives his passport and his boarding pass. "What is the purpose of your visit?" the officer asks. "It is for business," Fernando says. "How long are you staying?" "I am staying for one week." "Do you have anything to declare?" "No, I do not. I only have my luggage." His flight was good and the arrival was on time.

1. Por que dizemos "How long are you staying?" e nao "How long you staying?"
A Porque "staying" e plural.
B A pergunta com be precisa de "are" antes de "you".
C Porque a acao e no passado.
2. A resposta certa para "Do you have anything to declare?" e:
A "No, I have not."
B "No, I do not."
C "No, I am not."
3. Which question is correct?
A "Where you are staying?"
B "Where are you staying?"
C "Where you staying?"
