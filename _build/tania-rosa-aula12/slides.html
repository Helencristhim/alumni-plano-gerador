Chapter 3: Agree & Disagree

React in a Word

So do I · Neither do I · I'm not sure

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I love this city." -- "So do I."

"I don't like crowds." -- "Neither do I."

"In my opinion, the food is great." -- "I agree."

"The beach is the best part." -- "I'm not sure about that."

FunctionPhraseWhen
Agree (+)So do I. / I agree.After a positive opinion
Agree (-)Neither do I.After a negative opinion
Disagree (soft)I'm not sure about that.Polite, friendly doubt
Give opinionIn my opinion... / I think...To start your idea

To agree with a positive sentence, use So do I. To agree with a negative one, use Neither do I. To disagree politely, soften it: "I'm not sure about that."

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I don't like it." -- "So do I."

✓ RIGHT

"I don't like it." -- "Neither do I."

✗ WRONG

"In my opinion is good."

✓ RIGHT

"In my opinion, it is good."

Chapter 4: A Friendly Debate

Tânia and a Friend

Two travelers, two opinions

Dialogue

Sharing Opinions

F
So, what do you think of the city? In my opinion, the old town is the best part.
T
So do I! I love the little streets. But to be honest, I prefer the markets to the museums.
F
Really? I'm not sure about that. From my point of view, the museums are amazing.
T
That's a fair point. I think it depends on the day. Today I would rather walk around the market.
F
Fair enough. I don't want to argue on holiday!
T
Neither do I! Let's do the market today and the museum tomorrow.

The old town, especially the museums.
2. What does Tânia prefer?
She prefers the markets to the museums.
3. How do they solve their disagreement?
They agree to do the market today and the museum tomorrow.
