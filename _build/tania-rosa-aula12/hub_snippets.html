Stage 1.1: Opinion Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you share and react to opinions.

Opinion -- opinião
"In my opinion, this is the best city in Europe."
To agree -- concordar
"I agree with you -- the food here is wonderful."
To disagree -- discordar
"I disagree -- I think the beach is better."
Point of view -- ponto de vista
"From my point of view, mornings are the best time to travel."
To prefer -- preferir
"I prefer quiet cafes to busy restaurants."
Would rather -- preferir (fazer algo)
"I would rather walk than take a taxi."
It depends -- depende
"Beach or city? It depends on the weather."
To be honest -- para ser sincero(a)
"To be honest, I prefer the quiet streets."
I think so -- acho que sim
"Is this restaurant good? I think so."
I'm not sure -- não tenho certeza
"I'm not sure about that -- I see it differently."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the conversation and answer the questions.

After breaking the ice with a friend, Tânia shares her opinion about the trip. "In my opinion, the old town is the best part," she says. Her friend smiles: "So do I!" Then he adds, "But I prefer the museums." Tânia laughs. "I'm not sure about that. To be honest, I would rather walk around the market." They have a different point of view, but they don't argue. "It depends on the day," she says. They agree to do both, and the conversation stays warm and friendly.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Giving Opinions, Agreeing & Disagreeing

Grammar

Para dar sua opinião, comece com "In my opinion" ou "I think". Para concordar rápido, use "So do I" (depois de frase afirmativa) ou "Neither do I" (depois de frase negativa). Para discordar com educação, suavize: "I'm not sure about that". / To give an opinion, start with "In my opinion" or "I think". To agree quickly, use "So do I" (after a positive sentence) or "Neither do I" (after a negative one). To disagree politely, soften it: "I'm not sure about that".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A friend says, "I love this restaurant." You feel the same. What do you say?
A "So do I! The pasta is amazing."
B "Neither do I."
C "I don't know you."
Someone says, "The tour was boring." You disagree, but want to stay friendly.
A "You are wrong."
B "I'm not sure about that. In my opinion, it was great."
C "Be quiet."
A friend asks, "Taxi or walk?" You like walking more. What do you say?
A "I would rather to take a taxi."
B "I would rather walk. It depends, but today the weather is nice."
C "No opinion."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Think about your last trip or a place you love. Share your opinion: what is the best part and why? Use "In my opinion", say what you prefer with "I would rather", and add one honest detail with "To be honest". Take your time.
