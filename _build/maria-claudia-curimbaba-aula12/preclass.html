Aula 12 -- Pre-class

Fiscal and Tax Talk -- Communicating With Accountants

Talk to your accountants in English about the fiscal year: tax returns, deadlines, audits, and what is deductible. Say how long something has been true with the present perfect + for / since ("We have used this system for three years", "I have worked with them since 2015"), and use the passive voice for fiscal facts (the return has been filed, taxes are withheld at source). New vocabulary: tax return, file, withhold, deductible, audit, ledger, invoice, accrual, fiscal year, liability, reconcile, comply.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Tax return -- an official report of income and tax sent to the authorities
"We have already filed this year's tax return." (Declaração de imposto)
File -- to submit a document officially to the tax authority
"We file the return before the deadline." (Declarar / protocolar)
Withhold -- to deduct tax from a payment before the money is paid out
"Taxes are withheld at source on every dividend." (Reter na fonte)
Deductible -- an expense that can be subtracted from your taxable income
"Travel costs are deductible if you keep the receipts." (Dedutível)
Audit -- an official examination of a company's accounts and records
"We have been preparing for the audit since March." (Auditoria)
Ledger -- the book or system that records all financial transactions
"Every payment is entered in the ledger." (Livro-razão)
Invoice -- a document that requests payment for goods or services
"The supplier sent the invoice last week." (Nota fiscal / fatura)
Accrual -- revenue or cost recorded when it occurs, not when cash moves
"Under the accrual method, we record the expense now." (Regime de competência)
Fiscal year -- the 12-month period a company uses for accounting and tax
"Our fiscal year ends in December." (Ano fiscal)
Liability -- a debt or financial obligation that the company owes
"Deferred tax is recorded as a liability." (Passivo / obrigação)
Reconcile -- to make two sets of figures agree, so the totals match
"We reconcile the bank statement every month." (Conciliar)
Comply -- to act in line with the rules, laws, and regulations
"We comply with the new tax-reporting rules." (Cumprir as regras)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is the end of the fiscal year, and I am on a call with Marcelo, my accountant. I have worked with his firm since 2015, and we have used the same accounting system for three years. The good news: the tax return has been filed, two days before the deadline. The audit starts on Monday, and Marcelo has gathered the documents since the start of the month. Two things remain: the consulting fees are only deductible if we keep every invoice, and the December statement has not been reconciled with the ledger yet. Taxes are withheld at source on the dividends, so that is not a liability we still owe. We have complied with the new rules for three years, so the audit should be smooth.

1. How long has the writer worked with Marcelo's firm?
A Since 2015 -- present perfect with "since" + a starting point.
B For 2015 -- present simple.
C Since three years.
2. Which word goes with a period of time, like "three years"?
A "since" -- since three years.
B "for" -- for three years.
C Neither -- you use no word.
3. How is the completed tax return described?
A "It has been filed." -- passive: have been + past participle.
B "It is filing now."
C "It files itself."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect (for / since) & the Passive

GRAMMAR

Para dizer há quanto tempo algo é verdade, use o present perfect (have/has + particpio) com FOR + um período (for three years) ou SINCE + um ponto de partida (since 2015). Use a voz passiva (be / have been + particpio) para fatos fiscais quando quem fez a ação não importa. / To say how long something has been true, use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) with FOR + a period or SINCE + a starting point. Use the passive (be / have been + past participle) for fiscal facts when the doer does not matter.

ElementFormExample
Present perfecthave / has + past participle"have worked", "has filed"
forfor + a period"for three years"
sincesince + a starting point"since 2015"
Passive voicebe / have been + past participle"The return has been filed."

Key point / Ponto-chave: para "há quanto tempo", use o present perfect (have worked), NUNCA o presente simples (work). FOR + período (for ten years); SINCE + ponto de partida (since 2015). A passiva (has been filed, are withheld) é natural no mundo fiscal. / For "how long", use the present perfect, never the present simple. FOR + a period; SINCE + a starting point. The passive is natural in fiscal English.

Stage 2: Order the Fiscal Call

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of a call with your accountant in the correct order.

?He reminds you of the rules: which costs are deductible, and how taxes are withheld at source.
?The accountant tells you what has been done: the return has been filed and the books have been closed.
?You agree on a date to review everything together, well before the fiscal year closes.
?He flags what is still open, such as a bank statement that has not been reconciled yet.
?He gives you the deadline and asks you to send any missing invoices before the audit.
