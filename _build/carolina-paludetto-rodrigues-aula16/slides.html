Grammar Rule

Will for Predictions

TypeRuleExample
Positivewill + base verbI will study harder
Negativewon't + base verbI won't give up
QuestionWill + subject + verb?Will you join the club?
Almost sure (~80%)will + probablyI will probably join
Not sure (~50%)Maybe + subject + willMaybe I will join

Key idea: use will + base verb to predict the future (I will study). The negative is won't (I won't give up). After will, the verb never changes: she will learn, not "she will learns". When you are not sure, soften with probably (after will: I will probably) or maybe (at the start: Maybe I will).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She will studies more." / "I will to travel next year."
"She will study more." / "I will travel next year."

After will, always use the base verb (will study, will travel). Never add -s and never add to.
