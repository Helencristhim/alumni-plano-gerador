Chapter 3

Question Words

who, what, where, when, why, how + the right order

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Where do you work?"

"When did you finish the report?"

"Who is your manager?"

"How many people are on the team?"

StructureUseExample
WH + do / does + subject + verbPresent questionsWhere do you work?
WH + did + subject + verbPast questionsWhen did you finish?
WH + is / are / was / were + subjectQuestions with to be (no do)Who is your manager?
Who / What + verb (subject question)When the WH word is the subjectWho manages the team?

Order: question word + auxiliary + subject + verb. With "to be", no "do".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"What you do?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you do?"

✗ WRONG

"Where you went?"

✓ RIGHT

"Where did you go?"

✗ WRONG

"Why you are late?"

✓ RIGHT

"Why are you late?"

Use "do/does" for the present, "did" for the past. With "to be", put the verb before the subject.

Chapter 4

A Real Q&A

Two colleagues ask and answer about a project

Dialogue

Questions About the Project

R
Hi Danilo! I have a quick question about the new project. Who is on the team?
D
Good question! There are five people. I am the lead, and you are the product manager.
R
Nice. What does the project do? Can you give me more details?
D
Sure. It builds a data platform. It collects information from many systems.
R
That is a clear answer. What is the meaning of "platform"?
D
It is one place for all our data. For example, reports, numbers, and charts.
R
I see. And when do we start? Why is it urgent?
D
We start on Monday. The reason is simple: the client needs it in March.
R
Got it. I write the reports, and I collect feedback from the users.
D
Perfect. Thanks for all the questions, Rachel!
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Rachel ask about first?
Who is on the project team.
2. What word does Rachel want the meaning of?
The word "platform".
3. What does Rachel do on the project?
She writes the reports and collects feedback from the users.
