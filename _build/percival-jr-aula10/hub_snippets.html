Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Achieve -- to reach a goal after working for it
"We achieved our safety goal this quarter." (Alcançar)
Progress -- movement forward toward a goal
"The team made good progress this month." (Progresso)
Result -- what happens because of an action
"The result of the audit was positive." (Resultado)
Improve -- to make something better
"We need to improve our response time." (Melhorar)
Recommend -- to say which option is the best to choose
"I recommend the second supplier." (Recomendar)
Overall -- considering everything together; in general
"Overall, it was a strong quarter." (No geral)
Confident -- sure that something will go well
"I am confident about next quarter." (Confiante)
Feedback -- comments that help you do better
"Thank you for your feedback." (Retorno)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past, Compare & Future

GRAMMAR

Esta é uma aula de revisão. Juntamos os três pontos das Aulas 6 a 9: passado simples, comparativos/superlativos e futuro (going to / will). / This is a review lesson. We bring together the three points from Days 6 to 9: past simple, comparatives/superlatives, and future (going to / will).

Use it forPatternExample
Passado / Past (finished)verb + ed / irregularWe achieved the goal. / We chose B.
Comparar / Compare+er / more ... than; the +est / the mostB is more reliable than A; the safest
Plano / Planbe going to + verbWe are going to improve reports.
Previsão / Predictionwill + verbThe result will be better.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use o passado simples para o que já aconteceu (achieved, chose, reviewed), comparativos e superlativos para comparar opções (more reliable than, the safest), e going to para planos e will para previsões. / Use the past simple for what already happened, comparatives/superlatives to compare options, and going to for plans and will for predictions.
