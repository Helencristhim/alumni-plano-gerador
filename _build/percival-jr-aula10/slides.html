Chapter 3 -- Grammar Recap

Three Points, One Quarter

Past, compare, and future

Grammar Notice

Past, Compare, Future

Does each sentence talk about the past, compare two things, or talk about the future?

"Last quarter we achieved our goal."

"Supplier B is more reliable than Supplier A."

"Next quarter we are going to improve our reports."

"I think the result will be even better."

Grammar Recap

The Three Patterns

Use it forPatternExample
Past (finished)verb + ed / irregularWe achieved the goal. / We chose B.
Compare options+er / more ... than; the +est / the mostB is more reliable than A; the safest
Planbe going to + verbWe are going to improve reports.
Predictionwill + verbThe result will be better.

Key idea: use the past simple for what already happened, comparatives and superlatives to compare options, and going to for plans and will for predictions.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Last quarter we achieve our goal."
"Last quarter we achieved our goal."

For a finished action in the past, use the past simple (achieved, chose, reviewed), not the present.
