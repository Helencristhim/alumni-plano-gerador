Gallery -- a room or building where works of art are shown
"The paintings are in the main gallery." (Galeria)
Exhibit -- a group of works of art put on show together
"The new exhibit is on the second floor." (Exposição / mostra)
Painting -- a picture made with paint on a flat surface
"This painting is from the nineteenth century." (Pintura / quadro)
Sculpture -- a solid work of art in stone, metal, or wood
"The sculpture hall is downstairs." (Escultura)
Portrait -- a painting, drawing, or photo of a person
"That portrait is almost two hundred years old." (Retrato)
Masterpiece -- an artist's greatest and most famous work
"This portrait is the masterpiece of the museum." (Obra-prima)
Artist -- a person who creates art, such as paintings or sculptures
"The artist used to paint outside in the fields." (Artista)
Collection -- a group of works of art kept and shown together
"The museum has a famous collection of paintings." (Coleção / acervo)
Century -- a period of one hundred years
"These paintings are from the nineteenth century." (Século)
Admission -- the money you pay to enter a museum; entry
"The admission ticket includes all the galleries." (Entrada / ingresso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At an art museum, we often talk about the past. To describe a habit or a situation that was true before but is not true now, use used to + base verb: "My family used to visit this museum every summer." "The artist used to paint outside." For repeated past actions, you can also use would + base verb: "We would spend the whole afternoon in the galleries." Be careful: would works only with actions, not with states, so we say "I used to be shy", never "I would be shy". In the negative and the question, drop the -d: "I did not use to enjoy sculpture." "Did you use to draw as a child?" These forms help you describe how things were when you were younger.

1. Which sentence describes a past habit that is finished now?
A My family used to visit this museum every summer.
B My family visits this museum now.
C My family will visit this museum.
2. Which sentence is correct in the negative?
A I did not used to enjoy sculpture.
B I did not use to enjoy sculpture.
C I not used to enjoy sculpture.
3. For a past STATE (not an action), which word should you use?
A used to ("I used to be shy")
B would ("I would be shy")
C either one is fine

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Used to & Would

GRAMMAR

Como falar de hábitos do passado que não acontecem mais. / How to talk about past habits that do not happen now.

Structure / EstruturaExampleNote / Nota
used to + verbo baseI used to visit the museum.hábito ou estado do passado que acabou / a past habit, not true now
would + verbo baseWe would spend all day there.ação repetida no passado (não estados) / repeated action, not states
negativa: did not use toI did not use to like art.sem -d: "use to" / no -d
pergunta: Did you use to...?Did you use to draw?de novo "use to", sem -d / no -d

Key point / Ponto-chave: used to + verbo base fala de um hábito ou situação do passado que não é mais verdade (I used to visit = não visito mais). would + verbo base também fala de ações repetidas, mas NÃO de estados: diga "I used to be shy", nunca "I would be shy". Na negativa e na pergunta, tire o -d: "did not use to", "Did you use to...?" / Drop the -d in the negative and question.
