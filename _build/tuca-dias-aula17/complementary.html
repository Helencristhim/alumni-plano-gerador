Lesson 17 -- At the Museum: Art & History

Series / Travel
The Art Assignment (PBS) -- "How to Look at Art in a Museum" (YouTube)

A friendly guide to visiting an art museum: walking through the galleries, looking at paintings and sculptures, and finding the masterpiece. Full of natural museum vocabulary.

Watch with English subtitles. Then write four sentences about a museum you used to visit and what you would do there.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "Talking About the Past: used to and would in English"

A short, practical episode on describing past habits with used to and would, and when each one works. You will hear the structures used naturally in everyday stories.

After listening, record a one-minute voice note about your childhood. Use used to, would, and at least four museum words.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "used to / would: Talking About Past Habits"

A clear grammar video on used to + verb and would + verb, with the difference between actions and states. Exactly today's grammar focus.

After watching, write four sentences with used to, then change two of them into the negative (did not use to) and one into a question (Did you use to...?).
