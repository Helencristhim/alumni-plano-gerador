Chapter 3: The Code

Five Words, One Rule

who · what · where · when · how

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Onde a palavra em destaque cai, sempre?

Where is the gate?

Who is your manager?

When does the flight arrive?

How do you spell your name?

Sempre em segundo. A pergunta abre, o ajudante vem logo atrás.

PerguntaAjudanteExemplo
Who (quem)is / areWho is your manager?
What (o que / qual)is / doWhat is your flight number? · What do you do?
Where (onde)is / doWhere is the gate?
When (quando)is / doesWhen does the flight arrive?
How (como)is / doHow do you spell your name?
Uma regra só: PERGUNTA + AJUDANTE + resto. O ajudante é is/are quando a frase já tem o verbo to be (“the gate is there” → “Where is the gate?”) e do/does quando o verbo é normal (“the flight arrives” → “When does the flight arrive?”). E repare: quando entra o does, o verbo perde o S — só um S por pergunta, e ele fica no does. Nunca “does the flight arrives”.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Where the gate is?"
"Where is the gate?"
"When the flight arrives?"
"When does the flight arrive?"

Os dois erros vêm do português. Em português a pergunta tem a mesma ordem da afirmação — “o portão está ali” vira “onde o portão está?”, e só a voz sobe no fim. Em inglês a pergunta vira a frase: o ajudante pula na frente. E quando o verbo é normal, alguém tem de trazer o ajudante — esse alguém é o does.

Chapter 5: The Airport

“How Can I Help You?”

Miami, seis da tarde. Ninguém traduz.

Dialogue

Alone at Miami International

A
Good evening. Welcome to Miami. How can I help you?
P
Hi. My flight is on Friday, and I don't know the gate. Where is the gate?
A
On Friday? Then nobody knows yet. The gate is on the screen, two hours before the departure. Today you just arrive — where do you sleep tonight?
P
I have a reservation at the hotel. How do I get there?
A
The hotel bus is outside, door number four. It's free. What is your name?
P
Paula Morales. M — O — R — A — L — E — S.
A
Thank you. You spell it slowly — I like that. Most people don't.
P
One more question. When does the flight to São Paulo arrive on Saturday?
A
At nine in the morning. Door number four, on your right. Have a good trip, Ms. Morales.
Comprehension

About the Agent

1. Where does the agent say the gate is?
On the screen, two hours before the departure — not on paper. On Friday morning, nobody knows it yet.
2. How does Paula get to the hotel? Is it expensive?
The hotel bus, outside, at door number four. It is free.
3. What does the agent ask Paula?
Two things: "Where do you sleep tonight?" and "What is your name?"
4. Does the agent say anything about Paula's English?
He says nothing about her English. He says he likes that she spells her name slowly — "most people don't".

3. Where is the gate? Look carefully.

4. A colleague asks: "What is your flight number?" Answer.
"My flight is AA 906, from São Paulo to Miami."
Listening

David Before the Trip

Sound first — no text. Leia as perguntas antes de ouvir.

0:000:00
1. What time is the departure? What time is the arrival in Miami?
The departure is at ten in the morning. The arrival in Miami is at six in the evening.
2. What does David say about the gate?
It is on the boarding pass, but it changes — so look at the screen, always.
3. What is David's only advice?
Ask the question. Don't build a beautiful sentence in your head first. "Where is the gate?" — four words, and it works.
Listening 2

Sofia Was Lost in New York

Sound first — no text. Leia as perguntas antes de ouvir.

0:000:00
1. Which part is easy, and which part is hard? Why?
The work is easy — Paula knows the accounts and the numbers, and in the Miami office everybody speaks slowly for her. The airport is the hard part, because she is alone.
2. What happened to Sofia in New York, twenty years ago?
She did not ask anything. She walked for one hour, lost, because she did not want to say a wrong sentence.
3. What does Sofia say nobody wants at an airport?
A beautiful sentence. They want a question. "Where is the gate?" — four words, and somebody points. Ask the question, and let it be ugly.

Role-Play

Guided — Five Questions at the Miami Office

Scenario: First morning at the Miami office. You meet a colleague from the commercial team, and you know nothing about this office — who runs it, what he does, when the meetings start. Ask him five questions. He answers, but he says nothing you don't ask. The words are right here on the screen — use them.

Keywords:

Who is the manager here? What do you do? Where is the meeting room? When does the meeting start? How do you spell your name?
Role-Play

Semi-free — The Agent Is in a Hurry

Scenario: Friday, Miami airport. You go home today. The agent talks fast, there is a line behind you, and nobody is going to translate. Find out where your gate is, what time the departure is, and when the flight arrives in São Paulo. One question is not on the screen — do not stop, do not go back to Portuguese. Use the rescue kit from Lesson 1 and keep the conversation alive.

Keywords:

Where is the gate? What time is the departure? When does the flight arrive? Can you repeat that, please?
Free Practice

Ten Questions — No Screen

Scenario: Ten questions, out loud, alone. Five for a broker meeting at Zurich, and five for an international hotel — the arrival, the reservation, the room, the way back to the airport. Use at least four different question words. No screen, no notes, no help — and no need to be perfect.

Two times: with the bank, then alone. The second one is the one you take to Miami.
