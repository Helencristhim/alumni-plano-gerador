Chapter 3: The Code

There is · there are

Say what exists: one or many

Discover the Grammar

Listen and Notice

"There is a reception on the first floor."

"There are two elevators near the door."

"There are many patients on this floor."

"There isn't a kitchen on this floor."

How manyAffirmative (+)Negative (-)
One (singular)There is a room.There isn't a room.
Two or more (plural)There are two rooms.There aren't two rooms.

One thing = there is. Many things = there are. Question: Are there two elevators? / Uma coisa: there is. Muitas: there are. Pergunta: Are there...?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"There is two rooms."

✓ RIGHT

"There are two rooms."

✗ WRONG

"There are two elevator."

✓ RIGHT

"There are two elevators."
