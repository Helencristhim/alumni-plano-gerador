Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We value assets. We write reports. The end." (no signposts)

✓ RIGHT

"First we value assets. Then we write reports. To sum up..."

✗ WRONG

"Let us move to the results."

✓ RIGHT

"Let us move on to the results."

✗ WRONG

(starts talking with no opening)

✓ RIGHT

"Today I will give a short overview of our work."

Open the talk, signpost each point, say "move on to", and close with "to sum up".

Chapter 4

A Talk in Action

A short presentation, fully signposted

Dialogue

Let Me Walk You Through

M
Good morning, everyone. Today I will give a short overview of how our firm values industrial assets.
E
Sounds clear. What is on the agenda?
M
There are three points on the agenda. First, let me walk you through our process on this slide.
E
That overview is very helpful. And the results?
M
Now let us move on to the results. I want to highlight one number: our reports are accurate within five percent.
E
Impressive. So what should we remember most?
M
The key takeaway is this: accuracy builds trust. To sum up, we deliver reliable reports our clients can depend on.
M
Let me wrap up here. Thank you for your time, and I am happy to take any questions.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How many points were on the agenda?
Three points -- the speaker signposted them in order.
2. Which number did the speaker highlight?
That their reports are accurate within five percent.
3. What was the key takeaway?
Accuracy builds trust -- they deliver reliable reports clients can depend on.
