Chapter 4: Say Less, Mean More

One Small Word

How ellipsis and substitution work

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Are you free?" — "I think so."

"I need a pen." — "I have one."

"I love coffee." — "So do I."

"I do not like tea." — "Neither do I."

Short wordReplacesExample
soa whole idea"Is it open?" — "I think so."
one / onesa noun"Which cake?" — "The small one."
do / dida verb phrase"I love it." — "So do I."
neither / eithera negative idea"I cannot go." — "Neither can I."

Leave out the words you can guess, and use one short word instead. After "so" and "neither" at the start, the verb comes before the subject: So do I, Neither can I.

Language Focus

What Does It Replace?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Is it open?" — "I think yes."

✓ RIGHT

"Is it open?" — "I think so."

✗ WRONG

"I love it." — "So am I."

✓ RIGHT

"I love it." — "So do I."

Use "so", not "yes", to replace a whole idea. In "So do I", match the auxiliary of the first verb: I love it (do) → So do I; I am happy (am) → So am I.

Dialogue

Coffee and a Chat

A
Hi Greg. I am just waiting for my coffee. This place is always so busy in the morning.
G
It really is! I come here every day. Honestly, I love their coffee.
A
So do I. It is the best in the neighborhood.
G
Are they open on Sundays? I can never remember.
A
I think so, but I am not completely sure.
G
By the way, I do not really enjoy the long line at the counter.
A
Neither do I. It moves so slowly on busy mornings.
G
I want a small snack too. Which one do you recommend?
A
The lemon one is great. Anyway, it was nice to chat. Have a good day!
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. How does she agree that she loves the coffee?
She says, "So do I."
2. Is the cafe open on Sundays, according to her?
She thinks so, but she is not completely sure.
3. Which snack does she recommend?
The lemon one.
