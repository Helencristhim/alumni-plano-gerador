Lesson 16 -- Saying More With Less

Grammar Video
How to use "so do I" and "neither do I" (Perfect English Grammar)

A short, clear lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to agree with a short answer instead of repeating the whole sentence. The teacher shows how "so do I" answers a positive idea and "neither do I" answers a negative one, and how the verb changes with each tense. Connection to Lesson 16: this is the heart of ellipsis and substitution -- saying more with less.

Tip: after each example, pause and say your own true version out loud: "So do I" or "Neither do I."

Conversation
How to Keep Conversations Flowing in Natural English (BBC Learning English)

A friendly live class on how to keep a conversation going without awkward silences, using short reactions and follow-up questions. It is spoken at a natural, comfortable pace, so it is a great way to hear real English cohesion in action. Connection to Lesson 16: listen for short answers and reactions that let the speakers say less but stay connected, just like your "so", "one" and "neither".

Tip: every time you hear a short reaction, pause and repeat it out loud as if you were in the conversation.

English Conversation Practice: Greetings & Small Talk for A2-B1 Learners (Easy English Hub)

A calm, well-organized video that walks through natural greetings and small talk, with simple examples you can repeat.
