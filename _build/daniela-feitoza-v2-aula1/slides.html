Chapter 3

The Introduction Rule

Present simple to say who you are and what you do

Introduction Discovery

Listen and Notice

I am an IT Manager at SPF Group.

I manage the IT department.

I am responsible for IT operations.

I enjoy working with technology.

Which sentences use "am" (to be)? Which use a present-simple verb?

Introduction Rule

To Be vs Present Simple

FormUseExample
I am + roleSay who you are (identity, job title)"I am an IT Manager."
I + verb (present simple)Say what you do (actions, routine)"I manage the team. I enjoy it."
based in + placeSay where you or your company are"I am based in Sao Paulo."
responsible for + areaSay what you take care of"I am responsible for IT operations."

Use am with I; use the plain verb for what you do: I manage, I enjoy, we distribute.

Common Mistake

Wrong Form vs Right Form

✗ WRONG

"I am manage the IT department."

✓ RIGHT

"I manage the IT department." (action = present simple)

✗ WRONG

"I am responsible of IT operations."

✓ RIGHT

"I am responsible for IT operations." (responsible FOR)

Use "I am" for your role; use a plain verb for what you do. The phrase is "responsible for".
