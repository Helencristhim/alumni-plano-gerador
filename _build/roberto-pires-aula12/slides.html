Chapter 3: If This, Then That

The First Conditional

"If it rains, I will drive slowly."

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"If it rains, I will drive slowly."

"If the tank is low, we will stop for fuel."

"I will follow the GPS if I get lost."

"If there is a traffic jam, we will be late."

PartStructureExample
Condition (if)If + present simpleIf it rains,
Resultwill + base verbI will drive slowly.
Order can switchwill-part + if-partI will stop if the tank is low.
No comma if if is secondresult + if + presentWe will be late if there is a jam.

After if, use the present simple (it rains, the tank is low). In the other part, use will + verb (I will drive). We use it for real, possible situations on the road.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"If it will rain, I will drive slowly."

✓ RIGHT

"If it rains, I will drive slowly."

✗ WRONG

"If the tank will be low, we stop."

✓ RIGHT

"If the tank is low, we will stop."

Chapter 4: At the Rental Desk

Picking Up the Car

Roberto rents a car for his road trip

Dialogue

Roberto Rents a Car

R
Good morning. I have a reservation. I would like to pick up my rental car, please.
A
Of course. Here are the keys. If you return the car with a full tank, you will not pay an extra fee.
R
Good to know. Does the price include insurance?
A
Yes, full insurance is included. If anything happens on the highway, just call this number.
R
Perfect. And if I get lost, will the navigation work in the mountains?
A
It will. Set your route before you start. If the signal drops, the map still guides you. Safe travels!
