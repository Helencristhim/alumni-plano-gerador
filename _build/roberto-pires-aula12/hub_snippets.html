Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia a situacao e responda as perguntas.

Roberto goes to the rental desk to pick up his rental car. The agent gives him the keys and says: "If you return the car with a full tank, you will not pay an extra fee." Roberto asks about the insurance, and the agent confirms it is included. Then he sets his route on the navigation. "If I get lost, the GPS will guide me," he thinks. On the highway, he tells himself: "If there is a traffic jam, I will stay calm and follow the map."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

Grammar

Usamos o first conditional para falar de situacoes reais e possiveis no futuro. Depois de "if", use o present simple (it rains, the tank is low). Na outra parte, use "will + verbo" (I will drive). Nunca use "will" logo depois de "if". A ordem pode mudar: se "if" vem primeiro, use virgula; se vem depois, nao precisa de virgula. / We use the first conditional for real, possible future situations. After "if", use the present simple (it rains, the tank is low). In the other part, use "will + verb" (I will drive). Never use "will" right after "if". The order can switch: if "if" comes first, use a comma; if it comes second, no comma is needed.

Parte / PartEnglishPortugues
If + present simpleIf it rains,Se chover,
will + verb (result)I will drive slowly.eu vou dirigir devagar.
Negative resultIf I return a full tank, I will not pay a fee.Se eu devolver o tanque cheio, nao vou pagar taxa.
Order switched (no comma)I will stop if the tank is low.Eu vou parar se o tanque estiver baixo.
QuestionWill the navigation work if I get lost?A navegacao vai funcionar se eu me perder?
