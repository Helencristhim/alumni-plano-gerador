Chapter 3 -- Polite Requests

Find Attached & Grateful

Point to a file, then ask politely in writing

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Which phrase points to a file, and which one makes a polite request?

"Please find attached the supplier report."

"I would be grateful if you could send your comments by Thursday."

"I would be grateful if you could reply by Thursday."

"Please find attached the agenda. Best regards, Percival."

Grammar Rule

Point to a File & Ask Politely

Use it forPatternExample
Point to a filePlease find attached + the filePlease find attached the report.
Ask politelyI would be grateful if you could + verbI would be grateful if you could reply.
Ask politelyI would be grateful if you could + verbI would be grateful if you could confirm.
Close the emailBest regards, + your nameBest regards, Percival

Key idea: use Please find attached + the file to point to an attachment (Please find attached the report), and I would be grateful if you could + the base verb to ask politely (I would be grateful if you could reply). Close with Best regards + your name.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Please find in attach the report. I would be grateful if you could to confirm."
"Please find attached the report. I would be grateful if you could confirm."

We write Please find attached (not "find in attach"), and after if you could we use the base verb: confirm, send, reply -- no "to" and no "-ing".

Artefact

The Follow-Up Email

From: Percival JR -- Risk Director

To: Team   |   CC: Daniel

Subject: Risk Review, Next Steps

Dear team,

Thank you all for your time in today's meeting.

As a quick recap, we agreed to update the supplier list and to test the new control by Friday. The action items are below:

Update the supplier listOwner: Karen
Test the new controlOwner: Daniel

Please find attached the supplier report for your reference. I would be grateful if you could send your comments by Thursday.

Best regards,
Percival JR

Dialogue

Checking the Draft

P

"Hi Karen, I wrote a draft of the follow-up email. The subject is Risk Review, Next Steps. Can you check it before I send it?"

K

"Of course. The greeting and the recap look great. Did you attach the supplier report?"

P

"Yes. I wrote: Please find attached the report. And I added the line I would be grateful if you could send your comments by Thursday."

K

"Perfect. One small thing: please add Daniel in CC, so he can see it too. Then you are ready to send."

P

"Done. I added Daniel in CC. Should I also forward it to my manager?"

K

"Yes, please forward it to her. The email is clear and short. Great work, Percival. You can send it now."
