Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. São as palavras que você já usa em português todo dia — agora em inglês, no registro que o time canadense usa.

Organizational culture -- the shared values and behaviors that decide how work really happens (cultura organizacional)
"Organizational culture is what people do when nobody is measuring them."
Employee journey -- every stage a person goes through, from the first interview to the last day (jornada do colaborador)
"The employee journey starts long before the first day of work."
People strategy -- the long term plan for how a company attracts, grows and keeps its people (estratégia de pessoas)
"Our people strategy has three pillars, and culture is the first one."
Framework -- a structured model you use to organize and explain your approach (modelo estruturado, framework)
"I don't bring answers, I bring a framework."
Stakeholder -- anyone with a real interest in the outcome of your work (parte interessada)
"Every stakeholder wants to know what changes for them."
Alignment -- the point where leadership, strategy and culture pull in the same direction (alinhamento)
"Alignment is when leadership says one thing and the team lives the same thing."
To roll out -- to launch something across a whole organization, step by step (implantar, lançar em escala)
"We're rolling out the new employee journey across every region."
Headquarters -- the main office, where the senior decisions are made (matriz, sede)
"Headquarters owns the framework, but each market owns its own culture."
Franchisor -- the company that licenses its brand and its model to local operators (franqueadora)
"Maple Bear is a franchisor: we license the model, we don't run the schools."
Bilingual education -- teaching a full curriculum in two languages (educação bilíngue)
"We work in bilingual education, from kindergarten to high school."
Hybrid model -- a way of working that mixes days at the office and days at home (modelo híbrido)
"We're still learning how to build culture inside a hybrid model."
Senior leadership -- the executives who set the direction and own the decisions (alta liderança)
"Senior leadership sets the direction, but the middle managers set the tone."
To make headway -- to make real progress on something difficult (avançar de verdade, progredir)
"We're making headway on the hybrid model."
To take a stance -- to state a clear position and defend it (tomar uma posição)
"Take a stance, but leave room for their input."
To carry out -- to conduct something you planned: research, an assessment, a review (conduzir, realizar)
"Before I propose anything, I want to carry out a cultural assessment."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Danielle leads People and Culture at Maple Bear, a franchisor in bilingual education. Her role involves the employee journey — every stage, from the first interview to the last day — and the people strategy behind it. She works closely with senior leadership, and she reports to the Chief People Officer at headquarters. "I have been leading the culture transformation since 2023," she says, "and I have been working in People and Culture for eight years." She has carried out four cultural assessments, and she has built a framework that every stakeholder can read. Right now the picture is different: at the moment, her team is rolling out the new employee journey across every region, and this year she is preparing to take on Canada. They are making headway on the hybrid model, although alignment between leadership and the schools is still the hardest part. In August she will meet the Canadian academic team — and, before she proposes anything, she wants to carry out a cultural assessment and take a stance of her own.

1. Por que dizemos "I have been leading the culture transformation since 2023" e não "I am leading it since 2023"?
A Porque começou no passado e continua até agora — present perfect continuous com since.
B Porque a ação terminou e não tem mais ligação com o presente.
C Porque é uma rotina permanente do cargo dela.
2. "At the moment, her team is rolling out the new employee journey" usa present continuous porque:
A É uma responsabilidade permanente do cargo.
B É algo em andamento agora, temporário — não o estado permanente das coisas.
C É um resultado já concluído e contado.
3. Which sentence about her permanent role is correct?
A "She is leading People and Culture since eight years."
B "She leads People and Culture, and her role involves the employee journey."
C "She has been leading People and Culture in 2023."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous vs Present Perfect (Continuous)

GRAMMAR

As três formas que carregam a sua história profissional — e que o time canadense vai ouvir nos seus primeiros dois minutos (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb (she + s)		Cargo permanente e o que ele abrange. Permanent role and what it involves.I lead People and Culture.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + -ing		Em andamento agora, temporário. In progress right now (at the moment, this year).We are rolling out the framework.
Present Perfect Continuous
have been + -ing		Começou no passado e continua — com for / since. Started in the past, still going.I have been leading it since 2023.
Present Perfect
have + past participle		Resultado concluído e contável, sem tempo dito. A finished, countable result.I have carried out four assessments.
Negativahave / has + not + particípio. Na fala, contraído: haven't.We haven't rolled it out in Canada yet.
InterrogativaHow long + have + sujeito + been + -ing?How long have you been leading this?
for vs sincefor + período (for eight years) · since + ponto de partida (since 2023)

Atenção (erro de brasileiro sênior): em português dizemos "lidero essa transformação desde 2023" com o presente. Em inglês, duração até agora nunca usa present simple nem present continuous: "I am leading this transformation since 2023" está errado — o certo é "I have been leading this transformation since 2023" (na fala, I've been leading). Nada faz você soar júnior mais rápido do que esse deslize.

Collocation (o outro deslize): em inglês não se diz "make a research" nem "make an assessment" — diz-se carry out research / carry out an assessment. E não é "responsible by", é responsible for.

You want to say you will run a cultural diagnosis before proposing anything. The natural collocation is:
