Chapter 3: The Code

Your Story in Three Time Frames

I lead · we're rolling out · I've been leading

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I lead People and Culture at Maple Bear."

"At the moment, we are rolling out the new employee journey."

"I have been leading the culture transformation since 2023."

"I have carried out four cultural assessments."

One is permanent. One is happening right now. Two connect your past to this exact moment — but not in the same way.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb		Your permanent role and what it involvesI lead People and Culture.
Present Continuous
am / are + -ing		In progress right now, temporary (at the moment, this year)We are rolling out the framework.
Present Perfect Continuous
have been + -ing		Started in the past, still going — with for / sinceI have been leading it since 2023.
Present Perfect
have + past participle		A finished result you can count — no time saidI have carried out four assessments.

for + a period (for eight years) · since + a starting point (since 2023).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am leading this transformation since 2023."
"I've been leading this transformation since 2023."
"I work in People and Culture for eight years."
"I've been working in People and Culture for eight years."

Duration up to now takes have been + -ing with for / since. The present simple and the present continuous cannot carry it — that is a direct translation from Portuguese, and it is the fastest way to sound junior.
