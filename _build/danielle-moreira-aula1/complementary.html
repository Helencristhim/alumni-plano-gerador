Complementary Materials

Content to reinforce your learning outside class. Check it off when you finish.

Documentary
American Factory (Netflix)

A Chinese company takes over an American factory — and two organizational cultures collide in real time. This is literally your job, filmed: cultural artifacts, leadership behavior, and what gets lost when a model is imported with no diagnosis. Connection to Lesson 1: watch how each leader introduces and positions themselves — who sounds senior, who sounds defensive, and why.

Tip: watch with English subtitles only. Write down five organizational-culture terms in English that you would still say in your own language.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast — "The Leadership Skills That Make Transformation Stick"

Authentic executive vocabulary about exactly what you do: why culture transformation dies, and what senior leadership has to sustain for it to stick. Connection to Lesson 1: every word of this lesson (organizational culture, alignment, senior leadership, roll out, framework, stakeholder) shows up in native context and at real speed.

Tip: listen once, with no pauses. Then summarize the episode out loud in sixty seconds — record it and listen back. Use at least three words from the lesson.

TED Talk
Julian Treasure — "How to Speak So That People Want to Listen" (TED)

Ten minutes on the mechanics of positioning: register, pace, timbre, and the seven habits that erode a speaker's authority. It is the “how” half of what this lesson worked on as the “what”. Connection to Lesson 1: you will hear, in native English, the difference between communicating and positioning yourself — which is the reason this programme exists.

Tip: after the video, record your ninety-second executive introduction again — this time paying attention to pace and pauses, not only to the words.
