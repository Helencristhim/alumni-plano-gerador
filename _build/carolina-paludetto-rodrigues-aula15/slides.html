Grammar Rule

The Superlative

TypeRuleExample
Short adjectivethe + adj + esthard → the hardest
Short + double letterthe + double + estbig → the biggest
Adjective in -ythe + y → iesteasy → the easiest
Long adjectivethe most + adjthe most interesting
Irregularspecial formgood → the best, bad → the worst

Key idea: use the superlative to choose the TOP one of a group. Short adjectives use the ...-est (the hardest, the biggest, the easiest). Long adjectives use the most (the most interesting, the most helpful). Two adjectives are irregular: good → the best, bad → the worst. Always put the before the superlative.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"English is the most easiest subject." / "History is the interestingest."
"English is the easiest subject." / "History is the most interesting."

Use the ...-est OR the most, never both. Short adjectives take -est (the easiest, the hardest). Long adjectives take the most (the most interesting). Never forget the.
