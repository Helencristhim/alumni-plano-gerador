Lesson 8 -- Remote Team Management

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 4, Episode 1: "Fun Run"

Watch how the team shares updates and asks each other for help. Notice when a request sounds direct and a bit rude, and when it sounds polite and collaborative.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two direct requests and rewrite them as polite async messages using "Could you ..." or "Would you mind ...-ing".

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Making Polite Requests in English at Work"

A clear episode on softening requests with please, could you, and would you mind, so your written messages sound professional and friendly across a remote team.

Record a 90-second async update for your team: what is done, one handoff step, one blocker with a heads-up, and two polite requests.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Polite requests: could you, would you mind"

A focused lesson on the difference between imperatives and polite requests, including the -ing form after "would you mind". Directly reinforces today's grammar discovery.

After watching, write six short async messages about your project, mixing direct steps ("Please deploy ...") and polite requests ("Could you ...", "Would you mind ...-ing"). Read them aloud.
