Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Imperatives and polite requests

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The orange words all ask for a task. Which is direct? Which is polite?

"Send me a heads-up before you log off."

"Please review the thread when you have a moment."

"Could you share your availability for tomorrow?"

"Would you mind looping in Marta on this?"

Grammar Rule

Imperatives + Polite Requests

LevelStructureExample
Direct stepVerb + objectSend the file.
SoftenedPlease + verbPlease send the file when you can.
Polite requestCould you + verb?Could you send the file?
Very politeWould you mind + verb-ing?Would you mind sending the file?

Key idea: Use a clear imperative for steps and checklists. To ask a person for help, soften it: Please, Could you, or Would you mind + verb-ing. After "would you mind", always use the -ing form.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Reply now. I need this immediately."
"Could you reply by the end of your day? No rush before then."

In async writing, drop demanding words like now and immediately. Ask politely with could you and give a clear, fair deadline.
