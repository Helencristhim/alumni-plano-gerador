Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

Rafael arrives at his hotel in New York. He goes to the front desk and says, "I have a reservation under Gasparelli." The guest service is fast. "How many nights are you staying?" the receptionist asks. "Three nights," he says. She gives him his key card. Later, at the restaurant, the waiter brings the menu. "Is there any grilled fish today?" Rafael asks. "Yes, and we have some fresh salad too." He decides to order the fish. At the end, he asks, "How much is the bill?" and leaves a small tip.

1. Where does Rafael show his reservation?
A At the front desk.
B At the airport gate.
C On the plane.
2. Which question asks about a countable noun (nights)?
A "How much is the bill?"
B "How many nights are you staying?"
C "Is there any fish?"
3. What does Rafael do at the end of the meal?
A He cooks the dinner.
B He asks for the bill and leaves a tip.
C He cancels the reservation.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Some / Any & How much / How many

Grammar

Para pedir coisas, você precisa saber se o substantivo é contável (countável: one room, two nights) ou incontável (uncountable: water, coffee, money). Use some em frases afirmativas e any em perguntas e negativas. Para perguntar quantidade, use How many com contáveis e How much com incontáveis. / Use some in positive sentences and any in questions and negatives. Use How many with countable nouns and How much with uncountable nouns.

Tipo / TypeEnglishPortuguês
some (+ frase)I would like some water.Eu gostaria de um pouco de água.
any (? / negativo)Is there any coffee?Tem café?
How many (contável)How many nights are you staying?Quantas noites você vai ficar?
How much (incontável)How much is the room?Quanto custa o quarto?
any (negativo)There isn't any sugar.Não tem açúcar.
