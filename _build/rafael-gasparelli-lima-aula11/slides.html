Chapter 3: How Much / How Many

Some, Any & Quantities

The tools to ask for anything

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I would like some water."

"Is there any coffee?"

"How many nights are you staying?"

"How much is the room?"

WordWhen to useExample
somepositive sentencesI would like some water.
anyquestions & negativesIs there any coffee?
how manycountable nouns (nights, rooms)How many nights?
how muchuncountable nouns (water, money)How much is the room?

If you can count it (one, two, three), use how many. If you cannot count it, use how much.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"How much nights?"

✓ RIGHT

"How many nights?"

✗ WRONG

"I would like any water."

✓ RIGHT

"I would like some water."

Chapter 4: At the Front Desk

Rafael Checks In

Arriving at the Hudson Grand Hotel

Dialogue

Check-in at the Front Desk

R
Good evening. I have a reservation under Gasparelli.
A
Welcome, Mr. Gasparelli. How many nights are you staying with us?
R
Three nights. Is there any breakfast included?
A
Yes, breakfast is free for all guests, from six thirty to ten. Here is your key card for room 502.
R
Thank you. And how much is the room per night?
A
It is 180 dollars per night. Enjoy your stay, Mr. Gasparelli!
Listening

The Hotel Welcome

Listen first. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What time does breakfast start and end?
From six thirty to ten in the morning.
2. Where is the business center?
On the second floor, with printers and meeting rooms.
3. What number do you dial for help, and what time is check-out?
Dial zero from your room; check-out is twelve noon.
