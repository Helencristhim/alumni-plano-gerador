Grammar Rule

Health Patterns

PatternUseExample
I have a + problemsay a health problemI have a fever. I have a cough.
My + body part + hurtssay a part that is painfulMy head hurts. My arm hurts.
What is wrong?the doctor asksWhat is wrong? -- My throat hurts.

Key idea: use I have a for a problem (a fever, a cough), and My ... hurts for a part of the body. We say "My head hurts" (never "My head is hurt" or "I am fever").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am fever."
"I have a fever."

For a health problem, use I have a. Say "I have a fever", "I have a cough", "I have a headache".
