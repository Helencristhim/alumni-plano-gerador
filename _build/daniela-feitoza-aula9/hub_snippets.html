Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Vowel -- a speech sound made with an open mouth, like a or e (PT: vogal)
"English has many vowel sounds."
Consonant -- a sound made by blocking the air, like b or t (PT: consoante)
"The word stop starts with two consonants."
Voiced -- a sound with vibration in your throat, like the z in buzz (PT: sonoro)
"The b in big is a voiced sound."
Voiceless -- a sound with no throat vibration, just air, like the s in bus (PT: surdo)
"The p in stop is a voiceless sound."
Silent letter -- a letter you write but do not say, like the b in climb (PT: letra muda)
"The k in know is a silent letter."
Tongue -- the soft part inside your mouth that moves when you talk (PT: língua)
"For the th sound, your tongue goes between your teeth."
Minimal pair -- two words that differ by only one sound, like ship and sheep (PT: par mínimo)
"Live and leave are a minimal pair."
Mispronounce -- to say a word incorrectly (PT: pronunciar errado)
"It is normal to mispronounce a new word."
Tongue twister -- a sentence that is difficult to say quickly (PT: trava-língua)
"She sells seashells is a tongue twister."
Accent -- the special way someone pronounces a language; natural, not a mistake (PT: sotaque)
"You can keep your accent and still be clear."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — The -ED Ending

GRAMMAR

Os três sons da terminação -ed do passado (bilingue EN + PT).

Last sound-ed sounds likeExample
Voiceless (p, k, s, sh, ch, f)/t/ — no extra syllableworked, finished, stopped
Voiced (vowels, b, g, l, n, v)/d/ — no extra syllableplayed, lived, called
t or d/id/ — one extra syllablewanted, needed, started
Regra (PT)A terminação -ed quase nunca é uma sílaba extra. Só verbos terminados em t ou d ganham a sílaba /id/. Nos outros casos, é só um som rápido /t/ ou /d/ — sem vogal no final.

Short, friendly episodes (3 to 5 minutes) with clear models of difficult sounds. Perfect for practicing the th sound and minimal pairs like ship and sheep at your own pace.

Dica: ouça um episódio e repita cada frase em voz alta logo depois (shadowing).

Clear tutorials that show exactly where the tongue goes for the th sound and how the three -ed endings work. Maps directly onto Lesson 9, with slow-motion mouth views.
