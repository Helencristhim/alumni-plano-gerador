Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Polite request and confirmation language

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange phrases have in common?

"I am writing to confirm the contract terms." (opening)

"I would be grateful if you could confirm the effective date." (polite request)

"Please find enclosed the revised price table." (enclosing)

"I look forward to working with you." (closing)

Grammar Rule

The Polite Email Map

MoveFunctionExample
Opensay why you write"I am writing to confirm the terms."
Requestask politely"I would be grateful if you could confirm..." / "Would you kindly send..."
Enclosepresent an attachment"Please find enclosed the price table."
Closeend politely"I look forward to your reply. Best regards,"

Key idea: after could, would and kindly, use the base verb (could confirm, would send). After look forward to, use -ing or a noun (look forward to working / to your reply), because here "to" is a preposition, not part of the verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I look forward to hear from you."
"I look forward to hearing from you."

After look forward to, use the -ing form. After could, would and kindly, use the base verb.

ToClara Mendes, Trading Desk
FromWalyson Silva, Sucafina
SubjectConfirmation of Coffee Contract Terms

Dear Clara,

Further to our last call, I am writing to confirm the coffee contract terms.

I would be grateful if you could confirm the effective date and the delivery week. As per our agreement, delivery is in the first week of March.

I acknowledge receipt of the revised price table and I accept the amendment to clause two. To answer your clarification: yes, the warranty still covers the premium grade.

Please find enclosed the countersigned contract. I await your reply at your earliest convenience, and I look forward to working with you.

Best regards,
Walyson Silva
