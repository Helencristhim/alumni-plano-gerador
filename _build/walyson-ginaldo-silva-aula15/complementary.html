Lesson 15 -- Writing a Formal Email

Series
Suits -- an email or letter-writing scene (any season)

Watch a scene where a character sends a formal business email or letter to confirm terms. Notice how they open, make polite requests, and sign off.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one opening line ("I am writing to...") and one polite request you hear.

Podcast
Plain English -- "How to Write a Professional Email"

A practical episode on the structure of a formal business email: the opening line, polite requests, enclosing documents, and a professional sign-off.

Record three formal email lines: one opening ("I am writing to..."), one request ("Would you kindly..."), and one close ("I look forward to...").

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Polite requests in formal English"

A focused lesson on polite request language: would you, could you, I would be grateful if you could, and kindly. It directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, write six formal email sentences: two requests, two confirmations, and two sign-offs.
