Aula 15 -- Pre-class

Writing a Formal Email -- Confirming the Coffee Contract Terms

Writing a polite, formal email to request and confirm contract terms in writing. Learning to open ("I am writing to confirm..."), make polite requests ("I would be grateful if you could...", "Would you kindly..."), enclose documents ("please find enclosed"), and close formally ("I look forward to..."). New vocabulary: enclose, kindly, further to, revised, amendment, clarification, await, look forward to, as per, acknowledge receipt, at your earliest convenience, sign-off.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Enclose -- to attach or include a document with your email or letter
"Please find enclosed the revised price table." (Anexar / Incluir)
Kindly -- a polite, formal way to say "please" in a request
"Would you kindly confirm the effective date?" (Por gentileza / Por favor)
Further to -- following up on; referring back to an earlier message or call
"Further to our last call, I am writing to confirm the terms." (Em referência a / Dando seguimento a)
Revised -- changed and corrected; a newer, updated version
"I attach the revised contract with the new price." (Revisado / Atualizado)
Amendment -- a formal change made to a clause of a contract
"Do you accept the amendment to clause two?" (Alteração / Emenda)
Clarification -- a clear answer that removes doubt about a point
"I need one clarification about the warranty." (Esclarecimento)
Await -- to wait for; a formal word used in business writing
"I await your written reply." (Aguardar)
Look forward to -- to feel happy about something that will happen; a polite closing
"I look forward to closing this deal with you." (Aguardar com expectativa / Estar ansioso por)
As per -- according to; in line with what was agreed
"As per our agreement, delivery is in the first week." (Conforme / De acordo com)
Acknowledge receipt -- to confirm in writing that you received a document
"I acknowledge receipt of the revised price table." (Confirmar o recebimento)
At your earliest convenience -- as soon as you can; a polite way to ask for a quick reply
"Please reply at your earliest convenience." (Assim que possível / Na primeira oportunidade)
Sign-off -- the polite closing line of an email, like "Best regards"
"A formal sign-off is 'Best regards, Walyson'." (Despedida / Fecho do e-mail)
Countersign -- to sign a document that the other party has already signed
"Kindly countersign the contract and send it back." (Assinar também / Contra-assinar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the email and answer the questions.

Dear Clara,

Further to our last call, I am writing to confirm the coffee contract terms. I would be grateful if you could confirm the effective date and the delivery week. Please find enclosed the revised price table; I acknowledge receipt of your amendment to clause two and I accept it. Would you kindly send one clarification about the warranty? As per our agreement, I enclose the countersigned contract. I await your reply at your earliest convenience, and I look forward to working with you.

Best regards,
Walyson

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite request and confirmation language

GRAMMAR

Em e-mails formais, pedimos e confirmamos com frases corteses, não com ordens diretas. / In formal emails, we request and confirm with polite phrases, not direct orders.

Function / FunçãoFormal phrase / Frase formalUse / Uso
Open / AbrirI am writing to confirm the terms.diz por que você escreve (escrevo para...)
Request / PedirI would be grateful if you could confirm... / Would you kindly send...pedido corts (eu agradeceria se / poderia, por gentileza)
Enclose / AnexarPlease find enclosed the price table.apresenta um anexo (segue em anexo)
Close / FecharI look forward to your reply. Best regards,despedida corts (aguardo / atenciosamente)

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de could, would e kindly usamos o verbo base (could confirm, would send). Após look forward to usamos -ing ou um substantivo (look forward to working / to your reply), pois aqui "to" é preposição. / After could, would and kindly we use the base verb. After look forward to we use -ing or a noun, because here "to" is a preposition.

Order
