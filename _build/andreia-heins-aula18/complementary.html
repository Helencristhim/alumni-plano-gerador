Lesson 18 -- Negotiating Solutions

Series
Suits -- any episode where two sides reach a settlement

Watch how each side states a position, makes an offer, gives a concession, and asks for something in return until they reach a deal both sides can live with. A great model for trade-offs and counteroffers.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two offers and two trade-offs. Notice when a character says "If you did this, we would do that" -- that is the second conditional in action.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Negotiate So Everyone Wins"

The hosts explain how to negotiate the professional way: state your position, make an offer, trade one concession for another, and aim for a mutual win instead of accepting the first number.

Note three tips for reaching a fair deal. Then record a one-minute negotiation opener using at least four new words from today, such as offer, concession, trade-off and mutual.

YouTube
Business English -- "The Second Conditional for Negotiating"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to make offers and trade-offs with the second conditional -- "If you gave us thirty days, we would sign today" -- and how to ask for a concession in return.

After watching, write five sentences for a real situation at work: two offers with the second conditional, one trade-off, one counteroffer, and one sentence that proposes a mutual agreement. Then say them out loud.
