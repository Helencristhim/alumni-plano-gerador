Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Negotiate -- to talk with someone to reach an agreement both sides accept
"We negotiate the terms of the deal." (Negociar)
Offer -- something you propose to give or do in a deal
"Let me make you an offer." (Oferta)
Concession -- something you give up so the other side gains
"I am ready to make one concession on the date." (Concessão)
Trade-off -- when you give one thing in order to get another
"Let me offer a trade-off: a longer warranty for full payment." (Troca / contrapartida)
Term -- one of the conditions in a deal, such as price or date
"The terms are still flexible." (Termo / condição)
Flexible -- able to change your terms or plans easily
"Thank you for your flexibility; the terms are flexible." (Flexível)
Counteroffer -- a different offer you make in reply to theirs
"Listen for their counteroffer before you decide." (Contraproposta)
Deal -- an agreement where both sides accept the terms
"I am confident we can reach a deal today." (Acordo / negócio)
Mutual -- shared by both sides; good for everyone in the deal
"That feels like a mutual win." (Mútuo / recíproco)
Agreement -- the final deal that both sides accept and sign
"Shall we draft an agreement along these lines?" (Acordo / contrato)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia must negotiate a fair deal for the replacement console. The supplier wants full payment now, but the terms are still flexible. First she makes an offer using the second conditional: "If you gave us thirty days, we would sign today." Note: the if part uses the past simple (gave), and the result uses would + base verb (would sign) -- the situation is imagined, a soft way to propose. Then she proposes a trade-off: "If we paid up front, we would ask for a longer warranty." She listens for the supplier's counteroffer. She is ready to make one concession on the date if they make one on price. The goal is a mutual win. If both sides agreed, they would draft an agreement everyone can live with.

1. Andreia says "If you gave us thirty days, we would sign today." Why "gave", not "give"?
A In the second conditional, the if part uses the past simple.
B Because it already happened in the past.
C Both "give" and "gave" are correct here.
2. In the result part of a second conditional, which form do we use?
A Will + base verb: "we will sign today."
B Would + base verb: "we would sign today."
C The past simple: "we signed today."
3. What is the goal of Andreia's negotiation?
A A mutual win -- a deal good for both sides, with a trade-off and a concession.
B To win everything and give nothing in return.
C To accept the supplier's first number without question.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como fazer uma oferta ou troca imaginada numa negociação. / How to make an imagined offer or trade-off in a negotiation.

Parte / PartForma / FormExemplo / Example
Condição (if)If + passado simplesIf you gave us thirty days,
Resultadowould + verbo basewe would sign today.
Ordem invertidaresultado + if + passadoWe would sign if you gave us time.
Usooferta / troca imaginadaIf we paid up front, we would ask for more.

Atenção / Note: O second conditional fala de uma situação imaginada ou improvável. A parte do if usa o passado simples (If you offered...) e o resultado usa would + verbo base (we would accept...). É um jeito educado e suave de fazer uma oferta. Compare: first conditional = real/provável (If they confirm, we will sign); second conditional = imaginado (If they gave us time, we would sign). / The second conditional uses the past simple in the if part and would + base verb in the result, for an imagined offer.
