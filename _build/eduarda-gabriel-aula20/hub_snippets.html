Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key format-choice terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Audience -- the people who will read your writing
"Always start from your audience: who will read this?"
Purpose -- the reason you are writing
"What is the purpose: to ask, inform, or record?"
Format -- the type of document (email, memo, minutes...)
"Choose the format before you write a word."
Register -- the level of formality in the language
"A client needs a formal register; the team, a neutral one."
Tone -- the attitude or feeling of the message
"Match the tone to the reader: warm, not blunt."
Tailor -- to adjust the writing to a specific reader
"Tailor the length and tone to your audience."
Proofread -- to check carefully for errors before sending
"Proofread the text before you send it."
Revise -- to improve or rewrite after checking
"Revise it once before you circulate it."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduarda has built a writing portfolio: emails, memos, minutes, and status reports. The real skill is choosing the right format. She always starts from two questions: who is the audience, and what is the purpose? Then she decides whether to send an email or a memo, and she knows which format to use for a meeting record or a stakeholder update. The format sets the register: formal for a client, neutral for the team. She tailors the tone to each reader, and she knows when to write at all. Finally, she always proofreads the text and revises it once before sending.

1. After "decide", how does Eduarda talk about a yes/no choice of format?
A "decide whether she should send an email"
B "decide whether to send an email"
C "decide whether to sending an email"
2. What two questions does she ask first?
A Who is the audience, and what is the purpose.
B How long, and what color.
C When to send, and where to save.
3. What are her last two steps before sending?
A Print it and file it.
B Proofread it, then revise it once.
C Forward it and forget it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

After verbs like decide, know, ask, be sure, use a question word + to + verb to talk about a decision: whether to (a yes/no choice), which (+ noun) to (a choice of option), when / how to. No subject, no "should". Depois de decide/know/ask, use uma question word (whether/which/when/how) + TO + verbo base: "decide whether to send an email", "which format to use".

DecisionStructureExample
Yes / nowhether to + verb"Decide whether to send an email."
Choice of optionwhich (+ noun) to + verb"which format to use"
Time / waywhen / how to + verb"when to write a report"

Common Mistake:

"I must decide whether I should send an email or which format I should use."

"I must decide whether to send an email and which format to use."

Depois da question word, use so "to + verbo" — sem sujeito, sem "should".
