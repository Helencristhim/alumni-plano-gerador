Chapter 3 — The Choice

Whether To

Look at the purple words. How do we talk about a decision between formats?

Decide whether to send an email or a memo.
I am not sure which format to use.
She knows when to write a status report.
Ask yourself whether to write at all.
DecisionStructureExample
Yes / no choicewhether to + verb"Decide whether to send an email."
Choice of optionwhich (+ noun) to + verb"which format to use"
Choice of timewhen to + verb"when to write a status report"
After: decide, know, ask, be surequestion word + to + verb"I know how to format it."
Grammar Rule

Question Word + To

Longer (with should)Tighter (question word + to)
"Decide if you should send an email.""Decide whether to send an email."
"I do not know which format I should use.""I do not know which format to use."
"She knows when she should report.""She knows when to report."
"Ask yourself if you should write at all.""Ask yourself whether to write at all."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I must decide whether I should send an email or which format I should use."
"I must decide whether to send an email and which format to use."

After a question word, just use to + verb — no subject, no "should".

Dialogue

The Format Choice

J
Eduarda, the client wants the numbers and the team needs a record. How do you decide the format?
E
I start from the audience and the purpose. For the client, I decide whether to send an email.
J
And for the team record?
E
A memo, or minutes if it records a meeting. I know which format to use for each purpose.
J
Good. And the register?

Formal for the client, neutral for the team. I tailor the tone to each reader.
J
Excellent. One last step before you send anything?
E
I always proofread it, then revise once before it goes out.

Artifact

The Format Decision Guide

Step 1: Audience & Purpose
Step 2: Choose the Format
Step 3: Register, Tone, Polish

One client, one ask → Email (formal register, softened request).

Inform the team, recommend → Memo (headline, bullet points, concise).

Record a meeting → Minutes (attendees, resolutions, reported in the past).

Update stakeholders → Status report (present perfect, blockers, next steps).

Always last: tailor the tone, proofread, and revise once before sending.
