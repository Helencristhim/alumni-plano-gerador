Chapter 3

The Trend Code

Higher than · Lower than · Increased · Decreased

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Revenue is higher than last year."

"Costs are lower than the budget."

"Revenue increased 12 percent."

"We have more growth this year."

UseStructureExample
Compare upadjective + -er + thanRevenue is higher than last year.
Compare downlower thanCosts are lower than the budget.
A changeincreased / decreasedRevenue increased 12 percent.
More quantitymore + noun — We have more growth this year.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Revenue is more high than last year."

✓ RIGHT

"Revenue is higher than last year."

✗ WRONG

"Costs are lower that the budget."

✓ RIGHT

"Costs are lower than the budget."

✗ WRONG

"Revenue increase 12 percent."

✓ RIGHT

"Revenue increased 12 percent."

To compare, use the -er form with than: higher than, lower than (never "more high"). For a change that happened, add -d: increased, decreased.
