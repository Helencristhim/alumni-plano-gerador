Aula 14 -- Pre-class

Closing the Deal -- Confirming Agreements

Close a deal, confirm what was agreed, and report what people said with reported speech (said / told + backshift: is→was, will→would). New vocabulary: finalize, sign off, commitment, contract, paperwork, on the same page, going forward, deal-breaker, formalize, kick off.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Finalize -- to complete the last details and make something final
"Let's finalize the contract this week." (Finalizar)
Sign off -- to give formal approval to a document or decision
"Procurement will sign off on the contract by Friday." (Aprovar formalmente)
Commitment -- a serious promise or obligation to do something
"We made a clear commitment to deliver on time." (Compromisso)
Contract -- a written legal agreement between two or more parties
"Both sides signed the contract yesterday." (Contrato)
Paperwork -- the documents and forms you must complete for something official
"The paperwork was almost ready." (Papelada / documentação)
On the same page -- in agreement, understanding a situation the same way
"Let's repeat the terms so we are on the same page." (Em sintonia / de acordo)
Going forward -- from now on; in the future, after this point
"Going forward, we will send weekly updates." (Daqui em diante)
Deal-breaker -- a problem so serious that it can stop an agreement
"A late delivery would be a deal-breaker." (Impedimento decisivo)
Formalize -- to make an agreement official, usually in writing
"We will formalize the deal with a signed contract." (Formalizar)
Kick off -- to start a project, process, or meeting
"We will kick off the project in early March." (Dar início)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Usamos reported speech para contar o que alguém disse. Usamos said (sem pessoa) ou told + pessoa, e o verbo recua um tempo. / We use reported speech to tell what someone said. We use said (no person) or told + person, and the verb shifts back one tense.

DirectShiftReported
"are perfect"are → wereHe said the terms were perfect.
"will sign off"will → wouldHe told me they would sign off.
"can kick off"can → couldShe said we could kick off in March.
"have signed"have → hadShe said she had signed it.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use told com a pessoa (told me) e said sem a pessoa (said that). O verbo recua um tempo: is/are → was/were, will → would, can → could, have done → had done. Nunca diga "he said me".
