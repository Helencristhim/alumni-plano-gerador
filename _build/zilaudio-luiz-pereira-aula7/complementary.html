Complementary Activities

Three things to do after Lesson 7, the final lesson. They are all about the same skill you practiced today — making a deal: negotiating a better price, agreeing on the terms, and closing with confidence. Mark each one as done when you finish it.

YouTube
English at Work — Negotiating the Best Deal (BBC Learning English)

This is Lesson 7 as a story. In this short episode, the team negotiates the best deal — they push for a better price, talk about the terms, and try to close. You will hear the same phrases you used with Diana today: could, would, and "do we have a deal?"

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, just follow the story. The second time, write down every phrase you hear that pushes for a better deal, and say it out loud.

Video lesson
How to Negotiate in English — Oxford Online English

A clear, slow lesson on the exact skill of Lesson 7. Two American speakers show you how to make an offer, ask for a better price, talk about the terms and conditions, and close the deal — step by step, with real examples you can copy.

Tip: pause after each phrase and repeat it out loud. Then imagine your next real negotiation and use one of the phrases instead of accepting the first offer.

Business English: Negotiating Vocabulary — engVid

A friendly teacher explains the words people really use when they make a deal — how to agree, how to say a soft no, and how to talk about the terms. There is a video and a short quiz at the end, so you can check what you remember.

Tip: choose three new words from the video and use each one in a sentence about your own soybean deal. Then take the quiz to test yourself.
