The Truth

A Low Offer Is Not a No

Negotiating is not fighting. It is asking, politely.
The price is one thing. The terms are just as important.
You can want the deal and still say: "not at that price."

When the offer is low, you have a choice: walk away, or keep talking.
Tonight you learn the words that keep the deal alive.

Chapter 3

The Negotiation Code

Grammar Discovery

What Do Could & Would Do?

Could we agree on a better price?
I would accept a longer contract.
Would you consider a higher offer?
I could not sign at that price.
What you want to doSay this
Ask politely in a dealCould we agree on...? / Could you lower...?
Say what you accept or will doI would accept... / I would sign...
Suggest an idea gentlyWould you consider...?
Say a soft "no"I could not accept... / I couldn't sign...

Could asks. Would says what you accept or would do. Together they let you push for a better deal — and still sound polite, never weak.

Common Mistake

Rude vs Polite

"I want more money."
"Could we agree on a better price?"
"I no sign at that price."
"I could not sign at that price."

A direct "I want" sounds rude and can end the talk. Start with Could we or I would, and the deal stays open. Asking is not weak — it is how good deals are made.
