Chapter 3 -- The Passive

The Passive Voice

was / were + past participle -- the action matters most

Grammar Notice

Notice the Passive

What is the same in all of these?

"The audit was completed in March."

"The results were analysed by the team."

"Twelve risks were identified."

"The report was written last week."

Grammar Rule

The Passive Voice

Active (who did it)Passive (the action matters)
We completed the audit.The audit was completed.
The team analysed the results.The results were analysed.
I wrote the report.The report was written.
We collected the data.The data was collected.

Key idea: the passive is was (singular) or were (plural) + the past participle (completed, analysed, written, done). Use it in a report when the action matters more than the person. Add by + person only if the doer is important: "The results were analysed by the team." Watch the irregular participles: write → written, do → done, take → taken.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The report was wrote. The audit completed."
"The report was written. The audit was completed."

Use the past participle (written, not wrote), and never drop the verb to be -- it is was completed, not "completed".

Grammar Practice

Make it Passive

"The audit was _____completed in March." (complete → past participle)
"The results _____were analysed by the team." (were / was -- plural)
"The report was _____written last week." (write → past participle)
"The data was _____collected on three sites." (collect → past participle)

Artefact

Short Report

Monthly Safety Report -- March

Prepared by: Percival JR -- EGEA Saneamento

Objective

The objective of this report is to review the safety audit for March.

Findings

A survey was carried out on three sites. Twelve risks were identified. The data was checked twice, so the figures are accurate. The results were analysed by the audit team.

Conclusion

The audit was completed on time. It is recommended that two high risks are reviewed again next month.

"Three headings, factual sentences, accurate figures." -- Karen
