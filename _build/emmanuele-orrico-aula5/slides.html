Chapter 3: The Code

What Is Going to Happen

We are going to announce · I am going to present · We are meeting

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"In May, we are going to announce the product approval."

"I am going to present the business plan in August."

"The reimbursement decision is going to arrive in July."

"We are meeting the Global team on August 24."

Three purple sentences share the same two words. The green one does not. What is the difference?

BlockUseExample
I am going to · We / You / They are going to · He / She / It is going toA plan or an intention. You decided it — the date is not in the calendar yet.We are going to focus on the private market.
+ verb in the normal formAfter going to, the verb never changes. Nothing to conjugate.going to announce · going to present · going to organize
Negative: not inside the blockThe not goes in the middle, never at the end.We are not going to launch in the public market.
Question: turn the first two words around"You are going to" → "Are you going to?"What are you going to present in August?
Already confirmed? Use the -ing form — no "going to"The date is fixed, it is in the calendar, the ticket is bought.We are meeting the Global team on August 24. · They are arriving on Sunday.
Ready phrase: It depends on the market conditions.Nothing to build. One block, use it forever — the professional way to say "maybe"."Is July safe?" — "It depends on the market conditions."

You do not conjugate anything. You pick the block — I am going to, we are going to — and you add your verb.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We are going to launching the product in May."
"We are going to launch the product in May."
"I go to present the business plan in August."
"I am going to present the business plan in August."
"What you are going to do in August?"
"What are you going to do in August?"

After going to, the verb goes back to normal — launch, never "launching". And in a question, the first two words change places: are you going to.

Your Email to Paris

NEW MESSAGESanofi Brazil
From: Emmanuele Orrico
To: Marc Dubois — Global Immunology, Paris
Subject: Brazil — Business plan for the next quarter

Dear Marc,

Here is our plan for the next quarter.

Our goal is the product approval in dermatology, and we are going to announce it in May. Our strategy is to focus on the private hospitals: I am going to organize twenty new meetings with the medical teams.

The reimbursement decision is going to arrive in July, but it depends on the market conditions.

Best regards,

Emmanuele

"Our goal is the product approval in dermatology."
2. Find the strategy.
"Our strategy is to focus on the private hospitals. I am going to organize twenty new meetings."
3. One sentence has no "going to". Which one, and why?
"We are meeting you on August 24." The date is confirmed, so it takes the -ing form.

April 30 — and she is going to send the business plan before.
