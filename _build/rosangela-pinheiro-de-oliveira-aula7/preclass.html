Lesson 07 -- Pre-class

Shopping, Souvenirs, and Saying No Politely -- Navigating Stores and Markets in European Cities

Walk into a European market alone: browse without buying, ask the price when there is no price tag, say which one is the most beautiful, ask for a discount, pay in cash and keep your receipt. Key words: souvenir, market stall, to browse, price tag, to bargain, discount, fitting room, cash, receipt, to wrap. Structure: superlatives -- the cheapest, the most beautiful, the best. Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to every word and read the example. These are the ten words of a European market. Watch number nine: in Lesson 6 you asked for the bill before you paid at the restaurant. This one is the paper you get after you pay in a shop — and you keep it.

Souvenir -- a small thing you buy on a trip, to remember the place or to give to somebody at home
"I am looking for a souvenir for my son."
Market stall -- a small open shop in the street, with a table and no door
"Everything on this market stall is handmade."
To browse -- to look at the things slowly, with no plan to buy
"No, thank you. I am just browsing."
Price tag -- the small label on the thing, with the price written on it
"There is no price tag on this one. How much is it?"
To bargain -- to talk with the seller and ask for a lower price
"You can bargain here, but ask with a smile."
Discount -- money taken off the price, so you pay less than the price tag says
"If you take two, I can give you a discount."
Fitting room -- the small room in a shop where you try the clothes on before you buy
"Can I try this on? Where is the fitting room?"
Cash -- money in paper and coins, not a card
"Most stalls here take cash only."
Receipt -- the small paper the shop gives you after you pay, to prove that you bought it
"Keep the receipt: without it you cannot change anything."
To wrap -- to put paper around a thing, so that it is a gift and it does not break
"Could you wrap it, please? It is a gift."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

It is Saturday morning at the Feira da Ladra, the oldest market in Lisbon, and Rosângela is walking alone between the market stalls. At the first stall a man comes to her immediately. She does not walk away in silence. She smiles and says: "No, thank you. I am just browsing." He says "of course", and that is the end of it. Nobody is angry. At the second stall there are three scarves, and she is looking for a souvenir for her son. The silk one has no price tag. "How much is it, please?" — "Forty-five euros. It is the most beautiful thing I have." The cotton one is the cheapest on the stall, twenty euros, and it is also the lightest. "In my opinion, the silk one is the nicest," she says. "I would like both. Is this the best price you can offer?" She is not rude. She is going to bargain, with a smile, and the seller gives her a discount: sixty euros for the two. He does not take cards, so she pays in cash. "Could you wrap the blue one? It is a gift." He wraps it, and he gives her the receipt. "Keep it. If your son does not like the color, come back in fourteen days." She walks out into the sun with a bag in her hand, and there is nobody next to her translating. She did all of it herself.

1. "The cotton one is the cheapest on the stall." Why not "the most cheap"?
A Because cotton is not expensive.
B Because "cheap" is a short adjective, and short adjectives take -est. "The most" is only for long adjectives.
C Because there are only three scarves.
2. "It is the most beautiful thing I have." Why not "the more beautiful"?
A Because "more" compares two things. Here it is number one of everything on the stall, so it is "the most".
B Because "beautiful" has no comparative form.
C Because the seller is talking about a scarf and not about a person.
3. At the first stall she says "No, thank you. I am just browsing." What happens?
A The seller is offended and she has to leave the market.
B The seller does not understand her and calls somebody else.
C He says "of course", and that is the end of it. Nobody is angry.
4. Why does the seller tell her to keep the receipt?
A Because she paid in cash and the receipt is her money.
B Because if her son does not like the color, she can come back in fourteen days and change it.
C Because she needs it to get the discount on the two scarves.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Superlatives: Number One of All

GRAMMAR

In Lesson 6 you compared two things: "the fish is cheaper than the meat". Today one thing is number one of all of them.

FormUseExample
Short adjective
+ -est		One or two syllables: cheap, big, small, nice, old, light, warm.the cheapest scarf · the biggest market · the nicest one
Long adjective
the most + adjective		Three syllables or more: beautiful, expensive, popular, interesting.the most beautiful scarf · the most popular souvenir
Never
the most cheap		A short adjective never takes "most". Pick one road: -est, or the most. Never both."the cheapest" (never "the most cheap", never "the most cheapest")
Always
the		Number one of all of them is only one thing, so the article never leaves."It is the most popular souvenir." (never "It is most popular souvenir")
Two little
irregulars		These two do not follow the rule. Learn them as they are.good → the best ("the best price") · bad → the worst
Two things
vs all of them		Lesson 6 was two: -er / more ... than. Today is all: the -est / the most. And "than" never appears in a superlative.The silk one is more expensive than the cotton one (two) · The silk one is the most expensive of the three (all)

The one rule to remember: short word takes -est, long word takes the most, and both of them always keep the. If you hear yourself say "than", you are comparing two things — that is Lesson 6, not this one.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the prompt. There is no right or wrong version. Speak for 60 seconds, with no script.

Think about the best market or the best shop you have ever visited — in Brazil, in Italy, in Paris, anywhere. Where is it, and why is it the best one? What is the most beautiful thing you brought home from a trip, and who was it for? What was the cheapest thing, and was it the worst? Use at least four superlatives ("the most...", "the best...", "the cheapest...") and one sentence with "In my opinion...". And tell me one thing you would like to buy on your next trip — alone, with nobody translating the price for you. Take your time and do not read from a script.
