Series
Queer Eye — Season 1, Episode 1: "You Can't Fix Ugly"

Five men spend a week with Tom, who is fifty-seven and has not bought a new shirt in years. Watch the shopping part in the middle: Tan takes him to the store, they go to the fitting room, and Tan says out loud why one thing is better than another and why one is the best of all. It is the whole lesson, in a store, with a man who is nervous about it — exactly like the first stall of a market.

Tip: watch with English subtitles, and you do not have to watch the whole episode. Stop every time Tan gives an opinion — "this is the best cut for you", "this one is the most comfortable" — and say his sentence out loud. Then say one about your own things: "In my opinion, this is the most beautiful scarf I have."

Podcast
Speak English Now — Episode 281: Christmas Markets (A2-B1)

Georgiana walks through three holiday markets and uses, one after another, the exact words of this lesson: vendor, browse, souvenir, handcrafted — and a superlative in the middle of it ("one of the most iconic holiday markets"). She is American, she speaks slowly, and she never explains grammar. Your son suggested Christmas or January for the trip: this is what a European Christmas market sounds like, in English, before you are standing in one.

Tip: listen once with your eyes closed, and do not read — your ear before your eyes. Then listen again with the free text open, and count the superlatives. After that, close everything and describe your favorite market out loud: "In my opinion, it is the most beautiful market in the city."

YouTube
Oxford Online English — Going Shopping in English: Spoken English for Travel (free, no subscription)

Two teachers, on camera, doing exactly what you did in class today: asking the price, asking for a discount, asking to try something on, asking about cards, and saying no to something they do not want. Every sentence appears on the screen while they say it, so you can hear it and read it at the same time. Same channel as your hotel and transport lessons.

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, pause after every question they ask and say it out loud before they do — "Do you accept credit cards?", "Can I try this on?". Your ear already knows these: your mouth is the one practicing.
