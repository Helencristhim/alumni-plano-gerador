Chapter 3: The Code

Number One

The cheapest · the most beautiful · the best

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This is the most beautiful scarf in the market."

"That one is the cheapest thing on the stall."

"The blue tile is the most popular souvenir in Lisbon."

"Is this the best price you can offer?"

In all four, one thing is number one — of all of them. One little word is in every single sentence. Which one? Then look at the adjectives: cheap is short, beautiful and popular are long. What happens to each?

FormUseExample
Short adjective
+ -est		One or two syllables: cheap, big, small, nice, old, new.the cheapest stall · the biggest market · the nicest one
Long adjective
the most + adjective		Three syllables or more: beautiful, expensive, popular, interesting.the most beautiful scarf · the most expensive tile
Always
the		Number one of all of them is only one thing — so it always takes "the".the cheapest · the most popular (never "is most popular")
Two little
irregulars		These two do not follow anything. Learn them as they are.good → the best · bad → the worst
You already
know half of it		Lesson 6 gave you two things: cheaper than, more expensive than. Today is all of them.The fish is cheaper than the meat (two) · This is the cheapest dish (all)

Two things = -er / more ... than (Lesson 6). All of them = the -est / the most (today). The word than never appears in a superlative.

The Anchor

Three Scarves

Two things = -er / more ... than. All three = the -est / the most.

"The wool one is more expensive than the cotton one." (two)

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"This is the most cheap scarf on the stall."
"This is the cheapest scarf on the stall."
"This is the more beautiful thing in the market."
"This is the most beautiful thing in the market."
"It is most popular souvenir in Lisbon."
"It is the most popular souvenir in Lisbon."

Short adjective, add -est — never "most cheap". More is for two things (Lesson 6); most is for all of them. And the article never leaves: it is always the most popular.

The Question Kit

Three Questions, Any Shop

You do not need to understand the whole market. You need three questions.

"Can I try this on, please? Where is the fitting room?"
"Do you accept credit cards, or is it cash only?"
"Is this the best price you can offer?"

And when the price comes too fast, you already have the words from Lesson 3: "I am sorry, I did not catch that. Could you say it more slowly, please?"

Expression of the Day

In My Opinion

In my opinion, ...

= this is what I think (and it is in, never "for my opinion")

"Which one do you like?" — "In my opinion, the silk one is the most beautiful."
"In my opinion, this is the nicest souvenir for my son — and it is not the most expensive."

Now say two true sentences with In my opinion and a superlative — about Italy, about Lisbon, about anything.

The Hardest Words

How to Say No

Not rude. Not silent. Not walking away fast, looking at the ground. Just three sentences.

"No, thank you. I am just looking."
"It is beautiful, but it is too expensive for me."
"Thank you, but not today. Maybe on Saturday."

Say it, smile, and walk on. The seller is not offended — two hundred people look at his stall every Saturday, and ten of them buy. You are allowed to be one of the two hundred.

Chapter 4: At the Stall

He Sells, You Decide

One price · one opinion · and one moment you do not catch

Dialogue

At the Market Stall

T
Good morning, madam! Come, come. Everything on this market stall is handmade, here in Lisbon. Are you looking for something special?
R
Good morning. Thank you — I am just browsing for now. But I am looking for a souvenir for my son.
T
Take your time, please. These blue tiles are the most popular souvenir in Lisbon — everybody takes one home. And this scarf here is the most beautiful thing I have.
R
It is beautiful. But there is no price tag on it. How much is it?
T
Forty-five euros. It is silk, from a small workshop behind the church, and nothing on this stall is better. The cotton one next to it is the cheapest — twenty euros.
R
I am sorry, I did not catch that. Could you say the price more slowly, please?
T
Of course. Four... five. Forty-five for the silk one. Two... zero. Twenty for the cotton one. And if you take two, I can give you a discount.
R
In my opinion, the silk one is the nicest. I would like both, then. Is this the best price you can offer?
T
Sixty for the two. That is my best price, madam — and I am not going lower. Cash or card?
R
Cash, please. And could you wrap the blue one? It is a gift. May I have the receipt, too?
T
Here you are. Keep the receipt: if your son does not like the color, come back in fourteen days and change it. I am here every Saturday. Enjoy Lisbon!
Comprehension

About the Seller

1. What did Tomás say is the most popular souvenir in Lisbon?
The blue tiles — he said everybody takes one home.
2. What is the difference between the forty-five euro scarf and the twenty euro one?
The forty-five euro one is silk, from a small workshop behind the church. The cotton one is the cheapest on the stall — twenty euros.
3. She told him she was just browsing. What did he answer?
"Take your time, please." He was not annoyed at all — and she stayed, and bought two.
4. What did Tomás do when she did not catch the price?
He said "Of course" and repeated it slowly, number by number: four, five — forty-five.
5. Why did he say she should keep the receipt?
If her son does not like the color, she can come back in fourteen days and change it. He is there every Saturday.
Listening

The Market Information

A recorded message at the entrance of the market. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. Which days is the market open, and which day is the busiest?
Tuesday and Saturday only, from nine in the morning to six in the evening. Saturday is the busiest and the most beautiful, because every stall is open.
2. Can you pay by card at the stalls? What should you bring?
No — most stalls take cash only. Bring your cash from the hotel: there is one machine at the entrance, next to the church, and the line is long on Saturday.
3. The recording says one thing is the most important of all. What is it?
You can look at everything and buy nothing. Nobody is angry. Just say "no, thank you, I am just looking" and walk on.
4. What are the two rules about bargaining?
You can bargain and it is not rude — ask with a smile. But do not bargain on the food, and do not bargain for one euro.
Listening 2

The Other Side of the Stall

Cristina has sold scarves in this market for nineteen years. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What do many tourists do at Cristina's stall, and how does that make her feel?
They look at her scarves for two seconds, then walk away very fast and look at the ground. She wishes they were not afraid — "I am not a lion. I am a woman with scarves."
2. On a normal Saturday, how many people buy something?
People look all day, and about ten people buy. She says that is a normal Saturday — and it is not a problem, it is her job.
3. What is the only thing she does not like?
When a person cannot say no, buys her most expensive scarf, and goes home unhappy. She says she does not want your money like that.
4. What does she say about the most beautiful scarf on her stall?
It is never the most expensive one. The best thing there is the one you love — and it costs twenty-two euros.
