Grammar Rule

Present Perfect and Linking Words

FormExample
Affirmative (have/has + p.p.)We have signed two contracts.
Negative (have not + p.p. + yet)The report has not arrived yet.
Question (Have/Has + subject + p.p.?)Have you finished the report?
Linking wordsThere is a delay; however, we are on track.

Key idea: use already for something done before expected, and yet for something not done. Connect ideas with however, therefore, in addition, and as a result.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have finish the report already."
"I have already finished the report."

After have or has, use the past participle: finished, signed, done -- never the base verb.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Update

Briefing Diana

D

"Hi Walyson, thanks for coming. Can you give me a quick update on the Europe project?"

W

"Of course. So far, we have made good progress. We have already signed two contracts, and we have achieved our target volume."

D

"That is excellent. I am very pleased with the progress. Is there any problem I should know about?"

W

"I want to highlight one point. The samples have not arrived yet, therefore there has been a small delay. That order is still pending."

D

"Thank you for telling me. Please keep me informed. In addition, can you prepare a short overview for the board?"

"Certainly. I have not finished the full report yet, but I will send the overview by Friday. The project is on track."
