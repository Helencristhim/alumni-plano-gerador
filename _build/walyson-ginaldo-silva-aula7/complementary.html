Lesson 7 -- Reporting to the Director

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2, Episode 1: "The Dundies"

Watch how managers and staff report progress, give quick updates and brief each other in a real workplace. Notice how they talk about what has already happened.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three sentences in the present perfect that you hear (for example, "I have finished", "we have done") and one linking word (however, therefore, in addition).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Giving a Status Update at Work"

A clear episode on reporting progress to a manager: using the present perfect for results and linking words to connect a problem with its solution.

Record a 2-minute status update on a real project: give an overview, say what you have already achieved, highlight one delay or pending task, and use at least two linking words.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The Present Perfect Tense"

A focused lesson on have/has + past participle, and on the words already, yet and so far. Directly reinforces today's grammar for status updates.

After watching, build six sentences about your week using the present perfect: three affirmative with "already" and three negative with "yet". Then link two of them with "however" and "therefore".
