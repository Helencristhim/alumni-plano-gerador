Aula 07 -- Pre-class

Reporting to the Director -- Present Perfect and Linking Words

Giving a clear status update to a manager: present perfect for results (have done, has finished, just/already/yet/so far) and linking words (however, therefore, in addition, as a result). New vocabulary: update, progress, milestone, achieve, target, brief, highlight, pending, status, on track, delay, overview.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Update -- a short report on the latest progress
"I have an update on the Europe project." (Atualização)
Progress -- forward movement toward a goal
"We have made good progress this week." (Progresso)
Milestone -- an important stage or achievement in a project
"We have reached an important milestone." (Marco / etapa importante)
Achieve -- to reach a goal successfully
"We have achieved our target for the quarter." (Alcançar / atingir)
Target -- a result you plan to reach
"Our target is two hundred tons this month." (Meta / alvo)
Brief -- to give someone the key information they need
"Let me brief you on the situation." (Informar / dar um resumo)
Highlight -- to point out the most important part
"I want to highlight one risk in the report." (Destacar / ressaltar)
Pending -- waiting to be done or decided
"Two contracts are still pending." (Pendente)
Status -- the current state or situation of something
"What is the status of the shipment?" (Situação / estado atual)
On track -- going according to plan, on schedule
"The project is on track for next month." (No caminho certo / dentro do prazo)
Delay -- a situation where something happens later than planned
"There has been a small delay on one order." (Atraso)
Overview -- a short, general summary of a situation
"Here is a quick overview of the quarter." (Visão geral / panorama)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect and Linking Words

GRAMMAR

Usamos o present perfect para resultados até agora. / We use the present perfect for results up to now.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Affirmativehave / has + past participleWe have signed two contracts.
Negativehave not / has not + p.p. + yetThe samples have not arrived yet.
QuestionHave / Has + subject + p.p.?Have you finished the report?
Linking wordshowever / therefore / in addition / as a resultThere is a delay; however, we are on track.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use already para algo concluído antes do esperado e yet para algo ainda não feito (em frases negativas e perguntas). Ligue suas ideias: however (porém), therefore (portanto), in addition (além disso), as a result (como resultado).
