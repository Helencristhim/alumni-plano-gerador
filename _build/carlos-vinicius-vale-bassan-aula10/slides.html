Chapter 3 -- Reporting Progress

The "We Have Done It" Tense

Report what is done, and what is still open

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence reports progress. Notice the orange words.

"We have completed due diligence."

"The board has approved the structure."

"We haven't signed the final contract yet."

"Have you reviewed the valuation?"

The Rule

Have / Has + Past Participle

FormExample
Affirmative"We have completed due diligence."
Negative"We haven't signed the contract yet."
Question"Has the board approved it?"

Tip: use have / has + past participle for actions that are done but with no exact past time. Use has for he / she / it / the board. Add just (recently), already (sooner than expected), and yet (so far, in negatives and questions).

Common Mistake

No Past Time with the Present Perfect

"We have signed the deal yesterday."
"We signed the deal yesterday." / "We have just signed the deal."

If you say when it happened (yesterday, last week), use the past simple. Keep the present perfect for done actions with no exact time.

Practice
