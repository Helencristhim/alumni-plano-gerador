Block 2 -- Deal Structure & Synergies

Take the deal one level up: structure it as a merger or acquisition, set the valuation, explain the synergies and the equity, confirm due diligence, and present it to the board. New vocabulary: merger, acquisition, synergy, valuation, stakeholder, equity, due diligence, board. Grammar focus: the present perfect to report progress (have / has + past participle).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Merger -- when two companies combine into a single company
"The merger creates one stronger company." (fusão)
Acquisition -- when one company buys and takes control of another
"The acquisition gives us a new market." (aquisição)
Synergy -- extra value created when two companies join forces
"Most of the value comes from synergies." (sinergia)
Valuation -- the estimated financial worth of a company
"The valuation is fair for both sides." (avaliação de valor)
Stakeholder -- a person or group with an interest in the deal's outcome
"Every stakeholder reads the deal differently." (parte interessada)
Equity -- an ownership share in a company
"We keep a controlling equity stake." (participação acionária)
Due diligence -- the careful review of a company before a deal is signed
"We have completed due diligence." (auditoria prévia)
Board -- the group of directors who approve a company's big decisions
"The board has approved the structure." (conselho de administração)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about the deal and answer the questions.

Carlos presented the deal to the board. The structure was a merger, not an acquisition. He reported the progress: "We have completed due diligence, and we have agreed on the valuation." Most of the value came from synergies: lower costs and a wider market. The company would keep a controlling equity stake, and every stakeholder had a clear summary. One thing was still open: "We haven't signed the final contract yet." In the end, the board approved the structure in principle.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Para reportar o que já foi feito num negócio (sem dizer QUANDO), use o present perfect: have / has + past participle (particípio). Use has para he / she / it / the board. Negativa: haven't / hasn't. Pergunta: Have / Has + sujeito + particípio. Use just (há pouco), already (antes do esperado) e yet (até agora, em negativas e perguntas). Se você diz QUANDO (yesterday, last week), use o past simple. / Use have / has + past participle for done actions with no exact time. "Has" for he / she / it. Never use the present perfect with a finished past time.

FormExample
Affirmative"We have completed due diligence."
Negative"We haven't signed the contract yet."
Question"Has the board approved it?"
