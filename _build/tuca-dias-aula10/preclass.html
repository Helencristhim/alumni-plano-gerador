Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca lives on a coffee farm in the mountains -- this is a fact, so she uses the present simple. Ten lessons ago, she started English and she was very nervous -- a finished time in the past, so the past simple. Since then, she has learned hundreds of words and she has visited many places in her lessons -- experiences up to now, so the present perfect. Today she is going to re-record her introduction, because she planned it before -- a plan, so going to. She thinks she will become fluent one day -- an opinion about the future, so will. One short story, four different tenses.

1. "Tuca lives on a coffee farm." Which tense is this?
A Present simple -- a fact or routine
B Past simple -- a finished action
C Future -- a plan
2. "She has learned hundreds of words." Which tense is this?
A Past simple
B Present perfect -- experience up to now
C Present simple
3. "She started English ten lessons ago." Which tense is this?
A Present perfect
B Future with going to
C Past simple -- a finished time in the past

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Four Tenses Review

GRAMMAR

Revisão das quatro estruturas principais do seu curso. / A review of the four main structures from your course.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present simplefatos e rotinas / facts and routines"I live on a coffee farm."
Past simpleação terminada num tempo passado / a finished action in a past time"I started English in January."
Present perfectexperiência até agora (ever/never/already) / experience up to now"I have visited many places."
Future (will / going to)planos, decisões e previsões / plans, decisions, and predictions"I am going to practice. I will be fluent."

Key point / Ponto-chave: escolha o tempo pela ideia: fato (present simple), passado terminado (past simple), experiência até agora (present perfect), ou futuro (will / going to). A mesma história pode usar os quatro.
