Chapter 3 -- Tense Review

Grammar Discovery

Four tenses, one story

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

A fact? A finished past? An experience up to now? A future plan?

"I live on a coffee farm."

"I started English ten lessons ago."

"I have visited many places in my lessons."

"I am going to become fluent."

Grammar Rule

Four Tenses, One Story

TenseUseExample
Present simplefacts and routinesI live on a coffee farm.
Past simplea finished action in a past timeI started English in January.
Present perfectexperience up to now (ever, never, already)I have visited many places.
Futureplans, decisions, predictions (will, going to)I am going to practice. I will be fluent.

Key idea: choose the tense by the idea, not the words. A fact uses present simple, a finished time uses past simple, an experience up to now uses present perfect, and the future uses will or going to.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have started English ten lessons ago."
"I started English ten lessons ago."

With an exact past time (ten lessons ago, last year), use the past simple. Keep the present perfect for experiences with no exact time.
