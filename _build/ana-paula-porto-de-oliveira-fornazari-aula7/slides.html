Chapter 3: The Comparison Code

Cheaper vs The Cheapest

Two things · The top of the group

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This apartment is cheaper than that one."

"The countryside is more peaceful than the city."

"The subway is the most efficient option of all."

"Working from home is the best choice for me."

Which words compare two things, and which point to the top of a group?

TypeComparative (2 things)Superlative (the top)
Short adjective-er + than — cheaper thanthe + -est — the cheapest
Long adjectivemore + than — more modern thanthe most — the most modern
Irregulargood → better than / the best; bad → worse than / the worst

The comparative weighs two things; the superlative points to the top of the whole group.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"This cafe is more cheaper than that one."
"This cafe is cheaper than that one."
"Working from home is more better than the office."
"Working from home is better than the office."
"This is most modern gym in the area."
"This is the most modern gym in the area."

Never use more with an -er word or with better, and never forget the before a superlative.

About Greg's Advice

1. Which apartment does Greg recommend for a busy doctor?
Apartment B, because of the short commute.
2. Why does Greg think a short commute is the best option?
Because it saves time for a busy doctor.

If you prefer peace and quiet.
