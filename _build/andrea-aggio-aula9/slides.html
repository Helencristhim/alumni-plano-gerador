Chapter 4: The First Conditional

The First Conditional

if + present · will + verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If you book forty rooms, I will hold the rate."

"If you pay the deposit today, breakfast will be complimentary."

"If we travel in the low season, the price will drop."

Which part is the condition, and which part is the result? What tense follows "if"?

FormUseExample
If + present simpleThe condition -- what must happen firstIf you book early,
will + verbThe result -- what I promiseI will give you a discount.
Never 'will' after ifKeep the present simple in the if-partIf you pay (not 'will pay')...

First conditional = a real future possibility. Structure: If + present simple, ... will + verb.

Language Focus

Condition or Result?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If you will book early, I give you a discount."

✓ RIGHT

If you book early, I will give you a discount.

✗ WRONG

"If you paid the deposit today, breakfast is complimentary."

✓ RIGHT

If you pay the deposit today, breakfast will be complimentary.

In the first conditional, never use 'will' after 'if'. Use the present simple in the 'if' part and 'will' in the result part.

Dialogue

Negotiating a Group Rate

M
Hi Andrea, thanks for the proposal. Could you quote me a group rate for forty guests in October?
A
Of course, Miguel. If you confirm forty guests, I will give you our best net rate.
M
That sounds fair. And what happens if we need to cancel some rooms?
A
If you cancel thirty days before arrival, there will be no surcharge at all.
M
Good to know. October is peak season, so I expected a higher price.
A
If you book before the end of the month, I will hold the low season rate for you.
M
Excellent. And is breakfast included in the package?
A
Yes, breakfast is complimentary. If you sign today, I will add a welcome dinner for your group.
