Aula 09 -- Pre-class

Talking Prices, Packages, and Deals

How to quote prices and negotiate deals in tourism. Key words: net rate, markup, group rate, deposit, cancellation policy, peak season, low season, package, surcharge, to quote, complimentary, discount. Structure: the first conditional (if + present simple, will + verb) to trade a condition for an offer.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Net rate -- the base price before any markup is added (tarifa líquida)
"The hotel gave me a good net rate."
Markup -- the extra amount added to the base price to make a profit (margem)
"We add a small markup to the net rate."
Group rate -- a special reduced price for a large number of people (tarifa de grupo)
"Can you offer a group rate for forty guests?"
Deposit -- money paid in advance to hold a booking (depósito, sinal)
"We ask for a twenty percent deposit."
Cancellation policy -- the rules for changing or cancelling a booking (política de cancelamento)
"What is your cancellation policy?"
Peak season -- the busiest and most expensive time of the year (alta temporada)
"Prices rise in peak season."
Low season -- the quietest and cheapest time of the year (baixa temporada)
"The same room costs less in the low season."
Package -- several services combined and sold at one price (pacote)
"This package includes hotel and transfers."
Surcharge -- an extra charge added to the basic price (taxa extra)
"A small surcharge applies at the weekend."
To quote -- to state the price for a service (cotar, orçar)
"Could you quote me a rate for October?"
Complimentary -- free, included at no extra cost (cortesia, grátis)
"Breakfast is complimentary."
Discount -- a reduction in the normal price (desconto)
"If you book early, I will offer a discount."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea is preparing a quote for an important client. The hotel gave her a net rate, and she adds a small markup before sending the package to the client. Because the trip is in peak season, a weekend surcharge applies, but Andrea knows how to negotiate. 'If you book more than forty rooms, I will hold the low-season rate,' she says. She also explains the deposit and the cancellation policy clearly. To close the deal, she adds that breakfast is complimentary. The client is happy, and both sides feel they have won.

1. 'If you book more than forty rooms, I will hold the rate.' The 'if' part uses:
A the present simple (book).
B will + verb.
C the past simple.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A If you will pay the deposit, breakfast is free.
B If you pay the deposit, breakfast will be free.
C If you paying the deposit, breakfast free.
3. We use the first conditional to talk about:
A a real future possibility and its result.
B something that already happened.
C an impossible dream.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como negociar em inglês: 'se... então...' (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
If + present simpleA condição. The condition -- o que precisa acontecer primeiro.If you book early,
will + verbO resultado. The result -- o que você promete.I will give a discount.
Nunca 'will' depois de 'if' / Never 'will' after ifMantenha o presente simples na parte do 'if'.If you pay (não 'will pay')
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleif + presente simples, vírgula, will + verbo.If you book, I will offer a rate.
