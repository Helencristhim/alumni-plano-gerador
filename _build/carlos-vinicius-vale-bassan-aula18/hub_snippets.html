Block 3 -- The Signing: Reading the Agreement Before You Sign

The deal is agreed and legal has drafted the agreement -- now read it before you sign. New vocabulary: clause, provision, liability, binding, breach, stipulate, waive, enforce. Grammar focus: defining relative clauses (who / which / that) to point to the exact part of the contract -- "the clause that limits liability", "the provision which caps our exposure", "the party who signs".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clause -- one numbered section or paragraph of a contract
"Read the clause that limits liability first." (cláusula)
Provision -- a specific condition or term written into the agreement
"The provision which caps our exposure protects us." (disposição / condição)
Liability -- legal responsibility -- who pays if something goes wrong
"The clause that limits liability is the one I read first." (responsabilidade legal)
Binding -- legally enforceable -- a promise you must follow
"Never sign a binding agreement you have not read." (vinculante / obrigatório)
Breach -- a break or violation of the terms of an agreement
"A breach on timing would be expensive for us." (violação / quebra)
Stipulate -- to state clearly in the contract as a requirement
"The provision that stipulates the closing date sets the timeline." (estipular / determinar)
Waive -- to give up a right or claim on purpose
"Flag any clause that asks us to waive a right." (abrir mão / renunciar)
Enforce -- to make sure the terms of an agreement are followed
"The party that enforces the agreement can act on a breach." (fazer cumprir / executar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about reading the agreement and answer the questions.

The deal is agreed, and legal has drafted the agreement. Before Carlos signs, counsel briefs him on how to read it like an executive. First, find the clause that limits liability -- a clause is one numbered section, and liability is who pays if something goes wrong, so the provision which caps it protects the company. Second, check what is binding: any provision that is binding deserves a careful read. Third, look at the provision that stipulates the closing date, because a breach on timing is expensive. Stipulate just means to state clearly as a requirement. Fourth, watch a clause that asks you to waive a right -- it removes a protection. Finally, ask who enforces the agreement. The golden rule: never sign a binding agreement you have not understood.

1. Which clause does counsel say to read first, and why?
A The clause that limits liability -- it decides who pays if something goes wrong.
B The last page, because that is where the signature goes.
C Any clause, in any order -- they are all the same.
2. What does it mean if a provision stipulates the closing date?
A It suggests the date but does not require it.
B It states the date clearly as a requirement -- a breach on timing is expensive.
C It removes the closing date from the contract.
3. What is the golden rule before signing?
A Sign quickly so the other side does not change its mind.
B Let the lawyer read it -- you do not need to.
C Never sign a binding agreement you have not understood; read clause by clause.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Defining Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Um contrato tem várias cláusulas. Para dizer EXATAMENTE qual você quer, use uma defining relative clause: substantivo + who / which / that + verbo. Use who para pessoas ("the party who signs"), which para coisas ("the provision which caps...") e that para ambos ("the clause that limits..."). Ela DEFINE qual item entre vários, então NÃO leva vírgula. Cuidado: a palavra relativa JÁ é o sujeito -- NUNCA repita com it/he/she ("the clause that limits", não "the clause that it limits"). / A defining relative clause says which exact one you mean: who for people, which for things, that for both. No comma, and never repeat the subject.

Points toWordExample
A thingwhich / that"the clause that limits liability"
A thingwhich / that"the provision which caps our exposure"
A personwho / that"the party who signs first"
No commait defines, not adds"any provision that stipulates a deadline"
