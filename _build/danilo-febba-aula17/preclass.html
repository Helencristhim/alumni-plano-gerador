Aim -- the thing you want to do or reach (objetivo)
"My aim is to learn English this year."
Target -- a number or result you want to reach (alvo / meta numérica)
"Our target is one hundred new users."
Milestone -- an important point in a plan (marco importante)
"The new report is a big milestone."
Upcoming -- happening soon, in the near future (próximo / que vem aí)
"The upcoming month is very busy."
Soon -- in a short time from now (logo / em breve)
"We are going to start the project soon."
Intend -- to plan to do something (pretender / ter a intenção)
"I intend to finish this week."
Launch -- to start something new, like a product (lançar)
"We are going to launch a new tool."
Budget -- the plan for your money (orçamento)
"I am going to review the budget."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Be Going To (Future Plans)

GRAMMAR

Usamos be going to para falar de planos futuros. A estrutura é am / is / are + going to + verbo na forma base. (We use be going to for future plans: be + going to + base verb.)

FormaEstrutura (EN)
afirmativoI am going to learn. / She is going to launch.
negativoI am not going to start. / They are not going to wait.
perguntaAre you going to finish? / Is he going to help?

Atenção (erros comuns): não esqueça o be (am/is/are) antes de "going to" (I am going to, não "I going to"). Depois de "going to", use o verbo na forma base (going to learn, não "going to learning"). É going to, com -ing em go (não "go to"). Tradução: "I am going to study" = "Eu vou estudar".
