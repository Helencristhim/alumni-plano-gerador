Chapter 3

The Future Code

be going to + verb

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the green words have in common?

This month, I am going to learn Python.

We are going to launch a new tool.

Rachel is going to review the budget.

I am not going to start a big project.

FormPatternExample
Positiveam / is / are + going to + verbI am going to learn.
Negativeam / is / are + not + going to + verbI am not going to start.
QuestionAm / Is / Are + you + going to + verb?Are you going to finish?

We use be going to for future plans. The verb after "going to" is the base form: learn, launch, finish.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I going to learn Python."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to learn Python."

✗ WRONG

"I am going to learning."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to learn."

✗ WRONG

"I am go to finish it."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to finish it."

Always use be (am/is/are) before "going to". After "going to", use the base verb (learn, not learning). It is "going to", with -ing on go.
