Data-Driven -- Describing Trends and Numbers

Describe charts and figures with trend verbs (rose, fell, peaked, plateaued, dipped), adverbs of speed (sharply, gradually, slightly), and prepositions of change (rose to a final value, fell by a difference, grew from X to Y). New vocabulary: surge, plummet, peak, plateau, dip, fluctuate, steady, sharp, gradual, trend line.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Surge -- a fast, strong increase in a number or value
"Sales surged in the first quarter of the year." (Disparada / forte alta)
Plummet -- to drop quickly and sharply
"Demand plummeted after the price increase." (Despencar / cair vertiginosamente)
Peak -- the highest value a figure reaches; to reach that point
"Sales reached a peak of fifty-eight million in Q3." (Pico / atingir o pico)
Plateau -- to level off and stay at the same value for a time
"Growth plateaued in the middle of the year." (Estabilizar / estagnar)
Dip -- a small fall before the value recovers
"The line dipped a little in the fourth quarter." (Pequena queda / recuo)
Fluctuate -- to change irregularly, rising and falling repeatedly
"The exchange rate fluctuated all year." (Oscilar / flutuar)
Steady -- constant and regular; changing at an even pace
"Costs showed a steady rise of one percent a month." (Constante / estável)
Sharp -- sudden and large; a fast, steep change
"There was a sharp rise in revenue early in the year." (Acentuado / abrupto)
Gradual -- slow, smooth, and steady; the opposite of sharp
"Costs climbed gradually, with no sudden jumps." (Gradual / aos poucos)
Trend line -- a line on a chart showing the overall direction over time
"The trend line points up, despite the small dips." (Linha de tendência)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The quarterly chart tells a clear story. Sales rose sharply in the first quarter, from forty to fifty-two million reais -- a real surge. Then growth plateaued: in Q2 the figure increased only slightly. In Q3 sales reached a peak, climbing to fifty-eight million, the highest point of the year. After that the line dipped by three million in Q4 -- a small fall, not a plummet. Costs, by contrast, climbed gradually, a steady rise of about one percent a month, while the exchange rate fluctuated with no clear direction. Notice the pattern: the verb gives the direction, the adverb gives the speed, and the preposition gives the figure -- to for the final value, by for the difference. Despite the dips, the trend line still points up.

A Because fifty-eight million is the final value the line reached.

C Because fifty-eight million is the lowest point.
2. Why does the text say "dipped by three million"?
A Because three million is the final value.
B Because three million is the difference -- how much it fell.
C Because the line crashed completely.
3. How did costs behave compared with sales?
A They climbed gradually, a steady rise with no sudden jumps.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Verb + Adverb + Preposition

GRAMMAR

Para descrever um gráfico, combine três coisas: o VERBO de tendência dá a direção (rose, fell, peaked), o ADVÉRBIO dá a velocidade (sharply, gradually, slightly) e a PREPOSIÇÃO liga ao número (to = valor final, by = a diferença, from X to Y = os dois extremos). / To describe a chart, combine a trend verb (direction), an adverb (speed), and a preposition (the figure): to for the final value, by for the difference, from X to Y for both ends.

PartJobExample
Verb (direction)rose / fell / peaked / dipped"sales rose"
Adverb (speed)sharply / gradually / slightly"rose sharply"
to + final valuewhere the line ended"rose to 52 million"
by + differencehow much it changed"fell by 3 million"
from X to Y / at + peakboth ends / the high point"grew from 40 to 52m"; "peaked at 58m"

Key point / Ponto-chave: use to para o valor final ("rose to 52m" = onde terminou) e by para a diferença ("rose by 12m" = quanto mudou). O advérbio modifica o verbo: "rose sharp" → "rose sharply". / Use to for the final value and by for the difference. The adverb modifies the verb: not "rose sharp" but "rose sharply".
