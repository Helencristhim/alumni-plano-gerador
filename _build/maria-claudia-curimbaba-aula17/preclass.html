Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Data-Driven -- Describing Trends & Numbers

Read a chart like an analyst. Compare figures with comparatives (Q3 was higher than Q2) and superlatives (the highest figure of the year), show proportion with as ... as (twice as large as), tune the size with degree modifiers (significantly, considerably, slightly, marginally), and report every number in the passive voice (the target was exceeded; the figures were reported by finance). New vocabulary: benchmark, metric, figure, ratio, breakdown, segment, outlier, discrepancy, marginal, substantial, offset, threshold, baseline, variance.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Benchmark -- a standard or reference point used to compare performance
"Q3 revenue was measured against the industry benchmark." (referência / parâmetro)
Metric -- a number used to measure and track performance
"Customer retention is the metric we watch most closely." (métrica / indicador)
Figure -- a specific number that appears in a report or chart
"The sales figure for July was the highest of the year." (cifra / número)
Ratio -- the relationship between two numbers, shown as a proportion
"The cost-to-income ratio is lower than last year." (índice / proporção)
Breakdown -- a detailed split of a total into its separate parts
"The breakdown by region shows Asia growing the fastest." (detalhamento / discriminação)
Segment -- a distinct part of a market, business or set of data
"The premium segment is considerably more profitable." (segmento)
Outlier -- a value that is far higher or lower than all the others
"March was an outlier, far above every other month." (ponto fora da curva / valor atípico)
Discrepancy -- a difference between two figures that are expected to match
"There is a discrepancy between the forecast and the actual figure." (discrepância / divergência)
Marginal -- very small in size or effect; only just noticeable
"The change was marginal, only slightly above last quarter." (marginal / mínimo)
Substantial -- large and significant; clearly important in size
"Revenue showed a substantial increase, significantly higher than Q2." (substancial / considerável)
Offset -- to balance or cancel out one amount with an opposite one
"Higher costs were offset by stronger sales." (compensar / contrabalançar)
Threshold -- a level that must be reached before something takes effect
"Profit passed the threshold set by the board." (limiar / patamar)
Baseline -- the starting figure used as a basis for comparison
"Every result is compared against the January baseline." (linha de base / referência inicial)
Variance -- the difference between a planned figure and the actual one
"The variance between budget and actual was minimal." (variância / desvio)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before the board meeting, I read every figure on the quarterly dashboard. Q3 was the highest figure of the year, significantly higher than Q2 and well above the benchmark; the target was exceeded by a substantial margin. The breakdown by segment showed the premium one growing twice as fast as the standard one, and March stood out as an outlier. Q4 was marginally lower than Q3, but higher costs were offset by stronger sales, so it stayed above the threshold. The variance against the Q1 baseline was minimal, and all figures were reported by finance.

1. How is Q3 compared with Q2?
A "Q3 was significantly higher than Q2" -- comparative + than.
B "Q3 was more higher as Q2."
C "Q3 was the higher than Q2."
2. How is the top figure of the year described?
A "the most highest figure of the year."
B "the highest figure of the year" -- superlative with "the".
C "a higher figure of the year."
3. How is the target reported -- "the target ___"?
A "was exceeded" -- passive: be + past participle.
B "exceeded" -- with no "was".
C "is exceed".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives, Superlatives & the Passive

GRAMMAR

Para descrever dados, você compara números. Comparativo: adjetivo curto + -er + than ("higher than"), adjetivo longo + more ... than ("more profitable than"). Superlativo: the + -est ("the highest of the year") ou the most + adjetivo. Proporção: (twice / half) as + adjetivo + as ("twice as large as"). Use um modificador de grau antes do comparativo para dizer o tamanho da diferença: significantly > considerably > slightly > marginally. E relate cada número na voz passiva: be + partícipio ("the target was exceeded", "the figures were reported by finance"). / Comparatives take "than", superlatives take "the", and the passive reports the number.

Form / functionPatternExample
comparative-er / more ... than"Q3 was higher than Q2"
superlativethe -est / the most ..."the highest figure of the year"
proportion(twice) as ... as"twice as large as the budget"
report the number (passive)be + past participle"the target was exceeded"

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca dobre o comparativo ("more higher" está errado, diga "higher") e use sempre than, nunca "as" ("higher than", não "higher as"). O superlativo leva the ("the highest"). E a passiva SEMPRE precisa do be: "the target was exceeded", "the figures were reported", nunca "the target exceeded". / Never double a comparative, always use "than", and the passive always needs "be".
