Chapter 3 -- Compare the Figures

Language Discovery

Comparatives, superlatives, degree modifiers -- and the passive for the number

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words do -- compare two figures, pick the extreme, or say how big the gap is?

"Q3 was significantly higher than Q2."

"March was the highest figure of the year."

"The premium segment grew twice as fast as the standard one."

"The target was exceeded by a substantial margin."

The Rule

Comparatives & Superlatives

FormUseExample
-er + than / more ... thancompare two figures"Q3 was higher than Q2"; "more profitable than"
the -est / the most ... (of)pick the top or bottom"the highest figure of the year"; "the steepest of the three"
(twice / half) as ... asshow a proportion"twice as large as the budget segment"
degree modifier + comparativesay how big the gap is"significantly / slightly higher than Q2"

Key idea: a comparative always takes than (never "as"), and you never double it ("more higher" is wrong). A superlative takes the. Tune the size with a degree modifier: significantly > considerably > slightly > marginally. And report the number in the passive: "the target was exceeded", "the data is presented in the chart".

Language Focus

Reporting the Number

Reports state most numbers in the passive: be + past participle. The doer is often obvious, so you can leave it out -- or add "by + doer" when it matters.

"The figures were reported by finance."

"The target was exceeded in the third quarter."

"The data is presented in the chart below."

Pattern: subject + be (is / was / were) + past participle (reported, exceeded, presented). Add "by + doer" only if it matters ("the report was approved by the board"). When you report a number, the passive keeps the focus on the figure, not on who produced it.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Q3 was more higher as Q2."
"Q3 was significantly higher than Q2."

A comparative is never doubled ("more higher" is wrong, say "higher"), and it always takes than, never "as". And report a number in the passive with be: say "the target was exceeded", never "the target exceeded" when the result is the focus.

Practice

Complete the Sentence

"Q3 was significantly _____higher than Q2." (comparative + than)
"March was _____the highest figure of the year." (superlative + the)
"The premium segment is twice _____as large as the standard one." (proportion: as ... as)
"The target _____was exceeded by a substantial margin." (passive: be + participle)
