Chapter 3 -- The Code

Language Discovery

Verb + adverb + preposition

Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. Which one gives the direction, which one the speed, and which one the figure?

"Sales rose sharply in the first quarter."

"Revenue grew from forty to fifty-two million reais."

"Costs climbed gradually, and profit dipped by three million."

"Sales peaked at fifty-eight million, then plateaued."

The Rule

Verb + Adverb + Preposition

PartJobExample
Verb (direction)rose / fell / peaked / plateaued / dipped"sales rose"
Adverb (speed/size)sharply / gradually / steadily / slightly"sales rose sharply"
to + final valuethe value it reached"rose to 52 million"
by + the differencehow much it changed"fell by 3 million"
from X to Y / at + peakboth ends / the high point"grew from 40 to 52m"; "peaked at 58m"

Key idea: match the verb to the shape of the line, the adverb to the speed, and the preposition to the number. Use to for the final value, by for the difference, and from ... to ... for both ends. Quick test: "rose to 52m" answers where did it end?; "rose by 12m" answers how much did it change?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Revenue rose to twelve million." (when twelve million is the increase)
"Revenue rose by twelve million." (twelve million = the difference)

Use to for the value the line reached, and by for how much it changed. "Rose to 52m" = the end point; "rose by 12m" = the gap.

Maria Claudia's Presentation Plan

1. Which verbs does Maria Claudia use for a fast drop?

Plummeted, or fell sharply.

2. When does she use "to", and when does she use "by"?

"To" for the final figure (rose to 58 million); "by" for the difference (rose by 6 million); "from ... to" for both ends.

3. What is the difference between a sharp, a gradual, and a slight rise?

A sharp rise is fast and big; a gradual rise is slow and smooth; a slight rise is very small.
