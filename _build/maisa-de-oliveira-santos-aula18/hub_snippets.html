Vocabulary

The menu -- the list of food and drinks in a restaurant
"Here is the menu, please." (o cardápio)
To order -- to ask for food in a restaurant
"Are you ready to order?" (pedir)
A dish -- a plate of prepared food
"This dish is very good." (um prato)
A waiter -- the person who serves food at your table
"The waiter brings the menu." (o garçom)
The bill -- the paper that shows how much you pay
"Can I have the bill, please?" (a conta)
Delicious -- very good to eat; very tasty
"The fish is delicious." (delicioso)
Spicy -- food that is hot with pepper
"This soup is a little spicy." (apimentado)
A tip -- extra money you leave for good service
"We leave a tip for the waiter." (uma gorjeta)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Requests & Preferences

GRAMMAR

Para pedir comida com educação, use "I would like" + substantivo ("I would like the fish") ou "Can I have" + substantivo ("Can I have some water?"). "I want" soa rude. Para dizer o que você gosta mais, use "I prefer" + substantivo ("I prefer the salad") — sem "than". Para oferecer algo a outra pessoa, use "Would you like...?". / Use "I would like" and "Can I have" to order politely. Use "I prefer" for your preference and "Would you like" to offer.

Uso / UseEstrutura / PatternExemplo / Example
Pedido educado / Polite orderI would like + substantivoI would like the fish. (eu gostaria do peixe)
Pedir algo / Ask for somethingCan I have + substantivo?Can I have the bill? (pode me trazer a conta?)
Preferência / PreferenceI prefer + substantivoI prefer the salad. (prefiro a salada)
Oferecer / OfferWould you like + substantivo?Would you like some coffee? (você gostaria de um café?)

Atenção: para pedir comida, "I want" soa grosseiro — diga "I would like". E não use than depois de "prefer": diga "I prefer the salad", nunca "I prefer than the salad".
