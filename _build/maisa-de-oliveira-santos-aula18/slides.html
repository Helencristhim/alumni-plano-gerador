Chapter 4: The Code

Requests & Preferences

Polite ways to order and to say what you like

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I would like the fish, please."

"Can I have some water?"

"I prefer the salad."

"Would you like some coffee?"

UseFormExample
Polite request (order)I would like + nounI would like the fish.
Ask for somethingCan I have + noun?Can I have the bill?
Your preferenceI prefer + nounI prefer the salad.
Offer to someoneWould you like + noun?Would you like some coffee?

"I would like" and "Can I have" are polite ways to order — much better than "I want." Use "I prefer" to say what you like more: "I prefer fish." Use "Would you like" to offer food or drink to another person.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I want the fish.
I would like the fish, please.
I prefer than the salad.
I prefer the salad.

To order food, "I want" sounds rude. Say "I would like the fish, please" instead. And do not put than after "prefer": say "I prefer the salad", not "I prefer than the salad".
