Stage 1.1: Opinion Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you share what you think and react to other people's ideas.

Opinion -- opiniao
"In my opinion, the old town is the best part of the city."
To agree -- concordar
"I totally agree with you about the food here."
To disagree -- discordar
"I have to disagree -- I think the beach is better."
Point of view -- ponto de vista
"I understand your point of view, but I see it differently."
To prefer -- preferir
"I prefer mountains to the beach."
A good point -- um bom argumento
"That's a good point -- I hadn't thought of that."
To be honest -- para ser sincero(a)
"To be honest, I didn't enjoy the museum."
On the other hand -- por outro lado
"It's expensive; on the other hand, the view is amazing."
It depends -- depende
"It depends on the weather and on your mood."
To convince -- convencer
"He convinced me to try the local dish."
To see your point -- entender seu argumento
"I see your point, and you may be right."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the conversation and answer the questions.

On a tour bus, a traveler shares his opinion: "In my opinion, the food is the best part of this trip." Pricila agrees in part: "That's a good point, but I'd say the nature is just as special." The traveler smiles. "I see your point. On the other hand, you can taste the culture at the market." "To be honest," Pricila replies, "I prefer to do both. It depends on the day!" They both laugh -- he almost convinced her to visit the food stalls first.

1. What is the traveler's opinion?
A Food is the best part of the trip.
B Nature is the best part of the trip.
C The tour bus is too slow.
2. How does Pricila disagree politely?
A She says he is completely wrong.
B She says "That's a good point, but..." and adds her view.
C She says nothing at all.
3. What does Pricila finally prefer?
A Only the food.
B Only the nature.
C To do both -- it depends on the day.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Giving & Reacting to Opinions

Grammar

Para dar uma opiniao em ingles, voce abre a frase com expressoes como "In my opinion" ou "I think", e justifica com "because". Para concordar ou discordar com educacao, comece com algo gentil ("That's a good point") antes de adicionar sua propria ideia. / To give an opinion, open with "In my opinion" or "I think" and support it with "because". To agree or disagree politely, start with something kind ("That's a good point") before you add your own view.

Funcao / FunctionEnglishPortugues
Give an opinionIn my opinion, the city is lovely.Na minha opiniao, a cidade e linda.
AgreeI totally agree with you.Concordo totalmente com voce.
Disagree (polite)That's a good point, but I'd say...E um bom argumento, mas eu diria...
Give a reasonI prefer mountains because they are quiet.Prefiro montanhas porque sao calmas.
Stay neutralIt depends; on the other hand...Depende; por outro lado...

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A traveler says the museum was boring, but you loved it. How do you disagree politely?
A "You're completely wrong."
B "I see your point, but to be honest, I really enjoyed it."
C Say nothing and look away.
Someone asks if the beach or the mountains are better. You can't decide. What do you say?
A "It depends on the weather, but I prefer the mountains."
B "I don't know anything."
C "Both are the same."
You want to share an opinion and give a reason. Which sentence is best?
A "The food. Good."
B "In my opinion, the food is the best part because you taste the culture."
C "Maybe yes, maybe no."

Reflection
