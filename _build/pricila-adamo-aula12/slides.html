Chapter 3: Although & Whereas

Contrast in One Sentence

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Although I see your point, I still disagree."

"You love museums, whereas I prefer markets."

"Even though it was expensive, it was worth it."

"While you plan everything, I leave room for surprises."

LinkerUseExample
Although / Even thoughconcession (surprise contrast)Although it rained, we enjoyed it.
Whereas / Whiledirect contrast of two ideasYou like cities, whereas I like the coast.

All four join two full clauses (subject + verb on each side). When the linker opens the sentence, use a comma before the main clause: Although I agree, I still prefer the sea.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

Although the long trip, we loved it.
Although the trip was long, we loved it.

After although / even though / while, use a full clause (subject + verb), not just a noun.

Despite it was expensive, it was worth it.
Despite the high price, it was worth it.

Use despite / in spite of + a noun or -ing -- never a full clause.

Chapter 4: A Friendly Standoff

Disagreeing Gracefully

Dialogue

A Day, Two Plans

T
Honestly, I think we should skip the coast. It's a long drive for a beach.
P
That's a fair point, the drive is long. With all due respect, though, I beg to differ -- the sunset there is extraordinary.
T
Let me play devil's advocate. Whereas the coast is calm, the old town has the markets you love.
P
I'll concede that. Although I'd love the markets, I don't want to be at odds with you all day. Can we meet halfway?
T
I think we just found our common ground. Markets in the morning, coast for sunset.
P
Perfect. Even though we started at odds, we never had to agree to disagree. That's a good travel partner.

A Real Review

A Balanced Review

Cliff Walk & Old Town · A Traveler's Review

By P. Adamo · The Wanderer Review

Although some visitors find the cliff walk too long, I would happily beg to differ -- the sunset alone is a fair point in its favor.

Whereas the old town can feel crowded, the markets are a real treasure, and I'd gladly take issue with anyone who calls them overrated.

Even though my companion and I were briefly at odds about the plan, we found common ground and met halfway: town by day, coast at dusk.

What does the writer beg to differ about?
That the cliff walk is too long -- she values the sunset.
How did the writer and her companion solve their disagreement?
They found common ground and met halfway: town by day, coast at dusk.
