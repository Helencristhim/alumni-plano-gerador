Lesson 14 -- Pre-class

Getting Around the City

How to compare your transport options in a big city and recommend the smartest way across town. Key words: toll, gridlock, shortcut, roundabout, one-way street, ferry, timetable, crowded, convenient, reliable, scenic, to drop off. Structure: comparatives (-er / more + than) and superlatives (the -est / the most).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Toll -- a fee you pay to use a road, bridge, or tunnel
"The toll on the tunnel is higher than the bridge."
Gridlock -- a traffic jam so bad that cars cannot move
"There is heavy gridlock near the bridges."
Shortcut -- a quicker route than the usual one
"This alley is a shortcut to the station."
Roundabout -- a circular junction where traffic flows around a center
"Take the second exit at the roundabout."
One-way street -- a street where cars may drive in only one direction
"You cannot turn here; it is a one-way street."
Ferry -- a boat that carries people across water on a set route
"The ferry is the most scenic way to cross the river."
Timetable -- a schedule showing when buses or trains run
"Check the timetable before you leave."
Crowded -- full of people, with very little space
"The subway is crowded at rush hour."
Convenient -- easy to use and saving you time or effort
"A rideshare is more convenient with luggage."
Reliable -- working well and unlikely to fail or be late
"The subway is the most reliable option."
Scenic -- offering beautiful views along the way
"Take the scenic route by the water."
To drop off -- to take someone to a place and leave them there
"Can you drop me off at the corner?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Lucimara needs to cross New York at rush hour. The roads are more crowded than the tracks, and there is heavy gridlock near the bridges, so the subway is faster than a taxi. The express is quicker than the local, and it is the most reliable ride at this hour. On the way back she wants a nicer view, so she takes the ferry: it is slower, but it is the most scenic route and less crowded than the trains. If she drives later, the bridge is cheaper than the tunnel, because the toll is lower this week.

1. 'The subway is faster than a taxi' compares how many options, and which form is it?
A Two options -- a comparative (-er + than).
B Three options -- a superlative.
C One option -- no comparison.
2. Why does the text say 'the most reliable' and not 'more reliable'?
A Because it compares only two trains.
B Because it picks the top ride out of several options.
C Because 'reliable' is a short word.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A A taxi is more expensive than the train.
B A taxi is expensiver than the train.
C A taxi is more expensiver than the train.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

GRAMMAR

How to compare your options clearly.

FormUseExample
Comparative -- short wordadd -er + than: compare two thingsThe subway is faster than the bus.
Comparative -- long wordmore / less + word + than: compare two thingsA taxi is more expensive than the train.
Superlativethe + -est, or the most / least: top of three or moreThe ferry is the most scenic. Walking is the cheapest.
Irregularlearn these two by heartgood → better → the best; bad → worse → the worst.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real travel situation.

At rush hour, you compare the subway and a taxi. You say:
A The subway is faster than a taxi.
B The subway is more fast than a taxi.
C The subway is the faster than a taxi.
You recommend the top option out of several. You say:
A The ferry is the most scenic way back.
B The ferry is most scenic way back.
C The ferry is more scenic way back.
You compare the price of walking and a taxi. You say:
A Walking is cheaper than a taxi.
B Walking is cheapest than a taxi.
C Walking is more cheap than a taxi.
You describe the best ride using an irregular form. You say:
A The express is the best way to travel south.
B The express is the most good way to travel south.
C The express is the goodest way to travel south.
