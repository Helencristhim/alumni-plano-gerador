Chapter 4: Comparing Them

Comparing Your Options

-er / more + than · the -est / the most · good → better → best

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The subway is faster than the bus."

"A taxi is more expensive than the train."

"The ferry is the most scenic way to travel."

Two of these compare only two things; one picks the top of several. Which words change, and how?

FormUseExample
Comparative -- short wordadd -er + than: compare two thingsThe subway is faster than the bus.
Comparative -- long wordmore / less + word + than: compare two thingsA taxi is more expensive than the train.
Superlativethe + -est, or the most / least: top of three or moreThe ferry is the most scenic. Walking is the cheapest.
Irregularlearn these two by heartgood → better → the best; bad → worse → the worst.

Use a comparative (-er + than, or more/less + than) to compare two options, and a superlative (the + -est, or the most/least) to pick the top of three or more.

Language Focus

Complete the Comparison

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The subway is more fast than the bus."

✓ RIGHT

The subway is faster than the bus.

✗ WRONG

"A taxi is expensiver than the train."

✓ RIGHT

A taxi is more expensive than the train.

Short words take -er (fast → faster). Long words take more/less (expensive → more expensive). Never mix the two -- 'more faster' and 'expensiver' do not exist.

Voices on the Street

Register & Tone

Ask It Politely

Tap to see the friendlier, more natural version

Instead of: "What is the fastest way?"
Say: Excuse me, what is the fastest way to get to Central Park from here?
Instead of: "Subway or taxi?"
Say: At this hour, would the subway be quicker than a taxi?
Instead of: "Drop me here."
Say: Could you drop me off at the corner of 42nd and 5th, please?
Instead of: "Which is best?"
Say: Which do you think is the best way across town right now?
Dialogue

Asking a Local for the Best Route

L
Excuse me, I need to get to the Museum of Modern Art. What is the fastest way from here?
M
At this hour? The subway is faster than a taxi, for sure. The roads are more crowded right now -- there is real gridlock on 5th.
L
The subway it is. Is the express quicker than the local?
M
Much quicker. Take the express and it is the most reliable ride you will get. Just avoid the platform at the far end -- it is the most crowded one.
L
Good to know. And on the way back, I would love a nicer view. Any ideas?
M
Then take the ferry. It is slower, but it is by far the most scenic route, and it is less crowded than the trains.
L
Perfect. So the fastest way there, the prettiest way back. Thank you so much!
M
You got it. One last tip: if you drive later, the bridge is cheaper than the tunnel -- the toll is lower this week. Enjoy the city!

Listening 2

How to Compare

A short tip on comparing your options with the right words. Sound first -- no text.

0:000:00
1. For a long word like 'expensive', which words do you add to compare two things?
'more' or 'less' before the word, plus 'than'.
2. To pick the top of three or more, which form do you use?
A superlative with 'the' -- the fastest, or the most scenic.
3. What are the irregular forms of 'good' and 'bad'?
good - better - the best; bad - worse - the worst.
