Chapter 3

The Engine

to be + adjective, third person, and contrast

Grammar Discovery

What Are They Like?

"She is very reliable."

"He helps the team every day."

"He is direct, but he can also be thoughtful."

"What is she like? She is outgoing."

StructureExample
to be + adjectiveShe is reliable. They are patient.
third person + "-s"He helps the team. She works hard.
contrastHe is direct, but he can also be thoughtful.
questionWhat is she like? What does he do?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"He very patient."

✓ RIGHT

"He is very patient."

✗ WRONG

"She work hard."

✓ RIGHT

"She works hard."

✗ WRONG

"How she is like?"

✓ RIGHT

"What is she like?"

Use "is/are" before an adjective. With he/she/it, add "-s" to the verb. To ask about personality, say "What is she like?"

Well, she is very reliable and patient. She never misses a deadline.
D

V

How long have you known her?
