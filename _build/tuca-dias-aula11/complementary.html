Lesson 11 -- Getting Around: Public Transport

Series / Travel
Rick Steves' Europe -- City Transport Episodes (PBS / YouTube)

The host moves around big European cities using the subway, trams, and buses, explaining how to buy tickets, read maps, and transfer between lines. Full of natural advice with should and could.

Watch one episode with English subtitles. Write three sentences of advice for a tourist using should, could, and might.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "Travel English: Getting Around the City"

A short, clear episode about asking for directions and using public transport in English. You will hear the modal verbs should, could, and might used naturally in real travel situations.

After listening, record a one-minute voice note planning a trip across a city. Use the words subway, fare, transfer, and get off.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Modal Verbs: should, could, might"

A practical grammar video that explains how to give advice, offer options, and talk about possibilities, with clear examples. Exactly today's grammar focus.

After watching, write four sentences about your own city's transport, one with should, one with could, and two with might.
