Getting Around -- Public Transport

Learn to use the subway and the bus across a big city, and use the modal verbs should, could, and might to give advice, offer options, and talk about possibilities. New vocabulary: platform, fare, subway, schedule, transfer, line, rush hour, ticket machine, get on, get off.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Platform -- the flat area beside the track where you wait for a train
"Your train leaves from platform two." (Plataforma)
Fare -- the price you pay to travel by bus, train, or taxi
"A single fare costs two dollars." (Tarifa / passagem)
Subway -- an underground train system in a big city
"The subway is faster than a taxi at rush hour." (Metrô)
Schedule -- a list of the times when buses or trains arrive and leave
"Check the schedule before you leave home." (Horário)
Transfer -- to change from one bus or train line to another
"You transfer to the red line at the central station." (Fazer baldeação / trocar de linha)
Line -- a subway or bus service, often named by a color or number
"Take the green line and ride four stops." (Linha)
Rush hour -- the busy time when many people travel to or from work
"The platform is very crowded during rush hour." (Horário de pico)
Ticket machine -- a machine where you buy a ticket with coins or a card
"You can buy a ticket at the ticket machine." (Máquina de bilhetes)
Get on -- to enter a bus, train, or plane
"Get on the green line and ride four stops." (Entrar / embarcar)
Get off -- to leave a bus, train, or plane
"Get off at the market station and meet me at the exit." (Sair / desembarcar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca arrives in a new city and wants to reach the market. A local passenger gives her some advice: "You should buy a ticket at the ticket machine first." Advice uses should. Then he offers her two choices: "You could take the subway, or you could take the bus." When you talk about options you can choose, you use could. Finally, he warns her about something that is not certain: "The platform might be crowded at rush hour." For something that is only possible, you use might. Notice that after should, could, and might, the verb has no to: should buy, could take, might be.

1. "You should buy a ticket first." Why does he use should?
A To give advice -- the best thing to do
B To describe something only possible
C To talk about the past
2. "You could take the subway or the bus." What does could show here?
A A strong order
B Two options you can choose
C Something that already happened
3. "The platform might be crowded." What does might mean?
A It is certain and always true
B It is possible, but not sure
C It is the best advice

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Should / Could / Might

GRAMMAR

Três verbos modais para dar conselhos, oferecer opções e falar de possibilidades. / Three modal verbs for advice, options, and possibilities.

ModalUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
shouldconselho, a melhor ideia / advice, the best idea"You should buy a ticket first."
couldopção ou possibilidade que você pode escolher / an option you can choose"You could take the bus or the subway."
mightalgo possível, talvez, sem certeza / something possible, maybe"The train might be late."

Key point / Ponto-chave: após should, could e might, use o verbo na forma base, sem to: should buy, could take, might be. Negativo de conselho: should not (shouldn't). / After these modals, use the base verb with no to.
