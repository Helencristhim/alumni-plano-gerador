Chapter 3 -- Modal Verbs

Grammar Discovery

Should, Could, Might

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Advice? An option you can choose? Something only possible?

"You should buy a ticket before you get on."

"You could take the bus or the subway."

"The platform might be crowded at rush hour."

"You should not miss your stop."

Grammar Rule

Should, Could, Might

ModalUseExample
shouldadvice, the best ideaYou should buy a ticket first.
couldan option or a possibility you can chooseYou could take the bus or the subway.
mightsomething possible, maybe, not sureThe train might be late.

Key idea: after should, could, and might, use the base verb with no to: should buy, could take, might be. For advice in the negative, use should not (shouldn't).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You should to buy a ticket first."
"You should buy a ticket first."

After should, could, and might, use the base verb with no to: should buy, could take, might be.
