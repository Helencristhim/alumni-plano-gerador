Chapter 3: Background and Now

Your Story in Three Tenses

Past · Past-to-Now · Now

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I studied medicine in Brazil."

"I have worked as a doctor for ten years."

"I am a family doctor."

"Right now, I am pursuing a new license."

Which one is a finished moment, which one links past to now, which one is who I am today, and which one is happening right now?

UseForm
Past simplefinished time · "I studied", "I graduated in 2012"
Present perfectpast → now · have + past participle · "I have worked for ten years"
Present simplewho I am now · "I am a doctor", "I work at a clinic"
Present continuousright now · "I am pursuing", "I am learning"

Golden rule: for "how long", use the present perfect ("I have worked here for ten years"), never the present simple with "since".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work here since two years."
"I have worked here for two years."
"I am doctor from Brazil."
"I am a doctor from Brazil."
"I have graduated in 2012."
"I graduated in 2012."

Use present perfect + for (not "since" with present simple), remember the article a doctor, and use the past simple with an exact year.

That is great. Before nursing, I actually worked as a paramedic, so I understand career transitions well.

Really? So you changed your field too. How long did that transition take you?
