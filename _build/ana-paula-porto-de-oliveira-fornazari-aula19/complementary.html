Self introduction example at a job interview -- Practise Speaking English (mmmEnglish)

A clear, friendly lesson that models how to introduce yourself as a professional: your name, your field, your background, and your experience, in natural, confident English. Connection to Lesson 19: this is exactly the structure you practiced tonight -- who you are, what you have done, and where you are heading.

Tip: pause after the model introduction and record your own version out loud, using the present perfect for "how long" and the present simple for who you are now.

Two hosts talk about following a professional dream and what it really takes to build a career you love. They use clear, everyday English about work, training, and ambition. Connection to Lesson 19: it is the same language of professional identity and career choices you worked on tonight -- field, transition, and pursuing a goal.

TED Talk
Why Some of Us Don't Have One True Calling -- Emilie Wapnick (TED)

A warm, encouraging talk about people who follow many paths and reinvent their careers, and why changing direction is a strength, not a weakness. Connection to Lesson 19: it speaks directly to a professional in transition, celebrating adaptability and the value of everything you have already learned.

Tip: watch with English subtitles, then describe your own transition in three sentences, using "I studied", "I have worked", and "right now, I am".
