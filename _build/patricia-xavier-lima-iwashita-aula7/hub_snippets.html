Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Need -- something a customer must have to succeed (necessidade)
"Their main need is faster onboarding."
Requirement -- a specific thing that is necessary or demanded (requisito / exigência)
"Clear reporting is a key requirement."
Expectation -- what a customer believes will or should happen (expectativa)
"The solution should meet their expectation."
Fit -- how well a solution matches a need (encaixe / adequação)
"Let us find the right fit for your team."
Solution -- a product or way to solve a problem (solução)
"This solution fits your main need."
Recommend -- to say that something is good or right to do (recomendar)
"I would recommend the premium option."
Suggest -- to offer an idea for someone to consider (sugerir)
"Can I suggest a quick demo?"
Option -- one of several things you can choose (opção)
"You have two options for the rollout."
Benefit -- a good result or advantage (benefício)
"The main benefit is less manual work."
Outcome -- the final result of an action or process (resultado / desfecho)
"A happy customer is the best outcome."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

On a discovery call, Patrícia listens for the customer's main need. Mike says onboarding is slow, so she makes a recommendation. She says, "You should enable the new dashboard," and "You could start with a small rollout." For a softer idea, she adds, "Why don't you book a quick demo?" When she gives strong advice, she says, "I'd recommend training the team first," or "I recommend that you train the team first." Notice the form: after should, could, and why don't you, the verb has no to; after recommend, she uses -ing or that you + base verb — never "recommend you to".

1. Por que dizemos "You should enable" e não "You should to enable"?
A Porque "enable" é um verbo irregular.
B Porque depois de should/could usamos o verbo na forma base, sem "to".
C Porque "should" sempre pede o passado.
2. Depois de "recommend", qual forma do verbo usamos?
A "-ing" (recommend training) ou "(that) you + base" (recommend that you train).
B "to + verbo" (recommend you to train).
C sempre o passado (recommend trained).
3. Which recommendation is correct?
A "Why don't you to book a demo?"
B "Why don't you book a demo?"
C "Why don't you booking a demo?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Recommending (should / could / recommend)

GRAMMAR

Como recomendar uma solução de forma clara e educada (explicação em inglês e português).

FormStructure / EstruturaExample
should / couldConselho: should/could + verbo base (sem "to").You should enable it.
I'd recommendRecomendação: I'd recommend + verbo-ing.I'd recommend training the team.
I recommend thatAlternativa: I recommend (that) you + verbo base.I recommend that you train the team.
Why don't youSugestão leve: Why don't you + verbo base?Why don't you book a demo?
CuidadoNunca "should TO" nem "recommend you TO". Depois de should/could: verbo base. Depois de recommend: -ing ou "that you + base".
