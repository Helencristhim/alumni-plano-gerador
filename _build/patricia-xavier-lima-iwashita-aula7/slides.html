Chapter 3: The Code

Making a Recommendation

You should... · You could... · I'd recommend... · Why don't you...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should enable the new dashboard."

"You could start with a small rollout."

"I'd recommend training the team first."

"Why don't you book a quick demo?"

What form is the green verb in?

FormStructureExample
should / couldshould / could + base verb (no "to")You should enable it.
I'd recommendI'd recommend + verb-ingI'd recommend training the team.
I recommend thatI recommend (that) + you + base verbI recommend that you train the team.
Why don't youWhy don't you + base verb?Why don't you book a demo?
Watch outNo "to" after should / could. After recommend, use -ing or (that) you + base — never "recommend you to".

After should / could / why don't you, use the base verb: no "to", no "-s".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"You should to enable the dashboard."
"You should enable the dashboard."
"I recommend you to train the team."
"I recommend that you train the team."

After should or could, use the base verb with no to. After recommend, use -ing ("recommend training") or (that) you + base ("recommend that you train") — never "recommend you to".

Chapter 4: With the Customer

Find the Fit

Dialogue

The Discovery Call

P
Thanks for the call, Mike. To find the right fit, what is your team's main need right now?
M
Our biggest need is faster onboarding for new users.
P
I see. Then I'd recommend starting with a guided rollout.
M
That sounds good. What about reporting? That is a requirement for us.
P
You could enable the analytics dashboard. Why don't you try it for a week?
M
Great idea. I think that solution fits our expectations.
