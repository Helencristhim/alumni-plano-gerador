Grammar Rule

Could I Have...?

You wantStructureExample
A thingCould I have + thing, please?Could I have the menu, please?
A dishCould I have + dish, please?Could I have the fish, please?
An actionCould you + verb, please?Could you bring water, please?
The billCould I have + the bill, please?Could I have the bill, please?

Key idea: to ask for a thing, use Could I have + thing + please. To ask for an action, use Could you + verb + please. Could is more polite than can. Always add please.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want the menu." (not polite at a restaurant)
"Could I have the menu, please?"

At a restaurant, use Could I have... please? to be polite. Always name the thing, and add please.
