Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

Rafael prepares for a business trip. First, he writes an email with a clear subject line and sends the agenda as an attachment. He checks the figures for the quarter: the growth is better than last year. On the phone, a client speaks fast, so he says, "Could you speak more slowly, please?" At the airport, he passes customs and finds his gate. At the hotel, the clerk gives him his key card. Later, at dinner, he decides to order the fish and asks, "Is there any water?" Finally, he asks for the bill and remembers the deadline for the report on Friday.

1. How does Rafael send the agenda?
A As an attachment in an email.
B At the airport gate.
C On the hotel key card.
2. What does he say when the client speaks fast?
A "How many is the room?"
B "Could you speak more slowly, please?"
C "I would like any water."
3. How is the growth this quarter?
A The same as last year.
B Better than last year.
C Worse than last year.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Your Three Tools in Review

Grammar

Esta aula revisa as três ferramentas dos blocos 7 a 11. Peça com educação usando Can / Could you...?. Compare duas coisas com -er than (short adjectives) ou more ... than (long adjectives). E escolha some em frases afirmativas e any em perguntas e negativas; use How many com contáveis e How much com incontáveis. / Ask politely with Can / Could you. Compare with -er than or more ... than. Use some in positive sentences and any in questions and negatives; How many with countable nouns and How much with uncountable nouns.

Tool / FerramentaEnglishPortuguês
Could you...? (request)Could you repeat that, please?Você poderia repetir, por favor?
-er than (compare)Our profit is higher than last year.Nosso lucro é maior que no ano passado.
some (+ frase)I would like some water.Eu gostaria de um pouco de água.
any (? / negativo)Is there any news?Tem alguma novidade?
How much (incontável)How much is the room?Quanto custa o quarto?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

You finish a business email and want to attach the agenda. What do you write?
A "Please find the agenda in the attachment."
B "Where is the airport gate?"
C "Give me water now."
On a video call, the speaker is too fast. What is the polite request?
A "Speak slowly now."
B "Could you speak more slowly, please?"
C "How much you speak?"
You want to compare this quarter's profit with last year. What is correct?
A "Our profit is more better."
B "Our profit is higher than last year."
C "Our profit is the higher."
