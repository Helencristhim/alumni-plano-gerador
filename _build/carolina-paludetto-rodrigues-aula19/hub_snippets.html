Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Vegetable -- a plant you eat for a meal, like a carrot or a tomato
"I eat a lot of vegetables to stay healthy." (Legume / verdura)
Fruit -- a sweet food that grows on a tree or plant, like an apple
"An apple is my favorite fruit." (Fruta)
Breakfast -- the first meal of the day, in the morning
"I eat fruit and yogurt for breakfast." (Café da manhã)
Snack -- a small amount of food you eat between meals
"After school, I have a healthy snack." (Lanche)
Hungry -- when you want to eat because your stomach is empty
"I am hungry, so I want a snack." (Com fome)
Thirsty -- when you want to drink water because your body needs it
"After sports I am thirsty, so I drink water." (Com sede)
Sugar -- the sweet white thing in candy, cake and soda
"Too much sugar is not good for you." (Açúcar)
Fresh -- new, just made or just picked, not old
"I like fresh fruit from the market." (Fresco)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- how much, how many & quantities

GRAMMAR

Use How many + coisas contaveis (apples, eggs) e How much + coisas nao contaveis (sugar, water). Use a few para um numero pequeno (a few nuts) e a little para uma quantidade pequena (a little honey). Use some em frases afirmativas e any em negativas e perguntas. / Use How many + countable nouns (apples, eggs) and How much + uncountable nouns (sugar, water). Use a few for a small number (a few nuts) and a little for a small amount (a little honey). Use some in positive sentences and any in negatives and questions.

WordUseExample
How manythings you can countHow many apples?
How muchthings you can't countHow much sugar?
a fewa small number (count)a few nuts
a littlea small amount (can't count)a little honey
some / anysome (+), any (- / ?)some milk / not any juice

Key point / Ponto-chave: use How many + contaveis (apples), NUNCA "How much apples". Use a little + nao contaveis (a little sugar), NUNCA "a few sugar". Use a lot of (com of!) para uma quantidade grande de qualquer um. Diferença da aula 18: lá falamos de SAÚDE (I feel, I have a); aqui falamos de COMIDA e QUANTIDADES.
