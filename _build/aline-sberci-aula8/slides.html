Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

can / can't for ability

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes after can? Is there a to?

"I can give directions and order coffee."

"I can't write a formal email yet."

"How well can you talk about your routine?"

"Can you describe your last trip? Yes, I can."

Grammar Rule

Can & Can't

FormExample
Affirmative: can + base verbI can speak. She can give directions.
Negative: cannot / can'tI can't write a report yet.
Question: Can + you + base verb?Can you describe your routine?
Short answerYes, I can. / No, I can't.

Key idea: can is followed by the base verb with no to -- we say I can speak, not I can to speak. The verb never changes: she can speak, not she can speaks. To ask about ability, use How well can you...?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I can to give directions."
"I can give directions."

After can, use the base verb with no to and no s: say I can give and she can write, not I can to give or she can writes.
