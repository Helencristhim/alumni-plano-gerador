Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Used to -- a regular habit or state in the past, but not now
"I used to drink three coffees a day." (costumava / tinha o hábito de)
Habit -- an action you do regularly, often without thinking
"I changed one bad habit at a time." (hábito / costume)
Lifestyle -- the way you live: your habits, work, and routine
"My lifestyle changed over the years." (estilo de vida)
Work out -- to do physical exercise to stay fit and healthy
"I started to work out twice a week." (malhar / fazer exercício)
Give up -- to stop doing something completely
"I gave up the elevator and took the stairs." (desistir / parar de vez)
Cut down -- to reduce something; to do or have less of it
"I cut down on coffee, from three cups to one." (reduzir / diminuir)
Energy -- the strength to be active and not feel tired
"Today I have so much more energy." (energia / disposição)
Gradually -- slowly, little by little, over a period of time
"I did not change overnight; I changed gradually." (gradualmente / aos poucos)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A few years ago, my lifestyle was very different. I used to drink three coffees a day, and every night I would stay up late watching television. I never used to work out, so I had very little energy. Then I decided to change one habit at a time. I did not give up everything at once; I just cut down slowly. I changed gradually, step by step, and today I feel younger and have so much more energy.

1. What form of the verb comes after "used to" and "would"?
A the base verb (drink, stay)
B the -ing form (drinking, staying)
C the past form (drank, stayed)
2. Did the speaker give up everything at once?
A Yes, all habits in one day.
B No, he or she cut down slowly and changed gradually.
C No, nothing changed at all.
3. What is the result of the change?
A The speaker feels more tired now.
B The speaker feels younger and has much more energy.
C The speaker drinks more coffee than before.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Habits (Used To / Would)

GRAMMAR

Para falar de hábitos do passado, usamos used to + verbo na forma base (para hábitos E estados) ou would + verbo na forma base (somente para ações repetidas, não estados). Em perguntas e negativas, tiramos o -d: did you use to...? / I did not use to.... / To talk about past habits, use used to + base verb (for habits AND states) or would + base verb (for repeated actions only, not states). In questions and negatives, drop the -d: did you use to...? / I did not use to....

FormUseExample
used to + base verbhabits and states"I used to live downtown."
would + base verbrepeated actions only"I would walk every weekend."
did not use tonegative (no -d)"I never used to work out."
Did you use to...?question (no -d)"Did you use to live here?"
