Lesson 13 -- Pre-class

Used To, Be Used To, Get Used To -- Change and Adaptation

Ana joins a fully remote team and talks with her new team lead about the transition: what she used to do, what she is used to now, and what she is still getting used to. Key words: adaptation, transition, to adjust, mindset, comfort zone, to embrace, unfamiliar, gradual, to settle in, resilience, shift, to thrive. Structure: used to / be used to / get used to.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Adaptation -- the process of changing to fit a new situation
"Every big change needs a period of adaptation."
Transition -- a change from one situation, job, or state to another
"The transition to remote work took a few weeks."
To adjust -- to change slightly to fit a new situation
"It took time to adjust to the new schedule."
Mindset -- a person's usual way of thinking and attitude
"A growth mindset helps you learn from mistakes."
Comfort zone -- the familiar situation where you feel safe and relaxed
"Learning English pushes me out of my comfort zone."
To embrace -- to accept something new willingly and with enthusiasm
"Good teams embrace change instead of fearing it."
Unfamiliar -- not known to you; new and a little strange
"The new tools felt unfamiliar at first."
Gradual -- happening slowly, in small steps
"Getting used to a new team is a gradual process."
To settle in -- to become comfortable in a new place or role
"It took a month to settle in at the new company."
Resilience -- the ability to recover quickly from difficulty
"Resilience helps you keep going when work is hard."
Shift -- a change in position, focus, or direction
"There was a big shift in how we work after 2020."
To thrive -- to grow and do very well
"Some people thrive when everything changes."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana just joined a new, fully remote team. It is a big transition. She used to work in an office every day, so the silence at home is still unfamiliar. On her first week, almost everything is outside her comfort zone. But her mindset is good: she knows nobody is used to a new routine on day one. Adaptation is a gradual process. Little by little, she is getting used to writing things down instead of just talking. She used to hate video calls, but now she does not mind them. With a bit of resilience, she is starting to thrive. Notice the pattern: used to + base verb is a past habit ("I used to work"), while be/get used to + -ing means being or becoming accustomed ("I am used to working").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Used To / Be Used To / Get Used To

GRAMMAR

How to talk about a past habit, a state you are comfortable with, and a change in progress.

StructureMeaningForm & Example
used toa past habit, not true nowused to + base verb — I used to work in an office.
be used toalready accustomed (a state)be used to + noun / -ing — I am used to working alone.
get used tobecoming accustomed (a process)get used to + noun / -ing — I got used to the routine.
Negative (past)did not use toI didn't use to like video calls. (no -d after "did not")
Key ruleAfter used to (past habit) use the base verb. After be/get used to use a noun or -ing — never "I am used to work".
