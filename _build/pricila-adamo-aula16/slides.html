Chapter 3: Asking Politely

Ask the Polite Way

Indirect questions · softer · more polite

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Could you tell me what the rate is?"

"Do you know if there is a fee?"

"Could you tell me where the ATM is?"

"I'd like to know whether I can pay contactless."

Direct questionPolite (indirect)
What is the rate?Could you tell me what the rate is?
Is there a fee?Do you know if there is a fee?
Where is the ATM?Could you tell me where the ATM is?
Can I pay contactless?I'd like to know whether I can pay contactless.

After Could you tell me / Do you know, the word order is normal (subject + verb): "what the rate is", not "what is the rate". For yes/no questions, add if or whether.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me what is the rate?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me what the rate is?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you know there is a fee?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you know if there is a fee?"

Chapter 4: At the Bank

Changing Money at the Counter

Pricila exchanges her currency

Dialogue

Pricila Changes Money

P
Good morning. I'd like to change some money. Could you tell me what the rate is today?
T
Good morning. The rate is on the screen, and it's quite good today. How much would you like to change?
P
Two hundred, please. Do you know if there is a fee on the transaction?
T
Yes, we charge a small fee of two percent. Here are your banknotes. Would you like a receipt?
P
Yes, please. And could you tell me where the nearest ATM is?
T
Of course. There's one by the door, and it's contactless. Enjoy your trip!
