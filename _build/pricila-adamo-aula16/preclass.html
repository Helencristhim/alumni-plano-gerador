Stage 1.1: Words for Money & Tipping

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for handling bills and tips.

A service charge -- uma taxa de servico
"A ten percent service charge is added to the bill."
A surcharge -- uma sobretaxa
"A small surcharge is charged for paying by card."
A markup -- uma margem / sobrepreco
"There is a big markup on drinks at the hotel."
A hidden cost -- um custo oculto
"Watch out for hidden costs when you book online."
To tip -- dar gorjeta
"In the US, you are expected to tip your waiter."
Gratuity -- gorjeta (termo formal)
"A gratuity is included for large groups."
To split the bill -- dividir a conta
"Shall we split the bill evenly?"
To go Dutch -- cada um paga o seu
"We decided to go Dutch at the restaurant."
A flat rate -- uma taxa fixa
"The taxi to the airport is a flat rate."
Loose change -- trocado / moedas soltas
"I keep some loose change for tips."
To keep the change -- ficar com o troco
"Keep the change, please."
Petty cash -- dinheiro para pequenas despesas
"We use petty cash for small tips and snacks."
A daily allowance -- uma diaria / verba diaria
"The tour gives you a daily allowance for meals."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read Pricila's notes about travel money and answer the questions.

"When you travel, it helps to understand how prices are calculated. In many restaurants, a service charge is automatically added to the bill. A small surcharge is charged for card payments, and hidden costs are sometimes buried in the fine print. In tourist areas, drinks are usually marked up, and gratuities are expected in some countries. On my last trip, the taxi fare was set at a flat rate, and the whole meal was paid for by my friend as a treat."

1. In "A service charge is added to the bill", do we know who adds it?
A No, and it is not important (agentless passive).
B Yes, the customer adds it.
C Yes, it says the waiter's name.
2. How do we form the present passive?
A is/are + past participle ("is added").
B is/are + base verb ("is add").
C will + verb ("will add").
3. When do we use "by + agent"?
A Always, in every passive sentence.
B When the person who did it is new or important info.
C Only in questions.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Passive Voice

Grammar

Use a voz passiva (be + participio passado) quando a ACAO importa mais do que quem a fez. Se quem fez for desconhecido ou obvio, omita o agente. Use by + agente so quando for informacao nova e importante. / Use the passive (be + past participle) when the ACTION matters more than who did it. If the doer is unknown or obvious, leave the agent out. Use by + agent only when it is new, important information.
