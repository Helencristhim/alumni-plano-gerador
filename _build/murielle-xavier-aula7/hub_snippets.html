More, Less, the Best -- Comparatives and Superlatives

Comparing HR data and performance across regions with comparatives (lower than, more efficient than) and superlatives (the lowest, the most cost-effective), plus irregular forms (better/best, worse/worst). New words: rate, turnover, benchmark, increase, decrease, gap, ranking, metric, efficient, cost-effective, outperform, trend.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Rate -- a measured number or index (taxa / índice)
"Our turnover rate is nine percent."
Turnover -- how many employees leave and are replaced (rotatividade)
"Turnover in Australia is lower than in Europe."
Benchmark -- a standard used to compare (referência / parâmetro)
"Europe is our benchmark for cost per hire."
Increase -- to go up or rise (aumentar)
"Applications increased by thirty percent."
Decrease -- to go down or fall (diminuir)
"Our cost per hire decreased this year."
Gap -- a difference between two things (lacuna / diferença)
"There is a big gap between our fastest and slowest region."
Ranking -- a list ordered by performance (classificação / ranking)
"Australia is at the top of the ranking."
Metric -- a number you measure (métrica / indicador)
"Time to hire is our most important metric."
Efficient -- doing well with little waste (eficiente)
"Our process is more efficient than last year."
Cost-effective -- good value for the money (bom custo-benefício)
"Europe is the most cost-effective region."
Outperform -- to do better than others (superar)
"Australia outperforms the benchmark on engagement."
Trend -- a general direction of change (tendência)
"The trend is positive: turnover keeps falling."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Murielle is preparing the quarterly review. Her metrics tell a clear story. The turnover rate in Australia is lower than in Europe — in fact, it is the lowest in the company. On cost, however, the picture is different: Australia's cost per hire is higher than the European benchmark, so Europe is still the most cost-effective region. The biggest gap is onboarding time. The good news is that the process is more efficient than last year, applications have increased, and costs have decreased. Retention is better than before, and the trend is positive. On engagement, Australia outperforms the benchmark and sits at the top of the ranking.

1. Why does the text say "lower than" but "the lowest"?
A They mean exactly the same thing.
B "Lower than" compares two things; "the lowest" is the top of a whole group.
C "The lowest" compares only two regions.
2. Why is it "the most cost-effective" and not "the cost-effectivest"?
A "Cost-effective" is a long adjective, so it takes "the most", not "-est".
B Long adjectives always take "-est".
C It should be "more cost-effective".
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Retention is more better than before."
B "Retention is better than before."
C "Retention is the better than before."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives and Superlatives

GRAMMAR

Adjetivos curtos: -er + than (comparativo) e the -est (superlativo). Adjetivos longos: more + than e the most. Irregulares: good → better → the best; bad → worse → the worst. (Comparatives compare two; superlatives pick the top of a group.)

TypeComparativeSuperlative
short adjective (low, fast)lower thanthe lowest
long adjective (efficient)more efficient thanthe most efficient
good / bad (irregular)better / worse thanthe best / the worst
degree / equalitymuch lower; almost as fast as Europe
