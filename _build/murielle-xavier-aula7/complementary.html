Lesson 7 -- More, Less, the Best -- Comparatives and Superlatives

Podcast
Freakonomics Radio -- an episode comparing data and performance

The hosts compare numbers and rankings across companies and countries. You will hear comparatives and superlatives used to make sense of data (higher than, the lowest, the most efficient). Direct connection to Lesson 7: comparing HR metrics.

Tip: write down 5 comparisons you hear, and mark which are comparatives (than) and which are superlatives (the).

Listen on Freakonomics ↗
Series
Shark Tank -- any pitch where investors compare the numbers

Investors constantly compare revenue, margins, and growth: whose deal is better, which company is the most profitable. Notice the comparatives and superlatives in real business talk. Connection to Lesson 7: comparing performance data.

Tip: pause after a number and say a comparison out loud: "This margin is higher than the other company's."

YouTube
"Comparatives and Superlatives" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on comparatives and superlatives: short adjectives with -er/-est, long adjectives with more/the most, irregular forms, and common mistakes. Connection to Lesson 7: the exact grammar you use to compare data.

Tip: pause and build your own comparisons with efficient, cost-effective, and good.
