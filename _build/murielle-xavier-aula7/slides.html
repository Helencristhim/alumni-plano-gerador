More, Less, the Best

Comparatives and superlatives for HR data

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Our turnover is lower than Europe."

"Australia has the lowest rate in the company."

"Europe is more cost-effective than Australia."

"Retention is better than before — it is the best year yet."

TypeComparativeSuperlative
short adjective (low, fast)lower thanthe lowest
long adjective (efficient)more efficient thanthe most efficient
good / bad (irregular)better / worse thanthe best / the worst
degree wordsmuch / a lot lower; almost as fast as Europe
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Our turnover is more lower than Europe."

✓ RIGHT

"Our turnover is lower than Europe."

✗ WRONG

"Retention is more better than before."

✓ RIGHT

"Retention is better than before."

✗ WRONG

"Europe is the efficientest region."

✓ RIGHT

"Europe is the most efficient region."

Short adjectives: -er / the -est. Long adjectives: more / the most. Never use "more" with an -er form, and never use -est with a long adjective.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Comparing regional HR data before the review

Dialogue

Comparing Two Regions

L
Hi Murielle. Before the review, how does Australia compare to Europe on turnover?
M
Good news. Our turnover is lower than Europe. In fact, it is the lowest rate in the company.
L
Excellent. And what about cost per hire?
M
Honestly, our cost is higher than the European benchmark. Europe is still the most cost-effective region.
L
So where is the biggest gap between us?
M
The biggest gap is cost. But our process is more efficient than last year, and the trend is positive.
L
Good. So what is your headline for the board?
M
We are more efficient than last year, we outperform the benchmark on retention, but we can learn from Europe on cost.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Australia's turnover compare to Europe's?
It is lower than Europe; it is the lowest rate in the company.
2. Which region is the most cost-effective?
Europe; Australia's cost per hire is higher than the European benchmark.
3. What is Murielle's headline for the board?
They are more efficient than last year and outperform the benchmark on retention, but can learn from Europe on cost.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can compare two numbers with short adjectives and than: our turnover is lower than Europe.
I can use superlatives: Australia has the lowest rate; Europe is the most cost-effective region.
I can use long adjectives with more and the most: more efficient than, the most efficient.
I can use irregular forms: good/better/best and bad/worse/worst.
I can use the new words: rate, turnover, benchmark, increase, decrease, gap, ranking, metric, efficient, cost-effective, outperform, trend.
