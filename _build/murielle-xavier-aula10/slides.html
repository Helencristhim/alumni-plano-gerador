Chapter 3

Modal Verbs

Advice, possibility, and obligation with should, could, might, and must

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should follow the policy first."

"You could offer him a mentor."

"He might need more support."

"Managers must ensure compliance."

ModalUseExample
should / shouldn'tAdvice, recommendationYou should follow the policy.
couldA soft suggestion or optionYou could offer a mentor.
might / mayPossibility (not certain)He might need more support.
must / have toObligation (a strong rule)You must ensure compliance.

After every modal, use the base verb — no "to", no "-s", no "-ing".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You should to follow the policy."

✓ RIGHT

"You should follow the policy."

✗ WRONG

"She musts ensure compliance."

✓ RIGHT

"She must ensure compliance."

✗ WRONG

"He should follows the guideline."

✓ RIGHT

"He should follow the guideline."

A modal verb never takes "to" or "-s". The verb after it is always the base form. For the negative, put "not" after the modal: should not, must not.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Advising a colleague on an HR decision

Dialogue

What Should We Do?

L
Murielle, I need your judgment. We have an analyst on probation and his manager sent a complaint. What should we do?
M
We should not rush. First, we should follow the policy and check the guidelines.
L
Good point. And if he needs help?
M
You could offer him a mentor. He might just need more support during the probation period.
L
And do we have to document all of this?
M
Yes, we must document everything and stay in full compliance. Every manager is accountable.
L
And if the problem continues after that?
M
Then we might have to give a formal warning. But first, we should give him a fair chance.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What did Murielle say they should do first?

2. What option did Murielle suggest to support the analyst?

3. What did Murielle say they must do?
