Lesson 10 -- Advice and Judgment -- Modal Verbs

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- an episode on giving feedback and difficult decisions

Adam Grant and his guests advise leaders on tough people-decisions: what a manager should do, what they could try, and what they must not ignore. You will hear modal verbs used for advice and judgment. Direct connection to Lesson 10: advising a colleague on an HR case.

Tip: write down 5 sentences of advice with should, could, might, and must + a base verb.

Series
The Office (US) -- any HR scene with Toby or Holly giving policy advice

Watch a scene where the HR representative advises Michael on what he should, could, or must do about an employee issue. Notice how modal verbs signal advice versus obligation. Connection to Lesson 10: professional judgment in HR conversations.

Tip: pause and rephrase the advice: "He should apologize. He must follow the policy."

YouTube
"Modal Verbs: should, could, must, might" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on modal verbs for advice, possibility, and obligation, and why the verb after a modal stays in the base form. Connection to Lesson 10: the exact grammar you use to give professional advice.

Tip: pause and build your own advice with policy, compliance, and appraisal.
