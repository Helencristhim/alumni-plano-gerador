Lesson 10 -- Pre-class

Advice and Judgment -- Modal Verbs

Giving advice, suggesting options, and stating obligations in HR conversations with modal verbs: should / shouldn't (advice), could (option), might / may (possibility), and must / have to (obligation). New words: recommend, policy, compliance, guideline, mentor, warning, probation, appraisal, mediate, accountable, discretion, reprimand.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Recommend -- to say that something is a good idea (recomendar / aconselhar)
"I recommend a mentor for the new analyst."
Policy -- an official company rule or plan (política / norma interna)
"We should follow the company policy first."
Compliance -- the act of following the rules and laws (conformidade / cumprimento)
"Managers must stay in full compliance."
Guideline -- advice on the correct way to do something (diretriz / orientação)
"You should check the guidelines carefully."
Mentor -- an experienced person who advises a colleague (mentor)
"You could offer him a mentor for the year."
Warning -- a formal notice about bad behavior or performance (advertência / aviso formal)
"We might have to give a formal warning."
Probation -- a trial period for a new employee (período de experiência)
"He is still on probation this quarter."
Appraisal -- a formal review of an employee's performance (avaliação de desempenho)
"Managers must complete the annual appraisal."
Mediate -- to help two sides reach an agreement (mediar)
"You could ask a senior colleague to mediate."
Accountable -- responsible and required to explain your decisions (responsável / que presta contas)
"Every manager is accountable for a fair process."
Discretion -- the freedom to decide something yourself (discrição / bom senso)
"You may use your discretion on small issues."
Reprimand -- a formal expression of disapproval (repreensão)
"A reprimand should be fair and documented."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Verbs

GRAMMAR

Os verbos modais mudam a força da frase. Use should para conselho, could para uma opção, might/may para possibilidade e must/have to para obrigação. Depois do modal, o verbo fica sempre na forma base (sem to, sem -s): "You should follow the policy."

ModalUseExample
should / shouldn'tAdvice, recommendation (conselho)"You should follow the policy."
couldA soft option (uma opção)"You could offer a mentor."
might / mayPossibility (possibilidade)"He might need more support."
must / have toObligation (obrigação)"You must ensure compliance."

Atenção: o modal nunca leva -s (musts ✗) nem to (should to ✗). Para a negativa, coloque not depois do modal: should not, must not.
