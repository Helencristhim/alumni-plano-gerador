Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Confident -- feeling sure of yourself (confiante, seguro de si)
"I feel confident when I prepare before I speak."
Nervous -- worried or a little afraid (nervoso, ansioso)
"I feel nervous when I speak in front of new people."
Comfortable -- relaxed and at ease (confortável, à vontade)
"I feel comfortable when the conversation is calm."
Fluent -- able to speak smoothly and easily (fluente)
"My goal is to become fluent and speak without stopping."
Clear -- easy to understand, simple and direct (claro, nítido)
"I understand more when the speaker is slow and clear."
Calm -- peaceful, without stress (calmo, tranquilo)
"I feel calm when my day has a clear structure."
Organized -- good at planning, neat and ordered (organizado)
"I am an organized person, so I like a clear plan."
Goal -- something you want to achieve (objetivo, meta)
"My goal is to speak English with confidence."
Routine -- the things you usually do every day (rotina)
"My morning routine is calm: coffee, plan, and start work."
Speak -- to say words and talk to someone (falar)
"I want to speak more and stop feeling blocked."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

My name is Marta, and I am 45 years old. I am an organized person, so I have a clear routine every day. In the morning, I wake up early and I make a coffee. I like a calm start. At work, I speak with many people. Sometimes I feel nervous when I speak English, but I also feel more confident now. My friend Paula works with me. She is very clear when she explains things. My goal is simple: I want to speak English and feel comfortable, not afraid.

1. Why does Marta say "I am 45 years old" and not "I have 45 years"?
A Because "have" is the correct verb for age in English.
B Because in English we use "to be" for age: I am ... years old.
C Because both verbs are always correct for age.
2. Why does the text say "She works with me" and not "She work with me"?
A Because with he/she/it we add "-s" to the verb in the present simple.
B Because "work" is never correct in English.
C Because the sentence is about the past.
3. How does Marta feel about speaking English now?
A She always feels afraid and never speaks.
B She is already completely fluent.
C She sometimes feels nervous, but she feels more confident now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple (to be / to have / to do)

GRAMMAR

Como falar de você mesma no presente. / How to talk about yourself in the present.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
Affirmative (I)I am organized. I have a routine.Eu sou organizada. Eu tenho uma rotina.
He / She (verb + s)She works. He likes coffee.Ela trabalha. Ele gosta de café.
NegativeI do not like to speak fast.Eu não gosto de falar rápido.
QuestionDo you like English? What is your name?Você gosta de inglês? Qual é o seu nome?
Age / IdadeUse "to be": I am 46 years old. (NUNCA "I have 46 years" / NEVER use "have" for age.)
