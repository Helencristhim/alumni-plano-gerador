Chapter 3

The Code

Present simple, past simple and present perfect continuous to tell your professional story

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work as a gynecologist in São Paulo."

"I have been working in reproductive medicine for eight years."

"I completed my residency in 2015."

"I have built a strong track record with fertility patients."

TenseUseExample
Present simpleA permanent fact true nowI work as a doctor.
Present perfect continuousStarted in the past, still going onI have been working here for eight years.
Past simpleA finished action with a finished timeI completed my residency in 2015.
Present perfect simpleA result or achievement up to nowI have built a track record.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I work in reproductive medicine since 2016."

✓ RIGHT

"I have been working in reproductive medicine since 2016."

✗ WRONG

"I have been a specialist for eight years ago."

✓ RIGHT

"I have been a specialist for eight years."

✗ WRONG

"I am specialize in fertility care."

✓ RIGHT

"I specialize in fertility care."

For an ongoing career, English needs the present perfect, not the present simple: use "for" with a period and "since" with a start point. Never mix "for" with "ago".

Chapter 4

Real Introductions

A first conversation at an executive program reception

Dialogue

At the Reception

M
Hi, I don't think we've met. I'm Mark, from Chicago. And you are?
E
Hi Mark, I'm Eliane. I'm a doctor from São Paulo, in Brazil.
M
Nice to meet you, Eliane. So, what do you do back in Brazil?
E
I work as a gynecologist, and I specialize in reproductive medicine.
M
That sounds fascinating. How long have you been doing that?
E
I have been working in fertility care for about eight years now.
M
Impressive. And are you working on anything new at the moment?
E
Yes, actually. I am opening my own clinic next year, with American technology.
M
That's a big step. I'd love to hear more about it over dinner.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Where is Mark from?
He is from Chicago.
2. What does Mark want to know about Eliane's work?
What she does in Brazil, how long she has been doing it, and whether she is working on anything new.
3. What does Mark suggest at the end?
Continuing the conversation over dinner.
