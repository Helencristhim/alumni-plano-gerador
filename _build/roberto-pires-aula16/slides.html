Chapter 3: The Past Code

Telling It in the Past

"I walked, I saw, I went"

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Yesterday I explored the old town."

"Last week we relaxed on the beach."

"Two days ago I saw an amazing sunset."

"Last year I went to Paris."

Now (present)Past simpleType
explore / walkexplored / walkedregular (+ed)
relax / wantrelaxed / wantedregular (+ed)
go / seewent / sawirregular
have / takehad / tookirregular

For the past, most verbs add -ed (walk → walked). Some verbs are irregular and change shape (go → went, see → saw). Use time words like yesterday, last week, two days ago.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I go to the beach."

✓ RIGHT

"Yesterday I went to the beach."

✗ WRONG

"I explore the town last week."

✓ RIGHT

"I explored the town last week."

Chapter 4: Sharing the Story

Telling a Local

Roberto shares his trip story

Dialogue

Roberto Shares His Trip

L
So, how was your trip? Where did you go?
R
It was amazing! I went to the coast. The journey was long, but the scenery was beautiful.
L
That sounds wonderful! What did you do there?
R
I explored the old town all day. It was exhausting, but I had a great time.
L
What was the best part? The highlight of the trip?
R
One evening I relaxed on the beach and saw a sunset. It was a memorable moment.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Tell Your Own Story

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play

My Trip, Step by Step

Scenario: A curious local asks about your last trip. Tell the story in the past: where you went, what you did, and the highlight.

Key phrases:

Last week I went to... I explored... and relaxed... The highlight was...
Role-Play

More of the Story

Scenario: Tell a longer version of your trip story. Describe the journey, what you explored, and how you felt at the end of the day.

Tools:

four past verbs three new words yesterday / last week
Free Practice

Your Real Trip

Scenario: Tell the full story of a real trip you took. Say when you went, where you went, what you did each day, what the scenery was like, and what the highlight was. End with how the whole trip was.

No key phrases. Tell it like a real traveler!
