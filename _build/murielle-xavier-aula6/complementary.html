Lesson 6 -- Planning Ahead -- Future HR Projects with Going To

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast (Harvard Business Review) -- an episode on strategy and planning

Leaders describe what their companies are going to do next: goals, targets, budgets, and strategy. You will hear "going to" used for decided plans and evidence-based predictions. Direct connection to Lesson 6: talking about future HR projects.

Tip: write down 5 "going to" sentences you hear, then rewrite them as your own plans for next quarter.

Series
The Playbook (Netflix) -- any episode where a coach explains the plan

Coaches lay out their strategy and predict outcomes: what they are going to do and why they think it is going to work. Notice how "going to" signals decisions and predictions. Connection to Lesson 6: presenting goals, strategy, and forecasts.

Tip: pause after a plan and predict the result out loud: "This is going to work because..."

YouTube
"Going to for Future Plans and Predictions" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on "be going to": how to talk about plans already decided and predictions based on evidence, with negatives, questions, and common mistakes. Connection to Lesson 6: the exact grammar you use to plan ahead.

Tip: pause and build your own sentences with expand, deliver, set up, and launch.
