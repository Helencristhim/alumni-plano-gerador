Lesson 06 -- Pre-class

Planning Ahead -- Future HR Projects with Going To

Talking about decided future plans and evidence-based predictions for HR projects with be + going to + base verb. New words: goal, target, forecast, budget, roadmap, quarter, priority, expand, set up, aim to, deliver, strategy. Grammar: going to for plans already decided and predictions from evidence (affirmative, negative, question).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Goal -- a result you plan to achieve (meta / objetivo)
"Our main goal this quarter is faster hiring."
Target -- a specific number you aim for (alvo / meta numérica)
"Our target is ten new hires this quarter."
Forecast -- a statement of what will probably happen (previsão)
"The forecast shows strong hiring next year."
Budget -- the money planned for a project (orçamento)
"The budget for the new team is already approved."
Roadmap -- a plan showing the steps over time (roteiro / plano)
"I am going to send you the roadmap on Friday."
Quarter -- a three-month business period (trimestre)
"We are going to expand the team next quarter."
Priority -- what you do first because it matters most (prioridade)
"Please look at the priorities before our call."
Expand -- to grow or make bigger (expandir)
"We are going to expand the team by ten people."
Set up -- to create or establish something (montar / estabelecer)
"We are going to set up a new onboarding program."
Aim to -- to intend to do something (ter como objetivo)
"We aim to have every new hire ready in two weeks."
Deliver -- to produce a promised result (entregar / cumprir)
"We are going to deliver the growth plan this year."
Strategy -- a plan to reach a big goal (estratégia)
"Our strategy is to promote from inside."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

At Minerva Foods, the HR team has a clear plan for next quarter. Their main goal is faster hiring, and the budget is already approved. "We are going to expand the team by ten people," Murielle explains. "Our target is to have every new hire ready in two weeks." Their strategy is to promote from inside, so they are not going to hire externally for senior roles. Instead, they are going to set up a faster onboarding program. The numbers support the plan: applications are up thirty percent, so the forecast is strong. "Based on this evidence, next year is going to be a great year," Murielle says. She is going to deliver the full roadmap on Friday, and she asks the team to review the priorities first.

1. Why does Murielle say "We are going to expand the team"?
A It is a finished action from last year.
B It is a plan already decided for the future (be + going to + base verb).
C It is a daily routine that never changes.
2. Why does the text say "next year is going to be a great year"?
A It is a prediction based on evidence now (applications are up).
B It is an order given to the team.
C It describes a habit that happens every year.
3. Which sentence is a correct negative plan with going to?
A "We not going to hire externally."
B "We are not going to hire externally."
C "We are not going to hiring externally."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- 'Going To' for Plans and Predictions

GRAMMAR

Use be + going to + verbo base para planos já decididos e para previsões baseadas em evidência. Nunca misture "will" com "going to", e nunca use -ing depois de "going to". (Decided plans and evidence-based predictions.)

FormUse (PT)Example
am / is / are + going to + base verbPlano já decidido / decided planWe are going to expand the team.
going to (prediction)Previsão de evidência / prediction from evidenceApplications are up, so hiring is going to be strong.
negative: be + not + going toPlano negativo / negativeWe are not going to hire externally.
question: Be + subject + going toPerguntar sobre um plano / ask about a planAre you going to launch it in March?
