Chapter 3: If You..., We Will...

The Conditional Offer

The sentence that makes every deal.

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"If you sign this month, we will give you a discount."

"If you pay in advance, we will offer two percent more."

"If your numbers are reasonable, we will close the deal."

"If you order more, we will reduce the price."

PartFormExample
ConditionIf + present simpleIf you sign this month,
Resultwill + base verbwe will give you a discount.
TogetherIf + present, ... will + verbIf you pay now, we will reduce the price.

This is the first conditional: a real, possible future. Use if + present simple for the condition (if you sign, if you pay), and will + base verb for the result (we will give, we will offer). NEVER use "will" after "if" (not "if you will sign").

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"If you will sign today, we give a discount."

✓ RIGHT

"If you sign today, we will give a discount."

✗ WRONG

"If you pay now, we offering ten percent off."

✓ RIGHT

"If you pay now, we will offer ten percent off."

After "if", use the present simple (if you sign, if you pay), never "will". The result half always needs "will" + base verb (we will give, we will offer). One half has "if + present", the other half has "will".

Dialogue

At the Table

B
We like your project, but two hundred thousand is too high. Can you give us a discount?
R
I understand. If you sign this month, we will give you a five percent discount.
B
Five percent is not enough. Our counter-offer is one hundred and seventy thousand.
R
That is below our cost. But if you pay the whole amount in advance, we will accept one hundred and eighty.
B
We prefer to pay in three installments. Are you flexible on that?
R
We are flexible. If you pay a deposit today, we will accept three installments. Then we have a deal.
