Grammar Rule

Three Small Words: In, On, At

WordUse it forExample
INa month, a year, a part of the dayin November · in 2027 · in the morning
ONa day or a dateon Monday · on the seventh · on November 8th
ATa clock timeat ten o'clock · at 9 a.m. · at seven in the evening
DatesAmerican English: Month + ordinalNovember 7th (the seventh) · March 15th (the fifteenth)

The easy way to remember: big to small. IN the biggest (a month), ON the day, AT the exact clock time. And to say the order of events: first, then, next, after that, finally.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The meeting is in the seventh."
"The meeting is on the seventh."
"The ceremony is on nine o'clock."
"The ceremony is at nine o'clock."
"The conference is on November."
"The conference is in November."

Use on for a date (on the seventh), at for a clock time (at nine o'clock), and in for a month (in November).
