Grammar Rule

Ordering politely

FormExample
Order food (polite)I would like the chicken.
Short form in speakingI'd like a soup, please.
Ask for somethingCould I have the check?
Ask for adviceCan you recommend a dish?
Say how you want itMy steak, well-done, please.

Key idea: to order food politely, use I would like (short: I'd like), not "I want". To ask for something, use Could I have. To ask for advice, use Can you recommend. Always finish with please.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want the fish. Give me the check."
"I would like the fish, please. Could I have the check?"

To be polite, use I would like and Could I have, and finish with please.
