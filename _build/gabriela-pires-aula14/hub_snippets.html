Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. / Listen to each word and read the example.

Reservation -- a table you book before you arrive at a restaurant
"We have a reservation for tonight." (Reserva)
Table for two -- a table at a restaurant for two people
"A table for two, please." (Mesa para dois)
Appetizer -- a small dish you eat before the main course
"For an appetizer, I would like soup." (Entrada)
Main course -- the biggest and most important dish of the meal
"For the main course, I would like chicken." (Prato principal)
Spicy -- food that tastes hot in your mouth, like chili or pepper
"This fish is too spicy for me." (Apimentado)
Well-done -- meat that is cooked a lot, not pink inside
"I would like my steak well-done." (Bem passado)
Recommend -- to tell someone that a dish is good and they should try it
"Can you recommend a good dish?" (Recomendar)
The check -- the paper that shows how much you pay at the end of a meal
"Could I have the check, please?" (A conta)
Tip -- extra money you leave for good service at a restaurant
"I left a small tip for the waiter." (Gorjeta)

GRAMMAR

Gabriela goes to a restaurant in Paris with her family. They have a reservation for a table for two. The waiter brings the menu and Gabriela asks, "Can you recommend a good dish?" The waiter recommends the spicy fish, but Gabriela does not like spicy food. She says, "I would like the chicken, please. And a soup for an appetizer." Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Ordering politely

GRAMMAR

Usamos "I would like", "Could I have" e "Can you recommend" para pedir com educação em um restaurante. / We use "I would like", "Could I have" and "Can you recommend" to order politely.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Order foodI would like + foodI would like the chicken.
Short form (speaking)I'd like + foodI'd like a soup, please.
Ask for somethingCould I have + thing?Could I have the check?
Ask for adviceCan you recommend + ...?Can you recommend a dish?
Say how you want itnoun + well-doneMy steak, well-done.

Key point / Ponto-chave: para pedir comida com educação, use I would like (forma curta: I'd like), não "I want". Para pedir algo, use Could I have. Para pedir uma sugestão, use Can you recommend. Termine sempre com please.
