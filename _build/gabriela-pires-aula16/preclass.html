Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. / Listen to each word and read the example.

Tour -- a visit to many interesting places, often with a guide
"The city tour starts at ten o'clock." (Passeio / Tour)
Sightseeing -- the activity of visiting famous and interesting places
"We went sightseeing all morning in Paris." (Passear pelos pontos turísticos)
Landmark -- a famous building or place that is easy to recognize
"The Eiffel Tower is the most famous landmark in Paris." (Ponto de referência)
River cruise -- a trip on a boat along a river to see the city
"The river cruise gives you a great view of Paris." (Passeio de barco no rio)
View -- everything you can see from a place, often beautiful
"From the boat, the view of the city is wonderful." (Vista)
Brochure -- a small paper book with pictures and information about a place
"Take a brochure; it has a map of the tour." (Folheto)
Opening hours -- the times when a place is open for visitors
"The opening hours are from nine to six." (Horário de funcionamento)
Souvenir -- a small thing you buy to remember a place or trip
"I bought a souvenir for my mom at the shop." (Lembrança / Souvenir)
Nearby -- close to here, only a short distance away
"There is an old church nearby." (Por perto / Próximo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas. / Read the text and answer the questions.

Gabriela goes on a guided tour in Paris. At the start, she asks, "Can you tell me where the tour starts?" The guide shows her the first landmark. Then the group takes a river cruise, and the view from the boat is wonderful. Gabriela asks, "Is there a place to take photos?" The guide says yes. After the cruise, they go sightseeing and visit an old church nearby. She asks about the opening hours, and they are from nine to six. At the end, she takes a brochure with a map and buys a small souvenir for her mom.

1. How does Gabriela ask politely where the tour starts?
A "Can you tell me where the tour starts?"
B "Where starts the tour you?"
C "How much is the tour?"
2. What gives a wonderful view of the city?
A The brochure.
B The river cruise from the boat.
C The souvenir shop.
3. What are the opening hours of the church?
A From ten to two.
B From nine to six.
C All night.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Can you tell me...? & Is there...?

GRAMMAR

Usamos "Can you tell me...?" para pedir informação de forma educada e "Is there...?" para perguntar se algo existe. Depois de "Can you tell me", mantemos a ordem normal (sujeito + verbo). / We use "Can you tell me...?" to ask for information politely and "Is there...?" to ask if something exists.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Ask politely (place)Can you tell me where + subject + verb?Can you tell me where the tour starts?
Ask politely (time)Can you tell me what time + subject + verb?Can you tell me what time it opens?
Ask politely (length)Can you tell me how long + subject + verb?Can you tell me how long the tour is?
Ask if it existsIs there + a/an + noun?Is there a shop nearby?
Short answerYes, there is. / No, there isn't.Yes, there is.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de Can you tell me, mantenha a ordem normal (sujeito + verbo): "Can you tell me where the tour starts?" (não "where does the tour start"). Para perguntar se algo existe, use Is there + a/an + substantivo.
