Lesson 19 -- Pre-class

Asking and Answering Like a Professional

Ask polite, indirect questions and answer with active listening at an international meeting or panel, using openers like "Could you tell me..." and "I wonder if...". Key words: inquiry, moderator, panel, follow-up, response, feedback, to confirm, to wonder, polite, to facilitate.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Inquiry -- a formal question or request for information
"I have one inquiry about your timeline."
Moderator -- a person who leads a meeting and gives everyone a turn to speak
"The moderator gave me the turn to speak."
Panel -- a small group of experts who answer questions in front of people
"The panel answered questions from the audience."
Follow-up -- a second question that asks for more detail
"After her answer, I asked a follow-up."
Response -- what you say back when someone asks you a question
"Thank you for your clear response."
Feedback -- useful comments about someone's work or ideas
"The panel gave me good feedback."
To confirm -- to say that something is true or correct
"Let me confirm the time of the meeting."
To wonder -- to want to know something in a soft, polite way
"I wonder if you have any feedback."
Polite -- kind and respectful in the way you speak
"An indirect question is more polite."
To facilitate -- to help a meeting run in an easy, smooth way
"The moderator facilitates the panel."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is a guest at an international panel. The moderator facilitates the meeting and gives her the turn. She has one inquiry, and she asks it in a polite way: "Could you tell me when the meeting starts?" She does not say "when does the meeting start". After the response, she uses active listening. "So, if I understand correctly, we start at ten," she says, to confirm the idea. Then she wants more detail, so she asks a follow-up: "I wonder if the feedback comes after my talk." A good question and good listening make a strong meeting.

2. What does Flavia do after the response?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

How to ask any question in a soft, polite way.

OpenerUse it forExample
Could you tell mewh- questions...where the room is
Do you knowwh- questions...when it starts
I wonder ifyes/no questionsif you have feedback
RuleAfter the opener, use subject + verb order (not "is the room"). Never use do or does. Add if for a yes/no question. To confirm an answer, say "So, if I understand correctly, ...".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real situation when you ask and listen.

A "Could you tell me where the room is?"

You want to ask, politely, when the meeting starts. You say:
A "Do you know when does the meeting start?"

You want to ask a yes/no question in a soft way. You say:
A "I wonder if you have any feedback."
