Expressions + Reporting Verbs

Key Expressions

"reach a consensus" -- to come to a shared agreement or decision as a group
"bring about change" -- to cause or produce a transformation or improvement
"cut to the chase" -- to get directly to the main point without unnecessary detail

Reporting Verbs:

mentioned -- "She mentioned the launch would be delayed."
confirmed -- "He confirmed the budget had been approved."
suggested -- "They suggested we revisit the targets."
warned / insisted / denied / acknowledged

Extra Words (consensus, directive):

Consensus -- a general agreement reached by a group
Directive -- an official instruction or order from a higher authority
Chapter 3

The Backshift Engine

Reported speech: tense shifts, pronoun changes, time markers

Grammar Discovery

Direct vs Reported

What changes when you report what someone said?

PAIR 1

Direct: "We are cutting the budget next quarter."

Reported: She said they were cutting the budget the following quarter.

PAIR 2

Direct: "I have already submitted the report."

Reported: He told me he had already submitted the report.

PAIR 3

Direct: "We might revisit the targets."

Reported: They mentioned they might revisit the targets.

PAIR 4

Direct: "The launch will be delayed."

Reported: She said the launch would be delayed.

Grammar Rule

The Backshift Table

Direct SpeechReported SpeechExample
Present SimplePast Simple"I work" → He said he worked
Present ContinuousPast Continuous"We are cutting" → She said they were cutting
Present PerfectPast Perfect"I have submitted" → He said he had submitted
Past SimplePast Perfect"We decided" → They said they had decided
willwould"It will be delayed" → She said it would be delayed
cancould"We can proceed" → He said they could proceed
might / could / wouldno change"We might revisit" → They said they might revisit

Pronoun & Time Shifts:

I → he/she | we → they | my → his/her | today → that day | tomorrow → the next day | yesterday → the day before | next week → the following week | here → there

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She said she will come to the briefing."

✓ RIGHT

"She said she would come to the briefing."

✗ WRONG

"He told me he has already submitted the report."

✓ RIGHT

"He told me he had already submitted the report."

When reporting what someone SAID (past), shift the tense back. "will" becomes "would." "has" becomes "had."

Chapter 4

The Follow-Up

Debriefing a colleague who missed the call

Dialogue

The Debrief Call

E
Victor, I missed the leadership call this morning. Can you debrief me on what happened?
V
Of course, Elena. Let me cut to the chase. There were three main topics. I will outline them for you.
V
First, the CFO mentioned that they were cutting the LATAM marketing budget by fifteen percent. She confirmed it would take effect next quarter.
E
Fifteen percent? Did anyone push back? Was there any consensus on that?
V
Not really. James told me he had already submitted the headcount report, so that part is done. But he warned that the board would not approve any new hires this quarter.
E
That is frustrating. What about the growth targets? Any flexibility there?
V
Elena suggested they might revisit the targets if Q2 numbers improved. The director acknowledged that the current goals were ambitious but insisted we should proceed with the original plan for now.
E
Got it. Can you circulate the meeting notes so I can clarify a few things with the Buenos Aires team? We need to bring about change in how we communicate these directives.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Why did Elena need a debrief?
She missed the leadership call that morning and needed to know what was discussed.
2. What does Elena ask Victor to do at the end?
She asks him to circulate the meeting notes so she can clarify things with the Buenos Aires team.
3. What change does Elena want to bring about?
She wants to bring about change in how the company communicates directives across regions.
