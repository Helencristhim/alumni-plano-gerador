Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Briefing -- a short meeting to share key information or updates
"The morning briefing covered the new security protocols for all LATAM offices."
Debrief -- a session to review what happened after an event or meeting
"After the leadership call, Victor scheduled a debrief with the LATAM team."
Circulate -- to distribute a document or information to a group
"Please circulate the meeting minutes to all regional directors by end of day."
Confirm -- to verify that something is true, agreed, or final
"The CFO confirmed that the budget cuts would take effect in Q2."
Clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"I need to clarify what the director meant about the timeline changes."
Proceed -- to continue or move forward with a plan or action
"The VP said we should proceed with the original timeline despite the setbacks."
Outline -- to describe the main points or structure of something
"Let me outline the three key decisions from the leadership meeting."
Commit -- to pledge or dedicate yourself to a task or decision
"The regional directors committed to delivering the revised targets by Friday."
Consensus -- a general agreement reached by a group
"After two hours of discussion, the team finally reached a consensus on the new strategy."
Directive -- an official instruction or order from a higher authority
"The new directive from London requires all regions to reduce operational costs by ten percent."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the quarterly strategy meeting, the Growth Manager sat down to write a follow-up email to Elena Torres in Buenos Aires. He needed to debrief her on everything that had been discussed. The CFO had confirmed that the company was cutting the LATAM marketing budget by fifteen percent. She mentioned that this was a global directive from London, not a regional decision. The VP of Operations told the team he had already submitted the headcount report, but he warned that the board would not approve any new hires that quarter. The regional director suggested they might revisit the growth targets if Q2 numbers improved. She acknowledged the current goals were ambitious but insisted the team should proceed with the original plan. The Growth Manager decided to outline the three key decisions and circulate the notes before the end of the day. He knew the team needed to reach a consensus on next steps quickly. Everyone had committed to delivering revised targets by Friday, so there was no time to waste. He wrote: "Let me cut to the chase: the briefing confirmed three things." He hoped this level of clarity would bring about change in how the regions communicated directives.

1. Why did the Growth Manager need to write a follow-up email?
A Because he wanted to cancel the next meeting.
B Because he needed to debrief Elena on what had been discussed at the strategy meeting.
C Because Elena asked him to write a new strategy proposal.
2. In the sentence "The CFO had confirmed that the company was cutting the budget," why does the text use "was cutting" instead of "is cutting"?
A Because the budget cut was already completed.
B Because the writer is reporting what was said in the past, so the tense shifts back (present continuous becomes past continuous).
C Because the CFO was unsure about the decision.
3. What does the expression "cut to the chase" mean in this context?
A To reduce the number of meetings.
B To avoid sharing bad news.
C To get directly to the main point without unnecessary detail.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

How to report what someone said: tense backshifts, pronoun changes, and time shifts.

Direct SpeechReported SpeechExample
Present Simple (am/is/are)Past Simple (was/were)"I work here" → He said he worked there
Present ContinuousPast Continuous"We are cutting" → She said they were cutting
Present PerfectPast Perfect"I have submitted" → He said he had submitted
willwould"It will be delayed" → She said it would be delayed
cancould"We can proceed" → He said they could proceed
might / could / wouldno change"We might revisit" → They said they might revisit
Key rules: SAY + (that) clause. TELL + person + (that) clause. Never "said me" or "told that." Pronouns shift: I → he/she, we → they. Time words shift: today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, yesterday → the day before.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

A colleague asks what the CFO said in the meeting. She originally said: "We are cutting the budget next quarter." How do you report this?
A "The CFO said we are cutting the budget next quarter."
B "The CFO said they were cutting the budget the following quarter."
C "The CFO told that they cut the budget."
Your manager originally said: "I will circulate the notes tomorrow." You are reporting this the next week. What do you say?
A "He said he will circulate the notes tomorrow."
B "He said me he would circulate the notes."
C "He said he would circulate the notes the next day."
You want to quickly summarize the main points of a meeting for a colleague. Which expression is most appropriate?
A "Let me tell you everything from the beginning, step by step."
B "Let me cut to the chase: here are the three key decisions."
C "I do not remember much, but it was a long meeting."
The director said something negative: "We did NOT approve the new hires." Which reporting verb best captures the tone?
A "The director mentioned the new hires."
B "The director warned that the board would not approve any new hires."
C "The director suggested new hires."
