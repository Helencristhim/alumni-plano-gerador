Chapter 3

Acknowledge, Then Counter

Concession and contrast: although, even though, while, whereas, despite

Discovery

Agree and Disagree at Once

Although the team missed the deadline, they were working with a broken forecast. (although + clause)

Even though I had to be candid, I led with empathy. (even though = stronger although)

While I understand the pressure, the slip still hurt the client. (while = concession)

The cost stayed flat, whereas the lead time doubled. (whereas = direct contrast)

Despite the friction, we found common ground. (despite + noun)

The result was fine; the process, however, needs to change. (however = linker between sentences)

Grammar Rule

Concession & Contrast

LinkerWhat followsExample
Although / Even thougha full clause (subject + verb)Although the team missed the deadline...
Whilea clause; concedes a pointWhile I understand the pressure...
Whereasa clause; direct contrast of two factsCost stayed flat, whereas lead time doubled.
Despite / In spite ofa NOUN or -ing (never a clause)Despite the friction, we agreed.
Howeverlinks two sentences; needs a commaIt worked. However, the cost was high.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Despite the team missed the deadline, the forecast was broken." (despite cannot be followed by a full clause)
"Despite the missed deadline, the forecast was broken." / "Although the team missed the deadline, the forecast was broken." (noun after despite; clause after although)

Use despite / in spite of + a noun or -ing form, and although / even though / while + a full clause. They mean almost the same thing, but the grammar after them is different.

Comprehension

Concession Spotted

1. Acknowledge + counter: "_____ the deadline slipped, I know you had a broken forecast."
"Although the deadline slipped, I know you had a broken forecast." (although + clause)
2. Despite + noun: "_____ the friction, we can find common ground."
"Despite the friction, we can find common ground." (despite + noun, never a clause)
3. Direct contrast: "The forecast failed, _____ your team responded well."
"The forecast failed, whereas your team responded well." (whereas contrasts two facts)
