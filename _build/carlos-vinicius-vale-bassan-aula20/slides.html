Chapter 3 -- What Comes After

Look Ahead with the Future Perfect

"By the close, the teams will have integrated"

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence looks ahead to what will be finished by a future point. Notice the verb.

"By the close, the teams will have integrated."

"By the first review, we will have met the targets."

"By next week, we will have completed the handoff."

"By the time you call, we will have signed."

The Rule

The Future Perfect

Time markerStructureExample
By the closewill have + V3"the teams will have integrated"
By the first reviewwill have + V3"we will have met the targets"
By next weekwill have + V3"we will have completed the handoff"
By the time you callwill have + V3"we will have signed"

Tip: the future perfect is will have + past participle (the same for I, you, we, he, she, they). It looks back from a future point at something already finished by then. Use it with by + a future moment ("by the close") or by the time + present simple ("by the time you call"). Compare: "we will sign" (a future action) vs "we will have signed" (already done before that point). Negative: "by Friday we will not have finished".

Common Mistake

Use "Will Have" + Past Participle

"By the close, the teams will integrate. By the review, we will have meet the targets."
"By the close, the teams will have integrated. By the review, we will have met the targets."

For something already finished by a future point, use will have + past participle -- "will have integrated", "will have met" -- not the simple future and not the base form.

Practice

Comprehension

About the Brief

1. What does it mean to execute and countersign the agreement, and why is the effective date important?

To execute is to sign so the deal becomes legally effective; to countersign is to add a second signature that confirms the first. The effective date is the day the agreement starts to apply -- not always the day you sign -- and every deadline counts from it.

2. What does counsel say to do before signing, and what is the handoff?

Tie up the loose ends -- the small details still open -- so they do not become disputes. The handoff is the moment you pass the deal to the integration team, with the documents, the timeline, and the open points; a clean handoff protects everything you negotiated.

3. How does counsel say to plan what comes after, and what is the last step?

Plan it with the future perfect -- "by the close, the teams will have integrated", "by the first review, we will have met the targets" -- looking back from a future point at what is already finished. The last step is to debrief the deal: review, after it is done, what went well and what to do differently.
