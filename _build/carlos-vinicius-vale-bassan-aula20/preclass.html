Block 3 -- Sealing the Deal: The Sign-Off and What Comes After

The wording is final -- now sign and look ahead. New vocabulary: execute, countersign, effective date, disclose, debrief, wind down, loose end, handoff. Grammar focus: the future perfect (will have + past participle) to look back from a future point at what is already finished -- "By the close, the teams will have integrated", "By the first review, we will have met the targets".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Execute -- to sign a contract so it becomes legally binding and effective
"Both sides execute the agreement tomorrow morning." (assinar / formalizar)
Countersign -- to add a second signature that confirms the first one
"The board will countersign the agreement by noon." (referendar / contra-assinar)
Effective date -- the day an agreement starts to apply -- not always the signing day
"The effective date is the first of next month." (data de vigência)
Loose end -- a small detail that is still open after the main deal is done
"Tie up the loose ends before you sign." (ponta solta / pendência)
Handoff -- the moment you pass the deal to the integration team
"A clean handoff protects everything you negotiated." (passagem / transferência)
Disclose -- to share information openly -- the opposite of hiding it
"We must disclose the open points to the integration team." (revelar / divulgar)
Wind down -- to bring a project or activity to a gradual, planned end
"We wind down the deal team once the handoff is complete." (encerrar gradualmente)
Debrief -- to review, after a deal is done, what went well and what to improve
"Let us debrief the whole deal on Friday." (fazer o balanço / revisar ao final)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about sealing the deal and answer the questions.

The wording is final, so now Carlos seals the deal. On signing day, both sides execute the agreement, and the board countersigns it -- a second signature that confirms the first. Carlos confirms the effective date, the day the deal starts to apply. Before signing, he ties up the loose ends and discloses every open point to the other side. Once it is executed, he runs the handoff to the integration team, then they wind down the deal team. Finally, he looks ahead with the future perfect: by the close, the teams will have integrated; by the first review, we will have met the targets. A week later, they debrief the deal -- what went well and what to do differently.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Future Perfect

GRAMMAR

Para olhar de um ponto no futuro para algo que JÁ estará concluído até lá, use o future perfect: will have + particípio passado -- "the teams will have integrated", "we will have met the targets". A forma é igual para todas as pessoas e vem com um marcador de tempo futuro: by + momento ("by the close") ou by the time + presente ("by the time you call"). Compare: "we will sign" (ação futura) vs "we will have signed" (já concluída antes daquele momento). Negativa: "by Friday we will not have finished". / The future perfect (will have + past participle) looks back from a future point at something already finished.
