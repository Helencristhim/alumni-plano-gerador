Giving Recommendations

Prepare-se para aconselhar um colega numa decisão entre duas opções: recommend, suggest, option, drawback, benefit, worthwhile, advise, solution. Estruturas: modais de recomendação could, I'd recommend, should, had better. Missão: virar o conselheiro de confiança da equipe.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Recommend -- to say something is good and worth choosing
"I recommend the cloud option for your team." (Recomendar)
Suggest -- to offer an idea for someone to consider
"Let me suggest a simpler solution." (Sugerir)
Option -- one of the choices you can take
"You have two options on the table." (Opção)
Drawback -- a disadvantage or weak point of something
"The main drawback is the high price." (Desvantagem)
Benefit -- a good result or advantage
"The biggest benefit is faster reporting." (Benefício)
Worthwhile -- good enough to be worth the time, money, or effort
"The training is expensive, but worthwhile." (Que vale a pena)
Advise -- to tell someone what you think they should do
"I'd advise you to test it first." (Aconselhar)
Solution -- a way to solve a problem
"We found a solution that fits the budget." (Solução)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Helen advises James on a decision. "You could go with the cheaper option," she starts. "But I'd recommend the cloud plan, because the benefit is bigger." Then, clearly: "You should test it first." Finally, with a warning: "You had better decide soon, or you'll lose the discount." Different words, different strength, but all of them give a recommendation.

1. Qual recomendação é a MAIS forte (um aviso)? / Which recommendation is the strongest (a warning)?
A "You could go with the cheaper option."
B "You had better decide soon, or you'll lose the discount."
C "I'd recommend the cloud plan."
2. O que vem DEPOIS de "could" e "should"? / What comes after "could" and "should"?
A The base verb: "could go", "should test".
B "to" + verb: "could to go".
C verb + "-ing": "could going".
3. Qual recomendação é a MAIS suave (só uma possibilidade)? / Which is the softest (just one possibility)?
A "You could go with the cheaper option."
B "You had better decide soon."
C "You should test it first."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modais de recomendação

GRAMMAR

A força da recomendação muda com a estrutura. / The strength of the recommendation changes with the structure.

Estrutura / StructureForça / StrengthExample / Exemplo
couldmais suave, uma opção (softer)You could test both first.
I'd recommend / shouldrecomendação clara (standard)You should compare the costs.
had bettermais forte, com consequência (warning)You had better decide, or you'll lose the deal.

could, should e had better usam o verbo na base, SEM "to": could test. Já recommend e suggest levam o verbo em -ing: "I recommend testing it" (NUNCA "recommend you to test").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor recomendação para cada situação.

Um colega não sabe qual ferramenta comprar. / A colleague can't decide which tool to buy.
A "I recommend you to buy the cheaper one."
B "I'd recommend testing the cheaper one first."
C "You could to buy the cheaper one."
Há um prazo apertado e a equipe não decide. / There's a tight deadline and the team won't decide.
A "You had better decide now, or you'll miss the deadline."
B "You had better to decide now."
C "You decide better now."
Você sugere uma ideia gentilmente, sem pressão. / You suggest an idea gently, no pressure.
A "You had better try the new plan right now."
B "You could try the new plan and see how it feels."
C "You could trying the new plan."
Helen resume a ideia principal do conselho. / Helen sums up the main idea of her advice.
A "Pick the solution that fits today, not the one that looks impressive."
B "Pick the most expensive solution always."
C "The benefit is the drawback."

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Sem certo ou errado. Pense em inglês, toque no microfone e fale livremente.

A colleague must choose between two options for a project: one is cheaper with a clear drawback, the other has a bigger benefit but a higher cost. Coach them with at least three recommendations, using a different structure each time, a soft one (could), a standard one (I'd recommend / should), and a strong one (had better). Use the new words: option, benefit, drawback, worthwhile, solution.
