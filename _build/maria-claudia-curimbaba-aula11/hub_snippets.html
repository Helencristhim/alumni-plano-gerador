Conference Calls -- Managing Tech & Time Zones

Run a video call with your US partners like a pro: handle the tech (audio cutting out, frozen screens, mute, lag) and the time zones, and make polite requests with "Could you...?" and "Would you mind + -ing". Plus the passive voice for call problems (the line was dropped, you are being muted). New vocabulary: dial in, on mute, speak up, cut out, choppy, freeze, lag, bandwidth, drop, share your screen, time zone, reschedule.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Dial in -- to connect to a conference call by phone or computer
"Let me dial in to the call with Houston." (Entrar na chamada)
On mute -- with your microphone turned off, so no one can hear you
"Sorry, I was on mute. Let me start again." (No mudo)
Speak up -- to talk more loudly so others can hear you clearly
"Could you speak up? You are very quiet." (Falar mais alto)
Cut out -- when audio or video stops for a short moment, then returns
"You are cutting out -- I missed the last figure." (Cair o áudio)
Choppy -- broken and uneven, with short gaps -- said of audio or video
"The connection is a bit choppy on my end." (Picotado/falho)
Freeze -- when a video image stops moving and gets stuck
"Your screen froze for a second -- you are back now." (Congelar)
Lag -- a delay between speaking and being heard or seen
"There is a lag, so let us pause between sentences." (Atraso/lag)
Bandwidth -- the amount of data your internet can handle at once
"My bandwidth is low, so I will turn my camera off." (Largura de banda)
Drop -- when a call suddenly ends because the connection is lost
"The call dropped, but I have dialed back in." (Cair a chamada)
Share your screen -- to let everyone on the call see what is on your screen
"Would you mind sharing your screen with the slides?" (Compartilhar a tela)
Time zone -- a region of the world that shares the same local clock time
"With three time zones, we meet at my late afternoon." (Fuso horário)
Reschedule -- to change a meeting to a different date or time
"Let us reschedule the last item for tomorrow." (Reagendar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

I dial in five minutes early. David is in Houston, the team is in Pittsburgh, and I am in São Paulo -- three time zones on one call. David starts, but the audio is choppy. "Could you move closer to your router?" I ask. Then it is my turn. "Would you mind sharing your screen?" he says. Suddenly my video freezes: the line was dropped for a moment. I dial in again. "Sorry, I was on mute," I say. The Pittsburgh team is being muted by the host, so they type their questions. With only thirty minutes, we agree to reschedule the rest.

1. How does Maria politely ask David to fix his choppy audio?
A With "Could you move closer to your router?" -- a polite request.
B With "You must move now."
C She says nothing about it.
2. Which form follows "Would you mind"?
A The infinitive: "Would you mind to share..."
B The -ing form: "Would you mind sharing..."
C The past tense: "Would you mind shared..."
3. How does Maria describe the connection problem, using the passive?
A "The line was dropped for a moment." -- be + past participle.
B "The line is dropping me now."
C "I dropped the line on purpose."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests & the Passive

GRAMMAR

Em chamadas, peça as coisas com educação: "Could you" + verbo na forma base, ou "Would you mind" + verbo com -ING (gerundio). Use a voz passiva (be + particpio passado) para problemas tecnicos quando quem causou nao importa. / On calls, make polite requests: "Could you" + base verb, or "Would you mind" + the -ing form. Use the passive (be + past participle) for tech problems when the doer does not matter.

PatternFormExample
Could you...?Could you + base verb"Could you speak up?"
Would you mind...?Would you mind + verb-ing"Would you mind repeating that?"
Passive voicebe + past participle"The line was dropped."

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de "Could you" use o verbo na forma base (speak, share); depois de "Would you mind" use o gerundio com -ING (speaking, sharing), NUNCA "to + verbo". Para problemas de chamada, a passiva (was dropped, is being muted, has been rescheduled) e natural e elegante. E diga "on mute", nunca "in mute". / After "Could you" use the base verb; after "Would you mind" use the -ing form, never "to + verb". The passive is perfect for call problems.

Stage 2: Handle a Glitch in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of handling a tech problem on a call in the correct order.

?You suggest a fix: ask them to come off mute, to share their screen, or to turn the camera off.
?You notice the problem: the audio is choppy, or the other person has frozen.
?If nothing works, you agree to reschedule the rest for a time that suits all three time zones.
?You flag it politely: "Could you say that again?" or "Would you mind repeating the last point?"
?If the call drops, you stay calm and dial back in without panicking.
