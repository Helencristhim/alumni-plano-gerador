Chapter 3 -- Polite Requests

Language Discovery

Could you...? / Would you mind...? -- and the passive voice

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes right after "Could you...?" and right after "Would you mind...?"

"Could you speak up a little? You are very quiet."

"Could you share your screen, please?"

"Would you mind repeating the last point?"

"Would you mind turning your camera off to save bandwidth?"

The Rule

Polite Requests

PatternFormExample
Could you...?Could you + base verb"Could you speak up?"
Would you mind...?Would you mind + verb-ing (gerund)"Would you mind repeating that?"
Saying yesOf course / Sure / Not at all"Not at all -- sharing now."

Key idea: after "Could you" use the base verb (speak, share, repeat). After "Would you mind" use the -ing form (speaking, sharing, repeating) -- never "to + verb". "Would you mind" is the softer, more diplomatic of the two.

Language Focus

The Passive on a Call

When the doer is unknown or unimportant, use be + past participle. Perfect for tech problems.

"The line was dropped halfway through the call."

"You are being muted by the host."

"The meeting has been rescheduled for tomorrow."

Pattern: subject + be (was / is being / has been) + past participle (dropped, muted, rescheduled). Add "by + doer" only if it matters ("muted by the host").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Would you mind to speak up?"
"Would you mind speaking up?"

After "would you mind", always use the -ing form (speaking, sharing, repeating), never "to + verb". And it is "on mute", never "in mute".

Practice

Make the Request

"Could you _____share your screen?" (Could you + base verb)
"Would you mind _____speaking up?" (Would you mind + -ing)
"The call _____was dropped, so let me dial back in." (passive: be + participle)
"Would you mind _____repeating the last figure?" (Would you mind + -ing)
