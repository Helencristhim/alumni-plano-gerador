Chapter 3

The Code

The second conditional: if + past simple, would + base verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If we took the whole contract, it would create a bottleneck."

"If a rival undercut us, we would compete on quality."

"If they made one concession, I would sign today."

"If I were in their position, I would wait."

PartFormExample
The condition (unreal)If + past simpleIf we had more capacity,
The imagined resultwould / could + base verbwe would take the contract.
The verb "be"were for all subjectsIf I were you, I would wait.

Second conditional = UNREAL / hypothetical present or future. Never put "would" after "if".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If we would have more capacity, we would take the contract."

✓ RIGHT

"If we had more capacity, we would take the contract."

✗ WRONG

"If a competitor cut prices, we will respond."

✓ RIGHT

"If a competitor cut prices, we would respond."

✗ WRONG

"If I was in their position, I would wait."

✓ RIGHT

"If I were in their position, I would wait."

Never use "would" in the if-clause — the condition takes the past simple. The result takes "would + base verb", not "will". For hypotheticals, prefer "were" for every subject.

Chapter 4

In the Room

A what-if drill in the strategy session

Dialogue

The What-If Drill

A
Izabel, let us think in hypotheticals today. Suppose that global buyer offered you the big contract next month. What would you do?
I
Honestly, I would be cautious. If we took the whole contract, it would stretch our capacity and create a real bottleneck in production.
A
So you would turn it down?
I
Not exactly. Taking the whole thing would not be feasible, but if they made one concession on delivery, I would sign for half the volume. Our lead time would be my strongest bargaining chip.
A
Fair. And if a bigger rival tried to undercut you on price?
I
We would not follow them down. If we cut our price to match, our overhead would eat the margin. I would hedge: compete on quality and offer a small incentive on delivery instead.
A
And the worst-case scenario — what if that group offered to acquire AVRT?
I
If I sold now, I would lock in a good price, but I would lose my leverage over the roadmap. Every option is a trade-off. In the worst-case scenario, if the terms were not right, I would walk away.
