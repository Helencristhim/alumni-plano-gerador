Chapter 3

The Code

Present perfect vs present simple to talk about experience

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in HR for twelve years."

"She has been with Minerva since 2019."

"I have taken on several global projects."

"How long have you worked here?"

StructureUseExample
have / has + past participleExperience up to nowI have led projects.
... for + periodHow long it has lastedfor twelve years
... since + start pointWhen it startedsince 2019
Present simpleFacts true nowI report to the VP.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I work here since 2019."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked here since 2019."

✗ WRONG

"I have worked here for ten years ago."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked here for ten years."

✗ WRONG

"How long you have worked here?"

✓ RIGHT

"How long have you worked here?"

Use the present perfect with "for" and "since". Never mix "for" with "ago". In questions, put "have" before the subject.

Meeting Someone

Dialogue

On the Call

J
Nice to meet you. I'm James Carter, the People Director here in Australia.
M
It's a pleasure, James. I'm Murielle, an HR business partner at Minerva Foods.
J
Great. How long have you worked in HR?
M
I have worked in HR for over twelve years, and I've been with Minerva since 2019.
J
Impressive. What is your main area of expertise?
M
My expertise is people development. I've also taken on several international projects.
J
Have you ever worked abroad?
M
Not yet, but that's my goal. I report to the VP of People here in Brazil.
J
Wonderful. Let's keep in touch — our teams will work closely this year.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is James's role, and where is he based?
He is the People Director, based in Australia.
2. What does James want to know about Murielle's experience?
How long she has worked in HR, and whether she has ever worked abroad.
3. What does James say about the two teams?
They will work closely together this year.
