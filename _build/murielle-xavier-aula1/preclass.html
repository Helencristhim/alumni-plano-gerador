Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Murielle works at Minerva Foods. She is an executive HR manager, and she is in charge of a small team. "I am the business partner for the corporate areas," she says. Her company has headquarters in Brazil and operations in Australia. Murielle leads five people, and her team supports Finance, Legal, and Sustainability. She has a meeting with Australian executives every week. "My biggest challenge is the accent," she says. Her goal is to be fluent and to work in Australia. When she meets a new partner, she asks: "What do you do? Where are you based?"

1. Why do we say "Murielle works" with an -s at the end?
A Because the action happened in the past.
B Because in the present simple, she/he/it takes -s on the verb.
C Because "Murielle" is plural.
2. "Her company has headquarters in Brazil" uses the present simple to express:
A A permanent fact (where the company's head office is).
B An action that will happen in the future.
C Something that happened only once yesterday.
3. Which question does Murielle ask correctly?
A "What you do?"
B "What do you do? Where are you based?"
C "Where you based are?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & WH-Questions

GRAMMAR

When to use the present simple to introduce yourself.

FormUseExample
I / You / We + verbFacts and routines (no -s)I work in HR.
She / He / It + verb+sThird person always takes -sShe leads the team.
am / is / areIdentity, role, place. Use a/an before a jobI am an HR manager.
WH + do + you + verbAsk about someone (action verb)What do you do?
WH + are + youWith to be, the verb comes before the subject: Where are you based? / What field are you in?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each professional situation.

A partner asks you to describe your work. What do you say?
A "I do HR things."
B "I am an HR business partner. I am in charge of a team for the corporate areas."
C "I am manager, I make HR."
Someone asks: "Where are you based?" You respond:
A "I am based São Paulo."
B "Our headquarters is based in São Paulo, Brazil."
C "I based in São Paulo am."
You want to ask a new partner about their job. The correct question is:
A "What you do for work?"
B "Which team you are in?"
C "What do you do? Which team are you in?"
You want to end the conversation and keep the contact. You say:
A "It was great meeting you. Let's keep in touch after the call."
B "Okay, bye, finish now."
C "We keep in touch since the call."
