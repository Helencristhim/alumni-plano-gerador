Stage 10.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Committee -- comitê, comissão (a group chosen to make decisions)
"The committee meets once a month to make decisions."
Suggest -- sugerir (to offer an idea for others to consider)
"I would like to suggest a new date for the congress."
Propose -- propor (to put forward a plan formally)
"I propose that we open registration earlier."
Schedule -- agendar, cronograma (to plan a time for something)
"Let's schedule the next meeting for Friday."
Vote -- votar, voto (to make a group decision by choosing)
"Shall we put it to a vote?"
Chair -- presidente da reunião (the person who leads a meeting)
"Thank you, Chair, for leading the meeting."
Minutes -- ata (the written record of a meeting)
"Who is taking the minutes today?"
Approve -- aprovar (to officially accept a plan or decision)
"The committee approved the new budget."
Postpone -- adiar (to move something to a later time)
"Can we postpone the vote until next week?"
Attend -- comparecer, participar (to be present at a meeting or event)
"Will you attend the next committee meeting?"

Stage 10.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcos is in a UPAV committee meeting. The chair opens: "Welcome. Let's start with the agenda." Marcos wants to make an idea heard, so he uses polite suggestions. "I would like to suggest a new date for the congress," he says. "How about holding it in October? Why don't we open registration earlier?" Another member replies: "Good idea. We could also reduce the fee." The chair agrees: "Shall we put it to a vote?" The committee approves the plan, and someone writes it in the minutes.

1. Quando usamos "How about + verbo-ing" ou "Why don't we...?"
A Para dar uma ordem direta.
B Para fazer uma sugestão educada numa reunião.
C Para falar do passado.
2. O que o "chair" faz no fim para decidir?
A Adia a reunião.
B Propõe colocar a ideia em votação ("Shall we put it to a vote?").
C Sai da reunião.
3. Which suggestion is grammatically correct?
A "How about to hold it in October?"
B "How about holding it in October?"
C "How about we holding it in October?"

Stage 10.4: Grammar Tip -- Making Suggestions

GRAMMAR

Como sugerir ideias de forma educada numa reunião (explicação em inglês e português).

Phrase / FraseFollowed by / Seguido deExample
Let's...+ base verbLet's start with the agenda.
Why don't we...?+ base verbWhy don't we open registration earlier?
How about...?+ verb-ingHow about holding it in October?
Shall we...? / We could...+ base verbShall we vote? / We could reduce the fee.
DicaAtenção: How about + verbo-ing (holding), mas Why don't we / Let's / Shall we / We could + verbo base (hold).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento da reunião.

You open the meeting. You say:
A "Start now the meeting."
B "Let's start with the agenda."
C "I want talk."
You want to suggest a new date politely. You say:
A "How about holding it in October?"
B "How about to hold it in October?"
C "You hold it in October."
You want the group to decide formally. You say:
A "Decide it now."
B "Shall we put it to a vote?"
C "We vote it maybe."
You cannot decide today and need more time. You say:
A "Can we postpone the vote until next week?"
B "We postpone yesterday."
C "No vote forever."
