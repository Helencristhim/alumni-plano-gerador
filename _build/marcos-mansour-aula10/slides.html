Chapter 3

Making Suggestions

Let's / Why don't we / How about / Shall we / We could

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Let's start with the agenda."

"Why don't we open registration earlier?"

"How about holding it in October?"

"Shall we put it to a vote?"

Phrase+ FormExample
Let's+ base verbLet's start...
Why don't we+ base verbWhy don't we open...?
How about+ verb-ingHow about holding...?
Shall we / We could+ base verbShall we vote? / We could reduce...
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"How about to hold it in October?"

✓ RIGHT

"How about holding it in October?"

✗ WRONG

"Let's to start with the agenda."

✓ RIGHT

"Let's start with the agenda."

✗ WRONG

"You vote now." (too direct)

✓ RIGHT

"Shall we put it to a vote?"

How about + -ing; Let's / Why don't we / Shall we + base verb. Suggest, don't order.

Chapter 4

A Meeting in Action

The UPAV committee at the table

Dialogue

Shall We Vote?

E
Welcome, everyone. Let's start with the agenda. The main item is the date of the next congress.
M
Thank you, Chair. I would like to suggest a new date. How about holding it in October?
E
Interesting. Why October instead of November?
M
October works better for our international members, and the venue is cheaper. We could also open registration earlier.
E
Good points. Does anyone want to comment before we decide?
M
If the committee agrees, I can prepare a short plan for the next meeting. Shall we put it to a vote?
E
Yes, let's vote. All in favor of October? The committee approves. I will add it to the minutes.
M
Thank you all. I will send the plan before our next meeting.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What did the member suggest about the congress?
Holding it in October instead of November.
2. Why was October a better choice?
It works better for international members and the venue is cheaper.
3. How did the committee decide, and where was it recorded?
They put it to a vote and approved it; the chair added it to the minutes.
