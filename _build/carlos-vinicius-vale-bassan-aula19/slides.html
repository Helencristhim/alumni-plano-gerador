Chapter 3 -- Propose the Wording

Propose It Formally

The subjunctive: "I propose that the clause be amended"

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence proposes a change to the wording. Notice the verb after "that".

"I propose that the clause be amended."

"I recommend that the deadline be moved by two weeks."

"We ask that the wording remain neutral."

"I suggest that the clause include a carve-out."

The Rule

The Formal Subjunctive

Trigger + thatVerb formExample
I propose that...base (passive be + V3)"the clause be amended"
I recommend that...base (passive be + V3)"the deadline be moved"
We ask that...base form"the wording remain neutral"
I suggest that...base form"the clause include a carve-out"

Tip: after propose / recommend / suggest / ask / insist + that, the verb stays in its base form -- it never changes for he, she, or it. Say "the clause be amended" (not "is amended") and "the wording remain" (not "remains"). For a negative, put not before the verb: "I propose that the date not be changed".

Common Mistake

Keep the Verb in the Base Form

"I propose that the clause is amended. We ask that the wording remains neutral."
"I propose that the clause be amended. We ask that the wording remain neutral."

After propose / recommend / ask / suggest + that, the verb stays in its base form -- "be amended", "remain" -- and never takes an -s.

Practice

Choose the Verb Form

"I propose that the clause _____ amended." → _____be (base: be amended, not is amended)
"I recommend that the deadline _____ moved by two weeks." → _____be (passive: be moved)
"We ask that the wording _____ neutral." → _____remain (base: remain, not remains)
"I suggest that the clause _____ a carve-out." → _____include (base: include, not includes)

Comprehension

About the Brief

1. What does it mean to negotiate in good faith, and why does it matter?

Good faith means you are honest and you really intend to reach an agreement, not just win. Your counterpart reads it the same way, so it keeps the conversation constructive.

2. How does counsel say to propose a change, and what is a carve-out?

Propose it formally: "I propose that the clause be amended", "I recommend that the deadline be moved" -- the verb stays in its base form. A carve-out is a small, narrow exception written into a clause, so ask for one instead of rejecting the whole clause.

3. What are the five steps to reach the final wording?

Push back in good faith, name the sticking point, redline the wording, propose the amendment formally, and use leeway and a carve-out to close the gap.
