Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Redline -- to mark the exact changes you want directly in the contract
"I will redline the wording I want to amend." (marcar alterações no contrato)
Amend -- to make a formal, agreed change to the wording of a clause
"I propose that the clause be amended." (alterar / emendar)
Wording -- the precise language the clause uses -- the exact words
"A small change in wording protects the company." (redação / texto exato)
Leeway -- the room to move -- the flexibility you have on a term
"There is more leeway on the closing date than they admit." (margem / flexibilidade)
Sticking point -- the one issue that is blocking agreement
"The waiver clause is the last sticking point." (ponto de impasse)
Carve-out -- a small, narrow exception written into a clause
"Ask for a carve-out instead of rejecting the whole clause." (exceção / ressalva)
Good faith -- honest intention -- you really mean to reach an agreement
"Push back in good faith, and keep it constructive." (boa-fé)
Counterpart -- the person you negotiate with -- on the other side of the deal
"My counterpart is negotiating in good faith." (contraparte / interlocutor)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about negotiating the final terms and answer the questions.

Carlos read the agreement, and now he negotiates the final terms. Counsel briefs him: push back in good faith -- be honest and really intend to agree -- because his counterpart reads good faith the same way. Name the sticking point, the one issue that is blocking the deal, then redline the exact wording you want changed. Propose the change formally: I propose that the clause be amended, I recommend that the deadline be moved, we ask that the wording remain neutral -- notice the verb stays in its base form. To amend is to make a formal change. When the wording is too strict, do not reject the whole clause; use your leeway and ask for a carve-out, one narrow exception. That is how you reach the final wording.

1. What does it mean to negotiate in good faith?
A You are honest and really intend to reach an agreement, not just win.
B You hide your real position until the very end.
C You sign whatever the other side asks for.
2. How does counsel say to propose a change to the wording?
A "I propose that the clause is amended."
B "I propose that the clause be amended" -- the verb stays in its base form.
C "I propose the clause amends itself."
3. When the wording is too strict, what should you do instead of rejecting the whole clause?
A Walk away from the deal completely.
B Sign it and hope it never applies.
C Use your leeway and ask for a carve-out -- one narrow exception.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Formal Subjunctive

GRAMMAR

Para propor uma mudança num contrato de forma FORMAL, use o subjuntivo: depois de propose / recommend / suggest / ask / insist + that, o verbo fica na FORMA BASE -- "the clause be amended", "the wording remain". Ele NÃO muda com he/she/it (nunca "is amended" nem "remains") e, no passivo, usa-se be + particípio ("be moved", "be included"). Para a negativa, ponha not antes do verbo: "I propose that the date not be changed". / After propose / recommend / ask / suggest + that, the verb stays in its base form -- "be amended", "remain" -- and never takes an -s.

Trigger + thatVerb formExample
I propose that...base (be + V3)"the clause be amended"
I recommend that...base (be + V3)"the deadline be moved"
We ask that...base form"the wording remain neutral"
I suggest that...base form"the clause include a carve-out"
