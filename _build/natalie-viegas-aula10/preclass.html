Handling Difficult Conversations -- Giving and Receiving Feedback

Give and receive feedback in a calm, professional way, and report what people said with reported speech (say / tell / ask). New vocabulary: feedback, constructive, acknowledge, defensive, tactful, address, reassure, defuse, underperform, accountability.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Feedback -- comments about someone's work, meant to help them improve
"I asked my manager for honest feedback." (Retorno)
Constructive -- helpful and meant to improve, not just to criticize
"She gave constructive feedback, not just complaints." (Construtivo)
Acknowledge -- to accept or admit that something is true
"I acknowledge that I missed one deadline." (Reconhecer)
Defensive -- reacting as if you are attacked, instead of listening
"Try not to be defensive when you receive feedback." (Defensivo)
Tactful -- careful not to upset or offend other people
"Be tactful when you point out a mistake." (Diplomático)
Address -- to deal with a problem or issue directly
"We need to address this issue calmly." (Tratar)
Reassure -- to remove someone's worry and make them feel calm
"I reassured her that the project was on track." (Tranquilizar)
Defuse -- to make a tense or difficult situation calmer
"A calm tone helps defuse a difficult conversation." (Acalmar)
Underperform -- to work below the level that was expected
"The team underperformed last quarter." (Render abaixo do esperado)
Accountability -- taking responsibility for your results and actions
"Good feedback builds accountability and trust." (Responsabilização)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Usamos reported speech para dizer o que outra pessoa falou. O verbo costuma voltar um passo no tempo. / We use reported speech to say what another person said. The verb usually moves one step back in time.

StructureUseExample
say (that) ...report a statement (no person after say)"He said that the report was clear."
tell + somebody (that) ...report a statement to a person"She told me that I needed more data."
tell + sb + to + verbreport an instruction"My manager told me to add detail."
ask + sb + to + verbreport a request"She asked me to send it again."

Key point / Ponto-chave: o verbo volta um passo (is → was, need → needed, will → would, can → could). Use tell + pessoa ("told me"), mas say sem pessoa ("said that"). Nunca diga "said me" nem "told to me".

Stage 2: Put the Feedback in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of giving good feedback in the correct order.

?You stay calm and tactful, so the other person does not feel attacked.
?You start with something positive and specific that worked well.
?You agree on a clear next step and reassure the person about the goal.
?You address one issue at a time, not everything at once.
?You listen and acknowledge any valid points the person makes.
