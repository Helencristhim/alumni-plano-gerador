Chapter 3 -- The Reported Code

Grammar Discovery

Reported speech -- saying what other people said

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words do? What happens to the verb tense?

"He said that my report was clear."

"She told me that I needed more data."

"My manager told me to add more detail."

"She asked me to send the file again."

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

StructureUseExample
say (that) ...report a statement (no person after say)"He said that the report was clear."
tell + somebody (that) ...report a statement to a person"She told me that I needed more data."
tell + sb + to + verbreport an instruction"My manager told me to add detail."
ask + sb + to + verbreport a request"She asked me to send it again."

Key idea: the verb usually moves one step back in time: is → was, need → needed, will → would, can → could. Use tell + a person ("told me"), but say without a person ("said that"). For instructions and requests, use tell / ask + person + to + verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He said me that the report was good."
"He told me that the report was good."

Say does not take a person ("said that ..."); tell always does ("told me ..."). And after tell / ask + person, use to + verb ("told me to wait"), never "told to me".
