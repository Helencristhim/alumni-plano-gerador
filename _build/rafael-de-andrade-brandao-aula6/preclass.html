Host -- the person who organizes the event
"The host welcomed everyone with a smile." (Anfitrião)
Guest -- a person invited to the event
"Every guest received a warm welcome." (Convidado)
Badge -- a name tag you wear at an event
"Please write your name on your badge." (Crachá)
Mingle -- to move around and talk to many people
"I like to mingle and meet new people." (Circular / socializar)
Business card -- a small card with your name and contact details
"Here is my business card." (Cartão de visita)
Follow up -- to contact someone again after you meet
"I will follow up with an email tomorrow." (Dar um retorno)
Connection -- a useful person you meet for work
"This event helped me make new connections." (Conexão)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It is a busy evening, and Rafael arrives at a networking event. He puts on his badge and greets the host at the door. Then he starts to mingle with the other guests. He meets a new contact and asks: "What do you do? Where are you from?" The questions are simple, but they open the conversation. They exchange business cards, and Rafael says he will follow up by email. At the end of the night, he has three new connections. Good questions and a friendly hello make every event a success.

1. What does Rafael do first at the event?
A He puts on his badge and greets the host.
B He leaves the event early.
C He sits alone in the corner.
2. Which question is correct?
A "What do you do?"
B "What you do?"
C "You do what?"
3. How does Rafael keep his new contacts?
A He exchanges business cards and follows up by email.
B He forgets their names.
C He never speaks to them again.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Wh- Questions to Connect

GRAMMAR

Usado para iniciar conversas em um evento. / Used to start conversations at an event.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
What + do + youPerguntar a profissão / Ask the jobWhat do you do?
Where + are + youPerguntar a origem / Ask the originWhere are you from?
Which + noun + do + youEscolher entre opções / Choose between optionsWhich company do you work for?
How + was + itPerguntar uma opinião / Ask an opinionHow was the event?
Key point / Ponto-chaveA pergunta Wh- precisa do verbo auxiliar (do, are, was) depois da palavra interrogativa. Nunca pule o auxiliar: "What do you do", não "What you do".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each networking situation.

You meet a new guest at the event. The best way to start is:
A "Hello, nice to meet you. What do you do?"
B "What you do here?"
C "Give me your card now."
You want to know where a contact works. You ask:
A "Which company do you work for?"
B "Which company you work?"
C "You company what?"
The conversation ends well. The best closing is:
A "It was great to meet you. I will follow up by email."
B "Okay, bye."
C "I have to go now."
