Grammar Rule

Wh- Questions to Connect

Question wordHelper + subjectExample
Whatdo youWhat do you do?
Whereare youWhere are you from?
Whichdo youWhich company do you work for?
Howwas itHow was the event?

Key idea: A Wh- question starts with a question word (What, Where, Which, How) and then a small helper verb (do, are, was) before the subject. The helper is never skipped: What do you do, not What you do.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you do?"
"What do you do?"

A Wh- question always needs a helper verb (do, are, was) after the question word. Never skip it.
