Aula 7 — Presenting Data and Insights

Series
Industry — Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

Young analysts at a London investment bank live and die by their numbers. Watch how they present trends, defend a forecast, and highlight a single figure to win a room. Exactly the data language from Aula 7.

Tip: watch with English subtitles and note every phrase used to describe a number going up or down (rose sharply, a steep decline, a slight dip).

Podcast
The Indicator from Planet Money — any episode on quarterly earnings

Short, ten-minute episodes that explain one economic number and the trend behind it. The hosts constantly describe surges, declines, peaks, and forecasts in clear business English.

Tip: pick one episode and note three sentences where the hosts describe a trend. Rewrite each one using "there was a + adjective + noun".

YouTube
BBC Learning English — "The language of trends and graphs"

A focused lesson on describing data: verbs and adverbs of change (rise sharply, fall slightly, level off) and the noun structures (a sharp rise, a slight fall). It also covers the prepositions by, to, and from.

Tip: after watching, describe your last deal's quarter in five sentences, then record a 90-second audio using the new structures.
