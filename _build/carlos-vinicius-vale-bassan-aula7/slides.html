Chapter 3 -- Describing Trends

Up, Down, and How Fast

Two ways to describe the same change

Listen and Notice

What Changes?

Each pair means the same thing. Look at the orange words.

"Revenue rose sharply in the first quarter."

"There was a sharp rise in revenue in the first quarter."

"Costs fell slightly last month."

"There was a slight fall in costs last month."

The Rule

Two Ways to Describe Change

Verb + adverbThere was a + adjective + noun
Revenue rose sharply.There was a sharp rise in revenue.
Costs fell slightly.There was a slight fall in costs.
Demand grew steadily.There was steady growth in demand.

Prepositions: use by for the size of the change ("rose by 8 million"), to for the final value ("rose to 40 million"), and from ... to for both ("rose from 32 to 40 million").

Common Mistake

By vs To

"Revenue rose to 8 million. ... Sales increased sharp."
"Revenue rose by 8 million. ... Sales increased sharply."

Use by for the size of the change and to for the final value. With a verb, use an adverb (sharply), not an adjective.
