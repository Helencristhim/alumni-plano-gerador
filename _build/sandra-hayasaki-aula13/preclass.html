Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Device -- a piece of electronic equipment, like a phone or laptop
"I have been relying on my device for everything." (aparelho / dispositivo)
Screen time -- the amount of time you spend looking at a screen
"My screen time was almost six hours a day." (tempo de tela)
Notification -- a short alert from an app telling you something
"The first thing I do is check my notifications." (notificação / aviso)
Update -- a newer version of software that keeps a device safe
"My phone has been asking me to install an update." (atualização)
Rely on -- to depend on something or someone for help
"I have been relying on my phone for everything." (depender de / contar com)
Keep up -- to stay in contact with people, or follow what is new
"She has been keeping up with her friends through one app." (acompanhar / manter contato)
Distracted -- not able to focus, because something pulls your attention
"I have been getting distracted very easily." (distraído / sem foco)
Struggle -- to find something difficult; to have a hard time with it
"I am still struggling with my screen time." (ter dificuldade / batalhar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect Continuous (Have Been + -ing)

GRAMMAR

Para falar de uma ação que começou no passado e ainda continua agora, usamos have / has + been + verbo na forma -ing. Use have been para I / you / we / they e has been para he / she / it. Negativa: have not been / has not been. Pergunta: How long have you been...? Palavras de tempo comuns: for, since, lately, recently. / To talk about an action that started in the past and is still going on now, use have / has + been + the -ing form. Use have been for I / you / we / they and has been for he / she / it.

FormUseExample
have been + verb-ingI / you / we / they"I have been spending too much time online."
has been + verb-inghe / she / it"My phone has been asking for an update."
have not beennegative"I have not been sleeping well."
How long have you been...?question"How long have you been using it?"
