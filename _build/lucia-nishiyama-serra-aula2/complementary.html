Lesson 2 -- Present Perfect as an Executive Tool

YouTube · BBC Learning English
6 Minute Grammar: The Present Perfect with 'just', 'already' and 'yet'

Six minutes, two presenters, and exactly the three little words you focused on tonight -- just, already, yet -- explained again with fresh examples. They say the sentences at natural speed, so your ear learns where "have" becomes small and joins the word before it. A short, gentle way to review the grammar of the whole lesson.

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, pause after each example and build your own sentence out loud about your career: "I have already led a global team", "I have not finished my mission yet".

Interview Skills
Tell Me About Yourself -- A Good Answer to This Interview Question (Linda Raynier)

The first question in almost every American interview, answered by a career coach. She shows how to tell your career as a story with a clear point -- exactly what the present perfect helps you do. Listen for how she connects past experience to what matters now.

Tip: after watching, write a three-sentence answer to "Tell me about yourself" using the present perfect: "I have spent...", "I have led...", "I have just taken on...". Then say it out loud until it feels natural.

Podcast
How I Built This -- KAYAK: Paul English (with Guy Raz)

A technology leader tells the story of a whole career in his own words -- the wins, the doubts, the turning points. This is the register you are building: an executive telling a long story in natural, spoken American English, full of "I have built", "I have learned", "I have never". Your listening is strong, so use it.

Tip: this is long -- listen to the first twenty minutes only. Write down three present perfect sentences he uses to describe his experience, then say them out loud as if they were your own.
