Chapter 4: The Present Perfect

have · has + past participle

Experience, recent news, and duration

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked at six technology companies."

"I have just moved into AI safety."

"I have led teams for over forty years."

"Have you ever managed a global team?"

What do all four sentences share? Where is the exact date?

UseFormExample
Experience
(life so far, no date)		have / has + past participleI have led global teams.
Recent news
(just / already / yet)		have just / already + participleI have just moved into AI safety.
Duration
(for / since)		have + participle + for / sinceI have worked here for ten years / since 2010.

Time markers: ever, never, just, already, yet · duration: for (a period) · since (a point in time).

Grammar Practice

Say It in Present Perfect

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I ___have worked in technology for over forty years." (work)
"She ___has just moved into a new role." (just / move)
"I have led global teams ___since the early 2000s." (a point in time)
"I have worked in this field ___for four decades." (a period of time)
"I ___have never given up on a hard problem." (never / give)
Detail

For vs Since

FOR + a period

for ten years

for four decades

for a long time

SINCE + a point

since 2010

since I started

since the early 2000s

Ask yourself: is it a length of time (for) or a starting point (since)?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work here since 2010."
"I have worked here since 2010."
"I am in technology for forty years."
"I have been in technology for forty years."
"I have led that team in 2015."
"I led that team in 2015."

Use the present perfect for experience and duration up to now. A finished past time (in 2015, last year) takes the past simple.
