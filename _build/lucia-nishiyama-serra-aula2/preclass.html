Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

Present Perfect as an Executive Tool -- The Story of a 41-Year Career

Telling the story of a long career with the present perfect. Key words: tenure, milestone, to spearhead, pioneer, to transition, to accomplish, to navigate, legacy, transformation, to take on, to move on, track record. Structure: present perfect (have / has + past participle) for experience, recent news (just, already, yet) and duration (for, since).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Tenure -- the period of time you hold a job or an important position
"During my tenure as CIO, I rebuilt the whole security team."
Milestone -- an important point or achievement in a journey or a career
"Leading my first global launch was a real milestone."
To spearhead -- to lead an important new project or effort
"I have spearheaded three major transformations."
Pioneer -- a person who is among the first to do or develop something
"In AI safety, I want to be a pioneer, not a follower."
To transition -- to move from one role, stage or situation to another
"I have just transitioned from CIO into AI safety."
To accomplish -- to succeed in doing something difficult after real effort
"I have accomplished more than I ever expected."
To navigate -- to find your way through a difficult or complex situation
"I have navigated more than one crisis in my career."
Legacy -- the lasting impact or contribution you leave behind
"The engineers I have mentored are my real legacy."
Transformation -- a complete and important change in something
"I have led three major transformations."
To take on -- to accept a new responsibility or challenge
"I have just taken on the hardest challenge of my career."
To move on -- to leave a role or situation and go to the next one
"After eight great years, I decided to move on."
Track record -- the history of your past achievements and results
"I have built a track record that speaks for itself."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Lucia has worked in technology for over forty years. She has led teams on three continents, and she has spearheaded several major transformations. She has navigated more than one crisis, and she has reached important milestones along the way. She has been a leader since the early 2000s. Now she has just transitioned into AI safety, the most exciting challenge she has ever taken on. She has not finished her mission yet, and she has never stopped learning. Her track record speaks for itself, and it is becoming a real legacy.

1. Why does the text say "She has worked in technology for over forty years" and not "She works"?
A It is experience that started in the past and continues up to now (present perfect).
B It happened once, on a single day in the past.
C It is only her plan for next year.
2. "She has been a leader since the early 2000s" uses "since" because:
A "the early 2000s" is a length of time.
B "the early 2000s" is a starting point in time.
C "since" and "for" mean exactly the same thing.
3. The text says "She has just transitioned into AI safety." What does "just" tell us?
A It happened a very long time ago.
B It will happen next year.
C It happened very recently and still matters now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your experience, your recent news, and how long you have done something.

FormUseExample
Affirmative
have / has + past participle		Experience in your life so far, with no exact date.I have led teams on three continents.
Negative
have not / has not + participle		Something that has not happened up to now (often with "yet").I have not finished my mission yet.
Question
Have / Has + subject + participle		Ask about experience (often with "ever").Have you ever led a global team?
Recent news
just / already / yet		Something that happened very recently and still matters.I have just moved into AI safety.
Duration
for / since		for + a period (for ten years) · since + a point in time (since 2010). A finished past time takes the past simple: "I led that team in 2015."
