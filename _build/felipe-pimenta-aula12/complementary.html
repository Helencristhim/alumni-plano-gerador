Lesson 12 -- The Art of Pushback

Series
Succession -- Season 1, Episode 1 "Celebration" (HBO)

A media dynasty's boardroom, where every sentence is a power move. Connection to Lesson 12: watch how people disagree WITHOUT the word "no" -- and notice who concedes to win, and who attacks and loses the room.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down three moments where a character pushes back diplomatically -- and one where someone does it badly.

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- on disagreement and dissent (TED)

An organizational psychologist on why the healthiest teams argue well, and how to be a "disagreeable giver". Connection to Lesson 12: this is the exact skill of the lesson -- challenging ideas hard while keeping the relationship warm.

Tip: listen at 1x. Every time you hear a hedge ("I'd argue...", "could it be...?"), pause and say the sentence out loud.

TED Talk
Julia Dhar -- "How to disagree productively and find common ground"

A world-champion debater on how to argue a point hard while treating the other side as a partner, not an enemy. Connection to Lesson 12: it is the theory behind the whole lesson -- concede, question, and hold your ground without a fight.

Tip: watch at 0.75x if you need to. Then record 60 seconds disagreeing with a real decision, using her "find the common ground first" move.
