Chapter 4: The Code

Can You Count It?

How many patients? · How much evidence? · It used to be different.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"How many patients can take this treatment?" — 1, 2, 3...

"How much evidence do we have for the dose?" — you cannot count it

"There used to be very little access in the public sector." — before, not now

"Now there are several new programs." — more than a few

All four are correct. Look at the purple words — what makes English choose each one?

The list you must knowYou cannot count these, ever: data, evidence, access, funding, coverage, information. They have no plural and no "a": never "an evidence", never "two datas".

In Portuguese, "muito" is one word for everything, and "antigamente" is a whole word. In English: one question splits much from many — can I count it? — and one small chunk carries the past: used to.

Language Focus

Can You Count It, or Not?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We have many evidence for the new dose."
"We have a lot of evidence for the new dose."
"How much patients can take this treatment?"
"How many patients can take this treatment?"
"There used to been almost no access."
"There used to be almost no access."

One question decides the quantity: can I count it? And after used to, the verb stays in its base form: used to be, never "used to been".

Chapter 5: Voices

The Business Review

"So, how many patients can take this treatment?"

Dialogue

The Data Questions

M
Emmanuele, thank you. So, how many patients can take this treatment in Brazil?
E
The patient population is large, and it is growing. We have a lot of patients who can start the treatment this year.
M
Good. And how much evidence do we have for the higher dose?
E
A lot of new evidence, and more data every quarter. The dose is safe.
M
And the access? How much access is there in the public sector?
E
Two years ago there used to be almost no access. Today there is more: several new programs give better coverage in the private sector. But there is still not much public funding.
M
Thank you. That is exactly the picture I needed. Are there any updates on the reimbursement?
E
Not yet. The decision is slow. On Thursday I would like to discuss the public funding with you. So far, so good.
