Exactly the meeting you are training for: someone puts a chart of numbers on the screen, and you have to talk about it. This lesson gives you the sentences to describe the data, the trend and the amounts — how much went up, how many programs there are, what the figure shows — without freezing.

All American English — QUANTIFIERS: SOME or ANY? MUCH or MANY?

The grammar of this lesson in American English: when to use much and when to use many, and the difference between some and any. It all comes back to one question — can you count it? — explained with clear examples.

Tip: turn on the English subtitles. Pause at each example and say the SAME sentence with your own data: how many patients, how many programs, how much evidence, how much funding. Make 5 pairs: one with how many and one with how much.
