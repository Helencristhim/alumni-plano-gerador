Grammar Rule

Adjective Order and be / look like

FormUseExample
adjective + nounadjective before the nouna kind girl
opinion + size + colorthe usual ordera nice long brown dress
What is she like?personalityShe is generous.
What does she look like?appearanceShe has curly hair.
look like + personto resembleShe looks like her mother.

Key idea: put adjectives before the noun, in the order opinion + size + color. Use be like to ask about personality ("What is she like?") and look like to ask about appearance ("What does she look like?"). To say a person resembles another, use look like + person: she looks like her sister.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What does she like?" (to ask about her personality)
"What is she like?" (to ask about her personality)

"What does she like?" asks about her favorites (pizza, music). "What is she like?" asks about her personality (kind, shy). They are different questions.
