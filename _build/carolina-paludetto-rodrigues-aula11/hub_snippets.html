Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adjective order and be like / look like

GRAMMAR

Colocamos os adjetivos antes do substantivo e usamos be like para a personalidade e look like para a aparência. / We put adjectives before the noun and use be like for personality and look like for appearance.

FormUseExample
adjective + nounadjective before the nouna kind girl
opinion + size + colorthe usual ordera nice long brown dress
What is she like?personalityShe is generous.
What does she look like?appearanceShe has curly hair.
look like + personto resembleShe looks like her mother.

Key point / Ponto-chave: coloque os adjetivos antes do substantivo, na ordem opinião + tamanho + cor (a nice long brown dress). Use be like para perguntar sobre a personalidade ("What is she like?") e look like para a aparência ("What does she look like?"). Para dizer que alguém se parece com outra pessoa, use look like + pessoa: she looks like her sister. Cuidado: "What does she like?" pergunta os gostos, não a personalidade.
