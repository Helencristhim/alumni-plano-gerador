Travel Planning -- Booking

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Itinerary -- the full plan of a trip with times, flights, and stops
"Let me send you the itinerary for the trip." (Itinerário / roteiro)
Fare -- the price of a ticket for a flight, train, or bus
"The fare for the direct flight is higher." (Tarifa / preço da passagem)
Round trip -- a trip there and back, not one way
"I booked a round trip to Basel." (Ida e volta)
Layover -- a stop between flights where you change planes
"There is a short layover in Zurich." (Conexão / escala)
Departure -- the time a flight, train, or bus leaves
"The departure is at nine in the morning." (Partida / saída)
Baggage allowance -- how much luggage you are allowed to bring
"The baggage allowance is twenty-three kilos." (Franquia de bagagem)
Refundable -- a ticket you can cancel to get your money back
"The cheaper fare is not refundable." (Reembolsável)
Aisle seat -- a seat next to the corridor, easy to get up from
"I prefer an aisle seat on long flights." (Assento no corredor)
Travel insurance -- protection that pays you back if a trip goes wrong
"I always add travel insurance for work trips." (Seguro viagem)
Compare -- to look at two or more options and see the differences
"Let me compare the two flights." (Comparar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tom calls Aline to plan a trip to the Basel conference. "I found two flights, so let's compare them," he says. "Option A is a direct flight, so it is faster, but the fare is more expensive and it is not refundable." Aline checks the details: "Option B is cheaper, and it is refundable. It has a layover in Zurich, so it is longer, but the baggage allowance is bigger." They book Option B, choose an aisle seat, add travel insurance, and Tom sends the itinerary. Notice: to compare two things, add -er to short adjectives (cheaper, faster) or use more before long ones (more expensive), then add than.

1. Why is Option A faster?
A It is a direct flight with no layover.
B It is cheaper than Option B.
C It has a bigger baggage allowance.
2. Which comparative is correct?
A "Option A is more cheaper than B."
B "Option A is more expensive than B."
C "Option A is expensiver than B."
3. Why do they choose Option B?
A It is faster and direct.
B It is cheaper and refundable, with a bigger baggage allowance.
C It has no layover.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para comparar duas opções. / Use these forms to compare two options.

Tipo / TypeForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Adjetivo curto / Short adjectiveadjetivo + -er + thancheaper / faster than
Termina em -y / Ends in -y-y → -ier + thaneasy → easier than
Adjetivo longo / Long adjectivemore + adjetivo + thanmore expensive than
Irregularforma especial + thangood → better, bad → worse

Key point / Ponto-chave: para comparar duas coisas, acrescente -er a adjetivos curtos (cheaper, faster) ou use more antes de adjetivos longos (more expensive), e depois than. Nunca use os dois juntos: diga "cheaper than", não "more cheaper than"; e "more expensive", não "expensiver".
