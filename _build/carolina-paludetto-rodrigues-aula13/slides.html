Grammar Rule

The Past Simple

FormStructureExample
Regular verbverb + edI traveled. We walked.
Irregular verbnew past formgo → went, see → saw, eat → ate
More irregularsnew past formtake → took, swim → swam, buy → bought
Negativedid not + base verbI did not go home.
QuestionDid + subject + base verbDid you go to the beach?

Key idea: use the past simple for finished actions in the past. Regular verbs add -ed. Many common verbs are irregular (went, saw, ate, took, swam). After did / did not, always use the base verb (go, not went).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I goed to the beach." / "Did you went?"
"I went to the beach." / "Did you go?"

Irregular verbs do not take -ed: go becomes went, see becomes saw. After did or did not, always use the base verb: go, see, eat.
