Vocabulary

Vacation -- a time when you do not work or study and travel for fun

Trip -- a short journey to a place and back
"The trip to the coast took four hours." (Viagem)
Beach -- the sandy land next to the sea
"The beach was beautiful and quiet." (Praia)
Suitcase -- a large bag for your clothes when you travel
"I packed my suitcase on Friday night." (Mala)
Pack -- to put your things into a bag or suitcase
"We packed our bags before the trip." (Fazer as malas)
Souvenir -- a small gift you buy to remember a place
"She bought a colorful souvenir for Grandma." (Lembrança / souvenir)
Sunset -- the time when the sun goes down in the evening
"We watched a wonderful sunset on the beach." (Pôr do sol)
Postcard -- a card with a photo that you send to friends by mail
"I sent a postcard to my best friend Marina." (Cartão postal)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- the past simple

GRAMMAR

Usamos o past simple para ações terminadas no passado. Verbos regulares ganham -ed; muitos verbos comuns são irregulares. / We use the past simple for finished actions in the past. Regular verbs add -ed; many common verbs are irregular.

FormStructureExample
Affirmative (regular)verb + edI traveled. We walked.
Affirmative (irregular)new past formgo → went, see → saw, eat → ate
More irregularsnew past formtake → took, swim → swam, buy → bought
Negativedid not + base verbI did not go home.
QuestionDid + subject + base verbDid you go to the beach?

Key point / Ponto-chave: verbos regulares ganham -ed (travel → traveled, pack → packed). Muitos verbos comuns são irregulares e mudam de forma (go → went, see → saw, eat → ate, take → took, swim → swam, buy → bought). Para perguntas e negativas usamos did / did not + verbo na forma base: "Did you go?", "I did not go" -- nunca "Did you went".
