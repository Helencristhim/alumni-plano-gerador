Aula 19 — Pre-class

Softening Bad News (Hedging)

Dê uma notícia difícil com diplomacia: suavize o fato (it seems / there appears to be), o futuro (might / may) e o tamanho do problema (slight / minor) — "it seems the rollback caused a small delay".

Warm-up — Callback da Aula 18

Review

Na Aula 18 você aprendeu a ligar causa e resultado com because, so e as a result ("The server was overloaded, so requests timed out"). Agora você dá essa notícia ruim com tato. Use três ferramentas: suavize o fato com it seems / there appears to be; suavize o futuro com might / may / could; diminua o problema com slight / minor. Seja honesta, mas gentil. (Be honest, but gentle and diplomatic.)

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Setback -- a problem that delays or reverses progress (PT: contratempo / revés)
"It seems we have hit a small setback."
Shortfall -- a gap between what you have and what you need (PT: déficit / falta)
"There is a minor shortfall in the budget."
Drawback -- a disadvantage or negative side of an option (PT: desvantagem / ponto negativo)
"The main drawback is a longer timeline."
Workaround -- a temporary solution that avoids the problem (PT: solução de contorno / paliativo)
"We have a workaround in place for now."
Fallback -- a backup option to use if the first one fails (PT: plano de reserva / alternativa)
"We have a temporary fallback in place."
Caveat -- a warning or condition that limits what you say (PT: ressalva / advertência)
"One caveat: these dates are still tentative."
Tentative -- not final or confirmed yet; provisional (PT: provisório / não confirmado)
"The launch date is still tentative."
Preliminary -- early or initial, before the final version (PT: preliminar / inicial)
"These are preliminary findings, not final."
Oversight -- a small mistake made by not noticing something (PT: descuido / falha por desatenção)
"There was an oversight in the planning."
Tradeoff -- a balance where you gain one thing but lose another (PT: troca / compensação)
"The tradeoff is more time for a safer launch."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The direct version sounds harsh: "The project is late. The budget is short." Daniela softens it instead. First, she softens the fact: "It seems we have hit a small setback" and "There appears to be a slight delay." Second, she softens the future with modals: "We might need more time" and "We may need to revisit the numbers" — note that after might and may we use the base verb, never "to". Third, she shrinks the problem with words like slight and minor. She adds a caveat ("these figures are preliminary") and the workaround. The tone is gentle, but she stays clear and honest — she never hides the problem.

1. How does Daniela soften the fact that the project is late?
A She says "it seems" and "there appears to be".
B She hides the problem completely.
C She says it more directly.
2. What comes after "might" and "may"?
A "to" + verb
B the base verb, with no "to"
C the verb with -ing
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "We might to need more time."
B "We might need more time."
C "It seems is a small setback."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Hedging & Diplomacy

GRAMMAR

As três ferramentas para suavizar (bilingue EN + PT).

ToolComoExample
Suavizar o fatoit seems / there appears to be"It seems we are a little behind."
Suavizar o futuromight / may / could (+ verbo base)"We might need more time."
Diminuir o problemaslight / minor / small / a little"It is a slight setback."
Regra (PT)Suavize o fato com it seems / there appears to be; suavize o futuro com might / may / could (+ verbo base, NUNCA "might to"); diminua o problema com slight / minor. Suavize o tom, mas mantenha a clareza — nunca esconda o problema, e ofereça o workaround.

Quiz

A "It seems we are a little behind."
B "The project is a complete failure."
C "We are extremely late, sorry."

A "We might to need more time."
B "We might need a little more time."
C "We will definitely fail the deadline."
