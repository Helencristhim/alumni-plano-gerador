Aula 12 -- Pre-class

Coffee Shops and Cafes

Order at a cafe and make small talk: barista, to go, refill, straw, napkin, muffin, decaf, mug. Structures: "Can I get...?", "For here or to go?", "I'll have..." and "How's your day going?"

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Barista -- the person who makes and serves the coffee
"The barista is very friendly." (Atendente do café)
To go -- when you take your drink outside, not inside
"A coffee to go, please." (Para viagem)
Refill -- more of the same drink, often for free
"Is the refill free?" (Reabastecer / refil)
Straw -- the thin tube you drink through
"Could I have a straw?" (Canudo)
Napkin -- the paper you use to clean your hands or mouth
"Could I have a napkin?" (Guardanapo)
Muffin -- a small sweet cake you eat with coffee
"I'll have a muffin, too." (Bolinho)
Decaf -- coffee with no caffeine
"Do you have decaf?" (Descafeinado)
Mug -- a big cup for a hot drink
"A mug of coffee, please." (Caneca)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Elaine goes into a coffee shop. The barista smiles and asks what she wants. Can I get a medium coffee, please? says Elaine. For here or to go? asks the barista. For here, please, says Elaine. She also says, I'll have a muffin, too. The barista asks if she wants a straw and a napkin. While she waits, they make small talk. How's your day going? Pretty good, thank you! It is a nice, sunny morning.

A Give me a coffee.
B Can I get a medium coffee, please?
C I get coffee.
2. O que o barista pergunta? / What does the barista ask?
A For here or to go?
B Here or there go?
C You go here?
3. Qual e uma boa pergunta de small talk? / What is a good small talk question?
A How's your day going?
B How much your day?
C Where is your day?
