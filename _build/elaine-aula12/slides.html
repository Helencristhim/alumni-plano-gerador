Chapter 3

How to Order & Chat

Order, answer the barista, and make small talk

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Can I get a coffee, please?"

"For here or to go?"

"I'll have a muffin, too."

"How's your day going?"

StructureUseExample
Can I get / I'll have + drinkOrder at a cafe (casual)Can I get a coffee?
For here or to go?The barista asks where you drink itFor here, please.
How's your day going?Friendly small talkPretty good, thank you!

"Can I get" and "I'll have" are casual ways to order, like "Could I have" from Lesson 9.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ LESS POLITE

"I want a coffee."

✓ BETTER

"Can I get a coffee, please?"

✗ WRONG

"How is going your day?"

✓ RIGHT

"How is your day going?"

At the Cafe

Ordering a Coffee

M
"Hi! Welcome. What can I get for you?"
E
"Hi! Can I get a medium coffee, please?"
M
"Sure. For here or to go?"
E
"For here, please. Is the refill free?"
M
"Yes, the refill is free. Anything else?"
E
"I'll have a muffin, too. Do you have decaf?"
M
"Yes, we have decaf. Would you like a straw and a napkin?"
E
"Just a napkin, please. How's your day going?"
M
"Pretty good, thank you! It is busy today. Nice weather, isn't it?"
E
"Yes, it is lovely. How much is it?"
M
"Six dollars. Can I get your name for the cup?"
E
"It's Elaine. Thank you. Have a good one!"
