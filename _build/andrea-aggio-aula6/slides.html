Chapter 4: The Code

Polite Modals

could · would · should

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you send me your rate sheet?"

"I would love to schedule a call."

"You should visit our destination in high season."

Request, offer, or advice — and what comes after each modal?

ModalUseExample
could + base verbA polite request or a soft suggestionCould you send the rates?
would + base verbA warm offer or a professional wishI would love to help.
should + base verbA recommendation or adviceYou should visit in high season.

After every modal, use the base verb with no 'to': could send, would love, should visit.

Language Focus

Request, Offer, or Advice?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Could you to send me the rates?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you send me the rates?"

✗ WRONG

"You should to visit us in high season."

✓ RIGHT

"You should visit us in high season."

Modals (could, would, should) are always followed by the base verb, with no 'to' in between.

Dialogue

The Follow-Up Call

P
Hi Andrea, it is Paulo. It was a pleasure to meet you at the fair. I wanted to follow up.
A
Hi Paulo, so nice to hear from you! Could you send me your rate sheet for next season?
P
Of course. I would also love to give you a short product presentation.
A
That would be wonderful. I think there is a real mutual benefit here for both companies.
P
I agree. We would love to make Escape Turismo our preferred partner in Portugal.
A
Then we should also talk about a room block and a possible volume agreement.
P
Perfect. I will keep you posted, and I could send everything by Friday.
A
Wonderful. Let us stay in touch — this is the start of a great long-term relationship.
