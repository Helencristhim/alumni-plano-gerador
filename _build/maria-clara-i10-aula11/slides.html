Chapter 3: The Code

Should, Could, Might

you should go · you shouldn't wait · we could call · I might arrive late

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should confirm the appointment before the meeting."

"You shouldn't travel without your documents."

"We could invite the rector to the opening session."

"I might arrive late — the flight is delayed."

"Should I bring an interpreter?"

What comes after the orange word — confirm, travel, invite, arrive? And do they change with she or he?

ModalUse it forExample
shouldAdvice — a good idea, the right thing to do.You should confirm the date.
shouldn'tAdvice NOT to do — a bad idea.You shouldn't travel without a visa.
couldA suggestion — one possible option.We could invite the rector.
mightMaybe — you are not sure it will happen.I might arrive late.
The form: modal + base verbNo to, no -s, even with she/he. Always the base verb.She should go (NOT "should to go", NOT "shoulds").
Question: Should I...?Ask for advice — modal first, then I / we + verb.Should I bring an interpreter?
Expression: In my opinion...A soft, polite way to start your advice.In my opinion, we should wait.

The trick: after should, could, or might, always use the base verb — go, confirm, travel. Never "to", never "-s". Advice is that simple.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"You should to confirm the date."
"You should confirm the date."
"She shoulds travel with the group."
"She should travel with the group."
"We could to invite the rector."
"We could invite the rector."

One rule decides all three: after should, could, might, use the base verb. No to, no -s — ever.

Chapter 4: Asking for Advice

Bruno Asks You

Should he travel? Should he invite the rector? You decide.

Dialogue

Bruno Needs Your Advice

B
Maria Clara, can I ask for your advice? I have a few doubts before Seoul.
M
Of course. In my opinion, you should ask early — that is always the best option.
B
My visa might not be ready in time. Should I travel anyway?
M
No, you shouldn't travel without it. You could call the embassy today and explain the situation.
B
Good idea. And the rector — should I invite him to the opening session?
M
Yes, I recommend you invite him. But you might want to confirm the date first.
B
And the interpreter? She might cancel again.
M
Then prepare a second option. Better to be safe than sorry — I advise you to have a second plan.

The Delegation Advice Memo

MEMO — ADVICE FOR THE SEOUL DELEGATIONFrom: Maria Clara
DocumentsYou should carry your passport and visa with you. You shouldn't put them in your luggage.
ScheduleYou should confirm every appointment one day before. In my opinion, we could start earlier.
MoneyI recommend you take a little local currency, because you might need it at the airport.
InterpreterThe interpreter might cancel, so we should prepare a second option.
NoteYou shouldn't take a big risk before an important trip. Better to be safe than sorry.
1. Where should the team keep the documents — and where NOT?
With them, not in their luggage. "You should carry... you shouldn't put them in your luggage."
2. Find a soft suggestion in the memo. Which modal does it use?
"We could start earlier." It uses "could" — one option, not an order.
3. Why should the team take a little local currency?
Because they might need it at the airport.
4. What is the advice about the interpreter?
