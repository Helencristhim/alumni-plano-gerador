Chapter 1: The Setup

Four Moves to Win the Room

1. State your opinion clearly (In my view...)
2. Agree and build on good ideas (That's a valid point...)
3. Disagree politely (I see your point, but...)
4. Argue the consequence (If we do this, we'll...)

Chapter 5: Practice

Argue It Move by Move

State your opinion on the proposal.
"In my view, we should lower the commission for this one agency."
Agree and build on a colleague's idea.
"That's a valid point. Let me build on it: if we add a longer contract, we protect the margin."
Disagree politely with raising prices.
"I see your point, but I'm afraid I disagree. If we raise prices now, we'll lose smaller partners."
Express a reservation.
"I'm a bit on the fence. I have one reservation about the timing."
Argue the consequence with a first conditional.
"If we approve this today, we'll close the deal by Friday."
Find common ground to close the debate.
"I think we can find common ground. If we test it first, we'll have real data."
