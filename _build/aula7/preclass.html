Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of opinions and debate. Click Listen to check pronunciation.

Point of View -- a particular way of thinking about something; an opinion
"From my point of view, we should test the idea first."
Valid Point -- a reasonable, well-founded argument worth considering
"That's a valid point, and I'd like to build on it."
Build On -- to develop or expand an idea that someone else started
"Let me build on what you just said."
Common Ground -- shared opinions or interests that people agree on
"I think we can find common ground here."
Reservation -- a feeling of doubt or hesitation about something
"I have one reservation about the timing."
Push Back -- to express disagreement or resistance (politely)
"I'd like to push back on that idea a little."
On the Fence -- undecided; not sure which side to support
"I'm a bit on the fence about this one."
See Eye to Eye -- to fully agree with someone
"We don't always see eye to eye, but we respect each other."

Stage 1.2: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read a short debate. Notice the opinion frames and the first conditional, then answer.

"In my view, we should lower the rate for Agency X. If we win them, we'll open the whole region. I see your point, but there is a risk. I'm afraid I disagree on the full cut. If we offer the rate for six months only, we'll protect the margin and find common ground."
In "If we win them, we'll open the region", which form is correct after "if"?
A will + verb ("if we will win")
B present simple ("if we win")
C past simple ("if we won")
Which phrase disagrees politely?
A "You are wrong."
B "I see your point, but I'm afraid I disagree."
C "No way."

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Soften your opinion, then argue the result.

FunctionFrameExample
OpinionIn my view / From my perspective"In my view, we should wait."
AgreeThat's a valid point / Exactly"That's a valid point."
Disagree politelyI see your point, but / I'm afraid I disagree"I'm afraid I disagree."
First conditionalIf + present, will/can + base"If we wait, we'll lose the deal."

Watch out: never say "if we will" or "I am agree". Use "if we lower..., we'll win" and "I agree".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz
Your boss proposes something risky. You disagree. What's best?
A "That's wrong."
B "I see your point, but I'm afraid I disagree. If we do this, we'll lose smaller partners."
C "I am agree but no."
You like a colleague's idea and want to add to it. What's best?
A "That's a valid point. Let me build on it."
B "Yes yes, and also me too."
C "I have the same."
The team is split. You want to move forward. What's best?
A "We decide now, no talk."
B "Let's find common ground. If we test it first, we'll have data before deciding."
C "Everybody is on the fence forever."

Stage 5: Make Your Case

Reflection
Pick a real decision at Quinto Andar. Record 45 seconds: state your opinion, give one first-conditional argument for it, acknowledge one objection with a frame, and propose common ground.
Model: "In my view, we should lower the rate for Agency X. If we win them, we'll open the region. I see the risk, and I have one reservation about the margin, so if we offer the rate for six months only, we'll protect it and still win. I think we can find common ground there."
