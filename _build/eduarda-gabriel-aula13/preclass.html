Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key signing and contract terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Execute -- to sign a contract and make it legally valid
"Both parties will execute the agreement on Friday."
Binding -- legally obligating both sides to follow the deal
"Once signed, the contract is binding."
Terms and Conditions -- the rules and details written in an agreement
"The legal team reviewed the terms and conditions."
Default -- to fail to pay a debt or meet an obligation
"If the company defaults, the lender can act."
Waive -- to give up a right or claim on purpose
"The lender agreed to waive the late fee."
Breach -- a failure to follow the terms of a contract
"Missing the deadline would be a breach of contract."
In Writing -- recorded formally on paper, not just spoken
"Any change must be confirmed in writing."
Enforceable -- able to be required and upheld by law
"A signed contract is fully enforceable."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

The Vertex restructuring was ready to close. Our team sent the final draft, and the creditors reviewed the terms and conditions. We told them: "If both parties sign, the contract will be binding." They asked one question: "If the company defaults, what happens?" We promised a cure period, confirmed in writing. "Unless that period is met, it counts as a breach," we explained. Both sides agreed not to waive any clause without written consent. Once they execute the agreement, it becomes fully enforceable.

1. When does the contract become binding?
A As soon as the draft is sent.
B If both parties sign it.
C Only after a breach.
2. Why are "if" and "unless" used here?
A Because the actions already happened.
B To link a real condition to its result (first conditional).
C Because they are imaginary, impossible situations.
3. How can a clause be waived?
A Only with written consent from both sides.
B By one side alone, without telling the other.
C It can never be waived.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use the first conditional to link a real, possible condition to its result. Use if / unless + present simple in the condition, and will + base verb in the result. Usamos isso para condições reais e prováveis (não imaginárias). Unless significa "if not" (se não).

PartFormExample
Conditionif / unless + present simple"If both parties sign..."
Resultwill + base verb"...the contract will be binding."
unless= if not"Unless they pay = If they do not pay"

Common Mistake:

"If they will sign, the deal will close."

"If they sign, the deal will close."

Depois de if ou unless, use o presente simples. Nunca use "will" na parte da condição — só no resultado.
