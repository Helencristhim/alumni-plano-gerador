Chapter 3 — The Code

If, Unless & Will

Look at the purple words. How do we link a real condition to its result?

If both parties sign, the contract will be binding.
If the company defaults, the lender will act.
Unless we confirm in writing, the change will not count.
The deal will close on Friday if they approve today.
PartTenseExample
Condition (if / unless)present simple"If both parties sign..."
Resultwill + base verb"...the contract will be binding."
unless = if notpresent simple"Unless we confirm... = If we do not confirm..."
Grammar Rule

First Conditional

FunctionPatternExample
State a real conditionIf + present simple, ... will + base verb"If they sign, the deal will close."
State a negative conditionUnless + present simple (= if not)"Unless they pay, we will act."
Put the result first... will + base verb + if + present simple"The deal will close if they approve."
Warn about a consequenceIf + present simple, ... will not + base verb"If they default, we will not waive the fee."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"If they will sign, the deal will close."
"If they sign, the deal will close."

After if or unless, use the present simple — never "will". "Will" belongs only in the result.

Chapter 4 — Getting There

At the
Closing Table

See the vocabulary and the first conditional in a real signing.

Dialogue

The Closing Meeting

E
Mr. Vance, the final draft is ready. If your team approves the terms and conditions, we can sign today.
V
We approve, but we need one safeguard. If the company defaults, what happens?
E
If there is a default, you will get a thirty-day cure period, confirmed in writing.
V
Good. And unless that cure period is met, is the agreement still enforceable?
E
Yes. The contract stays fully binding. Unless both sides waive a clause, every term applies.
V
Understood. If anyone breaks a term, that is a breach, correct?
E
Exactly. So if we both sign now, we will execute the contract today.
V
Then let us sign. The deal is done.
Comprehension

About Mr. Vance

1. What safeguard does Mr. Vance ask about?
What happens if the company defaults.
2. What does Mr. Vance want to confirm about the agreement?
Whether it stays enforceable.
3. What does Mr. Vance decide to do at the end?
Sign and close the deal.
