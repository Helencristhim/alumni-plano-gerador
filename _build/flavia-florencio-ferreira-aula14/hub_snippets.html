Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clarification -- an explanation that makes something easier to understand
"I asked for clarification before I answered."
Rephrase -- to say the same thing again in different words
"Could you rephrase the question, please?"
Context -- the situation and facts around something
"Now I understand the context of your question."
Assumption -- something you think is true without proof
"He had a wrong assumption about my project."
Nuance -- a small difference in meaning
"There is a small nuance in her answer."
Unexpected -- surprising and not planned
"It was an unexpected question."
Moment -- a very short time
"Let me think for a moment."
Misunderstanding -- when people understand something in the wrong way
"It was just a small misunderstanding."
Redirect -- to guide the talk back to your main point
"She can redirect the talk to her main message."
Pause -- a short stop before you answer
"A short pause is not a problem."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the Q&A in Italy, a man asks Flavia an unexpected question. She does not know the answer at once, so she buys a moment: "That is a great question. Let me think for a moment." Then she is not sure, so she asks for clarification: "Could you say that again?" Now she sees the context and a small misunderstanding. Finally she answers with care: "It depends on the situation." Then she adds, "What I mean is," small businesses need different support.

1. Flavia says "Let me think for a moment." Why does she say this?
A To end the conversation.
B To buy a moment and get time to think.
C To ask for the price.
2. "Could you say that again?" What does Flavia want here?
A Clarification of the question.
B A new topic.
C The end of the event.
3. "It depends on the situation." What does this answer show?
A The answer is always the same.
B The answer can change with the situation.
C She does not want to answer.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Three Moves for a Hard Question

GRAMMAR

How to stay calm and clear when a question is difficult.

MoveUse it toSay
Buy timeget a moment to think"That is a great question. Let me think for a moment."
Ask for clarificationcheck the question"Could you say that again?" / "What do you mean exactly?"
Redirect & clarifyanswer and explain your point"It depends on the situation." / "What I mean is..."

A short pause is normal and professional. Use "It depends on..." when the answer can change, and "What I mean is..." to make your point clearer.
