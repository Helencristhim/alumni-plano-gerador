Chapter 3: Shall, Must, May

Duty, Obligation, Permission

The three modal verbs of every contract.

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"The supplier shall deliver the goods in thirty days."

"Each party must respect every clause."

"Either party may terminate the agreement."

"The parties must not share confidential data."

ModalMeaningExample
shall + base verbstrong duty (legal style)The supplier shall deliver the goods.
must + base verbobligation, you have toEach party must respect every clause.
may + base verbpermission, you are allowed toEither party may terminate it.
must not + base verbforbidden, you cannotThe parties must not share data.

After shall, must, may and must not, always use the base verb (deliver, pay, share), never "to" and never "-s". Shall and must are duties. May is permission. Must not is forbidden.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"The client must to pay each invoice."

✓ RIGHT

"The client must pay each invoice."

✗ WRONG

"The supplier shall delivers the goods."

✓ RIGHT

"The supplier shall deliver the goods."

After shall, must and may, never use "to" and never add "-s". Always use the base verb: must pay, shall deliver, may terminate. Remember: must and shall are duties, but may is only permission.

Chapter 4: Reading the Terms

Inside the Agreement

A real supply contract, clause by clause

Real Document

Gasparelli & Lima Supply Agreement

SUPPLY AGREEMENTValid 2 years
Clause 1The supplier shall deliver the goods within thirty days.
Clause 2The client must pay each invoice within fifteen days.
Clause 3Neither party may share confidential information.
Clause 4On a breach, either party may terminate the agreement.
Dialogue

Before We Sign

P
Rafael, before we sign, can you explain the main obligations of this contract?
R
Of course. The supplier shall deliver the goods within thirty days, and the client must pay each invoice within fifteen days.
P
Good. And may either party share the information with another company?
R
No. Clause three says the parties must not share confidential information with a third company.
P
And what happens if one party breaks a clause?
R
That is a breach of contract. In that case, the other party may terminate the agreement with written notice.
Comprehension

About the Contract

1. What must the client do, and when?
The client must pay each invoice within fifteen days.
2. May the parties share confidential information?
No. They must not share it with a third company.
3. What may a party do after a breach of contract?
It may terminate the agreement with written notice.
