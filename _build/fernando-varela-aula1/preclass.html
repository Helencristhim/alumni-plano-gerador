Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Role -- cargo, função (the job or position a person has)
"My role is to lead the oncology network."
In charge of -- responsável por (responsible for something or someone)
"I am in charge of the oncology network."
Based in -- localizado em (located in a particular place)
"Our hospital is based in São Paulo."
Colleague -- colega de trabalho (a person you work with)
"This is my colleague from the research team."
Partner -- parceiro (a hospital or company that works with you)
"We have many partners in Latin America."
Field -- área de atuação (the area of work you specialize in)
"I work in the field of oncology."
Network -- rede (a group of connected partners)
"I manage the oncology network."
Research -- pesquisa (a careful study to find new things)
"Our clinical research is in English."
Meeting -- reunião (when people come together to talk about work)
"I have a meeting with the consultant."
Get in touch -- entrar em contato (to make contact with someone)
"Let's get in touch after the meeting."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Fernando works at a big hospital. He is a business manager, and he is in charge of the oncology network. His hospital is based in São Paulo. "I work in the field of oncology," he says to a new partner. His team has many colleagues in Latin America. Fernando has a meeting with an American consultant two times a year. The research kick-off is in English too. When he meets a new person, he says: "Let's get in touch after the meeting." The partner asks: "What do you do? Where are you based?" and Fernando answers with confidence.

1. Por que dizemos "Fernando works" com -s no final?
A Porque a ação aconteceu no passado.
B Porque, no present simple, he/she/it leva -s no verbo.
C Porque "Fernando" é plural.
2. "His hospital is based in São Paulo" usa o present simple para expressar:
A Um fato permanente (onde o hospital fica localizado).
B Uma ação que vai acontecer no futuro.
C Algo que aconteceu só uma vez ontem.
3. Which question does the new partner ask correctly?
A "What you do?"
B "What do you do? Where are you based?"
C "Where you based are?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & WH-Questions

GRAMMAR

Quando usar o present simple para se apresentar (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
I / You / We + verbFatos e rotinas (sem -s)I work in oncology.
He / She / It + verb+s3ª pessoa SEMPRE leva -sShe leads the team.
am / is / areIdentidade, cargo, lugar. Use a/an antes da profissãoI am a manager.
WH + do + you + verbPerguntar sobre alguém (verbo de ação)What do you do?
WH + are + youCom to be, o verbo vem antes do sujeito: Where are you based? / What field are you in?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação profissional.

A partner asks you to describe your work. What do you say?
A "I do hospital things."
B "I am a business manager. I am in charge of the oncology network."
C "I am manager, I make oncology."
Someone asks: "Where are you based?" You respond:
A "I am based São Paulo."
B "Our hospital is based in São Paulo, Brazil."
C "I based in São Paulo am."
You want to ask a new partner about their job. The correct question is:
A "What you do for work?"
B "Which field you are in?"
C "What do you do? Which field are you in?"
You want to end the conversation and keep the contact. You say:
A "It was great meeting you. Let's get in touch after the meeting."
B "Okay, bye, finish now."
C "We get in touch since the meeting."
