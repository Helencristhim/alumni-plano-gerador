Chapter 3

The Code

Present simple & WH-questions to introduce yourself

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work in the field of oncology."

"I am in charge of the oncology network."

"My colleague works in the research team."

"What do you do? Where are you based?"

StructureUseExample
I / You / We workFacts and routines (no -s)I work in oncology.
He / She / It worksThird person adds -sShe leads the team.
am / is / areIdentity, role, locationI am a manager.
WH + do/are + youAsk about someoneWhat do you do? Where are you based?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"My colleague work in research."

✓ RIGHT

"My colleague works in research."

✗ WRONG

"I am manager."

✓ RIGHT

"I am a manager."

✗ WRONG

"What you do?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you do?"

Add -s after he/she/it. Use "a/an" before a job. In questions, use "do" before you: What do you do?

Meeting Someone

Dialogue

At the Meeting

S
Hi! I don't think we've met. I'm Sarah Bennett. Nice to meet you.
F
Nice to meet you too, Sarah. I'm Fernando. I work in the field of oncology.
S
Great! What do you do at the hospital?
F
I'm a business manager. I'm in charge of the oncology network.
S
Nice. Where are you based?
F
Our hospital is based in São Paulo. We have many partners in Latin America.
S
Wonderful. I'm the strategic consultant for your network. Let's stay in touch.
F
Thank you, Sarah. Let's get in touch after the meeting.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is Sarah's name and her job?
Her name is Sarah Bennett and she is the strategic consultant for the network.
2. What does Sarah want to do at the end?
She wants to stay in touch with Fernando.
3. Is Sarah a colleague or a consultant?
She is a consultant for the oncology network.
