Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Present Continuous vs Present Simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which sentences are about every week, and which are about right now?

"Carolina plays handball every Saturday."

"Right now, she is playing in the final."

"The team trains twice a week."

"Look! They are scoring again."

Grammar Rule

Now vs Every Day

TenseWhen we use itSignal words
Present Simple (play, trains)Habits, routines, rules, factsevery day, usually, always, on Saturdays
Present Continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing)Action happening now or temporarynow, right now, at the moment, look, listen

Key idea: "She plays every Saturday" is a habit (Present Simple). "She is playing now" is an action happening at this moment (Present Continuous). Spelling tip: play becomes playing, run becomes running, score becomes scoring.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She is playing handball every Saturday."
"She plays handball every Saturday."

Words like every Saturday, usually and always show a habit, so we use the Present Simple. Use the Present Continuous only for an action happening now.
