Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Carolina plays handball every Saturday, and her team trains twice a week. They usually defend very well. But right now, the final is on the court. Carolina is running fast and she is passing the ball to a teammate. Look! The coach is shouting instructions, and Carolina is throwing the ball. She scores! Her team is winning by two points.

1. What does Carolina do every Saturday?
A She plays handball (a habit, Present Simple).
B She is playing handball only now.
C She never plays handball.
2. Why do we say "she is running" and not "she runs" here?
A Because it is a habit.
B Because it is an action happening right now.
C Because running is a noun.
3. What is happening at the end of the text?
A Her team is winning by two points.
B Her team is losing the match.
C The match has not started.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous vs Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Usado para separar o que acontece AGORA do que acontece SEMPRE. / Used to separate what is happening NOW from what happens ALWAYS.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present SimpleHábitos, rotinas, regras, fatos / Habits, rules, factsShe plays every Saturday.
Present ContinuousAção acontecendo agora / Action happening nowShe is playing right now.
Form / Formaam / is / are + verbo-ingI am throwing, they are winning
Signal words / SinaisSimple: every day, usually / Continuous: now, lookevery Saturday vs right now

Key point / Ponto-chave: com "every day, usually, always", use o Present Simple. Com "now, right now, look, listen", use o Present Continuous (am/is/are + verbo-ing).
