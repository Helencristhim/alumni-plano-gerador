Lesson 18 -- Block 5 Review: The Global Meeting Simulation

Series
Succession / Industry — any high-stakes meeting where one person fields hard questions, owns a setback, brokers a deal, and runs the room

Watch a single scene with all five block skills in play: someone deflects a loaded question, delivers bad news (well or badly), pushes a compromise, wins backing from a peer, and chairs to a decision. The whole block, in one room.

Tip: pause at each turn and label the skill — Q&A, damage control, consensus, influence, or chair.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast / WorkLife with Adam Grant — any episode on leading global or cross-cultural teams and difficult conversations

Leaders unpack how the same five skills travel across cultures and accents: answering pressure, owning problems, finding common ground, persuading without authority, and running the room. Ideal prep before Victor's real cross-regional calls at Brink's.

Tip: jot one go-to line per skill that you'd actually say on a global call — circle back, own it, middle ground, win for you, recap and owners.

YouTube
TED / Harvard Business Review — "How to Run a Meeting" or any talk on communicating across accents and global teams

A practical bridge from the whole block into one global room: state a purpose, handle questions, deliver problems cleanly, broker compromise, win buy-in, and close with decisions and owners — while slowing down and confirming what you heard across accents.

Tip: script a five-line run sheet for your next real global call — one line per skill — and read it aloud before you dial in.
