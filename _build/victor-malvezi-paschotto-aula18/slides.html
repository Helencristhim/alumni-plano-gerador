Chapter 3

Structures Recap

Four grammar moves, one decision: which fits?

Grammar Discovery

Four Moves, Four Jobs

Same room, four different grammar tools — what does each one do?

"Could you tell me how the new figure was reached?" (indirect question — buy time, soften the ask)

"I'm afraid we've hit a setback, and we own it." (softener + active voice — deliver bad news)

"If we phased it, would you back it?" (second conditional — broker a deal)

"Shall we move on, and come back to that?" (soft imperative — steer the room)

One block, four structures. The skill is picking the right one in the moment.

The Rule

The Block 5 Grammar Map

StructureSkillExample
Indirect question (no inversion)handle Q&A, buy time"Could you tell me how it was reached?"
Softener + active voicedeliver a setback, take ownership"I'm afraid we missed the date."
Second conditional (if + past, would + verb)broker, build consensus"If we phased it, would you back it?"
Soft imperative (shall we / could we + bare verb)influence & chair the room"Shall we move on for now?"

The key thing to keep clean:

An indirect question keeps statement word order ("Could you tell me where it is," not "where is it"). A softener ("I'm afraid", "unfortunately") pairs with active voice — own the problem ("we missed it"), don't hide behind "mistakes were made". A second conditional brokers a deal with if + past and would + bare verb ("If we phased it, would you back it?"). A soft imperative steers with shall we / could we + bare verb or would you mind + -ing. Right tool, right moment — that is the whole block.

Common Mistake

Two Traps from the Block

✗ INVERTED

"Could you tell me where is the gap?"

✓ STATEMENT ORDER

"Could you tell me where the gap is?"

✗ EVASIVE PASSIVE

"Mistakes were made on the rollout."

✓ ACTIVE, OWNED

"We missed the date, and I own it."

Indirect questions stay in statement order. Bad news stays in active voice. Both keep you clear and credible.

Chapter 4

The Global Meeting

Five skills, one cross-regional call

Dialogue

The Cross-Regional Review

V
Thanks for joining from three time zones. We're here to lock the rollout plan and leave with an owner per region — two items, fifteen minutes each. Shall we start with the timeline?
B
Before the timeline — could you tell me how the new cost figure was reached? My region was quoted something very different last week.
V
That's a fair question. I'd hedge on the exact figure until finance confirms it, so let me defer the breakdown and circle back with the numbers right after this call.
B
Fine, as long as it actually comes. And I have to flag it — the pilot in my region is two weeks late. That worries everyone on this call.
V
You're right to raise it, and I'm afraid we did slip — we underestimated the training time, and I own that. Here's how we'll mitigate it: a second trainer this week, and the late region gets priority support. Let me reassure you, the launch date still holds.
B
I appreciate the honesty. But two regions want the full launch now, and two want to wait a quarter. We're at a stalemate, and I don't see how you bridge that.
V
Both sides have a real point. So here's a middle ground: if we phased it — launch in the two ready regions now and the others in Q3 — would your side back it? Each region concedes a little, and there's a clear win for you: your team isn't rushed. Can I count on your backing to align the others?
B
A phased launch I can sell to the others. You've got my backing. And honestly — that's the first global call this year that didn't end in a fight.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What two problems does Beatriz raise on the call?
She questions how the new cost figure was reached (her region was quoted differently), and she flags that the pilot in her region is two weeks late.
2. Why does Beatriz say the regions are stuck?
Two regions want the full launch now and two want to wait a quarter — a stalemate she can't see a way through.
3. What does Beatriz finally agree to, and why?
A phased launch — the ready regions now, the others in Q3 — because each region concedes a little and her team isn't rushed; she can sell it to the others.

Quick Fire
