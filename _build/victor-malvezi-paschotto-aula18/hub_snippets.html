Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The key words from across the block, back in your hands. Tap Listen to hear each one.

Hedge -- to avoid a firm answer when you are not certain yet (PT: evitar uma resposta definitiva)
"I'd hedge on the exact figure until the audit is final."
Defer -- to put off a question or detail until later (PT: adiar / deixar para depois)
"I'll defer the deep numbers and follow up by email."
Circle back -- to return to a topic at a later point (PT: voltar a um assunto depois)
"Good question -- let me circle back on that after the call."
Setback -- a delay or problem that holds back progress (PT: revés / contratempo)
"We hit a setback on the rollout, but it's recoverable."
Mitigate -- to make a problem or its damage less serious (PT: mitigar / reduzir o impacto)
"We added a backup supplier to mitigate the delay."
Align -- to bring teams into agreement on a direction (PT: alinhar)
"We need to align the two regions on one date."
Middle ground -- a compromise both sides can accept (PT: meio-termo)
"Phase one now, phase two in Q3 -- that's our middle ground."
Concede -- to give up a point, often to reach agreement (PT: ceder)
"I'll concede the timeline if you concede the budget."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the recap of the global call and answer the questions.

On the global call, four grammar moves did the heavy lifting. When a region pushed him on the numbers, the Growth Manager bought time with an indirect question — "Could you tell me how the figure was reached?" — notice the inside keeps statement order, "how the figure was reached", not "how was the figure reached". When a pilot slipped, he did not hide behind the passive; he softened it and used active voice: "I'm afraid we missed the date, and I own it" — "we missed it", not "mistakes were made". With two factions stuck, he brokered a deal using the second conditional: "If we phased it, would you back it?" — that is if + past, then would + bare verb, for a situation that is still hypothetical. And to keep the room moving he used a soft imperative: "Shall we move on?" — after shall we the verb is bare, with no to. Right tool, right moment — that is the whole block in one call.

1. Which word order is correct inside an indirect question?
A "Could you tell me how was the figure reached?" (inverted)
B "Could you tell me how the figure was reached?" (statement order)
C "Could you tell me how reached the figure was?"
2. Which is the stronger way to deliver bad news?
A "Mistakes were made and the date was missed." (evasive passive)
B "We missed the date, and I own it." (active voice, accountable)
C "The date might have been missed by someone."
3. How is the second conditional formed to broker a deal?
A "If we phase it, will you back it?" (first conditional)
B "If we phased it, would you back it?" (if + past, would + verb)
C "If we would phase it, you backed it."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Block 5 Grammar Map

GRAMMAR

Four structures, one decision in the moment: which one fits the job?

StructureSkillExample
Indirect question (no inversion)handle Q&A, buy time"Could you tell me how it was reached?"
Softener + active voicedeliver a setback, own it"I'm afraid we missed the date."
Second conditional (if + past, would + verb)broker, build consensus"If we phased it, would you back it?"
Soft imperative (shall we / could we + bare verb)influence & chair"Shall we move on for now?"
Watch the traps: an indirect question keeps statement order ("where it is", not "where is it"). Bad news stays in active voice with a softener ("we missed it", not "mistakes were made"). The second conditional is if + past + would + bare verb ("if we phased it, would you back it"). Soft imperatives take the bare verb after "shall we / could we" ("shall we start"). PT: quatro estruturas do bloco, uma decisao no momento. Indirect question NAO inverte ("could you tell me onde esta", e nao "onde esta isso"). Ma noticia em voz ATIVA com amortecedor ("nos perdemos o prazo", nunca "erros foram cometidos"). Segundo condicional = if + passado, would + verbo base ("if we phased it, would you back it"). Soft imperative = verbo base apos "shall we / could we". A habilidade do bloco e escolher a ferramenta certa na hora certa.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each moment on a global call.

A region fires a number you can't confirm. What's best?
A "It's roughly that, I think, probably." (a guess)
B "Fair question -- I'd hedge for now and circle back with the exact figure."
C "Could you tell me what is the number?" (inverted indirect question)
A milestone slipped and the room is worried. How do you deliver it?
A "Mistakes were made, but it's not really anyone's fault."
B "I'm afraid we hit a setback -- we own it, and here's how we'll mitigate it."
C "Everything is completely fine, ignore the dates." (denial)
Two regions are at a stalemate. What moves them?
A "One of you is just wrong, so let's vote."
B "If we phased it, would you back it? Each side concedes a little for a middle ground."
C "If we would phase it, you will back it." (wrong conditional)
You need backing from a peer who doesn't report to you. What's best?
A "You have to support this because I said so."
B "There's a real win in this for your region -- can I enlist your backing?"
C "It doesn't matter what you think anyway."
