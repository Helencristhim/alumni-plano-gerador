Chapter 3 -- Three Moves

Reply, Remind, Arrange

The three moves of the whole block, side by side

Grammar Notice

Notice the Three Moves

Which phrase replies, which one chases, and which one arranges?

REPLY
"I am sorry for the delay. We will ship a replacement today."

REMIND
"I am still waiting for your numbers. Could you reply by Friday?"

ARRANGE
"Are you free on Thursday? Could we move it to three?"

WRAP UP
"Thank you for your prompt reply. I will follow through and confirm it in writing."

Grammar Rule

One Move at a Time

MovePatternExample
Reply (offer a solution)I am sorry for... We will + verbI am sorry for the delay. We will ship it today.
Remind (still waiting)I am still waiting for... Could you ... by + day?I am still waiting for your numbers. Reply by Friday?
Arrange (ask for a time)Are you free on + day?Are you free on Thursday morning?
Arrange (move it)Could we move it to + new day?Could we move it to Thursday at three?

Key idea: every thread uses the same three moves -- reply (offer a solution with We will), remind (I am still waiting for + a deadline), and arrange (Are you free on / Could we move it to). Pick the move that fits.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I still wait your reply. Are you free in Thursday?"
"I am still waiting for your reply. Are you free on Thursday?"

Across all three moves: say still waiting for (not "still wait"), a deadline is by + a day, and we are free on a day -- then move it to a new one.
