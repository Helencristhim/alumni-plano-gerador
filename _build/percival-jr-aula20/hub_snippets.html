Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key linking words for this checkpoint. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Follow through -- to finish what you promised to do
"I always follow through on my emails." (Cumprir / dar continuidade)
Sort out -- to fix or solve a problem
"I will sort out the late delivery today." (Resolver)
Reach out -- to contact someone first, to start the talk
"Please reach out to the supplier this week." (Entrar em contato)
On hold -- paused for now; waiting to continue
"The contract review is on hold for now." (Em espera / pausado)
Wrap up -- to finish a task and close it
"Let's wrap up the supplier thread today." (Concluir / finalizar)
In writing -- written down, in an email -- not only spoken
"Please confirm it in writing." (Por escrito)
Prompt -- quick; done without delay
"Thank you for your prompt reply." (Rápido / imediato)
Look into -- to check or examine something carefully
"I will look into the missing numbers." (Investigar / verificar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the short text, then answer the questions. / Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Karen hands Percival three open jobs. First, a customer's delivery is late, so Percival replies: "I am sorry for the delay. We will ship a replacement today." The supplier will sort out the problem. Second, the supplier still owes the final numbers, so Percival sends a reminder: "I am still waiting for your numbers. Could you reply by Friday?" Thanks to a prompt reply, they arrive. Third, the contract review is on hold, so Percival writes: "Are you free on Thursday? Could we arrange it then?" He keeps everything in writing and will follow through to wrap up the thread.

1. How does Percival REPLY to the late delivery?
A He apologizes and offers a solution: "We will ship a replacement today."
B He ignores the customer.
C He blames the customer.
2. How does Percival REMIND the silent supplier?
A "I still wait your numbers until Friday."
B "I am still waiting for your numbers. Could you reply by Friday?"
C "Send the numbers now or else."
3. How does Percival ARRANGE the contract review?
A "Are you free on Thursday? Could we arrange it then?"
B "Are you free in Thursday?"
C "We cancel the review."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Three Moves

GRAMMAR

Todo o bloco usa três movimentos. RESPONDER: peça desculpa e ofereça uma solução com We will + verbo. LEMBRAR/COBRAR: mostre que espera com I am still waiting for + substantivo e marque um prazo com by + dia. MARCAR: pergunte Are you free on + dia? e proponha Could we move it to + novo dia? Use "on" com dias, "by" para prazos e "to" para o novo dia. / The whole block uses three moves: reply (We will + verb), remind (I am still waiting for + by + day), and arrange (Are you free on / Could we move it to).

Movimento / MovePatternExample
Responder / ReplyI am sorry for... We will + verbI am sorry for the delay. We will ship it today.
Cobrar / RemindI am still waiting for... by + dayI am still waiting for your numbers. Reply by Friday?
Marcar / ArrangeAre you free on + day?Are you free on Thursday morning?
Remarcar / Move itCould we move it to + new day?Could we move it to Thursday at three?

Key point / Ponto-chave: dizemos still waiting for (não "still wait"), um prazo é by + dia (não "until"), e marcamos free on + dia, depois move it to + novo dia. Mantenha tudo in writing e dê follow through. / Say still waiting for, a deadline is by + a day, and we are free on a day, then move it to a new one.

Aula 20 -- Communication Checkpoint

Tema da aula: checkpoint do bloco de comunicação -- juntar tudo num único thread profissional: responder a um e-mail (acknowledge + solution), cobrar uma resposta silenciosa (I am still waiting for / by Friday) e marcar/remarcar uma reunião (Are you free on...? / Could we move it to...?), mantendo tudo in writing e dando follow through. Reforço fora de aula -- marque como concluído ao terminar.
