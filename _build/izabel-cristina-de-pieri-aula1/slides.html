Chapter 3

The Code

Present simple for your role & forming questions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I deal with cash flow and taxes every day."

"My team takes care of the invoices."

"The partners do not have all the numbers yet."

"What do you do at AVRT? Who do you report to?"

StructureUseExample
I / You / We / They + verbFacts and routines (no -s)I deal with taxes.
He / She / It / My team + verb-sThird person adds -sShe reports to Andy.
do not / does notNegatives — they don't, he doesn'tThey don't have the data.
WH + do/does + subjectAsk about someone's job or routineWhat do you do? Who does she report to?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"They doesn't have an idea."

✓ RIGHT

"They don't have an idea."

✗ WRONG

"What would like to know?"

✓ RIGHT

"What would you like to know?"

✗ WRONG

"I report for the American partners."

✓ RIGHT

"I report to the American partners."

They / we / you use "don't"; he / she / it use "doesn't". Every question needs a subject. And you always report "to" someone.

Chapter 4

In the Room

Introducing yourself on the Thursday call

Dialogue

The Thursday Call

A
Good morning, Izabel. We have a couple of new partners on the call today, joining from Chicago. Could you introduce yourself for them?
I
Of course. Hi everyone, I am Izabel, the CEO of AVRT here in Brazil. I am in charge of finance and operations.
A
Great. And who do you report to on the group side?
I
I report to you and the board. On a daily basis, I deal with cash flow, taxes, and stock control.
A
Perfect. What would you say is your main challenge right now?
I
My main challenge is the new tax reform. We came up with a plan to wait before we change the ERP system.
A
That sounds sensible. The partners will want to hear more about that next week.
I
Happy to walk them through it. I will send a short summary before the call.
Comprehension

They will want to hear more about the tax reform and the ERP plan.
