Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Be in charge of -- to be responsible for an area, team, or decision
"I am in charge of finance and operations at AVRT."
Report to -- to have someone as your direct manager
"I report to Andy and the American partners."
Deal with -- to take action on a problem or task (phrasal verb -- lidar com)
"I deal with taxes, cash flow, and suppliers every day."
Take care of -- to be responsible for handling something (phrasal verb -- cuidar de)
"My team takes care of the day-to-day operations."
Come up with -- to think of an idea or solution (phrasal verb -- criar, bolar)
"We came up with a plan to wait for the tax reform."
Background -- your past experience, education, and training
"My background is in finance and taxation."
Current role -- the position you hold now
"In my current role, I lead the whole operation."
Responsibilities -- the duties and tasks your job requires
"My responsibilities cover finance, tax, and stock control."
Cash flow -- the money coming in and going out of a business
"I monitor the cash flow on a weekly basis."
In-house -- done inside the company, not outsourced
"We keep the accounting in-house instead of hiring a firm."
Challenge -- a demanding problem or task you must handle (desafio)
"My main challenge right now is the new tax reform."
On a daily basis -- every day, as part of your routine (diariamente)
"On a daily basis, I review invoices and approve payments."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Izabel is the CEO of AVRT. She is in charge of finance and operations, and she reports to Andy and the board. On a daily basis, she deals with cash flow, taxes, and stock control. Her team takes care of the invoices and payments, but the big decisions come to her. "My background is in finance," she says. Her main challenge right now is the new tax reform. The partners don't have all the numbers yet, so they ask her a lot of questions. When a new partner joins the call, Izabel says: "In my current role, I keep most of the accounting in-house." Then she asks back: "What do you do, and who do you report to?"

1. Why do we write "she deals with" and "she reports" with an -s?
A Because the action happened in the past.
B Because in the present simple, he/she/it adds -s to the verb.
C Because "Izabel" is plural.
2. "The partners don't have all the numbers yet." Why "don't" and not "doesn't"?
A Because "the partners" is plural (they), so we use "don't".
B Because "don't" is more polite than "doesn't".
C Because the sentence is a question.
3. Which question does Izabel form correctly?
A "What you do, and who you report to?"
B "What do you do, and who do you report to?"
C "What do you do, and who report you to?"
