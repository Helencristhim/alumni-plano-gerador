Tense Rule

Past Simple vs Present Perfect

TenseWhen to use itForm & example
Past simpleA finished action at an exact time (yesterday, in 2017, last year, ago)verb + ed / V2 — "I started in 2017."
Present perfectLife experience or a recent result, with no exact time (ever, never, already, yet, just, since, for)have / has + past participle — "I have worked here for years."
Key questionDo I say exactly when? Yes → past simple. No → present perfect.
Common Mistake

Wrong Tense vs Right Tense

✗ WRONG

"I have started this job in 2017."

✓ RIGHT

"I started this job in 2017." (exact time = past simple)

✗ WRONG

"Did you ever work abroad in your life?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you ever worked abroad?" (experience = present perfect)

Exact, finished time uses past simple. Experience with no time uses present perfect.

Chapter 4

The Practice Room

Daniela meets Tom, a new colleague

Dialogue

Catching Up at the Office

T
Hi Daniela, good to see you again. I’ve just moved to this office last month. Have you worked here long?
D
Welcome! Yes, I have worked here for eight years. I started in 2017 as an analyst.
T
Eight years, impressive. Have you ever managed an international experience like a global project?
D
Yes, I have. Last year I led a project with the Lisbon team, and we finished it in December.
T
That is great. I have never worked with that team, but I have heard they are excellent.
D
They are. We have already started a new project together, and we have not finished it yet.
T
So you have done a lot recently. When did you become a manager?
D
I became a manager three years ago. Since then, I have grown so much.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How long has Daniela worked at the company, and when did she start?
She has worked there for eight years (present perfect), and she started in 2017 (past simple).
2. What did the Lisbon team do last year, and when did they finish?
They did a project with Daniela and finished it in December — a past simple action with an exact time.
3. Why does Daniela use the present perfect for the new project?
Because it is not finished yet — they have already started it, and the action continues now.

Chapter 5

Tricky Tenses

Drill it until it feels easy

Quick Fire

Choose the Tense

1 / 5

1. Make a question with ever: managed a team?

"Have you ever managed a team?" (experience = present perfect)

2. Finish it: "I ___ this report yesterday."

"I finished this report yesterday." (yesterday = past simple)

3. Use yet in a negative: the email / not send.

"I have not sent the email yet." (yet = present perfect)

4. Use since: I / be a manager / 2021.

"I have been a manager since 2021." (since = present perfect)

5. Use just: we / launch / the new system.

"We have just launched the new system." (just = present perfect)
