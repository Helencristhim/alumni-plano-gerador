Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Experience -- the knowledge and skills you have built from things you have done (PT: experiência)
"I have ten years of experience in IT."
Ever -- at any time in your life, used in questions (PT: alguma vez)
"Have you ever led a big project?"
Never -- not at any time in your life, the opposite of ever (PT: nunca)
"I have never worked abroad."
Already -- before now, often sooner than expected (PT: já)
"We have already finished the first phase."
Yet -- up to now, used in negatives and questions (PT: ainda)
"I have not finished the report yet."
Just -- a very short time ago, used with the present perfect (PT: acabou de)
"We have just launched the new system."
Since -- from a point in the past until now (PT: desde)
"I have been a manager since 2021."
Recently -- a short time ago, not long before now (PT: recentemente)
"I have changed teams recently."
So far -- up to this point in time (PT: até agora)
"So far, the project has gone well."
Accomplish -- to complete something important, often after effort (PT: realizar)
"I have accomplished a lot this year."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Daniela talked about her career. "I have worked in IT for eight years," she said. That is her experience, with no exact time, so she uses the present perfect. Then she gave a finished moment: "I started here in 2017." That has an exact time, so she uses the past simple. "I have never worked abroad," she added, "but I have already led an international project. We have just launched a new system, and we have not finished the next one yet." So far, Daniela has accomplished a lot, and recently she has grown even more.

1. Why does Daniela use the present perfect for "I have worked in IT for eight years"?
A It is her experience, with no exact time.
B It happened at one exact moment.
C It is a plan for the future.
2. Why does she use the past simple for "I started here in 2017"?
A Because there is no exact time.
B Because 2017 is an exact, finished time.
C Because it is still happening now.
3. What does "we have not finished the next one yet" mean?
A The next project is already done.
B They never started it.
C It is not done now, but it is still in progress.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo verbal (bilingue EN + PT).

TenseWhenForm & example
Past simpleFinished action, exact time (yesterday, in 2017, ago)verb + ed / V2 — "I started in 2017."
Present perfect (affirmative)Experience / recent result, no exact timehave / has + past participle — "I have worked here for years."
Present perfect (negative & question)With never, yet, ever"I have not finished yet." / "Have you ever led a team?"
Regra (PT)Pergunte: eu digo exatamente quando? Se SIM, use o past simple (verbo no passado). Se NÃO — é experiência ou resultado recente — use o present perfect (have/has + particípio).

Stage 2: How to Choose the Tense

Order

Coloque os passos para escolher o tempo verbal na ordem correta.

?If there is an exact time, use the past simple.
?Think about the action you want to describe.
?If there is no exact time, use the present perfect.
?Ask yourself: do I say exactly when it happened?
?Add a signal word, like in 2017 or already.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesma dizendo-a com bom ritmo.

I have worked here for eight years.
Trabalho aqui há oito anos. (present perfect — experiência)
I started this job in 2017.
Comecei neste emprego em 2017. (past simple — tempo exato)
Have you ever led a big project?
Você já liderou um grande projeto? (pergunta de experiência)
I have not finished it yet.
Ainda não terminei. (negativa com yet)
We have just launched the new system.
Acabamos de lançar o novo sistema. (resultado recente com just)
