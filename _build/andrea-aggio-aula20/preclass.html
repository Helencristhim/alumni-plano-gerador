Aula 20 -- Pre-class

Sharing Your Vision and Inspiring the Room

How to share a bold vision and inspire an audience -- using inversion for emphasis. Key words: vision, keynote, to inspire, legacy, turning point, to rally, resilience, momentum, to champion, bold, to envision, aspiration. Structure: front a negative/limiting phrase, then invert (Never before HAVE WE...; Not only DID WE...; Only by... DID WE...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Vision -- a clear, ambitious picture of the future you want to build (visao de futuro)
"Every strong leader starts with a vision people can see."
Keynote -- the main speech that sets the theme of an event (palestra principal)
"She was invited to give the keynote at the tourism summit."
To inspire -- to fill people with the desire and confidence to act (inspirar)
"A good leader doesn't just inform -- she inspires."
Legacy -- the lasting impact you leave behind (legado)
"I want our legacy to be a company that raised the whole industry."
Turning point -- a moment of decisive change in a story or journey (ponto de virada)
"That deal was the turning point for the whole company."
To rally -- to bring people together and energize them around a cause (mobilizar, unir em torno de)
"She rallied the team around one bold goal for the year."
Resilience -- the ability to recover and keep going after difficulty (resiliencia)
"Our resilience during the downturn is why we're still here."
Momentum -- the force of steady, ongoing progress (impulso, embalo)
"Once the bookings started, the momentum was unstoppable."
To champion -- to actively support and promote a cause or idea (defender, promover ativamente)
"I've always championed sustainable tourism."
Bold -- brave, confident, and willing to take risks (ousado, audacioso)
"It was a bold move, and it paid off."
To envision -- to picture something clearly as a future possibility (vislumbrar, imaginar)
"I envision a company that leads, not follows."
Aspiration -- a strong hope or ambition to achieve something (aspiracao)
"Our aspiration is to be the region's most trusted operator."

GRAMMAR

Andrea is invited to give the closing keynote at a tourism summit. She wants to inspire the room with a bold vision: the company stopped following the market and started leading it. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Inversion for Emphasis

GRAMMAR

Como inverter a ordem para dar forca a uma frase -- a gramatica dos discursos e das visoes (em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
Never / Rarely / Seldomadverbio negativo na frente -> auxiliar antes do sujeito. negative adverb first -> invert.Never have we seen this.
Not only ... but also'not only' na frente, inverte, e acrescenta o ponto maior. front, invert, add.Not only did we recover, but we also grew.
Only by / when / after'only + ...' na frente -> inverte a clausula PRINCIPAL. invert the main clause.Only by being bold did we get here.
Regra de ouro / Golden ruleuse com moderacao, so no pico. a pinch, not a sauce -- peak moments only.inverter tudo soa estranho.
