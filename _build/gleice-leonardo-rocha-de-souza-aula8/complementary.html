Lesson 8 -- The Pharma World

Series / Documentary
Explained -- an episode about the medicine and pharma industry

A clear look at how a company develops a product, supplies pharmacies, and launches a new treatment for patients. Listen for present simple verbs with -s: develops, supplies, sells, launches, helps.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences in the present simple about what a pharma company does. Use the new words: patient, treatment, product, market, pharmacy, brand, supply, launch.

Podcast
The Readout -- a podcast about the pharma market

People talk about how a brand develops a product, reaches the market, and helps patients. Most of the talk is in the present simple, the grammar from today: the company develops, the team supplies, the pharmacy sells.

Choose one short episode. Note 3 present simple verbs with -s the speakers use to describe the pharma world. Compare them with the words from Carlos and Renata in class.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Present simple: he, she, it"

A short, clear lesson on the present simple with -s: he develops, she sells, it launches. It also shows how to make questions and negatives with does plus the base verb (Does the company sell?).

After watching, write five sentences about your world at Sanofi with present simple verbs, then record a 60-second audio describing what your company develops, supplies, and launches.
