Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Pricila wants to visit the Opera House. At the subway station she asks an officer the way. "Take the blue line and get off at Circular Quay," he says. "Go straight and the exit is across from the harbour." She buys a ticket from the machine — the fare is four dollars — and finds platform two. She does not have to transfer; the train is direct. Four stops later, she walks out of the station and the Opera House is right there, next to the water.

1. Which sentence GIVES a direction (imperative)?
A "Take the blue line and get off at Circular Quay."
B "The fare is four dollars."
C "She buys a ticket."
2. Which words are prepositions of place?
A "take, get off"
B "across from, next to"
C "ticket, fare"
3. Does Pricila have to transfer?
A Yes, twice.
B No, the train is direct.
C The text does not say.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Imperatives = verbo primeiro, sem sujeito (Go, Take, Turn, Get off)
Prepositions of place = onde algo fica (next to, across from, between, opposite, behind, on the corner)

UseStructureExample
DirectionVerb first"Take the blue line."
LocationIt is + preposition + place"It is across from the exit."
Ask the wayHow do I get to + place?"How do I get to the museum?"

Common Mistake:

"It is across the station."

"It is across from the station."

Use a preposição completa: across FROM, next TO, in front OF.
