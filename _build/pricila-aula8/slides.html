Chapter 3: The Code

Directions
& Places

Imperatives + prepositions of place

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Take the blue line and get off at Central.
Go straight and turn left at the corner.
The exit is across from the platform.

What do the orange words have in common?

UseStructureExample
Give a directionVerb first (no subject)Go straight. Take the blue line. Get off at Central.
Say where it isIt is + preposition + placeIt is across from the station.
Ask the wayHow do I get to + place?How do I get to the Opera House?

Key: direções = verbo primeiro (sem “you”). Lugares = preposições: next to, across from, between, opposite, on the corner, behind.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"Go to straight." / "It is across the station."
"Go straight." / "It is across from the station."

No “to” before straight. Use across from, not “across the”.

Grammar Practice

Find the Way

Tell someone to use the metro → ???Take the subway / Take the blue line.
Tell someone to leave the train → ???Get off at the next stop.
The exit / __ / the platform (other side) → ???The exit is across from the platform.
The station / __ / the hotel (very close) → ???The station is next to the hotel.
Ask the way to the museum → ???How do I get to the museum?

Comprehension

Check Your Understanding

1. Which line does she take, and where does she get off?
The blue line; she gets off at Circular Quay.
2. How many stops, and does she have to transfer?
Four stops; no transfer, it is direct.
3. How much is the fare, and where is the machine?
Four dollars; the machine is next to the exit.
4. Which platform, and how does she get there?
Platform two; go down the stairs and turn right.

Semi-Free Role-Play

Level 2: You Give Directions

A tourist is lost and asks YOU the way. Give clear directions to the station or the hotel. Use these starters:
"Go straight and..."
"Take the... line and get off at..."
"It is across from / next to..."
"You have to transfer at..."
Free Role-Play

Level 3: Speak Freely

No keywords. Plan a trip across the city: ask the way, buy a ticket, find the platform, transfer if needed, and find the exit — all in English. Be yourself.
