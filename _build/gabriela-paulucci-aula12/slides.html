Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What's the weather like? -- It's sunny / It's cold

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we ask and answer about the weather?

"What is the weather like? It is sunny."

"It is cold today, so I wear a coat."

"It is rainy, so I take my umbrella."

"Is it hot in summer? Yes, it is."

Grammar Rule

What's the Weather Like?

FormWhen we use itExample
What is the weather like?To ask about the weatherWhat is the weather like?
It is + weather wordTo answer / describeIt is sunny.
Is it...?To ask a yes/no questionIs it cold?
I wear + clothesTo say what you put onI wear a coat.

Key idea: ask with What is the weather like? and answer with It is + a weather word, like sunny, rainy, or cold.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"There is sunny today."
"It is sunny today."

For the weather, use It is. Say It is sunny, not There is sunny.
