Aula 04 -- Pre-class

Looking Ahead -- Future Plans

Talk about the future: plan, goal, soon, next month, improve, launch, promise, probably. Structures: "be going to" (plans) and "will" (predictions & promises).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Plan -- what you decide to do in the future
"I have a plan for the project." (Plano)
Goal -- what you want to achieve
"My goal is to speak English well." (Meta / objetivo)
Soon -- in a short time, not long from now
"We will start soon." (Em breve)
Next month -- the month after this one
"I will travel next month." (Mês que vem)
Improve -- to get better at something
"I want to improve my English." (Melhorar)
Launch -- to start a new product or project
"We are going to launch a product." (Lançar)
Promise -- to say you will surely do something
"I will finish it. I promise." (Prometer)
Probably -- almost sure, but not one hundred percent
"It will probably be ready in May." (Provavelmente)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline has a new project at Novartis. She is going to lead it. Her plan is simple: first, the team is going to meet next week. Her goal is to launch a new app in October. "Do you think it will be ready?" asks Daniel. "Yes, it will probably be ready soon," says Aline. "I promise it will be a success."

A A meeting next week.
B A new project.
C An English class.
2. Qual é a meta dela? / What is her goal?
A To launch a new app in October.
B To hire ten people.
C To travel next month.
3. O que Aline promete? / What does Aline promise?
A That it will be expensive.
B That it will be a success.
C That she will resign.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Going To vs Will

GRAMMAR

Duas formas de falar do futuro. / Two ways to talk about the future.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
be going to + verbPlano já decidido / A plan decided beforeI am going to lead it.
will + verbPrevisão, promessa, decisão na hora / Prediction, promise, quick decisionI will help you.
Negative / Negativanot going to / will not (won't)I am not going to resign. / It won't be late.

Atenção: depois de will nunca use "to" (will start, não "will to start"). Depois de going sempre use "to" (going to start).
