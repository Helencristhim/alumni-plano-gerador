Chapter 3

Going To vs Will

Two ways to talk about the future

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"I am going to start a new project."

"We are going to launch in May."

"I think it will be a success."

"I will send you the report tomorrow."

StructureUseExample
be going to + verbA plan decided before nowI am going to lead the project.
will + verbPredictions, promises, quick decisionsI will help you.
Time wordsWhen in the futuresoon, tomorrow, next month

Use "be going to" for plans you already decided. Use "will" for predictions and promises.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I will to start tomorrow."

✓ RIGHT

"I will start tomorrow."

✗ WRONG

"I am going start the project."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to start the project."
