Chapter 3: The Code

Cleft Sentences for Emphasis

Putting the spotlight on what mattered most

Grammar Discovery

How English Adds Emphasis

What I remember most is the people, not the places.

It was the harbor that stayed with me.

It was Liv who showed me the real town.

The thing that made the trip was one quiet afternoon.

To spotlight an idea: What ... is / was ... To spotlight a thing or person: It was ... that / who ...

To emphasise...StructureExample
an idea / actionWhat + clause + is / was ...What I loved was the silence.
a thingIt + is / was + noun + that ...It was the silence that I loved.
a personIt + is / was + person + who ...It was Liv who welcomed me.
the pointThe thing / reason / place that ... is / wasThe reason I went back was the people.

Do not add an extra it: say "What I loved was the food," never "What I loved it was the food." And after "the thing / the reason," use that, never "what": "the thing that mattered," not "the thing what mattered."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"What made the trip it was the people."
"What made the trip was the people."
"The thing what I remember is the harbor."
"The thing that I remember is the harbor."

After "What ...," do not add an extra it: "What I loved was...", not "What I loved it was...". And after "the thing / the reason," use that, never "what": "the thing that mattered."

Chapter 4: Looking Back with Liv

Remembering the Trip with Liv

Two travelers, one shared journey, and what really mattered

Dialogue

What Really Mattered

L
You know, people always ask me about the mountains on that trip. But honestly, it was not the mountains that stayed with me. What I remember most is that little kitchen where we cooked with your neighbors.
M
Exactly. The scenery was the icing on the cake, but it was that kitchen that made the whole week. What I cherish most is that nobody was in a hurry.
L
And it was your neighbor, the old fisherman, who made all the difference. Do you remember? The trip could have been ordinary. He was its saving grace.
M
He was, hands down. Come to think of it, the whole trip boils down to that one evening on his porch. That was the defining moment for me.
L
I still have the little wooden bird he carved for me. It is my favorite keepsake. Above all, that is what those weeks were really about, is it not? Not the views. The people.
M
It really is. Leaving was bittersweet, but that is the essence of a good trip. What you take home is not a photo. It is a face, a table, an evening you will never forget.
