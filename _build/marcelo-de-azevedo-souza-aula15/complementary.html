Aula 15 -- What Really Made the Trip

Film
Lost in Translation (2003) -- directed by Sofia Coppola

Two Americans, alone in a huge Tokyo hotel, form a quiet, fleeting friendship over a few days. The neon city is all around them, but the film knows exactly what the trip was really about, and it was never the sights. It was one conversation, one late night, one connection that neither of them expected. It is tonight's whole lesson turned into a film: what makes a journey is rarely the postcard, it is the person you meet. Connection to Lesson 15: the essence of the trip is a human moment, not a place.

Tip: after a scene, sum it up with a cleft sentence -- "What mattered to them was...", "It was that night that...", "The reason it stayed with them was...".

Podcast
Travel with Rick Steves -- any episode

Rick Steves talks each week with guests and callers about cultures, people, and the small things that give a trip its extra sparkle -- almost never the monuments, and almost always a meal, a market, a conversation. It is the perfect companion to tonight's lesson: a whole show about what really makes a place, told by travelers who know that the essence of a journey boils down to the people you meet. Great ear training, full of natural emphasis ("what I loved was...", "it was the locals who...").

Tip: after an episode, describe the place in three emphatic sentences -- "What makes it special is...", "It was... that...", "Above all,...".
