What Really Made the Trip -- Saying What Mattered Most

Saying what really made a trip special and putting the spotlight on it with cleft sentences. Key words: the highlight, hands down, to make all the difference, the icing on the cake, a saving grace, above all, to boil down to, a defining moment, to cherish, a keepsake, bittersweet, the essence. Focus: cleft sentences for emphasis (What I loved was...; It was... that / who...; The reason... was...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

The highlight -- the best or most memorable part of something (o ponto alto, o melhor momento)
"The highlight of the trip was a dinner with strangers."
Hands down -- without any doubt at all; used for strong emphasis (sem sombra de dúvida (para dar ênfase))
"That was, hands down, the best meal of my life."
To make all the difference -- to have a very important, positive effect (fazer toda a diferença)
"A friendly guide made all the difference to that week."
The icing on the cake -- an extra good thing that makes something even better (a cereja do bolo)
"The trip was wonderful, and the sunset was the icing on the cake."
A saving grace -- the one good quality that stops something from being a failure (o ponto que salva, a única coisa boa)
"The hotel was dull, but the view was its saving grace."
Above all -- most importantly of all; more than anything else (acima de tudo, mais que tudo)
"Above all, what I remember is how kind people were."
To boil down to -- to be reduced to the single most essential point (resumir-se a, reduzir-se ao essencial)
"It all boils down to the people you meet."
A defining moment -- a moment that shapes or reveals what something truly is (um momento decisivo, que define algo)
"That conversation was a defining moment of the whole trip."
To cherish -- to value and care for something deeply (prezar, valorizar profundamente)
"I cherish the memory of that quiet afternoon."
A keepsake -- a small object you keep to remember a person or place (uma lembrança (objeto guardado))
"I brought home a little stone as a keepsake."
Bittersweet -- happy and sad at the same time (agridoce, doce e amargo ao mesmo tempo)
"Leaving was bittersweet: joyful and a little sad."
The essence -- the most important quality that makes something what it is (a essência)
"The essence of the trip was the welcome, not the sights."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Marcelo talks about his favorite trip, he never starts with the mountains. What he remembers most is a small kitchen. It was an old fisherman who made all the difference; he was the trip's saving grace. Come to think of it, the whole week boils down to one evening on his porch. That was a defining moment, and it is a memory Marcelo will always cherish. The scenery was only the icing on the cake. When one thing matters most, English lets you put it in the spotlight. To emphasise an idea, we say What I loved was the welcome. To emphasise a thing, we say It was the welcome that I loved. To emphasise a person, we say It was Liv who welcomed me.

1. Marcelo diz "What he remembers most is a kitchen". Por que ele usa essa estrutura em vez de "He remembers a kitchen"?
A Para dar ênfase, colocar a cozinha no centro, no holofote (clivada: What ... is ...).
B Porque o verbo "remember" exige a palavra "what".
C Porque essa é a única forma gramatical possível.
2. Which sentence emphasises a PERSON correctly?
A "It was Liv what welcomed me."
B "It was Liv who welcomed me."
C "What I loved it was Liv."
3. You want to emphasise the food. Which sentence is correct?
A "What I loved was the food."
B "What I loved it was the food."
C "What I loved were the food."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences (Emphasis)

GRAMMAR

Como dar ênfase em inglês (explicação em inglês e português).

To emphasise... / Para enfatizar...StructureExample
an idea / uma ideiaWhat + clause + is / wasWhat I loved was the silence.
a thing / uma coisaIt was + noun + thatIt was the silence that I loved.
a person / uma pessoaIt was + person + whoIt was Liv who welcomed me.
the point / o pontoThe thing / reason that ... is / wasThe reason I went back was the people.

Atenção: não coloque um "it" a mais: "What I loved was...", nunca "What I loved it was...". Depois de "the thing / the reason", use that, nunca "what": "the thing that mattered". E "What ..." leva verbo no singular: "What I loved was", não "were".

Stage 2: Put the Steps in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de montar uma frase enfática na ordem correta.

?Decide what you want to spotlight: an idea, a thing, or a person.
?Build the sentence with one verb: What I loved was..., never What I loved it was...
?Notice the one thing that mattered most: the people, the food, a single moment.
?Say it with feeling, so the listener knows what really mattered.
?Choose your structure: What ... was, or It was ... that / who.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real.

1. A friend asks what you liked best about a city. Which puts the food in the spotlight?
A "What I liked best was the food."
B "I liked the food, it was nice."
C "What I liked best were the food."
2. You want to emphasise that Liv, a person, welcomed you. Which is best?
A "It was Liv what welcomed me."
B "It was Liv who welcomed me."
C "What welcomed me was Liv, surely."
3. You explain why you keep returning to one place. Which emphasises the reason?
A "The reason I keep going back is the people."
B "I go back, because."
C "It was because of the people that."
4. A whole trip came down to one dinner. Which sentence is correct?
A "What made the whole trip was one dinner."
B "What made the whole trip it was one dinner."
C "The thing what made the trip was one dinner."

Lesson 15 — What Really Made the Trip -- Saying What Mattered Most

Film
Lost in Translation (2003) -- directed by Sofia Coppola

Two Americans, alone in a huge Tokyo hotel, form a quiet, fleeting friendship over a few days. The neon city is all around them, but the film knows exactly what the trip was really about, and it was never the sights. It was one conversation, one late night, one connection that neither of them expected. It is tonight's whole lesson turned into a film: what makes a journey is rarely the postcard, it is the person you meet. Connection to Lesson 15: the essence of the trip is a human moment, not a place.

Tip: after a scene, sum it up with a cleft sentence -- "What mattered to them was...", "It was that night that...", "The reason it stayed with them was...".
