Lesson 8 -- How Much, How Many -- Quantifiers and Countable/Uncountable

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- an episode on workforce planning and staffing data

HR and business leaders discuss headcount, staffing shortages, and how much data teams really use. You will hear quantifiers everywhere: too many vacancies, not enough staff, a lot of feedback, very little budget. Direct connection to Lesson 8: talking about quantities in HR reports.

Tip: write down 5 quantifiers you hear and mark which noun is countable (many applicants) and which is uncountable (much overtime).

Series
The Office (US) -- any episode with a staffing or budget discussion

The branch is always talking about how many people they need, how much work there is, and how little budget is left. Listen for quantifiers in real workplace talk: too many meetings, not enough time, a few clients. Connection to Lesson 8: quantities at work.

Tip: pause after a sentence and rebuild it: "There are too many..." / "There is not enough..." out loud.

YouTube
"Much, Many, A lot of, Few, Little" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on quantifiers and countable/uncountable nouns: much vs many, a few vs a little, too much vs enough, and the most common mistakes. Connection to Lesson 8: the exact grammar you use to describe workforce data.

Tip: pause and build your own sentences with applicants (countable) and feedback (uncountable).
