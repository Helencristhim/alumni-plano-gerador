Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Workforce -- all the people who work in a company (força de trabalho)
"Our workforce is growing this year."
Headcount -- the total number of employees (quadro / número de funcionários)
"Our headcount is two hundred people."
Vacancy -- an open job position (vaga em aberto)
"We have several open vacancies right now."
Applicant -- a person who applies for a job (candidato / postulante)
"We received many applicants last month."
Feedback -- comments about performance (feedback / retorno)
"We collected a lot of feedback from the team."
Data -- facts and numbers you analyze (dados)
"The data is clear: turnover is falling."
Absence -- time when someone is not at work (ausência / falta)
"There is too much absence this quarter."
Overtime -- extra hours worked beyond normal (horas extras)
"The team is doing too much overtime."
Shortage -- when there is not enough of something (escassez / falta)
"We have a shortage of engineers."
Resource -- money, people, or tools you use (recurso)
"We have plenty of resources for training."
Staff -- the group of people who work somewhere (equipe / pessoal)
"We do not have enough staff on the night shift."
Training -- teaching employees new skills (treinamento)
"There is not much training this quarter."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Murielle is preparing the workforce report. Right now she has several open vacancies, and last month the team received many applicants, which is good news. However, there is a problem: they do not have enough staff on the night shift, so the team is doing too much overtime. There is also a lot of absence this quarter. On the positive side, the team collected a lot of feedback, and the data is clear. They have plenty of resources for training, but only a little time to organize it. Her headline is simple: many applicants, not enough staff, and a shortage to fix first.

1. Why does the text say "many applicants" but "much overtime"?
A They mean different amounts of the same thing.
B Applicants are countable (many); overtime is uncountable (much).
C "Many" and "much" are always interchangeable.
2. Why is it "a little time" and not "a few time"?
A "Time" is uncountable here, so it takes "a little".
B "Time" is always countable.
C "A few" and "a little" mean the opposite.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "We do not have enough staffs on the night shift."
B "We do not have enough staff on the night shift."
C "We do not have enough of staff on the night shift."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Quantifiers (Countable vs Uncountable)

GRAMMAR

Substantivos contáveis (applicants, vacancies) usam many, a few, several, too many. Substantivos incontáveis (feedback, overtime, data) usam much, a little, too much. "A lot of" e "enough" funcionam com os dois. Perguntas: How many (contável) / How much (incontável).

MeaningCountable (applicants)Uncountable (feedback)
a large amountmany / a lot ofmuch / a lot of
a small amounta few / severala little
excess / lacktoo many / too few / not enoughtoo much / too little / not enough
question wordHow many vacancies?How much overtime?
