Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We received much applicants."

✓ RIGHT

"We received many applicants."

✗ WRONG

"We got many feedback from the team."

✓ RIGHT

"We got much feedback from the team."

✗ WRONG

"We have a few overtime this week."

✓ RIGHT

"We have a little overtime this week."

Countable nouns take many, a few, several. Uncountable nouns take much, a little. "A lot of" works with both.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Reporting workforce numbers before the review

Dialogue

The Workforce Report

L
Hi Murielle. Before the report, how many open vacancies do we have right now?
M
We have several open vacancies, about eight. The good news is we received many applicants last month.
L
That is great. But how is the current team? Do you have enough staff?
M
Honestly, we do not have enough staff at night, so the team is doing too much overtime. There is also a lot of absence.
L
I see a connection there. Do we have data on it?
M
Yes, we collected a lot of feedback and the data is clear. We have plenty of resources for training, just a little less time.
L
Good. So what is your headline for the report?
M
Many applicants, not enough staff, and too much overtime. Let's fix the shortage first.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How many vacancies and applicants does Murielle mention?
Several vacancies (about eight) and many applicants last month.
2. What is the problem with the current team?
Not enough staff at night, so too much overtime and a lot of absence.
3. What is Murielle's headline for the report?
Many applicants, not enough staff, and too much overtime; fix the shortage first.
