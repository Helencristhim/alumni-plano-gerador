Lesson 17 -- Pre-class

How Much, How Many -- Quantifiers for HR Data

Talking about amounts and numbers with quantifiers: many / a few (countable), much / a little (uncountable), a lot of / plenty of (both), and too much / too many / not enough. New words: plenty, scarce, abundant, numerous, minimal, ample, surplus, insufficient, countless, handful, bulk, sufficient.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Plenty -- more than enough (de sobra / bastante)
"We have plenty of candidates for the role."
Scarce -- not enough; hard to find (escasso)
"Skilled staff is scarce this year."
Abundant -- existing in large quantities (abundante)
"Feedback was abundant after the survey."
Numerous -- many; a large number (numerosos / diversos)
"We received numerous applications."
Minimal -- very small in amount (mínimo)
"The budget increase was minimal."
Ample -- more than enough; plenty (amplo / suficiente)
"We have ample time to prepare."
Surplus -- an extra amount left over (excedente / sobra)
"There is a surplus of applications."
Insufficient -- not enough (insuficiente)
"The staff is insufficient for the workload."
Countless -- too many to count (incontáveis)
"We answered countless questions."
Handful -- a small number (punhado / poucos)
"Only a handful of candidates applied."
Bulk -- the largest part of something (maior parte / grosso)
"The bulk of the budget goes to salaries."
Sufficient -- enough for a purpose (suficiente)
"We have sufficient data to decide."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Quantifiers (Much / Many)

GRAMMAR

Use quantifiers para falar de quantidade. Substantivos contáveis (plural): many, a few, few (many roles). Substantivos incontáveis: much, a little, little (much data). Para ambos: a lot of, plenty of. Excesso ou falta: too much / too many / not enough.

Tipo de substantivoQuantifiersExemplo
Contável (plural)many / a few / fewWe have many candidates.
Incontávelmuch / a little / littleWe have a little time.
Ambosa lot of / plenty ofWe have plenty of feedback.
Excesso / faltatoo much / too many / not enoughThere is too much overtime.

Atenção: many/a few para o que se conta (candidates); much/a little para o que não se conta (budget, time). Enough vem antes do substantivo, sem of: enough staff.
