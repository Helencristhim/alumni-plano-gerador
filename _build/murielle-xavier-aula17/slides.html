Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

We have much vacancies.

✓ RIGHT

We have many vacancies.

✗ WRONG

There is many overtime.

✓ RIGHT

There is much overtime.

✗ WRONG

We have a few budget.

✓ RIGHT

We have a little budget.

✗ WRONG

We do not have enough of staff.

✓ RIGHT

We do not have enough staff.

Use many / a few with countable nouns (candidates). Use much / a little with uncountable nouns (budget, time). Enough goes before the noun, with no of: enough staff.

Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How much budget does the team have?
Not much; they do not have much budget to fill the vacancies.
2. How much time is left before the deadline?
Only a little; a few weeks, not enough for a full search.
3. What do most of the manager comments ask for?
The bulk of them ask for more staff.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can use many and a few with countable nouns: many roles, a few candidates.
I can use much and a little with uncountable nouns: much budget, a little time.
I can use a lot of and plenty of with both countable and uncountable nouns.
I can talk about excess and lack: too much, too many, not enough.
I can use the new words: plenty, scarce, abundant, numerous, minimal, ample, surplus, insufficient, countless, handful, bulk, sufficient.
