Lesson 6 -- Finding Common Ground

Series
Suits -- any negotiation scene (Season 1)

Watch how lawyers disagree, make concessions and reach an agreement without being aggressive. Notice the softening language they use to stay polite under pressure.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three softening phrases (could, might, perhaps, that said) and one concession that you hear.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Diplomatic English: Disagreeing Politely"

A focused episode on softening your message in meetings: raising a reservation, making a concession and finding middle ground in English.

Record a 2-minute role-play: disagree with a colleague's idea, then propose a compromise using today's vocabulary.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to disagree politely in English"

A clear lesson on the modals and phrases that soften disagreement (could, might, I would say, perhaps). It directly reinforces today's diplomatic language.

After watching, write six sentences: three that raise a reservation politely and three that offer a concession.
