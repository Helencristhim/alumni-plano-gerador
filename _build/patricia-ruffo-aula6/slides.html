Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Softening language: could, might, perhaps, I would say

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"Perhaps we could split the project into two phases."

"I would say the data is still tentative."

"We might need a little more time to confirm it."

"That said, I am flexible on the budget."

Grammar Rule

Softening Language

FunctionExample
Make a suggestionPerhaps we could split it into two phases.
Soften an opinionI would say the data is still tentative.
Express possibilityWe might need more time.
Concede a pointThat said, I am flexible on the budget.

Key idea: in business English, a direct "No" sounds rude. Use could, might, perhaps and I would say to soften, and markers like That said or On the other hand to make a concession.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Perhaps we could to split the project."
"Perhaps we could split the project."

After could and might, use the base verb with no to.

Grammar Practice

Make It Softer

"Perhaps we _____could split it into two phases." (suggestion)
"I _____would say the results are still tentative." (soft opinion)
"We _____might need a little more time." (possibility)
"That _____said, I am flexible on the budget." (concession)
