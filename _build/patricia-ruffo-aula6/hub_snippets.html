Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Compromise -- an agreement where each side gives up something
"We reached a fair compromise on the deadline." (Acordo / meio-termo)
Concession -- something you agree to give up to help an agreement
"As a concession, we can extend the timeline." (Concessão)
Flexible -- willing to change or adapt to other people's needs
"I am flexible on the budget, but not on the data." (Flexível)
Reasonable -- fair, sensible and easy to accept
"Your request is perfectly reasonable." (Razoável)
Soften -- to make a message gentler and less direct
"I soften my opinion so it sounds polite." (Suavizar)
Tentative -- not final yet; still open to change
"We have a tentative plan for the launch." (Provisório)
Reservation -- a doubt or concern about a decision
"I have one reservation about the timeline." (Ressalva / reserva)
Standpoint -- the position from which you see a topic
"From a scientific standpoint, we need more data." (Ponto de vista)
Middle ground -- a solution that is acceptable to both sides
"Let us find some middle ground together." (Meio-termo)
Reach an agreement -- to finally decide on something everyone accepts
"I am sure we can reach an agreement today." (Chegar a um acordo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

In the project meeting, Patricia wants to disagree without sounding rude, so she decides to soften her message. From a scientific standpoint, she has one reservation about the timeline. Instead of saying "No," she says, "I might suggest a different approach," and "Perhaps we could split the project into two phases." She is flexible on the budget, and she offers a concession on the launch date. The marketing team thinks her plan is reasonable, even though the schedule is still tentative. Both sides look for some middle ground, and after a short discussion they finally reach an agreement. A good compromise protects the science and keeps most of the original plan.

1. Why does Patricia choose to soften her message?
A She wants to disagree without sounding rude.
B She wants to cancel the whole project.
C She agrees completely with the marketing team.
2. What does she offer the marketing team?
A She offers nothing and refuses to change.
B She is flexible on the budget and offers a concession on the launch date.
C She offers to leave the company.
3. How does the meeting end?
A Both sides leave angry, with no decision.
B They find middle ground and reach an agreement.
C They decide to start the whole project again.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Softening with modals

GRAMMAR

Em negociações, suavizamos as ideias com modais e advérbios para soar diplomático. / In negotiations, we soften ideas with modals and adverbs to sound diplomatic.

Function / FunçãoSoftening structure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Make a suggestionwe could / perhaps we could + base verb"Perhaps we could split it into two phases."
Soften an opinionI might / I would say + idea"I would say the data is still tentative."
Raise a concernI have one reservation about ..."I have one reservation about the timeline."
Concede a pointThat said, / On the other hand, ..."That said, I am flexible on the budget."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Em inglês de negócios, "No" direto soa rude. Use could, might, perhaps e would para suavizar, e marcadores como That said ou On the other hand para fazer concessões e chegar a um meio-termo.
