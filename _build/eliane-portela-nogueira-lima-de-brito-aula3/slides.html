Chapter 3

Four Futures

Going to, present continuous, will, and the formal forms of business English

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am going to open my clinic next year."

"I am meeting a supplier next month."

"The technology will change how we treat patients."

"We plan to be fully operational by 2027."

FormMessageExample
going toA plan or intention you have decidedI am going to open a clinic.
Present continuousA fixed arrangement with a time or placeI am meeting a supplier on Friday.
willA prediction or a spontaneous decisionThe market will grow.
plan to / intend to / expect toA formal business plan (register)We plan to launch in 2027.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I go to hire two doctors next year."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to hire two doctors next year."

✗ WRONG

"We will to launch the clinic in 2027."

✓ RIGHT

"We will launch the clinic in 2027."

✗ WRONG

"The plan is be operational by March."

✓ RIGHT

"The plan is to be operational by March."

Use "going to" plus the base verb for a decided plan. After "will", the verb has no "to". After "the plan is", use "to be".

Chapter 4

The Timeline

A real conversation with an investor about your plan

Dialogue

Pitching the Vision

M
So, Eliane, tell me about this clinic. What is the big vision?
E
We are building a state-of-the-art fertility clinic in São Paulo. The plan is to be fully operational by 2027.
M
Ambitious. What is your timeline for getting there?
E
Right now we are on track. Our first milestone, the lease, is already signed, and the rollout starts next spring.
M
And how are you going to fund the capital investment?
E
We have closed our seed funding, and I am meeting two more investors next month.
M
Who is your target patient?
E
Women over 35 who want cutting-edge care. And genetic testing is a natural adjacent market for us.
M
I like it. This model sounds scalable. Let us schedule a longer call.
