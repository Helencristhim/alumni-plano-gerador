Lesson 20 -- Handling Objections: Answering Doubts & Saying No Politely

Series
Shark Tank -- a tough negotiation scene

Watch an entrepreneur answer hard questions from investors. Listen for how they stay calm, reassure the room, and never give a flat no. Notice the objection words: objection, doubt, flexible, and refund.

Write down one objection an investor raises and how the entrepreneur answers it politely. Then practice handling that objection yourself, out loud.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Handling Objections in Sales"

Practical phrases for answering doubts and saying no politely: reassuring the client, softening a no, and staying flexible. Review the new words: objection, doubt, hesitate, reassure, drawback, flexible, decline, and refund.

Note one example of "I am afraid ... , but ..." and one of "Unfortunately ... , however ...". Practice each one out loud as if a client is objecting to your offer.

YouTube
"How to Handle Objections -- Say No Politely in English"

A short, clear lesson on the three parts of a polite no: reassure the client, soften the answer, and add a but with something you can do. A doubt is not a no -- it is a chance to build trust.

After watching, record yourself handling three objections: price, risk, and timing. Reassure, soften every no, offer a refund, and stay flexible.
