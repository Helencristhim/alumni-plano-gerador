Aula 20 -- Pre-class

Handling Objections -- Answering Doubts & Saying No Politely

Close the program: handle objections with calm. When a client has a doubt, first reassure ("I understand your concern"), then soften the no ("I am afraid we cannot ..., but ..."), and decline politely ("Unfortunately ..., however ..."). A doubt is not a no. New vocabulary: objection, doubt, hesitate, reassure, drawback, flexible, decline, refund.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Objection -- a reason or doubt the client gives against the offer
"The price is the most common objection." (Objeção)
Doubt -- a feeling that you are not sure
"The client has a doubt about the price." (Dúvida)
Hesitate -- to pause because you are not sure
"If the client hesitates, give more information." (Hesitar / titubear)
Reassure -- to make someone feel calm and confident
"Reassure the client: I understand your concern." (Tranquilizar / dar segurança)
Drawback -- a small disadvantage or weak point
"The only drawback is the setup time." (Desvantagem / ponto fraco)
Flexible -- able to change your plan to help
"We can be flexible on the start date." (Flexível)
Decline -- to say no, but in a polite way
"I have to decline, but I can offer something else." (Recusar / declinar)
Refund -- money returned to the buyer
"If it does not work, we give a full refund." (Reembolso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A client raises an objection: the price is too high. Rafael never argues. First, he reassures the client: "I understand your concern." Then he softens the no: "I am afraid we cannot lower the price, but I can offer a longer guarantee." The client has another doubt: "What if it does not work?" Rafael says: "Let me reassure you. You get a full refund." When the client asks for delivery in one week, Rafael has to decline: "Unfortunately we cannot, however we can be flexible and start on Monday. A small drawback, but you save time later." A doubt is not a no.

1. What does Rafael do first when the client objects?
A He reassures the client and says he understands.
B He says the client is wrong.
C He says nothing.
2. How does Rafael answer "the price is too high"?
A "I am afraid we cannot lower it, but I can offer a longer guarantee."
B "No. We cannot do that."
C "Then do not buy it."
3. Which sentence is the polite way to decline?
A "Unfortunately we cannot, however we can be flexible."
B "No. Impossible."
C "We not can do that."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Saying No Politely

GRAMMAR

Como responder a uma objeção e dizer não de forma educada. / How to answer an objection and say no politely. Um no educado tem três partes: tranquilizar, suavizar e adicionar um but.

Tool / FerramentaPattern / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Reassure / TranquilizarI understand your concernI understand your concern. That is a fair point.
Soften / SuavizarI am afraid ... , but ...I am afraid we cannot, but I can offer a guarantee.
Decline / RecusarUnfortunately ... , however ...Unfortunately we cannot, however we can be flexible.
Open up / Abrir espaçoLet me + verboLet me reassure you. A doubt is not a no.
Key point / Ponto-chaveNunca diga um No seco a um cliente. Suavize com I am afraid ou Unfortunately e sempre adicione um but com algo que você PODE fazer.
