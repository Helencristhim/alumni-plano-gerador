Chapter 3 -- Saying No Politely

The Secret

Soften the no -- and always offer something you can do

Discover the Pattern

Listen and Notice

Reassure: "I understand your concern. That is a fair point."

Soften: "I am afraid we cannot lower the price, but I can offer a guarantee."

Decline: "Unfortunately we cannot do that, however we can be flexible."

Open up: "Let me reassure you. A doubt is not a no."

Polite Language

Four Tools

ToolPatternExample
1. ReassureI understand your concernI understand your concern. That is a fair point.
2. SoftenI am afraid ... , but ...I am afraid we cannot, but I can offer a guarantee.
3. DeclineUnfortunately ... , however ...Unfortunately we cannot, however we can be flexible.
4. Open upLet me + verbLet me reassure you. A doubt is not a no.

Key idea: A polite no has three parts. First reassure the client. Then say the soft no with I am afraid or Unfortunately. Then add a but or however with something you CAN do. Never give a flat no.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"No. We cannot do that."
"I am afraid we cannot do that, but we can be flexible."

A flat No is rude in business. Soften it with I am afraid or Unfortunately, and always add a but with something you CAN do.

1. What should Rafael do first when a client raises a doubt?

Never argue. He should reassure the client and show that he understands the concern.

2. How should Rafael answer when the price is too high?

Softly, never a flat no. He says "I am afraid we cannot lower the price, but I can offer a longer guarantee."
