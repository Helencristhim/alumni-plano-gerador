Chapter 3: The Code

All Four Together

welcome · small talk · order · opinion

Grammar Discovery

One From Each Lesson

"Please come in and have a seat."

"How was your weekend?"

"I would like the grilled fish, please."

"I think the plan is good, and I agree with you."

StructureUse forExample
imperatives / directionswelcome and give directionsCome in. Have a seat. Turn left.
small talk questionsbreak the iceHow was your weekend? What is the weather like?
would like / preferorder food politelyI would like the fish. I prefer a salad.
I think / I agreegive your opinionI think it is good. I agree with you.

A whole day with a visitor uses all four: come in / have a seat to welcome, small talk questions to break the ice, I would like to order lunch, and I think / I agree for the meeting.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I am agree with you.
I agree with you.
I like the fish, please.
I would like the fish, please.

Agree is already a verb, so never say "I am agree" — just say I agree. And to order politely, use I would like, not "I like".

Chapter 4: David's Day

A Day with David

welcome · small talk · lunch · meeting

Dialogue

The Whole Day

M
Good morning, David. Welcome to São Paulo. Please come in and have a seat.
D
Thank you, Maísa. It is great to be here.
M
How was your weekend? The weather is beautiful today.
D
My weekend was great. I visited a museum, and yes, the weather is wonderful!
M
Now, let us have lunch. Here is the menu. What would you like to order?
D
I would like the grilled fish, please. It looks delicious.
M
Perfect. This afternoon we have a meeting. I think our new plan is good.
D
I agree with you, Maísa. It was a wonderful day.
