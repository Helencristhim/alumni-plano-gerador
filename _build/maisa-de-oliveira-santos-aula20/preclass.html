Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards (Review)

Vocabulary

Estas oito expressões você já aprendeu nas aulas 16 a 19. Ouça cada uma e relembre. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Come in -- a polite way to invite someone to enter
"Welcome, David! Please come in." (Entre — aula 16)
Have a seat -- a polite way to ask someone to sit down
"Have a seat. The meeting starts soon." (Sente-se — aula 16)
The weather -- the sun, rain, heat or cold outside
"The weather is warm and sunny today." (O tempo / clima — aula 17)
The weekend -- Saturday and Sunday, your days off
"How was your weekend?" (O fim de semana — aula 17)
The menu -- the list of food and drinks at a restaurant
"Here is the menu. Take your time." (O cardápio — aula 18)
To order -- to ask for the food you want at a restaurant
"I am ready to order." (Pedir — aula 18)
I think -- the words you use to give your opinion
"I think our plan is good." (Eu acho — aula 19)
I agree -- to say you have the same opinion
"I agree with you." (Eu concordo — aula 19)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto de revisão — ele junta as quatro estruturas das aulas 16 a 19. Depois responda as perguntas.

It is David's big day in São Paulo. In the morning, Maísa welcomes him. "Come in, David. Please have a seat," she says. Then they make small talk. "How was your weekend? The weather is beautiful today," Maísa says. At noon, they go to a restaurant. The waiter brings the menu, and they are ready to order. "I would like the grilled fish, please," David says. In the afternoon, they have a meeting. "I think our plan is good," says Maísa. "I agree with you," David answers. It was a wonderful day from start to finish.

1. Como a Maísa recebe o David? / How does Maísa welcome David?
A "Please come in and have a seat."
B "Please to come in and have a seat."
C "Please you come in and sit."
2. O que eles conversam antes do almoço? / What do they talk about before lunch?
A "What is your weekend?"
B "How was your weekend? The weather is beautiful."
C "How your weekend is?"
3. Como o David pede no restaurante? / How does David order at the restaurant?
A "I am like the grilled fish, please."
B "I would to like the grilled fish."
C "I would like the grilled fish, please."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Four Structures Together

GRAMMAR

Um dia com um visitante usa as quatro estruturas das aulas 16 a 19: imperativos / direções para receber, perguntas de small talk para quebrar o gelo, would like / prefer para pedir no almoço, e I think / I agree para dar opinião na reunião. / A whole visit day uses all four structures together.

Estrutura / StructureUso / UseExemplo / Example
imperatives / directionsreceber e dar direções / welcomeCome in. Have a seat. Turn left.
small talk questionsquebrar o gelo / break the iceHow was your weekend? What is the weather like?
would like / preferpedir no almoço / order politelyI would like the fish. I prefer a salad.
I think / I agree / I disagreedar opinião / give your opinionI think it is good. I agree with you.

Atenção aos erros clássicos: agree já é um verbo, então diga I agree (nunca "I am agree"); para pedir com educação use I would like (não "I like"); imperativos vão sem "to": come in (nunca "to come in").
