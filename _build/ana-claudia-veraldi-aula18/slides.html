Language Focus

Name the Move

Common Mistake

Freeze vs Fluent

✗ FREEZE

"Um... eh... I don't know... maybe... it's hard to say..."

✓ FLUENT

"That's a great question — let me think for a moment."

✗ TRAIL OFF

"So the thing is that the fix... yeah... it's kind of..."

✓ FLUENT

"In other words, the fix only affects the login screen."

Do not fill the pause by rambling or letting your voice trail off. Use a purposeful phrase to stall or reword, then continue.

Dialogue

The Live Call with Greg

G
Ana, thanks for the update. Quick question — that bug from the other team, is it going to block our release on Thursday?
A
That is a great question, let me think for a moment. There are two parts to this. First, that bug is not ours, and second, it does not touch our release path.
G
Okay, but what about the old reporting system? We had so many issues with that last year...
A
I do not want to digress, so let me come back to the release. The old system is separate — can I come back to it at the end?
G
Fair enough. So, in plain terms, how risky is this fix?
A
In other words, is it safe to ship? Yes. The change only affects the login screen. It is a small, low-risk fix.
G
Great. And remind me of the date — you said Tuesday earlier, right?
A
Sorry, let me rephrase that. I said Tuesday, but the fix actually ships on Thursday. I will send you a written summary to confirm.
Did You Catch It?

About the Call

1. What is Greg's first concern about the release?
Whether the bug from the other team will block the Thursday release.
2. Which off-topic subject does Greg bring up, that Ana postpones to the end?
The old reporting system from last year.
3. What does Greg ask Ana to confirm at the end, and how does she fix it?
The release date. She backtracks and corrects Tuesday to Thursday, and offers a written summary.

Listening 2

Karen's Coaching

A tip from your team lead. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does Karen say Ana did really well on the call?
She held the floor -- when the call went off-script, she did not freeze or ramble; she bought a second with a simple phrase.
2. What is Karen's tip for next time?
