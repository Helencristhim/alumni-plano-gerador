Lesson 18 -- Speaking Without a Script: Holding the Floor and Staying Fluent

Speaking
"That's a Good Question..." -- How to Buy Time While Speaking (Go Natural English)

A short, practical lesson on the exact move Ana uses on her call: stalling with a natural phrase to buy a second before you answer, instead of freezing or rambling. Perfect for the moment a question goes off-script.

Tip: pick two stalling phrases from the video and say them out loud until they feel automatic, like "that's a great question, let me think for a moment".

Communication
Think Fast, Talk Smart: Communication Techniques (Stanford GSB)

A widely shared talk by Matt Abrahams on speaking spontaneously and staying calm when you have to answer without preparation -- the whole skill of this class. He explains how to handle anxiety, signpost your ideas, and improvise a structured answer on the spot.

Tip: notice his advice on using simple structures (like "what, so what, now what") to signpost. Try one on your next surprise question.

TED Talk
How to Speak So That People Want to Listen (Julian Treasure, TED)

A classic TED talk on holding the floor and speaking with control: pace, silence, and clarity. It shows why pausing on purpose -- instead of filling every gap with "um" -- makes you sound more fluent and confident, exactly what Ana does when she stalls to buy time.

Tip: after watching, record one minute of yourself explaining a QA result, using a deliberate pause instead of a filler word.
