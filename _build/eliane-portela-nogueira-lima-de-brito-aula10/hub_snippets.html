Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words founders use to read a market. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que fundadores usam para ler o mercado. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Incumbent -- the established leader that dominates a market / o líder estabelecido do mercado
"The incumbent still holds the biggest share of the sector."
Market share -- the percentage of a market a company controls / fatia de mercado
"They gained market share by moving faster than the leader."
Niche -- a small, specialized part of a market / nicho de mercado
"Our clinic found a niche the big group ignored."
Margin -- the profit left after costs / margem de lucro
"Their margins are thin because their overhead is high."
Overhead -- the ongoing running costs of a business / custos fixos de operação
"High overhead is why the leader moves so slowly."
Competitive edge -- the advantage that makes a company hard to beat / vantagem competitiva
"Their competitive edge is technology that cuts the cost per cycle."
Revenue stream -- a distinct source of regular income / fonte de receita
"Data and software are a new revenue stream for the sector."
Leverage -- to use what you have for maximum value / alavancar, tirar o máximo de
"Any group that cannot leverage technology will fall behind."
Benchmark -- a standard used to compare performance / referência, parâmetro
"I compare their numbers against an industry benchmark."
Portfolio -- the full range of products or services / portfólio de serviços
"Study the portfolio of services that made the leaders grow."
Due diligence -- the careful research done before a deal / análise prévia de um negócio
"I do the due diligence that every serious founder does."
Valuation -- the estimated financial worth of a company / avaliação de valor da empresa
"The start-up reported a valuation that surprised its investors."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is studying the companies that compete in her market. The largest of them, which analysts call the incumbent, holds the biggest market share, but it moves slowly. The founder who interests her most runs a start-up whose valuation jumped last year, because of a competitive edge that lowers the cost of each cycle. Notice the difference: "the founder who interests her" has no commas, because it tells us which founder -- that is a defining clause. But "the incumbent, which analysts call the leader," has commas, because it only adds extra information -- that is a non-defining clause, and it never uses "that".

1. In "the companies that compete in her market", is the clause defining or non-defining?
A Defining -- no commas, it tells us which companies.
B Non-defining -- it just adds extra information.
C It is not a relative clause at all.
2. Which relative pronoun shows possession (the start-up's valuation)?
A which
B whose
C who
3. Why does "the incumbent, which analysts call the leader," take commas?
A Because every relative clause needs commas.
B Because it only adds extra information -- it is non-defining.
C Because "which" is always followed by a comma.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Relative clauses add information about a noun. Defining clauses are essential and take no commas; non-defining clauses add extra detail and take commas -- and never use "that". / Relative clauses dão informação sobre um substantivo. As defining são essenciais e sem vírgula; as non-defining dão detalhe extra, levam vírgula e nunca usam "that".

Type / TipoPronoun / PronomeExample / Exemplo
Defining -- peoplewho / thatThe founder who funded us.
Defining -- thingswhich / thatThe clinic that she builds.
PossessionwhoseThe supplier whose terms are fair.
Non-defining -- extrawho / which (never that)The market, which is growing, ...

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each professional situation.

You want to add extra information about your clinic (it opens next year). Which is correct?
A "Our clinic that opens next year is small."
B "Our clinic, which opens next year, is small."
C "Our clinic, that opens next year, is small."
You want to describe the person who leads a company. Which pronoun is best?
A "The CEO which runs the incumbent."
B "The CEO who runs the incumbent."
C "The CEO whose runs the incumbent."

A "The incumbent has high overhead, so it moves slowly."
B "The incumbent has high revenue stream, so it moves slowly."
C "The incumbent has high niche, so it moves slowly."
