Chapter 4: Relative Clauses

One Sentence, More Detail

who · whose · which · that

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The founder who raised the funding is my mentor."

"The clinic that she is building has a clear niche."

"Our supplier, whose terms are fair, delivers on time."

"The market, which is growing fast, rewards focus."

"A group that cannot leverage technology will lose share."

TypePronounCommas?Example
Defining (people)who / thatNoThe founder who raised it...
Defining (things)which / thatNoThe clinic that she builds...
PossessionwhoseDependsThe supplier whose terms...
Non-defining (extra)who / which (never that)YesThe market, which is growing, ...

Defining = essential, no commas. Non-defining = extra information, commas, and never "that".

Practice

Choose the Pronoun

Say it out loud, then tap to check

"The investor ___who funded us sits on the board." (person, defining)
"The competitive edge ___that they have is technology." (thing, defining)
"The start-up, ___whose valuation jumped, raised again." (possession)
"Our clinic, ___which opens next year, is small." (thing, non-defining)
"A group ___that ignores the niche loses share." (thing, defining)
Language Focus

Defining or Non-defining?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Our clinic, that opens next year, is small.
Our clinic, which opens next year, is small.
The investor which funded us is on the board.
The investor who funded us is on the board.
The market that it is growing rewards focus.
The market that is growing rewards focus.

Three rules in one slide: never use "that" in a non-defining clause, use "who" for people, and drop the extra subject after the relative pronoun.

Dialogue

The Analyst Call

P
Eliane, I read the profile you sent. The company that worries you most is the incumbent, right?
E
It was, Paul. But the incumbent, which holds the biggest market share, has high overhead and thin margins, so it moves slowly.
P
Exactly. So the company that should interest you is the start-up whose valuation jumped last year.
E
Yes. Their competitive edge is technology that lowers the cost per cycle, and they opened a revenue stream — data and software — that no one else offers.
P
Good. And any group that cannot leverage that kind of technology will lose ground. So where does your clinic fit?
E
In a niche the big group ignores. The clinic that I am building does not need the most patients — it needs a margin it can sustain.
P
Smart. Before you sign with anyone, do the due diligence that protects you: compare their numbers against a real benchmark.
E
I will. And I will study the portfolio of services that made the leaders grow, so I copy what works and skip what does not.
P
That is a founder who reads her market, not just her medicine. The clinic that wins is the one that knows exactly where it competes.
