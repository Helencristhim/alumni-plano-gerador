Chapter 3: The Will Code

Will vs Won't

Prediction · Promise · Quick decision

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I think it will rain this afternoon."

"I promise I will call you tonight."

"That bag looks heavy. I'll help you."

"Don't worry, I won't forget your birthday."

Which sentence is a prediction or promise, and which is a decision made right now?

Use of willMeaningExample
Predictionwhat you think happensIt will rain later.
Promisea firm commitmentI will call you tonight.
Quick decisiondecided at this momentI'll help you now.
Formwill / won't + base verb (no to, no -s). Speech: I'll, you'll, she'll. Negative: won't.

Remember: going to is a plan you decided before; will is a prediction, a promise, or a decision made right now.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I will to call you tonight."
"I will call you tonight."
"I think it will raining tomorrow."
"I think it will rain tomorrow."
"She will calls you later."
"She will call you later."

After will, always use the base verb: no to, no -ing, and no -s.

Dialogue

Will It Be a Nice Day?

B
Hi Ana Paula! Did you check the weather forecast for our picnic on Saturday? I wonder if it will be a nice day.
A
I checked this morning. They say it will be warm and sunny, so I'm certain the picnic will be great.
B
That's a relief. I doubt many people will come if it rains, so this is good news.
A
Exactly. I expect at least ten friends. If you need help with the food, I'll bring a big salad — no problem.
B
Really? That's so kind. Thank you! I promise I will bring the drinks and the music.
A
Perfect. Oh, one warning: I think Chris will be late again, as always. But don't worry, it will be a wonderful afternoon.
