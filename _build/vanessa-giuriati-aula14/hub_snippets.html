Aula 14 -- Pre-class

Talking About Past Experiences

Tell stories that connect: sequence a narrative with time markers (a few years ago, in 2019, at first, then, suddenly, in the end) and keep the actions in the past simple. Choose one moment that matters. Key words: journey, moment, suddenly, eventually, unforgettable, turn out, look back, end up.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Journey -- a trip or experience, seen as a whole from start to end (jornada/viagem)
"It was a long journey, but worth it."
Moment -- a short point in time that feels special or important (momento/instante)
"That moment changed everything."
Suddenly -- quickly and unexpectedly, with no warning (de repente)
"Suddenly, it started to rain."
Eventually -- in the end, after a period of time or effort (por fim/eventualmente)
"Eventually, we found the right place."
Unforgettable -- impossible to forget, because it was so special (inesquecível)
"It was an unforgettable evening."
Turn out -- to happen or end in a certain way, often a surprise (acabar sendo/revelar-se)
"The trip turned out better than we thought."
Look back -- to think about something in the past, often fondly (relembrar/olhar para trás)
"When I look back, I smile."
End up -- to finish in a place or situation, often without planning it (acabar/terminar em)
"We ended up in a tiny village."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A few years ago, in 2019, Marina went on a journey to the south of Italy. At first, nothing went as planned. She missed her train, and then it started to rain, so she ended up in a tiny village she had never heard of. She was upset for an hour. But suddenly, an old man invited her into his home, and his family cooked dinner. That one moment changed the whole trip. In the end, the day turned out to be unforgettable. When she looks back now, she does not remember the famous sights — she remembers that evening. As she says, the best moment is almost never the one you planned.

1. What went wrong at first on Marina's journey?
A She arrived early and everything was perfect.
B She missed her train, it rained, and she ended up in a tiny village.
C She forgot her passport at home.
2. What does she remember when she looks back?
A The famous sights and museums.
B That one evening with the old man's family.
C The train she missed.
3. Which sentence keeps the story correctly in the past simple?
A "Then it starts to rain and we go to a village."
B "Then it started to rain and we went to a village."
C "Then it rain and we goed to a village."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple for Storytelling

GRAMMAR

Como sequenciar uma história no passado. / How to sequence a story in the past.

Time markerEnglish (past simple)Português
A few years ago / in 2019A few years ago, I traveled to Italy.Anos atrás... (cena)
At first / thenAt first nothing worked; then it changed.No começo... depois...
In the end / eventuallyIn the end, it turned out great.No final... (resultado)
TipYou already know the past simple (Lesson 4). Here, use it to sequence a story and build it around one moment.
⚠ CarefulWatch irregular verbs: go → went, eat → ate, take → took, see → saw (not "goed", "eated"). Say "in the end" for the result, not "at the end".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Think of a past experience you will never forget — a trip, a day, an evening. Tell the story out loud: set the scene with a time marker (a few years ago, in 2019, when I was younger), say what happened at first, the one moment that mattered, and how it turned out in the end. Use the past simple and at least three time markers. Speak slowly and take your time.
