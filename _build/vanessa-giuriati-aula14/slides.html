Chapter 3

The Sequence

Past simple, in the right order

Grammar Discovery

Telling It in the Right Order

"A few years ago, I traveled to Italy."

"At first, nothing went as planned."

"Then we ended up in a small village."

"In the end, it turned out unforgettable."

Time markerUseExample (past simple)
A few years ago / in 2019Set the scene (when)A few years ago, I traveled to Italy.
At firstThe beginning / first reactionAt first, nothing went as planned.
Then / suddenlyThe next step / the surpriseThen it started to rain. Suddenly, a stranger helped us.
In the end / eventuallyThe result: "In the end, the trip turned out unforgettable." The verbs stay past simple (traveled, went, started, turned).
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Last year we go to Italy."

✓ RIGHT

"Last year we went to Italy."

✗ WRONG

"At the end, it turned out great."

✓ RIGHT

"In the end, it turned out great."

✗ WRONG

"Then we eated and goed home."

✓ RIGHT

"Then we ate and went home."

In a story, keep the actions in the past simple (went, started, ate). Say "in the end" for the result. And watch irregular verbs: go → went, eat → ate, not "goed" or "eated".

Dialogue

A Trip She'll Never Forget

D
You look happy. What's the best trip you've ever taken?
V
Oh, easy. A few years ago, in 2019, I went to Italy. It was an unforgettable journey.
D
Italy sounds perfect. So everything went smoothly?
V
Not at all! At first we missed our train, and then it rained, so we ended up in a tiny village.
D
That sounds stressful. So how did it become the best trip?
V
Suddenly, an old man invited us into his home for dinner. That one moment changed the whole trip.
D
Wow. So it all turned out well in the end.
V
It did. When I look back, that evening is all I remember.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. When and where did her journey happen?
A few years ago, in 2019, she went to Italy.
2. What went wrong at first?
They missed their train, it rained, and they ended up in a tiny village.
3. What was the one moment that changed the trip?
An old man suddenly invited them into his home for dinner.

Listening

Why Stories Connect

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. What does the speaker say people connect through?
Not facts, but moments — a stranger turning a bad day into an unforgettable one stays with you.
2. What is the simple trick he gives?
Don't list everything; choose one moment that mattered and build the story around it.
3. How should you take the listener through the story?
Set the scene, then go step by step — at first this, then that, and in the end something changed.
