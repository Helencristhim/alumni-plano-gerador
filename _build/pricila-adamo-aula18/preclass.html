Stage 1.1: Reading People Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you make sense of confusing social moments abroad.

To misread a situation -- interpretar mal uma situacao
"I completely misread the situation -- he wasn't angry at all."
A faux pas -- uma gafe social
"Using first names too soon can be a faux pas in some countries."
To come across as -- passar a impressao de / parecer
"I didn't mean to, but I came across as cold."
To take offence -- ofender-se / sentir-se ofendido
"Please don't take offence -- it's just a local custom."
To mean no harm -- nao ter ma intencao
"He meant no harm -- it was just a misunderstanding."
A culture clash -- um choque cultural
"It was a small culture clash, nothing more."
To give the benefit of the doubt -- dar o beneficio da duvida
"I gave her the benefit of the doubt -- she was probably just shy."
To jump to conclusions -- tirar conclusoes precipitadas
"Don't jump to conclusions -- ask her what she meant first."
Body language -- linguagem corporal
"His body language told me he was uncomfortable."
To lose face -- perder a reputacao / passar vergonha em publico
"He didn't want to lose face in front of his colleagues."
An awkward silence -- um silencio constrangedor
"There was an awkward silence after my comment."
To smooth things over -- acalmar / contornar uma situacao tensa
"A smile and an apology smoothed things over."
To read between the lines -- entender as entrelinhas
"Read between the lines -- she might just be tired."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

In Japan, Pricila handed her business card with one hand and the man went quiet. "I must have done something wrong," she thought. But a friend explained the custom. "You can't have known the rule -- it's tiny," he said. "He might have just been surprised." Pricila realized she had jumped to conclusions. He can't have meant to be unkind, and she certainly didn't mean any harm. It was a small culture clash, and a smile soon smoothed it over.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Modals of Deduction

Grammar

Os modais de deducao no passado mostram o quanto voce tem CERTEZA sobre o passado: must have + particio = quase certo que SIM ("She must have misunderstood me"); can't have + particio = quase certo que NAO ("They can't have meant it" -- NUNCA "mustn't have"); might/could have + particio = era possivel. A forma nao muda para he/she/it (NAO "must has"). E "must have", nao "must of". / Past modals of deduction show how sure you are about the past: must have + past participle = almost sure it was true; can't have + past participle = almost sure it was NOT true (never "mustn't have"); might/could have = it was possible. The form never changes for he/she/it, and it is "must have", not "must of".

Funcao / FunctionEnglishPortugues
Almost sure it WAS trueShe must have misunderstood me.Ela deve ter me entendido mal.
It was possibleHe might have been confused by the custom.Ele pode ter ficado confuso com o costume.
Almost sure it was NOT trueThey can't have meant it rudely.Eles nao podem ter feito por grosseria.
Passive deductionThe card must have been offered with one hand.O cartao deve ter sido oferecido com uma mao so.
Common mistakeNOT "must of" / NOT "mustn't have"E "must have"; para certeza negativa, use "can't have".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Think of a confusing moment with a stranger -- abroad or at home. Deduce what they must have meant, what they might have felt, and why they can't have meant any harm. Finish with how you would give them the benefit of the doubt. Take your time.
