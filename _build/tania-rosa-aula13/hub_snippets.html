Stage 1.1: Planning Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you plan your next trip.

Plan -- plano
"I have a plan for our weekend trip."
To book -- reservar
"I am going to book the flights tonight."
Budget -- orçamento
"My budget for this trip is small, but enough."
Deadline -- prazo
"The deadline to book the cheap tickets is Friday."
To pack -- fazer as malas
"I always pack the night before."
Packing list -- lista de itens para a mala
"My packing list has shoes, a charger and sunscreen."
To save up -- economizar (juntar dinheiro)
"I am going to save up for a trip to Lisbon."
To look forward to -- ansiar por / esperar com vontade
"I look forward to my holidays every year."
Can't wait -- mal posso esperar
"I can't wait to see the beach!"
Brochure -- folheto
"The brochure shows three nice day tours."

Grammar

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future: Going to vs Will

Grammar

Use "going to" + verbo para um plano que você já decidiu antes (I am going to book a hotel). Use "will" + verbo para uma decisão no momento ou uma promessa (OK, I will take a taxi). Lembre: going to precisa do verbo to be (am / is / are); will nunca leva "to" depois. / Use "going to" + verb for a plan you already decided. Use "will" + verb for a decision in the moment or a promise. Remember: "going to" needs the verb to be (am / is / are); "will" never takes "to" after it.

Função / FunctionEnglishPortuguês
Plan (going to)I am going to book a hotel.Eu vou reservar um hotel.
Plan (going to)We are going to save up money.Nós vamos economizar dinheiro.
Quick decision (will)OK, I will take a taxi.Ok, eu vou pegar um táxi.
Promise (will)Don't worry, I will help you.Não se preocupe, eu vou te ajudar.
Negative (going to)I am not going to forget anything.Eu não vou esquecer nada.

Speaking

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A friend asks about your decided trip plan. How do you answer?
A "I am going to book the flights next week."
B "I will going to book."
C "I book yesterday."
Your friend's bag is heavy. You decide to help right now.
A "I am going to carry it." (you decided long ago?)
B "Don't worry, I will carry it."
C "I carried it."
The cheap tickets close on Friday. What do you say?
A "The budget is on Friday."
B "The deadline is Friday, so I am going to book today."
C "I pack on Friday only."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.
