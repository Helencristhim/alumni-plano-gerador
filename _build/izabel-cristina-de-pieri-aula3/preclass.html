Aula 03 -- Pre-class

What Happened This Week -- Reporting Past Actions

Reporting last week in the Thursday meeting. Key words: close a deal, launch, hire, deliver, resolve, figure out, follow up on, roll out, revenue, quarter, shipment, outcome. Structures: simple past (regular & irregular), negatives with did not + base verb, and questions with did.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Close a deal -- to reach a final agreement on a sale or contract (fechar um negócio)
"Last week we closed a big deal with a new client."
Launch -- to start or introduce a new product or project (lançar)
"We launched the new product line last month."
Hire -- to give someone a job in your company (contratar)
"We hired two engineers last quarter."
Deliver -- to finish and hand over work on time (entregar)
"The team delivered the report ahead of schedule."
Resolve -- to find a solution to a problem (resolver)
"We resolved the customs issue last week."
Figure out -- to find the answer to a problem after thinking (decifrar / descobrir)
"We figured out the tax calculation yesterday."
Follow up on -- to check the progress of something later (dar seguimento a)
"I followed up on the invoice this morning."
Roll out -- to introduce something new step by step (implantar aos poucos)
"We rolled out the new tracking portal last month."
Revenue -- the money a business earns from sales (receita)
"Revenue grew by nine percent last quarter."
Quarter -- a three-month period in the business year (trimestre)
"It was a strong quarter for the company."
Shipment -- a load of goods sent to a customer (remessa / carga)
"The shipment arrived on Monday, right on time."
Outcome -- the final result of an action or meeting (resultado)
"The outcome of the meeting was very positive."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

It was a busy week at AVRT. On Monday, Izabel closed the deal with the new supplier, and the first shipment arrived right on time. On Wednesday, the team resolved the customs issue, and Izabel followed up with the logistics team herself. She sent the invoices by email and delivered the cash-flow report by hand before the partners left on Friday. There was one open item: the team did not finish the tax restatement, because the new rules changed halfway through the quarter. At the Thursday call, a partner asked, "When did the shipment arrive?" and Izabel answered with a clear, confident recap.

1. Why do we write "closed", "arrived", and "resolved" with -ed?
A Because they are happening right now.
B Because they are regular verbs in the simple past.
C Because the subject is plural.
2. Look at "did not finish". Why is it "finish" and not "finished"?
A After "did not / didn't" we use the base verb, with no -ed.
B Because "finish" is always in the base form.
C Because the sentence is in the present.
3. The verbs "sent" and "left" do not end in -ed. Why?
A They are spelled wrong in the text.
B They are irregular verbs with a special past form (send→sent, leave→left).
C They are in the present simple.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Simple Past (Regular & Irregular)

GRAMMAR

Reporting finished actions in the past (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Regular verb + -edAção terminada no passado: a maioria leva -edWe closed the deal.
Irregular (forma própria)Alguns verbos mudam: send→sent, leave→left, grow→grewI sent the report.
did not + baseNegativa: they/we/he did not (didn't) + verbo base (SEM -ed)We did not finish it.
(WH) + did + subject + basePergunta SEMPRE com did + sujeito + verbo baseWhat did you do?
Time markerslast week, yesterday, on Monday, two days ago, last quarter (tempo terminado — sem "since")
