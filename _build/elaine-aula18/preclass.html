Taking Photos -- Asking Strangers

Ask a stranger for a photo: photo, stranger, view, background, selfie, smile, closer, permission. Structures: "Excuse me, could you...?", "Would you mind taking...?" and "Can you...?"

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Photo -- a picture you take with a camera
"Could you take a photo of me?" (Foto)
Stranger -- a person you do not know
"I asked a stranger to help." (Estranho / desconhecido)
View -- what you can see from a place
"The view is beautiful here." (Vista)
Background -- what is behind you in a picture
"Get the landmark in the background." (Fundo)
Selfie -- a photo you take of yourself
"Let's take a selfie together." (Selfie / autorretrato)
Smile -- what you do with your mouth for a photo
"Smile! One, two, three." (Sorrir / sorriso)
Closer -- near, with less distance
"Can you take a step closer?" (Mais perto)
Permission -- when you say "yes, you can"
"Ask for permission before a photo." (Permissao)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests to Strangers

GRAMMAR

Pedir educadamente a um estranho. / Asking a stranger politely.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Excuse me, ...Chamar a atencao / Get attentionExcuse me, could you help?
Could you + verb?Pedido educado / A polite requestCould you take a photo?
Would you mind + verb-ing?Pedido muito educado / Very politeWould you mind taking a photo?

Lembre: comece com Excuse me. Use Could you + verbo. Depois de mind use verbo + ing (taking, not "to take"). / Start with "Excuse me". Use "Could you + verb". After "mind" use verb + ing.
