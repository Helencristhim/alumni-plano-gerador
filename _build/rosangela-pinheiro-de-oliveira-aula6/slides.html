Chapter 3: The Code

The Polite Ask

I would like · would you like · cheaper than

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I would like the grilled fish, please."

"We would like a table for two."

"Would you like still or sparkling water?"

"I would like to pay, please."

The green words never change: not "he would likes", never. And after them: a thing, or to + verb. Which one is in each line?

FormUseExample
I / we would like + a thingAsking for something, politely. Same words for everybody: I, you, he, she, we.I would like the fish. · We would like the bill.
I would like to + verbWith a verb, "to" comes in the middle. The verb never takes -ing here.I would like to pay. · I would like to see the menu.
Question
Would you like + ...?		"Would" jumps to the front. This is the question the waiter asks you.Would you like a dessert? · Would you like to order?
Short answerAnswer with "Yes, please" or "No, thank you" — never a naked "yes"."Would you like a coffee?" — "Yes, please." / "No, thank you."
would like vs wantSame meaning. "I want" is for family and friends; "I would like" is for a waiter, a hotel, a stranger."I want the fish" → direct. "I would like the fish, please" → polite.

In a restaurant in Europe, would like is the normal way to ask. It costs one extra word, and it changes everything.

The Contrast

Want vs Would Like

Same meaning — two different rooms. One is for people who love you. The other is for people who are working.

Want — direct, at home

"I want the fish."
"Do you want a coffee?"

Would like — polite, in Porto

"I would like the fish, please."
"Would you like a coffee?"

Now order your dinner twice: once with want, once with would like. Hear the difference.

The Second Code

Comparing Two Dishes

Short word: add -er. Long word: put more in front. And than joins the two.

cheap → cheaper than (short: +er)
light → lighter than
fresh → fresher than
big → bigger than (double the last letter)
expensive → more expensive than (long: more)
good → better than (no rule — learn it)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I would like to the grilled fish, please."
"I would like the grilled fish, please."
"The fish is more cheap than the meat."
"The fish is cheaper than the meat."
"The tart is lighter that the cake."
"The tart is lighter than the cake."

to only before a verb: "I would like to pay", but "I would like the fish". Short word takes -er, not "more". And the little word that compares is than, never "that".

Listening

The Specials Tonight

Nuno, your waiter, before you order. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does Nuno recommend for the appetizer, and why?
The tomato soup. It is very light, and it is smaller than the cheese plate, so you still have room for the main course.
2. He compares the grilled fish and the beef. What does he say about each one?
The fish is the special tonight: fresh from the sea this morning, and cheaper than the beef. The beef is bigger and the most popular dish, but it is heavier.
3. What does Nuno want to know before you order, and why is it important?
If you are allergic to something. He says to tell him now, before you order: the kitchen can change the dish for you.
Listening 2

She Used to Wait

Marta is at the next table, with her sister. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What did Marta use to do in restaurants twenty years ago?
She used to wait for her husband, because he always ordered for her. She used to say "the same, please", and she never opened the menu.
2. What does she say about the tip in Portugal?
It is smaller than in the United States. You leave five or ten percent, and it is enough. Nobody is angry.
3. What did she use to eat, and what does she love now?
She used to eat only meat and never ate fish. Now she loves it: the fish here is fresher than at home, and cheaper than the meat.

R

Comprehension

About the Waiter

1. What does Nuno recommend, and what two reasons does he give?
The grilled fish. It is fresher than the meat, and it is cheaper. It is also the special of the night.
2. What are the two desserts he offers?
The chocolate cake and the orange tart.
3. What does he do after he hears about the nuts?
He thanks her for telling him, he asks the kitchen, and he comes back to say there are no nuts and it is safe.
4. When Nuno asks if she is ready, what other option does he offer her?
A few more minutes with the menu. He says: "Are you ready to order, or would you like a few more minutes?"
