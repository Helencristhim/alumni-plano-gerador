Salt Fat Acid Heat — Episode 1: "Fat" (Italy)

Start with episode 1, and you are in Italy: the olive groves of Liguria, a family making pesto by hand, the cheese caves of Parmigiano, the old balsamic vinegar of Modena. Samin Nosrat is American, she speaks slowly, and she laughs at every table she sits at. She eats the food and she says why it is good — which is exactly what your waiter does when you ask him what he recommends.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Every time Samin compares two things — "this one is fresher", "the old one is better" — stop and say the sentence out loud. Then say one about your menu: "The fish is cheaper than the beef."

Speak English Now — Episode 320: How to Order Food in a Restaurant (A2-B1)

Georgiana takes you through the whole meal, slowly and with no grammar talk: how a menu is divided into appetizers, main dishes and desserts; what to say when you do not know a dish; and how the tip works. Every word of this lesson is in this episode. The full text is free on the same page, under the audio player.

Tip: listen once with your eyes closed, and do not read — your ear before your eyes. Then listen again with the free text open. After that, close everything and order your dinner out loud: "I would like the fish, please. What do you recommend?"

Rick Steves' Europe — European Travel Skills: Eating and Drinking (free, no subscription)

This is the lesson of tonight, but from the other side of the table. Rick Steves explains how a European menu really works: why the restaurant full of locals is the good one, how to read the courses, what the waiter expects you to ask — and why the tip in Europe is much smaller than in the United States. He is American, he speaks slowly, and he never hurries.

Tip: watch with English subtitles, and stop the video every time he compares two places or two dishes. Say his sentence out loud, then say one of yours: "The tart is lighter than the cake."
