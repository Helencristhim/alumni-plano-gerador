Chapter 3: The Code

Before, or Now?

I am going to order · I'll have the fish

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The bag is heavy. "I'll carry it for you."

The waiter is here. "I'll have the fish, please."

Don't worry. "I won't be late. I promise."

A question for you. "Will you join us for dinner?"

UseFormExample
Offer / decision nowwill + base verbI will help → I'll help
Promise (negative)won't + base verbI won't be late
Request / questionWill + you + base verb?Will you join us?
A plan (decided before)going to + base verbI am going to present tomorrow

One question decides: decided before, or decided now? For a plan you already have, use going to. For a decision, an offer, or a promise at the moment of speaking, use will. After will, always use the base verb (will help, not will to help, not will helps).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I will to help you with the bags."
"I will help you with the bags."
"The rector no will be late."
"The rector won't be late."

After will, use the base verb with no "to" and no "-s": I will help, she will help. The negative is won't (will not), never "no will".

Chapter 4: The Official Dinner

A Seat Next to the Rector

"We have a small request for you..."

Dialogue

Offers and a Request at the Dinner

R
Good evening. I am Professor Lang, the rector of the university here. Welcome. May I sit next to you?
MC
Of course, Professor Lang. Please, sit down. I'll move my bag for you.
R
Thank you, that is very kind. The waiter is coming. Would you like water or wine?
MC
I'll have some water, please. Thank you.
R
Tell me, are you going to present at the conference tomorrow?
MC
Yes, I am going to present in the morning. It is on the agenda already.
R
Wonderful. We have a small request. Our speaker for Friday is not available. Will you say a few words?
MC
That is a big request. It depends on the schedule, but I'll do my best. I won't take much of your time.
R
Short and clear is always better. I think it will be a great session.
MC
I agree. I'll prepare a short talk about leadership immediately. I promise.

Key Expressions

Agree or Disagree

Two phrases to react to a proposal at a round table.

I agree. I think it will be a great idea.

"I agree" = you have the same opinion. A confident, simple yes.

I disagree. In my opinion, it depends on the time.

"I disagree" = you have a different opinion. Polite and clear, not rude.

Artifact

Plan vs Moment

THE PLAN
decided before · going to

I am going to present in the morning.

I am going to travel on Monday.

We are going to meet the rector.

I am going to send the agenda.

THE MOMENT
decided now · will

I'll have the water, please.

I'll handle it for you.

I won't be late, I promise.

I'll reply immediately.

Read the left with "going to" (the plan), and the right with "I'll" (the moment).

Chapter 6: Your Turn

At the Dinner Table

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play

Offers at the Table

Scenario: The rector offers you a seat and a drink at the dinner. Make quick decisions and small offers with "I'll...". Use will, not going to.

Keyword chips:

I'll move my bag. I'll have... Thank you.
Role-Play

The Request, and Your Promise

Scenario: The rector says the Friday speaker is not available and asks you to say a few words. React: give a condition, make a promise, and agree. If you don't understand, say so — don't stay silent.

Keywords:

It depends on... I'll do my best. I won't take much time. I agree. Could you repeat, please?
Free Practice

The Whole Dinner, on Your Own

Scenario: Have the whole dinner: take the offer of a drink (I'll...), answer the request with a promise (I'll do my best, I won't...), agree or disagree with the rector, and finish by asking him one question of your own.

No keywords. This is your moment.
