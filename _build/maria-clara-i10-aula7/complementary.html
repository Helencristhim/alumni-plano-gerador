Video — speaking
FUTURE: "will" or "going to"? (Learn English with Rebecca · engVid)

A calm teacher explains the one decision at the heart of lesson 7: use "going to" for a plan you already have, and "will" for something you decide now. She compares them with simple examples you can copy for your own work and travel.

Tip: pause after each example and make a similar sentence about your life: "Tomorrow, I am going to present..." (a plan) / "The phone is ringing — I'll answer it." (a decision now). Ask yourself each time: before, or now?

A2 English Listening Practice (Listening Time)

Slow, clear listening at your exact level. Understanding by ear is your hardest skill, and the only way to train it is to use it, a little every day — the same "sound first" work you did with the two listenings in class.

Tip: listen ONCE without stopping — don't worry about the words you miss. Then listen again and write down two sentences you understood. Bring them to class.
