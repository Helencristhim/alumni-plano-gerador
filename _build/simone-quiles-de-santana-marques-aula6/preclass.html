Meeting Basics -- Modal Verbs for Meeting Participation

How to take part in a meeting with the right words and tone. New vocabulary: agenda, minutes, chair, raise a point, interrupt, recap, action item, postpone, move on, show of hands. Modal verbs can, could, and should for requests, suggestions, and advice.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics planned for a meeting
"There are three items on today's agenda." (Pauta)
Minutes -- the written record of a meeting
"Could you take the minutes today?" (Ata)
Chair -- to lead and guide a meeting
"Daniel will chair the meeting on Thursday." (Presidir)
Raise a point -- to bring up a topic to discuss
"Can I raise a point before we move on?" (Levantar um ponto)
Interrupt -- to stop someone while they are speaking
"Sorry to interrupt, but can I add something?" (Interromper)
Recap -- to summarize the main points again
"Could you recap the main points before we close?" (Recapitular)
Action item -- a task someone must do after the meeting
"Let's note that as an action item." (Item de acao)
Postpone -- to move a meeting to a later time
"We should postpone the last item to next week." (Adiar)
Move on -- to go to the next topic
"Could we move on to the next item?" (Passar adiante)
Show of hands -- a quick vote by raising hands
"Let's have a show of hands on the new deadline." (Votacao com as maos)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Daniel replies, "Of course. Could you recap where we are with the contract?" After a short discussion, they agree they should postpone the last item to next week. Daniel asks Simone to take the minutes, and they note two action items. Finally, Simone asks, "Should we move on to the next item?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Can, Could & Should

GRAMMAR

Em reunioes, escolha o modal certo: can (informal), could (mais educado), should (conselho). Depois do modal, use o verbo na base, sem to e sem s. / In meetings, choose the right modal: can (informal), could (more polite), should (advice). After the modal, use the base verb, with no to and no -s.

ModalUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
canPermission / ability (informal) -- permissao"Can I raise a point?"
couldPolite request / suggestion -- pedido educado"Could you recap the agenda?"
shouldAdvice / recommendation -- conselho"We should postpone this item."
Regra de ouromodal + verbo base (sem "to", sem "-s"): "She should send" (nao "should sends").
