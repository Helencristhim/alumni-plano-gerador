The Modal Code

Can, Could & Should

ModalUseExample
canPermission / ability (informal)"Can I raise a point?"
couldPolite request / suggestion"Could you recap the agenda?"
shouldAdvice / recommendation"We should postpone this item."
Golden rulemodal + base verb -- no "to", no "-s": "She should send" (not "should sends").

Key idea: could is the most polite for requests, can is quick and informal, and should gives advice. After all three, use the base verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Can you to send the minutes? ... She should sends the agenda."
"Can you send the minutes? ... She should send the agenda."

After can, could, or should, use the base verb. Never add to and never add -s.

PROJECT MEETING -- AGENDA

Thursday, 10:00 -- Chair: Daniel Brooks -- Minutes: Simone Quiles

Item 1 -- Cross-border contract

Simone to raise the point and update the team. Priority item, discuss early.

Item 2 -- New deadline

Decide the new date. A show of hands if the team disagrees.

Item 3 -- Budget review

If we run out of time, postpone to next week.

Action items: Daniel to send the minutes; Simone to review the contract by Friday; team to send questions before the next meeting.
