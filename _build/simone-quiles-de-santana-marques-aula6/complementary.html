Lesson 6 -- Meeting Basics

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 3: "Inside Track"

A legal drama with sharp meeting scenes. Watch how the lawyers chair a discussion, raise points, recap decisions, and assign action items -- and notice the polite requests with could and the advice with should.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences with can, could, or should from a meeting scene. Then record yourself reusing one in your own meeting context.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run a Great Meeting"

Practical advice for meetings: setting an agenda, chairing the discussion, keeping it on track, recapping decisions, and following up with clear action items. Listen for the modal verbs speakers use to make polite requests and suggestions.

Note 3 phrases the speakers use to keep a meeting moving. Compare them with the questions Simone and Daniel use in the project meeting.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "English at Work: meetings" / "Modal verbs: can, could, should"

A short, practical lesson on taking part in meetings in English: opening with the agenda, raising a point, asking for a recap, and making polite requests with can, could, and should. Directly useful for your project meetings at Telefonica.

After watching, note three polite phrases with could or should, then record a 60-second meeting opening using at least four new words from today.
