Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Colleague -- a person you work with, often in the same field
"I would like you to meet a colleague from my lab." (Colega)
Acquaintance -- a person you know slightly, not a close friend
"She started as an acquaintance and became a research partner." (Conhecido(a))
Mingle -- to move around a room and talk to many different people
"During the coffee break, I like to mingle with new people." (Circular / socializar)
Rapport -- a friendly, comfortable connection between people
"We built a good rapport after just five minutes." (Sintonia / boa relação)
Exchange -- to give and receive something, such as contact details
"Before we left, we exchanged email addresses." (Trocar)
Contact -- a person you can reach later, useful for your work
"He is a valuable contact in the nutrition field." (Contato)
Collaboration -- the act of working together on a shared project
"The conversation led to a collaboration between our teams." (Colaboração / parceria)
Business card -- a small card with your name, role and contact details
"Here is my business card -- let us keep in touch." (Cartão de visita)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the conference, Patricia uses the coffee break to mingle. She walks up to a small group and opens with a question: "What did you think of the keynote?" Soon she is talking to Mark, a colleague from the same field. They quickly build a good rapport, because they are both interested in nutrition research. Mark was only an acquaintance before, but now there could be a real collaboration between their teams. Before they leave, they exchange business cards, and Patricia adds Mark as an important contact. "Here is my business card," she says. "It was great to meet you -- let's keep in touch." The next day, she sends a short follow-up message to keep the connection warm.

B With an open question about the keynote.
C By giving a formal presentation.
2. What do Patricia and Mark do before they leave?
A They exchange business cards.
B They cancel the conference.
C They never speak again.
3. What does Patricia do the next day?
A She forgets about Mark.
C She asks for a refund.
B She sends a short follow-up message.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Small talk & the follow-up

GRAMMAR

Para fazer networking, abrimos com perguntas, construimos rapport e fechamos com frases educadas. / To network, we open with questions, build rapport, and close with polite fixed phrases.

Function / FunçãoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Open (Wh- question)What / How + ...?"What did you think of the keynote?"
Open (yes/no + follow-up)Are you...? + a real question"Are you presenting? What is it about?"
Build rapportshow interest + follow up"We work in the same field! Tell me more."
Close politelyfixed phrase"It was great to meet you. Let's keep in touch."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Abra com perguntas abertas (What / How), que pedem mais do que sim/não; construa rapport fazendo follow-up sobre o que a pessoa diz; e feche com frases fixas educadas antes de trocar contatos. Uma pergunta fechada (Do you like it?) costuma matar a conversa.
