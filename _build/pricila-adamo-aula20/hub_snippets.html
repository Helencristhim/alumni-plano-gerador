Stage 1.1: Settling-In Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These expressions help you arrange services and settle in abroad.

To settle in -- instalar-se / se ambientar
"It took a month to settle in, but now it feels like home."
To get something sorted -- resolver / dar um jeito em algo
"It was a busy week, but I got everything sorted in the end."
An errand -- uma tarefa / um recado (fora de casa)
"I have a few errands to run before the shops close."
A service provider -- um prestador de servico
"The internet service provider sent a technician the next day."
To book an appointment -- marcar um horario
"I booked an appointment at the salon for Friday morning."
To drop something off -- deixar algo (para um servico)
"I'll drop the coat off at the dry cleaner's on my way."
A quote -- um orcamento
"The garage gave me a fair quote before fixing the car."
A deposit -- um sinal / deposito (adiantamento)
"They asked for a small deposit to confirm the appointment."
To get something fixed -- consertar algo (mandar consertar)
"I need to get the heater fixed before winter."
Red tape -- burocracia
"There was a lot of red tape before I could open a bank account."
To sign up for -- inscrever-se / contratar (um servico)
"I signed up for a local SIM card and a gym membership."
To set something up -- configurar / instalar algo
"The technician set up the internet in about an hour."
Paperwork -- papelada / documentacao
"There was a mountain of paperwork to sign at the office."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

Pricila's first week abroad was all about getting things sorted. On Monday she had the locks changed and got the internet set up. There was a lot of red tape, so she had a few documents translated for the paperwork. On Wednesday she booked an appointment and had her hair cut. She also dropped her coat off and got it cleaned. "I didn't do any of it myself," she laughs. "I just arranged it all. And honestly, once you've had things fixed in your own name, a strange place starts to feel like home."

1. "She had her hair cut." Who cut Pricila's hair?
A Pricila cut it herself.
B A hairdresser did it for her -- she arranged the service.
C Nobody -- her hair is still long.
2. Which form is correct for the causative?
A "She had the locks to change."
B "She had the locks changed." (have + object + past participle)
C "She had changed the locks." (= she changed them herself, earlier)
3. What does the story say makes a strange place feel like home?
A Doing every single job by yourself.
B Avoiding all the red tape and paperwork.
C Having things fixed and sorted in your own name.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Causative (have/get something done)

Grammar

Usamos a CAUSATIVA quando ARRANJAMOS que outra pessoa faca um servico para nos, em vez de fazer nos mesmos. Forma: have/get + OBJETO + particio passado. Compare: "I cut my hair" (eu cortei) vs "I had my hair cut" (o cabeleireiro cortou para mim). A parte have/get muda com o tempo verbal (had / am having / will have / am going to get), mas o segundo verbo e SEMPRE o particio passado (cut, translated, painted, fixed, set up). 'Have' e 'get' tem o mesmo sentido aqui; 'get' e um pouco mais informal. Ha tambem a causativa de EXPERIENCIA, para coisas ruins que acontecem COM voce: "She had her bag stolen" (roubaram a bolsa dela). NAO confunda com a voz passiva (Aula 16): na causativa VOCE continua sendo quem arranja o servico. / The causative = have/get + object + past participle, used when you arrange for someone else to do a job. The second verb is always the past participle. There is also an experience causative (She had her bag stolen). It is different from the plain passive: in the causative, YOU arranged it.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A hairdresser cut your hair last week. How do you say it?
A "I cut my hair last week."
B "I had my hair cut last week."
C "I had my hair to cut last week."
You want a mechanic to repair your car. How do you say it?
A "I need to get the car fixed."
B "I need to get fix the car."
C "I need to get the car to fix."
Someone stole your phone yesterday (an experience). How do you say it?
A "I had stolen my phone yesterday."
B "I had my phone to steal yesterday."
C "I had my phone stolen yesterday."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.
