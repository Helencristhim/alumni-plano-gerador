Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Making suggestions: shall we, why don't we, how about, I suggest

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What form of the verb comes after each way of making a suggestion?

"Shall we start with the first item?" (start -- base verb)

"Why don't we postpone item three?" (postpone -- base verb)

"How about taking a short break?" (taking -- verb + ing)

"I suggest we vote on this point." (vote -- base verb)

Grammar Rule

Making Suggestions

StructureVerb formExample
Shall we ...?base verbShall we begin?
Why don't we ...?base verbWhy don't we vote?
Let's ...base verbLet's recap the points.
I suggest we ...base verbI suggest we postpone item three.
How about ...? / I suggest ...verb + -ingHow about taking a break? / I suggest voting now.

Key idea: most suggestion forms take the base verb (shall we start, why don't we vote, let's recap). Only how about and I suggest without "we" take the -ing form (how about taking, I suggest voting).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I suggest to start with item one."
"I suggest starting with item one."

After I suggest and how about, use the -ing form, never to. You can also say "I suggest we start" with the base verb.

Brainstorm ideas with every participant. Anyone can raise a point or clarify a doubt.
