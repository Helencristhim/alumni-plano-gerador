Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics to discuss in a meeting
"The agenda has four items today." (Pauta)
Chair -- to lead a meeting, or the person who leads it
"Nilo will chair the meeting this week." (Presidir / conduzir)
Participant -- a person who takes part in a meeting
"Every participant can speak once." (Participante)
Raise -- to bring up a point or question in a meeting
"I would like to raise one point." (Levantar um ponto)
Interrupt -- to stop someone politely while they are speaking
"Sorry to interrupt, but we are short on time." (Interromper)
Clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"Could you clarify the second point?" (Esclarecer)
Recap -- to summarize the main points quickly
"Let me recap our three decisions." (Recapitular)
Consensus -- general agreement among the people in a group
"After a short discussion, we reached a consensus." (Consenso)
Brainstorm -- to share many ideas freely and quickly
"Let us brainstorm a few ideas first." (Debater ideias livremente)
Vote -- to make a group decision by choice
"Let us vote on the final budget." (Votar)
Postpone -- to move something to a later time or date
"We can postpone this item to the next meeting." (Adiar)
Assign -- to give a task or job to a person
"I will assign one action to each person." (Atribuir / designar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Next week, Nilo will chair a FIFA meeting for the first time in English. He has the agenda with four items, so he is preparing his suggestions. To open the meeting he will say, "Shall we start with the first item?" For the training plan he wants ideas from everyone, so he will say, "Why don't we brainstorm a few ideas?" When the group is tired, he can say, "How about taking a short break?" Notice the form: after how about we use the -ing form (taking), but after shall we and why don't we we use the base verb (start, brainstorm). For the budget, if there is no consensus, he will say, "I suggest we vote." He is careful not to say "I suggest to vote" -- the correct forms are "I suggest voting" or "I suggest we vote".

1. How does Nilo open the meeting?
A Shall we start with the first item?
B Shall we starting with the first item?
C Shall we to start with the first item?
2. Which suggestion uses the correct form after "how about"?
A How about take a short break?
B How about taking a short break?
C How about to take a short break?
3. Which sentence is correct for the budget vote?
A I suggest we vote on the budget.
B I suggest to vote on the budget.
C I suggest we voting on the budget.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Making Suggestions

GRAMMAR

Usado para propor uma ideia ao grupo numa reunião. / Used to propose an idea to the group in a meeting.

Structure / EstruturaVerb form / Forma do verboExample / Exemplo
Shall we ...?base verbShall we begin?
Why don't we ...?base verbWhy don't we vote?
Let's ...base verbLet's recap the points.
I suggest we ...base verbI suggest we postpone item three.
How about ...? / I suggest ...verb + -ingHow about taking a break? / I suggest voting.

Key point / Ponto-chave: a maioria das sugestões usa o verbo na forma base (shall we start, why don't we vote, let's recap). Só how about e I suggest (sem "we") usam a forma -ing (how about taking, I suggest voting). NUNCA use "to": "I suggest to vote" está errado. / Most suggestion forms take the base verb; only how about and I suggest (without we) take the -ing form. Never use "to".
