Lesson 11 -- Meeting Dynamics

Series
Succession -- any boardroom meeting scene

Executives chair meetings, share an agenda, interrupt each other, and push for a vote. You will hear real suggestion language and meeting vocabulary in a high-level corporate context close to your work.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three suggestions a character makes and label the structure (shall we, why don't we, how about, I suggest).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Chairing a Meeting"

Practical episodes on leading meetings in English: opening the session, sharing the agenda, brainstorming, managing participants, reaching a consensus, and wrapping up. Exactly today's vocabulary in real business situations.

Note three phrases the chair uses to make suggestions. Then record yourself opening a meeting with two different suggestion structures.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Making Suggestions"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to make suggestions with shall we, why don't we, how about, let's, and I suggest, including the base verb versus -ing form.

After watching, make five suggestions about your next real meeting. Use a different structure each time, and remember that how about and I suggest take the -ing form.
