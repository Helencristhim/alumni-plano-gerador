Aula 07 -- Pre-class

Navigating Real Places -- Hotels, Airports and the Unexpected

Solving real travel problems politely. Key words: front desk, check in, overbooked, connecting flight, luggage claim, complimentary, upgrade, availability, inconvenience, compensation, complaint, adjoining rooms. Structures: first conditional (If it is ready, I'll take it), second conditional (If I missed it, I'd wait) and polite requests (I was wondering if... / Is there any chance you could...?).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Front desk -- the reception counter where guests arrive and are helped (a recepção)
"I stopped at the front desk to ask about my reservation."
To check in -- to register on arrival at a hotel or an airport (fazer o check-in)
"We can check in after three in the afternoon."
Overbooked -- sold to more people than there is room for (com reservas em excesso)
"The hotel was overbooked, so my room was gone."
Connecting flight -- a second flight that takes you to your final destination (voo de conexão)
"My connecting flight to Oslo leaves in two hours."
Luggage claim -- the area where you collect your bags after a flight (esteira de bagagem)
"My bag never appeared at luggage claim."
Complimentary -- given free, as a courtesy (cortesia, gratuito)
"Breakfast is complimentary for every guest."
An upgrade -- a move to a better room, seat, or service (melhoria de categoria)
"They gave me an upgrade to a suite with a view."
Availability -- whether a room or a seat is free to be used (disponibilidade)
"Let me check availability for tonight."
Inconvenience -- trouble or difficulty caused to someone (transtorno, incômodo)
"Sorry for the inconvenience; we will fix it now."
Compensation -- something given to make up for a problem (compensação)
"They offered compensation for the delayed flight."
A complaint -- a formal statement that something is wrong (uma reclamação)
"I would like to make a complaint about the noise."
Adjoining rooms -- two rooms side by side with a connecting door (quartos conjugados)
"We booked adjoining rooms so the family could stay together."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcelo reached the front desk at eleven at night, exhausted. The hotel was overbooked, and his confirmed room was gone. He stayed calm. First a real plan: "If the room is not ready, I will wait at the bar." Notice the pattern — after if we use the present (is), and the result uses will. Then a hypothetical: "If I missed my connection tomorrow, I would take the next flight." Here, after if we use the past (missed), and the result uses would. To ask for help, he softened everything: "Is there any chance you could check availability?" and "I was wondering if it would be possible to change rooms." The receptionist offered a complimentary upgrade for the inconvenience.

1. Por que "If the room is not ready, I will wait" é primeira condicional (real)?
A Porque usa presente depois de if e will no resultado.
B Porque usa passado depois de if e would no resultado.
C Porque usa will logo depois de if.
2. Em "If I missed my connection, I would take the next flight", o que vem depois de if?
A would (If I would miss...)
B o passado (missed) — é hipotético.
C o futuro (will miss).
3. Which is the most polite request?
A "Change my room now."
B "I was wondering if it would be possible to change rooms."
C "You must give me another room."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Conditionals & Polite Requests

GRAMMAR

Como falar de planos reais, hipóteses e pedidos educados (explicação em inglês e português).

TypeUse / UsoExample
First conditional
if + present, ... will		Situação real e provável. A real, likely situation.If the room is ready, I will take it.
Second conditional
if + past, ... would		Situação hipotética ou improvável. An unreal or hypothetical situation.If I missed it, I would wait.
Polite requestSuaviza o pedido. Softens what you ask for.Is there any chance you could...?

Atenção: NUNCA coloque will ou would logo depois de if. Diga "If it is ready", nunca "If it will be ready".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real.

The hotel gave your room away. What do you say first?
A "Give me a room right now or else."
B "There seems to be a misunderstanding. Is there any chance you could check availability?"
C "This is the worst hotel in the world."
You want to ask, hypothetically, about missing your connection. Which is correct?
A "If I would miss my connection, I take the next."
B "If I missed my connection, I would take the next flight."
C "If I will miss my connection, I would take the next."
Your bag never arrived. How do you ask about compensation, politely?
A "Would it be alright if we discussed compensation for the lost bag?"
B "You owe me money for my bag, now."
C "Where is my bag, are you stupid?"
The room is ready. Which is the correct first conditional?
A "If the room will be ready, I take it."
B "If the room is ready, I'll take it."
C "If the room would be ready, I take it."
