Chapter 3: The Code

Conditionals & Polite Requests

If this... then that · and how to ask nicely

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If the room is not ready, I will wait at the bar."

"If I missed my connection, I would take the next flight."

"I was wondering if it would be possible to change rooms."

"Is there any chance you could check availability?"

After if, why does green use the present, but blue use the past?

TypeUse it forForm
First conditionala real, likely situationif + present, ... will + verb
Second conditionalan unreal or hypothetical situationif + past, ... would + verb
Polite requestsoftening what you ask forI was wondering if... / Would it be alright if...? / Is there any chance you could...?

Never put will or would right after if. The if-part uses present (first) or past (second).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the room will be ready, I will wait."
"If the room is ready, I will wait."
"If I would miss my flight, I would call."
"If I missed my flight, I would call."

Never put will or would right after if. Use the present for a real plan, the past for a hypothetical.

Practice

Fix the Conditional

Say the correct form first, then click to check

"If the flight ___is delayed, I'll wait." (first, real: present after if)
"If I ___missed my connection, I'd take the next one." (second: past after if)
"If the room is quiet, I ___will take it." (first: will in the result)
"If I had more time, I ___would stay another night." (second: would in the result)
"If you check in after noon, breakfast ___will still be included." (first, real)
Detail

Ask It Politely

turn the blunt line into a polite request · say it, then reveal

Blunt: "Change my room." Polite: "___I was wondering if it would be possible to change my room."
Blunt: "Check availability." Polite: "___Is there any chance you could check availability?"
Blunt: "I need a late checkout." Polite: "___Would it be alright if I checked out a little later?"
Blunt: "Fix this tonight." Polite: "___I'd really appreciate it if you could sort this out tonight."
Chapter 4: The Front Desk

Eleven at Night

A problem with the reservation in Reykjavik

Dialogue

A Problem at Check-In

S
Good evening, and welcome. Do you have a reservation with us tonight?
M
I do, under Souza. I was told my room was confirmed, but there seems to be a misunderstanding at check-in.
S
I am so sorry, Mr. Souza. I am afraid we are overbooked tonight. Let me check availability for you.
M
I understand it is not your fault. Is there any chance you could find me anything at all? If there is a quieter room, I'll happily take it.
S
Actually, if you don't mind adjoining rooms, I can offer you a suite as an upgrade — complimentary, for the inconvenience.
M
That would be perfect. I was wondering if it would be possible to have some breakfast sent up as well. It has been a very long trip.
