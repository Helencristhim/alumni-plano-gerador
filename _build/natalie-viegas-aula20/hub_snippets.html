Reaching Agreement -- Confirming Terms & Closing Strongly

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Confirm -- to say clearly and officially that something is agreed or true
"Let me confirm the final price before we move on." (Confirmar)
Restate -- to say something again clearly to make sure it is understood
"Let me restate the terms so we are both clear." (Reafirmar / repetir)
Sum up -- to give a short, final summary of the main points
"To sum up, we agree on the price, the term, and the date." (Resumir)
Iron out -- to solve the last small problems or differences
"We just need to iron out the payment dates." (Resolver / acertar detalhes)
Lock in -- to make a price or term final so it cannot change
"Let's lock in the rate for the next two years." (Fixar / travar)
In writing -- written down on paper or by email, so it is official
"I'll put the agreed terms in writing this afternoon." (Por escrito)
Mutually beneficial -- good and fair for both sides of the agreement
"This is a mutually beneficial deal -- we both win." (Vantajoso para ambos)
Seal the deal -- to finish a negotiation and make the agreement official
"A handshake and a signature, and we seal the deal." (Fechar o acordo)
Move forward -- to start the next steps after an agreement is reached
"Now that we agree, let's move forward with the contract." (Avançar / seguir em frente)
Touch base -- to make a short contact later to check on progress
"Let's touch base next week to check the rollout." (Manter contato / dar um retorno)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Future Perfect

GRAMMAR

Para fechar com confiança, projete o resultado pronto: antes de um marco no futuro, a tarefa estará concluída. Use o future perfect: will have + particípio passado (signed, sent, agreed). Sempre acompanhe de um marco com by: by Friday, by then, by the end of the call. / To close with confidence, project the finished result: by a future point, the task will be done. Use the future perfect: will have + past participle. Always pair it with a deadline using by.

FormPatternExample
Affirmativewill have + past participle"By Friday, we will have signed the contract."
Negativewon't have + past participle"By then, we won't have finished the paperwork."
QuestionWill + subject + have + p.p.?"Will we have agreed on everything by Friday?"
Time markerby + future point"by Friday, by then, by the end of the call"

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de will have use o particípio passado (signed, sent), nunca o verbo base. Errado: "we will have sign." Certo: "we will have signed." E use by (não "until") para o prazo de conclusão.
