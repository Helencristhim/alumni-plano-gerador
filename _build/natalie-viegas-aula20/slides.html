Chapter 3 -- The Finished Result

Grammar Discovery

The future perfect -- a result finished by a deadline

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What are the orange words? When is the action finished?

By Friday, we will have signed the contract.

By the end of the call, we will have agreed on every term.

I will have sent the terms in writing by tonight.

Compare: "We will sign on Friday." (future, no deadline of completion)

Grammar Rule

The Future Perfect

FormPatternExample
Affirmativewill have + past participle"By Friday, we will have signed the contract."
Negativewill not (won't) have + p.p."By then, we won't have finished the paperwork."
QuestionWill + subject + have + p.p.?"Will we have agreed on everything by Friday?"
Time markerby + future point"by Friday, by then, by the end of the call"

Key idea: use the future perfect (will have + past participle) to say an action will be complete before a future point. Always pair it with a time marker: by Friday, by then, by the end of the call. It is perfect for closing with confidence: you project the finished result.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"By Friday, we will have sign the contract."
"By Friday, we will have signed the contract."

After will have, use the past participle (signed, sent, agreed), never the base verb. And use by for the deadline of completion.

Grammar Practice

Project the Result

"By Friday, we _____will have signed the contract." (sign)
"By the end of the call, we _____will have agreed on every term." (agree)
"I _____will have sent the terms in writing by tonight." (send)
