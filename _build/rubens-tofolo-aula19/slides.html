Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Joining ideas with who, which, and that

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we add information about people, things, and places?

"She is the guide who knows every street." (person)

"It is the cathedral which is the oldest building." (thing / place)

"It is a market that sells local crafts." (thing -- that)

"He is a doctor that works long hours." (person -- that)

Grammar Rule

who / which / that

Use forExample
People: noun + who + verbShe is the guide who knows every street.
Things / places: noun + which + verbIt is the cathedral which is the oldest building.
People or things: noun + that + verbIt is a market that sells local crafts.
People or things: noun + that + verbHe is a doctor that works long hours.

Key idea: use who for people, which for things and places, and that for both. The relative word joins extra information to a noun. Do not repeat the subject -- say "the guide who knows", never "the guide who she knows".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She is the guide which knows the city." / "The guide who she knows the city."
"She is the guide who knows the city." / "The guide who knows the city."

Use who for people, which for things and places. Never repeat the subject after who / which / that.
