Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Sightseeing -- visiting the interesting places of a city as a visitor
"I kept a free morning for sightseeing." (Passeio turístico)
Tour guide -- a person who shows visitors around a place and explains it
"The tour guide who led us knew every street." (Guia turístico)
Attraction -- a place that many people visit because it is interesting
"The cathedral is the main attraction in this city." (Atração)
Viewpoint -- a high spot that gives a wide, open view over an area
"We rested at a viewpoint that looks over the river." (Mirante)
Admission -- the money you pay, or the right, to enter a place
"Admission to the gallery is included in your ticket." (Entrada / ingresso)
Exhibit -- an object or display shown to the public in a museum
"The exhibit that I liked most had old maps." (Exposição / peça exposta)
Gallery -- a building or room where art is shown to visitors
"The gallery has paintings from local artists." (Galeria)
Cathedral -- a very large and important church, often old and historic
"The cathedral which we visited is the oldest building here." (Catedral)
Scenic -- having beautiful natural views that are nice to look at
"We took the scenic route along the coast." (Panorâmico / com bela vista)
Vendor -- a person who sells things, often on the street or at a market
"A vendor who sells crafts gave me a good price." (Vendedor ambulante)
Heritage -- the history, buildings, and traditions a place is proud of
"This square is part of the city's heritage." (Patrimônio / herança cultural)
Bustling -- full of busy, lively activity and lots of people
"The market is a bustling place that sells fresh fruit." (Movimentado / agitado)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses (who / which / that)

GRAMMAR

Usamos relative clauses para juntar duas ideias e dar mais informação sobre um substantivo. Use who para pessoas, which para coisas e lugares, e that para pessoas ou coisas (informal). / We use relative clauses to join two ideas and give more information about a noun. Use who for people, which for things and places, and that for people or things (more informal).

Form / FormaExample / Exemplo
People: noun + who + verbShe is the guide who knows every street. / Ela é a guia que conhece todas as ruas.
Things / places: noun + which + verbIt is the cathedral which is the oldest building. / É a catedral que é o prédio mais antigo.
People or things: noun + that + verbIt is a market that sells local crafts. / É um mercado que vende artesanato local.
People or things: noun + that + verbHe is a doctor that works long hours. / Ele é um médico que trabalha muitas horas.

Key point / Ponto-chave: who = pessoas, which = coisas/lugares, that = pessoas ou coisas. Não repita o sujeito: diga "the guide who knows", nunca "the guide who she knows".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best phrase for each situation.

You want to describe a guide (a person). Which word do you use?
A "the guide which knows the city"
B "the guide who knows the city"
C "the guide where knows the city"
You want to describe a market (a thing). Which sentence is correct?
A "a market who sells crafts"
B "a market which sells crafts"
C "a market what sells crafts"
Which sentence does NOT repeat the subject correctly?
A "the vendor who he sells souvenirs"
B "the vendor who sells souvenirs"
C "the vendor that sells souvenirs"
