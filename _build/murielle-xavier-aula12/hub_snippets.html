Now vs. Always -- Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Delegate -- to give a task to someone else to do (delegar)
"I usually delegate the reports to my team."
Cover -- to do someone's work while they are away (cobrir / substituir)
"This week I am covering for Sofia."
Shift -- a set period of working time (turno)
"The night shift starts at ten."
Rotate -- to change tasks or roles in turns (revezar / alternar)
"We rotate the on-call duty every month."
Interim -- temporary, for a short period (interino / provisório)
"She is the interim manager right now."
Standby -- ready to act if needed (de prontidão / em espera)
"The support team is on standby today."
Workflow -- the sequence of steps in a work process (fluxo de trabalho)
"Our normal workflow has five steps."
Frequently -- often; many times (frequentemente)
"I frequently review the payroll on Fridays."
Occasionally -- sometimes; not often (ocasionalmente)
"We occasionally work on Saturdays."
Temporarily -- for a short time only (temporariamente)
"I am temporarily working from London."
Ordinarily -- usually; as a general rule (normalmente / em geral)
"Ordinarily, the team meets on Mondays."
Reassign -- to give a task or person to a different job (reatribuir / remanejar)
"We are reassigning two analysts this week."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Ordinarily, I manage the payroll and I delegate the reports to my team. I usually work from the Sao Paulo office, and I frequently review the numbers on Fridays. But this week is different. Right now, I am covering for Sofia because she is away, so I am temporarily handling two roles. At the moment, we are reassigning a few analysts and rotating the on-call shifts. The support team is on standby all week. Our normal workflow still works well, but everything is moving fast today.

1. Why does the text say "I usually work" but "I am covering for Sofia"?
A The two verbs mean the same thing.
B "usually work" is a routine (present simple); "am covering" is temporary, now (present continuous).
C "am covering" is a permanent fact.
2. Which words signal a routine (present simple)?
A ordinarily, usually, frequently.
B right now, at the moment, this week.
C yesterday, last week, ago.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Right now I usually cover for Sofia."
B "Right now I am covering for Sofia."
C "Right now I am cover for Sofia."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Simple vs. Continuous

GRAMMAR

Use o present simple para rotinas, fatos e hábitos (com advérbios como usually, frequently, ordinarily): "I delegate the reports." Use o present continuous (am/is/are + verbo-ing) para agora ou situações temporárias (com right now, at the moment, this week): "This week I am covering for Sofia." Lembre do -s na 3ª pessoa do present simple: she works.

TenseUseExample
Present simpleRoutine, fact, habit"I usually delegate the reports."
3rd person -she/she/it in present simple"She manages the London team."
Present continuousNow or temporary"This week I am covering for Sofia."
Stative verbsalways present simple"I know the process." (not "am knowing")

Atenção: rotina = present simple (usually, ordinarily); agora/temporário = present continuous (right now, this week). Não misture os dois.
