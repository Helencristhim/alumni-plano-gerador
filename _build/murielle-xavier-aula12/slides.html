Simple vs. Continuous

Routine and fact versus now and temporary

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I usually delegate the reports."

"This week I am covering for Sofia."

"She manages the London team."

"Right now, we are reassigning two analysts."

TenseSignalExample
Present simpleusually, ordinarily, frequentlyI usually delegate the reports.
3rd person -she / she / itShe manages the team.
Present continuousright now, this week, temporarilyI am covering for Sofia.
Stative verbsalways present simpleI know the process.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am usually delegating the reports."

✓ RIGHT

"I usually delegate the reports."

✗ WRONG

"Right now I cover for Sofia."

✓ RIGHT

"Right now I am covering for Sofia."

✗ WRONG

"She manage the London team."

✓ RIGHT

"She manages the London team."

Routine = present simple (with usually, ordinarily). Now or temporary = present continuous (with right now, this week). Do not forget the -s for he/she/it.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Routine versus temporary change at work

Dialogue

My Month at Work

L
Murielle, let me explain my month. Ordinarily, I manage the payroll and I delegate the reports to my team.
M
So that is your usual routine. What is different this week?
L
This week I am covering for Sofia, so I am temporarily handling two roles.
M
I see. Do you usually work from the main office?
L
Yes, I usually work from the main office and I frequently review the numbers on Fridays. But right now I am working from London.
M
And the team? Are you changing anything at the moment?
L
Yes, we are reassigning a few analysts and rotating the on-call shifts. The support team is on standby.
M
Got it. So the routine is stable, but a few things are temporary right now.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

He manages the payroll and delegates the reports to his team.
2. What is different this week?
He is covering for Sofia, temporarily handling two roles, and working from London.
3. What is the team doing at the moment?
They are reassigning a few analysts and rotating the on-call shifts.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can use the present simple for routines and facts: I usually delegate the reports.
I can use the present continuous for now or temporary: this week I am covering for Sofia.
I can add the -s for he/she/it in the present simple: she manages the team.
I can choose the right tense from the signal words (usually vs right now) and keep state verbs simple.
I can use the new words: delegate, cover, shift, rotate, interim, standby, workflow, frequently, occasionally, temporarily, ordinarily, reassign.
