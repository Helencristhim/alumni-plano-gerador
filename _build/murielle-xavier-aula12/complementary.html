Lesson 12 -- Now vs. Always -- Present Simple vs. Present Continuous

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- an episode on routines and change at work

Adam Grant and his guests talk about what people ordinarily do and what they are changing right now. You will hear the present simple for routines and the present continuous for temporary situations. Direct connection to Lesson 12: describing your routine versus what is happening now.

Tip: write 3 sentences about your routine and 3 about what you are doing this week.

Series
The Office (US) -- an episode where someone covers a different role temporarily

Watch a scene where a character explains what they normally do and what they are doing this week while covering for someone. Notice the contrast between present simple (routine) and present continuous (temporary). Connection to Lesson 12: now versus always.

Tip: pause and say: "He usually works in sales, but this week he is covering reception."

YouTube
"Present Simple vs Present Continuous" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on when to use the present simple (routines and facts) and the present continuous (now and temporary), plus the frequency adverbs and stative verbs. Connection to Lesson 12: the exact contrast you use to describe your work.

Tip: pause and build sentences with delegate, cover, and reassign.
