Chapter 3: The Code

I Think That...

start an opinion with I think + a full sentence

Discover the Grammar

What Comes After "I think"?

"I think the date is good."

"I agree with you."

"I do not agree with that."

UseFormExample
Give an opinionI think (that) + sentenceI think the date is good.
AgreeI agree (with you)I agree with you.
Disagree (polite)I do not agree / I am not sureI am not sure about that.
SuggestI suggest (that) we + verbI suggest we wait.

Start your opinion with "I think" and add a full sentence. Say "I agree with you" -- never "I am agree". To say no politely, use "I am not sure" or "I do not agree".

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am agree with you."

✓ RIGHT

"I agree with you."

✗ WRONG

"I think yes."

✓ RIGHT

"I think the date is good."

"Agree" is a verb, so we never say "I am agree". And after "I think", add a full sentence, not just "yes".

Chapter 4: At Work

A Meeting at Sucafina

agreeing and disagreeing about a shipment

Dialogue

The Shipment Meeting

D
Thanks for joining, Fernanda. So, the Colombia file is ready. What do you think?
F
In my opinion, the new date is good. I think it works for the whole team.
D
Good. I agree. I also suggest we ask the supplier for a small discount.
F
Hmm. I am not sure about that. The supplier is reliable, and I do not want a problem.
D
That is a fair point. So what is your idea?
F
I suggest we pay on time and ask for a discount next month.
D
I like it. I think that is a better plan.
F
Great. Let's decide this together and write it in the file.
