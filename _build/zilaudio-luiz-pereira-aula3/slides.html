Chapter 3

The Code

Grammar Discovery

What Do the Green Words Have in Common?

Where are you from?
Do you grow soybeans?
What do you sell here?
Could I have your card?
FormIt asksExample
am / is / are + youA fact: place, name, jobWhere are you from?
Do + you + verbAn action or a habitDo you have a brochure?
Could I / you + verbA polite requestCould I have your card?

A question starts with a special word: are, do, or could. Use could when you ask for something — it is the polite way.

Common Mistake

Direct vs Polite

"You have a business card?"
"Do you have a business card?"
"I want your card."
"Could I have your card, please?"

A question needs a starter word: Do you have...? And to ask for something, could is the polite key. "I want" is an order; "Could I have...?" is a question. In business, always the second one.
