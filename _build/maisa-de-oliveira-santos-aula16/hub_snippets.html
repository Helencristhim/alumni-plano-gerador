Welcoming a Visitor

Receber um visitante e dar direções no escritório: come in, have a seat, a visitor, follow me, this way, the elevator, turn left, go straight. Gramática: imperativos (verbo base, sem sujeito) e direções (go straight, turn left, on the second floor). Missão: receber o David no escritório pela primeira vez.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Come in -- you say this at the door to let a person enter
"Hello, David. Please come in." (entre)
Have a seat -- you say this to offer a chair to a guest
"Have a seat here, please." (sente-se)
A visitor -- a person who comes to visit your office
"We have a visitor today." (um visitante)
Follow me -- you say this to ask a person to walk with you
"Follow me to the meeting room." (venha comigo)
This way -- you point and say this to show the direction
"The elevator is this way." (por aqui)
The elevator -- a small room that goes up and down between floors
"Take the elevator to the second floor." (o elevador)
Turn left -- go to the left side at the corner
"Go to the corner and turn left." (vire à esquerda)
Go straight -- continue forward; do not turn
"Go straight to the end of the hall." (siga em frente)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Maísa has a visitor today. David arrives at the office, and Maísa welcomes him. "Welcome, David. Please come in," she says. Then she shows the way: "Follow me. This way, please. Go straight, and then turn left. The elevator is there. The meeting room is on the second floor." In the room, Maísa is a great host. "Have a seat," she says. "Would you like some coffee?"

1. O que a Maísa diz na porta? / What does Maísa say at the door?

B "Welcome, David. Please come in."

2. Como eles vão até a sala de reunião? / How do they go to the meeting room?
A Go straight, turn left, and take the elevator.
B Turn right and go down the stairs.
C Go back to the reception.
3. Onde fica a sala de reunião? / Where is the meeting room?
A On the first floor.
B On the second floor.
C At the reception.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives & Directions

GRAMMAR

O imperativo dá uma instrução. Use o verbo base e SEM sujeito: "Come in", nunca "You come in". Para andar, use "go straight" (siga em frente) e "turn left / right" (vire à esquerda/direita) — sem "to". Para o andar, use "on": "on the second floor". Para dizer não, use "Do not". / An imperative gives an instruction: base verb, no subject. Use "on" for a floor.

Uso / UseEstrutura / PatternExemplo / Example
Instrução / Instructionverbo base (sem sujeito)Come in. Have a seat. (entre / sente-se)
Direções / Directionsgo straight · turn left / rightGo straight, then turn left. (siga em frente, vire à esquerda)
Negativa / NegativeDo not + verbo baseDo not turn right. (não vire à direita)

Atenção: o imperativo não tem sujeito — diga "Have a seat", nunca "You have a seat". E não use to depois de "go": diga "go straight", nunca "go to straight". Para o andar, use on: "on the second floor", não "in the second floor".
