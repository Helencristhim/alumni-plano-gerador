Chapter 4: The Code

Imperatives & Directions

Short instructions to guide a visitor

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Come in, please."

"Follow me. This way."

"Turn left at the elevator."

"Have a seat, please."

UseFormExample
Instruction / invitationbase verb (no subject)Come in. Have a seat.
Directionsgo straight · turn left / rightGo straight, then turn left.
NegativeDo not + base verbDo not turn right. It is on the left.

An imperative gives an instruction. Use the base verb and no subject: "Come in," not "You come in." For a floor, use on: "The room is on the second floor." For no, add Do not: "Do not worry."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Go to straight, then turn to left.
Go straight, then turn left.
You have a seat, please.
Have a seat, please.

Do not put to after the direction verb: say "go straight" and "turn left", not "go to straight". And an imperative has no subject: say "Have a seat", not "You have a seat".
