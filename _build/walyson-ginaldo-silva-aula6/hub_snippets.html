Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Quote -- a stated price you offer for a product or service
"I will send you a quote for one container of coffee." (Cotação / orçamento)
Bid -- an offer to buy something at a certain price
"The buyer made a bid of four thousand dollars per ton." (Oferta de compra / lance)
Discount -- a reduction in the normal price
"For a large order, we can offer a small discount." (Desconto)
Margin -- the profit between the cost and the selling price
"Our margin is small when costs are high." (Margem de lucro)
Invoice -- a document that lists goods and the amount to pay
"Please pay the invoice within thirty days." (Fatura / nota fiscal)
Volume -- the total quantity or amount of something
"A bigger volume usually means a lower price." (Volume / quantidade)
Rate -- a fixed price or amount, for example per ton or per unit
"The rate today is higher than last week." (Taxa / preço unitário)
Estimate -- an approximate calculation of a cost or quantity
"My estimate is that prices will rise next month." (Estimativa)
Figure -- a number, especially in business or finance
"Let me check the final figure on the invoice." (Número / cifra)
Currency -- the money used in a country, like the dollar or the real
"We trade coffee in dollars, the usual currency." (Moeda)
Increase -- to go up; a rise in amount, number or price
"Prices increase when demand is strong." (Aumentar / aumento)
Decrease -- to go down; a fall in amount, number or price
"Prices decrease when the harvest is large." (Diminuir / queda)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson is preparing a price quote for a buyer in Europe. The buyer wants a large volume, so Walyson can offer a small discount. Coffee is more expensive this season than last year, because the harvest was smaller. Walyson checks the figure twice: his rate is higher than the market average, but it is cheaper than his main rival. He keeps a small margin, so his price is more competitive than before. He sends the invoice in dollars, the usual currency, and writes that prices may increase by ten percent next month. A bigger volume is always better than a small one, because the cost per ton is lower.

1. Why can Walyson offer a discount?
A Because the buyer wants a large volume.
B Because his margin is very big.
C Because the currency is the euro.
2. How does Walyson describe his price compared to his rival?
A It is more expensive than his rival.
B It is cheaper and more competitive than his rival.
C It is the same as the market average.
3. What may happen to prices next month?
A They may decrease by ten percent.
B They will stay exactly the same.
C They may increase by ten percent.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives, Big Numbers and Percentages

GRAMMAR

Comparamos preços e números com comparativos. / We compare prices and numbers with comparatives.

Type / TipoRule / RegraExample / Exemplo
Short adjective+ er + thanOur price is cheaper than the market.
Long adjectivemore + adj + thanCoffee is more expensive than last year.
Irregulargood/badThis rate is better than the last one.
Percentagesincrease / decrease by X%Prices increased by 10% this month.

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca diga "more cheaper" nem "more better". Use só UMA forma comparativa: cheaper, better, more expensive. Números grandes: 4,200 = four thousand two hundred.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each trading situation.

A buyer asks for a lower price on a very large order. The best reply is:
A "Because the volume is large, I can offer a small discount."
B "The price is the price. No discount."
C "Small order, big discount."
You want to say your price is good compared to the market:
A "My rate is more competitive than the market average."
B "My rate is more cheaper than market."
C "Price good. You buy."
You need to explain that prices went up this month. You say:
A "Prices increased by eight percent this month."
B "Prices increase eight percents."
C "Prices is more high now."
