Lesson 6 -- Numbers, Prices, and Contracts

Series
Billions -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

A finance world where professionals talk about prices, deals, percentages and numbers under pressure. Listen for how they compare figures and negotiate.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three comparatives you hear (for example, "higher than", "more expensive than") and one sentence with a percentage.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Talking About Numbers and Trends"

A clear episode on saying large numbers and percentages, and on describing prices that increase or decrease using comparatives.

Record a 90-second audio giving a price quote: say the rate in dollars, compare it with the market, offer a discount for a large volume, and predict a percentage increase.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Comparatives and Big Numbers"

A focused lesson on comparative adjectives (cheaper, more expensive, better) and on reading large numbers and percentages aloud. Directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, read these figures aloud: 4,200; 3,900; 8%; 10%. Then build six sentences comparing two prices, one for each verb: increase, decrease, and using cheaper, higher, lower, better.
