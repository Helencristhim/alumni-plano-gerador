Grammar Rule

Comparatives and Percentages

TypeExample
Short adjective (+er than)Our price is cheaper than the market.
Long adjective (more ... than)Coffee is more expensive than last year.
Irregular (good / bad)This rate is better than the last one.
Percentages (by X%)Prices increased by ten percent.

Key idea: use only ONE comparative form. Say "cheaper", never "more cheaper". Big numbers: 4,200 is "four thousand two hundred".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"This price is more cheaper than the market."
"This price is cheaper than the market."

With short adjectives use only cheaper, higher, lower -- never more cheaper.

Grammar Practice

Make the Comparative

"Our price is _____cheaper than the market." (cheap + er)
"Coffee is more _____expensive than last year." (long adjective)
"This rate is _____better than the last one." (good -> ___)
"Prices increased _____by ten percent." (increase ___ X%)
