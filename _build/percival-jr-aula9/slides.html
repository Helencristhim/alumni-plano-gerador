Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Comparatives and superlatives

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Do the orange words compare two things, or show the top of a group?

"Option A is safer than Option B."

"This system is more efficient than the old one."

"The third option is the most reliable."

"Supplier B is better than Supplier A."

Grammar Rule

Comparatives & Superlatives

TypeComparativeSuperlative
short adj (+er / +est)safe → safer thanthe safest
long adj (more / the most)efficient → more efficient thanthe most efficient
irregulargood → better thanthe best

Key idea: with short adjectives add -er and -est; with long adjectives use more and the most. Use than after a comparative and the before a superlative. Irregular: good, better, the best.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Option A is more safe that Option B."
"Option A is safer than Option B."

With short adjectives, add -er (not "more"). And always use than after a comparative, never "that".
