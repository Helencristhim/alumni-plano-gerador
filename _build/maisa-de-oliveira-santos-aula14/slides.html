Chapter 4: The Code

Will & Going To

Two ways to talk about the future

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I will send the report by Friday."

"I am going to check the numbers tonight."

"I will call you as soon as it is ready."

"I am going to finish the budget tomorrow."

UseFormExample
Promise / quick decisionwill + base verbI will send it by Friday.
Plan / intentionam/is/are going to + base verbI am going to check the numbers.
Negativewill not / am not going toI will not be late. I am not going to work late.

Use will for a promise or a decision you make now: "I will help you." Use going to for a plan you thought about before: "I am going to finish the report tomorrow." After both, use the base verb — no "to" after will, and always am/is/are before "going to".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I will to send it by Friday.
I will send it by Friday.
I going to finish the report.
I am going to finish the report.

Do not put to after will: say "I will send", not "I will to send". And always use am / is / are before "going to": "I am going to finish", not "I going to finish".
