Chapter 4: The Language of Advice

How Coaches Advise

Modals of advice and recommendation

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should set a boundary with your inbox."

"You ought to take real downtime each week."

"You had better rest, or you'll burn out."

"You might want to try a mindful walk."

ModalStrengthWhen to use
might want toSoftestA gentle suggestion: "You might want to unwind first."
should / ought toStandard adviceA clear recommendation: "You should prioritize sleep."
had betterStrongest (warning)Advice with a consequence: "You had better rest, or you'll burn out."

All four are followed by the base verb (no to after should / had better).

Meaning Guide

How Strong Is the Advice?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You should to rest more."

✓ RIGHT

✗ WRONG

Dialogue

At the Wellness Retreat

A
Welcome, Helen. So, tell me — how have you been sleeping lately?
H
Honestly, not well. We had two campaigns at once, and I feel close to burnout.
A
I hear that a lot. You should set a clear boundary with your inbox in the evening.
H
That's hard for me. If I stop checking, I feel I might miss something important.
A
I understand. But you had better protect your downtime, or the stress will follow you home.
H
You're right. I need to prioritize my health, not just the deadline.
A
Exactly. And you might want to start small — a ten-minute mindful walk after lunch.
H
I like that. A small habit to recharge — and to build some resilience.
A
That's the spirit. Rest is not a reward for the work — it's part of the work.
