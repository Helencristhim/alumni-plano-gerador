"Protect your downtime like a meeting." (Tempo de descanso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

A coach gives Helen some advice. "You should set a boundary with your inbox," he says. "And you ought to protect your downtime each week." Then, more firmly: "You had better rest, or you'll reach burnout." Finally, gently: "You might want to try a mindful walk to recharge." Different words, different strength — but all of them are advice.

1. Qual conselho é o MAIS forte (um aviso)? / Which advice is the strongest (a warning)?
A "You might want to try a walk."
B "You had better rest, or you'll burn out."
C "You should set a boundary."
2. O que vem DEPOIS de "should"? / What comes after "should"?
A The base verb: "should set".
B "to" + verb: "should to set".
C verb + "-ing": "should setting".
3. Qual conselho é o MAIS suave? / Which advice is the softest?
A "You ought to protect your downtime."
B "You might want to try a mindful walk."
C "You had better rest."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modais de conselho

GRAMMAR

A força do conselho muda com o modal. / The strength of the advice changes with the modal.

ModalForça / StrengthExample / Exemplo
might want tomais suave (gentle suggestion)You might want to unwind first.
should / ought toconselho padrão (advice)You should prioritize sleep.
had bettermais forte, com consequência (warning)You had better rest, or you'll burn out.

should e had better usam o verbo na base, SEM "to": should rest, had better rest. Já ought TO e might want TO levam "to".

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Sem certo ou errado. Pense em inglês, toque no microfone e fale livremente.

Imagine a colleague is close to burnout: no boundaries, no downtime, no sleep. Coach them with at least four pieces of advice, using a different modal each time — a soft one (might want to), a standard one (should / ought to), and a strong one (had better). Use the new words: recharge, unwind, prioritize, resilience.
