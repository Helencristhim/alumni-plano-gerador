Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Summarize -- to give the main points of something in a short way
"Let me summarize what these weeks have meant to me." (resumir)
Overall -- considering everything together; in total
"Overall, I feel more confident speaking English." (no geral / de modo geral)
In general -- usually; most of the time, not in every case
"In general, I used to avoid long conversations." (em geral / geralmente)
Challenge -- a difficult thing that tests your ability
"The biggest challenge for me was talking about the past." (desafio)
Handle -- to deal with a task or problem successfully
"Now I handle long conversations much better." (lidar com / dar conta de)
Smoothly -- in an easy way, without problems or interruptions
"On the whole, things go smoothly now." (tranquilamente / sem problemas)
On the whole -- after considering everything; generally speaking
"On the whole, my classes go smoothly, and I enjoy them." (no conjunto / de modo geral)
Clear up -- to explain something so a doubt or confusion goes away
"My teacher helped me clear up my doubts about grammar." (esclarecer / tirar a dúvida)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Let me summarize my journey in this block. Overall, I am surprised by how much I can say now. In general, I used to give one-word answers, but now I can tell a whole story. I have been practicing every day, and it shows. The biggest challenge for me was the grammar, but my teacher helped me clear up my doubts. On the whole, my classes go smoothly, and I handle difficult words much better. If I keep going, my world in English will keep growing.

1. Which grammar shows a past habit that stopped?
A "used to give one-word answers"
B "have been practicing every day"
C "will keep growing"
2. Which grammar shows something that started in the past and still continues?
A "used to give one-word answers"
B "have been practicing every day"
C "If I keep going"
3. What does the speaker do better now?
A Avoid all conversations.
B Handle difficult words and tell a whole story.
C Give one-word answers.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Bringing the Block Together

GRAMMAR

Esta aula junta as quatro ideias do bloco. Used to = hábito que parou no passado. Have been + -ing = começou no passado e continua até agora. If + presente, will + verbo = situação real e possível no futuro. Could you / Would you mind = pedido educado. / This lesson brings together the block: used to for a past habit that stopped; have been + -ing for something that continues until now; if + present, will + verb for a real future result; Could you for a polite request.

IdeaWhen we use itExample
Could you + verba polite request"Could you clear up this doubt?"
used to + verba past habit that stopped"I used to give one-word answers."
have been + -ingstarted in the past, still going now"I have been practicing every day."
If + present, will + verba real, possible future result"If I keep going, it will grow."

Stage 2: Put the Summary in Order

Order

Ouça primeiro, depois coloque os passos para fazer um bom resumo na ordem correta.

?Mention one challenge you had and how you handle it now.
?Say the topic in one line, so your listener knows what you will talk about.
?On the whole, give your overall feeling, so the summary ends in a positive way.
?In general, give the main idea before the details.
?Clear up anything that might be confusing, with a short example.
