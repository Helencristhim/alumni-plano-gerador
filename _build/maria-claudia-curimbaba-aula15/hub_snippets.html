Present Perfect in Business -- Experience & Results

Tell your professional story like a seasoned executive. Use the present perfect for your experience and the results that still matter today (I have managed this holding for over ten years; we have closed three deals this quarter), the simple past for finished, dated milestones (I led the restructuring in 2019; we opened the office last March), and the passive voice for results (the target was exceeded; the groundwork has been laid). New vocabulary: spearhead, turnaround, seasoned, proven, instrumental, hands-on, well-versed, longstanding, surpass, tenure, yield, groundwork.

Seasoned -- highly experienced and capable after years of practice

Proven -- demonstrated to work or to be reliable over time

Spearhead -- to lead an important project or initiative from the front

Turnaround -- a recovery that moves a business from difficulty to success
"The turnaround we started two years ago has paid off." (recuperação / virada)
Instrumental -- playing a key, decisive role in making something happen
"She was instrumental in closing the supplier deal." (fundamental / decisivo)
Hands-on -- directly involved in the practical, day-to-day work
"I take a hands-on approach with the accounting team." (prático / mão na massa)
Well-versed -- very knowledgeable and skilled in a particular area
"She is well-versed in fiscal and tax matters." (versado / conhecedor)
Longstanding -- having existed or continued for a long time
"We have a longstanding relationship with our US partners." (de longa data)
Surpass -- to go beyond or do better than a goal or expectation
"The results have surpassed every target this year." (superar / ultrapassar)
Tenure -- the period during which someone holds a role or position
"Sales have doubled during her tenure as partner." (mandato / período no cargo)
Yield -- to produce or generate something, such as results or returns
"The new strategy has yielded strong results." (render / gerar)
Groundwork -- the preparatory work that makes later success possible
"The groundwork has been laid for next year." (base / alicerce)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before the annual review, I prepare my story. I have managed this holding for over ten years, and I am a seasoned, hands-on manager, well-versed in the fiscal side. The turnaround I spearheaded two years ago has yielded a proven result: this has been our strongest year. We have closed three major contracts this quarter, and sales have grown since January. For the dated milestones, I switch to the simple past: I led the restructuring in 2019, we opened the Pittsburgh office last March, and we signed the supplier agreement in June. And I keep the passive for the results: every target has been surpassed, and the groundwork has been laid for next year. It is the strongest year of my tenure.

1. How does the writer describe her experience?
A "I have managed this holding for over ten years" -- present perfect, open period.
B "I managed this holding in over ten years."
C "I am managing this holding since ten years."
2. Why is "I led the restructuring in 2019" in the simple past?
A Because it is still happening now.
B Because it is a finished event at a definite time ("in 2019").
C Because the doer is unknown.
3. How is the result reported -- "every target ___"?
A "has been surpassed" -- passive: has been + past participle.
B "surpassed" -- with no "be".
C "is surpassing".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Simple Past & the Passive

GRAMMAR

Use o present perfect (have / has + partícipio) para experiência e resultados num período ainda ABERTO ("I have managed it for ten years", "we have closed three deals this quarter") -- sinais: for, since, this quarter, ever, already, so far. Use o simple past (verbo + -ed / irregular) para eventos TERMINADOS com tempo definido ("I led the restructuring in 2019", "we opened the office last March") -- sinais: in 2019, last March, ago, yesterday. Para relatar um resultado, use a voz passiva (be / was / have been + partícipio): "the target was exceeded", "the groundwork has been laid". / Present perfect for an open period, simple past for a finished time, the passive for a result.

Tense / functionFormExample
Present perfect (experience/result)have / has + past participle"I have managed it for ten years"
Simple past (finished, dated)verb + -ed / irregular"I led the restructuring in 2019"
Signal wordsfor / since vs in 2019 / last March"sales have grown since January"
Report a result (passive)be / was / have been + participle"the target was exceeded"

Key point / Ponto-chave: no momento em que você adiciona um tempo definido (in 2019, last March), troque para o simple past -- "I led it in 2019", nunca "I have led it in 2019". E a passiva SEMPRE precisa do BE: "the target was exceeded", "the groundwork has been laid", nunca "the target exceeded". / A definite past time takes the simple past, and the passive always needs "be".
