Present Perfect in Business -- Experience & Results

Present your track record like a leader: present perfect for experience and results (I have worked with US partners; we have closed three deals this quarter) vs simple past for finished, dated events (I led the restructuring in 2019). New vocabulary: achievement, milestone, track record, expertise, accomplish, promotion, mentor, breakthrough, contribution, launch.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Achievement -- a result you have worked hard for and completed successfully
"Growing the holding for ten years is her greatest achievement." (Conquista)
Milestone -- an important point or stage that marks real progress
"Our first export deal was a major milestone." (Marco)
Track record -- your history of past results and performance over time
"She has a strong track record of closing deals." (Histórico de resultados)
Expertise -- a high level of skill and knowledge in a particular field
"Her expertise is in finance and family governance." (Expertise / domínio)
Accomplish -- to finish something successfully, especially after effort
"We have accomplished every goal we set this year." (Realizar / cumprir)
Promotion -- a move to a higher, more senior position at work
"He earned a promotion to regional director." (Promoção)
Mentor -- an experienced person who guides and advises others
"She has mentored a strong management team." (Mentor / orientar)
Breakthrough -- an important success after a difficult period
"The new contract was a real breakthrough for us." (Avanço decisivo)
Contribution -- what you add or give to help reach a result
"Her contribution to the merger was decisive." (Contribuição)
Launch -- to start or introduce something new, like a product or plan
"We have launched two new product lines this year." (Lançar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Maria Claudia has run the family holding for fifteen years, and her track record speaks for itself. She has built three subsidiaries and has mentored a strong team -- her greatest achievement. This quarter, the holding has closed three new deals and has launched a product line. There was one turning point: in 2019, she led a difficult restructuring. That project was her biggest breakthrough. Notice the rule: she uses the present perfect (has built, has closed) for experience and open results, and the simple past (led, was) for the finished, dated event.

2. Why does the text say "in 2019, she led a difficult restructuring"?
A Because the project is still ongoing.
B It is a finished, dated event, so it takes the simple past.
C Because 2019 is in the future.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Simple Past

GRAMMAR

Use o present perfect (have/has + particípio) para EXPERIÊNCIA e RESULTADOS num período ainda aberto (this year, this quarter); use o simple past para um evento TERMINADO e datado (in 2019, last year). / Use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) for experience and results in an open period; use the simple past for a finished, dated event.

UsePatternExample
Experience (no exact time)have/has + past participle"I have worked with US partners"
Result in an open periodhave/has + past participle"we have closed three deals this quarter"
Finished, dated eventverb + -ed / irregular past"I led the restructuring in 2019"
Time markersfor, since, this year vs. in 2019, last year, ago"since 2010" (perfect) vs. "in 2010" (past)

Key point / Ponto-chave: teste rápido -- se você pode adicionar uma data terminada (in 2019, last year), use o simple past. Se o tempo é aberto ou não dito, use o present perfect. NUNCA junte os dois: "I have led the project in 2019" → "I led the project in 2019". / Quick test: if you can add a finished date, use the simple past; if the time is open, use the present perfect.
