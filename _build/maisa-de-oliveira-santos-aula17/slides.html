Chapter 3: The Code

Small Talk Questions

How was...? · What is ... like? · Have you been to...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"How was your flight?"

"What is the weather like?"

"Have you been to New York?"

"How was your weekend?"

StructureUse forExample
How was your + noun?ask about the pastHow was your weekend?
What is + noun + like?ask for a descriptionWhat is the weather like?
Have you been to + place?ask about experienceHave you been to New York?
By the way, ...?add a new light topicBy the way, do you like coffee?

Use How was your...? for the past, What is ... like? for a description, and Have you been to...? for experience. Never say "How is it like" — always "What is it like".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

How is the weather like?
What is the weather like?
How was your weekend was?
How was your weekend?

To describe something, say What is ... like?, never "How is ... like?". And with How was your...? use "was" only once — never repeat it at the end.

Chapter 4: A Real Conversation

Breaking the Ice with David

the flight, the weather, the weekend

Dialogue

Welcome to São Paulo

M
Hi David! Welcome to São Paulo. How was your flight?
D
Hi Maísa! Thank you. The flight was long, and I am a little tired.
M
I understand. What is the weather like in New York?
D
It is cold and rainy there. Here it is warm and sunny. I like it!
M
Yes, today is beautiful. How was your weekend?
D
It was great. I visited a museum. By the way, have you been to New York?
M
Not yet, but I want to go. Was there a lot of traffic from the airport?
D
A little, but it was fine. Thank you for the warm welcome!
