Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Small Talk That Works

Puxar conversa leve em inglês com o David na visita dele a São Paulo: the weather, the weekend, sunny, rainy, traffic, a flight, tired, by the way. Gramática: perguntas de small talk — How was your weekend? / What is the weather like? / Have you been to...? (A2). Missão: quebrar o gelo e manter uma conversa leve com o David do começo ao fim.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

The weather -- if it is sunny, rainy, hot or cold
"What is the weather like today?" (O tempo / clima)
The weekend -- Saturday and Sunday
"How was your weekend?" (O fim de semana)
Sunny -- with a lot of sun
"Today it is warm and sunny." (Ensolarado)
Rainy -- with a lot of rain
"It is cold and rainy in New York." (Chuvoso)
Traffic -- many cars on the road
"There was a lot of traffic this morning." (O trânsito)
A flight -- a trip on an airplane
"How was your flight to Brazil?" (Um voo)
Tired -- not full of energy; you want to rest
"I am a little tired today." (Cansado)
By the way -- a phrase to add a new, light topic
"By the way, do you like coffee?" (A propósito)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

David arrives in São Paulo, and Maísa welcomes him. "Welcome! How was your flight?" she asks. "It was long, and I am a little tired," David says. Maísa smiles. "What is the weather like in New York?" she asks. "It is cold and rainy there. Here it is warm and sunny. I like it!" David answers. "How was your weekend?" Maísa asks. "It was great. I visited a museum," David says. "By the way, have you been to New York?" Maísa says, "Not yet, but I want to go. Was there a lot of traffic from the airport?" David says it was fine. They talk for a few minutes, and the conversation is easy and friendly.

1. Como a Maísa pergunta sobre a viagem do David? / How does Maísa ask about David's trip?
A "How was your flight?"
B "How is your flight like?"
C "How your flight was?"
2. Como está o tempo em Nova York? / How is the weather in New York?
A "It is warm and sunny."
B "It is cold and rainy."
C "There is a lot of traffic."
3. Como a Maísa muda para um novo assunto leve? / How does Maísa change to a new light topic?
A "By the way is you tired?"
B "By the way, have you been to New York?"
C "What is New York like was?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Small Talk Questions

GRAMMAR

Para puxar conversa leve, use perguntas prontas: How was your...? (Como foi o/a...?) para algo no passado, What is ... like? (Como é o/a...?) para descrever, e Have you been to...? (Você já foi a...?) para experiência. / To make small talk, use ready-made questions: How was your...? for something in the past, What is ... like? to describe, and Have you been to...? for experience.

Estrutura / StructureUso / UseExemplo / Example
How was your + noun?perguntar sobre o passado / ask about the pastHow was your weekend?
What is + noun + like?pedir uma descrição / ask for a descriptionWhat is the weather like?
Have you been to + place?perguntar sobre experiência / ask about experienceHave you been to New York?
By the way, ...?trocar de assunto leve / add a new light topicBy the way, do you like coffee?

Atenção: para descrever, diga What is ... like?, nunca "How is ... like?". Com How was your...? nunca repita o verbo no fim: diga "How was your weekend?", nunca "How was your weekend was?". Have you been to...? pergunta se a pessoa já foi a um lugar alguma vez.

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Sem certo ou errado. Pense em inglês, toque no microfone e fale livremente.

Imagine: o David chega em São Paulo e você quer puxar conversa leve. Em 4-5 frases, quebre o gelo: pergunte sobre o voo, fale do tempo em São Paulo, pergunte sobre o fim de semana dele e, com "by the way", pergunte se ele já foi ao Brasil. Use: How was your flight?, What is the weather like?, sunny, tired, by the way, have you been to.
