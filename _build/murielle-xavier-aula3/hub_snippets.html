Looking Back -- Past Experiences & Career Milestones

Telling your professional story: past roles, projects, and the big moments of your career. New words: milestone, achievement, promotion, merger, restructuring, launch, lead a project, previous, spearhead, turnaround, downsizing. Grammar: past simple vs past continuous for narrating milestones, plus present perfect vs past simple for talking about experience.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Milestone -- an important moment or stage in a career (marco)
"Leading that merger was a real milestone for me."
Achievement -- a good result you reach through effort and hard work
"Keeping the whole team together was my biggest achievement."
Promotion -- a move to a higher, more senior position (promoção)
"I have had two promotions since I joined the company."
Merger -- when two companies join to become one (fusão)
"While the merger was happening, we protected our key people."
Restructuring -- a major reorganization of how a company works
"I was leading the restructuring when the results improved."
Launch -- to start a new program, product, or project
"We launched a new onboarding program across the region."
Lead a project -- to guide and be in charge of a project
"I have led three big projects over the last decade."
Previous -- earlier; the one that came before the current one
"In my previous role, I was managing recruitment for the region."
Spearhead -- to lead an important new effort or initiative (encabeçar)
"I spearheaded the new diversity program last year."
Turnaround -- a change from loss or failure to success (recuperação)
"That difficult year became a genuine turnaround for us."
Downsizing -- reducing the number of employees to cut costs (enxugamento)
"During the downsizing, we supported everyone who left."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

"I have worked in HR for fifteen years, and I have led three major restructurings. My career started in a small consultancy. While I was learning the basics, my manager gave me my first real project. In 2018, while the company was going through a merger, I spearheaded the downsizing. It was the hardest year of my career. Last year, I led the biggest turnaround of all: while the leadership team was debating the numbers, we kept our best people and morale slowly improved. I have never forgotten that achievement."

1. "While the company was going through a merger, I spearheaded the downsizing." Why is "was going" used here?
A It is past continuous: a longer background action while a shorter action happened.
B It is present simple: a habit that happens every day.
C It is a plural noun.
2. "I have worked in HR for fifteen years." Why present perfect and not past simple?
A Because the action finished long ago and has no link to now.
B Because the experience started in the past and continues up to now.
C Because it is a question about the future.
3. Which sentence correctly contrasts experience with a finished moment?
A "I have led the biggest turnaround last year."
B "I have led three turnarounds. Last year I led the biggest one."
C "I have led three turnarounds. Last year I have led the biggest one."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs Past Continuous (and Present Perfect for experience)

GRAMMAR

Use o past continuous para a ação longa de fundo e o past simple para a ação curta que aconteceu no meio. Use o present perfect para experiência sem tempo definido, e o past simple quando você diz QUANDO. (Background action vs finished action; experience vs specific moment.)

FormUse (PT)Example
was/were + verb-ingAção longa de fundo / background actionWhile I was leading the restructuring...
past simple (interruption)Ação curta no meio / shorter action...we merged two teams.
have/has + past participleExperiência, sem quando / experienceI have led three restructurings.
past simple + time markerMomento definido / finished, WHEN is saidLast year I led the biggest one.
while / when / for / everMarcadores: While the merger was happening, I spearheaded the downsizing. / Have you ever led a turnaround?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação. (Choose the best answer for each situation.)

A colleague asks: "How long have you worked in HR?" You answer:
A "I worked in HR for fifteen years." (the experience is still going)
B "I have worked in HR for fifteen years."
C "I am working in HR since fifteen years."
You describe a short action inside a longer one. The correct sentence is:
A "While I led the restructuring, we merged two teams."
B "While I was leading the restructuring, we merged two teams."
C "While I was leading the restructuring, we merge two teams."
You talk about your experience, without saying exactly when. You say:
A "I have spearheaded three downsizings."
B "I have spearheaded three downsizings in 2019."
C "I was spearheading three downsizings."
You want to say when one specific milestone happened. You say:
A "I have got my promotion two years ago."
B "I got my promotion two years ago."
C "I was getting my promotion two years ago."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo a pergunta abaixo. Não existe resposta certa ou errada. (Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer.)

Tell the story of your career so far. Where and when did you start? Which projects have you led or spearheaded? Describe one hard moment -- a merger, a downsizing, or a turnaround -- using "while" + past continuous (for example: "While I was leading the restructuring, ..."). Then say what you have learned. Use at least four new words. Take your time.
