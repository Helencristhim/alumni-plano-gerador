Lesson 03 -- Pre-class

Looking Back -- Past Experiences & Career Milestones

Telling your professional story: past roles, projects, and the big moments of your career. New words: milestone, achievement, promotion, merger, restructuring, launch, lead a project, previous. Grammar: past simple (regular -ed + irregular verbs) with did/didn't.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Milestone -- an important moment or achievement in a career (marco)
"My first leadership role was a real milestone."
Achievement -- a good result you reached through effort (conquista)
"Keeping the team together was my biggest achievement."
Promotion -- a move to a higher, more senior position (promoção)
"I got a promotion to manager in 2019."
Merger -- when two companies join to become one (fusão)
"The merger changed everything for our department."
Restructuring -- a major reorganization of a company (reestruturação)
"During the restructuring, we kept our key people."
Launch -- to start a new program, product, or project (lançar)
"We launched a new onboarding program last year."
Lead a project -- to guide and be in charge of a project (liderar um projeto)
"I led a project to open a new factory."
Previous -- earlier; the one before the current one (anterior)
"In my previous role, I worked in recruitment."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Murielle started her career in HR fifteen years ago. Her previous role was in a small consultancy. In 2014, she led her first big project: the team launched a new recruitment program and hired forty people in three months. It was a real milestone. A few years later, the company went through a merger, and there was a difficult restructuring. Murielle did not leave. She worked hard, kept her best people, and finally got a promotion. She joined Minerva Foods and never looked back.

1. Why do we write "started", "launched", and "worked" with -ed?
A Because they are happening right now.
B Because they are regular verbs in the past simple.
C Because they are plural nouns.
2. "The company went through a merger." The word "went" is:
A The irregular past form of "go".
B The present form of "go".
C A regular verb with -ed.
3. Which negative sentence is correct in the past simple?
A "She not left the company."
B "She did not leave the company."
C "She did not left the company."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Use o past simple para ações terminadas no passado. Verbos regulares ganham -ed; verbos irregulares mudam de forma. (Use the past simple for finished past actions.)

FormUse (PT)Example
verb + edRegulares no passado / regular pastI started / we launched
irregular formVerbos que mudam / irregular verbsgo → went, be → was/were, lead → led, get → got
did not (didn't) + base verbNegativa / negativeI didn't stay there.
Did + subject + base verbPergunta / questionDid you lead the project?
ago / last year / in 2018 / whenMarcadores de tempo no passado: I started fifteen years ago. / We launched it last year.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "I start my career in 2010."
B "I started my career in 2010."
C "I am started my career in 2010."
You want to ask about a past project. The correct question is:
A "Did you led a big project?"
B "Did you lead a big project?"
C "You leaded a big project?"
You want to say you did not stay at a company. You say:
A "I didn't stay there for long."
B "I didn't stayed there for long."
C "I not stayed there for long."
You describe a big career moment. You say:
A "It was a real milestone, I get a promotion."
