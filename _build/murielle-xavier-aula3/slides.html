Chapter 3

Past & Continuous

Simple, continuous, and perfect: telling a story with all three

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"While I was leading the merger, we merged two teams."

"While the team was debating, morale was falling."

"I have led three restructurings."

"Last year I led the biggest one."

FormUseExample
was/were + verb-ingLonger background actionWhile I was leading the merger...
past simpleShorter action inside it...we merged two teams.
have/has + past participleExperience, no time saidI have led three restructurings.
past simple + time markerFinished moment, WHEN is saidLast year I led the biggest one.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"While I led the merger, we merged two teams."

✓ RIGHT

"While I was leading the merger, we merged two teams."

✗ WRONG

"I have led the biggest one last year."

✓ RIGHT

"I led the biggest one last year."

✗ WRONG

"I have spearheaded three programs in 2019."

✓ RIGHT

"I spearheaded three programs in 2019."

Use the past continuous for the longer background action. Use the past simple, not the present perfect, whenever you say exactly when it happened.

Chapter 4

Telling Your Story

Sharing your career journey with a colleague

Dialogue

Murielle's Career Story

L
Murielle, I love a good career story. How long have you worked in HR?
M
I have worked in HR for fifteen years. My previous role was in a small consultancy.
L
Nice. What has been your biggest milestone?
M
While I was leading a recruitment project, we launched a new program and hired forty people.
L
Impressive. Were you working there during the merger?
M
Yes. While the company was going through the merger, I spearheaded the restructuring.
L
That sounds hard. Have you ever regretted it?
M
No, I haven't. It was tough, but I have never forgotten what it taught me.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can talk about my past roles and career milestones.
I can use the past continuous for background actions: "While I was leading the merger, we merged two teams."
I can use the present perfect for experience: "I have led three restructurings."
I can switch to the past simple with a time marker: "Last year I led the biggest one."
I can use the new words: milestone, achievement, promotion, merger, restructuring, launch, previous, spearhead, turnaround, downsizing.
