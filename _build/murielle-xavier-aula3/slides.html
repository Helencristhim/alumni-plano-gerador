Chapter 3

The Past Simple

Talking about finished actions in the past

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I started in HR fifteen years ago."

"We launched a new program."

"The company went through a merger."

"Did you lead the project?"

FormUseExample
Regular verb + edMost past verbsI started / we launched
Irregular verb (new form)Strong verbs changego → went, be → was/were, lead → led
did not (didn't) + base verbNegativeI didn't stay there.
Did + subject + base verbQuestionDid you lead the project?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I goed to the London office."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to the London office."

✗ WRONG

"Did you launched the program?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you launch the program?"

✗ WRONG

"We didn't hired anyone."

✓ RIGHT

"We didn't hire anyone."

Learn the irregular verbs by heart. After "did" and "didn't", always use the base verb.

Chapter 4

Telling Your Story

Sharing your career journey with a colleague

Dialogue

Murielle's Career Story

L
Murielle, I love career stories. How did you start in HR?
M
I started fifteen years ago. My previous role was in a small consultancy.
L
Nice. What was your first big milestone?
M
I led a recruitment project for a new factory. We hired forty people in three months.
L
Impressive. Did you stay at that company for long?
M
No, I didn't. After a merger, I moved to Minerva and got a promotion.
L
So the merger changed everything?
M
Yes, it did. It was hard, but I learned a lot and I never looked back.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Where was Murielle's previous role?
In a small consultancy.
2. How many people did the team hire, and how fast?
Forty people in three months.
3. What happened after the merger?
She moved to Minerva and got a promotion.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can talk about my past roles and career milestones.
I can use regular past verbs with -ed: started, launched, worked, moved.
I can use irregular past verbs: was, were, had, went, made, took, led, got.
I can make questions and negatives with did and didn't.
I can use the new words: milestone, achievement, promotion, merger, restructuring, launch, previous.
