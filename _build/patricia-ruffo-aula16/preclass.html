Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Synopsis -- a short summary of a paper, placed at the start
"The synopsis must state the aim, methods and results." (Resumo / sínteses)
Premise -- the main idea or assumption a study is built on
"The premise of the study is clearly argued." (Premissa)
Results -- the data and outcomes that a study found
"The results show a significant improvement." (Resultados)
Referee -- an expert who reviews a paper and writes a report
"Two referees will read the paper and send comments." (Revisor / parecerista)
Citation -- a reference that points to the source of an idea
"Add a citation for each study you mention." (Citação / referência)
Manuscript -- the written version of a paper sent to a journal
"She finished the manuscript and sent it for review." (Manuscrito / artigo)
Submission -- the act of sending a paper to a journal for review
"Check the citations before your next submission." (Submissão / envio)
Journal -- an academic publication that prints peer-reviewed research
"She submitted the paper to a clinical nutrition journal." (Revista científica / periódico)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Patricia is preparing her first paper for an international journal. She writes the manuscript in a formal, academic register: the trial was conducted over six months, and the markers were measured monthly. At the top she places a short synopsis that states the premise, the methods, the main results and the conclusion. For every earlier study she mentions, she adds a citation so readers can find the source. When her submission is complete, the editor sends the paper to two referees, who write a report. Their comments suggest the work is reliable, and the findings suggest the approach is effective.

1. What does the synopsis state?
A Only the title and the author's name.
B The premise, the methods, the results and the conclusion.
C A list of the patients.
2. Why does Patricia add a citation for each earlier study?
A So readers can find the original source.
B To make the paper longer.
C Because the journal pays per citation.
3. Why does she write "the trial was conducted" instead of "we did the trial"?
A Because the passive is wrong here.
C Because it is shorter.
B Because the academic register prefers the passive for methods.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The academic register

GRAMMAR

No artigo científico, a linguagem é mais formal e precisa que na fala. / In a paper, the language is more formal and precise than in speech.

Principle / PrincípioSpoken / FalaAcademic / Artigo
Verbos formais"We got results.""The study obtained results."
Passiva nos métodos"We did a test.""A test was conducted."
Cauteloso e preciso"This proves it works.""The findings suggest it is effective."
Sem palavras casuais"The numbers were huge.""The values were significantly higher."

Key point / Ponto-chave: O registro acadêmico troca verbos casuais (got, did) por formais (obtained, conducted), usa a voz passiva para descrever métodos (was conducted, were measured) e é cauteloso e preciso (suggest, indicate, nunca prove).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You describe your methods in a paper. You write:
A "A six-month trial was conducted with 240 patients."
B "We just did a thing for six months on people."
C "Trial six month patients we got."
You report a positive outcome cautiously. You write:
A "This proves for sure the diet works."
B "These findings suggest the diet is effective."
C "The diet is definitely the best one ever."
You explain the next step after writing the paper. You say:
A "I throw the manuscript and wait the magazine."
B "I make my submission to the journal, and then referees review it."
C "Paper finish so done now publish."
