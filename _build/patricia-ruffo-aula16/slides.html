Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Present perfect for findings, past simple for methods

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"We recruited ninety patients and measured their recovery." (method)

"The study has shown a strong association." (finding)

"We analyzed the data over six months." (method)

"We have found that the effect is significant." (finding)

Grammar Rule

Methods vs Findings

PartTenseExample
Method (finished)Past simple"We measured recovery over six months."
Finding (still relevant)Present perfect"The study has shown a strong effect."
Finding (we)have + past participle"We have found a clear association."
Negative findinghave not + past participle"We have not found any side effects."

Key idea: use the past simple for finished methods (we recruited, we measured) and the present perfect (has / have + past participle) for findings that are new and still matter (the study has shown, we have found).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The study has showed a strong effect."
"The study has shown a strong effect."

For a current finding, use the present perfect with the correct past participle: has shown, have found -- never has showed.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Tense

"We _____measured recovery over six months." (measure, method / past simple)
"The study _____has shown a strong association." (show, finding / present perfect)
"We _____recruited ninety patients for the trial." (recruit, method / past simple)
"We _____have found that the effect is significant." (find, finding / present perfect)

Comprehension

About the Abstract

1. What does the first sentence of the abstract give?

The background and the gap: little is known about how the supplement affects recovery in older patients.

2. Which tense is used for the methods, and which for the findings?

Past simple for the methods (we recruited, we measured); present perfect for the findings (the study has shown, we have found).

3. What does Patricia say about register?

There is no "I think" and no "maybe": we write "we found", "the data show", "the results suggest", and every claim is backed by the findings.

To use the present perfect for the findings: change "we found a strong association" to "we have found a strong association", while keeping the past simple for the methods.

Two more citations in the introduction, added to the reference list, and a sharper hypothesis sentence.
