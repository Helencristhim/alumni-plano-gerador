Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The academic register: formal, precise, often passive

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What makes the orange version sound academic?

Spoken: "We got good results." → "The study obtained positive results."

Spoken: "We did a test on the patients." → "A test was conducted on the patients."

Spoken: "The numbers were really big." → "The values were significantly higher."

Spoken: "This shows the diet works." → "These findings suggest that the diet is effective."

Grammar Rule

The Academic Register

PrincipleSpokenAcademic
Formal verbs"We got results.""The study obtained results."
Passive for methods"We did a test.""A test was conducted."
Precise, cautious"This proves it works.""These findings suggest it is effective."
No casual words"The numbers were huge.""The values were significantly higher."

Key idea: the academic register replaces casual verbs (got, did) with formal ones (obtained, conducted), uses the passive voice to describe methods, and stays cautious and precise (suggest, indicate, not "prove").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"In our study we got really good numbers."
"The study obtained significant results."

In a paper, replace casual verbs like got with formal ones like obtained, and prefer the passive for methods.

Grammar Practice

Make it Academic

"We did the experiment in March." → "The experiment _____was conducted in March." (passive for methods)
"We got these numbers." → "These results _____were obtained from the data." (formal verb, passive)
"This proves the diet works." → "These findings _____suggest the diet is effective." (cautious, precise)
"We measured the levels twice a day." → "The levels _____were measured twice a day." (passive for methods)
