Lesson 16 -- Abstracts & Papers

Documentary
The Bleeding Edge (Netflix) -- any segment on published medical research

Watch how medical findings move from a study to a published paper, and how peer review and citations shape what reaches patients.

Watch with English subtitles. Note one finding and rewrite it twice: once as a method (past simple) and once as a finding (present perfect).

Podcast
Nature Podcast -- any episode discussing a new paper

Researchers explain a recent paper in clear English: the hypothesis, the methods, the findings and what peer review added before publication.

Record a 2-minute abstract of the paper they discuss: state the hypothesis, the methods in the past simple, and the findings in the present perfect.

YouTube
"How to write a scientific abstract" -- any clear academic-writing tutorial

A short guide to the shape of an abstract: background, hypothesis, methods, findings and conclusion, with the tense choices that go with each part.

After watching, draft a six-sentence abstract for one of your own studies, using abstract, hypothesis, findings, citation, manuscript and journal.
