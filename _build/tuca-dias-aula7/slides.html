Chapter 3 -- Asking Questions

Grammar Discovery

Question forms and the past simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What is the order of the words? Where does "did" go, and what form is the main verb?

"Where did you grow up?"

"What did your family celebrate?"

"When did you move abroad?"

"Did you like the food?"

Grammar Rule

Past Simple Questions

TypeOrderExample
Wh- questionWh-word + did + subject + base verbWhere did you grow up?
Yes / No questionDid + subject + base verbDid you like the food?
Key point"did" carries the past -- main verb stays basego (not went), grow (not grew)
Wh-wordsto open a questionWhat, Where, When, Why, How

Key idea: for past questions, build them in order: question word, then "did", then the subject, then the base verb. The word "did" already shows the past, so never put the main verb in the past too.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Where you grew up? And did you went to school there?"
"Where did you grow up? And did you go to school there?"

Always use did in past questions, and keep the main verb in the base form (grow, go), because "did" already shows the past.
