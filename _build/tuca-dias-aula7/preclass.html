Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Custom -- a usual way of doing something in a culture or group
"Taking off your shoes at the door is a common custom." (Costume)
Tradition -- a belief or action passed down through generations
"A family dinner on Sundays is an old tradition." (Tradição)
Heritage -- the history and traditions a group receives from the past
"She is proud of her Japanese heritage." (Herança)
Background -- where a person comes from; their family and origins
"He comes from a farming background, like me." (Origem)
Belief -- something you accept as true or feel is real
"Respect for elders is an important belief there." (Crença)
Foreigner -- a person from another country
"As a foreigner, I was curious about local customs." (Estrangeiro)
Native -- belonging to a place because you were born there
"He is a native of Kyoto and speaks the local dialect." (Nativo)
Lifestyle -- the way a person lives, including their habits and choices
"Living on a coffee farm gives me a calm lifestyle." (Estilo de vida)
Etiquette -- the polite rules of behavior in a society or situation
"Table etiquette is different in every country." (Etiqueta)
Curious -- wanting to learn or know more about something
"I am very curious about other cultures." (Curioso)
Greeting -- what you say or do when you meet someone
"A bow is a common greeting in Japan." (Saudação)
Misunderstanding -- a failure to understand something correctly
"It was just a small misunderstanding about the custom." (Mal-entendido)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca was very curious about other cultures, so she interviewed a foreigner named Kenji about his background. She prepared her questions carefully. "Where did you grow up?" she asked. "I grew up in Kyoto," he answered. "What traditions did your family celebrate?" He told her about the festivals and the etiquette of the tea ceremony, part of his heritage. To build a good past question, Tuca remembered the order: question word, then did, then the subject, then the base verb. The word "did" already shows the past, so the main verb stays in its base form: "Did you like living abroad?", not "Did you liked."

1. Which question is built correctly?
A "Where did you grow up?"
B "Where you grew up?"
C "Where did you grew up?"
2. Why does the main verb stay in the base form?
A because the question is about the future
B because "did" already shows the past
C because base verbs are always polite
3. What is the correct order of a past Wh- question?
A question word + did + subject + base verb
B subject + did + question word + verb
C did + question word + verb + subject

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple Questions

GRAMMAR

Usado para perguntar sobre o passado e entrevistar alguém. / Used to ask about the past and interview someone.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Wh- questionWh-word + did + sujeito + verbo base / asks for informationWhere did you grow up?
Yes / No questionDid + sujeito + verbo base / sim ou nãoDid you like the food?
Verbo principalforma base, porque "did" já marca o passadogo (não went), grow (não grew)
Wh-wordspara abrir a perguntaWhat, Where, When, Why, How

Key point / Ponto-chave: Monte a pergunta na ordem: question word + did + subject + base verb. O "did" já mostra o passado, então o verbo principal fica na forma base ("Did you go?", nunca "Did you went?").
