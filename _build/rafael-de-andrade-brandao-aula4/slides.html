Present Continuous and Questions -- talking about right now

"Are they networking right now?"

Grammar Rule

Present Continuous

FormExample
am / is / are + verb-ingI am meeting a new contact.
Negative: is / are not + verb-ingShe is not working today.
Yes / No question: be + subject + verb-ingAre you enjoying the event?
Wh- question: Wh + be + subject + verb-ingWhat are you doing this weekend?

Key idea: We use the present continuous for actions happening now. Always use the verb be (am, is, are) before the verb with -ing. In questions, be comes before the subject.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you are doing this weekend?"
"What are you doing this weekend?"

In a question, the verb be (am, is, are) comes before the subject. And never forget the -ing on the main verb.
