Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The present perfect: have or has plus the past participle

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we talk about progress up to now?

"I have improved a lot since Lesson 1." (progress up to now)

"My confidence has grown every week." (a change up to now)

"I have studied English for six months." (from past to now)

"Last month I started Block 1." (a finished moment)

Grammar Rule

Talking About Progress

UseFormExample
Result up to nowhave / has + past participleI have improved a lot.
How long (duration)for + a periodI have studied for six months.
Starting pointsince + a point in timeI have studied since March.
A questionHow long have you + participle?How long have you studied English?
A finished momentsimple past + a finished timeLast month I started Block 1.

Key idea: use the present perfect for progress with no exact time. Use for with a period (for six months) and since with a starting point (since March). Use the simple past when the time is finished (last month, in Lesson 1).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have learn a lot."
"I have learned a lot."

After have or has, always use the past participle (learned, improved, grown), not the base verb. The participle is the form you reviewed in Lesson 4.
