Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the end of Block 1, Rubens sits down for his first review. He looks back and reflects on his progress. "I have studied English for six months," he thinks, "and I have improved a lot since Lesson 1." His confidence has grown every week, and his biggest strength is that he is not afraid to speak. Of course, he still has a weakness: he has struggled with the past tense, and he sometimes makes a small mistake. His teacher gives honest feedback, so he reviews his notes a little every day. "Last week I finished Lesson 7," he says, "and now I have a clear goal: to speak with confidence at a medical congress." His progress is slow and steady, but it is real.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect for Progress

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar do seu progresso ate agora. / Used to talk about your progress up to now.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
have / has + past participleResultado ate agora / a result up to nowI have improved a lot.
for + a periodDuracao / how longI have studied for six months.
since + a point in timePonto de inicio / a starting pointI have studied since March.
How long have you + participle?Pergunta / a questionHow long have you studied?
simple past + finished timeMomento terminado / a finished momentLast month I started Block 1.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de have / has, use o particicio passado (improved, grown, learned). Use for com um periodo (for six months) e since com um ponto de inicio (since March). Use o passado simples quando o tempo terminou (last month).

Lesson 8 -- Block 1 Review

Series
The Last Dance -- any episode (sports documentary)

Athletes and coaches reflect on their journey: they describe their progress, their strengths and weaknesses, and the mistakes they have learned from. Listen for the present perfect (we have improved, I have learned) used to talk about a career up to now.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three present perfect sentences from the episode, then describe your own English progress the same way using have or has plus a past participle.

Podcast
The Tim Ferriss Show -- any guest interview

Guests reflect on how they have improved over the years, the goals they have set, and the feedback that helped them grow. A great model for talking about progress, strengths, and weaknesses in natural English.

Note three sentences where the guest talks about progress or a goal. Then record a short reflection on your own learning, using progress, feedback, and goal at least once each.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "Present Perfect vs Past Simple"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: the present perfect for progress up to now (I have improved), for and since, and how it is different from the simple past for finished moments (last week I started).

After watching, write five sentences about your English: three with the present perfect and for or since, and two in the simple past with a finished time. Use progress, strength, and goal at least once each.
