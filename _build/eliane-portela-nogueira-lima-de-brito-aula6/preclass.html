Lesson 06 -- Pre-class

In the Room Where It Happens -- Running and Participating in Business Meetings

Everything you need to run and to join a business meeting entirely in English. The grammar focus is polite, indirect questions for professional contexts -- "I was wondering if you could...", "Could you tell me how...", "Do you know whether...", "Would you mind explaining...". New words: facilitate, minutes, consensus, align, weigh in, table, circle back, touch base, cross-functional, breakout, adjourn, on the same page.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it. / Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo.

Facilitate -- to lead a meeting and help the discussion run smoothly / conduzir a reunião e facilitar a discussão
"I will facilitate today's meeting and keep us on time."
Minutes -- the written record of what was decided in a meeting / a ata da reunião
"I will send the minutes right after the call."
Consensus -- general agreement among a group of people / consenso, acordo geral
"We reached a consensus on the training plan."
Align -- to bring people to the same understanding or goal / alinhar, deixar todos na mesma direção
"We need to align the finance and clinical teams."
Weigh in -- to give your opinion on a topic under discussion / dar sua opinião, opinar
"Would you like to weigh in on the budget?"
Table -- to postpone a topic to a later meeting (American English) / adiar um assunto para depois
"Let's table that point and come back to it."
Circle back -- to return to a topic later / voltar a um assunto mais tarde
"Let's circle back to this next week."
Touch base -- to make brief contact to share a quick update / fazer um contato rápido para atualizar
"Let's touch base tomorrow before the call."
Cross-functional -- involving people from several different departments / interfuncional, entre várias áreas
"This is a cross-functional project with four teams."
Breakout -- a small group that splits off from the main meeting / sessão ou sala de grupo menor
"The details will go to a breakout session."
Adjourn -- to formally end a meeting / encerrar formalmente a reunião
"Let's adjourn and continue on Friday."
On the same page -- in agreement, sharing the same understanding / na mesma página, de acordo
"Before we finish, are we all on the same page?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane joins a cross-functional kickoff meeting for her new clinic. Instead of asking blunt questions, she keeps them polite and indirect. Could you tell me how the new process works? she asks the facilitator. A little later she wants some data, so she says, I was wondering if you could send me the minutes. When the budget comes up, she does not say "Is it approved?"; she asks, Do you know whether the budget is approved? And when she is unsure who leads the training, she asks, Would you mind explaining who will facilitate the breakout session? Notice that after each polite opener the word order stays normal -- "how the process works", not "how does the process work" -- and there is no extra "do".

1. Why does Eliane use indirect questions in the meeting?
A To sound polite and professional instead of blunt.
B To make the meeting longer.
C To avoid getting any answer at all.
2. What happens to the word order after a polite opener?
A The verb goes before the subject, as in a direct question.
B It stays normal: subject first, then verb (the process works).
C You must add "do" or "does" before the verb.
3. Which word does Eliane use for a yes/no question?
A "how"
B "whether"
C "who"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect & Polite Questions

GRAMMAR

Indirect questions begin with a polite opener, then keep normal word order (subject + verb) and drop the auxiliary "do". Use "if" or "whether" for yes/no questions. / Perguntas indiretas começam com uma abertura educada e depois mantêm a ordem normal (sujeito + verbo), sem o auxiliar "do". Use "if" ou "whether" para perguntas de sim/não.

Direct / DiretaIndirect / IndiretaNote / Observação
Where is the report?Could you tell me where the report is?No inversion / sem inversão
When does it start?Do you know when it starts?No "do/does" / sem "do/does"
Can we move the deadline?I was wondering whether we can move the deadline.Yes/no → if / whether
Who is facilitating?Would you mind explaining who is facilitating?Very polite / muito educado

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

Imagine you are facilitating a cross-functional kickoff meeting for your clinic project. In about two minutes, open the meeting, go through a short agenda, and ask at least three polite, indirect questions -- "Could you tell me...", "I was wondering if you could...", "Do you know whether..." Then table one topic, say you will circle back, check that everyone is on the same page, and adjourn. Speak naturally and do not stop to edit.
