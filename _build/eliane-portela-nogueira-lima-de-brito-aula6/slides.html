Direct questionPolite / indirect question
Where is the report?Could you tell me where the report is?
When does it start?Do you know when it starts?
Who is leading the project?I was wondering who is leading the project.
Can we move the deadline?Would you mind telling me whether we can move the deadline?

After the polite opener, use normal word order (subject + verb) and drop the auxiliary "do". Use "if" or "whether" for yes/no questions.

Language Focus

Which Polite Opener?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me where is the report?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me where the report is?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you know when does it start?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you know when it starts?"

Inside an indirect question, keep normal word order (subject + verb) and never add "do", "does" or "did". Say "where the report is" and "when it starts".

Dialogue

The Kickoff Meeting

M
Good morning, Eliane. Thanks for joining. I will facilitate today, and we will keep minutes so nothing gets lost.
E
Thank you, Mark. Could you tell me how long the meeting will run?
M
About forty minutes. Please feel free to weigh in at any point.
E
I appreciate that. I was wondering if you could walk me through the agenda first.
M
Of course. Three items: the timeline, the budget, and the training plan. This is a cross-functional project, so we need everyone aligned.
E
That makes sense. Do you know whether the finance team reached a consensus on the budget?
M
Not yet. Let's table that point and circle back once they touch base with us.
E
Good idea. Would you mind explaining who will run the training breakout session?
M
Your team will lead it. I think we are on the same page. Let's adjourn, and I'll send the minutes this afternoon.
Did You Catch It?

About Mark

1. What role does Mark take in the meeting?
He facilitates the meeting and keeps the minutes.
2. Why does Mark table the budget point?
Because the finance team has not reached a consensus yet; he will circle back once they touch base.
3. What will Mark do after the meeting?
Adjourn the meeting and send the minutes that afternoon.
