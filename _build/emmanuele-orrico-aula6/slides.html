Chapter 4: The Code

Can You Count It?

How many centers? · How much progress?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"How many centers are on track?" — 1, 2, 3...

"How much progress is there in the private market?" — you cannot count it

"We have a few delays in the public market." — 1, 2, 3...

"There is not much information about the reimbursement." — you cannot count it

All four are correct. Look at the purple words — what makes English choose one or the other?

The list you must knowYou cannot count these, ever: progress, information, news, time, work, feedback. They have no plural and no "a": never "an information", never "two progresses".

In Portuguese, "muito" is one word for everything. In English it splits in two — and one question decides it: can I count it?

Language Focus

Can You Count It, or Not?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We have many progress this month."
"We have a lot of progress this month."
"How much centers are on track?"
"How many centers are on track?"
"I have an information about the reimbursement."
"I have some information about the reimbursement."

One question decides everything: can I count it? Centers, yes. Progress and information, never.

Did You Catch It?

About Marc

1. What does Marc want to know first?
How it is going — the progress, not the plan.
2. What news does he give about Paris?
Paris is going to send two more people in July.
3. What does he ask at the end of the call?
If there are any problems she cannot solve alone.
