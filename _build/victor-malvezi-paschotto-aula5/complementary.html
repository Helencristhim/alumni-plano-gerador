Lesson 5 -- Emails That Move: Requests, Follow-Ups, and Escalations

Series
Suits (Netflix) -- Season 2, Episode 5: "Break Point"

The lawyers at Pearson Hardman live and die by their correspondence. This episode is a masterclass in register: watch how Harvey and Jessica shift from warm and collegial to cold and formal depending on who the recipient is and what is at stake. Notice the hedged requests ("I would appreciate it if you would reconsider"), the tactful escalations, and the way a single carefully worded email can move a negotiation. Direct connection to Lesson 5: the difference between a blunt demand and a diplomatic request, how professionals escalate without burning a relationship, and why concise, pertinent messages win.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 5 moments where a character makes a request or an escalation. Mark each one as Direct, Neutral, or Formal register.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast (Harvard Business Review) -- "How to Write Email That People Actually Read"

This episode breaks down the psychology of professional email: why concise, well-structured messages get faster replies, how to make a request without sounding demanding, and when (and how) to escalate. The hosts discuss hedging language, subject lines, and the cost of vague or overly long emails. Direct connection to Victor's role: as a Growth Manager liaising with stakeholders across five regions, the way he phrases a request or an escalation directly affects how quickly things get done.

Tip: listen once for the main ideas, then again with a notepad. Write down 3 techniques you can apply to your next real email at Brinks this week.

YouTube
"Formal vs Informal Email: Requests, Follow-Ups and Escalations" -- Business English with Christina

A practical walkthrough of real email templates at three levels of formality. Christina shows how to soften a request with hedging language, how to follow up without sounding impatient, and how to escalate using conditional sentences. She contrasts direct phrases ("Send this now") with formal alternatives ("I would appreciate it if you could send this by..."), exactly the register ladder from Lesson 5. She also covers common mistakes Brazilian professionals make, such as being too direct or translating Portuguese politeness word for word.

Tip: watch the full video (about 12 min). Then rewrite one blunt request into a neutral version and a formal version. Read all three out loud and feel the difference in tone.
