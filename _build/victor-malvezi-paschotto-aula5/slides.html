Chapter 3

The Register Ladder

From blunt to diplomatic: hedging and conditionals

Grammar Discovery

Same Request, Three Registers

What changes as the tone gets more professional?

THE REQUEST

Direct: "Send me the figures."

Neutral: "Could you send me the figures?"

Formal: "I would appreciate it if you could send me the figures."

THE FOLLOW-UP

Direct: "You did not answer my email."

Formal: "I am writing to follow up on my previous email."

THE ESCALATION

Blunt: "Answer now or I will tell your boss."

Diplomatic: "If we do not receive a reply by Friday, we will escalate the matter."

Grammar Rule

The Register Ladder

RegisterStructureExample
Direct (avoid)Imperative"Send the report."
NeutralCould you...?"Could you send the report?"
FormalI would appreciate it if you could + base"I would appreciate it if you could send the report."
TentativeI was wondering if you could..."I was wondering if you could send it today."
EscalationIf + present, will + base"If we do not receive it, we will escalate."

Hedging devices (softeners):

would | could | might | I was wondering if | it would be helpful if | perhaps | at your earliest convenience  —  the more sensitive the request, the more you hedge.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I would appreciate if you can send the report."

✓ RIGHT

"I would appreciate it if you could send the report."

✗ WRONG

"If we will not receive it, we escalate."

✓ RIGHT

"If we do not receive it, we will escalate."

Formal requests need "it if you could" + base verb. First conditional: never "will" after "if"; "will" goes in the result clause.

Chapter 4

The Exchange

Aligning on a follow-up before it becomes an escalation

Dialogue

The Supplier Problem

P
Hi Victor. You mentioned a problem with the security hardware order. What is going on?
V
Thanks for making time, Priya. To be concise: the shipment is three weeks late, and there is a discrepancy between what we ordered and what the supplier confirmed.
V
I have sent two emails with no reply. I would appreciate it if you could liaise with the supplier from the London side, since they are your recipient of record.
P
Of course. Can you send me only the pertinent details? The order number, the dates, and the figures that do not match.
V
I will send a concise summary right after this call. As a courtesy, I will copy you on the follow-up so the supplier sees we are aligned.
P
Good. And if they still go quiet? I do not want this to drag on with two outstanding items.
V
Then we stay tactful but firm. If we do not receive a reply by Friday, we will escalate the matter to the regional director and reiterate the original requirements.
P
Perfect. Send me the summary and I will reply with a prompt update. Thank you, Victor.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Priya ask Victor to send her?
Only the pertinent details: the order number, the dates, and the figures that do not match.
2. Why is Priya concerned about the situation?
She does not want the problem to drag on with two outstanding items still unresolved.
3. What does Priya promise to do at the end of the call?
She promises to reply with a prompt update once Victor sends her the concise summary.
