Grammar Discovery

SubjectRuleExample
I / We / Theybase verbWe provide care.
He / She / Itverb + sThe hospital provides care.
Questionsdo / does + base verbDoes it provide care?
Sequencersfirst · then · next · after that · finally
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The hospital provide many services."

✓ RIGHT

"The hospital provides many services."

✗ WRONG

"We provides treatment to patients."

✓ RIGHT

"We provide treatment to patients."

✗ WRONG

"Does the network provides care?"

✓ RIGHT

"Does the network provide care?"

He, she, it add -s: the hospital provides. I, we, they use the base verb: we provide. In questions with does, the verb stays in the base form: Does it provide?

Dialogue

The Network in Plain Words

S
Fernando, I am not a doctor. What does the Oncology Network do?
F
We provide cancer treatment to patients in many cities. The hospital offers a full network of care.
S
How does the process work?
F
First, the patient arrives. Then, our doctors develop a plan. Next, we provide the care.
S
And the new therapies?
F
We develop new treatments every year. They improve the results, and many patients get a cure.
S
Impressive. Does the cost go down too?
F
Yes. A bigger network improves the service and lowers the cost for patients.
