Telling Stories

Conte uma história de verdade: arme o cenário com o past continuous, traga a surpresa com o past simple, e use as palavras de história. Novo vocabulário: suddenly, while, moment, realize, notice, happen, unexpected, meanwhile. Foco gramatical: past continuous + past simple (while / when).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Suddenly -- quickly and without warning
"Suddenly, the lights went out." (de repente)
While -- during the time that something else is happening
"While I was cooking, the phone rang." (enquanto)
Moment -- a very short point in time
"At that moment, everything changed." (momento)
Realize -- to understand something suddenly and clearly
"I realized I had left my keys at home." (perceber/se dar conta)
Notice -- to see or become aware of something
"I noticed a strange noise in the room." (reparar/notar)
Happen -- to take place, often by chance
"Tell me what happened next." (acontecer)
Unexpected -- surprising; not planned
"It was an unexpected but wonderful gift." (inesperado)
Meanwhile -- at the same time, in another place
"Meanwhile, my husband was waiting outside." (enquanto isso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia a história e responda às perguntas.

Mark was driving home on a normal Friday. The traffic was slow, and he was thinking about dinner. Suddenly, the car in front of him stopped. While he was braking, he noticed a small dog on the road. Meanwhile, a woman was running toward him; she was crying, because it was her dog. At that moment, Mark realized how lucky they both were. It was a completely unexpected end to an ordinary day.

1. What was Mark doing when the car in front of him stopped?
A He was driving home on a Friday.
B He was walking the dog.
C He was cooking dinner.
2. What did Mark notice while he was braking?
A A traffic light.
B A small dog on the road.
C A police car.
3. How did the story end?
A The dog ran away and was never found.
B Mark realized how lucky they were; it was an unexpected end to the day.
C Mark was late and missed dinner.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous + Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Toda história tem um fundo (ação longa) e uma surpresa (ação curta). O fundo usa o past continuous (was / were + verbo-ing); a surpresa que interrompe usa o past simple. Use while antes da ação longa e when antes da curta. / Every story has a long background action and a short action. The long one uses was / were + -ing; the short one uses the past simple. Use while for the long action and when for the short one.

Background (was/were + -ing)Surprise (past simple)
"While I was cooking,the phone rang."
"I was driving homewhen I noticed a dog."
"We were walkingwhen she smiled at me."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor frase para cada momento da história.

You want to set the scene of your story: cooking was the long action, the phone ringing was the surprise. What do you say?
A "While I cooked, the phone rang."
B "While I was cooking, the phone rang."
C "While I was cooking, the phone was ringing."
You want to bring a surprise into the story. What do you say?
A "Suddenly, the door opened."
B "Meanwhile, the door opened."
C "While, the door opened."
You want to say that you suddenly understood something. What do you say?
A "At that moment, I was realizing the truth."
B "At that moment, I notice the truth."
C "At that moment, I realized the truth."

Aula 3 — Telling Stories

Series
This Is Us — any episode (the family stories)

A gentle, warm show where the characters tell stories about their past. Listen for how they set the scene ("We were living in...") and bring a surprise ("Suddenly..."). Past continuous and past simple appear in almost every flashback.

Dica: escolha uma cena curta e anote uma frase com "while I was..." e uma com "suddenly". / Pick one short scene and write one sentence with "while I was..." and one with "suddenly".

Podcast
The Moth — real people telling true stories

Ordinary people tell short, true stories from their lives, in clear and natural English. It is the best place to hear how a real story is built: the background, the surprise, the moment everything changed.

Dica: escolha uma história, ouça uma vez só pelo prazer, e depois anote três "story words" que você ouviu. / Listen once for pleasure, then note three story words you heard.
