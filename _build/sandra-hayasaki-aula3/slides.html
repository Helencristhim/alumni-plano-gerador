Chapter 3 -- The Code

The Background and the Surprise

One long action, one short action, together

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each story has a long action and a short one. Notice the orange words.

"While I was cooking, the phone rang."

"I was driving home when I noticed a small dog."

"We were walking in the market when, suddenly, she smiled."

"I was looking at the bowls. Meanwhile, my husband took photos."

The Rule

Was/Were + -ing, When + Past Simple

Background (past continuous)Surprise (past simple)
While I was cooking,the phone rang.
I was driving homewhen I noticed a dog.
We were walkingwhen she smiled at me.

Tip: the long background action uses was / were + -ing. The short action that interrupts it uses the past simple. Use while before the long action and when before the short one.

Common Mistake

The Background Needs -ing

"While I cooked, the phone rang."
"While I was cooking, the phone rang."

The long background action takes was / were + -ing. Keep the past simple for the short action that happens.

Artefact

A Page from Mark's Journal

MY JOURNAL -- FRIDAY EVENING

A normal day that became unexpected

I was driving home after a long week. The traffic was slow, and I was thinking about dinner.

Suddenly, the car in front of me stopped. While I was braking, I noticed a small dog on the road.

Meanwhile, a woman was running toward me. She was crying -- it was her dog.

At that moment, I realized how lucky we both were. A completely unexpected end to an ordinary day.
