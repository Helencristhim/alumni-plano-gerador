Chapter 3 -- Still Waiting

Still Waiting, Politely

Show you are waiting, then ask by a clear day

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Which phrase shows you are waiting, and which one sets a clear day?

"I am still waiting for your reply."

"This is a friendly reminder about my last email."

"Could you reply by Friday, please?"

"We kindly ask for a prompt response."

Grammar Rule

Show You Wait & Ask by a Day

Use it forPatternExample
Show you are waitingI am still waiting for + nounI am still waiting for your reply.
Open the reminderThis is a friendly reminder about + nounThis is a friendly reminder about my email.
Ask by a clear dayCould you + verb + by + day?Could you reply by Friday?
Ask politely (formal)We kindly ask for + nounWe kindly ask for a prompt response.

Key idea: use I am still waiting for + a noun to show you are waiting (I am still waiting for your reply), and Could you + a verb + by + a day to set a clear deadline (Could you reply by Friday?).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I still wait your answer. Please reply until Friday."
"I am still waiting for your answer. Please reply by Friday."

We say I am still waiting for + a noun (not "I still wait"), and for a deadline we use by + a day -- by Friday, not "until Friday".

Artefact

The Reminder Email

From: Percival JR -- EGEA Saneamento

To: Mr. Santos   |   CC: Karen

Subject: Friendly Reminder -- Final Numbers (now overdue)

Dear Mr. Santos,

This is a friendly reminder about my two emails from last week. The request is still pending on your side.

I am still waiting for your final numbers. The report is now overdue, and this request has become urgent for our team.

We kindly ask for a prompt response. Could you reply by Friday at the latest? If anything is unclear, I am happy to help.

Thank you very much for your time and your help.

Best regards,
Percival JR

Dialogue

Checking the Reminder

P

"Hi Karen, the supplier is silent. I wrote a reminder. I open with: This is a friendly reminder about my last email. Can you check it?"

K

"Good start. Do you show that you are still waiting? And remember, the matter is still pending on their side."

P

"Yes. I wrote: I am still awaiting your final numbers. The report is now overdue, and this request is urgent."

K

"Perfect, but keep it kind. Set a clear day, and ask politely. Do not sound angry. What did you write?"

P

"I wrote: We kindly ask for a prompt response. Could you reply by Friday at the latest?"

K

"That is clear and kind. You showed you are waiting, you set a deadline, and you stayed polite. Add me in CC and send it. Great work, Percival."
