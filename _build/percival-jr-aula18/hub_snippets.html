Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key words for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Reminder -- a short message to make someone remember something
"This is a friendly reminder about my email." (Lembrete)
Pending -- not finished yet, still waiting to be done
"Your request is still pending on their side." (Pendente)
Awaiting -- waiting for something to arrive or happen
"We are awaiting your final numbers." (Aguardando)
Response -- the answer someone gives you
"We kindly ask for a prompt response." (Resposta)
Chase -- to ask again, politely, to get an answer
"Could you chase the supplier for me?" (Cobrar / ir atrás de)
Urgent -- needs an answer very soon
"This request is now urgent." (Urgente)
Overdue -- later than the deadline
"The report is now two days overdue." (Atrasado / vencido)
Kindly -- in a polite, gentle way
"We kindly ask for your reply by Friday." (Gentilmente / por gentileza)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the short text, then answer the questions. / Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A supplier never answered Percival's emails. The request is still pending, and the report is now overdue. Percival sends a polite reminder. He opens: "This is a friendly reminder about my last email." Then he shows he is waiting: "I am still waiting for your response, and this request is now urgent." He sets a clear day: "Could you reply by Friday?" He closes politely: "We kindly ask for a prompt response."

1. Why does Percival send a reminder?
A The supplier never answered, and the request is pending and overdue.
B He wants to cancel the order.
C He received a full reply already.
2. How does Percival show he is still waiting?
A "I still wait your response."
B "I am still waiting for your response, and this request is now urgent."
C "I do not need your response."
3. How does Percival set the deadline?
A "Could you reply by Friday?"
B "Could you reply until Friday?"
C "Reply when you want."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Show You Are Waiting & Ask by a Day

GRAMMAR

Num lembrete educado você mostra que está esperando com I am still waiting for + substantivo (your reply) e define um prazo claro com Could you + verbo + by + dia (Could you reply by Friday?). Use "by" + dia para prazo (não "until"). / In a polite reminder you show you are waiting with I am still waiting for + a noun, and you set a clear deadline with Could you + verb + by + a day.

Função / FunctionPatternExample
Mostrar que espera / Show you are waitingI am still waiting for + nounI am still waiting for your reply.
Abrir o lembrete / Open the reminderThis is a friendly reminder about + nounThis is a friendly reminder about my email.
Definir o prazo / Set a deadlineCould you + verb + by + day?Could you reply by Friday?
Pedir com educação / Ask politelyWe kindly ask for + nounWe kindly ask for a prompt response.

Key point / Ponto-chave: dizemos I am still waiting for + substantivo (não "I still wait") e, para prazo, usamos by + dia: by Friday (não "until Friday"). / We say I am still waiting for + a noun, and for a deadline we use by + a day.
