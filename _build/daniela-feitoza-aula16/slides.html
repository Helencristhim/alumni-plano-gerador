Chapter 3

The Order Rule

Past perfect for the earlier event, sequencers for the order

Order Discovery

Listen and Notice

I had already escalated it when the engineer arrived.

By the time he logged in, we had restored the service.

First, it spiked. Then, it crashed. After that, I escalated it.

An old script had triggered the problem before we noticed it.

Which action happened first? How do "first", "then" and "finally" help?

Order Rule

Before, After, in Order

FormUseExample
had + past participleThe earlier of two past actions"I had escalated it."
past simpleThe later action"...when he arrived."
had already / by the timeStress that it was done before"By the time he logged in, we had restored it."
first, then, after that, finallySequencers that order the story"First it spiked, then it crashed."
Common Mistake

Wrong Form vs Right Form

✗ WRONG

"I escalated it when he arrived." (loses the order)

✓ RIGHT

"I had escalated it when he arrived." (earlier action = had + done)

✗ WRONG

"We has restored the service."

✓ RIGHT

"We had restored the service." (past perfect is always "had")

For the earlier of two past actions, use "had" + the past participle — never "has", never plain past simple.
