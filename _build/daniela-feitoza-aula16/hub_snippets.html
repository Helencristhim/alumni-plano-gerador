Warm-up — Callback da Aula 15

Review

Na Aula 15 você aprendeu a montar a cena de um incidente com o past continuous ("I was testing the system when the server crashed"). Agora você coloca dois eventos passados em ordem: qual aconteceu primeiro? Para a ação mais antiga, você usa o past perfect (had + past participle): "I had escalated it when he arrived". E usa sequenciadores (first, then, after that, finally) para deixar a história clara. (Show what happened first, then what came next.)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

In the postmortem, Daniela told the story in order. First, the traffic spiked. Then, the service crashed. By the time the engineer arrived, she had already escalated the issue — the escalation happened before his arrival, so she uses the past perfect (had + past participle) for the earlier action and the past simple (arrived) for the later one. Before the meeting started, she had pinpointed the culprit: an old script had triggered the memory problem. Finally, the team mitigated the risk. Remember: the past perfect is always had + past participle, never "has", and sequencers (first, then, after that, finally) make the order clear.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Past Perfect & Sequencers

GRAMMAR

A ação mais antiga e os sequenciadores (bilingue EN + PT).

FormUseExample
had + past participleA ação mais antiga de duas no passado"I had escalated it."
past simpleA ação mais recente"...when he arrived."
first, then, after that, finallySequenciadores que ordenam a história"First it spiked, then it crashed."
Regra (PT)Use o past perfect (had + past participle) para a ação que aconteceu PRIMEIRO; use o past simple para a ação mais recente. Os sequenciadores (first, then, after that, finally) deixam a ordem clara. O past perfect é SEMPRE had, nunca "has".
