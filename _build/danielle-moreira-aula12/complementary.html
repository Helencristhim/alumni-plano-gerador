Film
The Intern (2015, Netflix)

Ben, a seventy-year-old “senior intern”, joins a fast company where he has authority over nothing and knowledge of everything — and wins the room by listening long before he offers a single opinion. It is your first ninety days in reverse: no mandate, all credibility, earned one honest conversation at a time. Connection to Lesson 12: watch how he sizes up the culture before trying to move it, and how he leans on being useful rather than being right.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Note THREE moments where he learns something by asking instead of telling, and rewrite each as reported speech (“She explained that...”, “He admitted that...”) for your own listening tour.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast — “Taking on a Senior Leadership Role Remotely” (with Michael Watkins)

Michael Watkins, who wrote the book that named the idea, walks through what a new leader should actually do in the entry period — diagnose before deciding, secure early wins that build credibility, and avoid the trap of proving yourself by changing something fast — and does it for exactly your situation: taking the reins of a senior role at a distance, part remote, part in person. It is the strategy of Lesson 12 from the person who defined it. Connection to Lesson 12: listen for how he frames the ramp-up period as learning, not delay, and how the listening tour is the work, not a warm-up to it.

Tip: listen once with no pauses. Then, in ninety seconds, relay two ideas he raised using reported speech and two different reporting verbs (“He argued that...”, “He pointed out that...”). Record it and check your backshift.

YouTube
Frances Frei — “How to build (and rebuild) trust” (TED, 15 min)

Frei ran the culture turnaround at Uber, and her whole talk is about the one currency a new leader has to earn from zero: trust — the everyday word for credibility. She shows why trying to prove yourself fast destroys it, and why authenticity, logic, and empathy build it back. This is the emotional engine of Lesson 12: you cannot spend a mandate you have not earned. Connection to Lesson 12: watch how she reframes the first weeks as listening and repair, not decisive action — exactly the discipline of the first ninety days.

Tip: watch it twice. On the second pass, write a thirty-second plan for your own first quarter — what you will listen for, whom you will lean on, and the one thing you will deliberately NOT change yet. Bring it to Lesson 13.
