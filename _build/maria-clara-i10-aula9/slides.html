Chapter 3: The Code

How Much, How Many

much time · many meetings · a little money · a few questions · enough seats

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We do not have much time before the conference."

"There are too many meetings on Thursday."

"I have a few questions for the rector."

"I only have a little money in foreign currency."

"Do we have enough seats for the delegation?"

Some orange words go with things you can COUNT. One goes with something you CANNOT count. Which is which?

WordUse it withExample
many · a few · too manyCOUNTABLE things (you can count: 1 seat, 2 seats, 3 seats)too many meetings · a few questions · not many seats
much · a little · too muchUNCOUNTABLE things (you cannot count: time, money, space)not much time · a little money · too much preparation
a lot of · enoughBOTH — countable and uncountable. Easy and safe.a lot of documents · a lot of time · enough seats · enough money
Questions: How many? / How much?How many for countable, How much for uncountable.How many documents? · How much time?
Idiom of the lesson: Time flies.Nothing to build — one ready phrase. It means time passes very fast. Perfect for November."November is close already. Time flies!"

The trick is one question: can I count it? Seats, meetings, documents — yes, so many. Time, money, space — no, so much. And when in doubt, a lot of and enough work for both.

no number in front

time · money · space · preparation

"We do not have ___much time." (time = uncountable)
"There are too ___many meetings this week." (meetings = countable)
"I have a ___few documents to sign." (documents = countable)
"There is only a ___little space in my luggage." (space = uncountable)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have much meetings today."
"I have many meetings today."
"We do not have many time."
"We do not have much time."
"There are too much documents."
"There are too many documents."

One question decides it: can you count it? Meetings and documents — yes, so many. Time — no, so much.

Chapter 4: The Logistics Call

Bruno Calls About Seoul

How much time, how many documents, enough seats?

Dialogue

The Pre-Trip Call

B
Maria Clara, we have a lot of things to prepare for Seoul. How much preparation do we still have — and how much time?
M
We have a little time — about six weeks. But there are too many meetings before November.
B
And how many documents do we need for the delegation?
M
We need a lot of documents. Each person needs a visa, and a few of them are still not ready.
B
And the budget? Do we have enough money for the flights?
M
We have enough for the flights, but only a little foreign currency for the hotel. I am going to check the amount.
B
Good. And do we have enough seats on the same flight?
M
Not many seats are left, so book them today. There is a lot of preparation — but I am looking forward to Seoul!
