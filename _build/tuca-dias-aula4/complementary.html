Lesson 4 -- Street Smart: Asking for Directions

Series
Somebody Feed Phil -- "Mexico City"

Phil wanders Mexico City on foot, asking locals where to go and how to get there. Perfect for your upcoming trip: listen for how he asks for directions and how people answer with turns, corners, and landmarks.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every direction phrase you hear (turn, go straight, next to, opposite). Then describe one route Phil takes using three new words.

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Asking for and Giving Directions"

A short, slow lesson dedicated to street language: imperatives, prepositions of place, and the polite phrases you need to stop a stranger and find your way in any city.

Pause after each direction and repeat it aloud. Note three prepositions of place the hosts use, and compare them with how Mateo guided Tuca in today's dialogue.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Directions: How to find your way"

A clear, practical video on giving and following directions in English, with on-screen maps showing intersections, roundabouts, crosswalks, and landmarks. Directly useful for travelling alone.

After watching, record a 60-second route from your hotel to a place you love. Use at least four new words and three imperatives (Go, Turn, Cross).
