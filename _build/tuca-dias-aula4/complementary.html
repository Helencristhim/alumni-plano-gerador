Lesson 4 -- Street Smart: Asking for Directions

Series
Somebody Feed Phil -- any episode (Netflix)

Phil explores new cities on foot, constantly asking locals for directions and recommendations. Perfect, friendly model of real travelers navigating unfamiliar streets and finding landmarks.

Watch with English subtitles. Note every time someone gives directions, and write down three imperatives and two prepositions of place you hear.

Podcast
Zero To Travel -- "How to Get Around in a New City"

Practical travel advice on orienting yourself, reading maps, and asking the way when you arrive somewhere new. Listen for how confident travelers describe routes and locations.

Write down 3 useful phrases for asking directions, then say them out loud as if you were lost in a new town.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Asking for and Giving Directions"

A short, clear lesson on the exact language from today: imperatives for directions and prepositions of place. Directly applicable to your next trip to a new city.

After watching, record a 60-second clip giving directions from your house to the nearest landmark. Use at least four new words and several imperatives.
