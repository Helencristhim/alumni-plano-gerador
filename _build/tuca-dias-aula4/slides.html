Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Imperatives + prepositions of place -- giving directions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which words give a command? Which words tell us WHERE?

"Go straight ahead and turn left at the traffic light."

"The hostel is on the corner, next to the bakery."

"Take the second street on the right."

"The museum is across from the church, behind the square."

Grammar Rule

Imperatives & Prepositions

StructureUse & Example
Imperative (base verb)A direct instruction, no subject -- "Turn left." / "Go straight ahead."
Negative imperative (Don't + verb)Tell someone NOT to do it -- "Don't turn here." / "Don't take the roundabout."
Prepositions of placeSay where something is -- on the corner, next to, across from, between, in front of, behind

Key idea: the imperative never uses a subject (not "you turn", just "turn"). And remember the full phrases: "across from", "in front of", "next to" -- the small words matter.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The bank is in the corner, in front the church."
"The bank is on the corner, in front of the church."

Use on the corner and in front of (with "of"). For directions, drop the subject: say "Go straight," not "You go straight."

"Exactly. Walk two blocks until the roundabout, then take the second exit. The museum is on the corner, across from the church."
