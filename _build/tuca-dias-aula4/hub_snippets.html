Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Intersection -- the point where two streets cross
"Turn left at the next intersection." (Cruzamento)
Roundabout -- a circular junction where roads meet
"Take the second exit at the roundabout." (Rotatória)
Block -- the section of a street between two corners
"Walk two blocks and you will see it." (Quarteirão)
Landmark -- a well-known place used as a reference
"The old church is a useful landmark." (Ponto de referência)
Crosswalk -- the marked place where you cross the street
"Cross at the crosswalk, not in the middle." (Faixa de pedestres)
Sidewalk -- the path beside the road for people walking
"Stay on this sidewalk and keep going." (Calçada)
Corner -- the place where two streets meet
"The shop is right on the corner." (Esquina)
Traffic light -- the signal that controls cars at a junction
"Turn right at the traffic light." (Semáforo)
Straight ahead -- directly forward, without turning
"Go straight ahead for two blocks." (Em frente)
Avenue -- a wide street in a city
"The shop is on a busy avenue." (Avenida)
Opposite -- directly across from something
"The bank is opposite the square." (Em frente a)
Square -- an open public space in a city
"Let's meet in the main square." (Praça)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca was lost downtown, so she asked a local for directions. He said: "Go straight ahead for two blocks until you reach the avenue. Turn left at the traffic light and cross at the crosswalk. Stay on the same sidewalk and walk past the square. The coffee shop is on the corner, next to a bookstore and opposite the old church. Don't go as far as the roundabout -- if you reach it, you have gone too far." The old church was the perfect landmark, so Tuca found the shop in five minutes.

1. Which sentence is a correct imperative for giving a direction?
A "You going straight ahead for two blocks."
B "Go straight ahead for two blocks."
C "To go straight ahead for two blocks."
2. Where is the coffee shop?
A On the corner, next to a bookstore and opposite the old church.
B At the roundabout, behind the square.
C Between the avenue and the traffic light.
3. What does "Don't go as far as the roundabout" tell us?
A The roundabout is too far; reaching it means she missed the shop.
B The shop is at the roundabout.
C She should cross at the roundabout.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives & Prepositions of Place

GRAMMAR

Usados para dar e localizar direcoes. / Used to give directions and locate places.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Imperative (base verb)Ordem ou direcao, sem sujeito / A command, no subjectGo straight. Turn left. Cross the street.
Negative imperativeDizer para NAO fazer algo / Tell someone NOT to do itDon't cross here. Don't turn yet.
Prepositions of placeMostram a posicao de um lugar / Show where a place isIt's next to / opposite / between the bank.
Key point / Ponto-chaveDiga "Turn left", nunca "Turn to left". E sempre "in front of", nunca "in front the".
