Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Corner -- Where two streets meet
"The bakery is on the corner of two streets." (esquina)
Block -- The distance between two streets
"Walk two blocks and you will see it." (quarteirão)
Intersection -- Where two roads cross
"Turn right at the next intersection." (cruzamento)
Roundabout -- A circular road junction
"Go around the roundabout and take the second exit." (rotatória)
Traffic light -- The red-yellow-green signal
"Stop at the traffic light and turn left." (semáforo)
Landmark -- An easy-to-recognize place
"The old church is a famous landmark." (ponto de referência)
Crosswalk -- Lines to cross the street
"Use the crosswalk to cross safely." (faixa de pedestres)
Sidewalk -- The path beside the road
"Walk along the sidewalk until the square." (calçada)
Get lost -- To lose your way
"It is easy to get lost in a new city." (se perder)
Straight ahead -- Directly in front of you
"The station is straight ahead." (em frente)
Nearby -- Close to here
"Is there a pharmacy nearby?" (por perto)
Turn -- To change direction
"Turn left after the bank." (virar / dobrar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca arrived in a new town and felt nervous about getting around. A friendly local named Diego helped her. "Go straight ahead along the sidewalk," he said, "and turn right at the first intersection. Walk two blocks until the roundabout. The coffee museum is on the corner, across from the church and next to the old fountain. Use the crosswalk at the traffic light so you cross safely. It is a famous landmark, so you won't get lost." Tuca repeated the directions and thanked him.

1. Which sentence is an imperative (a direct instruction)?
A "Tuca repeated the directions."
B "Go straight ahead along the sidewalk."
C "It is a famous landmark."
2. Where is the coffee museum?
A "On the corner, across from the church and next to the old fountain."
B "Inside the bus station."
C "Two blocks behind the park."
3. Why won't Tuca get lost?
A "Because she has a car."
B "Because the museum is a famous landmark, easy to find."
C "Because Diego walks with her."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives & Prepositions of Place

GRAMMAR

Usados para dar instruções e dizer onde algo está. / Used to give instructions and say where things are.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Imperative (base verb)Instrução direta, sem sujeito / Direct instruction, no subjectTurn left. / Go straight ahead.
Negative (Don't + verb)Dizer para NÃO fazer / Tell someone not to do itDon't turn here. / Don't take the roundabout.
Prepositions of placeDizer onde algo está / Say where something ison the corner, next to, across from, between, in front of, behind
Key point / Ponto-chaveO imperativo nunca usa sujeito (não "you turn", só "turn"). Use as frases completas: "in front of", "across from", "next to".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

A tourist asks where the bank is. It is at the end of the street, on the corner. Best response?
A "It is somewhere over there, I think."
B "Go straight ahead and the bank is on the corner at the end of the street."
C "You go to the bank and you turn."
You want to tell a visitor to cross the street safely. What do you say?
A "Cross at the crosswalk when the light is green."
B "Cross in the corner now."
C "Run across the road quickly."
A traveler asks if the pharmacy is close. It is just across from the square. What do you say?
A "It is very far, take a taxi."
B "I wish I knew where it is."
C "Yes, it is nearby, just across from the square."
