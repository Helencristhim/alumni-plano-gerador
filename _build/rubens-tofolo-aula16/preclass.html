Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Specialist -- a doctor who is an expert in one area of medicine
"An endocrinologist is a specialist." (Especialista)
Receptionist -- the person who greets you and books appointments
"The receptionist will call your name." (Recepcionista)
Checkup -- a routine medical exam when you are not really sick
"I go for a checkup once a year." (Check-up / exame de rotina)
Examine -- to look at a patient's body carefully to check their health
"The doctor will examine you now." (Examinar)
Medical history -- the record of a person's past illnesses and treatments
"The nurse will update your medical history." (Histórico médico)
Condition -- a health problem that a person has, often long-term
"He has a heart condition." (Condição / problema de saúde)
Treatment -- the care or medicine a doctor uses to make a patient better
"The treatment lasted two weeks." (Tratamento)
Blood pressure -- the force of the blood as it moves through your body
"The doctor checks my blood pressure first." (Pressão arterial)
Waiting room -- the room where patients sit before they see the doctor
"Please take a seat in the waiting room." (Sala de espera)
Reschedule -- to change an appointment to a different day or time
"I need to reschedule my appointment to Friday." (Remarcar)
Insurance -- a plan that helps pay for your medical costs
"Please bring your insurance card." (Seguro / convênio)
Regular -- happening often and at the same times; routine
"Do you take any regular medicine?" (Regular)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- used to + base verb

GRAMMAR

Used to + verbo na base descreve um hábito ou estado do passado que NÃO é mais verdade. No negativo e na pergunta, o auxiliar did assume o passado, então voltamos a use to (sem "d"). / Used to + base verb describes a past habit or state that is no longer true. In the negative and question, did carries the past, so we go back to use to.

Form / FormaExample / Exemplo
Affirmative: used to + base verbI used to smoke. / Eu costumava fumar.
Negative: did not use to + base verbI did not use to exercise. / Eu não costumava me exercitar.
Question: Did + subject + use to...?Did you use to take any medicine? / Você costumava tomar algum remédio?
Meaning / SentidoA past habit or state, not true now. / Hábito ou estado do passado, não verdadeiro agora.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de used to, sempre use o verbo na base (used to smoke, não "used to smoked"). Com did (negativo ou pergunta), volte para use to (did not use to, Did you use to...?).
