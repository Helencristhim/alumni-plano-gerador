Defining relative clauses — 6 Minute Grammar (free)

Six minutes on the little words that hold your descriptions together: that for a thing, where for a place, who for a person. It is exactly tonight's rule — "a thing that you use in the rain", "a place where you buy medicine" — from the BBC, with more examples so it sticks.

Tip: keep it short and sweet — six minutes while the coffee is on the stove. Every time they say "who", "that" or "where", pause and build a travel sentence: "It's a person who...", "It's a place where...". One note: they are British; your material is American. The rule is identical; only the music of the voice changes.
