Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Describing a process: the passive voice

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"The engines are made in Resende."

"Each unit is inspected before delivery."

"The parts are produced here and assembled on site."

"The product is shipped with a two-year warranty."

Grammar Rule

The Passive Voice

PartFormExample
Singularis + past participleThe engine is made here.
Pluralare + past participleThe parts are produced here.
Negativeis / are not + p.p.It is not made by hand.
Say who (optional)+ by + agentIt is designed by our team.

Key idea: the passive voice puts the product first, not the people. Use is with one thing and are with many, then the past participle (made, produced, inspected, shipped). You usually do not say who does it; add by + agent only when it matters.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The engines made in Resende."
"The engines are made in Resende."

The passive needs is / are before the past participle. Use is for one, are for many.
