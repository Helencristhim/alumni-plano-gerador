Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Manufacture -- to make products in a factory, in large numbers
"We manufacture engines in Resende." (Fabricar)
Assemble -- to put parts together to build a product
"The parts are assembled on site." (Montar)
Raw materials -- the basic materials used to make a product
"The raw materials are checked on arrival." (Matéria-prima)
Inspect -- to check something carefully for quality
"Every unit is inspected before delivery." (Inspecionar)
Produce -- to make goods or parts, especially in quantity
"The parts are produced in Resende." (Produzir)
Design -- to plan how a product looks and works
"The engine is designed for heavy use." (Projetar / desenhar)
Ship -- to send goods to a customer or place
"The product is shipped to the client." (Enviar / despachar)
Durable -- strong and able to last a long time
"Our engines are very durable." (Durável / resistente)
Warranty -- a promise to repair a product for free for a set time
"Each engine is covered by a two-year warranty." (Garantia)
Feature -- an important part or quality of a product
"Its best feature is the low fuel use." (Característica / recurso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A client visits Roberto's factory in Resende. Roberto presents the product. "Our engines are manufactured here," he says. "The raw materials are checked on arrival. The parts are produced in this plant and assembled on the line. Every unit is inspected before it is shipped." The client is impressed. Roberto adds: "The engine is designed for heavy use, so it is very durable. And each unit is covered by a two-year warranty." Notice: to describe a process, English uses the passive voice -- the verb to be (is / are) + the past participle (made, produced, inspected, shipped). The product comes first; we usually do not say who does the work.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Use a voz passiva para descrever um processo e colocar o produto em primeiro lugar. / Use the passive voice to describe a process and put the product first.

Part / ParteForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Singularis + past participleThe engine is made here.
Pluralare + past participleThe parts are produced here.
Negativa / Negativeis / are not + p.p.It is not made by hand.
Quem faz / Say who (opcional)+ by + agenteIt is designed by our team.

Key point / Ponto-chave: a voz passiva coloca o produto em primeiro lugar, não as pessoas. Use is com uma coisa e are com várias, depois o past participle (made, produced, inspected, shipped). Não esqueça o verbo to be: diga "The engines are made", não "The engines made". Para dizer quem faz, use by + agente -- mas em geral não precisa.
