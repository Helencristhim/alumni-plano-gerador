Supplementary Materials -- Lesson 5

Lesson theme: Block 1 Review -- the elevator pitch. Watch and listen for how professionals introduce themselves, state a value proposition, prove results with numbers, and make a clear ask in under a minute.

Series
Shark Tank (US) -- Season 1, Episode 1

Entrepreneurs deliver short, high-stakes pitches to investors. This is the elevator pitch in its purest form: who they are, their value proposition, their numbers, and the ask. Notice how the best pitchers lead with one clear sentence, then back it up with data ("We have sold...", "Our revenue is...").

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences where a founder states their value proposition or a result, then adapt them to Quinto Andar.

Podcast
Masters of Scale with Reid Hoffman

Founders explain how they pitch their business model and scale it. Perfect for hearing value proposition, competitive advantage, and growth metrics used naturally in present perfect ("We have grown...", "We have reached...").

Tip: listen at 1x speed. Note how each guest answers "what does your company do?" in one or two sentences, then practice the same for your own pitch.

YouTube
"The Best Way To Answer 'So What Do You Do?'" -- Clay Hebert | TEDxReno

A 12-minute talk on building a short, memorable introduction. Hebert shows how to replace a boring job title with a clear value statement -- exactly Block 1 of your pitch. Great model for your opening line.

Tip: watch the full talk. Then rewrite your opening so a stranger instantly understands what Quinto Andar does for clients.
