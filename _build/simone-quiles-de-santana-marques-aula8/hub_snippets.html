Giving Updates -- Present Continuous & Time Expressions

How to give a clear project update and report what is happening now. New vocabulary: progress, update, status, milestone, on track, delay, ongoing, phase, deliverable, pending. Grammar: the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) with time expressions like right now and this week.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Progress -- movement forward toward a goal
"We are making good progress on the contract." (Progresso)
Update -- new information about how something is going
"I will give you a quick update on the merger." (Atualização)
Status -- how something is right now
"What is the status of the merger?" (Situação)
Milestone -- an important point you reach
"We reached an important milestone this week." (Marco)
On track -- going well and on time
"The project is on track for next month." (No prazo)
Delay -- when something happens later than planned
"There is a small delay in the tax review." (Atraso)
Ongoing -- still happening, not yet finished
"The negotiation is ongoing this week." (Em andamento)
Phase -- one stage of a project
"We are in the final phase of the deal." (Fase)
Deliverable -- a result you must produce and hand in
"The first deliverable is the draft agreement." (Entregável)
Pending -- still waiting for a decision or approval
"The approval is still pending." (Pendente)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Para dizer o que esta acontecendo agora, use o present continuous: am/is/are + verbo com -ing. Negativo: am/is/are + not + -ing. Pergunta: Am/Is/Are + sujeito + -ing. Use com expressoes de tempo como now, right now, at the moment, this week, these days. Cuidado: precisa das DUAS partes (be + -ing) -- nunca diga "I working" nem "I am work". / To say what is happening now, use the present continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing. Negative: am/is/are + not + verb-ing. Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing. Use it with time expressions like now, right now, at the moment, this week, these days. Careful: you need BOTH parts (be + -ing).

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Affirmativeam/is/are + verb-ing"I am working on the report."
Negativeam/is/are + not + verb-ing"We are not waiting yet."
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing"Are you preparing the update?"
Time wordsnow, right now, at the moment, this week, these days
