Lesson 8 -- Giving Updates

Series
Industry -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Induction"

A drama set in a global investment bank, full of project updates and status meetings. Watch how the analysts report progress, name milestones, and explain what is on track or still pending -- and notice the present continuous when they say what is happening right now.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences in the present continuous about what someone is doing. Then record yourself giving a one-minute update on your own project.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Give a Great Status Update"

Practical advice on reporting project progress at work: how to share the status clearly, name a milestone, flag a delay, and say what is still pending without losing your audience. Listen for how speakers describe ongoing work.

Note 3 phrases the speakers use to report progress. Compare them with how Simone updates Daniel on the merger.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Present continuous" / "Talking about now"

A short, practical lesson on the present continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing for actions happening now, with time expressions like right now and at the moment. Directly useful for the project updates you give at Telefonica.

After watching, note three time expressions, then record a 60-second project update using at least four new words from today.
