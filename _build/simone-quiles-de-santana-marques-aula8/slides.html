Chapter 3 -- Happening Now

The Present Continuous

How to say what is happening right now

Listen and Notice

What Do They Share?

Look at the highlighted words. What do they have in common?

"We are reviewing the contract this week."

"The team is finalizing the clauses right now."

"I am preparing the report at the moment."

"Our partners are waiting for the draft these days."

The Update Code

Present Continuous

FormStructureExample
Affirmativeam / is / are + verb-ing"I am working on the report."
Negativeam / is / are + not + verb-ing"We are not waiting yet."
QuestionAm / Is / Are + subject + verb-ing"Are you preparing the update?"
Time wordsnow, right now, at the moment, this week, these days

Key idea: use the present continuous for actions happening now or around now. We do NOT use it for permanent facts -- that is the present simple (Aula 2).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I working on the report. ... We are review the contract."
"I am working on the report. ... We are reviewing the contract."

Use both parts: am / is / are + the verb with -ing. Never drop the verb be, and never drop the -ing.
