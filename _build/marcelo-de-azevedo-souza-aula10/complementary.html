Aula 10 -- Roads Not Taken

Film
Sliding Doors (1998) -- with Gwyneth Paltrow

The perfect film for tonight's grammar: it shows one woman's life split into two, depending on whether she catches a train or misses it. It is the third conditional turned into a whole movie -- what would have happened if she had, or hadn't. Watch how one small fork in the road changes everything.

Tip: after watching, describe the two lives in English. "If she had caught the train, she would have ...; if she hadn't, she would have ..."

Podcast
Hidden Brain -- with Shankar Vedantam -- any episode on choices or regret

A thoughtful, beautifully spoken podcast about why we do what we do -- including the science of decisions, regret, and the roads we don't take. Clear, calm American English at a natural pace, full of exactly the kind of reflective language you practiced tonight.

Tip: pick one episode. Pause once and finish this sentence out loud about your own life: "If only I had ..."

YouTube
Kathryn Schulz: Don't Regret Regret (TED)

A warm, funny, seventeen-minute talk that makes the case for embracing your regrets instead of hiding from them. It is tonight's theme in its purest form, in clear, expressive English -- and it will give you a new way to talk about the roads you did not take.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Note two sentences she uses with "I wish I had ..." or "if only", and say them out loud.
