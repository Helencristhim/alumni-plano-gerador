Roads Not Taken -- Regrets and What Might Have Been

Talking about an unreal past and regrets. Key words: hindsight, to play it safe, a leap of faith, a fork in the road, to second-guess yourself, a missed opportunity, on a whim, to make the most of, to look back on, to have second thoughts, to regret, to talk yourself out of. Grammar: third conditional (If I had known, I would have gone) + wish / if only + past perfect (I wish I had gone).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Hindsight -- understanding a situation only after it has happened (percepcao tardia, com o beneficio de olhar para tras)
"In hindsight, I should have said yes."
To play it safe -- to choose the careful, low-risk option (jogar na seguranca, ser cauteloso)
"Part of me just wanted to play it safe."
A leap of faith -- a bold act of trust in an uncertain outcome (um salto de fe)
"Saying yes to the trip was a real leap of faith."
A fork in the road -- a moment of decision between two very different paths (uma encruzilhada, uma bifurcacao)
"I stood at a fork in the road and had to choose."
To second-guess yourself -- to doubt a choice you have already made (ficar em duvida sobre a propria decisao)
"Once you decide, try not to second-guess yourself."
A missed opportunity -- a chance you did not take and later wish you had (uma oportunidade perdida)
"It became a missed opportunity I still think about."
On a whim -- suddenly, without planning it in advance (por impulso, de repente)
"I booked the whole trip on a whim."
To make the most of -- to use something as fully and well as possible (aproveitar ao maximo)
"These days I try to make the most of every trip."
To look back on -- to think about a past event from where you are now (relembrar, olhar para tras)
"I look back on it as my best decision."
To have second thoughts -- to begin to doubt a decision you have made (ter duvidas, repensar uma decisao)
"I had second thoughts right up until the flight."
To regret -- to feel sorry about something you did or did not do (arrepender-se)
"I do not regret a single day of that trip."
To talk yourself out of -- to persuade yourself not to do something you wanted to do (se convencer a nao fazer algo)
"I nearly talked myself out of the whole trip."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Years ago, Marcelo stood at a fork in the road. A friend had invited him on a cycling trip, and he almost talked himself out of it. In hindsight, he is very glad he went. If he had played it safe, he would have missed the best trip of his life. And if he had not taken that leap of faith, he would never have met his closest friends. Now he does not regret it for a second. His only regret is the trips he was too careful to take. As he often says, "I wish I had learned sooner" to make the most of every chance.

1. Por que "If he had played it safe, he would have missed the trip" é terceira condicional?
A Usa if + past perfect (had played) e would have + particípio (would have missed) para um passado imaginado.
B Usa if + presente e will para um plano real.
C Usa if + passado simples e would para uma hipótese no presente.
2. What does "I wish I had learned sooner" express?
A A plan for the future.
B A regret about the past.
C A promise to a friend.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If I would have known, I would have gone."
B "If I had known, I would have gone."
C "If I knew, I would have gone."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional & Wish

GRAMMAR

Como falar de um passado que NÃO aconteceu e de arrependimentos (explicação em inglês e português).

StructureUse / UsoExample
Third conditional
if + past perfect, ... would have + participío		Um passado irreal, imaginado. An imagined, unreal past.If I had known, I would have gone.
wish + past perfectArrependimento do passado. A regret about the past.I wish I had gone.
if only + past perfectArrependimento mais forte. A stronger regret.If only I had not waited.

Atenção: NUNCA diga "If I would have known". A parte do if usa had + participío: "If I had known, I would have gone".

Quiz
