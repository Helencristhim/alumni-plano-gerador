Chapter 4: The Code

The Unreal Past

Third conditional · wish · if only

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If I had said no, I would have missed everything."

"If I hadn't gone, I would never have met them."

"I wish I had started sooner."

"If only I hadn't been so careful."

The blue is the imagined condition (had + participle); the green is the imagined result (would have + participle).

StructureUse it forExample
Third conditional
if + past perfect, ... would have + participle		an imagined, unreal pastIf I had known, I would have gone.
wish + past perfecta regret about the pastI wish I had gone.
if only + past perfecta stronger regretIf only I hadn't waited so long.

Never say "If I would have known". The if-part uses had + participle: "If I had known, I would have gone".

Language Focus

Real or Imagined?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If I would have known, I would have gone."

✓ RIGHT

"If I had known, I would have gone."

✗ WRONG

"I wish I went on that trip."

✓ RIGHT

"I wish I had gone on that trip."

For an unreal past, use had + participle after "if", "wish" and "if only" — never "would have" after "if".

On the Trail

G
You've cycled all over — Albania, Morocco, Yemen. Was there ever a trip you almost didn't take?
M
Albania, actually. In hindsight, I nearly talked myself out of it. If I hadn't gone, I would have missed the best trip of my life.
G
It's funny, isn't it? We stand at a fork in the road and the safe path looks so tempting. I played it safe once, and I still regret it.
M
What was the road you didn't take?
G
A job abroad, when I was thirty. I had second thoughts, and I said no. If I had been braver, my whole life would have been different.
M
I know that feeling. These days I try to make the most of every chance. If only we could tell our younger selves to be less careful.
