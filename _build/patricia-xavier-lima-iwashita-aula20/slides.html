Quarterly Business Review — Northwind Logistics (Q3)

This quarterly business review covers the third quarter for Northwind Logistics, one of our premium accounts. The goal of a QBR is simple: to look back at the results, to align on what matters most, and to plan the next quarter together. Over the last three months, the team has made strong progress. Adoption has grown across all departments, and the account is now on track to reach the objectives we set at the kickoff.

The highlights are clear. Compared to last quarter, the adoption rate has increased from sixty to eighty percent, and the health score is higher than ever. The support team has resolved every critical ticket within the agreed deadline. In our last call, the customer said that the new walkthrough had saved their users a lot of time. There is one honest concern: two teams in finance are still slow to adopt, so their usage is lower than the rest.

Based on these results, we have two recommendations for next quarter. First, we suggest a short extra training for the finance teams; if they get more support, their adoption will catch up quickly. Second, because the partnership has delivered real value, we recommend that the customer renew the contract early and add ten new licenses. If the customer agreed to a longer contract, we could offer a better price. Finally, we will share a written success plan with clear objectives, and we will review the progress again in the next QBR.

Chapter 4: Your Whole Toolkit

Every Structure, in One Page

Present perfect · Comparatives · Reported speech · Conditionals

Structure Discovery

Find the Structures

"Adoption has grown across all departments."

"The health score is higher than last quarter."

"The customer said that the walkthrough had saved time."

"If they get more support, their adoption will catch up."

StructureUse in a QBRExample
Present perfectresults up to now (no exact time)Adoption has grown.
Comparativescompare to last quarterhigher than last quarter
Reported speechquote the customer (backshift)She said it had saved time.
First conditionala real plan for next quarterIf they get support, adoption will catch up.

A QBR uses your whole course at once: present perfect for results, comparatives for trends, reported speech to quote the customer, and conditionals for the plan.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We have worked together since six months."
"We have worked together for six months."
"The customer said the price is final."
"The customer said the price was final."

Use for with a length of time (not "since"). In reported speech, move the verb back: is becomes was.

Chapter 5: The Live QBR

Presenting the Quarter

Dialogue

The Live QBR

P
Thanks for joining, David. I would like to start with the results: this quarter, adoption has grown from sixty to eighty percent across your teams.
D
That is a great number. My director will be happy to hear it. How did the support team do on the critical tickets?
P
Every critical ticket was resolved within the deadline. There is one honest concern, though: the finance teams are still slow to adopt.
D
I appreciate the honesty. What do you recommend we do about it?
P
I recommend a short extra training for finance. If they get more support, their adoption will catch up quickly. Then we review it in the next QBR.
D
That works for me. The partnership has clearly delivered value, so let us talk about renewing the contract early.
Comprehension

About the Review

1. What does David ask about after hearing the adoption number?
How the support team did on the critical tickets.
2. How does David react to Patrícia's honest concern?
He appreciates the honesty and asks what she recommends.
3. What does David want to talk about at the end?
Renewing the contract early, because the partnership delivered value.
Listening

The Sponsor's Voice Note

The customer's sponsor says what he wants from the QBR. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does David want Patrícia to walk through first, on the call?
The highlights, especially how the support team handled the critical tickets.
2. What does David need before he takes the finance issue to the board?
A clear plan to fix the low usage.
3. Under what condition is David open to renewing early and adding licenses?
If the value is there — but he needs a strong business case to justify the cost.
Listening 2

The Manager's Coaching

A manager shares tips before the QBR. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does Karen suggest Patrícia open with?
The strongest highlight — the jump in adoption — not the agenda.
2. How should Patrícia handle the finance concern, according to Karen?
Do not hide it; pair the problem with a recommendation.
3. What should Patrícia talk about before price, for the renewal?
Value — once the customer sees the ROI, the price conversation is easier.
