Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

QBR -- Quarterly Business Review: encontro periódico para revisar os resultados do cliente e planejar o próximo trimestre (revisão trimestral de negócios)
"We open every QBR by looking back at the results."
Summary -- um relato curto que traz apenas os pontos principais (resumo)
"The executive summary fits the whole quarter on one page."
Highlight -- o melhor resultado que você quer que as pessoas percebam (destaque)
"The biggest highlight is the jump in adoption."
Recommendation -- uma sugestão clara sobre o que fazer a seguir (recomendação)
"Our main recommendation is a short training for finance."
Objective -- um resultado claro que você planeja e busca (objetivo)
"We set three objectives for next quarter."
Stakeholder -- pessoa com interesse no sucesso da conta (parte interessada) — revisão
"I present the QBR to the main stakeholders."
Achievement -- algo bom que você conquistou com esforço (conquista) — revisão
"Resolving every critical ticket on time was a real achievement."
Roadmap -- um plano que mostra o que vem e quando (roteiro) — revisão
"The roadmap shows two new features next quarter."
Align -- levar as pessoas ao mesmo entendimento ou objetivo (alinhar) — revisão
"A QBR helps us align on what matters most."
Next quarter -- o período de três meses que vem depois deste (próximo trimestre)
"We will review the progress again next quarter."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the end of each quarter, Patrícia runs a QBR with her customers. She has prepared the summary in advance, so the highlights are clear. Compared to last quarter, the adoption rate is higher, and the support team has resolved every critical ticket. In the last call, the customer said that the new walkthrough had saved a lot of time. There is one honest concern, so Patrícia adds a recommendation: if finance gets more training, their adoption will catch up. Finally, she aligns with the stakeholders on the objectives for next quarter.

1. Por que dizemos "adoption has grown" e não "adoption grew" num resumo de resultados?
A Porque "grew" não existe em inglês.
B Porque o present perfect mostra o resultado até agora, sem dizer exatamente quando.
C Porque o present perfect é sempre futuro.
2. No reported speech: "The customer said the walkthrough ___ time." Qual forma?
A "had saved" (o verbo recua — backshift).
B "has saved" (sem mudar nada).
C "will save".
3. Which sentence is correct (first conditional)?
A "If finance will get more training, adoption will improve."
B "If finance gets more training, adoption will improve."
C "If finance gets more training, adoption improve."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The QBR Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Uma QBR usa as estruturas de todo o curso ao mesmo tempo (explicação em inglês e português).

StructureUse / UsoExample
Present perfectresultados até agora, sem dizer quando.Adoption has grown.
Comparativescomparar com o trimestre anterior.higher than last quarter
Reported speechcitar o cliente; o verbo recua (is → was).She said it had saved time.
First conditionalum plano real: if + presente, will + verbo.If they get support, adoption will catch up.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

You open the QBR with your strongest result. You say:
A "The biggest highlight is that adoption has grown to eighty percent."
B "Let me start with the agenda and the small details."
C "Adoption grew, but I am not sure when."
You have one weak area and want to stay honest but helpful. You say:
A "Everything is perfect, no problems at all."
B "There is one honest concern, and my recommendation is a short extra training."
C "Finance is a disaster and it is their fault."
You quote what the customer told you last week. You say:
A "The customer says the walkthrough saves a lot of time."
B "The customer said the walkthrough had saved a lot of time."
C "The customer said the walkthrough will saved time."
You close the QBR by agreeing on what comes next. You say:
A "Let's align on the objectives for next quarter."
B "Let's align on the objectives for last quarter."
C "Let's not make any plan."
