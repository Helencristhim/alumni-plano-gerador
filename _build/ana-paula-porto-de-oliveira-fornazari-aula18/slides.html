FunctionPhraseExample
Ask to repeatCould you say that again?"Sorry, I did not catch that."

Buy timeLet me think for a second."Well... let me see."
Check / recapSo, just to recap...?"So you mean today, right?"

Keep the tone polite: start with "Sorry" or "Could you", and the request sounds friendly, never rude. Asking is what confident speakers do.

Language Focus

What Is Each Phrase Doing?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Repeat!" / "What?"

✓ RIGHT

"Sorry, I did not catch that. Could you say it again?"

✗ WRONG

"Please explain again the word."

✓ RIGHT

"Could you rephrase that, please?"

A single word like "What?" or an order like "Repeat!" can sound rude. Start with "Sorry" or "Could you", and the same request becomes polite and friendly.

Dialogue

A Change of Plans

A
Hi Greg. You said there is a change to our plan for Saturday?
G
Yes! We are meeting at the new place on Hazelwood Avenue, behind the old cinema, at half past six.
A
Sorry, I did not catch the street. Could you say that again?
G
Of course. Hazelwood Avenue, the street right behind the old cinema.
A
Got it. And what do you mean by half past six? Six thirty, right?
G
Exactly, six thirty. Bear with me, I know I explained it fast.
A
No problem. Hold on -- let me just recap so I do not get it wrong.
G
Please do. I would rather repeat it now than wait for you later!
A
So, Hazelwood Avenue, behind the cinema, at six thirty on Saturday. Does that make sense?
G
That is exactly right. See you there!
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. Where does Greg say they are meeting on Saturday?
On Hazelwood Avenue, behind the old cinema.
2. What time does Greg suggest?
Half past six -- six thirty.
3. How does Greg react when Ana Paula asks him to repeat and recap?
He is patient and happy to repeat it -- he would rather repeat it now.
