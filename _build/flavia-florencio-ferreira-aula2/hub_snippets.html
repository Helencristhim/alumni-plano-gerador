Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Colleague -- a person you work with
"My colleague and I plan the week together."
Office -- the place where you work at a desk
"I usually work at the office on Wednesdays."
Task -- a piece of work you have to do
"Each colleague has a different task."
To organize -- to arrange things in a clear order
"She always organizes the weekly schedule."
To plan -- to decide the steps before you do something
"We plan our projects every Monday."
Weekly -- something that happens one time every week
"The team has a weekly review."
Deadline -- the final day to finish a task
"The deadline for the report is on Friday."
Responsibility -- a duty that is yours to do
"Training is my main responsibility."
To develop -- to make something grow and get better
"We develop new ideas for local companies."
Schedule -- a plan of times and dates for your work
"I often check my schedule in the morning."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia works with a small team at Sebrae. Every Monday, they always meet to plan the week. Each colleague gets a task and a deadline. Flavia usually works at the office on Wednesdays, and she often works from home too. She always checks her schedule in the morning. Her team sometimes develops new ideas for small companies. "I never miss a deadline," she says. "Training my colleagues is my main responsibility, and I am always ready to help."

1. Why do we say "they always meet" and not "they meet always"?
A The adverb of frequency goes before the main verb.
B The adverb always goes at the end of the sentence.
C The adverb goes before the subject.
2. Why do we say "I am always ready" with "always" after "am"?
A The adverb always comes before the verb to be.
B With the verb to be, the adverb comes after it.
C The verb to be never uses adverbs.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "She checks never her schedule."
B "She never checks her schedule."
C "She checks her schedule never."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

How often something happens, and where the adverb goes in the sentence.

AdverbHow oftenExample
always100%She always plans the week.
usually~90%I usually work at the office.
often~70%We often plan on Monday.
sometimes~40%They sometimes work from home.
never0%I never miss a deadline.
PositionRuleExample
Main verbadverb before the verbI always check my schedule.
Verb "to be"adverb after am / is / areShe is always ready.
