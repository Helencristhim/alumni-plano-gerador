Chapter 4: Adverbs of Frequency

always · usually · never

How often, and where the adverb goes

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We always meet on Monday."

"I usually work at the office."

"She never misses a deadline."

"He is always ready."

AdverbHow oftenPosition
always100%before the main verb
usually / often~90% / ~70%before the main verb
sometimes~40%before the main verb
never0%before the main verb
to beThe adverb goes after am / is / are: "She is always ready."

Before the main verb (always meet), after the verb to be (is always).

Grammar Practice

Put the Adverb in Place

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"We ___always meet on Monday." (always, main verb)
"I ___usually work at the office." (usually, main verb)
"She ___never misses a deadline." (never, main verb)
"He is ___always ready." (always, verb to be)
"They ___sometimes work from home." (sometimes, main verb)
Language Focus

Before or After?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"We meet always on Monday."

✓ RIGHT

"We always meet on Monday."

✗ WRONG

"She always is ready."

✓ RIGHT

"She is always ready."

The adverb goes before the main verb (always meet), but after the verb "to be" (is always). Never put it after the main verb.

Dialogue

Pedro Asks About the Week

P
Hi Flavia. How often does the team meet?
F
We always meet on Monday to plan the week.
P
And do you usually work at the office?
F
I usually work at the office, but I often work from home too.
P
Who organizes the schedule and the deadlines?
F
That is my responsibility. I always organize the schedule.
P
Do you ever miss a deadline?
F
No, I never miss a deadline. I am always ready.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. What does Pedro ask about first?
How often the team meets.
2. When does the team meet to plan the week?
On Monday. They always meet on Monday.
3. Who organizes the schedule?
Flavia. It is her responsibility.
