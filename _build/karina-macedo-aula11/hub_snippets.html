Phone Calls -- Speaking with Confidence

Make and answer a phone call in English. Say who you are (This is Karina speaking), ask to speak with someone (May I speak with...?), leave a message, ask the other person to hold, and end the call politely. New vocabulary: phone, call, message, speak, hold, line, voicemail.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Phone -- the device you use to talk to someone far away
"I call Robert on the phone." (Telefone)
Call -- to talk to someone using the phone
"I call the office in the morning." (Ligar)
Message -- words you leave for a person who is away
"Can I leave a message?" (Recado)
Speak -- to talk with another person
"May I speak with Robert?" (Falar)
Hold -- to wait on the line for a moment
"Please hold for one moment." (Aguardar na linha)
Line -- the phone connection between two people
"The line is busy now." (Linha)
Voicemail -- a recorded message when nobody answers
"Please leave a voicemail." (Caixa postal)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Karina picked up the phone and made a call to Robert. "Hello, this is Karina speaking. May I speak with Robert, please?" Robert answered, "Please hold for one moment." A minute later he said, "Yes, how can I help you?" "I am calling to confirm our meeting on Tuesday at ten," said Karina. Sometimes the line is busy, so Karina knows she can also leave a message or a voicemail. "Thank you very much. Goodbye," she said at the end.

1. How does Karina say who she is on the phone?
A "This is Karina speaking."
B "I am a phone."
C "Karina is the line."
2. What does Robert ask Karina to do at first?
A Leave a voicemail.
B Hold for one moment.
C Call again tomorrow.
3. What can Karina do if the line is busy?
A Leave a message or a voicemail.
B Turn left at the corner.
C Buy a new aircraft.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Phone Phrases

GRAMMAR

Frases fixas usadas ao telefone. / Fixed phrases we use on the phone.

Phrase / FraseMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
This is + name + speakingDizer quem você é / Say who you areThis is Karina speaking.
May I / Can I speak with + name?Pedir para falar com alguém / Ask to talk to a personMay I speak with Robert?
Please hold for one moment.Pedir para aguardar / Ask the person to waitPlease hold for one moment.
Can I leave a message?Deixar um recado / Leave words for someoneCan I leave a message?

Key point / Ponto-chave: ao telefone, diga This is Karina (não "I am Karina") e use speak with uma pessoa. Para soar educado, adicione please: "May I speak with Robert, please?"

Lesson 11 -- Phone Calls: Speaking with Confidence

Series
The Office (US) -- any episode with phone calls

A workplace comedy with many short phone calls. You hear people answer the phone, say who they are, ask to speak with someone, and take messages in a real office.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two phone phrases you hear, such as "This is..." or "Can I take a message?".

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Telephone English: Making a Call"

A slow, clear podcast about phone calls in English: how to say who you are, ask to speak with a person, leave a message, and end the call politely.

Repeat each phrase out loud. Then record a short voicemail for a client telling them to call you back.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Telephone phrases: how to make a phone call"

A short, practical lesson on today's language: answering the phone, saying "This is... speaking", asking "May I speak with...?", holding the line, and leaving a message.

After watching, write five sentences for a business call: say who you are, ask to speak with someone, ask them to hold, leave a message, and say goodbye. Then read them aloud.
