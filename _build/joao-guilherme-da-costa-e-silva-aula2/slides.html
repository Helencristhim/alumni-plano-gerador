Chapter 3: The Code

The Story of What Never Happened

If the supplier had delivered on time, we would have finished in March.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If the supplier had delivered the interface document on time, we would have completed the handover in March."

"If procurement had placed the order in January, the lead time would not have been a problem."

"If I had prepared for that interview, I would not have frozen in the first two minutes."

"If we had more time, we would run another test tomorrow."

The green sentences are about a past that never happened. The blue one is about now. What changes?

FormUseExample
Third conditional
If + had + past participle, would have + past participle		An imagined past. The condition did NOT happen, so the result did not happen either.If the supplier had delivered on time, we would have completed commissioning by March.
Negativewouldn't have + past participleIf we had involved procurement earlier, we wouldn't have missed the milestone.
In speechBoth halves contract. This is what you actually hear on a call.If they'd sent the drawings, we'd have caught the error.
Second conditional
If + past simple, would + verb		An unreal PRESENT or future — not the past.If we had more time, we would run another test.
Why it mattersThe third conditional lets you review a failed milestone as a system problem, not a person's fault. It is the language of the professional retrospective.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the supplier would have delivered on time, we would have finished the handover."
"If the supplier had delivered on time, we would have finished the handover."
"If we had received the drawings, we would finished the review in one week."
"If we had received the drawings, we would have finished the review in one week."

The condition always takes had + past participle. Only the result takes would have + past participle. Never would have in both halves.

Hello, I'm Elena Vidal, senior engineer at Thales. I've read the brief, but I would rather hear it from you. What exactly do you own on this project?
J
I'm responsible for the signaling workstream on the Line 5 extension. I specialize in CBTC, I coordinate the design and field teams, and I liaise with every supplier on the package.

Good. And where is the project right now, honestly?
J

J

Then let us not repeat that. I will look into the interlocking with my team, and I will come back to you on Friday with a date.
