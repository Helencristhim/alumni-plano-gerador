Chapter 3 -- Polite Questions

The Indirect Question

Ask for clarity in a polite way

Discover the Pattern

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me what you mean by that?"

"Would you mind explaining the question again?"

"May I ask which figure you are asking about?"

"Could you rephrase the query, please?"

Grammar Rule

Make It Polite

OpenerDirect questionPolite (indirect)
Could you tell meWhat do you mean?Could you tell me what you mean?
Would you mindExplain the question.Would you mind explaining the question?
May I askWhich figure is it?May I ask which figure it is?
Could you + verbRephrase the query.Could you rephrase the query, please?

Key idea: After a polite opener, there is no do or does, and the verb comes after the subject: "Could you tell me what you mean" (not "what do you mean"). Use "Would you mind" + -ing.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you tell me what do you mean?"
"Could you tell me what you mean?"

After a polite opener, drop do/does and keep the normal order: subject + verb. Use Would you mind + the -ing form.
