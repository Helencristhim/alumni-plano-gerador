Aula 15 -- Pre-class

Handling Questions -- The Q&A

Handle the question and answer part of a presentation: buy time, ask for clarity with polite indirect questions (Could you tell me...? Would you mind explaining...?), and give honest answers. New vocabulary: query, rephrase, breakdown, figure, estimate, assume, address, frankly.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Query -- a question someone asks
"That is a fair query about the numbers." (Pergunta / dúvida)
Rephrase -- to say something in different words
"Could you rephrase the question, please?" (Reformular)
Breakdown -- a clear explanation of the parts or details
"Let me give you a quick breakdown of the costs." (Detalhamento)
Figure -- a number (often in business data)
"I do not have the exact figure here." (Número / cifra)
Estimate -- a number that is close but not exact
"My estimate is around five percent." (Estimativa)
Assume -- to think something is true without checking
"I do not want to assume what you mean." (Presumir / supor)
Address -- to deal with a question or problem
"I will check the data and address it later." (Tratar / lidar com)
Frankly -- honestly and directly
"Frankly, I do not have that number today." (Francamente / honestamente)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After his presentation, Rafael takes the board's questions. A member asks a difficult query about the summer numbers. Rafael stays calm. "That is a fair question," he says. "Let me give you a quick breakdown." He does not have the exact figure, so he gives an honest estimate: around five percent. Frankly, he does not want to assume what the board means, so he asks, "Could you tell me exactly which number you mean?" When one question needs more data, he promises to check and address it after the meeting.

1. What does Rafael do when he gets a difficult query?

B He guesses a random number.
C He ignores the question.
2. What does Rafael give when he has no exact figure?
A An honest estimate.
B A promise with no number.
C The wrong figure.
3. Which polite question is correct?
A "Could you tell me which number you mean?"
B "Could you tell me which number do you mean?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Usado para pedir esclarecimento de forma educada. / Used to ask for clarity politely. Depois da abertura educada, não use do/does e o verbo vem depois do sujeito.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Could you tell me + (wh)...Pedido educado / polite requestCould you tell me what you mean?
Would you mind + -ingPedido muito educado / very politeWould you mind explaining it?
May I ask + (wh)...Pedir permissão / ask permissionMay I ask which figure it is?
Key point / Ponto-chaveDepois da abertura, não use do/does e mantenha a ordem normal: sujeito + verbo. Diga "what you mean" (não "what do you mean").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best phrase for each Q&A situation.

A board member asks a hard question. You want to buy time. You say:
A "That is a fair query. Let me give you a breakdown."
B "I do not want to answer that."
C "That query is wrong."
You do not have the exact number. You say:
A "I do not have the exact figure, but my estimate is around five percent."
B "The figure is exactly five percent, I am sure."
C "I will not tell you the figure."
You did not understand the question. You ask, politely:
A "Could you tell me what you mean?"
