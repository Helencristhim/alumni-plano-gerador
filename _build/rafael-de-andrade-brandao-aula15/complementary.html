Lesson 15 -- Handling Questions

Series
Shark Tank (US) -- any pitch with a tough Q&A

Watch how entrepreneurs handle hard questions from the investors. Listen for the toolkit: how they buy time, give a breakdown, share an estimate, or admit they do not have a figure. Notice the polite indirect questions when they ask for clarity.

Write down every phrase used to handle a tough question. Then answer three imaginary investor questions out loud using query, breakdown, estimate, and address.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Handling Questions in Presentations"

Practical phrases for the Q&A part of a presentation: how to buy time, ask for a question to be rephrased, give an honest estimate, and promise to address something later. Listen for the new words: query, rephrase, breakdown, figure, estimate, assume, address, and frankly.

Note 3 polite indirect questions (Could you tell me... / Would you mind...). Practice each one out loud as if a board member just asked you something hard.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Asking polite, indirect questions"

A short, clear lesson on polite indirect questions: Could you tell me...?, Would you mind explaining...?, and May I ask...? Perfect for asking the board to clarify a query without sounding direct.

After watching, record yourself turning five direct questions into polite indirect ones. Remember: no do/does, and keep subject + verb order.
