Lesson 08 -- Pre-class

Checkpoint -- The Risk Meeting Simulation

Consolidation of Block 1: reading the minutes of a risk committee and learning the language that runs the meeting. Key words: agenda, chair, action point, owner, allocate, follow-up, sign off, recap, takeaway, brief. Focus: the question forms that a chair uses to open, review and close a meeting.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics to discuss in a meeting
"Marina sent the agenda before the meeting."
To chair -- to lead and run a meeting
"A senior manager will chair the risk committee."
Action point -- a task that someone must do after the meeting
"Each action point has an owner and a deadline."
Owner -- the person responsible for a task or action
"You are the owner of the remediation plan."
To allocate -- to give a task or time to someone
"The chair allocated ten minutes to each item."
Follow-up -- a later action to check or continue something
"The late figures need a follow-up next week."
To sign off -- to formally approve something
"The committee signed off the new control."
To recap -- to summarize the main points quickly
"Let me recap the three decisions from today."
Takeaway -- the main point to remember from a meeting
"The main takeaway is that every risk has an owner."
To brief -- to give someone the key information they need
"Sergio briefed the committee on the plan."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Marina will chair the risk committee this month, and she has already sent the agenda. There are three items, and she has allocated ten minutes to each one. The committee will first recap the year, then review the two open action points from the audit. Sergio is the owner of one of them, so he must be ready to give an update. If the committee agrees, it will sign off the remediation plan. The final item is a follow-up on the late figures, and the main takeaway is simple: every action point needs an owner and a deadline.

1. What is the correct word order for a meeting question?
A "Has the plan been signed off?" (helper before the subject)
B "The plan has been signed off?" (subject first)
C "The plan signed off has been?"
2. Which question asks about a finished meeting last month?
A "What did the committee decide last month?"
B "What has the committee decided last month?"
C "What the committee decided last month?"
3. Which question asks about a change up to now?
A "How has exposure changed since March?"
B "How exposure changed since March?"
C "How did exposure change since March until now?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Question Forms for the Meeting

GRAMMAR

How to build the questions that open, review and close a meeting. A question starts with a helper (or wh + helper), never with the subject alone.

TypeWord orderExample
Yes / No questionHelper (did / has / is) + subject + verb.Has the plan been signed off yet?
Wh- questionQuestion word + helper + subject + verb.What did the committee decide?
Tense insideFinished time → past simple; change up to now → present perfect.How has exposure changed since March?
The signalNever start a question with the subject alone. Put the helper first: not "The plan has been signed off?" but "Has the plan been signed off?"

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "The plan has been signed off?"
B "Has the plan been signed off yet?"
C "The plan is sign off?"
You ask about a decision at last month's meeting. Which is correct?
A "What did the committee decide last month?"
B "What has the committee decided last month?"
C "What the committee decided last month?"
You want to give a task to a colleague. Which is best?
A "I will follow-up you this task."
