Chapter 3: The Code

The Questions That Run the Room

how a chair asks · how you answer

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"What did the committee decide last month?"

"Has the plan been signed off yet?"

"Who is the owner of this action point?"

"How has exposure changed since March?"

What always comes right after the question word — the subject, or a helper like did / has / is?

Question typeWord orderExample
Yes / No questionHelper (did / has / is) + subject + verb.Has the plan been signed off?
Wh- questionQuestion word + helper + subject + verb.What did the committee decide?
The tense insideFinished time (last month, at the meeting) → past simple (did ... decide). Change up to now (yet, since, so far) → present perfect (has ... been signed off).

A question always starts with a helper (or wh + helper) — never with the subject alone. Keep the tense you learned: finished = past simple, up to now = present perfect.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"What the committee decided last month?"
"What did the committee decide last month?"
"The plan has been signed off?"
"Has the plan been signed off?"
"How long you are the owner of this plan?"
"How long have you been the owner of this plan?"

A question needs a helper (did, has, is) before the subject — never the statement word order. And keep the tense: a finished meeting takes the past simple, a change up to now takes the present perfect.

Listening

The Briefing Before the Meeting

A message from the chair before the committee. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What are the three items on Marina's agenda?
Recap the year, review the two open action points, and sign off the remediation plan.
2. Why does Marina ask Sergio to be ready?
He is the owner of one of the open action points and must give an update.
3. What follow-up might move to next week, and why?
The credit desk point, because the final figures have not arrived yet.
Listening 2

Paulo's Note

A message before the meeting. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What has Paulo already done about his action point?
He finished it: the new control was tested, and it works.
2. What does Paulo say about Sergio's remediation plan?
It is ready to be signed off, so Sergio can present it with confidence.
3. What two things does Paulo ask Sergio to do?
Recap the main takeaways after the committee, and allocate the follow-up on the late figures to him.

Chair One Full Item

Scenario: Chair the "open action points" item. Take questions from the committee, answer them, allocate each action point to an owner, and set a follow-up.
