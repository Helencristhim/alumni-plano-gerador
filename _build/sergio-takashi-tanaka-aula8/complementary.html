Talk
"How to run a meeting" -- David Grady / Jason Fried ideas (TED)

A short, sharp talk on why most meetings fail and how a clear agenda and real action points fix them. Connection to Lesson 8: this is the checkpoint theme -- a meeting only works when every point has an owner and a follow-up. Listen for how a good chair keeps the room on time.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Then, out loud, turn one idea into a meeting question: "Who is the owner of this action point?" or "Has this been signed off yet?"

Podcast
"HBR IdeaCast" -- Harvard Business Review

Clear, professional interviews on how managers lead teams, run committees and make decisions, in American English. Connection to Lesson 8: many episodes are, in effect, a well-run meeting -- an agenda of questions, clear points, and takeaways. Great to rebuild the ear for real business talk.

Tip: pick one episode, listen once for the main idea, then recap it in three sentences out loud, as if you were closing a meeting: "The main takeaways are..."

Read
"Running Effective Meetings" -- MindTools

A clear, practical guide to the language and structure of a meeting: setting an agenda, chairing, assigning action points, and following up. Connection to Lesson 8: reading is your strength, so use it to collect the exact phrases a chair says, then bring them to your next committee at XP.

Tip: read the article, then close it and run a 60-second mock meeting out loud: open with the agenda, allocate one action point, and end with the takeaways.
