Chapter 3: Indirect Questions

When the Question Softens

if / whether + statement word order

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Where were you?" → Could you tell me where you were?

"Did you see him?" → Can you tell me whether you saw him?

"What happened?" → Do you know what happened?

"Why did she leave?" → I would like to know why she left.

Direct questionIndirect questionWhat changes
"Where were you?"...where you werestatement word order
"Did you see?"...whether you sawadd if / whether, drop did
"What does it mean?"...what it meansdrop does, no inversion
"Why did she leave?"...why she leftdrop did, verb stays past

Open with "Could you tell me...", "Do you know...", or "I would like to know...". Inside, use statement order: subject + verb, no inversion, no do / does / did.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me where were you?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me where you were?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you know what time is it?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you know what time it is?"

Inside an indirect question the word order is the same as a statement: the subject comes before the verb, and the auxiliary do / does / is moves out of the question slot.

Chapter 4: In the Courtroom

Preparing the Questions

Luiz and a colleague plan the cross

Dialogue

How Will You Ask?

S
Luiz, how will you open your cross-examination tomorrow?
L
I will ask indirectly first. Could you tell me where you were at nine o'clock? Nothing aggressive.
S
And if the witness becomes evasive?
L
Then I rephrase the question and probe one fact at a time. I never badger her.
S
Do you plan to impeach her credibility?
L
Only if I find an inconsistency between her statement and her answer today.
S
What if she refuses to clarify?
L
I ask the judge whether the witness may be directed to answer. Calm, but firm.
Comprehension

About the Plan

1. How will the cross-examination open?
Indirectly, with a polite question.
2. What is the plan if the witness becomes evasive?
Rephrase the question and probe one fact at a time.
3. When will the credibility be impeached?
Only if an inconsistency is found.
4. What will be asked of the judge if she refuses to clarify?
Whether the witness may be directed to answer.
