Lesson 2 -- My Story So Far: Past Simple vs Present Perfect (Life Experiences)

Series
The Repair Shop (BBC) -- any episode where people share the story behind an old object

A gentle, warm show with no rush. People bring an object from their past and tell its story. The English is calm and clear, and the speakers naturally mix finished moments ("My father gave me this in 1975") with life experiences ("I have kept it ever since"). Perfect for hearing both pasts in real, heartfelt conversation.

Tip: pause after one story and write two sentences -- one with the past simple (a clear time) and one with the present perfect (a life experience).

Podcast
The British Council -- "LearnEnglish Podcasts" (episodes on travel and life experiences)

Made for English learners, so the pace is comfortable and the speakers are easy to follow. Many episodes have people describing experiences -- trips they have taken, things they have tried, places they have been. You will hear "Have you ever...?" and "I have already..." used naturally, exactly like in class.

Tip: after one episode, record three lines of your own life: one finished moment, one experience with "I have", and one thing you have not done yet.

YouTube
TED -- "Your elusive creative genius" by Elizabeth Gilbert (a clear talk on life and the courage to keep going)

A warm, encouraging talk about a life story, fear, and finding the courage to keep doing what you love -- a theme close to your own journey with English. The speaker is clear and calm, and she often reflects on what she has done and learned over the years, mixing finished moments with life experiences.

Tip: turn on English subtitles. Each time you hear "I have" + a verb, pause and decide: is it a finished moment, or a life experience?
