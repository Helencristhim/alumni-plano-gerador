Asking Questions in Sessions (Q&A)

Asking polite questions in a session. Key words: session, audience, raise, clarify, follow-up, briefly, point, wonder, appreciate, concern. Structures: indirect questions (Could you tell me... / I was wondering if... / May I ask...) with subject before verb.

Stage 6.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Session -- sessão (a period of time for talks and discussion)
"The morning session ends with a short Q&A."
Audience -- plateia, público (the people who watch and listen to a talk)
"The audience had many questions for the speaker."
Raise -- levantar (a mão), trazer (um ponto) (to lift your hand or bring up a point)
"May I raise a quick point about your data?"
Clarify -- esclarecer (to make something clearer)
"Could you clarify what you mean by fair value?"
Follow-up -- pergunta de acompanhamento (a second question that builds on the first)
"May I ask a quick follow-up question?"
Briefly -- brevemente (in a short way, using few words)
"Could you briefly explain your method?"
Point -- ponto, argumento (an idea or argument you want to make)
"That is a very good point. Let me answer it."
Wonder -- querer saber, indagar (to think about and want to know something)
"I was wondering how you reached that figure."
Appreciate -- agradecer, valorizar (to be grateful or thankful)
"I appreciate your clear answer, thank you."
Concern -- preocupação (a worry or important issue)
"My main concern is the accuracy of the data."

Stage 6.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After the talk, the host opens the floor: "Any questions?" Marcos raises his hand. Instead of a blunt question, he uses an indirect one: "May I ask what method you used?" The speaker answers. Then Marcos adds a follow-up: "I was wondering how you handled missing data." Finally, he asks to clarify a term: "Could you clarify what you mean by conservative?" Notice the word order in the second part -- subject before verb (where the data is, not "where is the data"). He ends politely: "I appreciate your clear answer."

1. Por que "Could you tell me where the data is?" é melhor que "Where is the data?"
A Porque é mais curta.
B Porque é uma indirect question e soa mais educada.
C Porque usa o passado.
2. Na indirect question, qual é a ordem das palavras na segunda parte?
A verbo antes do sujeito (where IS the data)
B sujeito antes do verbo (where the data IS)
C sempre com "do/did" no meio
3. What does Marcos do at the end of his question?
A He leaves without saying anything.
B He thanks the speaker: "I appreciate your clear answer."
C He asks the same question again.

Stage 6.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Como fazer perguntas educadas (explicação em inglês e português).

ToolUse / UsoExample
Could you tell me + (sujeito + verbo)Pedido educado de informaçãoCould you tell me where it is?
I was wondering if / how...Pergunta suave e indiretaI was wondering how you did it.
May I ask + (palavra interrogativa)Pedir permissão para perguntarMay I ask what method you used?
Ordem: sujeito ANTES do verboSem inversão na 2ª parte: ...where the data is (não "is the data").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação numa sessão.

You want to ask the speaker politely about their method. You say:
A "Tell me the method now."
B "May I ask what method you used?"
C "Method what is?"
Which indirect question has the correct word order?
A "Could you tell me where is the report?"
B "Could you tell me where the report is?"
C "Could you tell me where does the report?"
You did not understand a term. You ask:
A "Could you clarify what you mean by fair value?"
B "What? I no understand."
C "Repeat all, please."
The speaker gave a clear answer. You reply:
A "Okay, next."
B "I appreciate your clear answer, thank you."
C "That is wrong."
