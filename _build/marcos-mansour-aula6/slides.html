Chapter 3

Indirect Questions

Polite questions that keep the word order

Grammar Discovery

Direct vs Indirect

Direct: "Where is the data from?" → "Could you tell me where the data is from?"

Direct: "How did you reach this figure?" → "I was wondering how you reached this figure."

"May I ask what method you used?"

"Could you clarify what you mean by fair value?"

ToolUseExample
Could you tell me + (sub + verb)Polite request for informationCould you tell me where it is?
I was wondering if / how...Soft, indirect questionI was wondering how you did it.
May I ask + (question word)Ask permission to askMay I ask what method you used?
Word order: subject BEFORE verbNo inversion in the second part...where the data is (not "is the data")
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me where is the data?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me where the data is?"

✗ WRONG

"I was wondering how did you reach it."

✓ RIGHT

"I was wondering how you reached it."

✗ WRONG

"Give me the number now." (too direct)

✓ RIGHT

"May I ask what the final figure was?"

In indirect questions, keep subject before verb and drop the extra "do/did".

Chapter 4

The Q&A Moment

Asking a speaker after the talk

Dialogue

May I Ask a Question?

E
Thank you all for listening. We have a few minutes for questions. Yes, the gentleman in the front.
M
Thank you for the talk. May I ask what method you used to reach the final figure?
E
That is a great point. We compared similar recent sales and adjusted for the market.
M
I appreciate that. May I raise a quick follow-up? I was wondering how you handled missing data.
E
Good question. When data was missing, we used a conservative estimate and noted it clearly in the report.
M
Could you clarify what you mean by conservative? My main concern is accuracy.
E
By conservative, I mean the lower, safer value. This protects the client and keeps the figure reliable.
M
That makes it very clear. Thank you, I appreciate your time.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What method did the speaker use to reach the figure?
She compared similar recent sales and adjusted for the market.
2. How did the speaker handle missing data?
She used a conservative estimate and noted it clearly in the report.
3. What does the speaker mean by "conservative"?
The lower, safer value -- it protects the client and keeps the figure reliable.

To find the speaker after the session and ask there.
