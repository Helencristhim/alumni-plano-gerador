Lesson 17 -- Handling Problems & Complaints

Series
The Good Doctor -- any episode where a team handles a complaint or a delayed procedure

Watch how staff handle a problem under pressure: they apologize, explain the issue, and offer a clear solution. A natural model for handling a complaint at work calmly.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two ways someone apologizes ("I apologize for..." / "Sorry for the inconvenience") and one first-conditional sentence (If..., we will...).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Handle an Angry Customer or Colleague"

The hosts explain how to handle a complaint the professional way: apologize, stay calm, explain the problem, and offer a clear solution with a deadline.

Note three tips for handling a complaint. Then record a one-minute call for a real problem at work, using at least four new words such as complaint, apologize, solution and backup.

YouTube
Business English -- "The First Conditional & Handling Complaints on the Phone"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to use the first conditional (If..., we will...) and how to apologize and offer a solution on a call.

After watching, write five sentences for a real situation at work: one apology, two first-conditional solutions (If..., we will...), one offer of help (I can / I will), and one that reassures the other person. Then say them out loud.
