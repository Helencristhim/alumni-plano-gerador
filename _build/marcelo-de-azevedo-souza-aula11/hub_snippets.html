Small World -- Retelling the Conversations You Have on the Road

Meeting people while travelling and passing their stories on with reported speech. Key words: to run into someone, a chance encounter, a fellow traveler, to swap stories, an anecdote, to bring up, to rave about, to warn, to insist, to reckon, to keep in touch, it is a small world. Focus: reported speech (he told me he had..., she said she was..., she asked if...) -- stepping the tense one step back, say vs tell, and reported questions.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To run into someone -- to meet someone unexpectedly, by chance (encontrar alguém por acaso)
"I ran into an old colleague at the airport."
A chance encounter -- an unplanned meeting with someone (um encontro casual, sem planejar)
"A chance encounter on the ferry changed my whole trip."
A fellow traveler -- another person who is also travelling (outro viajante, um companheiro de viagem)
"A fellow traveler told me about a hidden valley."
To swap stories -- to exchange personal stories with someone (trocar histórias, contar causos um ao outro)
"We sat by the fire and swapped stories all night."
An anecdote -- a short, interesting personal story (uma anedota, um causo curto)
"He told a wonderful anecdote about a lost train ticket."
To bring up -- to introduce or mention a topic in conversation (trazer à tona, mencionar um assunto)
"She brought up her time working in Japan."
To rave about -- to talk about something with great enthusiasm (elogiar muito, falar maravilhas de algo)
"Everyone raves about the coffee in that little town."
To warn -- to tell someone about a possible danger or problem (avisar, alertar sobre um perigo)
"He warned me not to hike alone after dark."
To insist -- to say something firmly and repeatedly (insistir, fazer questão)
"She insisted that I try the local dish."
To reckon -- to think or believe something, informal (achar, considerar — informal)
"I reckon the north is the best part of the country."
To keep in touch -- to stay in contact with someone over time (manter contato)
"We met once but we still keep in touch."
It is a small world -- you say this when you find an unexpected connection (como o mundo é pequeno)
"You know my brother? It is a small world!"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At a refuge in Norway, Marcelo ran into a fellow traveler called Marit. They swapped stories by the fire, and Marit retold a conversation she had just had. She said a Chilean cyclist had cycled the whole coast alone. Notice the shift: he actually said, "I have cycled the coast", but when Marit retold it, "have cycled" became "had cycled". She added that he had warned her the wind was brutal, and that he insisted the north was the most beautiful part. Marcelo smiled: a fellow traveler in Albania once told him he had to see the fjords, and, he reckoned, she was right. When we retell what people said, each verb takes one step back in time — and we use tell + a person (he told me) but say with no person.

1. O ciclista disse "I have cycled the coast". Por que Marit reconta como "he had cycled"?
A Na fala reportada, o verbo recua um passo no tempo (present perfect → past perfect).
B Porque ele fez a viagem duas vezes.
C Porque "had cycled" e "have cycled" são iguais.
2. Which sentence uses "tell" correctly?
A "He told that the wind was brutal."
B "He told me that the wind was brutal."
C "He said me that the wind was brutal."
3. How do we report the question "Where are you from?"
A "He asked where was I from."
B "He asked where I was from."
C "He asked where do I come from."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Como reportar o que alguém disse (explicação em inglês e português).

Direct speechReported speechRegra / Rule
"I love Norway."She said she loved Norway.present → past (recua um passo)
"I have cycled the coast."He told me he had cycled the coast.present perfect/past → past perfect
"I will call you."She said she would call me.will → would; can → could
"Are you alone?"He asked if I was alone.pergunta: if/whether, sem inversão

Atenção: use tell + pessoa ("he told me") mas say sem pessoa ("she said that..."). Nunca "He told that..." nem "She said me...". Em perguntas reportadas, mantenha a ordem normal: "where I was", nunca "where was I".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real.

A guide said: "The trail is closed today." How do you report it later?
A "The guide told the trail is closed today."
B "The guide told me the trail was closed that day."
C "The guide say me the trail is close."
A stranger said: "I have lived here all my life." Which report is correct?
A "She said she had lived there all her life."
B "She said she has lived here all her life."
C "She told that she lived here."
Someone asked you: "Do you like it here?" How do you report the question?
A "He asked do I like it here."
B "He asked if I liked it there."
C "He asked if did I like it."
You want to say a friend was very enthusiastic about a café. Which is best?
A "She warned me about the café."
B "She raved about the café and insisted I go."
C "She reckoned about the café."

A father walks the Camino de Santiago and, along the way, keeps running into the same handful of fellow travelers -- a Dutchman, a Canadian, an Irish writer. They swap stories, argue, and slowly become friends. It is the whole spirit of tonight's lesson: the chance encounters on the road, and the stories you carry home and retell. Connection to Lesson 11: listen for how each character retells their own past -- what people told them, what they were warned about, why they came.

Tip: after one scene, pause and retell it out loud in reported speech -- "He told her he had come because...", "She said she wanted to...".

The Moth -- true stories told live -- any episode

Ordinary people stand on a stage and tell one true story from their own lives, with no notes. It is the purest school of storytelling there is -- and almost every story is full of reported speech ("she told me I was crazy", "he said he would never come back"). Perfect ear training for retelling: hear how native speakers report what others said, naturally and with feeling.

Tip: pick one story, and afterwards summarise it to yourself in three reported sentences -- "The storyteller said that...", "Her mother told her...", "In the end she realised...".
