Aula 11 -- Small World

Film
The Way (2010) -- directed by Emilio Estevez

A father walks the Camino de Santiago and, along the way, keeps running into the same handful of fellow travelers -- a Dutchman, a Canadian, an Irish writer. They swap stories, argue, and slowly become friends. It is the whole spirit of tonight's lesson: the chance encounters on the road, and the stories you carry home and retell. Connection to Lesson 11: listen for how each character retells their own past -- what people told them, what they were warned about, why they came.

Tip: after one scene, pause and retell it out loud in reported speech -- "He told her he had come because...", "She said she wanted to...".

Podcast
The Moth -- true stories told live -- any episode

Ordinary people stand on a stage and tell one true story from their own lives, with no notes. It is the purest school of storytelling there is -- and almost every story is full of reported speech ("she told me I was crazy", "he said he would never come back"). Perfect ear training for retelling: hear how native speakers report what others said, naturally and with feeling.

Tip: pick one story, and afterwards summarise it to yourself in three reported sentences -- "The storyteller said that...", "Her mother told her...", "In the end she realised...".

YouTube
Reported Speech: The Grammar Gameshow Episode 25 (BBC Learning English)

A fun, fast quiz-show lesson devoted entirely to tonight's grammar: how to report what people said, when to step the tense back, and the difference between say and tell. It puts the rules from your Grammar Tip into clear, memorable examples -- exactly what you need before retelling stories out loud.

Tip: pause before each answer and say your own version first, then check. Note every time the tense steps back (is to was, will to would).
