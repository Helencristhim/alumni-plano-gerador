Chapter 3: The Code

Reported Speech

Retelling what people said · one step back in time

Grammar Discovery

One Step Back

"I love Norway." → She said she loved Norway.

"I have cycled the coast." → He told me he had cycled the coast.

"I will call you." → She said she would call me.

"Where are you from?" → He asked where I was from.

Each orange verb takes one step back in time when we report it later.

Direct speechReported speech
present simple (love)past simple (loved)
present perfect / past (have cycled)past perfect (had cycled)
will / can / maywould / could / might
question "Where are you from?"asked where I was from (no inversion)

say vs tell: use tell + a person (he told me), but say takes no person (she said that...). Reported questions use if / whether and normal word order — no do, no inversion.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"He told that he had cycled alone."
"He told me that he had cycled alone."
"She asked where was I from."
"She asked where I was from."

tell needs a person (told me); say does not. And a reported question keeps normal word order — "where I was", never "where was I".

Chapter 4: At the Refuge

Swapping Stories with Marit

A fellow traveler by the fire in Norway

Dialogue

By the Fire

M
You know, I keep running into you at these refuges — it really is a small world. I was just swapping stories with a couple from Chile, and the husband told me he had cycled the whole coast of Norway alone.
M
No way. What a story. Did he say how long it had taken him?
M
He said it had taken him two months, and he warned me the wind on the coast was brutal. He insisted the north was the most beautiful part, though.
M
Funny you bring that up. A fellow traveler in Albania once raved about the north of Norway. She told me I had to see the fjords before I got too old, and I reckon she was right.
M
So here you are. See, that chance encounter changed your plans. What else did she tell you?
M
She asked me why I kept putting the trip off, and I did not have a good answer. So I promised myself I would finally come. We still keep in touch, actually.
