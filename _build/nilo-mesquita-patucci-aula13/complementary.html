Lesson 13 -- Handling Questions

Series
The Crown -- any press conference or briefing scene

Officials face sharp questions from the press and answer with calm, indirect language: they clarify, buy time, break a question down, and decline politely when they cannot answer. You will hear exactly the kind of pressure a Q and A creates and how confident people handle it.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two indirect questions and one polite way someone avoids answering without losing face.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Handling Questions and Q&A"

Practical episodes on managing the question and answer session: asking for clarification, rephrasing, breaking down complex questions, staying honest when you do not know, and using polite indirect questions. Exactly today's language in real business meetings.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to buy time or clarify. Then record yourself answering an imaginary tough question using two of them.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Indirect Questions"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to soften a direct question with openers like Could you tell me, May I ask, and Do you know if, using statement word order and adding if for yes/no questions.

After watching, turn five direct questions about your next Corinthians meeting into polite indirect questions, and say each one out loud.
