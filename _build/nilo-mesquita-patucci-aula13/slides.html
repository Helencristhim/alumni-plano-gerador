Chapter 3 -- Polite Questions

Grammar Discovery

Ask softly, sound professional

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me what the budget is?" (softer than: What is the budget?)

"May I ask why you think that?" (softer than: Why do you think that?)

"Do you know if the report is ready?" (softer than: Is the report ready?)

"I was wondering if you could clarify that." (a very polite request)

Grammar Rule

Indirect Questions

Direct questionPolite openerIndirect question
What is the budget?Could you tell meCould you tell me what the budget is?
Why do you think that?May I askMay I ask why you think that?
Is the report ready?Do you know ifDo you know if the report is ready?
Can you clarify that?I was wondering ifI was wondering if you could clarify that.

Key idea: after the polite opener, use statement word order (the budget is, not is the budget) and no do / does. For yes/no questions, add if or whether: Do you know if it is ready?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you tell me what is the deadline?"
"Could you tell me what the deadline is?"

After a polite opener, use statement order (what the deadline is) and never add do or does.

Grammar Practice

Make it Indirect

"Could you tell me what the figure _____is?" (statement order)
"Do you know _____if the report is ready?" (yes/no question)
"_____May I ask why you think that?" (polite opener)
"I was _____wondering if you could clarify that." (very polite)
