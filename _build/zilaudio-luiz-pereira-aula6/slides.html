Chapter 4

The Code

Grammar Discovery

Comparing Two Offers

Riverport is cheaper than Grainline.
Grainline's price is higher than Riverport's.
A big order is more expensive than a small one.
Free delivery is better than paid delivery.
AdjectiveComparativeRule
cheapcheapershort word — add -er
highhighershort word — add -er
expensivemore expensivelong word — use more
goodbetterirregular — a new word

To compare two things, use the comparative + than. Short adjectives take -er (cheap → cheaper). Long adjectives take more (more expensive). A few are irregular: good → better, bad → worse.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Riverport is more cheap than Grainline."
"Riverport is cheaper than Grainline."
"Grainline is expensiver than Riverport."
"Grainline is more expensive than Riverport."

Short adjectives take -er: cheap → cheaper. Long adjectives take more: expensive → more expensive. Never mix the two.

Grammar · Comparatives

Short, Long & Irregular

TypeAdjective → ComparativeIn a deal
Short (-er)low → lowerRiverport's price is lower.
Short (-er)fast → fasterGrainline's delivery is faster.
Long (more)important → more importantThe total is more important.
Irregularbad → worseA short payment time is worse for you.

Short adjectives are easy: just add -er. Long adjectives take more. Irregular ones are a new word — learn them one by one.
