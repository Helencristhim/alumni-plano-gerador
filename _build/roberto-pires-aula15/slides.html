Chapter 3: Keeping It Going

Keep the Chat Going

"Yes, I am. What about you?"

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Where are you from? — I am from Brazil. What about you?"

"Are you married? — Yes, I am. And you?"

"Do you like the city? — Yes, I do. It is lovely."

"Is this your first time here? — No, it is not. What about you?"

QuestionShort answerSend it back
Are you...?Yes, I am. / No, I am not.What about you?
Do you...?Yes, I do. / No, I do not.And you?
Where / What...?I am from... / I like...What about you?

To keep a chat going, do TWO things: give a short answer, then send the question back with "What about you?" or "And you?".

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Are you married?" — "Yes."

✓ RIGHT

"Are you married?" — "Yes, I am. What about you?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you like the city?" — "Yes, I like."

✓ RIGHT

"Do you like the city?" — "Yes, I do. And you?"

Chapter 4: A Chat with a Local

Meeting a Local

Roberto has a friendly chat on the street

Dialogue

Roberto Meets a Local

L
Hello! The weather is lovely today, isn't it? Are you enjoying our neighborhood?
R
Yes, I am! It is very nice. Are you a local?
L
Yes, I am. I live nearby. We are very friendly here and we love to chat. Where are you from?
R
My hometown is Sao Paulo, in Brazil. What about you? Do you know a good place with a nice view?
L
Yes, I do! There is a park nearby with a beautiful view. By the way, are you married?
R
Yes, I am. And you? It was a lovely chat. Thank you for being so friendly!

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Have a Real Chat

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play

On the Street

Scenario: A friendly local says hello on the street. Start a small talk: comment on the weather, answer their questions, and send a question back.

Key phrases:

The weather is lovely today. Yes, I am. What about you? Where are you from?
Role-Play

Keep It Going

Scenario: Have a longer chat with the local. Ask about their hometown and the neighborhood, and answer their questions with a short answer plus "What about you?".

Tools:

two questions a short answer + follow-up hometown / neighborhood
Free Practice

Your Own Chat

Scenario: You meet a friendly local at a cafe with a lovely view. Start the small talk, comment on the weather, ask where they are from, talk about your hometown, answer their questions with short answers and follow-ups, and end the chat in a warm, friendly way.
