From the Proposal to the Contract

Leadership approved your supplier-consolidation proposal. Now you negotiate the contract -- and every concession must be tied to a condition.

Weak: "Okay, fine, we can lower the penalty, and we will hold the rate, and let's just sign it."

Strong: "Were you to bring the lead time down to ten days, we would halve the penalty -- provided that the liability cap stays."

Today we negotiate terms:

How do you trade a bargaining chip for a condition, hold firm on the penalty clause, and send a sharp redline -- "provided that", "should the supplier...", "were you to..." -- so the contract is balanced and binding?

Chapter 3

Bargaining Conditionals

Were / Should / provided that / unless / on condition that

Discovery

Trade a Condition for a Result

Were you to bring the lead time down to ten days, we would halve the penalty. (inverted 2nd conditional)

Should the supplier miss a deadline, the penalty clause applies. (inverted 1st conditional)

We can hold the rate, provided that you commit to the freight volume. (condition = only if this is true)

Unless we agree a liability cap, I cannot recommend this. (unless = if not)

Either party may terminate on condition that all deliverables are settled. (strict, formal condition)

We will drop the penalty rate, as long as the concession is binding. (as long as = provided that)

Grammar Rule

Conditional Variations

FormMeaningExample
Should + subject + base verbif it happens (real, formal)Should the supplier miss a deadline, the penalty applies.
Were + subject + to + baseif you did (hypothetical offer)Were you to bring the lead time down, we would halve the penalty.
provided that / as long as / on condition thatonly if this is trueWe hold the rate provided that you commit to the volume.
unlessif not / except ifUnless we agree a cap, I cannot sign.
Key: commaWhen the condition comes first, put a comma before the result clause.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If were you to drop the rate, we would sign. Unless if we get a cap, no deal." (extra "if" in the inverted form and after "unless")
"Were you to drop the rate, we would sign. Unless we get a cap, no deal." (inverted "were" replaces "if"; "unless" already means "if not")

The inverted forms Were you to... and Should the supplier... already contain the "if" -- never add it. And unless = "if not", so never write "unless if".

Grammar Practice

Build the Condition

_____ the supplier miss a confirmed deadline, the penalty clause applies. (inverted, real condition) _____Should
_____ you to bring the lead time down to ten days, we would halve the penalty. (inverted, hypothetical) _____Were
We can hold the rate, _____ that you commit to the renegotiated volume. (only if this is true) _____provided
_____ we agree a liability cap, I cannot recommend this to the board. (if not) _____Unless

Listening 1

The Counter-Offer

Fabiana lays out the four-point counter-offer in the contract review

0:000:00
1. On what condition will Fabiana hold the rate for the full term?
She will hold the rate provided that the supplier commits to the renegotiated freight volume.
2. What is the concession on the penalty, and what condition is attached?
Were the supplier to bring the lead time down to ten days, she would drop the penalty rate by half -- as a sign of good faith.
3. What is non-negotiable, and what are the four points of the counter-offer?
A liability cap of two million per incident is non-negotiable (unless agreed, she cannot recommend it). The four points: hold the rate, halve the penalty for a ten-day lead time, cap liability at two million, and tighten termination -- terminate on condition that all deliverables are settled.

Counter-Offer Term Sheet

Bloomberg Supply Chain -- Supplier Contract: Counter-Offer Term Sheet (Lead: Fabiana)

We hold the rate for the full term provided that the supplier commits to the renegotiated freight volume.
PENALTY
Should the supplier miss a confirmed deadline, the penalty clause applies in full, with no waiver. Were the supplier to reach a ten-day lead time, we would halve the rate as our bargaining chip.
LIABILITY
