Lesson 19 -- Negotiating Contracts and Terms

Series
Suits (USA Network) -- The negotiation and closing scenes

Watch the scenes where the lawyers negotiate terms: holding firm on a clause, trading a concession for a condition, and refusing to sign unless a cap is in place. Notice how the strongest negotiators tie every "yes" to an "if" -- "provided that", "should they breach", "unless we agree" -- exactly the bargaining conditionals you practiced today.

Pick one negotiation and write three conditional offers from it, one using "Should...", one using "Were... to...", and one using "provided that" or "unless".

Podcast
Negotiate Anything -- "Making Concessions Without Losing the Deal"

An episode on conditional concessions: how to give something only in exchange for a condition, hold firm on the points that matter (the liability cap, the penalty clause), and push back in good faith. Directly relevant to negotiating a supplier contract precisely.

Note two concessions the guest makes, and rewrite each as a conditional offer ("Were you to..., we would..." / "provided that...").

YouTube
"Inverted Conditionals: Were, Should, Had" -- Advanced English Grammar

A clear guide to the exact structures in this lesson: how "Should the supplier miss..." and "Were you to bring..." replace plain "if" in formal English, plus "provided that", "as long as", "on condition that" and "unless" -- the precise conditional register of a contract negotiation.

After watching, write a four-line term sheet in which every line contains a different bargaining conditional (Should / Were / provided that / unless).
