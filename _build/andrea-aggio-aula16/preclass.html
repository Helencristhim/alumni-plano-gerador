Aula 16 -- Pre-class

Analyzing Results and Making Deductions

How to read business results and deduce the cause using past modals of deduction. Key words: figures, target, to exceed, to underperform, downturn, to bounce back, root cause, to account for, assumption, benchmark, margin, quarter. Structure: must have + pp (near certainty) vs might/could have + pp (possible) vs can't have + pp (near certainty it did not happen).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Figures -- the numbers or data in a report (numeros, dados)
"The quarterly figures are on the second slide."
Target -- the specific number or goal you aim to reach (meta)
"We set an ambitious target for the summer."
To exceed -- to go beyond a target or expectation (superar, exceder)
"Bookings exceeded the target by ten percent."
To underperform -- to do worse than expected (ter desempenho abaixo do esperado)
"The city hotels underperformed this quarter."
Downturn -- a fall in business activity or sales (queda, retracao)
"The downturn hit corporate travel hardest."
To bounce back -- to recover after a difficult period (se recuperar)
"Leisure travel bounced back in June."
Root cause -- the underlying reason something happened (causa raiz)
"The root cause was a pricing error."
To account for -- to explain the reason for something (explicar, justificar)
"The heatwave accounts for the drop in July."
Assumption -- something you believe is true without proof (suposicao, premissa)
"That was an assumption, not a fact."
Benchmark -- a standard used to compare performance (referencia, parametro)
"We measure ourselves against the industry benchmark."
Margin -- the difference between cost and selling price (margem de lucro)
"Discounts cut into our margin."
Quarter -- a three-month period in the business year (trimestre)
"Revenue rose in the third quarter."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea reviews the quarter's figures with her team. Leisure exceeded the target, so the campaign must have worked. Corporate underperformed, but since the whole market saw a downturn, the company can't have caused the drop alone. The margin was thinner, so the discounts might have been too deep -- though Andrea calls that an assumption, not a root cause. 'That accounts for part of it,' she says, 'but let's test it against the benchmark before we act. Leisure bounced back; now we find out why corporate didn't.'

1. 'The campaign must have worked' shows the speaker is:
A almost certain it happened.
B only guessing it is possible.
C sure it did NOT happen.
2. Which form means 'I am almost certain it did NOT happen'?
A might have + pp
B can't have + pp
C must have + pp
3. Which sentence is correct?
A The weather might have affected demand.
B The weather might affected demand.
C The weather might have affect demand.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Modals of Deduction

GRAMMAR

Como deduzir a causa de um resultado passado (explicacao em ingles e portugues).

FormUse / UsoExample
must have + ppquase certeza de que aconteceu. near certainty it happened.Sales rose -- it must have worked.
might / may / could have + pppossibilidade, sem certeza. possible, not sure.The weather might have affected it.
can't / couldn't have + ppquase certeza de que NAO aconteceu. near certainty it did NOT happen.They can't have seen the rates.
Regra de ouro / Golden rulesempre modal + have + participio. always modal + have + past participle.must / might / can't + have + done.
