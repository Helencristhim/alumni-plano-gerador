Stage 1.1: Chat Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight expressions for short, professional team chat. Listen to each one.

Ping -- to send a short message to get someone's attention
"If you need me, just ping me in the thread." (Cutucar / chamar)
Heads-up -- an early warning or advance notice about something
"Quick heads-up: the partner is now in this channel." (Aviso prévio)
Reach out -- to make contact with someone, often to ask for help
"Reach out to me directly if you are stuck." (Entrar em contato)
Circle back -- to return to a topic or person at a later time
"Let us circle back on pricing tomorrow." (Retomar depois)
Touch base -- to have a brief check-in to share or align on something
"Let us touch base before the partner call." (Alinhar rapidamente)
Async -- not at the same time; people reply in their own time
"Async is fine — answer when you get a moment." (Assíncrono)
Status update -- a brief note telling others where a task currently stands
"Quick status update: the deck is ready, sending now." (Atualização de status)
Typo -- a small spelling or typing mistake in written text
"Re-read before you send — a typo looks careless." (Erro de digitação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

The partner conversation moves into Eduardo's team Slack channel, so Sarah gives him a heads-up about tone. Chat is not email: he drops the long salutation and the formal sign-off, but he keeps the politeness in a softener. Instead of "Send the numbers" he writes, "Could you send the numbers when you get a sec? No rush." If he needs someone he will ping them in the existing thread, not start a new one. He promises to circle back on pricing by end of day and tells the team to reach out if anything is unclear. Async is fine — but he will re-read for a typo first, because a careless message in a partner channel looks bad.

2. What does Eduardo keep when he drops the formal scaffolding?
A The full "Best regards" sign-off.

3. What does he do before sending, even though async is fine?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

No chat profissional você CORTA o andaime formal (saudação longa, verbos pesados, despedida), mas MANTÉM um amaciador de polidez: um Could you..., um please, um thanks ou um no rush. Contrações (I'm, you're, I'll) são bem-vindas. Brevidade SEM amaciador vira ordem. / In professional chat you cut the formal scaffolding but always keep one polite softener — that single word keeps a two-line message warm instead of cold.

In chat you DROPIn chat you KEEP
The long salutation ("Dear Mr. Costa,")A softener: "Could you...", "Would you mind..."
Heavy formal verbs ("I would be grateful")A "please" or a "thanks"
The full sign-off ("Best regards, Eduardo")A warm closer: "No rush", "Appreciate it"
Full sentences where a phrase worksContractions ("I'm on it", "you're right")

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca mande só "Send the deck." — soa como ordem. Um "Could you send the deck?" com um "no rush" mantém a mensagem rápida e educada. Leia o canal: quanto mais sênior o leitor, mais você amacia.

Stage 2: Order the Message

Order

Put the five steps of writing a good chat message in the right order. Use Listen to hear them.

?You soften the ask with could you or a quick please so it never sounds cold.
?You read the channel and decide how formal to be.
?You re-read for typos and send.
?You keep the message short, dropping the long email salutation.
?You add one warm word, a heads-up or a no rush, so the reader does not feel chased.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best line for each chat moment.

You need a file from a teammate, fast but polite, in the team chat. Which is best?
A "Send the file."
B "Could you send the file when you get a sec? Thanks!"
C "Dear colleague, I am writing to request the file. Best regards."
A partner just joined the channel. How do you warn the team?
A "Quick heads-up — the partner is now in this channel, let us keep it warm."
B "FYI partner here behave."
C "I am hereby notifying you of the partner's arrival in this channel."
A partner sent a long, detailed message. What is a good reply?
A "Noted."
B "Got it, thanks for the detail — I'll circle back shortly."
C "ok"
