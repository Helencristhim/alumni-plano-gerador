Postmortem -- a review after an incident to learn from it

Retrospective -- a meeting to look back on what went well and badly
"In the retrospective, the team agreed on three takeaways."
Hindsight -- understanding a situation only after it has happened
"In hindsight, the warning signs were all there."
Incident -- an unplanned event that disrupts a service
"A bad deploy caused a serious incident."
Outage -- a period when a system is down and unavailable
"The outage lasted two hours, and users could not log in."
Blameless -- focused on causes, not on blaming people
"This is a blameless meeting: we look at causes, not people."
Preventable -- able to have been avoided
"The bug was completely preventable."
Takeaway -- a key lesson or point to remember
"The main takeaway is to test every login change."
Oversight -- an unintentional failure to notice or do something
"The switched-off alert was a small oversight."
Near miss -- an event that almost caused harm but did not
"A near miss last sprint was never written down."
Contributing factor -- one of several causes that led to a problem
"The old alert was a contributing factor, not the root cause."
To backfire -- to have the opposite effect to what was intended
"A postmortem can backfire if it turns into blame."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the outage, Ana wrote a postmortem. To look back at the past, she used the third conditional: "If we had tested the login flow in staging, the outage would not have happened" — if + past perfect, would have + past participle. To connect that past mistake to the present, she used a mixed conditional: "If we had documented the near miss, we would not be here now" — a past cause with a present result. And to show it was not just bad luck, she used another mixed form: "If our monitoring were better, we would have caught it sooner" — a present truth with a past result.

1. "If we had tested it in staging, the outage would not have happened" is:
A a third conditional about the past -- what did not happen.
B a plan for next week.
C a simple present routine.
2. "If we had documented the near miss, we would not be here now" connects:
A two future events.
B a past cause with a present result (a mixed conditional).
C two present habits.
3. What verb form comes after "if" in the third conditional?
A will + verb (if we will test).
B the past perfect: had + past participle (if we had tested).
C the -ing form (if we testing).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third & Mixed Conditionals

GRAMMAR

All of these use if and a would form. Only the tenses move.

ConditionalUse / FormExample
Thirda past that did not happen: if + past perfect, would have + past participleIf we had tested it, the outage would not have happened.
Mixed (past → present)past cause, present result: if + past perfect, would + base verbIf we had documented it, we would not be here now.
Mixed (present → past)present truth, past result: if + past simple, would have + past participleIf our monitoring were better, we would have caught it sooner.
Common mistakeDo not put a would form after "if": not "if we would have tested" — say "if we had tested". The would goes in the RESULT, never in the if-clause.
