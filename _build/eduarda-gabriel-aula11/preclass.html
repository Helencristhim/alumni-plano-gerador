Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key Chapter 11 terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

File for Bankruptcy -- to officially begin a legal bankruptcy process
"The company decided to file for bankruptcy."
Automatic Stay -- a court order that stops creditors from collecting
"The automatic stay protects the company."
Debtor in Possession -- a company that keeps control of its assets in Chapter 11
"As debtor in possession, the firm keeps running."
Reorganization Plan -- a formal plan to restructure debts and operations
"The reorganization plan was filed with the court."
Discharge -- to legally release a debtor from certain debts
"Some debts are discharged at the end of the case."
Insolvent -- unable to pay debts when they are due
"The company became insolvent last year."
Creditors' Committee -- a group that represents the creditors' interests
"The creditors' committee reviewed the plan."
Court Approval -- official permission from the court
"The sale needs court approval."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Vertex Industries became insolvent and decided to file for bankruptcy. A petition is filed with the court, and immediately an automatic stay is granted, so all collections are stopped. The company is protected while it keeps control as debtor in possession. A reorganization plan is prepared and, once it is accepted by the creditors' committee, it must be approved by the court. At the end, some debts are discharged.

1. What happens immediately after the petition is filed?
A The debts are discharged.
B An automatic stay is granted and collections are stopped.
C The company is sold.
2. Why is the passive used ("is filed", "are stopped")?
A Because the action is in the future.
B Because the process matters more than who does it.
C Because it is a question.
3. Who must approve the reorganization plan?
A The court must approve it.
B The creditors discharge it.
C No one needs to approve it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

The passive voice = be + past participle. Use it when the action matters more than who does it (muito comum em inglês jurídico). Adicione by + agente só quando o agente for relevante.

TenseStructureExample
Presentis / are + past participle"A petition is filed with the court."
Pastwas / were + past participle"The plan was filed last week."
Modalmodal + be + past participle"It must be approved by the court."

Common Mistake:

"The petition filed yesterday by the lawyer."

"The petition was filed yesterday by the lawyer."

A passiva sempre precisa de be (is/are/was/were) + particípio. Nunca omita o "be".
