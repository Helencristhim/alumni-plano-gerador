Aula 06 -- Pre-class

Holding Your Ground -- The Supplier Approval Meeting

O relê de segurança reprovou no FAT e o fornecedor chama de "minor deviation": como enunciar a falha sem acusar ninguém, exigir a ação corretiva sem fazer o trabalho, e manter o hold point fechado. Key words: deviation, submittal, approval workflow, inspection release, out of tolerance, calibration certificate, traceability, root cause, rework, sign-off, to downplay, to raise a concern, to hold your ground, to pass the buck. Structure: voz passiva avançada (was found to be... / had not been issued / must be implemented) + causativo have something done ("We need to have the report resubmitted by Friday").

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Na aula 5 você nomeou a si mesmo diante de uma banca. Aqui você nomeia o DEFEITO — e quem paga por ele. São as palavras da reunião de aprovação: o que foi encontrado, o que ainda não foi emitido, e o que o fornecedor vai tentar chamar de "menor". Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo.

Deviation -- a departure from what the approved specification requires (desvio (aprovado antes da execução))
"The deviation was accepted in writing, so no rework is required."
Submittal -- a document the supplier formally sends to the client for review and approval (documento submetido à aprovação)
"The submittal was returned with comments on Tuesday."
Approval workflow -- the sequence of reviews a document must pass before it is accepted (fluxo de aprovação)
"The drawing is stuck in the approval workflow, not on my desk."
Inspection release -- the formal authorization to ship, or to move to the next stage, after an inspection (liberação de inspeção)
"No inspection release has been issued, so nothing leaves the factory."
Out of tolerance -- outside the range of values the specification allows (fora de tolerância)
"The relay was found to be out of tolerance during the functional test."
Calibration certificate -- the document that proves an instrument was verified against a reference (certificado de calibração)
"The calibration certificate had not been issued at the time of the test."
Traceability -- being able to track a part back to its material, its test and its records (rastreabilidade)
"In signaling, a component without traceability does not exist."
Root cause -- the underlying reason a failure happened, not the symptom you can see (causa raiz)
"The root cause has not been identified yet, so the corrective action is premature."
Rework -- work that has to be done a second time to correct a defect (retrabalho)
"The rework will be carried out at the supplier's own cost."
Sign-off -- the formal written approval that closes a stage and lets the project move on (aprovação formal, aceite)
"I cannot give you sign-off until the calibration certificate is on file."
To downplay -- to make a problem sound smaller than it really is (minimizar (um problema))
"He downplayed the failure: 'It is a minor deviation, nothing more.'"
To raise a concern -- to put a risk formally on the record, before it becomes a problem (levantar uma preocupação formalmente)
"I would like to raise a concern about the traceability of that batch."
To hold your ground -- to keep your position when the other side pushes back (manter a sua posição)
"He downplayed it twice, and twice I held my ground."
To pass the buck -- to shift responsibility to somebody else instead of owning it (informal) (empurrar a responsabilidade para outro)
"Blaming the subcontractor is passing the buck: our contract is with you."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The factory acceptance test was carried out on a Tuesday morning, with João Guilherme watching on a video link from São Paulo. The safety relay was found to be out of tolerance: eleven milliseconds, against a specified range of five to seven. The test was witnessed, a hold point was triggered, and no inspection release was issued. Then the report arrived from the supplier, and every sentence in it had been written to make the failure disappear.

Two things had not been done at the time of the test. The calibration certificate for the reference instrument had not been issued, and the root cause had not been identified. The report calls the failure a minor deviation and asks for a waiver, which is the polite way of asking João to pay for somebody else's rework. On the call, the quality manager downplays it — "the unit works" — and then passes the buck: the calibration, she says, was performed by a subcontractor. João holds his ground. A deviation is approved before the work; this was found after it. The contract is with her company, not with the subcontractor.

So he states what has to happen, and he states it in the passive, because the obligation belongs to the procedure and not to him. "A corrective action must be implemented before the retest. We need to have the report resubmitted through the approval workflow by Friday, and we will have the relay retested with a valid certificate in place. I would also like to raise a concern about the traceability of that batch. Sign-off cannot be granted while the hold point remains open." Nobody was accused. Nothing was denied. And the relay is being retested on Thursday.

1. "The relay was found to be out of tolerance." Por que o engenheiro escolhe o passivo, em vez de "Your technician calibrated it badly"?
A Porque o passivo é mais educado, ainda que mais fraco.
B Porque o passivo tira a PESSOA da frase e deixa o FATO. Uma acusação se contesta e se defende; uma constatação só pode ser FECHADA — e é por isso que todo relatório de inspeção é escrito assim.
C Porque em inglês técnico é proibido usar a voz ativa.
2. "The calibration certificate had not been issued at the time of the test." O que o passivo PERFEITO faz aqui que o passivo simples não faria?
A Data a ausência: no MOMENTO do teste, aquele documento ainda não existia — e por isso ele não cobre o teste. Emitir depois não resolve.
B Apenas soa mais formal; o sentido é o mesmo de "was not issued".
C Indica que o certificado nunca será emitido.
3. "We need to have the report resubmitted by Friday." QUEM reenvia o relatório?
A Eu mesmo, porque have significa "ter que fazer".
B Ninguém — a frase apenas descreve uma intenção.
C O FORNECEDOR. O causativo have something done diz que a ação é EXIGIDA por mim e EXECUTADA por outro. Eu não faço o trabalho; eu o determino.
4. Which sentence is correct?
A "We need to have resubmitted the report by Friday."
B "We need to have the report resubmitted by Friday."
C "We need to have the report resubmit by Friday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Advanced Passive & Causative

GRAMMAR

Como enunciar uma falha sem acusar uma pessoa — e como exigir o conserto sem fazer o trabalho (explicação em inglês e português).

FormFunção / FunctionExample
Passivo simples
be + particípio		O objeto vira sujeito e o agente CAI — porque é óbvio, porque é desconhecido, ou porque nomeá-lo comecaria uma briga."The submittal was returned with comments."
Passivo de constatação
be + found / reported / believed + to be		O registro de todo relatório de inspeção. Você enuncia o FATO, não a pessoa. Repare: is believed to have been caused NÃO é o mesmo que has been identified — o fornecedor usa o primeiro justamente para não assumir o segundo."The relay was found to be out of tolerance."
Passivo perfeito
had been + particípio		Data a AUSÊNCIA: no momento do teste, aquele documento ainda não existia. É a frase que impede o fornecedor de resolver o problema emitindo o certificado depois."The certificate had not been issued at the time of the test."
Passivo com modal
modal + be + particípio		A obrigação pertence ao PROCEDIMENTO, não a você — e por isso ninguém a leva para o lado pessoal. Depois do modal vem be, sempre."A corrective action must be implemented." · "Sign-off cannot be granted."
Causativo
have + OBJETO + particípio		Você EXIGE a ação; outro a EXECUTA. A ordem é fixa: a coisa vem antes do particípio. get something done é o mesmo, menos formal."We need to have the report resubmitted." · "I will get the relay retested."
Causativo com pessoa
have somebody + FORMA BASE		Quando você PRECISA nomear quem faz. Aí o verbo volta à forma base — sem to, sem particípio."I will have your team resubmit the report."
Para que serve"You calibrated it badly" é uma acusação — e tudo o que vier depois será defesa. "The relay was found to be out of tolerance" é o MESMO fato com a pessoa removida: ninguém é atacado e nada pode ser negado. Depois, o causativo devolve o trabalho a quem pertence: "we need to have it retested" — não por nós, por ELES, e até sexta.

Atenção (dois erros de brasileiro): (1) esquecer o be ou o particípio depois do modal — "The report must be resubmit" e "A corrective action must implemented" estão errados; o certo é "must be resubmitted" e "must be implemented"; (2) a ordem do causativo — em português dizemos "preciso reenviar o relatório", e sai "We need to have resubmitted the report", que em inglês é outro tempo verbal e outro sentido. No causativo o OBJETO vem ANTES: "have the report resubmitted".

Collocações da reunião de aprovação: carry out a test, issue a certificate (NUNCA "emit" — o falso amigo clássico), trigger a hold point, identify the root cause, raise a concern, grant sign-off, resubmit a report through the approval workflow.

Stage 2: Put the Approval Meeting in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma reunião de hold point na ordem correta — da constatação até a recusa do aceite.

?Put the responsibility back where the contract is, when the supplier blames the subcontractor.
?Demand the work with the causative, and give the date: have the report resubmitted by Friday.
?Hold the gate: sign-off cannot be granted while the hold point remains open.
?Refuse the word: a deviation is approved before the work, so a failure found after it is a non-conformance.
?State the finding in the passive: the relay was found to be out of tolerance during the functional test.
?Date what is missing: the calibration certificate had not been issued at the time of the test.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. São as cinco frases que abrem, sustentam e fecham uma reunião de aprovação. Repare no acento: ele cai no particípio (was FOUND to be out of TOLerance) — e "certificate" termina em -kit, átono, nunca em -keit.

The relay was found to be out of tolerance during the functional test.
O relé foi constatado fora de tolerância durante o teste funcional.
The calibration certificate had not been issued at the time of the test.
O certificado de calibração não havia sido emitido na data do teste.
A corrective action must be implemented before the retest.
Uma ação corretiva precisa ser implementada antes do reteste.
We need to have the report resubmitted by Friday.
Precisamos que o relatório seja reenviado até sexta-feira.
Sign-off cannot be granted while the hold point remains open.
O aceite não pode ser concedido enquanto o hold point permanecer aberto.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real de uma reunião de FAT — inclusive quando o fornecedor minimiza a falha ou empurra a culpa para o subcontratado.

The supplier's quality manager opens with "It is a minor deviation, the unit works." You answer:
A "A deviation is approved before the work. This was found after it, on a safety relay, so I am afraid that is a non-conformance."
B "You are wrong and you know it. That relay failed."
C "All right, if the unit works, I suppose we can look at it as a deviation."
She says the certificate was issued the Monday AFTER the test. The strongest reply is:
A "That is good news, we can close the point then."
B "The calibration certificate had not been issued at the time of the test, so it does not cover the test. The unit will have to be retested with a valid certificate in place."
C "Please send it to me and I will see what I can do."
"The calibration was performed by our subcontractor, not by us." You answer:
A "Then I will take it up with your subcontractor directly."
B "That is not my problem, and it is not an excuse."
C "To some extent, I understand your position. However, our contract is with you, so the root cause still has to be identified on your side."
She asks what you need before the unit can be released. You want the work done by THEM, with a date:
A "We need to have the report resubmitted through the approval workflow by Friday, with the root cause and a corrective action plan."
B "We need to have resubmitted the report by Friday."
C "I will resubmit the report myself once you send me the data."
She asks for a provisional sign-off so the shipment is not delayed. You say no without closing the door:
A "I cannot sign anything, please stop asking me."
B "I am afraid sign-off cannot be granted while the hold point remains open. As soon as the retest passes and the certificate is on file, the inspection release can be issued the same day."
C "All right, provisionally, but do not tell anyone."
