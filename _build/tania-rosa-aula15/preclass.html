Stage 1.1: Describing Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you describe people and places.

Tall -- alto(a)

Curly -- cacheado / encaracolado

Friendly -- simpático(a) / amigável
"The people here are very friendly."
Narrow -- estreito(a)
"The old streets are very narrow."
Crowded -- cheio(a) / lotado(a)
"The square was crowded in the evening."
Peaceful -- tranquilo(a) / pacífico(a)
"The garden was peaceful in the morning."
Ancient -- antigo(a) / muito velho(a)
"We visited an ancient town by the sea."
Stunning -- deslumbrante / lindíssimo(a)
"The cafe had a stunning view of the sea."

"It was a small, cozy cafe."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the description and answer the questions.

On her last trip, Tânia visited a lovely small ancient town by the sea. The streets were narrow and quiet, and the old houses were beautiful. There she met a tall, friendly man with short curly hair. He worked at a cozy little cafe with a stunning rooftop view. In the morning the square was peaceful, but in the evening it became crowded and full of music. It is now her favorite place.

1. What kind of town did Tânia visit?
A A lovely small ancient town by the sea.
B A big modern city.
C A new town in the mountains.
2. What did the man look like?
A Short with long straight hair.
B Tall and friendly, with short curly hair.
C Old and quiet, with grey hair.
3. How was the square in the evening?
A Peaceful and empty.
B Crowded and full of music.
C Closed and dark.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adjective Order

Grammar

Quando você usa dois ou três adjetivos, em inglês eles seguem uma ordem natural: opinião > tamanho > idade > cor + substantivo. Por isso dizemos "a lovely small ancient town", não "an ancient small lovely town". Dois adjetivos já soam ótimos; três é o máximo para o A2. / When you use two or three adjectives, in English they follow a natural order: opinion > size > age > color + noun. So we say "a lovely small ancient town", not "an ancient small lovely town". Two adjectives already sound great; three is the maximum for A2.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Think about a person you admire and a place you love. Describe what the person looks like and what they are like, and paint a picture of the place. Use two or three adjectives in the right order (opinion > size > age > color). Take your time.
