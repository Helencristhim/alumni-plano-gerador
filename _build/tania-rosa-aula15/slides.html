Chapter 3: Word Order

Adjective Order

opinion > size > age > color + noun

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"He is a tall, friendly man." (size + opinion)

"She has short curly hair." (size + shape)

"It is a lovely small ancient town." (opinion + size + age)

"We saw a beautiful old blue door." (opinion + age + color)

OrderTypeExample
1Opinion (what you think)lovely, friendly, beautiful
2Sizetall, small, narrow
3Ageold, ancient, new
4Color + nounblue, white + door / hat

In English, describing words follow a natural order: opinion > size > age > color, then the noun. So we say "a lovely small ancient town", not "an ancient small lovely town". Two adjectives are common; three is enough for A2.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"a blue beautiful door"

✓ RIGHT

"a beautiful blue door"

✗ WRONG

"an old small lovely town"

✓ RIGHT

"a lovely small old town"

Chapter 4: Painting the Picture

Tânia Describes a Place

One town, one friendly face

Dialogue

A Place and a Person

F
You went to that little town by the sea, right? What was it like?
T
It was a lovely small ancient town. The streets were narrow and quiet, and the old houses were beautiful.
F
That sounds amazing. Did you meet anyone interesting there?
T
Yes! A tall, friendly man with short curly hair. He worked at a cozy little cafe with a stunning rooftop view.
F
A rooftop view! Was the place crowded?
T
In the morning it was peaceful, but in the evening it was crowded and full of music. It is my favorite place now.
