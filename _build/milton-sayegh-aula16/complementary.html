Lesson 16 -- Review & Consolidation: The Business Lunch

Series / Film
Succession -- any business-dinner scene

Watch how executives host, recommend, recap and reflect over a meal -- and how they slip between problem talk, plans and reported conversations in a single dinner. A live model of everything from Lessons 9-15 around one table.

Tip: each time someone hosts or recaps, pause and name the structure they use (conditional, modal, future form, reported speech).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- any episode on "socializing", "networking" or "business meals"

Practical English for the relaxed side of business: hosting a guest, recommending dishes, making small talk and raising a toast. The host models the warm phrases that turn a transaction into a relationship.

Tip: note one phrase you'd use to host a client, then say it out loud as if Karen were across the table.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "English at a business lunch / making conversation"

A short, clear lesson on the social English of a business meal: welcoming a guest, recommending food, keeping a friendly conversation going, and closing warmly. Native speakers model it in real situations.

Tip: after watching, record your own one-minute "host a client" speech using host, treat, recommend, recap and toast.
