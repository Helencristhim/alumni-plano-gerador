Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The friendly language of hosting a business lunch -- and of reflecting on your year. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario de receber alguem em um almoco de negocios e de refletir sobre o ano.

Host -- receber / ser anfitriao
to invite someone and take care of them, often paying for the meal
"Let me host you this time -- you hosted me in New York."
Treat -- pagar / oferecer (como cortesia)
to pay for someone's meal or drink as a generous gesture
"Please, this lunch is my treat."
Recommend -- recomendar / sugerir
to suggest something because you believe it is good
"I'd recommend the risotto -- it's their best dish."
Appetizer -- entrada (prato)
a small dish you eat at the start of a meal, before the main course
"Shall we share a couple of appetizers to start?"
Toast -- brinde
to raise your glass and say a few words to celebrate or wish someone well
"Let's raise a toast to a great year together."
Recap -- recapitular / resumir
to summarize the main points of something briefly
"Let me recap what we've built this year."
Takeaway -- licao principal / o que se leva
the main point or lesson you remember and keep from an experience
"My biggest takeaway this year is confidence."
Confident -- confiante / seguro
feeling sure of yourself and of what you can do
"I feel confident speaking English in any meeting now."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read how Milton recaps his year, then answer. Notice the structures from Lessons 9-15. / Leia como o Milton recapitula o ano e responda; observe as estruturas das Licoes 9-15.

Over lunch, Milton offered to host and said it was his treat. He recommended the appetizers and then chose to recap the year. "If a problem comes up, I will handle it calmly now," he said -- a first conditional from his problem-solving lessons. He added that in the US you should get to the point quickly, using a modal of advice. When Karen asked, "Could you tell me what's next?", he answered with a polite indirect question of his own and then shared the plan: "We're going to expand next quarter." Finally, reporting their talk to his team, he told them she had said the timing was right. His biggest takeaway, he admitted, was how confident he had become. They raised a toast to the year ahead.

1. "If a problem comes up, I will handle it." Which structure is this?
A First conditional -- present + will (a real, likely situation).
B Third conditional -- about an impossible past.
C Reported speech.
2. Karen said: "The timing is right." How does Milton report it?
A He said the timing is right.
B He said the timing was right. (present → past)
C He said the timing will be right.
3. Which is the correct polite indirect question?
A Could you tell me what is next?
B Could you tell me what's next? (no inversion after "tell me")
C Could you tell me what next is being?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation. / Escolha a melhor resposta.

Your client arrives for lunch. How do you host warmly?
A "You can pay for yourself."
B "Thank you for coming -- please, this lunch is my treat."
C "Why are you late?"
A piece might arrive damaged. How do you reassure the client (first conditional)?
A "If it will arrive damaged, I would reimburse you."
B "If a piece arrives damaged, I'll reimburse you right away."
C "If a piece arrived damaged, I will reimbursed you."
You want to ask politely about her plans. Which is correct?
A "Could you tell me what are you planning?"
B "Could you tell me what you're planning?"
C "Could you tell me what planning you?"
You report to your team what the client said: "I'll confirm next week." Which is right?
A "She said me she will confirm next week."
B "She told me she would confirm next week."
C "She told she will confirm next week."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the prompt. There is no right or wrong answer. / Grave-se respondendo. Nao ha resposta certa ou errada.

Record a two-minute reflection on your year of English. First, recap what you can do now that you couldn't before. Then use a third conditional ("A year ago I would have..."), a future form ("Next, I'm going to..."), and one reported-speech sentence ("My teacher said..."). End by naming your biggest takeaway and how confident you feel. / Grave dois minutos refletindo sobre seu ano de ingles, usando terceiro condicional, uma forma de futuro e reported speech.
