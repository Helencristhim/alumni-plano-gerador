Pharmacy -- a shop where you buy medicine and ask for health advice

Fever -- when your body temperature is too high because you are ill
"I have a fever and I feel very tired." (Febre)
Headache -- a pain in your head that makes it hard to think
"I have a headache, so I need to rest." (Dor de cabeça)
Sore throat -- when the inside of your neck hurts, often before a cold
"I have a sore throat and I cannot talk well." (Dor de garganta)
Medicine -- a pill or liquid you take to treat an illness
"Take this medicine twice a day after meals." (Remédio)
Painkiller -- a pill that stops or reduces pain, like a headache
"Can I have a painkiller for my headache?" (Analgésico)
Symptom -- a sign in your body that shows you are ill, like a fever
"A fever and a cough are common symptoms of a cold." (Sintoma)
Pharmacist -- the person who works at a pharmacy and gives medicine
"The pharmacist gave me some medicine for my fever." (Farmacêutico)
Emergency -- a serious problem that needs help right now
"In an emergency, call one, one, two." (Emergência)
Dizzy -- when your head spins and you feel like you may fall
"I feel dizzy, so I should sit down." (Tonto / com tontura)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca wakes up with a fever in a new city. She makes a simple plan. "If I have a fever, I will rest at the hotel." This is the first conditional: if + present simple, will + base verb, for a real future possibility and its result. Later, the hotel front desk reminds her of a strong safety rule: "In an emergency, you must call one, one, two." We use must for a strong rule, often about safety. At the pharmacy, the pharmacist says: "You have to take this medicine after meals." We use have to for an obligation, often an outside rule. Notice that in the if part we use the present, not will: "if it rains", not "if it will rain".

1. "If I have a fever, I will rest." Which part is the condition?
A "If I have a fever" -- the condition
B "I will rest" -- the condition
C There is no condition
2. "You must call one, one, two." What does must show here?
A A soft suggestion you can ignore
B A strong rule, often about safety
C Something that happened in the past
3. In the if part, which form do we use?
A will + base verb ("if it will rain")
B the present simple ("if it rains")
C the past simple ("if it rained")

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional + Must / Have to

GRAMMAR

Como falar de uma possibilidade real no futuro e dar regras fortes. / How to talk about a real future possibility and give strong rules.

StructureUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
If + present, will + basepossibilidade real no futuro e seu resultado / a real future possibility and its result"If I feel sick, I will rest."
must + baseregra forte, muitas vezes de segurança / a strong rule, often about safety"You must call the emergency number."
have to + baseobrigação, geralmente uma regra externa / an obligation, often an outside rule"You have to show your passport."

Key point / Ponto-chave: na parte do if, use o presente, não will: "if it rains", não "if it will rain". Após must e have to, use o verbo na forma base: must call, have to show. / In the if part, use the present simple; the will goes only in the result.
