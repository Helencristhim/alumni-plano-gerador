Chapter 3 -- First Conditional

If This Happens, I Will...

Talking about real future possibilities + must / have to

Listen & Repeat

First Conditional in Action

"If I have a fever, I will rest at the hotel."

"If the medicine does not help, I will see a doctor."

"In an emergency, you must call one, one, two."

"You have to stay calm and speak slowly."

Grammar Rule

First Conditional + Must / Have to

StructureUseExample
If + present, will + basea real future possibility and its resultIf I feel sick, I will rest.
must + basea strong rule, often for safetyYou must call the emergency number.
have to + basean obligation, often an outside ruleYou have to show your passport.

Key idea: in the if part, use the present simple (not will): "If it rains", not "if it will rain". After must and have to, use the base verb: must call, have to show. You can put the if part first or second: "If I feel sick, I will rest" = "I will rest if I feel sick".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If it will rain, I will stay at the hotel."
"If it rains, I will stay at the hotel."

In the if part, use the present simple. The will goes only in the result: if it rains, I will stay.

Dialogue

At the Pharmacy

T

"Excuse me. I have a fever and a sore throat. What do you recommend?"

P

"I'm sorry to hear that. This painkiller will help. If you take one tablet every six hours, your fever will go down."

T

"Thank you. Do I have to take it before or after meals?"

P

"After meals, with water. You must not take more than four a day. And you must not drink alcohol with it."

T

"Understood. And if I still feel sick tomorrow?"

P

"If the fever does not go down, you have to see a doctor. There is a clinic near the central station. Rest and drink water. You'll feel better soon!"
