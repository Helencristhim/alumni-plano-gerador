Chapter 4: The Code

The Voice of a Report

When the action matters more than the name

Grammar Discovery

Notice the Passive

"A serious bug was found in production."

"The issue was escalated to the backend team."

"A patch was deployed within the hour."

"The problem has been resolved."

FormUseExample
was / were + past participleReport a past action, name not importantA patch was deployed.
is / are + past participleA current fact or ruleThe bug is reproduced in three steps.
has / have been + past participleA finished action, still relevant nowThe problem has been resolved.

Passive = a form of be + the past participle. In a report, we usually drop the "by..." part, because the action matters more than the name.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Passive

"A serious bug ___was found in production." (report the event)
"The issue ___was escalated to the backend team." (action, not names)
"The broken release ___was rolled back immediately." (action, not names)
"A patch ___was deployed within the hour." (result matters)
"The problem ___has been resolved." (finished, still relevant)
Language Focus

Read the Report

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"The problem was resolve yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"The problem was resolved yesterday."

✗ WRONG

"A patch deployed within the hour."

✓ RIGHT

"A patch was deployed within the hour."

The passive needs a form of be (was/were/has been) plus the past participle. Do not drop the "be", and use the participle form: resolved, deployed, found.

Dialogue

The Update to David

D
Ana, thanks for the call. I heard the checkout was down this morning. How bad was it?
A
The impact was high. About fifteen percent of users could not pay, with high frequency during peak hours.
D
And what was the symptom? What did the users actually see?
A
A blank screen after they clicked Pay. It was reproducible, so the exact steps and a screenshot were attached.
D
Good. And what was done about it?
A
The issue was escalated to the backend team, the broken release was rolled back, and a patch was deployed. Downtime was under twenty minutes.
D
Perfect, that is exactly the level of detail I need. Is it fixed for good?
A
Yes. It was a regression from the last sprint. It has been resolved, and a new test was added so it cannot return.
Did You Catch It?

About the Update

1. What is the first thing David wants to know about the bug?
How bad it was -- the impact on users.
2. What does David say about the level of detail Ana gives?
He says it is exactly the level of detail he needs.
3. What is David's final question to Ana?
Whether the bug is fixed for good.
Listening 1

A Note from David

A voice note after he reads your report. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What two things does David ask Ana to add to the report?
A more concrete impact at the top (how many users, which hours), and a final line saying whether it has been fully resolved.
2. Why does David want Ana to keep the passive voice?
Because the leadership does not need the names, they need to know what was done.
Listening 2

Priya's Golden Rule

A tip from your team lead. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What is Priya's golden rule for an incident report?
Lead with the impact, not with the story -- the first line must show how bad it is and who is affected.
2. What example of a vague sentence does Priya say you should avoid?
"Some problems were seen" -- instead, be precise: "the payment call failed for fifteen percent of users during peak hours".
