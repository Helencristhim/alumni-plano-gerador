Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Learn the words for reporting a problem clearly. Tap Listen to hear each one.

Impact -- how badly a problem affects users or the business
"The impact was high: fifteen percent of users could not pay."
Symptom -- a visible sign that something is wrong
"The symptom was a blank screen after the user clicked Pay."
Frequency -- how often a problem happens
"The bug happened with high frequency during peak hours."
To resolve -- to fix a problem and close it
"The problem has been resolved and a new test was added."
Regression -- when something that worked before breaks after a change
"The bug was a regression from the last sprint."
Reproducible -- able to be made to happen again in the same way
"The bug was reproducible, so I added the exact steps."
Patch -- a small update that fixes a specific problem
"A patch was deployed within the hour."
Downtime -- a period when the system is not available to users
"Downtime was kept under twenty minutes."
To roll back -- to return the software to an earlier working version
"The broken release was rolled back immediately."
Evidence -- facts, like logs or screenshots, that show what happened
"I attached a screenshot and the logs as evidence."
Concise -- giving clear information in few words
"A good report is concise: five short lines, no story."
Vague -- not clear or not specific enough
"Do not be vague; say exactly how many users were affected."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When Ana wrote her incident report, she used the passive voice again and again. She wrote, "A serious bug was found in production" — the event, without naming who found it. She wrote, "The issue was escalated to the backend team and a patch was deployed" — the actions, because the names did not matter, only what was done. And she closed with, "The problem has been resolved" — a finished action that is still true now. The passive keeps a report neutral, clean and precise.

1. Why does Ana write "A bug was found" instead of "Someone found a bug"?
A The event matters, not who did it, so she uses the passive voice.
B "Was found" is only used for the future.
C The two sentences mean different things.
2. "A patch was deployed" is formed with:
A a past participle only, with no verb 'be'.
B a form of 'be' (was) plus a past participle (deployed).
C the present continuous.
3. Which sentence reports a finished action that is still true now?
A "A bug was found in production."
B "The issue was escalated to the team."
C "The problem has been resolved."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Passive = a form of be + the past participle. Use it in a report when the action or the result matters more than who did it.

FormUseExample
was / were + past participleReport a past action; the name is not importantA patch was deployed.
is / are + past participleA current fact or a ruleThe bug is reproduced in three steps.
has / have been + past participleA finished action, still relevant nowThe problem has been resolved.
Common mistakeDo not drop the 'be' or the participle: not "A patch deployed" and not "It was resolve" — say "A patch was deployed" and "It was resolved."
