Chapter 3 -- Softening

Gentle Requests & Soft Refusals

Ask gently, refuse politely, give a reason

Grammar Notice

Notice the Softening

What makes these polite, not rude?

I was wondering if you could bring the date forward."

Would it be possible to send the prices by Friday?"

I am afraid we cannot increase the order, as our budget is fixed."

"We must respectfully decline, but we could review it next quarter."

Grammar Rule

Softening a Message

Direct (too blunt)Softened (polite)
Send the report.I was wondering if you could send the report.
Bring the date forward.Would it be possible to bring the date forward?
No, we cannot.I am afraid we cannot, as our budget is fixed.
We will not do it.We must respectfully decline, but we could review it later.

Key idea: for a polite request, turn the order into an indirect question -- I was wondering if you could..., Would it be possible to...?, Could you possibly...? For a polite refusal, start with I am afraid, use cannot / we are unable to, give a reason (as / because), and offer an alternative when you can.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Send me the file now. No, we will not."
"I was wondering if you could send me the file. I am afraid we cannot, as the budget is fixed."

An order sounds rude in an email. Soften the request with an indirect question, and never refuse with a flat "no" -- always add I am afraid and a reason.
