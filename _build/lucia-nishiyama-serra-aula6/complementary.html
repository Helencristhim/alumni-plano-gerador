'May', 'Might' and 'Could' -- 6 Minute Grammar

Six minutes, two presenters, and exactly the modals you used tonight to hedge: may, might, and could for possibility. They say each example at natural speed, so your ear learns how a careful, non-committal sentence really sounds in English.

Tip: watch it twice. The first time, only listen. The second time, pause after each example and rebuild it for your world out loud: "This might create exposure at the regulatory level."

TED Talk · Executive Presence
Your Body Language May Shape Who You Are -- Amy Cuddy (TED)

A twenty-minute classic on how presence works before you even speak -- and the title itself uses tonight's hedging modal, "may". This is the confidence layer under your modal choices: when you carry yourself with authority, "must" and "will" land the way you intend them to.

Tip: notice how often she hedges with "may" and "might" when she talks about the science, and asserts firmly when she talks about her own story. That is exactly the balance you are training.
