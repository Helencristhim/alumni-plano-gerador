Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I can confirm the numbers, but I would recommend a review."

What form of the verb comes right after each highlighted modal?

PartFormExample
The modalcan/could, should/ought to, must, may/might, will/wouldWe must ...
The main verbbase form -- no "to", no "-s"... comply. (not "to comply", not "complies")
Negative & questionNegative: modal + not + base -- "We must not delay." Question: modal first -- "Should we prioritize this now?"

Pick the modal for the force you want: must = obligation, should = recommendation, might/may = possibility, can = ability.

Detail

Must vs Have To

MUST = your conviction

It comes from inside you.

"You must align on this."

Negative: "must not" = it is forbidden.

HAVE TO = an outside rule

It comes from a rule or a law.

"You have to submit the report by Friday."

Negative: "don't have to" = it is not necessary.

Both are followed by the base verb: "must comply", "have to submit".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Every team must to comply with the framework."
"Every team must comply with the framework."
"The board should prioritizes AI safety."
"The board should prioritize AI safety."
"This will maybe create exposure."
"This might create exposure."

After a modal, use the base verb -- no to, no -s. To sound careful, choose might or may; do not weaken will with maybe.

Dialogue

A Board Q&A

M
Lucia, before we vote -- how certain are you that this framework will actually work?
L
What I can tell you with certainty is that it will reduce our exposure. I would not assert that if I could not defend it.
M
And the parts that are less clear?
L
Where the data is still thin, I have to hedge. This might create some exposure, so we should not defer the review.
M
So what exactly do you need from the board today?
L
My recommendation is simple. The board should approve the framework, and every team must follow it. Let me clarify one point first.
M
Please do.
L
This is not optional. Compliance is an obligation, and the early data is a clear indication that the risk is real.
M
That is clear. You have my vote.

Listening 1

A Risk Update

An executive updates the board on risk. Sound first -- no text.

0:000:00
1. What is the speaker certain about?
That exposure is rising, and rising faster than last quarter -- the data is a clear indication.
2. What does he say the board should do, and every team?
The board should approve the new framework; every team must follow it, as an obligation.
3. What is he less sure about?
The regulatory picture might shift, so they may need a second review before the full rollout.
Listening 2

A Coach's One Habit

A presentation coach shares one habit. Sound first -- no text.

0:000:00
1. What should you decide before you speak?
How certain you really are -- then pick the modal that matches.
2. What is the most common mistake she sees?
Leaders who say "will" about everything, so nothing sounds certain.
3. What does she recommend for the things you are not sure about?
Hedge them with "might", and save your strongest modals for what you can defend.
