Modal Verbs for Executive Authority -- Certain When You Can, Careful When You Must

Controlling the force of every sentence with modal verbs. Key words: obligation, recommendation, certainty, to hedge, to assert, to clarify, to defer, indication. Structure: modal verbs (can, could, should, ought to, must, have to, may, might, will, would) for obligation, recommendation, possibility, and certainty, plus the base-verb rule after every modal.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Obligation -- something you must do because of a rule, a duty, or a promise
"Compliance is an obligation, not a preference -- every team must follow it."
Recommendation -- advice about the best action to take
"My recommendation is that the board should prioritize safety this quarter."
Certainty -- the state of being completely sure that something is true
"What I can tell you with certainty is that our exposure is rising."
To hedge -- to avoid a direct answer so you do not fully commit
"Where the data is thin, I hedge: this might create exposure."
To assert -- to state something firmly and with confidence
"A leader must assert a clear position, not apologize for it."
To clarify -- to make something easier to understand by explaining it
"Let me clarify what the new policy must cover before you decide."
To defer -- to delay a decision or an action until a later time
"We should not defer this decision much longer."
Indication -- a sign that shows what is true or what is likely
"The early data is a clear indication that the risk might grow."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Lucia is briefing the board on AI safety. "What I can tell you with certainty is that our exposure is rising," she asserts. "The board should prioritize this now, and every team must follow the new framework. Compliance is an obligation, not a preference." Then, where the data is thin, she hedges: "This might create exposure at the regulatory level, so we should not defer the review." She stops to clarify one point, and the early data is a clear indication that she is right. Each modal is a signal: must is a wall, might is a window. That is why the board listens.

1. Why does Lucia say "every team must follow the new framework"?
A It is an obligation -- "must" expresses a duty or a rule.
B It is only a soft suggestion she is not sure about.
C It is a hypothetical idea that may never happen.
2. "This might create exposure at the regulatory level." Why does she choose "might"?
A Because she is completely certain it will happen.
B Because she is not fully sure -- "might" hedges honestly.
C Because it is a rule everyone has to follow.
3. Which modal shows the strongest level of force in the text?
A must -- the text calls it "a wall".
B might -- the text calls it "a window".
C should -- a gentle recommendation.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Verbs

GRAMMAR

How to express obligation, recommendation, possibility, and certainty at the right level of force.

FormUseExample
Obligation
must / have to + base		A duty or a rule.Every team must comply. / You have to submit the report.
Recommendation
should / ought to + base		Advice -- the best action.The board should prioritize this.
Possibility (hedge)
may / might + base		You are not fully sure.This might create exposure.
Ability / request
can / could + base		What is possible; a polite request.I can confirm the numbers. / Could you clarify that?
Certainty
will / would		A confident prediction or a hypothetical: "This will scale." / "I would recommend a review." After a modal, use the base verb -- no "to", no "-s". Negative = modal + not ("must not" = forbidden; "don't have to" = not necessary).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real situation.

A board member asks how certain you are that the plan will work. You are sure. You answer:
A "What I can tell you with certainty is that it will work."
B "What I can tell you with certainty is that it might work."
C "Maybe it will certainly work."
You want to state a firm rule for every team. Which is correct?
A "Every team must to comply."
B "Every team must comply."
C "Every team must complies."
You are not fully sure about a regulatory risk. The best answer is:
A "This will create exposure."
B "This might create exposure."
C "This must create exposure."
You want to recommend an action to the board. Which is correct?
A "The board should prioritize this now."
B "The board should prioritizes this now."
C "The board should to prioritize this now."
