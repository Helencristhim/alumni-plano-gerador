Chapter 3 -- Question Forms

Open vs Closed

The questions that keep a conversation alive

Listen and Notice

Open or Closed?

Which questions invite a longer answer?

"What brings you to the conference?"

"How long have you worked in law?"

"Where are you from originally?"

"Do you know many people here?"

The Question Code

Open vs Closed Questions

TypeStarts withExample
OpenWhat, Where, How, Why, How long"What brings you to the conference?"
ClosedDo, Did, Are, Is, Have"Do you know many people here?"
Best moveOpen a closed question, then follow it with an open one to keep talking.

Key idea: open questions invite a story and keep small talk alive; closed questions are quick and easy to start with. Mix both, and always ask a follow-up.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you do? ... Where you from?"
"What do you do? ... Where are you from?"

Most English questions need an auxiliary like do, are, or have before the subject. Drop it and the question sounds broken.

Practice

"Where _____are you from?" (open)

"_____Do you know many people here?" (closed)
