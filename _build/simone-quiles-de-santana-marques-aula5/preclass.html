Small Talk That Works -- Conversation Starters & Question Forms

How to start, build, and keep a professional conversation when networking. New vocabulary: small talk, break the ice, get to know, catch up, in common, bring up, keep in touch, networking, conversation starter, wrap up. Open vs closed questions and correct question word order.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each expression and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Small talk -- light, casual conversation
"We made some small talk before the meeting started." (Conversa informal)
Break the ice -- to start a conversation in a relaxed way
"A simple question is a great way to break the ice." (Quebrar o gelo)
Get to know -- to become familiar with someone
"I would love to get to know your team better." (Conhecer)
Catch up -- to exchange recent news with someone
"Let us catch up over lunch and talk more." (Atualizar a conversa)
In common -- shared between people
"It turns out we have a lot in common." (Em comum)
Bring up -- to start talking about a topic
"She brought up an interesting point in the panel." (Mencionar)
Keep in touch -- to stay in contact with someone
"Let us keep in touch after the conference." (Manter contato)
Networking -- meeting people to build work relationships
"There is a networking lunch tomorrow at one." (Fazer contatos)
Conversation starter -- a topic or question to begin a chat
"Asking about the trip is a good conversation starter." (Assunto para iniciar conversa)
Wrap up -- to bring something to a close
"Let us wrap up and exchange contacts." (Encerrar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Open vs Closed Questions

GRAMMAR

Para manter uma conversa, use perguntas abertas; comece com What, Where, How. / To keep a conversation going, use open questions; start with What, Where, How.

Type / TipoStarts with / Comeca comExample / Exemplo
Open / AbertaWhat, Where, How, Why, How long"What do you do?" / "Where are you from?"
Closed / FechadaDo, Did, Are, Is, Have"Do you know many people here?"
Word order / OrdemAuxiliary + subject + verb"What do you do?" (nao "What you do?")
Key point / Ponto-chavePerguntas abertas mantem a conversa viva; sempre faca uma pergunta de acompanhamento.
