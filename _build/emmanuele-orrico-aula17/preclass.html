The halfway mark. This is a review: no new grammar, no new words. You look back at the first half and get ready to describe your role at Sanofi out loud. Review the consolidated words: results, challenge, launch, target, performance, benchmark, growth, gap. Review the five structures you already use — the past ("I presented"), the present ("I manage"), what is happening now ("I'm working on..."), your plans ("I'm going to..."), and comparatives ("stronger than last year"). Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Five Structures You Carry (Review)

GRAMMAR

No new grammar today — just the five structures you have used all half, side by side. Each one is for a different moment.

MomentStructureExample
The pastverb + -ed (or irregular)"Last quarter, I presented the results."
The present (routine)verb (base form)"I manage eight disease areas."
Happening nowam / is / are + verb-ing"Right now, I am working on the launch."
A planam / is / are + going to + verb"In August, I am going to present."
Comparingadjective + -er + than"This year is stronger than last year."

To talk about your progress, open with the new expression "Looking back at...": "Looking back at the first half, I'm proud of my progress." Careful: keep the -ing for now ("am working", not "am work"), and use -er + than for short adjectives ("stronger than", not "more strong than").
