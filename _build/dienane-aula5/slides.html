Chapter 3 -- The Clock

Telling Time

o'clock · half past · a quarter past/to

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"It is nine o'clock." (9:00)

"It is half past nine." (9:30)

"It is a quarter past ten." (10:15)

"What time is the meeting?"

ClockEnglishEasy way
9:00nine o'clocknine (a.m. / p.m.)
9:30half past ninenine thirty
9:15a quarter past ninenine fifteen
9:45a quarter to tennine forty-five

Two ways, both correct! The "easy way" (nine thirty) always works. Question: "What time...?"

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The meeting is at nine and half."

✓ RIGHT

"The meeting is at half past nine."

✗ WRONG

The Phone Call

Scheduling with Mr. Costa

C
Hello? Marcos Costa speaking.
D
Good morning, Mr. Costa! This is Dienane, from Human Resources. Are you available for an interview this week?
C
Oh, yes! Thank you for calling. What day is good?
D
Let me check my calendar... I am busy on Tuesday. Is Thursday good for you?
C
Thursday is perfect. What time?
D
At half past nine, in the morning. The meeting room is on the second floor.
C
Half past nine, Thursday. I am writing it in my schedule. Thank you very much!
D
Great! See you on Thursday at nine thirty, Mr. Costa. Have a good day!

The Call

Scenario: Call the candidate: say who you are, ask if he is available, and schedule the interview for Thursday at 9:30.

Keyword chips:

This is Dienane, from... Are you available...? at half past nine
Role-Play: Semi-free

Plan B

Scenario: The candidate is busy on Thursday! Check your calendar and propose another day and time. Negotiate until you both agree.

Keywords:

Is Friday good for you? a quarter past...
Role-Play: Free

The Full Call

Scenario: A new candidate, a new call — from "Hello?" to "See you": introduce yourself, check his availability, look at your calendar, agree on day and time, and confirm everything.

No keywords. The phone is yours!
