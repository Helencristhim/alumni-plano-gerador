Warm-up

You Ran the Room — Now Run the Review

The meeting is done. Now it's review season at Brink's. The hardest conversation isn't with a room — it's the one-on-one where you tell someone honestly how they did, and help them grow.

Three Moves of a Good Review

Balance It, Anchor It, Report It

1 — Balance

Open with specific recognition, then one honest development area.

2 — Anchor (STAR)

Situation, Task, Action, Result — keep it concrete, not opinion.

3 — Report

"She said the report had been late" — report what was said, accurately.

Chapter 3

Reporting What Was Said

From "I am stretched too thin" to "she said she was stretched too thin"

Grammar Discovery

What Shifts When You Report It?

From the quote to the report — watch the verb, the pronoun, and the time word

"I am stretched too thin." → She said she was stretched too thin. (present → past)

"I will fix the dashboard." → He told me he would fix the dashboard. (will → would; told + me)

"The report is late today." → She said the report was late that day. (is → was; today → that day)

One step back in time, one shift in person, one move in the time word.

The Rule

Reported Speech — the Backshift

Direct speechSteps back toReported speech
present simple "I am ready"past simple"she said she was ready"
past simple "I finished it"past perfect"he said he had finished it"
will "I will fix it"would"she said she would fix it"
can / mustcould / had to"he said he could / had to"

Three things move together:

1. The tense steps back one place (present → past, past → past perfect, will → would, can → could, must → had to). 2. Say vs tell: tell needs a person ("she told me..."); say does not ("she said that..."). 3. Time and place words shift: today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, now → then, yesterday → the day before. So "I'll send it tomorrow" becomes "she said she would send it the next day."

Common Mistake

Two Traps in Reporting

✗ NO BACKSHIFT

"She said she is stretched too thin."

✓ TENSE STEPS BACK

"She said she was stretched too thin."

✗ SAY + PERSON

"He said me he would fix it."

✓ TELL + PERSON

"He told me he would fix it."

Step the tense back, and remember: tell needs a person, say does not.

Chapter 4

The Appraisal

Victor gives Sofia her mid-year review

Dialogue

Sofia's Mid-Year Review

V
Thanks for making time, Sofia. I want to start with recognition, because your client work this cycle has been genuinely commendable.
S
Thank you. That account was a hard one, so it's good to hear that.
V
Let me be specific. When the renewal was at risk in March, you stepped in, rebuilt the proposal overnight, and we kept the contract. That is a real accomplishment, and it is exactly the strength I want to call out.
S
I appreciate you naming the result, not just saying "good job". So — where do I need to grow?
V
One development area: the quarterly dashboard underdelivered — it was two weeks late and missing the regional split. I want to make this actionable, so let's agree on a template and a check-in each Friday. That follow-through is on both of us.
S
That's fair. Honestly, I was stretched too thin that quarter — the client work took everything I had. A template and a Friday check-in would help.
V
Good — in my note to the director I'll say you told me you were stretched too thin, so we can balance the load. And a review is two-way: what feedback do you have for me on how I lead the team?
S
Since you ask — earlier in the year I wasn't sure what you expected from the dashboard. Clearer goals up front would help. But this review is the clearest I've had here.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Victor anchor his praise instead of just saying "good job"?
With STAR: he names the situation (the renewal at risk in March), the action (she rebuilt the proposal overnight), and the result (they kept the contract) — a real accomplishment.
2. What is the one development area, and how does Victor make it actionable?
The quarterly dashboard underdelivered (two weeks late, missing the regional split); he makes it actionable with an agreed template and a Friday check-in — follow-through on both of them.
3. What feedback does Sofia give Victor about how he leads?
That earlier in the year she wasn't sure what he expected from the dashboard — clearer goals up front would help.

Listening 2

The Coach's Voice Note

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. Why does Clara say "commendable" alone means nothing?
Because praise has to be specific — tell the person what they did and what it produced. Anchoring praise in a result (STAR) is what makes it stick.
2. What does she say about the one thing that has to change?
Don't bury it — name it, but make it actionable with a follow-through you both own. Vague feedback is unkind because the person can't act on it.
3. What grammar tip does she give for reporting up the chain?
When you report what your team said, backshift: "I am stretched" becomes "she said she was stretched" — the tense steps back, the pronoun shifts, and time words move (today → that day, will → would, can → could).
