Feedback & Performance Reviews

Open Block 6: run a fair performance review. Give feedback that helps people grow — open with specific recognition, anchor praise in a result with the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result), balance it with one honest, actionable development area, and ask for feedback in return. New vocab: appraisal, feedback, praise, recognition, strengths, development area, accomplishment, underdeliver, actionable, follow-through, raise the bar, commendable. Grammar: reported speech — the backshift. When you report what someone said, the tense steps back (present → past, past → past perfect, will → would, can → could), tell takes a person (told me) while say does not, and time words move (today → that day, tomorrow → the next day): "She told me she was stretched too thin", "He said he would fix it".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of a fair review. Tap Listen to hear each word.

Appraisal -- a formal meeting that evaluates how someone has performed (PT: avaliação de desempenho)
"Her mid-year appraisal is scheduled for Thursday."
Feedback -- information you give someone about their work to help them improve (PT: retorno / feedback)
"Can I give you some feedback on the deck?"
Recognition -- crediting someone openly for a real contribution or result (PT: reconhecimento)
"Public recognition matters more to her than a bonus."
Accomplishment -- a meaningful result a person achieved through their own effort (PT: realização / feito)
"Closing that account was a real accomplishment."
Development area -- a skill or behaviour a person still needs to improve (PT: ponto a desenvolver)
"Time management is his main development area this year."
Underdeliver -- to produce less or worse than was promised or expected (PT: entregar menos do que o esperado)
"He underdelivered on the dashboard, and we need to talk."
Actionable -- clear and specific enough that the person can actually act on it (PT: prático / acionável)
"Make the feedback actionable: tell her exactly what to change."
Commendable -- genuinely deserving of praise (PT: louvável / digno de elogio)
"Her work with the new client has been commendable."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read how a review gets reported up the chain, and answer the questions.

After the mid-year review, the Growth Manager wrote a short note to his director, and that note runs on reported speech. In the meeting Sofia had said, "I am stretched too thin," so in the note he wrote that she said she was stretched too thin — the present am steps back to the past was. She had also said, "I will fix the dashboard," which becomes she said she would fix it — will becomes would. And when she promised, "I finished the proposal," he reported that she said she had finished it — the past simple steps back to the past perfect. Notice the verb choice: he used told when he named the listener ("she told me she needed support") but said when he did not ("she said she was proud of the quarter"). The time words moved too: "I'll send it today" became she said she would send it that day. Get the backshift right and a report sounds precise, not sloppy.

1. "I am stretched too thin" reported becomes:
A "She said she is stretched too thin." (no backshift)
B "She said she was stretched too thin." (present → past)
C "She said she has been stretched too thin."
2. Which verb correctly names the listener?
A "She said me she needed support." (say + person)
B "She told me she needed support." (tell + person)
C "She telled to me she needed support."
3. "I will fix it" reported becomes:
A "He said he will fix it." (will unchanged)
B "He said he would fix it." (will → would)
C "He said he fixed it."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech & the Backshift

GRAMMAR

When you report what was said, three things move together.

Direct speechSteps back toReported speech
present "I am ready"past simple"she said she was ready"
past "I finished it"past perfect"he said he had finished it"
will "I will fix it"would"she said she would fix it"
can / mustcould / had to"he said he could / had to"
The three moves: (1) the tense steps back (present→past, past→past perfect, will→would, can→could, must→had to); (2) tell needs a person ("she told me..."), say does not ("she said that..."); (3) time words shift (today→that day, tomorrow→the next day, now→then, yesterday→the day before). PT: ao reportar o que alguem disse, tres coisas mudam juntas. O tempo verbal da um passo atras (presente vira passado, passado vira mais-que-perfeito, will vira would, can vira could). "Tell" pede uma pessoa ("she told me"), "say" nao ("she said that"). E as palavras de tempo mudam (today vira that day, tomorrow vira the next day). Assim "I'll send it tomorrow" vira "she said she would send it the next day".
