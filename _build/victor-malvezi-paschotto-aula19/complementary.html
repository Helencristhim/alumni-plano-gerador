Lesson 19 -- Feedback & Performance Reviews

Series
Ted Lasso / The Office (US) — any scene of a manager giving a one-on-one performance review or honest feedback

Watch how the feedback lands — or doesn't. Notice whether the manager opens with real recognition, anchors praise in a result, names a clear development area, or just gives a vague "good job". Both the good and bad examples are useful.

Tip: pause and rewrite one vague line as STAR feedback — situation, action, result.

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant / HBR IdeaCast — any episode on giving feedback, performance reviews, or radical candor

Leaders unpack how to make feedback specific, balanced, and actionable — recognition that names a result, and development areas the person can actually act on. Ideal prep before Victor's real review cycle at Brink's.

Tip: note one phrase the host uses to soften a hard message, and one to make it actionable.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review / TED — "How to Give Feedback" or a grammar clip on reported speech (backshift)

A practical bridge from a fair review into the words that carry it: recognition with a result, an actionable development area, and reporting what was said accurately when you take it up the chain — "she said she was...", "he told me he would...".

Tip: after watching, report three things from the video using the backshift — "the speaker said that...".
