Review Block 3 -- Presentation Mastery

Bring the whole presentation block together: open, signpost, present data, handle Q&A, close, and pitch. Review vocabulary: signpost, transition, data, audience, recap, takeaway, pace, momentum. Grammar recap: signpost the parts (first/then/finally), present data (rose / dropped by), and say WHY with "to" and "so that".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Review each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Signpost -- a word or phrase that tells the audience what comes next
"First, then, finally are signpost words." (Sinalização / indicar o próximo passo)
Transition -- a phrase that moves the talk from one part to the next
"Now, let me show you the numbers is a transition." (Transição)
Data -- the numbers and facts you present to the audience
"The data shows costs dropped by fifteen percent." (Dados)
Audience -- the group of people who listen to your talk
"Signpost the parts so the audience can follow." (Plateia / público)
Recap -- a short summary of the main points at the end
"To sum up, here is a quick recap of the talk." (Resumo / recapitulação)
Takeaway -- the one main idea you want the audience to remember
"My takeaway is simple: one tool saved two hours." (Mensagem principal / lição)
Pace -- how fast or slow you speak; a calm pace is steady
"Keep a calm pace and do not rush." (Ritmo)
Momentum -- the energy that keeps the talk moving forward
"Smooth transitions keep the momentum." (Impulso / momentum)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo presents Project Atlas to his team. First he opens and gives a signpost so the audience can follow: "first the status, then the numbers, finally the next steps." Each part has a smooth transition to the next, which keeps the momentum. Then he shows the data: "costs dropped by fifteen percent, and revenue rose." He always says WHY it matters: "I built one tool to save the team two hours, so that managers work on real decisions." He keeps a calm pace and does not rush. At the end, he gives a short recap and one clear takeaway: "one small tool saved two hours a week."

1. Why does Diogo use signpost words?
A So the audience can follow the parts.
B To make the talk longer.
C To hide the numbers.
2. How does he present the data?
A "Costs dropped by fifteen percent, and revenue rose."
B "Costs dropped of fifteen percent."
C "The numbers are somewhere."
3. What is his takeaway?
A One small tool saved two hours a week.
B The report is very long.
C He talks for a long time.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signpost, Data, and WHY

GRAMMAR

Recapitulação dos três padrões do bloco: sinalizar as partes, apresentar dados e dizer o porquê. / A recap of the three patterns of the block: signpost the parts, present data, and say why.

Job / FunçãoPattern / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Signpostfirst ... then ... finally"First the status, then the numbers, finally the next steps."
Datasujeito + rose / dropped by + número"Costs dropped by fifteen percent."
Why / Porquêto + verbo  /  so that + sujeito + verbo"I built it to save time, so that managers work less."
Common error / Erro comumPara dados use by + número ("dropped by 15%"), nunca "dropped of". Depois de so that use uma frase com sujeito + verbo, nunca "to" ("so that managers save", não "so that to save").
Key point / Ponto-chaveUma boa delivery junta os três: você sinaliza as partes, apresenta os dados, e diz o porquê. Termine com um recap curto e um takeaway.
