This Meeting Is Going Nowhere -- Ending a Meeting That Stalls

Stop a meeting that goes in circles: interrupt, park the topic, force the decision, close with owners and dates. New vocabulary: wrap up, park, circle back, off topic, drag on, next steps, out of time, on the same page.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Wrap up -- to finish a meeting, on purpose and on time
"We have ten minutes -- let us wrap up." (Encerrar)
Park -- to stop a topic now, and keep it for later
"Let us park that and come back to it on Thursday." (Deixar para depois)
Circle back -- to return to a topic at an agreed time
"We will circle back to the budget on Thursday." (Retomar)
Off topic -- away from the subject of the meeting
"We are off topic, and we have ten minutes left." (Fora do assunto)
Drag on -- to continue for too long, with no result
"The discussion dragged on for forty minutes." (Se arrastar)
Next steps -- what happens after the meeting: who does what, and when
"So the next steps are: Marina sends the file by Friday." (Próximos passos)
Out of time -- with no minutes left
"We are almost out of time -- one decision, please." (Sem tempo)
On the same page -- agreed: everybody understands it the same way
"Before we close: are we on the same page?" (Alinhados / de acordo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Fifty minutes gone, and the discussion has dragged on about the mobile app -- which is completely off topic. Nobody stops it, because stopping people feels rude. Diogo does it anyway: "Sorry to interrupt -- we are out of time, and we still have one decision to make." He does not kill the topic; he parks it: "Let us park that and circle back on Thursday, with the client on the call." Then he forces the decision the meeting was called for, and checks that everybody is on the same page. And he never closes without the next steps: "Marina sends the file by Friday, and I call the client on Monday." A name and a date, every time. Then he wraps up five minutes early -- the best meeting of the week.

1. Why does Diogo interrupt?
A Because they are out of time and the decision has not been made.
B Because he does not like the mobile app.
C Because the meeting is boring.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A We are running out of time.
B We are running out of the time.
C We are running out from time.
3. What makes a real next step?
A A name and a date: "Marina sends the file by Friday."
B A promise: "We will look into it."
C Another meeting next week.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Park, Decide, Close

GRAMMAR

Retomar o controle da reunião: interromper, adiar o assunto, decidir e fechar com dono e data. / Taking the room back.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Interromper / InterruptSorry to interrupt + o motivo (tempo, pauta)Sorry to interrupt -- we are running out of time.
Adiar / Park itLet us park that + circle back on + diaLet us park that and circle back on Thursday.
Fechar / CloseSo the next steps are: quem + verbo + by + diaSo the next steps are: Marina sends the file by Friday.
Ponto-chave / Key pointFalso amigo perigoso: resume em inglês = RETOMAR, não "resumir". Para resumir, diga to sum up / to summarize. E "run out of time" não leva artigo: nunca "out of the time".

Quiz

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real meeting at Oracle that always drags on. Close it out loud, for two minutes: (1) interrupt with the reason, (2) park the off-topic item, with a day, (3) force the decision, with two options, (4) check that everybody is on the same page, (5) give the next steps, with names and dates. Record it.
Now somebody answers: "Are you saying my topic is not important?" Answer out loud. Do not apologize twice, and do not reopen the topic -- park it with a date and an owner, and go back to the decision. Record it.
