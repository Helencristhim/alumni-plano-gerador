Useful Language

How to Take the Room Back

Interrupt → park it → the decision → next steps with an owner and a date → close early

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one stops the room? Which one saves the topic? Which one ends the meeting?

"Sorry to interrupt -- we are running out of time."

"Let us park that and circle back on Thursday."

"To sum up, we have one decision and two open points."

"So the next steps are: Marina sends the file by Friday."

Grammar Rule

Park, Decide, Close

MoveStructureExample
Interrupt politelySorry to interrupt + the reason (time, agenda)Sorry to interrupt -- we are running out of time.
Park the topicLet us park + that / circle back + on + dayLet us park that and circle back on Thursday.
Close with next stepsTo sum up, ... / So the next steps are: who + verb + by + daySo the next steps are: Marina sends the file by Friday.

Every next step needs a name and a date. "We will look into it" is not a next step -- it is a promise that nobody made. "Marina sends the file by Friday" is a next step, because if it does not happen, everybody knows who and when.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We are running out of the time."
"We are running out of time."
"Resuming, the next steps are three."
"To sum up, there are three next steps."

Run out of time não leva artigo. E cuidado com o falso amigo: resume em inglês significa RETOMAR. "Resumir" é to sum up / to summarize.

D

"Sorry to interrupt -- we are running out of time, and we still have one decision to make."

S

"This is important, Diogo. The app is the future of the account."

D

"It is important, and it is off topic today. Let us park it and circle back on Thursday, with the client on the call."

"Agreed. And we are finishing early, for once."

Your Turn -- Semi-free

They Keep Talking

"This is important. It is the future of the account."

They fight you twice: first they say the off-topic subject is more important, then they ask to meet again next week instead of deciding. Do not apologize twice, do not accept a new meeting. Park it with a date, force the decision, and close with owners.

Your Real Meeting

Two minutes. No notes. One decision.

Take a real meeting at Oracle that always drags on. Close it out loud: interrupt, park the off-topic item with a date, force the decision, confirm everybody is on the same page, and give the next steps with names and days.
