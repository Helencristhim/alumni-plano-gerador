Chapter 3

The Code

Modals of advice: should, ought to, had better, recommend

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should prioritize the reporting system."

"We ought to weigh the options first."

"We had better cut costs before the audit."

"I would recommend automating the report."

StructureUseExample
should / shouldn't + baseNormal advice (no "to" after should)You should prioritize it.
ought to + baseAdvice, slightly more formal ("to" is part of it)We ought to weigh the options.
had better + baseStrong advice or warning (no "to")We had better cut costs.
recommend / suggest + -ing (or that + base)Polite recommendation — never "recommend you to"I recommend automating it.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You should to review the numbers."

✓ RIGHT

"You should review the numbers."

✗ WRONG

"We had better to send it today."

✓ RIGHT

"We had better send it today."

✗ WRONG

"I recommend you to draft a plan."

✓ RIGHT

"I recommend drafting a plan."

After "should" and "had better," use the base verb with no "to." "Ought to" already contains "to." And "recommend" or "suggest" is followed by -ing or a "that" clause, never "someone to do."

Chapter 4

In the Room

Giving your recommendations on the Thursday call

Dialogue

The Recommendations Round

A
Izabel, the progress this year has been excellent. Now the board wants your view. What do you recommend we focus on next quarter?
I
Thank you, Andy. I would recommend automating the reporting first. We should prioritize that, because it still takes too much of my time.
A
That makes sense. And what about the new product line? Should we scale it up now?
I
I would suggest we hold off on that for one quarter. We ought to weigh the options carefully before we invest more.
A
Fair point. The partners also asked about costs. Any recommendation there?
I
Yes. We had better cut costs on the manual processes, and I would advise outsourcing the payroll to save time.
A
Good thinking. Should we hire more engineers to support the growth?
I
Not yet. I recommend delegating more to the current team first, and reconsidering the headcount after the audit.
