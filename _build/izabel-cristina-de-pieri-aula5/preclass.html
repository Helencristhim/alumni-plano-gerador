Aula 05 -- Pre-class

What Do You Recommend? -- Making Recommendations

Giving recommendations in the Thursday meeting. Key words: recommend, suggest, advise, prioritize, postpone, delegate, reconsider, draft, outsource, cut costs, hold off on, weigh the options. Structures: modals of advice -- should, ought to, had better + base verb, and I would recommend / suggest + -ing.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recommend -- to advise a course of action as the best choice (recomendar)
"Last week we closed a big deal, so I would recommend automating the reporting first."
Suggest -- to put an idea forward for people to consider (sugerir)
"I suggest we hold off on the launch for now."
Advise -- to give someone professional guidance (aconselhar)
"I would advise outsourcing the payroll."
Prioritize -- to deal with the most important things first (priorizar)
"We should prioritize the reporting system."
Postpone -- to move something to a later time (adiar)
"We might postpone the launch until July."
Delegate -- to give a task or responsibility to someone else (delegar)
"I recommend delegating the follow-ups to the team."
Reconsider -- to think about a decision again (reconsiderar)
"We ought to reconsider the timing."
Draft -- to prepare a first version of a plan or document (rascunhar (uma primeira versão))
"You should draft a twelve-month plan."
Outsource -- to pay an outside company to do work for you (terceirizar)
"It might be worth outsourcing the payroll."
Cut costs -- to reduce how much money you spend (cortar custos)
"We had better cut costs on the manual processes."
Hold off on -- to delay doing something until later (segurar / adiar (algo))
"I would suggest we hold off on that for one quarter."
Weigh the options -- to compare choices carefully before deciding (avaliar as opções)
"We ought to weigh the options before we invest."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the Thursday call, the board asks Izabel for her recommendations. She is ready. "First, we should prioritize the reporting system," she says. "I would recommend automating it, because it still takes too much of my time." On the new product line, she is careful: "I suggest we hold off on the launch for one quarter. We ought to weigh the options before we invest more." On costs, her advice is direct: "We had better cut costs on the manual processes, and I would advise outsourcing the payroll." And on the team, she recommends patience: "I recommend delegating more to the current team first, and reconsidering the headcount after the audit."

1. Why is it "should prioritize" and not "should to prioritize"?
A Because "prioritize" is a special verb.
B After a modal like "should," we use the base verb with no "to."
C Because the sentence is a question.
2. Look at "I would recommend automating". Why "automating" and not "to automate"?
A "Recommend" and "suggest" are followed by -ing (or a "that" clause), never "to + verb".
B Because "automating" is a noun here.
C Because the action is in the past.
3. Which structure gives the strongest advice or warning?
A "I suggest we hold off on the launch."
B "We had better cut costs on the manual processes."
C "We ought to weigh the options."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modals of Advice & Recommendation

GRAMMAR

Giving recommendations in a meeting (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
should / shouldn't + baseConselho normal: modal + verbo base, SEM "to"You should review it.
ought to + baseConselho, um pouco mais formal (o "to" já faz parte)We ought to weigh the options.
had better + baseConselho forte / aviso: melhor fazer, SEM "to"We had better cut costs.
recommend / suggest + -ingRecomendação polida: NUNCA "recommend you to"I recommend automating it.
recommend / suggest (that) + baseForma mais formal: I recommend that you draft a plan.
