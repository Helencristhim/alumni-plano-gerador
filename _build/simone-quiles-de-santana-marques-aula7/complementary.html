Lesson 7 -- Expressing Opinions

Series
The Good Wife -- Season 1, Episode 5: "Crash"

A legal drama full of courtroom and office debates. Watch how the lawyers state their point of view, build an argument, persuade others, and look for common ground -- and notice the polite ways they disagree.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences with I think, in my view, or I disagree from a debate scene. Then record yourself reusing one in your own work context.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Disagree Productively"

Practical advice on sharing opinions and disagreeing at work: how to give your point of view, build a strong argument, persuade colleagues, and find common ground without conflict. Listen for the phrases speakers use to open and defend an opinion.

Note 3 phrases the speakers use to disagree politely. Compare them with how Simone and Daniel debate the client proposal.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Giving opinions" / "Agreeing and disagreeing in English"

A short, practical lesson on expressing opinions in English: opening with I think and in my view, supporting them with an argument, and agreeing or disagreeing politely. Directly useful for your discussions and negotiations at Telefonica.

After watching, note three opinion phrases, then record a 60-second opinion on a work topic using at least four new words from today.
