The Opinion Code

Open and Defend

PhraseUseExample
I think ...Give your opinion (neutral)"I think we should wait."
In my view, ...Give your opinion (more formal)"In my view, the risk is high."
I agree / I disagreeReact to an opinion"I agree with you."
I see your point, but ...Disagree politely"I see your point, but I am not sure."
Golden ruleopinion phrase + full idea (subject + verb). Use a comma after "In my view," and "From my point of view,".

Key idea: in my view is a little more formal than I think. To disagree without conflict, start with "I see your point, but ...".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am agree with you. ... In my opinion I think it is risky."
"I agree with you. ... In my opinion, it is risky."

Say I agree, never I am agree. And do not mix in my opinion with I think -- choose one.

Argument FOR

The client wants speed, and a fast yes keeps the relationship strong.

Argument AGAINST
