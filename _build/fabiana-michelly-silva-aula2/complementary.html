Lesson 2 -- First Contact

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 2: "Errors and Omissions"

Harvey and Mike navigate their first client meeting together. Watch how professionals introduce themselves, establish credibility, and handle unexpected questions. The legal vocabulary parallels supply chain contract language.

Focus on how Harvey frames his introductions differently depending on who he is talking to. Notice the third conditional in retrospective discussions: "If we had filed earlier..."

Podcast
Supply Chain Now -- Episode: "Global Sourcing Strategies for 2025"

Industry leaders discuss how they evaluate and onboard new international suppliers. Listen for terms like lead time, purchase order, freight, and sourcing used in real professional contexts.

Write down 3 phrases the speakers use when introducing their companies or roles. Compare with how you introduced yourself in today's lesson.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Make a Great First Impression"

A 7-minute video on the psychology of first impressions in professional settings. Directly applicable to vendor introductions and initial meetings with international partners.

After watching, practice introducing yourself using the techniques mentioned. Record a 60-second self-introduction as if meeting Klaus from TechFlow for the first time.
