Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Lead time -- the time between placing an order and receiving it
"The lead time for this component is four weeks." (Prazo de entrega)
Purchase order -- a formal document requesting goods from a supplier
"We submitted the purchase order yesterday." (Ordem de compra)
Freight -- goods transported by truck, ship, or plane; also the cost of transport
"The freight cost was lower than expected." (Frete)
Sourcing -- the process of finding suppliers for goods or services
"We are sourcing new vendors for the APAC region." (Fornecimento)
Invoice -- a document showing what you owe for goods or services
"Please send the invoice to our finance department." (Fatura)
End-to-end -- covering the entire process from start to finish
"We offer an end-to-end supply chain solution." (Ponta a ponta)
Turnaround -- the time needed to complete a task or process
"They promised a two-week turnaround on repairs." (Tempo de resposta)
Carrier -- a company that transports goods
"Our carrier confirmed the delivery date." (Transportadora)
Clearance -- official permission for goods to pass through customs
"Customs clearance took longer than expected." (Desembaraço)
Allocation -- the distribution of resources or products to different areas
"The allocation of terminals to each office is decided quarterly." (Alocação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last month, Bloomberg received a purchase order from a new client in Tokyo. The lead time was tight, and the carrier could not guarantee clearance before the deadline. Fabiana's team had to find an alternative sourcing strategy. "If we had started the procurement process earlier, we would have avoided the delay," Fabiana explained. "And if the vendor had sent the invoice on time, we would not have missed the freight window." In the end, the team managed to carry out a fast turnaround by leveraging their end-to-end contract with a backup supplier. The allocation was adjusted, and the shipment arrived just two days late.

1. What structure does Fabiana use to talk about a past situation that did NOT happen?
A "If we start earlier, we will avoid the delay."
B "If we had started earlier, we would have avoided the delay."
C "If we started earlier, we would avoid the delay."
2. Why does the third conditional use "had + past participle" in the if-clause?
A Because the action is happening now.
B Because it refers to an unreal past situation that cannot be changed.
C Because it describes a future plan.
3. What does "carry out" mean in "carry out a fast turnaround"?
A To execute or complete a task.
B To transport goods outside.
C To cancel a process.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de situações irreais no passado. / Used to talk about unreal past situations.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Affirmative / AfirmativaIf + had + past participle, would have + past participleIf we had started earlier, we would have avoided the delay.
Negative / NegativaIf + had not + past participle, would not have + past participleIf the vendor had not sent the invoice late, we would not have missed the deadline.
Question / PerguntaWould + subject + have + past participle if + had + past participle?Would you have chosen a different carrier if you had known the freight cost?
Key point / Ponto-chaveO third conditional fala de um passado IRREAL. Não aconteceu. Serve para refletir: "O que TERIA acontecido se..."
