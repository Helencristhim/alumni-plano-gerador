Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The Third Conditional -- reflecting on past decisions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What pattern do the orange words follow?

"If we had contacted them earlier, we would have received the shipment on time."

"If the vendor had met compliance, we would not have cancelled the order."

"She would have approved the invoice if she had seen it yesterday."

"If I had known about the freight cost, I would have chosen a different carrier."

Grammar Rule

The Third Conditional

FormCondition (IF clause)Result (MAIN clause)
AffirmativeIf + had + past participlewould have + past participle
NegativeIf + had not + past participlewould not have + past participle
QuestionIf + had + past participleWould + subject + have + past participle?

Use: To talk about imaginary situations in the past -- things that did NOT happen. The third conditional reflects on how a different past action would have led to a different result.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If I would have known about the delay, I would have contacted you."
"If I had known about the delay, I would have contacted you."

The IF clause always uses had + past participle, never "would have." Reserve "would have" for the result clause only.
