Chapter 4: Can & Could

Can & Could

I can present · Can I share? · Could you repeat?

Discover the Grammar

Read and Notice

"I can share my screen."

"Can I open the document?"

"Can you send me the checklist?"

"Could you repeat that, please?"

FormUse it forExample
I / you can + verbabilityI can present.
Can I...?permissionCan I share my screen?
Can you...?requestCan you send it?
Could you...?polite requestCould you repeat that?

After can / could use the base verb (send, not to send). Negative: can't = cannot. / Depois de can/could use o verbo base; negativa = can't.

Language Focus

Ability or Request?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"He cans present the report."

✓ RIGHT

"He can present the report."

✗ WRONG

"Can you to repeat, please?"

✓ RIGHT

"Can you repeat, please?"

Can never adds -s. After can / could use the base verb, with no "to". / Can nunca leva -s; depois vem o verbo base, sem "to".

Yes, I can repeat it. There are eleven critical criteria.
E
Great. Can you send me the checklist after the meeting?

Yes, I can send it today. I can also schedule the next call.

Role-Play 1 of 3 -- Guided

Open the Meeting

Scenario: Open the online meeting with your colleague. Check the sound and ask to share your screen, using the keywords below.

Can you hear me? Can I share? I can present
Role-Play 2 of 3 -- Semi-free

Ask and Answer

Scenario: The colleague asks "Could you...?" and "Can you...?". Answer with can, and make your own polite requests with could.

Yes, I can... Could you repeat? Can you send...? clarify / explain
Role-Play 3 of 3 -- Free

Run the Whole Call

Scenario: Run the whole online meeting: open it, check the sound, share your screen, present, request a document, schedule the next call, and close.

No keywords. The meeting is yours!
