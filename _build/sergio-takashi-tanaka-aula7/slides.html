Chapter 3: The Code

Finished, or Up to Now?

rose last year · has fallen since January

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Exposure rose sharply last year."

"The desk cut the biggest positions in March."

"The trend has improved since March."

"Breaches have dropped to zero this year."

Why rose / cut for the finished time, but has improved / have dropped for the change up to now?

TenseUseExample
Past simple
verb + -ed (rose, cut)		A finished time that is over: last year, in March, in 2024, yesterday. When it happened matters.Exposure rose sharply last year.
Present perfect
have / has + past participle		A change that reaches up to now, or a result still true. Time words: since, so far, this year, already, yet, for.The trend has improved since March.
The signalFinished time word (last year, in March) → past simple. Up-to-now word (since, so far, this year, yet) → present perfect.

Finished & over = past simple · change up to now = present perfect (have / has + participle).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We have reduced the limit last year."
"We reduced the limit last year."
"The trend improved since March."
"The trend has improved since March."
"Breaches dropped to zero this year, and there are none now."
"Breaches have dropped to zero this year."

Use the past simple with a finished time word (last year, in March), and the present perfect (have / has + participle) with an up-to-now word (since, so far, this year, yet). Never put last year with have.

Chapter 4: The Year in Review

Report the Year

A colleague asks how the profile has changed

Dialogue

The Year-End Review

M
Sergio, thanks for joining. Before the committee, let me ask the big one: how has the risk profile on the desk changed this year?
S
In short, it has improved a lot. Last year was hard: exposure climbed to a new peak in the first quarter. But since March, the trend has been much better.
M
Good. And the limit breaches? Last year we had several.
S
They have dropped to zero this year. We fixed the controls in the spring, and so far we have kept a clean track record with the regulator.
M
That is a strong story. Was there any bad month, or has it been steady all the way?
S
There was one dip in April, but the trend rebounded fast. Since then it has been steady, and the buffer has held.
