Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Open questions and follow-ups

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which questions make the other person say more?

"How was your flight?" (open: invites a longer answer)

"What do you like to do on the weekend?" (open: invites a longer answer)

"Did you have a good trip?" (closed: only yes or no)

"I love hiking. What about you?" (follow-up: keeps the talk going)

Grammar Rule

Open Questions & Follow-ups

Closed question (yes / no)Open question (says more)
"Did you have a good trip?""How was your trip?"
"Do you like Santos?""What do you think of Santos?"
"Was your weekend nice?""What did you do on the weekend?"

Key idea: for small talk, use open questions with How, What, Why, Where, When -- they invite a longer answer and build rapport. Closed questions (Do, Did, Are, Was) only get yes or no. After you listen, keep the talk going with a follow-up: "What about you?" or "Really? Tell me more."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Did you have a nice weekend?" -- "Yes." (talk stops)
"What did you do on the weekend?" -- "I went hiking..." (talk continues)

A closed question ends the talk with one word. An open question (How, What, Why) makes the other person say more and builds rapport.

Grammar Practice

Open the Question

Closed: "Did you have a good trip?" Open: "_____How was your trip?" (use How)
Closed: "Do you like Santos?" Open: "_____What do you think of Santos?" (use What)
Closed: "Was your weekend nice?" Open: "What did you _____do on the weekend?" (open form)
After you answer, keep the talk going: "I love hiking. What _____about you?" (follow-up)

The Small-Talk Cheat Sheet

SMALL-TALK CHEAT SHEET -- HOST: WALYSON SILVA

1. Break the ice (icebreaker)

"Welcome to Santos! How was your flight? Do you have any jet lag?"

2. Safe, easy topics

The weather: "The weather is lovely today, isn't it?" The trip: "How was the traffic from the airport?"

3. Find common ground

"What did you do on your weekend? Hiking is my favorite hobby too -- so we have common ground!"
