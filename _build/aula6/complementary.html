Supplementary Materials -- Lesson 6

Lesson theme: leading meetings. Watch and listen for how a chair opens a meeting, sets the agenda, sequences items (first, then, next, finally), keeps people on topic, and closes with action items.

Series
Succession -- Season 1, Episode 2 ("Sh*t Show at the F**k Factory")

A high-stakes board meeting where the family fights for control. Watch how the chair opens, controls the floor, and how people interrupt, agree, and disagree. Notice the meeting structure under pressure.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 phrases used to open, redirect, or close the discussion, then adapt them to a Quinto Andar kickoff meeting.

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant

Organizational psychologist Adam Grant on how great teams run meetings and make decisions. Listen for the sequencing and facilitation language ("let's start with...", "let's move on to...", "to recap...").

Tip: pick an episode on meetings or teamwork. Note 5 phrases a facilitator uses to keep a discussion on track.

A clear lesson on chairing a meeting from start to finish: getting attention, presenting agenda items, moving between topics, inviting participation, and closing. Exactly the language of this lesson.
