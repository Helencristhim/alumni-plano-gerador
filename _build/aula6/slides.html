Chapter 1: The Setup

The Chair Does Four Jobs

1. Open — greet, state the goal, set the agenda
2. Sequence — move through items in order (first, then, next, finally)
3. Manage — keep people on topic, open the floor, table side-issues
4. Close — recap, assign action items, wrap up

Chapter 3: The Code

What Makes a Chair Sound Like a Chair?

Let's get started. First, let's review the agenda.
Then we'll look at the terms. Next, the timeline.
Let's move on to the next item.
Finally, let's agree on the action items.
ToolFormUse
Imperative (Let's + base verb)Let's start / Let's move on / Let's wrap upGuide the group, suggest the next move
Imperative (base verb)Open the floor / Keep it brief / Take a noteDirect, polite instructions
SequencersFirst, Then, Next, After that, FinallyOrder the agenda items clearly

Chapter 4: In Context

Running the Kickoff

Eduardo chairs; Sarah is the partner

E
Thanks for joining, everyone. Let's get started. The goal today is to align on our partnership plan.
S
Sounds good. What's on the agenda?
E
First, the onboarding timeline. Then the commission terms. And finally, next steps.
S
Before the timeline, can we talk about pricing?
E
Good point. Let's table pricing and cover it under terms. Let's stay on the timeline first.
S
Fair enough. The timeline works for us.
E
Great, so we're on the same page. Let me open the floor — any concerns?
E
Then let's wrap up. To recap: I'll send the minutes, and your team reviews the terms by Friday.

Chapter 5: Practice

Run It Function by Function

Open the meeting and state the goal.
"Thanks for joining. Let's get started. The goal today is to finalize the partnership plan."
Present the agenda with sequencers.
"First, the timeline. Then, the terms. Finally, next steps and action items."
Move from one item to the next.
"I think we're on the same page here. Let's move on to the commission terms."
Bring someone back on topic.
"That's a good point. Let's table it for now and come back to it at the end."
Invite participation.
"Let me open the floor. Are there any questions or concerns?"
Close and assign action items.
"Let's wrap up. To recap, I'll send the minutes and your team reviews the terms by Friday."
