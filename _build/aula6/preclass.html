Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The words every meeting chair needs. Click Listen to check pronunciation.

Agenda -- the list of topics to be discussed in a meeting
"The first item on the agenda is the onboarding timeline."
Action Item -- a specific task a person must complete after the meeting
"Your action item is to review the terms by Friday."
Minutes -- the official written record of what was discussed and decided
"I will send the minutes to everyone after the call."
Recap -- a short summary of the key points or decisions
"To recap, we agreed on the timeline and the terms."
Table an Item -- to postpone a topic to a later time (American English)
"Let's table that for now and come back to it at the end."
Wrap Up -- to finish or bring something to a close
"Let's wrap up. To recap, here are the action items."
On the Same Page -- in agreement; sharing the same understanding
"I think we're on the same page about the timeline."
Open the Floor -- to invite others to speak or ask questions
"Let me open the floor. Are there any questions?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Two tools make you sound like the person in charge.

ToolFormExample
Imperative (Let's + base verb)Let's + verb"Let's get started", "Let's move on"
Imperative (base verb)verb..."Open the floor", "Keep it brief"
SequencersFirst / Then / Next / Finally"First, the timeline. Then, the terms."

Watch out: never say "Let's to start" or "First we will to discuss". After "Let's" and after a sequencer, use the base verb: "Let's start", "First, let's discuss".

Stage 5: Lead a Meeting

Reflection
Plan and record the first 60 seconds of a real Quinto Andar meeting: open it, state the goal, and present a three-item agenda using first, then, and finally. Then close with two action items.
Model: "Thanks for joining, everyone. Let's get started. The goal today is to align on the partnership. First, the timeline. Then, the terms. Finally, next steps. Let me open the floor before we wrap up. To recap, I'll send the minutes and your team reviews the terms by Friday."
