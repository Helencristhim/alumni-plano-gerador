Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Rapport -- a friendly relationship in which people understand each other
"We built a good rapport at the conference." (Sintonia)
Mutual -- shared or felt by two or more people
"We have a mutual interest in cloud solutions." (Mútuo)
Reliable -- able to be trusted to do what is expected
"She is a reliable partner; she always delivers." (Confiável)
Referral -- when you recommend someone to one of your contacts
"I got the meeting through a referral." (Indicação)
Reach out -- to make contact with someone, often to start a relationship
"I will reach out to him after the event." (Entrar em contato)
Genuine -- sincere and honest, not pretended or fake
"She showed genuine interest in my work." (Genuíno)
Acquaintance -- a person you know a little, but who is not a close friend
"He is a business acquaintance, not a friend yet." (Conhecido)
Credibility -- the quality of being trusted and believed in
"Good results build your credibility." (Credibilidade)
Nurture -- to care for a relationship and help it grow over time
"You have to nurture professional relationships." (Cultivar)
Approachable -- friendly and easy to talk to
"A good leader is approachable." (Acessível)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Question Tags

GRAMMAR

Usamos question tags (mini-perguntas no fim da frase) para confirmar algo e convidar o outro a responder. Frase positiva leva tag negativo, e vice-versa. / We use question tags to confirm something and invite the other person to respond. A positive sentence takes a negative tag, and a negative sentence takes a positive tag.

StatementTagExample
positive (be)negative: aren't / isn't"You're new here, aren't you?"
positive (present simple)negative: don't / doesn't"You work in tech, don't you?"
positive (present perfect)negative: haven't / hasn't"We've met before, haven't we?"
negative statementpositive tag"You don't know Sarah, do you?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: o tag usa o mesmo auxiliar da frase (be → be, have → have, present simple → do/does, past → did) e o mesmo pronome do sujeito. Frase positiva → tag negativo; frase negativa → tag positivo. Nunca use "isn't it?" com um sujeito como you ("aren't you?").
