Chapter 3 -- The Tag Code

Grammar Discovery

Question tags -- confirming and connecting

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What are the orange words at the end? Why do they change?

"You work in tech, don't you?"

"We've met before, haven't we?"

"You're new at the company, aren't you?"

"You don't know Sarah, do you?"

Grammar Rule

Question Tags

StatementTagExample
positive (be)negative: aren't / isn't"You're new here, aren't you?"
positive (present simple)negative: don't / doesn't"You work in tech, don't you?"
positive (have / present perfect)negative: haven't / hasn't"We've met before, haven't we?"
negative statementpositive tag"You don't know Sarah, do you?"

Key idea: a positive sentence takes a negative tag, and a negative sentence takes a positive tag. The tag uses the same auxiliary as the sentence (be → be, have → have, present simple → do / does, past simple → did), and the same pronoun as the subject. We use tags to confirm and to invite the other person to respond.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You're from Rio, isn't it?"
"You're from Rio, aren't you?"

The tag must match the verb and the subject of the sentence. With you're, use aren't you, never "isn't it". With you work, use don't you.

Grammar Practice

Add the Tag

"You work in sales, _____don't you?" (positive present simple)
"We've met before, _____haven't we?" (positive present perfect)
"You're not leaving yet, _____are you?" (negative statement)
"She knows Tom, _____doesn't she?" (third person, present simple)
