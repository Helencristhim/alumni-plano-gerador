Language Focus

Ask Politely, Propose

Polite forms to make a request and to propose an idea.

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could I ask the committee to consider a second console?" (ask permission)

"Could you review the proposal?" (ask someone, softly)

"Would you mind drafting the proposal?" (very polite + -ing)

"I would like to propose a second console." (propose an idea)

Language Rule

Ask and Propose

FunctionFormExample
Ask permissionCould I + base verbCould I ask for a second console?
Ask someoneCould you + base verbCould you review the proposal?
Very politeWould you mind + verb-ingWould you mind drafting it?
ProposeI would like to propose...I would like to propose a console.

Key idea: Could is softer than Can, so it sounds more polite at work. After Could I / Could you, use the base verb (Could you send it?). After Would you mind, use the -ing form (Would you mind sending it?). To propose, use I would like to propose + an idea, or Would it be possible to + base verb.

Watch Out

Watch Out

✗ "Would you mind to review the proposal?"

✓ "Would you mind reviewing the proposal?" (mind + -ing)

✗ "Could you to send the draft?"

✓ "Could you send the draft?" (base verb, no "to")

Artefact

The Proposal Email

To: Equipment Committee  ·  From: Andreia Heins  ·  Subject: Proposal -- Second Training Console

Dear Committee,

Request: Could I ask you to consider a second training console for the robotic surgery team?

Reason: We currently share one console, so practice time is very limited.

Cost (budget): I know the budget is tight, so let me justify it: the console frees up a critical resource and lets us train without delaying real cases.

Priority: Above all, I would like to prioritize patient safety -- more practice means fewer errors.

Approval: I am asking for your approval to move forward, with a decision by the end of the month. A one-page proposal is attached.
