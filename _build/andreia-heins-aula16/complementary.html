Lesson 16 -- Making Requests & Proposals

Series
The Good Doctor -- any episode where a doctor pitches a treatment or new equipment

Watch how a doctor makes a case to the board or to a senior colleague: a polite request, a clear reason, the cost justified, and a request for approval. A natural model for proposing an idea at work.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two polite ways someone asks for something (for example, "Could I..." or "Would you mind...") and one sentence that justifies a cost or a decision.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Pitch an Idea to Your Boss"

The hosts explain how to propose an idea the professional way: open with a clear request, give the reason, justify the cost against a tight budget, and ask for approval with a deadline.

Note three tips for proposing an idea. Then record a one-minute spoken proposal for something you need at work, using at least four new words from today, such as proposal, budget, justify and approval.

YouTube
Business English -- "Making Polite Requests: Could you, Would you mind"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's language: how to ask for things politely with Could I and Could you, the very polite "Would you mind + -ing", and how to introduce a proposal.

After watching, write five sentences for a real situation at work: two polite requests, one with "Would you mind...", one proposal with "I would like to propose...", and one that asks for approval. Then say them out loud.
