Lesson 7 -- Kickoff: Opening and Leading a Meeting

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 2: "Conflict Resolution"

Watch how a leader opens and runs a meeting. Notice the agenda, the objective, and how people are invited to speak. Listen for imperatives ("Let's start") and modal verbs ("Could you...?").

Write down two opening lines you hear. Rewrite them for a real meeting at Oracle and say them out loud.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Chairing a Meeting"

Practical phrases to open a meeting, set the objective, outline the agenda, and move between topics. Review of today's words: agenda, objective, facilitate, proceed.

Note the host's opening phrases. Record your own thirty-second kickoff using one imperative and one modal verb.

YouTube
"How to Start a Meeting Professionally in English"

A short, clear guide to opening a meeting: greet the team, state the objective, outline the agenda, and invite updates. Great for practicing imperatives and polite modal verbs in real meetings.

After watching, record a sixty-second meeting opening. Use at least three new words and one modal verb with the base verb.
