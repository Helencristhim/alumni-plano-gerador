Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- a list of topics to discuss in a meeting
"Let's look at the agenda for today." (Pauta)
Objective -- the goal you want to reach
"Our main objective is to plan the launch." (Objetivo)
Kick off -- to start a meeting or a project
"Let's kick off the meeting now." (Dar início)
Facilitate -- to lead and guide a discussion
"I will facilitate the meeting today." (Conduzir)
Outline -- to describe the main structure or points
"Let me outline the three topics." (Delinear)
Minutes -- the written notes of a meeting
"Please take the minutes today." (Ata)
Proceed -- to continue to the next step
"Let's proceed to the next topic." (Prosseguir)
Ensure -- to make something certain or sure
"Please ensure everyone can join." (Garantir)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo leads the project kickoff tomorrow. Tonight he prepares the agenda. The main objective is to plan the new launch. He will open the meeting and say, "Let's start." Then he will outline three topics: the timeline, the team roles, and the budget. He knows the rule for leading: "We should cover each topic," and "Could you share your update?" After a modal verb he always uses the base verb, with no "to". He asks Marco to take the minutes, and he will facilitate the discussion. At the end he will proceed to the action items and ensure everyone knows the next steps.

1. What is the main objective of the meeting?
A To plan the new launch.
B To order lunch.
C To end the project.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A We should cover each topic.
B We should to cover each topic.
C We should covering each topic.
3. Who takes the minutes?
A Marco.
B Diogo.
C Sophia.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives & Modal Verbs

GRAMMAR

Dar direção e soar educado ao liderar. / Giving direction and sounding polite when you lead.

Tool / FerramentaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Let's + verbo baseConvidar o grupo / Invite the groupLet's start the meeting.
Please + verbo basePedido direto / Direct requestPlease share your update.
Could / Shall + verbo basePedido educado / Polite requestCould you go first? Shall we begin?
Should + verbo baseRecomendação / RecommendationWe should cover three topics.
Key point / Ponto-chaveDepois de um modal (can, could, should, shall) use o verbo base, SEM "to": "We should cover" (nunca "should to cover"). O mesmo vale para imperativos: "Please start".
