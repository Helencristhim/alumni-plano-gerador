Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key negotiation terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Counteroffer -- a new offer made in response to one you reject
"They rejected our bid and sent a counteroffer."
Make a Concession -- to give something up to move a deal forward
"We made a concession on the interest rate."
Sticking Point -- an issue that blocks an agreement
"The interest rate was the main sticking point."
Settlement -- a final agreement that ends a dispute
"Both sides reached a settlement."
Debt Haircut -- a reduction in the amount a creditor is owed
"Creditors accepted a thirty percent debt haircut."
Standstill Agreement -- a deal to pause all action for a set period
"The banks signed a standstill agreement."
Good Faith -- an honest intention to deal fairly
"We negotiated in good faith."
Walk Away -- to leave a negotiation without a deal
"If they refuse, we are ready to walk away."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Vertex Industries had to negotiate with its creditors. Our team opened with a proposal: "We could offer a longer repayment period." The creditors sent a counteroffer and asked, "Would you accept a smaller discount?" We decided to make a concession on the interest rate to find common ground. After a short standstill agreement, both sides felt a settlement might be possible. We negotiated in good faith, and our strong cash position gave us leverage.

1. How did our team open the negotiation?
A With a direct order: "Accept our terms."
B With a polite proposal using "could".
C By refusing to talk.
2. Why are "could" and "would" used here?
A Because the actions are in the past.
B Because they make proposals and requests sound polite and tentative.
C Because they are questions.
3. What gave our team an advantage in the negotiation?
A Their strong cash position (leverage).
B They refused every counteroffer.
C They had no money at all.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use could / would / might to sound polite and tentative in a negotiation. Eles deixam a proposta menos agressiva e mais profissional. Sempre vêm seguidos do verbo base (sem "to", sem -s, sem -ed).

ModalUseExample
couldsoft proposal / suggestion"We could offer a longer timeline."
wouldpolite request / hypothetical"Would you accept a haircut?"
mighttentative possibility"That might work for us."

Common Mistake:

"We could to offer a longer timeline."

"We could offer a longer timeline."

Modais (could/would/might) são sempre seguidos do verbo base. Nunca use "to" depois do modal.
