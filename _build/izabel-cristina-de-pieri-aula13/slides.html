Chapter 1: The Proposal

From Describing to Deciding

Last class you described who is who at AVRT: the engineer who leads R&D, the market, which is growing fast. That was relative clauses. But describing the company is not the same as deciding its next move. Today a proposal lands on your desk — a possible merger with a small sensor startup. You have to weigh it and say what AVRT will do. Welcome to the language of decisions: decide to move, consider merging, it is worth exploring.

Chapter 1: The Proposal

Three Business Decisions

Chapter 3

The Code

Verb patterns: to-infinitive vs gerund (-ing)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We decided to expand into a new market."

"The board suggested partnering with a local supplier."

"It is worth doing proper due diligence first."

"We cannot afford to ignore the downside."

PatternCommon verbsExample
verb + to + basedecide, agree, plan, manage, afford, hope, offer, refuse, aim, promiseWe decided to expand.
verb + -ingconsider, suggest, recommend, avoid, delay, postpone, risk, keep, mindWe considered opening a subsidiary.
it is worth + -ingworth, no point (in), spend timeIt is worth investing in the pitch.
preposition + -inglook forward to, interested in, committed to, before / afterWe look forward to meeting them.

The first verb decides the pattern. After a preposition — including the "to" in "look forward to" or "committed to" — always use the -ing form.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We decided expanding into Mexico."

✓ RIGHT

"We decided to expand into Mexico."

✗ WRONG

"It is worth to invest in the pitch."

✓ RIGHT

"It is worth investing in the pitch."

✗ WRONG

"We look forward to receive the term sheet."

✓ RIGHT

"We look forward to receiving the term sheet."

After decide, agree and plan, use "to" + base. After suggest, consider and "it is worth", use the -ing form. In "look forward to" the "to" is a preposition, so it also takes -ing.

Chapter 4

In the Room

A proposal to acquire a small sensor startup

Dialogue

The Proposal

A
Izabel, I want to put a proposal on the table. I suggest acquiring Nordic Sense, the small sensor startup in Campinas.
I
Interesting. What is the rationale? Before we decide to move, I need to understand the upside and the downside.
A
The upside is clear: their proprietary tech would let us expand faster. The board is keen on green-lighting a merger this year.
I
I agree it is worth exploring. But I refuse to rush. We cannot afford to skip due diligence, and I want a proper valuation before committing.
A
Fair. Do you have a ballpark in mind for what we would pay?
I
Not yet. I would avoid naming a number before the audit. What matters now is getting buy-in from the other directors.
A
So what do you suggest doing first?
I
I propose preparing a short pitch, then meeting the founders. If the numbers hold, I am happy to recommend green-lighting it. Let us aim to decide by the end of the quarter.
