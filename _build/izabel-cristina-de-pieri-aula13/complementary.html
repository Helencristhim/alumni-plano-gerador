Aula 13 -- The Proposal -- Deciding What to Do with Verb Patterns

Film
Moneyball (2011)

Billy Beane puts an unconventional proposal on the table: rebuild the team with data, not instinct. The whole film is the language of Lesson 13 — deciding to change, refusing to follow the old way, considering trades, avoiding the safe option, and choosing to commit to a plan. It is a masterclass in weighing a bold proposal, with a clear rationale, upside and downside.

Tip: watch the scene where Beane pitches his idea. Write down five verb patterns you hear or could use ("he decided to...", "they suggested trading...", "it is worth taking the risk"). Then rewrite one about a decision at AVRT.

"Verb patterns -- 6 Minute Grammar" -- BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on exactly the code from Lesson 13: when a verb is followed by the -ing form and when it takes "to" + base, and what to do when a verb can take either. It walks through the common groups — decide, agree and plan with "to"; suggest, avoid and consider with -ing.
