Aula 13 -- Pre-class

The Proposal -- Deciding What to Do with Verb Patterns

Weighing a business proposal — a possible merger with a small sensor startup. Key words: proposal, rationale, pitch, merger, buy-in, due diligence, valuation, upside, downside, green-light, ballpark, commitment. Structure: gerund vs infinitive (verb patterns) -- verb + to (decide to, agree to, afford to), verb + -ing (consider, suggest, avoid, recommend), it is worth + -ing, and preposition + -ing (look forward to doing).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Proposal -- a formal plan or offer put forward for consideration (proposta)
"Andy put a clear proposal on the table: to acquire Nordic Sense."
Rationale -- the reasons and logic behind a decision (justificativa / lógica)
"The rationale for the merger is faster access to the medical market."
Pitch -- a short, persuasive presentation of an idea or offer (apresentação persuasiva)
"We decided to prepare a short pitch before meeting the founders."
Merger -- the combining of two companies into one (fusão)
"The board is keen on green-lighting a merger this year."
Buy-in -- agreement and support from the people involved (adesão / apoio)
"What matters now is getting buy-in from the other directors."
Due diligence -- careful investigation of a deal before you commit (auditoria prévia / diligência)
"We cannot afford to skip due diligence on their numbers."
Valuation -- an estimate of how much a company is worth (avaliação de valor)
"I want an independent valuation before committing to anything."
Upside -- the potential benefit or gain (potencial de ganho / vantagem)
"The upside is expanding into a new market a year faster."
Downside -- the potential risk or disadvantage (risco / desvantagem)
"The downside is the cost of merging two engineering teams."
Green-light -- to give official approval to go ahead (aprovar / dar sinal verde)
"If the numbers hold, I am happy to green-light the deal."
Ballpark -- a rough, approximate figure (estimativa aproximada)
"I would avoid naming a ballpark before the audit is finished."
Commitment -- a firm decision to do or support something (compromisso)
"A merger is a long commitment, so we should not rush it."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andy puts a proposal on the table. "I suggest acquiring Nordic Sense," he says. Izabel listens. "Before we decide to move," she answers, "I need the rationale." The upside is clear: the merger would let AVRT expand faster. But Izabel refuses to rush. "We cannot afford to skip due diligence, and it is worth getting an independent valuation first." Andy asks for a ballpark. She avoids naming a number and focuses on buy-in from the directors. "I propose preparing a short pitch, then meeting the founders," she says. "We look forward to hearing their side. If the numbers hold, I am happy to recommend green-lighting it."

1. "I suggest acquiring Nordic Sense." Why "acquiring" and not "to acquire"?
A Because "suggest" is followed by the -ing form (gerund).
B Because it is a question.
C Because "acquire" has no infinitive.
2. "We cannot afford to skip due diligence." Which pattern is this?
A verb + -ing.
B verb + to-infinitive (afford + to + base).
C preposition + -ing.
3. "We look forward to hearing their side." Why "hearing"?
A Here "to" is a preposition, so it takes the -ing form.
B Because "hear" is irregular.
C Because the sentence is in the past.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Verb Patterns (Gerund vs Infinitive)

GRAMMAR

Choosing between "to do" and "doing" (explicação em inglês e português).

Pattern / PadrãoCommon verbs / VerbosExample
verb + to + base
verbo + to + base		decide, agree, plan, manage, afford, hope, offer, refuse, aim, promiseWe decided to expand.
verb + -ing
verbo + gerúndio		consider, suggest, recommend, avoid, delay, postpone, risk, keep, mindWe considered opening a subsidiary.
it is worth + -ingworth, no point (in), spend timeIt is worth investing in the pitch.
preposition + -ing
preposição + gerúndio		look forward to, interested in, committed to, before / afterWe look forward to meeting them.
Cuidado / Watch outO primeiro verbo decide o padrão. Depois de decide/agree/plan/afford use "to" + base. Depois de suggest/consider/avoid e depois de "it is worth" use -ing. Em "look forward to" e "committed to" o "to" é preposição → + -ing. Errado: "decided expanding", "worth to invest", "look forward to receive".
