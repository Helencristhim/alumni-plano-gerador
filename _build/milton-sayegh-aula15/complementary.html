Lesson 15 -- Handling Problems After Delivery

Series / Film
The Crown -- any episode where an advisor reports a problem to the Queen

A masterclass in handling bad news with composure: advisors acknowledge a problem, reassure, and relay exactly what someone said. Listen for the reported speech ("He said that...", "They told her...") as crises are escalated and resolved.

Tip: each time a character relays what someone said, pause and notice the backshift -- present to past, will to would.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- any episode on "complaints", "customer service" or "problem solving"

Practical business English for exactly this stage: acknowledging an issue, reassuring a client, escalating it, and offering a fix. The host models the phrases of apology and resolution, and reports back what the customer said.

Tip: note one line where a complaint is relayed, then say it back in reported speech ("She told me that...").

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Reported speech: say and tell"

A short, clear lesson on exactly the grammar from this class: how the verb shifts back in time when you report what someone said, and the difference between say and tell. Native speakers model it in real conversations.

Tip: after watching, report a recent message from a colleague in five sentences, backshifting each verb and using both say and tell.
