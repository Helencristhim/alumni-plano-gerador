Handling Problems After Delivery

When something goes wrong after the goods arrive, the relationship is on the line. Acknowledge the issue, reassure the client, escalate it to the right person, sort out a discrepancy, reimburse and reinstate, and protect the goodwill you built. New words: acknowledge, reassure, escalate, discrepancy, reimburse, reinstate, damaged in transit, liability, goodwill, outstanding. New structure: reported speech -- relay what a client said to your team (she said the box was damaged; he told me he would reimburse us). PT: quando algo da errado apos a entrega, salve a relacao -- admita, tranquilize, escale e conserte -- e relate ao time com reported speech.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of handling a problem after delivery. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario de lidar com um problema depois da entrega.

Acknowledge -- reconhecer / admitir
to admit openly that something is true, often a problem
"I acknowledge the box arrived damaged, and I'm sorry."
Reassure -- tranquilizar / dar seguranca
to say something that makes a worried person feel calm and confident
"Let me reassure you: we'll make this right quickly."
Escalate -- escalar / encaminhar a um superior
to pass a problem to a more senior person so it gets solved faster
"I'll escalate this to me directly, not the queue."
Discrepancy -- divergencia / inconsistencia
a difference between two things that should match, like an order and a delivery
"There's a discrepancy between the order and the shipment."
Reimburse -- ressarcir / reembolsar
to pay someone back for money they lost or spent
"We'll reimburse you for the two damaged pieces."
Reinstate -- repor / restabelecer
to bring back or restore something that was removed or missing
"We'll reinstate the missing bracelet on the next shipment."
Damaged in transit -- danificado no transporte
harmed while being transported from the seller to the buyer
"Two boxes were damaged in transit, not at our workshop."
Liability -- responsabilidade (legal)
legal responsibility for a problem, cost, or harm
"Once it ships, the liability for transit damage is the carrier's."
Goodwill -- boa vontade / gesto de cortesia
kind feeling and trust between people, often shown by a generous gesture
"We'll cover the cost as a goodwill gesture."
Outstanding -- pendente / nao resolvido
still unresolved or not yet dealt with
"While the issue is outstanding, I'll update you daily."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read how Milton reports the problem to his team, then answer. / Leia como o Milton relata o problema ao time e responda.

Karen called with a problem, so Milton briefed his team. He told them that two boxes were damaged in transit and that one bracelet was missing. She said there was a clear discrepancy between the order and what arrived, and that she had checked the paperwork twice. Milton explained that he would acknowledge the issue honestly and reassure her, then escalate it to himself. He promised her he would reimburse the damaged pieces and reinstate the missing one, covering it as a goodwill gesture while it was outstanding. The liability for transit damage, he noted, sat with the carrier.

1. Karen said: "The bracelet is missing." How does Milton report it?
A He said the bracelet is missing.
B He said the bracelet was missing. (present → past)
C He said the bracelet will be missing.
2. Which sentence uses "tell" correctly?
A She told that there was a discrepancy.
B She told me there was a discrepancy. (tell + a person)
C She said me there was a discrepancy.
3. Karen said: "I will send photos." How does Milton report it?
A She said she will send photos.
B She said she would send photos. (will → would)
C She said she sends photos.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech (say / tell + backshift)

GRAMMAR

When you relay what someone said, move the verb one step back in time, and choose say or tell.

Direct speechReported speechExample
present (is / are)past (was / were)"It's damaged." → She said it was damaged.
past simple (arrived)past perfect (had arrived)"It arrived broken." → She said it had arrived broken.
will / canwould / could"I'll send photos." → She said she would send photos.
say vs tell: use tell + a person (she told me) and say without one (she said). PT: ao relatar a fala de alguem, volte o verbo um passo no tempo -- presente vira passado, passado simples vira past perfect, will vira would, can vira could. Use "tell" com a pessoa (she told me) e "say" sem ela (she said). Nao diga "say me".
