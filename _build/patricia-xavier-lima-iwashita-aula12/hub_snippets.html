Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Feature -- a specific function or tool inside a product (recurso / funcionalidade)
"Each feature has a clear scope."
Roadmap -- a plan that shows what will be built and when (planejamento / mapa do produto)
"This roadmap shows what we are going to build."
Release -- a new version of the product you make available (versão / lançamento)
"This release includes two enhancements."
Enhancement -- an improvement to a feature that already exists (melhoria)
"Two enhancements that customers requested."
Request -- something a customer asks the team to build or change (pedido / solicitação)
"We deliver the features you have requested."
Launch -- to make a product or feature available to users (lançar)
"We are launching the mobile app on Tuesday."
Deliver -- to finish and hand over what you promised (entregar)
"Our goal is to deliver the features you asked for."
Backlog -- the list of requests and work still waiting to be done (fila de pendências)
"The rest stay on our backlog for a future release."
Prioritize -- to decide what to do first, by importance (priorizar)
"We prioritize the requests with the biggest impact."
Estimate -- a rough guess of how long or how much something will take (estimativa)
"We are still working on the estimate."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When a customer asks what is coming, Patrícia explains the plan. In June, the team is going to release version three of the dashboard. She tells the customer the new reports will save them time. They are launching the mobile app on Tuesday the fifteenth, so the date is already fixed. One feature is still on the backlog, so Patrícia is honest: she will not promise a date yet, but the team is going to review it with the customer next quarter.

1. "They are launching the app on Tuesday the fifteenth." Por que usamos o present continuous aqui?
A Porque a ação está acontecendo agora, neste momento.
B Porque é um arranjo futuro JÁ marcado, com data fixa.
C Porque present continuous só existe no passado.
2. Para um plano JÁ decidido (lançar a v3 em junho), qual forma é a mais natural?
A "is going to release" (plano decidido).
B "releases" (presente simples).
C "released" (passado).
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "We going to deliver it in June."
B "We are going to deliver it in June."
C "We will to deliver it in June."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Forms

GRAMMAR

Como falar de planos com três formas de futuro (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
will + base verbprevisão, promessa ou oferta. Depois de "will", verbo no infinitivo SEM "to".It will save time.
be going to + base verbplano ou intenção JÁ decidida. Sempre com am / is / are antes.We are going to release v3.
am / is / are + verb-ingarranjo fixo, com hora ou data marcada (present continuous).We are launching on Tuesday.
Negative / Question"I will not promise a date." / "Are you going to send it?"will not + verbo; Are you going to...?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

A customer asks what is coming next quarter. You share a plan you have already decided. You say:
A "We are going to release version three."
B "We released version three."
C "We will to release version three."
You give a fixed launch date on the calendar. You say:
A "We will launching the app on Tuesday."
B "We are launching the app on Tuesday the fifteenth."
C "We launch maybe the app on Tuesday."
A customer asks for a feature you have not built yet. You are honest. You say:
A "We already delivered it last year."
B "It is on the backlog; we are still working on the estimate."
C "We will to build it right now."
You make a confident prediction about a benefit of the new app. You say:
A "The new app will save your agents time."
B "The new app saving time."
C "The new app going to saves time."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection
