Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Signposting language: sequencing a presentation

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Where do the orange words go in each point?

"First, I will give you an overview of the harvest." (open point 1)

"Then, let us move on to the prices." (open point 2)

"After that, I will walk you through this chart." (open point 3)

"Finally, let me sum up the key takeaway." (close the talk)

Grammar Rule

The Signposting Map

StageFunctionSignpost
Opengive the outline"I have divided my talk into three parts."
Point 1start the sequence"First, I will give you an overview."
Move onchange point"Then / After that, let us move on to..."
Closesum up & open Q&A"Finally, to sum up... Any questions?"

Key idea: signposts are road signs for the audience. Open with the outline, start with first, change points with then or after that, and close with finally before you sum up. The audience always knows where you are.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Now prices. Now the harvest. And quality too."
"First, the harvest. Then, the prices. After that, the quality."

Use a signpost at the start of every point. Never jump between ideas without one.

Grammar Practice
