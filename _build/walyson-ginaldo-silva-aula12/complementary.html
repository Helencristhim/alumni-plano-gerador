Lesson 12 -- Making Presentations

TED Talk
TED -- a short business or data talk (under 6 minutes)

Watch a speaker open with a clear outline and move through their points. Notice how they signpost with "first", "then" and "finally", and how they sum up the key takeaway at the end.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three signposts the speaker uses and the one key takeaway you remember.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "Signposting Language for Clear Presentations"

A practical episode on structuring a talk: giving the outline, moving on between points, walking the audience through a visual aid, and summing up the key takeaway.

Record a 5-minute briefing: open with the outline, signpost three points with first, then and finally, and sum up your message.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Signposting language in presentations"

A focused lesson on the phrases that guide an audience through a talk: opening with an outline, sequencing points, and closing before the Q and A. It directly reinforces today's language.

After watching, write six sentences: one outline, three signposted points (first, then, finally), one to walk through a visual aid, and one to sum up.
