Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Outline -- a short plan that lists the main parts of a talk
"Let me give you the outline of my talk." (Estrutura / Roteiro)
Audience -- the group of people who listen to your talk
"I want to engage the audience from the start." (Plateia / Público)
Signpost -- a phrase that shows where the talk is going
"First, then and finally are signposts." (Sinalizador / Marcador)
Slide deck -- the full set of slides you use in a talk
"My slide deck has only five slides." (Conjunto de slides)
Walk through -- to explain something step by step
"Let me walk you through this chart." (Explicar passo a passo)
Move on -- to leave one point and go to the next
"Let us move on to the prices." (Seguir em frente / Passar para)
Emphasize -- to make a point stronger so people remember it
"I want to emphasize the strong harvest." (Enfatizar / Destacar)
Visual aid -- a chart or image that helps the audience
"This chart is my main visual aid." (Recurso visual)
Sum up -- to repeat the main message shortly at the end
"Let me sum up the three points." (Resumir)
Key takeaway -- the one main idea you want people to remember
"The key takeaway is the strong harvest." (Mensagem principal)
Handout -- a paper with information you give to the audience
"There is a handout with the numbers." (Material impresso)
Q and A -- the questions and answers part after a talk
"We will have a short Q and A at the end." (Sessão de perguntas)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson prepares a short briefing for the trading team. He starts with the outline, so the audience knows his plan: three points in five minutes. First, he presents the harvest and uses a clear signpost before each point. Then, he says "let us move on to the prices" and uses one chart as his main visual aid. He decides to walk the team through the numbers slowly and to emphasize the strong volume. Finally, he wants to sum up the key takeaway in one sentence and open a short Q and A. His goal is simple: a clear talk where the audience always knows where he is.

1. Why does Walyson start with the outline?
A So the audience knows his plan from the start.
B To make the talk longer.
C To surprise the audience at the end.
2. How does Walyson move between his points?
A He jumps with no warning.
B He uses signposts like first, then and finally.
C He reads every slide word by word.
3. What does Walyson do at the end?
A He stops with no conclusion.
C He starts a new presentation.
B He sums up the key takeaway and opens a Q and A.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signposting language for presentations

GRAMMAR

Usamos sinalizadores (signposts) para guiar a plateia por cada parte da apresentação. / We use signposts to guide the audience through each part of the presentation.

Stage / EtapaPhrase / FraseUse / Uso
OpenI have divided my talk into three parts.dar a estrutura (outline)
StartFirst, I will give you an overview.abrir o primeiro ponto
Move onThen / After that, let us move on to...mudar de ponto
CloseFinally, to sum up... Any questions?resumir e abrir o Q and A

Key point / Ponto-chave: os signposts são placas de sinalização para a plateia. Abra com o outline, comece com first, mude de ponto com then ou after that, e feche com finally antes de sum up. Assim a plateia sempre sabe onde você está. / Signposts are road signs for the audience. Open with the outline, start with first, change points with then or after that, and close with finally before you sum up.
