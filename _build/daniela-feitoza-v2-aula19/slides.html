Chapter 3

The Invitation Code

How to invite, accept, and decline politely

Invitation Discovery

Listen and Notice

Would you like to grab dinner with us tonight?

How about dinner at that new place?

I'd love to! That sounds great.

I'd love to, but I have an early flight.

The first two invite. The third accepts. The last says no — but kindly. What is the pattern?

Invitation Code

Invite, Accept, Decline

MoveStructureExample
InviteWould you like to + base verb"Would you like to join us?"
Invite (casual)How about + noun / verb-ing"How about dinner?" / "How about grabbing a bite?"
AcceptI'd love to / That sounds great"I'd love to! That sounds great."
Decline"I'd love to, but + short reason." The "but + reason" makes a no kind, not cold. Never just say "No."

Would you like to and Do you want to take to + base verb. How about takes a noun or verb-ing (never "to").

Common Mistake

Cold vs Warm

✗ WRONG

"How about to grab dinner tonight?"

✓ RIGHT

"How about grabbing dinner tonight?" (How about + -ing)

✗ TOO COLD

"No." (just no, no reason)

✓ WARM

"I'd love to, but I have an early flight." (but + reason)

"Would you like to" takes "to + verb". "How about" takes a noun or "-ing". A no always needs a kind reason.

I'd love that. I'll see you at seven thirty in the lobby, then.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Greg invite Daniela to, and how?
He invites her to dinner with the team, asking "Would you like to join us?"
2. Where is the venue, and when do they meet?
A nice Brazilian place near the hotel; they meet in the lobby at seven thirty.
3. Who is paying for the first round, and why does Greg want to go?
Greg is paying — he says "I'll treat everyone." He wants to catch up properly, away from the conference rooms.
