Warm-up — Callback da Aula 18

Review

Na Aula 18 você aprendeu a ligar uma condição a um resultado com os condicionais: first conditional (if + presente, will) para planos reais e second conditional (if + passado, would) para cenários imaginados. Use isso agora socialmente: se você for ao jantar da conferência, o que você vai dizer? ("If you go to the conference dinner, what will you say?") E se você fosse mais à vontade em inglês, o que você faria à mesa? Hoje a conferência acabou e começa a parte mais humana: convidar, aceitar e recusar com gentileza, e a linguagem de restaurante. Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Invitation -- when you ask someone to come to an event or join you (PT: convite)
"Thank you for the invitation to dinner."
Grab -- to get or eat something quickly and casually (PT: pegar / comer algo de forma casual)
"Would you like to grab dinner after the talk?"
Venue -- the place where an event, dinner or meeting happens (PT: local / lugar do evento)
"They found a nice venue near the hotel."
Cuisine -- the style of cooking or food of a country or region (PT: culinária / cozinha)
"You should try the local cuisine — it is excellent."
Recommend -- to suggest something because you think it is good (PT: recomendar)
"I recommend the fish — it is the best dish here."
Treat -- to pay for someone's food or drink as a kind gesture (PT: pagar para alguém / oferecer)
"Please, let me treat you tonight — it is on me."
Toast -- to raise your glass and say a few nice words together (PT: brinde / brindar)
"Let's make a toast — to a great conference!"
Catch up -- to talk and share news with someone after time apart (PT: colocar a conversa em dia)
"It would be great to catch up over dinner."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After the conference, the social language begins. To invite someone, there are three easy ways. Would you like to join us? and Do you want to grab dinner? both take to + base verb. How about is different: it takes a noun or a verb + -ing — "How about dinner?" or "How about grabbing a bite?" — never "to". To accept, say "I'd love to!" or "That sounds great." And to say no, never just say "No." Soften it: "I'd love to, but I have an early flight." The "but" plus a short reason makes a no kind, not cold.

1. Which form is correct after "How about"?
A "How about to grab a coffee?"
B "How about grabbing a coffee?"
C "How about grab a coffee?"
2. What is the best way to say no to an invitation?
A Just say "No."
B "I'd love to, but I have an early flight."
C "No, I will not."
3. Which invitation uses the correct structure?
A "Would you like join us?"
B "Would you like to join us?"
C "Would you like joining us?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Inviting, Accepting & Declining

GRAMMAR

Como convidar, aceitar e recusar com gentileza (bilingue EN + PT).

MovimentoStructureExample
InviteWould you like to / Do you want to + verbo base"Would you like to join us?"
Invite (casual)How about + substantivo / verbo-ing"How about dinner?" / "How about grabbing a bite?"
Accept / DeclineI'd love to / I'd love to, but + razão"I'd love to!" / "I'd love to, but I have an early flight."
Regra (PT)Use Would you like to e Do you want to com to + verbo base. How about leva substantivo ou verbo + -ing (NUNCA "to"). Para aceitar: I'd love to / That sounds great. Para recusar, NUNCA diga só "No": diga I'd love to, but... + uma razão curta — isso deixa o não gentil, não frio.
