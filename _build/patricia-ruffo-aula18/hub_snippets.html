Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Facilitate -- to help a group have a good discussion and reach decisions
"As the chair, I facilitate the meeting so everyone can speak." (Facilitar / conduzir)
Objective -- the clear goal or aim of a meeting or discussion
"The objective today is to agree on the next steps." (Objetivo / meta)
Contribution -- an idea or comment a person adds to a discussion
"Thank you, Maria -- that is a very useful contribution." (Contribuição)
Redirect -- to bring a discussion back to the topic when it goes off track
"Let me redirect us back to the main objective." (Redirecionar / retomar)
Allocate -- to give a task or resource to a specific person
"Let's allocate the literature review to David." (Atribuir / designar)
Action point -- a specific task someone must do after the meeting
"The first action point is to recruit ten more patients." (Item de ação / tarefa)
Minutes -- the official written notes of what was decided in a meeting
"Could you take the minutes for today's discussion?" (Ata da reunião)
Wrap up -- to bring a meeting to a close by summarizing the decisions
"Let's wrap up: here are our three action points." (Encerrar / finalizar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Patricia is chairing a research meeting at Abbott. She opens clearly: "Let's start. The objective today is to agree on the next steps." As the chair, she facilitates the discussion instead of doing all the talking. She invites each person by name, so even the quieter colleagues make a contribution. When David goes off topic, she redirects the group politely: "That's a fair point, but let's come back to that later." As decisions are made, she allocates each action point to a specific person, with a deadline. Finally, she wraps up: she summarizes the decisions and confirms that someone is taking the minutes. A good chair turns a long meeting into a short list of clear tasks.

1. How does Patricia open the meeting?
A By doing all the talking herself.
B By stating the objective clearly.
C By ending the meeting at once.
2. What does Patricia do when David goes off topic?
A She redirects the group politely.
B She tells him he is wrong.
C She ignores everyone.
3. What does Patricia do at the end of the meeting?
A She leaves without a summary.
C She starts a new study.
B She wraps up and confirms the minutes.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Chairing a discussion

GRAMMAR

Para liderar uma discussão, usamos frases fixas para cada função: abrir, convidar, redirecionar e encerrar. / To lead a discussion, we use fixed phrases for each function: open, invite, redirect and close.

Function / FunçãoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Open the meetingLet's start by + -ing"Let's start by looking at the numbers."
Invite a contributionWhat are your thoughts on...?"What are your thoughts on the sample?"
Redirect politelyacknowledge + Let's come back"Fair point, but let's come back to that."
Wrap upTo sum up,..."To sum up, we have three action points."

Key point / Ponto-chave: O bom facilitador tem uma frase pronta para cada função: abra com o objective, convide cada pessoa pelo nome, redirecione com educação (reconheça e depois retome), atribua cada action point e encerre com um resumo claro. Rejeitar uma ideia de forma seca (That's wrong) faz as pessoas pararem de contribuir.
