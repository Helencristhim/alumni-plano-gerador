Chairing Phrases

Open, Invite, Redirect, Close

The phrases that carry a discussion from start to finish

Open: "Let's start. The objective today is to agree on the next steps."

Invite: "Maria, what are your thoughts on the sample size?"

Redirect: "That's interesting, but let's come back to that later."

Close: "Let's wrap up the discussion now."

Grammar Rule

The Chair's Toolkit

FunctionExample
Open the meetingLet's start by looking at the numbers.
Invite a contributionWhat are your thoughts on the sample size?
Redirect politelyLet's come back to that. Can we move on?
Allocate a taskDavid, can you take the action point on recruitment?
Wrap upTo sum up, we have three action points.

Key idea: A good chair has a fixed phrase for each function -- open with the objective, invite each person by name, redirect off-topic comments politely, allocate every action point, and wrap up with a clear summary.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"That's wrong. Next topic, please."
"That's a fair point, but let's come back to it later."

To redirect, first acknowledge the comment, then bring the group back. A blunt rejection stops people from contributing.

Situational Quiz

Choose the Best Line

1. A colleague goes off topic and you want to stay polite.

"That's a fair point, but let's come back to that later." (redirect), not "That's wrong, next!"

2. You want a task to actually get done after the meeting.

Allocate the action point to a specific person with a deadline.

3. You are closing the meeting and want everyone to be clear.

"To sum up, we have three action points, and I'll take the minutes."
