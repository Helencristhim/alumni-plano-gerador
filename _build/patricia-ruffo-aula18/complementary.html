Lesson 18 -- Leading a Research Discussion

Series
The West Wing -- any senior staff meeting scene

Watch how the chair opens a meeting, invites contributions, keeps the discussion on track and closes with clear next steps. Notice the phrases used to redirect people politely.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one phrase used to open the meeting, one to invite an opinion, and one to bring the group back on track.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Chairing a Meeting in English"

A focused episode on the language of facilitation: opening the meeting, inviting contributions, redirecting off-topic talk, allocating action points and wrapping up.

Record yourself chairing a 1-minute meeting: open with the objective, invite one contribution, and wrap up with an action point.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "English for meetings: leading and chairing"

A clear lesson on the phrases a chair uses to open, invite, redirect and close a discussion, with realistic meeting-room examples.

After watching, write the three action points from an imaginary meeting and say who each one is allocated to.
