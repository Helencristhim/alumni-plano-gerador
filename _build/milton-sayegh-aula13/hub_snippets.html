Closing the Deal -- Negotiating Price & Terms

Negotiate price and terms like a pro: ballpark figures, trade-offs, win-win deals, leverage and closing. New words: negotiate, ballpark, win-win, trade-off, leverage, deadlock, bargain, markup, installment, settle. New structure: polite and indirect questions -- "Could you tell me...", "Do you know...", "Would you mind + -ing", "I was wondering if..." -- with statement word order and no "do/does/did" inside. PT: negociar preco e condicoes -- estimativa, troca, acordo vantajoso e fechamento -- pedindo o que voce quer com perguntas polidas e indiretas.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of the negotiating table. Tap Listen to hear each word. / O vocabulario da mesa de negociacao.

Negotiate -- negociar
to discuss the terms of a deal until both sides reach an agreement
"We still need to negotiate the price for the spring order."
Ballpark -- estimativa aproximada / faixa de preco
an approximate figure or price range, not the exact number yet
"Could you give me a ballpark for the spring order?"
Win-win -- vantajoso para os dois lados
a deal in which both sides gain something and leave satisfied
"A smaller discount but a bigger order — that's win-win."
Trade-off -- troca / contrapartida
when you give up one thing to get another in return
"A longer deadline for a better price is a fair trade-off."
Leverage -- vantagem / poder de barganha
an advantage that gives you power in a negotiation
"A strong waiting list gives us leverage on price."
Deadlock -- impasse
a point where neither side will move and nothing progresses
"We hit a deadlock over the discount, so we paused for a day."
Bargain -- pechincha / negociar preco
a purchase at a good price; also, to discuss a price to make it lower
"At that volume, the necklace is a real bargain for them."
Markup -- margem / acrescimo sobre o custo
the amount added to the cost of a product to set its selling price
"Luxury retail carries a high markup from factory to shelf."
Installment -- parcela / pagamento parcelado
one of several equal payments made over time instead of all at once
"They paid in three installments over ninety days."
Settle -- fechar / chegar a um acordo
to reach a final agreement and end the negotiation
"I think we can settle this today and both walk away happy."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the negotiation and answer the questions. / Leia a negociacao e responda.

A buyer wanted to negotiate the price on Milton's collection. "Your markup is high for our floor," she said, and made a counteroffer of twelve percent off. Milton asked softly, "Could you tell me what volume you can commit to?" — not "what volume can you commit". He had some leverage, since the pieces were a real bargain at that quality, so he replied, "I was wondering if eight percent would work." To avoid a deadlock, they found a compromise: ten percent, with one concession on payment — three installments. "Would you mind settling the rest on delivery?" he asked. They agreed and decided to settle the deal that day.

1. Why does Milton say "Could you tell me what volume you can commit to?" (not "what volume can you commit")?
A Inside an indirect question, you keep statement order (subject + verb).
B Both versions are wrong.
C "Could you tell me" must be followed by inversion.
2. What follows "Would you mind"?
A The base verb with "to" (mind to settle).
B A verb + -ing (mind settling).
C A noun only, never a verb.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Do you know when do they decide?"
B "Do you know when they decide?"
C "I was wondering you could accept installments."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite & Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Soften a question with an opener, then keep statement order with no "do/does/did".

OpenerWhat followsExample
Could you tell me... / Do you know...subject + verb (statement order)"Could you tell me what the markup is?"
Would you mind...verb + -ing"Would you mind lowering the price a little?"
I was wondering if...subject + could / would + base"I was wondering if you could accept installments."
Watch the traps: inside an indirect question, keep statement order and drop "do/does/did": "Do you know when they decide?" (NOT "when do they decide"). "Could you tell me what the price is?" (NOT "what is the price"). After "Would you mind", use -ing (mind lowering, not mind to lower). After "I was wondering", you need "if". PT: amacie a pergunta com um inicio polido ("Could you tell me...", "I was wondering if...") e mantenha a ORDEM DE FRASE AFIRMATIVA dentro (sujeito + verbo), sem "do/does/did" e sem inversao. Depois de "Would you mind", use o verbo com -ING.
