Lesson 07 -- Pre-class

Harvard, Stanford and Beyond -- Speaking Up in Executive Education

Everything you need to describe, apply to, and speak up in a top executive-education program. The grammar focus is the passive voice in professional and academic contexts -- "The program is taught by leading faculty", "Applicants are evaluated on their record", "Cases are discussed in small groups", "The capstone is presented at the end". New words: cohort, faculty, curriculum, case method, keynote, peers, admissions, capstone, tuition, accreditation, immersion, thought leadership.

Vocabulary

Cohort -- a group who begin and go through a program together / turma, grupo que faz o programa junto
"My cohort had sixty executives from twenty countries."
Faculty -- the teaching staff of a university or program / corpo docente, os professores
"The faculty came from both the medical and business schools."
Curriculum -- the full set of courses and content in a program / currículo, conjunto de matérias
"The curriculum covers strategy, finance, and leadership."
Case method -- learning by analyzing real business cases in class / método do caso, ensino por casos reais
"With the case method, a real story is discussed every day."
Keynote -- the main speech at a conference or event / palestra principal
"The keynote was given by a Nobel Prize winner."
Peers -- people at your own level; fellow participants / colegas, pares
"I learned as much from my peers as from the faculty."
Admissions -- the process of selecting and accepting students / admissões, processo seletivo
"Admissions look at leadership, not only at test scores."
Capstone -- a final project that brings the whole program together / projeto final, trabalho de conclusão
"My capstone was a plan for my new fertility clinic."
Tuition -- the money you pay to attend a course or program / mensalidade, valor do curso
"Tuition is high, but scholarships are offered."
Accreditation -- official recognition that a program meets high standards / credenciamento, acreditação
"The school holds international accreditation."
Immersion -- deep, full-time involvement in a learning experience / imersão
"The immersion week is intense but unforgettable."
Thought leadership -- being seen as an expert whose ideas lead a field / liderança de ideias, referência no setor
"Publishing builds your thought leadership in medicine."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is applying to an executive-education program abroad. On the website, everything is written in the passive voice. The curriculum is taught by leading faculty from the medical and business schools. Applicants are evaluated on their leadership record, so her work as a physician and a founder is a real strength. Most of the learning is done through the case method: real cases are discussed by the whole cohort, and every voice is expected to be heard. Each participant is placed in a group of sixty peers, and lifelong connections are formed during the immersion. At the end, a capstone project is presented to the cohort and is reviewed by a panel. The school's accreditation is international, and although the tuition is high, scholarships are offered.

1. "The curriculum is taught by leading faculty." Why the passive here?
A Because the curriculum is doing the action.
B Because the focus is the curriculum (the action received), not who teaches.
C Because it happened long ago.
2. "Applicants are evaluated on their record." Which is the correct structure?
A subject + are + past participle (be + evaluated).
B subject + evaluate (no "be").
C subject + will evaluate.
3. Which sentence is passive?
A "The cohort discusses the cases."
B "The cases are discussed by the cohort."
C "The cohort will discuss the cases."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

The passive voice puts the focus on the action, not on who does it. Form it with a form of "be" plus the past participle. Add "by ..." only when the doer matters. / A voz passiva foca na ação, não em quem a faz. Forma-se com o verbo "be" mais o particípio passado. Use "by ..." só quando importa quem fez.

FormUseExample
is / are + past participlePresent passive; the action matters most / Passiva no presente; a ação importa maisThe program is taught.
was / were + past participlePast passive / Passiva no passadoThe keynote was given.
... by + doerAdd "by" only if the doer is important / Use "by" só se quem faz importadiscussed by the cohort
Common errorNever drop "be" or use the base verb / Nunca tire o "be" nem use o verbo base: NOT "Applicants evaluate on..." → "Applicants are evaluated on...". NOT "was write" → "was written".
