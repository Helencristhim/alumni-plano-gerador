Aula 15 -- Pre-class

In the Meeting Room -- Starting and Sharing Opinions

Lead a short meeting. Open it (Let's start), read the agenda, ask for opinions (What do you think?), share yours (In my opinion), and agree on a decision. New vocabulary: meeting, agenda, opinion, idea, agree, disagree, point, decision.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Meeting -- when people talk together about work
"The meeting starts at ten." (Reunião)
Agenda -- the list of points to talk about
"The agenda has two points." (Pauta)
Opinion -- what you think about something
"In my opinion, it is a good plan." (Opinião)
Idea -- a new thought or plan you want to share
"I have a good idea for the project." (Ideia)
Agree -- to think the same as another person
"I agree with your plan." (Concordar)
Disagree -- to think a different thing, in a polite way
"I respectfully disagree." (Discordar)
Point -- one topic on the agenda
"The first point is the aircraft." (Ponto / Tópico)
Decision -- the choice you make at the end
"We make a decision together." (Decisão)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Karina opened the meeting and said, "Let's start. The agenda has two points." The first point was the new aircraft. She asked Robert, "What do you think?" Robert answered, "In my opinion, it is a very good idea." Karina said, "I agree with you," and they made a decision together.

1. How does Karina open the meeting?
A "Let's start. The agenda has two points."
B "Goodbye, see you later."
C "Please hold the line."
2. How does Karina ask for Robert's opinion?
A "Sit down, please."
B "What do you think?"
C "Would you like a coffee?"
3. What do they do at the end?
A They make a decision together.
B They go to the airport.
C They cancel the meeting.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Starting and Sharing Opinions

GRAMMAR

Como abrir uma reunião e pedir opiniões. / How to start a meeting and ask for opinions.

Phrase / FraseMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Let's start.Abrir a reunião / Open the meetingLet's start the meeting.
What do you think?Pedir a opinião / Ask for an opinionWhat do you think, Robert?
In my opinion...Dar a sua opinião / Give your opinionIn my opinion, it is a good idea.
I agree. / I disagree.Concordar ou discordar / Agree or disagreeI agree with you.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use do na pergunta -- What do you think? (não "What you think?"). E diga I agree (não "I am agree"). Agree já é o verbo.
