Lesson 15 -- In the Meeting Room: Starting and Sharing Opinions

Series
The Office (US) -- any conference room meeting scene

A workplace comedy. You see people open meetings, share opinions, agree and disagree, and make decisions in a real meeting room.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two meeting phrases you hear, such as "Let's start" or "What do you think?".

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Running a Meeting and Sharing Opinions"

A slow, clear podcast about meetings: how to start a meeting, read an agenda, ask for an opinion, agree or disagree, and make a decision politely.

Repeat each phrase out loud. Then record a short meeting opening for your own company.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Giving opinions and agreeing in English"

A short, practical lesson on today's language: starting a meeting, asking "What do you think?", giving opinions with "In my opinion...", and agreeing with "I agree".

After watching, write five sentences to lead a meeting: open it, read a point, ask for an opinion, give yours, and make a decision. Then read them aloud.
