Lesson 2 -- Growing Up in Butantan

Podcast
The Moth Radio Hour -- "American Dreams"

Real people telling true stories from their own lives on a stage, with no script. One woman learns to cook from her grandmother, who keeps one recipe secret. Another translates the world for her parents as a child. This is exactly what you practiced tonight: the past simple for what happened, the past continuous for the scene around it.

Tip: pick one story and listen for "was" and "were". Notice how the storyteller sets the scene before the thing happens. Then tell one of your own memories the same way.

YouTube
Sao Paulo: The Most Underrated City in the World

Your city, described in English by someone seeing it from outside. The neighborhoods, the food, the size of it, the reasons people love it and the reasons people leave. Useful vocabulary about city life, and a strange pleasure: hearing a stranger explain the place you know best.

Tip: pause whenever he describes a neighborhood and say your own version out loud: "Back then, my street was..." Compare his Sao Paulo with the one you remember.

Documentary
Sao Paulo City Mini-Documentary -- The Sao Paulo Series

A short documentary about daily life in the city: the people, the work, the rhythm, the traffic. Slower and more observational than the video above, and a good second listen once your ear has warmed up.

Tip: watch it once with subtitles and once without. The second time, write down three sentences about what life there was like when you lived it.
