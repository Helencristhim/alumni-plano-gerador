Grammar Video
Present Perfect with "for" and "since" -- 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

A short, clear lesson on the exact contrast at the heart of Lesson 2: "for" with a period of time (for six years) and "since" with a starting point (since 2018). This is the mistake to avoid when you talk about how long you have worked in QA.

Tip: after each example, pause and say one true sentence about your own career -- one with "for" and one with "since".

Present Perfect with "ever" and "never" -- 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

The other half of Lesson 2: how to ask about experience ("Have you ever led a team?") and how to say you have no experience of something ("I have never worked abroad"). Exactly the questions a recruiter asks and the honest answers you will give.

Tip: write three "Have you ever...?" questions you could ask an interviewer, and one honest "I have never..." answer about yourself.

Interview Skills
Job Interview in English: "Tell Me About Yourself" (Christina Rebuffet)

A practical lesson on structuring your answer to the most common interview question. Connection to Lesson 2: this is exactly where you tell your career story with the present perfect -- how long you have worked, your expertise, and the milestones you have achieved.

Tip: build your own 60-second answer. Use two present perfect sentences with "for/since" and one with a milestone. Record it and listen back.
