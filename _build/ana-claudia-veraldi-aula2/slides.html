Chapter 3: The Code

What Happened vs The Scene

I decided · I was sitting

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I grew up in Butantan."

"I was sitting in a traffic jam when I decided to leave."

"It was raining, and the radio was playing something sad."

"Two months later, we sold the apartment."

Why green for the scene around you, but blue for the thing that happened?

FormUseExample
Past Simple
verb + -ed (or irregular)		The event. A finished action, one after another.I decided to leave. We sold the flat.
Past Continuous
was / were + verb-ing		The scene. Something already in progress around the event.I was sitting in traffic. It was raining.
Togetherwhile + the scene, when + the event: "While I was driving, the phone rang."

The long action gets -ing. The short one that cuts it gets the past simple.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"When the phone rang, I was answering it."
"When the phone rang, I was cooking."
"I was growing up in Butantan."
"I grew up in Butantan."
"I was hating the noise back then."
"I hated the noise back then."

The long action in the background takes -ing; the short one that interrupts it takes the past simple. And state verbs (hate, love, know, want) stay simple in the past too.

Chapter 4: Telling the Story

Tom Asks About Before

Dialogue

Where Are You From?

T
So you have always lived in the countryside? Or did you move there later?
A
No, later. I grew up in Butantan, in Sao Paulo. My whole childhood was there.
T
What was it like back then?
A
Very noisy and very crowded. I commuted two hours a day. But there were good things too.
T
So what made you finally leave?
A
One afternoon I was sitting in a traffic jam downtown, and I just decided. Two months later we sold everything.
