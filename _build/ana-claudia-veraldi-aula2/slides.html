Chapter 4: Present Perfect

have · has · for / since

ever, never, and experience up to now

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in QA for six years."

"She has led a team since last year."

"Have you ever led a project?"

"I have never worked abroad."

UseFormExample
Experience up to nowhave / has + past participleI have worked in QA.
A period of timefor + periodI have worked here for six years.
A starting pointsince + point in timeI have worked here since 2018.
Questions / no experienceever in questions (Have you ever...?) · never for no experience (I have never...)

Present perfect = have/has + past participle. for + period · since + starting point · ever in questions · never for no experience.

Language Focus

For, Since, Ever, or Never?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I work in QA since 2018."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked in QA since 2018."

✗ WRONG

"I have worked here since six years."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked here for six years."

For experience up to now, use the present perfect, not the present simple. Use "for" with a period (for six years) and "since" with a starting point (since 2018).

Dialogue

David Asks About Your Career

D
Hi Ana. Thanks for joining. So, how long have you worked in quality assurance?
A
I have worked in QA for six years, and I have been in tech since 2018.
D
Impressive. And what is your area of expertise?
A
I have specialized in software testing. I have also led a small QA team since last year.
D
Great. Have you ever worked with a fully international team?
A
No, I have never worked abroad, but I have always wanted to. That is my next goal.
D
And what milestone are you most proud of?
A
I have mentored several junior testers, and I have earned two promotions. That means a lot to me.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. What is the first thing David asks Ana?
How long she has worked in quality assurance.
2. What does David want to know with "Have you ever...?"
If she has ever worked with a fully international team.
3. At the end, what milestone does David ask about?
The milestone she is most proud of.
