Lesson 02 -- Pre-class

Your Career Story -- Talking About Your Experience

Telling your professional story in a remote interview. Key words: career path, milestone, promotion, background, expertise, challenge, responsibility, to achieve, to specialize, to lead, to mentor, to earn. Structure: present perfect with for/since and ever/never for experience and duration up to now.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Career path -- the whole series of jobs and roles across your working life
"I have built a strong career path in technology."
Milestone -- an important achievement that marks real progress in your career
"Mentoring junior testers is one of my favorite milestones."
Promotion -- a move to a higher, more senior position at work
"I have earned two promotions in six years."
Background -- your past studies, training, and work experience
"My background is in computer science."
Expertise -- deep skill and knowledge in one specific field
"Software testing is my area of expertise."
Challenge -- a demanding task that tests your ability
"Every project brings a new challenge."
Responsibility -- a duty you are trusted to take care of
"Quality is my responsibility on every release."
To achieve -- to reach a goal after real effort
"I have achieved a lot in my career."
To specialize -- to focus your work on one particular area
"I have specialized in finding critical bugs."
To lead -- to guide and take charge of a team or project
"I have led a small QA team since last year."
To mentor -- to guide and support a less experienced colleague
"I have mentored several junior testers."
To earn -- to get something through your work, like a promotion or trust
"I have earned the trust of my stakeholders."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana has built a strong career path in technology. She has worked in quality assurance since 2018, and she has been in tech for more than ten years. Her background is in computer science, and her expertise is software testing. She has specialized in finding critical bugs, and every project is a new challenge. She has achieved a lot: she has earned two promotions and has mentored several junior testers — one of her favorite milestones. She has never worked abroad, but she has always wanted to. "Have you ever worked with an international team?" a recruiter asked. "No, I haven't," she said, "but that is my next goal."

1. Why do we say "She has worked in QA since 2018"?
A The experience started in 2018 and continues up to now (present perfect + since).
B The action is finished and has no link to the present.
C It is a plan for the future.
2. "She has been in tech for more than ten years" uses "for" because:
A "for" marks a fixed starting point.
B "for" is used with a period of time (ten years).
C "for" is only used in questions.
3. Which sentence about her experience abroad is correct?
A "She has worked abroad since never."
B "She has never worked abroad."
C "She never work abroad for years."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect with for / since / ever / never

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your experience up to now.

UseFormExample
Experience up to nowhave / has + past participleI have worked in QA.
A period of timefor + periodI have worked here for six years.
A starting pointsince + point in timeI have worked here since 2018.
Questions about experienceever + past participleHave you ever led a team?
No experience up to nowI have never worked abroad. (Do not use the present simple for experience: NOT "I work here since 2018".)

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best answer for each real interview situation.

The recruiter asks: "How long have you worked in QA?" You answer:
A "I work in QA for six years."
B "I have worked in QA for six years."
C "I am working in QA since six years."
He asks: "Have you ever led a team?" The best answer is:
A "Yes, I have. I have led a small QA team since last year."

C "I never work abroad for years."
