New vocabulary: agenda, recap, align, takeaway, action item, deadline, outcome, minutes. Signposting phrases for each stage. Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the planned list of topics for a meeting
"Let me set the agenda for today." (pauta)
Recap -- a short summary of what was said or decided
"Let me do a quick recap of last week." (resumo)
Align -- to bring people to the same goal or decision
"Can we align on one clear decision?" (alinhar)
Takeaway -- the key point to remember and act on
"The main takeaway is to move fast." (conclusão principal)
Action item -- a specific task assigned to a person in a meeting
"Your action item is to send the report." (tarefa)
Deadline -- the date by which a task must be finished
"The deadline is next Friday." (prazo)
Outcome -- the final result or decision of a discussion
"The outcome is clear: we invest." (resultado)
Minutes -- the written record of what a meeting decided
"I'll add it to the minutes." (ata)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signposting a Meeting

GRAMMAR

Quem conduz a reunião (the chair) usa frases fixas, os signposts, para marcar cada etapa: abrir, passar a palavra, resumir, mudar de assunto e fechar. São expressões prontas, não improvise. Cuidado: discuss não leva preposição ("discuss the agenda", não "discuss about"); e diga move on to, não "move to". / The chair uses fixed signposting phrases to mark each stage. "Discuss" takes no preposition, and use "move on to" the next item.

Stage / EtapaSignpost phrase
Open / Abrir"Let's get started. Let me set the agenda."
Hand over / Passar a palavra"The floor is yours." / "Over to you."
Recap / Resumir"So, to recap..." / "If I understand correctly..."
Move on / Mudar de assunto"Let's move on to the next item."
Close / Fechar"Let me capture the action items and the deadline."
