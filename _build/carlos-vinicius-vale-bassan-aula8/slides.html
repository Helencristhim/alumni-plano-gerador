Chapter 3 -- Signposting

Phrases That Guide the Meeting

Open, hand over, recap, move on, close

Listen and Notice

What Does Each Phrase Do?

Each phrase comes from the person running the meeting. Notice the orange words.

"Let me set the agenda. We have three items today."

"Marina, the floor is yours."

"So, to recap, we agreed to invest in the premium segment."

"Let me capture the action items before we close."

The Rule

One Signpost Per Stage

Stage of the meetingSignpost phrase
Open"Let's get started. Let me set the agenda."
Hand over"The floor is yours." / "Over to you."
Recap / check"So, to recap..." / "If I understand correctly..."
Move on"Let's move on to the next item."
Close"Let me capture the action items and the deadline."

Tip: these are fixed phrases. Say "move on to" (not "move to"), and "discuss the agenda" with no preposition (not "discuss about").

Common Mistake

Discuss vs Discuss About

"Let's discuss about the agenda, then move to the next item."
"Let's discuss the agenda, then move on to the next item."

"Discuss" takes no preposition: discuss the agenda. To change topic, say move on to, not "move to".

Dialogue

Carlos Chairs the Meeting

C

"Let's get started. On the agenda today: the south region and our forecast. Marina, the floor is yours."

M

"Thanks. To recap, revenue rose last quarter, but the south region is still in decline."

C

"So, if I understand you correctly, the trend is positive overall, but the south is the real risk?"

M

"Exactly. I would argue we should pause the south and invest in the premium segment."

C

"I see your point, and I mostly agree. Can we align on a smaller test first, as a compromise?"

M

"That works. Let's make it an action item, with a deadline of next Friday, and I'll add it to the minutes."
