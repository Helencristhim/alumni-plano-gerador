Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Talking about the future with going to

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we talk about the future?

"I am going to travel next week."

"She is going to visit her family."

"We are going to take a vacation soon."

"I am not going to work tomorrow."

Grammar Rule

Going to + Verb

SubjectStructureExample
Iam going to + verbI am going to travel.
He / She / Itis going to + verbShe is going to visit.
We / You / Theyare going to + verbWe are going to rest.
Negativeam/is/are + not going toI am not going to work.

Key idea: to talk about the future, use am / is / are + going to + verb. With I use am; with he/she use is; with we/you/they use are. To say no, add not: I am not going to.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I going to travel next week."
"I am going to travel next week."

Always use am, is, or are before going to. Never say "I going to" -- say "I am going to".
