Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dienane talks about the future. She says: "Tomorrow I am going to work. But next week is special. I am going to take a vacation. I am going to travel and visit my sister. The trip is soon!" In English, we talk about the future with am / is / are + going to + verb. With I we use am going to. With he or she we use is going to. With we, you, or they we use are going to. To say no, we add not: I am not going to.

1. How do we talk about the future in English?
A "I going to travel."
B "I am going to travel."
C "I am go travel."
2. Which is correct for "she"?
A "She is going to visit her sister."
B "She are going to visit her sister."
C "She going to visit her sister."
3. What is Dienane going to do next week?
A Take a vacation and travel.
B Work every day.
C Stay at home.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Going to + Verb

GRAMMAR

Em inglês, falamos do futuro com am/is/are + going to + verbo. / In English, we talk about the future with am/is/are + going to + verb.

Subject / SujeitoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
I (eu)am going to + verboI am going to travel.
He / She (ele/ela)is going to + verboShe is going to visit.
We / You / Theyare going to + verboWe are going to rest.
negativoam/is/are + not going toI am not going to work.

Key point / Ponto-chave: sempre use o verbo to be (am, is ou are) antes de going to. Nunca diga "I going to" -- diga I am going to. Para negar, coloque not depois do to be: I am not going to.
