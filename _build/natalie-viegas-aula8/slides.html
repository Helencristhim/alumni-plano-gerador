Chapter 3 -- The Present Perfect Code

Grammar Discovery

Present perfect and quantifiers

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words show about results and how we measure them?

"I have led several deals this year."

"We have closed a lot of contracts."

"Our team has delivered plenty of results."

"Have you ever pitched to many directors at once?"

Grammar Rule

Present Perfect and Quantifiers

FormUseExample
have/has + past participleresults and experience up to nowI have led several deals.
many / a few (countable)count things: clients, dealsWe have many clients.
much / a little (uncountable)things you cannot count: progressWe have made much progress.
a lot of / plenty of (both)a large amount, both typesWe have plenty of results.

Key idea: use the present perfect for results up to now, when the exact time is not important ("I have closed ten deals"). Use many / a few with countable nouns, much / a little with uncountable nouns, and a lot of / plenty of with both.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have led several deals last year."
"I have led several deals this year."

The present perfect connects to now. Use it with unfinished time like this year, not with finished time like last year (that takes the past simple: "I led several deals last year").
