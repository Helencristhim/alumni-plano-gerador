Presenting to Leadership -- Present Perfect and Quantifiers

Deliver a short elevator pitch about your results, using the present perfect (have/has + past participle) and quantifiers (many, several, a lot of, plenty of). New vocabulary: pitch, value proposition, highlight, audience, takeaway, impact, persuade, outcome, compelling, tailor.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Pitch -- a short, persuasive talk to present an idea or a result
"I gave a two-minute pitch to the leadership team." (Discurso de venda)
Value proposition -- the main reason your product is useful and worth the money
"Our value proposition is faster, safer cloud services." (Proposta de valor)
Highlight -- to give special attention to something important
"I want to highlight one key result this quarter." (Destacar)
Audience -- the people who listen to a presentation or pitch
"Know your audience before you present." (Público)
Takeaway -- the main message you want people to remember
"The takeaway is simple: we have doubled our deals." (Mensagem principal)
Impact -- a strong effect or result on people or business
"Our project had a real impact on costs." (Impacto)
Persuade -- to make someone agree by giving good reasons
"A clear pitch helps you persuade the board." (Persuadir)
Outcome -- the final result of an action or process
"The outcome of the deal was a three-year contract." (Resultado)
Compelling -- very interesting and convincing
"She told a compelling story about the customer." (Convincente)
Tailor -- to adapt something for a specific person or need
"Tailor your pitch to the people in the room." (Adaptar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect and Quantifiers

GRAMMAR

Usamos o present perfect para falar de resultados e experiências até agora, e quantificadores para medir quantidade. / We use the present perfect for results and experience up to now, and quantifiers to measure amount.

FormUseExample
have/has + past participleresults and experience up to nowI have led several deals.
many / a few (countable)things you can count: clients, dealsWe have many clients.
much / a little (uncountable)things you cannot count: progressWe have made much progress.
a lot of / plenty of (both)a large amount, both typesWe have plenty of results.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use o present perfect para resultados até agora, quando o tempo exato não importa ("We have closed ten deals"). Não use com tempo terminado: diga "this year" (não "last year"). Use many com contáveis, much com incontáveis, e a lot of / plenty of com os dois.
