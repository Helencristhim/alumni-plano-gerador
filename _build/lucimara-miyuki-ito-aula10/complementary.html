TED Talk — how to be understood
Julian Treasure — How to Speak So That People Want to Listen

This is the whole point of tonight's lesson in ten minutes: how to say what you mean so that people actually listen. It is not about big words — it is about being clear, warm, and confident. Exactly the skill you need at the Lyon fair or a dinner in New York, when someone asks what your company does.

Tip: watch it once with English subtitles. The second time, close your eyes and only listen. Then write, in one sentence, the one habit you want to steal for your own 90-second company pitch.

Listening — office and business English (B1)
BBC Learning English — English at Work: The Interview

A short, funny audio drama set in a real office, with the transcript on the page so you can check what you catch. The characters talk about work, roles, and what a company does — the exact register you will use when you explain your own job in English. It targets the gap you named yourself: understanding natural speech.

Tip (the one that pays the most): listen ONCE without reading, and just catch what you can. Then read the transcript and listen again. Finally, play it a third time in the car and repeat, out loud, any sentence that describes what someone does at work.
