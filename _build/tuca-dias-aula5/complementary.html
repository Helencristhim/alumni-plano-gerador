Lesson 5 -- Table Talk: Ordering & Food Culture

Series
Taco Chronicles (Las Cronicas del Taco) -- "Pastor"

A Netflix documentary on the history and culture of Mexican tacos. Perfect before your trip to Mexico City: listen for how people describe flavors, ingredients, and the experience of ordering at a real taqueria.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down five food words you hear (appetizer, main course, spicy, flavor, side dish). Then describe one dish using "I would like..." as if you were ordering it.

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "At the Restaurant"

A short, slow lesson dedicated to restaurant language: "I would like", asking for recommendations, ordering a main course and a beverage, and asking for the bill politely.

Pause after each phrase and repeat it aloud. Note three polite ways to order with "would like", and compare them with how Tuca ordered in today's dialogue.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Ordering food in a restaurant"

A clear, practical video on ordering in English: how to ask for recommendations, choose an appetizer, a main course and a dessert, and how to handle the bill and the tip. Directly useful for travelling alone.

After watching, record a 60-second order at an imaginary Mexican restaurant. Use at least four new words and three sentences with "I would like".
