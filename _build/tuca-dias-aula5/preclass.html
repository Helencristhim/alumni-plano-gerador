Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Appetizer -- a small dish served before the main course
"We ordered guacamole as an appetizer." (Entrada)
Main course -- the largest, central dish of a meal
"For the main course, I had chicken tacos." (Prato principal)
Side dish -- a smaller dish served with the main course
"Rice and beans are a great side dish." (Acompanhamento)
Dessert -- a sweet dish eaten at the end of a meal
"For dessert, we shared some churros." (Sobremesa)
Beverage -- a drink, especially one other than water
"What beverage would you like with your meal?" (Bebida)
Spicy -- having a strong, hot flavor from peppers or spices
"The green sauce is very spicy." (Apimentado)
Refreshing -- pleasantly cool and reviving to drink
"The lime water was so refreshing." (Refrescante)
Recommendation -- a suggestion about something good to choose
"I followed the server's recommendation." (Recomendação)
Bill -- the paper showing how much you must pay
"Could we have the bill, please?" (Conta)
Tip -- extra money you leave for good service
"I left a good tip for the server." (Gorjeta)
Server -- the person who takes your order and brings the food
"The server was very friendly." (Garçom / Garçonete)
Flavor -- the taste of a food or drink
"The flavor of the churros was amazing." (Sabor)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca sat down at a Mexican restaurant and the server brought the menu. "I would like to start with the guacamole appetizer, please," she said. The server asked, "Would you like a side dish with your main course?" Tuca answered, "Yes, I'd like some rice and a few tortillas." Because rice is uncountable, she said "some rice", but tortillas are countable, so she said "a few tortillas". She did not want too much chili, so she added, "Just a little spicy sauce, please." For a beverage, she chose a refreshing lime water, and she saved room for dessert.

1. Which sentence is the polite way to order?
A "I want the guacamole appetizer."
B "I would like the guacamole appetizer, please."
C "Give me the guacamole appetizer."
2. Why does Tuca say "a few tortillas" but "some rice"?
A Tortillas are countable, but rice is uncountable.
B Rice is countable, but tortillas are uncountable.
C There is no difference; both are correct either way.
3. How does Tuca ask for a small amount of sauce?
A "Just a few spicy sauce, please."
B "Just a little spicy sauce, please."
C "Just many spicy sauce, please."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- "Would like" & Countable/Uncountable

GRAMMAR

Usados para pedir com educacao e medir quantidades. / Used to order politely and measure quantities.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Would like (+)Pedido educado / A polite requestI would like / I'd like a coffee, please.
Would like (?)Oferecer algo / Offer somethingWould you like a dessert?
Countable (contaveis)Pode contar: a/an, some, many, a few / You can count thema taco, two tacos, a few tortillas, How many?
Uncountable (incontaveis)Nao se conta: some, much, a little / You cannot count themsome rice, a little water, How much?

Key point / Ponto-chave: Use "How many?" para contaveis (tacos) e "How much?" para incontaveis (rice, water). E sempre "I'd like", nunca "I want", para soar educado.
