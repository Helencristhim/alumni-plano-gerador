Talk
"The walk from 'no' to 'yes'" -- William Ury (TED)

A clear, practical talk by one of the world's best-known negotiators about how to reach agreement when the other side is hard. Connection to Lesson 5: this is exactly what you do when a partner bank pushes and you defend your terms. Notice how he imagines what would happen if each side moved -- the second conditional in action.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Then say one negotiation line out loud in the second conditional: "If they asked for more collateral, I would offer a small concession."

Podcast
"Cautionary Tales" -- Tim Harford

Short, well-told stories about decisions that went wrong and what people could have done differently, in clear British English. Connection to Lesson 5: every episode is a "what if" -- if they had acted sooner, if the warning had been heard. Perfect listening for the imagined world of the second conditional.

Tip: pick one episode, listen once for the main idea, then say two sentences about it in the second conditional: "If the team had more time, they would... If I were in that room, I would..."

Read
BBC News -- Business & Finance

Free, clearly written business and finance news, a lighter read than the Financial Times you already know. Connection to Lesson 5: reading is your strength, so use it to feed your speaking. Many stories describe risks and deals where you can imagine the "what if" behind the numbers.

Tip: read one short article, then close it and say one hypothetical out loud: "If this bank became insolvent, the market would..." Use the second conditional for the imagined result.
