Chapter 3: The Code

If It Happened...

If the market crashed · we would unwind

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If the market crashed, we would unwind the position."

"If the bank froze our credit line, we would post more collateral."

"If I were you, I would not accept their first offer."

"If liquidity falls next week, we will unwind the position."

Why if + past → would for the imagined, but if + present → will for the real plan?

TypeUseExample
Second conditional
If + past simple, ... would + verb		An imagined or unlikely situation, and its imagined result. Not a plan — a "what if".If the market crashed, we would unwind.
First conditional
If + present, ... will + verb		A real, likely future. A plan for what will happen.If liquidity falls, we will unwind.
The verb "to be"In the imagined "if" part, formal English uses were for every subject: if I were you; if the plan were clear.

Imagined = second conditional (if + past → would) · Real plan = first conditional (if + present → will).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the market would crash, we would sell."
"If the market crashed, we would sell."
"If the bank froze the line, we will post collateral."
"If the bank froze the line, we would post collateral."
"If I was the regulator, I would ask the same."
"If I were the regulator, I would ask the same."

In the second conditional the if part takes the past simple (crashed, froze), never would, and the result takes would + verb. In formal English, use were for every subject in the imagined "if" part: if I were you.

Chapter 4: The What-If Negotiation

Answer and Negotiate

The partner bank tests you

Dialogue

On the Call

S
Sergio, thank you for the call. Let me start with a hypothetical. If our bank froze your credit line tomorrow, what would your desk do?
S
That is a fair question. If you froze the line, we would post more collateral the same day and protect the desk.
S
And if the market crashed at the same time? Would you still cover the exposure?
S
If the market crashed, we would not panic. We would negotiate a waiver and offer a small concession, not a fire sale.
S
Good. So, a real proposal now: if we made a counteroffer with a lower credit line, would your committee accept it?
S
If the downside were limited, we would accept it. If it threatened the buffer, we would ask for more time.
Comprehension

About Sofia

1. What hypothetical does Sofia ask about first?
What Sergio's desk would do if her bank froze his credit line.
2. What harder situation does she add?
The market crashing at the same time.
3. What real proposal does she make at the end?
A counteroffer with a lower credit line.
