Negotiating Partnerships and Contracts

How to negotiate a partnership and report what was agreed. Key words: contract, terms, clause, to negotiate, partnership, to sign off on, deadline, draft, to renew, binding, to meet halfway, leverage. Structure: reported speech (backshift the tense, tell + person vs say, reported questions with if/whether).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Contract -- a formal written agreement between two sides (contrato)
"We signed the contract last week."
Terms -- the conditions of an agreement (termos, condicoes)
"The terms are good for both companies."
Clause -- one specific section of a contract (clausula)
"There is a clause about cancellations."
To negotiate -- to discuss in order to reach an agreement (negociar)
"We negotiated a better rate."
Partnership -- a business relationship between two companies (parceria)
"This partnership will grow every year."
To sign off on -- to give final approval to something (aprovar formalmente)
"My director signed off on the deal."
Deadline -- the latest time by which something must be done (prazo)
"The deadline for the draft is Friday."
Draft -- an early version of a document (rascunho, minuta)
"I will send you the first draft today."
To renew -- to extend an agreement for more time (renovar)
"We renewed the contract for two years."
Binding -- legally forcing you to do something (vinculante, obrigatorio)
"Once signed, the contract is binding."
To meet halfway -- to compromise so both sides win a little (chegar a um meio-termo)
"Let us meet halfway on the price."
Leverage -- an advantage that gives you power in a negotiation (poder de barganha)
"Volume gives us leverage in the deal."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea is negotiating a two-year partnership with Anders. After the meeting, she confirms every point using reported speech. 'You said the rate would be fixed for two years,' she reminds him, 'and you told me the deadline was the end of March.' Anders replies that his director asked if she could commit to more volume. Andrea offers to meet halfway. Every point they agree is written into a clause in the draft; once both sides sign off, the contract is binding and the partnership can renew for years.

1. 'He said the rate would be fixed' reports which sentence?
A 'The rate will be fixed.'
B 'The rate is fixed right now.'
C 'Fix the rate, please.'
2. To report a question, you:
A keep the inversion: 'asked could we meet'.
B use if/whether with no inversion: 'asked if we could meet'.
C just add a question mark.
3. Which sentence uses 'tell' correctly?
A He told me the deadline was Friday.
B He told the deadline was Friday.
C He said me the deadline was Friday.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Como relatar o que alguém disse ao confirmar um acordo (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Recuar 1 passo / Backshift one stepwill->would, is->was, has->had.He said it would start Monday.
say vs telltell + pessoa (told me); say sem pessoa. tell needs a person.She told me / She said that...
Perguntas reportadas / Reported questionssem inversão; use if/whether ou wh-.He asked if we could meet halfway.
Regra de ouro / Golden rulerelate o que foi dito, um tempo verbal atrás.You said the rate would be fixed.
