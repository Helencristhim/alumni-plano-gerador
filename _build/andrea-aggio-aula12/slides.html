Chapter 4: Reported Speech

Reported Speech

backshift the tense · tell + person · if / whether

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"He said the rates would stay fixed."

"She told me the contract had been signed."

"They asked if we could meet halfway."

What happens to the tense when we report what someone said? And how is a reported question different from a direct one?

FormUseExample
Backshift one stepwill -> would, is -> was, has -> hadHe said it would start Monday.
tell vs saytell needs a person (told me); say does notShe told me / She said that...
Reported questionsno inversion; use if / whether or wh-He asked if we could meet halfway.

Reported speech: move the tense back one step, use tell + person but say without one, and report questions with if/whether and no inversion.

Language Focus

Report the Deal

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"He said the rate will be fixed."

✓ RIGHT

He said the rate would be fixed.

✗ WRONG

"She told that the deal was ready."

✓ RIGHT

She told me that the deal was ready.

In reported speech, backshift the tense one step (will -> would), and remember: tell needs a person (told me), while say does not (said that). Reported questions use if/whether with no inversion.

Register & Tone

Sound Professional

Tap to see the polished, professional version

Instead of: "Take it or leave it."
Say: Let us find terms that work for both of us.
Instead of: "That price is final."
Say: There may be some room if we look at the volume.
Instead of: "You are wrong about the deadline."
Say: Let me check -- I understood the deadline was the end of March.
Instead of: "We will not change the contract."
Say: We could review that clause and meet halfway.
Dialogue

Closing the Partnership

A
Andrea, thanks for meeting. I spoke to my director yesterday, and he said we could offer you fifteen percent if the volume is high enough.
A
That is a good start, Anders. But last month you told me the rate would be fixed for two years. Is that still in the contract?
A
It is. The clause is already in the draft. My director asked if you could commit to fifty room nights a month.
A
We can meet halfway. If we commit to forty, would you sign off on the fifteen percent and renew for two years?
A
Let me be honest -- I need something in return. The deadline for the new season is tight.
A
Understood. I will confirm forty room nights in writing today, so you have leverage with your director. That gives us both a binding agreement we can trust.
A
That works. I will update the draft and send it over. Once we both sign off, the partnership is official.
A
Perfect. I appreciate how clearly you have laid out the terms, Anders. This is exactly how a good partnership should start.

The Draft

Partnership Terms

From: Anders Lindqvist -- Nordic Hotels
To: Andrea Aggio -- Escape Turismo
Subject: Draft agreement -- for your review

Dear Andrea, thank you for a productive meeting. As agreed, the key terms for our two-year partnership are below. Once you sign off, I will send the binding version.

1. Discount: fifteen percent on all group bookings, fixed for two years. 2. Volume: a minimum of forty room nights per month. 3. Deadline: both sides to sign before the end of the month.

You mentioned that clause seven, on cancellations, needed one small change, so I have updated it. If the terms work for you, reply to confirm and we will make it official. Best regards, Anders.
