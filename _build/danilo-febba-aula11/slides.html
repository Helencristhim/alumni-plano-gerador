Chapter 3

The Time Code

at / on / in and telling the time

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The meeting is at ten o'clock. / The call is at noon.

The deadline is on Friday. / We meet on May 5th.

I start in the morning. / The project ends in May.

It is half past two. / It is a quarter to five.

at a time, on a day, in a month. Special: in the morning / afternoon / evening, but at night.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The meeting is in Monday."

✓ RIGHT

"The meeting is on Monday."

✗ WRONG

"The call is in three o'clock."

✓ RIGHT

"The call is at three o'clock."

✗ WRONG

"I work at the morning."

✓ RIGHT

"I work in the morning."

at a time, on a day, in a month or part of the day. Days take on; clock times take at; mornings and afternoons take in.

Ten o'clock, perfect. And the client call?
R
The client call is on Wednesday, at half past two.
D

R

D
No problem. And our one-on-one?
R

R
Great. Remember: please be on time for the team meeting.
D
Always. I am never late. Thanks, Rachel.
