Numbers, Dates & Schedules -- Times and Deadlines

Fale de horários e prazos no trabalho. Palavras-chave: deadline, calendar, appointment, noon, hour, minute, early, on time. Gramática: preposições de tempo at / on / in (at + hora, on + dia/data, in + mês/ano/parte do dia) e números para dizer as horas (o'clock, half past, quarter past, quarter to).

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. (Escute cada palavra e leia a tradução.)

Deadline -- the day or time when work must be finished (prazo final)
"The deadline is on Friday."
Calendar -- a page or app that shows the days and months (calendário / agenda)
"I check my calendar every day."
Appointment -- a meeting planned for a set time (compromisso / hora marcada)
"I have an appointment at three."
Noon -- twelve o'clock in the day; midday (meio-dia)
"The deadline is at noon."
Hour -- a period of sixty minutes (uma hora)
"The meeting is one hour."
Minute -- a period of sixty seconds (um minuto)
"Wait a minute, please."
Early -- before the planned time (cedo / adiantado)
"I start work early."
On time -- at the exact right time, not late (no horário / pontual)
"Please be on time."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- at / on / in & Telling the Time

GRAMMAR

Use at para horas, on para dias e datas, in para meses, anos e partes do dia. Para dizer as horas: o'clock (hora cheia), half past (e meia), quarter past (e quinze), quarter to (quinze para).

PrepositionUso (PT)Example
athoras (at + hora)at ten o'clock, at noon
ondias e datas (on + dia)on Monday, on May 5th
inmeses, anos, partes do diain May, in 2025, in the morning
o'clock / half pastdizer as horasthree o'clock, half past two
quarter past / toe quinze / quinze paraquarter past four, quarter to five

Atenção (erro comum): dias levam on (não "in Monday"), horas levam at (não "in three o'clock"), e partes do dia levam in (não "at the morning"). Exceção: usamos at night (não "in the night"). Para datas, use números ordinais: the first, the second, the third, the fifth.
