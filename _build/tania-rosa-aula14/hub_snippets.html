Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the story and answer the questions.

Last summer, Tânia had a small adventure. One afternoon she was walking on a quiet path, and she was taking photos of the amazing scenery. The view was beautiful, so she was not looking at the map. Suddenly, she got lost! Then she saw a kind old man who was reading near a tree. He showed her the way back. Now, when she remembers that journey, she always smiles. It is her favorite memory.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous (was / were + -ing)

Grammar

Use o past continuous (was/were + verbo com -ing) para uma ação que estava acontecendo no passado, ou para montar o cenário de uma história. A ação curta que interrompe vai no past simple. Lembre: I/he/she/it = was; we/you/they = were. / Use the past continuous (was/were + verb-ing) for an action in progress in the past, or to set the scene of a story. The short action that interrupts it goes in the past simple. Remember: I/he/she/it = was; we/you/they = were.

Função / FunctionEnglishPortuguês
Action in progress (I/she)I was walking on the beach.Eu estava caminhando na praia.
Action in progress (they)They were singing in the square.Eles estavam cantando na praça.
Interruption (when + past simple)I was sleeping when the phone rang.Eu estava dormindo quando o telefone tocou.
Set the scene (while)While I was walking, I heard music.Enquanto eu caminhava, ouvi música.
NegativeI was not looking at the map.Eu não estava olhando para o mapa.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A friend asks what you were doing at 8 p.m. yesterday. How do you answer?
A "I was watching a film at home."

C "I will watch a film."
You want to describe two background actions in your story.
A "They was singing and dancing."

C "They are sing and dance."
You want to say a long action was cut by a short one.
