Chapter 3: Setting the Scene

The Past Continuous

was / were + verb + -ing

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I was walking on a quiet path." (background)

"We were taking photos of the view." (background)

"I was walking when it started to rain." (interruption)

"While she was reading, I took a photo." (two scenes)

FormUseExample
I / he / she / it + was + -ingAn action in progress in the pastI was walking on the beach.
we / you / they + were + -ingAn action in progress in the pastThey were singing in the square.
was/were + -ing + when + past simpleA long action, then a short one cuts itI was sleeping when the phone rang.
while + was/were + -ingSet the scene / two actions togetherWhile I was walking, I heard music.

Use the past continuous (was/were + -ing) for an action in progress in the past, or to set the scene. The short action that interrupts it goes in the past simple (I heard, it rang).

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I walking on the beach."

✓ RIGHT

"I was walking on the beach."

✗ WRONG

"They was singing a song."

✓ RIGHT

"They were singing a song."

Chapter 4: Telling the Story

Tânia Tells a Friend

One trip, one little adventure

Dialogue

An Adventure on the Trip

F
So, how was your journey to the mountains? Tell me everything!
T
It was an adventure! One afternoon I was walking on a quiet path, and I was taking photos of the amazing scenery.
F
That sounds beautiful. And then? What happened?
T
Well, I was not looking at the map, and suddenly I got lost! But then I saw a kind old man who was reading near a tree.
F
Oh, lucky you! Did he help you find the way back?
T
Yes! He showed me the way. Now, when I remember that journey, I always smile. It is my favorite memory.
Listening

An Adventure in the Mountains

Listen first. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What was the speaker doing on the quiet path?
He was walking and taking photos of the amazing scenery.
2. Why did he get lost?
He was so happy that he was not looking at the map.
3. What was the old man doing when the speaker saw him?
He was sitting near a tree and reading a book.
Comprehension

About the Story

1. What was Tânia doing on the path?
She was walking and taking photos of the scenery.
2. What happened suddenly?
She got lost, because she was not looking at the map.
3. What was the old man doing when she saw him?
He was reading near a tree, and he showed her the way.
Listening 2

My Favorite Memory

A reflection. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What time of day was it in the story?
It was early morning, and the streets were still quiet.
2. What did the speaker hear while she was walking?
She heard music; a group of people were dancing and singing.
3. Why is this her favorite memory?
She was not taking photos -- she was just living the moment.
