Chapter 3

The Order Code

first · then · next · after that · finally

Discovery

Listen and Notice

First, I open the file.

Then, I check the data.

Next, I run the report.

Finally, I save my work.

To mark the...Use
First stepFirst, + sentence: First, I open the file.
Middle stepsThen, / Next, / After that, + sentence.
Last stepFinally, + sentence: Finally, I save my work.
The verbPresent simple: I open, I check, I save.

Use First for the first step and Finally for the last step. For the middle, use Then, Next, or After that. Put a comma after the order word, and keep the verb in the present simple.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"At first, I open the file."

✓ RIGHT

"First, I open the file."

✗ WRONG

"After, I check the data."

✓ RIGHT

"After that, I check the data."

✗ WRONG

"In end, I save the file."

✓ RIGHT

"Finally, I save the file."

Use First for the first step and Finally for the last step. For a middle step, say "After that", not just "after".

Chapter 4

In Action

Explaining a process, line by line

Dialogue

The Weekly Report

R
Hi Danilo! I am new here. Can you show me the process for the weekly report?
D
Of course, Rachel. It is easy. It has four steps.
R
Great. I want to learn it well. What is the first step?
D
First, you open the file and prepare the data.
R
Okay. And then?
D
Then, you check the numbers. Next, you run the report.
R
I see. What do I do after that?
D
After that, you save the file. Finally, you complete the report and send it.
R
That sounds simple. I will start now. Thank you so much!
D
You are welcome. If you forget a step, just repeat the process.
