Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dienane makes a work call. She says: "Hello, can I speak to Marcos, please?" Marcos answers: "This is Marcos speaking." Dienane says her name: "This is Dienane speaking." Marcos is busy, so he says: "Hold on, please." When the person is away, we say: "Can I take a message?" In English, on the phone, the caller says Can I speak to + name. You answer with This is + name + speaking. These are special phone phrases.

1. How do you ask for a person on the phone?
A "I want talk Marcos."
B "Can I speak to Marcos, please?"
C "Marcos speak me?"
2. How do you say who you are on the phone?
A "This is Dienane speaking."
B "I am Dienane here."
C "Dienane is speaking me."
3. The person is away. What do you say?
A "Can I take a message?"
B "Can I make a message?"
C "Can I have a message?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Phone Phrases

GRAMMAR

No telefone, usamos frases fixas especiais. / On the phone, we use special fixed phrases.

Who / QuemPhrase / FraseWhen / Quando
caller (quem liga)Can I speak to + nome?para começar a ligação
você atendeThis is + nome + speaking.para dizer quem é
você esperaHold on, please.para aguardar um momento
pessoa ausenteCan I take a message?para anotar o recado

Key point / Ponto-chave: no telefone, quem liga diz Can I speak to + nome. Você responde com This is + nome + speaking (não "I am"). Para esperar, diga Hold on, please. Para anotar um recado, diga Can I take a message?
