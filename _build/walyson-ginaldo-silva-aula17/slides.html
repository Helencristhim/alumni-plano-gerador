Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Reported speech to confirm what was said

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What happens to the verb when we report what someone said?

"She said the deal was approved." (direct: "The deal is approved.")

"She told me the order form was the first deliverable." (direct: "...is the first deliverable.")

"He said we would finalize the timeline." (direct: "We will finalize the timeline.")

"She said we could proceed." (direct: "We can proceed.")

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

Direct speechReported speech
"The deal is approved."She said the deal was approved.
"We will finalize it."He said they would finalize it.
"You can proceed."She said we could proceed.
"We have a deal."He said we had a deal.

Key idea: to report what someone said, move the verb one step back (backshift): is/are → was/were, will → would, can → could, has → had. Use say (no person) or tell + person: "She said the deal was approved" / "She told me the deal was approved." The word that is optional.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She said me the deal was approved."
"She told me the deal was approved." / "She said the deal was approved."

Use tell + person (told me, told us) but say with no person (said that...). Never said me.
