Closing the Deal -- Confirming the Final Agreement in Writing

Confirming the final agreement and the next steps in writing after a negotiation. Learning reported speech (say / tell + backshift: is→was, will→would, can→could) to report what was decided. New vocabulary: agreement, as agreed, finalize, proceed, next steps, deliverable, timeline, outstanding, go-ahead, move forward, summary, on board.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agreement -- a deal that both sides have accepted on the terms
"We have reached an agreement on the price and volume." (Acordo)
As agreed -- a phrase that repeats what both sides already decided
"As agreed, the price is three dollars thirty-five per kilo." (Conforme combinado)
Finalize -- to complete the last details so something is final
"Let us finalize the shipping schedule this week." (Finalizar / Concluir)
Proceed -- to go ahead with the next part of the deal
"The deal is approved, so we can proceed." (Prosseguir / Seguir em frente)
Next steps -- the actions both sides will take after closing the deal
"Here are the next steps: sign the form and set the date." (Próximos passos)
Deliverable -- a document or result that one side must hand over
"The first deliverable is the signed order form." (Entregável)
Timeline -- the dates and stages in the order they will happen
"The timeline is: sign on Friday, ship next month." (Cronograma / Linha do tempo)
Outstanding -- still open and not yet finished or handed over
"The signed form is the only outstanding item on my side." (Pendente / Em aberto)
Go-ahead -- permission from a boss or client to start something
"My director gave the go-ahead, so we can sign." (Sinal verde / Autorização)
Move forward -- to continue with the deal and the next stages together
"Once the form is signed, we can move forward." (Avançar / Seguir adiante)
Summary -- a short review of the main points that were agreed
"Here is a summary of the deal for your records." (Resumo)
On board -- agreeing with the plan and willing to take part
"Reply to confirm you are on board, and we will sign." (De acordo / A bordo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the email and answer the questions.

Dear Clara,

Thank you for a fair negotiation. Here is a short summary to confirm our agreement. As agreed, the price is three dollars thirty-five per kilo, with free freight, for twenty containers. You said the deal was approved by your director, and you told me the signed order form was the first deliverable. The form is the only outstanding item on my side, and I will send it by Friday so we can proceed. The next steps and timeline are: sign by Friday, then finalize the shipping schedule and move forward. Please reply to confirm you are on board, and we will both sign off.

Best regards,
Walyson

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported speech

GRAMMAR

Usamos reported speech para contar o que alguém disse, ao confirmar uma negociação por escrito. O verbo recua um tempo (backshift). / We use reported speech to report what someone said, when we confirm a negotiation in writing. The verb moves one step back (backshift).

Direct / DiretoReported / Reportado
"The deal is approved."She said the deal was approved.
"We will finalize it."He said they would finalize it.
"You can proceed."She said we could proceed.
"We have a deal."He said we had a deal.

Key point / Ponto-chave: ao reportar, recue o verbo um tempo (backshift): is/are → was/were, will → would, can → could, has → had. Use say sem pessoa ("she said that...") ou tell + pessoa ("she told me that..."). NUNCA "said me". O that é opcional. / Move the verb one step back; use say (no person) or tell + person; never "said me".

Stage 2: Put the Steps of a Confirmation Email in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the steps of writing a confirmation email in the correct order.

?After that, report what was decided with reported speech, for example she said the deal was approved.
?First, open the email and thank the other side for the agreement.
?Finally, give a clear deadline, ask the other side to confirm, and say that you will both sign off and move forward.
?Next, confirm the final terms clearly, for example the price, the volume, and the freight, using as agreed.
?Then, list the next steps and the outstanding items, such as the signed order form and the shipping timeline.
