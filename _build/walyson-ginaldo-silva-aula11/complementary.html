Lesson 11 -- Agreeing and Disagreeing

Series
Suits -- a negotiation or debate scene

Watch two characters argue a business decision. Notice how they share a point of view, push back politely, and look for a compromise instead of just saying "no".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two softeners you hear (for example "I see your point, but...") and one phrase used to disagree.

Podcast
All Ears English -- "How to Disagree Politely in English"

A practical episode on diplomatic disagreement: softeners, conceding a valid point, and reaching a compromise so the conversation stays respectful.

Record a 2-minute debate: share your point of view, agree with one valid part, push back with a softener, and suggest a compromise.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Agreeing and disagreeing politely"

A focused lesson on the phrases for agreeing, softening, and disagreeing diplomatically in meetings and debates. It directly reinforces today's language.

After watching, write six sentences: two to agree, two to soften and push back, and two to suggest a compromise.
