Aula 11 -- Pre-class

Agreeing and Disagreeing -- Debating a Business Decision

Learning to agree and disagree in a diplomatic way at work: the language of opinions, softeners and polite pushback to debate a business decision. New vocabulary: point of view, agree with, disagree, softener, push back, concede, valid, on the same page, see your point, fair enough, compromise, raise a concern.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Point of view -- the way a person thinks about something
"Let me share my point of view." (Ponto de vista)
Agree with -- to have the same opinion as someone
"I agree with your idea." (Concordar com)
Disagree -- to have a different opinion
"I disagree, but I respect your point." (Discordar)
Softener -- a polite phrase that makes your words gentler
"Use a softener before you disagree." (Amenizador)
Push back -- to politely say no or argue against an idea
"I have to push back on the deadline." (Contestar)
Concede -- to accept that the other person is partly right
"I concede that the price is good." (Reconhecer/Ceder)
Valid -- reasonable and worth considering
"That is a valid concern." (Válido)
On the same page -- in full agreement about something
"Are we on the same page now?" (De acordo)
See your point -- to understand the other person's reason
"I see your point about quality." (Entendo seu ponto)
Fair enough -- a phrase to accept the other person's idea politely
"Fair enough, let us try that." (Faz sentido / Justo)
Compromise -- a solution where each side gives up a little
"Let us find a compromise." (Meio-termo / Acordo)
Raise a concern -- to mention a worry or a possible problem
"I want to raise a concern about the cost." (Levantar uma preocupação)

GRAMMAR

In the debate about the new coffee supplier, Walyson shares his point of view calmly. He agrees with Sofia on the price, and he says it is a valid option. He does not just disagree; he uses a polite phrase to push back. Sofia answers, "Fair enough." She is happy to concede that quality matters. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Softeners for diplomatic disagreement

GRAMMAR

Usamos amenizadores (softeners) antes de discordar, para soar educado e profissional. / We use softeners before we disagree, to sound polite and professional.

Function / FunçãoPhrase / FraseUse / Uso
AgreeI agree with you. / You are right.concordar diretamente
SoftenI see your point, but... / That is valid, however...reconhecer antes de discordar
DisagreeI am not sure I agree. / I have to push back on that.discordar com educação
ResolveFair enough. / Let us find a compromise.fechar com um acordo

Key point / Ponto-chave: em inglês profissional, raramente dizemos só "No, you are wrong". Primeiro use um softener ("I see your point, but..."), depois push back com um motivo claro, e termine buscando um compromise. Isso mantém o respeito e ajuda a chegar a um acordo. / In professional English, we rarely just say "No, you are wrong." First use a softener, then push back with a clear reason, and end by looking for a compromise.
