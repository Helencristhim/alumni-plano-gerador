Lesson 17 -- Networking Follow-up

Series
Suits -- a scene where a lawyer follows up after a meeting

Watch how strong professionals follow up after meeting a new contact. Listen for the warm, sincere language: thanking the person, mentioning something specific, and keeping the door open. Notice how a short, prompt message builds the relationship.

Write down every phrase used to thank or stay in contact. Then record a short follow-up out loud using appreciate, grateful, reach out, and keep in touch.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Networking & the Follow-up Message"

Practical phrases for writing a follow-up after a meeting or event: how to thank someone, mention something specific, and keep the door open. Listen for the new words: appreciate, grateful, reach out, look forward, sincerely, regards, keep in touch, and touch base.

Note the two thanking patterns (Thank you for + -ing, It was + adjective + to + verb). Practice each one out loud as if you are writing to a real contact.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to write a follow-up email"

A short, clear lesson on follow-up language: Thank you for..., It was great to..., I look forward to..., and warm closings like Best regards. Perfect for writing a message that keeps a new professional relationship alive.

After watching, record yourself speaking a full follow-up: thank the person, mention one specific thing you appreciate, keep the door open, and close warmly.
