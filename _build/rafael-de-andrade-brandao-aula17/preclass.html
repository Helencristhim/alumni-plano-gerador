Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Networking Follow-up -- The Thank-You Message

Write a short, warm follow-up after a meeting: thank the person (Thank you for meeting me), mention something specific (It was great to talk with you), and keep the door open (Please feel free to reach out). New vocabulary: appreciate, grateful, reach out, look forward, sincerely, regards, keep in touch, touch base.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Appreciate -- to value something and be thankful for it
"I really appreciate your time." (Valorizar / agradecer)
Grateful -- thankful in a strong, warm way
"I am grateful for your help." (Grato / agradecido)
Reach out -- to contact someone
"Please feel free to reach out any time." (Entrar em contato)
Look forward -- to feel happy about something in the future
"I look forward to working with you." (Aguardar com expectativa)
Sincerely -- in an honest, warm way -- used to close a message
"Sincerely, Rafael." (Atenciosamente)
Regards -- a polite way to end a message
"Best regards, Rafael." (Atenciosamente / cordialmente)
Keep in touch -- to stay in contact over time
"Let us keep in touch." (Manter contato)
Touch base -- to make quick contact to share news
"I will touch base with you next week." (Dar um retorno rápido)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The day after the conference, Rafael writes a follow-up message. He starts with thanks: "Thank you for meeting me yesterday. It was great to talk with you." Then he makes it personal: "I really appreciate your insights, and I am grateful for your time." He keeps the door open: "I look forward to working with you. Please feel free to reach out any time, and let us keep in touch." Finally, he closes warmly: "Best regards, Rafael." A good follow-up is short, sincere, and prompt.

1. How does Rafael start his message?
A By thanking the person for the meeting.
B By asking for a discount.
C By complaining about the meeting.
2. How does Rafael keep the door open?
A He invites the person to reach out any time.
B He says goodbye forever.
C He does not mention the future.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Thank you for meeting me yesterday."
B "Thank you for meet me yesterday."
C "Thank you for to meet me yesterday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Saying Thank You

GRAMMAR

Usado para agradecer em mensagens. / Used to thank someone in a message. Depois de "Thank you for" use a forma -ing; "It was + adjetivo" usa to + verbo; depois de "look forward to" use -ing.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Thank you for + -ingAgradecer uma ação / thank an actionThank you for meeting me.
It was + adjective + to + verbComentar a experiência / comment the experienceIt was great to talk with you.
look forward to + -ingExpectativa positiva / positive futureI look forward to working with you.
Key point / Ponto-chaveDepois de Thank you for e de look forward to, use o verbo com -ing (meeting, working). Em It was + adjetivo, use to + verbo (to talk).
