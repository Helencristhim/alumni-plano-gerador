Chapter 4: The Code

The Phrases That Carry Hard News

Deliver, push back, reframe -- diplomatically

Grammar Discovery

Notice the Diplomatic Moves

"I'm afraid we've hit a setback -- I can't recommend shipping on Friday."

"I completely understand, but I'd rather flag this now than ship a broken release."

"What I can do is give you a fix by Wednesday and a new date today."

"To be candid, it's safer to wait one day than to corrupt customer data."

MoveWhenPhrase
Deliverbreaking unwelcome news"I'm afraid we've hit a setback."
Push backyou disagree, but diplomatically"I understand, but I'd rather flag this now."
Reframepair the problem with a plan"What I can do is..."

You are not sugarcoating and you are not being blunt -- you are candid and diplomatic at the same time.

Language Focus

Name the Move

Common Mistake

Sugarcoat vs Blunt vs Candid

✗ SUGARCOAT

"Everything's basically fine, just a tiny thing, don't worry about it at all."

✓ CANDID

"I'm afraid we've hit a real setback, and here's exactly what it means."

✗ BLUNT

"The release is broken and it's not shipping Friday. That's it."

✓ DIPLOMATIC

"I can't recommend Friday, but what I can do is ship on Thursday."

Don't sugarcoat until the news disappears, and don't be so blunt that the client panics. Be candid, stay tactful, and offer a plan.

Dialogue

Breaking the News to Daniel

D
Hi Ana. We're still on for the Friday release, right? Marketing has the whole campaign booked for that day.
A
Thanks for checking, Daniel. I'm afraid we've hit a setback. We found a critical bug on the payment screen, and I can't recommend shipping on Friday.
D
Ana, we can't move Friday. The campaign goes live that day. Can't we just fix it after launch?
A
I completely understand the deadline matters, but I'd rather flag this now than ship something that corrupts customer data. I won't overpromise a fix I can't guarantee.
D
So what am I supposed to tell marketing?
A
Here's what I can do. We'll have a fix by Wednesday, full testing on Thursday, and a new release Thursday evening -- one day late, not a week.
D
One day I can work with. I just don't want any surprises.
A
No surprises. To be candid, I'd rather tell you today than on Friday. The way I see it, we caught a serious problem before your customers did.
Did You Catch It?

About the Call

1. What is Daniel's main worry about moving the Friday release?
The marketing campaign is already booked to go live that day.
2. What plan does Ana offer instead of shipping on Friday?
A fix by Wednesday, full testing on Thursday, and a new release Thursday evening -- one day late, not a week.
3. How does Daniel react by the end of the call?
He accepts the one-day delay; he just does not want surprises, and Ana reassures him there will be none.

Listening 2

Karen's Coaching

A tip from your team lead. Sound first -- no text.

0:000:00
1. What does Karen say Ana did really well on the call?
She stayed candid without being blunt -- she delivered bad news honestly but kindly -- and she pushed back on the deadline instead of caving and overpromising.
2. What is Karen's tip for next time?
