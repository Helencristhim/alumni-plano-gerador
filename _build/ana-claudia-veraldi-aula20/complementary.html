Lesson 20 -- Navigating Difficult Conversations: Delivering Unwelcome News and Pushing Back Diplomatically

TEDx Talk
The Good News About Giving Bad News (Kate Braestrup, TEDxDirigo)

A moving, practical talk from someone whose job is to deliver the hardest news there is. Her core message is exactly this lesson: bad news handled with honesty and care builds trust, while sugarcoating or dodging it destroys it. Watch how she stays candid and kind at the same time.

Tip: notice how she never softens the truth until it disappears -- she pairs honesty with presence. Try that framing on your next hard update at work.

The Secret to Having Difficult Conversations at Work (Mark Leruste)

A focused talk on the exact situation Ana faced: raising something uncomfortable with a colleague or a client without either avoiding it or blowing it up. He breaks down how to stay diplomatic, push back on what you disagree with, and keep the relationship intact.

Tip: pick one phrase from the talk for opening a hard conversation and rehearse it out loud, the way Ana rehearses "I'm afraid we've hit a setback."

TEDx Talk
Difficult Conversations Made Easy (Joy Baldridge, TEDxUCCI)

A short, tactical talk about the actual words that defuse tension when you have to disagree or deliver something the other person does not want to hear. It pairs perfectly with the diplomatic phrases in this lesson: acknowledge, then hold your line, without being blunt or overpromising.

Tip: write down two phrases from the talk that help you push back politely, and use one on a real request you need to say no to this week.
