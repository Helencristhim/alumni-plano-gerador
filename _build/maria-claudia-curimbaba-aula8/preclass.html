Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Scenario -- a possible situation or sequence of events
"Let us consider a different scenario for the expansion." (Cenário)
Contingency -- a plan for a possible future problem
"We need a contingency in case the deal falls through." (Contingência)
Downside -- the negative side or disadvantage of something
"The downside of the move is the higher cost." (Desvantagem)
Upside -- the positive side or potential advantage
"The upside is a much larger market share." (Vantagem)
Trade-off -- a balance between two things you cannot both have fully
"There is always a trade-off between speed and cost." (Compensação)
Outweigh -- to be greater or more important than something else
"The benefits outweigh the risks." (Superar)
Feasible -- realistically possible to do
"Is the timeline feasible for our team?" (Viável)
Mitigate -- to make something bad less serious
"We can mitigate the risk with a phased rollout." (Mitigar)
Allocate -- to give resources for a specific purpose
"We would allocate more capital to the US plant." (Alocar)
Prioritize -- to put things in order of importance
"We must prioritize the most profitable projects." (Priorizar)
Projection -- an estimate of a future amount or result
"The five-year projection looks promising." (Projeção)
Hypothetical -- based on a possible situation, not a real one
"This is a hypothetical scenario, not a decision." (Hipotético)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Imagine a hypothetical scenario. If we acquired the Pittsburgh plant, we would double our capacity on the East Coast. The upside would be faster delivery; the downside would be a heavy capital allocation. There is always a trade-off. My projection shows that the benefits would outweigh the costs within three years, but only if demand grew. If I were you, I would move in phases to mitigate the risk. Is that feasible? If the board approved a phased plan, we could prioritize the key contracts first and keep a contingency in reserve.

1. Is the situation in the text real or hypothetical?
A Hypothetical -- imaginary and not yet decided.
B Real -- the plant has already been acquired.
C A past event that already happened.
2. When would the benefits outweigh the costs?
A Immediately, with no conditions.
B Within three years, but only if demand grew.
C Never -- the costs are always higher.
3. What does the writer advise to reduce the risk?
A Move in phases to mitigate the risk and keep a contingency.
B Allocate all the capital at once.
C Cancel the proposal entirely.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Use o segundo condicional para propostas e cenários HIPOTÉTICOS ou improváveis: if + passado simples, would/could/might + verbo base. / Use the second conditional for HYPOTHETICAL or unlikely proposals and scenarios: if + past simple, would/could/might + base verb.

FunctionPatternExample
Condition (if-clause)if + past simple"If we acquired the plant..."
Resultwould / could / might + base verb"...we would double our capacity"
Were for all subjectsif + I/he/she/it + were"If I were you, I would wait"
QuestionWhat + would + subject + base verb"What would you do if the deal fell through?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: o if-clause usa o passado simples e o resultado usa would/could/might + verbo base. NUNCA use "would" no if-clause (errado: "if I would acquire"; certo: "if I acquired"). Use were para todos os sujeitos (If I were you). O segundo condicional é hipotético/improvável, não real -- diferente do primeiro condicional (if + presente, will), que descreve condições reais.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "If we acquired the plant, we would double our capacity."
B "If we would acquire the plant, we would double our capacity."
C "If we acquire the plant, we will double our capacity."
You want to give diplomatic advice to a colleague. Which is best?
A "If I were you, I would wait for the forecast."
B "If I was you, I will wait for the forecast."
C "If I am you, I would wait for the forecast."
You want to ask about a worst-case outcome. Which is best?
A "What you would do if the deal fell through?"

C "What will you do if the deal will fall through?"
