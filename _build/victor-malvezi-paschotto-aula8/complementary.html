Lesson 8 -- Negotiating the Deal: Holding Your Ground & Trading Concessions

Series
Suits (USA Network) -- any negotiation scene with Harvey Specter

Harvey rarely raises his voice, yet he wins almost every negotiation. Watch how he opens from common ground, names his non-negotiable calmly, and never gives a concession without taking one back. Notice the trade-offs: every time he gives something, he gets something. It is exactly the give-and-take you practiced in class, played out under pressure.

Tip: pause after each scene and write one trade you saw ("If X, then Y"), plus the one point where the character held firm.

Podcast
Negotiate Anything (Kwame Christian) -- any episode on trading concessions

A practical show on how real professionals hold their ground and stay calm under pressure. Episodes cover how to map common ground, how to trade rather than gift concessions, and how to break a deadlock by changing a variable instead of repeating yourself. It is the exact mindset Victor needs across the table from a firm supplier.

Tip: after listening, draft three lines for one real deal at Brinks: your common ground, one first-conditional trade, and your single non-negotiable.

YouTube
"First Conditional & 'unless / as long as / provided that'" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, compact lesson on the exact structures from this class: if + present simple, will + base verb, plus the connectors unless, as long as and provided that. The presenter shows how each one sets a single condition, and flags the most common error -- putting "will" inside the if-clause, or adding a second negative after "unless".

Tip: watch with a notebook. After each example, write one real trade you could offer a supplier using the same "If you... we will..." pattern.
