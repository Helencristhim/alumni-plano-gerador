Chapter 3: The Code

Right Now vs Routine

I am working · I send

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Right now, I am updating the report."

"The team is testing the connection at the moment."

"I send a status update every Friday."

"We support thirty premium accounts."

What is different about the green verbs?

FormUseExample
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		An action happening now or around nowI am updating the report now.
Present Simple
verb / verb-s		A routine, a habit, or a permanent factI send a report every Friday.
Signal wordsnow, right now, at the moment, currently, this week → continuous · usually, every day, always, often → simple

In speech we shorten it: I am = I'm, we are = we're, it is = it's.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Right now, I work on three accounts."
"Right now, I am working on three accounts."
"We are usually rolling out updates on Fridays."
"We usually roll out updates on Fridays."

Use the Present Continuous for what is happening now (right now, at the moment, currently). Use the Present Simple for routines (usually, every Friday, always).
