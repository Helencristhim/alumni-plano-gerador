Aula 3 -- Status Updates & Ongoing Work

Series
Silicon Valley -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Minimum Viable Product"

The team runs short stand-up meetings where everyone reports what they are working on, what is blocked, and what is on track. Listen for how they use the Present Continuous ("we are building...", "it is running...") to describe ongoing work. Connection to Lesson 3: giving a clear status update and naming blockers and priorities.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences where someone says what they are doing right now, and underline the am/is/are + verb-ing.

YouTube
"Present Continuous vs Present Simple" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's contrast: the Present Continuous for what is happening now (right now, at the moment, currently) versus the Present Simple for routines and facts (every Friday, usually, always). Connection to Lesson 3: this is the grammar that makes your status updates sound correct and confident.

Tip: pause and say one Present Continuous sentence (what you are doing now) and one Present Simple sentence (a weekly routine) about your own work after each example.
