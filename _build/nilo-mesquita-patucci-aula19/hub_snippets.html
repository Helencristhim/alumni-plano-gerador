Partnership -- a long working relationship between two sides

Commitment -- a serious promise to keep doing something
"A long-term commitment protects both clubs." (Compromisso)
Trust -- the belief that the other side will do what they say
"We have been building trust since the first deal." (Confiança)
Track record -- the history of what you have actually achieved
"Our track record shows growth every season." (Histórico de resultados)
Ongoing -- still happening now; not yet finished
"Our campaigns are an ongoing success." (Em andamento / contínuo)
Long-term -- lasting or planned for many years
"We are ready for a long-term commitment." (De longo prazo)
Reliable -- able to be trusted; always does what is promised
"We have been a reliable partner every season." (Confiável)
Proven -- shown to work, with real results over time
"We are a proven partner, not a risk." (Comprovado)
Accountable -- responsible and ready to answer for the results
"We are accountable for every campaign we run." (Responsável / prestável de contas)
Deliverable -- a clear, agreed result you promise to provide
"Every deliverable was completed on time." (Entregável)
Momentum -- strong forward energy that keeps something growing
"The momentum is on our side this season." (Impulso / embalo)
Sustain -- to keep something going strong over time
"A long-term deal helps us sustain the growth." (Sustentar / manter)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the yearly review, Nilo looks back at the whole partnership. To describe a relationship that started in the past and is still going on now, he uses the present perfect continuous: have/has been plus the -ing form. He says, "we have been working together for three seasons", and "the brand has been growing since the first contract." Notice the two little words: for goes with a length of time (for three seasons), and since goes with a starting point (since 2022). Nilo uses this tense to show his track record -- that he has been a reliable and proven partner. Then he uses the past to argue for the future: "we have been delivering results, so a long-term commitment now will sustain the growth."

1. Which form is the present perfect continuous?
A have/has been + verb-ing -- "we have been working".
B have/has + verb -- "we have work".
C will + been -- "we will been working".
2. Which sentence uses "for" and "since" correctly?
A "We have been working since three seasons."
B "We have been working for three seasons, since 2022."
C "We have been working for 2022."
3. Why does Nilo use his track record in the review?
A Only to talk about the past.
B To prove he is reliable and argue for a long-term commitment.
C To end the partnership.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect Continuous

GRAMMAR

Usado para uma ação que começou no passado e ainda continua agora. / Used for an action that started in the past and still continues now.

Subjecthave/has been + -ingfor / since
Wehave been workingfor three seasons.
The brandhas been growingsince 2022.
Wehave been building trustsince the first contract.
Your logohas been reaching fansfor two years.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use have/has + been + verbo-ing para ação que começou no passado e ainda continua. Nunca esqueça o been. For = duração (for three seasons); since = ponto de início (since 2022). / Keep been; for = a length of time, since = a starting point.
