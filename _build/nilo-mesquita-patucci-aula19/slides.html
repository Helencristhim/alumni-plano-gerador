Grammar Discovery

Past start, still going now

Grammar Rule

Present Perfect Continuous

Subjecthave/has been + -ingfor / since
Wehave been workingfor three seasons.
The brandhas been growingevery year.
Wehave been building trustsince the first contract.
Your logohas been reaching fansfor two years.

Key idea: use have/has + been + verb-ing for an action that started in the past and is still going on now. Use have with I/we/you/they and has with he/she/it. For goes with a length of time (for three seasons); since goes with a starting point (since 2022). Perfect for a review -- it shows how long the good work has lasted.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We have working together since three seasons."
"We have been working together for three seasons."

Keep been (have been working). Use for + a length of time, since + a starting point.

Comprehension

About the Briefing

1. What grammar does Sofia recommend for a partnership review, and why?

The present perfect continuous (have been + -ing). It shows how long something has been happening and that it continues -- perfect for a review: "we have been working together for three seasons".

2. What is the difference between "for" and "since"?

"For" goes with a length of time (for three seasons, for two years). "Since" goes with a starting point (since 2022, since the first contract).

3. According to Sofia, what is a partnership review really for?

Not just to look at the past -- to use a strong track record to make the case for the future: "we have been delivering results for three seasons, so the next three will be even better".
