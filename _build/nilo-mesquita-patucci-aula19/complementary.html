Lesson 19 -- Negotiation VI: Reviewing the Partnership

Series
Formula 1: Drive to Survive -- any season review or sponsor scene

Teams and sponsors review long partnerships, talk about their track record, and decide whether to continue. Listen for how people describe an ongoing relationship -- what they have been doing, for how long -- and how they use a strong record to argue for the future.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one present perfect continuous sentence you hear ("they have been...", "the team has been...") and one partnership word from today (track record, reliable, momentum).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Reviewing a Partnership and Making the Case to Continue"

Practical episodes on the language of a partnership review: describing your track record, talking about ongoing work, proving you are reliable, and using your record to argue for a long-term commitment. Exactly today's vocabulary and grammar.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to describe a long relationship. Then record yourself reviewing your Corinthians partnership with "we have been..." for three seasons.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Present Perfect Continuous (have been + -ing, for / since)"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to describe an action that started in the past and continues now, and the difference between "for" (a length of time) and "since" (a starting point).

After watching, make five sentences about your own work or partnership with "I have been..." -- three with "for" and two with "since" -- and say them out loud.
