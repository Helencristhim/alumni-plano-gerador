Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What time is it? -- I get up at seven.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we talk about time and routine?

"What time is it? It is seven o'clock."

"I get up at seven o'clock."

"What time do you go to work?"

"I have dinner at eight o'clock."

Grammar Rule

Telling the Time

FormWhen we use itExample
What time is it?To ask the timeWhat time is it?
It is + number + o'clockTo say the time on the hourIt is seven o'clock.
I + verb + at + timeTo say when you do somethingI get up at seven.
What time do you + verb?To ask when someone does somethingWhat time do you go to bed?

Key idea: ask the time with What time is it? and say when with at + the time.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I get up in seven o'clock."
"I get up at seven o'clock."

Use at for a clock time, and in for a part of the day.
