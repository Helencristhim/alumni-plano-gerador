Key Expressions

Phrases That Guide the Room

"Let me headline the result, then walk you through how we got there." — opens with the big point, then the story
"To put this in context, here is the backdrop." — sets the scene before the detail
"I'd like to draw your attention to this turning point." — signposts the key moment
"Before I move on, let me flag one risk and frame why it's manageable." — raises a concern and shapes how to see it
"If there's one takeaway today, it's this — and I want it to land." — closes on a single clear point
Chapter 3

Telling the Story

Narrative tenses & the dramatic present

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Layers

Same story — why does the speaker switch forms?

By March, we had rebuilt the pipeline. (the backstory — before the main events)

The market was sliding while we kept investing. (the scene — what was going on)

Then we launched the new route. (the key event — what happened)

So we go live, and within a week the orders double. (the vivid moment — present, but it's the past)

Each layer of the story has its own tense.

Grammar Rule

The Narrative Toolkit

Job in the storyTenseExample
The backstory (before)Past perfect: had + past participle"By March we had rebuilt the pipeline."
The scene (in progress)Past continuous: was/were + -ing"The market was sliding while we invested."
The key eventsPast simple"Then we launched the new route in March."
The vivid momentDramatic (historic) present"So we go live, and the orders double."

The past has layers:

Use the past perfect for what happened before the main events, the past continuous for the background that was in progress, and the past simple for the events themselves. Then switch to the dramatic present for the one moment you want the board to feel — "so we go live, and the orders double" — but use it sparingly, only for the turning point. Signpost as you move so nobody gets lost.

Common Mistake

Muddled vs Clear

✗ WRONG TENSE

"We have launched the new route last March."

✓ FINISHED PAST TIME

"We launched the new route last March."

✗ ORDER UNCLEAR

"When I presented, the market already recovered."

✓ EARLIER EVENT FIRST

"When I presented, the market had already recovered."

With a finished past time (last March, in Q1), use the past simple. For the event that happened earlier, use the past perfect (had + participle).
