Lesson 11 -- Presenting to the Board: Slides & Stories

Series
Mad Men (AMC) -- any episode with a Don Draper client pitch (e.g. "The Wheel", S1E13)

Don Draper is a master of the presentation as a story: he sets a backdrop, builds to one turning point, and lands a single takeaway that the room never forgets. Watch how he headlines the idea, walks the client through it, and switches into a vivid, present-tense moment to make them feel it — exactly the craft from this class.

Tip: pause after a pitch and write down the storyline in three lines — backdrop, turning point, takeaway. Notice where he switches to the present.

Podcast
Think Fast, Talk Smart (Stanford GSB) -- any episode on storytelling or structuring a message

Matt Abrahams breaks down how executives structure a message so it lands: lead with the headline, give the backdrop, signpost the path, and end on one takeaway. It is the exact playbook Victor needs before standing in front of the board at Brink's.

Tip: after listening, draft three signposting lines for a real presentation: an opening headline, one "let me drill down" transition, and a closing takeaway.

YouTube
Nancy Duarte -- "The secret structure of great talks" (TEDxEast)

Nancy Duarte shows that the best presentations are built like stories: they move between "what is" and "what could be", build to a turning point, and end on one memorable takeaway. A clear, practical map for turning a board deck into a compelling storyline.

Tip: watch with a notebook and sketch the backdrop, turning point, and takeaway of one presentation you have to give at work.
