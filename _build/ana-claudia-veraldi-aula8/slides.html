Chapter 4: The Code

Four Tenses, One Review

Choose the tense by the kind of time

Grammar Discovery

Notice the Time

"I have tested many features this block."

"In week five, I tested a difficult feature."

"Every morning I check the backlog."

"Right now I am working on an edge case."

TenseUseExample
Present perfectExperience up to now (no exact time)I have tested many features this block.
Past simpleFinished action at a past timeIn week five, I tested a feature.
Present simpleA routine or habitEvery morning I check the backlog.
Present continuousIn progress right nowRight now I am working on an edge case.

Choose the tense by the time: up to now, a finished moment, a routine, or right now.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Sentence

Say the whole sentence out loud, then tap to check

"I ___have tested three features this block." (experience up to now)
"In week five, I ___tested a hard feature." (finished past time)
"Every morning I ___check the backlog." (routine)
"Right now I ___am working on an edge case." (in progress now)
"I ___followed up until the blocker was fixed." (finished past)
Language Focus

Which Tense?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I have tested it yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"I tested it yesterday."

✗ WRONG

"Every morning I am checking the backlog."

✓ RIGHT

"Every morning I check the backlog."

With a finished past time (yesterday, week five), use the past simple, not the present perfect. For a routine, use the present simple, not the present continuous.

Dialogue

The Review with Marcus

M
So, Ana, tell me about your first block. What are you proud of, and what was hard?
A
I have learned a lot. My milestone was week five, when I helped deploy a difficult feature on time.
M
That is great. And your daily workflow? How do you decide what to test first?
A
Every morning I check the backlog and I prioritize the bugs by severity. Then I look for the root cause.
M
And the hard part? You said something was difficult.
A
My biggest challenge was a blocker in week three. The environment broke, but I raised it and I followed up until it was fixed.
M
Well done. One tip for the next block: when a requirement is not clear, clarify it early, and think about the edge cases.
A
Thank you, Marcus. I will clarify early and check the edge cases. I am ready for Block 2.
