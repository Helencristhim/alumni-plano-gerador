Challenge -- a difficult task that tests your skills

To prioritize -- to decide what is most important to do first
"I have learned to prioritize the bugs by severity."
Severity -- how serious a bug is
"I sort the bugs by severity, from critical to minor."
Root cause -- the real reason a problem happens
"When a test fails, I look for the root cause first."
Blocker -- something that stops you from making progress
"A broken environment was a blocker for a whole day."
To follow up -- to check on something again later
"I raised the blocker and followed up until it was fixed."
To clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"Could you clarify what you mean by this requirement?"
Edge case -- an unusual situation at the limit of what is expected
"Marcus told me to think about the edge cases early."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

In her review, Ana used three tenses without thinking about them. She said, "I have tested many features this block" — the present perfect, for her experience up to now. She said, "In week five, I tested a difficult feature" — the past simple, for a finished moment. And she said, "Every morning I check the backlog" — the present simple, for her routine. When Marcus asked what she was doing at that moment, she answered, "Right now I am working on an edge case" — the present continuous.

1. Why does Ana say "I have tested many features this block" and not "I tested"?
A The block is still going, so she uses the present perfect for experience up to now.
B Both are wrong; she should use the present continuous.
C "Have tested" is only for the future.
2. "In week five, I tested a difficult feature" uses the past simple because:
A it is a daily routine.
B it is a finished action at a specific past time.
C it is happening right now.
3. Which sentence describes something in progress at the moment of speaking?
A "Every morning I check the backlog."
B "In week five, I tested a feature."
C "Right now I am working on an edge case."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Block 1 Tenses

GRAMMAR

One review meeting, four tenses. Choose the tense by the kind of time.

TenseUseExample
Present perfectExperience up to now (no exact time)I have tested many features this block.
Past simpleA finished action at a specific past timeIn week five, I tested a hard feature.
Present simpleA routine or habitEvery morning I check the backlog.
Present continuousIn progress right nowRight now I am working on an edge case.
Common mistakeDo not say "I have tested it yesterday"; with a past time, use the past simple: "I tested it yesterday."
