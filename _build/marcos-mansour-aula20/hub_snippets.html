Stage 20.1: Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Aprenda as palavras do telefone e da videoconferência. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Dial -- discar (to enter a phone number to call)
"Please dial the international code first."
Caller -- quem liga (the person making the call)
"The caller did not leave a name."
Extension -- ramal (an internal number for a person at a company)
"You can reach me on extension 204."
Voicemail -- correio de voz (a recorded message you leave)
"I left a voicemail, but he hasn't called back."
Hang up -- desligar (to end a phone call)
"Don't hang up -- I'll transfer you now."
On hold -- na espera (waiting on a paused phone line)
"They put me on hold for ten minutes."
Get through -- conseguir falar / completar a ligação (to reach someone by phone)
"I tried all morning, but I couldn't get through."
Mute -- mudo (to turn your microphone off)
"You're on mute -- we can't hear you."
Share screen -- compartilhar a tela (to show your screen on a video call)
"Let me share my screen so you can see the figures."
Breaking up -- falhando / cortando (when the connection cuts in and out)
"Sorry, you're breaking up -- could you repeat that?"
Background noise -- ruído de fundo (other sounds during a call)
"There's a lot of background noise -- let me move to a quiet room."
Speak up -- falar mais alto (to talk more loudly)
"Could you speak up a little? You're very quiet."

Stage 20.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

After the call, Marcos passes on the message to his partner. "Rachel called," he says. "She told me she was happy with the proposal, and she said she would send a meeting link this afternoon. David asked if we could move our call to Friday, because he said he was stuck in another meeting." We use reported speech to tell someone what another person said. We use say with no person and tell with a person (told me). For questions, we use ask if / whether. The verb usually steps back one tense: is → was, will → would, can → could.

1. Qual verbo usamos COM uma pessoa (told me)?
A say (say me).
B tell (tell me / told me).
C ask (ask me that).
2. "I will call" reported becomes:
A He said he would call.
B He said he will call.
C He said he calls.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "He asked if I am free."
B "He asked if I was free."
C "He asked am I free."

Stage 20.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Como reportar o que alguém disse, com say, tell e ask if (inglês e português).

Forma / FormEstrutura / StructureExample
say (sem pessoa / no person)said (that) + fraseHe said he would call back.
tell (com pessoa / + person)told + pessoa + (that) + fraseShe told me the meeting was on Friday.
pergunta (question)asked + if / whether + fraseHe asked if I was available.
backshift (um passo atrás)is→was, will→would, can→could, have→had"I can hear you" → She said she could hear me.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação de chamada.

You answer a work call. You say:
A "Who are you?"
B "Marcos speaking. How can I help?"
C "What do you want?"
You want to report David's words: "I will be late." You say:
A "He said me he will be late."
B "He said he would be late."
C "He told he will be late."
A colleague's microphone is off. You say:
A "You're on mute -- we can't hear you."
B "You're on hold -- we can't hear you."
C "You hang up -- we can't hear you."
You want to report a question: "Are you free on Friday?" You say:
A "He asked if I was free on Friday."
B "He asked if I am free on Friday."
C "He asked am I free on Friday."
