Grammar Rule

Defining Relative Clauses

Nounwho / which / thatFull sentence (it defines which one)
a thing: the clausewhich / that + verbThis is the clause which sets out the fee.
a thing: the sectionthat + verbIt is the section that covers exclusivity.
a person: the partywho + verbThe party who signs first must keep a copy.
a person: the directorwho + verbI called the director who signed the deal.

Key idea: use who for people and which or that for things. The relative clause comes right after the noun it describes and tells the listener exactly which one you mean -- perfect for pointing to one clause in a long contract.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The party which signs first must keep a copy."
"The party who signs first must keep a copy."

Use who for people and which or that for things. Never use which for a person.

Grammar Practice

Who, Which or That?

"It is the section _____that covers exclusivity in Brazil." (a thing)
"I called the director _____who had signed off on the deal." (a person)

About the Briefing

1. According to Sofia, what is a contract built from, and what does each one cover?

Clauses -- each clause is one numbered section that covers one topic: the payment, the term, or the exclusivity.

2. Which relative words does Sofia say to use to describe a clause clearly?

Who for people and which or that for things -- "the party who signs first", "the clause which protects our logo".

Artefact

The Sponsorship Contract

SPONSORSHIP AGREEMENT -- FIRST DRAFT

Parties: Sport Club Corinthians Paulista & the Sponsor -- Dr. Nilo Patussi

Clause 1 -- The Fee

The clause which sets out the yearly fee, paid across the season. Correct.

Clause 2 -- The Term

The section that covers the length of the deal: four seasons. Correct.

Clause 3 -- Logo Rights

The clause which gives the sponsor logo rights in any country, with no limit. Too wide -- ask to amend to Brazil only.

Clause 4 -- Liability

The part that says who pays if something goes wrong puts all liability on the club. Ask to share it fifty-fifty.

Clause 5 -- Termination & Signing

The clause that lets either party terminate the deal after a breach. Once valid, both parties sign before a witness.

Dialogue

Discussing the Clauses

M

"Here is the first draft. The fee and the term look fine. Are you happy to sign today?"

N

"Not quite. The clause which gives you logo rights in any country is too wide. We only agreed to Brazil. I must ask you to amend it before I sign."

M

"Fair point. We will limit it to Brazil. Is there anything else?"

N

"Yes. The part that covers liability puts all the risk on the club. I would like to share the liability fifty-fifty, and to add a termination clause for any breach."

M

"Agreed. We will amend those clauses. The director who signs for us will be here tomorrow."

N

"Perfect. Once the draft is clean and valid, both parties sign before a witness, and the agreement is binding."
