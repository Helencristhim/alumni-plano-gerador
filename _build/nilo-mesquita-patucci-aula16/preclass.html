Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clause -- one numbered section of a contract, about one topic
"The clause about the fee is on page two." (Cláusula)
Party -- a person or company that signs the contract
"Both parties signed the agreement." (Parte / contratante)
Draft -- an early version of a document, before it is final
"Our lawyers sent over the first draft." (Minuta / rascunho)
Breach -- when one side breaks the rules of the agreement
"Missing the payment date is a breach of contract." (Quebra / violação)
Liability -- the legal responsibility you carry if something goes wrong
"The liability clause says who pays for the damage." (Responsabilidade)
Terminate -- to end a contract officially
"Either party can terminate the deal after a breach." (Rescindir / encerrar)
Amend -- to change or correct a clause before signing
"I asked him to amend the clause before anyone signed." (Alterar / emendar)
Valid -- legally correct and in force
"I checked that the agreement was valid before signing." (Válido)
Void -- with no legal power at all -- the opposite of valid
"Without a signature, the contract is void." (Nulo / sem efeito)
Binding -- legally forcing both sides to obey it
"Once both parties sign, the contract is binding." (Vinculante / obrigatório)
Witness -- a person who watches the signing and confirms it happened
"A witness was present when both parties signed." (Testemunha)
Indemnify -- to promise to cover another party's loss or cost
"The sponsor agreed to indemnify the club for any fine." (Indenizar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

When Nilo reads a contract, he points to one clause at a time and says exactly what it does. To do this, he uses a defining relative clause -- a small clause that defines which one he means. He uses who for people: "the party who signs first must keep a copy." He uses which or that for things: "this is the clause which sets out the fee", "it is the section that covers exclusivity". The relative word comes right after the noun it describes. If a clause is wrong -- for example, the part that covers liability puts all the risk on the club -- he asks the party to amend it before anyone signs. Only when the draft is clean and the agreement is valid do both parties sign before a witness.

1. Which relative word do we use for a person?
A who -- "the party who signs first".
B which -- "the party which signs first".
C what -- "the party what signs first".
2. Which sentence describes a clause correctly?
A This is the clause who sets out the fee.
B This is the clause which sets out the fee.
C This is the clause sets out the fee.
3. If a clause is wrong, what does Nilo do before signing?
A He signs anyway and complains later.
B He asks the party to amend it before anyone signs.
C He cancels the whole deal.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Defining Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Usado para dizer EXATAMENTE qual cláusula ou pessoa você quer dizer. / Used to say exactly which clause or person you mean.

Noun / Substantivowho / which / thatFull sentence / Frase completa
a thing / coisa: the clausewhich / thatThis is the clause which sets out the fee.
a thing / coisa: the sectionthatIt is the section that covers exclusivity.
a person / pessoa: the partywhoThe party who signs first keeps a copy.
a person / pessoa: the directorwhoI called the director who signed the deal.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use who para pessoas e which ou that para coisas. O relative clause vem logo depois do substantivo e diz exatamente qual você quer dizer -- nunca use which para uma pessoa. / Use who for people, which/that for things; the clause comes right after the noun and tells exactly which one.
