Stage 1.1: Email Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight words for the parts and tone of a professional email. Listen to each one.

Register -- the level of formality of language, from casual to very formal
"For a senior partner, use a formal register." (Registro / nível de formalidade)
Recipient -- the person who receives a message or email
"Always picture the recipient before you write." (Destinatário)
Salutation -- the greeting at the start of an email, such as "Dear Mr. Costa"
"A formal salutation sets the tone from the first line." (Saudação de abertura)
Sign-off -- the polite closing line before your name, such as "Best regards"
"Close with a sign-off that matches the register." (Despedida / fechamento)
Concise -- saying what is needed in few, clear words
"Keep the email concise so the reader gets the point fast." (Conciso / direto)
Attachment -- a file sent together with an email
"The proposal is in the attachment below." (Anexo)
Courteous -- polite and respectful toward the reader
"A courteous email opens a door; a blunt one closes it." (Cortês / educado)
Thread -- a connected series of replies in the same email conversation
"Please keep your reply in this thread." (Fio / encadeamento de emails)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduardo has to email a senior partner he has never met, so he thinks about register first. To a close colleague he would write, "Send me the contract." But to this recipient that line is too blunt, so he softens it: "Could you send the signed contract by Friday?" He adds a second request even more politely: "I would appreciate it if you could confirm the date." He opens with a courteous salutation, "Dear Mr. Costa," keeps the message concise, points to the proposal in the attachment, and ends with a clear sign-off: "Best regards." Same message, higher register.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Para subir o registro de um email, troque o imperativo direto por um pedido amortecido. Quanto mais longo e indireto o amortecedor, mais formal o tom. Combine o registro com o destinatário. / To raise the register of an email, swap a bare imperative for a softened request — the longer and more indirect the softener, the more formal the tone.

RegisterSoftenerExample
Direct / informalimperative"Send me the contract."
PoliteCould you...?"Could you send the contract?"
FormalI was wondering if you could..."I was wondering if you could confirm the date."
Very formalI would appreciate it if you could..."I would appreciate it if you could reply by Friday."

Key point / Ponto-chave: diga "I would appreciate it if" (com o it) e "Would you mind sending" (com -ing, nunca "mind to send"). O register deve combinar com o recipient: colega próximo aceita "Could you...?"; sócio sênior pede algo mais indireto.
