Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Check-in -- the desk where you show your ticket and hand over your suitcase
"Check-in opens two hours before the flight." (Check-in)
Boarding pass -- the card or ticket that lets you get on the plane
"Please have your boarding pass ready." (Cartão de embarque)
Gate -- the door at the airport where you get on your flight
"We are boarding at gate twenty-six." (Portão)
Security -- the area where staff check your bags and your body before the gate
"Take off your belt before security." (Segurança / raio-x)
Carry-on -- a small bag you take with you into the cabin of the plane
"My laptop stays in my carry-on." (Bagagem de mão)
Overhead bin -- the closed shelf above the seats where you store your bag
"Your bag must fit in the overhead bin." (Compartimento superior)
Aisle -- the path you walk along between the rows of seats
"I prefer an aisle seat, so I can stand up easily." (Corredor)
Window seat -- the seat right next to the window of the plane
"Could I have a window seat, please?" (Assento na janela)
Passport control -- the point where officers check your passport when you enter a country
"There was a long line at passport control." (Controle de passaportes)
Customs -- the place where officers check the things you bring into a country
"I had nothing to declare at customs." (Alfândega)
Connecting flight -- a second flight you take to reach your final destination
"My connecting flight to Belem leaves at noon." (Voo de conexão)
Delayed -- happening later than the planned time
"Our flight is delayed by twenty minutes." (Atrasado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rubens arrives at the airport for a medical conference. At check-in, he says, "Could I have a window seat, please?" The agent gives him seat fourteen A and his boarding pass. Rubens has one suitcase and one small bag. He asks, "Can I take this bag as a carry-on?" The agent says it is fine, as long as it fits in the overhead bin. Then Rubens asks for directions: "Where can I find gate twenty-six, and could you tell me where security is?" The agent also tells him that boarding is delayed by twenty minutes.

1. Why is Rubens at the airport?
A He is travelling to a medical conference.
B He is picking up a colleague.
C He works at the check-in desk.
2. How does Rubens ask for a seat and information?
A He demands a seat without asking.
B He uses polite requests: Could I have...? and Where can I...?
C He says nothing and just waits.
3. What does the agent tell him about boarding?
A Boarding is early.
B Boarding is delayed by twenty minutes.
C The flight is cancelled.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite requests (Could I / Can I / Where can I)

GRAMMAR

Para pedidos educados usamos Could I / Can I + verbo na base; Could é um pouco mais educado que Can. Para pedir informação, usamos Where can I / Could you tell me + verbo na base. / For polite requests we use Could I / Can I + base verb; Could is a little more polite than Can. To ask for information, we use Where can I / Could you tell me + base verb.

Form / FormaExample / Exemplo
Request: Could I + base verb?Could I have a window seat, please? / Eu poderia ter um assento na janela?
Permission: Can I + base verb?Can I take this bag as a carry-on? / Posso levar esta mala como bagagem de mão?
Information: Where can I + base verb?Where can I find gate twenty-six? / Onde posso encontrar o portão vinte e seis?
Asking someone: Could you + base verb?Could you tell me when boarding starts? / Você poderia me dizer quando começa o embarque?

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de Could / Can / Where can / Could you, use sempre o verbo na base (have, take, find -- nunca "to have"). Em Where can I...?, o verbo can vem antes de I.
