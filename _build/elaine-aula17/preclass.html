Sightseeing -- Tickets and Tours

Book a city tour: sightseeing, landmark, tour, guide, attraction, admission, brochure, souvenir. Structures: "I would like to...", "When does...?", "What time does... open?" and "How much is admission?"

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Sightseeing -- visiting famous places in a city
"We love sightseeing in New York." (Turismo / passeio)
Landmark -- a famous building or place tourists visit
"The bridge is a famous landmark." (Ponto de referência / marco)
Tour -- a visit with a group to see places
"I would like to book a tour." (Passeio guiado / tour)
Guide -- the person who shows you the city
"The guide is included." (Guia)
Attraction -- a place people enjoy visiting
"The park is a popular attraction." (Atração)
Admission -- the price to enter a place
"How much is admission?" (Entrada / ingresso)
Brochure -- a small book with photos and information
"Do you have a brochure?" (Folheto)
Souvenir -- a small gift you buy to remember a trip
"I want to buy a souvenir." (Lembrança / souvenir)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Elaine is at a tour office. She says, I would like to book a tour. There is a half-day tour and a full-day tour. She asks, When does the tour start? It starts at ten. How much is admission to the museum? It is included, and the guide is included too. Elaine takes a brochure of the attractions and buys one ticket.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests & Time Questions

GRAMMAR

Pedir educadamente e perguntar horarios. / Asking politely and asking times.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
I would like to + verbPedido educado / A polite requestI would like to book a tour.
When does + it + verb?Perguntar o horario / Ask the timeWhen does the tour start?
What time does + it + open?Perguntar abertura / Ask opening timeWhat time does the museum open?

Lembre: depois de would like use to + verbo. Nas perguntas de horario use does. / After "would like" use "to + verb". In time questions, use "does".
