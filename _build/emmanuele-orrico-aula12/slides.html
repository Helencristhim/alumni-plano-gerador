Chapter 4: The Code

Going to + Base Verb

We are going to launch · The rollout is going to happen · I am going to present.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"In July, we are going to start the kickoff." — we

"The rollout is going to happen in three phases." — one thing

"I am going to present the plan to Paris." — I

"The team is going to expand the program." — base verb

All four are correct. Look at the purple words — what changes, and what stays the same?

Subjectam / is / are + going toBase verb
Iam going topresent the plan.
The rollout / the team (one)is going tohappen · expand.
We / they (many)are going tolaunch · start.
When to use itFor a plan that is already decided: the kickoff, the launch, the rollout, the forecast. The verb after going to never changes: going to launch, never "going to launches" or "going to launched".

You already used going to for your plans. Today it becomes the language of the whole business plan — one pattern, from the kickoff to the forecast.

Language Focus

One Plan, One Pattern

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"In August we are going to launches the higher dose."
"In August we are going to launch the higher dose."
"We is going to start the kickoff in July."
"We are going to start the kickoff in July."
"The rollout is going happen in three phases."
"The rollout is going to happen in three phases."

After going to, the verb stays in its base form: going to launch, never "launches". The am / is / are follows the subject, and the to never disappears.

Dialogue

The Pre-Presentation Call

M
Emmanuele, thank you. So, walk me through it. What is the team going to do next quarter?
E
Here is the plan for Brazil. In July, we are going to start the kickoff of the new initiative.
M
Good. And the launch? When is that going to happen?
E
In August. That is the biggest milestone of the quarter. The rollout is going to happen in three phases, region by region.
M
And the numbers? What is the forecast for new patients?
E
The forecast is strong, and the budget is approved. We are also going to expand the program to two new regions.
M
One hard question: what is going to happen if a target is not met?
E
If a target is not met, we are going to adjust the timeline and focus on two key regions. I am going to present all of this in ninety days.
