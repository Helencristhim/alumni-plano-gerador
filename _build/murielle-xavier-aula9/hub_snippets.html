Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Incident -- an unexpected problem or event (incidente / ocorrência)
"There was an incident with the system on Friday."
Crisis -- a serious, urgent problem (crise)
"We stayed calm during the crisis."
Disruption -- an interruption to normal work (interrupção)
"The disruption lasted two hours."
Breakdown -- a sudden failure of a system or machine (pane / colapso)
"It was a complete breakdown of the payroll system."
Complaint -- a statement that something is wrong (reclamação / queixa)
"I was handling the complaints all morning."
Handover -- passing work or responsibility to someone else (passagem / repasse)
"They were in the middle of a handover."
Grievance -- a formal complaint from an employee (queixa formal)
"I was investigating a grievance last week."
Misconduct -- bad or unacceptable behavior at work (má conduta)
"It was not a case of serious misconduct."
Dispute -- a serious disagreement between people (disputa / conflito)
"There was a dispute in the sales team."
Investigation -- the act of examining a problem carefully (investigação / apuração)
"The investigation is closed now."
Witness -- a person who saw what happened (testemunha)
"I spoke to several witnesses."
Resolve -- to solve a problem or end a conflict (resolver / solucionar)
"We resolved the dispute yesterday."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Last Friday there was an incident at Minerva. At nine, the whole team was working normally: Liam was reviewing the payroll and two managers were interviewing candidates. Then, while they were working, the main system suddenly crashed. It was a total breakdown. When the system went down, three employees were in the middle of a handover, and they lost their notes. While the IT team was fixing the problem, Liam was handling the complaints. It took two hours, but they resolved it, and the crisis is over now.

1. Why does the text say "the team was working" and not "the team worked"?
A Because "worked" is incorrect English.
B It describes an action in progress (the background) when the incident happened.
C Because the action is in the present.
2. In "while they were working, the system crashed", which action interrupts the other?
A "crashed" (past simple) interrupts "were working" (background).
B "were working" interrupts "crashed".
C Both actions are finished and separate.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Two managers was interviewing candidates."
B "Two managers were interviewing candidates."
C "Two managers were interview candidates."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous

GRAMMAR

O past continuous descreve uma ação em andamento no passado. Forma: was (I/he/she/it) ou were (you/we/they) + verbo com -ing. Usamos while para a ação de fundo (longa) e o past simple para a ação que interrompe (curta): "While we were working, the system crashed."

FormStructureExample
Affirmativewas/were + verb-ing"The team was working at nine."
Negativewas/were + not + verb-ing"She was not taking notes."
QuestionWas/Were + subject + verb-ing?"Were you presenting?"
Interruptedwhile + past continuous, past simple"While we were working, it crashed."

Atenção: use was para I/he/she/it e were para you/we/they. Nunca esqueça o was/were antes do verbo-ing.
