Lesson 9 -- When It Was Happening -- Past Continuous

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant -- an episode on handling workplace conflict or crisis

Adam Grant and his guests describe real situations: what was happening when a conflict started and how people resolved it. You will hear the past continuous used to set the scene of an incident. Direct connection to Lesson 9: telling the story of a dispute or crisis.

Tip: write down 5 sentences with "was/were + verb-ing" and one interruption in the past simple.

Series
The Newsroom -- any scene during a breaking-news crisis in the control room

Watch a scene where a crisis unfolds and people describe what was happening: who was doing what when the incident started. Notice the past continuous for background actions. Connection to Lesson 9: describing an unfolding situation.

Tip: pause and narrate the scene: "While the team was working, the story broke."

YouTube
"Past Continuous vs Past Simple" -- BBC Learning English

A clear lesson on the past continuous (was/were + verb-ing), the difference from the past simple, and how to use while and when for interrupted actions. Connection to Lesson 9: the exact grammar you use to describe an incident.

Tip: pause and build your own sentences with breakdown, dispute, and investigation.
