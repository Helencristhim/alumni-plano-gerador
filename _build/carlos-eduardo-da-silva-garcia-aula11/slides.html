Chapter 3: Present Continuous

I am working. She is learning.

am / is / are + verb-ing · now vs routine

Discover the Grammar

Listen and Notice

"I am updating the checklist right now."

"She is learning the process this week."

"We are planning the next visit."

"Are you working from the office today?"

FormHowExample
Iam + verb-ingI am working from the office.
He / She / Itis + verb-ingShe is learning the checklist.
You / We / Theyare + verb-ingWe are planning the visit.
Negativeam / is / are + notI am not visiting farms today.
QuestionAm / Is / Are + subject + -ingAre you working today?
routine: I check every day now: I am checking right now

Present continuous = an action in progress now or this week. Present simple = a routine (every day). / Present continuous = acao em andamento agora; present simple = rotina.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I working on the report."

✓ RIGHT

"I am working on the report."

✗ WRONG

"I am updateing the checklist."

✓ RIGHT

"I am updating the checklist."

Always use am / is / are before verb-ing. Verbs ending in -e drop it: update -> updating. / Use sempre am/is/are antes do verbo-ing; verbo terminado em -e perde o -e.
