Chapter 3

The Question Engine

How to build follow-up questions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"What kind of projects do you work on?"

"How long have you been in IT?"

"How did you get into that?"

"What do you enjoy most about it?"

What do all these questions have in common?

Grammar Rule

The Structure

TypeStructureExample
Wh- follow-up (present)Wh- + do/does + subject + verbWhat do you work on?
Wh- follow-up (past)Wh- + did + subject + verbHow did you get into that?
Reply question (interest)Auxiliary + subject?"I work in IT." — "Oh, do you?"
Key pointMatch the auxiliary to the tense: do/does for the present, did for the past. A short reply question shows you are interested.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"How did you got into that?"

✓ RIGHT

"How did you get into that?"

✗ WRONG

"What you do in your free time?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you do in your free time?"

After "did", the main verb stays in its base form. Every Wh- question needs an auxiliary: do, does, or did.

Listening

The Host’s Welcome

0:000:00

To have a few real conversations, not to collect business cards.
2. What should you do after someone tells you what they do?
Ask a follow-up question, such as how they got into it or what they enjoy most.

The person who was curious about them, not the one who only talked about themselves.

You do not need perfect English — you need good questions, and you should ask one more thing.

Chapter 5

Keep It Going

Put it all together

Quick Fire

Your Move

1 / 5

1. Someone says: "I work in marketing." Ask a follow-up question.

"Oh, do you? What kind of marketing do you work on?"

2. Someone says: "I just moved to Berlin." React and ask one more thing.

"Really? How did you end up there?"

3. There is a silence. Change the topic politely.

"By the way, how do you know the host?"

4. You discover you both love hiking. Say it.

"Oh, we have something in common — I love hiking too!"

5. It is time to end the conversation. Wrap it up nicely.

"It was great to meet you. Feel free to reach out — I look forward to staying in touch."
