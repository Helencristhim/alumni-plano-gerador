Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Follow-up question -- a question that builds on the answer someone gave (PT: pergunta de continuação)
"She told me about her job, so I asked a follow-up question."
Small talk -- light, friendly conversation about easy topics (PT: conversa leve / bate-papo)
"We made small talk about the city before the meeting."
Have something in common -- to share an interest or experience (PT: ter algo em comum)
"We both love hiking, so we had something in common."
Get along with -- to have a good relationship with someone (PT: se dar bem com)
"I get along with everyone on the international team."
By the way -- a phrase to add or gently change the topic (PT: a propósito / aliás)
"By the way, how was your trip?"
Bring up -- to start talking about a new topic (PT: trazer um assunto / mencionar)
"He brought up the new project and we talked about it."
Look forward to -- to feel excited about a future thing, + noun or -ing (PT: estar ansioso por / aguardar)
"I look forward to working with you."
Mutual -- shared by two people (PT: mútuo / em comum)
"It turned out we had a mutual contact."
Reach out -- to contact someone, often first (PT: entrar em contato)
"Feel free to reach out if you have any questions."
Wrap up -- to finish or end something nicely (PT: encerrar / finalizar)
"Before we wrap up, let me give you my card."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At the mixer, Daniela met Mark. He said he worked in cloud security, so Daniela asked a follow-up question: "How did you get into that?" Mark explained that he started in support. "Oh, really?" Daniela said. "We have something in common — I started in support too. What do you enjoy most about it?" The conversation kept going. Later, Daniela brought up a new topic: "By the way, how do you know the host?" Before they left, Mark said, "We should stay in touch." Daniela smiled: "I look forward to it. Feel free to reach out."

1. Why did Daniela ask "How did you get into that?"
A It is a follow-up question to keep the conversation going.
B Because she wanted to end the conversation.
C Because she did not understand Mark.
2. Why does Daniela use "did" in "How did you get into that?"
A Because the action is happening now.
B Because she is asking about the past.
C Because "did" is always used in questions.
3. What is the function of "By the way" in the text?
A To say goodbye.
B To repeat the same topic.
C To introduce a new topic in the conversation.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Follow-up Questions

GRAMMAR

Como construir perguntas de continuação (bilingue EN + PT).

TipoStructureExample
PresentWh- + do/does + subject + verbWhat do you work on?
PastWh- + did + subject + verb (base)How did you start?
Reply questionauxiliary + subject?"I work in IT." — "Oh, do you?"
Regra (PT)Toda pergunta precisa de um auxiliar: do/does (presente) ou did (passado). Depois de did, o verbo fica na forma base (get, não got). A reply question curta mostra interesse.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

Someone says: "I work in data science." How do you keep the conversation going?
A "OK." (and stay silent)
B "Oh, do you? What kind of projects do you work on?"
C "Nice. Anyway, I have to go."
There is an awkward silence. What is the best move?
A "By the way, how do you know the host?"
B Look at your phone in silence.
C "I do not know what to say."
You both work in technology. How do you mention it?
A "You work in tech. I work in tech."
B "It sounds like we have something in common — I am in tech too."
