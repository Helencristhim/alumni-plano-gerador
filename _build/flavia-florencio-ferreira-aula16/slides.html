Chapter 4: Describe Your Organization

who · that · where

Add information with relative clauses

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

I am a representative who leads international missions.

Sebrae is an institution that supports small businesses.

Milan is the city where the event takes place.

WordUse it forExample
whopeoplea representative who leads missions
that / whichorganizations and thingsan institution that supports business
whereplacesthe city where the event takes place

Use these short chunks to add one piece of information. Say "an institution that..." (not "who"), and "a partner who..." (not "which").

Language Focus

Complete the Introduction

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"An institution who supports business."

✓ RIGHT

"An institution that supports business."

✗ WRONG

"A partner which shares our vision."

✓ RIGHT

"A partner who shares our vision."

Use "that" or "which" for organizations and things, and "who" for people. Keep the chunk short and clear.

Dialogue

Flavia and Marco

M
Good morning. I am Marco, from the Italian delegation. Nice to meet you.
F
Good morning, Marco. My name is Flavia. I am a representative of Sebrae, an institution that supports small businesses.
M
Interesting. Where is your headquarters?
F
Our headquarters is in Brazil, but we work with partners in many countries.
M
And what does Sebrae offer, exactly?
F
We offer a portfolio of programs that helps small companies grow. Sebrae has a strong reputation in this sector.
M
I would like to explore an affiliation between our two groups.
F
It is a pleasure to meet a partner who shares our vision. Let me explain the protocol for a bilateral project.
