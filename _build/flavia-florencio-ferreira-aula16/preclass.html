Lesson 16 -- Pre-class

First Impressions in Milan -- Introducing Yourself and Your Organization

Make a strong first impression at an international event, with no translator. Key words: institution, representative, portfolio, bilateral, protocol, affiliation, delegation, headquarters, sector, reputation. Grammar: relative clauses as chunks (who / that / where).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Institution -- an organization created for a public purpose
"Sebrae is an institution that supports small businesses."
Representative -- a person who speaks or acts for an organization
"I am a representative of Sebrae."
Portfolio -- the full range of programs or products an organization offers
"We offer a portfolio of programs for small companies."
Bilateral -- between two countries, groups, or sides
"This is a bilateral project between the two groups."
Protocol -- the formal rules and steps people follow
"She explains the protocol for the project."
Affiliation -- an official connection to an organization
"He asks about an affiliation between the two groups."
Delegation -- a group of people who represent a country or group
"A man from the Italian delegation comes to talk."
Headquarters -- the main office of an organization
"Our headquarters is in Brazil."
Sector -- a part of the economy or area of business
"Sebrae has a strong reputation in this sector."
Reputation -- the opinion people have about someone or something
"Sebrae has a strong reputation with international partners."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses as Chunks

GRAMMAR

How to add information to your introduction with short relative chunks.

WordUse it forSay
whopeople"I am a representative who leads international missions."
that / whichorganizations and things"Sebrae is an institution that supports small businesses."
whereplaces"Milan is the city where the event takes place."

Use these short chunks to add one piece of information to your introduction. Use who for people, that or which for organizations, and where for places.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection
