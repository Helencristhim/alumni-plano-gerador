Chapter 3 -- How to Ask

Ask and Do

"How do I get to...?" / "You have to..."

Grammar Discovery

What Do They Have in Common?

Read the sentences. Look at the orange words.

"You have to buy a ticket."

"I need to take the tram."

"How do I get to the station?"

The Rule

Necessity and Directions

UsePatternExample
Necessityhave to + verbYou have to buy a ticket.
Necessityneed to + verbI need to take the tram.
DirectionsHow do I get to + place?How do I get to the station?
QuestionDo I need to + verb?Do I need to change trains?

Key idea: use have to and need to for something necessary, and always keep to before the verb: "need to buy", "have to take".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I need buy a ticket."
"I need to buy a ticket."

Remember: after need and have, always use to + verb: "have to take", "need to buy".

Chapter 4 -- At the Station

Getting Directions

A conversation, an announcement, and a ticket

Dialogue

Asking a Local in Rome

M

"Excuse me. How do I get to Trastevere?"

L

"You have to take the number 8 tram. The tram stop is over there."

M

"Do I need to buy a ticket first?"

L

"Yes. You have to buy a ticket at the machine. It is one euro fifty."

M

"Do I need to change trams?"

L

"No. The number 8 goes straight to Trastevere. It is about ten minutes."
