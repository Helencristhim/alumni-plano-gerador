Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Recap

Vocabulary

Você já conhece estas 8 palavras das aulas 6 a 9. Ouça cada uma para reativar. Toque em Listen.

Usually -- almost every day, but not always
"I usually get to work at eight." (Geralmente)
Get to work -- to arrive at the office
"I get to work at nine o'clock." (Chegar ao trabalho)
Schedule -- the plan of your day and your meetings
"Let me check my schedule for Tuesday." (Agenda)
Appointment -- a fixed time to meet someone
"I have an appointment at ten." (Compromisso)
Answer the phone -- to pick up the phone when it rings
"I answer the phone at my desk." (Atender o telefone)
Leave a message -- to give words for someone who is not there
"Would you like to leave a message?" (Deixar um recado)
Help -- to do something for someone who needs it
"Can you help me, please?" (Ajudar)
Explain -- to make something clear and easy to understand
"Could you explain this number?" (Explicar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia a manhã de trabalho da Maísa e responda as perguntas. Repare nas quatro estruturas do mês.

Maísa usually gets to work at nine, but today she is early. David asks: "Can we schedule our meeting?" Maísa says: "I have an appointment at ten, so let us meet on Tuesday at three, in the afternoon." Then the phone rings and she answers the phone: "Good morning, Maísa speaking. He is not here. Would you like to leave a message?" Later, she needs help with a number in the report. She asks David: "Could you help me? I do not understand this number. Could you explain it, please?" David explains, and now it makes sense.

B "You leave message now."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Four Patterns Review

GRAMMAR

Esta aula junta as quatro estruturas do mês. Revise cada uma. / This lesson brings together the four patterns from the month.

Aula / LessonEstrutura / PatternExemplo / Example
Routine (6)usually / always + verboI usually get to work at eight.
Scheduling (7)on + dia, at + hora, in + parte do diaon Monday, at nine, in the morning
Phone (8)Can I speak to...? / leave a messageCan I speak to David?
Help (9)Can / Could you + verbo (sem "to")Could you explain that?

Atenção: use on para dias (on Monday), at para horas (at nine), in para partes do dia (in the morning). Depois de Can you / Could you vem o verbo direto, SEM "to". Afirmativo: I usually help David. Negativo: I do not understand. Interrogativo: Could you explain it?
