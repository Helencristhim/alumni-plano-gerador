Chapter 3: The Code

Four Patterns, One Page

everything you learned this month

Grammar Review

Remember These Four

"I usually get to work at eight."

"The meeting is on Monday at nine."

"Can I speak to David, please?"

"Could you explain this number?"

LessonPatternExample
Routineusually / always + verbI usually get to work at eight.
Schedulingon + day, at + time, in + part of dayon Monday, at nine, in the morning
PhoneCan I speak to...? / leave a messageCan I speak to David?
HelpCan / Could you + verb (no "to")Could you explain that?

Four patterns, one goal: talk about your day and get things done at work. Use on for days, at for clock times, and in for parts of the day.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

The meeting is in Monday.
The meeting is on Monday.
Can you to help me?
Can you help me?

Use on for days (on Monday), at for clock times (at nine). After Can you / Could you, use the verb with no to (help, explain, check).

Dialogue

A Full Morning

D
Good morning, Maísa. You are early today!
M
Good morning, David. Yes, I usually get to work at nine, but today I am early.
D
Great. Can we schedule our meeting for this week?
M
Sure. I have an appointment at ten, so let us meet on Tuesday at three.
D
Perfect. Oh, the phone is ringing. Can you get it?
M
Of course. Good morning, finance team, Maísa speaking. He is not here — would you like to leave a message?
D
Nice work. Now, are you busy with the report?
M
Yes. I do not understand one number. Could you help me?
D
Of course. Which number?
M
The total for March. Could you explain it, please?
