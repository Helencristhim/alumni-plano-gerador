Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Monitor -- to watch a system or screen closely (PT: monitorar / acompanhar)
"The team was monitoring the dashboard all night."
Alert -- an automatic warning that something is wrong (PT: alerta / aviso)
"An alert appeared on the screen at midnight."
Freeze -- when a screen or system stops responding (PT: travar / congelar)
"The whole store froze for ten minutes."
Lag -- a slow delay between an action and the response (PT: atraso / lentidão)
"The response time started to lag during the update."
Glitch -- a small, temporary fault in a system (PT: falha temporária / bug)
"A glitch in the payment step caused the error."
Occur -- to happen, often used for problems (PT: ocorrer / acontecer)
"The outage occurred at eleven fifteen."
Interrupt -- to stop an action that was already happening (PT: interromper)
"The crash interrupted the test."
Reboot -- to turn a system off and on again to fix it (PT: reiniciar / rebootar)
"While I was rebooting the server, Carla called the vendor."
Notice -- to see or realize that something is happening (PT: perceber / notar)
"We noticed the problem early because we were watching the monitor."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Last night the team had an incident. At eleven, they were running the final update and Daniela was monitoring the dashboard — these long actions set the scene, so she uses the past continuous (was/were + verb-ing). Then, while the update was loading, an alert appeared and the store froze. The short actions that interrupt the scene go in the past simple. Notice the words: while comes before the long action and when comes before the short one. Notice the form too: with I she says was (I was monitoring), never "I were"; and she always adds -ing (was monitoring), never "was monitor".

1. Why does Daniela say "I was monitoring the dashboard"?
A It is the long action that sets the scene.
B It is the short action that interrupts.
C It is a plan for the future.
2. Which form does she use for the alert that suddenly appeared?
A past continuous (was/were + verb-ing)
B past simple (appeared)
C present continuous
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I were monitoring the dashboard."
B "I was monitoring the dashboard when the alert appeared."
C "I was monitor the dashboard."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Past Continuous (was/were + verb-ing)

GRAMMAR

Como montar a cena no passado (bilingue EN + PT).

FormUseExample
was/were + verbo-ingAção longa em progresso (a cena)"I was testing the system."
past simpleAção curta que interrompe (o momento)"...when the server crashed."
while + ação longaIntroduz a ação em progresso"While we were deploying..."
Regra (PT)Use was com I/he/she/it e were com you/we/they, sempre com o verbo + -ing (was monitoring) — NUNCA "I were" nem "was monitor". While vem antes da ação longa, when antes da curta.
