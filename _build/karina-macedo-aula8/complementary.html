Lesson 8 -- Describing Your Aircraft: Features & Adjectives

Series
Mayday: Air Disaster -- "How a Plane Works" (any introductory episode)

Aviation experts describe aircraft parts and qualities in clear English: the engine, the cabin, how fast and how safe a plane is. Great to hear "is" and "has" used to describe aircraft.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two sentences: one quality with "is" (it is fast) and one feature with "has" (it has a powerful engine).

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "Describing Things: is and has"

A slow, clear podcast on describing objects: use "is" for a quality and "has" for a feature, with many easy examples and adjectives.

Repeat each example out loud. Then record a short message: describe your aircraft with three qualities and two features.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Adjectives: describing things"

A short, practical lesson on today's grammar: using "is" with adjectives to describe qualities, and "has" to describe the features a thing includes.

After watching, write five sentences about your aircraft. Use "is", "has", "fast", "modern", and "engine" at least once each, then read them aloud.
