Lesson 17 -- Numbers That Speak

Podcast
Making sense of the census -- 6 Minute English (BBC Learning English)

Two hosts talk about the census, the huge survey a country runs to count its people and collect data. They explain the figures in clear, natural English. Connection to Lesson 17: this is the exact language you practiced -- data, survey, percentage, figures, and trends -- used to talk about real numbers.

Tip: every time the hosts give a number, pause and say it out loud in a full sentence, using "roughly", "on average", or "the percentage of".

YouTube
Say & Write Numbers in English: percentages, fractions, decimals, large numbers (engVid)

A clear, friendly lesson that shows exactly how to read numbers aloud in English: percentages, fractions like "one in three", decimals like "two point five", and big figures. Connection to Lesson 17: it is a ready-made guide for the "read numbers aloud" part of today's Grammar Tip.

Tip: pause the video and read every number on the screen out loud before the teacher does; then check yourself.

TED Talk
The best stats you've ever seen -- Hans Rosling (TED)

A famous, energetic talk where a doctor and researcher makes data about health and the world come alive. He shows how numbers, averages, and trends tell a real human story. Connection to Lesson 17: it is a masterclass in describing data and trends clearly, in the same everyday, research-based English you are learning.

Tip: watch with English subtitles, then describe one trend from the talk in your own words, using "increased", "decreased", and "on average".
