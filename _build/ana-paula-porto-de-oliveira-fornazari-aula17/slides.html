Chapter 3: The Number Code

Reading the Numbers

Read · Count · Change · Estimate

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"In our survey, forty-five percent of people said yes."

"On average, adults sleep about seven hours."

"The number increased slightly last year."

"Roughly one in three people prefer tea."

Which one reads a percentage, which one gives an average, which one describes a change, and which one stays approximate?

FunctionPhrases
Read numbers45% = "forty-five percent" · 2.5 = "two point five"
Say how manyon average, ... · one in three ... · the majority of ...
Describe changeincreased / decreased · by 10% · sharply / slightly
Stay approximateroughly ... · about / around ... · nearly / over ...

Golden rule: we increase or decrease by an amount (not "in"), and percent never takes an "s".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"The number increased in ten percent."
"The number increased by ten percent."
"Twenty percents of people agreed."
"Twenty percent of people agreed."
"The average of age is thirty-five."
"The average age is thirty-five."

Numbers change by an amount, "percent" has no plural, and we say the average age (not "the average of age").

Dialogue

Screens and Sleep

M
Ana Paula, I read an interesting survey today. On average, people now spend over three hours a day on their phones.
A
Really? That is a lot. What percentage of that is just social media?
M
Roughly half, according to the data. And the figure has increased every year since 2015.
A
That does not surprise me. As a doctor, I see the results — more people say they sleep badly.
M
Exactly. The study estimated that one in three adults checks their phone in the middle of the night.
A
One in three? That is a worrying trend. We really should talk about screen time with patients.
