Lesson 10 -- Making Suggestions

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 6: "Tricks of the Trade"

Watch how the lawyers advise their clients and propose options under pressure. Notice how they make suggestions and recommend a plan using should, could, and would.

Write down every suggestion you hear with should, could, or would. Pause and repeat each one, then make a suggestion for an imaginary client.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Making Suggestions and Recommendations"

Practical tips for a sales proposal: how to make a suggestion with should and could, and how to recommend an option politely with would recommend. Listen for the new words: suggest, propose, option, advice, and alternative.

Note 3 suggestions the host makes. Practice each one out loud with should, could, or would and a base verb.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Modals for Advice: should, could, would"

A short, clear lesson on modal verbs for suggestions: when to use should for strong advice, could for a soft option, and would recommend for a polite tip. Perfect for a client proposal.

After watching, record yourself proposing two options to a client. Use should, could, and would, and at least three new words from this lesson.
