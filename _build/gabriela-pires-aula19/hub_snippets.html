Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. / Listen to each word and read the example.

Plan -- an idea about what you will do, where and when
"My plan for Saturday is to see my friends." (Plano)
Meet up -- to see your friends at a place you both choose
"Let's meet up at the cafe at four." (Encontrar-se)
Get together -- when a group of friends meet to spend time together
"We get together every Saturday." (Reunir-se / juntar-se)
Hang out -- to spend relaxed, easy time with friends
"We're going to hang out at the park." (Passar o tempo / curtir)
Free -- available, with nothing you must do at that time
"Are you free on Saturday afternoon?" (Livre / disponível)
Busy -- with a lot of things to do, so not available
"I'm busy in the morning with my class." (Ocupado/a)
On time -- at the right time, not early and not late
"Please be on time; the movie starts at six." (Na hora / pontual)
Downtown -- the central, busy part of a city, with shops and cafes
"We're meeting downtown near the park." (Centro da cidade)
Looking forward to -- feeling happy and excited about something that will happen
"I'm looking forward to the weekend." (Ansioso/a por / mal posso esperar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous for the Future & Let's / Why don't we

GRAMMAR

Para um plano futuro já combinado, usamos be (am/is/are) + verbo-ing, geralmente com um horário ou dia. Para sugerir, usamos "Let's + verbo" ou "Why don't we + verbo?". / For a future plan you arranged, use be + verb-ing. To suggest, use Let's or Why don't we.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Plan (I)I am + verb-ingI'm meeting Lucas at four.
Plan (we / they)We are + verb-ingWe're going to the movies.
Plan (he / she)He/She is + verb-ingLucas is coming too.
Suggest (idea)Let's + simple verbLet's meet up at the cafe.
Suggest (question)Why don't we + verb?Why don't we get together?

Key point / Ponto-chave: para um plano futuro use be + verbo-ing ("I'm meeting", não "I meet"). Depois de Let's, use o verbo simples ("Let's go", não "Let's to go").
