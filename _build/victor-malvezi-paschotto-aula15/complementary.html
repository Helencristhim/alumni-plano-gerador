Lesson 15 -- Building Consensus: Aligning a Divided Room

Series
12 Angry Men (the classic film), or any boardroom-deadlock scene in Succession / Industry

Watch a divided room get aligned in real time. Notice who acknowledges the other side first, who names the common ground, and who proposes tentatively versus who just bullies for a vote. The person who turns a stalemate into a yes is doing exactly what this class teaches.

Tip: pause whenever someone unblocks the room and label the move — acknowledge, converge, propose, build on, confirm.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast / WorkLife with Adam Grant -- any episode on building consensus, facilitation, or productive disagreement

Experts unpack how strong facilitators align divided teams: make both factions feel heard, converge on shared goals, propose tentatively, and confirm real buy-in instead of a lukewarm nod. The exact toolkit from this class, in real leadership voices — ideal prep before Victor brokers his own split meetings at Brink's.

Tip: write one go-to line for each move — acknowledge, converge, propose, build on, confirm — that you would actually say in a meeting.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review / TED -- "How to Build Consensus" or "Getting to Yes" negotiation breakdowns

A short, practical guide to aligning a divided room: acknowledge both positions, find the common ground, propose with tentative language, and check for genuine agreement. A clean bridge from delivering bad news (Lesson 14) to bringing two factions to a yes everyone owns.

Tip: draft a four-move alignment script for a real split decision you expect at work, then read it aloud and check each move.
