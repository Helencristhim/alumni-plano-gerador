Chapter 3

The Code

Future perfect and future continuous

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"By the March review, we will have retested every unit."

"By then, we will have shipped the first certified batch."

"This time next month, we will be running the pilot in your region."

"During April, we will be monitoring uptime around the clock."

TypeStructureMeaning
Future perfectwill have + past participleAn action that will be COMPLETED before a future point — "by March, we will have retested it".
Future continuouswill be + verb-ingAn action IN PROGRESS at a future point — "this time next month, we will be running the pilot".
Time markersFuture perfect loves by + a deadline (by March, by then, by the end of the quarter). Future continuous loves a point in time (this time next month, during April, at 3 p.m.).

Use will have + past participle for what will be DONE by a deadline, and will be + -ing for what will be HAPPENING at a moment. Never drop the participle ("will have retest") or the "be" ("we will running").

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"By the March review, we will retest every unit."

✓ RIGHT

"By the March review, we will have retested every unit."

✗ WRONG

"By then, we will have ship the first batch."

✓ RIGHT

"By then, we will have shipped the first batch."

✗ WRONG

"This time next month, we will running the pilot."

✓ RIGHT

"This time next month, we will be running the pilot."

For what will be finished by a deadline, use "will have + past participle" — never the past participle alone or the base verb. For what will be in progress at a moment, keep the "be": "will be + -ing".

Chapter 4

In the Room

A reassurance call with David Chen

Dialogue

The Reassurance Call

D
Izabel, thank you for the call. I will be honest: my board wants me to walk away. I need a timeline I can defend, not another apology.
I
Understood, David. So here are the dates. By the March review, we will have retested every unit, and we will have shipped the first certified batch to your warehouse.
D
The firmware was the failure. What about reliability — the downtime is what cost my customers.
I
The interim firmware is already live. By the end of the quarter, we will have restored full reliability, and we will be holding uptime above ninety-nine percent.
D
And what happens while my team tests the samples? I do not want silence.
I
During April, while your team evaluates the samples, we will be monitoring the sensors in real time and sending you a weekly report. Every line is a deliverable with a date.
D
That is what I needed. We have good rapport, Izabel — I would rather stay than start over.
I
Then give us this quarter. If we follow through on every date, we will have won your trust back — and we will keep your account.
