"Future perfect, or future perfect continuous?" -- Ask BBC Learning English

A short, clear lesson on exactly the code from Lesson 17: how the future perfect (will have + past participle) says what will be finished by a deadline, and how the continuous forms say what will be in progress at a future point. Perfect for locking in the difference before your reassurance call.

Tip: pause after each example and say your own version about the Halo timeline. Then record a 90-second reassurance call using at least four future perfect and two future continuous sentences.
