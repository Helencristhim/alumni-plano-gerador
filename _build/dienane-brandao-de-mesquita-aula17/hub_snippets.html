Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dienane talks about the past. She says: "Last weekend was wonderful. Yesterday I was at home. The house was quiet. My sister was here, and we were very happy. At night I was tired, but I was not sick." In English, we talk about the past of "to be" with was and were. With I, he, she, or it we use was. With we, you, or they we use were. To say no, we add not: I was not, we were not.

1. How do we talk about the past of "to be"?
A "I am at home yesterday."
B "I was at home yesterday."
C "I were at home yesterday."
2. Which is correct for "we"?
A "We were very happy."
B "We was very happy."
C "We are very happy yesterday."
3. How was Dienane at night?
A Tired, but not sick.
B Sick all weekend.
C At the office.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Was and Were

GRAMMAR

Em inglês, falamos do passado do verbo to be com was e were. / In English, we talk about the past of "to be" with was and were.

Subject / SujeitoPast / PassadoExample / Exemplo
I (eu)wasI was at home.
He / She (ele/ela)wasShe was here.
We / You / TheywereWe were happy.
negativowas not / were notI was not tired.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use was com I, he, she, it. Use were com we, you, they. Nunca diga "we was" -- diga we were. Para negar, coloque not depois: I was not, we were not.
