Chapter 3: The Code

Register & Connectors

however · therefore · in addition · regarding

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The report is ready; therefore, I have attached it here."

"The control was tested. However, one point is still open."

"In addition, we have updated the exposure limits."

"I am writing regarding your inquiry about the figures."

What do the orange words do — link one idea to the next and show the relation, or start a new topic with no link?

ConnectorWhat it showsExample
In addition / MoreoverAdds another point.In addition, the limits were updated.
HoweverShows a contrast or a problem.The control works. However, one point is open.
Therefore / As a resultShows a consequence.The figures are late; therefore, we will wait.
RegardingIntroduces the subject of the email: "I am writing regarding your inquiry." A connector at the start of a sentence takes a comma after it.

Connectors link your ideas and show the relation between them — adding, contrast, result, or topic. That is what makes an email read as one clear message.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"But the figures are late."
"However, the figures are late."
"I am writing for tell you about the delay."
"I am writing to inform you of the delay."
"Send me the report today."
"Could you please send me the report today?"

In a formal email, use however (not "but") to open a contrast, to + verb for the purpose ("I am writing to inform you"), and a polite question (Could you please...?) instead of a bare command.

Chapter 4: The Partner Bank's Email

Read the Inquiry

an email from Claire at the partner bank

The Email

Claire's Inquiry

Inbox — New Message
From: Claire Bennett, Meridian Bank Risk Team
To: Sergio Tanaka
Subject: Inquiry regarding Q3 exposure figures

Dear Mr. Tanaka,

I am writing regarding the quarterly exposure report for our joint positions. We have reviewed the figures. However, two numbers are unclear.

Could you please confirm the total exposure for September? In addition, we would be grateful if you could attach the updated limit table.

As the committee meets on Friday, a prompt reply would be very helpful.

Kind regards,

Claire Bennett

1. What does Claire want Sergio to confirm?
The total exposure for September.
2. What does she ask him to attach?
The updated limit table.
3. Why does she need a prompt reply?
Because the committee meets on Friday.
Dialogue

Planning the Reply

M
Sergio, I saw the email from Claire at the partner bank. Are you going to reply today?
S
Yes. I have a first draft, but I want it to sound formal. She asked me to confirm the September exposure and to attach the limit table.
M
Good. Start with the topic: "I am writing regarding your inquiry." Then confirm the figure, and use a connector for the second point.
S
Right. So: "I can confirm the total exposure for September. In addition, I have attached the updated limit table." Is that clear enough?
M
Very clear and concise. Just acknowledge her deadline — say a prompt reply, since the committee meets on Friday.
S
Perfect. I will close with "I look forward to hearing from you." Then I will send the draft to you before it goes out.
