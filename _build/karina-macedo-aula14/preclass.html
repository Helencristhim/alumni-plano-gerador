Aula 14 -- Pre-class

Welcoming a Visitor -- Receiving a Guest

Welcome a guest to your office. Say welcome, offer a seat (Please have a seat), offer a drink politely (Would you like a coffee?), and introduce a colleague. New vocabulary: welcome, visitor, seat, coffee, water, reception, introduce, colleague.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Welcome -- the kind word you say when a guest arrives
"Welcome to our office, Robert." (Bem-vindo)
Visitor -- a person who comes from outside to see you
"Robert is our visitor today." (Visitante)
Seat -- a chair you offer to a guest so they can sit
"Please have a seat." (Assento / Lugar)
Coffee -- a hot drink you offer to a guest
"Would you like a coffee?" (Café)
Water -- a cold drink you offer to a guest
"Here is a glass of water." (Água)
Reception -- the place at the entrance where a guest arrives
"Robert is at the reception." (Recepção)
Introduce -- to say two people's names so they can meet
"Let me introduce my colleague." (Apresentar)
Colleague -- a person you work with in the same company
"This is my colleague, Ana." (Colega de trabalho)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Robert arrived at the reception as the visitor for the ten o'clock meeting. Karina came to meet him and said, "Welcome to our office, Robert. Please have a seat." Then she offered him a drink: "Would you like a coffee or a glass of water?" Robert chose water. After that, Karina said, "Let me introduce my colleague," and they walked together to the meeting room.

1. How does Karina welcome Robert?
A "Welcome to our office, Robert."
B "Goodbye, Robert."
C "You are the line."
2. How does Karina offer a drink politely?
A "You want coffee."
B "Would you like a coffee or water?"
C "Hold the line, please."
3. What does Karina do after the drink?
A She introduces her colleague.
B She buys a new aircraft.
C She turns left at the corner.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Offers

GRAMMAR

Como oferecer algo de forma educada. / How to offer something politely.

Phrase / FraseMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Would you like + a + noun?Oferecer uma coisa / Offer a thingWould you like a coffee?
Would you like to + verb?Oferecer uma ação / Offer an actionWould you like to sit down?
Please have a seat.Oferecer a cadeira / Offer the chairPlease have a seat.
Let me introduce + person.Apresentar alguém / Present a personLet me introduce my colleague.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use Would you like para oferecer de forma educada (não "You want"). Depois dele, use a + substantivo (a coffee) ou to + verbo (to sit down).
