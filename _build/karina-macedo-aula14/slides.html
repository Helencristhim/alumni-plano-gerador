Chapter 3 -- The Offer

Grammar Discovery

Would you like...? -- offer politely

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we offer something to a guest?

"Would you like a coffee?"

"Would you like to sit down?"

"Please have a seat."

"Let me introduce my colleague."

Grammar Rule

Polite Offers

PhraseWhen we use it
Would you like + a coffee?Offer a thing (a noun)
Would you like to + sit down?Offer an action (to + verb)
Please have a seat.Offer the chair to the guest
Let me introduce + person.Present a person to the guest

Key idea: use Would you like to offer politely. After it, use a + noun (a coffee) or to + verb (to sit down). It is much more polite than "Do you want".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You want coffee? Sit down here."
"Would you like a coffee? Please have a seat."

To be polite, use Would you like for offers and Please have a seat for the chair.
