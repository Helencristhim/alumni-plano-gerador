Chapter 3

Polite Requests

Can / Could / Would it be possible to...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Can you send extra towels?"

"Could I have a quiet room, please?"

"Could you send someone to look at it?"

"Would it be possible to get a late check-out?"

PhrasePolitenessExample
Can you...?PoliteCan you send towels?
Could I / Could you...?More politeCould I have a room, please?
Would it be possible to...?Very politeWould it be possible to upgrade?
FormCan/Could + base verb; add please for extra politeness.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Give me a quiet room."

✓ RIGHT

"Could I have a quiet room, please?"

✗ WRONG

"Could you to send towels?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you send towels?"

✗ WRONG

"I want late check-out."

✓ RIGHT

"Would it be possible to get a late check-out?"

Use Could you / Could I + base verb, add please. Avoid direct orders.

Chapter 4

Check-in in Action

A guest at the front desk

Dialogue

Could I Have a Quiet Room?

R
Good evening, and welcome to the Capital View Hotel. How can I help you?
M
Good evening. I have a reservation under the name Mansour. Could I have a quiet room on a high floor, please?
R
Of course. I have a room on the eighth floor. Here is your key card.
M
Thank you. Would it be possible to get a late check-out on my last day? My flight leaves in the evening.
R
That is no problem. I will note a late check-out for two in the afternoon.
M
Wonderful. One more thing: the air conditioning is a little too cold. Could you send someone to take a look?
R
Certainly. I will send someone right away. Is there anything else?
M
Just one: can you tell me the wifi password? Thank you so much for your help.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What kind of room did the guest request?
A quiet room on a high floor.
2. What did he ask for on his last day, and why?
A late check-out, because his flight leaves in the evening.
3. What small complaint did he make?
The air conditioning was a little too cold, so he asked them to take a look.
