Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clause -- a separate, numbered part of a contract with one rule
"The delivery clause says the coffee ships by the tenth." (Cláusula)
Party -- a person or company that signs and is bound by a contract
"Each party must sign the last page of the contract." (Parte / Contratante)
Obligation -- a duty: something a party must do under the contract
"The seller's obligation is to deliver on time." (Obrigação / Dever)
Liability -- legal responsibility for a loss, damage or cost
"The liability clause limits the seller's responsibility." (Responsabilidade legal)
Breach -- a failure to do what the contract requires; a broken rule
"A late shipment would be a breach of the contract." (Quebra / Violação de contrato)
Comply with -- to act exactly as a rule or contract requires
"Both parties must comply with the delivery clause." (Cumprir / Obedecer)
Binding -- legally firm: once signed, both parties must follow it
"The contract is binding once both parties sign." (Vinculante / Obrigatório)
Warranty -- a written promise that a product meets a stated quality
"The warranty confirms the coffee is premium grade." (Garantia)
Indemnify -- to pay for the loss or damage another party suffers
"The party in breach must indemnify the other side." (Indenizar / Ressarcir)
Effective date -- the date when the contract begins to apply
"The seller shall deliver by the effective date." (Data de vigência / Início)
Expiry date -- the date when the contract ends and stops applying
"Check the expiry date before you renew the deal." (Data de término / Validade)
Governing law -- the country whose law decides any dispute in the contract
"The governing law of this contract is Brazilian law." (Lei aplicável / Foro)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson reads the Santos coffee contract before he signs it. Each party has duties written in numbered clauses. Under the obligation clause, the seller shall deliver the coffee by the effective date, and the buyer must pay within thirty days. The warranty clause promises that the coffee is premium grade. The liability clause is important: if a party is in breach, that party is required to indemnify the other side for any loss. Both parties must comply with every clause. The governing law is Brazilian law, and the contract is binding from the effective date until the expiry date.

B Pay within thirty days.
C Change the governing law.
2. What must a party in breach do?
A Sign the contract again.
B Indemnify the other side for any loss.
C Lower the price.
3. When is the contract binding?
A Only after the expiry date.
C Only if the buyer asks for a discount.
B From the effective date, once both parties sign.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Obligation language (shall / must / be required to)

GRAMMAR

Em contratos, a obrigação se escreve com palavras especiais: shall, must e be required to. Todas significam um dever forte. / In contracts, an obligation is written with special words: shall, must and be required to. They all mean a strong duty.

Word / PalavraPhrase / FraseUse / Uso
shallThe seller shall deliver the coffee.muito formal, típico de contrato (deverá / deve)
mustThe buyer must pay within thirty days.regra forte, também na fala (deve / tem que)
be required toEach party is required to comply.formal (= must) (é obrigado a)

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de shall e must usamos o verbo base (shall deliver, must pay) -- NUNCA "to + verbo". Só be required to mantém o "to". Para proibição, use shall not ou must not. / After shall and must we use the base verb -- never "to + verb". Only be required to keeps the "to". For a prohibition, use shall not or must not.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "The seller shall deliver the coffee by the effective date."
B "The seller shall to deliver the coffee."
C "Maybe the seller delivers the coffee."
A party broke a rule of the contract. You describe what happened. You say:
A "That party met halfway on the contract."
B "That party is in breach and must indemnify the other side."
