Lesson 14 -- Reading Contracts

Series
Suits -- a contract-signing scene (any season)

Watch a scene where lawyers read a contract clause by clause before signing. Notice how they talk about obligations, a possible breach, and who is liable.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one obligation ("X shall...") and one word for a broken rule (breach) that you hear.

Podcast
Plain English -- "How to Read a Business Contract"

A practical episode on the parts of a contract: clauses, obligations, warranties and liability, and the dates that make a contract binding.

Record three sentences about a coffee contract: one with "shall", one with "must", and one with "be required to".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Modals of obligation: must, have to, shall"

A focused lesson on the modals that express obligation and how "shall" is used in formal and legal English. It directly reinforces today's grammar.

After watching, write six contract sentences: two with "shall", two with "must", and two with "be required to".
