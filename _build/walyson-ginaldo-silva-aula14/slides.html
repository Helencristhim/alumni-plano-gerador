Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Obligation language: shall, must, be required to

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"The seller shall deliver the coffee by the effective date." (formal duty)

"The buyer must pay within thirty days." (strong rule)

"Each party is required to comply with the contract." (must, formal)

"The party in breach shall indemnify the other side." (formal duty)

Grammar Rule

The Obligation Map

WordRegisterExample
shallvery formal (contracts)"The seller shall deliver the coffee."
muststrong rule (also speech)"The buyer must pay within thirty days."
be required toformal (= must)"Each party is required to comply."

Key idea: all three show a strong obligation and are followed by the base verb (shall deliver, must pay, is required to comply). Shall is the formal word you see in contracts; must is also used in speech; be required to is a formal way to say must. For a prohibition, use shall not or must not.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The seller shall to deliver the coffee by the date."
"The seller shall deliver the coffee by the date."

After shall and must, use the base verb. Only be required to keeps the word to.
