Talking About the World -- Opinions, News and Intelligent Conversation

Giving a balanced, intelligent opinion. Key words: argue, claim, point out, acknowledge, challenge, perspective, bias, nuance, geopolitics, inequality, resilience, emerging economy. Structures: hedging with modals (It could be argued that... / There might be...) and concession (Even though... / That said... / On the other hand...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To argue -- to give reasons for or against an idea (argumentar, defender uma ideia)
"It could be argued that tourism helps poor regions."
To claim -- to say something is true, often without proof yet (afirmar, alegar)
"Critics claim that mass tourism damages local culture."
To point out -- to draw attention to a fact (apontar, destacar)
"Supporters point out that visitors bring jobs and respect."
To acknowledge -- to accept that something is true or valid (reconhecer, admitir)
"I acknowledge that the money can matter to a small town."
To challenge -- to question whether an idea is right (questionar, contestar)
"You challenged me to see the other side of the debate."
Perspective -- a particular way of seeing something (perspectiva, ponto de vista)
"There is another perspective worth considering."
Bias -- an unfair preference for or against something (viés, parcialidade)
"We all see the world through our own bias."
Nuance -- a small but important difference in meaning (nuance, sutileza)
"The honest answer has more nuance than either slogan admits."
Geopolitics -- how geography and power shape relations between countries (geopolítica)
"He follows a podcast on geopolitics every morning."
Inequality -- an unfair gap between rich and poor (desigualdade)
"Often the money flows away, deepening inequality."
Resilience -- the ability to recover and adapt after hardship (resiliência)
"The towns that do best are the ones with resilience."
Emerging economy -- a country whose economy is growing fast toward development (economia emergente)
"In an emerging economy, one popular valley can lift a whole town."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When people ask Marcelo about tourism, he never gives an absolute answer. It could be argued that visitors help a small town, he says, because they bring money and jobs. That said, he always adds the other side. Even though tourism creates income, it should be noted that prices often rise for locals. On the other hand, some towns handle it beautifully. His real view? There might be no single answer. The honest truth, he says, has more nuance than either side wants to admit.

1. Por que Marcelo usa "It could be argued that" em vez de "Tourism is good"?
A Para SUAVIZAR a opinião, deixando-a menos absoluta (hedge).
B Porque ele não tem certeza de nada.
C Porque é mais curto.
2. "Even though tourism creates income..." serve para:
A Repetir a mesma ideia.
B Admitir o outro lado antes de fazer o seu ponto (concessão).
C Fazer uma pergunta.
3. Which sentence sounds the most balanced and intelligent?
A "Tourism is terrible, end of story."
B "It could be argued that tourism helps, but that said, prices rise for locals."
C "I do not know and I do not care."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Hedging and Concession

GRAMMAR

Como dar uma opinião forte de forma equilibrada (explicação em inglês e português).

ToolUse / UsoExample
Hedge (modal)Suaviza a opinião para não soar absoluta. Softens a claim so it is not absolute.It could be argued that ... / There might be ...
ConcessionAdmite o outro lado antes do seu ponto. Admits the other side before your point.Even though ... / That said, ... / On the other hand, ...
Together"It could be argued that tourism helps, but that said, prices rise for locals."

Dica: uma opinião forte, suavizada e equilibrada, convence muito mais do que uma opinião alta e absoluta.

Stage 2: Put the Argument in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de uma opinião equilibrada na ordem correta.

?Give one reason that supports it.
?State your opinion, softened with a hedge (It could be argued that...).
?Admit the other side with a concession (That said... / Even though...).
?Land on where you stand: it depends on how you look at it.
?Point out the risk or the nuance the other side misses.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real.

Someone asks your opinion on a big topic. How do you sound intelligent, not absolute?
A "It is obvious, only an idiot would disagree."
B "It could be argued that... but on the other hand..."
C "I have no opinion at all."
You disagree with someone, but want to keep it friendly. Which is best?
A "I see your point, but it should be noted that there are risks."
B "You are completely wrong."
C "Whatever you say."
You want to admit the other side before making your point. Which phrase fits?
A "Therefore, in conclusion..."
B "Even though I agree, that said..."
C "First of all, secondly..."
A friend states a strong opinion with no evidence. You want to name that gently:
A "Prove it or be quiet."
B "That is a fair claim, but I would challenge the bias behind it."
C "Nobody cares about facts."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada.

Choose one current event you follow (geopolitics, technology, neuroscience). Give a ninety-second opinion: state your view, hedge it, admit the other side with a concession, and end with where you land. Record it. Take your time.
