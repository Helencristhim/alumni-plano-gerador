Chapter 3 -- The Code

Results vs Ongoing Work

present perfect simple vs continuous

Discovery

Two Ways to Use "Have"

Look at the orange verbs. Which sentences count a finished result? Which emphasise an action still going on?

"We have closed three deals this year."

"I have been overseeing the holding for ten years."

"We have consolidated two subsidiaries."

"We have been streamlining operations since January."

The Rule

Simple vs Continuous

FormUseExample
Present perfect simple
have / has + past participle		a finished, counted result up to now (how many)"we have closed three deals"; "we have consolidated two subsidiaries"
Present perfect continuous
have / has + been + verb-ing		an ongoing action and its duration (how long)"I have been overseeing it for ten years"; "we have been streamlining operations"

Key idea: count the result with the simple (three deals, two subsidiaries); emphasise the activity and its duration with the continuous (for ten years, since January). Time markers help: for and since often signal the continuous.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have been closing three deals this quarter." / "I oversee it for ten years."
"I have closed three deals this quarter." / "I have been overseeing it for ten years."

For a finished, counted result, use the simple (have closed). For an action that started in the past and still continues, use the continuous (have been overseeing) -- never the plain present.
