"Closing the Pittsburgh deal was a major milestone." (Marco)

"We leverage our scale to win better terms." (Alavancar)

"I led the turnaround of our ceramics unit." (Recuperação / reviravolta)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous

GRAMMAR

As duas formas usam have/has, mas contam histórias diferentes. PRESENT PERFECT SIMPLE (have/has + particípio) = resultado terminado e contado até agora -- quantos (we have closed three deals; we have consolidated two subsidiaries). PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS (have/has + been + verbo-ing) = ação em curso e sua duração -- por quanto tempo (I have been overseeing it for ten years; we have been streamlining operations). Marcadores for e since costumam pedir o continuous. / Both forms use have/has, but they tell different stories. PRESENT PERFECT SIMPLE (have/has + past participle) = a finished, counted result up to now -- how many. PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS (have/has + been + verb-ing) = an ongoing action and its duration -- how long. The markers for and since often signal the continuous.

FormUseExample
Present perfect simple (have + participle)finished, counted result (how many)"we have closed three deals"
Present perfect continuous (have + been + -ing)ongoing action + duration (how long)"I have been overseeing it for ten years"
Simple -- another resulta second completed achievement"we have consolidated two subsidiaries"
Continuous -- with "since"work continuing from a start point"we have been streamlining operations since January"

Key point / Ponto-chave: conte o resultado com o simple (three deals); enfatize a ação e a duração com o continuous (for ten years). Não use o continuous para um número terminado, nem o presente simples para algo que começou no passado e continua. / Count the result with the simple (three deals); stress the activity and its duration with the continuous (for ten years). Do not use the continuous for a finished count, nor the plain present for something that started in the past and still continues.
