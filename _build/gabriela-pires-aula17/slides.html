Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Choosing without repeating the noun

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"Which one would you like?"

"I'll take the blue one, please."

"I prefer the small ones."

"The silver ones are perfect for a present."

Grammar Rule

Choosing with one / ones

FormExample
Ask which (choose)Which one would you like?
One thing (singular)I'll take the blue one.
Many things (plural)I prefer the small ones.
Adjective + one/onesthe red one / the silver ones
Buy itI'll take this one, please.

Key idea: use one for a singular thing and ones for many things, so you do not repeat the noun. Ask with Which one and buy with I'll take.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I'll take the small one postcards. I prefer the blue one keychains."
"I'll take the small ones. I prefer the blue ones."

Use one for one thing and ones for many things, and do not repeat the noun.

Grammar Practice

Is it One or Ones?

"I'll take the blue _____one." (one keychain)
"I prefer the small _____ones." (many postcards)
"The silver _____ones are perfect." (many rings)
"Which _____one would you like?" (asking to choose)
