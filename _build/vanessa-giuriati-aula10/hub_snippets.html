Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada conector e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

However -- connects a contrasting idea (more formal than "but") (no entanto/porém)
"I am tired. However, I will finish this."
Although -- despite the fact that (starts a contrast) (embora/apesar de)
"Although it was hard, I kept going."
As a result -- because of this; as a consequence (como resultado/por isso)
"I practiced daily. As a result, I improved."
On the other hand -- introduces the opposite side of an idea (por outro lado)
"It is fast. On the other hand, it is risky."
In addition -- also; on top of that (adds information) (além disso)
"It is cheap. In addition, it is fast."
That said -- I accept that, but... (a soft concession) (dito isso/ainda assim)
"It is hard. That said, it is worth it."
Hesitant -- unsure; slow to speak or decide (hesitante/inseguro)
"I felt hesitant at first."
Accurate -- correct; exact and precise (preciso/correto)
"Her grammar is very accurate now."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Renata started learning English, she was very hesitant. She could barely speak. However, she practiced a little every day. As a result, after ten classes she could introduce herself, share opinions, and make requests. Although she still made mistakes, her English became more accurate. In addition, she learned to ask for clarification instead of pretending. Speaking fast is still difficult for her. That said, she no longer freezes. On the other hand, she knows there is more to learn. She is ready for the next block with real momentum.

1. How did Renata feel at the start?
A Hesitant; she could barely speak.
B Confident and fluent.
C Bored and tired.
2. Which connector shows the consequence of her daily practice?
A "Although"
B "As a result"
C "On the other hand"
3. What does "That said" do in the text?
A It adds new information.
B It accepts a difficulty, then stays positive.
C It shows the past.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Connecting Your Ideas

GRAMMAR

Como ligar ideias para uma fala mais fluente. / How to connect ideas for smoother speech.

Job / FunçãoEnglishPortuguês
ContrastHowever, ... / Although ... / On the other hand, ...No entanto... / Embora... / Por outro lado...
ResultAs a result, ... / Because of that, ...Como resultado... / Por isso...
AdditionIn addition, ... / Also, ...Além disso... / Também...
ConcedeThat said, ...Dito isso... / Ainda assim...
⚠ CarefulUse a period before "However," then a comma. Do not add "but" after "although". Say "in addition to that", not "in addition of".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

You want to connect two contrasting ideas formally. Best choice:
A "It is small. However, it is powerful."
B "It is small however it is powerful."
C "It is small how ever powerful."
Which sentence is correct?
A "Although I was tired, but I finished."
B "Although I was tired, I finished."
C "Although tired but I finished."
You want to show a result. Best choice:
A "I studied hard. As a result, I passed."
B "I studied hard. As a result of, I passed."
C "As result, I passed."
You accept a difficulty but stay positive. Best choice:
A "It is hard. That said, I keep going."
B "It is hard. That say, I keep going."
C "It is hard. In addition, I stop."
