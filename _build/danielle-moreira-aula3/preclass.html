Aula 03 -- Pre-class

Speaking Like a Senior -- Executive Communication in 1:1s, OKRs and Strategic Alignment

Conduzir um 1:1, ler um painel de OKR e liderar uma reunião de alinhamento estratégico em inglês: abrir pelo número, propor, discordar de um superior e fechar com um responsável e uma data. Key words: OKR, key performance indicator, cascading goals, deliverable, milestone, accountability, cross-functional, bandwidth, buy-in, to put forward, to push back, to move the needle, to cut to the chase, to bring about, to circle back to. Structure: mixed conditionals (causa no passado com custo hoje / condição do presente com resultado no passado) + discourse markers executivos (Building on that... / My take on this is... / What I would like to propose is...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Este é o vocabulário que os seus pares canadenses já usam todos os dias — o que separa quem relata a reunião de quem conduz a reunião.

OKR -- one goal you can say out loud, plus the numbers that prove you reached it (objetivos e resultados-chave)
"Our first OKR for Canada is one objective and three numbers. Nothing else."
Key performance indicator -- a number you watch all year to see whether the business is healthy (indicador-chave de desempenho)
"Engagement is the key performance indicator I will be judged on in Canada."
Cascading goals -- goals that flow down: the company sets one, and every team writes its own version (desdobramento de metas)
"Without cascading goals, nine academic teams will write nine different strategies."
Deliverable -- the concrete thing you promised to hand over, on a date (entregável)
"The onboarding journey is not an idea. It is a deliverable, and it has a date."
Milestone -- a checkpoint with a date on the way to the goal (marco, etapa-chave)
"Give me one milestone, one owner and one date. That is the whole meeting."
Accountability -- owning the result in public, whether it worked or not (responsabilização pelo resultado)
"A team that agrees with everything I say is not accountability. It is silence with good manners."
Cross-functional -- involving people from different teams who do not report to each other (interfuncional, entre áreas)
"The group is cross-functional, which is exactly why nobody owns the date."
Bandwidth -- the time and attention a person or a team actually has left (capacidade disponível)
"They do not lack commitment. They lack bandwidth, and those are different problems."
Buy-in -- the moment people stop obeying a plan and start wanting it (adesão genuína)
"Their buy-in is not a nice-to-have. It is the deliverable."
To put forward -- to formally offer an idea so that a group can decide on it (propor formalmente)
"What I would like to put forward is one owner and one date."
To push back -- to disagree with a plan, professionally, and say so out loud (discordar e se posicionar)
"Please push back if you think the target is wrong. I would rather hear it now."
To move the needle -- to produce a change big enough to show up in the numbers (mover o ponteiro, gerar impacto real)
"Tell me which single milestone would actually move the needle this quarter."
To cut to the chase -- to give the point first and the story afterwards (ir direto ao ponto)
"Let me cut to the chase: two of the four objectives are off track."
To bring about -- to cause a change, not merely to want one (provocar, causar (uma mudança))
"This is the one change that would bring about real accountability."
To circle back to -- to return, on purpose, to a point you left open (retomar (um ponto))
"Before we close, I would like to circle back to the onboarding deliverable."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

It is the last week of the quarter at Maple Bear Canada, and the OKR dashboard is not kind. Two of the four objectives are off track. The onboarding deliverable slipped by six weeks; the cross-functional group that was supposed to own it has met exactly twice; and the engagement key performance indicator is still marked as not measured, because there is nothing live to measure. If someone had owned that deliverable in the first quarter, the team would not be having this conversation today. If the goals had cascaded across the nine academic teams last year, every one of them would have its own version of the objective right now, instead of nine different interpretations of it.

The directors blame bandwidth. Claire, the VP, does not. Her reading is colder and more useful: nobody owns the date, so nobody carries the accountability for it, and a milestone without an owner is a wish with a calendar. What she wants on Monday is not a status report. She wants the one milestone that would actually move the needle, and a proposal to reach it. And she wants Danielle to push back on her if the target itself is wrong — because half the leadership team agrees with everything she says, and that is not accountability. That is silence with good manners. If Claire were not the kind of leader who asks to be contradicted, she would have lost this team a long time ago.

So Danielle opens with the number, not the story. She cuts to the chase: two of four, six weeks late, no owner. Then she puts forward one owner, one milestone and one date — September fifteenth. The academic directors will resist, and she wants them to resist in the room rather than after it, because their buy-in is not a nice-to-have: it is the deliverable itself. That single change, she argues, is what would bring about real accountability. Before she closes, she circles back to the engagement number, and names the week it will finally be measured.

1. Por que a frase "If someone had owned that deliverable, we would not be having this conversation today" usa would not be having e não would not have had?
A Porque a causa está no passado, mas o resultado é agora — e resultado no presente pede would + verbo, nunca would have.
B Porque as duas metades da frase estão no passado.
C Porque a conversa ainda pode acontecer no futuro.
2. "If Claire were not the kind of leader who asks to be contradicted, she would have lost this team a long time ago." Que direção é essa?
A Causa no passado, resultado no presente.
B Condição do presente (ela é assim, permanentemente) com resultado no passado — por isso were + would have lost.
C Uma condição real sobre o futuro.
3. According to the text, what is the real reason the onboarding deliverable is late?
A The cross-functional group does not have the bandwidth to do the work.
B Nobody owns the date, so nobody carries the accountability for it.
C The engagement KPI was measured too late in the quarter.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Mixed Conditionals

GRAMMAR

A gramática da comunicação executiva: as duas metades da frase ficam em tempos diferentes, porque a decisão está num tempo e o custo está em outro (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Direção 1 — condição
if + had + particípio		A decisão que NÃO foi tomada, no passado. The past cause.If we had shipped it in Q1...
Direção 1 — resultado
would + verbo (base)		O custo que você sente hoje. The present result....we would be measuring engagement right now.
Direção 2 — condição
if + past simple / were		Um limite permanente do presente. The present cause.If I were not leading two countries...
Direção 2 — resultado
would have + particípio		A consequência já encerrada, no passado. The past result....I would have delivered the diagnostic in July.
Negativahad not + particípio / would not be + -ingIf nobody had owned it, we wouldn't be here today.
InterrogativaWhere + would + sujeito + be + -ing + if...?Where would we be today if we had shipped it in Q1?
Discourse markersBuilding on that... (partindo disso...) · My take on this is... (a minha leitura é...) · What I would like to propose is... (o que eu gostaria de propor é...) · To circle back to... (retomando...)

Atenção (o erro do misto): se o resultado está acontecendo agora, o resultado é would be — nunca "would have been". "If we had launched in Q1, we would have been hitting our KPI now" está errado; o certo é "we would be hitting our KPI now".

Atenção (o erro de sempre): o would NUNCA entra na metade do if. "If I would be less busy" está errado — o certo é "If I were less busy". E no registro formal é sempre if I were, nunca "if I was".

Collocation: em inglês é align on priorities (nunca "align in"), reach a milestone (nunca "make a milestone"), e explain something to someone (nunca "explain me the OKR").

Quiz

B "Let me cut to the chase: it slipped six weeks, nobody owns the date, and I have a proposal."
C "Sorry, we are a bit late with that one. I will send you an update by email."
