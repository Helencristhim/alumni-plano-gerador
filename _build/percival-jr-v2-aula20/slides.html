Chapter 3 -- Reported Speech

Reported Speech

She said that... -- report what people told you

Grammar Notice

Notice the Change

What changes when we report the words?

Direct: "Meetings are difficult." → "She said that meetings were difficult."

Direct: "The audit is a success." → "She told me that the audit was a success."

Direct: "I will improve." → "He said that he would improve."

Direct: "I need more practice." → "I said that I needed more practice."

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

Direct words (now)Reported (one step back)
"Meetings are difficult."She said that meetings were difficult.
"The audit is a success."She told me that the audit was a success.
"I will improve."He said that he would improve.
"I need practice."I said that I needed practice.

Key idea: to report what someone said, use said (that) or told + person (that), and move the verb one step back: is/are → was/were, will → would, present → past. The word that is optional. Use said with no person ("she said that...") and told with a person ("she told me that..."). Never "she said me".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She said me that the report is ready."
"She told me that the report was ready."

Use told with a person (told me) and said with no person (said that), and move the verb one step back (is → was).

Grammar Practice
