Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Repare: cada exemplo usa TAMBÉM uma das seis estruturas que você aprendeu nas aulas 2 a 7.

Working capital -- the cash tied up in running the business day to day (capital de giro)
"If we had managed working capital better, we would not have needed the loan."
EBITDA -- earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization (EBITDA)
"EBITDA is not cash, and the board should have been told that."
Liquidity -- how easily you can turn what you own into cash (liquidez)
"What we lacked was liquidity, not profit."
Solvency -- whether you can pay your debts over the long term (solvência)
"Liquidity is this month. Solvency is this decade."
Audit committee -- the group of directors that oversees financial reporting (comitê de auditoria)
"The audit committee asked whether we had disclosed the provision."
Compliance -- following the rules and the regulations, on the record (conformidade, compliance)
"Not once have we had a compliance failure, and I intend to keep it that way."
Impairment -- writing an asset down because it lost value (impairment, redução ao valor recuperável)
"We had the asset tested and we booked an impairment."
Provision -- money set aside now for a loss that is likely later (provisão)
"I wish we had made a provision for the tax dispute."
Reconciliation -- checking that two sets of records actually agree (conciliação)
"I had the reconciliation done before the auditors arrived."
To restate -- to reissue financial statements that were wrong (reapresentar demonstrações)
"It was the revenue cut-off that forced us to restate."
Materiality -- whether a number is big enough to change a decision (materialidade)
"She cautioned that the error was below materiality, but not below embarrassment."
Headroom -- the space you still have before you hit a limit (folga antes do limite)
"We should have protected our headroom instead of spending it."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The annual close did not go well, and the audit committee wanted an explanation. It was the revenue cut-off that forced the restatement — not the audit, as the rumor had it. If the team had run the reconciliation a week earlier, they would have caught it before the close. Then there was the provision for the tax dispute, which should have been disclosed in March and was not. I wish I had brought it to the committee myself, Felipe said, instead of letting it sit in a report. The auditor cautioned that the error was below materiality, but not below embarrassment. So Felipe had the whole cut-off process re-tested, and he got the controller to sign it off personally. Not once will an unreconciled number reach that room again. On the covenant, the headroom is two points, monitored monthly. And on the question the committee actually cared about — what the company lacked was liquidity, not profit. EBITDA was never the problem. Solvency was never in doubt. Six structures, one letter: that is what a finance leader sounds like in English.

1. "It was the revenue cut-off that forced the restatement, not the audit." Qual ferramenta é essa, e para que serve?
A It-cleft — põe o holofote na causa REAL e exclui as alternativas (o boato de que foi a auditoria).
B Third conditional — analisa um passado que não aconteceu.
C Reported speech — carrega as palavras de outra pessoa.
2. "It should have been disclosed in March, and it was not." O que essa estrutura faz que o third conditional NÃO faria?
A Ela dá um VEREDITO sobre uma ação específica — não apenas analisa um cenário hipotético.
B Ela expressa arrependimento emocional, como o wish.
C Ela reporta o que outra pessoa disse.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A She asked me did we disclose the impairment.
B She asked me whether we had disclosed the impairment.
C She asked me if we disclose the impairment?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- As 6 Ferramentas do Bloco 1 (integração)

GRAMMAR

Uma tabela, seis ferramentas, seis trabalhos. O falante intermediário tem todas e pega a errada. O fluente pega sem olhar.

FormUse / UsoExample
Third conditional (Aula 2)ANALISAR um passado que não aconteceu. Sem culpa, só análise.If we had reconciled earlier, we would have caught it.
Modal perfects (Aula 3)VEREDITO sobre UMA ação. should julga a pessoa; could julga a situação.It should have been disclosed in March.
Wish / if only (Aula 4)REPARAR uma relação. A única ferramenta com emoção dentro.I wish I had brought it to you myself.
Reported speech (Aula 5)CARREGAR as palavras de outro com precisão. O tempo verbal recua.She cautioned that the error was below materiality.
Causative have/get (Aula 6)REPORTAR trabalho que aconteceu sem você. O autor desaparece.I had the process re-tested.
Inversion + cleft (Aula 7)COMANDAR a sala — e comprar meio segundo para pensar.What we lacked was liquidity, not profit.

O teste do meio do caminho: você já tem as seis ferramentas. O que separa o intermediário do fluente não é conhecer mais estruturas — é alcançar a certa sem olhar, sob pressão, quando alguém acabou de fazer uma pergunta difícil. E os três erros que voltam sempre: a metade com if nunca leva would; o wish sobre VOCÊ nunca leva would; e a pergunta reportada deixa de ser pergunta.

Stage 2: Put the Audit Committee Session in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma sessão de comitê de auditoria na ordem correta. A ordem é o que separa um CFO em quem se confia de um CFO que se interroga.

?Own what is yours before anyone has to ask — the undisclosed provision.
?Close by commanding the room: one takeaway, then stop talking.
?Name the real cause with a cleft — and kill the rumor.
?Say what you had done about it, and who signed it off.
?Report exactly what the auditor said, with the verb she earned.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

You want to ANALYZE a past that did not happen (no blame). You use:
A "We should have reconciled earlier."
B "If we had reconciled earlier, we would have caught it."
C "I wish we had reconciled earlier."
You want to give a VERDICT on one specific action that was not taken:
A "If it had been disclosed in March, this would not have happened."
B "It should have been disclosed in March, and it was not."
C "What we needed was disclosure in March."
You are talking to a PERSON you let down, and you want to repair it:
A "It should have been escalated to you."
B "I wish I had brought it to you myself."
C "If I had brought it to you, you would have known."
The auditor said the error was small but awkward. You REPORT her:
A "She said the error is below materiality."
B "She cautioned that the error was below materiality, but not below embarrassment."
C "She asked was the error below materiality."
You want to COMMAND the room and name the one thing that mattered:
A "We didn't have enough liquidity, and profit was fine."
B "What we lacked was liquidity, not profit."
C "I wish we had had more liquidity."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

Chair a full audit committee session, out loud, for 3 minutes. You must use all six structures at least once: analyze the restatement (third conditional), give a verdict on the undisclosed provision (modal perfect), repair it with the committee (I wish), report what the auditor said (reported speech), describe what you had re-tested (causative), and close by commanding the room (cleft or inversion). Nobody will tell you which tool to use. Record this one — it is your midpoint baseline.
