Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Product -- a thing that a company makes or sells
"Honey is a popular local product in my region."
Customer -- a person who buys things from a shop
"Every customer likes our fresh coffee."
Supplier -- a company that sells goods to another business
"Our supplier brings honey from a small farm."
Shelf -- a flat board where you put products in a shop
"There are many jars on the shelf."
Jar -- a glass container for food like honey or jam
"There are ten jars of honey in the box."
Honey -- a sweet natural food that bees make
"There is a lot of honey in our shop."
Coffee -- a hot drink made from brown beans
"There is fresh coffee from our region."
Quality -- how good or bad a product is
"Our products have very good quality."
Stock -- all the products a shop has ready to sell
"We have a lot of coffee in stock."
Price -- the amount of money you pay for a product
"The price of the honey is very good."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia has a stand at the local products fair. There is a big shelf with many products from her region. There are two hundred jars of honey on the table, and there is fresh coffee from a small supplier. "There is a special price for every customer today," she says. The quality is very high, so there are always many customers at her stand. There is not much stock left after lunch, because people love the honey. A buyer asks, "How many jars are there?" and Flavia answers with a smile.

1. Why do we say "There are two hundred jars"?
A "Jars" is plural and countable, so we use "are".
B "Jars" is uncountable, so we use "are".
C It is a question, so we use "are".
2. Why do we say "There is fresh coffee"?
A "Coffee" is plural.
B "Coffee" is uncountable, so we use "is".
C "Coffee" is always a question.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "How much jars are there?"
B "How many jars are there?"
C "How many honey is there?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There Is / There Are

GRAMMAR

How to say what exists, with countable and uncountable nouns.

UseWhenExample
There isone thing, or an uncountable nounThere is a jar. · There is honey.
There aretwo or more countable thingsThere are ten jars.
Countablea / an, numbers, many, How many?jars, products, customers, suppliers
Uncountablesome, much, a lot of, How much? (no plural, no a/an): honey, coffee, quality, stock. There is not much stock. Is there any coffee?
