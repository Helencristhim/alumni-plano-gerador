Chapter 3: There Is, There Are

There Is vs There Are

Countable and uncountable nouns

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"There is a shelf with local products."

"There is a lot of honey in stock."

"There are ten jars on the shelf."

"There are many customers at the fair."

Why is for a shelf and for honey, but are for jars and customers?

UseWhenExample
There isone thing, or an uncountable nounThere is a jar. · There is honey.
There aretwo or more countable thingsThere are ten jars.
QuestionsHow many (countable) · How much (uncountable)How many jars? · How much honey?

Count it (jars, customers) = there are. Cannot count it (honey, coffee, stock) = there is.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"There is many jars on the shelf."
"There are many jars on the shelf."
"There are many honeys in stock."
"There is a lot of honey in stock."
"How many honey is there?"
"How much honey is there?"

Count it (jars, customers) → there are and how many. Cannot count it (honey, coffee) → there is and how much.

Chapter 4: At the Product Fair

A Buyer Visits Your Stand

The conversation begins

Dialogue

Welcome to My Stand

M
Hello! Your stand is beautiful. Are all these products from your region?
F
Yes, welcome! There are many local products here. There is honey, and there is fresh coffee too.
M
I am a buyer for a small shop in Portugal, and I love honey. How many jars do you have in stock?
F
There are about two hundred jars. Every jar comes from a small supplier near my city.
M
Very good. And the quality — is it always this high?
F
The quality is excellent, and our customers come back every week. The price is fair for everyone.
