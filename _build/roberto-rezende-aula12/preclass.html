Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Discount -- a reduction in the normal price
"Can you give us a discount?" (Desconto)
Bulk order -- an order for a large quantity at once
"For a bulk order, we need a better price." (Pedido em grande quantidade)
Terms -- the conditions of an agreement or deal
"We need flexible terms." (Condições / termos)
Offer -- a price or proposal you put forward
"Do not accept the first offer." (Oferta / proposta)
Counteroffer -- a new offer made in reply to another
"I made a counteroffer with better terms." (Contraproposta)
Flexible -- able to change to fit a situation
"They were flexible on the price." (Flexível)
Margin -- the profit between cost and price
"We must protect our margin." (Margem de lucro)
Volume -- the total quantity or amount
"If the volume is high, we will lower the price." (Volume / quantidade)
Supplier -- a company that provides goods or services
"Our supplier is in Shanghai." (Fornecedor)
Meet halfway -- to compromise so both sides agree
"Let's meet halfway on the delivery cost." (Chegar a um meio-termo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Amy briefs Roberto before he meets the supplier. "Their first offer will be high," she says. "If you order a large volume, ask for a discount. If they offer five percent off, we will sign a one-year deal." At the table, the supplier asks forty dollars per unit. Roberto answers: "That is too high for a bulk order. If you give us flexible terms, we will sign today." They agree to meet halfway on the delivery cost, and Roberto protects his margin. Notice: to set a real future condition, we use If + present simple (you order, they offer) and will + base verb (we will sign, we will offer). The result part has will, never the if part.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Use o first conditional para combinar condições numa negociação. / Use the first conditional to set conditions in a negotiation.

Part / ParteForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Condição / Condition (if)If + present simpleIf you order 500 units,
Resultado / Resultwill + verbo basewe will offer a discount.
Ordem inversa / Reverseresult + if + presentWe will sign if you give us terms.
Negativa / Negative ifIf + present negativeIf they are not flexible, we will leave.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o first conditional fala de uma condição real no futuro. A parte com if usa o presente simples (order, is, give); o resultado usa will + verbo base (will offer, will sign). Depois de if, nunca use will -- diga "If you order", não "If you will order". Quando If vem primeiro, use vírgula.
