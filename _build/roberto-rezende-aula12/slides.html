Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Making conditions: the first conditional

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"If you order 500 units, we will offer a discount."

"If the volume is high, we will lower the price."

"We will sign the deal if you give us flexible terms."

"If their terms are not flexible, we will look at another supplier."

Grammar Rule

The First Conditional

PartFormExample
Condition (if)If + present simpleIf you order 500 units,
Resultwill + base verbwe will offer a discount.
Order can changeresult + if + presentWe will sign if you give us terms.
Negative ifIf + present negativeIf they are not flexible, we will leave.

Key idea: the first conditional talks about a real future condition. The if part uses the present simple (order, is, give); the result uses will + base verb (will offer, will sign). When If comes first, use a comma.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If you will order 500 units, we will offer a discount."
"If you order 500 units, we will offer a discount."

After if, use the present simple (order, is, give). Keep will only in the result part.
