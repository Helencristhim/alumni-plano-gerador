Chapter 3: The Code

First ... Then ... Finally

The words that order the steps

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"First, I set up the test account."

"Then I enter the input and click the button."

"After that, I wait for the outcome."

"Finally, I save the results and submit the report."

What do the orange words do in each sentence?

SequencerUsePosition
First, / To start,The very first stepStart of the sentence, + comma
Then / Next, / After that,The following steps, in order"then" no comma; "next / after that" + comma
Before / OnceOne action in relation to another"Before you proceed, ..." / "Once it loads, ..."
Finally,The last stepStart of the sentence, + comma

Use each sequencer once, in order. Do not say "first" twice or "after that" before the first step.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"After I enter the input, after that I click."
"I enter the input. After that, I click the button."
"Firstly then I set up the account."
"First, I set up the account. Then I enter the input."
"In the final, I submit the report."
"Finally, I submit the report."

Each sequencer marks ONE step, in order. Do not repeat "after", do not join "firstly" and "then", and say finally, not "in the final".

Chapter 4: Walk Me Through It

Helping Lucas

A new teammate needs your walk-through

Dialogue

Walk Him Through It

L
Hi Ana, I am new here. Could you walk me through this test case for the login page?
A
Of course. First, we set up the test account and make sure the precondition is ready.
L
Okay. What is the precondition here?
A
The test account must already exist. Then we open the login page and enter the input: the email and the password.
L
Got it. And after the input, what do we do to trigger the check?
A
We click the login button. After that, we wait for the outcome: the dashboard opens. We also try one edge case with a wrong password.
L
That is really clear. So finally, we save the results?
A
Exactly. Finally, we save the results and submit the report. The whole sequence is straightforward once you follow the steps in order.
Listening

How to Write a Test Case

A senior QA explains the routine. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What is the first thing you should always check?
The precondition, before you start.
2. In what order should you write the steps?
In sequence, one step at a time, in the order they happen.
3. Why should you add an edge case?
Because real users make mistakes, like a wrong password or an empty field.
