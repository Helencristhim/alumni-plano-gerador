Chapter 1: The Briefing Floor

The Long Story vs the Summary

Long: "First the server crashed, then we found a bug, then testing took longer, then we asked for more time, and..."

Summary: "In short, the launch is on track. The one risk is a minor delay, but we have a workaround."

The summary leads with the outcome and stays short.

Chapter 3

The Structure Rule

Headline, recap, takeaway, next step

Structure Discovery

Listen and Notice

In short, the launch is on track.

First, the system is live. Then, the team is trained.

Overall, adoption is high.

The key takeaway is that we are on schedule.

Which sentence is the headline? Which words guide the listener?

Structure Rule

Four Parts of a Summary

PartHowExample
1. Headlinein short / to sum up + the outcome"In short, the launch is on track."
2. Recapfirst / then / overall + key points"First, it is live. Overall, adoption is high."
3. Takeawaythe key takeaway is..."The key takeaway is we are on schedule."
4. Next stepClose with the next action. "The next step is the final review on Friday."
Common Mistake

Buried vs Headline-First

✗ BURIED

"First this, then that, then... and so the launch is on track."

✓ HEADLINE-FIRST

"In short, the launch is on track. Here is a quick recap..."

✗ WRONG

"Let me give you summary."

✓ RIGHT

"Let me give you a summary." (a / the + summary)

Lead with the result, and use the article: "a summary", "the takeaway".
