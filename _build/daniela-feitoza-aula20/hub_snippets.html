Warm-up — Callback da Aula 19

Review

Na Aula 19 você aprendeu a dar notícias ruins com diplomacia (it seems / might / a slight delay). Agora, na sua aula final, você junta tudo do ano em uma habilidade: o executive summary. Quatro partes: abra com o headline (in short / to sum up + o resultado); dê um recap curto com signposting (first / then / overall); dê o takeaway (the key takeaway is...); feche com o next step. Lidere com o resultado e seja concisa. (Headline first. Then the details.)

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Summary -- a short version that gives only the main points (PT: resumo)
"Here is a quick summary of the project."
Headline -- the opening line that states the main result first (PT: manchete / frase de abertura)
"The headline is: the launch is on track."
Recap -- a quick review of the key points so far (PT: revisão rápida / recapitulação)
"Let me give a quick recap of the results."
Overview -- a general view of the whole, without details (PT: visão geral / panorama)
"First, a brief overview of the project."
Highlight -- the most important and positive point to share (PT: destaque / ponto alto)
"The main highlight is high adoption."
Takeaway -- the one main message you want people to remember (PT: conclusão principal / lição)
"The key takeaway: we are on schedule."
Outcome -- the final result of a project or action (PT: resultado final / desfecho)
"The outcome is a successful launch."
Concise -- short and clear, with no extra words (PT: conciso / breve e claro)
"Keep the summary concise and clear."
Brief -- short and to the point; a short summary (PT: breve / resumido)
"I will give you a brief update."
Gist -- the main idea or essential point of something (PT: essência / ideia principal)
"The gist is that we are on track."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A busy board has little time, so Daniela leads with the result. First, the headline: "In short, the launch is on track." Then a brief recap with signposting: "First, the system is live. Then, the team is trained. Overall, adoption is high." She keeps it concise — short and clear, never a long story. She softens the one risk with the diplomacy from last lesson ("it seems there is a minor delay, but we have a workaround"). Finally, she gives the takeaway ("the key takeaway is that we are on schedule") and the next step. Remember: the result goes first, and "a summary" needs the article — "Let me give you a summary", never "Let me give you summary".

1. Where does the headline go in a good summary?
A First, before the details.
B Last, after the long story.
C It does not matter.
2. Which words guide the listener through the recap?
A because / so
B first / then / overall
C might / may
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Let me give you summary."
B "Let me give you a summary."
C "The takeaway are on schedule."
