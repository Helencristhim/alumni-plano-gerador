Lesson 9 -- Describing Products and Solutions

Series
Silicon Valley -- Season 1, Episode 5: "Signaling Risk"

Watch how the team pitches their product to clients and investors. Notice how they describe features and benefits and compare their solution with the competition using adjectives like faster, better, and more reliable.

Write down every adjective and comparative you hear. Pause and repeat each one, then describe an imaginary product with it.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Describing Products and Making Comparisons"

Practical tips for a sales pitch: how to describe a product with strong adjectives (reliable, affordable, durable, efficient) and how to compare two options with comparatives. Listen for the new words: feature, benefit, solution, and upgrade.

Note 3 comparatives the host uses. Practice each one out loud with -er or more, and the word than.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Comparative Adjectives: -er and more"

A short, clear lesson on comparatives in English: when to add -er to short adjectives, when to use more with long adjectives, and how to use than to compare two things. Perfect for a product pitch.

After watching, record yourself comparing two products with five comparatives. Use at least three new words from this lesson.
