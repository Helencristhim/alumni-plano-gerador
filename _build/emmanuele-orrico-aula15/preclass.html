Lesson 15 -- Pre-class

Have You Replied Yet? -- Handling the Global Inbox on a Follow-up Call

Answer your inbox out loud for David and the Global team: what you have already done, what you have just done, and what you haven't finished yet. Key words: inbox, reply, attachment, reminder, draft, thread, to forward, to reach out. The grammar of the lesson is the present perfect with just, already and yet. Say an action is done with have / has + the verb ("I have sent the report"). Use just for a moment ago ("I've just replied"), already for done sooner than expected ("I've already forwarded it"), and yet for what is still open, in questions and negatives ("Have you sent it yet?", "Not yet").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect: just / already / yet

GRAMMAR

To say an action is done — without saying exactly when — use have / has + the verb. Three small words tell the story.

FormStructureExample
Positive (+)
done		have + just / already + verbI have just sent it.
I have already replied.
Negative (−)
still open		haven't + verb + yetI haven't finished it yet.
Question (?)Have you + verb + yet?Have you sent it yet?

Careful: use the verb in the right form — "have sent", not "have send". Put just and already between have and the verb. Put yet at the end, and only in questions and negatives — in a positive sentence, use already, not "yet".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for two minutes, with no script.

David calls for a follow-up and asks: "What have you done today, and what is still open?" Give a full inbox update, out loud: open with "Hi David, quick update...", say two things you have already done or just done (already sent, just replied, forwarded the thread), say one thing you haven't finished yet (the access summary), and end with what you will do next (reach out to the team this afternoon). Use at least two positive sentences with already or just, and one negative with yet. Take your time and do not read from a script.
