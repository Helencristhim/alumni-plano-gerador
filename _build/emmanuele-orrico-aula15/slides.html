Chapter 3: Just, Already, Yet

Done, or Not Done Yet

I have sent it · I have just replied · not yet

Grammar Discovery

Have You Done It Yet?

"I have sent the report." (it is done)

"I have just replied to Claire." (a minute ago)

"I have already forwarded the thread." (sooner than expected)

"I haven't finished it yet." (still open)

HAVE + verb = it is done. JUST = a minute ago. ALREADY = done, sooner than expected. YET = still open (questions and negatives).

WordMeaningExample
justa moment agoI have just sent it.
alreadydone, sooner than expectedI have already replied.
yetstill open (question / negative)Have you sent it yet? / Not yet.

Form: have / has + the verb (sent, replied, forwarded). It says the action is DONE — without saying exactly when.

The Form

have + verb · just / already / yet

done (+)I have just / already sent it.
not done (−)I haven't sent it yet.
question (?)Have you sent it yet?

just and already go between HAVE and the verb. yet goes at the end — only in questions and negatives.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have send the report."
"I have sent the report."
"Did you reply to Claire yet?"
"Have you replied to Claire yet?"
"I have sent it yet."
"I have already sent it."

Use have / has + the verb: "have sent", "have replied". For a question about what is still open, use Have you...? yet. And yet is only for questions and negatives — in a positive sentence, use already.
