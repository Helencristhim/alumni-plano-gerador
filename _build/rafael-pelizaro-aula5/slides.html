Sprint 14 Review — Customer Portal

Every two weeks, my team holds a sprint review. A sprint is a short period of work, usually two weeks, in which we build one small part of the product. The sprint review is the meeting where we show that work to the people who pay for the project and use it. Most of them are not engineers, so my real job in the room is to explain our progress to an outsider, in plain terms.

I never open with jargon; if a word is too technical, I rephrase it in plain English. Instead of saying that we cleared the backlog, I say we finished the list of tasks that were waiting. Instead of a new feature, I talk about a new button that lets a customer reset a password alone. I also like a simple analogy: our app is like a house, and this sprint we built the front door. A good summary gives the big picture first, and the small details only if someone asks for them.

Of course, not everything goes to plan. This sprint we found a bug that slowed the login screen, so one feature is not ready yet. I tell the stakeholders the truth, but I keep it simple: the part that handles photos needs one more week. Then I show the demo, the short live version of what already works, because people trust what they can see. A review that anyone can follow is a review that builds confidence.

Adapted for class use from an internal agile sprint review summary

Chapter 4: The Code

Defining with that / which / who

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"It is a button that lets a customer reset a password."

"A backlog is the list which holds the waiting tasks."

"A stakeholder is a person who cares about the result."

"We found a bug that slowed the login screen."

WordUseExample
thatthings or people (neutral, very common)a button that lets you reset
whichthings onlythe list which holds the tasks
whopeople onlya person who cares

A defining relative clause adds the key information that says which thing or person you mean. It comes right after the noun: noun + that / which / who + verb. This is how you turn jargon into a plain definition.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

It is a button what lets you reset a password.
It is a button that lets you reset a password.

To define a thing, use that or which, never what.

A stakeholder is a person who he cares about the result.
A stakeholder is a person who cares about the result.

After who / that / which, do not repeat the subject (no extra he, it, they).

Chapter 5: In the Review

Presenting the Sprint

Dialogue

The Review Meeting

S
Thanks, Rafael. Before the demo, can you give me a quick summary, in plain English?
R
Of course. This sprint we built a new button that lets customers reset their own password.
S
Nice. And what is a backlog? Your team keeps using that word.
R
Good question. A backlog is the list of tasks that are still waiting to be built. Think of it as a to-do list for the whole product.
S
That helps. Is everything ready to launch?
R
Almost. We found a bug that slowed the login, so the photo feature needs one more week. Everything else works, and I will show it in the demo now.
Comprehension

About the Meeting

1. What did the team build this sprint?
A new button that lets customers reset their own password.
2. How does Rafael define a backlog for Sofia?
The list of tasks that are still waiting to be built; a to-do list for the product.
3. Why is the photo feature not ready?
A bug slowed the login, so it needs one more week.

Listening 2

Marketing's Feedback

A voicemail. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00

The house analogy and seeing the password button work live in the demo.
2. What does Sofia ask Rafael to do with words like backlog?
