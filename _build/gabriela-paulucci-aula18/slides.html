Grammar Rule

Do you have...? / I would like...

FunctionStructureExample
Receptionist asksDo you have + a + thing?Do you have a reservation?
You answerYes, I have + a + thing.Yes, I have a reservation.
You request (polite)I would like + a + thing.I would like a double room.

Key idea: use I would like to ask for something in a polite way. It is softer than "I want": say "I would like a room", not "I want a room".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You have a double room?" (no Do)
"Do you have a double room?"

To ask, start with Do you have...? To answer, say Yes, I have....
