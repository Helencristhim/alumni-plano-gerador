Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each presentation word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Audience — the group of people who listen to your talk
"The audience clapped at the end." (plateia)
Outline — a short plan that shows the main parts of your talk
"I wrote an outline before I started." (roteiro)
Rehearse — to practice your talk out loud before you give it
"I rehearsed three times at home." (ensaiar)
Takeaway — the main idea you want the audience to remember
"My takeaway is: keep practicing." (mensagem principal)
Visual aid — a picture, slide or object that supports your talk
"My visual aid was one simple slide." (recurso visual)
Engage — to hold the interest and attention of your audience
"Eye contact helps you engage people." (cativar)
Confident — sure of yourself; not afraid to speak
"She looked calm and confident on stage." (confiante)
Pace — the speed at which you speak during a talk
"Slow your pace so people follow you." (ritmo)
Recap — to say the main points again, in short, at the end
"Let me recap the three key ideas." (recapitular)
Highlight — to draw attention to an important idea
"I want to highlight one key point." (destacar)
Nervous — worried or anxious, especially before speaking
"I felt nervous, then I took a breath." (nervoso)
Transition — a smooth change from one part of a talk to the next
"A good transition links your ideas." (transição)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Signpost Language

GRAMMAR

Numa mini-apresentação, usamos frases de sinalização (signpost language) para guiar o público de uma parte para a outra. / In a mini-presentation, use signpost phrases to guide your audience from one part to the next.

Stage / EtapaSignpost phraseUse
Opening / AberturaLet me start with...introduce the first idea
Transition / TransiçãoMoving on to...move to the next idea
Emphasis / ÊnfaseI would like to highlight...stress one key point
Closing / FechamentoTo sum up...recap and give the takeaway

Key point / Ponto-chave: signpost phrases são como placas na estrada: dizem ao público onde você está e para onde vai. Comece com "Let me start with", mude de tema com "Moving on to", e termine com "To sum up" + um takeaway claro.
