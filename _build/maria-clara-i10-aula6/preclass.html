Lesson 06 -- Pre-class

Where Are We Going -- Future Plans with "Going To" for the Seoul Trip

Talk about your plans for the future: the flight, the visa, the meetings in Seoul. Key words: flight, itinerary, luggage, accommodation, visa, embassy, departure, arrival. Structure: "going to" (be + going to + base verb) for plans and intentions. And two phrases for planning: "In my opinion..." and "Better safe than sorry.".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Take your time — listen twice, then say it out loud. These are the eight words of the journey to Seoul.

Flight -- a trip in an airplane, from one place to another
"My flight to Seoul is going to depart in November."
Itinerary -- a plan of a trip, with the dates and the places
"The itinerary shows three days in Seoul."
Luggage -- the bags and suitcases you carry when you travel
"I am going to take only one small piece of luggage."
Accommodation -- the place where you stay on a trip, like a hotel
"The university is going to book my accommodation."
Visa -- an official document that lets you enter a country
"I am going to apply for a visa next week."
Embassy -- the official office of one country inside another country
"I am going to visit the Korean embassy for my visa."
Departure -- the moment when you leave a place
"The departure is on Monday morning."
Arrival -- the moment when you reach a place
"The arrival in Seoul is on Tuesday afternoon."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Future with "Going To" (I am going to travel / she is going to present)

GRAMMAR

Use going to for a plan or intention for the future. It always has three parts: the verb be (am / is / are) + going to + base verb. The verb after "going to" never changes.

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
II am going to travelI am not going to travelAm I going to travel?
he / she / itShe is going to travelShe is not going to travelIs she going to travel?
you / we / theyWe are going to travelWe are not going to travelAre we going to travel?

The base verb never changes: going to travel, going to meet, going to apply (never "travels", never "to travel"). Time words: next week, next month, in November, soon, before the trip. Useful phrase: In my opinion + your idea ("In my opinion, it is better to travel light").

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for about a minute, with no script. This is your rehearsal for telling a colleague about the trip.

Imagine a colleague asks about your trip to Seoul. Describe your plan in 4-5 sentences, all with "going to": what you are going to do before the trip, when your flight is going to depart, and what you are going to do in Seoul. Use: I am going to..., My flight is going to..., In my opinion... Take your time and don't read from a script.
