Chapter 4: The Code

Future with Going To

I am going to travel · she is going to present · are you going to meet the rector?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am going to travel to Seoul in November. My assistant is going to book the flight."

"We are going to meet the rector on Wednesday. She is going to present the agenda."

"I am not going to bring an interpreter. I am going to speak English on my own."

"Are you going to apply for a visa? Yes, I am."

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
II am going to travelI am not going to travelAm I going to travel?
he / she / itShe is going to travelShe is not going to travelIs she going to travel?
you / we / theyWe are going to travelWe are not going to travelAre we going to travel?

Use going to for a plan or intention for the future: be (am / is / are) + going to + base verb. The verb after "going to" never changes: going to travel, going to meet (never "travels", never "to travel"). Time words: next week, in November, soon, before the trip.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I going to travel to Seoul."
"I am going to travel to Seoul."
"She is going to travels tomorrow."
"She is going to travel tomorrow."

Going to always has three parts: be (am / is / are) + going to + base verb — and the base verb never changes.

Chapter 5: Talking About the Trip

The Plan for Seoul

A colleague asks: "So, are you going to Seoul in November?" — and this time, you answer with the whole plan

Dialogue

Are You Going to Seoul?

D
So, Maria Clara, I hear you are going to Seoul in November. Are you going to travel alone this time?
M
Yes, I am. I am going to travel alone, and I am going to speak English on my own.
D
That is wonderful. When is your flight going to depart?
M
My flight is going to depart on Monday morning. The arrival in Seoul is on Tuesday.
D
And your accommodation? Are you going to stay near the university?
M
Yes. My assistant is going to book a small hotel near the conference. In my opinion, that is easier.
D
Good idea. Do you have your visa already?
M
Not yet. Tomorrow, I am going to apply for my visa at the embassy. Better safe than sorry.
D
You are very organized. Are you going to present your work at the conference?
M
Yes, I am. I am going to present my work in English. I am a little nervous, but I am ready.
