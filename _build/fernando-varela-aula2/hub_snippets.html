Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Join -- entrar, juntar-se a (to become a member of a company — past: joined)
"I joined the hospital in 2004."
Start -- começar (to begin something — past: started)
"I started in the finance department."
Become -- tornar-se (to change into something over time — past: became)
"I became a business manager."
Hire -- contratar (to give a person a job — past: hired)
"We hired two new analysts last year."
Promotion -- promoção (a move to a higher job position)
"I got a promotion after five years."
Background -- formação, histórico (your past experience and studies)
"My background is in finance."
Experience -- experiência (knowledge you get from doing a job)
"I have 24 years of experience."
Career -- carreira (your work life over many years)
"My career started in finance."
Ago -- atrás (used to say how far back in the past)
"I joined twenty years ago."
Department -- departamento (a part of a company)
"I started in the finance department."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Fernando joined the hospital in 2004. He started in the finance department as a financial analyst. His background is in economics. After a few years, he got his first promotion. Then he became a business manager. Ten years ago, he moved to oncology. Later, the network grew very fast, and he hired new colleagues. Today, he has twenty-four years of experience and a long career at one hospital. "I started in finance, and then everything changed," he says.

1. Por que dizemos "Fernando joined" e não "Fernando join"?
A Porque a ação acontece agora, no presente.
B Porque é uma ação terminada no passado, e o verbo regular leva -ed.
C Porque "Fernando" é plural.
2. "He became a manager" usa "became" porque:
A "become" é um verbo irregular, e o passado não leva -ed.
B "becomed" também estaria correto.
C É o presente do verbo "become".
3. Which question about the past is correct?
A "When you started here?"
B "When did you start here?"
C "When did you started here?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple & Time Markers

GRAMMAR

Como usar o past simple para contar a sua carreira (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Regular verb + -edAção terminada no passadoI joined in 2004.
Irregular verb (forma nova)Não leva -ed: become → became, grow → grew, get → gotI became a manager.
did + verbo basePerguntas no passado (verbo SEM -ed)When did you start?
Time markersMarcam o tempo: in 2004, twenty years ago, then, after that, later

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação profissional.

A partner asks: "When did you start at the hospital?" You answer:
A "I start in 2004."
B "I joined the hospital in 2004, twenty years ago."
C "I am starting in 2004."
Someone asks about your background. The best answer is:
A "My background is in finance and economics."
B "My background make finance."
C "I background finance."
You want to describe how your career changed. You say:
A "I become a manager and grow the network."
B "I started in finance, then I became a manager and grew the network."
C "I becomed a manager and growed the network."
You want to ask a colleague about their first job. The correct question is:
A "What was your first role you had?"
B "What was your first role? When did you join?"
C "When you joined your first role?"
