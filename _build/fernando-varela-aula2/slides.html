Chapter 3

The Past Code

Past simple & time markers to tell your story

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I joined the hospital in 2004."

"I started in the finance department."

"Then I became a business manager."

"The network grew very fast."

TypeRuleExample
Regular verbsadd -edjoin → joined
Irregular verbsnew form (no -ed)become → became, grow → grew
Questionsdid + base verbWhen did you start?
Time markersin 2004 · twenty years ago · then · after that · later
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I join the hospital in 2004."

✓ RIGHT

"I joined the hospital in 2004."

✗ WRONG

"I becomed a manager."

✓ RIGHT

"I became a manager."

✗ WRONG

"When you started here?"

✓ RIGHT

"When did you start here?"

Regular verbs add -ed. Irregular verbs change form: become → became. In questions, use did + the base verb: When did you start?

Chapter 4

Telling Your Story

A conversation about your career

Dialogue

My Career in Three Minutes

S
Fernando, can I ask about your career? When did you start here?
F
Of course. I joined the hospital in 2004. I started in the finance department.
S
Interesting! What was your first role?
F
I was a financial analyst. Then, after a few years, I became a manager.
S
A great journey. When did you move to oncology?
F
About ten years ago. Later, I grew the oncology network.
S
Amazing. So you have a lot of experience here.
F
Yes. Twenty-four years of experience, and a long career at one hospital.
