Explaining your role, your team and your processes to an international colleague. New words: oversee, liaise with, handle, support, implement, recruit, retain, department, streamline, onboarding, workload, cross-functional. Grammar: defining relative clauses (who / which / that) + the passive for HR processes + frequency adverbs.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Oversee -- to manage and be responsible for an area or team (supervisionar)
"I oversee the team that handles compensation."
Liaise with -- to work closely and exchange information with another team
"I liaise with the managers who lead each unit."
Handle -- to be responsible for and deal with a task or process (lidar com)
"Recruitment is handled by my department."
Support -- to help an area by giving it the resources it needs
"We support Finance, Legal, and Sustainability."
Implement -- to put a plan, policy or system into action (implementar)
"The policies that we implement are reviewed every year."
Recruit -- to find, attract and hire new people for a company
"We recruit new talent for the operations in Chile."
Retain -- to keep your best employees so they stay in the company (reter)
"We work hard to retain our key people."
Department -- a section of a company responsible for one main function
"The people department manages talent and culture."
Streamline -- to make a process simpler, faster and more efficient (otimizar)
"I streamline any process that is too slow."
Onboarding -- the process of welcoming and training new employees (integracao)
"New hires are onboarded within their first week."
Workload -- the amount of work a person or team has to do (carga de trabalho)
"When the workload is heavy, the tasks are shared."
Cross-functional -- involving people from several different departments
"We work on cross-functional projects across the company."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Murielle is the HR manager who oversees the business partner team at Minerva Foods. Recruitment is handled by her department, and new hires are onboarded within their first week. She works closely with the managers who lead the operations in Chile and Australia. The policies that her team implements are reviewed every year. When the workload is heavy, the tasks are shared across the team. She usually streamlines any process that is too slow. "It depends on the business unit," she often says.

1. In "the managers who lead the operations", why do we use "who"?
A Because the action is in the past.
B Because "who" adds information about people (a relative clause).
C Because "managers" is singular.
2. "Recruitment is handled by her department" is an example of:
A The passive, because the focus is on the process, not on who does it.
B A question in the present simple.
C A future plan.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "New hires is onboarded within a week."
B "New hires are onboarded within a week."
C "New hires are onboard within a week."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses & the Passive for Processes

GRAMMAR

Use relative clauses (who / which / that) to add information, and the passive (is / are + past participle) to describe processes when the doer is not important.

FormUseExample
... who + verbRelative clause for people (pessoas)the managers who lead each unit
... which / that + verbRelative clause for things / processes (coisas)the policy that we implement
is / are + past participlePassive: focus on the process (voz passiva)Recruitment is handled by my team.
Is / Are + subject + past participle?Passive questionAre new hires onboarded quickly?
always / usually / often / sometimes / rarely / neverFrequency adverb before the main verb: I usually streamline slow processes.
