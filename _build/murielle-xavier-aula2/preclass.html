This Is What I Do -- Describing Your HR Role

Explaining your role, your team and your routine to an international colleague. New words: oversee, liaise with, handle, support, implement, recruit, retain, department. Grammar: present simple for habits and routines + adverbs of frequency.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Oversee -- to manage and be responsible for an area or team
"I oversee a team of HR business partners."
Liaise with -- to work and share information with other people
"I liaise with managers in Brazil and Australia."
Handle -- to deal with or be responsible for something
"My team handles compensation and benefits."
Support -- to help an area and give it what it needs
"We support Finance, Legal, and Sustainability."
Implement -- to put a plan or a policy into action
"We implement new HR policies every year."
Recruit -- to find and hire new people for a company
"We recruit new talent for the operations in Chile."
Retain -- to keep your good employees in the company
"We work hard to retain our key people."
Department -- a section of a company with one main function
"The people department manages talent and culture."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Murielle works at Minerva Foods, a global food company. She oversees a small HR team, and she liaises with managers in Brazil, Chile, and Australia. Every Monday, the team starts with a short call. Murielle usually handles compensation, and she supports the corporate areas. The department also recruits new talent and works hard to retain key people. She reports to the people director. She rarely leaves the office before eight, because the traffic is heavy. Does she enjoy her job? Yes, she always says her team is the best part.

1. Why do we write "She oversees" with an -s?
A Because the action happened in the past.
B Because in the present simple, she/he/it takes -s on the verb.
C Because "she" is plural.
2. "Every Monday, the team starts with a call" describes:
A A regular habit or routine.
B Something happening right now.
C A plan for next year.
3. Which question is correct in the present simple?
A "She enjoy her job?"
B "Does she enjoy her job?"
C "Do she enjoys her job?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & Frequency Adverbs

GRAMMAR

Use the present simple for habits, routines, and permanent facts. Frequency adverbs say how often.

FormUseExample
I / You / We + verbHabits and routines (no -s)I oversee the team.
She / He / It + verb+sThird person always takes -sShe handles compensation.
don't / doesn't + verbNegative formWe don't deploy on Fridays.
Do / Does + subject + verbQuestion formDoes your team handle salaries?
always / usually / often / sometimes / rarely / neverBefore the main verb, after be: I usually start at nine. / She is always busy.
