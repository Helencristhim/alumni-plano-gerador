Chapter 3

Describing with Precision

Relative clauses and the passive for processes

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I manage the team that handles compensation."

"I liaise with the managers who lead our units."

"Recruitment is handled by my department."

"New hires are onboarded within a week."

StructureUseExample
... who + verbRelative clause for peoplemanagers who lead units
... which / that + verbRelative clause for things / processesthe policy that we implement
is / are + past participlePassive: focus on the processRecruitment is handled.
Is / Are + subject + past participle?Passive questionAre new hires onboarded?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The managers which lead the units."

✓ RIGHT

"The managers who lead the units."

✗ WRONG

"Recruitment handled by my team."

✓ RIGHT

"Recruitment is handled by my team."

✗ WRONG

"New hires is onboarded fast."

✓ RIGHT

"New hires are onboarded fast."

Use "who" for people and "which / that" for things. In the passive, always keep is / are + past participle, and match the subject (is / are).

Chapter 4

Explaining Your Role

Onboarding a new international colleague

Dialogue

Liam's First Day

L
Hi Murielle, I'm Liam, from the Australia office. So, what does your team actually do here?
M
Hi Liam, welcome! I oversee the team that handles compensation and onboarding.
L
Nice. And who do you report to?
M
I report to the people director, and I liaise with the managers who lead each unit.
L
Got it. Is recruitment handled by your team too?
M
Yes. Recruitment and onboarding are handled by my department, and we run cross-functional projects.
L
Sounds busy. How do you manage the workload?
M
We streamline the processes that slow us down. It depends on the business unit.
