Chapter 3 -- The Present Perfect

What Is Already Done

have / has + past participle

Grammar Notice

Notice the Pattern

What do the highlighted words have in common?

"I have attached the report to this email."

"Karen has just sent the email to the supplier."

"Have you received my email yet?"

"I have not replied to the supplier yet."

Grammar Rule

The Present Perfect

FormPatternExample
PositiveI / you / we / they have + past participleI have attached the report.
Positive (he/she)he / she / it has + past participleKaren has sent the email.
Negativehave not / has not + past participleI have not replied yet.
QuestionHave / Has + subject + past participle?Have you received my email?

Key idea: use the present perfect (have / has + past participle) for a finished action with a result now, without an exact time -- "I have attached the report" (it is here now). Use just, already, and yet with it: "She has just sent it", "I have already replied", "Have you read it yet?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have sent the email yesterday. I have wrote a draft."
"I sent the email yesterday. I have written a draft."

With an exact time (yesterday, at 9) use the past simple: I sent it yesterday. The past participle of write is written, and of send is sent.
