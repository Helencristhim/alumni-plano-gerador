Lesson 9 -- The Way We Used to Work

Series
The Office (US) -- "Email Surveillance" (Season 3)

A company tries to phase out an old habit and roll out a new workflow, and the team struggles to get used to it. A light, clear example of how a transition meets resistance before people adapt.

Watch with English subtitles. Note one thing the office used to do and one new way they have to get used to. Then say each one aloud: one sentence with "used to", one with "get used to + -ing".

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Lead Through Change"

A practical conversation on how teams phase out a legacy system, ramp up on a new one, and finally get used to a different way of working. Directly relevant to the process evolution you manage at Bloomberg.

Note 3 changes the guest describes. For each, say one sentence with "we used to..." and one with "now we are getting used to...". Add the words rollout, streamline, and adopt where they fit.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "The Right Way to Roll Out a New Process"

A short, practical video on how to automate routine steps, streamline approvals, and run a rollout in iterations so people can get used to the change. A template you can reuse for your own process work.

After watching, record a 60-second comparison of an old and a new process at your work. Use at least four new words and three structures: one "used to", one "would" for a repeated past action, and one "get used to + -ing".
