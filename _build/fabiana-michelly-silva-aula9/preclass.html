Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Streamline -- to make a process simpler and more efficient
"We streamlined the vendor selection process." (Simplificar)
Transition -- the process of changing from one system to another
"The transition from manual to digital was challenging." (Transição)
Evolve -- to develop gradually over time
"Our supply chain has evolved significantly." (Evoluir)
Automate -- to use technology to do something without human effort
"We automated the tracking process last year." (Automatizar)
Anticipate -- to expect or predict something before it happens
"Now we anticipate problems instead of reacting." (Antecipar)
Optimize -- to make the best possible use of something
"We optimized the freight routing to reduce costs." (Otimizar)
Legacy system -- an old system or technology still in use
"We need to replace the legacy tracking system." (Sistema legado)
Overhaul -- a complete renovation of a system
"The procurement process needs an overhaul." (Revisão completa)
Adapt -- to change behavior to fit a new situation
"We had to adapt quickly to the new requirements." (Adaptar)
Mindset -- a person's way of thinking or attitude
"The shift to a proactive mindset took time." (Mentalidade)
Advocate -- a person who supports or recommends something
"Marcus became the biggest advocate for the new system." (Defensor)
Phase -- a distinct stage in a process of change
"The first phase of the digital transition is complete." (Fase)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Bloomberg's supply chain has evolved dramatically. Three years ago, the team used to rely entirely on manual tracking spreadsheets. Every Monday, someone would compile the data by hand and email it to the team. The transition to an automated dashboard was challenging. Many people struggled to get used to the new interface. But once they adapted, they became advocates for the new system. The legacy system was eventually phased out completely. The overhaul also changed the mindset: the team used to wait for problems to happen. Now they anticipate them. The entire process has been streamlined and optimized. Today, everyone is used to working with the real-time tools.

1. What structure does "used to rely" use, and what does it mean?
A Past habit that no longer exists -- they relied before but not anymore.
B A repeated future action.
C An ongoing present habit.
2. Why does the text use "would compile" instead of "used to compile"?
A They mean different things.
B Both work for repeated past ACTIONS. "Would" adds a storytelling, nostalgic tone.
C "Would" is more formal than "used to".
3. What is the difference between "get used to" and "be used to"?
A They mean exactly the same thing.
B "Get used to" = the PROCESS of adjusting. "Be used to" = ALREADY adjusted.
C "Get used to" is past, "be used to" is future.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Used to / Would / Get used to

GRAMMAR

Três estruturas para falar de hábitos passados e adaptação. / Three structures for past habits and adaptation.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
used to + verbHábito ou estado passado (não mais verdade) / Past habit or stateWe used to rely on spreadsheets.
would + verbAção repetida no passado (só ações, não estados) / Repeated past action onlySomeone would compile the data every Monday.
get used to + -ingProcesso de se acostumar / Adjusting to something newI struggled to get used to the new system.
Ponto-chavewould só para AÇÕES repetidas (compile, send, check). used to para ações E estados (be, have, rely).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

A new colleague asks how tracking worked before. The best way to describe the old habit is:
A "We would be using spreadsheets." (wrong use of would)
B "We used to track everything on spreadsheets. Someone would update them every Monday."
C "We are used to tracking on spreadsheets." (means currently adjusted)
Your manager asks if you are comfortable with the new system. You want to say you have adjusted. You say:
A "I used to the new system." (incorrect structure)
B "Yes, I am used to working with it now. It took time to get used to it, but the transition is complete."
C "I would work with the new system." (wrong meaning)
You are describing how the team's mindset changed. The most natural way is:
A "We used to think of change as disruptive. Now we see it as a competitive advantage."
B "We would think of change as disruptive." (would cannot be used with states like "think of")
C "We are getting used to think of change." (wrong: get used to + -ing)
