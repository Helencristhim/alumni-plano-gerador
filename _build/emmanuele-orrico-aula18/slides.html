Chapter 4: The Pattern

If This... Then We Will...

two halves, one pattern — and you already read it

Grammar Discovery

Find the Pattern

"If the launch is delayed, we will postpone it."

"If we are behind schedule, we will use the backup plan."

"If it rains on launch day, we will move online."

"If the numbers are strong, we will expand."

Two parts every time. After if — what tense? And the purple word — what is it?

UseStructureExample
A real future conditionIf + present simple, ... will + base verb"If the launch is delayed, we will postpone it."
Order can switch... will + base verb ... if + present"We will postpone it if the launch is delayed."
NegativeIf + present, ... will not (won't) + base"If we are on track, we won't need the plan."
The one trapAfter if, use the present — never will. Not "If it will rain", but "If it rains".

If + present, then will + simple verb. The "will" only lives in the second half.

Language Focus

Decided Plan, or Backup Plan?

Common Mistake

After "if", the Present

"If the launch will be delayed, we will postpone it."
"If the launch is delayed, we will postpone it."
"If we are behind schedule, we postpone the congress."
"If we are behind schedule, we will postpone the congress."
"If the numbers will be strong, we expand."
"If the numbers are strong, we will expand."

One rule covers all three: after if, use the present — never will. The will lives only in the second half: if + present, will + simple verb.

Chapter 5: Voices

The Backup Plan Call

"And what is your backup plan?" — and this time you have the answer.

Dialogue

You Present the Rain Plan

M
Thanks for the launch calendar, Emmanuele. It looks clear. Now the question the board always asks: what is your backup plan if things change?
E
Good question. The normal plan is fixed: we are going to launch in Q3, on schedule. But I have a Rain Plan too. If the product approval is late, we will postpone the launch by one month.
M
That is sensible. And if you are behind schedule in October?
E
If we are behind schedule, we will start with the private market first, and we will reschedule the congress for November.
M
Good. And the launch event is outside. What about the weather?
E
We thought about that too. If it rains on launch day, we will move the event online. Nothing stops.
M
I like this. A plan with a backup plan. One more: what if the numbers are strong?
E
Then it is good news. If the numbers are strong, we will expand the rollout to two more regions, ahead of schedule.
