Lesson 5 -- Emails That Get Results

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

A law firm where professionals write and discuss formal messages, make polite requests, and follow up on important matters. Listen for the polite, formal register used at work.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three polite requests you hear (for example, "could you...", "would it be possible to..."). Then rewrite one direct sentence to make it more polite.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Writing Professional Emails"

A clear episode on how to open, structure, and close a professional email, with polite phrases for making requests, confirming details, and following up.

Record a 90-second audio dictating a request email. Include a subject line, a polite opening, a soft request, the attachment, and a polite closing, using "could you" and "I was wondering if".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Polite Requests and Hedging"

A focused lesson on softening requests with modals (could, would) and polite phrases (I was wondering if, would it be possible to). Directly reinforces today's grammar discovery.

After watching, write six polite requests for work, one for each verb: send, confirm, clarify, forward, reply, and acknowledge. Read them aloud with a calm, polite tone.
