Emails That Get Results -- Formal Register and Hedging

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recipient -- the person who receives an email or message
"The recipient of the email is my counterpart in Geneva." (Destinatário)
Subject line -- the short title at the top of an email
"A clear subject line helps the recipient understand the email." (Assunto)
Attachment -- a file you send together with an email
"Please find the report in the attachment." (Anexo)
Draft -- a first version of an email before you send it
"I am writing a draft of the email now." (Rascunho)
Forward -- to send a received email on to another person
"Could you forward this email to the finance team?" (Encaminhar)
Reply -- to answer an email or a message
"I will reply to your message this afternoon." (Responder)
Regarding -- about, or concerning, a topic
"I am writing regarding the shipment to Europe." (A respeito de / sobre)
Acknowledge -- to confirm that you received something
"Please acknowledge receipt of this email." (Confirmar o recebimento)
Confirm -- to say that something is certain or correct
"Could you confirm the delivery date?" (Confirmar)
Clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"Could you clarify the terms of the contract?" (Esclarecer)
Follow up -- to check on something after a first message
"I am writing to follow up on my last email." (Dar seguimento / retomar)
Request -- to ask for something politely and formally
"I would like to request a copy of the invoice." (Solicitar / pedido)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Walyson is writing a professional email to a supplier. He keeps a polite, formal tone. He opens softly: I am writing regarding our order for next month. Then he makes his request in a gentle way: I was wondering if you could send the updated price list, and could you please confirm the delivery date? He mentions the file: please find the contract in the attachment. Near the end, he hedges again: would it be possible to reply by Friday? He closes with care: I would appreciate it if you could acknowledge this message. Best regards. A direct order, like "send it now", would sound rude, so Walyson softens every request.

1. Why does Walyson use phrases like "could you please" and "I was wondering if"?
A To make his request softer and more polite (hedging).
B To make the email shorter.
C To give a direct order.
2. How does he open the email politely?
A "Send me the price list now."
B "I am writing regarding our order for next month."
C "Reply fast."
3. Which closing sounds the most formal and polite?
A "Answer me today."
B "Do it soon."
C "I would appreciate it if you could reply by Friday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Hedging and Formal Register

GRAMMAR

Em emails formais, suavizamos os pedidos para soar educado. / In formal emails, we soften requests to sound polite.

When / QuandoPolite form / Forma educadaExample / Exemplo
Small requestPedido pequenoCould you please send the file?
Bigger requestPedido maiorI was wondering if you could help.
Asking permissionPedindo permissãoWould it be possible to meet Friday?
Closing politelyFechamento educadoI would appreciate it if you could reply.

Key point / Ponto-chave: evite ordens diretas ("send it now"). Suavize com could you, I was wondering if, would it be possible to e feche com Best regards.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each email situation.

You need to ask a supplier for an updated price list. The most polite request is:
A "I was wondering if you could send me the updated price list."
B "Send the updated price list now."
C "Price list. Fast."
You want to start an email about the shipment to Europe. The best opening is:
A "I am writing regarding the shipment to Europe."
B "About shipment Europe I write."
C "Shipment Europe, answer me."
The supplier has not answered. You write a polite follow-up:
A "I am writing to follow up on my last email. Could you please reply?"
B "Why no answer? Reply now."
C "You forgot my email. Bad."
