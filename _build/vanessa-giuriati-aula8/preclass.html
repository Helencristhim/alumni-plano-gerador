Expressing Opinions

Sharing opinions and agreeing or disagreeing gracefully. Key words: perspective, point of view, argument, consider, generally, personally, honestly, disagree. Toolkit: stating, agreeing, disagreeing politely, hedging with might/could.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Perspective -- your particular way of seeing something (perspectiva)
"From my perspective, it is a good idea."
Point of view -- what you think about a topic (ponto de vista)
"I respect your point of view."
Argument -- a reason that supports an idea (argumento)
"That is a strong argument."
Consider -- to think carefully about something (considerar)
"Let me consider that for a moment."
Generally -- in most cases; usually (geralmente/de modo geral)
"Generally speaking, I agree with you."
Personally -- from your own point of view (pessoalmente)
"Personally, I prefer working in the morning."
Honestly -- in a sincere, truthful way (honestamente/sinceramente)
"Honestly, I am not sure about that."
Disagree -- to have a different opinion (discordar)
"I disagree, but I see your point."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

In a friendly discussion, Renata shared her view. "In my opinion, remote work is great," she said. Her colleague replied, "I see your point, but I think the office helps me focus." Renata did not get upset. "That's a fair point," she answered. "From my perspective, both have value, and it might depend on the person." Her colleague smiled. "You're right about that. I generally agree now." They disagreed at first, but they stayed polite and found common ground. Honestly, that is the best kind of conversation.

1. How did the colleague disagree politely?
A "I see your point, but I think the office helps me focus."
B "No, you are wrong."
C "That is a bad idea."
2. Why does the text use "it might depend" and not "it depends"?
A "Might" softens the opinion; the speaker is not 100% sure.
B "Depends" is wrong in English.
C "Might" means the past.
3. What happened after they disagreed?
A They stopped talking.
B They stayed polite and found common ground.
C They got angry.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Opinion Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Como dar opinião e concordar/discordar com educação. / How to give opinions and (dis)agree politely.

Job / FunçãoEnglishPortuguês
StateIn my opinion... / From my perspective...Na minha opinião... / Do meu ponto de vista...
AgreeI (completely) agree. / That's a great point.Eu concordo (totalmente). / É um ótimo ponto.
Disagree politelyI see your point, but... / Actually, I think...Entendo seu ponto, mas... / Na verdade, eu acho...
Soften (hedge)It might / could be... / I'm not entirely sure, but...Pode ser que... / Não tenho certeza, mas...
⚠ CarefulSay "I agree" (NOT "I am agree"). After might/could, use the base verb (might be, not "might to be").
