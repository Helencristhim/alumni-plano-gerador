Chapter 3

The Engine

Opinion toolkit + hedging with might / could

Grammar Discovery

State, Agree, Disagree

"In my opinion, remote work is great."

"I completely agree. That's a great point."

"I see your point, but I think differently."

"It might work, but I'm not entirely sure."

JobPhrases
State opinionI think... / In my opinion... / From my perspective... / It seems to me that...
AgreeI completely agree. / That's a great point. / Exactly. / You're right about that.
Disagree politelyI see your point, but... / I'm not sure I agree. / Actually, I think...
Soften (hedge)It might / could be... / I'm not entirely sure, but... (NEVER "I am agree")
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am agree with you."

✓ RIGHT

"I agree with you."

✗ TOO DIRECT

"No. You are wrong."

✓ POLITE

"I see your point, but I think..."

✗ WRONG

"It might to work."

✓ RIGHT

"It might work."

"Agree" is a verb on its own (not "be agree"). Disagree politely — it is a skill, not rudeness. After "might" and "could", use the base verb with no "to".

Dialogue

A Friendly Debate

D
In my opinion, remote work is much better than the office.
V
I see your point, but I think a mix is better. The office helps me focus.
D
That's a fair point. Honestly, I hadn't thought about focus.
V
Exactly. From my perspective, both have value. It might depend on the person.
D
You're right about that. I generally agree now.
V
See? We disagreed and still found common ground.
D
That's the best kind of conversation. You explain your ideas so clearly.
V
Thank you. I'm learning to share my opinion with confidence.
