Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Role -- cargo, função (the job or position a person has)
"My role is to lead the valuation team."
Company -- empresa (an organization that sells products or services)
"I am a partner in a valuation company."
Field -- área de atuação (the area of work you specialize in)
"I work in the field of property valuation."
In charge of -- responsável por (responsible for something or someone)
"I am in charge of the appraisal department."
Based in -- localizado em (located in a particular place)
"Our company is based in São Paulo."
Colleague -- colega de trabalho (a person you work with)
"This is my colleague from the technical team."
Partner -- sócio (a co-owner of a company)
"I am a partner in a company with thirty-two employees."
Industry -- setor, ramo (a specific type of business activity)
"I work in the real estate and valuation industry."
Networking -- criar contatos profissionais (meeting people to build professional contacts)
"Networking at congresses helps me find new partners."
Get in touch -- entrar em contato (to make contact or communicate with someone)
"Let's get in touch after the congress."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Daniel works as an appraisal engineer at an international congress. He is a partner in his own company, which is based in Mexico City. His company works in the valuation industry, and he is in charge of the technical team. "I work in the field of property and asset valuation," he explains to a new colleague. "My role involves leading projects and meeting clients." Daniel loves networking at congresses because it helps him build new connections. When he meets someone interesting, he always says: "Let's get in touch after the event." His colleague asks: "What do you do? Where are you based?" and Daniel answers with confidence.

1. Por que dizemos "Daniel works" com -s no final?
A Porque a ação aconteceu no passado.
B Porque, no present simple, he/she/it leva -s no verbo.
C Porque "Daniel" é plural.
2. "His company is based in Mexico City" usa o present simple para expressar:
A Um fato permanente (onde a empresa fica localizada).
B Uma ação que vai acontecer no futuro.
C Algo que aconteceu só uma vez ontem.
3. Which question does Daniel's colleague ask correctly?
A "What you do?"
B "What do you do? Where are you based?"
C "Where you based are?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & WH-Questions

GRAMMAR

Quando usar o present simple para se apresentar (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
I / You / We + verbFatos e rotinas (sem -s)I work in valuation.
He / She / It + verb+s3ª pessoa SEMPRE leva -s (cuidado: o espanhol não tem isso)She leads the team.
am / is / areIdentidade, cargo, lugar. Use a/an antes da profissãoI am an engineer.
WH + do + you + verbPerguntar sobre alguém (verbo de ação)What do you do?
WH + are + youCom to be, o verbo vem antes do sujeito: Where are you based? / What field are you in?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação profissional.

A colleague at the congress asks you to describe your work. What do you say?
A "I do appraisal things."
B "I work as an appraisal engineer. I am a partner and I am in charge of the valuation team."
C "I am engineer, I make values."
Someone asks: "Where are you based?" You respond:
A "I am based São Paulo."
B "My company is based in São Paulo, Brazil."
C "I based in São Paulo am."
You want to ask a new colleague about their job. The correct question is:
A "What you do for work?"
B "Which field you are in?"
C "What do you do? Which field are you in?"
You want to end the conversation and keep the contact. You say:
A "It was great meeting you. Let's get in touch after the congress."
B "Okay, bye, finish now."
C "We get in touch since the congress."
