Chapter 3

The Code

Present simple & WH-questions to introduce yourself

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work as an appraisal engineer."

"I am responsible for the valuation team."

"My colleague works in the technical team."

"What do you do? Where are you based?"

StructureUseExample
I / You / We workFacts and routines (no -s)I work in valuation.
He / She / It worksThird person adds -sShe leads the team.
am / is / areIdentity, role, locationI am a partner.
WH + do/are + youAsk about someoneWhat do you do? Where are you based?
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"My partner work in São Paulo."

✓ RIGHT

"My partner works in São Paulo."

✗ WRONG

"I am engineer."

✓ RIGHT

"I am an engineer."

✗ WRONG

"What you do?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you do?"

Add -s after he/she/it. Use "a/an" before a job. In questions, use "do" before you: What do you do?

At the Congress

M
Nice to meet you too, Ellen. I'm Marcos Mansour. I work as an appraisal engineer.
E
Great to meet a fellow appraiser! Which company do you work for?
M
I'm a partner in my own company. We're based in São Paulo, and I'm in charge of the valuation team.

That's impressive. I'm based in Chicago, and I work in the same field. What brings you to the congress?
M
I'm here for the talks and for some networking. I'd love to meet more colleagues from the United States.
E
Same here! Let's stay in touch. Here is my business card.

Thank you, Ellen. Here is mine. Let's get in touch after the congress.

Spot the Error

"My colleague work in the technical team."
"My colleague works in the technical team." (he/she/it + -s)
"I am partner in my own company."
"I am a partner in my own company." (use a/an before a job)
"Where you are based?"
"Where are you based?" (verb before the subject in questions)
"I work in the field of valuation since many years."
"I work in the field of valuation." (present simple = a fact, no "since" here)
