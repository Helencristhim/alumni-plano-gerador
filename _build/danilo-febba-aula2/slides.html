Chapter 3

The Grammar

Adverbs of frequency: how often you do things

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I always check my email."

"I am always early."

"She usually starts at nine."

"We never miss the morning meeting."

RulePositionExample
Main verbAdverb goes before the verbI always check my email.
Verb "to be"Adverb goes after am / is / areI am always early.
He / She / ItThe verb still adds -sShe usually starts at nine.
Frequencyalways (100%) → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → never (0%)

Before the main verb, but after "to be". Add -s for he / she / it.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I check always my email."

✓ RIGHT

"I always check my email."

✗ WRONG

"She start usually at nine."

✓ RIGHT

"She usually starts at nine."

✗ WRONG

"I am work every day."

✓ RIGHT

"I work every day."

Adverb before the main verb. Add -s for he/she/it. No "am" with present simple verbs.

Chapter 4

A Chat About Routine

Talking about your day at the coffee break

Dialogue

At the Coffee Break

R
Good morning, Danilo! So, what is your daily routine like?
D
Hi Rachel! My workday usually starts at nine. I always check my schedule first.
R
Same for me. But I never start the day without a coffee!
D
Me too! My commute takes forty minutes, so I am sometimes a little tired.
R
Oh, my commute is short. I usually walk to the office in ten minutes.
D
Lucky you! We often have a meeting at ten, and I usually have three tasks a day.
R
That is busy! I always take a break after the meeting. Do you?
D
Yes, I always take a break after lunch. And I rarely work late.
R
Nice. I write my report on Fridays. When do you write yours?
D
I write my report every Friday too. I really love my routine!
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Rachel never start her day without?
A coffee. She never starts the day without one.
2. How does Rachel get to the office?
She usually walks. Her commute is only ten minutes.
3. When does Rachel write her report?
On Fridays.
