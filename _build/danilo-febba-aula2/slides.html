Chapter 3

Routines & Frequency

Present simple + adverbs of frequency

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I usually start my day with a standup."

"My team always tracks the deadlines."

"One engineer is always on call."

"We rarely deploy on Fridays."

StructureUseExample
I / You / We + verbRoutines and facts (no -s)I start at nine.
He / She / It / team + verb+sThird person adds -sShe handles the pipeline.
Adverb before the main verbalways, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, neverI often delegate tasks.
Adverb after the verb "to be"with am / is / areHe is usually busy.

Frequency word BEFORE a normal verb, AFTER the verb "to be".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I check always my email."

✓ RIGHT

"I always check my email."

✗ WRONG

"My team handle the pipelines."

✓ RIGHT

"My team handles the pipelines."

✗ WRONG

"I am usually start at nine."

✓ RIGHT

"I usually start at nine."

Frequency word before the verb. Add -s after he/she/it/team. No "am/is" before a main verb.

Chapter 4

Explaining Your Role

A one-to-one with your director

Dialogue

The One-to-One

S
Welcome, Danilo. So, tell me — what does a typical day look like for you?
D
Thanks, Sofia. I usually start with our team standup at nine, and then I check the pipelines.
S
Nice. And how do you manage the workload across the team?
D
I prioritize the urgent tasks first, and I often delegate the smaller ones to my engineers.
S
Good. Who do you report to on the data side?
D
I report to you now. My team tracks every deadline, and we catch up every Monday.
S
Perfect. And what about nights and weekends?
D
One engineer is always on call, so we can handle any urgent problem quickly.
S
That's a solid routine. I think you'll do great here.
D
Thank you, Sofia. I'm looking forward to it.
