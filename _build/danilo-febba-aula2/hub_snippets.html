Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Routine -- the set of things you do regularly, in the same order
"My morning routine starts with a team standup."
Handle -- to deal with or be responsible for a task or problem
"My engineers handle the data pipelines."
Report to -- to have someone as your manager and give them updates
"I report to the director of technology."
Standup -- a short daily meeting where the team shares progress
"We have a standup every morning at nine."
Workload -- the amount of work a person or team has to do
"The workload is heavy before a big release."
Prioritize -- to decide what is most important to do first
"I prioritize the urgent tasks every morning."
Track -- to follow the progress of a task or project
"We track every deadline on a shared board."
Delegate -- to give a task to someone else on your team
"I delegate the smaller tasks to my engineers."
On call -- available to respond to urgent problems outside work hours
"One engineer is always on call at night."
Catch up -- to meet briefly to share recent updates
"I catch up with my director every Monday."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Danilo leads a data team in São Paulo. He usually starts the day with a standup at nine, and then he checks the pipelines. His engineers handle the day-to-day work, and the team always tracks every deadline on a shared board. Danilo reports to the director, and they catch up every Monday. He often delegates the smaller tasks, but he prioritizes the urgent ones himself. The team rarely deploys on Fridays, because they keep that day for reviews. At night, one engineer is always on call, so they can handle any urgent problem. It is a busy routine, but a clear one.

1. Why do we say "He usually starts" with an -s?
A Because the action is in the past.
B Because in the present simple, he/she/it takes an -s.
C Because "He" is plural.
2. In "the team always tracks", where does the word "always" go?
A Before the main verb (tracks).
B After the object.
C At the end of the sentence only.
3. In "one engineer is always on call", why is "always" after "is"?
A Because "always" never changes position.
B Because frequency adverbs come after the verb "to be" (is/are/am).
C Because "on call" is a noun.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

Where to put the frequency word, and the -s with he/she/it.

FormUseExample
I / You / We + verbRoutines and facts (no -s)I start at nine.
He / She / It / team + verb+sThird person always takes -sShe handles the pipeline.
Adverb before the main verbalways, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, neverI often delegate tasks.
Adverb after the verb "to be"with am / is / areHe is usually busy.
Frequency word orderalways > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never (most to least often)
