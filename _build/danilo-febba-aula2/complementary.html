Lesson 2 -- My Daily Routine

Series
Abstract: The Art of Design -- "A Day in the Life" style episodes

A calm documentary series where professionals describe their daily routine and how they work. Clear American English at a friendly pace. Direct connection to Lesson 2: people use present simple and frequency words (usually, often, always) to describe a typical workday.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences with "always", "usually", or "often".

Podcast
BBC Learning English -- "6 Minute English: Daily Routines"

A short, clear episode at a friendly speed, perfect for A2. The hosts talk about morning routines, work, and habits. Direct connection to Lesson 2: it trains your ear with present simple and frequency adverbs, and gives you natural phrases to describe your own day.

Tip: listen twice. Note 5 things the speakers say they "always" or "never" do.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Adverbs of Frequency: always, usually, often..."

A short, practical lesson on how often we do things and where to put the frequency word in the sentence. Connection to Lesson 2: it shows the exact rule you practiced (adverb before the main verb, after "to be") with simple, ready-to-use examples about daily routine.

Tip: pause and repeat each example aloud. Then say 3 sentences about your own routine.
