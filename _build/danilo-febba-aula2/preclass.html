My Daily Routine

Descrevendo um dia típico de trabalho. Palavras-chave: routine, schedule, task, meeting, break, commute, report, workday. Gramática: present simple + advérbios de frequência (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. (Escute cada palavra e leia a tradução.)

Routine -- the things you do regularly (rotina)
"My morning routine is always the same."
Schedule -- your plan of times for the day (agenda / cronograma)
"I check my schedule every morning."
Task -- a piece of work to do (tarefa)
"I have three tasks today."
Meeting -- when people meet to talk about work (reunião)
"We have a meeting at ten."
Break -- a short rest during work (pausa / intervalo)
"I take a break after lunch."
Commute -- your trip from home to work (trajeto / deslocamento)
"My commute takes forty minutes."
Report -- a document with information (relatório)
"I write a report every Friday."
Workday -- your working day (dia de trabalho / expediente)
"My workday starts at nine."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas. (Repare nos advérbios de frequência em negrito.)

Danilo has a busy routine. His workday usually starts at nine. His commute takes forty minutes, so he always leaves home early. First, he always checks his schedule and his email. The team often has a meeting at ten, and Danilo usually has three tasks a day. He always takes a break after lunch, but he rarely works late. His manager Rachel is never late for the morning meeting. On Fridays, Danilo always writes a report. "I love my routine," he says.

1. Onde colocamos o advérbio em "He always checks his schedule"?
A Depois do verbo principal (checks always).
B Antes do verbo principal (always checks).
C No final da frase.
2. Em "Rachel is never late", onde fica o advérbio "never"?
A Depois do verbo "to be" (is never).
B Antes do verbo "to be" (never is).
C No começo da frase.
3. Por que dizemos "His workday starts" com -s no final?
A Porque a ação aconteceu no passado.
B Porque no present simple, he/she/it recebe -s.
C Porque "workday" é plural.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

Como dizer com que frequência você faz cada coisa na sua rotina.

RulePosition (PT)Example
Verbo principalO advérbio vem antes do verboI always check my email.
Verbo "to be"O advérbio vem depois de am/is/areI am always early.
He / She / ItO verbo ainda recebe -sShe usually starts at nine.
Frequênciaalways (100%) → usually → often → sometimes → rarely → never (0%)

Atenção (erro comum): Não coloque o advérbio depois do verbo principal. Diga "I always check" (não "I check always"). Mas com o verbo "to be" ele vem depois: "I am always early" (não "I always am early").
