Chapter 3: The Code

The Backshift

"We will raise" → She said they would raise

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We will raise the guidance." → She said they would raise the guidance.

"The numbers are audited." → He said the numbers were audited.

"We have already signed." → They said they had already signed.

"Have you disclosed it?" → She asked whether we had disclosed it.

Look at the verbs on the right. Every one of them took a step backwards in time. Who told them to?

FormUseExample
The backshift
(o recuo)		When you report, each verb steps ONE tense back. English does this automatically; Portuguese does not.will → would · is → was · have → had
say vs telltell always needs a person right after it. say never does.She told me that... / She said that...
Reported questionsUse whether or if, and go back to STATEMENT word order. No inversion, no question mark.She asked whether we had disclosed it.
Reporting verbssaid is the floor, not the ceiling. Choose the verb that carries the intent.She cautioned / reiterated / warned that...
No backshift neededIf it is STILL true, you may keep the present. Both are correct; the present sounds more confident.She said the covenant is still in force.

The trap is the question: in Portuguese you keep the question shape ("ela perguntou se nós divulgamos?"). In English the question DIES: She asked whether we had disclosed it. No inversion. No question mark.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She asked me did we disclose the covenant breach."
"She asked me whether we had disclosed the covenant breach."
"She said me that the term sheet would arrive on Friday."
"She told me that the term sheet would arrive on Friday."
"He said the round will cause serious dilution."
"He said the round would cause serious dilution."

tell takes a person (tell me), say does not (say that). And a reported question is NOT a question any more: whether/if + normal word order, no inversion.

Chapter 4: The Briefing

Your CEO Was Not in the Room

Everything she knows, she knows from you

Dialogue

What Did Ingrid Say?

C
Felipe, I could not join the call with Ingrid. Walk me through it. What did she actually say about the round?
F
She said the fund was still committed, and that the term sheet would arrive on Friday. But she cautioned that the market might not reopen this year.
C
Cautioned, or warned? Those are very different words, Felipe.
F
Cautioned. She was careful, not alarmed. And she reiterated twice that our numbers had been audited — she wanted that on the record.
C
Did she raise the covenant?
F
She asked whether we had disclosed it to the bank. I told her we had, in writing, in March. She then asked how much capital we intended to raise, and I said we had not decided — because that is your call, not mine.
Comprehension

About Claire

1. Why does Claire need Felipe to walk her through the call?
She could not join it — everything she knows about it comes from him.
2. Which single word does she stop him on, and why?
"Cautioned" — she wants to know if Ingrid was careful or alarmed. The verb changes the meaning.
3. What does she ask about directly?
Whether Ingrid raised the covenant.

Listening 2

The Quarterly Call

A CFO reports to the market. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What did the board conclude about the guidance?
That it should be raised — he reports that the board agreed to raise it.
2. What did the auditors confirm?
That the quarterly results were clean, with no restatement.

That the covenant headroom was narrow — two points — and that they were monitoring it monthly.
