Aula 05 -- Pre-class

Making Your Case -- Board Updates & Investor Communications

Carregar as palavras de um investidor para dentro do board sem perder uma sílaba. Key words: to disclose, guidance, covenant, dilution, term sheet, cap table, to raise capital, to reiterate, to caution, quarterly results, to walk through, investor deck. Structures: reported speech — o recuo do tempo verbal (backshift), say vs tell, perguntas indiretas (whether/if, sem inversão) e reporting verbs (cautioned, reiterated, denied).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. São as palavras que aparecem na ata de todo conselho de que você vai participar.

To disclose -- to make information official, on the record (divulgar oficialmente)
"She asked whether we had disclosed the covenant breach."
Guidance -- the forecast a company officially gives the market (projeção oficial ao mercado)
"The CEO said that we would raise our guidance for the year."
Covenant -- a promise inside a loan contract that you must not break (cláusula contratual, covenant)
"We are two points away from breaking the covenant."
Dilution -- the fall in your ownership when new shares are issued (diluição)
"He warned that the round would cause serious dilution."
Term sheet -- the summary of the key terms of an investment (term sheet, memorando de termos)
"She told me the term sheet would arrive on Friday."
Cap table -- the list of who owns what in the company (quadro societário, cap table)
"The cap table gets messy after three rounds."
To raise capital -- to get new money from investors (captar recursos)
"They asked how much capital we intended to raise."
To reiterate -- to say something again, deliberately, so it sticks (reiterar)
"I reiterated that the numbers had been audited."
To caution -- to warn carefully, without alarming anyone (alertar, ponderar)
"She cautioned that the market might not reopen this year."
Quarterly results -- the numbers a company reports every three months (resultados trimestrais)
"He walked the board through the quarterly results in ten minutes."
To walk through -- to explain something step by step, patiently (conduzir passo a passo)
"Let me walk you through what the investor actually said."
Investor deck -- the slide deck you present to investors (apresentação a investidores)
"The investor deck says one thing; the cap table says another."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Ingrid, the lead investor, called on a Tuesday. Felipe took the call alone; the CEO could not join. Ingrid said that the fund was still committed, and that the term sheet would arrive on Friday. She cautioned that the market might not reopen this year — she was careful, not alarmed, and the difference matters. She reiterated twice that the numbers had been audited, because she wanted that on the record. Then she asked whether the company had disclosed the covenant breach to the bank. Felipe told her that they had, in writing, in March. She also asked how much capital they intended to raise, and he said they had not decided. Notice what happens to every verb when Felipe repeats her: will becomes would, is becomes was, have been becomes had been. English steps back one pace. Portuguese does not. When he later walked the CEO through the call, she stopped him on a single word: cautioned, or warned? Two verbs, two different companies.

1. Ingrid disse "We will send the term sheet on Friday". Como o Felipe reporta isso?
A She said they will send the term sheet on Friday.
B She said they would send the term sheet on Friday.
C She said they send the term sheet on Friday.
2. Por que a CEO para o Felipe na palavra "cautioned"?
A Porque cautioned (ponderar, com cuidado) e warned (alertar, com alarme) mudam completamente a leitura da mensagem — e o board decide com base nela.
B Porque cautioned está gramaticalmente errado nessa frase.
C Porque ela não conhece a palavra cautioned.
3. Ingrid perguntou "Have you disclosed it?". Which report is correct English?
A She asked had we disclosed it.
B She asked whether we had disclosed it.
C She asked did we disclose it?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech (backshift, say/tell, perguntas indiretas)

GRAMMAR

Como repetir as palavras de outra pessoa — a gramática de metade do trabalho de um CFO (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
O recuo (backshift)Ao reportar, cada verbo recua UM tempo. O inglês faz isso automaticamente; o português NÃO. Each verb steps one tense back.will → would · is → was · have → had
say vs telltell SEMPRE pede uma pessoa logo depois. say NUNCA pede. tell needs a person; say does not.She told me that... / She said that...
Perguntas reportadasUse whether ou if e volte à ordem de AFIRMAÇÃO. Sem inversão, sem ponto de interrogação.She asked whether we had disclosed it.
Perguntas com wh-Mantém a palavra-pergunta, mas a ordem vira de afirmação.They asked how much we wanted to raise.
Reporting verbssaid é o chão, não o teto. O verbo carrega a INTENÇÃO — e o board lê a sua escolha.She cautioned / reiterated / denied that...
Quando NÃO recuarSe ainda é verdade agora, o presente é permitido — e soa mais confiante.She said the covenant is still in force.

Atenção (o erro nº 1 do brasileiro): a pergunta reportada. Em português a forma de pergunta sobrevive ("ela perguntou se nós divulgamos"). Em inglês a pergunta MORRE: "She asked did we disclose" está errado — o certo é "She asked whether we had disclosed it." Sem inversão, sem "?". E lembre: tell pede pessoa ("she told me"), say não ("she said that") — "she said me" não existe.

Stage 2: Put the Investor Briefing in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de um briefing ao board na ordem correta. Um board decide na sequência em que você entrega a informação.

?Report what she said, in her words, with the reporting verb she earned.
?Close with what you recommend the board decides — and what is not your call.
?State who you spoke to, when, and who else was on the call.
?Report the questions she asked, and exactly how you answered them.
?Separate what she stated as fact from what she merely cautioned about.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

Ingrid said: "We will send the term sheet on Friday." You report:
A "She said they will send the term sheet on Friday."
B "She said they would send the term sheet on Friday."
C "She said me they send the term sheet on Friday."
Ingrid asked: "Have you disclosed the covenant?" You report:
A "She asked had we disclosed the covenant."
B "She asked whether we had disclosed the covenant."
C "She asked did we disclose the covenant?"
She said it twice, deliberately, so it would stick. The right verb is:
A "She reiterated that the numbers had been audited."
B "She denied that the numbers had been audited."
C "She cautioned that the numbers had been audited."
She warned you gently, without alarming anyone. You report:
A "She warned that the market will not reopen."
B "She cautioned that the market might not reopen this year."
C "She said the market is not reopening."
A board member says your report softened her message. The best response is:
A "Maybe. I might have misheard her."
B "She cautioned, she did not warn. Let me walk you through exactly what she said."
C "You're right, I'll change it in the minutes."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

Your CEO could not join the investor call. Brief her for 2-3 minutes. Report what Ingrid said about the fund, the term sheet and the market; report the two questions she asked and how you answered them. Use the backshift throughout, use "told" at least once and "said" at least once (correctly), and choose your reporting verbs deliberately — cautioned is not warned. Finish with what you recommend, and what is not your call.
