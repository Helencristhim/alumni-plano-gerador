Word Pairs

Know the Difference

These pairs are often confused. Listen and notice the contrast.

Reliable = we can trust it.  |  Plausible = believable, but not proven.

Limitation = a weak point.  |  Conflict of interest = an unfair influence on results.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Hedging: it suggests, it seems, may, appears to

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"The data suggests a positive effect."

"The result seems reliable, but the cohort is small."

"These results may not apply to everyone."

"The treatment appears to help, but we need more data."

Grammar Rule

Hedging Language

FunctionExample
Report a finding carefullyThe data suggests a positive effect.
Give a cautious impressionThe result seems reliable.
Express possibilityThese results may not apply to everyone.
Make a careful observationThe treatment appears to help.

Key idea: in science, a strong claim sounds careless. Use suggests, seems, may and appears to to hedge. After may use the base verb; after appears use to plus the verb.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"These results may to apply to everyone."
"These results may apply to everyone."

After may and might, use the base verb with no to. Only appears takes to.

Grammar Practice

Make It Cautious

"The data _____suggests a positive effect." (report carefully)
"The result _____seems reliable, but the cohort is small." (cautious impression)
"These findings _____may not apply to everyone." (possibility)
"The treatment _____appears to help patients." (careful observation)
