Lesson 8 -- Questioning Research

Documentary
The Game Changers -- watch any 20-minute segment

A documentary full of bold nutrition claims. Practice your critical eye: notice when the data only suggests an effect, when the cohort is small, and when there may be conflict of interest.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two claims, then add a polite query and one limitation for each, using hedging (suggests, seems, may).

Podcast
Science Vs -- any episode reviewing a health study

Each episode weighs the evidence behind a popular claim, naming the study design, cohort and limitations. A perfect model of balanced, hedged appraisal in natural English.

Record a 2-minute appraisal of the study they discuss: comment on the study design, raise one query, and give a verdict using plausible rather than proven.

YouTube
"How to read a scientific paper critically" (any clear explainer)

A short guide to the language of appraisal: study design, cohort, conflict of interest, assumption and limitation. It directly reinforces today's vocabulary and the hedging used to question a study.

After watching, write six sentences: three that raise a query politely and three that name a limitation, using suggests, seems or may.
