Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Study design -- the set of methods used to design and run a study
"The study design is clear and well described." (Desenho do estudo)
Cohort -- the group of people or items chosen to be studied
"The cohort is quite small, only forty participants." (Coorte)
Conflict of interest -- an unfair influence that makes results less reliable
"There may be conflict of interest because the company funded it." (Conflito de interesse)
Limitation -- a weak point that reduces what a study can prove
"The main limitation is the short follow-up." (Limitação)
Reliable -- trustworthy; giving consistent, dependable results
"The study design itself is reliable." (Confiável)
Assumption -- something we accept as true without testing it
"The study makes one strong assumption about diet." (Suposição / pressuposto)
Query -- a question or point of doubt you want to raise
"My main query is about the cohort size." (Dúvida / questionamento)
Plausible -- believable and reasonable, but not yet proven
"The findings are plausible rather than proven." (Plausível)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the journal club, Patricia reviews a new study. The study design is clear, so she says it is reliable. However, the cohort is small, so the data only suggests an effect; it does not prove one. She also notices one assumption the authors do not test, and she raises a polite query about a possible conflict of interest, because the company funded the work. The main limitation, she says, appears to be the short follow-up. The treatment seems to help, and the results may be useful, but she is careful: the findings are plausible rather than proven.

1. Why does Patricia say the data only suggests an effect?
A Because the study design is weak and unreliable.
B Because the cohort is small, so the result is not proven.
C Because she did not read the study.
2. What query does she raise?
A A possible conflict of interest, because the company funded the work.
B Whether the authors used a computer.
C How long the journal club lasted.
3. What is her overall verdict?
A The findings are completely proven and certain.
B The findings are plausible rather than proven.
C The study should be deleted entirely.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Hedging your claims

GRAMMAR

Na ciência, afirmações fortes demais soam descuidadas. Suavizamos com hedging para soar honesto. / In science, claims that are too strong sound careless. We hedge to sound honest.

Function / FunçãoHedging structure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Report a findingthe data suggests + noun"The data suggests a positive effect."
Give an impressionit / the result seems + adjective"The result seems reliable."
Express possibilitymay / might + base verb"These results may not apply to everyone."
Make an observationappears to + base verb"The treatment appears to help."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Em inglês científico, evite "this proves". Use suggests, seems, may e appears to para suavizar. Depois de may e might use o verbo base (sem to); só appears leva to.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

A study reports a positive result, but the cohort is small. You want to comment carefully. You say:
A "The data suggests a positive effect, but the result may not apply to everyone."
B "This proves the product works for all people, certainly."
C "The study is useless and completely wrong."
You want to raise a polite query about a possible conflict of interest. You say:
A "Query conflict of interest the company funded is."
B "My main query is about a possible conflict of interest, as the company funded the study."
C "You are clearly lying about your data."
You want to give a balanced final verdict. You say:
A "Proven everything is, no questions at all."
B "The study design seems reliable, so I would say the findings are plausible rather than proven."
C "I refuse to say anything about this study."
