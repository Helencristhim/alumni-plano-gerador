American English Podcast — Tipping in the US

The tipping code explained by an American: how much you leave at a restaurant, in a taxi, at a hotel, and what happens when you leave nothing at all. This is lesson 3, word for word — tip, sales tax, receipt — and it answers, once and for all, the question that freezes you when the check arrives in New York.

Tip (do this in the car, on the way there): listen ONCE, all the way through, without pausing. Then write down EVERY number and EVERY percentage you caught — and 2 sentences you did NOT catch. Bring both to class: they become the mini listening lesson in lesson 4.

Podcast — 5-Minute English
American English Podcast — Ep. 153: Shopping for Clothes

Five minutes with the exact language of the store: asking for another size, asking where the fitting room is, trying a coat on (try it on), and the price that goes up at the register because of the sales tax. American English, natural speed, and short enough to hear twice on one trip.

Tip (on the way back): listen twice. The second time, repeat OUT LOUD every question the host teaches — your mouth needs the repetition, not only your ears. Then build 3 sentences about the coat you are going to buy on Fifth Avenue in September.

YouTube — conversation (you can listen without watching)
Present Perfect and the Past Simple — English Conversation about Experiences

A conversation in American English, about trips and life experiences, made for you to HEAR the difference between "I went to New York in 2023" (closed, with a date) and "I have never taken the subway" (a whole life, no date). It is the grammar of lesson 3 in the mouths of people talking, not on a board.

Tip: it works as pure audio — listen in the car, without looking at the screen. Every time you hear a verb after have / has, say the pair out loud: take → taken, go → gone, eat → eaten. That pair is what gives your level away when you speak.
