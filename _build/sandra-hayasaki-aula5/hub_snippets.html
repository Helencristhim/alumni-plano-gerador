Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Confident -- sure of yourself and your ability
"I feel confident when I speak English now." (confiante)
Fluent -- able to speak a language easily and smoothly
"She is fluent in three languages." (fluente)
Improve -- to get better at something
"I want to improve my pronunciation." (melhorar)
Progress -- the movement toward a better level
"I'm making good progress this year." (progresso)
Practice -- to do something repeatedly to get better
"I practice English every day." (praticar)
Proud -- happy about something good you did
"I'm proud of my progress." (orgulhoso)
Mistake -- something you do wrong by accident
"Every mistake helps me learn." (erro)
Review -- to look at something again to learn it well
"Let's review what we studied this month." (revisar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Hi, I'm Mark. Let me tell you about my English journey. Three years ago, I could hardly say a full sentence, and I felt nervous in every conversation. So I decided to practice every day. I have improved a lot since then. Of course, I still make mistakes sometimes, but I'm not afraid of them — every mistake helps me improve. Today I feel much more confident, and I'm really proud of my progress. I'm not completely fluent yet, but next year I'm going to take an advanced exam.

1. What did Mark decide to do?
A Practice every day.
B Stop studying English.
C Move to another country.
2. How does Mark feel about mistakes?
A He is afraid of them.
B They help him improve.
C He never makes any.
3. Which sentence is correct for something that started in the past and is still true?
A "I improve a lot since then."
B "I have improved a lot since then."
C "I am improve a lot since then."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Block 1 Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Revisão dos quatro tempos do bloco -- nada novo, só juntar. / A review of the four tenses from the block -- nothing new, just together.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present simple / presenteroutines & facts / rotinaI practice every day.
Present perfectlife so far / vida até agoraI have improved a lot.
Past cont. + past simplea story / uma históriaWhile I was studying, I made a mistake.
going to / willplans & predictions / planosNext year I 'm going to take an exam.
Key point / Ponto-chavePara algo que começou no passado e continua agora, use o present perfect (have + particípio), não o presente. Diga "I have lived here for ten years", não "I live here for ten years".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You want to describe your daily routine.
A "I have started work at eight every day."
B "I start work at eight every day."
C "I was starting work at eight every day."
You started English two years ago and still study now.
A "I have studied English for two years."
B "I study English for two years."
C "I am studying English for two years ago."
You want to talk about a fixed plan for next month.
A "Next month I take an exam."
B "Next month I took an exam."
C "Next month I'm going to take an exam."
