Vocabulary

Outline -- a short plan showing the main parts of a talk
"Let me start with a quick outline of my talk." (Esboço / roteiro)
Agenda -- the list of topics you will cover, in order
"Here is the agenda for today's session." (Pauta / agenda)
Transition -- a smooth move from one part of a talk to the next
"That is a good transition to my next point." (Transição)
Emphasize -- to give special importance to a point
"I would like to emphasize this result." (Enfatizar)
Recap -- a short repeat of the main points at the end
"Let me recap the three key results." (Recapitulação / resumo)
Takeaway -- the most important idea to remember
"The main takeaway is that structure matters." (Lição principal)
Framework -- the basic structure or model behind your work
"We built the study on a clear framework." (Estrutura / modelo)
Overview -- a short general picture before the details
"First, a quick overview of the project." (Visão geral)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Patricia is about to give a talk at Abbott, so she plans a clear outline. She opens with a short overview and shows the agenda on the first slide. "Firstly," she says, "I will explain our research framework." After that, she makes a smooth transition: "Moving on, let me present the main findings." She wants to emphasize one important result, so she slows down and repeats it. At the end, she gives a clear recap of the key points. "To summarize," she says, "the main takeaway is that a clear structure helps both the speaker and the audience." Her colleague David tells her the talk was easy to follow, because the signposts showed exactly where she was at every moment.

2. How does she move from the framework to the findings?

B She makes a smooth transition: "Moving on, let me present the main findings."
C She asks the audience to guess the results.
3. Why was the talk easy to follow?
A Because it was very short with no data.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Signposting language

GRAMMAR

Em apresentações, usamos signposts (marcadores) para guiar o público pela estrutura da fala. / In presentations, we use signposts to guide the audience through the structure of the talk.

Function / FunçãoSignpost / MarcadorExample / Exemplo
Start / list a pointFirstly, / Secondly, / To begin with"Firstly, I will give you a quick overview."
Change topicMoving on, / Now, let me turn to"Moving on, let me present the findings."
Give emphasisI would like to emphasize ..."I would like to emphasize this result."
Close / recapTo summarize, / In short, ..."To summarize, structure is the foundation."

Key point / Ponto-chave: signposts não são o conteúdo -- são o mapa. Use Firstly / Secondly para listar, Moving on / Now let me turn to para mudar de tópico, I would like to emphasize para destacar, e To summarize / In short para fechar. Cuidado: usamos Firstly (não "At first") para listar o primeiro ponto.
