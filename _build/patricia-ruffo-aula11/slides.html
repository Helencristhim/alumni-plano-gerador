Grammar Rule

Signposting Language

FunctionExample
Start a pointFirstly, I will give you a quick overview.
Change topicMoving on, let me present the findings.
Give emphasisI would like to emphasize this result.
Close the talkTo summarize, structure is the foundation.

Key idea: signposts are not the content -- they are the map. Use Firstly / Secondly to list, Moving on / Now let me turn to to change topic, I would like to emphasize for a key point, and To summarize / In short to close.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"At first, I will present the agenda."
"Firstly, I will present the agenda."

Use Firstly to list points. At first means "in the beginning," before something changes.
