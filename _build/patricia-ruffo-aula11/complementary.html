Lesson 11 -- Structuring a Scientific Presentation

Talk
TED -- any well-structured science talk (e.g. Hans Rosling)

Watch how a great speaker opens with an overview, signposts each section, and closes with a clear takeaway. Notice how the structure keeps you with the speaker the whole time.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three signposts (firstly, moving on, to summarize) and the one takeaway you remember.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Signposting Language for Presentations"

A focused episode on the phrases that structure a talk: opening with an outline, making transitions, emphasizing a point, and summarizing at the end.

Record a 2-minute mini-talk: open with an overview and agenda, then close with a recap using today's vocabulary.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Presentation language: signposting"

A clear lesson on the phrases that guide an audience through a presentation (firstly, moving on, to summarize). It directly reinforces today's structuring language.

After watching, write six sentences: two openings, two transitions, and two closings using today's signposts.
