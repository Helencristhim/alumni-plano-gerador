Chapter 4: The Code

Finished vs Up to Now

Past simple · Present perfect

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have listened to English podcasts for years."

"I started English at school back in the 1980s."

"I have never given up on languages."

"I learned French when I worked in France."

Why green for experience up to now, but blue for a finished time?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now (for, since, ever, never, over the years)I have listened for years.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		A finished action at a finished time (ago, in 2005, back in the 80s, when I was young)I learned French in France.

With ago and a finished year, always use the past simple. With for / since / never, use the present perfect.

Language Focus

Past or Present Perfect?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have visited Norway ten years ago."

✓ RIGHT

"I visited Norway ten years ago."

✗ WRONG

"I study English for forty years."

✓ RIGHT

"I have studied English for forty years."

With ago and a finished time, use the past simple. For duration up to now (for forty years), use the present perfect.

Dialogue

A Bar in Reykjavik

A
You have been quiet, just watching the room. I am Astrid, from Bergen. Mind if I join you?
M
Please do! I am Marcelo, from Brazil. I have been traveling for three weeks, and I am just enjoying the sound of all these languages.
A
Brazil! I have been to São Paulo once, years ago. So, how many languages do you speak?
M
Portuguese, of course, and French. I picked it up back in my thirties, when I worked for a French company for eighteen years.
A
Impressive. And your English is great too. Did you study it for long?
M
On and off for forty years! I started at school, I gave it up many times, but I have never really stopped. I keep coming back to it.
A
Well, it clearly worked. You have settled into the conversation like a local. I grew up with Norwegian and English, but I gave up on Spanish after a year.
M
Ha! That is the goal, to fit in and talk to everyone. Over the years, I have learned that you do not study a language, you live it.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can tell my language story using the past simple and the present perfect.
I can use the past simple for finished moments (ago, in 2005, back in the 80s).
I can use the present perfect for experience and duration (for, since, never, over the years).
I avoid mixing them (not 'I have visited Norway ten years ago').
I know the words of a life abroad: pick up, immerse, give up, stick with it, settle in, fit in, blend in.
