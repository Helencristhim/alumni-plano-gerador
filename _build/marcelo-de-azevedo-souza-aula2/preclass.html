The Story of Me -- Your Past, Your Languages, Your Life

Telling your life story in languages. Key words: to pick up, immerse yourself in, give up, come back to, resume, stick with it, move abroad, settle in, adapt to, fit in, stand out, blend in. Structures: past simple (finished moments) vs present perfect (experience and duration up to now, for/since/ago).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada expressão e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To pick up -- to learn something informally, by doing or using it (aprender na prática)
"I picked up French by living it, not by studying it."
To immerse yourself in -- to surround yourself completely with something (mergulhar de cabeça em)
"I immersed myself in French for eighteen years."
To give up -- to stop doing something before it is finished (desistir)
"I started English many times, and I gave it up many times."
To come back to -- to return to something after a break (voltar a algo)
"I always come back to English, no matter what."
To resume -- to start again after stopping (retomar)
"After a long break, I decided to resume my studies."
To stick with it -- to keep going despite difficulty (persistir, não largar)
"Learning is hard, but you have to stick with it."
To move abroad -- to go and live in another country (mudar-se para o exterior)
"Many of my friends moved abroad to learn a language."
To settle in -- to get used to a new place and feel at home (se ambientar)
"It took me a week to settle in when I traveled."
To adapt to -- to change so you fit a new situation (se adaptar a)
"A good traveler adapts to any culture."
To fit in -- to feel accepted as part of a group (se enturmar)
"I want to fit in and talk to everyone I meet."
To stand out -- to be noticed as different or special (se destacar)
"My accent makes me stand out a little."
To blend in -- to look or seem like everyone around you (se misturar)
"After a week abroad, I blend in like a local."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcelo has always loved languages. He started English at school back in the 1980s, but he gave it up many times over the years. Still, he has never really stopped — he has come back to it again and again. In his thirties, he moved into a French company and worked there for eighteen years. He did not study French in a classroom; he immersed himself in it and picked it up by living it. Today, he has listened to English podcasts for years, and right now he is reading two English books at the same time.

1. Por que dizemos "He has listened to English podcasts for years"?
A Porque a ação começou no passado e continua até agora (present perfect + for).
B Porque aconteceu num momento terminado do passado.
C Porque é uma rotina que ele começou hoje.
2. "In his thirties, he moved into a French company" usa o past simple porque:
A A experiência continua até hoje.
B É uma ação terminada, num tempo terminado do passado.
C Está acontecendo agora.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A I have picked up French in 2005.
B I picked up French in 2005.
C I am picking up French in 2005.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Como contar sua história com o tempo verbal certo (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experiência e duração até agora. Experience and duration up to now (for / since / ever / never).I have studied English for years.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		Ação terminada, tempo terminado. Finished action, finished time (ago, in 2005, back in the 80s).I learned French in France.
for vs sincefor + período (for 18 years) · since + ponto de partida (since I was young)
agoSEMPRE com past simple: "I moved abroad twenty years ago."
