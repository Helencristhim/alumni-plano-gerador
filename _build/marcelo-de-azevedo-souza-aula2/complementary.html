Aula 2 -- The Story of Me

Movie
The Motorcycle Diaries (2004)

A young traveler crosses a continent by motorbike and is changed forever -- adventure, culture, and a life story told on the road. It mirrors your own journeys and this lesson's theme: who you were, where you have been, and how the trip shaped you. Connection to Lesson 2: listen for how the narrator moves between the past (what happened) and the present perfect (what it has meant).

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Afterward, tell your teacher one thing the traveler did (past simple) and one thing the trip has taught him (present perfect).

Podcast
How I Built This -- Guy Raz (NPR), any episode

Every episode is a life story: how someone started, the times they gave up, and how they came back and stuck with it. It is a masterclass in the exact grammar of this lesson -- past simple for the events, present perfect for the journey up to now. Natural American English at a follow-able pace.

Tip: pick one episode. Write down three sentences the guest says in the past simple and two in the present perfect.

TED Talk
How to Learn Any Language in Six Months -- Chris Lonsdale (TEDx)

The talk that proves your own philosophy: you don't study a language, you live it. Lonsdale argues that adults learn fast through real immersion and communication, not grammar drills -- exactly how you picked up French. Authentic, clear English at natural speed, on a topic you love.

Tip: watch once, then say out loud how it matches your own story -- what you have always done, and what you did differently with French.
