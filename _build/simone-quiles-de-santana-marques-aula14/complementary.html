Lesson 14 -- Resolving Disputes

Series
Suits -- Season 2, Episode 6 "All In"

The lawyers face a dispute and weigh a settlement against going to a hearing. Listen for how they talk about a claim, a settlement and a binding decision, and notice the first-conditional sentences: "if we settle now, we will...", "if they refuse, we will...".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 first-conditional sentences you hear (if + present, will + verb) and the dispute words around them.

Podcast
Legal English Podcast -- "Dispute Resolution: Mediation and Arbitration"

A practical episode on how commercial parties resolve a dispute: when they choose mediation, when they go to arbitration, and why an arbitration award is binding. The hosts use exactly the language you need for the contracts and M&A disputes you handle at work.

Note 3 steps the hosts describe, and rewrite each one as a first-conditional sentence (if + present, will + verb).

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "The first conditional: if + present, will + verb"

A short, clear lesson on how to talk about real future results with the first conditional, and why the present goes after if. Directly useful for explaining the steps of a dispute clause in the contracts you review.

After watching, write five first-conditional sentences about a dispute, then record yourself reading them with at least four dispute words from today.
