Lesson 10 -- Phone and Video Calls

Series
Suits -- any episode with a conference call

Lawyers run phone and video calls, take minutes, and later report what clients said: he said, she told me, they would. Perfect for hearing reported speech in a legal, professional context close to your work.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three sentences reporting what a character said, using said and told me with the backshift (is to was, will to would).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Telephoning and Conference Calls"

Practical episodes on handling calls in English: starting a call, dealing with a weak connection, muting, taking minutes, and wrapping up. Exactly today's vocabulary in real business situations.

Note three phrases the speakers use to manage a call. Then record a short report of the call using she said that and he told me that.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Reported Speech"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to report what people said, the tense backshift, and the difference between say and tell. Short, with real examples.

After watching, report five things people said to you this week. Use said and told me at least twice each, and remember will becomes would and can becomes could.
