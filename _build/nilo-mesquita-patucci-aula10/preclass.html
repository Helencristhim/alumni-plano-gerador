Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Dial -- to enter a number to make a call
"I dial the number from the invitation." (Discar)
Hang up -- to end a phone call
"We say goodbye and then hang up." (Desligar)
Mute -- to turn off your microphone
"Please mute your microphone when you are not speaking." (Silenciar)
Connection -- the link that lets you talk on a call
"My connection is stable today." (Conexão)
Background noise -- unwanted sound around you during a call
"There is a lot of background noise in the office." (Ruído de fundo)
Call back -- to return a call to someone later
"I will call you back in ten minutes." (Retornar a ligação)
On hold -- waiting on the line until someone is ready
"The assistant put me on hold for a minute." (Em espera)
Reach -- to succeed in contacting someone
"I could not reach the director this morning." (Conseguir falar com)
Follow up -- to take further action after a call
"I will follow up with an email." (Dar seguimento)
Minutes -- the written notes of a meeting
"Sofia will send the minutes after the call." (Ata da reunião)
Host -- the person who runs an online meeting
"The coordinator is the host of the call." (Anfitrião)
Wrap up -- to bring a meeting to an end
"Let us wrap up the call in five minutes." (Encerrar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the video call, Nilo wrote his notes. On the call, Sofia said, "I am the host." In his notes, Nilo reported it as: Sofia said that she was the host. Sofia also said, "The call will start at three." Nilo reported: She said that the call would start at three. A colleague said, "I can join by phone." Nilo reported: He said that he could join by phone. Notice the pattern: when we report what someone said, the verb usually moves one step into the past. Notice also the difference between say and tell: Sofia told me that she would send the minutes, but she said that the connection was weak. We use tell with a person and say without one.

1. Sofia said "I am the host." How does Nilo report it?
A She said that she was the host.
B She said that she is the host.
C She told that she was the host.
2. The colleague said "I can join by phone." How do we report it?
A He said that he can join by phone.
B He said that he could join by phone.
C He said me that he could join by phone.
3. Which sentence uses tell correctly?
A She told me that she would send the minutes.
B She told that she would send the minutes.
C She said me that she would send the minutes.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Usado para dizer o que outra pessoa falou. / Used to say what another person said.

Direct / DiretoReported / ReportadoExample / Exemplo
am / iswas"I am busy" -- He said he was busy.
arewere"They are online" -- She said they were online.
present (work)past (worked)"I work late" -- He said he worked late.
willwould"I will call" -- She said she would call.
cancould"I can join" -- He said he could join.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use say sem pessoa (she said that...) e tell com pessoa (she told me that...). Ao reportar, o verbo recua um passo: is vira was, will vira would, can vira could. / Use say without a person and tell with a person; the verb moves one step into the past.
