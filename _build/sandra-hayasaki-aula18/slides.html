Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

Should Have + Past Participle

For regrets about the past -- and "I wish I had" for the feeling

Listen and Notice

What Do the Orange Words Share?

Each sentence is a regret about the past. Notice the orange parts.

"I should have said yes."

"I shouldn't have given up so easily."

"I wish I had studied more."

"If only I had been a little braver."

The Rule

Regrets About the Past

PartFormExample
Better choiceshould have + past participle"I should have said yes." (but I said no)
Wrong actionshouldn't have + past participle"I shouldn't have given up." (but I did)
The wishI wish I had + past participle"I wish I had studied more."
"If only"same form, stronger feeling"If only I had been braver."

Tip: These all talk about the PAST. Compare: I wish I had time (now -- past simple) vs I wish I had had time (the past -- had + past participle).

Common Mistake

Use the Past Participle

"I should have say yes." / "I wish I studied more back then."
"I should have said yes." / "I wish I had studied more back then."

After should have and I wish I had, always use the past participle (said, taken, studied, gone, been).

Practice
