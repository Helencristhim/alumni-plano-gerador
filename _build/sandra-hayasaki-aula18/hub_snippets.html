Talking About Regrets

Olhe para o passado de forma gentil, usando should have + particípio passado (I should have studied) e I wish I had + particípio passado (I wish I had traveled). Novo vocabulário: reflect, hindsight, turning point, path, outcome, lesson, move on, better off. Foco gramatical: arrependimentos do passado e a lição aprendida.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Reflect -- to think carefully and calmly about something that happened
"When I reflect on that year, I see what it taught me." (refletir / pensar com calma)
Hindsight -- understanding a decision clearly, but only after it is done
"With hindsight, I see I should have said yes." (visão em retrospecto)
Turning point -- a moment when something changes the direction of your life
"That decision was a real turning point for me." (momento decisivo)
Path -- the direction your life takes; the choices that lead you somewhere
"I chose the safe path, and I often think about it." (caminho / trajetória)
Outcome -- the result of a choice or event; the way something turns out
"One outcome of that choice is that I value courage more." (resultado)
Lesson -- something useful you learn from an experience, good or bad
"The lesson I learned is that it is never too late." (lição)
Move on -- to accept what happened and continue your life calmly
"I take the lesson, forgive myself, and move on." (seguir em frente)
Better off -- in a better or healthier situation than before
"I am better off thinking about it calmly." (em melhor situação)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Let me reflect on one decision. Years ago, I was offered a job abroad, and I said no. Looking back, I think I should have said yes; I wish I had been braver. That was a real turning point, and I chose the safe path. With hindsight, the answer is easy to see. For a while it felt like a heavy regret. But I shouldn't have blamed myself for so long. One outcome is that I value courage more now. So I take the lesson, I am better off, and I move on.

1. When the writer says "I should have said yes", what really happened?
A He said yes and was happy.
B He said no -- and now he regrets it.
C He has not decided yet.
2. Why does the writer say "I wish I had been braver" and not "I wish I were braver"?
A Because it is about now.
B Because it is about the past, so we use "had + past participle".
C Because "been" is more polite.
3. How does the writer feel about the regret in the end?
A Still angry and blaming himself.
B Calm -- he takes the lesson, is better off, and moves on.
C He pretends it never happened.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Should Have & Wish + Past Perfect

GRAMMAR

Para um arrependimento do passado, usamos should have + particípio passado (a escolha melhor que NÃO fizemos) e shouldn't have + particípio passado (algo que fizemos e lamentamos). Para o desejo em si, usamos I wish I had + particípio passado. If only usa a mesma estrutura, com sentimento mais forte. / For a past regret, use should have + past participle, shouldn't have + past participle, and I wish I had + past participle.

TypeFormExample
Better choiceshould have + past participle"I should have said yes."
Wrong actionshouldn't have + past participle"I shouldn't have given up."
The wishI wish I had + past participle"I wish I had studied more."
"If only"same form, stronger feeling"If only I had been braver."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe resposta certa ou errada.

Are you better off now, and can you move on? Use at least four of the new words and the structures "should have + past participle" and "I wish I had + past participle".
