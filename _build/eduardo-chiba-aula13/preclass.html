Stage 1.1: Q&A Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Twelve phrases to stay in control during a tough Q&A. Listen to each one.

Clarify -- to make a question clearer so there is no confusion
"Could you clarify which period you mean?" (Esclarecer)
Buy time -- to give yourself a few seconds to think before answering
"Let me buy a little time and think." (Ganhar tempo)
Take on board -- to accept and seriously consider someone's point
"I will take your concern on board." (Levar em consideração)
Hedge -- to answer carefully without committing to an exact figure
"I would hedge and say roughly eight percent." (Ser cauteloso / não se comprometer)
Ballpark -- an approximate figure, not the exact number
"As a ballpark, around ten percent." (Estimativa aproximada)
On the spot -- under pressure to answer something immediately
"The question put me on the spot." (Pressionado a responder na hora)
Rephrase -- to say the same question in different words to check it
"So, if I rephrase your question, you mean the full year?" (Reformular)
Defer -- to put off answering now and promise to answer later
"I will defer that point and get back to you." (Adiar)
Pushback -- a strong objection or challenge to what you said
"I expected some pushback on that number." (Resistência / contestação)
Stand by -- to keep supporting what you said, with confidence
"I stand by that forecast; the data supports it." (Manter / sustentar)
Off the cuff -- said with no preparation, straight away
"Off the cuff, I would say around thirty clients." (De improviso)
Get back to you -- to promise to send the answer after the meeting
"Let me confirm the figure and get back to you." (Retornar com a resposta)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

After his talk, an investor challenges Eduardo. "That is a fair question," he begins, to acknowledge the pushback. Then he buys time: "let me think for a second." To make sure he answers the right thing, he asks an indirect question: "Could you tell me whether you mean this quarter or the full year?" When the exact number is unclear, he hedges: "It is roughly eight percent." Honestly, he defers the precise figure: "Let me confirm and get back to you." He never invents a number off the cuff.

C He ignores the investor.
2. Why does he ask an indirect question?
A To change the subject.
B To clarify the period and answer the right thing.
C To end the meeting.
3. How does he handle the exact figure?
A He invents a precise number off the cuff.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Pergunta indireta: depois de Could you tell me / May I ask / I was wondering, a ordem volta a ser de afirmativa (sujeito + verbo), SEM inversão e SEM do/does. Hedging: para responder sem cravar, use roughly, around, a ballpark of, ou it is hard to say exactly, but.... / In an indirect question the word order goes back to subject + verb (no inversion, no "do"). To hedge, use roughly / around / a ballpark of / it is hard to say exactly, but.

ToolFormExample
Indirect questionCould you tell me + subject + verb"Could you tell me what you mean?"
Polite openerMay I ask / Do you mind if I ask"May I ask where it is from?"
Hedge a numberroughly / around / a ballpark of"It is roughly eight percent."
Hedge a claimit is hard to say exactly, but..."It is hard to say exactly, but growth is strong."

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca diga Could you tell me what is it? — após o abre-aspas educado, a pergunta vira afirmativa: Could you tell me what it is?. E quando não tiver certeza do número, faça hedging em vez de chutar.
