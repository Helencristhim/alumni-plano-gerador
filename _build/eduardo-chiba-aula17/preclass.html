Aula 17 -- Pre-class

The Executive Summary -- Reports & Briefings

Condense a long report into a half-page executive summary: give the background and objective, report the findings in the passive voice, and shorten with nominalization. New vocabulary: background, objective, findings, scope, recommendation, summarize, synopsis, deliverable.

Stage 1.1: Summary Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight words for the parts of a report and executive summary. Listen to each one.

Background -- the context that explains why the report or project exists
"One line of background tells the reader why this report was written." (Contexto / antecedentes)
Objective -- the specific question or goal the report sets out to address
"The objective was to assess the risk of entering a new market." (Objetivo)
Findings -- the main facts or results that a report discovered
"The findings show that demand was higher than expected." (Conclusões / resultados)
Scope -- the boundaries of what a report or project covers
"The scope of this review is the last quarter only." (Escopo / abrangência)
Recommendation -- the action a report advises the reader to take
"The recommendation is to approve the budget for next quarter." (Recomendação)
Summarize -- to give the main points of something in a short form
"I will summarize the report in five lines." (Resumir)
Synopsis -- a brief overview that captures the essence of a longer document
"The first paragraph is a synopsis of the whole report." (Sinopse / visão geral)
Deliverable -- a concrete output that a project must produce and hand over
"The next deliverable is the market analysis, due Friday." (Entregável)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

The board cannot read the full report, so Eduardo writes an executive summary. He opens with one line of background and states the objective. Then he reports the findings impersonally, in the passive voice: "Revenue was increased by twelve percent, the targets were met, and the costs were reduced." He keeps the focus on the result, not the person. To stay concise, he uses nominalization: instead of "we decided to scale", he writes "the decision to scale". He closes with a clear recommendation and notes the scope of what happens next. Same report, but the board reads it in thirty seconds.

1. Why does Eduardo write "Revenue was increased" instead of "I increased revenue"?
A A briefing is impersonal — the result matters, not the person.
B "I increased" is grammatically wrong.
C The passive is always longer.
2. How does he shorten "we decided to scale"?
A By repeating it.
B With nominalization: "the decision to scale".
C By using an imperative.
3. How long does the board take to read the summary?
A Thirty minutes.
B Thirty seconds.
C A full day.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Para relatar de forma impessoal num resumo, use a VOZ PASSIVA (be + past participle): foca no resultado, sem dizer quem fez. Para encurtar, use NOMINALIZAÇÃO: troque uma oração inteira por um substantivo ("we decided" vira "the decision"). / To report impersonally, use the passive voice (be + past participle); to shorten, turn a whole clause into a single noun.

ToolPersonal / longImpersonal / short
Passive voice"I increased revenue.""Revenue was increased."
Passive voice"The team met the targets.""The targets were met."
Nominalization"We decided to enter the market.""The decision to enter the market."
Nominalization"The team analyzed the data.""The analysis of the data."

Key point / Ponto-chave: a passiva precisa do verbo to be + past participle: "was increased", "were met", "was approved" — nunca "was increase". A nominalização troca um verbo por um substantivo ("decided" → "decision", "analyzed" → "analysis") para deixar o resumo mais curto.

Stage 2: Order the Summary

Order

Put the five steps of an executive summary in the right order. Use Listen to hear them.

?You report the main findings, using the passive voice to stay impersonal.
?You give one line of background, so the reader knows why the report exists.
?You point the reader to the full report in the attachment for the detail.
?You state the objective, what the report set out to answer.
?You give one clear recommendation.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best line for each summary moment.

You are reporting a result to the board. Which is most impersonal?
A "I made the revenue go up."
B "Revenue was increased by twelve percent."
C "My team is increasing the revenue."
You want to shorten "We decided to scale the channel". Which is best?
A "The decision to scale the channel."
B "We have been deciding to scale."
C "We decided that we would scale."
You are closing the summary. Which line fits a briefing?
A "The recommendation is to approve the budget; the full report is in the attachment."
B "Read everything and let me know."
C "I think maybe we should do something."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection
Record yourself dictating a full executive summary for the board. Open with one line of background, state the objective, report three findings in the passive voice (was/were + past participle), give one recommendation using a nominalization ("the decision to..."), and point the reader to the full report in the attachment. Keep it to the key points — half a page, impersonal.
