Aula 04 -- Pre-class

Reading the Numbers

Prepare-se para ler relatórios e apresentar dados no trabalho: trend, draft, overview, summary, feedback, figure, outcome, milestone. Estruturas: a linguagem da certeza (clearly show, suggests, seems to, proves). Missão: apresentar os resultados do trimestre à equipe.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Trend -- a general direction in which something is changing
"Sales show an upward trend this quarter." (Tendência)
Draft -- an early version of a document before it is final
"She sent me the first draft of the report." (Rascunho)
Overview -- a short, general description of something
"The first page gives an overview of the results." (Visão geral)
Summary -- a short statement of the main points
"Read the summary if you are short on time." (Resumo)
Feedback -- comments about how good or useful something is
"The manager gave clear feedback on the draft." (Retorno / feedback)
Figure -- a number that represents an amount or value
"The sales figure rose by ten percent." (Número / valor)
Outcome -- the final result of an action or event
"The outcome of the project was very positive." (Resultado)
Milestone -- an important point or stage in a project
"Launching the app was a major milestone." (Marco)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Helen reads the first draft of the report. The overview on page one is clear, and the sales figures are strong. "The numbers clearly show an upward trend," she thinks — the data is complete, so a strong claim is safe. On page two, the data is partial: "This suggests a small change," she writes, and "it seems to point to a new outcome." She does not say it proves anything yet. The team reached an important milestone, and Helen adds the client feedback to the summary.

1. Qual frase soa MAIS certa (100% segura)? / Which sentence sounds the MOST certain?
A "The numbers clearly show an upward trend."
B "This suggests a small change."
C "It seems to point to a new outcome."
2. Por que Helen usa "suggests" na página dois? / Why does Helen use "suggests" on page two?
A The data is complete and certain.
B The data is partial, so she is careful.
C She wants to sound 100% sure.
3. O que vem depois de "seems to"? / What comes after "seems to"?
A The base verb: "seems to point".
B verb + "-s": "seems to points".
C verb + "-ing": "seems to pointing".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- A linguagem da certeza

GRAMMAR

A força da afirmação muda com as palavras que você escolhe. / The strength of a claim changes with the words you choose.

LanguageForça / StrengthExample / Exemplo
clearly show / proveforte, 100% (only with solid data)The figures clearly show a rise.
suggests / seems tocuidadoso (careful, not fully sure)The data suggests a change.
may / might point topossível (an early signal)This may point to a new trend.

Depois de seems to use o verbo na base: seems to indicate (não "indicates"). Escolha palavras fortes só com dados sólidos; com dados parciais, suavize com suggests / seems to / may.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor frase para cada situação.

Os dados estão completos e claros. / The data is complete and clear.
A "The figures clearly show a rise."
B "The figures may show a rise, maybe."
C "The figures clearly shows a rise."
Os dados são parciais; você não tem certeza. / The data is partial; you are not sure.
A "This proves the new trend."
B "This suggests a possible new trend."
C "This is the new trend, for sure."
Você abre a apresentação do relatório. / You open the report presentation.
A "Let me give you a quick overview of the quarter."
B "Let me give you a quick summary first and last."
C "Let me overview you the quarter."
Você fecha a apresentação. / You close the presentation.
A "In summary, the outcome is positive. Thanks for your feedback."
B "In summary, the feedback is the outcome of the figure."
C "In outcome, the summary is your feedback."

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Sem certo ou errado. Pense em inglês, toque no microfone e fale livremente.

Imagine you are presenting this quarter's results to your team. Give a short overview, describe one clear trend in the figures, name an important milestone, and finish with the outcome. Use strong language (clearly show) where the data is solid, and soft language (suggests, seems to) where you are not sure.
