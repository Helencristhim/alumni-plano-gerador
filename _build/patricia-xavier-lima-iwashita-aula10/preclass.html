Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Revise cada palavra do Block 2 e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Escalation -- moving a problem to a higher, more urgent level of support (escalação)
"This needs a real escalation tonight."
Critical -- extremely serious; it needs action right now (crítico)
"This is a critical issue for the customer."
Severity -- how serious a problem is, on a scale (gravidade, severidade)
"The customer raised the severity to level one."
Impact -- the effect a problem has on the customer's work (impacto)
"The impact on the business was high."
Downtime -- time when a system is not working (tempo fora do ar)
"The downtime lasted almost three hours."
Root cause -- the real, underlying reason a problem happens (causa raiz)
"We confirmed the root cause in the logs."
Workaround -- a temporary way to keep working around a problem (solução temporária)
"We shared a quick workaround during the outage."
Resolve -- to solve a problem completely so it does not return (resolver)
"By the evening, the team has resolved the issue."
Commitment -- a promise to do something (compromisso)
"She made a firm commitment to the client."
Deadline -- the time by which something must be done (prazo)
"The team met the customer's deadline."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

A customer opened a critical ticket and raised the severity to level one because the impact was high. First, the team confirmed the root cause. Then they shared a workaround during the downtime. Patrícia made a clear commitment: "If the problem comes back, we will call you." By the evening, the team has resolved the issue and met the deadline.

1. Por que "the team has resolved the issue" usa o present perfect?
A Porque aconteceu num dia específico no passado.
B Porque é um resultado que importa agora (já foi feito).
C Porque é uma rotina de toda semana.
2. "If the problem comes back, we will call you" é um first conditional porque:
A Usa if + presente ("comes") e will + verbo ("will call").
B Usa will nas duas partes da frase.
C Fala de algo que já aconteceu.
3. Which sentence opens the steps in the right order?
A "Then we confirmed the cause, first we shared a workaround."
B "First we confirmed the cause, then we shared a workaround."
C "First we shared a workaround, first we confirmed the cause."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Block 2 Toolkit

GRAMMAR

As quatro ferramentas gramaticais do Block 2, juntas (explicação em inglês e português).

Tool / FerramentaUse / UsoExample
Sequencing
first / then / after that		Colocar os passos em ordem. Put the steps in order.First we found the cause, then we fixed it.
Suggestion modals
should / could / I'd recommend		Recomendar uma solução com educação. Recommend politely.I would recommend a workaround.
Present perfect
have / has + particípio		Reportar um resultado que importa agora. Report a result now.We have resolved the issue.
First conditional
if + presente, will + verbo		Condição real futura + resultado. Real future condition.If it comes back, we will call you.

No first conditional, a parte do if fica no presente (NUNCA "if it will come back"). Depois de have/has vem o particípio: resolved, found, shared.
