Case Study — A Critical Escalation on the Meridian Account

Last month, the Meridian account opened a critical ticket. Their main platform was down, and the whole sales team could not work. The downtime lasted almost three hours, so the impact on the business was high. The customer raised the severity to level one and asked for an urgent escalation. As the customer success manager, Patrícia took ownership of the case at once.

The team has followed a clear plan. First, the engineers confirmed the root cause in the server logs: a recent update had changed one setting. Then they shared a quick workaround, so most users could keep working during the outage. After that, Patrícia escalated the case to the senior engineers and made a firm commitment to the client: a full update every thirty minutes until the platform was stable.

By the evening, the team has resolved the issue and the platform is stable again. Patrícia told the client, “If the problem comes back, we will call you at once. Unless we see a new error, we will close the ticket tomorrow morning.” The team met the deadline and protected the relationship. The customer thanked Patrícia for the clear, calm communication under pressure.

Chapter 4: Block 2 Toolkit

Four Tools, One Escalation

Grammar Review

Spot the Four Tools

First we found the cause, then we shared a workaround.

I would recommend a quick workaround for now.

We have resolved the issue.

If the problem comes back, we will call you.

ToolUseExample
Sequencing
first / then / after that		put the steps of a plan in orderFirst we found the cause, then we fixed it.
Suggestion modals
should / could / I'd recommend		recommend a solution politelyI would recommend a workaround.
Present perfect
have / has + past participle		report a result that matters nowWe have resolved the issue.
First conditional
if + present, will + verb		a real future condition + resultIf it comes back, we will call you.

In the first conditional, the if part stays in the present (not "if it will come back"). After have / has, use the past participle (resolved, found, shared).

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

If the system will go down, we will lose data.
If the system goes down, we will lose data.

In the first conditional, keep the if part in the present simple (goes, not "will go"). Only the result uses will.

We have resolved it yesterday.
We resolved it yesterday.

With a finished past time (yesterday, an hour ago), use the past simple, not the present perfect.

Chapter 5: On the Escalation Call

The Critical Update

Dialogue

The Escalation Call

D
Patrícia, this is critical. The platform is down and the impact on my team is huge. What is the status?
P
I hear you, David. I have raised the severity to level one and made this a full escalation.
D
Good. Can my team do anything while you fix it?
P
Yes. We have found the root cause, and I would recommend a quick workaround so your team can keep working.
D
And the deadline? We cannot lose tomorrow.
P
Here is my commitment: a full update every thirty minutes. If the problem comes back, we will call you at once.
Comprehension

About the Call

1. What has Patrícia done about the severity?
She has raised it to level one and made a full escalation.
2. What does she recommend so David's team can keep working?
A quick workaround, because they have found the root cause.
3. What is Patrícia's commitment to David?
A full update every thirty minutes, and a call at once if the problem comes back.
