Chapter 3: At / On / In

At, On, In

Say the time · at the hour, on the day, in the month

Prepositions of Time

Say the Time

"at three o'clock" · the hour

"on Monday" · the day

"in May" · the month

"in the morning" → "I am available in the morning."

at = hora · on = dia · in = mes / parte do dia

Discover the Pattern

When to Use At / On / In

"Let's meet at three o'clock."

"The meeting is on Monday."

"My vacation is in May."

"I am busy in the morning."

WordUse it for
atthe hour — at three o'clock
onthe day or date — on Monday
inthe month / part of the day — in May, in the morning

at = hora · on = dia/data · in = mes ou parte do dia (manha, tarde)

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"The meeting is in Monday."

✓ RIGHT

"The meeting is on Monday."

✗ WRONG

"Let's meet on three o'clock."

✓ RIGHT

"Let's meet at three o'clock."

Chapter 4: Confirming with Carter

"Let's Confirm the Meeting"

Confirm or change a meeting time on a call

Dialogue

Confirming the Meeting

C
Hi Graziele, I want to confirm our meeting.
G
Hello, Doctor. Are you available on Monday?
C
I am busy on Monday morning. Sorry.
G
No problem. Are you free in the afternoon?
C
Yes, I am free at three o'clock.
G
Perfect. Can we change it to three, then?
C
Yes, please. Let's change it to three.
G
Great. I confirm the meeting on Monday at three.
C
Thank you. Please do not cancel it.
G
Don't worry. It is confirmed. See you Monday.
