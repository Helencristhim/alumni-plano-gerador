Grammar Rule

Polite Requests & Complaints

UseFormExample
Request a thingCould I have + noun?Could I have the menu, please?
Ask someone to actCould you + base verb?Could you bring the bill?
Very polite requestWould it be possible to + base verb?Would it be possible to change my order?
Soft complaintI'm afraid + sentenceI'm afraid the salmon is undercooked.
Soft complaintThere seems to be a problem with + nounThere seems to be a problem with the bill.

Key idea: to ask for something, use Could I have + noun or Could you + base verb. To complain politely, soften it with I'm afraid or There seems to be a problem with. Always add please and stay calm.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you to bring the bill, please?"
"Could you bring the bill, please?"

After Could you and Could I have, use the base verb or a noun, never to + verb. (But: Would it be possible to bring... -- that one keeps "to".)
