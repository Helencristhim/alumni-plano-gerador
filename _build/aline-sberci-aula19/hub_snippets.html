Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Reservation -- a table you booked at the restaurant in advance
"I have a reservation for two at eight." (Reserva)
Waiter -- the person who takes your order and serves food
"The waiter brought us the menu." (Garçom)
Menu -- the list of food and drinks you can order
"Could I see the menu, please?" (Cardápio)
Starter -- the first small dish before the main course
"For a starter, I'll have the soup." (Entrada)
Main course -- the biggest, central dish of the meal
"For my main course, the grilled salmon." (Prato principal)
Bill -- the paper that shows what you must pay
"Could we have the bill, please?" (Conta)
Undercooked -- not cooked enough; still raw inside
"I'm afraid the chicken is undercooked." (Mal passado / cru)
Complaint -- when you tell someone politely that something is wrong
"I'd like to make a small complaint." (Reclamação)
Overcharge -- to make someone pay more money than is correct
"I think the bill overcharged us." (Cobrar a mais)
Refund -- money you get back when there was a mistake
"They gave me a refund for the wine." (Reembolso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline arrives at the restaurant with a reservation. "Could I have the menu, please?" she asks the waiter. She orders a soup for a starter and the salmon for her main course. When the food arrives, the salmon is undercooked. "I'm afraid the salmon is undercooked. Could you bring a hot one, please?" she says politely. Later, the bill is wrong. "There seems to be a problem with the bill -- I think it overcharged us," she says, and asks for a refund. Notice: to request something, use Could I have + noun or Could you + base verb. To complain politely, start with I'm afraid or There seems to be a problem with.

1. How does Aline ask for the menu?
A "Could I have the menu, please?"
B "Give me the menu now."
C "Could I to have the menu?"
2. How does she complain about the salmon?
A "This salmon is terrible!"
B "I'm afraid the salmon is undercooked."
C "Salmon bad, change it."
3. What does she do about the bill?
A She pays it without saying anything.
B She says there seems to be a problem and asks for a refund.
C She shouts at the waiter.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests & Complaints

GRAMMAR

Use estas formas para pedir com educação e reclamar sem ser rude. / Use these forms to make polite requests and to complain without being rude.

Use / UsoForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
Pedir algo / Request a thingCould I have + substantivo?Could I have the menu, please?
Pedir uma ação / Ask to actCould you + verbo base?Could you bring the bill?
Pedido bem educado / Very politeWould it be possible to + verbo base?Would it be possible to change my order?
Reclamação suave / Soft complaintI'm afraid + fraseI'm afraid the salmon is undercooked.
Reclamação suave / Soft complaintThere seems to be a problem with + substantivoThere seems to be a problem with the bill.

Key point / Ponto-chave: para pedir, use Could I have + substantivo ou Could you + verbo base, e sempre acrescente please. Para reclamar, suavize com I'm afraid ou There seems to be a problem with. Depois de Could you, nunca use to + verbo -- diga "Could you bring", não "Could you to bring". (Com Would it be possible to, o "to" fica.)
