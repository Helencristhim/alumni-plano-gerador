Aula 14 — Block 3: Making the Business Case: Justifying the Investment

Series
Billions (Showtime) — any build-vs-buy or investment-decision scene

Watch how leaders justify an investment to a board: they compare two options side by side, lead with the return, and are honest about the downside. Notice how often they use comparatives and superlatives (cheaper than, more cost-effective than, the highest return, the most scalable).

Tip: pick one decision scene and write five sentences comparing the two options, using at least two comparatives and one superlative.

Podcast
M&A Science — an episode on building the investment case and justifying the spend

Leaders explain the exact language from this lesson: how to compare options on upfront cost, payback, and ROI, how to weigh scalable against cheaper, and how to recommend the most cost-effective path to a board. Clear American English, real decision context.

Tip: note three comparative or superlative sentences you hear, then rewrite them for an investment of your own.
