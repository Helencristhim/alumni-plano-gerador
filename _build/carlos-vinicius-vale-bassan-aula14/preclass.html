Aula 14 -- Pre-class

Block 3 -- Making the Business Case: Justifying the Investment

Take the change to the next decision and make the business case for the investment. Compare the options side by side -- build vs buy, this vendor vs that -- and justify the one that delivers the most value. New vocabulary: ROI, payback, upfront cost, scalable, cost-effective, feasible, allocate, downside. Grammar focus: comparatives & superlatives (cheaper than, more cost-effective than, the highest return, the most scalable).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

ROI -- return on investment: the value you get back compared to what you spend
"The ROI is higher if we buy than if we build." (retorno sobre o investimento)
Payback -- the payback period: the time it takes to earn back the money you spent
"The payback is quicker if we buy from the vendor." (retorno / tempo de recuperação)
Upfront Cost -- the money you pay at the start, before any return comes back
"The upfront cost is higher if we buy than if we build." (custo inicial)
Scalable -- able to grow easily to handle more, without rebuilding it
"The vendor option is more scalable than building in-house." (escalável)
Cost-effective -- giving the best value for the money, not always the cheapest
"Buying is the most cost-effective option over three years." (custo-benefício / vantajoso)
Feasible -- realistic and possible to do with the time and money you have
"Building in-house is feasible, but it is slower." (viável)
Allocate -- to give budget or resources to a specific use or option
"We should allocate the budget to the option with the best return." (alocar / destinar)
Downside -- the bad side, risk, or disadvantage of an option
"The downside of building is the longer lead time." (desvantagem / ponto negativo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read about the investment decision and answer the questions.

The change works, so now Carlos must make the business case for the next phase. There are two options: build the platform in-house or buy it from a vendor. Building is cheaper at the start, because the upfront cost is lower, and it is more flexible than the vendor product. But the payback is longer, and it is less scalable. Buying has the higher upfront cost, but it is more cost-effective over three years: the payback is quicker and the ROI is the highest of the two. Buying is also the most scalable option. The downside of buying is the price tag the board sees first, but it is the most feasible path. So Carlos recommends they allocate the budget to buy, because it delivers the most value.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

GRAMMAR

Para comparar duas opções, use o comparativo; para escolher a melhor de todas, use o superlativo. Com adjetivos curtos (1 sílaba), use -er than e the -est: "Building is cheaper than buying"; "The vendor has the highest return". Com adjetivos longos (2+ sílabas), use more ... than e the most ...: "Buying is more cost-effective than building"; "Buying is the most scalable option". Para igualdade, use as ... as / not as ... as: "Building is not as expensive as buying". NUNCA junte os dois: não diga "more cheaper" nem "the most highest". / Use comparatives (-er than / more ... than) to compare two options, and superlatives (the -est / the most ...) for the best of all; never combine "more" with "-er" or "the most" with "-est".

FormExample
short adjective + -er than"Building is cheaper than buying."
more + long adjective + than"Buying is more cost-effective than building."
the + short adjective + -est"The vendor has the highest return."
the most + long adjective"Buying is the most scalable option."
(not) as + adjective + as"Building is not as expensive as buying."
