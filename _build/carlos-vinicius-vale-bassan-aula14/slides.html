Chapter 3 -- Comparing the Options

The "Better, Best" Language

Comparatives and superlatives: cheaper than / the most cost-effective

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence compares the options. Notice the orange words.

"Building is cheaper than buying at the start."

"Buying is more cost-effective than building over three years."

"The vendor option has the highest return."

"Buying is the most scalable option of the two."

The Rule

Cheaper Than / The Most Cost-effective

FormExample
short adjective + -er + than"Building is cheaper than buying."
more + long adjective + than"Buying is more cost-effective than building."
the + short adjective + -est"The vendor option has the highest return."
the most + long adjective"Buying is the most scalable option."
(not) as + adjective + as"Building is not as expensive as buying at the start."

Tip: short adjectives (one syllable) take -er / the -est ("cheaper", "the highest"). Long adjectives (two or more syllables) take more / the most ("more cost-effective", "the most scalable"). Use than to compare two options, and the to pick the best of all.

Common Mistake

Never Say "More Cheaper" or "The Most Highest"

"Building is more cheaper than buying, and the vendor has the most highest return."
"Building is cheaper than buying, and the vendor has the highest return."

Short adjectives take -er or the -est only: "cheaper", "the highest". Long adjectives take more or the most only: "more cost-effective". Never use both at once.

Practice

Compare the Options

"Building is _____cheaper than buying at the start." (cheap, short adjective)
"Buying is _____more cost-effective than building." (cost-effective, long adjective)
"The vendor option has _____the highest return." (high, best of all)
"Building is _____not as expensive as buying at the start." (equality, expensive)

About the Brief

1. Which option is cheaper at the start, and which is more scalable?

Building is cheaper upfront and more flexible; buying is more expensive upfront but more scalable.

2. Why is buying the most cost-effective option over three years?

The payback is quicker and the return on investment is higher, even though the upfront cost is bigger.

3. What is the advisor's advice for the board?

Lead with the option that is the most cost-effective over three years, not the cheapest on day one; show the numbers side by side and name the trade-off.
