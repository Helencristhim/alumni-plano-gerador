Chapter 4: The Code

Short Word or Long Word?

bigger · higher · the most important · the best

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Brazil is bigger than Mexico." — big (1 beat)

"Mexico has a higher growth rate than Brazil." — high (1 beat)

"Atopic dermatitis is the most important priority." — im-por-tant (3 beats)

"Asthma has the best performance this quarter." — good, irregular

All four compare. Look at the purple words — why does English change some and add a word to others?

Type of wordComparative (two things)Superlative (number one)
SHORT word
(1 syllable: big, high, low, fast, small)		add -er + than
bigger than, higher than		the + -est
the biggest, the highest
LONG word
(2+ syllables: important, significant, expensive)		more + word + than
more important than		the most + word
the most important
IRREGULAR
(learn by heart)		good → better than
bad → worse than		good → the best
bad → the worst
The two rules you must never breakA comparison always ends with than (never "that"). A superlative always starts with the. And NEVER both roads at once: never "more bigger", never "the most biggest".

In your first language, one word can work for every case. In English it splits in two — and one question decides it: is the word short or long?

Language Focus

Short Word or Long Word?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Brazil is more big than Mexico."
"Brazil is bigger than Mexico."
"This is the more important priority."
"This is the most important priority."
"Asthma performs better that last year."
"Asthma performs better than last year."

Two roads, never both: short words change their end (bigger), long words take a helper (more important). And a comparison always ends with than, never "that".
