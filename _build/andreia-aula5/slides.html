Chapter 3

The Career Tense

Have you ever...? · I have... · I have never...

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Have you ever assisted in a robotic procedure?"

"Yes, I have. I have assisted in more than fifty."

"I have completed two certifications."

"I have never operated the console alone."

StructureUseExample
Have you ever + past participle?Ask about the whole careerHave you ever supervised a team?
I have + past participle (+ number)Your experience, quantifiedI have completed two certifications.
I have never + past participleNot once until nowI have never presented abroad.

No specific date — your whole career until now. Numbers make it powerful: "more than fifty procedures".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have worked there in 2019."

✓ RIGHT

"I worked there in 2019." (data = past simple)

✗ WRONG

"Have you ever assist in a surgery?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you ever assisted in a surgery?"
