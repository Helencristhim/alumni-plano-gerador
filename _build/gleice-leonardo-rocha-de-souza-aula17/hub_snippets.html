Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Talking About Experiences -- present perfect & past simple

A new block: talk about your experiences with the present perfect (have/has + past participle) when you do NOT say exactly when -- I have visited many clients, I have travelled abroad. Use ever in questions (Have you ever travelled abroad?), never for no times, already and yet. Then contrast it with the past simple for a finished moment with a time word -- last year, I went to a conference in London. New words: experience, abroad, client, conference, visit, negotiate, trip, recently.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect & Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Present perfect: have/has + particio (visited, gone, been) para EXPERIENCIAS, quando NAO dizemos exatamente quando -- I have visited many clients. Use ever em perguntas (Have you ever..?), never para nenhuma vez, already (ja) e yet (ainda). Past simple: verbo + -ed ou irregular (went, met, presented) para um MOMENTO TERMINADO com palavra de tempo (yesterday, last year) -- Last year, I went to London. / Present perfect for experiences (no exact time, with ever/never/already/yet); past simple for a finished moment with a time word.

TenseExample
Experience -- present perfectI have visited many clients.
Question -- everHave you ever travelled abroad?
Not once -- neverI have never worked abroad.
Already / not yetI have already visited Paris. I have not visited Rome yet.
Finished moment -- past simpleLast year, I went to London.
