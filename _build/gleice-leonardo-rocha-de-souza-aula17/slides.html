The Rule

Experiences & Finished Moments

TenseExample
Experience -- present perfect (have/has + participle)I have visited many clients.
Question -- everHave you ever travelled abroad?
Not once -- neverI have never worked abroad.
Already / not yetI have already visited Paris. I have not visited Rome yet.
Finished moment -- past simple (+ time word)Last year, I went to London.

Key idea: use the present perfect for an experience when you do NOT say exactly when (with ever, never, already, yet). Use the past simple when you give a finished time, like yesterday or last year.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have went to many conferences. Last year, I have visited London."
"I have gone to many conferences. Last year, I visited London."

The present perfect uses have/has + the past participle (gone, visited). With a finished time word, use the past simple (visited, went).
