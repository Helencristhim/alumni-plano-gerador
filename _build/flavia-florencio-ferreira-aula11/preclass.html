Lesson 11 -- Pre-class

Asking for and Giving Information

Ask for and give information at an event or business fair. Key words: information, registration, entrance, restroom, elevator, floor, badge, hall, directions, sign. Structure: can / could for polite requests.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Information -- facts and details that you ask for or give
"Can I ask you for some information, please?"
Registration -- the desk where you sign in when you arrive at an event
"The registration desk is at the main entrance."
Entrance -- the door where you go into a building
"Please wait for me at the main entrance."
Restroom -- a public toilet or bathroom
"Could you tell me where the restroom is?"
Elevator -- the machine that takes you up and down between floors
"You can take the elevator to the second floor."
Floor -- one level of a building
"The main hall is on the second floor."
Badge -- a card with your name that you wear at an event
"Could I have my badge, please?"
Hall -- a big room where an event takes place
"The speech is in the main hall at ten o'clock."
Directions -- the words that tell you how to get to a place
"Can you give me directions to the hall?"
Sign -- a board with words that shows you where to go
"Look for the sign near the elevator."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia arrives at the business fair. At the main entrance, she goes to the registration desk. "Could I have my badge, please?" she asks. Then she needs some information. "Can you tell me where the main hall is?" The assistant gives her directions: "You can take the elevator to the second floor. Just follow the sign." Flavia also asks, "Could you tell me where the restroom is?" With can and could, she asks for everything in a polite, professional way.

1. Why does Flavia say "Could you tell me..." and not "You tell me..."?
A Could makes the request polite and soft.
B Could is only for the past.
C There is no difference.
2. What comes after "can" and "could" in a request?
A The word "to" + a verb.
B The base verb: have, tell, take.
C A verb with -ing.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Can you to help me, please?"
B "Can you help me, please?"
C "Can you helps me, please?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Can / Could for Polite Requests

GRAMMAR

How to ask for and give information in a polite way. Both can and could are followed by the base verb.

StructureUseExample
Can you + verb?a normal, friendly requestCan you help me, please?
Could you + verb?a more polite, softer requestCould you tell me where the hall is?
Can / Could I + verb?to ask for something for yourselfCould I have my badge, please?
Giving an answerUse You can... to give information: "You can take the elevator." Always can / could + base verb — never "can to" and never "can helps".
