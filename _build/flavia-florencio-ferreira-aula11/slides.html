Chapter 3: Can / Could

The Language of Requests

can and could + base verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Can you help me, please?"

"Could you tell me where the hall is?"

"Could I have my badge, please?"

"You can take the elevator."

One thing is always there: can / could + the base verb. Why is could more polite than can?

StructureUseExample
Can you + verb?a normal requestCan you help me?
Could you + verb?a more polite requestCould you tell me...?
You can + verbgiving informationYou can take the elevator.

Always can / could + base verb. Never "can to" and never "can helps".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Can you to help me, please?"
"Can you help me, please?"
"Could you helps me, please?"
"Could you help me, please?"
"Can I to have a badge, please?"
"Can I have a badge, please?"

After can and could, always use the base verb: no "to", no -s.
