Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Negotiate -- to talk to agree on terms
"Let us negotiate the price." (Negociar)
Discount -- a lower price
"Can you give us a discount?" (Desconto)
Agreement -- when both sides say yes
"We have an agreement." (Acordo)
Terms -- the conditions of the deal
"I agree with the terms." (Condições / termos)
Deposit -- money you pay first
"The deposit is twenty percent." (Entrada / sinal)
Warranty -- a promise to fix for free
"The warranty is two years." (Garantia)
Sign -- to write your name to accept
"We are going to sign today." (Assinar)
Deal -- a business agreement
"Let us close the deal." (Negócio)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diana is ready to close the deal, but first she wants to negotiate the price. Rafael says: "If you sign a two-year contract, we will give a ten percent discount." She also asks about the warranty, because the system must be reliable. The warranty is two years, and the deposit is twenty percent. Rafael adds: "If you accept, we will include free installation." Diana accepts the terms, so now they have an agreement and they are going to sign the deal today.

1. What does Diana want to do first?
A Negotiate the price.
B Cancel the deal.
C Change the supplier.
2. What does Rafael offer if she signs a two-year contract?
A A ten percent discount.
B A free supplier visit.
C A longer deadline.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If you sign today, we will give a discount."
B "If you will sign today, we will give a discount."
C "If you signs today, we will give a discount."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usado para fazer uma oferta com uma condição. / Used to make an offer with a condition. Também revisamos as formas de Block 2.

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
If + presente, will + verbo baseOferta com condição / An offer with a conditionIf you sign today, we will give a discount
Wh- questionsPerguntar sobre necessidades / Ask about needsWhat are your priorities?
ComparativesComparar opções / Compare optionsmore reliable than
will / going toDecisões e planos / Decisions and plansWe are going to sign it
Key point / Ponto-chaveNo if, use o presente simples (não "will"). Diga "If you sign" (não "If you will sign").
