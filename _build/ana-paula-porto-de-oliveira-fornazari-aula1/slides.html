Chapter 3: The Code

Every Day vs Right Now

I study · I am studying

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I live in the US."

"I study English every day."

"Right now, I am learning English."

"This year, I am revalidating my diploma."

Why green for every day, but blue for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines, habits and permanent facts (every day, usually, always)I study English every day.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions now or around now, temporary situations (right now, this year, at the moment)Right now, I am studying.
Stative verbs
know, like, want, need, have		Feelings and states are not used in the continuous formI know the answer. (not "am knowing")

every day / usually point to the simple · right now / at the moment point to the continuous.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Right now, I study English."
"Right now, I am studying English."
"Every day, I am walking my dogs."
"Every day, I walk my dogs."
"I am knowing the answer."
"I know the answer."

Use the present continuous (am + -ing) for right now, the present simple for a routine, and never put a state verb (know, like, want) in the continuous.
