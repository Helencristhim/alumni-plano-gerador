Complementary Activities

Extra material to practice outside class. Mark each one as done after you watch or listen.

Lesson 1 -- Who Is Ana Paula?

Documentary
Lost World of the Maya (Full Episode) -- National Geographic

A beautiful documentary about the history and culture of the ancient Maya. It is real, everyday English about a subject you love -- history, culture and discovery. Connection to Lesson 1: notice how the narrator uses the present simple to describe facts and the present continuous for what the explorers are doing right now.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down three sentences: two in the present simple and one in the present continuous.

YouTube
Present Simple and Present Continuous -- 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

A short and clear lesson on the exact grammar from today: the present simple for routines and facts, and the present continuous for what is happening now. Connection to Lesson 1: this is the difference between "I study English" and "Right now, I am studying".

Tip: pause after each example and say one sentence about your own day -- one with the present simple, one with the present continuous.

TED Talk
How to Stay Calm When You Know You Will Be Stressed -- Daniel Levitin (TED)

A calm, clear talk about health and the mind -- a wellness subject you enjoy, in careful, natural English. The speaker talks about his own routine and habits, so you hear the present simple in real use. Connection to Lesson 1: listen for how he describes what he usually does to stay healthy.

Tip: listen twice. Note five everyday phrases the speaker uses, and try to say them with clear pronunciation.
