Vocabulary

Doctor -- a person who helps and treats people who are sick

Routine -- the things you do regularly, in the same order, every day
"My morning routine starts with coffee and English."
Neighborhood -- the area around your home, with its streets and people
"I love the quiet park in my neighborhood."
Documentary -- a film that tells true facts about real life, history or nature
"On weekends, I watch a documentary about history."
To improve -- to get better at something
"I practice every day to improve my English."
Pronunciation -- the way you say the sounds of words
"I want to improve my pronunciation."
To practice -- to do something many times to get better at it
"I practice speaking at dinners with friends."
Hobby -- an activity you do for fun in your free time
"My favorite hobby is walking my dogs."
Health -- the condition of your body and mind
"As a doctor, I care a lot about health."
Weekend -- Saturday and Sunday, when most people do not work
"On the weekend, I relax and see friends."
To describe -- to say what something or someone is like
"Can you describe your normal day?"
To study -- to learn something by reading, practicing or going to classes
"Right now, I am studying for my medical exams."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana Paula is a doctor from Brazil, and she lives in the United States now. Every morning, she studies English before breakfast, because she wants to improve her pronunciation. This year, she is studying for her medical exams and is revalidating her diploma. She has a busy routine, but she loves her new neighborhood. On weekends, she watches documentaries about history and walks her two dogs in the park. Right now, she is planning a small dinner to practice English with her neighbors.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your everyday life with the right tense.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines, habits and permanent facts (every day, usually, always).I study English every day.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions now or around now, temporary situations (right now, this year, at the moment).Right now, I am studying.
he / she / itIn the present simple, add -s to the verb for he, she or it.She watches a documentary.
Stative verbs
know, like, want, need, have		Verbs of feeling and state are not used in the continuous. Say I know, not "I am knowing".
