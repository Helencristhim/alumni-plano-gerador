Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

A sandwich, please. -- Anything else? -- For here or to go?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do you order at the counter, and what does the server ask?

"A sandwich, please."

"Anything else?"

"That is all, thanks."

"For here or to go?"

Grammar Rule

A ..., please. / That is all, thanks.

FunctionStructureExample
OrderA + snack + please.A sandwich, please.
FinishThat is all, thanks.That is all, thanks.
Server asksAnything else? / For here or to go?Anything else? -- For here or to go?

Key idea: at the counter, just say A sandwich, please to order, and That is all, thanks to finish. Answer For here (eat in) or To go (takeaway).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want sandwich." (no A, not polite)
"A sandwich, please."

For one snack, use a: a sandwich, a cookie, a muffin, a soda. And add please to be polite.
