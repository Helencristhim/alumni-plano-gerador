Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Prefer -- to like one thing more than another (preferir)
"I prefer tea to coffee."
Rather -- used to say what you would choose: "I'd rather..." (de preferência/antes)
"I'd rather walk than drive."
Option -- one of the things you can choose from (opção)
"Both options sound good."
Choice -- the decision you make; the thing you pick (escolha)
"It is a difficult choice."
Instead -- in the place of something else (em vez disso/no lugar)
"I'll have water instead."
Tricky -- difficult to decide or do; not simple (complicado/difícil)
"That is a tricky decision."
Worth it -- good enough to justify the time, money, or effort (vale a pena)
"The extra cost is worth it."
Either -- one or the other of two things; both are fine (qualquer um dos dois)
"Either day works for me."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Marina has two options, she never picks the obvious one right away. She prefers to slow down and weigh both sides. Honestly, she would rather take a little longer and feel sure than choose quickly and regret it. Take a simple example: working from home or going to the office. She enjoys the quiet of home more than the noise of the office, so most days she stays in. That said, she is not really into spending every day alone, so once or twice a week she goes in instead. The choice is tricky, but for her, focus is worth it. Either way, she asks one question: which option is best for me, right now?

1. What does Marina prefer to do when she has two options?
A Slow down and weigh both sides.
B Pick the obvious one right away.
C Ask someone else to decide.
2. Why does she go to the office sometimes?
A Because she dislikes working from home.
B Because she is not really into spending every day alone.
C Because the office is quieter.
3. Which sentence correctly uses "prefer"?
A "She prefers home than the office."
B "She prefers home to the office."
C "She is prefer home."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Expressing Preferences

GRAMMAR

Como dizer o que você prefere. / How to say what you prefer.

StructureEnglishPortuguês
prefer A to BI prefer tea to coffee. (not "than")eu prefiro A a B
would rather + base verbI'd rather walk than drive. (no "to")eu preferiria/antes...
enjoy ... more than ...I enjoy reading more than watching TV.eu gosto mais de... do que...
not really intoI'm not really into crowds.eu não curto muito... (jeito gentil)
⚠ CarefulAfter "would rather", use the base verb with no "to". Use "prefer A to B" (not "than"). After "into", use the -ing form: "into cooking".
