Chapter 3

The Pattern

How to say what you prefer

Grammar Discovery

Four Ways to Say "I Choose This"

"I prefer tea to coffee."

"I'd rather walk than drive."

"I enjoy reading more than watching TV."

"I'm not really into crowds."

StructureFormExample
preferprefer A to B · prefer + to + verbI prefer tea to coffee.
would ratherI'd rather + verb (no "to") + thanI'd rather walk than drive.
enjoy ... more thanenjoy + verb-ing more than + verb-ingI enjoy reading more than watching TV.
not really intoA soft, polite way to say you dislike something: "I'm not really into..."
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I'd rather to stay home."

✓ RIGHT

"I'd rather stay home."

✗ WRONG

"I prefer coffee than tea."

✓ RIGHT

"I prefer coffee to tea."

✗ WRONG

"I am prefer the first option."

✓ RIGHT

"I prefer the first option."

After "would rather", use the base verb with no "to". With "prefer", use "to" (not "than") to compare. "Prefer" is a normal verb, so never add "am".

Planning a Weekend

So, for Saturday: a big music festival or a quiet day at the beach?
V
Honestly, I'm not really into big crowds. I'd rather have a calm day.
D
Fair enough. The beach it is. Morning or afternoon?
V
Either works for me, but I prefer the morning to the afternoon.
D
Same here. And for lunch, should we cook or order in?
V
It's a tricky one. Cooking takes time, so let's order in instead.
D
Perfect. So a quiet morning at the beach, lunch delivered.
V
Exactly. For me, a relaxed day is always worth it.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Why does she say no to the festival?
She is not really into big crowds; she would rather have a calm day.
2. Does she care about morning or afternoon?
Either works, but she prefers the morning to the afternoon.
3. What do they decide about lunch, and why?
They order in instead of cooking, because cooking takes time.
Listening

When Two Options Appear

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. When two options appear, what does she prefer to do first?
She prefers to slow down and weigh both sides instead of picking right away.
2. Why does she go to the office once or twice a week?
She is not really into spending every day alone, so she goes in for the team's energy.
3. What question does she ask instead of "which is best in general"?
She asks which option is best for her, right now.
Listening

No Wrong Answer

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. According to the speaker, what is a preference NOT?
It is not a fact, and it is not a competition.
2. What is the difference between a flat "no" and "I'm not really into it"?
A flat no closes the door; saying you are not really into something keeps it open and stays kind.
3. What sentence does the speaker call impossible to argue with?
"I prefer this, because it suits me better."
