Chapter 4: The Code

The -ed Ending

Past simple · regular verbs

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I started a new project on Monday."

"I worked on the numbers."

"I called three clients."

"My team closed a deal."

FormPatternExample
Affirmativeverb + -edI worked late. She called a client.
Negativedid not + base verbI did not work on Saturday.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you finish the report?

For regular verbs, the past is easy: just add -ed — work → worked, call → called. In the negative and the question, use did / did not with the base verb: "Did you call?", not "Did you called?".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I did finished the report.
I finished the report.
Yesterday I work late.
Yesterday I worked late.

Do not use did with a past verb: say "I finished", not "I did finished". And with a past time (yesterday, last week), add -ed: "Yesterday I worked late."
