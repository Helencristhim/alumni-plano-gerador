Chapter 3: The Code

Can, Could

polite requests

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Can you help me, please?"

"Could you explain that?"

"Could you repeat, please?"

"Can you check this email for me?"

StructureUse forExample
Can you + verb?simple requestCan you help me?
Could you + verb?more politeCould you explain that?
..., please?very politeCould you repeat, please?

Can/Could you + verb (no "to"). Could is more polite than Can. Add please to be kind. To answer: "Sure.", "Of course.", "No problem.".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

Can you to help me?
Can you help me?
Please you repeat?
Could you repeat, please?

After Can you / Could you, use the verb with no to (help, explain, repeat). Start a request with Can or Could, and put please at the end.

Chapter 4: Getting Help

Asking David

a question about the report

Dialogue

Can You Help Me?

M
David, can you help me, please? I have a question about the report.
D
Of course, Maísa. What do you need?
M
I do not understand this number. Could you explain it to me?
D
Sure. This is the total for March. Let me show you on the spreadsheet.
M
Sorry, could you repeat that, please? A little slower.
D
No problem. This number is the total for March.
M
Now it makes sense. Can I ask you one more favor?
D
Yes, of course. Go ahead.
M
Could you check my email before I send it?
D
Sure. Send it to me, and I will check it now.
