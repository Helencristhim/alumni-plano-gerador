Lesson 3 -- Navigating Tough Conversations: Pushback, Objections, and Diplomatic Disagreement

Series
Succession (HBO) -- Season 1, Episode 3: "Lifeboats"

This episode features intense boardroom negotiations where family members and executives push back on each other's strategies, escalate conflicts, and attempt to leverage their positions diplomatically. Pay attention to how characters disagree without directly saying "you are wrong," how they use hedging language to soften their positions, and how they read between the lines of what others say. Direct connection to Lesson 3: diplomatic disagreement, navigating pushback from powerful stakeholders, and using language strategically in high-stakes corporate conflict.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 phrases characters use when they disagree with someone more powerful than them. Notice how they acknowledge the other person's point before presenting their own.

Podcast
Negotiate Anything (Kwame Christian) -- Episode: "How to Handle Professional Pushback"

Kwame Christian, a negotiation expert, breaks down practical strategies for handling pushback in professional settings. He covers how to stay calm when someone disagrees with you, how to reframe objections as opportunities, and how to use empathetic language to de-escalate tension. The episode includes real-world examples from corporate environments where leaders had to navigate tough conversations with stakeholders. Direct connection to Victor's role: pushing back on HQ mandates diplomatically, mediating between regional teams, and leveraging data to strengthen your position.

Tip: listen to the first 25 minutes. Note how Kwame uses phrases like "I understand your concern" and "What I would suggest is..." to disagree respectfully. Practice repeating his key phrases out loud.

YouTube
Chris Voss -- "Never Split the Difference" | Talks at Google

Former FBI hostage negotiator Chris Voss shares his most effective negotiation tactics, including tactical empathy, mirroring, and calibrated questions. He demonstrates how to disagree without creating conflict, how to read between the lines of what people say, and how to navigate high-pressure conversations where the stakes are enormous. His approach to "no-oriented questions" is particularly relevant for corporate pushback situations. Direct connection to Lesson 3: using diplomatic language in negotiations, leveraging emotional intelligence in conflict, and navigating conversations where the other party has more power.

Tip: watch the full talk (45 min). Focus on the section about "tactical empathy" and how Voss phrases disagreements. Practice his technique of repeating the last 1-3 words of what someone says to show you are listening before you push back.
