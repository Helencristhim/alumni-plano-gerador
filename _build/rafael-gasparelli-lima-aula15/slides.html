Chapter 3: Have and Has

Present Perfect: have/has + participle

The past that still touches now.

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"We have signed three clients this year."

"The firm has opened a new office."

"We have not finished the program yet."

"Have you ever worked abroad?"

FormStructureExample
Affirmativehave/has + participleWe have signed the deal.
Affirmative (he/she/it)has + participleShe has opened an office.
Negativehave/has not + participleWe have not finished yet.
QuestionHave/Has + subject + participleHave you ever worked abroad?

Use have with I, you, we, they and has with he, she, it. After have/has always comes the past participle (signed, opened, worked, grown). We use this for actions connected to now, not a finished time like yesterday.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"We have signed the deal yesterday."

✓ RIGHT

"We have already signed the deal." / "We signed the deal yesterday."

✗ WRONG

"She opened a new office this year."

✓ RIGHT

"She has opened a new office this year."

Use the Present Perfect for time still connected to now (this year, so far). For a finished time (yesterday, in 2005), use the Past Simple. And never forget have or has before the participle.

Dialogue

The Year Review

P
Rafael, before the board meeting, can you tell me what we have achieved so far this year?
R
Of course. We have already signed three major clients, and we have just opened a new office in the south.
P
Excellent. Has the revenue grown since January?
R
Yes, it has grown by twelve percent. We have recently hired two new managers too.
P
Great work. Have we finished the new training program?
R
Not yet. We have not finished it yet, but it is almost ready. The team has worked very hard lately.
Comprehension

About the Year

1. How many clients has the firm signed so far?
It has already signed three major clients.
2. How much has the revenue grown since January?
It has grown by twelve percent.
3. Has the firm finished the training program?
No, it has not finished it yet, but it is almost ready.
