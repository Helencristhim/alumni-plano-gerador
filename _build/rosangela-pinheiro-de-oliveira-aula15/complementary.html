Talking About the Future: "Going to" & Present Continuous (free lesson)

Two of today's four tenses, from a different teacher, with fresh examples: "going to" for the plan, and the present continuous for right now and for arrangements. This is the part of the lesson people mix up the most, so a second voice on it helps it settle. It is a little above your level in places — that is on purpose, good stretch for the ear.

Tip: you do not need every word. Watch for two things only: when the teacher says "going to", and when the teacher uses am/is/are + -ing. After each example, pause and make your own travel sentence: "I am going to take the train", "Right now I am waiting at the gate". Copy the shape, change the words.
