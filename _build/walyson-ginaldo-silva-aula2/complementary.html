Lesson 2 -- My Workday at Sucafina

Series
The Office (US) -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

A whole day at a paper company, from the morning commute to the end-of-shift wrap-up. Listen for how people describe what they usually, often, and always do at work.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down five sentences that use a frequency adverb (always, usually, often, sometimes, never). Then describe your own Monday at Sucafina using three of them.

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Talking About Your Daily Routine"

A short, clear episode on describing a typical day in the present simple, with natural use of adverbs of frequency. Perfect for picking up the rhythm and word stress you are training this week.

Record a 90-second audio describing your workday: your commute, your shift, your deadlines, and how you wrap up. Use at least four new words from today.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Adverbs of Frequency"

A focused lesson on where adverbs of frequency go in a sentence: before the main verb, but after the verb "to be". Directly reinforces today's grammar discovery.

After watching, write six true sentences about your routine at Sucafina, one for each adverb: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never. Read them aloud with the correct word stress.
