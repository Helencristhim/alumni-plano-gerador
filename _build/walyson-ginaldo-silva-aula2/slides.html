Present simple + adverbs of frequency

Where do the orange words go?

"We often ship the coffee on Wednesday."

"I am usually at the office before seven."

Grammar Rule

Adverbs of Frequency

RuleExample
Adverb before the main verbI always check the schedule.
Adverb after the verb "to be"I am usually early.
Frequency scalealways · usually · often · sometimes · rarely · never
Negative with do not / does notHe does not usually work late.

Key idea: the adverb sits right before a normal verb, but right after the verb "to be". The scale runs from always (100%) down to never (0%).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I check always the schedule first."
"I always check the schedule first."

The adverb of frequency comes before the main verb, not after it -- but after the verb "to be".

Grammar Practice

Put the Adverb in the Right Place

"I _____always start my shift with a coffee." (always)
"He _____usually reviews the inventory." (usually)
"I am _____often busy before the market opens." (often, with "to be")
"We _____rarely miss a deadline." (rarely)
