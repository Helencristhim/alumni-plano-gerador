My Career Story -- Simple Past (Regular Verbs)

Tell the story of where you started. New vocabulary: start, study, join, graduate, move, apply, career, promotion. The simple past of regular verbs: add -ed (started, joined), +d after e (moved), and y to ied (studied, applied).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Start -- to begin a job, a career, or a task
"I started my career in a small office." (começar)
Study -- to learn about a subject, often at school or at night
"I studied at night for two years." (estudar)
Join -- to become part of a company or a team
"I joined Sanofi in 2015." (entrar para)
Graduate -- to finish a university course and get a degree
"I graduated from university in 2010." (formar-se)
Move -- to change to a new place, city, or team
"I moved to a new team last year." (mudar de)
Apply -- to formally ask for a job or a position
"I applied for a new position." (candidatar-se)
Career -- your professional life and journey over the years
"My career grew step by step." (carreira)
Promotion -- a move to a higher or better position at work
"I earned a promotion last year." (promoção)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Simple Past (Regular Verbs)

GRAMMAR

Para falar do passado, a maioria dos verbos ganha a terminação -ed, e a forma é a mesma para todos os sujeitos. / To talk about the past, most verbs add -ed, and the form is the same for every subject.

Rule / RegraBasePast / Passado
most verbs: + edstart, joinstarted, joined
ends in e: + dmove, graduatemoved, graduated
consonant + y: y to iedstudy, applystudied, applied
Regra de ouroa forma do passado é igual para I, you, he, she, we, they: "She started", "We started".
