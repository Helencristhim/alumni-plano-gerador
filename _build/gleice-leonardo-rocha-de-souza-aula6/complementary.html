Lesson 6 -- My Career Story

Series
The Bold Type -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

Three young women talk about how their careers started and grew. Listen for simple past verbs: started, studied, joined, moved, applied. Notice how each woman tells the story of where she began.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences in the simple past about a character's career. Then record one sentence about your own career using -ed.

Podcast
How I Built This -- any founder's story

People tell the story of their career and company, step by step: where they started, what they studied, the jobs they joined, and the moves they made. Most of the story is in the simple past, the exact grammar from today.

Choose one episode. Note 3 simple past verbs the guest uses to tell their story. Compare them with Marcos's and Sandra's stories from class.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Past simple: regular verbs (-ed)"

A short, clear lesson on the simple past of regular verbs: when to add -ed, +d, and how to change y to ied (study to studied, apply to applied). Directly useful for telling your own career story.

After watching, write your career timeline with five sentences in the simple past, then record a 60-second audio telling your story.
