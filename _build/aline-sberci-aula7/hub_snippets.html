Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Corner -- the place where two streets meet
"The bakery is on the corner." (Esquina)
Block -- the part of a city between two streets
"Walk two blocks and turn left." (Quarteirão)
Crosswalk -- the marked place where you walk across a street
"Cross at the crosswalk, please." (Faixa de pedestres)
Traffic light -- the lights that tell cars to stop or go
"Turn right at the traffic light." (Semáforo)
Roundabout -- a circular place where several roads meet
"Go around the roundabout." (Rotatória)
Sidewalk -- the path beside a street where people walk
"Walk along the sidewalk." (Calçada)
Subway -- a train that runs underground in a city
"Take the subway to the center." (Metrô)
Square -- an open public space with streets around it
"The museum is next to the square." (Praça)
Intersection -- the place where two roads cross each other
"Stop at the intersection." (Cruzamento)
Avenue -- a wide street, often with trees or shops
"The office is on a busy avenue." (Avenida)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline is new in the city. From the subway station, she goes straight along the avenue for two blocks. At the traffic light, she turns right and crosses at the crosswalk. She walks past the square and stays on the sidewalk. Her office is on the corner, across from a cafe, between a bank and a pharmacy.

1. Where does Aline cross the street?
A At the crosswalk.
B At the roundabout.
C On the sidewalk.
2. Which sentence is an instruction (imperative)?
A Her office is on the corner.
B Turn right at the traffic light.
C She walks past the square.
3. Where is Aline's office?
A On the corner, across from a cafe.
B Inside the subway station.
C On the roundabout.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Prepositions of Place + Imperative

GRAMMAR

Usado para dizer ONDE algo fica e para dar instruções de como chegar. / Used to say WHERE something is and to give instructions on how to get there.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
next to / across fromAo lado de / em frente a / PlaceThe bank is next to the bakery
between / on the corner ofEntre / na esquina de / PlaceIt is between the bank and the cafe
Imperative (base verb)Instrução, sem sujeito / Instruction, no subjectGo straight. Turn left.
Take / Walk past / CrossPegue / passe por / atravesse / DirectionsTake the second street

Key point / Ponto-chave: o imperative usa o verbo base sem sujeito (dizemos Turn left, não You turn left). Algumas prepositions precisam de of: in front of, across from, on the corner of.
