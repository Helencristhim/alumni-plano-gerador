Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Prepositions of place + the imperative

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which words say WHERE -- and which words give an instruction?

"The bank is next to the bakery."

"Go straight and turn left at the traffic light."

"The museum is across from the square."

"Take the second street on your right."

Grammar Rule

Place & Directions

StructureExample
next to / across from (place)The bank is next to the bakery
between / on the corner of (place)It is between the bank and the cafe
Imperative: base verb, no subjectGo straight. Turn left. Cross the street.
Take / Walk past (instructions)Take the second street. Walk past the square.

Key idea: the imperative gives an instruction with the base verb and no subject -- we say Turn left, not You turn left. Prepositions of place say where something is: next to, across from, between, behind, in front of.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The store is in front the bank."
"The store is in front of the bank."

Some prepositions need of: in front of, across from, on the corner of. The imperative needs no subject: say Turn left, not You turn left.
