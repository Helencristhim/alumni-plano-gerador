Chapter 4: The Code

Comparatives & Superlatives

larger than · more formal than · the most important · the best

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The university in Seoul is larger than ours. It is also bigger and newer."

"This conference is more formal than the last one. It is also more significant."

"This is the most important meeting of the year, and the best round table of the week."

"My current English is better than last year. Seoul is farther than Paris."

AdjectiveComparative (two things)Superlative (the top one)
Short: largelarger thanthe largest
Short: bigbigger thanthe biggest
Long: formalmore formal thanthe most formal
Long: importantmore important thanthe most important
Irregular: goodbetter thanthe best
Irregular: badworse thanthe worst

Short adjective (1 syllable): add -er / -est (larger, the largest). Long adjective (2+ syllables): use more / the most (more formal, the most formal). Always than after a comparative, and the before a superlative. Irregular: good → better → the best; bad → worse → the worst; far → farther → the farthest.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Seoul is more big than Paris."
"Seoul is bigger than Paris."
"This is the most best meeting of the year."
"This is the best meeting of the year."

Short adjective → -er / -est (bigger, the biggest), never "more big". And good is irregular: better, the best — never "more best".

Chapter 5: The Delegation Debrief

How Was Seoul?

A colleague asks about the trip — and this time, you compare everything yourself

Dialogue

How Was Seoul?

P
Welcome back, Maria Clara! How was the conference in Seoul? Was it good?
M
It was wonderful, Paulo. This conference was more formal than the previous one, and much more significant.
P
Interesting. And the university — is it very different from ours?
M
Yes. The campus in Seoul is larger and more modern than ours. But our campus is more comfortable, in my opinion.
P
And the hotel? Was it better than last time?
M
The hotel was more comfortable, but it was also more expensive and more distant from the campus. Next time, I would rather stay in a local hotel.
P
That makes sense. What was the best moment of the week?
M
The final round table. It was the most important meeting, and I spoke in English, with no interpreter.
P
That is fantastic! Your English is really improving.
M
Thank you, Paulo. My current English is better than last year. The more I practice, the better I speak.
