Video — grammar
Comparative and Superlative Adjectives (Woodward English)

This is the exact grammar of lesson 8: short adjectives with -er / -est (larger, the largest) and long adjectives with more / the most (more formal, the most important). The teacher speaks slowly and clearly, with many examples on screen.

Tip: after the video, say five comparisons out loud about your world: "Seoul is larger than Maceio", "This meeting is more important than the last one". Don't write them first.

Video — speaking
Comparative & Superlative Adjectives (English with Lucy)

Lucy explains the same structures with clear, calm British-friendly English and shows the irregular forms too: good → better → the best, bad → worse → the worst. Perfect to hear the sounds of "than" and "the most" many times.

Tip: pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then compare two hotels or two cities: "This one is more comfortable, but that one is cheaper". Speak, don't just listen.

Podcast — listening
Comparatives and Superlatives — 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

A short audio programme with two hosts who talk about comparatives and superlatives in natural, everyday English. Only six minutes — perfect for your two hours of daily immersion. Understanding real speech by ear is the hardest skill, and the only way to train it is a little every day.

Tip: listen ONCE without stopping — don't worry about the words you miss. Then write three comparisons you heard or can use: "bigger than", "more expensive than", "the best". Bring them to the next class.
