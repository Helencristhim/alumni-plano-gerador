Email Essentials -- Opening, Body & Closing

How to write clear, professional corporate emails: open with a polite greeting and reason, keep the body short, and close with a clear next step. New vocabulary: recipient, subject line, attachment, greeting, regarding, forward, reply, CC, sign off, follow up.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recipient -- the person an email is sent to
"The recipient is the person who receives your email." (Destinatário)
Subject line -- the short title that shows the topic of the email
"A clear subject line tells the reader the topic at once." (Assunto)
Attachment -- a file you send together with an email
"Please find the contract as an attachment." (Anexo)
Greeting -- the polite opening line of an email
"A formal greeting is Dear Mr. Reed." (Saudação)
Regarding -- about; concerning a topic
"I am writing regarding the agreement." (A respeito de)
Forward -- to send a received email on to another person
"Could you forward this email to your partner?" (Encaminhar)
Reply -- to write an answer to an email
"I will reply to your message this afternoon." (Responder)
CC -- to send a copy of an email to other people
"Please put my manager in CC." (Com cópia)
Sign off -- to end an email with a polite closing
"I sign off with Best regards." (Despedir-se)
Follow up -- to send a reminder or check progress later
"I will follow up on Friday if I hear nothing." (Dar seguimento)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On Tuesday, Thomas Reed sent Simone an email regarding the NDA. The subject line was clear, and the greeting was polite: "Dear Ms. Quiles." He sent the draft as an attachment and asked her to reply with her comments. Simone decided to forward the email to her contracts team and put her manager in CC, so everyone stayed informed. In her answer she kept the body short, asked one clear question, and remembered to sign off politely with "Best regards." She promised to follow up on Friday if she did not hear back. The recipient, Thomas, answered within an hour.

1. Why was the email easy to understand?
A The subject line was clear and the greeting was polite.
B It had no subject line.
C It was written in capital letters.
2. What did Simone do so her team stayed informed?
A She deleted the email.
B She forwarded the email and put her manager in CC.
C She printed the contract.
3. How did Simone close her email?
A With no closing at all.
B "See you, bye."
C She signed off politely with "Best regards."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Email Code

GRAMMAR

Num e-mail profissional, siga três partes: abra, escreva o corpo e encerre. / In a professional email, follow three parts: open, write the body, and close.

Part / ParteWhat you do / O que fazerExample / Exemplo
Opening / AberturaSaudação + motivo / Greeting + reason"Dear Mr. Reed, I am writing regarding the NDA."
Body / CorpoUm pedido claro; cite o anexo / One clear request; mention the attachment"Could you please review the attached draft?"
Closing / EncerramentoPróximo passo + despedida / Next step + sign off"I look forward to your reply. Best regards, Simone."
Key point / Ponto-chaveComece com uma saudação formal e o motivo, faça um pedido claro no corpo e encerre com o próximo passo antes de se despedir.
