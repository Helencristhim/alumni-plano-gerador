Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Recall

Vocabulary

These are key words from your first eleven lessons. Listen to each one and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- a list of the topics to discuss in a meeting
"Let's review the agenda before we start." (Pauta)
Draft -- an early version of a document you will revise
"I sent the first draft of the report this morning." (Rascunho)
Tender -- a formal invitation for companies to submit bids
"We submitted our bid for the public tender." (Licitação)
Pitch -- a short, persuasive presentation of an idea or product
"My pitch convinced the leadership team." (Apresentação de venda)
Outcome -- the final result or effect of an action or process
"The outcome of the meeting was very positive." (Resultado)
Compromise -- an agreement where each side gives up something
"We reached a fair compromise on the price." (Acordo mútuo)
Common ground -- a topic or opinion that two sides share and agree on
"We found common ground on the deadline." (Ponto em comum)
Feedback -- helpful information about how well someone is doing
"Thank you for the constructive feedback." (Retorno / devolutiva)
Acknowledge -- to recognize or admit that something is true or valid
"I acknowledge your point; it is a fair one." (Reconhecer)
Rapport -- a friendly relationship in which people understand each other
"We built a strong rapport with the client." (Sintonia)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Structure Map

GRAMMAR

Esta aula nao traz gramatica nova: ela revisa as estruturas que voce ja aprendeu. Voce escolhe cada estrutura por um motivo. / This lesson has no new grammar: it reviews the structures you already learned. You choose each structure for a reason.

StructureUse it forExample
Present perfectexperience with a result now"I have presented the data."
Passive voicethe action matters, not who did it"The proposal was approved."
First conditionala real future possibility"If we keep this pace, we will win."
Comparativescomparing two things"This quarter is stronger than the last."
Question tagsconfirm and connect"You work in tech, don't you?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: a voz passiva e o present perfect usam o past participle (approved, written, presented), nunca o verbo base. O comparativo usa -er than (stronger than) ou more + adjetivo (more confident), nunca os dois juntos. O question tag combina com o auxiliar e o pronome da frase. / The passive and the present perfect use the past participle; comparatives use -er than or more, never both.
