Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Countable & Uncountable Nouns

Grammar

Alguns substantivos podem ser contados (one table, two tables) e outros nao (soup, rice, water). Use "many" e "how many" com contaveis, e "much" e "how much" com incontaveis. Use "some" em frases afirmativas e "any" em perguntas e frases negativas. Para pequenas quantidades, use "a few" (contavel) e "a little" (incontavel). / Some nouns can be counted (one table, two tables) and others cannot (soup, rice, water). Use "many" and "how many" with countable nouns, and "much" and "how much" with uncountable nouns. Use "some" in positive sentences and "any" in questions and negatives. For small amounts, use "a few" (countable) and "a little" (uncountable).
