Chapter 3: Some, Any, Much, Many

Countable & Uncountable

"How many tables? How much soup?"

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"We have some chicken and some rice."

"Is there any soup? We do not have any fish."

"How many tables are free?"

"How much soup do you want?"

WordWhen to useExample
somepositive sentencesWe have some rice.
anyquestions & negativesIs there any soup?
manycountable (tables, chairs)How many tables?
muchuncountable (soup, rice, water)How much soup?

Use many for things you can count (tables, chairs). Use much for things you cannot count (soup, rice, water). Use some in positive sentences and any in questions and negatives.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"How much tables are free?"

✓ RIGHT

"How many tables are free?"

✗ WRONG

"Is there some fish tonight?"

✓ RIGHT

"Is there any fish tonight?"

Chapter 4: At the Restaurant

Ordering the Meal

Roberto orders dinner at the restaurant

Dialogue

Roberto Orders Dinner

W
Good evening. Here is the menu. Are you ready to order, or do you need a few minutes?
R
Thank you. Is there any soup as a starter? And what do you recommend for the main course?
W
Yes, we have some tomato soup. For the main course, the grilled fish is excellent, with a side dish of rice.
R
Perfect. I will have the soup and the fish. How much is the main course?
W
It is eighteen euros. Would you like any dessert after that?
R
Yes, a little chocolate cake, please. And could we have the bill after dessert?
