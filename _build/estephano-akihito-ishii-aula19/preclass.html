Vocabulary

Post -- to put a photo or a message on your page

Share -- to send something so other people can see it
"I never share my password online." (compartilhar)
Follow -- to see a page every day in your feed
"I often follow new tech pages." (seguir)
Like -- to tap a heart when you enjoy a photo
"I usually like the photos from my friends." (curtir)
Comment -- a few words you write under a photo
"I usually answer the comments on my page." (comentário)
Upload -- to move a photo from your phone to a page
"I upload my best photos." (enviar / fazer upload)
Notification -- a little alert: a new like or message
"I get many notifications every day." (notificação)
Account -- your page with your name, where you log in
"I always check my account in the morning." (conta)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Frequency Adverbs

GRAMMAR

Como dizer com que frequência você faz algo. / How to say how often you do something.

AdverbFrequênciaExample
always100% -- sempreI always check my account.
usually / often~70-90% -- geralmenteI usually like the photos.
sometimes~50% -- às vezesI sometimes post a photo.
never0% -- nuncaI never share my password.
Posição: o advérbio vem antes do verbo principal (I always post), mas depois do verbo "to be" (I am usually online).

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You do something 100% of the time. What do you say?
A "I always check my account."
B "I check always my account."
C "I check my account always."
You want to ask about frequency. What do you say?
A "How much do you post photos?"
B "How often do you post photos?"
C "How many do you post photos?"
The adverb goes after "to be". What do you say?
A "I usually am online."
B "I am usually online."
C "I am online usually."
