Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

How Often

always -- usually -- sometimes -- never

Grammar Rule

Where Does It Go?

From 100% to 0%:

always (100%) > usually > often > sometimes > never (0%)

Before the verb

with normal verbs

"I always post photos."

"I never share my password."

After "to be"

with am / is / are

"I am usually online."

"It is sometimes slow."

Key idea: the adverb goes before a normal verb, but after the verb "to be".

The Question

Ask How Often

To ask about frequency, we use How often...?

"How often do you post photos?" -- "I sometimes post photos."

"How often do you check your account?" -- "I always check it."

Good to know:

You can also answer with: "every day", "once a week", "two times a day".

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

WRONG

"I post always photos."

RIGHT

"I always post photos."

The frequency adverb goes before the main verb.

Grammar Practice

Where Does It Go?

Put the adverb in the right place, then reveal the answer.

1. (always) I / check / my account.

I always check my account. (before the verb)

2. (never) I / share / my password.

I never share my password. (before the verb)

3. (usually) I / am / online in the evening.

I am usually online in the evening. (after "to be")

4. (sometimes) I / post / a photo of my project.

I sometimes post a photo of my project. (before the verb)
