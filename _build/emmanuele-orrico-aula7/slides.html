Chapter 3: The Code

Yes, I'll Do It Now

I'll send it · I'll check · I'll confirm · vs · I'm going to present

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You need the report? I'll send it right now."

"I'll check and get back to you today."

"Don't worry. I'll confirm the dates tomorrow."

"Next week, I am going to present the plan."

Three purple sentences decide something in this moment. The green one is a plan from before. What is the difference?

BlockUseExample
I'll · We'll · You'll · They'll · He'll · She'll (= I will, we will...)A decision, an offer or a promise you make in this moment — not planned before.I'll send it right now.
+ verb in the normal form — no "to", no -ingAfter will / 'll, the verb never changes.I'll send · I'll check · I'll confirm
Negative: won't (= will not)One short block for "I am not going to".I won't be late for the meeting.
Question: Will you...?Put will at the front.Will you send me the agenda?
going to = decided before · will = decided nowThe plan you already made uses going to. The offer you give on the spot uses will.I am going to present next week. · "You need it now? I'll send it."
Ready phrase: I'll get back to you.Nothing to build. The professional way to say "I'll answer you soon" — when you cannot say yes yet."Is July safe?" — "I'll check and get back to you."

You do not conjugate anything. You pick the block — I'll, we'll — and you add your verb.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I will to send you the report."
"I'll send you the report."
"I will sending it now."
"I'll send it now."
"You need it now? I send it to you."
"You need it now? I'll send it to you."

After will, there is no "to" and no -ing — just the normal verb: I'll send. And for an offer on the spot, use I'll, never the simple present.

Chapter 4: The Closing Call

Closing the Meeting

Your next steps — and one thing Marc needs today.

Dialogue

So, What Are Your Next Steps?

M
Thank you, Emmanuele. Before we close, tell me the next steps for Brazil.
E
Sure. Let me give you the next steps. This week, I am going to send the agenda, and next week I am going to follow up with the private hospitals.
M
Good. And the Brazil report — I need it today. Is that possible?
E
No problem. I'll send it right now, after the call.
M
Perfect. And the dates for the August visit — are they confirmed?
E
Not yet. I'll confirm them with my team and get back to you tomorrow.
M
Great. If you need help with the slides, I'll prepare a template for you.
E
Thank you for the offer. So the outcome today is clear: the report now, the dates tomorrow, and the hospitals next week.

1. Find one next step she planned before the meeting.
"I am going to follow up with the private hospitals next week."
2. Find one offer she decides on the spot, with "I'll".
"I'll send it right after this email." (or: "I'll confirm them... and I'll get back to you tomorrow.")
3. Why is it "I am going to follow up" but "I'll send it right after this email"?
The follow-up is a plan from before (going to). Sending the report now is a decision on the spot (will).

The outcome is clear, and Brazil is on track.
