Lesson 07 -- What's Next?

Series
Suits — Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot" (Netflix)

Harvey Specter is the master of the offer on the spot. Every time someone brings him a problem, he answers in the moment: "I'll handle it," "I'll take care of it," "Consider it done." That is exactly the "I'll" of this lesson — the yes you give without freezing, in the middle of a meeting.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Treasure hunt: write down 3 sentences that start with "I'll..." Do not try to understand 100% — look ONLY for the offer on the spot.

Podcast
Business English Pod — BEP 81c: Action Points and Wrapping Up

A whole lesson about the end of a meeting: how to summarize the next steps, assign the actions, and close with a clear outcome. It is the last five minutes of your Global call, in the mouth of real executives — exactly the moment this lesson trains.

Tip: listen ONCE on your way to work, with no pauses. Then listen again and repeat out loud every offer with "I'll" and every next step with "going to". You already listen to podcasts every day — give one of them to your English.

YouTube
Simple Future with Will — Grammar & Verb Tenses (Ellii)

The grammar of this lesson explained slowly, with examples: will and the contraction 'll for decisions, offers and promises, plus the negative won't and the question. It is the block you use for every "I'll send it" and "I'll get back to you".

Tip: turn on the English subtitles. Pause after each example and make the SAME sentence with your own work (I'll send the report, I'll confirm the dates, I'll follow up). Make 5 offers about YOUR real meetings.
