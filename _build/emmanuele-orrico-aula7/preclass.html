Lesson 07 -- Pre-class

What's Next? -- Next Steps and Offers in the Global Meeting

Close the Global meeting like a leader: give the next steps you already planned, and make offers on the spot without freezing. Key words: agenda, next steps, outcome, to follow up, to commit, to confirm, offer, to arrange, upcoming. Structures: will ('ll) for decisions, offers and promises you make in the moment (I'll send it right now / I'll get back to you), the negative (I won't be late) and the question (will you send it?); in contrast with going to for the next steps you planned before (I am going to follow up with the hospitals). Phrase of the lesson: "I'll check and get back to you."

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are the nine words you hear at the end of every meeting.

Agenda -- the list of topics for a meeting
"Let's look at the agenda for the Global meeting."
Next steps -- the actions you take after the meeting
"Let me tell you the next steps for the project."
Outcome -- the final result of a meeting or a decision
"The outcome of the meeting is a new plan for Brazil."
To follow up -- to check something again later, after the meeting
"I am going to follow up with the hospital next week."
To commit -- to promise you are going to do something
"I commit to send the report on Friday."
To confirm -- to say that something is certain
"I will confirm the dates with Paris tomorrow."
Offer -- when you say you will help or do something for someone
"Thank you for the offer. That is very kind."
To arrange -- to plan and organize a time or a meeting
"I will arrange a call with the field team."
Upcoming -- happening soon, in the near future
"The upcoming meeting is very important for Brazil."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The meeting is ending, and Marc wants the next steps for Brazil. Emmanuele is ready. "Let me give you the next steps," she says. This week she is going to send the agenda, and next week she is going to follow up with the private hospitals. Then Marc asks for the Brazil report today. She does not freeze. "No problem, I'll send it right now," she says. The dates for the August visit are not confirmed yet, so she adds: "I'll confirm them tomorrow and I'll get back to you." Marc offers to prepare a template. "Thank you for the offer," she answers. The outcome is clear, and the upcoming meeting has an agenda. She commits to send everything before Friday.

1. Why is it "I'll send it right now" and NOT "I am going to send it"?
A She decides it in THIS moment — it is an offer on the spot. will / 'll is for a decision made now.
B Because "send" is an irregular verb.
C Because the sentence is about the past.
2. "I will to send the report." Why is this WRONG?
A Because after will there is no "to". The correct form is "I'll send the report".
B Because "report" needs a capital letter.
C Because you must always say "going to".
3. "Next week she is going to follow up with the hospitals." Why going to here?
A Because it is a plan she made BEFORE the meeting, not a decision in the moment.
B Because "follow up" cannot be used with will.
C Because the action is happening right now.
4. Which sentence is correct at the end of a meeting?
A "You need it now? I'll send it right now, and next week I am going to follow up with the hospitals."
B "You need it now? I will sending it, and next week I go to follow up."
C "You need it now? I send it, and next week I will to follow up."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Offer Block: WILL

GRAMMAR

A meeting ends with two things: the steps you planned (going to) and the yes you give on the spot (will). This is the yes.

StructureUseExample
I'll · We'll · You'll
They'll · He'll · She'll
(= I will, we will...)		A decision, an offer or a promise you make in this moment — you did NOT plan it before. One ready block for everyone.I'll send it right now.
+ verb in the NORMAL formAfter will / 'll, the verb NEVER changes: no "to", no -ing, no -ed, no -s.I'll send · I'll confirm · I'll check
Negative: won't (= will not)One short block. It is the opposite of the offer: what you will not do.I won't be late for the meeting.
Question: Will you...?Put will at the front, then the person.Will you send me the agenda?
going to = planned before · will = decided nowThe next steps you planned use going to. The offer you give on the spot uses will. Simple test: did you plan it before, or decide it now?I am going to follow up next week. · "You need it now? I'll send it."
Ready phrase: I'll get back to you.Nothing to build. The professional way to say "I'll answer soon" when you cannot say yes yet — instead of freezing or promising wrong."Is July safe?" — "I'll check and get back to you."

Common mistake: many people say "I will to send" — that "to" does NOT exist after will. It is "I'll send". And do not put -ING after it: "I'll confirm", never "I will confirming". For a quick offer, always use I'll, never the simple present ("I send it").

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer — speak for two minutes, with no script.

You are closing a Global meeting with Marc Dubois. Do it in this order: first give your next steps (two or three things you are going to do, and when), using "going to"; then make two offers on the spot with "I'll" (for example, send the report now, or confirm a date); and when he asks about something you cannot promise yet, use the phrase of the lesson: "I'll check and get back to you." Finish with the outcome: "The outcome is a clear plan for the next quarter." Take your time and do not read from a script.
