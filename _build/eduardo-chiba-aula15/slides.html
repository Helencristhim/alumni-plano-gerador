Vocabulary -- Part 1

Words to Show Momentum

Early proof that it is working
Traction
Real, measurable early evidence that customers want the product
"We have strong traction in three new cities."
Months of cash you have left
Runway
How many months a company can operate before it needs more cash
"With this round, our runway extends to two years."
To grow fast without growing costs
Scale
To grow quickly while keeping costs under control
"We are raising capital to scale faster across Brazil."
How fast you spend cash each month
Burn rate
The speed at which a company spends its cash each month
"Our burn rate is low, so the runway is long."

Vocabulary -- Part 2

Words to Make the Ask

To ask investors for funding
Raise
To get money from investors to grow the company
"We are raising five million to enter two new markets."
What the whole company is worth
Valuation
The total value an investor or the market puts on a company
"The round is at a fifty-million valuation."
A share of ownership in the company
Equity
A share of ownership in a company given to an investor
"In return, we offer ten percent equity."
The document with the main deal terms
Term sheet
A short document listing the main terms of an investment, before the final contract
"If you are interested, we can sign a term sheet this week."

The Arc

The Investor Pitch Arc

MoveFrom Block 3Language
1. StructureLesson 11 -- signposting"First, the problem; then, our solution."
2. The numbersLesson 12 -- data trends"Revenue has grown sharply; churn has fallen."
3. The productLesson 14 -- demo & passive"The process is handled in one place."
4. The hard questionLesson 13 -- Q&A"That is a fair question. Let me come back to it."

Key idea: a pitch is not a new skill — it is the whole of Block 3 in one arc. You signpost the structure, you tell the story of the numbers, you show the product, and you stay calm for the hardest question. Then you make the ask.

Watch Out

Common Mistake

Let me list every feature our platform has.
First, the problem; then, how our solution fixes it.

A pitch opens with the problem the investor already feels, not a feature list. Frame the problem, signpost the solution, and only then bring in the numbers and the demo.
