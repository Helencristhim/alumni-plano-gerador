Stage 1.1: Investor Pitch Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight phrases investors expect to hear. Listen to each one.

Traction -- real, measurable early evidence that customers want the product
"We have strong traction in three new cities." (Tração / prova de demanda)
Runway -- how many months a company can operate before it needs more cash
"With this round, our runway extends to two years." (Fôlego de caixa)
Scale -- to grow quickly while keeping costs under control
"We are raising capital to scale faster across Brazil." (Escalar)
Burn rate -- the speed at which a company spends its cash each month
"Our burn rate is low, so the runway is long." (Velocidade de consumo de caixa)
Raise -- to get money from investors to grow the company
"We are raising five million to enter two new markets." (Captar / levantar capital)
Valuation -- the total value an investor or the market puts on a company
"The round is at a fifty-million valuation." (Avaliação / valuation)
Equity -- a share of ownership in a company given to an investor
"In return, we offer ten percent equity." (Participação societária)
Term sheet -- a short document listing the main terms of an investment, before the final contract
"If you are interested, we can sign a term sheet this week." (Carta de intenções)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduardo opens his pitch with the problem, not the product. "First, renting is slow; then, our solution," he signposts. The numbers come alive as a story: revenue has grown sharply and churn has fallen to a record low. "We have strong traction in three new cities," he adds. He shows the product in the passive: "the whole process is handled in one place." When the investor asks a hard question, he buys time: "That is a fair question. Let me come back to it." Finally, he makes the ask: "We are raising capital to scale faster, in return for equity."

1. How does Eduardo open the pitch?
A With the problem, signposted clearly.
B With a list of every feature.
C With the valuation.
2. Why does he say "revenue has grown" and "churn has fallen"?
A To describe a single day.
B To tell the story of a trend with the present perfect.
C To ask a question.
3. What does he do with the hard question?
A He guesses immediately.
B He buys time and stays calm before answering.
C He ignores it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Um pitch reúne o Bloco 3 inteiro num arco. Use signposting (first, then, finally) para estruturar, present perfect (has grown, has fallen) para contar a história dos números, a voz passiva (is handled) para focar no produto, e linguagem de Q&A (that is a fair question) para a pergunta difícil. / A pitch combines the four Block 3 skills into one ten-minute arc.

MoveFromExample
Structuresignposting"First, the problem; then, our solution."
The numberspresent perfect"Revenue has grown; churn has fallen."
The productpassive voice"The process is handled in one place."
The hard questionQ&A language"That is a fair question. Let me come back to it."

Key point / Ponto-chave: o pitch não é uma habilidade nova — é o Bloco 3 inteiro num só arco. Abra com o problema, prove com os números, mostre o produto, e só então faça o ask.
