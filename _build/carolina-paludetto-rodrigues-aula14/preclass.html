Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- the comparative

GRAMMAR

Usamos o comparativo para comparar DUAS coisas. Adjetivos curtos ganham -er; adjetivos longos usam more. Sempre conectamos com than. / We use the comparative to compare TWO things. Short adjectives add -er; long adjectives use more. We always connect with than.

TypeRuleExample
Short adjectiveadj + er + thanhard → harder than
Double letterdouble + erbig → bigger than
Adjective in -yy → ier + thaneasy → easier than
Long adjectivemore + adj + thanmore interesting than
Irregularspecial formgood → better, bad → worse

Key point / Ponto-chave: adjetivos curtos ganham -er (hard → harder, big → bigger, easy → easier). Adjetivos longos usam more (more interesting, more difficult). Dois adjetivos são irregulares: good → better, bad → worse. Nunca use more e -er juntos (não "more easier") e sempre use than entre as duas coisas: "Math is harder than English".
