Chapter 3: The Pattern Behind It

What Have You Been Doing?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I have been learning English for a year."

"She has been taking stock of her life."

"We have been travelling since I retired."

"He has been thriving in his new chapter."

FormExample
AffirmativeI have been learning English.
NegativeShe hasn't been sleeping well.
QuestionHave you been working hard?

Pattern: have / has + been + verb-ing. For an action that started in the past and is still going on (or just stopped, leaving a result). It stresses the activity and its duration -- often with for and since.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

I have learning English for a year.
I have been learning English for a year.

The continuous always needs been before the -ing verb.

I have been knowing her for years.
I have known her for years.

State verbs like know, love, believe stay in the simple form, not the continuous.

Chapter 4: Telling Your Story

A Friendly Interview

Dialogue

So, Tell Me About Yourself

I
Pricila, it's great to meet you. So, tell me -- what have you been doing lately?
P
It's lovely to meet you too. Well, I've been taking stock of my life. After a long, demanding career as a dentist, I've reached a real turning point.
I
That sounds like a big change. What have you been filling your time with instead?
P
All sorts of things! I've been travelling, and I've been learning English, which is the most fulfilling part. I've come a long way already.
I
I love that. And when you look back on your career, what are you most proud of?
P
Building my own clinic was a real milestone. But honestly, I'm most proud that I'm still thriving and still curious about the world.
