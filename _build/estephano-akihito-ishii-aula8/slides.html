Grammar Discovery

"What is your name?"

"What do you do?"

"What do you like doing?"

Grammar Rule

Questions to Meet Someone

QuestionWordAnswer
What is your name?to be (is)My name is Estephano.
Where are you from?to be (are)I am from Sao Paulo.
What do you like doing?doI like playing games.

Key idea: use is/are for name and origin; use do for actions. Never use both together.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Where you from?"
"Where are you from?"

Questions about name and origin need is or are. Do not forget the verb.
