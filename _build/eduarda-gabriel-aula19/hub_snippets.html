Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key status-report terms for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Status -- where something stands right now (on track / at risk)
"Headline status: the deal is on track."
Update -- the new information since the last report
"Here is the weekly update for the stakeholders."
Progress -- the advance already made toward a goal
"We have made strong progress this week."
Pending -- still waiting, not yet finished or approved
"The lender approval is still pending."
Blocker -- something that stops or delays progress
"The one blocker is the pending lender approval."
Deliverable -- a concrete item to be produced and handed over
"Each deliverable has an owner and a deadline."
Timeline -- the sequence of dates and deadlines
"The timeline shows closing by next Friday."
Highlight -- the single most important point to flag
"The highlight: we have raised thirty million so far."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduarda writes the weekly update for the stakeholders. She leads with the headline status and one highlight. To report progress, she uses the present perfect, because the result matters now: "We have completed due diligence", "the client has approved the timeline", "we have already booked the closing call", and "we have raised thirty million so far". For open items she uses "not... yet": "we have not closed the financing yet". She names the one blocker (the pending lender approval) and gives each deliverable an owner and a timeline.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) to report a result that matters now, without saying exactly when. Add already (done sooner than expected), yet (not done, in negatives), and so far / to date (up to now). Use o present perfect para um RESULTADO com relevancia agora, sem dizer quando: "We have completed it". Para um momento exato, use o past simple: "We completed it on Monday".

UseFormExample
Completed resulthave/has + p.p."We have completed due diligence."
Not finishedhave not + p.p. + yet"We have not closed it yet."
Up to nowhave + p.p. + so far"We have raised $30M so far."

Common Mistake:

"We have completed due diligence yesterday."

"We have completed due diligence." (no exact time)

Present perfect = resultado AGORA, sem tempo exato. Com um tempo exato (yesterday, on Monday), use o past simple.
