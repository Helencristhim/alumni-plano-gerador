Chapter 3 — The Result

Present Perfect

Look at the purple verbs. How do we report a result that matters now, without a time?

We have completed due diligence.
The client has approved the timeline.
We have already booked the closing call.
We have not received the final figures yet.
UseFormExample
Completed resulthave/has + past participle"We have completed due diligence."
Already donehave + already + p.p."We have already booked the call."
Not finishedhave not + p.p. + yet"We have not closed the financing yet."
Up to nowhave + p.p. + so far / to date"We have raised $30M so far."
Grammar Rule

Result Now

Past simple (when it happened)Present perfect (result now)
"We completed it on Monday.""We have completed due diligence."
"The client approved it yesterday.""The client has approved the timeline."
"We booked the call earlier.""We have already booked the closing call."
"We did not get the figures.""We have not received the figures yet."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"We have completed due diligence yesterday and we not signed the term sheet."
"We have completed due diligence, but we have not signed the term sheet yet."

Present perfect = result now, no exact time. Use "yet" for open items, and never drop "have / has".

Dialogue

The Status Review

J
Eduarda, is the weekly update for the stakeholders ready?
E
Almost. I led with the headline status: on track. Then I reported the progress.
J
Good. How did you phrase what is done?
E
In the present perfect: "We have completed due diligence" and "we have raised thirty million so far".
J
Perfect. And anything not finished? Any blocker?
E
One: "We have not closed the financing yet." The lender approval is still pending.
J
Make sure each deliverable has an owner and a timeline.
E
Done. And I put the one highlight on top: we are ahead of the timeline. I will send you the draft.
