Chapter 4: Email Structure

The Four Parts

Subject · Greeting · Body · Sign-off

Discover the Structure

Read and Notice

Subject: Updated farm checklist for review

"Hi Jean,"

"I attach the checklist. Please reply."

"Best regards, Carlos."

PartWhat it doesExample
Subjectwhat the email is aboutUpdated farm checklist for review
Greetinghow you startHi Jean, / Dear Jean,
Bodyyour messageI attach... please review...
Sign-offhow you endBest regards, Carlos.

Subject, greeting, body, sign-off. Hi = colega; Dear = mais formal (a sede na Suica). / Hi for a colleague, Dear is more formal.

Language Focus

Which Part?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Send me the checklist."

✓ RIGHT

"Hi Jean, could you send me the checklist?"

✗ WRONG

"Best regard, Carlos."

✓ RIGHT

"Best regards, Carlos."

Start with a greeting and be polite ("Could you...?"). The sign-off is "Best regards" -- with an s.

Dialogue

Emma Helps with the Email

E
Hi Carlos. You write to Jean in Rwanda today. What is your subject line?
C
My subject is: Updated farm checklist for review.
E
Good. Jean is a colleague, so start with "Hi Jean,". Now the body.
C
I attach the checklist and ask for feedback before the deadline.
E
Perfect. And how do you ask him to answer?
C
Could you confirm that you receive the file? Please reply to this email.
E
Excellent. Now the sign-off.
C
I look forward to your reply. Best regards, Carlos.
