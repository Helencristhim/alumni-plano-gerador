Verb patterns — 6 Minute Grammar (free)

Six minutes, two presenters, and a longer look at which verbs take -ing and which take to — the same two lists you learned tonight (like, enjoy, avoid vs. want, need, hope), with more verbs and more examples. Six minutes is nothing: you can do it while the coffee is on the stove.

Tip: keep a small notebook. Every time they say a verb, write it in one of two columns: the "-ing" list or the "to" list. By the end you will have your own little table, in your own hand — and that is the one you remember. One note: they are British; your material is American. The rule is identical; only the music of the voice changes.
