Chapter 3: The Code

-ing or to?

Two short lists of verbs, and one to learn by heart

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I love traveling by train."

"I want to see the small towns."

"I enjoy walking, but I avoid taking big tours."

"I have decided to rent a car, and I hope to see the coast."

Every sentence has two verbs. The second one sometimes wears -ing, and sometimes takes to. And the real question: what decides it — the second verb, or the first one?

The first verbmakes the second verb...Example
like, love, enjoy, prefer, avoid, don't mindwear -ingI enjoy walking. · I love traveling. · I avoid taking tours.
want, need, decide, plan, hope, would liketake to + verbI want to rent. · I hope to see. · I would like to go.
THE TRUTH
there is no deep reason		the first verb decides, always. It is two short lists to know by heart.Liking → -ing. Planning → to.

When you are not sure, ask: is this about what I like, or what I plan? Liking wears -ing. Planning takes to.

The Anatomy

Two Lists, One Special Case

The Contrast

The Heart vs the Plan

One list is what you love — and love wears -ing. The other is what you plan — and a plan takes to.

+ -ing — the heart

like, love, enjoy, prefer, avoid, don't mind

"I enjoy walking the small streets."
"I avoid taking big guided tours."

+ to — the plan

want, need, decide, plan, hope, would like

"I want to rent a car for a day."
"I would like to see the coast."

You do not translate between them — you feel them. Is it the heart, or the agenda? Love wears -ing. A plan takes to. That is the whole rule.

The Special Case

I Prefer A to B

When you choose one thing over another, the word is to, not "than". And with two actions, both wear -ing.

I prefer taking the train to flying.

two -ing verbs, one little "to" in the middle

"I prefer walking to driving."
"I prefer small hotels to big ones."

In Portuguese you say "do que", and it pulls you to "than". In English, for a choice, it is to. Say it three times, and it stops feeling strange.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I want going to the coast."
"I want to go to the coast." — after "want", always "to".
"I enjoy to walk in the old town."
"I enjoy walking in the old town." — after "enjoy", always "-ing".
"I prefer to travel by train than to fly."
"I prefer taking the train to flying." — both -ing, and "to", never "than".

One question, every time: after this first verb, is it the -ing list or the to list? Answer that, and this cannot beat you.

Expression of the Day

Let Me Explain

Let me explain.

= give me one second — I know what I like, and I am going to tell you

"Let me explain" is the door. It buys you the two seconds you need, and it tells the other person: something good is coming. Nobody ever says no to it.
It is the opposite of "just book me anything". It is the moment the trip stops being theirs and becomes yours.

Listening

The Agent's Secret

Two kinds of client, and the sentence he loves most. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What are the two kinds of client Miguel has, and what does the first kind say?
The first kind says "just book me something" -- a package, everything decided: flights, hotel, tours, meals, one price. The second kind sits down and says "let me explain," and tells him what they prefer: I like this, I avoid that, I want to rent a car.
2. What happens to many of the "book me anything" clients, and why?
Half of them come back a little sad. They spent two weeks on a bus with fifty strangers, doing things they do not enjoy. They saw the big landmarks and never saw a single small street -- because they never said what they wanted.
3. Miguel says the clients who get the trip they want are not the ones with the best English. So what do they have?
They know three little verbs -- I like, I prefer, I want -- and they are not afraid to use them. "Let me explain" is his favorite sentence in the world, because it is the moment the trip becomes theirs instead of his.
Listening 2

Three Verbs and a Little Courage

Teresa is seventy-four, and for most of her life other people planned her trips. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. For most of her life, who planned Teresa's trips, and what did she always say?
Her husband booked everything, and then her daughter. They chose the hotels, the tours and the restaurants, and she said yes to all of it. She went to beautiful places and did things she did not enjoy, because it was easier to say yes than to explain.
2. What changed everything for Teresa? Was it a course or a grammar book?
No -- three verbs. In a small office in Lisbon, an agent asked what she wanted, and instead of saying yes she said "let me explain": I love walking, I prefer small hotels to big ones, I avoid guided tours, I don't want an all-inclusive package. He built the trip she had wanted for forty years.
3. What does Teresa tell you that you need, especially if you always let somebody else decide?
You do not need perfect English. You need three verbs and a little courage -- I like, I prefer, I want. Learn to finish those three sentences, and no travel agent, in any country, will ever choose your life for you again.
Dialogue

The Agent and the List

M
Good morning! I am Miguel. Let me show you our most popular trip — here, in the brochure. It is an all-inclusive package: the hotel, the meals, a guided tour every morning. You pay a deposit today, and everything is decided.
R
Thank you, but — let me explain. I don't want an all-inclusive package. I enjoy choosing my own dinner, and I like planning my days myself.
M
Of course. So how do you like to travel between the cities? We can book flights for you.
R
No flights between cities. I prefer taking the train to flying — I love watching the countryside go by. But for the flight home, I want a nonstop one. I avoid long layovers.
M
Trains, and a nonstop flight home. Good. And the tours? We have a big guided tour every morning — very popular.
R
No, thank you. I avoid taking big guided tours. I enjoy walking the small streets with my husband. But I would like to rent a car for one day, for a day trip to the coast.
M
A rental car for one day, for the coast. Perfect. And your dates — do you have fixed dates?
R
My dates are flexible. I need to be able to change them. And can I pay only the deposit now, and decide the rest tomorrow?
M
Yes, only the deposit. You know, that is the clearest list I have had all month. Most people just say: book me anything.
R
For years my son booked everything, and I said yes to all of it. This time, I wanted to explain it myself.
Comprehension

About the Agent's Offer

1. What trip did Miguel offer first, and what was included in it?
The most popular one: an all-inclusive package. The hotel, the meals, and a big guided tour every morning — everything decided, with a deposit today.
2. How did Miguel say she could travel between the cities, and what did he offer for the tours?
He offered to book flights between the cities, and a big guided tour every morning — "very popular."
3. What did Miguel agree to let her pay today?
Only the deposit now, so she could decide the rest tomorrow. Her dates are flexible.
4. What does Miguel say most of his clients do, and why was her list different?
Most people just say "book me anything." Her list was the clearest he had had all month — and English is not even her first language. She explained exactly what she likes and what she wants.
