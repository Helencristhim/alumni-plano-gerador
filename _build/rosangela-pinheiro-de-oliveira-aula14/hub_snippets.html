Let Me Explain -- Planning the Trip Her Own Way

A travel agent, a shiny brochure, and a standard all-inclusive package -- and, for the first time, you say "let me explain" and plan your own trip. Key words: travel agent, a package, all-inclusive, a deposit, flexible, to rent, a day trip, a layover, a brochure, nonstop. Structure: gerund and infinitive. Some verbs take -ing -- like, love, enjoy, prefer, avoid ("I enjoy walking", "I love traveling by train"); some verbs take to -- want, need, decide, plan, hope, would like ("I want to rent a car", "I would like to see the coast"). And one to keep: I prefer taking the train to flying. Expression: Let me explain.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to every word and read the example. These are the ten words you need at a travel desk. Watch number ten: nonstop is the opposite of a layover (number eight) — a nonstop flight goes straight there, and a layover is the long wait in the middle when you change planes.

Travel agent -- a person whose job is to help you plan and book a trip
"The travel agent showed me a brochure with three different trips."
A package -- flights, hotel and tours sold together for one price
"I don't want a package -- I prefer choosing everything myself."
All-inclusive -- a price that already covers your room and all your meals
"The hotel is all-inclusive, so breakfast and dinner are included."
A deposit -- a small part of the price you pay first, to hold the booking
"Can I pay only the deposit now and the rest tomorrow?"
Flexible -- easy to change; not fixed to one date or one plan
"My dates are flexible -- I can leave on Monday or Tuesday."
To rent -- to pay to use something for a while and then give it back
"I would like to rent a car for one day, to see the coast."
A day trip -- a short trip that leaves and comes back on the same day
"We took a day trip to the little towns and came back for dinner."
A layover -- a stop between two flights, where you wait to change planes
"I avoid long layovers -- I don't enjoy waiting in airports."
A brochure -- a small book with photos and prices that shows you the trips
"The agent gave me a brochure to take home and read."
Nonstop -- going straight there, with no stop on the way
"I prefer a nonstop flight -- I don't want a layover."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Rosângela is sitting at a small travel agent's desk, planning the second week of her trip. Miguel, the agent, slides a brochure across the table. "This is the most popular one," he says. "An all-inclusive package. Everything is decided for you. You pay a deposit today, and a guided tour comes to the hotel every morning." Rosângela smiles and says, "Let me explain. I don't want an all-inclusive package. I enjoy choosing my own dinner."

"I prefer taking the train to flying," she goes on, one piece at a time. "I love watching the countryside go by. I avoid big guided tours; I like walking the small streets on my own. But I would like to rent a car for one day, for a day trip to the coast. My dates are flexible — I need to be able to change them. And for the flight home, I want a nonstop one. I don't enjoy waiting eight hours for a layover."

Miguel stops writing. "That is the clearest list I have had all month," he says. "Most people just say: book me anything." Four years ago, in Paris, her son did all of this for her, and she nodded along to a trip he chose. This morning, alone, Rosângela did not nod. She explained — a verb with -ing for what she loves, a verb with to for what she plans — and she built her own week, out loud, herself.

1. "I enjoy choosing my own dinner." Why "choosing" and not "to choose"?
A Because "enjoy" is a question word.
B Because after "enjoy" the next verb wears -ing. Like, love, enjoy, prefer and avoid all take -ing.
C Because "choose" has no infinitive form.
2. "I would like to rent a car." Why "to rent" here, and not "renting"?
A Because "would like" is a planning verb, and want, need, decide, plan, hope and would like all take "to".
B Because "rent" can never take -ing.
C Because it is a question.
3. "I prefer taking the train to flying." What is the shape of this sentence?
A prefer + to + verb, then "than".
B prefer + verb-ing + "to" + verb-ing. Both verbs wear -ing, and a little "to" sits in the middle.
C prefer + two infinitives with "than".
4. Miguel says most clients "just say: book me anything." Why is Rosângela's list different?
A She explains what she likes and what she wants, so the agent can build the trip she actually wants.
B She speaks perfect English and never makes a mistake.
C She lets Miguel and her son choose everything for her.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerund and Infinitive (-ing or to)

GRAMMAR

When you put two verbs together, the second one changes shape. Sometimes it wears -ing (walking, taking), and sometimes it takes to (to walk, to take). The first verb tells you which. There is no deep reason — it is two short lists to know by heart.

First verbSecond verbExample
like, love, enjoy, prefer, avoid, don't mindverb + -ingI enjoy walking. · I love traveling by train. · I avoid taking big tours.
want, need, decide, plan, hope, would likeverb + to + verbI want to rent a car. · I would like to see the coast. · I need to change my dates.
prefer A to B (a choice)both verbs + -ing, with "to" between themI prefer taking the train to flying. · I prefer walking to driving.
Careful: the common mistakenever mix them up"I want to go" (not "I want going"). · "I enjoy walking" (not "I enjoy to walk").

The one rule to remember: the liking verbs (like, love, enjoy, prefer, avoid) take -ing. The planning verbs (want, need, decide, plan, hope, would like) take to. When you are not sure, ask yourself: is this about what I like, or about what I plan? Liking → -ing. Planning → to.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Listen to the model, then record yourself. Take your time: listen twice before you speak. Two things to watch: "prefer" has the stress on the second part, pre-FER; and "to" is small and weak in real speech — "want to" often sounds like "wanna", so let it stay short and quick.

I enjoy walking the small streets on my own.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the prompt. There is no right or wrong version. Speak for 60 seconds, with no script.

Imagine you are sitting with a travel agent, planning your next trip. The agent wants to sell you the standard all-inclusive package. Say "Let me explain," and tell the agent what YOU like: how you prefer to travel between cities (train? nonstop flight?), what you enjoy doing (walking? small towns? the coast?), what you avoid (big guided tours? crowds?), and one thing you would like to do (rent a car for a day trip?). Use at least three -ing verbs (enjoy, love, prefer, avoid) and three "to" verbs (want, need, would like, plan), and three of today's words. Four years ago your son planned the trip and you said yes. This time, explain it yourself. Take your time and do not read from a script.
