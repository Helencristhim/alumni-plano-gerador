Chapter 3

The First Conditional

If you..., we will... -- the language of the deal

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If you lower the price, we will sign today."

"We will agree if you include the training."

"If we deliver on time, they will open their network."

"If you need more time, we will start with a pilot."

PartFormExample
if-clause (condition)if + present simpleIf you lower the price...
main clause (result)will + base verb...we will sign today.
Order is flexibleWe will agree if you include training. (no comma when "if" is in the middle)
Golden ruleNEVER "will" after "if". Use the present after "if".
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"If you will lower the price, we will sign."

✓ RIGHT

"If you lower the price, we will sign." (present after if)

✗ WRONG

"If you include training, we agree."

✓ RIGHT

"If you include training, we will agree." (will in the result)

✗ WRONG

"We will meet in the middle if you will wait."

✓ RIGHT

"We will meet in the middle if you wait." (present after if)

First conditional: if + present, will + base verb. NEVER put "will" after "if". The if-clause is the condition; the will-clause is the result.

Chapter 4

The Negotiation

Reaching a deal with a counterpart

Dialogue

Closing the Partnership

D
Marcos, we love your firm. Here is our offer: if you join our network this year, we will share our cross-border deals.
M
That sounds promising. But the terms matter. What if we also include a joint training program for both teams?
D
I like that idea. If your team completes the training, we will give you our valuation models too.
M
That works for us. I am afraid the first-year fee is too high, though. Can we meet in the middle?
D
We can make a concession. If you sign this week, we will cut the fee in half for the first year.
M
That is a fair compromise. If you put it in writing, I will take it to my board today.
D
Wonderful. So, do we have a deal?
M
We have a deal. This is a strong commitment, and it gives both firms real leverage. Welcome to the partnership.
