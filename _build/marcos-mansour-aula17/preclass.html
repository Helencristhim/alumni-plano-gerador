Aula 17 -- Pre-class

Negotiating & Building Partnerships

Negotiating a partnership: making proposals, bargaining terms and reaching agreement. Key words: negotiate, agreement, deal, terms, offer, compromise, concession, bargain, commitment, leverage. Grammar: first conditional for negotiation -- if + present, will + verb ("If you lower the price, we will sign today") + agreement language.

Vocabulary

Negotiate -- negociar (to discuss terms to reach an agreement)

Agreement -- acordo (an arrangement two sides accept)
"After two hours, we reached an agreement."
Deal -- negócio/acordo (a business arrangement both sides accept)
"I think we have a fair deal for both sides."
Terms -- termos/condições (the conditions of an agreement)
"We are flexible on the terms of the deal."
Offer -- oferta (something you are willing to give or do)
"Here is our offer: full access to the network."
Compromise -- meio-termo (an agreement where both sides give a little)
"We found a good compromise on the price."
Concession -- concessão (something you give up so the other side agrees)
"Both sides made a few concessions today."
Bargain -- barganhar/pechinchar (to discuss a price to get a better deal)
"They bargained over the fee for an hour."
Commitment -- compromisso (a serious promise to do something)
"We are ready to make a strong commitment."
Leverage -- vantagem/poder de barganha (an advantage that gives more power)
"This partnership gives us real leverage in the market."

Stage 17.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Marcos sits down to negotiate a partnership with a firm from Canada. The counterpart makes an offer: "If you join our network this year, we will share our cross-border deals." Notice the pattern: after if we use the present (you join), and in the result we use will + verb (we will share). Marcos replies with his own condition: "If your team includes a training program, we will sign." He bargains on the terms: "I am afraid the fee is too high. Can we meet in the middle?" The other side makes a concession: "If you sign this week, we will cut the fee in half." They reach a good compromise and shake hands: "We have a deal." Remember the golden rule: NEVER use will after if -- the if-clause uses the present.

1. Depois de "if", qual forma do verbo usamos?
A will + verbo (If you will join).
B presente simples (If you join).
C going to (If you are going to join).
2. No resultado (a outra parte da frase), o que usamos?
A will + verbo base (we will share).
B presente simples (we share).
C if + presente de novo.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "If you will lower the price, we will sign."
B "If you lower the price, we will sign."
C "If you lower the price, we sign."

Stage 17.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Como negociar com condições (explicação em inglês e português).

Part / ParteForm / FormaExample
if-clause (condição)if + presente simplesIf you lower the price...
resultado (result)will + verbo base...we will sign today.
Ordem flexívelWe will agree if you include training. (sem vírgula quando "if" vem no meio)
Regra de ouroNUNCA use "will" depois de "if". Acordo: What if...? / We could offer... / That works for us / I'm afraid that won't work / Can we meet in the middle? / We have a deal.
