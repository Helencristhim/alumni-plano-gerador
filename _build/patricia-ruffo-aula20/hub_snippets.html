Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Symposium -- a formal meeting where experts present and discuss a specific topic
"I am giving a talk at the international symposium." (Simpósio)
Panel -- a group of experts who discuss a topic and answer questions publicly
"After my talk, the panel will ask some tough questions." (Painel / mesa de especialistas)
Present -- to show and explain your work clearly to an audience
"I will present my study, then take questions from the panel." (Apresentar)
Defend -- to support your data or position with strong reasons and evidence
"I can defend my data with the robust results from the trial." (Defender)
Synthesize -- to bring many ideas or findings together into one clear summary
"In the closing session, each speaker will synthesize the main findings." (Sintetizar)
Integrate -- to combine separate ideas or studies into one connected whole
"The final talk integrates the different studies into one message." (Integrar)
Advocate -- to speak publicly in support of an idea, policy or position
"As a researcher, I advocate for clear, honest science." (Defender / advogar por)
Closing remarks -- the final words of a talk that sum up the message and leave an impression
"In my closing remarks, I thanked the panel and gave one clear takeaway." (Considerações finais)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Today is the big day. Patricia will present her study on glycemic control at the Abbott International Symposium. First, she opens with the objective; next, she explains the methods; finally, she shows the key results. After her talk, she joins the expert panel. When a colleague challenges her sample, she stays calm: she acknowledges the point, then she will defend her data with the robust results, as a result, the findings are convincing. In the closing session, she has to synthesize the main ideas of the whole symposium and integrate the different studies into one clear message. In her closing remarks, she thanks the audience and continues to advocate for clear, honest science.

A Objective, then methods, then results.

C She has no structure at all.
2. How does Patricia react when the panel challenges her sample?
A She gets angry and stops talking.
B She acknowledges the point, then defends the data with robust results.
C She agrees her study is wrong.
3. What does Patricia do in the closing session?
A She leaves before it starts.
C She reads only her own results again.
B She synthesizes and integrates the studies into one message.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Linking the whole talk

GRAMMAR

Um bom talk conecta as partes com linking language: signposting, causa/efeito, síntese e fechamento. / A strong talk connects its parts with linking language: signposting, cause/effect, synthesis and closing.

Job / FunçãoLinking languageExample
SignpostFirst / Next / Finally"First, I will present the objective."
Cause/effectas a result / therefore"The sample was large, as a result, the data is robust."
SynthesizeTo synthesize / To sum up"To synthesize, the studies integrate into one message."
CloseIn my closing remarks"In my closing remarks, thank you all."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Para o capstone, use linking language para integrate tudo: signpost as partes (first / next / finally), conecte causa e efeito (as a result / therefore), synthesize (to synthesize / to sum up) e feche com closing remarks. Assim você junta toda a sua jornada num talk fluido. / Use linking language to integrate everything into one smooth talk.
