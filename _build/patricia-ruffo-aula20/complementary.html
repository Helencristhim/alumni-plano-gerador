Lesson 20 -- Capstone: Abbott International Symposium

Series
The Newsroom -- any panel or live-defense scene

Watch how a speaker presents a position, defends it under tough questions and synthesizes the argument into one clear message. Notice the signposting and the calm, confident tone.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one phrase used to defend a claim and one used to sum up an argument.

Podcast
The Tim Ferriss Show -- any episode on public speaking & defending ideas

An interview on how experts present clearly, handle hard questions and advocate for their ideas in public. Useful models for the panel and your closing remarks.

Record yourself synthesizing the main idea of the episode in three sentences, then add one closing remark.

YouTube
TED -- "How to give a killer presentation" / a strong scientific TED talk

A model of a complete talk: a clear opening, signposted parts, strong evidence and memorable closing remarks. Watch how the speaker integrates everything into one message.

After watching, plan your own 5-minute symposium talk: opening, key result, one panel answer and your closing remarks.
