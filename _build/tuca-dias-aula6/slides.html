Chapter 3 -- The Experience Question

Grammar Discovery

Present perfect: ever, for, and since

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we ask about experiences? When do we use "for" and "since"?

"Have you ever been to this cafe before?"

"Have you tried the local coffee?"

"I have lived here for two years."

"I have been here since Monday."

Grammar Rule

Present Perfect for Experiences

ToolHow it works
Present perfecthave / has + past participle (been, tried, lived) -- links the past to now
Have you ever...?Asks about an experience at any time in your life
for + a periodHow long it has lasted: for two years, for an hour
since + a start pointWhen it began: since Monday, since 2020

Key idea: to make small talk, ask "Have you ever...?" about experiences. To say how long, ask yourself: is it a period of time or a starting point? A period uses "for"; a starting point uses "since".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am here since two years. Did you ever been to Mexico?"
"I have been here for two years. Have you ever been to Mexico?"

Use the present perfect (I have been), never the simple present, with for and since. And ask Have you ever been...?, never "Did you ever been...?"
