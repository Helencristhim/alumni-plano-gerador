Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Small talk -- light, friendly conversation about everyday topics
"We made small talk about the coffee while we waited." (Conversa casual)
Icebreaker -- something you say to start a conversation comfortably
"A question about the weather is a great icebreaker." (Quebra-gelo)
Acquaintance -- a person you know a little, but not well
"He is an acquaintance from my coffee class." (Conhecido)
Outgoing -- friendly and confident about meeting new people
"She is very outgoing and talks to everyone." (Extrovertido)
Awkward -- uncomfortable, when you are not sure what to say
"There was an awkward silence after my question." (Constrangedor)
Chat -- a relaxed, informal conversation
"We had a nice chat over coffee." (Bate-papo)
Get along -- to have a friendly relationship with someone
"I get along with almost everyone at the cafe." (Se dar bem)
In common -- shared between people, like interests or experiences
"We have a love of travel in common." (Em comum)
Conversation starter -- a topic or question that begins a conversation
"Travel is always a good conversation starter." (Assunto inicial)
Polite -- showing good manners and respect to other people
"It is polite to ask about the other person too." (Educado)
Topic -- the subject you are talking about
"The weather is a safe topic with strangers." (Tema)
Bring up -- to start talking about a subject
"Do not bring up money with new people." (Mencionar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca walked into a busy cafe for a language exchange. To break the ice, she made some small talk about the coffee. "Have you ever been to this cafe before?" she asked a friendly stranger. "Yes, I have. I have come here every week for two years," he answered. Tuca noticed they had a love of travel in common, so she asked a follow-up question: "How long have you studied English?" They got along very well. Because the present perfect connects the past to now, it is the perfect tool for small talk: you ask "Have you ever...?" for experiences, and you use for and since to say how long something has continued.

1. Which question asks about a life experience?
A "Have you ever been to this cafe?"
B "Are you at the cafe now?"
C "Will you go to the cafe?"
2. Why does the man say "for two years"?
A because "for" is used with a point in time
B because "for" shows a period of time
C because the action is finished and over
3. What is the present perfect good for in small talk?
A asking about experiences with "Have you ever...?"
B giving orders and commands
C describing the weather right now

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect (ever / for / since)

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de experiencias e iniciar conversas. / Used to talk about experiences and start conversations.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Present perfect (+)have/has + particio passado / links past to nowI have visited many cafes.
Have you ever...? (?)Pergunta sobre experiencia / asks about a life experienceHave you ever been to Mexico?
for (+ periodo)Duracao / a period of timeI have lived here for two years.
since (+ ponto)Ponto de inicio / a starting point in timeI have lived here since 2020.

Key point / Ponto-chave: Use "Have you ever...?" para perguntar sobre experiencias. Use for + periodo (two years) e since + ponto de inicio (Monday). Nunca use o presente simples ("I am here since...") -- use o present perfect.
