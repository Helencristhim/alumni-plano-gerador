Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Third conditional: a different past and its result

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"If you had ordered earlier, we would have offered a bigger discount."

"If we had answered faster, we would have closed the Orion deal."

"If I had known your bottom line, I would have proposed a trade-off."

"If they had pushed harder, I would have walked away."

Grammar Rule

The Third Conditional

FormIf + past perfect, would have + past participle
ConditionIf you had ordered earlier,
Resultwe would have offered a bigger discount.
NegativeIf we had not waited, we would not have lost the deal.
QuestionWould you have signed if we had moved faster?

Key idea: use the third conditional for a past condition that did not happen and its imagined result. Form: If + had + past participle, would have + past participle. Both parts are about the past. Short forms: I'd ordered = I had ordered; we'd have offered = we would have offered.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If I would have prepared, we would closed the deal."
"If I had prepared, we would have closed the deal."

In the if part use had + past participle (never would have). In the result use would have + past participle (closed, offered), never the base verb alone.
