Chapter 3

The Present Continuous

Actions in progress at the moment of speaking

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Sofia is facilitating the session."

"Twenty attendees are joining online."

"Nobody is screen-sharing yet."

"I know the answer." (state, not -ing)

FormUseExample
am / is / are + verb-ingAction in progress nowShe is facilitating the session.
negative: am/is/are + not + verb-ingNegativeNobody is sharing a screen yet.
question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ingQuestionIs she facilitating now?
stative verbspresent simple (no -ing)I know the answer.

Use am for I, is for he/she/it, are for you/we/they.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She presenting the report now."

✓ RIGHT

"She is presenting the report now."

✗ WRONG

"The attendees is joining online."

✓ RIGHT

"The attendees are joining online."

✗ WRONG

"I am knowing the answer."

✓ RIGHT

"I know the answer."

Always use am/is/are + verb-ing for actions now. State verbs like know, want, need, and like stay in the present simple.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can describe an action in progress now with am/is/are + verb-ing: she is presenting.
I can use am/is/are correctly (am for I, is for he/she/it, are for you/we/they).
I can make negatives and questions: nobody is sharing; is she facilitating?
I can keep state verbs in the present simple: I know, I want, I need (not am knowing).
I can use the new words: facilitate, attendee, presenter, poll, recap, breakout, host, chime in, walk through, multitask, sidebar, screen-share.
