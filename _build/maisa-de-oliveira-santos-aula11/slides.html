Chapter 3: The Code

There is, There are

one thing or many things

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"There is one chart on the page."

"There are three columns here."

"Is there a total at the bottom?"

"How many rows are there?"

StructureUse forExample
There is + onesingular (1)There is one chart.
There are + manyplural (2+)There are three columns.
Is there...?question (1)Is there a total?
How many...are there?question (2+)How many rows are there?

There is = one thing. There are = two or more things. To ask: Is there...? or How many...are there?. Negative: There is not a chart. / There are not any figures.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

There is three columns.
There are three columns.
There are one total.
There is one total.

Use There is for one thing (one total, one chart) and There are for two or more (three columns, five rows). Match the verb to the number after it.

Dialogue

Here Is My Report

M
David, here is my report. There is one main chart on the first page.
D
Great, Maísa. What does the chart show?
M
There are three columns here: January, February, and March.
D
I see. And how many rows are there?
M
There are five rows, one for each client. Is there a total at the bottom?
D
Yes, there is. The total is right here.
M
Perfect. There is one figure I do not understand on page two.
D
No problem. Let me check the data with you.
