The Report

Apresentar um relatório simples no trabalho, descrevendo o que há nele: chart, table, column, row, total, figure, data, page. Gramática: There is / There are (There is one chart. / There are three columns. / Is there a total? / How many rows are there?). Missão: apresentar um relatório ao David, do começo ao fim.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

A chart -- a picture that shows numbers with bars or lines
"There is one chart on the first page." (Um gráfico)
A table -- rows and columns with information
"There is a table with all the data." (Uma tabela)
A column -- numbers that go up and down (vertical)
"There are three columns in the chart." (Uma coluna)
A row -- numbers that go from left to right (horizontal)
"There are five rows, one for each client." (Uma linha)
A total -- all the numbers added together
"Is there a total at the bottom?" (Um total)
A figure -- a number in a report
"There is one figure I do not understand." (Um número / cifra)
Data -- information: numbers and facts
"All the data is correct." (Dados)
A page -- one side of a report or document
"There are two pages in this report." (Uma página)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Maísa presents her report to David. "Here is my report," she says. "There is one main chart on the first page. There are three columns: January, February, and March." David asks: "How many rows are there?" Maísa answers: "There are five rows, one for each client." Then she asks: "Is there a total at the bottom?" David says: "Yes, there is." Maísa is happy, but she has one question. "There is one figure I do not understand on page two." David checks the data with her, and now everything makes sense.

A "There is one chart."
B "There are one chart."
C "There is three charts."
2. Como a Maísa fala das três colunas? / How does Maísa talk about the three columns?
A "There is three columns."
B "There are three columns."
C "There are a three columns."
3. Como a Maísa pergunta sobre o total? / How does Maísa ask about the total?
A "There is a total?"
B "Is there a total at the bottom?"
C "Are there a total?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There is / There are

GRAMMAR

Para dizer o que existe, usamos There is para UMA coisa (singular) e There are para DUAS ou mais (plural). / To say what exists, use There is for one thing and There are for two or more.

Estrutura / StructureUso / UseExemplo / Example
There is + onesingular (1)There is one chart.
There are + manyplural (2+)There are three columns.
Is there...?pergunta 1 / question (1)Is there a total?
How many...are there?pergunta 2+ / question (2+)How many rows are there?

Atenção: o verbo combina com o número DEPOIS dele. Afirmativo: There is one total. / There are five rows. Negativo: There is not a chart. / There are not any figures. Interrogativo: Is there a total? / How many pages are there? Nunca diga "There is three columns" nem "There are one total".

Reflection
