Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Itinerary -- a detailed plan or route for a trip
"My itinerary has one city for each day." (Roteiro)
Neighborhood -- a district or small area of a city
"I booked a hotel in a quiet neighborhood." (Bairro)
Sightseeing -- visiting interesting places as a tourist
"We spent the morning sightseeing downtown." (Passeio turístico)
Bustling -- full of energy, noise, and busy activity
"The market was bustling with people." (Movimentado)
Mural -- a large painting made on a wall
"The street murals tell the city's history." (Mural)
Vendor -- a person who sells things, often on the street
"A street vendor sold us fresh fruit." (Vendedor ambulante)
Ancient -- very old, from a distant past
"The ancient temple is over a thousand years old." (Antigo)
Ruins -- the remains of old buildings from the past
"We climbed the pyramids at the ruins." (Ruínas)
Crowded -- full of people; with little free space
"The historic center gets crowded on weekends." (Lotado)
Affordable -- not expensive; reasonable in price
"Street food in Mexico City is very affordable." (Acessível)
Stroll -- a slow, relaxed walk
"We took a stroll through the park." (Caminhada)
Highlight -- the best or most memorable part of something
"The pyramids were the highlight of the trip." (Destaque)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca has always dreamed of visiting Mexico City. She has never traveled to Mexico before, so she planned her itinerary with care. Last week she booked a hotel in a quiet neighborhood and made a day-by-day plan for sightseeing. Her friend Daniel has been to the city three times. Last year he stayed in a bustling area and visited the ancient ruins. He told Tuca: "I have already seen the murals, and climbing the pyramids was the highlight of my trip." Remember: use the present perfect for experiences with no exact time ("I have visited the museum"), and the past simple for finished actions with a clear time ("I visited the museum last year").

1. Which sentence describes an experience with no exact time?
A "I have visited the ruins."
B "I visited the ruins last year."
C "I will visit the ruins tomorrow."
2. When do we use the past simple?
A for experiences with no exact time
B for finished actions with a clear past time
C for plans in the future
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I have seen the murals yesterday."
B "I saw the murals yesterday."
C "I have saw the murals yesterday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de viagens e experiências. / Used to talk about trips and experiences.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
Present Perfecthave / has + particípio: experiência, SEM tempo exato / experience, no exact time"I have visited the ruins."
Past Simpleverbo no passado: ação terminada COM tempo claro / finished action, clear time"I visited the ruins last year."
Sinais do present perfectpalavras que pedem present perfect / time wordsever, never, already, yet, just
Sinais do past simplepalavras com tempo definido / finished-time wordsyesterday, last year, in 2019, ago

Key point / Ponto-chave: Se a frase tem um tempo exato (yesterday, last year), use o past simple. Se é uma experiência sem tempo definido, use o present perfect. Nunca junte os dois: "I have visited it last year" está errado.
