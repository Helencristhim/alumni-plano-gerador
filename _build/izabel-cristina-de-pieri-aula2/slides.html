Chapter 4: The Code

Routine & How Often

Present simple · Frequency adverbs

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"She usually opens with the cash flow."

"The partners frequently ask difficult questions."

"She is always ready before the call."

"Izabel rarely reads from a script."

Where does the frequency adverb sit — before or after the verb?

PositionRuleExample
Before the main verbalways, usually, often, frequently, sometimes, rarely, neverShe usually opens the call.
After the verb "be"the adverb comes AFTER am / is / areShe is always ready.
Negatives & questionsThey don't often meet on Fridays. How often do you run the call?
Language Focus

Where Does It Go?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I open always with the cash flow."

✓ RIGHT

"I always open with the cash flow."

✗ WRONG

"My team doesn't always ready."

✓ RIGHT

"My team is not always ready."

✗ WRONG

"How often you run the call?"

✓ RIGHT

"How often do you run the call?"

Frequency adverbs go before the main verb but after "be". Questions and negatives use do / does + the base verb.

Dialogue

Opening the Thursday Call

A
Morning, Izabel. We are all here. Whenever you're ready, go ahead and open the call.
I
Good morning, everyone. As I usually do, I'll start with the cash flow and then move to operations.
A
Sounds good. Before you do, could you remind the new partners what you oversee here?
I
Of course. I run operations at AVRT and I lead a small team. I track the key figures every week and liaise with our suppliers.
A
Great. One of the new partners is asking about the ERP timeline. Do you have a date?
I
That's a good question. I don't have the exact date in front of me, so let me check that and get back to you today.
A
Perfect. That's exactly the right answer. Please go ahead with the cash flow.
I
Thank you, Andy. So, to bring everyone up to speed, here is where we stand this month.
