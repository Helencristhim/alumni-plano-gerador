Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Oversee -- to watch over work and make sure it is done well (supervisionar)
"I oversee the whole business at AVRT."
Liaise with -- to stay in contact and work closely with someone (articular-se com)
"I liaise with our suppliers every week."
Ensure -- to make certain that something happens
"I ensure that every figure is correct before the board sees it."
Track -- to follow the progress or status of something (acompanhar)
"I track the key figures during the week."
Handle -- to deal with a task, a client, or a problem
"My team handles the invoices and daily tasks."
Run operations -- to be in charge of running the daily business
"I run operations across the whole company."
Lead a team -- to guide and take responsibility for a group of people
"I lead a small team here in Valinhos."
Workflow -- the sequence of steps to complete work (fluxo de trabalho)
"Our approval workflow is clear and fast."
Streamline -- to make a process simpler and more efficient
"We streamlined the order process this year."
Milestone -- an important point or goal in a project (marco)
"Closing the tax review was a big milestone."
Supplier -- a company that provides goods to your business (fornecedor)
"Our main supplier ships the components in two weeks."
Deadline -- the time by which something must be finished (prazo)
"The reporting deadline is Wednesday evening."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Every Thursday, Izabel runs the weekly call. She oversees the whole business and leads a small team. She usually opens with the cash flow, and then she moves to operations. During the week she always tracks the key figures and liaises with the suppliers, so she is always ready before the meeting. Her team handles the invoices, and she often streamlines a process when she sees a problem. The partners frequently ask difficult questions, and she does not always have every answer. When that happens, she rarely freezes. She simply says, "Let me check that and get back to you."

1. Why do we write "she runs", "she oversees", and "she tracks" with an -s?
A Because the actions are finished in the past.
B Because in the present simple, he/she/it adds -s to the verb.
C Because "Izabel" is plural.
2. Look at "she usually opens" and "she is always ready". Where does the frequency adverb go?
A Before the main verb, but after the verb "be".
B Always at the very end of the sentence.
C Always at the very beginning of the sentence.
3. What does Izabel say when she does not know an answer?
A She stops talking and switches to Portuguese.
B "Let me check that and get back to you."
C "I don't know, next question please."
