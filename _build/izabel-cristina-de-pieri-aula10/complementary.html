Aula 10 -- The Meeting, Relayed -- Reporting What Was Said

Film
Spotlight (2015)

A gripping true story about Boston Globe journalists who relay and report what sources said. The whole film is reported speech in action: "She told us that...", "He said the records had been sealed", "They asked whether we could prove it." A masterclass in how professionals report what people said, accurately and on the record.

Tip: pick one interview scene. Write down five things a source says in direct speech, then rewrite each as reported speech with correct backshift (said that... had..., told... would...).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast (Harvard Business Review)

A weekly podcast where hosts and guests relay meetings, decisions, and research from the business world. Listen for the natural reporting language executives use: "The CEO explained that...", "She told the board that revenue had grown", "They asked whether the plan would work." Exactly the register you need to relay your own board meetings.

Tip: pick one episode about leadership or strategy. Note five sentences where the host reports what a guest said, and mark the reporting verb (said, told, explained, admitted) and the backshift.

YouTube
"Can you use REPORTED SPEECH? Grammar Lesson + Examples" -- mmmEnglish

A clear, practical lesson on reported speech: the backshift (present -> past, will -> would, can -> could), say vs tell, and how to report questions and commands. Exactly the code you practiced in Lesson 10 -- and why "she said me" should be "she told me", and "asked can we" should be "asked whether we could".

Tip: pause after each example and relay it about your own meeting. Then record a 90-second recap of a real meeting using said / told / explained, one reported question, and one reported command.
