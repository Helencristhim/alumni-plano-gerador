Chapter 4: The Code

Reported Speech

backshift · say vs tell · reported questions and commands

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The CFO said that revenue had grown."

"Andy explained that they would sign."

"She asked whether we could deliver on time."

"The chair said, 'The budget is approved.'"

Three sentences share a structure. One is different. Which one, and why?

OriginalReported (backshift)Example
present -> pastis/are -> was/were; has grown -> had grownShe said the margin had improved.
will / can -> would / couldthe modal shifts back one stepHe said they would sign.
say vs telltell + someone; say (to) with no personShe told me; he said that...
reported question / commandask if/whether (no inversion); told + sb + toShe asked whether we could deliver.
Language Focus

Which Form?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"She said me that the margin improved."

✓ RIGHT

"She told me that the margin had improved."

✗ WRONG

"The client asked can we deliver on time."

✓ RIGHT

"The client asked whether we could deliver on time."

✗ WRONG

"He said the plan is ready and we will start."

✓ RIGHT

"He said the plan was ready and we would start."

Use "tell + someone" but "say" with no person. In reported questions, use "if / whether" with normal word order. And shift the verb back one tense: is -> was, will -> would, can -> could.

Dialogue

Relaying the Board Meeting to Andy

A
Izabel, I missed the board meeting. Can you relay what was said?
I
Of course. The chair said that the year had been strong, and he explained that the sensor business had grown faster than the board expected. I circulated the minutes this morning.
A
Good. And what did the CFO report about the numbers?
I
She briefed the board and gave a short recap. She reported that the margin had improved, but she warned that overhead would rise if we opened a second plant too soon.
A
And the client on the call — what did they ask?
I
They asked whether we could shorten the lead time, and whether we would commit to a firm date. The board agreed to clarify both points before signing.
A
So what did the board finally decide?
I
They reached a consensus: they would endorse the launch, but defer the new plant for six months. I told the operations lead to review the schedule, and the key takeaway was to confirm the numbers first.
