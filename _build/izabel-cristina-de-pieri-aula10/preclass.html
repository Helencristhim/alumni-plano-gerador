Aula 10 -- Pre-class

The Meeting, Relayed -- Reporting What Was Said

Reading the minutes of a board meeting and relaying what was said. Key words: agenda, minutes, recap, brief, clarify, endorse, defer, action item, takeaway, circulate, debrief, consensus. Structure: reported speech -- backshift (present -> past, will -> would, can -> could), say vs tell, reported questions with "if / whether", and reported commands with "told + someone + to".

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Agenda -- the list of topics a meeting will discuss (pauta / agenda da reunião)
"The client was next on the agenda."
Minutes -- the official written record of a meeting (ata da reunião)
"Izabel circulated the minutes after the meeting."
Recap -- a short summary of the main points (resumo / retomada)
"Grace gave a quick recap of Thursday's meeting."
Brief -- to give someone the key information they need (informar / dar um briefing)
"The CFO briefed the board on the numbers."
Clarify -- to make something clearer or remove confusion (esclarecer)
"The board agreed to clarify the delivery date."
Endorse -- to give official or public support to something (endossar / apoiar oficialmente)
"The board decided to endorse the launch."
Defer -- to postpone a decision to a later time (adiar / postergar)
"The board chose to defer the second plant."
Action item -- a task assigned to someone after a meeting (tarefa / ação definida na reunião)
"Izabel assigned two action items."
Takeaway -- the most important point to remember (principal lição / ponto-chave)
"The key takeaway was to confirm the numbers first."
Circulate -- to send a document around to a group of people (distribuir / fazer circular)
"She asked finance to circulate an updated forecast."
Debrief -- to review and discuss an event after it happens (fazer um balanço / relato p&243;s-evento)
"Oscar called with a quick debrief after the client call."
Consensus -- general agreement among a group (consenso)
"The board reached a consensus on the launch."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After Thursday's board meeting, Izabel relays it to a partner who was away. The chair said that the year had been strong, and he explained that the sensor business had grown faster than expected. The CFO reported that the margin had improved, but she warned that overhead would rise with a second plant. A client asked whether AVRT could shorten its lead time. In the end, Izabel told the operations lead to review the schedule, and the board reached a consensus to endorse the launch.

1. Why is it "the year had been strong" and not "the year has been strong"?
A Because "year" is always in the past.
B In reported speech the verb shifts back one tense (backshift): has been -> had been.
C Because the chair spoke very slowly.
2. The client "asked whether AVRT could shorten its lead time." Why "could", not "can"?
A In reported questions, "can" shifts back to "could".
B "Can" is never used in business English.
C Because the client was unsure.
3. Why "told the operations lead to review" and not "said the operations lead to review"?
A "Said" and "told" are always interchangeable.
B "Tell" takes a person (told + someone); "say" does not. Reported commands use "told + someone + to + base".
C Because "review" starts with an R.

Quiz
