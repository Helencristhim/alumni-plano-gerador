Stage 1.1: Data Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Twelve words to describe charts, figures and movement. Listen to each one.

Chart -- a picture that shows numbers as bars, lines or slices
"This chart shows our revenue by quarter." (Gráfico)
Trend -- the general direction numbers move in over time
"The trend is clearly upward this year." (Tendência)
Figure -- a single, important business number
"Sixty-five million is our key figure this quarter." (Número / cifra)
Percentage -- a part of a whole, written out of one hundred
"Revenue grew by a high percentage." (Porcentagem)
Peak -- the highest point a number reaches
"Sales reached their peak in December." (Pico)
Breakdown -- a split of a total into its separate parts
"Here is the breakdown of revenue by region." (Detalhamento)
Rise -- to go up; an increase
"Revenue continued to rise all year." (Subir)
Fall -- to go down; a drop
"Costs began to fall after the new deal." (Cair)
Double -- to become two times as big
"Our user base doubled in twelve months." (Dobrar)
Halve -- to cut a number down to half its size
"We managed to halve our cost per lead." (Reduzir à metade)
Sharp -- fast and large; a quick, big change
"There was a sharp rise in the first quarter." (Acentuado / forte)
Steady -- slow, even and without big changes
"Growth was steady through the second quarter." (Estável / constante)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduardo opens his data slide for the board. "Let me walk you through this chart," he says. "Revenue rose sharply in the first quarter and reached its peak in December. Over the year, the trend was clearly upward — our growth was much faster than the market." He highlights one figure: "Sixty-five million is higher than any year before, and our cost per lead halved." He closes simply: "In short, the numbers tell a strong story."

1. How did revenue move in the first quarter?
A It rose sharply and reached its peak in December.
B It fell steadily all year.
C It stayed exactly the same.
2. How does Eduardo compare his growth with the market?
A Slower than the market.
B Much faster than the market.
C The same as the market.
3. What single figure does he highlight?
A The cost of the office.
B Sixty-five million in revenue.
C The number of slides.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Comparativos dão sentido aos números. Adjetivo curto: + -er (higher, faster). Adjetivo longo: more + adjetivo (more efficient). Igualdade: as + adjetivo + as. Reforce com much ou far. / Comparatives give numbers meaning. Short adjective: add -er; long adjective: more + adjective; equality: as ... as; emphasis: much / far.

TypeFormExample
Short adjectiveadj + -er + than"Revenue is higher than last year."
Long adjectivemore + adj + than"Costs are more efficient than before."
Equalas + adj + as"Churn is as low as the best."
Emphasismuch / far + comparative"Much higher than the market."

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca diga more higher — "higher" já é comparativo. Para tornar mais forte, use much ou far antes do comparativo. Compare cada número para que ele conte uma história.

Order
