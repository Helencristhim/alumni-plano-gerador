Vocabulary

Finalize -- to finish and complete something officially

Agreement -- a deal that both sides accept and agree to
"We have reached an agreement on delivery." (Acordo)
Commitment -- a firm promise to do or deliver something
"Thank you for your commitment to this order." (Compromisso)
Proceed -- to move forward to the next step
"We will proceed to signature on Friday." (Prosseguir / seguir adiante)
Attach -- to add a file or document to an email
"I have attached the draft contract for you." (Anexar)
Draft -- a first version of a document, ready to review
"They will review the draft this week." (Rascunho / minuta)
Sign-off -- a final approval, or the closing line of a message
"The client has given sign-off on the proposal." (Aprovação final)
Deliverable -- a product or result you promise to hand over
"The first deliverable is the signed contract." (Entregável)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The client has said yes, so Roberto closes the deal with a short follow-up email. He recaps the terms using the present perfect: "We have agreed on a five percent discount, and we have reached an agreement on delivery." Then he lists what is done: "I have attached the draft contract, and the client has confirmed the order." Finally, he states the next step: the team will proceed to sign-off on Friday, and then they can finalize the contract. Notice: to recap a finished action that matters now, English uses have / has + past participle, not the past simple alone.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Use o present perfect para recapitular uma ação terminada cujo resultado importa agora: have / has + particípio. / Use the present perfect to recap a finished action with a result now: have / has + past participle.

Sujeito + have / hasparticípio / past participle
We haveagreed on the price.
We havereached an agreement.
I haveattached the contract.
The client hasconfirmed the order.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o present perfect liga uma ação terminada ao resultado agora. Use have com I / you / we / they e has com he / she / it. Depois de have / has, use o particípio passado (agreed, attached). NUNCA "have agree" nem "has attach". É perfeito para recapitular um acordo, porque a decisão está feita e continua valendo.
