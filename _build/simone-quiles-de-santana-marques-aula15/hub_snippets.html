Reading Contracts IV -- Drafting & the Passive Voice

Learn the language lawyers use when they draft and read a contract, and the passive voice of legal English: the agreement is signed, the goods are delivered, the schedules are incorporated. New vocabulary: execute, incorporate, stipulate, deem, render, supersede, annex, construe. Grammar: the passive voice -- be + past participle.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Execute -- to sign a contract and make it legally official
"The contract is executed by both parties." (Assinar / formalizar)
Incorporate -- to include a document so it becomes part of the contract
"The schedules are incorporated into the agreement." (Incorporar)
Stipulate -- to state a condition or term clearly in the contract
"The payment terms are stipulated in clause eight." (Estipular)
Deem -- to treat something as true or accepted under the contract
"The change is deemed accepted when both parties sign." (Considerar / reputar)
Render -- to cause something to become a certain state
"A breach can render the clause invalid." (Tornar)
Supersede -- to replace and cancel an earlier document or term
"This agreement supersedes the old letter of intent." (Substituir / revogar)
Annex -- an extra document attached at the end of a contract
"The price annex is attached to the agreement." (Anexo)
Construe -- to interpret a word or clause and give it a meaning
"The word Goods is construed as the products delivered." (Interpretar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Contratos usam muito a voz passiva, porque o foco é a COISA, não a pessoa. Forma: be (is / are) + particípio passado ("is signed", "are delivered"). Para dizer quem faz a ação, use by ("is signed by both parties"). Não esqueça o verbo be e use o particípio passado, não o verbo base. / Contracts use the passive voice a lot, because the focus is the THING, not the person. Form: be (is / are) + past participle ("is signed", "are delivered"). To say who does the action, use by ("is signed by both parties"). Do not drop the verb be, and use the past participle, not the base verb.

Part / ParteForm / FormaExample / Exemplo
passive / passivabe + past participle"the agreement is signed"
plural / pluralare + past participle"the goods are delivered"
who / quem (by)be + past participle + by"is signed by both parties"
