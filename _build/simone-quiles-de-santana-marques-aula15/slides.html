Chapter 3 -- The Passive Voice

Be + Past Participle

How contracts focus on the thing, not the person

Listen and Notice

What Do They Share?

Look at the highlighted words. Where is "is" or "are", and what verb comes after?

"The agreement is signed by both parties."

"The goods are delivered under this contract."

"The schedules are incorporated into the agreement."

"The old letter of intent is superseded by this agreement."

The Passive Voice Code

The Passive

PartFormExample
passive formbe + past participle"is signed", "are delivered"
focusthe thing"the agreement is signed"
who does itby + person (optional)"is signed by both parties"
active vs passivethey sign / it is signedcontracts prefer the passive

Key idea: the passive puts the thing first. Use be (is/are) + the past participle. Add by + person only when you want to say who does the action.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The agreement signed by both parties. ... The goods are deliver under this contract."
"The agreement is signed by both parties. ... The goods are delivered under this contract."

Keep the verb be (is / are) and use the past participle (signed, delivered), not the base verb.

Artefact

The Definitions Clause

SERVICE AGREEMENT -- FINAL DRAFT

FOR SIGNATURE

Clause 1 -- Definitions

"Goods" is construed as the products that are delivered under this contract. "Services" is construed in the same way. Each term is interpreted as it is stipulated in this clause.

Clause 2 -- Schedules

All schedules and the price annex are incorporated into this agreement and are read as one document. The payment terms are stipulated in the annex.

Clause 3 -- Entire Agreement

The old letter of intent is superseded by this agreement. The contract is executed when it is signed by both parties, and any change is deemed accepted on that date.

The parties want a clear final draft, so every term is construed exactly as it is written.
