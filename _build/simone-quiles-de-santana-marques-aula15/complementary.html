Lesson 15 -- Reading Contracts IV

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 9 "Undefeated"

The lawyers draft and review a deal, reading clauses line by line before the contract is executed. Listen for how they talk about a draft, an annex and the definitions, and notice the passive voice: "the agreement is signed", "the terms are stipulated".

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 passive sentences you hear (be + past participle) and the drafting words around them.

Podcast
Legal English Podcast -- "Drafting and Interpreting Contracts"

A practical episode on how commercial contracts are drafted, how definitions are construed, and why so much legal English is written in the passive voice. The hosts use exactly the language you need for the contracts and M&A work you do at the office.

Note 3 drafting steps the hosts describe, and rewrite each one as a passive sentence (be + past participle).

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "The passive voice: be + past participle"

A short, clear lesson on how to form the passive voice and when to use it, including how to add "by" to say who does the action. Directly useful for reading and explaining the clauses of the contracts you review.

After watching, write five passive sentences about a contract, then record yourself reading them with at least four drafting words from today.
