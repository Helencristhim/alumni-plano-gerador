Boarding pass•cartão de embarque

Gate•portão de embarque

"Your flight departs from gate twenty-two."
Check-in•check-in
Where you register for your flight
"How many bags do you have for check-in?"

When the plane leaves
"Departure is at three forty."
Customs•alfândega
Where officials check what you bring into a country
"You have to go through customs."

"Could I have an aisle seat, please?"

Grammar Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Leia o texto. Os modais estão em negrito. Depois, responda as perguntas.

Rafael is at the airport for his flight to New York. At check-in, he shows his passport and boarding pass. The agent says he must be at the gate thirty minutes before departure. His bag is big, so the agent says he should check it in.

Rafael asks, "Can I have an aisle seat?" Then he has to go through security and customs. The flight is on time, with no delay. He can't bring large liquids in his hand luggage.
1. When must Rafael be at the gate?
A Five minutes before departure
B Thirty minutes before departure
C One hour after departure
2. Why does the agent say "you should check it in"?
A His bag is big (advice)
B The flight is delayed
C He has no passport
3. What can't Rafael bring in his hand luggage?
A His passport
B Large liquids
C His boarding pass

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Os modais para regras, conselho e permissão.

Modal Verbs
MODAL USO / EXEMPLO
must / have toobrigação (regra): "You must show your passport."
shouldconselho: "You should arrive early."
canpermissão: "Can I take this on board?"
can't / mustn'tproibição: "You can't bring large liquids."

✗ "You must to show your passport." (errado)

✓ "You must show your passport." (modal + verbo base, sem 'to')

Quiz Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação no aeroporto.

The agent asks for your documents. What do you say?
A "Here is my paper."
B "Here is my passport and boarding pass."
C "I have nothing."
You want permission to take a bag on board. What do you say?
A "Can I take this luggage on board?"
B "I take this bag, yes?"
C "This bag goes with me, must."
Which sentence is correct for a rule?
A "You must to show your passport."
B "You must show your passport."
C "You must showing your passport."
Your bag is too big. What is good advice?
A "You must to leave the bag."
B "You should check it in."
C "You can't have a bag."

Reflection Stage 5: Free Production

Grave sua resposta livremente. Não há certo ou errado — o objetivo é praticar.

Record a short airport conversation out loud in English. Check in for a flight: show your documents, check a bag, ask for an aisle seat, and ask one question (about the gate, the time, or a delay). Use must, have to, should, or can.
