Vocabulary

Trail -- a path you follow through nature or mountains
"The trail to the summit takes six hours." (Trilha)
Backpack -- a bag you carry on your back for your things
"My backpack is full of water and snacks." (Mochila)
Gear -- the equipment you need for an activity
"Good gear keeps you safe on the mountain." (Equipamento)
Boots -- strong shoes for walking on rough ground
"My boots are waterproof and very strong." (Botas)
Layer -- a piece of clothing worn over or under another
"On cold days I wear three warm layers." (Camada de roupa)
Waterproof -- able to keep water out; not letting rain in
"A waterproof jacket keeps you dry." (Impermeável)
Altitude -- the height of a place above sea level
"At high altitude the air is thin and cold." (Altitude)
Summit -- the highest point of a mountain
"We reached the summit just before noon." (Cume)
Slope -- the sloping side of a hill or mountain
"The slope was steep, so we walked slowly." (Encosta)
Forecast -- a report of what the weather will be like
"The forecast says it will snow tonight." (Previsão do tempo)
Shelter -- a place that protects you from bad weather
"We found shelter under a big rock." (Abrigo)
First-aid kit -- a box with supplies to treat small injuries
"The guide always carries a first-aid kit." (Kit de primeiros socorros)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca is going to trek Mont Blanc next summer. She already made the plan, so she uses going to for her firm decision: "I am going to climb the summit." Her guide Marco checks the forecast every morning. One day he sees dark clouds and says, "Look, it is going to rain" -- a prediction from evidence he can see now. On the trail, Tuca finds a heavy backpack, and Marco says, "I will carry it for you" -- a quick decision at that moment. When Tuca asks about the summit, Marco gives his opinion: "I think we will reach the top." Remember: use going to for plans and evidence, and will for quick decisions, offers, and opinions.

1. Which sentence is a plan decided before now?
A "I am going to climb the summit."
B "I will carry it for you."
C "I think we will reach the top."
2. When do we use will?
A for a plan we made last week
B for a quick decision, offer, or opinion
C for a finished action in the past
3. You see dark clouds now. Which is correct?
A "It will rain."
B "It is going to rain."
C "It rains."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- will vs going to

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar do futuro: planos, decisões e previsões. / Used to talk about the future: plans, decisions, and predictions.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
going toam/is/are going to + verbo: plano decidido antes / a plan decided before now"I am going to climb the summit."
going toprevisão com evidência agora / prediction from present evidence"The sky is dark; it is going to storm."
willwill + verbo: decisão na hora, oferta, promessa / quick decision, offer, promise"I will carry the backpack for you."
willopinião ou previsão / opinion or prediction (I think, probably)"I think it will be cold."

Key point / Ponto-chave: Se você já planejou ou vê a evidência agora, use going to. Se decide na hora, oferece ajuda ou dá uma opinião, use will. Após will nunca use "to": "I will carry" (não "I will to carry").
