Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Culture -- the shared values, habits, and ways of working of a group (cultura)
"Every team has its own work culture."
Directness -- how openly and plainly people say what they mean (franqueza / objetividade)
"Directness feels normal in the US, but strong elsewhere."
Etiquette -- the polite rules for how to behave in a group (etiqueta)
"Email etiquette changes from country to country."
Norm -- a usual, accepted way of doing something (norma / padrão)
"Quick replies are the norm on this team."
Adapt -- to change your style to fit a new situation (adaptar)
"I adapt my tone for each global team."
Context -- the full situation around what is said or done (contexto)
"The same words mean different things in another context."
Time zone -- a region that shares the same official time (fuso horário)
"India and the US are in very different time zones."
Overlap -- the shared hours when two teams are both online (sobreposição de horários)
"We only have a two-hour overlap each day."
Rapport -- a friendly, trusting connection with someone (sintonia / boa relação)
"A few minutes of small talk builds rapport."
Misunderstanding -- a failure to understand something correctly (mal-entendido)
"A short message avoided a big misunderstanding."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Patrícia works with teams in the US and India. She learns that the same request can sound polite in one culture and too direct in another, so she adapts. Instead of "Send the deck now," she writes, "Could you possibly send the deck?" Instead of "Move the call," she asks, "Would it be okay if we moved the call?" When there is no rush, she says, "When you get a chance, could you review the doc?" And she often uses "Would you mind sending the numbers?" Notice the patterns: could you possibly + base verb, would you mind + -ing, and would it be okay if + a past verb.

1. Por que a Patrícia troca "Send the deck now" por "Could you possibly send the deck?"
A Porque a ordem direta está gramaticalmente errada.
B Porque o opener educado suaviza o pedido sem perder a clareza.
C Porque "deck" precisa de um verbo no passado.
2. Depois de "Would you mind", qual forma do verbo usamos?
A O verbo com -ing: "Would you mind sending...?"
B O verbo no infinitivo com to: "Would you mind to send...?"
C O verbo no passado: "Would you mind sent...?"
3. Which request is polite and correct?
A "Would it be okay if we move the call?"
B "Would it be okay if we moved the call?"
C "Would it be okay if moving the call?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Directness (Softening Requests)

GRAMMAR

Como transformar uma ordem direta num pedido educado (explicação em inglês e português).

OpenerStructure / EstruturaExample
Could you possibly+ verbo na base. Pedido educado e claro.Could you possibly send the deck?
Would you mind+ verbo com -ing.Would you mind sending the deck?
Would it be okay if+ sujeito + verbo no passado.Would it be okay if we moved the call?
When you get a chance,+ um pedido normal. Tira a pressão.When you get a chance, could you review it?
Por quê?Uma ordem seca ("Send it now") pode soar rude em algumas culturas. O opener educado mantém o pedido claro, mas adiciona respeito — isso é polite directness.
