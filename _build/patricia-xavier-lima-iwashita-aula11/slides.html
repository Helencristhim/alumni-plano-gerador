Chapter 3: The Code

Polite Directness

Send it now. · Could you possibly send it when you get a chance?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you possibly share the deck before the call?"

"Would it be okay if we moved the sync to 8 a.m.?"

"When you get a chance, could you review the doc?"

"Would you mind sending me the final numbers?"

What do the green openers do to a direct order?

OpenerStructureExample
Could you possibly+ base verbCould you possibly send the deck?
Would you mind+ verb-ingWould you mind sending the deck?
Would it be okay if+ subject + past verbWould it be okay if we moved the call?
When you get a chance,+ a normal requestWhen you get a chance, could you review it?
Why it mattersA bare order ("Send it now") can sound blunt across cultures. A soft opener stays clear but adds respect — that is polite directness.

Be clear about what you need, but soft about how you ask.

Common Mistake

Too Direct vs Polite

"Send it now."
"Could you send it when you get a chance?"
"You must join the call at 9."
"Would it be okay if we met at 9?"

Directness is not "wrong" everywhere — the skill is to adapt. With a team that prefers a softer style, open with "Could you possibly..." or "Would it be okay if..." to stay clear and respectful.
