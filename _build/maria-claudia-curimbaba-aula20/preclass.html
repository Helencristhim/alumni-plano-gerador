Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Block 2 brought five tools together. Before the final negotiation, Maria Claudia reports her track record with the present perfect: "we have closed three deals, and revenue has risen sharply." She states the obligations with modals: "each party shall deliver, the contract must include a warranty, and either side may request a change." She describes the numbers with trend language, and gives advice with had better: "we had better confirm the deadline today." Finally, she compares the two styles fairly: "they are direct, whereas we build rapport first; their urgency is different from ours" -- the preposition is always "from", never "of". With a clear option and a fair trade-off on the table, she is ready to close the deal.

1. Why does the text use "have closed" and "has risen" instead of the simple past?
A The period is still open and the result matters now (present perfect).
B The events happened on a fixed past date.
C They are commands.
2. What follows the modal in "each party shall deliver"?
A "to" + verb ("shall to deliver").
B The base verb, with no "to" ("shall deliver").
C The past participle.
3. Which preposition is correct after "different"?
A "from" (different from ours).
B "of" (different of ours).
C "than" (different than ours).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Block 2 Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Esta aula revisa as cinco estruturas do Bloco 2 juntas. PRESENT PERFECT (have + particípio) = resultado até agora. MODAIS shall/must/may + verbo base (sem "to") = obrigação e permissão. TREND LANGUAGE (verbo + advérbio, ex.: rose sharply) = descrever números. ADVICE should/ought to/had better = conselho (had better tem consequência). COMPARISON whereas/while + oração, unlike + substantivo, different FROM. / This lesson reviews all five Block 2 structures together. PRESENT PERFECT (have + past participle) = result up to now. MODALS shall/must/may + base verb (no "to") = obligation and permission. TREND LANGUAGE (verb + adverb) = describe numbers. ADVICE should/ought to/had better = recommend; had better has a consequence. COMPARISON whereas/while + clause, unlike + noun, different FROM.

StructureUseExample
Present perfectresult up to now"we have closed three deals"
Modals (shall/must/may)obligation / permission (+ base verb)"each party shall deliver"
Trend languagedescribe numbers"revenue rose sharply"
Advice (should / had better)recommend; consequence"we had better confirm the deadline"
Comparison (whereas / unlike / different from)contrast fairly"they are direct, whereas we build rapport"

Key point / Ponto-chave: com período aberto (this quarter), use o present perfect, não o simple past; modal vem com verbo base (sem "to"); e a preposição é sempre "different from". / With an open period (this quarter), use the present perfect, not the simple past; a modal takes the base verb (no "to"); and the preposition is always "different from".
