The Rule

The Block 2 Toolkit

StructureUseExample
Present perfect (have + past participle)a result or experience up to now"we have closed three deals"
Modals: shall / must / may + base verbobligation and permission"each party shall deliver; either side may request a change"
Trend language (verb + adverb)describe numbers and change"revenue rose sharply"
Advice: should / ought to / had betterrecommend; had better adds a consequence"we had better confirm the deadline"
Comparison: whereas / unlike / different fromcontrast two ideas or cultures"they are direct, whereas we build rapport"

Key idea: a strong negotiation uses all five together -- report your track record (present perfect), state the obligations (shall / must), present the numbers (trend language), give a clear recommendation (should / had better), and compare fairly (whereas / unlike / different from).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We closed three deals this quarter." / "Their pace is different of ours."
"We have closed three deals this quarter." / "Their pace is different from ours."

When the period is still open (this quarter, this year), use the present perfect. And the preposition is always different from -- never "of" or "than".
