Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Chat -- to talk in a friendly, informal way (bater papo/conversar)
"We chatted for a few minutes before the meeting."
Mention -- to say something briefly, in passing (mencionar)
"She mentioned a great restaurant."
React -- to respond to what someone says (reagir)
"He reacted with a big smile."
Follow-up -- a question that continues the topic (pergunta de continuidade)
"A good follow-up question keeps the chat going."
Pause -- a short stop, for example to think (pausa)
"A small pause is completely normal."
Rephrase -- to say the same idea in another way (reformular)
"Let me rephrase that more simply."
Blank -- when your mind is empty and you forget (branco/dar branco)
"My mind went blank for a moment."
Topic -- the subject of a conversation (assunto/tópico)
"The weather is an easy topic for small talk."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At a work event, Lucas met a new colleague. He did not panic. He simply started to chat. "How do you know the host?" he asked. When she mentioned a new project, he reacted with interest: "Oh really? That's interesting! Tell me more." Then he asked a follow-up question. At one point, his mind went blank and he forgot a word. He stayed calm and said, "Let me think for a second." After a small pause, he found the word and continued. Later, he changed the topic naturally: "By the way, how was your weekend?" The conversation never stopped.

1. What did Lucas do when the colleague mentioned a project?
A He reacted with interest and asked a follow-up question.
B He said nothing.
C He changed the subject immediately.
2. What did Lucas do when his mind went blank?
A He stopped the conversation and walked away.
B He stayed calm and said, "Let me think for a second."
C He apologized many times.
3. How did Lucas change the topic?
A "By the way, how was your weekend?"
B "Stop talking about that."
C He did not change the topic.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Conversation Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Frases para manter o papo vivo. / Phrases to keep a conversation alive.

Job / FunçãoEnglishPortuguês
Show interestOh really? / That's interesting! / I know what you mean.Sério? / Que interessante! / Sei como é.
Keep it goingTell me more about that. / What about you?Me conta mais. / E você?
Change topicBy the way... / Speaking of which...A propósito... / Falando nisso...
Buy timeLet me think for a second. / How do I say this...Deixa eu pensar. / Como eu digo isso...
TipGoing blank is normal — even native speakers do it. Buy time calmly; do not over-apologize.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

A new person says: "I just moved to this city." The best reply is:
A "Oh really? How do you like it so far?"
B "Ok."
C "I don't care."
Your mind goes blank in a conversation. You should:
A Stop talking and feel embarrassed.
B Say "Let me think for a second" and stay calm.
C Apologize five times.
You want to gently change the subject. You say:
A "By the way, did you have a good weekend?"
B "Enough about that."
C "New topic now."
After you answer, you want to turn it back to them. You say:
A "What about you?"
B "You talk now."
C "And about you what?"
