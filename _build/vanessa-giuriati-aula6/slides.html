Chapter 3

The Toolkit

Phrases to keep any conversation alive

Discovery

Three Kinds of Phrase

"Oh really? That's interesting!"

"Tell me more about that."

"By the way, how is your project going?"

"Let me think for a second."

JobPhrases
Show interestOh really? / That's interesting! / I know what you mean. / That makes sense.
Keep it goingTell me more about that. / By the way... / Speaking of which... / What about you?
Recover (buy time)Let me think for a second. / How do I say this... / Can I rephrase that?
Common Mistake

Weak vs Strong

✗ WEAK

"Yes." (conversation stops)

✓ STRONG

"Yes! And what about you?"

✗ WEAK

(silence... panic)

✓ STRONG

"Let me think for a second."

✗ WEAK

"Sorry, sorry, my English is bad."

✓ STRONG

"How do I say this..."

Add a follow-up question to keep the conversation alive. When you go blank, buy a little time calmly. You do not need to apologize.

Meeting at an Event

D

V
Hi Daniel, I'm Vanessa. We work together. By the way, how do you know her?
D
We studied together years ago. So, what do you do?
V
I work in project management. It's busy, but I like it. What about you?
D
I'm in design. I just finished a big project, actually.
V
Oh really? That's interesting! Tell me more about it.
D
You're easy to talk to, Vanessa. This is a nice chat.
V
Thank you. I'm working on it, and it's getting easier.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Daniel know the host?
They studied together years ago.
2. What does Daniel do, and what did he just finish?
He works in design and just finished a big project.
3. What does Daniel say about Vanessa at the end?
He says she is easy to talk to and that it is a nice chat.
Listening

Be Interested

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. How did the speaker feel at events before?
He felt nervous and stood in a corner.
2. What simple idea did his friend give him?
Do not try to be interesting; try to be interested.
3. What did he realize about small talk?
It is about showing real interest in the other person, not clever words.
Listening

When the Mind Goes Blank

A different voice, a different story. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. What happened to the speaker in the meeting?
Her mind went blank and she could not find the word.
2. What phrase did she use to buy time?
"How do I say this, let me think for a second."
3. What is her main message about going blank?
It is normal; do not panic, buy time, stay calm, and keep talking.
