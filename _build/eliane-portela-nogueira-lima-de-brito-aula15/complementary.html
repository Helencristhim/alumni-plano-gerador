Film
The Intern (2015)

A seventy-year-old retiree becomes a senior intern at a fast-growing startup, mentoring a young founder through the chaos of her first year. It is Lesson 15 on screen: watch a leader confront her blind spots, her self-doubt and the trade-off between growth and control, and notice the many moments she wishes she had asked for help sooner or trusted her gut feeling.

Tip: after a key scene, say one wish about the founder out loud -- a present wish ("I wish she were...") and a past regret ("I wish she had...").

The Diary of a CEO with Steven Bartlett

Long-form interviews where founders and leaders reflect candidly on failure, setbacks and hard-won lessons. It is the exact register of Lesson 15: guests looking back on what they wish they had done differently, the wake-up calls that changed them, and the resilience that let them bounce back. Listen for the language of hindsight and regret.

Tip: pick one episode, then write two sentences about the guest -- one "I wish I had..." (past) and one "I wish I were..." (present).

Virtually Fluent -- "Hope, Wish & If Only -- Advanced English Grammar Explained"

A clear B2-level lesson on the exact grammar of this class: wish + past simple for a present regret, wish / if only + past perfect for a past regret, and wish + would for wanting others to change. Direct connection to Lesson 15: the structures that let you speak about regret with honesty and precision.

Tip: pause after each structure and say one wish about your own year out loud before you continue.
