Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words for looking back on a journey. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras para olhar para trás numa jornada. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Setback -- a problem that delays or reverses progress / um revés que atrasa o progresso
"Every setback in the first year taught us something."
Blind spot -- a weakness you cannot see in yourself / um ponto cego, fraqueza que você não enxerga
"Trying to do everything alone was my biggest blind spot."
Learning curve -- how hard and long it takes to learn something / curva de aprendizado
"The learning curve was steeper than I expected."
Gut feeling -- an instinct, not based on facts / intuição, palpite
"In hindsight, my gut feeling was usually right."
Hindsight -- understanding something only after it happens / retrospectiva, visão em retrospecto
"In hindsight, each hard moment made us stronger."
Resilience -- the ability to recover quickly from difficulty / resiliência
"Founders who last are the ones with resilience."
Double down -- to commit even more strongly to a decision / apostar ainda mais numa decisão
"Instead of quitting, we decided to double down."
Cut your losses -- to stop before you lose more / parar antes de perder mais
"There was a moment I almost cut my losses."
Wake-up call -- a shock that forces you to change / um alerta, chamado para mudar
"That bad month was a real wake-up call."
Bounce back -- to recover after a setback / se recuperar de um revés
"Every time we fell, we bounced back stronger."
Self-doubt -- a lack of confidence in yourself / dúvida sobre si mesmo, insegurança
"There were days of real self-doubt."
Trade-off -- giving up one thing to gain another / troca, concessão
"The biggest trade-off is growth versus control."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is looking back on her first year. When she talks about now, she uses the past simple after wish: I wish I had more time, I wish I were more patient. When she talks about the past, she uses the past perfect: I wish I had asked for help sooner, if only I hadn't ignored my gut feeling. And when she wants other people to change, she uses would: I wish the board would decide faster. Notice three traps: a present wish is not "I wish I have" but "I wish I had"; a past regret is not "I wish I would have asked" but "I wish I had asked"; and for your own state you say "I wish I were", never "I wish I would be".

1. To talk about a regret happening NOW, which form follows "wish"?
A The past simple: "I wish I had more time."
B The present simple: "I wish I have more time."
C The future: "I wish I will have more time."
2. Which sentence correctly expresses a PAST regret?
A "I wish I would have asked for help sooner."
B "I wish I had asked for help sooner."
C "I wish I asked for help sooner."
3. Why is it "I wish I were more confident", not "I wish I would be"?
A Because "would be" is too formal.
B Because for your own state you use "were", and keep "would" for other people.
C Because "were" is plural.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Wish & If Only

GRAMMAR

Wish and if only express regret. The tense you use after them signals the meaning. "If only" works like "wish" but feels stronger. / Wish e if only expressam arrependimento. O tempo verbal depois deles sinaliza o sentido. "If only" funciona como "wish", mas é mais forte.

Meaning / SentidoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Regret about NOWwish + past simple (were)I wish I had more time. / I wish I were braver.
Regret about the PASTwish / if only + past perfectI wish I had asked sooner. / If only I hadn't doubted it.
Wanting OTHERS to changewish + would + base verbI wish they would decide faster.
