Chapter 3

The Small Talk Code

like + -ing · and the questions that start a chat

Discovery

Listen and Notice

I like reading in my free time.

I enjoy cooking on weekends.

Do you like playing football?

What do you do on weekends?

When you want to...Structure
Say what you like doingI like / love / enjoy + verb-ing: I like reading.
Ask about likesDo you like + verb-ing? Do you like cooking?
Ask about the weekendWhat do you do on weekends? / How was your weekend?
Ask about free timeWhat do you do in your free time?
Pass the chat backHow about you? / What about you?

After like, love, enjoy, use the -ing form: I like reading (not "I like read"). To start a chat, use Do you...? or What do you do...?, then pass it back with How about you?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I like read."

✓ RIGHT

"I like reading."

✗ WRONG

"I enjoy to cook."

✓ RIGHT

"I enjoy cooking."

✗ WRONG

"What you do on weekends?"

✓ RIGHT

"What do you do on weekends?"

After like, love, and enjoy, use the verb + -ing. In questions about routines, do not forget "do".

Chapter 4

In Action

A real coffee-break chat, line by line

Dialogue

At the Coffee Machine

R
Good morning, Danilo! How was your weekend?
D
Hi Rachel! It was great, thanks. I relaxed a lot. How about you?
R
Pretty good! The weather was nice, so I went for a walk.
D
That sounds lovely. Do you have any hobbies?
R
Yes, I love painting. It is my favorite way to relax. What about you?
D
I like playing football and reading in my free time.
R
Nice! Do you enjoy cooking too?
D
Yes, I really enjoy cooking on weekends. It helps me relax.
R
Same here. It is nice to chat before the meeting.
D
I agree. Let us get a coffee and talk more.
