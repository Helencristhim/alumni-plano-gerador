Chapter 3

Simple vs. Continuous

Routine or right now — and the verbs that never take -ing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I usually run the Monday meeting, but this week Liam is running it."

"We assign one recruiter to each role, but right now we are testing a new process."

"I prefer email, but at the moment I am calling people directly."

"Do you attend this meeting every week?"

FormUseExample
present simple: subject + verbRoutine, always, factsI usually run the Monday meeting.
present continuous: am / is / are + verb-ingHappening now, temporaryThis week Liam is running it.
question & negative (simple)Do / does for routinesDo you attend it? We do not assign it.
stative verbs: prefer, notice, involve, know, wantNever take -ing, stay simpleI prefer email. It involves four steps. (not "am preferring")
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am running the meeting every Monday."

✓ RIGHT

"I run the meeting every Monday."

✗ WRONG

"She runs the meeting right now."

✓ RIGHT

"She is running the meeting right now."

✗ WRONG

"I am preferring the shorter process."

✓ RIGHT

"I prefer the shorter process."

Use the present simple for routines and stative verbs (prefer, notice, involve), and the present continuous (be + -ing) only for actions happening right now.

Chapter 4

In the Meeting

Talking about your routine and what is different this week

Dialogue

Planning the Weekly Calls

L
Hi Murielle. To plan our calls, tell me: what does a normal week look like for you?
M
Every Monday, I attend the regional meeting, and I am in charge of the recruitment budget. We usually assign one recruiter to each role.
L
That sounds organized. And is this week the same as usual?
M
No, this week is different. Right now, we are testing a shorter process, so I am following up with the candidates myself.
L
Interesting. How many steps does the new process involve?
M
It involves only two steps now. It is a one-off test this month, not a permanent change.
L
And which one do you prefer, the old one or the new one?
M
Honestly, I prefer the shorter one. I notice that we make faster decisions. Let's put it on the agenda for our recurring call.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Murielle do every Monday?
She attends the regional meeting and is in charge of the recruitment budget.
2. What is different this week?
They are testing a shorter process, so she is following up with the candidates herself.
3. Which process does Murielle prefer, and why?
She prefers the shorter one because she notices they make faster decisions.
