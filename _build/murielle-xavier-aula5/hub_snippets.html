Every Day vs. Right Now -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Separating routines from live action in HR meetings: what you always do vs what is happening right now, plus the stative verbs that never take -ing. New words: routine, agenda, attend, in charge of, assign, escalate, follow up, recurring, one-off, notice, prefer, involve. Grammar: present simple (routines, facts, stative verbs) vs present continuous (now, temporary).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está entre parênteses. (Listen and read the example.)

Routine -- a regular, fixed way of doing things (rotina)
"My morning routine starts at eight o'clock."
Agenda -- the list of topics for a meeting (pauta / agenda)
"Let me send you the agenda for tomorrow."
Attend -- to go to and take part in a meeting (comparecer / participar)
"I attend the regional meeting every Monday."
In charge of -- responsible for something or someone (responsável por)
"I am in charge of the recruitment budget."
Assign -- to give a task or role to a person (atribuir / designar)
"We assign one recruiter to each open role."
Escalate -- to pass a problem to a higher level (escalar / encaminhar)
"People escalate difficult cases to me."
Follow up -- to check on something later and act (dar seguimento / acompanhar)
"The recruiter follows up with the candidate every week."
Recurring -- happening again and again, regularly (recorrente)
"We have a recurring meeting every Monday."
One-off -- happening only once, not regular (pontual / único)
"This new process is a one-off test, not permanent."
Notice -- to see or realize something (perceber / notar)
"I notice that we work better when we talk."
Prefer -- to like one thing more than another (preferir)
"I prefer the shorter process so far."
Involve -- to include as a necessary part (envolver / incluir)
"Our hiring process involves four steps."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer the questions.)

Every week at Minerva Foods follows a clear routine. Murielle usually attends the regional meeting on Monday, and she is in charge of the recruitment budget. The team normally assigns one recruiter to each role, and that recruiter follows up with the candidate every week. Difficult cases escalate to Murielle. But this week is different. Right now, they are testing a shorter process, so Murielle is following up with the candidates herself. The new process involves only two steps — it is a one-off test this month, not a recurring change. "I prefer the shorter one," she says. "I notice that we make faster decisions." She adds the topic to the agenda for the next call.

1. Why does the text say "Murielle usually attends" but "she is following up... herself" this week?
A Both describe finished past actions.
B "Usually attends" is a routine (present simple); "is following up" is happening now (present continuous).
C "Is following up" is a permanent habit.
2. Why do we say "The new process involves only two steps" and not "is involving"?
A "Involve" is a stative verb, so it stays in the present simple even for now.
B "Involve" can never be used in a question.
C The action is finished and will not happen again.
3. Which sentence correctly contrasts a routine with what is happening right now?
A "I am usually assigning roles, but this week I assign them myself."
B "I usually assign roles, but this week I am following up myself."
C "I usually assigning roles, but this week I following up myself."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous (+ Stative Verbs)

GRAMMAR

Use o present simple (subject + verb) para rotinas e fatos, e o present continuous (am/is/are + verbo-ing) para o que acontece AGORA. Alguns verbos de estado (stative verbs: prefer, notice, involve, know, want) ficam SEMPRE no present simple, mesmo para agora. (Routine vs now vs stative.)

FormUse (PT)Example
present simple: subject + verbRotina, fatos / routine, factsI usually run the Monday meeting.
present continuous: am / is / are + verb-ingAgora, temporário / now, temporaryThis week Liam is running it.
question & negative (simple)Do / does para rotina / routineDo you attend it? We do not assign it.
stative verbs: prefer, notice, involve, know, wantNunca com -ing / never take -ingI prefer email. It involves four steps. (não "am preferring")
