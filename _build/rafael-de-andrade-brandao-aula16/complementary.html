Lesson 16 -- Closing a Meeting

Series
The Office (US) -- any episode that ends a meeting

Watch how a meeting ends (or fails to end well). Listen for the closing toolkit: how a leader summarizes the main points, names the action items, and assigns an owner to each task. Notice how a clear recap makes everyone know what to do next.

Write down every phrase used to close the meeting. Then close an imaginary meeting out loud using summarize, action item, owner, and conclude.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Closing a Meeting & Action Items"

Practical phrases for closing a meeting: how to wrap up, summarize the main points, assign action items to owners, and conclude with a recap. Listen for the new words: summarize, wrap up, action item, recap, assign, owner, outcome, and conclude.

Note 3 ways to assign a task (responsible for + -ing, take care of, the next step is to). Practice each one out loud as if you are ending a real meeting at TVT.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to summarize and close a meeting"

A short, clear lesson on closing language: To sum up..., The next step is to..., and assigning tasks with responsible for and take care of. Perfect for ending a meeting so everyone leaves with clear action items.

After watching, record yourself closing a meeting: summarize three points, assign three action items to owners, and conclude with a recap.
