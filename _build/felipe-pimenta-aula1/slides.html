Chapter 3: The Code

Your Career in Three Tenses

I've been · I oversee · I worked

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have been a CFO for three years."

"I oversee the finance team at a fintech."

"We have just closed our first funding round."

"I worked in private equity in 2022."

Why green for experience, but blue for a finished year?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now (for / since); very recent news (just)I have been a CFO for 3 years.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Permanent facts, your current role and routineI oversee the finance team.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		Finished action at a finished time (in 2022, from 2021 to 2023)I worked in private equity in 2022.

for + a period (for three years) · since + a starting point (since 2014).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am a CFO for three years."
"I've been a CFO for three years."
"I have worked in private equity in 2022."
"I worked in private equity in 2022."

Duration up to now takes have / has + past participle with for / since. A finished time (in 2022, last year) always takes the past simple.

A Headhunter from London

The call begins

Dialogue

Felipe, thanks for making the time. I'm Sarah Whitmore, from Hartley and Vance. Before we get into the role, tell me a bit about yourself.
F
Of course. In my current role, I'm the CFO of a fintech. I oversee the finance team, and I report to the chief executive.
S
And how long have you been a CFO?
F
I've been a CFO for three years, and I've worked in corporate finance since 2014. Before that, I spent two years in private equity.

That's exactly the background our client is looking for. So what's driving the move for you?

I'm looking for an international role. I already deal with investors and the board every week — I want to do that in English, on a bigger stage.
