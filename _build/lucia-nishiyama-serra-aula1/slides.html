Chapter 3: The Code

Always vs Right Now

I lead · I am moving into

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I lead security projects at a tech company."

"This year I am moving into AI safety."

"I give executive presentations every month."

"Right now I am preparing for an interview in the US."

Why blue for things that are always true, but green for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Facts, routines and permanent situations — always trueI lead security projects.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Now, these days, temporary or unfinishedI am moving into AI safety.
He / she / itPresent simple adds -s; continuous uses isShe leads it. / She is leading it.

Time words help: every day, always, usually → simple · right now, today, this year → continuous.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Every month I am giving a presentation."
"Every month I give a presentation."
"Right now I prepare for an interview."
"Right now I am preparing for an interview."
"I am knowing this field very well."
"I know this field very well."

Routines use the present simple. Things happening now use the present continuous. Verbs of state — know, want, need, like, believe — almost never take -ing.
