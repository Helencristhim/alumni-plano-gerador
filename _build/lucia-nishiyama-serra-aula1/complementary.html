Lesson 1 -- Who Is Lucia Serra in English?

Interview Skills
Tell Me About Yourself -- A Good Answer to This Interview Question (Linda Raynier)

The very first question in almost every American interview, answered by a career coach. She shows how to talk about your career as a story with a clear point, not a list of jobs. This is exactly the skill we started mapping in class today.

Tip: watch once for the idea, then write your own three-sentence answer to "Tell me about yourself" using the present simple for what you do and the present continuous for what you are working on now.

Talk (TED)
Your Body Language May Shape Who You Are -- Amy Cuddy (TED)

A famous, clear talk on presence and confidence before a high-stakes moment like an interview or a presentation. The English is slow and precise, and the message is exactly your goal this year: sounding as confident in English as you already are in Portuguese.

Tip: notice how she pauses. Executive English is not fast English -- it is confident, well-placed pauses. Copy one sentence out loud with the same rhythm.

Watch on YouTube ↗
Podcast
How I Built This -- KAYAK: Paul English (with Guy Raz)

A tech leader tells the story of a whole career in his own words -- the wins, the doubts, the turning points. This is the register you are building: an executive telling a long story in natural, spoken American English. Your listening is strong, so use it.

Tip: this is long -- listen to the first 20 minutes only. Write down three phrases he uses to move from one part of his story to the next ("so then", "at that point", "what happened was...").
