Lesson 18 -- Negotiation V: The Renewal Meeting

Series
The West Wing -- any boardroom or staff meeting scene

Formal meetings run on an agenda: someone makes a proposal, others support it, a vote is held, a decision is reached, and then people report it to those who were not there. Listen for how they run a meeting and how they later report what was said.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one reported sentence you hear ("she said... was...", "he told them they would...") and one boardroom word from today (motion, vote, resolution).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Chairing a Meeting and Reporting Decisions"

Practical episodes on running a formal meeting -- opening, motions, seconds, votes, resolutions -- and on reporting what people said afterwards with reported speech. Exactly today's vocabulary and grammar in real business meetings.

Note three phrases the chair uses to run the vote. Then record yourself reporting three decisions of a meeting with "they said... was..." and "they told me they would...".

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Reported Speech (say and tell, backshift)"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to step the tense back when you report (is becomes was, will becomes would) and the key difference between "say" and "tell".

After watching, take five things people said in a recent meeting and report each one out loud, stepping the tense back and choosing say or tell correctly.
