Vocabulary

Summary -- a short version of the main points
"I opened with a summary of why we were meeting." (Resumo)
Restate -- to say a point or proposal again clearly
"Let me restate the proposal so it is clear." (Reafirmar)
Outline -- to describe the main points of something
"I outlined two options for the new fee." (Esboçar / delinear)
Attendee -- a person who is present at a meeting
"Six attendees were present in the boardroom." (Participante)
Apologies -- the names of people who could not attend
"I read the apologies: two directors were away." (Justificativas de ausência)
Motion -- a formal proposal that the meeting votes on
"The director made a motion to accept the fee." (Moção / proposta formal)
Second -- to support a motion so the vote can happen
"Another director seconded the motion." (Apoiar / secundar)
Adjourn -- to close or end a meeting officially
"After the vote, I adjourned the meeting." (Encerrar a reunião)
Resolution -- the official decision a meeting agrees on
"The resolution to renew the deal passed." (Resolução / deliberação)
Abstain -- to choose not to vote yes or no
"One person chose to abstain from the vote." (Abster-se)
Unanimous -- agreed by everyone, with no one against
"The vote on the exclusivity was unanimous." (Unânime)
Ratify -- to formally approve a contract or decision
"The board ratified the new contract." (Ratificar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After the renewal meeting, Nilo must report what was said, so he uses reported speech. The key idea is that the verb usually steps back one tense. The director says, "the deal is final." Later Nilo reports: the director said the deal was final. They say, "we will extend." Nilo reports: they told us they would extend. There is one more rule that matters: you say something, but you tell someone. So Nilo says the fee is fair, but he tells the board the fee is fair. In the meeting itself, a director made a motion, another seconded it, they voted, and the resolution passed. Nilo then reported that the board had ratified the contract.

1. When you report "the deal is final", what happens to the verb "is"?
A It steps back to "was" -- "he said the deal was final".
B It stays "is" -- "he said the deal is final".
C It becomes "will be".
2. Which sentence uses "say" and "tell" correctly?
A "He said me the fee was fair."
B "He told me the fee was fair."
C "He told the fee was fair."
3. What needs to happen before a vote can take place?
A The meeting must be adjourned.
B Someone makes a motion and another person seconds it.
C Everyone must abstain.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Usado para contar a outras pessoas o que foi dito. / Used to tell other people what was said.

Direct / DiretoReported / Reportado
"The deal is final."He said the deal was final.
"We will extend."They told us they would extend.
"The fee is fair."The board said the fee was fair.
"We will sign."He told me they would sign.

Key point / Ponto-chave: ao reportar, o verbo recua um tempo (is→was, will→would). E lembre: você say algo (sem pessoa depois), mas tell alguém (sempre uma pessoa depois) -- "he said the deal was final" / "he told me the deal was final". Nunca "he said me". / Backshift the tense; say = no person, tell = a person.
