Chapter 3 -- Reporting the Words

Grammar Discovery

Tell others what was said

Listen and Notice

Listen and Notice

Direct: "The deal is final." → Reported: He said the deal was final.

Direct: "We will extend." → Reported: They told us they would extend.

Direct: "The fee is fair." → Reported: The board said the fee was fair.

Direct: "We will sign." → Reported: He told me they would sign.

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

Direct wordsSteps back toReported sentence
"The deal is final."is → wasHe said the deal was final.
"We will extend."will → wouldThey told us they would extend.
"The fee is fair."is → wasThe board said the fee was fair.
"We will sign."will → wouldHe told me they would sign.

Key idea: when you report, the verb usually steps back one tense (is→was, will→would). And the big difference: you say something (no person after it), but you tell someone (always a person after it). "He said the deal was final" / "He told me the deal was final".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He said me the deal was final."
"He told me the deal was final." (or: "He said the deal was final.")

Use tell + a person (told me, told the board). Use say with no person right after it.

Comprehension

About the Briefing

1. According to Sofia, what are the steps of a vote in a formal meeting?

One person makes a motion (a formal proposal), another seconds it (supports it so the vote can happen), then everyone votes -- yes, no, or abstain. A decision that passes becomes a resolution.

2. When Nilo reports the meeting, what happens to the tense of "is" and "will"?

The verb steps back one tense: "is" becomes "was", and "will" becomes "would" -- "the director said the deal was final", "they told us they would extend".

3. What difference between "say" and "tell" does Sofia stress?

You "say" something (no person after it), but you "tell someone" (always a person after it) -- "you say the price is fair", but "you tell the board the price is fair".

RENEWAL MEETING -- MINUTES

Sport Club Corinthians Paulista · Chair: Dr. Nilo Patussi

1. Opening

Summary read by the chair. Attendees: 6 present. Apologies: 2 directors absent.

2. The Fee

Motion to accept the higher fee, made by the marketing director, seconded by another director. Vote: 4 yes, 0 no, 1 abstain. Resolution passed.

3. The Exclusivity

Extension to four seasons outlined by the chair. Vote: unanimous.

4. Close

New contract ratified. Directors said the new fee was fair and told the chair they would sign that week. Meeting adjourned.
