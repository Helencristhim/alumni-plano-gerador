Lesson 18 -- Strategic Proposals

Series
Billions (Showtime) -- The pitch-room episodes

Watch the scenes where analysts present an investment case to leadership: a headline figure first, then the rationale, the cost-benefit, and a staged approach. Notice how the strongest speakers pack precise information into dense noun phrases -- "a fully modeled three-year consolidation plan" -- exactly what you practiced today.

Pick one pitch and write three complex noun phrases from it (pre-modifiers + a post-modifying "from / designed to" phrase).

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Make the Business Case for Anything"

An episode on building a compelling business case: leading with the headline figure, quantifying the value drivers, and underpinning every claim with real numbers. Directly relevant to presenting a strategic supply-chain proposal to leadership.

Note two phrases the guest uses to express ROI or payback, and rewrite each as a single dense noun phrase ("a sub-twelve-month payback period").

YouTube
"Complex Noun Phrases in Academic and Business English" -- Advanced Grammar

A clear guide to the exact structure in this lesson: how to pack information before the head noun (pre-modifiers) and after it (post-modifying "of / from / designed to" phrases) to build the precise, authoritative noun phrases of a written proposal -- "the projected savings from the renegotiated freight contracts".

After watching, write a four-line proposal summary in which every line contains at least one complex noun phrase with both a pre-modifier and a post-modifier.
