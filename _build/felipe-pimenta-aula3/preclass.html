Aula 03 -- Pre-class

What Should Have Happened -- Modal Perfects

Analisar um trimestre que não bateu a meta e dar (e receber) feedback duro sem destruir a relação. Key words: performance review, shortfall, variance, root cause, bottleneck, to escalate, to flag, accountability, to miss a target, turnaround, mitigation, milestone. Structures: modal perfects — should have / shouldn't have (veredito), could have (chance perdida, sem culpa), must have / might have (dedução sobre o passado).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. É o vocabulário de toda reunião de resultados que você já conduziu — agora em inglês.

Performance review -- the formal meeting where your work is evaluated (avaliação de desempenho)
"My performance review is on Thursday, and the forecast will come up."
Shortfall -- the gap between the number you promised and the one you delivered (déficit, diferença a menor)
"We closed the quarter with a two million shortfall."
Variance -- the difference between the budget and what actually happened (variância, variação orçamentária)
"The variance report explains every line above five percent."
Root cause -- the real reason behind a problem, not the symptom (causa raiz)
"The root cause was a hiring freeze nobody told us about."
Bottleneck -- the one point that slows everything else down (gargalo)
"Approvals were the bottleneck, not the sales team."
To escalate -- to take an issue to someone more senior (escalar, levar à instância superior)
"She should have escalated the problem in week two."
To flag -- to point out a risk early, before it becomes a problem (sinalizar, apontar)
"Somebody must have flagged this before the board meeting."
Accountability -- owning the result, whether it is good or bad (responsabilização, prestação de contas)
"Accountability is not blame — it is knowing whose call it was."
To miss a target -- to fail to reach the number you committed to (não bater a meta)
"We missed the target by eight percent, and I own that."
Turnaround -- the recovery of a business or a number that was failing (recuperação, virada)
"The turnaround took two quarters and a very hard conversation."
Mitigation -- the action you take to reduce a risk you cannot remove (mitigação)
"Our mitigation was to hold ten percent of the budget back."
Milestone -- a key checkpoint that proves a plan is on track (marco)
"We could have caught it at the first milestone."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The quarter closed eight percent below plan: a two million shortfall. In the variance report, one line stood out. The approvals cycle had stretched from seven days to twenty-one. That was the bottleneck, and it was the root cause — not the sales team, as everyone had assumed. Two analysts flagged it in February. It should have been escalated to the CEO in week two, and it was not. Somebody must have seen the number at the February close — it was on the first page. The team could have caught it at the first milestone, and Felipe says that he should have pushed harder when he first saw the cycle time. He does not blame his analysts: accountability, he tells the board, is not the same as fault. It means knowing whose call it was. The mitigation is now a standing rule: any variance above five percent reaches the CEO the same week. The turnaround took one quarter.

1. "It should have been escalated in week two." O que realmente aconteceu?
A Foi escalado na segunda semana, como devia.
B NÃO foi escalado — e o certo era ter sido.
C Será escalado na segunda semana do próximo trimestre.
2. Por que o texto usa "could have caught" para o time, e "should have pushed" para o Felipe?
A Porque could have é uma chance perdida (sem culpa) e should have é um veredito — ele cobra de si, não do time.
B Porque could have e should have significam exatamente a mesma coisa.
C Porque could have é passado e should have é futuro.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A Somebody must saw the variance in February.
B Somebody must have seen the variance in February.
C Somebody must of seen the variance in February.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Perfects (should / could / must have)

GRAMMAR

Como julgar o passado sem atacar a pessoa — a gramática de toda performance review (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
should have + past participleA ação certa que NÃO aconteceu. Um veredito educado. The right action that did not happen.She should have escalated it.
shouldn't have + past participleA ação errada que ACONTECEU. The wrong action that did happen.We shouldn't have signed off.
could have + past participleUma chance real que existia e não foi tomada. Mais suave — SEM culpa. A missed possibility.We could have caught it earlier.
couldn't have + past participleEra impossível. Protege você e a equipe. It was impossible.We couldn't have known about the freeze.
must have + past participleDedução CONFIANTE sobre o passado. A confident deduction.Somebody must have seen it.
might / may have + past participleDedução CAUTELOSA. O seu hedging — mantém a sala calma.It might have been a timing issue.

Atenção (dois erros de brasileiro): (1) o particípio é obrigatório — "she should have escalated", nunca "should escalated" nem "should have escalate". (2) Na fala, should've soa como "should of" — mas "should of" NÃO existe na escrita. E o de ouro: should julga a pessoa, could julga a situação. Numa review, comece sempre pelo could.

Stage 2: Put the Performance Review in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma review de resultados na ordem correta. É a sequência que transforma uma bronca numa análise.

?Name the root cause — and separate it from the symptom.
?Agree the mitigation and the next milestone.
?Open with the number: the shortfall and the variance, with no excuses.
?Take your own accountability before you assign anyone else's.
?Say what should have happened, and when.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

Your CEO asks why the problem never reached her. The most senior answer is:
A "My analysts didn't tell me. I only found out last week."
B "It should have been escalated in week two, and it wasn't. I own that."
C "It should of been escalated in week two."
You give feedback to a good analyst who missed the variance. You start with:
A "You should have caught this. It was on the first page."
B "You could have caught this at the first milestone — let's talk about how."
C "You must have seen this and ignored it."
The numbers were clearly on his desk. You are almost certain he saw them:
A "He must have seen the variance in the report."
B "He must saw the variance in the report."
C "He should have seen the variance in the report."
Nobody could have predicted the hiring freeze. You protect your team:
A "We should have known about the freeze."
B "Nobody told us about the freeze, so we couldn't have known."
C "We could have known about the freeze if we wanted."
You are not sure yet whether the gap is real or just a timing issue. You say:
A "The variance must have been a timing issue."
B "The variance might have been a timing issue — let me check the cut-off date."
C "The variance is definitely a timing issue."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

Your CEO says: "The quarter closed eight percent below plan. Walk me through it." Answer for 2-3 minutes. Give the root cause, use should have at least twice (once about yourself), could have at least once (about your team, without blaming them), and one deduction with must have or might have. Finish with the mitigation and the next milestone.
