Chapter 3: The Code

Should, Could, Must

The grammar of a verdict

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"She should have escalated the problem in week two."

"We could have caught it at the first milestone."

"Somebody must have flagged this before the board met."

"The variance might have been a timing issue."

All four are about the past. So which one is a criticism, which is a missed chance, and which is a deduction?

FormUseExample
should have + participleThe right action that did NOT happen. A verdict — polite, but a verdict.She should have escalated it.
shouldn't have + participleThe wrong action that DID happen.We shouldn't have signed before the audit.
could have + participleA real possibility that existed and was not taken. Softer than should — no blame.We could have caught it at the first milestone.
must have + participleA confident deduction: you are almost sure it happened.Somebody must have flagged this.
might / may have + participleA tentative deduction. Your hedging tool — it keeps the room calm.It might have been a timing issue.

In real speech they all contract to the same sound: should've, could've, must've — never "should of". Say them fast; that is how they are heard.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She should escalated the problem in week two."
"She should have escalated the problem in week two."
"We could of caught it at the first milestone."
"We could have caught it at the first milestone."
"Somebody must flagged this before the board meeting."
"Somebody must have flagged this before the board meeting."

The pattern never changes: modal + have + past participle. Never "should of" (that is only how should've sounds), and never a bare verb after have.

Chapter 4: The Review Meeting

Your CEO Wants the Whole Story

The review begins

Dialogue

The Quarterly Review

C
Felipe, the quarter closed eight percent below plan. Before we talk about the fix, I want your reading of the root cause.
F
The root cause was approvals. They were the bottleneck, not the sales team. And I should have seen it sooner — the signal was in the February numbers.
C
Somebody must have noticed that in February. Why did it not reach me?
F
Two people flagged it internally. It should have been escalated to you in week two, and it was not. That is on me — I own the accountability for what reaches this room.
C
I appreciate that. What could the team have done differently?
F
They could have caught it at the first milestone. The mitigation is a standing rule now: any variance above five percent goes to you the same week, not the same quarter.
Comprehension

About Claire

1. What does Claire want before they discuss the fix?
His reading of the root cause.
2. Which deduction does she make about February?
That somebody must have noticed the problem — and she asks why it never reached her.
3. How does she move the conversation from Felipe to the team?
She asks what the team could have done differently.
