Chapter 3 -- The Persuasive If

Open a Door for the Reader

If you approve this, we will deliver that

Discover the Pattern

What Makes It Persuade?

"If you approve the pilot, we will deliver results in thirty days."

"Should you decide to proceed, we will assign a dedicated team."

"Were we to partner, your onboarding time would fall by half."

"If you sign this quarter, we will waive the setup fee."

The Rule

The Persuasive Conditional

RegisterStructureExample
StandardIf + present, will + verb"If you approve the pilot, we will deliver in 30 days."
Formal (inversion)Should + you + verb, will..."Should you decide to proceed, we will assign a team."
Hypothetical (inversion)Were + subject + to + verb, would..."Were we to partner, your costs would fall."
Incentive closeIf + condition, will + reward"If you sign this quarter, we will waive the fee."

Key idea: a persuasive conditional links the reader's action to a benefit you promise. "Should you decide..." and "Were we to..." are formal, inverted ways to say "if" — no "if" word needed. They make the proposal sound confident and senior. Always tie the if to a concrete upside.

Watch Out

Common Mistake

If you will approve the pilot, we will deliver results.
If you approve the pilot, we will deliver results.

After "if", use the present tense, not "will". The "will" belongs only in the promise: "If you approve..., we will deliver...". The same rule holds for the formal versions: "Should you decide..." and "Were we to..." replace the "if" entirely.

Real Artefact

Eduardo's Proposal

A description, not an argument

We offer a B2B onboarding platform. It has many features. The price is competitive. Let us know if you are interested.

A proposal that persuades

The rationale: your team loses two weeks on every new partner onboarding.

The upside: if you approve a 30-day pilot, we will cut that time in half. Should you decide to proceed, we will assign a dedicated team.

The incentive: if you sign this quarter, we will waive the setup fee.

I would be glad to have you endorse the pilot — just reply with a yes.

Best regards,
Eduardo Chiba · Head of B2B Unit
