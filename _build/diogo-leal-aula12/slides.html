Useful Language

How to Say the Hard Thing

The behavior → the impact → the question → the ask → the support

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one attacks? Which one describes? Which one opens a conversation?

"When a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days for the test team."

"The last three sprints closed two days late."

"What is getting in the way?"

"I need you to flag the risk on day three."

Grammar Rule

Behavior, Impact, Question

MoveStructureExample
Describe the behaviorPast simple + a number (never an adjective about the person)The last three sprints closed two days late.
Name the impactWhen + present, the impact is + resultWhen a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days.
Open the conversationWhat is getting in the way? + I need you to + verbWhat is getting in the way? ... I need you to flag it earlier.

Attack the behavior, never the person. "You are careless" is an opinion, and the only possible answer is defence. "Three sprints closed two days late" is a fact, and a fact can be discussed, explained -- and fixed.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"You are always late. You are not committed."
"The last three sprints closed two days late."
"I want you send the risk on day three."
"I want you to send the risk on day three."

Facts, not labels. And after want / need / ask, English uses you TO + verb -- never "I want you send".

Dialogue

She Gets Defensive

D

"Marina, can I give you some feedback? The last three sprints closed two days late."

M

"The tickets came late. That is not my fault."

D

"I am not talking about fault. I am talking about the behavior, and I want to be specific: sprint twelve, thirteen and fourteen."

M

"So now I am the problem of the project?"

D

"No. Your code is strong -- the migration script was the best work on the team. But when a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days for the test team."

M

"Nobody told me it was a problem."

D

"That is on me, and I am sorry. You should be aware of it. So -- what is getting in the way?"

M

"The reviews. I wait three days for a code review, every sprint."

D

"That I can fix. I need you to flag it on day three, and I will adjust the review queue. Deal?"

Wrap-up

What I Learned

I can open a hard conversation: "Can I give you some feedback?"
I describe behavior with a number, never a label.
I can name the impact on the team.
I ask "What is getting in the way?" -- and then I am quiet.
I close with one clear ask, and praise only what is true.
