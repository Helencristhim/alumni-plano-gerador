Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Feedback -- what you tell someone about their work, so it improves
"Can I give you some feedback?" (Retorno / devolutiva)
Behavior -- what a person does, not who they are
"I am talking about the behavior, not about you." (Comportamento)
Specific -- exact: a date, a number, one event
"Be specific: three sprints, three delays." (Específico)
Defensive -- ready to protect yourself instead of listening
"She gets defensive when I mention the sprint." (Na defensiva)
Harsh -- too hard, and cruel
"Honest is good. Harsh is not." (Duro / cruel)
Aware -- knowing that something is happening
"I am not sure she is aware of the impact." (Ciente)
Adjust -- to change something a little, to make it work
"We can adjust the plan together." (Ajustar)
Praise -- to say clearly that someone did well
"Praise in public, correct in private." (Elogiar / elogio)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Marina is a strong developer, and her last three sprints closed late. Diogo does not give a speech. He is specific: "The last three sprints closed two days late." That is a fact about her behavior, not a label about her -- and a fact can be discussed. She gets defensive anyway: "That is not my fault." He does not get harsh. He goes back to the impact: "When a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days for the test team." Maybe she was not aware of that. Then he asks the question that changes everything -- "What is getting in the way?" -- and he is quiet. The answer is the code review queue, and he can adjust it. He ends with real praise: her migration script was the best work on the team. The feedback took fifteen minutes, and she did most of the talking.

1. Why does Diogo say "the last three sprints closed two days late"?
A Because it is specific behavior, not a label about her.
B Because he wants her to feel guilty.
C Because Sophia asked him to say it.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A I need you to flag the risk on day three.
B I need you flag the risk on day three.
C I need that you flag the risk on day three.
3. What happens after his question?
A He is quiet, and she gives the real reason.
B He explains the plan for ten minutes.
C He sends her an email.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Behavior, Impact, Question

GRAMMAR

Falar o difícil sem atacar a pessoa: fato, impacto, pergunta, pedido. / Saying the hard thing without attacking the person.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Descrever / DescribePast simple + número (nunca adjetivo sobre a pessoa)The last three sprints closed two days late.
Impacto / ImpactWhen + presente, the impact is + resultadoWhen a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days.
Abrir / Open it upWhat is getting in the way? + I need you to + verboWhat is getting in the way? ... I need you to flag it earlier.
Ponto-chave / Key pointDepois de want / need / ask, o inglês usa you TO + verbo ("I need you to flag") -- nunca "I need you flag" nem "I need that you flag". E a regra de ouro: fato, nunca rótulo. "You are careless" só tem uma resposta possível: defesa.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. Someone on your team is late for the third sprint. You open with:
A "Can I give you some feedback? The last three sprints closed two days late."
B "You are always late. Everybody has noticed."
C An email to the whole team about deadlines.
2. She says: "That is not my fault." You answer:
A "I am not talking about fault. When a sprint closes late, the impact is two lost days."
B "Yes, it is. You are the only one who is late."
C "Fine, forget it. It is not important."
3. After the feedback, the best next move is:
A Ask "What is getting in the way?" and stay quiet.
B Explain your plan for ten minutes.
C Give three more examples from last year.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take someone at Oracle you should have given feedback to, and never did. Speak for two minutes: (1) open the conversation, (2) the behavior, with a number, (3) the impact on the team, (4) the question, (5) one clear ask. Record it.
Now she answers: "Everybody on this project is late. Why me?" Answer out loud. Do not argue, do not get harsh -- go back to the fact, name the impact, and ask your question again. Record it.
