The phrases that keep a global discussion clear and respectful

Confirm: "Just to make sure I understand, do you mean the data is final?"

Soften: "I see your point. Perhaps we could look at it differently."

Adapt: "I'm happy to adapt to whatever works best for the team."

Grammar Rule

The Softening Toolkit

DirectSofter (cross-cultural)
We should redo it.Perhaps we could redo it.
No, that's impossible.I'm afraid that may be a little difficult.
Move the deadline.Would it be possible to move the deadline?
Ask the team in Japan.It might be worth asking the team in Japan.

Key idea: Across cultures, soften your message with tentative words -- perhaps, could, may, might, a little -- and with polite questions (Would it be possible to...?). This leaves room for the other person and respects more indirect cultures. Adapt to the context: with very direct colleagues you can be more direct too.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"No. That's impossible for us."
"I'm afraid that may be difficult, but let's find another way."

Soften a refusal to show sensitivity, but keep it clear so there is no misunderstanding. Politeness should never hide the real message.

Grammar Practice

Make It Softer

"We should redo it." → "_____Perhaps we could redo it." (soften with perhaps/could)
"No, that's impossible." → "I'm afraid _____that may be a little difficult." (soften a refusal)
"Move the deadline." → "_____Would it be possible to move the deadline?" (polite request)
"Ask the team in Japan." → "_____It might be worth asking the team in Japan." (soft suggestion)

Comprehension

About the Tips

1. Why should you listen to the whole message, not only the words?

Because directness changes across cultures -- a soft "that may be difficult" can really mean no.

2. What should you do about etiquette when you are not sure?

Start more formal -- use a title and family name -- and adapt later.

3. What does a good global communicator do with cultural differences?

Adapt the message to the listener and turn the differences into a strength for the team.
