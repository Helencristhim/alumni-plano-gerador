Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Cultural -- connected to the shared habits, values and customs of a group or country
"We need to respect the cultural differences in our global team." (Cultural)
Directness -- how openly and clearly people say what they really think
"German colleagues often value directness." (Franqueza / objetividade)
Etiquette -- the accepted polite rules of behaviour in a group or situation
"Email etiquette is different from one country to another." (Etiqueta / boas maneiras)
Norm -- a usual, accepted way of behaving in a group or culture
"Using first names at once is the norm in some teams." (Norma / padrão)
Adapt -- to change your message or behaviour to fit the situation or listener
"A good communicator can adapt the message to each culture." (Adaptar)
Misunderstanding -- when people understand a message in different ways, often by mistake
"To avoid a misunderstanding, I repeat the key point clearly." (Mal-entendido)
Context -- the whole situation around a message that helps explain its meaning
"The same words can mean different things in a different context." (Contexto)
Sensitivity -- the ability to notice and respect other people's feelings and customs
"Cultural sensitivity helps you build trust with a global team." (Sensibilidade)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Patricia works with a global research team at Abbott. She knows that the science is the same everywhere, but communication is not. She reads the directness of each colleague: her German colleagues say exactly what they think, while her colleagues in Japan are more indirect, so "it may be a little difficult" often means no. She respects the etiquette of each team and follows the local norm for names. When the meaning is not clear, she remembers that the same words can mean different things in a different cultural context, so she confirms understanding to avoid a misunderstanding. Most of all, she shows sensitivity: she assumes good intentions and is happy to adapt her message to the listener.

1. What does Patricia do about each colleague's directness?
A She treats everyone exactly the same.
B She reads it and adapts to each culture.
C She ignores cultural differences.
2. From the Japan team, what does "it may be a little difficult" often mean?
A It is a polite way of saying no.
B It means yes, no problem.
C It means they are angry.
3. How does Patricia avoid a misunderstanding?
A She says nothing at all.
C She speaks faster.
B She confirms understanding and adapts her message.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Softening across cultures

GRAMMAR

Para ser educado numa equipe global, suavizamos a fala com palavras tentativas (perhaps, could, may, might, a little) e perguntas educadas. / To be polite in a global team, we soften our message with tentative words (perhaps, could, may, might, a little) and polite questions.

Function / FunçãoDirectSofter / Mais educado
SuggestWe should redo it."Perhaps we could redo it."
RefuseNo, impossible."I'm afraid that may be a little difficult."
RequestMove the deadline."Would it be possible to move the deadline?"
ConfirmYou said yes."Just to make sure I understand, do you mean...?"

Key point / Ponto-chave: Em uma equipe global, suavize a mensagem com perhaps, could, may, might e a little, e confirme o entendimento (Just to make sure...) para mostrar sensitivity e evitar um misunderstanding. Adapte-se ao context: com colegas muito diretos, você também pode ser mais direto.
