Lesson 14 -- Cause, Effect & Association

Documentary
Veritasium (YouTube) -- "Is Most Published Research Wrong?"

A clear, engaging look at why association is not causation, what a confounding parameter is, and why a conclusive result still needs a controlled study before we attribute an endpoint to a cause.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down three sentences using "association", "causation" and "confounding parameter", and one first conditional (If we change X, then Y will...).

Podcast
More or Less: Behind the Stats (BBC) -- any "association vs causation" episode

Journalistic breakdowns of real headlines that confuse association with causation, showing how a hidden parameter can explain a surprising endpoint.

Record a 2-minute explanation of one example: describe the association, name a possible confounding parameter, and say why you cannot claim causation yet.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The Grammar Gameshow: Conditionals"

A practical review of the zero, first and second conditionals -- exactly the structures you use to express cause and effect when describing what happens, what will happen and what would happen.

After watching, write six conditional sentences about your own research: two zero (general truths), two first (real future), and two second (hypothetical).
