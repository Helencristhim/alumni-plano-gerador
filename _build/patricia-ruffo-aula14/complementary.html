Lesson 14 -- Cause, Effect & Correlation

Documentary
Connected (Netflix) -- "Surveillance" or any data-focused episode

Watch how a science documentary explains links in data: where there is a correlation, where there may be causation, and which variables could confound a result.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two claims and decide: is each one a correlation or real causation? Note any confounding variable.

Podcast
Freakonomics Radio -- any episode on "correlation vs causation"

A clear, accessible discussion of why two things that move together do not prove that one causes the other, and how hidden variables can confound a finding.

Record a 2-minute summary of one example from the episode: state the correlation, name the confounding variable, and use a first conditional to say how it could be tested.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The first conditional"

A clear lesson on forming the first conditional: if + present for the cause, will + verb for the result, and why we never use will after if.

After watching, write six first-conditional sentences about your own research, using correlation, variable, outcome, threshold, significant and attribute.
