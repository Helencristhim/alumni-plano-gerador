Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Menu -- the list of food and drinks a restaurant offers
"The waiter handed us the menu and we looked at the dishes." (Cardápio)
Starter -- the first, small dish of a meal, before the main course
"For a starter, I will have the tomato soup." (Entrada)
Main course -- the biggest, central dish of a meal
"For my main course, I will have the grilled fish." (Prato principal)
Dessert -- the sweet dish at the end of a meal
"For dessert, would you like some chocolate cake?" (Sobremesa)
Waiter -- the person who serves food and drinks at a restaurant
"The waiter was very polite and brought us some water." (Garçom)
Order -- to tell the waiter what food and drinks you want
"Are you ready to order, or do you need more time?" (Pedir / Fazer o pedido)
Recommend -- to tell someone something is good and they should try it
"I recommend the grilled fish; it is the best dish here." (Recomendar)
Dish -- one specific item of prepared food on the menu
"This dish is a local speciality from Santos." (Prato)
Bill -- the paper that shows how much you have to pay
"As the host, I quietly asked the waiter for the bill." (Conta)
Tip -- extra money you leave for good service
"The service was great, so we left a generous tip." (Gorjeta)
Spicy -- hot, with a strong, peppery taste
"My guest does not like spicy food, so I avoided the chili." (Apimentado)
Courteous -- polite and respectful in the way you treat people
"A courteous host always thanks the waiter." (Cortês / Educado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the short text and answer the questions.

Walyson takes his client Clara to dinner at a restaurant in Santos. As a courteous host, he welcomes her, gives her the menu, and helps her choose. Clara does not eat red meat, so Walyson says: "I would recommend the grilled fish; it is a lovely local dish and it is not spicy." Clara orders the tomato soup as a starter and the fish as a main course, but she skips dessert. Walyson speaks to the waiter politely: instead of "Bring us water," he says "Could you bring us some water?" and "Would you mind taking our order?" At the end of the meal, Walyson quietly asks for the bill and leaves a generous tip, because he is the host.

1. Why does Walyson recommend the grilled fish to Clara?
A Because she does not eat red meat and it is not spicy.
B Because it is the cheapest dish.
C Because she asked for red meat.
2. How does Walyson speak to the waiter?
A "Bring us water now."
B "Could you bring us some water?" and "Would you mind taking our order?"
C He does not speak to the waiter.
3. What does Walyson do at the end of the meal?
A He lets Clara pay the bill.
C He orders another dessert.
B He quietly asks for the bill and leaves a tip.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite requests (Could you / Would you mind)

GRAMMAR

Para fazer pedidos educados (a um garçom, a um cliente), use COULD YOU + verbo na forma base (Could you bring...?) ou WOULD YOU MIND + verbo no -ing (Would you mind bringing...?). Pedidos diretos como "Bring water" soam como uma ordem. / To make polite requests, use COULD YOU + base verb, or WOULD YOU MIND + the -ing form. Direct requests like "Bring water" sound like an order.

Direct / Direto (an order)Polite / Educado
"Bring us water.""Could you bring us some water?"
"Give me the menu.""Would you mind giving me the menu?"
"Take the fish.""I would recommend the fish."

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de Could you use o verbo na forma base (Could you bring...?); depois de Would you mind use a forma -ing (Would you mind bringing...?). Para recomendar um prato, diga "I would recommend the...". / After Could you, use the base verb; after Would you mind, use the -ing form. To suggest a dish, say "I would recommend the..."
