Chapter 3 -- The Polite Code

Grammar Discovery

Could you...? & Would you mind + -ing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which requests sound courteous, not like orders?

"Could you bring us some water, please?" (polite: Could you + base verb)

"Would you mind bringing the menu?" (very polite: Would you mind + -ing)

"Bring us the bill." (direct: sounds like an order)

"I would recommend the grilled fish." (recommending a dish)

Grammar Rule

Polite Requests

Direct (sounds like an order)Polite request
"Bring us water.""Could you bring us some water?"
"Give me the menu.""Would you mind giving me the menu?"
"Take the fish.""I would recommend the fish."

Key idea: after Could you, use the base verb (Could you bring...?). After Would you mind, use the -ing form (Would you mind bringing...?). To suggest a dish, say "I would recommend the..." These polite forms make you sound courteous with the waiter and your guest.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Would you mind bring the bill?"
"Would you mind bringing the bill?"

After Could you, use the base verb (bring). After Would you mind, use the -ing form (bringing). Both are polite ways to ask.

Grammar Practice

Make it Polite

Direct: "Bring us some bread." Polite: "Could you _____bring us some bread?" (base verb)
Direct: "Give me the menu." Polite: "Would you mind _____giving me the menu?" (-ing form)
Direct: "Bring the bill." Polite: "Would you mind _____bringing the bill?" (-ing form)
Suggesting a dish: "I would _____recommend the grilled fish." (recommend)
