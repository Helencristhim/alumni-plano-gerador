Lesson 18 -- Making Plans Together

Series
Extra English -- Episode 5 (the team plan)

A simple comedy made for English learners. The friends plan an activity in slow, clear English -- I want to..., We need to choose..., What do you want to do? Perfect for A1 listening about making plans with want to and need to.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every sentence with "want to" or "need to" that you hear. Then say out loud: "I want to..." and "We need to...".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "Making Plans with Want and Need"

A short, friendly lesson on planning: how to use "want to" for what you wish and "need to" for what is necessary, like "I want to plan a lunch" and "We need to choose a date". The hosts explain the structure slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then say one thing you want to do and one thing you need to do for your team.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Want to and Need to"

A clear video that teaches planning vocabulary (idea, decide, together, goal, choose, suggestion) and how to make plans, like "I want to organize a lunch" and "We need to choose a place", with pictures and simple sentences.

After watching, record a short voice message for your team: say one thing you want to do and two things the team needs to do.
