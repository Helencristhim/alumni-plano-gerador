Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Want to and need to + verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we say our plans?

"I want to plan a team lunch."

"We need to choose a date."

"She wants to help the team."

"I do not want to wait."

Grammar Rule

Want to / Need to + Verb

IdeaStructureExample
Desirewant to + verbI want to plan a lunch.
Necessaryneed to + verbWe need to choose a date.
He / Shewants to / needs to + verbShe wants to help.
Negativedo not / does not want toI do not want to wait.

Key idea: use want to for a desire and need to for something necessary, both with the base verb. With he or she, add an S: wants to, needs to. To say no, use do not / does not: I do not want to.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want choose a place."
"I want to choose a place."

Always use to + base verb after want and need. Never say "I want choose" -- say "I want to choose".
