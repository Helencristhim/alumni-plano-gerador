Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Reliable -- someone you can always trust to do their job
"My scrub nurse is the most reliable person on the team." (Confiável)
Skilled -- very good at a particular job or task
"She is more skilled than me with the robotic arms." (Habilidoso)
Supportive -- ready to help and encourage other people
"A good leader is supportive when the team feels nervous." (Solidário)
Confident -- sure about your own ability
"He is more confident now than in his first year." (Confiante)
Efficient -- working well and fast, without wasting time
"The new schedule made our team more efficient." (Eficiente)
Calm -- relaxed and not nervous, even under pressure
"In an emergency, she is calmer than anyone else." (Calmo)
Detail-oriented -- careful about small but important things
"A detail-oriented nurse checks every instrument twice." (Atento aos detalhes)
Experienced -- someone who has done something for a long time
"He is more experienced than the rest of the team." (Experiente)
Hardworking -- putting a lot of effort into the work
"My team is hardworking and never gives up." (Esforçado)
Easygoing -- relaxed and pleasant to work with
"Our new resident is friendly and easygoing." (Tranquilo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia is describing her surgical team. "Mariana, my scrub nurse, is the most detail-oriented person I know," she says. "She is more reliable than anyone on the floor." Pedro is the youngest resident. "He is less experienced than the rest of us, but he is incredibly hardworking, and he is more confident every week." Carla, the anesthesiologist, is the calmest member of the team. "In an emergency, nobody stays calmer than her." Andreia adds, "I am more efficient when I work with them, because they are supportive and easygoing. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

GRAMMAR

Usado para comparar pessoas e equipes. / Used to compare people and teams.

Form / FormaRule / RegraExample / Exemplo
Short adjective + er + than1 sílaba: + er + thanShe is calmer than me.
Adjective ending in -yy vira i + er: easy → easier, busy → busierToday is busier than yesterday.
more + long adjective + than2+ sílabas: more ... thanHe is more reliable than the others.
the + est / the most + adjectiveSuperlativo: o mais de todosShe is the most detail-oriented.
Irregulargood → better, bad → worse, far → furtherTeamwork is better than working alone.

Key point / Ponto-chave: nunca use "more" com adjetivo curto que já tem -er. Não diga "more calmer" nem "more better" -- diga apenas calmer e better. E não esqueça o than ao comparar duas pessoas.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best sentence for each situation.

You want to compare two colleagues using a short adjective. Which is correct?
A "Mariana is calmer than Pedro."
B "Mariana is more calmer than Pedro."
C "Mariana is calm than Pedro."
You want to compare two people with a long adjective. Which is best?
A "She is more experienced than me."
B "She is experienceder than me."
C "She is more experienced me."
Which sentence uses the irregular comparative correctly?
A "Teamwork is more better than working alone."
B "Teamwork is better than working alone."
