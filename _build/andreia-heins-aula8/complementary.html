Lesson 8 -- Describing People and Teams

Series
Grey's Anatomy -- Season 1, Episode 1: "A Hard Day's Night"

A surgical team where every member has different strengths: one is more skilled, one is calmer under pressure, one is more experienced. Listen for how the characters describe and compare each other.

Watch with English subtitles. Write three comparative sentences about the characters (more skilled than, calmer than, more confident than) and one superlative (the most reliable, the calmest). Then describe your own team the same way.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "What Makes a Great Team"

Leaders describe the people they work with: who is more reliable, who is more detail-oriented, who is the most supportive. Full of natural language for comparing colleagues at work.

Note three sentences that compare team members. Then record a short profile of two colleagues, using at least three new adjectives from today, such as reliable, supportive, and experienced.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "COMPARATIVES and SUPERLATIVES"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: short adjectives with -er than, long adjectives with more ... than, the spelling of -y adjectives, and the irregular forms better and worse.

After watching, write five sentences comparing the people on your team: three with -er than or more ... than, and two superlatives (the most ..., the best). Use skilled, calm, and reliable at least once each.
