Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Comparatives (calmer than, more reliable than) and superlatives (the most ...)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How does the adjective change when we compare?

"She is calmer than me."

"He is more reliable than the others."

"Today is busier than yesterday."

"Teamwork is better than working alone."

Grammar Rule

Comparatives & Superlatives

StructureExample
short adjective + er + thanShe is calmer than me.
adjective + y → ier + thanToday is busier than yesterday.
more + long adjective + thanHe is more reliable than the others.
the + est / the most + adjectiveShe is the calmest. He is the most reliable.
irregulargood → better, bad → worse, far → further

Key idea: never use more with a short adjective that already has -er. Say calmer, not more calmer. Say better, not more better. And do not forget than when you compare two people.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She is more calmer than me under pressure."
"She is calmer than me under pressure."

A short adjective takes -er (calmer). Use more only with long adjectives (more reliable). Never combine the two.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Sentences

"She is _____calmer than me." (calm -- short)
"He is _____more reliable than the others." (reliable -- long)
"Today is _____busier than yesterday." (busy -- ends in y)
"Teamwork is _____better than working alone." (good -- irregular)

I can use -er than with short adjectives (calmer than)
