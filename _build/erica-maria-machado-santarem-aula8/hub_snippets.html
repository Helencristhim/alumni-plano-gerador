Going Places -- Directions and Transport

Peça e dê direções: bus, station, map, corner, street, left, right, straight. Gramática: imperativos (Turn left, Go straight, Take the bus) e preposições de lugar (next to, in front of, behind). Aprenda a se localizar e a ajudar alguém a chegar.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Oito palavras novas para direções e transporte. Ouça cada uma e repita. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Bus -- ônibus — a big vehicle that takes many people on the street
"I take the bus to work."
Station -- estação — the place where you take a bus or a train
"The bus station is near my office."
Map -- mapa — a drawing that shows streets, places and the way
"I look at the map on my phone."
Corner -- esquina — the place where two streets meet
"The pharmacy is on the corner."
Street -- rua — a road in a city, with houses and shops
"My office is on a busy street."
Left -- esquerda — the side that is the opposite of right
"Turn left at the corner."
Right -- direita — the side that is the opposite of left
"Turn right at the station."
Straight -- em frente — forward, not to the left and not to the right
"Go straight for two streets."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. Veja os imperativos e as preposições em ação.

It is easy to come to my office! First, take the bus number five on your street. Go straight to the big station. Then turn left at the corner, next to the bank. My building is on the right, in front of a small cafe. Look at the map if you are lost. You can not miss it!

1. "Take the bus." Why is there no subject (no "you")?
A Because it is an imperative (an order or instruction).
B Because it is a question.
C Because it is a negative sentence.
2. "My building is in front of a cafe." What does "in front of" tell us?
A The time.
B Where the building is (the place).
C How many buses there are.
3. Where do you turn left?
A At the station.
B At the corner, next to the bank.
C On the bus.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Imperatives & Prepositions of Place

GRAMMAR

O imperativo é uma ordem ou instrução: usamos só o verbo, sem sujeito (Turn, Go, Take). As preposições de lugar dizem ONDE algo está. / The imperative is an order or instruction: we use only the verb, with no subject (Turn, Go, Take). Prepositions of place tell us WHERE something is.

FerramentaExemplo
imperativo (ordem)Turn left, Go straight, Take the bus — vire, siga, pegue (sem sujeito)
next to / nearthe bank is next to the station — o banco fica ao lado da estação
in front of / behindthe cafe is in front of the office — o café fica em frente ao escritório
on the cornerthe shop is on the corner — a loja fica na esquina

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

You want to tell someone to turn. Which is correct?
A "Turn to left at the corner."
B "Turn left at the corner."
C "You turn to the left corner."
A tourist asks for the station. You say:
A "Go straight and turn right at the corner."
B "Go to straight and turn to right."
C "You going straight the corner."
You describe where the cafe is. Which is correct?
A "The cafe is in front the bank."
B "The cafe is in front of the bank."
C "The cafe is front of the bank."
You say the office is beside the station. Which is correct?
A "The office is next the station."
B "The office is next to the station."
C "The office is next of the station."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você respondendo. Não há resposta certa ou errada.

Explain the way from your home to your office. What transport do you take? Which streets and corners do you pass? Where do you turn left or right? Use imperatives (Turn, Go, Take) and prepositions of place (next to, in front of). Take your time and speak calmly.
