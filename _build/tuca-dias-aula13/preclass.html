Cash -- paper money and coins, not a card or a check

Withdraw -- to take money out of your bank account
"I need to withdraw two hundred dollars in cash." (Sacar)
Exchange rate -- how much one currency is worth in another currency
"The exchange rate today is five reais to one dollar." (Taxa de câmbio)
Currency -- the type of money a country uses, like the dollar or the euro
"The dollar is the currency of the United States." (Moeda)
ATM -- a cash machine where you take out money with your card
"There is an ATM in the airport hall." (Caixa eletrônico)
Fee -- extra money you pay for a service, like changing money
"The bank charges a small fee for every exchange." (Taxa / tarifa)
Receipt -- a paper or message that shows what you paid and when
"Keep your receipt for every transaction." (Recibo / comprovante)
Balance -- the amount of money you have in your account right now
"I checked my balance online before I paid." (Saldo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca is planning a trip and thinking about money. She dreams a little: "If I had more time and money, I would travel to Europe." This is the second conditional: if + past simple, would + base verb, for an imaginary or unlikely situation. The past here does not mean past time -- it shows the idea is imaginary. A friend gives advice: "If I were you, I would use a card and carry only a little cash." With the verb to be, we often use were for all persons. Compare with last class: the first conditional (if + present, will) is about a real possibility -- "If I have cash, I will pay now" -- while the second conditional is about a dream or a what-if. Be careful: in the if part, use the past, not would: "if I had", not "if I would have".

1. "If I had more money, I would travel." Is this real or imaginary now?
A Real -- she has the money now
B Imaginary -- she does not have the money now
C It happened in the past
2. "If I were you, I would use a card." What does this express?
A Polite advice in an imaginary case
B A real plan for tonight
C Something that already happened
3. In the if part of the second conditional, which form do we use?
A would + base verb ("if I would have")
B the past simple ("if I had")
C the present simple ("if I have")

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Second Conditional (If + Past, Would)

GRAMMAR

Como falar de situações imaginárias e dar conselhos educados. / How to talk about imaginary situations and give polite advice.

StructureUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
If + past, would + basesituação imaginária ou pouco provável agora / an imaginary or unlikely situation now"If I had more time, I would travel."
If I were you, would + baseconselho educado em caso imaginário / polite advice in an imaginary case"If I were you, I would use a card."
Compare: first conditionalpossibilidade real (aula passada) / a real possibility (last class)"If I have cash, I will pay."

Key point / Ponto-chave: o passado na parte do if não é tempo passado -- mostra que a situação é imaginária: "if I had", não "if I would have". Após would, use o verbo na forma base: would travel. Com to be, use were para todas as pessoas: "if I were you". / The past in the if part signals an imaginary idea, not past time.
