Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have 62 years old."
"I am 62 years old."
"Yesterday I meet a colleague from Japan."
"Yesterday I met a colleague from Japan."
"I am agree with your opinion."
"I agree with your opinion."

Three habits to watch: age is to be ("I am 62"), the past changes the verb ("met", not "meet"), and agree is already a verb ("I agree", never "I am agree").
