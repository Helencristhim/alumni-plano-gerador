Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

Mid-Program Review + Real Talk -- Consolidating Fluency

A mid-program review: recycling your fluency and running real conversations under pressure. The focus is five discourse markers that keep you fluent and organized -- "To give you a bit of context...", "What I mean is...", "The reason I bring this up is...", "That said...", "Having said that...". New words: articulate, elaborate, clarify, outline, follow up, debrief, wrap up, agenda, takeaway, action item.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Articulate -- to express your ideas clearly and effectively in words
"She can articulate a complex plan in a few sentences."
Elaborate -- to add more detail or explanation to something you said
"Could you elaborate on the timeline?"
Clarify -- to make something clearer or easier to understand
"Let me clarify what I mean."
Outline -- to give the main points of something, without all the details
"I will outline the plan in three steps."
Follow up -- to take further action after a meeting or message
"I will follow up with an email tomorrow."
Debrief -- to review and discuss something after it happens, to learn from it
"Let us debrief after the call."
Wrap up -- to finish or conclude a meeting or conversation
"Let us wrap up with the next steps."
Agenda -- the list of topics to be discussed in a meeting
"The first item on the agenda is the budget."
Takeaway -- the main point or lesson you keep from a discussion
"The main takeaway is that quality matters."
Action item -- a specific task someone must do after a meeting
"Your action item is to send the proposal."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane is on a video call with a technology partner for her new clinic. To give you a bit of context, she begins, she is a gynecologist opening a fertility clinic in São Paulo. The reason I bring this up is that she does not want just a supplier; she wants a real partner. What I mean is, she needs reliable equipment and fast support. When the partner mentions the high price, she stays polite. That said, she explains, quality matters more to her than the lowest price. Having said that, she knows the budget is not unlimited. Before they finish, she asks the partner to clarify the timeline, to outline the next steps, and to follow up with a written proposal. They wrap up the call by agreeing on the action items.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Discourse Markers

GRAMMAR

Discourse markers are short, fixed phrases that organize your speech and buy you thinking time. They are not grammar rules -- they are fluency tools. / Marcadores do discurso são frases curtas e fixas que organizam sua fala e ganham tempo para pensar. Não são regras gramaticais -- são ferramentas de fluência.

MarkerFunction / FunçãoExample
To give you a bit of context...Open a topic, set the scene / Abrir um tema"To give you a bit of context, we open in March."
What I mean is...Clarify or rephrase / Esclarecer"What I mean is, I need fast support."
The reason I bring this up is...Explain why it matters / Explicar por que importa"The reason I bring this up is that I want a partner."
That said... / Having said that...Soften a contrast / Suavizar um contraste"That said, the budget is limited."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question below. There is no right or wrong answer.

Imagine you are on a video call with a technology partner for your new clinic. In about two minutes, introduce yourself and your project, explain what you need in a partner, and handle one difficult moment (for example, the price). Use at least three discourse markers -- "To give you a bit of context...", "What I mean is...", "That said..." -- to stay fluent. Then wrap up with the action items. Speak naturally and do not stop to edit.
