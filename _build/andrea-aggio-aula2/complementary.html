A real family agency describes its portfolio, manages clients, and works on new deals every episode. It is your professional world in English — roles, routines, and projects in motion. Connection to Lesson 2: notice how they describe what they do (present simple) and what they are working on right now (present continuous).

"Present Simple vs Present Continuous" -- BBC Learning English

A clear, practical lesson on today's exact contrast: permanent facts and routines (present simple) versus temporary things happening around now (present continuous). Connection to Lesson 2: the grammar that lets you describe your role and your current projects without hesitating.

Tip: pause after each example and say one sentence about your own work — one in the present simple, one in the present continuous.
