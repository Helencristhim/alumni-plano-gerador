Chapter 4: The Code

Always vs Right Now

Present simple · Present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I manage a team of eight people."

"We work with hotels all over Europe."

"Right now, we are expanding our hotel portfolio."

"This month, I am working on a new partnership."

Why green for what is always true, but blue for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
verb (he/she + s)		Permanent facts, your role, and routines (every year, usually, always)I manage the commercial team.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + -ing		Temporary things happening now or around now (right now, at the moment, this season)Right now, we are expanding the portfolio.
Signal wordsSimple: every day, usually, always · Continuous: right now, at the moment, currently, this week

Stative verbs (know, want, be) stay simple: "I know many hoteliers," never "I am knowing."

Language Focus

Simple or Continuous?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am knowing many hoteliers."

✓ RIGHT

"I know many hoteliers."

✗ WRONG

"Right now I work on a new partnership."

✓ RIGHT

"Right now I am working on a new partnership."

Stative verbs (know, want, be) stay in the simple. For a temporary project happening now, use am/is/are + -ing.

Reconnecting with a Partner

Andrea! It is so good to see you again. How are things at Escape Turismo?
A
Marco! Great to see you too. Things are busy — right now we are expanding our hotel portfolio in southern Europe.
M
That is exciting. I run a tour operator in Rome, and this season we are growing fast too.
A
Wonderful. Tell me, what does your company focus on these days?
M
We handle incoming tourism to Italy. At the moment, we are opening three new hotels in the south.
A
That is perfect timing. I manage our European itineraries, and I am always looking for strong local partners.
M
Then we should talk. I coordinate all our hotel deals, so I can be your direct contact.
A
I would love that. Let us set up a meeting and see how our teams can work together.
