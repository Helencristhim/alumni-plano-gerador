Lesson 17 -- Leading the Handover & Documenting the Process

Series
"The Office" -- onboarding and handover episodes

Watch scenes where a manager explains how the office works to a new hire. Notice how the clearest explanations focus on the process, not the person: "the form is filled out", "the order is approved".

Watch with English subtitles. Pick one scene and rewrite three steps in the passive voice: present (is documented) and past (was approved).

Podcast
Harvard Business Review -- "Onboarding New Hires the Right Way"

A practical guide to documenting a process so a team can run without one person. Listen for how the passive voice is used to describe steps clearly and neutrally.

Record a 90-second handover of one process you own. Explain each step in the passive, add one past result in the past passive, and use by to name who maintains it.

YouTube
"The Passive Voice in English -- When and How to Use It"

A clear lesson on be + past participle, present and past, and when to add by + the agent. Directly reinforces today's grammar with business examples.

After watching, take five active sentences about your work ("We test the build") and turn each one into the passive ("The build is tested"). Read them out loud.
