Chapter 3 -- Three Tenses

Grammar Discovery

Present Simple, Present Continuous, Past Simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

When does each action happen: every day, right now, or in the past?

"Every day I study English at home."

"Right now I am reviewing everything I learned."

"Last week I learned many new words."

"Two weeks ago I read a book in English."

Grammar Rule

Three Tenses Side by Side

TenseUseSignal wordsExample
Present Simplehabits, routinesevery day, usuallyI study every day.
Present Continuousactions nowright now, at the momentI am studying right now.
Past Simplefinished actionsyesterday, last weekI studied yesterday.

Key idea: the signal words tell you the tense. Every day = Present Simple. Right now = Present Continuous. Yesterday or last week = Past Simple. Remember that some past verbs are irregular: go becomes went, eat becomes ate.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Yesterday I am studying English."
"Yesterday I studied English."

The signal word yesterday shows a finished action, so we use the Past Simple: studied. Use the Present Continuous only for actions happening right now.
