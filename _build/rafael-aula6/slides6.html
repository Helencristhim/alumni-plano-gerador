Grammar Recap 1

Routines & Facts

"Rafael works at Grupo Picunha."
"The company operates in two industries."
"He always wakes up early."
"I rarely work overtime."
UseForm
I/You/We/Theyverb (base form): I work
He/She/Itverb + s: He works
Frequency adverbbefore the verb: I always work / He rarely works
Negative / Questiondon't / doesn't + verb · Do / Does + verb?
Grammar Recap 2

Requests & Descriptions

ToolUseExample
Canability / informal request"Can we arrange a meeting?"
Couldpolite request / offer"Could you send me an email?"
There isone thing (singular)"There is a conference room."
There aremore things (plural)"There are three meeting rooms."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"When I woke up, I take my son to school."
"When I wake up, I take my son to school."
"Does the company operates in agriculture?"
"Does the company operate in agriculture?"

Present Simple for routines · base form after "does"

Grammar Practice

Complete the Sentences

"Our company ___operates in two industries." (fact)
"I ___usually attend meetings in the morning." (frequency)
"___Could you send me an email, please?" (polite request)
"___There are three meeting rooms on this floor." (plural)
Spot the Error

Find the Mistake

"He don't work on weekends."
"He doesn't work on weekends." (doesn't for he/she/it)
"There is three meeting rooms on my floor."
"There are three meeting rooms on my floor." (plural → there are)
"Can you to send me the report?"
"Can you send me the report?" (no 'to' after can/could)
"I always am late for the commute."
"I am always late for the commute." (frequency adverb after 'to be')
