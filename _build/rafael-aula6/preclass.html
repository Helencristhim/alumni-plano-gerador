Grammar Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Leia o texto. As quatro estruturas das aulas 1-5 estão em negrito. Depois, responda as perguntas.

Today an international partner, Sarah, visits Grupo Picunha. Rafael is a director and he manages the legal department. The company operates in two industries, and its revenue comes from both divisions. Rafael usually attends meetings in the morning, and he rarely works overtime.

When Sarah arrives, Rafael says: "Could you tell me the topics for the meeting?" and "Can we arrange it for the afternoon?" Then he gives a tour. "There is a conference room next to my office," he says, "and there are three meeting rooms on this floor. There is a large parking lot behind the building."
1. How often does Rafael work overtime?
A Always
B Rarely
C Every weekend
2. Which sentence is a polite request?
A "The company operates in two industries."
B "Could you tell me the topics for the meeting?"
C "There are three meeting rooms."
3. Why does Rafael say "There are three meeting rooms" (not "There is")?
A Because "meeting rooms" is plural
B Because it is a question
C Because it is in the past

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

As Quatro Ferramentas (Lessons 1-5)
TOOL USO EXEMPLO
Present SimpleRotinas e fatos
(he/she/it + verbo + s)		He works. / She manages.
Frequency adverbsCom que frequência
(antes do verbo)		I usually work. / He rarely travels.
Can / CouldPedidos
(Could = mais formal)		Could you help me? / Can we meet?

(is = singular, are = plural)		There is a room. / There are rooms.

Quiz Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação profissional.

A partner asks what your company does. What do you say?
A "We operated in iron and agriculture." (past)
B "We operate in two industries: iron manufacturing and agriculture."
C "We are operate in iron."
You want to politely ask a partner to send a document. What do you say?
A "Could you send me the document, please?"
B "You send me the document now."
C "Send document you can?"
A visitor asks about meeting rooms on your floor. What do you say?
A "There is three meeting rooms."
B "There are three meeting rooms on this floor."
C "It has three meeting rooms."
How do you correctly describe your morning routine?
A "When I woke up, I take my son to school." (past for routine)
B "I usually wake up early and take my son to school."
C "I am wake up early every day."
