Chapter 1: When Things Go Wrong

Stay Calm, Get Help

Last class you learned to make a call and take a message. Sometimes that call is to ask for help -- you lose your wallet, you feel sick, or you need the police. On a trip, small problems happen. The skill is to stay calm and explain clearly: what happened, where, and what you need.

In one word: how do you usually feel in a stressful moment?

Chapter 3: Reporting a Problem

Say What Just Happened

Report · ask for help · describe the problem

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Someone has taken my wallet!"

"I've lost my passport."

"I need to report it to the police."

"There's something wrong with my phone."

FunctionPhraseExample
Report recent newshas / have + past participleSomeone has taken my bag.
Report your own lossI've + past participleI've lost my passport.
Say what you needI need to + base verbI need to report it.
Describe a problemThere's something wrong with...There's something wrong with my key.

Use has/have + past participle to report recent news (lost, taken, stolen), and I need to + base verb to say what you want to do next.

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Someone taken my wallet."

✓ RIGHT

"Someone has taken my wallet."

✗ WRONG

"I need to reporting it."

Chapter 4: On the Scene

Reporting to the Police

Pricila reports a stolen wallet

Dialogue

Pricila Reports a Theft

P
Excuse me, officer. Could you help me, please? Someone has taken my wallet.
O
I'm sorry to hear that. Stay calm. Where and when did it happen?
P
About ten minutes ago, near the train station. I need to report it before I cancel my cards.
O
Of course. Can you describe the wallet? And was there a witness nearby?
P
It's small and brown, with my ID inside. A woman saw it -- she's still over there.
O
Thank you. I'll fill in a report and give you a copy for your insurance. You did the right thing.
