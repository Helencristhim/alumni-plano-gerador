Stage 1.1: Emergency Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These words help you ask for help and handle emergencies.

An emergency -- uma emergencia
"In an emergency, dial one-one-two in Europe."
Missing -- perdido / desaparecido
"My passport is missing -- I can't find it."
To report -- reportar / registrar
"I'd like to report a stolen bag."
Stolen -- roubado
"My phone was stolen on the bus."
A pharmacy -- uma farmacia
"Is there a pharmacy near here? I need some medicine."
To faint -- desmaiar
"It was so hot that a man fainted in the queue."
First aid -- primeiros socorros
"The hotel staff gave him first aid until help came."
A wallet -- uma carteira
"I left my wallet in the taxi by mistake."
A witness -- uma testemunha
"A witness saw the man take the bag and run."
To break down -- quebrar / parar de funcionar
"Our rental car broke down on the highway."
To call for help -- pedir socorro / chamar ajuda
"She called for help, and a guard came at once."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the report and answer the questions.

Pricila walks up to a police officer near the station. "Excuse me, could you help me, please? Someone has taken my wallet." The officer stays calm. "Where did it happen?" Pricila answers, "About ten minutes ago, near the station. I need to report it before I cancel my cards." The officer asks her to describe it and asks if there was a witness. "There's something wrong -- my ID was inside too," Pricila adds. The officer fills in a report and gives her a copy for her insurance.

1. What has happened to Pricila?
A Someone has taken her wallet.
B She has lost her phone.
C Her car has broken down.
2. What does Pricila need to do before she cancels her cards?
A Find a pharmacy.
B Report it to the police.
C Call her hotel.
3. What does the officer give Pricila?
A A new wallet.
B Some medicine.
C A copy of the report for her insurance.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reporting Problems & Asking for Help

Grammar

Para reportar uma noticia recente (algo que acabou de acontecer), use "has/have" + participio passado (Someone has taken my bag / I've lost my passport). Para dizer o que voce precisa fazer, use "I need to" + verbo na forma base (I need to report it). Para descrever um problema, use "There's something wrong with..." + objeto. / To report recent news, use "has/have + past participle". To say what you need, use "I need to + base verb". To describe a problem, use "There's something wrong with...".

Funcao / FunctionEnglishPortugues
Report recent newsSomeone has taken my wallet.Alguem roubou minha carteira.
Report your own lossI've lost my passport.Eu perdi meu passaporte.
Say what you needI need to report it.Eu preciso reportar isso.
Describe a problemThere's something wrong with my phone.Tem algo errado com meu telefone.
Ask for helpCould you help me, please?Voce poderia me ajudar, por favor?

Quiz

A "My bag, gone, you fix."
B "Excuse me, someone has taken my bag. I need to report it."
C "Where is my bag now?"
You can't find your passport at the hotel. What do you say?
A "Could you help me, please? I've lost my passport."
B "Passport no. You give me."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Imagine you are on a trip and your wallet has just been stolen near a station. Go to a police officer. Stay calm and report it: say what has happened, where it happened, and what you need. Use at least two emergency phrases. Take your time.
