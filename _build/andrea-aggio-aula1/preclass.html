Reactivation Diagnostic -- Who Are You in English?

Reactivating your English at an international tourism fair. Key words: hospitality, trade fair, hotelier, supplier, itinerary, booking, venue, destination, to network, lead, to reach out, to follow up. Structures: present perfect (experience & duration with for/since) vs present simple (current role) vs past simple (finished time).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Hospitality -- the business of hotels and welcoming guests (hotelaria, hospitalidade)
"I have worked in hospitality for over twenty years."
Trade fair -- a big event where companies show their services (feira do setor)
"Every year, I go to two international trade fairs."
Hotelier -- a person who owns or manages a hotel (hoteleiro)
"I met a hotelier from Munich at the fair."
Supplier -- a company that provides goods or services (fornecedor)
"We are looking for a reliable local supplier."
Itinerary -- a detailed plan of a trip (roteiro de viagem)
"These hotels would be perfect for our itineraries."
Booking -- a reservation for a room or a tour (reserva)
"We can talk about bookings for next season."
Venue -- a place where an event is held (local do evento)
"We have just opened two new venues on the coast."
Destination -- a place that people travel to (destino)
"Right now, we are expanding our European destinations."
To network -- to meet people and build relationships (fazer contatos, networking)
"At the fair, I network with hoteliers worldwide."
Lead -- a possible new client or opportunity (contato, oportunidade de negócio)
"I came back from Berlin with ten strong leads."
To reach out -- to contact someone to start a conversation (entrar em contato)
"Please reach out whenever you have a moment."
To follow up -- to contact someone again after a meeting (dar sequência, retomar contato)
"I will follow up next week to talk about the details."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Andrea is the Commercial Director at Escape Turismo, a travel agency in Brazil. She runs the commercial team and works with hoteliers and suppliers around the world. "I have worked in hospitality for over twenty years," she says. When she was young, she lived in San Diego for two months, but then she stopped using English for a long time. Now she is back. Every year, she goes to two international trade fairs to network and find new leads. Last week, at the fair in Berlin, she met a hotelier from Munich. Right now, her team is expanding their European destinations, and they have just signed two new venues on the coast. Next week, Andrea will reach out to follow up and talk about bookings.

1. Por que dizemos "I have worked in hospitality for over twenty years"?
A Porque a experiência começou no passado e continua até agora (present perfect + for).
B Porque a ação terminou e não tem mais ligação com o presente.
C Porque é uma rotina que ela faz toda semana.
2. "When she was young, she lived in San Diego" usa o past simple porque:
A A experiência continua até hoje.
B É uma ação terminada, num tempo terminado do passado.
C É algo que está acontecendo agora.
3. Which sentence about her current role is correct?
A "She has run the commercial team since twenty years."
B "She runs the commercial team at Escape Turismo."
C "She is running the commercial team every day."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Present Simple vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Como falar da sua carreira com o tempo verbal certo (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experiência e duração até agora. Experience and duration up to now (for / since).I have worked here for 20 years.
just + present perfectNotícia muito recente. Very recent news.We have just opened two venues.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Papel atual e rotina. Current role and routine.I run the commercial team.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		Ação terminada, tempo terminado. Finished action, finished time (in 2005).I lived in San Diego in my twenties.
for vs sincefor + período (for 20 years) · since + ponto de partida (since 2004)
