Aula 06 -- Pre-class

Handling Pushback -- Disagreeing Professionally

Disagreeing with a stakeholder in a calm, professional way: the first conditional (If + present, ... will / may) and polite opinion frames (I see your point, but...; I am not sure I agree; Could we find a compromise?). New vocabulary: pushback, concern, trade-off, compromise, constraint, assumption, objection, align.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Pushback -- resistance or disagreement with a plan or idea
"The team gave some pushback on the new deadline." (Resistência / objeção)
Concern -- a worry about a possible problem
"I have a concern about the deadline on Friday." (Preocupação)
Trade-off -- a balance where you accept one loss to gain something else
"There is a trade-off between speed and quality." (Compensação / troca)
Compromise -- an agreement where each side gives up a little
"Could we find a compromise that protects the deadline?" (Meio-termo / acordo)
Constraint -- a limit that restricts what you can do
"Time is our main constraint this week." (Restrição / limitação)
Assumption -- something you accept as true without checking it
"My assumption is that the client wants it next month." (Suposição / premissa)
Objection -- a reason against a plan or an idea
"My objection is the timing, not the feature itself." (Objeção)
Align -- to bring people to agreement on a plan
"I want us to align before we call the client." (Alinhar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Rafael disagrees with a stakeholder, but he stays calm and professional. First, he sees her point: I see your point about the new feature. Then he raises a worry: but I have a concern about the deadline. He explains the consequence with a first conditional: if we add it now, we will miss the milestone. He makes his objection clear, but he does not just say no. Instead, he offers a compromise: if we move it to the next sprint, we will protect the deadline. He also names the trade-off with quality and the time constraint. Finally, he wants both sides to align before they call the client. A rude answer like "no, that is a bad idea" would break trust, so Rafael disagrees with respect.

1. Why does Rafael start with "I see your point"?
A To stay polite before he disagrees (opinion frame).
B To agree completely with the stakeholder.
C To end the conversation quickly.
2. How does he explain the consequence of adding the feature?
A "If we will add it, we miss the milestone."
B "If we add it now, we will miss the milestone."
C "Add it now and stop complaining."
3. Instead of just saying no, what does Rafael offer?
A A direct order.
B A complaint about the team.
C A compromise: move the feature to the next sprint.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional and Opinion Frames

GRAMMAR

Usamos a primeira condicional para falar de uma condição real e seu resultado. / We use the first conditional for a real condition and its result.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
If + present, ... will + verbResultado certoIf we add it, we will miss the deadline.
If + present, ... may + verbResultado possívelIf the vendor is late, we may need a backup.
I see your point, but...Discordar com educaçãoI see your point, but I have a concern.
Could we find a compromise?Propor um acordoCould we find a compromise on the date?

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de if, use o present simple (nunca will). O will ou may vai no resultado. Para discordar, abra com I see your point, but... e ofereça um compromise.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A stakeholder wants extra work in the sprint. The most professional reply is:
A "I see your point, but I have a concern about the deadline."
B "No. That is a bad idea."
C "Just do it my way."
You want to explain the consequence of a late vendor. The correct sentence is:
A "If the vendor will be late, we need a backup."
