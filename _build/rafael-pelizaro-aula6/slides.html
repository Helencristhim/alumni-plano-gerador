Handling Pushback — A Project Manager's Notes

Every IT project has a moment of pushback. On the Banco Atlas project, the product owner wanted a new login feature added to the current sprint. As the project manager, Rafael did not simply say no. He knew that good disagreement is not a fight; it is a calm, professional conversation. His goal was to raise his concern, explain the constraint, and still keep a strong relationship with the stakeholder.

Rafael started by seeing the product owner's point. Then he explained his objection: the timing, not the feature itself. "If we add this feature now, we will miss the milestone on Friday," he said. There was also a trade-off with quality, because the team would not have time to test it well. He used a first conditional to make the consequence clear and reasonable, instead of just refusing the idea.

Finally, Rafael offered a compromise. If the team moved the feature to the next sprint, they would protect the deadline and still deliver it. He justified the plan with clear reasons and asked the product owner to align before the client call. The pushback did not become a conflict. Instead, both sides reached a reasonable agreement — and that is the real skill of a project manager.

Adapted for class use from an internal IT project planning note

Chapter 4: The Code

The First Conditional

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"If we add this feature, we will miss the deadline."

"If the vendor is late, we may need a backup plan."

"If we cut the scope, the client will not be happy."

"If you agree, I will update the plan today."

If-clause (condition)Result clause
If + present simple: If we add this feature,will + base verb: we will miss the deadline.
If the vendor is late,may + base verb (possible): we may need a backup plan.

Use the first conditional for a real condition and its result: If + present simple, ... will / may + base verb. Use will for a certain result and may for a possible one. Pair it with polite frames: "I see your point, but..." and "I have a concern."

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

If we will add this feature, we will miss the deadline.
If we add this feature, we will miss the deadline.

After if, use the present simple, not will. The will or may belongs in the result clause.

I am agree with your concern.
I agree with your concern.

Agree is already a verb. Never use am / is / are with it: say "I agree", "I disagree".

Chapter 5: Into the Conversation

A Planning Call with Sandra

Dialogue

Disagreeing in the Call

S
Hi Rafael, I really want the login feature in this sprint. Can we add it now?
R
I see your point, Sandra, but I have a concern. If we add it now, we will miss the milestone on Friday.
S
That is a fair objection. But the client is waiting for it. What do you suggest?
R
As a compromise, if we move it to the next sprint, we will protect the deadline and still deliver it.
S
That is a reasonable trade-off. If you send a short summary, I will share it with the client today.
R
Perfect. I am glad we align on this. I will send the summary in one hour.
Comprehension

About Sandra

1. What does Sandra want at the start of the call?
She wants to add the login feature to this sprint.
2. How does Sandra react to Rafael's compromise?
She accepts it as a reasonable trade-off and will share it with the client.
3. What does Sandra ask Rafael to send?
A short summary, so they align before the client call.

Sandra's Reply

0:000:00
