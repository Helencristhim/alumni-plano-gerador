Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Pushback -- resistance or disagreement with a plan
"The team gave some pushback on the new deadline." (Resistência)
Concern -- a worry about a possible problem
"I have a concern about the deadline on Friday."
Objection -- a clear reason against a plan or idea
"My objection is the timing, not the feature itself." (Objeção)
Constraint -- a limit that restricts what you can do
"Time is our main constraint this week."
Trade-off -- accept one loss to gain something else
"There is a trade-off between speed and quality." (Compensação / troca)
Compromise -- an agreement where each side gives up a little
"As a compromise, we moved it to the next sprint."
Assumption -- something you accept as true without checking it
"My assumption is that the client wants it next month." (Suposição)
Align -- to bring people to the same plan or agreement
"I want us to align before we call the client."
Justify -- to give good reasons for a decision or position
"Let me justify my objection with the numbers." (Justificar)
Reasonable -- fair and sensible; not extreme
"That is a reasonable compromise for both sides."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The product owner gives Rafael some pushback: she wants a new login feature in the current sprint. Rafael does not say no. He raises a concern about the deadline. "If we add this feature now, we will miss the milestone on Friday," he writes. His objection is the timing, not the feature. He explains the constraint on time and the trade-off with quality. Then he offers a compromise: "If we move it to the next sprint, we will protect the deadline." He checks his assumption about the client, justifies the plan with clear reasons, and asks to align. In the end, both sides reach a reasonable agreement.

B He raises a concern and explains the consequence.
C He adds the feature without testing.
2. What is Rafael's objection?
A The timing of the feature, not the feature itself.
B The feature is a bad idea.
C The client is wrong.
3. What compromise does Rafael offer?
A To cancel the feature completely.
B To skip the testing phase.
C To move the feature to the next sprint to protect the deadline.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usamos a primeira condicional para falar de uma condição real e seu resultado provável. / We use the first conditional for a real condition and its likely result.

Form / FormaStructureExample / Exemplo
Certain result / Resultado certoIf + present simple, ... will + base verbIf we add it now, we will miss the deadline.
Possible result / Resultado possívelIf + present simple, ... may + base verbIf the vendor is late, we may need a backup plan.
Negative / Negativo... will not + base verbIf we cut the scope, the client will not be happy.
Opinion frame / Frase de opiniãoI see your point, but... / I agreeI see your point, but I have a concern.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de if, use o present simple, NUNCA "will" ("If we add", não "If we will add"). O will ou may vai no resultado. E lembre: agree já é verbo — diga "I agree", nunca "I am agree".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the most professional response for each situation.

A stakeholder wants to add work to your sprint. The best reply is:
A "I see your point, but I have a concern about the deadline."
B "No. That is a bad idea."
C "Just do it my way."
You want to explain the consequence of a late decision. You say:
A "If we will add it, we will miss the deadline."
B "If we add it now, we will miss the deadline."
C "If we add it now, we miss the deadline tomorrow."
You want to show agreement politely. The correct sentence is:
A "I am agree with your concern."
B "I agree with your concern, and I have a compromise."
C "I am agreeing your concern."
