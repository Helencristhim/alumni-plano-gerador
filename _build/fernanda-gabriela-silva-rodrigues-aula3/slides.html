Chapter 3: The Code

Dates & Times

March 15th · at 10:00 AM

Discover the Grammar

Read the Date

"The delivery is on March 15th."

"The deadline is March 10th."

"The meeting is at 10:00 AM."

"We have 500 tons."

UsePatternExample
DateMonth + ordinalMarch 15th (fifteenth)
Timeat + hour AM/PMat 10:00 AM
Quantitynumber + unit500 tons

Dates use ordinals; times use at. / Datas = ordinais; horas = "at".

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"The delivery is on March 15."

✓ RIGHT

"The delivery is on March 15th."

✗ WRONG

"The meeting is 10:00 AM."

✓ RIGHT

"The meeting is at 10:00 AM."

Chapter 4: Reading the Numbers

The Order Details

Listen, read and confirm

Dialogue

Confirming the Order

D
Hi Fernanda. Can you confirm the new order? How many tons?
F
Hi Daniel! We have a shipment of 500 tons.
D
Good. And the delivery date?
F
The delivery is on March 15th. The deadline for documents is March 10th.
D
Perfect. What time is the meeting about this order?
F
The meeting is at 10:00 AM. I will check the schedule again.
D
Great. Please confirm the due date with the supplier. Our quarter ends soon.
F
Sure. I confirm everything today. Thank you, Daniel!
