Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

A review of the key terms from Lessons 1 to 5. Tap Listen to refresh each one.

Stakeholder -- a person or group with an interest in a project or its outcome
"Every major stakeholder has signed off on the new security platform."
Oversee -- to watch over and be responsible for a process or team
"I oversee growth initiatives across five regional offices."
Benchmark -- a standard or point of reference used to measure performance
"Our division outperformed the industry benchmark this quarter."
Forecast -- a prediction of future results, numbers, or conditions
"The forecast for Q4 suggests a twelve percent revenue increase."
Leverage -- to use a resource or position to gain the greatest advantage
"We can leverage our Q3 results to negotiate a bigger budget."
Escalate -- to raise an issue to a higher level of authority for action
"If the supplier goes quiet, we will escalate the matter to the director."
Circulate -- to send a document or message around to a group of people
"I will circulate the meeting notes to all regional leads."
Consensus -- a general agreement reached by a group after discussion
"The team finally reached a consensus on the new targets."
Liaise -- to communicate and coordinate with others to work together
"Victor will liaise with the London office to align the rollout."
Concise -- giving a lot of information clearly and in a few words
"Keep the review concise so the board can act quickly."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the review summary and answer the questions.

The Growth Manager prepared his mid-block review. He opened with the wins: the team had outperformed its benchmark and had made real headway on the security platform, and the pipeline had grown well above forecast. He was honest about the Q2 slip too. If they had aligned the regional leads earlier, they would have avoided the friction over headcount. He speculated carefully about the cause: the delay must have cost a few weeks, and the client might have misunderstood the scope. Then he debriefed the leadership call. The director had confirmed that the budget would hold, and she mentioned they might revisit the targets. Finally, he closed with the right register: "I would appreciate it if you could review these figures, and I will circulate a concise summary at your earliest convenience." He knew that one report, built from five tools, would do the work of ten emails.

1. Which structure does the manager use to present the team's wins?
A The present perfect / past perfect, to show results achieved up to that point.
B The first conditional, to describe a future risk.
C The imperative, to give the board an order.
2. What does "If they had aligned the regional leads earlier, they would have avoided the friction" express?
A A real plan for next quarter.
B A third conditional: a past result that did not happen.
C A polite request for help.
3. Why does he say "the delay must have cost a few weeks" rather than "the delay cost a few weeks"?
A Because he is certain and has the exact figures.
B Because a modal perfect signals a careful, diplomatic guess about the past.
C Because it is a direct quotation from the director.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Five-Tool Cheat Sheet

GRAMMAR

One job, one tool. This table maps each function from the block to its structure.

JobStructureExample
Achievement (L1)have / has + past participle"We have made headway."
Unreal past (L2)If + had + p.p., would have + p.p."If we had launched earlier, we would have won."
Speculation (L3)must / might / could + have + p.p."The delay must have affected Q4."
Debrief (L4)reported speech (backshift)"She said the budget would be cut."
Register (L5)I would appreciate it if you could + base"I would appreciate it if you could confirm."
Watch the traps: present perfect never goes with a finished time word (use past simple); in a third conditional the if-clause is "had + p.p.", never "would have"; modal perfects always keep "have". PT: cada situacao tem UMA ferramenta -- conquista usa present perfect, passado irreal usa third conditional, suposicao usa modal perfect, relato usa discurso indireto, e pedido usa registro formal.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each review situation.

You want to highlight a result the team achieved this year, with no specific finished date. Which is correct?
A "We have reached the milestone last quarter."
B "We have reached an important milestone this year."
C "We are reaching the milestone two years ago."
The board asks why the Q2 launch underperformed. How do you explain the missed result diplomatically?
A "If we had aligned the team earlier, we would have hit the target."
B "If we would align the team, we will hit the target."
C "If we align the team earlier, we hit the target."
You are reporting what the director told you in a private call. Which sentence is correct?
A "She said the budget will be cut and they will revisit the targets."
B "She said the budget would be cut and they might revisit the targets."
C "She say the budget would be cut and revisit the targets."
You want to close your review by asking the board to send feedback, without sounding demanding. What do you say?
A "Send me your feedback now."
B "I would appreciate it if you could share your feedback at your earliest convenience."
C "Why have you not sent feedback yet?"
