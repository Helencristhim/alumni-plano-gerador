Lesson 01 -- Pre-class

Who Is Flavia? -- Introducing Your Work

First words to introduce yourself and your job at Sebrae. Key words: consultant, project, to support, small business, region, to manage, goal, team, meeting, partner. Structure: present simple (I / we + base verb; he / she + verb + s) to talk about your work and routine.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Consultant -- a person who gives expert advice to companies
"I am a consultant at Sebrae in Sao Paulo."
Project -- a planned piece of work with a clear goal
"My project helps small businesses in my region."
To support -- to help a person or a company
"We support small companies with their projects."
Small business -- a small company with a few employees
"A small business is very important for the local economy."
Region -- a part of a country, like a state or an area
"I work with businesses in my region of Sao Paulo."
To manage -- to be in charge of a project or a team
"I manage big projects for small companies."
Goal -- something you want to achieve
"My goal is to speak English in international meetings."
Team -- a group of people who work together
"My team works with 33 regional offices."
Meeting -- a time when people come together to talk about work
"We have a meeting with an international partner today."
Partner -- a person or company that works with you
"David is a partner from an innovation center in Italy."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is a consultant at Sebrae in Sao Paulo. She works with small businesses in her region, and she manages big projects. "I support local companies every day," she says. Her team helps 33 regional offices. Every week, they have a meeting to talk about their work. The goal of the project is to help local products reach international partners. David works at an innovation center in Italy. He likes Flavia's project, and he wants to talk again next week.

1. Why do we say "She works with small businesses"?
A The subject is "she", so the verb takes an s.
B The action is happening only right now.
C It is a question, so we need does.
2. Why do we say "I support local companies" without an s?
A The verb "support" never takes an s.
B The subject is "I", so the verb stays in the base form.
C It is a past action.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "David work at an innovation center."
B "David works at an innovation center."
C "David working at an innovation center."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple (Jobs & Routines)

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your work and routine, and when to add the -s.

SubjectVerbExample
I / you / we / theybase verb (no s)I work at Sebrae.
he / she / itverb + sShe works at Sebrae.
Questionsdo / does + base verbWhat do you do? · Where does she work?
Negativesdon't / doesn't + base verb: I don't work on Sundays. · He doesn't work on Sundays.
