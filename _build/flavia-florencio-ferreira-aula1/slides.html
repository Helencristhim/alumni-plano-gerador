Chapter 3: The Code

I Work vs She Works

The present simple for jobs and routines

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work at Sebrae in Sao Paulo."

"We help small businesses in the region."

"She manages a big project."

"He supports the team every day."

Why green for I and we, but orange with an extra s for she and he?

SubjectVerbExample
I / you / we / theybase verb (no s)I work at Sebrae.
he / she / itverb + sShe works at Sebrae.
Questions & negativesdo / does + base verbWhat do you do? · She doesn't work on Sunday.

I and we = base verb · he and she = verb + s.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She work at Sebrae with small businesses."
"She works at Sebrae with small businesses."
"What you do at your company?"
"What do you do at your company?"
"I no work on Sundays."
"I don't work on Sundays."

With he, she, and it, add an s to the verb. For questions and negatives, use do and does — never "no" before the verb.
