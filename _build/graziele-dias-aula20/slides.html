Chapter 3: Must & Should

Rules and Advice

must · mustn't · should

The Tools

Three Rule Tools

must = strong rule · "You must wear a badge."

mustn't = forbidden · "You mustn't enter the ICU alone."

should = advice · "You should wash your hands."

After must and should, use the simple verb (no to).

must = rule · mustn't = forbidden · should = advice

Discover the Pattern

What's the Pattern?

You must wear a badge.

You mustn't enter the ICU alone.

You should wash your hands.

You shouldn't run in the corridor.

WordMeaning + verb
muststrong rule — must wear (simple verb, no to)
mustn'tforbidden — mustn't enter (do not do it)
shouldadvice — should wash (a good idea)

must = rule · mustn't = forbidden · should = advice · + simple verb

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"You must to wear a badge."

✓ RIGHT

"You must wear a badge."

✗ WRONG

"You don't must enter."

✓ RIGHT

"You mustn't enter."

must + simple verb (no to). Forbidden = mustn't.
