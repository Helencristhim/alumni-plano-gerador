Lesson 19 -- Around Town -- Public Transport

Series
Easy English -- "Using Public Transport" (slow, clear)

A short, slow video showing how to get around a city by bus, train and subway: buying a ticket, finding the platform, and asking which transport goes where. Real English -- perfect for today's words.

Watch with English subtitles. Each time you hear a transport word (bus, train, subway, platform, ticket), say it out loud.

Podcast
Speak English Now -- "At the Train Station"

A slow, clear podcast about getting around town: asking which train goes to a place, buying a single or return ticket, and asking the price with "How much is...?".

Repeat each phrase out loud. Then practice: "Which train goes to the city center?" / "I would like a return ticket." / "How much is a ticket?"

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Public Transport: What to Say"

A short, practical lesson on today's language: how to ask which bus or train goes to a place, buy a ticket, and say what transport you take.

After watching, say the five Survival Card phrases out loud. Then imagine you are at a station and plan a trip across the city.
