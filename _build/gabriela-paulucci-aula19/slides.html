Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Which bus goes to...? -- I take the...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do you ask which transport to take, and how do you say what you use?

"Which bus goes to the museum?"

"I take the train to the city center."

"How much is a single ticket?"

"Which train goes to the airport?"

Grammar Rule

Which ... goes to ...? / I take the ...

FunctionStructureExample
Ask which oneWhich + transport + goes to + place?Which bus goes to the museum?
Say what you useI take the + transport.I take the train.
Ask the priceHow much is + a + ticket?How much is a single ticket?

Key idea: use Which ... goes to ...? to ask the right transport, and I take the ... to say what you use. Say "I take the bus", not "I get the bus".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Which bus go to the museum?" (no s)
"Which bus goes to the museum?"

The bus, the train, the subway goes (with s). And say I take the bus, not "I take bus".
