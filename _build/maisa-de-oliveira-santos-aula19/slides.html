Chapter 3: The Code

Giving Your Opinion

I think · In my opinion · I agree · I disagree

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I think we should start now."

"In my opinion, the plan is good."

"I agree with David."

"I disagree, but I understand."

StructureUse forExample
I think + sentencegive your opinionI think we should start now.
In my opinion, + sentencegive opinion (formal)In my opinion, the plan is good.
I agree (with + person)agreeI agree with David.
I disagree, but...disagree politelyI disagree, but I understand.

Say I agree and I disagree — never "I am agree". Use I think OR In my opinion, not both together.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I am agree with you.
I agree with you.
In my opinion I think the plan is good.
In my opinion, the plan is good.

Say I agree / I disagree — the verb is in "agree", so never add "am". And use I think OR In my opinion, never both in the same sentence.

Chapter 4: A Real Meeting

A Meeting with David

the budget, the deadline, opinions

Dialogue

The Budget Meeting

M
Good morning, David. Let's start the meeting.
D
Good morning, Maísa. The first point on the agenda is the budget.
M
I think we should look at the numbers first.
D
That is a good point. In my opinion, the budget is too high.
M
I agree with you. We can find a discount.
D
Great idea. Let's move on to the deadline.
M
I disagree a little. The deadline is too soon, but we can try.
D
OK, thank you for your opinion. Let's finish here.

The budget.

3. What does David want to do after the budget?
He wants to move on to the deadline.
