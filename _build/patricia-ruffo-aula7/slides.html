Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Cause and effect: because, therefore, as a result, lead to

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"Because the results are strong, I am confident."

"The numbers demonstrate the impact, therefore we should invest."

"The proof is clear, as a result the committee agreed."

"A bigger budget would lead to a bigger study."

Grammar Rule

Cause and Effect

FunctionExample
Give the causeBecause the data is strong, we will invest.
Give the resultThe data is strong, therefore we will invest.
Give the resultThe data is strong, as a result we will invest.
Connect cause to effectMore funding will lead to a bigger study.

Key idea: use because for the cause, and therefore or as a result for the effect. Use lead to + a noun to connect a cause directly to a result.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"More funding will lead to grow the study."
"More funding will lead to a bigger study."

After lead to, use a noun (a study, an improvement), not a base verb.

BUSINESS CASE -- FUNCTIONAL INGREDIENTS, PHASE TWO

Prepared byPatricia Ruffo, Abbott Research
ForSteering Committee
RequestApproval and budget for phase two

Proof: In the last trial, the new formula led to a clear improvement in glycemic control, with consistent data points across three groups.

Rationale: The data demonstrate a real impact on patient care. The main factor is the consistency of the results.

Conclusion: Because the proof is strong, a larger budget would lead to a larger study, and therefore I recommend that we approve phase two this quarter.
