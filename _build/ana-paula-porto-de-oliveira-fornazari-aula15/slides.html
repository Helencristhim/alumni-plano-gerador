Chapter 3: The Opinion Code

Opinion Phrases

Open · Agree · Disagree · Soften

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"In my opinion, we should try the new place."

"That is a really good point."

"I see your point, but I do not agree."

"I suppose you could be right."

Which sentence opens an opinion, which one agrees, which one disagrees, and which one softens?

FunctionPhrases
Open an opinionIn my opinion, ... · I think ... · If you ask me, ...
AgreeThat is a good point. · Exactly. · You are right.
Disagree politelyI see your point, but ... · Actually, I think ... · I am not sure I agree.
Soften (hedge)I suppose ... · It seems to me ... · kind of / sort of

Golden rule: to disagree in English, soften first. Start with "I see your point, but ..." — never just "You are wrong."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am agree with you."
"I agree with you."
"I am not agree with that."
"I do not agree with that."
"She insist she is right."
"She insists she is right."

"Agree" is a verb: say I agree (not "I am agree"). The negative is I do not agree or I disagree.

Dialogue

Home or the Office?

P
Ana Paula, can I bring up something? In my opinion, working from home is much better than going to the office.
A
That is an interesting point of view. Honestly, I am not sure I agree. I think the office helps me focus.
P
I see your point, but I would argue that home saves so much time. No traffic, no long commute.
A
Actually, that is a good point, I have to admit. But I suppose I miss talking to real people during the day.
P
Fair enough. I admit it can feel lonely sometimes. Maybe the best answer is a mix of both.
A
Exactly. I think we finally agree on something. A little of each is perfect.
