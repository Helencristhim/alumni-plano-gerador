Lesson 15 -- Making Your Voice Heard

Podcast
Keeping cars out of city centres -- 6 Minute English (BBC Learning English)

Two hosts discuss an everyday topic and share their opinions: should cars be banned from city centres? They agree, disagree, and give reasons in clear, natural English. Connection to Lesson 15: this is exactly the opinion language you practiced -- "in my opinion", "I see your point", and polite disagreement about a real issue.

Tip: every time a host gives an opinion, pause and say whether you agree or disagree, out loud, in one full sentence.

How to disagree productively and find common ground -- Julia Dhar (TED)

A short, inspiring talk on how to disagree with someone and still keep a good, respectful conversation, exactly the skill at the heart of today's lesson. The speaker talks slowly and clearly, and subtitles are available. Connection to Lesson 15: it shows why "I see your point, but ..." and finding common ground really matter.
