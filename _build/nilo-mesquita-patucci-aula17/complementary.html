Lesson 17 -- Negotiation IV: Managing the Relationship

Series
Billions -- any partnership or dispute scene

Powerful people manage long relationships full of deals: a partner defaults, they send a warning, they renegotiate and protect the goodwill before anyone goes to court. Listen for how they handle a problem without destroying the relationship.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down one first conditional you hear ("if you..., we will...") and one relationship word from today's lesson (notice, remedy, renew, dispute).

Podcast
Business English Pod -- "Managing Difficult Clients and Renewals"

Practical episodes on what happens after the deal: handling a late payment, sending a notice, renegotiating terms, protecting the goodwill and renewing the contract. Exactly today's vocabulary in real business relationships.

Note three phrases the speaker uses to handle a problem politely. Then record yourself making two first-conditional plans for your Corinthians deal ("if they default..., we will...").

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "The First Conditional (if + present, will + verb)"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: how to talk about a real future possibility and its result, why we use the present simple after "if", and where "will" goes in the sentence.

After watching, make five first conditionals about managing a partnership ("if the sponsor pays late, we will...", "if the season goes well, we will...") and say them out loud.
