Negotiation IV -- Managing the Relationship

After signing, manage the partnership: renewals, disputes and handling a breach. Talk about real consequences with the first conditional (if they default, we will send a notice). New relationship vocabulary: renew, renewal, extension, renegotiate, dispute, notice, default, remedy, escalate, goodwill, mutual, honor.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Renew -- to agree to continue a deal after it ends
"If the season goes well, we will renew the deal." (Renovar)
Renewal -- the act of continuing a deal for a new period
"We signed a two-season renewal with the sponsor." (Renovação)
Extension -- extra time added to an existing deal
"They offered a one-year extension on the exclusivity." (Prorrogação)
Renegotiate -- to discuss the terms again and agree new ones
"If the market grows, we will renegotiate the fee." (Renegociar)
Dispute -- a serious disagreement between two parties
"We solved the dispute without going to court." (Disputa / litígio)
Notice -- a formal written warning that there is a problem
"We sent a formal notice about the late payment." (Notificação)
Default -- a failure to do what the contract says, e.g. to pay
"Three late payments are a default on the deal." (Inadimplência)
Remedy -- the action that fixes a breach, e.g. a fine or repair
"The remedy for a missed payment is a penalty fee." (Solução / reparação)
Escalate -- to take a problem to a higher level or to a court
"If talks fail, we will escalate the dispute." (Escalar / levar adiante)
Goodwill -- the good feeling and trust between two partners
"A fight would destroy the goodwill we built." (Boa relação / confiança)
Mutual -- shared by both sides; good for both parties
"We found a mutual solution that helped both clubs." (Mútuo)
Honor -- to keep and respect the promises in a contract
"A good partner always honors the agreement." (Honrar / cumprir)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

After signing, Nilo must manage the relationship for four seasons. To plan for real situations, he uses the first conditional: if plus the present, then will plus the verb. He says: "If the sponsor pays late, we will send a notice." If a party fails to pay, that is a default, and the contract has a remedy -- a penalty fee. But Nilo does not fight first. He protects the goodwill: "If you pay on new dates, we will not apply the penalty." He prefers to renegotiate and find a mutual solution. Only as a last step does he escalate a dispute to a court. And when the season goes well, both sides renew the deal.

1. In the first conditional, what tense comes after "if"?
A The present simple -- "if the sponsor pays late".
B Will -- "if the sponsor will pay late".
C Would -- "if the sponsor would pay late".
2. When a party fails to pay, what is the first calm step?
A Go straight to court.
B Send a formal notice and offer a remedy.
C Cancel the partnership at once.
3. Why does Nilo prefer to renegotiate and find a mutual solution?
A Because escalating is always faster.
B To protect the goodwill so the deal can be renewed.
C Because the contract has no remedy.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The First Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de uma possibilidade REAL no futuro e seu resultado. / Used to talk about a real future possibility and its result.

If + present (a condição)will + verbo (o resultado)
If the sponsor pays late,we will send a notice.
If the season goes well,we will renew the deal.
If they default again,we will escalate the dispute.
If we do not agree,we will renegotiate the terms.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o primeiro conditional fala de uma possibilidade real. Depois de if, use o presente simples; nunca use will depois de if. A ordem pode inverter ("We will renew if the season goes well"); a vírgula só aparece quando começa com if. / First conditional = real future. Use if + present; will only in the result.
