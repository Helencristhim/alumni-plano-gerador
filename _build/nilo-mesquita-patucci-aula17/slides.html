Grammar Rule

The First Conditional

If + present (the condition)will + verb (the result)
If the sponsor pays late,we will send a notice.
If the season goes well,we will renew the deal.
If they default again,we will escalate the dispute.
If we do not agree,we will renegotiate the terms.

Key idea: the first conditional talks about a real future possibility. Use if + present simple for the condition and will + verb for the result. You can swap the order: "If they default, we will escalate" = "We will escalate if they default." A comma appears only when the sentence starts with if.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"If the sponsor will pay late, we will send a notice."
"If the sponsor pays late, we will send a notice."

After if, use the present simple. Will goes only in the result part.

Comprehension

About the Briefing

1. According to Sofia, is signing the end or the beginning, and what must Nilo do next?

Signing is the beginning, not the end. He must manage the relationship for four seasons -- renewals, renegotiation, and handling problems like a default.

2. When a party defaults, what calm steps does Sofia recommend before going to court?

Do not panic -- give a formal notice, apply the remedy in the contract (like a penalty fee), and renegotiate. Escalation to a court is the last step.

3. Why does Sofia say it is so important to protect the goodwill?

Goodwill is the trust between the two sides and it is hard to rebuild. A mutual solution that is good for both parties is always better than a fight, and it keeps the partnership strong.

Artefact

The Formal Notice

FORMAL NOTICE -- LATE PAYMENT

From: Sport Club Corinthians Paulista -- Dr. Nilo Patussi  |  To: The Sponsor

Dear partner,

We value our partnership and the goodwill between our clubs. However, three payments are now late, which is a default under Clause 4 of our agreement.

The condition

If the full payment does not arrive within thirty days,

The result (the remedy)

we will apply the remedy in the contract: a small penalty fee.

We would much prefer a mutual solution. If you need new dates, we will gladly renegotiate the payment schedule and protect our partnership.

With respect,
Dr. Nilo Patussi

Dialogue

Solving the Default

M

"Dr. Patussi, we got your notice. We are sorry the payments are late. Our own client paid us late. Please, do not escalate this."

N

"Thank you for calling. I do not want to escalate either. I value the goodwill between us. If you pay on new dates, we will not apply the penalty."

M

"That is very fair. So we will renegotiate the payment dates and sign an amendment?"

N

"Exactly. If you honor the new dates, the relationship stays strong. And if this season goes well, we will be happy to discuss a renewal."

M

"We would love that. We will also offer an extension on the exclusivity in Brazil. Thank you for protecting the partnership."

N

"A mutual solution -- that is what good partners do. We will send the amendment today, and we will never need a court."

Listening 2

A Difficult Season, Solved

0:00 / 0:00

1. What was the problem, and why did Nilo not go straight to court?

The sponsor was late with three payments -- a default. He did not attack them because that would destroy the goodwill, which is hard to rebuild.

2. What did Nilo do step by step to solve it?

He sent a polite notice with a remedy (a small penalty fee), then renegotiated the payment dates and offered to drop the penalty if they paid on time. They signed an amendment.

3. What was the final result of protecting the relationship?

At the end of the season the sponsor wanted to renew. They renewed for two more seasons and agreed an extension on the exclusivity -- and never needed to escalate to court.
