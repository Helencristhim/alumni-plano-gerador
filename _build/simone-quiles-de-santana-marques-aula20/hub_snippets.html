Social English -- Conversation at the Corporate Dinner

Learn the social English for a corporate dinner: how to mingle, give a compliment, recommend a dish, join a toast and leave the table politely. New vocabulary: host, mingle, compliment, cuisine, recommend, toast, cheers, excuse me. Grammar: the present perfect for experience -- have/has + past participle (Have you ever tried this cuisine? I have been to Tokyo twice).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Host -- the person who invites you and welcomes you to the event
"The host greeted every guest at the door." (Anfitrião / anfitriã)
Mingle -- to move around and talk to many different people at a social event
"Before dinner, the guests mingle and make small talk." (Circular / interagir)
Compliment -- a nice thing you say to please someone
"She gave the host a compliment about the meal." (Elogio)
Cuisine -- the style of cooking of a country or region
"Have you ever tried Japanese cuisine?" (Culinária / gastronomia)
Recommend -- to say that something is good and that someone should try it
"The host recommended the fish; it was excellent." (Recomendar)
Toast -- when people lift a glass and say words to celebrate together
"The host made a short toast to a successful deal." (Brinde)
Cheers -- what you say when you touch glasses to celebrate
"They lifted their glasses and said cheers." (Saúde! / Tim-tim!)
Excuse me -- a polite phrase to leave the table, pass by, or get attention
"Excuse me for a moment; I will be right back." (Com licença)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Usamos o present perfect (have / has + particípio passado) para falar de experiência na vida, quando o tempo exato não importa. Em perguntas usamos ever (alguma vez): "Have you ever tried this cuisine?". Em negativas usamos never: "I have never visited this city." Particípios: be → been, eat → eaten, meet → met; verbos regulares levam -ed (try → tried, visit → visited). Cuidado: depois de have/has vem o particípio, não o verbo base (tried, não try). / We use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) for experience in your life, when the exact time is not important. Use ever in questions and never in negatives. After have/has, use the past participle, not the base verb.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
QuestionHave you ever + participle?Have you ever tried this cuisine?
AffirmativeI/you/we have + participleI have been to Tokyo twice.
He/She/Ithas + participleShe has met the host before.
Negativehave/has never + participleI have never visited this city.
