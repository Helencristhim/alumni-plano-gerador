Talking about experience in your life

The Present Perfect Code

Have / Has + Participle

FormStructureExample
QuestionHave you ever + participle?Have you ever tried this cuisine?
AffirmativeI/you/we have + participleI have been to Tokyo twice.
He/She/Ithas + participleShe has met the host before.
Negativehave/has never + participleI have never visited this city.

Key idea: the present perfect (have/has + past participle) talks about experience in your life when the exact time is not important. Use ever in questions and never in negatives. Participles: be → been, eat → eaten, meet → met; regular verbs add -ed (try → tried, visit → visited).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Have you ever try this cuisine?"
"Have you ever tried this cuisine?"

After have / has, use the past participle (tried, been, met), not the base verb.

Practice

"I have _____been to this restaurant twice." (be)

Artefact

The Dinner Conversation Card

CORPORATE DINNER -- CONVERSATION CARD

PREP

Open the conversation

Mingle and meet a guest. Start light: "It is a lovely evening. Have you been to this restaurant before?"

Compliment and ask about experience

Give a compliment: "The food looks wonderful." Ask: "Have you ever tried this cuisine? Which dish do you recommend?"

The toast and leaving the table

When the host gives a toast, lift your glass: "Cheers!" To step away: "Excuse me for a moment."

Note: keep it light and friendly. Compliment, ask about experience with the present perfect, join the toast, and thank the host for a lovely evening.
