Series
Billions -- Season 1, Episode 1 ("Pilot")

A masterclass in reading the other side of the table. Every scene is a negotiation: characters make concessions, test each other's bottom line, and deduce what their rivals must be thinking and can't really mean. Direct connection to Lesson 11: watch how the best negotiators guess the other side's position before they speak.

Tip: pick one negotiation scene and say two deductions out loud -- "He must want...", "That can't be his bottom line."

One of the most listened-to negotiation podcasts in the world, built on the exact language of this lesson: concessions, deadlocks, reading intentions, and closing in good faith. Each episode is real professionals negotiating real situations, spoken in clear American English. Direct connection to Lesson 11: hearing the vocabulary and the deduction of a negotiation used live.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "'May', 'might' and 'could'" (6 Minute Grammar)

A short, clear BBC Learning English lesson on the modal verbs you used tonight to guess how likely something is -- may, might and could -- the heart of modals of deduction. Watch it, then extend it to "must" (sure) and "can't" (impossible). Direct connection to Lesson 11: reinforcement of the exact structure that lets you read the other side of a negotiation.

Tip: pause after each example and make the same deduction about a supplier, in the present and in the past.
