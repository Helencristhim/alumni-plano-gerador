Chapter 3

Reading the Other Side

Modals of deduction: must, might, could and can't — for the present and the past

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"They sent a counteroffer, so they must want to close."

"The price dropped, so that can't be their real bottom line."

"They moved on everything else, so they might have some leeway."

"They stopped moving, so they must have hit a real limit."

How sure?PresentPast (+ have + p.p.)
Certain (yes)It must be their plan.They must have hit a limit.
PossibleThey might / could be testing us.They might have moved already.
Certain (no)That can't be the bottom line.That can't have been final.

Deduction = a modal + base verb (no "to"). For the past, add "have" + past participle. The negative of certainty is "can't", never "mustn't" (mustn't means "not allowed").

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

They must to want the deal.
They must want the deal.
That mustn't be their bottom line.
That can't be their bottom line.
They must forgot the clause.
They must have forgotten the clause.

Three rules in one slide: a modal takes the base verb with no "to"; the negative of a sure guess is "can't", not "mustn't"; and a guess about the past uses "have" plus the past participle.

Chapter 4

The Negotiation

A founder and a supplier close a contract, one move at a time

Dialogue

Closing the Contract

V
Dr. Eliane, thank you for the counteroffer. We can move a little on the price, but the two-year term is non-negotiable.
E
Thank you, Victor. You have already moved on the price, so it can't be your bottom line. Let me offer one concession: I will accept two years if you shorten the delivery window.
V
That is fair. The window is one clause where we might have some leeway.
E
Good. Then let us meet halfway: six weeks instead of ten. If you can do that, I think we have a deal.
V
Let me check with my team. I do not want us to reach a deadlock over two weeks.
E
Neither do I. We are negotiating in good faith, and I would rather close than walk away over a small point.
V
They said yes. Six weeks it is. I will update that clause and send the revised contract tonight.
E
Perfect. I will read every line, and once it is fully binding, I will sign off and we start.
V
A pleasure doing business with you, Doctor. You must have negotiated before — you read the room well.
Comprehension

Did You Read Him?

1. Which term did Victor first call non-negotiable?
The two-year term of the contract.
2. Where did Victor admit the supplier had some leeway?
On the delivery-window clause, which they shortened from ten weeks to six.
3. What did Victor deduce about Eliane at the end?
That she must have negotiated before, because she read the room well.
