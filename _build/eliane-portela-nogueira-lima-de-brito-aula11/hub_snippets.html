Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words for the negotiating table. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras da mesa de negociação. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Concession -- something you give up to move a deal forward / concessão
"I offered one concession on the delivery window."
Counteroffer -- a new offer made in reply to the other side / contraproposta
"They sent a counteroffer this morning."
Bottom line -- the lowest offer you will accept / limite mínimo
"That price can't be their real bottom line."
Walk away -- to leave a deal when the terms are not good enough / desistir do acordo
"Do not walk away too early in a negotiation."
Meet halfway -- to agree where each side gives a little / chegar a um meio-termo
"If they meet us halfway, we sign today."
Deadlock -- a point where neither side moves and talks stop / impasse
"If we reach a deadlock, we pause and think."
In good faith -- honestly and fairly, meaning to agree / de boa-fé
"We negotiate in good faith, but we protect our margin."
Non-negotiable -- not able to be changed or discussed / inegociável
"Patient safety is non-negotiable for our clinic."
Clause -- a specific section or condition in a contract / cláusula
"They said one clause was non-negotiable."
Binding -- legally forcing both sides to keep the agreement / vinculante
"I will not sign until the contract is fully binding."
Sign off -- to formally approve something as final / dar aval final
"We sign off only when every term is clear."
Leeway -- room to move or make changes; flexibility / margem de manobra
"They might have some leeway on the delivery date."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane reads the other side before she replies. The supplier sent a counteroffer, so they must want to close the deal soon. Their price dropped, which means that first number can't have been their real bottom line. They said one clause was non-negotiable, but they moved on everything else, so they might have some leeway there too. When a negotiation goes quiet, Eliane knows the other side could be talking to another buyer -- or they must have hit a limit. Notice the pattern: a modal + a base verb for the present (must want), and a modal + have + a past participle for the past (must have hit). The negative of a sure guess is can't, never "mustn't".

1. Why does Eliane think the supplier must want to close?
A Because they sent a counteroffer -- a sure sign of interest.
B Because they walked away from the deal.
C Because they refused to answer.
2. Which form does English use to guess about the PAST?
A modal + "to" + verb ("must to hit").
B modal + "have" + past participle ("must have hit").
C modal + "-ing" ("must hitting").
3. How do you say an impossible guess (you are sure it is NOT true)?
A "It mustn't be true."
B "It can't be true."
C "It don't must be true."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modals of Deduction

GRAMMAR

Use modals to say how sure you are. Present = modal + base verb; past = modal + have + past participle. The negative of certainty is "can't", never "mustn't". / Use modais para dizer o quão certo você está. Presente = modal + verbo base; passado = modal + have + particípio. O negativo da certeza é "can't", nunca "mustn't".

How sure? / CertezaPresent / PresentePast / Passado
Certain yes / Certeza (sim)It must be their plan.They must have hit a limit.
Possible / PossívelThey might / could be testing us.They might have moved already.
Certain no / Certeza (não)That can't be the bottom line.That can't have been final.

Quiz

A "They can't read every clause that fast."
