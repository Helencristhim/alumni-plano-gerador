Lesson 8 -- What Should Have Happened

Series
The Playlist -- Episode 4: "The Coder"

A real product post-mortem told from one engineer's point of view: a system broke, a launch slipped, and the team replays what should have happened. A clear look at root cause, fallout, and corrective action.

Watch with English subtitles. Note two moments where someone explains a past mistake. Then rewrite each one as a mixed conditional: one with "If we had..., we would not be..." and one with "If ... were ..., ... would have...".

Podcast
Freakonomics Radio -- "Why Are Supply Chains So Fragile?"

A clear breakdown of how a single point of failure, a demand spike, and missing safety stock turn one stockout into a global ripple effect -- and what corrective action builds real resilience. Directly relevant to your work at Bloomberg.

Note 3 causes the host gives for a failure. For each one, say a mixed conditional out loud describing what should have happened to prevent a recurrence.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review -- "How to Run a Blameless Post-Mortem"

A short, practical video on reviewing a failure without blame: finding the root cause, documenting lessons learned, and setting corrective action. A template you can reuse for your own post-mortems.

After watching, record a 60-second post-mortem of a fictional disruption. Use at least three new words and three mixed conditionals -- two of the type "If we had..." and one of the type "If ... were ... would have...".
