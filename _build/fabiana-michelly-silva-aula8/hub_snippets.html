What Should Have Happened -- Reviewing Supply Chain Failures

Mixed conditionals for reviewing a failure: a past cause with a present result (If we had double-sourced, we would not be down a line now) and a present truth with a past result (If our forecasting were better, we would not have missed the spike). New vocabulary: post-mortem, hindsight, foresight, stockout, safety stock, dual-sourcing, single point of failure, ripple effect, stopgap, corrective action, resilience, fallout, overhaul, recurrence, demand spike.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Post-mortem -- a structured review after a failure to learn from it
"We ran a post-mortem after the line stopped." (Análise pós-evento)
Hindsight -- understanding of an event only after it has happened
"In hindsight, the warning signs were obvious." (Retrospectiva)
Foresight -- the ability to predict and prepare for future needs
"Good foresight prevents most disruptions." (Visão de futuro)
Stockout -- running completely out of inventory
"The stockout stopped production for days." (Ruptura de estoque)
Safety stock -- extra inventory kept as a buffer against surprises
"Safety stock would have covered the gap." (Estoque de segurança)
Dual-sourcing -- using two suppliers for the same item to reduce risk
"Dual-sourcing protects us from one supplier failing." (Dupla fonte)
Single point of failure -- one weak link that can stop the whole system
"One supplier was a single point of failure." (Ponto único de falha)
Ripple effect -- one problem that triggers a chain of others
"The delay caused a ripple effect across regions." (Efeito cascata)
Stopgap -- a temporary fix until a real solution is ready
"Air freight was only a stopgap." (Solução paliativa)
Corrective action -- a step taken to stop a problem from happening again
"Our corrective action is dual-sourcing." (Ação corretiva)
Resilience -- the ability to recover quickly from disruption
"Safety stock builds resilience." (Resiliência)
Fallout -- the negative results that follow a problem
"The fallout reached three regions." (Consequências negativas)
Overhaul -- a complete revision of a system or process
"The forecast needs a full overhaul." (Reformulação completa)
Recurrence -- when a problem happens again
"Our goal is to prevent a recurrence." (Recorrência)
Demand spike -- a sudden, sharp increase in demand
"The stockout hit during a demand spike." (Pico de demanda)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Mixed Conditionals

GRAMMAR

Usado para conectar dois tempos diferentes ao revisar uma falha. / Used to connect two different times when reviewing a failure.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
If + had + particípio ... would + baseCausa no passado, resultado agora / Past cause, present resultIf we had double-sourced, we would not be stuck today.
If + were / passado ... would have + particípioVerdade no presente, resultado no passado / Present truth, past resultIf we were more careful, we would not have failed.

Key point / Ponto-chave: a clausula com if NUNCA usa would. Use o past perfect (had + particípio) para uma causa terminada, ou were para uma verdade no presente. Não se diz "If we would have used"; diz-se If we had used.
