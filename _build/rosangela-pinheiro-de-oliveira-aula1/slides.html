Chapter 3: The Code

Your Story in Two Structures

I am · I live · I travel · My flight is

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am from São Paulo."

"My flight is at seven o'clock."

"I live in Vila Leopoldina."

"I travel to Europe once in a while."

Why green for São Paulo and seven o'clock, but blue for Vila Leopoldina and Europe?

FormUseExample
To be
I am / it is / you are		WHO you are, WHERE you are from, WHAT time something happensI am Rosângela. My flight is at seven.
Present simple
I live / I travel / I go		WHAT you do: your routine and the facts of your lifeI live in São Paulo. I travel with my husband.
Question with to be
Are you...? Is it...?		The verb comes FIRST, before youWhere are you from? Is my flight on time?
Question with do
Do you...?		For all the other verbs, you need DOWhere do you live? What do you do?

am / is / are for who you are · do for what you do.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have 68 years."
"I am 68 years old."
"I am live in São Paulo."
"I live in São Paulo."
"Where you are from?"
"Where are you from?"

In English you never HAVE an age — you ARE your age. And in a question, the verb comes before you.
