Complementary Materials

Extra listening to keep English alive between classes. You learn by ear, so let the sound do the work. Check the box once you have watched or listened to it.

Series
Somebody Feed Phil — Season 1, Episode 4: Lisbon (Netflix)

Phil Rosenthal is a man in his sixties who travels the world and talks to everybody — in slow, clear, warm American English. In this episode he lands in Lisbon and does exactly what you want to do: he arrives, he asks, he orders, he makes friends. He is never in a hurry, and neither are you.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Every time Phil meets somebody new, stop the episode and write down how he introduces himself. He uses the same two structures we studied: "I am..." and "I live / I travel...".

Podcast
Speak English Now — Episode 47: Travel by Plane in English

Georgiana tells one simple story about an airport, slowly, and then asks you easy questions so that you answer out loud. It is the same words of this lesson — boarding pass, carry-on, customs — but through your ear, which is your strongest channel. There is a free transcript if you want to read along.

Tip: listen one time with no text at all. Then listen again with the transcript. Then a third time with no text. Answer her questions out loud, in the car or in the kitchen — nobody is listening, and that is the point.

YouTube
Rachel's English — Checking In at an Airport (American English)

A real check-in conversation, in the American English you will hear at the counter, taken apart line by line. Rachel is the best teacher of American pronunciation on the internet, and here she uses the exact words of this lesson: passport, boarding pass, carry-on, gate.

Tip: watch it once to understand, then a second time and repeat every line of the passenger out loud, right after her. Copy the music of the sentence, not only the words.
