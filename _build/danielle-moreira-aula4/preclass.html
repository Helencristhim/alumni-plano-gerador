Aula 04 -- Pre-class

Influencing Without Authority -- Presenting Your People Strategy to Canadian Leadership

Apresentar a estratégia de pessoas a cinco líderes canadenses que NÃO se reportam a você: abrir pelo dado, responder o custo com o custo de não fazer nada, nomear o trade-off antes que a sala o nomeie, e sair da reunião com um dono para cada pilar. Key words: talent retention, engagement survey, performance cycle, succession planning, employer brand, people analytics, workforce planning, change management, psychological contract, diversity and inclusion, influence without authority, employee value proposition, to make a compelling case, to phase out, a double-edged sword. Structure: inversão para ênfase (Never have I seen... / Not only did we..., but we also... / Hardly had we... when...) + linguagem de apresentação executiva (What the data shows us is... / I would like to draw your attention to... / The rationale behind this approach is... / What this means in practice is...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Este é o vocabulário em que uma estratégia de pessoas é escrita — e o único em que um board canadense está disposto a financiar uma.

Talent retention -- keeping the people you cannot afford to lose, and knowing what it costs when you do not (retenção de talentos)
"Talent retention is not a value. It is a number, and this year the number is four."
Engagement survey -- the instrument that measures how invested people are in the work (pesquisa de engajamento)
"The engagement survey had been running for four years, and nobody had ever read it region by region."
Performance cycle -- the yearly loop of goals, feedback and review a company runs on its people (ciclo de avaliação de desempenho)
"That region has not completed a performance cycle since the pandemic. Nobody noticed."
Succession planning -- deciding today who is ready to take a critical role tomorrow, before anyone resigns (plano de sucessão)
"Succession planning is the question nobody asks until the answer is urgent."
Employer brand -- what the market says about you as a place to work, when you are not in the room (marca empregadora)
"Our employer brand is doing our recruiting for us. Unfortunately, it is doing it badly."
People analytics -- using workforce data to make a people decision defensible in a room full of finance people (análise de dados de pessoas)
"Retention as a value was ignored for four years. Retention as people analytics took eleven minutes."
Workforce planning -- deciding how many people, with which skills, you will need, and by when (planejamento de quadro de pessoal)
"Workforce planning is the difference between hiring in September and hiring in a panic in November."
Change management -- the discipline of taking people through a change they did not ask for (gestão da mudança)
"Nobody asked for this change. That is not an obstacle to change management. That is change management."
Psychological contract -- the unwritten expectations between a company and a person, the promises nobody signed (contrato psicológico)
"We never broke her contract. We broke the psychological contract, and she resigned six weeks later."
Diversity and inclusion -- who is in the room, and whether they can actually speak once they are there (diversidade e inclusão)
"Diversity is who you hire. Inclusion is whether they are still speaking in year two."
Influence without authority -- getting a decision made by people who do not work for you and never will (influenciar sem autoridade formal)
"Influence without authority is not a soft skill. In that room it is the only skill."
Employee value proposition -- the deal you offer: what a person actually gets in exchange for joining you and staying (proposta de valor ao colaborador)
"If our employee value proposition is only the salary, we will lose to whoever pays more."
To make a compelling case -- to argue something so well that saying no becomes the harder option (construir um argumento irresistível)
"Make a compelling case, and then stop talking. The silence after a strong argument is not awkward."
To phase out -- to retire an old practice gradually, on purpose, instead of stopping it overnight (descontinuar gradualmente)
"We phase out the old performance cycle over two quarters. We do not switch it off in one."
A double-edged sword -- a real advantage that carries a real cost, at the same time (uma faca de dois gumes)
"Flexible work is a double-edged sword: it widens the talent pool and it weakens the informal culture."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Marina Ortiz joined the Halden Group as its first Director of People and Culture, she was given a title, almost no budget, and no authority whatsoever. The five regional directors did not report to her, they had built the company, and they had never once been asked to adopt a people strategy written by somebody who had been in the building for six weeks. Her predecessor had presented a beautiful framework to the board, had watched it approved, and had watched nothing happen afterwards. The plan was never rejected. It was simply never carried by anyone. Influence without authority was not a soft skill in that room. It was the only skill available to her.

So Marina spent a quarter inside the numbers before she proposed anything. The engagement survey had been running for four years, and nobody had ever read it region by region. When she did, the story stopped being about culture and started being about money: one region was losing its senior teachers at three times the rate of the others, every departure was costing eleven months of hiring, and that region had not completed a performance cycle since the pandemic. Talent retention, presented as a value, had been ignored for four years. Talent retention, presented as people analytics, took eleven minutes.

The board adopted her strategy in a single meeting. Never had she seen a plan survive because it was right; plans survive because somebody in the room decides to carry them. So she made a compelling case, and then she named the trade-off before anyone else could. Not only did the flexible model widen the talent pool, she told them, but it also weakened the informal culture that had held the company together for twenty years. It was a double-edged sword, and she intended to manage it rather than deny it. Hardly had she finished when the regional director who had quietly killed her predecessor's framework asked to own the first pilot.

1. Por que a frase é "Never had she seen a plan survive" e não "Never she had seen a plan survive"?
A Porque, quando a palavra de ênfase (Never) vai para a frente, a frase pega emprestada a ordem de pergunta: o auxiliar vem ANTES do sujeito.
B Porque é uma pergunta retórica disfarçada.
C Porque o sujeito é feminino e vem depois do verbo.
2. "Not only did the flexible model widen the talent pool..." — por que widen e não widened?
A Porque a frase está no presente.
B Porque o did já carrega o passado — o verbo principal volta para a forma base, exatamente como em "Did you widen it?".
C Porque depois de "not only" o verbo nunca é conjugado.
3. According to the text, why did the previous framework fail?
A The board rejected it because it was badly written.
B The board approved it, and then nobody in the room ever carried it.
C The regional directors were never shown the framework at all.
4. What made the board adopt Marina's strategy, according to the text?
A Her authority over the five regional directors.
B The fact that her strategy was more correct than her predecessor's.
C She argued it with data, named the trade-off first, and somebody in the room decided to carry it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Inversion for Emphasis

GRAMMAR

A gramática do peso: a palavra de ênfase vai para a FRENTE da frase e o verbo auxiliar passa na frente do sujeito, como numa pergunta — mas não é pergunta, é autoridade (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Never / Rarely / At no point
+ auxiliar + sujeito		Convicção. Você põe a sua carreira inteira atrás de uma afirmação. Conviction.Never have I seen a strategy succeed without an owner.
Not only + did + sujeito + verbo base
..., but ... also ...		Escala. Duas conquistas num fôlego só, sem virar lista. Scale.Not only did we redesign the cycle, but we also rebuilt the onboarding.
Hardly / Scarcely + had
+ sujeito + particípio + when		Velocidade. O resultado chegou antes da dúvida. Speed.Hardly had we launched the pilot when the scores rose.
Only when / Only after
+ oração + auxiliar + sujeito		Condição única. Só assim, e de nenhum outro jeito. The only condition.Only when they owned it did the plan move.
NegativaA própria inversão JÁ é a negativa — nunca acrescente "not"At no point was succession planning discussed. (nunca "was not")
InterrogativaInversão é recurso de AFIRMAÇÃO. Em pergunta, use a ordem normal.Have you ever seen a plan survive without an owner?
Presentation languageWhat the data shows us is... (o que os dados nos mostram é...) · I would like to draw your attention to... (eu gostaria de chamar a atenção de vocês para...) · The rationale behind this approach is... (a razão por trás dessa abordagem é...) · What this means in practice is... (o que isso significa na prática é...)

Atenção (o erro nº 1): depois da palavra de ênfase, o sujeito NÃO pode vir antes do auxiliar. "Never I have seen" está errado — o certo é "Never have I seen". A frase pega a ordem da pergunta, e só a ordem: nada de ponto de interrogação.

Atenção (o erro nº 2): se a frase original não tem auxiliar, o inglês empresta um: We redesigned it → "Not only did we redesign it". E o verbo principal volta à forma base (redesign, nunca redesigned).

Dosagem: inversão é tempero, não o prato. Duas ou três numa apresentação de dez minutos soam a autoridade. Seis soam a teatro — e a sala pára de ouvir o argumento para ouvir a performance.

Collocation: em inglês é make a compelling case (nunca "do a case"), draw your attention to (nunca "call your attention for"), e phase out a practice (nunca "phase off").

Stage 2: Put the Board Presentation in Order

Order

Coloque os cinco movimentos da apresentação na ordem em que você vai conduzi-la diante da liderança canadense.

?Name the trade-off yourself, before the room does: it is a double-edged sword, and here is how I manage it.
?Open with the data, never with the belief: what the data shows us is that one senior departure costs eleven months of hiring.
?Close with the ask: one owner per pillar, one metric, and a review in ninety days.
?Draw their attention to the hole: succession coverage, two of nine critical roles.
?Answer the cost objection with the cost of doing nothing, in people analytics, and let them read both numbers.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua apresentação ao board canadense.

You have one slide and a board that only wants to talk about cost. How do you open?
A "People are our greatest asset, and I believe culture is what makes this company different."
B "What the data shows us is that one senior departure costs us eleven months of hiring, and we have had four this year."
C "I would like to start by thanking everyone for the opportunity to be here today."
The CFO says retention is a problem for a good year, not for a year of budget cuts. The most senior answer is:
A "I would not argue with the budget. Not only did our last two departures cost us eleven months of hiring, but they also took the succession plan with them — that is the cost of doing nothing."
B "I understand. Perhaps we can revisit the people strategy next year, then."
C "But retention is about our values, and our values cannot have a budget line."
Which sentence uses inversion correctly?
A "Not only we redesigned the performance cycle, but we also rebuilt the onboarding."
B "Never I have seen a people strategy succeed without an owner."
C "Hardly had we launched the pilot when the engagement scores began to rise."
Two directors will say the flexible model is destroying the culture they built. What do you do?
A Avoid the subject. If nobody raises it, you do not have to defend it.
B Name it first: "Flexible work is a double-edged sword — it widened our talent pool and it weakened the informal culture. I intend to manage that, not deny it."
C Tell them the data proves they are wrong about the culture.
Nobody in the room reports to you. What is the only way your strategy actually gets carried?
A Getting the board to approve it formally, in writing, before anyone leaves the room.
B Escalating to headquarters in Brazil whenever a director resists.
C Making a compelling case and then asking one of them to own the first pilot — approval is not adoption.
