Chapter 3: The Code

Prices &
Bargaining

Quantifiers + prices + too / enough

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

How much is this scarf?
It is too expensive.
It is not cheap enough for me.

How do we ask the price and react to it?

UseStructureExample
Ask the priceHow much is + singular / are + plural?"How much is this?" / "How much are these?"
Too much (problem)too + adjective"It is too expensive."
Enough / not enoughadjective + enough / not + adj + enough"It is not cheap enough."
Quantityhow many (count) / how much (uncount)"How many do you have?"

Key: too vem ANTES do adjetivo (too expensive); enough vem DEPOIS (cheap enough). Para bargainar: "Can you give me a discount?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"It is enough expensive." / "How much costs?"
"It is too expensive." / "How much is it?"

too + adjective (before). enough after the adjective.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Sentence

Say it is not low enough → ???It is not cheap enough.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Your Turn
to Speak

Three role-plays: guided, semi-free, and free

Guided Role-Play

Level 1: With Keywords

You are at a market in Sydney. Choose a souvenir, ask the price, say it is too expensive, ask for a discount, and decide to buy it. Use the keywords below.
How much is...? too expensive a discount size cash / change I will take it
Semi-Free Role-Play

Level 2: Bargain Harder

You want to buy two souvenirs. The seller will not lower the price easily. Bargain politely. Use these starters:
"How much are these two together?"
"That is too..."
"If I buy two, can you...?"
"I will take them if..."
Free Role-Play

Level 3: Speak Freely

No keywords. Buy a souvenir from start to finish: ask the price, react to it, bargain, check the size, pay with cash and get your change — all in English. Be yourself.
