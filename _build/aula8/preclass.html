Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

The language of the deal table. Click Listen to check pronunciation.

Counteroffer -- a return offer made in response to another offer
"They made a counteroffer of 3% instead of 4%."
Deal-Breaker -- an issue so important that it can stop the whole agreement
"Exclusivity is a deal-breaker for us."
Leverage -- an advantage you can use to get better terms
"Our size gives us leverage in the negotiation."
Concession -- something you give up in order to reach an agreement
"We made a small concession on the timeline."
Meet Halfway -- to compromise; each side gives up a little to agree
"Let's meet halfway on the rate."
Bottom Line -- the lowest offer or limit you can accept
"Three percent is below our bottom line."
Trade-Off -- a balance between two things you cannot both have fully
"A lower rate for a longer contract is a fair trade-off."
Walk Away -- to leave a negotiation without a deal
"If they won't move, we're ready to walk away."

Stage 1.2: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read a short negotiation. Notice the second conditional, then answer.

"3% is below our bottom line. If you committed to a longer term, we'd meet you at 3.5%. Exclusivity is a deal-breaker, though. If we dropped exclusivity, we'd hold the rate. If I were you, I'd accept that trade-off. Would you be open to it?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

The second conditional lets you propose trades without committing.

UseFormExample
Hypothetical tradeIf + past simple, would/could + base"If we lowered it, we'd need volume."
AdviceIf I were you, I'd..."If I were you, I'd accept."
Soft offerWould you be open to... / We could consider..."Would you be open to 3.5%?"

Watch out: never say "if we would lower". After "if" use the past simple: "if we lowered". And in formal English say "if I were you" (not "if I was you").

Stage 5: Close the Deal

Reflection
Pick a real deal at Quinto Andar. Record 45 seconds: make your offer, propose one trade-off with a second conditional, name your bottom line, and close by meeting halfway.
Model: "Here's our offer: 4% for two years. If you signed this week, we'd waive the setup fee. I know 3% is your target, but that's below our bottom line. If you committed to a longer term, we'd meet you halfway at 3.5%. Exclusivity is a deal-breaker, so let's drop it. That's a fair trade-off."
