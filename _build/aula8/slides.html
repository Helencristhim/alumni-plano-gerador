Chapter 4: In Context

The Negotiation

Eduardo makes the offer; Sarah counters

E
Here's our offer: 4% for a two-year contract. If you signed this week, we'd waive the setup fee.
S
Thanks, but 4% is above our bottom line. Our counteroffer is 3%.
E
I hear you. If you committed to a longer term, we'd meet halfway at 3.5%.
S
Possibly. But exclusivity is a deal-breaker for us.
E
Understood. Let me make a concession: if we dropped exclusivity, we'd hold the rate at 3.5%.
S
That's a fair trade-off. What's the volume commitment?
E
A thousand listings a year. And remember the leverage: you'd reach ten times more renters.
S
Then we have a deal. No need to walk away today.

Chapter 5: Practice

Deal It Move by Move

Make your opening offer.
"Here's our offer: 4% for a two-year contract. If you signed today, we'd waive the setup fee."
Respond to a lower counteroffer.
"3% is below our bottom line. If you committed to a longer term, we'd meet you at 3.5%."
Use leverage.
"If you partnered with us, you'd reach ten times more renters than you do today."
Make a concession.
"Let me make a concession: if we dropped exclusivity, we'd hold the rate at 3.5%."
Protect a deal-breaker.
"Exclusivity is a deal-breaker. If we lost it, we couldn't offer this rate."
Close by meeting halfway.
"Let's meet halfway: 3.5% for three years. That's a fair trade-off for both sides."
