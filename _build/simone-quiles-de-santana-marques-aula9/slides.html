The Question Code

Indirect Questions

TypeStructureExample
Wh- questionopener + question word + subject + verb"Could you tell me where the office is?"
Yes / No questionopener + if / whether + subject + verb"Do you know if the price is final?"
Polite openersCould you tell me..., Do you know..., I was wondering..., Would you mind telling me...

Key idea: after the opener, use normal word order (subject + verb) and NO do/does/did. Direct: "Where is it?" Indirect: "Could you tell me where it is?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could you tell me where is the office? ... Do you know when does it start?"
"Could you tell me where the office is? ... Do you know when it starts?"

After the opener, use subject + verb in normal order. Never invert, and never use do, does or did.

Practice

Complete the Question

"Could you tell me where _____the file is?" (direct: Where is the file?)
"Do you know when _____the deal closes?" (direct: When does the deal close?)
"I was wondering why _____they changed the clause." (direct: Why did they change it?)
"Could you tell me _____whether the price is final?" (direct: Is the price final?)

Dialogue

The Q&A Call

S

"Thank you for joining, Daniel. Could you tell me when the contract takes effect?"

D

"Of course. It takes effect on the first of next month, once both sides sign."

S

"I see. I was wondering why the indemnity clause changed. Could you specify the reason?"

D

"Good question. We changed it to limit the risk. I can confirm the new terms in writing."

S

"That helps. I have one concern: do you know whether the closing date is final?"

D

"The date is almost final. If anything is not clear, please ask me to rephrase it."
