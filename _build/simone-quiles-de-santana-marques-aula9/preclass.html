Asking Smart Questions -- Question Words & Indirect Questions

How to ask smart, polite questions to get the information you need. New vocabulary: inquire, wonder, specify, rephrase, confirm, assumption, concern, detail, relevant, probe. Grammar: indirect questions (Could you tell me..., Do you know...) with normal word order and if/whether for yes/no questions.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Inquire -- to ask for information politely or formally
"I would like to inquire about the contract terms." (Indagar)
Wonder -- to want to know something
"I was wondering about the closing date." (Querer saber)
Specify -- to state something exactly and clearly
"Could you specify the final price?" (Especificar)
Rephrase -- to say something again in a different way
"Could you rephrase the question, please?" (Reformular)
Confirm -- to check that something is true or correct
"I want to confirm the key terms." (Confirmar)
Assumption -- something you believe is true without proof
"Let us not make an assumption about the numbers." (Suposição)
Concern -- a worry or issue you want to ask about
"I have one concern about the timeline." (Preocupação)
Detail -- a small piece of information
"Could you give me more detail on the clause?" (Detalhe)
Relevant -- connected and important to the topic
"Please keep every question relevant to the deal." (Relevante)
Probe -- to ask deeper questions to find out more
"We need to probe the indemnity clause." (Investigar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

GRAMMAR

Para perguntar de forma educada, use perguntas indiretas: comece com um opener (Could you tell me..., Do you know..., I was wondering..., Would you mind telling me...) e depois use a ordem NORMAL (sujeito + verbo), SEM inversao e SEM do/does/did. Para perguntas de sim/nao, use if ou whether. Direto: "Where is the office?" Indireto: "Could you tell me where the office is?" / To ask politely, use indirect questions: start with an opener and then use normal word order (subject + verb), with no inversion and no do/does/did. For yes/no questions, use if or whether.

Type / TipoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Wh- questionopener + question word + subject + verb"Could you tell me where the office is?"
Yes / No questionopener + if / whether + subject + verb"Do you know if the price is final?"
Polite openersCould you tell me..., Do you know..., I was wondering..., Would you mind telling me...
