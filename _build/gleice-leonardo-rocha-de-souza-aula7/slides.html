Chapter 3 -- The Irregular Code

The Irregular Past

No -ed here -- the verb changes shape

Listen and Notice

What Is Different?

These verbs do not end in -ed. Look at the orange words.

"I went to a conference in 2015."

"She got a great job at Sanofi."

"We made a big decision."

"They left the company in 2019."

The Past Code

Learn Them One by One

BasePastBasePast
gowentbecomebecame
getgottaketook
makemadewinwon
leaveleftfindfound

Key idea: irregular verbs do not add -ed; each one has its own past form. The form is the same for I, you, he, she, we, they. For negatives and questions, use did plus the base verb: I did not go, Did you go?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I goed to the meeting. ... Did you went there?"
"I went to the meeting. ... Did you go there?"

Irregular verbs never take -ed. After did, always use the base verb.

Practice

Make it Past

"Last year I _____got a promotion." (get)
"In 2018 she _____made a big decision." (make)
"We _____went to a new office." (go)
"They _____won the prize in May." (win)
