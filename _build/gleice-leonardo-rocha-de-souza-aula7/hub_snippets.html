Key Moments -- Simple Past (Irregular Verbs)

Tell the turning points of your career. New vocabulary: go, get, make, leave, become, take, win, find. The simple past of irregular verbs does not add -ed: go to went, get to got, make to made. After did, use the base verb (Did you go?).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each verb and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Go -- to travel to a place or an event (past: went)
"I went to a conference in 2018." (ir)
Get -- to receive or obtain something (past: got)
"I got my first job in 2009." (conseguir / obter)
Make -- to create something or take a decision (past: made)
"I made an important decision." (fazer / tomar)
Leave -- to go away from a place or a job (past: left)
"I left my old company in 2015." (sair / deixar)
Become -- to start to be something new (past: became)
"I became a manager last year." (tornar-se)
Take -- to accept or choose a role or a chance (past: took)
"I took a new role at Sanofi." (assumir / aceitar)
Win -- to be the best and receive a prize (past: won)
"My team won an award." (ganhar / vencer)
Find -- to discover something for the first time (past: found)
"I found a great opportunity." (encontrar / achar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Paulo got his first job in 2009 at a small company, and he found a real passion for the work. In 2013 he made a big decision and left for a bigger company. He went to another city and became a team leader. Two years ago, his team won an important award.

1. When did Paulo get his first job?
A He got it in 2009.
B He got it in 2013.
C He never got a job.
2. What did Paulo do in 2013?
A He won an award.
B He made a decision and left for a bigger company.
C He stopped working.
3. What happened two years ago?
A He started university.
B He left the country.
C His team won an important award.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Simple Past (Irregular Verbs)

GRAMMAR

Alguns verbos não ganham -ed no passado: eles mudam de forma e você memoriza cada um. Para negativa e pergunta, use did + o verbo na base. / Some verbs do not add -ed in the past: they change shape and you memorize each one. For negatives and questions, use did + the base verb.

BasePast / PassadoBasePast / Passado
gowentbecomebecame
getgottaketook
makemadewinwon
leaveleftfindfound
Regra de ouroa forma do passado é igual para I, you, he, she, we, they. Depois de did, use a base: "Did you go?" (não "Did you went?").
