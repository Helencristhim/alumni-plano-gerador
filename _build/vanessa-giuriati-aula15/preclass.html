Aula 15 -- Pre-class

Describing People, Places & Feelings

Bring descriptions to life: paint the background of a scene with the past continuous (was/were + -ing) and use the senses. Contrast it with the past simple for the one action that breaks in (I was walking home when it started to rain). Key words: bright, crowded, peaceful, gorgeous, gloomy, narrow, breeze, scent.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Bright -- full of light, or having a strong, vivid color (claro/vivo)
"The room was bright and full of sunlight."
Crowded -- full of people, with little room to move (lotado/cheio)
"The square was crowded that morning."
Peaceful -- calm, quiet, and free from noise or worry (tranquilo/sereno)
"The garden was peaceful in the evening."
Narrow -- not wide; small in distance from one side to the other (estreito)
"We walked down a narrow street."
Gorgeous -- extremely beautiful or attractive (lindo/deslumbrante)
"She was arranging gorgeous flowers."
Gloomy -- dark and dull, in a way that feels sad or heavy (sombrio/lúgubre)
"It was a gloomy old building."
Breeze -- a light, gentle wind that feels pleasant (brisa)
"A soft breeze was moving through the stalls."
Scent -- a smell, usually a pleasant one (the 'c' is silent) (aroma/cheiro)
"The scent of fresh bread filled the air."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

It was early, and the old market was waking up. The sun was rising behind the narrow streets, and a soft breeze was blowing through the stalls. The square was already crowded. People were calling out prices, and the scent of fresh bread was filling the air. An old woman was arranging gorgeous flowers in bright buckets, in front of a gloomy old building. I was standing there, just watching, when a man started to play the violin. Suddenly the square went quiet, and everything felt peaceful.

1. What was happening in the background as the market woke up?
A Everything was closed and empty.
B The sun was rising, a breeze was blowing, and people were calling out prices.
C It was raining and nobody was there.
2. What interrupted the scene?
A The old woman closed her flower stall.
B A man started to play the violin.
C It suddenly got very crowded.
3. Which sentence uses the background and the interruption correctly?
A "I standing there when a man was starting to play."
B "I was standing there when a man started to play."
C "I was stand there when a man start to play."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Continuous for the Scene

GRAMMAR

Como pintar o pano de fundo de uma cena. / How to paint the background of a scene.

FormEnglishPortuguês
was / were + verb-ingThe sun was rising. People were calling out.O fundo (o que estava acontecendo)
was vs wereI/he/she/it = was; you/we/they = were.Escolha pelo sujeito
...-ing + when + past simpleI was walking home when it started to rain.Fundo + ação que interrompe
TipUse the past continuous for the background (things in progress) and the past simple for the one quick action that breaks in.
⚠ CarefulDo not drop was/were ("I standing" → "I was standing"). Match the subject ("people were", not "people was"). The interruption is past simple ("a man started", not "was starting").

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Think of a place you will never forget — a market, a beach, a street, a room. Describe it out loud: paint the background with the past continuous (the sun was rising, people were talking), add what you could see, hear, and smell, and end with one thing that happened while you were there (I was ... when ...). Use at least three of today's words and speak slowly.
