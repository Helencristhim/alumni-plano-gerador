Chapter 3

The Background

Past continuous: what was happening

Grammar Discovery

Painting the Background

"The sun was rising behind the streets."

"A soft breeze was blowing through the square."

"People were calling out prices."

"I was standing there when a man started to play."

FormUseExample
was / were + verb-ingBackground: what was happeningThe sun was rising. People were calling out.
was (I/he/she/it)
were (you/we/they)		Choose was or were by the subjectI was standing. We were watching.
was/were -ing + when + past simpleThe interruption: a long background action is broken by one quick action. "I was standing there when a man started to play." Background = past continuous; the action that breaks in = past simple.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I standing in the crowded square."

✓ RIGHT

"I was standing in the crowded square."

✗ WRONG

"People was calling out prices."

✓ RIGHT

"People were calling out prices."

✗ WRONG

"I was watching when a man was starting to play."

✓ RIGHT

"I was watching when a man started to play."

For the background, use was/were + -ing (and match the subject: I/he/she = was, you/we/they = were). For the one action that breaks in, use the past simple, not the continuous.

Listening

Why Details Matter

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. Why does the speaker say "nice" is not enough?
Because "nice" tells you nothing — you cannot see it. The small details, noticed with all your senses, are what bring a description to life.
2. What grammar trick does he give?
Use the past continuous (was/were + -ing) to paint the background, and the past simple for the one action that breaks through it.
3. What example shows the contrast?
The background was moving (the sun was rising, people were talking), and then one thing happened — suddenly, a man started to sing.
