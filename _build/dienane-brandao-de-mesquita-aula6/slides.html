Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Prepositions of place: in, on, at

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

When do we use in, on, and at?

"The office is in a quiet street."

"The bank is on the corner."

"I wait at the bus stop."

"The pharmacy is next to the bank."

Grammar Rule

In, On, At

WordUseExample
ininside a space or areain a quiet street
onon a surface or a streeton the corner
ata specific point or placeat the bus stop
next to / between / across fromto show position near other placesnext to the bank

Key idea: use at for a point (at the bus stop), on for a street or corner (on the corner), and in for inside a space (in the bank).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The bank is in the corner."
"The bank is on the corner."

The corner is a point on the street, so we always say on the corner.
