Lesson 1 -- Diagnostic + Reactivation

Series
Brooklyn Nine-Nine -- Season 1, Episode 1 (Netflix)

Your own show — this time with a target. In the pilot, a new captain walks in and every single character has to say who they are, what they do and how long they have been doing it. Same task as this lesson, at native speed: exactly the pace you said you struggle to follow.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 moments in which someone describes their job or their track record — and label the tense (present simple? present perfect? past simple?).

Podcast
How I Built This (NPR) -- any episode

Every episode is one person telling their own career story: what they DO today (present simple), how long they have been doing it (present perfect, for/since) and what they DID in one specific year (past simple). It is the grammar of this lesson in the wild, in the business register you need.

Tip: listen to 15 minutes ONE time, no pausing. Then retell the opening of the story out loud in 60 seconds — record it and play it back. Your bottleneck is production, not comprehension.

YouTube
Learn English Tenses: The Present Perfect Continuous

The structure you will lean on in every status meeting, and the one you have never produced with confidence: have been + verb-ing for whatever started and is STILL going on ("We've been waiting for the specification since March", "I've been working with German suppliers since 2019"). The lesson introduces it; this video digs deeper.

Tip: after the video, say 5 sentences about YOUR project at Motiva — two in the present perfect continuous (something still going on), two in the present perfect (for/since) and one in the past simple (finished time). Out loud, with nothing written down first.
