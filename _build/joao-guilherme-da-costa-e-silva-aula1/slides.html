Chapter 3: The Code

Your Career in Three Tenses

I oversee · I've been working · I spent

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I oversee the signaling design for two metro lines."

"I have worked in railway signaling for eight years."

"I have been working with German suppliers since 2019."

"I spent two months in San Francisco in 2023."

Why green for eight years and since 2019, but blue for 2023?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Your current role, your routine, permanent factsI oversee the signaling design.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now (for / since)I have worked in signaling for eight years.
Present Perfect Continuous
have been + verb-ing		An activity that started in the past and is still going on nowI have been working with German suppliers since 2019.
Past Simple
verb + -ed / irregular		Finished action at a finished time (in 2023, last year)I spent two months in San Francisco in 2023.

for + a period (for eight years) · since + a starting point (since 2019).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am a signaling engineer for eight years."
"I've been a signaling engineer for eight years."
"I have commissioned equipment in San Francisco in 2023."
"I commissioned equipment in San Francisco in 2023."

Duration up to now takes have / has + past participle with for / since. A finished time (in 2023, last year) always takes the past simple.
