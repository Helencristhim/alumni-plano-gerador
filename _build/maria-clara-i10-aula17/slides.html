Open, sustain, or close? What does each orange move do?

MoveWhat you doExample
OpenStart with an easy question — never a flat statement.Is this your first time here? · How are you finding it?
SustainReact with a short response, then ask a question back.That's interesting! And what about you?
Shift topicMove to a new subject gently, so it feels natural.By the way, what do you do exactly?
CloseEnd warmly with a thank-you — never in silence.It was really nice talking with you.
The key ideaOpen with a question, keep the ball moving by asking back, and close with warmth. This is how you break the ice.question → react + ask back → thank-you

The secret: small talk is not about clever words. It is a question to open, a reaction plus a question to keep going, and a warm thank-you to close.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"What you do at the university?"
"What do you do at the university?"
"You are from where?"
"Where are you from?"
"How you find the conference?"
"How are you finding the conference?"

One rule fixes all three: a question needs its helper. Do for present simple, are for present continuous — and the question word comes first.

Chapter 4: The Round Table

A Seat Next to a Stranger

Open it. Keep it going. Close it well.

Dialogue

The Conference Dinner

D
Good evening. I don't think we've met — I'm Daniel, a rector from a university here in Seoul.
M
Nice to meet you, Daniel. Let me introduce myself — I'm Maria Clara, from Brazil. I lead a team at a federal university.
D
A pleasure. So, is this your first time in Seoul? How are you finding the conversation at these dinners?
M
It's my first time, and I love it. The host is wonderful, and every guest is so friendly. It makes a great first impression.
D
I know what you mean. By the way, what is your background? What do you do exactly?
M
I'm responsible for institutional partnerships. We work closely with international delegations. And what about you?
D
I lead the international office. That's a great topic for us — and it is very polite of you to ask. Maybe we can continue this conversation later.
M
I'd love that. It was really nice talking with you. Let me give you my card.
Comprehension

About Daniel

1. Who is Daniel, and what does he do?
He is a rector from a university in Seoul, and he leads the international office.
2. What does Daniel ask Maria Clara about?
Her first time in Seoul, and her background — what she does exactly.
3. What does Daniel suggest at the end?
That they can continue the conversation later.
