Lesson 17 -- Small Talk That Works

Talk
Celeste Headlee — "10 Ways to Have a Better Conversation" (TED, YouTube)

This is your lesson, from a journalist who talks for a living. She shows the exact moves you practiced today: open, really listen, react, and ask a question back — and how a good conversation keeps the ball moving. It is the heart of sustaining small talk at any dinner or round table.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Treasure hunt: write down 3 things she says you should do to keep a conversation going. Do not try to understand 100% — listen ONLY for the "how to keep talking" ideas.

Podcast
Business English Pod — BEP 400: Socializing with Colleagues 1: Meeting New People

This is your conference dinner, in a lesson. It teaches the exact skill of today: how to meet a person for the first time, break the ice, and talk about your name, your job, travel, and the city — then build on what the other person says. Short, clear, and made for a leader who has to open a conversation with a stranger.

Tip: listen ONCE on your way to the university, with no pauses. Then listen again and write down every phrase they use to OPEN and to KEEP the conversation going. Practice one out loud: "Is this your first time here?"
