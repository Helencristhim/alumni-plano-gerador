Chapter 3

Asking the Past

Did you...? · What did you do?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Did you go to a party?"

"What did you do?"

"Where did you go?"

"Yes, I did. / No, I did not."

TypeHow to ask about the past
Yes / NoDid you + base verb? — Did you go? Did you enjoy it?
Wh- questionWhat/Where/Who did you + base verb? — What did you do?
Short answerYes, I did. / No, I did not.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Did you went to a party?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you go to a party?"

✗ WRONG

"What you did do?"

✓ RIGHT

"What did you do?"

✗ WRONG

"Yes, I went."

✓ RIGHT

"Yes, I did."

To ask about the past, use did. After did, the verb is in the base form: go, do, visit. For a short answer, use did: Yes, I did. No, I did not.
