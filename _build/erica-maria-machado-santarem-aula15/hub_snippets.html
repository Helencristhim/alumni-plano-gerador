A Memorable Day -- Past Simple Questions & a Weekend Recap

Aprenda a fazer perguntas sobre o passado e a falar do seu fim de semana: weekend, party, visit, enjoy, museum, special, ask, fun. Gramática nova: perguntas no past simple (Did you...? What did you do? Where did you go?) e respostas curtas (Yes, I did / No, I did not). Ritmo lento e claro.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. Ele usa perguntas no past simple.

On Monday, David asks Érica: "How was your weekend? What did you do?" Érica says: "It was special! On Saturday, I went to a party. I enjoyed it a lot." David asks: "Did you go out on Sunday?" Érica answers: "Yes, I did. I visited a museum. It was a lot of fun." David smiles: "Where did you go after the museum?" Érica says: "I went home and called my daughter."

1. David asks "Did you go out?" Why "go" and not "went"?
A After "did", the verb is in the base form.
B Because it is a future plan.
C Because "go" is always wrong.
2. What did Érica do on Saturday?
A She visited a museum.
B She went to a party.
C She stayed at work.
3. How do you give a short answer to "Did you go out?"
A "Yes, I went."
B "Yes, I did." or "No, I did not."
C "Yes, I do."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple Questions

GRAMMAR

Como perguntar sobre o passado. Use did + o verbo na forma base (go, do, visit). Para sim/não: Did you go? Para perguntas com palavra-pergunta: What/Where/Who did you...? Resposta curta: Yes, I did / No, I did not. / How to ask about the past: did + base verb.

Tipo / TypeEstrutura / Structure
Sim/Não / Yes-NoDid + you + verbo base — Did you go? Did you enjoy it?
Pergunta Wh-What / Where / Who did + you + verbo base — What did you do?
Resposta curtaYes, I did. / No, I did not.
Lembre-seDepois de did, o verbo fica na BASE: "Did you go?" (não "Did you went?").

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você respondendo. Não há resposta certa ou errada.

Tell the story of a memorable weekend. Where did you go (use "I went to...")? What did you do (use the past simple, for example: I visited, I enjoyed)? Did you have fun? Then write one question you can ask a friend about their weekend (for example: What did you do?). Take your time and speak calmly.
