Chapter 4: The Causative

I had it done...

the grammar of getting things done through others

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I had the clinic accredited last year."

"We got the system rebuilt by an outside firm."

"I had my deputy run the morning rounds."

"I got my team to take ownership of their cases."

"She had her proposal turned down twice before it passed."

UseStructureExample
Arrange a service (thing)have / get + object + past participleI had the clinic accredited. We got the system rebuilt.
Direct a person (have)have + person + base verb (no "to")I had my deputy run the rounds.
Persuade a person (get)get + person + to + infinitiveI got my team to take ownership.

The causative says someone else does the action for you. For a thing, put the object BEFORE the past participle: "I had the report translated", not "I had translated the report". With "have + person", use the base verb and no "to"; with "get + person", use "to".

Language Focus

Name the Pattern

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

I had translated the report by the agency.
I had the report translated by the agency.
I had my assistant to book the room.
I had my assistant book the room.
I got the team take ownership of their cases.
I got the team to take ownership of their cases.

For a thing, put the object BEFORE the past participle: "had the report translated", not "had translated the report". With have + person, use the base verb and no "to" (had my assistant book). With get + person, you need "to" (got the team to take).

Dialogue

Learning to Let Go

O
So, Eliane. Last time we spoke, you were still signing off on everything yourself. What did you finally let go of?
E
The billing, first. I had the whole system rebuilt by an outside firm, and I got it outsourced completely.
O
Good. And the rounds? You were the bottleneck there. Every morning waited for you.
E
I had my deputy run them without me in the room. It felt wrong for a week. Then it just worked.
O
That is the hard part — you stopped trying to micromanage. How did the team react?
E
Once I got them to take ownership, they rose to it. I made each lead accountable for real outcomes.
O
And what did all of that buy you?
E
I had the red tape cut, and it freed up the bandwidth for the science. I finally learned to step back.
O
Then you have it, Eliane. The clinic runs whether you are there or not. That is leadership done through others.
