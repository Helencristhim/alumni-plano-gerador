Film
The Founder (2016)

The true story of how Ray Kroc turned one hamburger stand into an empire -- not by cooking, but by building a system and getting the work done through other people. Watch it as a case study in delegation, accountability and scaling: everything that lets a business run whether the founder is in the room or not. It is exactly this lesson's idea of leadership done through others -- for better and for worse.

Tip: while you watch, note three causatives about Kroc ("He had the restaurants standardized", "He got the franchisees to follow the system"), then say two about your own clinic.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast (Harvard Business Review)

Harvard Business Review's weekly conversation with leadership thinkers and executives. Look for the many episodes on delegating, empowering teams, holding people accountable and scaling a business through others -- exactly this lesson's theme. It is the clearest way to hear how real leaders stop being the bottleneck and get the work done through their people, in natural professional English.

Tip: pick one episode on delegation and write two causatives about a leader in it ("She had the process redesigned", "She got the team to take ownership").

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "How to use 'have something done'" (Learners' Questions)

A short, clear lesson on exactly this lesson's grammar: the causative "have something done", used when someone else does the work for you. It walks through the have + object + past participle pattern with everyday examples and warns against the classic word-order mistake. Direct connection: the grammar that lets you describe everything you get done through your team.

Tip: pause after each example and say one causative about your own work out loud -- one with a thing, one with a person.
