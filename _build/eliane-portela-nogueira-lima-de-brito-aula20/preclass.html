Lesson 20 -- Pre-class

Done Through Others -- Leadership That Scales Beyond You

Learn to say the work you get done through other people. You will meet twelve words for leading through a team -- delegate, oversee, empower, accountable, micromanage, bottleneck, outsource, onboard, red tape, bandwidth, step back, entrust -- and master the causative (I had the clinic accredited / I had my deputy run the rounds / I got my team to take ownership). Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words for leading through other people. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras para liderar através das pessoas. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Delegate -- to give a task or authority to someone else instead of doing it yourself / delegar
"I finally learned to delegate the decisions I used to make alone."
Oversee -- to supervise work and be responsible for how it turns out / supervisionar
"She oversees three teams without doing their work for them."
Empower -- to give someone the authority and confidence to make their own decisions / dar autonomia, empoderar
"Good leaders empower the people around them."
Accountable -- required to explain your actions and answer for the results / responsável, que presta contas
"Each team lead is accountable for real outcomes."
Micromanage -- to control every small detail of other people's work / microgerenciar
"I stopped trying to micromanage every single case."
Bottleneck -- a single point that slows an entire process down / gargalo
"Nothing moved unless it moved through me -- I was the bottleneck."
Outsource -- to have work done by an outside company instead of your own team / terceirizar
"We outsource the billing now, so my team can focus on patients."
Onboard -- to bring a new person into a team and get them up to speed / integrar (alguém novo)
"I got the new doctors properly onboarded before the busy season."
Red tape -- excessive rules and paperwork that slow things down / burocracia
"I had the worst of the red tape cut so decisions could move."
Bandwidth -- the time and mental capacity you have to take on more work / capacidade, tempo disponível
"Delegating freed up my bandwidth for the science."
Step back -- to stop handling the details so that other people can lead / dar um passo atrás, se afastar
"I learned to step back so that others could step up."
Entrust -- to make someone responsible for something important / confiar (algo a alguém)
"To entrust a decision, you have to accept it may be done differently."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane no longer does everything herself. She had the billing system rebuilt by an outside firm and had the red tape cut so decisions could move -- notice the object before the past participle. With people, the pattern changes: she had her deputy run the morning rounds (have + person + base verb, no to), and she got her team to take ownership of their cases (get + person + to + infinitive). In every case, someone else does the action; Eliane only arranges it. That is the causative -- the grammar of getting the work done through others.

1. "She had the billing system rebuilt by an outside firm." Who rebuilt the system?
A Eliane rebuilt it herself.
B An outside firm did it; Eliane arranged it (have + object + past participle).
C Nobody rebuilt it yet.
2. Why is it "I had my deputy run the rounds" and not "run to"?
A After have + person you use the base verb with no "to" (have my deputy run).
B Because "run" is always used with "to".
C Because "deputy" is plural.
3. Which sentence uses the causative correctly?
A "I had translated the contract by the agency."
B "I had the contract translated by the agency."
C "I got the team take ownership of the project."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Causative

GRAMMAR

The causative says that someone else does an action for you: you arrange or receive it. / O causativo diz que outra pessoa faz a ação por você: você apenas arranja ou recebe.

Use / UsoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
A thing / uma coisahave / get + object + past participleI had the clinic accredited. We got the system rebuilt.
Direct a person / mandar alguém (have)have + person + base verb (no "to")I had my deputy run the rounds.
Persuade a person / convencer alguém (get)get + person + to + infinitiveI got my team to take ownership.

For a thing, the object goes before the past participle: had the report translated, not had translated the report. / Para uma coisa, o objeto vem antes do particípio: had the report translated, nunca had translated the report.
