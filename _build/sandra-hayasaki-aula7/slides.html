Chapter 3 -- The Code

Better, the Best, or as Good as

Three ways to compare two or more things

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence compares options. Notice the orange words.

"The first phone was cheaper than the second one."

"The mountains were more beautiful than the beach."

"It was the best choice I made all year."

"The two options were almost as good as each other."

The Rule

Comparative, Superlative & Equal

TypeShort adjectiveLong adjective
Comparative (2 things)cheap → cheaper thanmore expensive than
Superlative (all)cheap → the cheapestthe most expensive
Equal (the same)as good as / not as expensive as

Tip: short adjectives add -er / -est (cheaper, the cheapest). Long adjectives use more / the most (more expensive, the most expensive). Irregular: good → better / the best; bad → worse / the worst. Use as + adjective + as when two things are equal.

Common Mistake

Never Mix -er and more

"This phone is more cheap and more better than that one."
"This phone is cheaper and better than that one."

Short adjectives take -er (cheaper). Use more only with long adjectives (more expensive). Never use both together.

"So, for the weekend, which one do you prefer: the beach or the mountains?"

S

"They are very different. The beach is cheaper, but the mountains are more beautiful."

M

"True. So which option is worth it for you?"

"For me, the mountains give better value. The air is fresher and the views are the most beautiful."
