Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Quality -- how good or well-made something is
"This phone has a better camera quality." (qualidade)
Prefer -- to like one thing more than another
"I prefer tea to coffee in the morning." (preferir)
Similar -- almost the same; alike in some way
"The two phones are very similar in price." (parecido/semelhante)
Different -- not the same; not alike
"The two trips were completely different." (diferente)
Choice -- a decision between two or more things
"It was the best choice I made all year." (escolha)
Value -- how much something is worth or how useful it is
"This option gives me the best value for my money." (valor/custo-benefício)
Worth -- good enough to deserve the money, time, or effort
"Is this trip worth the extra cost?" (valer a pena)
Option -- one of the things you can choose
"There were so many options in the store." (opção)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Last month Mark needed a new phone. There were so many options. The first phone was cheaper, but the camera was worse and the battery did not last as long. The second one was more expensive, but the quality was much better. Mark does not take many photos, so the best camera was not the most important thing. He prefers something simple, so he chose the cheaper phone. It was the best choice of the year. The lesson: the most expensive option is not always worth it.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives, Superlatives & as...as

GRAMMAR

Para comparar DUAS coisas, use o comparativo: adjetivo curto + -er than (cheaper than) ou more + adjetivo longo + than (more expensive than). Para dizer que algo é o melhor de TODOS, use o superlativo: the + adjetivo + -est (the cheapest) ou the most + adjetivo (the most beautiful). Para dizer que duas coisas são iguais, use as + adjetivo + as (as good as). / To compare two things use the comparative; to name the top of all use the superlative; to say two things are equal use as + adjective + as. Irregular: good → better / the best; bad → worse / the worst.

TypeShort adjectiveLong adjective
Comparative (2)cheaper thanmore expensive than
Superlative (all)the cheapestthe most expensive
Equalas good as / not as expensive as
