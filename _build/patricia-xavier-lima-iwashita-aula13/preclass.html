Giving & Receiving Feedback

Giving honest, kind feedback and receiving it well. Key words: feedback, strength, improvement, acknowledge, appreciate, constructive, perspective, align, takeaway, tone. Structure: diplomatic softening (perhaps / it might be better to / I'd suggest / have you considered).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Feedback -- comments about your work, to help you improve (feedback, retorno)
"Thank you for the feedback on my demo."
Strength -- something a person does very well (ponto forte)
"Your clear demo was a real strength."
Improvement -- a change that makes something better (melhoria)
"There is one small area for improvement."
Acknowledge -- to accept that something is true or fair (reconhecer, admitir)
"I acknowledge that I rushed the slide."
Appreciate -- to be grateful for something (agradecer, valorizar)
"I appreciate your honest feedback."
Constructive -- helpful and meant to improve, not to criticize (construtivo)
"She always gives constructive feedback."
Perspective -- the way a person sees or thinks about something (perspectiva)
"May I share my perspective?"
Align -- to agree and move in the same direction (alinhar)
"Let's align on the next steps."
Takeaway -- the main point you remember and act on (conclusão principal)
"My takeaway is to send the deck earlier."
Tone -- the feeling or attitude in the way you speak (tom)
"Keep the tone warm and respectful."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

In her one-on-one, David told Patrícia that her demo was a real strength. She appreciated the feedback and asked about any area for improvement. Instead of saying "you talked too fast," David kept the tone warm: "Perhaps we could slow down on the pricing slide, and it might be better to share the deck first." Patrícia acknowledged the point calmly. "I'd suggest we align on the agenda earlier," she added. "Have you considered sending it the day before?" Her takeaway was clear, and the feedback stayed constructive from start to finish.

1. Por que David diz "Perhaps we could slow down" em vez de "You talk too fast"?
A Porque ele não tem certeza do que aconteceu.
B Porque suaviza a crítica e mantém o tom diplomático.
C Porque é uma ordem direta e firme.
2. "Have you considered sending it the day before?" usa esta estrutura porque:
A Oferece uma ideia como pergunta, de forma gentil (consider + verbo-ing).
B É uma acusação sobre um erro do passado.
C Fala de uma rotina que se repete toda semana.
3. Which sentence gives feedback most diplomatically?
A "You should have tested it."
B "Next time, it might be better to test it first."
C "Your work is wrong."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Diplomatic Softening

GRAMMAR

Como suavizar feedback e sugestões para soar diplomático (explicação em inglês e português).

SoftenerUse / UsoExample
Perhaps we could...Suaviza uma sugestão. Softens a suggestion.Perhaps we could check this.
It might be better to + verboSugere uma mudança gentilmente. Suggests a change gently.It might be better to slow down.
I'd suggest (that)...Dá conselho com educação (sem "to": suggest + frase). Polite advice.I'd suggest we align first.
Have you considered + verbo-ing?Oferece a ideia como pergunta. Offers an idea as a question.Have you considered sending it earlier?
Avoid / EviteNão use o acusatório "you should have..." → troque por "next time we could..."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

A teammate rushed the pricing slide. You want to say it kindly. You say:
A "You went too fast and it was bad."
B "It might be better to slow down on the pricing slide."
C "You should have gone slower."
Your manager gives you feedback. The best way to receive it is:
A "Thank you, I appreciate that. My takeaway is to send the deck earlier."
B "That is not my fault."
C "Why are you always criticizing me?"
You want to offer an idea gently, as a question. Which is correct?
A "Have you considered to send it earlier?"
B "Have you considered sending it earlier?"
C "You must send it earlier."
You disagree, but want to stay diplomatic. You say:
A "I see it a little differently. May I share my perspective?"
B "You are completely wrong."
C "No. Do it again."
