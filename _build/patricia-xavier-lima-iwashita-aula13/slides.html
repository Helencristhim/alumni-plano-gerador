Chapter 3: The Code

Soft & Diplomatic

Perhaps... · It might be better to...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Perhaps we could check this part again."

"It might be better to send the deck first."

"I'd suggest we align on the agenda."

"Have you considered testing it first?"

What do the green phrases do to the feedback?

SoftenerUseExample
Perhaps we could...Soften a suggestionPerhaps we could check this.
It might be better to...Suggest a change gentlyIt might be better to slow down.
I'd suggest (that)...Give advice politelyI'd suggest we align first.
Have you considered + -ing?Offer an idea as a questionHave you considered sending it earlier?

These phrases turn a hard order into a soft invitation. Swap accusatory "you should have..." for "next time we could..."

Common Mistake

Harsh vs Diplomatic

"You should have tested it."
"Next time, we could test it first."
"Your report is bad."
"Perhaps the report could be a little clearer."

Avoid blaming words like "you should have...". Lead with a strength, then offer the change as a soft suggestion with perhaps, it might be better to, or next time we could.

Chapter 4: Feedback with David

The One-on-One

Give feedback kindly, and receive it well

Dialogue

Thanks for the Call

D
Thanks for the call today, Patrícia. Overall, your demo was a real strength.
P
Thank you, David. I really appreciate that. Is there any area for improvement?
D
Perhaps it might be better to slow down a little on the pricing slide.
P
That is a fair point. I acknowledge that — I did rush it a bit.
D
And have you considered sharing the deck before the call?
P
Good idea. My takeaway is to send it earlier and align on the agenda. May I share my perspective on the timing too?
