Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read Tânia's little story and answer the questions.

Let me tell you an anecdote. Last year, while I was waiting at the airport, something incredible happened. I was reading a book when a woman sat next to me. As soon as she spoke, I realized she was from my hometown. It was so embarrassing, because I did not notice she was my old neighbor! Luckily, we talked for two hours, and eventually we became friends again. What an unbelievable coincidence!

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- When & While in the Past

Grammar

Para contar uma história, use while + ação longa (was + verbo-ing) e when + ação curta (passado simples): "I was reading when a man sat next to me." Use as soon as para "assim que". A ação longa é o fundo; a ação curta a interrompe. / To tell a story, use while + a long action (was + verb-ing) and when + a short action (past simple): "I was reading when a man sat next to me." Use as soon as for "the moment that". The long action is the background; the short action interrupts it.

Estrutura / StructureEnglishPortuguês
while + was/were + -ingWhile I was waiting, ...Enquanto eu esperava, ...
when + past simple... when a man sat down.... quando um homem sentou.
as soon as + past simpleAs soon as she spoke, I knew.Assim que ela falou, eu soube.
long action + short actionI was reading when it happened.Eu lia quando isso aconteceu.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Record yourself answering the question. There is no right or wrong answer.

Tell a friend a short story about something that happened to you. Say what you were doing, what happened next, and how it ended. Use "while", "when", "as soon as", and words like incredible, embarrassing, luckily, and eventually. Take your time.
