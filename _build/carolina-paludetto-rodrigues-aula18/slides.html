Grammar Rule

Talking About Health

TypeRuleExample
How you feelI feel + adjectiveI feel tired
StateI am + adjectiveI am sick
SymptomI have a + nounI have a headache
NegativeI don't feel + adjectiveI don't feel well
QuestionHow do you feel?How do you feel?

Key idea: use I feel + adjective for how you feel (I feel tired), not "I am feel". For symptoms, use I have a + noun (I have a headache, I have a fever). The negative is I don't feel well. To ask about health, say How do you feel?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am feel tired." / "I feel a headache."
"I feel tired." / "I have a headache."

For feelings, use I feel + adjective (not "I am feel"). For symptoms, use I have a + noun (not "I feel a headache").
