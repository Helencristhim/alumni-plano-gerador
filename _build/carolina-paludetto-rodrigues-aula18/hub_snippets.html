Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Health -- how well or strong your body and mind are
"Good health is more important than money." (Saúde)
Healthy -- strong and well, not sick
"I eat fruit to stay healthy." (Saudável)
Sick -- not well; your body feels bad or ill
"He is sick, so he is staying home today." (Doente)
Headache -- a pain in your head
"I have a headache, so I want to rest." (Dor de cabeça)
Fever -- when your body is too hot because you are ill
"She has a fever and feels very hot." (Febre)
Medicine -- something you take to feel better when you are sick
"I take medicine when I have a fever." (Remédio)
Energy -- the power to do things and feel active
"After a good sleep, I have a lot of energy." (Energia)
Rest -- to stop and relax so your body feels better
"When you are sick, you should rest in bed." (Descansar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- feel and have for health

GRAMMAR

Para dizer como você está, use I feel + adjetivo (I feel tired) ou I am + adjetivo (I am sick). Para sintomas, use I have a + substantivo (I have a headache / a fever). Para perguntar, How do you feel? / What's wrong? / We use I feel + adjective (I feel tired) or I am + adjective (I am sick). For symptoms, use I have a + noun (I have a headache). To ask, How do you feel? or What's wrong?

TypeRuleExample
How you feelI feel + adjectiveI feel tired
StateI am + adjectiveI am sick
SymptomI have a + nounI have a headache
NegativeI don't feel + adjectiveI don't feel well
QuestionHow do you feel?How do you feel today?

Key point / Ponto-chave: use I feel + adjetivo (I feel tired), NUNCA "I am feel". Para sintomas, use I have a + substantivo (I have a headache, I have a fever). Para perguntar, How do you feel?
