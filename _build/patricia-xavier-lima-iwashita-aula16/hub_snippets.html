Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Metric -- a number you measure to track how well something is going (métrica / indicador)
"Three metrics improved this quarter."
Health score -- a single number that shows how healthy a customer account is (pontuação de saúde)
"The overall health score is seventy-eight out of one hundred."
Churn -- the share of customers who stop using or cancel the product (perda de clientes / evasão)
"Churn decreased again, so we are below the target."
Retention -- keeping customers active and using the product over time (retenção)
"Retention is strong: almost all licenses are still active."
Adoption rate -- the percentage of users who actively use the product (taxa de adoção)
"The adoption rate increased to seventy-four percent."
Trend -- the general direction a number moves in over time (tendência)
"The trend over the year is positive."
Increase -- to go up, or a rise in a number (aumentar / aumento)
"The adoption rate increased from sixty to seventy-four percent."
Decrease -- to go down, or a fall in a number (diminuir / queda)
"Churn decreased again this quarter."
Compared to -- used to show the difference between two numbers or times (em comparação com)
"Compared to last quarter, the score is higher."
Target -- the number you plan and aim to reach (meta / alvo)
"Adoption is well above our target of seventy."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

In the quarterly review, Patrícia presents the metrics. The health score is higher than last quarter, and the adoption rate increased to seventy-four percent — higher than the target of seventy. Churn decreased again, so it is now lower than before. The new dashboard is more efficient than the old report, so the data is easier to read. There is one watch area: support tickets had a significant rise last month, not a small one, and the trend is something the team will follow every week.

1. Para um adjetivo curto como "high", qual forma de comparativo usamos?
A "higher than" (adjetivo curto recebe -er).
B "more high than" (sempre usamos "more").
C "more higher than" (more + -er juntos).
2. Para um adjetivo longo como "efficient", qual forma usamos?
A "efficienter than".
B "more efficient than" (adjetivo longo usa "more").
C "more efficienter than".
3. Which sentence describes a trend correctly?
A "Churn is more low than the target."
B "Churn decreased again this quarter."
C "Churn is gooder than last year."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Trends

GRAMMAR

Como comparar números e descrever tendências (explicação em inglês e português).

PatternUse / UsoExample
short adj + -er thanAdjetivo curto (high, low) recebe -er. Nunca "more higher".The score is higher than last quarter.
more + long adj + thanAdjetivo longo (efficient, positive) usa more, não "-er".It is more efficient than the old report.
increased / decreasedVerbos de tendência: o número subiu ou desceu.Adoption increased; churn decreased.
a slight / significant riseSubstantivo para o tamanho da mudança.a slight rise vs a significant rise

Irregulares: good → better, bad → worse. (Nunca "gooder" ou "more good".)
