Customer Health Report — Q3 Summary

This report gives a quick summary of your account's health for the third quarter. The overall health score is seventy-eight out of one hundred. Compared to last quarter, when the score was seventy-two, this is a clear increase, and the trend over the year is positive. A health score is a single number that combines how much your team uses the product, how happy your users are, and how many support issues you open. A higher score means a healthier account.

Three metrics improved this quarter. First, your adoption rate increased from sixty percent to seventy-four percent, which is higher than our target of seventy. In other words, more of your users now log in and work in the platform every week. Second, retention is strong: almost all of your licenses are still active, so very few users left. Third, churn decreased again. Churn is the share of users who stop using the product, and a lower number here is better. Compared to the same quarter last year, churn is much lower.

One area needs attention. Last month, the number of support tickets rose quickly — a significant rise, not a small one — and daily logins in the finance team decreased a little. We believe the new dashboard confused a few users, so the next steps are simple. First, we will run a short walkthrough for the finance team. After that, we will track the same metrics every week and compare them to the target. Our goal is for every key number to move in the right direction before the next review.

Adapted for class use from a customer success health report

Chapter 4: The Code

Comparing the Numbers

higher than · lower than · increased · a significant rise

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"The adoption rate is higher than last quarter."

"Churn is lower than our target."

"Support tickets increased last month."

"This dashboard is more efficient than the old one."

PatternUseExample
short adj + -er thancompare with a one-syllable adjective (high, low)higher than, lower than the target
more + long adj + thancompare with a longer adjective (efficient, positive)more efficient than the old report
increased / decreasedsay a number went up or down (trend verbs)adoption increased; churn decreased
a slight / significant risedescribe the SIZE of the change as a nouna slight rise vs a significant rise

Short adjective: add -er (higher). Long adjective: use more (more efficient). Never both: not "more higher". Irregular: good → better, bad → worse.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Our health score is more higher than before."
"Our health score is higher than before."
"Retention is more good than last year."
"Retention is better than last year."
"The new dashboard is efficienter than the old one."
"The new dashboard is more efficient than the old one."

Use -er OR more, never both. Good becomes better. Long adjectives take more (more efficient), not "-er".

Chapter 5: The Review Call

Presenting the Metrics

Dialogue

The Quarterly Review

P
Thanks for joining, David. Overall, your health score is seventy-eight, which is higher than last quarter.
D
That is good to hear. Which metric improved the most since our last review?
P
Adoption. Your adoption rate increased to seventy-four percent, well above the target of seventy.
D
Excellent. And what about churn? My board always asks about that.
P
Churn decreased again, so it is now lower than last quarter. Retention is strong too.
D
Is there anything that went the wrong way?
P
Yes. Support tickets rose significantly last month, so we will run a walkthrough and track the trend weekly.
Comprehension

About the Review

1. What is the health score, and how does it compare to last quarter?
Seventy-eight, which is higher than last quarter.
2. Which metric improved the most?
Adoption: the adoption rate increased to 74%, above the 70% target.
3. What went the wrong way, and what is Patrícia's plan?
Support tickets rose significantly; she will run a walkthrough and track the trend weekly.
