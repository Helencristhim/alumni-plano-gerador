Aula 5 -- Pre-class

Describing People & Places

Descreva sua cidade e seu mundo: crowded, humid, lively, charming, historic, affordable, welcoming, breathtaking. Estruturas: comparativos ("hotter than", "more lively than") e superlativos ("the biggest", "the most welcoming", "the best"). Missão: descrever Belém para uma estrangeira.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Crowded -- full of people, with little space
"The market is crowded on Saturdays." (Cheio / lotado)
Humid -- hot air, full of water
"Belém is hot and humid all year." (Úmido)
Lively -- full of life, energy, and movement
"The Ver-o-Peso market is very lively." (Animado / cheio de vida)
Charming -- beautiful in a delicate, attractive way
"The old town is small but charming." (Encantador)
Historic -- old and important in history
"Belém has a historic city center." (Histórico)
Affordable -- not expensive
"Life in Belém is more affordable than in Lisbon." (Acessível / em conta)
Welcoming -- friendly to visitors
"People in the North are very welcoming." (Acolhedor)
Breathtaking -- so beautiful it stops your breath
"The sunset over the river is breathtaking." (De tirar o fôlego)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

GRAMMAR

Comparar duas coisas e dizer o nº 1 do grupo. / Compare two things and say the number one.

AdjetivoComparativo / ComparativeSuperlativo / Superlative
curto: hot, bighotter / bigger than (mais ... que)the hottest / biggest (o mais ...)
longo: lively, affordablemore lively thanthe most lively
irregular: good, badbetter / worse than (melhor/pior que)the best / the worst (o melhor/pior)

Quiz

A "Not really. Belém is hotter and more humid than Lisbon."
B "Belém is more hot that Lisbon."
C "Lisbon and Belém is equal."
