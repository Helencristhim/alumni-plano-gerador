Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Revisão do Bloco 1. Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Account -- a company or customer you take care of (conta/cliente)
"I manage thirty premium accounts."
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest in the project (parte interessada)
"Every stakeholder joins the kickoff call."
Onboarding -- helping a new customer start using the product (integração inicial)
"We are onboarding the client this week."
Experience -- knowledge you gain from doing a job over time (experiência)
"I have ten years of experience."
Handle -- to deal with or manage something (lidar com)
"I handle the difficult escalations."
Update -- the latest news about a project's status (atualização)
"Let me give you a quick update."
On track -- going as planned, on schedule (no rumo certo)
"The rollout is on track for Friday."
Blocker -- something that stops the work from moving forward (impedimento)
"There is one blocker to solve."
Follow up -- to check or act on something after a meeting (dar seguimento)
"I will follow up with you tomorrow."
Agenda -- the list of topics for a meeting (pauta)
"Let me share the agenda for our call."
Clarify -- to make something clearer (esclarecer)
"Could you clarify the second point?"
Recap -- a short summary of the main points (recapitulação)
"Let me give you a quick recap."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Patrícia is preparing for a check-in call with Mark. She manages the Acme account, and she has worked in customer success for ten years. Right now, her team is onboarding Mark's users. Last week, they finished the data migration, and she has prepared the training sessions. On the call she will ask: "Could you tell me if you need extra licenses?" The next release goes live on Friday.

1. Por que "her team is onboarding Mark's users" usa o present continuous?
A Porque é uma rotina semanal.
B Porque a ação está acontecendo agora.
C Porque terminou num dia específico.
2. "She has worked in customer success for ten years" usa o present perfect porque:
A É experiência que começou no passado e continua até hoje (for ten years).
B É uma ação que acontece neste momento.
C É uma rotina de toda semana.
3. Which indirect question has the correct word order?
A "Could you tell me if do you need extra licenses?"
B "Could you tell me if you need extra licenses?"
C "Could you tell me if need you extra licenses?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Four Structures of Block 1

GRAMMAR

As quatro estruturas que você usa numa call (explicação em inglês e português).

StructureUse / UsoExample
Present Continuous
am / is / are + -ing		Ação acontecendo agora. Happening now.We are onboarding your team.
Present Simple
verb / verb-s		Seu papel e rotinas. Your role and routines.I manage thirty accounts.
Present Perfect
have / has + particípio		Experiência, sem hora exata; com since/for. Experience, no exact time.I have worked here for ten years.
Past Simple
verb-ed		Ação terminada com hora exata. Finished action, exact time.I joined in 2019.
Indirect Question
Could you tell me + sujeito + verbo		Pergunta educada; ordem normal (sem inversão). Polite question, normal order.Could you tell me when it is ready?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação real de trabalho.

Mark asks: "What are you working on right now?" You answer:
A "Right now, we are onboarding your team."
B "Right now, we onboard your team."
C "Right now, we have onboard your team."
You describe your experience. Which is correct?
A "I work in customer success since 2015."
B "I have worked in customer success since 2015."
C "I am working in customer success since 2015."
You want to ask the client politely. The best question is:
A "Could you tell me if the data is ready?"
B "Could you tell me if is the data ready?"
C "Could you tell me if does the data ready?"
You mention a finished task with an exact time. You say:
A "We finished the migration last week."
B "We have finished the migration last week."
C "We are finishing the migration last week."
