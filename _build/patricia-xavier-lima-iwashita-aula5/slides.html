Chapter 3: Four Structures

The Code of Block 1

Now · Routine · Experience · Questions

Grammar Discovery

Four Jobs, Four Structures

"Right now, we are onboarding your team."

"I manage thirty premium accounts."

"I have worked in customer success for ten years."

"Could you tell me when the next release is ready?"

Four colors, four jobs. What is each sentence for?

StructureJobExample
Present Continuous
am / is / are + -ing		Happening nowWe are onboarding your team.
Present Simple
verb / verb-s		Your role and routinesI manage thirty accounts.
Present Perfect
have / has + participle		Experience, no exact timeI have worked here for ten years.
Indirect Question
Could you tell me + statement order		Asking politely on a callCould you tell me when it is ready?

Past Simple gives the exact time: "I joined in 2019." Present Perfect keeps it open: "I have worked here since 2019."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Right now, I work on three accounts."
"Right now, I am working on three accounts."
"I work here since 2019."
"I have worked here since 2019."
"Could you tell me how is the rollout going?"
"Could you tell me how the rollout is going?"

Use the Present Continuous for now, the Present Perfect with since/for, and keep the normal word order (subject + verb) inside an indirect question.

Run the Check-in

Role-Play

Open the Call

Scenario: Mark asks "Can you give me a quick update?" Open the check-in: say what is happening right now and whether the rollout is on track.

Keyword chips:

Updates, Experience & Questions

Scenario: Give a fuller check-in: your experience with this account, what you have finished, and the next step. Then ask Mark one indirect question.

Keywords:

I have worked / finished... It goes live... Could you tell me if...?

The Full Check-in Call

Scenario: Pick a real account. Run a full check-in: open the call, give the status right now, say what you have finished, name what is pending, and close with one question for the client.
