Aula 05 -- Pre-class

Performing Under Pressure -- The International Job Interview

A entrevista internacional: como transformar sete anos de experiência em EVIDÊNCIA, controlar o que a banca ouve primeiro e responder à pergunta desconfortável sem pedir desculpas. Key words: track record, cross-functional, high-stakes, resilience, stakeholder management, value proposition, technical lead, subject matter expert, performance-driven, results-oriented, to set out, to account for, to burn the midnight oil, to throw in the towel. Structure: cleft sentences ("What I bring to this role is...", "It was in San Francisco that...", "The reason I... is that...") + os quatro padrões de resposta de entrevista.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Nas aulas 2 a 4 você nomeou o trabalho: as ações, a inspeção, o contrato. Aqui você nomeia a SI MESMO — as palavras que uma banca de entrevista está esperando ouvir. Você já FEZ tudo isto na Motiva; o que falta é a etiqueta em inglês. Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo.

Track record -- a documented history of results you can point to, with dates and numbers (histórico comprovado)
"I can demonstrate a track record of delivering signaling packages on time."
Cross-functional -- involving people from different departments and disciplines at the same time (multidisciplinar, entre áreas)
"I lead cross-functional teams: signaling, civil works and the client."
High-stakes -- where the consequences of getting it wrong are serious and public (de alto risco, com muito em jogo)
"Signaling is a high-stakes discipline: the failure mode is a collision."
Resilience -- the ability to keep performing when the pressure does not stop (resiliência)
"What that year taught me was resilience, not patience."
Stakeholder management -- keeping everyone with an interest in the project informed, aligned and calm (gestão de partes interessadas)
"Stakeholder management is half the job: the client, the supplier and the regulator."
Value proposition -- the one concrete reason to choose you instead of the other candidate (proposta de valor)
"My value proposition is simple: I read the standard and I run the site."
Technical lead -- the engineer who owns the technical decisions on a project, and answers for them (líder técnico)
"I was the technical lead on the Line 5 signaling package."
Subject matter expert -- the person the team goes to when the answer is not in the manual (especialista no assunto)
"On interlocking logic, I am the subject matter expert for my team."
Performance-driven -- working against a number: a metric, a KPI, a measurable target (orientado por desempenho)
"It is a performance-driven environment: every milestone has an indicator."
Results-oriented -- asking to be judged by what you delivered, not by how busy you looked (orientado a resultados)
"I am results-oriented: judge me by the commissioning date, not by my hours."
To set out -- to explain your background clearly and in order, so the listener can follow it (expor, apresentar em ordem)
"Let me set out my experience in three steps."
To account for -- to explain a decision or a period that somebody is entitled to ask about (justificar, explicar)
"I can account for the two years when I stopped using English."
To burn the midnight oil -- to work late into the night to finish something that matters (informal) (virar a noite trabalhando)
"We burned the midnight oil for a week before the factory acceptance test."
To throw in the towel -- to give up when it gets hard -- the thing you say you did NOT do (informal) (jogar a toalha, desistir)
"That interview went badly, but I did not throw in the towel."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

It is four o'clock on a Tuesday and João Guilherme is sitting in front of a panel of two. Sofie Claes, Head of Signaling Engineering, has twenty minutes and a shortlist of five engineers, all of them with seven years of experience. Experience is time, and everybody on that list has it. What wins the room is a track record: a project, a decision, an outcome.

He opens carefully. "Let me set out my background in three steps. What I do at Motiva is own the signaling design for metro line extensions, from the specification to commissioning. What I bring to this role is seven years of managing German, French and Chinese suppliers under contractual pressure." Sofie is not impressed yet. She asks the question that decides everything: what makes him the right person, and not the other four? "What sets me apart is that I read the standard and I run the site. Most engineers do one or the other. It was in San Francisco, in 2023, that I did both on the same project." That is a value proposition, and it takes one sentence.

Then comes the uncomfortable question, the one he did not prepare in March: there was a period when he was not using English. He does not apologize. He accounts for it. "What I learned from that experience was that fluency is a habit, not a memory. It was an interview, in March, that made me rebuild the habit." He did not throw in the towel, and the word for that, in an interview, is resilience. Stakeholder management, a cross-functional team, a high-stakes discipline where the failure mode is a collision: the engineering was always there. Tonight, so was the English.

1. "What I bring to this role is seven years in signaling." Por que o verbo é is, e não are, se "seven years" é plural?
A Porque What... funciona como "the thing that" — um sujeito SINGULAR. O verbo concorda com ele, nunca com o complemento.
B Porque o inglês ignora concordância depois de what.
C Porque "seven years" é tratado como dinheiro e vira singular.
2. "It was in San Francisco that I ran my first commissioning cycle." O que essa estrutura FAZ com a frase?
A Adiciona uma informação nova sobre São Francisco.
B Coloca UM fato sob o holofote — o lugar — e diz à banca onde olhar. A informação é a mesma; o peso, não.
C Torna a frase mais formal, mas o efeito é apenas decorativo.
3. Por que "What I bring to this role is seven years..." funciona melhor numa entrevista do que "I have seven years of experience"?
A Porque é mais longa, e frases longas soam mais profissionais.
B Porque anuncia que algo importante vem, guarda a informação decisiva para o FIM — e ainda compra meio segundo de tempo para você pensar.
C Porque evita o verbo have, que é informal demais.
4. Which sentence is correct?
A "It was in San Francisco where I ran my first commissioning cycle."
B "What I bring to this role, it is deep expertise in signaling."
C "It was in San Francisco that I ran my first commissioning cycle."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences

GRAMMAR

Como dividir a frase em duas para controlar o que a banca ouve primeiro — e comprar meio segundo para pensar (explicação em inglês e português).

FormFunction / FunçãoExample
wh-cleft
What + sujeito + verbo + is + o ponto		Você ANUNCIA que algo importante vem, e só então entrega. A banca se inclina para a frente. Em português: "O que eu trago para este cargo é..."."What I bring to this role is seven years in signaling."
it-cleft
It was + o fato + that + o resto		Você coloca UM fato sob o holofote: um lugar, uma data, uma pessoa, uma decisão. A palavra é sempre that — nunca where, nunca when."It was in San Francisco that I ran my first commissioning cycle."
The reason + oração + is thatVocê assume o controle da história por trás de uma decisão, antes que alguém a interprete mal."The reason I moved into signaling is that safety is non-negotiable."
ConcordânciaDepois de What..., o verbo é SEMPRE is / was, mesmo diante de um plural — porque What equivale a "the thing that", um sujeito singular."What I bring is seven years." (nunca are)
Interrogativa / negativaFunciona igual: What he wants is a date. / What I do not do is promise dates I cannot defend."What I do not do is promise a date I cannot defend."
Na falaA pausa cai logo ANTES do is. Essa pausa é tempo de pensar — e você tem direito a ela."What I learned from that experience … was that fluency is a habit."
Para que serveSob pressão, o candidato nervoso despeja tudo o que sabe numa frase longa — e a banca para de ouvir. A cleft sentence faz o contrário: ela diz à banca onde olhar. Mesma informação, candidato completamente diferente.

Atenção (dois erros de brasileiro): (1) o pronome repetido — em português dizemos "O que eu trago para esse cargo, isso é..."; em inglês o pronome NÃO volta: "What I bring to this role it is..." está errado, o correto é "What I bring to this role is..."; (2) o where — dizemos "foi em São Francisco ONDE...", mas o it-cleft inglês exige that: "It was in San Francisco that I ran my first commissioning cycle."

Os quatro padrões da entrevista: "One example that comes to mind is..." (a pergunta comportamental), "What I learned from that experience was..." (o fechamento de qualquer história), "The way I approach this kind of challenge is..." (a pergunta de método) e "I would say my greatest strength in this area is..." (a pergunta direta). São quatro esqueletos: você só troca o recheio.

Stage 2: Put the Interview in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de uma resposta de entrevista internacional na ordem correta.

?State your value proposition in one cleft sentence: what sets you apart from the other candidates.
?Close by asking the panel a question only an engineer would ask.
?Set out your background in three steps, starting from the value and not from the biography.
?Prove it with a track record: one project, the decision you took, and the outcome.
?Account for the uncomfortable question with evidence, not with an apology.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. São as cinco frases que abrem, sustentam e fecham qualquer entrevista em inglês. Repare na pausa que cai logo ANTES do is — ela é sua, e ela é tempo de pensar.

Let me set out my background in three steps.
Deixe-me expor a minha trajetória em três etapas.
What I bring to this role is seven years of managing international suppliers.
O que eu trago para esta posição são sete anos gerenciando fornecedores internacionais.
What sets me apart is that I read the standard and I run the site.
O que me diferencia é que eu leio a norma e eu conduzo o campo.
It was in San Francisco, in 2023, that I ran my first full commissioning cycle.
Foi em São Francisco, em 2023, que eu conduzi o meu primeiro ciclo completo de comissionamento.
I can account for it, and what I learned was that fluency is a habit, not a memory.
Eu consigo explicar isso, e o que eu aprendi foi que fluência é um hábito, não uma lembrança.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real de uma entrevista internacional — inclusive a pergunta desconfortável sobre o seu inglês.

The panel opens with "Tell me about yourself." You have ninety seconds. You start with:
A "I was born in São Paulo and I studied electrical engineering..."
B "Let me set out my background in three steps. What I do at Motiva is own the signaling design for metro line extensions."
C "Well, it is difficult to summarize seven years, but I will try."
"Everyone on this shortlist has seven years. What makes you the right person?" You answer:
A "What sets me apart is that I read the standard and I run the site. Most engineers do one or the other."
B "I am very dedicated and I always give my best."
C "I think my experience speaks for itself."
She asks for a conflict with an international supplier. The strongest opening is:
A "Suppliers are always late, it happens on every project."
B "One example that comes to mind is a supplier who missed three consecutive deadlines. What I did was put forward a recovery plan before anyone mentioned the penalty clause."
C "I would have to think about that one for a moment."
"You were not using English intensively for a while. How do I know that will not be a problem?" You answer:
A "I am sorry about my English, I know it is not perfect."
B "My English is fine, it was only a bad day in that interview."
C "I can account for it. What I learned from that period was that fluency is a habit, not a memory — and I have been in weekly training since March."
The technical director asks how you handle competing priorities. He wants ONE sentence, not three minutes. You say:
A "The way I approach this kind of challenge is to ask which item is on the critical path for safety approval."
B "It depends. Let me explain the relay logic first, and then the interlocking table, and then..."
C "I work hard and I burn the midnight oil until everything is done."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Fale por 2 minutos, sem script e sem parar para se corrigir. Tom: profissional, confiante, direto ao valor.

What I learned from that experience was...).
