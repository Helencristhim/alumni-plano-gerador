Lesson 5 -- Performing Under Pressure: The International Job Interview

Series
Suits — Season 1, Episode 1 "Pilot" (Netflix, 1h07)

The first 20 minutes are the best interview lesson ever filmed. Mike Ross walks into a room with no track record, no diploma and nothing to lose — and walks out hired, because he builds a value proposition in three sentences. Notice how often the lawyers use cleft sentences to push the decisive information to the end of the sentence: "What I do is...", "What you need is...". This is the English of somebody who owns the room.

Tip: watch it with English subtitles and write down 5 of Harvey's lines that start with What... or It was.... Then rewrite each one, swapping the law firm for YOUR signaling package at Motiva.

Matt Abrahams, from Stanford, with Andrew Seaman, careers editor at LinkedIn. The thesis is exactly the one behind this lesson: nobody hires your experience, they hire the NARRATIVE you build with it — and connecting the dots is your job, never the interviewer's. There is a section on your LinkedIn profile being "your first interview", which is your Task 2 this week.

Tip: listen to all 26 minutes ONCE, without pausing — native pace, no subtitles. Then record 60 seconds answering, out loud: "What I bring to my next role is..." If the sentence does not come out whole on the first try, do it again until it does. The bottleneck is the speaking, not the listening.

YouTube
CLEFT SENTENCES — advanced complex sentences in English + TEST!

The exact structure of this lesson, with a test at the end: it-cleft ("It was in San Francisco that...") and wh-cleft ("What I bring to this role is..."). Pay special attention to the point that catches most speakers out: after What... the verb is always is, never are — and the it-cleft takes that, never where.

Tip: after the video, say 5 sentences about YOUR career — two with What I... (what you deliver), two with It was... that (San Francisco, 2023; the interview in March) and one with The reason I... is that (why signaling). Out loud, with nothing written down first.
