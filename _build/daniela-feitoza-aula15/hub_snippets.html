Warm-up — Callback da Aula 14

Review

Na Aula 14 você aprendeu a falar do futuro — planejar com "going to", prever com "will" e marcar compromissos com o present continuous ("We are meeting on Friday"). Você planejou o piloto. Agora o piloto está no ar — e ontem à noite algo caiu. Hoje você aprende a contar essa história: pintar a cena com o past continuous (was/were + verbo-ing) e soltar o evento repentino com o past simple. (Paint the scene, then drop the surprise.)

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Outage -- a period of time when a service is not working (PT: indisponibilidade / queda do serviço)
"There was a short outage during the night."
Crash -- when a system suddenly stops working (PT: travar / cair de repente)
"The payment service crashed at nine."
Glitch -- a small, temporary fault in a system (PT: falha pequena e temporária)
"It was just a minor glitch, not a real failure."
Alert -- an automatic warning that something is wrong (PT: alerta / aviso automático)
"The first alert came in at nine fifteen."
Monitor -- to watch a system closely to find problems early (PT: monitorar / acompanhar)
"The team were monitoring the dashboard all night."
Spike -- a sudden, sharp rise, like a jump in traffic (PT: pico / subida repentina)
"The traffic spiked, and then the service crashed."
Escalate -- to pass a problem to someone with more authority (PT: escalar / encaminhar para nível acima)
"We were losing transactions, so I escalated it."
Downtime -- the time when a system is down and not available (PT: tempo de inatividade / fora do ar)
"The downtime lasted twenty minutes."
Recover -- to bring a system back to working order after a failure (PT: recuperar / restabelecer)
"The engineer recovered the service in twenty minutes."
Root cause -- the real, underlying reason a problem happened (PT: causa-raiz / origem real do problema)
"The root cause was an old script using all the memory."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Last night there was an incident. At nine, the team were monitoring the dashboard — a long action in progress, so Daniela uses the past continuous (was/were + verb-ing) to set the scene. Then a short, sudden event interrupted it: "While we were checking the database, the traffic spiked and the service crashed." Notice the pattern: the long background action is in the past continuous, and the short event that breaks it is in the past simple. The connector while comes before the long action, and when comes before the short event. Never drop "was" or "were": say "I was testing", never "I testing".

2. Which tense is used for the short event that interrupts?
A past continuous (was/were + verb-ing)
B past simple (spiked, crashed)
C present simple
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I testing the system when it crashed."
B "I was testing the system when it crashed."
C "While we checked the database, it crashing."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Past Continuous (Setting the Scene)

GRAMMAR

A cena longa e o evento curto (bilingue EN + PT).

FormUseExample
was/were + verbo-ingAção longa em progresso (a cena)"I was testing the system."
past simpleEvento curto e repentino que interrompe"...when the server crashed."
when + past simpleIntroduz o evento curto"...when the alert came in."
Regra (PT)Use o past continuous (was/were + verbo-ing) para a ação longa que estava em progresso — a cena. Use o past simple para o evento curto que a interrompe. while vem antes da ação longa, when vem antes do evento curto. NUNCA esqueça o was/were ("I was testing", nunca "I testing").
