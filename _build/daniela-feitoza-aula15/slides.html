Chapter 3

The Scene Rule

Past continuous + past simple, when and while

Scene Discovery

Listen and Notice

I was testing the system when the server crashed.

While we were monitoring the alerts, the outage started.

At nine, the team were watching the dashboard.

I was reviewing the logs when the first alert came in.

Which action was long and in progress? Which one was short and sudden?

Scene Rule

Scene and Surprise

FormUseExample
was / were + verb-ingA long action in progress (the scene)"I was testing the system."
past simpleA short, sudden event that interrupts"...when the server crashed."
when + past simpleIntroduces the short event"...when the alert came in."
while + past continuousIntroduces the long action"While we were monitoring..."
Common Mistake

Wrong Form vs Right Form

✗ WRONG

"I tested the system when it crashed."

✓ RIGHT

"I was testing the system when it crashed." (long action = was + -ing)

✗ WRONG

"We monitoring the alerts."

✓ RIGHT

"We were monitoring the alerts." (always use was or were)

The long action in progress always needs was or were + the verb with -ing.

Listening

The Coach’s Tip

A voice note about setting the scene in the past. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. What is the past continuous good for, according to Sarah?
For an action in progress in the past — the background or scene of a story.
2. When you have a long action interrupted by a short event, which tense goes with each?
Past continuous for the long action; past simple for the short, sudden event.
3. Which connector goes before the short event, and which before the long action?
"When" goes before the short past simple event; "while" goes before the long past continuous action.
