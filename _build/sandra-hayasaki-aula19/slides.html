Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

Because, So, Although, However

Reason and result -- and the contrast that follows

Listen and Notice

What Do the Orange Words Do?

Each sentence connects two ideas. Notice the orange linking words.

"I study English because I love to travel."

"I love to travel, so I study English."

"Although it is hard, I never stop."

"It is hard; however, I never stop."

The Rule

Connecting Ideas

LinkerUseExample
becausegives the reason"I study because I love to travel."
sogives the result"I love to travel, so I study."
althoughcontrast, joins two parts"Although it is hard, I never stop."
howevercontrast, new sentence"It is hard. However, I never stop."

Tip: because + reason, so + result. For contrast, although connects two parts in one sentence, while however starts a new sentence (with a comma after it).

Common Mistake

One Linker at a Time

"Because I love travel, so I study." / "It is hard however I never stop."
"I study because I love travel." / "It is hard. However, I never stop."

Use only one reason/result linker per sentence, and put however after a semicolon or in a new sentence.

Practice

Choose the Right Linker

"_____Although it is hard, I never stop." (Although / Because -- contrast)
