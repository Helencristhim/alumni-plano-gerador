Linking Ideas

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Reason -- why something happens, or why you do something; the answer to "why?"
"The reason I study is that I love to travel." (motivo / razão)
Cause -- the thing or event that makes something else happen
"Heavy rain was the cause of the delay." (causa)
Effect -- the result; what happens because of a cause
"The effect of practice is more confidence." (efeito / resultado)
Contrast -- a clear difference between two things you compare
"There is a big contrast between my old job and this one." (contraste)
Link -- to connect two ideas or things so they go together
"Linking words help me link my ideas smoothly." (ligar / conectar)
Despite -- even with a difficulty; in spite of something
"Despite the rain, we went for a walk." (apesar de)
Nevertheless -- in spite of that; a more formal way to say "but still"
"It was hard; nevertheless, I kept going." (mesmo assim / ainda assim)
On the other hand -- a phrase used to introduce a different or opposite side of an idea
"I love my city. On the other hand, it is very crowded." (por outro lado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

I want to explain why I keep studying English. The main reason is curiosity. I love other cultures, so English opens many doors for me. Because I travel often, a second language is very useful. The classes are not always easy; however, I look forward to each one. Although I am sometimes tired after work, I still join, because the lesson gives me energy. The effect is clear: my confidence grows every week. On the other hand, if I stopped now, I would lose all of that. Despite the difficulty, I will not stop.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Because, So, Although & However

GRAMMAR

Para dar o motivo, use because + a causa. Para dar o resultado, use so + a consequência. Para um contraste, although liga duas partes numa frase só (Although it is hard, I continue), enquanto however começa uma nova frase, com vírgula depois (It is hard. However, I continue). NÃO use because e so juntos. / Use because for the reason, so for the result, although to join two parts, and however to start a new sentence.

LinkerUseExample
becausegives the reason"I study because I love to travel."
sogives the result"I love to travel, so I study."
althoughcontrast, joins two parts"Although it is hard, I never stop."
howevercontrast, new sentence"It is hard. However, I never stop."

Stage 2: Put the Steps in Order

Order

Ouça primeiro, depois coloque os passos para conectar suas ideias na ordem correta.

?To show the effect, use so or as a result, like so I can talk to more people.
?Say your main idea, for example I keep studying English.
?End with a calm conclusion, so your listener understands your reason and your contrast.
?To give the reason, add because and the cause, like because I love to travel.
?To add a contrast, use although or however, like although it is not always easy.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

You want to add a contrast in one smooth sentence. What do you say?
A "Although it is hard, I keep going."
B "Although it is hard, but I keep going."
C "Because it is hard, so I keep going."
You want to start a new sentence with a contrast. What do you say?
A "It is hard however I continue."

C "It is hard so however I continue."
