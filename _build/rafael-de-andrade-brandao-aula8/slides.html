Grammar Rule

Open Wh- Questions

Wh- wordAuxiliary + subjectExample
Whatis + your goalWhat is your main goal?
Whyis + deliveryWhy is delivery slow?
How manyare + peopleHow many people are on your team?
Whatdo + youWhat do you need from us?

Key idea: An open question starts with a Wh- word (What, Why, When, Where, Who, How), then an auxiliary (is, are, do, does), then the subject and verb. Open questions get long answers and help you discover the client's real needs.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Why delivery is slow?"
"Why is delivery slow?"

In a Wh- question, the auxiliary (is, are, do, does) comes before the subject. Never put the subject first.
