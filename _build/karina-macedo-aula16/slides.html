Chapter 3 -- Offer and Compare

Grammar Discovery

I would like to offer -- cheaper than

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we make an offer and compare two options?

"I would like to offer you a good price."

"This aircraft is cheaper than the old one."

"This deal is better than the old deal."

"What about a bigger discount?"

Grammar Rule

Offers & Comparatives

PhraseWhen we use it
I would like to offer...Make a polite offer
cheaper / faster thanShort adjective + -er + than
better thangood is irregular (not "gooder")
What about...?Make a counter offer

Key idea: to make an offer say I would like to offer. To compare, add -er to a short adjective and use than: cheap → cheaper than, fast → faster than. good is irregular: better than. For a counter offer ask What about...?

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"This one is more cheap and more good than the old one."
"This one is cheaper and better than the old one."

For short adjectives add -er (cheaper, not "more cheap"). good is irregular: better.
