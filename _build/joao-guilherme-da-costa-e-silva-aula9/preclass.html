Aula 09 -- Pre-class

Say That Again -- The Commissioning Call at Full Speed

Uma diretora de projeto britanica, quarenta minutos e um cronograma de comissionamento em velocidade nativa: como PREVER com data (o que estará PRONTO x o que estará ACONTECENDO), onde o tempo verbal se esconde na fala rápida (o auxiliar some, o particípio fica), e as três frases que param a call sem perder a cara. Key words: integration test, site readiness, look-ahead schedule, open action, outstanding item, provisional acceptance, final acceptance, defects liability period, ramp-up, interface management, dry run, to phase out, to factor in, to run something by someone. Structure: future perfect e future continuous (By the twelfth we will have confirmed... / This time next week we will be running...).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Na aula 8 você escreveu a pergunta com tempo. Agora a fase final do projeto chega FALADA, rápida, e no ritmo de outra pessoa. Estas são as palavras do último quilômetro: quem aceita, quando, e o que ainda está em aberto. Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo.

Integration test -- the test that proves the separate systems talk to each other -- not that each one works alone (teste de integração)
"The integration test between the interlocking and the control center starts on the fifteenth."
Site readiness -- whether the place itself -- power, access, safety -- can actually receive the equipment and the crews (prontidão do canteiro / da obra)
"I will confirm site readiness on the twelfth, once the power supply is energized."
Look-ahead schedule -- the short window of the plan: the next three weeks, activity by activity, in detail (cronograma de curto prazo (próximas 3 semanas))
"The three-week look-ahead schedule shows the integration test starting on the fifteenth."
Open action -- a task with a name on it and a date on it, and it is not closed yet (ação em aberto (com dono e prazo))
"By Friday we will have closed every open action from the last call."
Outstanding item -- something that was agreed, is still not done, and is still on the list at the end of the meeting (pendência)
"Three outstanding items remain, and none of them can wait until the ramp-up."
Provisional acceptance -- the client takes the system into service -- with a list of defects still attached to it (aceitação provisória (entrada em serviço com pendências))
"Provisional acceptance is planned for the end of the third quarter."
Final acceptance -- nothing is left open, the warranty period is over, and the last payment is released (aceitação definitiva)
"Final acceptance will not be granted until every outstanding item is closed."
Defects liability period -- the window after handover in which the supplier still fixes, at its own cost, whatever fails (período de garantia / responsabilidade por defeitos)
"The defects liability period runs for twelve months between provisional and final acceptance."
Ramp-up -- the gradual climb from the first test run to full service, week by week (ramp-up: subida gradual até a operação plena)
"By the time the ramp-up begins in November, we will have trained every operator."
Interface management -- making two suppliers' systems meet without a gap -- and owning the gap when there is one (gestão de interfaces)
"Interface management between signaling and rolling stock sits with me."
Dry run -- a full rehearsal of the real operation, with nothing at stake and everybody watching (ensaio geral / simulado)
"This time next week we will be running a dry run of the failover procedure."
To phase out -- to remove something gradually, on a planned date, until it is gone for good (descontinuar, eliminar gradualmente)
"The manual workarounds will be phased out before provisional acceptance, not after."
To factor in -- to include something in the calculation BEFORE you commit to the date (levar em conta (no cálculo, antes de assumir o prazo))
"Have you factored in the night-shift restrictions on the site?"
To run something by someone -- to repeat or check something with somebody, so that they can follow it or approve it (repassar / confirmar algo com alguém)
"Could you run the acceptance dates by me one more time?"

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

The call starts at eight and Fiona Hartley, the project director in London, has another one at half past. She goes straight in. Where are we on site readiness? Because by the twelfth she needs the power supply energized, or the integration test does not happen. She speaks the way people speak when they are late: the auxiliaries collapse, will have becomes will'uv, will be almost disappears, and the dates arrive in the middle of long sentences with no emphasis at all.

So João Guilherme does the thing that feels wrong and is right: he stops her. I want to make sure I am following — the deadline you are referring to is the twelfth, and that is for site readiness, not for the test itself. She says "Correct" and carries on. Five seconds. Then he forecasts, and every sentence carries a date. By the fifteenth we will have energized the power supply and closed the two open actions on the cable route. We will be working nights from the eighth to hold that date. This time next week we will be running a dry run of the failover procedure. By the time the ramp-up starts in November, every operator will have been trained.

Then comes the part that decides the money. Provisional acceptance at the end of the third quarter; final acceptance twelve months later, with the defects liability period running in between. And the manual workarounds, Fiona says, get phased out BEFORE provisional acceptance, not after — she has seen too many projects where the temporary fix quietly becomes the design. João asks her to run the acceptance dates by him one more time, because he wants to factor them in before he sends the three-week look-ahead schedule. And he closes the call the way a call has to be closed: Just to confirm — are you saying that no workaround survives provisional acceptance? Then by the thirtieth we will have cleared every outstanding item on that list. He did not understand every word she said. He understood every date — and that is the entire job.

1. "By the fifteenth we will have energized the power supply." O que essa estrutura promete, exatamente?
A Que a energização vai ACONTECER no dia quinze.
B Que a energização estará CONCLUÍDA ANTES do dia quinze — will have + particípio é o tempo do PRAZO, e é ele que o contrato lê.
C Que a energização começa no dia quinze e continua depois.
2. "This time next week we will be running a dry run." Por que will be running, e não will run?
A Porque will be running é mais formal.
B Porque a ação estará EM ANDAMENTO naquele momento — nem terminada, nem começando. Will run soaria como uma decisão tomada na hora.
C Porque com next week o inglês proíbe o futuro simples.
3. "By the time the ramp-up starts, the workarounds will have been phased out." Por que starts, e não will start?
A Porque depois de by the time / when / before / as soon as o inglês escreve o futuro no PRESENTE. "By the time it will start" não existe.
B Porque o ramp-up já começou.
C Porque start é um verbo irregular.
4. Fiona diz a nova data no MEIO de uma frase longa, sem ênfase. Qual é a reação profissional?
A Anotar o que deu para entender e conferir depois por e-mail, para não parecer lento.
B Interromper e confirmar em voz alta: "Just to confirm — are you saying that provisional acceptance has moved to the twenty-third?" Cinco segundos ali evitam três semanas de trabalho para a data errada.
C Continuar a call e pedir que ela fale mais devagar da próxima vez.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Perfect & Future Continuous

GRAMMAR

Os dois tempos com que se comanda um projeto — e por que eles quase somem na fala rápida (explicação em inglês e português).

Estrutura / StructureO que promete / What it promisesNa call / On the call
will have + particípio
future perfect		CONCLUÍDO antes daquele ponto no futuro. É o tempo do PRAZO — o que o contrato lê e o que alguém pode cobrar. Em português: "até o dia doze teremos confirmado"."By the twelfth we will have confirmed site readiness."
will be + verbo -ing
future continuous		EM ANDAMENTO naquele momento: nem terminado, nem começando — acontecendo. Em português: "semana que vem a esta hora estaremos rodando"."This time next week we will be running the dry run."
will have been + verbo -ing
future perfect continuous		Mede a DURAÇÃO acumulada até aquele ponto. É com ela que se justifica um custo ou um pedido de prazo."By September the crews will have been working nights for six weeks."
by x untilBY = o prazo (não mais tarde que). UNTIL = a duração inteira. O português cobre os dois com "até" — e por isso o brasileiro escreve "until Friday we will have finished", que em inglês não quer dizer nada."By Friday" (prazo) · "We will be testing until Friday" (duração)
by the time + PRESENTEDepois de by the time / when / before / as soon as / until, o futuro se escreve no PRESENTE. "By the time the ramp-up will start" NÃO existe."By the time the ramp-up starts, the workarounds will have been phased out."
Como isso SOANinguém pronuncia esses tempos como eles são escritos. will have vira /wɪləv/ ("will'uv"), we will have vira "we'll've", e will be quase desaparece. O tempo verbal NÃO some junto: ele sobrevive no PARTICÍPIO, no fim da frase (...we'll've CONFIRMED it). Pare de caçar o auxiliar que você aprendeu na escola: escute a ÚLTIMA palavra.

Atenção (três erros de brasileiro): (1) "Until the twelfth we will have confirmed..." — é BY the twelfth: until é duração, by é prazo; (2) "By the time the test will start..." — depois de by the time vai o PRESENTE: starts; (3) "At ten we will run the test" quando o que se quer dizer é "estaremos rodando" — o correto é we will be running: o futuro simples soa como uma decisão tomada naquele instante.

Collocações do comissionamento: confirm site readiness (nunca "guarantee") · close an open action (nunca "finish") · clear the outstanding items · grant provisional acceptance (o cliente CONCEDE; o fornecedor OBTÉM) · phase out a workaround · factor in a constraint (NUNCA "consider in") · run a dry run · manage the interfaces.

Clarificação sob pressão (decore as três): "Could you run that by me one more time?" · "Just to confirm — are you saying that...?" · "I want to make sure I'm following: the deadline you're referring to is..." — nenhuma delas é um pedido de desculpas. São o que um nativo diz quando perde uma data, e custam cinco segundos.

Stage 2: Put the Conference Call in Order

Order

Coloque na ordem correta os passos de quem NAO perde uma data numa call rápida — de pegar a informação escondida até assumir o compromisso com o tempo verbal certo.

?Catch the date she buried in the middle of the sentence: provisional acceptance has moved to the twenty-third.
?Say what will be happening, not finished: "We will be working nights from the eighth to hold that date."
?Factor in what she cannot see: the night-shift restrictions on site.
?Ask her to repeat what you did not catch: "Could you run the acceptance dates by me one more time?"
?Stop her and confirm it out loud: "Just to confirm -- are you saying that provisional acceptance is now the twenty-third?"
?Commit with the deadline tense: "By the twenty-third we will have closed every outstanding item."

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. Diga rápido, colando as palavras: will have vira "will'uv" e will be quase some — produzir a forma reduzida é o que ensina o ouvido a reconhecê-la. Atenção ao stress: pro-VI-sion-al, li-a-BI-li-ty, RED-i-ness (curto, como em "ready").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da call — inclusive quando ela esconde a data nova no meio de uma frase longa.

Fiona abre a call em velocidade máxima: "Where are we on site readiness? By the twelfth I need that power supply energized." Você responde:
A "I want to make sure I'm following -- the deadline you're referring to is the twelfth, and that is for site readiness, not for the integration test itself. Is that right?"
B "Sorry, could you speak more slowly, please? My English is not very good."
C "Yes, yes, no problem, everything is on track."
Ela pede o status. A resposta mais forte é:
A "We are working on it and I think we will finish before the test."
B "By the fifteenth we will have energized the power supply and closed the two open actions on the cable route, and we will be working nights from the eighth to hold that date."
C "The site will be ready, do not worry about that."
No meio de uma frase longa ela diz que a aceitação provisória mudou para o dia 23. Você:
A Anota e confere depois no e-mail dela, para não interromper.
B Interrompe: "Just to confirm -- are you saying that provisional acceptance has moved to the twenty-third? Then by the twenty-third we will have cleared every outstanding item."
C Assume que ouviu errado e segue a call normalmente.
"Let us just keep the manual workarounds until final acceptance, to be safe." Você recusa:
A "The workarounds are phased out before provisional acceptance, not after. A temporary fix with no end date quietly becomes the design, and the operator pays to maintain it for thirty years."
B "All right, if that is easier for your team, we can keep them."
C "I do not have the authority to decide that."
Ela pergunta se você mantém a data mesmo com a restrição de trabalho noturno no canteiro. Você diz:
A "Yes, of course, we will make it work somehow."
B "I need to factor in the night-shift restrictions before I commit to that date. You will have the three-week look-ahead schedule tonight, and the date will be in it."
C "That is impossible. Nobody can work with these restrictions."
