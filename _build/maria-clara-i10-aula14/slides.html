Making Plans and Saying What You Prefer

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

After the first verb: TO + verb, or verb + -ING?

"I want to improve my English."

"I decided to travel without an interpreter."

"I enjoy meeting people from other countries."

"I keep practicing every day."

Grammar Rule

Two Patterns, One Choice

PatternThese verbsExample
verb + TO + base verbwant, need, decide, plan, hope, would like, arrange, tend, preferI want to improve. · I hope to travel.
verb + -INGenjoy, avoid, finish, consider, suggest, keep, postpone, mindI enjoy meeting. · I keep practicing.
Trap: looking forward to + -INGHere to is a preposition, not an infinitive — so the verb takes -ing.I am looking forward to meeting you. (NOT "to meet")

There is no easy rule. Some verbs take to (I want to go), some take -ing (I enjoy going). You learn them in groups and by using them. The only trap: after looking forward to, use -ing — because that to is a preposition.

Grammar Practice

To or -ing?

Say the correct form out loud. Then click to check.

1. I want ___ (improve) my English this year.
to improve — want + TO + verb
2. I enjoy ___ (meet) new people at the conference.
meeting — enjoy + verb-ING
3. She arranged ___ (meet) the rector at nine.
to meet — arrange + TO + verb
4. We keep ___ (practice) every morning.
practicing — keep + verb-ING
5. I decided ___ (travel) without an interpreter.
to travel — decide + TO + verb
6. Would you mind ___ (repeat) that, please?
repeating — mind + verb-ING
7. He tends ___ (arrive) late for dinners.
to arrive — tend + TO + verb
8. I am looking forward ___ (lead) the delegation.
to leading — after "looking forward to", use -ING (to = preposition)
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want improving my English."
"I want to improve my English."
"I enjoy to meet new people."
"I enjoy meeting new people."
"I am looking forward to meet you."
"I am looking forward to meeting you."

After want / decide / hope, use to + verb. After enjoy / keep / mind, use verb + -ing. And after looking forward to, use -ing — that "to" is a preposition.

Wrap-up

What I Learned

I can use verb + TO after want, need, decide, hope, arrange, tend, and prefer.
I can use verb + -ING after enjoy, avoid, keep, postpone, and mind.
I can use -ing after "looking forward to" -- because "to" is a preposition there.
I can ask politely with "Would you mind + verb-ing...?".
I can tell my English journey: what I want, what I enjoy, and what I avoid.
