Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Flavia is a consultant at Sebrae. She works with small businesses in many regions, and her team prepares projects every week. Next month, she travels on an international mission. A partner asks her, "What does Sebrae do?" and "Do you help companies that sell abroad?" Right now, Flavia is getting ready: she is presenting her ideas to her colleagues and preparing her agenda. She feels confident to introduce herself and her work.

1. Why does the partner say "What does Sebrae do?"
A Because Sebrae is like "it" (he / she / it), so we use "does."
B Because every question uses "does."
C Because "do" and "does" are the past tense.
2. Why does the partner say "Do you help companies that sell abroad?"
A We use "do" only with he, she, it.
B We use "do" with you, I, we, and they.
C We use "do" only for the future.
3. "Right now, Flavia is presenting her ideas." Which tool is this, and why?
A Present simple, because it is a routine.
B Present continuous, because it is happening now.
C A question, because it uses do.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Three Tools from Block 1

GRAMMAR

When to use the present simple, a question with do / does, and the present continuous.

ToolUse it forExample
present simplea routine or a factI work with small businesses.
do / doesto ask a question (do = you/we/they, does = he/she/it)What does your company do?
present continuousan action happening nowRight now, I am presenting my project.

Remember: routine = present simple · a question = do / does + subject + verb · now = am / is / are + verb-ing.
