Chapter 3

Then vs Now

present simple (every day) vs past simple (yesterday)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Every day I check my email. / Yesterday I checked my email.

I write a report every week. / Last week I wrote a report.

I go to meetings. / Yesterday I went to a meeting.

Now I am confident. / On Day 1 I was nervous.

TenseWhenExample
Present simpleevery day, usually, oftenI check / she checks
Past simple (regular)yesterday, last week, agoverb + -ed: check → checked
Past simple (irregular)yesterday, last week, agogo → went, write → wrote, have → had
to be (past)yesterday, on Day 1am/is → was, are → were

Present for routines (every day). Past for finished time (yesterday, last week).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Yesterday I go to the office."

✓ RIGHT

"Yesterday I went to the office."

✗ WRONG

"Last week I writed a report."

✓ RIGHT

"Last week I wrote a report."

✗ WRONG

"She have a meeting every day."

✓ RIGHT

"She has a meeting every day."

Use the past for "yesterday" and "last week". Remember irregular verbs. With she/he/it in the present, add -s.
