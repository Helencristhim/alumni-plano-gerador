Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas. (Repare nos marcadores de tempo e nos verbos em negrito.)

Danilo looks at his progress. On his first day, he was very nervous. He only said his name. Every day, now, he checks his email and goes to meetings. Yesterday, he had a meeting in English and asked three questions. Last week, he wrote a short report. He still makes mistakes, but he practices and improves. When he compares Lesson 1 and Lesson 8, he is happy. He is more confident now.

1. Na frase "Yesterday, he had a meeting", por que usamos "had"?
A Porque é uma rotina, acontece todo dia.
B Porque "yesterday" indica passado, e "have" no passado é "had".
C Porque toda frase com "he" usa "had".
2. "Every day, he checks his email." Por que "checks" (com -s)?
A É presente simples e o sujeito "he" pede o -s.
B É passado simples.
C Está errado, deveria ser "check".
3. Qual frase está no passado correto?
A "Last week he write a report."
B "Last week he wrote a report."
C "Last week he writed a report."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Presente para rotinas (every day). Passado para tempo terminado (yesterday, last week).

TenseUse (PT)Example
Present simplerotina: every day, usually, oftenI check / she checks
Past simple (regular)passado: yesterday, last week, agoverbo + -ed: check → checked
Past simple (irregular)passado: yesterday, last weekgo → went, write → wrote, have → had
to be (passado)passado: on Day 1, yesterdayam/is → was, are → were

Atenção (erro comum): Não use o presente para o passado. Diga "Yesterday I went to a meeting." (não "Yesterday I go"). Verbos irregulares mudam: "I wrote a report" (não "writed"). No presente com he/she/it, lembre do -s: "She has a meeting every day."

Quiz

Você conta o que fez ontem. Você diz:
A "Yesterday I go to a meeting."

Você descreve seu primeiro dia (passado). A frase correta é:
A "On my first day I was nervous."
B "On my first day I am nervous."
C "On my first day I were nervous."
Você fala da rotina de uma colega (she). Você diz:
A "She have a meeting every Monday."

C "She had a meeting every Monday."
