Chapter 3

The Polite Code

Could you...? · Would you...? · I would like to... · I am writing to...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am writing to confirm our meeting."

"Could you send me the report?"

"Would you please confirm the date?"

"I would like to arrange a call."

UseStructureExample
Polite requestCould you + verb?Could you send the file?
Polite requestWould you + verb?Would you confirm the date?
Say what you wantI would like to + verbI would like to arrange a meeting.
Open the emailI am writing to + verbI am writing to request information.
Common Mistake

Too Direct vs Polite

✗ TOO DIRECT

"Send me the report."

✓ POLITE

"Could you send me the report, please?"

✗ WRONG

"I writing to confirm the meeting."

✓ RIGHT

"I am writing to confirm the meeting."

✗ WRONG

"I would like arrange a call."

✓ RIGHT

"I would like to arrange a call."

Polite requests use Could you / Would you / please. Open the email with I am writing to + verb, and use I would like to + verb.

Chapter 4

Writing a Clear Email

Planning the email with Sarah

Dialogue

Planning the Email

F
Sarah, I am writing an email to our partner. Can you help me?
S
Of course. What do you want to say?
F
I would like to confirm the meeting and attach the report.
S
Good. Could you also request their new numbers?
F
Yes. Would you reply to me before I send it?
S
Sure. Send it to me, and I am going to check it.
F
Thank you. I am going to add a reminder about the deadline.
S
Perfect. It is a clear and polite email.
