Writing Professional Emails

Writing clear, polite professional emails: opening, requests, confirmations and a polite closing. Key words: email, attach, reply, forward, request, confirm, available, arrange, reminder, regards. Structure: polite requests and email language -- "Could you...?", "Would you...?", "I would like to + verb", "I am writing to + verb", and "Please".

Vocabulary

Email -- e-mail / mensagem (a written message online)
"I sent you an email."
Attach -- anexar (to add a file to an email)
"I will attach the report."
Reply -- responder (to answer a message)
"Please reply soon."
Forward -- encaminhar (to send a message to another person)
"I will forward your email to the team."
Request -- solicitar / pedido (to ask for something formally)
"I would like to request the numbers."
Confirm -- confirmar (to say yes, it is sure)
"Could you confirm the date?"
Available -- disponivel (free, able to meet)
"Are you available on Monday?"
Arrange -- organizar / marcar (to plan and set up)
"Let us arrange a meeting."
Reminder -- lembrete (a note to remember something)
"This is a friendly reminder."

"Best regards, Fernando."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Fernando writes an email to a partner. He opens politely: "I am writing to confirm our meeting next week." Then he makes a request: "Could you send me the report?" He adds another one: "Would you please confirm the date?" Finally, he says what he wants: "I would like to arrange a call." He closes the email: "Please reply when you are available. Best regards, Fernando."

1. Por que dizemos "Could you send me the report?" e nao "Send me the report"?
A Porque "could" e o passado de "can".
B Porque "Could you...?" faz um pedido educado; "Send me" e direto demais.
C Porque a frase esta no futuro.
2. Usamos "I am writing to confirm our meeting" para:
A Abrir o e-mail e dizer o motivo.
B Fechar o e-mail.
C Anexar um arquivo.
3. Which sentence is a polite way to say what you want?
A "I want a call now."
B "I would like to arrange a call."
C "Call me."

Quiz

A "Send me the report."
