Aula 02 -- Pre-class

Talking About What Went Wrong -- Past Financial Decisions

Explicar uma decisão financeira que deu errado sem negar o erro nem se humilhar. Key words: write-off, due diligence, valuation, burn rate, runway, red flag, assumption, to underestimate, downside, contingency, hindsight, to overpay. Structures: third conditional (if + past perfect, would have + particípio) e hedging (in hindsight, arguably, may have been).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. É o vocabulário de um negócio que dá errado — o que ninguém ensina num curso de inglês.

Write-off -- an amount a company accepts it has completely lost (baixa contábil, prejuízo reconhecido)
"The failed acquisition ended in a thirty million write-off."
Due diligence -- the detailed check you run on a company before you buy it (auditoria prévia)
"In hindsight, our due diligence was far too fast."
Valuation -- the price the market puts on a company (avaliação, valuation)
"The valuation looked cheap until we read the contracts."
Burn rate -- how fast a company spends its cash every month (taxa de queima de caixa)
"Their burn rate doubled in six months."
Runway -- how many months of cash a company has left (fôlego de caixa)
"That deal cut our runway from eighteen months to nine."
Red flag -- an early warning sign that something is wrong (sinal de alerta)
"The auditor's note was a red flag, and we ignored it."
Assumption -- something you accept as true without proof (premissa, suposição)
"The whole model rested on one optimistic assumption."
To underestimate -- to think something is smaller or easier than it really is (subestimar)
"We underestimated how long the integration would take."
Downside -- the loss you face if things go badly (cenário negativo, risco de perda)
"Nobody at the table modeled the downside."
Contingency -- money or a plan you keep in reserve for problems (reserva de contingência)
"We should have kept a contingency for the tax liability."
Hindsight -- understanding an event only after it has happened (retrospecto)
"Hindsight makes every bad deal look obvious."
To overpay -- to pay more than something is really worth (pagar acima do valor)
"If we had walked away, we would not have overpaid."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Two years ago, Felipe signed off on the acquisition of a small payments processor. The valuation looked cheap, and cheap is persuasive. The team ran the due diligence in six weeks instead of the usual twelve. The auditor left a short note about a tax liability — a red flag — and everyone underestimated it. The whole model rested on one optimistic assumption: that revenue would double in a year. It did not. The burn rate climbed, the runway fell from eighteen months to nine, and the company took a thirty million write-off. If the board had modeled the downside, it would have seen the risk. If Felipe had pushed back harder, the deal would have died — and he says today that he should have let it die. In hindsight, they overpaid. Nobody had kept a contingency. Today no deal closes without audited numbers, a modeled downside, and ten percent held back.

1. Por que dizemos "If the board had modeled the downside, it would have seen the risk"?
A Porque o conselho modelou o cenário negativo e viu o risco.
B Porque o conselho NÃO modelou — é um passado imaginário que não aconteceu.
C Porque o conselho vai modelar o cenário negativo no próximo negócio.
2. "If Felipe had pushed back harder, the deal would have died." O que realmente aconteceu?
A Ele insistiu e o negócio morreu.
B Ele NÃO insistiu, e o negócio foi fechado.
C Ele insistiu, mas o negócio foi fechado mesmo assim.
3. Which sentence is correct English?
A If we would have known the risk, we didn't buy the company.
B If we had known the risk, we wouldn't have bought the company.
C If we knew the risk, we would not bought the company.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional + Hedging

GRAMMAR

Como falar de um passado que NÃO aconteceu — a gramática que transforma uma confissão em análise (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Third Conditional
If + past perfect, would have + participle		Passado imaginário: a condição NÃO aconteceu, então o resultado também NÃO aconteceu. An imaginary past — neither half happened.If we had run diligence, we would have seen it.
Negativahadn't + particípio / wouldn't have + particípio.We wouldn't have overpaid if we hadn't rushed.
Ordem livreQualquer metade vem primeiro. Vírgula só quando a metade com if abre a frase.We would have kept our runway if we hadn't closed.
Na fala: contraçãoIf we'd run... we'd have seen... É assim que soa fluente.If we'd known, we'd have walked away.
Hedging
in hindsight / arguably / may have been		Suaviza a culpa e mantém a sua autoridade. Softens blame without denying the fact.In hindsight, the valuation may have been optimistic.
InterrogativaWhat would you have done differently?What would you have done differently?

Atenção (erro de brasileiro): em português dizemos "se eu soubesse, eu não teria comprado". Em inglês a metade com if NUNCA leva would: é if I had known (past perfect), e a outra metade é I wouldn't have bought. E a expressão é in hindsight — sem artigo, nunca "in the hindsight".

Stage 2: Put the Post-Mortem in Order

Order

Coloque as etapas de um post-mortem para o comitê de auditoria na ordem correta. É a sequência que mantém você sênior na sala.

?Name the red flag you missed, without blaming any individual.
?Close with the three rules that protect the company from now on.
?State the facts: the deal, the write-off, the impact on the runway.
?Say what you would have done differently, using the third conditional.
?Explain the assumption the whole model rested on.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

Ouça cada frase e depois grave você mesmo dizendo-a. São as cinco frases de um post-mortem — e as que respondem "tell me about a failure" numa entrevista.

If we had run proper due diligence, we would have found the problem.
Se tivéssemos feito uma auditoria prévia adequada, teríamos encontrado o problema.
In hindsight, the valuation may have been too optimistic.
Em retrospecto, a avaliação pode ter sido otimista demais.
We wouldn't have overpaid if the board had modeled the downside.
Não teríamos pago acima do valor se o conselho tivesse modelado o cenário negativo.
I signed off on it, so the decision was mine.
Eu aprovei, então a decisão foi minha.
Here is what the process requires now.
É isto que o processo exige agora.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada momento real da sua rotina de CFO.

Diana asks: "What would you have done differently?" The best answer is:
A "If I would have known the risk, I didn't sign the contract."
B "If I had known about the tax liability, I wouldn't have signed the contract."
C "If I knew about the tax liability, I would not sign the contract."
You want to soften the blame without denying the fact. You say:
A "In the hindsight, the valuation was completely wrong."
B "In hindsight, the valuation may have been too optimistic."
C "The valuation was not my responsibility."
An investor asks who was responsible for the write-off. The most senior answer is:
A "The deal team pushed it through. I had my doubts."
B "I signed off on it, so the decision was mine. Here is what changes now."
C "I'm very sorry. It was completely my fault and I feel terrible about it."
You want to say the cash position got worse because of the deal:
A "That deal cut our runway from eighteen months to nine."
B "That deal cutted our runway since eighteen months."
C "That deal is cutting our runway for nine months."
In a job interview: "Tell me about a decision you regret." You open with:
A "I don't really have any. My decisions have been good."
B "Two years ago I signed off on an acquisition. In hindsight, we moved too fast."
C "I regret many things but I prefer not to talk about it."

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesmo respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não há resposta certa ou errada — fale por 2 a 3 minutos, sem script.

A board member asks: "Walk me through a financial decision that went wrong, and tell me what you would have done differently." Answer for 2-3 minutes. Use the third conditional (if we had..., we would have...) at least three times, and hedge at least once with "in hindsight" or "arguably". Take responsibility without blaming your team — and finish with what the process requires now.
