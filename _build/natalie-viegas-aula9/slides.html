Chapter 3 -- The Contrast Code

Grammar Discovery

Linking words for contrast and concession

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words do between the two ideas?

"I agree with the plan; however, the budget is tight."

"Although the idea is good, we need more time."

"Your point is valid. On the other hand, the client may disagree."

"Even though we disagree, we can find common ground."

Grammar Rule

Linking Words for Contrast

Linking wordUseExample
howevercontrast between two sentences (period/semicolon before)"It is a good plan. However, it is expensive."
although / even thoughconcession at the start of a clause"Although I agree, I have one objection."
on the other handintroduce the opposite side"On the other hand, the cost is high."
but / whilecontrast inside one sentence"I see your point, but I disagree."

Key idea: use however and on the other hand to connect two sentences (with a period or semicolon before, comma after). Use although / even though at the start of a clause ("Although I agree, ..."). Use but / while to join a contrast inside one sentence.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Although the plan is good, but we need more time."
"Although the plan is good, we need more time."

Use although OR but for one contrast, never both together. And however connects two sentences ("I agree. However, the cost is high"), it does not start a clause like although.

Dialogue

Debating with a Colleague

M

"Natalie, I hear your standpoint, but I am not sure we should change the tool now. What is your main argument?"

N

"My argument is simple. The current system is slow. However, the new tool saves the team many hours every week."

M

"That is a valid point. On the other hand, the training will take time. That is my counterargument."

N

"I concede that. Although the training takes time, the long-term benefit is bigger. Can we find common ground?"

M

"Maybe. I have one objection about the cost. Is there a reasonable compromise?"

N

"Yes. Let us run a small pilot first. If it works, we move everyone. I think that is a fair consensus."
