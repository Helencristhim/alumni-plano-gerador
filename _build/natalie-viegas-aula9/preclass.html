Aula 9 -- Pre-class

Agreeing and Disagreeing -- Linking Words for Contrast

Take part in a professional debate: agree, disagree with respect, and connect your ideas with linking words (however, although, even though, on the other hand). New vocabulary: debate, standpoint, counterargument, compromise, valid, concede, common ground, reasonable, objection, consensus.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Debate -- a formal discussion where people present opposing ideas
"We had a healthy debate about the new tool." (Debate)
Standpoint -- your position or opinion on a topic or issue
"From my standpoint, we should change the tool." (Ponto de vista)
Counterargument -- a reason that goes against another person's idea
"That is a fair counterargument, but think about cost." (Contra-argumento)
Compromise -- an agreement where each side gives up something
"A small pilot is a good compromise for both teams." (Meio-termo)
Valid -- based on good reasons and worth accepting
"You make a valid point about the training time." (Válido)
Concede -- to admit that something is true or that someone is right
"I concede that the training will take time." (Admitir)
Common ground -- the ideas or interests that both sides share and agree on
"We disagree on the date, but we can find common ground." (Ponto em comum)
Reasonable -- fair, sensible and based on good judgment
"That sounds like a reasonable offer to me." (Razoável)
Objection -- a reason or statement that shows you disagree
"My only objection is the high cost." (Objeção)
Consensus -- a general agreement shared by a group of people
"After the debate, we reached a consensus." (Consenso)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

In the Thursday debate, Natalie takes a clear standpoint: the team should change the project tool. "The new tool saves hours," she says. "However, I know there is an objection about training time." A colleague gives a counterargument about the cost. "That is a valid point," Natalie answers, "and I concede it. Although the training takes time, the benefit is bigger." To find common ground, she offers a reasonable compromise: a small pilot first. In the end, the team reaches a consensus.

B She concedes the valid point, then explains the bigger benefit.
C She gets angry and leaves the meeting.
3. What compromise does she offer?
A A small pilot first.
B No change at all.
C Change everything in one day.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Linking Words for Contrast

GRAMMAR

Usamos linking words para conectar duas ideias que contrastam -- concordar numa parte e discordar na outra. / We use linking words to connect two ideas that contrast -- agreeing on one part and disagreeing on another.

Linking wordUseExample
howevercontrast between two sentences (period/semicolon before)"It is a good plan. However, it is expensive."
although / even thoughconcession at the start of a clause"Although I agree, I have one objection."
on the other handintroduce the opposite side"On the other hand, the cost is high."
but / whilecontrast inside one sentence"I see your point, but I disagree."

Key point / Ponto-chave: however e on the other hand conectam duas frases (ponto ou ponto-e-vírgula antes, vírgula depois). Although / even though começam uma cláusula ("Although I agree, ..."). Nunca use although e but juntos no mesmo contraste.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "Although the plan is good, but it is expensive."
B "The plan is good. However, it is expensive."
C "The plan is good, however is expensive."
You want to disagree with respect. What do you do first?
A Concede the valid points, then explain your objection.
B Say the other person is completely wrong.
C Ignore the counterargument.

A Common ground and a reasonable compromise.
