Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Reservation -- an arrangement to keep a room, table, or seat for you
"I made a reservation for two nights at the hotel." (Reserva)
Booking -- the act of reserving something, like a room or a tour
"My booking is for the fifteenth of next month." (Reserva / agendamento)
Confirm -- to say that something is sure and will happen
"The hotel called to confirm my reservation." (Confirmar)
Cancel -- to stop a plan or booking so it does not happen
"I had to cancel my booking because my plans changed." (Cancelar)
Availability -- whether a room or seat is free for the dates you want
"I checked the availability before I booked." (Disponibilidade)
Deposit -- money you pay first to hold a booking
"I paid a small deposit to hold the room." (Sinal / depósito)
Check-in time -- the time you can arrive and get your room at a hotel
"The check-in time is three in the afternoon." (Horário de entrada)
Voucher -- a paper or message that proves your booking is paid
"Show your voucher at the front desk." (Comprovante / voucher)
Refund -- money given back to you when you cancel a booking
"I got a full refund because I cancelled early." (Reembolso)
Deadline -- the last day or time to do something, like to cancel
"The deadline to cancel is seven days before arrival." (Prazo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tuca calls the hotel and then tells her friend the news. The receptionist said, "We have availability." Later, Tuca reports it: "The receptionist said that they had availability." This is reported speech: we tell someone what another person said, and the verb usually moves one step into the past. Present (have) becomes past (had); will becomes would; can becomes could. We use said with no person after it, but told + a person: "She told me that the room was free." For requests, we use asked + person + to + verb: "She asked me to confirm by Friday." Reported speech is useful when you pass on a message about a booking, a deposit, or a deadline.

1. The receptionist said, "We have availability." How do we report it?
A She said that they have availability.

C She said me they have availability.
2. Which verb needs a person directly after it?
A said ("She said me...")
B told ("She told me...")

3. To report a request, "Confirm by Friday," we say:
A She asked me to confirm by Friday.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech (Said / Told, Backshift)

GRAMMAR

Como contar o que outra pessoa disse. / How to tell someone what another person said.

Direct speechReported / IndiretoChange / Mudança
"We have a room."She said that they had a room.presente → passado / have → had
"I will send the voucher."He told me that he would send it.will → would; told + pessoa / + person
"Confirm by Friday."She asked me to confirm by Friday.pedido: asked + pessoa + to + verbo / request

Key point / Ponto-chave: em reported speech, o verbo geralmente recua um passo no tempo (backshift): have→had, will→would, can→could. Use said (sem pessoa) ou told + pessoa. Para pedidos: asked + pessoa + to + verbo. / The verb usually shifts one step back; use said (no person) or told + person; for requests, asked + person + to + verb.
