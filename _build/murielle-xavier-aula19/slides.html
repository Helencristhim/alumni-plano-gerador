Chapter 3

Modal Verbs

Should, could, might + base verb

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should talk to the manager first."

"We could offer a flexible schedule."

"They might accept the new policy."

"You should not ignore the feedback."

UseFormExample
Advice (recommend)should / should not + base verbyou should talk to HR
Option (possibility)could + base verbwe could offer training
Possibility (less sure)might + base verbthey might agree
All modalsmodal + base verb (no to, no -s)she should decide
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

You should to talk to HR.

✓ RIGHT

You should talk to HR.

✗ WRONG

She should decides now.

✓ RIGHT

She should decide now.

✗ WRONG

We could to offer training.

✓ RIGHT

We could offer training.

✗ WRONG

You not should ignore it.

✓ RIGHT

You should not ignore it.

After should, could, and might, use the base verb: no to and no -s. The most common negative advice is should not (shouldn't).

Chapter 4

The Advice

Advising on a retention dilemma

Dialogue

The Dilemma

E
Murielle, one of our best analysts wants to leave. What should we do?
M
We should talk to her first and understand why. It is advisable to listen before we act.
E
And if it is about money?
M
We could offer a raise, or we could propose a new role. Both are worthwhile options.
E
What are the drawbacks?
M
The main drawback is cost, but the downside of losing her is worse.
E
So what is your recommendation?
M
We should weigh up both options. She might stay if we act quickly and are cautious.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What should they do first, according to Murielle?
They should talk to the analyst and understand why she wants to leave.
2. What two options could they offer?
They could offer a raise or propose a new role.
3. What might happen if they act quickly?
The analyst might stay.
