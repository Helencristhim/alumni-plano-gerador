Chapter 4: The Future Code

Two Ways to Talk About the Future

Going to for intentions, present continuous for arrangements

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Next month I am going to start a new course."

"On Friday I am meeting my advisor at three."

"I am going to sign up for the exam."

"In December we are traveling to New York."

FormUse it forExample
Be going toplans and intentions (decided before now)I am going to start a course.
Present continuousfixed arrangements (with a time or place)I am meeting my advisor on Friday.

Intention or decision? going to. Already in the diary, with a time? present continuous.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Form

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"Next year I ___am going to change jobs." (intention)
"On Monday I ___am meeting the doctor at nine." (fixed arrangement)
"I ___am going to sign up for the course." (intention)
"We ___are traveling to Rio next week." (fixed arrangement)
"She ___is going to study medicine." (intention)
Language Focus

Name the Form

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"Next month I going to start a course."

✓ RIGHT

"Next month I am going to start a course."

✗ WRONG

"On Friday I meet my advisor at three."

✓ RIGHT

"On Friday I am meeting my advisor at three."

Always keep the verb be with going to (I am going to, not "I going to"). For a fixed arrangement with a time, use the present continuous (I am meeting), not the present simple.

Dialogue

A Plan for the Year

A
Lucas, I finally made up my mind about this year. I am going to be much more organized.
L
That sounds great! So what is your main goal?
A
To pass an advanced English exam. Next month I am signing up for a course.
L
Nice! When are you starting?
A
I am starting in April, and I am meeting my study group every Tuesday evening.
L
You are really organized. Do you have any plans outside of studying?
A
Yes! I am really looking forward to a trip in the spring. But I had to postpone my move.
L
That is a smart decision. It sounds like you have a busy year ahead.
Did You Catch It?

About the Conversation

1. In the conversation, what is the main goal?
To pass an advanced English exam.
2. What did she have to postpone, and what is she looking forward to?
She had to postpone her move, but she is looking forward to a trip in the spring.
3. What does Lucas think about her year?
He thinks she is organized and has a busy year ahead.
