Grammar Video
Present Continuous and 'Going To' -- 6 Minute Grammar (BBC Learning English)

A short, clear video on the exact grammar from today: how to use "going to" for a plan you have already decided, and the present continuous for a fixed future arrangement. It gives simple, natural examples and a quick quiz. Connection to Lesson 10: this is the difference between an intention (I am going to) and an appointment in your diary (I am meeting).

Tip: pause after each example and make one true sentence about your own plans, one with "going to" and one with the present continuous.
