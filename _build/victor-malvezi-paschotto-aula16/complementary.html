Lesson 16 -- Influencing Without Authority: Getting Buy-In Across the Org

Series
Suits or Industry — any scene where someone gets a peer or rival to act without any power over them

Watch how the best operators move people they cannot order. Notice who leads with the other person's win, who frames a request so the other side feels it was their idea, and who lines up an ally before the room rather than walking in cold. That is influence without authority in action.

Tip: pause whenever someone wins a peer over and label the move — find the win, frame, enlist, rally, reciprocity.

Podcast
WorkLife with Adam Grant / HBR IdeaCast — any episode on persuasion, influence, or leading without authority

Experts unpack how to move people you don't manage: lead with their win, pull instead of push so the plan becomes theirs, enlist a credible advocate, and build goodwill through reciprocity. The exact toolkit from this class, in real leadership voices — ideal prep before Victor runs his cross-functional asks at Brink's.

Tip: write one go-to line for each move — find the win, frame, enlist, rally, reciprocity — that you would actually say to a peer.

YouTube
Harvard Business Review / TED — "How to Influence Without Authority" or "The Science of Persuasion" (Cialdini)

A short, practical guide to moving the org when you can't pull rank: find the other side's win, frame so they own it, enlist an advocate, and use reciprocity. A clean bridge from building consensus inside your own room (Lesson 15) to winning over peers across the whole org.

Tip: draft a five-move influence script for a real cross-functional ask you expect at work, then read it aloud and check each move.
