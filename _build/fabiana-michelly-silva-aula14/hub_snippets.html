Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Diplomatic -- careful and tactful when dealing with people
"Keep the language diplomatic when you push back on price." (Diplomático)
Goodwill -- a friendly, cooperative attitude between two sides
"We protect the goodwill so both sides will sign." (Boa vontade / relacionamento)
Amenable -- willing to agree, cooperate, or be persuaded
"A supplier who feels respected is amenable." (Receptivo / aberto)
Constructive -- helpful and intended to improve a situation
"Instead of rejecting it, we offered a constructive alternative." (Construtivo)
Sticking point -- an issue that blocks agreement from being reached
"The penalty clause was the only sticking point left." (Ponto de conflito)
Wiggle room -- room or flexibility to change or negotiate
"Is there any wiggle room on the delivery schedule?" (Margem de negociação)
Caveat -- a warning or condition attached to a statement
"I agree, with one caveat: confirm it in writing by Friday." (Ressalva)
Reservation -- a concern or doubt about a proposal
"We have one or two reservations about the penalty clause." (Reserva / ressalva)
Hammer out -- to reach an agreement after long, hard discussion
"Let us hammer out the final terms before Friday." (Negociar até chegar a um acordo)
Defuse -- to reduce tension in a difficult situation
"If the room gets tense, defuse it with a question." (Amenizar / desarmar)
Understate -- to present something as smaller or less serious
"Understate the problem: say it is a slight concern." (Minimizar / suavizar)
Tentative -- not firm or final; provisional and open to change
"We reached a tentative agreement on price." (Provisório / preliminar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A skilled negotiator never sounds like an order. Instead of "Lower the price," she says the price might be a little high and asks whether there is any wiggle room. Instead of "This clause is wrong," she says she has one or two reservations about it. When a point becomes a sticking point, she does not reject it; she suggests it might be worth revisiting the wording. To ask for a favour, she uses an indirect question: "I was wondering whether you could be flexible on the deadline." Notice that each move adds a modal, a qualifier, or an indirect frame to soften the message and protect the goodwill, so the other side stays amenable.

1. Why does the negotiator say "the price might be a little high" instead of "the price is too high"?
A The modal "might" plus the qualifier "a little" soften the criticism.
B "Might" means she is unsure of the actual price.
C The two sentences mean exactly the same thing.
2. Why use "I was wondering whether you could..." instead of "Can you...?"
A It is grammatically required after a comma.
B The indirect frame makes the request more polite and less pushy.
C It is only used in writing, never in speech.
3. What does "it might be worth revisiting the wording" do?
A It turns a demand into a soft suggestion ("it might be worth + -ing").
B It states that the wording is definitely worthless.
C It is an order to rewrite the clause immediately.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each professional situation.

You want to push back on price without offending the supplier. You say:
A "The price might be a little high. Is there any wiggle room?" (modal + qualifier)
B "Your price is too high. Lower it." (blunt imperative damages goodwill)
C "The price is unacceptable and final." (no room to negotiate)
A clause is a problem, but you want to keep it constructive. The best line is:
A "This clause is completely wrong." (blunt judgement)
B "We have one or two reservations -- it might be worth revisiting it." (soft noun + suggestion)
C "Delete the clause now." (an order, not a negotiation)
You want to make a polite request for flexibility. You say:
A "I was wondering whether you could be flexible on the deadline." (indirect question)
B "Be flexible on the deadline." (a command)
C "You must change the deadline." (no goodwill)
