Chapter 3

Softening the Message

Diplomatic language: hedging, qualifiers, and indirect questions

Discovery

Say It More Softly

Blunt: "This is wrong." This might not be quite right. (modal + qualifier)

Blunt: "Lower the price." Would it be possible to lower the price a little? (would + question)

Blunt: "I disagree." I am not sure I fully agree. (understatement)

Blunt: "Change the clause." It might be worth revisiting the clause. (it might be worth + -ing)

Blunt: "Is there a discount?" I was wondering whether there might be a discount. (indirect question)

Blunt: "We refuse." We do have a few reservations about that. (soft noun + qualifier)

Grammar Rule

Diplomatic Language

MoveHowExample
Modal softenersmight / could / would instead of is / mustThe price might be a little high.
Qualifiersa little / a bit / somewhat / slightly / fairlyWe have one or two reservations.
Understatementsay less than you mean (negative + not quite)I am not sure I fully agree.
SuggestingIt might be worth + -ing; I'd suggest + -ingIt might be worth revisiting the clause.
Indirect questionsI was wondering whether / Would it be possible toI was wondering whether there might be wiggle room.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Lower the price. The offer is unacceptable." (bare imperative + blunt judgement sound like an order, not a negotiation)
"Would it be possible to lower the price a little? The offer might be slightly high for us." (modal + qualifier soften the demand)

To stay diplomatic, replace a bare imperative with a modal question (Would it be possible to...? Could we...?) and add a qualifier (a little, slightly, somewhat). Understate the problem instead of judging it.

Closing the Deal

Thank you for the revised offer. I have to say the price might be a little high for us. I was wondering whether there is any wiggle room.
M
We could look at a small adjustment, but the penalty clause would need to stay as it is.

I understand. To be honest, the penalty clause is our one sticking point. We have a couple of reservations about the notice period.

That is a fair point. What did you have in mind?

It might be worth shortening the notice period a little. We would be amenable to meeting you on price if you could be flexible there. That feels constructive for both sides.

I can live with that, with one caveat: we confirm it in writing by Friday. Then we have a tentative deal.

"It might be worth shortening the notice period." (Blunt: "Shorten the notice period.")
