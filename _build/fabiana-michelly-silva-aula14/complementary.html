Lesson 14 -- Negotiating the Contract

Series
Suits (2011) -- Contract negotiation scenes

A masterclass in diplomatic pressure. Watch how the lawyers push back, soften demands, concede small points, and protect the relationship while still winning the terms they need.

Pick one negotiation scene and rewrite three blunt lines as diplomatic ones (might be a little..., I was wondering whether..., it might be worth...).

Podcast
Negotiate Anything -- "Diplomatic Language and Tactical Empathy"

An episode on how the words you choose shape a negotiation: softening demands, hedging claims, and keeping the other side amenable. Directly relevant to closing a supplier contract without burning the relationship.

Note two softening phrases the host uses. Try each one in your own next work email or call.

YouTube
"How to Disagree Diplomatically in English" -- Business English

A short guide to the exact phrases that soften disagreement, qualify a claim, and turn a demand into a polite request -- the core moves of diplomatic negotiation.

After watching, draft a short term sheet for a real negotiation, using a different softening move for price, penalty, and delivery.
