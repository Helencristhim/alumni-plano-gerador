Chapter 3: The Code

Who Does It Belong To?

my · her · our · Clarinha's

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

My granddaughter is three years old.

Her school is bilingual.

Our weekends are for the playground.

Clarinha's smile is the best part of my week.

What comes after each orange word? And whose smile is it?

FormWhat it doesExample
my, your, his, her, our, theirPossessive adjective. It goes right before a noun to show who it belongs to.my granddaughter · her school · our weekend
name + 'sApostrophe + s after a person's name shows the owner.Clarinha's smile · my son's house
he → his, she → herThe word matches the person, not the noun after it.her name (a woman) · his name (a man)
The key ideaTo say who something belongs to, use my/her/our before the noun, or name + 's.Clarinha is my granddaughter, and this is her book.

The secret: put the little word my / her / our before the noun, or add 's to a name. Never say "the school of Clarinha" — say Clarinha's school.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"The toys of Clarinha are on the floor."
"Clarinha's toys are on the floor."
"She name is Clarinha."
"Her name is Clarinha."
"He is my son, and he house is big."
"He is my son, and his house is big."

One idea fixes all three: to show who owns something, use my / her / his before the noun, or add 's to the name — never "the noun of the person".

Chapter 4: Talking About Family

"Do You Have Family Back Home?"

A warm question at the end of a long day.

Dialogue

Marco Asks About Your Family

M
Good evening, Maria Clara. What a long day of sessions! Tell me something happy — do you have family back home in Brazil?
C
I do! I have three children, and a granddaughter. Her name is Clarinha, and she is three years old. I am so proud of her.
M
Three years old! What is she like?
C
She is very curious — she asks about everything. And her school is bilingual, so she is learning English and Portuguese at the same time.
M
That is wonderful. So you encourage her at home too?
C
Every day. I support her, and we spend time together. On weekends, we go to the playground, and I say small things in English. And what about you — do you have children?
M
I have two. They are older now — his name is Paolo, and her name is Sofia. Children grow up so fast, don't they?
C
They really do. That is why I am here, learning English. I want to speak it with Clarinha when she grows up. I am so proud to be her grandmother.
