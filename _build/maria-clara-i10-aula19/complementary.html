Lesson 19 -- Talking About Clarinha

Talk
TED-Ed — "The Benefits of a Bilingual Brain" (Mia Nacamulli, YouTube)

This is the science behind Clarinha's bilingual school, in six gentle minutes. It shows why a child who grows up with two languages has a stronger, more flexible brain — and why the small English moments you share with her matter so much. A warm reason to feel proud of what she is doing.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Do not try to understand every word. Listen only for the good news: what two languages do for a growing child. Then say it out loud: "Clarinha is bilingual, and I am so proud of her."

Podcast
Real English Real Talk — "Talk About Your Family in English" (Podcast for Beginners)

This is your lesson, in a slow and simple conversation. Two speakers talk about their family in easy English — who they are, how old they are, and what they are like. It is exactly the language you used today to describe Clarinha, at a speed made for you.

Tip: listen once in the car, with no pauses. Then listen again and write down every possessive you hear (my, her, our, his). Practice one sentence out loud about your own family: "My granddaughter is three years old."

YouTube
MosaLingua — "How To Raise a Bilingual Child: Personal Experience and Tips"

Simple, practical ideas for the very thing you do with Clarinha: helping a child learn a second language at home. It shows small daily habits — a few words, a book, a game — that encourage and support a bilingual child. Real tips you can use on your next weekend at the playground.

Tip: turn on the English subtitles. Pause after each tip and ask yourself: "Can I do this with Clarinha?" Choose one idea and try it this week. Then write down what you did and how she reacted.
