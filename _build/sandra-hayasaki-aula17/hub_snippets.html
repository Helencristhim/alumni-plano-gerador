Aula 17 -- Pre-class

I Wish, If Only

Fale sobre desejos do presente e pequenos arrependimentos de forma gentil, usando I wish + verbo no passado (I wish I had more time) e if only. Novo vocabulário: wonder, daydream, long for, miss, talent, imperfect, content, make the most of. Foco gramatical: wish + past simple (desejos sobre o agora).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Wonder -- to ask yourself a question; to feel curious about something
"I wonder how it would feel to be fluent already." (perguntar-se / imaginar)
Daydream -- to imagine pleasant things in your mind while you are awake
"I daydream about a slow morning with a long coffee." (devanear / sonhar acordado)
Long for -- to want something very much, deeply and quietly
"I long for the sound of the sea near my home." (anseiar por / ter saudade de)
Miss -- to feel the absence of something you used to have or enjoy
"I studied French years ago, and now I miss it." (sentir falta de)
Talent -- a natural ability or skill you are born good at
"I wish I had a talent for cooking." (talento)
Imperfect -- not perfect; having small faults -- and that is okay
"I am content being imperfect and still curious." (imperfeito)
Content -- quietly happy and at peace with what you have
"My life is full, and I am content with who I am." (satisfeito / em paz)
Make the most of -- to use and enjoy something as fully as possible
"I make the most of each evening, one page at a time." (aproveitar ao máximo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Let me share a few gentle wishes. I wish I had more hours in the day; I often daydream about a slower morning. I wish I knew more French, because I studied it years ago and now I miss it. If only I lived closer to the sea -- I long for the sound of water. And I wish I were a better cook, though my family is kind about it. None of these are big regrets. I am content with my life, imperfect and curious, and I make the most of each day.

1. When the writer says "I wish I had more hours", is it about the past or about now?
A About now -- a present wish, using a past verb.
B About the past -- something that happened before.
C A real future plan.
2. Why does the writer say "I wish I were a better cook" and not "was"?
A Because "was" is more polite.
B Because after "I wish", we use "were" for all people.
C Because it is a question.
3. How does the writer feel about life overall?
A Full of heavy regrets.
B Content, with only small, gentle wishes.
C Angry about the past.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Wish + Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Usamos I wish + verbo no passado para falar de um desejo sobre o presente (algo que queremos que seja diferente agora). O passado aqui NÃO fala do passado. Use were para todas as pessoas (I wish I were). If only usa a mesma estrutura, com um sentimento um pouco mais forte. / We use I wish + past simple for a wish about the present: I wish I had more time. Use were for everyone, and if only for a slightly stronger feeling.

Reflection
