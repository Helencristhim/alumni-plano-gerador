Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Let me tell you how far I've come. When I started, I used to be terrified of speaking English. I used to write every sentence in my head first, and by the time it was ready, the moment had passed. In meetings, I used to stay silent and just nod. I didn't use to ask questions, because I was afraid of sounding wrong. But things are different now. I don't freeze anymore. When there's a problem, I stay calm and explain. What I feel now is pride. I'm proud of the progress I've made, grateful for every small step, and I had the courage to keep going. I overcame the fear, and reaching this point feels like a real milestone — and it's only the beginning.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- "Used to" for Then vs Now

GRAMMAR

Como falar de um hábito ou estado do passado que não é mais verdade. / How to talk about a past habit or state that is no longer true.

FormEnglishPortuguês
used to + base verbI used to freeze. I used to be shy.Eu costumava... (antes, mas não mais)
didn't use to + base verbI didn't use to speak in meetings.Eu não costumava... (negativa: sem o "d")
used to ..., but now ...I used to be nervous, but now I stay calm.Contraste: antes vs agora
TipThe verb after "used to" stays in its base form (freeze, be, stay) — never the past form (froze, was, stayed).
⚠ CarefulDon't say "used to froze" → "used to freeze". In the negative, the "d" moves to "did": "didn't use to". For the past, it's "used to" (with the "d"), not "use to".
