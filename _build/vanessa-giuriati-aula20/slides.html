Chapter 3

Then vs Now

"Used to" for the way things were

Grammar Discovery

The Way Things Were

"I used to freeze when someone asked a hard question."

"I used to stay silent in meetings."

"I didn't use to ask questions."

"I used to be nervous, but now I stay calm."

FormUseExample
used to + base verbA past habit or state, no longer trueI used to freeze. I used to be shy.
didn't use to + base verbSomething you did NOT do beforeI didn't use to speak in meetings.
used to ..., but now ...Contrast the past with the present: "I used to be nervous, but now I stay calm." The verb after "used to" stays in its base form (freeze, be, stay) — never the past form.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I used to froze in meetings."

✓ RIGHT

"I used to freeze in meetings."

✗ WRONG

"I didn't used to speak up."

✓ RIGHT

"I didn't use to speak up."

✗ WRONG

"I use to be nervous." (about the past)

✓ RIGHT

"I used to be nervous."

After "used to," the verb stays in its base form (freeze, be, speak). In the negative, the "d" moves to "did": "didn't use to." For the past, it's "used to" with the "d," not "use to."
