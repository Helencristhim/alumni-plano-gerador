Chapter 3

The Engine

going to · will · present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Three Ways to the Future

"I'm going to prepare the report tomorrow."

"I'll send you the file right now."

"I'm meeting a friend on Saturday."

"I think it will go well."

FormUseExample
going toa plan or intentionI'm going to study tonight.
willa quick decision or predictionI think it will rain. I'll help you.
present continuousa fixed arrangement (a date set)I'm meeting her at noon.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am going to will travel."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to travel."

✗ WRONG

"I meet her tomorrow at ten."

✓ RIGHT

"I'm meeting her tomorrow at ten."

✗ WRONG

"I am going study tonight."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to study tonight."

Use only one future form per verb. "going to" always keeps the word "to". For a fixed date, use "I'm + verb-ing".

Detective

Spot the Error

0 / 4 errors found

"I am going to will study tonight."
"I am going to study tonight." (one future form only)
"I am going study tomorrow."
"I am going to study tomorrow." (keep "to")
"I meet the client at ten tomorrow."
"I am meeting the client at ten tomorrow." (fixed arrangement)
"I am will send it later."
"I will send it later." (no "am" before "will")
