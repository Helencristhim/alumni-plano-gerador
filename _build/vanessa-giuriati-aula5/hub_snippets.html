Making Plans and the Future

Future forms for plans and goals. Key words: schedule, appointment, deadline, arrange, confirm, cancel, postpone, attend. Grammar: going to (plans), will (decisions/predictions), present continuous (fixed arrangements).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Schedule -- a plan of times for things to happen (agenda/cronograma)
"My schedule is full this week."
Appointment -- a fixed time to meet or be seen (compromisso/consulta)
"I have a doctor's appointment at three."
Deadline -- the latest time to finish something (prazo final)
"The deadline for the report is Friday."
Arrange -- to organize or plan something (combinar/organizar)
"Let's arrange a call for next week."
Confirm -- to say something is definite or true (confirmar)
"Please confirm the meeting time."
Cancel -- to stop something that was planned (cancelar)
"I had to cancel my trip."
Postpone -- to move something to a later time (adiar)
"We postponed the call to Monday."
Attend -- to go to an event or meeting (comparecer/participar)
"I will attend the workshop on Friday."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Beatriz has a busy week ahead. On Monday, she is meeting her manager at nine — it is already in her schedule. On Tuesday, she is going to prepare a report, because the deadline is Wednesday. "I think it will be a lot of work," she says, "but I will finish it on time." On Thursday, she has an appointment, so she is going to work from home. If a meeting changes, she can postpone it or cancel it. On Friday, she is attending a workshop. "I'm really looking forward to the weekend," she adds with a smile.

1. Why does the text say "she is meeting her manager" and not "she will meet"?
A Because it is a fixed arrangement already in her schedule.
B Because "will" is wrong in English.
C Because the meeting is in the past.
2. Which form shows a plan or intention?
A "She is going to prepare a report."
B "She prepare a report."
C "She prepared a report."
3. What can Beatriz do if a meeting changes?
A She has to attend it anyway.
B She can postpone it or cancel it.
C She must finish it the same day.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Forms

GRAMMAR

Três maneiras de falar do futuro. / Three ways to talk about the future.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
going to (plan)I am going to finish the report.Eu vou terminar o relatório. (plano)
will (decision/prediction)I'll help you. It will rain.Eu (vou) te ajudo. Vai chover.
present continuous (fixed)I'm meeting her at noon.Eu vou encontrá-la ao meio-dia. (marcado)
NegativeI'm not going to work on Friday.Eu não vou trabalhar na sexta.
QuestionAre you going to attend? What are you doing on Friday? Keep the word "to" with "going to".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situação.

Someone asks: "What are your plans for tonight?" The best answer is:
A "I am going to cook dinner and relax."
B "I going cook dinner."
C "I will cooked dinner."
Your colleague drops some papers. You say:
A "I am going to help you." (pre-planned)
B "I'll help you." (decided now)
C "I help you now."
Which sentence describes a fixed arrangement?
A "I'm meeting the client at three on Friday."
B "I meet the client Friday."
C "I will meeting the client."
How do you ask about someone's weekend plans?
A "What are you going to do this weekend?"
B "What you do weekend?"
C "What will you doing weekend?"
