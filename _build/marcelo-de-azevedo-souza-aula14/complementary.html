Aula 14 -- The Places That Stay With You

Film
Local Hero (1983) -- directed by Bill Forsyth

An American oil executive is sent to buy a sleepy Scottish coastal village and, without meaning to, falls completely in love with the unassuming little place and the people who live there. It is the gentlest possible illustration of tonight's whole theme: a weathered, out-of-the-way town that quietly leaves a mark on you and never lets go. Connection to Lesson 14: listen for how the village and its characters are described -- the beach where he walks, the innkeeper whose phone he borrows, the town that changes him.

Tip: after a scene, describe the village out loud in two relative clauses -- "It is a place that...", "It is the kind of town where...".

Podcast
Amateur Traveler -- pick a destination episode

In every episode a guest describes one place in loving, vivid detail -- the town they keep going back to, the corner most tourists never find, the person who made the trip. It is a whole podcast of people describing the places that stayed with them, which is exactly tonight's language. A wonderful bath of natural relative clauses: "a village that...", "a market where...", "a guide whose...".

Tip: choose a place you are curious about, then afterwards describe it in three relative clauses of your own -- "It is a city that...", "It is a place where...", "The people who...".

How to use relative clauses -- a Mini English Lesson (British Council)

A short, clear British Council lesson on exactly tonight's grammar: how to identify defining and non-defining relative clauses, and when to add the commas. It puts the rules from your Grammar Tip into simple, memorable examples -- the perfect warm-up before you describe your own place out loud.

Tip: pause after each example and make your own version, one defining and one non-defining, about a place you know.
