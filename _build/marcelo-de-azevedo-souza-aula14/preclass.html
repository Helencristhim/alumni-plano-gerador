Aula 14 -- Pre-class

The Places That Stay With You -- People and Places That Made Your Trip

Reading and describing the places and people that stay with you, using relative clauses. Key words: to stick in your mind, a landmark, a well-kept secret, to stumble upon, unassuming, weathered, bustling, sleepy, to grow on you, a haven, to leave a mark on, to be steeped in history. Focus: relative clauses (who / which / that / where / whose) -- defining (no commas) and non-defining (extra information, with commas).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To stick in your mind -- to stay in your memory, hard to forget (ficar na memória, não sair da cabeça)
"That little town really stuck in my mind."
A landmark -- a famous building or place that is easy to recognize (um marco, um ponto de referência famoso)
"The cathedral is the city's most famous landmark."
A well-kept secret -- a wonderful place that few people know about (um segredo bem guardado, pouco conhecido)
"The beach is a well-kept secret among the locals."
To stumble upon -- to find something by chance, without looking for it (topar com, encontrar por acaso)
"We stumbled upon a tiny bakery down a side street."
Unassuming -- modest and simple, not trying to impress (despretensioso, modesto)
"It was an unassuming little cafe with the best coffee."
Weathered -- worn and marked by time and the weather (desgastado pelo tempo, curtido)
"The weathered old harbor was full of character."
Bustling -- full of busy, lively activity (movimentado, agitado, cheio de vida)
"The bustling market was packed by nine in the morning."
Sleepy -- very quiet and peaceful, with little happening (pacato, sonolento (de lugar))
"It is a sleepy fishing village most of the year."
To grow on you -- to become more likeable the more you know it (ir cativando aos poucos)
"The place is strange at first, but it grows on you."
A haven -- a calm, safe and pleasant place (um refúgio, um porto seguro)
"The garden was a haven from the noise of the city."
To leave a mark on -- to have a lasting effect on someone or something (deixar uma marca, marcar)
"That summer left a mark on me."
To be steeped in history -- to be full of history (ser cheio de história, impregnado de história)
"The old quarter is steeped in history."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

On a walk through her town, Sigrid showed Marcelo the square where she grew up. She pointed to a weathered old house which is nearly three hundred years old, and to a bakery whose owner still remembers every name. "The tourists all go to the city that has the famous cathedral," she said, "but this is the place that stayed with me." A defining clause tells you which one and takes no commas: "the place that stayed with me". A non-defining clause just adds extra information, takes commas, and uses which or who, never that: "the house, which is very old, ...".

1. Por que "the place that stayed with me" não leva vírgula?
A É uma defining clause — essencial para dizer QUAL lugar; sem vírgulas, e pode usar "that".
B Porque "that" está escrito errado.
C Porque toda relative clause leva vírgula.
2. Which is correct for a non-defining clause (extra information)?
A "Bergen, which sits between mountains, is stunning."
B "Bergen, that sits between mountains, is stunning."
C "Bergen what sits between mountains is stunning."
3. Which relative pronoun fits a place?
A "the town who I love"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Relative Clauses

GRAMMAR

Como descrever coisas, pessoas e lugares (explicação em inglês e português).

PronounPoints to / Aponta paraExample
whopeople / pessoasthe woman who ran the cafe
which / thatthings / coisasthe town that stayed with me
whereplaces / lugaresthe square where we sat
whosepossession / possea bakery whose owner knew me
whentime / tempothe day when I arrived

Atenção: uma defining clause é essencial (diz QUAL) e não leva vírgula: "the place that stayed". Uma non-defining clause só acrescenta informação extra, leva vírgulas e usa which/who, nunca "that": "Bergen, which...". Nunca use what como pronome relativo ("the place that I loved", não "what I loved").

Stage 2: Put the Steps in Order

Order

Coloque os passos de montar uma relative clause na ordem correta.

?Decide if it is essential (defining) or just extra information (non-defining).
?If it is extra information, add commas around the clause and use which or who.
?Start with the noun you want to describe (a town, a woman, a cafe).
?Join the two ideas into one smooth sentence.
?Choose the right pronoun: who for people, which/that for things, where for places, whose for possession.
