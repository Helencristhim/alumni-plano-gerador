American English Podcast — Ep. 111: Chats with Shana — A Vacation in Hawaii

Two American women telling the story of a real trip: the flight, the arrival, the hotel, what went well and what did not. This is exactly the language of Lesson 2 — the story of the arrival in the past simple (“we landed”, “we took a cab”) mixed with the present perfect of what has already happened (“we’ve already been there”). American English, natural speed, transcript on the website.

Tip (do it in the car, on the way there): listen once, all the way through, with no pauses. Then write down every time you hear just, already or yet. You do not need to understand the whole sentence — hunting for ONE word already trains your ear, and that word is our grammar this week.

American English Podcast — Ep. 168: Irregular Verbs with the Statue of Liberty

A New York story told slowly, made to train exactly the past participles (been, gone, taken, seen) — the missing piece of the present perfect. Twenty minutes, American English, and the setting is the city where you land in September.

Tip (on the way back): listen and repeat out loud every participle that trips you up. Then build sentences about YOUR trip: “I’ve been to New York three times”, “I’ve never taken a cab from JFK at night”.

Already vs. Still vs. Yet — Speak English With Vanessa

The grammar of Lesson 2, in American English, with a teacher who speaks slowly and repeats: when to use already (it is done) and when to use yet (not up to now) — and why yet always goes at the end of the sentence.

Tip: after the video, say five sentences out loud about your trip in September — two with already (“I’ve already booked the hotel”), two with yet (“I haven’t bought the tickets yet”) and one with just. Do not write them down first.
