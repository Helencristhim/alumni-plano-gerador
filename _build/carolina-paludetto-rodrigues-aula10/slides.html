Grammar Rule

Possessives and have / has

FormUseExample
haveI / you / we / theyI have two cousins.
hashe / she / itShe has one brother.
my, your, his, her, our, theirbefore a nounThis is my uncle.
name + 'sto say whoseCarolina's family is big.

Key idea: use have with I, you, we, they and has with he, she, it. To say a thing belongs to someone, use a possessive word (my, her, their) before the noun, or add 's to a name: my brother's dog, Carolina's notebook.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"My sister have two cats."
"My sister has two cats."

My sister is the same as she, so we use has, not have. Use have only with I, you, we, and they.
