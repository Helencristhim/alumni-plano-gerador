Explaining a Process

Present simple & sequencing words

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

ToolUseExample
Present simple (we + verb)Describe a routine processWe inspect, we compare.
first / then / after that / finallyShow the order of stepsFirst... then... finally...
In simple words, ... / Basically, ...Explain to a non-expertBasically, we find a fair price.
It is used to / Its job is to...Explain a purposeA valuation is used to set a fair price.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"The team inspect, compare, estimate." (no order)

✓ RIGHT

"First we inspect, then we compare, finally we estimate."

✗ WRONG

"The market value mean the selling price." (he/she/it)

✓ RIGHT

"The market value means the selling price."

✗ WRONG

"We do a USPAP-compliant DCF." (too much jargon)

✓ RIGHT

"In simple words, we find a fair price for the asset."

Use sequencing words. Add -s after it/the value. For non-experts, say "in simple words" and avoid jargon.

Chapter 4

Explaining It Clearly

A conversation with a non-expert

Dialogue

So, What Do You Actually Do?

E
Marcos, I work in marketing, so I have no idea — what exactly does a valuation engineer do?
M
In simple words, we decide how much something is worth. We value buildings, land, and machines.
E
Interesting. And how do you find that value?
M
First, we do an inspection of the property. Then we compare it with similar sales to find the market value.
E
So it has to be very exact, right?
M
Exactly. The value must be accurate and reliable, because banks use it before a loan. We follow strict standards.
E
That makes sense now. And what is the final result?
M
Finally, we put the final figure into a report for the client. That report explains the value and how we found it.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. In simple words, what does Marcos say valuation is?
Deciding how much something is worth — buildings, land, and machines.
2. What are the first two steps of the process?
First an inspection of the property, then a comparison with similar sales to find the market value.
3. Why must the value be accurate and reliable?
Because banks use it before giving a loan, and they follow strict standards.
