A Day in QA -- Describing Your Routine

Describing your typical workday and what you are working on right now, to a remote teammate. Key words: routine, workflow, backlog, to prioritize, to assign, to track, sprint, to collaborate, ongoing, to handle, update, estimate. Structure: present simple (routine) vs present continuous (right now), and stative verbs.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Routine -- a fixed set of tasks you do regularly, in the same way
"My morning routine starts with the team stand-up."
Workflow -- the series of steps a task moves through from start to finish
"Every bug follows the same workflow, from report to fix."
Backlog -- the list of tasks and bugs that are waiting to be done
"I check the backlog first thing every morning."
To prioritize -- to put tasks in order of importance
"I prioritize the critical bugs before the small ones."
To assign -- to give a specific task to a person on the team
"The lead assigns each ticket to one tester."
To track -- to follow the progress of a task over time
"We track every open bug on the same board."
Sprint -- a short, fixed period in which the team finishes planned work
"Our team works in two-week sprints."
To collaborate -- to work together with other people toward the same goal
"I collaborate with developers in three countries."
Ongoing -- happening now and not yet finished
"The migration is an ongoing task this month."
To handle -- to deal with a task, problem, or request
"I handle the urgent bugs while my colleague tests new features."
Update -- a short report on the current progress of your work
"I give a quick update at every stand-up."
Estimate -- a rough calculation of how long a task will take
"I give the manager an estimate for each new test."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana works in quality assurance, and every day follows the same routine. Each morning, she checks the backlog and prioritizes the critical bugs. Her team works in two-week sprints, and they track the progress on one board. Right now, Ana is testing a new payment feature, and the developers are fixing two bugs from yesterday. This week, she is collaborating with a team in India. "I know the workflow very well," she says, "so I usually handle the urgent tasks and I give the lead an estimate for each new test." At the moment, she is preparing a short update for the daily stand-up.

1. Why does the text say "she checks the backlog" and not "she is checking the backlog"?
A It is a daily routine, so we use the present simple.
B It is happening only at this moment.
C It is a finished action in the past.
2. "Right now, Ana is testing a new payment feature" uses the present continuous because:
A It is something she does every day.
B It is happening at this moment, in progress.
C It is a fact that is always true.
3. Why does the text say "I know the workflow" and not "I am knowing the workflow"?
A Because it is happening right now.
B "Know" is a stative verb and does not take the -ing form.
C Because it is a finished action.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to switch between your routine and what is happening right now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines and facts, what you do regularly (every day, usually, always, often).I check the backlog every morning.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions happening now or around now, temporary (right now, at the moment, today, this week).Right now, I am testing the login feature.
He / she / it formAdd -s in the present simple; use is + -ing in the continuous.The lead assigns tickets. She is fixing a bug.
Stative verbsknow, understand, need, want stay simple: "I know the workflow," never "I am knowing".

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A teammate asks: "What do you do every day?" You answer:
A "I am checking the backlog every morning."
B "Every morning, I check the backlog and prioritize the bugs."
C "I check the backlog right now."
The lead asks: "What are you working on right now?" The best answer is:
A "I test the payment feature every day."
B "Right now, I am testing the payment feature."
C "I am test the payment feature now."
You want to say you understand the process. Which is correct?
A "I know the workflow very well."
B "I am knowing the workflow very well."
C "I am know the workflow very well."
A colleague needs to know your progress. You say:
A "Could you give me an update, please?"
B "Update me now the thing."
