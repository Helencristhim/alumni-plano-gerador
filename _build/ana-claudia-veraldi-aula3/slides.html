Chapter 3: The Code

Every Day vs Right Now

I check · I am checking

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Every morning, I check the backlog."

"Right now, I am testing the login feature."

"I usually prioritize the critical bugs first."

"This week, we are working on a new release."

Why green for what you do every day, but orange for what is happening right now?

FormUseSignal words
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines and facts — what you do regularlyevery day, usually, always, often, on Mondays
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions happening now or around now (temporary)right now, at the moment, today, this week

Stative verbs (know, understand, need, want) stay simple: "I know the workflow," never "I am knowing".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am checking the backlog every morning."
"I check the backlog every morning."
"Right now, I test the login feature."
"Right now, I am testing the login feature."
"I am knowing the workflow well."
"I know the workflow well."

A routine (every morning) always uses the present simple. Something happening right now uses am / is / are + -ing. Stative verbs like know never take -ing.

Chapter 4: Meet the Newcomer

Onboarding Tom

A new teammate asks about your day

Dialogue

Walk Me Through It

T
Hi Ana, I am Tom. I just joined the QA team. Can you walk me through a normal day here?
A
Sure, Tom. Every morning, we start with a stand-up. Then I check the backlog and I prioritize the bugs.
T
Nice. And what are you working on right now?
A
Right now, I am testing the new payment feature, and I am tracking two open bugs.
T
How do you organize everything? Do you work alone?
A
No, I collaborate with developers in three countries. We work in two-week sprints and we track the progress on one board.
T
That is clear. Thanks. So who assigns the tickets?
A
The lead assigns each ticket. If I am busy, I give an estimate and we plan together.
