Lesson 3 -- Life in a Town of 40,000

Podcast
Urban Exodus, E69 -- Academics leave city life for a farm in the Australian bush

The same episode from Lesson 1, worth a second listen now that you have the language for it. Louise and David spend the whole conversation comparing: cheaper, slower, further from everything, better for them. Listen again and you will hear the grammar you just learned doing real work.

Tip: write down every comparison you catch. Then say your own version of each one about your town.

YouTube
Sao Paulo: The Most Underrated City in the World

Worth returning to with this lesson in mind. The whole video is one long comparison: Sao Paulo against Rio, against other megacities, against what people expect. Notice the title itself - "the most underrated" is a superlative doing exactly what you practiced.

Tip: every time he compares two places, pause and say the same sentence about your town and your old neighborhood.

Series
The Repair Shop -- Season 1, Episode 1 (full episode)

Keep going with this one. It is set in exactly the kind of small place you chose: a barn, a village, people who know each other. Good, slow English, and the restorers constantly compare - this is stronger, that one is older, this is the hardest repair they have done.

Tip: listen for "the oldest", "the hardest", "better than". The craftspeople use superlatives naturally all the time.
