Chapter 3: Reporting Questions & Requests

Reporting What They Asked

asked whether / if · asked where... · told / asked me to + verb

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"Have you stayed here before?" → She asked whether I had stayed there before.

"Where is the lift?" → I asked where the lift was.

"Please send up some towels." → I asked housekeeping to send up some towels.

"Do not leave valuables in the room." → The receptionist told me not to leave valuables in the room.

Their exact wordsWhen you report it
Yes/No question ("Is it ready?")→ asked whether / if it was ready (statement order)
Wh- question ("Where is it?")→ asked where it was (no inversion, no do/did)
Request ("Please send towels.")→ asked / told them to send towels
Negative order ("Don't leave it.")→ told me not to leave it

Drop the question mark. Never keep do / does / did and never invert the subject and verb. The tense backshifts just like statements (is → was, have → had).

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"She asked where was my room."

✓ RIGHT

"She asked where my room was."

✗ WRONG

"He asked if did I have a reservation."

✓ RIGHT

"He asked whether I had a reservation."

✗ WRONG

"The manager said me to wait."

✓ RIGHT

"The manager told me to wait."

Chapter 4: At the Hotel

A Problem at the Front Desk

Pricila sorts out a booking discrepancy with the receptionist

Dialogue

Sorting Out the Booking

P
Good evening. I have a reservation under Adamo, but there seems to be a discrepancy -- I booked a double room, and the confirmation shows a single.
R
I am so sorry about that. Let me check. You are quite right. As a courtesy, I will upgrade you to a suite at no extra charge.
P
That is very kind, thank you. While I have you -- could you arrange a wake-up call for six, and is late check-out possible tomorrow?
R
Of course. I have set the wake-up call, and I can offer late check-out until one o'clock. Would you like me to ask the concierge about a taxi as well?
P
Yes, please -- a taxi to the airport for half past two. And one last thing: does the suite have room service in the evening?
R
It does, until midnight. Everything is arranged. If anything is not right, just call the front desk and we will accommodate you.

The Confirmation

Read a Real Hotel Confirmation

★

Hotel Belmonte · Booking Confirmation

Guest: P. Adamo · Suite 612 · 2 nights

"Dear Ms Adamo, thank you for your booking. As a courtesy, we have arranged a complimentary upgrade to a suite. Your wake-up call is set for 6:00, and late check-out is confirmed until 13:00. Room service is available until midnight, and housekeeping will service the room each morning. Valet parking is available at the entrance. Should you notice any discrepancy on your bill, please contact the front desk and we will be glad to accommodate you. The concierge is on hand for any further requests."

Report it: what does the confirmation say guests should do if they find a discrepancy on the bill?
It told us to contact the front desk, and said they would be glad to accommodate us.
What two courtesies has the hotel arranged before arrival?
A free upgrade to a suite, and a wake-up call with confirmed late check-out.

At the Front Desk

Scenario: You just left the desk. Call a friend and report everything: the questions the receptionist asked you and the requests you made. Use "she asked whether..." and "I asked them to...".

Tools:

She asked whether I had... I asked them to... He told me to...
