Stage 1.1: Words for the Hotel

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. These are rich B2 phrases for a smooth hotel stay.

An upgrade -- melhoria de quarto ou servico
"Because it was our anniversary, they gave us a free upgrade."
A suite -- suite / conjunto de quartos
"They upgraded us to a suite with a separate living room."
Courtesy -- cortesia / gentileza
"As a courtesy, the hotel offered us a free breakfast."
The concierge -- concierge (funcionario que organiza pedidos)
"The concierge booked us a table at the best restaurant in town."
Room service -- servico de quarto
"We were too tired to go out, so we ordered room service."
Housekeeping -- arrumacao / equipe de limpeza
"I asked housekeeping for extra towels and a spare blanket."
A wake-up call -- chamada do hotel para te acordar
"I asked the front desk for a wake-up call at six."
Late check-out -- saida tardia do quarto
"Our flight was in the evening, so we requested a late check-out."
A discrepancy -- divergencia entre valores
"There was a discrepancy on my bill, so I asked them to check it."
To escalate a complaint -- levar uma reclamacao ao gerente
"The clerk could not help, so I had to escalate the complaint."
A vacancy -- quarto livre e disponivel
"Luckily the hotel had a last-minute vacancy for the night."
Valet parking -- servico de manobrista
"There was no space outside, so we used the hotel's valet parking."
To accommodate -- acomodar / atender a um pedido
"The hotel was happy to accommodate our request for an early check-in."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read Pricila's hotel story and answer the questions.

"At the front desk, I asked whether they had any vacancies, and the receptionist told me that they did. I asked if we could have a late check-out, and she explained that housekeeping would need the room by two. She asked me what time I wanted my wake-up call, and then she told me to keep my key card away from my phone. When I noticed a discrepancy on the bill, the concierge asked me to wait a moment and told me not to worry -- as a courtesy, he removed the charge."

1. How do you report a yes/no question?
A With if/whether + statement order ("I asked whether they had a room").
B With do/did ("I asked did they have a room").
C With will ("I asked will they have a room").
2. How do you report a request or command?
A With that + past simple.
B With told/asked + person + to + verb ("She told me to wait").
C With whether + verb.
3. What did the concierge do about the discrepancy?
A He asked her to pay it twice.
B He asked her to wait and, as a courtesy, removed the charge.
C He refused to help.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Questions & Commands

Grammar

Pergunta sim/nao vira asked if/whether + sujeito + verbo (ordem de afirmacao, sem do/did). Pergunta com wh- vira asked what/where/when + sujeito + verbo. Pedido ou ordem vira asked/told + pessoa + to + verbo (negativa: not to). / A yes/no question becomes asked if/whether + subject + verb (statement order, no do/did). A wh- question becomes asked what/where/when + subject + verb. A request or order becomes asked/told + person + to + verb (negative: not to).
