Chapter 3: The Code

"If This Happens, I'll Do That"

The first conditional — not new, just made automatic for the gate.

Grammar Discovery

You Already Know This — Listen Again

If the flight is delayed, I 'll ask the gate agent for a voucher.

If the gate changes, I 'll check the board.

I 'll take any seat if they call standby.

If we are held up at customs, I 'll miss my connection.

Every sentence is a real, possible future. What tense is in the if part, and what comes after will?

PartFormExample
The conditionIf + present simpleIf the flight is delayed,
The resultwill / 'll + base verbI 'll ask for a voucher.
Either orderresult + if ... (no comma)I'll check the board if the gate changes.
The one ideaThe first conditional is for a real, likely future. Present simple after if, will in the result — never will after if.

One trap: keep the if part in the present — "If it is delayed", never "If it will be delayed".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the flight will be delayed, I'll ask for a voucher."
"If the flight is delayed, I'll ask for a voucher."
"If I will miss my connection, I'll call the airline."
"If I miss my connection, I'll call the airline."

One rule, only one: the if part stays in the present simple. The will lives in the result, never after if.

In Action

Plan It in a Chain

One result becomes the next condition — this is how you plan out loud.

"If the flight is delayed, I'll ask about my connection. If I miss it, I'll ask them to rebook me."
"If my bag doesn't fit, I'll ask the gate agent to check it. If it's checked, I'll pick it up at baggage claim."
"If the flight is full, I'll go on standby. If a seat opens up, I'll take any seat they give me."

Train the Ear

Listening 1

The Gate Change

A boarding announcement with a new gate number. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What changed, and what is the new gate number?
There was a gate change — the flight now departs from gate B22, not B14.
2. What should you do if you have a tight connection in New York?
Come to the desk now and let them know, so they can do their best to help.
3. Who boards first?
Passengers who need a little more time, and priority boarding members.
4. What should you have ready when your boarding group is called?
Your boarding pass and your passport.
Listening 2

The Final Boarding Call

Everything at once, at real speed — standby, bags, the jet bridge. Harder than it sounds.

0:000:00
1. Why should standby passengers come to the desk?
Two seats have just been released, and they will be called in order.
2. What happens if your bag doesn't fit in the overhead bin?
The gate agent will check it through to Paris for free, and you pick it up at baggage claim.
3. How will connecting passengers find their next gate?
It will be on the screen when they land in Paris — so there's no need to worry now.
4. How long until the doors close?
Ten minutes.
Listening Strategy

Catch the Gate

At the airport, a letter and a number decide everything — and fast speech hides them.

The flight now departs from gate B14. — B is "bee", four-TEEN
Boarding at gate B22 in fifteen minutes. — fif-TEEN, not FIF-ty
Now boarding group three, flight four fifty-seven. — the numbers come fast
The doors will close in thirty minutes. — THIR-ty, not thir-TEEN

The whole difference between -teen and -ty is where the stress lands. Learn to hear the stress, and the gate stops hiding.
