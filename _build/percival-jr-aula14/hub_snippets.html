Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key words for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Hold on -- to ask someone to wait a moment
"Hold on, please. I will check for you." (Aguarde / um momento)
Call back -- to call the person again later
"I will call back this afternoon." (Retornar a ligação)
Voicemail -- a spoken message you leave when no one answers
"Please leave a voicemail after the tone." (Mensagem de voz / caixa postal)
Message -- the information you leave for the person
"Can I take a message for Karen?" (Recado / mensagem)
Available -- free to talk now
"Karen is not available right now." (Disponível)
Reach -- to contact someone and talk to them
"I could not reach Karen this morning." (Contatar / conseguir falar com)
Connection -- how clear or strong the phone line is
"The connection was bad, so I had to hang up." (Conexão / sinal)
Hang up -- to end the call
"Do not hang up. I will transfer you now." (Desligar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Taking & Leaving a Message

GRAMMAR

Para recados no telefone você usa frases educadas: pedir a pessoa (Could I speak to...?), oferecer anotar (Can I take a message?) e passar a informação (Could you tell her that... + frase normal). / For phone messages you use polite phrases: ask for the person, offer to write it down, and pass on the information with a normal sentence.

Função / FunctionPatternExample
Pedir a pessoa / Ask for someoneCould I speak to + name?Could I speak to Karen, please?
Oferecer anotar / Offer to takeCan I take a message?She is busy. Can I take a message?
Oferecer ao caller / Offer to callerWould you like to leave a message?Would you like to leave a message?
Passar o recado / Pass it onCould you tell him/her that + sentence?Could you tell her that Percival called?

Key point / Ponto-chave: dizemos speak to alguém (não "speak with") e tell her that + frase normal (não "tell to her"). O verbo tell leva a pessoa direto: tell her, tell him. / We say speak to someone and tell her that + a normal sentence. Tell takes the person directly.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection
