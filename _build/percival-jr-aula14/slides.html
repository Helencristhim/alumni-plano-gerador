Chapter 3 -- Taking a Message

Take & Leave a Message

The polite language of phone messages

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Who offers to take it, who leaves it, and what to say?

"Can I take a message? She is not available."

"Would you like to leave a message?"

"Could you tell her that Percival called?"

"Could I speak to Karen, please?"

Grammar Rule

Take a Message & Pass It On

Use it forPatternExample
Ask for the personCould I speak to + name?Could I speak to Karen, please?
Offer to take a messageCan I take a message?She is busy. Can I take a message?
Offer to the callerWould you like to leave a message?Would you like to leave a message?
Pass on the messageCould you tell him/her that + sentence?Could you tell her that Percival called?

Key idea: use Could I speak to...? to ask for someone, Can I take a message? to offer to write it down, and Could you tell her that... + a normal sentence to pass it on (Could you tell her that Percival called).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could I speak with Karen? Could you tell to her that I called."
"Could I speak to Karen? Could you tell her that I called."

On the phone we say speak to someone, and tell her that (not "tell to her"). The verb tell takes the person directly: tell her, tell him.
