Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Claim — to say that something is true, even without proof
"He claims that he scored the goal." (afirmar)
Deny — to say that something is not true or did not happen
"The club denied the story." (negar)
Headline — the title of a news story, written in big letters
"The headline said the team had won." (manchete)
Rumour — a story people repeat that may not be true
"There is a rumour that the coach will leave." (boato)
Witness — a person who saw an event and can describe it
"A witness said the stadium was full." (testemunha)
Announce — to tell people something officially and publicly
"The city announced a new centre." (anunciar)
Reveal — to make known something that was secret before
"The captain revealed his plan." (revelar)
Broadcast — to send out a programme on TV or radio
"They broadcast the final on TV." (transmitir)
Spokesperson — a person who speaks officially for a group
"A spokesperson announced the news." (porta-voz)
According to — as said or reported by a person or a source
"According to the report, the team won." (de acordo com)
Gossip — talk about other people's private lives, often unkind
"Please do not gossip about the players." (fofoca)
Exaggerate — to make something sound bigger than it really is
"Do not exaggerate the crowd size." (exagerar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Reported Speech (say, tell, backshift)

GRAMMAR

Usado para relatar o que alguém disse. O tempo verbal volta um passo. / Used to report what someone said; the tense moves one step back.

Direct / DiretoReported / RelatadoChange / Mudança
"I am tired"He said he was tiredam/is → was
"We will win"She said they would winwill → would
"I can play"He said he could playcan → could
"He told me..."tell + person; say + no persontold me / said that

Key point / Ponto-chave: use tell com uma pessoa (tell me, tell us) e say sem pessoa (say that...). Nunca diga "say me". Pronomes mudam: I → he, we → they.
