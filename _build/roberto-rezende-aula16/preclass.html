Conference Calls with HQ -- Giving Updates

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Liaise -- to communicate and coordinate between two groups
"I liaise between the client and the Shanghai team." (Fazer a ponte / intermediar)
Dial in -- to join a conference call by phone or video
"Please dial in five minutes early." (Entrar / conectar-se na chamada)
Status -- the current state of a project or task
"HQ asked for the status of the order." (Situação / andamento)
Brief -- to give someone the key facts before a meeting or task
"Amy briefed me before the call." (Passar as informações-chave)
Mute -- to turn off your microphone so others cannot hear you
"Please mute when you are not speaking." (Silenciar o microfone)
Highlight -- to point out the most important part
"Let me highlight the main result." (Destacar / ressaltar)
Time zone -- a region of the world with the same clock time
"Shanghai is in a different time zone." (Fuso horário)
Latest -- the newest or most recent
"Here is the latest on the order." (As novidades mais recentes)
Behind schedule -- later than planned; running late
"That part is behind schedule." (Atrasado / atras do prazo)
On behalf of -- speaking or acting for someone or a group
"I speak on behalf of the sales team." (Em nome de)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Roberto dials in to a conference call with headquarters. The team in Shanghai is in a different time zone, so for them it is the afternoon. HQ asks for the status of the big order. Roberto highlights the latest: the order is on track, but the client wants a change, so that part is behind schedule. Then he reports what people said. He says: "The client told me they needed a change. They said they wanted it the following week." The team replies that the new part would be ready in time. Roberto closes the call on behalf of the team. Notice: to report what someone said, English uses said or told + a person, and the verb moves back: is → was, present → past, will → would, can → could.

1. Why is it the afternoon for the Shanghai team?

2. Which sentence is correct reported speech?
A "The client said me they need a change."
B "The client told me they needed a change."

3. What does Roberto do at the end of the call?
A He leaves without speaking.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech (said / told)

GRAMMAR

Use said e told para reportar o que alguém disse; o verbo move um passo para o passado. / Use said and told to report what someone said; the verb moves one step back into the past.

Direto / DirectReportado / Reported
"The order is on track."Amy said the order was on track.
"We need a change."The client told me they needed a change.
"It will be ready."HQ said it would be ready.
"We can wait a week."They told me they could wait a week.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use said (sem pessoa) ou told + uma pessoa (told me, told HQ). O verbo do que a pessoa disse anda um passo para trás: is/are → was/were, presente → passado, will → would, can → could. Diga "told me", nunca "said me".
