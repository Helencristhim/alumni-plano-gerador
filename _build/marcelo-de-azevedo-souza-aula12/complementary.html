Aula 12 -- A Taste of Place

Series
Salt Fat Acid Heat (Netflix, 2018) -- with Samin Nosrat

A warm, beautiful docuseries in which cook Samin Nosrat travels to Italy, Japan, Mexico and beyond to show how the world's best food is made. It is exactly tonight's lesson brought to life: dishes explained, ingredients that are locally sourced, methods passed down for generations. Connection to Lesson 12: listen for the passive voice everywhere -- how the cheese is aged, how the miso is fermented, how the bread is baked.

Tip: after one episode, describe one dish out loud in the passive -- "It is made from...", "It is left to ferment for...".

Podcast
Gastropod -- Food with a Side of Science and History -- any episode

Hosts Cynthia Graber and Nicola Twilley dig into the hidden history and surprising science of a single food each episode -- how cheese is aged, how chocolate is made, how coffee is grown and traded. It joins your two loves, food culture and curiosity about how the world works, and it is a feast of natural passive voice, spoken at a clear, engaging pace.

Tip: pick a food you know well, then note three passive sentences the hosts use about how it is made or grown.

Present and Past Passives: The Grammar Gameshow Episode 16 (BBC Learning English)

A lively quiz-show lesson on exactly tonight's grammar: the present and past passive, when to use it, and the difference between active and passive. It turns the rules from your Grammar Tip into clear, memorable examples -- perfect before you describe a dish out loud.

Tip: pause before each answer, say your own passive version first, then check. Note every "be + past participle" you hear.
