A Taste of Place -- Food, Culture and How Things Are Made

Reading and talking about food and how it is made, with the passive voice. Key words: a delicacy, a staple, a specialty, an acquired taste, hearty, savory, to simmer, to ferment, locally sourced, seasonal, to be renowned for, a food market. Focus: the passive voice (be + past participle: is made, was invented, has been eaten) to describe how food is made and served.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

A delicacy -- a rare or luxurious food that is highly valued (uma iguaria, uma especiaria rara)
"Reindeer heart is considered a delicacy here."
A staple -- a basic food that is eaten very regularly (um alimento básico do dia a dia)
"Rice and beans are a staple in Brazil."
A specialty -- a dish a place is especially famous for (uma especialidade da região)
"The brown cheese is a local specialty."
An acquired taste -- something you only enjoy after trying it a few times (um gosto que se adquire com o tempo)
"Strong blue cheese is an acquired taste."
Hearty -- filling, generous and satisfying, of food (substancioso, reforçado)
"We shared a hearty bowl of stew."
Savory -- salty or spicy rather than sweet (salgado, temperado — o oposto de doce)
"I usually prefer savory food to sweet."
To simmer -- to cook gently in liquid, just below boiling (cozinhar em fogo baixo, apurar)
"The soup is simmered for hours."
To ferment -- to change food or drink using bacteria or yeast (fermentar — pão, queijo, kimchi)
"The cabbage is fermented for several weeks."
Locally sourced -- produced or grown nearby (de produção local, da região)
"Everything on the menu is locally sourced."
Seasonal -- available only at a certain time of year (sazonal, da estação)
"The menu is seasonal and changes often."
To be renowned for -- to be famous for something (ser renomado/famoso por algo)
"The town is renowned for its bread."
A food market -- a place where fresh food is sold (um mercado de alimentos, uma feira)
"We wandered through the food market all morning."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

At a food market in Norway, Marcelo asked how the brown cheese was made. The seller explained: the whey is simmered slowly for hours until it caramelizes, then it is pressed into blocks. The milk is all locally sourced, and the cheese is eaten on bread almost every morning. It is an acquired taste, but the whole country is renowned for it. Notice the pattern: we put the food first, not the cook, using be + past participle (is simmered, is pressed, is eaten). We use the passive because the action matters more than who does it. The recipe has been passed down for two hundred years.

1. Por que o texto usa "is simmered" e "is eaten" em vez de dizer quem faz a ação?
A Porque a ação importa mais do que quem a faz (voz passiva).
B Porque ninguém sabe cozinhar o queijo.
C Porque "is simmered" está no futuro.
2. Which sentence is a correct passive?
A "The whey is simmer for hours."
B "The whey is simmered for hours."
C "The whey simmered for hours by the cook is."
3. How do you say the recipe has existed for 200 years, in the passive?
A "The recipe has passed down for 200 years."
B "The recipe has been passed down for 200 years."
C "The recipe is passing down for 200 years."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Como descrever ações quando importa mais O QUE do que QUEM (explicação em inglês e português).

TensePassive (be + particípio)Meaning
Presentis / are madeé feito / são feitos (agora, em geral)
Pastwas / were inventedfoi inventado / foram inventados
Present perfecthas / have been eatentem sido comido (há séculos)
+ agente (by)a dish loved by everyoneuse "by" só quando o agente importa

Atenção: a passiva SEMPRE precisa do verbo be + particípio passado. Nunca "This dish served" (falta o "is"); nunca "is age" (use "is aged"). Use a passiva para descrever processos, receitas e tradições, onde "quem" é óbvio ou não importa.

Quiz

A "It is usually eaten with rice."
