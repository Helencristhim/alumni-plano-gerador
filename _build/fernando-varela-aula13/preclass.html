Aula 13 -- Pre-class

Handling Questions (Q&A)

Handling the Q&A at the clinical research kick-off: clarify, explain and answer questions, and buy time politely. Key words: clarify, explain, repeat, mean, point, raise, wonder, sure, briefly, doubt. Structure: polite, indirect questions (Could you tell me...? Do you know if...? Would you mind explaining...?) and clarifying (Sorry, do you mean...? Let me repeat that.).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouca cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Clarify -- esclarecer (to make something clearer)
"Let me clarify that point."
Explain -- explicar (to give details so people understand)
"Could you explain the plan?"
Repeat -- repetir (to say something again)
"Let me repeat the question."
Mean -- querer dizer (to want to say something)
"Do you mean the first phase?"
Point -- ponto / questao (an idea or topic in a talk)
"That is a good point."
Raise -- levantar (to bring up a question or topic)
"She raised a good question."
Wonder -- gostaria de saber (to want to know politely)
"I wonder if you can help."
Sure -- certo / seguro (certain)
"I am not sure about the date."
Briefly -- brevemente (in a short way)
"Let me answer briefly."
Doubt -- duvida (a feeling of not being sure)
"If you have a doubt, ask me."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

At the kick-off, the team can raise questions about the study. Sarah asks, "Could you tell me when the first phase starts?" Fernando wants to be sure he understands: "Sorry, do you mean the clinical phase? Let me clarify that point." Then Sarah asks, "Do you know if the budget is ready?" Fernando is not sure of the date, so he says he can explain it briefly. He adds, "If you have a doubt, please ask me again." It is a calm and clear Q&A.

1. Por que dizemos "Could you tell me when the first phase starts?" e nao "...when starts the first phase?"
A Porque "phase" e plural.
B Na pergunta indireta mantemos a ordem normal: sujeito + verbo (the phase starts).
C Porque a acao e no passado.
2. Usamos "Do you know if the budget is ready?" porque:
A "if" pede inversao: "if is the budget".
B Depois de "if" vem sujeito + verbo: "the budget is", sem inversao.
C "if" so e usado em frases no futuro.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "Would you mind to explain the timeline?"
B "Would you mind explaining the timeline?"
C "Would you mind explain the timeline?"

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situacao no Q&A.

You want to ask politely when the study starts. You say:
A "When starts the study?"
B "Could you tell me when the study starts?"
C "Tell me the study."
You ask politely if the budget is ready. The correct sentence is:
A "Do you know if is the budget ready?"
B "Do you know if the budget is ready?"
C "Do you know the budget ready is?"
You did not understand a word. You check the meaning. You say:
A "Sorry, do you mean the first phase?"
B "Sorry, do you mean about the first phase?"
C "Sorry, you mean what?"
A doctor still looks confused. You offer help. You say:
A "Let me clarify that point worst."
B "Let me clarify that point briefly."
C "Let me clarify that brief."
