Chapter 3

The Polite Question Code

Could you tell me... · Do you know if... · Would you mind...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me when the study starts?"

"Do you know if the budget is ready?"

"Would you mind explaining the timeline?"

"Sorry, do you mean the first phase?"

StructureUseExample
Could you tell me + when/where + subject + verbpolite request for infowhen the study starts
Do you know if + subject + verbpolite yes/no questionif the budget is ready
Would you mind + verb-ingvery polite requestmind explaining
Sorry, do you mean...? / Let me repeat thatto clarify and check understanding
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Could you tell me when starts the study?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you tell me when the study starts?"

✗ WRONG

"Do you know if is the budget ready?"

✓ RIGHT

"Do you know if the budget is ready?"

✗ WRONG

"Would you mind to explain?"

✓ RIGHT

"Would you mind explaining?"

In a polite, indirect question, keep the normal order: subject + verb (the study starts). After "mind", use the verb + -ing (mind explaining).
