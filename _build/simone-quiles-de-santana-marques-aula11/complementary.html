Lesson 11 -- Contract Vocabulary

Series
Suits -- Season 1, Episode 1 "Pilot"

A legal drama set in a corporate law firm. The lawyers constantly discuss clauses, liability, breach and the obligations of each party. Listen for how they describe one specific part of a contract with defining clauses ("the clause that...", "the term that...").

Watch the first 30 minutes with English subtitles. Write down 5 legal nouns you hear and 3 sentences with that or which.

Podcast
Legal English Podcast -- "Reading Commercial Contracts"

Short, practical episodes that explain contract vocabulary for non-native lawyers: clause, liability, breach, warranty and indemnity. The hosts read real clauses aloud and explain how each part works, which is exactly what you do for contracts and M&A at work.

Note 3 legal nouns the hosts define, and write one defining clause for each using that or which.

YouTube / BBC
BBC Learning English -- "Relative clauses: that, which and who"

A short, clear lesson on defining relative clauses: when to use that or which for things, and that or who for people. Directly useful for describing the clauses and parties in the contracts you review.

After watching, write five sentences about a contract using a defining clause in each, then record yourself reading them with at least four legal words from today.
