Contract Vocabulary -- Legal Nouns & Defining Clauses

Key legal nouns for reading a contract and how to point to one specific part with defining clauses. New vocabulary: clause, party, obligation, liability, breach, term, warranty, indemnity. Grammar: defining clauses with that / which (for things) and that / who (for people and parties).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Clause -- a separate part or rule in a contract
"This is the clause that limits liability." (Cláusula)
Party -- one of the sides that signs a contract
"Each party must sign the contract." (Parte)
Obligation -- something a party must do by law
"Payment on time is a key obligation." (Obrigação)
Liability -- legal responsibility for damage or loss
"This clause limits our liability." (Responsabilidade civil)
Breach -- when a party breaks a rule of the contract
"A breach of this clause is serious." (Violação / quebra de contrato)
Term -- a single condition or defined word in a contract
"This term defines what a delay is." (Termo / condição)
Warranty -- a promise that something will work or be true
"The warranty which covers the software lasts one year." (Garantia)
Indemnity -- a promise to pay for loss or damage you cause
"The indemnity clause protects the other party." (Indenização)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Defining Clauses

GRAMMAR

Para dizer DE QUAL parte do contrato você está falando, use uma defining clause. Para COISAS (cláusula, garantia, termo) use that ou which; para PESSOAS ou PARTES use that ou who. O verbo vem logo depois: "the clause that LIMITS liability". Nunca use what e não coloque vírgula antes de that/which numa defining clause. / To say WHICH part of the contract you mean, use a defining clause. For THINGS (clause, warranty, term) use that or which; for PEOPLE or PARTIES use that or who. The verb comes right after: "the clause that limits...". Never use what, and use no comma before that/which in a defining clause.
