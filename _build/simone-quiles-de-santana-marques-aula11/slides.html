The Defining Code

Defining Clauses

We talk aboutWordExample
A thingthat / which"the clause that limits liability"
A thingwhich"the warranty which covers the software"
A person / partythat / who"the party that signs first"
A negative ideathat + is not"a breach that is not defined"

Key idea: a defining clause tells the listener WHICH one you mean. The verb comes right after that or which: "the clause that limits..." -- no comma before it.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"This is the clause what limits liability. ... The party which signs first keeps a copy."
"This is the clause that limits liability. ... The party that signs first keeps a copy."

For things use that or which; for people or parties use that or who. Never use what.

"We removed the term _____that was not clear." (a thing)

Clause 7 -- Liability

The liability of each party that arises from a breach of this contract is limited to the value of the contract. This is the clause that protects both parties from very large claims.

Clause 9 -- Warranty

Party B gives a warranty which covers the software for twelve months. A breach of this warranty is an obligation that Party B must repair at no cost.

Each term that is defined in Clause 1 has the same meaning in every other clause of this contract.

Dialogue

The Contract Review

S

"Thank you for joining. I would like to start with the clause that limits our liability."

P

"Of course. That is the clause which sets the limit for each party. What is your concern?"

S

"The limit is too high for us. A breach that is small should not create a large obligation."

P

"I understand. We can add a term that links the limit to the type of breach. That is fair."

S

"Thank you. And the warranty which covers the software -- could we extend it to twelve months?"

P

"Agreed. I will send a new draft that includes the indemnity and the longer warranty by Friday."
