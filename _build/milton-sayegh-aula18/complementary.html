Lesson 18 -- Pitching for Investment

Series / Film
Shark Tank (US) -- any pitch episode

Watch founders pitch real investors: how they lead with their edge, present traction and numbers, handle sharp questions, and state a stake and a valuation. A live model of everything in this lesson.

Tip: each time a founder is challenged, pause and try to give a calm, one-line answer yourself before they do.

Podcast
The Twenty Minute VC -- any founder fundraising episode

Investors and founders talk through how real raises happen: what makes an investor lean in, how a stake and a valuation are set, and what counts as genuine traction. The vocabulary of this lesson, used by people who do it daily.

Tip: note one phrase a founder uses to frame their edge, then rewrite it as an emphasis sentence ("What sets us apart is...").

YouTube
Y Combinator -- "How to Pitch Your Startup" / "How to Talk to Investors"

A clear, practical talk on structuring an investor pitch: the story, the proof, and the ask. Native speakers model how to be direct, handle hard questions, and frame the return for a backer.

Tip: after watching, record your own one-minute pitch using stake, valuation, traction and one emphasis sentence.
