Video
"The Crisis of Credit Visualized" -- Jonathan Jarvis

A short, clear animation that shows how one shock spreads through the market -- liquidity dries up, exposures grow, and a downturn arrives. Connection to Lesson 4: it is the real story behind every stress test, so watch it as a "what happens if" scenario and notice the chain of triggers.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. After each step, pause and say one first-conditional sentence: "If liquidity falls, the banks will..." Write down three triggers you see.

Podcast
"The Indicator from Planet Money" -- NPR

Short, ten-minute episodes that take one number or one risk and explain what could happen next. Connection to Lesson 4: each episode is a small scenario, so it is perfect practice for thinking in "if this, then that" -- the language of the first conditional.

Tip: pick one recent episode, listen once for the main idea, then say two first-conditional sentences about it: "If X happens, the market will..." and "If it does not, we will..."

Read
The Guardian -- Business

Free, well-written business news in clear English -- close to the Financial Times and The Economist you already read. Connection to Lesson 4: reading is your strength, so use it to feed your speaking. Analysis pieces are full of scenarios: what will happen if rates rise, if a bank fails, if a market shock hits.

Tip: read one analysis article, then close it and talk through the scenario out loud in the first conditional, using today's words (shock, trigger, liquidity, downturn, contingency).
