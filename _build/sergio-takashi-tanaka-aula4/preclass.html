Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

The Stress Test -- What Happens If

Reading a stress-test memo and talking through risk scenarios. Key words: scenario, stress test, trigger, contingency, shock, downturn, resilient, worst-case, to unwind, liquidity. Structure: the first conditional (if + present, will + verb) to say what the desk will do if the market turns.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Scenario -- a possible future situation the team plans for
"The stress test looks at a worst-case scenario."
Stress test -- a test of how a portfolio behaves under extreme pressure
"We run a stress test before every committee."
Trigger -- an event that starts an action or a consequence
"If liquidity falls below the trigger, we act."
Contingency -- a plan for a possible future problem
"We move to the contingency plan in the worst case."
Shock -- a sudden, severe change in the market
"A market shock can hit without warning."
Downturn -- a period when the market or economy gets worse
"A deep downturn is the worst-case scenario."
Resilient -- able to recover quickly from a shock
"The portfolio is resilient in the base case."
Worst-case -- the most severe possible outcome
"In the worst-case, we unwind step by step."
To unwind -- to close or reduce a position, step by step
"If liquidity falls, we unwind the weakest positions."
Liquidity -- how easily an asset can be turned into cash
"Liquidity falls fast in a deep shock."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

The stress-test memo describes a plan. In the base case, the portfolio is resilient. But if a sudden shock hits the market, volatility will rise and liquidity will fall. If liquidity falls below the trigger, the desk will unwind the weakest positions first. If a partner bank shows stress, the team will cut the exposure and add a hedge. In the worst case, they will escalate to the committee. The finding is simple: if the team acts early, it will protect the desk.

1. Why do we say "if a shock hits" and not "if a shock will hit"?
A After "if" we use the present simple; "will" goes in the result.
B After "if" we always use "will" for the future.
C "If" is always followed by the past simple.
2. Which sentence uses the first conditional correctly?
A "If liquidity falls, we will unwind the positions."
B "If liquidity will fall, we unwind the positions."
C "If liquidity falls, we would unwind the positions."
3. What does the first conditional describe here?
A A real future possibility and what the desk will do about it.
B Something that already happened last quarter.
C A routine the team does every single day.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- First Conditional

GRAMMAR

How to say what will happen if a real future situation arrives.

PartFormExample
The condition
(if-clause)		if + present simple (never "will" here)If liquidity falls...
The resultwill + base verb...we will unwind the positions.
Word orderIf-clause first → add a comma. Result first → no comma: "We will act if the market falls."
UseA real, likely future situation and its result — the language of risk scenarios and contingency plans.
