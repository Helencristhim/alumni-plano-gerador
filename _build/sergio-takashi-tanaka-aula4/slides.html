Chapter 3: The Code

The First Conditional

if it happens · we will act

Grammar Discovery

Read and Notice

"If a shock hits the market, volatility will rise."

"If liquidity falls, we will unwind the weakest positions."

"If a partner bank shows stress, we will cut the exposure."

"If we act early, we will protect the desk."

What do the orange verbs have in common?

PartFormExample
The condition
(if-clause)		if + present simpleIf liquidity falls...
The resultwill + base verb...we will unwind the positions.
OrderIf-clause first → use a comma. Result first → no comma: "We will act if the market falls."
MeaningA real future possibility and its result — not certain, but likely.

After if, use the present — never will. The will goes in the result.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If the market will fall, we will act."
"If the market falls, we will act."
"If a shock hits, we cut the exposure."
"If a shock hits, we will cut the exposure."
"If liquidity falls, we would unwind."
"If liquidity falls, we will unwind."

Use the first conditional for a real future plan: after if use the present (never will), and put will in the result (not would).

Detail

Where Does IF Go?

if-clause first (comma) or result first (no comma)

"We will add a hedge ___if a partner bank shows stress." (result first)
"If the market ___is resilient, we will hold the position." (be)
"If we ___unwind the positions early, the loss will stay small." (unwind)
"If a deep downturn ___arrives, we will escalate to the committee." (arrive)
