Chapter 3 -- The Code

A Good Idea or a Necessity

should & ought to (advice) -- must & have to (necessity) -- can't (forbidden)

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence tells someone what to do. Notice the orange words before the verb.

"You should rest before the trip."

"You ought to call her today."

"You have to meet the deadline on Friday."

"You can't use your phone in class."

The Rule

Should, Must, Have to, Can't

ModalMeaningExample
should / ought toadvice (a good idea)"You should rest."
must / have tonecessity (no choice)"You have to pay first."
can'tforbidden (not allowed)"You can't park here."

Tip: all of these are followed by the base verb (you should go, you must pay). Only ought to keeps the word "to". For others, the "to" belongs to have to only.

Common Mistake

No "to" After Should

"You should to rest before the trip."
"You should rest before the trip."

After should, must and can't, use the base verb with no "to". Only ought to and have to keep the word "to".
