Vocabulary

Always -- 100% of the time, every time

Usually -- most of the time, but not always
"I usually eat fruit for breakfast." (geralmente)
Sometimes -- not often, only some days
"Sometimes I cook fish." (às vezes)
Every day -- on all the days, each day
"I work every day." (todo dia)
Weekend -- Saturday and Sunday
"I see my family on the weekend." (fim de semana)
Together -- with another person, not alone
"We are happy together." (juntos)
Friend -- a person you like and know well
"I meet my friend in the city." (amigo)
Busy -- with a lot of things to do
"Sometimes I am very busy." (ocupado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Hi! My name is Mark. This is my typical day. I always wake up at seven. I usually eat bread and coffee for breakfast. Then I go to work. I work every day, from nine to five. Sometimes I am very busy. In the evening, I cook fish for dinner. On the weekend, I see my family. We are happy together. Notice: the frequency word comes before the verb -- I always wake up, not "I wake up always".

1. When does Mark wake up?
A At nine.
B At seven.
C At five.
2. How often does Mark work?
A Every day.
B Only on the weekend.
C Sometimes.
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I eat always bread."
B "I always eat bread."
C "Always I eat bread."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- frequency before the verb

GRAMMAR

A palavra de frequência vem ANTES do verbo principal. / The frequency word comes BEFORE the main verb.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
100% / sempreI + always + verbI always wake up early.
80% / geralmenteI + usually + verbI usually eat fruit.
30% / às vezesSometimes + I + verbSometimes I cook fish.
Wrong / erradoverb + frequencyI wake up always
Key point / Ponto-chaveA frequência vem ANTES do verbo: "I always work", não "I work always". Words: always, usually, sometimes, never.
