Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Advice -- a helpful idea about what someone should do
"Can I give you some advice?" (conselho)
Recommend -- to say that something is good and worth choosing
"I recommend this book to everyone." (recomendar)
Suggest -- to offer an idea that someone can accept or refuse
"I suggest we leave a little earlier." (sugerir)
Allowed -- permitted by a rule; you can do it
"You are allowed to bring one guest." (permitido)
Rule -- an instruction that says what you must or must not do
"The first rule is to arrive on time." (regra)
Ought to -- a slightly more formal way to say "should"
"You ought to call her and apologize." (deveria)
Avoid -- to stay away from something or stop it from happening
"Try to avoid sugar late at night." (evitar)
Deadline -- the latest time by which something must be done
"The deadline for the report is Friday." (prazo final)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modals of Advice & Necessity

GRAMMAR

Para dar conselho (uma boa ideia), use should ou ought to. Para necessidade (sem escolha), use must ou have to. Para algo proibido, use can't. Todos vêm seguidos do verbo na forma base (you should go); só o ought to e o have to mantêm a palavra "to". / To give advice (a good idea), use should or ought to. For necessity (no choice), use must or have to. For something forbidden, use can't. All are followed by the base verb; only ought to and have to keep the word "to".

ModalMeaning / SignificadoExample
should / ought toadvice (uma boa ideia)"You should rest." / "You ought to rest."
must / have tonecessity (sem escolha)"You have to pay first."
can'tforbidden (proibido)"You can't park here."
