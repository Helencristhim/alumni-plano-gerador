Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Twelve words founders use to write a proposal. Listen and say each one. / Doze palavras que fundadores usam para escrever uma proposta. Ouça e diga cada uma.

Executive summary -- the short opening that summarizes the proposal / resumo executivo, a abertura
"An investor reads the executive summary first and decides in seconds."
Value proposition -- the reason a customer chooses you / proposta de valor
"Our value proposition is one line: we lower the cost per cycle."
Deliverable -- a concrete result you promise to hand over / entrega, resultado concreto
"Each deliverable is listed with a rationale beside it."
Rationale -- the logical reason behind a decision / justificativa, lógica
"She lists each promise with a clear rationale."
Projection -- an estimate of a future financial number / projeção financeira
"The projection shows steady growth over three years."
Feasibility -- whether a plan can realistically be done / viabilidade prática
"Test the feasibility of every promise before writing it down."
Contingency -- a backup plan for when things go wrong / plano de contingência
"I add a contingency for the months when cash runs thin."
Cost-effective -- giving good value for the money / com bom custo-benefício
"A cost-effective plan avoids overspending on things patients never see."
Streamline -- to make a process simpler and more efficient / simplificar, enxugar
"I streamline the document, cutting anything an investor does not need."
Break-even -- the point where income equals costs / ponto de equilíbrio
"The plan reaches break-even only in month eighteen."
Viable -- able to work and succeed in practice / viável
"A good plan proves the idea is viable, not just exciting."
Forecast -- a prediction of how a number will behave / previsão
"The forecast assumes a slow first year."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane has decided to write the proposal for her clinic. Her advisor recommends opening with a one-page executive summary, because investors avoid reading long documents. She plans to state one clear value proposition, and it is worth spending real time on the numbers. Notice the two patterns: after decide and plan we use the infinitive (to write, to state); after recommend, avoid, and after worth, we use the gerund (opening, reading, spending). And before signing anything, she tests the feasibility of every promise -- because any verb after a preposition takes the gerund.

1. After "recommend", which form is correct?
A recommend opening -- verb + gerund
B recommend to open -- verb + infinitive
C recommend open -- base verb
2. After "decide" and "plan", which form do we use?
A the gerund (deciding writing)
B the infinitive (decide to write, plan to state)
C the base verb (decide write)
3. Why is it "before signing", not "before to sign"?
A Because "before" always takes the infinitive.
B Because any verb after a preposition takes the gerund.
C Because "sign" is an irregular verb.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Gerunds and Infinitives

GRAMMAR

When one verb follows another, the first verb decides the form of the second: some take the gerund (-ing), some take the infinitive (to + verb). Any verb after a preposition takes the gerund. / Quando um verbo segue outro, o primeiro decide a forma do segundo: alguns pedem o gerund (-ing), outros o infinitive (to + verbo). Todo verbo depois de preposição fica no gerund.

Pattern / PadrãoVerbs / VerbosExample / Exemplo
Verb + gerund (-ing)recommend, suggest, avoid, considerWe recommend investing.
Verb + infinitive (to)decide, aim, plan, agree, offerWe decided to fund it.
worth + gerundit is worthIt is worth adding a plan.
Preposition + gerundbefore, after, withoutBefore writing, test it.
