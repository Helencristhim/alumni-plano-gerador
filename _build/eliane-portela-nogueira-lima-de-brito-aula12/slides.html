Chapter 4: Gerunds & Infinitives

-ing or to?

recommend doing · decide to do

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We recommend investing in one strong niche."

"The board decided to fund the pilot."

"It is worth adding a contingency plan."

"We aim to reach break-even in year two."

"Before writing the plan, test every promise."

PatternCommon verbsExample
Verb + gerund (-ing)recommend, suggest, avoid, consider, involveWe recommend investing.
Verb + infinitive (to)decide, aim, plan, agree, offer, manageWe decided to fund it.
worth / no use + gerundit is worth, there is no useIt is worth adding a plan.
Preposition + gerundbefore, after, without, interested inBefore writing, test it.

Some verbs take the -ing form, others take to + verb. Any verb after a preposition takes -ing. Learn the pattern with the verb.

Practice

Choose the Form

Say it out loud, then tap to check

"We recommend ___opening with a tight executive summary." (recommend + gerund)
"The board decided ___to fund the pilot for a year." (decide + infinitive)
"It is worth ___adding a contingency for the tight months." (worth + gerund)
"We plan ___to reach break-even in month eighteen." (plan + infinitive)
"Before ___writing a promise, we test its feasibility." (preposition + gerund)
Language Focus

Gerund or Infinitive?

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

We recommend to invest in one niche.
We recommend investing in one niche.
The board decided funding the pilot.
The board decided to fund the pilot.
It is worth to add a contingency.
It is worth adding a contingency.

Three patterns in one slide: recommend + -ing, decide + to, and worth + -ing. Learn the form together with the verb.

Dialogue

The Advisor's Call

M
Eliane, I read your draft. Before sending it, I recommend cutting the first three pages.
E
I agree, Marcus. I decided to open with a one-page executive summary instead.
M
Good. And the value proposition? Investors want to see one clear reason to choose you.
E
It is one line. We offer a technology that lowers the cost per cycle, so patients pay less.
M
That is worth stating first. Now, the numbers. When do you plan to reach break-even?
E
In month eighteen. The forecast assumes a slow first year, so I avoid overpromising.
M
Smart. Did you remember to add a contingency for the tight months?
E
Yes. And before writing each deliverable, I tested its feasibility with the team.
M
Then you are ready. The plan that wins is the one that is clear enough to trust.
