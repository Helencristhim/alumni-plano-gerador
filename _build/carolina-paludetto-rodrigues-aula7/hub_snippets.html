Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Last weekend Carolina borrowed a novel from the school library. The author is a famous writer from Brazil. On Saturday she started the first chapter and she loved it. She finished the whole book in two days because she really enjoyed the adventure. After she closed the book, she recommended it to her best friend and added a short review on the library website.

1. Where did Carolina borrow the novel?
A From the school library.
B From a bookshop in the city.
C From her best friend.
2. Why do we write "she finished" and not "she finish"?
A Because it is happening now.
B Because it is a finished action in the past (Past Simple, -ed).
C Because finish is a noun.
3. What did Carolina do at the end?
A She recommended the book and added a review.
B She returned the book without reading it.
C She started a new chapter.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple (Regular Verbs)

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de ações terminadas no passado. / Used to talk about finished actions in the past.

Rule / RegraBase verbPast Simple
Most verbs: add -ed / Maioria: +-edfinish, borrowfinished, borrowed
Ends in -e: add -d / Termina em -e: +-dlike, closeliked, closed
Consonant + y: -ied / Consoante + y: -iedstudy, carrystudied, carried
Signal words / Sinaisyesterday, last week, two days agoI finished it yesterday.

Key point / Ponto-chave: com "yesterday, last week, two days ago", a ação terminou no passado, então adicione -ed ao verbo. O -ed tem 3 sons: /t/ (finished), /d/ (enjoyed), /id/ (recommended).
