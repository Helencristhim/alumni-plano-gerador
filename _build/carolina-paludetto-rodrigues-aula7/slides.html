Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The Past Simple of regular verbs

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the blue words have in common? When do these actions happen?

"Last weekend I finished a great novel."

"She borrowed three books from the library."

"We recommended the novel to our friends."

"Yesterday I really enjoyed the adventure."

Grammar Rule

Add -ed

Spelling ruleBase verbPast Simple
Most verbs: add -edfinish, borrowfinished, borrowed
Verb ends in -e: add -dlike, closeliked, closed
Consonant + y: -iedstudy, carrystudied, carried
Short verb: double consonantstop, planstopped, planned

Key idea: use the Past Simple for finished actions in the past. Signal words: yesterday, last week, two days ago, in 2020. The -ed ending has three sounds: /t/ in finished, /d/ in enjoyed, and /id/ in recommended.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Yesterday I finish the novel."
"Yesterday I finished the novel."

Words like yesterday, last week and two days ago show a finished past action, so we add -ed to the verb. Do not leave the verb in the base form.
