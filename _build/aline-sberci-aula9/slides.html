Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Linking words and a polite tone

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which word connects the two ideas?

"I am writing because I need the report."

"The deadline is today, so please reply soon."

"I sent the draft, but I have one question."

"Please send the file. Also, confirm the date."

Grammar Rule

Linking Words

Linking wordUseExample
becausegive a reasonI am writing because I need help.
soshow a resultIt is urgent, so please reply soon.
but / howevershow a contrastI sent it, but I have a question.
and / alsoadd informationPlease reply, and also send the file.

Key idea: linking words connect two ideas in one clear sentence. In a formal email, keep a polite tone: use I would like to instead of I wanna, and Could you please instead of a direct order.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Hey Tom, send me the report now."
"Hi Tom, could you please send me the report?"

In a work email, a direct order can sound rude. Use a polite greeting and Could you please to make a request, not a command.

Artefact

Aline's Work Email

From: Aline Sberci · To: Mr. Costa (Project Manager)

Subject: Monthly Project Report

Dear Mr. Costa,

I am writing because the monthly report is ready. The attachment has all the numbers for this month.

The client needs it before Friday, so could you please review it today? I have one small request: please confirm the final date.

Thank you for your help. I will reply quickly to any question.

Best regards,
Aline Sberci
