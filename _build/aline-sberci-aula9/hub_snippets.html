Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Recipient -- the person you send the email to
"The recipient is my manager." (Destinatário)
Subject -- the short line that says what the email is about
"The subject is Monthly Report." (Assunto)
Greeting -- the polite opening, like Dear or Hi
"I start with a friendly greeting." (Saudação)
Attachment -- a file you send with your email
"The attachment is the report." (Anexo)
Reply -- to answer an email you received
"I will reply before lunch." (Responder)
Forward -- to send a received email to another person
"Please forward it to your team." (Encaminhar)
Request -- a polite way to ask for something
"I have a small request for you." (Pedido)
Regards -- a polite word to close an email
"I end with Best regards." (Atenciosamente)
Polite -- kind and respectful in the way you write
"A work email should be polite." (Educado)
Draft -- a first version you write before you send
"I read my draft before I send it." (Rascunho)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Aline writes a work email to the project manager. She chooses a clear subject so the recipient knows the topic. She starts with a polite greeting, because first impressions matter. She adds the attachment with the report and writes a short request. The client needs it today, so she asks for a quick answer. She reads her draft one more time, but finds no mistakes. Finally, she closes with Best regards.

1. Why does Aline choose a clear subject?
A So the recipient knows the topic.
B Because she has no attachment.
C So she can forward it.
2. Why does she start with a polite greeting?
A Because it is the end.
B Because first impressions matter.
C So she can delete the draft.
3. What does she do before she closes the email?
A She reads her draft to check for mistakes.
B She removes the greeting.
C She forgets the attachment.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Linking Words

GRAMMAR

Palavras de ligação conectam ideias num email claro. / Linking words connect ideas in a clear email.

Word / PalavraUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
becauseRazão (give a reason)I am writing because I need help
soResultado (show a result)It is urgent, so please reply
but / howeverContraste (contrast)I sent it, but I have a question
and / alsoAdicionar (add information)Please reply, and send the file

Key point / Ponto-chave: num email de trabalho, use um tom educado: Could you please em vez de uma ordem direta, e I would like to em vez de I wanna.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation.

You want to ask your manager for a file in a polite way. Which is best?
A "Send me the file now."
B "Could you please send me the file?"
C "I want the file."
You want to give a reason for your email. Which word do you use?
A "I am writing because I need an update."
B "I am writing so I need an update."
C "I am writing but I need an update."
You want to close a work email politely. Which is best?
A "Thank you for your help. Best regards, Aline."
B "Bye, Aline."
C "See ya."
