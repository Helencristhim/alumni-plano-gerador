Lesson 17 -- The Elevator Pitch, 90 Seconds That Count

Series
Shark Tank -- any short pitch scene on YouTube

Watch one founder give a short pitch. Notice the four parts: a hook, what they do, the value, and the ask. Listen for "to" and "so that" when they say WHY their idea matters.

Write down the hook and the ask you hear. Then say your own version out loud.

Podcast
Business English Pod -- Episode: "Your Elevator Pitch"

How to build a short, compelling pitch: a strong hook, a clear value, and a confident ask. Review of today's words: hook, value, solution, credible.

Record a ninety-second pitch about your work and time it. Keep only the lines that say WHY it matters.

YouTube
"How to Give a Great Elevator Pitch in English"

A clear guide to the four parts of a pitch and how to say the value with "so that". Learn to spark interest fast and end with a clear ask that keeps the momentum.

After watching, build a pitch card for one project at Oracle: hook, what you do, value, ask. Practice it until it feels natural.
