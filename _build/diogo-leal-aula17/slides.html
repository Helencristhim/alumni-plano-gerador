The Two-Minute Warm-Up

Call with Chicago -- 3:00 PM

Two Minutes. Four Moves.

Owner: Diogo Leal

Open: How is your week going? / Did you have a good weekend?

AReact: "Oh really?" / "That sounds great." / "I can imagine."
BAdd one thing about you -- or it becomes an interview
CSafe: weather, weekend, travel, sport, food. Dangerous: politics, salary, religion, age
✓Bridge: "Anyway, we have a full agenda -- shall we start?"

Two minutes, and you close it yourself. Small talk that has no end eats the meeting.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one opens? Which one keeps it alive? Which one ends it?

"How is your week going so far?"

"It is the end of the quarter for you too, isn't it?"

"By the way, did you have a good weekend?"

"Anyway, shall we start? We have a full agenda."

Grammar Rule

Open, React, Bridge

MoveStructureExample
OpenHow is + your week / your day + going? (word order!)How is your week going so far?
Keep it aliveReact (Oh really? / I can imagine.) + a question tag: isn't it? / doesn't it?Busy quarter for you too, isn't it?
Bridge to businessBy the way, ... (add) / Anyway, shall we start?Anyway, shall we start?

Small talk is not about the weather. It is a test: can this person have a normal human conversation? Answer the question, then add something about yourself -- an answer with no addition kills the conversation, and makes it feel like an interrogation.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"How is going your week?"
"How is your week going?"
"It is raining in Chicago, no?"
"It is raining in Chicago, isn't it?"

A ordem é How is your week going? (sujeito antes do verbo -ing). E o "né?" do português vira question tag: is + not → isn't it?, does + not → doesn't it?

Chapter 5 -- Talking It Through

The Warm-Up

Dialogue

She Answers in Three Words

D

"Hi Sarah, good to see you. How is your week going so far?"

S

"Busy. Same as always."

D

"I can imagine -- it is the end of the quarter for you too, isn't it? Mine started badly: my flight was three hours late."

S

"Three hours? That is painful. Where were you flying from?"

D

"Sao Paulo. Four hours at the gate, and I read a whole book. By the way, did you have a good weekend?"

S

"I did, actually. I played tennis on Saturday. First time in two years."

D

"Oh really? I did not know you played. My daughter started last year, and now she beats me -- which is not good for my ego."

S

"That happens fast. Wait until she is fifteen."

D

"I am not ready for that. Anyway, we have a full agenda today -- shall we start?"

Dialogue Review

About Sarah

1. How does Sarah answer the first question?
In three words: "Busy. Same as always."
2. What makes her open up?
Diogo adds something about himself -- the three-hour flight delay.
3. How does Diogo end the small talk?
He says "anyway", mentions the full agenda, and asks if they can start.
