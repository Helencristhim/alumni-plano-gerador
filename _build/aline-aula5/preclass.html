Aula 5 -- Pre-class

Have You Ever...? -- Travel Stories

Histórias de viagem com o present perfect: abroad, adventure, sightseeing, culture, foreign, memorable, destination, landmark. Estruturas: "Have you ever...?", "Yes, I have...", "I have never...", e o follow-up no past simple ("When did you go?").

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Abroad -- in or to a country that is not yours
"Have you ever worked abroad?" (No exterior)
Adventure -- an exciting, sometimes risky experience
"The boat trip was a real adventure." (Aventura)
Sightseeing -- visiting the famous places of a city
"We went sightseeing in Buenos Aires." (Passeio turístico)
Culture -- the art, food, and way of life of a place
"I love learning about new cultures." (Cultura)
Foreign -- from another country, not local
"It was my first foreign trip." (Estrangeiro)
Memorable -- special enough to remember for years
"It was the most memorable trip of my life." (Memorável)
Destination -- the place you are traveling to
"Barcelona is a popular destination." (Destino)
Landmark -- a famous building or place tourists visit
"The Sagrada Família is a famous landmark." (Ponto turístico)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At lunch, Daniel tells Aline he is going to Barcelona — his first foreign trip. "Have you ever been to Spain?" he asks. "No, I have never been to Spain," Aline answers, "but I have visited Portugal. We went sightseeing in Lisbon." Daniel is nervous: he has never traveled abroad. Aline smiles: "My trip to Buenos Aires was a real adventure — the most memorable trip of my life. When did I go? In 2021!"

1. Como Daniel pergunta sobre a experiência de vida? / How does Daniel ask about a life experience?
A "Did you ever been to Spain?"
B "Have you ever been to Spain?"
C "Are you ever in Spain?"
2. O que a Aline NUNCA fez? / What has Aline never done?
A She has never visited Portugal.
B She has never been to Spain.
C She has never traveled alone.
3. Para falar do ANO da viagem, qual tempo verbal ela usa? / For the YEAR of the trip, which tense does she use?
A Past simple: "When did I go? In 2021."
B Present perfect: "I have gone in 2021."
C Going to: "I am going to go in 2021."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect (ever/never)

GRAMMAR

have/has + particípio. A experiência importa, não a data. / The experience matters, not the date.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Have you ever + particípio?Perguntar sobre a vida toda / Ask about the whole lifeHave you ever been abroad?
I have + particípioSim, em algum momento / Yes, at some pointI have visited Portugal.
I have never + particípioNunca na vida / Not once in your lifeI have never tried tapas.
When did you...? (past simple)A data específica / The specific timeWhen did you go? In 2022.
