Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The passive voice: was / were + past participle

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"The sample was analyzed in the laboratory."

"The data were obtained from two hundred patients."

"The procedure was repeated every month."

"The protocol was approved by the committee."

Grammar Rule

The Passive Voice

FunctionExample
Singular subjectThe sample was analyzed.
Plural subjectThe samples were analyzed.
NegativeThe data were not changed.
QuestionWas the protocol approved?
Say who (optional)It was reviewed by a statistician.

Key idea: the passive is was or were + the past participle (analyzed, obtained, collected). It puts the focus on the action and the method, not on the person. In a methods section, the agent is usually left out.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The sample analyzed in the laboratory."
"The sample was analyzed in the laboratory."

The passive always needs was or were plus the past participle, never the bare verb.

Grammar Practice

Build the Passive

"The study _____was conducted over six months." (conduct, singular)
"The blood samples _____were collected by trained nurses." (collect, plural)
"Each participant _____was measured at the start." (measure, singular)
"The results _____were obtained within two days." (obtain, plural)
