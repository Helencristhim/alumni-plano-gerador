Stage 1.1: Product Demo Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Twelve phrases to show the platform and its value. Listen to each one.

Walkthrough -- a step-by-step guided tour of how something works
"Let me give you a quick walkthrough of the dashboard." (Demonstração passo a passo)
Feature -- a specific function or capability the product offers
"This feature scores every lead in real time." (Funcionalidade / recurso)
Use case -- a real situation that shows why the product is useful
"Let me start with a use case your team will recognize." (Caso de uso)
Under the hood -- the internal part the user does not see, but that does the work
"Under the hood, the engine updates prices every night." (Nos bastidores / por baixo dos panos)
Seamless -- smooth and without any interruption or friction
"The handover between teams is completely seamless." (Sem emendas / fluido)
Robust -- strong and reliable, even under heavy use
"The system is robust enough for peak season traffic." (Robusto / resistente)
Roll out -- to release a feature or product to users, step by step
"We will roll out the new module next month." (Lançar / implantar)
Integrate -- to connect two systems so they work together as one
"The platform integrates with your CRM in minutes." (Integrar)
Streamline -- to make a process simpler and faster by removing steps
"This streamlines onboarding from a week to an afternoon." (Simplificar / otimizar)
Drive -- to produce or cause a result, especially growth
"Faster onboarding drives higher retention." (Impulsionar / gerar)
Eliminate -- to remove a step or problem completely
"The integration eliminates double data entry." (Eliminar)
Dashboard -- the main screen where you see and control everything at a glance
"Everything you need is on one dashboard." (Painel de controle)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Eduardo opens his demo with a use case, not a list of buttons. "When a client signs up, the account is created automatically," he says, keeping the focus on the product. On the dashboard, every lead is scored in real time. "This means your team always knows what to prioritize," he adds, linking the feature to a result. Under the hood, the data is synced with the CRM, so nothing is entered twice. "It was designed to be seamless," he closes, "which leads to onboarding in a single afternoon."

1. How does Eduardo open the demo?
A With a use case the team will recognize.
B With a long list of every button.
C With the price.
2. Why does he say "the account is created automatically"?
A To name the developer who built it.
B To keep the focus on the product, using the passive.
C To ask the client a question.
3. How does he connect a feature to a benefit?
A He never mentions the benefit.
B With cause-and-effect language like "this means" and "which leads to".
C By repeating the feature name twice.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Voz passiva: be + particpio passado (is created, was designed, can be exported) — mantém o foco no produto, não em quem o fez. Cause-and-effect: ligue a funcionalidade ao resultado com this means, as a result, so (that) ou which leads to. / The passive (be + past participle) keeps the spotlight on the product; cause-and-effect language turns a feature into a benefit.

ToolFormExample
Passive (present)is / are + past participle"The report is generated automatically."
Passive (past)was / were + past participle"The flow was designed for speed."
Passive + modalcan be / will be + past participle"Reports can be exported in one click."
Cause & effectthis means / as a result / which leads to"It is automated, which leads to faster onboarding."

Key point / Ponto-chave: a voz passiva sempre pede o particpio passado depois de be — diga is created, não is create. E nunca mostre uma funcionalidade sem nomear o resultado que ela gera.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best line for each demo moment.

A "Our developers generate the report by hand."
B "The report is generated automatically."
C "The report is generate automatically."
You want to turn a feature into a benefit. Which sentence does that?
A "Leads are scored in real time, which means you prioritize faster."
B "There is a lead-scoring button here."
C "We have many features."

A "It is fully integrated with your CRM, so nothing is entered twice."
B "You have to start over from zero."
