Grammar Rule

The Four Tools

ToolExample
Schedule (L6)Can we schedule a meeting? I am available on Tuesday.
Future (L7)I am going to visit you. I will send the details.
Describe (L8)It is modern. It has a powerful engine.
Directions (L9)Take me to the office. Go straight. Turn left.

Key idea: use going to for plans and will for promises; is/has to describe; and start with the verb (no you) to give directions.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I am going visit you. It have a big engine. You turn left."
"I am going to visit you. It has a big engine. Turn left."

Remember to after going, has for it, and start a direction with the verb.
