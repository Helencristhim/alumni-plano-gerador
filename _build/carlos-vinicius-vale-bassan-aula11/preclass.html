Milestone -- an important target in a plan, with a date and an owner
"Each milestone has an owner and a date." (marco do projeto)
Rollout -- the gradual launch of a new system or plan
"The systems rollout starts in month two." (implantação gradual)
Transition -- the period of change from two companies to one
"The transition will take about six months." (transição)
Retention -- keeping the key people after the deal closes
"Retention is our biggest risk." (retenção de talentos)
Handover -- passing responsibility from one team or leader to another
"The handover happens at the end of the transition." (passagem de bastão)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Future Forms for Plans

GRAMMAR

Para falar do futuro de um plano, use três formas. Be going to + verbo base = um plano que você já tem ("We are going to announce the deal"). Present continuous (am/is/are + -ing) = um compromisso já marcado, com horário ("We are meeting the team on Monday"). Will + verbo base = previsão ou decisão ("The rollout will be slow"). Depois de will e de going to, use sempre o verbo base, nunca "will to" nem "going to + -ing". / Use be going to for plans, the present continuous for fixed arrangements with a time, and will for predictions and decisions. After will and going to, always use the base verb.

FormExample
Going to (plan)"We are going to announce the deal."
Present continuous (arrangement)"We are meeting the team on Monday."
Will (prediction)"The rollout will be slow at first."
