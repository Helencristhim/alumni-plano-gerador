Chapter 3 -- Planning the Future

The "Here Is the Plan" Language

Plans, arrangements, and predictions

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence talks about the future. Notice the orange words.

"We are going to announce the deal to both teams."

"We are meeting the new team on Monday."

"The rollout will be slow at first."

"Are you going to keep the regional leaders?"

The Rule

Going To / Present Continuous / Will

FormUse & Example
Going toA plan you already have: "We are going to announce the deal."
Present continuousA fixed arrangement with a time: "We are meeting the team on Monday."
WillA prediction or a decision: "The rollout will be slow at first."

Tip: use going to + base verb for plans, the present continuous for arrangements with a fixed time, and will + base verb for predictions and on-the-spot decisions. After will, never add to.

Common Mistake

After Will and Going To, Use the Base Verb

"We will to announce the deal. We are going to meeting the team."
"We will announce the deal. We are going to meet the team."

After will and after going to, use the base verb: never "will to", never "going to + -ing".
