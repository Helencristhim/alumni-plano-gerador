Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

This is my schedule for the week. I have a meeting with Dr. Carter on Monday, at half past two. The meeting is one hour. On Tuesday, there is a team meeting at nine o'clock. I put every appointment in the calendar. My holiday is in June.

1. Why do we say "at half past two" but "on Monday"? (Por que "at" e depois "on"?)
A at = a time; on = a day or date. (at = hora; on = dia/data.)
B They mean the same thing. (Significam a mesma coisa.)
C "at" is only for Mondays. (at e so para segundas.)
2. Which word do we use before a month (June)? (Qual palavra usamos antes de um mes?)
A at June. (at June.)
B in June. (in June.)
C on June. (on June.)
3. What time is the team meeting? (Que horas e a reuniao da equipe?)
A At half past two. (As duas e meia.)
B At nine o'clock. (As nove horas.)
C In June. (Em junho.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- at / on / in

Grammar

Como dizer a hora, o dia e o mes. / How to say the time, the day and the month.

Word / PalavraUse it for / Use paraExample / Exemplo
ata time / hora do relogioat ten o'clock, at half past two
ona day or a date / dia ou dataon Monday, on the third of June
ina month / mesin June

Horas: 9:00 = "nine o'clock" · 9:30 = "half past nine" · 9:45 = "a quarter to ten". / o'clock = hora cheia, half past = e meia, a quarter to = quinze para.
