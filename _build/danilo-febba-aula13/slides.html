Chapter 3

The Phone Code

Fixed phone phrases (+ can / could for requests)

Phone Phrases

Listen and Notice

Can I speak to Rachel, please?

This is Danilo speaking.

Could you hold on, please?

Can I take a message?

When you want to...Phone phrase
Ask for a personCan I speak to Rachel, please?
Say who you areThis is Danilo. / Speaking.
Ask someone to waitCould you hold on, please?
Offer a messageCan I take a message?
Check the lineCan you hear me? The line is bad.
Promise to returnI will call you back.

For polite requests, use Can / Could + base verb (no "to"): Could you repeat that? (not "Could you to repeat"). Could is a little more polite than can. On the phone we say This is Danilo, never "I am Danilo".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Who are you?"

✓ RIGHT

"Who is calling, please?"

✗ WRONG

"I am Danilo speaking."

✓ RIGHT

"This is Danilo speaking."

✗ WRONG

"Could you to repeat that?"

✓ RIGHT

"Could you repeat that?"

On the phone, ask "Who is calling?" and say "This is Danilo". For requests, use can / could + the base verb, with no "to".

Chapter 4

In Action

A real work call, line by line

Dialogue

A Call About the Report

R
Hello, can I speak to Danilo, please?
D
Speaking. Hi, this is Danilo. Who is calling, please?
R
Hi Danilo, it is Rachel. Can you hear me? The line is a little bad.
D
Now I can hear you. How can I help you, Rachel?
R
I am calling about the report. Could you send it today?
D
Of course. Could you hold on for one minute, please?
R
Sure, no problem. I will hold.
D
Thank you. I am sending it now. Can you check your email?
R
Got it. If I have a question, can I call you back later?
D
Of course. If I do not pick up, just leave a message. Bye, Rachel!
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Why is Rachel calling?
She is calling about the report.
2. What is the problem at the start of the call?
The line is a little bad; she asks if Danilo can hear her.
3. What does Danilo ask Rachel to do while he sends the report?
To hold on for one minute.
