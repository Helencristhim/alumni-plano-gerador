Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

If + Past, Would + Verb

The second conditional, for imaginary situations

Listen and Notice

What Do the Orange Words Share?

Each sentence imagines a situation that is not real now. Notice the two orange parts.

"If I had more time, I would learn the piano."

"If I won the lottery, I would build a school."

"If I were younger, I would study a new career."

"If I had the chance, I would not hesitate."

The Rule

The Second Conditional

PartFormExample
The "if" partIf + subject + past verb"If I had more time..."
The resultsubject + would + verb"...I would learn the piano."
"to be"use were for all people"If I were rich..." (not "was")
Switch orderwould part can come first"I would travel if I had money."

Tip: The past verb here does NOT mean the past. It means an imaginary now. Compare: If I keep going, I will grow (real, possible) vs If I had wings, I would fly (imaginary).

Common Mistake

Would, Not Will

"If I have more time, I will learn the piano." (this means it is real)
"If I had more time, I would learn the piano." (imaginary)

For an imaginary situation, use if + past and would + verb. Will and the present are for real, possible situations.
