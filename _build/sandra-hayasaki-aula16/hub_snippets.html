What Would You Do?

Imagine situações que não são reais agora e diga o que você faria, usando o second conditional (if + passado, would + verbo). Novo vocabulário: imagine, suppose, wish, would rather, hesitate, opportunity, regret, pretend. Foco gramatical: second conditional (situações imaginárias).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Imagine -- to form a picture or idea of something in your mind
"Imagine you had a free year -- where would you go?" (imaginar)
Suppose -- to think something is likely; to imagine as a possibility
"Suppose you won a prize. What would you buy first?" (supor / imaginar)
Wish -- to want something you do not have or cannot have now
"I wish I had more free time during the week." (desejar / queria)
Would rather -- to prefer one thing or action to another
"I would rather travel than buy an expensive car." (preferir)
Hesitate -- to pause before doing something because you are not sure
"If I had the chance, I would not hesitate." (hesitar)
Opportunity -- a good chance or moment to do something
"If I had the opportunity, I would live abroad for a year." (oportunidade)
Regret -- to feel sorry about a choice or action
"I have no regrets about the path I chose." (arrependimento / arrepender-se)
Pretend -- to act as if something is real when it is not
"Let's pretend you had a free year. What would you do?" (fingir / fazer de conta)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Let me imagine a different life for a moment. If I had more free time, I would learn to play the piano. Suppose I won a big prize -- I would not hesitate to travel the world. If I were a bit braver, I would take every opportunity that came. Of course, these are only imaginary situations, and I have no regrets about my real life. But I would rather dream big than sit still. I wish I had more hours in the day, but for now, I keep going.

1. Is the situation in the text real or imaginary?
A Imaginary -- it uses "if + past, would + verb".
B Real and possible -- it uses "if + present, will".
C A past habit that stopped.
2. What would the speaker do if they won a big prize?
A Stay home and rest.
B Not hesitate to travel the world.
C Sell the piano.
3. Which sentence shows the speaker's preference?
A "I have no regrets."
B "I would rather dream big than sit still."
C "These are only imaginary situations."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Second Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usamos o second conditional para situações imaginárias (que não são reais agora). A estrutura é If + verbo no passado, would + verbo. O passado aqui NÃO fala do passado, fala de um "agora imaginário". Use were para todas as pessoas (If I were rich). / We use the second conditional for imaginary situations: if + past, would + verb. Compare with the real first conditional (if + present, will) from last block.

TypeFormExample
Real (1st)if + present, will + verb"If I keep going, I will grow."
Imaginary (2nd)if + past, would + verb"If I had time, I would learn."
"to be"use were for everyone"If I were rich..." (not "was")
Orderthe would part can come first"I would travel if I had money."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a frase mais correta para uma situação imaginária.

You want to imagine what you would do with more time. What do you say?
A "If I have more time, I will learn the piano."
B "If I had more time, I would learn the piano."
C "If I had more time, I will learn the piano."
You want to imagine being a different age. What do you say?
A "If I were younger, I would study art."
B "If I was younger, I will study art."
C "If I am younger, I would study art."
You want to say which option you prefer in an imaginary case. What do you say?
A "I would rather to travel than stay home."
B "I would rather travel than stay home."
C "I will rather travel than stay home."
