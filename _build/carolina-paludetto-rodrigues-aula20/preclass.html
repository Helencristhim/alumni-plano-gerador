Shopping for Food -- prices and How much is it? at the supermarket

Go shopping for food and ask prices with How much is it? and How much are they?, and point with this, that, these and those. New vocabulary: price, cheap, expensive, cashier, receipt, customer, cart, change.

Vocabulary

Price -- how much money a thing costs
"The price of these apples is three reais." (Preço)
Cheap -- it costs little money, not a lot
"These apples are cheap, only three reais." (Barato)
Expensive -- it costs a lot of money
"That brand is too expensive for me." (Caro)
Cashier -- the person who takes your money at the store
"The cashier gives me my change." (Caixa / atendente)
Receipt -- the paper that shows what you bought and the price
"Please keep the receipt to check the prices." (Recibo / nota)
Customer -- the person who buys things at a store
"The customer asks the cashier for the price." (Cliente / freguês)
Cart -- the thing on wheels you push and fill with food
"I put my fruit and vegetables in the cart." (Carrinho)
Change -- the money you get back after you pay
"The cashier gives me my change and a receipt." (Troco)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

On Saturday, Carolina goes shopping for food. She takes a cart and walks to the fruit aisle. "How much are these apples?" she asks. "These apples are very cheap, only three reais," the cashier says. Then Carolina looks at the rice. "How much is that box?" "That brand is expensive, but this one here is cheaper," he answers. Carolina is a smart customer, so she takes the cheap one. At the checkout, she pays a good price. The cashier gives her a receipt and her change. "Thank you! Have a nice day," she says, and goes home with a bag of fresh food.

1. How much are the apples?
A They are cheap, only three reais.
B They are expensive, twenty reais.
C They are free today.
2. To ask about the box of rice (one box), which is correct?
A "How much are that box?"
B "How much is that box?"
C "How much is those box?"
3. What does the cashier give Carolina at the end?
A A receipt and her change.
B An expensive cart.
C Nothing at all.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- How much is it? & this/that/these/those

GRAMMAR

Use How much IS it? para uma coisa e How much ARE they? para varias. Use this (uma, perto) e that (uma, longe); these (varias, perto) e those (varias, longe). / Use How much IS it? for one thing and How much ARE they? for many. Use this (one, near) and that (one, far); these (many, near) and those (many, far).

WordUseExample
How much is it?price of one thingHow much is this apple?
How much are they?price of many thingsHow much are these apples?
this / thatone thing: this (near), that (far)this bag / that bag
these / thosemany things: these (near), those (far)these apples / those bananas

Key point / Ponto-chave: use is + uma coisa e are + varias, NUNCA "How much is these apples". Use this/that + singular e these/those + plural, NUNCA "these apple". Diferença da aula 19: lá falamos de QUANTIDADES (how much/how many sugar); aqui falamos de PREÇOS e apontar (How much is this? this/that/these/those).
