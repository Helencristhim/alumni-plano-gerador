Chapter 3 -- The Phone Code

Three Phases

Open -- Clarify -- Close

Listen and Notice

Which Phase?

Open, clarify, or close?

"Hi, this is Simone speaking. I'm calling about the agreement."

"Sorry, could you speak up? The connection is weak."

"Could you clarify the indemnity clause for me?"

"Thanks for your time. I'll call you back this afternoon."

The Phone Code

Open -- Clarify -- Close

PhaseWhat you doExample
OpeningSay who you are and why you are calling"This is Simone speaking. I'm calling about the agreement."
ClarifyingFix a bad line; confirm you understood"Sorry, could you speak up? Could you clarify that?"
ClosingConfirm the next step before you hang up"Thanks for your time. I'll call you back."

Key idea: identify yourself first, confirm you understood, and agree on the next step before you hang up. A clear close is what makes a call professional.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Who are you? ... Repeat!"
"May I ask who's calling? ... Sorry, could you repeat that?"

On a professional call, soften direct questions with May I ask...? and Could you...? A command sounds rude; a polite request keeps the relationship strong.

VIDEO CALL -- PREP NOTES

Call with Mark Pearson -- Share Purchase Agreement -- 2:30 pm

"This is Simone speaking. Thanks for the voicemail. I'm calling about the agreement."

- Confirm the final indemnity number.
- Clarify the new closing date and the timeline in section four.

Reminder: if a colleague is in the room, ask them to mute. Do not hang up without a clear next step.
