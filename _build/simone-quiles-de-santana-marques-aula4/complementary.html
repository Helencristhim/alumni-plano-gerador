Lesson 4 -- Phone & Video Calls

Series
Industry (HBO) -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Induction"

A fast-paced corporate drama set in finance, full of phone and conference calls. Notice how characters open a call, identify themselves, deal with bad lines, and close with a clear next step -- exactly the phone code from today.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 phrases people use to open or close a call. Then record yourself reusing one of them with your own context.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run a Great Virtual Meeting"

Practical advice for phone and video calls: opening clearly, handling a weak connection, asking people to speak up or clarify, and closing with action points. Listen for the polite phrases native speakers use to keep a call on track.

Note 3 expressions the speakers use to clarify or confirm. Compare them with the phrases Simone used with Mark in today's call.

YouTube
BBC Learning English -- "Telephone English: how to make a phone call"

A short, practical lesson on professional phone language: introducing yourself, asking someone to hold on, dealing with a bad connection, leaving a voicemail, and ending the call politely. Directly useful for your calls with international counsel.

After watching, record a 60-second voicemail in English about a contract point. Use at least four new words and the three phases: open, clarify, close.
