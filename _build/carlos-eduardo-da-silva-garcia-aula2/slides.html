Chapter 4: The Verb To Be

am · is · are

Yes, no, and questions

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I am an analyst."

"Sucafina is Swiss."

"The producers are in Brazil."

"It is not Brazilian."

Subjectto beExample
Iam (I'm)I am an analyst.
he / she / it / a nameis (he's)Sucafina is Swiss.
you / we / they / pluralare (they're)The producers are in Brazil.
no / questionNo: add not (is not / isn't). Question: to be first (Is Sucafina Swiss?).

I = am · he/she/it = is · you/we/they = are. / Com "I" use am; com nomes/empresas use is; com plurais use are.

Language Focus

Yes, No, or Question?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I analyst at Sucafina."

✓ RIGHT

"I am an analyst at Sucafina."

✗ WRONG

"You are from the Nairobi office?"

✓ RIGHT

"Are you from the Nairobi office?"

Never drop the verb "to be" (I am, not "I analyst"). For a question, put the verb first: "Are you...?" not "You are...?".

Dialogue

Emma Asks About Sucafina

E
Hi Carlos. A new colleague has a question. Is Sucafina a Brazilian company?
C
No, it is not. Sucafina is Swiss. It is headquartered in Switzerland.
E
Great. And are you a farmer?
C
No, I am not. I am an analyst. I am responsible for sustainability.
E
And the coffee producers — are they in Africa?
C
No, they are not. My producers are in Brazil, in southern Minas Gerais.
E
Perfect. Is the company international?
C
Yes, it is. Sucafina is big and international. We export coffee to many countries.
