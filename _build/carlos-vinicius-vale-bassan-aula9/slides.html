Chapter 3 -- Conditional Offers

The "If You..., We'll..." Sentence

Give something only when you get something back

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each offer has two parts. Notice the orange words.

"If you lower the price, we'll sign today."

"If you commit to two years, I can offer a discount."

"We'll move on price if you drop the exclusivity clause."

"Unless you meet our bottom line, we'll walk away."

The Rule

If + Present, Will / Can

Condition (if-part)Offer (result)
If you lower the price,we 'll sign today.
If you commit to two years,I can offer a discount.
Unless you meet our bottom line,we 'll walk away.

Tip: the if-part uses the present simple (not "will"). The offer uses will / 'll or can. "Unless" means "if not". Use a comma when the if-part comes first.

Common Mistake

No "Will" in the If-Part

"If you will lower the price, we will sign."
"If you lower the price, we will sign."

The if-part takes the present simple: "if you lower". Keep will for the offer only.

Practice

Complete the Offer

"If you _____drop the clause, we'll sign." (present of "drop")
"We _____'ll offer a discount if you commit to two years." (offer)
"_____If they make a fair counteroffer, we'll accept." (condition word)
"_____Unless you meet our bottom line, we'll walk away." ("if not")
