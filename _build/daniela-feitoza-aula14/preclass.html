Warm-up — Callback da Aula 13

Review

Na Aula 13 você aprendeu a dar a sua opinião com tato — abrir com "In my view", suavizar com "might / perhaps" e discordar com respeito ("I see your point, but..."). Agora você sabe dizer o que pensa. Hoje vamos transformar essa opinião em um plano: falar do que você vai fazer (going to), do que você prevê (will) e dos compromissos já marcados (present continuous). (Decide it, schedule it, predict it.)

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Forecast -- a statement of what will probably happen, based on data (PT: previsão)
"The forecast shows higher sales next quarter."
Intend -- to have a plan or aim to do something (PT: pretender / ter a intenção)
"We intend to finish the rollout by June."
Anticipate -- to expect something and get ready for it before it happens (PT: prever / antecipar)
"We anticipate some delays during the launch."
Roadmap -- a plan that shows the steps and timeline of a project (PT: roteiro / plano de etapas)
"The roadmap puts the new system in the third quarter."
Scope -- the boundaries of a project: what it includes and excludes (PT: escopo / abrangência)
"A small pilot keeps the scope under control."
Allocate -- to give a share of time, people or money to a task (PT: alocar / destinar)
"We will allocate two engineers to the project."
Kickoff -- the first meeting that officially starts a project (PT: reunião de início)
"The kickoff is next Monday at ten."
Deliverable -- a concrete result or product that a project produces (PT: entregável / entrega)
"The first deliverable is a working checkout screen."
Contingency -- a backup plan for a problem that might happen (PT: plano de contingência / plano B)
"Our contingency is to keep the old system for a month."
Projection -- an estimate of a future number, like sales or cost (PT: projeção / estimativa)
"The projection is two thousand new users by December."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Daniela is planning a pilot. "We are going to launch in two stores," she says — a plan she already decided. The kickoff is next Monday, and the team is presenting the results in May: these are fixed arrangements, so she uses the present continuous. She also makes a prediction: "I think the new system will save us time." Notice the forms: after going to and after will, she uses the base verb (launch, save) — never "will to" and never "going to launching". A good manager uses going to for plans, will for predictions, and the present continuous for arrangements with a time.

1. Why does Daniela say "We are going to launch in two stores"?
A It is a plan she already decided.
B It already happened in the past.
C It is only a guess with no plan.
2. Which form does she use for a meeting already in the calendar?
A will + base verb
B present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing)
C past simple
3. Which sentence is correct?
A "I will to send the report."
B "I will send the report."
C "We are going to launching on Monday."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Future Forms (going to, will, present continuous)

GRAMMAR

As três formas de falar do futuro (bilingue EN + PT).

FormUseExample
am/is/are going to + verbo basePlano já decidido / evidência clara"We are going to run a pilot."
will + verbo basePrevisão / decisão tomada na hora"It will save us time. I will send it now."
am/is/are + verbo-ingCompromisso marcado, com horário"We are meeting the vendor on Friday."
Regra (PT)Depois de will e de going to, use o verbo base (will send, going to launch) — NUNCA "will to" nem "going to launching". Use going to para PLANOS, will para PREVISÕES, e o present continuous para COMPROMISSOS já agendados.
