Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra, leia o exemplo e a tradução. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Catalog -- the full list of products a customer can browse and buy (PT: catálogo)
"The catalog shows every product in the store."
Inventory system -- the software that tracks how much stock a store has (PT: sistema de estoque)
"The inventory system shows the stock in real time."
Checkout -- the step where the customer pays and finishes the order (PT: caixa / finalização da compra)
"At the checkout, the customer pays in two seconds."
Payment gateway -- the service that processes a card payment securely (PT: gateway de pagamento)
"The payment gateway processes the card in two seconds."
Dashboard -- a screen that shows your most important live data in one place (PT: painel)
"The dashboard shows live sales to every store manager."
Analytics -- the study of data to find patterns and make smart decisions (PT: análise de dados)
"Our analytics show which products sell best."
User interface -- the screen and buttons the user sees and touches; the UI (PT: interface do usuário)
"The user interface is simple, so staff need little training."
Point-of-sale -- the terminal in a physical store where the customer pays; the POS (PT: ponto de venda)
"We integrate with your point-of-sale terminals."
Scalable -- able to grow with your business without breaking or slowing down (PT: escalável)
"The platform is scalable, so it grows with us."
Rollout -- the process of launching a product or system to all users, step by step (PT: implantação)
"The full rollout takes about six weeks."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Daniela presented her project. "The customer opens the mobile app and browses the catalog," she said. "The app connects to our inventory system in real time." Notice the words: inventory describes system, so the term is a compound noun — two nouns joined together, with the first one singular. "At the checkout, the payment gateway processes the card." Again, payment describes gateway. "The dashboard shows real-time analytics, and the user interface is simple. The platform is scalable, and the full rollout takes six weeks." Each part has a clear name, and that is what makes a pitch easy to follow.

1. In "inventory system", which noun is the main thing, and which one describes it?
A "system" is the main thing; "inventory" describes it.
B "inventory" is the main thing; "system" describes it.
C Both words are equally the main thing.
2. Why is "payment gateway" written with "payment" first?
A Because it sounds nicer that way.
B Because the describing noun comes first, then the main thing.
C Because "gateway" is a verb here.
3. What makes Daniela’s pitch easy to follow?
A She speaks very fast.
B She uses long, complex sentences.
C Each part of the system has a clear name.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Compound Nouns (noun + noun)

GRAMMAR

Como formar termos técnicos com dois substantivos (bilingue EN + PT).

PatternHow it worksExample
noun + nounFirst noun describes; second noun is the main thing"payment gateway" = a gateway for payments
first noun singularThe describing noun is almost always singular"inventory system" (NOT "inventories system")
order mattersDescriber first, main thing second"user interface" (NOT "interface user")
Regra (PT)Em inglês a ordem é inversa do português: a palavra que descreve vem PRIMEIRO e no SINGULAR; a coisa principal vem DEPOIS. "Sistema de estoque" → "inventory system". Pergunte: que tipo de [coisa principal]?
