Chapter 3: For & Since

For vs Since

Present perfect for duration

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have lived in Florida for three months."

"I have lived here since March."

"She has worked at the clinic for two years."

"We have known each other since we were kids."

When do we use for, and when do we use since?

WordUse it withExample
fora PERIOD of timefor three months, for two years
sincea POINT in the pastsince March, since 2024
QuestionHow long have you + past participle...? — How long have you lived here?
Formhave / has + past participle. Use it when something STARTED in the past and CONTINUES now.

Remember: for + a period (for a while); since + a point (since I moved). The action is still true now.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am living here since 2024."
"I have lived here since 2024."
"I live here for two years."
"I have lived here for two years."
"I have lived here since three months."
"I have lived here for three months."

For duration up to now, use the present perfect. Use for with a period of time and since with a point in the past.

Dialogue

Meeting the Neighbor

D
Hi! You must be my new neighbor. Have you lived in this building for a long time?
A
Hi! Nice to meet you. Actually, I moved here quite recently. I have lived here for about three months.
D
Oh, welcome! And how do you like it so far? It takes a while to settle in.
A
So far, I love it. I am still getting used to the hot weather, but I have started to fit in.
D
That is great. I have lived here since 2015, and honestly, it still feels like home. Do you ever feel homesick?
A
Sometimes, especially for my family. But ever since I joined the local community, I have started to feel like I belong here.
