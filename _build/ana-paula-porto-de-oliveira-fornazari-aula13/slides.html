Chapter 4: All the Tenses

One Story, Many Tenses

Past, present, present perfect and future

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"When I started, I learned to introduce myself."

"These days, I study English almost every day."

"So far, I have overcome many mistakes."

"One day, I am going to work abroad."

TenseUse it forExample
Past simplethe beginning, a finished timeI started, I learned.
Present simpleyour life now, routinesI study every day.
Present perfectthe journey so farI have overcome many mistakes.
Future (going to / will)plans and predictionsI am going to work abroad.
Modals (can)abilityI can hold a conversation.

Choose each tense by the TIME you mean, not by the word.

Grammar Practice

Choose the Right Tense

Say the full sentence out loud, then tap to check

"When I started, I ___could only say a few words." (the beginning)
"These days, I ___study English almost every day." (now)
"So far, I ___have reached a few milestones." (the journey)
"One day, I ___am going to work abroad." (a plan)
"Now I ___can hold a real conversation." (ability)
Language Focus

Which Time?

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"When I start the course, I know only a few words."

✓ RIGHT

"When I started the course, I knew only a few words."

✗ WRONG

"One day I work abroad as a doctor."

✓ RIGHT

"One day I am going to work abroad as a doctor."

Use the past simple for the beginning (I started, I knew), and going to or will for a future plan (I am going to work). Match the tense to the time.

Dialogue

Halfway There

A
Tom, can you believe it? We are already halfway through our English course. It has been quite a journey.
T
I know! Do you remember your very first class? How did you feel back then?
A
So nervous! When I started, I could only say a few words. Now I have a much better routine, and I study almost every day.
T
That is a big change. What did you learn in those first lessons?
A
I learned to talk about my neighborhood, my favorite hobby, and my daily schedule. Simple things, but they gave me confidence.
T
And now? What has been the biggest milestone for you so far?
A
Honestly, the biggest milestone is how many mistakes I have overcome. Every class is an opportunity to grow.
T
So what is next? Do you have any big goals?
A
Yes! One day I am going to work abroad as a doctor. That dream is at the top of my bucket list.
