Lesson 13 -- Pre-class

How Long? -- Settling into a New Life

Talking about how long you have done things since a new start. Key words: to move, to settle in, to get used to, so far, ever since, recently, lately, homesick, to fit in, a while, to belong, gradually. Structure: present perfect with for (a period) and since (a point in time) for actions that started in the past and continue now.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Ana Paula moved to Florida last year, and she has lived in the same apartment since April. She has studied English for ten months, so she can talk to her neighbors easily now. She has volunteered at a local clinic since June, and she has felt at home ever since she joined the community. So far, she loves her new life. She still feels homesick sometimes, but gradually, this city has become home.

1. Why do we say "since April" and not "for April"?
A We use since with a point in time, like a month or a date.
B April always takes since, but every other month takes for.
C Since and for mean exactly the same thing.
2. In "she has studied English for ten months", what does "for" show?
A The exact day she started.
B A period of time, from the past until now.
C Something she finished a long time ago.
3. Which sentence correctly says how long she has lived there?
A "She is living here since April."
B "She has lived here since April."
C "She lives here since April."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- For and Since

GRAMMAR

Use the present perfect (have / has + past participle) for something that started in the past and continues now. Use for with a period of time, and since with a point in the past.

WordAffirmativeNegative & Question
for + a periodI have lived here for three months.
for a while, for two years		I haven't lived here for long.
How long have you lived here?
since + a pointI have lived here since March.
since 2024, since I moved		She hasn't worked here since June.
Have you studied English since then?
RememberUse the present perfect, not the present: "I have lived here for two years" (not "I live here for two years"). for = how long; since = the start point.
