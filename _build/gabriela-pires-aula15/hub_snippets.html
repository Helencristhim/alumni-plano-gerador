Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir. / Listen to each word and read the example.

Museum -- a building where you go to see art and history
"The museum in Paris is very famous." (Museu)
Entrance -- the door or place where you go into a building
"The entrance is on your right." (Entrada)
Exhibition -- a group of works of art shown for people to see
"There is a beautiful exhibition of French art." (Exposição)
Gallery -- a large room in a museum where art is shown
"There are many paintings in this gallery." (Galeria)
Painting -- a picture an artist makes with colors and a brush
"This painting is very old and famous." (Pintura / Quadro)
Sculpture -- a work of art in three dimensions, often in stone or metal
"There are many sculptures on the second floor." (Escultura)
Artist -- a person who makes art, such as paintings or sculptures
"This artist is very famous in France." (Artista)
Guide -- a person who walks with you and explains the art
"A guide explained each room to us." (Guia)
Masterpiece -- a very special and important work of art by a great artist
"This painting is a real masterpiece." (Obra-prima)

GRAMMAR

Gabriela visits a famous museum in Paris. At the entrance, she asks, "How much is a student ticket?" It is only six euros, so she shows her student card. Inside, there is a beautiful exhibition of French art. On the first floor, there is a famous painting; it is a real masterpiece. On the second floor, there are many sculptures by a great artist. A guide walks with Gabriela and explains each gallery. She takes many photos, but she does not use the flash.

3. Who explains the galleries to Gabriela?
A The artist.
B A guide.
C Her mom.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Prices & There is / There are

GRAMMAR

Usamos "How much is/are" para perguntar preços e "There is/are" para dizer o que existe. Use "is" para uma coisa e "are" para várias. / We use "How much is/are" to ask prices and "There is/are" to say what exists.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Ask price (one)How much is + singular?How much is a ticket?
Ask price (many)How much are + plural?How much are two tickets?
One thing existsThere is + singularThere is a painting.
Many things existThere are + pluralThere are many sculptures.
Describe itIt is / They are + adjectiveIt is beautiful.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use is para uma coisa e are para várias. Para perguntar preço: How much is (uma) / How much are (várias). Para dizer o que existe: There is (uma) / There are (várias).
