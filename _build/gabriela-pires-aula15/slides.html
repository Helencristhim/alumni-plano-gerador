Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Asking prices and describing what you see

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common?

"How much is a student ticket?"

"How much are two tickets?"

"There is a famous painting on the first floor."

"There are many sculptures in this gallery."

Grammar Rule

Prices & What You See

FormExample
Ask the price (one thing)How much is a ticket?
Ask the price (many things)How much are two tickets?
Say one thing existsThere is a painting here.
Say many things existThere are many sculptures.
Describe what you seeIt is beautiful. They are amazing.

Key idea: use is for one thing and are for many things. To ask a price: How much is / How much are. To describe what exists: There is / There are.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"There is many paintings. How much are this ticket?"
"There are many paintings. How much is this ticket?"

Use is for one thing and are for many things.

Grammar Practice

Is it One or Many?

"How much _____is one ticket?" (one thing)
"How much _____are three tickets?" (many things)
"There _____is a famous painting here." (one thing)
"There _____are many sculptures upstairs." (many things)
