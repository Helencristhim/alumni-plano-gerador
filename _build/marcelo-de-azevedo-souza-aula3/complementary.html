Aula 3 -- What Do You Do?

Docuseries
Rotten (Netflix) -- any episode

A documentary series that follows food from the source all the way to your table -- the supply chain, the cold chain, and the last-mile delivery, told through real people and real companies. It is your professional world on screen. Connection to Lesson 3: listen for how experts describe what they do (present simple) and the problems they are dealing with right now (present continuous).

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Afterward, tell your teacher one thing a company does every day (present simple) and one thing it is doing now (present continuous).

Podcast
Planet Money -- NPR, any episode

Short, clear stories about how the economy really works: how products move, how companies coordinate, and how a supply chain can break. The hosts explain complex operations in plain, natural American English -- exactly the register you need to describe your own job to anyone.

Tip: pick one episode about shipping or supply chains. Write down three verbs the hosts use to describe what companies do (like oversee, handle, coordinate).

YouTube
How Overnight Shipping Works -- Wendover Productions

A clear, visual explainer of the exact system you run: how a package is sorted, routed and delivered overnight, with dozens of teams working together against the clock. Natural, well-paced American English on a topic you know better than the narrator.

Tip: watch once, then explain the process out loud in your own words -- what happens first, what the teams handle, and why it is time-sensitive.
