Chapter 3: The Code

What You Do vs What You Are Doing

I oversee · We are expanding

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I oversee operations for a pharmaceutical company."

"My team handles deliveries across the country."

"Right now, we are expanding into three new states."

"This month, I am working on a new cold chain system."

Why green for what is always true, but blue for right now?

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb (he/she + s)		Facts, roles and routines that are always true (every day, usually)I oversee operations.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Projects in progress right now, around this period (now, currently, this month)We are expanding right now.

every day, usually point to the present simple · right now, currently, this month point to the present continuous.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am working in logistics for many years."
"I work in logistics."
"Right now, we expand into three new states."
"Right now, we are expanding into three new states."

Use the present simple for a permanent fact or role. Use the present continuous (am / is / are + -ing) for a project happening right now.

Chapter 4: The Encounter

So, What Do You Do Back Home?

Dinner at a cycling weekend

Dialogue

Explaining Your World

I
So, Marcelo, we have talked about bikes all weekend, but I still don't know what you do back home. What do you do?
M
I oversee operations for a pharmaceutical logistics company. Basically, my team makes sure that medicine gets everywhere it needs to go, safely and on time.
I
That sounds simple, but I imagine it isn't. What is the hard part?
M
The cold chain. Medicine is time-sensitive, so we ensure it stays at the right temperature every hour. I liaise with suppliers, drivers and customs all day.
I
Fascinating. And are you working on anything new at the moment?
M
Yes, right now we are expanding into three new states, so I am building a whole new last-mile delivery team. It is the biggest project of my career.
