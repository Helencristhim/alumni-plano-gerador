Chapter 3: The Pattern

What You Do

I check · he checks · I do not write

Discover the Grammar

Listen and Notice

"I check the checklists."

"My manager checks the report."

"We attend the meeting."

"I do not write in English yet."

SubjectVerbExample
I / you / we / theycheck (base form)I check the checklists.
he / she / itchecks (add -s)She checks the report.
Negativedo not / does notI do not write. He does not write.
QuestionDo you attend meetings? Does she review reports?

He / she / it = verb + s. Negativa e pergunta usam do / does. / With he/she/it, add -s; for no and questions use do/does.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"He check the farms."

✓ RIGHT

"He checks the farms."

✗ WRONG

"I no write in English."

✓ RIGHT

"I do not write in English."

With he/she/it, add -s (he checks). For "no", use do not / does not, never just "no". "don't" = do not, "doesn't" = does not.

Chapter 4: A Week at Sucafina

Your Routine

Emma asks what you do every week

Dialogue

What Do You Do Every Week?

E
Hi Carlos. I have a quick question. What do you do at Sucafina every week?
C
On Mondays, I check the farm checklists. I also verify supplier compliance every month.
E
Do you attend meetings with the Rwanda team?
C
Yes, I often attend the alignment call on Zoom. Then I report the results to my manager.
E
And your manager? What does she do?
C
She reviews the reports and sends feedback to the team.
E
Do you write the documents in English alone?
C
Not yet. I do not write long documents alone, but I send short messages every day.
