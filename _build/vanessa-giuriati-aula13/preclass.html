Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Gathering -- a small social event where people meet (encontro/reunião social)
"We're having a small gathering on Friday."
Host -- the person who organizes an event and welcomes people (anfitrião/receber)
"I'll host the dinner this time."
Venue -- the place where an event or meeting happens (local do evento)
"Let's find a venue near the station."
Available -- free and able to meet at a certain time (disponível/livre)
"Are you available on Sunday?"
Suit -- to be convenient or right for someone (servir/convir a alguém)
"Does Saturday suit you?"
Come up with -- to think of an idea or a solution (bolar/pensar numa ideia)
"Let's come up with a plan together."
Settle on -- to finally choose or decide on something (decidir-se por/fechar)
"We settled on Sunday at noon."
Look forward to -- to feel happy and excited about something coming (estar ansioso/animado por algo)
"I'm looking forward to the weekend."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

When Marina plans a gathering, she never decides everything alone. She always starts with a suggestion: "Why don't we meet on Saturday?" or "How about a picnic in the park?" That way, nobody feels forced. Then she checks who is available, because there is no point in a date that suits her but not the group. For the place, she likes to settle on a venue that is easy to reach. If someone is not keen on her idea, that is fine — they come up with something else together. For Marina, a good plan is never one person deciding; it is a group agreeing on something everybody is looking forward to. And when she hosts, she always offers to help, never just orders.

1. How does Marina always start when she plans a gathering?
A With a suggestion, like "Why don't we...?"
B By deciding everything alone.
C By telling people what to do.
2. Why does she check who is available?
A Because she likes to be alone.
B Because a date that suits her but not the group is no good.
C Because she never asks anyone.
3. Which sentence correctly suggests something?
A "How about to meet on Saturday?"
B "How about meeting on Saturday?"
C "Why we don't meet on Saturday?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Making & Responding to Suggestions

GRAMMAR

Como sugerir e como responder. / How to suggest and how to respond.

StructureEnglishPortuguês
Why don't we + base verbWhy don't we meet on Saturday?Por que não...?
How about + -ingHow about meeting at noon? (not "to meet")Que tal...?
What if we + verbWhat if we tried a new place?E se a gente...?
Responding"That sounds great." / "Maybe, but I'm not sure."Aceitar / recusar com gentileza
⚠ CarefulAfter "How about", use the -ing form. The order is "Why don't we...?". And "look forward to" takes the -ing form: "looking forward to meeting you".

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Grave você mesma respondendo à pergunta abaixo. Não existe certo ou errado.

Imagine you want to organize a small gathering with friends or your team. Suggest a plan out loud: where to meet, when, and who hosts. Use at least two suggestion structures from this lesson (Why don't we...?, How about...?, What if we...?, We could always...) and end by saying you are looking forward to it. Speak slowly and take your time.
