Chapter 3

The Pattern

How to suggest and respond

Grammar Discovery

Five Ways to Say "Let's Plan It"

"Why don't we meet on Saturday?"

"How about meeting at noon?"

"What if we tried a new place?"

"We could always order in."

StructureFormExample
Why don't we...?Why don't we + base verbWhy don't we meet on Saturday?
How about...?How about + verb-ingHow about meeting at noon?
What if we...?What if we + verbWhat if we tried a new place?
We could (always)...A soft, open way to suggest an option: "We could always order in."
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"How about to meet at noon?"

✓ RIGHT

"How about meeting at noon?"

✗ WRONG

"Why we don't meet on Sunday?"

✓ RIGHT

"Why don't we meet on Sunday?"

✗ WRONG

"I'm looking forward to meet you."

✓ RIGHT

"I'm looking forward to meeting you."

After "How about", use the -ing form (not "to"). The word order is "Why don't we...?". And "look forward to" is followed by the -ing form: "looking forward to meeting".

Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is her first suggestion?
A small gathering at her place; she offers to host.
2. How does she react when Saturday might not work?
She agrees it's a good point and suggests asking first to come up with a day that works for all.
3. How does she feel about the plan at the end?
She is already looking forward to it.
Listening

How She Plans a Gathering

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. How does she always start when planning?
She starts with a suggestion and lets the others react, instead of deciding alone.
2. What does she think about when she has picked an idea?
She thinks about who is available, asks early, and stays flexible.
3. What does she say a good plan really is?
A group settling on something everybody is happy with and looking forward to, not one person deciding.
Listening

Offer, Don't Order

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. What happens if you say "we are doing this"?
The door closes and people feel pushed; it sounds like ordering, not offering.
2. What two responses does the speaker call honest and kind?
"That sounds great" and "Maybe, but I'm not sure that works for me."
3. When someone offers to host, what should you do?
Accept warmly but also offer to help: bring something and choose the venue and time together.
