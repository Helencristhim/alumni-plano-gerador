Plans and Intentions -- Your Project Kickoff

The future with going to (decided plans: I am going to launch the kickoff) and will (predictions, offers, and promises: I think it will work, I will help you). New vocabulary: kickoff, upcoming, deadline, goal, forecast, roadmap, outcome, commit to, estimate, allocate.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Kickoff -- the official start of a project or plan
"The committee is going to launch the kickoff next quarter." (Início do projeto)
Upcoming -- happening soon, in the near future
"I am going to share our plan for the upcoming program." (Próximo)
Deadline -- the final date or time to finish something
"The deadline for the first phase is the end of March." (Prazo)
Goal -- something you plan and aim to achieve
"Our main goal is to train forty nurses in two years." (Meta)
Forecast -- a statement about what will probably happen
"I forecast that the first group will be ready by December." (Previsão)
Roadmap -- a plan that shows the main stages ahead
"Let me walk you through the roadmap for the program." (Plano de etapas)
Outcome -- the final result of a plan or action
"I really believe the outcome will be excellent." (Resultado)
Commit to -- to promise firmly to do something
"I commit to supporting each of you at every step." (Comprometer-se)
Estimate -- to calculate an approximate number or time
"I estimate that each nurse will need twenty hours." (Estimar)
Allocate -- to give time, money, or people to a task
"I am going to allocate one mentor to every two nurses." (Alocar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Andreia is preparing the kickoff of an upcoming robotic training program. "We are going to launch it next quarter," she says, "and our main goal is to train forty nurses." She walks the team through the roadmap: the deadline for the first phase is the end of March. "I am going to allocate one mentor to every two learners," she adds. Then she makes a prediction: "I forecast that the outcome will be excellent, and I think the first group will be ready by December." Finally she promises, "I commit to support every nurse, and I will send the next steps after the meeting."

B "I think the first group will be ready by December."
C "I will send the next steps after the meeting."
2. Why does she use "will" in "the outcome will be excellent"?
A Because it is a prediction or opinion about the future.
B Because it is a plan already on the calendar.
C Because it already happened.
3. What is the deadline for the first phase?
A The end of March.
B Next December.
C Two years from now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- going to vs will

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar do futuro: planos já decididos e previsões ou decisões do momento. / Used to talk about the future: decided plans, and predictions or decisions made at the moment.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
am / is / are + going to + verbPlano já decidido / A plan already decidedWe are going to launch the kickoff.
going to (with evidence)Previsão com sinal visível agora / Prediction with present evidenceLook at the budget -- it is going to be tight.
will + base verbPrevisão ou opinião / Prediction or opinionI think the outcome will be excellent.
will + base verbDecisão do momento, oferta, promessa / On-the-spot decision, offer, promiseDo not worry, I will send it today.

Key point / Ponto-chave: plano já decidido = going to. Previsão, oferta, promessa ou decisão feita na hora = will. Depois de will, use o verbo na base -- nunca "will to".
