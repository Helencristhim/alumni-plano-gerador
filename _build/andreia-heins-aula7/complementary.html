Lesson 7 -- Plans and Intentions

Series
The Resident -- Season 1, Episode 1: "Pilot"

A hospital drama where leaders launch new programs, set goals, and plan their next steps. Listen for how characters talk about the future: decided plans with going to and predictions or promises with will.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down two sentences with going to (a decided plan) and two with will (a prediction or a promise). Then describe a plan of your own using the same pattern.

Podcast
HBR IdeaCast -- "How to Run a Great Kickoff Meeting"

Leaders explain how they open a project: how they set a goal, build a roadmap, agree on deadlines, and forecast outcomes. Full of natural future language for the workplace.

Note three sentences that describe future plans. Then record a short kickoff for your own project, using at least three new words from today, such as goal, roadmap, and deadline.

YouTube
English with Lucy -- "WILL vs GOING TO"

A clear, practical lesson on exactly today's grammar: going to for decided plans and evidence-based predictions, and will for opinions, offers, promises, and on-the-spot decisions.

After watching, write five sentences about your next quarter at work: three plans with going to and two predictions or promises with will. Use deadline, goal, and outcome at least once each.
