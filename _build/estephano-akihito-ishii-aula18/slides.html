Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

It Works

Present Simple -- describing what apps do

Grammar Rule

Add the -s

I / You / They

verb -- NO -s

"I download a lesson."

"They update the app."

It / The app

verb + -s

"It downloads a lesson."

"The app saves my files."

Key idea: the app = it. With it, the verb gets an -s: it works, it helps, it saves.

The Negative

What It Does Not Do

To say what the app does NOT do, we use does not:

"The app does not delete my files."

"The software does not work offline."

Question form:

"Does the app work on my phone?" -- Yes, it does. / "Does it save my files?" -- Yes, it does.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

WRONG

"The app work on my phone."

RIGHT

"The app works on my phone."

With it or the app, the verb always needs an -s.

Grammar Practice

Add the -s?

Say the correct verb before you reveal the answer.

1. The app (work) on my phone.

The app works on my phone. (it = +s)

2. I (download) a new lesson every day.

I download a new lesson every day. (I = no -s)

3. The software (save) my files in a folder.

The software saves my files in a folder. (it = +s)

4. It (not delete) my old files.

It does not delete my old files. (negative = does not + verb)
