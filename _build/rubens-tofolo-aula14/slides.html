Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Opinions and would rather for preferences

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we give an opinion and say what we prefer?

"I think the workshop was the best session." (opinion)

"In my view, communication matters most." (opinion)

"I would rather attend the workshop." (preference + base verb)

"I prefer small groups to big lectures." (prefer A to B)

Grammar Rule

Opinions & Preferences

PatternExample
Opinion: I think / I believe + sentenceI think the data was clear.
Opinion: In my view / In my opinion + sentenceIn my view, the workshop was useful.
Preference: prefer + noun (+ to + noun)I prefer workshops to lectures.
Preference: would rather + base verbI would rather discuss real cases.

Key idea: introduce an opinion with I think or in my view. For preferences, use prefer + noun (I prefer A to B) or would rather + base verb (I would rather go, not "to go").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I would rather to attend the workshop, and I am agree with you."
"I would rather attend the workshop, and I agree with you."

After would rather, use the base verb (no "to"). And agree is a verb: say I agree, not "I am agree".
