Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Opinion -- what a person thinks or believes about something
"In my opinion, the workshop was excellent." (Opinião)
Agree -- to have the same idea or opinion as someone
"I agree with you; communication matters." (Concordar)
Disagree -- to have a different idea or opinion from someone
"I disagree, but I respect your view." (Discordar)
Point of view -- the way one person sees or understands a topic
"I see your point of view, but I am not sure." (Ponto de vista)
Prefer -- to like one thing more than another
"I prefer the practical workshops." (Preferir)
Would rather -- a polite way to say you prefer to do something
"I would rather discuss real cases." (Preferir / preferiria)
Reason -- the fact that explains why you think or do something
"My reason is that patients learn faster." (Motivo / razão)
Depend -- to change according to the situation
"It depends on the topic of the talk." (Depender)
Suppose -- to think something is true, but not be totally sure
"I suppose both sessions were useful." (Supor / acho que)
In my view -- a polite phrase to introduce your opinion
"In my view, the workshop was the best." (Na minha opinião)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

At the panel lunch, Rubens and a colleague share their views on the morning. In my view, says Rubens, the communication workshop was the best session. The colleague agrees: "I prefer it to the lecture on new devices." For the afternoon, Rubens says, "I would rather attend the case discussion, because I learn more from real cases." The colleague sees his point of view but prefers the open panel. "I suppose it depends on the topic," she says. So they decide to go to both and compare notes. They share opinions with respect, and that is the best way to learn.

1. Which morning session does Rubens think was the best?
A The communication workshop.
B The lecture on new devices.
C The open panel debate.
2. Which afternoon session would Rubens rather attend, and why?
A The open panel, because it is lively.
B The case discussion, because he learns more from real cases.
C The lecture, because it is shorter.
3. How do Rubens and the colleague share their opinions?
A They argue and never agree.
B With respect, giving reasons and asking each other.
C They avoid the topic completely.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- opinions & would rather

GRAMMAR

Para dar uma opinião, use I think / in my view + frase. Para preferência, use prefer + substantivo (I prefer A to B) ou would rather + verbo na base (sem to). / To give an opinion, use I think / in my view + sentence. For preferences, use prefer + noun or would rather + base verb.

Pattern / PadrãoExample / Exemplo
Opinion / OpiniãoIn my view, the workshop was the best. / Na minha opinião, o workshop foi o melhor.
Prefer + noun / Preferir + substantivoI prefer workshops to lectures. / Prefiro workshops a palestras.
Would rather + base verbI would rather discuss real cases. / Eu preferiria discutir casos reais.
Agree / disagree (verbs)I agree / I disagree. / Eu concordo / discordo.

Key point / Ponto-chave: depois de would rather, use o verbo na base, sem to (I would rather go, não "to go"). Agree é um verbo: diga I agree, não "I am agree". Com prefer, ligue as duas opções com to (A to B), não "than".
