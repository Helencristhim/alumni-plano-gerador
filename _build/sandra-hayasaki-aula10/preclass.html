Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Conversation -- a talk between two or more people
"We had a long conversation over coffee." (conversa)
Respond -- to say or do something back; to answer
"She always responds with a kind word." (responder)
React -- to show your feelings about what happens
"How did you react to the good news?" (reagir)
Clarify -- to explain something so it becomes clear
"Could you clarify what you mean?" (esclarecer)
Express -- to say what you feel or think
"It is good to express your opinion clearly." (expressar)
Naturally -- in a relaxed, easy way, not forced
"After practice, you speak more naturally." (naturalmente)
Comfortable -- relaxed and at ease; not nervous
"I feel comfortable speaking English now." (confortável/à vontade)
Follow up -- to ask more or check again later
"I will follow up with a question next week." (dar seguimento/perguntar depois)

For Mark, a good conversation starts with listening. When the other person speaks, he does not think about his answer; he just listens, and then he responds naturally. He likes to express his own opinion, but he also likes to follow up later, because a small question shows that he cares.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Your Block 2 Toolkit

GRAMMAR

Esta aula é uma revisão — sem gramática nova. Você junta as quatro ferramentas do Bloco 2 numa conversa de verdade: opiniões, comparativos, conselhos (should/must) e descrições (relative clauses: who/which/that). / This is a review — no new grammar. You bring together the four tools from Block 2 in one real conversation: opinions, comparatives, advice (should/must) and describing (relative clauses: who/which/that).

Tool (use it to...)Example
Opinions — share what you think"In my opinion, this is the best choice."
Comparatives — say what is better or best"Coffee is stronger than tea."
Advice — say what to do (or not)"You should rest; you must not work so late."
Describing — add detail about a person/place"She is the friend who always helps me."

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor frase para cada momento de uma conversa real.

A friend asks if you like your job. You want a real answer, not just "yes". What do you say?
A "Yes."
B "Yes, I do. In my opinion, it is the best part of my week. What about you?"
C "I do not know."
You did not understand a fast speaker. What is the best thing to do?
A "Sorry, could you clarify that for me?"
B Pretend you understood and say nothing.
C Change the subject quickly.
A friend told you about a problem on Monday. How do you follow up on Friday?
A Say nothing about it.
B "How did that problem from Monday go in the end?"
C "Did something happen on Monday?"
