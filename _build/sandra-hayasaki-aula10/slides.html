Your Toolkit

Four Tools, When to Use Them

ToolUse it to...Example
Opinionsshare what you think"In my opinion..."
Comparingsay what is better or best"...is better than..."
Advicesay what to do (or not)"You should...", "You must not..."
Describingadd detail about a person or thing"the friend who...", "a place that..."

Tip: a real conversation mixes all four. You give an opinion, you compare, you give advice, and you describe, all in the same talk.

Common Mistake

More Than One Word

-- Do you like your job? -- Yes.
-- Do you like your job? -- Yes, I do. In my opinion, it is the best part of my week. What about you?

A real answer has a reason and a follow-up question. That is how a conversation keeps going.

Turn an Answer Into a Conversation

Artefact

Mark's Conversation Notes

MY NOTES -- HOW TO TALK WELL

Four small habits for a real conversation

1. Listen, then respond — do not just wait for my turn.

2. React with my face and voice, so the other person feels heard.

3. If something is not clear, ask them to clarify. Never pretend.

4. Express my own opinion, but stay comfortable and speak naturally.

5. Later, follow up — a small question shows I care.
