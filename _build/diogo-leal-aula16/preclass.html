Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Say it out loud after the audio.

Align -- to make sure everybody has the same picture
"Let us align on what we deliver in June." (Alinhar)
Upfront -- at the beginning, before the work starts
"I want to be upfront: the reports are not included." (Desde o início / de cara)
Deliverable -- a concrete thing you promise to hand over
"There are three deliverables in this phase." (Entregável)
Ground rules -- the rules of how we will work together
"Two ground rules: no changes after Friday, and one decision-maker." (Regras básicas)
Sign off -- to approve something formally, in writing
"Who signs off on the design?" (Aprovar formalmente)
Ownership -- being the person responsible for something
"Ownership of the test data is on your side." (Responsabilidade / dono)
Mutual -- shared by both sides
"This has to be a mutual agreement, not my decision." (Mútuo)
Boundaries -- the limits of what you will and will not do
"Clear boundaries protect the relationship." (Limites)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Every scope fight is born in silence, on day one. So Diogo is upfront: "Let me align with you on what is in and what is out." In scope: the migration, the training, and two standard deliverables. Out of scope in this phase: the custom reports. Sophia assumed they were included -- and that assumption, in silence, would have become a fight in September. He does not say a flat no. He says "not in this phase", and gives the phase. Then he proposes two ground rules: no new requests after Friday, and one decision-maker. The client has a committee, but ownership has to be with one person -- one name, one person who signs off on the design. Clear boundaries are not aggression: they protect both sides. And the agreement has to be mutual, in writing, the same day.

1. Why does Diogo say what is OUT of scope on day one?
A Because a silent assumption becomes a fight four months later.
B Because he does not want to do the extra work.
C Because the client asked him to.
2. Which sentence is correct?
A We will deliver the reports in June.
B We will delivery the reports in June.
C We will to deliver the reports in June.
3. What does he ask about the sign-off?
A That one person, not a committee, approves the design.
B That the whole committee approves everything.
C That nobody has to approve anything.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- In, Out, Agreed

GRAMMAR

Alinhar expectativa no dia um: o que entra, o que NÃO entra, e quem aprova. / Setting expectations on day one.

MoveEstrutura / StructureExemplo / Example
Incluir / IncludeX is / are in scopeThe migration is in scope.
Excluir / ExcludeY is / are out of scope + we will not + verboThe reports are out of scope in this phase.
Fechar / Close itCan we agree on + substantivo? / Let us agree on...Can we agree on two ground rules?
Ponto-chave / Key pointDeliver = verbo (entregar); delivery = substantivo (a entrega). Depois de let us vem o verbo puro ("let us agree on"). E nunca diga um não seco: diga not in this phase, e dê a fase.

Stage 3: Pronunciation

Speaking

The five lines of a kickoff. Record each one. Friendly but firm -- limits are not aggression, they are protection.

Let me be upfront about what is in and what is out.
Deixa eu ser direto sobre o que está dentro e o que está fora.
The custom reports are out of scope in this phase.
Os relatórios customizados estão fora do escopo desta fase.
They are not cancelled -- we plan them for the autumn.
Eles não estão cancelados: planejamos para o outono.
Two ground rules: no new requests after Friday, and one decision-maker.
Duas regras básicas: nada novo depois de sexta, e um único decisor.
Can we agree on that today, in writing?
Podemos fechar isso hoje, por escrito?

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Choose the best response for each situation at work.

1. At the kickoff, the strongest opening is:
A "Let me be upfront about what is in and what is out."
B "We will do our best to deliver everything."
C "Let us not talk about limits today."
2. The client assumes extra work is included. You say:
A "They are not cancelled -- they are out of scope in this phase. We can plan them for the autumn."
B "That was never part of the deal."
C "OK, we will find a way." (and you know you cannot)
3. A client committee of five wants to approve everything. You:
A Ask the committee to choose one name, with ownership of the sign-off.
B Accept: five approvals are safer.
C Decide alone, without approval.

Stage 5: Free Production

Reflection

Two recordings. Speak first, do not write. This is the exercise that matters most this week.

Take a real project at Oracle -- one that is starting, or one that went wrong. Run the kickoff out loud, for two minutes: (1) be upfront, (2) what is in scope, (3) what is OUT of scope in this phase, (4) two ground rules, with the reason, (5) who signs off, and by when. Record it.
Now the client answers: "We assumed all of that was included. That was always the deal." Answer out loud. Do not fight about the past, and do not say a flat no -- offer a phase, and close with one decision-maker. Record it.
