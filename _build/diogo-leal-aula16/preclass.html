Join the opening, signposting, data, Q&A, and closing into one smooth, polished talk. New vocabulary: rehearse, pace, flow, transition, poise, engage, deliver, polished. New language: the five moves of a full presentation and the transitions that connect them.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Rehearse -- to practice a talk out loud before the real day
"I rehearse the whole talk three times before the meeting." (Ensaiar)
Pace -- how fast or slow you speak; the speed of the talk
"Keep a calm pace and do not rush the slides." (Ritmo)
Flow -- the smooth way the parts of a talk move together
"Good transitions give the talk a natural flow." (Fluidez)
Transition -- a phrase that moves the talk from one part to the next
"Now, let me show you the numbers is a transition." (Transição)
Poise -- a calm, steady, confident way of standing and speaking
"He handled the hard question with poise." (Serenidade / postura)
Engage -- to keep the audience interested and connected to you
"Look at the room to engage your audience." (Envolver / cativar)
Deliver -- to give a talk to an audience, from start to finish
"I will deliver the full talk on Monday." (Apresentar)
Polished -- smooth and well-prepared, with no rough parts
"After rehearsing, the talk felt polished." (Polido / refinado)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Diogo can now open a talk, signpost the parts, present the data, take questions, and close. Today he joins them into one talk. He will deliver the full presentation about Project Atlas. To make it feel polished, he keeps a calm pace and does not rush. He stands with poise, breathes, and keeps his hands steady. Between each part he uses a clear transition, like "Now, let me show you the numbers", so the talk has a smooth flow and moves like one story. He looks at the room to engage the audience, not at the slides. The night before, he will rehearse the whole talk out loud, three or four times, until the words feel natural.

1. What does Diogo do to keep a calm pace?
A He does not rush and speaks slowly.
B He speaks as fast as he can.
C He skips the slides.
2. Why does Diogo use a clear transition between parts?
A So the talk has a smooth flow and moves like one story.
B So the talk sounds like five separate pieces.
C So he can read every slide aloud.
3. How does Diogo prepare the night before?
A He rehearses the whole talk out loud, three or four times.
B He writes new slides at midnight.
C He does nothing and hopes for the best.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Five Moves of a Full Talk

GRAMMAR

Os cinco movimentos de uma apresentação completa e a transição que liga cada parte. / The five moves of a full talk and the transition that links each part.

Move / MovimentoUse / UsoTransition / Transição
1. Open / AbrirCumprimentar e abrir / Greet and openLet me start with...
2. Signpost / SinalizarDizer as partes / Name the partsFirst... then... finally...
3. Data / DadosMostrar os números / Show the numbersAs you can see in the chart...
4. Q&A / PerguntasReceber perguntas / Take questionsAre there any questions?
5. Close / FecharEncerrar e agradecer / End and thankTo sum up... thank you.
Key point / Ponto-chaveCada movimento é ligado ao seguinte por uma transição. Sem ela, você pula da abertura direto para os dados e o talk fica abrupto. Com ela, a apresentação ganha fluidez e parece uma só história.
