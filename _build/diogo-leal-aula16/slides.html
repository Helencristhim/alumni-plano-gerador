Useful Language

How to Draw the Line

In scope → out of scope → the ground rules → who signs off → the yes in writing

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which one includes? Which one excludes? Which one closes the agreement?

"The migration and the training are in scope."

"The custom reports are out of scope in this phase."

"We will deliver the migration by June 20th. We will not deliver the mobile app."

"Can we agree on two ground rules?"

Grammar Rule

In, Out, Agreed

MoveStructureExample
IncludeX is / are in scopeThe migration is in scope.
Exclude (say it out loud)Y is / are out of scope + we will not + verbThe reports are out of scope. We will not deliver them in June.
Close the agreementCan we agree on + noun? / Let us agree on...Can we agree on two ground rules?

Silence is not politeness -- it is a bill you pay later. Saying what is OUT of scope on day one costs you ten uncomfortable minutes. Not saying it costs you the client, four months later.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We will delivery the reports in June."
"We will deliver the reports in June."
"Let us to agree the ground rules."
"Let us agree on the ground rules."

Deliver é o verbo; delivery é o substantivo (a entrega). E depois de let us vem o verbo puro: "let us agree on" -- nunca "let us to agree".

Dialogue

I Assumed That Was Included

D

"Before we start, let me be upfront about what is in scope and what is not."

S

"We know the scope. Migration, training, reports. Let us move on."

D

"Two standard reports are in. The custom reports are out of scope in this phase."

S

"Wait. I assumed the custom reports were included. The client expects them."

D

"That is exactly why I am saying it now, and not in September. They are not cancelled -- we plan them for the autumn."

S

"Fine. What else are you taking away from me?"

D

"Nothing. But I want two ground rules: no new requests after Friday, and one person who signs off on the design."

S

"The client has a committee. They all want to decide."

D

"Then the committee chooses one name. Ownership has to be with one person. Can we align on that today, in writing?"

She Assumes Everything

"We assumed all the reports were included. That was always the deal."

She pushes twice: first she says the reports were always included, then she refuses to name one decision-maker. Do not fight about the past. Say what is in, what is out, offer a phase for the rest, and do not close without one name.
