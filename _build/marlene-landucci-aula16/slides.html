Chapter 3 -- I Want to Visit

Language Discovery

I want to / I would like to + verb

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common? What comes after "to"?

"I want to visit the cathedral."

"I would like to see the statue."

"We want to take a tour."

"She would like to buy a ticket."

The Rule

I Want to / I Would Like to

Start+ to + verbExample
I wantto visitI want to visit the cathedral.
I would liketo seeI would like to see the statue.
We wantto takeWe want to take a tour.

Key idea: say what you plan with I want to + verb or, more politely, I would like to + verb. The verb after to never changes: visit, see, take, buy.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I want visit the museum."
"I want to visit the museum."

Always use to before the verb: "I want to visit", "I would like to see".

Practice

Complete the Plan

"I _____want to visit the cathedral." (plan)
"I _____would like to see the statue." (polite)
"We _____want to take a tour." (plan)
"I would like _____to buy a ticket." (polite)
Key Phrase

The Plan Phrase

"I want to visit the museum."

To be more polite, say: "I would like to visit the museum." Then ask: "How do I get there?"
