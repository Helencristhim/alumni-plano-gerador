Chapter 3: The Code

Too Much, Too Many, Not Enough

too much spending · too many requests · not enough time · too expensive

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"There is too much spending on accommodation."

"There are too many requests this year."

"We do not have enough money for the whole team."

"There are not enough seats for the full delegation."

"The hotel is too expensive, and the agenda is too long."

too much or too many? not enough or too expensive? What decides which one you use?

StructureUse it forExample
too much + uncountableMore than is good — of something you cannot count (money, spending, time).too much spending
too many + countableMore than is good — of something you can count (requests, meetings, seats).too many requests
not enough + nounLess than you need — something is MISSING. Works for both countable and uncountable.not enough money · not enough seats
too + adjectiveA problem of quality, not quantity. NO "much" here — just too + the adjective.too expensive · too long
The key questionCan you count it? → many. You cannot? → much. Is it an adjective? → just too, no much.too many seats · too much time · too expensive

The trap: "too much expensive" is wrong. With an adjective there is no much — only too expensive. And "not enough" always means something is missing.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"We have too much requests this year."
"We have too many requests this year."
"We do not have too many time to prepare."
"We do not have much time to prepare."
"The hotel is too much expensive."
"The hotel is too expensive."

One question decides all three: can you count it? many for requests, much for time — and with an adjective, just too, never "too much".

Chapter 4: The Budget Call

Bruno Brings the Numbers

Too much? Too many? Not enough? You decide the plan.

Dialogue

The Budget for the Second Trip

B
Maria Clara, here is the budget for the second trip. I have to be honest — it is limited this year.
M
I understand. How much money do we have, and how many people can travel?
B
We have enough for four people, but not enough for the whole delegation. And the spending on accommodation is too high.
M
I agree. The hotel near the center is too expensive. We could find a hotel with a better balance of price and location.
B
Good idea. But we still have too many requests and not enough approvals. Every department wants a seat.
M
Then we need a clear priority. Documentation is first — we can't cut corners with the visas.
B
So should I allocate more money to preparation and less to the extras?
M
Yes. Allocate a little more to the documents, and do not exceed the budget on the dinners. That is the safe plan.
Comprehension

About Bruno

1. What does Bruno say about the budget this year?
It is limited — there is not enough for the whole delegation.
2. According to Bruno, what is too high?
The spending on accommodation, because the hotel is too expensive.
3. What is Bruno's problem with the requests?
There are too many requests and not enough approvals.
