Too Many / Too Much / Not Enough — with Countable, Uncountable, and Adjectives

The exact code of lesson 13, explained slowly and in simple English: too much for what you cannot count, too many for what you can, not enough for what is missing, and too + adjective with no "much". It ends with the one question that decides all of them: can you count it?

Tip: turn on the English subtitles. Pause after each example and say it OUT LOUD before the teacher. Then rebuild each one with your budget: "There is too much spending on...".
