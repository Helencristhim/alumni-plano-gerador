Chapter 3: The Code

Three Futures

going to · will · present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We are going to expand our European portfolio."

"I will send you the rates after the fair."

"We are attending ITB in March."

Plan, decision, or fixed appointment — which future is which?

FormUseExample
going to
am/is/are going to + verb		Plans and intentions decided before nowWe are going to expand.
will
will + base verb		Decisions, offers and promises made nowI will send the rates.
Present Continuous
am/is/are + -ing		Fixed arrangements already bookedWe are attending ITB in March.

After look forward to, use the -ing form: "I look forward to working with you."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We will attending ITB in March."

✓ RIGHT

"We are attending ITB in March."

✗ WRONG

"Perfect — I am going to send the rates right now."

✓ RIGHT

"Perfect — I will send the rates right now."

Fixed appointments use the present continuous. A decision made in the moment (an offer or promise) uses 'will'.

Will or Going to?

going to = a plan · will = a decision made now

"We have already decided: we ___are going to open a new office."
"That is a great idea — I ___will do it right away."
"Our team ___is going to launch a MICE program next year."
"Do not worry — I ___will confirm the rates today."

Plan Out Loud

Role-Play 1 of 3 — Guided

Share Your Plans

Role-Play 2 of 3 — Semi-free

Make the Commitment

Scenario: You are finalizing a deal. Make two offers or promises with 'will' (send rates, confirm dates), and agree on the next step.

Keywords:

I will send... I will confirm... Let us schedule...
Role-Play 3 of 3 — Free

Design Next Season

Scenario: Plan the next season with a partner. Share your goals (going to), the fairs you are attending (present continuous), and make the offers and promises that close the deal (will).

No keywords. The plan is yours!

Bonus Challenge

Three Goals, Sixty Seconds

In one minute, name three goals for the next three months. Use 'going to' for a plan, 'will' for a decision, and the present continuous for something already booked — at least once each.

A clear plan, said out loud, is already half done.
