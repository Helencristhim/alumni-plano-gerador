Chapter 3 -- Reporting the Past

Past Simple + Signposting

Say what happened, in the right order

Discover the Pattern

Listen and Notice

"First, we signed three new contracts."

"Then, our sales increased by fifteen percent."

"After that, our costs decreased by five percent."

"Finally, we closed the biggest deal of the year."

Grammar Rule

Report in the Past

FormUseExample
Regular: verb + -edMost past verbsSales increased, costs decreased
Irregular: was / werePast of "to be"The growth was strong
First, ... / Then, ...Order your pointsFirst, the highlights. Then, the numbers.
Finally, ...Your last pointFinally, my plan for next quarter

Key idea: To report results, use the past simple (it already happened) and organize with signposting: First, Then, After that, Finally.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Last quarter, our sales increase by fifteen percent."
"Last quarter, our sales increased by fifteen percent."

When you report a finished period, use the past simple. Add -ed to regular verbs.

Grammar Practice

Complete the Report

"Last quarter, our sales _____increased by fifteen percent." (increase + ed)
"Our costs _____decreased by five percent." (decrease + ed)
"_____First, the highlights. Then, the numbers." (signposting: order)
"The growth _____was strong in the last three months." (past of "to be")
