Chapter 3 -- The Passive Voice

Grammar Discovery

be + past participle: the action, not the person

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. Who does the action? What do these sentences have in common?

"The report is reviewed every Friday."

"Tickets are assigned at the stand-up."

"The build was tested before launch."

"The runbook was written by the whole team."

The Rule

The Passive Voice

FormStructureExample
Presentam / is / are + past participleThe report is reviewed every Friday.
Present (plural)are + past participleTickets are assigned at the stand-up.
Pastwas / were + past participleThe build was tested before launch.
With the agentbe + past participle + by + personThe runbook was written by the team.

Key idea: Use the passive when the action matters more than the person, or when the person is obvious or unknown. The form is always be + past participle. Add by + person only if you really want to name who did it.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The report reviewed every week."
"The report is reviewed every week."

The passive always needs the verb be: is, are, was, or were, plus the past participle. Do not drop the be.

Grammar Practice

Make it Passive

"Every change _____is documented in the wiki." (present, document)
"Tickets _____are assigned at the stand-up." (present, assign)
"The build _____was tested before the launch." (past, test)
"The process _____was standardized last quarter." (past, standardize)

Comprehension

About the Process

1. What happens before a change goes live?

Every change is documented in the wiki first, and the procedure is followed by everyone.

2. How are tickets and the build handled?

Tickets are assigned at the Monday stand-up, progress is monitored on the board all week, and the build is tested and only approved when every check passes.

3. What was the result of standardizing the process?

Last quarter the process was standardized across all three squads, and incidents were cut in half; the runbook is maintained by everyone.

Artefact

The Handover Document

TEAM HANDOVER -- RELEASE PROCESS

Owner: Rafael Pelizaro

STEP 1 -- DOCUMENT

Every change is documented in the wiki before it goes live. (present passive)

STEP 2 -- ASSIGN

Tickets are assigned to an owner at the Monday stand-up.

STEP 3 -- MONITOR

Progress is monitored on the board all week.

STEP 4 -- TEST & APPROVE

The build is tested by the quality team and is approved only when every check passes.

RESULT -- STANDARDIZED (past passive)

Last quarter this process was standardized across all three squads, and incidents were cut in half.

OWNERSHIP -- with the agent (by)

The runbook was written by the whole team, so it is maintained by everyone.

Dialogue

The Handover with a New Teammate

C

"Hi, Rafael. It is my first day. How does the release procedure work here?"

R

"Welcome. It is simple. Every change is documented in the wiki, and tickets are assigned at the stand-up."

C

"And before we ship? How is the build checked and approved?"

R

"The build is tested by the quality team, and it is approved only when every check passes. Then it is monitored for two days."

C

"That is very clear. And who maintains the runbook?"

R

"It is maintained by everyone, not just me. Last quarter the process was standardized across the squads, so nothing depends on one person."
