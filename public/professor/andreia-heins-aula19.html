Language Focus

You Could Have, I Would Have

Past modals to give soft, constructive feedback about the past.

Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You could have explained the risk earlier." (modal + have + past participle)

"We should have tested the console first." (a better past choice)

"I would have asked for more time." (what I would do)

"Next time, you could ask the team sooner." (soft suggestion ahead)

Language Rule

Past Modals

ModalFormExample
could havecould have + past participleYou could have explained it earlier.
should haveshould have + past participleWe should have tested it first.
would havewould have + past participleI would have asked for more time.
Negativeshould not have + past participleWe should not have rushed the report.

Key idea: Past modals talk about a past action that did not happen. The form is modal + have + past participle. Could have = a possible or better past choice; should have = the right advice for the past; would have = what I personally would have done. They make feedback soft and polite. Compare: "you could ask" (a future suggestion) vs "you could have asked" (about the past).

Watch Out

Watch Out

✗ "You could have explain the risk earlier."

✓ "You could have explained the risk earlier." (have + past participle)

✗ "We should of tested it first."

✓ "We should have tested it first." (have, not of)

About the Briefing

1. What should Andreia start the feedback with?

She should start with praise -- a real strength of the team -- before any suggestion.

2. How should she give the difficult part?

Softly, with past modals: "You could have explained the risk earlier." One clear suggestion, not a long list.

3. What framework does David suggest, and how should she end?

Strengths first, then one suggestion to improve. End by being honest but kind, and acknowledge the team's perspective.

Artefact

The Feedback Note

To: Surgical Team  ·  From: Andreia Heins  ·  Subject: Project Review -- Console Rollout

Dear team,

Praise: First, your biggest strength was clear communication during the rollout. Thank you.

Perspective: From my perspective, the timing was the one thing to improve.

Suggestion: You could have explained the risk earlier, and we should have tested the console first.

Framework: Next time, let us use a simple framework: strengths first, then one constructive suggestion.

Honest & kind: I will always be honest but kind. I acknowledge your hard work, and I am happy to address any questions.
