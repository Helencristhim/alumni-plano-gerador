The Trip That Changed the Season

The Trip That Changed the Season

Last November, Andrea attended WTM in London, one of the biggest travel fairs in the world. She went with a clear goal: to find new hotel partners for the agency's European itineraries. The first two days were a real challenge. The halls were huge, the schedule was tight, and many of the exhibitors she wanted to meet were fully booked.

On the third day, everything changed. Andrea met Ricardo, a tour operator from Lisbon, and they immediately found common ground. He invited her on a short fam trip to Portugal the following week. There, she did a full hotel inspection of six properties, negotiated the rates, and agreed on a fair commission. The negotiation was tough, but in the end it turned out perfectly.

By the time she flew home, Andrea had signed three new contracts. The highlight, she says, was not the paperwork. It was the moment she realized her English no longer got in the way. 'A year ago, I would have stayed quiet at that fair,' she says. 'This time, I talked, I asked, I negotiated -- and I ended up with the best results of my career.'

Adapted from a travel-industry profile, Voices in Tourism

Notice the past forms: regular verbs add -ed (attended, signed, ended, turned), while many common verbs are irregular (go -> went, meet -> met, do -> did, fly -> flew). 'Turn out' means to happen in a certain way in the end; 'end up' means to finally be in a place or situation.

Chapter 4: The Code

The Past Simple

I attended · I went · Did you...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I attended WTM last November."

"We signed a new contract in Portugal."

"I went on a fam trip to Porto."

"Did you meet any new suppliers?"

Which verbs just add -ed, and which change completely?

FormUseExample
Regular past
verb + -ed		Finished action at a finished timeWe signed the contract.
Irregular past
special form		Common verbs change completelyI went / met / did / flew.
Questions & negatives
did + base verb		Use 'did' + the base form (no -ed)Did you meet them? / I did not go.

Common irregulars: go → went, meet → met, do → did, fly → flew, take → took.

Language Focus

Regular or Irregular?

AnalyseRegular or irregular past?
ALast year, we signed a new contract.
BI went to WTM in November.
CThe negotiation turned out perfectly.
DShe met a tour operator from Lisbon.
Notice: regular verbs (sign -> signed, turn -> turned) simply add -ed. Irregular verbs change completely (go -> went, meet -> met, do -> did). In questions and negatives, the past tense moves to 'did': Did you sign the contract? -- not 'Did you signed'.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I go to WTM last year."

✓ RIGHT

"I went to WTM last year."

✗ WRONG

"Did you signed the contract?"

✓ RIGHT

"Did you sign the contract?"

A finished time (last year) needs the past simple. After 'did', always use the base verb, never the -ed form.
