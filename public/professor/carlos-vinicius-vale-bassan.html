Every day, he assesses risks and opportunities in corporate transactions. He oversees a team of twelve consultants across three offices. Last quarter, he briefed the board on a new acquisition strategy. His team drives value creation in every deal. Carlos is known as an articulate communicator who can align complex stakeholder interests. He leverages data analytics to improve deal outcomes. Recently, he delivered a compelling pitch to a major private equity fund. Over the past decade, his practice has driven over two billion dollars in deal value.

1. What does Carlos do every day?
A He delivers pitches to private equity funds.
B He assesses risks and opportunities in corporate transactions.
C He has driven over two billion dollars in deal value.
2. Which sentence describes a completed action in the past?
A He leads the M&A advisory practice.

3. Why is Carlos described as "articulate"?
A Because he works in São Paulo.
B Because he leverages data analytics.
C Because he can express complex ideas clearly and align stakeholder interests.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are always true)
Past Simple = completed actions at a specific time in the past

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"He leads the M&A practice."
Past SimpleSubject + verb (past form)"He briefed the board last quarter."

Common Mistake:

"He lead the team yesterday."

"He led the team yesterday."

The past simple of "lead" is "led" (irregular verb). Do not confuse with "lead" (present).

Present Simple vs. Past Simple in executive contexts
Look at the highlighted words
"He leads the M&A advisory practice for the Americas."
"She oversees a team of twelve consultants."
"He briefed the board on the acquisition strategy last quarter."
"Our practice has driven over two billion dollars in deal value."

What do the highlighted words have in common?

TenseFormUse
Present Simpleleads, overseesFacts, routines, current roles
Past Simplebriefed, droveCompleted actions at a specific time
Present Perfecthas drivenPast action with present relevance
The Rule: When to Use Each
PRESENT SIMPLE
Facts, habits, current roles
"I lead the M&A practice."
"He assesses risks daily."
Subject + verb (base form / +s for he/she/it)
PAST SIMPLE
Completed actions at a specific time
"He briefed the board last quarter."
"We assessed the target company."
Subject + verb (past form / -ed or irregular)
Common Mistake
"He lead the team yesterday."
"He led the team yesterday."

Spot the Error

"He lead the team to a successful deal last year."

"He led the team to a successful deal last year." (led = past of lead)

"She assess the risks every quarter."

"She assesses the risks every quarter." (3rd person +es)

"We drived the integration forward."

"We drove the integration forward." (drove = past of drive)

"I need to brief the CEO in the deal updates."

"I need to brief the CEO on the deal updates." (brief someone ON something)
