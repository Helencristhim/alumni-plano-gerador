Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are always true)
Past Simple = completed actions at a specific time in the past

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"He leads the M&A practice."
Past SimpleSubject + verb (past form)"He briefed the board last quarter."

Common Mistake:

"He lead the team yesterday."

"He led the team yesterday."

The past simple of "lead" is "led" (irregular verb). Do not confuse with "lead" (present).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip: Present Perfect for Professional Achievements

Grammar
Present Perfect — For achievements and experiences connected to NOW
StructureExample
Subject + have/has + past participleI have specialized in M&A for over ten years.
We have delivered results in twenty transactions.
She has navigated complex deals across three continents.
When to use Present Perfect vs. Past Simple
Present PerfectPast Simple
No specific time: "I have worked in M&A."Specific time: "I joined Accenture in 2024."
Duration until now: "I have specialized for ten years."Completed: "I led that project last year."
Common Mistake
✗ "I have joined Accenture in 2024." (specific time → Past Simple)
✓ "I joined Accenture in 2024."
✗ "I worked in M&A for ten years." (still ongoing → Present Perfect)
✓ "I have worked in M&A for ten years."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip: Question Forms for Professional Small Talk

Grammar

Three types of questions for professional conversations:

Type Structure Example
Open questions What / How / Why + auxiliary + subject + verb "What brings you to the conference?"
"How did you get into M&A?"
Follow-up questions What do you mean by...? / Could you tell me more about...? "What do you mean by that?"
"Could you tell me more about your approach?"
Indirect questions I was wondering if... / Do you happen to know...? "I was wondering if you are involved in M&A."
"Do you happen to know the speaker?"

Common Mistake:

"What means this?" → "What does this mean?"

In English questions, the auxiliary verb (do/does/did) comes before the subject.

The Rule: Present Perfect
StructureExampleWhen to Use
have/has + past participle"I have specialized in M&A for over ten years."Achievements connected to NOW
"We have delivered results in twenty transactions."Duration until now
"She has navigated deals across three continents."Experience (no specific time)
Present PerfectPast Simple
"I have worked in M&A." (no specific time)"I joined Accenture in 2024." (specific time)
"I have specialized for ten years." (still true)"I led that project last year." (completed)
Common Mistake
"I have joined Accenture in 2024."
"I joined Accenture in 2024."
"I worked in M&A for ten years." (still true)
"I have worked in M&A for ten years."

Question Forms That Build Rapport
What do these have in common?
What brings you to the conference?
How did you get into M&A?
Could you tell me more about your approach?
I was wondering if you are involved in fintech.

Three question types for professional small talk:

OpenWh- word + auxiliary + subject + verb"What brings you here?"
Follow-upCould you + verb / What do you mean by...?"Could you tell me more?"
IndirectI was wondering if + subject + verb (no inversion)"I was wondering if you work in fintech."
Common Mistake
"What means this?"
"What does this mean?"
"Where you work?"
"Where do you work?"
"I wonder if are you in M&A."
"I was wondering if you are in M&A." (no inversion)
Grammar Practice
Transform into correct question forms. Click to reveal.

Open: You do what for a living? → ___"What do you do for a living?"

Follow-up: Tell me more. (polite) → ___"Could you tell me more about that?"

Indirect: Are you in fintech? (polite) → ___"I was wondering if you are in fintech."

Open: You got into this field how? → ___"How did you get into this field?"
