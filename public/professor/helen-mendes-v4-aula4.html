Brainstorming -- Listening for Gist

Listen: A Meeting Update

Bruno speaks at a project meeting. Listen once for the general idea — do not read the transcript yet.

Which sentence best summarizes Bruno's contribution?

A. Bruno wants to cancel the app project completely.
B. Bruno agrees the team needs more testing time, but disagrees about a December launch, and suggests early November.
C. Bruno thinks the meeting should end now and the budget is not important.
Answer key — click to reveal
B — he agrees about more testing, disagrees about December, and proposes an early-November launch.
Show transcript (after listening)
“Hi everyone, thanks for joining. The main item on our agenda today is the launch date for the new app. I want to share my point of view. First, I agree with Sofia that we need more time for testing. That is a good suggestion, and I think it makes the product stronger. However, I have to disagree with the idea of moving the launch to December. December is a very busy month for our clients, so I am not sure we are on the same page there. My suggestion is simple. Let us launch in early November and keep two extra weeks for testing before that. I think this is a good decision for the whole team. Shall we agree on this and move on to the budget? Thank you.”

Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Agreeing & Disagreeing Politely

Agree“I agree with you.”
Praise an idea“That is a good suggestion.”
Disagree politely“I see your point, but I am not sure about that.”
Propose a decision“Shall we make a decision and move on?”
1. Which line lets you disagree in a soft, polite way?
“I see your point, but I am not sure about that.” You agree a little first, then disagree gently.
2. How can you praise someone's idea before you add your own?
“That is a good suggestion.” Start positive, then say your view.
3. What does “Shall we...?” do in a meeting?
It makes a friendly suggestion to the group — here, to make a decision and move on.
4. Why is it important to disagree politely at work?
To keep a good relationship and sound professional — you disagree with the idea, not the person.

Your Turn -- Production

Argue For or Against

The motion: “All our team meetings should be held in English.” You are in a leadership meeting and it is your turn to speak.

Step 1 — Decide: Do you argue FOR or AGAINST the motion?
Step 2 — Argue: Give 2-3 reasons for your side. Use your real role and company, agree with one point, disagree politely with another, and finish by proposing a decision.

USEFUL LANGUAGE

My point of view is ...I agree that ...That's a good suggestionI see your point, but ...I have to disagree because ...My suggestion is ...Shall we ...?Let's move on

Then discuss

1. In real meetings, do you speak up more when you agree or when you disagree? Why?
2. What is one rule you follow to disagree without hurting the relationship?
