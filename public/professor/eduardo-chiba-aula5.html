  • Dificuldade em small talk e transições naturais em conversas

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Review

Vocabulary

These are the key words from Block 1. Review the meaning and click Listen to check your pronunciation.

Value Proposition -- the clear reason why a customer should choose your product or service
"Our value proposition is connecting agencies to millions of renters."
Competitive Advantage -- the thing that makes your company better than its competitors
"Our competitive advantage is our seamless technology integration."
Pipeline -- the series of stages in a business process or sales funnel
"We have a strong pipeline of potential partners for next quarter."
Onboarding -- the process of integrating new partners or employees
"The onboarding process for new brokers takes about two weeks."
Milestone -- an important achievement or event in a project
"We reached an important milestone when we signed our hundredth agency."
Revenue -- the total income generated by a business
"Our revenue has grown significantly since we launched the B2B unit."
Benchmark -- a standard or point of reference used to compare performance
"Our churn rate is well below the industry benchmark."
Follow-up -- a message or action that continues a previous conversation
"I will send a follow-up email after our meeting today."

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Your elevator pitch uses all four Block 1 structures. Keep this table in mind.

StructureUse it forExample
Present simple / continuousWho you are, what you do now"I am Head of B2B. We are scaling."
ComparativesWhy you are better"faster / more scalable than..."
Modals (could/would)Polite asks"Could we schedule a call?"
Present perfectResults that matter now"We have grown by 40%."

Watch out: use present perfect with "this year / this quarter" (period not finished), but past simple with "last year". And never say "more faster" — just "faster".

Chapter 5: Practice

Build It Block by Block

Block 1 — Say who you are and your role.
"I'm Eduardo Chiba, Head of B2B at Quinto Andar. We connect agencies to millions of renters."
Block 2 — State your value proposition.
"Our value proposition is speed: agencies can list properties in twenty-four hours."
Block 2 — Say what makes you better (comparative).
"Our platform is faster and more scalable than traditional agencies."
Block 4 — Give the proof (present perfect + data).
"We have grown our partner network by forty percent this year, well above the benchmark."
Block 3 — Make the ask (polite request).
"Could we schedule a call this week to discuss a partnership?"
Close — Offer a follow-up.
"I'll send a follow-up email today with all the details."
