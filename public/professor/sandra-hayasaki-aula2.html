Chapter 3

Two Pasts

Past simple and present perfect

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Finished moment, or life experience?

"Last year, I traveled abroad for the first time."

"I have traveled abroad, and it changed me."

"I have not become fluent yet, but I am close."

One has a clear, finished time. The others connect the past to now.

Grammar Rule

The Two Pasts

TenseUseExample
Past simplefinished moment, clear time"Last year I traveled abroad."
Present perfectlife experience, time not stated"I have traveled abroad."
Present perfect + evera question about your whole life"Have you ever traveled abroad?"
Present perfect + already / yetbefore now / up to now"I have already tried. I have not done it yet."

Two pasts, two stories:

Use the past simple for a finished moment with a clear time — "I started last year." Use the present perfect (have / has + past participle) for a life experience when the exact time does not matter — "I have started a new chapter." Keep ever, already, yet, and lately with the present perfect.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have traveled abroad last year."

✓ RIGHT

"I traveled abroad last year."

✗ WRONG

"I have went to many places."

✓ RIGHT

"I have gone to many places."

With a finished time like "last year", use the past simple (I traveled), not the present perfect. And use the past participle after "have": "gone", not "went".

Chapter 4

Sandra's Story

A warm talk about a life so far

Dialogue

My Story So Far

L
Sandra, I would love to hear your story. Have you ever traveled abroad?
S
Yes! Last year I took my first trip abroad. I ordered food in English, and people understood me.
L
That is wonderful. And when did you start learning English?
S
A few years ago. Back then I knew only a few words. I have made many mistakes, but each one has taught me something. It was a hard experience.
L
Mistakes are part of every story. What have you done lately?
S
I have already done things I never thought I could do. But I have not become fluent yet.
L
You will get there. So what do you want to accomplish next?
S
I want to accomplish a real conversation. My biggest achievement is the courage to keep speaking, even when it is hard.

Sharpen It

The two pasts, at your own pace

Quick Fire

Say It in One Line

1 / 5

1. Talk about a finished moment with a clear time (past simple).

"Last year I traveled abroad for the first time."

2. Talk about a life experience with no exact time (present perfect).

"I have traveled abroad, and it changed me."

3. Ask a friend about their whole life (with ever).

"Have you ever lived in another city?"

"I have not become fluent yet, but I am close."

"I have learned to keep speaking, and that is my achievement."

Detective

Spot the Error

0 / 4 errors found

"I have traveled abroad yesterday."
"I traveled abroad yesterday." (a finished time needs the past simple, not the present perfect)
"I have went to many wonderful places."
"I have gone to many wonderful places." (use the past participle after "have": gone, not went)
"Did you ever been to another country?"
"Have you ever been to another country?" (life-experience questions use have + past participle)
"I did not finish my story yet."
"I have not finished my story yet." (with "yet", use the present perfect)
