Jornada de Transformação

De: Profissional que já consegue se apresentar e contar a própria história (Aulas 1–7), mas trava quando o OUTRO fala — não sabe reagir, demonstrar interesse nem puxar a conversa adiante, e o papo morre.

Para: Networker que mantém qualquer conversa viva: reage com naturalidade, demonstra interesse genuíno e faz perguntas de follow-up — a habilidade que transforma um "oi" num contato real em eventos internacionais.

Promessa da Aula 08

After this lesson, Daniela will keep a networking conversation going — reacting, showing interest, and asking natural follow-up questions (What kind of...? How long...? Where exactly...?) so the other person wants to keep talking.

Forças

  • Já se apresenta e conta a própria história com segurança (Aulas 1–7)
  • Domina past simple — base para perguntas de follow-up (Did you...? How long...?)
  • Motivação concreta — eventos e vagas internacionais
  • Escuta atenta de gestora — bom ponto de partida para active listening

Pontos de Melhoria

  • Conversas morrem — responde mas não devolve a pergunta
  • Pouco repertório de reações ("Oh, really?", "That is interesting!")
  • Confunde interested / interesting
  • Medo de "puxar assunto" e parecer invasiva

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Read the rule carefully and study the examples.

Follow-up Questions & Active Listening

To keep a conversation going, do three things: react, then ask a follow-up question, then ask for a detail. Follow-up questions start with What kind of...?, How long...?, Where exactly...?, or Why...?. Short "echo" questions (Oh, really? Did you?) show you are listening.

ToolExampleWhen to use
React"Oh, really?" / "That is interesting!" / "Wow!"right after they speak
Follow-up"What kind of...?" / "How long...?" / "Where exactly...?"to learn more
Echo question"Did you?" / "Do you?" / "Really?"to show you listen
Ask a detail"Tell me more." / "Can you tell me one more detail?"to keep it open
Common Mistake:
"What you do?" ✗ → "What kind of work do you do?" ✓
"I am very interesting in your job." ✗ → "I am very interested in your job." ✓
A flat "Ok." kills a conversation. React first, then ask a follow-up — and remember: people are interested, topics are interesting.

Artifact

The Keep-It-Going Toolkit

KEEP-IT-GOING TOOLKIT

Four Steps

1. React

Show your feelings. "Oh, really? That is interesting!"

2. Ask a Follow-up

Ask for more. "What kind of project was it?"

3. Ask a Detail

Go deeper. "How long did it take? Tell me more."

4. Keep it Open

Stay curious; do not change the topic too fast. "I am really curious about this."

Use this shape at every networking event.
Dialogue

At the Coffee Break

S
Hi! I do not think we have met. I am Sophie, from the Berlin office.
D
Nice to meet you, Sophie! I am Daniela, from Sao Paulo. So, what do you do in Berlin?
S
I work in logistics. Last month, we opened a new distribution center.
D
Oh, really? That is interesting! What kind of center is it?
S
It is fully automated. It was a big challenge, but the result was great.
D
Wow, that sounds great! How long did the project take?
S
About eight months. Honestly, the deadline was very tight.
D
I can imagine. You mentioned automation. Can you tell me one more detail?
S
Sure! The robots move the boxes, so the team works faster now.
D
That is amazing. I am really curious about this topic. Let us keep in touch!
Comprehension

About the Conversation

1. What does Sophie do, and what did her team open?
She works in logistics. Last month, her team opened a new, fully automated distribution center.
2. Which follow-up questions did Daniela ask?
"What kind of center is it?" and "How long did the project take?" — plus "Can you tell me one more detail?"
3. How did Daniela show she was interested?
She reacted ("Oh, really? That is interesting! Wow!"), asked for details, and stayed curious.
