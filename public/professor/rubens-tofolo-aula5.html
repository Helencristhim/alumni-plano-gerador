Vocabulary

"People in the North are very welcoming." (Acolhedor)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Comparatives & Superlatives

GRAMMAR

Comparar duas coisas e dizer o nº 1 do grupo. / Compare two things and say the number one.

AdjetivoComparativo / ComparativeSuperlativo / Superlative
curto: hot, bighotter / bigger than (mais ... que)the hottest / biggest (o mais ...)
longo: lively, affordablemore lively thanthe most lively
irregular: good, badbetter / worse than (melhor/pior que)the best / the worst (o melhor/pior)

Chapter 3

Comparing Worlds

bigger than · more lively than · the best

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Belém is hotter than Lisbon."

"Lisbon is more compact than Belém."

"The Amazon is the largest forest in the world."

"Belém has the best açaí in Brazil."

AdjectiveComparativeSuperlative
short: hot, big, largehotter / bigger thanthe hottest / biggest
long: lively, crowded, affordablemore lively thanthe most lively
irregular: good, badbetter / worse thanthe best / the worst

Compare two things with "than". For number one in a group, use "the ...est / the most".

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Belém is more better."

✓ RIGHT

"Belém is better."

✗ WRONG

"It is the most big market."

✓ RIGHT

"It is the biggest market."
