Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Adverbs of Frequency tell us how often something happens:

always (100%) — I always check my emails.
usually (80%) — I usually start at 8 AM.
often (60%) — I often attend meetings.
sometimes (40%) — I sometimes work late.
rarely (10%) — I rarely miss deadlines.
never (0%) — I never work on Sundays.

Position: Before the main verb ("I usually start at 8") but after "be" ("I am always on time").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

There is / There are — used to say what exists or is present:

There is + singular noun — "There is a meeting room on each floor."
There are + plural noun — "There are fifty employees in our department."
Is there...? — "Is there a branch in Rio?"
Are there...? — "Are there any international clients?"
There isn't — "There isn't a branch in Europe."
There aren't — "There aren't any offices in Asia."

Key Rule: Use is with singular nouns and are with plural nouns.

GRAMMAR

Form / FormaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo

Chapter 3: The Code

Grammar Discovery

Look at the blue words. What do they have in common?

I work at TVT. I manage the sales team.
He deals with technology clients every day.
My team has ten experienced people.
Our clients are large international companies.
FormStructureExample
Affirmative (I/You/We/They)Subject + verbI work at TVT.
Affirmative (He/She/It)Subject + verb + sHe works at TVT.
NegativeSubject + do/does not + verbI do not work on weekends.
QuestionDo/Does + subject + verb?Do you work at TVT?
The Rule

Present Simple: The Pattern

I / You / We / They + verb (base form)
"I work at TVT." / "They deal with clients."
He / She / It + verb + s
"He works at TVT." / "She manages the team."
Negative: do not / does not + verb
"I do not work on weekends." / "He does not work on Saturdays."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"He work at TVT."
"He works at TVT."

Remember: He / She / It always needs the -s ending in Present Simple.
