Watch Out

Common Mistake

If it will arrive damaged, I would reimburse you.
If it arrives damaged, I will reimburse you.

In the first conditional, use the present after if and will in the result. Keep the structures separate when you mix them: present + will for a likely future, past + would for an imagined one.

Practice

Name the Structure

"If the order is late, we _____ you the moment it ships." → will tell (first conditional)
"In the US you _____ get straight to the point in a meeting." → should (modal of advice)
"_____ you tell me when the next collection is ready?" → Could (polite indirect question)
Karen: "The timing is perfect." → She said the timing _____ perfect. → was (reported speech, present → past)

Spot the Error

Fix the Line

"If it will arrive late, I would tell you."
"If it arrives late, I will tell you." (first conditional: present + will)
"Could you tell me when does it ship?"
"Could you tell me when it ships?" (indirect question: no inversion)
"She said me the timing was right."
"She told me the timing was right." (tell needs a person; say does not)
"He told me he will expand next quarter."
"He told me he would expand next quarter." (will → would when reported)
