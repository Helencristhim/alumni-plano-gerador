Chapter 3: Looking Back, Looking Ahead

Regret and Advice

Perfect modals · should have, ought to have, had better

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I should have set boundaries far sooner."

"I ought to have protected my downtime."

"I shouldn't have said yes to everything."

"You had better slow down before you burn out."

MeaningFormExample
Regret about the pastshould have + past participleI should have rested.
Regret (more formal)ought to have + past participleI ought to have said no.
Past mistake (negative)should not have + past participleI shouldn't have ignored it.
Strong advice / warning (now)had better + base verbYou had better rest soon.

Use should have / ought to have to look back with regret at the past. Use had better (NOT in the past) for a strong warning about now or the near future -- it suggests something bad will happen if you do not act.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I should of slowed down."

✓ RIGHT

"I should have slowed down."

✗ WRONG

"You had better to rest."

✓ RIGHT

"You had better rest."

"Should've" sounds like "should of", but the word is always have. And "had better" is followed by the base verb, never "to".

Read for the Main Idea

Slowing Down

The Art of Slowing Down

For most of her career, Marina treated rest as a reward she had to earn. She answered emails at midnight, skipped lunch to finish reports, and wore her exhaustion like a badge of honor. When colleagues praised her stamina she felt proud; when her body finally protested with months of insomnia and a creeping sense of dread, she was genuinely surprised. Looking back, she admits she should have listened to the early warnings instead of pushing through them.

Recovery did not come from a single dramatic change. It came from small, sustainable habits: a real lunch break, a walk without her phone, a firm boundary around her evenings. None of it felt heroic, and at first it even felt a little lazy. Yet within a few months she noticed she could bounce back from a hard day far more quickly than before, and the dread had quietly lifted.

Now Marina tells younger colleagues the advice she wishes someone had given her: you ought to protect your downtime as fiercely as you protect a deadline. Resilience, she has learned, is not about never falling down. It is about building a life steady enough that, when you do fall, you have somewhere soft to land.

From Text to Grammar

Looking Back Wisely

Notice how the writer talks about the past: "she should have listened" and "the advice she wishes someone had given her." Both look back and gently criticize a past choice. That is exactly what perfect modals do.
Looking back: perfect modals of advice
should have + p.p.
Past regret / mild criticism

A good idea you did NOT do. "I should have set boundaries earlier."

shouldn't have + p.p.
Past regret (you did it)

Something you did but now regret. "I shouldn't have skipped so many lunches."

ought to have + p.p.
Slightly more formal regret

A near-synonym of should have. "She ought to have listened to the warnings."

had better (+ base verb)
Strong warning about now

Urgent advice for the present, NOT the past. "You'd better slow down before you burn out."
