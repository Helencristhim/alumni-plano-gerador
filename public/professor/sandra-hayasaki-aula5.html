Blog — My Life So Far

A Life in a Few Lines

by Tomás Ferreira

My name is Tomás, and this is my life in a few lines. I grew up in a small town in the south, in a simple but happy family. My background is nothing special — my father was a mechanic and my mother was a teacher. One of my favourite memories is riding my bike to the river every summer.

A big turning point came when I was eighteen. I moved to the city alone to study. It was a real challenge: I knew no one, and the first months were hard. Life has its ups and downs, and that year had plenty of both. But it was also the year I became independent — a milestone I am still proud of.

These days, my life is calmer. I work as a designer, I have good friends, and I have learned to enjoy the small things. I do not have a perfect life, but it is mine.

Looking back, I would not change much. Every hard moment taught me something. And in the future? I dream of travelling more and one day living by the sea. I really look forward to whatever comes next.

Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Phrases that tell a life

When I was younger, life was simpler. (childhood / past)
A turning point was when I moved to the city. (a key moment)
These days, my life is calmer. (the present)
Looking back, I would not change much. (reflection)
In the future, I dream of living by the sea. (the future)
Analyze and answer: what do these phrases help us do?
They are signposts that move a life story through time: childhood (When I was younger), a key moment (A turning point was when), the present (These days), reflection (Looking back) and the future (In the future, I dream of). They are exactly the past, present and future from Block 1 — now used to talk naturally about you.
