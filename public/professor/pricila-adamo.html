Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (coisas que acontecem regularmente ou são verdades)
Present Perfect = experiences, achievements, actions connected to now (experiências, conquistas, ações com conexão ao presente)

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"She lives in Araras."
Present PerfectSubject + have/has + past participle"She has traveled to many countries."
Present Perfect ContinuousSubject + have/has been + verb-ing"She has been studying English since 2000."

Common Mistake:

"I study English since 2000."

"I have been studying English since 2000."

Use Present Perfect (Continuous) com "since" e "for" para indicar duração até o presente.

FormUseExample

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Look at the highlighted words. What do they have in common?

"I live in Araras." (now, always true)
"She goes to her clinic every day." (routine)
"I have traveled to many countries." (life experience)
"She has been studying English since 2000." (started then, continues now)
TenseUseSignal
Present Simplefacts, habits, routinesevery day, always, usually
Present Perfectexperiences, life achievementshave/has + past participle
Present Perfect Continuousactions that started in the past and continuehave/has been + verb-ing
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I study English since 2000."
"I have been studying English since 2000."

Use Present Perfect with "since" and "for" to connect the past to NOW.
