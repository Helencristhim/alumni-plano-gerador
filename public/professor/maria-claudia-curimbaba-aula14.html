Chapter 3 -- Trends & Value

Language Discovery

Superlatives and trend verbs -- and the passive for the result

Discovery

Listen and Notice

How does each sentence describe the top of the market, or the movement of prices?

"She is one of the most sought-after artists in the market."

"Prices for her work have soared over the last decade."

"This is the highest price ever paid for a contemporary piece."

"Latin American art is the fastest-growing segment, and the market is booming."

The Rule

Superlatives & Trend Verbs

TypeFormExample
Short adjectivethe + adj + -est"the highest price", "the rarest piece"
Long adjectivethe most + adjective"the most sought-after artist"
Irregularthe best / the worst"the best investment of the year"
Trend verbs (up)soar / climb / rise / boom"prices have soared", "the market is booming"

Key idea: use the + -est for short adjectives ("the highest") and the most + adjective for long ones ("the most sought-after"). Never both ("the most highest" is wrong). For movement, use trend verbs: prices soar, climb or rise; a market booms. They make the market come alive.

Language Focus

Reporting the Result

When you report what happened to a work, use be / was / have been + past participle. The doer is often obvious, so you can leave it out.

"The painting was sold for twelve million dollars."

"The work has been appraised at over two million."

"The collection was acquired by a private patron."

Pattern: subject + was / were or has / have been + past participle (sold, appraised, acquired). Add "by + doer" only if it matters ("acquired by a private patron"). You describe the trend in the active voice; you report the sale in the passive.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The work appraised at two million."
"The work was appraised at two million."

The passive always needs be (was / were / been). Say "the painting was sold" and "the work has been appraised", never "the work appraised". And avoid the double superlative: "the most expensive", not "the most expensivest".

About the Briefing

1. What is the trend David describes, and which segment is growing fastest?

Prices for contemporary Brazilian work have soared; that segment is the fastest-growing part of the auction market, so the best lots will not be cheap.

2. Why does he like lot forty-seven, and what is its valuation?

It is by an emerging, very sought-after artist; the provenance is clean -- the work has been authenticated and was acquired from the studio -- and it has been appraised at around three hundred thousand dollars.

Set your top bid before you walk in and do not chase it; in a booming market it is easy to overpay, so make sure the valuation supports the price.

At the Sale with David

M

"David, I love lot forty-seven. She is an emerging artist, but already one of the most sought-after names here. Should I bid?"

D

"I would. The provenance is clean -- the work has been authenticated and it was acquired from the studio. The valuation is solid."

M

"But prices have soared. The market is booming, and a similar piece was sold last month for almost double. Is it too speculative?"

D

"A little. She is not blue-chip yet. So set your top bid in advance and do not chase it -- the most expensive lot is not always the best buy."

M

"Then my top bid is three hundred and thirty thousand. It has been appraised at three hundred, so the valuation supports it. I will not go higher."

D

"Perfect. That is how a collection is built -- with a clear head, not in the heat of the room. Good luck with the bid."
