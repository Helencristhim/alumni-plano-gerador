Chapter 3 -- Two Past Tenses

Grammar Discovery

Present perfect vs past simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which sentences have an exact time? Which ones are about experience?

"I have visited Mexico City."

"I visited Mexico City last year."

"Have you ever climbed a pyramid?"

"We went to the market yesterday."

Grammar Rule

Two Past Tenses

TenseUseExample
Present Perfecthave / has + past participle -- experience, no exact timeI have visited the ruins.
Past Simplepast verb -- finished action, clear timeI visited the ruins last year.
Perfect signalswords that need the present perfectever, never, already, yet, just
Past signalswords with a finished timeyesterday, last year, in 2019, ago

Key idea: if the sentence has an exact past time, use the past simple. If it is an experience with no exact time, use the present perfect. Never mix them.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I have visited the pyramids last year."
"I visited the pyramids last year."

With an exact time word like last year, always use the past simple. Keep the present perfect for experiences with no exact time.
