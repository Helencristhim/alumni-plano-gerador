Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Pricila wants to visit the Opera House. At the subway station she asks an officer the way. "Take the blue line and get off at Circular Quay," he says. "Go straight and the exit is across from the harbour." She buys a ticket from the machine — the fare is four dollars — and finds platform two. She does not have to transfer; the train is direct. Four stops later, she walks out of the station and the Opera House is right there, next to the water.

1. Which sentence GIVES a direction (imperative)?
A "Take the blue line and get off at Circular Quay."
B "The fare is four dollars."
C "She buys a ticket."
2. Which words are prepositions of place?
A "take, get off"
B "across from, next to"
C "ticket, fare"
3. Does Pricila have to transfer?
A Yes, twice.
B No, the train is direct.
C The text does not say.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Imperatives = verbo primeiro, sem sujeito (Go, Take, Turn, Get off)
Prepositions of place = onde algo fica (next to, across from, between, opposite, behind, on the corner)

UseStructureExample
DirectionVerb first"Take the blue line."
LocationIt is + preposition + place"It is across from the exit."
Ask the wayHow do I get to + place?"How do I get to the museum?"

Common Mistake:

"It is across the station."

"It is across from the station."

Use a preposição completa: across FROM, next TO, in front OF.

Chapter 3: The Code

Directions
& Places

Imperatives + prepositions of place

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Take the blue line and get off at Central.
Go straight and turn left at the corner.
The exit is across from the platform.

What do the orange words have in common?

UseStructureExample
Give a directionVerb first (no subject)Go straight. Take the blue line. Get off at Central.
Say where it isIt is + preposition + placeIt is across from the station.
Ask the wayHow do I get to + place?How do I get to the Opera House?

Key: direções = verbo primeiro (sem “you”). Lugares = preposições: next to, across from, between, opposite, on the corner, behind.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"Go to straight." / "It is across the station."
"Go straight." / "It is across from the station."

No “to” before straight. Use across from, not “across the”.

Grammar Practice

Find the Way

Tell someone to use the metro → ???Take the subway / Take the blue line.
Tell someone to leave the train → ???Get off at the next stop.
The exit / __ / the platform (other side) → ???The exit is across from the platform.
The station / __ / the hotel (very close) → ???The station is next to the hotel.
Ask the way to the museum → ???How do I get to the museum?
