Phase 3

The Code

There is / There are + Present Simple

Look at these sentences

What do the blue words have in common?

"There is a meeting room on each floor."
"There are fifty employees in our department."
"Is there a branch in Rio?"
"There are over two hundred employees."

There is / There are

We use them to say what exists or is present

FormSingularPlural
AffirmativeThere is a branch in Rio.There are 200 employees.
NegativeThere isn't a branch in Europe.There aren't any offices in Asia.
QuestionIs there a meeting room?Are there any international clients?

Key Rule: There is + singular noun. There are + plural noun.
"There is a strong demand." / "There are many employees."

Common Mistake

"There is two hundred employees at TVT."
"There are two hundred employees at TVT."

Use There are with plural nouns (employees, branches, clients).
