Chapter 4 -- Getting There

David Explains Email Best Practices

Listen first, then answer the questions

1. What is David's first tip for professional emails?
Always state the purpose clearly in the first sentence using "I am writing to..."
2. What does David say about contractions?
Avoid contractions in formal emails. Use "I am" instead of "I'm," "do not" instead of "don't."
3. How does David recommend closing an email?
With "I look forward to hearing from you" followed by "Best regards" or "Kind regards."
Chapter 4 -- Getting There

Listening 2 -- Comprehension

1. Why does David recommend avoiding phrasal verbs in formal emails?
Because single Latinate verbs like "notify," "inquire," and "outline" sound more professional.
2. What does David say about the subject line?
The subject line should be specific and action-oriented, not vague like "Quick question."
3. What is David's advice about following through?
Always follow through on commitments mentioned in emails. If you say you will send something, send it promptly.

Maria Claudia Curimbaba -- Houston Project

From
Maria Claudia Curimbaba <mc.curimbaba@company.com>
To
David Chen <d.chen@houston-ops.com>
Subject
Regarding the Q3 Project Timeline -- Updated Milestones
Date
June 10, 2026

Dear David,

I am writing regarding the Q3 project timeline. I would like to inquire about the current status of the deliverables and whether the team can follow through on the original deadlines.

Please find attached the revised project outline with updated milestones. I would appreciate it if you could acknowledge receipt and notify the Houston team of any changes.

Should you need any additional information, please do not hesitate to contact me. I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience.

Best regards,
Maria Claudia Curimbaba
