Chapter 3

The Engine

Present simple vs. present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Habit or Happening Now?

"I usually check my emails at nine."

"Right now, I am checking a new report."

"I deal with reports every week."

"This week, I am preparing for a big meeting."

TenseJobForm
Present simpleHabits, routines, always trueI check / She checks
Present continuousHappening around now, temporaryI am checking / She is checking
Signal wordsSimple: usually, every day, on Mondays  ·  Continuous: right now, this week, at the moment
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I am working every day."

✓ RIGHT

"I work every day."

✗ WRONG

"Look, the phone rings right now."

✓ RIGHT

"Look, the phone is ringing right now."

✗ WRONG

"I am wanting a coffee."

✓ RIGHT

"I want a coffee."

Use the present simple for habits, even daily ones. Use the present continuous for what is happening at this moment. State verbs (want, like, know) stay simple.

Chapter 4

Real Voices

Two days, two tenses

Dialogue

A Normal Week, an Unusual Week

D
Vanessa, what does a typical workday look like for you?
V
I usually deal with emails first, and by noon I tend to tackle the hard tasks.
D
And the afternoon? Do you take long breaks?
V
Hardly ever. I run errands and then I prepare for the next day.
D
That sounds steady. Is this week the same?
V
Not really. This week I am working on a special project, so I am staying later than usual.
D
So your routine is normal, but right now something extra is happening.
V
Exactly. I constantly remind myself it is only temporary.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does she deal with first every day?
Her emails; by noon she tackles the hard tasks.
2. Why is this week different?
She is working on a special project, so she is staying later than usual.
3. How does she feel about the extra work?
She reminds herself it is only temporary.
