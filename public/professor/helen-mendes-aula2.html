Chapter 3: The Code

The Rhythm Words

always · usually · often · sometimes · never

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I always check my inbox at eight."

"We usually have a meeting at nine."

"I sometimes work late before a deadline."

"I never skip my coffee break."

AdverbHow oftenExample
always100%I always check my inbox first.
usually / often80% / 60%We usually meet at nine.
sometimes40%I sometimes work late.
never0%I never skip my break.

Position: BEFORE the main verb (I always check) — but AFTER "to be" (I am always busy).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I check my inbox always."

✓ RIGHT

"I always check my inbox."

✗ WRONG

"I always am busy on Mondays."

✓ RIGHT

"I am always busy on Mondays."

Chapter 4: The Call

James Is Calling

Tuesday, 10 a.m. sharp

Dialogue

Walk Me Through Your Day

J
Helen! Good to see you again. To plan the campaign, walk me through your day.
H
Of course! I always check my inbox at eight. My first task is the emails.
J
Early bird! And when does the team meet?
H
We usually have a team meeting at nine. On Mondays, we review the schedule of the week.
J
Got it. What is your priority this week?
H
The launch, for sure. I often work on the campaign in the afternoon — my creative time.
J
Perfect. And the monthly report? When is the deadline?
H
Every last Friday. I sometimes work late that week — but I never skip my coffee break!
J
Smart! So: campaign work in your afternoons, my mornings. Same time next Tuesday?
H
Same time next Tuesday. It is in my schedule, James!
