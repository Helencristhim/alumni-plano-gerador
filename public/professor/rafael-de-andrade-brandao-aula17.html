Chapter 3 -- Saying Thank You

How to Thank Someone

Two warm, natural patterns

Discover the Pattern

Listen and Notice

"Thank you for meeting me yesterday."

"Thank you for sharing your insights."

"It was great to talk with you."

"It was a pleasure to meet you."

Grammar Rule

Two Ways to Thank

PatternFormExample
Thank you for+ verb-ingThank you for meeting me.
Thank you for+ your + nounThank you for your time.
It was + adjective+ to + verbIt was great to talk with you.
I look forward to+ verb-ingI look forward to working with you.

Key idea: Use Thank you for + -ing (for meeting) or + your + noun (for your time). Use It was + adjective + to + verb (great to talk). After look forward to, use the -ing form (to working).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Thank you for meet me yesterday."
"Thank you for meeting me yesterday."

After Thank you for, use the -ing form. After look forward to, also use -ing (look forward to working). Use It was + adjective + to + verb (great to talk).

Artefact

Rafael's Follow-up Message

To: m.costa@globalmarkets.com

Subject: Great to meet you yesterday

Dear Mr. Costa,

Thank you for meeting me yesterday. It was a real pleasure to talk with you.

I really appreciate your insights on the new market, and I am grateful for your time.

I look forward to working together. Please feel free to reach out any time, and let us keep in touch.

Best regards,

Rafael de Andrade Brandão

Golden rule: Short, sincere, and prompt -- send it the same day.
