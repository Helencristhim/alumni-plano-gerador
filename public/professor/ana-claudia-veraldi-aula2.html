Ana's Career Story

Ana's Career Story

Ana is a systems analyst, and she has built a strong career path in technology. She has worked in quality assurance since 2018, and she has been in tech for more than ten years. Her background is in computer science. Testing is her area of expertise: she has specialized in finding critical problems before software reaches the user.

Ana has achieved a lot. She has earned two promotions, and she has led a small QA team since last year. She has mentored several junior testers, and that is one of her favorite milestones. Every project brings a new challenge, but she has never missed an important deadline. She has always been curious about the world, but she has never worked with a fully international team. That is her next goal: she wants to tell her career story in English and find remote work abroad.

To talk about your experience up to now, use the present perfect: have or has plus the past participle. Use 'for' with a period of time (for ten years) and 'since' with a starting point (since 2018). Use 'ever' in questions (Have you ever led a team?) and 'never' for no experience (I have never worked abroad).

UseFormExample
Experience up to nowhave / has + past participleI have worked in QA.
A period of timefor + periodI have worked here for six years.
A starting pointsince + point in timeI have worked here since 2018.
Questions / no experienceever in questions (Have you ever...?) · never for no experience (I have never...)

Present perfect = have/has + past participle. for + period · since + starting point · ever in questions · never for no experience.

Language Focus

For, Since, Ever, or Never?

AnalyseFor, since, ever, or never?
AI have worked here since 2018.
BI have been in QA for six years.
CHave you ever led a team?
DI have never worked abroad.
'for' + a period of time (for six years). 'since' + a starting point (since 2018). 'ever' goes in questions (Have you ever...?). 'never' means no experience up to now (I have never...).
Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I work in QA since 2018."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked in QA since 2018."

✗ WRONG

"I have worked here since six years."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked here for six years."

For experience up to now, use the present perfect, not the present simple. Use "for" with a period (for six years) and "since" with a starting point (since 2018).
