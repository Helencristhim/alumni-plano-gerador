Chapter 3 -- Reported Speech

Grammar Discovery

said / told + one step back in time

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Look at the orange words. What happens to the verb when we report what was said?

Direct: "The release is late." → He said the release was late.

Direct: "I need more time." → She told me she needed more time.

Direct: "I will fix it." → He said he would fix it.

Direct: "The tests passed." → She told me the tests had passed.

The Rule

Reported Speech

Direct saysReported (one step back)Example
is / arewas / wereHe said the release was late.
present simplepast simpleShe told me she needed more time.
willwouldHe said he would fix it.
past simplepast perfect (had + p.p.)She told me the tests had passed.

Key idea: Use say with no person (He said that...) and tell with a person (He told me that...). The word that is optional. The verb usually moves one step back in time.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He said me he is busy."
"He told me he was busy."

Use tell + person (told me), not "said me". And move the verb one step back: is → was.

Grammar Practice

Report it Back

"The build is broken." → He said the build _____was broken. (is → ?)
"I will flag it early." → She told me she _____would flag it early. (will → ?)
"We need more testers." → They said they _____needed more testers. (need → ?)
"Nobody reported the bug." → He told me nobody _____had reported the bug. (reported → ?)

Dialogue

Giving Feedback to a Developer

R

"Thanks for the time, Bruno. I want to address something honestly. The last two releases were late, and I would like to understand why."

B

"I know they were late. The test environment had been broken for days, and nobody had reported it."

R

"Thank you for your perspective. I acknowledge the workload has been heavy, and I want to reassure you: I am on your side."

B

"I appreciate that. I am not trying to be defensive. I should have raised the problem sooner."

R

"That is fair. The behavior I am asking for is simple: flag any blocker early. Can we agree on that?"

B

"Agreed. I will flag blockers the same day, and I will stay accountable for my part."

Reassure, Report, Agree

Reassure me, then report what I told you ("You told me the environment had been broken"), and agree on the behavior you want.

reassure you told me behavior
Your Turn -- Free

The Full Feedback Talk

Run the full conversation: address the behavior, acknowledge what is true, ask for and report my perspective, reassure me, and agree on a next step.

No keywords. Be honest and kind at the same time.
