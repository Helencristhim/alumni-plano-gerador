Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

Grammar Discovery

be + past participle (+ by + doer)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words tell us about who does the action?

"The order is processed in minutes." (present, doer not stated)

"The files are submitted online." (present, plural)

"The system was monitored all night." (past)

"The report was approved by the manager." (doer added with by)

Grammar Rule

be + past participle

TenseStructureExample
Present (singular)is + past participleThe order is processed.
Present (plural)are + past participleThe files are submitted.
Past (singular)was + past participleThe system was monitored.
Past (plural)were + past participleThe bugs were handled.

Key idea: the tense lives in the verb "be" (is/are for now, was/were for the past), and the main verb is always the past participle. The doer is added at the end with "by" only when it matters; in a process it is usually left out.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The order processed quickly." (no "be")
"The order is processed quickly."

A passive sentence always needs a form of be (is, are, was, were) before the past participle. Without it, there is no passive.

Comprehension

About the Tips

1. What is the form of the passive voice?

A form of "be" plus the past participle, for example "is installed".

2. Why is the doer often left out in a process?

Because the action matters more than the person, and the doer is obvious or general.

3. How do you add the doer when it matters?

With the word "by", for example "approved by the manager".
