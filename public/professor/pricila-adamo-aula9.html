Chapter 3: Making It Yours

Have It
Your Way

The causative (have / get something done) + so / such

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

I will have the steak cooked medium-rare, please.
Could I get the sauce served on the side?
The portion was so generous that I could not finish it.
It was such a treat that we splurged on dessert.

Who cooks the steak? And what do so and such do?

UseStructureExample
Causative (request)have + object + past participle"I will have the steak cooked rare."
Causative (informal)get + object + past participle"Could I get it changed?"
so + adjectiveso + adjective + that..."It was so rich that..."
such + (a) noun phrasesuch + (a/an) + adjective + noun"It was such a generous portion."

Key: com have/get + objeto + past participle, você pede que a cozinha faça algo (não você). Use so antes de adjetivo sozinho; use such antes de (a/an) + adjetivo + substantivo.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I want the steak cook well." / "It was so a generous portion." / "Could I get change my order?"
"I will have the steak cooked well." / "It was such a generous portion." / "Could I get my order changed?"

Use the past participle, and such + a + noun phrase.

Dialogue

Customising the Order

W
"Good evening. Have you decided, or shall I run you through the specials?"
P
"I am really in the mood for something hearty tonight. What do you recommend?"
W
"Then the slow-roasted lamb. It is wonderfully succulent, with a generous portion of potatoes."
P
"Perfect. Could I have it cooked without the garlic butter? And could I swap the potatoes for a salad?"
W
"Of course. We can easily cater for that. Anything to drink?"
P
"Just water, thank you. And honestly, I am only peckish, so I will skip the starter."
W
"Understood. If you have a craving for something sweet later, the chef can whip up a small dessert."
P
"That sounds lovely. If the portion is too big, could I have the rest boxed up?"
W
"Absolutely. It is such a popular dish that nobody ever leaves it behind. I will bring your water right away."

3. Why did she skip the starter?
She was only peckish.
4. What did the waiter say about the dish's popularity?
It is such a popular dish that nobody ever leaves it behind.

Read for the Main Idea

The Art of a Good Meal

Slow Food, Real Places: Why the Best Meals Are Never Rushed

Ask any seasoned traveller where they truly felt at home abroad, and the answer is rarely a famous monument. More often, it is a table. It might be a tiny family-run trattoria where the owner insists on having the pasta made fresh, or a market stall where a cook gets the fish grilled to order in front of you. Food, more than sightseeing, is how we read a culture from the inside.

The trouble is that tourists are often served a hollow version of this. The menus are translated into six languages, the portions are huge but bland, and the savoury depth that locals take for granted is quietly stripped away. A meal that should be hearty and memorable becomes a transaction. The clever traveller learns to step off the main square, to ask where the staff themselves eat, and to be a little adventurous with the menu.

There is also an art to letting yourself be looked after. In the best places you do not order so much as surrender: you let the kitchen whip up whatever is freshest, you have your wine chosen for you, and you splurge, just once, on the dish the chef is proudest of. The reward is not only a fuller stomach but a sharper sense of where, exactly, in the world you are.

Adapted from a food & travel essay, The Slow Journal

Three role-plays: guided, semi-free, and free

Guided Role-Play

Level 1: With Keywords

You are at a fine restaurant abroad. Order a main course, then customise it: change how it is cooked and swap a side. Use the keywords below.
in the mood for... I will have... Could I have it cooked...? swap... for... cater for boxed up
Semi-Free Role-Play

Level 2: A Special Request

Your friend has a dietary need and the kitchen must adapt the dish. Order for both of you and ask the kitchen to make a change. Use these openers:
"My friend cannot eat... Could you have it...?"
"Do you cater for...?"
"Could we get the... served on the side?"
"It looks such a generous portion that..."
Free Role-Play

Level 3: Speak Freely

No keywords. Have a full dinner abroad: discuss the specials, order a customised meal, react to a problem with the food, and ask for the leftovers to be boxed up — all in English. Be yourself.
