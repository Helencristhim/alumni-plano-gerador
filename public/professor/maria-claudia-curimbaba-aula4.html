Comparatives for Finance

TypePatternExample
Short adjectiveadj + -er + thanhigher than, lower than
Long adjectivemore/less + adj + thanmore profitable than
Irregularbetter/worse + thanbetter than Q2, worse than expected
Equalas + adj + asas strong as last year
Numberspercent / million / point12 percent, 3.5 million, 2 point 3

Key rule: Never double the comparative. "More higher" is WRONG. Use "higher" OR "more expensive", never both.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"Revenue is more higher than last quarter."

Double comparative: never use "more" + "-er"

"Revenue is higher than last quarter."

Correct: short adjective + -er (no "more")

"The profit is gooder than expected."

"Good" is irregular: good > better (not "gooder")

"The profit is better than expected."

Correct: good > better > best (irregular)
