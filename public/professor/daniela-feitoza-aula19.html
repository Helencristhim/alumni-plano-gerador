Chapter 1: The Difficult Update Floor

From Cause to Diplomacy

In Lesson 18 you explained the chain: "The server was overloaded, so requests timed out."

Today you soften the bad news: "It seems the rollback caused a slight delay."

Be honest, but gentle and diplomatic.

A leader delivers hard news without alarming people.

Chapter 1: The Difficult Update Floor

Direct vs Diplomatic

"The project is late." → "It seems we are a little behind."

"We will miss the deadline." → "We might need a little more time."

"It is a disaster." → "It is a slight setback."

"It seems", "might" and "slight" make the news gentler.

Chapter 3

The Softening Rule

It seems, might / may, and softening words

Softening Discovery

Listen and Notice

It seems the rollback caused a slight delay.

We might need a little more time.

There appears to be a minor shortfall.

We may need to revisit the numbers.

Which words soften the fact? Which make the future less certain?

Softening Rule

Three Ways to Soften

ToolHowExample
Soften the factit seems / there appears to be / it looks like"It seems we are a little behind."
Soften the futuremight / may / could (less certain)"We might need more time."
Shrink the problemslight / minor / small / a little"It is a slight setback."
Stay honestSoften the tone, but never hide the problem. Add the workaround.
Common Mistake

Too Harsh vs Diplomatic

✗ TOO HARSH

"The project failed completely."

✓ DIPLOMATIC

"It seems we hit a slight setback." (softened)

✗ WRONG

"We might to need more time."

✓ RIGHT

"We might need more time." (might + base verb, no "to")

Soften the tone, and after "might" or "may" use the base verb — never "to".

Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How does Daniela soften the news about the rollout?
She says "it seems we have hit a small setback" and adds that they have a workaround in place.
2. How does she present the timeline and budget problems?
"There appears to be a slight delay, so we might need more time" and "a minor shortfall, so we may need to revisit the numbers", with the caveat that the figures are preliminary.
3. How does she explain the cause and the tradeoff?
There was a small oversight in the planning; the tradeoff is a little more time for a safer launch.
