Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple + Going To

Grammar

Past Simple = completed actions in the past (Use o Past Simple para ações concluídas no passado)
Going To = future plans and intentions (Use going to para planos futuros)
Structure: Past Simple = Subject + verb (past form) | Going To = Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb

TypeRegular (arrive)Irregular (go)
AffirmativeI arrived at the airport.He went through customs.
NegativeI did not arrive late.He did not go to Miami.
QuestionDid you arrive on time?Did he go through customs?
Going ToI am going to stay for ten days. / He is going to visit Times Square.

Common Mistakes:

"I goed to the hotel."

"I went to the hotel."

"I going to stay ten days."

"I am going to stay for ten days."

Lembre-se: verbos irregulares têm formas próprias no passado (go → went, take → took). Para planos futuros, use am/is/are + going to + verbo base.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Reported Speech

GRAMMAR

Para reportar o que alguém disse, usamos said (that) ou told + pessoa (that), e o verbo recua um tempo: is/are → was/were, will → would, present → past. O that é opcional. Atenção: said NÃO leva pessoa direta ("she said that..."), e told leva pessoa ("she told me that..."). / To report what someone said, use said (that) or told + person (that), and move the verb one step back.

Direct words / Palavras diretasReported / Reportado (one step back)
"Meetings are difficult."She said that meetings were difficult.
"The audit is a success."She told me that the audit was a success.
"I will improve."He said that he would improve.
"I need practice."I said that I needed practice.

Key point / Ponto-chave: use told com pessoa ("told me"), said sem pessoa ("said that"), e recue o verbo um tempo (is → was, will → would). Nunca "she said me". / Use "told" with a person, "said" with no person, and move the verb one step back.
