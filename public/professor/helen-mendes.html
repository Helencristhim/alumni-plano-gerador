Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The rhythm of your day

GRAMMAR

Adverbs of frequency: how often you do each thing.

AdverbHow oftenExample
always100% — every timeI always check my inbox at eight.
usually / often80% / 60% — most of the time / oftenWe usually meet at nine.
sometimes40% — from time to timeI sometimes work late.
never0% — not onceI never skip my break.

Position: BEFORE the main verb (I always check) — but AFTER the verb BE (I am always busy).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modals of advice

GRAMMAR

The strength of the advice changes with the modal.

ModalStrengthExample
might want tosoftest — a gentle suggestionYou might want to unwind first.
should / ought tostandard adviceYou should prioritize sleep.
had betterstrongest — a warning with a consequenceYou had better rest, or you'll burn out.

should and had better take the base verb, with NO "to": should rest, had better rest. But ought TO and might want TO keep the "to".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The language of certainty

GRAMMAR

The strength of a claim changes with the words you choose.

LanguageStrengthExample
clearly show / provestrong, 100% — only with solid dataThe figures clearly show a rise.
suggests / seems tocareful — not fully sureThe data suggests a change.
may / might point topossible — an early signalThis may point to a new trend.

After seems to, use the base verb: seems to indicate (not "indicates"). Choose strong words only with solid data; with partial data, soften with suggests / seems to / may.
