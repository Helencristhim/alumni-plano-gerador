Stage 1.1: Key Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight essential words for presenting data and KPIs to the board.

KPI.A measurable value that shows how well a company is achieving its goals
"Our main KPI this quarter is customer acquisition cost."
Revenue.The total money a company earns from its business activities
"Revenue increased by 12 percent compared to last quarter."
Growth.An increase in size, value, or amount over time
"We are seeing strong growth in the cloud services division."
Decline.A decrease or reduction in numbers, quality, or importance
"There was a slight decline in hardware sales this month."
Quarter.A three-month period used to divide the business year (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)
"Q2 results were higher than Q1 across all departments."
Budget.The planned amount of money available for spending
"We need to stay within the budget for the next quarter."
Forecast.A prediction of future business results based on current data
"The forecast shows a 15 percent increase in revenue for Q3."
ROI.Return on Investment -- the profit earned relative to the money spent
"The ROI on our cloud migration project exceeded expectations."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Rafael is presenting Q2 results to the board. Read the text and pay attention to the words in bold.

"Good afternoon, everyone. Our Q2 revenue was 2.3 million dollars, which is higher than Q1 by 12 percent. Cloud services generated 1.4 million, making the cloud division more profitable than on-premise. Our customer acquisition cost is lower than our target, which is excellent. The ROI on the migration project was 3.2 times, which is better than the industry average. Looking at the forecast, we expect growth of 15 percent in Q3, but hardware may see a slight decline. Our budget utilization was 95 percent, and spending was lower than the approved amount."
1. How does Q2 revenue compare to Q1?
A It is the same as Q1.
B It is 12 percent higher than Q1.
C It is lower than Q1.
2. Which division is more profitable?
A On-premise is more profitable than cloud.
B Cloud is more profitable than on-premise.
C They are equally profitable.
3. What does the forecast show for Q3?
A A decline of 15 percent.
B No change from Q2.
C Growth of 15 percent.

Grammar Tip: Comparatives

Grammar

We use comparatives to compare two things. This is essential when presenting data and KPIs.

TypeRuleExample
Short adjectivesadjective + -er thanRevenue is higher than last quarter.
Long adjectivesmore + adjective + thanCloud is more profitable than on-premise.
Irregulargood → better thanQ2 results are better than Q1.
Irregularbad → worse thanHardware sales are worse than expected.

Common patterns for data: "X is [number] percent higher/lower than Y." | "Division A is more/less profitable than Division B." | "The ROI is better/worse than the forecast."

Chapter 3: The Code

Common Mistake

"Revenue is more higher than last quarter."
"Revenue is higher than last quarter."

Never use "more" with short comparatives (-er). "Higher" already means "more high."

Chapter 3: The Code

Complete the comparisons

"Revenue in Q2 was _____ Q1." (high)
"Revenue in Q2 was higher than Q1."
"Cloud is _____ on-premise." (profitable)
"Cloud is more profitable than on-premise."
"Our costs are _____ the approved budget." (low)
"Our costs are lower than the approved budget."
"The ROI was _____ the industry average." (good)
"The ROI was better than the industry average."
"Hardware sales are _____ expected." (bad)
"Hardware sales are worse than expected."
