Comparatives and Superlatives

Comparing places and finding the best

Chapter 3 -- The Code

What do the highlighted words have in common?

"Sydney is bigger than Araras." (comparing two)
"Canada was more beautiful than I expected." (comparing two)
"It was the most stunning place I have ever visited." (the best of all)
"That was the coziest hotel I have ever stayed in." (the best of all)
"A beautiful old Italian church." (adjective order)

The Rule

Comparative (two things)Superlative (the best of all)Adjective Order
Short adj: adj + -er thanShort adj: the adj + -estOpinion > Size > Age > Shape > Color > Origin > Material
bigger than, older thanthe biggest, the oldesta beautiful old Italian church
Long adj: more adj thanLong adj: the most adja cozy little ancient village
more beautiful thanthe most beautiful(opinion, size, age)
good > better thangood > the best
Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"Sydney is more better than I expected."
"Sydney is better than I expected."
Why? "Better" is already the comparative form of "good." Adding "more" creates a double comparative, which is incorrect. The same applies to "worse" (never "more worse") and "best" (never "most best").

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to reveal the correction.

"Sydney is more bigger than Araras."
"Sydney is bigger than Araras." (Short adjective: big > bigger, not more bigger)
"It was the most beautifulest place."
"It was the most beautiful place." (Long adjective: the most beautiful, not the most beautifulest)
"The food was more better than I expected."
"The food was better than I expected." (Irregular: good > better, never more better)
"It was the most ancientest temple in the city."
"It was the most ancient temple in the city." (Long adjective: the most ancient, not the most ancientest)
