Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Networking -- meeting new people to build useful work connections
"The summit is great for networking." (Fazer contatos profissionais)
First impression -- the opinion people form about you in the first moments
"A smile makes a good first impression." (Primeira impressão)
Introduce -- to tell someone your name and what you do
"Let me introduce myself." (Apresentar-se)
Colleague -- a person you work with
"She is a colleague from the New York office." (Colega de trabalho)
Industry -- a type of business or work area
"We both work in the same industry." (Setor / indústria)
Confident -- sure of yourself and calm
"He looks confident when he speaks." (Confiante)
Approachable -- friendly and easy to talk to
"A good host is warm and approachable." (Acessível / fácil de conversar)
Small talk -- light, friendly conversation about easy topics
"We made small talk about the coffee." (Conversa leve / bate-papo)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Daniel is at the São Paulo Marketing Summit. He works in design. During the coffee break, he sees a new face and breaks the ice: "Hi, I don't think we've met. What brings you here?" Laura introduces herself: "Let me introduce myself. I am Laura, and I work in marketing." They are in the same industry, so they have a lot in common. Laura is friendly and approachable, so the small talk is easy. Before they leave, they exchange business cards: "Here's my card. Let's stay in touch."

1. Como a Laura fala a área dela? / How does Laura say her area of work?
A "I work in marketing."
B "I'm work marketing."
C "Me marketing."
2. O que o Daniel diz para puxar conversa? / What does Daniel say to break the ice?
A "Who are you?"
B "What brings you here?"
C "Give me your card."
3. Como eles fecham a conversa? / How do they close the conversation?
A "Here's my card. Let's stay in touch."
B "Bye."
C "Call me maybe."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Introducing Yourself

GRAMMAR

As ferramentas para se apresentar. / The tools to introduce yourself.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
I'm a + cargoDizer seu cargoI am a brand manager.
I work in + áreaDizer seu setorI work in marketing.
What do you do? / What brings you here?Perguntar ao outroPerguntas abertas mantêm a conversa.

Atenção: present simple -- diga "I work in marketing" (nunca "I'm work"). E he/she ganha o -s: he works, she is.

Aula 02 -- Pre-class

Handling Phone Calls & Voicemail

Preview da aula V4 (listening): o vocabulário do telefone (voicemail, get through, hold on, call back, available, urgent, message, schedule) e a linguagem funcional para pedidos educados -- "Could I speak to...?", "Can I take a message?", "Could you call me back?". Você ouve um recado de voz e pratica deixar o seu.
0%
▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Voicemail -- a recorded message you leave when someone does not answer
"She left a voicemail after the beep." (Correio de voz / recado)
Get through -- to succeed in reaching someone by phone
"I could not get through; the line was busy." (Conseguir falar)
Hold on -- to wait a short time on the phone
"Hold on a moment, please." (Aguardar na linha)
Call back -- to return someone's phone call later
"I will call back this afternoon." (Retornar a ligação)
Available -- free and able to talk now
"Is Ms. Costa available now?" (Disponível)
Urgent -- very important and needing fast action
"It is urgent, so please call me today." (Urgente)
Message -- information you leave for someone
"Can I take a message?" (Mensagem / recado)
Schedule -- to arrange a time for something, like a call
"Let us schedule the call for Friday." (Agendar)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas.

Ana works at the front desk. The phone rings and she answers: "Good morning, Redpoint Marketing." A caller says, "Hello, could I speak to Laura, please?" Ana replies, "I am sorry, she is not available right now. Can I take a message?" The caller says, "Yes. Could you ask her to call me back? It is a little urgent." Ana says, "Of course. Could you hold on a moment while I check her schedule?"

1. Como o cliente pede para falar com a Laura? / How does the caller ask to speak to Laura?
A "Could I speak to Laura, please?"
B "Give me Laura."
C "Laura now."
2. O que a Ana oferece quando a Laura não está? / What does Ana offer when Laura is out?
A "Call again."
B "Can I take a message?"
C "Goodbye."
3. Como o cliente pede um retorno? / How does the caller ask for a call back?
A "Could you ask her to call me back?"
B "She calls me."
C "I want call."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Requests (can / could)

GRAMMAR

Como pedir com educação ao telefone. / How to make polite requests on the phone.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Could I + verbo...?Pedir algo com educaçãoCould I speak to Laura?
Can I + verbo...?Oferecer ajudaCan I take a message?
Could you + verbo...?Pedir que a pessoa faça algoCould you call me back?

Atenção: depois de can/could use o verbo na forma base, sem "to" -- diga "Could you hold on" (nunca "Could you to hold"). "Could" soa um pouco mais educado que "can".
