Reported Speech

The morning after a night incident, Ana must tell her manager exactly what the client, QA and the on-call engineer said on the escalation call. Tonight, she learns to report what was said, faithfully.

Chapter 1: Warm-Up

From the Postmortem to the Report

Last lesson, you rewrote the outage with third and mixed conditionals. Tonight, the job is different: not "what would have happened", but "what people said". Think of a time you had to tell your manager what someone else said on a call. How did you start the sentence?

Same Words, Reported Later

Direct vs Reported

On the call (direct)

"The build is ready." / "We deployed it today." / "I will fix it."

In your report (reported)

She said the build was ready. He said they had deployed it that day. She said she would fix it.

Reporting what someone said usually shifts the tense one step back. That backshift tells the listener you are quoting the past, not speaking in the present.

Chapter 3: The Code

The Backshift Rule

How the tense moves one step back when you report speech

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The build is ready." → She said the build was ready.

"We deployed it today." → He said they had deployed it that day.

"I will fix it." → She said she would fix it.

"Can you check the logs?" → He asked if I could check the logs.

What happens to the verb each time you report it? Which words move one step back?

Direct speechReported speechShift
"It is down."She said it was down.present → past
"We deployed it."He said they had deployed it.past → past perfect
"I will fix it."She said she would fix it.will → would
"Can you help?"He asked if I could help.can → could
Time and place words shift too: today → that day, yesterday → the day before, tomorrow → the next day, now → then, here → there.context
Key ruleReport with say / tell / ask / explain. Statements: said (that). Questions: asked if / whether + normal word order (no do/does/did, no question mark).

Use this to report what a client, an engineer or a manager said on a call, in an email, or up the chain.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She said the service is down."
"She said the service was down."
"He asked can we send a summary today?"
"He asked whether we could send a summary that day."
"She said me she would fix it."
"She told me she would fix it."

Do not forget the backshift: is becomes was. Reported questions use asked if / whether with normal word order and no question mark. Use tell with a person (told me), say without one (said that).

Dialogue

The Escalation Debrief

R
Ana, I missed the incident bridge last night. Can you tell me what the team said?
A
Of course. Priya said the login service was down for about forty minutes.
R
Did anyone explain the cause?
A
David said they had deployed a bad config the night before, and that he would relay the rollback to the team.
R
And what did the client want?
A
The client asked whether we could send a written summary that day, so I said we would.
R
Good. Did QA add anything?
A
Priya reiterated that the fix was live, but she acknowledged there was still a discrepancy in the logs.
R
Perfect. Relay all of that to the recipients on the incident thread, and keep their words verbatim.
Listening

Reporting Faithfully

A communication coach on backshift and honest wording. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. According to the coach, what usually happens to the tense when you report what someone said?
It shifts one step back (backshift): is becomes was, will becomes would, can becomes could.
2. What is the coach's first warning about reporting?
Do not editorialize; relay what was said, do not add your own opinion.
3. What should you do when the exact wording matters?
Quote it verbatim instead of paraphrasing it.
