Chapter 3: Going to vs Will

Two Ways to Talk About the Future

Going to · Will

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"I am going to book the flights tomorrow." (plan)

"We are going to save up this year." (plan)

"It's raining? OK, I will take a taxi." (quick decision)

"Don't worry, I will help you pack." (quick decision)

FormUseExample
am/is/are going to + verbA plan you already decidedI am going to book a hotel.
will + verbA decision in the moment / a promiseOK, I will take a taxi.
going to (prediction with proof)Something you can see comingLook at the clouds -- it is going to rain.
will (prediction you think)Something you believe or hopeI think you will love the city.

Use going to for plans you decided before. Use will for a decision you make right now, or a promise. Both talk about the future.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I going to book a hotel."

✓ RIGHT

"I am going to book a hotel."

✗ WRONG

"I will to call you later."

✓ RIGHT

"I will call you later."

Dialogue

Planning the Next Trip

So, where are we going to go next? I think we are going to need a real plan this time.
T
So do I! I am going to book the flights this week. First, let's set a budget.
F
Good idea. I will save up a little more this month, then. What about the hotel?
T
I am going to find a small one near the old town. I have a brochure with some great options.
F
Perfect. Don't worry about the packing list -- I will make one for both of us.
T
Thank you! I can't wait. I am really looking forward to this one.
