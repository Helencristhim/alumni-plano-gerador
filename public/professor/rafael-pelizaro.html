Grammar Tip: Present Simple

Grammar

Present Simple is used to describe routines, habits, facts, and permanent situations.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)He manages IT projects.
NegativeSubject + do/does + not + base verbHe does not manage the HR team.
QuestionDo/Does + subject + base verb?Does he manage IT projects?

Key rule: For he/she/it, add -s or -es to the verb. For I/you/we/they, use the base form. Time markers: every day, always, usually, never, on Fridays.

Grammar

Grammar Tip: Present Continuous

Form: Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

Use it for:

  • Actions happening right now: "We are discussing the budget."
  • Temporary situations: "I am working on the migration this week."
  • Future arrangements: "We are meeting the vendor on Thursday."
Subject Form Example
I am + verb-ing I am preparing the agenda.
He / She / It is + verb-ing He is leading the meeting.
We / You / They are + verb-ing We are discussing the deadlines.
Common mistake: "He leading the meeting" ✗ → "He is leading the meeting." ✓
