Grammar Tip: Present Simple

Grammar

Present Simple is used to describe routines, habits, facts, and permanent situations.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)He manages IT projects.
NegativeSubject + do/does + not + base verbHe does not manage the HR team.

Key rule: For he/she/it, add -s or -es to the verb. For I/you/we/they, use the base form. Time markers: every day, always, usually, never, on Fridays.

Grammar Tip: Present Continuous

Form: Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

Use it for:

Handling Pushback -- Disagreeing Professionally

Disagreeing with a stakeholder in a calm, professional way: the first conditional (If + present, ... will / may) and polite opinion frames (I see your point, but...; I am not sure I agree; Could we find a compromise?). New vocabulary: pushback, concern, trade-off, compromise, constraint, assumption, objection, align.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Pushback -- resistance or disagreement with a plan or idea
"The team gave some pushback on the new deadline." (Resistência / objeção)
Concern -- a worry about a possible problem
"I have a concern about the deadline on Friday." (Preocupação)
Trade-off -- a balance where you accept one loss to gain something else
"There is a trade-off between speed and quality." (Compensação / troca)

Constraint -- a limit that restricts what you can do
"Time is our main constraint this week." (Restrição / limitação)
Assumption -- something you accept as true without checking it
"My assumption is that the client wants it next month." (Suposição / premissa)
Objection -- a reason against a plan or an idea
"My objection is the timing, not the feature itself." (Objeção)
Align -- to bring people to agreement on a plan
"I want us to align before we call the client." (Alinhar)
