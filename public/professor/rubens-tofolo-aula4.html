Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Dr. Rubens is at a medical conference in Lisbon. A colleague asks, Have you ever been to Portugal? No, this is his first journey here. He has already visited the Belem Tower, a famous landmark, but he hasn't explored Alfama yet. He has never tried Portuguese cuisine, so tomorrow is on his itinerary. It has been an unforgettable trip.

1. Como o colega pergunta sobre a experiencia de vida? / How does the colleague ask about a life experience?
A Did you ever been to Portugal?
B Have you ever been to Portugal?
C Are you ever in Portugal?
2. O que ele ainda NAO fez? / What hasn't he done?
A He hasn't visited the tower.
B He hasn't explored Alfama yet.
C He hasn't registered yet.
3. Ele ja provou a culinaria local? / Has he tried the local cuisine?
A No, he has never tried it.
B Yes, he has already tried it.
C Yes, twice.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

have/has + past participle. Quatro palavras-chave. / Four key words.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Have you + ever + past participle?Perguntar experiencia de vida / Ask a life experienceHave you ever been to Japan?
I have never + past participleNunca na vida / Not once in your lifeI have never tried sushi.
I have already + past participleJa, antes de agora / Done before nowI have already seen it.
I haven't + past participle + yetAinda nao, mas vou / Not done, but expectedI haven't eaten yet.

Chapter 3

The Present Perfect

ever · never · already · yet

Discover the Rule

Listen and Notice

"Have you ever been to Lisbon?"

"I have never tried Portuguese cuisine."

"I have already visited the castle."

"I haven't seen the cathedral yet."

StructureUseExample
have/has + ever + past participle?Ask about a life experienceHave you ever been to Japan?
have/has + never + past participleNot once in your whole lifeI have never tried sushi.
have/has + already + past participleDone before nowI have already seen it.
haven't/hasn't + past participle + yetNot done, but expectedI haven't eaten yet.

No specific time. The experience matters, not when it happened.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"Did you ever been to Lisbon?"

✓ RIGHT

"Have you ever been to Lisbon?"

✗ WRONG

"I already have seen the tower."

✓ RIGHT

"I have already seen the tower."
