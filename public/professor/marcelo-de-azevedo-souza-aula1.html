Chapter 3: The Code

Your Story in Three Tenses

I have cycled · I run · I learned

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have cycled through Albania, Morocco and Yemen."

"I run operations for a pharma logistics company."

"I have been into soul music since I was a teenager."

"I learned French when I worked in France."

Why green for life experience, but blue for a finished time?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Life experience up to now, no specific time (ever / never / since)I have cycled through Morocco.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Permanent facts, who you are now, your role and routineI run operations.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		Finished action at a finished time (in 2005, when I was young)I learned French in France.

Have you ever...? for experience · since + a starting point (since I was young).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have visited Morocco in 2010."
"I visited Morocco in 2010."
"I am into soul music since I was young."
"I have been into soul music since I was young."

A finished time (in 2010, yesterday) always uses the past simple. Experience and duration up to now use have / has + past participle with since / for.

Chapter 4: The Encounter

Meeting a Cyclist

The conversation begins

Dialogue

Nice to Meet You

E
Hi! You have come a long way on that bike. I am Elin, and I cycle these fjords every summer.
M
Hi Elin, nice to meet you! I am Marcelo, from Brazil. I have cycled through Albania and Morocco, but these fjords blew me away.
E
Brazil! Now that is really off the beaten path for a cyclist up here. So, what do you do back home?
M
I run operations for a pharma logistics company. But honestly, what I am really into is the open road and old soul music.
E
A kindred spirit! I could talk about travel for hours on end. Shall we grab a coffee?
M
I would love that. I think we are going to hit it off, and I bet we will bond over some good stories.
