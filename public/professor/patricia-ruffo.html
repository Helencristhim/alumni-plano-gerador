Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (coisas que acontecem regularmente ou são verdades)
Present Perfect = experiences, achievements, actions connected to now (experiências, conquistas, ações com conexão ao presente)

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"She works at Abbott."
Present PerfectSubject + have/has + past participle"She has worked there for 20 years."

Common Mistake:

"I work here since 2015."

"I have worked here since 2015."

Use Present Perfect com "since" e "for" para indicar duração até o presente.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

First Conditional = real possibilities in the future (possibilidades reais no futuro)
Opinion Frames = phrases to introduce opinions politely (frases para introduzir opiniões educadamente)

StructureFormulaExample
First ConditionalIf + present simple, will + base form"If we change the formula, the results will improve."
Opinion FrameFrame + opinion"In my opinion, we should continue."

5 Opinion Frames

  • "In my opinion,..." / "Na minha opinião,..."
  • "I see your point, but..." / "Entendo seu ponto, mas..."
  • "Based on the evidence,..." / "Com base nas evidências,..."
  • "I would argue that..." / "Eu argumentaria que..."
  • "From my perspective,..." / "Da minha perspectiva,..."

Common Mistake:

"If we will change the formula, the results will improve."

"If we change the formula, the results will improve."

Depois de "if", use o presente simples -- NUNCA "will".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Second Conditional = situações hipotéticas ou irreais (hypothetical or unreal situations)
Past Simple vs Present Perfect = revisão aprofundada (deepened review)

StructureFormulaExample
Second ConditionalIf + past simple, would + base form"If we had a larger sample, the results would be more reliable."
Past SimpleSubject + verb (past) + specific time"We published the results last year."
Present PerfectSubject + have/has + past participle"We have published three papers on this topic."

5 Smart Question Frames

  • "Could you elaborate on...?" / "Poderia detalhar...?"
  • "Have you considered the possibility that...?" / "Você considerou a possibilidade de que...?"
  • "If you were to..., what would...?" / "Se você fosse..., o que...?"
  • "What are the implications of...?" / "Quais são as implicações de...?"
  • "Is there a correlation between...?" / "Há uma correlação entre...?"

Common Mistake:

"If we would have a larger sample, the results would be more reliable."

"If we had a larger sample, the results would be more reliable."

Depois de "if" no second conditional, use o passado simples -- NUNCA "would".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip: Reported Speech

Grammar

When we report what someone said, we shift the verb tense back (backshift).

Direct Speech Reported Speech Example
Present simple Past simple “The results are good.” → She said the results were good.
Present perfect Past perfect “We have completed it.” → She said they had completed it.
Will Would “I will present.” → He said he would present.
Can Could “We can finish.” → They said they could finish.
Reporting Verbs / Verbos de Relato
  • said (that) / disse (que)
  • told (someone) that / disse a (alguém) que
  • mentioned (that) / mencionou (que)
  • reported (that) / relatou (que)
Common Mistake:
Wrong: “He told that we need more data.”
Right: “He told us that we needed more data.”
‘Told’ sempre precisa de um objeto (told ME/US/HER). ‘Said’ nunca tem objeto direto de pessoa.

Present PerfectExperience / AchievementShe has published papers.

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I work here since 2015."
"I have worked here since 2015."

Use Present Perfect with "since" and "for" when the action continues to the present.
