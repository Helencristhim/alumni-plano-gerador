Objetivo do Programa

Este programa Private de 40 aulas foi desenhado exclusivamente para Vanessa Maluf, advogada corporativa da Braskem com nivel B2, que enfrenta um teto de aprendizado caracteristico de quem passou anos em turmas de grupo: tempo de fala reduzido, aulas que nao acompanham seu ritmo profissional e lacunas especificas em pronuncia e compreensão auditiva que persistem por falta de atenção individualizada. O programa resolve essas limitacoes ao oferecer 90 minutos inteiros de produção oral em cada aula, com foco triplo — fala, escuta e pronuncia — em contextos 100% relevantes para sua atuacao: reunioes com stakeholders internacionais, apresentacoes a boards, negociacoes contratuais cross-border e comunicação com escritorios no Reino Unido e EUA. A combinacao de 70% vocabulario corporativo com 30% juridico garante que Vanessa desenvolva fluencia com precisao lexical no seu campo, eliminando a inseguranca que a impede de performar nas interacoes que mais importam para sua carreira na Braskem.

Promessa Transformadora

De "tempo de fala menor e aulas arrastadas" a "pronuncia boa, ouvido bom e ingles fluindo nas reunioes que importam" — em 40 aulas sob medida, para mim.

Aula 01 Diagnostic Session + Personal & Professional Introduction

Objetivo: Apos esta aula, Vanessa sera capaz de se apresentar profissionalmente em ingles, descrevendo seu papel na Braskem com vocabulario corporativo apropriado e registro formal.

VOCABULARIO DA AULA

  1. deal with — lidar com | "I deal with corporate contracts on a daily basis."
  2. liaise with — fazer interface com | "I liaise with our international partners regularly."
  3. oversee — supervisionar | "I oversee the compliance department."
  4. ensure compliance — garantir conformidade | "My primary role is to ensure compliance with regulations."

  6. accountable for — responsavel por | "I am accountable for all corporate governance matters."
  7. regulatory framework — arcabouco regulatorio | "We operate within a complex regulatory framework."

Aula 03 Listening Lab 1: Tuning Into British English

Objetivo: Apos esta aula, Vanessa sera capaz de identificar e compreender padroes de connected speech em ingles britanico, incluindo linking, elision e weak forms.

VOCABULARIO DA AULA

  1. connected speech — fala conectada | "In connected speech, words blend together naturally."
  2. linking — ligacao entre palavras | "Linking makes 'an apple' sound like 'anapple'."
  3. elision — elisao/omissao de sons | "Elision means some sounds are dropped in fast speech."
  4. weak forms — formas fracas | "Function words often have weak forms in natural speech."
  5. catch (a phrase) — captar/entender | "I couldn't catch what he said in the meeting."
  6. gist — essencia/ideia geral | "I got the gist of the presentation but missed some details."

Aula 01 Diagnostic Session + Professional Introduction

Duracao: 90 minutos | Online

Criterio de sucesso: Ao final, Vanessa sera capaz de se apresentar profissionalmente por 2 minutos sem pausas longas, usando pelo menos 4 dos 7 itens de vocabulario ensinados.

Promessa transformadora: "Hoje voce vai descobrir exatamente onde esta e para onde vai — e ja vai sair falando."

Warm-up + Lead-in 10 min

Perguntas abertas para criar rapport e ativar ingles:

  1. "Vanessa, tell me about your week at Braskem. What's been keeping you busy?"
  2. "When was the last time you used English at work? How did it feel?"
  3. "If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about your English, what would it be?"
  4. "Do you interact more with British or American colleagues? Or both?"
  5. "What's the most challenging situation you've faced in English professionally?"

Presentation — Pre-teach Vocabulary 10 min

Introduza os 7 itens com drilling:

  • deal with — Drill: /diːl wɪð/ — "Repeat after me: I deal with corporate contracts." (3x)
  • liaise with — Drill: /liˈeɪz wɪð/ — ATENÇÃO: brasileiros tendem a pronunciar /liˈaɪz/. Corrija imediatamente.
  • oversee — Drill: /ˌəʊvəˈsiː/ — Stress on third syllable. "I oversee the compliance department."
  • ensure compliance — Drill: /ɪnˈʃɔːr kəmˈplaɪəns/ — ATENÇÃO: "ensure" nao e "insure".
  • stakeholder — Drill: /ˈsteɪkhəʊldər/ — Stress on first syllable.
  • accountable for — Drill: /əˈkaʊntəbl fɔːr/ — "I am accountable for..."
  • regulatory framework — Drill: /ˈreɡjʊlətɔːri ˈfreɪmwɜːk/ — ATENÇÃO: 6 silabas em "regulatory".

Presentation — Teach + CCQs 10 min

Contextualize o vocabulario no cenario profissional de Vanessa. Mostre como essas palavras aparecem em uma self-introduction formal.

Modelo: "Good morning everyone. I'm Vanessa Maluf, and I oversee corporate governance at Braskem. I deal with regulatory compliance and liaise with our international legal partners. I'm accountable for ensuring we operate within the regulatory framework across all jurisdictions."

CCQs (Concept Checking Questions)
  • CCQ 1: "If you 'deal with' contracts, does that mean you read them once, or you work with them regularly?"
    Resposta esperada: Work with them regularly.
  • CCQ 2: "If you 'liaise with' international partners, are you their boss, or do you communicate and coordinate with them?"
    Resposta esperada: Communicate and coordinate.
  • CCQ 3: "If you are 'accountable for' something, can you blame someone else if it goes wrong?"
    Resposta esperada: No, you are responsible.

Practice 15 min

Exercicio 1: Gap-fill oral (5 min)

Professor le frases com gaps. Vanessa completa oralmente:

  • "In my role, I ___ corporate contracts daily." (deal with)
  • "I ___ with our partners in London." (liaise)
  • "I ___ the legal team of 12 lawyers." (oversee)
  • "I am ___ all governance matters." (accountable for)

Exercicio 2: Sentence building (10 min)

Vanessa constroi frases sobre seu trabalho real usando cada um dos 7 itens. Professor corrige pronunica e registro em tempo real.

Instrucoes ao Professor
  • Se Vanessa travar em "liaise", reformule como: "Think of it as 'communicate and coordinate'. Who do you communicate with at work?"
  • Se usar "responsible for" ao inves de "accountable for", aceite mas mostre a diferenca: "Both work, but 'accountable' is stronger — it means you can't delegate the blame."
  • Anote todos os erros de pronuncia para feedback no final — NAO interrompa o fluxo durante a produção.

Production 20 min

Extended Speaking Task (Diagnostic)

Setup: "Vanessa, imagine you're at a corporate event in London. You've just been introduced to the Head of Legal at a FTSE 100 company. Introduce yourself — your name, your company, your role, what you deal with daily, and one current project or challenge. Speak for at least 2 minutes. I'll listen without interrupting."

Listening Diagnostic (10 min)

Play two short clips (1-2 min each): one UK speaker, one US speaker discussing a corporate topic. After each, ask Vanessa to summarise what she understood. Note comprehension gaps.

Aula 03 Listening Lab 1: Tuning Into British English

Duracao: 90 minutos | Online

Criterio de sucesso: Ao final, Vanessa sera capaz de identificar pelo menos 3 padroes de connected speech em um audio britanico e explicar por que perdeu compreensão em pontos especificos.

Promessa transformadora: "Hoje voce vai entender POR QUE nao entende — e isso muda tudo."

Warm-up + Lead-in 10 min

  1. "Did you bring the 5 phrases from the TED Talk homework? Tell me about one you found tricky."
  2. "When you listen to British English, what's the hardest part? Speed? Accent? Vocabulary?"
  3. "Have you noticed any difference between understanding Americans vs British people?"

Presentation — Pre-teach Vocabulary 10 min

  • connected speech — Explain: "In real life, people don't speak word... by... word. Words connect."
  • linking — Demo: "an apple" → /əˈnæpəl/. Show how final consonant links to next vowel.
  • elision — Demo: "next day" → /neksdeɪ/ (the /t/ disappears). "Last night" → /lɑːsnaɪt/.
  • weak forms — Demo: "to" in "I want to go" = /tə/, not /tuː/. "Can" = /kən/ not /kæn/.
  • catch — "I couldn't catch what he said" = understand in real time.
  • gist — /dʒɪst/ — "I got the gist" = I understood the main idea.

Presentation — Teach + CCQs 10 min

Play a 30-second BBC clip at normal speed. Then slow it down. Show how linking, elision and weak forms work in that specific clip.

CCQs
  • CCQ 1: "In 'next day', which sound disappears?"
    Resposta: The /t/ — elision.
  • CCQ 2: "When we say 'an apple' quickly, do we pause between the words?"
    Resposta: No — they link together.
  • CCQ 3: "In 'I want to go', is 'to' pronounced /tuː/ or /tə/?"
    Resposta: /tə/ — weak form.

Practice 15 min

Exercicio 1: Transcription challenge (8 min)

Play BBC Business Daily excerpt (2-3 min). Vanessa transcribes what she hears. Compare with actual transcript. Identify where connected speech caused comprehension loss.

Exercicio 2: Repeat-after-me drilling (7 min)

Take the sentences Vanessa missed. Break down the connected speech pattern. Drill at slow, medium, then natural speed. Focus on her producing the connected forms.

Production 20 min

Extended Listening + Discussion

Play a longer BBC clip (3-4 min) on a corporate topic. Vanessa listens once for gist, then again for detail. After each listen, she summarises orally. Third listen: she notes specific connected speech patterns she now recognizes.

Discussion: "What strategies helped you understand more on the second listen?"

Wrap-up + Feedback 5 min

  • "What was the most surprising thing you learned today about how English sounds?"
  • "Which connected speech feature affects your understanding the most?"

Homework: Listen to a TED Talk (UK speaker). Note 5 phrases you didn't catch. Re-listen until you understand. Bring them to class.

Obstaculos Antecipados
  • Frustracao: Vanessa pode se frustrar ao perceber o quanto perde. Normalize: "This is completely normal at B2. Awareness is the first step to improvement."
  • Querer entender tudo: Ensine a estrategia de "let go": "You don't need every word. Focus on stressed words for meaning."
  • Confusao entre linking e elision: Use exemplos claros. Linking = adding connection. Elision = removing a sound.
