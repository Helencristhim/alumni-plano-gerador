Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Present Perfect

Grammar

We use the Present Perfect to talk about past actions with a connection to the present — when the result matters more than the exact time.

Usamos o Present Perfect para ações passadas com conexão ao presente — quando o resultado importa mais do que o momento exato.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + have/has + past participle"We have finalized the contract."
NegativeSubject + haven't/hasn't + past participle"They haven't reviewed the minutes yet."
QuestionHave/Has + subject + past participle?"Have you submitted the report?"

Keywords: already, yet, just, recently, so far, ever, never

I have finish the report.

I have finished the report.

O Present Perfect usa sempre o particípio passado (finished, not finish).

She has submit the minutes yesterday.

She submitted the minutes yesterday.

Tempo específico (yesterday) = Past Simple, NÃO Present Perfect.

Stage 1.4 — Grammar Tip: Present Perfect + for and since

Use Structure Example
for + duration have/has + past participle + for + period "I have worked here for eight years."
since + point in time have/has + past participle + since + moment "She has been here since 2018."
Duration examples (FOR):

Point in time examples (SINCE):

Grammar

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — First & Second Conditional

Type If clause Result clause Example
First (real) If + present simple will + base verb "If they reject, we will escalate."
Second (hypothetical) If + past simple would + base verb "If I were in their position, I would push back."
Modal verbs in conditionals: will, would, might, could
Pronúncia: contrações — "we'll" /wiːl/, "I'd" /aɪd/, "they'd" /ðeɪd/. Entonação: sobe na cláusula if, desce na cláusula resultado.
Common Mistakes
"If they will reject the clause, we escalate." → "If they reject the clause, we will escalate."
PT: Na if clause do First Conditional, use present simple — NÃO will.
"If I was in their position, I will push back." → "If I were in their position, I would push back."
PT: No Second Conditional, use 'were' para todos os sujeitos e 'would' no resultado.

Same Action — Different Focus

First & Second Conditionals — Real vs. Hypothetical

Grammar Discovery — First Conditional

What do the orange words have in common?

"If the counterparty rejects the clause, we will escalate to arbitration."
"If they accept the amendment, we will finalize the agreement by Friday."
"If we miss the deadline, we will face penalties."
Grammar Discovery — Second Conditional

Now what's different?

"If I were in their position, I would push for a shorter deadline."
"If the contract were governed by UK law, we would need to revise three clauses."
"If they backed out of the deal, we would revise our entire strategy."
The Rule

First vs. Second Conditional

Type If clause Result clause Use
First (real) present simple will + base verb likely scenarios
Second (hypothetical) past simple / were would + base verb imaginary / unlikely
Common Mistake: "If they will reject..." → "If they reject..."
