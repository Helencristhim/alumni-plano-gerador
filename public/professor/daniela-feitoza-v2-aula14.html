Chapter 1: The Planning Floor

From Opinion to Plan

In Lesson 13 you learned to give your opinion with tact: "In my view, a small pilot is reasonable."

Chapter 1: The Planning Floor

Three Ways to See the Future

"We are going to launch the pilot." (a plan we decided)

"I think it will save us time." (a prediction)

"We are meeting the vendor on Friday." (a fixed arrangement)

Same future, three different jobs. Today we learn which is which.

Chapter 3

The Future Rule

Going to, will and the present continuous

Future Discovery

Listen and Notice

We are going to run a pilot in two stores.

I think it will save us time.

We are meeting the vendor on Friday.

Look at the data — sales are going to grow.

Which one is a decided plan? Which one is a prediction? Which one is a fixed arrangement?

Future Rule

Plan, Predict, Arrange

FormUseExample
am / is / are going to + verbA decided plan or strong evidence"We are going to run a pilot."
will + verbA prediction or a decision made now"It will save us time. I will send it."
am / is / are + verb-ingA fixed arrangement with a time"We are meeting the vendor on Friday."
Common Mistake

Wrong Form vs Right Form

✗ WRONG

"I will to send the report."

✓ RIGHT

"I will send the report." (will + base verb)

✗ WRONG

"We are going to meeting on Friday."

✓ RIGHT

"We are going to meet on Friday." (going to + base verb)

After "will" and after "going to", use the base verb — never "to" and never "-ing".

Chapter 4

The Planning Room

Daniela and Marcus plan the pilot kickoff

Dialogue

Planning the Kickoff

M
Daniela, the pilot is approved. When are we going to start?
D
We are going to launch in two stores. The kickoff is next Monday.
M
Good. What is the first deliverable on the roadmap?
D
A working checkout screen. I intend to ship it in three weeks, and I will allocate two engineers.
M
And if the new system fails on day one?
D
Our contingency is to keep the old system for a month. We anticipate small issues.
M
What does the forecast say about sales?
D
The projection is good. I think the pilot will work, and we are presenting the results in May.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. When is the kickoff, and how do they talk about it?
The kickoff is next Monday; it is a fixed arrangement ("the kickoff is next Monday", "we are presenting in May").
2. What is the first deliverable, and what does Daniela intend to do?
A working checkout screen; she intends to ship it in three weeks and will allocate two engineers.
3. What is the contingency, and what is the prediction?
The contingency is to keep the old system for a month; the prediction is that the pilot will work ("I think the pilot will work").
Listening

The Coach’s Tip

A voice note about talking about the future at work. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. According to Sarah, when do you use "going to"?
For a plan you already decided, like "We are going to run a pilot."
2. What is "will" good for?
For a prediction or a decision made at the moment, like "I think it will help" or "I will send it now."
3. Which form does Sarah use for a meeting already in the calendar?
The present continuous, like "We are meeting the vendor on Friday."

Free Plan

Present Your Project

Speak for one minute, presenting a project plan in a meeting:

Choose a real project at work. Say what you are going to do (the plan), when the kickoff is and what arrangements are fixed, what you predict will happen, the first deliverable, and your contingency if something goes wrong.

No keywords. Focus on choosing the right future form, not on speed.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I can talk about a decided plan with "going to" + base verb.
I can make a prediction or a decision now with "will" + base verb.
I can talk about a fixed arrangement with the present continuous.
I never say "will to" or "going to -ing" — I use the base verb.
I can use planning words: forecast, intend, roadmap, kickoff, deliverable, contingency.
