Chapter 1: The Open Floor

Same Idea, Three Tones

"You are wrong. The rollout is too fast." (too direct)

"In my view, the rollout is too fast." (clear)

"I see your point, but the rollout might be a little fast." (clear + soft)

Today we learn the words that make the third one work.

The Soft Rule

How to open, hedge and disagree politely

Which words open an opinion? Which ones soften it? Which one disagrees politely?

Opinion Rule

Open, Soften, Disagree

MovePhrasesExample
OpenIn my view, / From my perspective, / It seems to me that"In my view, we should test it."
Soften (hedge)might / could / perhaps / a little / I think"It might take a little longer."
Disagree politelyI see your point, but... / I am not sure that..."I see your point, but I would test it first."
Common Mistake

Wrong Tone vs Right Tone

✗ WRONG

"I am agree with you."

✓ RIGHT

"I agree with you." (agree is a verb, no "am")

✗ TOO DIRECT

"You are wrong."

✓ SOFTER

"I see your point, but I am not sure that is the best option."

"Agree" and "disagree" are verbs — never "I am agree". And soften strong statements in a meeting.

Chapter 4

The Debate Room

Daniela and Marcus discuss adopting new technology

Dialogue

Should We Adopt It?

M
Daniela, what do you think? Should we adopt the new platform this quarter?
D
From my perspective, it is a good tool, but it might be a little fast for this quarter.
M
I think we are ready. Waiting is the bigger risk.
D
I see your point, but I am not sure the team is trained yet. The downside is a rushed launch.
M
That is a valid concern. So what do you suggest?
D
It seems to me a small pilot first would be reasonable. Perhaps two stores.
M
A pilot. I like that. You almost persuaded me.
D
Then I agree: we pilot in two stores, and we do not overstate the risk.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is Marcus's opinion, and what is Daniela's main concern?
Marcus thinks they should adopt the platform now; Daniela is concerned the team is not trained yet and the downside is a rushed launch.
2. Which phrase does Daniela use to disagree politely?
"I see your point, but I am not sure the team is trained yet." She opens with "From my perspective" and "It seems to me".
3. What solution do they agree on in the end?
A small pilot first, in two stores, without overstating the risk.
Listening

The Coach’s Tip

A voice note about giving opinions at work. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. What is the first thing Sarah says you should do with an opinion?
Open with a clear phrase, like "In my view, the new tool will save us time."
2. What are softeners, according to Sarah, and what do they do?
Words like might, perhaps and a little; they make you sound careful, not weak.
3. How should you disagree, and what should you never say?
Stay polite ("I see your point, but I think we should test it first") and never say "you are wrong".
Listening

Marcus’s Feedback

A colleague gives feedback on how Daniela shared her opinion. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. What did Daniela do well, according to Marcus?
She opened with a strong, clear and confident phrase: "From my perspective, we should adopt the new platform."
2. What one thing does Marcus suggest she soften?
When the team disagreed, she said they were wrong; he suggests "I see your point, but I am not sure that is the best option."
3. According to Marcus, is hedging weak?
No — saying "it might work" or "perhaps we could try a pilot" is not weak, it is professional and sounds senior.

Chapter 6

Your Turn

From guided answers to your own opinion

Guided Drill

Give Your View

Task: Your teacher asks for your opinion. Open with a phrase and soften it when you are not sure.

Answer these:

Should we adopt the new tool now? Is six weeks enough for the rollout? Do you think the team is ready? What is the downside?
Semi-Free

Disagree with Respect

Task: Your teacher gives a strong opinion. Disagree politely and offer a softer idea, using the starters.

Use these starters:

I see your point, but... From my perspective,... It might be better to... The downside is...
Free Opinion

Share Your Opinion

Speak for one minute, giving your opinion in a meeting:

Should your company adopt a new technology this year? Open with your view, soften it where you are not sure, mention one downside, and disagree politely with one common objection. End with a reasonable next step, like Daniela's pilot.

No keywords. Focus on a clear, polite tone, not on speed.
