Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tânia is at a souvenir shop called Bella Souvenirs. She sees a beautiful blue scarf and asks: "How much is this scarf?" The shopkeeper says: "Thirty-five euros." Tânia also sees a red scarf and asks: "Is the red one cheaper?" The shopkeeper says: "Yes, the red one is twenty-eight euros. It is smaller, but very popular." Tânia wants both and asks: "Is there a discount if I buy two?" The shopkeeper gives her a good deal: fifty-five euros instead of sixty-three. Tânia says: "Could I pay with a credit card?" She gets her receipt and leaves happy with her souvenirs.

1. How much is the blue scarf?
A Twenty-eight euros.
B Thirty-five euros.
C Fifty-five euros.
2. Which scarf is cheaper?
A The blue scarf.
B The red scarf.
C They cost the same.
3. How does Tânia pay?
A In cash.
B With a credit card.
C The text does not say.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Aqui está a regra. Leia com calma e veja os exemplos.

"How much" + Comparatives (Preços e comparações)

Use "How much is/does" para perguntar preços. Use comparativos para comparar itens: adjetivos curtos + -er (cheap → cheaper) ou more + adjetivos longos (expensive → more expensive). / Use "How much is/does" for prices. Use comparatives: short adj + -er (cheap → cheaper) or more + long adj (expensive → more expensive).

StructureExampleTradução
How much is + nounHow much is this scarf?Quanto custa este lenço?
How much does + noun + costHow much does the bag cost?Quanto custa a bolsa?
Short adj + -er + thanThis one is cheaper than that one.Este é mais barato que aquele.
more + long adj + thanThe blue scarf is more expensive than the red one.O lenço azul é mais caro que o vermelho.
bigger / smaller / betterDo you have this in a bigger size?Você tem isso em um tamanho maior?
Common Mistake / Erro Comum:
"How much it costs?" ✗ → "How much does it cost?" ✓
"This is more cheap." ✗ → "This is cheaper." ✓
Lembre: How much + does/is (auxiliar obrigatório). Short adjectives use -er, not "more".
