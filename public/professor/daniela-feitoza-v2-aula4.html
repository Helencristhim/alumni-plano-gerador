Chapter 3

The Article Rule

a / an for general, the for specific

Article Discovery

Listen and Notice

I need a new server for the project.

She is an experienced IT manager.

The conference starts at nine.

I work at the SPF Group.

When is it general (a / an)? When is it specific (the)?

Article Rule

a / an vs the

ArticleUseExample
aGeneral, first time, before a consonant sound"a server, a meeting"
anGeneral, before a vowel sound"an alert, an hour, an IT manager"
theSpecific, both people know which one"the conference, the SPF Group"
no articleGeneral plurals and uncountable ideas"I manage servers and data."

Rhythm tip: a, an and the are weak — say them short and quiet, and stress the big word after.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am manager at company."

✓ RIGHT

"I am a manager at the company." (a + job, the + specific)

✗ WRONG

"She is a engineer."

✓ RIGHT

"She is an engineer." (vowel sound = an)

A singular job always needs a or an. Use an before a vowel sound.

Chapter 4

At the SPF Group

Daniela and Marcus talk about the week

Dialogue

Planning the Week

M
Daniela, the conference starts at nine on Monday, right?
D
Yes. It is an important event for the whole IT department.
M
And the new server? Do we have a decision yet?
D
Not yet. We need a final answer today. The vendor is sending an engineer.
M
Good. Where is the meeting?
D
The room on the third floor is busy, so we use the room next to reception.
M
Perfect. One last thing — how do you pronounce the vendor's name?
D
It is "No-VA-tech" — the stress is on the second syllable.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. When does the conference start, and what does Marcus ask about?
It starts at nine on Monday; Marcus asks about the conference and the new server decision.
2. Who is the vendor sending?
The vendor is sending an engineer for the new server.
3. Why do they change the meeting room?
The room on the third floor is busy, so they use the room next to reception.
Listening

The Coach’s Tip

A voice note about articles and rhythm. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. According to Sarah, when do we use a or an?
For something general, mentioned for the first time, like "I need a new laptop" or "She is an experienced manager."
2. When do we use the?
When both people know exactly which one, like "The conference starts at nine."
3. What does Sarah say about rhythm?
English is a stress language: we push the important words and keep the small words, like the article the, short and quiet.

Self-Assessment

What I Learned

I use a / an for something general and the for something specific.
I use an before a vowel sound — an alert, an hour, an engineer.

I keep articles weak and stress the strong words for natural rhythm.
I can talk about sound: stress, rhythm, syllable, intonation, vowel, consonant.
