Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Job Descriptions

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + base verbI work at Bloomberg. / She works in supply chain.
NegativeSubject + do/does + not + base verbI do not deal with clients. / She does not work in marketing.
QuestionDo/Does + subject + base verb?Do you work with vendors? / Does she manage the team?

Key expressions:

  • "I work at..." — descrever onde trabalha
  • "I am responsible for..." — descrever responsabilidades
  • "I deal with..." — descrever com o que lida no dia a dia
  • "My role involves..." — descrever o que o cargo envolve
  • "I report to..." — descrever para quem reporta
Note: Use "am responsible for" (NOT "am responsible to do").
Note: "My role involves + -ing" (gerund): "My role involves managing..."

Common Mistake
I am work at Bloomberg.
I work at Bloomberg.
She responsible for procurement.
She is responsible for procurement.

Do NOT use "am/is" before action verbs in Present Simple. But DO use "am/is" before adjectives like "responsible."
