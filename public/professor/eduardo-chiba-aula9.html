Objection -- a reason someone gives against a proposal
"Their main objection was the price."
Concern -- a worry or doubt about something
"I understand your concern about the timing."
Address -- to deal with or respond to a concern directly
"Let me address your concern about support."
Acknowledge -- to recognize and accept what someone says
"I acknowledge that the setup takes effort."
Reassure -- to remove someone's doubts and make them feel confident
"Let me reassure you: we'll run a free pilot first."
Hesitant -- unsure; slow to act because of doubt
"The client was hesitant at first."
Win-Win -- a result where both sides benefit
"Free support makes it a win-win for both of us."
Track Record -- a history of past performance and results
"Our track record speaks for itself."

Stage 1.2: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read a debrief of a lost deal. Notice the third conditional, then answer.

"The client was hesitant from the start. If we had addressed their concern about onboarding earlier, they wouldn't have hesitated. And if we had offered a pilot, they would have signed. I understand the price worried them. That said, our track record is strong. Next time, we reassure on day one."
In "If we had offered a pilot, they would have signed", what does it mean?
A They signed.
B They did NOT sign (imagined past).
C They will sign tomorrow.
Which phrase concedes a point but turns the argument?
A "You are wrong."
B "I understand the price worried them. That said, our track record is strong."
C "No comment."

Stage 1.3: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Use the third conditional to analyze the past; use concession to handle the present.

UseFormExample
Imagined past (debrief)If + past perfect, would have + past participle"If we had offered a pilot, they would have signed."
AcknowledgeI understand / I take your point"I understand your concern."
Concede & turnThat said... / Even so..."That said, our track record is strong."

Watch out: never say "if we would have". After "if" use the past perfect: "if we had offered". And say "I understand" (not "I am understand").

Chapter 5: Practice

Handle It Step by Step

Acknowledge a price objection.
"I understand your concern about the price. It's an important point."
Concede, then turn with your track record.
"That said, our track record speaks for itself: partners see results in the first quarter."
Reassure on risk.
"Let me reassure you: we'll run a free pilot, so you take no risk before you commit."
Reframe as a win-win.
"Free support makes this a win-win: you get help, and we get a happy partner."
Debrief a lost deal (third conditional).
"If we had addressed their concern earlier, they wouldn't have hesitated, and we would have closed it."
Concede a small point, hold your value.
"You're right that setup takes effort. Even so, the ROI is worth it within three months."
