“I want to share a story from Ghana. Three years ago, we had almost no compliance infrastructure. Match-fixing was a serious problem, and public trust in football was very low. We started by hiring five dedicated compliance officers and training them at FIFA headquarters in Zurich. We then implemented a whistleblower hotline and anonymous reporting system. Today, we have investigated over fifty cases and achieved twelve successful prosecutions. The key lesson for all of us is this: compliance is not a cost. It is an investment in the future of football.”
