Chapter 3 -- The Code

Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

When did it happen? Does it still matter now?

Chapter 3 -- The Code

What do the highlighted words have in common?

"I graduated from dental school in 1995."
"I traveled to Canada in 2000."
"I have worked as a dentist for over 25 years."
"I have traveled to many countries."

The Rule

Past SimplePresent Perfect
Finished action at a specific timeExperience or result connected to now
I graduated in 1995.I have worked as a dentist for 25 years.
I traveled to Canada in 2000.I have traveled to many countries.
Keywords: yesterday, in 2000, last year, agoKeywords: ever, never, already, yet, for, since
Subject + V2 (past)Subject + have/has + V3 (past participle)
Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"I have traveled to Canada in 2000."
"I traveled to Canada in 2000."
Why? "In 2000" is a specific time in the past. Use Past Simple with specific times. Use Present Perfect when the time is not important or not mentioned.

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to reveal the correction.

"I have graduated from dental school in 1995."
"I graduated from dental school in 1995." (Specific time = Past Simple)
"She traveled to many countries in her life."
"She has traveled to many countries in her life." (No specific time, life experience = Present Perfect)
"I have opened my clinic in 1998."
"I opened my clinic in 1998." (Specific time = Past Simple)
"He has went to Australia twice."
"He has gone to Australia twice." (Past participle of go = gone, not went)
