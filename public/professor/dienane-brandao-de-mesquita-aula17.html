Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Talking about the past with was and were

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we talk about the past?

"Yesterday I was at home."

"My sister was here too."

"We were very happy."

"The children were not tired."

Grammar Rule

Was and Were

SubjectPast of "to be"Example
IwasI was at home.
He / She / ItwasShe was here.
We / You / TheywereWe were happy.
Negativewas not / were notI was not tired.

Key idea: to talk about the past with "to be", use was and were. With I, he, she, it use was. With we, you, they use were. To say no, add not: I was not, we were not.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We was very happy."
"We were very happy."

Use was with I, he, she, it. Use were with we, you, they. Never say "we was" -- say "we were".

D

"Tired? Why? Were you sick?"

M

"No, I was not sick. Last week was busy at work. But the weekend was quiet."

D

"I am happy it was calm. Were your children at home too?"
