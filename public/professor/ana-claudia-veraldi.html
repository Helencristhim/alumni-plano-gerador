Metodologia

1. Da Leitura à Fala

A Ana já lê inglês técnico. Cada aula converte esse conhecimento passivo em produção oral ativa, com muita fala e repetição em contexto real.

2. Contexto Real de Tech & QA

Todo conteúdo usa o mundo dela: testes, bugs, releases, reuniões remotas, entrevistas. Ela pratica exatamente as conversas que vai ter de verdade.

3. Listening Sob Pressão

Exposição gradual a sotaques variados e fala rápida, treinando a captar o CONTEXTO geral sem travar em cada palavra — a habilidade que mais a bloqueia.

4. Disciplina e Estrutura

A Ana prefere um professor com certa rigidez e gosta de anotar. As aulas têm estrutura clara, metas por bloco e espaço para registro no caderno.

5. Correção Adiada e Diagnóstica

Na conversa, fluência primeiro; correção depois. O professor anota padrões (perfect tenses, condicionais) e devolve feedback focado, sem interromper a produção.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Present Simple vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your career with the right tense.

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now, with for / since, when the time is not finished.I have worked here for 6 years.
just + present perfectVery recent news.We have just deployed the new version.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Facts and routines, what you do every day.I test software every day.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		A finished action, in a finished time (in 2018, at WIPRO, yesterday).I worked at WIPRO for six years.
for vs sincefor + a period (for 6 years) · since + a starting point (since 2018)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to switch between your routine and what is happening right now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines and facts, what you do regularly (every day, usually, always, often).I check the backlog every morning.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions happening now or around now, temporary (right now, at the moment, today, this week).Right now, I am testing the login feature.
He / she / it formAdd -s in the present simple; use is + -ing in the continuous.The lead assigns tickets. She is fixing a bug.
Stative verbsknow, understand, need, want stay simple: "I know the workflow," never "I am knowing".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs Past Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to describe what happened when you found a bug.

UseFormExample
Action in progresswas / were + -ingI was testing the checkout.
Sudden, completed eventpast simpleThe page crashed.
Background + interruptionpast continuous + when + past simpleI was testing when it crashed.
Two background actionswhile + past continuousWhile the app was loading, I was watching the log.
Common mistakeDo not drop the -ing: NOT "I was test". Do not use the past continuous for a sudden event: NOT "the page was crashing when I clicked".

The Daily Stand-Up -- Giving Updates

Giving a clear daily stand-up update to your team: what you did yesterday, what you have done so far, and your blockers. Key words: stand-up, blocker, recap, to wrap up, to sync up, on track, behind schedule, heads-up, to follow up, deliverable, to loop in, action item. Structure: present perfect (news now) vs past simple (a finished time).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Stand-up -- a short daily meeting where the team shares quick progress updates
"We start every morning with a fifteen-minute stand-up."
Blocker -- something that stops you from making progress on a task
"My only blocker is that I am waiting for the new build."
Recap -- a short summary of what has happened so far
"Here is a quick recap of yesterday's testing."
To wrap up -- to finish or complete a task or discussion

On track -- going as planned, on schedule

Behind schedule -- later than the planned time

"Just a heads-up: the test server will be down at noon."

"I will follow up with the dev team after the stand-up."
Deliverable -- a specific piece of work you must finish and hand over
"The test report is my main deliverable for this sprint."
To loop in -- to add someone to a message so they have the information
"Can you loop in the developers on this thread?"
Action item -- a specific task the team agrees to do after a meeting

GRAMMAR

B It is happening at this moment.
C It is a general fact that is always true.
2. "So far this morning, she has found one more bug" uses the present perfect because:
A It happened at a specific, finished time.
B It is the news up to now, with no specific time.
C It is a plan for tomorrow.
3. In the report, "she has already written the first report" tells us that:
A She will write it later today.
B The report is finished now, and that is the news.
C She wrote it a long time ago and it does not matter now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

How to switch between a finished time and the news right now.

FormUseExample
Past Simple
verb + -ed / irregular		A finished action at a specific past time (yesterday, on Monday, last week, at 9 am).Yesterday, I tested the checkout page.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		News and results that matter now, with no specific time (just, already, yet, so far).So far, I have found two bugs.
Questions & negativesPresent perfect for news: have/has + participle. Past simple for a set time: did + base verb.Have you written it yet? / Did you write it yesterday?
Watch outNever use a specific past time with the present perfect, and learn your irregular participles: run (not ran), written (not wrote), done (not did).
