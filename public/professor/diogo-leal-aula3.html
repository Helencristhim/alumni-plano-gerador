Grammar Discovery

What do the highlighted words have in common?

I usually start my day at seven.
I always check my emails first.
I rarely miss a deadline.
I am never late for meetings.

Adverbs of Frequency + Position

FrequencyAdverbExample
100%alwaysI always check my emails first.
90%usuallyI usually start at seven.
75%oftenI often coordinate with LATAM.
50%sometimesI sometimes have five meetings.
10%rarelyI rarely miss a deadline.
0%neverI am never late.

Position rule: BEFORE the main verb ("I usually start...") but AFTER "to be" ("I am never late.").

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Watch Out!

"I check always my emails."

Adverb after the verb = incorrect

"I always check my emails."

Adverb BEFORE the main verb = correct

"Always I am busy."

Adverb at the start with "to be" = incorrect

"I am always busy."

Adverb AFTER "to be" = correct

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to see the correction

"I check always my emails."
"I always check my emails." (adverb BEFORE the main verb)
"Usually I am start at seven."
"I usually start at seven." (no double verb, adverb before main verb)
"She attend three meetings."
"She attends three meetings." (third person -s)
"I am rarely late."
CORRECT! No error. "Am" + adverb after "to be" = perfect.
"He never is on time."
"He is never on time." (adverb AFTER "to be")
