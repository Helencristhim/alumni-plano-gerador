Hedging Language

In an international release standup, Ana must flag a risky bug and question the ship date. Tonight, she learns to soften her claims and sound diplomatically precise.

Chapter 1: Warm-Up

From Firm Advice to Diplomacy

Last lesson, you gave firm recommendations: I would rather, we had better, we may as well. Tonight, the goal is different. Think of a time you had to disagree with someone at work. How did you say it, and how did it land?

Same Message, Different Tone

Blunt vs Measured

Too blunt

"This is a bug. You are wrong. It will never work."

Measured

"This might be a bug. I'm not sure that's quite right. It could be difficult."

Softening a claim is called hedging. It keeps the conversation open and makes you sound credible, not unsure.

Chapter 3: The Code

The Hedging Toolkit

Four ways to soften a claim or an opinion

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This might be a regression, but I need to confirm it."

"The logs seem to suggest a timeout under load."

"Apparently, it only happens on the latest build."

"I'd say it could be worth running one more test."

Are these claims 100% certain, or softened? What is doing the softening in each one?

DeviceFunctionExample
Modal: might / could / maysoften a claim you are not sure ofThis might be a regression.
seem / appear / suggest / tend toreport cautiously, not as factThe logs suggest a timeout.
Adverbs: apparently, presumably, roughly, seeminglyadd caution to a statementIt's apparently intermittent.
Tentative openers: I'd say / it could be worth / as far as I can tellsoften an opinion or suggestionI'd say we hold the release.
Key ruleOne hedge per sentence is usually enough. Hedging makes you sound measured and diplomatic, not unsure — but too many hedges sound weak.

Use these to flag a risk, report a bug you have not confirmed, or disagree without sounding blunt.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"It might can be a regression."
"It might be a regression."
"No, you are wrong about the root cause."
"I'm not sure that's quite right about the root cause."
"The deploy will definitely fix everything."
"The deploy should fix most of it, I'd say."

Modals do not stack: never might can. To disagree, soften instead of contradicting. When you are not sure, qualify the claim instead of overstating it.

Dialogue

The Release Standup

V
Ana, are we good to ship the payment update tomorrow?
A
I'd say we're mostly there, though there's one thing I'd like to flag, tentatively.
V
Sure, go ahead. What's on your mind?
A
The logs seem to suggest an intermittent timeout under heavy load. I'm being cautious because it's not confirmed yet.
V
So, presumably, you'd rather we hold the release?
A
I wouldn't put it that strongly. It could be worth running one more load test first.
V
Fair enough. And roughly how long would that take?
A
Apparently about two hours. I'm fairly confident we'd know for sure by the afternoon.
V
That sounds sensible. Let's hold it until we're sure, then.

Usually one; if you pile them up, you sound unsure.
