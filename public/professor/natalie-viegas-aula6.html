Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Passive Voice in Business Writing

The passive voice is used when the ACTION is more important than WHO did it. It is very common in business reports, legal documents, and formal writing.
A voz passiva e usada quando a ACAO e mais importante do que quem a realizou. E muito comum em relatorios, documentos legais e escrita formal.

Tense / TempoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Past Simple Passive
Passado Simples Passivo		was/were + past participle
was/were + particípio		"The report was drafted."
O relatório foi redigido.
Present Perfect Passive
Presente Perfeito Passivo		has/have been + past participle
has/have been + particípio		"Recommendations have been made."
Recomendações foram feitas.
Future Passive
Futuro Passivo		will be + past participle
will be + particípio		"Changes will be implemented."
As mudanças serão implementadas.

Common mistakes / Erros comuns: "The report draft by the team." (WRONG -- needs was), "The findings was presented." (WRONG -- use were for plural), "The changes have implement." (WRONG -- needs been implemented).

Passive Voice Focus

PAST SIMPLE: Describe when the executive summary was approved.
"The executive summary was approved by the VP on Tuesday."
PRESENT PERFECT: Explain that a decision has been reached.
"A decision has been made regarding the new benchmarks."
FUTURE: State when the report will be submitted.
"The report will be submitted by the end of the month."
PAST SIMPLE: Explain who reviewed the findings.
"The findings were reviewed by the compliance team."
