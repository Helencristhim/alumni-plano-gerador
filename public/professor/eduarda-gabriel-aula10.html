Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

At the strategy meeting, Eduarda uses everything from this block. She opens by breaking the ice: "Nice to see you all again." She gives her opinion: "In my view, we should act now," and backs it up: "The data suggests the market will recover." She argues a consequence with a first conditional: "If we wait, we will lose the deal." When David jumps in to ask about the risk, she answers and then yields the floor: "Anna, please go ahead." Finally she reports and sums up: "Daniel said that we will move now. To sum up: act now, review in two weeks." Everyone is on the same page.

1. Which skill does Eduarda use to OPEN the meeting?
A Reporting verbs.
B Breaking the ice.
C Summarizing.
2. Which sentence is a first conditional?
A "In my view, we should act now."
B "If we wait, we will lose the deal."
C "Nice to see you all again."
3. How does she report what Daniel said?
A "Daniel said that we will move now."
B "Daniel said me to move now."
C "Daniel told that move now."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

The four structures of the block, in the order of a meeting (os quatro skills, na ordem de uma reunião):

SkillStructureExample
Question form (A6)Wh-/aux + subject + verb?"What do you do?"
Turn-taking (A7)Sorry to jump in, but... / Go ahead"Sorry to jump in, but..."
First conditional (A8)If + present, ... will + base"If we wait, we will lose."
Reporting verb (A9)said / told someone (that) + clause"He told me that..."

Top mistakes of the block:

"If we will wait..." / "He said me that..."

"If we wait..." / "He told me that..."

No IF use present simple (sem "will"); use "tell" com pessoa, "say" sem pessoa.

Common Mistakes

Watch Out!

"If we will wait, we will lose." / "He said me that..."
"If we wait, we will lose." / "He told me that..."

Keep present simple in the if-clause, and use tell + a person (not "say me").

Chapter 4 — Getting There

Dialogue

The Strategy Meeting

D
Welcome to the strategy meeting. Eduarda, would you like to start?
E
Sure. Nice to see you all again. In my view, we should act now.
A
Interesting. Can you back that up?
E
Of course. The data suggests the market will recover. If we wait, we will lose the deal.
D
Sorry to jump in, but what is the downside?
E
If the market falls, our capital stays protected. Anna, please go ahead.
A
Thanks. So, to sum up, we act now but stay flexible?
E
Exactly. The key takeaway: act now, review in two weeks. Are we all on the same page?
Comprehension

About the Meeting

1. What does Anna ask Eduarda to do?
To back up her opinion.
2. What does David want to know?
The downside (the risk).
3. How does Anna paraphrase the decision?
"Act now but stay flexible."
Artifact

Strategy Meeting Summary

STRATEGY MEETING — SUMMARY
Chair: David · Lead: Eduarda · Also: Anna
Decision: Act now, review in two weeks.

Opinion: In my view, we should act now.

Evidence: The data suggests the market will recover.

Risk: If the market falls, our capital stays protected.

Action items: Eduarda updates the model; David calls creditors; Anna prepares the timeline.

1. Which opinion frame opens the case?
"In my view, we should act now."
2. Which sentence is a first conditional?
"If the market falls, our capital stays protected."
3. How many action items are there?
Three.
