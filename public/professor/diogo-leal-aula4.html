Question Formation in English

TypeStructureExample
Yes/NoDo/Does + subject + verbDo you work in tech?
WhatWhat + do/does + subject + verbWhat do you do at Oracle?
How longHow long + have + subject + past participleHow long have you been here?
WhereWhere + are/do + subject + (verb)Where are you from?

Key rule: In questions, the auxiliary verb (do/does/have/are) goes BEFORE the subject. NEVER "Where you work?" -- always "Where do you work?"

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Watch Out!

"Where you work?"

Missing auxiliary "do"

"Where do you work?"

Auxiliary "do" before the subject = correct

"What you do at Oracle?"

Missing auxiliary "do" after Wh-word

"What do you do at Oracle?"

Wh-word + do + subject + verb = correct

Oral Drilling

Formulate the right question for each situation

Situation
You meet a CTO from Japan at a networking event. Start a conversation.
"Hi, I am Diogo. What do you do at your company? How long have you been in the tech industry?"
Situation
A new colleague joins your team at Oracle. Make small talk.
"Welcome to Oracle! Where are you from? What did you do before joining us?"
Situation
You are at a conference coffee break and want to know about someone's projects.
"What projects are you working on nowadays? Do you recommend any sessions this afternoon?"
Situation
A speaker just finished an impressive presentation. Start a conversation.
"I was really impressed by your presentation! How long have you been working on cloud solutions?"
Situation
You meet someone at a company dinner. Ask about their industry and experience.
"What industry do you work in? How long have you been in this field? I work in the tech industry at Oracle."
Situation
A colleague mentions a great restaurant. Ask for more details.
"What restaurant do you recommend? Where is it? Do you go there often?"
