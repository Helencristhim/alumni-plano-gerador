Your passport is ready and your trip is starting. This week we go to the airport: how to check in, choose your seat, and board your flight — all with polite requests. Review these emergency phrases before class.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Pricila arrives early at the check-in desk for her flight to Sydney. She smiles and says, "Could I have a window seat, please?" The agent checks her boarding pass and asks how many bags she wants to check. "I would like to check one bag," Pricila answers. "This is my carry-on." Her luggage is a little heavy, so the agent asks, "Would you mind moving a few things?" "Not at all," says Pricila. Then she asks, "Could you tell me which gate it is?" The agent says gate B twelve, but there is a short delay. Pricila stays calm and polite — and that makes everything easier.

1. Which question asks FOR something (for herself)?
A "Could I have a window seat, please?"
B "Would you mind moving a few things?"
C "Could you tell me which gate it is?"
2. Which question asks someone else to DO something?
A "I would like to check one bag."
B "Could you tell me which gate it is?"
C "This is my carry-on."
3. After "Would you mind", which form is correct?
A "Would you mind to move a few things?"
B "Would you mind moving a few things?"
C "Would you mind moved a few things?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Could I...? = pedir algo para mim (more polite than "Can I")
Could you...? = pedir para o outro fazer algo
Would you mind + -ing? = pedido muito educado (resposta: "Not at all")
I would like + to + verb = dizer um desejo com educação

FunctionStructureExample
Ask for meCould I + have/get"Could I have a window seat?"
Ask you to actCould you + verb"Could you check my bag?"
Very politeWould you mind + V-ing"Would you mind waiting?"
State a wishI would like + to + verb"I would like to check one bag."

Common Mistake:

"Would you mind to help me?"

"Would you mind helping me?"

Depois de "Would you mind", o verbo SEMPRE termina em -ing.

Chapter 3: The Code

Polite
Requests

Could I...? · Could you...? · Would you mind...?

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

Could I have a window seat, please?
Could you check my bag, please?
Would you mind helping me with my luggage?

What do these polite questions have in common?

UseStructureExample
Ask FOR something (for me)Could I + have/get + thing?Could I have a window seat?
Ask someone to DO somethingCould you + verb?Could you check my bag?
Very polite requestWould you mind + verb-ing?Would you mind waiting here?
State a wish politelyI would like + to + verb / nounI would like to check one bag.

Key: Could is more polite than Can. After Would you mind, use the -ing form. A polite answer to “Would you mind...?” is “Not at all.”

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"Would you mind to help me?"
"Would you mind helping me?"

After Would you mind, the verb always takes -ing.
