Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Modal Verbs: can, should, must

Modal verbs add meaning to the main verb. They express ability, advice, or obligation. They do not change form (no -s, no -ing).

ModalMeaningExample
canability / possibilityWe can schedule the meeting for Tuesday.
shouldadvice / recommendationYou should send the agenda before the meeting.
mustobligation / necessityWe must meet the deadline for the SAP upgrade.

Remember: modal + base verb (no "to"). We must send (not "must to send").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Comparatives and Superlatives

We use comparatives to compare two things and superlatives to say something is the most or least in a group.

TypeRuleExample
Short adjectiveadj + -er than / the adj + -esthigher than / the highest
Long adjectivemore + adj + than / the most + adjmore successful than / the most successful
Irregularspecial formsgood: better / the best; bad: worse / the worst

Comparative: used to compare TWO things. Superlative: used to compare THREE or more things.
