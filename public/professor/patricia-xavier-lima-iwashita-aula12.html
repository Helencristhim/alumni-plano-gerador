Chapter 3: Reading the Roadmap

A Real Product Roadmap

Service Cloud — Q3 Product Roadmap & Release Notes

This roadmap shows what our product team is going to build over the next three months, and when each part will reach you. Our goal is simple: to deliver the features you have asked for and to make the product easier to use every day. Nothing here is a promise of an exact date, but the plan is clear, and we are going to keep you updated at every step.

Let me walk you through the highlights. In June, we are going to release version three of the dashboard. This release includes two enhancements that many customers have requested: faster reports and a cleaner mobile view. We are also launching a brand-new mobile app on Tuesday the fifteenth, so your agents will be able to work from anywhere. Each feature has a clear scope, and our team is testing them right now.

Of course, we cannot build everything at once. We prioritize the requests with the biggest impact first, and the rest stay on our backlog for a future release. If a feature is not on this roadmap yet, it does not mean we will forget it; it means we are still working on the estimate. Next quarter we are going to review the backlog with you and decide together what comes next. Thank you for helping us shape the product.

Chapter 4: The Code

Plans with Future Forms

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"We are going to release version three in June."

"The new dashboard will save your team time."

"We are launching the mobile app next Tuesday."

"I am going to review the backlog with you."

FormUseExample
will + base verba prediction, a promise or an offerIt will save time.
be going to + base verba plan or intention you have already decidedWe are going to release v3.
am / is / are + verb-inga fixed arrangement with a time or dateWe are launching on Tuesday.

Use going to for decided plans, will for predictions and promises, and the present continuous for arrangements that already have a date.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

We will to launch the new feature in May.
We will launch the new feature in May.

After will, use the base verb with no "to" (will launch, not will to launch).

We going to deliver the app in June.
We are going to deliver the app in June.

"Going to" needs am, is or are before it (we are going to deliver).

Chapter 5: On the Product Call

Presenting the Roadmap

Dialogue

The Product Call

D
Thanks for the walkthrough, Patricia. So what is coming on the dashboard?
P
In June, we are going to release version three, with the faster reports you requested.
D
That is great news. And the mobile app my agents keep asking about?
P
We are launching it on Tuesday the fifteenth, so your team will work from anywhere.
D
Perfect. One more thing, the merge-contacts feature we asked for in March?
P
I will be honest: it is still on the backlog. We are going to review it with you next quarter.
Comprehension

About the Call

1. When is the team going to release version three?
In June, with the faster reports David requested.
2. When are they launching the new mobile app?
On Tuesday the fifteenth.
3. What does Patricia say about the merge-contacts feature?
It is still on the backlog; they are going to review it next quarter.
