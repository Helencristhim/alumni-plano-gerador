Metodologia

1. PPP (Presentation, Practice, Production)

Cada aula segue o ciclo PPP adaptado a 60 min: aquecimento, vocabulário com contexto, prática guiada, produção oral em pares e role-play livre.

2. Personalização Pop Culture

Conteúdo ancorado em Gossip Girl, Friends, Gilmore Girls, celebridades e a viagem para Paris 2027.

3. Speaking First

50% do tempo dedicado à produção oral. Gabriela aprende fazendo — não ouvindo explicações longas.

4. Cinestésico + Lúdico

Flashcards físicos, role-plays, simulações, objetos reais. Jogos de pattern drilling com variação de contexto.

5. Survival Card Progressivo

A cada aula, Gabriela ganha 5 frases novas para sobreviver em Paris. O cartão cresce até a viagem.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

Imperatives for directions / Imperativo para direcções

DirectionGo straight.Siga reto.
DirectionTurn left / right.Vire à esquerda / direita.
DirectionCross the street.Atravesse a rua.
Ask the wayHow do I get to the hotel?Como eu chego ao hotel?
Ask locationWhere is the station?Onde fica a estação?

Prepositions of place / Preposições de lugar

next toThe hotel is next to the café.ao lado de
across fromIt is across from the station.em frente / do outro lado
in front ofThe tower is in front of the park.na frente de
behindThe park is behind the museum.atrás de
nearIt is near.perto
far fromIt is far from here.longe de
  • Direcção = verbo primeiro, sem “you”: Go straight (nunca “You go straight” ao dar ordem).
  • Sem “to”: Go straight — NUNCA “Go to straight”.
  • Sempre in front OF / across FROM / far FROM — não esqueça a preposição.

Chapter 3

The Code

Imperatives for directions + prepositions of place

Spot the Pattern

Go straight on this street.
Turn left at the corner.
The hotel is next to the bakery.
It is across from the station.

What do the pink words have in common?

UseStructureExample
Give a directionVerb first (no subject)Go straight. Turn left. Cross the street.
Say where it isIt is + preposition + placeIt is next to the café.
Ask the wayHow do I get to + place?How do I get to the hotel?
Ask locationWhere is + place?Where is the station?

Key: Directions start with the verb (no “you”). Locations use prepositions: next to, across from, in front of, behind, near, far from.

Common Mistake

“Go to straight.”
“Go straight.” — No “to” before straight!
“It is in front the hotel.”
“It is in front of the hotel.” — in front OF!

Find the Way

Tell someone to go forward → ???Go straight.
Tell someone to turn at the corner → ???Turn left / right at the corner.
The hotel / __ / the café (beside) → ???The hotel is next to the café.
The park / __ / the museum (other side) → ???The park is across from the museum.
Ask the way to the hotel → ???How do I get to the hotel?
Ask where the station is → ???Where is the station?
