Who's Who at AVRT

AVRT, which was founded in Valinhos in 2015, is a company that designs wireless sensors for industry. The founder who started it, Izabel, is a CEO whose background is in electronics. The product that made the company's name, its flagship sensor, is used by factories that need to track equipment in real time. It began as a small venture that grew into a serious player.

The headquarters where AVRT operates sits just outside Valinhos. The engineer who leads research, and whose designs won two awards, built a team that competes on quality. AVRT serves a niche which most large firms ignore: clients who need cutting-edge sensors and the proprietary software that runs them. The technology that AVRT owns is the reason customers stay.

Every company has stakeholders who care about its future: the investors who fund it, the employees who build it, and the clients who depend on it. AVRT recently opened a subsidiary, which handles its North American sales. The incumbent that once dominated the segment, a firm whose prices were always lower, is now the company AVRT is catching. A mentor who advised Izabel early on once said that a track record which clients trust is worth more than any advertisement.

Relative clauses add information about a noun. Defining clauses (no commas) tell us WHICH one and are essential: use who for people, which or that for things, where for places, whose for possession ('the engineer who leads R&D', 'the sensor that we make'). Non-defining clauses (with commas) add EXTRA information and use who or which, never 'that' ('AVRT, which was founded in 2015, ...').

Chapter 4: The Code

Relative Clauses

who · which · that · where · whose — defining and non-defining

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"AVRT is the company that makes the sensors."

"The founder who started it is Izabel."

"AVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing."

"The city where AVRT is based is Valinhos."

Three clauses define which one we mean (no commas). One just adds extra information (commas). Which one, and why?

TypeWordsExample
Defining (no commas)who (people), which/that (things), where (places), whose (possession)The engineer who leads research.
Non-defining (commas)who or which only — never thatAVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing.
Dropping the pronounonly in defining clauses, when it is the objectThe deal (that) we signed.
Language Focus

Which Word?

AnalyseRelative clauses: which word?
AAVRT is a company that makes sensors.
BThe founder who started it is Izabel.
CIzabel is a CEO whose background is in electronics.
DAVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing.
Defining (no commas): who = people, which/that = things, where = places, whose = possession. The clause is essential and cannot be removed. Non-defining (with commas): who or which only, never 'that'; it adds extra, non-essential information. In defining clauses you can drop the pronoun when it is the object ('the deal we signed'), but never in non-defining clauses.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"AVRT is the company what makes sensors."

✓ RIGHT

"AVRT is the company that makes sensors."

✗ WRONG

"AVRT, that was founded in 2015, is growing."

✓ RIGHT

"AVRT, which was founded in 2015, is growing."

✗ WRONG

"The engineer which leads research won two awards."

✓ RIGHT

"The engineer who leads research won two awards."

Never use "what" as a relative pronoun. In non-defining clauses (with commas) use "which" or "who", never "that". Use "who" for people and "which/that" for things.

Dialogue

Introducing AVRT to a New Board Member

A
Izabel, I am briefing a new board member tomorrow. In a few sentences, how would you describe AVRT?
I
AVRT, which I founded in Valinhos in 2015, is a company that designs wireless sensors. Our flagship product, the sensor that made our name, is used by factories that track equipment in real time.
A
Good. And who are the key people they should know?
I
The engineer who leads research, and whose designs won two awards, built the team. And there is a mentor who advised me early on and still shapes how we think.
A
And the market? Where does AVRT actually compete?
I
We serve a niche which most large firms ignore: clients who need cutting-edge sensors and the proprietary software that runs them. The headquarters where we operate sits just outside Valinhos.
A
And the competition? Who is the one to watch?
I
The incumbent that once dominated the segment, a firm whose prices were always lower, is the one we are catching. We recently opened a subsidiary, which handles North American sales, and our track record is what clients trust.
