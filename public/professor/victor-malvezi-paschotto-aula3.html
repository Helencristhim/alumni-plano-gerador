Chapter 3

The Modal Mirror

Should have, must have, might have -- looking back with judgment

Grammar Discovery

Post-Mortem Email

Read this internal email from a Brinks post-mortem review. What pattern do you notice?

INTERNAL -- POST-MORTEM: Q1 GROWTH STRATEGY

"They should have aligned the team before the launch."

"The delay must have affected Q4 results."

"The client might have misunderstood the scope."

"We could have avoided the friction if we had communicated earlier."

What do these structures express? Facts or judgments about the past?

Grammar Rule

The Structure

ModalStructureMeaningCertainty
should haveshould + have + past participleIt was the right thing, but it did NOT happenCriticism / regret
must havemust + have + past participleI am almost certain this happenedHigh certainty (90%+)
might havemight + have + past participleIt is possible this happenedLow certainty (40-60%)
could havecould + have + past participleIt was possible but did not happenMissed opportunity
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"They should have to align the team."

✓ RIGHT

"They should have aligned the team."

✗ WRONG

"The delay must have affect Q4."

✓ RIGHT

"The delay must have affected Q4."

After modal + HAVE, always use the PAST PARTICIPLE. Never add "to" and never use the base form.

Hedging Language

Soften the Blow

Use hedging to disagree without being confrontational

"One could argue that..."

"One could argue that the budget cuts are premature given our growth trajectory."

"To some extent..."

"To some extent, I agree with your assessment, but the LATAM data tells a different story."

Chapter 4

The Stakeholder Call

A tense call with a skeptical Regional Director

Dialogue

The Q1 Strategy Call

V
Priya, thanks for making time for this call. I wanted to walk you through the Q1 growth strategy for LATAM and get your input.
P
Sure, Victor. But I will be honest: I have some serious pushback on this plan. The numbers you are projecting do not take into consideration the current market conditions in Asia-Pacific.
V
I take your point, however, the LATAM context is different. We should have aligned on regional benchmarks earlier, and I take responsibility for that.
P
The board must have noticed the friction between our regions. If you read between the lines of their last email, they are considering cutting both budgets.
V
That is a fair concern, and what I would suggest is that we put forward a proposal together. The client might have misunderstood the scope, so let us clarify before this escalates.
P
One could argue that a joint proposal is risky. What leverage do we actually have?
V
With respect, the data shows a different picture. LATAM's pipeline grew thirty-two percent. We can leverage that to navigate the board's concerns. A unified front is our best path to resolution.
P
Fine. I will mediate with my counterpart in London. Send me the joint proposal by Thursday. But Victor, we should have had this conversation weeks ago.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What is Priya's main objection to Victor's Q1 plan?
She believes the projected numbers do not take into consideration the current market conditions in Asia-Pacific.
2. What does Priya say the board must have noticed?
The friction between the LATAM and APAC regions.
3. What does Priya agree to do at the end of the call?
She agrees to mediate with her counterpart in London and asks for the joint proposal by Thursday.

Extra Words

"The employee filed a grievance about the decision to relocate the team."

Mandate -- an official order or requirement from an authority

"The London office issued a mandate to cut regional marketing budgets by twenty percent."

Quick Fire

Respond Diplomatically

1 / 5

1. A colleague says: "Your strategy is wrong." Disagree diplomatically.

"I take your point, however, the data shows a different picture. One could argue that the current trajectory supports our approach."

2. HQ issued a mandate to cut budgets. Express that they should have consulted the regions first.

"They should have consulted the regional directors before issuing the mandate."

3. You think the client probably received wrong information. Express this uncertainty.

"The client might have received the wrong information from our counterpart."

4. You want to formally suggest a solution to the VP. What expression do you use?

"I would like to put forward a proposal that takes into consideration both regional priorities."

5. A peer escalated an issue without talking to you first. Express your view.

"To some extent, I understand the urgency, but they should have raised the grievance with me before escalating to the VP."
