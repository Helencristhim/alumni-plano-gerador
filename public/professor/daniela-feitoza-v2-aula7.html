Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Read the rule carefully and study the examples.

Past Simple — Telling a Story About the Past

Use the past simple to talk about finished actions in the past. Regular verbs add -ed (launch → launched). Irregular verbs change form (make → made, have → had, feel → felt). For negatives and questions, use did + the base verb. Useful time markers: last year, in 2023, then, after that, finally.

FormExampleNotes
Regular (+ -ed)We launched the system. / The team worked hard.launch → launched, work → worked
IrregularI made a plan. We had three months. I felt proud.make→made, have→had, feel→felt, lead→led
NegativeWe did not have much time.did not + base verb (not "did not had")
QuestionDid you solve the problem?Did + subject + base verb
Common Mistake:
"Did you launched the system?" ✗ → "Did you launch the system?" ✓
"We didn't had time." ✗ → "We didn't have time." ✓
After "did" / "did not", always use the base verb — the past form is already in "did".

Chapter 4: Getting There

A Success
Story

Telling a colleague about a project you completed

Artifact

The Story Structure

SUCCESS STORY CARD

Four Steps

1. The Project

What did you launch or lead? "Last year, we launched a new SAP system."

2. The Challenge

What was difficult? "At first, the deadline was very tight."

3. The Solution

How did you solve it? "We made a plan and worked together every day."

4. The Result

What did you achieve? "We met the deadline and improved the workflow."

Use this shape in interviews and at networking events.
