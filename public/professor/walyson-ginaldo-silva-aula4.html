Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The present continuous: is leading, are working

Grammar Discovery

What do the orange words have in common?

"I am leading the logistics project."

"She is handling the new accounts."

"We are launching a new platform."

"Rafael is working with the finance team."

Grammar Rule

The Present Continuous

RuleExample
Form: am / is / are + verb-ingShe is leading the project.
Use it for actions happening nowWe are launching a new platform.
Also for current projects around nowThis week he is setting up the calls.
Watch the spelling of -ingmake → making, run → running

Key idea: the present continuous describes what is happening now. Use a form of "to be" plus the verb-ing, often with now, this week, or at the moment.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"She handling the new accounts."
"She is handling the new accounts."

The present continuous always needs am / is / are + verb-ing (she is handling), never the verb-ing alone.

Grammar Practice

Make It Continuous

"Bruno _____is leading the shipping plan." (lead)
"Camila _____is handling the new samples." (handle)
"We _____are launching a new platform." (launch)
"I _____am setting up the call with Geneva." (set up)
