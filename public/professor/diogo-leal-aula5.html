Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What do the highlighted words have in common?

"I have worked at Oracle for three years."
"Have you ever attended a conference in Singapore?"
"She has managed international teams."
"We have collaborated on cloud projects."

Present Perfect

SubjectStructureExample
I / You / We / Theyhave + past participleI have worked at Oracle.
He / She / Ithas + past participleShe has managed teams.
QuestionsHave/Has + subject + past participleHave you ever attended...?
Negativehave/has + not + past participleI have not visited London.

Key words: for (duration), since (starting point), ever/never (experience), already (completed), yet (not completed). Use Present Perfect when the past connects to NOW.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Watch Out!

"I have work at Oracle for three years."

Missing past participle -- "work" is not correct here

"I have worked at Oracle for three years."

have + past participle (worked) = correct

"She have managed international teams."

Wrong auxiliary -- She uses "has" not "have"

"She has managed international teams."

He/She/It = has + past participle = correct

Chapter 5 -- Practice

Spot the Error

Click on each sentence to see the correction

"I have work at Oracle for three years."
"I have worked at Oracle for three years." (past participle needed)
"She have managed international teams."
"She has managed international teams." (She = has, not have)
"We have collaborated on projects since two years."
"We have collaborated on projects for two years." (for = duration, since = starting point)
"Have you ever went to a conference abroad?"
"Have you ever been to a conference abroad?" (past participle of go = gone/been)
"I have already connected with him on LinkedIn."
CORRECT! No error. Have + already + past participle = perfect Present Perfect.
