Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Verbs in Meetings

Grammar

Modal verbs help you sound polite and professional in meetings.

Modal Use Example
Could Polite requests / suggestions "Could we move to the next item?"
Would Polite offers / asking permission "Would you mind sharing your screen?"
Shall Suggestions / offering to do something "Shall we schedule a follow-up?"
Should Advice / recommendations "We should review the budget before Friday."
Common Mistake:
Wrong: "Can we move to next item?"
Right: "Could we move to the next item?"
"Could" is more formal and polite than "can" in business meetings.

Error Correction

Common modal verb mistakes in meetings:

"Can we move to next item?"
"Could we move to the next item?"
"You should to send the minutes."
"You should send the minutes." (no "to" after modals)
"I want that we discuss the budget."
"I would like to discuss the budget."
"Shall I to take the minutes?"
"Shall I take the minutes?" (no "to" after modals)
"We must to adjourn now."
"We should adjourn now." ("should" is softer for meetings)
