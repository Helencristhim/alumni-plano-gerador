Chapter 3: The Code

The Art of the Question

Do you...? · Are you...? · Have you...?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Do you come to this fair every year?"

"Are you enjoying the event so far?"

"How long have you worked in hospitality?"

"I have attended this fair three times."

Which little helper words start each question?

Question typeStructureExample
Present SimpleDo / Does + subject + base verbDo you come here often?
Present ContinuousAm / Is / Are + subject + -ingAre you enjoying the fair?
Present PerfectHave / Has + subject + past participleHave you been here before?

Use the present perfect for experience up to now: "How long have you worked...?", "I have just arrived."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"You are enjoying the fair?"

✓ RIGHT

"Are you enjoying the fair?"

✗ WRONG

"How long you work in hospitality?"

✓ RIGHT

"How long have you worked in hospitality?"

Questions need a helper word first (Do, Are, Have) and inversion. For duration up to now, use the present perfect.

Chapter 4: At the Booth

Breaking the Ice

The conversation begins

Dialogue

A Chat at the Booth

D
Hi there! Are you enjoying the fair so far?
A
I am, thanks! It is my third time here. Is this your first ITB?
D
It is, actually. I am an exhibitor from Lisbon. What brings you here?
A
I am looking for new partners. So, how long have you worked in hospitality?
D
For about ten years. I have just launched a small hotel group. Have you seen the keynote speaker yet?
A
Not yet, but I have heard it is excellent. We have real common ground — let us grab a coffee before I wrap up for the day.
D
I would love that. Here is my badge and card. Let us keep in touch.
A
Perfect. It was really nice to meet you, Daniel.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Work the Floor

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Role-Play 1 of 3 — Guided

Break the Ice

Scenario: A delegate is standing near your booth. Start a conversation: greet them, use an opener, and ask one question about their day at the fair.

Keyword chips:

Are you enjoying...? Is this your first...? What brings you...?
Role-Play 2 of 3 — Semi-free

Keep It Going

Scenario: You are chatting with an exhibitor. Keep the conversation alive with two follow-up questions, and find one thing you have in common.

Keywords:

How long have you...? common ground Have you been to...?
Role-Play 3 of 3 — Free

The Whole Conversation

Scenario: From "Hi!" to "Let us keep in touch": break the ice with a stranger at the fair, keep the conversation going with good questions, find common ground, and wrap up warmly.

No keywords. The conversation is yours!

Bonus Challenge

Sixty Seconds, No Silence

Keep a conversation alive for one full minute. Every time you ask a good follow-up question, you score a point. The goal is simple: no awkward silence.

Great small talk is not about talking more — it is about asking better.
