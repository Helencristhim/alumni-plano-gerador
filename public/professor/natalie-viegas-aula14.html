Chapter 3 -- Reporting the Agreement

Reported Speech

How to report what people said

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What changes when we report what someone said?

"The terms are perfect." → He said that the terms were perfect.

"Procurement will sign off." → She told me that procurement would sign off.

"We can kick off in March." → He said that we could kick off in March.

"I have signed the contract." → She said that she had signed the contract.

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

Direct speechReported speech
"The terms are perfect."He said that the terms were perfect.
"Procurement will sign off."She told me that procurement would sign off.
"We can kick off in March."He said that we could kick off in March.
"I have signed the contract."She said that she had signed the contract.

Key idea: use said (no person) or told + me / her / him. The verb usually shifts back one tense: am/is/are → was/were, present simple → past simple, will → would, can → could, have done → had done. The pronouns also change to fit the new speaker. "That" is optional after said and told.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"He said me that the terms are perfect."
"He told me that the terms were perfect."

Use told with a person (told me), and said without a person (said that). Remember to shift the verb back one tense: are → were, will → would.

Grammar Practice

Report the Sentence

"We can kick off in March." → He said that we _____could kick off in March. (can → could)
"I have signed off on it." → She said that she _____had signed off on it. (have → had)
