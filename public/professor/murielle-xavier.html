Cada aula prioriza compreensão auditiva e produção em cenários reais da Minerva Foods.

Metodologia

1. Listening de Sotaques no Centro

A maior dor da Murielle é entender australianos e árabe-inglês em reuniões rápidas. Todo listening tem âncora de atenção e treino direcionado de accent recognition (australiano, árabe, americano).

2. Cenários Reais da Minerva Foods

Reuniões executivas com a operação australiana, apresentação de turnover e acidentes, negociação de remuneração variável e grade salarial. Ela pratica o que vai fazer de verdade.

3. Autonomia em Tempo Real

O objetivo é eliminar a dependência do brasileiro ao lado. Frases de gerenciamento de reunião ("Could you slow down a bit?", "Just to confirm what we agreed...") em todas as aulas.

4. Fechar as Lacunas sem Perder o Avançado

Rewrite e drills focados nas lacunas estruturais (preposições, artigos) em contexto profissional, sem nunca reduzir a densidade da produção oral que ela já tem.

5. Método e Progressão Visível

A Murielle pediu método e ROI claro. Ritmo vivo na aula das 22h, etapas mensuráveis e checkpoints periódicos para ela enxergar a evolução rumo à imersão na Austrália.

GRAMMAR

Murielle has worked in HR for over twelve years, and she has been with Minerva Foods since 2019. Her background is in HR and labor relations, and her main expertise is people development. "I report to the VP of People, and I work with senior stakeholders every day," she says. Over the years, she has taken on several international projects and has built a strong track record. These days she speaks with her Australian counterpart almost every week, because the environment is very fast-paced. "I have never worked abroad, but that is my goal," she adds. When she meets a new partner, she asks: "How long have you worked in this area?"

C Because it is a plan for the future.
2. "She has been with Minerva since 2019" -- what does "since" tell us?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Use the present perfect to connect your past experience to now. Use the present simple for current facts. / Use o present perfect para ligar sua experiência ao presente; o present simple para fatos atuais.

FormUseExample
have / has + past participleExperience up to now (no exact time)I have led global HR projects.
... for + periodHow long the situation has lastedI have worked here for twelve years.
... since + start pointWhen the situation startedI have been here since 2019.
... ever / neverLife experience (or none)I have never worked abroad.
Present simpleFacts and routines true now: I report to the VP. / My expertise is people development.
