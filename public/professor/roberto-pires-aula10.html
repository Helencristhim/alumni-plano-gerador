The Code

Reveal the Rule

StructureExample
How do I get to + [place]?How do I get to Montmartre?
Which line goes to + [place]?Which line goes to the Eiffel Tower?
Do I need to + [verb]?Do I need to transfer?
Imperative: Take / Get off / ChangeTake line 12. Get off at Abbesses.
Prepositions: at (stops/stations) | to (destinations) | on (lines)

Metro vs Bus

Metro or Bus?

Speed: The metro runs underground on dedicated tracks. The bus shares the road with cars.
Compare: "The metro is faster than the bus."
Views: From the bus, you can see Paris through the window. The metro is underground.
Compare: "The bus is better for sightseeing than the metro."
Price: Both use the same T+ ticket (2.15 euros). A carnet of 10 costs 16.90 euros.
Compare: "The fare is the same, but a carnet is cheaper per trip."
