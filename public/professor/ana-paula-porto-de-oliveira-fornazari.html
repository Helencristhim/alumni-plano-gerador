Metodologia

1. Gramática na Fala, não no Livro

A Ana Paula já estudou a teoria. Cada aula transforma essa base em produção oral: ela constrói frases enquanto conversa, usando o tempo verbal e o vocabulário da aula.

2. Pronúncia em Primeiro Plano

A dor central. Correção imediata e pontual na hora do erro, drills de sons difíceis e muita repetição com áudio-modelo — sem quebrar a fluência da conversa.

3. General English, Zero Corporativo

Todo conteúdo usa o mundo dela: rotina, bem-estar, documentaries, história, cultura, city tours e as cachorras. Nada de temas corporativos, que ela considera chatos.

4. Exposição ao Inglês Falado

Ela tem pouca exposição no dia a dia. Muito listening, diálogos e role-plays reais recriam as conversas dos jantares que ela promove em casa.

5. Ritmo Estruturado e Calmo

Mudança de atividade a cada 15-20 minutos, com objetivo claro e progressão visível. Aula às segundas e quartas, entre 11h e 15h (horário de Brasília).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your everyday life with the right tense.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Routines, habits and permanent facts (every day, usually, always).I study English every day.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Actions now or around now, temporary situations (right now, this year, at the moment).Right now, I am studying.
he / she / itIn the present simple, add -s to the verb for he, she or it.She watches a documentary.
Stative verbs
know, like, want, need, have		Verbs of feeling and state are not used in the continuous. Say I know, not "I am knowing".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

How to say how often you do things, and where the adverb goes.

RuleDetailExample
The scalealways (100%) > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never (0%).I usually walk to work.
Main verbsThe adverb goes BEFORE the main verb.I always drink coffee.
The verb "to be"With am / is / are, the adverb goes AFTER the verb.She is never late.
Time expressionsPhrases like every day, once a week or on weekends usually go at the END of the sentence.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There is / There are + Prepositions of Place

GRAMMAR

How to say what exists in a place and where it is.

FormUseExample
There is
(There's)		One thing -- singular. Say what exists (never "have").There is a bakery on the corner.
There areTwo or more things -- plural.There are two parks near my home.
Negative & questionThere is not / There are not; Is there...? / Are there...?Is there a pharmacy near here?
Prepositions of placeSay WHERE it is: next to, across from, between, behind, in front of, on the corner of, near. (Never drop the "of": in front of.)
