The Code

Present Simple -- How it works

Grammar Discovery

What do the orange words have in common?

Maísa works in finance.

She manages portfolios.

They attend meetings.

We handle accounts.

He / She / It + verb + S

I / You / We / They + verb (base form)

Grammar Discovery

Negative & Questions

David does not speak Portuguese.

I do not go every day.

Does she like her job?

Do they work together?

Negative = do/does + not + base verb

Question = Do/Does + subject + base verb?

The verb stays in BASE form after do/does -- NO extra S!

Chapter 3

The Code

Numbers, Decimals & Percentages

Grammar Discovery

What Do You Notice?

Revenue was 2.3 million.

Growth was 8 percent.

We have 1,000 clients nationwide.

The rate is 4.5 percent.

The Rule:

POINT (.) = decimals: 2.3 = "two point three"

COMMA (,) = thousands: 1,000 = "one thousand"

% = "percent" (no S, no space): 8% = "eight percent"

million (no S with number): 2.3 million

Grammar Discovery

AT, ON, or IN?

The meeting is at 2:30 PM.

The deadline is on Friday.

I started in March 2020.

She has an appointment on March 15th.

The Rule:

AT + time: at 2:30 PM, at 10 AM, at noon

ON + day/date: on Friday, on March 15th, on Monday

IN + month/year: in March, in 2024, in the morning

Common Mistakes

Watch Out

"Revenue was 2,3 millions."

"Revenue was 2.3 million."

"The meeting is in 2:30 PM."

"The meeting is at 2:30 PM."

"Growth was 8 percents."

"Growth was 8 percent."

Chapter 3

The Code

To Be + Adjectives & Adverbs of Frequency

Grammar Discovery

What Do You Notice?

Ana is reliable.

He is professional.

They are busy.

My manager is easygoing.

The Rule:

Subject + IS/ARE + adjective

He is reliable. She is reliable. (same word!)

They are busy. We are professional.

Grammar Discovery

Adverbs of Frequency

She is always prepared.

He is usually in the office by 8 AM.

David is sometimes in São Paulo.

They are never unprofessional.

The Rule:

After TO BE: She is ALWAYS busy.

Before other verbs: She ALWAYS arrives early.

Scale: always (100%) > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never (0%)

Common Mistake

Spot the Error

"She very professional is."

Verb TO BE -- Questions
QuestionShort Answer (Yes)Short Answer (No)
Am I...?Yes, you are.No, you aren't.

Explicação em Português

Negativa: Para negar com TO BE, basta adicionar NOT depois do verbo. "She is not here" = "Ela não está aqui." Contrações são muito usadas: isn't, aren't.

Perguntas: Para fazer perguntas com TO BE, invertemos o verbo e o sujeito. "She is nervous" vira "Is she nervous?" A entonação sobe no final.

Respostas curtas: Em inglês, respondemos com "Yes, I am" ou "No, he isn't" (nunca só "Yes" ou "No").

Cuidado: NÃO use "no" em vez de "not". Errado: "She is no here." Correto: "She is not here."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

To be + Adjective
She is reliable.Ela é confiável.
He is professional.Ele é profissional.
They are busy.Eles/Elas estão ocupados.
Adverbs of Frequency

always (100%) > usually (80%) > often (60%) > sometimes (40%) > rarely (15%) > never (0%)

Posição: DEPOIS do to be → "She is ALWAYS busy"

Posição: ANTES de outros verbos → "She ALWAYS arrives early"

PT: Adjetivos em inglês NÃO mudam com gênero — "He is reliable, She is reliable" (não existe "reliabla")
