Stage 1.3: Grammar Explanation Grammar

Verb TO BE — English

Affirmative forms:
I am → You are → He/She/It is → We/They are

Contractions:
I’m • You’re • He’s • She’s • It’s • We’re • They’re

Business Tip: Use “It” for companies, projects, and meetings. “The meeting is at 2 PM. It is very important.”

Verbo TO BE — Português

O verbo TO BE equivale a “ser” e “estar” em português.

Formas:
I am = Eu sou/estou • You are = Você é/está • He/She/It is = Ele/Ela é/está • We/They are = Nós somos/estamos / Eles são/estão

Contrações: No inglês falado e em e-mails informais, usamos I’m, You’re, He’s etc. Em apresentações formais, use a forma completa.

Dica Profissional: Use “It” para empresas, projetos e reuniões. “It is a global company.” (Não use “She” ou “He” para empresas.)

Stage 1.3: Grammar Explanation Grammar

Present Simple — Affirmative

I/You/We/They + base verb: I work, They manage, We deal

He/She/It + verb + s: She works, He manages, It involves

Spelling rules: -s (works), -es after s/sh/ch/x/o (watches, goes), -ies after consonant+y (studies)

Time expressions: every day, usually, always, often, sometimes, three times a week

Business Tip: Use Present Simple to describe job responsibilities. “I manage portfolios. She handles accounts.”

Presente Simples — Afirmativa

Usamos o Present Simple para rotinas, fatos e responsabilidades.

Regra principal: Na 3ª pessoa do singular (he/she/it), acrescente -s ao verbo.

Atenção: “I manage” (sem -s) vs “She manages” (com -s). Erro comum de brasileiros!

Stage 1.3: Grammar Explanation Grammar

Adjectives & Adverbs of Frequency

Adjective position:

1. Before nouns: a friendly manager, a reliable colleague

2. After verb to be: She is professional. He is easygoing.

Adverbs of frequency (100% → 0%):

always • usually • often • sometimes • rarely • never

Position:

• AFTER verb to be: She is always busy.

• BEFORE other verbs: He usually starts early.

Business Tip: Use “tends to” for diplomatic descriptions. “He tends to be direct” sounds better than “He is direct.”

Adjetivos e Advérbios de Frequência

Adjetivos: Antes do substantivo OU depois do verbo to be. NUNCA depois do substantivo (diferente do português!).

Advérbios de frequência: Depois do verbo to be, mas ANTES de outros verbos.

Dica: “Tends to” (costuma) é ótimo para descrições diplomatas no ambiente profissional.
