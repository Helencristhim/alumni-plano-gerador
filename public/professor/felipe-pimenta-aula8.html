Chapter 3: The Letter

Six Tools, One Document

You already understand it. Tonight you see how it is built.

Read for the Main Idea

The Audit Committee Letter

Letter from the Audit Committee — Annual Close

The committee has reviewed the annual close and wishes to record the following. It was the revenue cut-off that forced the restatement, and not the external audit, as had been suggested in some quarters. Had the reconciliation been run a week earlier, the error would have been caught before the close, and the restatement would not have been necessary at all.

On the provision for the tax dispute, the committee is less forgiving. It should have been disclosed in March. It was not. The chief financial officer has acknowledged this without being prompted, and told the committee, in his own words, that he wishes he had brought the matter to us directly rather than allowing it to sit inside a report. The committee notes the candor and considers the matter closed.

The external auditor cautioned that the error fell below materiality but, in her phrase, not below embarrassment. The committee agrees. In response, the chief financial officer has had the entire cut-off process re-tested by an independent firm and has had the controller sign it off personally. Not once, he assured us, will an unreconciled figure reach this committee again.

On the covenant, headroom stands at two points and is now monitored monthly rather than quarterly. The committee wishes to underscore a final point, because it has been widely misunderstood inside the business. What the company lacked this year was liquidity. It was not profit, and it was never solvency. EBITDA was never the issue. Cash, as always, was.

Audit Committee — Annual Close, item 4

Chapter 4: The Six Tools

One Problem, One Tool

Analysis · Judgment · Regret · Report · Delegate · Command

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"If we had managed working capital better, we wouldn't have needed the loan." — which tool?

"We should have protected our headroom instead of spending it." — which tool?

"She asked whether we had disclosed the provision." — which tool?

"What we lacked was liquidity, not profit." — which tool?

Six tools. Six jobs. Name the tool, then name the JOB it is doing — that second answer is the one that matters.

FormUseExample
Third conditionalAnalyze a past that did not happen. No blame, pure analysis.If we had managed it, we wouldn't have needed the loan.
Modal perfectsDeliver a verdict on ONE action. should = person; could = situation.We should have protected our headroom.
Wish / if onlyRepair a relationship. The only tool with emotion in it.I wish we had made a provision.
Reported speechCarry someone else's words accurately. The tense steps back.She asked whether we had disclosed it.
Causative have/getReport work that happened without you. The doer disappears.I had the reconciliation done.
Inversion + cleftCommand the room — and buy half a second to think.What we lacked was liquidity, not profit.

The intermediate speaker owns all six and reaches for the wrong one. The fluent speaker reaches without looking. Tonight is about the reach.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If we would have known the risk, we didn't take the loan."
"If we had known the risk, we wouldn't have taken the loan."
"I wish I would have made a provision for the dispute."
"I wish I had made a provision for the dispute."
"She asked me did we disclose the impairment."
"She asked me whether we had disclosed the impairment."

Three lessons, three traps, one root: the if-half never takes would, wish about YOURSELF never takes would, and a reported question is not a question any more.

Practice

Pick the Right Tool

Read the job. Choose the structure. Say it, then click.

""(analyze: it did not happen) "___If we had made a provision, the restatement would not have hurt.""
""(verdict on one action) "We ___should have protected our headroom instead of spending it.""
""(repair: you are talking to a person) "I ___wish I had listened to the audit committee in March.""
""(carry her words) "She ___cautioned that the error was below materiality, but not below embarrassment.""
""(the work happened, not by you) "I ___had the reconciliation done before the auditors arrived.""
""(command the room) "___What we lacked was liquidity, not profit.""

Quick Fire

Six Tools. No Hints.

1 / 6

"Did the audit cause the restatement?"

  • It was the revenue cut-off that forced it, not the audit.
  • Tool: it-cleft. Kill the rumor, name the cause.

"Could this have been avoided?"

  • If we had run the reconciliation a week earlier, we would have caught it.
  • Tool: third conditional. Analysis, not blame.

"Why were we not told about the provision?"

  • It should have been disclosed in March, and it was not. That one is mine.
  • Tool: modal perfect. A verdict, and you take it.

"Do you regret how you handled it?"

  • I wish I had brought it to you myself.
  • Tool: wish. The only one with emotion in it.

"What did the auditor actually say?"

  • She cautioned that the error was below materiality, but not below embarrassment.
  • Tool: reported speech. Her verb, her words.

"So what have you actually done about it?"

  • I had the cut-off process re-tested, and I got the controller to sign it off.
  • Tool: causative. Then close with a cleft.
