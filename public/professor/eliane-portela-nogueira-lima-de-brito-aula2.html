The Art of the Introduction

Read for the Main Idea

The 60-Second Story

The 60-Second Story: How Experts Introduce Themselves at the Top

At an executive program or a medical congress, the first question is almost always the same: 'So, what do you do?' The best professionals do not list every job they have ever had. They tell a short, clear story. A strong introduction has five parts: a hook, your role, your area of expertise, what you are working on right now, and a question back to the other person.

Notice the grammar behind a good introduction. When you talk about experience and achievements, you use the present perfect: 'I have worked in fertility care for years,' or 'I have presented at international conferences.' The exact date does not matter; the experience does. But when you mention a finished event with a finished time, you switch to the past simple: 'I did my fellowship in 2016,' or 'I trained at a center in London.'

Finally, confident experts sound human, not robotic. They use natural bridges like 'I am in the process of opening my own clinic,' or 'What I am most excited about right now is our new partnership.' Then they turn the conversation around: 'And what about you?' A great introduction is not a speech. It is the start of a conversation.

Chapter 4: Then vs Now

Experience or Event?

Present perfect and past simple

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in fertility care for eight years."

"I have presented at three international conferences."

"I did my fellowship in London in 2016."

"I studied medicine in Teresina."

TenseWhen to useExample
Present perfect (have + past participle)Experience and achievements, no exact time"I have presented at conferences."
... for / sincefor + a period, since + a start point"I have worked here for eight years / since 2016."
Past simpleA finished event with a finished time"I did my fellowship in 2016."

If there is a finished time (2016, last year, in Teresina), use the past simple. If it is general experience, use the present perfect.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I have worked there in 2015."

✓ RIGHT

"I worked there in 2015."

✗ WRONG

"I work in fertility care since 2016."

✓ RIGHT

"I have worked in fertility care since 2016."

A finished time (in 2015) needs the past simple. For experience up to now with since or for, use the present perfect — never the present simple.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Build Your Bio

From guided to free — say each version out loud

Rehearsal 1 -- Guided

Give the five-part introduction: a hook, your role, your expertise, your current focus, and a question back. Use your notes.

Rehearsal 2 -- Tighter

Say the same introduction again in under forty-five seconds. Keep only the strongest details.

Rehearsal 3 -- From memory

Now do it with no notes. Sound natural, not memorized, and remember to breathe.

Curveball

A colleague asks: 'What did you do before fertility medicine?' Answer with the past simple, then bridge back to the present with the present perfect.
