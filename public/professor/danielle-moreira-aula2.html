Case Study

Read for the Main Idea

The Culture That Never Landed

Northline Academy was a network of bilingual schools with an excellent reputation and a serious problem. In three years, its turnover rate had climbed from nine percent to twenty six percent, and the exit interviews all told the same story. The company had written a beautiful set of core values, printed them, framed them, and hung them in every corridor. It had never once carried out a cultural diagnosis to find out whether anyone actually lived by them.

The warning signs were there long before the numbers moved. Managers stopped disagreeing with their directors in meetings. Two of the best academic coordinators resigned in the same quarter and gave polite, empty reasons. The onboarding experience was excellent for the first week and abandoned by the second. The people who stayed did their work, delivered the curriculum, and quietly stopped bringing new ideas. That is disengagement, and it is much more expensive than the attrition it produces.

When the board finally asked what had gone wrong, the answer was uncomfortable. If leadership had run a pulse survey in that first year, they would have seen psychological safety collapsing. If someone had looked into the exit interviews instead of filing them, they would have found the elephant in the room by the second one. The culture had not failed because it was toxic. It had failed because nobody was willing to name what everybody could already see.

Chapter 4: The Code

The Past That Never Happened

If they had asked · they would have known · we should have looked into it

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

“If leadership had run a pulse survey, they would have seen it coming.”

“If someone had looked into the exit interviews, they would have found the problem.”

“They should have addressed it during onboarding.”

“The two coordinators might not have left if the culture had felt safe.”

None of it happened. Every sentence describes a past that was possible and was never taken.

PartFormExample
The conditionif + had + past participleIf they had run a pulse survey...
The resultwould have + past participle...they would have seen it coming.
Less certainmight have / could have + past participleThey might not have left.
Criticismshould have + past participleWe should have addressed it earlier.
OrderEither half can come first. With if first, use a comma: If they had asked, we would have told them.

In speech it contracts hard: If they'd run it, they'd have seen it. Train the contraction — that is what you will actually hear in Canada.

Register

Three Modals, Three Levels of Blame

should have

Strong. It judges.

“You should have read the exit interviews.”

could have

Softer. It shows an option that existed.

“We could have run a pulse survey that year.”

might have

Careful. It speculates without accusing.

“The team might have felt they could not disagree.”

In a room of people who built the culture you are diagnosing, should have makes you the prosecutor. Might have keeps you the diagnostician — and keeps the room.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"If they would have run a pulse survey, they would have seen it."
"If they had run a pulse survey, they would have seen it."
"We should of addressed the disengagement earlier."
"We should have addressed the disengagement earlier."

The if half never takes would — it takes had + past participle. And in writing it is always should have, never "should of", even though the two sound identical when a native speaker says them fast.

Dialogue

Reading the Numbers

N
You have seen the pulse survey. Before the academic team joins us, give me your reading of it.
D
Two numbers do not agree with each other. Belonging is at seventy eight percent, and psychological safety is at forty one. It would appear that people are proud to be here, and they still do not feel safe enough to disagree.
N
Some of the directors will tell you the survey is noise. Nine people left last year and every one of them said they were happy.
D
They said they were happy because the exit interview was the first safe conversation they had. If someone had looked into those nine interviews, we would have had this cultural diagnosis a year ago.
N
That is a hard thing to say to the people who built this place. How do you plan to say it without losing them?
D
I will not call it a toxic culture, because it is not one. I will name the elephant in the room: disagreement is expensive here. And then I will ask them what it would take to foster a culture of speaking up.
