Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"I will visit Australia next year." (when it is a pre-made plan)
"I am going to visit Australia next year."
Why? You already decided to visit Australia. It is a plan, not a spontaneous decision. Use going to for plans you already made. Use will for predictions or instant decisions.

Chapter 5 -- Practice

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to reveal the correction.

"I will travel to Australia next year. I already bought the ticket."
"I am going to travel to Australia next year." (Pre-decided plan = going to)
"I am going to think retirement is amazing."
"I think retirement will be amazing." (Prediction/opinion = will)
"I will meet Tom on Friday at 3 PM. It is confirmed."
"I am meeting Tom on Friday at 3 PM." (Fixed arrangement = present continuous)
"Wait, I am going to help you right now!" (spontaneous)
"Wait, I will help you right now!" (Spontaneous decision = will)
