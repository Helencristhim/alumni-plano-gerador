Metodologia

1. PPP (Presentation, Practice, Production)

Cada aula segue o ciclo PPP adaptado a 60 min: aquecimento, vocabulário com contexto, prática guiada, produção oral em pares e role-play livre.

2. Personalização Pop Culture

Conteúdo ancorado em Gossip Girl, Friends, Gilmore Girls, celebridades e a viagem para Paris 2027.

50% do tempo dedicado à produção oral. Gabriela aprende fazendo — não ouvindo explicações longas.

Flashcards físicos, role-plays, simulações, objetos reais. Jogos de pattern drilling com variação de contexto.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

IS para a pessoa / HAS para a característica

is + adjetivoShe is tall.Ela é alta.
is + adjetivoHe is young.Ele é jovem.
has + característicaShe has long hair.Ela tem cabelo longo.
has + característicaHe has blue eyes.Ele tem olhos azuis.
Perguntar aparênciaWhat does she look like?Como ela é?
Perguntar característicaDoes he have glasses?Ele usa óculos?
  • IS com adjetivos da pessoa: tall, short, young, beautiful. NUNCA “She has tall”.
  • HAS com características: hair, eyes, a beard, glasses. NUNCA “He is blue eyes”.
  • Com hair não use “a”: She has long hair (não “a long hair”).
  • Pergunta com does: o verbo fica simples — “What does she look like?” (não “looks”).

Chapter 3

The Code

HAS vs IS — describing people

Spot the Pattern

She is tall.
He is young.
She has long hair.
He has blue eyes.

When do we use IS and when do we use HAS?

UseStructureExample
Describe the whole personis + adjectiveShe is tall. He is young.
Describe a featurehas + (adjective +) featureShe has long hair. He has a beard.
Ask about looksWhat does + person + look like?What does she look like?
Ask about a featureDoes + person + have...?Does he have glasses?

Key: IS goes with adjectives for the person (tall, short, young). HAS goes with features (hair, eyes, a beard, glasses).

Common Mistake

“She has tall.”
“She is tall.” — tall is an adjective, use IS!
“He is blue eyes.”
“He has blue eyes.” — eyes are a feature, use HAS!
“She has a long hair.”
“She has long hair.” — no “a” before hair!

Has or Is?

Say a girl is not short → ???She is tall.
Say a girl’s hair is long → ???She has long hair.
Say a boy’s eyes are blue → ???He has blue eyes.
Say a man has facial hair → ???He has a beard.
Ask about a person’s looks → ???What does she look like?
Ask if a boy wears glasses → ???Does he have glasses?
