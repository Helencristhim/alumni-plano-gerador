Chapter 3 -- How Much?

Language Discovery

How much...? + this / that / these / those

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What do the orange words have in common? One thing or many? Near or far?

"How much is this scarf?" (one, near)

"How much is that bag?" (one, far)

"How much are these postcards?" (many, near)

"How much are those shoes?" (many, far)

The Rule

This / That / These / Those

WordOne or Many?Near or Far?
thisone (How much is this?)near
thatone (How much is that?)far
thesemany (How much are these?)near
thosemany (How much are those?)far

Key idea: for one thing use this/that + is. For many things use these/those + are. Near you = this/these; far away = that/those.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"How much is these shoes?"
"How much are these shoes?"

One thing = is (How much is this?). Many things = are (How much are these?).

Practice

Complete the Question

"How much _____is this scarf?" (one, near)
"How much _____are these postcards?" (many, near)
"How much _____is that bag?" (one, far)
"How much _____are those shoes?" (many, far)
Key Phrase

The First Question

"How much is this?"

Point and ask. For many things: "How much are these?" Then ask about a discount: "Is there a discount?"

Make It Yours

Ask About a Price

Imagine you are shopping for a gift.

Ask two price questions and say how you will pay. For example: "How much is this scarf?" and "I will pay with cash."

How much is this? How much are these? pay with cash
