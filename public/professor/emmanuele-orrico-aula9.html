Chapter 3: The Flow

How a Conversation Moves

Open · Share · Ask · Bridge · Close

Grammar Discovery

Three Times, One Conversation

"I work in immunology." (your job)

"Right now, we are launching a new product." (now)

"In August, I am going to present." (the plan)

Same person, same minute — three different times. You already know all three.

TimeUse it forExample
Present simple
(I work / I am)		Your job and facts that are always true.I work in immunology. I am the national demand manager.
Present continuous
(we are launching)		What is happening now, this period. Say "right now" or "at the moment".Right now, we are launching a new product.
Going to
(I am going to present)		A plan for the future. You already decided it.In August, I am going to present the results.
Fixed opener: Have you been to this conference before?One ready block to break the ice. Do not build it — use it whole."Have you been to this conference before?"

Nothing new today. You already own these three blocks — the skill now is to switch between them without stopping.

The Flow

Five Steps, One Conversation

OPEN"Hi, I'm Emmanuele. Have you been to this conference before?"
SHARE"I work at Sanofi Brazil. I'm the national demand manager."
ASK"What brings you here?"
BRIDGE"Right now, we are launching a new product. What about you?"
CLOSE"It was great to catch up. Let's stay in touch."

You never improvise the whole thing. You just take the next step.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"What you do at Sanofi?"
"What do you do at Sanofi?"
"I working in immunology."
"I work in immunology."
"I go to present in August."
"I am going to present in August."

For your job, use present simple: "I work". For a question, add do: "What do you do?". And for a plan, use the whole block: "I am going to present".
