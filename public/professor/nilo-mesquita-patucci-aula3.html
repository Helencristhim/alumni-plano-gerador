Chapter 3: The Code

What Do You Notice?

What is the difference between for, since, ever, and never?

WordUseExample
forDuration (how long)for fifteen years, for six months
sincePoint in time (when it started)since 2010, since I graduated
everQuestions (at any time in your life)Have you ever worked abroad?
neverNegative (at no time)I have never missed a deadline.
Chapter 3: The Code

Common Mistake

“I have worked since fifteen years.”
“I have worked for fifteen years.”
“Did you ever work in sports law?”
“Have you ever worked in sports law?”
Chapter 3: The Code

Grammar Practice

Chapter 3: The Code

Quick Check

1. Which is correct: “for 2010” or “since 2010”?
Since 2010. SINCE + point in time.
2. “I have never missed a deadline.” Does this mean zero times?
Yes, never = at no time in my career.
3. “Have you ever presented at a conference?” What does ever mean here?
At any time in your life. It asks about experience.
