The Day I Stopped Translating

The Day I Stopped Translating

A year ago, every English conversation felt like hard work. I translated each sentence in my head before I spoke, so I was always one step behind. I would stumble on a word, freeze, and go tongue-tied. Small talk felt like a test I could fail, and I often stayed quiet just to be safe.

Then something changed. I stopped waiting for the perfect sentence. When I forgot a word, I did not panic -- I described it in other words and kept going. I learned to speak at a calm, steady pace and to use short connectors to sustain the conversation, even when I was not sure. Little by little, speaking became almost effortless.

Last week I talked with a colleague for ten minutes without translating once. I made mistakes, of course, but I got my point across, and I could keep up with everything she said. That was my real breakthrough: not speaking perfectly, but speaking freely. Fluency is not about zero mistakes. It is the confidence to express your ideas, keep the conversation going, and actually enjoy it.

Chapter 4: The Fluency Toolkit

Four Moves That Keep You Talking

How to speak spontaneously, without a script

Discover the Toolkit

Listen and Notice

MovePhraseExample
ParaphraseWhat I mean is... / It is the thing you use to..."It is the thing you use to cook."
Buy a secondGive me a second. / Let me think."That is a good question -- give me a second."
Pass the turnWhat about you? / Tell me more."It was great. What about you?"
ElaborateFor example... / because..."I love it, for example, when it saves time."

None of these need perfect grammar. They just keep you talking -- and that is what fluency really is.

Language Focus

What Is Each Phrase Doing?

AnalyseWhat is each phrase doing?
AWhen I forget a word, I say what I mean is... -- paraphrase.
BTo pass the turn back, I ask what about you? -- keep it going.
CTo buy a second, I say give me a moment -- stay calm.
DTo add detail, I say for example... -- elaborate.
Notice the four moves of a fluent speaker: paraphrase a missing word (what I mean is...), buy a little time (give me a second), pass the turn back (what about you?), and add detail (for example...). None of them need perfect grammar -- they just keep you talking and keep the conversation flowing.
Common Mistake

Freeze vs Keep Going

✗ FREEZE

[Silence] / switch to Portuguese

✓ KEEP GOING

"Sorry, I forgot the word -- what I mean is, the small kitchen tool."

✗ STOP

"I do not know. End."

✓ PASS THE TURN

"Hmm, I am not sure -- what about you?"

A forgotten word is not the end of a conversation -- it is just a small gap. Describe it, buy a second, or pass the turn, and you never have to stop.

Quick Fire

What Would You Say?

1 / 5

You forget the English word for something. Keep going without stopping.

  • Sorry, I forgot the word -- it is the thing you use to cook.
  • What I mean is, the small kitchen tool.

A friend asks about your weekend. Give a short answer, then pass the turn back.

  • It was great -- I relaxed a lot. What about you?
  • Pretty busy! And how was yours?

Someone asks a hard question and you need a second. Stay fluent, do not freeze.

  • That is a good question -- give me a second.
  • Hmm, let me think... I would say...

You want to add more detail to your idea. Elaborate.

  • For example, last week I tried it and it worked.
  • What I mean is, it helps me because it saves time.

You made a small mistake in the middle of a sentence. Keep going anyway.

  • ...sorry, I mean yesterday -- anyway, as I was saying...
  • Let me put that another way.
