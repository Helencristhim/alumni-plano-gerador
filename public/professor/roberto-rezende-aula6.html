Pronunciation

Say It Right

1. How would you follow up on a proposal?
"I am calling to follow up on the proposal we sent last week."
2. How would you ask about someone's schedule?
"Please check your availability for next Tuesday."
3. How would you promise to send a document?
"I will send the revised estimate as an attachment by Friday."
4. How would you say you plan to change a meeting?
"I am going to reschedule the call to Thursday."
5. How would you start a formal email about a past discussion?
"I am writing regarding our last conversation."

Common Mistakes

Watch Out

"I will to send you the attachment."
"I will send you the attachment." (will + base form, no TO)
"I going to reschedule the meeting."
"I am going to reschedule the meeting." (AM going to)

Real Context

Follow-Up Email

Follow-Up Email

From: Roberto Rezende | To: Karen Walsh | Subject: Follow-Up — Diesel Engine Proposal

Dear Karen,

I am writing regarding the proposal we sent last week. I hope you received the attachment with the pricing details.

I will send you the updated technical specifications by Wednesday. Our engineering team is going to prepare a revised estimate based on your requirements.

Could you please confirm your availability for a call next Tuesday? I will send the meeting agenda once we have a time. If Tuesday does not work, I am going to reschedule to Thursday.

I would appreciate your feedback on the proposal. I will forward any questions to the relevant team.

Please do not hesitate to send any inquiries.

Best regards,
Roberto Rezende
Sales Executive — Brazil Operations

Comprehension

Check Your Understanding

1. What will Roberto send by Wednesday?
He will send the updated technical specifications.
2. What is the engineering team going to prepare?
They are going to prepare a revised estimate based on Karen's requirements.
3. What will happen if Tuesday does not work?
Roberto is going to reschedule the call to Thursday.
