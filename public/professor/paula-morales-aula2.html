Chapter 3: The Code

One Verb. That's All.

I am · I am not · Are you?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

O que muda de uma frase para a outra?

I am a commercial professional.

I am not a broker.

Are you a manager?

É um verbo só, em três roupas. Você já usa a primeira desde a aula 1.

FormaInglêsEm português
AfirmativoI am a professional. / You are a manager.Eu sou uma profissional. / Você é gerente.
NegativoI am not a broker. (= I'm not)Eu não sou corretora.
PerguntaAre you a broker?Você é corretora?
Resposta curtaYes, I am. / No, I'm not.Sim, sou. / Não, não sou.
Duas peças novas, só: o not vem depois do am. E para perguntar, o are pula para a frente — em português basta a entonação, em inglês o verbo se mexe.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am broker."
"I am a broker."
"You are a manager?"
"Are you a manager?"

Em português “eu sou corretora” não tem artigo — em inglês o a é obrigatório antes da profissão. E a pergunta não se faz levantando a voz: o verbo pula para a frente. Todo brasileiro faz esses dois. Você acabou de parar de fazer.

Comprehension

About David

1. Is David a manager?
No, he is not. He says: "No, I'm not. I am an analyst."
2. What is his job?
He is an analyst.
3. Where is his desk?
Next to Paula's desk. He says: "My desk is next to yours." They are neighbors.
4. What does David say at the end?
"You are in the right place, Paula." Their clients are the same clients — so she is in the right place.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Now It's You

Do apoio total à fala livre — em três passos.

Quick Fire

One Question at a Time

1 / 6

The interviewer asks: "Are you a broker?" You were a broker for twenty years — but not anymore. Answer.

  • No, I'm not. Not anymore.
  • I am on the company side now.

"What do you do?" Say what you ARE, not only where you work.

  • I am a commercial professional.
  • I work in commercial insurance.

"Are you a manager?" You are not a manager yet. Answer honestly, with a full sentence.

  • No, I'm not — not yet.
  • I am a commercial professional.

A new colleague asks: "Who are your clients?" Say what kind of companies.

  • My clients are big companies.
  • Banks, factories, hospitals — big risks.

The person thinks you are a broker. Correct it: say what you are NOT, then what you ARE.

  • I am not a broker. I am a commercial professional.
  • I am on the company side now.

You want to know the other person's job. Ask a question with the verb "to be".

  • Are you a broker?
  • Are you a manager? / Are you new here?
