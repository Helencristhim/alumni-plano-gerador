Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Would like (a request) vs could I have (a question) vs will have (a decision)

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we order food politely in English?

"I would like the grilled shrimp, please." (a request)

"Could I have a glass of water?" (a polite question)

"Can I have the bill, please?" (a polite question)

"I will have the roasted lamb." (a decision)

Grammar Rule

Ordering Politely

UseFormExample
A polite requestI would like + nounI would like an appetizer.
A polite request (action)I would like + to + verbI would like to order now.
A polite questionCould / Can I have + noun?Could I have the menu?
A decision when orderingI will have + nounI will have the lamb.
Asking for adviceWhat do you recommend?What do you recommend tonight?

Key idea: would like is more polite than want. After could or can I have, use a noun (could I have the menu, never could I have to the menu). Finish your requests with please.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Give me the bill."
"Could I have the bill, please?"

In a restaurant, give me sounds like an order. Use could I have or would like with please to sound polite and friendly.
