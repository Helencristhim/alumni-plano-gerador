Chapter 3: The Code

The Passive Voice

it was found · it is required

Grammar Discovery

Read and Notice

"The controls were examined by the audit team."

"A limit was breached in March."

"The report was submitted to the regulator."

"A remediation plan is required by the committee."

What do the orange words have in common?

PartFormExample
The passivebe (is/are/was/were) + past participleThe report was submitted.
Present vs pastis/are + participle (now) · was/were + participle (past)A plan is required. A limit was breached.
The doerAdd by + person only if it matters; often the doer is dropped. The action is the point.

Passive = be + past participle. The subject receives the action; the doer can disappear.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"The report submitted to the regulator."
"The report was submitted to the regulator."
"The audit was complete last week."
"The audit was completed last week."
"The findings was disclosed to the board."
"The findings were disclosed to the board."

The passive is be + past participle. Never drop be (was/were), never use the base verb (submitted, not "submit"), and match be to the subject (findings were, not "was").

Practice

Make It Passive

Say it first, then click to check

"The controls ___were examined by the audit team last week." (examine)
"A remediation plan ___is required before the next quarter." (require, now)

"The plan must ___be approved by the committee." (approve, after a modal)

Read for the Findings

Internal Audit Report — Risk Desk Controls

This report was prepared by the internal audit team and will be submitted to the regulator next week. During the review, the desk's controls were examined and three areas were tested. In the base case, the controls are considered adequate, but two deficiencies were identified.

First, a limit was breached in March, and the breach was not disclosed to the committee on the same day. Second, the monthly statement was submitted late twice. These deficiencies are rated as medium risk, and remediation is required before the next quarter.

A remediation plan must be prepared by the desk and approved by the committee. The plan will be reviewed by the regulator, and the minutes of the meeting will be enclosed. If the actions are completed on time, no penalty will be applied. The main message is clear: when a limit is breached, it must be disclosed at once.
