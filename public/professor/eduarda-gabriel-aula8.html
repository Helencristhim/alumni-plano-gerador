Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

At the investment committee, Eduarda defends her recommendation. "In my view, we should move now," she begins. A skeptical partner asks her to back up the claim. "The data suggests the market will recover next quarter," she explains. She argues the consequence: "If we wait, we will lose the deal." The partner sees the upside but worries about the downside. Eduarda answers with another conditional: "If the market falls, our capital will stay protected." When a colleague proposes a lower estimate, she pushes back politely. Finally she gives the bottom line: "If we act now, we will lead the deal."

1. What does the partner ask Eduarda to do with her claim?
A To withdraw it.
B To back it up with evidence.
C To repeat it louder.
2. Which sentence is a first conditional?
A "In my view, we should move now."
B "If we wait, we will lose the deal."
C "The data suggests the market will recover."
3. What is Eduarda's bottom line?
A If they act now, they will lead the deal.
B They should cancel the deal.
C The market will never recover.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

First conditional = a real, possible future result. Structure: If + present simple, ... will + base verb (situação real e possível: a parte do IF fica no presente; o WILL só na consequência).

PartFormExample
If-clausepresent simple"If we wait,"
Resultwill + base verb"we will lose the deal."
Opinion frameIn my view, ..."In my view, we should act now."

Common Mistake:

"If we will wait, we will lose the deal."

"If we wait, we will lose the deal."

Nunca use "will" na parte do IF. O WILL fica só na consequência.

Chapter 3 — The Code

First Conditional

Look at the purple words. What is the relationship between the two parts?

If we wait, we will lose the deal.
If the market improves, the company will recover.
If we act now, we will lead the deal.
If the deal fails, our capital will stay protected.
PartTenseExample
If-clause (condition)present simple"If we wait,"
Main clause (result)will + base verb"we will lose the deal."
Grammar Rule

First Conditional & Opinion Frames

StructureUseExample
If + present, ... will + basereal future result"If we wait, we will lose the deal."
In my view, ...state an opinion"In my view, we should act now."
The data suggests (that) ...present evidence"The data suggests the market will recover."
The bottom line is ...conclude"The bottom line is we should act."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"If we will wait, we will lose the deal."
"If we wait, we will lose the deal."

In the first conditional, the if-clause uses present simple. Will goes only in the result.
