Chapter 3 — The Cut

Participle Phrases

Look at the purple words. How do we cut a relative clause and keep the meaning?

the deal that is closing on Friday → the deal closing on Friday
the terms that were agreed last week → the terms agreed last week
the partner who is leading the deal → the partner leading the deal
the figures that are shown in the deck → the figures shown in the deck
Cut thisKeep this (participle)Example
that is / that are + -ingjust the -ing verb"the deal closing on Friday"
that was / that were + past participlejust the past participle"the terms agreed last week"
who is / who are + -ingjust the -ing verb"the partner leading the deal"
that are + past participlejust the past participle"the figures shown in the deck"
Grammar Rule

Long vs Tight

Long (relative clause)Tight (participle phrase)
"the deal that is closing on Friday""the deal closing on Friday"
"the terms that were agreed last week""the terms agreed last week"
"the partner who is leading the deal""the partner leading the deal"
"the report that was sent yesterday""the report sent yesterday"
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"The memo includes the terms that were agreed last week and the deal that is closing on Friday."
"The memo includes the terms agreed last week and the deal closing on Friday."

In a memo, every extra "that is" or "that was" is dead weight — cut it and keep only the participle.

Practice

Cut the Clause

"the deal ___closing on Friday" (from: the deal that is closing)
"the terms ___agreed last week" (from: the terms that were agreed)
"the partner ___leading the deal" (from: the partner who is leading)
"the report ___sent yesterday" (from: the report that was sent)
"the figures ___shown in the deck" (from: the figures that are shown)
"the team ___reviewing the memo" (from: the team that is reviewing)

Dialogue

The Memo Review

J
Eduarda, I read your deal memo. The content is right, but it reads like an essay.
E
Understood. I will lead with the headline — the recommendation in the first line.
J
Good. And the key terms? They are buried in long sentences.
E
I will move them into bullet points. And I will write "the terms agreed last week", not "the terms that were agreed".
J
Exactly. Cut every clause you can. What about the reason for the recommendation?
E
I will state the rationale in one line, and define the scope so no one assumes more.
J
Perfect. One page, no more. Send it to me before you share it.
E
Will do. I will send you the draft first, then circulate the final version to the partners.

Artifact

The Tight Memo

To: Deal Team & Partners
From: Eduarda Gabriel
Re: Vertex Restructuring — Recommendation

Headline: We recommend proceeding with the Vertex deal closing on Friday.

Key terms:

• Purchase price agreed last week: $42M.
• Financing arranged through two lenders.
• Closing date confirmed for Friday.

Rationale: The returns shown in the deck exceed our threshold.

Scope: This memo covers the acquisition only, not the post-closing plan.

1. What is in the headline?
The recommendation: proceed with the deal closing on Friday.
2. How does the memo show the key terms?
As bullet points, not paragraphs.
3. Find a participle phrase in the memo.
"the price agreed last week" or "the deal closing on Friday".
