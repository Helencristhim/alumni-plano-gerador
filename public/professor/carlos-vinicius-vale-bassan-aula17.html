Chapter 3 -- To Do or Doing

The Verb Decides the Form

Gerund vs infinitive: "agreed to proceed" / "look forward to working"

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Look at the verb form right after each orange word.

"The board agreed to proceed with the acquisition."

"We look forward to working with your team."

"We plan to formalize the terms next week."

"I suggest signing on Friday and recommend finalizing the budget first."

The Rule

Gerund vs Infinitive

PatternVerbsExample
to + base verbagree, decide, plan, hope, promise"We agreed to proceed."
verb + ingsuggest, recommend, avoid"We suggest signing on Friday."
look forward TO + ing"to" is a preposition here"We look forward to working together."

Tip: the first verb decides the form of the second. There is no logic -- you learn the verbs in groups. The big trap is look forward to: the "to" is a preposition, so it takes the gerund: "look forward to working", never "to work".

Common Mistake

"Look Forward To" Takes -ing

"We look forward to work together, and we suggest to sign on Friday."
"We look forward to working together, and we suggest signing on Friday."

After look forward to and suggest, use the -ing form: "look forward to working", "suggest signing". But after agree, plan, and decide, use to + base verb: "agreed to proceed".

Practice

Comprehension

About the Brief

1. What three things does the coach say a strong close must do?

Recap the outcome in one clear line, name the outstanding items honestly (with owners), and agree the next steps and formalize them.

2. Which verbs take "to + verb", and which take the "-ing" form?

Agree, decide, and plan take "to": "we agreed to proceed". Look forward to, suggest, and recommend take "-ing": "look forward to working", "suggest signing".

3. What does the coach say a good close should sound like?

Short and clear: "To recap, the board agreed to proceed, two items are outstanding, and we plan to sign next Friday. I look forward to working with you." It turns a yes into an action.
