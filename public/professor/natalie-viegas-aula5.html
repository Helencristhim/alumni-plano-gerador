Stage 1.1: Key Vocabulary

Vocabulary
Agenda.A list of topics to discuss in a meeting
"Let me share the agenda before we begin."
Recap.To summarize the main points of a discussion
"Let me recap the main points before we close."
Action item.A specific task assigned to someone after a meeting
"We have three action items from today's meeting."
Consensus.General agreement among a group of people
"We should reach a consensus before making a decision."
Facilitate.To guide or manage a discussion or process
"She facilitates our weekly strategy sessions."
Objective.The goal or purpose of a meeting or project
"The objective of this meeting is to review our Q3 targets."
Minutes.The official written record of what was discussed in a meeting
"Could you send the minutes to the team after the meeting?"
Chair.To lead or preside over a meeting
"I will chair the quarterly review next Monday."
Adjourn.To formally end a meeting or session
"If there are no other questions, we should adjourn."
Clarify.To make something easier to understand by explaining it more clearly
"Could you clarify that point?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Modal Verbs: should, could, would

Modal verbs are used before the BASE FORM of a verb (no -s, no -ing, no "to"). The only exception is would like to.
Os modais vem antes do verbo na forma BASE (sem -s, sem -ing, sem "to"). A unica excecao e would like to.

ModalUse / UsoStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
shouldAdvice, recommendation
Conselho, recomendacao		should + base verb
should + verbo base		We should review the agenda.
Deveriamos revisar a pauta.
couldPossibility, polite request
Possibilidade, pedido educado		could + base verb
could + verbo base		Could you send the minutes?
Voce poderia enviar a ata?
wouldPolite preference, hypothetical
Preferencia educada, hipotese		would + base verb
would like to + verb
would + verbo base		I would like to recap the items.
Eu gostaria de recapitular os itens.

Common mistakes / Erros comuns: "She should reviews" (WRONG -- no -s after modal), "Could you sharing" (WRONG -- base form, not -ing), "I should to review" (WRONG -- no "to" after should/could).

Modal Focus

SHOULD: Suggest reviewing the proposal before Friday.
"We should review the proposal before Friday."
COULD: Ask politely for someone to take notes.
"Could you take the minutes today?"
