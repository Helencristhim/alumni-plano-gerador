Brainstorming -- Reading for Gist

Read: Sofia's Email

Sofia works in the sales team at a small company. On Monday morning, she opened her inbox and found a message from a new client, Marco. His email had a clear request: he wanted a price list, and he asked Sofia to confirm a meeting for Friday.

Sofia wanted to reply well, so she read Marco's email twice. First, she wrote a short, polite answer and thanked him for his message. Then she added the price list as an attachment and wrote: “Please find the price list attached.”

Before she sent the email, Sofia asked her manager, Bruno, to check it. Bruno read it and suggested one small change. “Add a clear deadline,” he said, “so the client knows when you need an answer. And forward a copy to me, please.” Sofia added a line: “I would appreciate a reply by Wednesday.” She also thanked Marco in advance for his time.

Two hours later, Marco replied and confirmed the Friday meeting. Sofia decided she would follow up on Thursday if she heard nothing more. Bruno smiled. “A good email is short, polite, and clear,” he said. “It always says what you need — and when.”

Put the events in order (say the sequence out loud)

A. Bruno checks the email and suggests adding a clear deadline.
B. Sofia opens her inbox and finds a message from Marco.
C. Marco replies and confirms the Friday meeting.
D. Sofia writes a polite answer and adds the price list as an attachment.
E. Marco asks for a price list and to confirm Friday's meeting.
F. Sofia adds a deadline and sends the email.
Answer key — click to reveal
Correct order: B → E → D → A → F → C.

Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Polite Requests in Writing

Make a soft, formal request“I would appreciate it if you could send the report.”
Ask directly but politely“Could you please send the file today?”
Introduce an attachment“Please find the report attached.”
Close the email“I look forward to your reply.”
1. Which line sounds the most formal and soft: “Could you please ...?” or “I would appreciate it if you could ...”?
I would appreciate it if you could ... is the softest and most formal. “Could you please ...?” is direct but still polite.
2. What form of the verb comes after “Could you please”: “Could you please to send” or “Could you please send”?
Could you please send — use the base verb, with no “to”.
3. You are sending a file with your email. Which line do you write?
“Please find the report attached.” (or “Please find the file attached.”)
4. Why do we use “would”, “could” and “please” in work emails?
To sound polite and professional — in writing, the reader cannot hear your tone, so the words must carry it.
