Chapter 3

Talking Forward

will · be going to · present continuous

Grammar Discovery

Three Ways to Talk Forward

All three are about the future — why does each one feel different?

I'll email the invoice right now. — a promise decided in the moment

I'm going to call her a week after delivery. — a plan I already have

We're shipping the first batch on Monday. — a fixed arrangement with a time

Same future, three shades: a promise, a plan, an arrangement.

Grammar Rule

Future Forms for the Follow-up

FormUse it forExample
will + basea promise, offer, or prediction decided NOW"I'll send the invoice today."
be going to + basea plan or intention you ALREADY have"We're going to onboard their team."
present continuous (am/is/are + -ing)a fixed arrangement, usually with a time"We're shipping on Monday at 9."
Quick testDecided on the spot → will. Already planned → going to. Booked/scheduled → -ing."I'll check." vs "I'm going to check." vs "I'm checking at 3."
Watch Out

Common Mistake

We will ship the batch on Monday at nine. (it's already booked)
We're shipping the batch on Monday at nine.

For a fixed, scheduled arrangement, use the present continuous (am/is/are + -ing). Save will for a promise or offer decided in the moment, and don't drop the be in going to.

Practice

Pick the Future Form

The client asks for the invoice now. You reply: "_____ send it right away." → I'll (decided on the spot)
You planned this last week: "We _____ onboard their team after delivery." → are going to (existing plan)
The shipping slot is booked: "We _____ the first batch on Monday." → are shipping (fixed arrangement)
Your prediction: "If we deliver on time, the client _____ stay with us." → will (prediction)

Spot the Error

Fix the Future

"We will ship the batch on Monday at nine." (already booked)
"We're shipping the batch on Monday at nine." (fixed arrangement → present continuous)
"We going to onboard your team next week."
"We're going to onboard your team next week." (don't drop the "be")
"I send you the invoice tomorrow." (a promise now)
"I'll send you the invoice tomorrow." (use "will" for a promise)
"We shipping the first order on Friday."
"We're shipping the first order on Friday." (add the "be": are)
