Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What do the highlighted words have in common?

I work at Oracle. I oversee IT projects.
I am expanding our operations to the American market.
My expertise is in project management.
I am launching a new application right now.

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

TenseUseExample
Present SimplePermanent facts, routines, general truthsI work at Oracle. I live in São Paulo.
Present ContinuousCurrent/temporary actions happening now or around nowI am expanding our scope. I am learning English.

Key signal words: Present Simple = always, every day, usually. Present Continuous = right now, currently, at the moment, this week.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Watch Out!

"I am working at Oracle." (permanent job)

Using continuous for a permanent fact = incorrect

"I work at Oracle." (permanent job)

Present Simple for permanent facts = correct

"I expand our operations right now."

Using simple for a current action = incorrect

"I am expanding our operations right now."

Present Continuous for current action = correct

Spot the Error

"I am based in São Paulo." (past participle "based", not "base")
"My background is on IT."
"My background is in IT." (background is IN, not on)
