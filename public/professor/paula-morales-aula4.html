Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

O que as palavras em destaque têm em comum?

I cover large risks.

Sofia manages the team.

She visits the brokers every month.

David handles the numbers.

Uma letra separa você de ela.

FormaInglêsEm português
Eu / vocêI cover. / You cover.Eu cubro. / Você cobre.
Ela / ele / issoShe covers. / He covers. / It covers.Ela cobre. / Ele cobre. / Isso cobre.
NegativoShe doesn't cover small risks. (= does not)Ela não cobre riscos pequenos.
PerguntaDoes she cover large risks?Ela cobre grandes riscos?
Resposta curtaYes, she does. / No, she doesn't.Sim, cobre. / Não, não cobre.
O S aparece em UM lugar só: he / she / it, no afirmativo. E tem uma troca justa: quando entra o does (pergunta ou negativo), o S sai do verbo — porque o does já carrega o S dentro dele. Nunca os dois na mesma frase.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"She manage the team."
"She manages the team."
"Does she manages the team?"
"Does she manage the team?"

O primeiro erro é de quem nunca aprendeu a regra. O segundo é de quem acabou de aprender — o S fica empolgado e aparece duas vezes. Só um S por frase: ou no verbo, ou dentro do does. Nunca nos dois.

Who Does What

Who Does What: The Commercial Team, São Paulo

Sofia Reyes manages the commercial team. She covers the south region. Her team works with brokers and big clients, every day. She visits the brokers every month — she doesn't stay in the office all week.

Paula Morales is the new commercial professional. She covers large risks: banks, factories, hospitals. David is the analyst. He handles the numbers, and he writes the report every Monday. Does he visit the brokers? No, he doesn't. That is not his job.

This is Sofia, in her own words: "My team is small, and it works. I manage six people. I visit our brokers every month. Paula visits them with me now."

"Does she know insurance?" people ask about Paula. "Twenty years of it," Sofia answers. "She knows the client, she knows the risk, and she knows the market. My job is the team. Her job is the client. His job is the report. Everybody covers one thing, and together we cover everything."

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Now It's You

Do apoio total à fala livre — em três passos.

Quick Fire

One Question at a Time

1 / 6

A new colleague asks: "What do you do here, exactly?" Say what you are responsible for — and what you cover.

  • I am responsible for large risks.
  • I cover big companies — banks, factories, hospitals.

"Who is your manager?" Say her name — and say what she does. Two sentences.

  • My manager is Sofia.
  • She manages the commercial team.

Someone from People & Culture asks: "Does Sofia visit the brokers?" She does — every month. Answer with the short answer first, then the full sentence.

  • Yes, she does.
  • She visits the brokers every month.

"Does Paula write the report?" No — David does. Correct it: say no, then say who.

  • No, she doesn't.
  • David writes the report. He handles the numbers.

Describe your team in two sentences: who manages it, and what it handles.

  • Sofia manages my team.
  • My team handles big companies — large risks.

You want to know what the other person does. Ask a question with "do" or "does".

  • What do you do here?
  • Does your team handle large risks?
