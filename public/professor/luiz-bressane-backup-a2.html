Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple + Adverbs of Frequency = para falar sobre rotinas e hábitos regulares.
Os advérbios de frequência vão ANTES do verbo principal, mas DEPOIS do verbo "to be".

AdverbFrequencyExample
Always100%"I always wake up at five thirty."
Usually80%"I usually take the flight on Fridays."
Often60%"I often work late at the courthouse."
Sometimes40%"I sometimes stay overnight in SP."
Rarely10%"I rarely have free time."
Never0%"I never work on Sundays."

Prepositions of Time:

  • on + days: "on Mondays", "on Fridays"
  • in + parts of the day: "in the morning", "in the afternoon"
  • at + specific times: "at night", "at five thirty"

Common Mistake:

"I always am tired in the morning."

"I am always tired in the morning."

Lembre-se: com o verbo "to be", o advérbio de frequência vai DEPOIS. Com outros verbos, vai ANTES.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Describing Your Profession = três maneiras de dizer o que você faz profissionalmente:

StructureUseExample
I am a + profissãoIdentidade profissional"I am a public defender."
I work as a + profissãoFunção que exerce"I work as a public defender."
I work for + organizaçãoOnde/para quem trabalha"I work for the Public Defender's Office."

Key Expressions for Describing Your Work:

  • I am responsible for + -ing: "I am responsible for defending people."
  • I deal with + noun: "I deal with homicide cases."
  • My role involves + -ing: "My role involves preparing cases."

Common Mistake:

Lembre-se: use "a/an" depois de "I am" para profissões. Use "as" (não "like") com "work".

Present Continuous vs Present Simple

Now (temporary): I am transitioning to criminal law.

Always (permanent): I work at the Criminal Court.

Now (temporary): My caseload is increasing this month.

Always (permanent): I handle criminal cases every day.

Now (temporary): We are going through jury selection this week.

Always (permanent): I commute between two cities every week.

Concept Check

Let's Check

"I am transitioning to criminal law." — Is this permanent or temporary?

Temporary — it is a change happening now.

"I work at the Criminal Court." — Is this happening right now or is it a permanent fact?

Permanent fact — it is his regular job.

"My caseload is increasing." — Is this a finished action or a change in progress?

A change in progress — it is still happening.
