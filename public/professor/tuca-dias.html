Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = habits, routines, facts that are always true.
Past Simple = completed actions in the past, with a specific time.

TenseFormulaExample
Present Simple (+)Subject + verb (base form / -s)"I travel every year." / "She grows coffee."
Present Simple (-)Subject + do/does + not + verb"I do not travel alone."
Present Simple (?)Do/Does + subject + verb?"Do you like traveling?"
Past Simple (+)Subject + verb (past form)"I visited Mexico last year."
Past Simple (-)Subject + did + not + verb (base)"I did not visit Japan."
Past Simple (?)Did + subject + verb (base)?"Did you enjoy the trip?"

Key Patterns for Travel

  • "I usually..." + present simple (habits)
  • "Last year I..." + past simple (completed action)
  • "The most interesting thing was..." + past simple (experience)
  • "I live in..." + present simple (fact)
  • "I met..." + past simple (specific event)

Common Mistake:

"I go to Mexico last year."

"I went to Mexico last year."

With "last year," "yesterday," or any specific past time, always use the past simple form of the verb.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Continuous = Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing. Use it for actions happening right now.

Going to = Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb. Use it for future plans and intentions.

FormPresent ContinuousGoing to
+I am waiting at the gate.I am going to fly to Mexico.
-She is not checking luggage.He is not going to check luggage.
?Are you waiting at the gate?Are you going to fly today?
Common Mistake: "I am go to the airport." This is wrong because present continuous needs -ing: "I am going to the airport." And for future plans: "I am going to go to the airport."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar
Present Perfect for Life Experiences

Use the present perfect (have/has + past participle) to talk about life experiences when the exact time does not matter.

FormStructureExample
+Subject + have/has + past participleI have visited 20 countries.
-Subject + have/has + not + past participleI have never been to Spain.
?Have/Has + subject + past participleHave you ever been to Asia?
Trigger Words

ever (questions) | never (negative experiences) | already (done before expected) | yet (not done, expected) | before (at some point in the past) | so far (up to now)

Common Mistake

Present Perfect = life experience (no specific time): "I have been to Japan."
Past Simple = specific moment: "I went to Japan last year."
WRONG: "I have been to Japan last year."

Look at the Orange Words

Grammar Discovery

What Do They Have in Common?

The first two sentences describe habits and facts (now).
The last two describe completed actions (past).

TenseUseExample
Present SimpleHabits, routines, facts"I travel every year."
Present SimpleWhat you do / where you live"I grow coffee."
Past SimpleFinished actions (specific time)"I went to Mexico last year."
Past SimpleExperiences in the past"I visited the market."
Grammar Table

Present Simple vs Past Simple

Present SimplePast Simple
I travel every year.I traveled to Mexico last year.
She grows coffee.She grew up in Sao Paulo.
I live in a small town.I lived in Pocos de Caldas for 10 years.
I love learning new things.I loved the food in Morocco.
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I go to Mexico last year."
"I went to Mexico last year."

With last year, yesterday, last month = always use Past Simple

Grammar

Reveal the Rule

Present Continuous

Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing

For actions happening NOW

Going to

Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb

For future plans and intentions

Grammar Forms

Present Continuous & Going to

FormPresent ContinuousGoing to
+I am waiting at the gate.I am going to fly to Mexico.
-She is not checking luggage.He is not going to check luggage.
?Are you waiting?Are you going to fly?
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"I am go to the airport now."
"I am going to the airport now."
"They going to miss the flight."
"They are going to miss the flight."

Remember: am/is/are is always needed!
