When You Don't Understand

You miss one word in a meeting. Tonight, you don't freeze — you ask.

Warm-Up

Before We Start

In Lesson 3 you talked at a booth. Was it easy to understand every word?
Think of a real call in English. What happens when you miss one word?
When you don't understand, do you ask — or do you stay quiet and lose the rest?
The Truth

You Don't Need Every Word

Nobody understands 100% — not even native speakers.
Missing a word is normal. Freezing is the only mistake.
Catch the main idea. Then ask for the rest.

When you don't understand, you have a choice: go quiet, or say one small phrase.
Tonight you learn the phrases.

Tonight

Three Missions

1. Eight words from a real business meeting — and no translation.
2. Five rescue phrases: ask to repeat, to slow down, and to explain a word.
3. Stay in a meeting where you miss words — and never go quiet.

The Rescue Code

Grammar Discovery

What Do These Green Phrases Do?

What does 'agenda' mean?
Sorry, I'm not sure I follow.
WhenSay this
You missed itSorry, could you repeat that?
It was too fastCould you say that more slowly?
You don't know a wordWhat does [word] mean?
You are lostSorry, I'm not sure I follow.

Each hole has its own phrase. You never stay quiet — you pick the phrase and ask. And you start with Sorry or Could you, so it always sounds polite.

Common Mistake

Silence vs Asking

"What?" (or silence)
"Sorry, could you repeat that?"
"I no understand."
"Sorry, I'm not sure I follow."

A short "What?" sounds rude, and silence makes you lose the meeting. Start with Sorry or Could you, and the door stays open. Asking is not weak — it is professional.

Listening · Hard on purpose

The Meeting Opener

Fast, with hard words. You do NOT need them all — just the three answers.

0:000:00
1. What kind of meeting is this?
A quarterly meeting.
2. How many main points are on the agenda?
Three.
3. What can you do if something is not clear?
Stop the speaker and ask him to repeat a point or explain a word.

Role-Play · Level 3 · Free

Nothing on the Screen

You are in a real meeting. The other person speaks fast and uses words you don't know.

Your job is NOT to understand every word. Your job is to stay in the conversation: each time you get lost, use a rescue phrase — repeat, slow down, or ask the meaning — and catch the main idea.

No script. No keywords. Just don't go quiet.
