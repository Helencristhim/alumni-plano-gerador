Jornada de Transformação

De: “Entendo quase tudo, mas na hora de falar, tô engessada — e fico dependendo dos outros para me comunicar.”

Para: “Faço contato lá fora sozinha, conduzo reuniões internacionais e represento meu negócio em inglês com confiança.”

Promessa do Programa

De “tô engessada na hora de falar” a fazer contato lá fora com autonomia — porque você já entende quase tudo. Agora é hora de destravar o que você já sabe.

Forças

  • Compreensão auditiva e leitora fortemente desenvolvida
  • Resiliência e senso de humor como ferramentas de aprendizagem
  • Histórico comprovado de progresso rápido em imersão (Berlitz Orlando)
  • Vocabulário receptivo robusto em contexto corporativo
  • Autoconhecimento pedagógico elevado

Pontos de Melhoria

  • Produção oral travada — verb tenses, pronomes e word order sob pressão
  • Consistência de prática — padrão de evolução seguida de abandono
  • Escuta de sotaques não americanos
  • Vocabulário ativo limitado para produção espontânea
  • Dificuldade em construir frases complexas em tempo real

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines. Use it to talk about things that are always true or happen regularly.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeI/You/We/They + verb"I manage a team."
Affirmative (3rd)He/She/It + verb+s"She manages a team."
NegativeI + do not + verb"I do not work alone."
QuestionDo/Does + subject + verb?"Does she lead the team?"

"She manages a team."

SubjectPossessiveExample

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Frequency adverbs tell us how often something happens. They go between the subject and the main verb.

SubjectAdverbVerbExample
Ialways (100%)review"I always review my schedule."
Sheusually (80%)starts"She usually starts at seven."
Weoften (60%)have"We often have meetings."
Hesometimes (40%)works"He sometimes works late."
Theynever (0%)miss"They never miss a deadline."

Time expressions go at the end: "I have meetings every day." / "She starts in the morning." / "I review reports at night."

Common Mistake:

"I go always to meetings."

"I always go to meetings."

The adverb goes between the subject and the verb, not after the verb.

A fórmula da pergunta. / The question formula.

Structure / EstruturaUse / UsoExample / Exemplo

Possessives

They show who something belongs to.

SubjectPossessiveExample
ImyMy department is Commercial Excellence.
youyourWhat are your responsibilities?
hehisHis role is to oversee the strategy.
sheherHer responsibility is to lead projects.
weourOur processes are improving.
theytheirTheir performance is strong.

Common Mistake

"She department is Commercial Excellence."

"Her department is Commercial Excellence."

Use possessives (my, your, his, her, our, their) before nouns, not subject pronouns.

3rd person -s: She reports to the Director. He manages the team.

The Code

Present Simple & Frequency Adverbs

Look at these sentences

I always review the numbers.
She usually has three meetings.
We sometimes work late.
He never misses a deadline.

What do the orange words have in common?

Frequency Adverbs

Subject + ADVERB + verb

AdverbFrequencyExample
always100%I always review my schedule.
usually80%She usually starts at seven.
often60%We often have long meetings.
sometimes40%He sometimes works late.
never0%I never miss a deadline.

Time expressions go at the end: every day, in the morning, at night.

Common Mistake

"I go always to meetings."

"I always go to meetings."

Rule: Subject + ADVERB + verb. The adverb goes BEFORE the main verb.

Complete with the correct adverb

I ___ review my schedule before meetings. (100%)
???
I always review my schedule before meetings.
She ___ starts at seven in the morning. (80%)
???
She usually starts at seven in the morning.
We ___ work late on Fridays. (40%)
???
We sometimes work late on Fridays.
He ___ misses a deadline. (0%)
???
He never misses a deadline.
I ___ coordinate projects with other teams. (60%)
???
I often coordinate projects with other teams.
