Chapter 3 -- The Code

Present Simple & Frames

For facts, schedules and roles -- the language that opens a meeting

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

These are facts about the meeting. What do the orange words have in common?

"The meeting starts at nine o'clock." (schedule)

"I chair the weekly meeting." (role)

"Lewis takes the minutes." (role -- he)

"Each item takes fifteen minutes." (fact -- it)

"We cover three items today." (fact -- we)

Grammar Rule

Present Simple for Facts & Roles

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theybase form (cover, take)We cover three items.
He / She / Itverb + s (covers, takes)Lewis takes the minutes.
The meeting (it)verb + s (starts, ends)The meeting starts at nine.

Meeting frames (fixed phrases): "Thank you all for coming." -- "Let's get started." -- "Our objective is..." -- "Let me go through the agenda." -- "The first item is..." -- "Let's move on to the next item." -- "Let's wrap up."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The meeting start at nine."
"The meeting starts at nine."

With he, she, it (and "the meeting" = it), add -s to the verb in the present simple: the meeting starts, Lewis takes, she chairs.
