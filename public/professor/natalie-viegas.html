Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are permanent truths)
Present Continuous = actions happening now, temporary situations, current projects (actions in progress or temporary situations)

TenseStructureExample
Present Simple (+)Subject + verb (base/s)"She oversees public sector accounts."
Present Simple (-)Subject + do/does not + verb"She does not handle private sector deals."
Present Simple (?)Do/Does + subject + verb?"Does she report to the VP?"
Present Continuous (+)Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing"She is driving a transformation project."
Present Continuous (-)Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing"She is not working on that project anymore."

Common Mistake:

"I am work at Microsoft."

"I work at Microsoft." (permanent fact = Present Simple)

Use Present Simple for permanent facts. Use Present Continuous (am/is/are + -ing) only for actions in progress or temporary situations.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Routines = actions that happen regularly, habits, general facts
Adverbs of Frequency = words that indicate HOW OFTEN something happens

AdverbFrequencyExample
always100%"I always check my schedule."
usually80%"I usually attend the 9 AM meeting."
often60%"She often briefs her team."
sometimes40%"He sometimes works late."
rarely10%"She rarely skips meetings."
never0%"I never leave without wrapping up."

Position Rule:

Before the main verb: "I always review my emails."

After the verb BE: "She is usually on time."

Before the main verb. After the verb BE (am/is/are).

Common Mistake:

"I check always my email."

"I always check my email."

The frequency adverb comes BEFORE the main verb, never after.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar
Passive Voice: Structure and Use

Use the passive voice when the action is more important than who performed it, or when the agent is unknown or obvious. This is very common in formal reports.

TenseActivePassive
Present Simple"The team writes the report.""The report is written by the team."
Past Simple"Natalie drafted the summary.""The summary was drafted by Natalie."
Present Perfect"They have revised the document.""The document has been revised."
Future (will)"We will implement the changes.""The changes will be implemented."

Key rule: Passive = be (conjugated) + past participle. The "by + agent" is optional and often omitted in formal writing when the agent is obvious or unimportant.

Use the First Conditional for real, likely situations in the future. Use polite requests (Could/Would) to sound professional with clients.

What form of the verb comes after should, could, and would?

Reveal the Rule

ModalUseExample
shouldadvice, recommendation"We should start with the budget."
couldpossibility, polite request"Could you share the data?"
wouldpolite preference, formal request"I would like to recap."

Rule: modal + base form (no -s, no -ing, no "to")

Common Mistake

"We should to review the agenda."
"We should review the agenda."
"Could you sharing the screen?"

"She would like to recap."

Choose the Right Modal

You want to recommend reviewing the agenda. "___ we review the agenda first?"
"Should we review the agenda first?"
You want to politely ask someone to share data. "___ you share the latest numbers?"
"Could you share the latest numbers?"
You want to express a polite preference. "I ___ like to recap the action items."
"I would like to recap the action items."
You want to suggest ending the meeting. "We ___ adjourn if there are no more questions."
"We could adjourn if there are no more questions."
