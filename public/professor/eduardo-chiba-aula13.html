Vocabulary -- Part 1

Words to Stay in Control

To make something clearer
Clarify
To make a point or question clearer so there is no confusion
"Could you clarify what period you mean?"
To give yourself thinking time
Buy time
To give yourself a few seconds to think before answering
"That is a great question; let me buy a little time."
To accept a point seriously
Take on board
To accept and seriously consider someone's point
"I will take your concern on board."
To avoid a clear answer
Hedge
To answer carefully without committing to an exact figure
"I would hedge and say it is roughly eight percent."
An approximate number
Ballpark
An approximate figure, not the exact number
"As a ballpark, I would say around ten percent."
Pressured to answer now
On the spot

Chapter 3 -- Hedging & Questions

The Q&A Code

Ask politely, answer carefully

Discover the Pattern

What Do the Orange Words Share?

"Could you tell me which period you mean?"

"May I ask what your main concern is?"

"Do you mind if I ask where that number is from?"

"I was wondering whether you meant this quarter."

The Rule

Clarify & Hedge

ToolFormExample
Indirect questionCould you tell me + subject + verb"Could you tell me what you mean?"
Polite openerMay I ask / Do you mind if I ask"May I ask where it is from?"
Hedge a numberroughly / around / a ballpark of"It is roughly eight percent."
Hedge a claimit is hard to say exactly, but..."It is hard to say exactly, but growth is strong."

Key idea: in an indirect question the word order goes back to normal — subject before verb, and no "do" or "does". Hedging buys you safety: you sound confident without committing to a number you are not sure of.

Watch Out

Common Mistake

Could you tell me what is the figure?
Could you tell me what the figure is?

After "Could you tell me", the question turns into a statement: subject before verb, and no question inversion. So it is "what the figure is", not "what is the figure".
