Chapter 3: The Code

What Is Happening Now

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"The flight is boarding right now."

"The flight attendant is standing at the door."

"We are staying at the Hotel Lisboa for six nights."

"I am waiting, and my husband is parking the car."

Every green verb has two pieces. What is the first piece? And what is at the end of the second?

FormUseExample
am / is / are
+ verb-ing		Two pieces, always. Never only one.I am waiting · she is boarding · we are staying
Now, not alwaysSomething happening at this moment, or only for a short time (this trip, this week).The plane is landing. We are staying for six nights.
The other one:
habits		For what you ALWAYS or USUALLY do, use the present simple — no -ing.I usually travel in September. / Right now I am traveling in July.
No
am / is / are + not		"not" goes in the middle, between the two pieces.He is not sleeping. I am not finding it.
Question
Am / Is / Are + you + verb-ing?		The first piece jumps to the front. The -ing never moves.Are you checking in? What is your husband doing?

Two pieces: am / is / are + the verb with -ing. One piece alone is never enough.

Spelling

Adding -ing

Most verbs just take -ing. Two small groups change first.

board → boarding (just add -ing)
land → landing
stay → staying
sit → sitting (double the last letter)
come → coming (the -e goes away)
travel → traveling (American English: one L)
The Contrast

Always vs Right Now

Same person, same verb — two different tenses, because the time is different.

Always / usually — your life

"I usually travel in September."
"My husband always talks to the receptionist."

Right now — this trip

"Right now I am traveling in July."
"Today I am talking to him, and my husband is sitting down."

Now say one true pair about you: "I usually... but right now I am..."

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I am go to the front desk."
"I am going to the front desk."
"Right now I travel with my husband."
"Right now I am traveling with my husband."
"I am looking forward to walk around Lisbon."
"I am looking forward to walking around Lisbon."

Two pieces, always: am / is / are + verb-ing. And after looking forward to, the verb also takes -ing — that "to" is not the "to" of "to walk".

Reading

The First Hour in Lisbon

The First Hour in Lisbon

It is four o'clock in the afternoon, and Rosângela is standing at gate twenty-two. The flight to Lisbon is boarding right now. She is holding her passport and her boarding pass in her hand, and she is listening to the announcement. Her husband is standing next to her, and he is not saying anything, because today he does not need to. Rosângela is reading the screen herself.

Now she is walking into the plane. A flight attendant is standing at the door, and she is smiling. "Good afternoon. Eighteen C? It is on the right." Rosângela is putting her carry-on in the overhead bin, above her seat, and she is closing her seat belt. She usually sleeps on a night flight, but tonight she is not sleeping: she is looking out of the window, and she is thinking about tomorrow. Nine hours later, the plane is landing in Lisbon. It is eight o'clock in the morning, and the sun is shining.

At half past nine, they are arriving at the hotel. Rosângela is walking into the lobby with two bags, and she is going to the front desk alone. Her husband is sitting on a sofa, and he is waiting with the bags. "Good morning. We have a reservation, under the name Oliveira." The receptionist is looking at his computer, and he is not finding it. Rosângela is not panicking, and she is not looking for her husband. She is trying again, slowly: "Our reservation is under the name Pinheiro de Oliveira. We are staying for six nights." Now he is smiling. "Here it is. Room four eleven, on the fourth floor. Here is your room key, and the elevator is on your left." It is her first hour in Europe, and she is doing it alone.

Dialogue

The Front Desk in Lisbon

T
Good morning, and welcome to the Hotel Lisboa. Are you checking in?
R
Good morning. Yes, we are. We have a reservation, under the name Oliveira.
T
Let me see... I am sorry. I am looking at the computer, and I am not finding anything under Oliveira.
R
Could you look under Pinheiro de Oliveira, please? It is a long name — my full name is on my passport.
T
Ah, here it is. Pinheiro de Oliveira. Two people, six nights. Is that right?
R
Yes, that is right. We are staying for six nights, and we are leaving on the twelfth.
T
Perfect. Room four eleven, on the fourth floor. Here is your room key. The elevator is on your left, next to the lobby.
R
Thank you. What time is breakfast? And I am sorry — could you repeat the room number, please?
T
Of course. Room four eleven. Breakfast is from seven to ten, here on the ground floor. Are you looking forward to Lisbon?
R
I am! I am looking forward to walking around the old city. My husband is parking the car right now — he is not looking forward to that!
