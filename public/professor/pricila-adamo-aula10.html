Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Pricila walks through the market and stops at a colorful stall. She sees a scarf and asks, "How much is this?" The seller says forty dollars. "That is too expensive," Pricila replies. "It is not cheap enough for me. Can you give me a discount?" They bargain, and the price drops to thirty-five. Pricila asks for a smaller size and pays with cash. The seller gives her the change. "I will take it," she smiles. "It is a perfect souvenir."

1. How does Pricila ask the price?
A "How much is this?"
B "How much costs?"
C "What price this?"
2. Which sentence uses "too" correctly?
A "That is too expensive."
B "That is expensive too much."
C "That is enough expensive."
3. How does she ask for a lower price?
A "Make discount."
B "Can you give me a discount?"
C "Price down please."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

How much is + singular / How much are + plural (preço)
too + adjetivo = demais (problema): too expensive
adjetivo + enough = suficiente: cheap enough / not cheap enough

UseStructureExample
Ask priceHow much is / are...?"How much is this?"
Too muchtoo + adjective"It is too expensive."
Enoughadjective + enough"It is not cheap enough."
BargainCan you give me a discount?"Can you give me a discount?"

Common Mistake:

"It is enough expensive."

"It is too expensive."

"too" vem ANTES do adjetivo; "enough" vem DEPOIS do adjetivo.

Chapter 3: The Code

Prices &
Bargaining

Quantifiers + prices + too / enough

Grammar Discovery

Spot the Pattern

How much is this scarf?
It is too expensive.
It is not cheap enough for me.

How do we ask the price and react to it?

UseStructureExample
Ask the priceHow much is + singular / are + plural?"How much is this?" / "How much are these?"
Too much (problem)too + adjective"It is too expensive."
Enough / not enoughadjective + enough / not + adj + enough"It is not cheap enough."
Quantityhow many (count) / how much (uncount)"How many do you have?"

Key: too vem ANTES do adjetivo (too expensive); enough vem DEPOIS (cheap enough). Para bargainar: "Can you give me a discount?"

Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"It is enough expensive." / "How much costs?"
"It is too expensive." / "How much is it?"

too + adjective (before). enough after the adjective.
