Can You Say That Again? -- Clarification and Survival Strategies
A aula em que não entender deixa de ser o fim da conversa. Ela trava sempre no mesmo ponto: o outro fala rápido, uma palavra passa voando, e ela congela — e por dentro pensa “pronto, ele descobriu que meu inglês é ruim”. Aqui está o segredo que muda tudo: o americano também não entende metade das vezes — e ele simplesmente pergunta. "Sorry, could you repeat that?" não é frase de iniciante: é a frase que o diretor de Miami mais usa. Esta aula não tem gramática nova — ela pega o could da aula 9 e as perguntas da aula 8 e monta um kit de sobrevivência de seis palavras (repeat, understand, mean, catch, again, moment) e cinco frases que mantêm qualquer ligação viva. A virada: no seguro, pedir para repetir não é fraqueza — quem adivinha um número perde a conta; quem pergunta, não. -- 35 slides

Chapter 2: The Words

Six Words to Say “Wait—What?”

Não são palavras para entender o mundo. São para dizer que você não entendeu.

Vocabulary

Words 1-3

0 / 3 words revealed

Repeat
repetir
"Sorry, could you repeat that?" (Desculpe, você poderia repetir?) Diga ri-PEET — força na segunda sílaba, e o fim é um T seco. Nunca "RE-pit".
What you do NOT do when the words are too fast. "I don't ___."
Understand
entender
"I don't understand." (Eu não entendo.) Diga un-der-STAND — a força cai no FIM, na terceira sílaba. O português quer pôr no meio; não deixe.
You ask this about ONE word you do not know. "What does it ___?"
Mean
significar / querer dizer
"What does that word mean?" (O que essa palavra significa?) Diga MIIN, com um E bem longo — nunca "men", que é outra palavra (homens).
Vocabulary

Words 4-6

0 / 3 words revealed

You heard the sound but it went past you. "Sorry, I didn't ___ that."
Catch
captar / pegar (o que foi dito)
"Sorry, I didn't catch that." (Desculpe, não peguei o que você disse.) Diga KATCH — o A é aberto e o final é um TCH curto. Não é pegar uma bola; é captar o que foi dito.
One more time. "Could you say that ___?"
Again
de novo
"Could you say that again?" (Você poderia dizer de novo?) Diga uh-GHEN — força na segunda, e o fim rima com "ten", não com "rain". Nunca "a-GUEIN".
Three seconds to find the file. "Just a ___, let me check."
Moment
momento / instante
"Just a moment, let me check." (Só um instante, deixa eu ver.) Diga MOH-ment — força na PRIMEIRA sílaba. O português bota no "MEN" (momENto); o inglês bota no "MO".

Chapter 3: The Survival Kit

Nothing New — Just Assembled

O could da aula 9 · as perguntas da aula 8 · juntos num kit

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Com que duas palavras começam as três primeiras frases?

Could you repeat that?

Could you speak more slowly?

Could you spell that?

Três pedidos começam com Could you — e o verbo vem pelado (aula 9). A pergunta de sentido usa does (aula 8). Nada novo.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Repeat, please!"
"Could you repeat that, please?"
"What means this word?"
"What does this word mean?"
"I no understand."
"I don't understand."

Nenhum dos três é falta de inteligência — são atalhos. O primeiro é a ordem seca: soa como um pedido a um garçom, e o conserto é o Could you que você já aprendeu na aula 9. O segundo é a tradução direta do “o que significa” — em inglês a pergunta precisa do does (aula 8): “What does it mean?”. O terceiro é o negativo sem o don't (aula 3): “I don't understand”. Os três consertos já estão com você — só falta o automático.

Read for the Main Idea

The Note That Says Ask

The Survival Kit — Zurich Brazil, Note for International Calls

Every week, someone at Zurich Brazil is on a call in English, and the other person says a number, a name, or a word too fast. This note is here to say one thing, and it is the whole note: when you do not catch it, you ask. You do not guess. A wrong number in our business is not an embarrassment — it is money.

How you ask. There are four lines, and you already have them. "Sorry, could you repeat that?" — for the whole sentence. "Could you speak more slowly?" — when you have the words but not the speed. "What does that word mean?" — when it is only one word. And "Just a moment" — when you need three seconds to find the file or the number. That is the entire kit. Four lines, and no call can beat them.

Here is the part people do not believe. The Americans on the call do it too. Every week. Greg, the director in Miami, asks "sorry, say that again?" more than anyone on this floor. He is not a beginner — he is the director. Asking someone to repeat is not weakness, and it never was. In twenty countries and twenty accents, everybody misses words. The professional asks. The beginner nods and guesses.

Two people from this office. Ricardo, broker relations: "For a year I nodded on every call and understood half of it. Then I started to ask — could you repeat that? Nobody thought less of me. They slowed down, and my numbers stopped being wrong." And Sofia, commercial manager: "The people who ask are the professionals. The ones who guess are the ones I worry about. Nobody at this company ever lost an account for asking someone to repeat. People lose accounts for guessing."

Comprehension

About the Call

1. What does Greg want to know?
The deductible on the factory account — how much it is. He has two minutes, so he is quick.
2. What does the word "deductible" mean? Greg explains it.
It's the part the client pays before the insurance pays — franquia in Portuguese. Paula did not know the word, so she asked.
3. When Paula did not understand, did she guess? What did she do?
She did not guess. She asked: "Could you speak more slowly?", "What does deductible mean?", and "Just a moment, let me check." Three tools from the kit, in one call.
4. What does Greg say at the end of the call?
"You just did that whole call in English without stopping once. That's the job." He never says her English is bad — he only wanted the number, and he got it.

1. The client speaks too fast, but you have the words. Which line?
"Could you speak more slowly?" — the problem is the speed, not the words, so you ask only about the speed.
2. There is one word you do not know. Which line — and why not "What means it"?
"What does that word mean?" The question needs does (Lesson 8): "What DOES it MEAN?", never "What means it".
3. You need three seconds to find the file. Which line? And what do you NOT do?
"Just a moment, let me check." What you do NOT do is go silent — silence sounds like you are lost. Say the line out loud.
4. Look at the whole sheet. When do you go back to Portuguese?
Never. That is the point of the sheet: five lines that keep the call in English. You do not have to understand everything — you have to keep the call alive.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

Now It's You

Do apoio total à fala livre — em três passos.

Quick Fire

One Situation at a Time

1 / 6

You are on a video call. The other person said a long sentence, and all of it went past you. Ask for the whole thing again -- politely.

  • Sorry, could you repeat that?
  • Sorry -- could you say that again, please?

You have the words, but the person is speaking very fast. Do not ask about the words -- ask only about the speed.

  • Could you speak more slowly?
  • Sorry -- could you speak more slowly, please?

The client said one word you do not know: "deductible". Everything else was clear. Ask what that one word means.

  • What does "deductible" mean?
  • Sorry, what does that word mean?

You need three seconds to find the number in your file. Do not go silent, and do not panic -- buy the time out loud.

  • Just a moment, let me check.
  • One moment, please.

You did not catch a name on the phone. You have most of it, but not the spelling. Ask.

  • Could you spell that, please?
  • How do you spell that?

The line was bad and you missed the last part of what Greg said. Say you didn't catch it -- and do not say sorry three times.

  • Sorry, I didn't catch that.
  • I didn't catch the last part. Could you repeat it?
Role-Play

Guided — Greg Uses a Word You Don't Know

Scenario: Greg calls again. He is quick, and he uses one word you do not know. Do not guess it — ask what it means, ask him to slow down, take a moment if you need it. The lines are right here on the screen. Keep the call in English.

Keywords:

Sorry, could you repeat that? Could you speak more slowly? What does that word mean? Just a moment, let me check.
Role-Play

Semi-free — The Line Is Bad

Scenario: Same call, but the line is bad and Greg is in a hurry. He talks fast, part of it is hard to hear, and he asks one question that is not on this screen. Do not stop, and do not go back to Portuguese — use the kit, catch what you can, and ask for the rest.

Keywords:

Sorry, I didn't catch that. Could you say that again? I don't understand.
Free Practice

The Whole Call — No Screen, No Portuguese

Scenario: A full call with Greg, alone, no screen. He is fast, the line is bad, he uses a word you do not know, and he asks something you did not prepare. Your job is not to understand every word. Your job is to finish the call — ask, take a moment, catch what you can, and never go back to Portuguese.

One rule, and it is the whole lesson: when you don't understand, you ask — you do not guess, and you do not go silent. Use at least four lines from the kit. Finishing the call, even imperfectly, is the win.

What I Can Do Now

I know my six words: repeat, understand, mean, catch, again, moment.
I can ask for the whole sentence: "Could you repeat that?" / "Could you say that again?"
