Metodologia — 5 Abordagens

1. Ativar a fala, não ensinar do zero

O inglês da Lucia está em modo leitura. O programa transforma leitura em FALA e em ESCRITA executiva — sobre a carreira e o mundo real dela, nunca frases genéricas.

2. Contexto real de carreira

Todo vocabulário e cenário vem do universo dela: entrevistas nos EUA, apresentações executivas, IA e Segurança de IA, o green card. O inglês serve à meta concreta.

3. Descoberta da gramática

A regra nunca vem primeiro: ela vê exemplos, percebe o padrão e só então revela a estrutura. Aprender fazendo — respeitando o nível intelectual de uma executiva sênior.

4. Sound-first listening

O áudio vem antes do texto. Como o listening já é forte, usamos essa força para modelar fala natural de executivos americanos e treinar a produção por imitação.

5. Diagnóstico e linha de base

A Aula 1 grava a linha de base ("This is who I am") que será recomparada nas aulas 24 e 48 — o progresso fica audível e mensurável, não abstrato.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to say what is always true about your career and what you are working on right now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he / she + -s)		Facts, routines and permanent situations - always true.I work in AI security.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Right now, today, these days - temporary or unfinished.I am preparing for an interview.
Negativedo not / does not + verb · am not / is not / are not + -ingI do not read from a script. / She is not leading that team now.

Time wordsevery day, always, usually, never → simple · right now, today, this year, these days → continuous

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your experience, your recent news, and how long you have done something.

FormUseExample
Affirmative
have / has + past participle		Experience in your life so far, with no exact date.I have led teams on three continents.
Negative
have not / has not + participle		Something that has not happened up to now (often with "yet").I have not finished my mission yet.
Question
Have / Has + subject + participle		Ask about experience (often with "ever").Have you ever led a global team?
Recent news
just / already / yet		Something that happened very recently and still matters.I have just moved into AI safety.
Duration
for / since		for + a period (for ten years) · since + a point in time (since 2010). A finished past time takes the past simple: "I led that team in 2015."

Lesson 04 -- Pre-class

First Conditional in Executive Presentations -- Owning the Room in an AI Safety Briefing

Building arguments and making predictions with the first conditional. Key words: governance, compliance, framework, exposure, to mitigate, threshold, to enforce, to roll out. Structure: first conditional (if + present simple, will + base verb) for predictions, risks, and recommendations, plus unless and as long as.

▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Governance -- the system of rules and decisions that controls how an organization is run
"If our governance is clear, the whole team will make better decisions."
Compliance -- the act of following the official rules, laws, or standards for your work
"If we ignore compliance, we will face serious problems later."
Framework -- a clear structure of rules and ideas you use to plan and decide
"If we build a strong framework, we will scale with confidence."
Exposure -- the amount of risk or possible harm you are open to
"If we act now, we will reduce our exposure significantly."
To mitigate -- to make a risk or a problem less serious or less likely
"If we add these controls, we will mitigate most of the risk."
Threshold -- the level at which something starts to happen or becomes acceptable
"If the risk passes this threshold, we will pause the launch."
To enforce -- to make sure a rule or a policy is actually followed
"If we do not enforce the policy, no one will follow it."
To roll out -- to introduce a new policy or program step by step
"If the board approves, we will roll out the framework next quarter."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Lucia is briefing the board on AI safety. "If we do not set a clear threshold, our exposure will grow," she says. "But if we roll out this framework and enforce it, we will mitigate most of the risk.
