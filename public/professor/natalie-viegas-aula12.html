Structure Map

Everything You Own

StructureUse it forExample
Present perfectexperience with a result now"I have presented the data."
Passive voicethe action matters, not who did it"The proposal was approved."
First conditionala real future possibility"If we keep this pace, we will win."
Comparativescomparing two things"This quarter is stronger than the last."
Modalssuggestions and possibility"We should review the agenda."
Question tagsconfirm and connect"You work in tech, don't you?"

Key idea: you do not choose a structure at random -- you choose it for a reason. Present perfect links the past to now; the passive moves the focus to the action; the first conditional talks about a real future; comparatives measure two things; modals soften and suggest; question tags confirm and invite a reply.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"The proposal was approve by procurement."
"The proposal was approved by procurement."

The passive voice always uses the past participle (approved, written, sent), never the base verb. The same care applies to the present perfect: "I have presented", never "I have present".
