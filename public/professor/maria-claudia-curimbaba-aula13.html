Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Focus on the that-clause structure

I recommend that the team review the data. (NOT "reviews")
She suggests that he present the findings. (NOT "presents")
We propose that the company invest in technology. (NOT "invests")
I suggest that we postpone the launch. (base form after "that")
Chapter 3 -- The Code

What is the pattern?

Three ways to complete a proposal

I would like to suggest / propose / recommend

PatternStructureExample
+ Nounverb + noun/noun phrase"I would like to suggest a partnership."
+ Gerundverb + -ing form"I would like to propose expanding to Miami."
+ That clauseverb + that + subject + base verb"I recommend that we hire two analysts."

Formality scale: "I want to" (informal) < "I suggest" (neutral) < "I would like to suggest" (formal). Use base form after "that" (subjunctive): "that he go" NOT "that he goes".

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"I suggest to change the timeline."

Never use "to" after suggest

"I suggest changing the timeline."

Correct: suggest + gerund (-ing)

"I recommend that he changes the approach."

Never conjugate after "that" (subjunctive)

"I recommend that he change the approach."

Correct: base form after "that"

After suggest/recommend/propose, use -ing (gerund) or "that + subject + base verb." NEVER "suggest to do."

Chapter 5 -- Practice

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to see the correction

"I suggest to postpone the meeting."
"I suggest postponing the meeting." (suggest + gerund, never + to)
"I recommend that she contacts the stakeholders."
"I recommend that she contact the stakeholders." (base form after "that")
"The ROI is the return of investment."
"The ROI is the return on investment." (Return ON Investment)
"I would like to propose that we should expand to Miami."
"I would like to propose that we expand to Miami." (no "should" with subjunctive)
"The key benefit are reduced costs."
"The key benefit is reduced costs." (benefit = singular, IS)
