Possessives and There is / There are

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What do the purple words have in common?

The company's revenue increased last quarter.
Maria Claudia's holding oversees three subsidiaries.
The board's decision was unanimous.
Our shareholders' meeting is in March.

Possessives: 's vs s'

FormUseExample
'sSingular noun or nameThe company's headquarters. Maria Claudia's holding.
s'Plural noun ending in -sThe shareholders' meeting. The directors' vote.
's (irregular)Plural NOT ending in -sThe people's choice. The children's school.

Key rule: Add 's to singular nouns and names. Add only ' to plurals ending in -s.

Practice: Make it possessive

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

What is the pattern here?

There are three subsidiaries in the holding.
There is one headquarters in Sao Paulo.
There are several branches across Brazil.

There is / There are

FormUseExample
There isSingular / UncountableThere is one headquarters. There is a lot of revenue.
There arePlural / CountableThere are three subsidiaries. There are many shareholders.

Tip: Look at the noun AFTER "there is/are" to decide. Singular = is. Plural = are.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"The company have three branch."

Missing possessive + wrong verb + missing plural

"The company has three branches."

Correct: has (3rd person) + plural noun

"There is many shareholders."

Singular "is" with plural "shareholders"

"There are many shareholders."

Plural "are" matches plural "shareholders"
