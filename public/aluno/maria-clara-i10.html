GRAMMAR

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Continuous (I am working / she is arriving)

GRAMMAR

Use the present continuous for something happening right now or around now (this week, these days). It always has two parts: the verb be (am / is / are) + verb-ing.

Subject+ Yes- No? Question
II am workingI am not workingAm I working?
he / she / itShe is workingShe is not workingIs she working?
you / we / theyWe are workingWe are not workingAre we working?

Spelling of verb-ing: make → making (drop the e), sit → sitting (double the consonant), travel → traveling. Compare: the present simple is for routines ("I travel once a month"), the present continuous is for now ("I am traveling right now"). GRAMMAR

English has two presents. Use the present simple for a habit, a routine, or a permanent fact (I travel). Use the present continuous (am/is/are + verb-ing) for something happening now or a temporary situation (I am traveling). One question decides: habit or now?

MeaningTenseFormSignal words
Habit / routine / factPresent simpleI travel / she travelsalways, usually, every day
Now / temporaryPresent continuousI am traveling / she is travelingnow, at the moment, this week, currently

Watch out: in the present simple, add -s for she/he/it: "she travels". In the present continuous, don't forget am / is / are: "I am preparing", never "I preparing". Two useful phrases for a dinner: It depends on... (a careful answer) and I would rather... (a polite preference).

GRAMMAR

To say who something belongs to, use a possessive adjective (my, your, his, her, our, their) right before the noun, or add 's to a person's name. The word matches the person, not the noun after it.

name + 'sApostrophe + s after a person's name shows the owner.Clarinha's smile · my son's house
he → his, she → herThe possessive matches the person, not the noun. A woman = her; a man = his.her name (Clarinha) · his name (my son)
