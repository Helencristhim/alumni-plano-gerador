Grammar

Read the text and answer the questions.

Tânia is at the airport. She is checking in for her flight to Paris. At the counter, she asks: "Could I have a window seat, please?" The agent says: "Can I see your boarding pass?" Tânia could not find her gate at first, but an agent helped her. She asks: "Could you tell me where Terminal B is?" Her departure time is 3:30 PM from gate B7.

1. What does Tânia ask for at the counter?
A A window seat.
B A meal on the plane.
C A new passport.

B 3:30 PM.
C 8:00 PM.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Aqui está a regra. Leia com calma e veja os exemplos.

Can / Could — Pedidos Educados (Polite Requests)

Usamos "Can" e "Could" para fazer pedidos educados. "Could" é mais formal e polido que "Can". / We use "Can" and "Could" for polite requests. "Could" is more formal and polite than "Can".

FormStructureExample
Request (pedido)Could I + verb...?Could I have a window seat, please?
Asking for help (pedir ajuda)Can/Could you + verb...?Can you help me with my luggage?
Negative (negativa)Subject + could not + verbI could not find my gate.
Common Mistake / Erro Comum:
"I want a window seat." ✗
"Could I have a window seat, please?" ✓
Use "Could I...?" para pedir com educação. NUNCA "I want" em pedidos!

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

You want a window seat. How do you ask politely?
A "I want a window seat."
B "Could I have a window seat, please?"
C "Give me a window seat."
D "Window seat now."
You cannot find your gate. What do you say?
A "Where gate?"
B "I want find gate."
C "Excuse me, could you tell me where gate B7 is?"
D "Gate B7 where?"
The agent asks: "Can I help you?" What do you say?
A "No."
B "Yes, please. I am looking for Terminal B."
