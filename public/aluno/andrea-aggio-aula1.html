Chapter 3: The Code

Your Story in Three Tenses

I have worked · I run · I lived

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I have worked in tourism for over twenty years."

"I run the commercial team at Escape Turismo."

"We have just opened two new venues on the coast."

"I lived in San Diego when I was twenty."

Why green for experience, but blue for a finished time?

FormUseExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience and duration up to now (for / since); very recent news (just)I have worked here for 20 years.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Permanent facts, your current role and routineI run the commercial team.
Past Simple
verb + -ed		Finished action at a finished time (in 2005, when I was 20, yesterday)I lived in San Diego in my twenties.

for + a period (for 20 years) · since + a starting point (since 2004).

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I work in tourism for twenty years."
"I have worked in tourism for twenty years."
"I have lived in San Diego in 2005."
"I lived in San Diego in 2005."

Duration up to now uses have / has + past participle with for / since. A finished time (in 2005, yesterday) always uses the past simple.

The Whole Conversation

Scenario: From "Hi!" to "Let us set up a meeting": strike up a conversation with a hotelier at the fair, introduce yourself and your company, explain what you are looking for, exchange contacts, and agree to follow up.

No keywords. The conversation is yours!
