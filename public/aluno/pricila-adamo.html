Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (coisas que acontecem regularmente ou são verdades)
Present Perfect = experiences, achievements, actions connected to now (experiências, conquistas, ações com conexão ao presente)

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"She lives in Araras."
Present PerfectSubject + have/has + past participle"She has traveled to many countries."
Present Perfect ContinuousSubject + have/has been + verb-ing"She has been studying English since 2000."

Common Mistake:

"I study English since 2000."

"I have been studying English since 2000."

Use Present Perfect (Continuous) com "since" e "for" para indicar duração até o presente.

Adjective Order = opinion > size > age > color > origin > material (ordem dos adjetivos)

TypeRuleExample
Short adj + -er1-2 syllables: add -er / -est"older than" / "the oldest"
Long adj + more3+ syllables: more/most + adj"more beautiful than" / "the most beautiful"

Common Mistake:

"Canada was more better than Malta."

"Canada was better than Malta."

NUNCA use "more" com adjetivos que já têm forma comparativa (better, worse, older).
