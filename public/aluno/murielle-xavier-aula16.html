Short vs Long

Use the before every superlative

high → the highest, big → the biggest

LONG ADJ (the most ...)

efficient → the most efficient, outstanding → the most outstanding

Chapter 3

Superlative Adjectives

The highest, the most efficient, the best

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"This is our most efficient region."

"She has the highest retention rate."

"It was the best quarter of the year."

"He is one of the most reliable managers."

TypeFormExample
Short adjectivethe + adj + -estthe highest, the biggest
Long adjectivethe most + adjthe most efficient
Irregularthe best, the worstthe best result
One of the...one of the + superlative + pluralone of the most reliable teams
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

This is the most biggest region.

✓ RIGHT

This is the biggest region.

✗ WRONG

She has the highest-est rate.

✓ RIGHT

She has the highest rate.

✗ WRONG

It was the goodest quarter.

✓ RIGHT

It was the best quarter.

✗ WRONG

He is most efficient manager.

✓ RIGHT

He is the most efficient manager.

Short adjectives take the ...-est. Long adjectives take the most ... Good and bad are irregular: the best, the worst. Always use the before a superlative.

Chapter 4

The Ranking

Reviewing a ranking to name the best

Dialogue

The Ranking

E
Murielle, here is the ranking. Which region is the strongest this year?
M
The North is our best region by far. It has the highest retention and the lowest turnover.
E
And the weakest?
M
The South is the weakest, but it made the most remarkable progress of all.
E
Good point. Who is your standout manager?
M
Diana. She is our most outstanding leader, and her track record is unmatched.
E
So Diana wins the most prestigious award this year?
M
Yes. She is the foremost example of what our best teams can do.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. Which region is the strongest, and why?
The North; it has the highest retention and the lowest turnover.
2. Which region made the most remarkable progress?
The South, even though it is the weakest.
3. Why does Diana win the award?
She is the most outstanding leader and her track record is unmatched.

I can use the new words: outstanding, exceptional, leading, remarkable, unmatched, foremost, prominent, prestigious, flagship, standout, peak, utmost.
