Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context Grammar

Leia o texto com calma. As palavras em negrito são do vocabulário de hoje.

Gabriela is in Paris. She is at the metro station. She wants to find her hotel. She asks a woman: “How do I get to Hotel Le Marais?” The woman says: “Go straight on this street. Turn right at the corner. Then cross the street.” Gabriela asks: “Is it far?” The woman says: “No, it is near. The hotel is next to a café, across from the station.” Gabriela says: “Thank you! Can you show me on the map?” Now Gabriela can find her hotel!

B Turn right
C Turn left

C The text does not say.
Where is the hotel?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

Imperatives for directions / Imperativo para direcções

DirectionGo straight.Siga reto.
DirectionTurn left / right.Vire à esquerda / direita.
DirectionCross the street.Atravesse a rua.
Ask the wayHow do I get to the hotel?Como eu chego ao hotel?
Ask locationWhere is the station?Onde fica a estação?

Prepositions of place / Preposições de lugar

next toThe hotel is next to the café.ao lado de
across fromIt is across from the station.em frente / do outro lado
in front ofThe tower is in front of the park.na frente de
behindThe park is behind the museum.atrás de
nearIt is near.perto
far fromIt is far from here.longe de
  • Direcção = verbo primeiro, sem “you”: Go straight (nunca “You go straight” ao dar ordem).
  • Sem “to”: Go straight — NUNCA “Go to straight”.
  • Sempre in front OF / across FROM / far FROM — não esqueça a preposição.
