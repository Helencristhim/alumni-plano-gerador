Lesson 01 -- Pre-class

Who Is Eliane? -- Rebuilding Your Professional Voice

Describing who you are and what you do, in English again. Key words: specialize in, background, expertise, reproductive medicine, fertility, clinical, procedure, hands-on, track record, residency, peer-reviewed, fluent. Structure: present simple (who you are now), present perfect continuous (your ongoing career), and past simple (your training).

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

Eliane works as a gynecologist in São Paulo, and she specializes in reproductive medicine. Her background is in gynecology and clinical research, and she completed her residency in 2015. Since then, she has been working in fertility care, and over the years she has built a strong track record with her patients. "I spend most of my week in clinical practice, and I perform the same procedure almost every day," she says. She is a very hands-on doctor, and she still reads peer-reviewed articles to stay up to date. Right now, she is opening her own clinic with American technology. When she meets a new colleague, she asks: "What do you specialize in?"

1. Why do we say "she has been working in fertility care" and not "she works"?
A Because the action finished a long time ago.
B Because it started in the past and still continues now.
C Because it is a plan for the future.
2. "She completed her residency in 2015." Why do we use the past simple here?
A It is a finished action with a finished time (2015).
B It is still happening now.
C It is a general fact true every day.
3. Which question uses the correct structure to ask a colleague?
A "What you specialize in?"
B "What do you specialize in?"
C "What are you specialize in?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Three Tenses for Your Professional Story

GRAMMAR

Use the present simple for facts true now, the present perfect continuous for an ongoing career, and the past simple for finished training. / Use o present simple para fatos atuais, o present perfect continuous para a carreira em curso e o past simple para a formação já concluída.

FormUseExample
Present simpleA permanent fact true nowI work as a gynecologist. / I specialize in fertility.
have/has been + verb-ingA career that started in the past and continuesI have been working in this field for eight years.
... for / sincefor + period, since + start pointfor eight years / since 2016
Past simpleA finished action with a finished timeI completed my residency in 2015.
Present perfect simpleA result up to now: I have built a strong track record.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Use the present perfect for experience and achievements with no exact date, and the past simple for a finished event with a finished time. / Use o present perfect para experiência e conquistas sem data exata, e o past simple para um evento finalizado com tempo definido.

FormUseExample
Present perfect (have/has + past participle)Experience and achievements, no exact timeI have worked in this field. / I have presented at conferences.
... for / sincefor + a period, since + a start pointfor eight years / since 2016
Past simpleA finished event with a finished timeI did my fellowship in 2016. / I studied in Teresina.
Common errorNOT "I have worked there in 2015" → with a finished time, use the past simple: "I worked there in 2015."

Lesson 03 -- Pre-class

The Clinic Is Happening -- Plans, Timelines & Business Vision

Talking about the future of your clinic with the four future forms: "going to" for plans and intentions, the present continuous for arranged future ("I am meeting a supplier next month"), "will" for predictions, and "plan to / intend to / expect to" for formal business goals. New words: timeline, milestone, rollout, capital investment, seed funding, stakeholder, operational, scalable, state-of-the-art, facility, adjacent market, on track.

Timeline -- a plan that shows when each stage happens
"Our timeline shows the clinic opening in eighteen months."
Milestone -- an important point or achievement in a project
"Signing the lease was our first big milestone."
Rollout -- the step-by-step launch of a product or service

"The new facility will have four consulting rooms."
Adjacent market -- a related market you can expand into
"Genetic testing is an adjacent market for us."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Four Future Forms

GRAMMAR

English has four ways to talk about the future, and each sends a different message. / O inglês tem quatro formas de falar do futuro, e cada uma manda uma mensagem diferente.

FormUseExample
going to (be going to + verb)A plan or intention you have decidedI am going to open a clinic.
Present continuous (am/is/are + -ing)A fixed arrangement with a time or placeI am meeting a supplier next month.
will (+ base verb, no "to")A prediction or a spontaneous decisionThe market will grow. / I will send it now.
plan to / intend to / expect toFormal business goals (register): We plan to launch in 2027. NOT "The plan is be..." → "The plan is to be...".
