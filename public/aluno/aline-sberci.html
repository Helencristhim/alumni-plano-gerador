P1. Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Aline works at Novartis in São Paulo. She manages four technology products. Last year, she went to Ireland for a business trip. She has been to the United States twice. Right now, she is studying for the PMP certification. Her biggest challenge is becoming fluent in English, but she feels more confident every day.
1. Which sentence is about a ROUTINE (something Aline does regularly)?
A She went to Ireland last year.
B She manages four technology products.
C She has been to the United States twice.
2. Which sentence is about a PAST EVENT (finished, specific time)?
A She went to Ireland last year.
B She works at Novartis.
C She has been to the United States.
3. Which sentence is about a LIFE EXPERIENCE (no specific time)?
A She manages four products.
B She went to Ireland last year.
C She has been to the United States twice.

P2. Grammar Tip

Grammar

Simple Present = facts and routines NOW
"I work at Novartis." / "She manages four products."

Past Simple = finished actions, specific time in the PAST
"I went to Ireland in 2022." / "She studied English last year."

Present Perfect = life experiences, no specific time
"I have been to the US." / "She has worked abroad."

Common mistake: "I have been to Ireland in 2022." Use past simple with specific time: "I went to Ireland in 2022."

P2. Grammar Tip

Grammar

Adverbs of Frequency tell HOW OFTEN something happens:

always (100%) > usually (80%) > often (60%) > sometimes (40%) > rarely (10%) > never (0%)

Position:

BEFORE the main verb: "I usually check my emails."

AFTER the verb BE: "I am always busy on Mondays."

Common mistake: "I check usually my emails." The adverb goes BEFORE the verb: "I usually check my emails."

P1. Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Aline graduated from university in 2008. She started her career at a small technology company. She worked there for ten years and accomplished many goals. Then she decided to look for new opportunities. She applied for a position at a bigger company and they hired her. Three years later, she transferred to Novartis. They promoted her to project manager. Looking back, every step was important.
1. "She graduated from university in 2008." What type of verb is "graduated"?
A Regular past simple (base + ed)
B Irregular past simple
C Present perfect
2. Which word is an IRREGULAR past simple form?
A worked
B transferred
C was
3. "She decided to look for new opportunities." When did this happen?
A It is happening now.
B It happened in the past (finished action).
C It will happen in the future.

P2. Grammar Tip

Grammar

Past Simple Regular (+ed)

work → worked | start → started | graduate → graduated | apply → applied | transfer → transferred | hire → hired

Past Simple Irregular (change form)

go → went | have → had | make → made | get → got | become → became | leave → left | choose → chose | speak → spoke | is/are → was/were

Negative: did not (didn't) + BASE form

"I did not apply for the job." (NOT "I did not applied")

Question: Did + subject + BASE form?

"Did you graduate in 2008?" (NOT "Did you graduated")

Erro comum: "I did not applied." Depois de did/did not, SEMPRE use a forma base: "I did not apply."
