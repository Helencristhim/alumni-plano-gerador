Chapter 3: Real Promises

If, When & As Soon As

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Find the Pattern

"If I get there early, I 'll call you."

"As soon as I land, I 'll text you."

"I won't leave until I hear back from you."

"Once we set a date, I 'll book the tickets."

Part 1: condition / timePart 2: result
if / when / as soon as / once / until / before / after + PRESENTwill (or won't) + base verb
As soon as I land (present)I 'll text you (will)

The future idea is in BOTH halves, but only the result clause uses will. After if / when / as soon as / once / until we use the present. When the time clause comes first, add a comma: When you arrive, call me.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

As soon as I will land, I'll text you.
As soon as I land, I'll text you.

After when / as soon as / once / until / if, use the present -- never will.

If she will come to Brazil, we'll catch up.
If she comes to Brazil, we'll catch up.

The will belongs only in the result clause, not after if.

Read for the Main Idea

Keeping the Bond Alive

How travelers keep the bond alive

Every good trip leaves you with a handful of people you genuinely do not want to lose. You share a long dinner, a delayed train or a sunrise hike, and suddenly a stranger feels like an old friend. The hard part is not meeting them; it is staying in touch once you are back home and life takes over. Many of us promise to keep in touch and then, slowly and silently, we drift apart.

The travelers who manage to reconnect tend to do one simple thing: they turn a vague promise into a concrete plan. Instead of a weak "let's stay in touch", they say something specific. "If I make it to Lisbon next spring, I'll let you know." "As soon as I land, I'll drop you a line." "Once things calm down at work, I'll reach out and we'll have a proper catch-up." Notice the pattern: a small condition or time clause, then a clear promise. The plan does the remembering for you.

It also helps to lower the stakes. You do not need a grand reunion to reconnect; a quick message to check in on someone, a photo, or a short voice note keeps the bond warm. Make a little effort every few weeks and you will rarely fall out of touch. The friends you keep from your travels are not the ones you met by chance; they are the ones you chose, on purpose, to look up again.
