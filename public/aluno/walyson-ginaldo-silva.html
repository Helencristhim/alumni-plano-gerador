Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo verbal. / When to use each tense.

Tense / TempoUse / UsoExample / Exemplo
Present SimpleRotinas, fatos permanentes / Routines, permanent factsWalyson works at Sucafina.
Present Simple (3rd person)He/she/it + verbo com -s / adds -sHe reports to the director.
Present PerfectExperiência acumulada, conexão passado-presente / Accumulated experienceI have worked with coffee for years.
Present Perfect (structure)have/has + past participleThe industry has grown a lot.
Diferença-chave / Key differencePresent simple = o que você FAZ sempre ("I work"). Present perfect = o que você já FEZ até agora ("I have worked for years").

My Workday at Sucafina -- Daily Routines and Adverbs of Frequency

Present Simple for routines + adverbs of frequency (always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never) and their position in the sentence. New vocabulary: schedule, commute, shift, deadline, spreadsheet, shipment, warehouse, inventory, forecast, batch, catch up on, wrap up.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Schedule -- a plan that lists the times of your tasks and meetings
"I check my schedule every morning before the market opens." (Agenda / cronograma)
Commute -- the regular trip between your home and your workplace
"My commute to the office takes about forty minutes." (Trajeto casa-trabalho)
Shift -- a fixed period of working hours
"I always start my shift with a strong coffee." (Turno)
Deadline -- the latest time by which a task must be finished
"There is always a tight deadline at the end of the month." (Prazo)
Spreadsheet -- a digital sheet of rows and columns for numbers
"I open the spreadsheet to check the weekend numbers." (Planilha)
Shipment -- a load of goods sent together to a destination
"Three shipments leave the warehouse on Wednesday." (Carregamento / remessa)
Warehouse -- a large building where goods are stored
"We keep the green coffee in the warehouse near the port." (Armazém / galpão)
Inventory -- the full amount of stock a company holds
"I review the inventory before the market opens." (Estoque / inventário)
Forecast -- a prediction of future numbers or events
"I check the price forecast around noon." (Previsão)
Batch -- a quantity prepared or handled at one time
"I prepare the next batch for export." (Lote)
Catch up on -- to do work that you are behind on
"I usually catch up on my emails after lunch." (Colocar em dia)
Wrap up -- to finish or complete something
"I always wrap up the day with a short summary." (Finalizar / encerrar)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Adverbs of Frequency

GRAMMAR

Usados para dizer com que frequência algo acontece. / Used to say how often something happens.

Rule / RegraDetail / DetalheExample / Exemplo
Before the main verbAntes do verbo principalI always check the schedule.
After the verb "to be"Depois do verbo to beI am usually early.
Frequency scalealways 100% · usually 90% · often 70% · sometimes 50% · rarely 10% · never 0%I sometimes join the call.
NegativesCom "do not / does not" (não use never com not)He does not usually work late.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o advérbio de frequência vem antes do verbo principal, mas depois do verbo to be. Nunca "I go always" -- diga "I always go".

Aula 03 -- Pre-class

The Coffee Supply Chain -- Passive Voice (Origin to Cup)

The passive voice (to be + past participle) to describe a process: the coffee is grown, the beans are roasted. New vocabulary: harvest, grower, ripe, mill, grade, roast, blend, origin, export, supply chain, brew, trace.
0%
▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Harvest -- to pick the ripe coffee cherries from the plants
"The cherries are harvested by hand between May and September." (Colher / colheita)
Grower -- a farmer or company that grows the coffee
"Each lot is traced back to a single grower." (Produtor / cafeicultor)
Ripe -- fully developed and ready to be picked
"The fruit is picked only when it is ripe." (Maduro)
Mill -- to process the cherries and remove the outer fruit
"At the mill, the beans are dried and cleaned." (Beneficiar)
Grade -- to sort the beans by size and quality
"The beans are graded before they are exported." (Classificar)
Roast -- to heat the green beans until they turn brown
"The beans are roasted in small batches." (Torrar)
Blend -- a mixture of beans from different origins
"Each blend is documented before it is roasted." (Blend / mistura)
Origin -- the place or country where the coffee is grown
"This origin is known for a sweet, balanced cup." (Origem)
Export -- to send goods to another country to be sold
"The best lots are exported from the port of Santos." (Exportar)
Supply chain -- the whole path a product follows from producer to consumer
"He walks the buyer through the supply chain." (Cadeia de suprimentos)
Brew -- to make coffee by passing hot water through the ground beans
"A small sample is brewed for the buyers." (Coar / preparar o café)
Trace -- to follow a product back to its source
"Every step is traced from the farm to the port." (Rastrear)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Usada para focar na ação, não em quem a faz. / Used to focus on the action, not on the doer.

Rule / RegraDetail / DetalheExample / Exemplo
am / is / are + past participleForma do passivo no presenteCoffee is grown at high altitude.
Agent with "by"O agente é opcional, introduzido por "by"The cherries are harvested by hand.
Focus on the actionUse quando importa a ação, não quem fazThe beans are roasted and ground.
Irregular participlesgrow→grown, take→taken, send→sentThe cherries are taken to the mill.

Key point / Ponto-chave: o passivo usa to be + past participle. Nunca "the beans are roast" -- diga "the beans are roasted".
