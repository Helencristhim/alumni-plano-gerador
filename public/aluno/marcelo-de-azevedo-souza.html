Aula 01 -- Pre-class

Who Is Marcelo? -- Diagnostic & Real-Life English

Reactivating forty years of English through travel and real conversation. Key words: to strike up, small talk, to be into, worldly, off the beaten path, a turning point, to be blown away, a kindred spirit, to hit it off, to bond over, for hours on end, down to earth. Structures: present perfect for life experience vs present simple for who you are now vs past simple for finished moments.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To strike up -- to start a conversation with someone you do not know (puxar conversa)
"I struck up a conversation with a cyclist from Albania."
Small talk -- light, informal conversation about everyday things (papo casual)
"We began with small talk about the weather and the roads."
To be into -- to be genuinely interested in or fond of something (curtir, ser fã de)
"I am really into soul music from the sixties."
Worldly -- experienced and knowledgeable about life and the world (vivido, de mundo)
"After forty years of travel, he is a worldly man."
Off the beaten path -- far from popular, touristy places (fora do circuito turístico)
"I love cycling in places off the beaten path."
A turning point -- a moment when an important change happens (ponto de virada)
"That first long trip was a turning point in my life."
To be blown away -- to be amazed or deeply impressed (ficar impressionado)
"I was blown away by the mountains in Morocco."
A kindred spirit -- a person who shares your feelings and interests (alma gêmea, espírito afim)
"On the trail, I met a kindred spirit who loves adventure."
To hit it off -- to like someone immediately and get along well (dar-se bem logo de cara)
"We hit it off right away and talked for hours."
To bond over -- to build a close connection through a shared interest (criar laço por algo em comum)
"We bonded over our love of long-distance cycling."
For hours on end -- continuously, for a very long time (por horas a fio)
"We talked for hours on end about travel and life."
Down to earth -- practical and friendly, not arrogant (pé no chão, sem frescura)
"Even very successful people can be wonderfully down to earth."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Present Simple vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Como falar de si mesmo com o tempo verbal certo (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experiência de vida, sem tempo específico. Life experience up to now (ever / never / since).I have cycled through Morocco.
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he/she + s)		Quem você é agora, papel e rotina. Who you are now, role and routine.I run operations for a pharma company.

ever / never / sinceHave you ever...? · I have never... · since + ponto de partida (since I was young)

The Story of Me -- Your Past, Your Languages, Your Life

Telling your life story in languages. Key words: to pick up, immerse yourself in, give up, come back to, resume, stick with it, move abroad, settle in, adapt to, fit in, stand out, blend in. Structures: past simple (finished moments) vs present perfect (experience and duration up to now, for/since/ago).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada expressão e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To pick up -- to learn something informally, by doing or using it (aprender na prática)
"I picked up French by living it, not by studying it."
To immerse yourself in -- to surround yourself completely with something (mergulhar de cabeça em)
"I immersed myself in French for eighteen years."
To give up -- to stop doing something before it is finished (desistir)
"I started English many times, and I gave it up many times."
To come back to -- to return to something after a break (voltar a algo)
"I always come back to English, no matter what."
To resume -- to start again after stopping (retomar)
"After a long break, I decided to resume my studies."
To stick with it -- to keep going despite difficulty (persistir, não largar)
"Learning is hard, but you have to stick with it."
To move abroad -- to go and live in another country (mudar-se para o exterior)
"Many of my friends moved abroad to learn a language."
To settle in -- to get used to a new place and feel at home (se ambientar)
"It took me a week to settle in when I traveled."
To adapt to -- to change so you fit a new situation (se adaptar a)
"A good traveler adapts to any culture."
To fit in -- to feel accepted as part of a group (se enturmar)
"I want to fit in and talk to everyone I meet."
To stand out -- to be noticed as different or special (se destacar)
"My accent makes me stand out a little."
To blend in -- to look or seem like everyone around you (se misturar)
"After a week abroad, I blend in like a local."

What Do You Do? -- Your Professional World in Natural English

Describing your work the way a native speaker would. Key words: to oversee, to ensure, to coordinate, to liaise with, to be in charge of, to deal with, to handle, to report to, supply chain, cold chain, last-mile delivery, time-sensitive. Structures: present simple for facts, roles and routines vs present continuous for projects in progress right now.
0%
▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

To oversee -- to be in charge of work and watch over it (supervisionar)
"I oversee operations for a pharmaceutical logistics company."
To ensure -- to make certain that something happens (garantir)
"We ensure that every medicine arrives safely and on time."
To coordinate -- to organize people and tasks to work together (coordenar)
"I coordinate teams in different cities every single day."
To liaise with -- to communicate and cooperate with a partner or team (fazer a ponte com)
"I liaise with suppliers and drivers across the country."
To be in charge of -- to have control and responsibility for something (ser responsavel por)
"I am in charge of the whole distribution network."
To deal with -- to take action on a task or a problem (lidar com)
"My team deals with delays before they become problems."
To handle -- to manage or take care of a task (dar conta de, cuidar de)
"One team handles customs, another handles the routes."
To report to -- to answer to a boss or a manager (se reportar a)
"Four managers report to me, and I report to the CEO."
Supply chain -- the whole path of a product from source to customer (cadeia de suprimentos)
"A supply chain has hundreds of moving parts."
Cold chain -- temperature-controlled delivery for sensitive products (cadeia do frio)
"The cold chain keeps vaccines at the right temperature."
Last-mile delivery -- the final step of delivery, to the customer's door (entrega de ultima milha)
"Last-mile delivery is the hardest and most expensive part."
Time-sensitive -- needing to be done within a strict time limit (sensivel ao tempo, com prazo apertado)
"Medicine is time-sensitive; a delay can ruin the product."

GRAMMAR

Como falar do seu trabalho com o tempo verbal certo (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Simple

Talking About Passion -- Cycling, Adventure and the Stories That Define You

Reading and telling adventure stories. Key words: thrill, endurance, solitude, rewarding, demanding, liberating, exhilarating, off-road, steep climb, rough terrain, scenic route, to pace yourself. Structures: gerunds (-ing) and infinitives (to ...) in opinion and preference (love riding, tend to avoid, find it hard to explain).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Thrill -- a sudden strong feeling of excitement (emoção intensa)
"The long descent gave me a real thrill."
Endurance -- the ability to keep going through effort or pain (resistência)
"Long climbs really test your endurance."
Solitude -- the peaceful state of being alone (solidão tranquila)
"I ride for the solitude of the open road."
Rewarding -- giving a satisfying sense of achievement (gratificante)
"Reaching the summit is incredibly rewarding."
Demanding -- needing a lot of effort and skill (exigente)
"The route was steep and very demanding."
Liberating -- making you feel free (libertador)
"Cycling alone is a liberating feeling."
Exhilarating -- making you feel very excited and alive (eletrizante)
"The mountain descent was absolutely exhilarating."
Off-road -- away from paved roads, on rough ground (fora de estrada)
"We took an off-road trail deep into the hills."
Steep climb -- a hard, sharp uphill section (subida íngreme)
"A steep climb was waiting for us after lunch."
Rough terrain -- hard, uneven ground (terreno acidentado)
"Rough terrain slows you down, but it feels wild."
Scenic route -- a longer road chosen for its beautiful views (rota panorâmica)
"We always take the scenic route, never the highway."
To pace yourself -- to control your effort so you don't tire too soon (dosar o próprio ritmo)
"On a long ride, you have to pace yourself."
