Chapter 3: The Polite Question

Ask It Politely

Could you tell me · Do you know · I wonder if

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"Could you tell me where the panel is?"

"Do you know when the meeting starts?"

"I wonder if you have any feedback."

"Could you tell me who the moderator is?"

Each sentence starts with a soft, orange opener. After it, the verb comes AFTER the subject, and there is no "do" or "does". What word do we add for a yes/no question?

OpenerUse it forExample
Could you tell mewh- questions...where the room is
Do you knowwh- questions...when it starts
I wonder ifyes/no questions...if you have feedback

After the opener, use subject + verb order (not "is the room"). Never use do or does. For a yes/no question, add if.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"Could you tell me where is the room?"
"Could you tell me where the room is?"
"Do you know when does it start?"
"Do you know when it starts?"
"I wonder you have any feedback."
"I wonder if you have any feedback."

After the opener, use subject + verb order, never do or does, and add if for a yes/no question.

Practice

Complete the Question

Detail

Say It Right

opener + subject + verb · add "if" for yes/no

"Could you tell me where the panel ___is?"
"Do you know when the meeting ___starts?"
"I wonder ___if the moderator has my inquiry."
"Could you ___confirm the time of the response?"
Chapter 4: Two Sides of the Table

The Q&A

A guest asks; a moderator answers

Before you listen

Listen for this

1. What is Peter's role?
2. When does Flavia's inquiry come?
3. What does the panel give after Flavia speaks?
Dialogue

Asking at the Panel

P
Welcome, Flavia. I am the moderator who facilitates today's panel.
F
Thank you, Peter. Could you tell me when my inquiry is scheduled?
P
Of course. Your inquiry comes right after the first response, at ten o'clock.
F
I see. So, if I understand correctly, I speak at ten. Do you know how long I have?
P
You have five minutes, and then the panel gives you feedback.
F
Thank you. I wonder if I can ask a follow-up after their feedback.
Comprehension

About Peter

1. What is Peter's role?
He is the moderator who facilitates the panel.
2. When does Flavia's inquiry come?
Right after the first response, at ten o'clock.
3. What does the panel give after Flavia speaks?
Feedback.

Listening 2

A Mentor on Listening

A mentor gives one tip on active listening. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What is active listening about?
Showing the other person that you understand.
2. What do you do to confirm an idea?

Better feedback, and people trust you.
