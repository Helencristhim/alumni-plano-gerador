Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional

GRAMMAR

Usado para falar de situações irreais no passado. / Used to talk about unreal past situations.

Form / FormaStructure / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Affirmative / AfirmativaIf + had + past participle, would have + past participleIf we had started earlier, we would have avoided the delay.
Negative / NegativaIf + had not + past participle, would not have + past participleIf the vendor had not sent the invoice late, we would not have missed the deadline.
Question / PerguntaWould + subject + have + past participle if + had + past participle?Would you have chosen a different carrier if you had known the freight cost?
Key point / Ponto-chaveO third conditional fala de um passado IRREAL. Não aconteceu. Serve para refletir: "O que TERIA acontecido se..."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Perfects

Usados para analisar, criticar ou deduzir sobre o passado. / Used to analyze, criticize, or deduce about the past.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
should have + p.p.Crítica ou arrependimento / Criticism or regretWe should have escalated the issue sooner.

Making Your Case -- Persuasive Language in Supplier Negotiations

New vocabulary: concession, counteroffer, value proposition, trade-off, bottom line, bargaining power, walk-away point, win-win, deadlock, incentive, rebate, common ground, ultimatum.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

Concession -- something you give up so the other side accepts a deal
"We made one concession on delivery to close the deal." (Concessão)
Counteroffer -- an offer made in response to another, with different terms
"Their price was high, so we sent a counteroffer." (Contraproposta)
Value proposition -- the combination of benefits that makes your offer worth choosing
"Our value proposition is faster delivery at a fair price." (Proposta de valor)
Trade-off -- a compromise where you accept less of one thing for more of another
"We accepted a longer term as a trade-off for a discount." (Compensação / troca)
Bottom line -- the limit you will not go below in a negotiation
"A two-year term is our bottom line." (Limite mínimo)
Bargaining power -- the strength that lets one side influence the terms of a deal
"A second supplier gives us more bargaining power." (Poder de barganha)
Walk-away point -- the point at which leaving is better than accepting the offer
"If they add a surcharge, that is our walk-away point." (Ponto de desistência)
Win-win -- a result in which both sides gain something they value
"A longer term at a fair price is a win-win." (Ganha-ganha)
Deadlock -- a situation where no agreement is reached because no one gives in
"The talks reached a deadlock over price." (Impasse)
Incentive -- something offered to encourage the other side to agree
"An early-payment incentive convinced the supplier." (Incentivo)
Rebate -- a partial refund returned after a condition is met
"They offered a rebate on orders above ten thousand units." (Abatimento)
Common ground -- the shared interests that both sides can build an agreement on
"We found common ground on delivery dates." (Pontos em comum)
Ultimatum -- a final condition that ends the talks if it is refused
"Avoid giving an ultimatum too early in a negotiation." (Ultimato)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Cleft Sentences

GRAMMAR

Usado para enfatizar exatamente o ponto que importa. / Used to emphasize exactly the point that matters.

Form / FormaMeaning / SentidoExample / Exemplo
It is/was + focus + that/whoIt-cleft: destaca UMA informação / It-cleft: emphasizes ONE thingIt is the price that worries us most.
What + clause + is/was + focusWh-cleft: destaca o que importa / Wh-cleft: highlights what mattersWhat we need is a firm commitment.
It is not X but Y that...Contraste: corrige e redireciona / Contrast: corrects and redirectsIt is not the term but the price that matters.
All + clause + is...Limita: faz o pedido parecer pequeno / Limiting: makes a demand sound smallAll we are asking for is a fair price.

Key point / Ponto-chave: no it-cleft o conector é that ou who, nunca "what". O wh-cleft começa com What e sempre usa verbo no singular: is ou was.
