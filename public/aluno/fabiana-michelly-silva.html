Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Perfects

Modal + have + past participle = falar sobre o passado com diferentes significados. / Modal + have + past participle = talk about the past with different meanings.

Modal PerfectMeaning / SignificadoExample / Exemplo
should have + ppO certo que NÃO aconteceu (crítica/arrependimento) / The right thing that did NOT happenWe should have flagged this earlier.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

A good chair relays the room before the vote. Marcus reminded everyone that the risk review had flagged the single-supplier exposure. Finance said the dual-supplier plan would cost more, but they advised that it would protect us against a default. Operations told the room they had drawn up a shortlist, and they recommended that the board ratify the top two vendors. Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed before the end of the month. Notice how each reported sentence shifts the tense back and drops the question mark. Nobody chose to veto, one colleague said she would abstain, and the rest signed off, so the room reached consensus.

1. The original was "The plan will cost more." Why does the report say "would cost"?
A After a past reporting verb, "will" shifts back to "would".
B "Would" expresses a polite request.
C "Would" and "will" are interchangeable.
2. Why is "whether" used in "Legal asked whether the contracts could be signed"?
A It introduces a wh- question.
B It reports a yes/no question, which uses if or whether.
C It shows the speaker is uncertain about the grammar.
3. Why is the base verb "ratify" used after "recommended that"?
A Recommend / suggest / propose that take the base verb for every subject.
B It is a typing error for "ratifies".
C The subject "board" is plural.
