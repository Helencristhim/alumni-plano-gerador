Chapter 3 -- How about / Why don't we

Inviting & Suggesting

How about + -ing & Why don't we + verb

Listen & Repeat

How about & Why don't we in Action

"How about meeting for coffee on Saturday?"

"Why don't we have a small get-together at my place?"

"How about inviting Daniel too?"

"Why don't we meet at the cafe near the square?"

Grammar Rule

Suggesting: How about / Why don't we

StructureExampleNote
How about + verb-ingHow about meeting for coffee?the verb takes -ing (meeting, going, having)
How about + nounHow about dinner on Friday?you can also use a noun after "how about"
Why don't we + base verbWhy don't we meet at the cafe?base verb: no -ing, no "to"
answers"That sounds great!" / "I'd love to, but..."accept warmly, or turn down politely

Key idea: Both phrases are friendly ways to suggest a plan together. After How about, use the verb with -ing ("How about meeting"), or a noun ("How about dinner"). After Why don't we, use the base verb with no -ing and no "to" ("Why don't we meet"). To answer, accept ("That sounds great!") or turn it down politely ("I'd love to, but I'm busy").

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"How about meet for coffee?" / "Why don't we to meet?"
"How about meeting for coffee?" / "Why don't we meet?"

After How about, the verb takes -ing (meeting). After Why don't we, use the base verb with no "to" (meet).

Accept or Turn Down

Your Turn -- Free

Plan a Get-together

Plan a full get-together. Invite a friend, suggest a day, time, and place, ask if they are available, and agree on the final plan as the host.

Use How about and Why don't we, and at least four social words such as invite, guest, occasion, and accept.
