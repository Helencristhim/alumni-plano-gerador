Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are permanent truths)
Present Continuous = actions happening now, temporary situations, current projects (actions in progress or temporary situations)

TenseStructureExample
Present Simple (+)Subject + verb (base/s)"She oversees public sector accounts."
Present Simple (-)Subject + do/does not + verb"She does not handle private sector deals."
Present Simple (?)Do/Does + subject + verb?"Does she report to the VP?"
Present Continuous (+)Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing"She is driving a transformation project."
Present Continuous (-)Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing"She is not working on that project anymore."

Common Mistake:

"I am work at Microsoft."

"I work at Microsoft." (permanent fact = Present Simple)

Use Present Simple for permanent facts. Use Present Continuous (am/is/are + -ing) only for actions in progress or temporary situations.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Routines = actions that happen regularly, habits, general facts
Adverbs of Frequency = words that indicate HOW OFTEN something happens

AdverbFrequencyExample
always100%"I always check my schedule."
usually80%"I usually attend the 9 AM meeting."
often60%"She often briefs her team."
sometimes40%"He sometimes works late."
rarely10%"She rarely skips meetings."
never0%"I never leave without wrapping up."

Position Rule:

Before the main verb: "I always review my emails."

After the verb BE: "She is usually on time."

Before the main verb. After the verb BE (am/is/are).

Common Mistake:

"I check always my email."

"I always check my email."

The frequency adverb comes BEFORE the main verb, never after.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Past Simple = completed actions in the past with a specific time reference (completed actions in the past with a specific time reference)

TypeStructureExample
Regular (+)Subject + verb-ed"I relocated to São Paulo."
Irregular (+)Subject + irregular past form"Microsoft gave me the opportunity."
Negative (-)Subject + did not + base verb"I did not expect to grow so fast."
Question (?)Did + subject + base verb?"Did you work in Rio before?"

Common irregular verbs: was/were, had, went, came, took, made, got, left, gave, led

Common Mistake:

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Continuous = actions happening RIGHT NOW or currently in progress

TypeStructureExample
Affirmative (+)Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing"I am working on a proposal."
Negative (-)Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing"We are not tracking that yet."
Question (?)Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?"Are you rolling out the new system?"

Present Perfect = actions that started in the past with results or relevance NOW

TypeStructureExample
Affirmative (+)Subject + have/has + past participle"I have allocated the budget."
Negative (-)Subject + have/has + not + past participle"We have not finished the review."
Question (?)Have/Has + subject + past participle?"Have you completed the sprint?"

Signal words: Present Continuous = now, currently, right now, at the moment, this week. Present Perfect = already, yet, so far, just, ever, never, recently.

Common Mistake:

"I am work on a new proposal right now."

"I am working on a new proposal right now." (Present Continuous = am/is/are + verb-ING)

Common Mistake:

"We have complete three sprints so far."

"We have completed three sprints so far." (Present Perfect = have/has + PAST PARTICIPLE)
