Grammar Rule

Imperatives + Prepositions

ToolHow it works
Imperative (Go, Turn, Take, Cross, Keep)Base verb, no subject -- used to give a direction or instruction
Negative imperative (Don't turn, Don't cross)Use "don't" + base verb to tell someone NOT to do something
Prepositions of place (next to, opposite, between, in front of, on the corner of)Show the position of a place in relation to another

Key idea: directions are short commands. Start with the verb (Go, Turn, Cross) and add a preposition of place (next to, opposite, on the corner of) to say exactly where. We do not add "to" after turn: say "Turn left," never "Turn to left."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Turn to the left and the shop is in front the square."
"Turn left and the shop is in front of the square."

Say turn left / turn right, never "turn to the left." And it is always in front of, never "in front the."

Dialogue

On the Street

T

"Excuse me, I'm a bit lost. Could you tell me how to get to the specialty coffee shop?"

M

"Of course. Go straight ahead for two blocks until you reach the big avenue. Then turn left at the traffic light."

T

"Got it. So I cross at the crosswalk after the traffic light. Is the shop far from there?"

M

"No, it's close. Walk past the square and keep going. The shop is on the corner, next to a bookstore and opposite the old church."

T

"Perfect. The old church is my landmark. And if I reach the roundabout?"

M

"Then you've gone too far, so just turn around. Don't worry, you can't miss it. Enjoy your coffee!"
