GRAMMAR

How to talk about imaginary past situations and their imaginary results.

PartStructureExample

How to express judgments, certainty, and speculation about past events.

ModalStructureMeaningExample
should haveshould + have + past participleCriticism or regret: it was the right thing but did NOT happen"They should have aligned the team."
must havemust + have + past participleHigh certainty (90%+): I am almost sure this happened"The delay must have affected Q4."

Key rule: After modal + HAVE, always use the PAST PARTICIPLE. Never add "to" (should have to align) and never use the base form (must have affect).
