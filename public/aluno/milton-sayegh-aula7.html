Vocabulary

Stage 1.1: Key Financial Vocabulary

Click on each card to listen and learn. These 10 words are essential for presenting financial results.

Quarter

A three-month period in a company's financial year (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4).
"Our profit increased in the second quarter."

Profit

The money a company keeps after paying all its costs.
"Our profit was higher than last year."

Growth

An increase in size, value, or amount over time.
"We achieved strong growth in the US market."

Forecast

A prediction of future figures or results.
"The forecast for the next quarter is positive."

Figure

A number that represents data or an amount.
"These figures show our best year ever."

Turnover

The total amount of sales in a period.
"Our annual turnover reached twelve million dollars."

Decline

A decrease or fall in figures.
"There was a small decline in European sales."

Exchange Rate

The value of one currency compared to another.
"A better exchange rate increased our profit."

Bottom Line

The final profit figure, after all costs.
"The bottom line is stronger than last year."

Chart

A visual graphic that shows data or numbers.
"This chart compares our four markets."

Grammar

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip - Comparatives & Superlatives

Use comparatives to compare two things, and superlatives to identify the top one in a group.

FormRuleExample
Comparative - shortadjective + -er than"higher than last year", "lower than Q1"
Comparative - longmore + adjective + than"more profitable than Europe"
Superlative - shortthe + adjective + -est"the highest", "the lowest figure"
Superlative - longthe most + adjective"the most profitable market"
Irregulargood → better → best; bad → worse → worst"better than last year", "the best quarter"

Numbers tip: "rose by 18%", "grew from 1.8 to 2.4 million", "a 15 percent increase". Say "point" for decimals: 2.4 = "two point four".

A "The US market is the most profitable."
