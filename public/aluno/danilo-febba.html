Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Quando usar o present simple para falar do seu trabalho e da sua rotina.

FormUse (PT)Example
I / You / We + verbFatos e rotinas (sem -s)I work at a tech company.
He / She / It + verb+s3ª pessoa sempre recebe -sShe manages the team.
am / is / are (to be)Profissão, nome, lugar, idadeI am a data engineer.
do not / does not + verbNegativoI do not work in sales.

Atenção (erro comum): Não use "am/is/are" junto com outro verbo no present simple. Diga "I work" (não "I am work"). E lembre do -s: "She works" (não "She work").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There is / There are

GRAMMAR

Como dizer o que existe em um lugar e onde as coisas estão.

FormUse (PT)Example
There is + singularExiste UMA coisa (há / tem)There is a laptop on the desk.
There are + pluralExistem DUAS+ coisasThere are two screens here.
There isn't / There aren'tNegativo (não há)There isn't a printer here.
Is there...? / Are there...?Pergunta (há...?)Is there a meeting room?

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple (Regular Verbs)

GRAMMAR

Como falar sobre ações terminadas no passado com verbos regulares (-ed).

FormUse (PT)Example
verbo + -edAfirmativo: ação terminada no passado (mesma forma para todos)I finished the report.
did not / didn't + verbo baseNegativo (o verbo volta à forma base, sem -ed)I did not finish it.
Did + sujeito + verbo base?Pergunta (verbo base, sem -ed)Did you finish?
-ed = /t/, /d/, /id/Pronúncia do -ed: fixed /t/, planned /d/, tested /id/

Atenção (erro comum): Depois de "did/didn't", o verbo NUNCA leva -ed. Diga "Did you finish?" (não "Did you finished?"). E não use "am/is/are" com o passado: diga "I finished the task" (não "I am finished the task").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Irregular Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Como usar o past simple com verbos irregulares, was/were e did.

FormUse (PT)Example
Irregular verb (no -ed)Muda de forma: go→went, have→had, make→madeI went to the office.
More irregularsfind→found, get→got, write→wrote, take→took, meet→metWe found the error.
was / were (to be)I/he/she/it = was; you/we/they = wereI was late. They were ready.
did not (didn't) + base verbNegativo (forma base, sem mudar)I didn't go home.
did + you + base verbPergunta: Did you go? / What did you do?

Atenção (erro comum): Verbos irregulares NÃO recebem -ed. Diga "I went" (não "I goed"). Depois de did, use a forma base: "Did you go?" (não "Did you went?"). E com we/you/they use were: "We were busy" (não "We was busy").
