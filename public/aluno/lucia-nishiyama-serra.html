Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

How to say what is always true about your career and what you are working on right now.

FormUseExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb (he / she + -s)		Facts, routines and permanent situations - always true.I work in AI security.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Right now, today, these days - temporary or unfinished.I am preparing for an interview.
Negativedo not / does not + verb · am not / is not / are not + -ingI do not read from a script. / She is not leading that team now.
QuestionDo / Does + subject + verb · Am / Is / Are + subject + -ingDo you lead a team? / Are you preparing for interviews?
State verbslike, love, want, need, know, believe, understand almost never take -ing: "I want a confident voice" (not "I am wanting").
Time wordsevery day, always, usually, never → simple · right now, today, this year, these days → continuous

Telling the story of a long career with the present perfect. Key words: tenure, milestone, to spearhead, pioneer, to transition, to accomplish, to navigate, legacy, transformation, to take on, to move on, track record. Structure: present perfect (have / has + past participle) for experience, recent news (just, already, yet) and duration (for, since).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

How to talk about your experience, your recent news, and how long you have done something.

FormUseExample
Affirmative
have / has + past participle		Experience in your life so far, with no exact date.I have led teams on three continents.
Negative
have not / has not + participle		Something that has not happened up to now (often with "yet").I have not finished my mission yet.
Question
Have / Has + subject + participle		Ask about experience (often with "ever").Have you ever led a global team?
Recent news
just / already / yet		Something that happened very recently and still matters.I have just moved into AI safety.
Duration
for / since		for + a period (for ten years) · since + a point in time (since 2010). A finished past time takes the past simple: "I led that team in 2015."

Lesson 03 -- Pre-class

Past Simple vs. Present Perfect in Job Interviews -- Choosing the Right Tense

Framing your career in an interview: past simple for what you did (with a date), present perfect for why it still matters today. Key words: to implement, to deploy, accountability, ownership, scope, stakeholder, outcome, to carry over, relevant, to frame. Structure: past simple (finished time -- in 2015, last quarter) vs. present perfect (since, so far, throughout my career).
0%
▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Listen to each word and read the example. Tap Listen to hear it.

To implement -- to put a plan, system, or decision into real action
"In 2015, I implemented a new security policy across the whole company."
To deploy -- to put a solution, tool, or team into active use
"We deployed the new framework across four countries last year."
Accountability -- being responsible and answerable for the results of your work
"As a leader, I take full accountability for every outcome."
Ownership -- the feeling of full personal responsibility for a result
"I have taken ownership of every project I lead."
Scope -- the size, range, and limits of a project or a role
"The scope of my role grew larger every single year."
Stakeholder -- a person or group with an interest in a project or a decision
"I aligned every stakeholder before we launched the program."
Outcome -- the final result that an effort or decision produces
"We delivered the outcome the board asked for, on time."
To carry over -- to bring a skill or lesson from one role into the next one
"The tools change, but my judgment carries over to every job."
Relevant -- clearly connected to, and useful for, the present moment
"I show why my past experience is still relevant today."
To frame -- to present something in a particular way, to shape how it is seen
"I frame my story so it points to the future, not just the past."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions.

In an interview, Lucia frames her story with two tenses. First come the facts. "In 2015, I joined a large company as a security lead. That year, I implemented a new policy and deployed a framework across four countries. We delivered every outcome on time." These are finished events with a clear date, so she uses the past simple. Then she builds the bridge to now. "Since that project, I have owned every rollout I touch. I have carried that accountability into every role, and I have aligned stakeholders my whole career." There is no exact date here, only experience that is still relevant today, so she uses the present perfect. The past is her archive; the present perfect is her argument.

1. Why does the text say "In 2015, I joined" and not "I have joined"?
A It is a finished event with a clear past date, so it takes the past simple.
B It is experience with no date, so it takes the present perfect.
C "Joined" and "have joined" mean exactly the same thing.
2. Why does she say "Since that project, I have owned every rollout"?
A "Since" points to a single finished day, so it needs the past simple.
B "Since" marks a starting point for something still true now, so it needs the present perfect.
C "Since" is only used with the past simple.
3. The text says "the past is her archive; the present perfect is her argument." What does this mean?
A The past simple is more important than the present perfect.
B You should only ever use one tense in an interview.
C The past simple states the facts; the present perfect shows why they still matter now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple vs. Present Perfect

GRAMMAR

The same career, two tenses. Choose the one that frames your story the way you want.

TenseUseExample
Past Simple
verb + -ed / irregular		A finished action with a clear past time -- the fact, the archive.I led that team in 2015.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experience or a result that still matters now, with no exact date.I have led teams ever since.
Past signal wordsin 2015 · last quarter · that year · when I was at Amazon → past simple
Present perfect signal wordsso far · up to now · since · throughout my career · ever / never → present perfect
