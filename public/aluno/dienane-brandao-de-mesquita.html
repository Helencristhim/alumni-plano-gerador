GRAMMAR Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Present Simple (Affirmative)

SubjectVerbExemplo
I / You / We / Theyverb (base form)I wake up at 5. / Eu acordo às 5.
He / She / Itverb + sShe wakes up at 5. / Ela acorda às 5.
Special cases:
• have → has: She has breakfast. (NOT "She haves")
• go → goes: He goes to work. (NOT "He gos")
• do → does: She does exercise. (NOT "She dos")
Importante: Em português, o verbo não muda na terceira pessoa. Em inglês, SIM: I wake → She wakeS.

GRAMMAR Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Articles a/an/some

Article Rule / Regra Examples
a Before consonant sounds / Antes de som de consoante a coffee, a menu, a sandwich
an Before vowel sounds / Antes de som de vogal an apple, an orange, an egg
some Uncountable or plural / Incontável ou plural some water, some bread, some rice
Polite requests: “Can I have a/an/some...?” / “I would like a/an/some...”
Nota: Em português não temos essa diferença. “Um café” = “a coffee”. “Uma laranja” = “an orange” (som de vogal!). “Água” = “some water” (incontável).

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- As horas em inglês

GRAMMAR

Duas formas de dizer as horas — as duas estão certas! / Two ways to tell the time — both correct!

RelógioClássico / ClassicFácil / Easy
9:00nine o'clocknine a.m.
9:30half past nine (e meia)nine thirty
9:15a quarter past nine (e quinze)nine fifteen
9:45a quarter to ten (quinze para as dez)nine forty-five

Pergunta: What time is it? (Que horas são?) — Dias usam on (on Thursday), horas usam at (at nine o'clock).
