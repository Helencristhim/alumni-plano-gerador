GRAMMAR Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Articles a/an/some

Article Rule / Regra Examples
a Before consonant sounds / Antes de som de consoante a coffee, a menu, a sandwich
an Before vowel sounds / Antes de som de vogal an apple, an orange, an egg
some Uncountable or plural / Incontável ou plural some water, some bread, some rice
Polite requests: “Can I have a/an/some...?” / “I would like a/an/some...”
Nota: Em português não temos essa diferença. “Um café” = “a coffee”. “Uma laranja” = “an orange” (som de vogal!). “Água” = “some water” (incontável).

Lesson 19 -- At the Restaurant

Series
Extra English -- Episode 6 (eating out)

A simple comedy made for English learners. The friends go to a restaurant and order in slow, clear English -- Could I have the menu?, Could I have the bill, please? Perfect for A1 listening about ordering politely.

Watch with English subtitles. Write down every sentence with "Could I have" that you hear. Then say out loud: "Could I have the menu, please?" and "Could I have the bill, please?".

Podcast
Coffee Break English -- "At the Restaurant"

A short, friendly lesson on ordering food: how to use "Could I have...?" to ask politely, like "Could I have the fish dish?" and "Could I have the bill, please?". The hosts explain the structure slowly, with many examples you can repeat.

Pause after each example and repeat it out loud. Then order a full meal: a dish, a dessert, and ask for the bill.
