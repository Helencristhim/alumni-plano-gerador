Chapter 3: Then and Now

2023 · and This Time

One trip is closed. The other one is still open. English hears the difference.

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"When I went to New York in 2023, I took a cab everywhere."

"This time I've already used the subway twice."

"I've never eaten at an American diner."

"Last night we walked three blocks and hailed a cab on Lexington."

Two sentences are blue, two are purple. What do the blue ones have that the purple ones do not?

TenseWhen you use itThe words that give it away
Past simple
went · took · walked		A finished moment. The trip is closed. You say when.in 2023 · last night · yesterday · two years ago
Present perfect
have used · have eaten		The trip is still open, or the experience is your whole life. The moment does not matter.already · never · ever · so far · this time · twice
The testAsk yourself one question: can this still change today? If yes → present perfect. If it is closed and dated → past simple.

2023 is closed. September is still open.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I have gone to New York in 2023."
"I went to New York in 2023."
"I have never took the subway at rush hour."
"I have never taken the subway at rush hour."

Say a date and English switches to the past simple. And after have, the verb changes shape: taken, not took — gone, not went — eaten, not ate.

How It Works

The Tip Is Not Optional

In the United States, the service is not in the price. The tip is how the person in front of you gets paid.

WhereHow muchWhat you say
Restaurant / diner18–20% of the subtotal"Is the tip included?"
Taxi15–20%, on the screen"Do I add it on the screen?"
Hotel, per bag$1–$2 in cash"Thank you — here you go."
StoreNothing — but sales tax is added"Is that with sales tax?"

Now do the math out loud: twenty percent of the $24.00 check is how much?

Chapter 6: Your Turn

The Cab, the Diner, the Store

Guided > Semi-free > Free

Dialogue

Fifth Avenue, Rush Hour

M
Hop in. Where are we headed?
L
The Lucerne Hotel, 79th and Amsterdam, please. Roughly how much is the fare from here?
M
From here? Thirty, thirty-two, depending on the traffic. It's rush hour, so the West Side is going to be slow.
L
Sorry, it's loud out here. Let me read that back to you: about thirty-two dollars, and it's slow because of rush hour. Is that right?
M
That's right. Honestly, if you're in a hurry, the subway is faster. The 1 line runs right up to 79th Street, and it's three blocks from your hotel.
L
I've never taken the subway here. When I came in 2023, I took a cab everywhere. But this time I've already promised myself I'd figure out the map.
M
Tonight, take the cab. You've got the bags. Card or cash? And do you want a receipt?
L
Card, please, and yes, a receipt — I need it for the company. Is the tip included, or do I add it on the screen?
M
You add it on the screen. Whatever you think is fair. Most people go fifteen or twenty percent.
L
Then let's say twenty. Thank you, Marcus — and tomorrow I'll try the 1 line.
Comprehension

What Did Marcus Say?

1. How much does Marcus say the fare will be, and why might it be slow?
About thirty to thirty-two dollars — it is rush hour, so the West Side is going to be slow.
2. Which subway line does he recommend, and where does it stop?
The 1 line — it runs right up to 79th Street, three blocks from the hotel.
3. Why does he say to take the cab tonight anyway?
Because she has the bags.
4. What does he say about the tip?
It is not included — you add it on the screen, whatever you think is fair. Most people give fifteen or twenty percent.
