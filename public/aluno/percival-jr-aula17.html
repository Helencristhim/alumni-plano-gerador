Chapter 3 -- Apologize & Offer

Apologize, Then Offer

Say sorry, then give a clear solution

Grammar Notice

Notice the Patterns

Which phrase apologizes, and which one offers a solution?

"We apologize for the late delivery."

"We are sorry for the inconvenience."

"We will send a replacement part this week."

"We can offer a replacement, free of charge."

Grammar Rule

Apologize & Offer a Solution

Use it forPatternExample
ApologizeWe apologize for + nounWe apologize for the delay.
Apologize (softer)We are sorry for + nounWe are sorry for the inconvenience.
Promise an actionWe will + base verbWe will resolve this by Friday.
Offer a solutionWe can offer + nounWe can offer a replacement part.

Key idea: use We apologize for + a noun to say sorry (We apologize for the delay), and We will + the base verb (We will send a replacement) or We can offer + a noun to give the solution.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We apologize about the delay. We will to send a replacement."
"We apologize for the delay. We will send a replacement."

We write apologize for + a noun (not "apologize about"), and after will we use the base verb: send, resolve, offer -- no "to".

Artefact

The Reply Email

From: Percival JR -- EGEA Saneamento

To: Mr. Costa   |   CC: Karen

Subject: Re: Your Order -- Late Delivery & Damaged Part

Dear Mr. Costa,

Thank you for your email. We acknowledge your complaint about the late delivery and the damaged part.

We apologize for the late delivery, and we are sorry for the inconvenience this caused your company.

We will send a replacement part by express delivery, free of charge, and we will resolve the problem this week. We will not ask for the damaged part back.

The new part will arrive within three working days. We will email you the tracking number as soon as it ships.

Best regards,
Percival JR

Dialogue

Checking the Reply

P

"Hi Karen, I wrote the reply to Mr. Costa's complaint. I start by saying we acknowledge his email. Can you check it?"

K

"Good start. Do you apologize for the problem? The customer needs to feel we are sorry."

P

"Yes. I wrote: We apologize for the late delivery, and we are sorry for the inconvenience."

K

"Perfect. Now the solution. Remember, no full refund -- we offer a replacement. What did you write?"

P

"I wrote: We will send a replacement part by express delivery, free of charge, and we will resolve this by Friday."

K

"That is clear and professional. You acknowledged, you apologized, and you offered a solution. Add me in CC and send it. Great work, Percival."
