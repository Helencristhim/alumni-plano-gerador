Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Saying what you can and cannot do

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes after can and cannot?

"I can take the bus to work."

"She can walk to the office."

"I can drive a car."

"He cannot drive the train."

Grammar Rule

Can and Cannot

FormStructureExample
Affirmativecan + verb (base form)I can drive a car.
Negativecannot + verb (base form)I cannot drive the train.
QuestionCan + subject + verb?Can you take the bus?

Key idea: after can and cannot, the verb is always in the base form -- no "to" and no "-s". In speaking, "cannot" is often short: "can't".

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I can to take the bus."
"I can take the bus."

After can and cannot, never use "to". The verb stays in the base form.
