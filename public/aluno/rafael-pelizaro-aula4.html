Stage 1.1: Key Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Eight essential words for vendor negotiation in IT.

Negotiate.To discuss terms and try to reach an agreement
"We need to negotiate better terms with the cloud provider."
Proposal.A formal plan or suggestion presented for consideration
"The vendor sent a proposal for the new infrastructure project."
Terms.The conditions or requirements of an agreement
"The terms of the agreement include a 99.9 percent uptime guarantee."
Discount.A reduction in the usual price
"They offered a 15 percent discount for a two-year commitment."
Contract.A formal written agreement between two parties
"We signed a three-year contract with the data center provider."
Clause.A specific condition or provision in a contract
"There is a penalty clause if they miss the delivery deadline."
Counteroffer.A response to an offer with different terms
"Our team prepared a counteroffer with adjusted pricing."
SLA.Service Level Agreement -- a contract defining expected service standards
"The SLA defines response times for critical support tickets."

Grammar Tip: Can/Could for Requests

Grammar

We use can and could to make requests and offers in negotiations. Could is more formal and polite than can.

FunctionPatternExample
Direct requestCan you + verb...?Can you send the proposal by Friday?
Polite requestCould you + verb...?Could you offer a better discount?
Making an offerWe can offer/include...We can offer a 10% discount.
Tentative offerWe could consider...We could consider extending the SLA.

Conditional pattern: "If you [do X], we can/could [do Y]." Example: "If you commit for two years, we can lower the price by 15 percent."

Quiz

A "Your price is wrong. Change it."

C "I do not like your proposal."
You want the vendor to add an uptime guarantee. What do you say?
A "Can you include a penalty clause for uptime below 99.9 percent?"
B "You must guarantee 100 percent uptime."
C "I hope the service works well."
