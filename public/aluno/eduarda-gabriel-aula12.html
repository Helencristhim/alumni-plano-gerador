Chapter 3 — The Code

Could, Would & Might

Look at the purple words. How do we propose, request, and agree without sounding pushy?

We could offer a longer repayment period.
Would you be willing to accept a haircut?
That might work for us.
We could meet halfway on the rate.
ModalUse in negotiationExample
couldsoften a proposal or suggestion"We could offer a longer timeline."
wouldpolite request / hypothetical"Would you be willing to accept a haircut?"
mighttentative possibility"That might work for us."
Grammar Rule

Tentative Language

FunctionPatternExample
Make a soft proposalcould + base verb"We could extend the deadline."
Make a polite requestWould you (be willing to) + verb"Would you lower the rate?"
Show tentative agreementmight + base verb"That might work for us."
Decline gentlymight not / could not + verb"That might not be possible for us."
Common Mistake

Watch Out!

"We could to offer a longer timeline."
"We could offer a longer timeline."

Modals (could / would / might) are always followed by the base verb — no "to", no "-s", no "-ed".

Chapter 4 — Getting There

At the
Negotiating Table

Dialogue

The Creditor Meeting

E
Mr. Vance, thank you for coming. We could start with the repayment terms.
V
Of course. But the current schedule is a real problem for my clients.
E
We understand. We could offer a longer timeline if you would accept a small debt haircut.
V
A haircut might be possible, but only if you sign a standstill agreement first.
E
That would work for us. Could we meet halfway on the interest rate?
V
I think we could find common ground there, if both sides act in good faith.
E
Then we would propose a ninety-day standstill and a twenty percent haircut.
V
That sounds fair. I would call that a settlement we can both accept.
Comprehension

About Mr. Vance

1. What is a real problem for Mr. Vance's clients?
The current repayment schedule.
2. What does Mr. Vance want before accepting a haircut?
A signed standstill agreement.
3. What does Mr. Vance call the final deal?
A settlement they can both accept.
Artifact

Settlement Proposal

Re: Vertex Industries — Proposed Restructuring Terms
From: Restructuring Team, Houlihan Lokey
To: Creditor Representatives

Dear all,

We would like to propose the following terms to resolve the outstanding debt. We could offer a 90-day standstill agreement, during which no enforcement action would be taken.

In return, we would ask creditors to accept a 20% debt haircut. We believe this might give both sides common ground for a final settlement.

We are negotiating in good faith and look forward to your counteroffer.

1. What pause does the team propose?
A 90-day standstill agreement.
2. What does the team ask creditors to accept?
A 20% debt haircut.
3. What do they hope this leads to?
Common ground for a final settlement.
