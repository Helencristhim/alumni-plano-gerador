Chapter 3

Reporting It

say · tell · the backshift of tense

Grammar Discovery

From Their Words to Yours

What happens to the verb when you report what someone said?

Karen: "The box is damaged."

She said the box was damaged. — present → past

Karen: "One bracelet arrived broken."

She said one bracelet had arrived broken. — past → past perfect

Karen: "I will send photos."

She told me she would send photos. — will → would

When you report, the verb takes one step back in time.

Grammar Rule

Reported Speech

Direct speechReported speechExample
present (is / are)past (was / were)"It's damaged." → She said it was damaged.
past simple (arrived)past perfect (had arrived)"It arrived broken." → She said it had arrived broken.
will / canwould / could"I'll send photos." → She said she would send photos.
say vs telltell needs a person; say does notShe told me it was missing. / She said it was missing.
Watch Out

Common Mistake

She said me that the box is damaged.
She told me the box was damaged.

Use tell + a person (she told me) and say without one (she said). And shift the tense back: is becomes was when you report it.

Practice

Report What She Said

Karen: "The shipment is late." → She said the shipment _____ late. → was (present → past)
Karen: "One bracelet arrived broken." → She said one bracelet _____ broken. → had arrived (past → past perfect)
Karen: "I will send photos." → She _____ me she would send photos. → told (tell + a person)
Karen: "We can't open without the stock." → She said they _____ open without the stock. → couldn't (can → could)

Milton handles Karen's problem

Dialogue

Milton & Karen

K
Milton, the shipment arrived, but two boxes are damaged and one bracelet is missing. The opening is in twelve days.
M
Thank you for telling me, Karen. I acknowledge the problem, and let me reassure you: we'll make this right before the opening.
K
I checked the paperwork twice, so there's a real discrepancy between what we ordered and what came.
M
Understood. I'm escalating this to me directly. We'll reimburse the two damaged pieces and reinstate the missing bracelet by express.
K
That's a relief. And the boxes were damaged in transit, not at your end?
M
In transit, yes. The liability is the carrier's, but I'll cover everything as a goodwill gesture while it's outstanding.

Spot the Error

Fix the Report

"She said me the box was damaged."
"She told me the box was damaged." (tell needs a person; say does not)
"She said the shipment is late." (reporting later)
"She said the shipment was late." (backshift: present → past)
"He told me he will reimburse us."
"He told me he would reimburse us." (will → would)
"She told that there was a discrepancy."
"She said there was a discrepancy." (or: told me there was)
