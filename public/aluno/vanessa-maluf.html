Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

Present Simple vs. Present Continuous — Corporate Context
TENSEUSEEXAMPLE
Present SimpleRotinas, fatos, responsabilidades permanentesI deal with compliance every day.
Present ContinuousAções temporárias, projetos em andamentoI am working on an acquisition right now.

Affirmative: I oversee the legal department. / I am overseeing a new project this month.

Negative: I don't deal with HR issues. / I'm not working on that case anymore.

Interrogative: Do you liaise with external counsel? / Are you ensuring compliance with the new regulation?

Dica: No contexto corporativo, usamos Present Simple para descrever seu cargo e responsabilidades fixas, e Present Continuous para projetos temporários ou em andamento.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context Grammar

When you listen to British English, you will notice that speakers use connected speech extensively. Words are linked together, vowels are reduced to weak forms, and consonants are dropped through elision. For example, "want to" becomes "wanna" and "going to" becomes "gonna." If you can't catch every word, don't worry — focus on getting the gist first. The more you are exposed to natural speech, the more your ear will adapt.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context Grammar

Pronunciation is not about sounding like a native speaker — it's about intelligibility. If your word stress is correct, people will understand you even if your accent is different. For example, the word "contract" can be a noun (CON-tract) or a verb (con-TRACT), and the stress changes the meaning. Sentence stress also matters: in English, content words (nouns, verbs, adjectives) are stressed, while function words (articles, prepositions) are reduced. Practicing minimal pairs like "ship/sheep" and "live/leave" helps you distinguish vowel sounds that don't exist in Portuguese.

1. What changes between "CON-tract" (noun) and "con-TRACT" (verb)?
A The spelling.
B The word stress position.
C The number of syllables.
2. What is the main goal of pronunciation practice according to the text?
A Sounding exactly like an American.
B Being understood (intelligibility).
C Eliminating any accent.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip Grammar

Word Stress Shifts: Noun vs. Verb
WORDNOUN (1st syllable)VERB (2nd syllable)
contractCON-tract (o contrato)con-TRACT (contratar)
recordRE-cord (o registro)re-CORD (registrar)
presentPRE-sent (o presente)pre-SENT (apresentar)

Dica: Em inglês, muitas palavras que funcionam como substantivo E verbo mudam o acento tônico. Substantivo = acento na 1a sílaba. Verbo = acento na 2a sílaba. Isso é muito comum no vocabulário jurídico: "a CONtract" vs. "to conTRACT".
