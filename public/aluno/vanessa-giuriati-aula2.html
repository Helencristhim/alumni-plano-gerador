Chapter 3

The Engine

Present simple + frequency adverbs

Grammar Discovery

How Often?

"I always wake up at six."

"She usually commutes by car."

"I rarely go to the gym on Mondays."

"He never goes to bed late."

AdverbHow oftenPosition
always100%before the main verb: I always wake up.
usually / often~80% / ~60%She usually commutes by car.
sometimes / rarely~40% / ~10%I rarely go to the gym.
never0%He never goes to bed late. (with "to be": I am always tired.)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I wake up always early."

✓ RIGHT

"I always wake up early."

✗ WRONG

"She commute by car."

✓ RIGHT

"She commutes by car."

✗ WRONG

"He does not goes to the gym."

✓ RIGHT

"He does not go to the gym."

Frequency adverbs go before the main verb. With he/she/it, add "-s" to the verb. After "does not," the verb has no "-s".

Chapter 4

Real Voices

Two friends talk about their weeks

Dialogue

A Curious Friend

D
So, tell me about a normal weekday. What do you usually do?
V
Well, I always wake up early, around six thirty. Then I get ready and I have breakfast.
D
Nice. And how do you get to work?
V
I usually commute by car. At work, I check emails and I often have meetings.
D
Sounds busy! Do you do anything to relax in the evening?
V
Yes. I sometimes work out, and I never go to bed late. I really need my sleep.
D
That is a healthy routine. You sound very organized.
V
Thank you! I am, and a clear routine keeps me calm.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What does Daniel ask about first?
He asks about a normal weekday and what she usually does.
2. What does Daniel ask about the evening?
He asks if she does anything to relax in the evening.
3. How does Daniel describe her routine at the end?
He says it is a healthy routine and that she sounds very organized.
