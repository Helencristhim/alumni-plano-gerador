Grammar Discovery

What happens to the structure?

Active: The team submits the report. → Passive: The report is submitted by the team.
Active: The CFO approves the budget. → Passive: The budget is approved by the CFO.
Active: The legal department reviews proposals. → Passive: Proposals are reviewed by the legal department.
Active: The manager assigns tasks. → Passive: Tasks are assigned by the manager.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

What is the pattern?

How do we form the present passive?

Present Passive Voice

ComponentActivePassive
StructureSubject + verb + objectObject (new subject) + is/are + past participle + (by agent)
SingularThe team submits the report.The report is submitted by the team.
PluralThey review proposals.Proposals are reviewed (by them).
No agentSomeone distributes the report.The report is distributed.
With agentThe CFO approves the budget.The budget is approved by the CFO.

When to use passive: When the action or result matters more than who performs it. Common in business processes, scientific writing, and formal descriptions.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Common Mistake

"The report is write by the team."

Wrong: base form instead of past participle

"The report is written by the team."

Correct: past participle (written)

"The paintings is displayed."

Wrong: plural subject with "is"

"The paintings are displayed."

Correct: plural subject = "are"
