Chapter 3 -- For & Since

Language Discovery

Present perfect with for / since -- and the passive voice

Discovery

Listen and Notice

What comes after "for", and what comes after "since"?

"We have used this accounting system for three years."

"I have worked with this firm since 2015."

"The team has prepared the audit since March."

"We have been compliant for a decade."

The Rule

Present Perfect: For / Since

ElementFormExample
Present perfecthave / has + past participle"have worked", "has prepared"
forfor + a period (length of time)"for three years", "for a decade"
sincesince + a starting point"since 2015", "since March"

Key idea: use the present perfect (have / has + past participle) for something that started in the past and is still true now. Use for with a length of time (for three years) and since with a starting point (since 2015). Ask "How long have you...?".

Language Focus

The Passive in Fiscal Talk

When the doer is the system or does not matter, use be / have been + past participle. Fiscal English loves it.

"The tax return has been filed before the deadline."

"Taxes are withheld at source on the dividends."

"The accounts have been audited every year since 2018."

Pattern: subject + be (is / are) or have been + past participle (filed, withheld, audited). Add "by + doer" only if it matters ("audited by an external firm"). Notice "has been filed since..." combines the passive with for/since.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"I work here since 2015."
"I have worked here since 2015."

For "how long" up to now, use the present perfect (have worked), never the present simple. And it is for + a period (for ten years), since + a starting point (since 2015).
