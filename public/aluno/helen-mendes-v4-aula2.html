Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Polite Requests on the Phone

Ask to speak to someone“Hello, could I speak to Laura, please?”
Offer to help“Can I take a message?”
Ask the caller to wait“Could you hold on a moment, please?”
Ask for a call back“Could you ask her to call me back?”
1. Which word sounds more polite and softer: “Can I...” or “Could I...”?
Could is a little softer and more polite. Both are correct and common on the phone.
2. What form of the verb comes after can/could: “Could you to hold on” or “Could you hold on”?
Could you hold on — use the base verb, with no “to”.
3. You answer the phone and the person is not there. What do you offer?
“Can I take a message?” (or “Would you like to leave a message?”)
4. Why do we add “please” and use “could” on business calls?
To sound polite and professional — on the phone, tone matters even more because the other person cannot see you.

Your Turn -- Production (Case Study)

Handle the Missed Call

The case: You are back from lunch and you see a missed call and a short note from reception: “A partner from the London office called — it sounded urgent. They are three hours ahead of us.” It is 2 p.m. in São Paulo.

Step 1 — Decide: Do you call back now or send an email? Give one reason.
Step 2 — Act: The partner does not answer, so leave a voicemail: introduce yourself (name, role, company), say why you are calling, propose a time to talk, and ask them to call you back.

USEFUL LANGUAGE

Hi, this is ... from ...I'm calling about ...It's a little urgentCould we schedule a call?I'm available before ...Could you call me back?You can reach me on ...Thanks, talk soon

Then discuss

1. Which is harder for you in English: understanding a voicemail, or leaving one? Why?
2. What is your rule for “call now” vs “email and wait” at work?
