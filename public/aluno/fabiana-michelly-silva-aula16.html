Chapter 3

Picture the Timeline

Future continuous: will be + verb-ing

Discovery

In Progress in the Future

Over the next three months, we will be onboarding four new suppliers. (action across a future period)

I will be running the cadence for the first few weeks. (in progress, not finished)

This time next week, we will be ramping up the second supplier. (at a point in the future)

While we ramp up, I will be tracking three success criteria. (two things at once)

Will the data stream be waiting on contracts next week? (question form)

By the end of week two, we will have delivered the charter. (future perfect = FINISHED -- the contrast)

Grammar Rule

Future Continuous

FormStructureExample
Affirmativewill + be + verb-ingWe will be onboarding four suppliers.
Negativewill not (won't) + be + verb-ingWe won't be ramping up all at once.
QuestionWill + subject + be + verb-ing?Will you be running the cadence?
Meaningan action IN PROGRESS at a future momentAt 3 pm we will be testing the pilot.
Contrast: Future Perfectwill have + past participle = FINISHED by thenBy week two we will have delivered the charter.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Next month we will onboard the suppliers all through the quarter." (will + base verb does not show the action in progress over time)
"All through the quarter we will be onboarding the suppliers." (will be + verb-ing shows the action in progress across the timeline)

Use will be + verb-ing for an action that is happening over a future period or at a future moment. Use will have + past participle only when the action is finished by that point.

Chapter 4

Run the Kick-off

Setting direction for the supplier-integration project

Listening 1

The Kick-off Brief

Marcus sets the direction for the project

0:000:00
1. What will the team be doing over the next three months?
Onboarding four new suppliers into the Bloomberg platform, while Marcus runs the kick-off cadence for the first few weeks.
2. What dependency does Marcus tell the team to watch?
The data stream depends on the contracts stream -- if contracts slip, data slips too.
3. How should the team handle a new request to avoid scope creep?
It goes through Marcus, not straight into a work stream -- the deliverables for phase one are the charter and the stakeholder map.
Artifact

Project Charter

Bloomberg Supply Chain -- Project Charter: Supplier Integration (Owner: Fabiana)

OBJECTIVE
Onboard four suppliers into the platform. Over Q3, we will be ramping up one supplier at a time.
WORK STREAMS
Contracts, Data, Training. Each has one owner, so ownership is clear. Note the dependency: Data cannot start until Contracts closes.
DELIVERABLES
By the end of week two, we will have delivered this charter and the stakeholder map.

Success criteria: on-time onboarding, clean data, zero disruption. Throughout the rollout, the owner will be tracking all three. Any new request is parked to prevent scope creep.

Dialogue

Aligning on the Plan

F
So for the kick-off, I will be assigning one owner to each work stream, so ownership is clear from day one.
M
Good. And what about the dependency between contracts and data?
F
This week I will be chasing legal daily so contracts close on time. We will not be starting data until that is done.
M
Makes sense. Will you be ramping up all four suppliers at once?
F
No, one at a time. By the end of week two we will have delivered the charter and the stakeholder map, and then I will be onboarding the first supplier.
M
Perfect. And keep an eye on scope creep -- if a new request lands, it comes to me first.
Listening 2

The Update

Fabiana updates Marcus after the kick-off

0:000:00
1. Which deliverables are already done?
The charter and the stakeholder map are signed off, so phase-one deliverables are done ahead of schedule, and ownership is clear for each work stream.
2. What dependency does Fabiana flag, and what will she be doing about it?

Comprehension

Timeline Spotted

1. Across a future period: "Over the next three months, we _____ four suppliers."
"...we will be onboarding four suppliers." (will be + verb-ing, action across a period)
2. At a future moment: "This time next week, we _____ the second supplier."
"...we will be ramping up the second supplier." (in progress at a future point)
3. Finished by then: "By the end of week two, we _____ the charter."
"...we will have delivered the charter." (future perfect -- finished, not in progress)

Spot the Error

Find the Mistake

0 / 4 errors found

"Next month we will onboarding the new suppliers."
"Next month we will be onboarding the new suppliers." (future continuous = will + BE + verb-ing)
"This time next week I will be deliver the charter."
"This time next week I will be delivering the charter." (after will be, the verb needs -ing)

"By week two we will have finished all the deliverables." (finished by a point = future perfect, not continuous)
