Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Can I see your...? -- Here is my...

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we ask for and show a document?

"Can I see your passport, please?"

"Here is my passport and my ticket."

"Here is your boarding pass."

"Where is my gate?"

Grammar Rule

My or Your

FunctionStructureExample
AskCan I see your + document?Can I see your passport?
ShowHere is my + document.Here is my ticket.
GiveHere is your + document.Here is your boarding pass.
Ask whereWhere is my + place?Where is my gate?

Key idea: use my for your own thing (Here is my passport) and your for the other person's (Can I see your passport?).

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Here is your passport." (you show your own)
"Here is my passport."

Use my for your own document; your is for the other person.
