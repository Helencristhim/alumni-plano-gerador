Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Past Simple for Storytelling Grammar

Use the Past Simple to talk about finished actions in the past. Regular verbs add -ed (launch → launched). Irregular verbs change form (make → made, have → had, feel → felt). For negatives and questions, use did + the base verb.

FormStructureExample
Regular (+ed)Subject + verb-edWe launched the system. / The team worked hard.
IrregularSubject + irregular formI made a plan. We had three months. I felt proud.
NegativeSubject + did not + base verbWe did not have much time.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did you solve the problem?

Common mistake: "Did you launched?" → "Did you launch?" (after "did", always use the base verb)

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

English has a music. Two tools make you clear and confident. First, word stress: every long word has one strong syllable, louder and longer — we say con-FE-rence and re-com-men-DA-tion. Second, sentence stress: we stress the content words (nouns, verbs) and keep the small function words (the, to, of, is) weak — "The PROJECT is on SCHEDULE." Then there is intonation, the up and down of the voice: questions usually go up ↗ and statements go down ↘. And for confidence, use emphatic stress: push one word hard to contrast or correct — "I said the FIRST option, not the second." Never speak in a flat monotone.
