Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Modal Verbs: can, should, must

Modal verbs add meaning to the main verb. They express ability, advice, or obligation. They do not change form (no -s, no -ing).

ModalMeaningExample
canability / possibilityWe can schedule the meeting for Tuesday.
shouldadvice / recommendationYou should send the agenda before the meeting.
mustobligation / necessityWe must meet the deadline for the SAP upgrade.

Remember: modal + base verb (no "to"). We must send (not "must to send").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Comparatives and Superlatives

We use comparatives to compare two things and superlatives to describe the extreme quality of one thing in a group.

TypeRuleExample
Short adj. (comparative)adj + -er + thanRevenue was higher than last quarter.
Short adj. (superlative)the + adj + -estThe fastest response time.
Long adj. (comparative)more + adj + thanMore successful than the previous one.
Long adj. (superlative)the + most + adjThe most significant improvement.
Irregulargood → better → bestThe best sales in the group.

Remember: Short adjectives (1-2 syllables) use -er/-est. Long adjectives (3+ syllables) use more/most. Some are irregular: good/better/best, bad/worse/worst.
