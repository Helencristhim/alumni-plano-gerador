Chapter 3: The Code

Quick Check

1. What is the difference between “must” and “should”?
“Must” expresses a strong obligation or rule. “Should” expresses a recommendation or advice.
2. Why is “You must to follow” incorrect?
Modal verbs (must, should, can) are followed directly by the base form of the verb, never by “to.”
3. What does “must not” mean?
It means something is prohibited. You are not allowed to do it.

Compliance Case Study

FIFA COMPLIANCE WORKSHOP — CASE STUDY

Case: Financial Compliance at South American Club

Presented by the FIFA Compliance Committee

A South American football club faced a major violation of FIFA’s financial fair play regulations. The club’s accounts lacked transparency, and there was no clear procedure for reporting financial decisions.

An independent audit revealed irregularities in player transfer fees. The FIFA Ethics Committee issued a sanction that included a transfer ban and a fine. To prevent future issues, the club must now implement a comprehensive compliance framework and ensure accountability at every level.

Chapter 4: Getting There

Case Study Comprehension

1. What violation did the club commit?
The club violated FIFA’s financial fair play regulations. Their accounts lacked transparency.
2. What did the audit reveal?
The audit revealed irregularities in player transfer fees.
3. What must the club do to prevent future issues?
The club must implement a comprehensive compliance framework and ensure accountability at every level.

Spot the Error

“You must to submit the report by Friday.”
“You must submit the report by Friday.” (no “to” after modal verbs)
“We should to review the framework.”
“We should review the framework.” (no “to” after should)
“Officers don’t must ignore violations.”
“Officers must not ignore violations.” (use “must not,” not “don’t must”)
“The team have to conducts an audit.”
“The team has to conduct an audit.” (base form after “have to”; “has” for 3rd person)

Chapter 5: Practice

Modal + Vocabulary

___ + regulation → Use a modal verb to make a sentence about regulations.
“All clubs must follow the new financial regulations.”
___ + audit → Use a modal verb to recommend audits.
“The club should conduct regular audits to prevent problems.”
___ + violation → Use a modal verb about prohibitions.
“Officers must not hide violations from the compliance team.”
___ + framework → Use a modal verb about obligations.
“We have to update the compliance framework every year.”
