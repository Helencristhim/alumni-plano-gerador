Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Could I have + countable / uncountable nouns

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which words can we count -- and which ones can we not?

"Could I have a coffee, please?"

"Could I have some milk in it?"

"How much sugar would you like?"

"How many muffins do you want?"

Grammar Rule

Countable & Uncountable

StructureMeaning
a / an + countable (a muffin, an apple)One thing we can count
some + uncountable / plural (some milk, some muffins)Requests and positive sentences
any + uncountable / plural (any sugar, any cups)Questions and negative sentences
How much (uncountable) / How many (countable)Asking about quantity

Key idea: uncountable nouns (milk, coffee, sugar, water) never take a or an. Use some or a little. Countable nouns (muffin, cup, sandwich) take a, an, or a number.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Could I have a milk, please?"
"Could I have some milk, please?"

Uncountable nouns like milk, sugar, coffee and water never take a or an. Use some or a little instead.
