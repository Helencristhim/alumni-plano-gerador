Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Look at each word, its definition, and the example. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Lead -- to be in charge of a team, project, or initiative
"I lead the due diligence team for global transactions."
Assess -- to carefully evaluate or judge the quality of something
"We need to assess the financial risks before the acquisition."
Oversee -- to supervise and manage a process or team
"I oversee post-merger integration projects across Latin America."
Brief -- to give someone essential information about a situation
"I need to brief the CEO on the latest deal updates."
Drive -- to be the main force behind something; to push forward
"Our team drives value creation in every transaction."
Articulate -- able to express thoughts clearly and effectively
"An articulate consultant builds trust with C-level executives."
Leverage -- to use a resource or advantage to achieve a goal
"We leverage data analytics to improve deal outcomes."
Align -- to bring into agreement; to make compatible
"We need to align our approach with the client's vision."
Pitch -- a presentation designed to persuade someone
"Carlos delivered a compelling pitch to the PE fund partners."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Carlos Bassan is a Senior Strategy Executive at Accenture in São Paulo. He leads the M&A advisory practice for the Americas region. Every day, he assesses risks and opportunities in corporate transactions. He oversees a team of twelve consultants across three offices. Last quarter, he briefed the board on a new acquisition strategy. His team drives value creation in every deal. Carlos is known as an articulate communicator who can align complex stakeholder interests. He leverages data analytics to improve deal outcomes. Recently, he delivered a compelling pitch to a major private equity fund. Over the past decade, his practice has driven over two billion dollars in deal value.

1. What does Carlos do every day?
A He delivers pitches to private equity funds.
B He assesses risks and opportunities in corporate transactions.
C He has driven over two billion dollars in deal value.
2. Which sentence describes a completed action in the past?
A He leads the M&A advisory practice.
B He briefed the board on a new acquisition strategy last quarter.
C He oversees a team of twelve consultants.
3. Why is Carlos described as "articulate"?
A Because he works in São Paulo.
B Because he leverages data analytics.
C Because he can express complex ideas clearly and align stakeholder interests.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are always true)
Past Simple = completed actions at a specific time in the past

TenseStructureExample
Present SimpleSubject + verb (base/s)"He leads the M&A practice."
Past SimpleSubject + verb (past form)"He briefed the board last quarter."

Common Mistake:

"He lead the team yesterday."

"He led the team yesterday."

The past simple of "lead" is "led" (irregular verb). Do not confuse with "lead" (present).
