Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Indirect Questions

Grammar

Indirect questions are more polite and professional. The key difference is the word order: in indirect questions, we use statement order (subject + verb), not question order (auxiliary + subject).

Direct QuestionIndirect Question
"What does the client want?""Could you clarify what the client wants?"
"When is the deadline?""Do you know when the deadline is?"
"Has the board approved it?""Could you tell me if the board has approved it?"
"What are the risks?""I would like to know what the risks are."

Common Mistake

Could you tell me what does the client want?

Could you tell me what the client wants? (no inversion after the introductory phrase)

Key starters: Could you tell me..., Do you know..., I would like to know..., Can you explain..., Could you clarify...

For yes/no questions: Use if or whether (e.g., "Do you know if the deal is on track?")

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Polite Interruptions

Grammar

In business meetings, interrupting directly can sound aggressive. Using conditional structures and softening phrases makes your interruption sound professional and respectful.

Direct (too blunt)Polite (professional)
"I want to say something.""If I may, I would like to add something."
"Wait, let me talk.""If I may jump in here, I think..."
"We should do X.""I would like to propose that we do X."
"Go back to the other topic.""Could we circle back to the other topic?"

Common Mistake

I want to say something about that.

If I may, I would like to add something to that point. (conditional + softener = professional)

Key patterns: If I may + verb..., I would like to + verb..., Could we + verb...?, Would it be possible to + verb...?

Redirecting: Use "Could we circle back to..." or "Could we address..." to steer the conversation back on track.
