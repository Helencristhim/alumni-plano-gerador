Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines
Present Continuous = actions happening now, current projects
Present Perfect = experiences, achievements connected to now

TenseSignal WordsExample
Present Simpleevery day, always, usually"He works with agencies."
Present Continuousright now, currently, this quarter"He is building partnerships."
Present Perfectsince, for, already, just"Revenue has grown significantly."

Common Mistake:

"I am work at Quinto Andar."

"I work at Quinto Andar." OR "I am working at Quinto Andar."

Never mix them: it is "I work" (simple) or "I am working" (continuous). "I am work" does not exist.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Facts = stating general truths about your business
Comparatives = comparing your business to competitors

StructureRuleExample
Present Simple (facts)Subject + verb (is, operates, connects)"The company operates in 50 cities."
Short adj + -er thanfast → faster, high → higher"Our platform is faster than traditional methods."
Long adj + more...thanefficient → more efficient"Our service is more efficient than competitors."
As...as (equal)as + adj + as"Our commission is as competitive as any other platform."

Common Mistake:

"Our platform is more faster than competitors."

"Our platform is faster than competitors."

Never mix them: short adjective = -er (faster, bigger). Long adjective = more (more efficient, more competitive). Never "more faster."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Modals for Polite Requests & Offers = using can, could, would, and would you mind to make polite requests and offers in business contexts.

ModalLevelExample
CanInformal, direct"Can you send me the proposal?"
CouldMore polite"Could you send me the proposal?"
WouldMost polite / hypothetical"Would you send me the proposal?"
Would you mind + -ingVery polite, formal"Would you mind sending me the proposal?"

Offers: "I could offer you flexible terms." / "We would be happy to adjust the commission."

Rules: Business English = Could/Would preferred. "Can" is OK for internal/casual. "Would you mind" for formal requests to clients.

Common Mistake:

"Would you mind to send me the proposal?"

"Would you mind sending me the proposal?"

"Would you mind" requires the -ing form, NEVER the infinitive with "to." Always: Would you mind + verb-ing.

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Meet the key words for this lesson. Click Listen to hear the pronunciation.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator) -- a measurable value that shows how well a company is achieving its goals

"We have onboarded fifty new agencies this quarter."
Forecast -- a prediction about future business performance
"Our revenue forecast for next year is very optimistic."
ROI (Return on Investment) -- the profit gained relative to the cost of an investment
"The ROI for our partners has been excellent — most see results within three months."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip: Present Perfect for Business Results

Grammar

Structure: have/has + past participle

Use: achievements, results, and changes that are relevant NOW

TypeEnglishMeaning
AffirmativeWe have grown by 40%.The growth happened and is still relevant now.
NegativeWe have not reached the benchmark.The goal was not achieved yet.
QuestionHas revenue increased?Asking about a result connected to now.

Data language: "increased by 25%", "grew to 500 partners", "reached a milestone", "compared to last year", "well below/above the benchmark"

Common mistake: "We have grew" → "We have grown" (irregular past participle)
