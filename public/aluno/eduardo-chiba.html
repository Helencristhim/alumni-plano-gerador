Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Modals for Polite Requests & Offers = using can, could, would, and would you mind to make polite requests and offers in business contexts.

ModalLevelExample
CanInformal, direct"Can you send me the proposal?"
CouldMore polite"Could you send me the proposal?"
WouldMost polite / hypothetical"Would you send me the proposal?"
Would you mind + -ingVery polite, formal"Would you mind sending me the proposal?"

Offers: "I could offer you flexible terms." / "We would be happy to adjust the commission."

Rules: Business English = Could/Would preferred. "Can" is OK for internal/casual. "Would you mind" for formal requests to clients.

Common Mistake:

"Would you mind to send me the proposal?"

"Would you mind sending me the proposal?"

"Would you mind" requires the -ing form, NEVER the infinitive with "to." Always: Would you mind + verb-ing.
