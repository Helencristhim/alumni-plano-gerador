Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Saying what HAPPENED: -ed and the five irregular verbs

GRAMMAR

A result is always in the past. This is how English says what is finished.

StructureUseExample
Verb + -ed (regular verbs)The action is FINISHED. The quarter is over. The form is the SAME for I, you, we, they, he and she.We achieved the target. / She presented the plan.
The 5 irregular verbs of the business reviewThey do NOT take -ed: they become a new word. There are only five — learn them and you cover most of the meeting.go → went · have → had · give → gave · meet → met · come → came

Careful (common mistake): in your language, the past is already inside the verb. That is why people say "we didn't achieved" — the past TWICE. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- What you are doing NOW: am / is / are + verb-ING

GRAMMAR

At a business dinner, the question is about NOW, not about always. This is how English shows the now.

StructureUseExample
I am / she is / we are + verb-INGThe project of NOW: this week, this month, this year. It started and it will finish.I am working on the launch. / My team is preparing the congress.
Always TWO pieces, never oneYou need both: am + working. Take one piece away and the sentence breaks.We are launching a product. (never "we launching", never "we are launch")

Always (present simple) x Now (-ING)I work in immunology = my job, it does not change · I am working on the asthma launch = this month, it will finish. The words that ask for the -ING: right now, currently, at the moment, this month.

Careful (a common mistake): in your language, one verb can say both things — the always and the now. In English it cannot. If you say "I work on the asthma launch" at the dinner, the executive hears that you do this every year and you always will. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Routine vs Right Now

GRAMMAR

A consolidation of the -ing form from lessons 4 and 6. Two times, one update: what you ALWAYS do, and what is happening NOW.

TimeUse it forExample
Present simple
(we visit)		Your routine and facts that are always true. Signals: every day, every week.We visit doctors every week.
Present continuous
(we are visiting)		What is happening NOW, this period. Signals: right now, at the moment, this week.This week, we are visiting doctors in São Paulo.
The formam / is / are + verb + -ing. The -ing never changes; only am/is/are changes.I am monitoring · the team is running · we are adjusting

Careful: "team" is singular in American English — "my team is running", not "my team are running". And with "right now" or "at the moment", always use the -ing form, never the simple.
