Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context Grammar

Daniela works at SPF Group, where the IT team manages all technology systems. Every day, SAP processes thousands of transactions automatically. The finance module tracks every payment, and the inventory module monitors stock levels in real time. When a customer places an order on the Centauro website, SAP sends the information to the warehouse. The system does not make mistakes because every step follows a programmed workflow. Daniela customizes these workflows to match the company's needs. Does SAP integrate with other tools? Yes, it connects sales, logistics, and finance into one platform.

What does SAP do every day at SPF Group?
A It processes thousands of transactions automatically.
B It creates new products for customers.
C It replaces the IT team completely.
What happens when a customer places an order on the Centauro website?
A Daniela calls the warehouse manually.
B SAP sends the information to the warehouse.
C The system shuts down for maintenance.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Present Simple for Processes Grammar

We use the Present Simple to describe how systems and processes work. These are facts that are always true.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb(s)SAP processes data automatically.
NegativeSubject + does not + verbThe system does not crash often.
QuestionDoes + subject + verb?Does SAP integrate with your CRM?

Remember: he/she/it + verbs (The module tracks payments.)
