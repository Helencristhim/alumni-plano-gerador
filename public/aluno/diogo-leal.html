Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple + Adverbs of Frequency = describing how often something happens in your routine.

FrequencyAdverbExample
100%always"I always check my emails first."
90%usually"I usually start at seven."
70%often"I often coordinate with my team."
50%sometimes"I sometimes work late."
10%rarely"I rarely miss a deadline."
0%never"I never skip a meeting."

Word Order Rule

Adverbs of frequency go BEFORE the main verb: "I usually start at seven."

But AFTER the verb "to be": "I am always at the office by 8:30."

Compare: "I usually check my emails." vs "I am usually busy in the morning."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Perfect = have/has + past participle. Used for experiences, duration, and recent actions connected to now.

UseStructureExample
ExperienceHave/Has + subject + ever + past participle?"Have you ever attended a tech conference?"
DurationSubject + have/has + past participle + for/since"I have worked at Oracle for three years."
Recent actionSubject + have/has + already/just + past participle"I have already sent the follow-up email."
NegativeSubject + have/has + not + past participle + yet"She has not replied yet."

Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Past Simple: "I worked at Oracle." (finished, you left)

Present Perfect: "I have worked at Oracle for three years." (still there)

Use the Present Perfect when the action is connected to NOW. Use the Past Simple when it is finished and in the past.
