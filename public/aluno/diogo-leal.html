Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (fatos, habitos, rotinas que sao verdadeiros no presente)

SubjectVerbExample
I / You / We / Theywork"I work at Oracle."
He / She / Itworks"She works at Abbott."

"I am responsible for" + verb-ing / noun

"I am responsible for managing projects."

After "responsible for", use a verb with -ing or a noun. Example: "I am responsible for managing projects."

Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple vs Present Continuous

TenseUseExample
Present SimplePermanent facts, routinesI work at Oracle.
Present ContinuousActions in progress, temporaryI am expanding our scope.

Present Simple: I work / He works / We manage
Present Continuous: I am + verb-ing → I am launching / She is expanding / They are achieving

Present Simple = permanent facts ("I work at Oracle every day."). Present Continuous = actions happening now ("I am expanding to the American market this year.").

2. How often does Diogo miss a deadline?

B He sometimes misses deadlines.
C He rarely misses a deadline.
3. Who does Diogo report to?
A The CEO of Oracle.
B The VP of Operations.
C His team in Colombia.

Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple + Adverbs of Frequency = describing how often something happens in your routine.

FrequencyAdverbExample
100%always"I always check my emails first."
90%usually"I usually start at seven."
70%often"I often coordinate with my team."
50%sometimes"I sometimes work late."
10%rarely"I rarely miss a deadline."
0%never"I never skip a meeting."

Word Order Rule

Adverbs of frequency go BEFORE the main verb: "I usually start at seven."

But AFTER the verb "to be": "I am always at the office by 8:30."

Compare: "I usually check my emails." vs "I am usually busy in the morning."
