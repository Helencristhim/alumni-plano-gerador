Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Past Simple + Going To

Grammar

Past Simple = completed actions in the past (Use o Past Simple para ações concluídas no passado)
Going To = future plans and intentions (Use going to para planos futuros)
Structure: Past Simple = Subject + verb (past form) | Going To = Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb

TypeRegular (arrive)Irregular (go)
AffirmativeI arrived at the airport.He went through customs.
NegativeI did not arrive late.He did not go to Miami.
QuestionDid you arrive on time?Did he go through customs?
Going ToI am going to stay for ten days. / He is going to visit Times Square.

Common Mistakes:

"I goed to the hotel."

"I went to the hotel."

"I going to stay ten days."

"I am going to stay for ten days."

Lembre-se: verbos irregulares têm formas próprias no passado (go → went, take → took). Para planos futuros, use am/is/are + going to + verbo base.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Discourse Connectors

Grammar

Discourse Connectors = words that link ideas and make your speech more professional (palavras que ligam ideias e tornam sua fala mais profissional)

FunctionConnectorExample
Additionalso, in addition, furthermore"In addition, we are reviewing the risk matrix."
Contrasthowever, on the other hand"However, we need more time for compliance."
Cause/Resultso, therefore, that's why, because of that"Therefore, we need a new action plan."
Sequencefirst of all, then, finally"First of all, we identified the issue."

Common Mistake:

"However we need more time."

"However, we need more time."

Lembre-se: SEMPRE use vírgula depois do conector (however, therefore, in addition, furthermore)!
