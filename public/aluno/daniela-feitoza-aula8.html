Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Read the rule carefully and study the examples.

Follow-up Questions & Active Listening

To keep a conversation going, do three things: react, then ask a follow-up question, then ask for a detail. Follow-up questions start with What kind of...?, How long...?, Where exactly...?, or Why...?. Short "echo" questions (Oh, really? Did you?) show you are listening.

ToolExampleWhen to use
React"Oh, really?" / "That is interesting!" / "Wow!"right after they speak
Follow-up"What kind of...?" / "How long...?" / "Where exactly...?"to learn more
Echo question"Did you?" / "Do you?" / "Really?"to show you listen
Ask a detail"Tell me more." / "Can you tell me one more detail?"to keep it open
Common Mistake:
"What you do?" ✗ → "What kind of work do you do?" ✓
"I am very interesting in your job." ✗ → "I am very interested in your job." ✓
A flat "Ok." kills a conversation. React first, then ask a follow-up — and remember: people are interested, topics are interesting.

Stage 2: Put in Order

Order

Listen first, then put the conversation in the correct order — from the opener to the friendly close.

?"That is interesting! How long did you work there?"
?"Wow, that sounds great. Let us keep in touch!"
?"So, what do you do?"
?"You mentioned a big project. Tell me more about it."
?"Oh, really? What kind of company is it?"
