Read for the Main Idea

The Dinner Is Part of the Congress

The Dinner Is Part of the Congress

Every year, thousands of people go to a medical congress. They listen to the presentations, they take notes, and they go home. But the executives who work in global companies know a secret: the real congress does not happen in the presentation room. It happens at the dinner, in the coffee line, and in the ten minutes before a session starts.

This is the moment for small talk. Small talk is not empty conversation. It is a door: you open it with an easy question, and the business walks through it. "What brings you to this congress?" is a better first question than any number. And the answer is almost always the same shape: "I am currently working on..." People love to talk about the project they are living with right now.

So do not stay with your own team, and do not wait for the perfect sentence. Introduce yourself, ask what the other person is working on, and listen. You are not selling anything tonight. You are networking: you are meeting colleagues, executives and stakeholders, and you are telling them, in one simple sentence, what you do. Tomorrow, in the meeting room, they already know your name.

Chapter 4: The Code

Every Day, or Right Now?

I work in immunology · Right now, I am working on the launch

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"I work in immunology." — always true

"Right now, I am working on the asthma launch." — true this month

"My team visits doctors every week." — always true

"This month, my team is preparing the congress." — true this month

Both are correct. Look at the purple words — what changes when the sentence is about right now?

FormUseExample
I am / she is / we are + verb-ingRIGHT NOW, or around now: this month, this year, this project. It has an end.I am working on the launch.
Two pieces, never oneYou need BOTH: the small word (am / is / are) AND the -ing. Drop either one and the sentence breaks.We are launching a product. (not "we launching")
Negative: I am not + verb-ingTo say what is NOT happening now.I am not working on that project this year.
Question: What are you working on?THE question of the dinner table. Learn it as one block — do not build it word by word.What are you working on at the moment?
Every day vs. right nowI work in immunology (my job — it does not change) · I am working on the asthma launch (this month — it will finish). The words that ask for -ing: right now, currently, at the moment, this month.

Ready phrase, no thinking: "I am currently working on..." — start with it and the rest of the sentence comes out.

Language Focus

Every Day, or Right Now?

AnalyseEvery day, or right now?
AI work in immunology.
BRight now, I am working on the asthma launch.
CMy team visits doctors every week.
DThis month, my team is preparing the congress.
A and C are always true -- they are your job, and they do not change: present simple. B and D are true THIS MONTH, and next month they change: am / is / are + verb-ING. Both are correct English. They simply answer different questions.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"I working on the asthma launch."
"I am working on the asthma launch."
"We are launch a new product this month."
"We are launching a new product this month."
"What you are working on?"
"What are you working on?"

Two pieces, always: am / is / are + verb-ing. And in a question, they swap places: are you.

Quick Fire

Answer Like the Dinner Is Tonight

1 / 6

You are alone at the dinner table. The executive next to you turns to you. Open the conversation.

  • Hello. I do not think we have met. I am Emmanuele, from Sanofi Brazil.
  • Good evening. May I introduce myself? I am Emmanuele Orrico.

"So, what brings you to this congress?"

  • I am here for the immunology sessions. I work in the Brazilian team.
  • I am currently working on the immunology portfolio, so this congress is important for me.

"And what are you working on at the moment?"

  • Right now, I am preparing the presentation for the August meeting.
  • I am currently working on the asthma launch with my field team.

You want to know what the other person does. Ask the question.

  • And you? What are you working on at the moment?
  • What is your team preparing this year?

The colleague says: "We are launching a new product in Europe this month." React, and keep the conversation alive.

  • That is very interesting. Is the launch going well?
  • That is interesting. We are preparing a launch in Brazil too.

The conversation is ending and you want to see this person again tomorrow. Close it, and use the expression from today.

  • I am looking forward to working with your team.
  • It was great to meet you. I am looking forward to your session tomorrow.
