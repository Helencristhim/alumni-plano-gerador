Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip: Present Simple + "Good at / Struggle with"

Grammar

We use the Present Simple to talk about facts, habits, and things that are always true.

FormI / You / We / TheyShe / He / It
AffirmativeI play handball.She plays handball.
NegativeI don't play tennis.She doesn't play tennis.
QuestionDo you play sports?Does she play sports?

Special expressions:

I am good at + noun/verb-ing = something I do well

I struggle with + noun/verb-ing = something I find difficult

Common Mistake

I am good in writing.

She don't like English.

Correct Form

I am good at writing.

She doesn't like English.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Adverbs of Frequency

Where do we put adverbs like always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never?

AdverbFrequencyExample
always100%I always wake up at seven.
usually80%She usually has breakfast at home.
often60%We often walk to school.
sometimes40%I sometimes practice on weekends.
rarely10%He rarely stays up late.
never0%I never skip breakfast.
Position Rule 1: BEFORE the main verb
"I always wake up at seven." / "She never skips homework."
Position Rule 2: AFTER the verb "be"
"She is usually tired after practice." / "I am always on time."
Common Mistake:
"I wake up always at seven."
"I always wake up at seven."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Present Simple Questions (Do / Does)

How do we form questions in the Present Simple?

SubjectAuxiliaryExample Question
I / You / We / TheyDoDo you like English?
He / She / ItDoesDoes she play handball?
Structure: Do/Does + subject + base verb (no -s!)
"Does she play handball?" (NOT "Does she plays")
Short Answers:
Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.
Common Mistake:
"Does she plays handball?"
"Does she play handball?"
