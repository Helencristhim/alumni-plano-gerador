Chapter 3 -- Start and Ask

Grammar Discovery

Let's start -- What do you think?

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we start a meeting and ask for opinions?

"Let's start the meeting."

"What do you think, Robert?"

"In my opinion, it is a good idea."

"I agree with you."

Grammar Rule

Meeting Phrases

PhraseWhen we use it
Let's start.Open the meeting
What do you think?Ask for an opinion
In my opinion...Give your opinion
I agree. / I disagree.Accept or politely refuse an idea

Key idea: a leader opens with Let's start, asks What do you think?, shares with In my opinion, and answers with I agree or I disagree. These four phrases run the whole meeting.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"What you think? I am agree with you."
"What do you think? I agree with you."

Use do in What do you think? and say I agree (not "I am agree").
