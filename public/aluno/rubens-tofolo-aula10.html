Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

Imperatives and prepositions of place

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

How do we give directions and say where things are?

"Go straight down the corridor." (an instruction)

"Turn left at the corner." (an instruction)

"Take the elevator to the third floor." (an instruction)

"The office is next to the pharmacy." (where it is)

Grammar Rule

Giving Directions

UseFormExample
Give an instructionbase verb (no subject)Go straight. Turn left.
Negative instructiondo not + base verbDo not use the stairs.
Say where it ispreposition of placeIt is next to the elevator.
Between / opposite / behindpreposition + placeReception is between the doors.
Order the stepsfirst, then, next, finallyFirst go straight, then turn left.

Key idea: an imperative gives an instruction and starts with the base verb with no subject (Go, Turn, Take). A preposition of place (next to, between, opposite, behind) says where something is. Use sequence words to keep the steps clear.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"To turn left at the corner."
"Turn left at the corner."

An imperative gives a direct instruction. Start with the base verb (go, turn, take), with no to and no subject. For a negative, use do not + base verb (do not use the stairs).
