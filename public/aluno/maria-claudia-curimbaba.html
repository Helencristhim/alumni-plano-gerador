Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar
Present Simple vs Present Continuous
Present Simple Habitual actions, routines, facts
I oversee the operations. Always, as a routine — habitual action
She deals with partners. Regular, repeated activity
Present Continuous Current projects, temporary actions
I am working on a joint venture. Right now, temporary project
We are planning an expansion. Current, ongoing project

Tip: Use the Present Simple for what you do regularly (routine) and the Present Continuous for what is happening right now or as a temporary project. Key words: always, usually, every day = Present Simple. Currently, right now, at the moment = Present Continuous.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar
Possessives: 's and s'
Rule Example
Singular noun + 's The company's headquarters = the headquarters of the company
Plural noun ending in s + ' The shareholders' meeting = the meeting of the shareholders
Irregular plural + 's The board's decision = the decision of the board
There is / There are
Structure Usage Example
There is Singular nouns There is a new branch in Texas.
There are Plural nouns There are three divisions under the holding.
