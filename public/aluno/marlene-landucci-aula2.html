Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto abaixo. As palavras em negrito são o vocabulário desta aula. Preste atenção no Present Simple e nos adverbs of frequency (always, usually, sometimes, never).

Marlene wakes up at six every morning. She has a morning routine. She always drinks coffee and has breakfast before work. She usually checks her properties at eight.

In the afternoon, she sometimes plays tennis. She exercises three times a week. Her schedule is very busy, but she loves it!

Marlene never goes to bed late. She says: "My routine keeps me healthy and happy."

1. When does Marlene wake up? (Quando a Marlene acorda?)
A At six in the morning
B At eight in the morning
C At noon
2. How often does Marlene exercise? (Com que frequência ela se exercita?)
A Every day
B Three times a week
C Never
3. Consider the sentence "She drinks coffee." Where does the adverb "always" go? (Considere a frase "She drinks coffee." Onde vai o advérbio "always"?)
A She always drinks coffee (before the verb)
B She drinks always coffee (after the verb)
C She drinks coffee always (at the end of the sentence)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple Affirmative + Adverbs of Frequency (Presente Simples Afirmativo + Advérbios de Frequência)

Subject (Sujeito)Verb (Verbo)Example (Exemplo)
I / You / We / They (eu/você/nós/eles)verb (base form)I wake up at six.
He / She / It (ele/ela)verb + s/esShe wakes up at six.

Adverbs of Frequency (Advérbios de Frequência):

Adverb (Advérbio)TraduçãoFrequency (Frequência)Example (Exemplo)
alwayssempre100%I always drink coffee.
usuallygeralmente80%I usually go at eight.
sometimesàs vezes40%I sometimes play tennis.
nevernunca0%I never skip breakfast.

Posição do advérbio (Adverb position):

Subject + adverb + verb: "I always drink coffee."

Exceção: Sometimes pode ir no início: "Sometimes I play tennis."

Erro comum / Common mistake:

She wake up at six. ✗

She wakes up at six. ✓ (He/She/It + verb + s)

Stage 5: Think and Speak

Reflection
