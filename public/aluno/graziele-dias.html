Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

Hello! My name is Graziele. I am a nurse. I work at the hospital in Morumbi. I work with doctors from many medical groups. Today we have a meeting on Zoom, and we have a special guest: Dr. Carter, a doctor from the United States. I welcome him, and we start the meeting.

1. Why do we say "My name is Graziele"? (Por que usamos "is"?)
A Because "name" needs the verb "is". (Nome usa "is".)
B Because it is a place. (Porque e um lugar.)
C Because it is plural. (Porque e plural.)
2. In "I work at the hospital", what does "work" tell us? (O que "work" diz?)
A Where she is from. (De onde ela e.)
B Her workplace / her job. (O trabalho dela.)
C Her age. (A idade dela.)
3. Who is the guest? (Quem e o convidado?)
A A nurse from Morumbi. (Uma enfermeira de Morumbi.)
B Dr. Carter, a doctor from the United States. (Um medico dos EUA.)
C A patient at the hospital. (Um paciente.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Introducing Yourself

Grammar

Como dizer quem voce e em ingles (present simple com "I"). / How to say who you are.

Use / UsoPattern / EstruturaExample / Exemplo
Your name / Seu nomeMy name is + nameMy name is Graziele. (Meu nome e Graziele.)
Your job / Seu trabalhoI am a + jobI am a nurse. (Eu sou enfermeira.)
Your workplace / Onde voce trabalhaI work at + placeI work at the hospital. (Trabalho no hospital.)

Key idea / Ideia-chaveCom "I": usamos am (to be) e work sem "s". / With "I": am and work (no s).

Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

My name is Graziele, and my role is special. I am a coordinator. I work with many teams and many departments. I do not work in only one department. My colleague Ana works with me. She manages the office. She does not work in cardiology -- she works with the surgery team.

1. Why do we say "she works" but "I work"? (Por que "she works" leva "s"?)
A With he/she/it the verb adds -s. (he/she/it usa work + s.)
B Because "she" is plural. (Porque "she" e plural.)
C It is a mistake. (E um erro.)
2. How do we make the negative with "she"? (Como fica o negativo com "she"?)
A She not works. (She not works.)
B She does not work / doesn't work. (She doesn't work.)
C She don't works. (She don't works.)
3. Does Graziele work in only one department? (Graziele trabalha em um setor so?)
A Yes, only cardiology. (Sim, so cardiologia.)
B No, she works with many departments. (Nao, com muitos setores.)
C No, she does not work. (Nao, ela nao trabalha.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple

Grammar

Como falar do que as pessoas fazem (present simple). / How to say what people do.

Subject / SujeitoAffirmative (+)Negative (-)
I / you / we / theyI work with teams.I don't work here. (do not)
he / she / itShe works (+ s).She doesn't work here. (does not)
Key idea / Ideia-chavehe/she/it ganham -s no afirmativo; o negativo usa don't ou doesn't + verbo sem "s". / Add -s for he/she/it; use don't / doesn't for the negative.

Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

Welcome to my hospital. It is a big building. On the first floor, there is a reception next to the door. There are two elevators near the reception. On the fourth floor, there are many patients and many rooms for them. On this floor, there isn't a kitchen, but there is a small office.

1. Why do we say "there is a reception" but "there are two elevators"? (Por que "there is" e depois "there are"?)
A there is = one thing; there are = many things. (there is = uma coisa; there are = muitas.)
B They mean the same thing. (Significam a mesma coisa.)
C "there is" is for the future. (there is e para o futuro.)
2. How do we say there is NO kitchen? (Como dizer que NAO ha cozinha?)
A There no is a kitchen. (There no is a kitchen.)
B There isn't a kitchen / there is not a kitchen. (There isn't a kitchen.)
C There aren't a kitchen. (There aren't a kitchen.)
3. Where are the patients? (Onde estao os pacientes?)
A On the first floor. (No primeiro andar.)
B On the fourth floor, in many rooms. (No quarto andar, em muitos quartos.)
C In the elevators. (Nos elevadores.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- There is / There are

Grammar

Como dizer que algo existe em um lugar. / How to say something exists in a place.

How many / QuantosAffirmative (+)Negative (-) / Question
One / Um (singular)There is a room.There isn't a room. / Is there a room?
Two+ / Dois+ (plural)There are two rooms.There aren't two rooms. / Are there two rooms?
Key idea / Ideia-chaveUma coisa = there is + a/an. Muitas coisas = there are + plural com -s. / One = there is + a/an; many = there are + plural -s.

Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

1. Why do we say "at half past two" but "on Monday"? (Por que "at" e depois "on"?)

B They mean the same thing. (Significam a mesma coisa.)
C "at" is only for Mondays. (at e so para segundas.)
2. Which word do we use before a month (June)? (Qual palavra usamos antes de um mes?)
A at June. (at June.)
B in June. (in June.)
C on June. (on June.)
3. What time is the team meeting? (Que horas e a reuniao da equipe?)
A At half past two. (As duas e meia.)
B At nine o'clock. (As nove horas.)
C In June. (Em junho.)

Etapa 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas. (Read the text and answer.)

Today a new doctor calls. I need his name for the calendar. I ask: "How do you spell your surname?" His surname is Bennett -- B, E, N, N, E, T, T -- with a double N and a double T. The vowels are E; the consonants are B, N and T. Then I write his email address: bennett@hospital.com. I spell every letter to be sure.

1. How do you ask someone for the spelling of a name? (Como pedir para soletrar?)
A "How do you spell your surname?" (Como se escreve o sobrenome?)
B "What time is the meeting?" (Que horas e a reuniao?)
C "Where is the room?" (Onde fica a sala?)
2. In "Bennett", which letters are double? (Em "Bennett", quais letras sao duplas?)
A Double B and double E. (B duplo e E duplo.)
B Double N and double T. (N duplo e T duplo.)
C There are no double letters. (Nao ha letras duplas.)
3. Which letters are vowels? (Quais letras sao vogais?)
A B, C, D, F, G. (B, C, D, F, G.)
B A, E, I, O, U. (A, E, I, O, U.)
C Only the letter E. (Somente a letra E.)

Etapa 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Spelling Out Loud

Grammar

Como pedir e dar a soletracao. / How to ask for and give the spelling.
