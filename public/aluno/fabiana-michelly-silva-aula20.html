Day 20 -- The Whole Story

End-to-End Communication

Advanced discourse markers: signposting a coherent, extended turn

Warm-up

From the Parts to the Whole

You have presented proposals and negotiated the contract. Now you tell the whole story -- problem to signature -- as one coherent narrative.

Weak: "We had a problem. We made a proposal. We negotiated. We signed. The savings are four million."

Strong: "To begin with, the problem. Moving on, the proposal. That said, the negotiation was tough. As a result, we signed. To sum up: four million in savings."

Today we tell the full story:

How do you use discourse markers to signpost each stage, build a clear throughline, and give your turn the cohesion that keeps leadership with you from the first word to the recap?

Chapter 3

Signpost the Story

Sequence -- Contrast -- Result -- Summary

Discovery

Four Moves That Connect

To begin with, the context. Moving on, the proposal. (sequence: new stage)

The savings were clear. That said, the negotiation was tough. (contrast / concession)

We held firm on the cap. Consequently, the contract is balanced. (result)

It was a risk. Nonetheless, it paid off. (contrast: in spite of)

In light of this, we recommend rollout in Q1. (result: because of this)

To sum up, four million in savings, contract signed. (summary / recap)

Grammar Rule

Advanced Discourse Markers

FunctionMarkersExample
Sequence (new stage)to begin with / moving on / turning toMoving on, the proposal.
Contrast / concessionthat said / nonetheless / whereas / having said thatThat said, the negotiation was tough.
Result / consequenceconsequently / as a result / in light of thisConsequently, the contract is balanced.
Summary / recapto sum up / in essence / the upshot isTo sum up, four million in savings.
Key: commaMost markers open the sentence and are followed by a comma.
Common Mistake

Watch Out

"We held firm on the cap. That said, the contract is balanced." (this is a RESULT, not a contrast -- wrong marker)
"We held firm on the cap. Consequently, the contract is balanced." (a result follows from a cause -- use a result marker)

Match the marker to the function: that said / nonetheless = contrast, consequently / as a result = result, to sum up = summary. And never stack two of the same type ("moving on, turning to,") back to back.

Telling It End-to-End

Listening 1

The Full Story

Fabiana presents the supplier-consolidation programme end to end

0:000:00
1. How does Fabiana describe the context, and what was the crux of the problem?
To begin with, twelve months ago they ran eleven overlapping regional suppliers; the crux of the problem was duplication -- overlapping contracts, inconsistent lead times, and no single point of accountability.
2. What was the overarching aim of the proposal, and how did the negotiation go?
The overarching aim was simple: fewer partners, higher volume, better rates. In the negotiation they held the rate provided that the supplier committed to the volume and made the penalty clause non-negotiable; that said, they offered to halve the penalty for a faster lead time as their bargaining chip, and as a result both sides signed in good faith.
3. What is the throughline of the whole programme, and how does she sum up?
The throughline is that every decision, from the first proposal to the final amendment, was underpinned by real figures. To sum up: from eleven suppliers to four, projected savings of four point two million over three years, and the contract signed -- the full story from standpoint to signature.

Listening 2

Marcus Reacts

Marcus leaves a voicemail after the leadership review

0:000:00
1. What was the upshot of the leadership review, and what landed most?
The upshot is that they loved it; what landed more than anything was the coherence -- she took them from the original problem to the signed contract without a single gap, and the signposting made it effortless to follow.
2. What two things does Marcus praise specifically?
The way she used discourse markers (to begin with, moving on, that said, consequently) to frame each stage and give the story a clear throughline; and the sharp recap at the end, synthesizing the $4.2M savings and the move from eleven suppliers to four in two sentences.
3. What one piece of advice does he give for next time?
When she segues from the negotiation to the results, she should leave a half-second pause -- it lets a big number land. By and large, though, it was a complete, end-to-end story, well told.
Comprehension

Markers Spotted

1. "_____, the context: eleven overlapping suppliers." What function is this?
"To begin with, the context..." -- a SEQUENCE marker that opens the first stage.
2. "We held firm on the cap. _____, we traded a bargaining chip." What function is this?
"That said, we traded a bargaining chip..." -- a CONTRAST / concession marker.
3. "We traded a bargaining chip. _____, both sides signed." What function is this?
"As a result, both sides signed." -- a RESULT marker showing the consequence.
