Soft, Not Transactional

Listen and Notice

What Do the Orange Words Share?

Each line is casual and warm. Notice the two familiar patterns.

"You might want to try that place on 5th Avenue."

"We could grab a coffee sometime."

"I should probably head out soon."

"If I lived here, I 'd come to this cafe every day."

The Rule (Recap)

Two Tools You Already Have

ToolSocial useExample
might want toa gentle suggestion"You might want to try that cafe."
coulda light, no-pressure offer"We could grab a coffee sometime."
should probablya soft intention"I should probably head out soon."
if + past, 'da social what-if (not real now)"If I lived here, I'd come every day."

Remember: nothing new here. Modals keep you soft and friendly; the second conditional lets you dream out loud in a relaxed way. Both make small talk feel easy, not pushy.

Common Mistake

Keep It Soft

If I would live here, I would come every day. / You must try that cafe.
If I lived here, I'd come every day. / You might want to try that cafe.

Inside if, use the past (not "would"). For casual tips, choose a soft modal ("might want to") over a strong one ("must").

A Chat in Brooklyn

R

L

R

"Sao Paulo? No kidding! My sister lived there for two years. Small world."

L

"That's funny! If I lived here, I'd probably miss the food back home. Speaking of food, is the coffee good?"
