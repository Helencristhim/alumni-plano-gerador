Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple vs Present Perfect

TenseUseSignalsExample
Present SimpleFacts, routines, permanent situationsevery day, usually, always, never"I specialize in endocrinology."
Present PerfectExperience, duration from past to nowfor, since, ever, never, already, yet"I have visited ninety-five countries."
Common Mistake

"I work since 2001." "I have worked here since 2001."

Use Present Perfect (not Present Simple) with "since" and "for" when the action continues to the present.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Routines + Adverbs of Frequency

StructureUseExample
Subject + verb(s)Facts, routines, habits"I wake up at five-thirty."
He/She + verb+sThird person singular adds -s"He prescribes medication daily."
Negative: do/does notNegation resets verb to base form"He does not skip exercise."
Question: Do/Does + subj + verb?Yes/no questions"Do you see patients every day?"
Adverbs of Frequency (position: before main verb, after "be")
always 100% usually 80% often 60% sometimes 40% rarely 10% never 0%
Common Mistake

"I go always to the clinic at seven." "I always go to the clinic at seven."

Adverbs of frequency go BEFORE the main verb (but AFTER the verb "be").

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Past Simple -- Regular and Irregular Verbs

TypeRuleBasePast
RegularAdd -edtravel, work, publishtraveled, worked, published
IrregularChanges formgo, meet, bringwent, met, brought
Negativedid not + base form--"He did not attend the meeting."
QuestionDid + subject + base?--"Did you live in California?"
Key Irregular Verbs for This Lesson
go → went meet → met bring → brought have → had get → got see → saw make → made take → took
Common Mistake

"I didn't went to the conference." "I didn't go to the conference."

After "did" or "did not," use the BASE form of the verb (never the past form).
