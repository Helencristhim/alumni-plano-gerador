Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple for Routines + Adverbs of Frequency

StructureUseExample
Subject + verb(s)Facts, routines, habits"I wake up at five-thirty."
He/She + verb+sThird person singular adds -s"He prescribes medication daily."
Negative: do/does notNegation resets verb to base form"He does not skip exercise."
Question: Do/Does + subj + verb?Yes/no questions"Do you see patients every day?"

always 100% usually 80% often 60% sometimes 40% rarely 10% never 0%
Common Mistake

"I go always to the clinic at seven." "I always go to the clinic at seven."

Adverbs of frequency go BEFORE the main verb (but AFTER the verb "be").

Grammar

Grammar Tip: Present Perfect

Present Perfect -- have/has + past participle

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + have/has + past participleI have visited ninety-five countries.
NegativeSubject + have/has + not + past participleI have never missed a deadline.
QuestionHave/Has + subject + past participle?Have you ever lived abroad?

Key time markers:

ever = at any time in life (questions)never = at no time (negatives)already = before now (done)yet = until now (not done)just = very recently

Present Perfect vs Past Simple:

"I have visited France." = life experience, no specific time

"I visited France in 1990." = specific past time

Erro comum: "I have visited France in 1990." Present Perfect NUNCA usa tempo específico (in 1990, yesterday, last week). Com tempo específico, use Past Simple.
