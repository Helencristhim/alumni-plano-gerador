figure out • to understand or solve something after thinking carefully
"I need to figure out how to beat this level."
keep up with • to move or progress at the same speed as someone or something
"It is hard to keep up with all the new updates."
come across as • to give others a particular impression of yourself
"She comes across as very confident in interviews."
open-minded • willing to consider new and different ideas
"Being open-minded helps you learn from different cultures."
competitive • having a strong desire to win or be better than others
"He is very competitive when it comes to FIFA matches."
perspective • a particular way of thinking about something
"Traveling gives you a new perspective on life."
underestimate • to think someone or something is less capable than they really are
"Never underestimate a quiet person."
self-conscious • feeling worried about how others perceive you
"Many teenagers feel self-conscious about speaking in public."

"She finally broke through her fear of public speaking."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar
Present Perfect Simple vs Continuous

Present Perfect Simple — focuses on the result or completion of an action.

FormStructureExample
+Subject + have/has + past participleI have played FIFA since 2020.
-Subject + have/has + not + past participleHe has not finished the project.
?Have/Has + subject + past participleHave you seen this movie?

Present Perfect Continuous — focuses on the duration or ongoing nature of an action.

FormStructureExample
+Subject + have/has + been + verb-ingI have been playing for three hours.
-Subject + have/has + not + been + verb-ingHe has not been studying enough.
?Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ingHave you been waiting long?
Key Difference

Simple = How many? How much? (result/quantity)
"I have read five books this month." (focus on the number)

Continuous = How long? (duration/process)
"I have been reading all morning." (focus on the ongoing activity)

Note: State verbs (know, believe, want, like) do NOT use the continuous form. "I have known him for years." (NOT "I have been knowing")
