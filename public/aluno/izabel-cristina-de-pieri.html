3. Which question does Izabel form correctly?
A "What you do, and who you report to?"
B "What do you do, and who do you report to?"
C "What do you do, and who report you to?"

C Because the action is in the past.

GRAMMAR

A Because "units" is plural.
B It is passive: the units receive the action, so we use be + past participle.
C Because it is a question.
2. Look at "has been audited". Why is "been" there?
A The present perfect passive is has/have + been + past participle.
B "been" is a spelling mistake.
C Because the action is in the future.
3. Why does the writer use the passive so much in this text?
A Because the doer is always unknown.
B Because the process and the steps matter more than who does each one.
C Because the passive is shorter than the active.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Passive Voice

GRAMMAR

Describing a process when the action matters more than the doer (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
is / are + past participlePassivo no presente: ação habitual; quem faz não importaThe units are manufactured.
was / were + past participlePassivo no passado: ação terminadaThe report was submitted.
has / have been + past participlePassivo no present perfect: resultado até agora (NÃO esqueça "been")The accounts have been audited.
