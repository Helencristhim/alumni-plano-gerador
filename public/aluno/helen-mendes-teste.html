Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

These are the key words for this lesson. Tap Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Agenda -- a list of topics to discuss at a meeting
"Let's review the agenda before we start."
Minutes -- the official written record of what was said and decided in a meeting
"Who is taking the minutes today?"
Action items -- specific tasks assigned to people after a meeting
"We need to assign action items before we adjourn."
Follow up -- to take further action after a meeting or discussion
"I will follow up with the marketing team next week."
Chair -- to lead or preside over a meeting
"Helen will chair today's meeting."
Stakeholder -- a person with an interest or concern in a project or decision
"All stakeholders should attend the quarterly review."
Deadline -- the latest time by which something must be completed
"The deadline for the proposal is next Friday."
Adjourn -- to formally close or end a meeting
"Let's adjourn the meeting. Thank you, everyone."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text and answer the questions.

Every Monday, Helen chairs the weekly team meeting. She always starts by reviewing the agenda. Last week, she said: "Could we go through the agenda quickly? We have a tight deadline." David asked: "Would you mind if I added a point about the budget?" Helen replied: "Of course. Shall we discuss it after the project update?" At the end, she assigned action items to each team member and asked: "Could you send the minutes by end of day?" The stakeholders should receive the update before Friday. Helen then said: "Let's adjourn. I will follow up with everyone next week."
1. Which modal verb did Helen use to make a polite request?
A "Could we go through the agenda quickly?"
B "We must go through the agenda now."
C "Go through the agenda immediately."
2. What does "Shall we discuss it after the project update?" express?
A An order to the team
B A polite suggestion
C A complaint about the agenda

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modal Verbs in Meetings

Grammar

Modal verbs help you sound polite and professional in meetings.

Modal Use Example
Could Polite requests / suggestions "Could we move to the next item?"
Would Polite offers / asking permission "Would you mind sharing your screen?"
Shall Suggestions / offering to do something "Shall we schedule a follow-up?"
Should Advice / recommendations "We should review the budget before Friday."
Common Mistake:
Wrong: "Can we move to next item?"
Right: "Could we move to the next item?"
"Could" is more formal and polite than "can" in business meetings.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz
You are chairing a meeting and want to move to the next topic. What do you say?
A "Stop talking about this."
B "Shall we move to the next item on the agenda?"
C "I want to change the subject now."
A colleague is going off-topic in a meeting. How do you politely redirect?
A "That is not what we are talking about."
B "Please stop."
C "That is a great point. Could we table this for the next meeting?"
The meeting is over. You need someone to send the summary. What do you say?
A "Could you send the minutes after the meeting?"
B "You have to write the minutes."
C "Give me the notes right now."
