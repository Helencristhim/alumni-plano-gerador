Chapter 3 -- The Code

Joining Two Ideas with Who / Which / That

One smooth sentence instead of two short ones

Listen and Notice

What Is the Pattern?

Each sentence joins two ideas with one word. Notice the orange words.

"Daniel is a friend who never lets you down."

"It is a town which sits on a green mountain."

"She is the person that helped me move."

"It is a square that is full of charming cafes."

The Rule

Who for People, Which for Things

Relative wordUse it forExample
whopeoplea friend who helps me
whichthings & placesa town which is quiet
thatpeople, things, placesthe person that helped me

Tip: the relative clause comes right after the noun it describes. Use who for people, which for things and places, and that for both.

Common Mistake

Don't Repeat the Subject

"He is a friend who he helps me a lot."
"He is a friend who helps me a lot."

The relative word who / which / that already works as the subject. Do not add he, she, or it after it.

Practice

Choose the Right Word

"She is a teacher _____who changed my life." (a person)
"It is a cafe _____which serves the best coffee." (a thing / place)
"Daniel is the friend _____that helped me move." (a person -- both work)
"This is the view _____that I will never forget." (a thing -- both work)

Artefact

A Profile of Daniel

DANIEL -- BEST FRIEND

The person who never lets you down

Daniel is a friend who is incredibly reliable. He is the one who calls on your birthday and who remembers the small things.

He is also a person who is very generous: he is the only one that spent a whole weekend helping me move.

The thing that I admire most is that he is the same with everyone -- a friend who is both reliable and generous is a rare thing.
