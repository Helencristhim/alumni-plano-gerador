Chapter 3 -- The Pattern

Grammar Discovery

had + past participle = the earlier action

Grammar Discovery

Which Happened First?

The orange action happened BEFORE the other one.

"By the time I arrived, the train had already left." (the train left first)

"She was sad because she had lost the match." (she lost first)

"When we got home, the storm had passed." (the storm passed first)

"The film started before we sat down, so we had missed the opening." (we missed it first, then realised)

Grammar Rule

The Past Perfect

FormUse it forExample
had + past participlethe EARLIER of two past actionsThe train had left.
past simplethe LATER past actionI arrived late.
Negativehad not (hadn't) + past participleWe had not brought a map.
Time wordsby the time, when, before, after, alreadyBy the time I called, she had gone.

Key idea: in a story, two past events do not always happen in the order you say them. The past perfect (had + past participle) is the time machine that says "this one came first". The past simple tells what came next.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"By the time we arrived, the film had start." (had + base verb)
"By the time we arrived, the film had started."

After had always use the past participle (started, gone, seen, written) — never the base verb and never the -ing form. "She had finish" is wrong — say "She had finished".

Comprehension

About the Tips

1. According to the teacher, what is the secret of a long, rich story?

Showing which event happened first, by using the past perfect for the earlier action.

2. In "by the time I arrived, the film had already started", which happened first?

The film started first; then the speaker arrived. "Had started" marks the earlier action.

3. Which words does the teacher say good storytellers use to guide the listener?

Words like meanwhile, out of the blue, and turning point.

Artefact

A Short Story

THE NIGHT OF THE STORM

A short story by Mark

By the time Mark got home, the power had gone out. His family had lit candles, and the kitchen looked like a scene from a film.

At first, everything was calm. Then, out of the blue, there was a loud knock at the door. That was the turning point.

It was their neighbour. The storm had broken her window, and she had not been able to call anyone.

To cut a long story short, they helped her, shared their dinner, and laughed all night. The twist? The blackout became the best evening of the year.
