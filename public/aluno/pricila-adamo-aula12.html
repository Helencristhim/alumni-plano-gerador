Chapter 3: Giving Opinions

The Opinion Recipe

Open + view + reason

Discover the Code

Listen and Find the Pattern

"In my opinion, the food is amazing."

"I think the city is beautiful because it is by the sea."

"That's a good point, but I'd say nature wins."

"I see your point, and you may be right."

StepPhraseExample
OpenIn my opinion / I thinkIn my opinion, the food is amazing.
Reasonbecause + sentence...because you taste the culture.
AgreeI (totally) agree / Good pointThat's a good point.
Disagree (soft)..., but I'd say... / I see your point, but...I see your point, but I'd say nature wins.

Open with an opinion phrase, support it with because, and soften disagreement with a kind phrase first.

Common Mistake

Wrong vs Right

✗ WRONG

"I am agree with you."

✓ RIGHT

"I agree with you."

✗ WRONG

"No! You are wrong."

✓ RIGHT

"I see your point, but I'd say..."

Chapter 4: A Friendly Debate

Food or Nature?

Pricila and a traveler share their views

Dialogue

Pricila Meets a Traveler

T
In my opinion, the food is the best part of any trip. Don't you agree?
P
That's a good point, but I'd say the nature is just as special.
T
I see your point. On the other hand, you really taste the culture at the local market.
P
To be honest, I prefer to do both. It depends on the day!
T
Well, you almost convinced me. Shall we explore the food market together?
P
I'd love that. See? We agree on the important things!
Listening

A Traveler's View

Listen first. Sound first, no text.

0:000:00
1. What is the traveler's opinion about traveling?
The best part of traveling is the food.
2. Why does he think food is special?
You get to know a culture through its dishes.
3. Where would he rather spend an afternoon?
At a food stall, rather than in a fancy restaurant.
Comprehension

About the Debate

1. What does the traveler think is the best part of a trip?
The food.
2. What does Pricila prefer in the end?
To do both -- it depends on the day.
3. What do they decide to do together?
To explore the food market together.

Chapter 6: Your Turn

A Friendly Debate

Key phrases:

Start the Discussion

Scenario: Over dinner abroad, share your opinion about the best way to travel and ask the other person what they think.

Tools:

Free Practice

Your Friendly Debate

Scenario: Debate this with the teacher: "Is it better to travel alone or with other people?" Give your opinion, support it with a reason, react to the other view politely, and find one thing you both agree on.
