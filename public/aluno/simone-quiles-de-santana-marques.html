Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple = facts, habits, routines (things that happen regularly or are always true)

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb (base/s)"She works at Telefonica."
NegativeSubject + do/does + not + verb"She does not work on weekends."
QuestionDo/Does + subject + verb?"Does she work with international partners?"

Common Mistake:

"She work at Telefonica."

"She works at Telefonica."

With he/she/it, add -s or -es to the verb. In questions: "Does she work?" (no -s on the verb).

"Does she works with partners?"

"Does she work with partners?"

After "does", the verb goes back to base form (no -s).

Stage 2.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Adverbs of Frequency = words that describe how often something happens. They go BEFORE the main verb (but AFTER "to be").

FrequencyAdverbExample
100%Always"She always checks her emails."
80%Usually"I usually start at eight thirty."
60%Often"We often coordinate with London."
40%Sometimes"She sometimes attends four meetings."
10%Rarely"He rarely misses a deadline."
0%Never"I never leave without checking."

Position rule: Subject + adverb + verb / Subject + be + adverb

Common Mistake:

"She reviews always her emails."

"She always reviews her emails."

Position rule: Subject + adverb + verb. With "to be": Subject + be + adverb.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Para dizer o que esta acontecendo agora, use o present continuous: am/is/are + verbo com -ing. Negativo: am/is/are + not + -ing. Pergunta: Am/Is/Are + sujeito + -ing. Use com expressoes de tempo como now, right now, at the moment, this week, these days. Cuidado: precisa das DUAS partes (be + -ing) -- nunca diga "I working" nem "I am work". / To say what is happening now, use the present continuous: am/is/are + verb-ing. Negative: am/is/are + not + verb-ing. Question: Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing. Use it with time expressions like now, right now, at the moment, this week, these days. Careful: you need BOTH parts (be + -ing).

Negativeam/is/are + not + verb-ing"We are not waiting yet."
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing"Are you preparing the update?"
Time wordsnow, right now, at the moment, this week, these days
