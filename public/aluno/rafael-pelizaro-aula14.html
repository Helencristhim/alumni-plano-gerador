Chapter 3 -- The Code

Grammar Discovery

The past continuous

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

The orange words show actions in progress in the past. What two parts make each verb?

"At eleven p.m., the payment service was returning errors."

"Customers were trying to check out when the system failed."

"When the engineer joined, the database was running out of memory."

"While the team was fixing the outage, I updated the status page."

Grammar Rule

The Past Continuous

PartStructureExample
Affirmativewas / were + verb-ingthe service was returning errors.
Plural / youwere + verb-ingcustomers were trying to check out.
Negativewasn't / weren't + verb-ingwe weren't expecting an outage.
Interrupted actionpast continuous + when + past simpleI was watching the dashboard when the alert fired.

Key idea: Use the past continuous (was/were + verb-ing) for an action in progress at a moment in the past. A longer action in progress is often interrupted by a shorter one in the past simple: "While the team was fixing the outage, the alert fired again."

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Customers trying to check out when the system failed."
"Customers were trying to check out when the system failed."

The past continuous always needs was or were before the verb-ing. Use was with I/he/she/it and were with you/we/they.

Comprehension

About the Update

1. What was happening at about eleven p.m.?

The payment service went down: customers were trying to check out, but the system was returning errors.

2. How did the team remediate the problem?

They opened a war room, declared a sev-one, and restarted the service, so the outage ended around midnight.

3. What will the team do tomorrow, and why?

They will run a postmortem to understand the root cause and prevent the incident next time.

Artefact

The Incident Status Page

SYSTEM STATUS -- PAYMENT SERVICE

Incident #4821 / Rafael Pelizaro

SEVERITY

Sev-one -- major outage affecting checkout for all customers.

11:02 p.m. -- INVESTIGATING

We are aware that the payment service is returning errors. Customers were unable to check out. A war room is open.

11:34 p.m. -- IDENTIFIED

The database was running out of memory. The team is remediating the issue by restarting the service.

12:05 a.m. -- RESOLVED

The outage is over and checkout is fully restored. The disruption lasted about one hour. We are sorry for the trouble. A postmortem will follow.

Dialogue

The Debrief with Marina

H

"Good work last night, Rafael. The war room was calm. Can you tell me what you were seeing when the incident started?"

R

"Customers were trying to check out, but the service was returning errors. We set the severity to one right away."

H

"I checked the logs. While the team was fixing the outage, I saw that a memory alert was firing twenty minutes before we declared the incident."

R

"So we were already in trouble before we noticed. We remediated the problem fast, but the disruption was longer than it needed to be."

H

"Exactly. Your message on the status page was clear and honest, so customers stayed calm. The fix was fine; detection is the real lesson."

R

"Agreed. Let us run the postmortem tomorrow and add a better alert as the first action item, so we catch it while it is small."
