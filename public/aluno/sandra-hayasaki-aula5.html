Brainstorming -- Reading

Read the story

Blog — A Learner's Story

Two Years, One Journey

by Marina Alves

When I first started learning English two years ago, I could hardly order a coffee. I remember my first online class: I was so nervous that I forgot every word I had practised. That was my first big setback, and honestly, I almost gave up.

But I kept going. Little by little, things began to change. I made speaking a daily habit — fifteen minutes every morning, even on busy days. Some weeks I made no progress at all. The hardest part was learning to overcome my fear of mistakes instead of fearing them.

My breakthrough came after about eight months. I joined a conversation group and had to introduce myself to ten strangers. My hands were shaking, but I did it. For the first time, people understood me. That moment was a real milestone. It showed me that stepping out of my comfort zone was worthwhile.

Looking back now, I am proud of how far I have come. I am still not fluent, and I still make mistakes every day. But I have learned that persistence matters more than talent. From now on, I am going to keep practising and aim higher — next year, I want to give a presentation in English at work.

Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Phrases that tell a journey

When I first started, I could hardly order a coffee. (the beginning)
Little by little, things began to change. (gradual change)
The hardest part was learning to accept my mistakes. (the difficult moment)
Looking back now, I am proud of how far I have come. (reflection)
From now on, I am going to keep practising. (future plan)
Analyze and answer: what do these phrases help us do?
They are signposts that organise a personal story across time: where you started (When I first started), how things changed (Little by little), the difficult moment (The hardest part was), your reflection (Looking back now), and your plans ahead (From now on, I am going to).
