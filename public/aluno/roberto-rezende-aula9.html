Chapter 3: The Code

Formal Register
& Linking Words

Grammar

Discover the Pattern

1. The proposal looks good. However, we need to review the pricing.
2. The client approved the contract. Therefore, we can proceed with production.
3. We offer competitive pricing. In addition, we provide free maintenance.
4. Regarding your inquiry, I have attached the updated catalog.

What do the highlighted words do?

Linking WordFunctionExample
HoweverContrast (formal "but")"The price is high. However, the quality is excellent."
ThereforeResult (formal "so")"The deadline passed. Therefore, we need a new timeline."
In additionAdding info (formal "also")"We offer training. In addition, we provide support."
FurthermoreStrong addition"The engine is efficient. Furthermore, it meets all standards."
Grammar Rule

Informal vs Formal

InformalFormalRule
I want to tell you about..."I am writing to notify you regarding..."State purpose clearly
But we have a problem."However, there is an issue we need to address."Use linking words
Also, we can help."In addition, we are able to provide support."Avoid "also" at start
So let us meet."Therefore, I suggest we schedule a meeting."Formal consequence
Thanks, bye."I look forward to hearing from you."Professional closing
Common Mistakes

Watch Out

"The price is high, however the quality is good."
"The price is high. However, the quality is excellent."
"I want to ask about the order."
"I am writing to inquire about the status of the order."

Chapter 4: Getting There

The Follow-Up
Email

Real Context

Professional Email

Follow-Up: Trade Fair Meeting

From: roberto.rezende@company.com | To: marcos.silva@agribrasil.com

Dear Mr. Silva,

I am writing regarding our meeting at the Agrishow trade fair last week. It was a pleasure to discuss your company's requirements for diesel engines.

As discussed, we can supply the 200 HP model for your fleet. However, I would like to clarify that the delivery timeline is eight to ten weeks from the date of order confirmation.

In addition, I have attached our updated catalog with the full specifications. Furthermore, our engineering team can customize the engine for your specific agricultural application.

I will outline the next steps below:
1. Review the attached catalog and confirm the model.
2. Our team will prepare a formal proposal.
3. We schedule a technical visit to your facility.

I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience.

Best regards,
Roberto Rezende | Sales Executive | Brazil Office
+55 24 99999-0000 | roberto.rezende@company.com
Comprehension

Check Your Understanding

1. What is the purpose of Roberto's email?
To follow up on a meeting at the Agrishow trade fair and provide information about diesel engines.
2. Which linking word does Roberto use to introduce a contrast?
However — to clarify that the delivery timeline is 8-10 weeks.
3. How does Roberto end the email professionally?
"I look forward to hearing from you at your earliest convenience."
Dialogue

The Email Review

LC
"Hi Roberto. I reviewed your email draft. The content is good, however, you need to use more formal language."
RR
"What do you mean by formal language? I thought my email was clear."
LC
"It is clear, but for clients, we use linking words like however, therefore, and in addition. Furthermore, we avoid contractions."
RR
"I see. So instead of writing 'I want to tell you about the deal,' I should write 'I am writing to notify you regarding the deal.'"
LC
"Exactly. In addition, always start with the purpose. For example, 'I am writing regarding our meeting on Friday.'"
RR
"What about ending the email? I usually write 'Thanks' and my name."
LC
"For formal emails, use 'I look forward to hearing from you' or 'Please do not hesitate to contact me.' Therefore, your closing feels more professional."
RR
"That makes sense. I will rewrite the email and forward it to you for review."
Comprehension

About the Review

1. What did Lisa say was wrong with Roberto's email?
It needed more formal language — linking words like however, therefore, and in addition, and no contractions.
2. What formal opening did Lisa suggest?
"I am writing regarding..." instead of informal openings.
3. What professional closings did Lisa recommend?
"I look forward to hearing from you" or "Please do not hesitate to contact me."
