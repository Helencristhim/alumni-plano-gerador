Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Olha a palavra, a definição e o exemplo juntos. Toca em Listen — o som ajuda muito a memória.

Wellness -- the state of being healthy and happy
"Wellness is not just about your body, it is about your mind too."
Balance -- having the right amount of different things in your life
"You should find a good balance between work and rest."
Mindful -- paying careful attention to the present moment
"Being mindful helps you reduce stress and enjoy the moment."
Symptom -- a sign that something is wrong with your body
"If you have any symptoms, you should see a doctor."
Prescription -- a written order from a doctor for medicine
"The doctor gave me a prescription for the medicine."
Relief -- the feeling when pain or worry goes away
"I felt relief when the headache finally went away."
Prevent -- to stop something from happening
"Exercise and good food can help prevent many diseases."
Recommend -- to suggest something as good or suitable
"I would recommend walking thirty minutes every day."
Recovery -- the process of getting better after illness
"Recovery after surgery takes time and patience."
Habit -- something you do regularly, often without thinking
"Drinking water is a simple but important habit."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

As a dentist, Pricila knows that wellness is about more than teeth. She should find a better balance between work and rest. She could try being more mindful — paying attention to the present moment instead of worrying about the future. When she had a bad headache last month, she noticed the symptoms early and went to the doctor. The doctor said she should rest more and gave her a prescription. She felt great relief when the pain went away. Her doctor recommended walking thirty minutes every day to prevent stress. Pricila thinks she might start yoga too — it could help her recovery from years of standing all day at the clinic. Drinking water and sleeping well are habits she should keep forever.

1. Which sentence gives strong advice (should)?
A "She should find a better balance between work and rest."
B "She might start yoga."
C "She could try being more mindful."
2. Which sentence shows a possibility, not certainty (might)?
A "She should rest more."
B "She thinks she might start yoga too."
C "Her doctor recommended walking."
3. Which sentence gives a softer suggestion (could)?
A "She should keep these habits forever."
B "She might start yoga."
C "She could try being more mindful."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Should = conselho forte, recomendação (strong advice)
Could = sugestão gentil, possibilidade (gentle suggestion)
Might = possibilidade fraca, talvez (weak possibility)

ModalStrengthExample
ShouldStrong advice (90% sure)"You should see a doctor."
CouldGentle suggestion (50-70%)"You could try yoga."
MightWeak possibility (30-50%)"It might help with stress."

Common Mistake:

"You should to rest more."

"You should rest more."

Modais NUNCA usam "to" depois: should + base form (sem "to").

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

A "You must stop working immediately."

C "You might want to ignore it."
2. You are at a pharmacy abroad and need medicine. What do you say?
A "Give me medicine for headache."

3. Your friend asks about your wellness routine. Which response uses modal verbs correctly?
A "I try to walk every day. I think everyone should have good habits. You could try yoga — it might help with stress."
