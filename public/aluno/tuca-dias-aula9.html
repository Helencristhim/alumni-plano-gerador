Chapter 3 -- Looking Ahead

Grammar Discovery

will vs going to

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

Which sentences are plans? Which are quick decisions or predictions?

"I am going to climb Mont Blanc next summer."

"Look at those clouds. It is going to rain."

"I think the trail will be hard."

"That bag is heavy. I will carry it for you."

Grammar Rule

Two Ways to Talk About the Future

FormUseExample
going toa plan decided before nowI am going to climb the summit.
going toa prediction from present evidenceThe sky is dark; it is going to storm.
willa quick decision, offer, or promiseI will carry the backpack for you.
willan opinion or prediction (I think, probably)I think it will be cold at the summit.

Key idea: use going to for a plan you already made or evidence you can see now. Use will for a decision you make at this moment, an offer, or an opinion.

Common Mistake

Watch Out

"Look at those clouds! It will rain."
"Look at those clouds! It is going to rain."

When you can see the evidence now, use going to. Keep will for quick decisions, offers, and opinions.

T

M

"You are going to need waterproof boots, warm layers, and a good backpack. The altitude is high, so warm clothes are essential."

T

"I see. And what will we do if the weather changes on the trail?"

M

"If a storm comes, we will stop and find shelter. I always check the forecast, so we will be ready. Safety comes first."

T

"That makes me feel better. Do you think we will reach the summit?"

M

"I think we will, if we go slowly. The slopes are steep, but the view from the top is going to be unforgettable."
