Grammar Tip: Present Simple

Grammar

Present Simple is used to describe routines, habits, facts, and permanent situations.

FormStructureExample
AffirmativeSubject + verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)He manages IT projects.
NegativeSubject + do/does + not + base verbHe does not manage the HR team.
QuestionDo/Does + subject + base verb?Does he manage IT projects?

Key rule: For he/she/it, add -s or -es to the verb. For I/you/we/they, use the base form. Time markers: every day, always, usually, never, on Fridays.

Vocabulary

Stage 1.1: Key Vocabulary

Click on each card to listen. Learn 8 essential meeting words.

Agenda
A list of topics to be discussed during a meeting.
"Let me share the agenda for today's meeting."
Action item
A specific task assigned to someone during or after a meeting.
"We have three action items from last week's meeting."
Follow up
To check on or continue something that was discussed.
"I will follow up with the vendor about the delivery date."
Status update
A report on the current progress of a project or task.
"Can you give us a status update on the deployment?"
Deadline
The final date by which something must be completed.
"The deadline for the new module is next Friday."
Prioritize
To decide the order of importance of tasks.
"We need to prioritize the critical bugs before the release."
Reschedule
To change the planned time or date of a meeting.
"Can we reschedule the meeting to Thursday afternoon?"
Minutes
The official written record of what was discussed in a meeting.
"I will send the meeting minutes by end of day."

Grammar

Grammar Tip: Present Continuous

Form: Subject + am / is / are + verb-ing

Use it for:

  • Actions happening right now: "We are discussing the budget."
  • Temporary situations: "I am working on the migration this week."
  • Future arrangements: "We are meeting the vendor on Thursday."
SubjectFormExample
Iam + verb-ingI am preparing the agenda.
He / She / Itis + verb-ingHe is leading the meeting.
We / You / Theyare + verb-ingWe are discussing the deadlines.
Common mistake: "He leading the meeting" ✗ → "He is leading the meeting." ✓
