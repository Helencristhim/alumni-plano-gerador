What do the small endings do?

StatementTag QuestionRule
Positive statement+ negative tag"It is great, is it not?"
Negative statement+ positive tag"You do not mind, do you?"
With BEuse BE in tag"This is fun, is it not?"
With DOuse DO in tag"You work here, do you not?"
Grammar Rule

The Full Picture

TypeStructureExampleUse
Yes/NoAux + subject + verb?"Do you work in sales?"Get information
Wh-Wh-word + aux + subject + verb?"What segment do you work in?"Get details
TagStatement + opposite mini-Q"You work in sales, do you not?"Confirm / connect
Common Mistakes

Watch Out

"You work here, is it not?"
"You work here, do you not?" (work = DO, not BE)
"This is great, is it?"
"This is great, is it not?" (positive statement → negative tag)

Safe Topics vs Topics to Avoid

✓
The event / conference: "This is a great event, is it not?"
✓
Travel / the city: "By the way, have you visited the city before?"
✓
Work / industry: "What segment do you work in?"
✓
Food / local culture: "How about we try the local restaurant?"
✓
Weekend / hobbies: "What do you do on weekends?"
✗
Politics / elections: Never bring up at business events
✗
Religion: Very personal, avoid
✗
Salary / money: Never ask "How much do you earn?"
✗
Age / weight: Too personal for business
Comprehension

Check Your Understanding

1. What are three safe topics for small talk at a business event?
The event itself, travel/the city, and work/industry.
2. What topics should you never bring up?
Politics, religion, salary/money, and age/weight.
3. How can you use a tag question to start small talk?
"This is a great event, is it not?" or "The keynote was interesting, was it not?"
