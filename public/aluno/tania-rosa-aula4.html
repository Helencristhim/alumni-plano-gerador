Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Tânia leaves her hotel and wants to visit the Art Museum. She stops a local person on the street. She asks: "Excuse me, could you help me? I am looking for the Art Museum." The local says: "Go straight on this street for two blocks. Then turn left at the corner." Tânia asks: "Is it far?" The local answers: "No, the museum is across from the bridge, next to a café. You can also take the subway. The station is on the next block." Tânia says: "Thank you! I will walk on the sidewalk. I have a map too."

3. What does Tânia turn at the corner?
A Right.
B Left.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Aqui está a regra. Leia com calma e veja os exemplos.

Imperative + Prepositions of Place (Imperativo + Preposições de Lugar)

O imperativo é usado para dar instruções diretas. Preposições de lugar indicam posição. / The imperative gives direct instructions. Prepositions of place indicate position.

TypeStructureExample
ImperativeGo / Turn / Walk / TakeGo straight for two blocks.
next toao lado deThe museum is next to the café.
betweenentreThe bank is between the café and the hotel.
across fromdo outro lado deThe museum is across from the bridge.
on the corner ofna esquina deThe pharmacy is on the corner of Main Street.
in front ofem frente aThe bus stop is in front of the hotel.
Common Mistake / Erro Comum:
"Go to the left" ✗ → "Turn left" ✓
"The bank is in the corner" ✗ → "The bank is on the corner" ✓
Use "Turn left/right" (nunca "Go to the left"). Use "ON the corner" (nunca "IN the corner").

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Aqui não tem resposta certa — é pra você pensar em inglês. Toque no microfone e responda como vier.

Imagine you are walking in a new city and you are lost. You need to find the Art Museum, a good restaurant, and the subway station. Ask a local person for directions to all three places. Use "Excuse me...", "Could you tell me...", "How do I get to...", and prepositions of place.
