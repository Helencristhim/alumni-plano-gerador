1.3 Grammar Explanation GRAMMAR

Verb TO BE — Present Simple

English:
The verb “to be” changes depending on the subject:
• I am (I’m) — “I am a financial professional.”
• You are (You’re) — “You are reading about her story.”
• He/She/It is (He’s / She’s / It’s) — “She is nervous.” / “It is important.”
• We/They are (We’re / They’re) — “They are colleagues.”

Contractions: In spoken English, we almost always use contractions: I’m, you’re, he’s, she’s, it’s, we’re, they’re.

Business Tip: Use “It” for companies, projects, and meetings. “The project is on track.” “It is very important.”
Português:
O verbo “to be” muda conforme o sujeito:
• I am (I’m) = Eu sou / Eu estou
• You are (You’re) = Você é / Você está
• He/She/It is = Ele/Ela é / está
• We/They are = Nós somos / Eles são

Dica profissional: Use “It” para empresas, projetos e reuniões. “The meeting is at 2 PM” = “A reunião é às 14h.”

1.3 Grammar Explanation GRAMMAR

• I am not (I’m not) from New York.
• He/She/It is not (isn’t) Brazilian.

• Is she from Brazil? = Ela é do Brasil?

Maísa’s Daily Work

Maísa works at a financial company in São Paulo. She manages client portfolios. Every morning, she reviews reports and handles international accounts. Her job involves meeting clients for lunch. She goes to the office three times a week. Maísa deals with numbers every day. Her team has five people. The deadline is always next Friday!
1. “She manages client portfolios.” Why “manages” and not “manage”?
A Because it is past tense
B Because “she” is 3rd person singular — add -s/-es
C Because it is a question
2. “Her job involves meeting clients.” — “involves” has -s because:
A “Her job” = it (3rd person singular)
B It is plural
C It is negative
3. Which sentence is WRONG?
A She goes to the office.
B She go to the office.
C I go to the office.

1.3 Grammar Explanation GRAMMAR

Present Simple — Affirmative

English:
Use Present Simple for routines, habits, and general truths.
• I/You/We/They + base verb: “I manage client portfolios.”
• He/She/It + verb + -s/-es: “She manages client portfolios.”

Spelling rules for -s:
• Most verbs: add -s (work → works)
• Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o: add -es (go → goes)
• Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to -ies (study → studies)

Business Tip: “My job involves...” is an elegant way to describe your work. Better than “I do...”
Português:
Use o Present Simple para rotinas e hábitos.
• I/You/We/They + verbo base: “I work” = Eu trabalho
• He/She/It + verbo + -s: “She works” = Ela trabalha

Dica: Não esqueça o -s na 3a pessoa! “She work” está ERRADO.

1.3 Grammar Explanation GRAMMAR

Adjectives + Adverbs of Frequency

English:
Adjectives describe nouns. Position:
• After verb to be: “He is professional.”
• Before a noun: “She is a reliable colleague.”

Adverbs of frequency: always > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > never
• Before main verb: “She always arrives on time.”
• After verb to be: “He is usually easygoing.”

Business Tip: “He/She tends to be...” is softer than “He/She is always...” Great for professional descriptions.
Português:
Adjetivos descrevem substantivos. “He is professional” = Ele é profissional.
Advérbios de frequência: always=sempre, usually=geralmente, often=frequentemente, sometimes=às vezes, rarely=raramente, never=nunca
• “She always arrives” = Ela sempre chega

5. Free Production — Roleplay SPEAKING

Apresente seus colegas para David. Grave suas respostas.

DAVID:

“Maísa, I’d love to know more about your team. Can you describe them?”

SUA VEZ:

Ver resposta sugerida

“Sure! Ana is very friendly and reliable. She always meets deadlines. Carlos is usually easygoing, but he is very strategic in meetings.”

DAVID:

“And what about your manager?”

SUA VEZ:

Ver resposta sugerida

“He is very professional and experienced. He tends to be direct, but he is also very reliable. He is an excellent manager.”

DAVID:

“It sounds like a great team! How would you describe yourself?”

SUA VEZ:

Ver resposta sugerida

“I am usually very organized and professional. I tend to be direct too. But I am also friendly — I think that is important in finance!”
