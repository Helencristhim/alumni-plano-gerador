Brainstorming -- Reading for Gist

Read: Renata's Year at Lumo

Renata is the CEO of Lumo, a small company that makes smart lamps. Last week, she gave a short presentation to her team about the company's year. “Let me give you a quick overview,” she began.

First, Renata shared the figures. Sales increased by fifteen percent, compared to last year. This growth came mostly from online orders. However, sales in physical stores decreased a little, because more people now buy online.

Next, she wanted to highlight the biggest news of the year: the launch of Lumo's new product, a lamp you can control with your phone. The launch was a big success, and it brought many new customers.

Renata also talked about the team's target for this year. “Our target is to double our figures and to launch two new products,” she said. She explained that the company would hire five new people to reach this goal. At the end, she thanked the team. “The growth, the successful launch, the strong figures — they are all your work,” she said, and everyone clapped.

Answer in your own words (say it out loud)

1. What is the main purpose of Renata's presentation?
2. Overall, did Lumo have a good year or a bad year? How do you know?
3. In one sentence, what are the three main points of the presentation?
Answer key — click to reveal
1. To give an overview of the company's year — the figures, the new product launch, and this year's target.   2. A good year: sales increased and growth was strong, and the launch was a success; only physical-store sales decreased a little.   3. The company grew (sales up), it launched a new product, and it has a clear target for this year.

Diving Deep -- Language Focus

Describing Trends in a Presentation

Open with an overview“Let me give you a quick overview.”
Describe an upward trend with a figure“Sales increased by ten percent.”
Highlight the key news“I would like to highlight our new product launch.”
State a target“Our target for this year is to double our figures.”
1. “Sales increased ___ ten percent.” Which word fits: “by” or “in”?
by — after increase / decrease we use “by” + the amount: “increased by ten percent”.
2. What is the difference between “increase” and “decrease”?
increase = go up / become more; decrease = go down / become less.
3. “Let me give you a quick overview” — is this general information or one specific detail?
General information. An overview is general; a highlight is one important point.
4. Why do we say “I would like to highlight...” in a presentation?
It is a polite, professional way to point the audience to the most important news.

Your Turn -- Production

Present Your Year

You are the executive director. Create and deliver a 60-second mini-presentation about your real company's year. Include all four parts:

1. A quick overview to open.
2. One trend with a figure — something that increased or decreased (by how much?).
3. One highlight of the year (a launch, a project, a win).
4. Your target for this year.
