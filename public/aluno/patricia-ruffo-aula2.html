Grammar Discovery

First Conditional

StructureUseExample
If + present simpleThe condition (real possibility)If we change the formula...
will + base formThe result (future consequence)...the results will improve.

Key rule: After "if", use present simple -- NEVER "will". The "will" goes in the result clause only.

Chapter 3 -- The Code

Watch Out!

"If we will change the formula, the results will improve."

will after if = incorrect

"If we change the formula, the results will improve."

if + present simple = correct

Opinion Frames

FrameUse
"In my opinion,..."Introducing your view
"I see your point, but..."Polite disagreement
"Based on the evidence,..."Data-backed argument
"I would argue that..."Formal disagreement
"From my perspective,..."Personal viewpoint

Chapter 5 -- Practice

Quick Challenge

0 / 6
Your colleague says: "We should cancel the trial." You disagree. What do you say?
"I see your point, but based on the evidence, I would argue that we should continue."
Your boss asks: "What will happen if we change the formula?" Respond using first conditional.
"If we change the formula, we will lose six months of valuable data."
A colleague presents weak evidence. How do you respond politely?
"I acknowledge your research, but from my perspective, we need more significant data."
You want to propose an alternative approach at a meeting. What do you say?
"I would like to propose a different approach. If we extend the trial, the outcomes will be more convincing."
Someone asks you to explain your counterargument. Respond.
"My counterargument is based on the evidence. The current approach is showing gradual but significant improvement."
Convince your team to continue the current trial.
"Based on the evidence, I am convinced that if we continue, we will see significant results by December."
Chapter 5 -- Practice

Spot the Error

Click each sentence to see the correction

"If we will change the formula, the results will improve."
"If we change the formula, the results will improve." (no "will" after "if")
"I am disagree with this proposal."
"I disagree with this proposal." (disagree is a verb, no "am" needed)
"Based in the evidence, we should continue."
"Based on the evidence, we should continue." (based ON, not in)
"From my perspective, the outcome are not significant."
"From my perspective, the outcomes are not significant." (plural subject needs plural verb)
"If she will propose a change, the team will consider it."
"If she proposes a change, the team will consider it." (present simple after "if")
