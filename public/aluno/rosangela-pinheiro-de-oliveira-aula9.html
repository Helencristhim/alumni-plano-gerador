From Your Mistake to Theirs

When It Is Not Your Fault

Look at the photo. What is happening right now? Give me four sentences: "A woman is setting...", "People are working...", "Somebody is carrying..."

In Lesson 8 the museum was sold out. Nobody made a mistake — it was just a full Saturday, and you fixed it yourself. Tonight is different. Tonight somebody else made the mistake, and it costs you your bed. And the only person who can say that out loud, in English, at eleven at night, is you.

Should · Could · Might

Chapter 3: The Code

Advice · an option · a maybe

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"You should speak to the manager."

"Could you check the reservation again, please?"

"The flight might be delayed."

"She could call the hotel across the street."

Look at the word right after should, could and might. What is missing from it? No to, no -s, no -ing. And look at the last one: she could call, not "she coulds calls".

FormUseExample
should
+ plain verb		Advice. The right next step. Not an order, not a maybe.You should ask for the manager. · We should keep the boarding pass.
could
+ plain verb		One option of many — and, in a question, the polite way to ask for anything.I could try the other desk. · Could you check it again, please?
might
+ plain verb		Maybe yes, maybe no. You are not sure, and you are not promising anything.The flight might be delayed. · She might have a room on the fourth floor.
The verb after
never changes		No to, no -s, no -ing. And the modal itself is the same for I, you, he, she, we.She should call (never "should to call", never "should calls")
You already
know one		Lesson 6 gave you would like, and Lesson 8 gave you will. Same family, same rule: modal + plain verb.I would like to speak... · I will ask · You should ask

Three words, three jobs: should = my advice. could = one way, or a polite question. might = I am not sure.

The Anchor

How Strong Is Each One?

Three words, three different strengths
should
advice -- the right next step

"You should speak to the manager." I am telling you what is the best thing to do. It is not an order, and it is not a maybe. It is advice.

could
an option -- one possible way of many

"Could you check the reservation again, please?" = a polite request. "I could try the other desk." = one possibility. Soft, open, never rude.

might
not sure -- maybe yes, maybe no

"The flight might be delayed." Nobody knows yet. Fifty-fifty. Use it when you are NOT certain, and nobody can say you were wrong.

Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

"You should to speak to the manager."
"You should speak to the manager."
"She should calls the manager tonight."
"She should call the manager tonight."
"I am agree with you."
"I agree with you."

After a modal the verb is naked: no to, no -s. And agree is already a verb — it never takes am, is or are.

The Sentence Kit

Three Sentences, Any Problem

You do not need to argue in English. You need three sentences.

"There seems to be a problem with my reservation." (opens the door)
"I was told that the room was confirmed." (a fact, not a feeling)
"I would like to speak to the manager, please." (the next level)

Notice sentence one: it does not say you made a mistake. It says there seems to be a problem. Nobody has to defend themselves — so everybody can start helping you.

Expression of the Day

I Agree · I Disagree

I agree. · I disagree.

= these are already verbs (never "I am agree")

"You should shout at them." — "I disagree. In my opinion, the calm people get more."
"You should ask for the manager early." — "I agree. I waited one hour, and that was my mistake."

Now use it with In my opinion from Lesson 7: some people say shouting works. Do you agree?

The Hard Part

Who Gets the Room?

Two guests. Same problem. Same night. Same receptionist.

The loud one

"This is unacceptable! I want my room NOW!"

The receptionist is frightened. He follows the rules, exactly, word by word — because the rules protect him. And the rules give you nothing.

The calm one

"There seems to be a problem with my reservation. I was told that it was confirmed. Could you check again, please?"

His hands are not shaking, so his head is working — and his head knows things the rules do not: the room they keep for the airline, the hotel across the street, the manager's phone.

And never this: "I am so sorry to bother you..." — you are not bothering anybody. It is their job, and they want to know.

Dialogue

Eleven at Night, Hotel Serrano

M
Good evening, madam. Welcome to the Hotel Serrano. Do you have a reservation with us tonight?
R
Good evening. Yes, I do — Oliveira. I booked a double room with a view, back in March.
M
Oliveira... one moment, please. Oh. I am very sorry, madam. Your name is here, but the screen says the room was given to another family this afternoon. And we are full tonight.
R
I am sorry, I did not catch that. Could you say it again, more slowly, please?
M
Of course. Your room... is not free. Another family is sleeping in it. I think we are double-booked. I am very sorry.
R
Then there seems to be a problem. I booked the room in March, and I was told that it was confirmed. I have the email here. Could you check it again, please?
M
I am checking... You are right, madam. Your confirmation is here, in the system. But the room is gone, and I am alone at the desk tonight. I do not know what I should do.
R
I understand. It is not your fault. But I am not going to sleep in the street tonight. I would like to speak to the manager, please.
M
She is coming now, madam. And... thank you for being calm. The gentleman before you shouted at me for ten minutes, and I could not think at all.
R
Nobody sleeps better after shouting. What could the manager do for us tonight?
M
She might have the room we keep on the fourth floor. And she could call the hotel across the street — she knows the manager there. Please sit down, and I am going to bring you two coffees. Again, our apology.
Comprehension

About the Receptionist

1. What did Mateo say happened to the room?
It was given to another family that afternoon — they are sleeping in it. He thinks the hotel is double-booked, and the hotel is full tonight.
2. She asked him to check again. What did he find, and what did he say about himself?
He found her confirmation in the system — she was right. But the room was gone, and he was alone at the desk. He said: "I do not know what I should do."
3. What did he say about the guest before her?
The gentleman before her shouted at him for ten minutes, and he could not think at all. And he thanked her for being calm.
4. What two things did he say the manager might or could do?
She might have the room they keep on the fourth floor, and she could call the hotel across the street — she knows the manager there.
5. What did he do for them while they waited?
He asked them to sit down and went to bring them two coffees, and he gave them the hotel's apology again.
Listening

The Gate Announcement

Your flight to Lisbon is late. This is what the gate agent says. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What is the new departure time, and why does he say "might"?
It might be ten fifteen tonight. He says "might" because they do not know yet — the aircraft is still in Paris. He is going to give more information at nine o'clock.
2. You have another flight after Lisbon tonight. What should you do, where, and before what time?
You should not wait at the gate. You should go to desk number four now and speak to an agent — before eleven o'clock, because then the desk closes.
3. What two things could the agent at that desk do for you?
They could put you on a different flight this evening, or on the first flight tomorrow morning — and they could book a hotel room for you tonight.
4. When could you have the right to compensation, and what should you keep?
If the flight leaves after eleven — a delay of more than three hours. You should keep your boarding pass, even after the flight: without it you cannot ask for anything.
Listening 2

The Other Side of the Desk

Pilar has been a duty manager in Madrid for twenty-two years. Sound first — no text.

0:000:00
1. What does Pilar say about the guests who shout, and why?
They get less, not more. When a man shouts, her heart goes fast and she follows the rules exactly, word by word, because that is how she protects herself — and the rules give him nothing.
2. What does the calm woman say, and what happens then?
"There seems to be a problem with my reservation. I booked a double room in March, and I was told that it was confirmed. Could you check it again, please?" Then Pilar's hands are not shaking and her head is working.
3. What three things does Pilar know that the rules do not know?
The room on the fourth floor that they keep for the airline; that the hotel across the street has space tonight; and the manager there, who she can call.
4. Pilar asks you to do one thing and to stop doing another. What are they?
Complain — calmly, early, and ask for her by name. And stop saying sorry for complaining: "You are not bothering me. This is my job, and I want to know."

Thank you for your visit. Please check your bill before you pay.

1. You drank water, and you did not order any wine. Read the bill: which line is wrong, and how much is it?
"The bottle of Rioja wine — twenty-four euros. We did not order it. There seems to be a problem with the bill."
2. Without that line, what should the total be?
"Fifty-nine euros and sixty, minus twenty-four euros. It should be thirty-five euros and sixty."
3. The waiter comes to your table. Say it — the whole sentence, calmly.
"Excuse me. There seems to be a problem with the bill. I think we were overcharged: we did not order this wine."
4. He says the wine is on the bill, so you should pay it. What do you say next?
"I disagree. We drank water — you can see it here. I would like to speak to the manager, please."
Quick Fire

Answer Like You Are There

It is eleven at night in Madrid. You are tired. The receptionist looks at the screen and says your room was given to another family. Open the conversation, and do not shout.

  • There seems to be a problem with my reservation. I booked a double room in March.
  • I was told that the reservation was confirmed. Could you check it again, please?

He looks again and says there is nothing free tonight. You want the person above him. Ask, politely.

  • I would like to speak to the manager, please.
  • Could I speak to the manager? I think she could help us.

Your suitcase arrives at the hotel from the airport, and the wheel is broken. Tell the front desk what is wrong with it.

  • My suitcase is damaged. The wheel is broken.
  • There seems to be a problem with my bag: it arrived damaged.

The restaurant bill has a bottle of wine on it, and you drank water. Say it, at the table, to the waiter.

  • Excuse me. I think we were overcharged. We did not order this wine.
  • There seems to be a problem with the bill. This bottle is not ours.

You are back home and your camera is not in your suitcase. You call the hotel in Madrid. Say what is missing, and say when.

  • I think I have a missing item: my camera. I stayed in room three one two on Saturday.
  • There seems to be a problem: a missing item from my room. Could you ask the cleaning staff, please?

At the gate, the agent says your flight might be delayed for four hours. You have another flight after that one. Ask her what your options are.

  • I have a connection in Lisbon. What could I do?
  • Could you put me on a different flight this evening?

The hotel gives you a bigger room and a free breakfast because of the mistake. Accept it, and be warm about it.

  • Thank you very much. That is very kind of you.
  • Thank you for the compensation, and thank you for the apology.

Your friend tells you that you should shout, because shouting is the only thing that works. You do not think so. Say it, and say why.

  • I disagree. In my opinion, calm is faster.
  • I disagree. The manager helped me because I was polite, not because I was loud.

Production

The Time It Went Wrong for You

Record 60 seconds. Tell me about a real trip when something went wrong — Italy, Paris, anywhere. What was the problem? Who complained: you, or somebody else? What did they say, and how did it end? Then the important part: next time it happens, and you are alone — what will you do? Use should, could and might, and finish with I agree or I disagree.
