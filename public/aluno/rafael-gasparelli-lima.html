Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Present Simple — 3rd Person Rules (he/she/it/the company)

Present Simple — 3rd Person Rules
RULE EXAMPLE
Most verbs: add -swork → works, operate → operates
Verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -s, -x, -o: add -esgo → goes, do → does
Consonant + y: change y to -iessupply → supplies, study → studies
Irregular: have → hasThe company has 500 employees.
Negative: doesn't + base formHe doesn't export directly.
Question: Does + subject + base form?Does the company have offices overseas?

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Frequency Adverbs — Position in the Sentence (Advérbios de Frequência — Posição na Frase)

Frequency Adverbs — Scale & Position
ADVERB FREQUENCY EXAMPLE
always100%I always wake up early.
usually80%I usually have lunch at 12.
often60%He often attends meetings.
sometimes40%I sometimes work late.
rarely10%She rarely works overtime.
never0%I never work on weekends.

Position Rule (Regra de posição):

✓ Before the main verb: I always wake up early.

✓ After TO BE: He is always on time.

✗ NUNCA no início (diferente do português): Always I wake up early. → I always wake up early.

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Can / Could

Entenda quando usar CAN e COULD em pedidos, ofertas e habilidades.

Use Can (informal) Could (more polite)
Request (pedido)Can you repeat that?Could you repeat that, please?
Offer (oferta)Can I help you?Could I give you my card?
Ability (habilidade)I can speak English.I could understand him yesterday.
Permission (permissão)Can I sit here?Could I ask you a question?

Diferença principal:

  • Can = informal, cotidiano — Pode/Posso
  • Could = mais educado, profissional — Poderia
  • Em contextos de negócios, prefira Could para pedidos
  • Estrutura: Can/Could + sujeito + verbo base (SEM "to")
✗ "Could you to send me an email?"
✓ "Could you send me an email?"

Grammar Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Leia o texto e responda as perguntas de compreensao.

Grupo Picunha's headquarters is in São Paulo. There is a modern building with ten floors. There are over five hundred employees working there every day.

On the ground floor, there is a reception area and a large parking lot behind the building. There is an elevator next to the stairs. The cafeteria is on the first floor — there are many tables and a coffee machine.

Rafael's office is on the fifth floor. There is a conference room next to his office, and there is a printer in front of the elevator. There are three meeting rooms on his floor. The CEO's office is on the tenth floor, above all the departments.

There aren't any international offices yet, but there is a plan to open one in the United States.

1. How many floors does the building have?
A Five floors.
B Ten floors.
C Three floors.
2. Where is the cafeteria?
A On the ground floor.
B On the first floor.
C On the fifth floor.
3. Where is the parking lot?
A Behind the building.
B In front of the building.
C Next to the elevator.
4. Does the company have international offices?
A Yes, there are many.
B No, but there is a plan to open one.
C Yes, in the United States.

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Estude a estrutura gramatical da aula.

There is / There are

Usamos there is para coisas no singular e there are para coisas no plural.

SingularPlural
AffirmativeThere is a conference room.There are three meeting rooms.
NegativeThere isn't an office overseas.There aren't any problems.
QuestionIs there a cafeteria? Yes, there is.Are there any meeting rooms? Yes, there are.
Prepositions of Place (Preposições de lugar)
  • in — dentro de: The printer is in the office.
  • on — sobre/em: My office is on the fifth floor.
  • at — em (local): I am at the reception.
  • next to — ao lado de: The elevator is next to the stairs.
  • between — entre: The cafeteria is between the lobby and the parking lot.
  • in front of — em frente a: The desk is in front of the window.
  • behind — atras de: The parking lot is behind the building.

Atenção: Em português dizemos "Tem uma sala de reunião" usando o verbo TER. Em inglês, NUNCA use "It has" para descrever existência. Use sempre There is / There are.

Quiz Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Escolha a melhor resposta para cada situacao.

1. A visitor asks: "Where is the conference room?" Best answer:
A "There is on the third floor."
B "The conference room is on the third floor, next to the elevator."
C "Conference room is third floor."
2. Someone asks: "Is there a parking lot?" What do you say?
A "Yes, there is. It is behind the building."
B "Yes, it has."
C "Yes, there are a parking lot."
3. Your colleague asks: "How many meeting rooms are there on your floor?" Best answer:
A "There are three meeting rooms on my floor."
B "There is three meeting rooms."
C "Have three meeting rooms."
4. Which sentence is correct?
A "There is many employees in the building."
B "There are many employees in the building."
C "It has many employees in the building."
