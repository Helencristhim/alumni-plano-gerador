Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Frequency Adverbs — Position in the Sentence (Advérbios de Frequência — Posição na Frase)

Frequency Adverbs — Scale & Position
ADVERB FREQUENCY EXAMPLE
always100%I always wake up early.
usually80%I usually have lunch at 12.
often60%He often attends meetings.
sometimes40%I sometimes work late.
rarely10%She rarely works overtime.
never0%I never work on weekends.

Position Rule (Regra de posição):

✓ Before the main verb: I always wake up early.

✓ After TO BE: He is always on time.

✗ NUNCA no início (diferente do português): Always I wake up early. → I always wake up early.

Grammar Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip — Can / Could

Entenda quando usar CAN e COULD em pedidos, ofertas e habilidades.

Use Can (informal) Could (more polite)
Request (pedido)Can you repeat that?Could you repeat that, please?
Offer (oferta)Can I help you?Could I give you my card?
Ability (habilidade)I can speak English.I could understand him yesterday.
Permission (permissão)Can I sit here?Could I ask you a question?

Diferença principal:

  • Can = informal, cotidiano — Pode/Posso
  • Could = mais educado, profissional — Poderia
  • Em contextos de negócios, prefira Could para pedidos
  • Estrutura: Can/Could + sujeito + verbo base (SEM "to")
✗ "Could you to send me an email?"
✓ "Could you send me an email?"

There is / There are

Usamos there is para coisas no singular e there are para coisas no plural.

SingularPlural
