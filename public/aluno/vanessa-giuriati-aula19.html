Chapter 3

Should Have

Taking responsibility for what went wrong

Grammar Discovery

Looking Back

"I should have called you earlier."

"We should have warned you about the delay."

"We could have avoided this problem."

"We should not have sent the wrong item."

FormMeaningExample
should have + past participleThe right thing to do — but you didn'tI should have called.
could have + past participleIt was possible — but it didn't happenWe could have avoided this.
should not have + past participleIt was a mistake to do it. "We should not have sent the wrong item." Use it to admit a fault honestly. (In speech, "should have" sounds like "should've" — never "should of".)
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"I should of called you."

✓ RIGHT

"I should have called you."

✗ WRONG

"We should have warn you."

✓ RIGHT

"We should have warned you."

✗ WRONG

"I should called earlier."

✓ RIGHT

"I should have called earlier."

Always: should/could + have + the past participle (called, warned, sent). It sounds like "should've", but you write "should have" — never "should of".

Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. How did she react to the complaint at first?
She did not make excuses. She agreed: "You're absolutely right, and I'm so sorry. It was our fault."
2. What did she admit she should have done?
"We should have warned you about the delay much earlier." She took responsibility instead of blaming someone else.
3. What solution did she offer?
A full refund, and to replace the item by urgent delivery the next day — and she apologized for the inconvenience.
Listening

The Apology That Worked

Listen first. No text. Sound only.

0:000:00
1. What went wrong, and what was her first instinct?
Her team missed an important delivery, and it was their fault. Her first instinct was to defend herself and explain why it was not really her problem.
2. What did she say instead?
"You're right, and I'm so sorry. We should have warned you about the delay much earlier. We could have avoided this whole situation."
3. What are the three parts of a good apology?
Take responsibility (no excuses), say what you should have done differently, and offer a real solution.
Listening

A Customer Complaint

A different voice, a different message. Listen carefully.

0:000:00
1. Why was the customer so angry?
Her order was three weeks late, the wrong item arrived, and nobody had replied to her emails.
2. What did he do differently from the "old me"?
He just listened and let her finish. Then he took responsibility: "We should have replied to your emails, and we definitely should not have sent the wrong item."
3. What is the key lesson about complaints?
People who complain want to feel heard and want the problem solved. Stay calm, take responsibility, and offer a clear solution — a complaint is a chance to show who you are.
