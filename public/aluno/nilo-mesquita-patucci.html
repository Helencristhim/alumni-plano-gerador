Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text below. Pay attention to the words in bold — they show two important verb structures.

Nilo Patussi works at Sport Club Corinthians Paulista as the Chief Compliance Officer. He oversees all compliance and governance operations at the club. His background is in labor law and sports law.

Nilo has worked in the legal field for over fifteen years. He has represented Brazil in international programs before, but this is his first time at a FIFA event. He is currently preparing for the Leadership in Football program, which will take him to Miami, Rabat, and Zurich.

He says: "I have been selected as the only representative from Brazil. I want to learn from the best practices in governance worldwide."

1. What does Nilo do at Corinthians?
A He teaches English
B He oversees compliance and governance
C He plays football
2. How long has Nilo worked in law?
A For five years
B Since last year
C For over fifteen years
3. Why is Nilo going to Miami?
A For a vacation
B For the FIFA Leadership in Football program
C For a football match

Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple — Use for facts, routines, and permanent situations.

  • I work at Corinthians. (fact — it is my current job)
  • He oversees the compliance department. (routine responsibility)

Present Perfect — Use for experiences, duration from past to now, and results.

  • I have worked in law for fifteen years. (duration — started in the past, continues now)
  • She has represented her country three times. (experience — we do not say when)

Key difference:

  • Present Simple = what is true NOW (permanent)
  • Present Perfect = what connects the PAST to NOW (experience or duration)

Common mistake:

❌ "I work here since 2010." (WRONG)

✔ "I have worked here since 2010." (CORRECT — since + point in time = Present Perfect)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple — Use for routines, habits, facts, and permanent situations.

FormExample
I/You/We/They + verbI manage the team.
He/She/It + verb + -sShe coordinates with FIFA.
Negative: do/does + not + verbHe does not work alone.
Question: Do/Does + subject + verbDoes he manage the team?
Common Mistake: He manage the department. → He manages the department.
Does she coordinates? → Does she coordinate? (no -s after does)

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Perfect with for, since, ever, never

WordUseExample
forDuration (how long)I have worked for fifteen years.
sincePoint in timeShe has been here since 2020.
everQuestions (any time)Have you ever worked abroad?
neverNegative (no time)I have never missed a deadline.
Common Mistake: I have worked since fifteen years. → I have worked for fifteen years.
