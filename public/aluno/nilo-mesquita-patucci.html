Stage 1.1: Vocabulary

Vocabulary

Learn these 8 key words. Click Listen to hear the pronunciation.

Introduce — to present yourself or someone to others
"Let me introduce myself. I am Nilo Patucci, Chief Compliance Officer at Corinthians."
Represent — to speak or act officially for a group or organization
"I represent Brazil in the FIFA Leadership in Football program."
Oversee — to watch and direct an activity or a group of workers
"I oversee all compliance and governance operations at the club."
Compliance — the act of following rules, laws, and regulations
"Compliance means following rules and regulations in an organization."
Governance — the system by which an organization is directed and controlled
"Good governance is essential for the future of football."
Background — a person's education, experience, and professional history
"My background is in law, specifically labor law and sports law."
Currently — at the present time; now
"I am currently preparing for the FIFA program in Miami."
Responsible — having the duty to manage or take care of something
"I am responsible for the entire compliance department at Corinthians."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

Grammar

Read the text below. Pay attention to the words in bold — they show two important verb structures.

Nilo Patucci works at Sport Club Corinthians Paulista as the Chief Compliance Officer. He oversees all compliance and governance operations at the club. His background is in labor law and sports law.

Nilo has worked in the legal field for over fifteen years. He has represented Brazil in international programs before, but this is his first time at a FIFA event. He is currently preparing for the Leadership in Football program, which will take him to Miami, Rabat, and Zurich.

He says: "I have been selected as the only representative from Brazil. I want to learn from the best practices in governance worldwide."

1. What does Nilo do at Corinthians?
A He teaches English
B He oversees compliance and governance
C He plays football
2. How long has Nilo worked in law?
A For five years
B Since last year
C For over fifteen years
3. Why is Nilo going to Miami?
A For a vacation
B For the FIFA Leadership in Football program
C For a football match

Grammar Tip

Grammar

Present Simple — Use for facts, routines, and permanent situations.

  • I work at Corinthians. (fact — it is my current job)
  • He oversees the compliance department. (routine responsibility)

Present Perfect — Use for experiences, duration from past to now, and results.

  • I have worked in law for fifteen years. (duration — started in the past, continues now)
  • She has represented her country three times. (experience — we do not say when)

Key difference:

  • Present Simple = what is true NOW (permanent)
  • Present Perfect = what connects the PAST to NOW (experience or duration)

Common mistake:

❌ "I work here since 2010." (WRONG)

✔ "I have worked here since 2010." (CORRECT — since + point in time = Present Perfect)
