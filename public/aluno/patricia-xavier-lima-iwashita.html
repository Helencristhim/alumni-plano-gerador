Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Patrícia is a Customer Success Manager at Salesforce. Every week, she manages twelve premium accounts and runs calls with global teams. "I am responsible for driving adoption," she says. Right now, she is leading the onboarding for Bradesco, her biggest account. Her team is rolling out a new dashboard this quarter, and the deployment is almost ready. On the first call, a new colleague asks: "What do you do? What are you working on right now?" Patrícia answers with confidence, because she knows the difference between her routine duties and her current project.

1. Por que dizemos "she manages" e "she runs" com -s no final?
A Porque a ação aconteceu no passado.
B Porque, no present simple, he/she/it leva -s no verbo.
C Porque "Patrícia" é plural.
2. "Right now, she is leading the onboarding" usa o present continuous para expressar:
A Um projeto que está acontecendo agora (não uma rotina).
B Uma ação que ela faz toda semana, sempre.
C Algo que terminou ontem.
3. Which sentence about a routine duty is correct?
A "Every week, I am running calls with global teams."
B "Every week, I run calls with global teams."
C "Every week, I running calls with global teams."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo para falar do seu trabalho (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Simple
I / you / we + verb		Rotinas e responsabilidades fixas. Routine duties and permanent facts.I manage twelve accounts.
he / she / it + verb+s3ª pessoa SEMPRE leva -s. Third person always adds -s.She leads the rollout.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Projetos de agora, ações temporárias. Current projects, happening now.I am leading the onboarding.
responsible for + verb-ingSempre "for" + verbo com -ing. Never "responsible to".I am responsible for managing adoption.
Signal wordsevery day, usually → simple · now, currently, this week → continuous

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Perfect vs Past Simple

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo para falar da sua experiência (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Past Simple
verb + ed / irregular		Ação terminada com tempo definido. Finished action with a clear past time.I joined in 2019.
Present Perfect
have / has + past participle		Experiência até agora, tempo não terminado. Experience up to now.I have worked here since 2019.
since vs forsince + ponto no tempo · for + duração. Point in time vs length of time.since 2019 · for eight years
Irregular participlesParticípios irregulares comuns. Common irregular participles.lead → led · be → been · do → done
Signal wordsin 2019, last year, ago → past simple · for, since, so far, recently, ever, never → present perfect

Status Updates & Ongoing Work

Giving a clear project update on a call. Key words: update, progress, on track, milestone, blocker, pending, ongoing, priority, follow up, timeline. Structure: Present Continuous vs Present Simple.
0%
▼

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. Toque em Listen para escutar.

Update -- the latest news about a project's status (atualização)
"Let me give you a quick update on the project."
Progress -- movement forward toward finishing something (progresso)
"We are making good progress this week."
On track -- going as planned, on schedule (no rumo certo)
"The rollout is on track for Friday."
Milestone -- an important stage you reach in a project (marco)
"We have reached an important milestone."
Blocker -- something that stops the work from moving forward (impedimento)
"There is one blocker we need to solve."
Pending -- waiting to be done or decided (pendente)
"The approval is still pending."
Ongoing -- still happening, not finished yet (em andamento)
"The migration is ongoing right now."
Priority -- the most important task to do first (prioridade)
"User training is our top priority."
Follow up -- to check or act on something after a meeting (dar seguimento)
"I will follow up with you tomorrow."
Timeline -- the planned schedule of dates for a project (cronograma)
"We are still on the timeline we agreed."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Continuous vs Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Quando usar cada tempo para dar um status update (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Continuous
am / is / are + verb-ing		Ação acontecendo agora ou nestes dias. An action happening now.I am updating the report now.
Present Simple
verb / verb-s		Rotina, hábito ou fato. Routine, habit or fact.I send a report every Friday.
Spelling -ingwork → working · prepare → preparing (tira o -e) · run → running (dobra a consoante).testing · migrating
ContractionsNa fala, encurtamos. We shorten it when speaking.I am = I'm · we are = we're · it is = it's
Signal wordsnow, right now, at the moment, currently, this week → continuous · usually, every day, always, often → simple
