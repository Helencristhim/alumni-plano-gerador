Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple vs Present Continuous vs Present Perfect (Continuous)

GRAMMAR

As três formas que carregam a sua história profissional — e que o time canadense vai ouvir nos seus primeiros dois minutos (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Present Simple
I / we + verb (she + s)		Cargo permanente e o que ele abrange. Permanent role and what it involves.I lead People and Culture.
Present Continuous
am / is / are + -ing		Em andamento agora, temporário. In progress right now (at the moment, this year).We are rolling out the framework.
Present Perfect Continuous
have been + -ing		Começou no passado e continua — com for / since. Started in the past, still going.I have been leading it since 2023.
Present Perfect
have + past participle		Resultado concluído e contável, sem tempo dito. A finished, countable result.I have carried out four assessments.
Negativahave / has + not + particípio. Na fala, contraído: haven't.We haven't rolled it out in Canada yet.
InterrogativaHow long + have + sujeito + been + -ing?How long have you been leading this?
for vs sincefor + período (for eight years) · since + ponto de partida (since 2023)

Atenção (erro de brasileiro sênior): em português dizemos "lidero essa transformação desde 2023" com o presente. Em inglês, duração até agora nunca usa present simple nem present continuous: "I am leading this transformation since 2023" está errado — o certo é "I have been leading this transformation since 2023" (na fala, I've been leading). Nada faz você soar júnior mais rápido do que esse deslize.

Collocation (o outro deslize): em inglês não se diz "make a research" nem "make an assessment" — diz-se carry out research / carry out an assessment. E não é "responsible by", é responsible for.

The Language of Culture -- Diagnosing and Describing Culture

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Este é o vocabulário em que um diagnóstico cultural é escrito — o que separa uma opinião sobre a cultura de uma leitura profissional dela.

Cultural diagnosis -- a structured reading of what a culture really is, based on evidence (diagnóstico cultural)
"Before I propose anything, I would like to run a cultural diagnosis."
Core values -- the few principles a company will not trade away, even under pressure (valores essenciais)
"Core values are not what is on the wall. They are what survives a hard decision."
Psychological safety -- the belief that you can speak up or disagree without being punished (segurança psicológica)
"Psychological safety is built in the meeting where someone disagrees with you."
Onboarding experience -- the first weeks of a new hire, and how those weeks actually feel (experiência de integração)
"The onboarding experience was excellent for one week, and abandoned by the second."
Culture fit -- how well a person's way of working matches the way the team already works (aderência cultural)
"We hire for culture fit, and then we punish the first person who thinks differently."
Belonging -- the feeling that you can be yourself at work and still be part of the team (pertencimento)
"Belonging is high here. Safety is not. Those are two different problems."
Disengagement -- when people still do the job but stop caring about the outcome (desengajamento)
"Disengagement is quiet. It is the last thing you see and the first thing that happens."
Attrition -- the slow loss of people over time, one by one (perda gradual de talentos)
"Attrition never arrives as a crisis. It arrives as a resignation, and then another one."
Turnover rate -- the percentage of people who leave the company in a year (taxa de rotatividade)
"Our turnover rate climbed from nine percent to twenty six percent in three years."
Toxic culture -- a culture where fear, blame or disrespect have become normal (cultura tóxica)
"It is not a toxic culture. It is a specific problem that nobody names."
Exit interview -- the conversation with someone who is leaving, where the truth comes out (entrevista de desligamento)
"They filed the exit interviews. Nobody ever read them."
Pulse survey -- a short, frequent survey that measures how people feel right now (pesquisa de pulso)
"If they had run a pulse survey that year, they would have seen it coming."
To foster a culture of -- to create the conditions for a behavior to grow (cultivar uma cultura de)
"You foster a culture of speaking up by protecting the first person who does it."
To look into -- to investigate something properly before you judge it (investigar, apurar)
"Before we judge the team, we should look into what happened in that meeting."
The elephant in the room -- the obvious problem everyone can see and nobody names (o elefante na sala)
"Let me name the elephant in the room: nobody here disagrees with a director."

Leia o caso e responda às perguntas.

Northline Academy was a network of bilingual schools with an excellent reputation and a serious problem. In three years, its turnover rate climbed from nine percent to twenty six percent. The company had written a beautiful set of core values, printed them and hung them in every corridor — and it had never once run a cultural diagnosis to find out whether anyone lived by them. They should have addressed it during onboarding, and they could have fostered a culture of speaking up at almost no cost. Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Third Conditional + Modal Perfects

GRAMMAR

A gramática do diagnóstico: todo diagnóstico cultural fala de um passado que NÃO aconteceu — "se tivessem feito X, não teria acontecido Y" (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
Condição (if)
if + had + particípio		O passado que NÃO aconteceu. The past that did not happen.If they had run a pulse survey...
Resultado
would have + particípio		A consequência que, por isso, também não aconteceu....they would have seen it coming.
should have + particípioCrítica: era obrigação e não foi feito. Judges.We should have addressed it earlier.
could have + particípioOpção que existia e não foi usada. Softer.They could have fostered a culture of speaking up.
might have + particípioEspeculação prudente, sem acusar. Speculates.The team might have felt unsafe.
Negativahad not + particípio / would not have + particípioIf they hadn't ignored it, she wouldn't have resigned.
InterrogativaWhat + would + sujeito + have + particípio + if...?What would you have done if you had known?
Hedging executivoIt would appear that... (tudo indica que...) · To some extent... (até certo ponto...) — a mesma verdade, dita de um jeito que a sala aceita ouvir.

Atenção (erro nº1 do brasileiro): o would NUNCA entra na metade do if. "If we would have known" está errado — o certo é "If we had known, we would have acted". Na fala, tudo contrai: If we'd known, we'd have acted.

Escrita: é sempre should have, nunca "should of" — mesmo que, na boca de um nativo em velocidade normal, os dois soem exatamente iguais.

Collocation: em inglês não se diz "do a diagnosis" — diz-se run / carry out / conduct a cultural diagnosis. E é discuss the results, nunca "discuss about the results".

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada termo e leia o exemplo. Este é o vocabulário que os seus pares canadenses já usam todos os dias — o que separa quem relata a reunião de quem conduz a reunião.

OKR -- one goal you can say out loud, plus the numbers that prove you reached it (objetivos e resultados-chave)
"Our first OKR for Canada is one objective and three numbers. Nothing else."
Key performance indicator -- a number you watch all year to see whether the business is healthy (indicador-chave de desempenho)
"Engagement is the key performance indicator I will be judged on in Canada."
Cascading goals -- goals that flow down: the company sets one, and every team writes its own version (desdobramento de metas)
"Without cascading goals, nine academic teams will write nine different strategies."
Deliverable -- the concrete thing you promised to hand over, on a date (entregável)
"The onboarding journey is not an idea. It is a deliverable, and it has a date."
Milestone -- a checkpoint with a date on the way to the goal (marco, etapa-chave)
"Give me one milestone, one owner and one date. That is the whole meeting."
Accountability -- owning the result in public, whether it worked or not (responsabilização pelo resultado)
"A team that agrees with everything I say is not accountability. It is silence with good manners."
Cross-functional -- involving people from different teams who do not report to each other (interfuncional, entre áreas)
"The group is cross-functional, which is exactly why nobody owns the date."
Bandwidth -- the time and attention a person or a team actually has left (capacidade disponível)
"They do not lack commitment. They lack bandwidth, and those are different problems."
Buy-in -- the moment people stop obeying a plan and start wanting it (adesão genuína)
"Their buy-in is not a nice-to-have. It is the deliverable."
To put forward -- to formally offer an idea so that a group can decide on it (propor formalmente)
"What I would like to put forward is one owner and one date."
To push back -- to disagree with a plan, professionally, and say so out loud (discordar e se posicionar)
"Please push back if you think the target is wrong. I would rather hear it now."
To move the needle -- to produce a change big enough to show up in the numbers (mover o ponteiro, gerar impacto real)
"Tell me which single milestone would actually move the needle this quarter."
To cut to the chase -- to give the point first and the story afterwards (ir direto ao ponto)
"Let me cut to the chase: two of the four objectives are off track."
To bring about -- to cause a change, not merely to want one (provocar, causar (uma mudança))
"This is the one change that would bring about real accountability."
To circle back to -- to return, on purpose, to a point you left open (retomar (um ponto))
"Before we close, I would like to circle back to the onboarding deliverable."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Participle Clauses

GRAMMAR

A gramática do sumário executivo: ela pega DUAS frases e transforma a menos importante numa orção curta, sem sujeito e sem conectivo — deixando a frase principal com todo o peso. É o que separa quem NARRA de quem RESUME (explicação em inglês e português).

FormUse / UsoExample
1. Perfect participle
Having + particípio		A ação que veio ANTES. Empacota o passado inteiro em cinco palavras. The action that came first.Having reviewed the findings, I would propose two quick wins.
= After I reviewed the findings, I would propose...
2. Present participle
verbo + -ing		O RESULTADO ou a consequência da frase principal. Vem quase sempre DEPOIS da vírgula. The result of what you just said.The index came back at fifty-eight, leaving us nine points below the benchmark.
= ...and this left us nine points below.
3. Past participle
particípio sozinho		Sentido PASSIVO: o sujeito SOFRE a ação. Something was done TO the subject.Asked about the timeline, most of the faculty said the same thing.
= When they were asked about the timeline...
4. Aberturas fixas
Given / Based on / Drawing on		A abertura de executivo. Ancora a recomendação na evidência, antes de dizer qual é.Given the response rate, the caveat belongs on slide one.

A regra de ouroO participle e a frase principal têm de ter o MESMO SUJEITO. Se não tiverem, a frase desaba (o clássico dangling participle) — e em inglês ela não soa estranha: ela soa errada.

Atenção (o erro nº 1 — dangling participle): "Having reviewed the findings, the response rate was disappointing" está ERRADO: quem revisou os findings foi VOCÊ, não a taxa de resposta. O sujeito da frase principal tem de ser quem executa o participle. Certo: "Having reviewed the findings, I found the response rate disappointing." Teste infalível: pergunte "quem fez isso?" → a resposta tem de ser o sujeito da frase principal.

Atenção (o erro nº 2): having pede PARTICÍPIO, nunca infinitivo nem presente. "Having review the data" → "Having reviewed the data". E o participle passivo é só o particípio, sem being: "Being asked about the timeline" é possível, mas o registro executivo prefere o seco "Asked about the timeline".

Por que isto importa para você: você tem 11 minutos e um COO que para de anotar no slide três. O participle clause é a ferramenta que comprime "Nós revisamos os achados, e depois disso eu preparei uma proposta" em "Having reviewed the findings, I would propose..." — e devolve os seus segundos para a única parte que a sala quer ouvir: o que você encontrou, e o que você vai fazer.
