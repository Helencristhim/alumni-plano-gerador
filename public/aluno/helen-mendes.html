A "You ought to protect your downtime."
B "You might want to try a mindful walk."

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Modais de conselho

GRAMMAR

A força do conselho muda com o modal. / The strength of the advice changes with the modal.

ModalForça / StrengthExample / Exemplo
might want tomais suave (gentle suggestion)You might want to unwind first.

had bettermais forte, com consequência (warning)You had better rest, or you'll burn out.

should e had better usam o verbo na base, SEM "to": should rest, had better rest. Já ought TO e might want TO levam "to".

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- A linguagem da certeza

GRAMMAR

A força da afirmação muda com as palavras que você escolhe. / The strength of a claim changes with the words you choose.

LanguageForça / StrengthExample / Exemplo
clearly show / proveforte, 100% (only with solid data)The figures clearly show a rise.
suggests / seems tocuidadoso (careful, not fully sure)The data suggests a change.
may / might point topossível (an early signal)This may point to a new trend.

Depois de seems to use o verbo na base: seems to indicate (não "indicates"). Escolha palavras fortes só com dados sólidos; com dados parciais, suavize com suggests / seems to / may.

Stage 4: Situational Quiz

Quiz

Escolha a melhor recomendação para cada situação.

Stage 5: Think About It

Reflection

Sem certo ou errado. Pense em inglês, toque no microfone e fale livremente.

A colleague must choose between two options for a project: one is cheaper with a clear drawback, the other has a bigger benefit but a higher cost. Coach them with at least three recommendations, using a different structure each time, a soft one (could), a standard one (I'd recommend / should), and a strong one (had better). Use the new words: option, benefit, drawback, worthwhile, solution.
