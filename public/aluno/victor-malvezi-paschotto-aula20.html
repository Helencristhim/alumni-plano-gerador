Lesson 20 — Block 6 Closes

The Coaching Conversation

Help someone grow — by asking, not telling

Warm-up

You Gave the Review — Now Coach

A review is a snapshot of how someone did. Coaching is the film in between — the one-on-ones where they actually grow. The shift: in a review you tell; in coaching you ask, so they find their own answer.

Three Moves of a Coach

Ask It, Open It, Map It

1 — Ask, don't tell

A manager gives answers; a coach asks questions, so the person owns the plan.

2 — Open questions

"What would success look like?" beats "Are you on track?" — what and how, not yes/no.

3 — Map cause & effect

"If you block two hours a day, the deep work gets done" — a plan that works like a system.

Chapter 3

Asking, Not Telling

Open questions + the zero conditional — the grammar of a growth plan

Grammar Discovery

From Closed to Open

Watch the first word of each question — and the tense in the growth plan

Closed: "Are you on track?" → Open: "What would being on track look like?"

Closed: "Do you have a problem?" → Open: "What's getting in the way?"

Plan: "If you block two hours a day, the deep work gets done." (if + present, then present)

Open questions start with what or how. The growth plan is present + present: a general truth, not a maybe.

The Rule

Open Questions & the Zero Conditional

ToolShapeExample
Open questionWhat / How + clause (no yes/no)"What would success look like?"
Closed questionDo / Are / Can + ... (gets yes/no)"Are you on track?" — conversation dies
Zero conditionalIf + present, → present"If you block your mornings, the work gets done."
vs 1st conditionalIf + present, → will"If you block tomorrow, you will finish the deck."

Two coaching tools:

1. Ask open questions. Start with what or how (also why/who/which), never with do/does/are/can if you want reflection — a closed question gets a yes or a no and the conversation stops. 2. Map the plan with the zero conditional: if + present simple, present simple. It is for general truths and reliable cause and effect — "if she protects her mornings, the deep work gets done" (it always works that way, like a system). Compare the first conditional, if + present, will, which is one specific future result: "if she protects tomorrow morning, she will finish the deck."

Common Mistake

Two Traps in Coaching

✗ CLOSED QUESTION

"Are you happy with your progress?"

✓ OPEN QUESTION

"What would make this feel like real progress?"

✗ WILL AFTER IF

"If you will block your mornings, the work gets done."

✓ PRESENT AFTER IF

"If you block your mornings, the work gets done."

Open the question with what or how, and never put will straight after if.

Chapter 4

The Coaching Session

Victor coaches Sofia toward a stretch goal

Dialogue

Sofia's Coaching Session

V
This isn't a review, Sofia — it's your time. So let me start with a question: what would success look like for you a year from now?
S
Honestly? I'd be running my own region. But I'm not sure I'm ready, so I keep waiting.
V
That's a real stretch goal — and you have the potential for it. So what's the one roadblock between you and it right now?
S
I never get deep, strategic time. The day eats it. I'm always reacting, never thinking ahead.
V
Let's map that. If you block two hours every morning for strategy, the thinking gets done. And if you shadow a regional lead once a week, you build the bigger picture. Does that logic hold for you?
S
It does. And saying it out loud, I realize I've been waiting for permission I don't actually need.
V
Right there — that's the breakthrough, and it's yours, not mine. So what's one step you'll take this week to own it?
S
I'll block tomorrow morning for strategy, and I'll ask the regional lead if I can shadow her Friday. This is the first plan that actually feels like mine.
Comprehension

Did You Catch It?

1. What stretch goal does Sofia name, and what's holding her back?
Running her own region — she keeps waiting because she isn't sure she's ready (and, she realizes, waiting for permission she doesn't need).
2. How does Victor map the plan as cause and effect?
With zero conditionals: if she blocks two hours every morning for strategy, the thinking gets done; if she shadows a regional lead once a week, she builds the bigger picture.
3. How does Victor hand Sofia ownership at the end?
He says the breakthrough is hers, not his, and asks an open question — "What's one step you'll take this week to own it?" — so she commits to her own next steps.

Sharpen the Coaching

Open questions and a cause-and-effect plan — at speed

Quick Fire

Run the Session

1 / 5

1. Open the session with a forward-looking question.

"What would success look like for you a year from now?"

2. Turn this closed question open: "Are you stuck?"

"What's the one roadblock getting in the way right now?"

3. Map a plan as cause and effect (zero conditional).

"If you block two hours every morning, the deep work gets done."

4. Hand over ownership of the next step.

"So what's one step you could take this week to own it?"

5. She pauses and looks unsure. What do you do?

Hold the silence — don't rescue. "Take your time. What comes to mind?"
