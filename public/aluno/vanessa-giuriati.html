Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada palavra e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen para ouvir.

Confident -- feeling sure of yourself (confiante, seguro de si)
"I feel confident when I prepare before I speak."
Nervous -- worried or a little afraid (nervoso, ansioso)
"I feel nervous when I speak in front of new people."
Comfortable -- relaxed and at ease (confortável, à vontade)
"I feel comfortable when the conversation is calm."
Fluent -- able to speak smoothly and easily (fluente)
"My goal is to become fluent and speak without stopping."
Clear -- easy to understand, simple and direct (claro, nítido)
"I understand more when the speaker is slow and clear."
Calm -- peaceful, without stress (calmo, tranquilo)
"I feel calm when my day has a clear structure."
Organized -- good at planning, neat and ordered (organizado)
"I am an organized person, so I like a clear plan."
Goal -- something you want to achieve (objetivo, meta)
"My goal is to speak English with confidence."
Routine -- the things you usually do every day (rotina)
"My morning routine is calm: coffee, plan, and start work."
Speak -- to say words and talk to someone (falar)
"I want to speak more and stop feeling blocked."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

My name is Marta, and I am 45 years old. I am an organized person, so I have a clear routine every day. In the morning, I wake up early and I make a coffee. I like a calm start. At work, I speak with many people. Sometimes I feel nervous when I speak English, but I also feel more confident now. My friend Paula works with me. She is very clear when she explains things. My goal is simple: I want to speak English and feel comfortable, not afraid.

1. Why does Marta say "I am 45 years old" and not "I have 45 years"?
A Because "have" is the correct verb for age in English.
B Because in English we use "to be" for age: I am ... years old.
C Because both verbs are always correct for age.
2. Why does the text say "She works with me" and not "She work with me"?
A Because with he/she/it we add "-s" to the verb in the present simple.
B Because "work" is never correct in English.
C Because the sentence is about the past.
3. How does Marta feel about speaking English now?
A She always feels afraid and never speaks.
B She is already completely fluent.
C She sometimes feels nervous, but she feels more confident now.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple (to be / to have / to do)

GRAMMAR

Como falar de você mesma no presente. / How to talk about yourself in the present.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
Affirmative (I)I am organized. I have a routine.Eu sou organizada. Eu tenho uma rotina.
He / She (verb + s)She works. He likes coffee.Ela trabalha. Ele gosta de café.
NegativeI do not like to speak fast.Eu não gosto de falar rápido.
QuestionDo you like English? What is your name?Você gosta de inglês? Qual é o seu nome?
Age / IdadeUse "to be": I am 46 years old. (NUNCA "I have 46 years" / NEVER use "have" for age.)

Aula 02 -- Pre-class

Talking About Your Everyday Life

Present Simple for Real Routines. Key words: wake up, get ready, have breakfast, commute, check emails, have meetings, finish work, work out, cook, go to bed. Grammar: present simple + frequency adverbs (always/usually/often/sometimes/rarely/never).

Stage 1.1: Vocabulary Cards

Vocabulary

Ouça cada expressão e leia o exemplo. A tradução em português está ao lado. Toque em Listen.

Wake up -- to stop sleeping in the morning (acordar)
"I usually wake up at six thirty."
Get ready -- to prepare yourself before going out (se arrumar/se preparar)
"I get ready quickly in the morning."
Have breakfast -- to eat your first meal of the day (tomar café da manhã)
"I have breakfast with my family."
Commute -- to travel between home and work (deslocar-se para o trabalho)
"I commute by car for thirty minutes."
Check emails -- to look at and read your messages (checar e-mails)
"I check emails first thing in the morning."
Have meetings -- to take part in work discussions (ter reuniões)
"I have two or three meetings every day."
Finish work -- to stop working at the end of the day (terminar o trabalho)
"I usually finish work at six."
Work out -- to do physical exercise (malhar/se exercitar)
"I work out three times a week."
Cook -- to prepare and make food (cozinhar)
"In the evening, I cook dinner at home."
Go to bed -- to go to sleep at night (ir dormir)
"I never go to bed after eleven."

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Leia o texto e responda às perguntas.

Paula always wakes up at six. She gets ready quickly and she has breakfast with her son. She usually commutes by train, and she starts work at eight. In the morning, she checks emails and she often has meetings. She rarely takes a long lunch. In the evening, she sometimes works out, and then she cooks dinner. She never goes to bed late, because she does not like to feel tired. "On weekdays, I follow a clear routine," she says. "But on weekends, I do not set an alarm."

1. Why does the text say "She gets ready" and not "She get ready"?
A Because with he/she/it we add "-s" to the verb in the present simple.
B Because "get" is never correct.
C Because the sentence is in the past.
2. Where is the word "always" placed in "She always wakes up at six"?
A Before the main verb.
B After the main verb.
C At the end of the sentence only.
3. How often does Paula go to bed late?
A She always goes to bed late.
B She never goes to bed late.
C She usually goes to bed late.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- Present Simple & Frequency Adverbs

GRAMMAR

Como falar de hábitos e rotinas. / How to talk about habits and routines.

Form / FormaEnglishPortuguês
AffirmativeI start work at eight. She starts at nine.Eu começo a trabalhar às oito. Ela começa às nove.
NegativeI do not work on weekends. He does not drive.Eu não trabalho nos fins de semana. Ele não dirige.
QuestionDo you work out? What time does she finish?Você malha? A que horas ela termina?
Frequency adverbalways > usually > often > sometimes > rarely > neversempre > geralmente > frequentemente > às vezes > raramente > nunca
Position / PosiçãoBefore the main verb: I always wake up early. / With "to be": I am always busy. (Antes do verbo principal; depois do verbo "to be".)

Atividades Complementares

Materiais extras para reforçar o aprendizado fora de aula. Marque como concluído após assistir/ouvir.

Film
The Intern (2015) -- First 20 minutes

A 70-year-old intern joins a modern company and introduces himself to many new people. Calm pace, clear American English, and a warm, low-pressure tone. Direct connection to Lesson 1: how an adult professional introduces themselves and talks about their daily life with confidence.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Note 3 phrases the character uses to introduce himself.

Podcast
The English We Speak (BBC Learning English) -- Any recent episode

Short episodes (3-4 minutes) that teach one everyday expression at a time, slowly and clearly. Perfect for building confidence in small steps without pressure. Direct connection to Lesson 1: simple, natural English at a calm pace, exactly the way we will work together.

Tip: listen to one episode. Repeat the key expression out loud three times.

YouTube
TED Talk -- Amy Cuddy: "Your body language may shape who you are"

A famous, encouraging talk about confidence and how the way we hold ourselves can change how confident we feel. Directly relevant to the main barrier we are working on: feeling nervous before speaking. Connection to the lesson: the vocabulary of confidence and a powerful idea you can use before every class.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 words she uses to talk about confidence.

Series
The Crown (Netflix) -- Any episode, first 15 minutes

Clear, formal British English at a calm, measured pace. Characters often describe schedules, daily duties, and routines. Direct connection to Lesson 2: listen for present simple and how people describe what they do every day.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Write down 3 sentences that describe a daily activity.

Podcast
Plain English (DEF Media) -- Any recent episode

A podcast designed for B1/B2 learners, with controlled speed and clear pronunciation. Speakers naturally use the present simple to talk about facts, habits, and routines. Direct connection to Lesson 2: real, slow English about everyday life.

Tip: listen at normal speed first, then again. Note 5 present-simple verbs you hear.

YouTube
"My Daily Routine in English" -- BBC Learning English (6 Minute English)

A short, clear video where presenters discuss daily routines and habits, using present simple and frequency adverbs throughout. Direct connection to Lesson 2: the exact vocabulary and grammar we practiced, modeled by native speakers at a friendly pace.

Tip: watch with English subtitles. Repeat 3 sentences out loud about your own routine.
