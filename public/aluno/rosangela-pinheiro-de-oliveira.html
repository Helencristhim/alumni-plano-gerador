Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Rosângela is at the airport. She is from São Paulo, and she lives in Vila Leopoldina. She is 68 years old, and she travels to Europe once in a while, with her husband. Today her destination is Paris. Her flight is at seven o'clock, and the departure is from Terminal 3. Her passport and her boarding pass are in her carry-on. The check-in is quick, and she goes to the gate. But there is a delay of two hours, and the gate is B14 now. A traveler sits next to her and asks: "Where are you from?" Rosângela answers alone, in English, for the first time.

1. Why do we say "I am from São Paulo" and not "I live from São Paulo"?
A With a country or a city after "from", English uses the verb TO BE: I am from...
B Because "from" is always followed by the verb "to live".
C Because it is a question, and questions need "to be".
2. "I live in Vila Leopoldina." Why not "I am live in Vila Leopoldina"?
A Because "live" is already the verb. It does not need "am" in front of it.
B Because "live" is a noun here.
C Because the sentence is in the past.
3. "I travel to Europe once in a while." What does "once in a while" mean?
A Every single week, without exception.
B Sometimes, but not often.
C Only one time in my whole life.
4. "Where do you live?" Why does this question need DO, but "Where are you from?" does not?
A Because "live" is an irregular verb.
B Because the question is about the future.
C Because "to be" makes its own question (are you...?), and every other verb needs DO.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Verb To Be vs Present Simple

GRAMMAR

Two structures tell your whole story at the gate: one for WHO you are, one for WHAT you do.

FormUseExample
To be
I am · you are · it is		WHO you are, WHERE you are from, WHAT time something happens.I am Rosângela. I am from São Paulo. My flight is at seven.
Present simple
I live · I travel · I go		WHAT you do: your routine and the facts of your life. No "am" in front of it.I live in Vila Leopoldina. I travel with my husband.
Question with to be
Are you...? Is it...?		The verb to be makes its own question: it jumps in front of you.Where are you from? Is my flight on time?
Question with do
Do you...?		Every other verb needs DO to make a question.Where do you live? Do you travel a lot?
Age
I am 68 years old		In English you never HAVE an age. You ARE your age.I am 68 years old.

The one rule to remember: use am / is / are for who you are, and the verb alone (live, travel, go) for what you do. Never both together: "I am live" does not exist.

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

Last month Rosângela booked two tickets to Lisbon. On the internet, she chose two seats: a window seat for her husband, and an aisle seat for her. Yesterday she packed her checked bag: three dresses, two pairs of shoes, and a present for her friend. This morning they took a taxi, and they arrived at Terminal 3 at nine o'clock. At the counter, the agent put her bag on the machine: twenty-six kilos. Her luggage allowance was twenty-three. "This is excess baggage," he said. "There is a fee of one hundred dollars, but it depends on the weight." Rosângela did not pay. She opened her bag, she took out the present, and she put it in her husband's bag. The second time, her bag was twenty-two kilos. Then she asked one more question, alone, in English: "What is the boarding time?"

1. "Last month I booked the flight." Why does the verb end in -ed?
A Because "book" is a regular verb, and regular verbs make the past with -ed.
B Because the subject is "I". With "he" it would be different.
C Because the sentence is a question.
2. She chose an aisle seat. Why not "she choosed"?
A Because "choose" never changes in the past.
B Because "choose" is irregular: the past is a new word, "chose". No -ed.
C Because you only use -ed after "I", not after "she".
3. "Rosângela did not pay." Why is it "pay" and not "paid" here?
A Because "pay" has no past form.
B Because the sentence is about the present.
C Because "did" already carries the past. One past per sentence, so the verb goes back to normal.
4. The agent says the fee "depends on the weight". What does this mean?
A The fee is always one hundred dollars, for everybody.
B There is no single answer: the fee changes with the weight of the bag.
C The fee is free if your bag is heavy.

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple

GRAMMAR

One tense answers every question at the counter: what you already did.

FormUseExample
Regular
verb + -ed		Most verbs. You add -ed and it is done.I booked the flight. I packed my bag. I asked the agent.
Irregular
a new word		A small group. They change completely: learn them one by one.choose → chose · take → took · go → went · have → had · buy → bought
Negative
did not + verb		"Did" carries the past, so the verb goes back to normal. One past per sentence.I did not pay the fee. (never "did not paid")
Question
Did you + verb?		Same rule: "did" is the past, the verb stays normal.Did you pack this bag yourself? When did you book it?
Always the sameThe verb does not change with I / you / he / she. One form for everybody.I booked · she booked · we booked
Time expressionsThey tell you it is the past. When you hear one, use the past.yesterday · last week · last night · two days ago · a few hours ago

The one rule to remember: one past per sentence. If did is in the sentence, the verb goes back to normal: "I did not pay", never "I did not paid".

Stage 1.3: Grammar in Context

GRAMMAR

Read the text and answer the questions below.

This morning Rosângela arrived at the security check. The officer asked: "Did you pack this bag yourself?" — "Yes, I did," she answered. "I packed it yesterday." Then he asked: "Did you bring any liquids?" She knew the liquid restriction, so she left her water at home. "No, I did not," she said. Her bag went through the X-ray machine, and she walked through the metal detector. Then the officer said something fast, and she did not understand. She did not guess, and she did not look for her husband. She said: "I am sorry, I did not catch that. Could you repeat that, please?" He repeated it slowly: her keys were still in her pocket. After that, she walked past the duty-free and waited in the departure lounge. In Lisbon, at customs, the immigration officer asked why she was visiting. She had nothing to declare, and she said it alone, in English. It was a piece of cake.

1. "Did you pack this bag yourself?" Why is it "pack" and not "packed"?
A Because "pack" is an irregular verb.
B Because "did" already carries the past. One past per sentence, so the verb goes back to normal.
C Because the question is about the present.
2. The officer asks "Did you bring any liquids?" and the true answer is no. She says:
A "No, I did not brought."
B "No, I did not."
C "No, I not bring."
3. "She did not understand." Why not "she not understood"?
A Because in the past "not" never works alone: it needs "did". And after "did", the verb goes back to normal.
B Because "understand" has no past form.
C Because she is talking about the future.
4. She did not understand the officer, and she did not guess. What did she do?
A She looked for her husband and asked him to translate.
B She said yes, and hoped it was the right answer.
C She asked the officer to repeat: "I did not catch that. Could you repeat that, please?"

Stage 1.4: Grammar Tip -- The Past Simple: No and Question

GRAMMAR

In Lesson 2 you learned to say yes. Now you learn to say no — and to ask.

FormUseExample
Yes
verb in the past		The past of Lesson 2: regular (-ed) or irregular (a new word).I packed my bag. I brought my passport.
No
did not + verb		"Did" carries the past, so the verb goes back to normal. One past per sentence.I did not bring liquids. (never "did not brought")
Question
Did you + verb?		Same rule: "did" opens the question, the verb stays normal.Did you pack this bag yourself? Did you declare it?
Short answerDo not repeat the verb. Just "did" or "did not". This is what an officer expects."Did you pack it?" — "Yes, I did." / "No, I did not."
"not" alone
does not exist		In the past, "not" always needs "did" next to it."I did not understand." (never "I not understand")
Always the same"Did" does not change with I / you / he / she. One form for everybody.I did not · she did not · we did not

The one rule to remember: when did arrives, the verb relaxes. "I did not bring", never "I did not brought". "Did you pack?", never "Did you packed?".
