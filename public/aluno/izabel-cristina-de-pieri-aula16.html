Warm-Up

From the Post-Mortem to the Recovery

Last class you said what you would have done differently. The review is over now, and there are ten fixes to make. A leader does not do all ten alone. She delegates each one, names an owner, and gets the work done by the right people.

When ten problems land on your desk, how do you say who fixes what?

The Halo Recovery Plan

The Halo Recovery Plan

After the post-mortem, this plan sets out how AVRT will rescue the Halo line. The message is simple: we will not fix everything ourselves; we will have each problem handled by the right specialist and hold one owner accountable for each fix. The firmware defect comes first. We are having the code rewritten by an external security firm, and we will get the whole system retested before any customer sees it. This is the turnaround, and it starts with a full overhaul of the release process.

Each workstream has a clear handover. On forecasting, we are getting an independent analyst to rebuild the model, because it was the unrealistic forecast that caused the excess stock. On supply, we have had the contract reworked so that a missed date is escalated the same day, and we have asked the vendor to flag every delay in writing. Nobody signs off on a shipment now until quality has certified it. Where the team cannot rectify a gap in-house, we bring in a third party rather than let the problem drift.

What makes this plan different is ownership. In the past, a fault was noticed and nobody took ownership; this time each item has a named owner who must get the fix done and report on it every Thursday. Remediation is not optional, and it is not open-ended: every task has a date. The reason we will recover is not luck; it is that we are having the right work done by the right people, and we are getting the whole organization up to speed on the new process. Read fairly, the plan turns a setback into a turnaround.

The causative means you ARRANGE for someone else to do the work. Have / get + something + past participle focuses on the RESULT: 'We had the firmware rewritten' (someone else wrote it, not us). Have + someone + base verb, or get + someone + to + base, names the DOER: 'We had the vendor rebuild it' / 'We got the vendor to rebuild it'. Word order matters: have + OBJECT + past participle, never 'have rewritten the firmware' (that means you did it yourself).

Chapter 4: The Code

The Causative

have / get something done · have someone do · get someone to do

Grammar Discovery

Listen and Notice

"We are having the firmware rewritten by a firm."

"We got the process audited by a third party."

"We had the vendor flag every delay."

"We got an analyst to rebuild the model."

In each sentence, who does the work — you, or someone else?

PatternFormExample
Result (thing)have / get + object + past participleWe had the firmware rewritten.
Doer (have)have + person + base verbWe had the vendor flag delays.
Doer (get)get + person + to + base verbWe got an analyst to rebuild it.

Keep the order have + OBJECT + past participle. "We had the firmware rewritten" means someone else wrote it. "We had rewritten the firmware" (present perfect) means you did it.

Language Focus

Which Pattern?

AnalyseCausative: which pattern?
AWe are having the code rewritten by a firm.
BWe got the vendor to flag every delay.
CWe had the report audited by a third party.
DEach owner must get the fix done on time.
Two patterns. Result: have / get + OBJECT + past participle ('We had it fixed'). The doer: have + person + base, or get + person + to + base ('We had them fix it' / 'We got them to fix it'). Use 'get ... to' when there is effort or persuasion; use plain 'have ... do' for a neutral arrangement.
Common Mistake

Right vs Wrong

✗ WRONG

"We had rewritten the firmware by a firm."

✓ RIGHT

"We had the firmware rewritten by a firm."

✗ WRONG

"We got the vendor rebuild the module."

✓ RIGHT

"We got the vendor to rebuild the module."

✗ WRONG

"We had the vendor to flag every delay."

✓ RIGHT

"We had the vendor flag every delay."

For a result, keep the order have + object + past participle. With "get someone", use "to" + base verb. With "have someone", use the base verb with no "to".

Testing the Plan

A
Izabel, I read the recovery plan. It looks strong, but I want to test it. Are you and your team fixing all of this yourselves?
I
No. That is the point. We are having each problem handled by a specialist. We are having the firmware rewritten by an external security firm, not in-house.
A
Good. And the forecast that caused the excess stock?
I
We are getting an independent analyst to rebuild the model. I do not want the same people who built the bad forecast to grade their own work.
A
What about the process itself? A launch like this should never slip through again.
I
I have had the whole release checklist overhauled. Nobody signs off on a shipment now until quality has certified it, and any missed date is escalated to me the same day.
A
And when the team cannot fix something?
I
Then we bring in a third party rather than let it drift. Every item has one accountable owner who must get the fix done. That is how a setback becomes a turnaround.
